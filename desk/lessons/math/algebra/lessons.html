Lesson 1 — Order of Operations (PEMDAS)

Goal

Develop mastery of numerical and algebraic expression evaluation using conventional order of operations.

Learning objectives

  • State and explain PEMDAS (grouping, exponents, M/D left→right, A/S left→right).
  • Evaluate expressions correctly and rewrite ambiguous expressions clearly.

Lesson content

Introduce PEMDAS and emphasize that Multiplication and Division share a priority and are performed left-to-right; likewise Addition and Subtraction share priority (left-to-right). Discuss grouping symbols including parentheses, brackets, braces, and fraction bars.

Worked examples

  1. 6 + 3 × 2 = 12
  2. 2 × (3 + 2)^2 = 50
  3. 16 ÷ 4 × 2 = 8 (left-to-right)
  4. 8 ÷ 2(2 + 2) — show ambiguity and rewrite

Practice

  1. 9 + 6 ÷ 3
  2. (5 + 4) × 2
  3. 3^2 + 7
  4. 18 ÷ 3 × 2 + 5

Assessment & resources

Quiz: 10-minute in-class problems. Downloadable worksheet: PEMDAS worksheet (PDF).

Lesson 2 — Properties of Real Numbers

Goal

Learn and apply the commutative, associative, and distributive properties to simplify expressions.

Objectives

  • Identify and use commutative and associative laws for addition and multiplication.
  • Apply the distributive property a(b + c) = ab + ac.

Content & examples

Show examples, such as 3 + 5 = 5 + 3 (commutative), (2 + 3) + 4 = 2 + (3 + 4) (associative), and 2(x + 3) = 2x + 6 (distributive).

Practice

  1. Rewrite: 4(2 + x)
  2. Use distributive property to expand: (x + 5)2
