Lesson: The Nine Parts of Speech
Introduction

Every word in English belongs to a part of speech. Understanding parts of speech is like knowing the positions in a sports team‚Äîeach one plays a different role, but together they make the sentence work. There are nine traditional parts of speech in English.

1. Noun

Definition: A noun names a person, place, thing, or idea.
Examples: teacher, Africa, book, freedom
In a sentence: The teacher reads a book in the library.

2. Pronoun

Definition: A pronoun takes the place of a noun.
Examples: he, she, it, they, we, you, who
In a sentence: Maria is kind. She helps everyone.

3. Verb

Definition: A verb shows action or a state of being.
Examples: run, jump, think, is, was
In a sentence: The dog runs fast. / She is happy.

4. Adjective

Definition: An adjective describes or modifies a noun or pronoun.
Examples: tall, blue, difficult, three
In a sentence: The tall building has three floors.

5. Adverb

Definition: An adverb modifies a verb, adjective, or another adverb. It often tells how, when, where, or to what extent.
Examples: quickly, very, yesterday, here
In a sentence: She ran quickly. / He is very smart.

6. Preposition

Definition: A preposition shows the relationship between a noun (or pronoun) and another word in the sentence.
Examples: in, on, at, by, with, under, through
In a sentence: The cat is under the table.

7. Conjunction

Definition: A conjunction joins words, phrases, or clauses.
Examples: and, but, or, because, although
In a sentence: I like tea and coffee. / He stayed home because it rained.

8. Interjection

Definition: An interjection expresses sudden feeling or emotion.
Examples: Wow! Ouch! Hey! Oh!
In a sentence: ‚ÄúWow! That‚Äôs amazing,‚Äù she said.

9. Article/Determiner

Definition: An article (or determiner) introduces a noun and defines it as specific or unspecific.
Examples: a, an, the, this, that, these, those
In a sentence: I saw a bird in the tree. / Please close the door.

Summary

Nouns = names

Pronouns = replace nouns

Verbs = action/being

Adjectives = describe nouns

Adverbs = describe verbs, adjectives, or adverbs

Prepositions = show relationships

Conjunctions = join words/ideas

Interjections = express emotion

Articles/Determiners = introduce nouns

Together, these nine parts of speech are the foundation of English grammar.
