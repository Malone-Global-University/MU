K-12 Department

The K-12 Department provides a structured academic foundation across core and developmental disciplines, guiding students from early education through advanced secondary study. Programs build cumulative knowledge, strengthen critical thinking, and develop essential skills in literacy, quantitative reasoning, scientific analysis, and personal development. The curriculum integrates academic, technical, and physical domains to prepare students for higher education, professional pathways, and informed civic participation.

K-12 Programs

  • Arts

    The Arts program develops creativity and cultural expression through visual arts, music, and performance. Students build aesthetic awareness and the ability to communicate ideas through creative mediums.

  • English

    The English program strengthens reading comprehension, writing proficiency, and communication skills. Students engage with literature and composition to develop clarity of expression, critical interpretation, and effective argumentation.

  • Foreign Language

    The Foreign Language program provides early exposure to additional languages and cultures. Students develop basic communication skills and cultural awareness, preparing for advanced study in specialized language programs.

  • History

    The History program explores historical events, societies, and political developments that shape the modern world. Students analyze cause and effect, evaluate sources, and develop a structured understanding of civic and global dynamics.

  • Mathematics

    The Mathematics program develops numerical fluency, logical reasoning, and problem solving skills across grade levels. Students progress from foundational arithmetic to algebra, geometry, and advanced mathematical concepts, building the analytical capacity required for academic and technical fields.

  • Physical Education and Health

    This program promotes physical fitness, health literacy, and overall wellness. Students learn principles of exercise, nutrition, and personal health to support long term well being.

  • Science

    The Science program introduces students to biological, chemical, and physical systems through observation and experimentation. Learners build an understanding of scientific principles while developing inquiry based thinking and evidence driven analysis.

  • Social Studies

    The Social Studies program integrates history, civics, geography, and economics to build civic awareness and global understanding. Students analyze societal systems, governance structures, and historical developments shaping modern society.

  • Technology

    The Technology program introduces digital literacy, computational thinking, and foundational coding skills. Students learn to navigate, create, and analyze digital systems in a modern technological environment.
