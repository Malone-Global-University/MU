Course Overview

Wireless Networking teaches students how radio-based communication systems provide network connectivity without physical cabling. Learners develop practical competency in Wi-Fi standards, wireless infrastructure, RF fundamentals, access point deployment, security protocols, interference management, and wireless network design.

Wireless Networking Topics

  • Wireless Networking Overview

    Introduction to wireless communication systems and how WLAN technologies enable untethered connectivity.

  • Wi-Fi Standards and Protocols

    Instruction in 802.11 standards, protocol generations, throughput capabilities, and wireless evolution.

  • RF Fundamentals and Signal Behavior

    Practical guidance on radio frequency principles, signal propagation, attenuation, and environmental effects.

  • Wireless Access Points and Controllers

    Training in wireless infrastructure components, controller systems, and AP deployment architectures.

  • Wireless Security and Encryption

    Instruction in WPA/WPA2/WPA3, authentication methods, encryption, and wireless access control security.

  • Interference and Performance Optimization

    Guidance on diagnosing wireless interference, channel planning, signal tuning, and performance optimization.

  • Wireless Networking Best Practices

    Best practices for secure, scalable, and reliable wireless network deployment and management.
