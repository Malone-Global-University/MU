Course Overview

Enterprise Networking teaches students how large-scale organizational networks are architected, deployed, segmented, secured, and maintained across complex operational environments. Learners develop practical competency in enterprise network design, WAN/LAN architecture, segmentation strategy, redundancy, monitoring, and infrastructure planning.

Enterprise Networking Topics

  • Enterprise Network Architecture

    Introduction to layered enterprise network design and hierarchical infrastructure architecture models.

  • LAN, WAN, and Site Connectivity

    Instruction in local and wide-area networking models and inter-site communication design.

  • Segmentation and Security Zones

    Practical guidance on network segmentation, trust boundaries, VLAN zoning, and traffic isolation strategies.

  • Redundancy and High Availability Networking

    Training in failover design, redundant paths, resilient architectures, and availability planning.

  • Enterprise Monitoring and Telemetry

    Instruction in monitoring infrastructure health, telemetry systems, alerting, and operational visibility.

  • Capacity Planning and Growth

    Guidance on forecasting demand, scaling infrastructure, and planning enterprise network expansion.

  • Enterprise Networking Best Practices

    Best practices for designing and maintaining scalable, resilient, and secure enterprise networks.
