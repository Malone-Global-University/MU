Branch Overview

The Networking Practice branch provides hands-on instruction in configuring, managing, troubleshooting, and optimizing networked environments. Students learn how devices communicate, how modern networks are structured, and how to administer the hardware and protocols that enable reliable connectivity across local, wireless, and enterprise-scale infrastructure.

Networking Practice Courses

  • Network Configuration

    Practical instruction in configuring network hardware, IP addressing, subnetting, DHCP, DNS, NAT, and foundational LAN setup for real-world connectivity and infrastructure deployment.

  • Routing and Switching

    Instruction in packet forwarding, VLANs, switching logic, routing methods, inter-VLAN communication, and enterprise traffic management across networked systems.

  • Wireless Networking

    Practical training in Wi-Fi systems, wireless standards, RF considerations, wireless security, and deployment of consumer and enterprise wireless infrastructure.

  • Network Troubleshooting

    Diagnostic instruction in identifying, isolating, and resolving connectivity, latency, routing, hardware, and protocol issues using structured troubleshooting methodologies and network analysis tools.

  • Enterprise Networking

    Advanced network design and administration focused on segmentation, redundancy, WAN connectivity, monitoring, scalability, and large-scale organizational network architecture.
