Course Overview

Computational Thinking introduces students to the reasoning habits used to solve problems with computers and formal systems. Learners study decomposition, abstraction, modeling, algorithmic reasoning, logical structure, and computational problem design so they can approach technical challenges with clarity, precision, and repeatable problem-solving methods.
