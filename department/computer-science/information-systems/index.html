Program Overview

The Information Technology & Systems program provides a comprehensive foundation in enterprise IT operations, systems integration, cloud management, virtualization, and IT governance. Students learn how to build, deploy, and maintain the technical infrastructure that supports modern organizations.

Emphasis is placed on high-availability systems, infrastructure automation, service management frameworks, and cross-platform ecosystem design. The curriculum prepares learners for operational and administrative roles across corporate, nonprofit, and governmental environments.

Degree Pathways

  • Associate of Science in Information Technology (ASIT)
    A foundational degree emphasizing systems support, hardware management, and help-desk operations.
  • Bachelor of Science in Information Technology & Systems (BSITS)
    A comprehensive technical degree covering system architecture, cloud operations, infrastructure automation, database administration, and enterprise technology strategy.
  • Professional Certificate in IT Operations
    A focused credential covering essential enterprise tooling, monitoring, virtualization, and service management processes.

Course Catalog

  • ITS 101 — Fundamentals of Information Technology
    Introduction to enterprise systems, operating environments, and organizational IT roles.
  • ITS 120 — Hardware & Systems Support
    Diagnostics, troubleshooting, hardware architectures, and user-support models.
  • ITS 150 — Enterprise Networking Concepts
    Switching, routing, VLANs, subnets, and network service architecture.
  • ITS 201 — Cloud Platforms & Virtualization
    VM provisioning, hypervisors, cloud services, containerization fundamentals.
  • ITS 230 — Systems Integration & Automation
    DevOps concepts, configuration management, and cross-platform orchestration.
  • ITS 250 — IT Service Management (ITSM)
    Incident, problem, change, and asset management frameworks (ITIL-aligned).
  • ITS 301 — Enterprise Systems Administration
    Windows/Linux server management, directory services, and identity operations.
  • ITS 320 — Database Systems & Administration
    SQL, database performance, replication, and backup strategies.
  • ITS 350 — Cloud Security & Compliance
    Governance, configuration hardening, and regulatory frameworks for cloud platforms.
  • ITS 410 — IT Infrastructure Capstone
    Students design and deploy a full enterprise IT architecture with documentation.
