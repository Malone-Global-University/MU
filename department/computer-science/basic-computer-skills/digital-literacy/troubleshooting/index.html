Course Overview

Troubleshooting teaches students how to systematically identify, diagnose, and resolve common computer and technology problems. Learners develop practical problem-solving frameworks for isolating faults, testing assumptions, applying corrective actions, and determining when issues require escalation to advanced technical support.

Troubleshooting Topics

  • Troubleshooting Methodology

    Introduction to structured troubleshooting processes, including problem identification, hypothesis formation, testing, isolation, and resolution workflows.

  • Common Hardware Problems

    Recognition and diagnosis of typical hardware issues involving power, peripherals, displays, storage devices, cables, and physical system components.

  • Common Software Problems

    Practical diagnosis of application crashes, freezes, configuration issues, compatibility problems, and software malfunction scenarios.

  • Network and Connectivity Issues

    Instruction in diagnosing internet, Wi-Fi, router, DNS, and general network connectivity problems affecting user systems.

  • Updates, Drivers, and System Maintenance

    Understanding how software updates, device drivers, and maintenance operations affect system stability and troubleshooting outcomes.

  • Researching Errors and Finding Solutions

    Practical instruction in interpreting error messages, searching technical documentation, evaluating solutions, and using online resources responsibly.

  • When to Escalate or Seek Help

    Guidance on recognizing when a problem exceeds basic troubleshooting scope and requires specialized technical assistance or escalation.
