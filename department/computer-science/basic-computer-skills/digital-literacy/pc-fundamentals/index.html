Course Overview

PC Fundamentals introduces students to the essential components, functions, and operational concepts of modern personal computers. Learners develop baseline competency in computer hardware, operating systems, storage, desktop environments, settings, and routine digital operation, building the foundational understanding required for effective everyday computer use and future technical study.

PC Fundamentals Topics

  • What Is a Computer?

    An introduction to the purpose, role, and function of computers in modern society, including how computing devices process, store, and present information.

  • Computer Hardware Components

    Overview of the major physical components of a personal computer, including CPU, RAM, storage, motherboard, power supply, peripherals, and expansion devices.

  • Operating Systems Basics

    Introduction to the role of operating systems, desktop environments, system interfaces, and how users interact with software through modern operating platforms.

  • Storage and File Systems

    Explanation of how computers store data, organize files, manage drives, and structure information across local and removable storage media.

  • User Accounts and Settings

    Practical instruction in account management, permissions, personalization, accessibility settings, and user-level system configuration.

  • Input, Output, and Peripherals

    Study of how users interact with computers through keyboards, mice, monitors, printers, webcams, speakers, and external accessories.

  • Safe and Effective Computer Use

    Best practices for responsible, secure, and efficient daily computer operation, including updates, shutdown procedures, maintenance habits, and digital safety basics.
