Course Overview

Internet and Web Literacy teaches students how to navigate, evaluate, and use the modern internet effectively and responsibly. Learners develop practical competency in web browsing, online research, digital communication, account management, web safety, and understanding how online systems function in everyday personal and professional life.

Internet and Web Literacy Topics

  • How the Internet Works

    Introduction to the structure and function of the internet, including networks, servers, websites, browsers, and how digital information travels online.

  • Web Browsers and Navigation

    Practical instruction in browser use, tabs, bookmarks, navigation controls, browser settings, and efficient web navigation habits.

  • Search Engines and Online Research

    Instruction in effective searching, search operators, evaluating results, and conducting efficient online information gathering.

  • Accounts, Passwords, and Logins

    Practical guidance on creating, managing, and securing online accounts, passwords, authentication systems, and login workflows.

  • Downloads, Uploads, and Cloud Services

    Instruction in transferring files online, understanding download/upload workflows, and using cloud-based platforms and storage systems.

  • Internet Safety and Scam Awareness

    Training in identifying phishing, scams, malicious links, unsafe websites, suspicious downloads, and common online security threats.

  • Digital Citizenship and Online Conduct

    Instruction in responsible online behavior, privacy awareness, communication etiquette, platform conduct, and digital reputation management.
