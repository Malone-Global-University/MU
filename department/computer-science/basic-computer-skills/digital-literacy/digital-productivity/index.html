Course Overview

Digital Productivity teaches students how to use modern computing tools and workflow practices to complete tasks efficiently, stay organized, and operate effectively in digital work environments. Learners develop habits and systems for managing time, information, communication, multitasking, and routine digital workflows across personal and professional contexts.

Digital Productivity Topics

  • Typing and Keyboard Efficiency

    Practical methods for improving typing speed, keyboard familiarity, shortcut usage, and efficient text-entry workflow across digital environments.

  • Window and Desktop Management

    Instruction in managing multiple applications, desktops, windows, and workspace layouts for efficient multitasking and workflow organization.

  • Note Taking and Information Capture

    Best practices for capturing, organizing, and retrieving notes, ideas, research, and task-related information using digital tools.

  • Task Management and To-Do Systems

    Instruction in organizing work through digital task lists, productivity frameworks, scheduling systems, and workload planning methods.

  • Cloud Storage and File Sharing

    Practical instruction in using cloud storage platforms, shared folders, synchronization tools, and collaborative file distribution systems.

  • Calendar and Scheduling Tools

    Training in managing appointments, reminders, deadlines, recurring events, and coordinated scheduling through digital calendar systems.

  • Workflow Optimization Habits

    Practical strategies for reducing friction, improving efficiency, minimizing repetitive work, and building sustainable digital productivity systems.
