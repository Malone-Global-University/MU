Course Overview

Workflow Automation teaches students how to identify repetitive digital processes and improve them through templates, rules, integrations, automation platforms, and structured workflow design. Learners develop practical competency in streamlining business and administrative tasks through low-code, no-code, and platform-native automation methods.

Workflow Automation Topics

  • Identifying Automation Opportunities

    Instruction in recognizing repetitive, rule-based, and time-consuming tasks suitable for automation within digital workflows.

  • Templates and Standardization

    Practical methods for reducing repetitive work through reusable templates, structured forms, and standardized digital assets.

  • Rules, Filters, and Platform Automation

    Training in built-in automation features such as inbox rules, triggers, filters, conditional formatting, and workflow rules.

  • Integrations and Connected Workflows

    Instruction in linking software platforms together through integrations, connectors, APIs, and automation services.

  • Low-Code and No-Code Automation Tools

    Introduction to automation platforms that allow workflow construction without traditional programming.

  • Process Documentation and Maintenance

    Best practices for documenting automated workflows, maintaining reliability, updating logic, and managing operational dependencies.

  • Automation Risk and Governance

    Guidance on managing the risks, limitations, permissions, and governance considerations associated with workflow automation.
