Catalog of Computer Science Courses

  • Cybersecurity & Network Administration

    A comprehensive track focused on enterprise security posture, threat-surface management, and operational hardening. Students develop competencies in network architecture, defensive tooling, incident response, and governance frameworks. The curriculum emphasizes real-world security operations, including SIEM utilization, firewall strategy, intrusion detection, and regulatory compliance.

  • Data Science & Artificial Intelligence

    An analytics-driven program engineered to build proficiency across the modern data lifecycle. Learners gain expertise in statistical modeling, machine learning, neural networks, and large-scale data engineering. The coursework blends quantitative rigor with applied AI deployment, preparing students for advanced work in predictive analytics and automation.

  • Degree Programs

    A centralized catalog of structured academic pathways across the department. Degree tracks provide vertically integrated learning sequences, capstone requirements, and industry-aligned competencies designed to prepare graduates for high-impact technical roles.

  • Information Technology & Systems

    A systems-focused curriculum covering enterprise IT environments, systems integration, cloud operations, virtualization, and IT service management. Students develop operational mastery of infrastructure support, business information systems, and technology governance frameworks leveraged across modern organizations.

  • Network Adminstration

    A specialized program emphasizing hands-on configuration, optimization, and administration of networked environments. Coursework spans routing, switching, server management, DNS, DHCP, and network performance engineering. The track prepares candidates for operational roles in corporate infrastructure and distributed systems management.

  • Pan African Computer Science

    A culturally anchored, globally scoped program exploring the intersection of computer science, innovation economies, and Pan-African development. The curriculum frames technology as a strategic lever for economic expansion, digital sovereignty, and regional capacity-building across the continent and its diaspora.

  • Software Engineering & Fullstack Development

    A production-level engineering sequence focused on end-to-end application development. Students gain fluency in front-end and back-end frameworks, system design, API development, CI/CD pipelines, and quality assurance engineering. The program prioritizes scalable architecture and real-world code-delivery practices.

  • Understanding The Web

    An introductory deep dive into the mechanics of the modern internet. Topics include web protocols, browser architecture, hosting models, HTTP lifecycle operations, and the foundational technologies enabling online communication. The course equips learners with baseline literacy for navigating and building within the web ecosystem.

  • X Lab

    An experimental innovation hub where students prototype emerging technologies, test unconventional ideas, and work on cross-disciplinary research initiatives. The Lab functions as an R&D pipeline for advanced compute concepts, hardwareâ€“software integration, and entrepreneurial exploration.

  • Tools

    A curated overview of the technical toolchain utilized across the department. Students gain operational exposure to IDEs, version-control systems, cloud platforms, analytics stacks, and development utilities required for both academic and enterprise-grade execution.
