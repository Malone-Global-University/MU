Division Overview

The Degree Programs Division organizes structured academic pathways that combine theory, applied coursework, laboratory work, and capstone experiences into comprehensive credentialed programs. These pathways provide vertically integrated learning progressions designed to guide students from foundational competency to advanced specialization while aligning technical education with professional and academic standards.

Degree Program Categories

  • Associate Degrees

    Two-year foundational academic pathways designed to provide broad technical competency, practical skills, and introductory specialization across major computing disciplines. Associate programs serve as both workforce preparation and stepping stones into advanced study.

  • Bachelor’s Degrees

    Comprehensive undergraduate programs integrating theory, applied practice, laboratories, projects, and advanced specialization. Bachelor’s degrees prepare students for professional technical roles, advanced independent work, and graduate-level study.

  • Master’s Degrees

    Advanced graduate-level programs focused on specialized expertise, strategic systems understanding, research competency, and high-level professional or leadership preparation within technical and computational disciplines.

  • Certificates

    Focused short-form credential programs centered on targeted technical competencies, practical skill development, and role-specific knowledge. Certificates provide accelerated pathways for learners seeking concentrated expertise without full degree commitment.

  • Specialization Tracks

    Curated developmental pathways that organize recommended coursework into structured sequences aligned to particular industries, job functions, or technical specializations. Tracks help learners navigate the department according to professional goals and learning objectives.
