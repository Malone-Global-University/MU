Monetary Theory Core Curriculum Outline

Module 1: Foundations of Money

  • The Evolution of Money: Barter → Commodity → Fiat → Digital.html
  • Functions of Money: Medium, Unit, Store, Standard
  • Chararteristics of "Good" Money: Durability, Divisibility, Recognizability, Scarcity

Module 2: Theories of Money Supply and Demand

  • Quantity Theory of Money (MV = PY)
  • Monetarist Perspective (Milton Friedman)
  • Keynesian Liquidity Preference and Money Demand
  • Endogenous Money Theory (Post-Keynesian / MMT View)
  • Endogenous of Money and its Stability

Module 3: Inflation, Deflation, and Price Stability

  • Types: Demand-Pull, Cost-Push, Built-In Inflation
  • Hyperinflation: Causes, Cases, and Consequences
  • Deflation and Liquidity Traps
  • Inflation Targeting: Pros and Pitfalls
  • Real vs. Nominal Variables

Module 4: Interest Rates and Monetary Transmission

  • Value of Money & Interest Rate Determination
  • Funds vs. Liquidity Preference Theories
  • Yield Curve, Term Structure of Interest Rates
  • How Monetary Policy Affects the Real Economy

Module 5: Central Banking and Monetary Policy

  • Role and Tools of Central Banks:
  • Open Market Operations
  • Reserve Requirements
  • Discount Window / Interest Rates
  • Inflation Targeting vs. Dual Mandate
  • Policy Rules: Taylor Rule vs. Discretion
  • Independence and Credibility of Central Banks

Module 6: Money Creation and Banking System

  • Fractional Reserve Banking
  • Commercial Bank Money vs. Central Bank Money
  • Credit Creation Multiplier
  • Shadow Banking System
  • Bank Runs and Deposit Insurance

Module 7: Unconventional and Modern Tools

  • Quantitative Easing (QE) and QT (tightening)
  • Negative Interest Rates
  • Forward Guidance
  • Yield Curve Control
  • Central Bank Digital Currencies (CBDCs)
  • Modern Monetary Theory (MMT): Premises and Criticisms

Module 8: Expectations and Monetary Dynamics

  • Rational vs. Adaptive Expectations
  • Policy Credibility and Time Inconsistency (Kydland & Prescott)
  • Role of Forward Guidance and Transparency
  • Inflation Expectations and Anchoring

Module 9: Exchange Rates and Monetary Sovereignty

  • Fixed, Floating, and Managed Exchange Rates
  • Currency Pegs and Speculative Attacks
  • Purchasing Power Parity (PPP)
  • Interest Rate Parity & Capital Mobility
  • Trilemma of International Finance

Module 10: Money and the Macro Economy

  • IS-LM Model and Monetary Equilibrium
  • AD-AS Model and Policy Shocks
  • Phillips Curve: Short-Run Tradeoffs, Long-Run Breakdown
  • Money Neutrality vs. Superneutrality
  • Fiscal-Monetary Coordination

Module 11: Digital & Alternative Monetary Systems

  • Cryptocurrencies and Decentralized Finance (DeFi)
  • Stablecoins and Programmable Money
  • Private Scrips and Corporate Monetary Zones (e.g., Malone Corporate Scrip)
  • Dual Circulation Systems (e.g., China’s strategy)

Module 12: Political Economy of Monetary Systems

  • Central Bank Governance and Democratic Accountability
  • Monetary Imperialism and Currency Hegemony (e.g., U.S. Dollar System)
  • Sanctions, SWIFT, and Weaponized Finance
  • Monetary Systems as Tools of Power, Liberation, or Control
