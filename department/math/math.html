Level 1 – Foundations
  • Precalculus & Algebra – algebraic manipulation, functions, exponents, logarithms
  • Trigonometry & Geometry – trigonometric functions, Euclidean geometry, proofs
  • Foundations of Mathematical Thinking – logic, sets, induction, proofs
  • Calculus I (Differential Calculus) – limits, derivatives, applications
  • Mathematical Communication – writing solutions, notation, LaTeX basics
Level 2 – Core Mathematics
  • Calculus II (Integral Calculus) – integrals, techniques, series
  • Linear Algebra – vectors, matrices, linear systems, eigenvalues
  • Discrete Mathematics – combinatorics, graph theory, algorithms foundations
  • Probability & Statistics I – probability models, distributions, descriptive stats
  • Differential Equations (Intro) – ODEs, models, solution techniques
  • Numerical Methods (Intro) – numerical root finding, interpolation, error analysis
Level 3 – Applied & Specialized
  • Real Analysis – rigorous treatment of limits, continuity, sequences, series
  • Abstract Algebra – groups, rings, fields, symmetry
  • Multivariable Calculus – partial derivatives, multiple integrals, vector calculus
  • Probability & Statistics II – inference, regression, hypothesis testing
  • Partial Differential Equations – classification, separation of variables, Fourier methods
  • Mathematical Modeling – building and analyzing models from real-world systems
Level 4 – Advanced / Elective Specializations
  • Topology – continuity, compactness, connectedness, algebraic topology intro
  • Optimization & Convex Analysis – linear/quadratic programming, convexity, duality
  • Computational Mathematics & Scientific Computing – algorithms for large-scale problems
  • Cryptography & Number Theory – modular arithmetic, public-key basics
  • Stochastic Processes – Markov chains, Poisson processes, Brownian motion
  • Advanced Statistics & Machine Learning – inference, classification, dimensionality reduction
  • History & Philosophy of Mathematics – development of key ideas and their cultural context
Practical Training & Projects
  • Applied Project Lab – multidisciplinary projects with CS, engineering, finance partners
  • Computational Project – implement algorithms, simulations, and numerical experiments
  • Research Seminar – student-led research presentations and paper reading groups
  • Internship / Industry Placement – data science, actuarial, research or engineering roles
  • Capstone Thesis / Project – original research or intensive modeling/analysis project
Electives & Cross-Disciplinary Options
  • Data Science for Mathematicians – pipelines, visualization, applied ML for math problems
  • Actuarial Mathematics – life contingencies, risk pools, exam preparation tracks
  • Mathematics Education – pedagogy, curriculum design, community tutoring practicum
  • Financial Mathematics – stochastic calculus intro, option pricing basics
  • Bio-mathematics – population models, epidemiology, systems biology modeling
Program Features & Learning Outcomes
  • Students will construct and write rigorous mathematical proofs and reason abstractly.
  • Students will apply calculus, linear algebra, and probability to model and solve real problems.
  • Students will implement numerical algorithms and validate results computationally.
  • Graduates will be prepared for graduate study or quantitative roles in industry (data science, engineering, finance).
  • Opportunities for research, cross-disciplinary collaboration, and community-focused math outreach.

Recommended progression: solidify foundation (Levels 1–2), choose applied vs theoretical track in Level 3, and pursue deep specialization or research capstone in Level 4.
