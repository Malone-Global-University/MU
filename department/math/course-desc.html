Topics include: Variables, expressions, and operations with real numbers Solving linear equations and inequalities, including absolute value and compound inequalities Graphing linear equations, inequalities, and functions Systems of equations and inequalities (graphing, substitution, elimination) Polynomials and factoring techniques (including special products, synthetic division, and the Remainder Theorem) Quadratic functions and equations (factoring, completing the square, quadratic formula, complex roots) Rational expressions and equations, radicals and rational exponents Exponential and logarithmic functions, sequences, and series Conic sections (circles, parabolas, ellipses, hyperbolas) Probability and statistics basics (permutations, combinations, binomial probability, normal distribution) Introduction to trigonometry (angles, unit circle, trigonometric functions, graphs, identities, basic equations) Course Objectives By the end of this course, students will be able to: Solve linear, quadratic, radical, rational, exponential, and logarithmic equations. Analyze and graph a wide variety of functions. Apply algebraic methods to solve real-world problems. Prepare for College Algebra, Pre-Calculus, and other advanced mathematics courses. Instructional Methods Lecture with guided examples Problem-solving workshops Online exercises and assessments Projects involving real-world applications Evaluation Homework and in-class exercises: 30% Quizzes and unit tests: 40% Final cumulative exam: 30% Notes Students who have successfully completed Algebra 1 elsewhere may receive credit for the first half of this course. This course is appropriate for students preparing for College Algebra, Pre-Calculus, or STEM-related programs.
