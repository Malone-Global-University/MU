Learn classical geometric constructions through precise, step-by-step compass and straightedge methods.

Lesson Description

This lesson introduces the classical tools of geometry: the compass and straightedge. Students learn how to perform precise constructions without measuring tools, relying only on geometric reasoning. These constructions form the foundation for more advanced geometric work.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand rules for compass and straightedge constructions.
  • Construct perpendicular bisectors and angle bisectors.
  • Construct perpendicular and parallel lines.
  • Construct basic geometric figures using classical methods.

Topics Covered

  • Rules of classical constructions
  • Copying segments and angles
  • Perpendicular bisector construction
  • Angle bisector construction
  • Constructing perpendicular and parallel lines

Key Terms

  • Compass: Tool used to draw circles and arcs.
  • Straightedge: Tool used to draw straight lines without measurement marks.
  • Construction: Drawing figures using only compass and straightedge.
  • Bisector: A line or ray that divides something into two equal parts.
  • Perpendicular Lines: Lines intersecting at right angles.

Step-by-Step Constructions

Example 1: Constructing a Perpendicular Bisector

  1. Draw segment AB.
  2. Set compass radius greater than half of AB.
  3. Draw arcs from A above and below the segment.
  4. Without changing radius, repeat from point B.
  5. Connect arc intersections to form the bisector.

Example 2: Constructing an Angle Bisector

  1. Draw an arc centered at the vertex intersecting both rays.
  2. From both intersection points, draw arcs that cross.
  3. Draw a line from the vertex through the arc intersection.

Example 3: Constructing a Perpendicular Line Through a Point

  1. Draw a line and mark point P on it.
  2. Use the compass to mark equal points on both sides of P.
  3. Construct arcs above or below the line.
  4. Connect arc intersections through P.

Practice Problems

  1. Construct the perpendicular bisector of a given segment.
  2. Copy a given angle using compass and straightedge.
  3. Construct a line parallel to a given line through a point.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Do not change compass width mid-construction unless instructed.
  • Light arcs help keep diagrams readable.
  • Ensure arcs intersect cleanly for accurate results.

Summary

Compass and straightedge constructions rely on geometric reasoning rather than measurement. Mastery of these basics prepares students for more advanced constructions involving polygons, loci, and classical geometric challenges.

Challenge Problems

  1. Construct an equilateral triangle using only a given segment.
  2. Construct the midpoint of a segment without measuring.
  3. Construct a square given one side.
