Lesson Description

This lesson introduces tessellations, the tiling of a plane using shapes without gaps or overlaps, and explores repeating patterns with regular polygons.

Learning Objectives

  • Define tessellations and identify tessellating shapes.
  • Understand how interior and exterior angles influence tiling.
  • Create and analyze repeating geometric patterns.

Topics Covered

  • Definition of tessellation
  • Regular polygons and tiling rules
  • Interior and exterior angles in tessellations
  • Creating repeating patterns
  • Applications in art and architecture

Key Terms

  • Tessellation: A pattern of shapes that completely covers a plane without gaps or overlaps.
  • Regular Polygon: A polygon with all sides and angles equal.
  • Interior Angle: The angle formed inside a polygon.
  • Exterior Angle: The angle formed outside a polygon, supplementary to the interior angle.
  • Edge-to-Edge Tessellation: Tessellation where sides of polygons coincide exactly.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Check that the sum of angles around a point equals 360Â° for tessellation.
  • Not all regular polygons tessellate the plane (e.g., pentagons cannot alone tessellate).

Summary

Tessellations cover planes using repeated shapes without gaps. Only triangles, squares, and hexagons among regular polygons can tessellate alone. Understanding interior and exterior angles is key to analyzing patterns.
