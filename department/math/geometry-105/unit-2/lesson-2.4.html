Lesson Description

This lesson covers counterexamples and proof by contradiction, two essential techniques in geometric reasoning for validating or disproving statements.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the concept of a counterexample in geometry.
  • Learn how to construct a proof by contradiction.
  • Apply these methods to evaluate geometric statements.

Topics Covered

  • Counterexamples: definition and examples
  • Proof by contradiction methodology
  • Logical reasoning in geometry
  • Evaluating statements using counterexamples

Key Terms

  • Counterexample: An example that shows a statement is false.
  • Proof by Contradiction: A proof that assumes the negation of the statement and derives a contradiction.
  • Negation: The opposite of a statement.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Using a Counterexample

  1. Statement: "All quadrilaterals are rectangles."
  2. Provide a counterexample: a square or trapezoid.
  3. Conclusion: The statement is false because the counterexample exists.

Practice Problems

  1. Find a counterexample to the statement: "All triangles have two equal sides."
  2. Use proof by contradiction to show that there is no smallest positive rational number.
  3. Determine whether a given statement about angles is true or false using a counterexample.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Ensure the counterexample satisfies all conditions of the original statement.
  • In proof by contradiction, clearly identify the assumption and the contradiction.
  • Avoid confusing counterexamples with special cases that do not disprove the statement.

Summary

Counterexamples are a powerful tool for disproving false statements, while proof by contradiction allows us to confirm truths by showing the impossibility of their negation.

Challenge Problems

  1. Provide a counterexample for the statement: "All parallelograms have right angles."
  2. Use proof by contradiction to show that the sum of a rational and an irrational number is irrational.
  3. Determine a statement about triangles and prove it using a contradiction.
