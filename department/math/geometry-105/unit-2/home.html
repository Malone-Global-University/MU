Unit 2: Logic and Geometric Reasoning

Develop the reasoning skills required for geometry by mastering logic, conditional statements, postulates, and formal proof writing.

Home Department Mathematics Geometry 105 Unit 2: Logic and Geometric Reasoning

Lessons

  • Lesson 2.1: Conditional Statements
    Understand if-then statements, hypotheses, conclusions, converses, inverses, and contrapositives.
  • Lesson 2.2: Truth Tables & Logical Equivalence
    Use truth tables to evaluate logical statements and determine equivalence.
  • Lesson 2.3: Postulates & Theorems
    Learn the difference between postulates and theorems and how they form the foundation of geometric reasoning.
  • Lesson 2.4: Introduction to Proofs
    Write two-column proofs using logical steps, definitions, postulates, and theorems.
  • Lesson 2.5: Algebraic Reasoning in Geometry
    Apply algebraic techniques to solve geometric equations and justify reasoning.
