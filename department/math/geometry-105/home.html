Geometry 105

A structured introduction to classical Euclidean geometry and deductive reasoning.

Home Department Mathematics Course: Geometry 105 - Main Page

Course Description

Geometry 105 develops formal logical reasoning through the study of classical Euclidean geometry. Students progress from foundational definitions and postulates to triangle congruence, similarity, circle theorems, spatial reasoning, and geometric transformations.

Course Information

  • Course Code: MATH 105
  • Course Title: Geometry 105
  • Department: Mathematics
  • Prerequisite: Algebra I or equivalent
  • Course Length: 16 Weeks

Learning Outcomes

  • Construct formal two-column and paragraph proofs
  • Apply triangle congruence and similarity criteria
  • Solve right triangle and trigonometric problems
  • Analyze circle relationships including arcs, chords, and tangents
  • Calculate area, surface area, and volume
  • Apply rigid and non-rigid transformations
  • Demonstrate deductive reasoning and logical precision

Course Units

Unit 1: Foundations of Geometry

Points, lines, planes, segments, angle relationships, and basic constructions.

Unit 2: Logic and Geometric Reasoning

Conditional statements, converses, biconditionals, and formal proof writing.

Unit 3: Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

Transversal angle relationships, proving lines parallel, and coordinate applications.

Unit 4: Triangle Congruence

SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS, HL, CPCTC, and triangle inequalities.

Unit 5: Properties of Triangles

Medians, altitudes, angle bisectors, perpendicular bisectors, and concurrency points.

Unit 6: Polygons and Quadrilaterals

Interior and exterior angle sums, parallelograms, rectangles, rhombuses, squares, trapezoids, and kites.

Unit 7: Similarity

Similarity criteria, proportional reasoning, and indirect measurement.

Unit 8: Right Triangles and Trigonometry

Pythagorean Theorem, special right triangles, and basic trigonometric ratios.

Unit 9: Area and Volume

Area of polygons and circles; surface area and volume of three-dimensional solids.

Unit 10: Circles

Arcs, inscribed angles, chords, secants, tangents, and sector measures.

Unit 11: Transformations

Translations, reflections, rotations, dilations, symmetry, and composition of transformations.

Unit 12: Advanced Geometric Constructions

Compass-and-straightedge constructions including inscribed polygons, locus constructions, and classical geometric construction problems.

Unit 13: Vectors in the Coordinate Plane

Vector representation, magnitude and direction, vector addition and subtraction, and geometric applications in the coordinate plane.

Unit 14: Truth Tables and Logic Rigor

Logical connectives, truth tables, equivalence, and rigorous deductive structures supporting formal geometric proofs.

Assessment Structure

  • Unit Quizzes
  • Proof Writing Assessments
  • Problem-Solving Exams
  • Midterm Examination
  • Cumulative Final Examination

Pathway Progression

Geometry 105 prepares students for advanced mathematics including Pre-Calculus, Calculus, Discrete Mathematics, and Linear Algebra.
