Lesson Description

This lesson introduces the dot product of vectors and shows how it is used to compute angles between vectors. Students will also examine proofs related to orthogonality, magnitude relationships, and geometric applications.

Learning Objectives

  • Compute the dot product of two vectors.
  • Determine angles between vectors using formulas.
  • Recognize perpendicular vectors using dot product rules.
  • Understand and apply proofs involving vector relationships.

Topics Covered

  • Dot product formula
  • Angle between vectors
  • Orthogonal (perpendicular) vectors
  • Magnitude relationships
  • Vector proofs and applications

Key Terms

  • Dot Product: Scalar result of multiplying vector components and summing them.
  • Orthogonal Vectors: Vectors whose dot product equals zero.
  • Angle Between Vectors: Angle determined using dot product and magnitudes.
  • Magnitude: Length of a vector.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Computing Dot Product

  1. Let \( \vec{a} = \langle 2, 3 \rangle \) and \( \vec{b} = \langle 4, -1 \rangle \).
  2. Dot product: \( (2)(4) + (3)(-1) = 8 - 3 = 5 \).

Example 2: Finding Angle Between Vectors

  1. Use formula: \( \vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}| \cos \theta \).
  2. Solve for angle: \( \theta = \cos^{-1} \left( \frac{\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b}}{|\vec{a}||\vec{b}|} \right) \).

Example 3: Perpendicular Vectors

  1. If dot product equals zero, vectors are perpendicular.
  2. Example: \( \langle 1, 2 \rangle \cdot \langle 2, -1 \rangle = 2 - 2 = 0 \).

Practice Problems

  1. Find the dot product of \( \langle 3,1 \rangle \) and \( \langle 2,5 \rangle \).
  2. Determine if vectors \( \langle 4,2 \rangle \) and \( \langle 1,-2 \rangle \) are perpendicular.
  3. Find the angle between vectors \( \langle 1,0 \rangle \) and \( \langle 1,1 \rangle \).

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Do not confuse dot product with cross product.
  • Always compute magnitudes correctly when finding angles.
  • Remember: dot product equals zero implies perpendicular vectors.

Summary

The dot product connects algebra and geometry by allowing calculation of angles and relationships between vectors. It is a key tool for determining perpendicularity and proving geometric properties.

Challenge Problems

  1. Prove that vectors with zero dot product are perpendicular.
  2. Find vectors perpendicular to \( \langle 3,4 \rangle \).
  3. Show algebraically how dot product relates to the Law of Cosines.
