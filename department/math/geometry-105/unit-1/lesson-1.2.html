Lesson Description

This lesson introduces line segments, rays, and angles. Students will learn how to measure angles, classify angle types, and understand basic angle relationships such as complementary and supplementary angles.

Learning Objectives

  • Define and identify line segments and rays.
  • Measure angles using degree notation.
  • Classify angles (acute, right, obtuse, straight).
  • Solve simple problems involving complementary and supplementary angles.

Topics Covered

  • Line Segments and Rays
  • Angle Notation and Measurement
  • Types of Angles
  • Complementary and Supplementary Angles

Key Terms

  • Line Segment: A portion of a line with two endpoints.
  • Ray: A part of a line with one endpoint extending infinitely in one direction.
  • Angle: Formed by two rays with a common endpoint (vertex).
  • Vertex: The common endpoint of two rays forming an angle.
  • Complementary Angles: Two angles whose measures sum to \(90^\circ\).
  • Supplementary Angles: Two angles whose measures sum to \(180^\circ\).

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Classifying an Angle

If \( m\angle ABC = 35^\circ \), classify the angle.

Since \(35^\circ < 90^\circ\), the angle is acute.

Example 2: Complementary Angles

If one angle measures \(50^\circ\), find its complement.

\[ 90^\circ - 50^\circ = 40^\circ \]

Example 3: Supplementary Angles

If one angle measures \(120^\circ\), find its supplement.

\[ 180^\circ - 120^\circ = 60^\circ \]

Practice Problems

  1. Classify a \(75^\circ\) angle.
  2. Find the complement of a \(28^\circ\) angle.
  3. Find the supplement of a \(145^\circ\) angle.
  4. If two angles are complementary and one is \(x\), write an equation to represent the relationship.

  • Always include degree symbols when writing angle measures.
  • Remember that a straight angle measures exactly \(180^\circ\).

Summary

Segments and angles build directly from points and lines. Understanding how to measure and classify angles, along with recognizing complementary and supplementary relationships, forms a critical foundation for future geometric proofs and constructions.

Challenge Problems

  1. If two supplementary angles are equal, what is the measure of each?
  2. An angle is three times its complement. Find the angle.
  3. Two angles are supplementary. One is \(x + 20\), the other is \(2x - 10\). Solve for \(x\).
