Lesson Description

This lesson introduces the Pythagorean Theorem and special right triangle ratios (45-45-90 and 30-60-90). Students will calculate missing side lengths and apply structured algebraic reasoning to right triangle problems.

Learning Objectives

  • Apply the Pythagorean Theorem to find missing sides.
  • Identify and use 45-45-90 and 30-60-90 triangle ratios.
  • Simplify radical expressions accurately.

Topics Covered

  • Pythagorean Theorem: \( a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \)
  • Identifying the hypotenuse and legs
  • 45-45-90 triangle ratio: \( x, x, x\sqrt{2} \)
  • 30-60-90 triangle ratio: \( x, x\sqrt{3}, 2x \)
  • Radical simplification

Key Terms

  • Pythagorean Theorem: Relationship between sides of a right triangle.
  • Legs: The two sides forming the right angle.
  • Hypotenuse: The side opposite the right angle.
  • Special Right Triangle: A right triangle with predictable side ratios.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Using the Pythagorean Theorem

Given legs 6 and 8, find the hypotenuse.

\[ c^2 = 6^2 + 8^2 = 36 + 64 = 100 \]

\[ c = 10 \]

Example 2: 45-45-90 Triangle

If a leg is 5, the hypotenuse is:

\[ 5\sqrt{2} \]

Example 3: 30-60-90 Triangle

If the shortest leg is 4, then:

Longer leg = \(4\sqrt{3}\)

Hypotenuse = \(8\)

Practice Problems

  1. Find the hypotenuse if legs are 9 and 12.
  2. In a 45-45-90 triangle, if one leg is 7, find the hypotenuse.
  3. In a 30-60-90 triangle, if the hypotenuse is 10, find the shorter leg.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always verify which side is the hypotenuse before applying the formula.
  • Do not round prematurely unless instructed.
  • Simplify radicals fully.

Summary

The Pythagorean Theorem and special right triangle ratios provide efficient tools for solving right triangle problems. Mastery of these relationships reduces computational complexity and improves problem-solving speed.
