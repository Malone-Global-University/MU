Lesson Description

This lesson applies trigonometric ratiosâ€”sine, cosine, and tangentâ€”to solve contextual word problems involving right triangles. Emphasis is placed on modeling, equation setup, calculator usage, and interpretation of results.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify appropriate trig ratios based on known information.
  • Set up and solve equations using sine, cosine, and tangent.
  • Interpret solutions within real-world contexts.

Topics Covered

  • \( \sin \theta = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} \)
  • \( \cos \theta = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}} \)
  • \( \tan \theta = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} \)
  • Angle of elevation and angle of depression
  • Calculator use and rounding conventions

Key Terms

  • Sine (sin): Ratio of opposite side to hypotenuse.
  • Cosine (cos): Ratio of adjacent side to hypotenuse.
  • Tangent (tan): Ratio of opposite side to adjacent side.
  • Angle of Elevation: Angle measured upward from horizontal.
  • Angle of Depression: Angle measured downward from horizontal.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Label triangles clearly before choosing a ratio.

Summary

Trig ratios allow us to convert angle measures into measurable distances. Proper setup, ratio selection, and interpretation are essential for accurate modeling.
