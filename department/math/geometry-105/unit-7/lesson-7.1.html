Lesson Description

This lesson introduces triangle similarity and the formal criteria used to prove triangles are similar: AA (Angle-Angle), SAS (Side-Angle-Side), and SSS (Side-Side-Side). Students will learn to identify corresponding parts and apply proportional reasoning.

Learning Objectives

  • Define similar triangles and corresponding parts.
  • Apply AA, SAS, and SSS similarity criteria.
  • Set up and solve proportions using similar triangles.
  • Justify similarity using geometric reasoning.

Topics Covered

  • Definition of similar polygons
  • Corresponding angles and sides
  • AA Similarity Postulate
  • SAS Similarity Theorem
  • SSS Similarity Theorem
  • Writing similarity statements

Key Terms

  • Similar Figures: Figures with congruent corresponding angles and proportional corresponding sides.
  • Corresponding Parts: Matching sides or angles in similar figures.
  • AA Similarity: Two pairs of corresponding angles are congruent.
  • SAS Similarity: Two sides are proportional and the included angle is congruent.
  • SSS Similarity: All three pairs of sides are proportional.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: AA Similarity

If \( \angle A = \angle D \) and \( \angle B = \angle E \), then \( \triangle ABC \sim \triangle DEF \) by AA similarity.

Example 2: SAS Similarity

If \( \frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{AC}{DF} \) and \( \angle A = \angle D \), then triangles are similar by SAS.

Example 3: SSS Similarity

If \( \frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{BC}{EF} = \frac{AC}{DF} \), then triangles are similar by SSS.

Summary

Triangles are similar when corresponding angles are congruent and corresponding sides are proportional. AA, SAS, and SSS provide reliable criteria for proving similarity and solving proportional problems.

Challenge Problems

  1. Prove two triangles are similar given a parallel line cuts a triangle.
  2. Explain why AAA is sufficient for similarity but not congruence.
  3. If two sides are proportional but the included angle is not equal, are triangles similar? Explain.
