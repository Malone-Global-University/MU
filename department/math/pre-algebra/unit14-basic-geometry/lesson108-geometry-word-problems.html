Lesson 107: Introduction to Coordinate Geometry

Understand the coordinate plane, ordered pairs, and how points are located and graphed in coordinate geometry.

Lesson Description

Coordinate geometry combines algebra and geometry by placing points on a grid called the coordinate plane. Students learn how ordered pairs describe locations, how axes divide the plane into quadrants, and how to plot and identify points. This lesson forms the foundation for graphing shapes and analyzing geometric relationships using coordinates.

Learning Objectives

  • Identify parts of the coordinate plane.
  • Plot and read ordered pairs correctly.
  • Determine which quadrant a point lies in.

Topics Covered

  • The coordinate plane and axes
  • Ordered pairs (x, y)
  • Quadrants and point locations

Key Terms

  • Coordinate Plane: A grid formed by horizontal and vertical number lines.
  • Ordered Pair: A pair of numbers (x, y) that locates a point.
  • Quadrant: One of four sections of the coordinate plane.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Plotting a Point

  1. Plot the point (3, 2).
  2. Move 3 units right on the x-axis.
  3. Move 2 units up on the y-axis and mark the point.

Example 2: Identifying a Quadrant

  1. Consider the point (-4, 5).
  2. x is negative and y is positive.
  3. The point lies in Quadrant II.

Practice Problems

  1. Plot the point (2, 6).
  2. Plot the point (-3, -4).
  3. Identify the quadrant of (5, -2).
  4. Identify the quadrant of (-7, 3).

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always move along the x-axis first, then the y-axis.
  • Watch the signs of numbers to choose the correct direction.
  • Do not switch the order of coordinates.

Summary

Coordinate geometry uses ordered pairs to locate points on a coordinate plane. Understanding axes, quadrants, and plotting points is essential for graphing shapes and solving geometry problems involving location and distance.

Challenge Problems

  1. List two points that lie in Quadrant III.
  2. Explain why the point (0, 5) is not in any quadrant.
  3. Plot three points and connect them to form a triangle.
