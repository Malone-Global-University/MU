Lesson 104: Perimeter & Area Formulas

Learn how to measure the distance around shapes and the space inside them using standard perimeter and area formulas.

Lesson Description

This lesson introduces perimeter and area formulas for common geometric shapes. Students learn how to calculate the distance around figures and the amount of surface they cover, forming a foundation for later geometry and real-world measurement problems.

Learning Objectives

  • Calculate perimeter of common geometric shapes.
  • Apply area formulas correctly for rectangles, triangles, and circles.
  • Solve practical measurement problems involving area and perimeter.

Topics Covered

  • Perimeter formulas for polygons
  • Area formulas for rectangles and triangles
  • Area of circles

Key Terms

  • Perimeter: The total distance around a shape.
  • Area: The amount of space inside a shape.
  • Radius: Distance from the center of a circle to its edge.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Perimeter of a Rectangle

  1. Rectangle length = 8 m and width = 5 m.
  2. Perimeter formula: P = 2(L + W).
  3. P = 2(8 + 5) = 26 m.

Summary

Perimeter measures distance around shapes, while area measures space inside them. Knowing and applying the correct formulas allows you to solve many real-life measurement problems.
