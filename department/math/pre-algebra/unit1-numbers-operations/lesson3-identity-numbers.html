Lesson 3: Identity Numbers

Learn about identity numbers for addition and multiplication and how they affect arithmetic operations.

Home Pre-Algebra Unit 1: Numbers & Operations Lesson 3: Identity Numbers

Lesson Overview

Identity numbers are special numbers in mathematics that do not change other numbers in an operation. Understanding these numbers helps simplify calculations.

Topics Covered

  • Additive identity (0): Any number plus 0 equals the number itself.
  • Multiplicative identity (1): Any number multiplied by 1 equals the number itself.
  • Examples in addition and multiplication
  • Why identity numbers are important in algebra

Lesson Examples

Example 1: 7 + 0 = 7 (0 is the additive identity)

Example 2: 9 √ó 1 = 9 (1 is the multiplicative identity)

Example 3: -5 + 0 = -5; 0 √ó 12 = 0

Practice Problems

  1. Identify the additive identity and multiplicative identity.
  2. Compute: 12 + 0, -8 + 0, 15 √ó 1, -6 √ó 1.
  3. Explain why multiplying any number by 0 does not follow the identity property of multiplication.
  4. Give a real-life example of the additive identity.
