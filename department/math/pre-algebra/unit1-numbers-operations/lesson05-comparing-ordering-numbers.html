Lesson 05: Comparing & Ordering Numbers

Master comparing numbers, identifying greater and lesser values, and arranging numbers in ascending or descending order using number lines and symbols.

Home Department Pre-Algebra Unit 1: Numbers & Operations Lesson 05: Comparing & Ordering Numbers

Lesson Description

This lesson teaches students to compare numbers using symbols and number lines, and to order numbers from least to greatest or greatest to least.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand and use comparison symbols (<, >, =).
  • Compare positive and negative numbers.
  • Arrange numbers in ascending and descending order.

Topics Covered

  • Comparison symbols
  • Comparing positive and negative numbers
  • Ordering numbers on the number line

Key Terms

  • Greater Than (>): Indicates the first number is larger than the second.
  • Less Than (<): Indicates the first number is smaller than the second.
  • Ascending Order: Arranging numbers from least to greatest.
  • Descending Order: Arranging numbers from greatest to least.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Comparing Numbers

  1. Compare -3 and 2.
  2. Since -3 is to the left of 2 on the number line, -3 < 2.
  3. Write: -3 < 2.

Example 2: Ordering Numbers

  1. Order the numbers: 4, -2, 0, -5.
  2. Place them on a number line: -5, -2, 0, 4.
  3. Ascending order: -5, -2, 0, 4; Descending order: 4, 0, -2, -5.

Practice Problems

  1. Compare 7 and -3 using < or >.
  2. Order these numbers from least to greatest: 5, -1, -7, 3.
  3. Order these numbers from greatest to least: 0, -4, 2, -6.
  4. Determine which number is greater: -8 or -2.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Remember negative numbers are always less than positive numbers.
  • Use a number line to visualize positions of numbers.
  • Do not confuse magnitude with value; -2 is greater than -5.

Summary

Comparing and ordering numbers helps students understand relative values. Using number lines and comparison symbols ensures clarity when working with positive and negative numbers.

Challenge Problems

  1. Arrange -12, 7, 0, -5, 3 in ascending order.
  2. Compare -6, -9, 4, 2 using < or > symbols.
  3. Create a real-life scenario where ordering numbers is useful (e.g., temperatures, bank balances).
