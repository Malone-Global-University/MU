Lesson Description

This lesson introduces the concepts of rational and irrational numbers, explains their properties, and provides examples to distinguish between the two.

Learning Objectives

  • Define rational and irrational numbers.
  • Identify examples of rational and irrational numbers.
  • Compare and classify numbers within the number system.

Topics Covered

  • Rational Numbers (fractions, integers, terminating/repeating decimals)
  • Irrational Numbers (non-repeating, non-terminating decimals)
  • Number line placement
  • Real Number System overview

Key Terms

  • Rational Number: A number that can be expressed as a fraction a/b, where a and b are integers and b â‰  0.
  • Irrational Number: A number that cannot be expressed as a simple fraction; its decimal expansion is non-repeating and non-terminating.
  • Real Numbers: The set of all rational and irrational numbers.
