Lesson Description

This lesson introduces factor trees as a visual tool to break numbers into their prime factors. You will learn how to express numbers in prime factorization form.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand what prime factorization means.
  • Use factor trees to break down numbers.
  • Write numbers as a product of prime factors.

Key Terms

  • Prime Number: A number greater than 1 with exactly two factors (1 and itself).
  • Composite Number: A number with more than two factors.
  • Prime Factorization: Writing a number as a product of prime numbers.
  • Factor Tree: A diagram used to break a number into factors.

Example 1: Factor Tree of 24

  1. Start with 24.
  2. Break into 4 × 6.
  3. Break 4 into 2 × 2.
  4. Break 6 into 2 × 3.
  5. Prime factorization: 2 × 2 × 2 × 3 or 2³ × 3.

Example 2: Factor Tree of 36

  1. Start with 36.
  2. Break into 6 × 6.
  3. Break each 6 into 2 × 3.
  4. Prime factorization: 2 × 2 × 3 × 3 or 2² × 3².

Practice Problems

  1. Find the prime factorization of 30.
  2. Find the prime factorization of 45.
  3. Find the prime factorization of 72.
  4. Use exponents to write the prime factorization of 100.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always continue factoring until all numbers are prime.
  • Different factor trees may look different but result in the same prime factorization.
  • Use exponents to simplify repeated prime factors.

Summary

Factor trees help break numbers into their prime components. Prime factorization is essential for understanding GCF, LCM, and simplifying expressions.

Challenge Problems

  1. Find the prime factorization of 84.
  2. Find the prime factorization of 150.
  3. Explain why prime factorization is unique for every number.
