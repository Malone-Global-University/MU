Lesson 14: Multiples

Understand how to find multiples of numbers and apply this knowledge in factoring, LCM, and problem-solving exercises.

Home Department Math Pre-Algebra Unit 2: Factors & Multiples Lesson 14: Multiples

Lesson Description

This lesson teaches how to identify multiples of numbers, explore patterns, and apply this knowledge to solve Pre-Algebra problems involving factors, LCM, and divisibility.

Learning Objectives

  • Define multiples and recognize them for any given number.
  • Use multiples to solve problems involving LCM and factors.
  • Apply multiples in real-world problem-solving scenarios.

Topics Covered

  • Definition of multiples
  • Listing multiples of a number
  • Using multiples to find LCM
  • Patterns in multiples

Key Terms

  • Multiple: The product of a number and an integer.
  • Least Common Multiple (LCM): The smallest number that is a multiple of two or more numbers.
  • Factor: A number that divides another number exactly.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Start multiplying by 1 and proceed sequentially to avoid missing multiples.
  • Check multiples carefully when finding LCM to ensure accuracy.

Summary

Multiples are key to understanding number relationships and solving problems involving LCM, GCF, and divisibility. Mastery of multiples strengthens foundation for higher-level Pre-Algebra concepts.
