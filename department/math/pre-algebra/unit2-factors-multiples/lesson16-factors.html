Lesson 16: Factors

Learn to find all factors of a number and understand their properties, laying the foundation for prime factorization and divisibility concepts.

Home Department Math Pre-Algebra Unit 2: Factors & Multiples Lesson 16: Factors

Lesson Description

This lesson focuses on identifying all factors of numbers, understanding factor properties, and using factors to solve problems.

Learning Objectives

  • Define what a factor is.
  • List all factors of a given number.
  • Apply factors to solve problems and simplify calculations.

Topics Covered

  • Definition of factors
  • Listing factors of numbers
  • Properties of factors
  • Using factors in problem-solving

Key Terms

  • Factor: A number that divides another number evenly without leaving a remainder.
  • Divisor: Another term for factor.
  • Composite Number: A number with more than two factors.

Lesson Examples

Example 2: Factors of 18

  1. 1√ó18=18 ‚Üí 1, 18
  2. 2√ó9=18 ‚Üí 2, 9
  3. 3√ó6=18 ‚Üí 3, 6
  4. All factors: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18

Practice Problems

  1. List all factors of 24.
  2. List all factors of 36.
  3. Is 7 a factor of 56?
  4. Identify whether 12 is a factor of 144.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always start with 1 and the number itself when listing factors.
  • Check divisibility by integers sequentially to avoid missing factors.
  • Do not include numbers that do not divide evenly.

Summary

Factors are numbers that divide evenly into a given number. Identifying all factors is crucial for understanding prime factorization, LCM, GCF, and solving arithmetic problems efficiently.

Challenge Problems

  1. List all factors of 60 and 90. Identify the common factors.
  2. Find all factors of a 2-digit prime number and explain the result.
  3. Create a word problem involving factors and solve it.
