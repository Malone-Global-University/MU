Learn how to compare quantities using ratios and solve proportion problems in everyday situations.

Lesson Description

This lesson introduces the concept of ratios to compare quantities and shows how to set up and solve proportions in real-world contexts.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand and write ratios in different forms.
  • Solve proportion problems using cross-multiplication.
  • Apply ratios and proportions to real-life scenarios.

Topics Covered

  • Definition of a ratio
  • Equivalent ratios
  • Solving proportions

Key Terms

  • Ratio: A comparison of two quantities by division.
  • Proportion: An equation stating that two ratios are equal.
  • Cross-Multiplication: A method for solving proportions by multiplying diagonally.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Writing a Ratio

  1. Compare 8 apples to 4 oranges.
  2. Write the ratio as 8:4.
  3. Simplify to 2:1.

Example 2: Solving a Proportion

  1. Solve for x: 3/4 = x/12
  2. Cross-multiply: 3*12 = 4*x ‚Üí 36 = 4x
  3. Divide both sides by 4: x = 9

Practice Problems

  1. Write the ratio of 15 pens to 5 pencils in simplest form.
  2. Solve: 5/8 = x/32
  3. Two quantities are in the ratio 7:3. If the larger quantity is 21, find the smaller.
  4. Solve: x/9 = 4/6

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always simplify ratios to their lowest terms.
  • Check units when applying ratios to real-world problems.
  • Use cross-multiplication carefully and maintain equality.

Summary

Ratios allow you to compare quantities, and proportions let you solve for unknown values. Mastery of these concepts is essential for scaling, rates, and percent calculations in later lessons.

Challenge Problems

  1. The ratio of cats to dogs in a shelter is 5:2. If there are 35 cats, how many dogs are there?
  2. A map uses a scale of 1 inch : 50 miles. How many miles are represented by 7 inches on the map?
  3. Solve for x: 9/x = 12/16
