Lesson Description

This lesson introduces simple interest, a fundamental concept in finance, and teaches how to apply the formula I = P √ó R √ó T (Interest = Principal √ó Rate √ó Time) to solve real-life problems involving loans, savings, and investments.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the formula for simple interest.
  • Calculate interest earned or owed for a given principal, rate, and time.
  • Apply simple interest to real-life scenarios, such as loans and savings.

Topics Covered

  • Simple interest formula: I = P √ó R √ó T
  • Calculating interest for different time periods
  • Applications to loans, savings, and real-world finance

Key Terms

  • Principal (P): The initial amount of money borrowed or invested.
  • Rate (R): The annual interest rate as a decimal or percentage.
  • Time (T): The time the money is borrowed or invested, usually in years.
  • Simple Interest (I): The extra money earned or paid, calculated as P √ó R √ó T.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Calculating Simple Interest

  1. A loan of $5000 is taken at 6% simple interest per year for 3 years.
  2. Interest: I = P √ó R √ó T = 5000 √ó 0.06 √ó 3
  3. I = $900. Total repayment = 5000 + 900 = $5900

Example 2: Savings Account

  1. $2000 is invested at 4% simple interest for 5 years.
  2. Interest: I = 2000 √ó 0.04 √ó 5 = $400
  3. Total amount after 5 years: 2000 + 400 = $2400

Practice Problems

  1. A person invests $1500 at 5% simple interest for 4 years. Calculate the interest earned.
  2. Find the total amount to repay for a $2500 loan at 7% simple interest over 2 years.
  3. If $1200 is invested at 3% for 6 years, what is the total interest earned?
  4. A loan of $800 at 6% simple interest is repaid after 1.5 years. Calculate the interest.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always convert the interest rate to decimal before calculating (e.g., 5% ‚Üí 0.05).
  • Ensure the time period matches the rate period (years vs months).
  • Remember that simple interest does not compound.

Summary

Simple interest is a straightforward method for calculating interest on loans or investments. Understanding it helps in making informed financial decisions and solving practical money-related problems.

Challenge Problems

  1. An investment of $5000 earns 6% simple interest for 7 years. Find the total interest earned.
  2. A person borrows $2000 at 8% simple interest for 3.5 years. How much interest is owed?
  3. If a bank offers $1000 at 5% simple interest per year, how long will it take to earn $250 interest?
