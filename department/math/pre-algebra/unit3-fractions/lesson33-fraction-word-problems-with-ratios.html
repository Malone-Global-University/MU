Lesson Description

This lesson focuses on solving fraction-based word problems involving ratios and real-world applications.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand how ratios compare quantities.
  • Translate ratio problems into fraction equations.
  • Solve real-world fraction word problems.
  • Evaluate answers for real-world accuracy.

Topics Covered

  • Understanding ratios
  • Ratio-to-fraction conversion
  • Fraction operations in word problems
  • Real-world problem solving

Key Terms

  • Ratio: A comparison of two quantities.
  • Part-to-Part Ratio: Compares two different parts of a whole.
  • Part-to-Whole Ratio: Compares one part to the total.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Ratio Distribution

  1. The ratio of boys to girls is 3:4 with 28 students total.
  2. Total ratio parts = 3 + 4 = 7.
  3. 28 √∑ 7 = 4 students per part.
  4. 3 √ó 4 = 12 boys.

Example 2: Fraction of a Recipe

  1. Recipe uses 3/4 cup sugar.
  2. Half recipe = 3/4 √ó 1/2.
  3. Multiply numerators and denominators.
  4. Answer: 3/8 cup.

Example 3: Comparing Fractions

  1. Compare 2/3 and 3/4.
  2. Common denominator = 12.
  3. 2/3 = 8/12 and 3/4 = 9/12.
  4. 3/4 is greater.

Practice Problems

  1. The ratio of red to blue marbles is 2:5. If there are 35 marbles, how many are red?
  2. A class finished 5/6 of a 240 page book. How many pages were read?
  3. Which is larger: 5/8 or 7/12?
  4. A recipe uses 2/3 cup of milk. You make 3 batches. How much milk is needed?
  5. The ratio of cats to dogs is 4:3. If there are 28 animals, how many are dogs?

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always identify total parts before solving ratio problems.
  • Translate ratios into fractions or equations.
  • Check units and real-world reasonableness.

Summary

Fraction word problems often involve ratios and real-world comparisons. Converting ratios into fractions helps simplify calculations and improves accuracy.

Challenge Problems

  1. The ratio of apples to oranges is 5:3 with 64 fruits total. How many apples?
  2. A tank is 7/8 full. If it holds 400 gallons, how much water is inside?
  3. Create your own ratio word problem and solve it.
