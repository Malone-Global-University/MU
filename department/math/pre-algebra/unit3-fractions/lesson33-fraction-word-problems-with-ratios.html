Understanding Ratios

A ratio compares two quantities.

Ratios can be written in three ways:

  • a to b
  • a : b
  • a/b

When written as a fraction, ratios can be solved using fraction operations.

Example 1: Simple Ratio

The ratio of boys to girls in a class is 3:4. There are 28 students total. How many boys are there?

Step 1: Add ratio parts.

3 + 4 = 7 total parts

Step 2: Divide total students by total parts.

28 ÷ 7 = 4 students per part

Step 3: Multiply by number of boy parts.

3 × 4 = 12 boys

Example 2: Fraction of a Whole

A recipe uses 3/4 cup of sugar. If you make half the recipe, how much sugar do you use?

Step 1: Multiply by 1/2.

3/4 × 1/2 = 3/8

You need 3/8 cup of sugar.

Example 3: Comparing Fractions

A tank is 2/3 full. Another tank is 3/4 full. Which tank has more water?

Step 1: Find common denominator (12).

2/3 = 8/12 3/4 = 9/12

9/12 is greater than 8/12, so the second tank has more water.

Problem-Solving Strategy

  1. Read carefully.
  2. Identify what is being compared.
  3. Write the ratio or fraction.
  4. Use fraction operations.
  5. Check that your answer makes sense.

Practice Problems

  1. The ratio of red to blue marbles is 2:5. If there are 35 marbles total, how many are red?
  2. A class finished 5/6 of a book. If the book has 240 pages, how many pages were read?
  3. Which is larger: 5/8 or 7/12?
  4. A recipe calls for 2/3 cup of milk. You make 3 batches. How much milk is needed?
  5. The ratio of cats to dogs is 4:3. If there are 28 animals total, how many are dogs?
