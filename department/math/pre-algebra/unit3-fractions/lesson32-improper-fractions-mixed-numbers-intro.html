Lesson 32: Improper Fractions & Mixed Numbers

Learn how to convert between improper fractions and mixed numbers.

Home Department Math Pre-Algebra Unit 3: Fractions Lesson 32

What Is an Improper Fraction?

An improper fraction is a fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator.

Examples:

  • 7/4
  • 9/3
  • 11/5

Improper fractions represent numbers that are greater than or equal to 1.

What Is a Mixed Number?

A mixed number has a whole number and a fraction.

Examples:

  • 1 3/4
  • 2 1/5
  • 4 2/3

Mixed numbers also represent values greater than 1.

Converting Improper Fractions to Mixed Numbers

Step 1: Divide the numerator by the denominator.

Step 2: The quotient is the whole number.

Step 3: The remainder becomes the new numerator.

Step 4: Keep the same denominator.

Example:

7 ÷ 4 = 1 remainder 3

7/4 = 1 3/4

Converting Mixed Numbers to Improper Fractions

Step 1: Multiply the whole number by the denominator.

Step 2: Add the numerator.

Step 3: Keep the same denominator.

Example:

1 3/4

(1 × 4) + 3 = 7

1 3/4 = 7/4

Why This Matters

Most fraction operations (especially multiplication and division) are easier when written as improper fractions.

Being able to switch between forms gives you flexibility in solving problems.

Practice Problems

  1. Convert 9/5 to a mixed number.
  2. Convert 13/6 to a mixed number.
  3. Convert 2 2/3 to an improper fraction.
  4. Convert 5 1/8 to an improper fraction.
  5. Explain why 4/4 is both improper and equal to 1.
