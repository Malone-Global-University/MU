What Is a Reciprocal?

A reciprocal is what you get when you flip a fraction.

If a number is written as:

a/b

Its reciprocal is:

b/a

Examples

  • The reciprocal of 3/5 is 5/3
  • The reciprocal of 7/2 is 2/7
  • The reciprocal of 4 is 1/4
  • The reciprocal of 1/6 is 6

Important: Whole numbers can be written as fractions over 1.

Example: 4 = 4/1

Key Rule

When you multiply a number by its reciprocal, the result is always:

1

Example:

(3/5) × (5/3) = 15/15 = 1

This is why reciprocals are also called multiplicative inverses.

Why Reciprocals Matter

Reciprocals are used when dividing fractions.

Division Rule:

Keep → Change → Flip

  • Keep the first fraction
  • Change division to multiplication
  • Flip the second fraction (use its reciprocal)

Example:

2/3 ÷ 4/5

Keep → Change → Flip:

2/3 × 5/4 = 10/12 = 5/6

Important Exception

Zero does not have a reciprocal.

Why?

Because you cannot divide by zero. There is no number that you can multiply by 0 to get 1.

Practice Problems

  1. Find the reciprocal of 9/11.
  2. Find the reciprocal of 8.
  3. Find the reciprocal of 2/3.
  4. Multiply 5/7 by its reciprocal.
  5. Rewrite 4/9 ÷ 2/5 using Keep-Change-Flip.
