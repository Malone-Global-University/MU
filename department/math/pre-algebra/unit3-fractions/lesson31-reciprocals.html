Lesson 31: Reciprocals

Learn how reciprocals work and how they simplify dividing fractions.

Lesson Description

This lesson introduces reciprocals, explains how to find them, and demonstrates how they are used to convert division of fractions into multiplication.

Learning Objectives

  • Define reciprocals and multiplicative inverses.
  • Find reciprocals of fractions and whole numbers.
  • Use reciprocals to divide fractions using Keep-Change-Flip.
  • Recognize why zero does not have a reciprocal.

Topics Covered

  • Definition of reciprocals
  • Reciprocals of fractions and whole numbers
  • Multiplicative inverse property
  • Division of fractions using reciprocals

Key Terms

  • Reciprocal: A fraction formed by flipping the numerator and denominator.
  • Multiplicative Inverse: A number that, when multiplied by the original number, equals 1.
  • Keep-Change-Flip: A method used to divide fractions by converting division into multiplication using the reciprocal.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Finding Reciprocals

  1. The reciprocal of 3/5 is 5/3.
  2. The reciprocal of 7/2 is 2/7.
  3. The reciprocal of 4 is 1/4 (rewrite 4 as 4/1 first).
  4. The reciprocal of 1/6 is 6/1 = 6.

Example 2: Multiplying by a Reciprocal

  1. Multiply 3/5 √ó 5/3.
  2. Multiply numerators: 3 √ó 5 = 15.
  3. Multiply denominators: 5 √ó 3 = 15.
  4. Result: 15/15 = 1.

Example 3: Dividing Using Keep-Change-Flip

  1. Divide 2/3 √∑ 4/5.
  2. Keep the first fraction: 2/3.
  3. Change division to multiplication.
  4. Flip the second fraction: 5/4.
  5. Multiply: 2/3 √ó 5/4 = 10/12 = 5/6.

Practice Problems

  1. Find the reciprocal of 9/11.
  2. Find the reciprocal of 8.
  3. Find the reciprocal of 2/3.
  4. Multiply 5/7 by its reciprocal.
  5. Rewrite 4/9 √∑ 2/5 using Keep-Change-Flip.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always rewrite whole numbers as fractions over 1 before finding reciprocals.
  • Do not flip the first fraction when dividing.
  • Remember that zero does not have a reciprocal.

Summary

Reciprocals allow division of fractions to be rewritten as multiplication. Understanding multiplicative inverses simplifies complex fraction operations and strengthens algebra skills.

Challenge Problems

  1. Explain why multiplying a number by its reciprocal equals 1.
  2. Solve 5/8 √∑ 10/3 using Keep-Change-Flip.
  3. Create your own division problem involving reciprocals and solve it step-by-step.
