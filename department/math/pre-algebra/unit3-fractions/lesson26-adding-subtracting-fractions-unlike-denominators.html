Lesson 26: Adding & Subtracting Fractions (Unlike Denominators)

Learn to add and subtract fractions with different denominators by finding common denominators and simplifying results.

Home Department Math Pre-Algebra Unit 3: Fractions Lesson 26: Adding & Subtracting Fractions (Unlike Denominators)

Lesson Description

This lesson teaches students how to add and subtract fractions with unlike denominators by finding a common denominator, converting fractions, performing the operation, and simplifying results.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the need for a common denominator when adding or subtracting fractions.
  • Convert fractions to equivalent fractions with a common denominator.
  • Add and subtract fractions with unlike denominators and simplify the results.

Topics Covered

  • Identifying unlike denominators
  • Finding the least common denominator (LCD)
  • Converting to equivalent fractions
  • Adding and subtracting fractions
  • Simplifying the answer

Key Terms

  • Unlike Denominators: Denominators that are not the same in two or more fractions.
  • Least Common Denominator (LCD): The smallest number that is a multiple of each denominator.
  • Equivalent Fraction: A fraction that has the same value as another fraction but a different numerator and denominator.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Adding Fractions

  1. Add 1/4 + 1/6.
  2. Find the LCD of 4 and 6: 12.
  3. Convert each fraction: 1/4 = 3/12, 1/6 = 2/12.
  4. Add: 3/12 + 2/12 = 5/12.

Example 2: Subtracting Fractions

  1. Subtract 5/8 - 1/3.
  2. Find the LCD of 8 and 3: 24.
  3. Convert: 5/8 = 15/24, 1/3 = 8/24.
  4. Subtract: 15/24 - 8/24 = 7/24.

Practice Problems

  1. Add 2/5 + 3/10.
  2. Subtract 7/12 - 1/4.
  3. Add 3/8 + 5/6.
  4. Subtract 9/10 - 2/3.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always find the LCD before performing the operation.
  • Convert both fractions to equivalent fractions with the common denominator.
  • Don‚Äôt forget to simplify your final answer if possible.

Summary

Adding and subtracting fractions with unlike denominators requires finding a common denominator, converting fractions, performing the operation, and simplifying. Mastery of this skill is crucial for working with fractions in Pre-Algebra.

Challenge Problems

  1. Add 7/9 + 2/5 and simplify your answer.
  2. Subtract 11/12 - 3/8 and simplify your answer.
  3. Create a word problem involving adding or subtracting fractions with unlike denominators.
