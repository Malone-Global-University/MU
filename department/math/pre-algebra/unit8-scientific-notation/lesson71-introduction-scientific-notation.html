Lesson Description

Scientific notation is a method of writing extremely large or extremely small numbers in a compact, standardized form. A number is written as a value between 1 and 10 multiplied by a power of 10. This form makes calculations easier and helps scientists, engineers, and mathematicians work efficiently with very large or very small quantities.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the structure of scientific notation.
  • Identify the coefficient and exponent in scientific notation.
  • Write large and small numbers in scientific notation.

Key Terms

  • Scientific Notation: A way of writing numbers as a coefficient multiplied by a power of 10.
  • Coefficient: The number between 1 and 10 in scientific notation.
  • Standard Form: A number written in ordinary decimal form.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • The coefficient must be at least 1 but less than 10.
  • Positive exponents represent large numbers.
  • Negative exponents represent small decimals.

Summary

Scientific notation simplifies the representation of very large and very small numbers. Every number is written as a coefficient between 1 and 10 multiplied by a power of 10. Understanding this structure prepares you for converting between forms and performing operations in later lessons.
