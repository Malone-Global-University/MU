Lesson Description

In this lesson, we learn to add mixed numbers and mixed measures, such as combining feet & inches, hours & minutes, or pounds & ounces. You will practice proper conversion and addition techniques to ensure accurate results.

Learning Objectives

  • Add mixed numbers correctly by separating whole numbers and fractions.
  • Convert and align units when adding mixed measures.
  • Simplify the final sum for clarity and accuracy.

Topics Covered

  • Adding mixed numbers
  • Converting and adding mixed measures
  • Handling carrying/renaming in mixed units

Key Terms

  • Mixed Number: A whole number combined with a proper fraction.
  • Mixed Measure: A quantity expressed in two or more units (e.g., feet & inches).
  • Carry / Rename: Adjusting values when sums exceed the unit threshold (e.g., 12 inches = 1 foot).

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Adding Mixed Numbers

  1. Add 2 3/4 + 1 2/5.
  2. Convert to fractions: 2 3/4 = 11/4, 1 2/5 = 7/5.
  3. Find LCM of denominators (4 & 5 = 20), convert: 11/4 = 55/20, 7/5 = 28/20.
  4. Add fractions: 55/20 + 28/20 = 83/20.
  5. Convert back to mixed number: 83 √∑ 20 = 4 3/20.

Example 2: Adding Mixed Measures (Feet & Inches)

  1. Add 5 ft 9 in + 3 ft 7 in.
  2. Add inches: 9 + 7 = 16 in ‚Üí 1 ft 4 in (carry 12 in = 1 ft).
  3. Add feet: 5 + 3 + 1 = 9 ft.
  4. Final result: 9 ft 4 in.

Practice Problems

  1. Add 3 1/2 + 2 3/8.
  2. Add 7 ft 5 in + 4 ft 10 in.
  3. Add 1 2/3 + 3 1/6 + 2 5/6.
  4. Add 2 hr 45 min + 1 hr 50 min.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always align units before adding mixed measures.
  • Convert fractions to common denominators before adding mixed numbers.
  • Check if carrying is needed when units exceed their maximum (like 12 inches or 60 minutes).

Summary

Adding mixed numbers and mixed measures requires careful alignment of units and proper fraction addition. Converting and simplifying your answers ensures accuracy and clarity.
