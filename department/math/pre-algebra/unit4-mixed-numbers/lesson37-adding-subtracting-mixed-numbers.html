Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will learn how to add and subtract mixed numbers. You will review finding common denominators and learn how to regroup (borrow) when subtracting.

Learning Objectives

  • Add mixed numbers with like and unlike denominators.
  • Subtract mixed numbers using regrouping when necessary.
  • Simplify answers and write them in proper form.

Topics Covered

  • Finding common denominators
  • Regrouping in subtraction
  • Simplifying fractional results

Key Terms

  • Common Denominator: A shared multiple of two or more denominators.
  • Regrouping: Borrowing 1 whole and converting it into a fraction.
  • Simplest Form: A fraction reduced so numerator and denominator share no common factors except 1.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Adding Mixed Numbers (Like Denominators)

  1. Add whole numbers: 2 + 1 = 3.
  2. Add fractions: 3/5 + 1/5 = 4/5.
  3. Final Answer: 3 4/5.

Example 2: Subtracting Mixed Numbers (Regrouping)

  1. Problem: 4 1/3 ‚àí 2 2/3.
  2. Regroup: 4 1/3 becomes 3 4/3.
  3. Subtract: (3 ‚àí 2) and (4/3 ‚àí 2/3) = 1 2/3.
  4. Final Answer: 1 2/3.

Practice Problems

  1. 3 2/5 + 1 1/5
  2. 5 3/4 ‚àí 2 1/4
  3. 6 1/2 + 2 3/8
  4. 7 2/3 ‚àí 4 5/6

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always check if denominators are the same before adding or subtracting.
  • Regroup carefully when the top fraction is smaller than the bottom fraction.
  • Simplify your final answer.

Summary

Adding and subtracting mixed numbers requires attention to both whole numbers and fractions. Use common denominators and regroup when necessary. Always simplify your final answer.
