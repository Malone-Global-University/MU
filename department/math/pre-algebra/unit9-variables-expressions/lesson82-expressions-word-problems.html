Lesson Description

Word problems allow algebra to be applied to real-world situations. In this lesson, students learn how to translate written scenarios into algebraic expressions and evaluate them using variables and mathematical operations.

Learning Objectives

  • Translate word problems into algebraic expressions.
  • Identify key words that signal mathematical operations.
  • Evaluate algebraic expressions derived from real-world situations.

Topics Covered

  • Identifying variables in real-world contexts
  • Translating verbal phrases into algebraic expressions
  • Evaluating expressions from word problems

Key Terms

  • Variable: A symbol that represents an unknown or changeable value.
  • Algebraic Expression: A mathematical phrase that contains numbers, variables, and operations.
  • Translation: Converting verbal statements into mathematical expressions.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Writing an Expression from a Word Problem

A taxi charges a $4 starting fee plus $2 for each mile traveled. Write an expression for the total cost if m miles are traveled.

  1. Identify the starting fee ‚Üí 4
  2. Identify cost per mile ‚Üí 2m
  3. Combine both parts ‚Üí 4 + 2m

Example 2: Evaluating an Expression from a Word Problem

A movie ticket costs $8, and snacks cost $3 per item. Find the total cost if a person buys 2 tickets and 3 snacks.

  1. Write the expression ‚Üí 8(2) + 3(3)
  2. Multiply ‚Üí 16 + 9
  3. Add ‚Üí 25

Practice Problems

  1. A gym charges $20 per month plus a $15 sign-up fee. Write an expression for total cost after m months.
  2. A store sells notebooks for $2 each. Write and evaluate the expression if a student buys n notebooks.
  3. A ride costs $5 plus $1.50 per mile. Write the expression and evaluate it for 6 miles.
  4. A streaming service costs $12 per month. Write and evaluate the cost for 9 months.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Look for keywords like "per," "each," "plus," or "total."
  • Assign variables clearly before writing expressions.
  • Check units to ensure the expression represents the correct quantity.

Summary

Expressions in word problems allow mathematical models to describe real-world situations. By identifying variables, translating language into mathematical operations, and evaluating expressions, students develop essential algebra skills.

Challenge Problems

  1. A rental company charges $40 per day plus $0.25 per mile driven. Write and evaluate the expression for 3 days and 120 miles.
  2. A fundraiser sells tickets for $6 each and raises $300 in donations. Write an expression for total money raised if t tickets are sold.
  3. A phone plan costs $25 per month plus $10 for each extra data package. Write and evaluate the expression for 5 months and 3 packages.
