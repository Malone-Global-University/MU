Lesson 84: Two-Step Equations

Learn to solve equations requiring two inverse operations while maintaining equation balance.

Lesson Description

This lesson introduces two-step equations, which require applying two inverse operations to isolate a variable. Students will practice following the correct order of operations and maintaining equality while solving algebraic equations.

Learning Objectives

  • Solve two-step equations using inverse operations.
  • Apply order of operations when solving equations.
  • Verify solutions by substitution.

Topics Covered

  • Combining inverse operations
  • Order of operations in equations
  • Checking algebraic solutions

Key Terms

  • Two-Step Equation: An equation requiring two operations to isolate the variable.
  • Isolate: To get the variable alone on one side of the equation.
  • Balance: Maintaining equality by performing the same operation on both sides.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Addition and Division

  1. Solve: x / 3 + 4 = 10
  2. Subtract 4 from both sides: x / 3 = 6
  3. Multiply both sides by 3: x = 18

Example 2: Multiplication and Subtraction

  1. Solve: 5x - 7 = 18
  2. Add 7 to both sides: 5x = 25
  3. Divide both sides by 5: x = 5

Practice Problems

  1. x / 4 + 3 = 11
  2. 6x - 8 = 28
  3. 2x + 9 = 19
  4. x / 5 - 2 = 6

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Undo addition or subtraction before multiplication or division when appropriate.
  • Always perform operations in reverse order from how they appear.
  • Double-check your solution by substituting back into the original equation.

Summary

Two-step equations require performing two inverse operations to isolate the variable. By carefully applying order of operations and maintaining equation balance, students can solve more complex algebraic expressions.

Challenge Problems

  2. x / 6 - 5 = 7
  3. 8x - 4 = 60
