Welcome to the Pre-Algebra Course

What Pre-Algebra is

Pre-Algebra is the bridge between basic arithmetic and Algebra. It helps students understand the “why” behind math operations, introduces new concepts, and develops problem-solving skills.
Think of Pre-Algebra as the training ground for Algebra:it takes the numbers, operations, and ideas you already know and extends them into new ways of thinking.

Key Goals of Pre-Algebra:  

  • Learn to work confidently with numbers, fractions, decimals, and negative numbers
  • Understand ratios, proportions, and percentages
  • Explore exponents, radicals, and scientific notation
  • Introduce variables, expressions, and equations
  • Solve word problems using logical reasoning
  • Begin graphing and understanding patterns

Why Pre-Algebra Matters:  

  • Prepares students for Algebra I and higher math courses
  • Builds a strong foundation in critical thinking and problem solving
  • Helps students connect arithmetic skills with abstract concepts
  • Pre-Algebra is not just about learning “rules”—it’s about thinking mathematically and being ready to tackle Algebra with confidence.
