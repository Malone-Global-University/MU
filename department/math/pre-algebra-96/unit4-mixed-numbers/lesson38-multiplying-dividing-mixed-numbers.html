Lesson Description

In this lesson, you will learn how to multiply and divide mixed numbers by first converting them to improper fractions. After performing the operation, you will simplify the result and, if needed, convert back to a mixed number.

Learning Objectives

  • Convert mixed numbers to improper fractions.
  • Multiply mixed numbers by multiplying fractions.
  • Divide mixed numbers using the reciprocal method.
  • Simplify and convert answers back to mixed numbers.

Topics Covered

  • Improper fractions and mixed numbers
  • Multiplication of fractions
  • Division of fractions using reciprocals
  • Converting back to mixed numbers

Key Terms

  • Improper Fraction: A fraction where the numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator.
  • Reciprocal: Flipping a fraction so the numerator becomes the denominator and vice versa, used in division.
  • Mixed Number: A whole number combined with a proper fraction.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Multiplying Mixed Numbers

  1. Convert 2 1/3 √ó 1 2/5 into improper fractions: 7/3 √ó 7/5.
  2. Multiply the numerators and denominators: 7√ó7 / 3√ó5 = 49/15.
  3. Convert back to a mixed number: 49 √∑ 15 = 3 4/15.

Example 2: Dividing Mixed Numbers

  1. Convert 3 1/2 √∑ 1 3/4 to improper fractions: 7/2 √∑ 7/4.
  2. Multiply by the reciprocal: 7/2 √ó 4/7 = 28/14.
  3. Simplify: 28/14 = 2.

Practice Problems

  1. 1 2/3 √ó 2 1/4
  2. 3 3/5 √∑ 1 1/2
  3. 2 1/2 √ó 3 2/3
  4. 4 1/4 √∑ 2 2/3

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always convert to improper fractions first before multiplying or dividing.
  • Remember to flip the second fraction when dividing.
  • Simplify fractions at the end and convert back to mixed numbers if necessary.

Summary

Multiplying and dividing mixed numbers is easier when converting to improper fractions. Multiplication is straightforward, while division requires using the reciprocal. Always simplify your final answer.
