Lesson 24: Reducing Fractions

Learn how to simplify fractions to their lowest terms to make calculations easier and improve fraction comprehension.

Lesson Description

This lesson teaches students how to reduce fractions to their simplest form by finding the greatest common factor and dividing both numerator and denominator accordingly.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the concept of reducing fractions.
  • Identify the greatest common factor (GCF) of numerator and denominator.
  • Simplify fractions to their lowest terms.

Topics Covered

  • Finding the GCF of two numbers
  • Dividing numerator and denominator by the GCF
  • Recognizing when a fraction is fully reduced

Key Terms

  • Greatest Common Factor (GCF): The largest number that divides both numerator and denominator.
  • Reducing a Fraction: Dividing numerator and denominator by their GCF to simplify the fraction.
  • Lowest Terms: A fraction in which numerator and denominator share no common factors other than 1.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Reducing 8/12

  1. Find the GCF of 8 and 12, which is 4.
  2. Divide both numerator and denominator by 4: 8 √∑ 4 = 2, 12 √∑ 4 = 3.
  3. The fraction in lowest terms is 2/3.

Example 2: Reducing 15/20

  1. Find the GCF of 15 and 20, which is 5.
  2. Divide numerator and denominator by 5: 15 √∑ 5 = 3, 20 √∑ 5 = 4.
  3. The fraction in lowest terms is 3/4.

Practice Problems

  1. Reduce 9/12 to lowest terms.
  2. Reduce 14/21 to lowest terms.
  3. Reduce 24/36 to lowest terms.
  4. Reduce 18/24 and explain each step.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always check for common factors before simplifying.
  • Do not divide by numbers that are not common to both numerator and denominator.
  • Verify the fraction cannot be reduced further after simplifying.

Summary

Reducing fractions to their simplest form is a key skill in Pre-Algebra. By finding the GCF and dividing both parts of the fraction, students can simplify problems and better understand fractional relationships.

Challenge Problems

  1. Reduce 45/60, 32/48, and 56/72 to lowest terms.
  2. Identify which of the fractions 18/27, 12/20, 9/15 are equivalent after reducing.
  3. Create your own fraction and reduce it to lowest terms, explaining your steps.
