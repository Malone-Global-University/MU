Lesson Description

Volume measures how much space a three-dimensional object occupies. In this lesson, students learn formulas for calculating volume in common solids such as cubes, rectangular prisms, and cylinders, and apply these formulas in practical problems.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand what volume represents in three dimensions.
  • Apply volume formulas for cubes, prisms, and cylinders.
  • Solve practical problems involving volume measurements.

Topics Covered

  • Volume of rectangular prisms and cubes
  • Volume of cylinders
  • Units of volume and conversions

Key Terms

  • Volume: The amount of space inside a three-dimensional object.
  • Rectangular Prism: A box-shaped solid with rectangular faces.
  • Cylinder: A solid with circular bases connected by a curved surface.

Summary

Volume tells us how much space is inside an object. Using formulas for prisms, cubes, and cylinders allows us to solve real-world storage and capacity problems.
