Lesson Description

In this lesson, students are introduced to triangles and their basic structure. Special attention is given to right triangles and the Pythagorean Theorem, which allows us to calculate a missing side length when the other two sides are known. This theorem is a foundational tool in geometry and many real-world applications.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the basic parts and types of triangles.
  • Identify right triangles and their side relationships.
  • Use the Pythagorean Theorem to find missing side lengths.

Topics Covered

  • Basic triangle terminology
  • Right triangles and side names
  • Pythagorean Theorem calculations

Key Terms

  • Triangle: A polygon with three sides and three angles.
  • Right Triangle: A triangle that contains one 90¬∞ angle.
  • Hypotenuse: The longest side of a right triangle, opposite the right angle.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Finding the Hypotenuse

  1. A right triangle has legs of length 3 and 4.
  2. Apply the formula: a¬≤ + b¬≤ = c¬≤ ‚Üí 3¬≤ + 4¬≤ = c¬≤.
  3. Compute: 9 + 16 = 25, so c = 5.

Example 2: Finding a Missing Leg

  1. Hypotenuse is 13 and one leg is 5.
  2. Use formula: 5¬≤ + b¬≤ = 13¬≤.
  3. Compute: 25 + b¬≤ = 169 ‚Üí b¬≤ = 144 ‚Üí b = 12.

Practice Problems

  1. Find the hypotenuse if legs are 6 and 8.
  2. Find a missing leg if hypotenuse is 10 and one leg is 6.
  3. A right triangle has legs 9 and 12. Find the hypotenuse.
  4. If the hypotenuse is 15 and one leg is 9, find the other leg.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Only use the Pythagorean Theorem with right triangles.
  • Make sure the hypotenuse is always the longest side.
  • Square numbers carefully to avoid arithmetic mistakes.

Summary

Triangles are key geometric shapes, and right triangles follow a special rule known as the Pythagorean Theorem. By squaring the two legs and adding them, we can find the hypotenuse or solve for a missing side. This concept is essential for future geometry and real-world measurement problems.

Challenge Problems

  1. A ladder reaches 24 feet up a wall and sits 7 feet from the wall. How long is the ladder?
  2. A right triangle has hypotenuse 25 and one leg 7. Find the other leg.
  3. A rectangular field measures 30 m by 40 m. What is the diagonal distance across the field?
