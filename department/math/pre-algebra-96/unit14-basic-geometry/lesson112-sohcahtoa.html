Lesson Description

This lesson introduces the SOHCAHTOA mnemonic to help students calculate unknown sides or angles of right triangles using sine, cosine, and tangent ratios. Students will learn to apply trigonometric ratios step by step to solve practical geometry problems.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the meaning of SOHCAHTOA.
  • Apply sine, cosine, and tangent ratios to find missing sides or angles in right triangles.
  • Use trigonometry in practical geometric problem solving.

Topics Covered

  • Sine, Cosine, and Tangent Ratios
  • Right triangle side and angle calculations
  • Using SOHCAHTOA mnemonic

Key Terms

  • Sine (sin): Ratio of the opposite side to the hypotenuse.
  • Cosine (cos): Ratio of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse.
  • Tangent (tan): Ratio of the opposite side to the adjacent side.

Practice Problems

  1. In a right triangle, the hypotenuse is 13 units and an angle is 30¬∞. Find the opposite side using sine.
  2. Given opposite side 7 units and adjacent side 24 units, find the angle using tangent.
  3. In a right triangle, adjacent side = 8 units, hypotenuse = 10 units. Find the angle using cosine.
  4. Find the missing side in a right triangle where angle = 45¬∞ and adjacent side = 6 units using tangent.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always identify the correct opposite, adjacent, and hypotenuse sides relative to the given angle.
  • Check that your calculator is in the correct mode (degrees or radians).
  • Use the mnemonic SOHCAHTOA to remember which ratio to use.

Summary

SOHCAHTOA provides a simple way to apply sine, cosine, and tangent ratios to solve right triangles. By identifying sides relative to a given angle, students can find missing sides or angles efficiently.
