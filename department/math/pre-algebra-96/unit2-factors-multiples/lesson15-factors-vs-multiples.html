Lesson Description

This lesson explains the difference between factors and multiples, how to find them, and their importance in solving arithmetic and real-world problems.

Learning Objectives

  • Define factors and multiples.
  • Identify factors and multiples of given numbers.
  • Apply factors and multiples to solve problems.

Topics Covered

  • Factors: definition and examples
  • Multiples: definition and examples
  • Relationship between factors and multiples

Key Terms

  • Factor: A number that divides another number exactly without leaving a remainder.
  • Multiple: A number obtained by multiplying a number by an integer.
  • Divisibility: The ability of one number to be divided by another without a remainder.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Identifying Factors

  1. Find the factors of 12.
  2. List numbers that divide 12 evenly: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12.
  3. Conclusion: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12 are factors of 12.

Example 2: Identifying Multiples

  1. Find the first 5 multiples of 4.
  2. Multiply 4 by integers 1 to 5: 4√ó1=4, 4√ó2=8, 4√ó3=12, 4√ó4=16, 4√ó5=20.
  3. Conclusion: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20 are multiples of 4.

Practice Problems

  1. List all factors of 18.
  2. Find the first 5 multiples of 6.
  3. Is 15 a factor or multiple of 45?
  4. Identify whether 24 is a factor or multiple of 72.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Factors divide the number evenly; multiples are products of the number.
  • Do not confuse factors with multiples‚Äîfactors are smaller or equal, multiples can be larger.
  • Use multiplication tables to quickly find multiples.

Summary

Factors are numbers that divide evenly into another number, while multiples are numbers obtained by multiplying a given number by integers. Recognizing the difference is essential for understanding divisibility, LCM, GCF, and problem-solving strategies.

Challenge Problems

  1. List all factors and first 5 multiples of 10.
  2. Find a number that is a multiple of both 4 and 6.
  3. Create a real-life scenario illustrating factors vs multiples.
