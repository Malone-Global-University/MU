Lesson Description

This lesson focuses on graphing linear inequalities on a number line. Students will learn to differentiate between open and closed circles and use arrows to represent the direction of the solution set.

Key Terms

  • Number Line Graph: A visual representation of all solutions of an inequality.

Lesson Examples

Example 1: Graphing x > 3

  1. Draw a number line and locate the point 3.
  2. Place an open circle at 3 because 3 is not included in the solution.
  3. Draw an arrow to the right to indicate all numbers greater than 3.

  3. Draw an arrow to the left to indicate all numbers less than or equal to -2.

Tips & Common Mistakes

  • Always check whether the endpoint is included to choose open or closed circle.
  • Use arrows to indicate the correct direction of the solution set.
  • Double-check your graph against the inequality symbol.

Summary

Graphing inequalities requires correctly placing open or closed circles on a number line and using arrows to represent the solution set. Mastery of this skill helps visualize solutions and interpret inequalities efficiently.
