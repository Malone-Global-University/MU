What is the Coordinate Plane?

The coordinate plane is a flat surface formed by two number lines:

  • The horizontal number line is called the x-axis.
  • The vertical number line is called the y-axis.

They intersect at the origin, \((0, 0)\). Points on the plane are located using ordered pairs \((x, y)\), where x is the horizontal value and y is the vertical value.

The Four Quadrants

The axes divide the plane into four regions called quadrants:

  • Quadrant I: \(x > 0, y > 0\)
  • Quadrant II: \(x < 0, y > 0\)
  • Quadrant III: \(x < 0, y < 0\)
  • Quadrant IV: \(x > 0, y < 0\)

Practice Problems

Plot the following points on a coordinate plane and identify their quadrants:

  1. \((2, 5)\)
  2. \((-4, -3)\)
  3. \((0, 6)\)
  4. \((-5, 0)\)

Lesson Summary

  • The coordinate plane has an x-axis (horizontal) and y-axis (vertical) that meet at the origin \((0,0)\).
  • Points are located with ordered pairs \((x,y)\).
  • The plane is divided into four quadrants based on the signs of \(x\) and \(y\).
