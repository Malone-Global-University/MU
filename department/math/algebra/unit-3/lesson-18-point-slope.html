Lesson 18: Point-Slope Form of a Line

The point-slope form of a linear equation is used when you know the slope of a line and one point on the line. It is written as:

y - y‚ÇÅ = m(x - x‚ÇÅ)

  • (x‚ÇÅ, y‚ÇÅ) = a point on the line
  • m = slope of the line

Examples

Example 1: Find the equation of a line with slope m = 2 passing through (3, 4)

  • Use the formula: y - y‚ÇÅ = m(x - x‚ÇÅ)
  • y - 4 = 2(x - 3)
  • Optionally, expand to slope-intercept form: y = 2x - 2

Example 2: Line with slope m = -1/2 through (2,5)

  • y - 5 = -1/2(x - 2)
  • Expanded: y = -1/2x + 6

Practice Problems

  1. Write the point-slope form for a line through (1,2) with slope 3.
  2. Convert y - 3 = 4(x - 1) into slope-intercept form.
  3. Write the equation of a line with slope -2 passing through (0,5).
  4. Graph y - 1 = 1/3(x - 3) using the point-slope method.

Lesson Summary

  • Point-slope form: y - y‚ÇÅ = m(x - x‚ÇÅ)
  • Useful when you know a point and the slope.
  • Can be converted to slope-intercept form for graphing.
