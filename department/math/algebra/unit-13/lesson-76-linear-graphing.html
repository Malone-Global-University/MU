Lesson 76: Solving Linear Systems by Graphing

One way to solve a system of linear equations is by graphing. The solution to the system corresponds to the point(s) where the lines intersect.

Types of solutions:

  • One solution: Lines intersect at a single point.
  • No solution: Lines are parallel and never intersect.
  • Infinitely many solutions: Lines coincide (are identical).

Steps to Solve by Graphing

  1. Rewrite each equation in slope-intercept form (y = mx + b).
  2. Graph both lines on the same coordinate plane.
  3. Identify the intersection point, if any.
  4. Check the solution by substituting the coordinates into both equations.
