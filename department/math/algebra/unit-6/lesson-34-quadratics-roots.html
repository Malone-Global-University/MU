Lesson 34: Solving Quadratics by Square Roots

Introduction

Some quadratic equations can be solved by taking the square root of both sides. This method works when the equation is in the form ax² + c = 0 or can be rewritten in that form.

Steps to Solve by Square Roots

  1. Isolate the x² term on one side of the equation.
  2. Take the square root of both sides.
  3. Remember to include both the positive and negative roots (±).
  4. Solve for x.

Examples

Example 1: Solve x² = 25

  1. Take the square root: x = ±√25
  2. Solution: x = ±5

Example 2: Solve 3x² − 12 = 0

  1. Isolate x²: 3x² = 12 → x² = 4
  2. Take the square root: x = ±√4
  3. Solution: x = ±2

Example 3: Solve (x − 3)² = 16

  1. Take the square root: x − 3 = ±√16 → x − 3 = ±4
  2. Solve each case: x = 3 + 4 = 7 or x = 3 − 4 = −1

Practice Problems

  1. Solve x² = 36
  2. Solve 5x² = 20
  3. Solve (x + 2)² = 9
  4. Solve 2x² − 18 = 0
  5. Solve (3x − 1)² = 25
