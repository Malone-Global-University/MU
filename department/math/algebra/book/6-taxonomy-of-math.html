Introduction

Algebra is the bridge between arithmetic (working with concrete numbers) and higher, structural mathematics. It introduces symbolic language, rules for manipulating expressions and equations, and ways to generalize patterns. These skills will be used in geometry, calculus, and advanced fields like abstract algebra or linear algebra.

Mathematics is a very broad field, and just like biology has a taxonomy for organisms, math has a taxonomy for its subjects and subfields. The taxonomy can be arranged in levels, starting from the foundations and branching into specialized areas. The "taxonomy of math" is a branching hierarchy:

Foundations ‚Üí Pure Math (Algebra, Geometry, Analysis, Number Theory, etc.) ‚Üí Applied Math (Probability, Computation, Physics, etc.) ‚Üí Interdisciplinary Fields.

1. Foundations of Mathematics

These provide the logical and conceptual bedrock:

  • Logic (propositional, predicate, modal, intuitionistic, etc.)
  • Set Theory
  • Category Theory
  • Foundations/Formal Systems (Peano arithmetic, ZFC, type theory, etc.)
  • Philosophy of Mathematics (constructivism, formalism, platonism, etc.)
