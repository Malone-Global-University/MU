Lesson 20: Rational Expressions – Simplifying, Multiplying/Dividing, Adding/Subtracting

A rational expression is a fraction in which both numerator and denominator are polynomials: \(\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}, Q(x) \neq 0\) Operations on rational expressions follow fraction rules combined with polynomial factoring techniques.

Overview

Key operations: - Simplifying rational expressions - Multiplying and dividing rational expressions - Adding and subtracting rational expressions with common denominators - Identifying restrictions (denominators ≠ 0)

  • Factor numerator and denominator first
  • Apply multiplication and division rules carefully
  • Use least common denominator for addition/subtraction
  • State restrictions explicitly

Why it matters

- Models rates, proportions, and real-world relationships - Essential for solving rational equations and inequalities - Bridges algebra to calculus (limits, asymptotes) - Prepares students for engineering applications

Worked examples

**Simplifying:** \(\frac{x^2-9}{x^2-6x+9} = \frac{(x-3)(x+3)}{(x-3)^2} = \frac{x+3}{x-3}, x \neq 3\)

**Multiplying:** \(\frac{x^2-4}{x^2+5x+6} \cdot \frac{x+3}{x-2} = \frac{(x-2)(x+2)}{(x+2)(x+3)} \cdot \frac{x+3}{x-2} = 1, x \neq -3,-2,2\)

**Dividing:** \(\frac{x^2-9}{x+1} \div \frac{x-3}{x+2} = \frac{x^2-9}{x+1} \cdot \frac{x+2}{x-3} = \frac{(x+3)(x+2)}{x+1}, x \neq -2,-1,3\)

**Adding/Subtracting:** \(\frac{1}{x+2} + \frac{2}{x+3} = \frac{x+3}{(x+2)(x+3)} + \frac{2(x+2)}{(x+2)(x+3)} = \frac{3x+7}{(x+2)(x+3)}, x \neq -2,-3\)

Practice problems

  1. Simplify: \(\frac{x^2-8x+16}{x^2-16}\)
  2. Multiply: \(\frac{x^2-1}{x+4} \cdot \frac{x+4}{x-1}\)
  3. Divide: \(\frac{x^2-x-6}{x+2} \div \frac{x-1}{x-3}\)
  4. Add: \(\frac{3}{x+1} + \frac{4}{x+2}\)
  5. Subtract: \(\frac{x}{x-3} - \frac{2}{x-3}\)
  6. Simplify and state restrictions: \(\frac{x^2-9}{x^2-x-6}\)

Answer key (brief): - Fill in simplified expressions and restrictions for each practice problem as needed

Teaching tips

  • Always factor numerator and denominator first
  • Identify restricted values (denominator ≠ 0)
  • Remind students that division flips the second fraction
  • Encourage checking restrictions after simplification
  • Watch for common mistakes: adding fractions without a common denominator, not cancelling factors correctly
