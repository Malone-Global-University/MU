Lesson 5 — Linear Inequalities: Solving and Graphing on a Number Line

Linear inequalities are statements where one expression is greater than, less than, greater than or equal to, or less than or equal to another. Solving them is similar to solving linear equations, with the additional rule that multiplying or dividing by a negative number reverses the inequality. Solutions are often represented on a number line or in interval notation.

Overview

Linear inequalities appear frequently in real life, including budgets, constraints in optimization, scientific tolerances, and data thresholds. Mastery of inequalities prepares students for functions, systems of inequalities, and calculus concepts like domains and ranges.

Why it matters

Inequalities are everywhere in real life. Understanding them builds a foundation for problem solving in mathematics, science, and real-world applications.

Worked examples

Simple inequality: x + 5 < 12 ⇒ x < 12 − 5 = 7

Subtraction: y − 3 ≥ 10 ⇒ y ≥ 10 + 3 = 13

Multiplication: 4m ≤ 20 ⇒ m ≤ 20 / 4 = 5

Division by a negative: −3x > 9 ⇒ x < 9 / −3 = −3 (note reversal of inequality)

Two-step inequality: 2x + 3 ≤ 11 ⇒ 2x ≤ 8 ⇒ x ≤ 4

Graphing on a number line: - For x < 7: draw an open circle at 7 and shade all numbers to the left. - For y ≥ 13: draw a closed circle at 13 and shade all numbers to the right.

Practice problems

  1. Solve and graph: x + 6 > 10
  2. Solve and graph: y − 5 ≤ 8
  3. Solve: 3a ≥ 12
  4. Solve: −2b < 6
  5. Solve: 5x − 4 > 11
  6. Solve and graph: z / 3 + 2 ≤ 6
  7. Solve: −7p + 3 ≥ −11
  8. Solve and graph: 4q − 5 < 3

Answer key (brief): 1 → x > 4 2 → y ≤ 13 3 → a ≥ 4 4 → b < −3 5 → x > 3 6 → z ≤ 12 7 → p ≥ 2 8 → q < 2

Teaching tips

  • Emphasize the rule of reversing the inequality when multiplying or dividing by a negative number.
  • Encourage students to draw number lines for visual reinforcement.
  • Watch for compound inequalities; treat both sides carefully.
  • Remind students that inequalities have infinite solutions, represented by ranges.
  • Reinforce the difference between < vs. ≤ and > vs. ≥.
