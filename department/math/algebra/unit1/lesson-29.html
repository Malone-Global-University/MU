Lesson 29: Applications of Exponential and Logarithmic Functions

This lesson connects exponential and logarithmic functions to practical applications, including: - Population growth and decay - Radioactive half-life - Compound interest and finance - Sound intensity (decibels) - Earthquake magnitudes (Richter scale) - pH in chemistry Students learn to translate a word problem into a mathematical equation, solve it using logarithms or exponentials, and interpret the solution in context.

Overview

Why it matters

- Shows practical use of algebra and logarithms - Builds critical thinking for modeling real-world scenarios - Provides foundation for science, engineering, finance, and computer modeling - Reinforces understanding of inverse functions and growth/decay phenomena

Worked examples

**Population Growth**
A town has 5,000 people and grows at 3% per year. How long until it reaches 10,000?
\[
P = P_0 e^{rt} \quad \Rightarrow \quad 10,000 = 5,000 e^{0.03 t}
\]
\[
2 = e^{0.03 t} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \ln 2 = 0.03 t \quad \Rightarrow \quad t = \frac{\ln 2}{0.03} \approx 23.1 \text{ years}
\]
**Half-Life (Radioactive Decay)**
Carbon-14 half-life = 5,730 years. Initial mass = 20 g. Amount after 10,000 years?
\[
A = A_0 \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{t/T} \quad \Rightarrow \quad A = 20 \left(\frac{1}{2}\right)^{10,000/5730} \approx 7.95 \text{ g}
\]

**Compound Interest** $1,000 invested at 5% continuous interest for 10 years:

\[
A = P e^{rt} = 1000 e^{0.05 \cdot 10} \approx 1000 e^{0.5} \approx 1648.7
\]
**pH Calculation**
\[
\text{pH} = -\log[H^+] , \quad [H^+] = 3.2 \times 10^{-4} \text{ M} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \text{pH} \approx 3.49
\]
**Decibels**
\[
\text{dB} = 10 \log_{10}(I/I_0), \quad I = 2 \times 10^{-4}, \ I_0 = 10^{-12}
\]
\[
\text{dB} = 10 \log_{10}(2 \times 10^8) \approx 83 \text{ dB}
\]
