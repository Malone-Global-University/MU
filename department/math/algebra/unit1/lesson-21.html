Lesson 21: Rational Equations & Applications

A rational equation is an equation in which at least one term is a rational expression: \(\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)} = \frac{R(x)}{S(x)}, \quad Q(x), S(x) \neq 0\) Solving involves finding a common denominator, simplifying, and checking for extraneous solutions.

Overview

- Determine the least common denominator (LCD) - Multiply through by LCD to eliminate denominators - Solve resulting polynomial or linear equation - Check solutions against restrictions

Why it matters

- Appears in real-world problems: rates, proportions, work problems, chemical concentrations - Reinforces algebraic manipulation and critical thinking - Prepares for advanced topics: rational functions, asymptotes, limits in calculus

Worked examples

**Simple Rational Equation:** \(\frac{x}{x+2} = 3 \implies x = 3, \quad x \neq -2\)

**Rational Equation with Multiple Terms:** \[ \frac{2}{x} + \frac{3}{x+1} = 1 \] Step 1: LCD = \(x(x+1)\) Step 2: Multiply through by LCD: \(2(x+1) + 3x = x(x+1)\) Step 3: Simplify: \(5x + 2 = x^2 + x \implies x^2 -4x -2 = 0\) Step 4: Solve using quadratic formula: \(x = 2 \pm \sqrt{6}\) Step 5: Check restrictions: \(x \neq 0, -1\) → Both solutions valid

**Applied Problem (Work Rate):** Tank fills from two pipes: Pipe A in 3 hours, Pipe B in 6 hours. \[ \frac{t}{3} + \frac{t}{6} = 1 \implies t = 2 \text{ hours} \]

Practice problems

  1. Solve: \(\frac{x}{x+1} = 2\)
  2. Solve: \(\frac{3}{x-1} - \frac{2}{x+2} = 1\)
  3. Solve: \(\frac{2}{x} + \frac{5}{x+3} = 1\)
  4. Word problem: Two people paint a fence. Person A 4 hours, Person B 6 hours. How long together?
  5. Word problem: A car travels 60 miles at speed \(x\) mph and 30 miles at \(x+10\) mph. Total time 2 hours. Solve for \(x\)

Answer key (brief): - Include simplified expressions and valid solutions for each practice problem, respecting restrictions

Teaching tips

  • Always check for extraneous solutions (denominators ≠ 0)
  • Emphasize finding and using the LCD
  • Simplify carefully before solving
  • Use word problems to demonstrate real-world relevance
