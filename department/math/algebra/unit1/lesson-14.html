Lesson 14 — Laws of Exponents

Exponents describe repeated multiplication of the same base. Key laws: * Multiplication of powers: $a^m \cdot a^n = a^{m+n}$ * Division of powers: $\frac{a^m}{a^n} = a^{m-n}$ * Power of a power: $(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$ * Power of a product: $(ab)^n = a^n b^n$ * Power of a quotient: $(\frac{a}{b})^n = \frac{a^n}{b^n}$ * Zero exponent: $a^0 = 1$ for $a \neq 0$ * Negative exponent: $a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$

Overview

Why it matters

* Exponents are essential in algebra, calculus, physics, engineering, and finance. * Scientific notation relies on exponent rules. * Foundational for polynomials, exponential growth/decay, and logarithms.

Worked examples

Multiplying powers:

$$
2^3 \cdot 2^4 = 2^{3+4} = 2^7 = 128
$$

Dividing powers:

$$
\frac{5^6}{5^2} = 5^{6-2} = 5^4 = 625
$$

Power of a power:

$$
(3^2)^4 = 3^{2\cdot4} = 3^8 = 6561
$$

Negative exponent:

$$
10^{-3} = \frac{1}{10^3} = \frac{1}{1000}
$$

Zero exponent:

$$
7^0 = 1
$$

Scientific notation:

$$
6.02 \times 10^{23} \cdot 2 \times 10^3 = 12.04 \times 10^{26} = 1.204 \times 10^{27}
$$
