Lesson 28: Solving Logarithmic Equations

Logarithmic equations are equations in which the variable appears inside a logarithm, e.g., \[ \log_2(x) = 5 \quad \text{or} \quad \ln(x+3) = 2 \] To solve: - Rewrite in exponential form, using the definition \(\log_b(a) = c \iff b^c = a\) - Apply logarithmic properties (product, quotient, power) to simplify - Solve the resulting algebraic equation

Overview

Why it matters

- Models inverse exponential phenomena: half-life, pH in chemistry, decibel scales - Essential for science, engineering, finance, and technology - Reinforces the concept of inverse functions and links algebra with exponents

Worked examples

**Simple logarithmic equation**
Solve \(\log_2(x) = 5\)
\[
2^5 = x \quad \Rightarrow \quad x = 32
\]
**Variable inside natural logarithm**
Solve \(\ln(x) = 3\)
\[
e^3 = x \quad \Rightarrow \quad x \approx 20.085
\]
**Using logarithmic properties**
Solve \(\log_3(2x) = 4\)
\[
3^4 = 2x \quad \Rightarrow \quad 81 = 2x \quad \Rightarrow \quad x = 40.5
\]
**Equation with subtraction inside log**
Solve \(\log_5(x-2) = 3\)
\[
5^3 = x-2 \quad \Rightarrow \quad 125 = x-2 \quad \Rightarrow \quad x = 127
\]
**Multiple logarithms**
Solve \(\log(x) + \log(x-3) = 1\)
\[
\log(x(x-3)) = 1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad x^2 - 3x = 10 \quad \Rightarrow \quad x^2 - 3x - 10 = 0
\]
\[
x = \frac{3 \pm \sqrt{9+40}}{2} = \frac{3 \pm 7}{2} \quad \Rightarrow \quad x = 5 \ (\text{discard } x=-2)
\]
