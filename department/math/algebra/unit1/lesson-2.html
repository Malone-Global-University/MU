Lesson 2 — Properties of Real Numbers

This lesson teaches the key properties of real numbers, demonstrating how they govern addition, multiplication, and algebraic manipulation.

Overview

Real numbers follow certain fundamental rules that allow us to manipulate and simplify expressions consistently. These properties form the foundation of algebraic manipulation.

Why it matters

Without these properties, algebra would not work as a system. They guarantee that operations like addition and multiplication behave predictably. Mastering these rules helps students simplify expressions, solve equations, and avoid mistakes later in algebra and beyond.

Worked examples

Commutative Property: Addition: 3 + 5 = 5 + 3 Multiplication: 4 × 7 = 7 × 4

Associative Property: Addition: (2 + 3) + 4 = 2 + (3 + 4) Multiplication: (6 × 2) × 5 = 6 × (2 × 5)

Distributive Property: 2(3 + 5) = 2 × 3 + 2 × 5 = 16

Identity Property: Addition: 7 + 0 = 7 Multiplication: 9 × 1 = 9

Inverse Property: Additive inverse: 8 + (−8) = 0 Multiplicative inverse: 5/6 × 6/5 = 1

Practice problems

  1. Simplify: (4 + 6) + 9 using a property
  2. Rewrite: 7 × (2 × 3) in another form
  3. Expand: 3(x + 4) using distributive property
  4. Solve: a + 0 = a. What property is this?
  5. Find the multiplicative inverse of 7/9

Answer key (brief): 1 → 19 2 → 7 × 2 × 3 3 → 3x + 12 4 → Identity property (addition) 5 → 9/7

Teaching tips

  • Students often confuse commutative (order) with associative (grouping). Use visuals (number lines, parentheses) to distinguish.
  • Stress that subtraction and division are not commutative or associative.
  • Reinforce that identity elements are unique (0 for addition, 1 for multiplication).
  • When teaching distributive property, connect it to area models to make it concrete.
