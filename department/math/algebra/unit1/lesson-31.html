Lesson 31: Introduction to Limits

Students learn to: - Understand what a limit represents graphically and numerically - Evaluate limits using substitution and simplification - Recognize indeterminate forms - Prepare for derivatives, continuity, and series Limits describe the behavior of a function as it approaches a specific point or infinity, enabling analysis of instantaneous rates of change and areas under curves.

Overview

Why it matters

- Provides the bridge from algebraic functions to calculus concepts - Explains why derivatives and integrals exist - Helps analyze functions at points of discontinuity or rapid change - Foundation for series, asymptotes, and continuity

Worked examples

**Basic Substitution**
\[
\lim_{x \to 3} (2x+5) = 2(3)+5 = 11
\]
**Factor and Simplify**
\[
\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2-4}{x-2} = \lim_{x \to 2} \frac{(x-2)(x+2)}{x-2} = x+2 \quad \Rightarrow \quad 2+2=4
\]
**Limits at Infinity**
\[
\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3x+5}{2x-1} = \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3 + 5/x}{2 - 1/x} = \frac{3}{2}
\]
**Indeterminate Form \(0/0\)**
\[
\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^2-1}{x-1} = \lim_{x \to 1} \frac{(x-1)(x+1)}{x-1} = x+1 \quad \Rightarrow \quad 1+1=2
\]
