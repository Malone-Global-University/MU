Lesson 15: Scientific Notation & Applications

Introduction to scientific notation and its uses in science, engineering, and everyday life.

Overview

Scientific notation is a way to express very large or very small numbers compactly using powers of ten. It is widely used in science, engineering, and finance for clarity and convenience. Format: \(N \times 10^p\) Where \(1 \le N < 10\) and \(p\) is an integer.

  • Express very large or very small numbers in a concise form
  • Simplify calculations with extreme values
  • Prepare for exponents, logarithms, and calculus

Why it matters

- Simplifies calculations with extremely large or small numbers - Used in astronomy, chemistry, physics, and computer science - Prepares students for logarithms, exponents, and calculus applications

Worked examples

Large number: \(300,000,000 = 3 \times 10^8\)

Small number: \(0.000045 = 4.5 \times 10^{-5}\)

Multiplication: \((3 \times 10^4) \cdot (2 \times 10^3) = 6 \times 10^7\)

Division: \(\frac{6 \times 10^8}{3 \times 10^2} = 2 \times 10^6\)

Application — Distance in space: Distance from Earth to Sun ≈ \(9.3 \times 10^7\) miles

Application — Size of a virus: Diameter ≈ \(1.2 \times 10^{-7}\) meters

Practice problems

  1. Write 12,500,000 in scientific notation
  2. Write 0.00000378 in scientific notation
  3. Multiply: \((5 \times 10^6) \cdot (4 \times 10^3)\)
  4. Divide: \(\frac{8 \times 10^9}{2 \times 10^3}\)
  5. Convert \(6.02 \times 10^{23}\) to standard notation

Answer key (brief): - \(1.25 \times 10^7\) - \(3.78 \times 10^{-6}\) - \(2 \times 10^{10}\) - \(4 \times 10^6\) - \(602,000,000,000,000,000,000,000\)

Teaching tips

  • Remind students only one non-zero digit goes before the decimal in standard form
  • Clarify positive vs. negative exponents for large vs. small numbers
  • Use real-world examples: distances in space, atomic scales, money in billions/trillions
  • Avoid calculator errors: some students enter numbers incorrectly in scientific notation mode
