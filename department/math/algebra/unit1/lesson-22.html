Lesson 22: Exponential Functions

An exponential function has a variable in the exponent: \[ f(x) = a \cdot b^x, \quad a \neq 0, \, b>0, \, b \neq 1 \] - \(a\) is the initial value (y-intercept) - \(b\) is the base: growth if \(b>1\), decay if \(0

Overview

Why it matters

- Models population growth, compound interest, radioactive decay, epidemics - Fundamental for science, finance, engineering, and computing - Forms the foundation for logarithms and calculus applications

Worked examples

**Basic Exponential Function:** \[ f(x) = 3 \cdot 2^x \] - \(f(0) = 3 \cdot 2^0 = 3\) - \(f(1) = 3 \cdot 2^1 = 6\) - \(f(2) = 3 \cdot 2^2 = 12\)

**Exponential Decay:** \[ N(t) = 100 \cdot (0.5)^t \] - \(N(0) = 100\) - \(N(1) = 100 \cdot 0.5 = 50\) - \(N(2) = 100 \cdot 0.5^2 = 25\)

**Applied Problem:** Bacteria culture doubles every 3 hours, initial count 200: \[ P(t) = 200 \cdot 2^{t/3} \implies P(9) = 200 \cdot 2^{9/3} = 200 \cdot 2^3 = 1600 \]

Practice problems

  1. Evaluate \(f(x) = 5 \cdot 3^x\) for \(x=0,1,2\)
  2. A substance decays: \(A(t) = 50 \cdot (0.8)^t\). Find \(A(5)\)
  3. Solve for \(t\): \(100 \cdot 2^t = 800\)
  4. Population grows 4% annually, initial 1,000. Find population after 10 years
  5. Radioactive element halves every 6 hours, initial 200g. Find amount after 18 hours
