Lesson 16: Polynomial Functions – Degree, Leading Coefficient, End Behavior

Polynomial functions are algebraic expressions involving powers of \(x\). This lesson introduces key properties like degree, leading coefficient, and end behavior, and demonstrates how to predict graph shapes without plotting every point.

Overview

A polynomial function is an expression of the form: \(f(x) = a_n x^n + a_{n-1} x^{n-1} + \dots + a_1 x + a_0\) Where \(a_n \ne 0\) and \(n\) is a non-negative integer. Key concepts: - Degree: The highest power of \(x\) - Leading coefficient: The coefficient of the highest-degree term - End behavior: The direction the graph heads as \(x \to \pm\infty\)

  • Identify degree and leading coefficient of polynomials
  • Predict end behavior of polynomial graphs
  • Understand applications in algebra, calculus, and modeling
  • Sketch graphs using degree and leading coefficient only

Why it matters

- Understanding polynomials is fundamental for algebra, calculus, and modeling - Helps predict graph shapes and behavior without plotting every point - Forms the foundation for factoring, solving equations, and applications in physics and economics

Worked examples

Identify degree and leading coefficient: \(f(x) = 4x^5 - 3x^3 + 2x - 7\) Degree = 5 Leading coefficient = 4 End behavior: Degree = 5 (odd), leading coefficient = positive \(\Rightarrow\) As \(x \to \infty, f(x) \to \infty\) and as \(x \to -\infty, f(x) \to -\infty\)

Another example: \(g(x) = -2x^4 + 5x^2 - 1\) Degree = 4 (even) Leading coefficient = -2 End behavior: \(f(x) \to -\infty\) as \(x \to \infty\) or \(x \to -\infty\)

Sketching quickly using end behavior: \(h(x) = 3x^3 - x + 1\) Degree = 3 (odd), leading coefficient = 3 (positive) Graph rises to the right, falls to the left

Practice problems

  1. Identify the degree and leading coefficient: \(5x^6 - 4x^3 + x - 9\)
  2. Determine end behavior: \(-x^5 + 2x^2 - 1\)
  3. Sketch a rough graph of \(f(x) = 2x^4 - 3x^3 + x - 7\) using only degree and leading coefficient
  4. Identify degree, leading coefficient, and end behavior: \(7 - x^2 + 6x^5\)
  5. Determine which polynomial has a graph that rises to the left and falls to the right:
  6. a) \(x^3 - 2x + 1\)
  7. b) \(-x^3 + x^2 - 4\)
  8. c) \(2x^4 - x + 5\)

Answer key (brief): - Degree = 6, Leading coefficient = 5 - Degree = 5, Leading coefficient = -1 - Sketch based on degree and leading coefficient rules - Degree = 5, Leading coefficient = 6, End behavior: odd/positive → rises right, falls left - a) rises left/falls right

Teaching tips

  • Emphasize highest power dominates end behavior, lower powers have less impact far from origin
  • Ensure students don’t confuse leading coefficient sign with the constant term
  • Use graphing calculators or software for visualization but stress prediction first
  • Highlight difference between odd and even degrees: odd = opposite ends, even = same ends
