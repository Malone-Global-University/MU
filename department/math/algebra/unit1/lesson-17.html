Lesson 17: Operations on Polynomials – Addition, Subtraction, Multiplication

Polynomial operations involve combining like terms and applying distributive rules. This lesson covers addition, subtraction, multiplication, and special products such as the square of a sum and the difference of squares.

Overview

Polynomial operations involve combining like terms and applying distributive rules. Key operations: - Addition: Combine like terms - Subtraction: Distribute negative sign and combine like terms - Multiplication: Apply distributive property; multiply each term in one polynomial by every term in the other Special products: - Square of a sum: \((a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2\) - Difference of squares: \(a^2 - b^2 = (a-b)(a+b)\)

  • Combine like terms carefully
  • Distribute negative signs properly
  • Multiply polynomials using distributive property
  • Use special products formulas after mastering distributive multiplication

Why it matters

- Mastery of polynomial operations is essential for solving equations, factoring, and calculus - Simplifies expressions and enables real-world modeling - Prepares students for advanced operations: long division, synthetic division, algebraic transformations

Worked examples

**Addition:** \((3x^2 + 2x - 5) + (4x^2 - 3x + 7) = (3x^2+4x^2) + (2x-3x) + (-5+7) = 7x^2 - x + 2\)

**Subtraction:** \((5x^3 - 2x + 4) - (3x^3 + x - 1) = 5x^3 - 2x + 4 - 3x^3 - x + 1 = 2x^3 - 3x + 5\)

**Multiplication (distributive):** \((x+3)(x^2 + 2x + 1) = x(x^2 + 2x +1) + 3(x^2 + 2x +1) = x^3 + 2x^2 + x + 3x^2 + 6x +3 = x^3 + 5x^2 +7x +3\)

**Special product – difference of squares:** \((2x+5)(2x-5) = (2x)^2 - 5^2 = 4x^2 - 25\)

Practice problems

  1. Add: \((x^3 + 4x^2 - 2) + (3x^3 - x^2 + 7x)\)
  2. Subtract: \((5x^2 - 3x + 6) - (2x^2 + 4x -1)\)
  3. Multiply: \((x+2)(x^2 - x +3)\)
  4. Expand: \((x+5)^2\)
  5. Factor: \(x^2 - 9\)
  6. Multiply: \((2x-1)(x^2 + x +1)\)

Answer key (brief): - \(4x^3 + 3x^2 + 7x -2\) - \(3x^2 -7x +7\) - \(x^3 + x^2 + 5x +6\) (example simplified) - \(x^2 + 10x +25\) - \((x-3)(x+3)\) - \(2x^3 + x^2 - x -1\) (example simplified)

Teaching tips

  • Always combine like terms carefully; misaligning powers is the most common error
  • Check signs when subtracting polynomials
  • Introduce special products formulas after students are comfortable with distributive property
  • Encourage step-by-step organization; vertically aligning terms prevents mistakes
