Lesson 39: Curve Sketching & Critical Points

Students will learn to: - Find the first and second derivatives - Identify critical points (\(f'(x) = 0\) or undefined) - Determine intervals of increase/decrease - Identify local maxima and minima using the First Derivative Test - Determine concavity and points of inflection using the Second Derivative Test - Sketch the function incorporating asymptotes, intercepts, and behavior at infinity

Overview

Why it matters

- Curve sketching builds intuition about function behavior - Critical points show where functions attain extreme values - Concavity indicates “bending” of the graph, relevant in physics, economics, and optimization - Prepares for optimization, motion analysis, and modeling real-world situations

Worked examples

**Example 1: Find Critical Points** Given \(f(x) = x^3 - 6x^2 + 9x + 1\)

1. First derivative: \(f'(x) = 3x^2 - 12x + 9 = 3(x^2 - 4x + 3) = 3(x-1)(x-3)\) 2. Critical points: \(x = 1, 3\) 3. Second derivative: \(f''(x) = 6x - 12\)

- \(x=1\): \(f''(1) = 6 - 12 = -6 < 0\) → local maximum - \(x=3\): \(f''(3) = 18 - 12 = 6 > 0\) → local minimum

**Example 2: Increasing/Decreasing Intervals** - \(f'(x) = 3(x-1)(x-3)\) - Sign chart: - \(x<1\): \(f'<0\) → decreasing - \(10\) → increasing - \(x>3\): \(f'<0\) → decreasing

**Example 3: Concavity and Inflection Points** - \(f''(x) = 6x - 12\) - Inflection point where \(f''(x)=0 \Rightarrow x=2\) - Concavity: - \(x<2\): \(f''<0\) → concave down - \(x>2\): \(f''>0\) → concave up

**Example 4: Sketching Summary** - Critical points: \(x=1\) max, \(x=3\) min - Inflection point: \(x=2\) - Intervals of increase/decrease and concavity used to draw smooth graph
