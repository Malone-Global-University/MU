Lesson 6 — Absolute Value Equations & Inequalities

Absolute value measures the distance of a number from zero on the number line, regardless of direction. Absolute value equations and inequalities involve expressions like |x| = a, |x| < a, or |x| > a. Solving requires considering both the positive and negative scenarios.

Overview

Absolute value is essential for understanding distance, error margins, deviations, and constraints in real-world problems. It also prepares students for piecewise functions and complex analysis in higher mathematics.

Why it matters

Understanding absolute value allows students to solve problems involving ranges, tolerances, and deviations. It builds foundations for higher algebra, calculus, and applied mathematics.

Worked examples

Absolute value equation: |x| = 5 ⇒ x = 5 or x = −5

Equation with addition inside: |x − 3| = 7 ⇒ x − 3 = 7 or x − 3 = −7 ⇒ x = 10 or x = −4

Inequality: less than: |y| < 6 ⇒ −6 < y < 6

Inequality: greater than: |z| > 4 ⇒ z < −4 or z > 4

Two-step inequality: |2x − 3| ≤ 7 ⇒ −7 ≤ 2x − 3 ≤ 7 ⇒ −4 ≤ 2x ≤ 10 ⇒ −2 ≤ x ≤ 5

Practice problems

  1. Solve: |x| = 8
  2. Solve: |x + 4| = 10
  3. Solve and graph: |y| < 3
  4. Solve and graph: |z − 5| > 2
  5. Solve: |3x + 1| ≤ 7
  6. Solve: |2a − 5| ≥ 9
  7. Solve: |x / 2 − 3| < 4
  8. Solve and graph: |x + 1| > 0

Answer key (brief): 1 → x = 8 or x = −8 2 → x = 6 or x = −14 3 → y ∈ (−3, 3) 4 → z < 3 or z > 7 5 → x ∈ [−8/3, 2] 6 → a ≤ −2 or a ≥ 7 7 → x ∈ (−2, 14) 8 → x ∈ (−∞, −1) ∪ (−1, ∞)

Teaching tips

  • |x| = a always yields two solutions if a > 0
  • |x| < a produces a bounded interval; |x| > a produces two unbounded intervals
  • Draw number lines to visualize solutions
  • |x| = −a has no solution
  • Watch for compound expressions inside the absolute value requiring careful handling
