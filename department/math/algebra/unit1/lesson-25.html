Lesson 25: Laws of Logarithms

Logarithms follow rules that simplify computation and manipulation of expressions. The main laws are: **Product Law:** \[ \log_b(M \cdot N) = \log_b(M) + \log_b(N) \] **Quotient Law:** \[ \log_b\left(\frac{M}{N}\right) = \log_b(M) - \log_b(N) \] **Power Law:** \[ \log_b(M^p) = p \cdot \log_b(M) \] **Change of Base Formula:** \[ \log_b(M) = \frac{\log_k(M)}{\log_k(b)} \] These laws allow simplification of complex expressions, solving logarithmic equations, and analyzing exponential growth and decay.

Overview

Why it matters

- Essential for solving equations with variables in exponents - Applications in science and engineering: decibels, pH, half-life, finance - Foundation for calculus: logarithmic differentiation and integrals of exponential functions

Worked examples

**Product Law:** \[ \log_2(8 \cdot 4) = \log_2(32) = 5 \] \[ \log_2(8) + \log_2(4) = 3 + 2 = 5 \]

**Quotient Law:** \[ \log_3\left(\frac{81}{3}\right) = \log_3(27) = 3 \] \[ \log_3(81) - \log_3(3) = 4 - 1 = 3 \]

**Power Law:** \[ \log_5(25) = \log_5(5^2) = 2 \cdot \log_5(5) = 2 \cdot 1 = 2 \]

**Change of Base:** Solve \(\log_2(10)\) using base 10: \[ \log_2(10) = \frac{\log_{10}(10)}{\log_{10}(2)} = \frac{1}{0.3010} \approx 3.3219 \]

Practice problems

  1. Simplify \(\log_4(16 \cdot 2)\)
  2. Simplify \(\log_6(36/6)\)
  3. Simplify \(\log_2(8^3)\)
  4. Solve for \(x: \log_3(x^2) = 4\)
  5. Use change of base to approximate: \(\log_5(50)\)

Teaching tips

  • Laws work only with the same base
  • Watch for negative or zero arguments (undefined)
  • Convert products and quotients to sums and differences before solving
  • Use graphs to connect log rules with exponential properties
