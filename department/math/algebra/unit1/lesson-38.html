Lesson 38: Related Rates

Students will learn to: - Identify variables that change with time - Express the relationship between variables as an equation - Differentiate implicitly with respect to time (\(t\)) - Solve for the desired rate (\(\frac{dy}{dt}, \frac{dx}{dt}\), etc.) - Interpret results in real-world contexts

Overview

Why it matters

- Related rates problems appear in physics, engineering, chemistry, and biology - Reinforces implicit differentiation and the chain rule - Connects algebraic modeling to calculus applications - Builds problem-solving skills with multiple changing quantities

Worked examples

**Example 1: Ladder Sliding Down a Wall** A 10 ft ladder leans against a wall. The bottom slides away at 1 ft/s. How fast is the top sliding down when the bottom is 6 ft from the wall?

1. Let \(x\) = distance from bottom to wall, \(y\) = height of top above ground. 2. Pythagorean relationship: \(x^2 + y^2 = 10^2\) 3. Differentiate w.r.t \(t\): \(2x \frac{dx}{dt} + 2y \frac{dy}{dt} = 0 \Rightarrow x \frac{dx}{dt} + y \frac{dy}{dt} = 0\) 4. Solve for \(\frac{dy}{dt}\): \(\frac{dy}{dt} = - \frac{x}{y} \frac{dx}{dt}\) 5. When \(x = 6\), \(y = \sqrt{100 - 36} = 8\) 6. \(\frac{dy}{dt} = -\frac{6}{8} \cdot 1 = -0.75\) ft/s

**Example 2: Expanding Circle** A circular oil spill is expanding such that the radius increases at 0.2 m/min. How fast is the area increasing when radius = 5 m?

1. Area: \(A = \pi r^2\) 2. Differentiate w.r.t \(t\): \(\frac{dA}{dt} = 2 \pi r \frac{dr}{dt}\) 3. Substitute \(r = 5\), \(\frac{dr}{dt} = 0.2\): \(\frac{dA}{dt} = 2 \pi (5)(0.2) = 2 \pi\) m²/min

**Example 3: Conical Tank** Water drains from a conical tank with height 12 m and base radius 4 m. If water level drops at 0.5 m/min, find the rate the volume decreases when water height is 6 m.

1. Volume of cone: \(V = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2 h\) 2. Relate \(r\) and \(h\) using similar triangles: \(r = \frac{1}{3}h \Rightarrow r^2 = \frac{1}{9}h^2\) 3. Substitute: \(V = \frac{1}{3} \pi \cdot \frac{1}{9} h^2 \cdot h = \frac{\pi}{27} h^3\) 4. Differentiate: \(\frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{\pi}{9} h^2 \frac{dh}{dt}\) 5. Substitute \(h = 6\), \(\frac{dh}{dt} = -0.5\): \(\frac{dV}{dt} = \frac{\pi}{9} \cdot 36 \cdot (-0.5) = -2\pi\) m³/min
