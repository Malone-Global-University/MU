Lesson 35: Higher-Order Derivatives & Applications

Students will learn to: - Compute second, third, and nth derivatives - Interpret higher-order derivatives in context - Apply derivatives to motion problems (velocity, acceleration, jerk) - Connect concavity and inflection points to second derivatives

Overview

Why it matters

- Provides insight into how functions change beyond instantaneous rate - Essential in physics (motion, acceleration), economics (marginal analysis), and engineering - Prepares students for curve sketching, optimization, and differential equations

Worked examples

**Second Derivative** \[ f(x) = x^4 - 3x^3 + 2x \] First derivative: \[ f'(x) = 4x^3 - 9x^2 + 2 \] Second derivative: \[ f''(x) = 12x^2 - 18x \]

**Third Derivative**
\[
f'''(x) = 24x - 18
\]

**Applications in Motion** Position: \(s(t) = t^3 - 6t^2 + 9t\)

Velocity: \[ v(t) = s'(t) = 3t^2 - 12t + 9 \]

Acceleration: \[ a(t) = v'(t) = s''(t) = 6t - 12 \]

Jerk: \[ j(t) = a'(t) = s'''(t) = 6 \]

**Concavity and Inflection Points** - Concave up: \(f''(x) > 0\) - Concave down: \(f''(x) < 0\) - Inflection point: \(f''(x) = 0\) and changes sign

Example: \[ f(x) = x^3 - 3x^2 + 4 \] \[ f''(x) = 6x - 6 \] Set \(f''(x) = 0 \Rightarrow x = 1\) (possible inflection point)
