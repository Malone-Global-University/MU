Lesson 30: Sequences & Series

Students learn to: - Identify arithmetic and geometric sequences - Compute nth terms - Find finite and infinite sums - Recognize patterns and model growth/decay This lesson bridges algebraic manipulation with mathematical modeling, showing how repeated operations form predictable patterns.

Overview

Why it matters

- Provides foundation for summation notation (\(\Sigma\)), crucial in calculus - Teaches pattern recognition and formula derivation - Connects algebra to real-world applications: population growth, financial modeling, and physics - Prepares students for limits, series convergence, and infinite sums

Worked examples

**Arithmetic Sequence** Find the 15th term: 3, 7, 11, 15…

\[
a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d \quad \Rightarrow \quad a_{15} = 3 + (15-1)\cdot4 = 3 + 56 = 59
\]

**Sum of an Arithmetic Series** Sum the first 20 terms:

\[
S_n = \frac{n}{2}(a_1 + a_n) = \frac{20}{2}(3+59) = 10 \cdot 62 = 620
\]

**Geometric Sequence** Find the 8th term: 2, 6, 18, 54…

\[
a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{n-1} \quad \Rightarrow \quad a_8 = 2 \cdot 3^7 = 2 \cdot 2187 = 4374
\]

**Sum of a Finite Geometric Series** Sum the first 6 terms:

\[
S_n = a_1 \frac{r^n - 1}{r-1} = 2 \frac{3^6 - 1}{3-1} = 2 \frac{728}{2} = 728
\]
**Infinite Geometric Series**
\(|r|<1\), sum 5 + 2.5 + 1.25 + …
\[
S_\infty = \frac{a_1}{1-r} = \frac{5}{1-0.5} = \frac{5}{0.5} = 10
\]

Practice problems

  3. Find the 10th term of 3, 6, 12, 24, …
