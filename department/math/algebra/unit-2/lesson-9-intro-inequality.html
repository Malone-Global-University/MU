What Are Inequalities?

In mathematics, an inequality is a statement that compares two expressions using symbols instead of just an equals sign. Inequalities tell us whether one side is greater than, less than, or possibly equal to another.

Common Inequality Symbols

  • < means "less than" (e.g., \(3 < 7\))
  • > means "greater than" (e.g., \(10 > 4\))
  • ≤ means "less than or equal to" (e.g., \(x \le 5\))
  • ≥ means "greater than or equal to" (e.g., \(y \ge 2\))

Examples of Inequalities

Here are a few simple inequalities:

  • \(8 > 2\) ‚Üí 8 is greater than 2.
  • \(-3 < 4\) ‚Üí -3 is less than 4.
  • \(x \ge 0\) ‚Üí \(x\) can be 0 or any positive number.
  • \(y \le 12\) ‚Üí \(y\) can be 12 or any smaller number.

Graphing Inequalities on a Number Line

Inequalities are often shown on a number line. The symbol determines whether the endpoint is included:

  • Use an open circle if the number is not included ( < or > ).
  • Use a closed circle if the number is included ( ‚â§ or ‚â• ).

Example: \(x > 2\) ‚Üí open circle at 2, shading to the right.
