Lesson 11: Compound Inequalities

A compound inequality combines two simple inequalities with the words ‚Äúand‚Äù or ‚Äúor‚Äù. The solution is the set of all numbers that make the statement true.

  • AND ‚Üí both inequalities must be true at the same time (intersection).
  • OR ‚Üí either inequality can be true (union).

AND Inequalities

Example: Solve \(2 < x \le 5\).

This means \(x\) is greater than 2 and less than or equal to 5. So the solution is all numbers between 2 and 5, not including 2 but including 5.

OR Inequalities

Example: Solve \(x < -1 \; \text{or} \; x \ge 3\).

This means \(x\) is less than -1 or \(x\) is greater than or equal to 3. The solution has two separate parts.

Steps to Solve Compound Inequalities

  1. Solve each inequality separately.
  2. For AND: take the overlap (intersection) of the solutions.
  3. For OR: combine (union) the solutions.
  4. Graph the solution on a number line.

More Examples

Example 1 (AND):

Solve: \(x - 1 > 2 \; \text{and} \; x + 3 \le 10\).

  • First: \(x > 3\)
  • Second: \(x \le 7\)
  • Combined: \(3 < x \le 7\)

Example 2 (OR):

Solve: \(2x - 5 < -3 \; \text{or} \; x + 4 \ge 10\).

  • First: \(2x < 2 \Rightarrow x < 1\)
  • Second: \(x \ge 6\)
  • Combined: \(x < 1 \; \text{or} \; x \ge 6\)

Practice Problems

  1. Solve and graph: \(0 < x \le 4\)
  2. Solve and graph: \(x \le -2 \; \text{or} \; x > 5\)
  3. Solve and graph: \(2x - 1 > 3 \; \text{and} \; x + 2 \le 9\)
  4. Solve and graph: \(x - 4 < 0 \; \text{or} \; 3x \ge 12\)

Lesson Summary

Compound inequalities combine two inequalities with AND or OR. AND ‚Üí solutions overlap (between two values). OR ‚Üí solutions combine (two separate regions). Always solve each inequality separately, then merge the results based on the connector.
