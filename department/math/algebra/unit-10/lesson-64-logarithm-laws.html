Lesson 64: Laws of Logarithms

Logarithms follow specific rules that make it easier to simplify and solve logarithmic expressions.

Key Laws of Logarithms

  • Product Rule: log_b(M √ó N) = log_b(M) + log_b(N)
  • Quotient Rule: log_b(M √∑ N) = log_b(M) ‚àí log_b(N)
  • Power Rule: log_b(M^p) = p √ó log_b(M)
  • Change of Base Formula: log_b(M) = log_k(M) √∑ log_k(b), for any positive base k ‚â† 1

Examples

Example 1 (Product Rule): log_2(8 √ó 4) = log_2(8) + log_2(4) = 3 + 2 = 5

Example 2 (Quotient Rule): log_5(125 √∑ 5) = log_5(125) ‚àí log_5(5) = 3 ‚àí 1 = 2

Example 3 (Power Rule): log_3(9^2) = 2 √ó log_3(9) = 2 √ó 2 = 4

Example 4 (Change of Base): log_2(32) = log_10(32) √∑ log_10(2) ‚âà 5

Practice Problems

  2. log_7(49 √∑ 7)
  3. log_5(25^3)
  4. log_10(1000) using log_2 (change of base formula)
  5. Simplify: log_3(27 √ó 9) ‚àí log_3(3)
