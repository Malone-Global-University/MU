Lesson 38 — Adding and Subtracting Radicals

🎯 Goal

Learn to combine like radicals by addition and subtraction.

📖 Lesson Description

  • Only like radicals (same radicand) can be added or subtracted
  • Example: \( 3\sqrt{2} + 5\sqrt{2} = 8\sqrt{2} \)
  • Different radicands cannot be combined: \( \sqrt{2} + \sqrt{3} \) stays as is

✏️ Practice

  1. Compute \( 4\sqrt{5} + 7\sqrt{5} \)
  2. Compute \( 6\sqrt{3} - 2\sqrt{3} \)
  3. Compute \( 3\sqrt{2} + 5\sqrt{7} \)
