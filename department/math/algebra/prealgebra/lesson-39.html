Lesson 39 — Multiplying Radicals

🎯 Goal

Learn to multiply radicals using the product rule.

📖 Lesson Description

  • Product rule: \( \sqrt{a} \cdot \sqrt{b} = \sqrt{a \cdot b} \)
  • Example: \( \sqrt{3} \cdot \sqrt{12} = \sqrt{36} = 6 \)
  • Can also multiply coefficients: \( 2\sqrt{3} \cdot 4\sqrt{2} = 8\sqrt{6} \)

✏️ Practice

  1. Compute \( \sqrt{5} \cdot \sqrt{20} \)
  2. Compute \( 3\sqrt{2} \cdot 4\sqrt{3} \)
  3. Compute \( \sqrt{6} \cdot \sqrt{24} \)
