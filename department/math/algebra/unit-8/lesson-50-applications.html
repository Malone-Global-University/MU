Lesson 50: Applications of Rational Equations (Rates, Proportions)

Introduction

Rational equations are commonly used to solve real-world problems involving rates, work, distance, and proportions. These applications often require setting up an equation where the variable represents time, speed, or another quantity.

Steps to Solve Application Problems

  1. Identify the variable and what it represents.
  2. Write expressions for each quantity in terms of the variable.
  3. Set up a rational equation relating the quantities.
  4. Find a common denominator if necessary, and solve for the variable.
  5. Check that your solution is reasonable and does not make any denominator zero.

Examples

Example 1: Work Problem

Two pumps fill a tank. Pump A can fill the tank in 4 hours, Pump B in 6 hours. How long to fill the tank working together?

  • Rate of A: 1/4 tank/hour
  • Rate of B: 1/6 tank/hour
  • Together: 1/4 + 1/6 = 5/12 tank/hour
  • Time to fill tank: t = 1 √∑ (5/12) = 12/5 = 2.4 hours

Example 2: Distance/Rate Problem

A car travels 150 miles. If it travels x mph, time = distance/rate = 150/x. If speed increases by 10 mph, time decreases by 0.5 hours. Find original speed.

  • Equation: 150/x ‚àí 150/(x + 10) = 0.5

  • Solution: x = 50 mph (discard negative solution)

Practice Problems

  1. Two pipes fill a pool. Pipe A fills in 5 hours, pipe B in 3 hours. How long to fill together?
  2. A boat travels 60 miles downstream in 2 hours and returns upstream in 3 hours. Find speed of boat in still water.
  3. A man can paint a house in 8 hours, another in 6 hours. How long together?
  4. A car travels 120 miles. Increasing speed by 20 mph saves 1 hour. Find original speed.
  5. Two taps fill a tank in 4 and 6 hours. A leak empties 1/12 tank per hour. Time to fill tank with both taps and leak?
