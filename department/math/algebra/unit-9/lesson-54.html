Lesson 54: Operations with Radicals (Add, Subtract, Multiply, Divide)

Introduction

Once radicals are simplified, we can perform operations on them: addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Similar to algebraic expressions, like radicals can be combined, while multiplication and division follow special rules.

Addition and Subtraction of Radicals

Only radicals with the same radicand and index can be combined:

  • √2 + 3√2 = 4√2
  • 5√3 − 2√3 = 3√3
  • √5 + √2 cannot be simplified further

Multiplication of Radicals

Multiply the coefficients and radicands separately:

  • √2 × √3 = √6
  • 3√2 × 4√5 = 12√10
  • (√2)(√8) = √16 = 4

Division of Radicals

Divide coefficients and radicands separately. Rationalize denominators if needed:

  • √18 / √2 = √9 = 3
  • (6√3)/(2√2) = 3√6 / 2
  • 1 / √2 = √2 / 2 (rationalized)

Examples

Example 1: 2√5 + 3√5 = 5√5

Example 2: √3 × √12 = √36 = 6

Example 3: 4√10 ÷ 2√5 = 2√2

Practice Problems

  1. √8 + 2√8
  2. 3√7 − √7
  3. √5 × √20
  4. 6√6 ÷ 3√2
  5. 1 ÷ √3 (rationalize the denominator)
