Trade & Cities

Between the 10th and 15th centuries, Europe‚Äôs economic life expanded. Towns grew, markets flourished, and trade networks connected distant regions.

From Manors to Markets

As feudal structures loosened, peasants sought independence. Surpluses were traded in local fairs that became the seeds of new urban centers.

Merchant Guilds & Trade Routes

Guilds organized production and quality control. Trade routes connected the Mediterranean with northern Europe through hubs like Venice, Bruges, and the Hanseatic League.

Urban Life & Civic Growth

City walls, cathedrals, and town halls symbolized self-governance and wealth. The rise of the bourgeoisie laid foundations for the modern economy.
