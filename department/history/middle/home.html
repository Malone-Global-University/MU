The Middle Ages: Faith, Feudalism, and Foundations

Discover the world between the fall of Rome and the dawn of the Renaissance.

Introduction

The Middle Ages, also called the Medieval period, spanned roughly from the 5th to the 15th century. This era saw the rise of kingdoms, the spread of Christianity, the establishment of feudal systems, and the foundations for modern Europe.

Key Themes

Feudalism

A hierarchical system where kings granted land to nobles in exchange for military service, while peasants worked the land under strict obligations.

Religion

Christianity dominated daily life, politics, and culture. The Church influenced kings, law, education, and art across Europe.

Kingdoms and Empires

Notable rulers and states included Charlemagne's Holy Roman Empire, Anglo-Saxon England, and the Capetian dynasty in France.

Culture and Learning

Monasteries preserved knowledge, universities emerged, Gothic architecture flourished, and literature evolved with works like Dante's Divine Comedy.
