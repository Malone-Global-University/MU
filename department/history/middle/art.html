Medieval Art & Architecture

Art and architecture in the Middle Ages reflected faith, hierarchy, and craftsmanship. From cathedrals to manuscripts, beauty was a form of devotion.

Romanesque Beginnings

Early medieval architecture was sturdy and functional, with thick walls, round arches, and small windows. Churches like Cluny Abbey emphasized order and faith.

Gothic Innovation

The 12th century introduced pointed arches, flying buttresses, and stained glass, reaching its peak in cathedrals such as Chartres and Notre-Dame. Light became a symbol of divine presence.

Castles and Civic Architecture

Beyond the church, fortifications and guild halls displayed feudal strength and growing urban pride, blending defense with artistry.
