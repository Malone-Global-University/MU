Ancient Egypt

Home of the pharaohs and pyramids, Egypt thrived along the Nile with remarkable architecture, writing, and centralized government.

Introduction

Ancient Egypt was a civilization along the Nile River that flourished for thousands of years, known for monumental architecture, hieroglyphs, and a complex society.

Timeline

  • c. 3100 BCE – Unification of Upper and Lower Egypt
  • c. 2600 BCE – Great Pyramids of Giza built
  • c. 1279–1213 BCE – Reign of Ramses II

Culture and Achievements

Ancient Egyptians developed hieroglyphic writing, constructed pyramids and temples, advanced in medicine, mathematics, and astronomy, and established a centralized pharaonic state.
