Overview

Melanesia includes New Guinea, the Solomon Islands, Vanuatu, and Fiji. It is one of the earliest centers of agriculture outside the Fertile Crescent, with taro and yam cultivation dating back thousands of years. Rich cultural and linguistic diversity defines the region.

Key Themes

Agriculture

New Guinea highlands pioneered early crop domestication, sustaining dense populations with taro, bananas, and sugarcane.

Exchange Systems

The Kula Ring in the Trobriand Islands linked islands through ceremonial exchange of valuables.

Social and Cultural Diversity

Over 1,000 languages are spoken in Melanesia, making it one of the most diverse regions on Earth.

Legacy

Melanesian traditions of agriculture, exchange, and ritual continue to shape the region, providing insights into early complex societies.
