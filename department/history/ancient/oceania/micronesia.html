Overview

Micronesia is a region of small islands scattered across the western Pacific. Despite limited land, its peoples developed remarkable navigational systems, including star maps and wave-piloting techniques, allowing for communication and exchange across wide stretches of ocean.

Key Themes

Navigation

Stick charts recorded wave patterns and currents, serving as teaching tools for apprentice navigators.

Subsistence

Coconut, breadfruit, taro, and reef fishing sustained island communities.

Social Systems

Chiefdoms and clan systems coordinated resources, while inter-island alliances maintained stability.

Legacy

Micronesian wayfinding represents a living tradition of maritime knowledge and cultural resilience in the Pacific.
