Language: Latin and Communication

Latin was the official language of Rome and the Roman Empire, used in administration, law, and literature. It evolved into the Romance languages (Italian, French, Spanish, Portuguese, Romanian) and remained the language of scholarship and the Catholic Church for centuries.

Visual Arts

Romans created realistic sculpture, portraiture, and frescoes. Art served both decorative and propagandistic purposes, honoring emperors, gods, and civic achievements.

  • Sculpture: Busts, statues, and reliefs capturing realistic human features.
  • Painting: Frescoes in villas depicting mythology, landscapes, and daily life.
  • Mosaics: Decorative floors illustrating geometric patterns and narrative scenes.

Architecture and Public Monuments

Rome innovated in engineering and design:

  • Public Buildings: Forums, basilicas, and baths facilitated civic life.
  • Monuments: Triumphal arches and columns celebrated military victories.
  • Engineering: Roads, aqueducts, bridges, and amphitheaters demonstrated practical and aesthetic mastery.
