Founding the Republic

After overthrowing Tarquin the Proud, Romans vowed never again to have a king. Power was vested in two annually elected consuls and a Senate of aristocrats, checked by popular assemblies.

Conflict of the Orders

Plebeians demanded rights through strikes and secessions, gaining tribunes, laws, and access to political offices. The Twelve Tables codified Roman law around 450 BCE, ensuring transparency.

Expansion and Empire

Rome conquered Italy, defeated Carthage in the Punic Wars, and expanded into Greece, Egypt, and Spain. Military success fueled social tensions and inequality, eventually undermining republican ideals.
