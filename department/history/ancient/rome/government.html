The Roman Kingdom

Early Rome was ruled by kings advised by the Senate. These monarchs combined religious, military, and judicial powers. The last king, Tarquin the Proud, was overthrown in 509 BCE, paving the way for a new republican system that distrusted absolute rule.

The Roman Republic

The Republic was a complex system balancing power among different institutions:

  • Consuls: Two annually elected leaders commanding the army and presiding over the Senate.
  • Senate: Advisory council of patricians guiding foreign and financial policy.
  • Assemblies: Citizen bodies voting on laws and magistrates.
  • Checks and Balances: Offices limited by term, veto powers, and shared responsibilities to prevent tyranny.

The Roman Empire

Augustus (27 BCE) transformed the Republic into the Empire. Emperors concentrated authority but preserved republican titles and forms to maintain legitimacy. The Senate continued, though largely symbolic, while provincial administration and imperial bureaucracy expanded.
