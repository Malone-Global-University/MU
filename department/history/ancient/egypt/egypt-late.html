Origins

The Late Period began after the collapse of the New Kingdom and Third Intermediate Period, a time of political fragmentation with competing dynasties in Upper and Lower Egypt. Despite instability, native rulers like the 26th Dynasty (Saite kings) attempted to restore centralized authority.

What We Know, Assume, and Still Wonder

What We Know

What We Assume

  • Economic activity relied on trade and Nile agriculture but was affected by political instability.
  • Some provincial governors may have held semi-autonomous power under central authority.
