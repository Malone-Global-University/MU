Early West African society in central Nigeria, famous for terracotta art and ironworking.

Overview

The Nok culture is one of West Africaâ€™s earliest known societies with advanced **ironworking** and distinctive **terracotta sculptures**. They laid the foundation for later civilizations in the region.

Settlements & Society

  • Villages organized around farming and iron production.
  • Specialized craftsmen created terracotta figurines depicting humans and animals.
  • Evidence of social stratification through burial practices and craft specialization.

Culture & Achievements

- Early **iron smelting and toolmaking** in West Africa.
- Terracotta sculptures with realistic human and animal features.
- Agricultural innovations including millet and sorghum cultivation.

Legacy

Nok culture influenced subsequent West African societies, contributing to technological, artistic, and cultural development that would shape empires like Ghana, Mali, and Songhai centuries later.
