Early West African Trading Societies (pre-500 CE)

Early riverine and inland trading centers along the Niger and Senegal rivers, precursors to Ghana and Mali empires.

Overview

Before the rise of the Ghana Empire, West Africa had thriving trading communities along the Niger River and in the Sahel. They exchanged **gold, ivory, and salt** with regional neighbors and laid the groundwork for later empires.

Major Settlements & Sites

  • Jenne-Jeno (Mali): Early urbanized center with craft specialization and trade.
  • Tichitt Tradition (Mauritania): Stone settlements with agriculture and cattle herding.
  • Other riverine towns controlling local trade and agriculture.

Culture & Achievements

- Early urban planning and community organization.
- Ironworking, pottery, and craft specialization.
- Agriculture, cattle herding, and local trade networks.
- Foundations for later West African kingdoms.

Legacy

These early trading societies established the economic, technological, and social patterns that enabled the **Ghana, Mali, and Songhai empires** to flourish centuries later.
