Lugalzagesi, king of Umma, rose in the mid-3rd millennium BCE to unify much of Sumer under his rule by c. 2350 BCE. His campaigns demonstrate the height of city-state consolidation before the advent of the Akkadian Empire under Sargon. Textual inscriptions and later king lists record his victories over neighboring city-states, including Lagash, Uruk, and Ur.

Historical context

After decades of inter-city warfare in Early Dynastic Sumer, Lugalzagesi emerged as a dominant figure. His campaigns aimed to establish centralized authority, control trade routes, and secure agricultural lands throughout Sumer. The political landscape included Lagash, Umma, Uruk, Ur, and other city-states, each with its own claims of kingship.
