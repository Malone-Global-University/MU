Around 2004 BCE, the Sumerian city of Ur, the capital of the Ur III dynasty, fell to Elamite invaders. This event marked the definitive end of Sumerian political dominance in southern Mesopotamia and paved the way for the rise of the Isin-Larsa period.

Historical context

The Ur III dynasty, established by Ur-Nammu and continued by Shulgi, had maintained relative stability for over a century. Economic pressures, Amorite migrations, internal revolts, and Elamite aggression eventually overwhelmed Urâ€™s defenses. The fall of Ur ended centralized Sumerian rule and shifted power northward to Isin.

Principal actors

  • Ur III rulers: Ibbi-Sin, the last king of Ur, struggling to maintain authority.
  • Elamites: Forces from Susa and surrounding highlands, raiding and ultimately sacking Ur.
  • Amorites: Opportunistic groups who settled in weakened territories following the collapse.

Sources & evidence

  • Cuneiform administrative records showing declining control, tax collection, and military mobilization.
  • Royal inscriptions of Ur III kings documenting campaigns, tribute, and losses.
  • Archaeological layers in Ur showing destruction and abandonment, including burned structures.
