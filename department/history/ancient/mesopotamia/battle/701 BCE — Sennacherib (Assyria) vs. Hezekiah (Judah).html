The siege of Jerusalem in 701 BCE was a defining confrontation between the mighty Assyrian Empire under King Sennacherib and the Kingdom of Judah under King Hezekiah. Though Assyria captured many Judean cities, Jerusalem itself survived, an outcome remembered both in Assyrian royal inscriptions and the Hebrew Bible.

Why this matters

  • One of the best-documented events in Near Eastern antiquity, recorded in Assyrian inscriptions, biblical texts, and archaeology.
  • Highlights the interplay of empire and small states in the ancient Near East.
  • Became central to Judean and later Jewish identity, remembered as divine deliverance.

Historical context

Date: 701 BCE. Assyria dominated the Near East under Sennacherib, son of Sargon II. Judah, under King Hezekiah, resisted Assyrian control after the fall of Samaria (722 BCE). Egypt encouraged anti-Assyrian resistance among Levantine states.
