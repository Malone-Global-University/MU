Origins

Byblos began as a Neolithic fishing village around 5000 BCE on the coast of present-day Lebanon. Its natural harbor made it a gateway for maritime trade. Over time, it evolved into a powerful Bronze Age city, known to the Egyptians as "Kbn" or "Gubal."

Rise and Influence

Byblos grew rich from trade, especially in timber and papyrus, which gave the city its Greek name. It became a central link between Egypt and the Levant, with Egyptian pharaohs commissioning temples and palaces in the city.
