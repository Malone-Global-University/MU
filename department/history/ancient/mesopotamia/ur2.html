Origins

Ur began as a small settlement in southern Mesopotamia, flourishing around 3800 BCE. Its proximity to the Euphrates River and fertile lands helped it grow into a significant urban and trade center.

Rise and Influence

Ur rose to prominence during the Early Dynastic and Ur III periods. It became a cultural, political, and religious hub, known for monumental architecture and sophisticated administration.

Decline

Ur declined after the fall of the Ur III dynasty around 2004 BCE due to invasions by the Elamites and Amorites. While it remained inhabited, its political prominence diminished, eventually becoming part of Babylonian and later empires.
