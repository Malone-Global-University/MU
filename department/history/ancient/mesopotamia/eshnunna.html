Origins

Eshnunna emerged as a city-state along the Diyala River around 2500 BCE. Its fertile lands and strategic location enabled control of regional trade routes between Sumer, Akkad, and Elam.

Rise and Influence

Eshnunna reached prominence as a center of trade, military power, and law. It became an important intermediary between Mesopotamian empires and neighboring regions, influencing commerce and political affairs.
