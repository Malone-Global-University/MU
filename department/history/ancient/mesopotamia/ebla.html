Origins

Ebla emerged around 2500 BCE along key trade routes connecting Mesopotamia, Anatolia, and the Levant. Its strategic location facilitated economic growth and allowed it to become a diplomatic and cultural center in the region.

Rise and Influence

Ebla flourished as a powerful city-state, maintaining extensive archives in cuneiform that record administrative, economic, and diplomatic activities. Its influence extended to nearby city-states and trade partners.
