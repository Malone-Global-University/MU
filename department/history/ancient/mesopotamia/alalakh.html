Origins

Alalakh developed around 2000 BCE as a strategic city along trade routes connecting Anatolia, Mesopotamia, and the Levant. Fertile surroundings and access to rivers supported agriculture and urban growth.

Rise and Influence

The city became a regional hub for trade and diplomacy, maintaining relations with neighboring powers like Mari, Ebla, and Yamhad. Alalakh is noted for its palace archives, which record administration, treaties, and economic activity.
