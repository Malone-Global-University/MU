The Sumerian King List

The Sumerian King List is an ancient record of rulers written on clay tablets in cuneiform. It mixes myth and history, beginning with legendary kings said to rule for tens of thousands of years, then shifting into rulers we can confirm from archaeology. The list claims that kingship was a sacred gift from the gods, and that it passed from one city to another across Mesopotamia.

Before 2900 BCE

c. 2600 BCE

The list begins to include rulers we can verify. Enmebaragesi of Kish is the first king confirmed by archaeology, and Gilgamesh of Uruk appears as both a king and a legendary hero.

Why It Matters

The King List is one of the earliest attempts to write history. It reveals how the Sumerians saw kingship: a divine trust that moved between cities. It also preserves the memory of both mythic heroes and real rulers.
