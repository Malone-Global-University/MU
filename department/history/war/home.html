The Clash of Civilizations Through Time

The Empires and Wars archive serves as a living record of human conflict and cooperation. Every border on the map, every collapsed city, and every victory monument tells a story of power, resistance, and consequence. This section provides a place to gather battle analyses, strategic reflections, and lesser-known historical lessons that may not fit neatly into other courses. From Sumer to the Space Age this archive examines every major war, empire, and border reshaping event that defined world history.

Ancient Era (c. 3500 BCE – 500 CE)

  • 3300–2500 BCE: Early Sumerian city wars – Lagash vs. Umma; Egypt’s Unification Wars.
  • 2300–2000 BCE: Akkadian conquests under Sargon; Gutian invasions.
  • 1800–1200 BCE: Babylonian, Hittite, and Egyptian wars; Trojan War (mythic/legendary context).
  • 1200–800 BCE: Collapse of Bronze Age empires; Assyrian expansion.
  • 800–300 BCE: Greek-Persian Wars; Punic Wars begin; rise of Rome and China’s Warring States.
  • 300 BCE–500 CE: Roman conquests and civil wars; Han-Xiongnu conflicts; Fall of Western Rome.

Medieval Era (c. 500 – 1500)

  • 500–800: Barbarian invasions; Islamic conquests; Tang expansion in East Asia.
  • 800–1100: Viking raids; Byzantine-Arab wars; early African and Indian Ocean conflicts.
  • 1100–1300: Crusades; Mongol invasions; feudal wars in Europe and Japan.
  • 1300–1500: Hundred Years’ War; Ottoman conquests; rise of gunpowder empires.

Early Modern Era (c. 1500 – 1800)

  • 1500–1600: Spanish conquests in the Americas; Ottoman-Habsburg wars; African resistance campaigns.
  • 1600–1700: Thirty Years’ War; English Civil War; Mughal and Qing expansion.
  • 1700–1800: Seven Years’ War; American Revolution; wars of empire in India and Africa.

Modern Era (c. 1800 – 1945)

  • 1800–1850: Napoleonic Wars; Latin American independence wars; Zulu and Ashanti conflicts.
  • 1850–1900: U.S. Civil War; Crimean War; colonial wars in Africa and Asia.
  • 1900–1945: World War I; World War II; anti-colonial revolts.

Contemporary Era (1945 – Present)

  • 1945–1991: Cold War conflicts — Korea, Vietnam, Afghanistan, decolonization wars in Africa.
  • 1991–2020: Gulf Wars; Balkan wars; African civil wars; War on Terror.
  • 2020–Present: Ukraine War; Israel–Hamas conflicts; coups and insurgencies in Africa and Asia.

  • Europe — Dynastic wars, crusades, revolutions, world wars.

Statistics

Total wars since 3500 BCE: Approx. 14,000 (including civil and tribal conflicts).

Average wars per year: About 2–3 ongoing somewhere in the world.

Modern recorded wars (since 1816): 315 interstate wars, thousands of civil conflicts.
