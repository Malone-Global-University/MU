The American Revolutionary War arose from growing colonial discontent over British taxation and governance. Key Figures

  • George Washington: Commander-in-Chief of the Continental Army.
  • King George III: British monarch during the war.
  • Benjamin Franklin: Diplomat who secured French support.
  • Thomas Jefferson: Principal author of the Declaration of Independence.
  • Marquis de Lafayette: French officer aiding Washington.
  • Benedict Arnold: American general who defected to Britain.

Consequences

  • Birth of a Nation: Formation of the United States as a republic.
  • Global Influence: Inspired revolutions in France, Haiti, and Latin America.
  • Political Innovation: Paved the way for the U.S. Constitution (1787).
  • Impact on Native & African Peoples: Outcomes varied; promises of freedom were often unfulfilled.

Legacy

The American Revolution demonstrated that governments derive power from the consent of the governed, challenging traditional empires. Its success reshaped political thought worldwide and set the stage for modern democratic states.
