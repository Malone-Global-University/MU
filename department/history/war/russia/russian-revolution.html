After his abdication, Nicholas II and his family were placed under house arrest. In July 1918, during the Russian Civil War, they were executed by Bolshevik guards in Yekaterinburg. Their deaths symbolized the definitive end of the old imperial order and the rise of a new revolutionary era.

8. Legacy and Historical Debate

Historians debate whether Nicholas II was a victim of circumstance or the architect of his own downfall. His personal piety and sense of duty could not compensate for his political weakness, inflexibility, and disconnection from the realities of his empire. The revolution that overthrew him reshaped global politics for the next century, inspiring both communist movements and anti-communist responses worldwide.
