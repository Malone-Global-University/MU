Invasion of Panama (1989)

Operation Just Cause and the Fall of Manuel Noriega

Background

The Invasion of Panama, code-named Operation Just Cause, began on December 20, 1989. It marked one of the largest U.S. military actions since the Vietnam War and the last major operation of the Cold War era.

The operation aimed to depose General Manuel Noriega, who had seized power and was implicated in drug trafficking, election fraud, and suppression of democratic opposition. Noriega had previously been a U.S. intelligence asset, but his growing independence and alliance with drug cartels turned Washington against him.

Causes of the Conflict

  • Drug Trafficking Allegations: Noriega was indicted in U.S. courts for smuggling cocaine and laundering drug money through Panamanian banks.
  • Threat to American Citizens: Rising violence endangered the large U.S. community living in Panama, particularly near the Canal Zone.

Noriega surrendered on January 3, 1990 after taking refuge in the Vatican embassy. He was extradited to the United States, convicted of drug charges, and imprisoned.
