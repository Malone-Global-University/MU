🇺🇸 Modern U.S. Wars & Military Operations Cold War Era (1945–1991) Korean War (1950–1953) – First major Cold War conflict; U.S.-led UN forces fought North Korean and Chinese troops. Vietnam War (1955–1975) – Long, costly conflict between communist North Vietnam and U.S.-backed South Vietnam. Cuban Missile Crisis (1962) – 13-day nuclear standoff between the U.S. and Soviet Union. Bay of Pigs Invasion (1961) – Failed U.S.-backed invasion to overthrow Fidel Castro in Cuba. Invasion of Grenada (1983) – U.S. intervention to oust a Marxist government. Invasion of Panama (1989) – U.S. removed Manuel Noriega from power. Post–Cold War Era (1990s) Gulf War (1990–1991) – U.S.-led coalition expelled Iraq from Kuwait. Somalia Intervention (1992–1993) – Humanitarian mission turned battle, remembered for “Black Hawk Down.” Bosnian War / NATO Intervention (1994–1995) – U.S. and allies halted ethnic cleansing in the Balkans. Kosovo War (1999) – NATO bombing campaign against Serbia to stop persecution of ethnic Albanians. War on Terror (2001–Present) September 11 Attacks (2001) – Coordinated terrorist attacks by al-Qaeda on the U.S. War in Afghanistan (2001–2021) – Longest U.S. war; aimed at dismantling al-Qaeda and removing the Taliban. Iraq War (2003–2011) – U.S.-led invasion to remove Saddam Hussein and neutralize alleged WMDs. ISIS Conflicts (2011–2017) – U.S. and coalition efforts to destroy the Islamic State’s caliphate. Drone Wars (2004–Present) – Targeted counterterrorism operations in Yemen, Somalia, and Pakistan. Middle East & North Africa Interventions Libya Intervention (2011) – NATO bombing campaign during the Arab Spring. Syrian Civil War (2011–Present) – U.S. supported rebels and fought ISIS. Yemen Civil War (2014–Present) – Ongoing proxy conflict involving U.S.-Saudi and Iranian-backed forces. Domestic & Global Security Incidents Iran Hostage Crisis (1979–1981) – 52 Americans held for 444 days in Tehran. Beirut Bombings (1983) – Suicide attacks on U.S. Marine barracks. Airliner Hijackings (1970s) – Wave of politically motivated skyjackings. Oklahoma City Bombing (1995) – Domestic terrorist attack on federal building. USS Cole Bombing (2000) – al-Qaeda suicide attack on U.S. Navy destroyer. Benghazi Attack (2012) – Assault on U.S. diplomatic compound in Libya. Cyber & Proxy Conflicts Cyber Warfare (2000s–Present): U.S., China, Russia, and Iran engaged in espionage and infrastructure hacking. Ukraine Conflict (2014–Present): U.S. and NATO support Ukraine against Russian aggression. Iran–U.S. Tensions (2019–Present): Drone strikes, proxy skirmishes, and naval incidents in the Persian Gulf.
