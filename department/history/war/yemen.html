Historical Context

The Yemen Civil War began in 2014 when Houthi rebels, from the Zaidi Shia minority, seized control of the capital Sana‚Äôa and much of northern Yemen. The internationally recognized government fled, prompting a Saudi-led coalition to intervene in 2015. The conflict has become a complex proxy war, exacerbating Yemen‚Äôs humanitarian crisis.

Causes of the War

  • Houthi Rebellion: Discontent with the Yemeni government and historical marginalization of the Zaidi Shia population.
  • Political Instability: Transition challenges following the Arab Spring and President Saleh‚Äôs resignation in 2012.
  • Regional Rivalries: Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Iran backing different sides in a proxy struggle for influence.
  • Economic Collapse: Poverty, unemployment, and resource scarcity intensified the conflict.

Key Figures

  • Abdrabbuh Mansur Hadi: Yemeni President ousted by Houthi forces.
  • Houthi Leadership: Rebels controlling northern Yemen and opposing the government.
  • Saudi-led Coalition: Military alliance supporting the Yemeni government, including Saudi Arabia, UAE, and others.
  • Iran: Provided support to the Houthis as part of regional influence strategy.

Military Strategy & Operations

The war involves conventional, asymmetric, and proxy strategies:

  • Houthi Tactics: Guerilla warfare, ballistic missiles, drone attacks, and control of strategic regions.
  • Government & Coalition: Airstrikes, naval blockades, and support to loyalist militias.
  • Proxy Dynamics: Iran and Saudi Arabia providing weapons, training, and logistics to opposing sides.
  • Humanitarian Impact: Civilian areas frequently caught in the crossfire and subject to siege tactics.

Impact on Civilians

Yemen faces one of the world‚Äôs worst humanitarian crises. Millions are displaced, widespread famine exists, and healthcare infrastructure is devastated. Civilian casualties and cholera outbreaks have compounded human suffering.

Outcome and Consequences

The war remains ongoing with no decisive resolution. The country is fragmented, and peace negotiations have repeatedly stalled. Regional and global actors continue to influence the conflict, maintaining Yemen as a focal point of proxy warfare.

Legacy and Lessons

The Yemen Civil War highlights the human cost of proxy wars, regional rivalries, and failed state governance. Lessons include the importance of humanitarian intervention, conflict resolution mechanisms, and addressing local grievances to prevent long-term instability.
