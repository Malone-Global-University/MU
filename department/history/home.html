Historians speak of the Middle Ages, archaeologists divide prehistory into the Stone, Bronze, and Iron Ages, and geologists measure the Holocene or Jurassic Ages. Yet all of these ideas trace back to a deeper, unified concept: an age is simply the measure of time since the origin of something. From the birth of the universe to the rise of human civilizations, ‚Äúages‚Äù provide a framework that allows us to situate events and developments on different scales of time.

    Cosmic Ages

    At the largest scale are the ages of the cosmos. The universe itself is about 13.8 billion years old, dating back to the Big Bang. Shortly after, the Milky Way Galaxy formed, around 13.6 billion years ago. Much later, our solar system came into being about 4.57 billion years ago, along with the Earth and Moon. These cosmic ages remind us that the story of humanity is only a very recent chapter in a much older universe.

    Planetary Ages

    Within the solar system, Earth has its own layered history.
