Revolutions and the Modern Era

From political upheaval to industrial transformation, explore the events that shaped the modern world.

Introduction

This lesson covers major revolutions and key developments from the 17th to 20th centuries. Political, social, and technological changes transformed societies and laid the foundations for the modern world.

Key Themes

Political Revolutions

Movements like the American and French Revolutions challenged monarchies and promoted democracy, citizenship, and individual rights.

Industrialization

The Industrial Revolution transformed economies, urbanized societies, and reshaped labor and technology in Europe and beyond.

Social and Cultural Change

Enlightenment ideas, abolition movements, suffrage campaigns, and educational reforms transformed societies.

Global Conflicts

Major wars such as the World Wars shaped geopolitics, national identities, and international systems in the modern era.
