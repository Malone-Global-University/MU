World War I (1914â€“1918)

Overview: Began in Europe and rapidly expanded. Characterized by trench warfare on the Western Front and large territorial battles on the Eastern Front.

Major Causes â€” M.A.I.N.

  • Militarism: Arms race and military planning.
  • Alliances: Entangling treaties (e.g., Triple Entente vs. Triple Alliance).
  • Imperialism: Competition for colonies and resources.
  • Nationalism: Ethnic tensions, especially in the Balkans.

Trigger

Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in Sarajevo, June 28, 1914.

Major Fronts & Features

  • Western Front: trench warfare, stalemate.
  • Eastern Front: more movement between Russia and Central Powers.
  • Colonial theaters in Africa, the Middle East, and Asia.

Outcomes

  • ~16 million fatalities and major social upheaval.
  • Collapse of empires: Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, Russian, and German monarchies weakened.

World War II (1939â€“1945)

Overview: A global conflict that grew out of political, economic, and social instability after WWI and the rise of fascist regimes in Germany, Italy, and militarism in Japan.

Major Causes

  • Aftermath of the Treaty of Versailles and economic instability.
  • Rise of fascist and totalitarian regimes.
  • Expansionist aims (Germany's Lebensraum, Japan in Asia).
  • Failure of collective security mechanisms (League of Nations).

Trigger

Germany's invasion of Poland on September 1, 1939.

Major Theaters

  • European Theater: Blitzkrieg, occupation, resistance.
  • Pacific Theater: island-hopping, naval and air warfare.
  • African & Mediterranean campaigns: control of resources and supply routes.

Outcomes

  • ~70 million deaths worldwide; massive civilian casualties.
  • The Holocaust: systematic genocide of ~6 million Jews and millions of other victims.
  • Creation of the United Nations (1945) and start of the Cold War between the U.S. and USSR.

Teaching tips

  • Use primary documents (diaries, treaties, speeches) to let students practice source analysis.
  • Create role-play debates (e.g., Treaty of Versailles negotiations).
  • Assign mapping projects to visualize front movement and colonial theaters.
