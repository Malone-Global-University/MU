Paleolithic Humans (~300,000–10,000 BCE)

Before farming and cities, humans were hunter-gatherers. They developed stone tools, mastered fire, and created early symbolic art. Bands of humans moved seasonally, hunting game and gathering plants.

Neolithic Revolution (~10,000–6000 BCE)

Humans began farming and domesticating animals, leading to permanent settlements. Early villages like Jarmo in northern Iraq show the transition from nomadic life to agriculture.

  • Farming of wheat, barley, and legumes
  • Domestication of goats, sheep, and cattle
  • Development of pottery and weaving

Chalcolithic Cultures (~6200–3200 BCE)

Before Sumer, several cultures emerged in Mesopotamia:

  • Halaf (6200–5700 BCE): Northern Mesopotamia; known for painted pottery.
  • Samarra (6000–5300 BCE): Central Mesopotamia; early irrigation and village life.
  • Hassuna (6000–5000 BCE): Northern Mesopotamia; small farming villages with simple temples.
  • Ubaid (5400–3200 BCE): Southern Mesopotamia; larger settlements, irrigation, and proto-temples.

Timeline to Sumer (~4500 BCE)

  1. ~7000 BCE – Jarmo village established (early farming)
  2. 6200–5700 BCE – Halaf culture (northern Mesopotamia)
  3. 6000–5300 BCE – Samarra culture (central Mesopotamia)
  4. 6000–5000 BCE – Hassuna culture (northern Mesopotamia)
  5. 5400–3200 BCE – Ubaid culture (southern Mesopotamia)
  6. ~4500 BCE – Early Sumerian city-states begin to emerge

Next Steps

From Ubaid villages, the first urban civilizations arose in southern Mesopotamia, giving birth to Sumer and the earliest known cities in human history.
