Indigenous and Pre-Colonial Period

Iowa was originally inhabited by the Ioway, Sioux, Meskwaki, and Sauk tribes. These communities relied on riverine ecosystems for fishing, hunting bison and deer, and cultivating maize, beans, and squash. Social organization emphasized kinship networks, seasonal mobility, and careful ecological management.
