Indigenous and Pre-Colonial Period

Before European arrival, Louisiana was home to Caddo, Choctaw, Natchez, and other Native American nations. Societies relied on agriculture, riverine trade, and mound-building. Social, political, and religious institutions were sophisticated and closely tied to the Mississippi River system.

European Colonization

French explorers, led by La Salle, claimed Louisiana in 1682. French settlements established trade, plantations, and Catholic missions. Spanish control followed intermittently in the 18th century, influencing architecture, law, and trade networks. Both colonial powers relied heavily on Indigenous alliances and African enslaved labor.
