Indigenous and Pre-Colonial Period

Oregon was inhabited by Chinook, Nez Perce, Klamath, and other Indigenous nations. These groups had complex social, political, and trade systems and managed salmon fishing, hunting, and agriculture. Seasonal migrations and intertribal alliances ensured resource sustainability and cultural continuity.
