Indigenous and Pre-Colonial Period

Maryland was inhabited by the Piscataway, Susquehannock, and other Algonquian-speaking groups. Communities relied on fishing, maize cultivation, and complex trade networks along the Chesapeake Bay. Social organization was clan-based, and spiritual life was closely tied to seasonal cycles and natural resources.

Colonial Foundation

Founded in 1632 as a proprietary colony for Lord Baltimore, Maryland was intended as a haven for English Catholics and a model of religious tolerance.
