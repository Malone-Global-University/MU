Pre-Colonial and Indigenous Foundations

Before European arrival, Delaware was home to the Lenape (Delaware) and Nanticoke peoples, whose trade routes linked the Chesapeake to the Atlantic. These communities managed forests and rivers sustainably, forming kinship-based networks rather than centralized hierarchies. Disease and European encroachment disrupted these systems, displacing entire villages by the late 17th century.
