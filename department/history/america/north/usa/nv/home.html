Indigenous and Pre-Colonial Period

Nevada was home to Paiute, Shoshone, and Washoe peoples. These groups practiced seasonal hunting, gathering, and irrigation-based agriculture along rivers and oases. Social, economic, and spiritual networks governed life, with extensive trade routes linking regions across the Great Basin.

European Exploration

Spanish and later American explorers entered the region in the 18th and 19th centuries. Initial contact brought mapping, trade, and sporadic settlement. European influence was limited until discovery of mineral resources attracted migration and investment in the mid-19th century.
