Indigenous and Pre-Colonial Period

The region was inhabited by Cherokee, Shawnee, and other Indigenous peoples. Hunting, agriculture, and trade along the Ohio and Mississippi Rivers supported complex societies. Cultural and spiritual practices shaped governance, land use, and social hierarchies.

European Exploration

French and English explorers entered Kentucky in the 17th and 18th centuries. Trading posts, forts, and early settlements facilitated commerce and territorial claims. European contact introduced firearms, horses, and diseases, dramatically affecting Indigenous populations.

Economic Development and Labor

Agriculture dominated, with tobacco, hemp, corn, and horse breeding central to the economy. Coal mining, timber, and later manufacturing expanded labor opportunities. Labor strikes occurred in mining and industrial sectors, influencing state labor laws and union organization. Mechanization and transport networks transformed rural labor.
