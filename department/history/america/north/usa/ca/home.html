Indigenous & Pre-Colonial Period

Home to perhaps the most linguistically diverse Indigenous region in North America, California hosted dozens of nations with complex seasonal economies based on coastal fisheries, acorn processing, and inland hunting/gathering. Social organization ranged widely, and sustained land stewardship practices shaped fire regimes and ecosystems. These systems were catastrophically disrupted by missionization, disease, and settler colonization.

Statehood & Expansion

California entered the Union in 1850 as a free state, but the legal and extralegal dispossession of Indigenous peoples continued. The Gold Rush created extreme wealth concentration, boomtown economies, and entrenched patterns of racial exclusion (anti-Chinese laws, property restrictions). Over time, railroads, agribusiness, oil, and entertainment industries produced a diversified capitalist economy heavily integrated into national and global circuits.
