Core Principles

The future of the African American community depends on whether our youth have access to education, mentorship, cultural identity, economic mobility, and safe environments.

A society that does not invest in its children guarantees its own decline. My approach is grounded in a simple principle: every Black child must have the tools to thrive, compete, and leadâ€”without apology.

Key Challenges Facing Black Youth

  • Underfunded schools and a lack of enrichment programs.
  • Exposure to violence and unsafe neighborhood conditions.
  • Limited mentorship from professionals and community leaders.
  • Barriers to economic participation including job access and entrepreneurship.
  • Mental health challenges without adequate culturally competent support.
  • Over-policing that criminalizes rather than supports youth.
  • Digital divide that limits access to modern opportunities.

Policy Positions

1. Education & Academic Support

  • Fund advanced placement, STEM, language, and arts programs in Black-majority schools.
  • Provide free tutoring and college readiness support.
  • Expand scholarship programs for Black high achievers and first-generation students.

2. Mentorship & Leadership Development

  • Create a national network of Black mentors and professionals.
  • Fund leadership camps, rites-of-passage programs, and youth councils.
  • Support culturally rooted mentorship programs run by community institutions.

3. Economic Pathways for Youth

  • Establish youth employment programs with fair pay and structured training.
  • Offer grants for young Black entrepreneurs and teen business incubators.
  • Provide financial literacy education in all Kâ€“12 schools.

4. Safety, Health & Community Wellbeing

  • Invest in safe recreation centers, sports programs, and after-school activities.
  • Fund mental health services tailored to Black youth experiences.
  • Reduce youth exposure to policing by substituting community support workers.

5. Technology, Digital Access & Future Careers

  • Guarantee universal high-speed internet for all children.
  • Provide coding, robotics, and AI training programs.
  • Partner with tech firms to create internship pipelines for Black youth.

6. Cultural Identity & Community Strength

  • Support programs connecting youth to Black history and diaspora culture.
  • Fund arts, music, and cultural expression as tools of empowerment.
  • Protect youth from harmful media stereotypes through cultural literacy education.
