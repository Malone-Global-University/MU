Immigration Policy

Home Department Defense Immigration Policy

Overview

Immigration policy must balance compassion, national security, and economic stability. For the African American community, immigration intersects with jobs, wages, political power, and access to public resources. This page clarifies the official stance on how U.S. immigration policy should be structured while protecting the long-term interests of Black Americans.

Key Positions

1. Protecting Economic Opportunities for Black Workers

Immigration policy should not depress wages or increase competition for entry-level and working-class jobs, which disproportionately affects African Americans. Enforcement of labor protections, employer accountability, and fair-wage standards are essential.

2. Border and Visa System Reform

A secure and organized immigration system is necessary. This includes strengthening border management, fixing visa backlogs, and creating transparent pathways for legal immigration that do not undermine opportunities for U.S. citizens.

3. Opposition to Exploitative Labor Systems

Guest-worker and exploitative temporary labor programs often displace Black workers. Reform must prevent corporations from using foreign labor to avoid fair wages and fair hiring practices.

4. Support for Humanitarian Protections

The U.S. should remain a refuge for individuals fleeing violence or persecution, while ensuring that humanitarian processes do not create uncontrolled population inflows that overwhelm urban Black communities.

5. Safeguarding Political Representation

Sudden demographic shifts can affect political redistricting and dilute the collective voting power of African American communities. Immigration reform must include protections for fair representation.

6. Prioritizing Domestic Investment

Policy focus must remain on improving living standards, education, employment, and economic development for African Americans before expanding large-scale immigration programs.

Policy Goals

  • Enforce hiring laws to prevent wage theft and unfair labor competition.
  • Rebuild industrial and urban economies most affected by job displacement.
  • Ensure immigration laws do not reduce Black political influence in key districts.
  • Protect American workers while maintaining humanitarian support systems.
