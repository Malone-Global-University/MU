Economic Development Policy Stance

Malone Global University – Office of Public Policy & Cultural Affairs

Overview

Economic power is the backbone of community stability, political leverage, and generational progress. African American communities have faced centuries of barriers to wealth creation, including exclusion from fair credit markets, employment discrimination, redlining, and limited access to capital. My official stance prioritizes strong entrepreneurship, asset building, workforce advancement, and financial inclusion.

Official Position Statements

1. Black Entrepreneurship & Business Growth

Position: Expand support for Black-owned businesses through grants, incubators, mentorship programs, and improved access to capital.

Rationale: Entrepreneurship creates local jobs, circulates dollars within the community, and builds durable wealth.

Recommended Action: Establish municipal and state small-business development funds specifically targeting historically underserved populations.

2. Access to Credit & Fair Banking

Position: Strengthen enforcement against discriminatory lending, expand community development financial institutions (CDFIs), and promote alternative lending models.

Rationale: African Americans are disproportionately denied loans or offered higher interest rates, limiting business creation and homeownership.

Recommended Action: Implement transparent credit evaluation systems and federally backed credit programs.

3. Homeownership & Property Ownership

Position: Encourage homeownership, protect against predatory lending, and address remaining effects of redlining.

Rationale: Homeownership remains the primary driver of generational wealth in the United States.

Recommended Action: Expand down-payment assistance, enforce fair housing laws, and create community land trusts in gentrifying neighborhoods.

4. Workforce Advancement & Skills Training

Position: Prioritize job training in high-growth industries—technology, healthcare, skilled trades, green energy, finance.

Rationale: African American workers are often concentrated in low-wage sectors; accessible training opens pathways to upward mobility.

Recommended Action: Establish partnerships between community colleges, HBCUs, workforce boards, and employers to deliver low-cost certification programs.

5. Wage Equity & Anti-Discrimination

Position: Enforce equal pay protections and strengthen oversight of discriminatory practices in hiring and promotions.

Rationale: African Americans—especially women—face persistent wage gaps even in comparable positions.

Recommended Action: Implement blind hiring practices and require annual pay transparency reports for large employers.

6. Reparative Economic Policies

Position: Support research-based approaches to reparative economic initiatives addressing the compounded effects of slavery, segregation, and discriminatory policy.

Rationale: Wealth gaps were engineered through public policy and require policy-based remediation.

Recommended Action: Form commissions to evaluate viable models of compensation, investment, or structural reform.

7. Community-Owned Assets & Collective Economics

Position: Promote cooperative economics, community investment trusts, and locally controlled development projects.

Rationale: These models allow communities to own the businesses, land, and revenue streams around them.

Recommended Action: Provide technical assistance for cooperative formation and fund community wealth-building pilots.
