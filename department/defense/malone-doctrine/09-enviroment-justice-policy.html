Environmental Justice

Home Department Defense Environmental Justice

Core Position

Environmental justice is essential for the health, safety, and economic well-being of African American communities. These communities have historically been disproportionately exposed to pollution, toxic waste, and environmental hazards due to discriminatory zoning, industrial siting, and systemic neglect. Policies must ensure equitable access to clean air, water, and green spaces, while reducing harm from climate change and industrial development.

Key Principles & Policy Stances

1. Reducing Pollution & Toxic Exposure

  • Ban or heavily regulate industries that disproportionately impact Black neighborhoods.
  • Require environmental impact assessments for projects in historically underserved areas.
  • Hold corporations accountable for environmental damage and health impacts.

2. Equitable Access to Clean Water and Air

  • Invest in clean water infrastructure in urban and rural African American communities.
  • Reduce air pollution near schools, housing, and community centers.
  • Ensure enforcement of environmental standards in all communities.

3. Climate Justice

  • Support policies that reduce climate vulnerability in Black communities.
  • Provide resources for climate-resilient housing, energy efficiency, and disaster preparedness.
  • Include African American communities in climate policy planning and decision-making.

4. Sustainable Development & Green Jobs

  • Invest in renewable energy and sustainable infrastructure in underserved neighborhoods.
  • Create green jobs and workforce programs targeted to Black communities.
  • Support local environmental entrepreneurship and innovation.

5. Community Empowerment & Environmental Advocacy

  • Fund community-led environmental monitoring and advocacy programs.
  • Encourage civic participation in local and federal environmental decision-making.
  • Educate communities on environmental rights and protections.

6. Addressing Legacy Environmental Injustice

  • Remediate brownfields, abandoned industrial sites, and contaminated land in historically Black neighborhoods.
  • Provide healthcare support for residents affected by historical environmental hazards.
  • Integrate environmental justice considerations into urban planning and housing policies.

Position Summary

Environmental justice is a civil and human rights issue. African American communities must have equal access to clean air, water, and safe living conditions. Addressing historical and current inequities requires proactive federal, state, and local policies, community engagement, and corporate accountability.

Our goal is to ensure sustainable, healthy, and safe environments for all African Americans, today and for future generations.
