Foreign Policy & Africa Relations

Political Science Department — Malone Global University

Overview

Foreign policy shapes global stability, national security, and economic development. For African Americans, foreign policy also affects diaspora relations, international trade, and the political relationship between the United States and the African continent. A strong Africa is in the strategic interest of Black communities worldwide.

Core Africa Relations Priorities

  • Strengthen U.S.–Africa partnerships through equitable trade agreements and economic cooperation.
  • Support African Union integration and continent-wide infrastructure, technology, and development initiatives.
  • Diaspora engagement that recognizes African Americans as a foreign-policy stakeholder group.
  • End exploitative aid models that create dependency rather than sovereignty.
  • Promote Pan-African diplomacy linking U.S. policy to global Black empowerment.

Economic & Trade Policy

  • Fair bilateral trade agreements prioritizing African industrial growth and technology transfer.
  • Investment incentives for U.S. companies partnering ethically with African firms.
  • Infrastructure development support for energy, transportation, and digital connectivity.
  • Reform global lending practices to prevent predatory IMF/World Bank conditionalities.

Diplomacy & Security

  • Promote African-led peacekeeping instead of foreign military footprints.
  • Limit U.S. support for authoritarian regimes and prioritize human rights standards.
  • Expand cultural diplomacy including academic exchange, arts collaboration, and youth engagement.
  • Counter external exploitation by ensuring African nations retain control of natural resources.

African American Diaspora Engagement

  • U.S.–Africa diaspora council to include Black Americans in shaping Africa-related policy.
  • Streamlined cultural and educational exchanges between African countries and U.S. Black institutions.
  • Support for dual-citizenship pathways for African-descended Americans wishing to return or invest.
  • Strengthen transnational business networks linking Black entrepreneurs across continents.

A Pan-African Vision for the Future

The future of global Black empowerment depends on the stability, prosperity, and sovereignty of African nations. U.S. policy must abandon old models of extraction and interference and instead support cooperative partnership. A unified Africa strengthens the diaspora, and a politically mobilized diaspora strengthens Africa.
