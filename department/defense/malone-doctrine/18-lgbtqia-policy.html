Core Principles

LGBTQ African Americans face unique challenges shaped by the intersection of race, gender, and sexuality. Regardless of personal beliefs, policy must remain rooted in fairness, safety, dignity, and equal treatment under the law.

Every person deserves protection from violence, discrimination, and economic marginalization. This platform prioritizes community well-being while rejecting political fear-mongering, division, or culture-war exploitation.

Key Issues Affecting Black LGBTQ Individuals

  • Higher rates of homelessness, especially among youth rejected by families or systems.
  • Disproportionate violence toward Black transgender women.
  • Healthcare barriers, including discrimination and lack of culturally competent providers.
  • Mental health risks amplified by stigma and lack of support networks.
  • Employment discrimination and workplace hostility.
  • Over-policing and vulnerability to harassment or profiling.
  • Limited representation in policymaking, advocacy, and community institutions.

Policy Positions

1. Safety and Anti-Violence Protections

  • Strengthen protections for LGBTQ individuals against hate crimes and targeted violence.
  • Improve police accountability in cases involving LGBTQ victims.
  • Support community-based violence-prevention programs serving LGBTQ youth and adults.

2. Housing Security

  • Expand housing protections for LGBTQ individuals facing discrimination.
  • Fund shelters and supportive housing programs specifically for LGBTQ youth.
  • Ensure public housing agencies enforce non-discrimination policies.

3. Healthcare Access & Mental Health Support

  • Guarantee equal access to healthcare without discrimination.
  • Expand mental health services tailored to the needs of Black LGBTQ individuals.
  • Train providers in culturally competent care to reduce fear and mistrust.

4. Youth Protection & Support

  • Prevent discrimination against LGBTQ students in schools.
  • Support safe-space programs and counseling in high-need communities.
  • Protect LGBTQ youth from bullying, homelessness, and predatory exploitation.

5. Employment & Economic Protections

  • Enforce workplace anti-discrimination laws.
  • Support job-training programs targeting marginalized LGBTQ individuals.
  • Encourage Black-owned businesses to adopt inclusive workplace standards.

6. Community Autonomy & Respect for Diversity of Beliefs

  • Acknowledge diverse opinions within the Black community without endorsing hostility.
  • Protect religious freedom while preventing discrimination in public services.
  • Support dialogue-based community healing rather than political polarization.
