Part I: Foundations of Conduct

Human Nature, Moral Order, Identity, Responsibility, and Discipline

Overview

Part I establishes the philosophical and ethical foundation of Malone Doctrine: Volume I. It defines the person as a morally responsible member of society, explains the relationship between individual purpose and community contribution, and identifies the principles that distinguish ordered conduct from social chaos.

These articles provide the premises upon which the remainder of the volume depends. They address human nature, moral agency, identity, responsibility, community role, and discipline so that later doctrines concerning family conduct, community organization, economic advancement, and political development rest upon a consistent understanding of the individual and the obligations of social life.

Articles

The articles progress from the nature and purpose of the individual to the standards required for responsible participation in family and community life.

  • Article 1.1 — Human Nature and Purpose

    Defines the person, examines the relationship between environment and choice, and establishes contribution to society as a central moral duty.

  • Article 1.2 — Moral Order and Social Chaos

    Distinguishes stable moral order from social disorder and explains how shared standards, accountability, and ethical conduct preserve community life.

  • Article 1.3 — Identity, Responsibility, and Community Role

    Explains how identity creates obligations and how individuals fulfill meaningful roles within families, institutions, and communities.

  • Article 1.4 — Discipline

    Defines discipline as the learned capacity to govern conduct, sustain productive habits, and act according to principle despite pressure or inconvenience.

Supporting Material

  • Arguments and Supporting Reasoning

    Collects preliminary arguments, questions, distinctions, and supporting reasoning used in the development of the doctrines established in Part I.
