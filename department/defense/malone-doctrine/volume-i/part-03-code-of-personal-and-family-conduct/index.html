Part III: Code of Personal and Family Conduct

The Standards of Character, Discipline, and Responsible Citizenship

Overview

Part III establishes the ethical standards expected of every individual and family operating under the Malone Doctrine. Having identified the causes of community decline, this section prescribes the personal behaviors necessary to restore trust, stability, and disciplined civic life.

The articles define practical expectations for self-mastery, public conduct, family leadership, mentorship, and social responsibility. Together they form the core code of conduct that governs individual behavior before broader community and institutional reforms can succeed.

Articles

The articles progress from individual discipline to public conduct, then to family leadership and broader social responsibility.

  • Article 3.1 — Self-Mastery and Discipline Systems

    Defines disciplined habits, personal accountability, and systems of self-governance required for ethical conduct.

  • Article 3.2 — Speech, Reputation, and Public Conduct

    Establishes standards for truthful communication, honorable reputation, respectful conduct, and responsible public behavior.

  • Article 3.3 — Family, Mentorship, and Social Responsibility

    Explains the obligations individuals owe to families, future generations, mentorship networks, and the wider community.

  • Code of Conduct

    Presents the consolidated ethical standards and behavioral expectations established throughout Part III.
