Core Principles

African Americans living in rural areas face unique structural disadvantages compared to urban communities. Policies must address disparities in education, healthcare, economic opportunity, infrastructure, and access to social services, while preserving community cohesion and local culture.

Investments in rural Black communities are not charity—they are essential to national prosperity, social equity, and the future of Black families across America.

Key Challenges Facing Rural Black Communities

  • Lack of healthcare access, including mental health and primary care providers.
  • Poor infrastructure: roads, broadband internet, and public transportation gaps.
  • Limited economic opportunities, job training, and small-business support.
  • Educational inequities, including underfunded schools and teacher shortages.
  • Higher rates of food insecurity and lack of grocery access (“food deserts”).
  • Underrepresentation in political decision-making and policy development.
  • Vulnerability to environmental hazards, climate change, and resource scarcity.

Policy Positions

1. Healthcare & Wellness

  • Expand rural clinics, telehealth, and mobile medical units.
  • Provide culturally competent mental health and substance abuse treatment.
  • Increase incentives for healthcare professionals to serve in rural Black communities.

2. Education & Workforce Development

  • Invest in rural schools with technology, enrichment programs, and teacher support.
  • Offer vocational training, apprenticeships, and STEM programs tailored to rural economies.
  • Support scholarships for students attending historically Black colleges and local colleges serving rural areas.

3. Economic Development & Small Business

  • Provide grants and low-interest loans for Black-owned businesses in rural areas.
  • Encourage cooperatives, local agriculture initiatives, and green energy projects.
  • Invest in broadband, utilities, and infrastructure to enable remote work and e-commerce.

4. Housing & Infrastructure

  • Repair and expand affordable housing stock.
  • Improve roads, bridges, water systems, and broadband connectivity.
  • Support community-led infrastructure projects to retain local autonomy.

5. Food Security & Environmental Sustainability

  • Develop community gardens, co-ops, and farm-to-table initiatives.
  • Protect farmland from industrial exploitation and environmental hazards.
  • Provide nutrition education and access programs for families in need.

6. Political Representation & Community Autonomy

  • Encourage voter registration and civic engagement in rural areas.
  • Support local councils and advisory boards for rural Black communities.
  • Prioritize policies developed in consultation with residents to reflect real needs.
