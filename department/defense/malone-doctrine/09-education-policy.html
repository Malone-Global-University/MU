Overview

Education is the foundational determinant of economic mobility, cultural continuity, and political empowerment within African American communities. My official stance emphasizes high-quality instruction, modern curriculum reform, equal funding, culturally grounded literacy, and expanded pathways to postsecondary success.

Official Position Statements

1. Curriculum Reform

Position: Schools should integrate accurate African and African American history, cultural literacy, and global Black contributions as core‚Äînot elective‚Äîcontent.

Rationale: Students gain identity, confidence, and critical awareness through representation. Curriculum erasure contributes to disengagement and generational misinformation.

Recommended Action: Establish curriculum standards requiring verified, peer-reviewed historical sources and diaspora studies at every grade level.

2. Funding Equity

Position: Per-pupil funding should be equalized across district lines to eliminate disparities created by property-tax‚Äìbased models.

Rationale: African American students disproportionately attend underfunded schools lacking labs, technology, modern materials, and experienced teachers.

Recommended Action: State-level restructuring to ensure guaranteed minimum funding regardless of zip code.

3. Teacher Quality & Representation

Position: Recruit, train, and retain more African American educators, particularly in STEM and advanced placement fields.

Rationale: Teacher diversity improves academic outcomes, reduces bias, and enhances cultural understanding in classrooms.

Recommended Action: Expand teacher scholarship pipelines and loan forgiveness programs for educators serving in majority-Black districts.

4. School Choice & Accountability

Position: Families should have access to high-quality public, charter, virtual, and private educational options‚Äîprovided each institution meets strict accountability standards.

Rationale: Many African American students live in areas with failing schools; choice can provide immediate alternatives while reform occurs.

Recommended Action: Develop transparent reporting metrics and community oversight boards for all publicly funded schools.

5. Technology Access

Position: All students should have equal access to modern technology, digital literacy education, and high-speed internet.

Rationale: The digital divide disproportionately affects Black communities, limiting opportunities in STEM, remote work, and higher education readiness.

Recommended Action: Federal subsidies and local infrastructure investment to guarantee broadband access in underserved communities.

6. Early Childhood Education

Position: Universal access to preschool and early literacy programs is essential.

Rationale: Achievement gaps appear before kindergarten; early interventions dramatically improve long-term outcomes.

Recommended Action: Expand public preschool, literacy screening programs, and parent education workshops.
