Core Principles

Community safety is essential for stable, thriving neighborhoods. African American communities have been historically over-policed while under-resourced, resulting in harm rather than protection. Safety can be achieved through local, preventative, and restorative approaches that empower residents rather than rely solely on traditional law enforcement.

Key Challenges

  • High rates of violence and environmental hazards in under-resourced neighborhoods.
  • Lack of community-led crisis response and support systems.
  • Limited recreational, youth, and mentorship programs to prevent engagement in violence.
  • Insufficient mental health and trauma support for residents exposed to violence.
  • Distrust of policing due to historical abuse and disproportionate criminalization.

Policy Positions

1. Community-Based Safety Programs

  • Fund neighborhood safety initiatives led by local residents.
  • Support block-watch programs with professional training and resources.
  • Implement safety councils to coordinate housing, lighting, and environmental improvements.

2. Violence Prevention & Youth Engagement

  • Invest in mentorship, after-school programs, and recreational spaces.
  • Support conflict resolution and life-skills education for adolescents.
  • Provide targeted job training and entrepreneurship programs for at-risk youth.

3. Restorative Justice & Mediation

  • Expand community-based mediation and restorative justice programs.
  • Offer alternatives to incarceration for non-violent offenses.
  • Provide training for residents in restorative conflict resolution.

4. Trauma & Mental Health Support

  • Establish neighborhood mental health clinics for trauma counseling.
  • Train community health workers to identify and respond to crises.
  • Integrate mental health services with youth, housing, and employment programs.

5. Environmental & Infrastructure Safety

  • Improve street lighting, parks, and public spaces to reduce crime risks.
  • Maintain vacant lots, sidewalks, and housing to prevent accidents and criminal activity.
  • Support community gardens and shared spaces to foster social cohesion.

6. Empower Residents as Stakeholders

  • Provide grants for community organizations to implement local safety initiatives.
  • Include residents in safety planning, budgeting, and policy evaluation.
  • Promote neighborhood councils and advisory boards for ongoing oversight.
