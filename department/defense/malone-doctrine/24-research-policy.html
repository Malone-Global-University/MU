Overview

These research priorities outline the core areas of study necessary to advance understanding, improve public policy, and support the African American community. Each priority reflects emerging challenges, historical patterns, and unmet needs that require scholarly attention and rigorous analysis.

Primary Research Areas

  • Educational Equity: Structural barriers, funding gaps, curriculum bias, and innovative models for student success.
  • Criminal Justice Reform: Sentencing patterns, police accountability, reentry outcomes, and restorative justice methods.
  • Economic Mobility: Wealth-building barriers, business development, labor trends, and strategies for closing racial income gaps.
  • Health Disparities: Chronic disease patterns, mental health access, maternal health outcomes, and environmental impacts.
  • Cultural Influence & Media: Stereotype formation, representation trends, cultural production, and narrative framing in public discourse.

Emerging Research Frontiers

  • Artificial Intelligence & Algorithmic Bias: How automated systems reinforce inequality and ways to prevent discriminatory outcomes.
  • Climate Vulnerability: Disproportionate environmental exposure, resilience strategies, and community-driven climate solutions.
  • Migration & Demographic Change: Internal migration trends, diaspora relations, and the impact of immigration policy on Black communities.
  • Digital Economy Participation: Access to high-tech jobs, remote work opportunities, and digital literacy infrastructure.
  • Family & Social Dynamics: Policy influences on household stability, caregiving structures, and community support networks.

Policy-Oriented Research Goals

  • Data Transparency: Improving government reporting systems on race-related outcomes.
  • Community-Centered Solutions: Incorporating lived experience into research design and policy testing.
  • Evidence-Based Legislation: Translating academic findings into actionable policy frameworks.
  • Longitudinal Impact Studies: Understanding multigenerational effects of social, economic, and legal policies.
  • Global Comparative Research: Studying Black communities worldwide for insights into successful policy models.
