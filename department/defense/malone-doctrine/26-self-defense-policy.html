Core Principles

The right to self-defense, including the right to bear arms under the 2nd Amendment, is a fundamental protection for all Americans. African American communities historically face higher risks of violent crime and systemic neglect. Policies must ensure safe, legal, and responsible access to firearms while promoting education and community safety initiatives.

Key Challenges

  • Disproportionate impact of gun violence in African American communities.
  • Over-criminalization and enforcement disparities affecting law-abiding Black gun owners.
  • Limited access to legal firearms education and training programs.
  • High rates of illegal firearms trafficking and unsafe storage in some areas.
  • Balancing public safety with individual constitutional rights.

Policy Positions

1. Legal Access & Rights Protection

  • Protect the constitutional right to own firearms for self-defense.
  • Ensure African American communities are not unfairly targeted in firearm legislation enforcement.
  • Provide clear guidelines on lawful firearm ownership and self-defense rights.

2. Firearm Education & Safety

  • Fund community-based firearm safety and training programs.
  • Promote safe storage, handling, and responsible usage of firearms.
  • Integrate conflict resolution and de-escalation training alongside self-defense education.

3. Community Safety & Crime Prevention

  • Invest in neighborhood safety initiatives and community policing alternatives.
  • Support programs addressing root causes of violence, including youth development and economic opportunity.
  • Encourage safe self-defense practices without increasing community risk.

4. Legal Support & Advocacy

  • Provide resources for legal aid and representation related to self-defense cases.
  • Advocate for policies that protect responsible gun owners from discriminatory enforcement.
  • Include African American voices in firearm policy discussions and regulatory decisions.
