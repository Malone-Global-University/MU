Core Principles

Food security is essential to health, well-being, and economic stability. African American communities face disproportionate barriers to accessing nutritious, affordable, and culturally appropriate food. Policy must ensure equitable access to healthy diets, support local food systems, and combat food deserts and nutritional inequities.

Key Challenges

  • High prevalence of food deserts in urban and rural African American communities.
  • Limited access to fresh produce, whole foods, and culturally appropriate nutrition options.
  • Disproportionate rates of diet-related diseases, including diabetes, hypertension, and obesity.
  • Economic barriers that limit affordability of nutritious food.
  • Insufficient local agriculture, community gardens, and food co-ops.
  • Limited nutrition education and awareness programs.

Policy Positions

1. Access to Nutritious Food

  • Expand availability of grocery stores, farmers markets, and food co-ops in underserved communities.
  • Support urban and rural agriculture initiatives producing fresh, culturally relevant food.
  • Invest in mobile markets and delivery programs for areas lacking physical stores.

2. Economic Support & Affordability

  • Increase funding for food assistance programs, including SNAP and local nutrition initiatives.
  • Provide subsidies for healthy foods to low-income families.
  • Support incentives for retailers to stock fresh and affordable products in underserved areas.

3. Nutrition Education

  • Implement community-based nutrition and cooking programs for families and youth.
  • Promote culturally relevant dietary education to prevent chronic illnesses.
  • Integrate nutrition education into schools and after-school programs.

4. Community-Led Food Initiatives

  • Fund community gardens, cooperatives, and local farming projects.
  • Support local entrepreneurs creating healthy food products for African American communities.
  • Encourage partnerships between community organizations and municipal food programs.

5. Health & Disease Prevention

  • Integrate nutrition programs with chronic disease prevention initiatives.
  • Promote access to dieticians, nutritionists, and public health guidance.
  • Address systemic factors contributing to poor dietary health, including marketing of unhealthy products.

6. Policy & Advocacy

  • Include African American community representatives in food policy planning and decision-making.
  • Monitor and evaluate program effectiveness to ensure equitable impact.
  • Promote federal, state, and local policies that prioritize food justice and equity.
