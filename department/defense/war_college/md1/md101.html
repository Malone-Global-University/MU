Course Overview

MD101 is a foundational course in the Malone Doctrine War College. It outlines the historical evolution of centralized power—from tribal chaos to modern democracy—through the lens of strategic leadership, control theory, and institutional manipulation. This course teaches how governments form, collapse, and transform, and how to lead or overthrow them with precision.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the six stages of centralized government evolution.
  • Analyze the tools used by rulers across history: religion, bloodlines, fear, ideology, and money.
  • Compare control mechanisms in monarchy, communism, and democracy.
  • Formulate your own system of power based on structural analysis, not imitation.

Curriculum Breakdown

  1. Stage I – Anarchy & Tribal Chiefdom
    How raw power fills the void; might makes right; tribal sovereignty without infrastructure.
  2. Stage II – Despotism
    Strongmen rise as protectors; centralization without formal bureaucracy; cult of personality.
  3. Stage III – Religious Totalitarianism
    Government fused with priesthood; divine justification for rule; Pharaoh models.
  4. Stage IV – Bloodline Monarchy
    Power passed through family; religion relegated to utility; subjects pacified through ritual and guilt.
  5. Stage V – Communist Revolt
    Science and rebellion dethrone the monarch; all are equal, except the party; scarcity weaponized.
  6. Stage VI – Constitutional Democracy
    Rights-based rule of law; fragile but fair; taxes, elections, courts, and collapse risk.
  7. Stage VII – Custom Systems
    Designing your own state model based on national identity, economy, and threat profile.

Assignments & Exercises

  • Essay: Compare two stages and their tools of control. How did the leader maintain obedience?
  • Case Study: Analyze a failed revolution. What did it misunderstand about system evolution?
  • Simulation: Build a hypothetical nation from scratch. Choose which stage to emulate or bypass.
