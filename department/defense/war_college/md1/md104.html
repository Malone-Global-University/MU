Course Overview

MD103 explores the invisible architecture of societal control: the use of belief, money, and narrative to govern families, steer cultures, and consolidate elite influence. Beyond armies and elections lies a deeper reality—one where religion frames obedience, finance dictates options, and soft power replaces swords. This course breaks down those systems and teaches you how to use them—righteously or ruthlessly.

Learning Objectives

  • Decode how religious institutions shape societal values and political compliance.
  • Understand the true function of central banking, credit systems, and generational wealth.
  • Analyze propaganda, education, and media as tools of cultural engineering.
  • Design frameworks for economic and moral authority within a family, movement, or nation.

Curriculum Breakdown

  1. Faith as Framework
    Religion as a soft cage; how belief stabilizes or radicalizes a population.
  2. Financial Leverage & Generational Power
    Debt as control; dynastic wealth-building; how banks dictate behavior at scale.
  3. The Media-Class Mind Trap
    Storytelling as domination; education vs indoctrination; manufacturing the Overton Window.
  4. Family as State: Micronations of Control
    How elite families govern themselves like nations—with laws, secrecy, and loyalty oaths.
  5. Institutions as Immortal Tools
    Foundations, NGOs, and think tanks; how power survives beyond the ruler’s death.
  6. Creating Belief Systems
    Designing doctrines, codes, and currencies that create buy-in from the masses.

Assignments & Exercises

  • Institutional Analysis: Pick a religious or financial institution and map its true power functions.
  • Family Constitution: Draft a 10-rule doctrine for multi-generational family control.
  • Propaganda Design: Create a piece of persuasive media that shifts a population's beliefs or behaviors.
