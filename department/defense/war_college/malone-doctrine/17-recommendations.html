Policy Recommendations

Political Science Department — Malone Global University

Overview

These policy recommendations synthesize the key issues affecting the African American community into practical, implementable solutions. Each recommendation is designed to reduce inequity, improve opportunity, and build sustainable progress across social, economic, and political systems.

Education

  • Universal early childhood education with full public funding for ages 3–5.
  • Equitable school funding models that decouple resources from local property taxes.
  • Afrocentric and culturally competent curriculum integrated into social studies and history programs.
  • Tuition-free community college and expanded vocational apprenticeship pathways.

Criminal Justice & Policing

  • National police accountability standards including body cameras and third-party review boards.
  • Eliminate mandatory minimum sentencing for nonviolent drug offenses.
  • Invest in reentry programs with housing, job placement, and education services.
  • Expand community-based public safety models that reduce reliance on armed response.

Economic Development

  • Targeted investment zones in historically redlined neighborhoods.
  • Federal loan guarantees for Black-owned small businesses.
  • Workforce development initiatives focused on tech, green jobs, and skilled trades.
  • National living wage policy indexed to inflation.

Healthcare

  • Universal healthcare access including mental health and dental services.
  • Maternal health reform targeting racial disparities in prenatal and postnatal outcomes.
  • Community health clinics funded in underserved areas.
  • Environmental health protections against pollutants concentrated in Black communities.

Environmental Justice

  • Clean air and water enforcement in industrial-adjacent Black neighborhoods.
  • Climate resilience infrastructure for heat waves, flooding, and extreme weather.
  • Transition incentives for communities moving from fossil-fuel economies.

Technology & AI Equity

  • Anti-bias audits for algorithms used in hiring, lending, and law enforcement.
  • Public investment in digital literacy from K–12 through adult education.
  • Support for Black participation in STEM fields through scholarships and training.

Housing

  • Strengthened fair housing enforcement and penalties for discriminatory lending.
  • Expansion of affordable housing stock through federal and local partnerships.
  • Down-payment assistance for first-generation homebuyers.
  • Anti-displacement protections in rapidly gentrifying neighborhoods.

Family & Social Policy

  • Paid family leave available to all workers regardless of employer size.
  • Expanded childcare subsidies and sliding-scale affordability programs.
  • Strengthening community support organizations such as youth programs and family counseling.

Resilience Through Culture & Media

  • Increased diversity standards in media production and newsrooms.
  • Support for Black arts and cultural institutions through grants and tax incentives.
  • Community media initiatives including local journalism and digital storytelling.

Labor & Workers’ Rights

  • Strengthen collective bargaining rights and union protections.
  • Increase OSHA enforcement for worker safety across industries.
  • Portable benefits systems for gig and contract workers.
  • Wage transparency laws to reduce discrimination.

Reparations

  • Federal commission to design, cost, and implement reparations programs.
  • Wealth-building initiatives such as land grants, tuition remission, and business capital.
  • Community restitution investments in cities harmed by redlining and displacement.
