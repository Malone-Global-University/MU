MD101: Evolution of Government Systems

Malone Doctrine War College

Course Overview

MD101 is a foundational course in the Malone Doctrine War College. It outlines the historical evolution of centralized power—from tribal chaos to modern democracy—through the lens of strategic leadership, control theory, and institutional manipulation.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand the six stages of centralized government evolution.
  • Analyze tools of control: religion, bloodlines, fear, ideology, money.
  • Compare mechanisms in monarchy, communism, and democracy.
  • Formulate your own system of power based on structure, not imitation.

Curriculum Breakdown

  1. Stage I – Anarchy & Tribal Chiefdom: Might makes right; no state.
  2. Stage II – Despotism: Cult of personality; central power.
  3. Stage III – Religious Totalitarianism: Rule by divine claim.
  4. Stage IV – Bloodline Monarchy: Dynasties and rituals.
  5. Stage V – Communist Revolt: Party control and enforced equality.
  6. Stage VI – Constitutional Democracy: Fragile rights-based order.
  7. Stage VII – Custom Systems: Build your own strategic model.

Assignments & Exercises

  • Essay: Compare stages and obedience mechanisms.
  • Case Study: Analyze a failed revolution.
  • Simulation: Design a nation from scratch.

Required Reading

  • Democracy in America by Tocqueville (Selections)
