Initiate Tier

MD 100: Foundations of Sovereignty, Perception, and Leadership

Home Department Defense War College Curriculum Initiate Tier: MD-100

Course Overview

The Initiate Tier is the entry point for students seeking a foundational understanding of power. Students explore historical governance, core Malone Doctrine concepts, and practical habits of leadership. This tier establishes the intellectual and moral groundwork required to progress to Strategist and Commander Tiers. You will study the architecture of control, the psychology of obedience, and frameworks for operating outside traditional power structures while serving a purpose higher than yourself.

Initiate-Level Outcomes

  • Understand the structure of modern power and its engineering.
  • Identify institutional conditioning and dismantle imposed limitations.
  • Develop a personal doctrine rooted in sovereignty, clarity, and responsibility.
  • Learn fundamentals of narrative warfare, discipline, and leadership formation.
  • Grasp the six-stage model of government evolution and its strategic implications.
  • Internalize Malone values: integrity, transparency, militant truth-to-power, and results.
  • Develop basic organizational, rhetorical, and strategic habits for effective leadership.
  • Begin building a personal sovereignty plan: financial, social, and intellectual.

Curriculum Breakdown

  1. Module I: The Architecture of Control
    Examine how governments, corporations, and culture shape human behavior and compliance.
  2. Module II: Breaking Programming
    Identify mental blocks, social conditioning, and internalized limits placed on you since birth.
  3. Module III: Foundations of Sovereignty
    Build self-discipline, clarity, and personal mission orientation.
  4. Module IV: Strategic Literacy
    Study basic political strategy, information asymmetry, and systems thinking.
  5. Module V: Leadership Formation
    Learn fundamentals of leading small groups, communicating with authority, and projecting stability.

Field Assignments

  • Personal Doctrine Draft: Write your first version of a personal code of conduct and strategic purpose.
  • System Deconstruction: Analyze a major institution (school, workplace, media, religion) and identify its control mechanisms.
  • Leadership Initiation: Perform one act of leadership in real life and document the results.
