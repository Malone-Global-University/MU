Sen 先

Initiative, timing, and control of action in engagement

Home Department Defense Martial Arts M.A.L. Sen

Definition

Sen refers to initiative and timing in martial engagement. It describes the relationship between one’s action and the opponent’s intention, including who acts first, who controls the exchange, and how timing determines dominance in movement.

Concept

Sen is not only about speed or reaction time. It is about controlling the moment when action becomes inevitable. It exists in the space between intention and execution, where initiative can be taken, intercepted, or absorbed.

Functional Role

  • Determines who controls the initiative in engagement
  • Defines timing relationships between attack and response
  • Enables interception of movement before full development
  • Links intention to physical execution through timing control

Related Concepts

  • Ma-ai (Distance & Engagement Range)
  • Tai Sabaki (Body Movement)
  • Ma (Interval)
  • Zanshin (Continuity of Awareness)

Application Across Arts

In aikido, Sen is used to enter or redirect before an attack fully forms. In karate, it determines whether one strikes first, counters, or intercepts. In judo and jujutsu, Sen governs timing of off-balancing and entry into throws. Across all systems, Sen is the control of action through timing.

Common Misunderstanding

Sen is often mistaken for simply “going first.” In reality, initiative can be taken before, during, or after an opponent’s movement depending on timing. Poor understanding of Sen leads to delayed reactions and loss of control over engagement rhythm.
