YONKYO (Fourth Teaching)

Home Directory YONKYO (Fourth Teaching)

System Classification

HIJI WAZA — Control Techniques (4th Teaching)

Alternative Name

Yonkyo Ude Osae (Nerve Pressure Arm Control / Fourth Principle Control)

Core Principle

YONKYO is the principle of structural control through targeted pressure on nerve pathways of the forearm, combined with alignment control of the arm and body.

It differs from prior teachings by shifting emphasis from: - mechanical leverage (IKKYO) - rotational torque (NIKKYO / SANKYO)

to:

neurological disruption under structural restraint

Functional Purpose

  • Apply controlled pain-based compliance pressure
  • Disrupt grip strength and arm function
  • Maintain structural control while inducing neurological response
  • Force immobilization or submission-level compliance

Technical Concept

YONKYO works by applying precise pressure along the radial nerve line of the forearm while maintaining structural control of the arm and posture.

It is not a “squeeze” technique—it is a targeted pressure system layered onto controlled immobilization.

Key idea: - Structure holds the body - Pressure overrides the nervous system

Execution Overview

1. Entry

  • Establish control of opponent’s wrist and forearm
  • Position body off-line to prevent counterforce

2. Structural Lock

  • Stabilize arm against controlled frame (not loose grip)
  • Align opponent’s forearm into controlled exposure angle

3. Nerve Pressure Application

  • Apply focused pressure along forearm nerve line
  • Maintain consistent, controlled compression

4. Stabilization Phase

  • Prevent opponent rotation or withdrawal
  • Maintain structural immobilization while pressure continues

5. Resolution

  • Option A: Transition into pin control
  • Option B: Force compliance and disengagement
  • Option C: Flow into follow-up technique under control

Key Mechanical Details

  • Pressure must be precise, not wide or scattered
  • Structural immobilization is required before pressure is effective
  • Wrist and elbow alignment determine effectiveness
  • Body positioning prevents escape or reversal
  • Control must be stable before increasing intensity

Common Failure Points

  • Applying pressure without structural control
  • Using force instead of targeted nerve alignment
  • Allowing opponent to rotate forearm out of line
  • Poor body positioning leading to escape or counter
  • Losing stabilization while increasing pressure

Variations

  • Standing compliance control version
  • Kneeling pin-based version
  • Fast entry reactive version
  • Resistant opponent escalation version
  • Transition-to-pin focused version

Related Techniques

Within HIJI WAZA

  • IKKYO (First Teaching — structural elbow control foundation)
  • NIKKYO (Second Teaching — rotational compression system)
  • SANKYO (Third Teaching — extended spiral control system)
  • GOKYO (Fifth Teaching — dynamic resistance control system)
  • ROKKYO (Sixth Teaching — advanced structural integration system)

Within NAGE WAZA

  • KOTEGAESHI (wrist reversal throw system)
  • SHIHONAGE (directional structural throw system)
  • IRIMINAGE (entry collapse throw system)

Strategic Notes (Pressure Reality)

Under resistance:

  • YONKYO is highly sensitive to angle and precision
  • Improper alignment reduces effectiveness significantly
  • Strong opponents will attempt rotation or wrist withdrawal
  • It is most effective after structure has already been broken

YONKYO is a finishing control system, not an entry technique.

Concept Summary

YONKYO is the transition from mechanical dominance to neurological control within the HIJI WAZA system.

It teaches:

“When structure holds the body, pressure controls the mind.”
