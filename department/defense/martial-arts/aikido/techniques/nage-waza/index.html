NAGE WAZA — Master Index (Throwing Systems)

Home Directory NAGE WAZA — Master Index (Throwing Systems)

Overview

NAGE WAZA is the dynamic resolution layer of Malone Ryu, governing how controlled structure is converted into throws, directional displacement, and controlled takedowns.

Where HIJI WAZA focuses on control and containment, NAGE WAZA focuses on:

redirection, off-balancing, and structural collapse in motion

NAGE WAZA is not “throw techniques” in isolation.

It is a movement consequence system that emerges when structure is properly controlled.

System Philosophy

All throws in NAGE WAZA are based on one core principle:

You do not throw the opponent.

You remove their ability to remain upright.

This means every technique is built from:

  • Balance disruption (kuzushi)
  • Structural misalignment
  • Timing exploitation
  • Centerline removal

System Structure

NAGE WAZA is composed of ten core throwing systems:

1. IRIMINAGE — Entry Throw

Entering Structural Collapse

  • Direct entry into opponent’s space
  • Removes posture through forward penetration
  • Converts resistance into directional collapse

“Enter the space where balance cannot exist.”

2. SOKUMEN IRIMINAGE — Side Entry Throw

Lateral Structural Breakdown

  • Entry from angular displacement
  • Breaks alignment from the side
  • Forces spiral collapse through off-axis movement

“Balance fails when the center cannot align.”

3. KAITENAGE — Rotation Throw

Rotational Structural Override

  • Uses circular motion to destabilize base
  • Converts resistance into rotational fall
  • Emphasizes continuous motion over impact

“Rotation replaces resistance.”

4. SHIHONAGE — Four Direction Throw

Multi-Directional Control System

  • Controls all directional escape paths
  • Locks structure through unified directional constraint
  • Forces collapse regardless of exit attempt

“There is no direction where balance survives.”

5. USHIRONAGE — Backward Throw

Rear Structural Reversal

  • Converts forward pressure into backward collapse
  • Exploits overcommitment
  • Redirects momentum into reverse fall

“Commitment becomes collapse.”

6. KOKYONAGE — Breath Throw

Timing-Based Structural Disruption

  • Uses timing and rhythm rather than force
  • Breaks structure through breath and momentum mismatch
  • Highly adaptive and situational

“Timing is stronger than force.”

7. KOTEGAESHI — Wrist Reversal Throw

Rotational Joint Inversion

  • Uses wrist rotation to break structure
  • Converts grip into destabilization
  • Forces spiral collapse through reversal mechanics

“Control the hand, and the body follows.”

8. TENSHINAGE — Heaven and Earth Throw

Vertical Axis Displacement

  • Splits structure between upward and downward forces
  • Creates vertical imbalance
  • Forces collapse through axis separation

“When heaven and earth separate, structure falls.”

9. KUBISHIME — Neck Control Throw

Cervical Structural Override

  • Direct control of head and neck alignment
  • Removes balance through head positioning
  • High-risk, high-control system

“The body follows the head.”

10. JUJINAGE — Cross Throw

Intersecting Structural Collapse

  • Uses crossing arm structures (+ shape logic)
  • Locks opponent in intersecting force lines
  • Removes escape vectors through structural entanglement

“Crossed structure cannot move freely.”

11. OTOSHINAGE (AGE UKI) — Drop Throw

Gravity-Based Structural Collapse

  • Removes support structure entirely
  • Uses downward release rather than projection
  • Emphasizes drop timing over force

“When structure is removed, gravity decides.”

System Relationships

NAGE WAZA is directly dependent on HIJI WAZA:

  • HIJI WAZA = structure creation and control
  • NAGE WAZA = structure failure and resolution

Without HIJI WAZA:

Throws become unreliable or forced.

With HIJI WAZA:

Throws become inevitable outcomes.

Structural Principles of All NAGE WAZA

All techniques in this system rely on:

  • Kuzushi (balance breaking)
  • Maai (distance control)
  • Timing displacement
  • Structural misalignment
  • Centerline disruption

These are not optional—they are the foundation of all throws.

Pressure Reality Notes

Under resistance:

  • Throws rarely appear as clean techniques
  • Most become transitions of imbalance
  • Success depends on prior structural control
  • Timing becomes more important than form

NAGE WAZA is not “executed.”

It is completed when structure fails naturally.

Training Philosophy

NAGE WAZA is trained through:

  • Flow drills from HIJI WAZA control states
  • Resistance-based kuzushi testing
  • Entry repetition under pressure
  • Transition chaining (control → throw → reset)
  • Sparring integration with incomplete technique states

Core Principle Statement

NAGE WAZA is the study of how structure fails under controlled conditions.

System Summary

NAGE WAZA is the resolution layer of Malone Ryu combat structure, converting control into motion outcomes.

It teaches:

  • Control creates opportunity
  • Timing completes technique
  • Structure determines outcome
  • Motion is a consequence, not an action
