Structural Breakdown

This page decomposes aikido techniques into functional stages to expose mechanical intent and failure points.

1. Entry Phase

The entry establishes positioning relative to the opponent’s structure. This includes angle creation, hand positioning, and timing interception.

2. Control Phase

Control is achieved through balance disruption (kuzushi), joint alignment manipulation, and positional dominance.

3. Resolution Phase

Resolution includes throw, pin, or disengagement. In live resistance environments, this phase is the most failure-prone.

Key Insight

Aikido techniques are not singular movements—they are chained systems dependent on uninterrupted transitions between phases.

Three Phase Model

Entry

  • Distance bridging
  • Timing alignment

Control

  • Balance disruption (kuzushi)
  • Structural manipulation

Resolution

  • Throw or immobilization
  • Exit positioning
