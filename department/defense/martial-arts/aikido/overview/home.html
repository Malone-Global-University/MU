Aikido Overview

Aikido is studied here not as a tradition to be preserved, but as a mechanical system of motion, timing, and control. Within Malone Ryu, aikido is analyzed for what it does under cooperative, semi-resistant, and fully resistant conditions. Aikido is analyzed here as a system of mechanical principles focused on timing, balance disruption, and directional redirection of force. System Definition

Aikido is a movement-based control system that prioritizes redirection of force, structural off-balancing, and joint control through timing rather than impact.

Core Evaluation Lens

  • What works under compliant input
  • What survives partial resistance
  • What fails under active striking or grappling pressure

Primary Focus

MGU treats aikido as a study of structure, kuzushi (balance breaking), and motion economy—not as a complete combat system in isolation.
