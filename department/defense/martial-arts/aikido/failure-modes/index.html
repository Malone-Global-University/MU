Failure Modes

This section identifies where aikido techniques degrade under resistance and why.

Timing Failure

When entry timing is late or early, kuzushi does not establish, and the technique collapses into grappling or clinch survival.

Non-Compliant Opponent

Aikido assumes partial cooperation. When the opponent actively resists structure rather than following it, joint locks and redirections lose efficiency.

Structural Assumption Breakdown

Many techniques assume linear force or predictable attacks. Real resistance introduces angle variation, chaining strikes, and base recovery.

Conclusion

Failure is not random—it is systematic and repeatable under specific stress conditions.

Core Breakdown Points

1. Cooperative Dependency

Technique assumes compliant partner behavior.

2. Timing Collapse

Entry fails under unpredictability or speed variance.

3. Structural Overreach

Technique requires ideal alignment that does not exist under pressure.

4. Control Loss

Kuzushi fails and opponent re-stabilizes base structure.
