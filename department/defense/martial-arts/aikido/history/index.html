History

Aikido developed in the early 20th century as a synthesis of older Japanese jujutsu systems filtered through a philosophical reinterpretation of conflict resolution.

Development Context

Its evolution occurred during a period of cultural transformation in Japan, where martial systems were transitioning from battlefield utility to educational and philosophical practice.

Institutional Influence

Modern aikido was shaped by institutional teaching structures that emphasized form preservation, harmony principles, and controlled training environments.

MGU Position

History is treated as context, not authority. Functional validity is separate from lineage.

Contextual Origin

Aikido evolved from classical Japanese jujutsu systems and was formalized in the early 20th century.

MGU Interpretation

Historical context is used only as environmental data, not authority. Techniques are evaluated independently of lineage claims.
