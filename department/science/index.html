Science Department

The Science Department builds disciplined inquiry into the natural world through observation, experimentation, and quantitative analysis. Students develop the ability to model systems, test hypotheses, and interpret empirical data across biological, chemical, and physical domains. Programs emphasize scientific reasoning, methodological rigor, and real world application, preparing learners to engage complex problems with precision and evidence based thinking.

Science Programs

  • Biology

    Focused on living systems, the Biology program explores cellular processes, genetics, ecology, and human physiology. Students analyze how organisms function, adapt, and interact within environments, building a foundation for health sciences, research, and environmental study.

  • Chemistry

    The Chemistry program examines the composition, structure, and transformation of matter. Learners develop proficiency in chemical reactions, laboratory techniques, and quantitative analysis, enabling application in industry, medicine, and materials science.

  • Earth Science

    The Earth Science program examines the structure, systems, and processes of the planet. Students study geology, climate, oceans, and environmental dynamics to understand how Earth functions as an integrated system. Emphasis is placed on resource management, environmental change, and data driven analysis, preparing learners to address challenges related to sustainability, natural hazards, and planetary stewardship.

  • Physics

    Centered on the fundamental laws governing energy and motion, the Physics program develops mathematical modeling and problem solving skills. Students study mechanics, electricity, and wave behavior to understand how physical systems operate at multiple scales.

  • Space

    The Space program introduces astronomy and astrophysics, exploring planetary systems, stars, and the structure of the universe. Students engage with observational methods and scientific theories to understand cosmic phenomena and humanity’s expanding role beyond Earth.
