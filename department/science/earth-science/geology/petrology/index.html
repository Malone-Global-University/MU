Petrology

Petrology examines rocks as records of Earth’s internal and surface processes. Students study igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks to understand how materials form, change, melt, crystallize, compact, deform, and preserve evidence of past environments and tectonic conditions.

  • Igneous Petrology

    Study rocks formed from molten material, including magma generation, cooling, crystallization, volcanic activity, and intrusive rock formation.

  • Sedimentary Petrology

    Examine rocks formed from sediments through weathering, transport, deposition, compaction, cementation, and environmental change.

  • Metamorphic Petrology

    Explore how existing rocks transform under heat, pressure, fluids, and tectonic stress without fully melting.
