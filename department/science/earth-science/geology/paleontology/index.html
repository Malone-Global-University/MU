Paleontology

Paleontology studies ancient life through fossils, rock context, and geological time. Students examine preserved organisms, traces, extinction patterns, evolutionary change, and ancient environments to understand how life on Earth developed, diversified, adapted, and disappeared across deep history.

  • Fossil Identification

    Learn how fossils are classified and interpreted through morphology, preservation, anatomy, rock context, and comparison with known fossil groups.

  • Evolutionary History

    Study major patterns in the history of life, including diversification, adaptation, mass extinction, recovery, and long-term biological change.

  • Paleoecology

    Examine ancient ecosystems by interpreting fossils, sediments, environmental clues, food relationships, habitats, and organism interactions from the geological record.
