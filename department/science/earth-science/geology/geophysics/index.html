Geophysics

Geophysics applies physics and quantitative measurement to the study of Earth’s interior, surface, and surrounding fields. Students examine seismic waves, gravity, magnetism, heat flow, and electrical properties to understand planetary structure, tectonic activity, resource distribution, and deep Earth processes that cannot be observed directly.

  • Seismology

    Study earthquakes and seismic waves to understand fault movement, wave propagation, subsurface structure, and the internal layering of the Earth.

  • Gravity and Magnetism

    Examine variations in Earth’s gravitational and magnetic fields to interpret crustal structure, buried features, mineral resources, and planetary dynamics.

  • Earth Interior

    Explore the structure and behavior of Earth’s crust, mantle, outer core, and inner core through geophysical evidence and physical models.
