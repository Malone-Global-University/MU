Geology

Geology is the study of the Earth’s physical structure, material composition, and long-term development through geologic time. Students examine the processes that shape the planet’s crust, form natural resources, produce landforms, and drive tectonic and volcanic activity across Earth’s surface.

  • Mineralogy

    Study the chemical composition, crystal structure, classification, and physical properties of minerals as the foundational materials of Earth’s crust.

  • Petrology

    Examine the origin, composition, and transformation of igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks within geological systems.

  • Structural Geology

    Analyze deformation of Earth’s crust including folds, faults, fractures, and the tectonic forces that shape geological structures.

  • Stratigraphy

    Explore rock layering, sediment deposition, and geologic sequencing to reconstruct Earth’s historical development through layered strata.

  • Geomorphology

    Study the formation and evolution of landforms through erosion, weathering, tectonics, and surface processes acting over time.

  • Geophysics

    Apply physics to geological systems in order to study Earth’s interior structure, seismic behavior, gravity fields, and magnetic properties.

  • Paleontology

    Investigate ancient life through fossils and geological context to understand the evolutionary and environmental history of Earth.
