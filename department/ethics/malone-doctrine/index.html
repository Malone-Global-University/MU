Malone Doctrine

The ethical foundation for principled conduct, institutional responsibility, public service, and commercial integrity.

Overview

The Malone Doctrine is the governing ethical framework of Malone Global University and Malone-aligned organizations, authored by Joseph Malone. It establishes a systematic body of principles governing moral conduct across every sphere of human interaction. Rather than functioning as a textbook or political manifesto, the Doctrine serves as a lasting ethical code intended to guide responsible conduct, institutional leadership, civic participation, and commercial activity.

Organized into four progressively expanding volumes, the Doctrine begins with the ethical obligations individuals owe one another before extending those principles outward to institutions, government, and economic life. Together, the volumes provide a coherent framework for evaluating conduct, resolving ethical questions, and promoting principled decision-making across Malone-aligned organizations and initiatives.

Volumes

  • Volume I — Conduct Toward Individuals

    Establishes the ethical principles governing personal character, responsibility, discipline, family life, interpersonal relationships, and community engagement. This volume forms the moral foundation upon which all later volumes depend.

  • Volume II — Conduct Toward Institutions and Communities

    Examines the ethical responsibilities individuals and organizations owe to families, voluntary associations, educational institutions, religious organizations, civic bodies, and the broader community. The volume defines principled institutional conduct and community stewardship.

  • Volume III — Conduct Toward Government

    Defines ethical principles governing the relationship between citizens, public institutions, and civil authority. Topics include public responsibility, justice, accountability, lawful governance, civic participation, and the moral exercise of political power.

  • Volume IV — Conduct Within the Economy and Commercial Markets

    Establishes ethical standards for commerce, production, trade, entrepreneurship, employment, finance, and voluntary exchange. The volume addresses the moral obligations of buyers, sellers, employers, workers, investors, and institutions participating in economic life.
