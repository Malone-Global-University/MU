Ethics Department

The study of moral philosophy, ethical leadership, and principled decision-making.

Overview

The Ethics Department serves as the moral and philosophical foundation of Malone Global University. Its purpose is to examine the principles that govern responsible conduct, moral reasoning, leadership, institutional integrity, and the ethical obligations individuals owe to one another and to society.

Ethical instruction within the department extends beyond abstract philosophy by emphasizing practical application across personal conduct, community life, public institutions, government, and commercial activity. The department seeks to cultivate disciplined judgment, principled leadership, and consistent moral reasoning capable of guiding decision-making in complex environments.

Department Divisions

  • Malone Doctrine

    The official ethical and philosophical doctrine of Joseph Malone. Organized into four volumes, the Malone Doctrine establishes standards of moral conduct governing relationships between individuals, institutions and communities, government, and the economy. It serves as the ethical foundation for Malone-aligned organizations and initiatives.

  • Moral Philosophy

    A study of ethical theories, systems of moral reasoning, virtue, duty, justice, and the philosophical foundations of right and wrong. Coursework examines both historical and contemporary approaches to ethics while developing rigorous analytical thinking.

  • Applied Ethics

    An examination of ethical decision-making in professional, civic, technological, legal, scientific, and organizational settings. Students learn to evaluate difficult situations using consistent moral principles and structured ethical analysis.

  • Leadership Ethics

    The study of ethical leadership, stewardship, accountability, organizational culture, and responsible exercise of authority. Instruction emphasizes the relationship between character, trust, and effective leadership across public and private institutions.
