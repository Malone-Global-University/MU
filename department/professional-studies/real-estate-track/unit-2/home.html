Unit 2: Foundations of Real Estate

Understand property types, ownership rights, real estate as an asset class, and the key metrics used to evaluate deals. This unit builds the conceptual foundation students need before moving deeper into markets, valuation, financing, and operations.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Real Estate Investing Track Unit 2: Foundations of Real Estate

Unit Overview

Before investors can evaluate a deal well, they need to understand what real estate actually is. This means learning how property differs across asset types, how legal ownership works, and why location, control, income potential, and marketability shape investment outcomes.

This unit introduces real estate as both a physical asset and a bundle of legal and economic rights. Students will study the basic forms of ownership, compare common property categories, and begin using the introductory metrics investors rely on when screening opportunities. The goal is to build fluency with the language and structure of real estate before moving into deeper market and valuation analysis.

What You’ll Learn

  • Identify the major property types and explain how their risk and income characteristics differ
  • Describe common ownership forms and the bundle of rights associated with real estate
  • Explain why real estate functions differently from stocks, bonds, and other financial assets
  • Interpret basic screening metrics such as price per square foot, price per unit, gross rent multiplier, and cap rate
  • Recognize why local market conditions and submarket dynamics matter in property investing

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 2.1: Real Estate as an Asset Class

    Learn how real estate combines income, appreciation potential, operating risk, and illiquidity, and how it differs from stocks and bonds.

  • Lesson 2.2: Property Types

    Compare residential, multifamily, retail, office, industrial, land, and mixed-use assets, including how demand and risk differ across categories.

  • Lesson 2.3: Ownership Structures

    Study fee simple, leasehold, condominium, cooperative, and other basic ownership forms used in real estate transactions and investing.

  • Lesson 2.4: Rights in Real Estate

    Understand the bundle of rights, easements, encumbrances, and the legal interests that shape property control and value.

  • Lesson 2.5: Core Real Estate Metrics

    Build familiarity with introductory deal metrics such as price per square foot, price per unit, gross rent multiplier, and cap rate.

  • Lesson 2.6: Why Real Estate Markets Are Local

    Examine how neighborhood conditions, submarket dynamics, and local demand drivers shape property performance more than national averages alone.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to distinguish major asset types, interpret common ownership arrangements, and use simple market and pricing metrics to compare properties at an introductory level. These ideas provide the framework for later units on markets, valuation, debt, and underwriting.

  • Real Estate Investing Glossary

    Review core terms such as fee simple, leasehold, cap rate, gross rent multiplier, bundle of rights, easement, and encumbrance.

  • Templates & Tools

    Use simple comparison worksheets and screening tools to practice classifying property types and comparing basic deal metrics.
