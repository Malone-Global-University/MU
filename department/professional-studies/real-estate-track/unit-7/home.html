Unit 7: Mortgage & Debt Structuring

Learn how debt financing shapes real estate investing. This unit introduces leverage, loan structures, amortization, debt service metrics, interest rate risk, refinancing decisions, and the ways debt can amplify both returns and losses.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Real Estate Investing Track Unit 7: Mortgage & Debt Structuring

Unit Overview

Most real estate is not bought entirely with cash. Debt is a central part of how properties are acquired, financed, and managed over time. Mortgage structure affects cash flow, risk, flexibility, and investor return, which means understanding debt is essential to understanding real estate itself.

This unit teaches students how leverage works economically and contractually. Students study major loan types, lender categories, amortization mechanics, debt service obligations, DSCR analysis, interest rate exposure, and the strategic role of refinancing and recapitalization in asset ownership.

What You’ll Learn

  • Why leverage is widely used in real estate investing
  • How loan types and lender structures differ across the market
  • How mortgage amortization changes loan balances and payment composition over time
  • How debt service obligations are measured and evaluated through DSCR
  • How fixed-rate and floating-rate loans create different forms of interest rate risk
  • How refinancing and recapitalization alter capital structure and investor outcomes
  • How leverage can magnify both upside returns and downside losses

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 7.1: Why Real Estate Uses Leverage

    Explore why investors use borrowed capital in real estate and how leverage affects purchasing power, returns, and risk exposure.

  • Lesson 7.2: Loan Types and Lender Structures

    Learn the differences among common mortgage products and the roles of banks, credit unions, agencies, debt funds, and other lenders.

  • Lesson 7.3: Mortgage Amortization

    Understand how loan payments are divided between principal and interest and how amortization shapes outstanding balance over time.

  • Lesson 7.4: Debt Service and DSCR

    Study how lenders and investors evaluate debt burden using debt service, debt service coverage ratio, and cash flow support for borrowing.

  • Lesson 7.5: Fixed vs Floating Rate Risk

    Compare the stability of fixed-rate debt with the volatility and exposure that come with floating-rate borrowing.

  • Lesson 7.6: Refinancing and Recapitalization

    Examine how owners restructure debt or replace capital to improve terms, extract equity, solve distress, or reposition an investment.

  • Lesson 7.7: How Leverage Changes Investment Outcomes

    Analyze how debt amplifies returns, increases fragility, changes downside exposure, and alters the range of possible investment results.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to explain how real estate debt works, interpret basic lending metrics, compare loan structures, and understand how borrowing changes both cash flow performance and investment risk.

  • Templates & Tools

    Use loan comparison sheets, amortization tools, DSCR calculators, and refinancing scenario templates to evaluate debt structure decisions.

  • Glossary Support

    Review key terms such as leverage, amortization, debt service, DSCR, fixed-rate loan, floating-rate loan, refinancing, and recapitalization.
