Unit 7: Mortgage & Debt Structuring

Understand loan types, amortization, DTI, refinancing basics, and how leverage changes risk and outcomes. This unit explains how debt works in real estate and why financing structure can improve returns or create fragility depending on how it is used.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Real Estate Investing Track Unit 7: Mortgage & Debt Structuring

Unit Overview

Debt is one of the most powerful tools in real estate investing. It allows investors to control larger assets with less equity, but it also introduces fixed obligations, refinancing exposure, and the possibility that small operating problems become serious financial problems. Financing is not just about getting approved for a loan. It is about choosing a structure that matches the investment strategy and the risk tolerance of the investor.

This unit introduces the basic mechanics of mortgage borrowing, including loan types, amortization, borrower qualification, and refinancing. Students will learn how debt service affects cash flow, how leverage amplifies both gains and losses, and why prudent investors analyze financing as carefully as they analyze the property itself.

What You’ll Learn

  • Explain why investors use debt and how leverage changes the economics of a deal
  • Compare common loan structures such as fixed-rate, adjustable-rate, and interest-only loans
  • Interpret amortization and understand how principal and interest change over time
  • Recognize the role of qualification metrics such as DTI, LTV, and DSCR
  • Describe how refinancing can improve, preserve, or damage investment outcomes depending on market conditions

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 7.1: Why Real Estate Uses Debt

    Learn why investors borrow against real estate, how debt increases capital efficiency, and why leverage also increases downside risk.

  • Lesson 7.2: Loan Types

    Study fixed-rate, adjustable-rate, and interest-only loans, and compare how different structures affect payment stability and risk.

  • Lesson 7.3: Mortgage Amortization

    Understand how mortgage payments are divided between principal and interest and how amortization affects cash flow and equity buildup.

  • Lesson 7.4: Debt Service and Borrower Qualification

    Examine how lenders use DTI, LTV, DSCR, and related measures to evaluate risk and determine loan terms.

  • Lesson 7.5: Refinancing Strategy

    Learn how refinancing can reduce borrowing costs, change loan structure, unlock equity, or create new risk depending on market timing.

  • Lesson 7.6: Leverage Sensitivity

    Study how debt changes returns under different rent, vacancy, interest rate, and exit scenarios, and why leverage increases outcome volatility.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to compare common financing structures, interpret basic lending metrics, and explain how mortgage terms influence cash flow, risk, and equity returns. These concepts prepare students for later work in underwriting, advanced debt analysis, and long-term portfolio strategy.

  • Templates & Tools

    Use amortization worksheets, debt comparison tools, and leverage sensitivity models to practice evaluating mortgage structures.

  • Glossary Support

    Review terms such as amortization, debt service, DTI, DSCR, LTV, fixed-rate loan, adjustable-rate mortgage, and cash-out refinance.
