Unit 6: The Property Acquisition Process

Walk through the transaction from pre-approval to closing: offers, negotiation, contingencies, inspections, and execution. This unit shows how disciplined investors move from interest in a property to a completed acquisition without losing control of risk.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Real Estate Investing Track Unit 6: The Property Acquisition Process

Unit Overview

A real estate acquisition is not a single decision. It is a sequence of decisions made under time pressure, incomplete information, and competing incentives. Investors must prepare financing, define criteria, source opportunities, structure offers, negotiate terms, investigate risk, and close only when the facts support the original thesis.

This unit walks through the acquisition process step by step. Students will learn how transactions move from preparation to execution, how contingencies and due diligence protect the buyer, and why strong process discipline matters as much as finding an attractive deal. The goal is to help students understand how acquisitions are actually executed in practice rather than only in theory.

What You’ll Learn

  • Explain how financing readiness and investment criteria shape acquisition discipline
  • Identify common ways investors source and screen potential opportunities
  • Interpret the major components of an offer, including price, earnest money, and contingencies
  • Describe how negotiation affects both economics and deal structure
  • Recognize the role of inspections, due diligence, and closing procedures in reducing transaction risk

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 6.1: Preparing to Acquire Property

    Learn why financing readiness, written criteria, and market focus matter before beginning a serious property search.

  • Lesson 6.2: Property Search and Deal Sourcing

    Study how investors find opportunities through brokers, listings, relationships, direct outreach, and disciplined market scanning.

  • Lesson 6.3: Offer Structure

    Understand how purchase price, earnest money, contingencies, timing, and other terms shape the risk and competitiveness of an offer.

  • Lesson 6.4: Negotiation Strategy

    Examine how buyers negotiate price, timelines, repairs, credits, and other terms to improve both economics and execution certainty.

  • Lesson 6.5: Due Diligence

    Review inspections, document review, financial verification, and practical investigation steps used to confirm or challenge the investment thesis.

  • Lesson 6.6: Closing Process

    Learn how transactions move through escrow, final review, document execution, and settlement to complete a legally effective closing.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to map the basic flow of a real estate acquisition, identify the major decision points, and explain how offer structure, negotiation, and due diligence affect transaction risk. These concepts prepare students for later units on financing, operations, and investment analysis.

  • Templates & Tools

    Use acquisition checklists, due diligence worksheets, and offer review tools to practice transaction planning and execution.

  • Glossary Support

    Review terms such as earnest money, contingency, due diligence, escrow, closing, inspection, and purchase agreement.
