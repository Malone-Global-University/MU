Unit 13: Commercial Real Estate Fundamentals

Learn how commercial assets differ: lease structures (including NNN concepts), tenant improvement allowances, and commercial underwriting principles. This unit introduces the operating, leasing, and risk characteristics that distinguish commercial real estate from smaller residential investing.

Unit Overview

Commercial real estate differs from residential investing in important ways. Lease structures are more varied, tenants often carry different operating responsibilities, income can be more dependent on tenant credit quality, and underwriting usually requires a closer look at rent rolls, lease terms, rollover schedules, and property-specific operating assumptions. Commercial assets may offer stronger income characteristics, but they also introduce more structural complexity.

This unit introduces the foundational logic of commercial real estate. Students will learn how lease structure affects risk allocation, why tenant improvement allowances matter, how tenant credit influences income stability, and how commercial underwriting differs from simpler property analysis. The goal is to build a practical understanding of how commercial deals are evaluated and managed.

What You’ll Learn

  • Explain how commercial real estate differs from residential property in leasing, operations, and underwriting
  • Compare common commercial lease structures such as gross, modified gross, and triple net leases
  • Recognize why tenant credit quality and lease duration influence income stability and valuation
  • Understand the purpose of tenant improvement allowances and how they affect leasing economics
  • Describe the basic principles investors use when underwriting commercial assets

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 13.1: How Commercial Real Estate Differs

    Learn how commercial property differs from residential investing in lease terms, tenant profiles, operating structure, and underwriting depth.

  • Lesson 13.2: Commercial Lease Structures

    Study gross, modified gross, and triple net leases, and examine how expenses and responsibilities are allocated between landlord and tenant.

  • Lesson 13.3: Tenant Credit Risk

    Understand why tenant financial strength, business durability, and lease enforceability matter for commercial income stability.

  • Lesson 13.4: Tenant Improvement Allowances

    Examine how tenant improvement allowances are used in leasing, how they affect economics, and why they matter in underwriting.

  • Lesson 13.5: Commercial Underwriting

    Learn the core components of commercial underwriting, including rent rolls, lease terms, expense analysis, rollover risk, and market assumptions.

  • Lesson 13.6: Lease Duration and Value Stability

    Study how lease term, rollover timing, and tenant retention influence income durability, valuation, and long-term asset risk.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to explain how commercial lease structure affects property economics, recognize the importance of tenant credit and rollover risk, and describe the main differences between residential and commercial underwriting. These concepts prepare students for development analysis, portfolio construction, and more advanced investment decision-making.

  • Templates & Tools

    Use lease comparison worksheets, rent roll review templates, and basic underwriting tools to practice analyzing commercial property structure.

  • Glossary Support

    Review terms such as gross lease, modified gross lease, triple net lease, tenant improvement allowance, rent roll, and rollover risk.
