Unit 1: Financial & Economic Foundations

Build core investing math and property-level financial literacy. This unit introduces time value of money, inflation, leverage, and the logic of how cash flow drives investment decision-making.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Real Estate Investing Track Unit 1: Financial & Economic Foundations

Unit Overview

Real estate investing begins with financial reasoning. Before students can evaluate a property, compare financing structures, or estimate long-term returns, they need to understand how money changes over time, how inflation affects purchasing power, and how leverage alters both risk and reward.

This unit builds that foundation. The goal is not abstract math for its own sake, but practical literacy: understanding the financial logic that underlies valuation, underwriting, and disciplined investment decisions.

What You’ll Learn

  • Why money today is worth more than money in the future
  • How inflation changes real returns and purchasing power
  • How leverage magnifies both gains and losses
  • How basic cash flow logic supports investment analysis
  • How investors make decisions under uncertainty using conservative reasoning

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 1.1: Time Value of Money

    Learn why future cash flows must be discounted and why present capital has greater economic utility than future capital.

  • Lesson 1.2: Interest, Compounding, and Inflation

    Understand nominal vs real returns, how compound growth works, and how inflation alters long-term investing outcomes.

  • Lesson 1.3: Cash Flow vs Appreciation

    Examine the difference between income-producing return and value growth, and why disciplined investors separate the two.

  • Lesson 1.4: Introduction to Leverage

    Study how debt changes investment outcomes by amplifying both upside potential and downside risk.

  • Lesson 1.5: Basic Investment Math

    Build fluency with percentage returns, break-even logic, and the arithmetic needed for introductory deal evaluation.

  • Lesson 1.6: Decision Making Under Uncertainty

    Learn why disciplined investors use scenario thinking, margin of safety, and conservative assumptions when outcomes are unknown.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to interpret simple return scenarios, recognize the effect of leverage on outcomes, and understand why discounted reasoning matters in real estate.

  • Templates & Tools

    Use worksheets and lightweight models to practice return calculations, leverage effects, and foundational cash flow logic.

  • Glossary Support

    Review key terms such as present value, future value, inflation, leverage, and cash flow.
