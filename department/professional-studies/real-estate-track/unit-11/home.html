Unit 11: Investment Analysis

Evaluate deals with NOI, cap rate, cash-on-cash return, IRR, required return frameworks, and sensitivity testing. This unit brings together the financial and strategic logic of the track into practical deal analysis.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Real Estate Investing Track Unit 11: Investment Analysis

Unit Overview

Real estate investing becomes actionable when concepts are translated into structured analysis. Investors need a way to compare opportunities, estimate returns, test assumptions, and decide whether a deal is attractive enough to justify the risks involved. That requires more than intuition. It requires a disciplined framework for evaluating income, price, financing, and uncertainty together.

This unit introduces the core metrics and reasoning tools used in real estate underwriting. Students will learn how to analyze NOI, cap rate, cash-on-cash return, and introductory IRR, and how to compare projected returns against a required return standard. The unit also emphasizes sensitivity testing so that students understand not only what a deal could earn, but how easily those outcomes can change.

What You’ll Learn

  • Explain how NOI isolates property-level operating performance before debt service
  • Interpret cap rate and cash-on-cash return as different ways of evaluating deal economics
  • Understand the basic logic of IRR and when multi-year return analysis matters
  • Compare projected returns against a required return framework rather than relying on raw output alone
  • Recognize how sensitivity testing reveals the fragility or durability of an investment thesis

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 11.1: Net Operating Income (NOI)

    Learn how NOI measures property profitability before financing and why it is one of the most important figures in real estate analysis.

  • Lesson 11.2: Cap Rate Analysis

    Study how cap rates connect income to value and how investors use them to compare properties, pricing, and market risk.

  • Lesson 11.3: Cash-on-Cash Return

    Understand how cash-on-cash return measures the annual cash yield on invested equity and why leverage changes this metric.

  • Lesson 11.4: Internal Rate of Return (IRR) Introduction

    Examine how IRR incorporates timing and magnitude of cash flows over multiple years and why it differs from simpler return measures.

  • Lesson 11.5: Required Return Frameworks

    Learn how disciplined investors compare deal projections against minimum acceptable returns based on risk, opportunity cost, and strategy.

  • Lesson 11.6: Sensitivity Analysis

    Study how changes in rents, vacancy, expenses, financing, and exit assumptions can materially change outcomes and investment conclusions.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to compare deals using core return metrics, explain what different measures do and do not capture, and identify how sensitive a deal is to changes in assumptions. These concepts prepare students for advanced debt structuring, commercial underwriting, development feasibility, and the capstone investment plan.

  • Templates & Tools

    Use underwriting worksheets, return calculators, and scenario testing tools to practice evaluating simple and multi-year deal structures.

  • Glossary Support

    Review terms such as NOI, cap rate, cash-on-cash return, IRR, required return, underwriting, and sensitivity analysis.
