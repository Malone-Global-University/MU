Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Non-Recourse Loan

Definition

A non-recourse loan is a type of financing in which the lender’s recovery in the event of default is limited to the collateral securing the loan — typically the property itself — and not the borrower’s personal assets.

However, most non-recourse loans include specific exceptions known as carve-outs.

How It Works

If the borrower defaults:

  • The lender may foreclose on the property.
  • The lender generally cannot pursue the borrower personally for deficiency.
  • Recovery is limited to the collateral value.

Non-Recourse vs. Recourse

  • Recourse Loan: Borrower is personally liable for remaining debt after foreclosure.
  • Non-Recourse Loan: Lender’s recovery is limited to the property, subject to carve-outs.

Smaller or higher-risk deals often require a personal guarantee, converting the loan to recourse.

Common Carve-Outs ("Bad Boy" Guarantees)

  • Fraud or intentional misrepresentation
  • Misuse of rents or security deposits
  • Bankruptcy filing
  • Environmental violations
  • Transfer of property without consent

If triggered, these carve-outs can create full or partial personal liability.

Why Lenders Offer Non-Recourse Loans

  • Lower risk stabilized assets
  • Strong cash flow history
  • Institutional-quality sponsorship
  • Conservative leverage ratios

Advantages for Investors

  • Limits personal asset exposure
  • Encourages portfolio scalability
  • Separates entity risk from individual wealth
  • Improves capital structure flexibility

Risks and Trade-Offs

  • Higher interest rates compared to recourse loans
  • Stricter underwriting standards
  • Lower maximum leverage
  • Complex carve-out provisions

Example

An investor acquires a stabilized apartment complex with a non-recourse loan. If the market declines and the property value drops below the loan balance, the lender may foreclose but cannot pursue the investor’s personal assets — unless carve-out triggers apply.

Investor Checklist

  • Review carve-out provisions carefully.
  • Maintain strict compliance with loan covenants.
  • Avoid actions that could trigger bad-boy liability.
  • Understand lender enforcement rights.
  • Align entity structure with loan terms.

Strategic Insight

Non-recourse financing is a powerful scaling tool. It protects personal balance sheets while allowing investors to deploy leverage across multiple assets — provided operational discipline is maintained.
