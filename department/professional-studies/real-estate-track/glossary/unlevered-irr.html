Unlevered IRR

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Unlevered IRR

Definition

Unlevered IRR (Unlevered Internal Rate of Return) measures the annualized return generated by a property's cash flows before considering the impact of debt financing. It reflects the asset’s pure operating performance.

What It Measures

  • Cash flows from operations before debt service.
  • Purchase price, capital expenditures, and sale proceeds.
  • Return driven solely by property fundamentals.

Unlevered vs. Levered IRR

  • Unlevered IRR: excludes loan proceeds and debt payments.
  • Levered IRR: includes financing effects on equity returns.

Unlevered IRR allows investors to compare properties independent of capital structure decisions.

Example

A property is purchased for $10 million in all cash. Over five years, it generates annual net cash flows from operations and sells for $13 million. The IRR calculated using only purchase, operating, and sale cash flows represents the unlevered IRR.

Why It Matters

  • Asset comparison: standardizes return metrics across deals.
  • Capital structure neutrality: isolates operational performance.
  • Underwriting discipline: ensures property fundamentals justify acquisition.

When It Is Used

  • Core and core-plus investment analysis.
  • Pre-financing underwriting evaluations.
  • Institutional investment benchmarking.
  • Comparing across different leverage scenarios.

Limitations

  • Does not reflect equity-level volatility.
  • Ignores financing risk or refinancing exposure.
  • May understate return amplification from leverage.

Strategic Considerations

  • Use unlevered IRR to test deal fundamentals.
  • Compare to weighted average cost of capital (WACC).
  • Evaluate sensitivity to exit cap rates.
  • Layer leverage only after fundamentals are validated.
