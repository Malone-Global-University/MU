Price Per Unit

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Price Per Unit

Definition

Price per unit is a valuation metric calculated by dividing a property’s purchase price by the total number of rentable units. It is most commonly used in multifamily real estate to compare asset pricing across similar properties.

Formula

  • Price Per Unit = Purchase Price ÷ Number of Units

Example

A 40-unit apartment building sells for $8,000,000.

  • Price Per Unit = 8,000,000 ÷ 40 = $200,000 per unit

Investors can compare this to recent sales in the same submarket to assess whether pricing is in line with current demand and asset quality.

Why It Matters

Price per unit allows for quick comparison across multifamily transactions. It helps investors determine whether a deal is:

  • Above or below recent comparable sales
  • Premium-priced due to renovations or location
  • Discounted due to deferred maintenance or operational issues

What It Does NOT Tell You

Price per unit is a surface-level metric. It does not account for:

  • Unit size differences (a 500 SF studio vs. a 1,200 SF 2-bedroom)
  • Current or in-place rent levels
  • Operating expenses or efficiency
  • Capital expenditure requirements
  • Income growth potential

For income-focused investors, metrics like cap rate, NOI, and price per square foot often provide deeper insight.

Investor Perspective

Professional investors rarely rely on price per unit alone. Instead, they use it as a screening metric before conducting full underwriting. A property with a lower price per unit may still be overpriced if rents are weak or expenses are excessive.

Common Pitfalls

  • Ignoring unit mix: Larger or luxury units justify higher pricing.
  • Overlooking condition: Deferred maintenance can distort comparisons.
  • Confusing it with value: Income drives value, not unit count alone.
  • Comparing different markets: Urban core assets will trade higher than suburban properties.

Related Terms

  • Cap Rate
  • Net Operating Income (NOI)
  • Price Per Square Foot
  • Gross Rent Multiplier (GRM)
