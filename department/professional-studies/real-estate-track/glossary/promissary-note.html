Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Promissory Note

Definition

A Promissory Note is a written legal promise by a borrower to repay a specific sum of money to a lender under defined terms.

It outlines the financial obligations of the borrower, including repayment schedule, interest rate, maturity date, and default provisions.

Key Components

  • Principal Amount: The original loan balance.
  • Interest Rate: Fixed or variable rate charged on the loan.
  • Payment Schedule: Monthly, quarterly, or other agreed structure.
  • Maturity Date: Date the loan must be fully repaid.
  • Default Provisions: Rights and remedies if borrower fails to pay.
  • Prepayment Terms: Rules governing early repayment.

Promissory Note vs. Deed of Trust

  • Promissory Note: The borrower’s promise to repay.
  • Deed of Trust (or Mortgage): The security instrument that uses the property as collateral.

The note creates the debt; the deed of trust secures it.

Why It Matters to Investors

  • Defines repayment obligations and loan economics
  • Controls interest accrual and amortization
  • Determines lender remedies in default
  • Affects refinance and exit flexibility

Example

An investor borrows $8 million at 6.5% interest, amortized over 30 years, with a 5-year maturity. The promissory note specifies monthly payments, late fees, and a balloon payment at maturity.

Common Pitfalls

  • Ignoring prepayment penalties
  • Overlooking default interest rates
  • Misunderstanding recourse provisions
  • Failing to review acceleration clauses
