Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)

Definition

Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) measures how well a property’s operating income can cover its required loan payments. It compares Net Operating Income (NOI) to debt service (principal + interest, and sometimes required reserves depending on lender definition).

Think of DSCR as the property’s cash flow cushion: higher DSCR means more room for error if rents soften, expenses rise, or vacancy increases.

How It’s Measured

DSCR is expressed as a ratio (e.g., 1.25x). A DSCR of 1.00x means NOI exactly equals required debt payments. Anything below 1.00x means the property does not generate enough NOI to fully cover debt service.

Common formulas

  • DSCR = Net Operating Income (NOI) ÷ Annual Debt Service
  • Annual Debt Service = Total required principal + interest payments per year
  • NOI (simplified) = Effective Gross Income − Operating Expenses (excluding debt service, capex, and depreciation)

Why It Matters

DSCR is one of the most common lender constraints in commercial real estate. It influences: maximum loan proceeds, interest rate and terms, and whether a deal is financeable under lender underwriting.

For investors, DSCR is also a fast way to stress-test durability. If a deal is barely above a lender minimum, small changes in NOI can force refinancing risk, cash traps, or capital calls.

Investor Uses

  • Sizing leverage: determine how much debt the property can safely carry.
  • Stress testing: model rent drops, vacancy spikes, tax reassessments, or insurance increases.
  • Refinance planning: evaluate future DSCR under higher rates or lower NOI at loan maturity.
  • Covenant monitoring: track performance vs. lender DSCR tests (especially on floating-rate loans).

Example

A property generates $450,000 of NOI. Annual principal and interest payments total $360,000. DSCR is:

  • DSCR = 450,000 ÷ 360,000 = 1.25x

A 1.25x DSCR means the property generates 25% more NOI than required debt payments—generally a healthier cushion than 1.05x, especially in volatile expense or rate environments.

Typical Benchmarks (Context-Dependent)

DSCR requirements vary by lender, asset type, market, and loan structure (fixed vs. floating). Many lenders target a minimum DSCR (often in the ~1.15x to 1.30x range for stabilized assets), with higher requirements for riskier deals. Treat DSCR as a deal-specific constraint, not a universal rule.

Common Pitfalls

  • Mixing NOI definitions: lender NOI may include/omit items differently than your underwriting.
  • Ignoring reserves: some lenders underwrite with required replacement reserves or escrows.
  • Using pro forma NOI: underwriting to future rent bumps can mask current coverage risk.
  • Forgetting rate resets: floating-rate debt can crush DSCR if interest expense rises.
  • Not separating CapEx from OpEx: DSCR is NOI-based; recurring CapEx still matters for true durability.
