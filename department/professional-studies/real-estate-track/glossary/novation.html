Novation

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Novation

Definition

A Novation is a legal agreement that replaces one party to a contract with another party, with the consent of all involved parties, and releases the original party from future obligations.

In simple terms: one party exits a contract and is fully replaced by someone else.

Key Elements of Novation

  • Existing valid contract.
  • Agreement of all parties.
  • Substitution of a new party.
  • Release of the original party from liability.

Novation vs. Assignment

  • Assignment: Rights are transferred, but original party often remains liable.
  • Novation: Original party is fully released from obligations.

In real estate, this distinction is critical in leases and development contracts.

Common Real Estate Applications

  • Tenant transferring lease obligations to a new tenant.
  • Developer transferring a purchase agreement to another entity.
  • Substitution of borrower in a loan agreement.
  • Replacement of general contractor in a development project.

Example

A developer signs a purchase agreement to acquire land. Before closing, the developer transfers the agreement to a newly formed LLC. With seller consent and a novation agreement, the LLC replaces the developer and the original developer is released from liability.

Why It Matters

  • Limits ongoing liability exposure.
  • Clarifies responsibility in complex transactions.
  • Ensures lenders and counterparties consent to substitution.
  • Protects original contracting parties from future claims.

Risks

  • Failure to obtain written consent.
  • Assuming release without formal documentation.
  • Lender restrictions on borrower substitution.
  • Tax or structural implications of entity changes.

Investor Checklist

  • Confirm contract permits novation.
  • Obtain written agreement signed by all parties.
  • Ensure lender consent if debt is involved.
  • Review indemnification provisions.
  • Consult legal counsel before executing substitution.

Novation in Development

In large development projects, novation is often used when:

  • Land contracts are transferred to special purpose entities (SPEs).
  • Construction contracts are reassigned to financing entities.
  • Joint venture partners restructure ownership.

Related Terms

  • Purchase Agreement
  • Lease
  • Single Purpose Entity (SPE)
