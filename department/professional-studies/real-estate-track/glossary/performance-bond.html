Performance Bond

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Performance Bond

Definition

A performance bond is a type of surety bond issued by a third-party guarantor (the surety) that guarantees a contractor will complete a construction project according to the terms of the contract. If the contractor defaults or fails to perform, the surety compensates the project owner or arranges for completion.

How It Works

A performance bond involves three parties:

  • Principal: The contractor performing the work.
  • Obligee: The owner or developer protected by the bond.
  • Surety: The bonding company guaranteeing performance.

If the contractor defaults, the surety may:

  • Finance the existing contractor to complete the project.
  • Hire a replacement contractor.
  • Pay damages up to the bond amount.

Why It Matters in Real Estate Development

  • Risk mitigation: Protects against contractor bankruptcy or abandonment.
  • Lender requirement: Often required by construction lenders.
  • Investor protection: Reduces downside risk during development.
  • Completion assurance: Supports timely stabilization and lease-up.

Bond Amount

Performance bonds typically equal 100% of the construction contract value, though terms may vary depending on project size and contractor credit strength.

Performance Bond vs. Payment Bond

  • Performance Bond: Guarantees completion of the project.
  • Payment Bond: Guarantees payment to subcontractors and suppliers, helping prevent mechanics' liens.

Example

A developer signs a $20 million construction contract for a multifamily project. The lender requires a performance bond for the full contract amount. If the contractor fails mid-project, the surety steps in to ensure completion or compensate the developer for losses.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming automatic payout: Claims may require proof of contractor default.
  • Coverage limits: Bond only covers up to stated amount.
  • Delay exposure: Completion may still be delayed even with bond protection.
  • Cost impact: Bond premiums increase project costs.

Investor Applications

  • Development underwriting: Evaluate contractor strength and bonding capacity.
  • Loan structuring: Required protection for construction financing.
  • Risk allocation: Transfers part of execution risk to the surety.
  • Capital preservation: Reduces catastrophic construction failure risk.
