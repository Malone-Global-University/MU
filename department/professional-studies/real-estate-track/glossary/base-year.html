Base Year

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Base Year

Definition

In a commercial lease, the base year is the reference year used to establish a benchmark level of operating expenses. Tenants pay their proportionate share of any increases in operating expenses above the base year amount.

How a Base Year Lease Works

  • The landlord establishes operating expenses for a specific year (often the first full calendar year of the lease).
  • That year becomes the “base year.”
  • If expenses increase in subsequent years, tenants reimburse their share of the increase.

Example

  • Base Year Operating Expenses (2026): $1,000,000
  • 2027 Operating Expenses: $1,100,000
  • Increase: $100,000
  • Tenant Pro Rata Share: 10%

Tenant pays 10% × $100,000 = $10,000 in additional rent.

Common in Gross Leases

Base year structures are typically used in:

  • Full-service gross leases
  • Modified gross leases
  • Office properties

Base Year vs. Expense Stop

  • Base Year: Based on actual historical operating expenses.
  • Expense Stop: Fixed dollar amount per square foot.

Gross-Up Provisions

Landlords often “gross up” base year expenses to reflect stabilized occupancy (e.g., 95%) so that artificially low expenses during a lease-up year do not unfairly benefit tenants.

Advantages

  • Predictable baseline expense structure.
  • Aligns tenant payments with rising property costs.
  • Protects landlord NOI from inflationary expense growth.

Risks and Considerations

  • If base year expenses are unusually low, tenants may face higher reimbursements later.
  • Unusual one-time expenses may distort base year benchmark.
  • Disputes may arise over what qualifies as recoverable expenses.

Underwriting Considerations

  • Review lease abstracts for base year details.
  • Analyze historical expense trends.
  • Confirm gross-up methodology.
  • Model reimbursement growth conservatively.

Impact on Valuation

  • Proper expense recovery supports stable NOI.
  • Understated base years can increase reimbursement growth.
  • Expense volatility may impact tenant retention.

Common Pitfalls

  • Using partial-year expenses as base year.
  • Failing to gross up variable expenses.
  • Misclassifying capital expenses as operating expenses.
  • Overlooking audit rights in lease agreements.
