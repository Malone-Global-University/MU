Supply Pipeline

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Supply Pipeline

Definition

The Supply Pipeline refers to all real estate projects that are planned, approved, under construction, or recently delivered and expected to add new inventory to a specific market or submarket.

It represents future competitive supply that can impact occupancy, rent growth, absorption, and overall market performance.

Pipeline Stages

  • Proposed: Early planning stage; may not break ground.
  • Permitted: Approved for construction but not yet started.
  • Under Construction: Actively being built.
  • Recently Delivered: Completed and entering lease-up.

Why It Matters to Investors

  • Rent pressure: Large deliveries can slow rent growth.
  • Absorption risk: Markets must absorb new units to maintain occupancy.
  • Valuation impact: Future supply influences cap rates and underwriting.
  • Competitive positioning: Newer assets may attract higher-paying tenants.

How It’s Analyzed

  • Compare pipeline units to existing inventory (percentage growth).
  • Evaluate delivery timing relative to demand growth.
  • Assess product type (luxury vs workforce vs industrial class).
  • Analyze pre-leasing activity and construction delays.

Example

A submarket with 10,000 existing multifamily units has 1,500 units under construction and 1,000 more permitted. If all deliver within 24 months, inventory could expand by 25%. Investors must evaluate whether projected job growth and household formation can support that increase.

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming all proposed projects deliver: Many projects stall.
  • Ignoring delivery timing: Simultaneous completions increase risk.
  • Overlooking quality differences: New supply may target a different renter profile.
  • Failing to adjust underwriting: Rent growth assumptions should reflect supply risk.
