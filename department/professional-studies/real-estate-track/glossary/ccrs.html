CC&Rs

Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions – Real Estate Investing Glossary

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary CC&Rs

Definition

CC&Rs (Covenants, Conditions & Restrictions) are recorded governing documents for a subdivision, condominium, or planned community. They establish legally binding rules regarding property use, architectural standards, and the authority of the homeowners association (HOA).

What CC&Rs Typically Cover

  • Use restrictions: Residential-only, no commercial operations, pet limitations.
  • Architectural controls: Exterior modifications, fencing, paint colors, additions.
  • Rental policies: Lease minimums, rental caps, or prohibition of short-term rentals.
  • Maintenance obligations: Owner vs. HOA responsibility boundaries.
  • Assessment authority: HOA power to levy dues and special assessments.

Legal Status

CC&Rs are recorded with the county and typically “run with the land,” meaning they bind current and future owners. Violations can result in fines, liens, or legal enforcement actions by the HOA.

Investor Implications

  • Rental flexibility: Caps or prohibitions can limit income potential.
  • Value stability: Standards may protect neighborhood quality and resale value.
  • Liquidity impact: Strict rules can reduce buyer pool.
  • Compliance risk: Violations can lead to fines or forced corrective action.

Example

An investor purchases a condo unit intending to lease it short-term. The CC&Rs require a minimum 12-month lease and cap total rentals at 20% of units. The investor must comply or risk penalties from the HOA.

Common Pitfalls

  • Not reviewing full documents: Summary sheets may omit critical clauses.
  • Ignoring amendments: CC&Rs can be updated by vote.
  • Confusing rules with bylaws: CC&Rs differ from board-adopted rules and regulations.
  • Assuming zoning overrides: CC&Rs may be stricter than local zoning laws.
