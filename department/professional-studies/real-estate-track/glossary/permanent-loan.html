Permanent Loan

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Permanent Loan

Definition

A Permanent Loan is long-term financing used for a stabilized real estate asset. It typically replaces short-term financing such as a construction or bridge loan.

These loans are structured based on the property's in-place income and performance.

When It Is Used

  • After construction completion
  • After lease-up and stabilization
  • To refinance higher-cost short-term debt
  • To return capital to investors

Key Characteristics

  • Term: Typically 5–30 years
  • Amortization: Often 25–30 years
  • Lower Interest Rates than construction loans
  • Underwritten to NOI and DSCR

Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)

Permanent loans are often sized based on DSCR:

DSCR = Net Operating Income ÷ Annual Debt Service

Lenders commonly require DSCR of 1.20x–1.30x or higher.

Example

A developer completes a 200-unit apartment project. After achieving stable occupancy, the property generates $3 million in annual NOI.

The developer refinances a construction loan with a 10-year permanent loan at a lower fixed interest rate.

Why It Matters to Investors

  • Reduces interest rate volatility
  • Stabilizes long-term cash flow
  • May allow equity recapitalization
  • Improves refinance flexibility

Common Pitfalls

  • Prepayment penalties
  • Lockout periods
  • Overestimating stabilized NOI
  • Interest rate timing risk

Permanent Loan vs. Construction Loan

  • Construction Loan: Short-term, higher risk, funds development.
  • Permanent Loan: Long-term, income-based, funds stabilized asset.
