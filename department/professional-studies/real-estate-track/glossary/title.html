Definition

Title refers to the legal ownership of a property and the bundle of rights that come with it, including the right to use, lease, sell, or encumber the real estate.

What Title Includes

Holding title means possessing legally recognized ownership rights. These rights may include:

  • Right of possession – to occupy or control the property
  • Right of control – to determine how the property is used (subject to zoning/law)
  • Right of exclusion – to prevent others from entering or using the property
  • Right of disposition – to sell, lease, or transfer ownership

Clear vs. Clouded Title

A clear title means ownership is free from competing claims, liens, or legal defects. A clouded title contains issues such as unpaid liens, recording errors, boundary disputes, or unresolved ownership claims that may impair transferability.

Title in Transactions

During acquisition, buyers conduct a title search to verify ownership history and uncover liens, easements, covenants, or encumbrances. Most transactions involve title insurance to protect against undiscovered defects.

Why It Matters to Investors

  • Risk management: undisclosed liens or claims can impair value.
  • Financing: lenders require clean title to secure their loan position.
  • Exit liquidity: buyers demand marketable title at disposition.
  • Operational control: easements and covenants may limit use or redevelopment.

Common Title Issues

  • Tax liens or mechanic’s liens
  • Unreleased mortgages
  • Boundary or survey disputes
  • Easements affecting development
  • Errors in public records

Related Terms

  • Deed
  • Lien
