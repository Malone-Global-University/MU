Soft Costs

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Soft Costs

Definition

Soft costs are non-physical expenses incurred during real estate development, construction, or renovation. Unlike hard costs, which relate directly to building materials and labor, soft costs cover professional services, financing, legal work, and administrative activities.

Common Categories of Soft Costs

  • Architectural & engineering fees
  • Legal and accounting services
  • Permits and entitlement fees
  • Development management fees
  • Appraisal and environmental studies
  • Financing costs: Loan origination fees, interest carry during construction
  • Insurance premiums
  • Marketing and leasing commissions

Soft Costs vs. Hard Costs

  • Hard costs: Concrete, steel, framing, labor, equipment.
  • Soft costs: Planning, design, financing, and oversight.
  • Budget impact: Soft costs typically represent 15–30% of total project cost.

Why Soft Costs Matter

  • Feasibility analysis: Underestimating soft costs can derail returns.
  • Financing sensitivity: Interest carry increases with delays.
  • Project risk: Entitlement and permitting issues can escalate expenses.
  • Timeline impact: Longer predevelopment phases increase overhead.

Example

A ground-up apartment project has $10 million in hard construction costs. Soft costs total $2.5 million, including:

  • $800,000 in architectural and engineering fees
  • $400,000 in permits and entitlements
  • $900,000 in interest carry and loan fees
  • $400,000 in legal, insurance, and administrative expenses

Total project cost becomes $12.5 million, materially affecting required rents and projected returns.

Common Pitfalls

  • Ignoring contingency: Soft cost overruns are common.
  • Underestimating interest carry: Delays amplify financing costs.
  • Overlooking entitlement risk: Regulatory hurdles increase fees and timelines.
  • Weak budgeting discipline: Soft costs can quietly expand without oversight.

Investor Implications

  • Return sensitivity: Small soft cost changes impact IRR significantly.
  • Execution risk: Experienced teams manage soft cost control more effectively.
  • Capital planning: Adequate reserves are critical for timeline delays.
  • Feasibility thresholds: Soft costs often determine project viability.
