Bankruptcy Remoteness

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Bankruptcy Remoteness

Definition

Bankruptcy Remoteness refers to a legal and structural framework designed to minimize the risk that a property-owning entity becomes part of a parent company’s or affiliate’s bankruptcy proceeding. It is commonly achieved through the use of a Single-Purpose Entity (SPE).

Purpose

  • Protect lenders: ensures collateral remains insulated from unrelated financial distress.
  • Preserve asset value: prevents disruption from cross-defaults or affiliate insolvency.
  • Support non-recourse lending: gives lenders confidence in risk isolation.

How Bankruptcy Remoteness Is Achieved

  • SPE structure: entity formed solely to own and operate a single asset.
  • Separateness covenants: no commingling of funds, separate accounting, independent operations.
  • Independent manager provisions: required approval before filing bankruptcy.
  • Restrictions on additional debt: limits unrelated liabilities.

Why It Matters

In commercial real estate finance, bankruptcy remoteness reduces systemic risk. Without it, lenders could face delays, automatic stays, or asset pooling if a sponsor’s broader portfolio enters bankruptcy.

Relationship to Carve-Out Guaranties

Violating bankruptcy-remote provisions—such as voluntarily filing bankruptcy without required approvals—may trigger liability under a Carve-Out Guaranty, potentially converting a non-recourse loan into full recourse.

Example

A sponsor owns multiple properties through separate SPEs. If one property underperforms and the parent company faces financial distress, properly structured bankruptcy-remote entities help prevent healthy properties from being pulled into broader insolvency proceedings.

Common Pitfalls

  • Commingling funds: undermines separateness and weakens protection.
  • Ignoring corporate formalities: failure to maintain independent records.
  • Unauthorized guarantees: cross-collateralizing assets.
  • Technical covenant breaches: can trigger recourse exposure.

Investor Considerations

  • Maintain disciplined accounting and governance.
  • Understand independent manager requirements.
  • Review loan documents carefully for bankruptcy triggers.
  • Consult legal counsel for structuring and compliance.
