Definition

Insurance is a contractual agreement in which an insurer agrees to compensate an insured party for specified losses in exchange for regular premium payments. It is a mechanism for risk transfer.

Why Insurance Matters in Real Estate

Real estate investing involves exposure to physical damage, liability claims, and operational risks. Insurance protects both cash flow and equity by transferring catastrophic risk away from ownership.

  • Protects property value
  • Preserves investor capital
  • Required by lenders
  • Stabilizes long-term returns

Core Types of Real Estate Insurance

  • Property Insurance: Covers damage to the building itself.
  • General Liability Insurance: Covers third-party injury or property damage.
  • Umbrella Insurance: Provides excess liability coverage above primary limits.
  • Builder’s Risk: Covers construction-related property risk.
  • Workers’ Compensation: Covers employee injury claims.

Key Insurance Concepts

  • Premium: Cost paid for coverage.
  • Deductible: Amount insured must pay before coverage applies.
  • Policy Limit: Maximum payout amount.
  • Exclusion: Specific losses not covered.
  • Endorsement: Policy modification or addition.

Risk Allocation Tools

Insurance works in combination with contractual protections:

  • Additional insured
  • Waiver of subrogation
  • Hold harmless agreements
  • Certificates of insurance

Example

A fire damages a multifamily building. Property insurance covers repair costs, while liability insurance responds if a tenant alleges negligence. Umbrella coverage may apply if damages exceed primary limits.

Common Investor Mistakes

  • Underinsuring replacement value.
  • Ignoring policy exclusions.
  • Failing to increase limits as portfolio grows.
  • Not coordinating insurance with lease terms.
  • Overlooking renewal dates.

Investor Checklist

  • Review coverage annually.
  • Align limits with asset risk profile.
  • Require tenant and vendor insurance.
  • Track endorsements and certificates.
  • Coordinate coverage with lender requirements.
