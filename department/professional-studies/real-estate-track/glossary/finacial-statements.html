Financial Statements

Definition

Financial Statements are formal accounting reports that summarize a property's financial performance and financial position over a specific period.

In real estate investing, they provide the data needed for underwriting, valuation, lender review, and long-term capital allocation decisions.

The Three Core Statements

1. Income Statement (Profit & Loss)

Reports revenue and expenses over a period to determine Net Operating Income (NOI) and net profit.

  • Rental income
  • Other income (fees, parking, reimbursements)
  • Operating expenses
  • Net Operating Income

2. Balance Sheet

Provides a snapshot of assets, liabilities, and equity at a specific point in time.

  • Assets (property, cash, receivables)
  • Liabilities (loans, payables, deposits)
  • Equity (owner’s capital)

Basic equation: Assets = Liabilities + Equity

3. Cash Flow Statement

Tracks actual cash inflows and outflows, reconciling net income to real cash movement.

  • Operating cash flow
  • Investing activities (CapEx)
  • Financing activities (loan payments, distributions)

Why They Matter to Investors

  • Validate reported NOI and operating trends
  • Assess leverage and liquidity risk
  • Identify capital expenditure needs
  • Support valuation via cap rate or DCF analysis
  • Meet lender and investor reporting requirements

Example

A property reports $2,000,000 in annual rental income and $800,000 in operating expenses. The income statement shows $1,200,000 in NOI. The balance sheet shows a $10,000,000 loan, and the cash flow statement reveals $400,000 in annual principal payments.

Together, the three statements provide a complete picture of performance, leverage, and capital requirements.

Common Pitfalls

  • Relying only on NOI: Ignoring debt and CapEx can distort true returns.
  • Confusing accrual and cash accounting: Timing differences matter.
  • Not reviewing supporting schedules: General Ledger detail is essential.
  • Ignoring trend analysis: Year-over-year changes reveal operational quality.

Related Terms

  • General Ledger
  • Net Operating Income (NOI)
  • Operating Expenses
  • Capital Expenditures (CapEx)
  • Time Value of Money (TVM)
  • Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
