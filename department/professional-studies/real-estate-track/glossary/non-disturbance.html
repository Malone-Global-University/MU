Non-Disturbance

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Non-Disturbance

Definition

Non-disturbance is a provision in a lease or SNDA Agreement in which a lender agrees that a tenant’s lease will remain in effect if the lender forecloses on the property—provided the tenant is not in default.

Why Non-Disturbance Matters

  • Protects tenants from eviction after foreclosure.
  • Preserves rental income for lenders.
  • Stabilizes property value during distress.
  • Enhances marketability of commercial leases.

How It Works

  1. Landlord obtains financing secured by the property.
  2. Tenant signs an SNDA with lender.
  3. If foreclosure occurs, lender becomes new landlord.
  4. Tenant continues occupancy under existing lease terms.

Conditions for Protection

  • Tenant must not be in lease default.
  • Lease must comply with mortgage requirements.
  • Tenant must honor attornment provisions.

Non-Disturbance vs Subordination

  • Subordination: Lease is junior to mortgage.
  • Non-Disturbance: Lease survives foreclosure.

These two concepts are typically paired within an SNDA agreement.

Investor Perspective

  • Provides income continuity during foreclosure.
  • Maintains occupancy rates.
  • Supports property valuation stability.

Tenant Perspective

  • Reduces risk of business disruption.
  • Ensures long-term occupancy security.
  • Provides contractual certainty.

Common Pitfalls

  • Failing to execute SNDA with major tenants.
  • Ambiguous language limiting tenant protections.
  • Conflict between lease and mortgage documents.
  • Overlooking lender approval requirements.

Investor Checklist

  • Confirm executed SNDAs for anchor tenants.
  • Review lease compliance with mortgage terms.
  • Ensure non-disturbance protections are enforceable.
  • Assess tenant credit quality.
