Collections

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Collections

Definition

Collections refers to the process and outcome of receiving rent and fees owed by residents. In operations and underwriting, “collections” usually means the actual cash received during a period compared to what was billed or expected.

Why It Matters

Collections are the bridge between “paper income” and real money. A property can show strong scheduled rent, but if collections are weak, cash flow can fail. Consistent collections support:

  • Stable NOI and distributions
  • Debt service reliability (DSCR protection)
  • Lower bad debt, fewer evictions, and smoother operations
  • More accurate underwriting and valuation

Collections vs. Delinquency vs. Bad Debt

  • Collections: rent/fees actually received.
  • Delinquency: balances currently past due (may still be collected later).
  • Bad debt: balances that were not collected and were written off (permanent loss).

How It’s Measured

Properties commonly track collections as a percent of rent billed and with aging reports that show how long balances remain unpaid.

Common formulas

  • Collections % = Rent collected ÷ Rent billed (or scheduled rent) for the period
  • Loss to Collections = (Rent billed − Rent collected) ÷ Rent billed

Collections Workflow (Typical)

  • Pre-due reminders: texts/emails before the 1st and before late fees trigger.
  • Due date + grace period: clear expectations and resident-friendly payment options.
  • Early follow-up (days 1–5): fast contact, confirm issues, resolve friction.
  • Formal notices: late notices and pay-or-quit notices (as permitted).
  • Payment plans: short, documented plans for qualified hardship cases.
  • Escalation: filing timeline and consistent enforcement where nonpayment persists.

Operational Best Practices

  • Make paying easy: online portal, ACH, auto-pay, and multiple payment methods.
  • Standardize policy: written collections timeline used consistently across all residents.
  • Track aging daily: prioritize older balances; aging predicts collectability.
  • Reduce friction: clear ledger statements, real-time receipts, and fast posting.
  • Align incentives: train teams and score performance on on-time payment and delinquency aging, not just occupancy.
  • Prevent problems: strong tenant screening reduces delinquency before it starts.

Underwriting Implications

Underwriting should be based on economic collections, not just “scheduled rent.” Review trailing 12-month collections, delinquency aging, eviction volume, and bad debt write-offs. If a property is running elevated delinquency, assume slower improvement and underwrite with a buffer until leading indicators improve.

Common Pitfalls

  • Counting billed rent as collected rent: scheduled rent is not cash.
  • Inconsistent enforcement: trains residents that deadlines are optional.
  • Delayed action: small balances become large, uncollectible balances.
  • Poor documentation: weak ledgers and missing notes make escalation and court processes harder.
  • Overusing long payment plans: long plans can increase loss severity if residents re-default.
