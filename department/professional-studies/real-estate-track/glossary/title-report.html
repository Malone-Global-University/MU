Title Report

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Title Report

Definition

A Title Report is a document issued by a title company that outlines the current legal ownership of a property and identifies recorded liens, encumbrances, easements, restrictions, and other matters affecting title.

Purpose

  • Verify ownership: confirms the seller has legal authority to transfer title.
  • Identify liens: mortgages, tax liens, judgments, mechanic’s liens.
  • Reveal encumbrances: easements, covenants, restrictions (CC&Rs).
  • Facilitate title insurance: forms the basis of the title policy.

Key Components

  • Legal description: formal property boundaries.
  • Vesting information: current recorded owner.
  • Schedule B exceptions: items not covered by title insurance.
  • Recorded documents: deeds, liens, easements.

Why It Matters

  • Risk management: protects against unexpected claims.
  • Closing readiness: ensures clear title before transfer.
  • Lender requirement: required for most financed transactions.
  • Development feasibility: identifies restrictions that may limit use.

Example

During due diligence, a title report reveals a recorded utility easement crossing part of a development site. This may restrict building placement and require design adjustments. The buyer can assess the impact before closing.

Common Issues Found

  • Unreleased prior mortgages.
  • Unpaid property taxes.
  • Boundary disputes.
  • Restrictive covenants limiting property use.

Investor Considerations

  • Review Schedule B exceptions carefully.
  • Consult legal counsel for complex encumbrances.
  • Confirm title insurance coverage aligns with risk tolerance.
  • Address curable title defects before closing.
