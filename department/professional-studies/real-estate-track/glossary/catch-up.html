Catch-Up

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Catch-Up

Definition

Catch-up is a real estate private equity waterfall provision that shifts a larger share of distributions to the sponsor/GP after the preferred return is satisfied. The catch-up continues until the GP has “caught up” to an agreed profit split for that tier.

Where It Fits in a Waterfall

Catch-up typically appears after two earlier steps: (1) return of capital and/or (2) payment of the preferred return to investors (LPs). Once the pref is met, a catch-up tier can accelerate GP distributions so the GP reaches the negotiated share of profits.

Common structure (conceptual)

  • Tier 1: Distribute to LPs until they receive their preferred return (and often return of capital).
  • Tier 2 (Catch-Up): Distribute primarily to GP until GP reaches target split.
  • Tier 3+: Split remaining cash flows by the ongoing promote percentages (e.g., 80/20, then 70/30, etc.).

Why It Exists

The catch-up is designed to align incentives: LPs receive a minimum return first (the pref), then the GP is rewarded for performance by getting to the promoted split more quickly once that hurdle is cleared.

How It’s Measured

Catch-up math depends on the partnership agreement, but most provisions aim to ensure that once the catch-up tier ends, the GP’s share of profits (to that point) equals the negotiated promote for that tier.

Two common catch-up types

  • 100% Catch-Up: After the pref, all incremental distributions go to the GP until the GP reaches the target split. Then distributions revert to the normal split.
  • Partial Catch-Up (e.g., 50%/50% or 70%/30%): After the pref, distributions are split in a GP-favored ratio until the catch-up target is reached.

Example

Assume a deal has an 8% preferred return to LPs and then an 80/20 split (LP/GP), with a 100% catch-up to GP after the pref.

  • Step 1: LPs receive distributions until they’ve been paid their 8% pref (and any required return of capital).
  • Step 2 (Catch-Up): Next distributions go 100% to GP until the GP’s total share of profits reaches 20%.
  • Step 3: Remaining distributions are split 80% to LP and 20% to GP.

The effect is that once the deal is performing above the pref, the GP moves more quickly into the promoted economics.

Why It Matters for Underwriting

  • Impacts net returns: Catch-up changes the timing and amount of LP cash flows, especially in mid-performing outcomes.
  • Increases sensitivity to performance: Slightly better performance can cause an earlier shift into promote economics.
  • Can change IRR dynamics: Catch-up often reduces LP IRR relative to a no-catch-up structure at similar total profits.

Common Pitfalls

  • Confusing “pref” with “guarantee”: the preferred return is a priority, not a promise.
  • Not modeling tiers correctly: small errors in waterfall logic can materially distort net LP returns.
  • Ignoring catch-up cap/definition: confirm how “catch-up achieved” is defined (profits, distributions, cumulative basis, etc.).
  • Comparing deals using gross metrics: always compare on net LP cash flows after promote/catch-up.
