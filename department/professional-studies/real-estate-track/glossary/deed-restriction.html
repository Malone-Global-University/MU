Deed Restriction

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Deed Restriction

Definition

A deed restriction is a legally binding limitation recorded in a property’s deed that restricts how the property may be used, developed, or transferred. These restrictions typically “run with the land,” meaning they remain enforceable against future owners.

Common Types of Deed Restrictions

  • Use limitations: Residential-only, no industrial use, no specific businesses.
  • Development limits: Height restrictions, density caps, setback requirements.
  • Affordability requirements: Income-based occupancy restrictions.
  • Non-compete clauses: Preventing competing retail uses.
  • Transfer restrictions: Limits on resale timing or eligible buyers.

Deed Restriction vs. Zoning

  • Zoning: Public law imposed by local government.
  • Deed restriction: Private agreement recorded against title.
  • Hierarchy: Property must comply with both; the stricter rule generally governs.

Enforcement

  • Neighboring property owners
  • Homeowners associations (if applicable)
  • Original grantor or developer
  • Municipalities (for affordability or public-benefit restrictions)

Investor Implications

  • Development risk: May block redevelopment or value-add plans.
  • Income constraints: Rental or use caps can reduce revenue potential.
  • Valuation impact: Restrictions often reduce highest-and-best-use flexibility.
  • Financing review: Lenders examine title commitments for restrictive clauses.

Example

A land parcel is sold with a deed restriction prohibiting multi-family construction for 25 years. Although zoning permits apartments, the private restriction prevents development until expiration or formal release.

Common Pitfalls

  • Skipping title review: Restrictions may appear in older recorded documents.
  • Assuming expiration: Some restrictions automatically renew.
  • Overlooking amendment requirements: Release may require multiple parties’ consent.
  • Confusing with CC&Rs: CC&Rs are broader governing documents; deed restrictions are title-based limitations.
