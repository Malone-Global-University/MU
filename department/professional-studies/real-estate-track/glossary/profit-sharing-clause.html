Profit Sharing Clause

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Definition

A profit sharing clause is a lease provision requiring a tenant to share with the landlord any excess profits generated from assigning or subleasing the leased premises at a higher rent than the tenant is obligated to pay under its original lease.

Purpose

  • Prevents tenants from fully capturing market rent arbitrage.
  • Allows landlords to participate in upside value.
  • Balances tenant flexibility with landlord control.

How It Works

  1. Tenant subleases or assigns space at a higher rent.
  2. Calculate net profit (after deducting allowable costs).
  3. Tenant shares agreed percentage (e.g., 50%) with landlord.

Typical Calculation

Sublease Rent − Original Lease Rent − Reasonable Transaction Costs = Net Profit

Example

  • Original Lease Rent: $20/SF
  • Sublease Rent: $30/SF
  • Allowable Costs: $2/SF

Net Profit = $30 − $20 − $2 = $8/SF If 50% sharing applies → Landlord receives $4/SF

Allowable Deductions

  • Brokerage commissions.
  • Legal fees.
  • Tenant improvement costs.
  • Marketing expenses.

Profit Sharing vs. Recapture Clause

  • Profit Sharing: Tenant may proceed, but landlord shares upside.
  • Recapture Clause: Landlord may terminate lease instead of approving transfer.

Landlord Perspective

  • Captures value in rising markets.
  • Discourages speculative leasing.
  • Maintains leverage during assignment negotiations.

Tenant Perspective

  • Retains partial upside opportunity.
  • Maintains flexibility to restructure business.
  • Must carefully review profit definition language.

Underwriting Considerations

  • Review lease abstracts for profit-sharing provisions.
  • Assess potential recapture alternatives.
  • Model effect of below-market lease positions.

Common Pitfalls

  • Ambiguous definition of “profit.”
  • Disputes over allowable deductions.
  • Ignoring partial assignment scenarios.
  • Failure to align with recapture rights.
