Building Permit

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Building Permit

Definition

A Building Permit is official authorization issued by a local government allowing construction, renovation, demolition, or structural modification of a property in accordance with applicable building codes and zoning regulations.

In simple terms: it’s the legal approval required before you build or significantly alter a structure.

When Is a Building Permit Required?

  • New construction (ground-up development)
  • Major renovations or structural changes
  • Additions or expansions
  • Electrical, plumbing, or mechanical upgrades
  • Change of use projects
  • Demolition of existing structures

Permit Approval Process

1. Plan Submission

  • Architectural drawings
  • Engineering plans
  • Site plans

2. Plan Review

  • Zoning compliance check
  • Building code compliance
  • Fire safety review
  • Accessibility (ADA) compliance

3. Permit Issuance

Once approved, the permit is issued and construction may begin.

4. Inspections

  • Foundation inspection
  • Framing inspection
  • Electrical/plumbing inspections
  • Final inspection

Building Permit vs. Certificate of Occupancy

  • Building Permit: Authorizes construction.
  • Certificate of Occupancy: Authorizes occupancy after completion.

Impact on Development Timeline

  • Permit delays can extend project timelines.
  • Revisions may require resubmittals.
  • Inspections can slow construction if deficiencies are found.

Financial Implications

  • Permit fees add to development costs.
  • Delays increase interest carry on construction loans.
  • Failure to obtain permits can invalidate insurance or financing.

Risks of Non-Compliance

  • Stop-work orders.
  • Fines or penalties.
  • Forced removal of unpermitted work.
  • Difficulty selling or refinancing the property.

Example Scenario

A developer converting an office building to residential use must secure:

  • Building permit for interior reconfiguration.
  • Updated occupancy classification approval.
  • Final inspection approval before tenants move in.

Investor Considerations

  • Confirm permits are obtained before closing or funding.
  • Verify inspection milestones align with loan draw schedule.
  • Ensure compliance for insurance coverage validity.
  • Include permit timing in project schedule.

Investor Checklist

  • Review jurisdiction-specific permit requirements.
  • Budget permit and impact fees.
  • Track inspection timelines.
  • Maintain documentation for lenders and buyers.
  • Confirm final inspection approval before stabilization.
