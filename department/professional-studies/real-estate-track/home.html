Real Estate Investing Track

Learn the financial, analytical, and strategic principles of real estate investment. Study valuation, cash flow, financing structures, market cycles, and portfolio strategy—built around disciplined decision-making under uncertainty.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Real Estate Investing Track

Track Overview

This free, self-directed track teaches how to evaluate real estate investments with discipline and economic reasoning. Students learn to interpret property-level financial statements, value assets, structure financing, assess market conditions, manage operational risk, and design long-term portfolio strategy.

The through-line is simple: real estate is the disciplined acquisition, improvement, and management of income-producing assets under conditions of uncertainty. The goal is not hype—it's durable decision-making.

Track Units

  • Unit 1: Financial & Economic Foundations

    Build core investing math and property-level financial literacy: TVM, inflation, leverage concepts, and introductory cash flow logic.

  • Unit 2: Foundations of Real Estate

    Understand property types, ownership rights, real estate as an asset class, and the key metrics used to evaluate deals.

  • Unit 3: Real Estate Markets & Cycles

    Study supply and demand, credit cycles vs property cycles, behavioral forces, cap rate movement, and liquidity dynamics.

  • Unit 4: Property Valuation Basics

    Learn CMA logic, cost approach, income approach (intro), and the difference between price and value in real markets.

  • Unit 5: Legal & Regulatory Fundamentals

    Review titles and deeds, zoning and land use basics, purchase contracts, and practical compliance awareness.

  • Unit 6: The Property Acquisition Process

    Walk through the transaction from pre-approval to closing: offers, negotiation, contingencies, inspections, and execution.

  • Unit 7: Mortgage & Debt Structuring

    Understand loan types, amortization, DTI, refinancing basics, and how leverage changes risk and outcomes.

  • Unit 8: Rental Property Operations

    Learn tenant screening, lease structure, rent setting, maintenance planning, and cash flow monitoring for income property.

  • Unit 9: Property Management Systems

    Build operational workflows: rent collection, vendor coordination, inspections, and systems that reduce preventable risk.

  • Unit 10: Risk & Common Mistakes

    Study common failure modes—over-leverage, rehab overruns, vacancy shocks, liquidity traps, and behavioral errors—and how to mitigate them.

  • Unit 11: Investment Analysis

    Evaluate deals with NOI, cap rate, cash-on-cash, IRR (intro), required return frameworks, and sensitivity testing.

  • Unit 12: Advanced Debt & Risk Structuring

    Examine capital stack strategy, refinance risk, interest rate sensitivity, fixed vs floating debt structures, and how leverage volatility impacts IRR and long-term durability.

  • Unit 13: Commercial Real Estate Fundamentals

    Learn how commercial assets differ: lease structures (including NNN concepts), tenant improvement allowances, and commercial underwriting principles.

  • Unit 14: Development & Construction Economics

    Understand site acquisition, feasibility analysis, pro forma modeling, construction budgeting, contingencies, and structured exit strategy design.

  • Unit 15: Portfolio Strategy & Asset Scaling

    Move from individual deal evaluation to portfolio construction: diversification strategy, scaling logic, capital allocation, and long-term cash flow durability.

  • Unit 16: Institutional Capital Discipline

    Build written investment criteria, formalize an Investment Policy Statement (IPS), implement dry powder strategy, and develop behavioral discipline under market volatility.

  • Capstone: Strategic Real Estate Plan

    Apply the full track: select a market, define strategy, project 10-year IRR, run sensitivity analysis, design worst-case planning, establish risk mitigation, and justify capital allocation decisions.
