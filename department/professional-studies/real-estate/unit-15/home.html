Unit 15: Commercial Real Estate Fundamentals

Learn how the major commercial property sectors work economically. This unit introduces office, retail, industrial, and multifamily real estate, along with the lease structures and lease-duration metrics that shape income stability, rollover risk, and valuation.

Unit Overview

Commercial real estate is not a single category. Different property sectors operate under different demand drivers, lease conventions, capital needs, and risk profiles. An office building, shopping center, warehouse, and apartment community may all be income-producing assets, but they behave differently because tenant use, lease structure, space design, and market cycles differ across sectors.

This unit gives students a practical foundation for understanding the economics of the main commercial property types. Students learn how each sector earns income, what operating and leasing risks matter most, how lease structure shifts responsibility between landlord and tenant, and why lease duration metrics such as Weighted Average Lease Term help investors evaluate cash flow durability.

What You’ll Learn

  • How the major commercial property sectors differ economically
  • What drives office property performance and leasing risk
  • How retail real estate depends on tenant mix, location, and consumer demand
  • Why industrial real estate is shaped by logistics, functionality, and location efficiency
  • How multifamily properties generate income and respond to market conditions
  • How commercial lease structures allocate costs, risk, and control
  • How Weighted Average Lease Term helps measure lease rollover exposure and income durability

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 15.1: Overview of Commercial Property Types

    Survey the major commercial sectors, including office, retail, industrial, and multifamily, and compare how they differ in income patterns, tenant relationships, and investment behavior.

  • Lesson 15.2: Office Real Estate Economics

    Learn how office buildings earn income through tenant leasing, how vacancy and rollover affect performance, and why location, layout, and demand cycles matter deeply in office markets.

  • Lesson 15.3: Retail Real Estate Economics

    Examine how retail assets depend on trade area strength, tenant mix, co-tenancy dynamics, sales productivity, and the relationship between consumer behavior and rent sustainability.

  • Lesson 15.4: Industrial Real Estate Economics

    Understand the economics of warehouses and logistics facilities, including location efficiency, clear height, loading functionality, supply chain demand, and lease stability.

  • Lesson 15.5: Multifamily Real Estate Economics

    Study how apartment properties produce income through unit leasing, how turnover and expense management affect returns, and why multifamily often behaves differently from other commercial sectors.

  • Lesson 15.6: Commercial Lease Structures

    Learn the differences among gross leases, modified gross leases, and net leases, and see how lease structure changes expense responsibility, landlord exposure, and underwriting logic.

  • Lesson 15.7: Weighted Average Lease Term (WALT)

    Understand how WALT measures lease duration across a tenant base and why it matters for rollover risk, cash flow visibility, financing, and valuation.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to distinguish among major commercial property sectors, explain their different economic drivers, interpret basic lease structures, and use lease-duration concepts to evaluate cash flow quality and sector-specific risk.

  • Templates & Tools

    Use sector comparison worksheets, lease structure summaries, WALT calculators, and property-type review templates to analyze commercial real estate fundamentals.

  • Glossary Support

    Review key terms such as office, retail, industrial, multifamily, gross lease, triple net lease, tenant rollover, lease term, and WALT.
