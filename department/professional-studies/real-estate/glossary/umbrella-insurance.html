Umbrella Insurance

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Umbrella Insurance

Definition

Umbrella insurance is a liability policy that provides additional coverage above the limits of underlying insurance policies such as general liability insurance, auto liability, or employer’s liability.

It is designed to protect against large or catastrophic claims that exceed primary policy limits.

How It Works

Umbrella coverage sits “on top of” primary policies. Once the underlying policy limit is exhausted, the umbrella policy begins paying up to its own limit.

Example Structure

  • General Liability: $1,000,000 per occurrence
  • Umbrella Policy: $5,000,000 excess

If a claim results in $3,000,000 in covered damages, the general liability policy pays the first $1,000,000 and the umbrella covers the remaining $2,000,000.

Why It Matters for Real Estate Investors

Real estate ownership exposes investors to high-severity risk:

  • Major slip-and-fall injuries
  • Construction accidents
  • Fire-related injuries
  • Multi-party liability claims

A single serious incident can exceed standard liability limits. Umbrella insurance helps preserve capital and protect long-term assets.

Typical Coverage Limits

  • $1M increments (e.g., $1M, $2M, $5M, $10M+)
  • Larger portfolios often carry $10M–$25M+
  • Required limits may depend on lender or investor agreements

Umbrella vs. Excess Liability

  • Umbrella Policy: May broaden coverage beyond underlying policy in some cases.
  • Excess Policy: Strictly follows the underlying policy terms without expanding coverage.

Common Requirements

  • Underlying policy minimum limits must be maintained.
  • All scheduled properties must be listed.
  • Often required by lenders for multifamily and commercial assets.
  • May need to align with additional insured endorsements.

Example

A catastrophic accident at a retail center results in a $6 million jury award. The property’s $1M general liability policy is insufficient. The umbrella policy provides the additional $5M needed, protecting ownership from severe financial exposure.

Common Pitfalls

  • Failing to maintain required underlying limits.
  • Not updating umbrella limits as portfolio size grows.
  • Assuming umbrella covers excluded risks.
  • Ignoring lender insurance requirements.
  • Overlooking per-location aggregate limits.

Investor Checklist

  • Evaluate portfolio-wide exposure annually.
  • Align umbrella limits with asset size and risk profile.
  • Confirm underlying policies meet required minimums.
  • Review exclusions carefully.
  • Coordinate with insurance broker and legal counsel.

Related Terms

  • General liability insurance
  • Additional insured
  • Certificate of insurance
  • Hold harmless agreement
  • Waiver of subrogation
