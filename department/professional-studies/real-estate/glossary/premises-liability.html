Premises Liability

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Premises Liability

Definition

Premises liability is a legal doctrine that holds property owners and occupiers responsible for injuries or damages that occur on their property due to unsafe or hazardous conditions.

Core Legal Principle

Owners have a duty to maintain reasonably safe conditions and to warn visitors of known dangers that are not obvious.

Common Premises Liability Claims

  • Slip and fall accidents
  • Poor lighting in stairwells or parking lots
  • Broken handrails or uneven flooring
  • Snow and ice hazards
  • Negligent security claims

Visitor Categories

  • Invitees: Customers or tenants owed highest duty of care.
  • Licensees: Social guests owed reasonable warning of known risks.
  • Trespassers: Limited duty, except for willful harm.

Relationship to Negligence

Premises liability claims typically rely on proving negligence:

  • Duty of care existed
  • Owner breached that duty
  • Breach caused injury
  • Damages resulted

Insurance Protection

Most premises liability claims are covered under general liability insurance, often supplemented by umbrella insurance.

Risk Mitigation Strategies

  • Regular property inspections
  • Maintenance logs and documentation
  • Prompt hazard repair
  • Proper lighting and signage
  • Snow and ice removal contracts
  • Vendor insurance verification

Example

A tenant slips on a wet lobby floor without warning signage. If the property manager failed to address the hazard in a reasonable timeframe, the owner could face a premises liability claim.

Common Investor Mistakes

  • Delaying minor repairs
  • Failing to document inspections
  • Underinsuring liability exposure
  • Not requiring contractor insurance
  • Ignoring recurring safety complaints

Investor Checklist

  • Implement scheduled inspection protocols.
  • Maintain written maintenance records.
  • Verify tenant and vendor insurance compliance.
  • Align lease provisions with maintenance obligations.
  • Review liability coverage annually.

Strategic Insight

Premises liability is an operational risk — disciplined maintenance and documentation reduce exposure more effectively than reactive litigation defense.

Related Terms

  • Negligence
  • General liability insurance
  • Umbrella insurance
  • Risk allocation
  • Hold harmless agreement
