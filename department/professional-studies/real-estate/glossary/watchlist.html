Definition

A watchlist is a designation used by loan servicers to identify performing loans that exhibit signs of potential risk. While the borrower may still be current on payments, financial, operational, or market indicators suggest the loan warrants closer monitoring.

Who Places Loans on Watchlist?

In CMBS structures, the master servicer typically manages the watchlist for performing loans. If issues escalate or default becomes imminent, the loan may transfer to the special servicer.

Common Watchlist Triggers

  • Declining DSCR: Debt service coverage ratio approaching covenant thresholds.
  • Occupancy drops: Significant tenant rollover or anchor loss.
  • Upcoming maturity: Refinance risk due to market conditions.
  • Deferred maintenance: Capital needs impacting valuation.
  • Market weakness: Submarket rent pressure or oversupply.
  • Reporting concerns: Delays or inconsistencies in borrower submissions.

What Watchlist Status Means

  • Heightened scrutiny: Increased financial review and communication.
  • Additional reporting: More frequent or detailed updates may be required.
  • Limited flexibility: Consents may face greater review.
  • Early intervention: Servicer may recommend proactive steps before default.

Investor Implications

  • Early warning signal: Watchlist status flags refinance or cash flow risk.
  • Pricing indicator: May affect bond pricing in CMBS markets.
  • Opportunity creation: Distress cycles often begin with watchlist placements.
  • Underwriting discipline: Monitor covenant cushions and lease rollover exposure.

Example

A retail property with a CMBS loan experiences a 20% drop in occupancy due to tenant rollovers. DSCR declines to 1.15x from 1.35x the prior year. Although payments remain current, the master servicer places the loan on the watchlist due to elevated refinance risk at maturity.

Common Pitfalls

  • Ignoring early signs: Watchlist is not default, but it is a warning.
  • Underestimating refinance gaps: Market shifts can widen quickly.
  • Overlooking communication: Proactive borrower engagement improves outcomes.
  • Confusing with special servicing: Watchlist status does not mean default has occurred.
