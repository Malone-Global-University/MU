Development

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Development

Definition

Development is the process of creating new real estate improvements on land or substantially redeveloping existing property. It typically includes land acquisition, entitlement approvals, design, construction, financing, lease-up, and stabilization before sale or long-term hold.

Stages of Development

1. Land Acquisition

  • Site selection and market analysis
  • Purchase agreement and due diligence

2. Entitlements & Approvals

  • Zoning review and potential rezoning
  • Permits and governmental approvals
  • Environmental studies

3. Design & Planning

  • Architectural and engineering plans
  • Budget creation and refinement

4. Construction

  • Groundbreaking and vertical build
  • Cost monitoring and lender draw schedules

5. Lease-Up & Stabilization

  • Marketing and tenant placement
  • Achieving stabilized occupancy and NOI

6. Exit or Hold

  • Disposition to a long-term investor
  • Refinance and hold strategy

Types of Development

  • Ground-up: New construction from vacant land.
  • Redevelopment: Major repositioning or adaptive reuse.
  • Build-to-suit: Designed for a specific tenant.
  • Mixed-use: Combination of residential, retail, office, or hospitality.

Risk Factors

  • Entitlement risk: Regulatory approval delays.
  • Construction risk: Cost overruns or contractor issues.
  • Market risk: Demand shifts during multi-year timeline.
  • Financing risk: Interest rate volatility.
  • Lease-up risk: Slower-than-expected absorption.

Example

A developer acquires a 2-acre parcel for $3 million and invests $18 million to construct a 120-unit apartment complex. After stabilization, the project achieves $1.6 million in annual NOI and is sold at a 6% cap rate for approximately $26.7 million.

Investor Implications

  • Higher return potential: Often targets opportunistic returns.
  • Greater volatility: Multiple execution risks.
  • Capital intensity: Significant upfront equity required.
  • Cycle sensitivity: Profits depend heavily on timing.
