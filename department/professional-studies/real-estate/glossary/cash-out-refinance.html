Cash-Out Refinance

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Cash-Out Refinance

Definition

A Cash-Out Refinance is a refinancing transaction in which a new loan replaces existing debt and generates excess proceeds for the borrower. The additional cash typically results from increased property value, improved net operating income (NOI), or both.

How It Works

  • Property value increases through rent growth or renovations.
  • Lender underwrites a new loan at a higher valuation.
  • Existing loan is paid off.
  • Remaining proceeds are distributed to ownership.

Why Investors Use It

  • Return capital: repay initial equity contributions.
  • De-risk investment: recover invested cash while retaining ownership.
  • Fund new acquisitions: redeploy capital into additional projects.
  • Improve financing terms: lock in lower interest rates or longer maturities.

Example

An investor acquires a multifamily property for $10 million using $3 million in equity and $7 million in debt. After renovations, the property is valued at $13 million. A lender refinances at 70% loan-to-value (LTV), providing a new loan of $9.1 million. After paying off the original $7 million loan, approximately $2.1 million is distributed to investors.

Benefits

  • Unlocks trapped equity.
  • Maintains long-term ownership.
  • May offer tax advantages compared to sale proceeds.

Risks

  • Increased leverage: higher loan balance raises financial risk.
  • Market exposure: downturns may stress higher debt levels.
  • Refinance timing risk: interest rates may move unfavorably.

Strategic Considerations

  • Evaluate debt service coverage ratio (DSCR).
  • Stress-test cash flow at higher vacancy or rates.
  • Assess long-term hold strategy before increasing leverage.
  • Review partnership agreements regarding distributions.

Related Terms

  • Recapitalization
  • Leverage
  • Loan-to-Value (LTV)
  • Non-Recourse Loan
  • Net Operating Income (NOI)
