Make-Ready

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Make-Ready

Definition

Make-ready is the process of preparing a unit for the next resident after a move-out. It includes the standard “turn” work required to deliver a clean, safe, and rentable unit— and may include light upgrades if the business plan calls for it.

What Make-Ready Typically Includes

  • Inspection: document condition, identify damages, confirm scope.
  • Cleaning: full deep clean, appliances, bathrooms, floors.
  • Paint & patch: touch-up or full repaint depending on standards.
  • Minor repairs: fixtures, outlets/switch plates, doors/locks, blinds, caulking.
  • Maintenance checks: plumbing leaks, smoke/CO detectors, HVAC filters, water heater.
  • Final quality walk: confirm rent-ready condition before marketing/showings.

Make-Ready vs. Unit Turn vs. Renovation

  • Make-ready: baseline work to get a unit rent-ready (often “standard turn”).
  • Unit turn: umbrella term that includes make-ready and the turnover workflow (move-out → ready → re-leased).
  • Renovation (value-add): discretionary upgrades (e.g., new cabinets/counters) intended to increase rents— typically planned as CapEx.

Why It Matters

Make-ready performance drives vacancy loss and leasing velocity. Every extra day a unit sits not rent-ready is lost revenue, and inconsistent quality can lead to more concessions, weaker reviews, and higher churn.

Investor Uses

  • Underwriting downtime: assumptions for days vacant between tenants (economic vacancy).
  • Budgeting turns: forecasting recurring turn costs vs. value-add CapEx upgrades.
  • Operational KPIs: tracking turn time, cost per turn, and first-pass quality.
  • Revenue protection: minimizing “down units” and stabilizing occupancy.

Example

A property averages 12 move-outs per month. Each unit takes 8 days from move-out to rent-ready and then 7 days to re-lease, for a total of 15 days of downtime.

  • Total downtime days/month = 12 × 15 = 180 unit-days
  • If average market rent is $1,700/month, daily rent is roughly $1,700 ÷ 30 ≈ $56.67. That’s about $10,200/month of vacancy exposure (180 × $56.67), before concessions.

Cutting make-ready time from 8 days to 5 days reduces downtime by 36 unit-days/month (12 × 3), improving revenue and occupancy without changing rents.

Common Pitfalls

  • No standard: inconsistent “rent-ready” criteria creates rework and complaints.
  • Scope creep: turning every make-ready into a renovation blows budgets and schedules.
  • Vendor bottlenecks: paint/cleaning delays create down-units that compound quickly.
  • Weak scheduling: not pre-ordering materials or lining up trades before move-out.
  • Skipping QA: poor quality leads to immediate maintenance tickets and negative reviews.
