Loss to Lease

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Loss to Lease

Definition

Loss to Lease is the difference between a property’s current in-place rents and higher achievable market rents.

It represents unrealized income potential that can be captured as leases renew or units turn over.

Basic Formula

Loss to Lease = Market Rent – In-Place Rent

Example

A unit is currently leased at $1,700 per month, but comparable units are leasing for $1,900 per month.

  • Monthly loss to lease = $200
  • Annual loss to lease per unit = $2,400

If 50 units are under market by $200 per month, total annual embedded upside equals $120,000.

Why It Matters to Investors

  • Indicates embedded rent growth without relying on market appreciation
  • Supports value-add business plans
  • Improves Net Operating Income (NOI) over time
  • Can increase property valuation through NOI expansion

How It Occurs

  • Rapid market rent growth outpaces renewal increases
  • Long-term leases signed below current market
  • Conservative prior management
  • Lease structures with delayed increases

Underwriting Considerations

  • Analyze lease expiration schedule
  • Evaluate tenant turnover assumptions
  • Adjust for concessions and effective rent
  • Avoid assuming instant mark-to-market across all units

Common Pitfalls

  • Overestimating capture speed: Rent growth may require tenant turnover.
  • Ignoring market softening: Market rent may decline before renewal.
  • Underestimating renewal resistance: Tenants may negotiate or vacate.
  • Failing to account for concessions: Effective rent may differ from face rent.
