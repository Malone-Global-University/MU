Condominium

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Condominium

Definition

A Condominium (or “condo”) is a form of property ownership in which an individual owns a specific unit within a building or development, while jointly owning common areas—such as hallways, roofs, parking, and amenities— with other unit owners.

Ownership Structure

  • Individual ownership: exclusive title to a defined interior unit space.
  • Shared ownership: proportional interest in common areas.
  • Homeowners Association (HOA): governs rules, maintenance, and budgeting.

Condominium vs. Apartment

  • Condominium: individually owned units.
  • Apartment: entire building owned by a single landlord.

In a condo building, units may be owner-occupied or rented individually, depending on association rules.

Key Financial Components

  • HOA dues: monthly fees covering common maintenance and reserves.
  • Special assessments: additional charges for major repairs.
  • Reserves: funds set aside for long-term capital expenses.

Investor Considerations

  • Review HOA financial statements and reserve studies.
  • Understand rental restrictions or leasing caps.
  • Evaluate special assessment risk.
  • Assess market liquidity compared to multifamily assets.

Example

An investor purchases a condo unit in a 50-unit building. The investor owns the interior unit space but shares responsibility for the building’s roof and common areas. Monthly HOA dues fund maintenance and future capital improvements.

Advantages

  • Lower purchase price compared to detached homes.
  • Shared maintenance responsibility.
  • Urban market accessibility.

Risks

  • Limited control over association decisions.
  • Exposure to HOA mismanagement.
  • Special assessments impacting cash flow.

Related Terms

  • Homeowners Association (HOA)
  • Special Assessment
  • Reserve Study
  • Cooperative (Co-op)
