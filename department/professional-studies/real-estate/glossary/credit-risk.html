Credit Risk

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Credit Risk

Definition

Credit Risk is the potential that a borrower or tenant will fail to meet their debt obligations, resulting in a loss for the lender or investor. In real estate, credit risk is especially relevant for mortgages, commercial loans, and lease agreements.

Key Components

  • Borrower creditworthiness: assessed using credit history, income, assets, and experience.
  • Loan-to-value (LTV) ratio: higher LTV increases credit exposure.
  • Debt service coverage ratio (DSCR): measures ability to repay debt from property cash flow.
  • Collateral quality: the value and liquidity of underlying real estate.
  • Market and economic conditions: downturns can increase default risk.

Why It Matters

Lenders and investors evaluate credit risk to determine interest rates, loan terms, and approval likelihood. Proper assessment reduces losses and ensures the property generates predictable returns.

Example

A developer applies for a $5 million construction loan. The lender evaluates their financial statements, credit score, and projected cash flow from the property. If the lender believes the developer might default, they may charge higher interest, require more collateral, or deny the loan entirely.

Investor Uses

  • Assessing tenant or borrower reliability
  • Adjusting financing terms or pricing for risk
  • Structuring deals to protect equity and debt positions
  • Managing portfolio risk across multiple properties

Common Pitfalls

  • Overlooking macroeconomic factors that affect repayment ability
  • Ignoring borrower financial transparency
  • Failing to adjust for property-specific risks
  • Assuming historical performance guarantees future credit reliability

Related Terms

  • Loan-to-Value (LTV)
  • Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)
  • Default Risk
  • Underwriting
  • Cash Flow Before Taxes (CFBT)
