Right of First Offer (ROFO)

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Right of First Offer

Definition

A Right of First Offer (ROFO) gives a specified party the opportunity to make the first offer to purchase a property before the owner markets it to third parties.

How It Works

  1. Owner decides to sell the property.
  2. Owner notifies the ROFO holder.
  3. ROFO holder submits an offer within a specified period.
  4. If accepted, transaction proceeds.
  5. If rejected, owner may market the property to others.

ROFO vs ROFR

  • ROFO: Holder makes first offer before market exposure.
  • ROFR: Holder matches a third-party offer.

ROFO is generally viewed as less restrictive than ROFR because it does not require matching an outside offer.

Where It Appears

  • Joint venture agreements.
  • Partnership buy-sell agreements.
  • Commercial leases.
  • Adjacent land development arrangements.

Why It Matters

  • Provides acquisition opportunities to strategic partners.
  • Encourages early negotiation.
  • Maintains relationship alignment.
  • May streamline private transactions.

Negotiation Considerations

  • Time allowed to submit offer.
  • Definition of “fair market value.”
  • Exclusions for affiliate transfers.
  • Reset or re-offer provisions.

Transaction Impact

  • May delay marketing timeline.
  • Can reduce competitive bidding tension.
  • Provides preemptive negotiation leverage.

Example

A joint venture partner holds a ROFO. Before listing the property, the managing partner must allow the ROFO holder to submit an offer. If declined, the asset can then be publicly marketed.

Common Pitfalls

  • Ambiguous valuation methodology.
  • Unclear notice procedures.
  • Failure to define offer expiration timelines.
  • Misalignment with partnership agreements.

Investor Checklist

  • Identify ROFO rights during due diligence.
  • Understand timing obligations.
  • Evaluate effect on liquidity strategy.
  • Confirm legal enforceability.
