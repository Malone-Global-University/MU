Operating Agreement

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Operating Agreement

Definition

An operating agreement is a legally binding document that governs the ownership, management, capital structure, and decision-making framework of a limited liability company (LLC). In real estate investing, most properties are held in LLCs, making the operating agreement a core risk-management instrument.

Core Provisions

1. Ownership & Capital Contributions

  • Member ownership percentages
  • Initial capital contributions
  • Future capital call procedures

2. Governance & Control

  • Manager-managed vs. member-managed structure
  • Voting thresholds (majority, supermajority)
  • Authority for refinancing or disposition

3. Profit & Loss Allocation

  • Distribution waterfall structure
  • Preferred returns (if applicable)
  • Promote or carried interest provisions

4. Transfers & Exit

  • Restrictions on membership transfers
  • Buy-sell provisions
  • Dissolution procedures

Why It Matters

  • Risk containment: Clarifies liability and responsibilities.
  • Dispute prevention: Defines conflict resolution mechanisms.
  • Capital discipline: Establishes funding obligations.
  • Investor alignment: Ensures transparent profit allocation.

Example

A 3-member LLC acquires a $5 million apartment building. The operating agreement states:

  • 70% ownership for the capital partner
  • 30% ownership for the managing partner
  • 8% preferred return to capital partner
  • 70/30 profit split after preferred return

The agreement governs cash distributions and decision authority throughout the holding period.

Common Pitfalls

  • Ambiguous capital call language
  • Unclear voting thresholds
  • Failure to define dispute resolution
  • Ignoring buyout mechanisms

Investor Implications

  • Capital protection: Clear waterfall structure protects returns.
  • Control clarity: Defines who makes major financial decisions.
  • Exit certainty: Structured dissolution provisions reduce friction.
  • Reputation impact: Professional agreements increase credibility with lenders and investors.
