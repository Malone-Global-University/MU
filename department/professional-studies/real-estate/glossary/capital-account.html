Capital Account

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Capital Account

Definition

A capital account is an internal accounting record maintained for each partner or member in a partnership or LLC. It tracks that investor’s economic interest in the entity.

The capital account increases with contributions and allocated profits, and decreases with distributions and allocated losses.

Basic Structure

Beginning Capital Account

+ Capital Contributions + Allocated Net Income − Allocated Net Loss − Distributions = Ending Capital Account

Why Capital Accounts Matter

  • Determine economic ownership of partners.
  • Drive liquidation and exit distributions.
  • Ensure compliance with partnership tax rules.
  • Support complex waterfall structures.

Capital Account vs Cash Invested

A capital account is not the same as the amount of cash an investor originally contributed. It fluctuates over time based on allocations and distributions.

For example:

  • Initial investment: $100,000
  • Allocated profits over time: +$40,000
  • Cash distributions received: −$30,000

Ending Capital Account = $110,000

Role in Real Estate Syndications

In syndications, capital accounts are central to how preferred returns, promote structures, and return of capital are calculated.

  • Used in preferred return accrual calculations.
  • Impact distribution sequencing.
  • Determine residual split at sale.

Tax Considerations

  • Capital accounts must comply with IRS partnership regulations.
  • Often reported annually on Schedule K-1.
  • May differ from tax basis and outside basis.

Capital Account vs Tax Basis

While related, a capital account and an investor’s tax basis are not always identical.

  • Capital Account: Tracks economic allocations.
  • Tax Basis: Determines tax consequences of distributions and losses.

Common Pitfalls

  • Confusing capital account with ownership percentage.
  • Ignoring how promote structures impact allocations.
  • Failing to reconcile capital accounts upon exit.
  • Overlooking deficit restoration obligations (DROs).

Investor Checklist

  • Review operating agreement allocation provisions.
  • Understand preferred return mechanics.
  • Confirm waterfall references capital account balances.
  • Review annual K-1 capital account statements.
