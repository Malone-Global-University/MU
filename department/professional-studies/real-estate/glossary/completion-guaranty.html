Completion Guaranty

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Completion Guaranty

Definition

A Completion Guaranty is a legally binding agreement in which a sponsor or guarantor promises to complete a construction or development project in accordance with approved plans and budget. If the project runs over budget, the guarantor must contribute additional funds to ensure completion.

Purpose

  • Lender protection: ensures the project reaches stabilization.
  • Risk mitigation: reduces exposure to partially completed collateral.
  • Capital discipline: encourages accurate budgeting and oversight.

When It Is Required

  • Ground-up development loans.
  • Major renovation or repositioning projects.
  • Construction-to-permanent financing structures.

Scope of Liability

Completion guaranties typically cover:

  • Cost overruns beyond contingency reserves.
  • Unfinished construction at loan maturity.
  • Failure to deliver required certificates of occupancy.

Unlike a full recourse guaranty, liability is usually limited to the amount necessary to complete the project.

Example

A developer secures a $20 million construction loan to build a multifamily property. The approved budget includes a 7% contingency. Unexpected labor and materials increases cause costs to exceed budget by $1.5 million. Under the completion guaranty, the sponsor must contribute the additional funds to finish the project.

Why It Matters

  • Personal exposure: guarantors may face substantial liability.
  • Project feasibility: lenders rely on completion to protect collateral value.
  • Negotiation leverage: experienced sponsors may limit scope or duration.

Common Pitfalls

  • Underestimating construction risk.
  • Insufficient contingency reserves.
  • Failure to monitor contractor performance.
  • Assuming non-recourse loans eliminate completion liability.
