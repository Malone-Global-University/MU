Positive Leverage

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Positive Leverage

Definition

Positive leverage occurs when the cost of borrowed funds is lower than the property’s capitalization rate (cap rate), allowing debt to increase returns to equity investors.

Core Concept

Leverage enhances equity returns when the return on the asset exceeds the cost of debt. The difference between the two is known as the positive spread.

Simple Example

Property Cap Rate: 7%
Loan Interest Rate: 5%

Because the property yields 7% and debt costs 5%, the 2% spread increases the return on invested equity.

Mathematical Relationship

If:
Cap Rate > Cost of Debt → Positive Leverage

If:
Cap Rate < Cost of Debt → Negative Leverage

Impact on Investors

  • Higher cash-on-cash returns.
  • Increased internal rate of return (IRR).
  • Greater return on equity (ROE).
  • Magnified upside performance.

When It Occurs

  • Low interest rate environments.
  • Value-add strategies increasing NOI.
  • Conservative acquisition pricing.
  • Fixed-rate long-term debt structures.

Risk Considerations

  • Leverage amplifies downside as well as upside.
  • Refinancing risk at maturity.
  • Market cap rate expansion risk.
  • Over-leveraging during economic cycles.

Strategic Use

  • Optimize loan-to-value (LTV).
  • Lock favorable interest rates.
  • Maintain healthy debt service coverage ratio (DSCR).
  • Model conservative exit assumptions.

Temporary vs Structural Positive Leverage

  • Temporary: Created through operational improvements.
  • Structural: Sustained long-term spread between yield and debt cost.

Investor Checklist

  • Compare cap rate to all-in borrowing cost.
  • Stress test interest rate increases.
  • Evaluate exit cap rate sensitivity.
  • Ensure adequate liquidity reserves.

Related Terms

  • Negative Leverage
  • Capitalization Rate (Cap Rate)
  • Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
  • Loan-to-Value (LTV)
  • Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)
