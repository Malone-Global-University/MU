Foreclosure

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Foreclosure

Definition

Foreclosure is the legal process through which a lender takes possession of a property after the borrower defaults on a loan secured by that property. It is a remedy that allows the lender to recover unpaid debt.

How Foreclosure Works

  • Default occurs (missed payments or covenant breach).
  • Notice of default is issued.
  • Cure period may be provided.
  • Legal process begins (judicial or non-judicial depending on state law).
  • Property sale occurs to satisfy outstanding debt.

Judicial vs. Non-Judicial Foreclosure

  • Judicial: Requires court involvement and formal litigation.
  • Non-Judicial: Conducted through a power-of-sale clause without court proceedings.

Investor Implications

  • Loss of equity if property value declines below loan balance.
  • Damage to borrower creditworthiness.
  • Potential acquisition opportunities for distressed buyers.
  • Legal costs and timeline uncertainty.

Risk Management

  • Maintain conservative leverage ratios.
  • Stress-test cash flow under vacancy scenarios.
  • Monitor loan covenants regularly.
  • Preserve liquidity reserves for downturns.

Related Terms

  • Default
  • Loan Covenant
  • Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)
  • Short Sale
  • Real Estate Owned (REO)
