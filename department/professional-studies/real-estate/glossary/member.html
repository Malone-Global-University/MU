Definition

A Member is an owner of a Limited Liability Company (LLC). In real estate investing, properties are often held in LLCs, and members hold equity interests in those entities.

Members share in profits, losses, distributions, and governance rights according to the LLC’s Operating Agreement.

Types of Members

  • Managing Member: Has authority to operate and make decisions.
  • Non-Managing Member: Passive investor without daily control.
  • Majority Member: Holds controlling ownership interest.
  • Minority Member: Holds a smaller, often non-controlling interest.

Member Rights

  • Share in profits and losses
  • Receive cash distributions
  • Access financial reporting
  • Vote on major decisions (if provided)
  • Protection from personal liability (limited to investment amount)

Why LLC Membership Matters in Real Estate

  • Liability protection: Shields personal assets from property-level risks.
  • Tax flexibility: Pass-through taxation avoids corporate double taxation.
  • Capital structuring: Allows flexible ownership splits and profit waterfalls.
  • Investor alignment: Clarifies control and economic participation.

Example

A real estate LLC acquires a $10 million multifamily property. One sponsor owns 30% as managing member, and several passive investors collectively own 70% as non-managing members.

Profits are distributed according to the operating agreement, often including a preferred return and promote structure.

Common Pitfalls

  • Unclear operating agreements: Ambiguity can cause disputes.
  • Lack of voting protections: Minority members may lack control.
  • Improper capitalization: Failing to document capital contributions correctly.
  • Ignoring transfer restrictions: Membership interests may have resale limitations.

Related Terms
