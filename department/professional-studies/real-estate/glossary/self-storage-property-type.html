Self-Storage (Property Type)

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Self-Storage (Property Type)

Definition

Self-storage is a specialized real estate property type consisting of individual storage units rented to consumers or businesses for short- or medium-term use. Tenants typically lease units month-to-month.

Property Characteristics

  • Climate-controlled and non-climate-controlled units
  • Drive-up or interior corridor access
  • Security systems (gated entry, cameras, coded access)
  • Minimal tenant improvements
  • Low ongoing maintenance requirements

Revenue Model

  • Monthly rent per unit
  • Ancillary income: Insurance, late fees, admin fees, retail sales (locks, boxes)
  • Dynamic pricing: Frequent rate adjustments based on occupancy

Performance Drivers

  • Population growth and mobility
  • Residential density
  • Life events (moving, downsizing, divorce, death)
  • Business storage demand
  • Local supply pipeline

Advantages

  • Short lease terms: Ability to adjust rents quickly.
  • Low operating expenses: Compared to other property types.
  • Recession resilience: Demand often driven by life transitions.
  • Minimal buildout costs: No heavy tenant improvements.

Risks

  • New supply sensitivity: Rapid overbuilding can reduce rents.
  • Short-term tenancy: Higher turnover.
  • Revenue volatility: Month-to-month leasing.
  • Location dependence: Strong micro-market influence.

Example

A 70,000-square-foot self-storage facility operating at 92% occupancy can quickly adjust rental rates if local demand increases, improving NOI without long-term lease constraints.

Underwriting Considerations

  • Occupancy stabilization timeline
  • Average rent per square foot
  • Local competition within a 3–5 mile radius
  • Operating expense ratio
  • Revenue growth sensitivity

Investor Applications

  • Market density analysis: Evaluate population per facility.
  • Dynamic pricing strategy: Adjust rates by unit size and demand.
  • Operational efficiency review: Leverage automation systems.
  • Supply monitoring: Track new construction permits.
