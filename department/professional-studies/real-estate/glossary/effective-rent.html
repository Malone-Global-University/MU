Effective Rent

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Effective Rent

Definition

Effective Rent is the actual economic rent received by a landlord after accounting for concessions, such as free rent, tenant improvement allowances, or other leasing incentives.

It reflects the true income generated by a lease rather than the advertised or face rent.

Face Rent vs. Effective Rent

  • Face (or Gross) Rent: The stated rent in the lease agreement.
  • Effective Rent: Face rent minus the value of concessions, averaged over the lease term.

Basic Formula

Effective Rent = (Total Lease Payments – Total Concessions) ÷ Lease Term

Example

A tenant signs a 12-month lease at $2,000 per month but receives 1 month free.

  • Total scheduled rent = $2,000 × 12 = $24,000
  • Free rent value = $2,000
  • Total received = $22,000
  • Effective rent = $22,000 ÷ 12 = $1,833 per month

Why It Matters to Investors

  • Accurately measures true income for underwriting
  • Reveals market softness masked by high face rents
  • Impacts Net Operating Income (NOI)
  • Affects valuation and refinancing outcomes

Market Implications

In competitive or oversupplied markets, landlords may maintain high face rents but increase concessions to attract tenants. Effective rent often provides a more accurate indicator of real market conditions.

Common Pitfalls

  • Ignoring concessions in underwriting
  • Assuming face rent equals market rent
  • Not annualizing short-term incentives properly
  • Failing to track renewal concessions
