Economic Cycle

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Economic Cycle

Definition

The economic cycle refers to the recurring phases of expansion, peak, contraction (recession), and recovery in overall economic activity. These cycles influence employment, income growth, capital markets, and ultimately real estate performance.

The Four Phases

1. Expansion

  • Strong job growth and consumer spending
  • Rising rents and occupancy
  • Cap rate compression
  • Increased development activity

2. Peak

  • Economic growth slows
  • Supply may outpace demand
  • Construction pipelines are elevated
  • Credit conditions begin tightening

3. Contraction (Recession)

  • Declining employment and spending
  • Rising vacancy rates
  • Rent stagnation or decline
  • Cap rate expansion and reduced transaction volume

4. Recovery

  • Stabilizing employment and demand
  • Gradual occupancy improvement
  • Limited new supply due to prior slowdown
  • Early acquisition opportunities

Impact on Real Estate

Real estate typically lags broader economic shifts because of lease structures, development timelines, and capital market friction. However, the economic cycle strongly influences:

  • Rental growth
  • Occupancy levels
  • Cap rates and valuations
  • Debt availability and pricing

Example

During an expansion phase, multifamily rents may grow 5% annually. When recession hits, job losses reduce demand, rent growth slows or turns negative, and cap rates rise—lowering property values even if the asset remains operational.

Investor Strategy by Phase

  • Expansion: Develop or reposition assets.
  • Peak: Lock in long-term leases and reduce leverage.
  • Contraction: Preserve liquidity and acquire discounted assets.
  • Recovery: Acquire undervalued assets ahead of rent growth.

Common Pitfalls

  • Timing overconfidence: Cycles are difficult to predict precisely.
  • Overleveraging at peak: Amplifies downside risk.
  • Ignoring supply lag: Construction initiated during expansion may deliver in recession.
