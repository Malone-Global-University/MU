Pro Rata Share

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Pro Rata Share

Definition

Pro rata share is a tenant’s proportional share of a property, typically based on rentable square footage (RSF). It is used to allocate shared costs and expense reimbursements such as CAM, real estate taxes, insurance, and other operating expenses in commercial leases.

How It’s Calculated

Pro rata share is usually expressed as a percentage. The core idea is simple: the tenant pays their share of expenses based on how much space they occupy relative to the whole property.

Common formula

  • Pro Rata Share = Tenant RSF ÷ Total RSF (as defined in the lease)

The Denominator Is the Whole Game

“Total RSF” sounds straightforward, but leases can define the denominator differently—and that changes who pays what. Common denominator definitions include:

  • Total rentable area of the property: fixed building size (common in office).
  • Total leasable area of the center: may include or exclude certain pads/outparcels (common in retail).
  • Leased area (variable): denominator changes with occupancy; often paired with a gross-up clause.
  • Allocations by category: some expenses are allocated differently (e.g., parking lot costs shared by retail tenants but not a separately metered pad tenant).

Where Pro Rata Share Shows Up

  • CAM allocations: tenants pay their share of common area maintenance. (CAM)
  • Taxes and insurance: common in NNN structures.
  • Operating expense reimbursements: monthly estimates plus annual reconciliation. (Expense Reimbursements)
  • Common utilities/services: shared utilities, security, janitorial, and similar (as allowed by lease).

Gross-Up and Occupancy Effects

In properties with vacancy, some leases include a gross-up provision that adjusts certain expenses as if the property were at a stabilized occupancy (for example 95%). This prevents remaining tenants from paying an unfairly large share of expenses that are driven by occupancy (like janitorial in common areas).

Gross-up rules can materially change reimbursements, so underwriters should model them explicitly when vacancy is present.

Example

A tenant leases 7,500 RSF in a retail center with 75,000 RSF total rentable area. Annual CAM is $300,000.

  • Pro Rata Share = 7,500 ÷ 75,000 = 10%
  • Tenant CAM = 10% × $300,000 = $30,000/year
  • Monthly estimate ≈ $30,000 ÷ 12 = $2,500/month

Why It Matters

Pro rata share determines how operating expenses flow through to tenants. For owners, it affects recoverability and the stability of net income. For tenants, it affects all-in occupancy cost. For investors, small definitional differences can cause major underwriting errors.

Investor Uses

  • Lease review: confirm the pro rata definition, denominator, and whether outparcels/pads are included.
  • Reimbursement modeling: estimate recoveries accurately, including gross-up mechanics and caps.
  • Tenant risk assessment: high operating expense loads can pressure renewals and drive concessions.
  • Dispute prevention: clean definitions and documentation reduce CAM/expense challenges and audit risk.

Common Pitfalls

  • Wrong denominator assumption: underwriting “total building RSF” when the lease uses “leased RSF” can materially change recoveries.
  • Pad/outparcel exceptions: some tenants are excluded from certain costs; don’t assume everyone shares everything.
  • Expense category mismatches: different categories can have different allocation rules.
  • Ignoring caps: CAM caps can limit recoverability even if pro rata share is clear.
  • No gross-up awareness: vacancy without gross-up can shift more expense to the landlord than expected.
