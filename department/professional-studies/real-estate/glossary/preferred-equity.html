Preferred Equity

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Preferred Equity

Definition

Preferred equity is a capital position in a real estate deal that sits above common equity but below all forms of debt in the capital stack.

Preferred equity investors typically receive:

  • A fixed or structured preferred return
  • Priority in distributions over common equity
  • Priority in return of capital upon refinance or sale

Capital Stack Position

From lowest risk to highest risk (typical structure):

  • Senior Debt
  • Mezzanine Debt
  • Preferred Equity
  • Common Equity

Preferred equity is subordinate to lenders but senior to common equity investors.

How Returns Are Structured

  • Fixed coupon-style return (e.g., 10% annually)
  • Current pay or accrual structure
  • May include upside participation
  • Often structured with limited voting rights

Preferred Equity vs Mezzanine Debt

  • Mezzanine Debt: Structured as a loan, often secured by equity pledges.
  • Preferred Equity: Structured as an ownership interest with priority economics.

Mezzanine lenders typically have stronger enforcement remedies than preferred equity investors.

Why Sponsors Use Preferred Equity

  • Bridge funding gaps without increasing senior loan balance.
  • Reduce required common equity contribution.
  • Maintain control while obtaining structured capital.

Risk Profile

  • Higher risk than senior and mezzanine debt.
  • Lower risk than common equity.
  • Dependent on project cash flow and exit value.
  • Limited foreclosure rights compared to lenders.

Underwriting Considerations

  • Projected cash flow coverage.
  • Subordination terms in loan documents.
  • Intercreditor or subordination agreements.
  • Exit assumptions and refinance risk.

Common Pitfalls

  • Confusing preferred equity with preferred return.
  • Overleveraging the capital stack.
  • Ignoring intercreditor restrictions.
  • Underestimating enforcement limitations.

Investor Checklist

  • Review capital stack and subordination terms.
  • Understand payment structure (current vs accrual).
  • Analyze sponsor track record.
  • Stress test exit valuation assumptions.
