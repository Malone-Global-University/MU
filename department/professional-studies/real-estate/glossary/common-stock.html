Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Common Stock

Definition

Common stock represents residual ownership in a corporation. Common shareholders participate in profits after all debts and preferred equity holders are paid.

It is the most junior position in a traditional corporate capital structure.

Key Characteristics

  • Voting rights on major corporate matters
  • Potential dividend payments (not guaranteed)
  • Residual claim on assets upon liquidation
  • Highest risk and highest potential upside

Position in the Capital Stack

Typical corporate capital stack (top = lowest risk):

  • Senior Debt
  • Subordinated Debt
  • Preferred Equity
  • Common Stock

Common stockholders are paid last in both distributions and liquidation.

How Returns Are Generated

  • Dividends (if declared by the board)
  • Appreciation in share value
  • Capital gains upon sale

Common Stock in Real Estate

In real estate investment structures (including REITs and development entities), common stock often represents the sponsor’s or public shareholders’ equity interest.

  • Receives profits after debt and preferred layers
  • Absorbs losses first
  • Captures upside beyond preferred returns

Common Stock vs Common Equity

  • Common Stock: Corporate share ownership instrument.
  • Common Equity: Broader term for residual ownership, including LLC interests.

Risk Profile

  • Most exposed to operational volatility
  • Dependent on property performance and leverage
  • No guaranteed return
  • Subject to market fluctuations

Underwriting Considerations

  • Projected cash flow
  • Leverage levels
  • Exit valuation assumptions
  • Dividend sustainability

Common Pitfalls

  • Assuming dividends are guaranteed
  • Ignoring dilution risk
  • Overlooking subordination to debt and preferred layers
  • Confusing book value with market value

Investor Checklist

  • Review capital structure hierarchy
  • Analyze leverage risk
  • Evaluate management track record
  • Assess long-term growth prospects
