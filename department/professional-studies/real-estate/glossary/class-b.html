Class B

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Class B

Definition

Class B properties are mid-tier real estate assets that are typically older than Class A properties but remain well-maintained and functional. They are usually located in solid, established markets but not in the most prime or trophy locations.

Typical Characteristics

  • 10–30+ years old (varies by market)
  • Functional layouts and moderate finishes
  • Stable but not premium rental rates
  • Good occupancy levels
  • Potential for operational improvements

By Property Type

  • Multifamily: Workforce housing with moderate amenities.
  • Office: Older but serviceable buildings in secondary submarkets.
  • Retail: Established neighborhood centers without luxury positioning.
  • Industrial: Functional but not state-of-the-art logistics facilities.

Investment Profile

  • Moderate risk / moderate return
  • Higher cap rates than Class A
  • Value-add potential
  • Appeals to workforce or mid-market tenants

Advantages

  • Opportunity to renovate and increase rents
  • Stronger yield compared to Class A
  • Stable tenant base in established areas
  • Often less volatile than Class C assets

Risks

  • Capital expenditure needs: Aging systems and deferred maintenance.
  • Competition: Pressure from new Class A developments.
  • Economic sensitivity: Workforce tenant exposure.

Example

A 1998-built apartment complex in a strong suburban neighborhood with limited amenities and below-luxury rent levels would typically be classified as Class B.

Class A vs. Class B vs. Class C

  • Class A: Newer, prime, premium rents.
  • Class B: Mid-tier, stable, moderate rents.
  • Class C: Older, lower rents, higher operational risk.

Investor Applications

  • Value-add strategy: Renovate to push rents toward upper-tier levels.
  • Core-plus strategy: Blend stability with upside potential.
  • Income generation: Higher yield than Class A with manageable risk.
  • Portfolio diversification: Balance between growth and stability.
