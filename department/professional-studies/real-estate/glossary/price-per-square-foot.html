Price Per Square Foot

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Price Per Square Foot

Definition

Price per square foot (PSF) is a valuation metric calculated by dividing a property’s purchase price by its total square footage. It allows investors to compare pricing across properties of different sizes.

Formula

  • Price Per Square Foot = Purchase Price ÷ Total Square Footage

Example

A retail property sells for $6,000,000 and contains 20,000 square feet.

  • Price Per Square Foot = 6,000,000 ÷ 20,000 = $300 per SF

Investors compare this number with recent comparable sales (“comps”) to determine whether the asset is priced at, above, or below market.

Where It’s Commonly Used

  • Office properties
  • Retail centers
  • Industrial warehouses
  • Multifamily (in addition to price per unit)
  • Single-family home markets

Why It Matters

Price per square foot provides a quick snapshot of market pricing trends. It helps investors:

  • Benchmark acquisitions against recent sales
  • Assess relative value within a submarket
  • Identify pricing premiums for location or renovations
  • Evaluate redevelopment or repositioning potential

Limitations

While useful for comparison, PSF does not measure investment performance. It ignores:

  • Income and NOI
  • Operating expenses
  • Lease terms and tenant quality
  • Building efficiency and layout
  • Capital expenditure needs

Two properties may have identical PSF pricing but vastly different income profiles and risk levels.

Investor Perspective

Professional investors use price per square foot as a comparison metric, not a valuation method. Ultimately, income drives value in commercial real estate. PSF is most powerful when paired with cap rate, NOI, and rent per square foot analysis.

Common Pitfalls

  • Mixing gross and rentable SF: Be consistent when comparing comps.
  • Ignoring ceiling heights and functionality: Industrial assets vary greatly in utility.
  • Overlooking build quality: Class A properties command higher PSF pricing.
  • Comparing different markets: Urban core pricing differs significantly from suburban.
