Impact Fees

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Impact Fees

Definition

Impact Fees are charges imposed by local governments on developers to help fund public infrastructure and services required due to new development.

In simple terms: developers pay fees to cover the cost of growth.

Purpose of Impact Fees

  • Expand roadways and transportation systems.
  • Fund schools and public facilities.
  • Upgrade water and sewer infrastructure.
  • Support parks and emergency services.

When They Are Paid

  • At building permit issuance.
  • Before certificate of occupancy.
  • As part of development approval conditions.

How Impact Fees Are Calculated

  • Per residential unit.
  • Per square foot of commercial space.
  • Based on trip generation (traffic impact).
  • Utility capacity demand.

Impact on Development Budgets

  • Increase total project costs.
  • Affect land residual value.
  • Influence feasibility and IRR projections.
  • May alter optimal density decisions.

Impact Fees vs. Property Taxes

  • Impact Fees: One-time development-related charges.
  • Property Taxes: Recurring annual taxes based on assessed value.

Example

A municipality charges $12,000 per multifamily unit in impact fees. A 200-unit development would owe $2.4 million in impact fees, directly affecting total project capitalization.

Negotiation and Credits

  • Fee credits for infrastructure improvements provided by developer.
  • Fee waivers in targeted redevelopment areas.
  • Incentives in Opportunity Zones or urban infill districts.

Risks

  • Unexpected fee increases during entitlement.
  • Policy changes affecting project underwriting.
  • Delayed approvals impacting financing timelines.

Investor Checklist

  • Confirm impact fee schedule early in due diligence.
  • Model fee escalations in underwriting.
  • Explore credits or abatements.
  • Evaluate effect on break-even rents.
  • Assess impact on land acquisition price.
