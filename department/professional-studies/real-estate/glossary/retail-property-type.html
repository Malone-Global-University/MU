Retail (Property Type)

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Retail (Property Type)

Definition

Retail is a commercial real estate property type designed for businesses that sell goods or services directly to consumers. Retail assets generate income primarily through leases with tenants such as grocery stores, restaurants, apparel shops, and service providers.

Common Retail Formats

  • Neighborhood Strip Center: Small centers serving local communities.
  • Power Center: Large-format tenants and national retailers.
  • Regional Mall: Enclosed shopping centers with anchor tenants.
  • Lifestyle Center: Open-air retail with experiential focus.
  • Single-Tenant Net Lease: Standalone property leased to one tenant.

Lease Structures

  • Triple Net (NNN): Tenant pays taxes, insurance, and maintenance.
  • Percentage Rent: Base rent plus a percentage of sales.
  • Gross Lease: Landlord covers certain operating expenses.

Key Performance Drivers

  • Consumer spending trends
  • Tenant sales performance
  • Foot traffic and location quality
  • Anchor tenant strength
  • E-commerce competition

Risks

  • Tenant default: Retailers may be sensitive to economic downturns.
  • Co-tenancy risk: Loss of anchor tenant may trigger rent reductions.
  • Changing consumer behavior: Shift toward online shopping.
  • Lease rollover concentration: Multiple tenants expiring at once.

Example

A grocery-anchored strip center with long-term leases to national tenants may offer stable income, while a fashion-focused mall may be more exposed to economic cycles and shifting consumer preferences.

Investor Applications

  • Sales analysis: Review tenant sales per square foot.
  • Credit evaluation: Assess tenant financial strength.
  • Anchor dependency review: Analyze exposure to major tenants.
  • Market study: Evaluate trade area demographics.
