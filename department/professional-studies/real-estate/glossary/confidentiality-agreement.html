Confidentiality Agreement

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Confidentiality Agreement

Definition

A Confidentiality Agreement (often called a Non-Disclosure Agreement or NDA) is a legal contract that protects sensitive information shared between parties during a real estate transaction.

When It Is Used

  • Before receiving offering memoranda
  • Prior to accessing financial statements or rent rolls
  • During due diligence
  • When discussing off-market opportunities

What It Typically Covers

  • Financial data and projections
  • Tenant information
  • Lease terms
  • Operational strategies
  • Purchase price discussions

Key Provisions

  • Non-Disclosure: Information cannot be shared with unauthorized parties.
  • Non-Circumvention: Buyer cannot bypass broker or seller.
  • Use Restriction: Information may only be used to evaluate the transaction.
  • Term: Duration of confidentiality obligation.

Why It Matters to Investors

  • Builds trust in competitive processes
  • Protects sensitive tenant relationships
  • Reduces legal exposure
  • Maintains professionalism in negotiations

Example

A sponsor signs a confidentiality agreement before receiving detailed rent rolls and trailing twelve-month financials for a 300-unit apartment offering.

Common Pitfalls

  • Overly restrictive non-circumvention clauses
  • Ambiguous definition of “confidential information”
  • Unlimited duration obligations
  • Failing to involve legal counsel

Related Terms

  • Due Diligence
  • Offering Memorandum (OM)
  • Sponsor
  • Letter of Intent (LOI)
  • Purchase and Sale Agreement (PSA)
