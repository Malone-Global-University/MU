Negative Leverage

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Negative Leverage

Definition

Negative leverage occurs when the cost of borrowed funds exceeds the property’s capitalization rate (cap rate), causing leverage to reduce rather than enhance returns to equity investors.

Core Concept

Leverage increases equity returns only when the return on the property exceeds the cost of debt. When borrowing costs are higher than the property yield, the spread becomes negative.

Simple Example

Property Cap Rate: 5%
Loan Interest Rate: 7%

Because the debt costs 7% and the asset yields 5%, borrowing reduces overall return to equity — creating negative leverage.

Mathematical Relationship

If:
Cap Rate < Cost of Debt → Negative Leverage

If:
Cap Rate > Cost of Debt → Positive Leverage

Impact on Investors

  • Reduced cash-on-cash returns.
  • Lower internal rate of return (IRR).
  • Increased risk exposure.
  • Heightened refinancing risk.

When It Happens

  • Rising interest rate environments.
  • Low cap rate markets.
  • Overpriced acquisitions.
  • Floating-rate debt structures.

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider lower leverage levels.
  • Negotiate better financing terms.
  • Focus on value-add strategies to increase NOI.
  • Model conservative exit cap rates.

Temporary vs Structural Negative Leverage

  • Temporary: Short-term while executing value-add plan.
  • Structural: Persistent mismatch between yield and debt cost.

Risk Amplification

Leverage magnifies outcomes. When the spread is negative, debt accelerates downside exposure rather than upside returns.

Investor Checklist

  • Compare cap rate to all-in debt cost.
  • Stress test rising rate scenarios.
  • Evaluate refinance assumptions.
  • Model equity returns under multiple leverage levels.

Related Terms

  • Positive Leverage
  • Capitalization Rate (Cap Rate)
  • Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
  • Loan-to-Value (LTV)
  • Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)
