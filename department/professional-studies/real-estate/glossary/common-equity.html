Common Equity

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Common Equity

Definition

Common equity is the most junior ownership interest in a real estate investment. It sits at the bottom of the capital stack and receives residual cash flow and profits after all senior obligations — including debt and preferred equity — are satisfied.

Position in the Capital Stack

  • Senior Debt: Paid first
  • Mezzanine / Preferred Equity: Paid after senior debt
  • Common Equity: Paid last (highest risk, highest upside)

Return Structure

Common equity investors typically receive:

  • Pro rata share of distributable cash flow
  • Residual profits after preferred returns are paid
  • Participation in appreciation upon sale

Example

A property is acquired for $20,000,000:

  • $12,000,000 Senior Debt
  • $3,000,000 Preferred Equity (8% pref)
  • $5,000,000 Common Equity

Cash flow first services debt, then pays the preferred return. Remaining profits are distributed to common equity investors, often according to a negotiated waterfall structure.

Risk Profile

  • Highest risk position in the structure
  • First to absorb losses if value declines
  • Unlimited upside if property performs well

Waterfall Structures

Common equity distributions are often governed by a waterfall agreement that may include:

  • Preferred return hurdles
  • Catch-up provisions
  • Promote structures for sponsors
  • IRR-based tiers

Investor Perspective

Common equity investors must underwrite carefully, as their returns depend on both operational performance and exit valuation. Conservative assumptions and downside protection are critical at this level of the capital stack.

Common Pitfalls

  • Underestimating risk relative to debt holders
  • Misunderstanding waterfall structures
  • Overleveraging the capital stack
  • Relying on optimistic exit assumptions
