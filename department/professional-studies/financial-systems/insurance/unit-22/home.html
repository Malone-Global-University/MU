Unit 22: Claims Settlement, Subrogation, and Recovery

Learn how insurers move from approved claim decisions to final payment, file closure, and post-settlement recovery activity. This unit introduces the workflow that completes the claims process while also protecting insurer interests through subrogation, salvage, and third-party recovery operations.

Where This Unit Fits

This unit completes Layer 4: Execution Workflows. After studying claims intake, investigation, adjustment, and settlement decisions, students now examine the final execution stage of the claims lifecycle. This is where approved claim outcomes are paid, files are closed, and recovery rights are pursued when losses can be shifted back to responsible third parties.

Understanding this workflow prepares students for later units covering reserving, fraud controls, compliance, and operational performance measurement. It also connects directly to earlier claims units by showing how investigation and settlement decisions translate into financial completion and recovery activity.

Unit Overview

Once a claim decision has been approved, insurers must process payment accurately, document settlement completion, and close the claim file in a controlled way. But the claims process does not always end with payment alone. In some cases, insurers may seek reimbursement from responsible third parties, recover value through salvage activity, or exercise subrogation rights after indemnifying the policyholder.

This unit introduces the workflow used in claims settlement and recovery operations, including claims payment processing, file closure, recovery from third parties, subrogation procedures, salvage operations, and financial reconciliation of recoveries. The emphasis is practical: how insurers complete claims responsibly while preserving recovery opportunities and maintaining accurate financial records.

Why This Matters in Insurance Operations

Claims operations are not complete until payment is accurate, settlement records are finalized, and any available recovery rights are addressed. If settlement execution is weak, insurers may overpay, under-document, or create disputes after the claim is supposedly closed. If recovery opportunities are missed, insurers may absorb losses that should have been recovered from responsible parties or through salvage value.

Understanding this workflow helps insurance professionals interpret how claim payments are processed, why final closure requires careful documentation, how subrogation protects insurer interests after indemnity is paid, and why recovery reconciliation matters for accurate financial reporting. This unit provides the final execution logic of the claims process.

What You’ll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How claims payments are processed after settlement decisions are approved
  • How settlement completion and file closure finalize the claim lifecycle
  • How insurers pursue recovery from third parties after paying covered losses
  • How subrogation rights allow insurers to step into the insured’s position for recovery purposes
  • How salvage operations recover value from damaged property or residual assets
  • Why recovery tracking and reconciliation are essential to accurate claims finance and reporting

Operational Competencies

  • Identify the major stages of claims settlement and recovery workflows
  • Explain how approved claim amounts are processed into payments and completed settlements
  • Recognize when third-party recovery or subrogation may apply after a loss
  • Understand how salvage activity affects final claim economics
  • Interpret how recovery records and reconciliations support controlled claim closure

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • How does an approved claim become a completed payment and closed file?
  • When can an insurer recover money from another party after paying a claim?
  • What is the operational difference between subrogation and salvage?
  • Why must recovery activity be tracked and reconciled after settlement is complete?

Lessons in This Unit

Settlement Completion

  • Lesson 22.1: Claims Payment Processing

    Learn how insurers convert approved settlement decisions into controlled payment activity through disbursement, approval, and financial processing workflows.

  • Lesson 22.2: Settlement Completion and File Closure

    Study how insurers finalize claims, document completed settlements, and close files once payment and required processing steps are complete.

  • Lesson 22.3: Recovery from Third Parties

    Examine how insurers seek reimbursement from other responsible parties after paying losses that may be recoverable beyond the original claim settlement.

  • Lesson 22.4: Subrogation Rights and Procedures

    Understand how insurers exercise subrogation rights to pursue recovery after indemnifying the insured under covered policy terms.

Recovery Operations and Financial Control

  • Lesson 22.5: Salvage and Recovery Operations

    Learn how insurers recover residual value through salvage processes and related recovery operations tied to damaged property and claim settlements.

  • Lesson 22.6: Recovery Tracking and Financial Reconciliation

    Study how insurers track recovered amounts, reconcile recovery balances, and maintain accurate financial records after settlement and subrogation activity.

  • Lesson 22.7: The Claims Recovery Framework

    Connect payment processing, settlement completion, subrogation, salvage, and recovery reconciliation into one practical framework for final claims execution and recovery operations.

Connected Units

  • Unit 21: Claims Adjustment and Settlement Decisions

    Build on claims decision-making by examining how approved outcomes are paid, closed, and followed by recovery activity when applicable.

  • Unit 23: Loss Reserving and Capital Adequacy Management

    Extend settlement and recovery concepts into reserve adequacy, adverse loss management, and the broader financial protection framework of insurance institutions.

  • Unit 25: Fraud Detection and Claims Integrity

    Connect final claims execution to integrity controls that help insurers detect suspicious recovery patterns, protect payment accuracy, and preserve proper claims discipline.

Study Support

  • Templates & Tools

    Use claims closure checklists and recovery tracking templates to practice payment processing, subrogation review, salvage handling, and reconciliation logic.

  • Glossary Support

    Review key terms such as indemnity, settlement, file closure, third-party recovery, subrogation, salvage, recoverable, and reconciliation.

  • Case Examples

    Study scenarios showing how insurers complete payments, close claims, pursue recoveries, and reconcile recovered amounts after settlement activity.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit students should understand how insurers process final claim payments, complete settlements, close files, pursue subrogation and other recovery rights, and maintain accurate reconciliation of recovered value within the broader claims lifecycle.
