Where This Unit Fits

This unit continues Layer 2 of the track, which focuses on insurance product structures. After studying life insurance and property insurance, students now examine casualty and liability coverage. These insurance products protect policyholders against legal claims arising from accidents, negligence, or other events that cause harm to third parties.

Understanding liability coverage prepares students for later units involving claims investigation, claims settlement, litigation support, and risk management practices across both personal and commercial insurance markets.

Unit Overview

Casualty and liability insurance address situations where individuals or organizations are legally responsible for damage, injury, or financial loss experienced by others. Unlike property insurance, which focuses on physical assets, liability coverage focuses on legal obligations and financial responsibility.

Policies in this category often include coverage for legal defense costs, settlement payments, and judgments arising from covered incidents. Auto insurance and general liability policies are among the most common examples. These products play a critical role in protecting individuals, businesses, and institutions from potentially severe financial exposure.

Why This Matters in Insurance Operations

Liability insurance requires careful underwriting and claims administration because legal disputes can involve uncertain outcomes and large financial consequences. Insurers must interpret policy language, evaluate responsibility, coordinate legal defense, and determine appropriate settlement amounts.

Understanding casualty and liability coverage helps insurance professionals interpret legal exposure, support claims resolution, and administer policies designed to protect policyholders against financial responsibility for harm caused to others.
