Unit 20: Claims Intake and Loss Investigation

Learn how insurers receive claims, perform initial triage, investigate losses, coordinate adjusters, and document claim activity. This unit introduces the workflow that begins when a loss occurs and a claim is reported.

Where This Unit Fits

This unit continues Layer 4: Execution Workflows. After studying underwriting, policy administration, and billing operations, students now examine claims workflows. Claims intake marks the moment when insurance coverage is activated in response to a real-world loss event.

Understanding claims intake prepares students for later units covering claims adjustment, settlement decisions, recovery, and risk controls. It also connects directly to earlier study of claims management systems and operational infrastructure.

Unit Overview

When a loss occurs, policyholders submit claims to insurers. The process begins with first notice of loss, where key information is captured. Claims are then triaged, assigned, and investigated. Insurers evaluate damage, gather facts, coordinate field adjusters, and build a documented record of the claim.

This unit introduces claims reporting, first notice of loss, triage and assignment, damage evaluation, investigation procedures, adjuster coordination, and claims documentation. The focus is operational: how insurers organize the early stages of claims processing to ensure accurate, consistent, and well-documented outcomes.

Why This Matters in Insurance Operations

Claims are the core promise of insurance. If claims are mishandled, delayed, or incorrectly investigated, insurers face financial loss, legal exposure, and reputational damage. Effective intake and investigation workflows ensure that claims are handled efficiently and fairly.

Understanding claims workflows helps insurance professionals interpret how losses are reported, how investigations are structured, and how documentation supports later decisions about coverage, liability, and settlement. This unit provides the foundation for all claims-related operations.

What You'll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How claims reporting initiates the insurance response to loss events
  • How first notice of loss captures essential claim information
  • How claims triage and assignment direct cases to appropriate handlers
  • How insurers evaluate damage and gather evidence
  • How adjusters coordinate field assessments and investigations
  • Why claims documentation is critical for accuracy and accountability

Operational Competencies

  • Identify the stages of claims intake and investigation workflows
  • Explain how claims are triaged and assigned for handling
  • Understand how evidence and documentation are collected
  • Recognize the role of adjusters in evaluating loss events
  • Interpret how investigation outcomes support later settlement decisions
