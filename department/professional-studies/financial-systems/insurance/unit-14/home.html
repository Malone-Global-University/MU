Unit 14: Claims Management Systems

Learn how insurers manage claims from first notice of loss through investigation, evaluation, payment, and reporting. This unit introduces the systems and tools that support claims intake, adjuster workflows, claims data management, and financial settlement infrastructure.

Where This Unit Fits

This unit continues Layer 3: Operational Infrastructure. After studying policy administration platforms, students now examine claims management systems, which handle one of the most critical functions in insurance: paying losses.

Understanding claims systems prepares students for later units covering claims workflows, settlement decisions, fraud detection, reserving, and operational risk management across insurance institutions.

Unit Overview

Claims management systems support the full lifecycle of an insurance claim, from initial reporting through investigation, evaluation, payment, and closure. These systems organize claim data, coordinate adjuster workflows, manage documentation, and ensure that payments are processed accurately and in accordance with policy terms.

This unit introduces claims intake platforms, investigation systems, adjuster workflow tools, payment infrastructure, claims data management systems, and reporting platforms that support monitoring and oversight of claims activity.

Why This Matters in Insurance Operations

Claims are where insurance promises are fulfilled. If claims are mishandled, policyholders may be harmed, legal risks may arise, and insurers may experience financial and reputational damage. Claims systems ensure that claims are handled consistently, documented properly, and resolved in line with policy obligations.

Understanding claims infrastructure helps insurance professionals manage claims data, coordinate investigations, support adjuster decision-making, and ensure that claims payments are accurate and well-controlled.

What You'll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How claims intake systems capture and register new claims
  • How investigation systems support evidence gathering and evaluation
  • How adjuster workflows manage claims handling processes
  • How payment infrastructure processes claim settlements
  • How claims data is stored, tracked, and maintained
  • How reporting systems monitor claims performance and trends

Operational Competencies

  • Identify key systems used in claims management operations
  • Explain how claims are processed from intake to closure
  • Understand how adjusters use systems to evaluate claims
  • Recognize how claims payments are controlled and recorded
  • Interpret how claims reporting supports operational oversight
