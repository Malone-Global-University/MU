Unit 12: Financial Statement Analysis

Learn how lenders evaluate borrower financial strength using income statements, balance sheets, and cash flow performance. This unit introduces the core analytical tools used to assess repayment capacity.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Credit & Lending Operations Track Unit 12: Financial Statement Analysis

Where This Unit Fits

This unit continues Layer 3: Credit Analysis. After learning how institutions establish lending policy and scoring systems, students now study how analysts evaluate borrower financial performance directly.

Financial statement analysis is especially important in business, commercial, and corporate lending, where lenders must assess whether a borrower generates sufficient income and cash flow to repay debt obligations.

Unit Overview

Financial statement analysis is one of the most important tools in credit evaluation. Lenders examine financial reports to understand a borrower’s profitability, liquidity, leverage, and operating stability.

Three primary financial statements guide this analysis: the income statement, which shows profitability; the balance sheet, which shows assets and liabilities; and the cash flow statement, which reveals how money moves through the business.

By interpreting these documents, lenders can estimate repayment capacity, identify financial risks, and determine whether a borrower can sustain debt obligations.

Why This Matters in Lending Operations

For many types of credit—especially commercial and corporate lending— loan repayment ultimately depends on a borrower’s financial performance. If a business cannot generate sufficient earnings or cash flow, it may struggle to service its debt.

Credit analysts therefore rely heavily on financial statement analysis to measure profitability, operating efficiency, capital structure, and repayment strength. These insights influence underwriting decisions, loan structuring, covenant design, and portfolio monitoring.

What You’ll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How lenders interpret income statements and profitability metrics
  • How balance sheets reveal leverage, liquidity, and financial structure
  • How cash flow analysis measures repayment capacity
  • How financial ratios support credit evaluation
  • How analysts identify warning signs of financial stress
  • How financial analysis supports underwriting and monitoring decisions

Operational Competencies

  • Interpret borrower financial statements in a lending context
  • Explain how lenders estimate debt repayment capacity
  • Identify financial strengths and weaknesses in borrower reports
  • Recognize how leverage and liquidity influence credit risk
  • Connect financial analysis to loan structuring and covenant design

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • How do lenders determine whether a borrower can repay a loan?
  • Why are income and cash flow more important than revenue alone?
  • How does leverage affect credit risk?
  • Why do lenders analyze financial ratios when evaluating credit?
  • How do analysts identify financial warning signs before defaults occur?

Lessons in This Unit

Financial Statement Foundations

  • Lesson 12.1: What Financial Statement Analysis Does

    Learn how lenders interpret financial reports to evaluate profitability, liquidity, leverage, and repayment capacity.

  • Lesson 12.2: Income Statement Analysis

    Study how revenue, expenses, operating profit, and net income reveal a borrower’s earning power.

  • Lesson 12.3: Balance Sheet Analysis

    Examine how assets, liabilities, and equity reveal leverage, liquidity, and capital structure.

  • Lesson 12.4: Cash Flow Analysis

    Understand how operating cash flow supports loan repayment and long-term financial stability.

Credit Evaluation Tools

  • Lesson 12.5: Financial Ratios and Performance Indicators

    Learn how ratios such as leverage, liquidity, and coverage metrics support credit evaluation.

  • Lesson 12.6: Debt Capacity and Repayment Strength

    Study how analysts estimate whether borrowers can sustain new debt obligations.

  • Lesson 12.7: Connecting Financial Analysis to Lending Decisions

    Bring together financial statement interpretation, ratio analysis, and repayment capacity to support underwriting decisions.

Connected Units

  • Unit 11: Credit Scoring Systems

    Compare automated credit scoring with analyst-driven financial evaluation.

  • Unit 17: Underwriting Workflows

    Study how analysts apply financial analysis within structured underwriting processes.

  • Unit 23: Covenant Monitoring

    Return to financial ratios and performance metrics when studying borrower monitoring.

Study Support

  • Templates & Tools

    Use financial ratio calculators and simple cash flow models to practice credit analysis.

  • Glossary Support

    Review key terms such as EBITDA, leverage ratio, debt service coverage, liquidity ratio, and working capital.

  • Case Examples

    Study real-world borrower financial statements and evaluate credit risk scenarios.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to interpret financial statements, analyze borrower profitability and leverage, estimate repayment capacity, and explain how financial performance affects lending decisions.
