Unit 19: Trade Capture and Post-Trade Processing

Learn how executed trades move into operational systems. This unit introduces trade capture processes, validation and enrichment workflows, confirmation procedures, trade record creation, post-trade messaging, and exception handling within securities operations infrastructure.

Where This Unit Fits

This unit continues Layer 4: Execution Workflows by examining what happens immediately after trades are executed. Unit 18 introduced the order-to-execution workflow. Unit 19 now focuses on how executed trades are captured and processed inside operational systems.

Trade capture is the moment when execution events become structured operational records. These records are used to support clearing submission, allocations, settlement instructions, reconciliation, and regulatory reporting. Later units in this layer rely heavily on the post-trade processes introduced here.

Unit Overview

When a trade executes, multiple systems must record the event. Broker-dealers, exchanges, clearinghouses, and custodians all rely on consistent trade records to coordinate downstream processes. Trade capture systems collect execution details such as instrument identifiers, quantities, prices, counterparties, and timestamps.

Once trades are captured, they undergo validation and enrichment. Systems confirm that the trade details match internal expectations, regulatory requirements, and counterparty information. Operational confirmations are generated, messages are distributed across systems, and trade records are stored for future processing.

This unit introduces the operational logic behind these processes and explains how post-trade systems transform execution events into structured records that support the rest of the trade lifecycle.

Why This Matters in Securities Operations

Accurate trade capture is essential for market integrity. Every downstream process — including clearing, settlement, reconciliation, and reporting — depends on the correct recording of executed trades.

Operational teams must ensure that trade data is complete, validated, and correctly distributed across systems. Errors in post-trade processing can lead to reconciliation breaks, settlement failures, and regulatory issues.

What You’ll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How trade capture systems record executed trades
  • How trade validation and enrichment processes verify trade data
  • How operational confirmations are generated
  • How post-trade messages distribute information across systems
  • How trade records are stored and maintained
  • How institutions manage post-trade processing exceptions

Operational Competencies

  • Explain how executed trades become operational records
  • Understand how trade validation ensures accurate processing
  • Recognize the importance of confirmation workflows
  • Describe how post-trade messaging connects institutional systems
  • Identify processes used to resolve trade processing exceptions
