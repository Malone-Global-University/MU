Unit 3: Payment Participants and Use Cases

Learn who uses payment systems and why. This unit explores how consumers, merchants, billers, platforms, governments, and institutions generate payment activity across the economy.

Where This Unit Fits

This unit completes Layer 1 by examining the real-world participants who generate payment activity. After learning the financial logic of payments in Unit 1 and the institutional structure of the ecosystem in Unit 2, students now focus on the people, organizations, and institutions that actually use payment systems.

Payments exist because economic actors need to exchange value. Consumers make purchases, merchants accept payments, billers collect recurring obligations, governments distribute funds, and corporations transfer money across supply chains. Understanding these use cases helps students interpret why payment infrastructure exists and how transaction demand flows through the system.

Unit Overview

Payment systems support an enormous range of economic activity. Individuals buy goods and services. Businesses collect revenue. Governments distribute benefits and collect taxes. Platforms facilitate digital commerce. Corporations move funds across suppliers, employees, and partners.

This unit introduces the major payment participants and the types of payment activity they generate. Students learn how consumers, merchants, billers, digital platforms, governments, and institutional organizations interact with payment systems. The goal is to connect infrastructure with real-world financial activity across the economy.

Why This Matters in Payments

Payment infrastructure is built around participant needs. Merchant systems exist because businesses need to accept payments. Consumer products exist because individuals need convenient ways to pay. Bill payment systems exist because recurring obligations must be collected reliably. Corporate transfer rails exist because institutions move large amounts of value regularly.

By understanding who uses payment systems and why, students can better interpret how payment products are designed, why different payment rails exist, and how infrastructure evolves to support changing economic activity.

What You’ll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How consumers generate payment demand through everyday transactions
  • How merchants rely on payment acceptance to support commercial activity
  • How billers use recurring payment systems for utilities, subscriptions, and services
  • How digital platforms integrate payments into marketplaces and software ecosystems
  • How governments use payment systems for taxes, benefits, and public transfers
  • How corporations and institutions generate large-scale payment flows

Operational Competencies

  • Identify major payment participants and their roles in the economy
  • Recognize common payment use cases across consumer, commercial, and institutional contexts
  • Explain how payment infrastructure supports different transaction needs
  • Interpret how payment demand drives the design of payment systems

Lessons in This Unit

Payment Participants

  • Lesson 3.1: Consumers and Personal Payments

    Learn how individuals generate payment activity through purchases, transfers, and everyday financial interactions.

  • Lesson 3.2: Merchants and Commercial Payment Acceptance

    Study how businesses rely on payment acceptance infrastructure to receive revenue from customers.

  • Lesson 3.3: Billers and Recurring Payment Systems

    Examine how organizations collect recurring obligations such as utilities, subscriptions, and service payments.

  • Lesson 3.4: Platforms and Embedded Payment Flows

    Understand how digital platforms integrate payments directly into software, marketplaces, and digital ecosystems.

Institutional Use Cases

  • Lesson 3.5: Government Payments and Public Transfers

    Learn how governments distribute funds, collect taxes, and manage public payment programs.

  • Lesson 3.6: Institutional and Corporate Payment Flows

    Study how corporations and institutions move money across payroll, suppliers, partners, and financial obligations.

  • Lesson 3.7: Understanding Payment Use Cases Across the Economy

    Bring together consumer, commercial, government, and institutional payment activity into one economic picture.

Connected Units

  • Unit 2: Structure of the Payments Ecosystem

    Understand the institutions that support payment participants across the financial system.

  • Unit 4: Revenue Models and Network Economics

    Learn how payment participants and infrastructure providers earn revenue from payment activity.

  • Unit 9: Merchant Acceptance Channels

    Explore the systems merchants use to accept payments across physical, digital, and mobile environments.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to identify the major participants in payment systems, explain how different types of economic activity generate payment demand, and understand how payment infrastructure supports consumers, merchants, institutions, and governments across the economy.
