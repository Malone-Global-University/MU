Unit 18: Transaction Initiation and Authorization Workflows

Learn how payments begin and how authorization decisions are made. This unit introduces payment initiation, credential validation, authorization routing, issuer decision processing, and the approval responses that confirm or reject payment requests.

Where This Unit Fits

This unit begins Layer 4: Execution Workflows. After studying payment infrastructure systems in previous units, students now examine the operational sequence through which individual payment transactions move from initiation to approval.

The transaction lifecycle begins when a payer attempts to make a payment. This request must pass through credential validation, authorization routing, issuer decision logic, and response delivery before the merchant receives confirmation. Understanding this process provides the foundation for later units covering transaction processing, settlement preparation, disputes, and operational investigations.

Unit Overview

Every payment transaction begins with an initiation event. This may occur at a physical terminal, an ecommerce checkout page, a mobile application, or another payment interface. Once the payment request is created, the system must verify the credentials provided by the payer and transmit the authorization request through payment networks to the issuing institution.

The issuer evaluates the request using available account information, fraud controls, and balance checks before returning an approval or decline. This unit explores each step in that workflow, from the moment a payment begins to the moment an authorization response is returned.

Why This Matters in Payments

Authorization workflows sit at the center of modern payment systems. These workflows determine whether a transaction will proceed or fail, and they must operate with extremely high speed and reliability. Millions of authorization decisions occur every minute across global payment networks.

Students who understand this workflow are better prepared to interpret why transactions are approved or declined, how fraud checks operate, why authorization responses contain specific codes, and how merchants receive confirmation of payment decisions.

What You’ll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How payment transactions begin through initiation systems
  • How credential validation confirms cardholder or account legitimacy
  • How authorization requests travel through payment networks
  • How issuing institutions evaluate authorization requests
  • How authorization responses communicate approval or decline outcomes
  • How merchants receive confirmation of authorization decisions

Operational Competencies

  • Explain the steps involved in the authorization workflow
  • Recognize how payment credentials are validated
  • Understand how issuers make approval or decline decisions
  • Interpret authorization response messages and approval codes
  • Describe how authorization confirmation reaches the merchant

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • What happens when a customer attempts to make a payment?
  • How do payment systems validate credentials?
  • Why do issuers approve or decline transactions?
  • How do merchants receive confirmation that a payment is authorized?

Lessons in This Unit

Transaction Initiation

  • Lesson 18.1: Payment Initiation

    Learn how payment requests begin through merchant systems, mobile applications, and checkout interfaces.

  • Lesson 18.2: Credential Validation

    Study how payment systems validate credentials such as card numbers, tokens, or account identifiers.

  • Lesson 18.3: Authorization Request Routing

    Examine how authorization requests travel through payment networks toward issuing institutions.

  • Lesson 18.4: Issuer Decision Processing

    Understand how issuing institutions evaluate authorization requests using account balances, fraud signals, and risk controls.

Authorization Completion

  • Lesson 18.5: Authorization Response Delivery

    Learn how approval or decline responses travel back through the payment network to merchant systems.

  • Lesson 18.6: Merchant Approval Processing

    Study how merchants receive authorization decisions and proceed with payment confirmation.

  • Lesson 18.7: The End-to-End Authorization Workflow

    Bring together payment initiation, credential validation, routing, issuer decisions, and response delivery into a complete authorization lifecycle.

Connected Units

  • Unit 17: Payment Data, Messaging, and Recordkeeping Systems

    Build on payment messaging and transaction records that support authorization workflows.

  • Unit 19: Merchant Boarding and Payment Enablement

    Learn how merchants are onboarded and prepared to accept payments through operational enablement workflows.

  • Unit 20: Transaction Processing and Posting

    Follow authorized transactions into processor workflows that record activity and prepare settlement.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to explain how payment transactions begin, describe the authorization workflow used across payment networks, interpret issuer approval decisions, and understand how merchants receive confirmation that a payment has been authorized.
