Payments & Financial Infrastructure Track • Layer 2: Payment Instruments and Channels

Unit 10: Digital Wallets and Platform Payments

Learn how digital wallets store payment credentials, enable tokenized transactions, and support embedded payment flows across mobile devices, apps, and digital platforms.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Payments & Financial Infrastructure Track Unit 10: Digital Wallets and Platform Payments

Where This Unit Fits

This unit completes Layer 2: Payment Instruments and Channels by examining digital wallet systems and platform-based payment experiences. After studying card systems, account-based transfers, instant payment networks, wire transfers, and merchant acceptance environments, students now explore the technologies that increasingly sit on top of those rails.

Digital wallets allow payment credentials to be stored, secured, and used across devices and applications. Many modern commerce experiences rely on wallet infrastructure or embedded payment functionality within larger platforms. Understanding these systems helps students see how payment networks, merchant systems, and consumer devices connect into a seamless payment experience.

Unit Overview

Digital wallets are applications or platforms that store payment credentials, enable authentication, and initiate payment transactions on behalf of users. Rather than requiring customers to enter card details or bank credentials repeatedly, wallet systems securely store credentials and allow them to be used through mobile devices, web platforms, and integrated applications.

This unit introduces the architecture of digital wallet systems, including tokenized credentials, mobile wallet platforms, embedded payment functionality, authentication methods, and integration with card networks and bank payment infrastructure. Students learn how wallets simplify consumer payment experiences while maintaining strong security and operational controls.

Why This Matters in Payments

Digital wallets have become one of the fastest-growing components of the modern payment ecosystem. Consumers increasingly rely on wallet applications for in-store payments, ecommerce checkouts, peer-to-peer transfers, and subscription services. Platforms such as marketplaces, ride-sharing apps, and delivery services also embed payment functionality directly within their applications.

Because wallets sit between users and payment infrastructure, they influence authentication methods, transaction security, user experience design, and payment routing decisions. Students who understand wallet systems are better prepared to interpret how platform-based payments operate and how payment credentials are securely transmitted across financial networks.

What You’ll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How digital wallets store and manage payment credentials
  • How tokenized credentials protect payment information
  • How mobile wallet platforms enable device-based payments
  • How embedded payment systems operate inside digital platforms
  • How authentication methods secure wallet-based transactions
  • How wallet systems connect to payment networks and financial institutions

Operational Competencies

  • Explain how wallet platforms interact with payment networks
  • Distinguish between stored credentials and tokenized payment credentials
  • Describe how mobile devices enable secure wallet transactions
  • Recognize the role of authentication in wallet security
  • Understand how embedded payments integrate with platform ecosystems

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • How do digital wallets securely store payment credentials?
  • Why are tokenized credentials used in wallet-based transactions?
  • How do mobile devices enable contactless payments?
  • How do platform businesses integrate payments directly into their applications?

Lessons in This Unit

Wallet Foundations

  • Lesson 10.1: Digital Wallet Foundations

    Learn how digital wallets store credentials and enable simplified payment experiences.

  • Lesson 10.2: Stored Credentials and Tokenized Payments

    Study how tokenization protects payment information during wallet transactions.

  • Lesson 10.3: Mobile Wallet Platforms

    Examine how mobile wallet applications enable contactless payments and secure credential storage.

  • Lesson 10.4: Platform and Embedded Payments

    Understand how digital platforms integrate payment functionality directly into apps and services.

Security and Integration

  • Lesson 10.5: Wallet Authentication and Device Security

    Learn how authentication methods protect wallet transactions and confirm user identity.

  • Lesson 10.6: Wallet–Network Integration

    Study how wallet platforms connect to payment networks, issuers, and processors.

  • Lesson 10.7: The Digital Wallet Payment Model

    Bring together wallet architecture, tokenization, authentication, and network integration into a unified operating model.

Connected Units

  • Unit 9: Merchant Acceptance Channels

    Apply wallet-based payment methods within merchant acceptance environments.

  • Unit 11: Authorization Systems and Transaction Messaging

    Study how wallet-initiated transactions move through authorization infrastructure.

  • Unit 14: Payment Gateway and Integration Platforms

    Explore how payment gateways support wallet-based and embedded platform transactions.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to explain how digital wallet systems operate, describe how tokenized credentials and authentication secure transactions, and understand how wallet infrastructure connects mobile devices, platform applications, and payment networks into a unified payment experience.
