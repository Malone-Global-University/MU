Wealth & Asset Operations Track

Learn the operational mechanics of wealth and asset management organizations. Study portfolio administration, asset servicing, trading support, reporting systems, and institutional controls through applied professional training.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Wealth & Asset Operations Track

Track Overview

This free, self-directed track teaches the operational mechanics of wealth and asset management organizations. Students learn how firms administer portfolios, maintain custody and records, process investment activity, support advisors and portfolio managers, generate client and internal reporting, and manage operational controls across complex asset environments.

The through-line is simple: wealth and asset management depends on disciplined operating systems that keep client assets, portfolio data, transactions, records, and reporting accurate over time. Investment performance matters, but institutions only function when administration, controls, and infrastructure operate reliably.

Students gain practical literacy in portfolio administration, custody relationships, asset servicing, trading operations, reconciliation processes, reporting systems, operational controls, and institutional governance.

Track Units

  • Layer 1: Foundations

    How wealth and asset management function as institutional operating systems.

  • Unit 1: Financial Foundations for Wealth and Asset Management

    Portfolio value, investment returns, compounding, fees, settlement cycles, and client asset administration.

  • Unit 2: Structure of the Wealth and Asset Management Industry

    Advisors, asset managers, custodians, administrators, broker-dealers, and institutional service providers.

  • Unit 3: Client Types and Asset Pools

    Retail clients, high-net-worth households, retirement plans, trusts, endowments, and institutional portfolios.

  • Unit 4: Revenue Models and Operating Economics

    Asset-based fees, advisory fees, performance fees, fund expenses, and operating scale economics.

  • Layer 2: Products / Activities

    What wealth and asset organizations manage and administer.

  • Unit 5: Advisory Accounts and Managed Portfolios

    Separately managed accounts, discretionary mandates, wrap programs, and advisor-managed portfolios.

  • Unit 6: Funds and Pooled Investment Vehicles

    Mutual funds, ETFs, private funds, hedge funds, trusts, and collective investment structures.

  • Unit 7: Asset Classes and Position Administration

    Equities, fixed income, cash equivalents, derivatives, alternatives, and multi-asset portfolio holdings.

  • Unit 8: Cash and Liquidity Administration

    Cash balances, sweep programs, liquidity buffers, and portfolio cash positioning.

  • Unit 9: Income and Distribution Processing

    Dividends, interest, capital gains, fund distributions, and client income payments.

  • Unit 10: Retirement, Trust, and Fiduciary Accounts

    IRAs, retirement plans, trust accounts, fiduciary obligations, and specialized account structures.

  • Layer 3: Operational Infrastructure

    Systems that maintain portfolio records, asset custody, and operational accuracy.

  • Unit 11: Custody and Safekeeping Infrastructure

    Custodian banks, asset segregation, beneficial ownership records, and safekeeping frameworks.

  • Unit 12: Portfolio Accounting Systems

    Position tracking, cost basis accounting, income accruals, and portfolio bookkeeping.

  • Unit 13: Security Master and Reference Data Systems

    Security identifiers, asset reference data, instrument metadata, and portfolio data standards.

  • Unit 14: Pricing and Valuation Data Infrastructure

    Market price feeds, valuation vendors, pricing models, and valuation data management.

  • Unit 15: Trading and Settlement Infrastructure

    Trade capture systems, settlement cycles, confirmation systems, and trade lifecycle infrastructure.

  • Unit 16: Reconciliation Systems and Break Management Infrastructure

    Cash reconciliation, position reconciliation, break detection systems, and operational matching tools.

  • Unit 17: Reporting and Books-and-Records Infrastructure

    Client reporting platforms, statement generation, regulatory recordkeeping, and archival systems.

  • Layer 4: Execution Workflows

    How wealth and asset operations move from instruction to completed process.

  • Unit 18: Account Onboarding and Client Setup

    Account applications, documentation intake, regulatory verification, and client approval workflows.

  • Unit 19: Asset Transfers and Account Conversion

    ACAT transfers, inbound/outbound asset transfers, account migrations, and custody transitions.

  • Unit 20: Cash Movement and Payment Processing

    Deposits, withdrawals, wires, ACH transfers, and payment authorization procedures.

  • Unit 21: Trade Support and Portfolio Implementation

    Order support, portfolio allocations, execution coordination, and settlement follow-through.

  • Unit 22: Corporate Actions and Lifecycle Event Processing

    Dividends, stock splits, mergers, tender offers, and mandatory or voluntary corporate events.

  • Unit 23: Client Reporting and Service Operations

    Statement delivery, performance reviews, service inquiries, and operational client communication.

  • Layer 5: Risk & Controls

    How firms maintain operational accuracy and protect client assets.

  • Unit 24: Authorization Controls and Asset Protection

    Dual control procedures, approval hierarchies, segregation of duties, and client asset safeguards.

  • Unit 25: Reconciliation Break Management and Error Resolution

    Break investigation, transaction corrections, operational adjustments, and remediation procedures.

  • Unit 26: Valuation Oversight and Pricing Controls

    Price validation, stale price monitoring, illiquid asset valuation review, and pricing governance.

  • Unit 27: Investment Compliance and Mandate Monitoring

    Portfolio guideline testing, investment restrictions, concentration limits, and mandate compliance.

  • Unit 28: Operational Risk and Incident Management

    Operational disruptions, system failures, incident response, and operational risk monitoring.

  • Unit 29: Fraud Prevention, Cybersecurity, and Access Controls

    Access management, cybersecurity practices, fraud detection systems, and account protection.

  • Layer 6: Institutional Management / Governance

    How wealth and asset organizations manage operational scale and oversight.

  • Unit 30: Operations Team Structure and Functional Roles

    Front office, middle office, operations teams, accounting groups, and service departments.

  • Unit 31: Front-to-Back Operational Coordination

    Advisor support, portfolio management interaction, trading coordination, and service escalation.

  • Unit 32: Operational Reporting and Performance Management

    Processing metrics, reconciliation performance, service standards, and operational dashboards.

  • Unit 33: Vendor, Custodian, and Platform Relationship Management

    Custodian oversight, fund administrators, technology vendors, and service-level management.

  • Unit 34: Governance, Audit Readiness, and Institutional Oversight

    Policies, procedures, audit preparation, internal controls, governance frameworks, and accountability.
