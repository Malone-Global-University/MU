Wealth & Asset Operations Track • Layer 6: Institutional Management / Governance

Unit 30: Operations Team Structure and Functional Roles

Understand the structure and coordination of front, middle, and back office teams, portfolio accounting groups, and client service departments in wealth and asset operations.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Wealth & Asset Operations Track Unit 30: Operations Team Structure and Functional Roles

Where This Unit Fits

Unit 30 completes the Wealth & Asset Operations Track by focusing on organizational structure, team roles, functional responsibilities, and role coordination to manage scale and operational oversight.

Unit Overview

This unit explores front, middle, and back office functions, portfolio accounting teams, client service departments, organizational design principles, scaling operations, and coordination across roles.

Why This Matters in Wealth & Asset Operations

Effective organizational design ensures that operational responsibilities are clearly defined, teams coordinate efficiently, and scaling is managed without compromising operational integrity or client service.

What You'll Learn

Core Concepts

  • Front office functions and responsibilities
  • Middle office roles in monitoring and control
  • Back office operations and support functions
  • Portfolio accounting and reporting teams
  • Client service and support operations
  • Organizational design and scaling
  • Role coordination and interdependencies

Operational Competencies

  • Define and distinguish front, middle, and back office functions
  • Understand team interactions and dependencies
  • Coordinate portfolio accounting and client service workflows
  • Assess organizational structures for efficiency and scalability
  • Implement functional roles to support operational oversight
  • Manage role clarity to prevent duplication and gaps
  • Integrate governance principles into team design

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • How are operations teams structured to manage wealth and asset functions?
  • What roles exist across front, middle, and back offices?
  • How do portfolio accounting and client service teams coordinate?
  • What principles guide scaling and organizational design?
  • How do role interdependencies affect operational oversight?

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 30.1: Front Office Functions

    Explore the responsibilities, workflows, and decision-making of front office teams.

  • Lesson 30.2: Middle Office Responsibilities

    Understand monitoring, risk, and compliance roles within middle office operations.

  • Lesson 30.3: Back Office Operations

    Examine transaction processing, settlements, and support functions in the back office.

  • Lesson 30.4: Portfolio Accounting Teams

    Learn how accounting groups manage positions, income, and reporting in portfolios.

  • Lesson 30.5: Client Service and Support Teams

    Explore client interactions, service operations, and support team workflows.

  • Lesson 30.6: Organizational Design and Scaling

    Understand principles for structuring teams and scaling operations efficiently.

  • Lesson 30.7: Role Coordination and Dependencies

    Study the interactions and dependencies between different operational teams.

Practical Application

Upon completing this unit, students can define and structure operations teams, coordinate workflows across departments, ensure role clarity, support operational scale, and integrate governance principles into organizational management.
