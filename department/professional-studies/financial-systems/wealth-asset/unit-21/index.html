Unit 21: Trade Support and Portfolio Implementation

Learn how trade instructions flow from portfolio strategy to execution, including allocations, pre-trade compliance, booking, settlement monitoring, and post-trade adjustments.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Wealth & Asset Operations Track Unit 21: Trade Support and Portfolio Implementation

Where This Unit Fits

Unit 21 builds on cash movement, account setup, and asset transfer processes to cover trade support and portfolio implementation. Students learn how trades are executed according to portfolio allocations, compliance rules, and settlement requirements.

Unit Overview

This unit provides a structured look at trade support workflows. It explains how trade instructions move through portfolio allocation processes, pre-trade compliance, execution coordination, booking, settlement monitoring, and post-trade adjustments to ensure operational integrity.

Why This Matters in Wealth & Asset Operations

Trade support and portfolio implementation are critical to achieving client investment objectives while managing operational risk. Errors in instruction flow, booking, or settlement can result in financial losses, regulatory breaches, and client dissatisfaction. Understanding these workflows is essential for operational excellence.

What You'll Learn

Core Concepts

  • Trade instruction flow from portfolio strategy to execution
  • Portfolio allocation processes and compliance checks
  • Execution coordination across systems and teams
  • Trade booking and system entry procedures
  • Settlement monitoring and risk management
  • Post-trade adjustments and corrections
  • Operational controls to maintain accuracy and compliance

Operational Competencies

  • Manage trade instructions through allocation and execution workflows
  • Perform pre-trade compliance checks
  • Coordinate with execution desks and operations teams
  • Book trades accurately into systems
  • Monitor settlement and resolve exceptions
  • Apply post-trade adjustments and reconciliations

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • How do trade instructions flow from portfolio strategy to execution?
  • What operational checks ensure compliance before trades are executed?
  • How are trades booked, monitored, and settled across accounts?
  • What steps are required for post-trade adjustments?
  • How do portfolio implementation workflows maintain operational integrity and minimize risk?

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 21.1: Trade Instruction Flow

    Understand how trade instructions are initiated, routed, and tracked through the operational workflow.

  • Lesson 21.2: Portfolio Allocation Processes

    Examine how portfolio allocations are determined, communicated, and implemented within trading systems.

  • Lesson 21.3: Pre-Trade Compliance Checks

    Learn how trades are screened for compliance before execution, including limits, restrictions, and regulatory rules.

  • Lesson 21.4: Execution Coordination

    Explore how trade execution is coordinated across desks, systems, and counterparties to ensure timely and accurate completion.

  • Lesson 21.5: Trade Booking and System Entry

    Understand procedures for accurate trade entry into systems and the recording of all necessary details for downstream processes.

  • Lesson 21.6: Settlement Monitoring

    Learn how settlements are tracked, exceptions managed, and operational risk minimized.

  • Lesson 21.7: Post-Trade Adjustments

    Study how trade corrections and adjustments are performed and communicated to maintain portfolio and client accuracy.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students will be able to manage trade instructions from initiation to settlement, ensure compliance, perform accurate trade booking, monitor settlements, and apply necessary post-trade adjustments while maintaining operational controls.
