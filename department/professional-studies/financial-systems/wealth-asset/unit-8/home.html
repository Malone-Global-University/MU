Unit 8: Settlement and Custody Flow

Learn how trades reach final completion across wealth and asset institutions. This unit introduces settlement mechanics, custody coordination, and the movement of securities and cash so students can understand how transactions become fully completed positions and balances.

Where This Unit Fits

This unit follows trade lifecycle and post-trade processing in Unit 7. After learning how trades are allocated, matched, affirmed, and prepared, students now study the point at which transactions actually complete through the movement of securities and cash. This is where operational preparation becomes institutional completion.

Students begin here because later units on account administration, position reporting, reconciliation, exception handling, and client servicing all depend on whether settlement occurred correctly and whether custody records reflect the true completed state of the trade. This unit provides the completion logic that underlies reliable securities operations.

Unit Overview

A trade is not fully complete until assets and cash actually move. Settlement is the process through which buyers receive securities, sellers receive payment, and custody records are updated to reflect the completed transaction. This requires coordination across brokers, custodians, counterparties, settlement systems, and operational teams.

This unit introduces the mechanics of settlement and custody flow in wealth and asset operations. Students study how settlement instructions are carried through, how securities and cash move, how custodians support safekeeping and record integrity, and why timing, accuracy, and control discipline are critical. The goal is to help students understand how a transaction reaches true operational completion inside institutional systems.

Why This Matters in Wealth & Asset Operations

Settlement is one of the most important control points in securities operations. If a trade does not settle correctly, positions may be misstated, cash may be missing or delayed, client reports may be inaccurate, and downstream processes can break. Custody flow is equally important because institutions rely on custodians to maintain the asset records that support safekeeping, reporting, and portfolio servicing.

In practical terms, students who understand this unit are better prepared to explain why a trade can be executed but not yet completed, why failed settlement creates operational problems, how custody relationships support record integrity, and why asset movement and record movement must stay aligned.

What You’ll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How settlement completes a trade through the actual movement of cash and securities
  • The difference between execution, post-trade preparation, and final settlement
  • How custodians support safekeeping, recordkeeping, and settlement coordination
  • Why settlement timing matters for positions, balances, and operational accuracy
  • How cash flow and asset flow must align across institutions
  • Why custody and settlement failures create downstream operational risk

Operational Competencies

  • Describe the basic mechanics of settlement in wealth and asset environments
  • Explain how custody supports asset holding and completed recordkeeping
  • Recognize the operational difference between a pending trade and a settled trade
  • Interpret how settlement affects account balances, positions, and portfolio records
  • Use settlement and custody knowledge to support later study of reconciliation, exceptions, and client reporting

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • What actually happens when a trade settles?
  • How do securities and cash move between institutions after execution?
  • Why is custody so important for safekeeping and record integrity?
  • What operational problems arise when settlement is delayed, incomplete, or fails?

Lessons in This Unit

Settlement Foundations

  • Lesson 8.1: What Settlement and Custody Flow Do

    Learn how wealth and asset institutions complete trades through the coordinated movement of cash and securities while maintaining accurate custody records.

  • Lesson 8.2: Cash Movement and Securities Delivery

    Study how settlement requires both payment and asset delivery, and why both sides of the transaction must be completed correctly for the trade to finish.

  • Lesson 8.3: Custodians, Safekeeping, and Record Integrity

    Examine how custodians hold assets, maintain records, and support institutional confidence that positions are properly safeguarded and accounted for.

  • Lesson 8.4: Settlement Timing and Completion Status

    Understand how transactions move from pending to settled status and why timing affects cash balances, portfolio positions, and operational reporting.

Operational Completion and Control

  • Lesson 8.5: Settlement Instructions and Institutional Coordination

    Learn how firms, brokers, custodians, and counterparties coordinate instructions so asset and cash movement can occur accurately and on time.

  • Lesson 8.6: Failed Settlements and Operational Consequences

    Study what happens when trades do not settle correctly and why delays, errors, or unmatched movement can create broader operational problems.

  • Lesson 8.7: Bringing Settlement and Custody Together

    Connect settlement timing, cash flow, asset delivery, custody records, and institutional coordination into one operating picture of transaction completion.

Connected Units

  • Unit 7: Trade Lifecycle and Post-Trade Processing

    Use the post-trade stages from Unit 7 to understand how trades reach the settlement and custody completion point studied here.

  • Unit 9: Client Accounts and Account Administration

    Build on settlement and custody flow by studying how completed positions and balances are maintained inside client account structures.

  • Unit 10: Reconciliation and Break Resolution

    Apply settlement knowledge when studying how firms detect and resolve mismatches in positions, balances, and transaction records.

Study Support

  • Templates & Tools

    Use settlement flow maps, custody diagrams, and asset-movement worksheets to trace how trades move from post-trade preparation into completed institutional records.

  • Glossary Support

    Review key terms such as settlement, custody, safekeeping, delivery, cash movement, settlement date, failed trade, and custody record.

  • Case Examples

    Study practical scenarios showing how a trade settles, how custody records update, and how operational problems emerge when settlement does not complete correctly.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to explain how settlement completes a trade, describe how custodians support safekeeping and record integrity, distinguish between pending and settled transactions, and understand how settlement outcomes affect positions, balances, and downstream wealth and asset operations.
