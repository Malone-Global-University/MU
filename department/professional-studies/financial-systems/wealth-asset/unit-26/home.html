Unit 26: Valuation Oversight and Pricing Controls

Learn how to implement pricing governance, validate market prices, monitor stale pricing, review illiquid asset valuations, and meet regulatory expectations for portfolio oversight.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Wealth & Asset Operations Track Unit 26: Valuation Oversight and Pricing Controls

Where This Unit Fits

Unit 26 builds on pricing and valuation infrastructure units, emphasizing governance and oversight to ensure that portfolio valuations are accurate, reliable, and compliant with regulatory standards.

Unit Overview

Valuation oversight ensures that market prices, pricing models, and illiquid asset valuations are validated, exceptions are identified, and pricing decisions meet both operational and regulatory standards. This unit covers verification processes, fair value committee review, exception handling, and governance procedures.

Why This Matters in Wealth & Asset Operations

Proper valuation oversight protects client assets, ensures accurate performance measurement, and maintains regulatory compliance. Professionals trained in these controls can detect stale or incorrect pricing, address exceptions, and uphold governance standards.

What You'll Learn

Core Concepts

  • Price verification processes and independent pricing sources
  • Stale price detection and monitoring
  • Fair value committee oversight procedures
  • Illiquid asset review methodologies
  • Exception reporting and follow-up procedures
  • Regulatory expectations for pricing governance
  • Operational controls for valuation accuracy

Operational Competencies

  • Verify market prices using multiple sources
  • Monitor and flag stale or unusual pricing data
  • Participate in fair value committee reviews
  • Assess illiquid asset valuations and methodology
  • Generate exception reports and track resolutions
  • Ensure compliance with regulatory pricing standards
  • Implement governance frameworks for pricing accuracy

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • How can firms ensure portfolio prices are accurate and validated?
  • What procedures detect stale or incorrect pricing?
  • How should illiquid assets be valued and reviewed?
  • What is the role of fair value committees in governance?
  • How can exception reporting and regulatory oversight prevent valuation errors?

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 26.1: Price Verification Processes

    Learn methods for verifying market prices using independent data sources.

  • Lesson 26.2: Independent Pricing Sources

    Explore alternative vendors and feeds to ensure price accuracy and reliability.

  • Lesson 26.3: Stale Price Detection

    Understand how to detect outdated or stale pricing and address anomalies.

  • Lesson 26.4: Fair Value Committee Oversight

    Examine the role of committees in approving valuations and maintaining governance standards.

  • Lesson 26.5: Illiquid Asset Review Procedures

    Study how illiquid assets are valued, reviewed, and approved for reporting.

  • Lesson 26.6: Exception Reporting

    Learn to generate, review, and resolve exceptions related to pricing and valuation data.

  • Lesson 26.7: Regulatory Expectations

    Understand regulatory requirements for pricing governance, oversight, and documentation.

Practical Application

Upon completion, students will be able to validate prices, detect stale pricing, review illiquid asset valuations, participate in fair value governance, produce exception reports, and apply regulatory-compliant valuation controls.
