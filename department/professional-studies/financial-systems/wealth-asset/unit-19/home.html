Wealth & Asset Operations Track • Layer 1: Financial Foundations

Unit 19: Financial Planning and Advisory Strategy

Learn how advisors develop financial plans and investment strategies that guide long-term client portfolio decisions. This unit introduces the frameworks used to align client goals, risk tolerance, and portfolio structure within wealth management practice.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Wealth & Asset Operations Track Unit 19

Where This Unit Fits

This unit follows client relationship management in Unit 18. After students learn how institutions maintain long-term client relationships, they now study how advisors develop financial strategies that guide those relationships over time.

Financial planning connects client goals, investment strategies, portfolio construction, and long-term advisory guidance. Operational teams support these planning frameworks by implementing portfolio changes and maintaining accurate account structures.

Unit Overview

Financial planning is a structured process that helps clients define their financial goals and determine how investments, savings, and risk management strategies can support those objectives.

Advisors gather information about a client's financial situation, risk tolerance, and long-term priorities. Using this information, they develop strategies that guide portfolio allocation, asset selection, and financial decision-making.

This unit introduces the planning frameworks used in wealth management and explains how financial plans influence portfolio management decisions.

Why This Matters in Wealth & Asset Operations

Financial planning provides the foundation for advisory decisions. Without a clear strategy, portfolio management becomes reactive rather than intentional.

Operational teams play an important role by supporting the execution of planning decisions. When advisors recommend portfolio adjustments, operations teams help implement trades, update account records, and maintain alignment between the financial plan and the portfolio.

Students who understand financial planning frameworks are better prepared to interpret advisory decisions and support the operational processes that bring those strategies into practice.

What You'll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How financial planning aligns investments with long-term client goals
  • The relationship between risk tolerance and portfolio structure
  • How advisory strategies guide portfolio allocation decisions
  • Why goal-based investing supports long-term financial planning
  • How financial plans evolve as client circumstances change

Operational Competencies

  • Explain the purpose of financial planning in wealth management
  • Recognize how advisory strategies influence portfolio decisions
  • Understand how operational teams support financial planning execution
  • Describe how planning frameworks guide portfolio adjustments over time

Advisory Strategy Implementation

    Connect client goals, advisory strategy, portfolio allocation, and operational execution into one financial planning framework.

Connected Units
