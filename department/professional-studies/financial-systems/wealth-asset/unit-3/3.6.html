Unit 3: Asset Classes and Position Administration

Lesson 3.6: Positions, Balances, and Portfolio Records

Study how firms track quantities, balances, market values, and position details across accounts so holdings can be administered accurately.

Where This Lesson Fits

This lesson builds on asset types and account structures by explaining how institutions actually track holdings. Once assets are classified and accounts are defined, firms must maintain precise records of positions and balances.

Lesson Objective

Understand how positions and balances are recorded, how portfolio records are maintained, and why accurate tracking is essential to wealth and asset operations.

Lesson Overview

Wealth institutions maintain detailed records of what each account owns. A position reflects the quantity of a specific asset, while a balance reflects its value or amount. Together, these form the portfolio record.

These records support valuation, reporting, reconciliation, and client servicing.

Why This Matters

Accurate records ensure correct reporting, prevent errors, and support operational control.

Core Concept

Positions and balances form the foundation of portfolio records, enabling firms to track ownership and value across accounts.

How It Works

  • Positions track quantity (shares, units, balances)
  • Balances track value
  • Records update with transactions
  • Systems reconcile records for accuracy

Example

A client holds 100 shares of stock. The position is 100 shares. If priced at $50, the balance is $5,000.

Key Terms

Position — Quantity of an asset

Balance — Value of an asset

Portfolio Record — Full account holdings data

Summary

  • Positions track quantity
  • Balances track value
  • Records support operations
