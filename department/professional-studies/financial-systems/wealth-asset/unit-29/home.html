Wealth & Asset Operations Track • Layer 5: Risk & Controls

Unit 29: Fraud Prevention, Cybersecurity, and Access Controls

Learn to manage fraud risks, implement cybersecurity measures, control access, and protect client assets in wealth and asset operations.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Wealth & Asset Operations Track Unit 29: Fraud Prevention, Cybersecurity, and Access Controls

Where This Unit Fits

Unit 29 builds on operational risk and control frameworks by focusing on cybersecurity, access management, fraud prevention, and regulatory safeguards to protect client accounts.

Unit Overview

This unit examines the types of fraud and cyber threats facing financial operations, methods for managing user access and authentication, monitoring and detection tools, incident response workflows, and regulatory and security standards.

Why This Matters in Wealth & Asset Operations

Fraud prevention and cybersecurity protect client assets, maintain operational integrity, and prevent financial and reputational losses. Professionals who understand access control and monitoring frameworks can reduce risk exposure and respond effectively to security incidents.

What You'll Learn

Core Concepts

  • Fraud risks in financial operations
  • Cybersecurity threat landscape
  • User access controls and account protection
  • Authentication and authorization systems
  • Monitoring and detection tools
  • Incident response procedures
  • Regulatory and security standards

Operational Competencies

  • Identify potential fraud and cybersecurity threats
  • Configure and monitor user access and authentication
  • Implement detection and monitoring tools effectively
  • Respond to security incidents in operational workflows
  • Ensure compliance with regulatory standards and security policies
  • Develop controls to prevent unauthorized access
  • Integrate fraud and cybersecurity awareness into daily operations

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • How are financial operations protected from fraud and cyber threats?
  • What controls ensure only authorized access to client accounts?
  • How are incidents detected, investigated, and remediated?
  • What regulatory standards govern cybersecurity and fraud prevention?
  • How do monitoring and detection tools improve operational security?

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 29.1: Fraud Risks in Financial Operations

    Explore common fraud risks, schemes, and vulnerabilities in wealth and asset operations.

  • Lesson 29.2: Cybersecurity Threat Landscape

    Understand cyber threats affecting financial systems and client data.

  • Lesson 29.3: User Access Controls

    Study methods to manage user permissions and account access securely.

  • Lesson 29.4: Authentication and Authorization Systems

    Learn authentication protocols, multi-factor systems, and authorization management.

  • Lesson 29.5: Monitoring and Detection Tools

    Examine tools for detecting unauthorized activity and monitoring system integrity.

  • Lesson 29.6: Incident Response Procedures

    Explore structured workflows for responding to security incidents and breaches.

  • Lesson 29.7: Regulatory and Security Standards

    Review industry standards, compliance requirements, and best practices for operational security.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students can identify and mitigate fraud and cybersecurity risks, implement access and authentication controls, monitor for unauthorized activity, respond to incidents, and comply with relevant security regulations.
