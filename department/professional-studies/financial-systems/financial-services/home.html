Financial Services Administration Track

Learn the administrative and operational mechanics of financial service firms. Study client account systems, advisory support, brokerage operations, reporting processes, and institutional controls through applied professional training.

Track Overview

This free, self-directed track teaches the administrative and operational mechanics of modern financial service firms. Students learn how firms open and maintain client accounts, support advisory and brokerage activity, process transactions, manage service operations, maintain records, generate reports, and coordinate compliance and control functions across the organization.

The through-line is simple: financial service firms are administrative systems that organize client relationships, financial products, transactions, records, and obligations within a controlled operating environment. Firms succeed when they can deliver reliable service, maintain accurate infrastructure, and support revenue-producing professionals without operational failure.

Students gain practical literacy in account administration, service models, operational platforms, transaction support, reporting systems, internal controls, and institutional management.

Track Units

  • Layer 1: Foundations

    How financial service firms function as organized business systems.

  • Unit 1: Financial Foundations for Service Firms

    Core financial concepts, account relationships, fee structures, cash movement, asset custody, and service economics.

  • Unit 2: Structure of the Financial Services Industry

    Broker-dealers, RIAs, custodians, insurers, platforms, service providers, and institutional roles.

  • Unit 3: Client Types and Service Models

    Retail clients, high-net-worth households, businesses, institutions, advisors, and branch vs centralized service structures.

  • Unit 4: Revenue Models and Firm Economics

    Advisory fees, commissions, spreads, platform revenue, service charges, and operating economics.

  • Layer 2: Products / Activities

    What financial service firms administer and support.

  • Unit 5: Investment and Brokerage Accounts

    Taxable accounts, retirement accounts, custodial accounts, margin accounts, and registration structures.

  • Unit 6: Advisory Programs and Managed Accounts

    Discretionary management, advisory platforms, wrap programs, and managed account structures.

  • Unit 7: Cash Management and Client Money Movement

    Deposits, withdrawals, transfers, sweep features, disbursements, and cash handling controls.

  • Unit 8: Retirement and Tax-Advantaged Account Administration

    IRAs, rollover processing, beneficiary structures, contribution limits, and distribution workflows.

  • Unit 9: Client Fee Structures and Billing Systems

    Advisory billing, fee calculation, fee debiting, billing cycles, and fee reconciliation.

  • Layer 3: Operational Infrastructure

    The systems and institutional relationships that keep the firm running.

  • Unit 10: Client Onboarding Intake and Account Setup

    Account applications, documentation intake, identity verification, and onboarding workflows.

  • Unit 11: Suitability Review and Regulatory Documentation

    Suitability data, risk profiling, investment objectives, regulatory disclosures, and documentation review.

  • Unit 12: Custody Relationships and Asset Safekeeping

    Custodian roles, asset segregation, custody controls, and safekeeping systems.

  • Unit 13: Clearing Firms and Brokerage Processing

    Clearing broker relationships, trade settlement, and brokerage processing infrastructure.

  • Unit 14: Client Service Platforms and CRM Systems

    Client databases, service tickets, workflow tracking, and relationship management tools.

  • Unit 15: Data Management and Recordkeeping Systems

    Books and records, document retention, archival systems, and administrative data controls.

  • Unit 16: Reporting Infrastructure and Statement Generation

    Client statements, confirmations, internal reporting, and reconciliation systems.

  • Layer 4: Execution Workflows

    How work is processed inside the firm.

  • Unit 17: Client Service Requests and Case Handling

    Account updates, beneficiary changes, request routing, and escalation management.

  • Unit 18: Money Movement Processing and Approval

    Wire transfers, ACH requests, disbursements, approvals, and fraud checks.

  • Unit 19: Trade Operations and Transaction Support

    Trade corrections, settlement support, operational review, and exception processing.

  • Unit 20: Advisor and Representative Administrative Support

    Advisor onboarding, licensing coordination, administrative support, and practice management.

  • Unit 21: Client Communication and Service Resolution

    Service standards, issue resolution, complaint intake, documentation, and follow-up.

  • Layer 5: Risk & Controls

    How firms prevent operational failure and misconduct.

  • Unit 22: Account Authorization and Access Controls

    Signature authority, approval rights, standing instructions, and account security.

  • Unit 23: Operational Errors and Exception Management

    Processing errors, failed transactions, correction procedures, and remediation workflows.

  • Unit 24: Compliance Coordination and Supervisory Review

    Surveillance support, documentation review, policy adherence, and compliance coordination.

  • Unit 25: Fraud Prevention and Client Protection

    Identity theft prevention, suspicious activity escalation, cybersecurity awareness, and financial abuse detection.

  • Layer 6: Institutional Management / Governance

    How service organizations manage operations at scale.

  • Unit 26: Service Team Structure and Department Design

    Operations teams, branch support, specialization, and workflow ownership.

  • Unit 27: Operational Metrics and Performance Management

    Processing volumes, turnaround times, service metrics, quality indicators, and dashboards.

  • Unit 28: Vendor, Platform, and Outsourced Service Management

    Custodians, software vendors, third-party administrators, and service-level oversight.

  • Unit 29: Operational Risk and Internal Audit Coordination

    Audit preparation, internal reviews, operational risk reporting, and control monitoring.

  • Unit 30: Governance, Policy, and Institutional Oversight

    Operating procedures, supervisory frameworks, governance systems, and accountability.
