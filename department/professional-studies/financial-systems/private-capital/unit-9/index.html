Private Capital & Alternative Investments Track • Layer 2: Investment Strategies and Capital Deployment

Unit 9: Distressed and Special Situations Investing

Learn how investors identify opportunities in financially troubled companies, acquire distressed debt, restructure capital structures, and participate in corporate turnarounds. This unit explores how risk, recovery analysis, and restructuring strategy shape investment outcomes in distressed markets.

Where This Unit Fits

Distressed and special situations investing represents a unique strategy within private capital markets. Rather than financing growth or expansion, distressed investors focus on companies facing financial difficulty, liquidity challenges, or capital structure stress.

These opportunities often arise when companies cannot meet debt obligations, face operational disruptions, or require restructuring to stabilize their financial position. Distressed investors seek to acquire debt or equity positions at discounted valuations and profit from restructuring outcomes, asset recoveries, or operational turnarounds.

Unit Overview

Distressed investing involves analyzing companies with impaired balance sheets, defaulted obligations, or declining financial performance. Investors assess the underlying business viability, evaluate creditor priorities, and determine how restructuring processes may affect capital recovery.

This unit introduces the main components of distressed investing: distressed debt acquisition, restructuring and turnaround situations, special situations investing, recovery analysis, and portfolio monitoring. Students learn how investors position themselves within the capital structure to maximize recovery outcomes.

The unit also highlights how distressed investing differs from traditional lending and equity strategies. Returns often depend on legal rights, restructuring negotiations, and capital structure dynamics rather than purely operational growth.

Why This Matters in Private Capital

Distressed and special situations investing plays an important role in financial markets because it helps reorganize troubled companies and reallocate capital to more productive uses. Investors who specialize in these situations provide liquidity when other market participants may be unwilling to engage.

Understanding distressed investing helps students interpret how capital structures affect recovery outcomes, why creditor hierarchy matters during restructuring, and how investors evaluate downside protection when companies face financial distress.

What You'll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How distressed investing differs from traditional credit and equity strategies
  • How distressed debt is acquired and priced
  • How restructuring and turnaround processes affect investors
  • How recovery analysis evaluates potential investment outcomes
  • How capital structure position influences creditor rights

Operational Competencies

  • Interpret distressed investment opportunities
  • Explain how restructuring processes affect capital recovery
  • Understand creditor hierarchy and priority claims
  • Recognize the role of distressed investors in turnaround situations
  • Describe portfolio monitoring for distressed assets

Lessons in This Unit

Distressed Strategy Foundations

  • Lesson 9.1: Distressed Investing Foundations

    Learn how investors identify distressed opportunities and analyze financially troubled companies.

  • Lesson 9.2: Distressed Debt Acquisition Strategies

    Study how investors purchase discounted debt positions and seek recovery through restructuring outcomes.

  • Lesson 9.3: Restructuring and Turnaround Situations

    Examine how companies restructure obligations, renegotiate terms, and stabilize operations.

  • Lesson 9.4: Special Situations and Event-Driven Opportunities

    Understand how investors pursue opportunities created by unusual corporate events or financial stress.

Recovery and Monitoring

  • Lesson 9.5: Recovery Analysis and Capital Structure Positioning

    Learn how investors evaluate recovery potential by analyzing creditor hierarchy and asset value.

  • Lesson 9.6: Distressed Portfolio Monitoring

    Study how investors track restructuring progress, operational changes, and recovery outcomes.

  • Lesson 9.7: The Distressed Investment Model

    Connect opportunity identification, debt acquisition, restructuring strategy, and recovery outcomes.

Connected Units

  • Unit 8: Private Credit and Direct Lending

    Compare performing credit investments with distressed debt opportunities.

  • Unit 20: Transaction Structuring and Financing

    Apply distressed capital structure analysis when studying financing arrangements.

  • Unit 26: Investment Valuation and Performance Measurement

    Understand how distressed asset valuation and recovery expectations influence performance measurement.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to explain how distressed investors identify financially troubled companies, analyze capital structures, evaluate recovery potential, and participate in restructuring situations that reshape corporate finances.
