Private Capital & Alternative Investments Track

Learn the operational mechanics of private capital and alternative investment strategies. Study private equity, venture capital, hedge funds, private credit, fund structures, and portfolio management through applied professional training.

Track Overview

This free, self-directed track teaches the operational mechanics of private capital and alternative investment strategies. Students learn how private investment firms and funds raise capital, evaluate opportunities, execute transactions, manage portfolio companies or assets, and ultimately realize investment returns across alternative asset classes.

The through-line is simple: private capital investing deploys long-term capital into privately negotiated opportunities where value is created through financial structuring, operational improvement, strategic growth, and disciplined portfolio oversight.

Students gain practical literacy in private equity, venture capital, private credit, hedge fund strategies, fund structures, capital commitments, due diligence, transaction execution, valuation, investor relations, and institutional governance.

Track Units

  • Layer 1: Foundations

    How private capital investing operates as an institutional investment system.

  • Unit 1: Financial Foundations for Private Capital

    Return targets, leverage, illiquidity premiums, time horizons, and private investment economics.

  • Unit 2: Structure of the Private Capital Industry

    Private equity firms, venture capital funds, hedge funds, private credit lenders, and institutional investors.

  • Unit 3: Private Investment Asset Classes

    Buyouts, venture capital, private credit, distressed investing, hedge strategies, and alternative assets.

  • Unit 4: Risk and Return in Private Investments

    Illiquidity risk, leverage exposure, operational risk, and long-term return expectations.

  • Layer 2: Investment Strategies and Capital Deployment

    What private investment firms deploy capital into.

  • Unit 5: Private Equity Buyout Investing

    Control acquisitions, leveraged buyouts, recapitalizations, and private company ownership.

  • Unit 6: Venture Capital Investing

    Seed funding, venture rounds, startup financing, and early-stage investment risk.

  • Unit 7: Growth Equity and Expansion Capital

    Minority investments, growth financing, and scaling private businesses.

  • Unit 8: Private Credit and Direct Lending

    Direct lending funds, mezzanine debt, structured credit, and private loan markets.

  • Unit 9: Distressed and Special Situations Investing

    Distressed debt acquisitions, restructurings, and turnaround opportunities.

  • Unit 10: Hedge Funds and Alternative Trading Strategies

    Long-short equity, macro strategies, event-driven investing, and relative value trades.

  • Layer 3: Operational Infrastructure

    Systems supporting private investment organizations.

  • Unit 11: Investment Fund Structures and Legal Vehicles

    Limited partnerships, general partners, fund vehicles, and investment entities.

  • Unit 12: Capital Commitments and Investor Funding Structures

    Capital commitments, drawdown mechanisms, and investor capital structures.

  • Unit 13: Fund Administration and Accounting Systems

    Capital accounts, NAV calculations, fund accounting, and investor recordkeeping.

  • Unit 14: Investor Reporting and Limited Partner Communication

    Investor reports, quarterly updates, and transparency requirements.

  • Unit 15: Deal Pipeline and Investment Tracking Systems

    Deal sourcing databases, pipeline tracking, and investment documentation systems.

  • Unit 16: Portfolio Monitoring and Valuation Systems

    Portfolio company reporting, valuation models, and investment monitoring tools.

  • Layer 4: Execution Workflows

    How private investment transactions move from opportunity to realized investment.

  • Unit 17: Deal Sourcing and Opportunity Identification

    Deal flow networks, banker relationships, proprietary sourcing, and opportunity screening.

  • Unit 18: Preliminary Screening and Investment Committee Review

    Initial deal evaluation, internal investment discussions, and screening approvals.

  • Unit 19: Investment Due Diligence

    Financial analysis, operational review, market analysis, and management assessment.

  • Unit 20: Transaction Structuring and Financing

    Capital structures, leverage arrangements, investor protections, and financing structures.

  • Unit 21: Negotiation and Deal Documentation

    Term sheets, purchase agreements, shareholder rights, and legal documentation.

    Board representation, governance rights, and oversight of portfolio companies.

    How private investment firms manage risk and institutional discipline.

    Scenario analysis, downside modeling, and investment risk evaluation.

  • Unit 25: Portfolio Diversification and Exposure Monitoring

    Industry exposure, geographic diversification, and portfolio balance.

  • Unit 26: Investment Valuation and Performance Measurement

    Portfolio valuation methodologies, performance metrics, and return measurement.

  • Unit 27: Investment Compliance and Investor Protection

    Fund governance, investor rights, disclosure obligations, and regulatory compliance.

  • Unit 28: Operational Risk and Institutional Safeguards

    Fraud prevention, operational controls, and institutional risk management.

  • Layer 6: Institutional Management / Governance

    How private investment firms operate and maintain investor relationships.

  • Unit 29: Investment Team Structure and Functional Roles

    Partners, deal teams, analysts, operating partners, and organizational roles.

  • Unit 30: Investment Committee Governance and Decision Frameworks

    Investment committee approvals, deal governance, and oversight processes.

  • Unit 31: Investor Relations and Fundraising Processes

    Capital raising, institutional investors, fund marketing, and investor communication.

  • Unit 32: Exit Planning and Liquidity Strategies

    Strategic sales, secondary transactions, and exit planning.

  • Unit 33: Investment Realization and Return Distribution

    IPOs, recapitalizations, distributions to investors, and carried interest allocation.
