Corporate Finance & Treasury Operations Track

Learn the operational mechanics of corporate finance and treasury management. Study capital structure, liquidity management, funding operations, financial planning systems, and governance through applied professional training.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Corporate Finance & Treasury Operations Track

Track Overview

This free, self-directed track teaches the operational mechanics of corporate finance and treasury management. Students learn how corporations manage capital structure, raise financing, control liquidity, plan financial resources, and operate treasury infrastructure within complex organizations.

The through-line is simple: corporations must continuously manage cash, funding, and financial risk while allocating capital toward productive investment. Treasury and corporate finance functions ensure the organization remains solvent, funded, and strategically positioned.

Students gain practical literacy in corporate funding structures, treasury systems, liquidity management, financial planning infrastructure, operational controls, and institutional governance.

Track Units

  • Layer 1: Foundations

    How corporate finance and treasury systems function inside organizations.

  • Unit 1: Financial Foundations for Corporate Finance

    Cash flow, cost of capital, leverage, financial risk, and the economics of corporate finance.

  • Unit 2: Structure of Corporate Financial Management

    Roles of treasury, corporate finance, accounting, financial planning, and executive financial leadership.

  • Unit 3: Corporate Capital Structure

    Debt, equity, hybrid instruments, leverage strategy, and corporate funding structures.

  • Unit 4: Financial Risk and Corporate Exposure

    Liquidity risk, refinancing risk, market exposure, operational risk, and financial resilience.

  • Layer 2: Products / Activities

    What financial instruments and activities corporations use to manage capital.

  • Unit 5: Corporate Debt and Financing Instruments

    Bank loans, bonds, revolving credit facilities, syndicated loans, and structured corporate financing.

  • Unit 6: Equity Financing and Shareholder Capital

    Equity issuance, shareholder ownership, dividends, share repurchases, and equity capital management.

  • Unit 7: Cash Management and Corporate Liquidity

    Cash balances, working capital management, liquidity buffers, and short-term funding structures.

  • Unit 8: Capital Allocation and Investment Decisions

    Capital budgeting, internal investment review, project financing decisions, and corporate capital allocation.

  • Layer 3: Operational Infrastructure

    What systems track financial resources and corporate obligations.

  • Unit 9: Treasury Systems and Cash Infrastructure

    Treasury management systems, bank account structures, payment platforms, and cash monitoring tools.

  • Unit 10: Corporate Financial Reporting Systems

    Internal reporting, management dashboards, financial statements, and accounting coordination.

  • Unit 11: Funding and Capital Market Infrastructure

    Debt issuance systems, banking relationships, investor communication channels, and financing infrastructure.

  • Unit 12: Treasury Data and Financial Monitoring Systems

    Cash forecasting tools, liquidity dashboards, exposure monitoring, and treasury analytics.

  • Layer 4: Execution Workflows

    How treasury and corporate finance processes operate in practice.

  • Unit 13: Corporate Funding Workflows

    Loan negotiations, bond issuance processes, investor coordination, and financing execution workflows.

  • Unit 14: Treasury Cash Management Operations

    Cash positioning, bank transfers, payment approvals, liquidity adjustments, and operational treasury activity.

  • Unit 15: Financial Planning and Forecasting Processes

    Budget preparation, forecasting cycles, scenario analysis, and financial planning coordination.

  • Unit 16: Corporate Financial Decision Processes

    Investment review committees, financial analysis workflows, capital approval structures, and executive review.

  • Layer 5: Risk & Controls

    How corporations control financial risk and maintain operational discipline.

  • Unit 17: Internal Treasury Controls and Authorization Systems

    Payment authorization, banking controls, segregation of duties, and treasury operational safeguards.

  • Unit 18: Liquidity Risk Monitoring

    Cash stress testing, liquidity buffers, refinancing risk monitoring, and contingency planning.

  • Unit 19: Financial Risk Management and Hedging

    Interest rate risk, currency exposure, hedging strategies, derivatives usage, and financial protection systems.

  • Unit 20: Financial Governance and Compliance

    Internal controls, financial policies, compliance oversight, and audit coordination.

  • Layer 6: Institutional Management / Governance

    How corporations manage financial strategy and oversight.

  • Unit 21: Treasury and Finance Department Structure

    Roles of treasury teams, corporate finance staff, accounting coordination, and executive financial leadership.

  • Unit 22: Financial Reporting to Executive Leadership

    Board reporting, executive dashboards, financial summaries, and strategic financial communication.

  • Unit 23: Banking Relationships and Capital Market Partners

    Bank partnerships, credit relationships, investor communication, and financial institution coordination.

  • Unit 24: Governance, Policy, and Institutional Oversight

    Corporate financial governance, treasury policies, audit oversight, and institutional accountability.
