Wealth & Asset Operations Track

Learn the operational mechanics of wealth and asset management organizations. Study portfolio administration, asset servicing, trading support, reporting systems, and institutional controls through applied professional training.

Track Overview

This free, self-directed track teaches the operational mechanics of wealth and asset management organizations. Students learn how firms administer portfolios, maintain custody and records, process investment activity, support advisors and portfolio managers, generate client and internal reporting, and manage controls across complex asset environments.

The through-line is simple: wealth and asset management depend on disciplined operating systems that keep client assets, portfolio data, transactions, records, and reporting accurate over time. Investment performance matters, but the institution can only function when administration, controls, and infrastructure operate reliably.

Students gain practical literacy in portfolio administration, asset servicing, investment operations, reporting systems, reconciliations, operational controls, and institutional management.

Track Units

  • Layer 1: Foundations

    How wealth and asset management function as institutional operating systems.

  • Unit 1: Financial Foundations for Wealth and Asset Management

    Core concepts in portfolio value, returns, fees, custody, cash balances, settlement, and client asset administration.

  • Unit 2: Structure of the Wealth and Asset Management Industry

    Advisors, asset managers, custodians, fund sponsors, administrators, brokerages, and institutional service providers.

  • Unit 3: Client Types and Asset Pools

    Retail households, high-net-worth clients, retirement assets, endowments, pensions, trusts, and institutional pools of capital.

  • Unit 4: Revenue Models and Operating Economics

    Asset-based fees, advisory fees, fund expenses, service charges, scale economics, and operating cost structure.

  • Layer 2: Products / Activities

    What wealth and asset organizations manage, administer, and support.

  • Unit 5: Advisory Accounts and Managed Portfolios

    Individually managed accounts, wrap programs, discretionary mandates, model portfolios, and household account structures.

  • Unit 6: Funds, Vehicles, and Pooled Assets

    Mutual funds, ETFs, private funds, trusts, retirement vehicles, and pooled investment administration.

  • Unit 7: Asset Classes and Position Administration

    Equities, fixed income, cash, alternatives, derivatives, position records, valuation inputs, and holding structures.

  • Unit 8: Cash, Income, and Distribution Operations

    Dividends, interest, capital gains, distributions, sweeps, withdrawals, and client cash movement workflows.

  • Layer 3: Operational Infrastructure

    What systems keep portfolios, records, and assets coordinated correctly.

  • Unit 9: Custody, Safekeeping, and Asset Records

    Custodial structures, asset segregation, beneficial ownership records, safekeeping systems, and account registration.

  • Unit 10: Portfolio Accounting and Bookkeeping Systems

    Cost basis, lot accounting, accrued income, valuation records, NAV support, and portfolio bookkeeping infrastructure.

  • Unit 11: Trading, Settlement, and Reconciliation Infrastructure

    Trade capture, settlement cycles, failed trade controls, reconciliations, breaks management, and post-trade operations.

  • Unit 12: Reporting, Performance, and Data Systems

    Client statements, portfolio reporting, performance measurement, benchmark comparison, data quality, and administrative reporting.

  • Layer 4: Execution Workflows

    How wealth and asset operations move from instruction to completed process.

  • Unit 13: Account Onboarding and Asset Transfer Workflows

    New account intake, ACAT-style transfers, registration setup, funding workflows, and onboarding quality control.

  • Unit 14: Trade Support and Portfolio Implementation

    Order support, model implementation, allocations, execution coordination, settlement follow-through, and portfolio maintenance.

  • Unit 15: Corporate Actions and Income Processing

    Splits, mergers, tender offers, mandatory events, dividend processing, interest postings, and event-driven asset adjustments.

  • Unit 16: Client Reporting and Service Workflows

    Statement delivery, performance review support, client inquiries, service requests, exception follow-up, and communication workflow.

  • Layer 5: Risk & Controls

    How firms reduce errors, protect assets, and maintain operating integrity.

  • Unit 17: Operational Controls and Asset Protection

    Authorization rules, dual controls, segregation of duties, account permissions, custody safeguards, and client asset protection.

  • Unit 18: Reconciliation Breaks and Error Resolution

    Cash breaks, position breaks, valuation discrepancies, failed transactions, correction workflows, and remediation procedures.

  • Unit 19: Investment Compliance and Mandate Monitoring

    Portfolio restrictions, guideline testing, concentration rules, mandate breaches, documentation, and supervisory review.

  • Unit 20: Operational Risk, Fraud, and Security Controls

    Fraud prevention, cybersecurity awareness, vendor risk, operational incidents, access control, and escalation procedures.

  • Layer 6: Institutional Management / Governance

    How wealth and asset organizations manage operating scale, oversight, and accountability.

  • Unit 21: Team Structure and Functional Oversight

    Operations teams, trading support, service teams, accounting groups, middle-office functions, and management responsibilities.

  • Unit 22: Operational Reporting and Performance Management

    Volume metrics, service standards, reconciliation aging, processing quality, dashboard reporting, and management review cycles.

  • Unit 23: Vendor, Custodian, and Platform Management

    Custodian oversight, administrator relationships, technology vendors, service-level monitoring, and outsourced dependency management.

  • Unit 24: Governance, Audit Readiness, and Institutional Control

    Policies, procedures, governance frameworks, audit support, control testing, escalation pathways, and organizational accountability.
