Insurance & Risk Management Track

Learn the operational mechanics of insurance and risk management systems. Study underwriting, policy administration, claims operations, actuarial frameworks, and reinsurance through applied professional training.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Insurance & Risk Management Track

Track Overview

This free, self-directed track teaches the operational mechanics of insurance and risk management systems. Students learn how insurers evaluate risk, price policies, administer contracts, process claims, manage capital reserves, and distribute risk across reinsurance markets.

The through-line is simple: insurance institutions transform uncertain future losses into predictable financial structures. By pooling risk across many policyholders and managing exposure carefully, insurers provide financial protection while maintaining solvency and long-term stability.

Students gain practical literacy in underwriting systems, policy administration, claims management, actuarial models, reinsurance structures, risk controls, and institutional governance.

Track Units

  • Layer 1: Foundations

    How insurance functions as a financial risk transfer system.

  • Unit 1: Financial Foundations for Insurance

    Risk pooling, probability concepts, premiums, reserves, and the economics of insurance.

  • Unit 2: Structure of the Insurance Industry

    Insurance carriers, brokers, agents, reinsurers, regulators, and global insurance markets.

  • Unit 3: Types of Insurance Coverage

    Life insurance, property and casualty coverage, health insurance, liability coverage, and specialty insurance markets.

  • Unit 4: Risk Exposure and Loss Dynamics

    Loss frequency, severity, catastrophe exposure, and uncertainty in insurance risk.

  • Layer 2: Products / Activities

    What insurance policies and coverage structures institutions offer.

  • Unit 5: Life Insurance Products

    Term life, whole life, universal life, annuities, and long-term policy structures.

  • Unit 6: Property and Casualty Insurance

    Homeowners insurance, auto coverage, commercial property insurance, and liability protection.

  • Unit 7: Commercial and Specialty Insurance

    Professional liability, directors and officers insurance, cyber coverage, and industry-specific risk products.

  • Unit 8: Reinsurance and Risk Transfer

    Reinsurance treaties, facultative coverage, risk sharing structures, and global risk distribution.

  • Layer 3: Operational Infrastructure

    What systems administer policies, premiums, and risk exposure.

  • Unit 9: Underwriting Systems

    Risk assessment tools, underwriting guidelines, exposure evaluation, and pricing frameworks.

  • Unit 10: Policy Administration Systems

    Policy issuance, contract records, premium billing systems, and coverage tracking infrastructure.

  • Unit 11: Claims Processing Infrastructure

    Claims intake systems, investigation processes, payment approval structures, and claims data management.

  • Unit 12: Actuarial and Risk Modeling Systems

    Loss modeling, actuarial analysis, pricing models, reserve calculations, and risk analytics platforms.

  • Layer 4: Execution Workflows

    How insurance operations move from policy issuance to claim resolution.

  • Unit 13: Risk Evaluation and Underwriting Workflow

    Application review, risk classification, premium calculation, and underwriting approval.

  • Unit 14: Policy Issuance and Administration

    Contract issuance, policy documentation, endorsements, and ongoing administration.

  • Unit 15: Claims Investigation and Adjustment

    Loss investigation, damage assessment, adjuster coordination, and claim resolution processes.

  • Unit 16: Claims Settlement and Recovery

    Claims payments, subrogation, dispute resolution, and financial settlement procedures.

  • Layer 5: Risk & Controls

    How insurers manage financial risk and protect solvency.

  • Unit 17: Reserve Management and Capital Protection

    Loss reserves, capital adequacy, solvency requirements, and financial risk management.

  • Unit 18: Fraud Detection and Claims Integrity

    Fraud monitoring, suspicious claim detection, investigative processes, and loss prevention.

  • Unit 19: Regulatory Compliance and Consumer Protection

    Insurance regulation, consumer safeguards, reporting requirements, and supervisory oversight.

  • Unit 20: Enterprise Risk Management

    Company-wide risk oversight, catastrophe exposure management, and institutional risk governance.

  • Layer 6: Institutional Management / Governance

    How insurance organizations manage oversight and long-term financial stability.

  • Unit 21: Insurance Operations Teams and Organizational Structure

    Underwriters, claims adjusters, actuaries, brokers, and insurance company organizational roles.

  • Unit 22: Financial Reporting and Insurance Performance Metrics

    Loss ratios, combined ratios, premium growth, reserve adequacy, and operational reporting.

  • Unit 23: Broker, Agent, and Distribution Networks

    Insurance distribution systems, broker relationships, sales channels, and policyholder outreach.

  • Unit 24: Governance, Policy, and Institutional Oversight

    Board oversight, risk committees, corporate governance structures, and long-term institutional accountability.
