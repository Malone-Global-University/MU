Private Capital & Alternative Investments Track

Learn the operational mechanics of private capital and alternative investment strategies. Study private equity, venture capital, hedge funds, private credit, fund structures, and portfolio management through applied professional training.

Track Overview

This free, self-directed track teaches the operational mechanics of private capital and alternative investment strategies. Students learn how private investment funds raise capital, evaluate opportunities, execute transactions, manage portfolio companies or assets, and ultimately generate investment returns.

The through-line is simple: private capital involves deploying long-term capital into privately negotiated opportunities where value must be created through operational improvement, financial structuring, and strategic growth.

Students gain practical literacy in fund structures, deal sourcing, investment underwriting, transaction execution, portfolio management, exit strategies, and institutional governance.

Track Units

  • Layer 1: Foundations

    How private capital investing operates as an institutional investment system.

  • Unit 1: Financial Foundations for Private Capital

    Return targets, leverage, illiquidity premiums, time horizons, and private investment economics.

  • Unit 2: Structure of the Private Capital Industry

    Private equity firms, venture capital funds, hedge funds, private credit lenders, and institutional investors.

  • Unit 3: Private Investment Asset Classes

    Buyout investments, venture capital, private credit, distressed investing, hedge strategies, and alternative assets.

  • Unit 4: Risk and Return in Private Investments

    Illiquidity risk, operational risk, leverage exposure, long-term return expectations, and investment cycles.

  • Layer 2: Products / Activities

    What types of private investments institutions deploy capital into.

  • Unit 5: Private Equity Investments

    Buyouts, growth equity, recapitalizations, and private company ownership structures.

  • Unit 6: Venture Capital Investing

    Startup funding stages, venture financing rounds, founder ownership, and early-stage investment risk.

  • Unit 7: Private Credit and Direct Lending

    Direct lending funds, mezzanine financing, structured credit, and private debt markets.

  • Unit 8: Hedge Funds and Alternative Strategies

    Long-short equity, macro strategies, arbitrage, event-driven investing, and alternative trading approaches.

  • Layer 3: Operational Infrastructure

    What systems support private investment organizations.

  • Unit 9: Investment Fund Structures

    Limited partnerships, general partners, fund vehicles, capital commitments, and investor structures.

  • Unit 10: Fund Administration and Accounting

    Capital accounts, NAV calculations, fund reporting, accounting systems, and investor recordkeeping.

  • Unit 11: Deal Pipeline and Investment Tracking Systems

    Deal sourcing databases, pipeline tracking, due diligence documentation, and investment monitoring platforms.

  • Unit 12: Portfolio Monitoring and Reporting Infrastructure

    Portfolio company reporting, valuation updates, investor reports, and operational tracking systems.

  • Layer 4: Execution Workflows

    How private investment transactions move from idea to completed deal.

  • Unit 13: Deal Sourcing and Opportunity Identification

    Deal flow networks, banker relationships, proprietary sourcing, and opportunity evaluation.

  • Unit 14: Investment Due Diligence

    Financial analysis, operational review, market evaluation, management assessment, and risk investigation.

  • Unit 15: Transaction Structuring and Negotiation

    Term sheets, capital structure design, investor protections, negotiation strategies, and deal documentation.

  • Unit 16: Portfolio Company Management

    Board oversight, operational improvements, growth initiatives, strategic repositioning, and performance monitoring.

  • Layer 5: Risk & Controls

    How private investment firms manage investment risk and operational exposure.

  • Unit 17: Investment Risk Assessment

    Downside analysis, scenario planning, financial modeling, and investment risk frameworks.

  • Unit 18: Portfolio Diversification and Exposure Management

    Industry exposure, geographic diversification, stage diversification, and portfolio balance.

  • Unit 19: Investment Compliance and Investor Protection

    Fund governance, investor rights, disclosure requirements, and compliance monitoring.

  • Unit 20: Operational Risk and Institutional Safeguards

    Fraud prevention, operational controls, governance structures, and institutional accountability.

  • Layer 6: Institutional Management / Governance

    How private investment firms manage oversight and long-term investment strategy.

  • Unit 21: Investment Team Structure and Roles

    Partners, analysts, operating partners, deal teams, and organizational investment roles.

  • Unit 22: Investment Committee Governance

    Investment committee decisions, approval processes, governance structures, and institutional oversight.

  • Unit 23: Investor Relations and Capital Raising

    Fundraising processes, institutional investors, reporting obligations, and investor communication.

  • Unit 24: Exit Strategies and Investment Realization

    IPOs, strategic sales, recapitalizations, secondary transactions, and return realization for investors.
