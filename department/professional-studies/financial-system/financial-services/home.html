Financial Services Administration Track

Learn the administrative and operational mechanics of financial service firms. Study client account systems, advisory support, brokerage operations, reporting processes, and institutional controls through applied professional training.

Track Overview

This free, self-directed track teaches the administrative and operational mechanics of modern financial service firms. Students learn how firms open and maintain client accounts, support advisory and brokerage activity, process transactions, manage service operations, maintain records, generate reports, and coordinate compliance and control functions across the organization.

The through-line is simple: financial service firms are administrative systems that organize client relationships, financial products, transactions, records, and obligations within a controlled operating environment. Firms succeed when they can deliver reliable service, maintain accurate infrastructure, and support revenue-producing professionals without operational failure.

Students gain practical literacy in account administration, service models, operational platforms, transaction support, reporting systems, internal controls, and institutional management.

Track Units

  • Layer 1: Foundations

    How financial service firms function as organized business systems.

  • Unit 1: Financial Foundations for Service Firms

    Core financial concepts, account relationships, fee structures, cash movement, asset custody, and basic service economics.

  • Unit 2: Structure of the Financial Services Industry

    Broker-dealers, RIAs, banks, custodians, insurers, platforms, service providers, and institutional roles across the industry.

  • Unit 3: Client Types and Service Models

    Retail clients, high-net-worth households, businesses, institutions, advisors, branch models, and centralized service structures.

  • Unit 4: Revenue Models and Firm Economics

    Advisory fees, commissions, spreads, service charges, platform revenue, operating margins, and administrative cost structure.

  • Layer 2: Products / Activities

    What financial service firms administer and support for clients.

  • Unit 5: Investment and Brokerage Accounts

    Taxable accounts, retirement accounts, custodial accounts, margin accounts, and account registration structures.

  • Unit 6: Advisory Programs and Managed Accounts

    Discretionary management, advisory programs, portfolio platforms, wrap structures, and managed account administration.

  • Unit 7: Cash Management and Client Money Movement

    Deposits, withdrawals, transfers, disbursements, linked accounts, sweep features, and cash handling controls.

  • Unit 8: Retirement and Tax-Advantaged Account Administration

    IRAs, rollover processing, beneficiary structures, contribution rules, distribution workflows, and retirement account servicing.

  • Layer 3: Operational Infrastructure

    What systems and structures keep the firm running.

  • Unit 9: Client Onboarding and Account Setup Systems

    New account forms, identity verification, suitability data, account coding, document collection, and setup workflows.

  • Unit 10: Custody, Clearing, and Recordkeeping

    Custodian relationships, clearing arrangements, books and records, document retention, and operational responsibility mapping.

  • Unit 11: Service Platforms and CRM Systems

    Client service platforms, contact records, workflow tools, task queues, service tickets, and relationship management systems.

  • Unit 12: Reporting, Statements, and Administrative Data

    Client statements, confirmations, internal reports, data fields, reconciliations, and reporting accuracy controls.

  • Layer 4: Execution Workflows

    How financial service work is processed from request to completion.

  • Unit 13: Client Service Requests and Case Handling

    Address changes, beneficiary updates, account maintenance, request routing, escalation, and service turnaround management.

  • Unit 14: Transaction Processing and Operational Support

    Trade support, money movement approval, operational review, exception handling, and transaction completion processes.

  • Unit 15: Advisor and Representative Support Workflows

    Administrative support for advisors, sales assistants, branch personnel, service teams, and cross-functional coordination.

  • Unit 16: Client Communication and Service Resolution

    Service standards, issue resolution, complaint intake, follow-up communication, documentation, and client experience management.

  • Layer 5: Risk & Controls

    How firms prevent mistakes, misconduct, and operational breakdown.

  • Unit 17: Account Controls and Authorization Standards

    Access rights, signature authority, approval limits, standing instructions, and administrative control structures.

  • Unit 18: Operational Errors, Exceptions, and Remediation

    Failed transactions, processing errors, exception queues, correction procedures, loss events, and remediation protocols.

  • Unit 19: Compliance Coordination and Supervisory Oversight

    Supervisory review, surveillance support, documentation standards, policy adherence, and coordination with compliance teams.

  • Unit 20: Fraud Prevention, Security, and Client Protection

    Identity theft prevention, suspicious activity escalation, account protection, cybersecurity awareness, and financial abuse controls.

  • Layer 6: Institutional Management / Governance

    How financial service organizations manage people, performance, and control at scale.

  • Unit 21: Service Team Management and Operating Structure

    Department roles, service team design, branch support models, staffing, specialization, and workflow ownership.

  • Unit 22: Operational Reporting and Performance Management

    Service metrics, processing volumes, turnaround times, quality indicators, dashboard reporting, and management review.

  • Unit 23: Vendor, Platform, and Outsourced Service Management

    Third-party administrators, custodians, software vendors, service-level agreements, oversight, and operational dependency management.

  • Unit 24: Governance, Policy, and Institutional Control

    Operating procedures, supervisory frameworks, internal governance, escalation channels, audit readiness, and organizational accountability.
