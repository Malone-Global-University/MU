Capital Markets & Securities Operations Track

Learn the operational mechanics of capital markets and securities infrastructure. Study exchanges, brokerage systems, trade processing, clearing, settlement, and custody frameworks through applied professional training.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Capital Markets & Securities Operations Track

Track Overview

This free, self-directed track teaches the operational mechanics of securities markets. Students learn how exchanges, broker-dealers, trading systems, clearinghouses, settlement infrastructure, and custodians coordinate to move securities and capital through global markets.

The through-line is simple: securities markets function through highly coordinated infrastructure that matches orders, records trades, transfers ownership, settles obligations, and maintains accurate records of securities ownership. Without reliable operations, markets cannot function safely or efficiently.

Students gain practical literacy in brokerage operations, trading infrastructure, clearing systems, settlement workflows, custody records, operational controls, and institutional governance.

Track Units

  • Layer 1: Foundations

    How securities markets function as financial infrastructure.

  • Unit 1: Financial Foundations for Capital Markets

    Securities pricing, liquidity, order flow, capital allocation, settlement timing, and transaction economics.

  • Unit 2: Structure of the Securities Market

    Exchanges, broker-dealers, market makers, institutional investors, custodians, and clearing organizations.

  • Unit 3: Securities Instruments and Market Participants

    Equities, bonds, ETFs, derivatives, institutional investors, asset managers, hedge funds, and trading participants.

  • Unit 4: Market Structure and Trading Venues

    Primary markets, secondary markets, exchanges, alternative trading systems, and electronic market structure.

  • Layer 2: Products / Activities

    What securities and trading activities markets actually support.

  • Unit 5: Equity Market Operations

    Stock issuance, secondary trading, order types, market liquidity, and exchange trading mechanics.

  • Unit 6: Fixed Income Market Operations

    Government bonds, corporate debt, trading conventions, dealer markets, and bond settlement processes.

  • Unit 7: Derivatives and Structured Products

    Options, futures, swaps, structured notes, exchange-traded derivatives, and over-the-counter instruments.

  • Unit 8: Securities Issuance and Capital Formation

    Initial public offerings, debt issuance, underwriting processes, syndicates, and primary market infrastructure.

  • Layer 3: Operational Infrastructure

    What systems record, clear, and settle securities transactions.

  • Unit 9: Brokerage and Order Management Systems

    Order management systems, trade capture, execution routing, client instructions, and broker-dealer infrastructure.

  • Unit 10: Clearinghouses and Counterparty Infrastructure

    Central counterparties, netting processes, margin requirements, risk management, and clearing operations.

  • Unit 11: Settlement Systems and Securities Transfer

    Delivery versus payment, settlement cycles, ownership transfer, settlement instructions, and operational controls.

  • Unit 12: Custody, Safekeeping, and Securities Records

    Custodians, beneficial ownership records, asset safekeeping, reconciliation processes, and account recordkeeping.

  • Layer 4: Execution Workflows

    How securities transactions move from trade to completed settlement.

  • Unit 13: Trade Capture and Execution Workflows

    Order entry, execution confirmation, trade reporting, and transaction validation workflows.

  • Unit 14: Post-Trade Processing and Allocation

    Trade matching, allocation across accounts, broker confirmations, and post-trade operational workflows.

  • Unit 15: Clearing and Settlement Processing

    Clearing submissions, settlement instructions, fails management, and completion of securities settlement.

  • Unit 16: Corporate Actions and Securities Lifecycle Events

    Dividends, splits, mergers, tender offers, rights issues, and operational processing of corporate actions.

  • Layer 5: Risk & Controls

    How securities institutions manage operational risk and market integrity.

  • Unit 17: Trade Controls and Market Surveillance

    Order controls, surveillance systems, compliance checks, market abuse detection, and supervisory oversight.

  • Unit 18: Operational Breaks and Exception Resolution

    Settlement failures, reconciliation breaks, trade disputes, and operational resolution processes.

  • Unit 19: Counterparty Risk and Clearing Controls

    Margin systems, counterparty exposure monitoring, clearinghouse risk management, and systemic safeguards.

  • Unit 20: Security, Data Integrity, and Market Protection

    Access controls, system security, data integrity standards, and protection of market infrastructure.

  • Layer 6: Institutional Management / Governance

    How securities organizations manage operational scale and oversight.

  • Unit 21: Operations Teams and Market Infrastructure Roles

    Brokerage operations, clearing operations, exchange infrastructure teams, and institutional responsibilities.

  • Unit 22: Operational Reporting and Market Metrics

    Trading volumes, settlement rates, reconciliation metrics, operational dashboards, and management reporting.

  • Unit 23: Vendor, Exchange, and Infrastructure Relationships

    Exchange connectivity, clearing relationships, service providers, technology vendors, and infrastructure partnerships.

  • Unit 24: Governance, Policy, and Institutional Oversight

    Operational policies, supervisory frameworks, governance structures, audit readiness, and institutional accountability.
