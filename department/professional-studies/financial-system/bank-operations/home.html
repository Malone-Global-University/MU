Bank Operations Track

Learn the operational mechanics of modern banking institutions. Study deposits, lending, payments, liquidity, risk, compliance, and institutional infrastructure through applied professional training.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Bank Operations Track

Track Units

  • Layer 1: Financial Foundations

    How banking works financially.

  • Unit 1: Financial Foundations for Banking

    Interest, compounding, credit fundamentals, balance sheets, liquidity, and time value of money.

  • Unit 2: Structure of the Banking System

    Central banks, regulators, payment networks, and the roles of commercial banks.

  • Unit 3: Bank Balance Sheets and Accounting

    Assets, liabilities, net interest income, loan loss reserves, and bank profitability.

  • Unit 4: Bank Capital and Financial Stability

    Capital ratios, risk-weighted assets, leverage limits, and systemic stability.

  • Layer 2: Bank Products & Customer Relationships

    What banks actually sell and how customers enter the system.

  • Unit 5: Deposit Products and Account Structures

    Checking, savings, CDs, custodial accounts, and account servicing.

  • Unit 6: Customer Onboarding and Identity Verification

    KYC, identity verification, documentation, and customer approval.

  • Unit 7: Branch Banking and Retail Operations

    Teller systems, branch workflows, cash handling, and customer service operations.

  • Unit 8: Bank Product Ecosystem

    Consumer banking, commercial banking, treasury services, merchant services, and wealth services.

  • Layer 3: Core Banking Infrastructure

    The operational machinery of banking.

  • Unit 9: Core Banking Systems

    Account ledgers, transaction systems, reconciliation, and operational infrastructure.

  • Unit 10: Bank Operations and Transaction Processing

    Deposits, withdrawals, transfers, posting systems, and daily transaction processing workflows.

  • Unit 11: Payment Systems and Settlement Networks

    ACH, wire transfers, clearing systems, and settlement cycles.

  • Unit 12: Card Networks and Digital Payments

    Credit cards, debit cards, payment processors, gateways, and digital wallets.

  • Unit 13: Digital Banking Platforms and Financial Technology

    Online banking, mobile apps, API banking, fintech integrations, and digital account services.

  • Layer 4: Lending and Credit Operations

    How deposits become loans.

  • Unit 14: Consumer Lending Operations

    Personal loans, auto loans, credit cards, and consumer credit workflows.

  • Unit 15: Commercial Lending Operations

    Business loans, credit facilities, and commercial real estate lending.

  • Unit 16: Credit Analysis and Underwriting

    Credit scoring, financial analysis, collateral, and risk ratings.

  • Unit 17: Loan Origination and Credit Approval

    Credit committees, documentation, collateral perfection, and loan closing.

  • Unit 18: Loan Servicing and Credit Administration

    Payment processing, amortization tracking, covenant monitoring, and portfolio reporting.

  • Unit 19: Problem Loans and Credit Recovery

    Delinquency management, restructuring, collections, and charge-offs.

  • Layer 5: Risk Management and Regulatory Controls

    How banks avoid failure.

  • Unit 20: Credit Risk Management

    Portfolio monitoring, concentration limits, and provisioning.

  • Unit 21: Operational Risk and Internal Controls

    Process failures, operational breakdowns, internal fraud, and system risk.

  • Unit 22: Fraud Prevention and Financial Crime Detection

    Transaction monitoring, identity fraud, account takeover, and fraud analytics.

  • Unit 23: Anti-Money Laundering and Regulatory Compliance

    AML frameworks, reporting requirements, suspicious activity monitoring, and regulatory oversight.

  • Unit 24: Regulatory Reporting and Supervisory Filings

    Call reports, capital reporting, regulatory examinations, and supervisory oversight.

  • Layer 6: Treasury and Institutional Management

    How banks stay liquid and stable.

  • Unit 25: Treasury and Liquidity Management

    Reserves, deposit stability, funding flows, and liquidity buffers.

  • Unit 26: Asset–Liability Management and Interest Rate Risk

    Duration mismatch, interest rate exposure, and balance sheet management.

  • Unit 27: Interbank Markets and Bank Funding

    Repo markets, interbank lending, wholesale funding, and central bank facilities.

  • Unit 28: Bank Governance, Audit, and Institutional Oversight

    Board governance, risk committees, audit functions, and regulatory supervision.
