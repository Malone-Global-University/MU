Public Markets & Portfolio Management Track

Learn the operational mechanics of public market investing and portfolio management. Study security analysis, portfolio construction, trading execution, performance measurement, and risk monitoring through applied professional training.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Public Markets & Portfolio Management Track

Track Overview

This free, self-directed track teaches the operational mechanics of investing in public financial markets. Students learn how investors analyze securities, construct portfolios, execute trades, measure performance, and manage risk across equity, fixed income, and multi-asset portfolios.

The through-line is simple: portfolio management is the disciplined allocation of capital across publicly traded assets. Professional investors continuously evaluate opportunities, adjust exposures, manage risk, and monitor performance while maintaining structured investment processes.

Students gain practical literacy in security analysis, portfolio construction, investment decision workflows, trading coordination, performance measurement, risk monitoring, and institutional governance.

Track Units

  • Layer 1: Foundations

    How public markets and portfolio management systems function.

  • Unit 1: Financial Foundations for Public Markets

    Asset pricing, return measurement, risk concepts, diversification, and the economics of portfolio investing.

  • Unit 2: Structure of Public Financial Markets

    Exchanges, broker-dealers, institutional investors, market makers, and global securities markets.

  • Unit 3: Asset Classes in Public Markets

    Equities, fixed income, ETFs, derivatives, and multi-asset portfolio environments.

  • Unit 4: Investment Risk and Market Volatility

    Market cycles, volatility, drawdowns, correlation, and systemic market risk.

  • Layer 2: Products / Activities

    What investors buy, sell, and allocate inside portfolios.

  • Unit 5: Equity Investing

    Public companies, valuation metrics, earnings analysis, and stock investment strategies.

  • Unit 6: Fixed Income Investing

    Government bonds, corporate debt, interest rate dynamics, and bond portfolio structures.

  • Unit 7: Exchange-Traded Funds and Index Investing

    ETF mechanics, index construction, passive investing structures, and market exposure.

  • Unit 8: Multi-Asset Portfolio Allocation

    Asset allocation frameworks, diversification strategies, and portfolio structure design.

  • Layer 3: Operational Infrastructure

    What systems track portfolios, securities, and investment data.

  • Unit 9: Portfolio Accounting Systems

    Position tracking, valuation inputs, performance records, and portfolio accounting infrastructure.

  • Unit 10: Market Data and Research Infrastructure

    Market data feeds, research platforms, analyst reports, and investment information systems.

  • Unit 11: Trading Systems and Broker Connectivity

    Order management systems, broker relationships, trading execution platforms, and market connectivity.

  • Unit 12: Performance Measurement and Reporting Systems

    Portfolio returns, benchmarking, attribution analysis, client reporting, and investment performance tracking.

  • Layer 4: Execution Workflows

    How investment decisions move from analysis to implemented portfolio positions.

  • Unit 13: Security Research and Investment Analysis

    Fundamental analysis, valuation models, investment memos, and research workflows.

  • Unit 14: Portfolio Construction and Rebalancing

    Position sizing, diversification targets, portfolio adjustments, and rebalancing processes.

  • Unit 15: Trade Execution and Order Routing

    Order placement, broker coordination, execution quality, and trading workflow management.

  • Unit 16: Portfolio Monitoring and Adjustment

    Performance review, position monitoring, risk exposure tracking, and portfolio adjustment workflows.

  • Layer 5: Risk & Controls

    How investors manage risk and maintain disciplined investment processes.

  • Unit 17: Portfolio Risk Measurement

    Volatility metrics, drawdown analysis, scenario testing, and portfolio risk frameworks.

  • Unit 18: Diversification and Exposure Controls

    Concentration limits, sector exposure, geographic diversification, and portfolio balance.

  • Unit 19: Investment Policy and Compliance Monitoring

    Portfolio guidelines, mandate restrictions, compliance monitoring, and policy enforcement.

  • Unit 20: Behavioral Discipline and Decision Risk

    Investor psychology, decision bias, process discipline, and maintaining consistent investment judgment.

  • Layer 6: Institutional Management / Governance

    How investment organizations manage oversight and long-term portfolio strategy.

  • Unit 21: Investment Team Structure and Roles

    Portfolio managers, analysts, traders, risk officers, and institutional investment teams.

  • Unit 22: Investment Committee and Decision Governance

    Investment committees, decision review frameworks, approval structures, and portfolio oversight.

  • Unit 23: Client Reporting and Stakeholder Communication

    Investor reporting, performance communication, transparency standards, and institutional client relationships.

  • Unit 24: Long-Term Portfolio Strategy and Governance

    Strategic asset allocation, long-term investment philosophy, governance frameworks, and institutional accountability.
