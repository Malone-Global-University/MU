Royalties

Learn how music royalties are generated, tracked, collected, divided, and paid across songs, recordings, performances, licenses, and platforms.

Overview

Royalties are payments connected to the use of music. Different royalty types may flow to songwriters, publishers, recording artists, labels, producers, performers, and other rights holders depending on the use.

This page belongs to the Music Business and Careers area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Business and career pages should help students connect musical knowledge with professional literacy, rights awareness, practical planning, ethical decision-making, and sustainable creative work.

Royalty Types

Music royalties can include performance royalties, mechanical royalties, sync fees, master royalties, streaming royalties, neighboring rights, and print royalties.

Royalty Collection

Different organizations, publishers, distributors, labels, and platforms may collect and distribute different royalty streams.

Splits and Statements

Royalty statements and splits show how income is allocated, but they can be complex and require careful recordkeeping.
