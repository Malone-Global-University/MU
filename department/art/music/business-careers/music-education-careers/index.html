Music Education Careers

Explore music education careers in schools, studios, ensembles, communities, colleges, online platforms, and independent teaching.

Overview

Music education careers involve teaching people how to listen, perform, read, create, analyze, and understand music. Educators may work in schools, private studios, community programs, churches, colleges, online settings, or independent businesses.

This page belongs to the Music Business and Careers area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Business and career pages should help students connect musical knowledge with professional literacy, rights awareness, practical planning, ethical decision-making, and sustainable creative work.

Teaching Settings

Music educators may teach in public schools, private schools, colleges, private studios, community programs, online courses, and ensemble settings.

Curriculum and Skills

Music education includes listening, performance, notation, theory, history, creativity, technology, assessment, classroom management, and inclusive teaching.

Career Development

Music educators build careers through training, certification, performance experience, pedagogy, networking, curriculum design, and continued study.
