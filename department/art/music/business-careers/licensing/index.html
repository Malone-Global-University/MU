Licensing

Learn how permission to use music is granted through licenses for recordings, compositions, performances, samples, covers, media, and public use.

Overview

Licensing is the process of granting permission to use music under specific terms. Licenses help define what music can be used, by whom, where, for how long, in what media, and for what payment.

This page belongs to the Music Business and Careers area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Business and career pages should help students connect musical knowledge with professional literacy, rights awareness, practical planning, ethical decision-making, and sustainable creative work.

Sync Licensing

Sync licensing allows music to be paired with visual media such as film, television, advertising, games, and online video.

Mechanical and Master Licenses

Mechanical licensing relates to reproducing compositions, while master licensing relates to using specific sound recordings.

Sampling and Permission

Sampling may require permission for both the sound recording and the underlying composition.
