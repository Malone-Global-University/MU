Music Business and Careers

Study music as a profession and industry through rights, revenue, contracts, management, touring, streaming, promotion, education, and entrepreneurship.

Overview

Music is both an art and a profession. Music business study helps students understand how music moves through legal, financial, technological, promotional, and organizational systems.

This page belongs to the Music Business and Careers area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Business and career pages should help students connect musical knowledge with professional literacy, rights awareness, practical planning, ethical decision-making, and sustainable creative work.

Professional Literacy

Musicians, producers, composers, managers, educators, and entrepreneurs need business literacy to protect creative work and make informed professional decisions.

Rights and Revenue

Copyright, publishing, licensing, royalties, contracts, and distribution determine how music is owned, used, credited, paid, and managed.

Career Pathways

Music careers include performance, production, education, composition, management, promotion, live events, media, entrepreneurship, and many hybrid roles.
