Harmony

Learn how pitches sound together through chords, keys, progressions, cadences, consonance, dissonance, and harmonic motion.

Overview

Harmony describes how pitches combine at the same time and how chords move from one sound to another. It can support melody, create tension and release, establish a key, and shape emotional color.

This page belongs to the Music Foundations area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Foundations pages are designed for beginners, independent learners, teachers, performers, producers, and general readers who need clear starting points before moving into deeper music study.

Chords

A chord is a group of pitches heard together. Chords can support melodies, create color, and build musical direction.

Chord Progressions

A chord progression is a sequence of chords. Progressions help music move, settle, surprise, or return home.

Tension and Release

Harmony often works through tension and release. Dissonance can create instability, while consonance can create rest or arrival.
