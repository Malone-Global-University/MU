Sound and Vibration

Understand music as sound: vibration, frequency, pitch, loudness, timbre, resonance, and acoustic space.

Overview

All music begins with sound. Sound is created by vibration and travels through a medium such as air. Musical study uses sound concepts to explain pitch, loudness, tone color, resonance, instruments, recording, and acoustics.

This page belongs to the Music Foundations area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Foundations pages are designed for beginners, independent learners, teachers, performers, producers, and general readers who need clear starting points before moving into deeper music study.

Vibration

Sound begins when something vibrates. Strings, vocal cords, drumheads, reeds, air columns, speakers, and electronic systems can all produce musical sound.

Pitch and Frequency

Frequency describes how fast a vibration occurs. In music, frequency is closely connected to pitch, though musical pitch is also shaped by context and hearing.

Timbre and Resonance

Timbre is tone color: the difference between a trumpet, piano, voice, guitar, or synthesizer playing the same pitch. Resonance helps shape that color.
