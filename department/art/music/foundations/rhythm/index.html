Rhythm

Learn how music organizes time through beat, pulse, tempo, meter, pattern, syncopation, groove, and silence.

Overview

Rhythm is the organization of sound and silence over time. It gives music motion, energy, pattern, and feel. Rhythm may be written, improvised, repeated, layered, danced, counted, or felt physically.

This page belongs to the Music Foundations area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Foundations pages are designed for beginners, independent learners, teachers, performers, producers, and general readers who need clear starting points before moving into deeper music study.

Beat and Pulse

The beat is the regular unit of musical time that listeners often tap or move to. Pulse is the felt steadiness behind the rhythm.

Tempo and Meter

Tempo describes speed. Meter organizes beats into recurring groups, often shown in notation by a time signature.

Syncopation and Groove

Syncopation emphasizes unexpected parts of the beat. Groove describes the rhythmic feel that makes music move.
