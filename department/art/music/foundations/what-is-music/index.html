What Is Music?

Ask what music is and how people organize sound, silence, rhythm, pitch, texture, form, and expression into meaningful experience.

Overview

Music can be described as organized sound, but that short definition is only a beginning. Music is also performance, memory, movement, technology, worship, entertainment, work, protest, dance, storytelling, identity, craft, and community.

This page belongs to the Music Foundations area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Foundations pages are designed for beginners, independent learners, teachers, performers, producers, and general readers who need clear starting points before moving into deeper music study.

Music as Organized Sound

Music uses sound and silence in intentional patterns. Those patterns may be simple or complex, written or improvised, traditional or experimental.

Music as Human Meaning

People use music to express feeling, mark important events, shape identity, teach memory, build community, and communicate ideas that words alone may not carry.

Many Definitions of Music

Different cultures, traditions, and historical periods define music differently. A good music education leaves room for this variety while still teaching useful concepts.
