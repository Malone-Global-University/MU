Rock and Roll

Study rock and roll through electric sound, backbeat, guitar, bands, blues roots, youth culture, amplification, and performance energy.

Overview

Rock and roll developed from blues, R&B, gospel, country, boogie-woogie, and dance music. It became a major force in popular music through electric instruments, backbeat, performance energy, and youth culture.

This page belongs to the Genres and Traditions area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Genre pages should teach musical sound, history, community, performance practice, recordings, artists, instruments, technologies, and cultural context together.

Electric Sound

Rock and roll is strongly associated with electric guitar, bass, drums, amplification, and energetic performance.

Musical Roots

Rock and roll draws from blues, R&B, country, gospel, jump blues, and dance music traditions.

Subgenres

Rock expanded into many styles, including garage rock, folk rock, psychedelic rock, punk, metal, progressive rock, alternative rock, and indie rock.
