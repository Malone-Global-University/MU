Reggae

Study reggae through Jamaican musical history, offbeat rhythm, bass, drums, sound systems, roots, dub, dancehall, and global influence.

Overview

Reggae is a Jamaican musical tradition shaped by ska, rocksteady, sound system culture, Rastafari, social commentary, offbeat rhythm, strong bass lines, dub production, and global circulation.

This page belongs to the Genres and Traditions area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Genre pages should teach musical sound, history, community, performance practice, recordings, artists, instruments, technologies, and cultural context together.

Rhythm and Bass

Reggae often uses offbeat guitar or keyboard patterns, deep bass lines, steady drums, and relaxed but powerful groove.

Jamaican Context

Reggae grows from Jamaican history, sound systems, ska, rocksteady, Rastafari, politics, spirituality, and community life.

Dub and Dancehall

Dub production and dancehall performance expanded reggae's influence on electronic music, hip-hop, remix culture, and global popular music.
