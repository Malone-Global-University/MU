Film Music

Study film music through themes, leitmotifs, orchestration, underscoring, soundtracks, source music, and visual storytelling.

Overview

Film music supports moving images through mood, pacing, character, setting, memory, tension, emotion, and narrative structure. It can include orchestral scoring, songs, electronic sound, source music, and sound design.

This page belongs to the Genres and Traditions area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Genre pages should teach musical sound, history, community, performance practice, recordings, artists, instruments, technologies, and cultural context together.

Theme and Leitmotif

Film scores often use themes or leitmotifs to connect music with characters, places, ideas, or emotional states.

Underscoring

Underscoring supports action, mood, pacing, and dramatic shape without always drawing attention to itself.

Soundtrack Culture

Film music includes scores, songs, soundtrack albums, trailers, games, television, streaming media, and broader visual culture.
