Soul

Study soul music through gospel power, R&B rhythm, expressive singing, groove, emotion, and cultural context.

Overview

Soul music grows from gospel and rhythm and blues, emphasizing expressive vocals, groove, emotional directness, and strong connections to Black cultural life and the civil rights era.

This page belongs to the Genres and Traditions area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Genre pages should teach musical sound, history, community, performance practice, recordings, artists, instruments, technologies, and cultural context together.

Voice and Feeling

Soul music often uses intense vocal expression, gospel-influenced phrasing, call and response, and emotional storytelling.

Regional Sounds

Soul developed through regional scenes such as Motown, Memphis, Muscle Shoals, Chicago, Philadelphia, and others.

Legacy

Soul influenced funk, disco, R&B, hip-hop, pop, neo-soul, and modern vocal performance.
