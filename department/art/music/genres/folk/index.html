Folk Music

Study folk music through oral tradition, storytelling, community, acoustic instruments, regional memory, work, protest, and revival.

Overview

Folk music is rooted in community, oral transmission, regional practice, everyday life, storytelling, dance, work, ritual, memory, and social participation.

This page belongs to the Genres and Traditions area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Genre pages should teach musical sound, history, community, performance practice, recordings, artists, instruments, technologies, and cultural context together.

Oral Tradition

Folk music is often passed through listening, memory, repetition, family, community, and local performance.

Story and Place

Folk songs often preserve stories, place, work, migration, humor, struggle, ritual, and shared identity.

Folk Revivals

Folk revival movements collect, reinterpret, record, and perform older songs for new audiences.
