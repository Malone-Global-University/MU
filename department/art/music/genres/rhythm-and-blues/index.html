Rhythm and Blues

Study rhythm and blues as a bridge between blues, gospel, jazz, soul, rock and roll, funk, and modern popular music.

Overview

Rhythm and blues developed from blues, jazz, gospel, dance music, and postwar Black popular music. It emphasizes groove, vocal expression, rhythm sections, backbeat, and strong song forms.

Groove and Vocals

R&B often centers groove, strong vocal phrasing, emotional delivery, and rhythm section interaction.

Historical Bridge

R&B links blues and gospel traditions to soul, funk, rock and roll, pop, and contemporary R&B.

Modern R&B

Contemporary R&B includes electronic production, hip-hop influence, smooth vocal layering, and modern studio techniques.
