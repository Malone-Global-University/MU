Country Music

Study country music through storytelling, voice, guitar, fiddle, banjo, steel guitar, regional traditions, radio, and recording history.

Overview

Country music is a broad American tradition shaped by folk music, ballads, string bands, rural and working-class experience, radio, recording, dance, storytelling, and regional performance traditions.

This page belongs to the Genres and Traditions area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Genre pages should teach musical sound, history, community, performance practice, recordings, artists, instruments, technologies, and cultural context together.

Storytelling

Country songs often emphasize narrative, place, memory, work, family, heartbreak, faith, humor, and everyday life.

Instruments

Common country instruments include guitar, fiddle, banjo, mandolin, dobro, steel guitar, bass, drums, and voice.

Styles

Country includes old-time music, bluegrass, honky-tonk, western swing, Nashville sound, outlaw country, country pop, Americana, and modern country.
