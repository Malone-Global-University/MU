Blues

Study blues through feeling, voice, guitar, repetition, blue notes, twelve-bar form, call and response, and cultural memory.

Overview

Blues is a foundational African American musical tradition shaped by work songs, spirituals, field hollers, dance, guitar traditions, vocal expression, and social history.

This page belongs to the Genres and Traditions area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Genre pages should teach musical sound, history, community, performance practice, recordings, artists, instruments, technologies, and cultural context together.

Form and Feeling

Blues often uses repeated lines, call and response, expressive vocal tone, blue notes, and twelve-bar form.

Guitar and Voice

Many blues traditions center the relationship between voice and instrument, especially guitar, harmonica, piano, and rhythm section.

Influence

Blues deeply shaped jazz, rhythm and blues, rock and roll, soul, country, funk, hip-hop, and global popular music.
