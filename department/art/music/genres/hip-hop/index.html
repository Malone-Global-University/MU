Hip-Hop

Study hip-hop through MCing, DJing, beats, sampling, flow, lyricism, production, community, technology, and cultural expression.

Overview

Hip-hop is a major cultural and musical movement shaped by MCing, DJing, breakbeats, sampling, dance, visual art, technology, neighborhood culture, social commentary, and global exchange.

This page belongs to the Genres and Traditions area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Genre pages should teach musical sound, history, community, performance practice, recordings, artists, instruments, technologies, and cultural context together.

MCing and Flow

MCs use rhythm, rhyme, voice, breath, phrasing, storytelling, and wordplay over beats.

DJing and Production

Hip-hop production uses breakbeats, samples, drum programming, bass, loops, turntablism, and studio creativity.

Scenes and Subgenres

Hip-hop includes many regional and stylistic traditions, including East Coast, West Coast, Southern, Midwest, boom bap, G-Funk, hyphy, trap, and many global scenes.
