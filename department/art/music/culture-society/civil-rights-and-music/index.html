Civil Rights and Music

Study how music supported civil rights movements through freedom songs, worship, protest, courage, memory, and collective action.

Overview

Music played an important role in civil rights movements by strengthening community, carrying messages, sustaining courage, marking collective identity, and connecting protest to worship, history, and public witness.

This page belongs to the Music, Culture, and Society area of the Malone Global University Music Department. These pages should connect musical sound to people, communities, histories, institutions, media systems, memory, identity, public life, and social meaning.

Freedom Songs

Freedom songs drew from spirituals, hymns, gospel, folk, and community singing to support marches, meetings, and direct action.

Movement Context

Civil rights music should be studied alongside churches, student movements, organizers, mass meetings, segregation, migration, and media coverage.

Legacy

Civil rights music influenced later protest music, soul, folk revival, hip-hop, public memory, and the broader use of music in social movements.
