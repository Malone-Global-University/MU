Music, Culture, and Society

Study music as part of social life: worship, work, protest, celebration, memory, media, identity, community, and public culture.

Overview

Music is part of social life. It appears in worship, work, protest, celebration, mourning, education, dance, politics, media, memory, and identity. This area studies music as a cultural force and asks how music reflects communities while also helping communities imagine, organize, and express themselves.

This page belongs to the Music, Culture, and Society area of the Malone Global University Music Department. These pages should connect musical sound to people, communities, histories, institutions, media systems, memory, identity, public life, and social meaning.
