Sacred Music

Study sacred music through worship, ritual, chant, hymnody, spirituals, gospel, devotion, choir practice, and religious community.

Overview

Sacred music is music connected to worship, devotion, ritual, religious teaching, ceremony, spiritual memory, and communal belief. It appears across many faith traditions and takes many forms.

This page belongs to the Music, Culture, and Society area of the Malone Global University Music Department. These pages should connect musical sound to people, communities, histories, institutions, media systems, memory, identity, public life, and social meaning.

Worship and Ritual

Sacred music often supports prayer, teaching, ceremony, meditation, praise, lament, procession, and congregational participation.

Voice and Community

Many sacred traditions use voice, choir, chant, call and response, instruments, and communal singing to shape shared experience.

History and Continuity

Sacred music preserves memory while adapting to new languages, technologies, communities, performance settings, and musical styles.
