Intervals

Learn how the distance between pitches shapes melody, harmony, voice leading, tuning, and musical expression.

Overview

An interval is the distance between two pitches. Intervals can be heard one after another as melodic intervals or at the same time as harmonic intervals.

This page belongs to the Music Theory and Composition area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Theory pages should connect written concepts to listening, performance, creativity, analysis, and musical communication.

Half Steps and Whole Steps

Half steps and whole steps are basic interval distances used to build scales, chords, melodies, and harmonic patterns.

Interval Quality

Intervals are described by number and quality, such as major third, minor sixth, perfect fifth, or diminished seventh.

Hearing Intervals

Interval study should connect written theory to listening. Students should hear how intervals sound in melodies, chords, bass lines, and vocal parts.
