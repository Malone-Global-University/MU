Counterpoint

Learn how independent melodic lines combine into polyphonic musical textures.

Overview

Counterpoint is the study of how separate melodies work together. It helps students understand polyphony, imitation, voice leading, melodic independence, and the balance between consonance and dissonance.

This page belongs to the Music Theory and Composition area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Theory pages should connect written concepts to listening, performance, creativity, analysis, and musical communication.

Melodic Independence

Counterpoint depends on lines that have their own shape while still fitting together harmonically.

Imitation

Imitation occurs when one voice repeats or echoes another musical idea. It is common in canons, fugues, and many contrapuntal textures.

Consonance and Dissonance

Counterpoint teaches students to manage stable and unstable sounds through preparation, motion, and resolution.
