Music Notation

Learn how written symbols represent pitch, rhythm, silence, expression, structure, and performance instructions.

Overview

Music notation is a system for representing musical sound on a page or screen. It allows musicians to preserve, share, rehearse, analyze, and perform music with greater precision.

This page belongs to the Music Theory and Composition area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Theory pages should connect written concepts to listening, performance, creativity, analysis, and musical communication.

Staff and Clefs

The staff organizes pitch visually. Clefs show how the lines and spaces of the staff should be read.

Notes, Rests, and Rhythm

Notes show sounds. Rests show silence. Rhythmic values show duration and help musicians coordinate time.

Scores and Parts

A score shows multiple musical parts together. A part shows one performer's music extracted from the full score.
