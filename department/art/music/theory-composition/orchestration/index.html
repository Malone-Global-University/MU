Orchestration

Learn how composers and arrangers write for instruments to create color, balance, texture, and dramatic effect.

Overview

Orchestration is the art of assigning musical ideas to instruments. It studies range, timbre, balance, blend, contrast, register, doubling, and instrumental technique.

This page belongs to the Music Theory and Composition area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Theory pages should connect written concepts to listening, performance, creativity, analysis, and musical communication.

Instrument Families

Orchestration begins with understanding instrument families, including strings, woodwinds, brass, percussion, keyboards, voices, and electronic instruments.

Timbre and Color

Different instruments create different tone colors. Orchestration uses those colors to shape musical character.

Balance and Register

Good orchestration considers how instruments balance with one another and how register affects clarity, weight, and intensity.
