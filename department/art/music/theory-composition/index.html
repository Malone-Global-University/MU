Overview

Music Theory and Composition explains how musical materials are organized. It gives students tools for reading, hearing, describing, writing, analyzing, arranging, and composing music.

This page belongs to the Music Theory and Composition area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Theory pages should connect written concepts to listening, performance, creativity, analysis, and musical communication.

Purpose of Music Theory

Music theory helps students name and understand musical patterns. It does not replace listening or creativity; it gives musicians a clearer language for hearing, writing, and communicating musical ideas.

Purpose of Composition

Composition applies musical knowledge creatively. Students use rhythm, melody, harmony, texture, form, notation, and instrumentation to shape original musical works.

Suggested Study Path

Begin with notation, scales, keys, intervals, chords, and harmony. Then move into counterpoint, musical form, songwriting, arranging, orchestration, and composition.
