Songwriting

Learn how songs combine lyrics, melody, harmony, rhythm, form, hooks, arrangement, and performance.

Overview

Songwriting is the craft of creating songs. It connects musical ideas to language, feeling, structure, voice, audience, recording, performance, and memory.

This page belongs to the Music Theory and Composition area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Theory pages should connect written concepts to listening, performance, creativity, analysis, and musical communication.

Lyrics and Melody

Lyrics and melody shape how a song communicates. A strong melody can make words memorable, and strong words can give melody emotional focus.

Hooks

A hook is a memorable musical or lyrical idea that helps a song stay in the listener's mind.

Song Form

Songwriters use sections such as verse, chorus, bridge, intro, and outro to organize ideas and guide the listener.
