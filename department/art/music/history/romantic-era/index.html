Romantic Era

Study Romantic music through emotion, expanded harmony, virtuosity, nationalism, program music, opera, and individual expression.

Overview

Romantic-era music often emphasizes emotional intensity, expanded forms, richer harmony, expressive melody, virtuosity, nationalism, programmatic ideas, and the public image of the composer-performer.

This page belongs to the Music History area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Music history pages should connect musical sound to time, place, people, institutions, technologies, communities, performance practices, and cultural meaning.

Expanded Harmony
