Ancient Music

Explore music in the ancient world through instruments, ritual, memory, oral tradition, early notation, and social life.

Overview

Ancient music refers to musical practices from early human communities and ancient civilizations. Much of it is known through instruments, images, writings, archaeology, ritual evidence, and later traditions.

This page belongs to the Music History area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Music history pages should connect musical sound to time, place, people, institutions, technologies, communities, performance practices, and cultural meaning.

Early Instruments

Ancient music used voices, drums, rattles, flutes, lyres, harps, horns, and other instruments made from natural materials.

Ritual and Community

Music often appeared in ritual, worship, work, storytelling, dance, healing, and public ceremony.

Historical Evidence

Because recordings do not survive from ancient times, scholars study instruments, texts, artwork, notation fragments, and living traditions connected to older practices.
