American Music History

Study American music through Indigenous traditions, migration, sacred music, blues, jazz, country, popular song, recording, and cultural exchange.

Overview

American music history is shaped by Indigenous traditions, African diaspora traditions, European settler traditions, immigration, migration, slavery, worship, labor, entertainment, recording industries, radio, cities, regions, and social movements.

This page belongs to the Music History area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Music history pages should connect musical sound to time, place, people, institutions, technologies, communities, performance practices, and cultural meaning.

Many Roots

American music grows from many traditions, including Indigenous music, African American music, European folk and classical practices, Latin American traditions, Caribbean influences, and immigrant communities.

Regions and Scenes

Cities and regions such as New Orleans, Chicago, Detroit, New York, Memphis, Nashville, Los Angeles, Atlanta, and the Bay Area shaped distinctive musical scenes.

Industry and Media

Publishing, touring, recording, radio, film, television, streaming, and music business institutions helped shape American musical life.
