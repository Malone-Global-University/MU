Medieval Music

Study chant, modes, early notation, sacred music, secular song, and the growth of polyphony in the medieval period.

Overview

Medieval music developed in religious, courtly, civic, and folk settings. It includes chant, early polyphony, modal thinking, sacred music, secular song, and major changes in notation.

This page belongs to the Music History area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Music history pages should connect musical sound to time, place, people, institutions, technologies, communities, performance practices, and cultural meaning.

Chant

Chant is a central medieval sacred tradition, often sung in unison and shaped by modes rather than modern major-minor tonality.

Notation

Medieval notation made it possible to preserve and transmit more complex musical practices across institutions and generations.

Early Polyphony

Polyphony developed as musicians combined independent vocal lines, opening a major path toward later Western composition.
