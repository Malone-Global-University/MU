Renaissance Music

Explore Renaissance music through vocal polyphony, sacred and secular song, humanism, printing, and expressive text setting.

Overview

Renaissance music is known for rich vocal polyphony, careful text setting, sacred forms such as masses and motets, secular songs such as madrigals, and the growing influence of printing and humanism.

This page belongs to the Music History area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Music history pages should connect musical sound to time, place, people, institutions, technologies, communities, performance practices, and cultural meaning.
