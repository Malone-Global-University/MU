African Diaspora Music

Study African diaspora music through rhythm, call and response, voice, community, migration, resistance, sacred practice, and global influence.

Overview

African diaspora music refers to musical traditions shaped by African peoples and their descendants across the world. It includes continuities, transformations, and new forms created through migration, enslavement, resistance, cultural memory, worship, labor, celebration, and innovation.

This page belongs to the Music History area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Music history pages should connect musical sound to time, place, people, institutions, technologies, communities, performance practices, and cultural meaning.

Musical Continuities

African diaspora traditions often preserve and transform practices such as layered rhythm, call and response, communal performance, dance, improvisation, and flexible vocal expression.

New World Traditions

Diasporic communities created new musical forms in the Americas, the Caribbean, Europe, and beyond, often under difficult historical conditions.

Global Influence

African diaspora music has profoundly shaped blues, jazz, gospel, reggae, rhythm and blues, soul, funk, hip-hop, electronic music, and global popular culture.
