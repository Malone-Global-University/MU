Classical Era

Explore the Classical era through clarity, balance, phrase structure, sonata form, symphony, quartet, concerto, and opera.

Overview

The Classical era emphasized clarity, balance, formal organization, elegant phrase structure, and public musical life. It developed major instrumental forms such as the symphony, string quartet, concerto, and sonata.

This page belongs to the Music History area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Music history pages should connect musical sound to time, place, people, institutions, technologies, communities, performance practices, and cultural meaning.
