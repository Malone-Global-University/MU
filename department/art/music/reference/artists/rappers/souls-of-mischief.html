Souls of Mischief

Oakland hip-hop group known for intricate lyricism and jazz-influenced alternative rap

Biography

Souls of Mischief is a hip-hop group from Oakland, California, consisting of A-Plus, Opio, Phesto, and Tajai. Emerging in the early 1990s, they became known for their sharp lyricism, complex rhyme structures, and youthful energy. They are closely associated with the Hieroglyphics collective, a key force in West Coast underground hip-hop.

Their debut album 93 ’til Infinity is widely regarded as a classic of alternative hip-hop, featuring smooth, jazz-infused production and highly technical lyricism. The title track became one of the most iconic songs of 1990s underground rap. Their music emphasized creativity, wordplay, and abstract storytelling rather than mainstream trends.

Over time, Souls of Mischief remained active within the Hieroglyphics crew, continuing to tour and release music. Their influence helped define the identity of independent West Coast hip-hop.

Legacy

Souls of Mischief’s legacy is rooted in lyrical complexity and underground innovation. They are widely respected as pioneers of intelligent, jazz-influenced West Coast hip-hop. Their influence continues across independent rap, technical lyricism, and alternative hip-hop collectives.
