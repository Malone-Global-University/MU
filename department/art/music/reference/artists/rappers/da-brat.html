Da Brat

Chicago rapper and pioneer female MC known for fast delivery and early mainstream success in hip-hop

Biography

Da Brat, born Shawntae Harris in Chicago, Illinois, is a rapper and one of the first female solo MCs to achieve major commercial success in hip-hop. She emerged in the mid-1990s after being discovered by Jermaine Dupri and signed to So So Def Recordings. Her rapid-fire delivery and confident style helped her stand out in a male-dominated rap landscape.

Her debut album Funkdafied became a breakthrough success, establishing her as a prominent voice in hip-hop and making her one of the earliest female rap artists to go platinum. The project combined West Coast-influenced production with Southern and Midwest rap sensibilities. Her follow-up work continued to build her reputation as a skilled and versatile MC.

Da Brat later expanded into television and radio while remaining a respected figure in hip-hop culture. Her influence helped open doors for future generations of female rappers in mainstream music.

Legacy

Da Brat’s legacy is rooted in breaking barriers for female MCs in mainstream hip-hop. She is widely recognized as one of the first solo female rappers to achieve commercial success and visibility on a national scale. Her influence continues through artists inspired by her flow, confidence, and early industry breakthroughs.
