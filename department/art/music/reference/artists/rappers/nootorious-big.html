The Notorious B.I.G.

Brooklyn rapper known for elite storytelling, 1990s East Coast dominance, and timeless hip-hop influence

Biography

The Notorious B.I.G., born Christopher Wallace in Brooklyn, New York, was a rapper widely regarded as one of the greatest storytellers in hip-hop history. He rose to prominence in the mid-1990s through his debut album and collaborations with the East Coast rap scene. His style combined smooth delivery, vivid narratives, and effortless charisma.

He became a central figure in East Coast hip-hop during the 1990s, helping define the sound of New York rap alongside other major artists of the era. His music often depicted street life, ambition, and personal reflection with cinematic detail. His second album further solidified his reputation as a dominant force in rap before his untimely death in 1997.

The Notorious B.I.G. is widely regarded as one of the most influential rappers of all time.

Legacy

The Notorious B.I.G.’s legacy is defined by storytelling excellence and foundational East Coast hip-hop influence. He is widely considered one of the greatest rappers in music history. His influence continues across lyrical rap, storytelling techniques, and global hip-hop culture.
