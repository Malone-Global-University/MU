ScHoolboy Q

Los Angeles rapper known for gritty storytelling and West Coast hip-hop style

Biography

ScHoolboy Q, born Quincy Matthew Hanley in Germany and raised in South Central Los Angeles, is a rapper known for his distinctive voice, gritty storytelling, and West Coast hip-hop influence. He emerged from the Black Hippy collective alongside artists like Kendrick Lamar, helping shape a new era of West Coast rap. His music often reflects street life, personal struggles, and growth.

He gained mainstream attention with projects such as Oxymoron, which debuted at the top of the charts and showcased his ability to balance commercial appeal with raw lyricism. Later albums like Blank Face LP further expanded his artistic depth and cinematic storytelling style. His sound blends trap energy with classic West Coast production influences.

ScHoolboy Q is known for his versatility, shifting between aggressive tracks, introspective storytelling, and club-focused music.

Legacy

ScHoolboy Q’s legacy is defined by West Coast revival and versatile street storytelling. He is widely recognized as one of the key figures in modern Los Angeles hip-hop. His influence continues across mainstream rap, West Coast revivalism, and narrative-driven hip-hop.
