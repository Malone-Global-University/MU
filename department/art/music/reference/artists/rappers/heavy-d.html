Heavy D

Rapper, producer, actor, and frontman of Heavy D & The Boyz

Biography

Heavy D, born Dwight Arrington Myers in Mandeville, Jamaica, and raised in Mount Vernon, New York, became one of hip-hop’s most charismatic and versatile performers. As the leader of Heavy D & The Boyz, he helped shape a sound that blended hip-hop with R&B melodies, reggae influences, and upbeat, radio-friendly production. His presence brought a smoother, more musical energy to late 1980s and early 1990s rap.

The group debuted with Living Large in 1987, but achieved wider recognition with Big Tyme (1989), which included the hit “Somebody for Me.” Heavy D’s confident delivery and melodic sense helped bridge the gap between hip-hop and mainstream pop audiences. His work stood out for its positivity, charisma, and emphasis on groove and performance.

In 1991, Heavy D & The Boyz released Peaceful Journey, which included the hit single “Now That We Found Love.” The song became one of their most successful records and helped define the sound of early 1990s crossover hip-hop and dance music. The album also reflected a more mature tone following the death of group member Trouble T-Roy.

Heavy D continued his success with Nuttin’ but Love (1994), which featured collaborations with major artists and further refined his blend of rap and R&B. Beyond music, he became an actor and television presence, appearing in films and shows while also working behind the scenes as a producer and executive in the entertainment industry.

Legacy

Heavy D is remembered as one of the key figures who helped bring hip-hop into mainstream popular culture without abandoning musicality or authenticity. His ability to merge rap with melody influenced later generations of artists working at the intersection of hip-hop and R&B.

He is also recognized for his positive image, stage presence, and contributions as a bridge between early hip-hop culture and the more polished commercial sound of the 1990s. His influence extends beyond music into film, television, and production.

Death

Heavy D died on November 8, 2011, at the age of 44 in Los Angeles, California. His death was caused by complications from a pulmonary embolism. He was widely mourned across the entertainment industry and remains celebrated as a foundational figure in hip-hop and crossover R&B.
