Roxanne Shanté

Queens MC and battle rap pioneer known for the Roxanne Wars and early female hip-hop leadership

Biography

Roxanne Shanté, born Lolita Shanté Gooden in Queens, New York, is a pioneering rapper widely recognized as one of the first major female MCs in hip-hop history. She gained early fame as a teenager during the mid-1980s through her sharp battle rap responses in the Roxanne Wars. Her rapid lyrical delivery and confrontational style helped establish female presence in competitive rap.

She became a central figure in early hip-hop culture, particularly within the Juice Crew collective associated with Marley Marl. Her recordings demonstrated that women could compete at the highest level of lyrical battle rap during hip-hop’s formative years. She later expanded her career into media and mentoring roles.

Roxanne Shanté is widely regarded as one of the foundational voices of female rap and battle MC culture.

Legacy

Roxanne Shanté’s legacy is defined by pioneering female MC status and early battle rap innovation. She is widely recognized as a key figure in establishing women’s presence in hip-hop’s competitive era. Her influence continues across battle rap culture, female hip-hop artistry, and early New York rap history.
