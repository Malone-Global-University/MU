Gangsta Boo

Trailblazing Memphis rapper and one of Southern hip-hop’s most influential female voices

Biography

Gangsta Boo, born Lola Mitchell, was a pioneering rapper from Memphis, Tennessee, known for her distinctive voice, confident delivery, and role in shaping Southern hip-hop during its rise in the 1990s. She emerged in a male-dominated rap scene and quickly established herself as one of its most recognizable and influential figures.

Raised in Memphis, she became connected to the city’s underground rap movement at a young age. Her lyrical style often combined vivid street imagery with sharp rhythm and assertive performance, helping define the darker, atmospheric sound associated with Memphis rap.

Gangsta Boo was also known for breaking barriers for women in Southern rap, proving that female MCs could play a central role in shaping regional hip-hop movements rather than existing on the margins.

Legacy

Gangsta Boo remains a key figure in the history of Memphis rap and Southern hip-hop. Her influence can be heard in later generations of artists who adopted her confident delivery and atmospheric style.

Her contributions helped expand the role of women in hip-hop and solidified her place as one of the defining voices of the Memphis rap era.
