Killer Mike

Atlanta rapper known for politically charged lyricism, activism, and Run the Jewels collaboration

Biography

Killer Mike, born Michael Render in Atlanta, Georgia, is a rapper, activist, and entrepreneur known for his politically conscious lyricism and strong Southern rap foundation. He first gained attention through his work with OutKast’s Big Boi, appearing on the hit single “The Whole World.” His early career established him as a force in Atlanta hip-hop.

He later gained critical acclaim as part of the duo Run the Jewels alongside producer El-P, where his aggressive delivery and socially focused writing reached a global audience. His solo work also explores themes of inequality, community, and systemic issues. He has become a prominent voice in both music and public political discourse.

Killer Mike is also involved in business ventures and activism focused on economic empowerment and community development.

Legacy

Killer Mike’s legacy is defined by political rap influence and activist-driven artistry. He is widely regarded as one of the most impactful socially conscious rappers of his generation. His influence continues across hip-hop, activism, and artist-led entrepreneurship.
