Master P

Rapper, entrepreneur, and founder of No Limit Records who transformed Southern hip-hop business and culture

Biography

Master P, born Percy Miller in New Orleans, Louisiana, is a rapper, entrepreneur, and record executive best known as the founder of No Limit Records. Emerging in the 1990s, he became one of the most influential figures in Southern hip-hop, both as an artist and as a business innovator. His approach to independent ownership reshaped how rap labels operated nationally.

He gained early recognition through his independent releases before building No Limit Records into a dominant force in hip-hop. The label’s signature sound combined heavy Southern beats, street-centered storytelling, and a high-volume release strategy that saturated the market. Albums such as Ghetto D helped propel him and his roster into mainstream success.

Master P also launched and guided the careers of numerous artists, including Silkk the Shocker, C-Murder, and Mystikal. His influence extended beyond music into film, business, and sports management, establishing him as a multifaceted entrepreneur.

Legacy

Master P’s legacy is rooted in independence and entrepreneurship in hip-hop. He is widely recognized for revolutionizing the business model of Southern rap through No Limit Records. His impact continues through artists and executives inspired by his approach to ownership and self-determination in the music industry.
