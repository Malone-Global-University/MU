SPM

Houston rapper and underground Texas hip-hop figure known for early South Park Mexican era influence

Biography

SPM (South Park Mexican), born Carlos Coy in Houston, Texas, is a rapper who played a notable role in the development of underground Southern hip-hop. Emerging in the 1990s, he built a following through independent releases that reflected street narratives and Houston neighborhood culture. His early work contributed to the broader visibility of Texas rap outside the mainstream industry.

He founded Dope House Records, an independent label that helped launch and support a network of Houston-area artists. His music often incorporated storytelling focused on life in South Park, Houston, and surrounding communities. This independent approach helped solidify his presence in the Southern underground rap scene.

Over time, his career became controversial and legally constrained, but his early influence on Houston hip-hop remains part of regional rap history. His catalog is still referenced in discussions of independent Southern rap development during the 1990s and early 2000s.

Legacy

SPM’s legacy is tied to the rise of independent Houston rap and early Southern underground distribution networks. He is recognized for contributing to the regional identity of Texas hip-hop through independent production and storytelling. His influence remains part of the broader history of Houston’s rap evolution.
