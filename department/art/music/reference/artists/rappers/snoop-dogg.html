Snoop Dogg

Rapper, songwriter, producer, actor, and global icon of West Coast hip-hop

Biography

Snoop Dogg, born Calvin Cordozar Broadus Jr. in Long Beach, California, is one of the most recognizable figures in hip-hop history. He emerged in the early 1990s after being discovered by Dr. Dre, quickly becoming a defining voice of the G-funk era. His laid-back delivery, melodic flow, and distinctive vocal style helped shape the sound of West Coast rap during its most influential period.

Snoop first gained widespread attention through his appearances on Dr. Dre’s landmark album The Chronic, followed by his debut solo album Doggystyle, which became a major commercial and cultural success. These releases established him as a leading artist in hip-hop and introduced a signature style that blended funk-inspired production with smooth, conversational rapping.

Over the course of his career, Snoop Dogg expanded far beyond music into film, television, business, and media. He became a cultural ambassador for hip-hop, collaborating with artists across genres and generations while maintaining his West Coast identity. His versatility allowed him to remain relevant across multiple decades of changing musical trends.

Legacy

Snoop Dogg’s legacy is defined by longevity, cultural influence, and global recognition. He helped popularize West Coast hip-hop worldwide and became one of the genre’s most enduring personalities. His influence extends beyond music into entertainment, branding, and popular culture, making him one of the most iconic figures to emerge from hip-hop.
