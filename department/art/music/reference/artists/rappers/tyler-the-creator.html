Tyler, The Creator

Rapper, producer, and designer known for genre-blending alternative hip-hop and bold creative direction

Biography

Tyler, The Creator, born Tyler Gregory Okonma in Los Angeles, California, is a rapper, producer, and creative director known for his genre-blending approach to hip-hop. He first emerged in the late 2000s as a founding member of the collective Odd Future, gaining attention for his unconventional production style and provocative artistic persona. His early work helped reshape alternative hip-hop culture.

His solo career began with albums that emphasized experimental production and vivid storytelling, later evolving into more melodic and musically diverse projects. Albums such as Flower Boy, IGOR, and Call Me If You Get Lost showcased his growth into a highly versatile composer and arranger. His music often blends hip-hop, jazz, R&B, and pop influences.

Beyond music, Tyler has built a strong presence in fashion, design, and visual art through brands like Golf Wang. His work consistently emphasizes full creative control across music, visuals, and branding.

Legacy

Tyler, The Creator’s legacy is defined by artistic reinvention and genre fusion. He is widely recognized as one of the most influential creative figures in modern alternative hip-hop. His impact continues across music, fashion, and visual culture worldwide.
