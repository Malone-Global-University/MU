Big Boi

Rapper, songwriter, and half of OutKast who helped redefine Southern hip-hop

Biography

Big Boi, born Antwan André Patton in Savannah, Georgia, is a rapper, songwriter, and one half of the influential hip-hop duo OutKast. Alongside André 3000, he helped transform Atlanta into one of the most important creative centers in modern hip-hop. His style is known for sharp lyricism, confident delivery, and a strong connection to Southern musical traditions.

OutKast emerged in the early 1990s under the Atlanta-based collective Dungeon Family, releasing their debut album Southernplayalisticadillacmuzik. The project introduced a distinctly Southern sound to mainstream hip-hop, blending funk, soul, and storytelling rooted in Atlanta culture. Big Boi’s grounded lyrical style provided a strong balance to the duo’s experimental direction.

Over the course of OutKast’s career, Big Boi contributed to landmark albums such as Aquemini and Stankonia, which expanded the boundaries of hip-hop. He later pursued solo projects while continuing to perform and collaborate across genres. His work helped solidify Atlanta’s global influence in music and popular culture.

Legacy

Big Boi’s legacy is closely tied to the rise of Southern hip-hop and the global success of OutKast. He is recognized for his lyrical precision, versatility, and role in establishing Atlanta as a major hub of musical innovation. His influence continues through artists who draw on Southern storytelling and genre-blending production styles.
