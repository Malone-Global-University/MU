Atlanta rapper known for trap-era hits, charismatic delivery, and mainstream Southern hip-hop success

Biography

2 Chains, born Tauheed Epps in College Park, Georgia, is a rapper known for his distinctive voice, punchline-heavy lyricism, and strong presence in Southern hip-hop. He initially gained recognition as part of the duo Playaz Circle before achieving major solo success in the early 2010s. His music blends trap production with humorous, luxury-focused, and lifestyle-driven themes.

He rose to mainstream prominence with a series of charting singles and high-profile collaborations across hip-hop and pop music. His solo career established him as one of the defining voices of Atlanta’s modern rap era. His style is marked by charisma, comedic timing, and confident delivery over heavy Southern beats.

2 Chains is widely recognized as a key figure in modern trap music’s mainstream expansion.

Legacy

His legacy is defined by trap-era mainstream success and a distinctive lyrical personality. He is widely associated with Atlanta’s dominance in 2010s hip-hop and the commercialization of Southern trap. His influence continues across rap culture, fashion-forward hip-hop branding, and modern Southern rap style.
