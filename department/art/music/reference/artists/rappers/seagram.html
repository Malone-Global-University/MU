Seagram

Houston rapper known for 1990s gangsta rap, raw Southern storytelling, and underground hip-hop influence

Biography

Seagram, born Seagram Miller in Oakland, California, was a rapper known for his raw storytelling and contributions to early 1990s West Coast and Southern-influenced gangsta rap. He later became associated with Houston’s hip-hop scene, reflecting a blend of regional influences in his music. His lyrical style emphasized street narratives, realism, and emotional intensity.

He released music during a formative period for Southern rap, contributing to the underground development of Houston hip-hop culture. His work is often remembered for its authenticity and unfiltered depiction of street life. His career was tragically cut short, but his recordings continue to circulate within underground hip-hop communities.

Seagram is remembered as a respected voice in early underground gangsta rap.

Legacy

Seagram’s legacy is defined by raw street storytelling and early Houston underground hip-hop influence. He is widely recognized for his authentic lyrical style and regional cross-influence between West Coast and Southern rap. His impact continues within underground gangsta rap and Houston hip-hop history.
