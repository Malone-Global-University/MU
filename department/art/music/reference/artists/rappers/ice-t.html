Ice-T

Los Angeles rapper and actor known for pioneering gangsta rap and long-standing influence in hip-hop and television

Biography

Ice-T, born Tracy Lauren Marrow in Newark, New Jersey, is a rapper, actor, and cultural figure closely associated with the rise of West Coast gangsta rap. He moved to Los Angeles and became one of the earliest artists to popularize street-centered narratives in hip-hop. His music often reflects urban survival, social commentary, and street experience.

He gained recognition in the 1980s with early recordings that helped define gangsta rap before the genre became mainstream. He later expanded his career into acting, most notably in television roles that brought him long-term mainstream visibility. His work spans both music and screen performance, making him a crossover figure in entertainment.

Ice-T is widely regarded as one of the foundational voices of gangsta rap.

Legacy

Ice-T’s legacy is defined by pioneering gangsta rap and bridging hip-hop with mainstream television. He is widely respected as one of the earliest artists to shape West Coast rap storytelling. His influence continues across hip-hop history, crime narrative rap, and entertainment crossover culture.
