50 Cent

Queens rapper known for breakout success, G-Unit leadership, and 2000s hip-hop dominance

Biography

50 Cent, born Curtis Jackson in Queens, New York, is a rapper, entrepreneur, and cultural figure who rose to fame in the early 2000s. He gained major attention through his mixtape work before achieving global success with his debut album Get Rich or Die Tryin’. His style blends aggressive delivery, street narratives, and mainstream-ready hooks.

He became one of the defining artists of 2000s hip-hop through both solo success and leadership of the G-Unit collective. His influence extended beyond music into film, television, and business ventures. He helped shape the commercial direction of hip-hop during the early digital era.

50 Cent is widely recognized as one of the most influential rappers and hip-hop entrepreneurs of his generation.

Legacy

50 Cent’s legacy is defined by mainstream hip-hop dominance and entrepreneurial expansion. He is widely regarded as a key figure in 2000s rap commercialization and branding strategy. His influence continues across music, television production, and hip-hop business culture.
