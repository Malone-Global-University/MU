New Orleans producer known for shaping Cash Money Records sound and defining Southern hip-hop production

Biography

:contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}, born Byron Thomas in New Orleans, Louisiana, is a producer and rapper best known for his work with Cash Money Records. He played a central role in defining the label’s signature sound during its rise in the late 1990s and early 2000s. His production style blends bounce music influences with Southern hip-hop rhythms and melodic synth-driven beats.

He is widely credited with producing much of Cash Money’s early catalog, helping launch the careers of major Southern rap artists. His sound became a defining element of the “Hot Boys” era and the broader Cash Money movement. He later pursued solo work while maintaining his legacy as a foundational Southern hip-hop producer.

:contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2} is widely regarded as one of the key architects of modern Southern hip-hop production.

Legacy

His legacy is defined by shaping the Cash Money Records sound and elevating New Orleans bounce into mainstream hip-hop. He is widely associated with the golden era of Southern rap production. His influence continues across modern trap, bounce-influenced beats, and hip-hop production culture.
