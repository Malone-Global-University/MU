Mac 10

Rapper and West Coast artist known for his work in Westside Connection and gangsta rap

Biography

Mac 10, born Dedrick D’Mon Rolison in Inglewood, California, is a rapper known for his role in the West Coast hip-hop scene during the 1990s and early 2000s. Emerging during a period when gangsta rap was shaping mainstream music culture, he developed a style rooted in street narratives, aggressive delivery, and West Coast identity. His work placed him within the broader Los Angeles rap ecosystem alongside some of the genre’s most influential figures.

He became widely recognized as a member of Westside Connection, the supergroup formed with Ice Cube and WC. The group’s music emphasized West Coast pride and hard-edged commentary on regional tensions within hip-hop during the 1990s. Their projects helped solidify Mac 10’s visibility on a national level and positioned him within one of the era’s most prominent collaborative rap formations.

In addition to his group work, Mac 10 released solo material that reflected the sound and themes of West Coast gangsta rap. His recordings often focused on street survival, loyalty, and the realities of urban life in Southern California. While not always positioned in the mainstream spotlight, he maintained a steady presence in the West Coast underground and collaboration circuits.

Legacy

Mac 10’s legacy is tied to his contributions to Westside Connection and the broader West Coast rap movement of the 1990s. He represents a generation of Los Angeles artists who helped define the sound, identity, and cultural narratives of gangsta rap. His work continues to be referenced in discussions of regional collaboration and the evolution of West Coast hip-hop collectives.
