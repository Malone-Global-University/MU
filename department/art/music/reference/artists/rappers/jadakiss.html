Fat Joe

Bronx rapper known for Terror Squad leadership, East Coast hip-hop longevity, and crossover success

Biography

Fat Joe, born Joseph Antonio Cartagena in the Bronx, New York, is a rapper, entrepreneur, and founder of the Terror Squad collective. Emerging in the early 1990s, he became one of the most prominent Latino figures in East Coast hip-hop. His music combines street-oriented lyricism with mainstream accessibility.

He gained recognition through albums such as Jealous One’s Envy and later achieved major commercial success with hit singles including “Lean Back.” Throughout his career, he collaborated with artists across multiple generations of hip-hop and helped introduce emerging talent to wider audiences. His leadership of Terror Squad played a major role in the careers of several notable artists, including Big Pun.

Fat Joe has maintained a long and influential presence in hip-hop through music, media appearances, and business ventures.

Legacy

Fat Joe’s legacy is defined by East Coast hip-hop longevity, Terror Squad leadership, and Latino representation in rap. He is widely regarded as one of the most successful and enduring figures in New York hip-hop. His influence continues across rap entrepreneurship, artist mentorship, and mainstream East Coast rap culture.
