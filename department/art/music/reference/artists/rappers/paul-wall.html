Paul Wall

Rapper and DJ from Houston known for chopped-and-screwed era influence and Southern hip-hop culture

Biography

Paul Wall, born Paul Michael Slayton in Houston, Texas, is a rapper and DJ closely associated with the Southern hip-hop movement and the city’s chopped-and-screwed culture. Emerging in the late 1990s and early 2000s, he became known for his collaborations with Swishahouse and his involvement in Houston’s underground mixtape scene. His early work helped popularize the slowed-down aesthetic of Texas rap.

He gained mainstream attention with his solo projects and collaborations, including his breakout success with The Peoples Champ. His music often features laid-back delivery, Houston slang, and production influenced by DJ Screw’s legacy. Paul Wall also became widely recognized for bridging underground Houston rap with national commercial audiences.

Beyond music, he became a cultural figure representing Houston hip-hop identity, often highlighting regional pride and the city’s unique rap traditions. His collaborations with artists across Southern rap further solidified his presence in the broader hip-hop landscape.

Legacy

Paul Wall’s legacy is tied to the mainstream visibility of Houston’s chopped-and-screwed culture. He is recognized for helping bring Southern underground rap aesthetics into national awareness. His influence continues through artists inspired by Houston’s slowed, melodic, and regional rap style.
