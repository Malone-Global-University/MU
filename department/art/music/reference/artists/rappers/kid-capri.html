Kid Capri

New York DJ known for mixtape innovation, live turntablism, and hip-hop DJ culture influence

Biography

Kid Capri, born David Anthony Love Jr. in New York City, is a DJ and producer known for his influence on mixtape culture and live hip-hop performances. He became one of the most prominent DJs of the late 1980s and 1990s, known for energetic sets and technical turntablism. His work helped define the sound and structure of modern hip-hop DJing.

He gained early fame through mixtapes that circulated widely in New York and beyond, establishing him as a key figure in DJ-driven hip-hop culture. He later expanded into mainstream recognition through tours, television appearances, and collaborations with major artists. His influence extends into both club DJing and hip-hop performance traditions.

Kid Capri is widely regarded as one of the foundational DJs in modern hip-hop history.

Legacy

Kid Capri’s legacy is defined by pioneering mixtape culture and elevating the role of the DJ in hip-hop. He is widely respected as one of the most influential turntablists of his generation. His impact continues across DJ performance culture, mixtape history, and hip-hop production standards.
