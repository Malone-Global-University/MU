EPMD

Hip-hop duo known for funk-based production, smooth delivery, and East Coast rap influence

Biography

EPMD, short for “Erick and Parrish Making Dollars,” is an influential hip-hop duo from Long Island, New York, consisting of Erick Sermon and Parrish Smith. Emerging in the late 1980s, the group became known for their laid-back delivery, funk-heavy production, and confident lyrical style. Their sound helped define a major strand of East Coast hip-hop rooted in groove, repetition, and sample-based beats.

The duo debuted with Strictly Business in 1988, an album that quickly became a classic of hip-hop’s golden age. Built around funk and rock samples, the project showcased their chemistry as both MCs and producers. Songs like “Strictly Business” and “You Gots to Chill” helped establish their reputation for smooth, minimal, and highly effective production.

In 1989, they released Unfinished Business, continuing their signature sound while solidifying their place in hip-hop’s elite ranks. The album included tracks such as “So Wat Cha Sayin’,” which became another defining record of the era. Their approach emphasized rhythm, swagger, and consistency rather than lyrical excess, giving their music a distinctive identity.

Throughout the early 1990s, EPMD continued to release albums that influenced both underground and mainstream rap. Despite internal tensions that led to an initial breakup, their music maintained a strong presence in hip-hop culture. They later reunited, reinforcing their legacy as one of the most important production-rap duos in the genre’s history.

Legacy

EPMD’s legacy is rooted in their pioneering use of funk sampling and minimalist production techniques. Their sound helped shape the sonic foundation of East Coast hip-hop and influenced countless producers who followed. They demonstrated that hip-hop could be both relaxed and powerful, built on groove rather than density.

The duo also helped establish the blueprint for rapper-producer partnerships in hip-hop groups. Their influence can be heard in later generations of artists who prioritize rhythm, sample craftsmanship, and steady lyrical flow.

Reunions and Influence

After their split in the early 1990s, EPMD reunited and continued to perform and record intermittently. Their reunions were widely celebrated by fans who viewed them as essential architects of hip-hop’s golden age sound.

Today, EPMD is regarded as one of the foundational duos in rap history, with a catalog that continues to be sampled, studied, and respected across generations of artists and producers.
