Peter Gunz & Lord Tariq

Bronx hip-hop duo known for 1990s East Coast rap and the hit single “Deja Vu (Uptown Baby)”

Biography

Peter Gunz and Lord Tariq are a hip-hop duo from the Bronx, New York, who rose to prominence in the mid-to-late 1990s. Emerging from the East Coast rap scene, they became known for their sharp lyricism, street-oriented storytelling, and connection to the neo-golden (platinum) era of New York hip-hop.

The duo gained national attention with their breakout single “Deja Vu (Uptown Baby)”, which sampled Steely Dan’s “Black Cow” and became a major commercial success. The track helped define their legacy as one of the notable crossover acts from the late 1990s Bronx rap movement.

Outside their work as a duo, both artists pursued solo careers and collaborations within the broader hip-hop industry. Peter Gunz became known for his role in various group projects and media appearances, while Lord Tariq maintained a presence in East Coast rap circles and collaborations.

Legacy

Their legacy is primarily anchored in “Deja Vu (Uptown Baby),” a defining record of late 1990s New York hip-hop. The song remains a recognizable anthem of Bronx rap culture and is often cited as a key example of sample-driven East Coast production.

Peter Gunz and Lord Tariq are remembered as a duo who briefly broke into mainstream success while maintaining strong ties to the underground New York rap identity that shaped their sound and influence.
