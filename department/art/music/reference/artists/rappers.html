List of African American Rap and Hip-Hop Artists

A reference directory of major African American rap and hip-hop artists, organized by historical era. Each name links to a biography page.

Overview

African American artists shaped rap and hip-hop from its earliest roots in block parties, DJ culture, MCing, spoken-word traditions, funk, soul, disco, gospel, reggae sound systems, street dance, visual art, and urban youth culture. Across the late twentieth and twenty-first centuries, Black hip-hop artists helped create and transform old school rap, golden age rap, gangsta rap, political rap, Southern hip-hop, East Coast hip-hop, West Coast hip-hop, Midwest rap, alternative hip-hop, trap, drill, melodic rap, and contemporary hip-hop.

This page is organized by broad musical era rather than by birth date alone. Some artists belong to more than one period, but each name is placed where that artist is most strongly associated for introductory navigation. Within each era, names are alphabetized by last name, stage name, or group name.
