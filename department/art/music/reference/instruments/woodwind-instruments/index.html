Woodwind Instruments

Study woodwind instruments through breath, air columns, reeds, keys, tone color, articulation, range, and ensemble writing.

Overview

Woodwind instruments produce sound through vibrating air columns. Some use reeds, while others use air directed across an opening. They are important in orchestras, bands, chamber music, jazz, theater, film, and many traditions.

Sound Production

Woodwind sound is shaped by breath, air column, embouchure, reed or opening, keys, fingerings, and instrument design.

Single Reed, Double Reed, and Air Instruments

Clarinets and saxophones use single reeds. Oboes and bassoons use double reeds. Flutes create sound through air directed across an opening.

Musical Uses

Woodwinds can carry melody, harmony, color, counterpoint, solos, ensemble blend, jazz improvisation, and orchestral character.
