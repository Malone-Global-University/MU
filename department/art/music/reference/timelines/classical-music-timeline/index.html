Classical Music Timeline

Trace classical music through notation, forms, composers, instruments, performance institutions, and historical periods.

Overview

The Classical Music Timeline organizes the long development of Western classical and concert traditions. It connects composers, forms, instruments, notation, institutions, performance practice, and major historical shifts.

This page belongs to the Music Timelines area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Timeline pages should connect dates and periods to listening, artists, recordings, technologies, communities, institutions, and cultural context.

Early Periods

Medieval and Renaissance music establish important traditions in chant, notation, vocal polyphony, sacred music, and secular song.

Common-Practice Periods

Baroque, Classical, and Romantic music develop major forms, harmonic systems, orchestral practices, opera, chamber music, and public concert traditions.

Modern and Contemporary Classical Music

Twentieth-century and contemporary composers expand harmony, rhythm, timbre, technology, notation, and performance practice.
