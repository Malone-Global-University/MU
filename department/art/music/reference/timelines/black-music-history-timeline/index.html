Overview

The Black Music History Timeline connects African musical continuities, African American history, spirituals, blues, jazz, gospel, R&B, soul, funk, disco, hip-hop, house, techno, and global popular music.

This page belongs to the Music Timelines area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Timeline pages should connect dates and periods to listening, artists, recordings, technologies, communities, institutions, and cultural context.

Continuities and Transformations

Black music history includes continuities in rhythm, voice, call and response, improvisation, dance, community, and spiritual practice alongside constant innovation.

Modern Genre Formation

Blues, jazz, gospel, R&B, soul, funk, disco, hip-hop, house, and techno show the breadth of Black musical creativity.

Global Impact

Black music has transformed global popular music, production, performance, dance, media, and public culture.
