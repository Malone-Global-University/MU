Reference and Directories

Use music reference tools to locate artists, composers, instruments, terms, genres, albums, songs, timelines, biographies, and listening guides across the Music Department.

Overview

The Reference and Directories section of the Malone Global University Music Department is designed as a navigation and research hub. It helps students locate names, terms, instruments, recordings, genres, periods, biographies, and listening materials without having to search through every course or lesson page one by one.

This section supports both quick lookup and deeper study. A beginner may come here to learn the meaning of a musical term, identify an instrument family, or find a listening guide. A researcher may use the same section to compare artists, trace historical periods, locate biographies, study genre vocabulary, or connect albums and songs to broader musical traditions.

Reference pages should remain connected to the larger department. Artist directories should link to genre, history, culture, album, and biography pages. Glossaries should connect terms back to theory, performance, technology, and foundations pages. Timelines should connect individual events to broader historical movement. The goal is not to isolate facts, but to make the Music Department easier to navigate and study.

How to Use This Section

Students who are new to music study can begin with the Music Terms Glossary, Instrument Guide, Genre Glossary, and Beginner Listening pages. These pages build the vocabulary needed to understand other music lessons and reference materials.

Students studying artists can begin with Artist Directories and Biography Index, then move into Album Guides, Song Guides, Timelines, and genre pages. This path helps connect individual artists to larger musical traditions.

Students studying history can begin with Music Timelines, then use Composer Directories, Artist Directories, Genre Glossary pages, and Listening Guides to connect dates and events to actual music. Students studying production can use the recording terms, album guides, song guides, and production listening pages alongside Music Technology and Production lessons.

Page Building Notes

Each reference page should have a clear scope. A glossary page should define what kind of terms it includes. A directory page should explain what kinds of people, groups, works, or resources are listed. A timeline page should explain its date range and organizing logic. A listening guide should explain what the listener should focus on.

Reference pages should be easy to scan. Use short introductions, grouped lists, alphabetical organization when appropriate, and links to related department pages. When a page grows large, it can be divided into child pages by letter, genre, era, region, instrument, role, or study purpose.

To avoid duplicate maintenance, the department should prefer one canonical page for each major artist, composer, album, song, term, instrument, or genre. That canonical page can then be linked from multiple directories. This keeps the Music Department organized while still allowing the same topic to appear in several useful study contexts.
