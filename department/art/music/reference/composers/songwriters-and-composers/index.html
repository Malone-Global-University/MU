Songwriters and Composers

Study songwriters and composers through melody, lyrics, harmony, form, collaboration, performance, recording, and musical craft.

Overview

Songwriters and composers create musical works in many traditions. Some write lyrics, some write melodies, some create complete songs, and many work collaboratively with performers, producers, arrangers, publishers, theater teams, or recording artists.

This page belongs to the Composer Directories area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Composer directory pages should help students connect creators to works, genres, forms, media, historical periods, instruments, performance settings, and listening paths.

Songcraft

Songwriters shape musical ideas through melody, lyric, rhythm, harmony, hook, phrase, form, and emotional focus.

Collaboration

Many songs are collaborative works involving writers, artists, producers, arrangers, instrumentalists, publishers, and recording teams.

How to Study Songwriters

Students should compare lyrics, melody, chord progressions, song form, performance, recording context, and the relationship between songwriter and artist.
