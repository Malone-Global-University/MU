Sampling

Learn how sampling reuses, transforms, chops, loops, layers, and recontextualizes recorded sound in new music.

Overview

Sampling uses recorded sound as musical material. Producers may loop, chop, pitch, stretch, filter, layer, rearrange, or transform samples to create new rhythms, textures, hooks, and compositions.

This page belongs to the Music Technology and Production area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Technology pages should connect sound, creativity, tools, workflows, production decisions, listening, distribution, and musical meaning.

Sample Sources

Samples may come from records, live recordings, field recordings, sound libraries, spoken word, drums, instruments, or self-created audio.

Chopping and Looping

Chopping divides a sample into smaller pieces. Looping repeats a sound or phrase to create a musical pattern.

Legal and Cultural Context

Sampling has artistic, cultural, historical, and legal dimensions. Students should understand creativity, credit, licensing, clearance, and musical transformation.
