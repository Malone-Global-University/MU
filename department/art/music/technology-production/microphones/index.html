Microphones

Learn how microphones capture sound and how placement, polar patterns, gain, and microphone type shape recordings.

Overview

Microphones convert sound into electrical signals. Different microphone types, polar patterns, placements, rooms, and gain settings can dramatically change the recorded sound.

Microphone Types

Common microphone types include dynamic, condenser, and ribbon microphones. Each type responds to sound differently and is useful in different situations.

Polar Patterns

A polar pattern describes how a microphone picks up sound from different directions. Common patterns include cardioid, omnidirectional, figure-eight, and supercardioid.

Microphone Placement

Microphone placement affects tone, balance, room sound, proximity effect, clarity, and noise. Small changes in placement can make a large difference.
