Recording

Learn how musical performances become recordings through studios, microphones, signal flow, tracking, overdubbing, editing, and monitoring.

Overview

Recording is the process of capturing sound so it can be preserved, edited, mixed, distributed, and heard later. It can involve live performance, studio production, field recording, home recording, or digital audio workflows.

This page belongs to the Music Technology and Production area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Technology pages should connect sound, creativity, tools, workflows, production decisions, listening, distribution, and musical meaning.

Signal Chain

A recording signal chain describes the path sound takes from source to storage, often moving through a microphone, cable, preamp, interface, software, and monitoring system.

Tracking and Overdubbing

Tracking captures performances. Overdubbing adds new parts over existing material, allowing recordings to be built in layers.

Recording Contexts

Recording can happen in professional studios, home studios, live venues, classrooms, churches, field locations, and mobile setups.
