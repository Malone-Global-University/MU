Music Distribution

Learn how music moves from finished recordings to listeners through platforms, metadata, distributors, formats, promotion, and archives.

Overview

Music distribution is the process of delivering music to audiences. Today it includes streaming platforms, digital stores, social media, physical media, licensing channels, archives, and direct-to-fan systems.

This page belongs to the Music Technology and Production area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Technology pages should connect sound, creativity, tools, workflows, production decisions, listening, distribution, and musical meaning.

Platforms and Formats

Music may be distributed through streaming, downloads, CDs, vinyl, video platforms, radio, sync licensing, direct sales, and archives.

Metadata

Metadata includes artist names, titles, credits, identifiers, release dates, labels, rights information, artwork, and other information needed for discovery and payment.

Release Planning

Distribution connects to mastering, artwork, promotion, audience strategy, rights management, publishing, royalties, and long-term catalog care.
