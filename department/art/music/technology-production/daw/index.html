Digital Audio Workstations

Learn how DAWs organize audio, MIDI, editing, plugins, automation, virtual instruments, mixing, and production sessions.

Overview

A digital audio workstation, or DAW, is software used to record, edit, arrange, process, mix, and produce music. DAWs are central to modern home studios, professional studios, film scoring, beatmaking, podcasting, and live playback.

This page belongs to the Music Technology and Production area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Technology pages should connect sound, creativity, tools, workflows, production decisions, listening, distribution, and musical meaning.

Editing

DAWs allow precise editing of timing, pitch, structure, noise, fades, takes, and arrangement.

Plugins and Virtual Instruments

Plugins add effects, processing, analysis, and sound generation. Virtual instruments allow users to create sounds inside the computer.
