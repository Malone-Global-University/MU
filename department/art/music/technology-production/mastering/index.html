Mastering

Learn how mastering prepares finished mixes for release, translation, sequencing, loudness, formats, and quality control.

Overview

Mastering is the final technical and artistic stage before distribution. It prepares a mix or set of mixes so they translate well across playback systems and meet release requirements.

This page belongs to the Music Technology and Production area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Technology pages should connect sound, creativity, tools, workflows, production decisions, listening, distribution, and musical meaning.

Final Polish

Mastering can adjust EQ, dynamics, stereo image, loudness, spacing, fades, and overall consistency.

Translation

A master should work across headphones, speakers, cars, phones, clubs, streaming platforms, and other playback systems.

Release Preparation

Mastering may prepare files for streaming, download, CD, vinyl, broadcast, video, or archive formats.
