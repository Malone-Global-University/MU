Music Technology and Production

Study how music is recorded, produced, edited, mixed, mastered, performed, distributed, and preserved through technology.

Overview

Music Technology and Production connects creative musical decisions to technical systems. Students learn how sound is captured, shaped, arranged, edited, mixed, mastered, amplified, distributed, and heard through modern and historical technologies.

This page belongs to the Music Technology and Production area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Technology pages should connect sound, creativity, tools, workflows, production decisions, listening, distribution, and musical meaning.

Purpose of Music Technology Study

Music technology study helps students understand the tools and systems that shape modern sound. It connects musical creativity to recording equipment, software, signal flow, production methods, media platforms, and listening environments.

Production Workflow

A production workflow may include writing, arranging, recording, editing, MIDI programming, sampling, sound design, mixing, mastering, distribution, and live performance preparation.

Creative and Technical Work

Music production is both artistic and technical. Strong producers, engineers, and musicians learn to make choices that serve the song, performance, genre, artist, audience, and listening context.
