List of African American Jazz Musicians

A reference directory of major African American jazz artists, organized by historical era. Each name links to a biography page.

Overview

African American musicians shaped jazz from its earliest roots in New Orleans, blues, ragtime, spirituals, brass bands, dance music, and improvisational traditions. Across the twentieth and twenty-first centuries, Black jazz artists helped create and transform early jazz, swing, bebop, cool jazz, hard bop, soul jazz, avant-garde jazz, free jazz, jazz fusion, and contemporary jazz.

This page is organized by broad musical era rather than by birth date alone. Some artists belong to more than one period, but each name is placed where that musician is most strongly associated for introductory navigation. Within each era, names are alphabetized by last name.

Early Jazz, New Orleans, and Classic Jazz

These musicians represent the foundations of jazz performance, including early brass-band traditions, New Orleans improvisation, cornet and trumpet styles, clarinet traditions, early drumming, and the first major recordings that brought jazz to national attention.
