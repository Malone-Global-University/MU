List of African American Pop Music Artists

A reference directory of major African American pop music artists, organized by historical era. Each name links to a biography page.

Overview

African American artists have shaped pop music from its earliest modern forms through rhythm and blues, gospel, jazz, blues, soul, rock and roll, doo-wop, disco, funk, dance music, hip-hop, electronic music, and contemporary R&B. Pop music is not one narrow sound; it is the public-facing space where many musical traditions meet mass audiences, radio, television, film, streaming platforms, dance culture, fashion, and performance.

This page is organized by broad musical era rather than by birth date alone. Some artists belong to more than one period, and many also belong to R&B, soul, funk, disco, hip-hop, rock, gospel, jazz, or dance music history. Each name is placed where that artist is most strongly associated for introductory navigation. Within each era, names are alphabetized by last name, stage name, or group name.

Early Pop and Vocal Traditions

These artists helped shape American popular song before and during the rise of modern pop. Their work connected jazz singing, blues, theater, gospel, film, radio, cabaret, swing, and ballad performance. They helped open pathways for later Black pop performers by bringing vocal artistry, style, elegance, phrasing, and stage presence to national and international audiences.

  • Nat King Cole
  • Sam Cooke
  • Dorothy Dandridge
  • Duke Ellington
  • Ella Fitzgerald
  • Billie Holiday
  • Lena Horne
  • Mahalia Jackson
  • Pearl Bailey
  • Nina Simone
  • Sarah Vaughan
  • Dinah Washington
  • Ethel Waters

Doo-Wop, Rhythm and Blues, and Early Pop Crossover

Doo-wop and rhythm and blues helped form the sound of modern pop through vocal harmony, teenage romance, dance rhythms, short single-driven records, catchy hooks, and strong lead vocals. These artists helped move Black popular music across radio formats, jukeboxes, television, and national charts.
