Piano

Study piano as a keyboard instrument for melody, harmony, accompaniment, solo performance, composition, and musical understanding.

Overview

Piano performance connects physical technique with reading, rhythm, harmony, tone, pedaling, coordination, accompaniment, and interpretation. The piano is also a major tool for theory and composition.

This page belongs to the Performance Studies area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Performance pages should connect musical knowledge to physical skill, listening, interpretation, collaboration, rehearsal, and expressive communication.

Keyboard Technique

Piano technique includes posture, hand shape, fingering, touch, coordination, scales, arpeggios, and efficient movement.

Melody and Harmony

The piano can present melody, harmony, bass, rhythm, and texture at the same time.

Practice Skills

Pianists develop through slow practice, hands-separate work, rhythm control, sight-reading, listening, and interpretive decisions.
