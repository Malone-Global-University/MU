Percussion

Study percussion through rhythm, touch, timekeeping, groove, texture, timbre, drums, mallets, and global performance traditions.

Overview

Percussion instruments are played by striking, shaking, scraping, or otherwise activating a sound source. Percussion may provide pulse, rhythm, color, emphasis, texture, melody, or dramatic effect.

This page belongs to the Performance Studies area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Performance pages should connect musical knowledge to physical skill, listening, interpretation, collaboration, rehearsal, and expressive communication.

Timekeeping

Percussion often supports musical time through pulse, meter, groove, subdivision, and rhythmic pattern.

Drums and Mallets

Percussion includes drums, cymbals, hand percussion, tuned mallet instruments, auxiliary percussion, and electronic percussion.

Global Traditions

Percussion is central to many musical cultures and should be studied with respect for its cultural and performance context.
