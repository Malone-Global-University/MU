Brass

Study brass instruments through breath, embouchure, vibration, valves, slides, tone, articulation, and ensemble power.

Overview

Brass instruments produce sound through vibrating lips and resonating tubing. Common brass instruments include trumpet, French horn, trombone, euphonium, and tuba.

This page belongs to the Performance Studies area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Performance pages should connect musical knowledge to physical skill, listening, interpretation, collaboration, rehearsal, and expressive communication.

Breath and Embouchure

Brass players use breath and lip vibration to produce tone, pitch, articulation, and dynamic contrast.

Valves and Slides

Valves and slides change tubing length, allowing brass instruments to play different pitches.

Brass Sound

Brass instruments can be brilliant, warm, powerful, lyrical, ceremonial, rhythmic, or dramatic depending on context and technique.
