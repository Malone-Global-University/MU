Voice

Study the human voice as an instrument of breath, tone, language, expression, identity, and performance.

Overview

Voice performance uses the body as the instrument. Singers coordinate breath, vocal production, resonance, language, pitch, rhythm, phrasing, interpretation, and presence.

This page belongs to the Performance Studies area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Performance pages should connect musical knowledge to physical skill, listening, interpretation, collaboration, rehearsal, and expressive communication.

Breath and Support

Singers use breath control and body coordination to support tone, phrase length, dynamics, and expressive shaping.

Tone and Diction

Vocal tone is shaped by resonance, vowel formation, style, register, and technique. Diction helps words remain clear and expressive.

Solo and Ensemble Singing

Solo singing emphasizes individual interpretation, while ensemble singing requires blend, balance, tuning, listening, and shared phrasing.
