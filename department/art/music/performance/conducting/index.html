Conducting

Study conducting as musical leadership through gesture, listening, score study, rehearsal, interpretation, and ensemble communication.

Overview

Conducting is the art of leading an ensemble. Conductors communicate tempo, meter, entrances, dynamics, phrasing, balance, and interpretation through gesture, preparation, listening, and rehearsal leadership.

This page belongs to the Performance Studies area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Performance pages should connect musical knowledge to physical skill, listening, interpretation, collaboration, rehearsal, and expressive communication.

Gesture

Conducting gestures show beat, tempo, character, dynamics, articulation, and musical direction.

Score Study

Conductors study the full score to understand form, instrumentation, entrances, balance, harmony, and expressive structure.

Rehearsal Leadership

Conductors guide rehearsal by diagnosing problems, communicating clearly, shaping interpretation, and helping the ensemble perform together.
