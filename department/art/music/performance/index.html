Performance Studies

Study music as embodied practice through voice, instruments, ensembles, interpretation, technique, improvisation, rehearsal, and performance.

Overview

Performance Studies focuses on the practice of making music. It connects listening, technique, expression, timing, breath, movement, interpretation, collaboration, preparation, and presentation.

This page belongs to the Performance Studies area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Performance pages should connect musical knowledge to physical skill, listening, interpretation, collaboration, rehearsal, and expressive communication.

Purpose of Performance Study

Performance study helps students understand how musical knowledge becomes sound through bodies, instruments, voices, rehearsal, interpretation, and communication.

Technique and Expression

Technique gives performers control, but expression gives performance meaning. Strong performance study connects both.

Collaboration

Many performances require coordination with other musicians. Ensemble awareness, listening, balance, timing, and shared interpretation are essential.
