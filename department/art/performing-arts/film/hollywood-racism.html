The Cost of Fame

V. Power, Profit, and Accountability

Every industry follows its incentives. But art also carries moral responsibility. Whether a producer is Black, white, Jewish, Asian, or Latino, the same question applies: does this work liberate or dehumanize? Does it tell the truth, or recycle fear for profit?

Calling out racism is not about punishing individuals; it is about exposing systems. The men and women who built Hollywood were shaped by their own struggles for survival. Understanding that context allows critique without hatred. The task now is not to erase the past, but to create new power structures that reward honesty and equity.

The next generation must build realities that include everyone.
