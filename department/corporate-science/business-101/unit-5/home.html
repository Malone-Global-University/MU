Unit 5: Business Ethics & Law

Learn the rules and responsibilities of business. Explore contracts, corporate responsibility, ethical decision frameworks, and the regulatory environment that shapes modern organizations.

Home Department Corporate Science Business-101 Unit-5

Unit Overview

Every organization operates inside a legal system—and every decision carries ethical consequences. Strong businesses build trust by doing what is lawful, what is fair, and what they can stand behind when nobody is watching.

In this unit, you’ll learn the basics of contracts and enforceable agreements, how regulation shapes business behavior, and how corporate responsibility extends beyond profit to stakeholders and society. You’ll also practice ethical decision-making using clear frameworks that help you navigate real-world gray areas.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain why ethics and law matter in business decisions, reputation, and long-term performance.
  • Identify the core elements of a contract and recognize common contract risks.
  • Apply ethical decision-making frameworks to practical business scenarios.
  • Describe corporate responsibility, stakeholder obligations, and basic governance principles.
  • Understand how regulations shape business behavior and why compliance systems exist.

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 5.1: Ethics in Business

    Why ethics matters, common dilemmas, and the difference between what’s legal and what’s right.

  • Lesson 5.2: Ethical Decision Frameworks

    Practical tools for tough decisions: stakeholders, consequences, duties, and long-term trust.

  • Lesson 5.3: Contracts & Agreements

    Offer, acceptance, consideration, enforceability, and the risks hidden in “standard terms.”

  • Lesson 5.4: Regulation & Compliance

    Why regulation exists, how compliance works, and what organizations do to reduce legal risk.

  • Lesson 5.5: Corporate Responsibility & Governance

    Stakeholders, accountability, corporate responsibility, and the basics of organizational governance.

  • Lesson 5.6: Common Legal & Ethical Risk Areas

    Employment issues, discrimination, privacy, consumer protection, fraud, conflicts of interest, and more.

Unit Assessment

After completing all lessons, students should:

  • Complete the Unit 5 Quiz
  • Submit a short written ethical analysis using a decision framework (stakeholders, options, tradeoffs, recommendation)
  • Review a simple contract and identify key terms, risks, and questions you would ask before signing

Self-Paced Learning Guidance

This unit rewards slow, careful thinking. When you encounter a case study, pause and write your reasoning down before reading any “recommended answer.” Ethics and law aren’t about memorizing rules—they’re about building judgment you can use under pressure.
