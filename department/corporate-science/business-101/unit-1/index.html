Unit 1: Introduction to Business

Start with the fundamentals: what businesses do, why they exist, how they’re organized, and how entrepreneurs create value. Compare nonprofit and for-profit models and learn the basic “language” of business.

Home Department Corporate Science Business-101 Unit-1

Unit Overview

Business is the organized effort to create value—by solving problems, serving customers, and coordinating people, capital, and resources. In this unit, you’ll build a clear foundation for how businesses operate and how different organization types shape incentives, responsibility, and strategy.

You’ll also explore entrepreneurship: how new ventures form, how ideas become offerings, and what it means to take calculated risk. Finally, you’ll compare for-profit and nonprofit models to understand how mission, revenue, and accountability differ across sectors.

Learning Objectives

  • Define business and explain how businesses create value for customers and society.
  • Compare common organization types (sole proprietorships, partnerships, corporations, and LLCs).
  • Explain the role of entrepreneurship in innovation, competition, and job creation.
  • Distinguish between nonprofit and for-profit models, including mission, funding, and governance.
  • Identify the core functions of a business (marketing, operations, finance, and management) at a high level.

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 1.1: What Is Business?

    What businesses do, why they exist, how value is created, and how success is measured.

  • Lesson 1.2: Types of Organizations

    Sole proprietorships, partnerships, corporations, and LLCs—structures, tradeoffs, and real-world use cases.

  • Lesson 1.3: Entrepreneurship & New Ventures

    How ventures start: opportunity recognition, risk, customer discovery, and early-stage execution.

  • Lesson 1.4: Nonprofit vs For-Profit Models

    Mission, revenue, accountability, and impact—how organizations differ across sectors.

  • Lesson 1.5: Business Functions & the Value Chain

    A practical map of how departments work together—marketing, operations, finance, and leadership.

Unit Assessment

After completing all lessons, students should:

  • Complete the Unit 1 Quiz
  • Submit a short written analysis comparing two organization types for a specific scenario
  • Create a one-page “business model sketch” for a simple venture idea (customer, value, revenue, costs)

Self-Paced Learning Guidance

This course is designed to be clear, practical, and self-directed. Move through lessons at your own pace. Each lesson includes explanations, examples, practice questions, and reflection prompts.

Tip: Keep a running “Business Glossary” as you go. Writing down key terms in your own words will make later units (management, marketing, and strategy) much easier.
