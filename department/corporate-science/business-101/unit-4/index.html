Unit 4: Operations & Strategy

Learn how organizations create value at scale. Explore production systems, process design, quality control, business models, and the foundations of sustainable competitive advantage.

Home Department Corporate Science Business-101 Unit-4

Unit Overview

Strategy answers, “Where will we compete and how will we win?” Operations answers, “How will we deliver—reliably, efficiently, and at the quality customers expect?” Together, operations and strategy determine whether an organization can execute its goals in the real world.

In this unit, you’ll learn the basics of production systems and process design, how quality is measured and improved, and how business models create (and capture) value. You’ll also explore competitive advantage—why some organizations outperform others over time—and how to think clearly about tradeoffs, constraints, and performance metrics.

Learning Objectives

  • Describe how operations systems transform inputs into outputs in goods and services.
  • Identify bottlenecks and constraints and explain how they impact throughput, cost, and customer experience.
  • Explain quality control concepts and continuous improvement methods.
  • Compare common business models and describe how value is created and captured.
  • Define competitive advantage and evaluate strategic tradeoffs using simple frameworks.

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 4.1: Operations Basics

    Inputs, processes, outputs—how operations creates value in products and services.

  • Lesson 4.2: Process Design & Bottlenecks

    Flow, capacity, constraints, and how bottlenecks shape performance and customer wait times.

  • Lesson 4.3: Quality Control & Continuous Improvement

    Standards, measurement, root-cause analysis, and improving systems over time.

  • Lesson 4.4: Supply Chains & Inventory

    How materials and information move through a business—plus inventory tradeoffs and risk.

  • Lesson 4.5: Business Models & Value Creation

    How organizations make money (or sustain a mission): revenue, costs, and value capture.

  • Lesson 4.6: Strategy & Competitive Advantage

    Where to compete, how to win, and what makes an advantage durable over time.

Unit Assessment

After completing all lessons, students should:

  • Complete the Unit 4 Quiz
  • Submit a short analysis identifying a bottleneck in a real process and proposing one improvement
  • Create a one-page business model summary (value proposition, revenue, costs, and key resources)

Self-Paced Learning Guidance

Pick one organization (a small business, a nonprofit, or a well-known company) as your “running case.” As you move through lessons, map its operations: where inputs come from, what steps create the product or service, how quality is ensured, and what metrics matter most. Strategy becomes easier when you can see the system.
