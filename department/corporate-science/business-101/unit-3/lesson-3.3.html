Lesson 3.3: Segmentation, Targeting, & Positioning

Choosing who you serve, what you stand for, and how you win in a crowded marketplace.

Home Department Corporate Science Business 101 Unit 3 Lesson 3.3

Lesson Overview

Most business failures aren’t caused by “bad ideas.” They’re caused by being too vague: trying to serve everyone, saying the same thing as competitors, and hoping customers magically understand why you’re different.

Segmentation, Targeting, and Positioning (STP) is a practical way to avoid that trap. It helps you: identify groups of customers, choose the ones you can serve best, and clearly communicate why your offer is the right choice.

In this lesson, you’ll learn a simple STP method you can apply to products, services, personal brands, and startups.

Learning Objectives

  • Define segmentation, targeting, and positioning (STP).
  • Identify multiple ways to segment a market (B2C and B2B).
  • Create an Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) and a simple customer persona.
  • Select a target segment using practical criteria (fit, pain, reachability, economics).
  • Write a clear positioning statement and choose points of differentiation.

STP in One Sentence

Segmentation is how you divide a broad market into meaningful groups. Targeting is how you choose which group(s) to focus on. Positioning is how you explain why your offer is the best choice for that target.

If you do STP well, your marketing becomes clearer, your product decisions get easier, and your sales conversations feel more natural.

Step 1: Segmentation (Divide the Market)

A segment is a group of customers with similar needs, behaviors, or constraints. The goal is to find differences that matter for buying decisions—so you’re not just creating categories for the sake of it.

Common Segmentation Methods (B2C)

  • Demographic: age, income, household size, occupation
  • Geographic: region, climate, urban vs rural
  • Behavioral: usage level, loyalty, buying triggers, preferred channels
  • Psychographic: values, lifestyle, motivations, identity

Common Segmentation Methods (B2B)

  • Firmographic: industry, company size, revenue, growth stage
  • Use case: what job they need done, workflows, integrations
  • Buying process: decision maker, procurement rules, budget cycles
  • Constraints: compliance needs, security requirements, training capacity

Practical tip: the best segments often show up as differences in pain, urgency, and willingness to pay.

Step 2: Targeting (Choose Your Focus)

Targeting is a strategic choice. When you choose a target, you’re saying: “We will be especially good for these people—and we will not try to be everything to everyone.”

Targeting Strategies

  • Concentrated (Niche): focus on one segment deeply (common for startups)
  • Differentiated: serve multiple segments with tailored messaging/offers
  • Broad (Mass): one offer for many segments (requires strong brand/distribution)

How to Evaluate a Target Segment

  • Problem intensity: is the pain real and frequent?
  • Urgency: do they need a solution now, or “someday”?
  • Ability to pay: do they have budget (or spending power)?
  • Reachability: can you find them and get in front of them?
  • Fit: are you uniquely capable (skills, credibility, advantage)?
  • Competition: are there strong incumbents—or a gap you can own?

A strong early target is often a group that is easy to identify, has a painful problem, and is already spending money on alternatives.

Ideal Customer Profile (ICP) and Persona

Two tools help you make targeting concrete:

Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

An ICP is a description of the type of customer that is the best fit for your offer. ICP is common in B2B, but it’s useful in B2C too.

  • Who they are (role/company type or lifestyle)
  • What problem they need solved
  • What makes them a good fit (budget, urgency, constraints)
  • Where they can be reached (channels, communities)

Persona

A persona is a human-centered snapshot that helps you write messaging and design experiences. It includes language, anxieties, goals, and buying triggers.

Simple Persona Template:

  • Goal: what are they trying to achieve?
  • Pain: what frustrates or blocks them?
  • Trigger: what causes them to look for a solution?
  • Objections: what makes them hesitate?
  • Success: what outcome would make them say “this worked”?

Step 3: Positioning (Explain Why You Win)

Positioning is how you claim a clear place in the customer’s mind. It answers: “Why should I choose you instead of the alternatives?”

Positioning Statement Template

Use this simple structure:

  • For [target customer]
  • Who [has this need/problem]
  • Our [product/service]
  • Is [category/frame of reference]
  • That [key benefit]
  • Unlike [primary alternative]
  • We [main differentiation/proof]

Common Differentiators (Pick 1–2 to Lead With)

  • Speed: faster delivery or faster results
  • Quality: more reliable, higher performance
  • Convenience: simpler workflow, easier onboarding
  • Customization: tailored to a niche
  • Price: cheaper or better value for the price
  • Trust: credibility, compliance, transparency, proof
  • Experience: better design, support, brand feel

Good positioning is specific and testable. If anyone can copy your sentence and it still sounds true, it’s too generic.

Putting STP into Practice: A Quick Example

Imagine a meal-prep business.

  • Segmentation: busy professionals, athletes, parents, seniors, people with dietary needs
  • Targeting: busy professionals who want healthy lunches delivered weekly
  • Positioning: “Chef-prepared lunches for busy professionals—delivered every Monday—so you eat healthy without thinking about it.”

Notice how targeting makes the offer and messaging sharper. The business can now design menus, packaging, and delivery schedules around a clear customer.

Practice: Check Your Understanding

  1. What’s the difference between segmentation and targeting?
  2. Name two ways to segment a market in B2C and two ways in B2B.
  3. Write a simple ICP for a product or service you know well.
  4. Use the positioning statement template to position a brand you like.
