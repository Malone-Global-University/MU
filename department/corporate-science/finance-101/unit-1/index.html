Unit 1: Financial Statements

Learn how to read the three core financial statements and use ratios to turn accounting data into decision-ready insights.

Home Department Corporate Science Finance-101 Unit-1

Unit Overview

Financial statements are the language of business. Whether you are evaluating a company, managing a budget, or analyzing an investment, the ability to interpret financial reports is a foundational skill.

In this unit, you will learn how the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement connect—and how analysts use financial ratios to assess profitability, risk, and operating efficiency.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain the purpose of the income statement, balance sheet, and cash flow statement.
  • Identify how the three statements connect (net income, retained earnings, and cash flow).
  • Distinguish cash flow from profit and explain why the difference matters.
  • Calculate and interpret core financial ratios across profitability, liquidity, leverage, and efficiency.
  • Use financial statements to form a basic view of business health and financial risk.

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 1.1: The Language of Financial Statements

    What financial statements are, why they matter, and how to approach them like an analyst.

  • Lesson 1.2: The Income Statement

    Revenue, expenses, profit margins, and how performance is measured over time.

  • Lesson 1.3: The Balance Sheet

    Assets, liabilities, equity, and what a company owns versus what it owes.

  • Lesson 1.4: The Cash Flow Statement

    Operating, investing, and financing cash flows—where cash really comes from and where it goes.

  • Lesson 1.5: How the Statements Connect

    Tracing net income to equity and reconciling profit to cash with real-world examples.

  • Lesson 1.6: Financial Ratios I — Profitability & Efficiency

    Margins, ROA, ROE, turnover ratios, and what they reveal about operations.

  • Lesson 1.7: Financial Ratios II — Liquidity & Leverage

    Current ratio, quick ratio, debt metrics, coverage ratios, and how to gauge financial risk.

Unit Assessment

After completing all lessons, students should:

  • Complete the Unit 1 Quiz
  • Analyze a simplified set of financial statements and summarize business performance in 300–500 words
  • Calculate a set of key ratios and interpret what they imply about profitability and risk

Self-Paced Learning Guidance

This course is designed to be clear, practical, and self-directed. Move through lessons at your own pace. Each lesson includes explanations, worked examples, practice questions, and reflection prompts to help you build real financial fluency.
