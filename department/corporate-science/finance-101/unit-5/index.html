Unit 5: Personal Finance

Build lifelong money skills—budgeting, credit, debt management, retirement planning, and long-term wealth strategies.

Home Department Corporate Science Finance-101 Unit-5

Unit Overview

Personal finance is about building stability and options. It combines day-to-day money habits with long-term planning so you can handle surprises, reduce stress, and pursue meaningful goals.

In this unit, you will learn how to create a realistic budget, manage credit responsibly, reduce high-cost debt, plan for retirement, understand essential insurance concepts, and build a long-term wealth strategy grounded in consistency.

Learning Objectives

  • Create a simple budget and track cash flow using a repeatable system.
  • Explain how credit works and what influences a credit score.
  • Compare debt payoff strategies and identify high-cost borrowing traps.
  • Describe retirement accounts and the role of compounding in long-term planning.
  • Identify key insurance types and explain how insurance reduces financial risk.

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 5.1: Budgeting & Cash Flow Basics

    Income, expenses, and building a budget that works in real life (not just on paper).

  • Lesson 5.2: Emergency Funds & Financial Stability

    Preparing for the unexpected and reducing financial stress with a safety buffer.

  • Lesson 5.3: Credit, Credit Scores & Responsible Borrowing

    How credit works, what affects scores, and how to use credit without getting trapped.

  • Lesson 5.4: Debt Management & Payoff Strategies

    Interest, minimum payments, payoff plans, and evaluating refinancing options.

  • Lesson 5.5: Retirement Planning

    Goals, timelines, compounding, and how retirement accounts support long-term growth.

  • Lesson 5.6: Insurance & Risk Management

    Protecting what you build: deductibles, premiums, coverage, and common insurance types.

  • Lesson 5.7: Long-Term Wealth Strategy

    Putting it all together: habits, automation, diversified investing, and staying consistent over time.

Unit Assessment

After completing all lessons, students should:

  • Complete the Unit 5 Quiz
  • Create a one-page personal financial plan (budget + goals + next steps)
  • Write a short reflection (250–500 words) describing a realistic wealth-building strategy over 5–10 years

Self-Paced Learning Guidance

Personal finance improves through small, consistent actions. As you study, apply one idea at a time: track spending for a week, build a simple budget, automate savings, or outline a debt payoff plan. Progress is the goal—perfection is not required.
