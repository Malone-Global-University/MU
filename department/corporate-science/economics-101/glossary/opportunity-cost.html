Definition

Opportunity cost is the value of the next best alternative that must be given up when a decision is made.

Why It Matters

Opportunity cost is the foundation of economic thinking. Because resources are scarce, every choice involves a trade-off. Differences in opportunity costs determine comparative advantage and guide specialization and trade.

Example

If a student spends three hours studying economics instead of working a job that pays $20 per hour, the opportunity cost of studying is $60 in forgone wages.
