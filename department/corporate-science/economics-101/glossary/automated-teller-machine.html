Definition

Automated Teller Machine (ATM) is an electronic banking device that enables customers to perform financial transactions such as withdrawing cash, depositing funds, transferring money between accounts, and checking balances without needing direct assistance from bank personnel.

Why It Matters

ATMs provide convenient, 24/7 access to banking services, reducing the need for branch visits and enabling efficient cash flow management. They play a crucial role in modern banking infrastructure and help banks expand their reach while minimizing operational costs.

A customer can use an ATM to withdraw $100 from their checking account at any time, even outside of normal bank hours. The machine connects to the bankâ€™s network to verify the account balance and dispense cash instantly.
