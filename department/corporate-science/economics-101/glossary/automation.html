Definition

Automation is the implementation of technology and machines to perform tasks with minimal human intervention. It is widely used in business, manufacturing, and economic processes to enhance productivity, reduce errors, and control costs.

Why It Matters

Automation improves operational efficiency and consistency, lowers labor costs, and allows organizations to scale processes rapidly. In economics, it can influence labor markets, productivity, and the competitiveness of firms or industries.

Example

A company may use robotic systems to assemble products on a factory line. These machines perform repetitive tasks automatically, reducing the need for manual labor while maintaining high quality and speed.
