Definition

A Flat Tax is a taxation system where all taxpayers pay the same constant tax rate regardless of income level. It simplifies the tax code and treats every dollar of income equally.

Why It Matters

Flat taxes are valued for their simplicity and predictability. They reduce administrative costs and make tax compliance easier. However, they are debated in terms of fairness, because they do not adjust for the taxpayerâ€™s ability to pay, unlike progressive taxes.

Example

A flat tax system imposes a 15% income tax on all individuals. Someone earning $30,000 pays $4,500, and someone earning $300,000 pays $45,000, both at the same rate.
