Definition

Automatic Transfer is a pre-set arrangement that moves funds between accounts without manual intervention. It is commonly used for transferring money from checking to savings, making recurring bill payments, or maintaining minimum account balances.

Why It Matters

Automatic transfers simplify financial management, ensure timely payments, encourage regular savings, and reduce the risk of overdrafts. They are a key tool for both personal finance and organizational cash flow management.

Example

A person may set up an automatic transfer of $200 every month from their checking account to a savings account. Each month, the transfer occurs automatically, helping build savings without requiring manual effort.
