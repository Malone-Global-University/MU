Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary Commission

Definition

A fee paid to a broker, agent, or salesperson for executing a transaction or providing a service, typically calculated as a percentage of the transaction value.

Why It Matters

Commissions influence incentives in financial markets and sales environments, affecting behavior, pricing structures, and overall transaction costs.

Example

A stockbroker earns a commission each time a client buys or sells shares through their brokerage account.
