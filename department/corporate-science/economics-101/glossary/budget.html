Definition

Budget is a financial plan that estimates income and sets limits or targets for spending, saving, and investment over a specific period such as a month, year, or fiscal cycle.

Why It Matters

Budgets help households, businesses, and governments manage limited resources efficiently. By planning expenditures relative to expected income, a budget reduces financial risk, supports long-term goals, and helps prevent unsustainable debt or unexpected shortfalls.

Example

A household expecting $4,000 in monthly income may allocate $1,500 for housing, $600 for food, $400 for transportation, $500 for savings, and the remainder for utilities and discretionary spending. This allocation forms the householdâ€™s monthly budget.
