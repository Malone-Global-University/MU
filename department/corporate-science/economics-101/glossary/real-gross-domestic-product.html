Real Gross Domestic Product

Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary Real Gross Domestic Product

Definition

Real Gross Domestic Product is the total value of final goods and services produced within a country over a period of time, adjusted for inflation so it reflects changes in actual output.

Why It Matters

It is the key measure used to track true economic growth and compare living standards over time.

Example

Real GDP increases when the economy produces more goods and services, even if prices stay the same.
