Definition

Adjustable-Rate Mortgage (ARM) is a home loan whose interest rate adjusts periodically according to a financial index or market interest rates, causing the borrowerâ€™s monthly payment to increase or decrease over time.

Why It Matters

ARMs often start with lower introductory rates than fixed-rate mortgages, reducing initial borrowing costs. However, if market interest rates rise, future payments may increase significantly, making these loans important for understanding household financial risk and housing market dynamics.

Example

A borrower takes out a 30-year mortgage with a 3% introductory rate fixed for five years. After five years, the rate adjusts annually based on market conditions. If rates rise to 5%, the borrowerâ€™s monthly payment increases.
