Definition

Absolute advantage is the ability of a producer to create more of a good or service than another producer using the same quantity of resources.

Why It Matters

Absolute advantage helps explain productivity differences between firms or countries. However, international trade is usually driven by comparative advantage, not absolute advantage.

Example

If Country A can produce 10 computers per worker while Country B produces 5 computers per worker, Country A has an absolute advantage in computer production.
