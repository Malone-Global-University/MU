Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary Checkable Deposits

Definition

Checkable Deposits are bank account balances that depositors can access on demand using checks, debit cards, or electronic transfers. They are a key component of the money supply, providing liquidity for everyday transactions.

Why It Matters

Checkable deposits allow individuals and businesses to make payments conveniently without carrying cash. They support commerce, facilitate bill payments, and serve as a foundation for banking operations, influencing overall economic activity and the money supply.

Example

When John writes a check to pay his rent, he is using funds from his checkable deposit account. The bank transfers the money from John's account to the landlord's account immediately or within a short clearing period, making these deposits highly liquid.
