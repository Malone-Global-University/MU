Definition

Capital and Financial Account is the portion of a country's balance of payments that records cross-border financial transactions, including capital transfers, foreign direct investment, portfolio investment, and loans. It complements the current account by tracking how a nation finances its trade and investment activities internationally.

Why It Matters

The capital and financial account provides insight into a country's economic stability, investment attractiveness, and ability to finance deficits. Large inflows can stimulate growth, while sudden outflows may trigger currency depreciation or financial crises. Policymakers monitor these flows to guide monetary and fiscal strategies.

Example

If a U.S. company invests in a factory in Germany (foreign direct investment) and foreign investors buy U.S. stocks (portfolio investment), these transactions are recorded in the capital and financial account. Similarly, loans and repayments between countries also appear here, affecting the overall balance of payments.
