Definition

International trade is the exchange of goods and services across national borders. It allows countries to specialize in producing goods where they have a comparative advantage and trade for others.

Why It Matters

International trade increases total global output, expands consumer choice, and promotes economic growth. It is largely driven by comparative advantage and supported by specialization.

Example

A country with abundant oil resources may export petroleum products while importing manufactured electronics from a country that specializes in advanced technology production.

  • Specialization
  • Absolute Advantage
  • Opportunity Cost
