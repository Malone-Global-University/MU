Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary Capital Investment

Definition

Capital Investment refers to the funds spent by a business or government to acquire, improve, or maintain long-term assets such as machinery, buildings, or technology. These investments aim to increase productive capacity and generate future economic returns.

Why It Matters

Capital investment drives economic growth, productivity, and competitiveness. By investing in new equipment, technology, or infrastructure, organizations expand capacity, improve efficiency, and support long-term profitability. Policymakers also monitor capital investment to gauge economic health.

Example

A manufacturing company purchasing new machinery to increase production efficiency or a government building a highway to improve trade and logistics are both examples of capital investment. These expenditures enhance the economy's capacity to produce goods and services over time.
