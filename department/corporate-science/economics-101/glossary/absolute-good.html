Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary Absolute Good

Definition

An Absolute Good is a good or resource considered inherently valuable or beneficial. Its utility or contribution to welfare is independent of individual preferences, circumstances, or comparative measures.

Why It Matters

Recognizing absolute goods helps economists and policymakers distinguish between resources that universally enhance well-being and those whose value is context-dependent. This concept underpins debates on essential services, universal rights, and baseline standards of living.

Example

Clean air and potable water are often classified as absolute goods because they are universally necessary for health and survival, regardless of income, location, or personal preference.
