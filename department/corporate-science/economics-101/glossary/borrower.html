Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary Borrower

Definition

Borrower refers to any individual, business, or government entity that receives money from a lender and agrees to repay the principal amount along with interest, according to the terms of a loan or credit agreement.

Why It Matters

Borrowers play a critical role in finance and economics. Their demand for credit fuels consumption, investment, and economic growth. Responsible borrowing can build creditworthiness, while excessive borrowing can increase financial risk and default probability.

Example

A student who takes out a student loan, a company issuing bonds to fund expansion, or a government borrowing to finance infrastructure projects are all examples of borrowers. Each agrees to repay the lender under specified terms.
