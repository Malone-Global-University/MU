Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary Board of Governors

Definition

Board of Governors is a governing body responsible for overseeing the operations, policy-making, and strategic direction of a central bank, corporation, or institution. They provide leadership, guide financial and operational decisions, and ensure regulatory compliance and accountability.

Why It Matters

The Board of Governors plays a critical role in maintaining financial stability, enforcing governance standards, and shaping policy decisions that impact the economy, markets, and institutional operations. Their decisions influence interest rates, investment strategies, and corporate oversight.

Example

The Federal Reserve Board of Governors in the United States sets monetary policy, supervises banks, and provides guidance on economic strategy. Similarly, a corporate board of governors oversees the CEO, approves major initiatives, and ensures fiduciary responsibility to shareholders.
