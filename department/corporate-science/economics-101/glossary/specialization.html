Definition

Specialization is the concentration of productive effort on a limited range of goods or services in order to increase efficiency and total output.

Why It Matters

Specialization increases productivity by allowing individuals, firms, and countries to focus on tasks where they are most efficient. It works together with comparative advantage to form the foundation of voluntary trade and global economic growth.

Example

A worker who focuses solely on coding software becomes more efficient over time than someone who divides time between coding, marketing, and accounting. Likewise, countries often specialize in industries where they have lower opportunity costs.
