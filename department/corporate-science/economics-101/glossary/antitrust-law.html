Definition

Antitrust Law refers to regulations that prevent monopolies and promote fair competition in the marketplace. These laws prohibit unfair business practices such as price fixing, collusion, and market allocation agreements.

Why It Matters

Antitrust laws protect consumers and ensure efficient markets. By limiting monopolistic behavior, they encourage innovation, lower prices, and increase choices for buyers. Governments enforce these laws to maintain competitive economic environments.

Example

If two major tech companies agree secretly to fix prices for their software products, this violates antitrust law. Regulatory authorities can impose fines, break up monopolies, or enforce corrective measures to restore fair competition.
