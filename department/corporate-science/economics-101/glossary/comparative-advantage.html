Home Department Corporatate Science Economics Glossary Comparative Advantage

Definition

Comparative advantage is the ability of a producer to create a good or service at a lower opportunity cost than another producer.

Why It Matters

Comparative advantage explains why voluntary trade benefits all parties involved, even when one country has an absolute advantage in producing every good. Trade patterns are determined by opportunity cost differences, not by productivity alone.

Example

Suppose Country A can produce either 10 cars or 5 computers per worker, while Country B can produce either 6 cars or 3 computers per worker. Even if Country A is more productive in both goods, it benefits by specializing in the good with the lower opportunity cost and trading with Country B.
