Definition

Clearing Banks are financial institutions that facilitate the settlement of payments and interbank transactions, including the processing of checks, electronic transfers, and other forms of payment between banks and their customers.

Why It Matters

Clearing banks ensure that financial transactions between institutions are accurate, secure, and timely. They reduce the risk of errors or fraud in payment systems and maintain the stability and efficiency of the banking network.

Example

When a customer deposits a check from another bank, the clearing bank processes the transaction, ensuring that funds are transferred from the issuing bank to the recipient's account. Without clearing banks, interbank payments would be slower and less reliable.
