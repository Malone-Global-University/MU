Definition

Characteristics of Money are the essential properties that enable money to function effectively as a medium of exchange, a unit of account, and a store of value.

Key Characteristics

  • Durability: Money must withstand physical wear and tear.
  • Portability: Money should be easy to carry and transfer.
  • Divisibility: Money can be divided into smaller units for transactions of varying sizes.
  • Uniformity: Units of money are standardized and easily recognizable.
  • Limited Supply: Money must be scarce enough to maintain value.
  • Acceptability: Money must be widely accepted as a method of payment.

Why It Matters

Understanding the characteristics of money helps economists and policymakers ensure that currency maintains its value, is trusted by the public, and facilitates trade efficiently.

Example

A durable, portable, and divisible currency like modern banknotes and coins allows people to pay for groceries, pay wages, or make large purchases easily. Uniformity ensures each note has the same value, and acceptability ensures it is recognized by everyone in the economy.
