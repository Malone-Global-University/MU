Definition

Collections is the process by which creditors recover overdue debts from borrowers, often using collection agencies, legal notices, or other formal measures to secure repayment.

Why It Matters

Collections are essential for maintaining credit market stability. Efficient debt recovery reduces lender risk, encourages future lending, and ensures borrowers are held accountable. Poor collection practices or high delinquency rates can increase interest costs and restrict credit availability.

Example

If a credit card holder falls behind on payments, the bank may attempt to collect the debt through phone reminders, letters, or by hiring a collections agency. In some cases, legal action may be taken to recover the owed amount.
