Definition

Welfare refers to the overall well-being and standard of living of individuals or society, typically measured through income levels, consumption, health, access to education, and availability of goods and services.

Why It Matters

Welfare is central to economic policy because governments and institutions often evaluate success not only by output or growth, but by improvements in living standards. Policies affecting taxation, public spending, and market regulation are frequently assessed based on their impact on social welfare.

Example

If economic growth raises average incomes while also improving healthcare access and education, overall welfare increases. However, if income rises but inequality worsens and essential services decline, welfare may not improve.
