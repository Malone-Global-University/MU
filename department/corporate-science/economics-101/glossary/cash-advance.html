Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary Cash Advance

Definition

Cash Advance is a short-term loan or withdrawal that provides immediate access to funds, usually from a credit card or financial institution. Cash advances often carry higher fees or interest rates than regular transactions.

Why It Matters

Cash advances allow individuals and businesses to meet urgent financial needs without waiting for traditional payment methods or bank processing. However, they can be costly if used frequently due to higher interest rates and additional fees.

Example

A credit card holder may use an ATM to take a $500 cash advance to cover an unexpected expense. The bank charges a cash advance fee and a higher interest rate, which begins accruing immediately until the balance is repaid.
