Definition

Potential Output is the maximum quantity of goods and services an economy can sustainably produce when all resources are fully employed, without causing inflationary pressures.

Why It Matters

Potential output provides a benchmark for evaluating economic performance. The gap between actual output and potential output indicates underutilized resources or overheating, guiding policymakers in fiscal and monetary decisions.

Example

If an economyâ€™s potential output is 1,000 units but actual production is only 900 units, the economy is producing below capacity. This shortfall may suggest a recessionary period or unused labor and capital.
