Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary Bank Suspensions

Definition

Bank Suspensions occur when a bank temporarily halts its normal operations, often due to financial distress, liquidity shortages, or government/regulatory intervention. During a suspension, the bank may limit withdrawals, delay loans, or cease other standard services.

Why It Matters

Bank suspensions can have far-reaching consequences for the financial system and broader economy. They can erode public trust, reduce liquidity, and trigger panic among depositors. Regulatory bodies may enforce suspensions to prevent systemic collapse and protect the stability of the banking sector.

Example

During a financial crisis, a bank may announce a temporary suspension of withdrawals for 48 hours to stabilize its cash reserves.
