Definition

Consumer Preferences describe how individuals rank and choose among goods and services based on perceived utility, tastes, and personal priorities. Preferences guide decision-making in consumption and the allocation of resources.

Why It Matters

Understanding consumer preferences helps economists predict demand, evaluate market behavior, and design policies that maximize overall welfare. They form the foundation for concepts like utility, marginal benefit, and choice under scarcity.

Example

A consumer may prefer water over soda when thirsty (absolute-good utility) or may choose a luxury watch over other items for social status (relative-good utility). Preferences vary across individuals and situations.
