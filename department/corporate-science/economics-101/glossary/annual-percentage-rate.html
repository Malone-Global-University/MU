Definition

Annual Percentage Rate (APR) is the yearly interest rate charged on borrowed money, expressed as a percentage. It includes both the nominal interest and any mandatory fees or costs associated with the loan.

Why It Matters

APR provides borrowers a standardized measure to compare the cost of different loans or credit products. Unlike nominal interest alone, APR reflects the total cost of borrowing, helping consumers make informed financial decisions.

Example

A credit card advertises a 15% interest rate with a $50 annual fee. The APR incorporates both the 15% interest and the $50 fee, yielding a higher effective annual cost than the nominal rate alone, which borrowers should consider when evaluating credit options.
