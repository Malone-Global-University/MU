Definition

Allocation is the process of distributing resources, goods, or services among competing uses within an economy, organization, or system. It aims to achieve the most efficient outcomes given limited resources.

Why It Matters

Resource allocation is central to economics because scarcity requires choices. Efficient allocation maximizes productivity, reduces waste, and ensures that resources are used where they provide the greatest value. Misallocation can lead to shortages, surpluses, or economic inefficiency.

Example

A government deciding how to distribute a limited budget among healthcare, education, and infrastructure is performing allocation. By analyzing needs and potential impact, it aims to maximize social and economic benefits while staying within resource constraints.
