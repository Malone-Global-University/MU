Definition

Capital Ratio is a financial metric used to determine the proportion of a bankâ€™s or firm's capital relative to its risk-weighted assets. It measures financial strength and the ability to absorb potential losses while meeting regulatory requirements.

Why It Matters

Maintaining an adequate capital ratio is essential for financial stability. Regulators set minimum capital ratio requirements to protect depositors, reduce systemic risk, and ensure that institutions can withstand economic shocks. A higher capital ratio generally indicates a more resilient institution.
