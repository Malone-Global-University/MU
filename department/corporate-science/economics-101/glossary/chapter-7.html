Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary Chapter 11 Bankruptcy Protection

Definition

Chapter 11 Bankruptcy Protection is a legal procedure in the United States that allows businesses to reorganize their debts while continuing normal operations. This process provides companies the opportunity to restructure finances, renegotiate contracts, and attempt to return to profitability under court supervision.

Why It Matters

Chapter 11 is important because it helps prevent the immediate liquidation of viable businesses, protecting jobs, creditors, and the economy. By allowing restructuring, firms can emerge stronger, maintain critical operations, and provide creditors with a greater chance of repayment compared to Chapter 7 liquidation.

Example

A large retailer facing insolvency files for Chapter 11. The company continues operating its stores while renegotiating leases, reducing debt, and reorganizing management. Over time, it can return to profitability and eventually exit bankruptcy, preserving both the business and jobs.
