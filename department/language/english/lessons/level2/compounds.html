Compound Words

Compound words are formed when two or more words are combined to create a new word with a unique meaning. Understanding compounds enhances vocabulary and writing skills.

Definition / Explanation

A compound word consists of two or more words joined together to act as a single unit. Compounds can be written as closed words, hyphenated words, or open words (written as separate words but understood as one concept).

Types of Compound Words

  • Closed compounds: Words joined together without spaces. Example: toothpaste, basketball, notebook
  • Hyphenated compounds: Words joined with a hyphen. Example: mother-in-law, well-known, check-in
  • Open compounds: Words written separately but used together as one idea. Example: ice cream, post office, full moon

Rules / Guidelines

  • Use closed compounds when the words form a single idea that is commonly recognized: toothpaste, keyboard.
  • Use hyphens to avoid ambiguity or when combining words with prefixes or suffixes: long-term, self-esteem.
  • Use open compounds for phrases that function as a single noun but are not yet commonly recognized as a single word: high school, living room.
  • Always check a dictionary for proper spelling, especially for words in transition from open to closed forms.

Examples

Closed: basketball, notebook, snowman
Hyphenated: mother-in-law, well-known, check-in
Open: ice cream, post office, full moon
Multiple examples in sentences:
- I bought a notebook for school.
- My mother-in-law is visiting next week.
- We went to the post office to mail a package.

Practice Exercises

  1. Identify the type of compound: "sunflower"
  2. Identify the type of compound: "well-being"
  3. Identify the type of compound: "living room"
  4. Create a hyphenated compound using "self" and "confident"
  5. Rewrite as a closed compound: "book case"

Answers / Explanation

  1. Closed compound
  2. Hyphenated compound
  3. Open compound
  4. self-confident
  5. bookcase
