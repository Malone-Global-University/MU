Parentheses and Brackets

Parentheses and brackets are punctuation marks used to add extra information, clarify meaning, or indicate editorial notes. Correct usage improves readability and prevents confusion.

Definition / Explanation

  • Parentheses ( ) – Enclose additional information, explanations, or afterthoughts in a sentence.
  • Brackets [ ] – Enclose editorial notes, clarifications, or modifications within quoted material, or mathematical content.

Rules / Guidelines

Parentheses

  • Use parentheses to provide extra or nonessential information: She moved to Paris (France) last year.
  • Do not overuse; parentheses interrupt the flow of the sentence.
  • Periods go outside parentheses unless the entire sentence is within them.

Brackets

  • Use brackets to add clarification or correction within a quote: "He [the manager] approved the project."
  • Use brackets in academic writing to indicate editorial additions or notes.
  • Use brackets in mathematical expressions: [2 × (3 + 4)] ÷ 5

Examples

Parentheses: The conference (originally scheduled for June) has been postponed.
Entire sentence in parentheses: (This is an important reminder.)
Brackets for clarification: "She said she was 'excited [about the trip] to start her new job.'"
Mathematical brackets: Calculate [3 × (2 + 5)] ÷ 4.

Practice Exercises

  1. Add parentheses: She moved to London last year the capital of England.
  2. Use brackets to clarify: "He said she would arrive on Friday."
  3. Use parentheses correctly: The book was published in 2020 it was very popular.
  4. Identify the correct punctuation: Calculate 5 × (2 + 3).
  5. Correct the sentence: "The witness stated I saw him [the suspect] leave the building."

Answers / Explanation

  1. She moved to London (the capital of England) last year.
  2. "He said [that] she would arrive on Friday."
  3. The book was published in 2020 (it was very popular).
  4. Correct: Calculate 5 × (2 + 3).
  5. "The witness stated, 'I saw him [the suspect] leave the building.'"
