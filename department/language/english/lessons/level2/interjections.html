Interjections

Interjections are words or phrases that express strong emotion or sudden feeling. They are often standalone and punctuated with an exclamation point.

Definition / Explanation

An interjection is a word or phrase used to express emotions, reactions, or exclamations. Interjections are not grammatically connected to other parts of the sentence and often stand alone.

Common Types of Interjections

  • Emotion / Surprise: express strong feelings. Example: Wow! Oh no! Hooray!
  • Pain / Discomfort: indicate sudden pain or discomfort. Example: Ouch! Yikes! Ah!
  • Attention / Greeting: call attention or greet someone. Example: Hey! Hello! Yo!
  • Agreement / Disagreement: express approval or disapproval. Example: Yes! Nope! Uh-oh!

Rules / Guidelines

  • Interjections are often followed by an exclamation point to show strong emotion. Example: Wow! That’s amazing.
  • They can be used in the middle of a sentence, set off by commas. Example: Well, I didn’t expect that.
  • Use interjections sparingly in formal writing; they are more common in speech or creative writing.
  • Interjections do not have grammatical relationships with other words in a sentence.

Examples

Emotion / Surprise: Wow! That fireworks display is incredible.
Pain / Discomfort: Ouch! I stubbed my toe.
Attention / Greeting: Hey! Can you help me?
Agreement / Disagreement: Yes! I will join you. / Nope! That’s not correct.

Practice Exercises

  1. Identify the interjection: "Ouch! That hurt."
  2. Write a sentence using an interjection to express surprise.
  3. Insert an appropriate interjection for attention: "(____), come here quickly!"
  4. Decide if this interjection shows agreement or disagreement: "Uh-oh!"
  5. Rewrite this sentence using an interjection at the beginning: "I can’t believe I won the prize."

Answers / Explanation

  1. Ouch!
  2. Answers will vary. Example: "Wow! That was amazing!"
  3. Answer: "Hey, come here quickly!"
  4. Disagreement / warning (Uh-oh!)
  5. Example: "Wow! I can’t believe I won the prize."
