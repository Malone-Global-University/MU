Apostrophes

Apostrophes are punctuation marks used to show possession, indicate omitted letters, or form some plural nouns. Proper usage clarifies meaning in writing.

Definition / Explanation

An apostrophe (’) is a punctuation mark that primarily serves three functions:

  • To show possession
  • To indicate omitted letters in contractions
  • To form plural of letters, numbers, or symbols (less common)

Rules / Guidelines

  • Possession: Add ’s to show ownership: Jessica’s book (the book belongs to Jessica).
  • For plural nouns ending in s, add only an apostrophe: The dogs’ toys (toys of the dogs).
  • Contractions: Use an apostrophe to replace missing letters: don’t (do not), it’s (it is).
  • Plural of letters, numbers, or symbols: Use apostrophes to avoid confusion: Mind your p’s and q’s, There are two 7’s in the code.
  • Do not use apostrophes to form regular plurals: Incorrect: apple’s for apples

Examples

Possession: The teacher’s desk, the children’s playground
Contractions: I’m (I am), they’ve (they have), you’ll (you will)
Plural of letters/numbers: She got all A’s on her report card.
Incorrect plural: Not: apple’s, book’s

Practice Exercises

  1. Show possession: The hat belonging to Michael
  2. Form contraction: do not → ?
  3. Plural letters: Mind your ___
  4. Show possession for plural noun ending in s: The toys of the kids
  5. Correct the sentence: I cant find its cover

Answers / Explanation

  1. Michael’s hat
  2. don’t
  3. p’s and q’s
  4. the kids’ toys
  5. I can’t find its cover
