Verb Voice

Verb voice indicates whether the subject of the sentence performs the action or receives it. Understanding voice helps clarify sentence meaning and emphasis.

Definition / Explanation

Voice is a grammatical feature that shows the relationship between the action of the verb and the subject of the sentence. The two main voices are active and passive.

Types of Voice

  • Active Voice: The subject performs the action. Example: The chef cooked the meal.
  • Passive Voice: The subject receives the action. Example: The meal was cooked by the chef.

Rules / Guidelines

  • Use active voice for clear, direct, and concise sentences.
  • Use passive voice to emphasize the action or the receiver rather than the doer.
  • Form the passive voice with the correct form of to be + past participle. Example: The letter was written by John.
  • Check subject-verb agreement when converting between active and passive voice.

Examples

Active: The teacher explained the lesson.
Passive: The lesson was explained by the teacher.
Active: The dog chased the ball.
Passive: The ball was chased by the dog.
Active: The students completed the assignment.
Passive: The assignment was completed by the students.

Practice Exercises

  1. Convert to passive: "The cat caught the mouse."
  2. Convert to active: "The book was read by the student."
  3. Identify the voice: "The manager approved the project."
  4. Identify the voice: "The project was approved by the manager."
  5. Rewrite in passive voice to emphasize the receiver: "The chef baked a cake."

Answers / Explanation

  1. The mouse was caught by the cat.
  2. The student read the book.
  3. Active Voice
  4. Passive Voice
  5. A cake was baked by the chef.
