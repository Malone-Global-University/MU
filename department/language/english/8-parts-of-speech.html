Lesson: The Different Parts of Speech

Intro

English words can be categorized into eight main parts of speech, each with a specific role in a sentence. Understanding these roles helps you read, write, and speak clearly and effectively.

1. Nouns

Definition: A noun is a word that names a person, place, thing, or idea.

Types of nouns:

  • Common nouns: general names (e.g., city, dog, book)
  • Proper nouns: specific names, capitalized (e.g., New York, Fido, Shakespeare)
  • Abstract nouns: ideas or qualities (e.g., love, freedom, happiness)
  • Concrete nouns: things you can touch or see (e.g., chair, apple)

Examples:

  • The cat slept on the sofa.
  • Freedom is important to everyone.

2. Pronouns

Definition: Pronouns replace nouns to avoid repetition.

Types of pronouns:

  • Personal pronouns: I, you, he, she, it, we, they
  • Possessive pronouns: mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs
  • Demonstrative pronouns: this, that, these, those
  • Relative pronouns: who, whom, whose, which, that

3. Verbs

Definition: Verbs show action or state of being.

  • Types of verbs:
  • Action verbs: run, eat, write
  • Linking verbs: be, seem, appear (connect subject to a state or description)
  • Auxiliary (helping) verbs: have, do, will, can

Examples:

  • He runs every morning.
  • She is a teacher.

4. Adjectives

Definition: Adjectives describe nouns or pronouns, giving more detail.

Examples:
  • The red apple is sweet.
  • She wore a beautiful dress.

Tip: Adjectives often answer: What kind? Which one? How many?

5. Adverbs

Definition: Adverbs describe verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, often showing how, when, where, or to what extent.

Examples:

  • He runs quickly.
  • She is very talented.
  • They arrived yesterday.

Tip: Many adverbs end in -ly, but not all (e.g., fast, well, very).

6. Prepositions

Definition: Prepositions show the relationship between a noun (or pronoun) and other words in a sentence, often indicating location, direction, or time.

Examples:

  • The book is on the table.
  • She walked to the park.
  • He will arrive after lunch.

Common prepositions: in, on, at, by, with, under, over, between

7. Conjunctions

Definition: Conjunctions connect words, phrases, or clauses.
