A legal studies hub for constitutional structure, legal philosophy, rights, courts, and practical legal reasoning.

Overview

The Law section introduces students to legal systems, constitutional authority, rights, legal hierarchy, courtroom practice, and the reasoning habits needed to read statutes, cases, and founding documents carefully.

Core Law Topics

Constitutional Law

The Constitution, judicial review, civil rights, federal power, and limits on government.

Natural Law

The idea that law is connected to reason, justice, moral order, and human nature.

Supremacy Clause Hierarchy

How federal law, state law, constitutions, treaties, and court rulings relate to one another.

John Locke and Legal Foundations

Natural rights, consent, property, government legitimacy, and political authority.

Legal Reasoning

A starting point for learning how legal claims, rules, evidence, and arguments are organized.
