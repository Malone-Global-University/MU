The Scandal Unfolds

Investigations under Presidents Rutherford B. Hayes and James A. Garfield revealed that senior postal officials and contractors colluded to skim profits from inflated mail contracts. Prominent figures like Senator Stephen W. Dorsey and Thomas J. Brady, Second Assistant Postmaster General, were accused of accepting bribes.

Aftermath

Multiple trials followed between 1882 and 1883 under President Chester A. Arthur. Although several defendants were acquitted due to weak evidence and political influence, the scandal damaged public confidence and highlighted deep corruption in federal contracting systems.

Impact

The Star Route Scandal spurred calls for civil service reform and greater transparency in government procurement. It helped build momentum for the Pendleton Civil Service Reform Act of 1883, which established merit-based hiring and reduced patronage in federal appointments.
