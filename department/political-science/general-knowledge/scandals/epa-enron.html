Background

In 2001, Enron was one of the largest energy companies in the United States. The company engaged in market manipulation, including trading practices that circumvented environmental regulations and led to artificially inflated energy prices. Oversight agencies, including the EPA, were criticized for failing to detect and prevent these abuses.

Enronâ€™s manipulation involved fraudulent reporting, exploiting loopholes in environmental regulations, and leveraging influence over energy markets. Impact & Legacy

The controversy prompted calls for stronger regulatory oversight, increased transparency, and reforms in how federal agencies monitor corporate compliance. It became a case study in agency failure, demonstrating the importance of proactive enforcement and interagency coordination to prevent corporate misconduct.
