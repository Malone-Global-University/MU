Background

The Veterans Bureau, created to care for World War I veterans, was led by Charles Forbes during President Hardingâ€™s administration. Forbes exploited the agency by embezzling funds and selling hospital supplies for personal profit, exposing deep vulnerabilities in federal oversight and management of veteran services.

Investigation and Consequences

Congressional and Justice Department investigations revealed the scale of corruption. Forbes was eventually convicted of conspiracy to defraud the government and sentenced to prison. The scandal highlighted the need for stronger oversight and reforms in federal agencies responsible for public welfare.

Impact on Public Trust

The scandal undermined public confidence in the Veterans Bureau and the Harding administration. It became an early example of federal corruption during the 1920s and contributed to a broader pattern of scrutiny toward government agencies and their leadership.
