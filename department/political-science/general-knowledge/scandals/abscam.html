Background

The Abscam operation was initiated by the FBI to investigate corruption and bribery within local and federal government. Agents posed as representatives of a fictitious Arabian company offering bribes to public officials in exchange for political favors.

Investigation and Results

Between 1978 and 1980, the operation led to the conviction of one U.S. Senator and six members of the House of Representatives for bribery and conspiracy. The sting highlighted the vulnerabilities in governmental oversight and ethics compliance.

Impact & Legacy

Abscam set a precedent for the use of undercover operations in investigating public corruption. It also fueled debates about entrapment and the ethical limits of law enforcement methods.

Discussion Questions

  • Was the Abscam operation an ethical approach to uncover political corruption?
  • How did the scandal impact public trust in Congress?
  • Should undercover operations have limits when targeting elected officials?
