Background

During the mid-1980s, the Reagan administration secretly sold arms to Iran, which was under an arms embargo, in the hope of securing the release of American hostages. The operation was exposed in November 1986, leading to national outrage. Impact & Legacy

The Iran-Contra Affair raised serious questions about constitutional checks and balances, executive accountability, and congressional authority. It prompted legislative reforms strengthening congressional oversight of covert operations and contributed to public skepticism of government transparency.
