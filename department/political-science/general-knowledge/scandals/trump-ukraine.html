Background

In 2019, allegations arose that President Donald Trump solicited the government of Ukraine to investigate former Vice President Joe Biden and his son Hunter Biden. This was tied to withholding nearly $400 million in military aid as leverage, raising concerns of abuse of presidential power for political gain.

Events

The scandal was revealed through a whistleblower complaint and led to a House of Representatives investigation. In December 2019, the House approved two articles of impeachment against President Trump: abuse of power and obstruction of Congress. The Senate trial in early 2020 resulted in acquittal, allowing him to complete his term.

The Trumpâ€“Ukraine scandal reinforced national debates on executive power, foreign policy ethics, and the impeachment process. It highlighted the role of whistleblowers, congressional oversight, and media scrutiny in maintaining democratic accountability.
