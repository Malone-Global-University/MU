Background

In 2013, former NSA contractor Edward Snowden leaked classified documents revealing large-scale surveillance programs that monitored phone records, internet activity, and communications of both American citizens and foreign nationals. These disclosures exposed the breadth of NSA operations and prompted worldwide concern over privacy rights.

Controversy & Public Debate

The leaks ignited debates over government overreach, constitutional rights, and the balance between national security and individual privacy. Critics argued that the surveillance violated civil liberties, while proponents emphasized its role in counterterrorism and intelligence gathering. The revelations led to calls for reform, oversight, and legal challenges.

Impact & Legacy

The NSA Mass Surveillance scandal led to policy reforms, including the USA Freedom Act, which limited bulk data collection. It also influenced international relations, public trust in government, and the technology sectorâ€™s approach to encryption and data protection. Snowden's actions remain a polarizing example of whistleblowing and its consequences.
