Imperial Ambitions

McKinley supported the annexation of the Philippines, Guam, and Puerto Rico after the Spanish-American War. While these moves expanded American influence, they sparked debate over imperialism and the ethics of overseas colonial rule.

Economic Elitism

His protectionist policies favored industrialists over farmers and workers in some regions, creating tension between urban and rural populations.

Political Caution and Indecision

McKinley often relied on advisors and Congress to make decisions, which critics argue limited his decisiveness in domestic reforms and delayed responses to emerging social issues.

Verdict

William McKinley was an experienced and moderate president whose good lay in economic policy, national unity, and wartime leadership. His bad was cautious domestic policy and contentious imperial expansion; his ugly was assassination and the bloody consequences of overseas colonization. McKinley illustrates the rise of the U.S. as a global power and the moral and political dilemmas of imperial ambition.
