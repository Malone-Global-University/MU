The Great Society Programs

Johnson launched the Great Society, which aimed to eliminate poverty and expand social welfare. Programs included Medicare, Medicaid, Head Start, and food stamps, transforming the social safety net.

Economic Expansion

Under Johnson, the U.S. economy experienced low unemployment and moderate growth, partially fueled by domestic spending and social programs.

Education and Infrastructure

Johnson prioritized education reform with the Elementary and Secondary Education Act (1965) and invested in infrastructure improvements, including urban development and highways.

Verdict

Lyndon B. Johnson was a transformative domestic leader whose good lay in civil rights, social welfare, and education reform. His bad included coercive political tactics and growing domestic division; his ugly was the Vietnam War, which overshadowed his achievements and complicated his historical legacy. Johnson illustrates the tension between visionary domestic leadership and the moral and political burdens of foreign conflict.
