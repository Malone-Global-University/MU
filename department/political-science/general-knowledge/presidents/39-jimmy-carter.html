Verdict

Jimmy Carter was a principled and moral president whose good lay in human rights advocacy, the Camp David peace process, and environmental leadership. His bad included economic mismanagement, limited legislative achievements, and struggles with Congress; his ugly was the Iran Hostage Crisis and public perception of weakness. Carter demonstrates the challenges of ethical, idealistic leadership in the face of complex domestic and international crises.
