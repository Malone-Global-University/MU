Imperialist Policies

Roosevelt supported U.S. imperial expansion and intervention in Latin America and the Caribbean, often disregarding the sovereignty of other nations through policies like the Roosevelt Corollary to the Monroe Doctrine.

Overbearing Leadership Style

Roosevelt could be domineering and controlling, centralizing power in the executive branch and sometimes undermining Congress or other institutions.

Mixed Record on Race

While Roosevelt invited Booker T. Washington to the White House, he also sanctioned segregation in federal offices in some cases and largely ignored systemic racial inequality.

Legacy

Verdict

Theodore Roosevelt was a bold, reform-minded president whose good lay in progressive domestic policies, conservation, and dynamic leadership. His bad was aggressive, sometimes overbearing executive behavior and imperialist policies; his ugly was the moral and political trade-offs of interventionism and his mixed record on race. Roosevelt embodies the energetic, transformative presidency of the early 20th century, with both inspiration and controversy in equal measure.
