Support for Presidential Authority

Johnson believed in a strong executive branch. He aimed to direct Reconstruction personally, emphasizing presidential discretion over Congressional authority in restoring Southern states to the Union.

Johnson moved swiftly to readmit Confederate states with minimal conditions, believing that reconciliation should be prioritized to rebuild the nation quickly after the Civil War.

Verdict

Andrew Johnson was a Unionist from humble origins who sought to restore the nation quickly after the Civil War. His good lay in loyalty and commitment to executive authority; his bad was ineffective, combative leadership; his ugly was the failure to protect African Americans and the exacerbation of sectional tensions. Johnson demonstrates how presidential power without moral and political foresight can deepen national wounds.
