Verdict

Bill Clinton was a politically skilled and economically effective president whose good lay in strong economic management, centrist policies, and domestic reforms. His bad included personal scandals, flawed social policies, and controversial trade agreements; his ugly was impeachment, political polarization, and long-term social consequences of some domestic programs. Clinton illustrates how personal flaws and policy trade-offs can complicate a historically successful presidency.
