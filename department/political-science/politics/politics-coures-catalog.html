Political Science — Course Catalog

Comprehensive curriculum overview: foundational cores, advanced seminars, regional tracks, and the capstone experience for a political science degree.

Program goals

The Political Science program trains students to analyze power, institutions, and political behaviour; to conduct evidence-based research; and to apply political thinking to real-world problems.

Degree outcomes

  • Theoretical literacy in political thought
  • Methodological competence in qualitative and quantitative research
  • Policy analysis and applied problem solving
  • Effective civic and professional communication

Core foundational courses

Introduction to Political Science

Overview of the field: major concepts, institutions, and branches of political inquiry.

Comparative Politics

Comparative methods for studying political systems, regime types, and institutions across countries.

International Relations

State behaviour, international organizations, security studies, and global political economy.

Political Theory

Canonical and contemporary political thought: justice, liberty, rights, and legitimacy.

American Government & Politics

U.S. constitutional structure, institutions, federalism, and public opinion.

Research Methods in Political Science

Quantitative and qualitative methods, data literacy, survey design, and case-study research.

Advanced & specialized courses

Public Policy & Administration

Policy design, implementation, evaluation, and the role of bureaucracy.

Political Economy

How political institutions shape economic outcomes, regulation, and development.

Constitutional Law

Courts, rights, and judicial review; examination of landmark cases.

Political Psychology

Persuasion, identity, emotion, and cognitive biases in political behaviour.

Political Communication

Media, framing, propaganda, and modern political campaigning.

Environmental Politics

Politics of climate, resources, global agreements, and sustainability policy.

Capstone & applied learning

The degree culminates in a senior seminar or capstone project: an original research paper, policy brief, or applied practicum. Internships with government agencies, NGOs, or think tanks are strongly encouraged.
