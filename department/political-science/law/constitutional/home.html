About Constitutional Law

Constitutional Law defines the powers and limits of government, the rights of individuals, and the balance between freedom and authority. It governs how laws are made, how disputes are resolved, and how the structure of the state functions under the rule of law.

This course explores foundational documents such as the U.S. Constitution, the Bill of Rights, the Federalist Papers, and landmark Supreme Court decisions that have shaped American legal history. Students will gain an understanding of federalism, separation of powers, judicial review, and constitutional interpretation.

Core Topics

  • Articles of Confederation
  • The U.S. Constitution
  • The Federalist Papers
  • Bill of Rights
  • Amendments and Evolution
  • Landmark Supreme Court Cases
