Debate

Argument • Evidence • Speaking • Cross-Examination • Judgment

A practical hub for building, testing, presenting, and judging arguments in public debate.

Overview

The Debate section turns political and philosophical study into disciplined public argument. Students learn how to form claims, support them with evidence, answer objections, question opponents, and evaluate which side has carried the burden of proof.

Debate Practice Areas

Debate Basics

Rules, roles, speech order, burdens, and the basic structure of a debate round.

Argument Structure

Claims, warrants, evidence, impacts, counterarguments, and weighing mechanisms.

Evidence and Research

Finding, testing, citing, and explaining evidence responsibly.

Cross-Examination

Asking useful questions, exposing weak claims, and clarifying disputed points.

Judging and Ballots

How debates are evaluated and how judges explain decisions fairly.

How to Use This Section

This section should help students argue with structure instead of volume: define the issue, prove the claim, answer the strongest objection, and explain why the argument matters.
