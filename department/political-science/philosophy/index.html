A political philosophy hub for the ideas behind law, government, justice, liberty, and moral responsibility.

Overview

The Philosophy section studies the ideas beneath political life: What is justice? Why should government have authority? What rights do people have? What makes a law legitimate? How should liberty, order, equality, and responsibility be balanced?

Philosophy Study Areas

Ethics and Public Life

Moral reasoning, civic virtue, responsibility, and ethical decision-making in public life.

Justice

Competing ideas of fairness, desert, equality, rights, law, and public order.

Rights and Liberty

Natural rights, civil liberties, freedom, property, conscience, and human dignity.

Authority and Legitimacy

Why governments claim authority and when political power is justified or abused.

Social Contract Theory

Consent, obligation, sovereignty, citizenship, and the foundations of political order.

How to Use This Section

This section should prepare students to identify the hidden assumptions inside political arguments: what the speaker believes about people, rights, morality, power, law, and justice.
