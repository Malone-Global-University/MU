Early Life

Born in Monroe, Louisiana, in 1942, Huey P. Newton moved to Oakland, California, as a child. He experienced systemic racism and poverty, which shaped his future activism and political views.

Rise to Prominence

Newton co-founded the Black Panther Party in 1966 with Bobby Seale, creating a revolutionary movement focused on self-defense, community programs, and challenging police brutality.

Main Achievements

  • Co-founded the Black Panther Party, pioneering community self-defense programs.
  • Established free breakfast programs, health clinics, and educational initiatives in Black communities.
  • Authored key writings on revolutionary theory and African-American liberation.

Legacy

Huey P. Newton is remembered as a symbol of radical Black empowerment, community activism, and the fight against systemic oppression, inspiring generations of activists worldwide.
