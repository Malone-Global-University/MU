Early Life

Thurgood Marshall was born in Baltimore, Maryland, in 1908. He attended Lincoln University and Howard University School of Law, where he excelled as a student and became inspired to fight racial injustice through the law.

Rise to Prominence

Marshall became a leading civil rights attorney, arguing cases that challenged segregation and inequality, including the landmark Brown v. Board of Education case in 1954 that ended school segregation.

Challenges

Marshall faced systemic racism, legal obstacles, and resistance to civil rights reforms while advocating in a hostile social and political environment.

Legacy

Thurgood Marshall is remembered as a legal pioneer, champion of civil rights, and trailblazer for African Americans in law and government, inspiring generations of lawyers and activists.
