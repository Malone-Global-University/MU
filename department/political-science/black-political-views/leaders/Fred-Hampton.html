Early Life

Born in Chicago, Illinois, in 1948, Fred Hampton grew up in a working-class African-American family. Early exposure to racial injustice inspired his commitment to activism and social change.

Rise to Prominence

Hampton became chairman of the Illinois chapter of the Black Panther Party and rose quickly as a skilled organizer and orator. He built coalitions with other community groups to address systemic inequality and poverty.
