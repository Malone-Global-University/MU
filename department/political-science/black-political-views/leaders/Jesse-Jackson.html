Early Life

Jesse Louis Jackson was born in Greenville, South Carolina, in 1941. He became active in the civil rights movement while attending the University of Illinois and later North Carolina A&T State University.

Rise to Prominence

Jackson worked closely with Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. and became a key organizer in the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC). He gained national recognition for his activism and advocacy for racial equality, voting rights, and social justice.

Main Achievements

  • Founded the organizations Operation PUSH (People United to Save Humanity) and the Rainbow/PUSH Coalition.
  • Ran for U.S. President in 1984 and 1988, inspiring millions and building a coalition for African American and progressive voters.
  • Advised multiple U.S. presidents on civil rights and social justice issues.

Challenges

Jackson faced significant political opposition, controversy, and personal attacks throughout his career, but remained a resilient advocate for civil rights and social change.

Legacy

Jesse Jackson is remembered as a transformative civil rights leader, political trailblazer, and tireless advocate for justice and equality in the United States and beyond.
