Early Life

Born in St. Annâ€™s Bay, Jamaica, in 1887, Garvey grew up witnessing racial oppression and colonial inequality. He traveled extensively in the Caribbean, Central America, and the U.S., developing his vision for African unity.

Rise to Prominence

In 1914, he founded the Universal Negro Improvement Association (UNIA), aiming to unite people of African descent worldwide politically, economically, and socially.

Main Achievements

  • Founded the UNIA and promoted Pan-Africanism globally.
  • Launched the Black Star Line shipping company to facilitate trade and repatriation to Africa.
  • Inspired future African independence movements and leaders.

Challenges

Garvey faced political opposition, deportation from the U.S., and financial difficulties with the Black Star Line, but his ideas endured beyond these setbacks.

Legacy

Marcus Garvey remains a symbol of Black empowerment, Pan-African unity, and economic self-determination. His message influenced leaders like Kwame Nkrumah and Malcolm X.
