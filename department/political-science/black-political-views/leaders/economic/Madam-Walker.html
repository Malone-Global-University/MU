Early Life

Madam C.J. Walker, born Sarah Breedlove in 1867 in Louisiana, was the daughter of formerly enslaved parents. Orphaned at a young age, she worked as a laundress before developing her business empire.

Rise to Prominence

Walker developed hair care products for African American women, founded the Madam C.J. Walker Manufacturing Company, and trained a network of sales agents, empowering Black women economically.

Main Achievements

  • First self-made female millionaire in America.
  • Founded a successful cosmetics and haircare business for African American women.
  • Philanthropist: supported education, civil rights causes, and political activism for Black communities.

Challenges

Walker faced racial and gender discrimination in business and society, but she overcame barriers through innovation, entrepreneurship, and empowerment of her employees and communities.

Legacy

Madam C.J. Walker remains an enduring symbol of African American entrepreneurship, female empowerment, and philanthropy.
