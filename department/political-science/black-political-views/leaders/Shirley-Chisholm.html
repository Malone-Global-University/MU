Early Life

Shirley Anita Chisholm was born in Brooklyn, New York, in 1924 to immigrant parents from the Caribbean. She attended Brooklyn College and Columbia University, later becoming a teacher and social worker.

Challenges

Chisholm faced racism and sexism in politics, both as a Black woman in Congress and during her presidential campaign, but she persisted in advocating for underrepresented communities.

Legacy

Shirley Chisholm is remembered as a trailblazer for women and African Americans in politics, inspiring generations to participate in public service and leadership.
