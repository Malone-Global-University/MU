Maurice Bishop was a Grenadian revolutionary and Prime Minister who led the New Jewel Movement, a Marxist-Leninist organization that overthrew the government in 1979. His leadership brought widespread social reform, improved education and healthcare, and fostered grassroots political participation throughout Grenada.

Bishop was born in Aruba to Grenadian parents and moved to Grenada as a child. He studied law in London, where he became deeply influenced by socialist and Pan-Africanist ideas. Upon returning home, Bishop co-founded the New Jewel Movement (NJM) in 1973, advocating for independence from neocolonial influence, economic equality, and social justice. His charisma and oratory made him a popular figure among workers, students, and rural citizens, propelling him to national leadership.
