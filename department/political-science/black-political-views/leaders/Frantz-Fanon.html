Early Life

Born in Martinique in 1925, Frantz Fanon was exposed to the effects of colonialism and racial inequality from a young age. He trained as a physician and psychiatrist, eventually focusing on the psychological impacts of colonization.

Rise to Prominence

Fanon worked in Algeria during its fight for independence, gaining firsthand experience of anti-colonial struggles. He became an influential voice through his writings on decolonization, racial identity, and liberation.

Main Achievements

  • Author of seminal works including "Black Skin, White Masks" and "The Wretched of the Earth."
  • Analyzed the psychological effects of colonization on both colonizers and the colonized.
  • Advised revolutionary movements in Algeria and other African liberation struggles.

Challenges

Fanon faced colonial opposition, exile, and personal health challenges, including tuberculosis, which ultimately led to his death at age 36.
