Early Life

Elijah Robert Poole, later known as Elijah Muhammad, was born in Sandersville, Georgia, in 1897. He moved north and eventually became a prominent leader in the Nation of Islam.

Rise to Prominence

In the 1930s, Muhammad became a disciple of Wallace Fard Muhammad and rose to leadership of the Nation of Islam. Under his guidance, the organization expanded rapidly, promoting self-reliance, economic empowerment, and religious discipline for African Americans.

Main Achievements

  • Led the Nation of Islam for decades, establishing mosques, schools, and businesses to support the African American community.
  • Mentored influential figures, including Malcolm X and Muhammad Ali.
  • Promoted economic independence, education, and social upliftment for African Americans in urban communities.
