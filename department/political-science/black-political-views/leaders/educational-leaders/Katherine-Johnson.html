Early Life

Katherine Coleman Johnson was born on August 26, 1918, in White Sulphur Springs, West Virginia. From a young age, she demonstrated exceptional talent in mathematics and science. She attended West Virginia University at only 15 years old, graduating summa cum laude in mathematics and French.

Career at NASA

Katherine Johnson joined the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA), which later became NASA, in 1953. She calculated critical flight trajectories for early U.S. space missions, including:

  • The Mercury-Atlas 6 flight of John Glenn, America's first orbital mission.
  • The Apollo 11 mission that landed the first humans on the Moon.
  • Various shuttle missions and satellite launches.

Her mathematical precision helped ensure the safety and success of these pioneering missions.
