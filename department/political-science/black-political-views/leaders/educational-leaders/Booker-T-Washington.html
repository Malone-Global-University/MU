Early Life

Born into slavery in Virginia in 1856, Washington experienced freedom after the Civil War. He valued education from an early age, working odd jobs to attend school and pursue literacy.

Rise to Prominence

He founded the Tuskegee Normal and Industrial Institute in Alabama in 1881, emphasizing vocational training as a path to African-American self-sufficiency.

Main Achievements

  • Founded Tuskegee Institute, a model educational institution.
  • Promoted industrial education as a strategy for economic empowerment.
  • Became a national advisor to U.S. presidents on African-American education.

Challenges

Criticized by contemporaries like W. E. B. Du Bois for accommodating segregation and advocating a gradual approach to civil rights.
