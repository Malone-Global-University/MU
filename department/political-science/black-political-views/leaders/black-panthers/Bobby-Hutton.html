Bobby Hutton

Black Panther Party Organizer and Early Movement Figure (1950–1968)

Home › Political Science › Black Political Views › African Leader › Bobby Hutton

Early Life

Robert James Hutton, often called “Lil’ Bobby,” was born on April 21, 1950, in Jefferson County, Arkansas. At a young age, his family moved to Oakland, California as part of the Great Migration, seeking safety and opportunity after facing racial intimidation in the South. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Entry into the Black Panther Party

At just sixteen years old, Hutton became the first official member of the Black Panther Party and served as its first treasurer. He joined after meeting founders Huey Newton and Bobby Seale at a community center in Oakland. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

Role and Activities

Hutton was involved in early Party activities, including community engagement and public demonstrations. He participated in the 1967 protest at the California State Capitol opposing gun control legislation targeting the Panthers. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Confrontation and Death

On April 6, 1968, following a confrontation between Black Panther members and Oakland police, Hutton was killed during a shootout. Accounts of the event differ: some witnesses and Party members stated he was shot while surrendering, while police reports claimed he attempted to flee. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

Historical Impact

Hutton’s death became a defining moment for the Black Panther Party and the broader Black Power movement. As the first Panther to be killed, he became a symbol of the risks faced by members and intensified public attention on the Party’s conflict with law enforcement. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}

Legacy

Bobby Hutton is remembered as one of the earliest figures in the Black Panther Party and as a symbol of youth involvement in political movements. His legacy continues through memorial events, cultural references, and recognition of his role in the early development of the organization. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
