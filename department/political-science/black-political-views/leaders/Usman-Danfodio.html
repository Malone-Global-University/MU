Early Life

Usman dan Fodio was born in 1754 in Maratta, in present-day northern Nigeria. A devout Islamic scholar, he studied the Quran extensively and gained recognition for his knowledge, teaching, and moral integrity from an early age.

Religious Leadership

Dan Fodio preached Islamic reform, emphasizing morality, justice, and education. He criticized corruption and syncretic practices among local rulers and advocated for governance in line with Islamic principles.

Founding the Sokoto Caliphate

In 1804, Usman dan Fodio led a jihad (religious and social reform movement) that overthrew the Hausa kingdoms and established the Sokoto Caliphate. It became one of the largest and most influential states in West Africa, known for Islamic scholarship, law, and governance.
