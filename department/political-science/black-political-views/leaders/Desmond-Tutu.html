Early Life

Desmond Mpilo Tutu was born on October 7, 1931, in Klerksdorp, South Africa. He trained as a teacher before becoming an Anglican priest, eventually rising to Archbishop of Cape Town. His early experiences with racial segregation deeply shaped his commitment to social justice.

Anti-Apartheid Activism

Tutu was a leading voice against apartheid, advocating nonviolent resistance and international sanctions against the South African regime. He emphasized moral and spiritual leadership, calling for equality and justice for all South Africans.

Legacy

Desmond Tutu remains a symbol of courage, compassion, and unwavering moral leadership. He received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1984 and inspired generations worldwide to fight injustice through nonviolent means.
