Early Life

Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela was born on July 18, 1918, in Mvezo, Transkei, South Africa. He belonged to the Thembu royal family and was educated at mission schools and the University of Fort Hare. Mandela developed early awareness of racial injustice under the oppressive apartheid system.

Political Activism

Mandela became a leader of the African National Congress (ANC) and co-founded its militant wing, Umkhonto we Sizwe, to resist apartheid. He was arrested in 1962 and sentenced to life imprisonment for sabotage and conspiracy. Mandela spent 27 years in prison, mostly on Robben Island.

Legacy

Mandela is celebrated globally as a symbol of resistance, forgiveness, and leadership. He received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993 and inspired generations of activists and political leaders worldwide.
