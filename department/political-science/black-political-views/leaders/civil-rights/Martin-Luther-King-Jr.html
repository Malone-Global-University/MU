Early Life

Born in Atlanta, Georgia, in 1929, Martin Luther King Jr. was raised in a religious household. He excelled academically and attended Morehouse College, Crozer Theological Seminary, and Boston University.

Rise to Prominence

King became pastor of Dexter Avenue Baptist Church in Montgomery, Alabama, and rose to national prominence by leading the 1955 Montgomery Bus Boycott and advocating nonviolent protest.

Main Achievements

  • Led the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC).
  • Organized the 1963 March on Washington, delivering his iconic "I Have a Dream" speech.
  • Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize in 1964.
  • Instrumental in the passage of the Civil Rights Act (1964) and Voting Rights Act (1965).

Challenges

King faced violent opposition, arrests, and constant threats from segregationists and government surveillance. His life was ultimately cut short by assassination in 1968.

Legacy

Martin Luther King Jr. remains a global symbol of nonviolent resistance, civil rights, and social justice, inspiring movements worldwide.
