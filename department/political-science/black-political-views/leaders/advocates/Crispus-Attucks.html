Early Life

Crispus Attucks was born around 1723, likely in Framingham, Massachusetts, of African and Native American descent. He worked as a sailor and rope maker, experiencing the hardships of slavery and oppression during colonial America.

Rise to Prominence

Attucks became a symbol of resistance due to his participation in colonial protests against British rule. He gained historical prominence when he was killed during the Boston Massacre in 1770, marking the first death in the fight for American independence.

Main Achievements

  • Recognized as the first casualty of the American Revolution.
  • Symbol of African-American contribution to American independence.
  • Posthumously honored for his role in the struggle for freedom and justice.

Challenges

Attucks faced systemic racial oppression as a Black man in colonial America and lived under the constant threat of slavery and discrimination. His life was cut short in a violent confrontation with British soldiers.

Legacy

Crispus Attucks is remembered as a patriotic martyr and early symbol of African-American courage and resistance. Monuments, schools, and historical commemorations honor his contribution to American independence.
