Early Life

Born LeRoi Jones on October 7, 1934, in Newark, New Jersey, Baraka was a child of the segregated North. He showed early interest in literature and music and pursued writing from a young age.

Career and Contributions

Baraka became a central figure in the Black Arts Movement, using poetry, plays, and essays to advance African-American cultural pride and political consciousness. His works include:

  • Blues People (1963)
  • Black Art (Poem, 1966)
  • The Dutchman (Play, 1964)

Political and Cultural Activism

Amiri Baraka was deeply engaged in politics, promoting Black nationalism, civil rights, and community empowerment. He founded cultural organizations to support African-American artists and activists.
