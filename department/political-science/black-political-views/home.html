Introduction to African American Politics 101

This course is for building African American World Leaders. It will showcase leading political views of the African diaspora past and present from an American point of view. Then it hightlight the successes, failures, and lessons learned from them. At the end a distillation of strongest strategies will be presented that rejects inherent miscalculations of previous leadership. Only Strongest Moves - No Compromise.

Key Topics

  • Reparations
  • White Supremacy
  • Black Betrayal
  • Classical, Liberal, Capitalist, and Postmodern Theories
  • Applications in Revolutionary and Colonial Contexts
  • The failures and successes of cointel pro-esque subversive groups and African American agents who serve them
  • Official Malone Doctrine as a sovereign institution and its non-alignment to anti-black collaborators

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the origins and evolution of black political thought
  • Analyze systems of power from a critical, strategic perspective
  • Apply political theory to contemporary and historical scenarios
  • Challenge mainstream paradigms with rigorous logic and evidence

Format

This is a self-paced online course. Videos, readings, and discussion prompts are provided. No prior coursework required. Instructor guidance is optional via scholarship or paid enrollment.
