African American Politics 101

History • Leadership • Grievances • Political Thought • Self-Determination

A study hub for Black political thought, historical argument, leadership, civic struggle, and political self-definition.

Introduction

This course is for building African American World Leaders. It will showcase leading political views of the African diaspora past and present from an American point of view. Then it hightlight the successes, failures, and lessons learned from them. At the end a distillation of strongest strategies will be presented that rejects inherent miscalculations of previous leadership. Only Strongest Moves - No Compromise.

Key Topics

  • Reparations
  • White Supremacy
  • Black Betrayal
  • Classical, Liberal, Capitalist, and Postmodern Theories
  • Applications in Revolutionary and Colonial Contexts
  • The failures and successes of cointel pro-esque subversive groups and African American agents who serve them
  • Official Malone Doctrine as a sovereign institution and its non-alignment to anti-black collaborators

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand the origins and evolution of black political thought
  • Analyze systems of power from a critical, strategic perspective
  • Apply political theory to contemporary and historical scenarios
  • Challenge mainstream paradigms with rigorous logic and evidence

Format

This is a self-paced online course. Videos, readings, and discussion prompts are provided. No prior coursework required. Instructor guidance is optional via scholarship or paid enrollment.

Courses

These course links organize Black political thought into structured study paths that represent official political positions of the African American demographic. Courses move from historical foundations to political ideas, leadership, grievance, independence, and modern civic argument.

Introduction to Black Political Thought

An introductory course on major questions, traditions, leaders, documents, and debates in Black political life.

Slavery, Citizenship, and the State

A course on slavery, law, citizenship, state power, civil status, and the political consequences of bondage and emancipation.

Black Leadership and Political Movements

A study of leaders, organizations, speeches, movements, and strategies used to pursue rights, power, justice, and self-determination.

Grievance, Repair, and Political Justice

A course on historical grievance, public complaint, reparative justice, institutional responsibility, and political demands for remedy.

Black Nationalism and Self-Determination

A course on political independence, sovereignty arguments, nationalism, separatism, community control, and self-definition.

Black Political Theory and Modern Democracy

An advanced course on democracy, constitutionalism, race, citizenship, protest, public memory, political identity, and civic power.

Essays & Documents

These essays and documents support independent reading, political analysis, and source-based study. They may include interpretive essays, historical summaries, declarations, arguments, and issue-focused pages.

Political Traditions

An overview of major traditions in Black political argument, public identity, citizenship, justice, and political power.

Leaders

Essays on major leaders, organizations, speeches, and movement traditions in Black political history.

Transatlantic Slavery

Historical essays on slave trade systems, law, economics, memory, resistance, and political consequences.

Grievances

Essays on historical injuries, civic complaints, political claims, institutional responsibility, and demands for remedy.

Declaration of Black Independence

A declaration-style political document focused on self-definition, historical grievance, public argument, and political independence.

Acts of War

A source-based political analysis of conflict language, historical grievance, sovereignty claims, resistance arguments, and public accountability.
