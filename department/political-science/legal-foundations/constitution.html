The U.S. Constitution: Structure & Principles

A focused reading of the Constitution and the mechanisms that make it binding.

Home › Department › Political Science ›

Learning Objectives

  • Summarize the content and purpose of Articles I–VII.
  • Explain separation of powers, checks and balances, judicial review, and the amendment process.
  • Identify key clauses (Commerce Clause, Necessary and Proper, Supremacy Clause) and their institutional effects.

Core Content

Articles — a quick map

  • Article I — Legislative Branch: powers of Congress, lawmaking process, enumerated powers.
  • Article II — Executive Branch: presidential powers, duties, and selection.
  • Article III — Judicial Branch: federal courts and jurisdiction.
  • Article IV — Relations among the States; full faith and credit; privileges and immunities.
  • Article V — Amendment process.
  • Article VI — Supremacy Clause: Constitution, federal laws, and treaties as supreme.
  • Article VII — Ratification rules.

Important clauses

Commerce Clause: grants Congress power to regulate interstate commerce and has historically been central to federal regulatory authority.
Necessary & Proper Clause: permits Congress to use means reasonably adapted to carry out its enumerated powers.
Supremacy Clause: federal law displaces conflicting state law.

Amendment procedure

Amendments can be proposed by two-thirds of both Houses of Congress or by a constitutional convention called by two-thirds of state legislatures; ratification requires approval by three-fourths of the states (state legislatures or conventions).

Primary Readings

  • U.S. Constitution — read Preamble and Articles I–V carefully.
  • Selected Federalist Papers: No. 10 (factions), No. 51 (checks and balances).

Activities

  • Article brief: assign students one Article to summarize and present institutional implications.
  • Clause analysis: students analyze a clause (e.g., Commerce Clause) and debate its modern scope.

Assessment

Written assignment: Explain how the amendment process is designed to balance adaptability and stability (500–750 words).
