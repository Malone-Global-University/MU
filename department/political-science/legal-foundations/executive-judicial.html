Executive Actions & Judicial Review

The executive branch, administrative rulemaking, and how courts review official action.

Learning Objectives

  • Explain the nature and limits of executive orders and presidential directives.
  • Describe administrative law and the rulemaking/notice-and-comment process.
  • Understand judicial review and how courts assess the constitutionality of statutes and executive actions.

Core Content

Executive Actions

Presidents use executive orders, memoranda, and proclamations to direct the executive branch. These acts must be grounded in constitutional or statutory authority; they are subject to judicial review if challenged.

Administrative Rulemaking

Federal agencies implement statutes through rules and regulations. The Administrative Procedure Act (APA) sets procedures for notice, comment, and judicial review of agency action (students should consult the APA text in lessons).

Judicial Review

Courts evaluate the constitutionality of statutes and executive acts. Review includes assessing statutory interpretation, administrative deference doctrines (e.g., Chevron-style frameworks where applicable), and constitutional scrutiny levels (rational basis, intermediate, strict).

Activities

  • Rulemaking simulation: students draft a proposed regulation and run a mock notice-and-comment session.
  • Judicial memo: brief a case that tests an executive order for constitutionality; advise the court on applicable standard of review.

Assessment

Write a 600-word memo advising whether a hypothetical executive order exceeds statutory authority, citing separation of powers principles.
