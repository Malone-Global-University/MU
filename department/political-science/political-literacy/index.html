Political Literacy

Power • Institutions • Elections • Policy • Civic Judgment

A practical guide to understanding politics without becoming trapped by slogans, parties, or surface-level arguments.

Overview

Political Literacy helps students understand how political systems work, how public arguments are built, how power moves through institutions, and how citizens can evaluate claims without relying only on partisan identity.

Learning Pathway

Politics 101

A beginner-friendly introduction to political systems, public authority, and basic civic vocabulary.

Personal Political Literacy

How individuals form political beliefs, evaluate claims, and participate responsibly.

Local Politics

City, county, school board, and local administrative systems that shape daily life.

National Politics

Federal institutions, national parties, elections, legislation, and policy debates.

Nonpartisan Analysis

Tools for separating evidence, ideology, rhetoric, interest, and institutional incentives.
