Political Science Course Catalog

Explore the core and elective courses that form the foundation of political understanding at Malone Global University.

Core Foundational Courses

Introduction to Political Science

This course introduces the systematic study of politics, government, and power. Students explore key concepts such as authority, legitimacy, justice, and participation through a survey of political systems and ideologies. The class serves as a gateway to the discipline, emphasizing critical analysis of political structures and their impact on societies worldwide. By the end, students will be able to identify political patterns, assess the role of government institutions, and understand how citizens shape governance.

Comparative Politics

Comparative Politics examines the similarities and differences among political systems across nations. Students analyze how governments are organized, how power is distributed, and how institutions influence public policy. Topics include democratization, authoritarianism, revolution, and state-building. Case studies from Africa, Asia, Europe, and Latin America illustrate the diversity of political experiences. The course equips students with frameworks for comparing nations and understanding global political trends.

International Relations

This course explores how states, international organizations, and non-state actors interact in the global arena. Students examine major theories such as realism, liberalism, and constructivism to understand war, peace, and diplomacy. Topics include international law, global security, humanitarian intervention, and globalization. Through simulations and policy analysis, students learn to evaluate global conflicts and cooperative efforts like the United Nations, NATO, and trade agreements.

Political Theory / Philosophy

This course investigates the philosophical foundations of political life, from ancient thinkers like Plato and Aristotle to modern theorists such as Locke, Marx, and Rawls. Students engage with fundamental questions about justice, liberty, equality, and the nature of power. The class encourages critical reading and discussion of seminal texts, fostering the ability to connect theory to modern issues such as democracy, rights, and governance.

American Government and Politics

This course offers a detailed study of the U.S. political system, including the Constitution, federalism, and the three branches of government. Students explore the roles of political parties, interest groups, and the media, as well as patterns in public opinion and voter behavior. Case studies highlight key moments in American political development and provide context for current policy debates. Emphasis is placed on civic participation and constitutional literacy.

Research Methods in Political Science

Students learn the methodological tools political scientists use to study power and governance. The course covers qualitative and quantitative methods, hypothesis formation, data collection, and statistical analysis. Through practical projects, students conduct surveys, interpret polling data, and design comparative studies. The goal is to build analytical literacy and prepare students for advanced research or policy work.

Advanced & Specialized Courses

Public Policy and Administration

This course examines how governments create and implement policies to address public issues. Students explore the policy cycle—agenda setting, formulation, implementation, and evaluation—and the roles of bureaucracy, ethics, and intergovernmental relations. Emphasis is placed on real-world policy analysis, budget processes, and administrative decision-making within democratic systems.

Political Economy

Political Economy investigates the dynamic relationship between economics and politics. Students study how power and institutions shape markets, taxation, labor, and global trade. The course covers classical and modern theories of capitalism and socialism and explores economic inequality, globalization, and development. Students will learn to analyze how political forces influence economic policy and vice versa.

Constitutional Law

This course delves into the structure and interpretation of constitutional law, focusing on judicial review, the separation of powers, and civil rights. Students analyze landmark Supreme Court cases and explore how constitutional principles evolve through precedent. The course also examines the tension between liberty and security, the balance between federal and state power, and the enduring impact of constitutional debates on American society.

Political Psychology

Political Psychology explores how psychological processes influence political behavior. Topics include identity formation, group dynamics, propaganda, and decision-making under stress. Students examine how emotion, bias, and perception shape voting behavior, public opinion, and leadership. The course integrates psychology with political science to better understand why citizens and leaders think and act as they do.

Political Communication

This course investigates how information, media, and language shape political life. Students study campaign strategies, propaganda, and the role of digital media in modern politics. Topics include framing, agenda-setting, and misinformation. Through media analysis and communication projects, students gain insight into how narratives influence elections and governance.

Environmental Politics

Environmental Politics explores the political challenges of sustainability, resource management, and climate change. Students analyze global environmental governance, including treaties, NGOs, and green movements. The course discusses the political economy of environmental issues, balancing growth with ecological responsibility, and the ethical implications of environmental decision-making.

Gender and Politics

This course examines how gender shapes political participation, representation, and policy. Students explore feminist theory, the history of women’s movements, and the intersection of gender with race and class. Through case studies, the class highlights ongoing struggles for equality and evaluates the role of gender in both domestic and international politics.

Race, Ethnicity, and Politics

Students analyze how race and ethnicity influence political identity, participation, and power. The course explores historical and contemporary struggles for civil rights, representation, and equity. Case studies and primary sources are used to discuss systemic inequality, social movements, and policy reform in comparative and global contexts.

Global & Regional Focus

African Politics

This course explores the political history, governance challenges, and postcolonial dynamics of African nations. Topics include state-building, democratization, and regional integration, emphasizing indigenous political traditions and modern reform movements.

European Politics

Examines political systems, institutions, and ideologies across Europe, including the European Union. Students study nationalism, populism, and integration, comparing Western and Eastern European political developments.

Latin American Politics

Analyzes the interplay of democracy, revolution, and economic development in Latin America. Students explore the effects of colonialism, populism, and globalization on regional stability and reform.

Middle Eastern Politics

Surveys political structures, religion, and conflict in the Middle East. Topics include authoritarianism, reform movements, oil politics, and international relations in a turbulent region.

Asian Politics

Explores political systems and development across East, South, and Southeast Asia. The course compares democracies, monarchies, and one-party states, highlighting cultural and economic diversity.

Global Governance and International Organizations

Focuses on how international institutions manage cooperation and conflict. Students study the UN, WTO, IMF, and regional organizations, assessing their impact on global order and justice.

Foreign Policy Analysis

Examines the process and strategy behind state decision-making in foreign affairs. Students analyze diplomacy, national interests, and crisis management through case studies of major world powers.

Capstone & Applied Learning

Senior Seminar / Capstone Project

In their final year, students complete a major research project or policy paper demonstrating mastery of political analysis. This seminar integrates theoretical learning with empirical research, culminating in a professional presentation or publication-ready work.

Internship / Practicum

Provides practical experience in a political or public service environment. Students may work in government offices, NGOs, advocacy groups, or research institutions, applying classroom knowledge to real-world contexts.

Political Data Analysis

This advanced methods course focuses on quantitative analysis using datasets, polls, and statistical software. Students learn regression, causal inference, and visualization techniques to interpret complex political data accurately.
