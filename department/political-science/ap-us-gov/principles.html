Democracy is guided by core principles that ensure government by the people, for the people. These principles create a system that is fair, accountable, and protective of individual freedoms.

  • Popular Sovereignty: Government derives its authority from the people, who exercise power through voting and civic participation.
  • Rule of Law: Everyone, including leaders, must follow the law, ensuring fairness and accountability.
  • Separation of Powers: Power is divided among branches (executive, legislative, judicial) to prevent concentration of authority.
  • Checks and Balances: Each branch limits the powers of the others, promoting accountability and preventing abuse.
  • Individual Rights and Freedoms: Citizens enjoy fundamental freedoms, including speech, religion, and assembly, protecting them from government overreach.
  • Majority Rule with Minority Rights: Decisions reflect the majority while safeguarding minority rights and perspectives.
  • Free and Fair Elections: Regular, transparent elections allow citizens to choose their leaders and hold them accountable.
  • Political Pluralism and Participation: Multiple parties and interest groups operate freely, encouraging civic engagement and diverse opinions.
  • Accountability and Transparency: Officials are answerable to the people, with transparent policies and decisions to prevent corruption.
  • Equality: All citizens have equal rights and opportunities to participate in government, fostering fairness and social justice.
