Founding Documents

The United States was founded on key documents that defined its ideals and institutions. These include The Declaration of Independence, The Constitution, and the Bill of Rights. Collectively these three Documents are known as the "Charters of Freedom". The other two founding documents are the Nation's first constitution the Articles of Confederation, and the Federalist Papers which were a series of essays arguing for adoption of the constitution.

  • Declaration of Independence (1776): Declared the colonies free from Britain and asserted the right to self-government.
  • Constitution (1787): Established the framework of government and the balance of powers.
  • Bill of Rights (1791): The first ten amendments securing individual liberties.
  • Articles of Confederation (1781): Established the framework of government and the balance of powers.
  • Federalist Papers (1781): Established the framework of government and the balance of powers.

    Federal Structure

    The U.S. government is built on a federal system that divides power among branches and levels:

    The Three Branches

    Legislative: Congress makes the laws.
    Executive: The President enforces the laws.
    Judicial: The Courts interpret the laws.

    Federal, State, and Local Levels

    Each level of government has its own powers and responsibilities, ensuring a balance between national unity and local autonomy.
