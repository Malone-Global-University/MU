The Fourth Amendment

Text

The right of the people to be secure in their persons, houses, papers, and effects, against unreasonable searches and seizures, shall not be violated, and no Warrants shall issue, but upon probable cause, supported by Oath or affirmation, and particularly describing the place to be searched, and the persons or things to be seized.

Key Points

  • Protects against unreasonable searches and seizures by the government.
  • Requires warrants to be based on probable cause and specific descriptions.

Historical Background

Ratified in 1791, the Fourth Amendment was influenced by colonial experiences with British search practices such as writs of assistance, ensuring privacy and security for citizens.

Modern Relevance

The amendment guides law enforcement practices, digital privacy, and surveillance regulations. Courts frequently interpret the balance between security and personal rights.
