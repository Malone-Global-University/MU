The Second Amendment

Text

“A well regulated Militia, being necessary to the security of a free State, the right of the people to keep and bear Arms, shall not be infringed.”

Key Points

  • Right to Bear Arms: Protects the individual’s right to possess and carry weapons.
  • Militia Clause: References the importance of a “well regulated Militia” for the security of the state.
  • Legal Interpretation: Courts have debated whether this protects individual rights, collective rights through state militias, or both.

Historical Background

Ratified in 1791 as part of the Bill of Rights, the Second Amendment was influenced by colonial experiences with militias and fears of a standing army. It was intended to allow citizens to defend themselves, their communities, and their state governments.

Modern Relevance

The Second Amendment remains central to debates on gun ownership, gun control, and public safety in the United States. Supreme Court rulings, such as District of Columbia v. Heller (2008), have affirmed an individual’s right to possess firearms for lawful purposes, including self-defense at home.
