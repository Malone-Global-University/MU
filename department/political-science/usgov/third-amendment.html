The Third Amendment

Text

No Soldier shall, in time of peace be quartered in any house, without the consent of the Owner, nor in time of war, but in a manner to be prescribed by law.

Key Points

  • Protects homeowners from having soldiers forced into their homes without consent.
  • Reflects colonial grievances under British rule.

Historical Background

Adopted in 1791 as part of the Bill of Rights, this amendment was influenced by colonial resistance to British quartering of troops. It ensures private homes cannot be used as military housing without permission.

Modern Relevance

Although rarely litigated today, it underscores privacy rights and the principle of civilian control over the military.
