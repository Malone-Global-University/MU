Founding Documents • Federal Structure • Civics • Modern Governance

A study hub for American government, civic institutions, constitutional foundations, and practical citizenship.

Overview

The U.S. Government section introduces students to the structure, powers, limits, and responsibilities of American government. It connects founding documents with the practical work of citizenship, civic literacy, lawmaking, elections, federalism, and public accountability.

Core Study Areas

Civics

Citizen responsibilities, public participation, voting, civic duties, and practical government knowledge.

Federal Government

The national government, separation of powers, federal agencies, and constitutional authority.

Founding Documents

Primary texts such as the Constitution, Declaration of Independence, and Federalist writings.

Founding Fathers

Major figures, debates, compromises, and political ideas from the founding period.

Modern Government

Contemporary institutions, public administration, political conflict, and modern policy questions.

Supplement

Extra readings, study aids, classroom references, and supporting materials.
