Early Life & Background

George Washington was born in 1732 in Westmoreland County, Virginia. A surveyor, soldier, and planter, he rose to prominence during the French and Indian War. His reputation for discipline, honor, and leadership made him a natural candidate to command the Continental Army during the American Revolution.

Political Aftermath & Legacy

Washington's presidency set the tone for the nation: a strong but limited executive, nonpartisan leadership, and peaceful transitions of power.
