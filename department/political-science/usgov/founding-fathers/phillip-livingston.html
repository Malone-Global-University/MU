Early Life & Background

Philip Livingston was born in 1716 in Albany, New York. Coming from a prominent merchant family, he became a successful businessman and influential political figure in colonial New York. His wealth and connections helped him play a key role in revolutionary politics.

Political Beliefs & Philosophy

Livingston supported colonial self-governance and was an early advocate for independence from Britain. He believed in the protection of individual liberties and the right of colonies to manage their own affairs, aligning with the revolutionary cause.

How His Beliefs Changed Over Time

Initially involved in local politics and trade, Livingston gradually embraced the idea of complete independence as British policies became increasingly restrictive and taxation without representation escalated tensions in the colonies.
