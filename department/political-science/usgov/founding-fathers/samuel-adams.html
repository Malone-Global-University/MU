Early Life & Background

Born in 1722 in Boston, Massachusetts, Samuel Adams was educated at Harvard and became a brewer and businessman. He was deeply involved in local politics and became a vocal critic of British taxation policies.

Political Beliefs & Philosophy

Adams strongly advocated colonial rights and self-government. He believed in active civic participation, the power of public opinion, and the importance of resisting tyranny.

How His Beliefs Changed Over Time

Adams initially worked within colonial legislative structures but increasingly embraced revolutionary activism as Britain imposed heavier taxes and restrictive laws. He became a leading figure in organizing protests and shaping revolutionary ideology.

Rivalries

Adams often clashed with more moderate colonial leaders who advocated compromise with Britain. His confrontational approach to resistance created tension but also galvanized support for independence.
