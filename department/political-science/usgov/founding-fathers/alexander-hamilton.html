Early Life

Born in 1755 or 1757 on Nevis in the West Indies, Hamilton was orphaned young and rose through talent and ambition. He was educated in New York and became a key aide to General George Washington during the Revolutionary War.

Political Career

Hamilton served as the first Secretary of the Treasury, creating the foundation for American finance: a national bank, assumption of state debts, and promotion of manufacturing. He co-authored The Federalist Papers to support ratification of the Constitution and became a leading Federalist in American politics.

Political Philosophy

Hamilton favored a strong central government, a robust executive, a national bank, and a commercial economy. He viewed the wealthy and educated elite as natural leaders in governance and often clashed with Jeffersonian agrarians.
