Early Life & Background

John Morton was born in 1725 in Ridley Township, Pennsylvania. Coming from a family of Swedish and Finnish settlers, he trained in law and became an active member of Pennsylvania politics, gaining respect for his judgment and integrity.

Political Beliefs & Philosophy

Morton supported colonial rights and independence while emphasizing moderation and compromise. He valued unity among the colonies and saw participation in the Continental Congress as a duty to represent Pennsylvania responsibly.

How His Beliefs Changed Over Time

Initially hesitant to break with Britain, Morton gradually supported independence as events made compromise impossible. His evolution reflected both personal conviction and the influence of Pennsylvania colleagues pressing for decisive action.
