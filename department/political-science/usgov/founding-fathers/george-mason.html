Early Life

Born in 1725 in Fairfax County, Virginia, George Mason was a planter and political leader. He received a classical education and became deeply interested in law, government, and civil rights.

Political Career

Mason authored the Virginia Declaration of Rights in 1776, which influenced the U.S. Bill of Rights. He served in the Virginia House of Burgesses and the Constitutional Convention, where he notably refused to sign the Constitution due to lack of a bill of rights.

Political Philosophy

Mason championed individual liberties, limited government, and the protection of natural rights. He believed that government should derive power from the consent of the governed and that civil liberties must be explicitly safeguarded.

Rivalries

Mason vs. Federalists: Opposed Hamilton, Madison, and others over the absence of a bill of rights.
Mason vs. Anti-Federalists (some): Occasionally disagreed on strategies for influencing ratification debates.
