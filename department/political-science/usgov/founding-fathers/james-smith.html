Early Life & Background

James Smith was born in 1719 in Pennsylvania. He was a lawyer by training and became involved in colonial politics, representing Pennsylvania in the Continental Congress during the American Revolution.

Political Beliefs & Philosophy

Smith was a strong advocate for independence from Britain and supported the principles of self-governance, liberty, and civic responsibility. He believed in the rights of the colonies to manage their own affairs free from monarchical interference.

How His Beliefs Changed Over Time

Initially cautious in political matters, Smith gradually embraced revolutionary principles as British policies became more oppressive. His experiences in the Continental Congress reinforced his commitment to independence and the establishment of a representative government.
