Early Life

Thomas Jefferson was born in 1743 in Shadwell, Virginia. Educated in classical languages, history, and Enlightenment philosophy, Jefferson developed a lifelong passion for science, architecture, and civic republicanism.

Political Career

Jefferson served as the principal author of the Declaration of Independence, Governor of Virginia, the first Secretary of State, Vice President under John Adams, and third President of the United States. His administration saw the Louisiana Purchase and the Lewis and Clark Expedition.

Political Philosophy
