Early Life & Background

Born in 1749 in Charleston, South Carolina, Rutledge was educated locally and studied law in England before returning to practice in Charleston. He came from a prominent family and was deeply involved in colonial politics.

Political Career

Rutledge served in South Carolina's colonial assembly and the Continental Congress, signing the Declaration of Independence at the age of 26. He later served as Governor of South Carolina from 1798 to 1800.

Political Philosophy

He supported American independence, Southern interests, and civic responsibility. He emphasized unity and lawfulness during the revolutionary era and the early Republic.

How His Beliefs Changed Over Time

Rutledge grew from a young colonial lawyer advocating for local rights to a national leader committed to independence and governance under the Constitution, reflecting the shift from resistance to nation-building.
