Early Life & Background

Thomas McKean was born in 1734 in New London, Pennsylvania. He studied law and became a prominent lawyer and politician, later representing Delaware in colonial and revolutionary affairs. His sharp legal mind and commitment to liberty made him a key figure in the American independence movement.

Political Beliefs & Philosophy

McKean was a committed advocate for independence, colonial rights, and republican governance. He supported strong state sovereignty but recognized the need for a federal structure. His political philosophy balanced liberty, legal order, and practical governance.

How His Beliefs Changed Over Time

Initially cautious regarding separation from Britain, McKean became an outspoken supporter of independence as tensions escalated. Over time, he focused on state leadership and governance, emphasizing legal frameworks and civic responsibility while maintaining support for national unity.
