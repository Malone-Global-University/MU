Early Life & Background

Richard Bassett was born in 1745 in Sussex County, Delaware. He studied law and became a respected lawyer, planter, and political leader in Delaware. His legal expertise and political experience helped secure his role as a key delegate during the founding era.

Political Beliefs & Philosophy

Bassett supported a strong national government under the Constitution while protecting the interests of his state. He believed in a balanced government that safeguarded both individual rights and effective governance.

How His Beliefs Changed Over Time

Bassett initially focused on local Delaware politics but increasingly engaged with national issues as the American independence movement advanced. He came to embrace the importance of a unified federal government for the stability of the new nation.
