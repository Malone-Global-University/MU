Early Life & Background

Born circa 1735 in England, Gwinnett moved to Georgia as a merchant and became involved in revolutionary politics.

Political Career

Gwinnett served in the Georgia Provincial Congress and was a delegate to the Continental Congress, signing the Declaration of Independence in 1776.

Political Philosophy

He supported colonial rights, independence, and the establishment of representative government in Georgia.

How His Beliefs Changed Over Time

Gwinnett grew from a colonial merchant to a committed revolutionary, embracing independence as tensions with Britain escalated.

Critical Events in His Career

  • 1775: Member, Georgia Provincial Congress
  • 1776: Signs Declaration of Independence
  • 1777: Lieutenant Governor of Georgia
  • 1777: Dies from duel wounds

Rivalries

Gwinnett clashed with fellow Georgia leaders over political control, ultimately leading to a fatal duel with Lachlan McIntosh.
