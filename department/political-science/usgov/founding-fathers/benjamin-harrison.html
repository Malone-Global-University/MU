Early Life & Background

Born in 1726 in Charles City County, Virginia, Harrison came from a prominent family of planters and politicians. He studied law and became active in colonial politics.

Political Career

Harrison served in the Virginia legislature and was a delegate to the Continental Congress. He signed the Declaration of Independence and contributed to early revolutionary governance.

Political Philosophy

Harrison advocated colonial rights and independence, emphasizing law, civic duty, and self-governance.
