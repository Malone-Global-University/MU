Early Life & Background

William Paterson was born in 1745 in County Antrim, Ireland, and emigrated to New Jersey as a child. Trained in law, he quickly became a prominent attorney and legislator, representing New Jersey in both colonial and national affairs.

Political Beliefs & Philosophy

Paterson supported a balanced federal system that protected smaller states while ensuring a strong national government. He emphasized the rule of law, equitable representation, and compromise as essential to a functioning republic.
