Early Life & Background

Born in 1713 in Llandaff, Wales, Lewis immigrated to New York as a young man, became a successful merchant, and actively supported the Patriot cause during the American Revolution. He endured personal losses, including the British occupation of his family home.

Political Career

Lewis served in the Continental Congress representing New York and signed the Declaration of Independence. He participated in debates over commerce, taxation, and national finance, reflecting his mercantile background.

Political Philosophy

Lewis supported independence, property rights, and commerce-driven prosperity. He valued civic responsibility and believed in a balanced government to protect individual and business interests while ensuring national security.

Lewis occasionally clashed with fellow New York delegates over representation and commercial policies, advocating strongly for merchant interests and equitable treatment of property owners.

Lewis is remembered as a steadfast supporter of independence and commerce in early America. His dedication during the Revolutionary War and sacrifices by his family exemplify the personal costs endured by many Founding Fathers.
