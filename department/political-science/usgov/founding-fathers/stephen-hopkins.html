Early Life & Background

Stephen Hopkins was born in 1707 in Providence, Rhode Island. A self-taught lawyer, he became involved in colonial politics early, serving in the Rhode Island General Assembly and becoming a respected voice in matters of governance and colonial rights.

Political Beliefs & Philosophy

Hopkins championed independence from Britain and advocated for colonial self-rule. He believed strongly in representative government and legal protections for citizens, combining a pragmatic approach to politics with Enlightenment principles of liberty.

How His Beliefs Changed Over Time

Initially cautious about breaking ties with Britain, Hopkins grew more assertive as colonial grievances mounted. His early legal and legislative experience informed his commitment to a balanced government that protected individual rights while ensuring effective governance.
