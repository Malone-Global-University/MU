Early Life & Background

Thomas Nelson Jr. was born in 1738 into a prominent Virginia family. Educated in law and plantation management, he became a dedicated supporter of colonial rights and an active leader in Virginia's revolutionary movement.

Nelson advocated for independence, self-governance, and strong local leadership. He believed in the importance of civic responsibility and the protection of liberty, balancing the interests of his state with the needs of the emerging nation.
