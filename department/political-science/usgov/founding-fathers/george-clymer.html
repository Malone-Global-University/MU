Early Life & Background

Born in 1739 in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, George Clymer became a merchant and civic leader. He was active in revolutionary politics and helped organize committees supporting independence and resistance to British policies.

Political Philosophy

He advocated for independence, civic engagement, and a strong but accountable government. Clymer supported measures to stabilize finances and maintain unity among the states.

Clymer initially focused on local colonial concerns but gradually shifted toward national priorities, supporting the Constitution and federal authority while maintaining a focus on commerce and civic responsibility.
