Early Life & Background

Samuel Huntington was born in 1731 in Scotland, Connecticut. He became a lawyer and prominent local leader before entering national politics during the American Revolution.

Political Beliefs & Philosophy

Huntington believed in strong legislative authority, civic responsibility, and the importance of a unified national government. He valued both state rights and effective national coordination.

How His Beliefs Changed Over Time

Initially focused on state-level governance, Huntington embraced the need for national cohesion during the Continental Congress and as a signer of the Articles of Confederation, emphasizing cooperation among states.
