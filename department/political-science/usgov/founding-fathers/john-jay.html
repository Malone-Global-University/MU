Early Life

John Jay was born in 1745 in New York City. He studied law under a prominent attorney and quickly rose as a talented lawyer, eventually engaging in revolutionary politics and diplomacy.

Political Career

Jay served as President of the Continental Congress, co-authored The Federalist Papers, and became the first Chief Justice of the United States. He also negotiated the Treaty of Paris (1783) ending the Revolutionary War and later served as Governor of New York.

Political Philosophy

A Federalist, Jay emphasized strong central government, rule of law, and international diplomacy. He valued negotiation and moderation and helped define the judicial branch in the new republic.
