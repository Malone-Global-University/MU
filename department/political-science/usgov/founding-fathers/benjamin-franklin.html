Franklin was a key diplomat to France, securing crucial support during the American Revolution. He helped draft the Declaration of Independence and the U.S. Constitution and served as a major advocate for colonial unity and independence.

Political Philosophy

Franklin believed in pragmatic government, diplomacy, and compromise. He valued civic responsibility, education, and public welfare, emphasizing the importance of reason and negotiation in political affairs.
