Early Life

Born in 1736 in Hanover County, Virginia, Patrick Henry became a prominent lawyer and ardent advocate for colonial rights. His fiery oratory made him a key voice against British rule.

Political Career

Henry served in the Virginia House of Burgesses, vocally opposed the Stamp Act, and became Governor of Virginia. He was a strong critic of centralized authority and a champion of individual liberties.

Political Philosophy

Patrick Henry believed in limited government, the primacy of individual rights, and the necessity of vigilance against tyranny. He famously argued that liberty should be defended at all costs.

Rivalries

Henry vs. Federalists: Opposed the Constitution without a bill of rights.
Henry vs. George Washington and Madison: Disagreed on the scope of federal authority and ratification strategies.
