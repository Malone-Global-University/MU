Early Life & Background

Jacob Broom was born in 1752 in Wilmington, Delaware. He trained as a businessman and became involved in civic affairs in Delaware, eventually earning a position as a delegate to the Constitutional Convention of 1787.

Political Beliefs & Philosophy

Broom supported a strong federal government capable of regulating commerce and maintaining national cohesion. He believed in compromise and practical governance rather than radical ideology, reflecting his moderate political stance during the Convention.
