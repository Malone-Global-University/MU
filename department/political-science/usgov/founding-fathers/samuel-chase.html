Early Life & Background

Samuel Chase was born in 1741 in Maryland. He trained as a lawyer and quickly became involved in colonial politics, advocating for the rights of Maryland colonists against British rule.

Political Beliefs & Philosophy

Chase strongly supported independence, emphasizing colonial self-governance and protection of individual rights. He valued civic duty and was outspoken against what he saw as tyranny from the British Crown.

How His Beliefs Changed Over Time

Initially working within colonial legal structures, Chase increasingly embraced revolutionary activism as British policies became more oppressive. He later integrated his commitment to independence with a judicial philosophy emphasizing the rule of law.

Rivalries

Chase was known for his confrontational style and frequent clashes with fellow politicians and later members of the Supreme Court. His outspoken criticisms led to an impeachment trial in 1804, though he was acquitted.
