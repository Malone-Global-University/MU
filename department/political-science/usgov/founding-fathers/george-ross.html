Early Life & Background

George Ross was born in 1730 in New Castle County, Delaware. He became a lawyer and influential political leader in Pennsylvania and Delaware. His legal and political skills positioned him as a key figure during the American Revolution.

Political Career

Ross served as a delegate to the Continental Congress and was a signer of the Declaration of Independence. He also held judicial positions in Pennsylvania and contributed to legal and constitutional development during the revolutionary period.

Political Philosophy

Ross emphasized rule of law, legal integrity, and measured political action. He supported independence but maintained careful attention to the legal structures necessary to sustain governance in the new nation.

How His Beliefs Changed Over Time

Initially cautious about revolutionary action, Ross gradually embraced independence as British policies became more restrictive. His perspective remained rooted in legal reasoning and the importance of institutional stability.
