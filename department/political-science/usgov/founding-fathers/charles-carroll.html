Early Life

Born in 1737 in Annapolis, Maryland, Charles Carroll was the only Catholic signer of the Declaration of Independence. He became a wealthy planter and a strong advocate for colonial rights and religious liberty.

Political Career

Carroll served in Maryland's legislature, was a delegate to the Continental Congress, and helped shape policies supporting independence and religious freedom. He promoted unity among diverse colonial interests.

Political Philosophy

Carroll championed religious tolerance, individual rights, and civic responsibility. He believed that government should protect the rights of all citizens and foster equality before the law.

Rivalries

Carroll vs. Anti-Catholic Sentiment: Advocated for inclusion and fought prejudice against Catholics.
Carroll vs. Loyalists: Opposed those loyal to the British crown and pushed for independence.

Legacy

Charles Carroll is remembered as a principled advocate for independence and religious liberty. He was the last surviving signer of the Declaration of Independence and a key figure in Maryland politics.
