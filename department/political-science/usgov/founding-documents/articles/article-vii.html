The Constitution became effective once nine of the thirteen states ratified it. Not all thirteen states were needed initially; nine states provided a legal majority to establish the government.

Allowed the Constitution to take effect quickly, while still encouraging eventual ratification by all states. Ensured a strong and functioning federal government from the outset.
