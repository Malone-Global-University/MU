Doughs (Baking)

Learning Objectives

  • Understand types of dough (lean, enriched, laminated).
  • Control gluten development and fermentation.
  • Shape and score basic loaves.

Core Concepts

  1. Flour protein and hydration ratios.
  2. Kneading vs. stretch-and-fold.
  3. Bulk fermentation and proofing.

Tools & Equipment

Stand mixer, dough scraper, proofing baskets, oven with steam, scales, thermometer.

Step-by-step Guided Lesson

  1. Measure ingredients precisely by weight.
  2. Mix to develop initial gluten; autolyse if applicable.
  3. Knead or perform stretch-and-folds according to recipe timing.
  4. Bulk ferment at target temperature; degas and shape.
  5. Final proof, score, and bake with appropriate steam/temperature.

Practice Assignments (Track 1)

  • Classic Bread — Track 1 recipes
  • Assignment: Bake two loaves using different hydration levels; submit photos and notes on proof times.

Assessment & Mastery Checklist

  • Can weigh and convert a recipe to baker's percentages.
  • Can judge dough hydration and tackiness by touch.
  • Can shape a boule and a bâtard with consistent crumb.

Batter Methods (Baking)

Objectives

  • Identify common batter methods (creaming, muffin, sponge, reverse creaming).
  • Choose the right method for desired texture.

Core Concepts

Air incorporation, emulsification, gluten minimization in cakes.

Guided Steps

  1. Creaming: Beat butter and sugar until light and aerated; add eggs slowly.
  2. Muffin: Mix wet ingredients then fold dry until just combined.
  3. Sponge: Whip eggs/sugar to ribbon stage; fold in flour carefully.

Practice

  • Track 1 recipe: Basic sponge cake
  • Assignment: Produce both a muffin-method loaf and a creamed-method loaf; compare crumb and volume.

Assessment

  • Can explain when to use each method.
  • Can produce repeatable crumb on two methods.
Pastry Skills — Module

Pastry Skills

Objectives

  • Make shortcrust, puff, and choux doughs reliably.

Core Concepts

Fat temperature, laminating techniques, steam leavening, gluten control.

Guided Lesson

  1. Shortcrust: Keep fat cold; minimal water; rest before rolling.
  2. Laminated: Build layers with consistent roll-and-folds; ensure proper chilling.
  3. Choux: Cook-stir mixture; incorporate eggs to proper viscosity; pipe and bake to set.

Practice

  • Track 1 recipe: Basic tart shell
  • Assignment: Produce 12 identical croissants (laminated) and a tart with clean edges.

Chocolate & Sugar

Objectives

  • Understand tempering, ganache ratios, and sugar stages.

Guided Steps

  1. Temper chocolate using seeding or tabling to target temps.
  2. Make ganache: determine chocolate:cream ratio based on use.
  3. Sugar: learn soft-ball, firm-ball, hard-crack stages by temperature and texture.

Practice & Assessment

  • Assignment: Temper chocolate and produce dipped truffles with glossy finish.
  • Achieve shiny snap on tempered sample.

Guided Steps
