4. Venting to Dry Out the Crust

In the second half of the bake, moisture must escape so the crust can crisp. Venting removes steam and allows the crust to harden.

5. Extending or Reducing Bake Time

To adjust crust thickness, modify the final stage of baking.

  • Thicker crust: longer bake at a slightly lower heat
  • Thinner crust: shorten the drying phase
  • Softer crust: cool under a towel to trap moisture

6. Cooling Procedures That Affect Crust Texture

Crust continues to evolve while cooling. Ventilation and humidity determine whether it stays crisp.

  • Cool on a wire rack for crisp crust
  • Wrap in a towel for softer, chewier crust
  • Do not slice until fully cooled to preserve crust structure
