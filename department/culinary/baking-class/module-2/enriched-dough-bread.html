Introduction to Enriched Doughs

Enriched doughs contain higher levels of sugar, fat, and eggs, resulting in tender, flavorful breads. These breads require careful handling for optimal texture.

Key Ingredients

  • Flour: All-purpose or bread flour for structure
  • Fat: Butter or oil for tenderness
  • Eggs: For richness, color, and structure
  • Sugar: Provides sweetness and fermentation fuel
  • Yeast: Instant, active dry, or sourdough starter
  • Milk or liquid: Enhances softness and browning

Mixing & Kneading

  • Incorporate fats and eggs gradually for even distribution
  • Knead to develop moderate gluten; avoid over-kneading
  • Dough should be smooth, elastic, and slightly tacky

Proofing & Fermentation

  • Allow for bulk fermentation until doubled in size
  • Perform gentle folds if necessary to maintain structure
  • Second proofing after shaping ensures light crumb and proper rise

Shaping & Baking

  • Shape dough according to desired style: braids, rolls, loaves
  • Brush with egg wash for color and shine
  • Bake at recommended temperature; monitor for proper browning
