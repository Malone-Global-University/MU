Overview

Proper timing is critical for scoring success. This lesson explains when to score relative to proof, cold retardation, and oven entry â€” and how timing interacts with steam to determine oven spring, ear formation, and final texture.

Learning objectives: Learn to evaluate proof readiness, choose the correct moment to score, handle cold-proofed dough, and coordinate scoring with steam and oven entry.

Proof checks â€” decide if the dough is ready

  1. Poke test: Gently press with an oiled finger ~1 cm into the dough:
    • Springs back immediately = underproofed (wait).
    • Springs back slowly and partially = ideal (score and bake).
    • Leaves an indentation or collapses = overproofed (handle carefully; shallow scoring or cold bake may help).
  2. Visual cues: Dough should look slightly rounded, with visible surface tension. Overproofed dough often looks loose and flat.
  3. Timing by recipe: Use recipe proof windows as guides â€” but always confirm with poke test because ambient conditions change proof speed.

When to score â€” practical rules

  • Score immediately before oven entry: Scoring too early allows the cut to seal and the edge to dry, reducing oven spring. Aim to score within 1â€“2 minutes of placing the loaf in the oven.
  • Cold-proof (retarded) dough: Score directly from the refrigerator for many styles â€” chilled dough is firmer and gives cleaner cuts. If using chilled loaves, preheat oven fully and transfer quickly to avoid warming the dough surface too much.
  • Room-temperature proofed dough: Score at bench and move immediately to the oven. If you must wait, keep covered and lightly floured; re-flour blade if it sticks.
  • Multiple loaves handling: Stagger scoring and baking so each loaf is scored just before it goes into the oven. Work in a production line: score â†’ place on peel â†’ transfer to oven.

Timing scoring with steam and oven conditions

  1. Steam window: Steam is most effective in the first 8â€“12 minutes of baking. Ensure your scoring is done immediately before oven entry so the steam interacts with a fresh cut.
  2. Preheated oven: Always score when oven is fully preheated. A cold oven reduces oven spring and can collapse loaves.
  3. Placement: Score with the intention of how steam will shape the ear â€” too much steam with a shallow cut may reduce ear lift; moderate steam with a low-angle, slightly deeper cut creates a crisp ear.

Scoring from cold proof (best practices)

  1. Keep loaves covered: When removing from fridge, keep loaves on parchment or transfer carefully â€” avoid sudden handling that collapses structure.
  2. Scoring technique: Use a very sharp blade and confident single motion. Because the dough is firm, you may need slightly less depth (3â€“8 mm depending on dough).
  3. Heat shock: For maximum ear, place chilled loaf into a very hot oven; the sudden heat and steam help create a strong ear. For delicate dough, allow 10â€“15 minutes of tempering at room temperature if pastry integrity is at risk.

Delays, rescues & troubleshooting

If you must delay after scoring

  • Lightly cover scored loaves with a clean damp towel or parchment to slow drying â€” this is a temporary fix and not ideal.
  • If the surface dries and the cut seals, re-flour the blade and re-score quickly and confidently before baking.

Overproofed loaves

  • Shallow, multiple small cuts can encourage controlled expansion without collapse.
  • Consider an immediate bake with reduced steam to avoid excessive oven spring that causes collapse; or very shallow scoring and a gentle bake if crumb tolerance is low.

Underproofed loaves

  • Underproofed dough may resist opening â€” avoid very deep cuts; instead allow a brief final bench rest (10â€“20 minutes) if time allows, then score and bake.

Step-by-step checklist

  1. Confirm proof with poke test and visual cues.
  2. Preheat oven fully and prepare steam (tray, steam injector, or dutch oven).
  3. If cold-proofed, move loaves from fridge onto peel briefly; keep covered until scoring.
  4. Score each loaf confidently within 1â€“2 minutes of oven entry.
  5. Place loaf into oven, add steam immediately (first 8â€“12 minutes critical).
  6. Observe oven spring and adjust timing/depth in subsequent bakes based on results.

Practice exercises & assessment

Exercises

  1. Timed sequence: proof 6 small batards and practice scoring and loading them into the oven with 30-second spacing â€” aim to score each loaf within 90 seconds of its oven entry.
  2. Cold vs. room: cold-proof 3 loaves and room-proof 3 loaves; score appropriately and compare ear, crust and crumb.
  3. Steam timing: bake identical loaves with steam applied immediately, delayed by 5 minutes, and no steam â€” observe differences.
