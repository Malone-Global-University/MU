Troubleshooting Shaping Collapse

Step-by-step instructions to prevent dough from collapsing during shaping and to improve loaf structure.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Shaping Collapse

Step 1: Assess Dough Strength

Check if the dough is too weak or overproofed. Weak gluten development or excess fermentation often causes collapse during shaping.

Step 2: Adjust Handling Technique

Handle the dough gently. Avoid excessive degassing and over-stretching. Use a light touch while forming loaves to preserve gas pockets.

Step 3: Proper Shaping

Create surface tension by folding and rolling techniques. Tighten the dough sufficiently to hold shape without compressing it completely.

Step 4: Monitor Proofing

Shaping collapse often occurs when dough is overproofed. Proof for the correct duration, using the finger-poke test to check readiness.

Step 5: Support During Baking

Use parchment, bannetons, or loaf pans to support delicate dough. Proper oven steam helps maintain structure in the early stages of baking.

Step 6: Evaluate Ingredients

Use high-protein bread flour and fresh yeast to enhance gluten strength. Weak flour or old yeast can contribute to collapse during shaping.

Step 7: Post-Bake Analysis

Observe loaf shape, crumb, and height. Make adjustments to shaping technique, proofing, or flour choice in future batches to prevent collapse.
