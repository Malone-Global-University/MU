Retardation (Cold Fermentation)

Understand how cold fermentation slows yeast activity, allowing dough to develop deeper flavors, better texture, and improved handling characteristics.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Retardation

What is Retardation?

Retardation, also known as cold fermentation, is the practice of slowing down yeast activity by placing dough in a cool environment, usually a refrigerator. This technique enhances flavor, improves crumb structure, and allows flexible baking schedules.

Benefits of Cold Fermentation

  • Develops more complex, robust flavors
  • Strengthens dough structure
  • Allows longer bulk fermentation for artisan breads
  • Gives flexibility in baking times

Tips for Retardation

  • Keep dough tightly covered to prevent drying
  • Allow dough to warm slightly before shaping
  • Adjust proofing times when returning from cold storage
  • Use retardation to schedule baking for optimal freshness
