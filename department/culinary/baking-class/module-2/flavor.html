Introduction to Flavor Evaluation

Flavor is the combined perception of taste and aroma. Evaluating flavor helps determine ingredient balance, fermentation quality, and overall bread appeal.

Key Flavor Components

  • Sweetness from sugars and malt
  • Acidity from fermentation
  • Salt balance
  • Complexity and depth of taste
  • Aftertaste and lingering flavors

Step-by-Step Evaluation

  1. Taste a small piece of bread at room temperature
  2. Chew slowly to release all flavor compounds
  3. Identify primary tastes: sweet, sour, salty, and umami
  4. Evaluate balance, intensity, and complexity
  5. Note aftertaste and lingering impressions
  6. Document findings for comparison with other sensory attributes
