Troubleshooting Overproofed Dough

Step-by-step instructions to prevent dough collapse and improve bread structure after overproofing.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Overproofing

Step 1: Identify Overproofing

Look for dough that has expanded excessively, feels weak, and shows surface bubbles. Overproofed dough collapses easily when touched.

Step 2: Adjust Proofing Time

Shorten proofing for future batches. Keep an eye on environmental temperature and dough behavior to prevent overproofing.

Step 3: Handle Gently

Shape overproofed dough carefully to avoid degassing. Gently tighten the loaf while preserving some gas pockets.

Step 4: Bake Immediately

If dough is slightly overproofed, bake immediately instead of letting it sit longer. This reduces risk of collapse.

Step 5: Use Oven Steam

Introduce steam during the first 10â€“15 minutes of baking to maintain crust elasticity and allow slight expansion.

Step 6: Check Ingredients

Use strong bread flour and fresh yeast. This helps dough tolerate proofing better and maintain structure during baking.

Step 7: Post-Bake Assessment

Examine crumb, texture, and loaf height. Adjust proofing, shaping, or baking conditions for next batch to prevent overproofing.
