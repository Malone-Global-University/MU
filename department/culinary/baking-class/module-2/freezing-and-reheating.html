Freezing and Reheating

Step-by-step guidance on freezing baked goods and reheating them properly to preserve taste and texture.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Freezing and Reheating

Introduction

Freezing baked goods is an effective way to extend shelf life. Proper reheating ensures they retain freshness, flavor, and texture.

Freezing Baked Goods

  • Cool completely before freezing to prevent condensation.
  • Wrap tightly in plastic wrap, foil, or freezer-safe bags.
  • Label items with date and contents.
  • Store in single layers or separate with parchment paper to avoid sticking.

Thawing Tips

  • Thaw at room temperature for most breads and pastries.
  • Keep wrapped during thawing to retain moisture.
  • Small items like rolls or muffins can be reheated directly from frozen.
