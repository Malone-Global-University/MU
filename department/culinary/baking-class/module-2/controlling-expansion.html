Scoring Patterns

Choose the right scoring pattern for shape, structure and oven spring. Detailed, step-by-step examples and practice drills for bakers.

Overview

Scoring (slashing) controls where and how a loaf expands in the oven. This lesson explains common scoring patterns, the mechanical reasoning behind them, recommended depth/angle, and when to use each pattern to achieve desired oven spring and aesthetics.

Learning objectives: Identify appropriate patterns for common loaf shapes, execute consistent cuts, and predict expansion behavior based on pattern, depth and dough strength.

  • Dough strength: high-gluten dough tolerates deeper, more decorative cuts; tender dough needs shallow, controlled cuts.
  • Proof level: underproofed dough may resist opening; overproofed dough may collapse â€” adjust depth accordingly.
  • Desired ear: shallow low-angle cuts create pronounced ears; straight down cuts give more blunt openings.
  • Steam & oven conditions: good steam encourages nicer ears and cleaner openings.

Common issues & fixes

  • Ragged cuts / tearing: Replace blade; use a single confident stroke; reduce depth/angle.
  • Cut seals immediately: Work faster and score just before oven entry; keep surface lightly floured; use steam.
  • No ear / dull expansion: Angle too steep; try lower angle and deeper cut if dough strength allows.
  • Uneven openings: Inconsistent depth/angle â€” practice on proofed scraps to build muscle memory.
