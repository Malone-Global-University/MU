Humidity Control During Proofing

Learn how proper humidity keeps your dough moist, prevents crusting, and ensures optimal final rise.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Humidity Control

Why Humidity Matters

Proper humidity prevents the dough from forming a dry skin, which can restrict expansion and result in dense bread.

Methods to Control Humidity

  • Cover the dough with a damp cloth or plastic wrap.
  • Use a proofing box or enclosed container with a small water tray.
  • Misting lightly with water to maintain moisture on the surface.

Monitoring During Proofing

Check the dough periodically. If the surface begins to dry or form cracks, increase humidity immediately to avoid affecting rise and texture.
