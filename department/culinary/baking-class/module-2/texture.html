Texture Analysis

Step-by-step guide to evaluating bread texture, including chewiness, softness, crumb resilience, and mouthfeel for professional sensory assessment.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Texture Analysis

Introduction to Texture Evaluation

Texture refers to how bread feels in the mouth, including chewiness, softness, and resilience. Proper texture indicates correct fermentation, baking, and ingredient balance.

Key Texture Attributes

  • Softness and crumb tenderness
  • Chewiness and elasticity
  • Crumb resilience and spring
  • Moisture content and dryness
  • Mouthfeel and overall satisfaction

Step-by-Step Evaluation

  1. Slice a portion of bread at room temperature
  2. Press gently to assess crumb resilience
  3. Chew slowly to evaluate softness, chewiness, and moisture
  4. Observe how the bread breaks and crumbles
  5. Note the mouthfeel and tactile sensations
  6. Document observations for comparison with other sensory skills
