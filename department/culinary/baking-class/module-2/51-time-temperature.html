Understanding Dough Fermentation

Dough fermentation is highly sensitive to temperature. Warmer temperatures accelerate yeast activity, while cooler temperatures slow it down, affecting the timing of your final rise.

Adjusting Proofing Time

  • Light doughs may need shorter proofing times.
  • Denser or whole-grain doughs often require longer proofing to fully expand.
  • Monitor dough visually and with the finger-poke test rather than relying solely on the clock.
