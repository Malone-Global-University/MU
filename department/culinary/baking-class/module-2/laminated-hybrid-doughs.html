Master laminated and hybrid doughs such as croissants and puff pastry by learning proper lamination, folding, and baking techniques for flaky, layered breads.

Introduction to Laminated & Hybrid Doughs

Laminated doughs involve folding butter into dough multiple times to create layers that puff during baking. Hybrid doughs combine laminated techniques with enriched dough for flavor and texture.

Key Ingredients

  • Flour: Bread flour for strength and elasticity
  • Butter: Cold, high-fat butter for lamination
  • Yeast: Instant or active dry for fermentation
  • Milk or water: Hydration for dough consistency
  • Sugar & Salt: For flavor balance
  • Eggs (for hybrid doughs): Adds richness and color

Mixing & Dough Preparation

  • Mix ingredients to form a smooth, elastic dough
  • Chill dough before lamination for easier handling
  • Prepare butter block to match dough temperature

Lamination & Folding

  • Enclose butter in dough and perform a series of folds (turns)
  • Chill between folds to maintain layer integrity
  • Repeat until desired number of layers is achieved

Shaping & Baking

  • Roll dough to final thickness and shape croissants, danishes, or hybrid pastries
  • Proof until slightly puffy before baking
  • Bake at high temperature for steam and proper lift
