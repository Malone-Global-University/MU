Sourdough Starter Maintenance

Step-by-step instructions to keep your sourdough starter healthy and active for consistent baking results.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Sourdough Starter Maintenance

Introduction

A sourdough starter is a living culture of flour and water containing wild yeast and beneficial bacteria. Maintaining it correctly ensures reliable fermentation and flavor development in breads.

Feeding Schedule

  • Feed daily at room temperature if using frequently.
  • For infrequent baking, store in the refrigerator and feed once per week.
  • Use equal parts flour and water by weight for standard hydration (100%).

Hydration and Consistency

  • Adjust water content to maintain a thick but stirrable consistency.
  • Stiffer starters ferment slower and develop more acetic flavor.
  • More liquid starters ferment faster and produce milder flavor.

  • Slow activity can be revived with more frequent feedings at slightly warmer temperature.

Tips for Success

  • Use non-chlorinated water for best microbial growth.
  • Keep starter in a loosely covered container to allow gas release.
  • Record feeding times and ratios to maintain consistency.
