Overview

Relying on internal temperature is the most consistent way to determine doneness across different ovens and recipes. This lesson covers instruments, target temperatures for common products, probe technique, and how to interpret readings to make baking decisions.

Learning objectives: Choose and use the correct thermometer, probe correctly, know target temperatures (Â°F & Â°C), and troubleshoot inconsistent readings.

Tools & thermometer types

  • Instant-read digital thermometer: Fast, accurate for quick checks (recommended for most kitchens).
  • Leave-in probe thermometer: Useful for long bakes when connected to oven display or remote reader.
  • Thermocouple / thermistor probes: High-accuracy probes used in professional settings.
  • Calibration kit: Ice bath and boiling point checks to verify accuracy periodically.

Correct probe placement & technique

  1. Insert to the center: For loaves, insert the probe into the geometric center (both longitudinally and vertically) without touching the bottom pan, stone, or any large air pocket.
  2. Angle & depth: For wider loaves insert from the side pointing toward center; for tall loaves insert from the top. Depth should place the tip at the center mass â€” typically two-thirds into the loaf for large shapes.
  3. Quick checks vs leave-in: Use instant-read for quick checks (insert, wait 2â€“5 seconds for thermocouple, 5â€“10 seconds for cheaper probes). Use leave-in probes for long bakes to monitor rise over time.
  4. Multiple checks: For very large loaves or mixed loads, check more than one loaf or spot to ensure uniformity.
