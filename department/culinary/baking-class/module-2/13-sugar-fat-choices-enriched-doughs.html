Sugar & Fat Choices for Enriched Doughs

Explore how different types of sugar and fat influence enriched doughs, impacting texture, flavor, fermentation, and finished bread quality.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Sugar & Fat Choices for Enriched Doughs

Role of Sugar in Enriched Doughs

Sugar feeds yeast for fermentation, adds sweetness, tenderizes crumb, and promotes browning. Different sugars (white, brown, honey) alter flavor and moisture retention.

Role of Fat in Enriched Doughs

Fats like butter, oil, or shortening improve texture, flavor, and shelf life. They weaken gluten for a softer crumb and add richness, as seen in brioche and challah.

Balancing Sugar and Fat

Proper ratios ensure dough rises properly and achieves desired texture. Too much sugar slows yeast; too much fat can inhibit gluten development. Experimentation is key for mastery.
