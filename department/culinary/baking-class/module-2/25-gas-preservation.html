Gas Preservation in Dough

Discover techniques for preserving gas in dough to maintain structure, improve crumb texture, and ensure high-quality bread during baking.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Gas Preservation

Understanding Gas Preservation

During fermentation, yeast produces carbon dioxide that creates bubbles in dough. Proper handling preserves these gas bubbles, ensuring the bread rises evenly and has a light, airy texture.

Techniques to Preserve Gas

  • Handle dough gently to avoid deflating air pockets
  • Minimize excessive folding or degassing
  • Use proper shaping methods to retain gas
  • Control proofing times to prevent over-proofing

Benefits of Gas Preservation

  • Improved crumb structure and even texture
  • Enhanced volume and rise of bread
  • Better flavor development due to optimal fermentation
  • Professional-quality appearance and consistency
