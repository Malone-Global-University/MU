Troubleshooting Poor Oven Spring

Learn how to identify and fix factors that prevent bread from rising properly in the oven.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Poor Oven Spring

Step 1: Check Proofing

Ensure dough is properly proofed. Underproofed dough may not expand fully in the oven, while overproofed dough may collapse. Use the finger-poke test to check readiness.

Step 2: Evaluate Shaping Technique

Proper shaping creates surface tension that supports oven spring. Avoid loose shaping; gently tighten the loaf without degassing excessively.

Step 3: Optimize Oven Temperature

Preheat the oven fully before baking. Oven that is too cool or uneven can inhibit expansion. Use an oven thermometer to verify correct baking temperature.

Step 4: Use Steam

Steam in the first 10-15 minutes of baking helps the crust remain elastic, allowing maximum expansion. Use a pan of water or spritz the oven walls to generate steam.

Step 5: Check Dough Hydration

Dough that is too dry or stiff will resist expansion. Adjust hydration carefully according to flour absorption to maintain a soft, elastic dough.

Step 6: Evaluate Flour and Yeast

Use strong bread flour for better gluten structure. Ensure yeast is active and not expired. Weak flour or old yeast can reduce oven spring.

Step 7: Post-Bake Analysis

After cooling, assess crumb and height. Adjust proofing, shaping, steam, or oven temperature for next bake to improve oven spring.
