Home Department Culinary Baking Class Lame Handling

Overview

This lesson covers the fundamentals of lame handling and scoring (also called slashing) so students can shape intentional expansion on proofed dough. Mastery of these skills improves oven spring, crumb structure and final loaf appearance.

  • Learning objectives: Proper lame grip and safety; common scoring patterns; controlling expansion; scoring timing relative to proof and oven entry.
  • Prerequisites: Dough properly mixed, bulk fermented, shaped and proofed to the appropriate stage (see proofing lesson).

Tools & Safety

Required equipment

  • Lame (single-use blade holder or replaceable blade holder)
  • Fresh razor or #11 blade (sharp blades give cleaner cuts)
  • Bench scraper
  • Proofing basket (banneton) or couche as appropriate
  • Flour for dusting

Safety

Always point the blade away from your body. Replace blades when nicked. Keep fingers clear of the blade path. Use a blade cover when walking across the kitchen.

Step-by-step: Lame Handling & Scoring

1. Prepare the dough (final proof stage)

  1. Confirm proof level: the dough should be airy but still hold shape; perform a gentle poke test â€” if the indentation springs back slowly and partially, it's ready to bake.
  2. Transfer the dough to the peel or baking surface seam-side down (if required by your bake).
  3. Lightly dust the surface of the dough and your lame so the blade does not stick to the dough.

Execution tips & troubleshooting

  • Cut seals immediately: If cut closes, re-flour the blade and re-score quickly but confidently.

Practice exercises & assessment

Exercises

  1. Practice three basic patterns on proofed dough rounds: single central slash, cross, three parallel slashes. Observe oven spring and ear definition.
  2. Vary angle/depth while keeping other variables constant to see direct effects on expansion.
  3. Timed drill: score and bake 5 loaves in sequence to practice speed, safety and consistency under pressure.
