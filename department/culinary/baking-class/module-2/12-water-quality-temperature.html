Water Quality & Temperature

Understand how water characteristics and temperature affect yeast activity, dough fermentation, and the final bread texture for professional baking success.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Water Quality & Temperature

Impact of Water Quality on Dough

Water minerals, pH, and purity affect gluten development, yeast activity, and dough elasticity. Soft water produces lighter crumb, while hard water can strengthen dough but may slow fermentation.

Optimal Water Temperature

Dough temperature influences yeast activity and fermentation speed. Warm water accelerates yeast, while cold water slows fermentation. Targeting proper dough temperature ensures consistent rise and texture.

Practical Tips

Measure water temperature carefully. Adjust recipe for water hardness or chlorine content. Use filtered water when possible for predictable dough performance and superior baked goods.
