Introduction to Crumb Structure

Crumb structure defines the interior quality of bread. Evaluating it helps bakers understand fermentation, mixing, and baking performance.

Key Factors to Evaluate

  • Air pockets: Size, distribution, and uniformity
  • Moisture content: Indicates proper hydration
  • Elasticity: Springiness when pressed
  • Density: Light vs. heavy crumb
  • Consistency: Even texture throughout the loaf

Step-by-Step Evaluation

  1. Slice the bread carefully using a serrated knife
  2. Observe air pockets and note any irregularities
  3. Press lightly to assess spring and moisture retention
  4. Smell the crumb to detect freshness and fermentation notes
  5. Compare against standard expectations for the bread type
