Troubleshooting Fermentation Problems

Learn step-by-step how to correct fermentation issues and achieve optimal dough rise and texture.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Fermentation Problems

Step 1: Check Yeast Quality

Ensure your yeast is active and not expired. Use a yeast proofing test if unsure: dissolve in warm water with a pinch of sugar and wait for bubbles.

Step 2: Monitor Dough Temperature

Dough that is too cold or too hot can prevent proper fermentation. Aim for an optimal dough temperature of 24â€“27Â°C (75â€“80Â°F).

Step 3: Adjust Hydration

Too dry dough slows fermentation. Ensure correct hydration according to the recipe, adjusting gradually for humidity or flour variations.

Step 4: Allow Adequate Time

Under-proofing results in dense bread. Allow dough to rise until it roughly doubles in size. Over-proofing can collapse gluten structure.

Step 5: Consider Dough Handling

Rough handling or excessive punching can degas dough prematurely. Fold gently during bulk fermentation to preserve air pockets.

Step 6: Control Environmental Conditions

Humidity and drafts affect fermentation. Keep dough covered and in a stable environment to ensure consistent rise.

Step 7: Observe Dough Development

Use visual and tactile cues: smooth, elastic dough with small air bubbles is ready for shaping. Adjust your process based on observation.
