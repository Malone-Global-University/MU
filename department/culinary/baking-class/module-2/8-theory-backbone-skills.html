1. Gluten Formation Theory

Gluten forms when water hydrates the proteins glutenin and gliadin, allowing them to bond into elastic networks. Kneading strengthens these networks by aligning proteins and creating structure.

2. Hydration Ratio Analysis

3. Dough vs. Batter Theory

Doughs have stronger gluten matrices, while batters rely more on chemical leavening and contain higher liquid ratios. Understanding this divide helps predict texture, rise, and handling properties.

4. Salt, Sugar, and Fat Behavior

Salt tightens gluten and slows yeast; sugar increases browning and yeast activity; fats tenderize and shorten dough structure. Each ingredient modifies dough chemistry in predictable ways.
