Proofing with Bannetons

Master the final rise of your dough with bannetons, proper humidity, time-temperature adjustments, and the finger-poke test to avoid over-proofing.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Bannetons

Bannetons

Bannetons, also known as proofing baskets, help shape the dough and provide structure during the final rise. They prevent spreading and create beautiful patterns on your bread crust.

Humidity Control

Maintaining the right humidity prevents the dough from drying out during proofing. Use a covered container or mist lightly to keep moisture levels optimal.

Time & Temperature Adjustments

Adjust proofing times and temperatures based on dough type, room temperature, and desired bread texture. Cooler temperatures slow fermentation for better flavor development.

Finger-Poke Test

Gently poke the dough with your finger to check readiness. If the indentation springs back slowly, the dough is properly proofed. If it springs back quickly, it needs more time; if it doesn't spring back at all, it may be over-proofed.

Avoiding Over-Proofing

Over-proofing can collapse the dough structure. Monitor proofing closely and remove dough from the banneton when it has just reached its maximum expansion.
