Hydration Ratios in Bread Baking

Learn how to calculate hydration, understand dough texture, and adjust techniques for various bread types.

1. Definition of Hydration

Hydration is the percentage of water relative to flour weight in a dough. Using baker's percentages, flour is always 100%, and other ingredients are calculated as percentages of flour.

4. Why Hydration Matters

Higher hydration gives open crumb, longer fermentation, and requires gentle folding. Lower hydration yields tighter crumb, shorter fermentation, and is easier to shape.

8. Tips

  • Hydration affects technique, not correctness.
