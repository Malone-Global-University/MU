Understanding Fermentation Temperature

Dough temperature affects yeast activity, fermentation speed, and flavor development. Warm fermentation accelerates activity, while cool fermentation slows it down, allowing complex flavors to develop.

Warm Fermentation

  • Faster rise, often completed in hours
  • Produces milder flavors
  • Best for quick bread production
  • Requires close monitoring to avoid overproofing

Cool Fermentation

  • Slower rise, often overnight
  • Develops deeper, more complex flavors
  • Improves dough strength and handling
  • Common in artisan bread baking
