Recognizing Windowpane

Master the windowpane test to determine gluten development in your dough. Achieve perfectly elastic and well-kneaded bread every time.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Recognizing Windowpane

What is the Windowpane Test?

The windowpane test checks gluten development in dough. Stretch a small piece of dough; it should form a thin, translucent membrane without tearing.

Why it Matters

  • Ensures proper gluten network formation
  • Predicts dough elasticity and gas retention
  • Critical for artisan bread quality

How to Perform the Test

  1. Pinch a small piece of dough between your fingers.
  2. Gently stretch it into a thin sheet.
  3. If it stretches without tearing and forms a translucent membrane, gluten is properly developed.
  4. If it tears easily, continue kneading and test again.

Next Steps

Once the dough passes the windowpane test, proceed with bulk fermentation and shaping for your bread recipe.
