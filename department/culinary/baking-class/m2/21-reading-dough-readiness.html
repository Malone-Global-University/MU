Reading Dough Readiness

Understand how to determine when dough is properly fermented and ready for shaping, ensuring optimal texture and flavor in your bread.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Reading Dough Readiness

Overview

Reading dough readiness is a crucial skill in baking. It involves observing the dough's elasticity, volume, surface, and gas retention to determine if it is properly fermented.

Signs of Proper Fermentation

  • Dough has expanded to the expected volume
  • Surface is smooth with slight doming
  • Dough springs back slowly when gently pressed
  • Gas pockets are evenly distributed

Techniques for Assessment

  • Finger poke test to check elasticity
  • Visual inspection for uniformity
  • Smell test to assess fermentation aroma
