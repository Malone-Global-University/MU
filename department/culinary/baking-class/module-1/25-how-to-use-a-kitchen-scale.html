How to Use a Kitchen Scale

Learn to measure ingredients accurately for consistent and professional baking results.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Module 1 How to Use a Kitchen Scale

Introduction

Using a kitchen scale ensures precise measurement of ingredients, which is essential for reproducible and successful baking. This lesson teaches how to properly use digital and mechanical scales for both dry and liquid ingredients.

Objectives

  • Understand why accurate measurement improves baking consistency.
  • Learn how to properly zero (tare) a scale.
  • Measure dry and liquid ingredients accurately using a scale.
  • Convert volume-based recipes to weight-based measurements for precision.

Step-by-Step Instructions: Using a Kitchen Scale

Step 1: Prepare the Scale

Place the scale on a flat, stable surface. Turn it on and ensure it reads zero before use.

Step 2: Tare the Container

Place an empty bowl or container on the scale. Press the "tare" or "zero" button to subtract the container weight.

Step 3: Measure Dry Ingredients

Add ingredients slowly until the desired weight is reached. Avoid spilling and always check that the scale is still reading accurately.

Step 4: Measure Liquid Ingredients

Use a container suitable for liquids. Tare the container first, then pour liquid gradually until the desired weight is reached.

Step 5: Converting Recipes

When converting recipes from volume to weight, refer to ingredient-specific conversion charts for accurate results.

  • Use consistent units (grams or ounces) throughout the recipe.
  • Weighing ingredients reduces errors caused by packing or settling.
  • Keep the scale clean, especially after measuring sticky ingredients.
