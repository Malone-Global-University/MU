Culinary Science Basics

This lesson introduces the science behind baking and cooking, including ingredient behavior, chemical reactions, and techniques every culinary student should understand.

Objectives

  • Understand how ingredients interact during cooking and baking.
  • Recognize chemical and physical changes in common culinary processes.
  • Learn basic kitchen techniques through the lens of food science.
  • Develop awareness of how measurements, temperature, and timing affect outcomes.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Identify the main ingredients and their roles (proteins, starches, fats, liquids).
  2. Observe how heat affects these ingredients in baking versus stovetop cooking.
  3. Practice measuring ingredients precisely for controlled results.
  4. Record and analyze changes in texture, color, and flavor during cooking/baking.
  5. Experiment with basic recipes to see science principles in action.

Notes & Tips

  • Temperature and timing are crucial; small changes can affect texture and flavor.
  • Understanding ingredient chemistry allows you to troubleshoot recipes more effectively.
  • Always read recipes fully before starting to ensure accurate preparation.
  • Keep notes on experimental adjustments to learn from successes and mistakes.
