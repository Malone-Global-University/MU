Definition of Baking

Baking is the process of using dry, consistent heatâ€”usually in an ovenâ€”to transform raw ingredients into finished foods. Heat causes physical and chemical changes such as protein coagulation, starch gelatinization, evaporation, browning reactions, and fat melting.

Why Baking Works

The goal of baking is to create a stable structure. Ingredients like flour, eggs, fat, and leavening agents work together to produce a network that sets as heat is applied. Once set, the product can hold its shape even after cooling.

The Role of Controlled Heat

Unlike stovetop cooking, baking relies on indirect heat that surrounds the food. This creates even cooking conditions and predictable results. Proper temperature control ensures correct rise, texture, color, and moisture retention.

Common Foods That Are Baked

Bread, pastries, cookies, cakes, pies, biscuits, crackers, custards, and many savory dishes all rely on baking principles.
