What Baking Is

Learn the fundamentals of baking, how it differs from cooking, and why precise methods matter.

Objectives

  • Understand the definition of baking.
  • Recognize the key differences between baking and cooking.
  • Identify common baking ingredients and their roles.
  • Gain awareness of the importance of precision in measurements and timing.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Read about the history and evolution of baking to understand its cultural significance.
  2. Learn the main types of baked goods: bread, pastries, cakes, and cookies.
  3. Identify the role of ingredients:
    • Flour: structure
    • Sugar: sweetness and browning
    • Leavening agents: rising
    • Fats: texture and flavor
    • Liquids: moisture and cohesion
  4. Compare baking with cooking:
    • Baking uses dry heat, often in an oven, while cooking can involve various methods like boiling or frying.
    • Baking requires precise measurements and timing, whereas cooking is often more flexible.
  5. Review basic baking terminology, such as kneading, proofing, creaming, and folding.
  6. Practice reading and interpreting simple baking recipes to recognize measurement conventions and techniques.

  • Always measure ingredients accurately; baking is science-based.
