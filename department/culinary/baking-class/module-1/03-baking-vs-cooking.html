Baking vs Cooking

This lesson explains the core differences between baking and cooking, helping students understand why baking requires greater accuracy and ingredient balance than many stovetop methods.

Objectives

  • Understand what makes baking a precise science.
  • Identify the differences between dry heat (baking) and moist or direct heat (cooking).
  • Recognize why ingredient ratios matter more in baking.
  • Learn how heat transfer influences food texture and structure.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Review how baking relies on controlled oven temperatures.
  2. Compare stovetop cooking methods (boiling, sautÃ©ing, frying) with oven-based methods.
  3. Study examples of baking reactions: gluten formation, caramelization, and leavening.
  4. Examine how improvisation affects cooking differently than baking.
  5. Practice identifying which foods require strict measurements versus flexible seasoning.

Notes & Tips

  • Baking is fundamentally chemistry; small changes can dramatically alter results.
  • Cooking allows more improvisationâ€”seasoning, timing, and technique are flexible.
  • Always preheat your oven; baking reactions depend on hitting temperature targets.
  • Baking rewards precision; cooking rewards creativity.
