Kitchen Safety Basics

Learn essential safety practices to prevent injuries and maintain a safe, efficient kitchen environment.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Module 1 Kitchen Safety Basics

Introduction

This lesson covers fundamental kitchen safety practices, from proper handling of knives and hot surfaces to preventing slips, burns, and contamination.

Objectives

  • Recognize common kitchen hazards and how to avoid them.
  • Learn safe knife handling and cutting techniques.
  • Understand proper handling of hot equipment, ovens, and stovetops.
  • Practice cleanliness and hygiene to prevent foodborne illnesses.

Step-by-Step Safety Guidelines

  1. Always wash hands thoroughly before cooking.
  2. Keep workstations clean and free from clutter.
  3. Use knives with proper technique and cut away from your body.
  4. Handle hot pans and pots with oven mitts or heat-resistant gloves.
  5. Clean spills immediately to prevent slips and falls.
  6. Store chemicals and cleaning agents away from food prep areas.
  7. Follow fire safety protocols and know the location of fire extinguishers.

Notes & Tips

  • Always remain alert and focused while cooking.
  • Do not leave cooking equipment unattended.
  • Label and store all ingredients properly to avoid cross-contamination.
  • Keep first aid supplies accessible in the kitchen.
