Simple Cakes, Brownies & Bars

Techniques and tips for creating simple cakes, brownies, and bars with ideal texture and flavor.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Module 1 Simple Cakes, Brownies & Bars

Introduction

This lesson covers simple, versatile baked goods like cakes, brownies, and bars. Students will learn mixing methods, ingredient ratios, baking times, and techniques to produce consistent texture and flavor. Mastering these basics provides a foundation for more complex desserts.

Objectives

  • Understand the differences between simple cakes, brownies, and bars.
  • Follow proper mixing and baking methods for each type.
  • Achieve ideal texture: tender cakes, fudgy brownies, or chewy bars.
  • Identify common mistakes and learn corrective tips.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Ingredients and Pans

Measure ingredients accurately. Preheat the oven to the recipe temperature. Grease and line pans with parchment paper if necessary.

Step 2: Mix Batter

Cakes: Use the creaming or all-in-one method as indicated. Brownies: Melted butter or oil can be combined with sugar before adding eggs. Bars: Mix dry and wet ingredients until just combined to prevent toughness.

Step 3: Add Flavorings & Mix-ins

Fold in chocolate chips, nuts, or extracts carefully to maintain texture.

Step 4: Transfer to Pans

Pour batter evenly into prepared pans, smoothing the top if necessary.

Step 5: Bake

Follow recommended times and temperatures. Cakes generally bake 25â€“35 minutes, brownies 20â€“30 minutes, bars 20â€“25 minutes. Test doneness with a toothpick or cake tester.

Step 6: Cool

Allow baked goods to cool in the pan for 10â€“15 minutes, then transfer to a wire rack. Cooling improves texture and prevents sogginess.

Step 7: Slice and Serve

Use a sharp knife for clean edges, especially for bars and brownies. Serve at room temperature.

Notes & Tips

  • Do not overmix; overdeveloped gluten makes baked goods dense.
  • Check oven temperature with an oven thermometer to ensure accurate baking.
  • For fudgy brownies, reduce flour slightly; for cakier brownies, increase flour or eggs.
  • Line pans with parchment for easy removal and clean edges.
  • Cool completely before slicing to maintain structure, especially for bars.
