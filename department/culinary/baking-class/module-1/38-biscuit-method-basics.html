Biscuit Method Basics

Learn the biscuit mixing method for tender, flaky layers and uniform rise.

Home Department Culinary Baking Class Module 1 Biscuit Method Basics

Introduction

The biscuit method is a mixing technique for producing tender, flaky quick breads. By cutting cold fat into flour before adding liquids, layers are formed that create a light, airy texture. Students will learn the proper method, fat handling, and dough shaping techniques to achieve consistent results.

Objectives

  • Understand the steps of the biscuit mixing method.
  • Apply proper cutting and folding techniques to create flakiness.
  • Recognize the effects of ingredient temperature and ratios on texture.
  • Identify common mistakes, such as overworking the dough, and their impact.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Prepare Ingredients

Measure flour, leavening agents, salt, sugar, cold butter or shortening, and liquids (milk, buttermilk, or cream).

Step 2: Combine Dry Ingredients

Whisk together flour, baking powder/soda, salt, and sugar.

Step 3: Cut in Fat

Using a pastry cutter, forks, or fingers, cut cold butter or shortening into the dry mixture until pea-sized pieces remain. This creates flakiness.

Step 4: Add Liquid

Pour in cold liquid gradually, mixing gently with a spatula until just combined. The dough should be slightly sticky and lumpy.

Step 5: Shape and Cut

Turn the dough onto a lightly floured surface, gently pat to 1-inch thickness. Cut biscuits using a round cutter without twisting to preserve layers.

Step 6: Bake

Place biscuits on a baking sheet, optionally brush with milk or butter, and bake at 425Â°F (220Â°C) for 12â€“15 minutes until golden brown.

Step 7: Cool and Serve

Allow biscuits to cool briefly on a wire rack. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Notes & Tips

  • Keep all fat and liquid ingredients cold to maintain flakiness.
  • Do not overmix; this develops gluten and reduces tenderness.
  • Use minimal handling when shaping and cutting to preserve layers.
  • For added flavor, brush tops with melted butter or add cheese/herbs.
