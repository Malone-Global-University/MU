Prep Skills & Foundations Modules

  • Module 16: Mise en Place & Workflow Optimization

    This module reinforces disciplined workstation setup, sequencing logic, and production readiness. Students learn standardized prep maps, station zoning, labeling systems, and time allocation models used in professional kitchens. Emphasis is placed on throughput efficiency, reduction of delays, and developing intuitive operational flow to support both Ã  la carte and batch service environments.

  • Module 17: Ingredient Knowledge & Selection

    A structured review of raw ingredient categories including produce, proteins, dairy, grains, spices, and specialty goods. The module covers sourcing criteria, freshness indicators, quality grading, storage protocols, and vendor management. Learners gain the ability to evaluate ingredients from a cost, quality, and performance standpoint, ensuring consistent culinary outcomes across menu types.

  • Module 18: Food Safety & Sanitation

    Provides foundational compliance training aligned with industry standard food safety regulations. Topics include contamination control, HACCP practices, temperature monitoring, allergen management, personal hygiene requirements, cleaning cycles, and preventive maintenance. Students build real-world competency in establishing safe, inspection-ready kitchen environments.

  • Module 19: Knife Skills & Cutting Techniques

    A practical module focusing on blade ergonomics, stability, and precision cut execution. Students practice julienne, brunoise, batonnet, mincing, segmenting, and advanced production cuts while learning sharpening routines and equipment safety standards. Training emphasizes speed, uniformity, and reduced waste.

  • Module 20: Efficient Kitchen Prep for Professional Environments

    An applied module that consolidates prep strategy across multiple operational formats. Coursework includes batch planning, multi item sequencing, cross utilization of ingredients, prep day scheduling, and handoff coordination between prep and line teams. The goal is to build workforce ready efficiency that supports high volume, fast paced culinary operations.
