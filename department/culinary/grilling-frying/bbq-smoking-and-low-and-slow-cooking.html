Module Overview

Students explore traditional and modern BBQ smoking techniques including wood selection, smoker types, temperature regulation, and low & slow cooking methods. Focus is placed on achieving optimal flavor, tenderness, and visual appeal while maintaining food safety standards.

Core Learning Objectives

  • Select appropriate smoking woods and fuels to complement proteins.
  • Operate a variety of smokers and grills for low & slow cooking.
  • Control internal cooking temperatures and smoke penetration.
  • Season and marinate meats to achieve complex flavor profiles.
  • Ensure sanitation, cross-contamination prevention, and consistent results in long-duration cooking.

Practical Competencies

  • Setup and maintain smokers for temperature consistency over extended periods.
  • Apply rubs, brines, and marinades optimized for smoking and low & slow techniques.
  • Monitor internal temperatures and adjust airflow/fuel to maintain optimal cooking.
  • Evaluate protein texture, doneness, and smoke infusion for quality assurance.
  • Manage long-duration cooking processes without compromising food safety.
  • Plan multi-protein cooking sequences to coordinate timing and flavor balance.
