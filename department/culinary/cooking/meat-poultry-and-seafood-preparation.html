Module Overview

This module equips students with professional-grade butchery and protein preparation competencies expected in commercial kitchens. Training includes carcass breakdown, primal-to-portion fabrication, trimming, deboning, yield assessment, and global cooking methods for red meat, poultry, and seafood. Emphasis is placed on sanitation, cross-contamination prevention, temperature discipline, and production efficiency.

Core Learning Objectives

  • Perform safe, efficient, and accurate fabrication of beef, pork, lamb, poultry, and seafood.
  • Apply industry sanitation, storage, and handling protocols for raw and cooked proteins.
  • Utilize trimming strategies to maximize yield and minimize waste.
  • Execute global cookery methods including braising, roasting, grilling, pan-searing, sous-vide, and steaming.
  • Analyze texture, doneness, marbling, muscle structure, and connective tissue behavior.
  • Identify and prepare finfish, shellfish, mollusks, and crustaceans using classical and modern techniques.

Practical Competencies

  • Beef and pork primal breakdown, portion control cutting, trimming, and value-added processing.
  • Poultry fabrication: spatchcocking, quartering, deboning, and yield-optimized butchery.
  • Seafood preparation: filleting, skinning, deboning, shucking, scaling, and shellfish sanitation.
  • Temperature control mastery: carryover cooking, resting protocols, and safe hold procedures.
  • Execution of marinades, rubs, brines, cures, and flavor-infused preparation techniques.
  • Assessment and troubleshooting of protein quality, freshness indicators, and procurement standards.
