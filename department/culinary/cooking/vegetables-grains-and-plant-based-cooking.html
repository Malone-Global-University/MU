Module Overview

This module equips learners with a disciplined understanding of plant-derived ingredients and their operational behavior in professional kitchens. The curriculum emphasizes thermal management, structural transformation, nutrient retention, and the strategic use of global plant-based products. Participants will refine their ability to deploy vegetables, grains, and legumes with precision across classical and contemporary culinary models.

Core Competencies

  • Vegetable classification, fabrication standards, and heat-based transformation
  • Moist-heat and dry-heat methodologies for plant-based ingredients
  • Grain and legume hydration science, timing, and structural control
  • Plant-based protein systems and global alternatives (tofu, tempeh, seitan, mycoprotein)
  • Flavor architecture for plant-forward menu engineering

Key Topics

  • Root, stem, leaf, and allium families: operational behavior & flavor strategy
  • Whole grains, refined grains, pseudo-grains, and specialty varieties
  • Legume utilization: soaking protocols, pressure cooking, and textural optimization
  • Plant-based fats, emulsifiers, and natural thickeners
  • Vegetable-forward global cuisines: Mediterranean, Asian, Latin American, African
