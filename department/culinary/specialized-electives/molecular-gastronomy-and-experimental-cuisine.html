Module Overview

This module introduces students to the principles of molecular gastronomy and experimental culinary techniques. Students learn how to manipulate texture, flavor, and presentation using scientific methods and modernist tools, fostering creativity and innovation in professional kitchens.

Core Learning Objectives

  • Understand the science behind flavor, texture, and chemical reactions in cooking.
  • Explore techniques including spherification, gelification, foams, emulsions, and sous-vide applications.
  • Analyze and innovate ingredient combinations and plating concepts.
  • Apply sensory evaluation and critique experimental culinary creations.
  • Integrate modernist techniques into practical, professional culinary workflows.

Practical Competencies

  • Perform spherification, gelification, foaming, and emulsification with precision and safety.
  • Utilize sous-vide cooking and temperature-controlled techniques for consistent results.
  • Create visually striking and conceptually innovative plated dishes.
  • Develop experimental recipes incorporating modernist techniques while maintaining flavor balance.
  • Evaluate and troubleshoot chemical and physical reactions in culinary preparations.
