Specialized Electives Modules

  • Module 21: Food Science & Ingredient Chemistry

    This module provides a scientific framework for understanding how ingredients behave under heat, pressure, pH shifts, and mechanical manipulation. Students evaluate protein coagulation, starch gelatinization, emulsification dynamics, and flavor compound interactions to strengthen decision-making during recipe development. Emphasis is placed on controlled testing, comparative analysis, and applying chemistry driven insights to optimize consistency, yield, and culinary performance across varied production environments.

  • Module 22: Plating & Presentation Techniques

    A comprehensive study of visual composition, balance, and structural plating logic used in modern culinary service. Learners examine color theory, negative space, height, textural contrast, and portion geometry to build high-impact presentations. The module incorporates fine-dining methodology alongside scalable plating systems for banquets, institutional dining, and fast-casual concepts. Students develop plate maps, rehearse timing workflows, and execute plating under speed constraints aligned with real world brigade operations.

  • Module 23: Menu Planning & Recipe Development

    This module focuses on constructing economically viable, operationally efficient menu systems. Coursework covers costing models, yield calculations, flavor sequencing, dietary considerations, and seasonality planning. Students develop recipes using structured R&D methodology, integrate forecasting principles, and create production-ready menu cycles for diverse service formats. The curriculum reinforces scalability, cross utilization of inventory, and alignment with brand positioning and customer expectations.

  • Module 24: Culinary Entrepreneurship & Business Skills

    A strategic overview of the financial, operational, and managerial competencies required to launch or scale a culinary venture. Learners examine business models, licensing, risk management, supply chain logistics, HR and labor planning, and brand development. The module prioritizes revenue optimization, margin protection, and long range operational forecasting. Students build a foundational business plan and practice executive decision making aligned with industry standard performance metrics.

  • Module 25: Molecular Gastronomy & Experimental Cuisine

    An exploration of modernist techniques and the scientific manipulation of food structure, texture, and form. Students engage with spherification, foams, gels, emulsions, dehydrations, and temperature-controlled precision cooking. The curriculum encourages boundary pushing creativity, experimental prototyping, and visual innovation while maintaining strong operational standards. Learners apply scientific analysis to refine outcomes and integrate contemporary culinary technology into professional workflows.
