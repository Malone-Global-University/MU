American Biscuits

Fluffy, tender quick bread perfect for breakfast or dinner.

Overview

American biscuits are a classic quick bread, light and fluffy due to chemical leavening agents like baking powder or baking soda. Ideal for breakfast, brunch, or as a side for dinner.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 3/4 cup milk or buttermilk

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425Â°F (220Â°C) and line a baking sheet.
  2. Whisk together flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.
  3. Cut in cold butter until mixture resembles coarse crumbs.
  4. Add milk gradually, mixing until dough forms.
  5. Turn dough onto floured surface and gently pat 1-inch thick.
  6. Use a biscuit cutter to cut shapes; place on baking sheet.
  7. Bake 12â€“15 minutes until golden brown.
  8. Serve warm with butter, jam, or honey.

Tips

  • Keep butter cold to ensure flaky layers.
  • Do not overmix dough; it should be slightly sticky.
  • For extra rise, stack two biscuit layers before baking.
  • Brush tops with milk or butter for a golden finish.
