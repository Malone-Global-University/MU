Chocolate in Baking: Key Concepts

Types of Chocolate

Chocolate used in baking varies by cocoa content, fat content, sweetness, and texture. Common forms include dark, milk, white, ruby, cocoa powder, couverture, and compound chocolate.

Flavor Development

Chocolate flavor depends heavily on roasting, fermentation, and fat ratios. Proper technique enhances bitterness, fruitiness, and caramel notes.

How Chocolate Behaves in Batter

Chocolate affects structure, tenderness, and moisture. Cocoa powder dries batter; melted chocolate enriches it. Ganaches and glazes add shine and texture.
