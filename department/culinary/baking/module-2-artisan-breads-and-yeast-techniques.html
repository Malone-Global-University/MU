Overview

This module focuses on yeast-based baking and artisan bread techniques. Students will learn the science behind yeast fermentation, proper kneading methods, and techniques to create flavorful, textured breads.

Learning Objectives

  • Understand yeast fermentation and its role in bread making.
  • Learn proper kneading, shaping, and proofing techniques.
  • Master the creation of artisan breads with diverse textures and flavors.
  • Develop skills for scoring, slashing, and finishing breads.
  • Experiment with sourdough, enriched, and whole grain breads.

Topics Covered

  1. Introduction to Yeast and Fermentation
  2. Kneading Techniques and Dough Development
  3. Proofing, Bulk Fermentation, and Final Proof
  4. Artisan Bread Techniques (sourdough, ciabatta, baguette)
  5. Scoring and Crust Finishing
  6. Enriched Breads: Brioche, Challah, and Specialty Rolls
  7. Common Bread Troubleshooting Tips

Assignments & Practice

  • Bake a classic artisan bread and document each step of fermentation and proofing.
  • Experiment with an enriched dough recipe (e.g., brioche or challah) and note texture differences.
  • Practice scoring techniques and present your finished loaves for review.
