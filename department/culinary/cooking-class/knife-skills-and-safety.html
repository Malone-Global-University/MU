Module Overview

This module builds the performance foundation required for all kitchen operations. Students learn how to select, handle, and maintain knives in alignment with professional standards. Emphasis is placed on safety enforcement, grip discipline, cutting accuracy, and production efficiency across classic and contemporary culinary environments.

Core Learning Outcomes

  • Establish proper grip posture (pinch grip, guiding-hand claw technique).
  • Execute foundational cuts: julienne, brunoise, batonnet, dice variations, chiffonade, and precision slicing.
  • Maintain workstation organization aligned with safety protocols and line-production workflows.
  • Perform sharpening and honing using whetstones, steels, and mechanical systems.
  • Identify knife types, materials, and use cases across culinary operations.
  • Apply sanitation, storage, and hazard-prevention standards to mitigate operational risk.

Topics Covered

  • Knife Anatomy & Materials
  • Knife Selection for Task-Specific Workflows
  • Safe Handling & Ergonomic Control
  • Classic French Cuts & International Variations
  • Sharpening, Honing, and Long-Term Blade Care
  • Workstation Layout & Risk Mitigation
  • Speed Drills and Precision Development
