Module Overview

This module operationalizes a global understanding of cuisine, equipping learners with the strategic foundation required to navigate regional food systems, cultural histories, ingredient frameworks, and traditional cooking methodologies. Participants gain exposure to both classical and contemporary approaches from major world regions, reinforcing their ability to execute diverse culinary models with professional consistency.

Core Competencies

  • Identification and classification of global culinary regions
  • Mastery of regional flavor systems, aromatics, and staple ingredients
  • Execution of essential dishes across major world cuisines
  • Operational understanding of cultural food histories and traditions
  • Adaptation of global techniques within modern, professional applications

Key Topics

  • Mediterranean frameworks: olive oil systems, herbs, grains, and regional variations
  • East and Southeast Asia: wok-based methods, fermentation systems, broths, and aromatic layering
  • Latin American culinary models: masa processing, chiles, coastal and Andean traditions
  • Middle Eastern and North African techniques: spice profiles, preserved goods, and slow-cooking systems
  • African regional cuisines: indigenous grains, stews, root vegetables, and historical cross-cultural influences
  • Pan-European building blocks: classical foundations, regional specialties, and modern adaptation
