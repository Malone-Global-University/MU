🌍 Pan-African School – Geography Curriculum Tree

Level 1 – Foundations
  • Introduction to Geography – physical vs. human geography, map skills
  • Earth Systems – atmosphere, hydrosphere, lithosphere, biosphere
  • Basic Cartography – map reading, scales, symbols, coordinates
  • Weather & Climate Basics – weather patterns, seasons, climate zones
  • Introduction to African Geography – regions, landscapes, key features
Level 2 – Core Geography
  • Physical Geography I – landforms, plate tectonics, volcanism, earthquakes
  • Physical Geography II – rivers, coasts, glaciers, deserts
  • Human Geography – population, migration, cultural landscapes
  • Economic Geography – agriculture, industry, trade, resource distribution
  • Geospatial Skills – GIS basics, remote sensing introduction
Level 3 – Applied & Regional Studies
  • Regional Geography of Africa – political boundaries, resources, urbanization
  • Regional Geography of the World – continents, major countries, comparative studies
  • Climatology – climate systems, climate change, extreme weather events
  • Urban Geography – city development, planning, challenges
  • Environmental Management – conservation, sustainable resource use
  • Advanced Cartography – thematic maps, digital mapping techniques
Level 4 – Advanced / Specializations
  • Advanced GIS & Remote Sensing – spatial analysis, satellite imagery
  • Development Geography – inequality, economic development, policy impacts
  • Political Geography – borders, geopolitics, territorial disputes
  • Biogeography – species distribution, ecosystems, biodiversity conservation
  • Hydrology – water systems, watershed management
  • Disaster Risk Management – hazard mapping, disaster preparedness
Practical Training & Projects
  • Map-Making Projects – hand-drawn and digital maps
  • Field Studies – local and regional field trips, data collection
  • GIS Lab Work – mapping exercises, spatial data analysis
  • Environmental Impact Assessments – case study analysis
  • Capstone Project – applied geographic research project
Electives & Cross-Disciplinary Options
  • Tourism Geography – destinations, cultural heritage, ecotourism
  • Transport Geography – infrastructure, trade routes, mobility patterns
  • Geography of Health – disease mapping, health infrastructure
  • Historical Geography – changing landscapes, human-environment interaction
  • Geopolitics of Resources – oil, minerals, water conflicts
