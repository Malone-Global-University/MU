Axum

A powerful Ethiopian kingdom famous for trade, early adoption of Christianity, and monumental stelae.

Introduction

The Kingdom of Axum (100 CE – 940 CE) in modern-day Ethiopia was a major trading empire connecting Africa, Arabia, and the Mediterranean. It became one of the first states to adopt Christianity officially.

Timeline

  • c. 100 CE – Rise of Axumite Kingdom
  • c. 330 CE – Christianity adopted as state religion
  • c. 940 CE – Decline of Axum due to trade shifts

Culture and Achievements

Axumites built monumental stelae, developed a written script (Ge’ez), minted coins, and traded ivory, gold, and frankincense across Africa and the Mediterranean.
