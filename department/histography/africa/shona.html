Great Zimbabwe

The Shona civilization famous for its stone city, trade, and cultural influence in Southern Africa.

Introduction

Great Zimbabwe (c. 1100 – 1450 CE) was a major urban center of the Shona people. It became a hub of trade, linking the interior of Southern Africa with the Indian Ocean coast.

Timeline

  • c. 1100 CE – Great Zimbabwe founded
  • c. 1300 CE – Peak of trade and population
  • c. 1450 CE – Decline of Great Zimbabwe

Culture and Achievements

The city is famous for its dry-stone walls, trade in gold and ivory, complex society, and influence on later kingdoms in the region.
