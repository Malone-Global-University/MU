The Roman Empire

From Republic to Empire, Rome spread law, engineering, and administration across Europe and the Mediterranean.

Introduction

Rome began as a small city-state in Italy, grew into a Republic, and later became a vast Empire influencing politics, law, architecture, and culture for millennia.

Timeline

  • 509 BCE – Roman Republic founded
  • 27 BCE – Augustus becomes first emperor, Roman Empire begins
  • 117 CE – Empire reaches its greatest territorial extent under Trajan
  • 476 CE – Fall of Western Roman Empire

Culture and Achievements

Romans advanced engineering (roads, aqueducts), developed legal codes, spread Latin language and culture, built monumental architecture, and established enduring governance systems.
