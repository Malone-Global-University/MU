Kingdom of Kush

A powerful Nubian kingdom south of Egypt, known for pyramids, gold, and trade networks.

Introduction

The Kingdom of Kush, located in modern-day Sudan, flourished from 1070 BCE to 350 CE. Kushite kings ruled their own empire while also conquering and interacting with Egypt at various points.

Timeline

  • c. 1070 BCE – Rise of Kushite Kingdom
  • c. 750 BCE – Kushite Pharaohs rule Egypt (25th Dynasty)
  • c. 350 CE – Kingdom declines under pressure from Aksumites

Culture and Achievements

Kushites built pyramids, traded gold and ivory, developed their own writing and religious practices, and influenced Egyptian art and architecture.
