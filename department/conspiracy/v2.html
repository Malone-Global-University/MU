Conspiracies Against African Americans

Classification System of Conspiracies

This taxonomy provides a framework for analyzing conspiracy theories involving African Americans and related global figures. Each category distinguishes between intent, evidence, and impact.

Category Intent Example Case Evidence Strength Teaching Angle
Political Suppression Silence activism or dissent COINTELPRO vs. MLK High Government transparency and accountability
Economic & Corporate Exploitation Control or profit from Black creativity Michael Jackson catalog disputes Medium Industry ethics and intellectual property rights
Scientific, Medical & Technological Experimentation or neglect Tuskegee Syphilis Study High Bioethics and informed consent
Cultural & Psychological Manipulation Shape public perception through media Hollywood stereotypes Medium Media literacy and image politics
Esoteric, Spiritual & Fringe Explain oppression through supernatural or hidden order Illuminati celebrity myths Low Symbolic meaning and myth analysis
Cover-Up & Erasure Suppress historical truth or evidence Tulsa Massacre of 1921 High Archival reconstruction and historical justice

Each conspiracy can be tagged by Type, Evidence Tier, and Cultural Impact. This helps students and researchers evaluate patterns, not just claims.

Rumors and Realities: Key Examples

The following sections explore notable cases, categorized by their primary conspiracy type.

Political Suppression

Cases such as COINTELPRO, the surveillance of Malcolm X, and the deaths of Black leaders illustrate patterns of suppression justified as national security operations.

Economic & Corporate Exploitation

Corporate control over creative output—music catalogs, film rights, or invention patents—often fuels theories about ownership theft and exploitation.

Scientific, Medical & Technological

Historical malpractice, such as the Tuskegee Study and Henrietta Lacks, forms the factual basis for modern mistrust in medicine and science among African Americans.

Cultural & Psychological Manipulation

Media narratives and industry patterns influence identity formation and reinforce stereotypes, sometimes leading to suspicion of deliberate image control.

Esoteric, Spiritual & Fringe

From celebrity “Illuminati” myths to lost-tribe theories, these conspiracies often express deeper cultural anxieties and spiritual interpretations of power.

Cover-Up & Erasure

Lost records, suppressed archives, and erased histories—such as those surrounding Black Wall Street—represent the tangible side of conspiratorial silencing.
