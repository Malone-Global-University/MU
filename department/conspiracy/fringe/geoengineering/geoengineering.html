Geoengineering & Conspiracy Theories

Geoengineering is frequently linked with other conspiracy topics such as chemtrails, HAARP, and secret military weather programs. These theories often claim ongoing covert spraying, climate manipulation, or hidden experiments.

Although scientific literature describes proposed geoengineering techniques openly, the combination of:

  • visible aircraft trails,
  • classified research budgets,
  • government interest in atmospheric control,
  • and the scale of global climate politics,

makes geoengineering a natural magnet for public suspicion.

International Governance

Some geoengineering activities fall under environmental treaties, and several organizations have called for moratoriums or strict regulation. The central question is whether any state or institution should be allowed to influence the global climate unilaterally.

Malone University Approach

We treat geoengineering as a blend of documented science, speculative proposals, ethical tension, and conspiracy narratives. Students in the Conspiracy Studies department are encouraged to:

  • distinguish peer-reviewed proposals from online speculation
  • examine real historical programs involving atmospheric modification
  • compare the scale of proposed interventions to natural climate forces
  • evaluate the political, ethical, and geopolitical risks

Understanding geoengineering requires both scientific literacy and awareness of how secrecy and mistrust shape public narratives.
