Electronics

Electronics studies semiconductor devices and the circuits that control, amplify, switch, and process electrical signals. Students learn how modern electronic components function together to form the basis of digital systems, analog devices, sensors, and computing hardware.

  • Semiconductor Physics

    Study the electrical behavior of semiconductor materials and the physics underlying diodes, transistors, and integrated circuits.

  • Analog Electronics

    Examine continuous-signal circuits including amplifiers, filters, and analog control systems.

  • Digital Electronics

    Learn how logic gates, flip-flops, digital circuits, and binary systems create computational hardware.

  • Transistor Design

    Explore transistor operation, switching, amplification, biasing, and circuit implementation in electronic systems.
