Circuit Analysis

Circuit Analysis studies how electrical current and voltage behave within interconnected electrical components. Students learn to model, calculate, and predict the behavior of resistive, capacitive, inductive, and complex electrical networks used throughout modern electronics and power systems.

  • DC Circuits

    Study direct current circuits involving voltage sources, resistors, and steady-state electrical flow through simple and complex networks.

  • AC Circuits

    Examine alternating current systems including sinusoidal voltage, frequency, phase, and time-varying circuit behavior.

  • Network Theorems

    Learn analytical methods such as Thevenin, Norton, superposition, and nodal analysis for solving complex electrical circuits.

  • Impedance and Reactance

    Explore how capacitors and inductors resist changing current and shape AC circuit performance.
