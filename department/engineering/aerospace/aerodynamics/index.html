Aerodynamics

Aerodynamics studies how air interacts with moving bodies and how those interactions generate lift, drag, stability, and control forces. Students examine airflow behavior, pressure distributions, boundary layers, and aerodynamic performance across aircraft, vehicles, and high-speed systems.

  • Lift and Drag

    Study the aerodynamic forces generated by moving air and how they influence flight performance and resistance.

  • Airfoil Design

    Examine wing and blade shapes engineered to optimize lift, efficiency, and stability in airflow.

  • Boundary Layers

    Learn how thin layers of fluid near surfaces affect drag, separation, turbulence, and aerodynamic efficiency.

  • Compressible Flow

    Explore high-speed airflow, shockwaves, Mach effects, and gas compression in supersonic and transonic regimes.
