Flight Mechanics

Flight Mechanics studies the motion, control, and performance of aircraft in flight. Students examine stability, maneuvering, climb, descent, turning, control surfaces, and the physical dynamics governing aircraft behavior in the atmosphere.

  • Aircraft Stability

    Study longitudinal, lateral, and directional stability in aircraft design and flight behavior.

  • Aircraft Control

    Examine how pilots and automated systems use control surfaces to maneuver and stabilize aircraft.

  • Flight Performance

    Learn how speed, altitude, thrust, drag, and lift affect aircraft range, climb, endurance, and maneuverability.

  • Maneuver Dynamics

    Explore the mechanics of turning, climbing, descending, accelerating, and complex aircraft maneuvering.
