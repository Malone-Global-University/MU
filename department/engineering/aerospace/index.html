Aerospace & Systems Design

Aerospace Engineering studies the design and operation of aircraft, spacecraft, propulsion, and flight systems. Students learn the integrated engineering disciplines required to build vehicles capable of controlled atmospheric and orbital flight.

  • Aerodynamics

    Study airflow, lift, drag, stability, and aerodynamic forces acting on moving bodies.

  • Propulsion Systems

    Examine jet engines, rockets, thrust generation, and propulsion mechanics.

  • Flight Mechanics

    Analyze aircraft stability, maneuvering, control, and flight performance.

  • Spacecraft Engineering

    Study spacecraft systems, environmental constraints, orbital hardware, and mission design.

  • Systems Engineering

    Learn integrated design methodology for managing complex multidisciplinary engineering systems.
