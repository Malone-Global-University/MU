Spacecraft Engineering

Spacecraft Engineering studies the design of vehicles and systems intended to operate beyond Earth’s atmosphere. Students learn how spacecraft are engineered to survive launch, vacuum, radiation, thermal extremes, and orbital conditions while performing mission tasks.

  • Spacecraft Structures

    Study the physical framework and load-bearing design of spacecraft and orbital platforms.

  • Space Environment

    Examine vacuum, radiation, micrometeoroids, thermal extremes, and environmental hazards affecting spacecraft.

  • Orbital Systems

    Learn how spacecraft operate in orbit through propulsion, attitude control, and orbital positioning systems.

  • Mission Design

    Explore the engineering planning and integration required to execute successful space missions.
