Structural Engineering

Structural Engineering studies the analysis and design of load-bearing systems that support buildings, bridges, towers, and infrastructure. Students learn how engineered structures resist gravity, wind, seismic forces, dynamic loading, and long-term environmental stress while maintaining safety and stability.

  • Structural Analysis

    Study how forces distribute through beams, columns, frames, and trusses to evaluate structural behavior under load.

  • Steel Design

    Examine the engineering of steel members, connections, and framed systems for structural applications.

  • Concrete Design

    Learn how reinforced and prestressed concrete elements are designed for structural strength and durability.

  • Structural Failure and Safety

    Explore structural failure modes, redundancy, safety factors, and collapse prevention in engineered systems.
