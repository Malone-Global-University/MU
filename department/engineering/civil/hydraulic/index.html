Hydraulic Engineering

Hydraulic Engineering studies the controlled movement and management of water within engineered systems. Students examine pipelines, pumps, dams, drainage systems, channels, reservoirs, and flood-control infrastructure used to direct and utilize fluid flow.

  • Open Channel Flow

    Study water movement in rivers, canals, drainage systems, and other gravity-driven open-surface flow environments.

  • Pipeline Systems

    Examine pressurized fluid transport through engineered piping, pumps, valves, and distribution networks.

  • Dam and Reservoir Design

    Learn how large-scale water storage and flow-control systems are engineered for safety, efficiency, and environmental management.

  • Flood Control and Drainage

    Explore engineered systems used to manage stormwater, runoff, flooding, and water diversion in urban and natural environments.
