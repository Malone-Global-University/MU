Transportation Engineering

Transportation Engineering studies the planning, design, and optimization of systems used to move people and goods efficiently across physical infrastructure. Students examine roads, highways, rail systems, transit networks, traffic operations, and transportation logistics.

  • Roadway Design

    Study the geometric and structural design of roads, intersections, highways, and transportation corridors.

  • Traffic Engineering

    Examine traffic flow, signal timing, congestion analysis, and intersection control systems.

  • Transit Systems

    Learn how buses, rail, subways, and mass transit infrastructure are planned and integrated into urban systems.

  • Transportation Planning

    Explore strategic planning of transportation networks based on demand, growth, efficiency, and regional development.
