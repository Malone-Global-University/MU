Civil & Structural Engineering

Civil and Structural Engineering studies the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure systems including buildings, bridges, transportation networks, foundations, and public works. Students learn how engineered structures safely support human civilization.

  • Structural Engineering

    Study load-bearing systems, structural analysis, and engineered building frameworks.

  • Transportation Engineering

    Examine the design of roads, transit systems, traffic networks, and transportation infrastructure.

  • Geotechnical Engineering

    Study soil, rock, foundations, and subsurface conditions affecting engineered structures.

  • Hydraulic Engineering

    Analyze fluid systems used in dams, pipelines, drainage, and water infrastructure.
