Geotechnical Engineering

Geotechnical Engineering studies soil, rock, groundwater, and subsurface conditions as they affect foundations, earthworks, and structural stability. Students learn how ground materials behave under load and how engineers design structures to safely interact with the earth beneath them.

  • Soil Mechanics

    Study the physical and mechanical behavior of soil under loading, drainage, and environmental change.

  • Foundation Design

    Examine shallow and deep foundation systems used to transfer structural loads safely into the ground.

  • Slope Stability

    Learn how engineers analyze and prevent landslides, embankment failure, and unstable earth movement.

  • Rock Mechanics

    Explore the engineering properties of rock masses and their use in tunnels, excavations, and structural support systems.
