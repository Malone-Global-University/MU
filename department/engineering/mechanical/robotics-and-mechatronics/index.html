Robotics and Mechatronics

Robotics and Mechatronics combines mechanical design, electronics, sensors, actuators, control systems, and software into integrated machines. Students learn how automated systems perceive their environment, make controlled movements, and perform useful work through coordinated hardware and computation.

  • Robot Mechanisms

    Study arms, joints, linkages, grippers, wheels, tracks, and mechanical structures used to create robotic motion.

  • Sensors and Actuators

    Learn how robots detect position, force, distance, light, motion, and contact while using motors and actuators to respond.

  • Robot Control

    Examine feedback, motion planning, control loops, and command systems used to guide robotic movement.

  • Automation Systems

    Explore industrial automation, programmable machines, production robotics, and integrated systems used in modern manufacturing.
