Machine Design

Machine Design studies how mechanical components are selected, shaped, connected, and tested so machines can perform reliably under load. Students learn how gears, shafts, bearings, springs, fasteners, frames, and power transmission systems are designed for strength, motion, durability, and maintainability.

  • Gears and Power Transmission

    Study how mechanical power moves through gears, belts, chains, shafts, and couplings to produce controlled motion.

  • Bearings and Shafts

    Examine rotating components, support systems, friction control, alignment, and load transfer in mechanical assemblies.

  • Fasteners and Joints

    Learn how bolts, screws, welds, pins, rivets, and joints connect parts while managing force, vibration, and failure risk.

  • Failure and Fatigue

    Explore how repeated loading, cracks, stress concentration, wear, and material limits cause mechanical parts to fail over time.
