Fluid Mechanics

Fluid Mechanics studies how liquids and gases behave when at rest, in motion, or interacting with engineered systems. Students examine pressure, flow, turbulence, drag, lift, pumps, pipes, channels, and aerodynamic behavior across mechanical and industrial applications.

  • Fluid Properties

    Study density, viscosity, pressure, compressibility, and other properties that shape how fluids move and respond to force.

  • Fluid Statics

    Examine fluids at rest, including pressure variation, buoyancy, hydrostatic force, and stability in tanks and submerged objects.

  • Pipe Flow

    Learn how fluids move through pipes, valves, pumps, and ducts under friction, pressure loss, and flow-rate constraints.

  • Turbulence and Drag

    Explore chaotic flow behavior, boundary layers, resistance, and the forces fluids apply to moving objects.
