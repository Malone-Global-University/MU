Dynamics and Kinematics

Dynamics and Kinematics studies motion, force, acceleration, velocity, and the behavior of moving mechanical systems. Students learn how machines, vehicles, mechanisms, and rotating systems move through space and how forces change that motion over time.

  • Motion Analysis

    Study position, displacement, velocity, and acceleration as the basic measurements used to describe mechanical movement.

  • Force and Acceleration

    Learn how forces create changes in motion and how Newtonian mechanics explains moving bodies.

  • Rotational Motion

    Examine angular velocity, torque, moment of inertia, and rotational dynamics in wheels, shafts, gears, and machines.

  • Mechanism Motion

    Explore how linkages, cams, sliders, and mechanical assemblies convert one kind of motion into another.
