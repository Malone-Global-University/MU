Statics and Mechanics of Materials

Statics and Mechanics of Materials studies how structures and machine parts carry loads without unwanted motion, deformation, or failure. Students examine force balance, stress, strain, bending, torsion, shear, and material response so engineered components can be designed safely and efficiently.

  • Force Equilibrium

    Learn how forces and moments balance in stationary systems such as beams, frames, supports, and mechanical structures.

  • Stress and Strain

    Study how materials internally resist loads and how they deform under tension, compression, shear, and bending.

  • Beams and Bending

    Examine how beams carry loads, develop internal shear and bending moments, and deflect under structural demand.

  • Torsion and Shear

    Explore twisting loads, shear stress, shaft behavior, and the failure risks created by rotational force.
