Thermodynamics

Thermodynamics studies heat, work, energy transfer, and the limits that govern physical systems. Students learn how engines, refrigerators, turbines, power plants, and thermal devices convert energy from one form to another under real engineering constraints.

  • Energy and Work

    Study how energy moves through mechanical systems and how work is produced, stored, transferred, and limited by physical laws.

  • Heat Transfer Basics

    Examine conduction, convection, and radiation as the primary ways thermal energy moves through materials, fluids, and environments.

  • Engines and Cycles

    Learn how thermodynamic cycles describe the operation of engines, turbines, compressors, refrigerators, and power systems.

  • Entropy and Efficiency

    Explore why no energy system is perfectly efficient and how entropy explains irreversible losses in real machines.
