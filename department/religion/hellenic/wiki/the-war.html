The Trojan Cycle

A chronological collection of myths surrounding the Trojan War, from the events leading to the conflict to the fates of heroes after the fall of Troy.

Stories in the Trojan Cycle

  • The Judgment of Paris — How the war began with the golden apple and Paris’ choice.
  • The Abduction of Helen — The catalyst that brought the Greeks to Troy.
  • The Siege of Troy — Key battles, heroes, and divine interventions during the decade-long war.
  • The Death of Hector — Achilles’ confrontation with Troy’s greatest warrior.
  • The Wrath of Achilles — The pivotal events following Patroclus’ death.
  • The Trojan Horse — The stratagem that ended the war.
  • The Fall of Troy — Sacking the city and the fates of survivors.
  • Aeneas and the Survivors — The escape of Trojan heroes and the founding myths of Rome.

Purpose of the Trojan Cycle

  • To provide a focused study of all events surrounding the Trojan War.
  • To link the heroic age with the consequences of human ambition and divine influence.
  • To allow students and readers to navigate Greek myths in chronological and thematic order.
