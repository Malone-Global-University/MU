The Fall of Troy and Aeneas’ Escape

The city of Troy fell to the Greeks, but some survivors, led by Aeneas, escaped to continue the Trojan legacy.

1. The Sack of Troy

After the Greeks infiltrated the city using the Trojan Horse, they opened the gates to their army. Troy was sacked, its walls burned, and most defenders killed. Women and children were enslaved or taken as concubines.

2. Fate of Trojan Royalty

  • Priam, the king, was killed at the altar.
  • Hecuba, queen, and Cassandra were taken captive.
  • Other members of the royal family were either killed or scattered.

3. Aeneas’ Escape

Aeneas, son of Anchises and the goddess Aphrodite, gathered survivors and fled Troy. According to later Roman myth, he carried his father on his shoulders and led his household gods to safety, eventually journeying to Italy to lay the foundations for Rome.

4. Symbolic Meaning

  • The fall of Troy illustrates the destructive consequences of human pride, divine manipulation, and prolonged conflict.
  • Aeneas’ survival symbolizes resilience, duty, and the continuation of cultural and divine legacy.
  • The story connects Greek and Roman mythologies, showing how one culture interpreted the consequences of another’s heroic age.
