Roman Catholicism

The Largest Christian Tradition

Introduction

Roman Catholicism is the largest branch of Christianity, with more than a billion adherents worldwide. It traces its authority to the teachings of Jesus Christ, the leadership of the Apostle Peter, and the unbroken succession of popes in Rome. The Catholic Church emphasizes the unity of faith, sacraments, and tradition under the guidance of the Magisterium (teaching authority).

Global Influence

Today, Catholicism is a worldwide faith, deeply embedded in Europe, Latin America, Africa, Asia, and North America. The Vatican, an independent city-state within Rome, serves as the global center of Catholic governance, theology, and diplomacy.
