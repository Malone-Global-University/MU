Protestantism

The Churches of the Reformation

Introduction

Protestantism emerged in the 16th century during the Reformation, challenging the authority of the Roman Catholic Church. It emphasizes the authority of Scripture over church hierarchy, salvation by faith alone, and the priesthood of all believers. Today, Protestantism is highly diverse, encompassing thousands of denominations worldwide.

Historical Development

Protestantism began with figures like Martin Luther in Germany and John Calvin in Switzerland, who sought reform within Catholicism but ultimately created new movements. Over time, Protestantism fragmented into many denominations including Lutheran, Reformed, Anglican, Baptist, Methodist, and Pentecostal traditions.

Global Presence

Protestantism is particularly strong in North America, Northern Europe, sub-Saharan Africa, and parts of Latin America and Asia. Its adaptability and emphasis on scripture and preaching have contributed to rapid global expansion, especially through missionary movements and evangelical revivals.
