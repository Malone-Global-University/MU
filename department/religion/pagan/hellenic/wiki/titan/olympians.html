The Birth of the Olympians

How Rhea saved Zeus, how he grew strong, and how the Olympian gods were born and prepared to overthrow the Titans.

1. Rheaâ€™s Plan to Save Zeus

When Cronus continued swallowing his children, Rhea sought help from Gaia and Uranus. On the birth of her youngest son, Zeus, she hid him in a cave on the island of Crete. In his place, she gave Cronus a stone wrapped in swaddling clothes, which he swallowed, believing it to be the child.

2. Zeusâ€™ Secret Upbringing

Zeus was raised in secret by divine caretakers. Different traditions describe his upbringing:

  • The nymph Amalthea nourished him with milk from a goat.
  • The Curetes, armored dancers, clashed their weapons to drown out his cries so Cronus would not hear him.
  • Bees brought honey to sustain him in the cave.

Once grown, Zeus confronted Cronus. Depending on the tradition:

Cronus vomited up the stone (later placed at Delphi as the Omphalos) and his five children: Hestia, Demeter, Hera, Hades, and Poseidon.

4. The Olympians United

Reunited with their youngest brother, the six children of Cronus and Rhea became known as the Olympian gods. They prepared to wage war against the Titans for control of the cosmos â€” a conflict later called the Titanomachy.

5. Symbolic Meaning

The birth of the Olympians marks a new order in myth:

  • Zeus represents cunning, destiny, and inevitability in the cycle of succession.
  • The Olympians symbolize a new, more just order compared to the unstable rule of the Titans.
  • The swallowed stone, later venerated, symbolizes truth hidden and later revealed.
