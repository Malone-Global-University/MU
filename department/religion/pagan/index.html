Pagan Traditions

Explore Wicca, Druidry, and other contemporary pagan and polytheistic spiritual practices across cultures.

Course Overview

This course provides an introduction to Pagan spiritual traditions, exploring beliefs, rituals, festivals, mythology, and the revival of polytheistic practices in modern contexts.

Major Pagan Religious Traditions

  • Babylonian Religion

    A Mesopotamian religious tradition that developed from earlier Sumerian beliefs, featuring gods such as Marduk and famous mythological texts like the Enuma Elish.

  • Baltic Religion

    The indigenous polytheistic traditions of the Baltic peoples, including Lithuanians and Latvians, centered on nature deities such as Perkūnas and rich seasonal rituals.

  • Canaanite Religion

    The religious traditions of the ancient Levant involving deities such as El, Baal, and Asherah, which influenced many neighboring cultures and early Israelite religion.

  • Celtic Religion

    The spiritual traditions of ancient Celtic societies across Britain, Ireland, and Gaul featuring nature worship, sacred sites, seasonal festivals, and the priestly Druids.

  • Egyptian Religion

    The ancient religious system of the Nile Valley involving a vast pantheon of gods, sacred temples, priesthoods, and elaborate beliefs about the afterlife.

  • Germanic Paganism

    The pre-Christian religious traditions of continental Germanic tribes involving gods such as Wodan, Donar, and Tiw, closely related to later Norse mythology.

  • Greek (Hellenic) Religion

    The ancient religious system of Greece centered on the Olympian gods such as Zeus, Athena, and Apollo, with rich mythological traditions, temple worship, and civic festivals.

  • Near Eastern Religions

    A broader category encompassing the religious traditions of Mesopotamia, the Levant, and surrounding civilizations during the Bronze and Iron Ages.

  • Norse Religion

    The pre-Christian religious traditions of Scandinavia involving gods such as Odin, Thor, and Freyja, heroic mythology, sacred groves, and the cosmology of Yggdrasil.

  • Roman Religion

    The state religion of ancient Rome featuring gods like Jupiter, Mars, and Venus, emphasizing ritual practice, household spirits, and public ceremonies that reinforced Roman civic life.

  • Slavic Religion

    The traditional pagan beliefs of early Slavic societies involving gods such as Perun, Veles, and Mokosh, with strong connections to nature, ancestor reverence, and seasonal rites.

  • Sumerian Religion

    The earliest known organized religious system of Mesopotamia, featuring gods such as Anu, Enlil, and Inanna and centered around temple cities and priestly administration.

Topics Covered

  • Wicca

    Study Wiccan beliefs, the Wheel of the Year, ritual practice, and contemporary covens.

  • Druidry

    Explore Celtic-inspired spiritual practice, nature veneration, seasonal rituals, and philosophy.

  • Other Pagan Traditions

    Examine Heathenry, Hellenism, Kemetic, and other modern polytheistic practices.

  • Mythology & Symbolism

    Learn about mythological figures, sacred symbols, and ritual significance.

  • Modern Paganism

    Understand the cultural revival of pagan traditions, community building, and contemporary practice.
