Lessons Learned

I learned that large data needs deep compartmentalization.

Conclusion

Since so much information is planned the best approach is to build in layers. The first layer is article driven content summarizing the subject and its key topics. This starts as lists that can be later expanded to content rich story based paragraphs. This serves as a "magazine layer". The next layer adds questions and tasks to build skills such as memory recall, analytical thinking, and original essay writing. Trivia questions and written work serves as an "educational layer", effectively it is "homework". The final layer is my own personal take and insights serving as a "doctrine layer". Presenting information this way covers three main objectives: Inform working professionals who are in a hurry; Provide guided coursework for those who have more time; Explain the hidden meanings and finer points for those who want mastery and more than just the "official lesson" given by standard education sources.
