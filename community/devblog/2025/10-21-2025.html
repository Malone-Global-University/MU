Details

The Politics department has been renamed as Politacl Literacy. The home page is required reading. It explains politics as it pertains tot he individual not the collective. It teaches how to think indeoendently and form unbiased opinions that are resistant to manipulation or fake news. People who are Serious about Political Participation Need to Read it.

Purpose

The purpose is to educate citizens on how to form world views that are free from outside influence. Instead of "Hi, I am a politician, do you like me? Will you help me defeat my opponent?" the frame of thinking shifts to "what are my values and who better indetifies with them?". In order for politics to be meaningful people have to participate at the local level and understand the role governemnt plays in their personal lives.

Lessons Learned

Start with with identifying your personal struggles then see if government can help you directly or not. If they can, work with them. If they cannot abandon them and solve your own problems. Once you have solved your own problems generalzie the solution for all people and run for office, or build community based initiatives to educate your fellow citizens.
