WORKFLOW NOTE

Title
MGU Business and History Anchor Textbook Planning

Date
2026-05-17

Area
MGU Library textbook planning, Business Series, History Series, curriculum architecture

Problem
The MGU Library needed major anchor textbook structures for business and history. The business textbook needed to combine general business literacy with practical entrepreneurship. The world history textbook needed to become a large chronological and regional survey instead of a narrow or Europe-centered outline. The United States history textbook needed to function as an essential student reference covering Indigenous history, colonization, founding, slavery, Civil War, Reconstruction, industrialization, world wars, civil rights, modern politics, and contemporary America.

Intended Goal
Create durable chapter-outline frameworks that can be used as production blueprints for full MGU Library Textbook Edition books. Each outline needed to be broad enough for student reference, organized enough for future chapter generation, and useful as a backbone for later library guides, encyclopedia pages, timelines, primary source collections, maps, and companion reference systems.

Root Cause
The textbook shelf was expanding from individual subject outlines into a larger MGU Library reference system. Without stable anchor outlines, later chapter writing, prompt bundles, encyclopedia linking, and library navigation would risk becoming inconsistent, shallow, duplicated, or poorly organized.

Steps Taken

1. Defined **Business Foundations and Entrepreneurship** as a practical introductory business textbook built around the idea of business as organized value creation and entrepreneurship as opportunity testing, venture building, customer service, resource management, and sustainable operation.

2. Created a full **Business Foundations and Entrepreneurship** outline with **28 chapters in 7 parts**, covering business basics, markets, ownership, business models, planning, ethics, legal foundations, entrepreneurship, customer discovery, prototyping, launch, management, operations, marketing, sales, customer service, accounting, finance, pricing, metrics, growth, global business, technology, and a capstone business portfolio.

3. Added recommended business textbook back matter and final student portfolio components, including a business idea sheet, customer profile, competitor map, business model canvas, legal and ethical checklist, prototype plan, launch plan, operations map, marketing plan, sales outline, pricing worksheet, break-even analysis, metrics dashboard, business plan, and final pitch.

4. Defined **World History: Civilizations, Empires, and Global Change** as a major MGU History Series anchor text organized chronologically and regionally, with global coverage across Africa, the Americas, Asia, Europe, the Middle East, Oceania, and exchange networks.

5. Created a full **World History** outline with **60 chapters in 11 parts**, beginning with human origins and early migration and continuing through agriculture, early civilizations, classical societies, late antiquity, Islam, medieval worlds, the Mongol Empire, global trade before 1500, the early modern world, revolutions, industrialization, imperialism, world wars, the Cold War, decolonization, globalization, technology, climate, contemporary conflict, and world history in the present.

6. Added recommended world history back matter and companion systems, including a world history glossary, master timeline, regional timelines, civilization index, empire index, dynasty index, religion and philosophy index, war and conflict index, revolution index, trade route index, primary source reader, historical maps collection, biographical dictionary, and world history by era and region guides.

7. Defined **United States History** as an essential MGU student reference text that should be chronological but not narrowly presidential, covering Indigenous history, colonization, African slavery, revolution, constitutional development, expansion, Civil War, Reconstruction, industrialization, immigration, reform, world wars, civil rights, modern politics, and contemporary America.

8. Created a full **United States History** outline with **48 chapters in 10 parts**, covering Indigenous North America, contact and colonization, Native resistance, African slavery, colonial regions, imperial conflict, revolution, founding documents, Constitution, early republic, citizenship, westward expansion, Market Revolution, Jacksonian democracy, Indian Removal, slavery, reform, Manifest Destiny, sectional crisis, Civil War, Reconstruction, Jim Crow, industrial capitalism, labor, immigration, urbanization, western expansion, segregation, Populism, imperialism, Progressivism, World War I, the 1920s, Great Depression, New Deal, World War II, Cold War, civil rights, Vietnam, social movements, conservative resurgence, globalization, technology, immigration, and contemporary America.

9. Added recommended U.S. history back matter and companion systems, including a U.S. history glossary, master timeline, Indigenous sovereignty reference, founding documents guide, Constitution and amendments reference, Supreme Court cases index, presidents and administrations index, political parties and elections guide, slavery and abolition reference, Civil War and Reconstruction guide, civil rights reference, labor history guide, immigration history guide, primary source reader, maps collection, and historical biography dictionary.

Key Change
Three major MGU anchor textbook frameworks were created: one for introductory business and entrepreneurship, one for global world history, and one for essential United States history.

Outcome
MGU now has stable production-ready outlines for three major Library Textbook Edition projects. The business outline supports practical student entrepreneurship and business literacy. The world history outline provides a global chronological-regional backbone for future history reference systems. The U.S. history outline provides a broad student reference structure that includes political, social, constitutional, Indigenous, racial, economic, military, and contemporary history.

Validation
The outlines were checked for scope, sequence, and reference usefulness. Each book includes a clear title, series placement, organizing purpose, part structure, chapter list, chapter purposes, section-level structure, recommended back matter, and companion library systems. The history books were structured to avoid overly narrow political narratives and to support later MGU Library expansion.

Rules Going Forward
Future MGU anchor textbooks should be designed as both textbooks and reference backbones. Major survey texts should use chronological structure while also preserving regional, social, institutional, and thematic balance. History outlines should avoid reducing the subject to presidents, wars, or Europe-centered narratives. Business outlines should avoid motivational-only entrepreneurship and should include operations, accounting, finance, law, ethics, marketing, sales, and practical venture-building checkpoints.

Notes
The next logical production step is to create master instruction sheets and chapter prompt bundles for these three textbooks. The world history and U.S. history outlines can also be used to generate library companion pages such as era guides, region guides, primary source collections, timelines, maps, biography indexes, and glossary systems.
