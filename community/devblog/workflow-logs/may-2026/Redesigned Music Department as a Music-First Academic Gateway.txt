WORKFLOW NOTE

Title
Redesigned Music Department as a Music-First Academic Gateway

Date
2026-05-16

Area
MGU Music Department / `music/index.html` / department information architecture

Problem
The Music Department content was drifting toward artist-directory pages organized by race. The uploaded model page was an African American jazz musician directory, and that structure was reused for hip-hop, rock and roll, country, and pop artist pages. This created the wrong implication that the department itself should be organized race-first or artist-first.

Intended Goal
The Music Department should function like a real university-style music department. The main `music/index.html` should be organized around music as a field of study: foundations, theory, composition, history, performance, genres, technology, production, culture, society, business, careers, and reference resources. Race-specific and heritage-specific artist lists should remain available, but only as specialized reference or cultural-history pages within a broader inclusive structure.

Root Cause
The initial page model was a specialized African American jazz musician directory, and its framing was carried forward too broadly. Instead of separating a heritage directory from the department-level architecture, the early generated pages repeated the race-specific artist-directory pattern across multiple genres.

Steps Taken

1. Used the uploaded jazz musician HTML file as a model to generate genre-specific artist directory pages for rap/hip-hop, rock and roll, country, and pop music.

2. Identified that the generated pages were incorrectly extending the “African American artists” framing beyond its intended role.

3. Reframed the department architecture around music-first academic categories rather than artist lists or racial categories.

4. Established that African American and Black music pages should be preserved as part of a broader Black history, heritage, and music culture narrative.

5. Defined a conventional department hierarchy with major sections for Music Foundations, Music Theory and Composition, Music History, Performance Studies, Genres and Traditions, Music Technology and Production, Music, Culture, and Society, Music Business and Careers, and Reference and Directories.

6. Wrote a full `music/index.html` page using the provided MGU department template.

7. Added a hero section, overview, “On This Page,” and detailed department sections with explanatory paragraphs and internal links.

8. Positioned Black music history, African American music, African diaspora music, civil rights music, protest music, and sacred music under “Music, Culture, and Society” rather than making them the organizing principle of the entire department.

9. Positioned artist directories, composer directories, instrument guides, glossaries, album guides, song guides, timelines, listening guides, and biography indexes under “Reference and Directories.”

Key Change
The Music Department was redesigned from an artist-first and race-specific direction into a music-first academic department structure.

Outcome
`music/index.html` now works as a true department gateway. It introduces music as sound, art, performance, history, theory, technology, culture, and profession. The page can guide students into foundational study, genre study, performance, music history, production, business, and reference resources without reducing the department to artist lists. Heritage pages still have a proper place, but they now support the larger structure rather than control it.

Validation
The corrected structure was checked conceptually against the intended role of a university music department. The final page includes conventional academic categories, clear navigation sections, and inclusive genre coverage. It also preserves space for Black music history and African American music without making race the primary organizing logic for all music content.

Rules Going Forward
Do not use a specialized artist-directory page as the model for an entire department index.

Keep main department pages field-first, not biography-first.

Use race-specific, heritage-specific, or identity-specific pages only where they serve a clear cultural, historical, or reference purpose.

Genre pages should be inclusive and music-first, covering sound, history, traits, subgenres, recordings, artists, and cultural context.

Artist directories should live under reference, genre, or heritage sections rather than replacing the academic structure.

Before generating multiple related pages, confirm whether the model page represents the whole system or only one specialized branch.

Notes
The previously generated African American artist pages can still be useful if relocated into a Black music history, African American music, or reference directory structure. Future work should likely include building index pages for `genres/`, `history/`, `culture-society/`, and `reference/` so the new Music Department homepage has complete supporting branches.
