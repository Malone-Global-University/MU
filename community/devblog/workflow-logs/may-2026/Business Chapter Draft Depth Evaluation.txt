WORKFLOW NOTE

Title
Business Chapter Draft Depth Evaluation

Date
2026-05-17

Area
MGU Business Series | Business Foundations and Entrepreneurship | Library Textbook Drafting Workflow

Problem
Claude-generated textbook chapters were coming in shorter than the preferred MGU Library Textbook Edition depth. The user normally expects chapters around 50,000–70,000 characters, but simpler prompts were producing chapters closer to 30,000 characters. This created uncertainty about whether the drafts were too shallow to use, especially compared with fuller probability/statistics chapter drafts.

Intended Goal
Establish whether the shorter Claude-generated chapters are still usable as first-draft textbook chapters, determine how they compare to the preferred MGU standard, and decide whether the business textbook workflow should continue with Claude generating base drafts while the user expands them later.

Root Cause
The prompt style being used was producing “chapter-shaped” drafts rather than full-depth Library Textbook Edition chapters. Claude was creating coherent structure and readable prose, but not expanding each chapter with enough examples, case studies, review depth, practice variety, business math, and applied scaffolding to reach the 50,000–70,000 character target.

Steps Taken

1. Compared two versions of a calculus-based Probability and Statistics Chapter 1 draft and identified Version 1 as the stronger full textbook chapter because it included a complete apparatus: conceptual sections, worked examples, misconceptions, key terms, review questions, practice, error analysis, checkpoint, and bridge note. 

2. Clarified the chapter-size issue: the user’s ideal MGU textbook chapters usually run 50,000–70,000 characters, while simpler prompts often produce approximately 30,000-character chapters.

3. Reviewed four uploaded Markdown chapters from the Business Foundations and Entrepreneurship textbook: Chapter 1, Chapter 2, Chapter 3, and Chapter 4.    

4. Estimated that the four business chapters were approximately 29,000–32,000 characters each, confirming that they matched the shorter “simple prompt” output pattern.

5. Evaluated the chapters for textbook usability, consistency, student usefulness, structure, and alignment with the MGU “thin but good now, expand later” workflow.

6. Determined that the chapters are usable first-draft base layers, not final full-depth Library Textbook Edition chapters.

7. Recommended continuing with Claude’s current business chapter workflow for the core draft structure, then adding an expansion layer later through user editing or targeted follow-up prompts.

Key Change
The shorter Claude-generated business chapters were reclassified from “possibly inadequate final chapters” to “usable thin-but-good first drafts that can be expanded later.”

Outcome
The business textbook workflow can continue without restarting. Claude’s drafts were judged coherent, teachable, and structurally consistent across Chapters 1–4. The user can now treat them as base manuscripts rather than failed outputs. This preserves momentum while acknowledging that later expansion will be needed to reach full MGU Library Textbook Edition depth.

Validation
The uploaded business chapters were checked against the user’s expected chapter-size range and reviewed for chapter structure. Chapter 1 established the foundation of business as organized value creation and exchange. Chapter 2 developed needs, wants, markets, customer segments, demand, competition, and value propositions. Chapter 3 placed business inside the larger economic environment through scarcity, government, technology, globalization, and risk. Chapter 4 addressed ethics, law, trust, conflicts of interest, privacy, stakeholders, and ethical decision-making. The consistent structure across all four confirmed that the workflow is stable even if the drafts are shorter than ideal.

Rules Going Forward
Future MGU textbook prompts should explicitly state the desired 50,000–70,000 character range when full Library Textbook Edition depth is required.

Simple prompts should be expected to produce approximately 30,000-character chapter drafts unless the prompt specifically requires expansion.

Claude-generated 30,000-character chapters may be accepted as first-draft base layers if they are coherent, complete, and structurally consistent.

Expansion can be handled as a second pass by adding worked examples, applied cases, business math, common mistakes, visual/table material, deeper review questions, and stronger chapter checkpoints.

Do not discard stable shorter drafts merely because they are not full-length. Evaluate whether they are useful as a foundation before deciding to regenerate.

Notes
The current business chapters appear well suited for a layered workflow: Claude produces the readable core chapter, then the user or a later prompt expands the chapter into full MGU depth. This approach may be more efficient than forcing every first draft to hit the full character target immediately.
