WORKFLOW NOTE

Title
MGU Core Humanities and Civic Textbook Outline Planning

Date
2026-05-17

Area
MGU Library Textbook Edition / Humanities, Social Science, and Library Support Curriculum

Problem
Several high-priority MGU textbook areas needed clear structure before chapter drafting could begin. The subjects were broad and could easily become scattered: government and civics could become either too U.S.-specific or too political; composition could become only grammar instead of a universal academic writing tool; literature could become an author survey instead of a reading-skills book; and research methods could become only a citation guide instead of a full information literacy system.

Intended Goal
Create durable textbook plans that define each book’s purpose, scope, chapter sequence, recurring features, and role inside the MGU Library system. Each book should function as a serious Library Textbook Edition and support future chapter drafting, course conversion, reference-page creation, and student navigation.

Root Cause
The books covered foundational academic skills and civic knowledge, but they did not yet have stable outlines. Without outlines, future drafting could drift in length, emphasis, tone, or organization. The missing structure made it harder to know what each textbook should include, what should be saved for companion books, and how each title should support the wider MGU curriculum.

Steps Taken

1. Defined **Government, Civics, and Political Systems** as a broad social science anchor text covering constitutional government, branches of government, federalism, rights, elections, ideologies, public policy, international systems, and comparative government.

2. Created a full **51-chapter outline** for Government, Civics, and Political Systems, organized across 9 parts from foundations of government through comparative political systems.

3. Defined **English Composition and Academic Writing** as a high-priority humanities infrastructure book that supports all other MGU textbooks by teaching sentences, paragraphs, essays, argument, research, citation, revision, academic tone, and writing across the curriculum.

4. Created a full **48-chapter outline** for English Composition and Academic Writing, organized across 8 parts from academic thinking and grammar through research, citation, revision, and cross-curricular writing.

5. Defined **Literature: Reading, Interpretation, and World Texts** as a literary reading and interpretation textbook rather than only an author or period survey.

6. Created a full **48-chapter outline** for Literature: Reading, Interpretation, and World Texts, organized across 8 parts covering close reading, literary language, poetry, fiction, drama, genre, historical context, world traditions, and literary analysis.

7. Defined **Research Methods and Information Literacy** as a top-priority MGU Library support book that teaches students how to ask questions, find sources, evaluate evidence, avoid misinformation, use databases, organize notes, cite sources, and use information responsibly.

8. Created a full **48-chapter outline** for Research Methods and Information Literacy, organized across 8 parts covering research foundations, source types, search strategy, MGU Library use, evidence evaluation, misinformation, synthesis, citation, responsible digital tool use, and lifelong information literacy.

9. Added recommended back matter, recurring chapter features, reusable MGU learning models, and writing philosophies for the major textbook plans.

Key Change
Four broad textbook ideas were converted into structured MGU Library Textbook Edition plans with clear chapter architecture and instructional purpose.

Outcome
MGU now has complete planning frameworks for four foundational books: one civic/social science anchor, two humanities anchors, and one library research infrastructure text. These outlines can be used directly for chapter prompt bundles, textbook drafting, course development, library guide pages, department support pages, and future student reference tools.

Validation
The session confirmed that each title has a complete part-and-chapter structure, clear scope, and defined instructional role. Each outline includes chapter titles, section-level topics, back matter recommendations, recurring features, and reusable models where appropriate. The books also align with the broader MGU strategy of treating major textbooks as Library Textbook Editions rather than isolated classroom handouts.

Rules Going Forward
Use the Library Textbook Edition pattern for major MGU anchor books.
Begin broad academic subjects with a “How should I write this?” planning pass before producing the final chapter outline.
Keep composition, research methods, and literature close together because they support reading, writing, interpretation, and information use across the whole university.
Keep civic and government writing neutral, explanatory, institution-centered, and comparative.
For foundational support books, include recurring chapter features, reusable student models, and practical back matter.
Avoid letting broad textbooks become only surveys of names, dates, or terms; each book should teach transferable skills and methods.

Notes
The strongest infrastructure pair identified this session is **English Composition and Academic Writing** plus **Research Methods and Information Literacy**. Together, they teach students how to know what they are talking about and how to write about it clearly. **Literature: Reading, Interpretation, and World Texts** strengthens deep reading and interpretation, while **Government, Civics, and Political Systems** establishes a major social science/civic reference text.
