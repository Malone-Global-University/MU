WORKFLOW NOTE

Title
MGU Mathematics Series Textbook Outline Expansion

Date
2026-05-17

Area
MGU Library Textbook Planning / Mathematics Series / Chapter Prompt Production

Problem
The MGU Mathematics Series needed a broader textbook roadmap beyond the existing Arithmetic and Pre-Algebra Foundations model. Algebra I and Algebra II needed to be separated into standalone books, and the larger mathematics pathway needed consistent outline structures for future Library textbook production. There was also a risk that some later books were being incorrectly labeled as “calculus-based” when their correct foundation should be algebra-, proof-, statistics-, or computing-based.

Intended Goal
Create a consistent, reusable textbook-planning structure for the MGU Mathematics Series using the uploaded Arithmetic and Pre-Algebra Foundations outline as the model. Algebra I and Algebra II should have master Claude.ai instructions and chapter-generation prompts. Additional major mathematics books should be outlined in the same 14-chapter, 5-part Library Textbook Edition format. The correct prerequisite basis for each advanced book should be clarified.

Root Cause
The Arithmetic textbook had already established a strong Library textbook format, but the rest of the mathematics sequence did not yet have matching outlines, master instructions, or prompt bundles. Because several topics overlap across algebra, calculus, statistics, computer science, and AI, some books were initially described too broadly as calculus-based even when that was not the best instructional foundation.

Steps Taken

1. Used the uploaded Arithmetic and Pre-Algebra Foundations outline/master-instruction model as the structural pattern for new MGU Mathematics Series books.

2. Drafted a combined Algebra I and Algebra II outline, then separated Algebra I into a standalone textbook plan.

3. Wrote a full Claude.ai master handoff prompt for Algebra I, including textbook identity, audience, instructional philosophy, chapter structure, worked-example format, practice categories, Markdown/LaTeX expectations, HTML awareness, MGU Library connections, and chapter purposes.

4. Wrote a matching Claude.ai master handoff prompt for Algebra II using the same structure and tone.

5. Created an Algebra I Chapter 1 prompt using the model format: chapter title, chapter purpose, chapter sections, back matter, and final instruction to write a full Markdown textbook chapter without an answer key.

6. Generated a zipped download folder containing separate Markdown chapter prompts for Algebra I Chapters 2–14, plus a README index.

7. Generated a zipped download folder containing separate Markdown chapter prompts for Algebra II Chapters 1–14, plus a README index.

8. Drafted full 14-chapter / 5-part outline structures for these MGU Mathematics Series books: Geometry, Trigonometry and Precalculus, High School Statistics, Calculus, Discrete Mathematics, Linear Algebra, Differential Equations, Probability and Statistics, Combinatorics, Numerical Methods, Data Science Foundations, Scientific Computing, Artificial Intelligence Foundations, and Machine Learning Mathematics.

9. Clarified which books should and should not be described as calculus-based. Numerical Methods, Scientific Computing, and Machine Learning Mathematics were confirmed as calculus-based or calculus-dependent. Discrete Mathematics and Combinatorics were clarified as non-calculus, algebra/proof/discrete-based. Data Science Foundations and Artificial Intelligence Foundations were clarified as better treated as broader foundations books unless intentionally made advanced mathematical versions.

10. Identified remaining major mathematics branches still missing from the outline plan, including Calculus II, Calculus III / Multivariable Calculus, Mathematical Proof and Reasoning, Number Theory, Abstract Algebra, Real Analysis, Optimization and Operations Research, Graph Theory, Complex Analysis, and Topology.

Key Change
The MGU Mathematics Series was expanded from a single Arithmetic model into a broad, reusable textbook roadmap with consistent outline architecture, Algebra I/II production prompts, and corrected prerequisite classifications.

Outcome
The mathematics library now has a coherent production framework for multiple textbook levels: preparatory, high school, undergraduate foundations, applied mathematics, computational mathematics, statistics, data science, and AI. Algebra I and Algebra II are now ready for chapter-by-chapter Claude.ai generation using downloadable prompt bundles. The larger series has a clear direction for future outlines and avoids forcing calculus prerequisites onto books where algebra, proof, or statistics would be more appropriate.

Validation
The work was validated by repeatedly matching each new outline to the uploaded Arithmetic textbook structure: 5 parts, 14 chapters, chapter purpose paragraphs, numbered section lists, chapter back matter, full-book appendices, and series placement notes. The Algebra I and Algebra II prompt bundles were created as zipped folders with individual Markdown files and README indexes. The prerequisite correction was checked by comparing each book’s mathematical nature against its likely audience and dependency structure.

Rules Going Forward
Use the Arithmetic and Pre-Algebra Foundations outline as the default structure for MGU Mathematics Series textbook planning unless a specific book requires a different organization.

Keep Algebra I and Algebra II as standalone books rather than one combined text.

Use downloadable chapter prompt bundles when a textbook is ready for Claude.ai chapter production.

Do not label a book “calculus-based” unless calculus is structurally necessary for the main text.

Use “algebra-based,” “proof-based,” “statistics-based,” “discrete-based,” “calculus-based,” or “calculus/linear-algebra/probability-based” more precisely when describing prerequisites.

Keep foundational books accessible and reserve advanced versions for later specialized titles when needed.

Notes
The next highest-priority missing outlines are likely Calculus II, Calculus III / Multivariable Calculus, Mathematical Proof and Reasoning, Number Theory, Abstract Algebra, Real Analysis, Optimization and Operations Research, Graph Theory, Complex Analysis, and Topology. Data Science Foundations and Artificial Intelligence Foundations may later need revised non-calculus-based versions if they are intended as broader entry texts for the MGU Library.
