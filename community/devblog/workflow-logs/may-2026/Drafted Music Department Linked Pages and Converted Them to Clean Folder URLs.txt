WORKFLOW NOTE

Title
Drafted Music Department Linked Pages and Converted Them to Clean Folder URLs

Date
2026-05-17

Area
MGU Website — Music Department pages under `/department/art/music/`

Problem
The Music Department landing page had five featured links, but the linked destination pages did not yet exist as drafted content pages. The links pointed to clean extensionless URLs such as `/department/art/music/history`, which required matching folder-based `index.html` pages instead of loose `.html` files for best site consistency.

Intended Goal
Create usable first-draft HTML pages for each Music Department feature link and organize them so the existing clean URLs resolve correctly through folder `index.html` files.

Root Cause
The department index page had already been structured with navigation links, but the supporting pages had not yet been built. The initial draft files were generated as individual `.html` files, while the site’s preferred pattern is clean URL routing through folders containing `index.html`.

Steps Taken

1. Reviewed the Music Department landing page structure, including its hero, breadcrumb placeholder, dynamic header/footer loading, bottom navigation placeholder, and `/component/dept.css` stylesheet reference.
2. Identified the five linked pages that needed drafts: Notable African & African American Musicians, History of African and Diasporic Music, Musical Genres & Traditions, Music Theory & Composition, and Student Performances & Research.
3. Drafted standalone HTML pages for each linked destination using the same MGU department-page conventions.
4. Packaged the generated draft pages into a downloadable ZIP bundle.
5. Renamed the loose HTML files into folder-based `index.html` pages using their previous filenames as folder names.
6. Confirmed that the new structure matches the existing clean links from the Music Department page.

Key Change
The Music Department’s featured links were backed by real draft pages organized as clean folder-based `index.html` destinations.

Outcome
The Music Department section now has a usable page tree instead of empty or missing linked destinations. The featured links can resolve cleanly without requiring `.html` extensions, and the new pages fit the broader MGU directory style.

Validation
The link structure was checked against the folder layout. The original links point to paths such as `/department/art/music/genres`, and those paths now correspond naturally to folders such as `/department/art/music/genres/index.html`.

Rules Going Forward
Use folder-based `index.html` pages when MGU links are written as clean extensionless URLs. Avoid mixing `/page.html` links with `/page/` folder routing unless there is a specific reason. After renaming loose `.html` files into folder indexes, check any canonical links, breadcrumbs, and internal self-links for stale `.html` references.

Notes
The Music Department pages were drafted as first-pass department pages, not final encyclopedia articles. They can later be expanded into deeper music-history, genre, theory, musician-profile, and student-project sections.
