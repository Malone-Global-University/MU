WORKFLOW NOTE

Title
MGU Textbook Outline Expansion Across Science, Computer Science, Economics, and Finance

Date
2026-05-17

Area
MGU textbook planning, curriculum architecture, chapter outline drafting, and production workflow

Problem
Several major textbook ideas existed as broad subject goals, but they did not yet have complete chapter-outline structures. The missing piece was a reproducible first-planning layer for each book: official title, course role, chapter sequence, part structure, chapter purposes, back matter expectations, companion resources, and placement in the larger MGU curriculum.

Intended Goal
Create complete chapter outline drafts for multiple MGU textbooks so each subject can move into the next production stage: master instruction sheet, chapter prompt bundle, Markdown drafting, editing, PDF conversion, and eventual MGU Library hosting.

Root Cause
The MGU textbook pipeline had grown beyond isolated textbooks. New science, computer science, business, finance, and social science books needed consistent curriculum architecture before chapter drafting could begin. Without outlines, later prompts would risk uneven depth, missing prerequisites, duplicated coverage, or unclear placement in the larger MGU pathway.

Steps Taken

1. Defined the recommended role and production approach for High School Chemistry, then created a full chapter outline draft for an algebra-based MGU science textbook covering matter, atoms, bonding, reactions, stoichiometry, gases, thermochemistry, acids and bases, redox, organic foundations, nuclear chemistry, environmental chemistry, and biological chemistry.

2. Defined the recommended role and production approach for High School Biology, then created a full chapter outline draft centered on life, cells, biomolecules, energy, genetics, evolution, classification, organisms, human systems, ecology, and Earth-life relationships.

3. Defined the recommended role and production approach for High School Earth and Environmental Science, then created a full chapter outline draft centered on Earth systems, maps and models, minerals, rocks, geologic time, plate tectonics, hazards, water, oceans, atmosphere, weather, climate, ecosystems, resources, pollution, resilience, and sustainability.

4. Defined the recommended role and production approach for Astronomy and Cosmology Foundations, then created a full chapter outline draft covering sky observation, timekeeping, telescopes, astronomical scale, light, spectra, gravity, the solar system, exoplanets, stars, galaxies, dark matter, the expanding universe, the Big Bang model, dark energy, and open cosmology questions.

5. Defined the recommended role and production approach for Computer Science Foundations, then created a full chapter outline draft covering computation, information, algorithms, programming basics, data structures, systems, hardware, operating systems, databases, networks, the web, cybersecurity, privacy, HCI, automation, data science, AI, software projects, ethics, and careers.

6. Defined the recommended role and production approach for Programming with Python, then created a full chapter outline draft covering setup, variables, input/output, Boolean logic, conditionals, loops, debugging, functions, modules, strings, lists, dictionaries, nested data, files, CSV, exceptions, automation, classes, testing, refactoring, and capstone projects.

7. Defined the recommended role and production approach for Web Development: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, then created a full chapter outline draft covering web foundations, project structure, browser tools, semantic HTML, links, images, forms, metadata, CSS selectors, cascade, box model, Flexbox, Grid, responsive design, CSS variables, JavaScript, DOM, events, forms, reusable components, JSON, accessibility, performance, publishing, and maintenance.

8. Defined the recommended role and production approach for Data, Databases, and Information Systems, then created a full chapter outline draft covering data quality, records, tables, data modeling, keys, constraints, relational design, normalization, SQL, joins, transactions, indexes, security, information systems, dashboards, workflows, enterprise systems, CSV, APIs, database-backed websites, backups, governance, ethics, and capstone system design.

9. Defined the recommended role and production approach for Economics: Principles of Microeconomics and Macroeconomics, then created a full chapter outline draft covering scarcity, opportunity cost, incentives, markets, demand, supply, equilibrium, elasticity, firms, market structures, labor markets, externalities, public goods, taxes, poverty, inequality, GDP, unemployment, inflation, AD-AS, business cycles, money, banking, monetary policy, fiscal policy, growth, trade, exchange rates, globalization, and public decision-making.

10. Defined the recommended role and production approach for Personal Finance and Financial Systems, then created a full chapter outline draft covering financial goals, income, careers, budgeting, banking, payment systems, records, fraud prevention, saving, interest, credit, debt, taxes, insurance, investing, retirement, education costs, housing, transportation, life transitions, financial institutions, economic conditions, consumer rights, ethics, and a capstone personal financial plan.

11. Standardized each outline around the MGU textbook pattern: working model, course identity, chapter parts, chapter purposes, numbered sections, chapter back matter, standard back matter model, full textbook appendices, companion resources, suggested running project where useful, suggested course placement, official title, subtitle, and course description.

Key Change
A large group of MGU textbook ideas was converted from broad subject intentions into structured, production-ready chapter outline drafts.

Outcome
MGU now has complete first-stage planning documents for major textbooks across science, computer science, economics, and finance. These outlines can be used directly to create master instruction sheets, chapter prompt bundles, Markdown chapter drafts, future PDF textbooks, and MGU Library reference pages.

Validation
Each requested textbook was checked for a coherent course role, logical chapter progression, appropriate student level, prerequisite assumptions, part structure, recurring skill spine, chapter back matter, companion resources, and placement in the broader MGU curriculum. The outlines also remained consistent with the current MGU preference for Markdown-first textbook production after Physics, while preserving the earlier layered workflow idea of outline → master instructions → chapter prompts → chapter drafts.

Rules Going Forward
Use chapter outlines as the first required planning layer before generating full textbook chapters.
Prefer Markdown-first production for new non-Physics textbooks unless there is a specific reason to use DOCX.
Keep each textbook distinct from adjacent books so introductory courses do not become overloaded or duplicative.
Include course placement, companion resources, appendices, and recurring chapter back matter in every major outline.
Use a consistent MGU structure while allowing each subject to have its own spine: laboratory spine for chemistry, diagram and systems spine for biology and Earth science, scale and evidence spine for astronomy, code/debugging spine for programming, data-modeling spine for databases, graph/data spine for economics, and decision/calculation spine for personal finance.

Notes
The next production step for any of these textbooks is to create a master instruction sheet, then a chapter prompt bundle. The strongest candidates for immediate next-stage production are the books most directly connected to MGU site-building and library growth: Computer Science Foundations, Web Development, Programming with Python, Data/Databases/Information Systems, and the science survey books.
