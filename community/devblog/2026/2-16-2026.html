Details

There are 112 lessons, 14 unit home pages, and 1 course home page. Assignments wil be added later. These lessons teach procedure. The Plan was to upgrade from 48 to 73 lessons but I went all out and made as complete of a course as possible to better prepare for Algebra 101. Lessons are brief, direct, and well organized. If a student feels comfortable moving past pre-algebra straight to algebra that is perfectly acceptable. Many early topics are repeated for retention.

Specifically Pre-algebra 96 shifts some topics from away from Algebra 101 to keep Algebra 101 lighter and more focused on advanced topics. Algebra 101 is very complete topic wise and leads almost straight into pre-calculus. Algebra 101 covers basic euclidean geometry, basic and some advanced trigonometry, and early probability/combinatorics/matrices. Shifting more early "rules of algebra" to Pre-Algebra makes it possible to gloss over early material since it has already been introduced.

The old pre-algebra course files have been moved to 'arithmetic'. They are a less complete pre-algebra course that can be expanded to serve as a general purpose arithmetic class. They will need to have their pre-algebra branding removed and replaced with Arithmetic 74 over time.

Purpose

The purpose is to build up the credibility of the Math department one course at a time. My larger math projects are coming later and will be masterpieces of academic compilation. I need the following math courses: Pre-calculus, Non-calc Statistics, Calculus, Linear Algebra, Calculus based statistics. Optional courses: HS Geometry, HS Trigonometry, College Geometry, Applied Discrete Math, Combinatorics, and Abstract algebra. Optional courses should be included even if they are minimal so as to represent all the main branches of math faithfully.
