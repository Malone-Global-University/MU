Content Production

Notes on producing lessons, textbooks, reference pages, images, and academic materials.

Purpose

The Content Production page records how academic and media content is planned, generated, edited, converted, packaged, and published for Malone Global University.

Use this section when the work concerns lessons, textbooks, ebook pages, library articles, encyclopedia entries, infographics, course maps, or large content sets.

What to Record

Content production notes should explain the structure of a project, the production method, the naming convention, the editing rule, and the expected output format.

Useful entries may include textbook workflows, Markdown chapter standards, HTML lesson templates, infographic folder taxonomy, naming conventions, conversion notes, front matter and back matter systems, and course sequencing decisions.

Suggested Entry Types

Textbook pipeline notes, lesson-template updates, image-series production rules, file-naming standards, content taxonomy decisions, Markdown conversion notes, and quality-control procedures all belong here.

Entries should make it easier to produce future content consistently without rebuilding the instructions from memory.

Connection to the Devblog

Content Production connects the educational mission of MGU to the practical work of building the site. It records how large academic systems become usable pages, files, images, and reference materials.
