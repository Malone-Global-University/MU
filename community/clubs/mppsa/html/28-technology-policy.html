Core Position

Technology and artificial intelligence (AI) are rapidly transforming society, but unequal access, biased algorithms, and underrepresentation in tech threaten to leave African American communities behind. Equity in technology is essential for economic opportunity, political influence, and cultural preservation.

Our position focuses on ensuring fair access, unbiased systems, workforce inclusion, and protections against digital harm.

Key Principles & Policy Stances

1. Digital Access & Inclusion

  • Expand high-speed internet access in underserved urban and rural African American communities.
  • Ensure affordable access to computers, software, and digital tools for students and families.
  • Support public digital literacy programs and technical training initiatives.

2. Algorithmic Bias & AI Accountability

  • Require transparency in AI systems used for hiring, policing, credit scoring, or education.
  • Mandate independent audits of AI algorithms to identify and remove racial bias.
  • Hold companies accountable for discriminatory outcomes caused by automated systems.

3. Workforce Equity in Tech

  • Promote recruitment, retention, and advancement of African Americans in STEM and AI sectors.
  • Fund mentorship programs, scholarships, and coding bootcamps for Black youth and professionals.
  • Encourage diverse leadership on boards and decision-making teams in technology companies.

4. Data Privacy & Consumer Protections

  • Protect African Americans from exploitative data collection, surveillance, and profiling.
  • Require informed consent and ethical use of personal data by corporations and governments.
  • Support legislation to prevent AI-driven discrimination in credit, housing, or employment.

5. Ethical Tech Development

  • Fund research into AI and tech that benefits historically marginalized communities.
  • Ensure community participation in defining AI ethics and priorities.
  • Encourage open-source and community-driven technological solutions for local problems.

6. Representation & Cultural Inclusion

  • Support African American content creators and developers in digital media and gaming.
  • Promote inclusive design in AI, apps, and platforms that reflect diverse experiences.
  • Prevent erasure of Black voices in technology, media, and algorithmic representation.

Position Summary

Technology and AI should empower, not marginalize, African American communities. Equity requires access, accountability, representation, and protections from harm. Our policies aim to close digital divides, remove algorithmic bias, and ensure that Black communities are fully included in the technological future.
