Core Principles

African Americans with disabilities face compounded systemic barriers due to the intersection of race and disability. Policy must prioritize equity, accessibility, and inclusion, ensuring that individuals with disabilities have opportunities for education, employment, healthcare, and full participation in community life.

Key Challenges

  • Higher rates of poverty and unemployment among Black individuals with disabilities.
  • Limited access to culturally competent healthcare and rehabilitation services.
  • Inadequate infrastructure for accessibility, including transportation and housing.
  • Educational disparities and insufficient support in schools and vocational programs.
  • Social isolation and lack of community-based support networks.
  • Underrepresentation in policymaking, advocacy, and civic leadership.

Policy Positions

1. Healthcare & Rehabilitation Access

  • Expand access to primary, specialty, and mental healthcare tailored to Black communities.
  • Fund culturally competent rehabilitation and therapy programs.
  • Support home- and community-based services for individuals with mobility challenges.

2. Education & Workforce Development

  • Ensure inclusive education with adaptive technology and resources for students with disabilities.
  • Provide vocational training, apprenticeships, and job placement programs for adults with disabilities.
  • Promote mentoring programs linking disabled youth with professionals in their field of interest.

3. Accessibility & Infrastructure

  • Implement ADA-compliant housing, public buildings, and transportation in Black communities.
  • Fund community-led projects to improve accessibility in neighborhoods.
  • Support universal design initiatives in schools, workplaces, and public spaces.

4. Economic Security

  • Provide equitable access to disability benefits, grants, and financial assistance programs.
  • Encourage small-business support and entrepreneurship for disabled individuals.
  • Protect against workplace discrimination and ensure reasonable accommodations.

5. Community Support & Inclusion

  • Fund peer support networks and community centers focused on disabled individuals.
  • Promote awareness campaigns to reduce stigma around disability in Black communities.
  • Integrate disability inclusion into all local programs, from youth initiatives to senior services.

6. Advocacy & Representation

  • Ensure Black disabled individuals are represented in policymaking and civic organizations.
  • Support training and mentorship programs for disabled leaders.
  • Monitor and evaluate policies to ensure accessibility and equity standards are met.
