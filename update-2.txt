    <h2 id="overview">Overview</h2>
    <p>
      African American musicians were central to the creation of rock and roll. The music grew from
      blues, gospel, rhythm and blues, boogie-woogie, jump blues, vocal harmony, electric guitar,
      dance music, and the performance traditions of Black churches, clubs, theaters, and touring
      circuits. Early rock and roll did not appear separate from these traditions; it emerged from
      them through stronger backbeats, amplified instruments, youth-oriented performance, and a new
      commercial language for popular music.
    </p>

    <p>
      This page is organized by broad musical era rather than by birth date alone. Some artists
      belong to more than one period, and many also belong to blues, soul, funk, gospel, R&amp;B,
      pop, or punk history. Each name is placed where that artist is most strongly associated for
      introductory navigation. Within each era, names are alphabetized by last name, stage name,
      or group name.
    </p>
