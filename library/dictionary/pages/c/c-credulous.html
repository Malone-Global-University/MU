credulous

Part of Speech: Adjective

Pronunciation: /ˈkre-jə-ləs/

Definition: Too ready to believe things; easily convinced without sufficient evidence.

Synonyms: gullible, trusting, naive, unwary

Antonyms: skeptical, discerning, critical, wary

Etymology: From Latin crēdulūs (“believing readily”), from crēdere (“to believe”).

Examples:

  • A credulous market can fuel unsustainable valuations.
  • Scammers target credulous users with sophisticated phishing.
  • Investors must avoid credulous acceptance of vanity metrics.

Explanation: Highlights the risk of low evidentiary standards in decision-making and security.
