corroborate

Part of Speech: Verb

Pronunciation: /kə-ˈrä-bə-ˌrāt/

Definition: To support with additional evidence; to confirm or make more certain.

Synonyms: confirm, substantiate, validate, verify

Antonyms: refute, contradict, rebut, undermine

Etymology: From Latin corroborāre (“to strengthen”), from robur (“strength”).

Examples:

  • Independent audits corroborated the financial statements.
  • Logs corroborate the incident timeline.
  • Customer interviews corroborated the product-market fit hypothesis.

Explanation: A core verb in research, legal, and assurance workflows indicating evidentiary support.
