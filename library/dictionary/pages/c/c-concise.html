concise

Part of Speech: Adjective

Pronunciation: /kən-ˈsīs/

Definition: Expressing much in few words while preserving clarity and completeness.

Synonyms: succinct, terse, compact, pithy

Antonyms: verbose, wordy, prolix, diffuse

Etymology: From Latin concīsus (“cut short”), from concīdere (“to cut down”).

Examples:

  • Provide a concise executive summary on page one.
  • Her concise brief enabled fast, high-confidence decisions.
  • Concise documentation accelerates onboarding.

Explanation: Valued in executive communication where time-to-clarity is critical.
