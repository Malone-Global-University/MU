cosmopolitan

Part of Speech: Adjective / Noun

Pronunciation: /ˌkäz-mə-ˈpä-lə-tən/

Definition: (adj.) Sophisticated and at ease in multiple countries or cultures; (n.) a person who is cosmopolitan.

Synonyms: worldly, international, urbane, global

Antonyms: parochial, provincial, insular, local

Etymology: From Greek kosmopolitēs (“citizen of the world”), from kosmos (“world”) + polītēs (“citizen”).

Examples:

  • Our cosmopolitan team operates seamlessly across time zones.
  • The city’s cosmopolitan vibe attracts global talent.
  • She’s a cosmopolitan who adapts quickly to new markets.

Explanation: Often used in branding and HR contexts to signal global fluency and cultural agility.
