Keiko 稽古

Deliberate practice, reflection, and structured repetition in training

Home Department Defense Martial Arts M.A.L. Keiko

Definition

Keiko refers to structured, repeated training with awareness and intention. It is not mere repetition, but practice that includes reflection, correction, and refinement of movement and understanding.

Concept

Keiko is the process by which technique becomes internalized. Through repeated exposure under controlled conditions, the practitioner develops timing, structure, and awareness. It bridges knowledge and embodiment.

Functional Role

  • Transforms conceptual knowledge into physical ability
  • Builds consistency in structure, timing, and movement
  • Allows identification and correction of errors
  • Integrates principles such as Ma-ai, Sen, and Kuzushi into action

Related Concepts

  • Shuhari (Learning Progression)
  • Kihon → Oyo → Henka
  • Mushin (No-Mind)
  • Fudoshin (Immovable Mind)

Application Across Arts

In aikido, Keiko is the repeated practice of technique under controlled attack and response conditions. In karate, it is kata and drilling under variation. In judo and jujutsu, it is repetition of throws, entries, and positional control under resistance. Across all systems, Keiko is the mechanism of embodied learning.

Common Misunderstanding

Keiko is often mistaken for mindless repetition. In reality, it requires active attention and correction. Without awareness, Keiko becomes mechanical repetition rather than developmental practice.
