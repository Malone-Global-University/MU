Arithmetic Overview

Arithmetic studies numbers and the basic operations used to combine, compare, measure, and transform quantities. It provides the foundation for mathematical fluency by explaining how addition, subtraction, multiplication, division, fractions, decimals, ratios, proportions, percentages, and number properties work together in calculation. This discipline is essential because nearly every later area of mathematics depends on a reliable understanding of numerical relationships, place value, equivalence, estimation, and operational reasoning. By studying arithmetic, learners develop the skills needed to compute accurately, recognize patterns, interpret quantities, solve practical problems, and prepare for algebra, geometry, statistics, and applied mathematics.

Categories

  • Basic Operations

    Study addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division as the core operations used to combine quantities, compare values, scale numbers, and solve numerical problems.

  • Fractions and Decimals

    Learn how parts of wholes and place-value expressions represent quantities, including equivalence, conversion, comparison, operations, simplification, and practical measurement.

  • Number Properties

    Explore properties of numbers and operations, including factors, multiples, primes, divisibility, parity, place value, commutativity, associativity, distributivity, and numerical patterns.

  • Percentages

    Study percentages as ratios out of one hundred, including percent change, discounts, interest, proportions, comparisons, conversions, and real-world quantitative interpretation.

  • Ratios and Proportions

    Examine relationships between quantities through ratios, rates, proportions, scaling, unit rates, equivalent relationships, and proportional reasoning used in practical problem solving.
