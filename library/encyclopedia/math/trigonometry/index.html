Trigonometry Overview

Trigonometry studies the relationships among angles, side lengths, circles, and periodic functions. It began as a tool for measuring triangles and has expanded into a major mathematical language for describing waves, rotation, oscillation, sound, light, navigation, engineering, physics, and repeating patterns. This discipline includes trigonometric functions, identities, equations, inverse trigonometry, and applications, each of which helps learners connect geometric relationships to algebraic and graphical models. By studying trigonometry, learners gain tools for solving angle and distance problems, analyzing periodic behavior, modeling circular motion, and preparing for calculus, physics, engineering, and advanced applied mathematics.

Categories

  • Applications

    Study how trigonometry is used in measurement, navigation, physics, engineering, architecture, waves, sound, light, circular motion, surveying, and real-world problem solving.

  • Equations

    Learn how to solve trigonometric equations involving angles, identities, periodic solutions, inverse functions, unit circle values, graphs, and algebraic transformations.

  • Identities

    Explore trigonometric identities that express equivalent relationships among sine, cosine, tangent, reciprocal functions, angle sums, double angles, and Pythagorean relationships.

  • Inverse Trigonometry

    Study inverse trigonometric functions, including arcsine, arccosine, arctangent, restricted domains, angle recovery, graph behavior, and solving for unknown angles.

  • Trigonometric Functions

    Examine sine, cosine, tangent, and related functions through right triangles, the unit circle, graphs, amplitude, period, phase shift, and periodic behavior.
