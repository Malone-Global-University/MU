Statistics and Probability Overview

Statistics and probability studies how data are collected, summarized, modeled, interpreted, and used to reason under uncertainty. Probability provides mathematical tools for describing chance, randomness, likelihood, and uncertain outcomes, while statistics uses data to estimate patterns, test claims, compare groups, model relationships, and support decisions. This discipline is essential in science, business, medicine, public policy, education, finance, engineering, and everyday reasoning because modern decisions often depend on incomplete information. By studying statistics and probability, learners gain methods for describing data, measuring variation, making inferences from samples, evaluating hypotheses, understanding risk, and building models that connect evidence to conclusions.

Categories

  • Descriptive Statistics

    Study methods for organizing and summarizing data, including tables, graphs, measures of center, measures of spread, distributions, outliers, and descriptive comparisons.

  • Hypothesis Testing

    Learn how statistical claims are evaluated using null hypotheses, alternative hypotheses, significance levels, test statistics, p-values, confidence, errors, and decision rules.

  • Inferential Statistics

    Explore how sample data are used to estimate population values, measure uncertainty, build confidence intervals, compare groups, and draw conclusions beyond observed data.

  • Probability Theory

    Examine the mathematical study of chance, including sample spaces, events, conditional probability, independence, random variables, probability distributions, expectation, and uncertainty.

  • Regression Analysis

    Study methods for modeling relationships between variables, including correlation, linear regression, prediction, residuals, explanatory variables, response variables, and model interpretation.
