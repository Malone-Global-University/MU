Linear Algebra Overview

Linear algebra studies mathematical objects and relationships that can be represented through vectors, matrices, systems of linear equations, transformations, and abstract vector spaces. It provides a language for working with multidimensional quantities, making it essential for computer graphics, engineering, physics, data science, machine learning, economics, statistics, and many areas of applied mathematics. This discipline explains how quantities can be organized into arrays, how transformations move or reshape space, how determinants measure scaling and orientation, and how eigenvalues and eigenvectors reveal stable directions within linear systems. By studying linear algebra, learners gain tools for representing complex relationships in compact form, solving large systems efficiently, and understanding the structure of high-dimensional mathematical models.

Categories

  • Determinants

    Study scalar values associated with square matrices, including area and volume scaling, invertibility, orientation, linear independence, and their role in solving systems.

  • Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

    Explore special vectors and scaling factors that reveal stable directions, transformation behavior, matrix structure, dynamic systems, dimensional reduction, and long-term patterns.

  • Matrices

    Learn how rectangular arrays of numbers represent systems, transformations, data, coefficients, operations, and relationships across multiple variables or dimensions.

  • Vector Spaces

    Examine abstract spaces of vectors, including span, basis, dimension, subspaces, linear independence, linear combinations, and the structural rules that define vector systems.

  • Vectors

    Study quantities with magnitude and direction, including components, operations, dot products, linear combinations, geometric interpretation, and applications in multidimensional systems.
