Security Systems Overview

Security systems study the protection of digital assets, computational infrastructure, data, and communications against unauthorized access, misuse, disruption, and attack. The field examines defensive technologies, security protocols, risk management, and operational response. Across cybersecurity, cryptography, forensics, network security, application security, and security operations, the discipline governs digital trust and resilience.

Disciplines

  • Application Security

    Study protection of software applications against vulnerabilities and exploitation.

  • Cryptography

    Examine mathematical systems for secure communication, encryption, and authentication.

  • Cybersecurity

    Study defensive strategies and technologies for protecting digital systems from threats.

  • Digital Forensics

    Learn investigation and analysis of digital evidence after incidents or attacks.

  • Network Security

    Study defense of network infrastructure against intrusion, attack, and misuse.

  • Security Operations

    Explore ongoing monitoring, detection, response, and management of security events.
