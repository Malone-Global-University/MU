Human Interaction Systems Overview

Human interaction systems study how users engage with digital systems through interfaces, experiences, accessibility structures, and interaction design. The field combines psychology, design, usability, and engineering to improve communication between humans and machines. Across HCI, interface design, UX, accessibility, and interaction models, the discipline focuses on making systems understandable, usable, and effective.

Disciplines

  • Accessibility Systems

    Study design and engineering practices that make systems usable across diverse abilities.

  • Human-Computer Interaction

    Examine theory and design of interactions between users and computational systems.

  • Interaction Models

    Study conceptual frameworks describing how users engage with digital systems.

  • User Experience Design

    Learn design of complete user journeys emphasizing usability, utility, and satisfaction.

  • User Interface Design

    Study creation of visible controls, layouts, and interaction surfaces for digital systems.
