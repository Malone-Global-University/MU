Mythology Overview

Mythology studies the symbolic stories, divine figures, heroic cycles, creation accounts, underworld journeys, cosmic conflicts, and sacred narratives through which cultures interpret the world and human life. This discipline includes Greek mythology, Norse mythology, Egyptian mythology, comparative mythology, and mythological symbolism, each of which helps explain how societies use narrative to organize memory, morality, identity, nature, death, power, and the unknown. Myths are not merely fictional tales; they often function as cultural frameworks that preserve values, explain origins, dramatize cosmic order, and give visible form to spiritual or philosophical ideas. By studying mythology, learners gain a framework for understanding how stories shape religion, literature, art, ritual, politics, psychology, and civilization across time.
