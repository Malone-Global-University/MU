Dharmic Religions Overview

Dharmic religions studies religious traditions that developed in South Asian contexts and share major concerns with dharma, karma, rebirth, liberation, spiritual discipline, ethical conduct, ritual practice, and the nature of reality. This discipline includes Hinduism, Buddhism, and Jainism, each of which developed distinct philosophical systems, sacred texts, communities, practices, and paths of spiritual transformation. Although these traditions differ in their views of self, divinity, ritual, authority, liberation, and metaphysics, they also share overlapping historical environments and long patterns of intellectual exchange. By studying Dharmic religions, learners gain a framework for understanding how South Asian religious thought shaped philosophy, meditation, ethics, art, literature, social life, and global religious practice.

Categories

  • Buddhism

    Study Buddhist philosophy, texts, schools, meditation, ethics, monastic traditions, ritual practice, and teachings on suffering, impermanence, non-self, compassion, and liberation.

  • Hinduism

    Explore Hindu traditions, including Vedic studies, philosophy, ritual practice, devotional movements, sacred texts, sects, temple worship, ethics, cosmology, and spiritual paths.

  • Jainism

    Examine Jain religious thought and practice, including nonviolence, karma, asceticism, liberation, ethical discipline, monastic communities, cosmology, scripture, and traditions of spiritual restraint.
