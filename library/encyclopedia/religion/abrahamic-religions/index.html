Abrahamic Religions Overview

Abrahamic religions studies the religious traditions that trace their spiritual lineage to Abraham and share major concerns with revelation, covenant, prophecy, scripture, worship, law, ethics, community, and the relationship between humanity and God. This discipline includes Christianity, Islam, and Judaism, each of which developed distinct sacred texts, theological systems, historical institutions, interpretive traditions, rituals, and communal identities. Although these religions differ significantly in doctrine, authority, worship, and religious law, they also share overlapping historical contexts, scriptural figures, ethical themes, and long patterns of interaction. By studying Abrahamic religions, learners gain a framework for understanding how these traditions shaped theology, philosophy, law, culture, politics, civilization, and religious life across much of world history.

  • Judaism
