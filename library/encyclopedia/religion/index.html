Religion Overview

Religion is the study of systems of belief, worship, sacred tradition, theology, ritual practice, spiritual philosophy, and humanity’s understanding of the divine, transcendent, or sacred. It examines how religious communities develop doctrines, preserve scriptures, organize institutions, interpret revelation, and shape moral, cultural, and civilizational life. Religious study spans comparative analysis of world traditions, theological reasoning, sacred texts, mythological systems, ethical thought, and the historical development of faith communities.

Categories

  • Religious Foundations

    Study the philosophical, theological, epistemological, and comparative foundations of religious belief and sacred thought.

  • Abrahamic Religions

    Explore the traditions, scriptures, theology, history, and sects of Christianity, Islam, and Judaism.

  • Dharmic Religions

    Study the religious and philosophical traditions of Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and related dharmic systems.

  • East Asian Religions

    Examine Confucianism, Taoism, Shinto, and East Asian folk religious traditions.

  • Indigenous and Traditional Religions

    Explore indigenous spiritual systems, shamanic traditions, animistic belief structures, and traditional religious practices.

  • Mythology

    Study mythological traditions, symbolic narratives, comparative mythology, and sacred legendary systems across cultures.

  • Religious History

    Analyze the historical development of religions, reformations, schisms, missionary movements, and religion’s role in civilization.

  • Ethics and Religious Thought

    Examine moral theology, spiritual ethics, virtue traditions, doctrine, and applied religious ethical reasoning.

  • Apologetics and Polemics

    Study methods of defending, critiquing, debating, and comparing religious truth claims and faith systems.
