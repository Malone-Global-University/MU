Rehabilitation and Supportive Care Overview

Rehabilitation and supportive care studies the medical, therapeutic, and interdisciplinary approaches used to restore function, reduce suffering, support independence, manage symptoms, and improve quality of life. This discipline includes physical medicine, occupational therapy, pain management, palliative care, and rehabilitation medicine, each of which addresses a different aspect of patient recovery and long-term support. It is especially important for patients recovering from injury, surgery, stroke, chronic illness, neurological disease, musculoskeletal impairment, disability, or serious progressive illness. By studying rehabilitation and supportive care, learners gain a framework for understanding how healthcare continues beyond diagnosis and acute treatment, focusing instead on practical function, comfort, adaptation, dignity, and sustained participation in daily life.

Categories

  • Occupational Therapy

    Study therapeutic approaches that help patients perform daily activities, adapt environments, rebuild skills, use assistive tools, and maintain independence after illness, injury, or disability.

  • Pain Management

    Explore the assessment and treatment of acute and chronic pain through medications, procedures, rehabilitation, behavioral strategies, interdisciplinary care, and quality-of-life support.

  • Palliative Care

    Learn how serious illness care supports comfort, symptom relief, communication, decision-making, emotional support, family needs, and quality of life alongside or beyond curative treatment.

  • Physical Medicine

    Examine medical care focused on restoring physical function, mobility, strength, coordination, and participation after injury, neurological illness, musculoskeletal disease, or disabling conditions.

  • Rehabilitation Medicine

    Study coordinated medical rehabilitation for patients recovering from stroke, trauma, surgery, spinal cord injury, chronic disease, disability, and complex functional limitations.
