Mental and Behavioral Health Overview

Mental and behavioral health studies the diagnosis, treatment, prevention, and support of conditions that affect thought, emotion, behavior, relationships, identity, coping, and daily functioning. This discipline includes psychiatry, clinical psychology, behavioral health, psychotherapy, and addiction medicine, each of which approaches mental health from a different professional, clinical, or therapeutic perspective. It connects biological, psychological, social, developmental, and environmental factors to understand why symptoms arise, how they affect patients, and how treatment can support recovery and improved quality of life. By studying mental and behavioral health, learners gain a framework for understanding mental disorders, therapeutic care, behavioral change, substance use conditions, crisis intervention, patient support, and the relationship between mental health and whole-person medical care.

Categories

  • Addiction Medicine

    Study the medical treatment of substance use disorders and addictive behaviors, including withdrawal, medication-assisted treatment, relapse prevention, harm reduction, recovery support, and co-occurring conditions.

  • Behavioral Health

    Explore the connection between behavior, mental health, physical health, coping patterns, lifestyle, prevention, treatment adherence, emotional regulation, and integrated care.

  • Clinical Psychology

    Examine psychological assessment, diagnosis, therapy, testing, research-based treatment, emotional functioning, cognitive patterns, personality, and mental health care across diverse populations.

  • Psychiatry

    Learn about the medical diagnosis and treatment of mental disorders, including psychiatric evaluation, medication management, biological factors, crisis care, and coordination with therapy and support services.

  • Psychotherapy

    Study structured therapeutic approaches used to address emotional distress, thought patterns, behavior, trauma, relationships, coping, identity, and long-term psychological growth.
