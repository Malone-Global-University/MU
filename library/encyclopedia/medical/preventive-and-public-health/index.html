Preventive and Public Health Overview

Preventive and public health studies how disease, injury, disability, and health risks are prevented across individuals, communities, and populations. This discipline examines patterns of disease, strategies for prevention, public health programs, policy decisions, community interventions, and global health challenges that shape the well-being of large groups. Unlike clinical medicine, which often focuses on diagnosing and treating individual patients after illness appears, preventive and public health emphasizes risk reduction, early detection, health promotion, surveillance, access, equity, and coordinated action before problems become widespread. By studying preventive and public health, learners gain a framework for understanding how medical knowledge, data, policy, education, and community systems work together to improve health outcomes at scale.

Categories

  • Community Health

    Study health promotion and disease prevention within communities, including local needs assessment, outreach, education, access barriers, health equity, and community-based interventions.

  • Epidemiology

    Examine how diseases and health outcomes are distributed across populations, including incidence, prevalence, risk factors, outbreaks, surveillance, study design, and population-level evidence.

  • Global Health

    Explore health challenges across countries and regions, including infectious disease, health systems, inequality, maternal and child health, humanitarian crises, environmental risks, and international cooperation.

  • Health Policy

    Learn how laws, institutions, funding decisions, regulations, insurance systems, public programs, and political choices shape healthcare access, quality, cost, and population health.

  • Preventive Medicine

    Study medical strategies that reduce risk before disease progresses, including screening, vaccination, lifestyle counseling, risk assessment, early intervention, and population-based prevention.
