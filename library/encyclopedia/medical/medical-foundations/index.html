Medical Foundations Overview

Medical foundations introduces the essential concepts, vocabulary, body systems, disease processes, and medication principles needed to understand healthcare and clinical decision-making. This discipline provides the groundwork for studying clinical medicine, surgery, diagnostics, public health, mental health, pediatrics, reproductive health, rehabilitation, medical specialties, and healthcare systems. It explains how the body is organized, how normal function is maintained, how disease disrupts that function, how medical language communicates precise information, and how drugs affect biological processes. By studying medical foundations, learners gain the basic framework needed to interpret symptoms, understand diagnoses, follow treatment logic, and connect scientific knowledge to patient care.

Categories

  • Anatomy

    Study the structure of the human body, including organs, tissues, bones, muscles, nerves, vessels, body regions, anatomical relationships, and the organization of major systems.

  • Medical Terminology

    Learn the language of medicine, including roots, prefixes, suffixes, abbreviations, body system terms, diagnostic language, procedural vocabulary, and clinical documentation terms.

  • Pathology

    Examine the study of disease, including causes, mechanisms, tissue changes, inflammation, injury, infection, degeneration, neoplasia, and how disease alters normal function.

  • Pharmacology

    Explore how medications affect the body, including drug classes, mechanisms of action, dosing, metabolism, therapeutic effects, side effects, interactions, and safe medication use.

  • Physiology

    Study how the body functions, including organ systems, cellular processes, homeostasis, circulation, respiration, digestion, movement, signaling, regulation, and coordinated biological activity.
