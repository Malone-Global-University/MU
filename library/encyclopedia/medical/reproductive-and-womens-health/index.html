Reproductive and Womens Health Overview

Reproductive and womens health studies the medical care of reproductive systems, pregnancy, childbirth, fertility, hormonal function, and conditions affecting gynecologic and maternal health. This discipline includes obstetrics, gynecology, reproductive endocrinology, maternal-fetal medicine, and fertility medicine, each of which addresses a different part of reproductive care across life stages. It connects anatomy, physiology, endocrinology, preventive care, diagnostics, surgery, prenatal monitoring, childbirth management, and long-term health support. By studying reproductive and womens health, learners gain a framework for understanding how medical professionals care for patients before pregnancy, during pregnancy, after childbirth, through reproductive transitions, and across conditions involving fertility, hormones, pelvic health, and reproductive disease.

Categories

  • Fertility Medicine

    Study the evaluation and treatment of infertility, including reproductive testing, ovulation support, assisted reproductive technologies, fertility preservation, donor options, and patient counseling.

  • Gynecology

    Explore medical and surgical care of the female reproductive system, including preventive exams, menstrual disorders, pelvic pain, infections, contraception, menopause, and gynecologic conditions.

  • Maternal-Fetal Medicine

    Examine specialized care for high-risk pregnancies, including maternal conditions, fetal complications, prenatal diagnosis, pregnancy monitoring, risk management, and coordinated obstetric care.

  • Obstetrics

    Learn how pregnancy, labor, delivery, postpartum recovery, prenatal care, fetal monitoring, complications, and childbirth-related medical decisions are managed.

  • Reproductive Endocrinology

    Study hormones that regulate reproduction, including ovulation, menstrual cycles, fertility, puberty, menopause, endocrine disorders, and hormone-related reproductive conditions.
