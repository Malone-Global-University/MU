Environmental Science Overview

Environmental science studies the relationships among ecosystems, natural resources, human activity, pollution, conservation, sustainability, and environmental policy. This discipline combines biology, chemistry, earth science, ecology, toxicology, public policy, and systems thinking to understand how natural and human-made systems affect one another. It includes conservation, environmental policy, environmental toxicology, sustainability, and waste management, each of which addresses a major challenge in protecting ecological integrity and human well-being. By studying environmental science, learners gain a framework for understanding biodiversity loss, resource use, pollution control, environmental risk, sustainable development, ecosystem protection, and the scientific basis for responsible environmental decision-making.

Categories

  • Conservation

    Study the protection of species, habitats, ecosystems, biodiversity, natural resources, and ecological processes through preservation, restoration, management, and science-based environmental planning.

  • Environmental Policy

    Examine laws, regulations, agreements, institutions, incentives, and public decisions used to manage pollution, protect resources, reduce environmental harm, and guide sustainable development.

  • Environmental Toxicology

    Explore how chemicals, pollutants, toxins, heavy metals, pesticides, industrial waste, and environmental exposures affect organisms, ecosystems, food chains, and human health.

  • Sustainability

    Learn how societies balance environmental protection, resource use, economic activity, equity, resilience, and long-term ecological health across local and global systems.

  • Waste Management

    Study systems for reducing, collecting, treating, recycling, composting, storing, and disposing of waste while minimizing pollution, health risks, and environmental damage.
