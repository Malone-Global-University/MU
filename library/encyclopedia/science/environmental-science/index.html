Environmental Science Overview

Environmental science is the interdisciplinary study of natural systems, ecological processes, and the interactions between human activity and the environment. It examines how physical, biological, and chemical systems shape ecosystems and how those systems are affected by resource use, pollution, climate change, and environmental management. By integrating principles from biology, chemistry, geology, and ecology, environmental science seeks to understand environmental challenges and develop informed strategies for sustainability, conservation, and responsible stewardship of the natural world.

Categories

  • Conservation

    Study the protection, restoration, and sustainable management of ecosystems, biodiversity, wildlife populations, and natural resources.

  • Environmental Policy

    Examine the laws, regulations, governance structures, and policy frameworks used to manage environmental issues and ecological risk.

  • Environmental Toxicology

    Investigate the effects of pollutants, hazardous substances, and chemical contaminants on organisms, ecosystems, and environmental health.

  • Sustainability

    Explore strategies for balancing environmental stewardship, economic development, and long-term resource viability across human systems.

  • Waste Management

    Study the collection, treatment, recycling, disposal, and environmental handling of waste materials across industrial and public systems.
