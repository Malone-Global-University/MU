Earth Science Overview

Earth science is the scientific study of Earth’s structure, systems, processes, and history. It examines the dynamic interactions between the planet’s interior, surface, oceans, atmosphere, and biosphere to explain how Earth forms, changes, and sustains life. Through the study of geology, meteorology, oceanography, and related disciplines, earth science investigates natural forces such as tectonics, erosion, climate, and planetary evolution. By analyzing Earth as an interconnected system, the field provides critical insight into the processes that shape the planet’s past, present, and future.

Categories

  • Atmospheric Science

    Study Earth’s atmosphere including weather systems, climate dynamics, atmospheric composition, circulation patterns, and meteorological processes.

  • Geology

    Examine the composition, structure, history, and processes of Earth’s solid materials including rocks, minerals, tectonics, and crustal formation.

  • Hydrology

    Explore the movement, distribution, and properties of water across Earth’s surface, subsurface, and atmospheric systems.

  • Oceanography

    Investigate the physical, chemical, biological, and geological systems of Earth’s oceans and marine environments.

  • Paleontology

    Study prehistoric life through fossil evidence to understand the history of organisms, ecosystems, and evolutionary development on Earth.
