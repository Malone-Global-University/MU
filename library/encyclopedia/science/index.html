Science Overview

Science is the systematic study of the natural world through observation, experimentation, measurement, and reasoned analysis. It seeks to develop reliable knowledge by identifying patterns, testing hypotheses, and constructing evidence-based explanations for physical, biological, and environmental phenomena. Across its many disciplines, science provides structured methods for understanding how the universe operates, from the smallest particles of matter to the largest cosmic systems. As a framework for organized inquiry, science forms the foundation of modern knowledge, technological advancement, and empirical understanding of reality.

Categories

  • Astronomy

    Study celestial bodies, cosmic systems, planetary science, stellar evolution, galactic structures, and the large-scale nature of the universe.

  • Biology

    Study living organisms and life processes including cellular systems, genetics, evolution, physiology, ecology, and the diversity of plant, animal, and microbial life.

  • Chemistry

    Examine the composition, structure, properties, and transformations of matter through chemical reactions, atomic theory, molecular behavior, and material interactions.

  • Earth Science

    Investigate the structure, history, and dynamic systems of Earth including geology, atmosphere, oceans, climate, hydrology, and planetary processes.

  • Environmental Science

    Analyze interactions between natural systems and human activity including conservation, sustainability, environmental impacts, and ecosystem management.

  • Physics

    Explore the fundamental laws governing matter, energy, force, motion, space, and time across classical, quantum, and relativistic systems.

  • Scientific Foundations

    Learn the principles and methodologies underlying scientific inquiry including the scientific method, laboratory practices, measurement, experimentation, and research design.
