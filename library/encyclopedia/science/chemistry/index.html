Chemistry Overview

Chemistry is the scientific study of matter, its composition, structure, properties, and the transformations it undergoes. It examines how atoms and molecules interact, bond, react, and reorganize to form the substances that make up the physical world. Through the study of chemical principles, energy transfer, and reaction mechanisms, chemistry explains the behavior of materials ranging from simple elements to complex biological compounds. As the central science connecting physics, biology, and the applied sciences, chemistry provides the foundation for understanding material processes throughout nature and technology.

Categories

  • Analytical Chemistry

    Study the techniques and methods used to identify, separate, quantify, and characterize chemical substances and material composition.

  • Biochemistry

    Examine the chemical foundations of biological systems including proteins, nucleic acids, metabolism, enzymatic reactions, and biomolecular pathways.

  • Inorganic Chemistry

    Explore the structure, properties, and reactions of inorganic compounds including metals, minerals, salts, coordination complexes, and catalysts.

  • Organic Chemistry

    Study carbon-based compounds including hydrocarbons, functional groups, reaction mechanisms, synthesis pathways, and molecular structure.

  • Physical Chemistry

    Investigate the physical principles governing chemical systems including thermodynamics, kinetics, quantum behavior, and molecular energetics.

  • Polymer Chemistry

    Examine the synthesis, structure, properties, and applications of polymers, plastics, elastomers, and macromolecular materials.

  • Quantum Chemistry

    Study chemical behavior through quantum mechanical models of atomic and molecular structure, electron behavior, and bond formation.

  • Thermochemistry

    Explore heat transfer and energy changes associated with chemical reactions, phase transitions, and thermodynamic chemical systems.
