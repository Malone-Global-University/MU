Electric Charge

Electric charge is a fundamental property of matter that causes objects to experience electric forces and interact through electric fields.

Overview

Electric charge is one of the basic properties of matter. It comes in two kinds, positive and negative, and it is responsible for electric forces, electric fields, electric currents, circuits, and many electromagnetic effects. Charge is conserved, meaning the total charge in an isolated system remains constant even when charge moves from one object to another.

Positive and Negative Charge

Electric charge has two signs, positive and negative. Like charges repel each other, while opposite charges attract each other. This simple rule underlies static electricity, atomic structure, electric force, and circuit behavior.

Charge Carriers

In ordinary matter, protons carry positive charge and electrons carry negative charge. In metals, electrons can move relatively freely and serve as the main charge carriers in electric current.

Conservation of Charge

Charge cannot be created or destroyed in ordinary physical processes. It can be transferred, separated, or redistributed, but the total charge of an isolated system remains the same.

Quantization of Charge

Electric charge is quantized, meaning it appears in discrete amounts. Ordinary observable charge is built from multiples of the elementary charge.

Static Electricity

Static electricity involves an imbalance or separation of charge. Rubbing, contact, and induction can move charge between objects or redistribute it across surfaces.

Conductors and Insulators

Conductors allow charge to move relatively easily, while insulators resist charge motion. Metals are common conductors, while rubber, glass, plastic, and dry air are common insulators.

Charging Methods

Objects can be charged by friction, conduction, or induction. Friction transfers charge through rubbing, conduction transfers charge through contact, and induction redistributes charge without direct contact.

Common Mistakes

A common mistake is thinking positive charge means an object gained protons in ordinary charging. Most everyday charging happens through electron transfer, not proton movement.

Why This Matters in Physics

Electric charge is the starting point for electricity and magnetism. It prepares students for electric force, electric fields, voltage, current, circuits, capacitance, and electromagnetism.
