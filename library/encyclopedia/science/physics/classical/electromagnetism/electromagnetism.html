Electromagnetism

Electromagnetism is the unified study of electric and magnetic phenomena and their interactions.

Overview

Electromagnetism connects electric charge, electric fields, magnetic fields, currents, forces, induction, light, and electromagnetic waves. Electric and magnetic effects are not separate topics in the deeper theory. They are related aspects of one electromagnetic interaction.

Electric and Magnetic Connection

Electric charges create electric fields. Moving charges and currents create magnetic fields. Changing magnetic fields can create electric fields, and changing electric fields are connected to magnetic fields.

Currents and Magnetism

An electric current produces a magnetic field around the conductor. Coils of wire can strengthen and shape this magnetic field.

Electromagnets

An electromagnet uses electric current to produce magnetism. Turning the current on or off controls the magnetic field.

Maxwell's Unification

Maxwell's equations unified electricity, magnetism, and light by showing that changing electric and magnetic fields can sustain electromagnetic waves.

Electromagnetic Waves

Electromagnetic waves include radio waves, microwaves, infrared, visible light, ultraviolet, X-rays, and gamma rays.

Technology

Electromagnetism underlies motors, generators, transformers, radio, antennas, power grids, electronics, MRI, speakers, sensors, and communication systems.

Force and Energy

Electromagnetic systems transfer energy and exert forces through fields. These effects power machines, transmit signals, and structure matter.

Common Mistakes

A common mistake is treating electricity and magnetism as unrelated. Moving charges and changing fields connect the two.

Why This Matters in Physics

Electromagnetism is one of the major pillars of physics and one of the foundations of modern technology.
