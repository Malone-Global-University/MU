Physics Overview

Physics studies the fundamental behavior of matter, energy, motion, forces, fields, waves, particles, and spacetime. This discipline seeks to explain how objects move, how energy is transferred, how forces act, how light and sound behave, how atoms and nuclei are structured, and how the universe operates from the smallest particles to the largest cosmic scales. It includes acoustics, astrophysics, atomic physics, classical mechanics, electromagnetism, nuclear physics, optics, particle physics, quantum mechanics, relativity, statistical mechanics, and thermodynamics, each of which provides a different way to investigate physical reality. By studying physics, learners gain a framework for understanding natural laws, mathematical modeling, experimental measurement, technological systems, and the physical principles behind engineering, astronomy, chemistry, materials science, and modern scientific discovery.

Categories

  • Acoustics

    Study sound, vibration, resonance, wave propagation, hearing, musical acoustics, noise control, and the behavior of mechanical waves in gases, liquids, and solids.

  • Astrophysics

    Explore the physical laws governing stars, galaxies, black holes, cosmic radiation, planetary systems, dark matter, dark energy, and the large-scale behavior of the universe.

  • Atomic Physics

    Examine the structure and behavior of atoms, including electrons, energy levels, spectra, atomic transitions, ionization, quantum states, and interactions with light.

  • Classical Mechanics

    Learn how forces, motion, energy, momentum, rotation, gravity, equilibrium, and mechanical systems are described through Newtonian principles and mathematical models.

  • Electromagnetism

    Study electric and magnetic fields, charges, currents, circuits, electromagnetic waves, induction, radiation, and the unified laws governing electrical and magnetic phenomena.

  • Nuclear Physics

    Explore atomic nuclei, including protons, neutrons, nuclear forces, radioactivity, fission, fusion, nuclear reactions, decay processes, and applications in energy and medicine.

  • Optics

    Examine light and its behavior, including reflection, refraction, lenses, mirrors, interference, diffraction, polarization, imaging, lasers, and optical instruments.

  • Particle Physics

    Study fundamental particles and interactions, including quarks, leptons, bosons, particle accelerators, conservation laws, the Standard Model, and high-energy physics experiments.

  • Quantum Mechanics

    Learn the physics of microscopic systems, including wave-particle behavior, superposition, uncertainty, quantum states, operators, measurement, tunneling, and atomic-scale phenomena.

  • Relativity

    Study special and general relativity, including spacetime, time dilation, length contraction, mass-energy equivalence, gravity as curvature, black holes, and cosmological implications.

  • Statistical Mechanics

    Explore how large-scale physical properties emerge from many particles, including probability, microstates, entropy, temperature, distributions, gases, and links between mechanics and thermodynamics.

  • Thermodynamics

    Study heat, work, temperature, energy transfer, entropy, engines, equilibrium, phase changes, and the laws governing macroscopic physical systems.
