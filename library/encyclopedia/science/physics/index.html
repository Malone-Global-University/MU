Physics Overview

Physics is the scientific study of matter, energy, motion, forces, and the fundamental laws governing the universe. It seeks to explain how physical systems behave across all scales, from subatomic particles to galaxies, through mathematical models and empirical observation. By examining principles such as mechanics, electromagnetism, thermodynamics, and quantum behavior, physics provides the foundational framework for understanding the structure and dynamics of the physical world. As the most fundamental natural science, it underpins and informs nearly every other scientific discipline.

Categories

  • Acoustics

    Study the generation, transmission, behavior, and applications of sound waves in gases, liquids, solids, and engineered systems.

  • Astrophysics

    Apply physical laws to the study of stars, galaxies, black holes, cosmic radiation, and large-scale structures of the universe.

  • Atomic Physics

    Examine the structure, behavior, and interactions of atoms including electron configurations, spectra, and atomic-scale phenomena.

  • Classical Mechanics

    Explore motion, force, momentum, energy, and the behavior of macroscopic bodies under Newtonian and Lagrangian frameworks.

  • Electromagnetism

    Study electric and magnetic fields, charges, currents, circuits, waves, and the unified behavior of electromagnetic phenomena.

  • Nuclear Physics

    Investigate the structure, behavior, and reactions of atomic nuclei including radioactivity, fission, fusion, and nuclear forces.

  • Optics

    Explore the behavior of light including reflection, refraction, lenses, wave optics, optical systems, and photonic technologies.

  • Particle Physics

    Study the fundamental particles and interactions that compose matter and govern subatomic physical reality.

  • Quantum Mechanics

    Examine the probabilistic and wave-based behavior of matter and energy at microscopic scales including superposition and quantum states.

  • Relativity

    Study the relativistic structure of spacetime, gravity, motion, and the behavior of matter and energy at high velocities and massive scales.

  • Statistical Mechanics

    Analyze the aggregate behavior of particle systems using probabilistic methods to explain thermodynamic and emergent macroscopic properties.

  • Thermodynamics

    Explore heat, work, energy transfer, entropy, and the laws governing thermal systems and energetic transformations.
