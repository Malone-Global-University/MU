Biology Overview

Biology is the scientific study of living organisms and the processes that sustain, regulate, and evolve life. It examines the structure, function, growth, origin, and interactions of life across all scales, from molecular mechanisms within cells to ecosystems spanning the planet. Through the study of genetics, physiology, ecology, and evolution, biology seeks to explain how organisms develop, adapt, reproduce, and interact with their environments. As the foundational science of life, biology provides the framework for understanding the complexity, diversity, and continuity of living systems on Earth.

Categories

  • Biochemistry

    Study the chemical processes and molecular interactions that enable life, including metabolism, enzymes, biomolecules, and cellular chemistry.

  • Bioinformatics

    Explore the computational analysis of biological data including genomic sequencing, protein modeling, biological databases, and systems biology.

  • Biotechnology

    Examine the application of biological systems and organisms in technology, medicine, agriculture, and industrial production.

  • Botany

    Study plant life including plant anatomy, physiology, classification, reproduction, ecology, and evolutionary development.

  • Cell Biology

    Investigate the structure, function, and behavior of cells as the fundamental units of life across all organisms.

  • Developmental Biology

    Explore how organisms grow and develop from fertilization through maturity, including differentiation, morphogenesis, and developmental regulation.

  • Ecology

    Analyze interactions between organisms and their environments including ecosystems, population dynamics, biodiversity, and environmental adaptation.

  • Evolutionary Biology

    Study the mechanisms and patterns of biological evolution including natural selection, adaptation, speciation, and phylogenetics.

  • Genetics

    Examine heredity, gene expression, DNA structure, inheritance patterns, mutation, and genomic organization.

  • Human Biology

    Study the biological structure and function of the human organism including anatomy, physiology, genetics, and human development.

  • Marine Biology

    Explore life within marine environments including ocean ecosystems, aquatic organisms, marine adaptations, and oceanic ecological processes.

  • Microbiology

    Investigate microscopic organisms including bacteria, viruses, fungi, protozoa, and their biological roles and behaviors.

  • Molecular Biology

    Examine biological activity at the molecular level including DNA replication, transcription, translation, and cellular regulatory mechanisms.

  • Neuroscience

    Study the nervous system including neural structure, signaling, cognition, brain function, and neurobiological processes.

  • Physiology

    Explore the functional mechanisms of living organisms and their internal systems across cellular, organ, and organismal levels.

  • Zoology

    Study animal life including anatomy, behavior, classification, physiology, evolution, and species diversity across the animal kingdom.
