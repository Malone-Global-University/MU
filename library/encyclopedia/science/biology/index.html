Biology Overview

Biology studies living organisms and the processes that make life possible. This discipline examines life at many scales, from molecules, cells, and genes to tissues, organs, organisms, populations, ecosystems, and evolutionary lineages. It includes biochemistry, genetics, ecology, physiology, neuroscience, microbiology, botany, zoology, evolutionary biology, and many related fields that explain how organisms develop, function, reproduce, adapt, interact, and change over time. By studying biology, learners gain a framework for understanding the unity and diversity of life, the mechanisms that sustain living systems, the relationships between organisms and environments, and the scientific foundations of medicine, agriculture, conservation, biotechnology, and human health.

Categories

  • Biochemistry

    Study the chemical processes of life, including proteins, enzymes, carbohydrates, lipids, nucleic acids, metabolism, cellular energy, and molecular interactions within living organisms.

  • Bioinformatics

    Explore the use of computational tools to analyze biological data, including genomes, proteins, sequences, databases, evolutionary relationships, and large-scale biological information systems.

  • Biotechnology

    Learn how biological systems are applied to medicine, agriculture, industry, research, genetic engineering, diagnostics, therapeutics, and the development of useful biological technologies.

  • Botany

    Examine plant life, including plant anatomy, physiology, reproduction, classification, evolution, ecology, photosynthesis, growth, adaptation, and the role of plants in ecosystems.

  • Cell Biology

    Study cells as the basic units of life, including organelles, membranes, signaling, division, transport, metabolism, gene expression, and cellular organization.

  • Developmental Biology

    Explore how organisms grow and develop from single cells into complex bodies through cell differentiation, gene regulation, embryology, morphogenesis, and developmental pathways.

  • Ecology

    Study interactions among organisms and their environments, including populations, communities, ecosystems, food webs, habitats, biodiversity, energy flow, and ecological change.

  • Evolutionary Biology

    Examine how life changes over generations through natural selection, mutation, genetic drift, adaptation, speciation, common ancestry, and evolutionary relationships.

  • Genetics

    Learn how traits are inherited and expressed through genes, DNA, chromosomes, mutations, heredity, genetic variation, gene regulation, and patterns of inheritance.

  • Human Biology

    Study the biological structure and function of the human body, including organ systems, health, disease, development, genetics, physiology, and human adaptation.

  • Marine Biology

    Explore ocean life, including marine organisms, ecosystems, food webs, coral reefs, fisheries, adaptations, biodiversity, and biological processes in aquatic environments.

  • Microbiology

    Examine microscopic life, including bacteria, viruses, fungi, archaea, protists, microbial genetics, infection, immunity, ecology, and the roles of microbes in living systems.

  • Molecular Biology

    Study life at the molecular level, including DNA, RNA, proteins, replication, transcription, translation, gene expression, molecular pathways, and cellular regulation.

  • Neuroscience

    Explore the nervous system, including neurons, synapses, brain structure, sensory processing, movement, cognition, memory, behavior, and neural communication.

  • Physiology

    Learn how living organisms function through organ systems, cellular processes, homeostasis, circulation, respiration, digestion, movement, regulation, and coordinated biological activity.

  • Zoology

    Study animals, including anatomy, physiology, behavior, classification, evolution, reproduction, ecology, adaptation, and the diversity of animal life across habitats.
