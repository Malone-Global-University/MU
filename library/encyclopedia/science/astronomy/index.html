Astronomy Overview

Astronomy is the scientific study of celestial objects, space, and the physical laws governing the universe beyond Earth. It examines planets, stars, galaxies, black holes, cosmic structure, and the origins and evolution of the universe itself. Through observation, mathematics, and physics, astronomy seeks to explain how matter, energy, gravity, and time interact across cosmic scales. This field ranges from studying nearby planetary systems to investigating the earliest moments of the universe, making astronomy one of the broadest and most foundational disciplines in modern science.

Categories

  • Celestial Mechanics

    Study the motion of celestial bodies under gravitational influence including orbital dynamics, planetary motion, trajectories, and multi-body interactions.

  • Cosmology

    Examine the origin, evolution, structure, and large-scale behavior of the universe including expansion, dark matter, dark energy, and cosmological models.

  • Observational Astronomy

    Explore the tools, techniques, and methodologies used to observe celestial phenomena including telescopes, spectroscopy, imaging, and data acquisition systems.

  • Planetary Science

    Study planets, moons, asteroids, comets, and planetary systems including their composition, atmospheres, geology, and formation processes.

  • Stellar Astronomy

    Investigate the formation, structure, evolution, and classification of stars across their life cycles from protostars to stellar remnants.

  • Telescopes and Instrumentation

    Learn about the devices and engineering systems used to detect, measure, and analyze astronomical signals across the electromagnetic spectrum.
