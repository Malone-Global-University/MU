Learning Sciences Overview

Learning sciences study the cognitive, developmental, motivational, and environmental processes through which learning occurs. The field integrates psychology, neuroscience, pedagogy, and assessment theory to understand how knowledge is acquired, retained, and applied. It provides scientific frameworks for designing effective educational experiences.

Disciplines

  • Cognitive Learning Theory

    Study mental processes involved in knowledge acquisition, understanding, and reasoning.

  • Developmental Learning

    Examine how learning changes across stages of human development.

  • Learning Assessment Theory

    Study theoretical frameworks for measuring and interpreting learning outcomes.

  • Memory and Retention

    Explore how information is encoded, stored, and recalled in learning contexts.

  • Motivation and Engagement

    Study drivers of learner interest, effort, persistence, and participation.
