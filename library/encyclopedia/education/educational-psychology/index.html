Educational Psychology Overview

Educational psychology studies how psychological principles explain learning, motivation, development, behavior, and cognition in educational environments. The field examines learner differences, developmental processes, cognitive functioning, and instructional implications of psychological theory. It provides a behavioral and cognitive basis for effective teaching.

Disciplines

  • Behavioral Development

    Study behavioral growth, regulation, and developmental behavioral patterns in learners.

  • Intelligence and Cognition

    Examine mental processes, reasoning, intelligence, and cognitive variation in learners.

  • Learning Development

    Study developmental changes in learning capacity across age and maturity.

  • Motivation Theory

    Explore psychological theories explaining motivation and engagement in learning.

  • Special Needs Learning

    Study psychological and instructional considerations for diverse learner needs.
