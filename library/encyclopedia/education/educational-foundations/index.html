Educational Foundations Overview

Educational foundations study the underlying philosophical, historical, ethical, and social principles that shape educational thought and institutional design. The field examines why societies educate, how educational systems developed, and what values and assumptions guide educational practice. It provides the conceptual framework for understanding education as a human institution.

Disciplines

  • Comparative Education

    Study educational systems across cultures and nations through comparative analysis.

  • Ethics of Education

    Examine moral principles and ethical issues in educational practice and policy.

  • History of Education

    Study historical development of educational institutions, practices, and ideas.

  • Philosophy of Education

    Explore theoretical questions concerning the purpose, nature, and goals of education.

  • Sociology of Education

    Study how educational institutions interact with society, culture, and inequality.
