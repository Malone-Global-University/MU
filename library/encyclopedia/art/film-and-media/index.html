Film and Media Overview

Film and media study the creation, structure, and interpretation of moving-image works across cinema, television, video, streaming, and digital platforms. The field combines storytelling, performance, camera technique, sound, editing, design, and post-production. Across directing, cinematography, screenwriting, editing, and visual effects, media disciplines examine how images, time, sound, and narrative are shaped into cultural experience.

Disciplines

  • Cinematography

    Study camera movement, framing, lenses, lighting, exposure, shot design, visual style, and cinematic image construction.

  • Directing

    Learn creative leadership of film production through performance, staging, visual decisions, story interpretation, and crew coordination.

  • Editing

    Examine post-production structure through sequencing, pacing, continuity, rhythm, transitions, narrative clarity, and emotional timing.

  • Screenwriting

    Study scriptwriting for visual media through plot, character, dialogue, scene structure, conflict, and cinematic storytelling.

  • Production Design

    Explore the visual world of film through sets, props, locations, color palettes, period detail, and environmental storytelling.

  • Visual Effects

    Learn digital enhancement of moving images through compositing, CGI, simulation, matte painting, tracking, and post-production effects.
