Design Overview

Design connects artistic form with practical function, communication, usability, and human experience. It studies how objects, spaces, images, products, clothing, and visual systems are planned to serve needs while carrying aesthetic and cultural meaning. Across graphic, industrial, interior, fashion, and product design, the field examines how creative decisions shape perception, behavior, identity, and everyday life.

Disciplines

  • Graphic Design

    Study visual communication through typography, layout, branding, imagery, print media, digital interfaces, and information design.

  • Industrial Design

    Examine the design of manufactured objects through form, usability, materials, production methods, ergonomics, and commercial function.

  • Interior Design

    Explore the planning of interior environments through space, furniture, lighting, materials, circulation, comfort, and atmosphere.

  • Fashion Design

    Study clothing, textiles, garment construction, style systems, body presentation, fashion history, and visual identity.

  • Product Design

    Learn the development of useful objects through research, prototyping, aesthetics, function, user needs, and production constraints.

  • Visual Communication Design

    Examine how images, symbols, typography, layout, and media systems communicate information, identity, instruction, and meaning.
