Performing Arts Overview

Performing arts study live artistic expression presented through movement, speech, gesture, music, staging, and embodied performance before an audience. The field combines creative interpretation with physical technique, dramatic structure, production systems, and theoretical analysis. Across acting, dance, theater production, stagecraft, and performance theory, the discipline examines how performers use presence, timing, space, and expression to create shared artistic experience in real time.

Disciplines

  • Acting

    Study character performance through voice, movement, emotional expression, scene work, interpretation, improvisation, and dramatic embodiment.

  • Dance

    Learn choreographed movement through rhythm, physical technique, expression, body control, composition, and performance practice.

  • Theater Production

    Examine organizational and technical systems supporting live performance including directing, management, scheduling, rehearsal, and coordination.

  • Stagecraft

    Study technical theater through scenery, props, lighting, sound, rigging, backstage operations, and physical production support.

  • Performance Theory

    Explore conceptual frameworks for analyzing performance, embodiment, spectatorship, ritual, representation, and performative meaning.
