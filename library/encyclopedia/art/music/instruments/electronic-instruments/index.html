Electronic Instruments

Study electronic instruments through synthesis, sampling, controllers, drum machines, software instruments, effects, and digital performance.

Overview

Electronic instruments use electrical, analog, digital, or software systems to generate, shape, trigger, process, or control sound. They are central to electronic music, pop, hip-hop, film scoring, experimental music, and live performance.

This page belongs to the Instrument Guide area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Instrument pages should connect sound production, performance technique, musical role, ensemble use, history, technology, and listening practice.

Electronic Instrument Types

Electronic instruments include synthesizers, samplers, drum machines, MIDI controllers, electric pianos, electric guitars, electronic drums, software instruments, and custom controllers.

Sound Design

Electronic instruments often allow musicians to design sound through oscillators, samples, filters, envelopes, modulation, effects, and digital processing.

Performance and Production

Electronic instruments blur the line between performance, composition, production, sequencing, programming, improvisation, and sound design.
