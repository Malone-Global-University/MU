Voice

Study the human voice as an instrument of breath, tone, language, melody, expression, identity, and performance.

Overview

The voice is the oldest and most personal musical instrument. It connects sound to breath, body, language, memory, emotion, culture, worship, storytelling, and performance.

This page belongs to the Instrument Guide area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Instrument pages should connect sound production, performance technique, musical role, ensemble use, history, technology, and listening practice.

Vocal Production

Vocal sound is produced by breath, vocal fold vibration, resonance, articulation, and coordinated body support.

Range and Register

Voice study often includes vocal range, register, tone color, tessitura, breath control, and healthy production.

Solo and Ensemble Use

The voice appears in solo song, choir, opera, gospel, jazz, pop, rap, theater, folk traditions, sacred music, and community singing.
