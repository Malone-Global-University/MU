Music Overview

Music studies organized sound as artistic expression, performance practice, cultural tradition, and technical production. It examines melody, harmony, rhythm, texture, timbre, form, notation, interpretation, and listening across historical and contemporary contexts. Across theory, composition, performance, conducting, music history, and audio production, the discipline connects creative expression with structure, technique, technology, and human experience.

Disciplines

  • Music Theory

    Study the structure of music through harmony, melody, rhythm, notation, scales, chords, form, analysis, and musical syntax.

  • Composition

    Learn original music creation through melody, harmony, orchestration, arrangement, form, notation, style, and expressive development.

  • Performance

    Examine the practice of presenting music through instrumental, vocal, ensemble, interpretive, technical, and expressive skill.

  • Conducting

    Study musical leadership through gesture, rehearsal technique, score interpretation, tempo control, ensemble coordination, and expression.

  • Music History

    Explore musical development across periods, cultures, genres, composers, traditions, technologies, institutions, and performance practices.

  • Audio Production

    Learn recording, mixing, mastering, editing, microphone technique, sound design, signal flow, and technical production workflows.
