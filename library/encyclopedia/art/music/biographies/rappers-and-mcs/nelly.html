Nelly

St. Louis rapper known for melodic hip-hop, crossover hits, and early 2000s mainstream success

Biography

Nelly, born Cornell Iral Haynes Jr. in Austin, Texas and raised in St. Louis, Missouri, is a rapper, singer, and entrepreneur known for blending melodic hooks with hip-hop production. He rose to prominence in the early 2000s as a leading figure in Midwestern rap and achieved widespread commercial success with his debut album Country Grammar. His sound helped bridge regional rap styles with mainstream pop audiences.

His follow-up albums, including Nellyville, produced multiple chart-topping singles and solidified his status as a global hip-hop star. Nelly’s style often incorporates sing-rap delivery, catchy choruses, and club-oriented production. He became one of the most commercially successful rappers of his era.

Beyond music, Nelly expanded into acting, business ventures, and fashion, further establishing himself as a multimedia entertainer. His influence played a major role in bringing Midwest hip-hop into mainstream global recognition.

Legacy

Nelly’s legacy is tied to the commercialization and global reach of early 2000s hip-hop. He is recognized for helping popularize melodic rap styles that influenced later mainstream artists. His impact continues through crossover hip-hop and pop-rap fusion styles.
