Latto

Atlanta rapper known for confident delivery, Southern hip-hop style, and mainstream success

Biography

Latto, born Alyssa Michelle Stephens in Columbus, Ohio, and raised in Atlanta, Georgia, is a rapper known for her confident delivery and Southern-influenced hip-hop style. She first gained attention as a teenager after appearing on reality television and releasing early music under the name Mulatto before rebranding professionally as Latto. Her sound blends Southern trap energy with mainstream pop-rap appeal.

She rose to prominence with viral singles and mixtapes before achieving major commercial success with tracks like “Big Energy,” which became a mainstream hit. Her debut album Queen of Da Souf helped establish her presence in the modern rap landscape. She later expanded her reach through high-profile collaborations and award-nominated releases.

Latto has built a strong reputation for assertive lyricism, chart success, and crossover appeal within hip-hop and pop markets.

Legacy

Latto’s legacy is defined by Southern rap influence and mainstream crossover success. She is widely recognized as one of the leading female voices in contemporary hip-hop. Her impact continues across trap music, pop-rap fusion, and modern commercial rap culture.
