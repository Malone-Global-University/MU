T.I.

Rapper, actor, and entrepreneur known as a pioneer of trap music and Atlanta hip-hop

Biography

T.I., born Clifford Joseph Harris Jr. in Atlanta, Georgia, is a rapper, actor, and entrepreneur widely recognized as one of the pioneers of trap music. Emerging in the early 2000s, he helped define the Atlanta rap sound that would later become a dominant force in global hip-hop. His music blends Southern street storytelling with polished mainstream production.

He gained early attention with his debut album I'm Serious, but achieved major commercial success with Trap Muzik, which helped establish the term and sound of trap music. Subsequent albums such as Urban Legend and King solidified his status as a leading voice in Southern hip-hop. His lyrical style often balances street narratives with charisma and social commentary.

Beyond music, T.I. expanded into acting, entrepreneurship, and media, becoming a prominent cultural figure in entertainment. His influence helped bring Atlanta hip-hop into global mainstream recognition alongside other major artists of the era.

Legacy

T.I.’s legacy is closely tied to the birth and popularization of trap music. He is widely recognized for helping establish Atlanta as a global center of hip-hop innovation. His influence continues through artists who build on the trap foundation he helped define.
