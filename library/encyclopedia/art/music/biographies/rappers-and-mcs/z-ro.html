Z-Ro

Houston rapper known for emotional storytelling, melodic flow, and Southern hip-hop influence

Biography

Z-Ro, born Joseph Wayne McVey IV in Houston, Texas, is a rapper known for his emotional delivery, melodic flow, and deeply personal songwriting. He emerged from the Screwed Up Click-influenced Houston rap scene, developing a style shaped by slowed-down production and reflective lyricism. His music often focuses on pain, resilience, and life struggles.

He gained recognition through a prolific catalog of independent releases and underground acclaim, building a loyal fanbase in Southern hip-hop. His albums frequently blend singing and rapping, creating a signature emotional tone. He is often referred to as a “Mo City Don,” reflecting his connection to Missouri City, Texas.

Z-Ro is widely respected for his consistency and emotional honesty in Southern rap.

Legacy

Z-Ro’s legacy is defined by emotional Southern rap and melodic storytelling. He is widely regarded as one of Houston’s most influential underground-to-mainstream artists. His influence continues across pain rap, chopped-and-screwed culture, and Southern hip-hop expression.
