Yo Gotti

Memphis rapper known for trap-era success, street-focused storytelling, and independent label leadership

Biography

Yo Gotti, born Mario Mims in Memphis, Tennessee, is a rapper and music executive known for his influence on Southern hip-hop and trap music. He began his career in the underground Memphis rap scene before achieving mainstream success through consistent releases and regional popularity. His music reflects street narratives, ambition, and entrepreneurial themes.

He rose to wider prominence in the 2010s with charting albums and hit singles that helped define modern Southern rap. He later expanded his influence as a label executive, helping develop new artists and shaping Memphis’ modern hip-hop presence. His career combines music performance with business leadership in the rap industry.

Yo Gotti is widely recognized as a major figure in modern Memphis rap.

Legacy

His legacy is defined by Southern trap influence and independent artist development. He is widely associated with Memphis’ continued relevance in modern hip-hop culture. His impact extends across rap entrepreneurship, label building, and mainstream Southern hip-hop success.
