Juvenile

Rapper and Cash Money Records artist known for shaping the sound of New Orleans hip-hop

Biography

Juvenile, born Terius Gray in New Orleans, Louisiana, is a rapper and one of the key figures in the rise of Cash Money Records. Emerging in the 1990s, he became a central voice in shaping the sound of Southern hip-hop, particularly the New Orleans bounce-influenced style that defined the region. His energetic delivery and street-centered storytelling helped bring national attention to the Cash Money roster.

He achieved mainstream breakthrough success with the album 400 Degreez, which became a defining release in late-1990s hip-hop. The project included major hits that elevated both Juvenile and Cash Money Records into national prominence. His style combined rhythmic bounce elements with accessible hooks and vivid depictions of New Orleans street life.

Juvenile remained a key figure in Cash Money’s rise alongside artists such as Lil Wayne and B.G. Even after leaving and later rejoining the label, he maintained a strong presence in Southern hip-hop and continued releasing music that reflected his New Orleans roots.

Legacy

Juvenile’s legacy is tied to the mainstream breakthrough of New Orleans hip-hop. He is widely recognized for helping establish the national reach of Cash Money Records and defining the sound of late-1990s Southern rap. His influence continues through artists who draw on bounce rhythms and Southern street storytelling.
