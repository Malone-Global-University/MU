Above the Law

Rappers, producers, and pioneers of West Coast gangsta rap and G-funk

Biography

Above the Law was a pioneering hip-hop group from Pomona, California, known for helping shape the sound and attitude of West Coast gangsta rap. Formed in the late 1980s, the group became one of the early artists associated with Ruthless Records and helped define the hard-edged, streetwise style that would later become central to West Coast hip-hop. Their music combined sharp storytelling, funk-heavy production, and a polished but aggressive delivery that made them stand out during a rapidly changing era in rap.

The group is closely linked to producer Dr. Dre and to the early development of the G-funk sound. Their 1990 debut album, Livin’ Like Hustlers, established Above the Law as an important force in hip-hop, while later releases such as Black Mafia Life and Uncle Sam’s Curse further strengthened their reputation. The group’s work often blended street narratives, social commentary, and stylized confidence, reflecting both the realities and mythology of West Coast urban life.

Above the Law also played a major role in bridging the gap between the raw, independent energy of early gangsta rap and the smoother, funk-driven production that later came to define much of 1990s California hip-hop. Their influence can be heard in the music of later artists who drew from their blend of menace, melody, and bass-heavy sound design. Over time, the group became respected not only for their recordings, but also for their place in the broader history of rap innovation.

Legacy

Above the Law’s legacy is tied to the rise of West Coast rap as a dominant force in American popular music. They helped lay the foundation for the G-funk era and showed how gangsta rap could be both commercially powerful and musically distinctive. Their influence remains important in discussions of early 1990s hip-hop, especially in any account of the artists and producers who shaped the sound of California rap.
