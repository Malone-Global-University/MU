Kurupt

Rapper, songwriter, and West Coast lyricist known for his work with Tha Dogg Pound

Biography

Kurupt, born Ricardo Emmanuel Brown, is a rapper and songwriter known for his complex lyricism and contributions to West Coast hip-hop. Emerging in the early 1990s, he became widely recognized through his association with Death Row Records and his role in the duo Tha Dogg Pound alongside Daz Dillinger. His rapid delivery, battle-style rhyming, and intricate wordplay helped distinguish him from many of his contemporaries in the Los Angeles rap scene.

He first gained major exposure through appearances on landmark West Coast releases, including collaborations with Dr. Dre and Snoop Dogg. His debut album Kuruption! showcased his versatility, featuring both West Coast and East Coast influences and highlighting his dual presence in multiple hip-hop regions. Over time, he became known for balancing aggressive battle rap energy with reflective storytelling and technical precision.

As a member of Tha Dogg Pound, Kurupt played a key role in defining the G-funk era sound and expanding the lyrical depth of West Coast rap. His collaborations throughout the 1990s and 2000s helped maintain his relevance across shifting hip-hop styles. He also became recognized for his role in bridging stylistic divides within hip-hop during a period of regional competition and creative expansion.

Legacy

Kurupt’s legacy is built on technical lyricism and his contribution to the golden era of West Coast rap. He is respected as one of the more lyrically intricate voices to emerge from the Death Row era, and his work with Tha Dogg Pound remains influential in discussions of 1990s hip-hop. His career reflects both the competitive nature and creative richness of West Coast rap during its most influential period.
