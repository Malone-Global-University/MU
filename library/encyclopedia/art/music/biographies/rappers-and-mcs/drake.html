Drake

Canadian rapper and singer known for shaping modern hip-hop and global chart dominance

Biography

Drake, born Aubrey Drake Graham in Toronto, Canada, is a rapper, singer, and producer who became one of the most influential figures in modern hip-hop and R&B. He first gained recognition as an actor on the television series Degrassi: The Next Generation before transitioning into music in the late 2000s. His early mixtapes helped redefine the emotional and melodic direction of rap music.

His breakout project So Far Gone led to a major record deal and rapid rise in global popularity. Albums such as Take Care, Nothing Was the Same, and Views established him as a dominant force in mainstream music. His sound blends rap, singing, and atmospheric production, often focusing on relationships, ambition, and introspection.

Drake has consistently broken streaming and chart records, becoming one of the most commercially successful artists of all time. His influence extends across hip-hop, pop, and global music trends.

Legacy

Drake’s legacy is defined by emotional rap, melodic innovation, and unprecedented commercial success. He is widely regarded as one of the most influential artists of the streaming era. His impact continues across hip-hop, pop crossover music, and global music culture.
