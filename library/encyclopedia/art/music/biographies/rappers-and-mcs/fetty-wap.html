Fetty Wap

New Jersey rapper known for melodic trap hits and major 2010s crossover success

Biography

Fetty Wap, born Willie Maxwell II in Paterson, New Jersey, is a rapper and singer known for blending melodic vocals with trap-influenced production. He rose to fame in the mid-2010s with a string of viral and chart-topping singles that defined his early career. His signature sound combines singing and rapping over catchy, melodic beats.

He achieved rapid mainstream success with multiple hit records that dominated radio and streaming platforms. His music often focuses on themes of love, relationships, and street life, delivered in a melodic and accessible style. He became one of the most recognizable voices of mid-2010s hip-hop crossover music.

Fetty Wap is widely recognized as a key figure in melodic trap’s mainstream breakthrough.

Legacy

His legacy is defined by melodic trap innovation and viral chart success. He is widely associated with shaping mid-2010s hip-hop radio and streaming culture. His influence continues across melodic rap and crossover hip-hop styles.
