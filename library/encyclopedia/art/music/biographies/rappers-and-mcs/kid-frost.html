Kid Frost

Los Angeles rapper known for pioneering Latino hip-hop and West Coast crossover rap

Biography

Kid Frost, born Arturo Molina Jr., is a rapper from Los Angeles, California, widely recognized as one of the pioneers of Latino hip-hop. He emerged in the late 1980s and early 1990s, helping bring Chicano culture into mainstream West Coast rap. His music blends English and Spanish influences with classic West Coast production.

He gained widespread recognition with his breakout hit “La Raza,” which became a defining anthem for Latino representation in hip-hop. His work helped open doors for future generations of Latin and Chicano rappers in the United States. He continued releasing music that emphasized cultural identity and street storytelling.

Kid Frost is widely regarded as a foundational figure in Latino hip-hop history.

Legacy

Kid Frost’s legacy is defined by Latino representation in hip-hop and West Coast cultural expansion. He is widely respected as a pioneer who helped establish Chicano rap as a recognized genre. His influence continues across Latin hip-hop, West Coast rap, and multicultural hip-hop identity.
