Scarface

Rapper, producer, and Geto Boys member known for emotional storytelling and Southern hip-hop influence

Biography

Scarface, born Brad Terrence Jordan in Houston, Texas, is a rapper, producer, and one of the most influential figures in Southern hip-hop. He rose to prominence as a member of the Geto Boys, a group that helped define the sound and tone of early Houston rap. His solo career further established him as a deeply introspective and narrative-driven lyricist.

With albums such as The Diary and The Fix, Scarface became known for vivid storytelling, emotional depth, and reflections on street life, mental health, and survival. His work expanded the lyrical boundaries of hip-hop by blending personal vulnerability with street realism. He is widely respected for his consistency and artistic integrity.

Beyond his solo work, Scarface played a key role in shaping Southern hip-hop’s national identity. His influence extends across generations of rappers who prioritize storytelling and emotional complexity in their music.

Legacy

Scarface’s legacy is foundational to Southern hip-hop storytelling. He is regarded as one of the greatest narrative lyricists in rap history. His influence continues through artists who emphasize emotional depth, realism, and lyrical craftsmanship.
