DJ Screw

Houston DJ and innovator of chopped and screwed music that defined a generation of Southern hip-hop

Biography

DJ Screw, born Robert Earl Davis Jr. in Smithville, Texas, was a pioneering DJ and producer from Houston who created the chopped and screwed style of hip-hop. Emerging in the late 1980s and early 1990s, he became a central figure in shaping the sound of Houston’s underground rap scene. His technique involved slowing down tracks and applying cuts, scratches, and repeats to create a heavy, atmospheric sound.

Through his legendary mixtapes known as “Screw Tapes,” DJ Screw built a massive underground following across Houston and beyond. These tapes featured slowed-down versions of popular rap songs along with freestyles from local artists, helping launch the careers of many members of the Screwed Up Click. His home studio became a cultural hub for Southern hip-hop creativity.

DJ Screw’s influence extended far beyond Houston, as his chopped and screwed style became a defining aesthetic in Southern rap music. His innovations impacted production techniques, vocal delivery styles, and the overall sonic identity of Houston hip-hop.

Legacy

DJ Screw’s legacy is foundational to Southern hip-hop culture. He is credited with creating one of the most influential regional subgenres in rap history. His chopped and screwed technique continues to influence artists, producers, and remix culture worldwide.
