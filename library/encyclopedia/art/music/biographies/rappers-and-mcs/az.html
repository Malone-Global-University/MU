AZ

Brooklyn rapper known for smooth lyrical flow, Nas collaborations, and underground East Coast influence

Biography

AZ, born Anthony Cruz in Brooklyn, New York, is a rapper known for his smooth delivery, polished lyricism, and refined storytelling style. He first gained major attention through his appearance on Nas’s classic album Illmatic, contributing a standout verse on “Life’s a Bitch.” His voice and flow quickly became recognized for their calm precision and melodic cadence.

He later built a respected solo career, releasing albums that strengthened his reputation as one of the most consistent lyricists from New York. His work often blends street narratives with introspective themes and soulful production. AZ has remained a key figure in underground and classic-era East Coast hip-hop appreciation.

AZ is widely respected as one of the most underrated yet highly skilled lyricists in hip-hop.

Legacy

AZ’s legacy is defined by lyrical smoothness and consistency across decades of hip-hop. He is widely regarded as one of the most technically refined and underappreciated voices from the golden era. His influence continues across lyrical rap, underground hip-hop culture, and classic East Coast storytelling traditions.
