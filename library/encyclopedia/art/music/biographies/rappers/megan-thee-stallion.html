Megan Thee Stallion

Houston rapper known for confident delivery, Southern rap influence, and modern hip-hop dominance

Biography

Megan Thee Stallion, born Megan Jovon Ruth Pete in Houston, Texas, is a rapper known for her confident delivery, lyrical strength, and Southern rap influence. She first gained attention through viral freestyles and social media performances that highlighted her commanding flow and personality. Her style draws heavily from Houston hip-hop traditions while pushing into mainstream pop-rap success.

She achieved breakthrough success with projects such as Fever and Suga, followed by her debut album Good News. The global hit “Savage (Remix)” featuring Beyoncé helped solidify her status as a major figure in modern hip-hop. Her music often emphasizes empowerment, confidence, and lyrical control.

Megan Thee Stallion has become one of the most prominent voices in contemporary rap, with influence spanning music, fashion, and pop culture.

Legacy

Megan Thee Stallion’s legacy is defined by lyrical confidence and Houston rap tradition. She is widely recognized as one of the most influential female rappers of her generation. Her impact continues across Southern hip-hop, mainstream rap, and cultural empowerment movements.
