Grandmaster Caz

Legendary lyricist and member of the Cold Crush Brothers

Biography

Grandmaster Caz, born Curtis Fisher on May 18, 1961, emerged as one of the most respected MCs of hip-hop's formative era. Raised in the Bronx, he developed his skills during a period when rap music existed primarily as a live performance tradition rather than a recorded industry.

Known for his creativity, confidence, and verbal dexterity, Caz became one of the leading lyricists of the old-school era. His performances helped establish standards for MC excellence that influenced countless future rappers.

Career and Contributions

As a leading member of the Cold Crush Brothers, Grandmaster Caz participated in some of the most famous rap battles and live performances of the 1970s and early 1980s. The group earned a reputation for showmanship, lyrical skill, and stage presence.

Caz became particularly known for his complex rhymes and carefully crafted routines. At a time when much rap performance relied on crowd interaction and improvisation, he demonstrated how sophisticated written lyrics could elevate the art form.

His influence extends into one of hip-hop's most discussed historical controversies. Portions of lyrics associated with Caz were later used during the creation of "Rapper's Delight," one of rap's first commercial hits. This connection highlights the importance of early Bronx MCs in shaping recorded hip-hop.

Legacy

Many historians regard Grandmaster Caz as one of the greatest lyricists of hip-hop's pioneering generation. His emphasis on writing, performance, and technical skill helped define what it meant to be an MC.

His contributions continue to be studied by students of rap history seeking to understand the origins of modern lyricism.
