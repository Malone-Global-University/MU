T.R.U.

Hip-hop collective associated with 2 Chainz known for Southern trap influence and collaborative rap projects

Biography

T.R.U. (The Real University) is a hip-hop collective founded by 2 Chainz, built as a platform for collaborative artistry and Southern rap development. The group includes a rotating roster of affiliated artists and producers who contribute to its releases and projects. It represents both a music collective and a brand identity within modern trap music culture.

The collective gained visibility through projects that showcased a blend of trap production, Southern hip-hop lyricism, and collaborative performances. T.R.U. releases often emphasize unity among artists and highlight emerging voices alongside established acts. The group operates as both a creative label structure and a musical identity under the broader 2 Chainz brand.

T.R.U. is recognized as part of the modern evolution of Southern rap collectives and trap-era collaboration models.

Legacy

T.R.U.’s legacy is defined by Southern rap collaboration and modern trap collective structure. It is widely regarded as an extension of 2 Chainz’s influence in shaping contemporary Atlanta hip-hop. Its impact continues across trap collectives, independent rap branding, and collaborative Southern hip-hop projects.
