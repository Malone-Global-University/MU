Chief Keef

Chicago rapper and pioneer of drill music known for shaping modern trap and street rap culture

Biography

Chief Keef, born Keith Farrelle Cozart in Chicago, Illinois, is a rapper widely recognized as one of the founding figures of drill music. Emerging in the early 2010s, he gained attention through viral success and a raw, unfiltered style that reflected street life in Chicago’s South Side. His music became a defining force in a new wave of regional rap.

His breakout single “I Don’t Like” brought national attention and led to a major-label deal, solidifying his influence on mainstream hip-hop. His debut album Finally Rich became a landmark release in drill music and helped establish the genre’s global reach. His minimalist production style and laid-back vocal delivery became widely imitated.

Despite legal challenges and industry controversies, Chief Keef maintained a strong independent presence and continued releasing music that influenced a generation of rappers. His work reshaped the sound and aesthetics of modern trap and drill music worldwide.

Legacy

Chief Keef’s legacy is defined by his foundational role in drill music and modern street rap. He is widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in 2010s hip-hop culture. His impact continues across drill, trap, and internet-driven rap movements globally.
