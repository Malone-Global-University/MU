C-Murder

New Orleans rapper known for No Limit Records era, street-centered lyricism, and Southern gangsta rap influence

Biography

C-Murder is a rapper from New Orleans, Louisiana and a member of the influential No Limit Records roster during its late-1990s rise. He is known for his deep vocal tone and street-oriented lyrical style that reflects Southern gangsta rap traditions. His music contributed to the broader recognition of New Orleans as a major force in hip-hop during the No Limit era.

He gained recognition through solo releases and collaborations within the No Limit collective, helping shape the label’s signature sound. His work often emphasizes street realism, loyalty themes, and Southern identity in hip-hop. He remains a notable figure in discussions of 1990s Southern rap history.

C-Murder is widely regarded as a key artist in the No Limit Records movement.

Legacy

His legacy is defined by contributions to the No Limit Records era and Southern gangsta rap development. He is widely associated with the rise of New Orleans hip-hop in the 1990s mainstream breakthrough. His influence continues across Southern rap culture and No Limit historical legacy.
