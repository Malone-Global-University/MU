Tim Dog

Bronx rapper known for early 1990s diss records, controversy, and influence on battle rap culture

Biography

Tim Dog, born Timothy Blair in the Bronx, New York, was a rapper known for his aggressive delivery and highly controversial diss tracks in the early 1990s. He became widely recognized for his confrontational style and his role in escalating East Coast and West Coast tensions in hip-hop during that era. His music was marked by raw production and provocative lyrical themes.

He gained attention with records that directly challenged prominent West Coast artists, helping define a period of intense rivalry in hip-hop culture. While controversial, his work became part of the broader narrative of competitive rap expression in the 1990s. He later continued releasing music independently with a cult following.

Tim Dog is remembered as a controversial but influential figure in early battle-style hip-hop.

Legacy

Tim Dog’s legacy is defined by early rap controversy and battle rap escalation. He is widely recognized for contributing to the East Coast–West Coast rivalry narrative. His influence continues across diss-track culture and competitive hip-hop history.
