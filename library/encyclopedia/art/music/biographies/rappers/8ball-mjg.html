8Ball & MJG

Memphis hip-hop duo and pioneers of Southern rap and underground street storytelling

Biography

8Ball & MJG are a hip-hop duo from Memphis, Tennessee, consisting of Premro Smith (8Ball) and Marlon Goodwin (MJG). Emerging in the early 1990s, they became two of the most important figures in the development of Southern hip-hop, helping define the Memphis rap sound. Their music blends street storytelling, Southern slang, and smooth but hard-hitting production.

Their debut album Comin’ Out Hard introduced their gritty yet melodic style and established them as rising voices in Southern rap. Follow-up projects such as On the Outside Looking In and On Top of the World expanded their influence beyond Memphis and helped bring national attention to Southern hip-hop. Their work is often characterized by cinematic storytelling and reflective narratives about street life.

Throughout their career, 8Ball & MJG collaborated with a wide range of artists and later joined major label platforms that increased their visibility in mainstream hip-hop. Despite shifts in the industry, they maintained a consistent identity rooted in Memphis culture and Southern authenticity.

Legacy

8Ball & MJG’s legacy is foundational to Southern hip-hop. They are widely recognized as pioneers who helped establish Memphis as a major center of rap innovation. Their influence extends through generations of Southern artists who draw on their storytelling style and musical approach.
