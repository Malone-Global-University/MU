Xzibit

Rapper, actor, and West Coast artist known for lyrical skill and mainstream crossover success

Biography

Xzibit, born Alvin Nathaniel Joiner in Detroit, Michigan, is a rapper, actor, and television personality who became a prominent figure in West Coast hip-hop. He relocated to Los Angeles in his youth and developed his career within the Southern California rap scene, where his lyrical ability and commanding delivery quickly gained attention. His style combined aggressive battle rap energy with polished mainstream production.

He released his debut album At the Speed of Life in 1996, which introduced him as a rising voice in hip-hop. His breakthrough came with later projects such as 40 Dayz & 40 Nightz and Restless, the latter of which was produced by Dr. Dre and helped elevate him to international recognition. His collaboration with major West Coast artists positioned him within the core of late-1990s rap evolution.

In addition to his music career, Xzibit became widely known as a television host, most notably for the series *Pimp My Ride*. This role expanded his visibility beyond hip-hop and introduced him to a global mainstream audience. He has continued to release music and act in film and television while maintaining his identity as a West Coast artist.

Legacy

Xzibit’s legacy spans music, television, and acting, making him one of the more versatile figures to emerge from West Coast hip-hop. He is recognized for his lyrical consistency and for bridging underground credibility with mainstream visibility. His work helped extend the reach of West Coast rap into global entertainment culture.
