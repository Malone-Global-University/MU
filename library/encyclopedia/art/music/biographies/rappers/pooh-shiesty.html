Pooh Shiesty

Memphis rapper known for drill-influenced trap and street-focused storytelling

Biography

Pooh Shiesty, born Lontrell Dennell Williams Jr. in Memphis, Tennessee, is a rapper known for his aggressive delivery and drill-influenced trap style. He emerged in the late 2010s Memphis rap scene, gaining attention for his raw street narratives and high-energy flow. His music reflects themes of loyalty, survival, and street life.

He gained mainstream attention through his affiliation with Gucci Mane’s 1017 label, which helped elevate his profile in the hip-hop industry. His breakout single “Back in Blood” became a major hit and solidified his presence in mainstream trap music. His mixtape work further expanded his reputation as a rising voice in Southern rap.

Despite legal setbacks, Pooh Shiesty’s music continues to maintain strong streaming performance and cultural relevance within modern trap and drill scenes.

Legacy

Pooh Shiesty’s legacy is defined by drill-influenced Memphis trap and raw street storytelling. He is widely recognized as part of the new generation of Southern rap artists. His influence continues across trap, drill, and modern streaming-era hip-hop.
