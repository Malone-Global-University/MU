Style and Influence

The Lady of Rage is known for a hard-edged flow, sharp delivery, and a commanding voice that made her one of the standout female MCs in the Death Row era. Her style paired aggressive lyricism with a strong sense of identity and presence. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}

Her recorded work is comparatively compact, but the strength of her guest verses and the endurance of “Afro Puffs” give her discography a lasting place in West Coast hip-hop history. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

Legacy

The Lady of Rage remains best known for turning a small solo catalog into a major legacy through elite features, soundtrack work, and a memorable debut album that captured the sound of 1990s West Coast rap. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Her influence continues through female rap performance, Death Row nostalgia, and the enduring reputation of “Afro Puffs” as one of the classic songs of the era. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
