Style and Influence

Poor Righteous Teachers are known for combining Afrocentric philosophy, Five-Percent Nation teachings, and socially conscious messaging with jazz-influenced East Coast production.

Their lyrical approach emphasized education, cultural awareness, and political critique, placing them firmly within the conscious rap movement of the early 1990s.

Legacy

Poor Righteous Teachers remain one of the most important voices in conscious hip-hop, with Holy Intellect widely regarded as a foundational album in politically charged rap.

Their influence continues in modern socially conscious hip-hop and Afrocentric rap traditions, especially within underground and educational music movements.
