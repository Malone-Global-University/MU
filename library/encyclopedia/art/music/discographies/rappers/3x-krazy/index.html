Associated Acts

3X Krazy was closely associated with the Oakland and Bay Area rap scene, particularly artists connected to the Sick Wid It Records movement led by E-40. The group's members would later pursue successful solo careers, especially Keak da Sneak, who became one of the most recognizable voices in Bay Area hip-hop.

Legacy

3X Krazy is remembered as one of the defining groups of 1990s Bay Area rap. Their music blended mobb music, funk influences, and regional slang into a distinctive sound that helped establish Oakland as one of hip-hop's most important creative centers.

The group's recordings influenced the development of the hyphy movement and introduced listeners across the country to artists who would remain important figures in West Coast hip-hop for decades.
