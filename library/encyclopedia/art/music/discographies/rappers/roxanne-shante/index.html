Style and Influence

Roxanne Shanté is recognized as one of the first prominent female battle rappers in hip-hop, emerging from Queensbridge during the formative years of East Coast rap.

Her sharp lyrical responses in the “Roxanne Wars” helped establish competitive rap as a central element of hip-hop culture in the 1980s.

Legacy

Roxanne Shanté remains a foundational figure in hip-hop history, particularly for her role in shaping battle rap and opening space for women in competitive MC culture.

Her influence continues to be recognized in discussions of early rap innovation and the evolution of lyrical battles.
