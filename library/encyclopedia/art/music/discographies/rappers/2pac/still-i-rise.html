Still I Rise is a posthumous collaborative album by 2Pac and the Outlawz. Released in 1999, the project pairs unreleased Tupac recordings with contributions from the Outlawz, creating a continuation of the sound and ideas associated with his later career. The album focuses on endurance, loyalty, violence, faith, and survival, while preserving the force and personality of Tupac Shakur's voice after his death.

Background

Following Tupac Shakur's death in 1996, his estate continued organizing unreleased recordings for future albums. Still I Rise was one of the most prominent posthumous projects to feature the Outlawz alongside Tupac's vocals, helping present the music as both a tribute and a continuation of the Death Row era.

The album's title reflects the central message running through much of Tupac's work: rising above hardship, betrayal, and violence despite the conditions surrounding him. The project was assembled from recordings made during the 1990s and released after his passing to extend his artistic presence.

Because of its collaborative nature, the album also reinforces the role of the Outlawz as part of Tupac's creative circle and final era.

Music and Themes

The album blends hard street records with reflective and spiritually charged songs. The production is often heavy and dramatic, while the lyrics move between aggression, loyalty, loss, and perseverance.

Recurring themes include:

  • Survival and determination
  • Street violence and retaliation
  • Loyalty, brotherhood, and trust
  • Faith, purpose, and redemption
  • Loss, memory, and legacy
  • Resistance in the face of oppression

The album carries the emotional weight of Tupac's posthumous catalog while also giving the Outlawz a visible role in carrying the message forward.

Notable Songs

Still I Rise

The title track captures the album's core message of endurance and defiance. Its confident tone and powerful hook make it one of the project's defining songs.

Baby Don't Cry (Keep Ya Head Up II)

This song continues Tupac's tradition of writing respectfully and empathetically about women and hardship. It became one of the album's most recognized tracks.

Letter 2 My Unborn

A deeply personal song, "Letter 2 My Unborn" reflects on fatherhood, hope, and responsibility, offering a more vulnerable side of Tupac's writing.

Secretz of War

This track delivers one of the album's hardest-edged performances, combining urgency, tension, and a strong sense of confrontation.

Commercial Performance

Still I Rise was a commercial success and continued Tupac Shakur's strong posthumous chart presence. The album demonstrated that his unreleased material remained in high demand among fans and listeners.

Its collaborative format also helped broaden the appeal of the release by connecting Tupac's legacy with the Outlawz's identity as part of his circle.

Reception

Critics and fans generally welcomed the album as a worthy addition to Tupac's posthumous catalog. Many praised the emotional power of the unreleased material and the way the album preserved his intensity and message.

Some listeners viewed the project as uneven in places, but it was still respected for its strong title track, memorable singles, and continuation of the themes that defined Tupac's career.

Legacy

Still I Rise remains an important posthumous release because it keeps Tupac's voice active while also documenting the final creative ecosystem around him. The album stands as a reminder of his influence and the lasting appeal of the Outlawz era.

It is often remembered as one of the key records that helped sustain public interest in 2Pac's music at the turn of the millennium.
