R U Still Down? (Remember Me) is a posthumous double album by 2Pac, released in 1997. The project consists primarily of unreleased tracks recorded before his death and represents a curated collection of his earlier material. The album highlights Tupac Shakur’s emotional depth, storytelling ability, and commitment to addressing social issues while also showcasing his evolving artistic voice.

Background

Following Tupac Shakur’s death in 1996, his estate and record labels began organizing unreleased recordings for future projects. R U Still Down? (Remember Me) was the first major posthumous album released under the Amaru Entertainment banner, managed by his mother, Afeni Shakur.

The album draws from recordings made primarily during Tupac’s early to mid-career period, before the final Death Row Records era. As a result, it offers a different tone compared to All Eyez on Me and The Don Killuminati: The 7 Day Theory, focusing more on reflection and lyrical clarity than urgency.

The project was assembled to preserve Tupac’s legacy while introducing unreleased material to a global audience that had grown significantly after his death.

Music and Themes

The album contains a wide range of material, from hardcore street narratives to deeply introspective songs. The production style varies due to the different recording periods, but Tupac’s lyrical presence remains consistent throughout.

Recurring themes include:

  • Survival and street life
  • Loss, grief, and remembrance
  • Social injustice and inequality
  • Loyalty and betrayal
  • Hope, struggle, and resilience
  • Personal reflection and emotional honesty

The album is often noted for its balance between raw energy and thoughtful storytelling, making it a key entry in his posthumous catalog.

Notable Songs

Do for Love

One of the album’s standout singles, "Do for Love" explores relationships, trust, and emotional vulnerability. Its smooth production and accessible style helped it become a major posthumous hit.

I Wonder If Heaven Got a Ghetto

A reflective and socially conscious track, this song became one of the most iconic posthumous releases in Tupac’s catalog, addressing poverty, violence, and hope for peace.

Hellrazor

"Hellrazor" is a darker, more aggressive track that reflects Tupac’s street perspective and intensity, showing the contrast between his reflective and hardened personas.

Never Call U Bitch Again

This song highlights Tupac’s ability to shift between confrontation and emotional reflection, focusing on relationships and personal accountability.

Commercial Performance

R U Still Down? (Remember Me) was a major commercial success, debuting at number two on the Billboard 200 and later achieving multi-platinum certification. It helped solidify Tupac’s continued dominance on the charts even after his death.

The singles received strong radio play and introduced younger audiences to previously unreleased material from his earlier recording sessions.

Reception

Critical response highlighted the album’s emotional consistency and the quality of its unreleased material. Some reviewers noted the uneven production typical of posthumous releases, while still praising Tupac’s lyrical strength.

Fans embraced the album as a meaningful continuation of his legacy, particularly appreciating its mix of reflective songs and classic storytelling.

Legacy

The album stands as one of the most important posthumous releases in hip-hop history, helping establish the framework for how Tupac’s unreleased work would be curated and presented in the years following his death.

It also reinforced the emotional and cultural impact of his earlier recordings, ensuring that his voice remained present in hip-hop long after his passing.
