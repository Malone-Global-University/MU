Style and Influence

Migos are known for their triplet-heavy flows, catchy ad-libs, and hook-driven songwriting. Their style helped popularize a distinctive Atlanta trap sound that spread across mainstream rap.

The group's music emphasized momentum, repetition, and swagger, making their records especially effective in the streaming era and in club-driven popular culture.

Legacy

Migos became one of the most influential rap groups of the 2010s, with Culture, Culture II, and singles like Bad and Boujee helping define a generation.

Their catalog remains central to the sound of modern trap, and their success helped make group rap a major force in contemporary hip-hop.
