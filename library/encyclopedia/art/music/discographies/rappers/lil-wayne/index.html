Style and Influence

Lil Wayne is known for rapid-fire punch lines, elastic wordplay, and a highly influential mixtape approach to rap. His music helped shape the sound of mainstream hip-hop in the late 2000s and 2010s.

He is also recognized for turning the Tha Carter series into one of rap's defining album franchises, while his mixtapes helped establish a standard for free-form, high-volume lyrical output.

Legacy

Lil Wayne is widely credited with helping bridge the gap between Southern rap, punchline-driven lyricism, and the modern era of melodic and internet-driven hip-hop.

His catalog includes some of the most influential releases of the 2000s and 2010s, and his influence can be heard across multiple generations of rappers who followed him.
