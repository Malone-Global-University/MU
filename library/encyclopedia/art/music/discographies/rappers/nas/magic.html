Magic is the fourteenth studio album by American rapper Nas. Released on December 24, 2021, through Mass Appeal Records, the album continued Nas's late-career collaboration with Hit-Boy and served as a short, focused release that bridged the success of King's Disease II and the next phase of his work.

Background

Magic was released as a surprise project and arrived only months after King's Disease II. The album extended the creative partnership between Nas and Hit-Boy and helped maintain the momentum of Nas's late-career resurgence.

The project was announced on December 23, 2021, along with its artwork and track list. Its compact format and sudden release gave it the feel of a spontaneous statement rather than a heavily promoted studio cycle.

Although brief, the album reflected Nas's confidence as a veteran artist who could still deliver sharp writing, layered references, and vivid storytelling in a condensed format.

Music and Themes

The album balances bravado, reflection, and lyrical precision. Nas moves between street memory, cultural commentary, and self-assurance, while Hit-Boy's production gives the record a polished but gritty atmosphere.

Recurring themes include:

  • Confidence and artistic longevity
  • Street history and memory
  • Wealth, power, and survival
  • Hip-hop legacy and reputation
  • Reflection and directness

The production is concise and muscular, with each track designed to support Nas's storytelling without unnecessary filler.

Notable Songs

Speechless

The opening track sets the tone with a calm but forceful delivery, immediately showing Nas's command of tone and narrative control.

Meet Joe Black

One of the album's standout songs, "Meet Joe Black" presents Nas in a confident, battle-ready mode with a memorable title and sharp verse structure.

Wave Gods

Featuring A$AP Rocky and DJ Premier, "Wave Gods" is one of the album's most notable collaborations and links Nas's modern era with one of his classic production partners.

The Truth

This song continues the album's direct style, with Nas reflecting on his career, his reputation, and the realities of staying relevant over time.

Dedicated

As the closing track, "Dedicated" brings the album to a focused finish and reinforces its compact, statement-like quality.

Reception

Upon release, Magic received generally positive reviews from critics, many of whom praised its brevity, atmosphere, and strong chemistry between Nas and Hit-Boy.

Listeners and critics often described the album as a sharp and effective stopgap release that sustained the momentum of Nas's run of acclaimed late-career projects.

Legacy

Magic is regarded as an important part of Nas's recent creative resurgence. Its concise structure and confident delivery helped strengthen the reputation of the Nas and Hit-Boy partnership.

The album also set the stage for the continuation of this era with King's Disease III and later projects.

Today, Magic is often seen as a compact but powerful entry in Nas's catalog.
