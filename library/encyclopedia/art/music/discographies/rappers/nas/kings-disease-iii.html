King's Disease III is the sixteenth studio album by American rapper Nas. Released on November 11, 2022, the album serves as the final installment in the King's Disease trilogy and continues his highly successful collaboration with producer Hit-Boy. The project is widely praised for its consistency, lyrical focus, and strong conclusion to the trilogy.

Background

Following the success of King's Disease and King's Disease II, Nas and Hit-Boy quickly returned to complete a third installment. The album was developed during the same creative run that also produced Magic and other related projects, marking one of the most productive periods of Nas’s late career.

Unlike the earlier entries, King's Disease III was announced relatively close to its release, building anticipation for the conclusion of the trilogy. The project continued the refined, mature tone established in the previous two albums while emphasizing sharper storytelling and more direct lyricism.

Music and Themes

The album focuses heavily on legacy, longevity, reflection, and lyrical mastery. Nas revisits themes from across his career while maintaining a present-day perspective shaped by decades in hip-hop.

Recurring themes include:

  • Legacy and artistic longevity
  • Queensbridge memory and street history
  • Success, wealth, and responsibility
  • Reflection on aging and time
  • Hip-hop culture and personal evolution
  • Competition and lyrical excellence

Hit-Boy’s production is cohesive and stripped of excess, allowing Nas’s writing to remain at the center. The album is often described as more focused and lyrically dense than its predecessors.

Notable Songs

Legit

A standout lyrical performance that showcases Nas’s technical skill and confidence, often highlighted as one of the strongest tracks in the trilogy.

Thun

A nostalgic track that revisits Queensbridge culture and early hip-hop identity, blending memory with modern reflection.

Michael & Quincy

A tribute-style record referencing greatness, legacy, and cultural influence, frequently praised for its emotional depth.

Once a Man, Twice a Child

A reflective closing-style track that emphasizes maturity, wisdom, and the cyclical nature of life and experience.

Beef

A lyrical showcase built around competition and hip-hop tradition, reinforcing Nas’s status as a veteran MC still engaged in rap’s competitive spirit.

Reception

Upon release, King's Disease III received widespread critical acclaim. Many reviewers considered it the strongest entry in the trilogy, praising its consistency, production quality, and lyrical focus.

The album was also noted for its lack of guest features, which placed full emphasis on Nas’s writing and performance throughout the project.

Fans and critics alike viewed the album as a high point in Nas’s modern career and a strong continuation of his late-career resurgence.

Legacy

King's Disease III is widely regarded as one of Nas’s most accomplished late-career albums. It successfully concluded the trilogy with a sense of completeness and artistic confidence.

The project strengthened Nas’s partnership with Hit-Boy and solidified this era as one of the most consistent and critically respected phases of his career.

Today, the album is frequently ranked among the best entries in the entire King's Disease series.
