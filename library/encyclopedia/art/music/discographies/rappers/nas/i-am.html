I Am... is the third studio album by American rapper Nas. Released on April 6, 1999, through Columbia Records, the album debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 and became one of the most commercially successful releases of his career. Originally conceived as a more ambitious double-album project, the final release blended street narratives, personal reflections, philosophical themes, and mainstream-oriented singles.

Background

Following the success of It Was Written, Nas began work on a large-scale project intended to trace a narrative spanning multiple stages of life and personal development. However, many unreleased recordings were leaked before the album's release, forcing significant changes to the final track selection.

As a result, portions of the original concept were abandoned, and several songs intended for the project later appeared on other releases. Despite these challenges, I Am... remained one of the most anticipated hip-hop albums of 1999.

The album arrived during a period when Nas was firmly established as one of rap's leading artists and represented an effort to balance artistic ambition with commercial success.

Music and Themes

The album explores a wide range of subjects, including spirituality, mortality, ambition, crime, fame, and self-reflection. Compared to some of Nas's earlier releases, I Am... shifts frequently between introspective material and commercially accessible songs.

Recurring themes include:

  • Personal growth and self-examination
  • Success and celebrity
  • Street life and survival
  • Spirituality and destiny
  • The consequences of violence
  • Legacy and mortality

The album's diverse subject matter reflects Nas's attempt to portray multiple dimensions of his life and worldview.

Notable Songs

Nas Is Like

Produced by DJ Premier, "Nas Is Like" is often considered one of the strongest songs in Nas's catalog. The track showcases his lyrical skill, intricate wordplay, and ability to construct vivid imagery.

Hate Me Now

Featuring Sean Combs, "Hate Me Now" became one of the album's biggest hits. Its triumphant production and themes of perseverance made it a defining single of Nas's career.

N.Y. State of Mind Pt. II

A sequel to the classic song from Illmatic, this track revisits themes of urban life and survival while reflecting Nas's evolution as an artist.

Undying Love

The album closes with an extended narrative song that is frequently praised as one of Nas's most ambitious storytelling performances.

Reception

Am... debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 and achieved platinum certification. Critics praised several individual songs, particularly "Nas Is Like," while opinions on the album as a whole were more mixed than for Nas's earlier releases.

Many reviewers noted the effects of the leaked material and speculated about how the original double-album concept might have differed from the final release. Despite such discussions, the album remained commercially successful and produced several enduring songs.

Legacy

Over time, I Am... has gained appreciation for its strongest tracks and its glimpse into a pivotal period of Nas's career. Songs such as "Nas Is Like" and "Hate Me Now" remain staples of his catalog and are frequently included in discussions of his greatest recordings.

The album also occupies an important place in hip-hop history because of its connection to one of the most famous unreleased and leaked projects of the late 1990s.

Today, I Am... is viewed as an important transitional work between the commercial success of It Was Written and the artistic resurgence that would later culminate in Stillmatic.
