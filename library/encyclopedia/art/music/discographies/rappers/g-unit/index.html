Group Members

The most recognizable lineup of G-Unit consisted of 50 Cent, Lloyd Banks, Tony Yayo, Young Buck, and later The Game during the group's commercial peak. Various members entered and exited the collective over time, while the G-Unit brand expanded into a record label and artist-development platform.

Legacy

G-Unit was one of the most successful hip-hop groups of the 2000s. Built around the success of 50 Cent, the group helped launch the careers of several major rap artists and became known for its mixtape culture, aggressive street-oriented sound, and strong commercial performance.

Beg for Mercy remains one of the best-selling rap group albums of its era, while the collective's influence can still be seen in modern artist collectives, label imprints, and mixtape-based promotional strategies.
