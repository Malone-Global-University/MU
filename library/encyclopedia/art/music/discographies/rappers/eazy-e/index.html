Style and Influence

Eazy-E is known for his distinctive high-pitched delivery and direct lyrical style that helped define early West Coast gangsta rap alongside N.W.A.

His work played a central role in bringing Compton’s street narratives into mainstream hip-hop culture during the late 1980s and early 1990s.

Legacy

Eazy-E remains one of the most influential figures in hip-hop history, particularly as a founding architect of gangsta rap and a key force behind Ruthless Records.

His impact continues through N.W.A’s legacy and the artists who emerged from the West Coast rap movement he helped establish.
