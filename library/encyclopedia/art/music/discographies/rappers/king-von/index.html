King Von Discography

A chronological overview of the recordings released by King Von, the Chicago drill rapper whose vivid storytelling and street narratives made him one of the most influential artists of the late 2010s drill movement. His catalog includes one studio album released during his lifetime, two posthumous studio albums, two mixtapes, numerous singles, and appearances on Only the Family projects. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Style and Influence

King Von became known for highly detailed storytelling, cinematic narratives, and first-person street tales that distinguished him from many of his contemporaries in drill music. Critics frequently pointed to his ability to turn neighborhood stories into compelling songs. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

His music combined traditional Chicago drill production with strong narrative structure, making records such as "Crazy Story" and "Wayne's Story" stand out within the genre. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

Legacy

Despite a recording career that lasted only a few years, King Von left behind one of the most influential catalogs in modern drill music. His releases continue to attract new listeners and remain highly discussed among fans of Chicago rap. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

The combination of Grandson, Vol. 1, Levon James, Welcome to O'Block, What It Means to Be King, and Grandson forms the core of a catalog that helped define a generation of drill music. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}
