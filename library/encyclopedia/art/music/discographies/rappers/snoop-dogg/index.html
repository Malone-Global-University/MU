  • Modern pop and hip-hop features with Pharrell Williams, Katy Perry, and others
  • Tha Eastsidaz and 213 group recordings (with Warren G and Nate Dogg)

Style and Sound

Snoop Dogg’s style is defined by laid-back rhythmic delivery, West Coast G-funk production, and conversational flow that became a signature of 1990s hip-hop.

His adaptability across decades has allowed him to remain relevant through gangsta rap, pop crossover, reggae experimentation, and modern streaming-era releases.

Legacy

Snoop Dogg is one of hip-hop’s most enduring cultural figures, with a career spanning multiple generations of West Coast rap.

His influence extends beyond music into media, branding, and global pop culture, making him one of the most recognizable artists in modern entertainment.
