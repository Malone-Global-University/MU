Style and Influence

Kurtis Blow helped define the early structure of recorded rap music, combining rhythmic spoken delivery with funk-based production that laid the groundwork for later hip-hop styles.

His success demonstrated that rap could exist as a commercially viable genre, influencing labels to invest in hip-hop artists throughout the 1980s.

Legacy

Kurtis Blow is widely recognized as one of the pioneers who helped transition hip-hop from underground block parties to mainstream recorded music culture.

His work remains foundational in the history of rap, particularly in establishing early album structures, single-driven success, and major-label hip-hop distribution.
