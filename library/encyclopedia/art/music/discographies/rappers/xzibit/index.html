Style and Sound

Xzibit’s music combines aggressive West Coast gangsta rap with cinematic production, heavily shaped by Dr. Dre’s influence during his commercial peak.

His earlier work emphasizes underground lyricism and battle-ready delivery, while later releases incorporate more polished, orchestral West Coast production styles.

Legacy

Xzibit remains a key figure in the evolution of West Coast hip-hop, bridging underground credibility with mainstream visibility through both music and television.

His catalog reflects the transition of 1990s Los Angeles rap into globally recognized mainstream hip-hop.
