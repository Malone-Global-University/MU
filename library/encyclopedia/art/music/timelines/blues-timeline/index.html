Blues Timeline

Trace blues through voice, guitar, community, migration, recording, electric sound, and influence on modern music.

Overview

The Blues Timeline connects blues music to African American history, work songs, spirituals, field hollers, guitar traditions, early recording, migration, urban scenes, and later popular music.

This page belongs to the Music Timelines area of the Malone Global University Music Department. Timeline pages should connect dates and periods to listening, artists, recordings, technologies, communities, institutions, and cultural context.

Roots and Early Forms

Blues roots include field hollers, work songs, spirituals, ballads, dance music, and African American vocal traditions.

Recording and Migration

Early recording and the Great Migration helped spread blues styles through records, radio, cities, and touring circuits.

Electric Blues and Influence

Electric blues shaped rhythm and blues, rock and roll, soul, funk, hip-hop sampling, and global guitar traditions.
