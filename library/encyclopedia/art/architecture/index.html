Architecture Overview

Architecture studies the planning, design, interpretation, and construction of built environments. It combines artistic judgment with structural logic, environmental awareness, spatial experience, and cultural meaning. Across buildings, cities, landscapes, and public spaces, architectural disciplines examine how humans organize shelter, movement, memory, community, and place through physical design.

Disciplines

  • Architectural History

    Study the historical development of buildings, styles, movements, civilizations, construction traditions, and architectural periods.

  • Architectural Theory

    Examine the philosophical, aesthetic, social, and conceptual ideas that shape architectural design and interpretation.

  • Building Design

    Learn the practical design of structures through spatial planning, function, form, circulation, materials, and human use.

  • Urban Design

    Study the organization of cities, streets, districts, public spaces, infrastructure, density, movement, and urban experience.

  • Landscape Architecture

    Explore the design of outdoor environments through landform, ecology, planting, circulation, public space, and environmental integration.
