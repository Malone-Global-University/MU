Food Science Overview

Food science studies the chemical, biological, and physical properties of food and the ways those properties change during preparation, storage, and consumption. The field explains why ingredients behave as they do and how culinary processes alter structure, flavor, and safety. It provides the scientific basis for both traditional and modern culinary practice.

Disciplines

  • Emulsions and Foams

    Study dispersed food systems formed by stabilized mixtures of immiscible phases.

  • Fermentation

    Examine microbial transformation of food through controlled biological processes.

  • Food Chemistry

    Study molecular composition and chemical reactions occurring in food systems.

  • Food Microbiology

    Learn microbial activity in food including spoilage, fermentation, and safety concerns.

  • Molecular Gastronomy

    Explore scientific and experimental culinary techniques based on food behavior principles.
