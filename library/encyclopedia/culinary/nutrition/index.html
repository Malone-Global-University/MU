Nutrition Overview

Nutrition studies how food and nutrients affect human health, physiology, growth, and disease prevention. The field examines dietary composition, nutritional requirements, meal planning, and population-level dietary systems. Across macro and micronutrients, dietary planning, public nutrition, and special diets, nutrition connects food science with health outcomes.

Disciplines

  • Dietary Planning

    Study structured meal design based on nutritional goals and dietary requirements.

  • Macronutrients

    Examine proteins, fats, and carbohydrates as primary dietary energy sources.

  • Micronutrients

    Study vitamins, minerals, and trace nutrients required for physiological function.

  • Public Nutrition

    Explore nutrition policy, population health, and large-scale dietary guidance systems.

  • Special Diets

    Study dietary systems designed for medical, ethical, or specialized nutritional needs.
