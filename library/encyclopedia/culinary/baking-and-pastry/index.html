Baking and Pastry Overview

Baking and pastry study the preparation of breads, desserts, pastries, confectionery, and laminated dough products through controlled chemical, thermal, and mechanical processes. The field emphasizes precision, measurement, timing, and technical consistency. Across breads, pastry work, desserts, confectionery, and laminated doughs, the discipline governs structured sweet and baked culinary production.

Disciplines

  • Bread Making

    Study dough fermentation, gluten development, shaping, proofing, and bread baking techniques.

  • Cake and Dessert Production

    Examine preparation of structured desserts, cakes, fillings, frostings, and plated sweets.

  • Confectionery

    Study candy-making, sugar work, chocolate handling, and confection production techniques.

  • Laminated Doughs

    Learn layered dough preparation through folding, butter incorporation, and lamination technique.

  • Pastry Techniques

    Study preparation of pastry doughs, fillings, shaping, finishing, and decorative methods.
