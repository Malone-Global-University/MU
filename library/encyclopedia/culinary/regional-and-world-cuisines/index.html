Regional and World Cuisines Overview

Regional and world cuisines study the culinary traditions, ingredients, preparation methods, and food cultures associated with major geographic and cultural regions. The field examines how environment, history, trade, religion, and social practice shape food systems. It provides a comparative understanding of global culinary diversity and tradition.

Disciplines

  • African Cuisine

    Study culinary traditions, ingredients, and preparation methods from African regions.

  • Asian Cuisine

    Explore food traditions and cooking systems across Asian culinary cultures.

  • European Cuisine

    Study classical and regional culinary traditions of Europe.

  • Latin American Cuisine

    Examine culinary traditions of Central and South America and related cultures.

  • Middle Eastern Cuisine

    Study food systems, flavors, and culinary practices of Middle Eastern cultures.

  • North American Cuisine

    Explore culinary traditions and food systems of North American regions.
