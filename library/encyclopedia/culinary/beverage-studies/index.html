Beverage Studies Overview

Beverage studies examine the preparation, service, pairing, and sensory evaluation of drinks used in culinary and hospitality settings. The field includes brewed beverages, specialty preparations, service standards, and pairing methodologies. Across coffee, tea, non-alcoholic beverages, pairing, and beverage service, the discipline explores beverage craftsmanship and integration into dining experiences.

Disciplines

  • Beverage Pairing

    Study matching beverages with foods based on flavor, texture, balance, and complementarity.

  • Beverage Service

    Examine standards and techniques for professional beverage presentation and service.

  • Coffee

    Study coffee cultivation, roasting, brewing, tasting, and preparation methods.

  • Non-Alcoholic Beverages

    Explore preparation and service of juices, sodas, mocktails, and specialty drinks.

  • Tea

    Study tea varieties, brewing methods, preparation standards, and sensory characteristics.
