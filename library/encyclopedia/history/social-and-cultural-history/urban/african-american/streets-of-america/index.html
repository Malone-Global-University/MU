Streets of America

Explore the origins, growth, transformation, and decline of African American street organizations through the history of migration, segregation, urban change, and community life.

Book Overview

Streets of America: The Origins, Rise, and Decline of African American Street Gangs examines the historical development of African American street organizations within the broader context of urban history. The work explores migration, segregation, housing, labor, deindustrialization, the War on Drugs, incarceration, gentrification, and neighborhood change.

The book approaches street organizations as historical institutions shaped by social, economic, and political forces rather than simply as criminal phenomena.

Chapters

  • Chapter 1 — The Great Migration and Urban Foundations

    Examine the migration of African Americans from the rural South and the formation of urban Black communities.

  • Chapter 2 — Segregation and the Making of Black Neighborhoods

    Explore segregation, restrictive housing practices, and the development of urban Black neighborhoods.

  • Chapter 3 — Youth Groups and Early Street Organizations

    Study the emergence of neighborhood clubs, peer groups, and early street organizations.

  • Chapter 4 — Chicago, Los Angeles, and Organizational Growth

    Examine the rise of major street organizations and the influence of urban geography and local conditions.

  • Chapter 5 — Deindustrialization and Urban Crisis

    Analyze economic decline, unemployment, and the transformation of American cities.

  • Chapter 6 — The Crack Era and Drug Economy

    Explore the impact of crack cocaine markets and the escalation of violence during the 1980s and 1990s.

  • Chapter 7 — The War on Drugs and Mass Incarceration

    Examine policing, sentencing policy, and the expansion of the American prison system.

  • Chapter 8 — Media, Music, and Public Perception

    Study the portrayal of gangs in journalism, film, television, and popular music.

  • Chapter 9 — Fragmentation and Changing Street Culture

    Explore the decline of traditional organizations and the rise of smaller, less structured street groups.

  • Chapter 10 — Community Responses and Violence Prevention

    Examine grassroots efforts, intervention programs, and local attempts to reduce violence.

  • Chapter 11 — Gentrification and Urban Transformation

    Study redevelopment, displacement, and the changing geography of urban neighborhoods.

  • Chapter 12 — Legacy, Decline, and the Future of Urban Street Organizations

    Consider the historical legacy of street organizations and the future of urban communities.
