Early Modern History Overview

Early modern history studies the transitional period between the medieval and modern worlds, marked by major intellectual, religious, scientific, political, and exploratory transformation. The field examines developments that reshaped institutions, knowledge systems, and global interaction between roughly the fifteenth and eighteenth centuries.

Disciplines

  • Age of Exploration

    Study global exploration, maritime expansion, and early colonial encounters.

  • Enlightenment

    Examine intellectual movements emphasizing reason, science, and liberal thought.

  • Reformation

    Study religious reform movements and transformation of Christian institutions.

  • Renaissance

    Explore revival of classical learning, art, and humanistic thought.

  • Scientific Revolution

    Study emergence of modern scientific thought and method.
