Historical Foundations Overview

Historical foundations study the conceptual, methodological, and philosophical principles used to investigate and interpret the past. The field examines historiography, source criticism, chronology, historical method, and competing theories of historical explanation. It provides the analytical framework for rigorous historical scholarship.

Disciplines

  • Chronology and Periodization

    Study systems for organizing and dividing history into temporal frameworks.

  • Historical Methodology

    Examine structured methods for researching and reconstructing historical events.

  • Historiography

    Study how history is written, interpreted, and debated by historians.

  • Philosophy of History

    Explore theoretical questions concerning historical meaning and causation.

  • Source Analysis

    Study evaluation and interpretation of primary and secondary historical sources.
