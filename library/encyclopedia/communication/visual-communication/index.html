Visual Communication Overview

Visual communication studies how meaning is created and transmitted through images, layout, symbols, typography, diagrams, graphics, and visual systems. It combines design principles with communication theory to organize information, persuade audiences, and support understanding. Across graphic messaging, information design, visual rhetoric, presentation design, and communication graphics, the discipline examines how visual form shapes interpretation.

Disciplines

  • Graphic Messaging

    Study visual messages built through images, symbols, typography, layout, hierarchy, style, and communicative intent.

  • Information Design

    Examine organization of complex information through diagrams, charts, labels, layouts, navigation, and visual clarity.

  • Visual Rhetoric

    Explore persuasion through images, composition, symbolism, framing, emphasis, visual evidence, and audience interpretation.

  • Presentation Design

    Study visual support for spoken or written presentations through slide structure, hierarchy, pacing, graphics, and readability.

  • Communication Graphics

    Learn creation of graphics used to explain, guide, compare, instruct, summarize, and communicate structured information.
