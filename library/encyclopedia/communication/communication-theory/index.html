Communication Theory Overview

Communication theory studies the conceptual frameworks used to explain how information, meaning, symbols, and messages are produced, transmitted, interpreted, and socially understood. It examines communication as both a technical process and a human meaning-making system. Across models, rhetoric, persuasion, semiotics, and discourse analysis, the field provides foundational theory for understanding communication in all contexts.

Disciplines

  • Models of Communication

    Study formal frameworks describing how messages move between senders, receivers, channels, and contexts.

  • Rhetoric

    Examine persuasive language, argument structure, audience adaptation, and strategic discourse construction.

  • Persuasion

    Learn mechanisms of influence through messaging, framing, appeals, credibility, and behavioral change.

  • Semiotics

    Study signs, symbols, representation, and systems of meaning in communication.

  • Discourse Analysis

    Explore how language functions within social, cultural, institutional, and conversational contexts.
