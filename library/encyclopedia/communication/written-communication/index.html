Written Communication Overview

Written communication studies the structured use of language in textual form to inform, persuade, document, explain, and coordinate across personal, organizational, and public settings. It emphasizes clarity, audience adaptation, organization, and rhetorical effectiveness. Across professional, technical, persuasive, business, and editorial writing, the discipline examines how written language functions in practical communication systems.

Disciplines

  • Professional Writing

    Study workplace communication through formal documents, correspondence, and structured written materials.

  • Technical Writing

    Learn specialized explanatory writing for technical systems, instructions, and documentation.

  • Persuasive Writing

    Examine written argumentation designed to influence beliefs, attitudes, or actions.

  • Business Writing

    Study organizational writing used in commercial, managerial, and operational contexts.

  • Editorial Writing

    Explore opinion-based and interpretive writing for public commentary and publication.
