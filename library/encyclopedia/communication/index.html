Communication Overview

Communication is the study of how information, meaning, emotion, and persuasion are transmitted between individuals, groups, institutions, and societies through verbal, written, visual, and mediated forms. It examines the structures, strategies, and systems through which messages are created, interpreted, and influenced by context, culture, audience, and medium. Communication disciplines analyze both interpersonal interaction and large-scale media environments to understand how ideas shape behavior, relationships, institutions, and public discourse.

Categories

  • Communication Theory

    Study foundational models and theories of communication including rhetoric, persuasion, semiotics, discourse analysis, and message systems.

  • Interpersonal Communication

    Examine one-to-one and small-group interaction including conversation dynamics, negotiation, conflict resolution, emotional intelligence, and relationship communication.

  • Public Speaking

    Explore oral presentation disciplines including speech writing, audience analysis, debate, rhetorical delivery, and presentation performance.

  • Written Communication

    Study structured writing for professional, technical, persuasive, editorial, and business communication contexts.

  • Journalism

    Analyze news production, reporting methods, investigative journalism, editorial writing, broadcast communication, and feature storytelling.

  • Media Studies

    Investigate mass media systems, digital platforms, audience behavior, media ethics, and theoretical approaches to mediated communication.

  • Public Relations

    Study strategic reputation management, crisis communication, stakeholder messaging, press relations, and institutional brand communication.

  • Marketing Communication

    Explore persuasive promotional messaging including advertising, copywriting, campaign strategy, and brand-focused communication systems.

  • Organizational Communication

    Examine internal and corporate communication systems including leadership messaging, change communication, and institutional information flow.

  • Visual Communication

    Study communication through visual design including information graphics, presentation design, visual rhetoric, and graphical messaging systems.
