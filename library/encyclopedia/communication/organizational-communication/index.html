Organizational Communication Overview

Organizational communication studies how information, meaning, direction, and coordination move through formal institutions. It examines internal messaging, leadership communication, corporate identity, change processes, and communication infrastructure. Across internal communication, leadership communication, corporate messaging, change communication, and communication systems, the discipline explains how organizations align people, decisions, culture, and action.

Disciplines

  • Internal Communication

    Study employee-facing messaging through announcements, meetings, policies, updates, culture-building, and operational coordination.

  • Leadership Communication

    Examine how leaders communicate vision, priorities, decisions, expectations, feedback, trust, and organizational direction.

  • Corporate Messaging

    Learn coordinated organizational language used to express identity, mission, strategy, values, and institutional position.

  • Change Communication

    Study communication during transformation through explanation, timing, stakeholder alignment, resistance management, and adoption.

  • Communication Systems

    Explore channels, tools, structures, protocols, and routines that organize information flow inside institutions.
