Journalism Overview

Journalism studies the collection, verification, interpretation, and publication of information for public audiences. It emphasizes accuracy, timeliness, sourcing, ethics, narrative structure, and civic responsibility. Across news reporting, investigative journalism, broadcast journalism, feature writing, and editorial journalism, the discipline examines how information becomes public knowledge through professional reporting and media institutions.

Disciplines

  • News Reporting

    Study factual reporting of current events through sourcing, verification, interviewing, structure, deadlines, and public information.

  • Investigative Journalism

    Examine in-depth reporting through documents, sources, evidence, accountability, public records, and long-form investigation.

  • Broadcast Journalism

    Learn news presentation for television, radio, and streaming through scripts, visuals, voice, timing, and live reporting.

  • Feature Writing

    Explore narrative journalism through human-interest stories, profiles, descriptive reporting, scene-building, and long-form structure.

  • Editorial Journalism

    Study opinion journalism through argument, commentary, institutional voice, public issues, interpretation, and editorial judgment.
