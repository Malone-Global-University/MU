Interpersonal Communication Overview

Interpersonal communication studies direct human interaction between individuals in personal, professional, and social settings. It examines conversation, emotional expression, listening, conflict, negotiation, and relationship maintenance. Across conversation dynamics, conflict resolution, negotiation, relationship communication, and emotional intelligence, the discipline analyzes how people exchange meaning and manage relationships.

Disciplines

  • Conversation Dynamics

    Study turn-taking, flow, timing, cues, interruptions, and conversational structure.

  • Conflict Resolution

    Examine communication strategies for managing disagreement, tension, and interpersonal disputes.

  • Negotiation

    Learn structured bargaining, persuasion, compromise, and agreement-building techniques.

  • Relationship Communication

    Study communication patterns within friendships, partnerships, and interpersonal bonds.

  • Emotional Intelligence

    Explore recognition, regulation, and communication of emotion in social interaction.
