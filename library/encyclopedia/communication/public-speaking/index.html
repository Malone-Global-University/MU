Public Speaking Overview

Public speaking studies the preparation and delivery of spoken communication before audiences. It examines rhetorical structure, speech composition, oral presentation, persuasion, and audience engagement in formal speaking contexts. Across speech writing, presentation skills, debate, oral rhetoric, and audience analysis, the discipline focuses on effective spoken persuasion and public expression.

Disciplines

  • Speech Writing

    Study preparation of structured oral presentations through organization, messaging, and rhetorical planning.

  • Presentation Skills

    Learn vocal, physical, and visual delivery techniques for live presentations.

  • Debate

    Examine structured argumentative exchange through logic, rebuttal, evidence, and oral advocacy.

  • Oral Rhetoric

    Study persuasive spoken language and strategic audience-directed communication.

  • Audience Analysis

    Explore adaptation of messages based on audience characteristics, expectations, and needs.
