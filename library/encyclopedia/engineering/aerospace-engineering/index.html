Aerospace Engineering Overview

Aerospace engineering studies the design, analysis, and operation of aircraft, spacecraft, and related systems operating within atmospheric and space environments. The field examines flight mechanics, propulsion, aerodynamics, structural design, and spacecraft systems. It applies advanced engineering principles to vehicles operating in air and space domains.

Disciplines

  • Aerodynamics

    Study airflow, lift, drag, and aerodynamic forces acting on moving bodies.

  • Aerospace Structures

    Examine structural design and stress behavior of aerospace vehicles and components.

  • Flight Mechanics

    Study motion, stability, control, and performance of flight vehicles.

  • Propulsion Systems

    Explore engines and thrust-generation systems for aerospace applications.

  • Spacecraft Engineering

    Study design and operation of spacecraft and orbital systems.
