Civil Engineering Overview

Civil engineering studies the design, construction, and maintenance of infrastructure and the built environment. The field examines structures, transportation systems, geotechnics, water management, and construction methods. It focuses on engineering safe, durable, and functional physical systems supporting human society.

Disciplines

  • Construction Engineering

    Study planning, execution, and management of construction projects and systems.

  • Geotechnical Engineering

    Examine soil, rock, and earth-material behavior in engineering applications.

  • Structural Engineering

    Study design and analysis of load-bearing structures and frameworks.

  • Transportation Engineering

    Explore engineering of transport systems including roads, transit, and traffic networks.

  • Water Resources

    Study management and engineering of water systems and hydraulic infrastructure.
