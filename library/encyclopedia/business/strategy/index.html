Strategy Overview

Strategy studies how organizations choose direction, compete, allocate resources, and build durable advantage. It examines the relationship between internal capabilities, external markets, rivals, customers, uncertainty, and long-term goals. Across competitive strategy, corporate strategy, strategic analysis, market positioning, and decision frameworks, the discipline explains how leaders make coherent choices that shape organizational outcomes.

Disciplines

  • Competitive Strategy

    Study firm rivalry through advantage, differentiation, cost position, industry structure, barriers, competition, and market defense.

  • Corporate Strategy

    Examine organization-wide direction through diversification, portfolio management, acquisitions, resource allocation, and enterprise scope.

  • Market Positioning

    Study how organizations define their place in the market through value proposition, customer segments, differentiation, and perception.

  • Decision Frameworks

    Explore structured methods for making strategic choices through tradeoffs, models, scenarios, criteria, and judgment under uncertainty.

  • Strategic Analysis

    Learn evaluation of markets, competitors, capabilities, risks, opportunities, constraints, and strategic alternatives.
