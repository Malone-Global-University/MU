Marketing Overview

Marketing studies how organizations understand customers, define value, communicate offerings, and build relationships with markets. It connects research, positioning, branding, promotion, persuasion, sales, and customer behavior into practical systems for demand creation. Across branding, consumer behavior, digital marketing, market research, advertising, and sales strategy, marketing examines how organizations earn attention, trust, preference, and revenue.

Disciplines

  • Branding

    Study the creation of organizational identity through naming, visual systems, messaging, reputation, differentiation, and recognition.

  • Consumer Behavior

    Examine how customers make decisions through perception, motivation, culture, emotion, habits, needs, and purchasing patterns.

  • Digital Marketing

    Learn online promotion through search, social media, email, content, analytics, advertising platforms, and conversion systems.

  • Market Research

    Study methods for gathering customer and market insight through surveys, interviews, data analysis, segmentation, and competitive research.

  • Advertising

    Explore paid communication through campaign strategy, creative messaging, media placement, persuasion, audience targeting, and performance measurement.

  • Sales Strategy

    Examine revenue generation through prospecting, positioning, negotiation, pipeline management, customer relationships, and sales process design.
