Real Estate Overview

Real estate studies land, buildings, property rights, financing, development, valuation, investment, and asset operation. It connects physical space with financial analysis, market demand, legal structures, construction, leasing, and long-term ownership strategy. Across property investment, real estate finance, development, appraisal, and property management, the discipline examines how built assets are acquired, valued, improved, and operated.

Disciplines

  • Property Investment

    Study acquisition and ownership of real estate assets through returns, risk, cash flow, market cycles, and portfolio strategy.

  • Real Estate Finance

    Examine funding of property transactions through mortgages, leverage, underwriting, debt structures, equity, and investment analysis.

  • Development

    Learn transformation of land and buildings through planning, entitlement, construction, feasibility, design, and project delivery.

  • Appraisal

    Study property valuation through comparable sales, income analysis, cost approaches, market evidence, and professional judgment.

  • Property Management

    Explore operation of real estate assets through leasing, maintenance, tenant relations, budgeting, compliance, and asset performance.
