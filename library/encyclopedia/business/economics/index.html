Economics Overview

Economics studies how individuals, firms, governments, and societies allocate scarce resources under conditions of constraint and incentive. It analyzes production, exchange, distribution, labor, markets, money, and policy using theoretical and quantitative frameworks. Across micro, macro, monetary, international, behavioral, and econometric disciplines, economics explains systemic patterns of decision-making and resource allocation.

Disciplines

  • Microeconomics

    Study individual decision-making, firm behavior, pricing, incentives, and market interactions.

  • Macroeconomics

    Examine aggregate economic systems including inflation, growth, unemployment, and fiscal policy.

  • International Economics

    Learn trade, exchange rates, global capital flows, and cross-border economic systems.

  • Monetary Economics

    Study money supply, banking systems, inflation, and monetary policy mechanisms.

  • Development Economics

    Explore economic growth, poverty, institutional development, and emerging market systems.

  • Labor Economics

    Study labor markets, wages, employment, human capital, and workforce incentives.

  • Behavioral Economics

    Examine economic decisions influenced by psychology, bias, and bounded rationality.

  • Econometrics

    Learn statistical and quantitative methods for economic measurement and modeling.
