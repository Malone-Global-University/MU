Accounting Overview

Accounting studies the systematic measurement, classification, reporting, and analysis of financial activity within organizations. It provides the information infrastructure used to evaluate performance, maintain compliance, allocate resources, assess risk, and support decision-making. Across financial, managerial, cost, tax, auditing, and forensic disciplines, accounting translates economic activity into structured financial intelligence.

Disciplines

  • Financial Accounting

    Study preparation of financial statements, external reporting standards, disclosures, and stakeholder-facing accounting records.

  • Managerial Accounting

    Learn internal financial analysis used for planning, budgeting, forecasting, and management decision-making.

  • Cost Accounting

    Examine cost measurement, allocation, tracking, and operational cost analysis across business activities.

  • Tax Accounting

    Study taxation rules, tax planning, compliance, filings, and reporting obligations for entities.

  • Auditing

    Explore independent examination of financial records, controls, procedures, and compliance verification.

  • Forensic Accounting

    Learn investigative accounting techniques used in fraud detection, litigation, and financial dispute analysis.
