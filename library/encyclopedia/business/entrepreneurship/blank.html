Entrepreneurship Overview

Entrepreneurship studies the creation, development, financing, and scaling of new ventures. It examines how founders identify opportunities, validate demand, design business models, assemble resources, manage uncertainty, and pursue growth. Across startup formation, venture finance, product-market fit, business model design, and growth strategy, entrepreneurship connects innovation with execution, risk, markets, and organizational development.

Disciplines

  • Startup Formation

    Study the process of creating new ventures through legal structure, founding teams, early planning, validation, and launch preparation.

  • Venture Finance

    Learn startup funding through equity financing, venture capital, angel investment, fundraising strategy, dilution, and investor relations.

  • Product-Market Fit

    Examine alignment between customer needs, product value, market demand, adoption behavior, retention, and commercial viability.

  • Business Model Design

    Explore how ventures create, deliver, and capture value through revenue logic, customer segments, channels, and operating structure.

  • Growth Strategy

    Study methods for scaling ventures through customer acquisition, market expansion, operational capacity, partnerships, and repeatable growth.
