Management Overview

Management studies how organizations coordinate people, resources, decisions, and systems to achieve goals. It examines leadership, behavior, planning, execution, operations, adaptation, and performance across formal institutions. Across organizational behavior, leadership, strategic management, operations management, project management, and change management, the discipline connects human coordination with organizational structure and practical execution.

Disciplines

  • Organizational Behavior

    Study human behavior in organizations through motivation, teams, culture, communication, conflict, decision-making, and workplace dynamics.

  • Leadership

    Examine influence, direction, judgment, communication, responsibility, team development, vision-setting, and organizational guidance.

  • Strategic Management

    Learn organizational direction through competitive analysis, planning, resource allocation, execution, performance, and long-term positioning.

  • Operations Management

    Study production and service systems through capacity, workflow, scheduling, quality, process design, and operational efficiency.

  • Project Management

    Explore structured execution of temporary work through scope, scheduling, budgeting, risk management, coordination, and delivery.

  • Change Management

    Study organizational transition through adoption, communication, resistance, implementation planning, stakeholder alignment, and adaptation.
