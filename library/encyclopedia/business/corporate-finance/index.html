Corporate Finance Overview

Corporate finance studies how organizations acquire capital, allocate financial resources, evaluate investments, manage liquidity, and maximize enterprise value. It focuses on strategic financial decision-making surrounding funding, valuation, budgeting, and transaction execution. Across capital structure, modeling, valuation, M&A, treasury, and budgeting disciplines, corporate finance governs the financial strategy of firms.

Disciplines

  • Capital Structure

    Study organizational financing mix through debt, equity, leverage, and capital optimization.

  • Financial Modeling

    Learn quantitative forecasting and valuation model construction for strategic analysis.

  • Valuation

    Examine methods for determining enterprise, equity, and asset value.

  • Mergers and Acquisitions

    Study corporate transaction strategy, deal structuring, integration, and acquisition analysis.

  • Treasury Management

    Explore liquidity, cash management, funding operations, and financial risk control.

  • Capital Budgeting

    Learn long-term investment evaluation and project selection using financial criteria.
