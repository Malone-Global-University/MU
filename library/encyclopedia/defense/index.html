Defense Overview

Defense is the study of military power, strategic competition, warfare, security institutions, and the systems used to protect national interests through organized force and deterrence. It examines how armed forces are structured, trained, equipped, sustained, and employed across tactical, operational, and strategic levels of conflict. Defense disciplines integrate history, doctrine, logistics, intelligence, technology, and policy to understand how military institutions prepare for, conduct, and adapt to war and security challenges.

Categories

  • Military History

    Study the evolution of warfare from ancient conflicts to contemporary combat, including campaigns, doctrine shifts, and historical military development.

  • Martial Arts

    Explore systems of armed and unarmed combat, including striking, grappling, weapons training, self-defense, discipline, physical conditioning, historical traditions, and the role of martial practice in military, cultural, and personal development.

  • Strategic Studies

    Examine high-level military and geopolitical strategy including deterrence, escalation, campaign design, and strategic analysis.

  • Tactics

    Explore battlefield-level combat methods including infantry tactics, maneuver warfare, armored operations, and artillery employment.

  • Operational Art

    Study the planning and execution of campaigns and theater operations linking tactical actions to strategic objectives.

  • Intelligence

    Analyze intelligence collection, analysis, and tradecraft including SIGINT, HUMINT, GEOINT, counterintelligence, and the intelligence cycle.

  • Logistics

    Examine military sustainment systems including supply chains, transportation, maintenance, force support, and operational sustainment.

  • Weapons Systems

    Study the design, employment, and operational roles of weapon platforms across land, sea, air, and missile domains.

  • Defense Technology

    Explore battlefield technologies including communications, surveillance, electronic warfare, autonomous systems, and advanced combat infrastructure.

  • National Security

    Investigate homeland defense, security institutions, strategic risk, crisis management, and defense policy systems.

  • Military Profession

    Study leadership, doctrine, ethics, training systems, and civil-military relations within professional armed forces.
