Military Profession Overview

The military profession studies the institutional, ethical, doctrinal, and leadership foundations of military service as a professional vocation. The field examines command responsibility, training systems, military ethics, doctrine development, and the relationship between armed forces and civilian authority. It defines the norms and standards governing professional military conduct.

Disciplines

  • Civil-Military Relations

    Study the relationship between military institutions and civilian political authority.

  • Doctrine

    Examine formal principles guiding military planning, operations, and conduct.

  • Ethics of War

    Study moral principles governing warfare and military conduct.

  • Leadership

    Study command, influence, responsibility, and professional leadership in military settings.

  • Training

    Examine systems for preparing military personnel for operational performance.
