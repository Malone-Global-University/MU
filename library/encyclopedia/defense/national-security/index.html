National Security Overview

National security studies the protection of a state’s people, institutions, territory, and strategic interests from internal and external threats. The field examines policy, institutions, risk assessment, emergency response, and strategic planning across governmental security systems. It connects defense, intelligence, law enforcement, diplomacy, and domestic resilience into an integrated framework for national protection.

Disciplines

  • Crisis Management

    Study planning and response systems for emergencies, disasters, and strategic crises.

  • Defense Policy

    Examine formulation of national military and defense strategic policy.

  • Homeland Security

    Study domestic protection systems against terrorism, disaster, and internal threats.

  • Security Institutions

    Explore organizations and agencies responsible for national security functions.

  • Strategic Risk

    Study assessment of large-scale threats and vulnerabilities affecting national security.
