Social Science Foundations Overview

Social science foundations introduces the major concepts, theories, methods, and philosophical questions used to study human societies and social behavior. This discipline examines how researchers explain institutions, culture, power, identity, inequality, group behavior, social change, and patterns of collective life through both empirical evidence and theoretical interpretation. It includes social theory, research methodology, quantitative analysis, qualitative analysis, and philosophy of social science, each of which supports a different part of disciplined social inquiry. By studying social science foundations, learners gain a framework for understanding how social knowledge is produced, how evidence is interpreted, how theories guide analysis, and how different methods reveal different dimensions of human life.

Categories

  • Philosophy of Social Science

    Study questions about explanation, objectivity, interpretation, causation, evidence, values, human agency, social structure, and the nature of knowledge in the social sciences.

  • Qualitative Analysis

    Explore methods for interpreting meanings, experiences, narratives, interviews, observations, texts, communities, symbols, and social practices through close contextual analysis.

  • Quantitative Analysis

    Learn how numerical data are used to study social patterns through measurement, statistics, surveys, models, variables, comparison, correlation, and evidence-based inference.

  • Research Methodology

    Examine how social research is designed and evaluated through questions, sampling, data collection, ethics, validity, reliability, case selection, comparison, and interpretation.

  • Social Theory

    Study major frameworks for explaining society, including power, institutions, culture, class, identity, interaction, social order, inequality, modernization, and social change.
