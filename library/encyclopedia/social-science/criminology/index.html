Criminology Overview

Criminology studies crime as a social, legal, psychological, institutional, and behavioral phenomenon. This discipline examines why crimes occur, how criminal behavior develops, how societies define and respond to offenses, how law enforcement systems operate, how correctional institutions manage punishment and rehabilitation, and how criminal justice policy shapes public safety and legal accountability. It includes crime theory, criminal behavior, law enforcement systems, corrections, and criminal justice policy, each of which provides a different lens for understanding the causes, consequences, and control of crime. By studying criminology, learners gain a framework for analyzing crime patterns, offender behavior, justice institutions, public safety strategies, punishment systems, and the social conditions that influence criminal activity and justice outcomes.

Categories

  • Corrections

    Study systems of punishment, supervision, rehabilitation, incarceration, probation, parole, reentry, prison administration, correctional policy, and the management of people convicted of crimes.

  • Crime Theory

    Explore theories explaining crime, including social strain, social learning, rational choice, control theory, labeling theory, biological factors, environmental influences, and structural inequality.

  • Criminal Behavior

    Examine the psychological, social, developmental, environmental, and situational factors that influence offending, victimization, repeat behavior, decision-making, and patterns of criminal activity.

  • Criminal Justice Policy

    Learn how laws, sentencing rules, policing strategies, diversion programs, rehabilitation models, public safety priorities, and institutional reforms shape criminal justice outcomes.

  • Law Enforcement Systems

    Study police institutions, investigative practices, patrol systems, community policing, accountability, use of force, intelligence gathering, crime prevention, and coordination with courts and corrections.
