Psychology Overview

Psychology studies mental processes, behavior, emotion, development, personality, social interaction, and the biological foundations of human experience. This discipline examines how people perceive, learn, remember, think, feel, relate to others, form identities, respond to stress, and adapt across the lifespan. It includes cognitive psychology, developmental psychology, social psychology, personality psychology, abnormal psychology, clinical psychology, and behavioral neuroscience, each of which provides a different lens for understanding human behavior. By studying psychology, learners gain a framework for analyzing individual experience, social behavior, mental health, brain-behavior relationships, developmental change, and the scientific methods used to study the mind.

Categories

  • Abnormal Psychology

    Study patterns of psychological disorder, including symptoms, classification, causes, risk factors, diagnosis, treatment approaches, distress, impairment, and models of mental health.

  • Behavioral Neuroscience

    Examine how the brain, nervous system, hormones, genes, and biological processes influence behavior, emotion, learning, memory, perception, and mental health.

  • Clinical Psychology

    Explore psychological assessment, diagnosis, therapy, research-based treatment, emotional functioning, cognitive patterns, personality, and mental health care across diverse populations.

  • Cognitive Psychology

    Learn how people perceive, attend, remember, reason, solve problems, make decisions, use language, and process information through mental systems.

  • Developmental Psychology

    Study psychological growth and change across the lifespan, including infancy, childhood, adolescence, adulthood, aging, cognition, emotion, identity, and social development.

  • Personality Psychology

    Examine individual differences in traits, motives, identity, temperament, self-concept, behavior patterns, personality development, and theories of stable psychological characteristics.

  • Social Psychology

    Explore how thoughts, emotions, attitudes, identity, perception, behavior, conformity, persuasion, prejudice, relationships, and group dynamics are shaped by social contexts.
