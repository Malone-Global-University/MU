Social Science Overview

Social Science is the systematic study of human behavior, social interaction, institutions, culture, and the structures through which societies organize collective life. It applies empirical, theoretical, and interpretive methods to understand individuals, groups, populations, and social systems across historical and contemporary contexts. Social science integrates psychology, sociology, anthropology, behavioral analysis, demography, and related disciplines to explain how people think, behave, organize, and influence one another within society.

Categories

  • Social Science Foundations

    Study the theories, methodologies, and philosophical foundations underlying social scientific inquiry and analysis.

  • Anthropology

    Explore human cultures, biological development, archaeology, language, and comparative cultural systems.

  • Psychology

    Study mental processes and behavior including cognition, development, personality, abnormal psychology, and clinical practice.

  • Sociology

    Examine social structures, institutions, inequality, family systems, social change, and collective movements.

  • Criminology

    Analyze crime, criminal behavior, justice systems, law enforcement, corrections, and criminal policy.

  • Human Geography

    Study the spatial organization of human societies including culture, demographics, economics, politics, and urban development.

  • Behavioral Science

    Explore decision-making, group dynamics, behavioral models, social influence, and organizational behavior.

  • Demography

    Examine population systems including migration, fertility, mortality, census analysis, and population policy.

  • Economics and Society

    Study the interaction of economic systems and social structures including labor, inequality, development, and institutional analysis.

  • Interdisciplinary Social Studies

    Explore cross-disciplinary analysis of identity, ethnicity, gender, media, culture, and urban social systems.
