Behavioral Science Overview

Behavioral science studies how people think, choose, act, cooperate, conform, compete, and respond to social environments. This discipline draws from psychology, sociology, economics, anthropology, organizational studies, and cognitive science to understand behavior at individual, group, and institutional levels. It includes decision-making, group behavior, behavioral modeling, social influence, and organizational behavior, each of which examines a different part of how behavior is shaped by perception, incentives, norms, identity, emotion, structure, and context. By studying behavioral science, learners gain a framework for understanding why people behave as they do, how social settings shape choices, and how behavioral patterns can be analyzed, predicted, influenced, or improved in real-world systems.

Categories

  • Behavioral Modeling

    Study methods used to represent, predict, and analyze behavior through models, simulations, assumptions, variables, incentives, patterns, and evidence-based behavioral frameworks.

  • Decision-Making

    Explore how individuals and groups make choices under certainty, risk, uncertainty, pressure, emotion, habit, bias, social influence, and limited information.

  • Group Behavior

    Examine how people act in groups, including cooperation, conflict, conformity, leadership, roles, norms, identity, coordination, collective action, and group decision-making.

  • Organizational Behavior

    Learn how behavior operates within organizations, including motivation, leadership, culture, teamwork, communication, conflict, performance, change, and workplace decision-making.

  • Social Influence

    Study how attitudes and actions are shaped by persuasion, norms, authority, peer pressure, reputation, conformity, obedience, identity, and social context.
