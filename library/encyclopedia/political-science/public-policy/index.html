Public Policy Overview

Public policy studies how governments define problems, compare possible solutions, make collective decisions, and use law, funding, regulation, administration, and public programs to shape social outcomes. This discipline examines the full policy cycle, from agenda setting and policy analysis to implementation, evaluation, reform, and political conflict over priorities. It includes domestic policy, economic policy, policy analysis, regulatory policy, and social policy, each of which focuses on different public needs and governing tools. By studying public policy, learners gain a framework for understanding how political values, evidence, institutional constraints, budgets, interest groups, and public needs interact when governments attempt to solve problems and improve civic life.
