Political Science Overview

Political Science is the study of power, governance, political institutions, public decision-making, and the systems through which societies organize authority and collective action. It examines political thought, state structures, policy formation, electoral behavior, administrative systems, international relations, and geopolitical strategy. Political science integrates theoretical, comparative, historical, institutional, and strategic analysis to understand how governments function, how power is exercised, and how political systems shape domestic and global affairs.

Categories

  • Political Theory

    Study foundational ideas about politics including political philosophy, ideologies, justice, rights, and major traditions of political thought.

  • Comparative Politics

    Examine political systems across countries including democratization, authoritarianism, electoral systems, and regime comparison.

  • American Government

    Analyze the structure and operation of the United States government including constitutional design, branches of government, and federalism.

  • Public Administration

    Study the management and operation of government institutions including bureaucracy, administrative theory, public management, and regulatory administration.

  • Public Policy

    Explore policy design and implementation including domestic, economic, social, and regulatory policy analysis.

  • International Relations

    Examine global political systems including diplomacy, foreign policy, international organizations, and global governance.

  • Geopolitics

    Study the influence of geography on political power including strategic regions, great-power competition, and resource politics.

  • Political Economy

    Analyze the interaction of politics and economics including state-market relations, fiscal governance, trade policy, and development strategy.

  • Electoral and Party Politics

    Study elections, political parties, campaign strategy, voting behavior, and political messaging.

  • Security and Statecraft

    Examine national security, strategic policy, intelligence, conflict management, and crisis governance.
