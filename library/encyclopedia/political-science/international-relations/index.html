International Relations Overview

International relations studies the political interactions among states, international organizations, diplomatic actors, regional alliances, global institutions, and non-state forces that shape world politics. This discipline examines how governments pursue foreign policy, negotiate agreements, manage conflict, cooperate through institutions, respond to global problems, and operate within broader international systems. It includes international systems, diplomacy, international organizations, foreign policy, and global governance, each of which explains a different layer of political life beyond national borders. By studying international relations, learners gain a framework for understanding power, cooperation, conflict, sovereignty, interdependence, diplomacy, institutional coordination, and the political challenges that arise in an interconnected world.

Categories

  • Diplomacy

    Study the formal and informal practices states use to communicate, negotiate, manage disputes, build relationships, represent interests, and coordinate action across borders.

  • Foreign Policy

    Examine how governments define national interests, set external priorities, use diplomatic and military tools, manage alliances, respond to crises, and engage the international system.

  • Global Governance

    Explore how international rules, institutions, agreements, networks, and cooperative arrangements address problems that cross borders, including security, trade, health, climate, and human rights.

  • International Organizations

    Learn how intergovernmental and transnational organizations structure cooperation, set norms, coordinate policy, manage disputes, deliver programs, and influence global political outcomes.

  • International Systems

    Study the overall structure of world politics, including polarity, sovereignty, alliances, balance of power, interdependence, institutions, norms, and patterns of conflict and cooperation.
