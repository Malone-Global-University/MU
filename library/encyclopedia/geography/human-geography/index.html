Human Geography Overview

Human geography studies the spatial organization of human activity, culture, population, economy, and political systems across geographic space. The field examines how people shape and are shaped by places, environments, and spatial relationships. It connects human systems with geographic distribution and regional variation.

Disciplines

  • Cultural Geography

    Study spatial distribution and geographic expression of cultural systems and practices.

  • Demographic Geography

    Examine population distribution, density, movement, and demographic patterns.

  • Economic Geography

    Study spatial organization of production, trade, industry, and economic activity.

  • Political Geography

    Explore geographic dimensions of political systems, boundaries, and governance.

  • Rural Geography

    Study geographic patterns and systems of non-urban human settlement.

  • Urban Geography

    Examine spatial structure and development of cities and urban systems.
