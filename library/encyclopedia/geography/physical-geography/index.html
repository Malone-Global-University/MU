Physical Geography Overview

Physical geography studies the natural processes, systems, and features of Earth’s surface and environment. The field examines climate, landforms, water systems, ecosystems, ice, and coastal dynamics. It focuses on the physical mechanisms shaping the planet and the spatial distribution of natural environments.

Disciplines

  • Biogeography

    Study geographic distribution of ecosystems, species, and biological communities.

  • Climatology

    Examine long-term atmospheric patterns and climate systems.

  • Coastal Geography

    Study physical processes and landforms of coastal environments.

  • Geomorphology

    Study formation and evolution of landforms and terrain features.

  • Glaciology

    Examine glaciers, ice sheets, and cryospheric processes.

  • Hydrology

    Study movement, distribution, and properties of water on Earth.
