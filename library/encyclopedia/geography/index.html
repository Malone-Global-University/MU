Geography Overview

Geography is the study of Earth’s spaces, places, environments, and spatial relationships. It examines both the physical characteristics of the planet and the ways human societies organize, perceive, and interact with geographic space. Geography integrates natural science, social science, spatial analysis, and mapping systems to understand landscapes, populations, regions, environmental processes, territorial dynamics, and the strategic significance of place.

Categories

  • Cartography

    Study the design and production of maps including thematic, topographic, navigational, and digital cartographic systems.

  • Geographic Information Systems

    Explore geospatial data systems including GIS analysis, remote sensing, satellite imagery, and spatial modeling.

  • Human Geography

    Examine the spatial dimensions of human society including culture, demographics, economics, politics, migration, and settlement patterns.

  • Physical Geography

    Study Earth’s natural systems including climate, landforms, water systems, environmental processes, and biogeographic patterns.

  • Regional Geography

    Analyze the physical and human characteristics of world regions, continents, and major geographic zones.

  • Geopolitics

    Explore the relationship between geography and power including borders, strategic regions, territorial disputes, and global resource competition.

  • Navigation and Spatial Systems

    Study coordinate systems, geodesy, surveying, navigation methods, and positioning technologies used to measure and traverse geographic space.
