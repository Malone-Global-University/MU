Cartography Overview

Cartography studies the design, construction, interpretation, and use of maps for representing spatial information. The field examines visual communication of geographic data through physical, digital, thematic, and navigational mapping systems. It combines geographic knowledge with visual design and spatial abstraction to communicate place and space effectively.

Disciplines

  • Digital Cartography

    Study computer-based creation, editing, and distribution of digital maps.

  • Map Design

    Examine visual principles and layout methods for effective cartographic communication.

  • Navigation Maps

    Study specialized maps designed for route planning and navigational use.

  • Thematic Mapping

    Explore maps representing specific data themes or geographic variables.

  • Topographic Mapping

    Study mapping of terrain elevation, relief, and physical land features.
