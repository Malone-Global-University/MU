Geopolitics Overview

Geopolitics studies how geography influences political power, strategic competition, territorial control, and international relations. The field examines strategic regions, resources, borders, maritime positioning, and spatial power distribution in global affairs. It connects geographic realities with political and strategic behavior.

Disciplines

  • Borders and Territory

    Study territorial boundaries, disputes, sovereignty, and border systems.

  • Global Power Distribution

    Examine geographic distribution of strategic influence and international power.

  • Maritime Geopolitics

    Study strategic significance of oceans, sea lanes, and maritime territories.

  • Resource Geopolitics

    Explore strategic competition over geographically distributed natural resources.

  • Strategic Regions

    Study geographically significant areas of strategic political or military importance.
