Tai Sabaki 体捌き

Body movement, angling, and positional control in relation to attack

Home Department Defense Martial Arts M.A.L. Tai Sabaki

Definition

Tai Sabaki refers to the movement of the body to avoid, redirect, or reposition in response to an attack. It is not simply evasion, but the intelligent management of body position in relation to incoming force and spatial structure.

Concept

Tai Sabaki is the physical expression of spatial intelligence. It determines whether a practitioner meets force directly, avoids it entirely, or transforms its direction through positioning. Effective Tai Sabaki prevents conflict from becoming static resistance.

Functional Role

  • Repositions the body outside of attack lines
  • Creates angles for control or counter-attack
  • Maintains structural alignment during movement
  • Enables entry into advantageous Ma-ai

Related Concepts

  • Ma-ai (Distance & Engagement Range)
  • Sen (Initiative & Timing)
  • Structure & Alignment
  • Hara (Center)

Application Across Arts

In aikido, Tai Sabaki is used to redirect incoming force without collision. In karate, it creates angles for striking while avoiding counterattack. In jujutsu, it enables entry into grappling and off-balancing positions. Across all systems, it is the foundation of intelligent movement under pressure.

Common Misunderstanding

Tai Sabaki is often mistaken for simple stepping or avoidance. In reality, it is structured positional control. Poor Tai Sabaki leads to overextension, loss of structure, and failure to integrate movement with timing and distance.
