Propaganda and Disinformation Overview

Propaganda and disinformation study intentional efforts to shape perception, influence belief, control narratives, and manipulate public understanding through strategic communication. The field examines both overt persuasion and covert informational distortion across political, institutional, and media contexts. It analyzes how messaging systems can be used to direct thought, behavior, and public interpretation.

Disciplines

  • Disinformation Campaigns

    Study organized efforts to spread false or misleading information strategically.

  • Information Warfare

    Examine strategic use of information as a tool of conflict and influence.

  • Media Manipulation

    Study influence over media narratives, framing, coverage, and informational agendas.

  • Narrative Control

    Explore strategic shaping and maintenance of dominant interpretive frameworks.

  • Propaganda Techniques

    Study rhetorical and psychological methods used in persuasive mass messaging.
