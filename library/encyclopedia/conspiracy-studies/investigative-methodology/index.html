Investigative Methodology Overview

Investigative methodology studies systematic approaches for examining disputed claims, evaluating evidence, analyzing sources, and verifying allegations. It emphasizes structured reasoning, evidentiary standards, research discipline, and critical analysis. Across source analysis, evidence evaluation, OSINT, archival research, and verification methods, the field provides practical frameworks for rigorous investigation.

Disciplines

  • Archival Research

    Study use of historical records, documents, and archives in investigative inquiry.

  • Claim Verification

    Examine structured methods for validating or disproving contested assertions.

  • Evidence Evaluation

    Study methods for weighing credibility, relevance, and reliability of evidence.

  • Open Source Intelligence

    Learn intelligence gathering from publicly available information sources.

  • Source Analysis

    Study credibility, bias, provenance, and reliability assessment of information sources.
