Civil Law Overview

Civil law studies the legal rules that govern disputes between individuals, organizations, families, property holders, consumers, businesses, and other private parties. Unlike criminal law, which concerns offenses prosecuted by the state, civil law focuses on rights, duties, liability, compensation, enforcement of agreements, ownership, family obligations, and procedures for resolving private claims. This discipline includes core areas such as tort law, contract law, property law, family law, and civil procedure, each of which explains a different way legal systems organize ordinary social and economic relationships. By studying civil law, learners gain a practical understanding of how courts resolve conflicts, assign responsibility, protect private interests, enforce obligations, and provide remedies when legal duties are violated.

Categories

  • Civil Procedure

    Study the rules governing civil lawsuits, including pleadings, jurisdiction, discovery, motions, trials, judgments, appeals, remedies, and the process for resolving non-criminal disputes.

  • Contract Law

    Examine how legally enforceable agreements are formed, interpreted, performed, breached, and remedied through rules concerning offer, acceptance, consideration, defenses, damages, and obligations.

  • Family Law

    Explore the legal rules governing marriage, divorce, custody, child support, adoption, domestic relationships, parental rights, family obligations, and court involvement in family matters.

  • Property Law

    Learn how legal systems define, transfer, protect, regulate, and limit interests in land, personal property, ownership, possession, estates, leases, titles, and property rights.

  • Tort Law

    Study civil wrongs and liability for harm, including negligence, intentional torts, strict liability, causation, damages, defenses, personal injury, and compensation for legal injuries.
