Humanities Overview

Humanities is the study of human culture, thought, expression, values, and meaning through interpretive and critical analysis of philosophy, literature, language, religion, ethics, and cultural systems. Rather than focusing primarily on empirical measurement, the humanities investigate how people understand existence, create symbolic systems, construct moral frameworks, and express identity through ideas, texts, traditions, and artistic or intellectual production. The field provides foundational tools for interpreting human experience across civilizations and historical periods.

Categories

  • Humanities Foundations

    Study the theoretical and methodological foundations of humanities inquiry including interpretation, criticism, and comparative analysis.

  • Philosophy

    Explore fundamental questions of reality, knowledge, ethics, logic, aesthetics, mind, politics, and scientific reasoning.

  • Literature and Literary Theory

    Analyze written texts through literary criticism, comparative literature, narrative studies, genre theory, and interpretive frameworks.

  • Classical Studies

    Examine the civilizations, literature, philosophy, and cultural legacy of the ancient Mediterranean world.

  • Cultural Studies

    Study culture, identity, symbolism, traditions, and the social construction of meaning across communities and societies.

  • Religious Studies

    Explore religions, mythologies, sacred texts, ritual systems, and the role of belief in culture and civilization.

  • Ethics and Moral Thought

    Examine systems of moral reasoning including normative ethics, applied ethics, virtue ethics, and ethical frameworks.

  • Linguistics

    Study the structure and function of language including syntax, semantics, phonetics, phonology, morphology, and pragmatics.

  • Rhetoric and Composition

    Analyze persuasive communication, argumentation, writing theory, discourse structures, and composition methods.
