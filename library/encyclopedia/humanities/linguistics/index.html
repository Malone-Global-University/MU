Linguistics Overview

Linguistics studies the structure, function, and systematic properties of language. The field examines sound systems, grammar, meaning, word formation, and contextual language use to understand how human language is organized and operates.

Disciplines

  • Morphology

    Study structure and formation of words from linguistic units.

  • Phonetics

    Examine physical production and acoustic properties of speech sounds.

  • Phonology

    Study abstract sound systems and sound patterns in language.

  • Pragmatics

    Explore how context influences language meaning and interpretation.

  • Semantics

    Study meaning in language and semantic relationships.

  • Syntax

    Study rules governing sentence structure and grammatical arrangement.
