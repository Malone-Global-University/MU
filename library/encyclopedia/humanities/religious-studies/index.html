Religious Studies Overview

Religious studies examine belief systems, mythologies, rituals, sacred texts, and the cultural role of religion across societies. The field analyzes religion as a historical, social, philosophical, and cultural phenomenon through comparative and interpretive methods.

Disciplines

  • Comparative Religion

    Study religions comparatively across traditions, doctrines, and practices.

  • Mythology

    Examine mythic narratives and symbolic religious storytelling traditions.

  • Religion and Culture

    Study interaction between religious systems and broader cultural life.

  • Ritual and Practice

    Explore ceremonial, devotional, and liturgical religious behaviors.

  • Sacred Texts

    Study canonical religious writings and their interpretation.
