Rhetoric and Composition Overview

Rhetoric and composition study persuasive communication, argumentative structure, writing systems, discourse, and the theory of effective written expression. The field examines how ideas are organized, communicated, and made persuasive through language and composition.

Disciplines

  • Argumentation

    Study structure and evaluation of reasoned persuasive arguments.

  • Composition Theory

    Examine theoretical frameworks governing writing and composition practice.

  • Discourse Analysis

    Study structure and function of language use in discourse contexts.

  • Persuasion

    Explore rhetorical methods of influencing belief and behavior.

  • Writing Structures

    Study organizational frameworks and formal structures in written composition.
