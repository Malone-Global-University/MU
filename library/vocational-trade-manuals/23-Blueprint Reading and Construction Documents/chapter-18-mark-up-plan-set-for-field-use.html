Chapter Overview

This chapter qualifies you to take one current approved residential plan set, together with its specifications, approved RFIs, approved submittals, and the Chapter 17 conflict register, and convert it into one controlled field-markup set. The finished work product is a legible, current, and controlled markup set that highlights critical dimensions, trade interfaces, sequencing dependencies, inspection hold points, approved changes, verified field conditions, and unresolved questions, without obscuring or altering the underlying source information.

You will establish a markup legend and version-control platform, mark controlling dimensions and field-verification requirements, mark cross-trade coordination and sequencing dependencies, mark inspection hold points and required approvals, cloud and tag approved revisions while keeping unresolved items visibly open, add cross-sheet references and field observations, and finish with a review, publication, and distribution process that removes superseded information from the active field set.

This chapter depends directly on Chapter 17. Every conflict, RFI, and approved response in the Chapter 17 conflict register must be reflected in this markup set before it is issued. This chapter also feeds Chapter 19: the field-markup set produced here is one of the governing sources used to build the build-ready trade-scope checklist.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BP-103 — Highlight critical dimensions, controlling references, and field-verification requirements on a current plan set without altering the source information.
  • BP-104 — Mark trade responsibilities and cross-trade interfaces only where the approved scope and documents establish them.
  • BP-105 — Note sequencing dependencies that govern the order of field work across trades.
  • BP-106 — Flag inspection and approval hold points at the correct location in the marked set.
  • BP-107 — Add field reminders, verified conditions, and open questions using a consistent markup legend that separates observation from design.
  • BP-108 — Produce a clean, current, and controlled field-markup plan set ready for distribution and field use.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 1 through Chapter 16 built the discipline-by-discipline interpretation packages this chapter now draws on. Chapter 17 built the cross-trade conflict register and RFI-style question list that this chapter must incorporate. Chapter 19 will use this field-markup set, alongside the Chapter 17 register, as a governing source for the build-ready trade-scope checklist. Chapter 20 repeats this entire markup discipline at full scale across a complete residential capstone package.

The document-control habits built here — preserving source linework, separating field observation from design information, and never converting an open question into a presumed direction — are used without exception for the remainder of this book.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • establishing a markup legend, color and symbol system, and version-control platform
  • marking controlling dimensions, references, and field-verification requirements
  • marking cross-trade coordination points and sequencing dependencies
  • marking inspection, approval, and hold-point locations
  • clouding and tagging approved revisions while keeping unresolved items visibly open
  • adding cross-sheet references, field observations, and a field-checklist index
  • reviewing, publishing, protecting, and distributing the finished field-markup set

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • erase, overwrite, or obscure any source linework, text, dimension, or reference on the underlying documents
  • assign trade responsibility that is not established by the approved scope, contracts, division responsibilities, and coordination process
  • resolve a structural, life-safety, system, accessibility, or waterproofing conflict through an undocumented field markup
  • convert an unanswered RFI or an unresolved conflict into a presumed field direction

Any markup that would require a design decision not present in the approved documents must be logged as an open item and routed through the project's document-control and RFI process instead.

Trade Note

A field-markup set is a controlled communication tool, not a record drawing. It becomes a record document only after it has been reviewed, consolidated, and formally accepted through the project's record-document process. Until then, treat every markup as a working annotation layered on top of, never a replacement for, the current approved documents.

Document-control discipline for this chapter: record markup author, date, purpose, and source revision on every marked sheet, and never distribute a field set that mixes current and superseded information.

Setup Standard

Do not begin marking any sheet (Step 9) until the source documents have been confirmed current (Steps 1–2), the markup platform and version control have been established (Step 3), and the markup legend and color system have been created (Steps 4–5). A markup set begun before the legend is fixed must be redone.

Field Tip

Mark controlling dimensions and "do not scale" flags before marking anything else on a sheet. Every later markup on that sheet — trade interfaces, sequencing, hold points — is easier to place correctly once the controlling references are already visible.

Markup Versus Design

A markup restates, highlights, or flags information that is already present in the approved documents, or records a verified field observation. A markup that supplies a dimension, material, connection, or method not present in the source documents is an unauthorized design decision, not a markup. When a field condition or coordination problem cannot be resolved by restating existing document information, record it as an open item and route it through the RFI process instead of marking a resolution.

Critical Cue — Never Erase or Obscure Source Information

Every markup is layered on top of the current approved documents. Do not erase, overwrite, or place a heavy marking over any original linework, dimension, or note. A field set that has lost legibility of its underlying source information can no longer be trusted, even if the markup itself is correct.

RFI Threshold — Open Items Stay Open

An unresolved RFI or conflict from the Chapter 17 register must be marked clearly as open until an approved response is received and incorporated through document control. Do not cloud an open item as though it were resolved, and do not let a field crew infer a direction from an unanswered question.

Field-Use Cue — Separate Observation From Design

A field observation records what was measured, seen, or verified, tied to an exact location, date, and author. It is not design information and must never be labeled or treated as though it modifies the approved documents. Label every field observation clearly as an observation.

Scale Warning — Verify Scale Before Publishing

If the field set will be printed or plotted at a size different from the original reproduction size, verify the graphic scale before publication under Step 32. A field set with an unverified or invalidated scale must be marked "scale unverified" rather than treated as reliable for field measurement.

E. Preparation — Confirm Source Documents and Markup System

  1. Obtain the current approved plan set, specifications, revision log, approved RFIs, approved submittals, and conflict register. Collect the complete set of governing documents identified in Section C before marking any sheet.
  2. Confirm every source document's issue and revision before markup begins. Cross-check each sheet against the current revision log; do not mark a sheet whose current status has not been confirmed.
  3. Select the authorized paper or digital markup platform and establish version control. Record the platform and version-control method on the Markup Authorization Record.
  4. Create a markup legend for dimensions, field verification, trade interface, sequence, inspection, approved change, question, completed item, and hold point. Define one distinct symbol or color for each category before marking begins.
  5. Assign markup colors, symbols, clouds, tags, and comment formats consistently. Record the finished assignment on the markup legend template and apply it without exception.
  6. Record markup author, date, purpose, source revision, and distribution status. Enter these fields on every marked sheet before adding further annotation.
  7. Preserve original linework, text, dimensions, and references so they remain readable. Verify legibility on a sample sheet using the selected markup colors before marking the full set.
  8. Do not erase, overwrite, or obscure source information. Confirm the markup platform supports layered annotation without altering the underlying source file or sheet.

F. Critical Dimension and Field-Verification Marking

  1. Mark the controlling overall dimensions, grids, baselines, elevations, centerlines, and layout references. Use the legend's dimension symbol on every sheet where a controlling reference governs later work.
  2. Highlight dimensions that must not be scaled or inferred. Apply the "do not scale" flag from the legend at each written dimension identified during Chapters 1 through 16 as controlling.
  3. Mark dimensions and conditions requiring field verification before fabrication or installation. Apply the field-verification symbol at every location carried forward from the discipline chapters or the Chapter 17 register.
  4. Identify property, survey, benchmark, utility, and site-control information needed in the field. Mark these locations on the site and foundation sheets using the field-verification symbol.

G. Coordination, Trade Responsibility, and Sequencing Marks

  1. Mark foundation, framing, opening, equipment, and penetration locations requiring cross-trade coordination. Apply the trade-interface symbol at every location identified in the Chapter 17 conflict register or the discipline checklists.
  2. Identify trade responsibility only where the approved scope and documents establish it. Label a trade interface only when the contract, division responsibilities, or approved coordination process assigns it; leave unassigned interfaces marked as open items.
  3. Label owner-furnished, contractor-furnished, delegated-design, work-by-others, allowance, alternate, and excluded items. Apply the appropriate label directly at each item's location from the governing specification or schedule.
  4. Mark sequencing dependencies such as backing before closure, rough-in before cabinets, flashing before cladding, or inspection before concealment. Use the sequence symbol and a brief cross-reference to the governing note or detail.
  5. Mark pre-pour, framing, rough-in, air-seal, waterproofing, fire-stop, roofing, envelope, equipment, testing, and final inspection hold points. Apply the hold-point symbol at each location where work must stop for inspection, testing, or approval.
  6. Mark approved mockups, samples, shop drawings, submittals, and manufacturer-review requirements. Apply the approved-change or hold-point symbol as appropriate, referencing the submittal number.
  7. Add field reminders that restate document requirements without introducing new design. Write each reminder as a restatement of an existing note, detail, or specification requirement, with its source reference.
  8. Mark access, service, clearance, protection, storage, and temporary-condition concerns. Apply the field-use symbol at each location documented in the discipline chapters.

H. Revision, RFI, and Open-Item Marking

  1. Cloud and tag approved revisions and approved RFI responses incorporated into the set. Use the approved-change symbol and cite the revision number or RFI number and its approval date.
  2. Identify unresolved RFIs and conflicts clearly as open items. Use the question symbol and cite the Chapter 17 conflict register item number at each open location.
  3. Do not convert an unanswered question into a presumed field direction. Confirm no open item has been marked with the approved-change or completed-item symbol.
  4. Mark areas where work must stop or remain exposed pending clarification or inspection. Apply the hold-point symbol together with the related open-item citation at each affected location.

I. Cross-Reference, View, and Field-Observation Marking

  1. Add cross-sheet references to details, sections, schedules, specifications, and submittals used most frequently in the field. Record each reference in the cross-sheet reference index with source and destination sheet numbers.
  2. Create room, elevation, area, or trade-specific views where authorized while retaining source references. Build any additional view only from information already present in the approved documents, and cite its source.
  3. Link photographs, field measurements, and verified conditions to exact locations and dates. Attach each field observation to its marked location with author, date, and method of verification.
  4. Label field observations separately from design information. Apply a distinct observation label from the legend so no observation is mistaken for a design requirement.
  5. Create a field-checklist index linked to the marked sheets. List every discipline checklist from Chapters 3 through 16 that applies to each marked sheet.

J. Review and Publication

  1. Review the markup against the Chapter 17 conflict register and approved responses. Confirm every register item is reflected as either an approved change or a clearly marked open item.
  2. Check that superseded revisions, closed questions, and outdated reminders have been removed or archived. Compare every marked sheet against the current revision log a second time before publication.
  3. Print or publish the field set at legible size and verify graphic scales where scaling is authorized. Confirm reproduction size and check the graphic scale before authorizing any field scaling.
  4. Protect the field set from weather, uncontrolled editing, and unauthorized distribution. Confirm the platform or physical set has an appropriate protective cover, access control, or file permission.
  5. Create a distribution log identifying recipients, issue date, revision, and replacement instructions. Record every recipient of the current field set and the replacement instructions for any prior issue.
  6. Perform a final quality check for legibility, current status, complete references, consistent symbols, and unresolved-item visibility. Use the Acceptance Standards table in Section D as the final check.
  7. Archive the prior field set and issue the new set through document control. Move every superseded field set to the archive record and confirm the new set is the only version distributed as current.

Common Markup Inspection

Inspect the finished field set using the same checks regardless of which discipline or sheet group is involved.

  • Legibility check: confirm every marked sheet remains readable as a source document, with no erased or obscured original information.
  • Currency check: confirm every marked sheet reflects the current approved revision and no superseded sheet remains active.
  • Open-item check: confirm every unresolved RFI or conflict is visibly marked as open and none is presented as resolved.
  • Attribution check: confirm markup author, date, purpose, and source revision are recorded on every marked sheet.
  • Distribution check: confirm the distribution log accounts for every current recipient and every replaced prior issue.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Original Dimensions or Notes Become Unreadable

Probable cause: markup colors or heavy clouding were applied directly over source linework instead of alongside it.
Confirmation: compare the marked sheet against the unmarked source document for any obscured text or line.
Correction: remove or relocate the offending markup and re-mark the location using a lighter symbol or an offset callout that does not cover the source information.
Reinspection: repeat the legibility check on the corrected sheet.

A Superseded Revision Remains in the Field Set

Probable cause: the revision log was not rechecked between initial markup and publication, or a reissued sheet was not compared against the version already marked.
Confirmation: compare every marked sheet's revision date against the current revision log a second time.
Correction: remove the superseded sheet from the active field set, archive it, and re-mark the current sheet using the same markup discipline.
Reinspection: repeat the currency check across the full field set.

An Open RFI Is Being Treated as Resolved in the Field

Probable cause: the open-item symbol was omitted, or an approved-change symbol was mistakenly applied before a response was received.
Confirmation: cross-check every marked item against the current status in the Chapter 17 conflict register.
Correction: replace the incorrect symbol with the open-item symbol and add the hold-point mark at the affected location until an approved response is incorporated.
Reinspection: repeat the open-item check across the full field set.

A Field Observation Is Mistaken for a Design Requirement

Probable cause: the observation was recorded without the observation label, author, or date, so it reads as though it modifies the approved documents.
Confirmation: check every field note against the markup legend to confirm it carries the observation label.
Correction: relabel the note as a field observation with author, date, and verification method, and confirm it does not alter any dimension, material, or method shown in the source documents.
Reinspection: repeat the attribution check on the corrected sheet.

Field Crews Are Using Different Versions of the Field Set

Probable cause: the distribution log was not updated when a revised field set was issued, or a prior issue was not formally replaced.
Confirmation: compare the distribution log against every known recipient and confirm which version each is currently holding.
Correction: issue the current field set to every recipient, collect or mark prior issues as superseded, and update the distribution log with replacement instructions.
Reinspection: repeat the distribution check once all recipients confirm receipt of the current set.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with one controlled, distributed field set, not a collection of loose or conflicting markups.

  • Archive every superseded field set separately from the active field-use set
  • Store the completed markup legend, marked sheets, cross-sheet reference index, field-checklist index, and distribution log together as the field-markup package
  • Protect any printed working set from weather and damage and confirm it is legible and current before storage
  • Route every remaining open item through the project's document-control and RFI process
  • Retain the field-markup package for direct reuse in Chapter 19
