Chapter Overview

This chapter qualifies you to take one residential foundation package — the foundation plan, its structural notes, sections, schedules, details, and any issued geotechnical information — and convert it into a build-readiness checklist that a crew can use from pre-layout through post-pour verification. The finished work product is one foundation layout checklist organized by pre-layout, excavation, forming, reinforcement, embeds, pre-pour inspection, placement, cure, and post-pour verification, with every item tied to a sheet, detail, schedule, or specification reference.

You will confirm the foundation system in use, read overall dimensions before interior dimension chains, locate footings, piers, stem walls, and slabs, match every tag to its schedule and detail, trace elevations from top-of-footing through top-of-slab, identify reinforcement and anchor references without inventing a placement schedule the documents do not show, and locate every penetration, joint, and moisture-control reference the foundation drawings provide.

This chapter depends directly on the navigation package built in Chapter 1 and the site-control sheet built in Chapter 2. You must already know which foundation sheets are current, where their references lead, and how the building footprint relates to the site before this chapter's foundation interpretation can be trusted.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BP-013 — Identify the foundation type used by the project and confirm it against related architectural and structural documents.
  • BP-014 — Read footing dimensions, including width, depth, elevation, and step conditions, from the foundation plan, schedules, and details.
  • BP-015 — Locate piers and stem walls and match their tags to the governing schedules and details.
  • BP-016 — Interpret anchor-bolt notes and match anchor and hold-down tags to structural schedules and details.
  • BP-017 — Identify slab areas, including thickness, elevation, slope, recess, edge, and joint references as documented.
  • BP-018 — Create a foundation layout checklist that carries the interpreted information through excavation, forming, reinforcement, embeds, inspection, placement, and post-pour verification.

Where This Skill Fits

The foundation is the first constructed system in the sequence this book follows, and every later chapter assumes the foundation has already been correctly read. Chapter 4 uses the foundation footprint and elevations to cross-check the floor plan. Chapter 7 uses the foundation's bearing lines and anchor information as the base of the structural load path. Chapter 20 rebuilds this same foundation-reading discipline at full scale as an early step of the capstone.

The document-control habits carried forward from Chapter 1 and the site-control habits carried forward from Chapter 2 — verifying current revisions, never scaling without verification, and following references in both directions — apply without exception to every foundation sheet read in this chapter.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • identifying the foundation system or systems used on the project
  • reading overall and interior foundation dimensions and their controlling reference points
  • locating continuous footings, isolated footings, piers, pilasters, grade beams, stem walls, and slab areas
  • matching footing, pier, and anchor tags to schedules and details
  • tracing top-of-footing, top-of-wall, top-of-slab, finished-floor, and grade elevations
  • identifying reinforcement, anchor, penetration, and moisture-control references exactly as documented
  • producing a foundation layout checklist with sheet, detail, schedule, and specification references attached to every item

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • infer footing dimensions, reinforcement, bearing capacity, or anchor spacing from typical practice when a schedule or detail is missing — this must be recorded as a clarification item instead
  • calculate or add dimensions that are not written, without checking the controlling dimension chain, reference points, tolerances, and field conditions
  • resolve a conflict between the foundation plan and the floor plan, structural framing plan, or site plan by choosing the answer that seems most reasonable
  • treat a typical detail as governing a specific condition without confirming the detail applies to that exact location and condition

Any dimension, tag, elevation, or reinforcement reference that cannot be resolved during this chapter must be recorded on the RFI-style question list rather than assumed.

Trade Note

A foundation plan shows what the design team intends to be built below and at grade. It does not show the sequence a crew must follow to build it safely, nor does it substitute for a geotechnical report, an approved shop drawing, or a manufacturer's connector specification. This chapter converts the foundation plan's documented content into a checklist; it does not add missing engineering.

Field Tip

Reading Overall Dimensions Before Interior Chains

The foundation plan's overall footprint dimension is the controlling reference for every footing, pier, and stem-wall dimension inside it. Read and record the overall footprint dimension first. Only then read the interior dimension chains, and confirm each chain's subtotal against the overall dimension before recording any individual footing or pier location as verified.

Scale Warning — Foundation Dimensions Are Written, Not Scaled

Footing widths, depths, elevations, and step conditions must come from written dimensions, schedules, and approved details, not from scaling the foundation plan. Only use a verified graphic scale to locate a general area for field reference, never to establish a dimension that will control excavation, forming, or reinforcement placement.

Critical Cue — Do Not Infer a Missing Reinforcement or Anchor Value

If a footing, pier, or anchor schedule does not state a reinforcement size, spacing, embedment, or edge distance for a specific condition, do not substitute a value from general practice or from a similar-looking condition elsewhere on the sheet. Record the missing value as a clarification item and route it through the RFI-style process before the affected work proceeds.

Cross-Reference Cue — Match Every Tag to Its Schedule and Detail

A footing, pier, anchor, or hold-down tag on the foundation plan is only complete once it has been traced to its schedule entry and, where called out, its detail. Confirm the detail applies to the exact location and condition being interpreted before recording the checklist item as resolved.

RFI Threshold — Unmatched Tags and Conflicting Elevations

Record any footing or pier tag that does not appear in its schedule, any elevation that conflicts between the foundation plan and a related section or detail, and any anchor or penetration note that cannot be matched to a structural reference. Do not resolve the conflict by selecting the value that seems more likely correct.

E. Preparation — Confirm System, Revision, and Coordination

  1. Confirm the foundation-plan sheet, revision, scale, north or orientation reference, grids, levels, and controlling notes. Verify the sheet's current revision against the Chapter 1 sheet register before reading any dimension.
  2. Identify whether the foundation uses slab-on-grade, crawlspace, basement, stem wall, continuous footing, pier, grade beam, pile, or a combination. Record the identified system or systems on the Foundation Document Identity Record.
  3. Compare the foundation plan with the site plan, floor plan, structural notes, sections, details, and geotechnical information where issued. Confirm the foundation footprint is consistent with the Chapter 2 site-control sheet before proceeding.

F. Dimension Verification

  1. Locate the overall foundation footprint and controlling gridlines, centerlines, wall faces, and offsets. Record the overall footprint dimension on the dimension-verification record before any interior dimension is read.
  2. Read overall dimensions before interior dimension chains. Confirm the overall dimension is complete and unambiguous before proceeding to interior chains.
  3. Identify dimensions to footing centerlines, wall faces, slab edges, beam pockets, piers, columns, and openings. Record each interior dimension chain and its controlling reference point.

G. Footing, Pier, and Stem-Wall Register

  1. Locate continuous footings and record width, depth, elevation, step conditions, and detail references. Enter one row per continuous footing segment in the footing register.
  2. Locate isolated footings, pads, piers, pilasters, thickened slabs, and grade beams. Enter one row per isolated component, tagged to match the foundation plan.
  3. Match footing and pier tags to schedules and details. Confirm every tag entered in the register has a corresponding schedule entry; record unmatched tags as clarification items.
  4. Identify stem walls, foundation walls, frost walls, retaining walls, and interior bearing lines. Record the documented function of each wall type; do not assume function from location alone.
  5. Locate slab-on-grade, structural slab, garage slab, porch slab, crawlspace slab, and depressed or raised slab areas. Record each slab area with its documented boundary and function.
  6. Identify slab thickness, elevation, slope, recess, edge, control-joint, and reinforcement references where documented. Record only the values the documents provide.
  7. Locate steps in footing or wall elevation and trace the related section or detail. Confirm each step condition has a governing detail before recording it as resolved.

H. Elevation Tracing

  1. Identify top-of-footing, top-of-wall, top-of-slab, finished-floor, crawlspace, basement, and exterior-grade elevations. Record each elevation on the elevation-trace record, referenced to the project datum.
  2. Locate reinforcement notes, dowels, laps, hooks, bars, welded-wire reinforcement, fibers, and special reinforcing details without creating a placement schedule not shown. Record only the documented reinforcement notes; do not draft a placement schedule the documents do not provide.

I. Anchors, Reinforcement References, and Penetrations

  1. Identify anchor bolts, straps, hold-downs, embedded plates, post bases, sill anchors, and connector references. Record each anchor or connector tag on the reinforcement and anchor reference record.
  2. Match anchor and hold-down tags to structural schedules and details. Confirm every anchor tag has a corresponding schedule entry; record unmatched tags as clarification items.
  3. Identify required edge distances, spacing, embedment, projection, templates, and inspection notes only where the documents provide them. Record only documented values; leave undocumented fields marked as not provided rather than estimated.
  4. Locate plumbing sleeves, blockouts, trenches, pits, drains, sumps, conduit, grounding, utility entries, and other penetrations. Record each penetration on the penetration and moisture-control record.
  5. Compare penetrations with plumbing, electrical, mechanical, and floor-plan information. Confirm each recorded penetration is consistent with the related discipline's documents; record conflicts as clarification items.
  6. Identify vapor barrier, capillary break, insulation, termite, dampproofing, waterproofing, drainage, and radon-system references. Record each documented moisture- and pest-control reference on the penetration and moisture-control record.
  7. Locate crawlspace access, vents, areaways, basement stairs, window wells, and egress openings. Record each access or opening feature with its documented location and dimension.
  8. Identify construction joints, control joints, keyways, waterstops, and pour-sequence notes. Record each joint type and its documented location.
  9. Identify soil-bearing, excavation, subgrade, compaction, frost, groundwater, and inspection requirements referenced by the documents. Record only the values and requirements the documents or referenced geotechnical report state.
  10. Do not infer footing dimensions, reinforcement, or bearing requirements from typical practice when a schedule or detail is missing. Record every missing value as a clarification item on the RFI-style question list.

J. Cross-Check, Checklist Assembly, and Closeout

  1. Cross-check foundation dimensions against the floor plan and structural framing bearing points. Confirm every bearing point on the structural framing plan has a corresponding footing, pier, or stem wall on the foundation plan.
  2. Identify conflicts involving grids, wall thickness, column locations, slab edges, openings, anchors, utilities, or elevations. Record each conflict with its exact location and source references.
  3. Create a foundation layout checklist organized by pre-layout, excavation, forming, reinforcement, embeds, pre-pour inspection, placement, cure, and post-pour verification. Assemble the checklist from the registers and records built in Sections F through I.
  4. Attach sheet, detail, schedule, and specification references to each checklist item. Confirm no checklist item lacks a source reference before finalizing.
  5. Prepare RFI-style questions for missing dimensions, unmatched tags, conflicting elevations, unclear anchors, or penetrations affecting structure. State the document references, observed conflict or missing information, and required clarification for each question without proposing an unauthorized design as the only answer.
  6. Review the final checklist for current revisions, complete references, field verification, and inspection hold points. Confirm every deliverable component in Section B is complete before closing the chapter.

Common Foundation Inspection

Inspect the finished checklist using the same checks regardless of which foundation system or area is involved.

  • System check: confirm the identified foundation system matches the architectural and structural documents at every area of the footprint.
  • Dimension check: confirm every interior dimension chain coordinates with the overall footprint dimension.
  • Tag-matching check: confirm every footing, pier, and anchor tag has a corresponding schedule entry and, where called out, a matched detail.
  • Elevation check: confirm top-of-footing, top-of-wall, top-of-slab, finished-floor, and grade elevations trace to a single controlling datum without contradiction.
  • Documented-value check: confirm no reinforcement, anchor, embedment, or bearing value was estimated or inferred where the documents did not provide it.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

An Interior Dimension Chain Does Not Match the Overall Footprint

Probable cause: an interior dimension was read from an isolated string without checking its subtotal against the overall footprint dimension.
Confirmation: add the interior dimension chain and compare the sum directly against the recorded overall footprint dimension.
Correction: do not average or split the discrepancy; record the exact mismatch as a clarification item and do not use either value for layout until it is resolved.
Reinspection: repeat the dimension-coordination check on the corrected dimension-verification record.

A Footing or Pier Tag Has No Matching Schedule Entry

Probable cause: the schedule was revised without a corresponding plan update, or the tag was misread from the plan.
Confirmation: recheck the exact tag characters against the plan and search the current schedule revision for a match.
Correction: record the unmatched tag on the RFI-style question list rather than assuming which schedule entry applies.
Reinspection: repeat the tag-matching check once the schedule or plan clarification is received.

Elevations Do Not Trace Consistently From Footing to Slab

Probable cause: elevations were read from more than one datum, or a step condition was not traced to its governing detail.
Confirmation: confirm every elevation is referenced to the same project datum and trace each step condition to its detail.
Correction: record the specific elevation conflict as a clarification item and do not select the elevation that appears more convenient for layout.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation-tracing check on the corrected elevation-trace record.

A Reinforcement or Anchor Value Is Missing for a Documented Condition

Probable cause: the schedule or detail governing that specific condition was not issued, or the condition was assumed to be typical without confirmation.
Confirmation: check every schedule and detail referenced by the condition's tag and confirm none provides the missing value.
Correction: record the missing value as a clarification item; do not substitute a value from a similar condition elsewhere on the sheet.
Reinspection: repeat the documented-value check once the missing schedule or detail is received.

A Penetration Conflicts With Foundation Reinforcement or Bearing

Probable cause: the penetration was located from the plumbing or mechanical plan without comparing it against the foundation plan's footings, walls, and reinforcement.
Confirmation: overlay or compare the penetration location directly against the foundation plan and structural notes at that exact location.
Correction: record the conflict as a clarification item; do not authorize relocating, notching, or resizing a structural component to resolve it.
Reinspection: repeat the penetration cross-check once the approved response is incorporated.
