Chapter Overview

This chapter qualifies you to interpret one residential roof document package — roof plan, exterior elevations, building sections, structural roof framing, roofing details, drainage information, specifications, and approved roofing product information — and convert it into a controlled roof-scope checklist before any roofing work is planned or performed. The finished work product is a document-control record, a roof-scope checklist organized by document control, geometry, deck, underlayment, roofing, flashing, penetrations, ventilation, drainage, accessories, inspection, and warranty, and a penetration register.

You will confirm the current roof documents, read roof geometry and slope directly from the plan rather than assuming a form, trace structural roof framing without redesigning it, follow drainage from ridge to discharge point, register every penetration against the disciplines that created it, trace the complete roof assembly from interior ceiling through structure to exterior covering, and confirm that ventilation strategy and flashing details are documented rather than assumed from generic practice.

This chapter builds directly on the navigation package from Chapter 1, the exterior-elevation checklist from Chapter 5, the assembly interpretation sheet from Chapter 6, and the structural framing-scope checklist from Chapter 7. It supplies the roof-specific inputs required for the envelope-detail work in Chapter 15 and the cross-trade coordination work in Chapter 17.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BP-079 — Identify roof slopes, pitch notes, and drainage direction from the current roof plan.
  • BP-080 — Locate ridges, hips, valleys, eaves, rakes, and other roof-geometry features and coordinate them with elevations and sections.
  • BP-081 — Interpret roof penetrations and match penetration tags to the disciplines and details that govern them.
  • BP-082 — Read flashing details and trace material sequence, laps, and terminations.
  • BP-083 — Locate gutter, downspout, and roof-drainage notes and compare them with the site drainage plan.
  • BP-084 — Create a roof-scope checklist coordinating geometry, structure, drainage, penetrations, membranes, flashing, ventilation, and inspection hold points.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 5 confirmed roof forms and heights against exterior elevations. Chapter 6 traced representative roof assembly layers through building and wall sections. Chapter 7 established structural roof framing, bearing, and connectors without authorizing field changes. This chapter brings those threads together specifically at the roof: geometry, structure, drainage, penetrations, and flashing are read as one coordinated system rather than as isolated sketches.

The roof-scope checklist and penetration register produced here carry forward into Chapter 15, where envelope continuity is traced at roof-wall intersections, and into Chapter 17, where roof-related conflicts are compared against structural, MEP, and site drainage information.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • confirming the current roof plan, elevations, sections, structural roof framing, roofing details, drainage information, specifications, and approved roofing product information
  • reading roof geometry, slope, and drainage direction
  • comparing roof geometry with exterior elevations and building sections
  • identifying overhangs, edge conditions, gutters, downspouts, and discharge references
  • registering roof penetrations and matching them to the disciplines that created them
  • tracing the roof assembly from interior ceiling or attic through structure to exterior covering
  • reading flashing and edge-metal details
  • producing a roof-scope checklist and a penetration register

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • relocate a penetration, cricket, or drainage feature to resolve a framing or coordination conflict
  • infer a ventilation strategy from generic practice rather than the wall and roof sections
  • invent fastening, adhesion, or attachment values not stated by the roofing details, specifications, or manufacturer instructions
  • scale a roof dimension unless the reproduction size and graphic scale have been verified under the Chapter 1 procedure

Any geometry conflict, missing cricket, incomplete flashing detail, or uncoordinated penetration that cannot be resolved during this chapter must be recorded on the roof-scope checklist and carried into an RFI-style question rather than assumed.

Trade Note

A roof plan shows geometry. A roofing detail shows assembly. A specification shows performance and installation requirements. None of the three is complete by itself, and this chapter treats them as three separate sources that must be cross-checked rather than one source read in isolation.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every roof condition in this chapter is read the same way: confirm the current document, read geometry and slope directly from the plan, trace the assembly through sections and details, register penetrations against the disciplines that created them, and confirm drainage from ridge to discharge point before any roof-scope item is marked complete.

Reading Slope Arrows Versus Reading Sections

A slope arrow on the roof plan tells you drainage direction. It does not by itself confirm pitch, deck material, or assembly layers. Confirm pitch and assembly information from the roofing details, sections, and specifications rather than assuming them from the plan symbol alone.

Critical Cue — Do Not Relocate a Penetration to Avoid a Framing Conflict

A penetration shown on the roof plan is coordinated with the discipline that requires it. Relocating a chimney, vent, or skylight in the field to avoid a rafter, truss, or beam is an unauthorized design decision. Record the conflict and route it through the RFI process instead.

Code Alert — Confirm the Ventilation Strategy From the Sections, Not From General Practice

Vented and unvented roof assemblies require different ventilation, insulation, and vapor-control strategies. Confirm the strategy shown in the wall and roof sections before assuming eave, ridge, or soffit ventilation is required or prohibited.

Cross-Reference Cue — Verify Every Detail Applies to the Exact Condition

A detail labeled "typical" may not apply to every ridge, valley, or penetration on the roof. Confirm the location, exposure, material, and condition of the detail before applying it, and do not use a typical detail where a specific or revised detail governs.

RFI Threshold — Missing Crickets and Unresolved Drainage

Record any missing cricket, saddle, unclear low point, or drainage path that cannot be confirmed from the roof plan and sections as a clarification item. Do not add a cricket or drainage feature that is not shown in the documents.

E. Document Control and Geometry Extraction

  1. Confirm current roof plan, elevations, sections, structural roof framing, roofing details, drainage information, specifications, and approved roofing product information. Verify each against the Chapter 1 navigation package before proceeding.
  2. Identify roof areas, levels, orientation, material types, and whether the plan is architectural, structural, drainage, or coordinated. Record this classification in the document-control log.
  3. Locate ridges, hips, valleys, eaves, rakes, gables, sheds, crickets, saddles, parapets, curbs, and changes in plane. Mark each on the roof-scope checklist under Geometry.
  4. Read slope arrows, pitch notes, spot elevations, high points, low points, and drainage direction. Record the slope and direction for every roof area.
  5. Compare roof geometry with exterior elevations and building sections. Confirm ridge and eave heights, roof forms, and edge conditions are consistent across all three document types.

F. Drainage and Edge Conditions

  1. Identify overhangs, edge conditions, fascia, soffit, gutters, scuppers, drains, and overflow provisions. Record each in the roof-scope checklist under Drainage.
  2. Locate downspouts, leaders, conductor heads, splash blocks, underground connections, and discharge references. Trace each downspout from its gutter connection to its discharge point.
  3. Compare roof discharge with the site drainage plan. Confirm every discharge point shown on the roof documents is also addressed on the site plan reviewed in Chapter 2.

G. Penetration Registration

  1. Locate chimneys, plumbing vents, flues, exhausts, intakes, skylights, hatches, solar equipment, antennas, and other penetrations. Add each to the penetration register with its roof-plan location.
  2. Match penetration tags to architectural, plumbing, HVAC, electrical, and specialty documents. Confirm each penetration's governing discipline and cross-reference in the register.
  3. Identify roof curbs, support frames, blocking, clearances, and structural references. Record the structural support condition for each penetration.
  4. Do not relocate a penetration merely to avoid a roof-framing conflict. Log any conflict between a penetration and a framing member on the RFI-style question list instead.

H. Assembly and Ventilation

  1. Identify roofing material, underlayment, ice or water membrane, cover board, insulation, vapor control, deck, and attachment references where documented. Record each layer in the roof-scope checklist under Assembly.
  2. Trace the roof assembly from interior ceiling or attic through structure to exterior covering using sections and details. Confirm the assembly sequence matches the Chapter 6 assembly interpretation sheet for this roof condition.
  3. Identify ventilation at eaves, soffits, ridges, gables, roof decks, or unvented assemblies. Record the ventilation strategy exactly as shown.
  4. Confirm the ventilation strategy matches wall and roof sections and is not inferred from generic practice. Cross-check against the Chapter 6 deliverable before recording the strategy as confirmed.

I. Flashing and Detail Verification

  1. Locate eave, rake, valley, ridge, hip, step-flashing, headwall, sidewall, chimney, skylight, curb, pipe, and edge-metal details. List each detail reference in the roof-scope checklist under Flashing.
  2. Follow each applicable detail and verify material sequence, laps, upturns, counterflashing, sealant, and support references. Confirm the detail matches the exact roof condition it is applied to.
  3. Identify conditions where manufacturer standard details or approved submittals supplement project details. Record the source of each supplemental detail used.
  4. Identify fastening, adhesion, welding, seaming, priming, temperature, substrate, and warranty references without inventing installation values. Record only values stated in the details, specifications, or manufacturer instructions.
  5. Identify roof access, fall-protection, staging, temporary dry-in, weather, and protection requirements shown. Record these under Inspection in the roof-scope checklist.
  6. Identify required mockups, samples, substrate inspections, moisture conditions, adhesion tests, flood tests, or manufacturer inspections. Record each as a hold point in the roof-scope checklist.

J. Coordination Review and Checklist Closeout

  1. Compare roof penetrations and curbs with framing, trusses, ducts, pipes, conduits, and interior equipment. Confirm every registered penetration has a coordinated structural and MEP reference.
  2. Identify conflicts at valleys, crickets, low points, ridge ventilation, gutters, overflows, and adjacent walls. Record each conflict with its exact location and source references.
  3. Identify missing slope, unresolved drainage, incomplete flashing, incompatible material transitions, or unclear termination details. Add each unresolved item to the RFI-style question list.
  4. Create a roof-scope checklist organized by document control, geometry, deck, underlayment, roofing, flashing, penetrations, ventilation, drainage, accessories, inspection, and warranty. Consolidate all findings from Sections E through I into this single checklist.
  5. Attach sheet, section, detail, specification, submittal, and manufacturer references to every critical item. Confirm no checklist row is missing a source reference.
  6. Create a penetration register identifying type, location, support, flashing detail, trade, and field-verification need. Finalize the register built in Section G.
  7. Prepare RFI-style questions for geometry conflicts, missing crickets, drainage ambiguity, flashing gaps, or uncoordinated penetrations. State the document references, observed issue, field impact, and required-by date for each question.
  8. Review the checklist against current architectural, structural, MEP, site, and exterior-envelope information. Confirm consistency with the Chapter 1, 2, 5, 6, and 7 deliverables before closing the chapter.

Common Roof-Scope Inspection

Inspect the finished checklist using the same checks regardless of which roof area or condition is involved.

  • Currency check: confirm every roof plan, elevation, section, structural, detail, and specification reference used is the current approved revision.
  • Geometry check: confirm every ridge, hip, valley, eave, rake, and change in plane is identified and cross-checked against elevations and sections.
  • Drainage check: confirm every roof area's slope direction is traced to a discharge point that is also addressed on the site plan.
  • Penetration check: confirm every penetration is registered with its governing discipline, support, and flashing detail, or logged as unresolved.
  • Detail check: confirm every flashing detail used is verified to apply to the exact condition it is assigned to.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Roof Geometry Does Not Match the Exterior Elevations

Probable cause: the roof plan and elevations were read from different revisions, or a ridge or eave height was assumed rather than confirmed.
Confirmation: compare the revision dates on the roof plan and elevation sheets and re-trace the ridge and eave heights against the vertical dimension chain established in Chapter 5.
Correction: rebuild the affected geometry entries directly from the current roof plan and elevations, and record any remaining discrepancy on the RFI-style question list.
Reinspection: repeat the geometry check across the full roof-scope checklist.

A Roof Area Has No Traceable Discharge Point

Probable cause: a gutter or downspout shown on the roof plan was not carried through to the site plan, or the roof plan shows a discharge condition the site plan does not address.
Confirmation: re-trace the slope direction for the affected roof area and compare it against the Chapter 2 site-control sheet.
Correction: log the unresolved discharge point on the RFI-style question list rather than assuming a destination.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage check once a response is received.

A Penetration Has No Matching Flashing Detail

Probable cause: the penetration was added or relocated in a later revision without a corresponding detail reference, or a typical detail was assumed to apply without verification.
Confirmation: check the roofing details and specifications for a detail matching the exact penetration type, location, and roofing material.
Correction: record the penetration as unresolved in the penetration register and raise an RFI-style question rather than applying an unverified typical detail.
Reinspection: repeat the penetration check once the correct detail is confirmed and received.

Ventilation Strategy Cannot Be Confirmed

Probable cause: the roof plan does not show ventilation, and the wall and roof sections were not checked for a vented or unvented assembly statement.
Confirmation: re-review the Chapter 6 assembly interpretation sheet and the current roof and wall sections for an explicit ventilation strategy.
Correction: record the ventilation strategy as unresolved rather than assuming a vented or unvented condition, and raise an RFI-style question.
Reinspection: repeat the assembly and ventilation check once the strategy is confirmed.

A Conflict Exists Between a Penetration and a Roof-Framing Member

Probable cause: the architectural roof plan and the structural roof framing plan were not compared at the exact penetration location.
Confirmation: overlay or directly compare the penetration location on the roof plan with the framing member locations on the structural roof framing plan.
Correction: record the conflict with its exact location and source references and raise an RFI-style question; do not relocate the penetration or modify the framing member.
Reinspection: repeat the penetration and coordination check once an approved response is incorporated.

Field Terms

Slope Arrow
The plan symbol indicating the direction water drains across a roof plane.
Cricket / Saddle
A small secondary roof structure built to divert water around a penetration or wall.
Valley
The internal angle formed where two roof planes meet and drain toward each other.
Hip
The external angle formed where two roof planes meet and slope away from each other.
Penetration
Any element passing through the roof plane, such as a vent, flue, skylight, or chimney.
Roof Curb
A raised frame built to support and flash equipment or a skylight above the roof plane.
Underlayment
The water-resistive layer installed beneath the exterior roof covering.
Counterflashing
Flashing installed to overlap and protect the top edge of a base flashing at a wall or penetration.
Discharge Point
The location where roof drainage is directed after leaving the gutter or downspout system.
Roof-Scope Checklist
The document-control table coordinating roof geometry, assembly, drainage, penetrations, flashing, ventilation, and inspection requirements.
