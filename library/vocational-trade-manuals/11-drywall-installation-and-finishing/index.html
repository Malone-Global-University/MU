11-Drywall Installation and Finishing

This field manual provides practical, project-based instruction for residential drywall installation, finishing, repair, and surface preparation. Students learn to measure, cut, hang, fasten, tape, finish, texture, and repair drywall systems while developing professional finishing skills.

Overview

Drywall Installation and Finishing emphasizes hands-on skill development through complete wall and ceiling finishing projects. Beginning with inspection and workspace preparation, students progress through drywall layout, cutting, installation, fastening, corner treatment, joint finishing, sanding, texturing, repair methods, and a complete practice room capstone project.

Learning Outcomes

  • Inspect framing and prepare surfaces before drywall installation.
  • Safely set up and organize a drywall installation work area.
  • Measure, cut, and install drywall panels accurately.
  • Hang ceiling and wall drywall using proper fastening techniques.
  • Finish inside corners, outside corners, soffits, and edge transitions.
  • Apply drywall tape, joint compound, and multiple finishing coats.
  • Sand, prime, and inspect finished drywall surfaces.
  • Apply basic wall texture and prepare surfaces for painting.
  • Repair common drywall damage including holes and damaged sections.
  • Complete a full drywall installation, finishing, and repair project.
