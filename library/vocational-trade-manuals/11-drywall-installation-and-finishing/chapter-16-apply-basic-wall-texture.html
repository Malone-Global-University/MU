Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to apply one approved basic texture system to a controlled practice wall or room and produce a uniform, matched pattern ready for paint. The finished work product is one textured wall or room surface that matches an approved sample panel, plus the recorded mix, equipment settings, and inspection results that document how that match was achieved.

You will select the approved texture option from the finish schedule; confirm the drywall is primed or otherwise prepared as the texture system requires; mix a small batch and produce a sample panel; adjust equipment settings until the sample matches the approved standard; apply the texture with consistent distance, angle, overlap, and travel speed; blend passes while the material is still workable; knock down peaks where the system calls for it; and inspect the finished pattern under raking light for thin spots, heavy spots, lap lines, runs, contamination, or inconsistent knockdown.

Texture is optional under the approved finish schedule. This chapter assumes the schedule calls for orange peel, knockdown, or another named basic texture; where the schedule calls for a smooth finish instead, the same sample-matching and application-control skills are demonstrated on a practice panel rather than on the finished room. Chapter 15 primer inspection must be complete and approved before this chapter begins, and this chapter's approved surface is the condition Chapter 20 expects when the capstone room reaches its texture stage.

Where This Skill Fits

Texture is the last material applied to the drywall surface before paint, and it is the least forgiving to correct after the fact. A pattern that is too heavy, too light, inconsistent in density, or poorly blended at a boundary will telegraph under every future raking light and under every coat of paint. The sample-panel discipline you practice in this chapter — mixing a small batch, testing settings, adjusting one variable at a time, and only then committing to the wall — is the same discipline that prevents an entire room from being redone.

This chapter depends directly on Chapter 15's primed and approved surface, since texture applied over unprimed or improperly cleaned drywall will absorb unevenly and telegraph joint lines. The equipment-control skills here — spray distance, angle, overlap, and travel speed — also apply directly to Chapter 17 through 19 repair work, where matching existing texture on a patch is one of the required repair competencies.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • selecting the approved texture option from the finish schedule
  • confirming surface preparation, primer condition, and environmental limits before mixing texture material
  • mixing a small test batch and producing a sample panel matched to the approved standard before wall application begins
  • applying orange-peel or knockdown texture using controlled equipment settings
  • maintaining a consistent pattern across field areas and boundary transitions
  • blending texture at repair or boundary edges
  • knocking down peaks for a knockdown finish using a controlled blade angle and set time
  • inspecting the finished texture under normal and raking light and correcting localized defects

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • select or invent a texture pattern, mix ratio, or equipment setting that is not specified by the approved finish schedule or the texture-product manufacturer's instructions
  • apply texture over drywall that has not passed the Chapter 15 primer inspection, or over an unresolved moisture, mold, or contamination condition
  • operate a compressor or spray equipment that has not passed the pre-use inspection required in Step 6 below
  • claim a finished texture matches an approved standard without producing and approving a sample panel first

Any condition outside the learner's qualification, including a texture-product compatibility question or an equipment malfunction, must be referred to the manufacturer's instructions, a qualified equipment technician, or the responsible supervisor.

Trade Note

Texture is optional under the Book 11 finish schedule. A smooth-finish project may omit texture entirely while the learner still completes this chapter's sample-panel and equipment-control competencies on a practice panel, as permitted by the System-Sequence Control rule in the book outline.

Setup Standard

Do not begin field application (Step 11) until the sample panel produced in Steps 7–10 has been compared to and approved against the finish schedule's approved standard. A wall textured before the sample is approved must be scraped, reprimed as needed, and retextured at the learner's expense of time and material.

Shop Tip

Change only one variable — pressure, distance, nozzle size, or pass speed — between sample attempts. Changing two variables at once makes it impossible to know which change produced the match, and that knowledge is exactly what you need to reproduce the pattern consistently across an entire room.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every texture application in this book follows the same sequence regardless of pattern: confirm surface readiness, protect adjacent surfaces, mix a small batch, produce and approve a sample, record the approved settings, apply with controlled distance and speed, blend and knock down as required, and inspect under raking light before removing protection.

Sample Matching Before Field Application

Do not apply texture to the finished wall or ceiling until a sample panel produced under the planned settings has been compared to and approved against the finish schedule's approved standard. Adjust only one variable — pressure, distance, nozzle size, or pass speed — between attempts, and record the settings that produced the approved match before proceeding.

Manufacturer Cue — Mix Ratio, Working Time, and Application Settings

Mix texture material to the product-specified water ratio and working time. Do not exceed the stated water-addition limit. Follow the manufacturer's stated nozzle size, air pressure, and spray distance as the starting point for the sample panel, and record any deviation required to match the approved standard.

Critical Safety Cue — Ventilation and Combustion Appliances

Confirm that ventilation and overspray containment will not backdraft a combustion appliance in or near the work area. Do not operate spray equipment in a space with an unventilated fuel-burning appliance running.

Field Safety Cue — Overspray and Eye Protection

Wear eye protection during spray application and during knockdown, since rebounding material and blade-flung compound can strike the face at close working distance. Keep the spray path clear of other workers.

Safety Cue — Respiratory Protection During Mixing and Spraying

Wear the respiratory protection specified by the texture product's data sheet during mixing and spray application. Mixing dry powder texture materials produces airborne dust independent of the drywall dust addressed in Chapter 14.

G. Preparation and Protection Procedure (Steps 1–6)

  1. Select the approved texture option: orange peel, knockdown, or another specified basic texture. Confirm the selection against the approved finish schedule for the room or wall system.
  2. Review surface preparation, primer requirements, material, water ratio, equipment, nozzle, pressure, working time, and sample standard. Record each value on the texture application worksheet before proceeding.
  3. Confirm that the drywall is primed or prepared as required by the texture system. Verify the Chapter 15 primer inspection is approved for the surface with no unresolved defects.
  4. Protect all floors, ceilings, trim, fixtures, doors, windows, vents, and adjacent untextured surfaces. Seal sheeting edges to prevent overspray migration into adjoining rooms.
  5. Establish ventilation and overspray containment. Confirm ventilation will not backdraft a combustion appliance in or near the work area.
  6. Inspect the hopper gun, compressor, spray equipment, mixing tools, and electrical supply. Confirm hoses, fittings, and cords are free of damage before connecting power or air.

H. Sample Matching Procedure (Steps 7–10)

  1. Mix a small batch according to the product instructions. Do not exceed the product-stated water-addition limit.
  2. Produce a sample panel using the planned nozzle, pressure, distance, and pass speed. Apply the sample on the sample board or an inconspicuous primed area.
  3. Adjust only one variable at a time until the sample matches the approved standard. Use the Equipment Setting Schedule in Section D to guide each adjustment.
  4. Record the selected settings and mix. Enter the approved nozzle size, pressure, distance, pass speed, and mix ratio on the texture application worksheet before field application begins.

I. Field Application Procedure (Steps 11–18)

  1. Apply texture beginning at a controlled edge or corner. Start at the location identified in the finish schedule or, where none is specified, at the least visible corner of the room.
  2. Maintain consistent spray distance, angle, overlap, and travel speed. Use the approved settings recorded in Step 10 without deviation.
  3. Blend passes while the material remains workable. Overlap each pass into the previous pass before the material begins to set.
  4. For knockdown, allow the specified set time. Do not begin knockdown before the material has stiffened to the product-specified condition.
  5. Knock down peaks using a clean blade at the controlled angle and pressure. Wipe the blade clean between passes to avoid dragging material.
  6. Maintain consistent pattern across field areas and transitions. Compare the developing pattern to the approved sample at intervals during application.
  7. Feather texture at repair or boundary edges. Reduce density gradually into any adjoining existing texture to avoid a visible seam.
  8. Stop and clean equipment before material hardens. Do not allow texture material to set inside the hopper gun, hoses, or mixing tools.

J. Inspection and Documentation Procedure (Steps 19–22)

  1. Inspect under normal and raking light for thin spots, heavy spots, lap lines, runs, contamination, or inconsistent knockdown. Walk the full surface with a raking light source held close to the wall.
  2. Correct localized defects using the approved scrape, recoat, or blend method. Match the correction to the surrounding pattern before it dries.
  3. Allow the texture to dry before removing protection or painting. Confirm the product-stated drying time has elapsed.
  4. Document product, mix, equipment settings, sample approval, and final inspection. File the completed texture application worksheet with the room's project records.

Common Texture Inspection

Inspect every textured surface using the same checks regardless of pattern or room size.

  • Preparation check: confirm the surface passed Chapter 15 primer inspection and is dry, clean, and free of contamination.
  • Sample check: confirm a sample panel was produced and approved against the finish schedule's standard before field application began.
  • Pattern check: confirm density and pattern size are consistent across field areas and transitions under raking light.
  • Boundary check: confirm no visible seam remains at any boundary between new and existing texture.
  • Defect check: confirm no thin spots, heavy spots, lap lines, runs, contamination, or inconsistent knockdown remain uncorrected.
  • Drying check: confirm the texture is fully dry before protection is removed or paint is applied.

Complete the Texture Application Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before removing protection or scheduling paint.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Field Pattern Does Not Match the Approved Sample

Probable cause: a change in air pressure, spray distance, or material consistency between the sample and field application, or a different operator applying the field coat.
Confirmation: compare the field pattern to the sample panel under the same raking-light condition and check the recorded settings against the equipment's current settings.
Correction: stop application, restore the recorded settings, and produce a new sample to confirm the match before continuing.
Reinspection: reinspect the corrected field area under raking light against the approved sample.

Thin or Heavy Spots Appear Across the Field

Probable cause: inconsistent travel speed, uneven overlap between passes, or a partially clogged nozzle.
Confirmation: inspect under raking light and identify whether the defect follows a pass line or is randomly distributed, and inspect the nozzle for buildup.
Correction: clean or replace the nozzle, and reapply texture to the affected area using the approved settings and full overlap.
Reinspection: reinspect the corrected area under raking light after drying.

A Visible Seam Appears at a Boundary Edge

Probable cause: the new texture pass stopped at a hard edge instead of feathering gradually into the existing texture.
Confirmation: inspect the boundary under raking light from both sides of the transition.
Correction: lightly retexture the boundary with a reduced-density pass, feathering the density gradually across the transition.
Reinspection: reinspect the boundary under raking light after drying, from both sides of the transition.

Knockdown Pattern Is Smeared Instead of Flattened

Probable cause: the material was knocked down before reaching the product-specified set time, or the blade carried excess material between passes.
Confirmation: check the time elapsed since spraying against the product data sheet and inspect the blade for buildup.
Correction: allow remaining areas to reach the full set time before knockdown, and wipe the blade clean between passes; scrape and reapply any smeared area.
Reinspection: reinspect the corrected area under raking light after drying.

Contamination Appears in the Finished Texture

Probable cause: debris, dried material chips, or dust entered the hopper, mix, or spray path during application.
Confirmation: inspect the contaminated area closely and check the mixed material and equipment for the source.
Correction: scrape out the contaminated area, clean the equipment and mix, and reapply texture matching the approved settings.
Reinspection: reinspect the corrected area under raking light after drying, and confirm no further contamination is introduced.

Cleanup

Clean all equipment and the work area only after the texture has fully dried and the final inspection is complete.

  • Disassemble and clean the hopper gun, hoses, and mixing tools before texture material hardens inside them
  • Remove protection sheeting and tape only after the texture is fully dry per the product-stated drying time
  • Sweep and vacuum any dried overspray debris from floors and adjacent surfaces
  • Store remaining texture material, sample boards, and equipment per manufacturer instructions
  • File the completed texture application worksheet with the room's project records alongside the Chapter 15 primer inspection record

Field Terms

Orange Peel
A fine, stippled spray texture applied and left as-sprayed, without a knockdown step.
Knockdown
A spray texture that is partially flattened with a blade after a controlled set time, producing a mottled, flattened-peak pattern.
Sample Panel
A small test application of texture, produced under the planned settings and compared to the approved standard before field application begins.
Approved Standard
The physical sample, photograph, or manufacturer reference identified by the finish schedule that the finished texture must match.
Set Time
The interval between spraying knockdown texture and flattening it, during which the material stiffens enough to be knocked down without smearing.
Boundary Edge
The line where new texture meets an existing textured surface, an untextured surface, or a repair area, requiring feathering to avoid a visible seam.
Hopper Gun
A pneumatic tool that holds mixed texture material and sprays it through an adjustable nozzle at a regulated air pressure.
Overspray Containment
Sheeting, taping, and ventilation measures used to prevent sprayed texture material from reaching unprotected surfaces or adjoining spaces.
Working Time
The period during which mixed texture material remains sprayable or blendable before it begins to set.
Feathering
Gradually reducing applied material density at an edge so the transition is not visible after drying.

Performance Check

With application complete and the texture fully dry, review the surface against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm that the surface was primed and approved before texture began, the sample panel was produced and approved before field application, pattern density and size are consistent across all field areas and boundary transitions under raking light, no visible seam remains at any boundary edge, and no thin spots, heavy spots, lap lines, runs, contamination, or inconsistent knockdown remain uncorrected. Approve the finished texture only after every defect identified during inspection has been corrected and reinspected.

Project Closeout

You have completed the texture stage of the drywall finishing sequence. This chapter required you to prepare the surface for texture (DW-091), mix texture material (DW-092), apply orange-peel or knockdown texture (DW-093), maintain a consistent pattern (DW-094), blend edges (DW-095), and inspect texture quality (DW-096).

You now have a textured wall or room surface matched to the approved standard, with the mix, equipment settings, sample approval, and final inspection recorded on the texture application worksheet. That record is the controlling document for any future repair work in this room, since Chapters 17 through 19 depend on matching this exact texture when patching later damage.

File your completed texture application worksheet with the room's project records. Do not remove protection or schedule paint until the final texture decision on the worksheet is marked Approved, or Conditional with every remaining item corrected and reinspected.
