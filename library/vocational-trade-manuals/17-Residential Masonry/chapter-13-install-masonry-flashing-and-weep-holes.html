Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install and inspect one complete masonry drainage mockup: the flashing, end dams, cavity drainage material, and weep system that carry incidental moisture out of a masonry wall before it can reach the structure. The finished work product is one documented base, opening, or shelf-angle drainage mockup with continuous flashing, formed end dams, an open cavity, and functioning weeps, verified against the approved wall section.

This chapter treats flashing and weeps as a single connected system, not as separate accessories. A flashing lap that reverses direction, an end dam that is missing, a cavity bridged with mortar droppings, or a weep spaced incorrectly all defeat the same drainage path. You will mark every flashing and weep location from the approved wall section, install compatible materials in the correct lap sequence, form end dams at every termination, keep the cavity open, and install weeps aligned with the flashing before any veneer or wall work conceals these details.

This is the thirteenth chapter in the book. It depends on the readiness plan from Chapter 1, the controlled work area from Chapter 2, and the wall layout from Chapter 4, and it supplies the flashing and weep details that Chapter 11 and Chapter 12 install veneer around and that the Chapter 20 capstone must reproduce in full.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RM-073 — Identify flashing locations, marking every base, through-wall, head, sill, lintel, and shelf-angle location and every weep on the approved wall section before work begins.
  • RM-074 — Install base flashing, including the required back leg, outward slope, WRB integration, and end dams.
  • RM-075 — Install through-wall flashing above lintels, openings, shelf angles, and interruptions, maintaining laps and end dams.
  • RM-076 — Form weep holes at the specified spacing, aligned with flashing and kept free of mortar and sealant.
  • RM-077 — Maintain the drainage cavity, keeping it free of mortar bridging and mortar-dropping obstruction.
  • RM-078 — Inspect water-management details, verifying laps, adhesion, end dams, slope, outlets, cavity condition, and weeps before concealment.

Where This Skill Fits

Every veneer, cavity wall, and shelf-angle transition in this book depends on the flashing and weep system built here. Chapter 11 installs brick veneer directly on top of the base flashing and weeps this chapter forms, and Chapter 12 relies on the same water-management logic when the veneer type is stone. The photograph-before-concealment discipline you practice here — documenting laps, end dams, and weeps before veneer covers them — is the same discipline the Chapter 20 capstone inspection requires at final acceptance.

The stop-work judgment you use here — refusing to seal a reversed lap, a missing end dam, or a blocked weep with sealant rather than correcting the detail — carries into every later chapter that touches moisture management, including Chapter 15 crack repair and Chapter 19 cleaning and sealing.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved wall section to identify every water-entry and drainage point
  • selecting compatible flashing, tapes, primers, end-dam material, drip edge, and sealants
  • preparing the substrate and integrating flashing with the WRB in shingle-lap sequence
  • installing base flashing with the required back leg and outward slope
  • forming end dams at every flashing termination
  • installing through-wall flashing above lintels, openings, shelf angles, and interruptions
  • installing cavity drainage material or mortar-dropping control and keeping the cavity open
  • installing weeps at the specified spacing, aligned with flashing
  • installing termination or counterflashing where specified
  • performing a limited drainage observation or mockup test where approved
  • inspecting and photographing every concealed detail before veneer or wall work proceeds

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • invent flashing dimensions, lap lengths, weep spacing, cavity widths, or end-dam geometry not supplied by the approved wall section, adopted code, or manufacturer instructions
  • install veneer, brick, block, or stone units over an unapproved or unverified flashing and weep system; unit installation belongs to Chapter 11 and Chapter 12
  • seal pinholes, reverse laps, blocked outlets, or incompatible materials with sealant as a substitute for correcting the detail
  • proceed past a flashing or weep deficiency without correction and reinspection before concealment

Any flashing, end-dam, cavity, or weep condition that fails the inspection in this chapter must be corrected and reinspected before veneer or wall work conceals it.

Trade Note

Every flashing lap, end dam, and weep is photographed at the point it is installed — never reconstructed from memory after veneer has been laid over it. A concealed detail that cannot be verified by photograph is the single most common cause of the drainage failures diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not install veneer, brick, block, or stone over any flashing, end-dam, cavity, or weep detail until the inspection in Step 20 has passed and every pinhole, reverse lap, blocked outlet, or incompatible material has been corrected. A detail that fails this inspection must not be concealed.

Shop Tip

Photograph each flashing lap and weep immediately after installation, with a tape measure in frame showing the lap dimension or weep spacing. A photograph taken after the next course of masonry is laid can no longer verify the detail underneath it.

Reference Conventions

Establish these references before any flashing or weep is installed, and use them consistently across the mockup.

TermDefinition
Back legThe vertical portion of base flashing that turns up behind the WRB or backup wall to prevent water from tracking behind the flashing
End damA formed or applied upturn at the end of a flashing run that prevents water from flowing off the end of the flashing into the wall
Shingle lapThe overlap sequence in which each higher flashing or WRB course overlaps the course below it, so water always sheds outward and downward
Drainage cavityThe open air space behind veneer or cladding that carries incidental moisture down to the flashing and out through the weeps
WeepAn opening in the mortar joint or flashing system, at the specified spacing, that allows drainage water to exit the cavity above the flashing

Flashing as a Single Connected System

Treat every flashing run in this chapter as one connected water-management system rather than a series of separate patches. A back leg without a matching end dam, an end dam without a matching weep, or a weep installed above rather than at the flashing elevation each defeats the system at the point of the missing connection. Verify the full path from entry point to exterior outlet before installing the next component.

Measurement Cue — Lap and Spacing Verification

Verify every flashing lap length and every weep spacing against the approved wall section before installation, and record the as-built measurement on the Flashing and Weep Inspection Log. Do not estimate a lap or spacing from general trade practice when the wall section specifies an exact value.

Critical Safety Cue — Concealment Before Inspection

Do not allow veneer, brick, block, stone, or any wall finish to conceal a flashing, end-dam, cavity, or weep detail before the Step 20 inspection has passed and been photographed. A concealed, unverified detail cannot be corrected without demolition, and a failed detail that is concealed anyway creates a hidden moisture path into the structure.

Field Safety Cue — Cutting and Overhead Work

Wear cut-resistant gloves and eye protection when cutting or forming metal flashing with tin snips or a hand seamer. Support flashing stock on a stable work surface while cutting. Keep hands clear of formed edges until they are dressed.

Code Alert — Weep Spacing and Flashing Requirements

Confirm weep type, spacing, and required flashing locations against adopted code and the approved wall section before installation. Requirements vary by jurisdiction and wall assembly; do not apply the spacing or flashing locations from a prior project without verifying them for the current wall section.

F. Layout and Substrate Preparation

  1. Review the approved wall section and identify every water-entry and drainage point. Mark base flashing, through-wall flashing, head flashing, sill flashing, lintel flashing, shelf-angle flashing, end dams, and weeps directly on the mockup or a site sketch.
  2. Mark base flashing, through-wall flashing, head flashing, sill flashing, lintel flashing, shelf-angle flashing, end dams, and weeps. Record each location and dimension on the Flashing and Weep Inspection Log before cutting any material.
  3. Confirm substrate and WRB are clean and continuous. Repair any WRB damage using the approved building-envelope method before flashing begins.
  4. Select compatible flashing, tapes, primers, end-dam material, drip edge, and sealants. Verify every product against the approved wall section and manufacturer literature.
  5. Verify corrosion and material compatibility. Confirm flashing metal, fasteners, and adjacent materials will not create a galvanic or chemical incompatibility.
  6. Prepare the substrate within product limits. Confirm surface temperature, moisture condition, and cleanliness meet the flashing and tape manufacturer's application requirements.

G. Base and Through-Wall Flashing Installation

  1. Install base flashing with the required back leg and outward slope. Verify slope with a level before proceeding to the next step.
  2. Integrate the flashing with the WRB in shingle-lap order. Confirm each lap directs water outward and downward with no reverse condition.
  3. Form end dams at terminations. Form or apply an end dam at every flashing end so no gap allows water to bypass the termination.
  4. Extend flashing to the exterior face or drip as specified. Confirm the flashing extends beyond the wall face by the dimension in the approved wall section.
  5. Install through-wall flashing above lintels, openings, shelf angles, and interruptions. Repeat the back-leg, slope, lap, and end-dam requirements at each location.
  6. Seal penetrations only with compatible accessories. Use manufacturer-specified boots, collars, or patches for any penetration; do not rely on sealant alone.

H. Cavity and Weep Installation

  1. Install cavity drainage material or mortar-dropping control. Fasten per manufacturer instructions and confirm full coverage of the specified cavity height.
  2. Keep the cavity open and free of mortar bridging. Inspect for droppings or debris and remove any obstruction before installing the next component.
  3. Install open-head weeps, tubes, wicks, vents, or proprietary weeps at specified spacing. Measure and mark each location before installation.
  4. Keep weeps aligned with flashing. Confirm each weep sits at the flashing elevation, not above or below it.
  5. Do not fill weeps with mortar or sealant. Inspect each weep after installation to confirm it remains open.
  6. Install termination or counterflashing where specified. Confirm the termination detail matches the approved wall section.

I. Verification and Inspection

  1. Perform a limited drainage observation or mockup test where approved. Follow the specific test method, water volume, and duration identified in the approved wall section; do not perform an unapproved or improvised water test.
  2. Inspect laps, adhesion, end dams, slope, outlet, cavity, weeps, and penetrations. Record each item as Pass or Deficient on the Flashing and Weep Inspection Log.
  3. Photograph concealed details. Photograph every flashing lap, end dam, cavity condition, and weep location with a tape measure in frame showing the as-built dimension.
  4. Correct pinholes, reverse laps, blocked outlets, and incompatible materials before veneer work. Reinspect every corrected item and record the reinspection result before the mockup is released to Chapter 11 or Chapter 12 veneer work.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every flashing and weep mockup using the same checks regardless of the selected location.

  • Lap and slope check: confirm every flashing lap sheds water outward and downward and that base flashing slope was verified with a level before concealment.
  • End-dam check: confirm an end dam is present and effective at every flashing termination.
  • Cavity check: confirm the drainage cavity is open from top to weep elevation with no mortar bridging or debris.
  • Weep check: confirm weeps are installed at the specified spacing, aligned with the flashing, and free of mortar or sealant obstruction.
  • Documentation check: confirm every concealed detail was photographed with a measurement reference before veneer or wall work proceeded.

Complete the Flashing and Weep Inspection Log in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the mockup to veneer work.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Water Stains Appear Below a Flashing Lap

Probable cause: a reverse lap directed water behind the flashing instead of over it.
Confirmation: inspect the lap sequence at the stained location and compare it against the shingle-lap requirement.
Correction: remove the affected flashing section and reinstall it in the correct shingle-lap order with compatible tape or sealant at the reworked lap only.
Reinspection: reinspect the corrected lap and reperform the drainage observation if one was part of the original acceptance.

A Weep Is Blocked or Filled

Probable cause: mortar was allowed to enter the weep during installation, or a weep was sealed to "clean up" the appearance of the wall.
Confirmation: probe the weep opening; a blocked weep will not allow a probe to pass through to the cavity.
Correction: clear the obstruction or replace the weep accessory; never seal a weep with mortar or sealant as a substitute for clearing it.
Reinspection: confirm the weep is fully open and aligned with the flashing before concealment.

The Cavity Is Bridged With Mortar Droppings

Probable cause: mortar-dropping control was omitted, damaged, or installed after masonry work had already begun above the cavity.
Confirmation: inspect the cavity from the weep opening or an accessible course for visible mortar bridging between the backup wall and the veneer or unit face.
Correction: remove the bridging material without damaging the flashing, and install or repair the cavity drainage material before further masonry work continues.
Reinspection: confirm the cavity is open from top to weep elevation before veneer work resumes.

No End Dam Is Present at a Flashing Termination

Probable cause: the flashing run was cut to length without forming or applying the required end dam.
Confirmation: inspect every flashing end and termination for a formed upturn or applied end-dam accessory.
Correction: form or apply the end dam using the manufacturer-specified method before the location is concealed.
Reinspection: confirm the end dam is continuous and photograph it before veneer work covers the location.

A Detail Was Concealed Before Inspection

Probable cause: veneer or wall work proceeded before the Step 20 inspection and photographic record were completed.
Confirmation: compare the Flashing and Weep Inspection Log against the photographic record; a missing photograph or inspection entry for a now-concealed location confirms premature concealment.
Correction: stop veneer or wall work at the affected location and, where the detail cannot be verified without removal, remove the concealing work to inspect and photograph the flashing and weep detail underneath.
Reinspection: complete the inspection and photographic record before veneer or wall work resumes at that location.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, verified drainage mockup, not a visually finished wall with unverified details underneath.

  • Confirm every photograph is labeled with the component, location, and date it documents
  • File the completed Flashing and Weep Inspection Log with the approved wall section it references
  • Dispose of flashing offcuts and packaging without leaving sharp metal edges in the work area
  • Clean and store cutting and forming tools
  • Retain the inspection log and photographs for use in Chapter 11, Chapter 12, and the Chapter 20 capstone

Field Terms

Back Leg
The vertical portion of base flashing that turns up behind the WRB or backup wall.
End Dam
A formed or applied upturn at a flashing termination that prevents water from bypassing the end of the run.
Shingle Lap
An overlap sequence in which each higher course of flashing or WRB overlaps the course below it.
Drainage Cavity
The open air space behind veneer or cladding that carries moisture down to the flashing and out through the weeps.
Weep
An opening in a mortar joint or flashing system that allows drainage water to exit a masonry cavity.
Mortar-Dropping Control
A material installed in the cavity to prevent mortar droppings from bridging or blocking the drainage path.
Through-Wall Flashing
Flashing installed above lintels, openings, shelf angles, or other interruptions in a masonry wall.
Counterflashing
A termination flashing installed to cover and protect the top edge of another flashing component.
Flashing and Weep Inspection Log
The document recording every flashing, end-dam, cavity, and weep finding, correction, and photographic reference.
