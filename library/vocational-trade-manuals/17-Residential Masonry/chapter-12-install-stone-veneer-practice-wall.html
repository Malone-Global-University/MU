Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install one complete stone-veneer practice section using the exact approved system named in your project documents — adhered manufactured stone, adhered natural stone, anchored stone veneer, or another approved assembly. The finished work product is one inspected, cured stone-veneer section with confirmed substrate preparation, WRB continuity, lath and scratch coat where required, full-contact setting, consistent joint width, preserved drainage, and documented adhesion.

This chapter depends on the readiness plan produced in Chapter 1, the controlled work area and silica protections established in Chapter 2, and the cutting procedures in Chapter 10. It follows directly from Chapter 11, which installed brick veneer using ties, cavity, and flashing; this chapter installs stone veneer using the different but equally exact system requirements for scratch coat, lath, and setting mortar. Do not treat the two chapters as interchangeable procedures.

Stone veneer fails most often from a mismatched system — natural-stone setting methods applied to manufactured stone, or anchored details substituted where an adhered system was specified. This chapter requires you to identify one system, confirm its exact requirements, and follow it without substitution.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RM-067 — Prepare the substrate, confirming WRB continuity, lath installation, or anchor supports exactly as the selected system specifies.
  • RM-068 — Apply a scratch coat where the selected system requires one, to specified thickness, scoring, and cure.
  • RM-069 — Select the stone layout, blending size, shape, color, and texture and reserving corner and special units before setting begins.
  • RM-070 — Set stone veneer with full mortar contact, correct orientation, and no voids behind the unit.
  • RM-071 — Maintain joint spacing consistent with the specified style, whether tight-fit, mortar-joint, or dry-stack appearance.
  • RM-072 — Inspect veneer adhesion and drainage, confirming support, joint depth, color distribution, clearances, and preserved weeps and cavities.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 11 installed a drained cavity veneer system using ties, flashing, and an open air space behind brick. This chapter installs a different water-management approach: an adhered or anchored stone system where the scratch coat, lath, and setting mortar themselves control moisture management, or where an anchored assembly preserves a drainage cavity behind the stone. Both approaches depend on the same upstream readiness, staging, and mortar-mixing skills from Chapters 1 through 3.

The system-identification judgment practiced here — confirming which stone-veneer system is specified and refusing to blend its requirements with another system's details — is the same judgment tested again in Chapter 13's flashing and weep work and in the Chapter 20 capstone if the veneer option is selected.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • identifying whether the approved project is adhered manufactured stone, adhered natural stone, anchored stone veneer, or another approved assembly, and using only that system's requirements
  • confirming substrate, WRB, drainage plane, lath, fasteners, scratch coat, mortar, weeps, clearances, and movement joints against approved documents
  • installing WRB layers, drainage mat, lath, or anchored supports exactly as specified, lapping and fastening lath correctly and wrapping corners and openings
  • applying, scoring, and curing a scratch coat where the system requires one
  • laying out stone for size, shape, color, and texture blend, reserving corner and special units
  • setting stone with full contact, correct joint width, and no drainage-cavity contamination
  • cutting stone using Chapter 10 controls and cleaning finished faces using compatible, nonaggressive methods
  • inspecting adhesion, support, joint depth, color distribution, drainage, and clearances

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • mix requirements from two different stone-veneer systems on the same wall section
  • install veneer on unsupported trim, sheathing, finish cladding, loose concrete, or an unverified ledge
  • proceed without a passed Chapter 1 substrate and water-management inspection
  • use sealers, caulk, or cosmetic patching to correct missing flashing, failed drainage, incompatible mortar, or a structural deficiency discovered during this chapter
  • alter structural backup framing, add unapproved fasteners to structural members, or change the specified drainage plane design

Any substrate, WRB, or support condition that fails inspection in this chapter must be corrected and reinspected, following the same stop-work discipline established in Chapter 1, before setting stone.

Trade Note

The single most damaging error in stone veneer work is treating "stone veneer" as one procedure. Adhered manufactured stone, adhered natural stone, and anchored stone veneer have different substrate, weight, drainage, and fastening requirements. Confirm the system in Step 1 of the work order, and do not let a familiar detail from a different system substitute for the one actually specified.

Setup Standard

Do not apply scratch coat or setting mortar until the substrate, WRB, and lath or anchor installation have passed the inspection required in Step 4 and the wet-cutting or extraction controls from Chapter 2 and Chapter 10 are staged and confirmed operational.

Shop Tip

Lay out and blend stone on the ground or a work table before mixing any setting mortar. A layout decided unit-by-unit while mortar is open leads to visible color clustering and rushed joint spacing.

System Confirmation and Substrate Judgment

The system identified in Step 1 controls every later step in this chapter. An adhered manufactured-stone system, an adhered natural-stone system, and an anchored stone-veneer system have different substrate weight limits, different lath and scratch-coat requirements, and different drainage strategies. Confirm the system before touching the substrate, and do not substitute a detail from one system into another because it is more familiar or convenient.

Critical Safety Cue — Unsupported or Unverified Substrate

Do not install stone veneer on unsupported trim, sheathing, finish cladding, loose concrete, or an unverified ledge. Confirm the substrate has passed the Chapter 1 base inspection and can carry the specified system weight before installing WRB, lath, or scratch coat. Stop work and refer to the responsible design authority if the substrate condition does not match the approved drawings.

Field Safety Cue — Silica and Wet-Mortar Controls

Cut stone only using the wet-cutting or extraction controls established in Chapter 10. Wear waterproof gloves and eye protection whenever mixing, applying, or handling scratch coat or setting mortar. Do not kneel in wet mortar without approved waterproof protection.

Manufacturer Cue — Product-Specific Requirements

Lath type, fastener spacing, scratch-coat thickness, scoring pattern, cure time, setting-mortar type, and maximum stone weight per square foot are controlled by the approved system specification and manufacturer instructions. Do not substitute a different lath, fastener, or mortar type without confirming compatibility with the identified system.

E. System Selection and Substrate Preparation

  1. Identify whether the selected system is adhered manufactured stone, adhered natural stone, anchored stone veneer, or another approved assembly. Record the selection on the System Identification Record.
  2. Do not mix system requirements. Confirm every later step references only the identified system's specification.
  3. Review support, sheathing, WRB, drainage plane, lath, fasteners, scratch coat, mortar, weeps, clearances, and movement joints. Cite the specific specification section for each.
  4. Confirm substrate and water-management inspection. Verify the Chapter 1 readiness decision is Approved or Conditional-cleared before proceeding.

F. WRB, Lath, and Scratch Coat

  1. Install WRB layers, drainage mat, lath, or supports exactly as specified. Confirm continuity across the entire installation area.
  2. Lap and fasten lath in the required orientation. Use the fastener type and spacing named in the system specification.
  3. Wrap corners and openings. Maintain continuous lath coverage without gaps at transitions.
  4. Apply scratch coat to required thickness. Measure thickness at multiple points across the installation area.
  5. Score the scratch coat horizontally or as specified. Use the scoring pattern named in the system specification.
  6. Allow it to cure. Confirm cure using the moisture meter or the method specified before proceeding to setting.

G. Layout, Setting, and Jointing

  1. Lay out stone on the ground or work table. Arrange units across the full installation area before setting any of them.
  2. Blend size, shape, color, and texture. Distribute color and texture variation evenly rather than clustering similar units.
  3. Reserve corner units and special pieces. Set these aside for their designated locations before general setting begins.
  4. Clean or dampen stone backs where required. Follow the system specification for surface preparation.
  5. Apply setting mortar to the wall or unit according to the system. Use the application method and coverage specified.
  6. Press and move the stone into place to achieve full contact. Use a slight back-and-forth motion where the system method calls for it.
  7. Maintain joint width and avoid mortar voids. Check joint width against the specified style at regular intervals.
  8. Do not contaminate drainage cavities or weeps. Remove excess mortar before it reaches a cavity or weep opening.
  9. Cut stone using Chapter 10 controls. Apply wet-cutting or extraction controls to every cut unit.
  10. Work from corners and openings according to the approved sequence. Follow the layout sequence stated in the system specification.
  11. Fill and tool joints where the style requires them. Tool at the firmness specified for the mortar in use.
  12. Clean stone faces without aggressive acid or high pressure. Use only the cleaning method approved for the identified stone type.

H. Protection, Inspection, and Documentation

  1. Protect and cure the veneer. Shield the installation from rain, freezing, rapid drying, and impact during cure.
  2. Inspect adhesion, support, joint depth, color distribution, drainage, and clearances. Record findings on the Stone-Veneer Installation Worksheet.
  3. Document the complete system. File the System Identification Record, component schedule, batch records, photographs, and inspection results together.

Common Installation Inspection

Inspect every stone-veneer section using the same checks regardless of the selected system.

  • System check: confirm every installed component traces to the single system identified in Step 1, with no detail borrowed from a different system.
  • Substrate and WRB check: confirm continuity, laps, fastener spacing, and corner wrapping match the approved specification.
  • Scratch-coat check: confirm thickness, scoring, and cure time were measured and recorded before setting mortar was applied.
  • Setting check: confirm full mortar contact behind a sample unit, correct joint width, and no drainage-path contamination.
  • Closeout check: confirm adhesion, support, joint depth, color distribution, drainage, and clearances were inspected and documented before the section is called complete.

Complete the Stone-Veneer Installation Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before proceeding to Chapter 13.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Stone Detaches or Sounds Hollow When Tapped

Probable cause: setting mortar did not achieve full contact behind the unit, or the scratch coat was not cured before setting.
Confirmation: test-remove a sample unit near the affected area and inspect the mortar coverage on both the wall and unit back.
Correction: remove the affected unit, clean the cavity, reapply setting mortar using the full-contact method specified, and reset the unit.
Reinspection: test-remove and reinspect an adjacent sample unit before continuing to the next section.

Color or Texture Clusters in One Area of the Wall

Probable cause: stone was set directly from boxes or pallets without a ground layout and blend step.
Confirmation: compare the installed pattern against the layout record; missing or incomplete layout documentation indicates the blend step was skipped.
Correction: where mortar has not yet set, redistribute units to correct the clustering; where mortar has cured, document the condition and correct only within the approved system's repair method.
Reinspection: reinspect color and texture distribution across the full section once corrected.

Mortar Has Bridged the Drainage Cavity or Blocked a Weep

Probable cause: excess setting mortar was not removed before it reached the cavity or weep opening.
Confirmation: inspect the cavity or weep visually and with a probe where the system allows access.
Correction: remove the mortar bridge or obstruction using a method compatible with the system before the mortar fully cures.
Reinspection: reinspect the cavity and weep for a clear drainage path before proceeding.

Joint Width Is Inconsistent Across the Section

Probable cause: joint width was not checked at regular intervals during setting, or units were adjusted after mortar began to set.
Confirmation: measure joint width at multiple points and compare against the specified style tolerance.
Correction: where mortar has not yet set, reposition affected units; where mortar has cured, treat as a repointing item under Chapter 14 scope.
Reinspection: remeasure joint width across the corrected area.

A Detail From a Different System Was Installed

Probable cause: a lath, scratch-coat, or setting detail from a different stone-veneer system was substituted because it was more familiar.
Confirmation: compare every installed component against the single system identified in the System Identification Record.
Correction: remove and reinstall the affected component using the specified system's exact requirement; do not leave a mixed-system detail in place.
Reinspection: reinspect the corrected component and confirm it now matches the identified system before continuing.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a cured, documented stone-veneer section, not an assumption that it will bond and drain correctly.

  • Confirm every concealed WRB, lath, and scratch-coat photograph is labeled with the item, location, and date it documents
  • File the System Identification Record, component schedule, batch records, and inspection results with the Chapter 1 readiness plan for this project
  • Clean mixing tools, trowels, and cutting equipment, and dispose of slurry without drain discharge
  • Store leftover stone, lath, and mortar materials protected from moisture and contamination
  • Retain the worksheet and photographs for use in Chapter 13 flashing and weep work and any later inspection of this section

Field Terms

Selected System
The single controlling stone-veneer assembly confirmed before any material is installed.
Scratch Coat
A base mortar coat applied to lath and scored to receive setting mortar, used only where the selected system requires it.
Setting Mortar
The mortar bonding stone to the substrate or scratch coat.
Full Contact
Complete mortar coverage across the back of a set unit, with no voids.
Lath
The metal reinforcement fastened over the WRB to receive scratch coat.
Drainage Plane
The continuous water-shedding layer behind the veneer assembly.
Joint Style
The specified joint appearance controlling joint width and finishing method.
Weep
An opening that allows drainage water to exit a masonry or veneer cavity.
Stone-Veneer Installation Worksheet
The document recording system identification, component verification, batch records, and inspection results for this chapter.
Movement Joint
A designed gap that accommodates differential movement without rigidly bonding veneer across it.
