Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to batch, mix, test, and stage one approved mortar mix and to prepare the masonry units that will be laid in that mortar. The finished work product is one documented mortar batch, accepted by a workability check, together with a set of cleaned and correctly conditioned masonry units ready for the layout work in Chapter 4 and the wall construction work in Chapters 5 through 9.

You will not select a mortar type in this chapter. The mortar type, proportioning method, packaged product, pigment, aggregate, and admixture requirements were identified and documented on the Masonry-Readiness Worksheet in Chapter 1 and confirmed against the approved drawings and specifications. This chapter teaches you to batch and mix exactly what was specified, to control water content within the permitted range, and to recognize and correct a batch that does not meet the workability standard before it is used.

This chapter depends directly on the readiness plan from Chapter 1 and the staged work area, mixing area, and silica controls from Chapter 2. The mortar batch and prepared units produced here are the direct inputs to the layout work in Chapter 4 and every wall, corner, veneer, repair, and capstone project later in this book.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RM-013 — Select mortar type, confirming the specified mortar type, proportioning method, packaged product, pigment, aggregate, water, admixtures, and weather conditions against the approved drawings and specifications.
  • RM-014 — Measure mortar ingredients, batching cementitious materials, aggregate, pigment, admixtures, and water to the specified proportions using a consistent measuring method.
  • RM-015 — Mix mortar to workable consistency, observing color, consistency, aggregate distribution, cohesion, and water retention, and performing the approved workability check.
  • RM-016 — Control water content, adding only the permitted initial water and, where allowed, only the permitted retempering water within the allowed window.
  • RM-017 — Prepare units for laying, cleaning mud, ice, dust, and loose material from masonry units and conditioning high-absorption units only where the specification requires it.
  • RM-018 — Inspect mortar workability, rejecting mortar with incorrect consistency, segregation, contamination, excessive age, or freezing, and documenting the accepted batch.

Where This Skill Fits

Every masonry project in this book depends on a mortar batch that matches the specified type and stays within its permitted water and working-time limits. The batching, mixing, water-control, and workability skills taught here are reused without re-teaching in Chapters 5 through 9, the reinforced and grouted work in Chapter 7, the veneer work in Chapters 11 and 12, the repointing and repair work in Chapters 14 and 15, and the Chapter 20 capstone.

The unit-preparation skill taught here — cleaning mud, ice, and dust from units and conditioning high-absorption units only where specified — controls bond strength in every later laying chapter. A unit prepared incorrectly here produces a bond failure that cannot be corrected after the wall is built.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the specified mortar type, proportioning method, packaged product, pigment, aggregate, water, admixtures, and weather conditions from the Chapter 1 readiness plan and approved drawings
  • confirming mortar compatibility with the specified unit type, exposure, structural role, and any existing work being matched
  • inspecting cementitious materials, aggregate, packaged mortar, pigments, and admixtures for contamination, moisture damage, or expiration before batching
  • staging clean measured water and clean mixing equipment
  • batching, mixing, resting, and retempering one approved mortar batch within the permitted proportions, water content, and working time
  • preparing masonry units for laying by cleaning and, where specified, conditioning them
  • performing the approved workability check and rejecting or accepting the batch
  • documenting batch proportions, water, time, weather, pigment, and acceptance

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • select or substitute a mortar type, proportioning method, packaged product, pigment, aggregate, or admixture that was not identified in the Chapter 1 readiness plan or the approved drawings and specifications
  • add water to mortar beyond the permitted retempering time or after set has begun
  • lay masonry units, cut units, install reinforcement, grout, veneer, or flashing; those tasks belong to Chapters 4 through 13
  • mix grout as a substitute for mortar, or mortar as a substitute for grout; grout mixing is taught in Chapter 7
  • use frozen units, ice-contaminated aggregate, partially set mortar, or contaminated materials under any circumstance

Any mortar batch or unit condition that fails the acceptance standards in this chapter must be rejected, corrected where correction is permitted, or discarded before laying begins.

Trade Note

This chapter assumes the Chapter 1 Masonry-Readiness Worksheet and Chapter 2 work-area readiness inspection are both complete and on hand. The mortar type, proportioning method, and material sources documented in those records are the only ones used in this chapter; nothing in this chapter authorizes selecting a different mortar on-site.

Setup Standard

Do not begin batching until the Chapter 1 readiness decision is Approved or Conditional-cleared and the Chapter 2 work-area readiness inspection has confirmed the mixing area, water supply, silica controls, and weather protection are in place. Mortar mixed before these conditions are met must be discarded rather than used.

Shop Tip

Batch only the quantity of mortar that the laying crew can place within the mortar's working time. A large batch that sits on the board past its working time is not corrected by adding water; it is discarded and replaced with a fresh, smaller batch.

Common Field Fundamentals

Mortar performance depends on using the specified type and proportions, controlling water content from the first mix through every permitted retempering, and laying units that are properly cleaned and, where specified, conditioned for absorption. This chapter teaches that sequence once so every later laying chapter can rely on a compatible, workable, well-documented batch.

Mortar Compatibility and Weather Judgment

The mortar type confirmed in Step 1 controls every later batching decision. Stronger mortar is not automatically better; mortar must match the specified unit type, exposure, and structural role exactly as documented on the Chapter 1 Masonry-Readiness Worksheet. Do not substitute a different mortar type, proportion, or packaged product to compensate for cold, hot, wet, or windy conditions; instead, apply the specific weather control identified for the anticipated conditions.

Measurement Cue — Consistent Proportioning

Measure every ingredient using the same measuring containers and method for every batch of the project. Do not estimate proportions by eye after the first batch; inconsistent proportioning between batches produces visible color and strength variation in the finished wall.

Critical Safety Cue — Caustic Materials

Treat mortar, cementitious materials, and mixing water as caustic. Wear waterproof gloves, eye protection, and long sleeves whenever handling wet mortar or opening cementitious material bags. Wash exposed skin immediately if contact occurs. Do not kneel in wet mortar without approved waterproof protection.

Field Safety Cue — Mixer and Dust Control

Inspect the mortar mixer for guarding, stable footing, and a clear area around moving parts before starting it. Control dust when opening and charging bags per the Chapter 2 silica-control plan. Do not dry sweep, blow, or brush cementitious dust into occupied or outdoor air.

Code Alert — Mortar Type Substitution

Do not substitute a mortar type, proportion, or packaged product different from the one identified in the approved drawings and specifications, even if a different mortar is on hand or was used on a prior project. Confirm compatibility with the specified unit type and exposure before batching.

F. Preparation — Review and Material Inspection

  1. Review specified mortar type, proportioning method, packaged product, pigment, aggregate, water, admixtures, and weather conditions. Confirm the exact requirements against the approved drawings, specifications, and Chapter 1 Masonry-Readiness Worksheet before proceeding.
  2. Confirm mortar compatibility with unit type, exposure, structural role, and existing work where applicable. Record the confirmation on the Mortar Batch Record before batching.
  3. Inspect cementitious materials, aggregate, packaged mortar, pigments, and admixtures for contamination, moisture damage, or expiration. Reject and set aside any material failing this inspection.
  4. Stage clean measured water and clean mixing equipment. Confirm the water source is approved and the mixer, mixing board, and tools are free of hardened residue from prior batches.
  5. Wear wet-cement and dust PPE. Put on waterproof gloves, eye protection, long sleeves, and dust or respiratory protection as required before opening any bagged material.

G. Batching and Mixing

  1. Add materials in the sequence required by the approved mix. Follow the exact order stated in the specification or packaged-product manufacturer instructions.
  2. Control dust when opening and charging bags. Use the local exhaust, wetting, or handling method established in the Chapter 2 silica-control plan.
  3. Measure all ingredients consistently. Use the same measuring containers and method for every batch of the project.
  4. Add initial water within the permitted range. Record the quantity added on the Mortar Batch Record.
  5. Mix for the specified time. Time the mix with a stopwatch or timer and record the actual mix time.
  6. Observe mortar color, consistency, aggregate distribution, cohesion, and water retention. Compare the observed batch against the description in the specification or manufacturer instructions.
  7. Allow required rest or tempering time. Follow the rest period stated in the specification or manufacturer instructions before remixing.
  8. Remix as specified. Remix for the duration stated in the specification before the batch is used.

H. Water Control and Batch Transfer

  1. Add only permitted water during the allowed retempering window. Confirm the batch is still within its permitted retempering window before adding any water, and add no more than the permitted quantity.
  2. Do not add water to mortar beyond the permitted time or after set begins. Discard any batch that has passed its retempering window or shows signs of initial set rather than adding water to restore workability.
  3. Transfer mortar to clean boards in quantities that can be used within working time. Size each board transfer to the crew's placement rate so no mortar exceeds its working time on the board.
  4. Protect mortar from sun, wind, rain, freezing, and contamination. Apply the cover, shade, windbreak, or heat protection established in the Chapter 2 work-area setup.

I. Unit Preparation

  1. Prepare masonry units by cleaning mud, ice, dust, and loose material. Use a brush, cloth, or clean water as appropriate to the unit type without saturating units that must remain dry.
  2. Condition high-absorption units only where the specification requires it. Apply the specific conditioning method and timing stated in the specification; do not condition units where the specification does not call for it.
  3. Do not lay saturated, frozen, or visibly damaged units. Set aside any unit failing this check and record it on the Mortar Batch Record's unit-preparation section.

J. Workability Check and Documentation

  1. Perform the approved board-life, cone, trowel, or field workability check. Use the specific test method identified in the specification and record the result.
  2. Reject mortar with incorrect consistency, segregation, contamination, excessive age, or freezing. Discard a rejected batch; do not attempt to correct it by remixing with additional dry material or water beyond the permitted retempering limits.
  3. Document batch proportions, water, time, weather, pigment, and acceptance. Complete the Mortar Batch Record before the batch is used for layout or laying work.

Common Batch Inspection

Inspect every mortar batch and unit-preparation cycle using the same checks regardless of the specified mortar type.

  • Type check: confirm the batched mortar matches the specified type, proportioning method, or packaged product identified before batching began.
  • Water check: confirm initial water, rest period, and any retempering fall within the permitted range and time window, with no water added after that window closes.
  • Workability check: confirm the batch passed the approved board-life, cone, trowel, or field test before use.
  • Unit check: confirm units are free of mud, ice, dust, and loose material and, where specified, correctly conditioned for absorption.
  • Documentation check: confirm the Mortar Batch Record is complete before the batch or units are used in Chapter 4 or later chapters.

Complete the Mortar Batch Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 4.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Mortar Stiffens Quickly on the Board

Probable cause: hot, dry, or windy conditions increased the evaporation rate beyond what the batch size and board protection can offset, or the batch exceeded its working time before use.
Confirmation: check the ambient temperature and wind recorded on the Mortar Batch Record against the time elapsed since initial mixing.
Correction: retemper only if still within the permitted window and water quantity; otherwise discard the batch and mix a smaller batch sized to the crew's placement rate, with board protection appropriate to the weather.
Reinspection: repeat the workability check on the new or retempered batch before use.

Batch Color Varies From a Previous Batch

Probable cause: pigment, aggregate, or cementitious material was measured inconsistently between batches, or a different lot of packaged material was used without verification.
Confirmation: compare the measuring method and material lot numbers recorded for each batch on the Mortar Batch Record.
Correction: remeasure future batches using the same containers and method, and confirm material lot consistency before batching; discard a batch with confirmed proportioning error.
Reinspection: compare the corrected batch's color against the accepted reference batch before use.

Mortar Fails the Workability Check

Probable cause: incorrect water content, insufficient mix time, segregated or contaminated aggregate, or an expired or damaged cementitious material.
Confirmation: review the recorded initial water, mix time, and material inspection notes on the Mortar Batch Record against the specified values.
Correction: discard the failed batch and mix a new batch, correcting the specific deviation identified during confirmation.
Reinspection: repeat the workability check on the new batch and record the result before use.

Units Fail to Bond After Laying Begins in a Later Chapter

Probable cause: units were laid saturated, frozen, or with residual mud or dust, or a high-absorption unit required conditioning that was not performed.
Confirmation: check the unit-preparation section of the Mortar Batch Record for the cleaning and conditioning steps actually performed on the affected units.
Correction: for units not yet laid, reclean or recondition per the specification before laying; for units already laid, refer the condition to the applicable repair chapter rather than concealing it.
Reinspection: repeat the unit-preparation check on the remaining unit supply before further laying continues.

A Batch Was Retempered After the Permitted Window Closed

Probable cause: the elapsed time since initial mixing was not tracked against the specified retempering window before water was added.
Confirmation: compare the time of water addition recorded on the Mortar Batch Record against the specified retempering window.
Correction: discard the batch; a batch retempered outside the permitted window is not corrected by any further action and must not be used.
Reinspection: confirm the discarded batch was removed from the work area and a new batch was mixed and time-tracked from initial mixing.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, accepted mortar batch and prepared units, not an estimate of what was mixed.

  • Clean the mixer, mixing board, and tools before mortar hardens on them
  • Return unused measured materials to sealed, labeled, contamination-free storage
  • Contain and dispose of washout water per the Chapter 2 washout plan; do not discharge washout to a storm drain
  • File the completed Mortar Batch Record with the Chapter 1 Masonry-Readiness Worksheet and Chapter 2 work-area record
  • Store prepared, cleaned units under the weather protection established in Chapter 2 until Chapter 4 layout begins

Field Terms

Batch
One complete mixing cycle of mortar from initial water addition through the end of its working time.
Proportioning Method
The specified ratio or measured-batch method used to combine cementitious material, aggregate, pigment, and admixtures.
Working Time
The period after initial mixing during which mortar may be used without retempering.
Retempering
The addition of a limited, specified quantity of water within a permitted window to restore workability lost to evaporation.
Board Life
The maximum time mortar may remain on the mortar board before it must be used or discarded.
Workability Check
The approved board-life, cone, trowel, or field test used to confirm mortar consistency before use.
Conditioning
A specified pre-wetting or absorption-control treatment applied to high-absorption masonry units before laying.
Segregation
The separation of aggregate, cementitious material, or water within a mortar batch, indicating an unacceptable or failed mix.
Mortar Batch Record
The document recording batch proportions, water, mix and rest time, weather, pigment, unit preparation, and acceptance.
Rejection Condition
A defined batch condition — incorrect consistency, segregation, contamination, excessive age, or freezing — that requires the batch to be discarded rather than used.

Performance Check

With batching complete, review the Mortar Batch Record against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the mortar type, proportions, water content, mix time, and workability check all match the specified values. Confirm every unit to be laid has been cleaned and, where specified, conditioned, and that no saturated, frozen, or visibly damaged unit remains in the prepared supply. Accept the batch only if every standard is met; a batch or unit condition that fails any standard is discarded, corrected where correction is permitted, or reinspected before Chapter 4 begins.
