Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to cut brick, concrete block, and stone accurately and safely using both hand chisel methods and masonry-saw methods. The finished work product is one documented set of chisel-cut and saw-cut specimens, each marked, cut, dressed, test-fit, and inspected for dimension, face quality, and edge condition, together with a written record of the cutting method and silica controls used for each specimen.

You will cut a controlled quantity of brick, block, and stone units identified in the chapter project specification, using an established contained cutting zone, wet cutting or local exhaust, and approved PPE. Nothing in this chapter authorizes cutting inside an occupied space without the specified containment, nor does it authorize dry-cutting where the silica-control plan requires wet cutting or extraction.

Every wall, corner, pier, veneer, step, and repair project in this book that requires a cut unit depends on the marking, cutting, dressing, and fit-checking procedure taught here. Chapter 5 through Chapter 9 field walls reference this chapter for every cut unit; Chapter 20 references it directly.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RM-055 — Mark masonry cuts, identifying the cut line on all required faces and the waste side before cutting begins.
  • RM-056 — Use masonry chisels, scoring and breaking a unit on a stable support using controlled hand-tool technique.
  • RM-057 — Use masonry saws safely, operating a wet or extraction-equipped saw within guard, feed, and shutdown requirements.
  • RM-058 — Control silica dust, applying wet cutting, local exhaust, HEPA cleanup, and slurry containment throughout the task.
  • RM-059 — Fit cut units, verifying dimension, face quality, edge condition, and joint allowance against the specification.
  • RM-060 — Inspect cut quality, accepting or rejecting each specimen against the stated acceptance standards and documenting the result.

Where This Skill Fits

Every masonry wall in this book eventually reaches an opening, corner, or termination that will not accept a full unit. The marking, chiseling, saw-cutting, silica-control, and fit-checking sequence in this chapter is the exact procedure referenced by name in Chapter 5 through Chapter 9, Chapter 11 through Chapter 17, and the Chapter 20 capstone whenever a cut unit is required.

The silica-control discipline practiced here — wet cutting, local exhaust, contained cleanup — is the same discipline required whenever masonry is cut, ground, routed, raked, drilled, or chipped anywhere else in this book, including repointing removal in Chapter 14 and crack-repair unit removal in Chapter 15.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • marking a cut line on all required faces of brick, block, and stone units and identifying the waste side
  • establishing a contained cutting zone away from occupied spaces and other workers
  • inspecting saw guards, blades, tables, carts, water systems, electrical protection, and emergency stops before use
  • inspecting chisels, hammers, supports, and striking faces before use
  • hand-cutting units on a stable sand bed or approved surface using scoring and controlled breaking technique
  • saw-cutting units using wet cutting or local exhaust, controlled feed, and safe hand position
  • collecting slurry and dust and disposing of it without drain discharge
  • dressing sharp edges using an approved wet or extracted method
  • test-fitting, measuring, and inspecting every cut unit against the specification
  • rejecting units with cracks extending into the retained piece or unacceptable visible damage
  • documenting cut method and silica-control measures for each specimen

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • dry-cut any unit where the project's silica-control plan requires wet cutting or local exhaust
  • operate a masonry saw with a missing or defective guard, damaged blade, or disabled emergency stop
  • cut units inside an occupied or unventilated space without the specified containment and extraction
  • discharge slurry, wash water, or dust-laden runoff into a storm drain, sanitary drain, or unprotected ground surface
  • install a cut unit into a structural, load-bearing, or veneer assembly; installation is governed by the chapter covering that assembly

Any unit with a hidden crack, void, or reinforcement conflict discovered during marking or cutting must be documented and set aside, not forced to fit.

Trade Note

A cut is only as good as the mark that controlled it. Marking every required face before the first chisel blow or saw pass, and identifying the waste side in writing on the worksheet, is the single control that prevents the mismatched, undersized, and unusable cut units diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not begin cutting until the contained cutting zone is established, the saw or chisel and its supports have passed inspection, and water flow or local exhaust is confirmed operating. A saw with a missing guard, damaged blade, or inoperative emergency stop must not be used until repaired or replaced.

Shop Tip

Mark the waste side with a visible "X" in addition to the cut line. A cut line alone can be read from either side once the unit is repositioned on the saw table or sand bed; the waste-side mark prevents cutting the wrong piece to the finished dimension.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every cut in this chapter, whether by hand chisel or saw, follows the same sequence: inspect the unit, establish the contained zone, inspect the tool, mark every required face, identify the waste side, cut using the method-specific technique, control silica throughout, dress the edge, test-fit, and inspect. Learning that sequence here, on a controlled specimen set, means every later wall, veneer, or repair chapter can reference this procedure without repeating it.

Wet Cutting and Local Exhaust as the Default Method

Wet cutting or approved local exhaust is the default method for every cut in this chapter. Dry cutting is permitted only where the project's silica-control plan explicitly allows it. Confirm the required method before the first cut and do not switch to dry cutting for convenience.

Measurement Cue — Mark Every Required Face

Transfer the cut line to every face the specification requires, not only the most visible face. A line marked on one face only can drift out of square by the time the cut reaches the opposite face, producing an out-of-tolerance retained piece.

Critical Safety Cue — Guard, Blade, and Emergency Stop

Do not operate a masonry saw with a missing or defective guard, a cracked, glazed, or damaged blade, or an emergency stop that does not function. Stop work immediately and tag the saw out of service if any of these conditions is found. Do not bypass a guard interlock or remove a guard to improve visibility of the cut.

Field Safety Cue — Hands and Feed

Keep hands outside the blade zone at all times during saw operation. Feed the unit into the blade without twisting or forcing it, and allow the blade to come to a complete stop before removing offcuts or adjusting the unit.

Code Alert — Silica Exposure and Waste Disposal

Local rules govern permissible silica exposure limits, required exposure monitoring, and disposal of slurry and dust-laden wash water. Verify local requirements before disposing of any cutting byproduct; never discharge slurry to a storm drain, sanitary sewer, or unprotected ground.

D. Preparation — Review and Setup

  1. Review unit type, cut dimensions, finished-face orientation, cut method, blade, water, extraction, PPE, and waste handling. Confirm every value against the chapter project specification before staging tools.
  2. Inspect units for hidden cracks, reinforcement, voids, and irregular grain. Set aside any unit showing a defect before marking begins.
  3. Establish a contained cutting zone away from occupied spaces and other workers. Confirm the zone boundary and post it if other workers are on site.
  4. Inspect saw guard, blade, table, cart, water system, electrical protection, and emergency stop. Tag out and do not use any saw failing this inspection.
  5. Inspect chisels, hammers, supports, and striking faces. Replace any chisel with a mushroomed head or damaged cutting edge.

E. Marking

  1. Mark the cut on all required faces. Transfer the cut line from the project specification to every face identified there, using the framing square to verify squareness.
  2. Identify the waste side. Mark a visible "X" on the waste side of the cut line before any tool contacts the unit.

F. Hand Chiseling

  1. For hand cutting, support the unit on a stable sand bed or approved surface. Confirm the support is level and will not shift during striking.
  2. Score the cut line using light, even chisel blows. Hold the chisel vertically on the marked line and strike with controlled force.
  3. Work around the unit until the score is continuous. Score every marked face before attempting to break the unit.
  4. Increase force gradually to complete the break. Strike progressively harder along the continuous score until the unit separates along the line.

G. Saw Cutting

  1. For saw cutting, start water flow or local exhaust before the blade enters the unit. Confirm visible water flow at the blade or confirm exhaust airflow before feeding the unit.
  2. Feed the unit without twisting or forcing it. Guide the unit along the marked line at a steady rate matched to the blade and material.
  3. Keep hands outside the blade zone. Use a push stick, fixture, or clamp for any cut that would otherwise bring hands near the blade.
  4. Allow the blade to stop before removing offcuts. Do not reach toward the blade area until rotation has fully stopped.

H. Silica Control and Dressing

  1. Do not dry-cut where the silica-control plan requires wet cutting. Confirm the required method before each cut and do not substitute dry cutting for convenience.
  2. Collect slurry and dust. Contain wet slurry at the saw table and vacuum dry dust using HEPA equipment immediately after cutting.
  3. Dress sharp edges using an approved wet or extracted method. Remove ragged or sharp arrises from the cut face without introducing dry dust.

I. Fit Check, Inspection, and Documentation

  1. Test-fit the cut unit. Position the retained piece against the corresponding opening, corner, or adjacent unit specified in the project.
  2. Check dimensions, face quality, edge condition, and joint allowance. Measure against the Acceptance Standards table and record the result on the Cutting Worksheet.
  3. Reject units with cracks extending into the retained piece or unacceptable visible damage. Set rejected units aside and record the rejection reason.
  4. Clean equipment and dispose of slurry without drain discharge. Follow the local disposal requirement identified in the Code Alert for this chapter.
  5. Document cut method and control measures. Record the cut method, silica control, dimension, and Accepted/Rejected result for every specimen on the Cutting Worksheet.

Common Cutting Inspection

Inspect every cut specimen using the same checks regardless of unit type or cutting method.

  • Marking check: confirm the cut line was transferred to every required face and the waste side was marked before cutting began.
  • Tool check: confirm the saw or chisel passed inspection before use and that any defective tool was tagged out rather than used.
  • Silica-control check: confirm wet cutting or local exhaust was running throughout the cut and that dust and slurry were collected rather than dispersed.
  • Dimension and fit check: confirm the retained piece meets the stated dimension, squareness, face quality, edge condition, and joint allowance.
  • Documentation check: confirm every specimen has a recorded cut method, control measure, and Accepted/Rejected result.

Complete the Cutting Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before installing any cut unit in a later chapter.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Cut Ran Out of Square

Probable cause: the cut line was marked on only one face, or the saw or chisel drifted from the line during cutting.
Confirmation: check the framing square against the retained piece's cut face and compare it against the marked lines on the remaining faces.
Correction: reject the unit if it is undersized after correction; otherwise recut following the marked line on every face with reduced feed rate or lighter chisel blows.
Reinspection: recheck squareness and dimension before test-fitting.

A Crack Extended Into the Retained Piece

Probable cause: a hidden defect was present in the unit before cutting, or excessive force was applied during the final break or saw feed.
Confirmation: inspect the crack path against the retained piece; any crack crossing into the area required for installation is disqualifying regardless of visible severity.
Correction: reject the unit and select a replacement from inspected stock.
Reinspection: inspect the replacement unit for hidden defects before marking and cutting it.

Dry Dust Was Visible During Cutting

Probable cause: water flow was not confirmed before the blade entered the unit, or local exhaust was not running or was undersized for the cut.
Confirmation: check the water line, pump, and nozzle, or check exhaust airflow at the shroud, before resuming cutting.
Correction: stop cutting, correct the water or exhaust system, and do not resume until visible dust is eliminated.
Reinspection: confirm sustained water flow or exhaust airflow through a complete test cut before returning to production cutting.

The Cut Unit Did Not Fit the Opening or Corner

Probable cause: the cut dimension was taken from the wrong reference, or joint allowance was omitted from the marked line.
Confirmation: recheck the project specification's stated cut dimension and joint allowance against the marked line used for the rejected unit.
Correction: remark and recut a replacement unit using the corrected dimension and joint allowance.
Reinspection: test-fit the replacement unit against the same opening or corner before accepting it.

Slurry or Dust Was Discharged Outside the Contained Zone

Probable cause: containment at the saw table or cutting area was inadequate, or cleanup was delayed until after multiple cuts.
Confirmation: inspect the area surrounding the cutting zone for visible slurry residue or dust deposits.
Correction: clean the affected surfaces immediately using the approved method and correct the containment before further cutting.
Reinspection: confirm containment holds through a complete test cut before resuming production cutting.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, dressed, and fit-checked specimen set, not a pile of unlabeled cut pieces.

  • Confirm every accepted specimen is labeled with its cut dimension, method, and intended use
  • Confirm every rejected specimen is labeled and set aside from usable stock
  • Vacuum and wipe down the saw table, chisel support, and surrounding contained zone
  • Dispose of collected slurry and dust according to the local requirement identified in the Code Alert for this chapter
  • Return saws, chisels, and PPE to storage and file the completed Cutting Worksheet with the project it supports

Field Terms

Cut Line
The marked line identifying where a unit is to be cut, transferred to every required face before cutting begins.
Waste Side
The portion of a unit marked for discard and identified with a visible "X" before cutting.
Retained Piece
The portion of a cut unit intended for installation, inspected against dimension and quality standards.
Contained Cutting Zone
An area separated from occupied spaces and other workers where marking, cutting, dressing, and cleanup occur.
Dressing
The wet or extracted removal of sharp or ragged edges from a cut face.
Local Exhaust
Mechanical airflow used at the point of cutting to capture and remove airborne silica dust.
Slurry
The wet mixture of water and cutting debris produced during wet cutting, requiring contained collection and approved disposal.
Joint Allowance
The additional dimension included in a cut unit to preserve the specified mortar joint width at installation.
Score
The continuous chisel groove cut around a unit before the final controlled break.
Cutting Worksheet
The document recording marking, cut method, silica control, dimensions, and Accepted/Rejected results for every specimen.
