Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install structural or fence posts on footings and post bases that have already passed inspection and reached their approved condition, and to bring those posts to plumb, alignment, and finished elevation before any framing that depends on them begins. The finished work product is a set of posts installed on the approved supports, connected with the specified connector and fasteners, braced plumb in two directions, aligned to the required faces or centers, cut to the specified top elevation, end-treated, and documented for the framing chapters that follow.

You will confirm the footings and post bases are ready to load, inspect and prepare each post, position and temporarily brace the first controlling posts, establish alignment lines from those controlling posts, set every intermediate post to the same line and spacing, transfer a consistent top elevation across the run, cut and treat the post tops, and recheck the entire installation before releasing it to the next chapter.

This project depends entirely on Project 04. No post is set on a footing or post base that has not passed its required inspection and reached the approved cure and connection condition. The posts installed here carry every beam, joist, rail, wall, or roof load addressed in the chapters that follow, so plumb, alignment, and connection accuracy established now are not corrected later without disturbing completed work.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • OS-025 — Select post material appropriate to the approved size, grade, treatment, and exposure rating for its actual location.
  • OS-026 — Position posts on accepted footings, post bases, or other approved supports at the correct orientation and elevation.
  • OS-027 — Brace posts plumb in two directions using temporary bracing sufficient to hold position through subsequent work.
  • OS-028 — Verify plumb on adjacent faces and correct any deviation before permanent connection is finalized.
  • OS-029 — Align post tops and faces to a consistent elevation and reference line across the full run.
  • OS-030 — Inspect and document the completed post installation, including connectors, fasteners, plumb, alignment, and end treatment.

Where This Skill Fits

This project follows directly from the accepted footings and post bases in Project 04 and precedes every framing chapter that depends on a post. Chapter 6's beams bear on the posts set here. Chapter 9's guard posts require the same plumb and load-path standard. Chapter 10's fence posts and Chapter 12's pergola posts use this same sequence adapted to their own spacing and bracing requirements. A post that is out of plumb, misaligned, or under-braced when this project closes carries that error into every later chapter that builds on it.

The habit built here — brace before you trust a post, verify plumb on two adjacent faces rather than one, and hold bracing until a permanent load path exists — is the same habit that governs every post installation in this book, from a single mailbox post to a full deck or shed corner.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming footings and post bases have passed inspection and reached the approved condition before any post is loaded
  • inspecting posts for grade, treatment, straightness, and damage before installation
  • positioning, plumbing, and temporarily bracing posts in two directions
  • connecting posts to their approved base or footing using the specified connector and fasteners
  • establishing and holding alignment across a run of posts
  • transferring and cutting a consistent top elevation, treating exposed end grain, and installing caps where specified
  • inspecting and documenting the completed post installation before it is released to framing

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • set a post on a footing or post base that has not passed its required inspection or reached its approved cured condition
  • substitute generic screws, nails, or an unlisted connector for the specified post-base connector and fastener schedule
  • remove temporary bracing before a beam, rail, permanent bracing, or other stabilizing member establishes a complete load path
  • proceed to Chapter 6, 9, 10, or 12 framing with a post that has not passed the plumb, alignment, connector, and elevation checks in this project

Any post that cannot be brought to plumb, alignment, or a secure connection within the approved tolerance must be corrected and rechecked before this project is declared complete.

Trade Note

A post is only as reliable as its bracing until a permanent load path exists. Every post set in this project is treated as unstable until it is fastened to its approved base and braced in two directions, regardless of how solid it feels by hand. Removing bracing early, even briefly, is one of the most common sources of movement discovered later in framing.

Setup Standard

Do not fasten a post permanently to its base until plumb has been verified on two adjacent faces and the post has been checked against the alignment line. A post fastened out of plumb or off the alignment line must be unfastened, corrected, and rechecked before work continues.

Shop Tip

Set and brace the two controlling end posts of a run first, stretch the alignment line between them, and only then set the intermediate posts against that line. Setting posts in sequence without a controlling line invites a gradual drift that is only visible once the last post is in place.

Support Confirmation Before Any Post Is Loaded

No post is positioned on a footing or post base until Project 04's inspection record confirms the concrete has reached its approved cured condition and the anchor or post base is in its accepted position. Loading a footing before it has cured to the approved condition, or before its inspection is complete, risks movement that is difficult to detect once framing conceals it.

Code Alert — Connector and Fastener Schedule

Post-base connectors and fasteners are controlled by the approved connection detail, not by what happens to be on hand. Verify the exact connector model, fastener type, size, and quantity specified before installation, and do not substitute a generic screw or nail for a listed structural fastener.

Critical Safety Cue — Bracing Before Trust

Never treat a positioned post as stable until it is braced in two directions and permanently fastened to its approved base. Do not lean framing, tools, or materials against an unbraced post, and do not remove temporary bracing until a beam, rail, or permanent bracing establishes a complete load path. An unbraced post can shift or fall without warning.

Field Safety Cue — Handling and Cutting Posts

Support posts securely during cutting so the cut section cannot bind the blade or fall unexpectedly. Keep hands and cords clear of the cutting path, and treat every field cut and drilled hole in pressure-treated material as a required preservative-treatment point before the connection is finalized.

Measurement Cue — Check Plumb on Two Adjacent Faces

Checking plumb on only one face of a post can miss a lean in the perpendicular direction. Check every post on two adjacent faces before final connection, and recheck after tightening fasteners, since driving a connector can shift a post slightly out of plumb.

E. Preparation — Review and Post Inspection

  1. Review post size, grade, treatment, exposure rating, support detail, orientation, top elevation, connectors, and bracing plan. Confirm the approved specification before touching any post.
  2. Confirm footings and post bases have passed inspection and reached the approved condition. Cross-check against the Project 04 records before proceeding.
  3. Inspect posts for grade, treatment, straightness, splits, checks, damage, and correct length. Set aside any post that does not meet the specification.
  4. Apply required field-cut preservative to approved cuts and holes. Treat any cut made to bring a post to length before it is set.
  5. Mark post orientation and finished elevation. Record the reference marks on each post before positioning it.

F. Controlling Post Installation Procedure

  1. Position the first post in its base, hole, or approved support. Confirm correct orientation before bracing.
  2. Install temporary braces in two directions. Secure each brace to a stake or fixed reference so the post cannot shift.
  3. Check plumb on two adjacent faces. Adjust bracing until both faces read plumb.
  4. Fasten the post to the base using the specified connector and fasteners. Do not substitute generic screws or nails.
  5. Set the opposite controlling post. Repeat positioning, bracing, plumb-checking, and fastening at the far end of the run.

G. Intermediate Post and Alignment Procedure

  1. Stretch alignment lines at the required faces or centers. Reference the line from the two accepted controlling posts.
  2. Install intermediate posts. Position each post on its accepted support along the run.
  3. Maintain post spacing and face alignment. Check each intermediate post against the stretched line before bracing.
  4. Adjust braces to hold plumb. Recheck plumb on two adjacent faces at every intermediate post before final fastening.

H. Top Elevation, Cutting, and Finish Procedure

  1. Transfer top elevations using a laser, level, or stringline. Mark the same reference elevation on every post in the run.
  2. Mark cut lines. Confirm each mark against the transferred reference before cutting.
  3. Support posts during cutting. Prevent binding or uncontrolled fall of the cut section.
  4. Cut post tops square or to the specified shape. Follow the approved detail for the finished top profile.
  5. Treat exposed end grain. Apply preservative to every freshly cut top before it is exposed to weather.
  6. Install caps where specified. Confirm cap fit and fastening match the approved detail.

I. Recheck and Closeout Procedure

  1. Recheck plumb, alignment, spacing, top elevation, base clearance, and connector fasteners. Correct any post that falls outside the approved tolerance.
  2. Keep bracing until beams, rails, walls, or permanent bracing stabilize the posts. Do not remove temporary bracing before a complete load path exists.
  3. Document post and connector installation. Record plumb checks, alignment, connector schedule, and top elevation on the worksheet, and label and retain the completed record for reference throughout the rest of this book.

Common Inspection Review

Inspect the completed post installation using the same checks regardless of structure type.

  • Support check: confirm every post sits on a footing or post base already accepted and cured.
  • Material check: confirm post size, grade, treatment, and exposure rating match the approved specification.
  • Plumb check: confirm every post reads plumb on two adjacent faces after final fastening.
  • Alignment check: confirm every intermediate post is set to the alignment line with correct spacing.
  • Connector check: confirm the specified connector and fastener schedule was installed at every post.
  • Elevation and finish check: confirm top elevation is consistent, end grain is treated, and caps are installed where specified.

Complete the Post Installation Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 6, 9, 10, or 12 framing.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Post Reads Plumb on One Face but Not the Adjacent Face

Probable cause: plumb was checked and corrected on only one face before fastening.
Confirmation: recheck both adjacent faces with a level after loosening the connector.
Correction: loosen the connection, re-brace in both directions, correct plumb on both faces, and refasten.
Reinspection: repeat the plumb check on both faces after refastening.

Intermediate Post Drifts Off the Alignment Line

Probable cause: the post was set by eye rather than checked directly against the stretched alignment line.
Confirmation: measure the offset between the post face and the alignment line at top and bottom.
Correction: loosen bracing, reposition the post against the line, and rebrace before final fastening.
Reinspection: repeat the alignment check on the corrected post and its neighbors.

Post Base Connector Uses Substitute Fasteners

Probable cause: generic screws or nails were used in place of the specified structural fastener because the correct fastener was not on hand.
Confirmation: compare the installed fasteners against the approved connector's fastener schedule.
Correction: remove the substitute fasteners and install the specified structural fasteners in every required hole.
Reinspection: repeat the connector check on the corrected post.

Temporary Bracing Removed Before a Permanent Load Path Exists

Probable cause: bracing was removed to clear the work area before beams, rails, or permanent bracing were installed.
Confirmation: check whether a beam, rail, or permanent brace is fully connected at the post in question.
Correction: re-brace the post in two directions immediately and leave bracing in place until a permanent load path is confirmed.
Reinspection: repeat the plumb and bracing check before removing bracing again.

Post Tops Are Not at a Consistent Elevation

Probable cause: the top elevation was marked at each post independently instead of transferred from a single reference.
Confirmation: recheck each post-top mark against the laser, level, or stringline reference.
Correction: re-transfer the elevation from the original reference and re-cut any post that is out of tolerance.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation check across the full run.

Post Was Loaded Before the Footing Reached the Approved Cured Condition

Probable cause: the post was positioned and braced against schedule pressure before Project 04's cure and inspection requirements were met.
Confirmation: check the footing's inspection and cure record against the date the post was loaded.
Correction: remove the post, confirm the footing has reached the approved condition and passed inspection, then reset the post.
Reinspection: repeat the support confirmation check before resetting the post.

Cleanup

This project ends with every post braced, connected, and documented — not left loose or unrecorded.

  • Leave all temporary bracing in place until beams, rails, walls, or permanent bracing establish a complete load path
  • Photograph the completed, plumb, aligned run of posts for the installation record before concealment by later framing
  • Store unused brace stock, connectors, and fasteners for reuse in later framing chapters
  • Label and file the completed post installation worksheet with the Project 04 footing records
  • Return the documented post installation record to the project records for reference throughout the rest of this book
