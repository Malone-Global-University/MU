Job Overview

This project qualifies you to calculate stair geometry from a measured total rise, lay out and cut approved stringers, install those stringers against an accepted platform and a stable lower landing, hang risers and treads, and inspect the finished stair before it carries foot traffic. The finished work product is one complete exterior stair section: uniform risers and treads, securely connected stringers, and a documented inspection record.

You will measure total rise from the finished deck surface to the finished lower landing, calculate the number of uniform risers the approved design allows, lay out and cut a template stringer, verify it against the approved throat depth and notch limits, use it to produce the remaining stringers, install and connect every stringer, hang treads and risers, and inspect the completed stair for uniformity, attachment, and safe use before it is opened.

This project assumes Project 06 (deck framing) and Project 07 (decking) are complete and inspected, and it builds the stair that Project 09 will fit with guards and a graspable handrail. No stair built under this project is opened to use until barricading is removed under the acceptance standards in this chapter, and no guard or handrail work in Project 09 depends on a stair that has not passed the checks in this chapter first.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • OS-043 — Calculate stair rise and run from a measured total rise and the approved design geometry.
  • OS-044 — Lay out stair stringers using a framing square, stair gauges, and an approved template method.
  • OS-045 — Cut stringers without overcutting inside corners, preserving the required throat depth and notch limits.
  • OS-046 — Install stringers with approved upper and lower connections, spacing, and lateral restraint.
  • OS-047 — Install risers and treads with approved overhang, spacing, support, and fasteners.
  • OS-048 — Inspect stair safety, including uniformity, attachment, landing condition, and slip and drainage control.

Where This Skill Fits

This project depends on the accepted deck frame and decking surface from Project 06 and Project 07, and it depends on the site drainage and grade information recorded back in Project 01. The stair geometry, stringer attachment, and landing condition documented here are the load path and reference that Project 09 uses to install guards, handrail, and stair-transition components. A stair that has not passed the acceptance standards and inspection in this chapter cannot be handed to Project 09 for guard and handrail work.

The discipline built in this chapter — calculating uniform rise and run before any cut is made, verifying a template stringer before committing the remaining stock, and treating a stair as unsafe to use until it is fully inspected — is the same discipline that governs stair and access work throughout the rest of this book, including the Chapter 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • measuring total rise from the finished deck surface to the finished lower landing
  • calculating a uniform number of risers and the corresponding tread run from the approved design
  • laying out, cutting, and verifying a template stringer
  • producing and installing the remaining stringers from the verified template
  • installing approved upper and lower stringer connections, blocking, and lateral restraint
  • installing risers and treads with approved overhang, spacing, support, and fasteners
  • inspecting the completed stair for uniformity, attachment, and safe access before it is opened

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • invent riser height, tread run, stringer notch depth, stair width, or landing dimensions that are not supplied by the approved design, adopted code, or engineering documents
  • cut stringers before the total rise has been measured from the actual finished platform and landing elevations
  • open the stair to regular use before guards, handrails, and the checks required by Project 09 are complete where the approved design requires them
  • proceed to Project 09 with a stair that has not passed the acceptance standards and inspection in this chapter

Any riser, tread, or stringer condition that cannot be confirmed as uniform, or any stringer connection that cannot be verified against the approved detail, must be resolved before this project is declared complete.

Trade Note

Every stair dimension in this chapter comes from a measurement taken at the actual site, not from a number remembered from a previous job or estimated from a plan without confirming the built condition. Total rise changes with the finished elevation of the deck and the finished elevation of the landing, and both must be confirmed on site before any stringer layout begins.

Setup Standard

Do not cut a second stringer until the first stringer has been test-fit against the platform and landing and accepted as the template. A stringer set cut before the template is verified must be re-laid-out and re-cut from the corrected pattern.

Shop Tip

Mark the framing square's stair-gauge settings once and leave the gauges clamped in place until every stringer is cut. Resetting the square between stringers is how a single riser ends up out of uniform with the rest of the run.

Reference Conventions

Establish these references before any stringer is laid out, and do not change them mid-project.

TermDefinition
Total riseThe vertical distance from the finished lower landing to the finished deck surface, measured on site before layout begins
Riser heightThe vertical height of one step, calculated by dividing total rise by the approved number of uniform risers
Tread runThe horizontal depth of one step, set by the approved design and confirmed against nosing and finish thickness
StringerThe cut, notched framing member that supports risers and treads and carries the stair's load to the platform and landing
Throat depthThe remaining solid width of a stringer after the rise-and-run notch is cut; a minimum required to keep the stringer from failing at the notch
LandingThe stable surface at the top or bottom of a stair where a full stride is taken; must be confirmed level, stable, and at the correct elevation before layout
NosingThe projection of a tread beyond the riser face below it, included in tread-run calculations where the approved design specifies it

Uniform Rise and Run

A stair with even one riser out of tolerance from the rest of the run is a fall hazard, because a foot expects the same step height every time. Every riser is calculated from the same measured total rise and the same approved number of risers, and every stringer is laid out from the same square settings, so the finished run is uniform top to bottom.

Code Alert — Stair Geometry and Approved Design

Riser height, tread run, stringer notch limits, stair width, and landing requirements are set by the approved plans and adopted code for the jurisdiction. Do not invent or estimate these values. Confirm the current requirement for the specific stair before cutting any stringer.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Overcut Inside Corners

An overcut at the inside corner of a stringer notch weakens the stringer at its most highly loaded point and can lead to a sudden structural failure under foot traffic. Finish every inside corner with a handsaw or an approved tool, never by extending the circular saw cut past the layout line.

Field Safety Cue — Cutting and Handling Stringer Stock

Support stringer stock fully during cutting, keep hands clear of the cut line, and use eye and hearing protection for every cut. Carry cut stringers with a partner where length or weight makes single-person handling unstable.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Total Rise on Site

Measure total rise from the actual finished deck surface to the actual finished landing surface, not from a plan dimension alone. A landing that settles, or a deck surface that sits slightly different from the plan elevation, changes the total rise and every riser calculated from it.

E. Preparation — Rise Measurement and Geometry Calculation

  1. Review the approved design. Confirm total rise range, riser and tread standard, stair width, stringer design, attachment details, footings, and any guard or handrail requirements this stair must accommodate later.
  2. Confirm the platform and lower landing are stable and at final elevations. Do not begin layout against a landing that has not reached its finished, accepted condition.
  3. Measure total rise from the finished lower landing to the finished deck surface. Take the measurement at the actual stair location, not from a plan dimension alone.
  4. Calculate the approved number of uniform risers. Use the total rise and the approved design's riser-height range to determine the count.
  5. Determine finished riser height and tread run from the approved design. Confirm both values fall within the applicable requirement.
  6. Include tread thickness, decking thickness, nosing, and landing finish in calculations. Adjust the layout dimensions so the first and last riser height match the rest of the run once finish materials are accounted for.

F. Stringer Layout and Template Verification

  1. Prepare a full-scale stringer layout or approved template. Select straight, correctly graded and treated stock for the first stringer.
  2. Mark rise and run using a framing square and stair gauges. Set the gauges once to the calculated riser height and tread run and leave them clamped for the remainder of the layout.
  3. Mark top and bottom cuts. Account for the platform and landing connection details in the top and bottom cut lines.
  4. Verify remaining throat depth and notch limits. Confirm the layout leaves the required solid width at every notch before any cut is made.
  5. Cut the first stringer without overcutting inside corners. Stop the circular saw cut short of the inside corner on every notch.
  6. Finish inside cuts with a handsaw or approved tool. Complete each inside corner precisely to the layout line without extending past it.
  7. Test-fit the first stringer. Set it in position against the platform and landing and confirm rise, run, and bearing match the calculated geometry.
  8. Use the accepted first stringer as a template only after verification. Do not trace or cut additional stringers from a stringer that has not been test-fit and accepted.

G. Remaining Stringers, Connections, and Bracing

  1. Cut remaining stringers. Trace each from the accepted template and repeat the inside-corner finishing method on every notch.
  2. Treat all field cuts in pressure-treated material. Apply the specified preservative to every cut end and notch face before installation.
  3. Install approved upper and lower stringer connections. Use the listed connector and fastener schedule specified for the platform and landing details.
  4. Maintain stringer spacing and lateral restraint. Set every stringer to the approved spacing and install the specified restraint or blocking.
  5. Install required blocking. Add blocking between stringers where the approved detail calls for it, before treads are installed.

H. Risers, Treads, and Coordination with Guards

  1. Install risers where specified. Fasten each riser to the stringer layout line, keeping the top edge consistent across the run.
  2. Install treads with approved overhang, spacing, support, and fasteners. Confirm each tread sits level and fully supported before fastening.
  3. Install or coordinate landings, guards, and handrails. Confirm the stair location and geometry match what Project 09 will need for guard-post and handrail placement, without installing guard or handrail components under this project.
  4. Measure every finished rise and tread. Record each measurement on the worksheet and confirm the run is uniform top to bottom.

I. Recheck, Barricade, and Closeout Procedure

  1. Inspect uniformity, attachment, stringer cuts, tread support, landing, slip, drainage, and movement. Classify any gap as a geometry error, a connection deficiency, or a landing condition before correcting it.
  2. Barricade stairs until all safety components and inspections are complete. Do not open the stair to regular use until this chapter's acceptance standards, and any guard or handrail requirement from Project 09, are satisfied.

Common Inspection Review

Inspect the completed stair using the same checks regardless of stair width, riser count, or landing type.

  • Geometry check: confirm every riser height and tread run is uniform and matches the calculated and approved values.
  • Stringer check: confirm no inside-corner overcuts, throat depth within the approved detail, and every connection installed to the specified schedule.
  • Attachment check: confirm upper and lower stringer connections, spacing, and lateral restraint are installed and secure.
  • Tread and riser check: confirm approved overhang, spacing, support, and fasteners on every tread and riser.
  • Landing and drainage check: confirm the landing is stable and at the correct elevation, and the stair surface sheds water without a slip hazard.

Complete the Stair Inspection Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before opening the stair to use or beginning Project 09.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Riser Heights Are Not Uniform Across the Run

Probable cause: the stair-gauge settings were changed between stringers, or total rise was estimated from a plan dimension rather than measured on site.
Confirmation: measure every finished riser height and compare each to the calculated value.
Correction: re-measure total rise on site, recalculate riser height, and re-lay-out and re-cut the affected stringers using a single verified gauge setting.
Reinspection: repeat the geometry check on the corrected run.

Stringer Notch Was Overcut at an Inside Corner

Probable cause: the circular saw cut was extended past the layout line instead of being finished with a handsaw.
Confirmation: inspect each inside corner for a cut that extends beyond the intended notch line, and check remaining throat depth against the approved minimum.
Correction: reject the overcut stringer, cut a replacement from the accepted template, and finish every inside corner with a handsaw.
Reinspection: repeat the stringer check on the replacement.

Stringer Was Installed Without the Approved Upper or Lower Connection

Probable cause: a stringer was set and treads were started before the specified connector or fastener schedule was completed.
Confirmation: inspect each stringer's upper and lower connection against the approved detail and fastener schedule.
Correction: remove treads as needed to access the connection, install the specified connector and fasteners, and confirm spacing and lateral restraint.
Reinspection: repeat the attachment check on the corrected stringer.

Treads Are Not Level or Fully Supported

Probable cause: a tread was fastened without confirming full bearing on the stringer, or a stringer notch was cut slightly out of alignment with the others.
Confirmation: check each tread with a level and inspect bearing at every stringer contact point.
Correction: remove and reset the affected tread, correct stringer alignment where needed, and refasten with full bearing confirmed.
Reinspection: repeat the tread and riser check on the corrected installation.

Landing Is Unstable or at the Wrong Elevation

Probable cause: the landing was assumed to be at its final condition without confirming it on site before stringer layout began.
Confirmation: check the landing for level, stability, and elevation against the approved design.
Correction: correct the landing condition before continuing, and recheck total rise and riser height once the landing is accepted.
Reinspection: repeat the landing and drainage check, and recheck geometry if the landing elevation changed.

Stair Was Opened to Use Before Inspection Was Complete

Probable cause: barricades were removed once the stair appeared finished, without completing the full acceptance-standards review.
Confirmation: review the stair inspection worksheet for any unchecked or incomplete item.
Correction: re-barricade the stair, complete every remaining check, and correct any deficiency found.
Reinspection: repeat the full inspection before removing barricades again.

Cleanup

This project ends with the stair barricaded and its records compiled, not with the stair opened for casual use.

  • Leave barricades in place until guard, handrail, and any remaining Project 09 requirements are complete
  • Photograph the completed stair, connections, and landing for the inspection record before covering or enclosing any component
  • Store leftover stringer, riser, and tread stock and label it for reuse or reference elsewhere in the book
  • Label and file the completed stair layout worksheet, geometry calculations, and photographs together
  • Return the signed stair inspection record to the project records for reference throughout the rest of this book
