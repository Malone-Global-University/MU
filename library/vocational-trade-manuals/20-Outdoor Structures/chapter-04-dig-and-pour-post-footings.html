Job Overview

This project qualifies you to excavate, prepare, form where required, embed hardware, place, finish, cure, and document one set of post footings from an approved footing schedule. The finished work product is a set of accepted post footings: holes excavated to accepted bearing at the approved diameter and depth, gravel base placed where specified, forms and reinforcement or embedded hardware installed to the approved detail, concrete placed and finished to the specified elevation, and complete field records of measurements, concrete, weather, and inspections.

You will confirm footing centers and utility markings, excavate each hole, inspect the hole for suitable bearing, place gravel and forms where the approved detail requires them, set anchors or post bases, complete any required pre-pour inspection, place and finish the concrete, protect it through cure, and verify the finished footing before any post, load, or subsequent framing depends on it.

This project depends entirely on Project 03. Footing centers, elevations, and dimensions used here must come from the approved and documented layout — never re-measured from memory or approximated in the field. No post is set, no beam is installed, and no load is placed on a footing until it has reached the approved cured condition documented in this project.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • OS-019 — Dig footing holes to the approved location, diameter, and depth using hand tools, an auger, or approved equipment.
  • OS-020 — Verify depth, diameter, bottom elevation, and bearing condition against the approved footing schedule.
  • OS-021 — Place a compacted gravel base only where the approved footing detail specifies one.
  • OS-022 — Set forms, reinforcement, anchor bolts, and post bases according to the approved detail where specified.
  • OS-023 — Mix or receive, place, consolidate, and finish concrete footings to the approved specification and elevation.
  • OS-024 — Inspect footing placement, protect and cure the concrete, and document the completed footing.

Where This Skill Fits

This project depends on the layout completed in Project 03 and controls everything that follows it. Project 05 cannot set a post until the footing beneath it has reached the approved cured condition documented here. Every beam in Project 06, every guard post in Project 09, every fence post in Project 10, and the Chapter 20 capstone all rely on footings that were excavated to accepted bearing and finished to the correct elevation and anchor position.

An incorrect footing cannot be corrected by careful framing later. A footing poured off center, at the wrong depth, on unsuitable bearing, or with a misplaced anchor forces rework in concrete — the most expensive and time-consuming correction in the entire sequence. That is why this project treats every measurement, inspection, and cure step as a hold point rather than a formality.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • excavating footing holes at the approved centers, diameters, and depths from Project 03's documented layout
  • inspecting each hole for suitable bearing and removing unsuitable material to the accepted condition
  • placing gravel base only where the approved footing detail requires it
  • setting tube forms, pier forms, reinforcement, anchor bolts, post bases, or sleeves according to the approved detail
  • completing any required pre-pour or footing inspection before concrete is placed
  • mixing or receiving, placing, consolidating, and finishing concrete to the approved specification and elevation
  • protecting and curing the concrete, and inspecting and documenting the completed footing

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • invent footing diameter, depth, reinforcement, anchor spacing, or concrete specification not supplied by approved plans, adopted code, or engineering documents
  • excavate before utility locating and Project 03 layout approval are confirmed
  • place concrete on organic soil, loose fill, standing water, frozen soil, or disturbed material that has not been accepted
  • load, strip forms from, or set a post on a footing before it reaches the approved cured condition

Any hole with unresolved groundwater, caving soil, buried obstructions, or unsuitable bearing must be stopped and resolved — with engineering input where required — before concrete is placed.

Trade Note

Do not assume that burying a wood post directly in concrete is an acceptable substitute for an approved footing and post-support detail. Use the exact footing, form, anchor, and post-base detail supplied by the approved plans or manufacturer instructions for every footing in this project.

Setup Standard

Do not excavate any hole until Project 03's layout has been approved and every utility marking within the footing area is confirmed and visible. A hole excavated from an unapproved or unconfirmed layout must be backfilled and re-excavated from confirmed centers.

Shop Tip

Dig, inspect, and form one hole completely before starting the next, and record its measurements immediately. Working hole by hole in sequence — rather than digging every hole first — makes it far easier to trace a bearing problem or measurement error back to the exact footing that produced it.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every footing in this book, from a single garden-trellis anchor to the Chapter 20 capstone, follows the same sequence: confirm layout and utilities, excavate to accepted bearing, prepare and form, embed hardware, inspect before pouring, place and finish concrete, then protect and cure. Skipping or reordering any of these steps is the most common source of footing failures that surface only after a post, beam, or deck has already been built on top of them.

Bearing, Frost, and Soil Conditions

Every hole is inspected at the bottom and sides before anything is placed in it. Loose soil, organic material, standing water, roots, or disturbed fill at the bottom of a hole cannot be built on and must be removed to firm, undisturbed material before the footing proceeds.

Critical Safety Cue — Excavation and Buried Utilities

Confirm the utility locate is current and visible before opening any hole. Do not dig, auger, or probe outside a confirmed and marked area. Contact with a buried utility line during excavation can cause serious injury, service outages, or death. Keep workers clear of rotating augers and suspended spoil at all times.

Code Alert — Footing Schedule and Frost Depth

Footing diameter, depth, reinforcement, and frost-depth requirements are set by the approved plans, adopted code, and local jurisdiction. Do not invent or estimate these values. Confirm the current requirement for the specific site before excavating, and obtain any required footing or pre-pour inspection before concrete is placed.

Field Safety Cue — Open Holes and Spoil

Mark, barricade, or cover open holes whenever they are left unattended. Keep spoil piles clear of walkways and away from hole edges to prevent collapse or refill of an inspected hole. Watch footing on uneven ground around excavation and staged materials.

Measurement Cue — Depth, Diameter, and Center Verification

Measure and record depth, diameter, bottom elevation, and center location for every hole before placing gravel, forms, or hardware. Re-verify the center location against the Project 03 layout after excavation, since digging can shift loose offsets or disturb reference stakes near the hole.

E. Preparation — Schedule Review and Excavation Planning

  1. Review the footing schedule for diameter, depth, bell or pier shape, reinforcement, anchor type, elevation, frost depth, soil-bearing assumptions, and inspection requirements. Do not proceed with any value not supplied by the approved plans or engineering documents.
  2. Confirm utility markings and Project 03's approved footing centers are current and visible. Re-flag any marking that has faded or been disturbed.
  3. Establish an excavation exclusion zone and a separate spoil zone for each hole. Keep both clear of walkways and staged materials.

F. Excavation and Bearing Verification Procedure

  1. Excavate each hole using hand tools, an approved auger, or approved equipment. Hand-expose any utility within or adjacent to the excavation where required.
  2. Keep workers clear of rotating augers and suspended spoil at all times. Never reach into an active excavation zone.
  3. Inspect the hole sides and bottom for loose soil, organic material, water, roots, fill, or signs of collapse. Remove any unsuitable material down to firm, undisturbed bearing.
  4. Stop work and resolve the condition before proceeding if groundwater, caving soil, buried obstructions, unsuitable bearing, or any changed condition is found. Obtain engineering input where the approved plans require it.
  5. Measure and record depth, diameter, bottom elevation, and center location for the completed hole. Compare each measurement against the approved footing schedule before proceeding.

G. Forming, Reinforcement, and Embedded Hardware

  1. Place compacted gravel only where the approved footing detail specifies a gravel base. Do not add gravel where the detail does not call for it.
  2. Set tube forms, pier forms, or above-grade forms to the specified line, elevation, and plumb. Brace forms to hold position through concrete placement.
  3. Install reinforcement, anchor bolts, post bases, sleeves, or other embedded hardware exactly as the approved detail specifies. Maintain the required concrete cover over reinforcement.
  4. Recheck anchor or post-base position, elevation, and orientation before the pre-pour inspection. Correct any misalignment before concrete arrives.

H. Pre-Pour Inspection and Concrete Placement

  1. Complete the required footing or pre-pour inspection before any concrete is placed. Do not proceed on a hole that has not passed this inspection where one is required.
  2. Calculate and stage the concrete quantity needed for the approved footing dimensions. Confirm the mix matches the approved specification.
  3. Mix or receive concrete within the approved specification. Reject any load or batch that does not meet the specified mix or condition.
  4. Place concrete without segregating the mix, and consolidate it around reinforcement and embedded hardware to eliminate voids. Work methodically from the first hole placed to the last.
  5. Finish the top of each footing to the specified elevation and drainage shape. Recheck anchor or post-base position immediately after placement, before the concrete begins to set.

I. Protection, Cure, and Closeout

  1. Protect and cure each footing according to the approved specification and weather conditions. Do not load the footing or strip forms before the approved condition is reached.
  2. Inspect the final center, elevation, anchor position, concrete condition, and curing progress for every footing. Recheck every incomplete or unclear item and classify each gap before closing it out.
  3. Document measurements, concrete quantities, weather conditions, and inspection results for every footing, and label and retain the completed record. Store it for reference throughout the rest of this book.

Common Inspection Review

Inspect every completed footing using the same checks regardless of footing count or structure type.

  • Location check: confirm each footing's center, diameter, and depth match the approved footing schedule and Project 03 layout.
  • Bearing check: confirm the hole bottom and sides were firm and undisturbed before anything was placed.
  • Hardware check: confirm gravel, forms, reinforcement, and anchor or post-base hardware match the approved detail and finished position.
  • Inspection check: confirm the required pre-pour and final inspections were completed and passed for every footing.
  • Cure check: confirm every footing reached the approved cured condition before any load, form-stripping, or post-setting occurred.

Complete the Footing Inspection Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 05.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Hole Excavated at the Wrong Center, Depth, or Diameter

Probable cause: the hole was excavated from an approximated location or reference rather than the confirmed offsets and elevations documented in Project 03.
Confirmation: re-measure the hole's center, depth, and diameter against the approved footing schedule and protected offsets.
Correction: backfill the hole with suitable material if it must be abandoned, or re-excavate to the correct location, depth, and diameter from the confirmed reference.
Reinspection: repeat the location check on the corrected hole before proceeding to forming.

Concrete Placed Before Bearing Was Confirmed Suitable

Probable cause: loose soil, organic material, or standing water at the hole bottom was not removed before concrete was placed.
Confirmation: review the bearing inspection record and photographs for the footing in question.
Correction: where the concrete has already been placed on unsuitable bearing, stop work and obtain engineering input on removal and replacement; do not conceal the condition.
Reinspection: repeat the bearing check on the corrected footing before it is accepted.

Anchor Bolt or Post Base Shifted During Concrete Placement

Probable cause: the anchor or post base was not braced or rechecked immediately after concrete placement, allowing it to move before the concrete began to set.
Confirmation: measure the finished anchor or post-base position against the approved detail and the intended post centerline.
Correction: if the concrete has not yet set, reposition the hardware and recheck; if it has set out of tolerance, consult the approved detail or engineering input for an accepted correction method.
Reinspection: repeat the hardware check on the corrected footing.

Footing Loaded Before Reaching the Approved Cured Condition

Probable cause: a post was set, forms were stripped, or equipment was staged on a footing before the specified cure time or condition was reached.
Confirmation: compare the date and condition of the footing against the approved cure specification.
Correction: remove the load immediately and allow the footing to complete the approved cure period before reloading.
Reinspection: repeat the cure check before any post or load is reapplied.

Pre-Pour Inspection Was Skipped or Undocumented

Probable cause: concrete was placed before the required inspection was requested or completed.
Confirmation: review the inspection record for the footing in question against the jurisdiction's required inspection sequence.
Correction: where concrete has already been placed without the required inspection, contact the authority having jurisdiction for guidance before proceeding further.
Reinspection: repeat the inspection check on the corrected sequence for all remaining footings.

Footing Records Are Missing Measurements or Photographs

Probable cause: field measurements or photographs were deferred until after backfilling or form removal, when the condition could no longer be verified.
Confirmation: review the completed footing records against the Assessed Footing Schedule for missing components.
Correction: record any condition still visible, or note on the worksheet any item that must be reconfirmed before Project 05 begins.
Reinspection: repeat the relevant check on the completed record.

Cleanup

This project ends with cured, protected footings and complete records, not with tools left on site.

  • Remove excess spoil, unused gravel, and form scraps from the work area without disturbing curing footings
  • Rinse and store concrete tools before residue hardens
  • Direct concrete washout only to an approved washout area, never to soil, drains, or waterways
  • Leave protective coverings on curing footings for the full specified cure period
  • Label and file the completed footing worksheet, measurements, and photographs together for reference throughout the rest of this book

Field Terms

Footing Schedule
The approved document specifying footing diameter, depth, reinforcement, anchor type, and elevation.
Accepted Bearing
Firm, undisturbed soil or approved fill capable of supporting the design load.
Frost Depth
The minimum footing depth required to place bearing below the frost line.
Post Base
Approved hardware that ties a post to a footing without embedding wood directly in concrete or soil.
Consolidation
Working concrete around reinforcement and embeds to eliminate voids without segregating the mix.
Cure
The time and protection required for concrete to reach the specified strength and condition before loading.
Pre-Pour Inspection
A required inspection of the excavated, formed, and hardware-equipped hole before concrete placement.
Segregation
Separation of concrete components during placement that weakens the finished footing.
Anchor Bolt
A bolt embedded in concrete that connects a post base or sill to the footing.
Footing Inspection Record
The document recording every footing's measurements, bearing condition, hardware position, and inspection results.
