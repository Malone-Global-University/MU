Job Overview

This project qualifies you to close the exterior envelope of a small detached shed built in Projects 14 through 16. The finished work product is a weather-managed shed exterior: structural sheathing completed where it was not already present, a water-resistive barrier integrated with roof and opening flashing where the siding system requires a separate layer, siding installed with correct clearances and fasteners, corner and trim boards installed and flashed, and a framed, hung, and hardware-complete shed door.

You will review the approved wall assembly and product instructions, complete any remaining sheathing, install the WRB and flashing sequence, install siding from a established reference line, install corner and opening trim, build and fit the door frame, hang the door, install latch and security hardware, and inspect the finished exterior for drainage, ventilation, and weather resistance.

This project depends on the accepted, inspected wall and roof framing from Projects 15 and 16. No siding, trim, or door work begins until those framing inspections are complete. This project's accepted exterior envelope is, in turn, the surface every later maintenance decision in Project 19 and the Chapter 20 capstone will reference.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • OS-097 — Install wall sheathing or siding according to the approved wall assembly.
  • OS-098 — Install corner trim, base trim, rake trim, fascia, and opening trim to the approved reference.
  • OS-099 — Install the door frame to the approved rough opening and operating clearances.
  • OS-100 — Hang the shed door on exterior-rated hinges with adequate structural backing.
  • OS-101 — Install latch, handle, stop, and security hardware appropriate to the design.
  • OS-102 — Inspect the completed exterior finish for weather resistance, clearance, and function.

Where This Skill Fits

This project closes the shed envelope begun in Project 14's floor, Project 15's walls, and Project 16's roof. Every wall clearance, ventilation opening, and drainage path established in those chapters must be preserved here rather than covered over or blocked by siding, trim, or the door assembly.

The completed exterior from this project becomes a reference point for Project 19's maintenance inspections and for the Chapter 20 capstone if the shed option is used there. A siding or trim detail that traps water or blocks ventilation here becomes a decay problem those later chapters must diagnose and correct.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • completing structural wall sheathing where it was not already installed during framing
  • installing a water-resistive barrier and integrating it with roof, base, opening, and penetration flashing where the siding system requires a separate WRB layer
  • installing siding panels, boards, or sheets with correct clearances and compatible fasteners
  • installing corner trim, base trim, rake trim, fascia, and trim around the door opening
  • building or installing the approved shed door frame and fitting the door to the opening
  • installing hinges, latch, handle, stop, hold-open, threshold, and security hardware
  • inspecting the finished exterior for siding plane, fastening, clearances, flashing, trim, door operation, and weather resistance

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin siding, trim, or door work before the Project 15 wall-framing inspection and the Project 16 roof-framing and sheathing inspection are complete
  • install siding, trim, or a door assembly that blocks required clearances, drainage paths, or ventilation openings established in earlier chapters
  • substitute an unapproved WRB, flashing sequence, siding fastener, or door hardware for the one specified in the approved wall assembly or manufacturer instructions
  • declare the exterior complete with an unresolved flashing gap, missing WRB integration, or door that does not operate smoothly and latch securely

Any siding, trim, or door detail that cannot be confirmed against the approved wall assembly and manufacturer instructions must be resolved before this project is declared complete.

Trade Note

Siding, trim, and a door are the last line of defense keeping water away from the framing this book has built since Project 14. A siding detail installed for appearance alone, without regard to the drainage plane behind it, converts a sound structure into a hidden decay problem that will not surface until Project 19's maintenance inspection finds it — often after real damage has already occurred.

Setup Standard

Do not install siding over an unintegrated WRB or an unflashed opening. Every flashing lap must shed water onto the layer below it, from the top down, before the covering siding course goes on. A siding course installed over an unresolved flashing gap must be removed and corrected.

Shop Tip

Dry-fit the door frame in the rough opening and check operating clearance on all four sides before fastening anything permanently. A door frame fastened before clearance is confirmed is far harder to correct than one still held with temporary shims.

Reference Conventions

Establish these references before any siding or trim begins, and do not change them mid-project.

TermDefinition
Water-resistive barrier (WRB)A separate weather-resistant layer installed over sheathing where the siding system requires one, integrated with flashing to drain water outward and downward
FlashingMaterial installed at openings, laps, and penetrations to direct water away from the wall assembly and prevent it from reaching the sheathing or framing
Drainage planeThe continuous path water takes down and out of the wall assembly, formed by the WRB, flashing, and siding lap sequence working together
Rough openingThe framed opening sized to receive the door frame with its specified operating clearance on all sides
RevealThe consistent exposed face dimension of a trim board or siding course, established from a reference line and held constant around the structure
Ground clearanceThe minimum required distance between the bottom edge of siding or trim and grade, deck, slab, or roof surfaces below

Common Field Fundamentals

Every exterior-envelope project in this book depends on the same sequence: confirm the assembly, integrate water control from the top down, then finish with trim and functioning hardware. Skipping ahead to siding before the WRB and flashing sequence is resolved is the most common source of hidden decay this project is designed to prevent.

Water-Resistive Barrier and Flashing Sequence

Every flashing detail is installed so that each layer sheds water onto the layer below it, working from the top of the wall down to the base. A flashing lap installed backward, or a WRB course installed under rather than over the flashing above it, can direct water into the wall rather than away from it.

Manufacturer Cue — Siding and WRB Compatibility

Follow the siding and WRB manufacturer's fastening schedule, lap dimensions, and flashing-tape compatibility exactly. Some WRB products are not compatible with certain flashing tapes or sealants; using an incompatible product can fail the drainage plane even when every board looks correctly installed.

Critical Safety Cue — Elevated Work on Ladders and Platforms

Use an approved, stable ladder or platform for upper siding courses, fascia, and rake trim. Do not overreach from a ladder, and do not carry full siding or trim boards up a ladder one-handed. Maintain three points of contact and have materials staged within easy reach.

Code Alert — Door Egress and Security Hardware

Confirm local requirements for shed door hardware, including any restrictions on hasp, padlock, or interior-release provisions where the shed could be entered and occupied. Requirements vary by jurisdiction; verify before finalizing the hardware schedule.

Measurement Cue — Maintain Ground and Roof Clearances

Hold the specified minimum clearance between the bottom edge of siding and trim and the grade, slab, deck, or roof surfaces below. Siding installed tight to grade or a roof surface wicks moisture upward and accelerates decay regardless of the treatment rating of the material.

E. Preparation — Assembly Review and Sheathing Completion

  1. Review the wall assembly, structural sheathing, WRB, siding type, flashing, clearances, trim, door, threshold, hardware, ventilation, and finish. Confirm every product against its manufacturer instructions before ordering fasteners.
  2. Confirm wall and roof framing inspections are complete. Do not begin sheathing, WRB, or siding work on framing that has not passed inspection.
  3. Install structural sheathing where not already present. Complete any panels deferred from Project 15 or 16.
  4. Maintain panel orientation, gaps, fastening, and edge support. Confirm every edge lands on framing before fastening.

F. Water-Resistive Barrier and Flashing Installation

  1. Install WRB where the siding system requires a separate layer. Work from the bottom of the wall upward, lapping each course over the one below.
  2. Integrate WRB with roof, base, opening, and penetration flashing. Confirm every flashing piece sheds water onto the layer beneath it.
  3. Maintain drainage and ventilation at wall bases. Do not seal or block the base drainage path established in Project 14 and 15.

G. Siding Installation

  1. Install siding panels, boards, or sheets from the approved reference. Establish a level starting line and work from it consistently around the structure.
  2. Maintain ground, roof, slab, deck, and hardscape clearances. Confirm the minimum clearance at every point before fastening the lowest course.
  3. Use compatible exterior fasteners. Match fastener material to siding and framing treatment to prevent corrosion staining or galvanic reaction.
  4. Do not overdrive fasteners or restrict siding movement. Follow the manufacturer's fastening pattern and depth exactly.

H. Trim, Door Frame, and Hanging

  1. Install corner trim, base trim, rake trim, fascia, and opening trim. Hold a consistent reveal from the established reference line.
  2. Flash horizontal trim and openings where specified. Confirm no trim piece traps water against the siding or sheathing behind it.
  3. Build or install the approved shed door frame. Confirm frame dimensions match the rough opening and approved clearances.
  4. Fit the door to the opening with required clearances. Dry-fit and check swing clearance on all sides before final fastening.
  5. Install exterior-rated hinges and structural fasteners. Confirm adequate backing at every hinge location; do not fasten hinges into trim alone.
  6. Hang and support the door. Use temporary support until hinges and fasteners are fully set.

I. Hardware, Finishing, and Closeout Inspection

  1. Install latch, handle, stop, hold-open, threshold, and security hardware. Confirm engagement and holding force before release.
  2. Prevent pinch, sharp-edge, and wind-slam hazards. Install a stop or closer where wind exposure could slam the door.
  3. Operate the door repeatedly. Confirm smooth swing, secure latch engagement, and no binding at any point in the cycle.
  4. Seal field cuts and finish exposed wood. Treat every cut end of treated lumber and finish exposed trim as specified.
  5. Inspect siding plane, fasteners, clearances, flashing, trim, door swing, latch, weather resistance, and ventilation. Record findings on the exterior-finish worksheet.
  6. Document products and exterior-envelope handoff. Compile product labels, fastener records, and photographs of the completed exterior for the project file.

Common Inspection Review

Inspect the completed shed exterior using the same checks regardless of which siding or door product was selected.

  • Sheathing and WRB check: confirm sheathing is complete and any WRB and flashing lap sequence runs top-down with no reverse laps.
  • Siding check: confirm siding is plane-true, correctly fastened, and holds required clearances at grade, roof, and hardscape surfaces.
  • Trim check: confirm corner, base, rake, fascia, and opening trim are installed to the approved reveal and flashed where specified.
  • Door check: confirm the frame fits the approved rough opening, the door is hung on structural backing, and it operates smoothly.
  • Hardware check: confirm latch, handle, stop, threshold, and any security hardware are installed and functioning without hazard.

Complete the Exterior Finish Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before this project is declared complete.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Flashing Lap Installed in the Wrong Direction

Probable cause: a flashing piece or WRB course was installed so that an upper layer tucks under a lower one instead of over it, directing water inward.
Confirmation: inspect the lap direction at the suspect location before covering it with siding.
Correction: remove the affected siding or trim, reverse the lap so each layer sheds onto the one below it, and reinstall.
Reinspection: repeat the WRB and flashing check on the corrected section.

Siding Installed Without Required Ground Clearance

Probable cause: the first siding course was fastened without confirming clearance to grade, a slab, or an adjacent hardscape surface.
Confirmation: measure the actual clearance at multiple points against the approved minimum.
Correction: remove and reinstall the affected course at the correct height, or adjust the base detail to restore clearance.
Reinspection: repeat the siding check on the corrected course.

Door Frame Installed Without Confirming Operating Clearance

Probable cause: the frame was fastened permanently before a dry-fit confirmed swing clearance on all sides.
Confirmation: check the door for binding, uneven gaps, or contact with the frame or threshold during a full swing cycle.
Correction: loosen or remove the frame, re-shim to the approved clearance, and refasten.
Reinspection: repeat the door check through a full operating cycle.

Hinges Fastened Without Adequate Structural Backing

Probable cause: hinge screws were driven into trim or thin frame stock rather than into solid backing behind the frame.
Confirmation: check for movement or fastener pull-out at the hinge location under normal door operation.
Correction: add blocking or backing behind the frame at each hinge location and refasten with the specified structural fasteners.
Reinspection: repeat the door check after the door has been cycled repeatedly.

Trim Trapping Water Against Siding

Probable cause: a horizontal trim piece was installed without the specified flashing or drip detail, allowing water to sit against the siding below it.
Confirmation: inspect the trim-to-siding transition after a rain event or with a water test for standing moisture or staining.
Correction: remove the trim, install the specified flashing or drip edge, and reinstall.
Reinspection: repeat the trim check on the corrected detail.

Latch or Security Hardware Fails to Engage Reliably

Probable cause: the strike plate or latch was installed without accounting for door settling, frame movement, or seasonal swelling.
Confirmation: cycle the door repeatedly and check engagement consistency across multiple attempts.
Correction: adjust the strike plate position, latch depth, or hinge alignment until engagement is consistent.
Reinspection: repeat the hardware check after adjustment.

Field Terms

Water-Resistive Barrier (WRB)
A separate weather-resistant layer installed over sheathing where the siding system requires one.
Flashing
Material installed at openings, laps, and penetrations to direct water away from the wall assembly.
Drainage Plane
The continuous path water takes down and out of the wall assembly.
Rough Opening
The framed opening sized to receive the door frame with its specified operating clearance.
Reveal
The consistent exposed face dimension of a trim board or siding course.
Ground Clearance
The minimum required distance between siding or trim and the surfaces below it.
Strike Plate
The hardware component that receives and holds the latch in the closed position.
Threshold
The sill component at the base of the door opening that manages water and air infiltration.
Structural Backing
Solid framing behind trim or a door frame sufficient to carry hinge and hardware loads.
Exterior Envelope
The complete assembly of sheathing, WRB, siding, trim, and openings that manages water and air at the building exterior.
