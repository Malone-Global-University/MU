Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare one residential structure for the insulation, air-sealing, lath, drywall, fire-resistance, and energy inspections that occur after rough framing, plumbing, electrical, mechanical, and gas approvals but before interior finishes conceal the work. The finished work product is one complete interior concealment-readiness package: verified insulation and air-barrier installation, verified fire blocking and rated assemblies, verified drywall or lath readiness, product evidence, photographs, a corrected deficiency list, and a documented inspection result that authorizes drywall to proceed.

You will confirm every prerequisite approval, verify insulation and air-sealing installation against approved documents, verify fire blocking and rated assemblies remain visible, verify drywall or lath readiness, collect product evidence and photographs, complete a room-by-room preinspection, correct deficiencies, schedule and attend the required inspections, and control drywall delivery and installation so approved assemblies are not changed afterward.

This project depends directly on the framing approval from Project 07, the roof and envelope approval from Project 08, the plumbing rough-in from Project 09, the electrical rough-in from Project 10, the HVAC rough-in from Project 11, the energy-code matrix from Project 12, and the life-safety checklist from Project 13. It feeds directly into the final building inspection in Project 16 and the correction-tracking discipline in Project 17. Nothing in this chapter authorizes the learner to approve a concealed assembly in place of the inspector or design professional.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CP-085 — Verify insulation coverage, type, value, and installation against approved documents.
  • CP-086 — Inspect vapor-control and air-barrier details for continuity and approved installation.
  • CP-087 — Verify fire-blocking, draft-stopping, and rated-assembly conditions remain visible for inspection.
  • CP-088 — Inspect drywall or lath readiness, including backing, support, and fastener schedule.
  • CP-089 — Review interior safety items, including clearances, ventilation terminations, and accessible components.
  • CP-090 — Complete an interior inspection checklist, correct deficiencies, and control the drywall release.

Where This Skill Fits

Every prior inspection project in this book — foundation, framing, envelope, plumbing, electrical, and HVAC — protects one condition: that regulated work remains visible until it is verified. This project is the last point at which insulation, air sealing, fire blocking, and rated assemblies can be checked before drywall makes them permanently inaccessible. The energy matrix built in Project 12 and the life-safety checklist built in Project 13 both converge here, at the pre-drywall walk, because insulation coverage and fire-blocking continuity cannot be confirmed after the wall is closed.

The discipline in this chapter — confirm prerequisite approvals, verify against approved documents rather than assumption, photograph what will be concealed, and control the release of the next trade — is the same discipline the Chapter 20 capstone applies across the entire authority-to-closeout sequence.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming framing, rough plumbing, rough electrical, rough HVAC, gas, fire, and other prerequisite approvals before proceeding
  • verifying insulation type, location, value, thickness, density, facing, and installation against approved documents
  • verifying air-barrier continuity, vapor-control layers, and required clearances from heat-producing components
  • verifying fire blocking, draft stopping, and rated-assembly framing remain visible at the required stage
  • verifying drywall type, thickness, layer count, fastener schedule, and backing against approved documents
  • collecting product evidence, photographs, and a room-by-room deficiency list
  • scheduling and attending the required inspections and controlling drywall release after approval

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • add or omit a vapor-control layer, insulation value, or fire-rated assembly based on a generic assumption instead of the approved design
  • install drywall before every required concealed-work approval is documented
  • conceal a rejected or uninspected area, or represent an educational checklist as an inspection approval
  • perform blower-door, duct, or other required energy tests outside the qualified party identified in Project 12

Any condition that cannot be verified against approved documents must be labeled unresolved and referred to the responsible design professional, energy rater, or authority having jurisdiction before concealment.

Trade Note

This is a concealment hold point. Every item verified in this chapter — insulation, air sealing, fire blocking, and rated assemblies — becomes unverifiable the moment drywall is installed. Treat any incomplete verification as a reason to delay drywall delivery, not a reason to proceed and document later.

Setup Standard

Do not begin the insulation and air-sealing verification until framing, rough plumbing, rough electrical, rough HVAC, gas, and fire prerequisite inspections are approved. Verifying insulation over unapproved rough work does not correct a missing prerequisite approval.

Shop Tip

Photograph every cavity before it is insulated and again after insulation is installed. A single before-and-after photo pair, keyed to the room and wall line, resolves more inspector questions than a written description alone.

Documentation Cue — Photograph Before Concealment, By Location

Photograph each concealed wall, ceiling, roof, rim, penetration, and rated detail by room and wall line before drywall covers it. A photograph without a recorded location cannot support a later correction or a compliance-file entry.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Conceal Before Required Approval

Do not install drywall before all required concealed-work approvals are documented, and do not conceal a rejected or uninspected area. If a required inspection has not yet occurred or has not yet been approved, stop drywall delivery and installation until the approval is recorded.

Field Safety Cue — Access for the Learner and the Inspector

Provide safe stairs, ladders, platforms, and lighting to every cavity and inspection area before beginning the room-by-room walk, and remove stored material, scrap, protruding hazards, and obstructions that would block access for either the learner or the inspector.

Professional Boundary Cue — Reserved Determinations

Do not add or omit a vapor-control layer, insulation value, or rated-assembly detail based on a generic assumption. Vapor-control and rated-assembly requirements are set by the approved design documents and listed systems; where they are unclear or appear to conflict, refer the condition to the responsible design professional or authority having jurisdiction rather than selecting the least costly interpretation.

E. Confirm Prerequisites and Approved Documents

  1. Identify the required insulation, air barrier, lath, drywall, fire-resistance, energy, or interior inspection stages. Record every stage applicable to this project on the interior inspection checklist before beginning the walk.
  2. Confirm framing, structural, rough plumbing, rough electrical, rough HVAC, gas, fire, and other prerequisite approvals. Verify each approval date against the permit card and prior inspection records before proceeding.
  3. Confirm approved architectural, structural, energy, wall-type, rated-assembly, penetration, and manufacturer documents are onsite. Confirm the plan revision in hand matches the currently approved revision.

F. Verify Rough Work, Insulation, and Air-Barrier Installation

  1. Verify framing and cavities are dry, clean, complete, and free of unresolved leaks or contamination. Do not proceed with insulation verification in a cavity showing active moisture.
  2. Verify fire blocking, draft stopping, backing, rated construction, and concealed structural connectors remain visible. Compare each condition against the approved structural and life-safety notes.
  3. Verify all rough utilities, supports, nail plates, boxes, ducts, piping, wiring, and penetrations are complete and approved. Confirm each rough-in trade's approval is recorded before insulating over it.
  4. Verify insulation type, location, value, thickness, density, facing, and installation requirement by assembly. Check each assembly against the Project 12 energy-code compliance matrix.
  5. Verify insulation fills intended cavities without gaps, voids, compression, misalignment, or blocked ventilation. Inspect every cavity scheduled for insulation, not a representative sample only.
  6. Verify baffles, wind dams, attic dams, access insulation, eave ventilation, and clearance from heat-producing components. Confirm required clearances match the approved documents and manufacturer instructions.
  7. Verify air-barrier continuity at top and bottom plates, rims, openings, penetrations, chases, attic transitions, and service entries. Trace the air barrier around the entire enclosure rather than checking isolated points.
  8. Verify sealants, foams, membranes, tapes, and gaskets are approved and installed within product conditions. Confirm the products used match the manufacturer instructions collected for the compliance file.
  9. Verify vapor-control layers match approved assemblies and are not added or omitted based on generic assumptions. Refer any unclear vapor-control condition to the design professional rather than deciding independently.

G. Verify Clearances, Ventilation, Rated Assemblies, and Drywall Readiness

  1. Verify ducts, pipes, equipment, recessed fixtures, fireplaces, chimneys, and other components maintain required clearances. Measure clearances directly rather than estimating by eye.
  2. Verify bath, kitchen, laundry, dryer, and whole-house ventilation rough-ins are complete and terminate as approved. Confirm each termination matches the approved mechanical documents from Project 11.
  3. Verify rated wall, ceiling, floor, garage, shaft, and penetration assemblies use approved materials and details. Compare installed materials against the listed-system documentation collected for the compliance file.
  4. Verify firestop systems, joint systems, putty pads, collars, sleeves, backing, and installation conditions remain visible where inspection requires. Do not accept a firestop condition that cannot be visually confirmed against its listing.
  5. Verify drywall type, thickness, layer count, orientation, support, fastener schedule, joints, control joints, and moisture classification from approved documents. Confirm the fastener schedule matches the approved specification for each assembly.
  6. Do not install drywall before all required concealed-work approvals. Confirm this condition explicitly before releasing drywall for delivery.
  7. Verify backing and support for cabinets, fixtures, rails, guards, accessories, and heavy finishes. Confirm backing locations match the approved documents and identified fixture layout.
  8. Verify exterior openings, flashings, roof, plumbing, and envelope are sufficiently weather-tight before interior concealment. Coordinate this check with the Project 08 envelope-inspection record.

H. Collect Evidence, Provide Access, and Complete the Preinspection

  1. Collect insulation labels, product certificates, energy documents, listed assemblies, test reports, and photographs. File each item in the compliance record with its assembly and location reference.
  2. Photograph each concealed wall, ceiling, roof, rim, penetration, and rated detail by location. Label every photograph with room, wall line, and date before continuing.
  3. Provide safe stairs, ladders, platforms, lighting, and access to all cavities and inspection areas. Confirm access is safe for both the learner and the scheduled inspector.
  4. Remove stored material, scrap, protruding hazards, and obstructions. Clear every inspection area before scheduling.
  5. Complete a room-by-room insulation, fire, energy, and drywall-readiness inspection. Use the interior inspection checklist to record every room and assembly checked.
  6. Create and correct a deficiency list before scheduling. Assign each deficiency to a responsible trade and verify correction before proceeding.

I. Test, Schedule, Attend, and Control the Drywall Release

  1. Confirm required blower-door, duct, ventilation, or other tests occur at the specified stage. Confirm the qualified testing party identified in Project 12 performs and documents the test.
  2. Schedule the correct inspection types in the required sequence. Confirm inspection type, permit number, address, contact, and access before scheduling.
  3. Attend with approved documents, product data, photographs, and responsible trades. Bring the complete interior inspection checklist to the scheduled inspection.
  4. Record approval, partial approval, corrections, and areas that must remain open. Enter the official result exactly as given, without paraphrasing a rejection as a pass.
  5. Do not conceal rejected or uninspected areas. Hold drywall delivery for any area not yet approved.
  6. After approval, control drywall delivery and installation so required assemblies are not changed. Confirm no field substitution occurs after approval without a documented revision.
  7. File checklists, photographs, labels, test results, inspection records, and correction status. Archive the complete package in the compliance record before Project 16 begins.

Common Interior Readiness Inspection

Inspect the finished readiness package using the same checks regardless of which room or assembly is being reviewed.

  • Prerequisite check: confirm framing, rough plumbing, rough electrical, rough HVAC, gas, and fire approvals are recorded before verification began.
  • Insulation check: confirm type, value, thickness, density, and facing match the approved assembly schedule for every cavity.
  • Air-barrier check: confirm continuity at plates, rims, openings, penetrations, chases, and attic transitions.
  • Fire-blocking check: confirm fire blocking, draft stopping, and rated-assembly components remain visible and match approved details.
  • Photographic check: confirm every concealed condition is photographed by location before drywall.
  • Release-control check: confirm drywall delivery and installation began only after documented approval.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Insulation Does Not Match the Approved Assembly Schedule

Probable cause: a different insulation product, thickness, or facing was installed than the one specified in the approved energy documents.
Confirmation: compare the installed product label against the Project 12 energy-code compliance matrix for the affected assembly.
Correction: remove and replace the nonconforming material with the approved product, or obtain a documented revision before proceeding.
Reinspection: repeat the insulation compliance check for the corrected assembly.

Air-Barrier Continuity Is Broken at a Plate, Rim, or Penetration

Probable cause: a sealant, membrane, or gasket was omitted, damaged, or installed outside the manufacturer's approved conditions.
Confirmation: trace the air barrier from the affected point along the full enclosure and identify every gap.
Correction: install the approved sealing product following manufacturer instructions and re-photograph the corrected detail.
Reinspection: repeat the air-barrier continuity check at every affected location.

A Fire-Blocking or Rated-Assembly Detail Cannot Be Confirmed Against Approved Documents

Probable cause: the installed material or method does not match the listed system or approved detail, or the approved detail was never located onsite.
Confirmation: compare the installed condition against the listed-system documentation and approved life-safety notes from Project 13.
Correction: replace or complete the assembly using only approved or listed materials and details, obtaining design clarification where the documents are unclear.
Reinspection: repeat the fire-blocking and rated-assembly visibility check before scheduling.

A Concealed Condition Was Not Photographed Before Drywall

Probable cause: the photographic record was skipped for one or more rooms or assemblies during the readiness walk.
Confirmation: compare the photographic record against the room-by-room deficiency list and identify any location without a photograph.
Correction: where drywall has not yet been installed, complete the missing photograph immediately; where drywall has already concealed the area, treat it as an unresolved item requiring inspector or design-professional review before proceeding.
Reinspection: repeat the photographic completeness check on the full record.

Drywall Was Delivered or Installed Before Required Approval

Probable cause: the drywall release was authorized on a schedule assumption rather than a documented inspection approval.
Confirmation: compare the drywall delivery or installation date against the recorded inspection approval date on the permit card.
Correction: stop further installation, notify the responsible parties, and obtain the required inspection or documented exception before proceeding.
Reinspection: repeat the release-control check once approval is documented.

A Prerequisite Trade Approval Was Missing When Verification Began

Probable cause: the framing, rough plumbing, rough electrical, rough HVAC, gas, or fire inspection was not actually completed, or its result was not recorded on the permit card.
Confirmation: review the permit card and prior inspection records for the missing trade's approval date.
Correction: obtain the missing inspection and recorded approval before continuing any further verification in this chapter.
Reinspection: repeat the prerequisite completeness check before resuming the interior readiness walk.

Field Terms

Concealment Hold Point
The stage at which insulation, drywall, or another finish would make prior work unverifiable, requiring documented approval before proceeding.
Air Barrier
The continuous material system controlling air movement through the building enclosure, as defined by the approved energy documents.
Vapor-Control Layer
A material controlling water-vapor movement, installed only where the approved assembly specifies it.
Fire Blocking
Material or assemblies closing concealed draft openings between stories or spaces as specified in the approved documents.
Rated Assembly
A wall, floor, ceiling, or penetration system with a required fire-resistance rating, built only from approved or listed materials and details.
Drywall-Readiness
The condition in which backing, support, insulation, air sealing, and prerequisite approvals are complete and drywall may be released for installation.
Listed System
A tested and documented assembly, such as a firestop or rated wall, that must be built exactly as tested rather than field-modified.
Deficiency List
The record assigning, tracking, and verifying correction of every incomplete or nonconforming item found during the readiness walk.
Drywall Release
The controlled authorization point after which drywall delivery and installation may proceed without changing approved assemblies.
Compliance Record
The filed collection of checklists, photographs, product evidence, and inspection results supporting this project's closeout.
