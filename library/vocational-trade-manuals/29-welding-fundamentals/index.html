29-Welding Fundamentals

This field manual provides practical, project-based instruction in basic welding skills and metal fabrication. Students learn welding safety, equipment operation, material preparation, cutting, grinding, arc control, and fundamental shielded metal arc welding techniques.

Overview

Welding Fundamentals introduces students to the essential skills required for safe and effective welding operations. Beginning with work area preparation and personal protective equipment, students progress through welding equipment identification, steel preparation, cutting, grinding, arc control, and basic SMAW weld practice.

Learning Outcomes

  • Prepare a safe welding work environment.
  • Select and properly use welding personal protective equipment.
  • Identify welding machines, tools, electrodes, and consumables.
  • Prepare steel surfaces for welding operations.
  • Cut, grind, deburr, and finish metal components.
  • Understand basic shielded metal arc welding principles.
  • Strike and control a welding arc safely.
  • Produce consistent SMAW practice weld beads.
  • Create basic SMAW fillet welds.
  • Develop foundational welding and fabrication skills.
