Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to inspect and identify one complete welding station before it is used for any practice or project weld. You will identify the power source, its process capability and duty cycle, the electrode lead and work lead, the electrode holder, gun, or torch, the work clamp, the wire feeder where present, the shielding-gas system where present, and the approved consumables staged for the process. You will record every defect, tag unsafe components out of service, and complete an equipment-readiness form before requesting authorization to energize the station.

No arc is struck in this chapter. The station remains de-energized throughout the inspection except for a brief, instructor-authorized test at the end of the chapter that confirms the equipment responds correctly before any welding practice begins.

This chapter builds directly on Chapter 1's signed welding-area readiness record and Chapter 2's staged and inspected PPE. It produces the equipment-readiness record that Chapters 7 through 20 rely on every time a station is energized.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 1 established the area you are working in, and Chapter 2 staged the PPE you are wearing. This chapter turns to the station itself: the machine, leads, holder or gun, clamp, gas system where applicable, and consumables that every later chapter depends on. Chapter 4 assumes this equipment-readiness record exists before steel preparation begins, and Chapters 7, 10, 13, and 14 each repeat a shortened version of this inspection at the start of their own process-specific setup.

The identify-inspect-document sequence taught here — machine, leads, clamp, holder or gun, consumables, record — is the same sequence you repeat every time you change stations or processes for the rest of the book.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • identifying the welding power source by manufacturer, model, serial number, input rating, output range, process capability, and duty cycle
  • tracing and inspecting the electrode lead and work lead from machine terminal to holder, gun, torch, or clamp
  • identifying and inspecting the SMAW electrode holder, GMAW or FCAW gun, or GTAW torch associated with the station
  • inspecting the work clamp for jaw condition, spring pressure, cable attachment, and contact area
  • identifying the wire feeder, gas cylinder, regulator, flowmeter, and hose where the station includes them
  • identifying approved SMAW electrodes, GMAW or FCAW wire, or GTAW tungsten and filler rod by classification, diameter, and condition
  • completing and signing an equipment-readiness form before the station is authorized for use

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • strike an arc, run a practice bead, or weld on a project; this chapter ends with a documented station, not a completed weld
  • open the machine enclosure, remove service panels, or perform any repair beyond normal operator setup
  • service a wire feeder beyond loading and adjusting it, clear a severe jam, or perform any maintenance that requires isolating internal machine power
  • place a defective or tagged component back into service without qualified repair or replacement

Any machine, lead, holder, gun, clamp, gas component, or consumable that fails this chapter's inspection must be corrected, repaired by qualified personnel, or removed from service before the responsible person signs the equipment-readiness form.

Trade Note

A welding station is not "ready" because it is plugged in and the cylinder is full. It is ready only after the machine, leads, holder or gun, clamp, gas system, and consumables have each been identified, inspected, and matched to the approved process. Skipping the identification step — welding with an unidentified electrode or an untraced lead — is a common cause of both defective welds and equipment damage traced back to setup.

Setup Standard

Do not energize the welding machine, open the cylinder valve for use, or authorize any hot work at this station until the responsible person has signed the equipment-readiness record produced in this chapter. Do not return a tagged or defective component to service without qualified repair or replacement and a repeated inspection.

Shop Tip

Trace each lead with your hand and your eyes from the machine terminal all the way to the holder, gun, torch, or clamp before you inspect any single point along its length. A damaged section hiding under a cable tie or behind the machine case is easy to miss if you inspect only the visible portions.

Power Source and Lead Identification

Identify the power source before touching any control. Read the data plate and locate the operator manual before assuming the machine's type, output range, or duty cycle. Do not open the enclosure or remove a service panel to answer a question the data plate and manual can already answer.

Measurement Cue — Trace Leads End to End

Trace the electrode lead and work lead from the machine terminal to the holder, gun, torch, or work clamp before inspecting any single section. Confirm insulation is intact along the full traced length, not only at the visible ends. Measure electrode, wire, or tungsten diameter against the package label rather than estimating by eye.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Open the Machine Enclosure

Do not open the machine enclosure, remove service panels, or attempt any repair beyond normal operator setup. Do not use a machine with damaged insulation, exposed conductors, loose connectors, overheated leads, cracked holders, wet equipment, or improvised repairs. Tag the component and stop until qualified personnel repair or replace it.

Field Safety Cue — Dry Hands, Dry Equipment

Keep your hands, gloves, and the equipment dry throughout the inspection. Avoid body contact between the electrode circuit and the workpiece while tracing leads or handling the holder, gun, or torch.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Consumable Identification

Identify electrodes, wire, tungsten, and filler rod by the classification, diameter, and condition printed on the manufacturer package label. Do not use loose, unidentified, or mixed-classification consumables. Confirm polarity requirements from the consumable manufacturer information before recording the station's approved process.

E. Power Source Identification

  1. Identify the machine manufacturer, model, serial number, input rating, output range, process capability, and duty cycle. Record each value exactly as printed on the data plate.
  2. Locate the operator manual and machine data plate. Confirm the manual matches the model shown on the data plate.
  3. Identify the power switch, disconnect, output terminals, polarity markings, controls, display, alarms, and cooling openings. Point to and name each item before proceeding.
  4. Identify whether the power source is transformer, inverter, constant current, constant voltage, multiprocess, or another approved type. Record the type from the manual, not from appearance alone.
  5. Do not open the machine enclosure or remove service panels. Stop and tag the machine for qualified service if enclosure access appears necessary.
  6. Inspect the case, feet, handles, vents, cord, plug, strain relief, and input connection. Record any damage, looseness, or missing hardware.

F. Lead, Holder, and Work-Clamp Inspection

  1. Identify the electrode lead and work lead. Confirm each lead's identity against the machine's output terminal markings.
  2. Trace each lead from machine terminal to holder, gun, torch, or work clamp. Follow the full physical length by hand and by eye.
  3. Inspect lead insulation for cuts, burns, crushed sections, exposed conductor, taped repairs, and overheated connectors. Record the location of any defect found.
  4. Inspect lugs, twist-locks, terminals, strain reliefs, and connector engagement. Confirm every connector is fully seated.
  5. Identify the SMAW electrode holder, GMAW or FCAW gun, or GTAW torch associated with the station. Confirm the component matches the station's approved process.
  6. Inspect holder jaws, insulation, spring tension, handle, trigger, switch, cable, neck, nozzle, collet, cap, or other process components. Record condition for each part present.
  7. Inspect the work clamp for jaw condition, spring pressure, cable attachment, heat damage, contamination, and contact area. Confirm the clamp will grip clean sound metal firmly.
  8. Clean the clamp contact faces where permitted. Use the approved method and confirm improved contact after cleaning.

G. Wire Feeder and Gas System Identification

  1. Identify the wire feeder, drive rolls, tension adjustment, inlet guide, liner, gun connection, spool hub, and brake where applicable. Skip this step and record "not applicable" for stations without a feeder.
  2. Identify gas cylinder, label, chain, valve, regulator, flowmeter, hose, solenoid, and torch or gun connection where applicable. Skip this step and record "not applicable" for stations without a gas system.
  3. Verify the gas is correct for the approved process and consumable. Compare the cylinder label against the process and consumable manufacturer information.
  4. Inspect cylinder security, regulator condition, hose damage, and fitting compatibility. Confirm the cylinder is upright, chained or otherwise secured, and free of impact damage.

H. Consumable Identification

  1. Identify approved SMAW electrodes by classification, diameter, coating condition, storage, and package label. Skip and record "not applicable" if SMAW is not the station's approved process.
  2. Identify approved GMAW or FCAW wire by classification, diameter, spool, condition, and manufacturer information. Skip and record "not applicable" if GMAW/FCAW is not the station's approved process.
  3. Identify approved GTAW tungsten and filler rod by type, diameter, color code or marking, package, and condition. Skip and record "not applicable" if GTAW is not the station's approved process.
  4. Do not use loose unidentified consumables. Tag and set aside any consumable that cannot be matched to a package label.
  5. Inspect consumables for moisture, rust, dirt, oil, damaged coating, kinks, contamination, or mixed classification. Record condition for each consumable staged at the station.
  6. Identify polarity requirements from the consumable and process information. Record the required polarity on the equipment-readiness form.
  7. Identify recommended starting parameter range without yet energizing the machine. Record the range from the consumable manufacturer or approved procedure.

I. Final Verification and Authorization

  1. Verify the work clamp will attach to clean sound base metal close to the practice area. Confirm the intended attachment point matches Chapter 1's cleaned work-surface zone.
  2. Confirm leads and hoses can reach without tension, sharp bends, hot surfaces, or trip hazards. Walk the intended cable path from machine to work location.
  3. Check machine cooling clearances and duty-cycle limitations. Confirm nothing blocks the machine's vents or cooling openings.
  4. Verify the station has approved tools, spare contact tips, nozzles, collets, cups, brushes, and setup items. Confirm each item present matches the station's approved process.
  5. Record all equipment defects and remove unsafe components from service. List every defect found in Sections E through H on the equipment-readiness form.
  6. Tag equipment that requires qualified repair. Attach an out-of-service tag and note the required repair before the component may return to service.
  7. Complete the equipment-readiness form with machine, leads, clamp, holder or gun, gas, consumables, and approved process. Confirm every field in Section D is addressed.
  8. Obtain authorization before energizing the station. Present the completed form to the responsible person for review and signature before any brief test or practice weld.

Common Equipment Inspection

Inspect any welding station using the same checks regardless of process, once the process-specific components have been identified.

  • Power-source check: confirm manufacturer, model, rating, and duty cycle are recorded from the data plate and manual.
  • Lead check: confirm both leads are traced end to end with intact insulation and sound connectors.
  • Process-component check: confirm the holder, gun, or torch matches the approved process and every part is inspected.
  • Work-clamp check: confirm the clamp will grip clean sound metal firmly and its cable attachment is sound.
  • Consumable check: confirm every consumable is identified against a manufacturer package label with no mixed classification.
  • Authorization check: confirm the equipment-readiness form is fully completed and signed before the station is energized.

Complete the Equipment Readiness Checklist in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 4.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Lead Defect Is Found Only After the Machine Is Energized

Probable cause: the lead was inspected only at its visible ends rather than traced along its full length before energizing.
Confirmation: de-energize the machine, repeat the full lead trace described in Section F, and inspect insulation along the entire length.
Correction: tag the damaged lead out of service and replace it before continuing.
Reinspection: repeat the lead check for the replacement lead before re-authorizing the station.

The Work Clamp Will Not Hold a Firm Connection

Probable cause: the clamp jaws, spring, or contact faces are contaminated, worn, or damaged, or the clamp was attached to painted, rusted, or otherwise unclean metal.
Confirmation: inspect the jaw condition, spring pressure, and contact area, and confirm the attachment point is clean sound metal.
Correction: clean the contact faces where permitted, move the clamp to a cleaned attachment point, or tag and replace a damaged clamp.
Reinspection: repeat the work-clamp check at the corrected attachment point.

A Consumable Cannot Be Matched to a Package Label

Probable cause: loose electrodes, wire, or tungsten were stored or staged without their original packaging or identification.
Confirmation: compare the consumable's markings, color code, or physical characteristics against every package label available at the station.
Correction: tag and set aside the unidentified consumable; do not use it for any practice or project weld.
Reinspection: confirm only identified, labeled consumables remain staged at the station before authorization.

The Gas Type Does Not Match the Process or Consumable

Probable cause: the cylinder staged at the station was not checked against the approved process and consumable manufacturer information before setup.
Confirmation: compare the cylinder label against the process and consumable requirements recorded in Section H.
Correction: replace the cylinder with one matching the approved gas type, or correct the recorded process if the mismatch was a documentation error.
Reinspection: repeat the gas-system check with the corrected cylinder.

A Component Is Tagged but Still Physically Connected to the Station

Probable cause: the defective component was tagged but not disconnected or physically separated from the rest of the station.
Confirmation: walk the station and confirm every tagged component is disconnected and cannot be inadvertently used.
Correction: disconnect and remove the tagged component from the station until qualified repair or replacement is complete.
Reinspection: repeat the final verification steps in Section I before requesting authorization.

The Equipment-Readiness Form Is Completed but Not Signed

Probable cause: the inspection was completed, but the responsible person was not available or was not asked to review and sign the form.
Confirmation: check the equipment-readiness form for a completed authorization signature and date.
Correction: locate the responsible person, review the completed form with them, and obtain the signature before energizing the station.
Reinspection: confirm the signed record is stored where it can be produced before later chapters begin.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, authorized station, not a completed weld. Housekeeping here protects the readiness record for the chapters that follow.

  • Confirm the machine is de-energized and the disconnect is in the required position if the station is not proceeding directly into a later chapter
  • Confirm the gas cylinder valve is closed and line pressure is relieved where required
  • Coil leads and hoses without kinks, sharp bends, or trip hazards
  • Return identified consumables to their labeled storage location, keeping classifications separated
  • Store the signed Equipment Readiness Checklist where it can be referenced before every later chapter

Skill Check

  1. Why must the electrode lead and work lead be traced along their full length rather than inspected only at the visible ends?
  2. What information must be identified from the machine data plate and manual before any control is adjusted?
  3. Why is it not permitted to open the machine enclosure or remove service panels during this inspection?
  4. Why must the work clamp attach to clean sound metal close to the weld rather than any convenient nearby surface?
  5. Why must an unidentified electrode, wire, or tungsten never be used, even if it appears similar to an identified consumable?

Performance Check

With the inspection complete, review the finished station against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the power source is identified with recorded ratings and duty cycle, both leads are traced and sound end to end, the holder, gun, or torch matches the approved process, the work clamp grips clean metal firmly, the wire feeder and gas system where present match the wire and process, every consumable is identified against its package label, every defect is recorded, and every unsafe component is tagged and disconnected. Do not proceed to Chapter 4 until the readiness record is signed.
