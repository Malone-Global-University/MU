Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to select, fit, inspect, use, clean, and store a complete welding PPE system for one approved process, plus the grinding and chipping tasks associated with it. You will work inside the welding area you established and had authorized in Chapter 1.

You will identify the process, current range, position, grinding tasks, material, fume hazard, and hot-metal exposure that your PPE must protect against; select and inspect a welding helmet and lens shade; select and inspect safety glasses and a face shield; select and inspect welding gloves; select and inspect flame-resistant clothing and footwear; select hearing protection; identify exposed-skin areas and cover them; evaluate whether respiratory protection is required; practice movement in your PPE; and complete a documented PPE readiness record.

No arc is struck, no cut is made, and no grinder is energized in this chapter. This chapter ends with a fully staged, inspected, and documented PPE system, ready for the equipment identification work in Chapter 3 and the striking of the first practice arc in Chapter 7.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • WF-007 — Select a welding helmet approved for the process and task.
  • WF-008 — Select the required lens shade from approved guidance.
  • WF-009 — Use welding gloves appropriate to the process and task.
  • WF-010 — Use flame-resistant clothing appropriate to the spark and heat exposure.
  • WF-011 — Protect skin from arc exposure by identifying and covering exposed areas.
  • WF-012 — Inspect the complete PPE system before welding.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 1 established and authorized the area in which this chapter's PPE will be worn. Chapter 3 inspects the welding station itself while you wear the safety glasses and footwear staged here. Chapters 7, 10, and 14 depend directly on the helmet, lens shade, gloves, and clothing selected in this chapter before the first SMAW, GMAW, or GTAW arc is struck. Chapters 5 and 6 depend on the face shield and hearing protection selected here before any cutting or grinding tool is energized.

The select-inspect-fit-use-clean-store sequence taught in this chapter is repeated, in compressed form, at the start of every remaining chapter that involves hot work: confirm the area, confirm PPE, confirm equipment, then proceed.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • identifying the process, current range, position, grinding tasks, material, fume hazard, and hot-metal exposure that drive PPE selection
  • selecting, inspecting, and adjusting a welding helmet and the correct lens shade for the approved process
  • selecting and inspecting safety glasses, a face shield, welding gloves, flame-resistant clothing, and leather footwear
  • selecting hearing protection and identifying and covering exposed-skin areas
  • evaluating the need for respiratory protection
  • performing a dry movement check and a final PPE inspection before welding
  • cleaning, storing, and documenting the PPE system after use

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • strike an arc, cut, or grind; this chapter ends before any tool is energized
  • select or use a respirator without the required medical evaluation, fit testing, training, cartridge selection, inspection, and written program; this chapter only evaluates whether respiratory protection is required
  • substitute a lighter lens shade for improved visibility in place of the approved guidance
  • weld, cut, or grind without the safety glasses required underneath the helmet or face shield

Any PPE component that fails inspection in this chapter must be replaced or corrected before it is staged for use in any later chapter.

Trade Note

A welding helmet on the shelf is not "PPE." It becomes PPE only after it has been matched to the process, inspected for damage, fitted for stable coverage, and set to the correct lens shade. Skipping the inspection and fit steps in this chapter, rather than any single missing item, is the most common cause of arc-flash and hot-metal injuries traced back to PPE.

Required PPE for this chapter: safety glasses with side protection and closed-toe leather footwear at all times during selection, inspection, and fitting. The full PPE system assembled in this chapter is not energized against an arc, grinder, or cutting tool until later chapters.

Setup Standard

Do not stage a helmet, glove, or clothing item for use in any later chapter until it has passed the inspection steps in this chapter. A PPE item that fails inspection must be replaced or corrected and reinspected before it is recorded as ready.

Shop Tip

Inspect and fit PPE in the same order every time: helmet shell and lens first, then safety glasses, then gloves, then clothing, then footwear, then hearing and respiratory items. Working the same order each session makes a missing or damaged item easier to notice before it reaches the hot-work zone.

Helmet, Lens Shade, and Auto-Darkening Function

PPE selection begins with the process, current range, position, grinding tasks, material, fume hazard, and hot-metal exposure identified for the session — not with whatever helmet is closest at hand. The helmet, lens shade, and every other item selected in this chapter must match those identified conditions.

Measurement Cue — Select Lens Shade from Approved Guidance

Select the lens shade from the shop's approved lens-shade guidance for the specific process, current range, and task. Do not select a shade by personal preference and do not use a lighter shade merely to improve visibility. Record the selected shade on the PPE readiness checklist.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Weld Behind a Failed Helmet

Do not use a helmet with a cracked shell, cracked cover lens, cracked filter lens, failed sensor coverage, dead or unverified battery, or a filter lens that does not darken correctly when tested. Stop and replace or repair the affected component before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Safety Glasses Stay On Under the Helmet

Wear safety glasses with side protection under the welding helmet at all times, and use a face shield over safety glasses — not in place of them — for grinding, chipping, and wire brushing.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Auto-Darkening Helmet Verification

For an auto-darkening helmet, verify shade range, sensitivity, delay, battery status, and sensor exposure against the manufacturer's instructions, and confirm the helmet darkens correctly before any arc exposure.

E. Preparation — Task Review and Requirement Check

  1. Identify the welding process, expected current range, position, grinding tasks, material, fume hazard, and hot-metal exposure. Record each condition on the PPE readiness checklist before selecting any item.
  2. Review shop PPE requirements and applicable lens-shade guidance. Confirm the guidance matches the process and current range identified in Step 1.

F. Helmet and Lens Selection

  1. Select a welding helmet approved for the process and task. Confirm the helmet's rated coverage matches the identified process before continuing.
  2. Inspect helmet shell, headgear, pivot points, cover lenses, filter lens, sensors, battery, controls, and seals. Check each component individually rather than assessing the helmet as a whole.
  3. Replace cracked, deeply scratched, contaminated, loose, or nonfunctioning components. Do not stage a helmet with any unresolved defect.
  4. For an auto-darkening helmet, verify shade range, sensitivity, delay, battery status, sensor exposure, and test function. Follow the manufacturer's verification procedure exactly.
  5. Confirm the helmet darkens correctly before arc exposure. Do not proceed to any later chapter's arc work with an unverified helmet.
  6. Select the required lens shade from the approved guidance rather than personal preference alone. Record the selected shade on the readiness checklist.
  7. Do not use a lighter shade merely to improve visibility. Stop and reselect if a lighter shade was installed for this reason.
  8. Install clean cover lenses in the correct orientation. Confirm the cover lens seats fully and does not distort the view through the filter lens.
  9. Adjust headgear for stable fit without excessive pressure. Confirm the fit remains stable through a head-movement check.
  10. Verify the helmet lowers and remains in position without exposing the face. Repeat the lowering motion until the result is consistent.

G. Eye, Face, and Hand Protection

  1. Select safety glasses with side protection for continuous wear beneath the helmet. Confirm the glasses fit under the helmet without gaps.
  2. Inspect glasses for cracks, deep scratches, distortion, loose arms, and contamination. Replace glasses that fail any part of this inspection.
  3. Select a face shield for grinding, chipping, and wire brushing. Confirm the shield covers the face fully in the intended working position.
  4. Confirm the face shield is used with safety glasses rather than as a replacement. Do not stage a face shield as the sole eye protection for these tasks.
  5. Select welding gloves appropriate to SMAW, GMAW, FCAW, GTAW, or handling hot material. Match the glove selection to the specific process approved for the session.
  6. Inspect gloves for holes, wetness, oil, embedded wire, burned seams, and damaged cuffs. Replace gloves that fail any part of this inspection.

H. Clothing, Footwear, and Hearing Protection

  1. Select flame-resistant shirt, jacket, sleeves, apron, or leathers appropriate to the spark and heat exposure. Match the selection to the process and position identified in Section E.
  2. Verify clothing covers the neck, wrists, waist, and ankles. Check coverage in the learner's actual working position, not only standing upright.
  3. Button collars and cuffs and keep pockets, pant cuffs, and folds from collecting sparks. Correct any open collar, cuff, or fold before continuing.
  4. Remove synthetic outerwear and combustible contamination. Confirm no synthetic garment remains over flame-resistant clothing.
  5. Select leather footwear that covers the foot and resists sparks and hot metal. Confirm footwear meets this standard before continuing.
  6. Inspect footwear for exposed synthetic mesh, open tops, damaged soles, and trapped metal. Replace or correct footwear that fails this inspection.
  7. Select hearing protection appropriate to grinding, chipping, and shop noise. Confirm the selection matches the tasks identified for the session.
  8. Use ear protection that also prevents hot particles entering the ear when exposure requires it. Confirm this requirement against the position identified in Section E.

I. Skin Coverage, Respiratory Evaluation, and Final Verification

  1. Secure hair, remove jewelry, and control loose clothing and cords. Correct each item before continuing to the fit check.
  2. Identify exposed-skin areas visible during normal welding positions. Check the learner's actual flat, vertical, or seated position, not only a standing position.
  3. Add sleeves, cap, hood, neck protection, or other approved coverage. Recheck exposed-skin areas after adding coverage.
  4. Evaluate whether respiratory protection is required by the material and ventilation assessment. Base the determination on the material and ventilation identified in Chapter 1 and Section E of this chapter.
  5. Do not use a respirator without required program controls. If respiratory protection is determined to be required, record the requirement and route it through the shop's written respiratory-protection program before use.
  6. Practice lowering the helmet, positioning hands, and moving without disturbing PPE. Repeat until helmet, glove, and clothing position remain stable.
  7. Perform a dry movement check in flat, vertical, and seated positions as applicable. Confirm PPE coverage and fit hold through each position used in this book.
  8. Inspect the complete PPE system immediately before welding. Repeat this full inspection before every hot-work session in later chapters, not only once at the end of this chapter.

J. Cleaning, Storage, and Documentation

  1. Clean PPE after use without damaging lenses or flame-resistant properties. Use only cleaning methods approved for each specific PPE material.
  2. Store helmet, lenses, gloves, clothing, and respiratory equipment in clean dry protected locations. Confirm the storage location is identified on the readiness checklist.
  3. Record PPE selection, defects, replacements, and readiness. Complete the PPE Readiness Checklist in full before ending the chapter.

Common PPE Inspection

Inspect the complete PPE system using the same checks regardless of which process the session will use.

  • Helmet check: confirm shell, headgear, lenses, sensors, battery, and darkening function are inspected and pass, with the correct shade selected from approved guidance.
  • Eye and face check: confirm safety glasses are worn continuously and a face shield is staged for grinding, chipping, and wire brushing.
  • Hand check: confirm gloves are matched to the approved process and free of holes, wetness, oil, embedded wire, and damaged cuffs.
  • Body check: confirm flame-resistant clothing covers the neck, wrists, waist, and ankles, with collars and cuffs closed and no synthetic outerwear.
  • Foot check: confirm leather footwear covers the foot and is free of exposed mesh, open tops, and damaged soles.
  • Hearing and respiratory check: confirm hearing protection matches the task and the respiratory-protection determination is recorded.
  • Documentation check: confirm the PPE readiness checklist is fully completed before hot work is authorized.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Auto-Darkening Helmet Does Not Darken Consistently

Probable cause: a low or failing battery, a blocked or contaminated sensor, or a sensitivity setting outside the manufacturer's recommended range.
Confirmation: follow the manufacturer's test procedure and check battery status, sensor coverage, and current sensitivity and delay settings.
Correction: replace the battery, clean the sensor, or reset sensitivity and delay to the manufacturer's recommended range.
Reinspection: repeat the darkening-function test before any arc exposure.

Skin Remains Exposed After PPE Is Fitted

Probable cause: exposed-skin areas were checked only in a standing position, not in the flat, vertical, or seated position the learner will actually use.
Confirmation: repeat the exposed-skin check in the specific working position planned for the session.
Correction: add sleeves, a cap, a hood, or neck protection to cover the newly identified area.
Reinspection: repeat the dry movement check in the same working position.

Gloves Selected Do Not Match the Approved Process

Probable cause: gloves were selected by availability rather than by matching them to the specific process — SMAW, GMAW, FCAW, or GTAW — approved for the session.
Confirmation: compare the glove type against the process identified in Section E and the glove manufacturer's process rating.
Correction: replace the gloves with a pair matched to the approved process.
Reinspection: repeat the glove inspection and record the corrected selection.

A Lighter Lens Shade Was Installed for Visibility

Probable cause: the lens shade was selected to make the puddle easier to see rather than from the approved lens-shade guidance for the process and current range.
Confirmation: compare the installed shade against the approved guidance for the identified process and current range.
Correction: install the correct shade from the approved guidance and do not substitute a lighter shade for visibility.
Reinspection: repeat the lens-shade selection check and record the corrected shade.

Footwear Has Exposed Synthetic Mesh Panels

Probable cause: footwear was selected for comfort or general work use without checking for synthetic mesh panels that can melt or ignite from sparks.
Confirmation: inspect the full exterior of the footwear for mesh panels, open tops, and damaged soles.
Correction: replace the footwear with leather footwear that fully covers the foot.
Reinspection: repeat the footwear inspection and record the corrected selection.

The PPE Readiness Checklist Is Completed but a Respiratory Determination Is Missing

Probable cause: the material and ventilation assessment from Chapter 1 was not carried forward into this chapter's respiratory-protection evaluation.
Confirmation: compare the material and ventilation information recorded in Chapter 1 against the respiratory-determination field on this chapter's checklist.
Correction: complete the respiratory-protection evaluation and record the determination before continuing.
Reinspection: confirm the completed determination is present on the signed or dated readiness record.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, inspected PPE system, not a completed weld. Housekeeping here protects the readiness record for the chapters that follow.

  • Clean PPE using only methods approved for each specific material
  • Confirm lenses and flame-resistant properties were not damaged during cleaning
  • Store the helmet, lenses, gloves, clothing, and respiratory equipment in a clean, dry, protected location
  • Confirm any replaced or corrected component is recorded on the checklist
  • Store the completed PPE readiness checklist where it can be referenced before every later chapter

Field Terms

  3. Why must an auto-darkening helmet's shade range, sensitivity, delay, and battery condition be verified before the first arc exposure?

  5. Why must exposed-skin areas be checked in the learner's actual working position rather than only while standing?
  6. What conditions require a respirator to be evaluated, and why can a respirator not be used without program controls even if the evaluation identifies a need?

  8. Why must footwear be free of exposed synthetic mesh, even if the mesh area is small?
  9. Why is the complete PPE system inspected again immediately before welding, in addition to the inspection performed in this chapter?

Chapter Closeout

You have completed the second chapter in the welding fundamentals sequence: selecting and using welding PPE correctly. This chapter required you to select a welding helmet approved for the process and task (WF-007), select the required lens shade from approved guidance (WF-008), use welding gloves appropriate to the process and task (WF-009), use flame-resistant clothing appropriate to the spark and heat exposure (WF-010), protect skin from arc exposure by identifying and covering exposed areas (WF-011), and inspect the complete PPE system before welding (WF-012).

Store the signed Welding PPE Readiness Checklist with your other project records. You will confirm this PPE system's readiness before every remaining chapter in this book.
