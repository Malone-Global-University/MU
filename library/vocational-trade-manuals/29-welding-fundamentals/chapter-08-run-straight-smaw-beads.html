Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to run a documented series of straight SMAW stringer beads on flat mild-steel coupons. Chapter 7 taught you to strike and control a short arc; this chapter extends that control across a full-length bead with consistent arc length, electrode angle, travel speed, tie-in, and crater termination.

You will prepare coupons with parallel bead lines, verify the machine and electrode against the approved starting range, run a series of stringer beads across the coupon, remove slag between beads, and inspect each bead against measured acceptance criteria. Every bead is recorded — settings, measurements, and inspection results — before you move to the fillet-weld joints in Chapter 9.

This chapter does not introduce weaving, joints, or out-of-position welding. Those skills build on the straight-bead control demonstrated here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • WF-043 — Position work coupons for controlled flat-position SMAW bead welding.
  • WF-044 — Set an amperage range appropriate to the approved electrode and coupon thickness.
  • WF-045 — Run stringer beads with a consistent start, travel path, and crater termination.
  • WF-046 — Maintain a consistent electrode work angle and travel angle throughout the bead.
  • WF-047 — Maintain bead consistency in width, reinforcement, and ripple spacing across a series of beads.
  • WF-048 — Inspect bead profile against measured acceptance criteria and identify probable causes of defects.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 7 qualified you to strike an arc and control it over a short practice path. This chapter is the direct extension of that skill across a full working bead length, with the added requirement of parameter documentation and measured inspection. The straight-bead control demonstrated here — arc length, work angle, travel angle, and travel speed — is the same control you apply to the lap and T-joint fillet welds in Chapter 9, and it remains the foundation for every later SMAW joint in this book, including the Chapter 20 capstone.

Do not begin this chapter until the welding area from Chapter 1, the PPE from Chapter 2, the equipment identification from Chapter 3, the prepared coupons from Chapters 4 through 6, and the arc-starting competency from Chapter 7 are complete.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • marking parallel bead lines and start/stop points on a flat mild-steel coupon
  • setting and recording the approved starting amperage for the selected electrode
  • running a series of straight stringer beads with consistent arc length, work angle, travel angle, and travel speed
  • terminating each bead with controlled crater fill and removing slag completely before inspection
  • measuring bead width and length and comparing results against the project acceptance sample
  • identifying probable causes of bead defects and adjusting one variable at a time under instructor direction

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • weld a joint, lap, T-joint, or any multi-member assembly; joint welding begins in Chapter 9
  • weave the electrode or change technique beyond the approved straight-stringer exercise
  • weld out of the flat position or on a coupon that has not passed Chapter 4–6 preparation
  • exceed the machine's duty-cycle rating identified in Chapter 3

Any bead, coupon, or machine condition that fails this chapter's inspection criteria must be corrected or the bead remade before the coupon is recorded as accepted.

Trade Note

A straight bead is not "good" because the arc stayed lit. It is good only when width, reinforcement, and ripple spacing stay consistent along the full bead length, the start and stop show no defects, and the measured profile matches the acceptance sample. Consistency across multiple beads — not a single lucky bead — is what this chapter assesses.

Setup Standard

Shop Tip

Common Field Fundamentals

A consistent stringer bead depends on four variables held steady at the same time: arc length, work angle, travel angle, and travel speed. Changing more than one variable between beads makes it impossible to identify which change caused a difference in the result, so this chapter's troubleshooting steps require single-variable adjustment.

Layout zoneCoupon marked with parallel bead lines and start/stop points before welding
Welding positionCoupon flat and stable with clear, unobstructed travel path for the lead
Slag-removal zoneCooled coupon, chipping hammer and wire brush, eye and face protection
Inspection zoneSteel rule and bead gauge, acceptance sample, bead-series log

Arc Length, Angle, and Travel Speed Together

Arc length, work angle, travel angle, and travel speed must be established at the start of the bead and held through the full length. A bead that starts correctly and drifts in any one variable will show inconsistent width, reinforcement, or ripple spacing even if the arc never goes out.

Measurement Cue — Mark Bead Lines Before Welding

Mark parallel bead lines and start/stop points on the coupon before striking the first arc. Confirm spacing between bead lines is consistent across the coupon and matches the project specification. Do not estimate bead spacing by eye while welding.

Critical Safety Cue — Duty Cycle and Continuous Arc Time

Running a full series of beads in immediate succession can exceed the machine's duty-cycle rating identified in Chapter 3. Allow the machine to cool within its rated duty cycle between beads. Do not override machine thermal-protection shutdowns or continue welding through an overheating alarm.

Field Safety Cue — Lead Management During Travel

Arrange the electrode lead so it does not pull the holder off the marked path or drag across hot metal during travel. Confirm the lead has enough slack to complete the full bead length without tension.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Electrode Amperage Range

Set the starting amperage from the electrode manufacturer's published range for the selected classification and diameter, adjusted for coupon thickness and position per the approved shop procedure. Do not select amperage by feel alone before recording the starting value.

E. Preparation — Review and Coupon Setup

  1. Review the approved coupon layout, electrode, polarity, position, bead length, spacing, and parameter range. Confirm the shop procedure for this exercise before staging materials.
  2. Verify the work area, ventilation, fire controls, PPE, machine, leads, holder, and clamp. Confirm the Chapter 1 area record, Chapter 2 PPE, and Chapter 3 equipment record are current.
  3. Prepare the coupon to clean bright metal. Confirm the weld zone matches the cleanliness standard from Chapter 4.
  4. Mark parallel bead lines and start and stop points. Space bead lines per the shop procedure and confirm spacing with a steel rule.
  5. Secure the coupon flat with stable access. Confirm the coupon cannot shift during welding.

F. Machine and Electrode Setup

  1. Attach the work clamp to clean metal. Position the clamp close to the coupon on sound, unpainted metal.
  2. Select and inspect the approved electrodes. Reject electrodes with damaged, moist, or contaminated coating.
  3. Set the machine to the approved starting amperage. Confirm polarity matches the electrode manufacturer's requirement.
  4. Record machine, setting, electrode, polarity, and coupon identification. Complete the bead-series log before the first arc.

G. Running the Bead Series

  1. Assume a balanced position allowing uninterrupted travel. Confirm the body position supports the full bead length without repositioning mid-bead.
  2. Arrange the lead so it does not pull the holder or contact hot metal. Check lead slack before striking the arc.
  3. Strike the arc at the first start point. Use the approved start method from Chapter 7.
  4. Establish the puddle before beginning travel. Confirm a stable puddle is visible before advancing the electrode.
  5. Maintain the approved arc length. Keep arc length consistent from start to finish.
  6. Maintain the approved work angle relative to the coupon. Hold the specified angle without drifting through the bead.
  7. Maintain the approved travel angle. Confirm the electrode leads or trails per the shop procedure.
  8. Travel along the marked line without weaving unless the exercise specifies otherwise. Follow the marked line to the stop point.
  9. Observe puddle edges and slag location. Use puddle shape and slag trailing to confirm fusion at both edges of the bead.
  10. Maintain a consistent travel speed. Avoid speeding up or slowing down mid-bead.
  11. Fill the crater at the endpoint through the approved method. Use the crater-fill technique specified in the shop procedure.
  12. Break the arc without creating an uncontrolled arc strike. Lift the electrode cleanly away from the coupon.

H. Cooling, Cleaning, and Repeat

  1. Place the holder in the insulated rest. Confirm the holder cannot contact grounded metal.
  2. Mark the coupon hot and allow safe cooling. Do not touch or measure the bead until it is safe to handle.
  3. Remove slag completely from the bead. Wear eye and face protection during chipping.
  4. Brush the bead and adjacent area. Use the approved wire brush for mild steel.
  5. Inspect bead straightness, width, reinforcement, ripple spacing, start, stop, undercut, overlap, porosity, slag inclusion indicators, and spatter. Compare against the acceptance sample.
  6. Measure bead width and length using approved gauges or tools. Record measurements in the bead-series log.
  7. Compare the bead with the project acceptance sample. Note any deviation.
  8. Identify whether defects relate to current, arc length, angle, travel speed, contamination, or electrode handling. Record the probable cause for any defect found.
  9. Adjust one parameter or technique variable at a time. Do not change more than one variable between beads.
  10. Run the next bead parallel to the first with consistent spacing. Confirm spacing against the marked lines.
  11. Repeat across the coupon without overlapping beads unless specified. Continue until the required number of beads is complete.
  12. Allow machine cooling within duty-cycle requirements. Pause between beads as required by the machine's rated duty cycle.

I. Final Review and Closeout

  1. Inspect the full coupon for heat distortion and arc strikes. Check flatness against the table and inspect for stray arc marks.
  2. Select the best and weakest bead for comparison. Identify which bead best matches the acceptance sample and which shows the most deviation.
  3. Prepare brief corrective-action notes. Record what would change to improve the weakest bead.
  4. Obtain instructor or qualified evaluator review. Present the coupon and bead-series log for review.
  5. Record final settings, bead measurements, defects, corrections, and competency result. Complete the bead-series log and inspection checklist.
  6. Shut down the station, store electrodes, coil leads, and complete fire watch. Follow the Chapter 1 shutdown and fire-watch requirements.

Common Bead Inspection

Inspect every bead using the same checks regardless of which position it occupies on the coupon.

  • Straightness check: confirm the bead follows the marked line within the stated tolerance.
  • Profile check: confirm width, reinforcement, and ripple spacing stay consistent along the full bead length.
  • Start/stop check: confirm the start shows full fusion and the stop shows a filled crater.
  • Cleanliness check: confirm slag is completely removed and spatter is within the stated limit.
  • Defect check: confirm no undercut, overlap, visible porosity, or arc strikes are present.
  • Documentation check: confirm settings, measurements, and inspection results are recorded before moving to the next bead.

Complete the Straight Bead Inspection Checklist in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 9.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Bead Width Varies Along the Length of a Single Bead

Probable cause: travel speed was not held constant, or arc length drifted during travel.
Confirmation: measure width at three points along the bead and compare against the recorded arc-length and travel-speed notes.
Correction: reduce speed variation and hold a consistent arc length on the next bead; adjust only travel speed or arc length, not both at once.
Reinspection: measure width at the same three points on the corrected bead and compare.

Undercut Appears Along One Toe of the Bead

Probable cause: the work angle favored one side of the bead, or travel speed was too fast for the set amperage.
Confirmation: compare the recorded work angle against the approved procedure and review travel speed on the affected bead.
Correction: center the work angle and reduce travel speed slightly on the next bead, adjusting only one variable.
Reinspection: inspect both toes of the corrected bead for undercut.

The Bead Start Shows a Cold, Unfused Spot

Probable cause: travel began before the puddle was fully established at the start point.
Confirmation: review the start technique against Chapter 7's puddle-establishment step.
Correction: pause briefly at the start point to establish the puddle before beginning travel on the next bead.
Reinspection: inspect the new start for fusion before continuing the series.

Surface Porosity Appears in Isolated Spots

Probable cause: contamination on the coupon surface, damaged electrode coating, or arc length that was too long in that section.
Confirmation: inspect the coupon surface near the defect and inspect unused electrodes from the same package for coating damage.
Correction: clean the coupon surface, replace damaged electrodes, and shorten arc length on the next bead.
Reinspection: inspect the corrected bead for porosity along its full length.

Beads Are Not Evenly Spaced Across the Coupon

Probable cause: bead lines were not measured and marked before welding began.
Confirmation: measure actual spacing between completed beads and compare against the marked layout.
Correction: re-mark remaining bead lines with a steel rule before continuing the series.
Reinspection: measure spacing on the next bead against the corrected layout.

The Machine Shuts Down or Alarms During the Series

Probable cause: continuous welding exceeded the machine's rated duty cycle.
Confirmation: check the duty-cycle rating recorded in the Chapter 3 equipment-readiness record against the elapsed arc time.
Correction: allow the machine to cool for the period specified by the manufacturer before resuming.
Reinspection: confirm normal machine operation before striking the next arc.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with an inspected, documented coupon, not a finished project. Housekeeping here protects the bead-series record for the joint welding that follows in Chapter 9.

  • Confirm the electrode holder is in the insulated rest and the machine is shut down
  • Store remaining electrodes dry and identified per the shop's consumable-control procedure
  • Coil leads without sharp bends and route them away from foot traffic
  • Sweep or vacuum slag chips and abrasive residue from the work surface
  • Store the completed bead-series log and inspection checklist with the coupon
  • Confirm the fire-watch period from Chapter 1 is complete before leaving the area
