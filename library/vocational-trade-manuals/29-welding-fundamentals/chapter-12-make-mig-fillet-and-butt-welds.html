Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to fit, tack, weld, clean, dimension, and inspect three approved GMAW joint types — a lap joint, a T-joint, and a square-butt joint — in the flat or horizontal position. You will move directly from the stringer-bead control built in Chapter 11 into joints that must hold fit-up, fuse two members, and pass a dimensional and visual inspection.

You will prepare joint surfaces, fit each joint to the specified overlap, angle, or root gap, place and inspect tack welds, weld each joint in a planned sequence that balances heat, clean and inspect the finished welds, check approved penetration indicators on the butt joint, and complete a corrective-action plan for any defect before final documentation.

This chapter assumes the GMAW station setup from Chapter 10 and the flat-position bead control from Chapter 11 are already demonstrated. It does not introduce new machine setup, new wire installation, or new gas-system procedures; those remain governed by Chapter 10.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • WF-067 — Prepare joint edges for lap, T-, and square-butt GMAW joints.
  • WF-068 — Tack weld lap, T-, and square-butt assemblies to the specified fit-up.
  • WF-069 — Weld lap joints with correct gun angle, travel, and fusion at both members.
  • WF-070 — Weld T-joints with correct gun angle, travel, and fusion at both toes.
  • WF-071 — Weld butt joints with correct travel and puddle control.
  • WF-072 — Inspect penetration indicators and bead profile against approved acceptance criteria.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 10 configured the GMAW station. Chapter 11 built stable bead control on flat coupons using that station. This chapter combines both into welded joints — the same lap and T-joint configurations you already welded with SMAW in Chapter 9, now performed with GMAW, plus a square-butt joint that introduces root-gap fit-up and penetration-indicator inspection for the first time in this book.

The fit-up, tacking, sequencing, and inspection skills demonstrated here carry directly into Chapter 13's flux-cored welding, Chapter 16's assembly fit-up and tacking, and the Chapter 20 capstone, which uses SMAW or GMAW fillet welds on a complete frame assembly.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • preparing lap, T-, and square-butt joint edges on identified mild-steel coupons to clean metal
  • fitting each joint to the specified overlap, angle, root gap, and offset using fixtures, stops, shims, clamps, and squares
  • placing, inspecting, and correcting tack welds before final welding
  • welding each joint using the approved GMAW parameters, gun angle, and travel technique established in Chapters 10 and 11
  • inspecting completed joints for profile, size, fusion indicators, and approved penetration indicators
  • identifying probable causes of defects and completing an approved corrective-action or repair plan

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • perform destructive testing — break, bend, or section tests — unless the chapter specification for a given coupon set explicitly includes it, and only with guards, tools, and instructor control in place
  • claim internal fusion or complete penetration from visual appearance alone; penetration indicators are inspected, not treated as proof of internal soundness
  • weld structural, code-controlled, pressure, lifting, or vehicle-frame joints; every coupon produced in this chapter is a noncritical training piece
  • invent a repair procedure for any joint; repairs use only the approved corrected setup and instructor-controlled defect removal

Any joint, tack, or completed weld that fails the acceptance standards in this chapter must be corrected or the coupon remade before the chapter's records are completed.

Trade Note

A tack weld that looks fine from the outside can still hide a crack, poor fusion, or trapped porosity that will carry directly into the final weld. Inspecting and, where necessary, removing a bad tack before welding is not optional housekeeping — it is the point in the sequence where a hidden defect is cheapest to catch.

Setup Standard

Do not strike an arc on any joint in this chapter until the welding-area readiness record from Chapter 1 is current for this session, the GMAW station has passed the Chapter 10 setup inspection, and the specific joint has passed its post-tack fit-up check. A joint that shifts after tacking must be corrected before final welding begins.

Shop Tip

Recheck overlap, angle, gap, and squareness immediately after tacking, not only before it. Tack welds pull metal as they cool, and a joint that was square before the first tack can move enough to fail the acceptance standard by the time the last tack is placed.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every joint in this chapter follows the same sequence: prepare, fit, tack, inspect the tacks, recheck fit-up, weld, clean, inspect, and correct. The joint geometry changes between the lap, T-, and butt configurations, but the sequence does not.

Lap jointOverlap set by rule or stop; fillet weld along the overlapping edge
T-jointVertical member set to the specified angle against the horizontal member; fillet weld at one or both toes
Square-butt jointTwo edges aligned with a specified root gap and offset; groove weld along the joint line
Tack sequencePlaced at approved locations, inspected, and incorporated or removed before final welding

Fit-Up Before Tacking

Fit-up is established and verified before the first tack is placed, not adjusted after welding has already begun. Clamps, fixtures, and squares hold the joint in position while tacks are placed, and the fit-up is rechecked after tacking because tack welds can pull the joint out of tolerance as they cool.

Measurement Cue — Recheck After Every Tack Sequence

Recheck lap overlap, T-joint angle, butt gap, offset, and flatness after placing the tack welds, not only before them. If the joint has moved outside the specified tolerance, correct the fit-up before adding any final weld metal.

Critical Safety Cue — Defective Tack Welds

Do not weld over a tack that shows cracking, porosity, poor fusion, or contamination. Remove a defective tack using the approved method before continuing. A defective tack incorporated into a final weld carries its defect into the finished joint.

Field Safety Cue — Clamp Placement and Gun Access

Position clamps so they hold the joint securely without blocking the gun's approach angle or your view of the puddle. Confirm you can reach the full weld length before striking the arc, not partway through the pass.

Code Alert — Noncritical Training Joints Only

The lap, T-, and butt joints produced in this chapter are noncritical training coupons. They are not qualified structural, pressure, or code welds and must not be represented as such regardless of how they inspect.

E. Joint Review and Surface Preparation

  1. Review joint drawings, material thickness, gaps, overlap, alignment, weld size, length, wire, gas, polarity, and parameters. Confirm every value against the chapter coupon specification before cutting or cleaning any coupon.
  2. Prepare all joint surfaces to clean metal. Remove rust, mill scale, oil, and contamination from every weld and faying surface identified in the drawing.
  3. Measure and label each coupon set. Mark each member with a traceable identifier for the lap, T-, and butt-joint sets before fit-up begins.

F. Fit-Up

  1. Fit the lap joint to the approved overlap. Set the overlap distance from the chapter coupon specification using a rule, stop, or fixture.
  2. Fit the T-joint to the specified angle and alignment. Hold the vertical member against the horizontal member at the specified angle using a square and fixture.
  3. Fit the butt joint to the specified root gap and offset. Set both values using shims, stops, or a fixture and confirm both members lie flat against the table.
  4. Use fixtures, stops, shims, clamps, and squares to maintain fit-up. Confirm every joint stays within tolerance before the first tack.
  5. Confirm clamps do not block gun access or create unstable restraint. Reposition any clamp that blocks the planned gun path along the full weld length.
  6. Attach the work clamp to clean metal. Position the clamp on sound base metal close to the joint being tacked.

G. Machine Verification and Tacking

  1. Verify gas, wire, tip, nozzle, polarity, voltage, and wire-feed speed. Confirm the station matches the parameters recorded on the joint traveler before tacking.
  2. Place tack welds at the approved locations. Tack each joint at the positions specified in the chapter coupon specification.
  3. Inspect tack size, fusion, porosity, cracking, and distortion. Examine every tack before proceeding to the next.
  4. Remove and replace defective tacks before final welding. Use the approved method to remove any tack that fails inspection, then replace it and reinspect.
  5. Recheck lap overlap, T-joint angle, butt gap, offset, and flatness. Confirm the joint remains within tolerance after the full tack sequence is complete.
  6. Plan weld order to balance shrinkage. Identify the sequence for each joint that distributes heat input and limits angular distortion before striking the first final-weld arc.

H. Final Welding

  1. Weld the lap-joint fillet using the approved gun angle and travel. Apply the gun angle and travel technique established in Chapter 11 along the full joint length.
  2. Maintain fusion at both members. Direct the arc so both the overlapping and underlying members show visible fusion along the weld toe.
  3. Inspect and clean the lap joint. Remove spatter and inspect the completed fillet before moving to the next joint.
  4. Weld the T-joint fillet with attention to both toes. Maintain the specified work and travel angle so both the vertical and horizontal member toes show fusion.
  5. Use the approved sequence if welding multiple sides. Follow the planned order from Section G when the specification requires welds on both sides of the T-joint.
  6. Inspect and clean the T-joint. Remove spatter and inspect both completed fillets.
  7. Weld the butt joint using the approved travel and puddle control. Maintain a stable puddle along the full joint length using the technique from Chapter 11.
  8. Do not weave unless the procedure specifies it. Use a straight stringer pass unless the chapter coupon specification calls for a weave technique.
  9. Control starts, stops, and crater fill. Apply the approved crater-control method at the end of each weld pass.
  10. Allow joints to cool in the designated area. Mark each joint hot and move it to the hot-metal cooling zone established in Chapter 1.

I. Cleaning, Inspection, Testing, and Records

  1. Remove spatter without reducing weld or base-metal dimensions. Use a wire brush, scraper, or chipping tool that does not gouge the weld face or toes.
  2. Inspect all joints for profile, size, length, tie-in, undercut, overlap, porosity, crater, start, stop, and distortion. Complete this inspection on all three joints before recording results.
  3. Use approved fillet and dimensional gauges. Measure fillet leg size on the lap and T-joints and record the results.
  4. Inspect the reverse side of the butt coupon for approved penetration indicators without claiming internal fusion from appearance alone. Compare the reverse-side condition with the approved acceptance sample.
  5. Perform an approved break, bend, or section test only when the chapter specification includes it. Do not perform destructive testing unless the coupon specification explicitly calls for it.
  6. Do not perform destructive testing without guards, tools, and instructor control. Stop and obtain instructor authorization before any destructive test.
  7. Compare results with the approved acceptance sample. Hold each completed joint against the acceptance sample identified in the chapter coupon specification.
  8. Identify probable causes of each defect. Connect any observed porosity, undercut, or profile issue to a likely machine, parameter, technique, or fit-up cause.
  9. Prepare a corrective-action plan before repair. Define the defect-removal method and corrected setup before any repair welding begins.
  10. Repair only approved defects with adequate remaining material. Confirm sufficient base-metal thickness remains before removing and rewelding a defect.
  11. Reinspect repaired areas. Apply the same inspection criteria used on the original weld.
  12. Record fit-up, tacks, parameters, sequence, dimensions, defects, tests, repairs, and final acceptance. Complete the joint traveler for all three coupons.
  13. Complete gas shutdown, machine shutdown, hot-metal control, and fire watch. Close the cylinder, isolate the machine, confirm all coupons are marked or verified cool, and complete the fire-watch period.

Common Joint Inspection

Inspect all three completed joints using the same core checks, adjusted for each joint's geometry.

  • Fit-up check: confirm the recorded overlap, angle, gap, and offset matched the specification through tacking and final welding.
  • Tack check: confirm every tack was inspected and any defective tack was removed and replaced before final welding.
  • Fusion check: confirm both members show fusion at the toe on the lap and T-joints, and confirm the butt joint shows a continuous, tied-in bead.
  • Dimensional check: confirm fillet size, weld length, and profile meet the chapter's gauged criteria.
  • Penetration-indicator check: confirm the butt joint's reverse side was inspected against the approved acceptance sample without overstating what the visual check proves.
  • Defect check: confirm any identified defect has a documented probable cause and, where repaired, a completed reinspection.

Complete the GMAW Joint Inspection Checklist in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 13.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Joint Has Moved Out of Tolerance After Tacking

Probable cause: tack-weld shrinkage pulled the joint out of alignment, or clamps were released before the fit-up was rechecked.
Confirmation: measure overlap, angle, gap, or offset against the chapter coupon specification immediately after the tack sequence.
Correction: remove the affected tack using the approved method, reposition the joint to the specified fit-up, and retack.
Reinspection: repeat the fit-up check before final welding begins.

The Lap or T-Joint Fillet Shows Fusion at Only One Member

Probable cause: the gun angle directed too much heat toward one member, or travel speed was too fast to allow the arc to reach both surfaces.
Confirmation: compare the weld toe on both members against the acceptance sample and check the recorded gun angle and travel speed.
Correction: adjust gun angle toward the member showing incomplete fusion and slow travel speed slightly under instructor direction.
Reinspection: run a short verification bead on matching scrap before rewelding the joint.

The Butt Joint Shows Excessive or Inconsistent Reverse-Side Reinforcement

Probable cause: root gap was set outside the specified tolerance, or voltage and wire-feed speed were not matched to the actual gap present.
Confirmation: measure the root gap on the remaining coupon stock and compare recorded parameters with the approved starting range for that gap.
Correction: remake the coupon with the root gap set to the specified tolerance, or adjust parameters within the approved range under instructor review.
Reinspection: repeat the penetration-indicator check on the corrected coupon.

Spatter Removal Has Left Gouges or Thinned Base Metal

Probable cause: a scraper or grinder was used with excessive pressure or the wrong tool for the amount of spatter present.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area for reduced base-metal thickness or a rough surface beyond normal spatter removal.
Correction: use a wire brush or lighter scraping technique for remaining spatter and stop grinding if base-metal thickness is affected.
Reinspection: measure remaining base-metal thickness at the affected location and compare with the coupon specification.

A Defect Is Identified but the Cause Is Not Clear

Probable cause: more than one variable — parameter, technique, fit-up, or contamination — changed between the acceptable and defective welds.
Confirmation: compare the joint traveler entries for the defective weld against a previously accepted weld from this chapter.
Correction: do not repair the joint until a single probable cause is identified; consult the instructor if more than one variable changed at once.
Reinspection: after identifying the cause, repeat the affected weld with only that variable corrected and reinspect before repairing the original.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with three inspected and documented practice joints, not a completed assembly. Housekeeping here protects both the coupons and the station for Chapter 13.

  • Close the shielding-gas cylinder valve and relieve line pressure as required
  • Shut down the GMAW machine and isolate input power
  • Coil the gun cable and work lead without sharp bends
  • Confirm all three coupons are cool or clearly marked before leaving the hot-metal cooling zone
  • Remove spatter, wire clippings, and abrasive residue from the work table and floor
  • Store the completed joint traveler with your other project records
