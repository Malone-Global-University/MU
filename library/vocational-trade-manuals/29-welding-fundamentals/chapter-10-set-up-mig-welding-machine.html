Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to configure one GMAW (MIG) machine for approved mild-steel practice welding. You will install a wire spool in the correct feed direction, set up the wire-feed drive system, connect and leak-check the shielding-gas system, verify machine polarity for the selected solid wire, set starting voltage and wire-feed speed from the approved chart, attach the work clamp, and complete a documented wire-feed and gas-flow inspection before running a short test weld under instructor authorization.

This chapter does not teach welding technique. It ends with a verified, ready-to-weld GMAW station and a documented setup record. Running practice beads on that station is taught in Chapter 11.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • WF-055 — Install a wire spool in the correct feed direction with approved drive-roll tension and spool brake settings.
  • WF-056 — Set up the wire-feed system, including drive rolls, guides, liner, and gun components matched to the wire.
  • WF-057 — Connect and leak-check the shielding-gas system for the approved process and material.
  • WF-058 — Set voltage and wire-feed speed from the approved starting chart or procedure.
  • WF-059 — Verify machine polarity for the selected solid wire before energizing the station.
  • WF-060 — Perform a documented MIG setup inspection, including a wire-feed and gas-flow check and a short authorized test weld.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 1 established the welding area, Chapter 2 staged your PPE, and Chapter 3 identified and inspected the station's power source, leads, clamp, gun, and consumables. This chapter builds directly on that equipment-readiness record: you are configuring the same GMAW station for the specific wire, gas, and parameters required by the practice project in Chapter 11.

The setup you complete here — wire installed, feed system tuned, gas leak-checked, polarity verified, parameters set, and a passed test weld — is the setup you return to at the start of Chapter 11 and Chapter 12, and it is the model for the GMAW portion of the Chapter 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • installing an identified solid GMAW wire spool in the correct feed direction with approved drive-roll and brake settings
  • setting up the wire-feed drive rolls, guides, liner, and gun components to match the wire type and diameter
  • connecting, securing, and leak-checking the shielding-gas cylinder, regulator, flowmeter, and hose system
  • verifying machine polarity and setting starting voltage and wire-feed speed from the approved chart or procedure
  • performing a wire-feed and gas-flow check and a short authorized test weld on matching scrap
  • completing and recording the GMAW setup inspection

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • weld a practice bead or joint beyond the short authorized test weld described in this chapter; bead practice is taught in Chapter 11
  • open the machine enclosure or service internal components beyond normal operator setup
  • select a wire, gas, or parameter range other than the one identified by the chapter project specification, machine manufacturer, or wire manufacturer
  • proceed with a station that has not first passed the Chapter 3 equipment-readiness inspection

Any wire-feed, gas, polarity, or parameter item that fails the setup inspection in this chapter must be corrected before the test weld is authorized.

Trade Note

A GMAW station is not ready because wire is loaded and gas is connected. It is ready only after the feed system runs smoothly under no-arc conditions, the gas system has been leak-checked, and a short test weld confirms the machine's actual response — rather than only its displayed settings.

Setup Standard

Do not energize the station's output, feed wire past the contact tip, or open the shielding-gas cylinder valve until the Chapter 1 area readiness record and the Chapter 3 equipment-readiness record are both in effect for this station. Do not strike the short test weld arc in this chapter without instructor authorization.

Shop Tip

Feed the wire fully through the gun before installing the contact tip. Feeding against an installed tip is a common cause of birdnesting at the drive rolls and makes the true cause of a feed problem harder to diagnose later.

Reference Conventions

Establish these references before setup begins, and do not change them without re-running the affected portion of the setup inspection.

Common Field Fundamentals

A GMAW setup follows the same authorize-inspect-install-verify-test sequence every time, regardless of wire type or shop: de-energize before opening the wire compartment, match every feed-system component to the wire, connect and leak-check gas before opening the cylinder valve for use, verify polarity before energizing output, and confirm the whole system with a no-arc feed check before ever striking a test arc.

Wire-Feed System and Gun Match

Every component the wire passes through — drive-roll groove, inlet guide, liner, diffuser, contact tip, and nozzle — must match the wire type and diameter identified for this project. A mismatch at any one point is a common cause of birdnesting, erratic feed, or arc instability that is easy to misdiagnose as a machine fault.

Measurement Cue — Trim Wire Extension Before the Test Weld

After installing the contact tip, trim the wire to the approved extension length before the wire-feed and gas-flow check. Confirm contact-tip-to-work distance separately once the coupon is in position. Do not estimate stickout by eye once the gun is already positioned over the coupon.

Critical Safety Cue — Hands Clear of Drive Rolls and Pinch Points

Keep hands clear of the drive rolls and feed-path pinch points while feeding wire, closing the drive rolls, or clearing a jam. Never feed wire toward your hand, face, or body. Trigger the gun in a safe direction, away from the face and body, during any feed check.

Field Safety Cue — Gas Cylinder Security Before Connection

Confirm the shielding-gas cylinder is upright and secured before connecting the regulator. Verify the cylinder label and regulator compatibility before opening the valve. Open the valve from a safe position, according to the gas and regulator manufacturer's instructions.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Drive-Roll Tension and Spool Brake

Set drive-roll tension and spool brake only to the starting condition identified by the machine manufacturer for the installed wire type and diameter. Excess drive-roll tension deforms wire and accelerates liner wear; excess spool brake drag causes feed motor strain and inconsistent feed speed.

E. Preparation — Review and Machine Isolation

  1. Review the machine manual, approved wire, wire diameter, shielding gas, polarity, contact tip, material thickness, and starting parameters. Confirm every value against the chapter project specification before touching the machine.
  2. Complete work-area, ventilation, fire, cylinder, and PPE checks. Confirm the Chapter 1 area readiness record remains in effect and required PPE is staged.
  3. Turn off and isolate the welding machine before opening the wire compartment. Confirm the machine is fully de-energized before proceeding.

F. Wire Installation and Feed-System Setup

  1. Inspect the wire feeder, spool hub, brake, drive rolls, guides, liner, gun connection, and compartment cleanliness. Clear any debris before installing wire.
  2. Verify the drive-roll groove matches the wire type and diameter. Replace mismatched drive rolls before continuing.
  3. Inspect the wire spool label and wire condition. Confirm classification and diameter match the chapter project specification.
  4. Do not install rusty, dirty, kinked, wet, or unidentified wire. Set aside and tag any spool that fails this check.
  5. Install the spool in the correct feed direction. Confirm the wire pays off without twisting against the hub.
  6. Secure the spool retaining device. Confirm the spool cannot shift or fall during feeding.
  7. Adjust spool brake only to the approved starting condition. Use the machine manufacturer's starting value; do not estimate.
  8. Release drive-roll pressure. Confirm the rolls are open before threading wire.
  9. Cut the wire end cleanly and remove bent or contaminated wire. Discard any deformed length before feeding.
  10. Feed the wire through the inlet guide, drive rolls, outlet guide, liner, and gun. Guide the wire by hand only up to the drive rolls.
  11. Keep hands clear of drive rolls and pinch points. Do not guide wire past the closed drive rolls with fingers.
  12. Close the drive rolls and apply only the required tension. Use the manufacturer's starting tension value.
  13. Verify the gun liner, diffuser, contact tip, and nozzle match the wire and process. Confirm bore size and thread type before installing.
  14. Remove the contact tip if required for initial wire feeding. Follow the machine manufacturer's sequence for first feed-through.
  15. Close the compartment before energizing where required. Confirm the compartment cover is secured.

G. Polarity, Gas Connection, and Leak Check

  1. Verify machine polarity for the selected solid wire. Confirm the required polarity from the wire manufacturer's information.
  2. Connect or confirm output terminals according to the manual. Do not change internal connections beyond normal operator setup.
  3. Secure the shielding-gas cylinder upright. Confirm the cylinder is chained or otherwise secured before connecting the regulator.
  4. Verify cylinder label and regulator compatibility. Confirm the gas identified on the label matches the approved process gas.
  5. Inspect regulator, flowmeter, hose, fittings, and gun gas connection. Replace any damaged component before connecting.
  6. Install the regulator using the approved clean fitting procedure. Do not use oil or grease on the fitting.
  7. Open the cylinder valve according to gas-specific instructions from a safe position. Stand to the side of the regulator gauge face while opening.
  8. Check all connections for leakage using the approved method. Never use a flame to check for a gas leak.
  9. Set initial gas flow using the approved starting range and actual shop conditions. Record the value on the setup form.
  10. Do not compensate for wind or leakage by uncontrolled high flow. Correct the cause instead of increasing flow beyond the approved range.

H. Parameter Setting and No-Arc Verification

  1. Trigger the gun in a safe direction to feed wire. Direct the gun away from your body and any bystander.
  2. Keep the gun away from the face and body during feeding. Maintain a safe stance throughout the check.
  3. Install the correct contact tip and trim wire to the approved extension. Cut the wire cleanly at the specified stickout length.
  4. Set the starting voltage and wire-feed speed from the approved chart or procedure. Record both values on the setup form.
  5. Confirm inductance, arc control, burnback, or other controls remain at approved settings. Do not adjust beyond the manufacturer's or procedure's starting values.
  6. Attach the work clamp to clean test metal. Confirm sound contact close to the intended test-weld location.
  7. Perform a gas-flow and wire-feed check without striking an arc. Trigger the gun briefly and observe both systems together.
  8. Listen for smooth feeding and inspect for birdnesting, slipping, surging, or leaks. Note any irregularity on the setup form.
  9. Correct feeder tension, spool brake, tip, liner, or guide problems before welding. Re-run the feed check after each correction.

I. Authorized Test Weld and Setup Record

  1. Complete a short test weld on matching scrap under instructor authorization. Confirm authorization before lowering the helmet and striking the arc.
  2. Inspect arc sound, bead, spatter, gas coverage, and machine response. Compare the result with the approved acceptance sample.
  3. Record machine, wire, gas, polarity, tip, flow, voltage, wire speed, and inspection result. Complete every field on the GMAW setup inspection form.
  4. Close the cylinder and shut down if the station is not proceeding directly to welding. Relieve line pressure and isolate the machine before leaving the station unattended.

Common Setup Inspection

Inspect the station using the same checks regardless of machine brand or shop layout.

  • Wire and drive check: confirm the spool is identified, correctly installed, and feeds smoothly through a full trigger pull.
  • Gun-match check: confirm liner, guides, contact tip, and nozzle all match the wire diameter and type.
  • Gas check: confirm correct gas, leak-checked connections, and approved starting flow.
  • Polarity check: confirm polarity matches the wire manufacturer's requirement before energizing output.
  • Parameter check: confirm voltage and wire-feed speed are set from the approved chart and recorded.
  • Test-weld check: confirm a short authorized test weld shows a stable arc, adequate gas coverage, and no feed disruption.

Complete the GMAW Setup Inspection Form in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 11.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Wire Birdnests at the Drive Rolls During the Feed Check

Probable cause: excess feed resistance downstream, such as a mismatched or dirty liner, a wrong-size contact tip, or a contact tip installed before initial wire feed-through.
Confirmation: release drive-roll pressure, clear the jammed wire, and inspect the liner, guides, and contact tip for obstruction or mismatch.
Correction: replace or clean the mismatched component and re-thread the wire following the approved feed-through sequence.
Reinspection: repeat the no-arc wire-feed check through a full trigger pull before continuing.

Wire Feeds but Slips at the Drive Rolls

Probable cause: drive-roll tension is set below the approved starting value, or the roll groove does not match the wire diameter.
Confirmation: compare the installed drive-roll groove size to the wire diameter and check the tension setting against the manufacturer's starting value.
Correction: install the correct drive rolls or increase tension only to the approved starting condition.
Reinspection: repeat the wire-feed check and confirm smooth, consistent feed without deforming the wire.

A Gas Connection Fails the Leak Check

Probable cause: a fitting was not fully tightened, a seal or washer is damaged, or the regulator was installed without the approved clean fitting procedure.
Confirmation: re-apply the approved leak-check method to each connection individually to isolate the source.
Correction: tighten, reseat, or replace the leaking fitting or seal.
Reinspection: repeat the full leak check on all connections before setting gas flow.

The Test Weld Shows Porosity or an Unstable Arc

Probable cause: inadequate shielding-gas coverage from low flow, a leaking connection, wind disturbance, or excessive contact-tip-to-work distance.
Confirmation: recheck gas flow against the approved starting range, repeat the leak check, and confirm wire extension and contact-tip-to-work distance.
Correction: correct gas flow, seal the leaking connection, add wind protection, or adjust stickout to the approved distance.
Reinspection: repeat the short test weld and reinspect bead appearance and gas coverage.

The Arc Will Not Start or the Machine Trips on Output

Probable cause: polarity is set incorrectly for the installed wire, or the work clamp is not making sound contact with clean metal.
Confirmation: verify polarity against the wire manufacturer's information and inspect the work-clamp contact point for cleanliness and grip.
Correction: correct the polarity connection or move the work clamp to a clean, sound contact point.
Reinspection: repeat the polarity check and the short test weld under instructor authorization.

The Setup Inspection Form Is Missing Recorded Values

Probable cause: the test weld was completed before the wire, gas, polarity, tip, flow, voltage, and wire-speed values were fully recorded.
Confirmation: compare the setup inspection form against the required fields in Section D.
Correction: record every missing value from the current machine settings before proceeding to Chapter 11.
Reinspection: confirm the completed form is stored where it can be referenced before the next chapter begins.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, ready-to-weld station, not a completed practice project. Housekeeping here protects the setup record for Chapter 11.
