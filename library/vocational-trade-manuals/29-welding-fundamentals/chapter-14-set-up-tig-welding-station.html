Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to configure one gas tungsten arc welding (GTAW) station for approved mild-steel practice. You will assemble the torch, select and prepare the tungsten electrode, connect and leak-check the shielding gas, verify polarity and current controls, attach the work clamp, select and clean filler rod, and complete a documented setup inspection before any sustained arc is struck.

No practice beads are run in this chapter beyond a brief instructor-authorized test arc used to verify that the station is functioning. Running controlled autogenous and filler-added beads on mild steel is the work of Chapter 15, and that chapter depends entirely on the station you configure and document here.

This chapter assumes the welding area, PPE, machine, leads, work clamp, and gas system have already passed the readiness inspections in Chapters 1 through 3. This chapter adds the process-specific GTAW setup: torch assembly, tungsten preparation, and filler-rod handling.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapters 7 through 13 built SMAW, GMAW, and FCAW competence on the same authorize-inspect-connect-verify pattern used throughout this book. This chapter applies that same pattern to a process with different setup demands: a nonconsumable tungsten electrode, a separately handled filler rod, and gas shielding that must be leak-checked and flow-verified before any arc is struck. Chapter 15 uses the station documented here to run autogenous and filler-added beads. Every later GTAW reference in this book assumes the torch, tungsten, and gas system were set up according to this chapter.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • identifying and assembling torch body, back cap, collet, collet body or gas lens, cup, and tungsten
  • selecting, inspecting, grinding, and preparing tungsten for the approved procedure
  • connecting, leak-checking, and setting flow on the shielding-gas system
  • verifying polarity and setting current type, amperage limit, and other controls only as specified by the approved procedure
  • selecting, inspecting, cleaning, and staging filler rod
  • performing a brief instructor-authorized test arc to confirm arc stability, gas coverage, and machine response
  • completing a documented GTAW setup readiness inspection

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • run controlled practice beads; bead technique, puddle control, and filler coordination are taught in Chapter 15
  • service the machine enclosure, internal wiring, or any component beyond normal operator setup
  • select tungsten type, gas type, polarity, or current settings outside the approved GTAW procedure for mild steel
  • use a general-purpose grinding wheel to prepare tungsten

Trade Note

GTAW setup fails most often at the tungsten, not the machine. A contaminated, wrong-diameter, or incorrectly ground electrode will produce an unstable arc regardless of how correctly the gas, polarity, and current are set. Treat tungsten preparation as its own inspection point, separate from the rest of the station.

Setup Standard

Do not strike a sustained arc on this station until the GTAW setup readiness checklist is complete and the responsible person has authorized the brief test arc. Any tungsten contamination, gas leak, or unstable current response identified during setup must be corrected and reinspected before that authorization is given.

Shop Tip

Assemble the torch, prepare the tungsten, and connect the gas system in that order, and inspect each stage before moving to the next. Chasing a bad arc after everything is already assembled makes it far harder to tell whether the problem is the tungsten, the gas, or the machine setting.

Torch Assembly and Tungsten Contamination Control

Tungsten contamination and torch-component mismatch are the two most common causes of an unstable GTAW arc at setup. Torch components must be matched to the cup and gas-flow requirement of the approved procedure, and tungsten must be prepared only on equipment reserved for tungsten.

Measurement Cue — Tungsten Extension and Cup Size

Set tungsten extension according to the cup size, joint access, and the approved procedure. Do not extend tungsten beyond the length the cup and gas flow are designed to shield. Confirm the extension after installing the back cap, not before.

Critical Safety Cue — Machine Isolation Before Torch Assembly

Turn off and isolate the machine before assembling the torch, installing tungsten, or changing collet, cup, or back cap components. Do not assemble or adjust the torch on an energized station.

Field Safety Cue — Dedicated Tungsten Grinding Equipment

Prepare tungsten only on grinding equipment reserved for tungsten. Do not use a contaminated general-purpose grinding wheel. Control tungsten dust according to the material and shop requirements for the tungsten type in use.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Gas System and Fitting Compatibility

Verify gas label, regulator or flowmeter, hose, and fitting compatibility against the torch and cylinder manufacturer information before connecting the system. Use only clean, undamaged, compatible fittings.

E. Preparation — Procedure Review and Area Checks

  1. Review the machine manual, approved GTAW procedure, base metal, tungsten, filler rod, shielding gas, polarity, current control, and starting range. Confirm every value comes from the approved procedure, not personal preference.
  2. Complete hot-work, ventilation, cylinder, fire, and PPE checks. Confirm the Chapter 1 area readiness record and Chapter 2 PPE inspection remain current for this session.
  3. Inspect the power source, torch lead, work lead, work clamp, foot pedal or hand control, gas hose, and cooling system. Confirm each item is undamaged and correctly routed before proceeding.

F. Torch Identification and Isolation

  1. Identify torch body, back cap, collet, collet body or gas lens, cup, tungsten, and power connection. Confirm each component is present and matched to the approved procedure before assembly.
  2. Turn off and isolate the machine before assembling the torch. Confirm the machine remains de-energized for the full torch-assembly sequence.

G. Tungsten Selection and Preparation

  1. Select the approved tungsten type and diameter. Confirm the selection against the approved GTAW procedure's color code or marking.
  2. Inspect tungsten for contamination, splits, excessive erosion, mixed identification, and wrong diameter. Set aside any tungsten that fails this inspection.
  3. Prepare tungsten using the approved dedicated grinding equipment. Do not use grinding equipment shared with other materials.
  4. Direct grinding marks in the required orientation for the approved procedure. Confirm the orientation matches the procedure before continuing.
  5. Do not use a contaminated general-purpose grinding wheel. Stop and locate dedicated tungsten grinding equipment if none is available.
  6. Control tungsten dust according to material and shop requirements. Confirm the dust-control method matches the tungsten type in use.
  7. Prepare the specified point, taper, or end shape without overheating the tungsten. Inspect the ground end against the approved procedure's shape requirement.
  8. Clean the tungsten after grinding. Remove grinding debris before installing the tungsten in the torch.

H. Torch Assembly

  1. Install the collet, collet body or gas lens, cup, tungsten, and back cap. Confirm each component seats correctly before tightening.
  2. Set tungsten extension according to cup, joint access, and procedure. Measure the extension after the back cap is installed.
  3. Do not overtighten torch components. Confirm components are secure without deforming seals or threads.
  4. Verify torch parts are compatible and undamaged. Inspect the assembled torch before connecting gas.

I. Shielding-Gas Connection and Leak Check

  1. Secure the shielding-gas cylinder upright. Confirm the cylinder is chained or otherwise secured before connecting the regulator.
  2. Verify gas label, regulator or flowmeter, hose, and fitting compatibility. Confirm the gas matches the approved GTAW procedure before connecting.
  3. Connect the gas system using clean approved fittings. Do not use damaged, contaminated, or mismatched fittings.
  4. Open the cylinder from a safe position. Stand to the side of the regulator gauge face while opening the valve.
  5. Leak-check the system using the approved method. Never use a flame to check for gas leaks.
  6. Set initial gas flow from the approved procedure. Record the flow rate on the readiness checklist.
  7. Verify pre-flow and post-flow settings where adjustable. Confirm both settings match the approved procedure.

J. Polarity, Current Controls, and Work Clamp

  1. Verify machine polarity for mild-steel GTAW. Confirm the polarity setting matches the approved procedure before proceeding.
  2. Set current type, start method, amperage limit, slope, pulse, or other controls only as specified. Do not set a control to a value not supplied by the approved procedure.
  3. Connect and position the foot pedal or hand control. Confirm stable footing or hand position before the test arc.
  4. Attach the work clamp to clean sound metal. Position the clamp close to the test coupon.

K. Filler Rod Selection and Staging

  1. Select the approved filler-rod classification and diameter. Confirm the selection against the approved GTAW procedure.
  2. Inspect filler rod for rust, oil, paint, mixed identity, bent ends, and contamination. Set aside any rod that fails this inspection.
  3. Clean filler rod using the approved method. Remove any surface contamination before staging.
  4. Position filler rods in a clean labeled holder. Keep the holder within reach of the test coupon.

L. Test Arc and Setup Verification

  1. Prepare a clean matching scrap coupon. Confirm the coupon is identified mild steel and free of coating or contamination.
  2. Perform a gas-flow check without striking an arc. Confirm flow at the cup before energizing the arc.
  3. Verify torch cooling where applicable. Confirm the cooling system is functioning before the test arc.
  4. Strike a brief test arc under instructor authorization. Do not strike the test arc without the responsible person's authorization.
  5. Inspect arc stability, gas coverage, tungsten condition, and machine response. Confirm the arc is stable and the tungsten remains uncontaminated after the test.
  6. Stop for tungsten contamination, gas turbulence, leaks, overheating, or unstable current. Correct any failed condition before continuing.
  7. Complete the GTAW setup checklist. Verify every item in Section D against the physical station.
  8. Record machine, torch parts, tungsten, grind, extension, gas, polarity, controls, filler, and readiness. Confirm the record is complete before ending the session.

Common Setup Inspection

Inspect the station using the same checks regardless of tungsten diameter, cup size, or gas type in use.

  • Torch check: confirm every component is matched, correctly seated, and not overtightened.
  • Tungsten check: confirm type, diameter, grind, extension, and freedom from contamination.
  • Gas check: confirm cylinder security, fitting compatibility, leak-free connections, and correct flow, pre-flow, and post-flow settings.
  • Control check: confirm polarity and current controls match the approved procedure exactly.
  • Filler check: confirm filler rod is identified, clean, and correctly staged.
  • Test-arc check: confirm the brief test arc was authorized, stable, and did not contaminate the tungsten.

Complete the GTAW Setup Readiness Checklist in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 15.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Test Arc Is Erratic or Wanders

Probable cause: tungsten contamination from the base metal, filler rod, or an unclean grind; or a loose torch connection.
Confirmation: inspect the tungsten tip for a blue, black, or ball-shaped contaminated end and check torch fitting tightness.
Correction: regrind or replace the contaminated tungsten on the dedicated grinder and reseat torch connections.
Reinspection: repeat the tungsten condition check and the test arc under instructor authorization.

Shielding Gas Does Not Adequately Cover the Weld Zone

Probable cause: incorrect cup size for the flow rate, a leak in the gas line, or drafts in the work area disturbing the shielding envelope.
Confirmation: repeat the leak check on all fittings and confirm cup size against the approved procedure's flow recommendation.
Correction: replace damaged fittings, correct the cup and flow match, or relocate the test coupon away from drafts.
Reinspection: repeat the gas-flow check and leak check before the next test arc.

The Torch Overheats During the Brief Test Arc

Probable cause: the torch cooling system is not functioning, or the torch is rated below the current in use.
Confirmation: verify torch cooling per the manufacturer instructions and confirm the torch's rated current against the machine setting.
Correction: correct the cooling system or reduce current only within the approved procedure's range, or substitute the correctly rated torch.
Reinspection: repeat the torch-cooling verification before any further test arc.

Machine Current Response Does Not Match the Foot Pedal or Hand Control

Probable cause: the pedal or hand control is not correctly connected, or a control setting was changed outside the approved procedure.
Confirmation: verify the pedal or hand control connection and compare every current-related setting against the approved procedure.
Correction: reseat the control connection and reset any setting that does not match the approved procedure.
Reinspection: repeat the control check and confirm response during a brief authorized test arc.

Filler Rod Shows Contamination Before Use

Probable cause: filler rod was stored without protection from rust, oil, or mixed identification with other rod.
Confirmation: inspect the rod surface and packaging label against the approved GTAW procedure's classification and diameter.
Correction: clean the rod using the approved method or replace it with correctly identified stock.
Reinspection: repeat the filler-rod inspection before staging it in the holder.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, ready station, not a completed weld. Housekeeping here protects the setup record for Chapter 15.

  • Close the cylinder valve and relieve line pressure where required
  • Isolate the machine if the station is not proceeding directly to Chapter 15
  • Protect the torch, tungsten, and cup from impact and contamination
  • Store filler rod in its clean labeled holder
  • Store the signed GTAW setup readiness checklist where it can be referenced before Chapter 15
