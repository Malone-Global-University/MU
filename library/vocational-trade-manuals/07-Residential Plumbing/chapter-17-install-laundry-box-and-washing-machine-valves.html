Job Overview

This project qualifies you to rough-in, test, and document one approved recessed laundry outlet box, complete with hot and cold washing-machine valves, a designed trap and standpipe arrangement, and a vent connection built to the approved DWV pattern. The finished work product is one inspected laundry rough-in: a securely mounted box at the correct height and setback, a tested supply branch, a tested DWV branch, and a complete concealment record.

You will review the approved laundry plan; confirm framing and clearances; mark the box location and all piping routes; secure the box and framing support; install the trap, standpipe, and vent using the approved DWV fitting pattern; route and support hot and cold supply piping to listed washing-machine valves; install any required water-hammer arrestors or backflow protection; complete supply and DWV pressure tests; and protect the finished rough-in through wall closure.

This project depends on Chapter 1 work-area isolation and drain-down, Chapter 2 system mapping, the pipe-preparation skills from Chapter 3, the approved joining method taught in Chapters 4 through 6, and the PVC DWV assembly sequence from Chapter 8. It does not repeat those procedures in full; it applies them to one designed laundry outlet.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • PL-097 — Lay out the laundry box location, elevation, and piping routes from the approved laundry plan.
  • PL-098 — Secure the laundry box to framing at the specified height, level, and finished-wall setback.
  • PL-099 — Connect hot and cold water supplies to the box using the approved material and joining method.
  • PL-100 — Connect the standpipe to the approved trap, trap arm, and vent arrangement.
  • PL-101 — Install hot and cold washing-machine shutoff valves in the specified orientation.
  • PL-102 — Test the completed supply and DWV connections and document the laundry rough-in before concealment.

Where This Skill Fits

This project brings together the water-supply joining skills from Chapters 4 through 6, the fixture-stop and valve skills from Chapter 7, and the PVC DWV assembly sequence from Chapter 8, applied to a single dedicated appliance outlet. The same isolation, drain-down, and documentation discipline established in Chapter 1 controls how this rough-in begins and how it is protected until finish work.

The trap, standpipe, and vent design used here follows the same DWV fitting-pattern, slope, and venting principles taught in Chapters 8 and 9, and the completed pressure and DWV tests use the same test-and-document standard applied throughout the book. This project does not teach a new DWV design method; it applies the approved design supplied by the laundry plan.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing and laying out the approved laundry plan, box model, valve type, supply sizes, standpipe and trap design, vent route, drain size, and height requirements
  • securing the laundry box to framing at the specified location and setback
  • installing the approved trap, standpipe, and vent connection using the approved DWV fitting pattern and slope
  • routing and supporting hot and cold supply piping to the box
  • installing listed washing-machine valves in the specified orientation, plus any required water-hammer arrestors, backflow protection, or auxiliary pan connection
  • completing the required supply pressure test and DWV test before concealment

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • invent a standpipe height, trap size, trap-arm length, vent arrangement, or branch size that is not supplied by the approved laundry plan or adopted code
  • weaken or unsafely cut structural framing members to install the box or route piping
  • conceal any supply or DWV connection before the required pressure test, DWV test, and inspection have passed
  • proceed with a drain-flow test before the approved DWV system is complete and inspected

Any condition that conflicts with the approved laundry plan, adopted code, or manufacturer instructions must be resolved before work continues rather than substituted with an assumed design.

Trade Note

A laundry outlet is a dedicated appliance connection, not a generic sink drain. The standpipe height, trap seal, and vent relationship are sized for a washing-machine discharge pump, not for gravity fixture drainage. Do not substitute a trap, standpipe height, or vent arrangement from another chapter's project without the approved laundry-plan design.

Setup Standard

Do not cap, plug, or conceal any laundry-box supply or DWV connection until the approved pressure test and DWV test have both passed and the rough-in inspection has been obtained. Do not run a drain-flow test through the standpipe until the vent and trap arrangement have been completed exactly to the approved DWV design.

Shop Tip

Mark the box centerline, elevation, and every penetration on the framing before cutting anything. A laundry box moved even an inch after the trap and standpipe are roughed in usually means recutting DWV pipe rather than adjusting the box.

Common Field Fundamentals

A laundry rough-in fails most often when the box, trap, and supply piping are installed to convenient field dimensions instead of the approved plan. Every dimension in this project — box height, setback, standpipe height, trap-arm length, vent size — must come from the laundry plan or adopted code, never from an estimate made at the wall.

Design Fidelity and Test Sequencing

The DWV design supplied by the laundry plan controls the standpipe height, trap size, trap-arm length, and vent arrangement. Do not adjust these values to make the rough-in easier to build. Complete the supply pressure test and the DWV test independently, and do not run a drain-flow test through the standpipe until the vent and trap arrangement have passed inspection.

Measurement Cue — Box Height, Setback, and Standpipe Height Come From the Plan

Mark the box centerline, elevation, and finished-wall setback using the exact values in the approved laundry plan. Mark the standpipe height using the same source. Do not estimate these values from the appliance box, a similar prior installation, or a convenient stud location.

Code Alert — Standpipe, Trap, and Vent Design Are Not Optional Substitutions

Do not invent a standpipe height, trap size, trap-arm length, vent arrangement, or branch size that is not supplied by the approved plans or adopted code. If the plan is missing a required dimension, stop and obtain the missing design information before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Framing Openings and Structural Members

Confirm stud position with a stud finder before marking any penetration. Install required framing support without weakening structural members. Stop work and consult the framing design if the specified box location or piping route requires cutting a load-bearing member beyond an approved opening.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Valve Orientation and Auxiliary Devices

Install washing-machine valves in the hot-left, cold-right orientation unless the approved project specifies otherwise. Install water-hammer arrestors, backflow protection, drain funnels, or auxiliary pan connections only where the manufacturer instructions or approved plan require them, and install them exactly as specified.

E. Plan Review and Layout Definition

  1. Review the approved laundry plan, appliance dimensions, box model, valve type, supply sizes, standpipe and trap design, vent route, drain size, height requirements, and inspection sequence. Record every design value on the Laundry Rough-In Worksheet before marking anything.
  2. Confirm that framing is open and the wall location provides appliance, hose, electrical, dryer, and service clearances. Verify against the approved plan's stated clearance values.
  3. Verify that the DWV design has been approved; do not invent standpipe height, trap size, trap-arm length, vent arrangement, or branch size. Stop and obtain the missing design value if any of these is not supplied by the plan or adopted code.
  4. Mark box centerline, elevation, stud position, drain route, vent route, and supply entries. Confirm stud position with a stud finder before marking any penetration.

F. Framing Support and Box Installation

  1. Install required framing support without weakening structural members. Confirm the support method against the framing design before fastening.
  2. Secure the laundry box square, level, and at the specified finished-wall setback. Verify with a level and tape measure against the recorded design values.

G. DWV Rough-In: Trap, Standpipe, and Vent

  1. Install the approved trap and standpipe arrangement below the box. Confirm the standpipe height matches the recorded design value before proceeding.
  2. Connect the trap arm and vent using the approved DWV fitting pattern and slope. Follow the Chapter 8 assembly sequence for measuring, dry-fitting, priming, and solvent-cementing each joint.
  3. Support DWV piping and protect framing penetrations. Install supports at the specified intervals and protection plates at every penetration within the prohibited fastener zone.

H. Water-Supply Rough-In and Valve Installation

  1. Route hot and cold supply piping to the box using the approved material and joining method. Follow the applicable Chapter 4, 5, or 6 joining procedure exactly.
  2. Install protection plates and pipe supports. Confirm spacing meets the manufacturer- and code-controlled interval.
  3. Connect the listed washing-machine valves in the specified hot-left and cold-right orientation or approved project orientation. Verify orientation against the valve schedule before final tightening.
  4. Install any required water-hammer arrestors, backflow protection, drain funnel, or auxiliary pan connection. Install each device exactly as specified by the manufacturer instructions or approved plan.

I. Testing and Inspection

  1. Cap or plug supplies and DWV openings for testing. Confirm every cap and plug is secure before pressurizing or filling the system.
  2. Complete the required supply pressure test. Apply only the permitted test pressure and duration under the required supervision.
  3. Complete the required DWV test and inspection. Use the approved DWV test method and observe every joint for leakage.

J. Flush, Connect, and Operational Testing

  1. Flush the supply lines before connecting appliance hoses. Direct flushed water into approved containment.
  2. Install listed washing-machine hoses or leave documented capped connections for appliance installation. Record which condition applies on the worksheet.
  3. Operate both valves and inspect for leakage. Cycle each valve through its full range and observe the valve body, stem, and connections.
  4. Conduct a controlled drain-flow test only after the approved DWV system is complete. Confirm the vent and trap arrangement have passed inspection before running water through the standpipe.

K. Protection and Documentation

  1. Protect the box and connections during wall finishing. Cover exposed piping and the box opening before insulation, drywall, or finish work begins.
  2. Document concealed piping, tests, and inspection approval. Complete every field on the Laundry Rough-In Worksheet before concealment.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed laundry rough-in using the same checks regardless of box model or specific DWV design.

  • Layout check: confirm box centerline, elevation, and setback match the approved plan.
  • Framing check: confirm the box is square, level, and secured to structural backing without weakened framing.
  • DWV check: confirm standpipe height, trap size, trap-arm length, and vent arrangement match the approved design exactly.
  • Supply check: confirm piping is routed, supported, and protected without kinks or twists.
  • Valve check: confirm both valves are installed in the specified orientation and operate through their full range without leakage.
  • Test check: confirm both the supply pressure test and DWV test have passed before concealment.

Complete the Laundry Rough-In Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 18.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Box Location Does Not Match the Approved Plan

Probable cause: the centerline, elevation, or setback was marked from an estimate rather than the recorded plan values.
Confirmation: re-measure the installed box against the recorded design values on the worksheet.
Correction: relocate the box before any DWV or supply connection is made permanent; do not adjust the standpipe or trap to compensate for a mislocated box.
Reinspection: repeat the layout check against the approved plan.

Standpipe Height or Trap-Arm Length Does Not Match the Design

Probable cause: a pipe length was cut from an estimate rather than the plan-supplied dimension, or the trap was seated at the wrong elevation.
Confirmation: measure the installed standpipe height and trap-arm length against the recorded design values.
Correction: cut out and rebuild the affected DWV section to the approved dimension; do not force a mismatched length into place.
Reinspection: repeat the DWV dimension check and the required DWV test.

Supply Pressure Test Fails at a Connection

Probable cause: an incomplete joint in the applicable Chapter 4, 5, or 6 joining method, or a damaged fitting.
Confirmation: depressurize and inspect the failed connection using the method specific to the joining system in use.
Correction: remake the connection using the system-approved correction method; do not add external sealant to a leaking joint.
Reinspection: repeat the supply pressure test on the corrected connection and the full branch.

DWV Test Fails at the Trap, Standpipe, or Vent Connection

Probable cause: an incomplete solvent-cement joint, insufficient cure time, or a joint disturbed before curing was complete.
Confirmation: depressurize or drain the test water and inspect the joint for insertion depth, alignment, and cement coverage.
Correction: cut out and rebuild the failed joint following the Chapter 8 assembly sequence; do not smear solvent cement over a leaking joint.
Reinspection: allow the required cure time and repeat the DWV test.

Washing-Machine Valve Leaks or Will Not Fully Close

Probable cause: an incomplete connection to the supply piping, a damaged valve seat, or excessive tightening during installation.
Confirmation: isolate and depressurize the branch, then inspect the valve body, stem, and supply connection.
Correction: remake the connection or replace the valve using the manufacturer's specified procedure.
Reinspection: repeat the valve operational check and the supply pressure test.

Drain-Flow Test Backs Up or Drains Slowly

Probable cause: insufficient trap-arm slope, an obstructed vent, or a standpipe height that does not match the approved design.
Confirmation: verify slope, vent clearance, and standpipe height against the recorded design values before assuming an appliance-side cause.
Correction: correct the DWV arrangement to match the approved design; do not proceed to appliance connection until drainage performs to the approved design.
Reinspection: repeat the drain-flow test after correction.

Cleanup

This project ends with a tested, documented, and protected laundry rough-in ready for wall finishing or, where the schedule allows, appliance hookup.

  • Remove test caps only after both tests have passed and inspection approval is recorded
  • Clean solvent-cement residue and supply-joint debris from the box and surrounding framing
  • Confirm protection remains over the box opening and exposed piping before finish work begins
  • Label the hot and cold valves and the vent location for future reference
  • Store the Laundry Rough-In Worksheet with the project's concealed-work records
