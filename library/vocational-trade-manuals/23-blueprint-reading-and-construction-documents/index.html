23-Blueprint Reading and Construction Documents

This field manual provides practical instruction for reading, interpreting, organizing, and applying residential construction drawings and documents. Students learn to navigate plan sets, understand architectural and engineering drawings, coordinate building systems, identify conflicts, and prepare documents for field construction use.

Overview

Blueprint Reading and Construction Documents develops the skills needed to interpret residential construction plans from design through execution. Beginning with plan set organization and site plans, students progress through architectural drawings, structural plans, mechanical systems, specifications, construction details, document coordination, field markups, and trade scope development.

Learning Outcomes

  • Navigate and organize residential construction plan sets.
  • Interpret architectural drawings, dimensions, symbols, and notes.
  • Read site plans, foundation plans, and framing documents.
  • Understand elevations, sections, schedules, and construction details.
  • Interpret plumbing, electrical, HVAC, and roofing documents.
  • Understand specifications, general notes, and product requirements.
  • Identify conflicts between drawings and building systems.
  • Prepare plan markups for field construction use.
  • Create trade scope checklists from construction documents.
  • Apply blueprint reading skills to residential construction projects.
