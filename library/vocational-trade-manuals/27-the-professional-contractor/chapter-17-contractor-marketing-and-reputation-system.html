Job Overview

This project produces one approved contractor marketing and reputation system. The finished work product is a set of controlled documents — a brand-position statement, approved service descriptions, licensing and insurance statements, a project-photo consent form, a testimonial and review-request procedure, a complaint and negative-review response procedure, and a basic annual marketing plan — built entirely from the business model, service menu, and current licensing and insurance status established earlier in this book.

You will define the brand position, remove any claim that cannot be supported with specific evidence, build approved profile standards for every advertising channel the business uses, create consent-based photo and testimonial procedures, create an ethical review-request and negative-review response procedure, build a capacity-aligned annual marketing plan, and test the entire system on one service page, one project case study, one review request, and one referral campaign before approval.

This project does not create new services, new licenses, or new insurance coverage. It packages and presents, truthfully and within active authority, the business already defined and authorized in Chapters 1 through 16.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • PC-097 — Define a brand position supported by specific, producible evidence.
  • PC-098 — Create service descriptions drawn from the approved scope library with accurate geography, size, and exclusion statements.
  • PC-099 — Collect project photographs under a written consent procedure that protects customer privacy.
  • PC-100 — Request customer reviews through an ethical, non-coercive procedure.
  • PC-101 — Organize testimonials with an approval and editing rule that preserves the customer's meaning.
  • PC-102 — Build a basic, capacity-aligned marketing plan with measurable objectives.

Where This Skill Fits

This project depends directly on the Chapter 1 business model, the Chapter 3 licensing file, the Chapter 4 insurance file, and the Chapter 6 service menu and scope library: every claim, service description, and licensing statement produced here must trace back to those approved records. It also depends on the Chapter 12 sales pipeline for lead-volume history and the Chapter 18 job-cost system for the capacity data that keeps the marketing plan from generating more leads than the business can serve.

The marketing and reputation system built here feeds the Chapter 20 capstone operating system, where brand position, service claims, credentials, photo consent, testimonial rules, review requests, complaint responses, campaigns, and performance measurement are reviewed as one integrated component alongside every other chapter's system.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • defining the brand position and verifying supporting evidence for each promised advantage
  • building approved business-name, logo, color, typography, voice, and contact standards
  • creating service descriptions from the Chapter 6 scope library with accurate geography, project size, and exclusions
  • creating approved licensing and insurance statements with correct jurisdictional context
  • creating channel profile standards for website, directory, social, print, vehicle, sign, proposal, and email
  • creating a project-photo consent form and photo-library controls
  • creating testimonial and review-request procedures and a negative-review response procedure
  • creating a basic annual marketing plan with objectives, audience, channel, message, budget, owner, and measurement
  • testing the system on one service page, one project case study, one review request, and one referral campaign

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • advertise a service, geography, project size, license, or insurance status not currently authorized in the Chapter 1, Chapter 3, Chapter 4, and Chapter 6 records
  • condition a warranty response, discount, payment, or correction on a positive review
  • create, purchase, direct, or conceal a fake review, or use a deceptive before-and-after comparison
  • publish a claim, photograph, or testimonial without the required consent and professional review in Section H below

Any claim, photograph, or testimonial that cannot be supported by current authority, evidence, or consent must be removed before the system is approved.

Trade Note

Every claim in this chapter is checked against a document that already exists — the business model, the licensing file, the insurance file, or the scope library — before it is published. A claim invented for marketing purposes and not traceable to an approved record is the single most common source of the false-advertising, unauthorized-work, and lead-overload problems diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every claim, photograph, and testimonial published under this system starts from the same rule: nothing appears in a marketing channel unless it is traceable to an approved record and supported by evidence the business can produce on request. Getting this discipline right before the first service page or social post is published means the reputation system stays defensible as it scales.

Claim Verification Discipline

A candidate claim is only added to the marketing system after it is checked against the business model, license file, insurance file, or scope library and matched to a specific piece of evidence the business can produce today. A claim that fails this check is removed, not softened into vaguer language; it is only added back after the specific missing authority or evidence is obtained and documented.

Documentation Cue — Record Every Removed Claim

When a candidate claim is removed in Section E, record the claim, the reason it was removed, and the condition that would allow it to be reconsidered. Do not simply delete it. The removed-claim record protects the business from silently republishing an unsupported claim later.

Critical Compliance Cue — No Publication Without Consent and Authority

Do not publish a project photograph without the Section F consent form on file, a testimonial without the Section G approval, or a licensing or insurance statement not currently accurate. A claim, photograph, or testimonial that appears in a draft is not yet authorized for publication.

Operational Discipline Cue — One System, One Copy of Record

Maintain a single controlled copy of the approved marketing and reputation system in the company record system established in Chapter 2. Do not allow separate, informal versions of claims, photo permissions, or testimonial edits to circulate among owners, sales, or field staff.

Professional Advisor Cue — Claims and Consent Language Require Review

Advertising, telemarketing, privacy, and consumer-protection rules vary by jurisdiction and platform. Do not publish licensing statements, warranty references, discount offers, financing references, or consent language without the attorney or advertising-compliance review required in Section H.

E. Brand, Claims, and Channel Standards

  1. Import the approved business model, service area, service menu, licensing status, insurance statements, capacity, and customer profile. Record each source document and its current date on the brand-position statement template before continuing.
  2. Define the brand position in one clear statement. Write one sentence naming who the business serves and why it is chosen.
  3. Define the evidence supporting each promised advantage. For every advantage named in the brand position, record the specific, producible evidence supporting it.
  4. Remove claims that are vague, exaggerated, unverifiable, or outside authority. Move each removed claim to a documented removed-claims log with the reason and reconsideration condition.
  5. Create approved business-name, logo, color, typography, voice, and contact standards. Record each standard on the channel profile-standards template.
  6. Verify trade name, domain, social account, and advertising-name consistency. Confirm every public-facing name matches the Chapter 2 registered trade name.
  7. Create service descriptions from the Chapter 6 scope library. Draft one description per approved service using scope-library language, not new claims.
  8. State service geography, project size, exclusions, and qualification accurately. Match each description to the Chapter 1 geography and Chapter 6 project-size limits.
  9. Create approved licensing and insurance statements with jurisdictional context. Draft the exact statement wording using current Chapter 3 and Chapter 4 status.
  10. Do not imply that insurance guarantees workmanship or customer recovery. Review each insurance statement for this specific overreach and correct it.
  11. Create website, directory, social, print, vehicle, sign, proposal, and email profile standards. Complete one row per channel on the channel profile-standards template.
  12. Identify advertising, platform, accessibility, privacy, telemarketing, email, text, review, promotion, and consumer-protection rules requiring professional review. List each open compliance question and the professional responsible for answering it.

F. Photo Consent and Testimonial Procedures

  1. Create a project-photo consent form covering interior, exterior, address, people, possessions, schedule, and publication. Draft the form using the fields named in this step.
  2. Obtain permission before photography is used for marketing. State the point in the project timeline at which consent must be obtained and confirmed.
  3. Create photo-capture standards for safety, cleanliness, progress, details, before, after, and final work. List the required shot categories and the condition each must meet before use.
  4. Do not stage deceptive before-and-after comparisons or use work performed by others without disclosure. Add this rule directly to the photo-capture standards.
  5. Create a secure photo library with project, date, permission, scope, photographer, and usage rights. Define the library's field structure and access controls.
  6. Create testimonial and review-request procedures after legitimate service milestones. Name the specific project milestones at which a request may be sent.
  7. Do not condition warranty, discount, payment, or correction on a positive review. Add this prohibition directly to the request procedure.
  8. Do not create, purchase, direct, or conceal fake reviews. Add this prohibition directly to the request procedure.
  9. Create a testimonial approval and editing rule that preserves meaning. State the specific limits on shortening or editing a customer's words.

G. Complaint Response, Referral Sources, and Marketing Plan

  1. Create a complaint and negative-review response procedure. Draft the response timing, tone, and approval steps.
  2. Respond factually without revealing private customer or project information. Add this rule directly to the response procedure.
  3. Escalate threats, extortion, defamation, legal claims, safety, or insurance matters to professionals. Name the specific triggers and the professional each is routed to.
  4. Create referral-source categories such as customers, trades, suppliers, designers, property managers, search, and community. List each category to be tracked.
  5. Create a basic annual marketing plan with objectives, audience, channel, message, budget, owner, and measurement. Complete one row per planned campaign on the marketing-plan template.
  6. Align lead volume with estimating, field, and customer-service capacity. Compare the planned lead-volume target against the Chapter 18 capacity data.
  7. Do not generate more leads than the business can screen and serve professionally. Reduce the plan's targets if the comparison in the previous step shows a mismatch.
  8. Create campaign identifiers and source tracking. Assign a unique identifier to each planned campaign.
  9. Measure qualified leads, site visits, proposals, conversion, contract value, gross profit, acquisition cost, repeat work, referrals, reviews, and backlog. List each measurement the plan will track.
  10. Do not evaluate marketing only by likes, impressions, or gross revenue. Confirm the measurement list includes at least the qualified-lead and conversion figures from the previous step.
  11. Create a content calendar based on real services, education, maintenance, project stories, and seasonal needs. Draft a basic calendar referencing only approved services and claims.

H. Testing, Review, and Approval

  1. Review all claims, photographs, promotions, financing references, discounts, and testimonials before publication. Route the complete draft system to the reviewing professional.
  2. Test one service page, one project case study, one review request, and one referral campaign. Produce one sample of each using the approved templates and standards.
  3. Approve the marketing system and schedule quarterly reputation and compliance review. Sign and date the final system and set the first quarterly review date.

Common Model Inspection

Inspect the completed system and its supporting documents using the same checks regardless of which channel or campaign is involved.

  • Claim check: confirm every published claim traces to the business model, license file, insurance file, or scope library, and every removed claim is documented with a reason.
  • Consent check: confirm no photograph is used in marketing without a completed consent form on file.
  • Testimonial check: confirm every published testimonial preserves the customer's meaning and was collected without a conditioned benefit.
  • Complaint-response check: confirm the response procedure protects private information and names its escalation triggers.
  • Capacity check: confirm the marketing plan's lead-volume target does not exceed documented capacity.
  • Professional-review check: confirm at least one qualified reviewer's comments are recorded and addressed before approval.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Published Claim Cannot Be Supported

Probable cause: the claim was written directly into a channel without being checked against the business model, license file, insurance file, or scope library.
Confirmation: ask what specific evidence the business could produce today to support the claim; if none exists, the claim fails.
Correction: remove the unsupported claim from every channel where it appears, or replace it with a narrower claim the business can currently prove, and log the correction.
Reinspection: repeat the claim check on the corrected channel.

A Project Photograph Is Published Without Consent on File

Probable cause: a photograph was taken and used before the consent form was completed, or the completed form was not filed in the photo library.
Confirmation: search the photo library for a matching consent record for the photograph in question.
Correction: remove the photograph from publication until a completed consent form is obtained, or permanently remove it if consent cannot be obtained.
Reinspection: repeat the consent check on the entire active photo library.

A Testimonial Reads Differently Than the Customer Intended

Probable cause: the editing rule from Section F, Step 9 was not followed, or the testimonial was shortened in a way that changed its meaning.
Confirmation: compare the published testimonial against the original customer statement on file.
Correction: restore the original meaning, obtain the customer's confirmation of the corrected version, and retrain whoever edited it on the approval and editing rule.
Reinspection: repeat the testimonial-integrity check on all recently published testimonials.

A Review Response Discloses Private Customer Information

Probable cause: the response was drafted reactively without following the complaint and negative-review response procedure.
Confirmation: review the published response against the factual, privacy-protecting standard in Section G, Step 2.
Correction: edit or remove the disclosure where the platform allows, issue a corrected response, and route future responses through the assigned responsible manager.
Reinspection: repeat the complaint-response check on the corrected response and on the procedure's approval step.

Lead Volume Exceeds the Business's Screening and Delivery Capacity

Probable cause: the marketing plan's targets were set without comparison to Chapter 18 capacity data, or a campaign outperformed its planned volume without a corresponding capacity check.
Confirmation: compare current lead volume against the Chapter 12 pipeline and Chapter 18 capacity figures.
Correction: pause or scale back the overperforming channel, and revise the marketing plan's targets to match documented capacity.
Reinspection: repeat the capacity check on the revised plan before resuming the paused channel.

The System Was Approved Without Professional Review

Probable cause: a qualified reviewer was not identified early enough, or the review step was skipped to meet an internal publication deadline.
Confirmation: check the approval record for a named reviewer, review date, and recorded comments.
Correction: suspend publication of new claims, photographs, or testimonials under the system until a qualified reviewer's comments are obtained and addressed.
Reinspection: repeat the professional-review check before re-approving the system.
