Job Overview

This project produces one complete, documented closeout of a single residential contracting project. The finished work product is a closed project file containing a verified final punch list, corrected and re-inspected deficiencies, a reconciled final account, required lien and payment documentation, delivered warranty and maintenance materials, a signed customer acceptance record, and confirmation that every internal, subcontractor, and permit obligation attached to the project has been closed.

You will conduct a contractor pre-punch inspection before the customer ever walks the project, run the formal customer walkthrough, classify every item the customer raises, correct only legitimate contract deficiencies, reconcile allowances and changes into the final contract total, prepare and deliver the final invoice, assemble jurisdiction-specific lien and payment documentation, deliver warranty and manufacturer information, and archive the completed record.

This is the closeout project in the book. It depends directly on the contract scope written in Chapter 9, the change-order and allowance system built in Chapter 10, the job-startup packet from Chapter 13, the customer-communication discipline from Chapter 14, and the job-cost record from Chapter 18. It feeds directly into the Chapter 20 capstone, which requires this exact closeout process to run on a representative sample project.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • PC-109 — Conduct a final walkthrough that distinguishes contract deficiency, warranty item, maintenance item, preference, and unrelated condition.
  • PC-110 — Create a punch list with location, description, photograph, responsibility, and acceptance criterion for every accepted item.
  • PC-111 — Verify each correction against the same acceptance criterion used to accept the item.
  • PC-112 — Prepare a final invoice reconciled to the contract, approved changes, allowances, credits, and prior payments.
  • PC-113 — Deliver warranty and manufacturer information with defined coverage, exclusions, and claim procedure.
  • PC-114 — Complete a documented customer handoff and close every associated permit, purchase order, subcontract, and communication item.

Where This Skill Fits

This project applies the contract scope from Chapter 9, the change-order and allowance discipline from Chapter 10, the job-startup verification from Chapter 13, the customer-communication record from Chapter 14, and the job-cost review from Chapter 18 to a single defined closeout event. Every deficiency raised during the walkthrough is checked against the scope actually contracted for, not against an unwritten expectation; every allowance and change is reconciled using the logs already built in Chapter 10; and the final job-cost figures produced here feed the lessons-learned and pricing-feedback loop from Chapter 18 back into Chapter 6 and Chapter 7.

The discipline used here — classifying every customer concern before agreeing to correct it, verifying corrections against the same standard used to accept them, and never conditioning a legally required correction on a review or testimonial — is the same discipline the Chapter 20 capstone requires the learner to demonstrate on a full sample project.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming permits, inspections, tests, startup, commissioning, and occupancy conditions are complete before closeout begins
  • completing a contractor pre-punch inspection before the customer walkthrough
  • conducting a systematic customer walkthrough and classifying every item raised
  • creating and executing a final punch list with verified corrections
  • reconciling allowances, change orders, credits, and unit-price quantities into the final contract total
  • preparing the final invoice and required lien, release, waiver, or payment documentation
  • delivering warranty documents, manufacturer information, manuals, and care instructions
  • obtaining a documented customer acceptance and closing all associated project records

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • use this chapter as legal, tax, or lien-law advice; every lien, waiver, notice, and payment-documentation requirement must be confirmed with jurisdiction-specific counsel per Chapter 9 and Chapter 16 controls
  • agree to added scope, warranty terms, or legal conclusions during the walkthrough itself
  • condition a legally required correction or warranty response on a positive review, testimonial, or final payment beyond what the contract allows
  • represent coverage, dates, or terms beyond what the approved warranty documents state
  • close the project file while a required permit, inspection, lien document, or subcontractor payment record remains outstanding

Any classification, correction, or figure that cannot be supported by the contract, approved changes, or a qualified reviewer's guidance must be resolved before the project file is closed.

Trade Note

The single most common source of closeout disputes diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section is an unclassified customer concern — an item that was neither accepted as a legitimate punch item nor explained as a warranty, maintenance, preference, or unrelated condition before the walkthrough ended. Classify every item before the walkthrough closes, not afterward.

Setup Standard

Do not schedule the customer walkthrough until the contractor pre-punch inspection is complete and obvious incomplete work, damage, debris, and protective materials have already been addressed. A customer walkthrough conducted on an unprepared project manufactures avoidable punch items and damages the professional impression of the closeout.

Shop Tip

Bring the acceptance criterion from the Chapter 9 contract scope to the walkthrough itself, not just the punch-list template. When a customer raises a concern, checking it against the actual written acceptance standard settles most classification questions on the spot.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every closeout in this book follows the same sequence: verify the project is actually ready to close, inspect it before the customer does, walk it with the customer using a consistent classification method, correct only what the contract and evidence support, reconcile the money, deliver the paperwork, and obtain a signed acceptance before the file is archived. Skipping or reordering these steps is the most common source of the disputes diagnosed later in this chapter.

Classification Discipline

Every item a customer raises during the walkthrough is recorded with a location, a description, a photograph, and a classification: accepted punch, disputed, change request, owner damage, manufacturer issue, maintenance, excluded, or referred. An item is not corrected as a punch item until it has been classified and checked against the contract acceptance criterion. A disputed item is documented and routed for review; it is not argued to resolution on the walkthrough itself.

Documentation Cue — Record Every Item Before Classifying

Record the location, description, and photograph of every item the customer raises before assigning it a classification. Do not classify an item verbally and skip the written record; the punch list is the only record that controls correction, verification, and closeout.

Critical Compliance Cue — No Added Scope or Legal Conclusions During the Walkthrough

Do not agree to added scope, warranty terms, or legal conclusions while walking the project with the customer. Record the item, classify it using the contract and evidence, and route anything outside the contractor's authority to the Chapter 10 change process or to counsel.

Operational Discipline Cue — One Punch List, One Copy of Record

Maintain a single controlled punch list in the project closeout file. Do not allow a separate informal list to circulate among the field crew, the customer, and the office. Every correction, verification, and closeout signature refers to the same document.

Professional Advisor Cue — Lien, Waiver, and Warranty Language Requires Review

Lien releases, waivers, notices, and warranty terms delivered at closeout must use jurisdiction-specific, attorney-approved language and manufacturer-approved warranty terms. Do not insert unreviewed language copied from a prior project or another jurisdiction's form.

E. Confirm Closeout Readiness

  1. Review the contract scope, approved changes, allowances, plans, selections, inspection approvals, quality records, and prior punch items. Assemble the complete document set on the Final Walkthrough and Punch List template's reference section before continuing.
  2. Confirm required permits, inspections, tests, startup, commissioning, and occupancy conditions are complete. Record the confirming document or inspector approval for each condition.
  3. Confirm subcontractor and supplier closeout documents are available. Verify warranties, lien releases, and final invoices are on file for every party required before customer handoff.
  4. Confirm utilities, fixtures, equipment, doors, locks, alarms, and customer functions are restored. Record the specific items checked and their restored condition.

F. Pre-Punch and Customer Walkthrough

  1. Complete contractor pre-punch before inviting the customer. Walk the project internally and record every observed deficiency on the punch-list template.
  2. Inspect work by room, area, system, exterior elevation, and contract deliverable. Use the same room-by-room or system-by-system route that will be used with the customer.
  3. Correct obvious incomplete work, damage, debris, labels, protection, and operation issues. Complete these corrections before the customer walkthrough is scheduled.
  4. Create the final-walkthrough route and checklist. Sequence the walkthrough by room, area, system, or elevation and list it on the template.
  5. Schedule the walkthrough with authorized customer representatives. Confirm attendee names, date, and expected duration in writing.
  6. Explain the purpose and distinction among contract deficiency, approved change, warranty item, maintenance item, preference, and unrelated condition. State this distinction to the customer before the walkthrough route begins.
  7. Walk the project systematically and record each customer concern. Follow the route from Step 4 without skipping areas.
  8. Use location, description, photograph, responsibility, acceptance criterion, and due status for each item. Complete every field on the punch-list template before moving to the next item.
  9. Do not agree to added scope or legal conclusions during the walkthrough. Record any request outside the contracted scope as a change request for Chapter 10 processing, not as a punch item.
  10. Classify each item as accepted punch, disputed, change request, owner damage, manufacturer issue, maintenance, excluded, or referred. Assign exactly one classification to each recorded item before the walkthrough closes.
  11. Create the final punch list and obtain acknowledgment of the recorded list where appropriate. Issue the completed list to the customer and file the acknowledged copy.

G. Correction and Verification

  1. Assign responsible person and target condition for each accepted item. Record the assigned party and the specific acceptance criterion on the punch list.
  2. Complete corrections using current safety, quality, permit, and protection controls. Perform each correction under the same site controls used during original construction.
  3. Document before, during, and after conditions. Photograph each corrected item at each stage and attach the photographs to the punch-list record.
  4. Verify each correction with the same acceptance criterion. Reinspect against the exact standard recorded when the item was accepted, not a new or relaxed standard.
  5. Obtain customer or authorized manager confirmation of closure where appropriate. Record the confirming signature, initials, or written acknowledgment for each closed item.

H. Final Account, Warranty, and Handoff

  1. Reconcile allowance selections, change orders, credits, unit-price quantities, and contract total. Complete the Final Account Reconciliation worksheet against the Chapter 10 logs.
  2. Confirm all invoices, payments, deposits, financing, retainage, tax, and credits are correctly recorded. Cross-check the reconciliation against the Chapter 18 job-cost and billing record.
  3. Prepare the final invoice using the approved accounting and contract process. Generate the invoice from the reconciled final account.
  4. Do not bill unapproved work or falsely represent completion. Confirm every billed item traces to an approved contract, change, or allowance entry before the invoice is issued.
  5. Prepare required lien, release, waiver, notice, affidavit, or payment documentation through jurisdiction-specific counsel-approved procedures. Use the current approved forms for the project's jurisdiction.
  6. Confirm subcontractor and supplier payment documentation required before customer handoff. Verify subcontractor lien releases are on file before the customer handoff proceeds.
  7. Prepare warranty documents identifying contractor coverage, manufacturer coverage, exclusions, claim procedure, contact, and dates. Assemble the Warranty and Handoff Package using approved terms only.
  8. Do not promise coverage beyond approved terms. Compare the delivered warranty language against the approved company and manufacturer terms before handoff.
  9. Prepare manuals, care instructions, paint information, product information, spare materials, keys, codes, and service contacts. Assemble each item into the handoff package.
  10. Demonstrate operation, shutoffs, maintenance, cleaning, access, and safety devices included in the work. Conduct the demonstration with the customer present and record what was demonstrated.
  11. Deliver permits, approvals, final photographs, as-built notes, and closeout documents required by contract. Include each item in the handoff package and record its delivery.
  12. Create a customer acceptance and handoff record. Complete the Customer Acceptance and Closeout Record with a summary of work, punch closure, and materials delivered.
  13. Do not condition legally required correction or warranty performance on final review or testimonial. Confirm no such condition appears anywhere in the invoice, warranty, or acceptance documents.
  14. Request feedback or review only through the Chapter 17 ethical procedure. Route any review request through the approved marketing procedure, separate from the closeout documents.
  15. Close project access, temporary protection, permits, purchase orders, subcontracts, and communication items. Confirm each is marked closed in the applicable company record system.
  16. Transfer warranty and callback responsibilities to the designated company system. Record the responsible department and contact for future warranty claims.
  17. Archive the complete closeout record and schedule required follow-up. File the signed acceptance record and all supporting documents in the project closeout file and set any required warranty follow-up date.

Common Closeout Inspection

Inspect the completed closeout file using the same checks regardless of which project or trade is involved.

  • Readiness check: confirm permits, inspections, tests, and subcontractor closeout documents were verified complete before the walkthrough was scheduled.
  • Classification check: confirm every walkthrough item has one recorded classification, with photograph, location, and responsibility.
  • Correction and verification check: confirm every accepted punch item was corrected and reverified against its original acceptance criterion, with before, during, and after photographs.
  • Financial reconciliation check: confirm the final account reconciles the contract sum, approved changes, allowances, credits, and payments without unexplained difference.
  • Lien and payment check: confirm required lien, waiver, notice, and affidavit documentation is complete and uses current jurisdiction-specific counsel-approved forms.
  • Warranty and handoff check: confirm delivered warranty terms match approved company and manufacturer language, and manuals, care instructions, and demonstrations were completed.
  • Acceptance and closure check: confirm the signed customer acceptance record is on file and every associated permit, purchase order, subcontract, and communication item is closed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Customer Disputes the Punch List After the Walkthrough

Probable cause: an item was verbally discussed during the walkthrough but not recorded, or its classification was not clearly communicated before the walkthrough closed.
Confirmation: compare the customer's current claim against the recorded punch-list entries and their classifications.
Correction: add any legitimately omitted item to the list with its correct classification, and explain the classification standard applied to any item the customer disputes.
Reinspection: repeat the classification check on the corrected list before proceeding to correction work.

A Corrected Item Fails Reinspection

Probable cause: the correction was verified against a general impression rather than the specific acceptance criterion recorded when the item was accepted.
Confirmation: re-measure or re-test the corrected item against the exact acceptance criterion on the punch-list record.
Correction: perform the correction again to the stated criterion and document the repeated correction.
Reinspection: repeat the verification check and confirm the same criterion is met before closing the item.

The Final Invoice Does Not Match the Contract and Change History

Probable cause: an approved change, allowance variance, or credit was not carried into the Final Account Reconciliation worksheet.
Confirmation: compare the reconciliation worksheet line by line against the Chapter 10 change-order and allowance logs and the Chapter 18 job-cost record.
Correction: add the missing entry, recalculate the final total, and reissue the invoice with the correction documented.
Reinspection: repeat the financial reconciliation check on the corrected invoice before it is delivered to the customer.

Required Lien or Payment Documentation Is Missing at Handoff

Probable cause: subcontractor or supplier lien releases were not collected before the customer handoff was scheduled, or the wrong jurisdiction's form was used.
Confirmation: check the subcontractor and supplier closeout file against the readiness-verification record from Section E.
Correction: obtain the missing documentation and confirm the correct jurisdiction-specific, counsel-approved form is used before final payment is accepted.
Reinspection: repeat the lien and payment check before closing the project file.

The Warranty Package States Coverage Beyond Approved Terms

Probable cause: a field team member or salesperson described coverage informally, and that description was carried into the written warranty document without comparison to the approved terms.
Confirmation: compare the delivered warranty language word-for-word against the approved company and manufacturer warranty terms.
Correction: correct the document to the approved terms, notify the customer of the correction, and retrain the responsible team member.
Reinspection: repeat the warranty and handoff check on the corrected package before it is redelivered.

A Legally Required Correction Is Conditioned on a Review or Final Payment

Probable cause: a team member informally linked correction of a legitimate punch or warranty item to a positive review or to release of final payment beyond what the contract allows.
Confirmation: review the communication record and the punch-list and invoice documents for any such condition.
Correction: remove the condition, complete the required correction regardless of review or payment status beyond the contracted terms, and correct the record.
Reinspection: repeat the acceptance and closure check on the corrected file.
