27-The Professional Contractor

This field manual provides practical instruction for building and operating a professional residential contracting business. Students learn business organization, licensing, financial management, estimating, contracts, customer communication, and operational systems required for successful contracting.

Overview

The Professional Contractor develops the business and operational skills needed to transition from skilled tradesperson to professional contractor. Beginning with business formation and compliance, students progress through licensing, insurance, accounting, estimating, proposals, contracts, customer management, change orders, and job startup systems.

Learning Outcomes

  • Define a professional residential contracting business model.
  • Establish business structures and maintain company records.
  • Understand contractor licensing, insurance, and bonding requirements.
  • Organize contractor banking, accounting, and tax documentation.
  • Create service menus and standardized scope libraries.
  • Develop accurate pricing systems for labor, materials, and profit.
  • Create professional estimates, proposals, and contracts.
  • Manage change orders and project allowances.
  • Build customer intake and communication workflows.
  • Create organized systems for starting residential construction projects.
