Job Overview

This project produces one professionally reviewed business structure and one controlled company record system built on the Chapter 1 business model. The finished work product is the completed official registration or formation documents, a documented ownership and authority record, a secure corporate-record book with defined sections, and a completed business-setup checklist.

You will compare available business structures with qualified advisors, select and register the entity or proprietorship, document owners, contributions, and authority, obtain the required taxpayer identification, and build the record system that will hold every license, policy, contract, employee file, and financial record produced by the rest of this book.

This project depends directly on Chapter 1: the services, jurisdictions, and ownership group approved in the Business Model Statement control which structures are even available for review. Nothing in Chapters 3 through 20 may proceed ahead of this project: the licensing applications in Chapter 3, the insurance applications in Chapter 4, and the banking setup in Chapter 5 all require the legal name, entity type, and taxpayer identification established here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • PC-007 — Compare available business structures against ownership, liability, taxation, continuity, and licensing considerations with qualified advisors.
  • PC-008 — Identify the registration, taxpayer-identification, and local requirements that apply to the selected structure.
  • PC-009 — Organize official formation, ownership, and authority documents in a controlled company record system.
  • PC-010 — Set up recordkeeping sections for governance, licenses, insurance, contracts, employees, and property.
  • PC-011 — Document ownership percentages, contributions, signing authority, and continuity information.
  • PC-012 — Prepare a completed business-setup checklist showing completed, pending, and professionally reviewed items.

Where This Skill Fits

This project builds directly on the approved Business Model Statement from Chapter 1. The service list, jurisdiction list, and ownership group defined there determine which business structures are appropriate to compare, and the risk boundaries defined there inform the liability discussion with the attorney and CPA.

Every later system in the book references the company record system built here. The licensing matrix in Chapter 3, the insurance and bond file in Chapter 4, the banking and accounting system in Chapter 5, the employee files in Chapter 15, and the subcontractor and vendor records in Chapter 16 are all stored inside the sections created in this project. The signing-authority matrix created here also controls who may execute contracts, changes, and payments throughout the remaining chapters.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • comparing sole proprietorship, partnership, limited liability company, corporation, or other permitted structures with qualified advisors
  • confirming whether the proposed structure is permitted for the applicable contractor license or professional relationship
  • documenting owners, percentages, voting rights, managers, officers, qualifiers, and authorized signers
  • registering the legal name and obtaining official formation and taxpayer-identification documents
  • creating a secure corporate-record book with defined sections and access controls
  • creating a signing-authority matrix, governance calendar, and asset register
  • completing a business-setup checklist reviewed by qualified professionals

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select an entity structure solely from online summaries or liability-marketing claims instead of qualified professional advice
  • treat entity formation as authorization to contract; licensing in Chapter 3, insurance in Chapter 4, and banking in Chapter 5 must still be completed
  • rely on this chapter for legal, tax, or accounting advice; every structure decision must be confirmed with a qualified attorney or CPA
  • commingle personal and business names, funds, contracts, or property under the new structure

Any structure, registration, or record decision that has not been confirmed with a qualified advisor or the official registry must be treated as provisional until Section H review is complete.

Trade Note

Do not select a structure because it is common among other contractors. The correct structure depends on this specific business's ownership, licensing relationship, tax posture, and risk exposure, each of which must be confirmed with a qualified advisor before registration.

Setup Standard

Do not operate, advertise, or contract under a new trade name or entity until the official registration, taxpayer identification, and required local registrations are confirmed issued. A pending filing is not an issued registration.

Shop Tip

Build the confidential-record section of the corporate-record book at the same time as the general sections, not afterward. Deciding what needs restricted access now is far easier than restricting access to records that have already been freely shared.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every later system in this book depends on the legal name, entity type, taxpayer identification, and record system created here being accurate and singular. A business operating under an unclear or informally changed name, or with records scattered across separate personal and business files, cannot support the licensing, insurance, banking, contract, or job-cost systems built in later chapters.

Structure Selection Discipline

A structure is only finalized after it is confirmed permitted for the contractor license or professional relationship the business intends to use, and after an attorney or CPA has reviewed ownership, liability, taxation, continuity, and administration considerations specific to this business. A structure that has not received this review is a draft selection, not an approved structure.

Documentation Cue — Record Every Structure Considered

When comparing structures in Section E, record each structure considered, the specific reason it was or was not selected, and the reviewing professional's comments. Do not simply record the final choice without the comparison record; the comparison record protects the business if the structure must be revisited later.

Critical Compliance Cue — No Operating Under an Unregistered Name

Do not advertise, contract, invoice, or open a bank account under a legal or trade name until the official registration is confirmed issued and the taxpayer identification is obtained. A submitted application is not an issued registration.

Operational Discipline Cue — One Record System, No Informal Copies

Maintain a single controlled corporate-record book. Do not allow formation documents, ownership records, or signing-authority information to exist only in an owner's personal email, phone, or informal notes. Every later chapter's records are filed inside this single system.

Professional Advisor Cue — Entity Selection Requires Attorney or CPA Review

Do not select a final structure based solely on this chapter, a template, or a general online comparison. Ownership, liability, taxation, continuity, and licensing considerations must be reviewed by a qualified attorney or CPA before the structure is registered.

E. Structure Comparison and Ownership Documentation

  1. Identify the jurisdiction of formation and the jurisdictions where the business will operate. Copy this list directly from the approved Chapter 1 service-area and project-fit matrix.
  2. List the business structures available for professional review, including sole proprietorship, partnership, limited liability company, corporation, or other permitted form. Record each candidate structure on the business-setup checklist.
  3. Compare ownership, control, liability, taxation, continuity, administration, payroll, financing, licensing, and recordkeeping considerations with qualified advisors. Record the attorney's or CPA's specific comments for each structure compared.
  4. Do not select an entity solely from online summaries or liability marketing claims. Confirm the final selection is supported by the recorded professional comments, not by an unreviewed generic comparison.
  5. Confirm whether the proposed structure is permitted for the contractor license or professional relationship involved. Record the licensing authority's confirmation or the attorney's confirmation of this compatibility.
  6. Identify owners, percentages, voting rights, managers, officers, directors, qualifiers, and authorized signers. Record each on the ownership and authority record.
  7. Document contributions of cash, tools, vehicles, equipment, intellectual property, customer lists, and other assets. Record the contributing owner, the asset, and its value for each contribution.
  8. Identify buy-sell, death, disability, departure, dispute, and ownership-transfer issues requiring attorney review. List each issue and the attorney assigned to address it before final registration.

F. Registration and Identification

  1. Select the legal name and verify availability through the official registry. Record the search date and result on the business-setup checklist.
  2. Check trade name, assumed name, trademark, domain, social-media, and advertising consistency. Record each name's availability status before filing.
  3. Register the entity or proprietorship through the official process. Record the filing date, confirmation number, and official contact used.
  4. Obtain official formation, registration, charter, partnership, assumed-name, or similar documents. Store the issued documents in the corporate-record book's formation section.
  5. Obtain the appropriate taxpayer identification through the official tax process. Record the identification number's issuance date and store the confirmation document.
  6. Identify required state, local, payroll, sales-tax, unemployment, and industry registrations with professional guidance. List each required registration and its current status.

G. Company Record System

  1. Create the company information sheet with legal name, trade name, addresses, contacts, identification numbers, and authorized persons. Place this sheet at the front of the corporate-record book.
  2. Create a signing-authority matrix for contracts, checks, credit, payroll, hiring, purchases, debt, and legal filings. Name the specific authorized person for each category.
  3. Create a governance calendar for reports, meetings, minutes, annual filings, registered agent, renewals, and ownership updates. Record each required date and the responsible person.
  4. Create a secure physical or digital corporate-record book. Confirm the storage method and its access controls before filing any document.
  5. Create sections for formation, governance, ownership, licenses, insurance, bonds, taxes, banking, contracts, employees, subcontractors, vehicles, equipment, property, and legal matters. Label each section and confirm it is empty and ready for later chapters.
  6. Create a confidential-record section with restricted access. Name the specific persons permitted access to this section.
  7. Create a document-naming, revision, backup, retention, destruction, and release procedure. Write the specific naming convention and backup schedule to be used going forward.
  8. Store certified or official copies separately from working copies. Confirm the physical or digital location of each.
  9. Create a company seal, resolution, consent, meeting-minute, or authorization system only where advised or required. Record whether this applies to the selected structure.
  10. Create an asset register showing ownership, purchase date, serial number, financing, insurance, assignment, and disposition. Record every company-owned tool, vehicle, or equipment item currently known.
  11. Create a vehicle and equipment title and registration file. Store titles and registration documents in this file as they are obtained.
  12. Create a business-address, mail, registered-agent, and official-notice procedure. Record who receives official notices and how they are routed.
  13. Create a record of licenses, accounts, platforms, subscriptions, passwords, and responsible administrators without exposing credentials. Record the account name and responsible administrator only; store credentials in a separate secured system.
  14. Create a continuity plan for access if an owner or manager is unavailable. Name the backup authority and the specific access this plan restores.

H. Separation Review, Checklist, and Approval

  1. Review whether personal assets, vehicles, tools, or property are being used by the business without written ownership, lease, reimbursement, or insurance treatment. List every item found in this condition.
  2. Correct commingling of names, invoices, contracts, bank accounts, or property before operations begin. Record the correction taken for each item listed in the previous step.
  3. Prepare the business-setup checklist with completed, pending, not applicable, and professional-review items. Mark every item from Sections E through G with its current status.
  4. Review the completed structure and records with the attorney, CPA, licensing authority, broker, and bank as applicable. Record each reviewer's name, role, date, and comments.
  5. Obtain required approvals and archive evidence. Store each approval document in the corporate-record book's legal-matters section.
  6. Set annual and event-triggered reviews for ownership, authority, address, registered agent, and company records. Record the next scheduled review date and the specific events that would trigger an earlier review.

Common Model Inspection

Inspect the completed structure and record system using the same checks regardless of which structure or jurisdiction is involved.

  • Structure check: confirm at least two structures were compared with a qualified advisor and the selected structure is confirmed compatible with the applicable contractor license.
  • Registration check: confirm the legal name, official formation documents, and taxpayer identification are issued, not merely submitted.
  • Ownership and authority check: confirm owners, contributions, and signing authority are documented and consistent with the registered structure.
  • Record-system check: confirm every required section exists in the corporate-record book with a defined access-control rule.
  • Separation check: confirm no personal asset, contract, or account is being used by the business without documented treatment.
  • Professional-review check: confirm attorney, CPA, and other applicable reviewers' comments are recorded and addressed before approval.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Business Operates Under a Name Not Yet Registered

Probable cause: advertising, banking, or contracting began based on a submitted application rather than an issued registration.
Confirmation: compare the name in use against the official registry's issued-status record.
Correction: stop using the unregistered name in advertising, contracts, and banking until the registration is confirmed issued.
Reinspection: repeat the registration check on the corrected record.

Personal and Business Funds or Property Are Commingled

Probable cause: a personal vehicle, tool, or account is being used by the business without a written ownership, lease, or reimbursement treatment.
Confirmation: review the asset register and banking records for any item lacking a documented business treatment.
Correction: execute the missing lease, reimbursement, or ownership-transfer document with attorney or CPA guidance.
Reinspection: repeat the separation check on the corrected asset register.

Signing Authority Is Unclear or Disputed

Probable cause: the signing-authority matrix was not completed before contracts, purchases, or payroll began.
Confirmation: compare recent contracts, purchase orders, or payroll actions against the signing-authority matrix.
Correction: complete or correct the matrix with attorney input and notify all affected parties of the corrected authority.
Reinspection: repeat the ownership and authority check on the corrected matrix.

Company Records Are Scattered Across Personal Devices or Accounts

Probable cause: the corporate-record book was not established before formation documents, licenses, or contracts began arriving.
Confirmation: identify every location where a company record currently exists outside the corporate-record book.
Correction: consolidate every record into the appropriate section of the corporate-record book and apply the documented backup and access procedure.
Reinspection: repeat the record-system check after consolidation.

The Structure Was Selected Without Attorney or CPA Review

Probable cause: the structure was chosen from a generic online comparison to save time or cost.
Confirmation: check the business-setup checklist for a named attorney or CPA reviewer, review date, and recorded comments.
Correction: suspend reliance on the structure for licensing, insurance, banking, or contract decisions until a qualified attorney or CPA reviews it and comments are addressed.
Reinspection: repeat the professional-review check before re-approving the checklist.

The Structure Is Incompatible With the Contractor License

Probable cause: the structure was registered before confirming it is a permitted form for the applicable contractor license or qualifier relationship.
Confirmation: compare the registered structure against the licensing authority's requirements from Chapter 3.
Correction: consult the attorney and licensing authority on whether the structure can be amended or must be re-registered.
Reinspection: repeat the structure-and-license compatibility check before proceeding to Chapter 3.

Cleanup

This project ends with the registered structure and company record system filed for reuse, not left as informal notes.

  • Store the issued formation documents, taxpayer identification, and ownership and authority record in the corporate-record book's formation and ownership sections
  • Store the signing-authority matrix, governance calendar, asset register, and business-setup checklist in their designated sections
  • Record each reviewing professional's name, role, and date of review
  • Record the next scheduled governance and structure review date and the specific update-triggering conditions
  • Distribute access to the corporate-record book only to persons named on the signing-authority matrix or the confidential-record access list
