Job Overview

This capstone chapter qualifies you to carry a complete drywall project from pre-hanging inspection through final finish handoff without treating any stage as isolated from the stages before or after it. The finished work product is one complete small practice room — hung, fastened, beaded, taped, coated, sanded, textured or smooth-finished, repaired, primed, inspected, and formally handed off — supported by a full set of project records.

You will perform the Chapter 1 readiness inspection on the capstone room; stage the work area; lay out, cut, and hang ceiling and wall panels; fasten every panel to the Chapter 7 standard; install corner bead, edge trim, and control-joint or access-panel trim; tape, coat, and finish every joint through three compound coats; sand under dust control; complete one small-damage repair and one large-hole or section-replacement repair on a designated practice area; clean, prime, and inspect the surface; apply the approved texture or complete the specified smooth-finish practice panel; issue a final deficiency list; and hand off the room clean, dry, primed, and ready for paint and interior finish carpentry.

This is the last chapter in the book. Every competency demonstrated here was first taught in an earlier chapter; this chapter does not introduce new technique, it requires you to sequence and integrate technique you already hold, and to prove that you can carry a real project through to a documented, inspected handoff.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • DW-115 — Integrate layout, panel cutting, ceiling hanging, and wall hanging into one sequenced project.
  • DW-116 — Integrate fastening, bead installation, and hanging-stage inspection.
  • DW-117 — Integrate taping, mudding, sanding, and finish-coat inspection.
  • DW-118 — Integrate texture or smooth-finish work with primer and defect correction.
  • DW-119 — Integrate small- and large-damage repair into a finished project.
  • DW-120 — Complete final inspection and drywall closeout documentation.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapters 1 through 19 each isolated one competency so it could be learned and verified on its own. A real drywall project does not arrive in isolated pieces: the Chapter 1 readiness decision determines what Chapter 5 and 6 can hang; the Chapter 7 fastening standard governs every panel installed under Chapters 5, 6, 8, 18, and 19; Chapter 9 bead cannot go on until Chapter 6 panels are secure; Chapter 10 tape cannot go on until Chapter 9 bead is aligned; and Chapters 11 through 13 cannot proceed out of order without producing a finish that will not sand or prime correctly.

This chapter requires you to hold that whole sequence in your head at once, on one room, while documenting every stage the way a working drywall crew documents a real project for handoff. The records you produce here — readiness release, fastening inspection, finish-level record, repair record, and final acceptance — are the same categories of record required throughout the book, now compiled for a single completed project.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • performing the Chapter 1 pre-drywall readiness inspection on the capstone room and correcting every deficiency before hanging begins
  • staging the work area and materials using Chapter 2
  • laying out, cutting, and hanging ceiling panels using Chapters 3, 4, and 5
  • laying out, cutting, and hanging wall panels, soffits, corners, and returns using Chapters 3, 4, 6, and 8
  • fastening every panel to the Chapter 7 standard and correcting every fastening defect
  • installing corner bead, edge trim, and control-joint or access-panel trim using Chapter 9
  • taping and finishing every joint through three compound coats using Chapters 10, 11, 12, and 13
  • sanding under dust control using Chapter 14
  • completing one small-damage repair using Chapter 17 and one large-hole or section-replacement repair using Chapter 18 or 19 on a designated practice area
  • cleaning, priming, and inspecting the surface using Chapter 15
  • applying the approved texture using Chapter 16, or completing the specified smooth-finish practice panel
  • issuing a final deficiency list, correcting and reinspecting every item, and completing formal project handoff

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • skip, reorder, or merge any stage-controlling requirement established in Chapters 1 through 19, including the Chapter 1 readiness release, the Chapter 7 fastening standard, and the tape-before-fill-coat sequence
  • alter a rated, moisture-resistant, or other protected assembly without the exact approved assembly detail identified in the capstone room plan
  • claim a Level 4 or Level 5 finish, or any code-compliance, fire-rating, or moisture-rating claim, unless the specified operations and inspection criteria in the capstone room plan are actually met
  • hand off the room before every item on the final deficiency list is corrected and reinspected

Any condition outside the learner's qualification — including unresolved framing, rough-in, or rated-assembly conditions identified during the Chapter 1 readiness inspection — must be referred to the responsible trade, an inspector, or an engineer or architect before this project proceeds.

Trade Note

The Capstone Continuity Rule permits this project to use the small insulated practice structure completed through Book 10, or another approved practice room with completed framing, rough-ins, insulation, air sealing, and required inspections. The room must remain accessible for inspection throughout hanging and finishing and must be handed off clean, dry, primed, and finish-ready.

Common Field Fundamentals

This chapter does not introduce new technique. It requires the sequence discipline established across the book: readiness before hanging, ceiling before wall where the assembly requires it, fastening before bead, bead before tape, tape before fill coats, drying or setting time respected between every coat, sanding limited to what is necessary, and primer never used to substitute for incomplete finishing. Every deviation from that sequence risks rework that this chapter's final inspection is designed to catch before handoff.

Sequence Discipline Across Stages

Treat each stage boundary in this chapter as a checkpoint rather than a formality. The hanging-stage inspection at Step 19 exists because bead installed over an unfastened or misaligned panel, and tape embedded over an unbeaded corner, cannot be corrected without removing finished work. Complete every correction identified at each checkpoint before advancing to the next stage.

Measurement Cue — Carry Chapter 7 Fastening Into Every Panel

Apply the Chapter 7 fastening standard to ceiling panels, wall panels, soffit and return pieces, and the replacement panel used in the large-hole or section-replacement repair. Do not treat fastening as complete for the room until every panel installed under Steps 10 through 18 has passed the fastener-field inspection described in Chapter 7.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Conceal Unresolved Conditions

Do not proceed past the Chapter 1 readiness inspection, and do not conceal with drywall, any active water leak, wet insulation, mold, damaged wiring, unapproved splice, missing box cover, unsupported pipe, blocked duct, missing fireblocking, or unresolved structural defect discovered at any stage of this project. Stop work on the affected area and refer the condition to the responsible trade, an inspector, or an engineer or architect as required.

Field Safety Cue — Lifting, Dust, and Access Throughout the Project

Maintain panel-lift, cart, and team-lift use for every ceiling and wall panel; maintain HEPA dust containment during every cutting, sanding, and texture-preparation step; and keep no-step, no-storage, access-panel, and emergency-exit zones clear for the duration of the project.

Code Alert — Rated and Protected Assemblies

Do not alter a fire-rated, smoke-rated, shaft, garage-separation, corridor, stair, moisture-resistant, or other protected assembly identified in the capstone room plan without the exact approved assembly detail. Verify rated- and moisture-resistant assembly continuity at Step 41 before completing final inspection.

F. Readiness and Staging Procedure (Steps 1–9)

  1. Obtain the approved capstone room plan, wall types, ceiling plan, drywall schedule, rated details, panel layout, finish level, texture schedule, and inspection sequence. Confirm the documents match the designated practice room.
  2. Confirm framing, backing, rough-ins, fireblocking, insulation, air sealing, and required inspections are complete. Obtain prior inspection records from each responsible trade.
  3. Perform the Chapter 1 pre-drywall readiness inspection. Complete the full deficiency-list worksheet and photograph log for the practice room.
  4. Correct all framing, backing, moisture, rough-in, access, and insulation deficiencies. Refer corrections to the responsible trade and reinspect before proceeding.
  5. Prepare the work area using Chapter 2. Complete the work-area readiness checklist for the practice room.
  6. Stage panels by type, size, ceiling, and wall sequence. Label staged panels by intended location.
  7. Establish ceiling and wall reference lines. Mark reference lines consistent with the approved panel layout.
  8. Lay out ceiling panels to minimize joints and avoid four-corner intersections. Confirm the layout against the capstone room plan before cutting.
  9. Mark joist, truss, channel, and backing locations. Transfer marks to perimeter walls and adjoining panels as needed.

G. Hanging and Fastening Procedure (Steps 10–19)

  1. Cut ceiling panels and openings using Chapters 3 and 4. Dry-fit and verify against the marked layout before installation.
  2. Lift and install ceiling panels using Chapter 5. Maintain supported edges, staggered joints, and controlled gaps.
  3. Inspect ceiling joints, support, fasteners, and openings. Correct any deficiency before wall panels conceal ceiling edges.
  4. Lay out wall panels around doors, windows, boxes, and returns. Confirm the layout avoids prohibited joint alignments.
  5. Cut and install wall panels using Chapter 6. Maintain floor and ceiling gaps and factory-edge alignment.
  6. Install soffit, corner, and return pieces using Chapter 8. Verify backing and consistent return depth at every location.
  7. Apply Chapter 7 fastening standards to every panel. Complete the fastener-field inspection for ceiling, wall, soffit, and return panels.
  8. Correct all overdriven, missed, proud, or improperly spaced fasteners. Install replacement fasteners at the specified offset and reinspect.
  9. Install corner bead, edge trim, control joints, and access-panel trim. Verify flange attachment and straightness per Chapter 9.
  10. Complete the hanging-stage inspection. Confirm every panel, fastener, bead, and trim item meets the Acceptance Standards table before pre-fill begins.

H. Taping, Coating, and Sanding Procedure (Steps 20–30)

  1. Pre-fill approved gaps and defects. Allow pre-fill material to set or dry before taping.
  2. Tape flat seams and inside corners. Embed tape per Chapter 10 and inspect for bubbles, wrinkles, and dry spots.
  3. Apply first coats over fasteners, bead, trim, and assigned joints. Follow Chapter 11 coverage and feathering requirements.
  4. Allow full drying or setting. Confirm the coat is dry before scraping or recoating.
  5. Apply the second coat and build joint width. Widen and level flat seams, butt joints, and corners per Chapter 12.
  6. Inspect flatness and correct buildup. Correct hollows, heavy centers, and edge ridges before the coat sets further.
  7. Apply the final coat and minimize sanding requirements. Feather transitions per Chapter 13 and remove excess compound rather than leaving it for sanding.
  8. Allow the final coat to dry fully. Confirm no moisture discoloration remains before sanding.
  9. Establish sanding containment and HEPA dust control. Confirm ventilation will not spread dust or backdraft combustion equipment.
  10. Sand and inspect under raking light. Use the least aggressive abrasive that corrects each defect, per Chapter 14.
  11. Apply touch-up compound and resand localized defects. Allow touch-ups to dry before resanding.

I. Repair, Priming, and Texture Procedure (Steps 31–38)

  1. Complete the required small-hole or nail-pop repair. Confirm the cause is corrected, then repair per Chapter 17 and inspect the blend.
  2. Complete the required large-hole or section-replacement demonstration on a designated practice area. Confirm the cause is corrected, install backing and a matching patch or replacement section per Chapter 18 or 19, and finish the seams.
  3. Clean all dust. HEPA-vacuum the entire room before priming.
  4. Apply drywall primer. Maintain a wet edge and uniform film per Chapter 15.
  5. Inspect primed surfaces and mark defects. Identify ridges, pinholes, exposed paper, and texture differences under raking light.
  6. Correct and spot-prime all defects. Sand and clean localized repairs before spot-priming.
  7. Apply the approved texture, or complete the specified smooth finish and texture practice panel. Match the approved sample standard per Chapter 16, or complete the smooth-finish practice panel required by the finish schedule.
  8. Allow texture or finish coats to dry. Confirm full drying before removing protection.

J. Final Inspection and Closeout Procedure (Steps 39–46)

  1. Inspect walls, ceilings, corners, openings, bead, seams, fasteners, texture, and repairs under normal and raking light. Complete a full-room inspection pass against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Verify electrical boxes, access panels, valves, dampers, vents, and service points remain usable. Confirm no access component was concealed or obstructed during finishing.
  3. Verify rated, moisture-resistant, and special assemblies match the approved documents. Compare finished conditions against the capstone room plan.
  4. Prepare a final deficiency list. Enter every remaining defect found during Steps 39–41.
  5. Correct and reinspect every item. Do not proceed to handoff with any open item on the final deficiency list.
  6. Remove protection and complete HEPA cleanup. Clear the room of dust, debris, and protective coverings.
  7. Compile panel records, rated-assembly records, photographs, compound records, primer records, repair records, inspections, and corrections. Assemble the complete closeout package.
  8. Hand off the room as clean, dry, primed, and ready for paint and interior finish carpentry. Deliver the closeout package with the formal handoff.

Common Capstone Inspection

Inspect the completed practice room using the same checks across every stage regardless of room size or configuration.

  • Readiness check: confirm the Chapter 1 decision was approved or conditionally approved with every item corrected before hanging.
  • Hanging check: confirm supported edges, staggered joints, controlled gaps, and no four-corner intersections.
  • Fastening check: confirm correct depth, spacing, and substrate engagement across every panel.
  • Bead and finish check: confirm straight bead, bonded tape, flat and feathered joints, and a smooth sanded surface.
  • Repair check: confirm both required repairs are stable, corrected at the cause, and visually blended.
  • Primer and texture check: confirm uniform primer coverage and a texture or smooth finish matching the approved standard.
  • Access and rated-assembly check: confirm every service point remains usable and every rated or moisture-resistant assembly matches the approved documents.

Complete the Final Deficiency List in the Inspection Checklist section below before issuing project handoff.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Bead Installed Before the Hanging-Stage Inspection Is Complete

Probable cause: proceeding to Step 18 bead installation without completing the Step 19 hanging-stage inspection on the adjoining panels.
Confirmation: check panel fastening and alignment behind the installed bead using a straightedge.
Correction: remove and reset the bead after correcting the underlying panel defect; do not bury a misaligned corner in thick compound.
Reinspection: repeat the hanging-stage inspection for the affected area before retaping.

A Finish Coat Was Applied Before the Prior Coat Fully Dried

Probable cause: moving to the next coat on a project schedule rather than a drying-condition check.
Confirmation: inspect for moisture discoloration, soft compound, or cracking under the new coat.
Correction: scrape back to sound, dry compound and reapply the coat once the prior coat is confirmed dry or set.
Reinspection: recheck flatness and drying condition before proceeding to the next coat.

The Required Repairs Do Not Match the Surrounding Finish

Probable cause: the repair area was primed or textured before confirming the surrounding room's finish schedule and sample standard.
Confirmation: compare the repair area against the approved sample standard under raking light.
Correction: sand, reprime, and reapply texture or smooth finish to the repair area using the same product, mix, and application method as the surrounding surface.
Reinspection: reinspect the blended repair area under normal and raking light.

An Access Component Was Concealed During Finishing

Probable cause: the panel layout or finish work did not cross-check access-component locations against the rough-in plan carried over from Chapter 1.
Confirmation: compare the finished room against the rough-in plan and mark every junction box, valve, damper, cleanout, and service point.
Correction: cut and install an access panel at the affected location, finished to match the surrounding surface.
Reinspection: reverify access to every identified component before final inspection.

A Rated or Moisture-Resistant Assembly Detail Does Not Match the Capstone Room Plan

Probable cause: a panel type, joint treatment, or fastening detail was substituted during hanging without reference to the approved assembly detail.
Confirmation: compare the installed assembly against the approved capstone room plan detail, component by component.
Correction: remove and replace the nonconforming portion with the exact approved assembly detail; obtain engineer or architect approval where required before proceeding.
Reinspection: reverify the corrected assembly against the approved detail before completing final inspection.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a formal project handoff, not a routine end-of-day cleanup.

  • Complete HEPA cleanup of all dust from cutting, sanding, and texture preparation
  • Remove all protection from floors, trim, fixtures, and adjacent surfaces
  • Return all tools, lifts, scaffolds, and equipment to storage
  • Compile and file the complete closeout package: panel records, rated-assembly records, photographs, compound records, primer records, repair records, all inspections, and all corrections
  • Deliver the formal handoff of the clean, dry, primed, finish-ready room to painting or finish carpentry

This chapter required you to integrate layout, panel cutting, ceiling hanging, and wall hanging (DW-115); integrate fastening, bead installation, and the hanging-stage inspection (DW-116); integrate taping, mudding, sanding, and finish-coat inspection (DW-117); integrate texture or smooth-finish work with primer and defect correction (DW-118); integrate small- and large-damage repair into the finished project (DW-119); and complete final inspection and drywall closeout documentation (DW-120).

You now have a completed practice room, hung, fastened, beaded, taped, coated, sanded, repaired, primed, and textured or smooth-finished, with a full set of project records compiled into a closeout package. That room is ready for handoff to painting or interior finish carpentry.

File the complete closeout package — readiness release, work-area checklist, cut and opening logs, hanging-stage inspection, fastener-field inspection, bead inspection, finish-level records, sanding inspection, repair records, primer record, texture or smooth-finish record, final deficiency list, and handoff decision — with the room's project records. This closeout package is the controlling document for Book 11.
