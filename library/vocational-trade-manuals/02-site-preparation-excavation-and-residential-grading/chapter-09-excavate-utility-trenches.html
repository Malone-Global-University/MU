Job Overview

This project qualifies you to excavate and prepare utility trenches consisting of a marked centerline, a controlled excavation to the approved depth and slope, verified separation among utility systems, protected trench walls, and a prepared bearing surface, using the approved utility plan, route, depth, slope, separation, bedding, and inspection requirements. The finished work product is a safe, correctly aligned and graded trench, clean of loose soil and water, with bedding placed where required and ready for the specified utility installation and inspection.

You will review the approved utility plan and confirm current utility-locate markings; mark the trench centerline, width limits, access points, crossings, and termination points; establish grade references; excavate in controlled passes while maintaining required width, depth, slope, and separation; install protective systems, ladders, or ramps; remove loose soil and water from the trench bottom; place bedding where required; protect existing utilities and crossings; and inspect and document the completed trench before utility installation.

This is the ninth project in the site preparation sequence. It follows the temporary drainage and erosion-control system installed and commissioned in Project 8, which must remain functional and undisturbed throughout this trenching work. The trenches prepared here support the utility installation that precedes the rough grading of Project 10 and must remain protected until that installation and backfill are complete.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • SP-049 — Review the approved utility plan, route, depth, slope, separation, bedding, and crossing requirements, and confirm current utility-locate markings before excavation.
  • SP-050 — Mark the trench centerline, width limits, access points, crossings, and termination points, and establish grade stakes, invert references, or laser controls.
  • SP-051 — Excavate in controlled passes to maintain required trench width, depth, slope, and alignment, using hand-dig or vacuum methods within required tolerance zones.
  • SP-052 — Install protective systems, ladders, ramps, or other required controls, and keep spoil, equipment, and materials at safe distances from trench edges.
  • SP-053 — Remove loose soil, rocks, organic material, and water from the trench bottom, place and shape approved bedding material, and protect existing utilities and support exposed crossings.
  • SP-054 — Verify separation among utility systems, document unexpected conditions, and inspect the trench bottom, bedding, slope, dimensions, access, and protective controls before utility installation.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 8 installed the temporary drainage and erosion-control system that protects every excavation performed from this point forward, including the trenches in this project. Project 5 established the footing excavation method and inspection discipline that this project extends to linear utility work, and Project 4 established the cleared, stripped work area within which these trenches are routed.

The trenches prepared here remain open, protected, and under inspection only until utility installation and backfill are complete. The rough grading of Project 10 depends on these trenches being backfilled and compacted, and the final drainage work of Project 14 depends on utility trench settlement having been corrected. Do not proceed to Project 10 while a trench required for this project remains open, unprotected, or uninspected.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved utility plan, route, depth, slope, separation, bedding, crossing, and inspection requirements
  • confirming current utility-locate markings and resolving conflicts before excavation
  • marking trench centerline, width limits, access points, crossings, and termination points
  • establishing grade stakes, invert references, or laser controls
  • defining equipment routes, spoil locations, barricades, and public-protection measures
  • inspecting excavation equipment and confirming operator and competent-person responsibilities
  • excavating in controlled passes, using hand-dig or vacuum methods within required tolerance zones
  • maintaining required trench width and avoiding unnecessary disturbance
  • checking depth, slope, and alignment at regular intervals
  • keeping spoil, equipment, and materials at safe distances from trench edges
  • installing protective systems, ladders, ramps, or other required controls
  • removing loose soil, rocks, organic material, and water from the trench bottom
  • placing and shaping approved bedding material where required
  • protecting existing utilities and supporting exposed crossings using the approved method
  • verifying separation among utility systems
  • marking and documenting unexpected utilities, unsuitable soil, groundwater, or instability
  • inspecting trench bottom, bedding, slope, dimensions, access, and protective controls
  • preparing the trench for utility installation and inspection, and protecting the open trench until installation and backfill are complete

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • excavate before current utility-locate markings are confirmed and every marked conflict is resolved
  • change the approved trench route or depth without first obtaining direction
  • enter or work within an unprotected trench that requires a protective system at its excavated depth
  • leave a trench open, unbarricaded, or unprotected at the end of a work period when the plan requires protection
  • backfill a trench before the required bedding, separation, and pre-installation inspection are complete
  • proceed past a stop-work condition, such as an unexpected utility, unsuitable soil, groundwater, or instability, without the direction of the responsible party

Any condition requiring a revised route, a different depth, an engineer's determination, or a decision outside the approved utility plan must be escalated before work proceeds in that area.

Trade Note

The trench route, depth, slope, separation, bedding material, crossing details, protective-system requirements, and inspection triggers referenced throughout this project are supplied by the approved utility plan, manufacturer or product installation instructions, the applicable regulatory authority, or the competent person responsible for excavation safety, and are not generated by this chapter. Where this chapter refers to "the approved utility plan" or "the required protective system," it means those supplied documents and determinations; do not select a depth, slope, separation, or protective-system configuration from general trade familiarity.

Setup Standard

Do not excavate any trench segment until current utility-locate markings for that segment have been confirmed (Step 2) and every marked conflict has been resolved. No trench excavation may proceed in a segment where a conflicting utility marking remains unresolved.

Shop Tip

Walk the full marked centerline with the utility-locate markings and the approved plan in hand before the first excavation pass. Confirming every crossing and termination point against both references at once catches a routing conflict before it becomes a mid-trench stop-work condition.

Common Field Fundamentals

A utility trench only supports safe installation and long-term performance if it is excavated to the correct route, depth, slope, and separation, with its walls protected and its bearing surface clean before the utility is installed. A trench excavated past the required tolerance zone near an existing utility, or left unprotected at a depth requiring a protective system, endangers both the crew and the completed work every later project depends on.

Working Around the Protected Site

Every trench excavated in this chapter depends on the temporary drainage and erosion-control system commissioned in Project 8 remaining functional and the cleared limits established in Project 4 remaining respected. Recheck both at Step 2 of this project before excavation begins. If a field condition suggests the approved route conflicts with an erosion-control feature or a cleared-limit boundary, stop and obtain direction before excavating around the conflict; do not reroute a trench or relocate an erosion control to resolve a conflict discovered during this project.

Measurement Cue — Trench Width, Depth, and Slope

Excavate to the specified trench width and depth, and maintain the specified slope toward the approved outlet, sump, or connection point, checking depth, slope, and alignment at the regular interval identified by the plan. Where no specific interval is stated, treat this as an unresolved condition and obtain direction before proceeding, since an unverified interval may allow a slope deviation to go undetected over a long trench run.

Code Alert — Utility-Locate Status and Separation Requirements

Confirm current utility-locate markings, required separation among utility systems, and any permit conditions from the approved utility plan or applicable regulatory authority before excavation begins in each segment. These requirements vary by jurisdiction, utility owner, and soil condition; do not assume a separation distance or locate validity period used on a prior site applies here.

Manufacturer Cue — Protective-System Rated Configuration

Install and configure the trench box or shoring system according to the manufacturer's rated depth, soil type, and configuration for the specific excavation. A protective system installed outside its rated configuration may not provide the protection it is designed to provide.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work Before Entering an Unprotected Trench

Do not enter a trench at a depth requiring a protective system until that system is installed and inspected by the competent person. Stop work immediately if an unexpected utility, unsuitable soil, groundwater, or instability is discovered, and obtain direction before continuing excavation or entering the affected segment.

Field Safety Cue — Spoil Placement, Access, and Equipment Movement

Keep spoil, equipment, and materials at the specified safe distance from trench edges to avoid surcharge and edge collapse, and use only the ladder or ramp locations established at the specified access interval when entering or leaving the trench.

E. Preparation — Plan Review and Locate Confirmation

  1. Review the approved utility plan, route, depth, slope, separation, bedding, crossing, and inspection requirements. Confirm these values against the current approved plan before any trench is marked or excavated.
  2. Confirm current utility-locate markings and resolve conflicts. Verify the locate markings remain valid and identify any point where the approved route conflicts with a marked utility.

F. Layout and Grade Reference

  1. Mark trench centerline, width limits, access points, crossings, and termination points. Transfer the approved route to the ground using the specified marking method.
  2. Establish grade stakes, invert references, or laser controls. Set references sufficient to verify depth and slope throughout excavation.
  3. Define equipment routes, spoil locations, barricades, and public-protection measures. Mark these locations before excavation equipment enters the work area.
  4. Inspect excavation equipment and verify operator and competent-person responsibilities. Confirm equipment condition and identify the competent person responsible for excavation safety before work begins.

G. Controlled Excavation

  1. Excavate in controlled passes, using hand-dig or vacuum methods within required tolerance zones. Reduce excavation method to hand-dig or vacuum excavation whenever the trench approaches a marked utility tolerance zone.
  2. Maintain required trench width and avoid unnecessary disturbance. Limit the excavated width to the specified dimension along the full route.
  3. Check depth, slope, and alignment at regular intervals. Verify against the established grade references at the specified checking interval.
  4. Keep spoil, equipment, and materials at safe distances from trench edges. Maintain the specified clearance to prevent surcharge and edge collapse.

H. Protection, Bearing Surface, and Bedding

  1. Install protective systems, ladders, ramps, or other required controls. Configure each protective system to its manufacturer-rated depth and soil type before entry.
  2. Remove loose soil, rocks, organic material, and water from the trench bottom. Clear the bearing surface immediately before bedding placement.
  3. Place and shape approved bedding material where required. Place bedding to the specified thickness and gradation along the prepared trench bottom.
  4. Protect existing utilities and support exposed crossings using the approved method. Install the specified support or protection at every crossing before further excavation nearby.

I. Separation, Documentation, and Inspection

  1. Verify separation among utility systems. Measure horizontal and vertical clearance at every location where systems run in proximity.
  2. Mark and document unexpected utilities, unsuitable soil, groundwater, or instability. Record the location and nature of every unexpected condition encountered.
  3. Stop work before changing the approved route or depth. Obtain direction before any deviation from the approved plan proceeds.
  4. Inspect trench bottom, bedding, slope, dimensions, access, and protective controls. Complete the internal pre-installation inspection before utility installation begins.
  5. Prepare the trench for utility installation and inspection. Confirm the trench is ready for the specified utility and any required official inspection.
  6. Protect the open trench until installation and backfill are complete. Maintain barricades, coverings, and protective systems for the full duration the trench remains open.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the completed utility trench using the same checks regardless of trench length or utility type.

  • Route and layout check: confirm the centerline, width limits, crossings, and terminations match the approved plan and remain marked and readable.
  • Dimension check: confirm trench width, depth, and slope match the approved plan within the stated tolerance.
  • Bearing surface check: confirm the trench bottom is free of loose soil, rocks, organic material, and standing water.
  • Protection check: confirm protective systems and access ladders or ramps are installed per the manufacturer's rated configuration and the specified interval.
  • Separation check: confirm required separation among utility systems and protection of every crossing.
  • Documentation check: confirm the pre-installation inspection record is complete and current.

Complete the Utility Trench Excavation Record Sheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before utility installation proceeds.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Trench Depth or Slope Deviates From the Approved Plan

Probable cause: grade references were not checked at the specified interval, or the excavation equipment overexcavated a section between checks.
Confirmation: measure depth and slope against the established grade references at closer intervals along the affected section.
Correction: obtain direction before correcting overexcavation; add approved fill or adjust the invert reference as directed.
Reinspection: re-check depth and slope along the corrected section before proceeding.

Unexpected Utility Encountered During Excavation

Probable cause: the utility was not marked, was marked incorrectly, or the locate markings had expired before excavation began.
Confirmation: stop excavation at the location, expose the utility using hand-dig or vacuum methods, and document its position.
Correction: obtain direction from the responsible party before continuing excavation in the affected segment.
Reinspection: reverify the resolved route and updated utility-locate status before excavation resumes.

Trench Wall Shows Signs of Instability

Probable cause: the protective system was not rated for the excavated depth or soil type, or soil conditions changed from what was anticipated.
Confirmation: inspect the wall condition and compare the installed protective system against its manufacturer rating for the actual depth and soil type.
Correction: stop work, evacuate the trench, and obtain direction from the competent person before installing a correctly rated protective system.
Reinspection: reverify wall stability and protective-system configuration before reentry.

Water Accumulates in the Trench Bottom

Probable cause: groundwater was encountered, or surface runoff entered the trench due to inadequate erosion control or grading around the excavation.
Confirmation: inspect the source of water entry and check the Project 8 erosion-control system for a failure near the trench.
Correction: remove standing water from the trench bottom, correct the erosion-control failure if one is found, and obtain direction if groundwater is present.
Reinspection: reverify the trench bottom is clear of water before bedding placement resumes.

Required Separation Not Met at a Utility Crossing

Probable cause: the trench was excavated without measuring separation against the adjacent system, or the adjacent system was not at its plan elevation.
Confirmation: measure horizontal and vertical separation at the affected crossing against the approved plan requirement.
Correction: obtain direction before adjusting trench depth, route, or crossing support to achieve the required separation.
Reinspection: reverify separation at the corrected crossing before installation proceeds.

Bedding Material Displaced or Contaminated Before Installation

Probable cause: the trench was left open and exposed to traffic, rainfall, or sloughing soil after bedding was placed.
Confirmation: inspect the bedding surface for displacement, mixing with trench-wall soil, or visible contamination.
Correction: remove and replace the affected bedding material to the specified thickness and gradation.
Reinspection: reverify bedding condition immediately before utility installation.
