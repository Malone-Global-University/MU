Job Overview

This project qualifies you to restore designated disturbed areas by correcting grade, replacing topsoil, stabilizing soil, reestablishing vegetation, and repairing affected property. The finished work product is restored and stabilized disturbed areas that support drainage, vegetation, and property handoff. This project begins only after the final site drainage of Project 14 and the completed foundation inspection preparation of Project 16 have established the grades and site conditions this restoration must match, and it does not include structural backfill or compaction addressed in Project 12, does not include establishing the final drainage grades themselves as covered in Project 14, and does not include the general site housekeeping and equipment demobilization covered in Project 18.

You will review restoration limits, the final-grade plan, vegetation specification, seasonal conditions, and owner requirements; remove construction waste, excess aggregate, temporary materials, and contaminated soil; repair compacted, rutted, eroded, or settlement-damaged areas; re-establish drainage grades and confirm restoration will not block swales or outlets; scarify compacted subsoil where appropriate; place approved topsoil to the specified depth; fine-grade the surface and remove rocks, roots, clods, and debris; prepare the seedbed or planting area using the specified method; apply seed, sod, plants, or other specified vegetation; apply mulch, tackifier, or erosion-control blanket where required; anchor blankets and protect edges against undermining; stabilize slopes, drainage paths, and outlet areas; restore fences, gates, paving edges, property markers, and affected improvements; water or establish the required initial maintenance condition; inspect for bare areas, washout, ponding, poor soil contact, displaced blanket, and damage; correct failed areas and document the correction; establish the owner or contractor maintenance schedule; and complete the restoration record with photographs and material disposition.

This is the seventeenth project in the site preparation sequence. It depends on the final site drainage established in Project 14, the temporary access built in Project 15, and the internal pre-inspection record produced in Project 16, and it uses the erosion-control and stockpile information recorded in Projects 4 and 8. The restoration record produced here becomes part of the evidence reviewed during the final site cleanup of Project 18 and the complete site-readiness inspection of Project 19.

Where This Skill Fits

The final site drainage established in Project 14 and the site conditions verified during the pre-inspection review of Project 16 must already be in place; restoration performed before these grades are settled can be undone by later corrective grading. This project restores only the areas designated for restoration and must not block or alter the drainage established in Project 14.

This project stands between the completed sitework of Projects 1 through 16 and the final site cleanup and readiness inspection of Projects 18 and 19. Its stabilization and vegetation work protects exposed soil from erosion during the remainder of construction, and its restoration record becomes part of the evidence reviewed during the complete site-readiness inspection of Project 19. A restoration that is rushed, performed out of season without an approved alternate method, or performed before drainage is finalized can fail and require rework before site handoff.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing restoration limits, the final-grade plan, vegetation specification, seasonal conditions, and owner requirements
  • removing construction waste, excess aggregate, temporary materials, and contaminated soil from restoration areas
  • repairing compacted, rutted, eroded, or settlement-damaged areas
  • re-establishing drainage grades within the restoration area without blocking swales or outlets
  • scarifying compacted subsoil where appropriate
  • placing approved topsoil to the specified depth
  • fine-grading the surface and removing rocks, roots, clods, and debris
  • preparing the seedbed or planting area using the specified method
  • applying seed, sod, plants, or other specified vegetation
  • applying mulch, tackifier, or erosion-control blanket where required
  • anchoring blankets and protecting edges against undermining
  • stabilizing slopes, drainage paths, and outlet areas
  • restoring fences, gates, paving edges, property markers, and affected improvements
  • watering or establishing the required initial maintenance condition
  • inspecting, correcting, and documenting restoration areas, and establishing the maintenance schedule

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • alter the final drainage grades established in Project 14 to make restoration easier
  • restore an area outside the approved restoration limits without owner or supervisor authorization
  • substitute an unspecified seed mix, sod type, or plant material for the specified vegetation without approval
  • perform structural backfill or compaction work that belongs to Project 12
  • conceal a settlement, erosion, or drainage defect by covering it with topsoil or vegetation instead of correcting it
  • remove or relocate a property marker, fence, or boundary improvement without the approved reference from Project 1

Any condition where topsoil supply, seasonal planting window, or an owner requirement conflicts with the approved restoration plan must be escalated before proceeding.

Trade Note

The final-grade plan, vegetation specification, seasonal planting window, and owner requirements referenced throughout this project are supplied by the project plan, landscape specification, or property owner, and are not generated by this chapter. Where this chapter refers to "the specified topsoil depth" or "the specified seed mix," it means those supplied values; do not substitute a value from general trade familiarity or from a prior unrelated project.

Setup Standard

Do not begin topsoil placement or seedbed preparation until compacted, rutted, eroded, or settlement-damaged areas are repaired and drainage grades within the restoration area are re-established (Step 4). Do not request final acceptance of the restoration until every inspected deficiency is corrected, documented, and the owner or contractor maintenance schedule is established (Step 17).

Shop Tip

Confirm the seasonal planting window before scheduling seed or sod work. Restoring an area outside the specified vegetation's establishment season can produce a bare or washed-out area even when every other step is performed correctly.

Common Field Fundamentals

Restoration only serves its purpose if it matches the final-grade plan established in Project 14 and stabilizes the soil before the next rainfall or wind event. Placing topsoil or vegetation over an uncorrected grade, drainage, or compaction defect conceals a problem instead of solving it, and a restoration performed out of season without an approved alternate method can fail before vegetation establishes.

Working Through the Restoration Sequence

Each category of this sequence depends on the categories before it. Topsoil placed over an uncorrected grade defect will not hold the specified elevation, and vegetation applied over a poorly prepared seedbed will not establish reliably. Work through Steps 3 through 14 of this project in the order given, and recheck an earlier category if a later step reveals a discrepancy. If topsoil supply, seasonal timing, or an owner requirement conflicts with the approved restoration plan, stop and escalate before continuing; do not substitute an unapproved material or method.

Measurement Cue — Grade and Drainage Verification

Verify restored elevations and slopes against the final-grade plan and established benchmark before and after topsoil placement. Confirm that restored areas maintain the positive drainage established in Project 14 and do not block or reverse the flow of any swale or outlet.

Code Alert — Seasonal and Owner Requirements

Confirm the seasonal planting window and any owner, jurisdictional, or erosion-control requirement governing the vegetation specification before beginning seedbed preparation. Requirements vary by location, season, and project type, and do not assume a method used on a prior site applies here.

Manufacturer Cue — Seed, Sod, and Erosion-Control Blanket

Follow the manufacturer's instructions for seed application rate, sod installation, and erosion-control blanket anchoring pattern. A blanket anchored at wider than the specified spacing, or seed applied below the specified rate, may fail to establish or stabilize the area as intended.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Conceal an Uncorrected Defect

Do not place topsoil, seed, sod, mulch, or blanket over a settlement, erosion, or drainage defect that has not been corrected. Stop work immediately if a defect is found that exceeds this project's authorized scope, and escalate to the responsible supervisor, engineer, or owner before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Slopes and Restoration Equipment

Maintain stable footing and equipment control when working on restored slopes, and keep restoration equipment clear of active drainage paths and adjacent excavation. Confirm the work area is free of trip, slip, and equipment hazards before and during restoration work.

E. Preparation — Scope and Removal

  1. Review restoration limits, the final-grade plan, vegetation specification, seasonal conditions, and owner requirements. Confirm every value against the supplied project documents before proceeding.
  2. Remove construction waste, excess aggregate, temporary materials, and contaminated soil. Clear the restoration area to bare, disturbed ground before grade repair begins.

F. Grade and Drainage Repair

  1. Repair compacted, rutted, eroded, or settlement-damaged areas. Correct each condition using the approved fill, scarification, or reshaping method.
  2. Re-establish drainage grades and confirm restoration will not block swales or outlets. Verify slope and flow direction against the Project 14 final-grade plan before continuing.

G. Topsoil and Seedbed Preparation

  1. Scarify compacted subsoil where appropriate. Loosen the surface to promote topsoil bonding and root penetration where the specification requires it.
  2. Place approved topsoil to the specified depth. Verify depth at intervals across the restoration area.
  3. Fine-grade the surface and remove rocks, roots, clods, and debris. Prepare a uniform, plantable surface before seedbed preparation.
  4. Prepare the seedbed or planting area using the specified method. Follow the vegetation specification for seedbed preparation, tilling, or planting-hole requirements.

H. Vegetation and Stabilization

  1. Apply seed, sod, plants, or other specified vegetation. Apply at the rate and method specified by the vegetation specification.
  2. Apply mulch, tackifier, or erosion-control blanket where required. Cover seeded or sensitive areas per the approved method.
  3. Anchor blankets and protect edges against undermining. Install anchors at the manufacturer-specified spacing and secure all terminating edges.
  4. Stabilize slopes, drainage paths, and outlet areas. Confirm stabilization extends to every area exposed to concentrated flow or slope instability.

I. Property Repair, Inspection, and Closeout

  1. Restore fences, gates, paving edges, property markers, and affected improvements. Repair or replace each affected improvement to its required condition.
  2. Water or establish the required initial maintenance condition. Provide the watering or establishment condition specified for the vegetation applied.
  3. Inspect for bare areas, washout, ponding, poor soil contact, displaced blanket, and damage. Record every condition requiring correction.
  4. Correct failed areas and document the correction. Complete each correction and update the restoration record.
  5. Establish the owner or contractor maintenance schedule. Record the required watering, mowing, or inspection frequency until vegetation is established.
  6. Complete the restoration record with photographs and material disposition. Confirm every item, correction, and material quantity used is documented before closeout.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the completed restoration using the same checks regardless of restoration area size or vegetation type.

  • Waste and debris check: confirm construction waste, excess aggregate, and contaminated soil are removed from the restoration area.
  • Grade and drainage check: confirm repaired grades match the final-grade plan and do not block swales or outlets.
  • Topsoil and seedbed check: confirm topsoil depth and fine-grade condition meet the specification.
  • Vegetation check: confirm seed, sod, or plant material is applied per the vegetation specification.
  • Stabilization check: confirm mulch, tackifier, or blanket is applied, anchored, and free of undermined edges.
  • Property-repair check: confirm fences, gates, paving edges, and markers are restored to the required condition.

Complete the Restoration Record Sheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before restoration is presented for acceptance.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Restored Area Ponds Water or Reverses Slope

Probable cause: topsoil was placed without confirming grade against the Project 14 final-grade plan, or settlement occurred after grading.
Confirmation: measure slope and elevation across the restored area and compare against the final-grade plan and benchmark.
Correction: remove and reset topsoil to restore the specified slope and elevation, directing flow toward the approved outlet.
Reinspection: reverify slope and flow direction across the corrected area before closing the deficiency.

Seed or Sod Fails to Establish

Probable cause: seedbed preparation, application rate, seasonal timing, or initial watering did not match the vegetation specification.
Confirmation: inspect the bare or thin area and compare seedbed condition, application method, and watering record against the specification.
Correction: reprepare the seedbed and reapply seed, sod, or plant material per the specification, and reestablish the required watering condition.
Reinspection: reinspect the area at the interval specified for vegetation establishment and document the result.

Erosion-Control Blanket Is Displaced or Undermined

Probable cause: anchors were installed at wider than the manufacturer-specified spacing, or a terminating edge was not secured against runoff.
Confirmation: inspect the affected edge and compare anchor spacing and termination against the manufacturer's instructions.
Correction: reset or add anchors at the specified spacing and rebuild the terminating edge to prevent undermining.
Reinspection: reinspect the corrected edge after the next rainfall or controlled water test.

Restored Grade Conceals an Uncorrected Settlement or Drainage Defect

Probable cause: topsoil or vegetation was applied before the underlying compaction, rut, or drainage condition was corrected.
Confirmation: probe or excavate a test area and compare subsurface condition against the Project 14 acceptance standard.
Correction: remove topsoil and vegetation from the affected area, correct the underlying defect, and reapply topsoil and vegetation.
Reinspection: reverify the corrected subsurface and surface condition before closing the deficiency.

Restored Fence, Gate, or Marker Does Not Match Pre-Construction Condition

Probable cause: repair used a non-matching material, or the original reference location was not confirmed before repair.
Confirmation: compare the repaired improvement against the Project 1 existing-conditions record and photographs.
Correction: reposition or replace the repair using matching or approved-equivalent material at the confirmed location.
Reinspection: reinspect the corrected improvement against the existing-conditions record before closing the deficiency.

Restoration Extends Beyond the Approved Limits

Probable cause: the restoration limits were not clearly marked before work began, or adjacent disturbed ground was mistaken for the authorized area.
Confirmation: compare the disturbed area against the approved restoration limits recorded before work began.
Correction: obtain owner or supervisor authorization for the additional area, or restore only the approved area and document the discrepancy.
Reinspection: reverify the restored area against the approved or amended limits before closing the deficiency.
