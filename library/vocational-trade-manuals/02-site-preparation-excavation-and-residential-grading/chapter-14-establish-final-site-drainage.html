Job Overview

This project qualifies you to shape and stabilize final site grades so water drains away from the building and reaches the approved discharge areas. The finished work product is a stable final grade that provides positive drainage and approved runoff control. This project begins only after the foundation drainage installed in Project 13 is complete, protected, and connected to an approved outlet, and after the backfill of Project 12 has settled sufficiently for final shaping; it does not include installing foundation drainage pipe, aggregate, or filter fabric, does not include paving, driveway, or walkway construction, and does not include planting or seeding beyond the topsoil and stabilization required to protect the finished grade.

You will review the approved final-grading and drainage plan; verify foundation, finished-floor, paving, and topsoil-allowance elevations; inspect backfill settlement, utility trenches, low areas, discharge routes, and boundaries; re-establish final grade stakes and drainage-control points; place or remove soil to achieve the required grade; establish positive slope away from the foundation; shape swales with continuous flow lines and stable side slopes; blend transitions without ponding; coordinate hardscape and downspout drainage; protect septic, well, utility, and easement areas; add topsoil only after structural grading requirements are met; stabilize concentrated-flow paths; direct downspout and sump discharge to approved locations; conduct a measured slope review and observe runoff; correct deficiencies; and complete the final drainage inspection.

This is the fourteenth project in the site preparation sequence. It depends directly on the completed foundation drainage of Project 13 and the protected backfill of Project 12, and it uses the benchmark and layout controls established in Projects 1 through 3. The final grade established here must be complete and inspected before the restoration work of Project 17 begins, and it directly controls the runoff behavior evaluated in the site-readiness inspection of Project 19.

  • SP-079 — Review the approved final-grading and drainage plan, and verify foundation reference elevations, finished-floor elevation, paving elevations, and topsoil allowance.
  • SP-080 — Inspect backfill settlement, utility trenches, low areas, discharge routes, and boundaries, and re-establish final grade stakes and drainage-control points.
  • SP-081 — Place or remove soil to achieve required grade below building components, establish positive slope away from the foundation for the required distance, and shape swales with continuous flow lines and stable side slopes.
  • SP-082 — Blend high and low points without creating ponding areas, coordinate driveway, walkway, patio, downspout, and landscape drainage, and protect septic, well, utility, and easement areas.
  • SP-083 — Compact or settle fill sufficiently for the intended surface treatment, add topsoil only after structural grading requirements are met, stabilize concentrated-flow paths, and direct downspouts and sump discharge to approved locations.
  • SP-084 — Conduct a measured slope review, observe runoff during rainfall or a controlled water test where appropriate, correct reverse slope, ponding, erosion, overtopping, and discharge conflicts, document final elevations, slopes, swales, outlets, and corrections, and complete the final drainage inspection.

Where This Skill Fits

The foundation drainage installed and inspected in Project 13 must already be discharging correctly at its verified outlet before this project ties final grades into that same discharge point; a foundation drainage system that has not been tested and inspected is a stop-work condition for beginning final grading in that area. The protected backfill of Project 12 must have settled sufficiently that final shaping will not be undone by ongoing consolidation.

The final grade established here must remain stable and functional through the restoration work of Project 17, the housekeeping of Project 18, and the complete site-readiness inspection of Project 19; do not allow restoration planting, stockpile removal, or cleanup traffic to disturb a verified swale or reverse a verified slope. The positive drainage confirmed in this project becomes one of the required conditions the Project 19 inspection checks before the site is certified ready for foundation construction handoff.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved final-grading and drainage plan
  • verifying foundation reference elevations, finished-floor elevation, paving elevations, and topsoil allowance
  • inspecting backfill settlement, utility trenches, low areas, discharge routes, and boundaries
  • re-establishing final grade stakes and drainage-control points
  • placing or removing soil to achieve required grade below building components
  • establishing positive slope away from the foundation for the required distance
  • shaping swales with continuous flow lines and stable side slopes
  • blending high and low points without creating ponding areas
  • coordinating driveway, walkway, patio, downspout, and landscape drainage
  • protecting septic, well, utility, and easement areas
  • compacting or settling fill sufficiently for the intended surface treatment
  • adding topsoil only after structural grading requirements are met
  • stabilizing concentrated-flow paths using approved methods
  • directing downspout and sump discharge to approved locations
  • conducting a measured slope review and observing runoff during rainfall or a controlled water test where appropriate
  • correcting reverse slope, ponding, erosion, overtopping, and discharge conflicts, documenting final conditions, and completing the final drainage inspection

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin final grading before foundation drainage is inspected, connected, and functioning at its verified outlet
  • install or modify foundation drainage pipe, aggregate, or filter fabric
  • construct paving, driveway, walkway, or patio surfaces
  • add topsoil before structural grading and required elevations are met
  • create or accept a reverse slope, low spot, or ponding area anywhere within the graded scope
  • direct downspout, sump, or swale discharge to an unapproved location, a neighboring property, or a septic or well protection zone
  • disturb protected utility, easement, septic, or well areas without required protection measures in place
  • conceal an unverified grade or an untested drainage path by proceeding directly to restoration or cleanup

Any condition requiring a revised elevation, an alternate discharge point, or an engineer's determination must be escalated before work proceeds in that area.

Trade Note

The finished-floor elevation, paving elevations, topsoil allowance, minimum slope distance and percentage, swale locations, and approved discharge points referenced throughout this project are supplied by the approved final-grading and drainage plan, geotechnical report, or responsible design professional, and are not generated by this chapter. Where this chapter refers to "the required slope" or "the approved discharge route," it means those supplied values; do not select a slope, swale location, or discharge point from general trade familiarity.

Setup Standard

Do not begin final grading until the foundation drainage of Project 13 is inspected and functioning at its verified outlet (Step 1). Do not add topsoil until structural grading and required elevations are verified and met (Step 12).

Shop Tip

Check slope away from the foundation first, before shaping the rest of the site. If that primary slope is wrong, every swale, blend, and topsoil lift built off of it inherits the same error, and the correction becomes far more disruptive once secondary grading is complete.

Common Field Fundamentals

Final site drainage only performs its function if every graded surface maintains a continuous downward slope toward an approved discharge point, transitions between high and low areas are blended without flat or reverse sections, and concentrated-flow paths are stabilized before they can erode. A single flat spot near the foundation, an unstabilized swale, or an unapproved discharge location can defeat an otherwise correctly graded site, and once topsoil, planting, or paving is placed, correcting these defects becomes far more disruptive.

Working Across the Graded Site

Every area shaped depends on the grade-control point established before it and on the drainage route it feeds into downstream. Recheck slope continuity at every transition, swale, and discharge point throughout Steps 5 through 11 of this project. If a field condition suggests the finished-floor elevation is incorrect, backfill has settled beyond an acceptable range, or a required slope cannot be achieved as specified, stop and obtain direction before continuing; do not force a grade to an incorrect elevation to avoid a delay.

Measurement Cue — Slope, Distance, and Discharge Verification

Verify slope and elevation at every grade-control point, swale, transition, and discharge location, and confirm the required slope distance away from the foundation before topsoil is placed. Where no specific verification interval is stated by the approved plan, verify at intervals no greater than the spacing of the grade stakes established for the site. An unverified slope discovered after topsoil placement may require removing and regrading completed work.

Code Alert — Minimum Slope and Discharge Requirements

Confirm the required minimum slope away from the foundation, permitted discharge locations, easement protections, and any required final-grading inspection from the approved plan or applicable regulatory authority before work begins. These requirements vary by jurisdiction, lot condition, and neighboring property, and do not assume a slope percentage or discharge condition used on a prior site applies here.

Manufacturer Cue — Erosion-Control and Stabilization Materials

Follow the manufacturer's instructions for erosion-control blanket anchoring and overlap, tackifier application, and seed or sod establishment where these materials are used to stabilize swales and slopes. Installing stabilization material without the specified anchoring or overlap can allow the material to fail during the first significant rainfall.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work Before Accepting an Unverified Grade

Do not place topsoil, stabilization material, or proceed to restoration work over a graded area until slope, elevation, and drainage continuity have been verified and the required inspection has been completed. Stop work immediately if a reverse slope, ponding area, unstabilized concentrated-flow path, or an unapproved discharge condition is discovered, and obtain direction before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Grading Equipment and Slope Hazards

Maintain safe footing and equipment routes on graded slopes and around swales, keep workers clear of equipment operating on cut-and-fill areas, and maintain clear access for inspection before topsoil and stabilization conceal the finished grade.

E. Preparation — Readiness Confirmation and Plan Review

  1. Review the approved final-grading and drainage plan. Confirm finished-floor elevation, paving elevations, topsoil allowance, minimum slope, swale locations, and discharge points against the approved plan before work begins.
  2. Verify foundation reference elevations, finished-floor elevation, paving elevations, and topsoil allowance. Confirm each value at its corresponding control point before shaping begins.

F. Site Inspection and Reference Re-Establishment

  1. Inspect backfill settlement, utility trenches, low areas, discharge routes, and boundaries. Walk the full graded area and identify every condition requiring correction before shaping proceeds.
  2. Re-establish final grade stakes and drainage-control points. Restore stakes and control points from the protected benchmark and layout references.

G. Structural Grading and Slope Establishment

  1. Place or remove soil to achieve required grade below building components. Cut or fill to the elevation established by the approved plan, verified against grade-control points.
  2. Establish positive slope away from the foundation for the required distance. Verify the slope percentage and distance at intervals along the full foundation perimeter within scope.
  3. Shape swales with continuous flow lines and stable side slopes. Verify each swale's flow line elevation from origin to its approved discharge point.

H. Coordination and Protection

  1. Blend high and low points without creating ponding areas. Verify each transition against the required continuous slope on both sides.
  2. Coordinate driveway, walkway, patio, downspout, and landscape drainage. Confirm each connection routes water consistently with the approved plan and does not conflict with an adjacent drainage feature.
  3. Protect septic, well, utility, and easement areas. Confirm protective measures remain in place throughout grading operations in these areas.

I. Topsoil, Stabilization, and Discharge

  1. Compact or settle fill sufficiently for the intended surface treatment. Confirm the fill condition matches the requirement for the planned surface before proceeding.
  2. Add topsoil only after structural grading requirements are met. Verify structural grade elevations before placing topsoil.
  3. Stabilize concentrated-flow paths using approved methods. Install erosion-control blanket, mulch, or other specified stabilization at every identified concentrated-flow location.
  4. Direct downspouts and sump discharge to approved locations. Confirm each discharge point matches the approved plan and does not create a conflict with a neighboring property or protected area.

J. Verification, Documentation, and Inspection

  1. Conduct a measured slope review. Measure and record slope at every grade-control point, swale, and transition within scope.
  2. Observe runoff during rainfall or a controlled water test where appropriate. Confirm water moves continuously toward the approved discharge points without collecting or overtopping.
  3. Correct reverse slope, ponding, erosion, overtopping, and discharge conflicts. Resolve every deficiency identified during the review or water test before proceeding.
  4. Document final elevations, slopes, swales, outlets, and corrections. Photograph and record the completed grade and drainage features.
  5. Complete the final drainage inspection. Obtain the required sign-off before restoration work of Project 17 begins in the graded area.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the completed final site drainage using the same checks regardless of site area or number of swales.

  • Slope check: confirm continuous positive slope away from the foundation for the required distance, with no reverse sections or flat spots.
  • Swale check: confirm continuous flow lines and stable side slopes from origin to approved discharge points.
  • Transition check: confirm high and low points are blended without ponding areas.
  • Coordination check: confirm hardscape and downspout drainage routes match the approved plan without conflicts.
  • Protected-area check: confirm septic, well, utility, and easement areas remain protected and undisturbed beyond the approved scope.
  • Documentation check: confirm the slope review, runoff observation, and inspection record is complete and current, with photographs taken before restoration proceeds.

Complete the Final Site Drainage Record Sheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before restoration work begins.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Slope Away From the Foundation Shows a Flat Spot or Reverse Section

Probable cause: grade-control points were not verified at sufficient intervals, or fill settled unevenly after placement.
Confirmation: measure elevation at the affected section and at adjacent grade-control points, and compare against the required minimum slope.
Correction: remove or add soil at the affected section to restore the required continuous slope, and recheck the sections on both sides of the correction.
Reinspection: reverify slope continuity across the corrected section and the adjoining grade before topsoil is placed.

Swale Holds Water Instead of Conveying It to the Discharge Point

Probable cause: the swale's flow line was not verified along its full length, or a transition point was blended incorrectly.
Confirmation: measure the flow-line elevation at multiple points along the swale and compare against the approved continuous grade.
Correction: reshape the affected section to restore a continuous downward flow line toward the approved discharge point.
Reinspection: observe water movement through the corrected section during a controlled water test or the next rainfall.

Downspout or Sump Discharge Conflicts With a Neighboring Property or Protected Area

Probable cause: the discharge point was routed without confirming it against the approved plan, or an existing grade change redirected the discharge path.
Confirmation: trace the discharge route from its origin to its termination and compare it against the approved final-grading and drainage plan.
Correction: reroute the discharge to the approved location, or escalate to the responsible design professional if the approved discharge point cannot be achieved.
Reinspection: reverify the discharge route and termination before the system is documented as complete.

Concentrated-Flow Path Shows Erosion or Undermining

Probable cause: stabilization material was not installed at the concentrated-flow location, or it was installed without the required anchoring or overlap.
Confirmation: inspect the affected path and compare the installed stabilization against the manufacturer's anchoring and overlap requirement.
Correction: repair the eroded section, reshape as needed, and reinstall stabilization material per the manufacturer's instructions.
Reinspection: observe the corrected path during a controlled water test or the next rainfall.

Topsoil Placed Before Structural Grade Requirements Were Verified

Probable cause: topsoil was placed by estimate without confirming structural grade elevations first.
Confirmation: measure elevation beneath the topsoil layer at several points and compare against the required structural grade.
Correction: remove topsoil in the affected area, correct the structural grade, and replace topsoil to the specified allowance.
Reinspection: recheck structural grade and topsoil depth across the corrected section.

Protected Septic, Well, Utility, or Easement Area Is Disturbed During Grading

Probable cause: protective markings were not confirmed before cut-and-fill operations, or equipment routes were not adjusted to avoid the protected area.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area and compare its condition against the approved plan's protection requirements.
Correction: stop work in the affected area and obtain direction from the responsible trade or design professional before continuing; do not conceal the disturbance with additional fill or topsoil.
Reinspection: confirm the area is restored or accepted per the responsible party's direction before grading resumes nearby.

Cleanup

This project ends with a stable, inspected final grade ready for restoration, not a restored or landscaped site.

  • Confirm the completed grade and swales are protected from traffic, erosion, and disturbance
  • Confirm grade stakes and drainage-control points remain visible and undisturbed where still needed for restoration
  • Confirm all discharge points remain accessible and free of blockage
  • Remove surplus fill, stabilization material scraps, and debris from the work area and equipment routes
  • File the completed final site drainage record, slope review results, and photographs with the Project 01 through 13 records
