Job Overview

This project qualifies you to construct and maintain a temporary aggregate access route suitable for approved construction traffic and site conditions. The finished work product is a stable, drained, maintainable temporary access route suitable for approved construction traffic. This project begins only after the rough grade of Project 10 has established the general site elevations the route will follow, and it does not include installing the permanent driveway, does not include final paving or curbing, and does not include grading beyond the approved access limits.

You will review the access plan, vehicle types, route width, turning requirements, grades, drainage crossings, and road connection; verify permission, right-of-way limits, utility conflicts, and sight-distance requirements; mark the access centerline, edges, staging areas, and no-entry zones; clear only the approved route and preserve protected vegetation and drainage; strip unsuitable organic material where required; shape and compact the subgrade; install geotextile or separation fabric where specified; place and compact aggregate in the required lifts and thickness; install culvert, ditch crossing, or edge drainage where required; stabilize the entrance connection; verify route dimensions and bearing condition; conduct a controlled vehicle pass; correct deficiencies; and establish inspection and maintenance procedures.

This is the fifteenth project in the site preparation sequence. It depends on the rough grade established in Project 10 and the erosion and sediment controls installed in Project 8, and it uses the site-evaluation and hazard information recorded in Project 1. The access route built here must remain functional and safe through the remaining sitework, inspection, and restoration projects, and it is one of the conditions reviewed during the site-readiness inspection of Project 19.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • SP-085 — Review the access plan, vehicle types, route width, turning requirements, grades, drainage crossings, and road connection, and verify permission, right-of-way limits, utility conflicts, and sight-distance requirements.
  • SP-086 — Mark access centerline, edges, staging areas, and no-entry zones, clear only the approved route while preserving protected vegetation and drainage, and strip unsuitable organic material where required.
  • SP-087 — Shape and compact the subgrade, install geotextile or separation fabric where specified, and place the first aggregate lift using the approved stone size.
  • SP-088 — Spread aggregate uniformly while maintaining required crown or cross slope, compact each lift with suitable equipment, and place and compact additional aggregate to the required thickness.
  • SP-089 — Install culvert, ditch crossing, or edge drainage where required, and stabilize the entrance connection to reduce mud tracking.
  • SP-090 — Verify route width, clearance, slope, turning radius, and bearing condition, conduct a controlled vehicle pass using an approved representative vehicle, inspect for rutting, pumping, edge failure, loose stone, drainage blockage, and unsafe sight conditions, correct failed areas before delivery traffic proceeds, establish inspection and maintenance procedures, and remove or restore the route when its temporary purpose ends.

Where This Skill Fits

The rough grade established in Project 10 must already reflect the general elevations the access route will follow, and the erosion and sediment controls installed in Project 8 must remain functional along and adjacent to the route; a rough grade or erosion-control system that has not been verified is a stop-work condition for beginning access construction in that area. The route depends on the site-evaluation record from Project 1 to confirm access constraints, hazards, and neighboring conditions identified before any site work began.

The access route built here must remain safe and functional through excavation, utility, backfill, drainage, and restoration work in Projects 5 through 17, and it must support delivery, equipment, and emergency-vehicle traffic until its temporary purpose ends. The route's condition, drainage performance, and maintenance record become part of the evidence reviewed during the complete site-readiness inspection of Project 19, and its removal or restoration is completed as part of the final cleanup in Project 18.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the access plan, vehicle types, route width, turning requirements, grades, drainage crossings, and road connection
  • verifying permission, right-of-way limits, utility conflicts, and sight-distance requirements
  • marking access centerline, edges, staging areas, and no-entry zones
  • clearing only the approved route while preserving protected vegetation and drainage
  • stripping unsuitable organic material where required
  • shaping and compacting the subgrade
  • installing geotextile or separation fabric where specified
  • placing and compacting aggregate in the approved stone size and required lift thickness
  • maintaining required crown or cross slope while spreading aggregate
  • installing culvert, ditch crossing, or edge drainage where required
  • stabilizing the entrance connection to reduce mud tracking
  • verifying route width, clearance, slope, turning radius, and bearing condition
  • conducting a controlled vehicle pass using an approved representative vehicle
  • inspecting for rutting, pumping, edge failure, loose stone, drainage blockage, and unsafe sight conditions
  • correcting failed areas, establishing inspection and maintenance procedures, and removing or restoring the route when its temporary purpose ends

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin route construction before permission, right-of-way limits, utility-locate clearance, and sight-distance requirements are verified
  • clear or disturb ground outside the approved access limits, staging areas, or no-entry zones
  • install or modify a permanent driveway, curb, or paved surface
  • open the route to delivery or construction traffic before the controlled vehicle pass and correction of any failed area are complete
  • connect the route to a public road or right-of-way without the required permit, permission, or sight-distance verification
  • conceal an unverified subgrade or bearing condition by placing additional aggregate over it
  • remove the temporary route or its erosion controls before an authorized decision confirms its temporary purpose has ended

Any condition requiring a revised route location, an alternate road connection, or an engineer's determination must be escalated before work proceeds in that area.

Trade Note

The approved route width, turning radius, stone size, lift thickness, total compacted thickness, crown or cross-slope percentage, and culvert or drainage-crossing details referenced throughout this project are supplied by the approved access plan, geotechnical report, or responsible design professional, and are not generated by this chapter. Where this chapter refers to "the required thickness" or "the approved stone size," it means those supplied values; do not select a stone size, thickness, or route width from general trade familiarity.

Setup Standard

Do not begin clearing or subgrade work until permission, right-of-way limits, utility-locate clearance, and sight-distance requirements are verified (Step 2). Do not open the route to delivery or construction traffic until the controlled vehicle pass is complete and every identified deficiency is corrected (Step 17).

Shop Tip

Verify the subgrade compaction and bearing condition before placing the first aggregate lift, not after. Aggregate placed over a soft or unverified subgrade will pump and rut under the first loaded delivery truck, and correcting it then requires removing aggregate that has already been placed and compacted.

Common Field Fundamentals

A temporary access route only performs its function if the subgrade beneath it can bear the approved vehicle loading, the aggregate thickness and stone size match the specification for that loading, the route maintains a crown or cross slope that sheds water instead of holding it, and the entrance connection is stabilized enough to prevent mud from reaching the public or site road. A route that looks complete but has not been verified with a controlled vehicle pass can fail on the first loaded delivery, and a failed route can delay every trade that depends on site access.

Working Along the Access Route

Every segment of the route depends on the subgrade condition beneath it and on the drainage path it must not obstruct. Recheck compaction, thickness, and crown at every station along the route throughout Steps 6 through 12 of this project. If a field condition suggests the subgrade cannot bear the approved vehicle loading, an unexpected utility or drainage conflict is found, or the required route width or turning radius cannot be achieved as specified, stop and obtain direction before continuing; do not narrow the route or reduce aggregate thickness to avoid a delay.

Measurement Cue — Width, Thickness, Crown, and Turning Verification

Verify route width, aggregate thickness, crown or cross slope, and turning radius at intervals no greater than the spacing of the grade stakes established for the route. Confirm these values against the approved access plan at the start, middle, and end of the route, and at every turn or drainage crossing. An unverified dimension discovered after the controlled vehicle pass may require removing and reconstructing completed work.

Code Alert — Right-of-Way, Permit, and Sight-Distance Requirements

Confirm right-of-way limits, any required entrance or access permit, sight-distance requirements at the road connection, and any restrictions on construction-traffic hours from the approved plan or applicable regulatory authority before work begins. These requirements vary by jurisdiction and road classification, and do not assume a connection detail used on a prior site applies here.

Manufacturer Cue — Geotextile Fabric and Culvert Installation

Follow the manufacturer's instructions for geotextile fabric overlap, anchoring, and orientation, and for culvert bedding, joint assembly, and cover depth. Installing fabric without the specified overlap or a culvert without the specified bedding can allow subgrade migration or crossing failure under vehicle loading.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work Before Opening the Route to Traffic

Do not open the access route to delivery or construction traffic until the controlled vehicle pass is complete, every identified deficiency is corrected, and the route has been reinspected. Stop work immediately if rutting, pumping, edge failure, loose stone, drainage blockage, or an unsafe sight condition is discovered during the vehicle pass or afterward, and obtain direction before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Equipment, Delivery Traffic, and Sight Distance

Maintain safe separation between workers and grading equipment operating on the route, keep the road connection clear of obstructions that reduce sight distance, and maintain flagging or traffic control as required whenever the route intersects a public road or active site traffic.

E. Preparation — Plan Review and Permission Verification

  1. Review the access plan, vehicle types, route width, turning requirements, grades, drainage crossings, and road connection. Confirm each value against the approved plan before work begins.
  2. Verify permission, right-of-way limits, utility conflicts, and sight-distance requirements. Confirm utility-locate clearance and resolve conflicts before marking the route.

F. Route Marking, Clearing, and Stripping

  1. Mark access centerline, edges, staging areas, and no-entry zones. Install stakes, paint, or flags at intervals sufficient to guide clearing and construction.
  2. Clear only the approved route and preserve protected vegetation and drainage. Confirm protected areas remain undisturbed throughout clearing.
  3. Strip unsuitable organic material where required. Remove organic material to the depth specified for the route subgrade.

G. Subgrade and Fabric

  1. Shape and compact the subgrade. Verify compaction and bearing condition before proceeding to fabric or aggregate placement.
  2. Install geotextile or separation fabric where specified. Install with the manufacturer's required overlap and anchoring.
  3. Place the first aggregate lift using the approved stone size. Confirm stone size against the approved specification before placement.

H. Aggregate Placement and Compaction

  1. Spread aggregate uniformly and maintain required crown or cross slope. Check crown or cross slope across the route width at regular intervals.
  2. Compact the lift with suitable equipment. Verify compaction before placing the next lift.
  3. Place and compact additional aggregate to required thickness. Repeat placement and compaction until the specified total thickness is achieved.

I. Drainage Crossings and Entrance Stabilization

  1. Install culvert, ditch crossing, or edge drainage where required. Confirm each crossing maintains drainage continuity per the approved detail.
  2. Stabilize the entrance connection to reduce mud tracking. Construct the entrance per the approved detail before the route is opened to traffic.

J. Verification, Inspection, and Maintenance

  1. Verify route width, clearance, slope, turning radius, and bearing condition. Measure and record each value at intervals along the full route.
  2. Conduct a controlled vehicle pass using an approved representative vehicle. Observe the route surface during and after the pass.
  3. Inspect for rutting, pumping, edge failure, loose stone, drainage blockage, and unsafe sight conditions. Document every deficiency identified.
  4. Correct failed areas before delivery traffic proceeds. Resolve every deficiency and reverify the corrected area.
  5. Establish inspection, grading, stone-replacement, dust-control, snow, and mud-cleanup procedures. Record the maintenance schedule and responsible party.
  6. Remove or restore the route when its temporary purpose ends. Complete removal or restoration only upon an authorized decision that the route's temporary purpose has ended.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the completed temporary access route using the same checks regardless of route length or number of crossings.

  • Dimension check: confirm route width, turning radius, and grades match the approved plan at every station.
  • Bearing check: confirm subgrade compaction and aggregate thickness were verified before the route was opened to traffic.
  • Drainage check: confirm culverts, ditch crossings, and edge drainage maintain continuity without blockage.
  • Entrance check: confirm the stabilized entrance reduces mud tracking onto the connecting road.
  • Vehicle-pass check: confirm the controlled vehicle pass was completed and every deficiency was corrected before delivery traffic proceeded.
  • Documentation check: confirm the inspection and maintenance record is complete and current, with photographs of the completed route and vehicle pass.

Complete the Temporary Construction Access Record Sheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before the route is opened to delivery traffic.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Route Ruts or Pumps During the Controlled Vehicle Pass

Probable cause: subgrade compaction was not verified before aggregate placement, or aggregate thickness is insufficient for the approved vehicle loading.
Confirmation: inspect the affected section, measure aggregate thickness, and compare against the approved specification.
Correction: remove affected aggregate, correct subgrade compaction or drainage, and replace aggregate to the required thickness.
Reinspection: repeat the controlled vehicle pass over the corrected section before opening it to traffic.

Route Edge Fails or Shoulders Erode Under Traffic

Probable cause: the route width does not match the approved plan, or the edge was not compacted to the same standard as the route center.
Confirmation: measure route width at the affected station and inspect edge compaction against the approved specification.
Correction: widen or reconstruct the affected edge and compact to the required standard.
Reinspection: reverify width and edge condition at the corrected section.

Drainage Crossing Blocks or Redirects Flow

Probable cause: the culvert or ditch crossing was installed without verifying invert elevation or continuity with the existing drainage path.
Confirmation: inspect the crossing for blockage, reverse slope, or misalignment with the drainage path it serves.
Correction: clear, realign, or reinstall the crossing per the approved detail.
Reinspection: observe flow continuity through the corrected crossing during a controlled water test or the next rainfall.

Mud or Sediment Is Tracked Onto the Connecting Road

Probable cause: the entrance stabilization does not extend far enough, or the stone size at the entrance is insufficient for the approved detail.
Confirmation: inspect the entrance length, stone size, and condition against the approved detail.
Correction: extend or reconstruct the stabilized entrance per the approved detail, and clean the connecting road without washing sediment into drainage systems.
Reinspection: observe track-out conditions during the next several delivery-vehicle passes.

Turning Radius or Sight Distance Is Inadequate at the Road Connection

Probable cause: the route connection was constructed without verifying turning geometry or sight-distance requirements against the approved plan and applicable regulatory authority.
Confirmation: measure the turning radius and sight distance at the connection and compare against the approved requirement.
Correction: reconstruct the connection geometry, or escalate to the responsible design professional or regulatory authority if the required geometry cannot be achieved.
Reinspection: reverify turning radius and sight distance before the route is opened to traffic.

Route Is Opened to Traffic Before the Controlled Vehicle Pass Is Complete

Probable cause: delivery traffic began using the route before verification and correction steps were completed.
Confirmation: inspect the route for damage consistent with unverified use, and compare its current condition against the acceptance standards.
Correction: stop traffic on the route, correct every identified deficiency, and complete the controlled vehicle pass before reopening the route.
Reinspection: reverify the full route against the acceptance standards before authorizing continued traffic.

Cleanup

This project ends with a verified, inspected temporary access route ready for construction traffic, not a permanent driveway or a completed final surface.

  • Confirm route markings, staging-area limits, and no-entry zones remain visible where still needed
  • Confirm the stabilized entrance remains free of blockage and functioning as constructed
  • Remove surplus aggregate, fabric scraps, and debris from the work area and staging locations
  • File the completed temporary construction access record, vehicle-pass results, and photographs with the Project 01 through 14 records
