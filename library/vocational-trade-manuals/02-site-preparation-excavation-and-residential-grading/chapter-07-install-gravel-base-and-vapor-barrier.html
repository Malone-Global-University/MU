Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install a level, compacted aggregate base and a continuous, sealed vapor barrier over the subgrade prepared in Project 06, using approved aggregate, controlled lift placement, compaction equipment matched to the aggregate and work area, and a vapor barrier installed, lapped, sealed, and penetrated according to the project detail. The finished work product is a compacted aggregate base at the specified thickness and elevation, and a continuous vapor barrier with sealed seams and penetrations, free of tears, punctures, and bridging, that is fully documented and photographed before concealment.

You will verify the Project 06 subgrade has passed inspection; review the required aggregate type, compacted thickness, finished elevation, vapor-barrier rating, seam overlap, and penetration details; spread and compact aggregate in uniform lifts; fine-grade the surface so it will not puncture the barrier; place, lap, and seal the vapor barrier; terminate it at edges and penetrations; repair any damage; and document the completed assembly before it is concealed by reinforcement, forms, or concrete.

This is the seventh project in the site preparation sequence. It begins with the inspected, protected subgrade produced in Project 06, and it does not authorize disturbing that subgrade's elevation or the Project 03 layout controls preserved through it. The compacted base and sealed vapor barrier produced here become the surface on which reinforcement, forms, and concrete are placed in later foundation and slab work, and the elevation reference used in Project 11.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • SP-037 — Spread approved aggregate in uniform lifts within the specified compacted thickness over the Project 06 subgrade.
  • SP-038 — Compact the aggregate base using equipment appropriate to the aggregate and work area, verifying elevation, thickness, and plane at regular intervals.
  • SP-039 — Maintain required aggregate thickness and correct high, low, loose, or contaminated areas before the vapor barrier is placed.
  • SP-040 — Install the vapor barrier in the manufacturer-controlled orientation, lapping and sealing seams using the approved method.
  • SP-041 — Seal pipe, conduit, and other penetrations using compatible accessories, and repair tears, punctures, and damaged seams using the approved patch method.
  • SP-042 — Inspect and document the completed aggregate base and vapor-barrier assembly for continuity, seam adhesion, and penetration seals before concealment.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 06 produced a compacted, verified subgrade at the required elevation, free of organic material, debris, and standing water, with a completed compaction record. That subgrade, and the Project 03 benchmark it preserves, control this project. Aggregate is placed to bring the surface to the finished base elevation required by the plan; where the Project 06 subgrade already sits at the required base elevation for a portion of the work, the aggregate steps in this project may be minimal in that area, but the vapor-barrier installation and inspection steps still apply in full.

The compacted base and sealed vapor barrier produced here become the working surface for the slab building pad in Project 11 and the surface concealed by reinforcement, forms, and concrete in the foundation work that follows. A puncture, unsealed seam, or thickness deficiency missed here is not caught again until the pre-inspection review in Project 16, by which time it is concealed under concrete.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • verifying the Project 06 subgrade has passed inspection and remains protected before aggregate is staged
  • reviewing the required aggregate type, compacted thickness, finished elevation, vapor-barrier rating, seam overlap, and penetration details before work begins
  • inspecting the work area for mud, debris, sharp objects, standing water, and damaged grade controls
  • establishing final base elevation references and staging aggregate without overloading or rutting the subgrade
  • spreading and compacting aggregate in uniform lifts within the specified thickness, checking elevation, thickness, plane, and edge conditions at regular intervals
  • correcting high, low, loose, or contaminated areas and completing required compaction verification
  • fine-grading the surface so it will not puncture or bridge the vapor barrier
  • placing the vapor barrier in the manufacturer-controlled orientation, lapping and sealing seams, and extending and terminating the barrier at edges according to the project detail
  • sealing penetrations using compatible accessories and repairing tears, punctures, and damaged seams using the approved patch method
  • protecting the barrier with controlled access routes and approved supports, and inspecting and documenting continuity, seam adhesion, penetration seals, and surface damage before concealment

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • place aggregate over a subgrade that has not passed the Project 06 inspection, or over any area showing damage, contamination, or standing water since that inspection, without first obtaining direction
  • compact an aggregate lift thicker than the specified loose thickness, or skip the required compaction verification, to save time
  • place the vapor barrier in an orientation, lap width, or seam method other than the one specified by the manufacturer or the project detail
  • leave a known tear, puncture, unsealed seam, or unsealed penetration in the barrier before it is concealed by reinforcement, forms, or concrete
  • disturb, relocate, or re-square any Project 03 control point or Project 06 benchmark while placing aggregate or installing the vapor barrier
  • proceed past a stop-work condition, such as a failed compaction verification or an unresolved barrier defect, without the direction of the responsible party

Any condition requiring a revised aggregate specification, a different vapor-barrier product, an engineer's determination, or a decision outside the approved base and barrier plan must be escalated before work proceeds in that area.

Trade Note

The aggregate type, compacted thickness, finished elevation, vapor-barrier rating, seam overlap, and penetration details referenced throughout this project are supplied by the project, engineer, or manufacturer instructions and are not generated by this chapter. Where this chapter refers to "the project specification" or "the manufacturer's instructions," it means those supplied documents; do not select an aggregate gradation, barrier product, or seam method from general trade familiarity.

Setup Standard

Do not begin aggregate placement until the Project 06 subgrade has been confirmed as passed and protected (Step 1), the required aggregate type, thickness, elevation, vapor-barrier rating, seam overlap, and penetration details have been reviewed (Step 2), and the work area has been inspected for mud, debris, sharp objects, standing water, and damaged grade controls (Step 3). No aggregate may be staged before Step 3 is complete.

Shop Tip

Mark the required compacted aggregate thickness on a grade stake driven at the edge of the work area before spreading the first lift. Checking spread aggregate against a fixed mark is faster and more consistent than measuring thickness with a tape at multiple points, and it reduces the risk of the barrier being placed over a high spot that later punctures under load.

Working From the Verified Subgrade

Every aggregate and barrier action in this chapter is controlled by the benchmark and dimensions verified in Project 03 and re-confirmed through Project 06. Recheck the benchmark at Step 4 of this project before establishing final base elevation references. If a field condition suggests the required base elevation should be adjusted, stop and obtain direction before placing aggregate; do not raise or lower the base to compensate for a subgrade discrepancy discovered during this project.

Measurement Cue — Aggregate Lift Thickness

Spread each aggregate lift within the specified thickness before compaction. Where no specific thickness is stated, treat this as an unresolved condition and obtain direction before proceeding, since an aggregate base placed too thick may not compact to a uniform density throughout its full depth regardless of the equipment used.

Manufacturer Cue — Vapor-Barrier Orientation and Seam Overlap

Install the vapor barrier in the orientation specified by the manufacturer, with seams lapped to the minimum width stated in the manufacturer's instructions or the project detail. A seam lapped less than the specified width, or sealed with an incompatible tape or adhesive, does not provide the continuous protection the barrier is installed to achieve.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work Before Concealing a Known Barrier Defect

Do not allow reinforcement, forms, or concrete placement to proceed over a vapor barrier with a known unsealed seam, unsealed penetration, tear, or puncture. Stop work and repair the defect using the approved patch method before the assembly is concealed.

Field Safety Cue — Operating Compaction Equipment Near Barrier Materials and Edges

Keep staged vapor-barrier rolls, seam tape, and penetration accessories clear of compaction-equipment travel paths. Maintain clearance between the operator and confined work-area edges while compacting aggregate.

Code Alert — Vapor-Barrier Rating and Aggregate Requirements

Confirm the required vapor-barrier permeance rating, aggregate gradation, and compacted thickness from the project specification or applicable regulatory authority before work begins. These requirements vary by jurisdiction, soil condition, and intended floor system; do not assume a rating or thickness used on a prior site applies here.

E. Preparation — Subgrade Verification and Specification Review

  1. Verify that the subgrade has passed inspection and is protected from damage. Confirm the Project 06 compaction record shows a passed inspection and inspect the surface for damage since that inspection.
  2. Review required aggregate type, compacted thickness, finished elevation, vapor-barrier rating, seam overlap, and penetration details. Confirm these values against the current project specification and manufacturer instructions before aggregate is staged.
  3. Inspect the work area for mud, debris, sharp objects, standing water, and damaged grade controls. Walk the full work area and identify every condition inconsistent with a sound aggregate base or barrier installation.

F. Aggregate Placement and Compaction

  1. Establish final base elevation references. Mark aggregate-thickness and finished-elevation references from the Project 06 benchmark before the first lift is spread.
  2. Deliver and stage aggregate without overloading or rutting the subgrade. Place delivered aggregate outside the immediate spreading area to avoid concentrated loading on the subgrade.
  3. Spread aggregate in uniform lifts within the specified thickness. Distribute aggregate evenly across the work area to the marked thickness reference.
  4. Compact each lift with equipment appropriate to the aggregate and area. Select and operate compaction equipment matched to the aggregate gradation and the confined dimensions of the work area.
  5. Check elevation, thickness, plane, and edge conditions at regular intervals. Measure the compacted surface against the benchmark and inspect edges and corners for consistent thickness and plane.
  6. Correct high, low, loose, or contaminated areas. Add, remove, or recompact aggregate at any location that fails the elevation, thickness, or plane check.
  7. Complete required compaction verification. Perform or arrange the density, proof-roll, or other verification method identified by the project specification.

G. Vapor-Barrier Placement and Sealing

  1. Fine-grade the surface so it will not puncture or bridge the vapor barrier. Remove or bed any protruding stone, sharp edge, or depression identified across the compacted base.
  2. Place the vapor barrier in the manufacturer-controlled orientation. Roll out the barrier across the prepared base in the orientation specified by the manufacturer's instructions.
  3. Lap and seal seams using the approved method. Overlap adjacent sheets to the specified minimum width and seal using the manufacturer-specified tape or sealing method.
  4. Extend and terminate the barrier at edges according to the project detail. Carry the barrier to the required edge extension and terminate it using the method specified in the project detail.
  5. Seal pipe, conduit, and other penetrations using compatible accessories. Fit and seal a boot or collar compatible with the barrier product around every penetration present in the work area.

H. Repair, Protection, and Inspection

  1. Repair tears, punctures, and damaged seams using the approved patch method. Patch every identified defect using material and a method compatible with the installed barrier, then re-verify the repaired area.
  2. Protect the barrier with controlled access routes and approved supports. Establish walk paths and material-staging supports that avoid unnecessary traffic across the installed barrier.
  3. Inspect continuity, seam adhesion, penetration seals, and surface damage. Walk the completed assembly and confirm every seam, penetration, and repaired area meets the Acceptance Standards table.
  4. Photograph and document the completed assembly before concealment. Complete the installation record with every required entry and photograph the assembly from fixed reference points before reinforcement, forms, or concrete work begins.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the completed aggregate base and vapor barrier using the same checks regardless of pad size or barrier product.

  • Aggregate check: confirm placed aggregate matches the project specification and is compacted to the specified thickness, elevation, and plane.
  • Surface check: confirm the compacted base is free of protruding stone, loose material, and depressions that could puncture or bridge the barrier.
  • Orientation and lap check: confirm the vapor barrier is installed in the manufacturer-specified orientation with seams lapped to the required minimum width.
  • Seal check: confirm every seam, edge termination, and penetration is sealed per the manufacturer's instructions and the project detail.
  • Damage check: confirm the barrier is free of unrepaired tears, punctures, or damaged seams.
  • Documentation check: confirm the installation record, compaction results, and photographs are complete.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Compacted Aggregate Fails Elevation or Plane Check

Probable cause: aggregate was spread unevenly, the lift was placed thicker than the specified thickness in some areas, or the elevation reference was not checked frequently enough during placement.
Confirmation: re-measure elevation and thickness at multiple points across the affected area against the Project 06 benchmark.
Correction: add, remove, or recompact aggregate in the affected area until it matches the specified thickness and elevation.
Reinspection: re-check elevation and plane across the entire affected area before proceeding to vapor-barrier installation.

Vapor Barrier Punctured During or After Placement

Probable cause: the aggregate surface was not fine-graded to remove protruding stone before barrier placement, or traffic crossed the barrier outside the protected access route.
Confirmation: inspect the underlying aggregate at the puncture location and identify the protruding point or traffic path responsible.
Correction: remove or bed the protruding material, then patch the barrier using the approved patch method.
Reinspection: re-verify the patched area for continuity and re-establish the protected access route before further work.

Seam Fails to Seal or Separates After Installation

Probable cause: the lap width was less than the manufacturer's specified minimum, the sealing material was incompatible with the barrier product, or the seam surface was contaminated with dust or moisture during sealing.
Confirmation: measure the lap width at the affected seam and compare the sealing material against the manufacturer's compatibility requirement.
Correction: clean the seam surface, re-lap to the specified minimum width, and reseal using a compatible method.
Reinspection: re-verify seam adhesion along the full length of the corrected seam.

Penetration Seal Shows a Gap or Incomplete Fit

Probable cause: the boot or collar selected was not sized or rated for the penetrating item, or the accessory was not fully seated before sealing.
Confirmation: inspect the penetration against the manufacturer's sizing and compatibility requirements.
Correction: replace or reseat the accessory and reseal using the approved method.
Reinspection: re-verify the penetration seal shows no visible gap around the full circumference of the penetrating item.

Aggregate Base Placed Over an Unresolved Subgrade Condition

Probable cause: the Project 06 subgrade inspection status was not confirmed before aggregate delivery, or the subgrade was damaged after inspection but before this project began.
Confirmation: compare the affected area against the Project 06 compaction record to identify what was inspected and when.
Correction: stop work, remove the placed aggregate in the affected area if directed, and obtain re-inspection of the subgrade before resuming.
Reinspection: re-verify the subgrade meets the Project 06 acceptance standard before aggregate placement resumes.

Barrier Installed in the Wrong Orientation or With Insufficient Edge Extension

Probable cause: the manufacturer's orientation requirement or the project's edge-extension detail was not reviewed before installation began.
Confirmation: compare the installed barrier against the manufacturer's instructions and the project detail for orientation and edge extension.
Correction: reposition or extend the barrier to match the required orientation and edge extension, resealing any seam disturbed during correction.
Reinspection: re-verify orientation and edge extension across the full work area before documentation.

Cleanup

This project ends with a compacted aggregate base and sealed vapor barrier left in place and protected, not a surface that has already been covered by reinforcement, forms, or concrete.

  • Confirm the completed assembly remains clean, undamaged, and free of standing water

Field Terms

Aggregate Base
The compacted layer of approved stone placed over the subgrade to the required thickness and elevation.
Vapor Barrier
The continuous sheet material installed over the aggregate base to restrict moisture movement into the slab or foundation assembly.
Lap
The overlap between adjacent vapor-barrier sheets at a seam, measured to the width required by the manufacturer or project detail.
Penetration
Any pipe, conduit, or other item passing through the plane of the vapor barrier, requiring a sealed boot or collar.
Bridging
A condition where the vapor barrier spans a void or depression without full contact with the aggregate surface, increasing puncture risk.
Permeance Rating
The manufacturer-stated resistance of the vapor barrier to moisture-vapor transmission.
Boot or Collar
A manufactured accessory used to seal the vapor barrier around a pipe, conduit, or other penetration.
Proof-Roll
A verification method using loaded equipment passes to identify soft or unstable areas in a compacted surface.
Stop-Work Condition
A field condition requiring work to halt until a competent person, engineer, or responsible party provides direction.
