Rough Grade a Residential Building Site

Shape the building site to the required rough elevations and drainage pattern by cutting high areas, filling low areas with approved compacted material, and directing runoff away from excavations and active work, producing a stable rough-graded site that supports construction and channels water away from the work.

Job Overview

This project qualifies you to rough grade a residential building site consisting of re-established grade stakes and benchmarks, controlled cutting of high areas, controlled filling and compaction of low areas, shaped temporary drainage, and a verified rough-grade surface, using the approved grading plan, benchmark, proposed elevations, drainage arrows, building-pad limits, and tolerances. The finished work product is a stable rough-graded site that supports construction traffic and directs water away from excavations, foundations, and active work areas.

You will review the grading plan and re-establish disturbed grade stakes and benchmarks; identify cut areas, fill areas, haul routes, stockpiles, and no-disturbance zones; confirm fill suitability and remove unsuitable material from fill areas; cut high areas and place compacted fill in low areas; shape temporary drainage and rough slopes away from the building area; check elevations and drainage; and correct high spots, low spots, loose fill, rutting, and blocked drainage before updating the grading record.

This is the tenth project in the site preparation sequence. It follows the utility trenches excavated and prepared in Project 9, which must be backfilled and compacted before rough grading proceeds over them. The rough grade established here supports the concrete slab building pad of Project 11 and the foundation backfill and drainage work of Projects 12 through 14, and must remain protected until those projects proceed.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • SP-055 — Review the grading plan, benchmark, proposed elevations, drainage arrows, building-pad limits, and tolerances, and re-establish disturbed grade stakes and protected benchmarks.
  • SP-056 — Identify cut areas, fill areas, haul routes, stockpiles, and no-disturbance zones, and shape temporary drainage so runoff does not enter excavations or neighboring property.
  • SP-057 — Confirm fill material suitability, remove unsuitable material from fill areas, and place approved fill in compactable lifts in low areas.
  • SP-058 — Cut high areas in controlled passes, moisture-condition and compact fill as required, and blend transitions without abrupt humps, depressions, or ponding pockets.
  • SP-059 — Maintain required elevation below future finished surfaces, provide rough slopes away from the building area, and maintain equipment access without rutting prepared subgrades.
  • SP-060 — Check elevations and perform a visual and measured drainage review, correct high spots, low spots, loose fill, rutting, and blocked drainage, and update the grading record with final rough elevations and unresolved conditions.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 9 prepared and protected the utility trenches that must be backfilled and compacted before this project shapes grade over them; a trench that is still open or unprotected is a stop-work condition for this project, not a grading obstacle to work around. Project 6 established the compaction discipline this project extends to broader site areas, and Project 4 established the cleared, stripped limits within which cutting and filling occur.

The rough grade established here remains in place, protected from traffic, rain, and rutting, until Project 11 builds the concrete slab building pad and Projects 12 through 14 backfill and drain the foundation. Do not proceed to Project 11 while a required rough elevation, drainage shape, or compaction condition established in this project remains uncorrected.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the grading plan, benchmark, proposed elevations, drainage arrows, building-pad limits, and tolerances
  • inspecting the site after clearing, excavation, and utility work is complete
  • re-establishing disturbed grade stakes and protected benchmarks
  • identifying cut areas, fill areas, haul routes, stockpiles, and no-disturbance zones
  • confirming fill material suitability before use
  • removing remaining organic, saturated, frozen, or unsuitable material from fill areas
  • cutting high areas in controlled passes
  • placing approved fill in compactable lifts in low areas
  • moisture-conditioning and compacting fill as required
  • shaping temporary drainage so runoff does not enter excavations or neighboring property
  • maintaining required elevation below future finished surfaces
  • providing rough slopes away from the building area
  • blending transitions without abrupt humps, depressions, or ponding pockets
  • maintaining equipment access and preventing rutting of prepared subgrades
  • checking elevations using the approved grade-control method
  • performing a visual and measured drainage review
  • correcting high spots, low spots, loose fill, rutting, and blocked drainage
  • updating the grading record with final rough elevations and unresolved conditions, and protecting the rough grade until subsequent work proceeds

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • rough grade over a utility trench from Project 9 that has not been backfilled, compacted, and cleared for grading
  • place fill material that has not been confirmed suitable against the project specification
  • establish a rough elevation, slope, or drainage pattern that differs from the approved grading plan without first obtaining direction
  • direct shaped drainage onto neighboring property or into an excavation
  • operate grading or compaction equipment over an unprotected trench, unmarked utility, or preserved control point
  • leave the rough grade unprotected from traffic, rain, or rutting once shaping and compaction are complete

Any condition requiring a revised elevation, a different fill source, an engineer's determination, or a decision outside the approved grading plan must be escalated before work proceeds in that area.

Trade Note

The proposed elevations, drainage arrows, building-pad limits, fill material specification, lift thickness, moisture range, compaction requirement, and grading tolerances referenced throughout this project are supplied by the approved grading plan, geotechnical report, or responsible design professional, and are not generated by this chapter. Where this chapter refers to "the grading plan" or "the approved elevation," it means those supplied documents; do not select an elevation, slope, or fill source from general trade familiarity.

Setup Standard

Do not begin cutting or filling any area until the grade stakes, benchmarks, and cut/fill limits for that area have been re-established (Step 3) and verified against the approved grading plan (Step 1). No grading equipment may operate over a utility trench that has not been confirmed backfilled, compacted, and cleared for grading.

Shop Tip

Walk the full grading limits with the approved plan and the drainage arrows in hand before the first cutting pass. Identifying every low point, haul route, and no-disturbance zone at once prevents fill from being placed on a route that equipment will need later.

Common Field Fundamentals

A rough grade only supports the work that follows it if it reaches the correct elevation, drains water away from the building area and active excavations, and is built from material that will remain stable under later construction traffic and loading. A rough grade that traps water against a foundation, buries unsuitable material, or leaves loose or rutted fill compromises every project that depends on it.

Working Around the Protected Site

Every area graded in this chapter depends on the Project 9 utility trenches being confirmed backfilled and compacted before equipment travels over them, and on the Project 4 cleared limits and protected topsoil stockpile locations remaining respected. Recheck both at Step 2 of this project before grading begins. If a field condition suggests the approved grading plan conflicts with an unbackfilled trench or a protected stockpile location, stop and obtain direction before grading around the conflict; do not reroute equipment over an unprotected trench to avoid a delay.

Measurement Cue — Rough Elevation and Slope

Cut and fill to the proposed rough elevation shown on the grading plan, maintaining the required elevation below future finished surfaces and the specified slope away from the building area. Check elevations at the regular interval identified by the plan. Where no specific interval is stated, treat this as an unresolved condition and obtain direction before proceeding, since an unverified interval may allow a low spot or reverse slope to go undetected over a large graded area.

Code Alert — Drainage Direction and Neighboring Property

Confirm required drainage direction, discharge locations, and any permit conditions affecting runoff onto neighboring property or public drainage systems from the approved grading plan or applicable regulatory authority before shaping temporary or rough drainage. These requirements vary by jurisdiction and site condition; do not assume a drainage direction used on a prior site applies here.

Manufacturer Cue — Compaction Equipment Operating Limits

Operate grading and compaction equipment within its manufacturer-rated slope, load, and moisture-condition limits for the fill material being placed. Equipment operated outside its rated limits may fail to achieve the required compaction or may become unstable on a graded slope.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work Before Grading Over an Unprotected Trench

Do not operate grading or compaction equipment over a utility trench that has not been confirmed backfilled, compacted, and cleared for grading. Stop work immediately if unsuitable material, groundwater, or instability is discovered in a cut or fill area, and obtain direction before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Haul Routes, Stockpiles, and Equipment Movement

Keep haul routes, stockpile locations, and equipment travel within the areas identified in Step 4, and maintain clearance from preserved grade stakes, benchmarks, and no-disturbance zones at all times during cutting, filling, and compaction.

E. Preparation — Plan Review and Site Inspection

  1. Review the grading plan, benchmark, proposed elevations, drainage arrows, building-pad limits, and tolerances. Confirm these values against the current approved plan before any cutting or filling begins.
  2. Inspect the site after clearing, excavation, and utility work. Confirm the Project 9 trenches are backfilled, compacted, and cleared for grading, and that Project 4 cleared limits and stockpiles remain undisturbed.
  3. Re-establish disturbed grade stakes and protected benchmarks. Restore every control point needed to verify elevation throughout the graded area.

F. Layout and Material Control

  1. Identify cut areas, fill areas, haul routes, stockpiles, and no-disturbance zones. Mark each on the ground before equipment enters the area.
  2. Confirm fill material suitability before use. Verify the fill source meets the project specification before any material is placed.
  3. Remove remaining organic, saturated, frozen, or unsuitable material from fill areas. Clear each fill area to expose a suitable base before placing approved fill.

G. Cutting, Filling, and Compaction

  1. Cut high areas in controlled passes. Reduce material to the proposed rough elevation without overcutting past the tolerance.
  2. Place approved fill in compactable lifts in low areas. Build up low areas to the proposed rough elevation using the specified lift thickness.
  3. Moisture-condition and compact fill as required. Bring each lift to the specified moisture range before compaction and compact to the required condition.

H. Drainage Shaping and Access Protection

  1. Shape temporary drainage so runoff does not enter excavations or neighboring property. Direct surface water according to the drainage arrows on the approved plan.
  2. Maintain required elevation below future finished surfaces. Verify the rough grade leaves the specified allowance for later finish work.
  3. Provide rough slopes away from the building area. Shape the area immediately around the building pad to direct water outward.
  4. Blend transitions without abrupt humps, depressions, or ponding pockets. Smooth grade breaks between cut, fill, and undisturbed areas.
  5. Maintain equipment access and prevent rutting of prepared subgrades. Confine equipment travel to the approved haul routes.

I. Verification and Documentation

  1. Check elevations using the approved grade-control method. Verify rough elevation at the regular interval identified by the plan.
  2. Perform a visual and measured drainage review. Confirm shaped drainage matches the approved plan and identify any ponding or reverse-slope condition.
  3. Correct high spots, low spots, loose fill, rutting, and blocked drainage. Resolve every deficiency found during the elevation check and drainage review.
  4. Update the grading record with final rough elevations and unresolved conditions. Record the completed rough-grade condition before the site is protected.
  5. Protect the rough grade until subsequent work proceeds. Maintain the graded surface against traffic, rain, freezing, contamination, and rutting.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the completed rough grade using the same checks regardless of site size or cut-and-fill volume.

  • Elevation check: confirm rough elevation matches the approved grading plan within the stated tolerance at every checked location.
  • Fill condition check: confirm fill material is approved, properly moisture-conditioned, and compacted to the required condition.
  • Drainage check: confirm shaped drainage matches the drainage arrows on the approved plan with no ponding pockets or reverse slope.
  • Building-area slope check: confirm rough slope away from the building area is maintained per the approved plan.
  • Access check: confirm equipment access has not rutted prepared subgrades.
  • Documentation check: confirm the rough grading record is complete and current.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Rough Elevation Deviates From the Approved Plan

Probable cause: grade stakes were not re-established accurately, or elevation was not checked at the specified interval during cutting or filling.
Confirmation: measure elevation against the re-established benchmark at closer intervals along the affected area.
Correction: obtain direction before correcting overcut or underfill areas; add approved fill or remove excess material as directed.
Reinspection: re-check elevation along the corrected area before proceeding.

Drainage Directs Water Toward the Building or a Neighboring Property

Probable cause: the shaped slope did not follow the drainage arrows on the approved plan, or a transition created an unintended low point.
Confirmation: observe or measure runoff direction against the approved drainage arrows.
Correction: reshape the affected area to restore the required slope and flow direction; obtain direction before altering a discharge point.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage review after reshaping.

Fill Settles or Shows Loose Material After Placement

Probable cause: fill was placed in lifts exceeding the specified thickness, moisture was outside the permitted range, or unsuitable material was not fully removed before placement.
Confirmation: inspect the fill area for soft spots, and compare lift thickness and moisture records against the specification.
Correction: remove and replace the affected fill in properly conditioned lifts and recompact.
Reinspection: re-check elevation and compaction condition of the corrected area.

Ponding Pocket Forms in a Graded Area

Probable cause: a transition between cut and fill areas was not blended, leaving a low point that does not drain.
Confirmation: observe the area during or after rainfall, or measure elevation across the transition.
Correction: reshape the transition to eliminate the low point and restore continuous flow toward the approved drainage direction.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage review after reshaping.

Equipment Ruts a Prepared Subgrade

Probable cause: equipment traveled outside the approved haul route, or the subgrade was worked while saturated.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area for rutting depth and compare against the approved haul route markings.
Correction: obtain direction before recompacting or replacing rutted material; restrict equipment to the approved haul route going forward.
Reinspection: re-check the repaired area for elevation and compaction condition.

Unsuitable Material Discovered in a Fill Area

Probable cause: organic, saturated, frozen, or contaminated material was not fully identified and removed during Step 6.
Confirmation: inspect the fill area before further placement and compare the exposed material against the project specification.
Correction: remove the unsuitable material to the approved limits before placing further fill; obtain direction if the extent of unsuitable material is unclear.
Reinspection: reverify the exposed base before fill placement resumes.
