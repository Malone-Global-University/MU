Job Overview

This project qualifies you to perform the final inspection, verify drainage, verify access, verify layout controls, document deficiencies, and certify site readiness. The finished work product is a documented site-readiness decision supported by inspection evidence and correction records. This project begins only after the final site cleanup of Project 18 is complete, and it does not include performing the housekeeping, waste removal, or material recovery work of Project 18, does not include correcting a deficiency that requires reopening excavation, drainage, or grading work beyond the corrective scope authorized by the responsible party, and does not include the formal handoff itself beyond conducting it as the final step of this project.

You will assemble approved plans, site records, permits, test reports, inspection reports, photographs, and change documentation; define the required site-readiness condition and inspection boundaries; inspect property protection, boundaries, setbacks, control points, benchmarks, and preserved layout references; inspect clearing limits, stockpiles, disturbed areas, and restoration; verify excavation, subgrade, building pad, utility trenches, and prepared bearing surfaces within scope; inspect temporary and permanent drainage features; verify positive drainage and absence of uncontrolled ponding or erosion; inspect construction access, delivery routes, emergency access, and public-road protection; inspect utility markings, exposed utilities, sleeves, crossings, and protected terminations; inspect erosion and sediment controls and determine which must remain; review compaction, density, material, elevation, and inspection records; compare completed work against approved plans and acceptance criteria; prepare a deficiency list identifying location, condition, correction, responsible party, and reinspection method; correct or assign each deficiency; reinspect completed corrections; photograph final conditions from established reference points; complete the site-readiness certification or conditional-readiness record; identify controls, maintenance, monitoring, or protection required after handoff; and conduct a formal handoff to the foundation or construction team.

This is the nineteenth project in the site preparation sequence. It depends on the records and completed work from every prior project in this book, including the layout of Projects 1 through 3, the earthwork of Projects 4 through 7, the drainage and grading work of Projects 8 through 14, the construction access of Project 15, the foundation-inspection preparation of Project 16, the restoration of Project 17, and the final cleanup of Project 18. The certification produced here becomes the controlling record for the Chapter 20 capstone handoff.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • SP-109 — Assemble approved plans, site records, permits, test reports, inspection reports, photographs, and change documentation, and define the required site-readiness condition and inspection boundaries.
  • SP-110 — Inspect property protection, boundaries, setbacks, control points, benchmarks, preserved layout references, clearing limits, stockpiles, disturbed areas, and restoration.
  • SP-111 — Verify excavation, subgrade, building pad, utility trenches, and prepared bearing surfaces within scope, and inspect temporary and permanent drainage features, verifying positive drainage and absence of uncontrolled ponding or erosion.
  • SP-112 — Inspect construction access, delivery routes, emergency access, public-road protection, utility markings, exposed utilities, sleeves, crossings, protected terminations, and erosion and sediment controls, determining which must remain.
  • SP-113 — Review compaction, density, material, elevation, and inspection records, compare completed work against approved plans and acceptance criteria, prepare a deficiency list identifying location, condition, correction, responsible party, and reinspection method, and correct or assign each deficiency.
  • SP-114 — Reinspect completed corrections, photograph final conditions from established reference points, complete the site-readiness certification or conditional-readiness record, identify controls, maintenance, monitoring, or protection required after handoff, and conduct a formal handoff to the foundation or construction team.

Where This Skill Fits

The final site cleanup completed in Project 18 must already be in place before this inspection begins; inspecting a site still cluttered with waste, unsecured hazardous material, or unresolved housekeeping deficiencies produces an unreliable readiness decision. This project reviews the cumulative result of every prior project in the book rather than performing new earthwork, drainage, or grading work itself.

This project stands between the final cleanup of Project 18 and the formal handoff to the foundation or construction team, which this project itself conducts as its final step. Its certification or conditional-readiness record becomes the controlling document the receiving trade relies on to begin foundation work, and it is also the primary record reviewed during the Chapter 20 capstone integration. A readiness certification issued without full verification of layout, drainage, access, and prior inspection records can allow foundation work to begin on a site that is not actually ready.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • assembling the complete plan, permit, test, inspection, photograph, and change-documentation record
  • defining the required site-readiness condition and inspection boundaries
  • inspecting property protection, boundaries, setbacks, control points, benchmarks, and preserved layout references
  • inspecting clearing limits, stockpiles, disturbed areas, and restoration
  • verifying excavation, subgrade, building pad, utility trenches, and prepared bearing surfaces within scope
  • inspecting temporary and permanent drainage features and verifying positive drainage
  • inspecting construction access, delivery routes, emergency access, and public-road protection
  • inspecting utility markings, exposed utilities, sleeves, crossings, and protected terminations
  • inspecting erosion and sediment controls and determining which must remain
  • reviewing compaction, density, material, elevation, and inspection records against approved plans and acceptance criteria
  • preparing, assigning, correcting, and reinspecting a deficiency list
  • completing the site-readiness certification or conditional-readiness record and conducting the formal handoff

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • certify site readiness without independently verifying at least the items listed in this project's inspection sequence, relying solely on prior project sign-offs
  • correct a deficiency requiring new excavation, drainage redesign, or engineering review without the required professional or supervisor approval
  • remove an erosion or sediment control this inspection identifies as still required
  • issue an unconditional readiness certification while any critical deficiency remains uncorrected
  • conduct the formal handoff before every corrected item has been reinspected
  • substitute a verbal readiness statement for the completed written certification or conditional-readiness record

Any condition discovered during this inspection that exceeds this project's corrective authority must be escalated to the responsible engineer, jurisdiction, or supervisor before the readiness decision is issued.

Setup Standard

Do not begin the inspection sequence (Steps 3 through 12) until the complete record package is assembled and the site-readiness condition and inspection boundaries are defined (Step 2). Shop Tip

Organize the assembled records by project number before starting the walkthrough. Matching each inspection item to its originating project record makes it far faster to confirm compliance and identify genuine discrepancies rather than re-deriving information already documented.

Common Field Fundamentals

A complete site-readiness inspection only serves its purpose if it independently verifies the cumulative result of the prior eighteen projects rather than simply compiling their sign-offs. Certifying readiness on the assumption that prior inspections were correct, without confirming drainage still functions, layout controls remain intact, and access remains safe, can allow a site defect to reach the foundation crew undetected.

Working Through the Inspection Sequence

Each category of this sequence depends on the categories before it. Reviewing records before defining the readiness condition, or certifying readiness before reinspecting corrected deficiencies, produces an unreliable decision. Work through Steps 3 through 15 of this project in the order given, and recheck an earlier category if a later step reveals a discrepancy. If a deficiency exceeds this project's corrective authority, stop and escalate to the responsible engineer, jurisdiction, or supervisor before continuing toward certification.

Measurement Cue — Verifying Compaction, Elevation, and Drainage Records

Compare compaction, density, material, and elevation records against the approved plan and acceptance criteria before relying on them in the readiness decision. Do not accept a record as current without confirming it corresponds to the final, as-built condition of the site.

Code Alert — Jurisdictional Inspection Requirements

Confirm the current jurisdictional inspection checklist and acceptance thresholds before certifying readiness. Requirements vary by jurisdiction and may have changed since earlier projects in this book were completed; do not assume an earlier checklist remains current.

Manufacturer Cue — Verifying Installed Drainage and Erosion-Control Products

Where the inspection covers an installed drainage, geotextile, or erosion-control product, verify it against the manufacturer's specified inspection or verification method referenced in the originating project record.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Certify Over an Uncorrected Critical Deficiency

Do not issue an unconditional site-readiness certification while any critical deficiency involving drainage failure, unstable excavation, blocked emergency access, or an unresolved utility conflict remains uncorrected. Issue a conditional-readiness record identifying the condition and the responsible party instead, or escalate before proceeding.

Field Safety Cue — Walking Excavations, Trenches, and Access Routes

Wear steel-toe boots and a high-visibility vest while inspecting excavations, trenches, drainage features, and active construction access. Maintain safe distance from unprotected excavation edges and active delivery traffic during the walkthrough.

E. Record Assembly and Scope Definition

  1. Assemble approved plans, site records, permits, test reports, inspection reports, photographs, and change documentation. Organize the complete record package from Projects 1 through 18 before beginning the inspection walkthrough.
  2. Define the required site-readiness condition and inspection boundaries. Confirm both against the project plan, permits, and jurisdictional checklist before proceeding.

F. Layout, Boundary, and Restoration Verification

  1. Inspect property protection, boundaries, setbacks, control points, benchmarks, and preserved layout references. Confirm every reference remains intact, legible, and consistent with the Project 1 through 3 records.
  2. Inspect clearing limits, stockpiles, disturbed areas, and restoration. Verify the Project 4 and Project 17 records against current site conditions.

G. Earthwork and Drainage Verification

  1. Verify excavation, subgrade, building pad, utility trenches, and prepared bearing surfaces within scope. Compare current conditions against the Project 5, 6, 9, and 11 records.
  2. Inspect temporary and permanent drainage features. Confirm each feature installed under Projects 8, 13, and 14 is present, intact, and functioning.
  3. Verify positive drainage and absence of uncontrolled ponding or erosion. Walk the full inspection boundary and record every drainage discrepancy found.

H. Access, Utility, and Erosion-Control Verification

  1. Inspect construction access, delivery routes, emergency access, and public-road protection. Confirm the Project 15 access route and any temporary provisions remain safe and functional.
  2. Inspect utility markings, exposed utilities, sleeves, crossings, and protected terminations. Confirm every item remains protected and correctly documented.
  3. Inspect erosion and sediment controls and determine which must remain. Confirm the Project 18 determination of continued-service systems is still accurate at the time of this inspection.

I. Deficiency Management

  1. Review compaction, density, material, elevation, and inspection records. Confirm every referenced record is complete, current, and applicable to the final as-built condition.
  2. Compare completed work against approved plans and acceptance criteria. Identify every discrepancy between recorded and observed conditions.
  3. Prepare a deficiency list identifying location, condition, correction, responsible party, and reinspection method. Record every discrepancy found in Steps 4 through 12.
  4. Correct or assign each deficiency. Complete corrections within this project's authorized scope and assign or escalate every deficiency that exceeds it.

J. Certification and Handoff

  1. Reinspect completed corrections. Verify every corrected deficiency now meets the acceptance criteria before proceeding.
  2. Photograph final conditions from established reference points. Document the verified, corrected condition of the full inspection boundary.
  3. Complete the site-readiness certification or conditional-readiness record. Record the final readiness decision, supporting evidence, and any remaining conditions.
  4. Identify controls, maintenance, monitoring, or protection required after handoff. Document what the receiving trade must maintain or protect going forward.
  5. Conduct a formal handoff to the foundation or construction team. Transfer the completed record package, certification, and post-handoff requirements, and obtain the receiving party's signed acknowledgment.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the completed readiness package using the same checks regardless of site size or project count within scope.

  • Record check: confirm the assembled record package is complete, current, and cross-referenced to the inspection boundaries.
  • Layout check: confirm boundaries, control points, benchmarks, and layout references remain intact and consistent with the approved plan.
  • Earthwork check: confirm excavation, subgrade, building pad, and trench conditions match the approved plans and acceptance criteria.
  • Drainage check: confirm positive drainage with no uncontrolled ponding or erosion.
  • Access and utility check: confirm construction access, emergency access, and utility protections remain functional.
  • Deficiency check: confirm every deficiency is corrected, assigned, or escalated, and every correction is reinspected.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Preserved Control Point, Benchmark, or Layout Reference Is Missing or Disturbed

Probable cause: the reference was disturbed during cleanup, restoration, or later site activity and was not re-established.
Confirmation: compare the current condition against the Project 2 and 3 layout records.
Correction: re-establish the reference from the controlling survey or benchmark record and document the correction.
Reinspection: reverify the reestablished reference against the original record before continuing.

Uncontrolled Ponding or Erosion Is Found Within the Inspection Boundaries

Probable cause: a drainage feature has failed, settled, or was disturbed since the Project 14 final-drainage inspection.
Confirmation: inspect the affected drainage feature and compare its condition against the Project 8, 13, or 14 record.
Correction: correct the failed grade, feature, or obstruction using the method established in the originating project, or escalate if correction exceeds this project's authorized scope.
Reinspection: reverify positive drainage and absence of ponding after correction.

A Compaction, Density, or Elevation Record Does Not Match Current Field Conditions

Probable cause: the record is outdated, does not correspond to the final as-built condition, or the area was disturbed after the record was generated.
Confirmation: compare the record against a current field measurement or verification at the affected location.
Correction: obtain a current test or measurement, or escalate to the responsible party for a new verification.
Reinspection: reverify the corrected record is consistent with current field conditions before certification.

An Erosion or Sediment Control Was Removed Before Its Status Was Confirmed

Probable cause: the control's continued-service status was not reconfirmed at the time of this inspection.
Confirmation: compare the current site condition against the Project 18 determination and the current drainage and erosion-control records.
Correction: reinstall the control per its original installation requirements, or escalate for a documented determination that it is no longer required.
Reinspection: reverify the reinstalled control's function before certification.

A Deficiency Was Corrected but Not Reinspected Before Certification

Probable cause: the corrected item was marked complete without a follow-up verification against the acceptance criteria.
Confirmation: compare the deficiency list against the reinspection record for each item.
Correction: conduct the missing reinspection before proceeding with certification.
Reinspection: confirm every item on the deficiency list has a documented reinspection result before completing the certification record.

The Formal Handoff Was Conducted Before the Certification Record Was Complete

Probable cause: the handoff was scheduled or conducted before every corrected deficiency was reinspected and the certification or conditional-readiness record was signed.
Confirmation: compare the certification record's completion date and signatures against the date the handoff was conducted.
Correction: complete the certification record and reissue the formal handoff documentation to the receiving party.
Reinspection: confirm the receiving party has an accurate, complete, and signed certification or conditional-readiness record before resuming foundation work.
