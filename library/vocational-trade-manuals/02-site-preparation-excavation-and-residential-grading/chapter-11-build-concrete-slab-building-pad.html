Project 11

Build a Concrete Slab Building Pad

Prepare the earthwork and aggregate building pad for a future concrete slab by re-establishing the slab perimeter and benchmark, correcting subgrade deficiencies, placing and compacting fill and aggregate to the required elevation and plane, and protecting embedded work, producing a compacted, fine-graded slab building pad ready for foundation and concrete work. This project does not include reinforcement or concrete placement.

Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare the earthwork and aggregate building pad for a future concrete slab, using the slab dimensions, finished-floor elevation, base thickness, subgrade requirement, drainage, and inspection criteria supplied by the approved plan. The finished work product is a compacted, fine-graded slab building pad ready for foundation and concrete work. This project does not include placing reinforcement, forms, vapor barrier, or concrete; it ends with the pad prepared and inspected for those following operations.

You will review slab dimensions and finished-floor elevation; verify underground utility and plumbing work is complete or coordinated; re-establish the slab perimeter, control lines, and benchmark; inspect the pad for unsuitable soil, trenches, soft spots, organic material, and standing water; correct utility-trench settlement and disturbed areas; cut or fill the pad to the required subgrade elevation; compact fill in controlled lifts; establish and place the aggregate base; fine-grade the base to the required plane and thickness; verify elevations and slopes; protect embedded work; and prepare the pad for the vapor barrier, reinforcement, forms, and inspection that follow in later work.

This is the eleventh project in the site preparation sequence. It follows the rough grade shaped and protected in Project 10, which must remain at its verified elevation and undisturbed until this project begins. The compacted, fine-graded pad established here supports the vapor barrier of Project 7 where included in scope, and the foundation backfill and drainage work of Projects 12 through 14 that follow around the completed foundation.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • SP-061 — Review slab dimensions, finished-floor elevation, base thickness, subgrade requirement, drainage, and inspection criteria, and verify underground utility and plumbing work is complete or coordinated before pad preparation begins.
  • SP-062 — Re-establish the slab perimeter, control lines, and benchmark, and inspect the pad for unsuitable soil, trenches, soft spots, organic material, and standing water.
  • SP-063 — Correct utility-trench settlement and disturbed areas using approved fill and compaction, and cut or fill the pad to the required subgrade elevation.
  • SP-064 — Compact fill in controlled lifts and document tests, establish aggregate-base elevation, and place approved aggregate in uniform lifts.
  • SP-065 — Compact aggregate without disturbing embedded work, fine-grade the base to the required plane and thickness, and verify perimeter and interior elevations and required slopes.
  • SP-066 — Check for loose material, sharp objects, rutting, pumping, and contamination; correct failed elevation, compaction, or surface conditions; protect plumbing, conduit, sleeves, and penetrations; and complete the slab-base pre-inspection.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 10 shaped and protected the rough grade this project refines to slab-pad tolerance; a rough elevation, drainage shape, or compaction condition left uncorrected from Project 10 is a prerequisite deficiency for this project, not a condition to grade around. Project 6 established the compaction discipline this project applies at slab-pad precision, and Project 9 established the utility trenches whose settlement must be checked and corrected before this pad is finished.

The compacted, fine-graded pad established here remains in place, protected from traffic, weather, and disturbance, until the vapor barrier, reinforcement, forms, and concrete placement that follow. Do not proceed to reinforcement or concrete work while a required elevation, compaction condition, or embedded-work protection established in this project remains uncorrected.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing slab dimensions, finished-floor elevation, base thickness, subgrade requirement, drainage, and inspection criteria
  • verifying underground utility and plumbing work is complete or coordinated before pad preparation begins
  • re-establishing the slab perimeter, control lines, and benchmark
  • inspecting the pad for unsuitable soil, trenches, soft spots, organic material, and standing water
  • correcting utility-trench settlement and disturbed areas using approved fill and compaction
  • cutting or filling the pad to the required subgrade elevation
  • compacting fill in controlled lifts and documenting tests
  • establishing aggregate-base elevation and placing approved aggregate in uniform lifts
  • compacting aggregate without disturbing embedded work
  • fine-grading the base to the required plane and thickness
  • verifying perimeter and interior elevations and required slopes
  • checking for loose material, sharp objects, rutting, pumping, and contamination
  • correcting failed elevation, compaction, or surface conditions
  • protecting plumbing, conduit, sleeves, and penetrations
  • preparing access for vapor barrier, reinforcement, forms, and inspection, recording final dimensions, elevations, compaction results, and penetrations, and completing the slab-base pre-inspection

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin pad preparation before underground utility and plumbing work is confirmed complete or coordinated for this stage
  • place fill or aggregate over an unresolved trench settlement, soft spot, or unsuitable material
  • select a subgrade elevation, base thickness, or drainage slope that differs from the approved plan without first obtaining direction
  • compact aggregate in a manner that disturbs, crushes, or displaces embedded plumbing, conduit, sleeves, or penetrations
  • place reinforcement, forms, vapor barrier, or concrete as part of this project
  • release the pad for the next operation without completing the slab-base pre-inspection

Any condition requiring a revised elevation, a different fill or aggregate source, an engineer's determination, or a decision outside the approved plan must be escalated before work proceeds in that area.

Trade Note

The slab dimensions, finished-floor elevation, base thickness, subgrade requirement, drainage slope, aggregate specification, lift thickness, compaction requirement, and pad tolerances referenced throughout this project are supplied by the approved plan, geotechnical report, or responsible design professional, and are not generated by this chapter. Where this chapter refers to "the required elevation" or "the specified thickness," it means those supplied values; do not select an elevation, thickness, or material source from general trade familiarity.

Setup Standard

Do not place fill or aggregate over any area until underground utility and plumbing work for that stage has been confirmed complete or coordinated (Step 2) and the slab perimeter, control lines, and benchmark have been re-established (Step 3). No compaction equipment may operate directly over unprotected plumbing, conduit, sleeves, or penetrations.

Shop Tip

Walk the pad with the plumbing and electrical trades before the first fill pass if their work is not yet complete. Confirming exactly which penetrations remain pending, and where, prevents a coordination gap from becoming a disturbed sleeve buried under compacted aggregate.

Common Field Fundamentals

A slab building pad only supports the concrete work that follows it if the subgrade and aggregate base reach the correct elevation beneath the finished-floor elevation, the base is uniformly compacted and free of soft spots or trench settlement, and every embedded penetration remains protected and locatable. A pad that buries an unresolved soft spot, crushes a sleeve, or falls outside tolerance compromises the slab, the vapor barrier, and every finish that depends on them.

Working Around Embedded Work

Every area of this pad depends on underground utility and plumbing work being confirmed complete or coordinated before fill or aggregate is placed over it, and on every remaining penetration being identified and protected before compaction equipment operates nearby. Recheck coordination status at Step 2 of this project before pad preparation begins. If a field condition suggests the approved plan conflicts with the location of an installed penetration, stop and obtain direction before compacting around the conflict; do not compact over an unprotected or unconfirmed penetration to avoid a delay.

Measurement Cue — Subgrade and Aggregate-Base Elevation

Set the subgrade elevation and the aggregate-base elevation below the finished-floor elevation by the specified subgrade allowance and slab thickness shown on the approved plan. Verify perimeter and interior elevations at the regular interval identified by the plan. Where no specific interval is stated, treat this as an unresolved condition and obtain direction before proceeding, since an unverified interval may allow a high or low area to go undetected beneath the finished aggregate surface.

Code Alert — Base Thickness and Drainage Requirements

Confirm the required aggregate-base thickness, subgrade drainage condition, and any vapor-retarder or inspection requirements affecting the slab building pad from the approved plan or applicable regulatory authority before placing fill or aggregate. These requirements vary by jurisdiction and soil condition; do not assume a base thickness used on a prior site applies here.

Manufacturer Cue — Compaction Equipment Near Embedded Work

Operate compaction equipment within its manufacturer-rated limits for the aggregate being placed, and follow the manufacturer's clearance guidance for operating near installed plumbing, conduit, or sleeves. Equipment operated too close to unprotected embedded work may crush or displace it even when operated within its rated limits.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work Before Compacting Over Unresolved Settlement

Do not place fill or aggregate over a utility trench showing settlement, a soft spot, or unsuitable material that has not been corrected. Stop work immediately if unsuitable material, groundwater, or an unconfirmed penetration is discovered during pad preparation, and obtain direction before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Equipment Movement Around Penetrations

Keep compaction equipment travel clear of protected plumbing, conduit, sleeves, and penetrations identified in Step 2, and maintain clearance from the re-established perimeter, control lines, and benchmark at all times during fill, aggregate placement, and compaction.

E. Preparation — Plan Review and Coordination

  1. Review slab dimensions, finished-floor elevation, base thickness, subgrade requirement, drainage, and inspection criteria. Confirm these values against the current approved plan before any fill or aggregate is placed.
  2. Verify underground utility and plumbing work is complete or coordinated. Confirm with the responsible trades which penetrations are installed, which remain pending, and where each is located before pad preparation begins.
  3. Re-establish slab perimeter, control lines, and benchmark. Restore every control point needed to verify elevation throughout the pad area.

F. Subgrade Inspection and Correction

  1. Inspect the pad for unsuitable soil, trenches, soft spots, organic material, and standing water. Walk the full pad area and mark every deficient location before further work proceeds.
  2. Correct utility-trench settlement and disturbed areas using approved fill and compaction. Remove settled or disturbed material and recompact using approved fill.
  3. Cut or fill the pad to required subgrade elevation. Bring the full pad area to the subgrade elevation established from the finished-floor elevation and specified base thickness.

G. Fill Compaction and Aggregate Base

  1. Compact fill in controlled lifts and document tests. Place and compact each lift to the specified condition and record required test results.
  2. Establish aggregate-base elevation. Mark the required top-of-aggregate elevation from the re-established benchmark.
  3. Place approved aggregate in uniform lifts. Spread aggregate evenly across the pad within the specified lift thickness.

H. Fine Grading, Verification, and Embedded-Work Protection

  1. Compact aggregate without disturbing embedded work. Operate compaction equipment clear of protected plumbing, conduit, sleeves, and penetrations.
  2. Fine-grade the base to the required plane and thickness. Screed or hand-shape the surface to the specified elevation and thickness across the full pad.
  3. Verify perimeter and interior elevations and required slopes. Check the finished base against the approved plan at the regular interval identified.
  4. Check for loose material, sharp objects, rutting, pumping, and contamination. Inspect the full surface before protecting embedded work.
  5. Correct failed elevation, compaction, or surface conditions. Resolve every deficiency found during the elevation check and surface inspection.
  6. Protect plumbing, conduit, sleeves, and penetrations. Confirm every embedded item remains undamaged and locatable.
  7. Prepare access for vapor barrier, reinforcement, forms, and inspection. Clear and stage the pad for the operations that follow.
  8. Record final dimensions, elevations, compaction results, and penetrations. Complete the slab building pad record before the pre-inspection.
  9. Complete the slab-base pre-inspection. Verify every acceptance standard is met and document the result before the pad is released for the next operation.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the completed slab building pad using the same checks regardless of pad size or fill volume.

  • Elevation check: confirm perimeter and interior elevations match the required subgrade and aggregate-base elevations within the stated tolerance.
  • Compaction check: confirm fill and aggregate are approved material, properly compacted, and documented by test.
  • Plane and thickness check: confirm the base is fine-graded to the required plane and specified thickness across the full pad area.
  • Surface condition check: confirm the base is free of loose material, sharp objects, rutting, pumping, and contamination.
  • Embedded-work check: confirm plumbing, conduit, sleeves, and penetrations are undamaged and locatable.
  • Documentation check: confirm the slab-base pre-inspection record is complete and current.

Complete the Slab Building Pad Record Sheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before vapor barrier, reinforcement, form, and concrete work proceeds.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Subgrade or Aggregate-Base Elevation Deviates From the Approved Plan

Probable cause: the perimeter, control lines, or benchmark were not re-established accurately, or elevation was not checked at the specified interval during fill or aggregate placement.
Confirmation: measure elevation against the re-established benchmark at closer intervals across the affected area.
Correction: obtain direction before correcting overcut or underfill areas; add approved material or remove excess as directed.
Reinspection: re-check elevation across the corrected area before proceeding.

Trench Settlement Reappears After Correction

Probable cause: the full depth of settled or disturbed material was not removed before recompaction, or moisture was outside the permitted range.
Confirmation: probe or excavate the corrected area to compare depth and material condition against the specification.
Correction: remove the affected material to the full depth of settlement and recompact in properly conditioned lifts.
Reinspection: re-check elevation and surface condition of the corrected area.

Aggregate Base Shows Loose Material, Rutting, or Pumping

Probable cause: aggregate was placed in lifts exceeding the specified thickness, compaction equipment was not suited to the material, or the subgrade beneath was not adequately compacted.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area for depth of looseness or rutting and compare lift thickness and compaction records against the specification.
Correction: remove and replace the affected aggregate in properly sized lifts and recompact; correct the subgrade beneath if it is the underlying cause.
Reinspection: re-check elevation, plane, and compaction condition of the corrected area.

Embedded Plumbing, Conduit, or Sleeve Is Damaged or Displaced

Probable cause: the penetration was not identified or protected before compaction equipment operated nearby, or equipment traveled closer than the manufacturer's clearance guidance.
Confirmation: inspect the penetration and compare its condition and location against the plumbing or electrical trade's installation record.
Correction: stop work in the affected area and obtain direction from the responsible trade before continuing; do not conceal a damaged penetration with additional aggregate.
Reinspection: confirm the penetration is repaired or replaced and re-protected before pad work resumes in that area.

Base Plane Shows an Abrupt High or Low Area

Probable cause: fine grading was not carried consistently across the full pad, or a screed pass was interrupted without matching the adjoining surface.
Confirmation: check the plane with a straightedge or string line across the affected area.
Correction: re-screed the affected area to restore a continuous plane at the required thickness.
Reinspection: re-check the plane and thickness of the corrected area.

Unsuitable Material Discovered During Subgrade Inspection

Probable cause: organic, saturated, or contaminated material was not fully identified and removed before fill or aggregate placement began.
Confirmation: inspect the pad area before further placement and compare the exposed material against the project specification.
Correction: remove the unsuitable material to the approved limits before placing further fill or aggregate; obtain direction if the extent of unsuitable material is unclear.
Reinspection: reverify the exposed base before placement resumes.
