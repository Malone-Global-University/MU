Job Overview

This project qualifies you to excavate foundation footing trenches on a residential building site to the approved plan dimensions, elevations, soil requirements, and excavation-safety plan, using the cleared and stripped work surface produced in Project 04 and the verified layout established in Project 03. The finished work product is a set of footing excavations that match the approved plan in width, location, and bottom elevation; that expose a clean, undisturbed bearing surface at the correct depth; that are stable and safe under the excavation-safety plan; and that are fully documented and ready for inspection.

You will verify the approved footing dimensions and excavation-safety plan, recheck the Project 03 layout, mark excavation limits and equipment exclusion zones, establish spoil placement, excavate in controlled passes, hand-trim the footing bottoms and corners, check dimensions and elevations at regular intervals, remove loose and disturbed material from the bearing surface, identify and escalate unsuitable soil or groundwater conditions, install required protective systems, maintain drainage, verify steps and crossings against the plan, and prepare the excavation for inspection.

This is the fifth project in the site preparation sequence. It begins with the cleared, stripped, and documented work surface produced in Project 04 and the Project 03 layout preserved on that surface, and it does not authorize disturbing that layout or excavating outside the marked limits. The inspected footing excavations produced here become the controlling surface for the subgrade preparation performed in Project 06.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • SP-025 — Lay out footing trenches from the verified Project 03 layout, marking excavation limits and equipment exclusion zones before excavation begins.
  • SP-026 — Excavate footing trenches safely, using controlled equipment passes, hand-trimming, and required protective systems.
  • SP-027 — Maintain approved footing dimensions, including width, location, steps, pads, and service crossings, throughout excavation.
  • SP-028 — Verify footing depth and bottom elevation at regular intervals against the benchmark established in Project 03.
  • SP-029 — Stabilize the excavation, identifying and escalating soft spots, unsuitable soil, groundwater, or caving conditions.
  • SP-030 — Prepare the completed excavation for inspection, including safe access, exposed bearing surfaces, and a complete dimensional record.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 04 produced a cleared, stripped, protected, and documented work area with every Project 03 control point re-confirmed or re-established. That cleared surface, and the layout it preserves, control this project. Excavation in this project follows the footing lines, offsets, and benchmark established in Project 03 and re-confirmed in Project 04; it does not re-establish or adjust that layout.

The inspected footing excavation produced here becomes the working surface for the subgrade preparation performed in Project 06 and, once foundation work is placed, the surface that backfill in Project 12 and drainage in Project 13 are built against. A dimension, elevation, or bearing condition missed here is not caught again until the pre-inspection review in Project 16.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • verifying approved footing dimensions, elevations, soil information, and the excavation-safety plan before equipment is staged
  • rechecking the Project 03 building layout, footing lines, offsets, and benchmark
  • marking excavation limits and equipment exclusion zones
  • establishing spoil placement areas at a safe distance from trench edges
  • excavating in controlled equipment passes and hand-trimming footing bottoms and corners to the approved dimensions
  • checking trench width, location, bottom elevation, and continuity at regular intervals
  • identifying and escalating soft spots, unsuitable soil, groundwater, caving, or unexpected fill
  • installing required access, barricades, protective systems, or competent-person controls
  • maintaining drainage away from the excavation
  • preparing the excavation for inspection and protecting it afterward

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • excavate outside the marked excavation limits or below the approved bottom elevation without direction, regardless of equipment access or convenience
  • continue mechanical excavation below the point where the plan or the excavation-safety requirement calls for hand-trimming above final grade
  • enter or direct workers into an excavation that lacks the protective system, access, or competent-person control required for its depth and soil condition
  • correct overexcavation, unsuitable bearing conditions, groundwater, or caving without first stopping work and obtaining required direction
  • disturb, relocate, or re-square any Project 03 control point while establishing spoil areas, equipment routes, or excavation limits
  • proceed past a stop-work condition identified in this project without the direction of the responsible party

Any condition requiring a soil report, a competent-person determination, a protective-system design, or a decision outside the approved excavation plan must be escalated before work proceeds in that area.

Trade Note

The excavation-safety plan referenced throughout this project, including protective-system selection, competent-person responsibilities, and access requirements, is supplied by the project, employer, or a qualified/competent person and is not generated by this chapter. Where this chapter refers to "the excavation-safety plan," it means that supplied document; do not select a protective system or safe-depth threshold from general trade familiarity.

Setup Standard

Do not begin excavation until the approved footing dimensions, elevations, soil information, and excavation-safety plan have been verified (Step 1), the Project 03 layout has been rechecked (Step 3), excavation limits and equipment exclusion zones have been marked (Step 4), and spoil placement areas have been established at a safe distance from trench edges (Step 5). No mechanical excavation may begin before Step 5 is complete.

Shop Tip

Set a story pole or grade rod at the benchmark before excavation begins and mark the required footing-bottom reading directly on the rod. Reading a fixed mark on a story pole against the trench bottom during excavation is faster and less error-prone than recalculating an elevation difference at every check point.

Working From the Verified Layout

Every excavation action in this chapter is controlled by the footing lines, offsets, and benchmark verified in Project 03 and re-confirmed in Project 04. Recheck these references at Step 3 of this project before excavating. If a field condition suggests the footing lines or benchmark should be adjusted, stop and obtain direction before excavating; do not shift a footing line to avoid an obstruction discovered during excavation.

Measurement Cue — Stop Mechanical Excavation Above Final Grade

Stop mechanical excavation at the distance above final bottom elevation specified in the approved plan or excavation-safety plan, and complete the remaining depth by hand-trimming. Where no specific distance is stated, treat this as an unresolved condition and obtain direction before proceeding, since mechanical equipment operating to final grade risks disturbing or over-excavating the bearing surface.

Measurement Cue — Check Bottom Elevation at Regular Intervals

Check trench bottom elevation against the Project 03 benchmark at regular intervals along the footing length, at every corner, step, and pad, and at every point where the trench changes direction. Footing bottom elevation commonly varies along a residential foundation line; a single check point at one end of the trench will not detect a low or high area elsewhere along its length.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work Before Entering an Unprotected Excavation

Do not enter, and do not direct any worker to enter, an excavation that lacks the access, protective system, or competent-person evaluation required by the excavation-safety plan for its depth and soil condition. Stop work and obtain the competent person's evaluation before proceeding whenever the excavation depth, soil condition, or water condition changes from what was anticipated.

Field Safety Cue — Spoil and Equipment Placement at the Trench Edge

Keep spoil piles, equipment, and materials at the distance from the trench edge established in Step 5 and the excavation-safety plan. Material or equipment surcharging the edge of an excavation increases the risk of wall collapse regardless of the protective system in use.

Code Alert — Excavation Depth and Protective-System Requirements

Confirm protective-system requirements, maximum unsupported depths, and inspection frequency from the excavation-safety plan, employer policy, or the applicable regulatory authority before excavation begins. These requirements vary by soil classification, depth, and jurisdiction; do not assume a protective-system threshold used on a prior site applies here.

E. Preparation — Plan Verification and Layout Recheck

  1. Verify approved footing dimensions, elevations, soil information, and excavation safety plan. Confirm the footing plan, benchmark elevation, soil report where available, and excavation-safety plan against the current site condition before staging equipment.
  2. Confirm utility-locate status and resolve all conflicts before excavation. Confirm current utility-locate markings and resolve any conflict identified before excavating.
  3. Recheck the building layout, footing lines, offsets, and benchmark. Compare every Project 03 control point re-confirmed in Project 04 against the layout record sheet before transferring footing lines to the ground.

F. Limit Marking and Spoil Placement

  1. Mark excavation limits and equipment exclusion zones. Flag or paint the approved footing width and length, and mark the exclusion zone around the excavation before equipment enters.
  2. Establish spoil placement areas far enough from trench edges to avoid surcharge and collapse hazards. Mark spoil locations at the distance from the trench edge required by the excavation-safety plan, and confirm the location does not block drainage or access routes established in Project 01.
  3. Inspect excavation equipment, attachments, alarms, access routes, and operator qualifications. Confirm equipment condition, backup alarms, attachment security, and operator qualification before excavation begins.

G. Excavation and Hand-Trimming

  1. Excavate in controlled passes while preserving required offsets and control points. Remove soil in successive passes toward the approved dimensions, checking progress against the marked limits and preserved control points as work proceeds.
  2. Stop mechanical excavation above final grade when required to avoid overexcavation. Stop the equipment at the distance above final bottom elevation specified in the approved plan or excavation-safety plan.
  3. Hand-trim footing bottoms and corners to the approved dimensions. Complete the remaining depth and all corners by hand, working to the exact width, location, and elevation shown on the approved plan.

H. Dimensional Verification and Condition Checks

  1. Check trench width, location, bottom elevation, and continuity at regular intervals. Measure and record width, location, and elevation at each corner, step, pad, and regular interval along the footing length.
  2. Remove loose soil, organic material, water, and disturbed material from the bearing surface. Clean the trench bottom immediately before each elevation check so the bearing surface reflects true undisturbed material.
  3. Identify soft spots, unsuitable soil, groundwater, caving, or unexpected fill. Inspect the bearing surface and trench walls for any condition inconsistent with the anticipated soil report or plan assumption.
  4. Stop work and obtain required direction before correcting overexcavation or unsuitable bearing conditions. Do not backfill, add material, or otherwise correct an unsuitable bearing condition without direction from the competent person, engineer, or responsible party identified in the excavation-safety plan.

I. Protection, Drainage, and Inspection Preparation

  1. Install required access, barricades, protective systems, or competent-person controls. Install the protective system, access, and barricades identified by the competent person for the excavation's depth and soil condition.
  2. Maintain drainage and prevent rainwater or runoff from entering the excavation. Shape surrounding grade or install temporary diversion so runoff does not enter the open trench.
  3. Verify footing steps, pads, intersections, and service crossings against the plan. Confirm every step, pad, intersection, and utility crossing matches the approved plan location, dimension, and elevation.
  4. Record final dimensions and benchmark readings. Complete the excavation record with final width, location, elevation, and benchmark readings at every check point.
  5. Prepare safe inspector access and expose all required areas. Provide a safe route for the inspector and confirm every area requiring inspection is visible and accessible.
  6. Correct deficiencies identified during pre-inspection. Walk the completed excavation against the Acceptance Standards table and correct any failed item before scheduling the official inspection.
  7. Protect the excavation until reinforcement, forms, or concrete work begins. Maintain barricades, drainage, and protective systems, and keep equipment and spoil clear of the excavation until the next operation begins.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the footing excavation using the same checks regardless of footing length or soil condition.

  • Dimension check: confirm trench width, location, and length match the approved plan and the Project 03 layout.
  • Elevation check: confirm bottom elevation matches the approved plan at every corner, step, pad, and regular interval.
  • Bearing-surface check: confirm the trench bottom is clean, undisturbed, and free of water or loose material.
  • Stability check: confirm soft spots, unsuitable soil, groundwater, and caving conditions have been identified, escalated, and resolved.
  • Protection check: confirm required protective systems, barricades, and drainage measures are installed and functional.
  • Documentation check: confirm the excavation record, benchmark readings, and photographs are complete.

Complete the Excavation Record Sheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 06.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Trench Bottom Elevation Fails the Benchmark Check at One or More Locations

Probable cause: mechanical excavation was not stopped far enough above final grade, or bottom elevation was checked at too few points along the footing length.
Confirmation: re-check elevation at additional intervals along the affected section using the Project 03 benchmark.
Correction: hand-trim high areas to the approved elevation; stop work and obtain direction before correcting any area found below the approved elevation.
Reinspection: re-check elevation across the entire affected section before continuing.

Overexcavation Below the Approved Bottom Elevation

Probable cause: mechanical excavation continued past the point where hand-trimming should have begun, or the operator was not given a clear stop-elevation reference.
Confirmation: measure the depth of overexcavation against the approved elevation at the affected location.
Correction: stop work and obtain required direction before adding any material to correct the overexcavated area; do not backfill loose material into a footing excavation without that direction.
Reinspection: re-verify the corrected elevation and bearing condition before proceeding.

Soft Spots, Groundwater, or Unsuitable Soil Found in the Bearing Surface

Probable cause: subsurface conditions differed from the anticipated soil report, or water intrusion occurred after a rain event.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area and compare its condition against the soil information referenced in Step 1.
Correction: stop work in the affected area and obtain direction from the competent person, engineer, or responsible party before proceeding; do not excavate further or place material based on an assumption about the correct depth or treatment.
Reinspection: re-inspect the corrected area against the direction provided before continuing excavation.

Trench Width or Location Does Not Match the Approved Plan

Probable cause: excavation limits were marked imprecisely relative to the Project 03 footing lines, or equipment worked beyond the marked limit.
Confirmation: measure the affected section against the approved plan and the Project 03 layout record sheet.
Correction: correct the width or location within the approved tolerance; escalate any condition that cannot be corrected without affecting an adjacent control point or protective system.
Reinspection: re-measure the corrected section and confirm it matches the approved plan.

Standing Water or Runoff Enters the Excavation

Probable cause: surrounding grade was not shaped to direct runoff away from the trench, or a rain event occurred before drainage measures were installed.
Confirmation: inspect the source of water entry and the surrounding grade condition.
Correction: remove standing water from the bearing surface, shape or install diversion to prevent further entry, and re-inspect the bearing surface for softening before proceeding.
Reinspection: re-inspect the bearing surface and drainage condition after the correction and after the next rain event.

A Worker Enters the Excavation Without the Required Protective System

Probable cause: the protective-system requirement for the excavation's depth or soil condition was not confirmed before access was permitted.
Confirmation: compare the excavation's depth and soil condition against the excavation-safety plan's protective-system requirement.
Correction: stop work immediately, remove personnel from the unprotected excavation, and install the required protective system before access resumes.
Reinspection: confirm the installed protective system with the competent person before any worker re-enters.

Cleanup

This project ends with a protected, open footing excavation left in place for Project 06, not a trench that has been backfilled or concealed.

  • Confirm barricades, protective systems, and drainage measures remain installed and functional
  • Confirm every Project 03 control point remains flagged, protected, and reflects its recorded location
  • Remove loose spoil, tools, and equipment from the excavation exclusion zone and equipment routes
  • Confirm the bearing surface remains clean, undisturbed, and free of standing water
  • File the completed excavation record, photographs, and any escalation or direction documentation with the Project 01 through 04 records
