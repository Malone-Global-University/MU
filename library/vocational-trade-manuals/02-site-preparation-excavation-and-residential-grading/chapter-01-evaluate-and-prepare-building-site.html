Job Overview

This project qualifies you to evaluate a proposed residential building site and produce a documented readiness plan before any layout, clearing, excavation, or grading begins. The finished work product is a complete existing-conditions record — including photographs, a sketch, hazard and access notes, and drainage observations — plus a signed site-readiness checklist that ends in a written proceed, conditional-proceed, or stop-work decision.

You will confirm entry permission, walk the site perimeter, identify existing improvements and hazards, evaluate worker and vehicle access, observe drainage, record approximate existing grades, photograph and sketch the site from fixed reference points, and complete a written checklist that becomes the controlling document for Project 02.

This is the first project in this book. It does not disturb soil, drive stakes, or operate excavation equipment. Every later project in this book — staking, squaring, clearing, excavating, grading, and drainage work — depends on the existing-conditions record and readiness decision produced here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • SP-001 — Conduct a documented site assessment of an assigned residential building site using a fixed sequence of observation, photography, and recordkeeping.
  • SP-002 — Identify and record site hazards, including unstable slopes, soft ground, standing water, overhead and underground utility indicators, contaminated material, and abandoned structures.
  • SP-003 — Verify and document access constraints for workers, delivery trucks, excavation equipment, concrete trucks, and emergency vehicles.
  • SP-004 — Identify and record existing site conditions, including structures, fences, retaining walls, driveways, wells, septic components, drainage features, overhead lines, vegetation, slopes, and neighboring improvements.
  • SP-005 — Locate and record visible property boundary markers without relocating or disturbing them.
  • SP-006 — Complete a written site-readiness checklist and issue a documented proceed, conditional-proceed, or stop-work decision.

Where This Skill Fits

This project opens the book. It has no prior site-preparation project to build on, so it establishes the reference points, documentation habits, and readiness decision that every later chapter relies on. The existing-conditions record you create here — property markers, hazards, access routes, drainage direction, and approximate grades — is the document Project 02 uses to establish the first building reference corner and baseline.

The stop-work discipline introduced here — recording an immediate stop-work condition and naming who must evaluate it — is the same discipline used throughout this book, including the pre-inspection review in Chapter 16 and the final site-readiness certification in Chapter 19.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • obtaining and reviewing the available site plan, survey, property information, utility records, and permit conditions
  • confirming permission for non-destructive entry, inspection, marking, and photography
  • walking the site perimeter and identifying visible property markers without relocating or disturbing them
  • identifying existing structures, improvements, vegetation, slopes, and neighboring conditions
  • identifying access constraints and visible hazards
  • observing surface drainage and recording approximate existing grades
  • photographing and sketching existing conditions from fixed reference points
  • preparing a written site-readiness checklist and issuing a documented decision

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • relocate, remove, or drive out any existing property marker, survey monument, or utility marking
  • drive stakes, disturb soil, clear vegetation, or operate excavation or grading equipment, which are outside this chapter's scope
  • enter a fenced, posted, unstable, flooded, or otherwise restricted area without separate written permission and, where required, a qualified escort
  • treat this assessment as a substitute for a licensed survey, geotechnical report, or utility-locate service; those remain separately required per Section 12 of this project
  • proceed to Chapter 2 staking work until this chapter's readiness checklist has been completed and a proceed or conditional-proceed decision has been issued

Any condition that meets the stop-work criteria in this chapter must be evaluated or corrected by the identified responsible party before site work continues.

Trade Note

Every observation in this chapter is recorded from a fixed reference point — a photograph station, a sketch baseline, or a named property corner — and dated. An undated, unreferenced note is difficult to act on later and is the most common reason a site-readiness record has to be redone before Chapter 2 layout begins.

Setup Standard

Do not enter, mark, photograph, or evaluate the site until permission for non-destructive entry, inspection, marking, and photography has been confirmed and documented (Step 2). An assessment conducted without confirmed permission must be discarded and repeated.

Shop Tip

Photograph and sketch from the same handful of fixed points every time — for example, each visible property corner and the approximate building-footprint center. Consistent reference points make it possible to compare this record against the Chapter 2 layout and the Chapter 19 final inspection without guessing where an earlier photo was taken.

Property Markers and Non-Disturbance

Visible property markers establish the legal boundary of the site and control every layout measurement in Chapter 2. Locate and record each visible marker's approximate position relative to a fixed reference point. Do not pull, reset, tap in, or otherwise relocate a marker during this assessment, and do not assume a missing or ambiguous marker can be estimated; record it as unresolved instead.

Measurement Cue — Record From a Named Reference Point Every Time

Record every distance and photograph note relative to a named, fixed reference point — a property corner, an existing structure corner, or a flagged station. Do not record a distance as "near the fence" or "by the old shed" without also naming the reference point it was measured or estimated from. An unreferenced note cannot be reliably used to control Chapter 2 layout.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop-Work Conditions

Treat unstable slopes, soft or collapsing ground, standing water of unknown depth, exposed or damaged overhead lines, indicators of an underground utility strike, visibly contaminated material, and unsecured abandoned structures as immediate stop-work conditions. Do not approach, probe, or attempt to resolve these conditions. Flag the area from a safe distance, record it, and identify who must evaluate it before work continues.

Field Safety Cue — Perimeter Walk Awareness

Walk the perimeter in daylight with a second person present when the site is unfamiliar, overgrown, or bordered by traffic. Watch footing on uneven ground, holes, debris, and vegetation. Stay outside posted or fenced restricted areas unless separately authorized to enter.

Code Alert — Utility-Locate and Permit Requirements

Confirm whether a utility-locate request has been filed and cleared for this site before any flag, stake, or marker is placed in soil, and confirm which permits or professional reviews apply before site work proceeds. Utility-locate, permit, and inspection requirements vary by jurisdiction and utility provider; verify current local requirements rather than assuming those used on a prior site.

E. Preparation — Document Review and Entry Permission

  1. Obtain the available site plan, survey, property information, utility records, and permit conditions. Assemble every document before the site visit and note any document that is missing or unavailable.
  2. Confirm permission to enter, inspect, mark, and photograph the site. Obtain and record documented, non-destructive-only permission from the property owner or authorized representative before proceeding to Step 3.

F. Perimeter Walk and Existing-Conditions Identification

  1. Walk the site perimeter and identify visible property markers. Locate each marker relative to a named reference point without relocating or disturbing it, and log it in the boundary marker log.
  2. Identify existing structures, improvements, vegetation, slopes, and neighboring conditions. Record buildings, fences, retaining walls, driveways, wells, septic components, drainage features, overhead lines, vegetation, slopes, and neighboring improvements as they are encountered.
  3. Identify access constraints for workers, delivery trucks, excavation equipment, concrete trucks, and emergency vehicles. Log each route, width or clearance limitation, and the vehicle types affected.
  4. Identify visible hazards. Log unstable slopes, soft ground, standing water, overhead utilities, buried-utility indicators, contaminated material, abandoned structures, and restricted areas as they are encountered.
  5. Mark immediate stop-work conditions and record who must evaluate or correct each one. Flag the location from a safe distance without approaching or probing the hazard, and name the responsible party in the hazard log.

G. Drainage, Elevation, and Zone Planning

  1. Observe the direction of existing surface drainage during or after rainfall when practical. Record the observed or inferred flow direction, the date, and the weather condition at the time of observation.
  2. Identify likely locations for the building footprint, material storage, topsoil stockpile, spoil stockpile, equipment movement, temporary access, and erosion controls. Mark each likely zone on the site sketch.
  3. Record existing grades or approximate elevation changes using the approved site-measurement method. Note the method used and the reference point each elevation observation was taken from.
  4. Photograph and sketch existing conditions from fixed reference points. Complete the required photograph set and finish the site sketch with north arrow, scale note, and all identified reference points and improvements.

H. Site-Readiness Checklist and Decision

  1. Prepare a written site-readiness checklist. List acceptable conditions, unresolved conditions, required professional reviews, permits, utility-locate requirements, and next actions.
  2. Review the proposed work sequence against access, drainage, neighbor protection, and environmental constraints. Confirm the checklist reflects every constraint identified during the walk.
  3. Correct administrative and housekeeping deficiencies that can be resolved without disturbing soil. Document each correction made and each deficiency that remains open.
  4. Issue a documented proceed, conditional-proceed, or stop-work decision for Chapter 2. Select one decision, state the reasoning, and sign and date the checklist.

I. Recut and Closeout Procedure

  1. Resolve every open item before issuing a proceed decision. Classify each open item as documentation, permission, boundary, access, hazard, drainage, or professional-review before resolving it.
  2. Label and retain the completed record. Store the existing-conditions record and site-readiness checklist for use as the controlling reference in Project 02.

Common Assessment Inspection

Inspect the completed record using the same checks regardless of site size or configuration.

  • Reference-point check: confirm every photograph and sketch entry references a named, fixed point and a date.
  • Boundary check: confirm every visible property marker is located and logged, and that no marker was relocated or disturbed.
  • Hazard and access check: confirm every identified hazard and access constraint is logged, and every stop-work condition names a responsible party.
  • Drainage and grade check: confirm the drainage observation and approximate grade notes are recorded with method, date, and reference point.
  • Decision check: confirm the site-readiness checklist is complete, signed, dated, and ends in one of the three defined decisions.

Complete the Site-Readiness Checklist in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 02.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Photographs and Sketch Notes Cannot Be Matched to a Location Later

Probable cause: photographs or sketch entries were recorded without naming a fixed reference point, direction faced, or date.
Confirmation: review the photograph captions and sketch entries against the reference-point check in the Common Assessment Inspection section.
Correction: return to the site, re-shoot the affected photographs from the correct reference points, and complete the missing captions and sketch entries.
Reinspection: repeat the reference-point check for the full photograph set and sketch.

A Property Marker Was Disturbed During the Walk

Probable cause: a marker was mistaken for site debris, or a marking flag was driven too close to an existing marker and displaced it.
Confirmation: compare the current marker position and condition against the boundary marker log and the available survey.
Correction: stop and do not attempt to reset the marker; notify the property owner or a licensed surveyor and record the condition and notification in the checklist.
Reinspection: confirm the surveyor's resolution is documented before the boundary marker log is closed.

A Hazard Was Identified but Not Logged as Stop-Work

Probable cause: the hazard was recorded in general notes without being checked against the stop-work criteria in this chapter's Critical Safety Cue.
Confirmation: review each hazard-log entry against the stop-work criteria: unstable slopes, soft or collapsing ground, standing water of unknown depth, exposed or damaged overhead lines, underground utility-strike indicators, visible contamination, and unsecured abandoned structures.
Correction: reclassify any hazard that meets the stop-work criteria, flag it, and name the responsible party who must evaluate it.
Reinspection: repeat the hazard and access check on the corrected log before completing the checklist.

Drainage Direction Could Not Be Observed During Rainfall

Probable cause: the assessment window did not include a rainfall or post-rainfall period.
Confirmation: review the drainage observation entry for a recorded weather condition and date.
Correction: record the inferred drainage direction from visible ground contours, staining, sediment patterns, or vegetation, and schedule a follow-up observation during or after the next rainfall; note this as an open item on the checklist rather than leaving it blank.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage and grade check once the follow-up observation is complete or the inferred entry is documented.

The Checklist Was Signed Before All Sections Were Complete

Probable cause: the decision line was completed before the hazard log, access notes, or drainage observation sections were finished.
Confirmation: review the checklist for blank or partially completed sections above the decision line.
Correction: complete every open section, re-review the decision against the completed record, and re-sign and date the checklist.
Reinspection: repeat the decision check to confirm the checklist is complete and the decision matches the recorded conditions.

Cleanup

This project ends with a complete record filed for reuse, not discarded.

  • Retrieve all temporary marking flags placed during the walk that are not required to remain for Chapter 2, unless they mark an unresolved stop-work condition
  • Confirm no property marker was left disturbed or relocated
  • Compile photographs, sketch, boundary marker log, hazard log, access notes, and drainage observation into a single existing-conditions record
  • File the signed and dated site-readiness checklist with the project records
  • Notify the property owner, general contractor, or other named responsible party of any open stop-work or conditional item before leaving the site

Field Terms

Property Marker
A visible survey monument, pin, or marker indicating a property corner or boundary line.
Reference Point
A fixed, named location used consistently for photographs, sketch measurements, and elevation notes.
Stop-Work Condition
A hazard or unresolved condition serious enough that work may not proceed until a named party evaluates or corrects it.
Existing-Conditions Record
The combined photographs, sketches, measurements, and notes documenting the site as found.
Site-Readiness Checklist
The document recording acceptable conditions, unresolved conditions, required reviews, and the final decision.
Proceed Decision
A determination that site work may begin without additional conditions.
Conditional-Proceed Decision
A determination that site work may begin only after specific named conditions are met.
Stop-Work Decision
A determination that site work may not begin until a specific named condition is resolved.
Utility Locate
The jurisdictional or utility-provider process of marking buried utility locations before ground disturbance.
Benchmark
A fixed reference point of known or assumed elevation used to control grade measurements.

Skill Check

  2. What is the difference between logging a hazard in general notes and logging it as a stop-work condition, and why does that distinction matter?
  3. Why must a visible property marker never be relocated, reset, or disturbed during this assessment, even if it appears to be in the wrong place?
  4. What information must accompany every fixed-reference-point photograph for it to satisfy the acceptance standard?
  5. What are three examples of visible hazards that meet the stop-work criteria in this chapter?
  6. Why is observing drainage during or after rainfall preferred over inferring it from ground contours alone, and what should the learner do when rainfall observation is not practical?
  7. What is the difference between a proceed decision, a conditional-proceed decision, and a stop-work decision?
  8. What must the learner do with administrative or housekeeping deficiencies that can be resolved without disturbing soil?
  9. Why must every access note identify the specific vehicle types affected by a route or clearance limitation?
  10. Why does Chapter 2 depend on the existing-conditions record and readiness decision produced in this chapter, rather than starting its own independent site review?

Performance Check
