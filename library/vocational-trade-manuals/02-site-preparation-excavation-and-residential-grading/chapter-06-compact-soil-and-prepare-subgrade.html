Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare a stable subgrade within the footing excavations produced in Project 05, using approved fill material, controlled lift thickness, moisture conditioning, and compaction equipment matched to the soil type and work area. The finished work product is a subgrade that meets the project's specified elevation, material, and compaction requirements; that is free of organic material, debris, frozen soil, mud, and standing water; that has been verified by density, proof-roll, penetration, or another required method; and that is fully documented and ready for the aggregate base or foundation work that follows.

You will review the required subgrade elevation, material specification, compaction requirement, and test method; inspect the exposed excavation for unsuitable material; place approved fill in controlled lifts; moisture-condition each lift within the permitted range; compact each lift with equipment suited to the soil and work area; check elevation and surface condition at each lift; verify compaction; correct failed areas; shape the final lift to the required line, grade, and drainage condition; protect the finished subgrade; and document every material, lift, test result, and correction.

This is the sixth project in the site preparation sequence. It begins with the inspected footing excavations produced in Project 05, and it does not authorize disturbing the bearing surface, the excavation dimensions, or the Project 03 layout controls preserved through Project 05. The verified subgrade produced here becomes the controlling surface for the gravel base and vapor barrier installed in Project 07.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • SP-031 — Identify suitable fill material and unsuitable soil conditions before placing fill in the Project 05 footing excavation.
  • SP-032 — Place approved fill in controlled lifts at or below the specified loose thickness.
  • SP-033 — Compact each lift using equipment suited to the soil type and work area, following a consistent pass pattern.
  • SP-034 — Verify compacted density, elevation, and surface condition at each lift against the project specification.
  • SP-035 — Prepare the bearing surface by correcting failed areas and shaping the final lift to the required line, grade, and drainage condition.
  • SP-036 — Inspect and document the finished subgrade for readiness before aggregate base or foundation work begins.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 05 produced footing excavations matching the approved plan, with a clean, undisturbed bearing surface, an inspected condition, and a completed excavation record. That excavation, and the benchmark and dimensions it preserves, control this project. Fill placement and compaction in this project proceed only where the approved plan requires fill; where the Project 05 bearing surface already meets the required elevation, this project's inspection and documentation steps still apply, but no fill is placed.

The verified subgrade produced here becomes the working surface for the aggregate base and vapor barrier installed in Project 07 and, for slab construction, the surface built upon in Project 11. A compaction deficiency or elevation error missed here is not caught again until the pre-inspection review in Project 16, by which time it is concealed under aggregate, vapor barrier, or concrete.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the required subgrade elevation, material specification, compaction requirement, drainage condition, and test method before fill is staged
  • inspecting the exposed excavation for organic material, debris, frozen soil, mud, pumping, soft spots, and standing water
  • removing unsuitable material to the approved limits and obtaining approval before filling overexcavated or weak areas
  • inspecting imported or reused fill for compliance with the project specification
  • establishing grade stakes or elevation references that remain visible during placement
  • placing fill in lifts at or below the specified loose thickness and conditioning moisture within the permitted range before compaction
  • compacting each lift using equipment suited to the soil type and work area, following a consistent pass pattern that covers edges, corners, and confined areas
  • checking compacted elevation and surface condition, and performing or arranging the required density, proof-roll, or penetration verification
  • correcting failed areas and shaping the final lift to the required line, grade, and drainage condition
  • protecting the finished subgrade and documenting material, lift thickness, equipment, moisture, test results, and corrections

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • place fill in an overexcavated or weak area without first obtaining approval, regardless of how minor the correction appears
  • compact a lift thicker than the specified loose thickness, or skip a lift, to save time
  • compact fill outside the permitted moisture range without first conditioning it, or proceed with a lift the project specification identifies as too wet or too dry
  • declare a lift acceptable without performing or arranging the required density, proof-roll, penetration, or other verification method
  • disturb, relocate, or re-square any Project 03 control point or Project 05 benchmark while placing fill or operating compaction equipment
  • proceed past a stop-work condition, such as unresolved unsuitable soil or a failed verification result, without the direction of the responsible party

Any condition requiring a soil report, an engineer's determination, a revised fill specification, or a decision outside the approved subgrade plan must be escalated before work proceeds in that area.

Trade Note

The material specification, compaction requirement, and test method referenced throughout this project, including permitted fill sources, lift thickness, moisture range, and required density or verification method, are supplied by the project, engineer, or geotechnical report and are not generated by this chapter. Where this chapter refers to "the project specification," it means that supplied document; do not select a fill material, lift thickness, or compaction standard from general trade familiarity.

Setup Standard

Do not begin fill placement until the required subgrade elevation, material specification, compaction requirement, drainage condition, and test method have been reviewed (Step 1), the exposed excavation has been inspected for unsuitable material (Step 2), unsuitable material has been removed to approved limits (Step 3), and any fill placement in an overexcavated or weak area has received approval (Step 4). Shop Tip

Mark the required loose lift thickness directly on a grade stake driven at the edge of the work area before placing the first lift. Checking a spread of fill against a fixed mark on the stake is faster and more consistent than measuring loose thickness with a tape at multiple points across the lift.

If a field condition suggests the required subgrade elevation should be adjusted, stop and obtain direction before placing fill; do not raise or lower a lift to compensate for an excavation discrepancy discovered during this project.

Measurement Cue — Loose Lift Thickness

Place each lift at or below the specified loose thickness before compaction. Where no specific thickness is stated, treat this as an unresolved condition and obtain direction before proceeding, since a lift placed too thick may not achieve the required density throughout its full depth regardless of the compaction equipment used.

Measurement Cue — Moisture Range Before Compaction

Confirm fill moisture is within the permitted range using the test method identified by the project specification before compacting each lift. Fill compacted outside the permitted moisture range commonly fails density verification even when the correct equipment and pass pattern are used.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work Before Filling an Unresolved Excavation Condition

Do not place fill over a soft spot, unsuitable soil, standing water, or any condition identified but not resolved during Project 05 or during the inspection at Step 2 of this project. Stop work and obtain approval from the responsible party before placing fill in any overexcavated or weak area.

Field Safety Cue — Operating Compaction Equipment in a Confined Excavation

Maintain clearance between the operator, the excavation walls, and any protective system installed under Project 05 while operating compaction equipment within the footing excavation. Confined operating space increases the risk of equipment contact with trench walls or protective-system components.

Code Alert — Compaction and Density-Verification Requirements

Confirm required compaction percentage, verification method, and testing frequency from the project specification, geotechnical report, or applicable regulatory authority before fill placement begins. These requirements vary by soil classification, fill type, and jurisdiction; do not assume a compaction standard used on a prior site applies here.

E. Preparation — Specification Review and Surface Inspection

  1. Review the required subgrade elevation, material specification, compaction requirement, drainage condition, and test method. Confirm these values against the current project specification and geotechnical report before fill is staged.
  2. Inspect exposed soil for organic material, debris, frozen soil, mud, pumping, soft spots, and standing water. Walk the full excavation area and identify every condition inconsistent with a sound fill base.
  3. Remove unsuitable material to the approved limits. Excavate and dispose of identified unsuitable material to the depth and extent required by the project specification or responsible party's direction.
  4. Obtain approval before placing fill in overexcavated or weak areas. Stop work and secure direction from the responsible party before filling any area exposed by unsuitable-material removal or found overexcavated in Project 05.

F. Fill Sourcing and Reference Setup

  1. Inspect imported or reused fill for compliance with the project specification. Confirm material type, gradation, and condition against the specification before the material enters the excavation.
  2. Establish grade stakes or elevation references that remain visible during placement. Mark loose-lift-thickness and finished-elevation references from the Project 03 benchmark before the first lift is placed.
  3. Scarify or prepare the existing surface when required for bonding. Loosen the exposed subgrade surface where the project specification requires scarification before the first lift is placed.

G. Lift Placement and Compaction

  1. Place the first lift at or below the specified loose thickness. Spread approved fill evenly across the work area to the marked loose-thickness reference.
  2. Adjust moisture within the permitted range before compaction. Test and condition the lift's moisture content using the method identified by the project specification before operating compaction equipment.
  3. Compact the lift using equipment suitable for the soil type and work area. Select and operate compaction equipment matched to the fill material and the confined dimensions of the excavation.
  4. Follow a consistent pass pattern that covers edges, corners, and confined areas. Overlap each pass by the amount required to compact the full lift area, including corners and edges against excavation walls.

H. Verification and Repeat Lifts

  1. Check compacted elevation and surface condition. Measure the compacted lift elevation against the benchmark and inspect for rutting, pumping, or loose material.
  2. Perform or arrange the required density, proof-roll, penetration, or other verification. Complete the verification method identified by the project specification before placing the next lift.
  3. Correct failed areas by drying, conditioning, removing, replacing, or recompacting material. Address any area that fails verification using the method appropriate to the identified cause before proceeding.
  4. Repeat placement and compaction for each additional lift. Continue placing, conditioning, compacting, and verifying successive lifts until the specified subgrade elevation is reached.

I. Final Shaping, Protection, and Documentation

  1. Shape the final lift to the required line, grade, and drainage condition. Fine-grade the finished surface to the elevation, slope, and drainage pattern required by the project specification.
  2. Protect the subgrade from traffic, rain, freezing, contamination, and rutting. Install barricades, covers, or access controls appropriate to the site conditions and anticipated weather.
  3. Record material source, lift thickness, equipment, moisture observations, test results, corrections, and final elevation. Complete the compaction record with every required entry for each lift placed.
  4. Complete the subgrade inspection before aggregate base or foundation work begins. Walk the completed subgrade against the Acceptance Standards table and correct any failed item before releasing the surface for Project 07.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the prepared subgrade using the same checks regardless of fill depth or soil condition.

  • Material check: confirm placed fill matches the project's material specification and is free of organic material, debris, and contamination.
  • Lift check: confirm every lift was placed at or below the specified loose thickness and moisture-conditioned before compaction.
  • Compaction check: confirm every lift meets the required density, proof-roll, or penetration standard.
  • Elevation check: confirm finished subgrade elevation matches the approved plan against the Project 03 benchmark.
  • Surface check: confirm the finished surface is free of loose material, rutting, pumping, and standing water, and is shaped for required drainage.
  • Documentation check: confirm the compaction record, test results, and photographs are complete.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Compacted Lift Fails Density or Proof-Roll Verification

Probable cause: fill moisture was outside the permitted range at the time of compaction, the lift was placed thicker than the specified loose thickness, or the compaction equipment did not match the soil type.
Confirmation: re-test moisture content and re-measure the lift thickness at the failed location.
Correction: dry, condition, remove, replace, or recompact the affected lift as appropriate to the identified cause, then re-verify.
Reinspection: re-perform the required verification method across the entire affected area before placing the next lift.

Finished Subgrade Elevation Does Not Match the Benchmark

Probable cause: lift thickness was not tracked accurately against the grade-stake reference, or an intermediate lift was compacted more or less than anticipated.
Confirmation: re-check elevation at multiple points across the affected area using the Project 03 benchmark.
Correction: add or remove fill in a controlled lift to bring the surface to the required elevation, then re-compact and re-verify.
Reinspection: re-check elevation across the entire affected area before shaping the final lift.

Soft Spot or Pumping Discovered Beneath a Placed Lift

Probable cause: unsuitable material beneath the fill was not fully identified and removed during the surface inspection at Step 2, or groundwater intruded after placement began.
Confirmation: remove the affected lift and inspect the underlying surface against the soil information referenced in Step 1.
Correction: stop work in the affected area and obtain direction before removing additional material or placing new fill; do not compact over a confirmed soft spot.
Reinspection: re-inspect the corrected area against the direction provided before resuming fill placement.

Fill Placed in an Overexcavated Area Without Prior Approval

Probable cause: the requirement to obtain approval before filling an overexcavated or weak area was overlooked during Step 4.
Confirmation: compare the affected area against the Project 05 excavation record to identify where overexcavation was noted.
Correction: stop work, notify the responsible party, and obtain retroactive evaluation of the placed fill; remove and replace the fill if directed.
Reinspection: re-verify the corrected area meets the compaction and elevation standard before proceeding.

Standing Water or Rutting Appears on the Finished Subgrade

Probable cause: the final lift was not shaped to the required drainage condition, or rain occurred before protection measures were installed.
Confirmation: inspect the surrounding grade and drainage pattern and identify the source of water entry or traffic causing rutting.
Correction: remove standing water, re-compact and re-shape the affected area to the required grade, and install or improve protection measures.
Reinspection: re-inspect the surface and drainage condition after correction and after the next rain event.

Fill Material Does Not Match the Project Specification

Probable cause: imported or reused fill was not inspected against the specification before delivery, or a substitute material was used without approval.
Confirmation: compare the delivered material against the project's material specification, including gradation and source requirements.
Correction: stop placement of the nonconforming material, remove any already placed, and obtain approved material before resuming.
Reinspection: re-inspect the replacement material before it is placed and re-verify any lift built with the nonconforming material.

Cleanup

This project ends with a protected, verified subgrade left in place for Project 07, not a surface that has been covered with aggregate or concealed.

  • Confirm barricades or protection measures remain installed and functional over the finished subgrade
  • Confirm every Project 03 control point and Project 05 benchmark remains flagged, protected, and reflects its recorded location
  • Remove loose fill, tools, and compaction equipment from the excavation and equipment routes
  • Confirm the finished subgrade surface remains clean, correctly shaped, and free of standing water
  • File the completed compaction record, test results, and photographs with the Project 01 through 05 records

Field Terms

Subgrade
The prepared soil surface, at the required elevation and compaction, on which the aggregate base or foundation work is built.
Lift
A single layer of fill material placed and compacted before the next layer is placed.
Loose Thickness
The depth of a lift as placed, before compaction reduces it to its compacted thickness.
Moisture Conditioning
Adding or reducing water content in fill material to bring it within the permitted range before compaction.
Proof-Roll
A verification method using loaded equipment passes to identify soft or unstable areas in a compacted surface.
Scarify
Loosening the surface of an existing lift or subgrade to promote bonding with the next lift.
Pumping
Visible movement or ejection of water and fine soil from a compacted surface under load, indicating excess moisture or unsuitable material.
Overexcavation
Removal of soil below the approved bottom elevation, requiring direction before correction with fill.
Density Verification
A test method used to confirm that a compacted lift meets the required compaction standard.
Stop-Work Condition
A field condition requiring work to halt until a competent person, engineer, or responsible party provides direction.
