02 – Site Preparation, Excavation, and Residential Grading

This field manual teaches the complete process of preparing a residential construction site for foundation work. Through twenty progressively challenging projects, students learn professional site evaluation, excavation, grading, drainage, soil preparation, utility trenching, erosion control, and final site readiness using industry-standard construction practices.

Overview

Proper site preparation establishes the foundation for every successful construction project. This manual develops practical skills in surveying, layout, excavation, grading, drainage, soil stabilization, environmental protection, and quality control. Each project builds upon previous skills, culminating in the preparation of a complete residential building site ready for foundation construction and inspection.

Learning Outcomes

  • Evaluate residential building sites for safety, suitability, and constructability.
  • Lay out and verify building footprints using professional construction methods.
  • Perform excavation, grading, and soil preparation operations.
  • Install drainage systems and erosion-control measures that protect the jobsite.
  • Prepare foundations, utility trenches, and slab subgrades according to industry standards.
  • Restore disturbed areas and complete final grading after construction activities.
  • Conduct comprehensive site inspections to verify readiness for foundation construction.
  • Execute an entire residential site preparation project from initial evaluation through final inspection.
