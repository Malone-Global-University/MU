Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to plan, execute, inspect, document, and hand off a complete residential building site by integrating the applicable competencies of Projects 1 through 19. The finished work product is a construction-ready residential building site with verified layout, access, earthwork, drainage, subgrade, environmental controls, records, and a formal handoff to the foundation or construction team. This project begins only after Projects 1 through 19 have been completed individually and their competencies demonstrated, and it does not teach any new technique that was not already taught in an earlier project; it requires you to plan, sequence, and integrate that prior work on one continuous site under a single project scope, permit set, and readiness decision.

You will review the approved capstone site plan, scope, permits, utility information, soil information, drainage requirements, and handoff criteria; prepare a complete site logistics, safety, access, erosion-control, inspection, and documentation plan; conduct the existing-conditions assessment and issue the site-readiness decision; establish property, baseline, benchmark, building, and protected-area controls; stake, square, verify, record, and protect the building layout; install required temporary drainage, erosion control, access, and property-protection systems; clear and strip only the approved disturbance area; separate and protect topsoil, structural fill, spoil, reusable material, and waste; perform approved excavation and trenching operations; identify and escalate unsuitable soil, groundwater, utility conflicts, overexcavation, instability, and plan conflicts; prepare and compact subgrades, fills, building pads, and access surfaces; install aggregate, vapor-control, drainage, or bedding assemblies included in scope; maintain elevations, slopes, setbacks, dimensions, and drainage throughout the work; protect utilities, waterproofing, drainage, and completed work during backfill and grading; establish rough and final drainage conditions required for handoff; complete restoration, stabilization, and housekeeping work; perform chapter-level inspections and arrange required official inspections; compile measurement records, compaction results, photographs, material records, approvals, and correction records; conduct the complete site-readiness inspection; prepare and complete the punch list; reinspect every corrected item; and deliver the final site record, maintenance requirements, remaining controls, and foundation-construction handoff.

This is the twentieth and final project in the site preparation sequence. It depends on every prior project in this book: the existing-conditions assessment of Project 1; the layout, squaring, and benchmarking of Projects 2 and 3; the clearing and stripping of Project 4; the footing excavation of Project 5; the subgrade compaction of Project 6; the aggregate base and vapor barrier of Project 7; the temporary drainage and erosion control of Project 8; the utility trenching of Project 9; the rough grading of Project 10; the slab building pad of Project 11; the foundation backfill of Project 12; the foundation drainage of Project 13; the final site drainage of Project 14; the temporary construction access of Project 15; the foundation-inspection preparation of Project 16; the restoration of Project 17; the final site cleanup of Project 18; and the complete site-readiness inspection of Project 19. There is no further project in this book; the certification produced here is the controlling record delivered to the foundation or construction trade.

Where This Skill Fits

Every prior project in this book must already be individually complete before this capstone begins; this project does not substitute for demonstrating any single earlier competency and does not reteach any technique already taught in Projects 1 through 19. Its purpose is to require the learner to plan and sequence that entire body of work as one continuous, permit-governed site-preparation effort, rather than as nineteen disconnected exercises.

This project stands at the end of Book 2 and produces the final record handed to the foundation or construction trade. Because the site-readiness certification or conditional-readiness record produced in Project 19 is the controlling document for this handoff, this capstone does not repeat that certification process from scratch; it relies on the Project 19 record as the final readiness decision for the completed capstone site, updated only for any additional corrective work identified during this integration.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • planning and sequencing the applicable work of Projects 1 through 19 as one continuous, permit-governed site-preparation effort
  • conducting the existing-conditions assessment and layout controls required before earthwork begins
  • installing the temporary drainage, erosion control, access, and property-protection systems the capstone site requires
  • clearing, stripping, excavating, trenching, and preparing subgrades, fills, building pads, and access surfaces within the approved scope
  • installing aggregate, vapor-control, drainage, and bedding assemblies included in the capstone scope
  • maintaining elevations, slopes, setbacks, dimensions, and drainage across every phase of the integrated sequence
  • completing restoration, housekeeping, and the complete site-readiness inspection
  • preparing, completing, and reinspecting a punch list and delivering the final foundation-construction handoff

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin this capstone before individually completing and being assessed on Projects 1 through 19
  • substitute this capstone's integration work for the individual competency assessment required in any earlier project
  • omit, merge, or reorder a required phase of the integrated sequence without documenting the reason, consistent with the book-level authoring rule controlling every project in this book
  • invent a site dimension, equipment limit, trench depth, slope, compaction value, code requirement, or inspection tolerance not supplied by the capstone site plan, manufacturer, engineer, utility authority, or local jurisdiction
  • certify site readiness or conduct the formal handoff while any critical deficiency identified during the capstone inspection remains uncorrected

Any condition discovered during this capstone that exceeds the learner's authorized corrective scope must be escalated to the responsible engineer, jurisdiction, or supervisor before the affected phase continues.

Trade Note

The capstone site plan, permits, utility information, soil information, drainage requirements, and handoff criteria referenced throughout this project are supplied by the project plan, approved permits, or the authority having jurisdiction, and are not generated by this chapter. Where this chapter refers to "the approved capstone plan" or "the acceptance criteria," it means those supplied documents, together with the dimensions, tolerances, and specifications established in Projects 1 through 19; do not substitute personal judgment for a documented requirement.

Common Field Fundamentals

A capstone integration only serves its purpose if each phase is completed, verified, and protected in the order this project requires before the next phase begins. Treating the capstone as a checklist to complete in any convenient order, rather than as a controlled sequence where each phase depends on the one before it, can allow an undetected deficiency in an early phase to compromise every phase built on top of it.

Working Through the Integrated Sequence

Each section of this capstone depends on the sections before it. Beginning excavation before the layout is squared and protected, or attempting the site-readiness inspection before restoration and housekeeping are complete, produces an unreliable result. Work through Sections E through K of this project in the order given, and recheck an earlier section if a later phase reveals a discrepancy. If a deficiency exceeds this project's corrective authority, stop and escalate to the responsible engineer, jurisdiction, or supervisor before continuing.

Measurement Cue — Carrying Dimensions and Elevations Forward

Confirm that every dimension, benchmark elevation, and tolerance established in an earlier project is still current before relying on it in a later phase of this capstone. Do not assume a control point, elevation reference, or measurement remains valid without reverifying it at the point of use.

Code Alert — Confirming Current Requirements Across the Capstone

Confirm current permits, utility-locate status, and jurisdictional inspection requirements before each phase of the capstone, not only at project start. Requirements may change over the duration of a multi-day or multi-week capstone; do not assume conditions confirmed at planning remain unchanged throughout.

Manufacturer Cue — Proprietary Products Used Across Multiple Phases

Where a proprietary drainage, geotextile, vapor-barrier, or erosion-control product is installed in one phase and inspected or protected in a later phase, follow the manufacturer's specified verification method referenced in the originating project record at every point the product is inspected.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Advance a Phase Over an Uncorrected Critical Deficiency

Do not begin a later integration phase while a critical deficiency involving drainage failure, unstable excavation, blocked emergency access, or an unresolved utility conflict remains uncorrected in an earlier phase. Stop, correct or escalate the deficiency, and reverify before advancing.

Field Safety Cue — Moving Among Active Phases on One Site

Wear steel-toe boots at all times, and add a hard hat and high-visibility vest during excavation, equipment operation, and construction-access phases. Maintain clear separation between active excavation, equipment movement, and material staging areas as the capstone moves through its integrated sequence.

E. Capstone Planning and Documentation

  1. Review the approved capstone site plan, scope, permits, utility information, soil information, drainage requirements, and handoff criteria. Confirm every document required by Projects 1 through 19 is available and current for the full capstone site.
  2. Prepare the complete site logistics, safety, access, erosion-control, inspection, and documentation plan. Sequence the applicable phases of Projects 1 through 19 into one continuous plan and identify every internal and official inspection required along the way.

F. Existing-Conditions Assessment and Layout Controls

  1. Conduct the existing-conditions assessment and issue the site-readiness decision. Apply the Project 1 sequence to the full capstone site before any layout, clearing, or excavation begins.
  2. Establish property, baseline, benchmark, building, and protected-area controls. Apply the Project 2 sequence to install offset stakes, batter boards, and string lines from the approved site plan.
  3. Stake, square, verify, record, and protect the building layout. Apply the Project 3 sequence to square the layout using diagonal measurements and the 3-4-5 method, verify dimensions, and protect the finished layout.

G. Protection Systems, Clearing, and Material Separation

  1. Install required temporary drainage, erosion control, access, and property-protection systems. Apply the Project 8 sequence before additional soil disturbance beyond the approved clearing limits.
  2. Clear and strip only the approved disturbance area. Apply the Project 4 sequence, preserving protected vegetation, utilities, and neighboring property.
  3. Separate and protect topsoil, structural fill, spoil, reusable material, and waste. Establish and stabilize stockpiles per the Project 4 sequence before earthwork begins.

H. Excavation, Trenching, and Subgrade Preparation

  1. Perform approved excavation and trenching operations. Apply the Project 5 and Project 9 sequences to excavate footing and utility trenches to approved dimensions and depths.
  2. Identify and escalate unsuitable soil, groundwater, utility conflicts, overexcavation, instability, and plan conflicts. Stop work and obtain direction before correcting any condition exceeding this project's authorized scope.
  3. Prepare and compact subgrades, fills, building pads, and access surfaces. Apply the Project 6, 10, 11, and 15 sequences to compact fill in controlled lifts and verify each required elevation.

I. Assemblies, Drainage, and Backfill Protection

  1. Install aggregate, vapor-control, drainage, or bedding assemblies included in scope. Apply the Project 7, 9, and 13 sequences for the aggregate base, vapor barrier, utility bedding, and foundation drainage included in the capstone scope.
  2. Maintain elevations, slopes, setbacks, dimensions, and drainage throughout the work. Reverify controls from Sections F through H at each subsequent phase before continuing.
  3. Protect utilities, waterproofing, drainage, and completed work during backfill and grading. Apply the Project 12 sequence when backfilling around the foundation.
  4. Establish rough and final drainage conditions required for handoff. Apply the Project 10 and Project 14 sequences to shape rough and final grades that direct water away from the building area.

J. Restoration, Housekeeping, and Readiness Inspection

  1. Complete restoration, stabilization, and housekeeping work. Apply the Project 17 and Project 18 sequences to restore disturbed areas and perform final site cleanup.
  2. Perform chapter-level inspections and arrange required official inspections. Apply the Project 16 sequence to prepare the site for foundation inspection and coordinate jurisdictional inspections at the required stages.
  3. Compile measurement records, compaction results, photographs, material records, approvals, and correction records. Assemble the complete record package from every phase of this capstone.
  4. Conduct the complete site-readiness inspection. Apply the Project 19 sequence to the full capstone site and confirm or update its readiness decision for the completed capstone scope.

K. Punch List and Final Handoff

  1. Prepare and complete the punch list. Consolidate every unresolved deficiency identified during the capstone inspection, assign a responsible party and reinspection method to each.
  2. Reinspect every corrected item. Confirm every punch-list item now meets the acceptance criteria before proceeding to handoff.
  3. Deliver the final site record, maintenance requirements, remaining controls, and foundation-construction handoff. Transfer the complete record package, site-readiness decision, punch-list closure, and post-handoff requirements, and obtain the receiving party's signed acknowledgment.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the completed capstone using the same checks regardless of site size or the number of applicable phases within scope.

  • Planning check: confirm the capstone logistics, safety, access, erosion-control, inspection, and documentation plan was prepared and followed.
  • Layout check: confirm boundaries, control points, benchmarks, and the squared layout remain intact and consistent with the approved plan.
  • Protection check: confirm temporary drainage, erosion control, and clearing limits were installed before soil disturbance beyond approved limits.
  • Earthwork check: confirm excavation, subgrade, fill, and building-pad conditions match the approved plans and acceptance criteria.
  • Assembly and drainage check: confirm aggregate, vapor-control, drainage, and bedding assemblies are installed, protected, and functioning.
  • Restoration and housekeeping check: confirm restoration, stabilization, and final cleanup are complete.
  • Readiness and handoff check: confirm the complete site-readiness inspection, punch list, and formal handoff are complete and documented.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

An Earlier Integration Phase Was Not Fully Verified Before a Later Phase Began

Probable cause: the sequence in Sections E through K was not followed in order, or a required internal inspection was skipped to save time.
Confirmation: compare the capstone documentation plan and phase records against the required order in Sections E through K.
Correction: stop the later phase, return to the unverified earlier phase, complete its required inspection, and correct any discrepancy found before resuming.
Reinspection: reverify the corrected earlier phase, then confirm the later phase still meets its own acceptance criteria before continuing.

A Control Point, Benchmark, or Dimension From an Earlier Project Was Assumed Current Without Reverification

Probable cause: a reference established in Projects 2 or 3 was disturbed by later earthwork and not reconfirmed before use in a later phase.
Confirmation: compare the current field condition against the original Project 2 and 3 layout records.
Correction: re-establish the disturbed reference from the controlling record and document the correction.
Reinspection: reverify every later-phase measurement that depended on the disturbed reference.

A Deficiency Identified in the Project 19 Inspection Was Not Carried Onto the Capstone Punch List

Probable cause: the punch list in Section K was built independently instead of reconciling it against the Project 19 deficiency list.
Confirmation: compare the capstone punch list against the Project 19 deficiency list item by item.
Correction: add any missing item to the punch list, assign a responsible party and reinspection method, and correct it.
Reinspection: confirm every Project 19 deficiency and every capstone-phase deficiency appears on the punch list with a documented reinspection result.

The Formal Handoff Was Conducted Before Every Punch-List Item Was Reinspected

Probable cause: the handoff was scheduled before Section K's reinspection step was completed for every item.
Confirmation: compare the punch list's reinspection column against the date of the formal handoff.
Correction: complete the missing reinspections, update the final site record, and reissue the formal handoff documentation.
Reinspection: confirm the receiving party has an accurate, complete, and signed final site record before foundation work begins.

A Later-Phase Deficiency Exceeded the Learner's Authorized Corrective Scope but Was Not Escalated

Probable cause: the deficiency was treated as a routine correction instead of being recognized as requiring engineering, jurisdictional, or supervisor review.
Confirmation: compare the deficiency's nature against the corrective-scope limits stated in this project's Scope and Intended Use section.
Correction: stop the affected phase and escalate to the responsible engineer, jurisdiction, or supervisor before resuming.
Reinspection: reverify the affected phase once the escalated review or correction is complete and documented.

Cleanup

This capstone ends with a delivered final site record and a documented formal handoff, not the start of foundation construction itself.

  • Confirm every phase of the integrated sequence in Sections E through K was completed in order and independently verified
  • Confirm every punch-list item, including every carried-forward Project 19 deficiency, is corrected or assigned and reinspected
  • Confirm the complete record package from every prior project and this capstone is compiled and filed
  • Confirm post-handoff controls, maintenance, and monitoring requirements are documented
  • Confirm the receiving foundation or construction team has acknowledged the formal handoff in writing
