Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install the sitework portion of a foundation drainage system and connect it to an approved discharge point. The finished work product is a continuous, protected, inspected foundation drainage path connected to an approved discharge system. This project begins only after the foundation surfaces and waterproofing are complete and undamaged; it does not include installing or repairing the waterproofing membrane itself, does not include sump pump selection or electrical connections, and does not include the backfill and compaction that follow in Project 12 where backfill occurs after this drainage work in the approved sequence.

You will review the approved drainage detail; confirm the foundation and waterproofing are complete and undamaged; inspect the footing perimeter and remove debris, standing water, and loose soil; establish pipe invert references and verify the outlet elevation; place filter fabric and the initial drainage aggregate bed; install perforated pipe in the required orientation and maintain continuous slope toward the outlet; join pipe sections and install cleanouts, transitions, and outlet protection; verify pipe elevation and slope at regular intervals; place additional drainage aggregate around and above the pipe; test continuity; and complete the required inspection before the assembly is concealed.

This is the thirteenth project in the site preparation sequence. It depends directly on the completed and inspected foundation waterproofing that precedes it, and on the footing excavation and layout controls established in Project 5. Where the approved scope includes foundation drainage, this project must be complete, inspected, and protected before the backfill work of Project 12 begins; the drainage installed here also supports the final site drainage established in Project 14.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • SP-073 — Review the approved drainage detail, pipe type, elevation, slope, outlet, cleanouts, aggregate, and filter-fabric requirements, and confirm foundation surfaces and waterproofing are complete and undamaged.
  • SP-074 — Inspect the footing perimeter and remove debris, standing water, and loose soil, and establish pipe invert references and verify outlet elevation.
  • SP-075 — Place filter fabric where required without damaging waterproofing, place the initial drainage aggregate bed to the specified thickness and grade, and install perforated drainage pipe in the required orientation while maintaining continuous slope toward the approved outlet or sump.
  • SP-076 — Join pipe sections using compatible fittings, preserve flow continuity, and install corners, cleanouts, transitions, and outlet protection while keeping soil and debris out of the open pipe.
  • SP-077 — Verify pipe elevation and slope at regular intervals, place drainage aggregate around and above the pipe without displacing it, wrap or separate aggregate with filter fabric where specified, and protect waterproofing and insulation during aggregate placement.
  • SP-078 — Test continuity using the approved method, correct low spots, reverse slope, crushed pipe, open joints, blocked outlets, or damaged fabric, photograph and document the system before concealment, complete the required inspection, and protect the assembly during backfill.

Where This Skill Fits

The footing excavation and layout controls from Project 5 define the perimeter this drainage system must follow, and the completed waterproofing this project builds against was installed and inspected before this work begins. This project does not repair or install waterproofing; a damaged or incomplete membrane found during Step 1 or Step 2 is a stop-work condition until the responsible trade corrects it.

The inspected, protected drainage path installed here must remain undisturbed and functional through the backfill of Project 12; do not allow backfill to proceed over an undocumented or unprotected drainage run. The outlet, cleanouts, and slope established here also control the final site drainage of Project 14, which must tie into an outlet condition that already functions correctly.

This project does not authorize the learner to:

Trade Note

The pipe type, invert elevation, slope, outlet location, cleanout spacing, aggregate specification, and filter-fabric requirement referenced throughout this project are supplied by the approved drainage detail, geotechnical report, or responsible design professional, and are not generated by this chapter. Where this chapter refers to "the specified slope" or "the approved outlet," it means those supplied values; do not select a pipe size, slope, or outlet condition from general trade familiarity.

Setup Standard

Do not begin drainage installation until foundation surfaces and waterproofing are confirmed complete and undamaged (Step 1). Do not place aggregate or backfill over the completed pipe run until the required continuity test and inspection are complete (Step 19).

Shop Tip

Set and check every invert reference before the first section of pipe goes in the trench. A pipe run that starts even a fraction of an inch off the intended slope compounds over a long perimeter, and the error is far easier to correct at the first joint than after the whole run is aggregate-covered.

Common Field Fundamentals

Foundation drainage only performs its function if the pipe maintains a continuous downward slope to an approved outlet along its entire length, joints remain sealed and aligned, and the surrounding aggregate and fabric keep the system from clogging with fines. A single reverse section, an open joint, or a blocked outlet can defeat an otherwise correctly installed system, and once the run is covered with aggregate and backfill, correcting these defects becomes far more disruptive.

Working Along the Footing Perimeter

Every section of pipe placed depends on the invert reference established before it and on the protective fabric or covering remaining undamaged over the waterproofing beneath and beside it. Recheck slope continuity at every joint and cleanout throughout Steps 7 through 12 of this project. If a field condition suggests the outlet elevation is incorrect, the waterproofing is damaged, or the pipe run cannot maintain continuous slope as installed, stop and obtain direction before continuing; do not force a pipe run to an incorrect grade to avoid a delay.

Measurement Cue — Invert, Slope, and Outlet Verification

Verify pipe invert elevation and slope at every joint, cleanout, corner, and transition, and confirm the outlet elevation before the first section of pipe is installed. Where no specific verification interval is stated by the approved detail, verify at every fitting and at intervals no greater than the length of one pipe section. An unverified slope discovered after aggregate placement may require removing and replacing completed work.

Code Alert — Pipe, Slope, and Inspection Requirements

Confirm the permitted pipe type, minimum slope, aggregate specification, filter-fabric requirement, and any required drainage inspection from the approved plan or applicable regulatory authority before installation begins. These requirements vary by jurisdiction, soil condition, and foundation type; do not assume a pipe type or slope used on a prior site applies here.

Manufacturer Cue — Pipe Fittings and Fabric Compatibility

Follow the pipe manufacturer's instructions for compatible fittings, joint assembly, and perforation orientation, and follow the filter-fabric manufacturer's instructions for overlap and anchoring. Mixing incompatible pipe and fitting systems, or installing fabric with insufficient overlap, can allow soil intrusion or joint separation that is difficult to detect after concealment.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work Before Concealing an Unverified System

Do not place aggregate cover, backfill, or any concealing material over the drainage assembly until pipe elevation, slope, and continuity have been verified and the required inspection has been completed. Stop work immediately if damaged waterproofing, a reverse slope, a crushed section, or a blocked outlet is discovered, and obtain direction before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Open Trench and Perimeter Hazards

Maintain safe footing and material staging along the open footing perimeter, keep the open pipe ends capped or covered when work is not in progress to prevent debris intrusion, and maintain clear access for inspection before concealment.

E. Preparation — Readiness Confirmation and Plan Review

  1. Review the approved drainage detail, pipe type, elevation, slope, outlet, cleanouts, aggregate, and filter-fabric requirements. Confirm these values against the approved plan or engineer's requirements before materials are staged.
  2. Confirm foundation surfaces and waterproofing are complete and undamaged. Verify with the responsible trade before drainage work begins.

F. Perimeter Preparation and Reference Establishment

  1. Inspect the footing perimeter and remove debris, standing water, and loose soil. Walk the full drainage route and clear every section before further work proceeds.
  2. Establish pipe invert references and verify outlet elevation. Set references at the required interval and confirm the outlet elevation before installing pipe.

G. Bedding, Fabric, and Pipe Installation

  1. Place filter fabric where required without damaging waterproofing. Install fabric against or beneath the specified surfaces before aggregate placement.
  2. Place the initial drainage aggregate bed to the specified thickness and grade. Shape the bed to support the pipe at the established invert.
  3. Install perforated drainage pipe in the required orientation. Position perforations as specified by the approved detail.
  4. Maintain continuous slope toward the approved outlet or sump. Verify slope against invert references as each section is placed.

H. Joints, Fittings, and Connections

  1. Join pipe sections using compatible fittings and preserve flow continuity. Confirm each joint is fully seated before proceeding to the next section.
  2. Install corners, cleanouts, transitions, and outlet protection. Place these components at the locations specified by the approved detail.
  3. Keep soil and debris out of the open pipe. Cap or cover open ends whenever work is not actively in progress.

I. Verification, Cover, and Protection

  1. Verify pipe elevation and slope at regular intervals. Check every joint, cleanout, corner, and transition against the established invert references.
  2. Place drainage aggregate around and above the pipe without displacing it. Place material carefully to maintain verified pipe position.
  3. Wrap or separate aggregate with filter fabric where specified. Follow the approved detail for fabric placement around the completed pipe run.
  4. Protect waterproofing and insulation during aggregate placement. Maintain protective covering throughout this step.

J. Testing, Documentation, and Inspection

  1. Test continuity using the approved method. Perform or arrange the specified test and record the results.
  2. Correct low spots, reverse slope, crushed pipe, open joints, blocked outlets, or damaged fabric. Resolve every deficiency identified during testing before proceeding.
  3. Photograph and document the system before concealment. Capture the pipe run, joints, cleanouts, and outlet connection.
  4. Complete required inspection. Obtain the required sign-off before the assembly is concealed.
  5. Protect the drainage assembly during backfill. Maintain protective measures until the backfill of Project 12 begins.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the completed foundation drainage system using the same checks regardless of perimeter length or pipe run distance.

  • Pipe type and orientation check: confirm approved pipe installed in the required orientation with compatible fittings.
  • Slope continuity check: confirm continuous downward slope to the approved outlet, with no reverse sections or low spots.
  • Joint and connection check: confirm all joints are sealed, aligned, and preserve flow continuity.
  • Aggregate and fabric check: confirm bed and cover meet specified thickness and grade, and fabric is placed without waterproofing damage.
  • Outlet check: confirm the outlet connects to the approved discharge point and is free of blockage.
  • Documentation check: confirm the continuity test and inspection record is complete and current, with photographs taken before concealment.

Complete the Foundation Drainage Record Sheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before the perimeter is concealed by backfill.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Pipe Run Shows a Reverse Slope or Low Spot

Probable cause: invert references were not verified at every joint, or aggregate placement shifted pipe position before it was checked.
Confirmation: measure elevation at the affected section and at the adjacent invert references, and compare against the required continuous slope.
Correction: remove aggregate cover in the affected area, reset the pipe to the correct invert, and recheck slope on both sides of the correction.
Reinspection: reverify slope continuity across the corrected section and the adjoining runs before aggregate cover is replaced.

Joint Is Open, Separated, or Not Fully Seated

Probable cause: the fitting was not fully engaged before backfill or aggregate loading, or an incompatible fitting was used.
Confirmation: inspect the joint directly and compare the fitting against the manufacturer's installation instructions.
Correction: disassemble and reassemble the joint using the compatible fitting and confirm full engagement before proceeding.
Reinspection: retest continuity across the corrected joint before concealment resumes.

Waterproofing Is Damaged During Fabric or Aggregate Placement

Probable cause: protective covering was not maintained over the waterproofing during fabric or aggregate placement, or aggregate contained sharp or oversized material.
Confirmation: inspect the exposed area and compare the damage against the waterproofing manufacturer's installation record.
Correction: stop work in the affected area and obtain direction from the responsible trade before continuing; do not conceal damaged waterproofing with additional aggregate.
Reinspection: confirm the repair is complete and re-protected before drainage work resumes in that area.

Continuity Test Fails or Reveals a Blocked Section

Probable cause: soil, debris, or aggregate fines entered the open pipe before joints were completed, or a section was crushed during placement.
Confirmation: inspect the pipe run for obstruction and compare test results against the approved test method and pass/fail standard.
Correction: clear or replace the obstructed or damaged section and reassemble affected joints.
Reinspection: repeat the continuity test on the corrected section and confirm results meet the pass standard.

Outlet Does Not Discharge to the Approved Point or Shows Blockage

Probable cause: the outlet elevation was not verified before installation, or the outlet connection was not completed to the approved discharge point.
Confirmation: inspect the outlet connection and compare its elevation and routing against the approved drainage detail.
Correction: correct the outlet connection or elevation to match the approved detail, or escalate to the responsible design professional if the approved outlet condition cannot be achieved.
Reinspection: reverify outlet elevation and discharge condition before the system is documented as complete.

Aggregate Bed or Cover Does Not Meet Specified Thickness

Probable cause: aggregate was placed by estimate rather than checked against invert references and the specified thickness.
Confirmation: measure aggregate thickness at several points along the run and compare against the approved detail.
Correction: add or remove aggregate to restore the specified thickness without displacing pipe position.
Reinspection: recheck thickness and pipe position across the corrected section.

Cleanup

This project ends with a protected, inspected foundation drainage system ready for backfill, not a backfilled or restored perimeter.

  • Confirm the completed drainage system is protected from traffic, erosion, and disturbance
  • Confirm grade stakes and invert references remain visible and undisturbed where still needed for backfill
  • Confirm the outlet connection remains accessible and free of blockage
  • Remove surplus pipe cuttings, fabric scraps, and debris from the work area and equipment routes
  • File the completed foundation drainage record, continuity test results, and photographs with the Project 01 through 12 records
