Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install and commission a temporary drainage and erosion-control system consisting of diversion swales, temporary drainage pipe or culverts, silt fence, inlet protection, check dams, and stockpile stabilization, using the approved erosion-control plan, drainage routes, discharge points, and inspection frequency. The finished work product is a functioning system that diverts clean upslope water away from disturbed soil, controls sediment before it leaves the site, protects storm inlets, stabilizes exposed slopes and stockpiles, and maintains construction access, together with a completed inspection and maintenance log.

You will review the erosion-control plan and walk the site to identify upslope water sources and flow paths; install perimeter controls before further soil disturbance; shape diversion swales; install temporary drainage pipe and inlet protection; install silt fence to the specified post spacing, embedment, and termination method; stabilize stockpiles and slopes; provide a stabilized construction entrance; observe the system under controlled or natural flow; and inspect, correct, and maintain the system through an established log.

This is the eighth project in the site preparation sequence. It follows the cleared, stripped work area of Project 04 and the completed footing excavation of Project 05, and it must be installed, where the plan requires it, before additional soil disturbance proceeds in Projects 9 through 15. The system installed here remains in service and under inspection through the rough and final grading work of Projects 10 and 14, and portions of it are removed only when authorized in the final cleanup work of Project 18.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • SP-043 — Review the erosion-control plan and identify upslope water sources, low points, concentrated flow paths, storm inlets, exposed soil, and stockpiles before installing controls.
  • SP-044 — Install diversion swales and temporary drainage pipe or culverts to the approved route and slope, and provide a stabilized construction entrance.
  • SP-045 — Install silt fence at the specified post spacing and embedment, and install inlet protection without creating unsafe flooding or traffic conditions.
  • SP-046 — Stabilize stockpiles and exposed slopes, and install check dams or flow-spreading devices where required.
  • SP-047 — Observe system performance under controlled or natural flow and inspect for bypass, undermining, ponding, erosion, sediment release, and damage.
  • SP-048 — Establish and maintain an inspection and maintenance log, correcting failed conditions and removing accumulated sediment at the required threshold.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 4 produced a cleared and stripped work area with topsoil and spoil stockpiles established outside drainage paths, and Project 5 produced excavated footing trenches. This project installs the temporary drainage and erosion-control system that protects both of those completed work products, and every excavation and grading operation that follows, from uncontrolled runoff and sediment release for the remainder of construction.

The system installed here is not a one-time installation. It remains active and under inspection through the utility trenching of Project 9, the rough grading of Project 10, the backfill and drainage work of Projects 12 through 14, and the construction-access work of Project 15. Controls are only removed, and only in the authorized sequence, during the final cleanup of Project 18 once permanent stabilization is accepted.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the erosion-control plan, drainage routes, discharge points, protected areas, and required inspection frequency
  • walking the site to identify upslope water sources, low points, concentrated flow paths, storm inlets, exposed soil, and stockpiles
  • installing perimeter controls before additional soil disturbance occurs
  • excavating or shaping temporary diversion swales to the approved route and slope
  • installing temporary drainage pipe, culverts, or crossings required to maintain access
  • preparing the silt-fence trench or anchoring system, installing posts at the specified spacing and orientation, and attaching and embedding fabric according to the approved method
  • turning fence ends upslope or providing the required termination so runoff cannot bypass the control
  • installing inlet protection, check dams, or flow-spreading devices where required
  • stabilizing stockpiles and exposed slopes using approved methods
  • providing a stabilized construction entrance to reduce track-out
  • conducting a controlled water-flow observation where permitted, and inspecting for bypass, undermining, ponding, erosion, sediment release, and damage
  • establishing an inspection and maintenance log, inspecting after qualifying rainfall and before predicted severe weather, and removing accumulated sediment at the required maintenance threshold

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • disturb additional soil in an area before the perimeter erosion controls required for that area are installed
  • route a diversion swale, drainage pipe, or discharge point to a location other than the one shown on the approved plan without first obtaining direction
  • install silt fence at a post spacing, fabric embedment depth, or termination method other than the one specified by the plan or manufacturer instructions
  • leave a fence end, low point in the fence line, or inlet-protection gap that allows runoff to bypass the control
  • remove or relocate a temporary control before permanent stabilization is accepted, except as authorized in the final cleanup sequence
  • proceed past a stop-work condition, such as an undermined fence, a blocked outlet, or an unstabilized breach, without the direction of the responsible party

Any condition requiring a revised drainage route, a different erosion-control device, an engineer's determination, or a decision outside the approved erosion-control plan must be escalated before work proceeds in that area.

Trade Note

The drainage routes, discharge points, post spacing, fabric embedment depth, check-dam stone size, and inspection frequency referenced throughout this project are supplied by the approved erosion-control plan, manufacturer instructions, or the applicable regulatory authority, and are not generated by this chapter. Where this chapter refers to "the erosion-control plan" or "the manufacturer's instructions," it means those supplied documents; do not select a route, spacing, or device from general trade familiarity.

Setup Standard

Do not disturb additional soil in an area until the perimeter erosion controls required for that area, as identified during the site walk (Step 2), have been installed (Step 3). No excavation or clearing beyond what Projects 4 and 5 already authorized may proceed in an unprotected area.

Shop Tip

Flag every silt-fence post location and every low point along the fence line before driving the first post. Walking the full run first, with the plan in hand, catches a low point that would otherwise become an undetected bypass after the fence is fully installed and backfilled.

Common Field Fundamentals

A temporary drainage and erosion-control system only functions if every control is installed at the correct location, to the correct dimension, before the soil it protects is disturbed further. A control installed after disturbance has already occurred, or installed with a gap, low point, or bypass, does not provide the protection the plan assumes it provides for every later chapter.

Working From the Protected Site

Every control installed in this chapter depends on the stockpile locations, clearing limits, and excavation limits established in Projects 4 and 5. Recheck those limits at Step 2 of this project before installing any control. If a field condition suggests a stockpile, swale, or discharge point should be relocated, stop and obtain direction before installing a control around the new location; do not relocate a stockpile or discharge point to compensate for a drainage conflict discovered during this project.

Measurement Cue — Silt-Fence Post Spacing and Fabric Embedment

Install posts at the specified spacing and embed fabric to the specified trench depth before backfilling. Where no specific spacing or embedment depth is stated, treat this as an unresolved condition and obtain direction before proceeding, since a fence installed at an unverified spacing or embedment may not withstand the sediment load and hydraulic pressure it is designed to resist.

Manufacturer Cue — Fabric Attachment and Inlet-Protection Installation

Attach fabric to posts using the fastener type and spacing specified by the manufacturer, and install inlet-protection devices according to the manufacturer's method for the specific inlet type. A device installed by improvisation, rather than the manufacturer's method, may not seat correctly and may allow sediment bypass.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work Before an Undermined or Bypassed Control Is Left in Service

Do not leave a silt fence, swale, or inlet-protection device in service if it shows undermining, a low point, a gap, or evidence of sediment bypass. Stop work in the affected area, correct the condition, and reverify performance before further soil disturbance proceeds downslope of that control.

Field Safety Cue — Trenching, Post Driving, and Stone Handling

Keep hands and feet clear of the post-driving path during post installation, and maintain stable footing when handling wheelbarrows loaded with check-dam or entrance stone on uneven or wet ground.

Code Alert — Erosion-Control Plan and Inspection Requirements

Confirm the required inspection frequency, permitted discharge points, and any permit conditions from the approved erosion-control plan or applicable regulatory authority before work begins. These requirements vary by jurisdiction, site size, and receiving water; do not assume an inspection frequency or discharge point used on a prior site applies here.

E. Preparation — Plan Review and Site Walk

  1. Review the erosion-control plan, drainage routes, discharge points, protected areas, and inspection frequency. Confirm these values against the current approved plan before any control is installed.
  2. Walk the site and identify upslope water sources, low points, concentrated flow paths, storm inlets, exposed soil, and stockpiles. Record every condition affecting where and how each control must be installed.

F. Perimeter Controls, Swales, and Drainage Pipe

  1. Install perimeter controls before additional soil disturbance. Complete silt fence or other perimeter controls around each area before that area is disturbed further.
  2. Excavate or shape temporary diversion swales to the approved route and slope. Shape each swale to maintain continuous flow toward its approved discharge point.
  3. Install temporary drainage pipe, culverts, or crossings where required to maintain access. Set pipe or culvert sections to the specified size and slope at each required crossing.

G. Silt Fence and Inlet Protection

  1. Prepare the silt-fence trench or anchoring system. Excavate the trench to the specified embedment depth along the approved fence line.
  2. Install posts at the specified spacing and orientation. Drive posts to the specified depth at the specified spacing along the fence line.
  3. Attach and embed fabric according to the approved installation method. Attach fabric to posts using the specified fastener and method, and embed the fabric bottom in the prepared trench.
  4. Turn fence ends upslope or provide required termination so runoff cannot bypass the control. Terminate every fence end per the approved method to prevent flow from passing around it.
  5. Install inlet protection without creating unsafe flooding or traffic conditions. Fit each device to its inlet per the manufacturer's method and confirm it does not obstruct required drainage or traffic flow.

H. Stabilization, Check Dams, and Construction Entrance

  1. Stabilize stockpiles and exposed slopes using approved methods. Cover each stockpile and exposed slope in scope with the specified blanket, seed/mulch, or other approved cover.
  2. Install check dams or flow-spreading devices where required. Place stone or fabric structures at each location shown on the approved plan.
  3. Provide a stabilized construction entrance to reduce track-out. Place entrance stone to the specified depth and dimensions at each active access point.

I. Performance Observation, Inspection, and Maintenance

  1. Conduct a controlled water-flow observation where permitted. Introduce or observe water flow through the installed system and record its behavior at each control.
  2. Inspect for bypass, undermining, ponding, erosion, sediment release, and damage. Walk the full system and identify any condition inconsistent with the Acceptance Standards table.
  3. Correct failed grades, loose fabric, blocked outlets, unstable stone, or inadequate stabilization. Repair or reinstall any control that fails inspection before the affected area is disturbed further.
  4. Establish an inspection and maintenance log. Record the installation date, initial performance observation, and every subsequent inspection date, finding, and correction.
  5. Inspect after qualifying rainfall and before predicted severe weather. Maintain the inspection schedule required by the approved plan or applicable regulatory authority.
  6. Remove accumulated sediment at the required maintenance threshold. Clean out sediment from behind fences, in swales, and at inlet-protection devices when it reaches the specified depth.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the completed temporary drainage and erosion-control system using the same checks regardless of site size or control layout.

  • Route and slope check: confirm swales and drainage pipe follow the approved route and maintain positive slope to the approved discharge point.
  • Fence installation check: confirm post spacing, fabric embedment, and end terminations match the specification.
  • Inlet protection check: confirm every required storm inlet has a properly seated protection device.
  • Stabilization check: confirm stockpiles and exposed slopes are fully covered per the approved method.
  • Performance check: confirm no bypass, undermining, ponding, erosion, or sediment release was observed during the controlled or natural-flow review.
  • Documentation check: confirm the inspection and maintenance log is established and current.

Complete the Temporary Drainage and Erosion Control Record Sheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before additional soil disturbance proceeds in the protected area.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Silt Fence Undermined or Shows a Gap at the Base

Probable cause: the fabric-embedment trench was not excavated to the specified depth, or backfill over the embedded fabric was not adequately compacted.
Confirmation: excavate a short section at the affected location and measure the actual embedment depth against the specification.
Correction: re-excavate the trench to the specified depth, re-embed the fabric, and compact the backfill.
Reinspection: re-verify embedment depth and observe the corrected section under flow before declaring it complete.

Runoff Bypasses the Fence at an End or Low Point

Probable cause: the fence end was not turned upslope or terminated per the approved method, or a low point in the fence line was not identified during the site walk.
Confirmation: observe flow at the affected location during a controlled or natural-flow event and trace the bypass path.
Correction: extend or re-terminate the fence end per the approved method, or raise the low point to match the required fence-line elevation.
Reinspection: re-observe flow at the corrected location before further disturbance proceeds downslope.

Diversion Swale Ponds Instead of Draining to the Approved Discharge Point

Probable cause: the swale was shaped with a reverse or insufficient slope, or debris has blocked the flow line.
Confirmation: check the swale slope with a string line and line level along its full length.
Correction: reshape the swale to the approved slope and remove any blocking debris.
Reinspection: re-check slope and observe flow along the corrected section.

Inlet Protection Device Creates Unsafe Flooding or Traffic Condition

Probable cause: the device selected does not match the inlet type, or it was installed in a location that obstructs required drainage or a traffic route.
Confirmation: compare the installed device against the manufacturer's inlet-type compatibility chart and inspect surrounding drainage and traffic conditions.
Correction: replace the device with one compatible with the inlet type, or relocate the installation per the approved plan.
Reinspection: re-verify the inlet drains as intended and does not obstruct required traffic flow.

Stockpile or Slope Shows Erosion Despite Stabilization

Probable cause: the stabilization method did not achieve full coverage, or the blanket or seed/mulch application was disturbed before establishment.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area for bare spots, displaced blanket, or washout patterns.
Correction: reapply the approved stabilization method to achieve full coverage and re-anchor any displaced blanket.
Reinspection: re-inspect the area after the next qualifying rainfall.

Sediment Accumulation Exceeds the Maintenance Threshold

Probable cause: the inspection and maintenance log was not reviewed at the required frequency, or accumulated sediment was not removed when it reached the specified depth.
Confirmation: measure sediment depth behind the fence, in the swale, or at the inlet-protection device against the specified maintenance threshold.
Correction: remove accumulated sediment to an approved disposal location and record the removal on the maintenance log.
Reinspection: re-verify sediment depth is below the maintenance threshold and confirm the control continues to perform as designed.

Cleanup

This project ends with a functioning, inspected temporary drainage and erosion-control system left in service, not a system removed before permanent stabilization is accepted.

  • Confirm every installed control remains in place, undamaged, and free of accumulated sediment above the maintenance threshold
  • Confirm every Project 03 control point and Project 04 stockpile boundary remains flagged, protected, and reflects its recorded location
  • Remove surplus fabric, posts, pipe, stone, and packaging from the work area and equipment routes
  • Confirm the stabilized construction entrance remains functional and free of excessive mud accumulation
  • File the completed inspection and maintenance log, performance observation record, and photographs with the Project 01 through 05 records

Field Terms
