Job Overview

This project qualifies you to clear the approved work limits and strip unsuitable organic surface material from a residential building site while preserving everything established in Projects 01 through 03. The finished work product is a cleared, stripped, protected, and documented work area: vegetation and debris removed only within the approved clearing limits, topsoil separated and stockpiled outside drainage paths and traffic routes, reusable and disposal materials sorted, protected trees and neighboring improvements undamaged, the verified layout and benchmark from Project 03 intact and re-confirmed, and a complete written record of what was cleared, stripped, stockpiled, and disposed of.

You will review the approved clearing limits and erosion-control requirements, mark no-entry and tree-protection zones with the equipment operator, install required temporary erosion and sediment controls before disturbing soil, remove debris and clear approved vegetation, sort materials by disposition, establish and protect a topsoil stockpile, strip topsoil to the specified depth, rough-level the cleared area, re-inspect and re-establish preserved control points, and document the completed work before excavation begins.

This is the fourth project in the site preparation sequence. It begins with the exact stakes, batter boards, benchmark, and string lines verified and protected at the end of Project 03, and it does not authorize disturbing, relocating, or re-squaring that layout. The cleared and stripped area produced here becomes the controlling work surface for the footing excavation performed in Project 05.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • SP-019 — Remove vegetation within the approved clearing limits without damaging retained trees, fences, utilities, or neighboring improvements.
  • SP-020 — Clear debris, trash, and abandoned materials from the work area using the required handling and sorting method.
  • SP-021 — Strip topsoil and organic material to the specified depth or until suitable subsoil is exposed, keeping topsoil separate from structural fill, subsoil, rubble, and contaminated material.
  • SP-022 — Establish, stabilize, and protect reusable-material stockpiles outside drainage paths, excavation limits, and traffic routes.
  • SP-023 — Prepare the cleared and stripped work area, including rough-leveling, hazard removal, and re-establishment of any disturbed control point using the approved record.
  • SP-024 — Protect surrounding property, preserved vegetation, utilities, and the Project 03 layout throughout clearing and stripping, and document the completed work.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 03 produced a documented and independently verifiable building layout: squared corners, equal diagonals, confirmed setbacks, and a benchmark transferred to protected batter boards or grade stakes. That layout now controls every operation that follows. This project clears vegetation and debris and strips topsoil across the approved work limits while treating every stake, batter board, string line, and benchmark reference established in Project 03 as fixed and undisturbed.

The cleared, stripped, and documented site produced here becomes the working surface for the footing excavation performed in Project 05 and the subgrade preparation performed in Project 06. Topsoil separated and protected in this project is the material returned to disturbed areas during the restoration performed in Project 17.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing approved clearing limits, erosion-control requirements, protected areas, utility markings, and site-access routes before any equipment enters the site
  • marking no-entry, no-disturbance, and tree-protection zones with the equipment operator
  • installing required temporary erosion and sediment controls before disturbing soil
  • removing debris and clearing approved vegetation within the marked limits
  • separating reusable, recyclable, vegetative, contaminated, and disposal materials
  • establishing, stripping to, and stabilizing a topsoil stockpile outside drainage paths, excavation limits, and traffic routes
  • rough-leveling the cleared area, re-establishing any disturbed control point, and documenting the completed work before the clearing and stripping inspection

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • clear, grub, or strip outside the approved clearing limits marked in this project, regardless of equipment access or convenience
  • remove, damage, or relocate any stake, batter board, string line, or benchmark reference verified in Project 03; a disturbed control point must be re-established from the approved record, not re-created from memory
  • begin footing excavation before the clearing and stripping inspection required by this project has been completed
  • remove a tree, stump, root mass, or structure requiring special removal approval or equipment without first obtaining that approval
  • mix topsoil with structural fill, subsoil, rubble, or contaminated material at any point during stripping or stockpiling
  • disturb soil before the required temporary erosion and sediment controls are installed

Any condition requiring special removal approval, a soil report, an environmental determination, or a decision outside the approved clearing plan must be escalated before work proceeds in that area.

Trade Note

Clearing limits and tree-protection zones in this chapter are walked and marked jointly with the equipment operator before any machine enters the site, per Step 2 of the required sequence. Marking limits after equipment has already begun work does not satisfy this requirement; the walk-and-mark step is a precondition for disturbing soil, not a documentation step performed afterward.

Setup Standard

Do not begin clearing, grubbing, or stripping until the approved clearing limits, erosion-control requirements, and protected areas have been reviewed (Step 1), the limits have been walked and marked jointly with the equipment operator (Step 2), and required temporary erosion and sediment controls have been installed (Step 3). No soil may be disturbed before Step 3 is complete.

Shop Tip

Flag the Project 03 control points, batter boards, and benchmark with a distinct flag color before clearing equipment enters the site, and record that color on the clearing record. A control point flagged in a color the operator has been briefed to avoid is far less likely to be disturbed than one identified only by its physical appearance among cleared brush and debris.

Working Within the Approved Clearing Limits

Every clearing, grubbing, and stripping action in this chapter takes place within the clearing limits reviewed in Step 1 and marked jointly with the equipment operator in Step 2. If a condition in the field suggests the marked limits should be adjusted — an unmarked tree worth retaining, an unexpected slope, or a buried feature — stop and obtain approval before clearing beyond or differently from the approved limits. Do not expand the clearing limits to make equipment access easier.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Stripping Depth Against the Approved Plan

Strip topsoil to the depth specified in the approved plan, or, where no specific depth is stated, until subsoil suitable for the next operation is exposed. Check depth at regular intervals across the stripped area rather than relying on a single reference point, since organic depth commonly varies across a residential lot. Record the achieved depth at each check point on the clearing record.

Measurement Cue — Locate the Stockpile Before Stripping Begins

Establish the topsoil stockpile location and boundary before stripping begins, and confirm the location falls outside drainage paths, the excavation limits established for Project 05, and equipment or delivery traffic routes identified in Project 01. Relocating an established stockpile after material has already been placed increases handling and the risk of contaminating the topsoil with subsoil or debris.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Disturb Soil Before Erosion Controls Are Installed

Do not begin clearing, grubbing, or stripping until the required temporary erosion and sediment controls identified in the erosion-control plan are installed. Disturbing soil before these controls are in place allows sediment to reach drainage paths, neighboring property, or storm systems before any control exists to intercept it, and may violate permit conditions.

Field Safety Cue — Working Around Clearing and Grubbing Equipment

Maintain visual contact with the equipment operator at all times when working on foot near operating clearing or grubbing equipment. Approach equipment only after it has come to a complete stop and the operator has acknowledged your presence. Keep hand-clearing crews outside the equipment's swing radius and travel path.

Code Alert — Tree Protection, Erosion Control, and Disposal Requirements

Confirm tree-protection, erosion-control, and debris-disposal requirements from the approved plan, permit conditions, or local jurisdiction before clearing begins. Requirements for protected-species vegetation, tree-removal permits, burn restrictions, and landfill or recycling acceptance vary by jurisdiction and by site; do not assume a requirement used on a prior site applies here.

E. Preparation — Plan Review and Limit Marking

  1. Review approved clearing limits, erosion-control requirements, protected areas, utility markings, and site-access routes. Confirm the clearing plan, erosion-control plan, and any tree-protection or utility marking against the current site condition before staging equipment.
  2. Walk the limits with the equipment operator and mark no-entry, no-disturbance, and tree-protection zones. Flag or paint the approved clearing boundary, tree-protection zones, and no-entry areas jointly with the operator before equipment enters the site.
  3. Install required temporary erosion and sediment controls before disturbing soil. Install every control identified in the erosion-control plan and confirm it is functional before any clearing, grubbing, or stripping begins.

F. Debris Removal and Vegetation Clearing

  1. Remove loose trash, abandoned materials, and surface debris using the required handling method. Clear debris from the approved area first, sorting it by disposition as it is removed.
  2. Cut and remove brush, weeds, and small vegetation within the approved limits. Clear vegetation only within the marked clearing limits, working outward from cleared debris areas.
  3. Identify trees, stumps, roots, or structures requiring special removal approval or equipment. Flag any feature that requires approval or specialized equipment before attempting removal, and escalate for approval where required.
  4. Remove approved vegetation without damaging retained trees, fences, utilities, or neighboring improvements. Use hand-clearing methods near tree-protection zones, fences, utilities, and property lines where machine clearing risks damage.

G. Material Sorting and Stockpile Establishment

  1. Separate reusable, recyclable, vegetative, contaminated, and disposal materials. Direct each cleared and stripped material to its designated container or stockpile as it is generated, rather than sorting a mixed pile afterward.
  2. Establish the topsoil stockpile outside drainage paths, excavation limits, and traffic routes. Confirm and mark the stockpile boundary against the Project 01 access and drainage records before placing any material.

H. Topsoil Stripping

  1. Strip topsoil and organic material to the specified depth or until suitable subsoil is exposed. Strip in controlled passes, checking depth at regular intervals across the stripped area.
  2. Keep topsoil separate from structural fill, subsoil, rubble, and contaminated material. Direct stripped topsoil to the established stockpile only, and route subsoil, rubble, or contaminated material to its own designated location.
  3. Stabilize and protect stockpiles from erosion and runoff. Apply the specified stabilization method to the topsoil stockpile promptly after each addition of material.

I. Site Preparation, Control-Point Verification, and Documentation

  1. Rough-level the cleared area enough to support safe access and the next operation. Level the stripped surface sufficiently for safe foot and equipment access without attempting final subgrade preparation.
  2. Inspect preserved stakes, benchmarks, batter boards, and utility markings. Compare every Project 03 control point against the layout record sheet and confirm it remains in its recorded location and condition.
  3. Re-establish any disturbed control point using the approved record. Where a control point cannot be confirmed, re-establish it from the Project 03 layout record sheet and the controlling reference identified there; do not re-create it from memory or from an adjacent stake.
  4. Remove protruding hazards and clean equipment routes. Clear roots, stakes, debris, and other protruding hazards from equipment travel paths and pedestrian routes.
  5. Document stripped limits, stockpile locations, removed material, and unresolved conditions. Record the final stripped area, stockpile boundary, sorted-material disposition, and any unresolved condition on the clearing and stripping record.
  6. Complete the clearing and stripping inspection before excavation begins. Walk the completed work against the Acceptance Standards table, correct any failed item, and sign off the clearing and stripping record before Project 05 begins.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the cleared and stripped site using the same checks regardless of lot size or vegetation density.

  • Limit check: confirm clearing and stripping occurred only within the approved clearing limits.
  • Protection check: confirm retained trees, fences, utilities, neighboring improvements, and the Project 03 layout remain undamaged.
  • Erosion-control check: confirm required temporary controls were installed before soil disturbance and remain functional.
  • Stockpile check: confirm the topsoil stockpile is stabilized, correctly located, and kept separate from subsoil, fill, and contaminated material.
  • Documentation check: confirm the clearing and stripping record, photographs, and control-point verification are complete.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Clearing Extends Beyond the Marked Limits

Probable cause: the equipment operator worked beyond the flagged boundary, or the clearing limits were marked imprecisely relative to the approved plan.
Confirmation: measure the disturbed area against the approved clearing plan and the flagged boundary.
Correction: stop clearing in the affected area, notify the responsible party, and document the extent of the overclearing; restoration of the overcleared area is addressed under Project 17 unless corrected immediately.
Reinspection: re-walk the entire clearing boundary with the operator before clearing resumes.

A Project 03 Control Point Is Missing or Disturbed

Probable cause: the control point was not flagged distinctly from surrounding vegetation, or clearing equipment traveled through the marked no-disturbance zone.
Confirmation: compare the affected area against the Project 03 layout record sheet and determine which control point is missing or moved.
Correction: re-establish the control point from the layout record sheet and the controlling reference identified in Project 03, Step 3; do not estimate its position from nearby stakes.
Reinspection: re-verify the re-established point against the layout record sheet and confirm no other control point was affected.

Topsoil Is Mixed With Subsoil or Debris

Probable cause: stripping equipment cut below the topsoil layer in one pass, or material was placed in the wrong stockpile.
Confirmation: inspect the affected stockpile and compare its material against the topsoil specification.
Correction: separate the contaminated material from the reusable topsoil, and relocate misplaced material to its correct stockpile or disposal location.
Reinspection: re-inspect the corrected stockpile and confirm the remaining topsoil meets the project specification.

The Topsoil Stockpile Shows Erosion or Runoff

Probable cause: the stockpile was not stabilized promptly after placement, or it was located where runoff concentrates.
Confirmation: inspect the stockpile surface and surrounding area for rilling, sediment tracks, or ponding.
Correction: apply the specified stabilization method to the exposed surface, and relocate the stockpile if its current location cannot be adequately stabilized.
Reinspection: re-inspect the stockpile after the next rainfall or at the required maintenance interval.

A Tree or Feature Requiring Special Approval Was Removed Without Authorization

Probable cause: the feature was not correctly identified during the Step 2 joint walk, or work proceeded before approval was obtained.
Confirmation: compare the removed feature against the approved clearing plan and any special-approval requirement on file.
Correction: stop work in the affected area, document the removal, and notify the responsible party or jurisdiction as required.
Reinspection: confirm remaining features requiring special approval are correctly flagged before clearing resumes.

The Stripped Area Fails the Depth Check at Some Locations

Probable cause: organic depth varies across the site and was not checked at enough intervals during stripping.
Confirmation: re-check depth at additional intervals across the affected area and compare against the specified stripping depth.
Correction: strip the affected area to the specified depth or until suitable subsoil is exposed, directing any additional stripped material to the correct stockpile.
Reinspection: re-check depth across the entire stripped area before completing the clearing and stripping inspection.

Cleanup
