Job Overview

This project qualifies you to conduct a complete internal pre-inspection and prepare the site, excavation, controls, records, and access for the required foundation inspection. The finished work product is a foundation site that is safe, documented, accessible, and ready for official inspection. This project begins only after the building layout of Project 3, the footing excavation of Project 5, and the site drainage and utility work of Projects 8 through 14 have been completed within the scope reviewed here, and it does not include performing the official jurisdictional inspection itself, does not include correcting deficiencies outside the authorized scope of this project, and does not include foundation forming, reinforcement placement, or concrete work.

You will obtain the jurisdictional inspection checklist and approved foundation documents; confirm the inspection stage and identify work that must remain visible; verify permit documents, approved plans, revisions, engineering, soil reports, and prior approvals are available; inspect building location, setbacks, orientation, dimensions, and diagonals; verify footing lines, widths, depths, steps, pads, and bearing surfaces; verify benchmarks, bottom elevations, and required design depths; inspect excavation access, protective systems, spoil placement, barricades, and housekeeping; check for water, loose soil, mud, overexcavation, soft spots, and disturbed bearing material; verify sleeves, utility crossings, drainage provisions, and embedded items scheduled for this stage; confirm layout strings and reference points are readable and protected; prepare measurement records, photographs, test reports, and correction documentation; create a deficiency list organized by safety, layout, excavation, grade, documentation, and access; correct all deficiencies within the authorized scope; escalate engineering, survey, soil, structural, or code conflicts; reinspect every corrected item; provide clean and safe inspector access; mark items requiring explanation and prepare controlling documentation; and complete and sign the internal pre-inspection record.

This is the sixteenth project in the site preparation sequence. It depends on the squared layout of Project 3, the footing excavation of Project 5, the subgrade and drainage work of Projects 6 through 14, and the temporary access constructed in Project 15, and it uses the site-evaluation and hazard information recorded in Project 1. The internal pre-inspection record produced here becomes part of the evidence reviewed during the complete site-readiness inspection of Project 19, and a passed official foundation inspection is the condition that allows the foundation trade to begin work on the site prepared by this book.

Where This Skill Fits

The squared building layout of Project 3, the footing excavation of Project 5, the subgrade preparation of Project 6, and the drainage and utility work of Projects 8 through 14 must already be complete within the scope of this inspection; work that has not been verified in an earlier chapter is a stop-work condition for beginning this pre-inspection review. The temporary access built in Project 15 must remain functional to provide safe inspector access to the site.

This project stands between the completed sitework of Projects 1 through 15 and the official jurisdictional foundation inspection. Its correction and reinspection cycle protects the schedule by finding deficiencies before an official inspector does, and its records become part of the evidence reviewed during the complete site-readiness inspection of Project 19. A site that fails the official inspection because this internal review was skipped or performed incompletely can delay every trade waiting to begin foundation work.

Setup Standard

Do not begin the internal pre-inspection review until the jurisdictional checklist and approved foundation documents are on hand and the inspection stage is confirmed (Step 1). Do not request official inspection until every identified deficiency within scope has been corrected and reinspected, and the internal pre-inspection record is complete and signed (Step 17).

Shop Tip

Organize the deficiency list by category — safety, layout, excavation, grade, documentation, and access — as you inspect rather than afterward. A category-sorted list makes it easier to assign corrections to the right person and to confirm nothing was missed before requesting official inspection.

Common Field Fundamentals

An internal pre-inspection only serves its purpose if it is measured against the same checklist, plans, and design values the official inspector will use. Reviewing layout, excavation, grade, and documentation against assumed or remembered values instead of the approved jurisdictional checklist and plans can pass a site internally that fails the official inspection, which delays every trade waiting to begin foundation work.

Working Through the Pre-Inspection Review

Every category of this review depends on the categories before it. A layout that has not been reverified cannot support a reliable excavation-dimension check, and an excavation that has not been reverified cannot support a reliable bearing-surface check. Work through Steps 4 through 13 of this project in the order given, and recheck any earlier category if a later check reveals a discrepancy. If a field condition suggests the approved design cannot be met as specified, an engineering or soil condition conflicts with the plan, or a required document cannot be obtained, stop and escalate before continuing; do not correct the deficiency using an assumed value.

Measurement Cue — Layout, Depth, and Elevation Verification

Verify building location, setbacks, dimensions, diagonals, footing widths, depths, and bottom elevations against the approved plan and established benchmark at every corner, step, pad, and service crossing within scope. Record each measured value alongside the approved value on the pre-inspection record. A discrepancy discovered after the official inspector arrives may require rescheduling the inspection.

Code Alert — Jurisdictional Checklist and Documentation Requirements

Confirm the exact checklist, required documentation, and inspection stage from the responsible jurisdiction before beginning the review. Checklist items, required permits, and documentation vary by jurisdiction and project type, and do not assume a checklist used on a prior site applies here.

Manufacturer Cue — Embedded Items and Sleeves

Verify that any manufactured sleeve, waterstop, or embedded item scheduled for this inspection stage is installed according to the manufacturer's instructions and remains visible for inspection. An embedded item installed without following its manufacturer's instructions may require removal and reinstallation before the official inspection.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work Before Concealing Required Work

Do not backfill, cover, or otherwise conceal any excavation, footing, sleeve, crossing, or embedded item that must remain visible for the official foundation inspection. Stop work immediately if a deficiency is found that cannot be corrected within this project's authorized scope, and escalate to the responsible engineer, surveyor, geotechnical professional, or code authority before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Open Excavation and Inspector Access

Maintain barricades, protective systems, and safe access routes around open excavation throughout the pre-inspection review, correction, and reinspection process. Confirm the inspector access route is clean, stable, and free of trip, fall, and collapse hazards before requesting official inspection.

E. Preparation — Documentation and Scope Confirmation

  1. Obtain the jurisdictional inspection checklist and approved foundation documents. Confirm the checklist and documents are current before beginning the review.
  2. Confirm the inspection stage and identify work that must remain visible. Record which completed work cannot be concealed before official inspection.
  3. Verify permit documents, approved plans, revisions, engineering, soil reports, and prior approvals are available. Resolve any missing or outdated document before proceeding.

F. Layout and Excavation Verification

  1. Inspect building location, setbacks, orientation, dimensions, and diagonals. Compare each measured value against the approved plan.
  2. Verify footing lines, widths, depths, steps, pads, and bearing surfaces. Confirm each value against the approved plan at every corner, step, and pad.
  3. Verify benchmarks, bottom elevations, and required design depths. Confirm elevations at intervals sufficient to detect any deviation along the footing system.

G. Excavation Condition and Embedded Work

  1. Inspect excavation access, protective systems, spoil placement, barricades, and housekeeping. Confirm each meets the safety plan established in Project 5.
  2. Check for water, loose soil, mud, overexcavation, soft spots, and disturbed bearing material. Record every condition requiring correction before the bearing surface is accepted.
  3. Verify sleeves, utility crossings, drainage provisions, and embedded items scheduled for this stage. Confirm each remains visible and matches the approved detail.
  4. Confirm layout strings and reference points are readable and protected. Restore any disturbed reference before continuing.

H. Documentation and Deficiency Management

  1. Prepare measurement records, photographs, test reports, and correction documentation. Assemble records sufficient to support every verified item.
  2. Create a deficiency list organized by safety, layout, excavation, grade, documentation, and access. Record every condition that does not meet the approved plan, checklist, or acceptance standard.
  3. Correct all deficiencies within the authorized scope. Complete each correction and update the deficiency list.
  4. Escalate engineering, survey, soil, structural, or code conflicts. Refer any condition outside this project's authorized scope to the responsible professional or authority before continuing.

I. Final Reinspection and Handoff Preparation

  1. Reinspect every corrected item. Confirm each correction meets the acceptance standard before closing it on the deficiency list.
  2. Provide clean and safe inspector access. Confirm access routes, housekeeping, and protective systems are ready for the official inspector.
  3. Mark items requiring explanation and prepare controlling documentation. Identify any condition the official inspector may question and assemble the supporting record.
  4. Complete and sign the internal pre-inspection record. Confirm every checklist item, correction, and reinspection result is documented before requesting official inspection.

Common Site Inspection

Inspect the completed pre-inspection package using the same checks regardless of building size or number of deficiencies found.

  • Documentation check: confirm the jurisdictional checklist, approved plans, engineering, and soil reports are current and complete.
  • Layout check: confirm building location, setbacks, dimensions, and diagonals match the approved plan.
  • Excavation check: confirm footing widths, depths, bearing surfaces, and elevations match the approved plan and design depth.
  • Safety and housekeeping check: confirm excavation access, protective systems, spoil placement, and barricades meet the safety plan.
  • Embedded-item check: confirm sleeves, crossings, and drainage provisions match the approved detail and remain visible.
  • Deficiency-resolution check: confirm every deficiency is corrected and reinspected, or escalated with a documented determination.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Measured Dimension Does Not Match the Approved Plan

Probable cause: the layout shifted after Project 3 verification, or the excavation was hand-trimmed beyond the approved footing line.
Confirmation: remeasure from the protected control points and compare against the recorded Project 3 layout values.
Correction: reestablish the correct line from the protected control point and correct the excavation or reset the affected stake or string.
Reinspection: remeasure the corrected dimension and diagonal before closing the deficiency.

Bearing Surface Shows Water, Loose Soil, or Disturbance

Probable cause: rainfall entered the excavation, spoil sloughed from the trench edge, or equipment traffic disturbed the bottom after Project 5 verification.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area and compare its current condition against the Project 5 acceptance standard.
Correction: remove water, loose soil, or disturbed material and expose sound bearing material to the required depth.
Reinspection: reverify the bearing surface condition at the corrected location before closing the deficiency.

Benchmark or Bottom Elevation Does Not Match the Design Depth

Probable cause: the benchmark was disturbed, or the excavation was not rechecked against the design depth after subsequent site work.
Confirmation: reread the benchmark elevation and remeasure the footing bottom against the approved design depth.
Correction: reestablish the benchmark from the original approved reference, or correct the excavation depth to match the design requirement.
Reinspection: reverify the elevation reading before closing the deficiency.

Required Sleeve, Crossing, or Embedded Item Is Missing or Concealed

Probable cause: the item was not installed as scheduled, or it was covered by subsequent grading or drainage work before this review.
Confirmation: compare the approved detail against the current condition and determine whether the item is missing or simply concealed.
Correction: install the missing item per the approved detail, or expose the concealed item so it remains visible for inspection.
Reinspection: reverify the item's presence, orientation, and visibility before closing the deficiency.

Deficiency Requires an Engineering or Code Determination

Probable cause: a soil condition, structural conflict, or code question exceeds this project's authorized scope to resolve independently.
Confirmation: document the condition with measurements and photographs and compare it against the approved plan, engineering, and soil report.
Correction: escalate to the responsible engineer, surveyor, geotechnical professional, or code authority and obtain a documented determination before proceeding.
Reinspection: verify the site against the determination once received and close the deficiency accordingly.

Inspector Access Is Unsafe or Obstructed

Probable cause: housekeeping, barricades, or the temporary access route were not reviewed as part of this project's scope.
Confirmation: walk the access route and compare its condition against the safety plan and Project 15 acceptance standard.
Correction: clean the access route, repair or reinstall barricades, and restore the access route condition established in Project 15.
Reinspection: walk the corrected access route and confirm it is clean and safe before requesting official inspection.

Cleanup

This project ends with a signed internal pre-inspection record and a site ready for official inspection, not the official inspection itself.

  • Confirm all excavation, footing, sleeve, crossing, and embedded work that must remain visible is protected from concealment
  • Confirm the deficiency list shows every item corrected and reinspected or formally escalated
  • Confirm inspector access remains clean, stable, and safe until the official inspection occurs
  • Remove tools, materials, and debris from the excavation and access route that are not required for the official inspection
  • File the completed internal pre-inspection record, measurement records, photographs, and correction documentation with the Project 01 through 15 records
