Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to cut, place, tie, support, and inspect the approved reinforcement system for one exterior slab. The finished work product is a correctly sized, lapped, tied, supported reinforcement system at the approved elevation and cover, photographed while still visible and ready for the required pre-pour inspection.

You will review the reinforcement drawings and material specification, confirm the forms, base, blockouts, penetrations, and isolation materials are complete, inspect the reinforcement itself, cut it using approved tools and eye protection, place it in the approved orientation, maintain edge and opening clearance, lap and tie it as specified, install chairs or other supports, install dowels and embeds where specified, and check elevation and cover before obtaining the required pre-pour inspection.

This is the eighth chapter in Book 16. It depends directly on the completed form system from Chapter 6 and the finished elevations and slope from Chapter 7, and it produces the concealed reinforcement condition that Chapter 9 and Chapter 10 must not disturb once concrete placement begins. Nothing in this chapter invents a bar size, mesh spacing, lap length, cover dimension, or support schedule that the approved reinforcement drawings and material specification do not already supply.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FW-043 — Select reinforcement type according to the approved reinforcement drawings and material specification.
  • FW-044 — Cut welded-wire mesh to the approved dimensions using approved tools and eye protection.
  • FW-045 — Place rebar where required, in the approved orientation, lap, and spacing.
  • FW-046 — Support reinforcement at the specified elevation using chairs, bolsters, or dobies compatible with the base.
  • FW-047 — Maintain specified concrete cover from soil, forms, surfaces, edges, and openings.
  • FW-048 — Inspect reinforcement placement and obtain the required pre-pour inspection before concrete conceals it.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter cannot begin until Chapter 6 has produced a rigid, square, aligned form system and Chapter 7 has set and verified the finished elevations and drainage slope. The reinforcement placed here must sit at the elevation and cover Chapter 7 established, not at an elevation the learner selects independently.

The reinforcement system completed and inspected here is what Chapter 9's mixed concrete and Chapter 10's placement, screeding, and bull floating will conceal. A displaced, unsupported, wrong-size, short-lap, or contaminated reinforcement condition accepted here becomes a defect that cannot be corrected once concrete placement begins. The same photograph-before-concealment discipline used here is required again in Chapter 13 through Chapter 16 and in the Chapter 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing reinforcement drawings, material, bar size, mesh type, spacing, laps, supports, cover, and edge conditions
  • inspecting reinforcement for damage, contamination, and incorrect size before placement
  • cutting welded-wire reinforcement or rebar using approved tools and eye protection
  • placing reinforcement in the approved orientation with the specified clearance, lap, and tie pattern
  • installing chairs, bolsters, dobies, dowels, sleeves, anchors, or thickened-edge reinforcement where specified
  • checking reinforcement elevation, cover, and joint or drain interference
  • photographing concealed reinforcement and obtaining the required pre-pour inspection

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • invent a bar size, mesh spacing, lap length, cover dimension, tie pattern, or support schedule that is not supplied by the approved reinforcement drawings, material specification, or manufacturer instructions
  • use loose stones, wood scraps, broken masonry, or other unapproved material as reinforcement supports
  • leave reinforcement lying on the base and attempt to pull it upward during placement unless the approved method explicitly permits it
  • place concrete before the required pre-pour inspection is obtained

Any reinforcement condition that is displaced, unsupported, wrong-size, short-lapped, or contaminated must be corrected and reinspected, not concealed by proceeding to concrete placement.

Trade Note

Reinforcement that looks approximately correct from a standing position is not the same as reinforcement verified at the specified elevation with a tape measure or cover gauge. Photograph every concealed reinforcement condition before concrete placement begins; it is the only record available once the pour is complete.

Setup Standard

Do not begin cutting or placing reinforcement until the forms, base, blockouts, penetrations, and isolation materials from Chapter 6 and Chapter 7 are confirmed complete. Reinforcement placed against an incomplete form or base condition must be removed and reset once the prerequisite work is corrected.

Shop Tip

Stage reinforcement with capped exposed ends before placement begins, and control the spring-back of welded-wire sheets and rolls as they are unrolled or unfolded. Reinforcement that springs back unexpectedly during placement is a common cause of displaced supports and inconsistent cover.

Common Field Fundamentals

Reinforcement placement is a concealment point: everything installed here becomes invisible once concrete placement begins in Chapter 10. Treating this chapter as a system of size, position, support, and cover — rather than as loose wire or bar set on top of the base — is what keeps the finished slab performing as designed.

Position, Cover, and Support as One System

Reinforcement that is the correct size but sitting on the base with no support will not be at the specified elevation once concrete is placed around it. Reinforcement that is correctly supported but has inadequate cover at an edge or opening does not meet the approved specification either. Every reinforcement check in this chapter must confirm size, position, support, and cover together, not any one factor in isolation.

Measurement Cue — Verify Cover With a Gauge, Not by Eye

Check concrete cover using a cover gauge, spacer block, or tape measure at multiple points across the placed reinforcement, not by visual estimate. Cover that looks adequate from a standing position frequently measures short at edges, corners, and around embedded items.

Critical Safety Cue — Cap Exposed Vertical Bar Ends

Install approved end caps or equivalent capping material on exposed vertical rebar ends before placement work continues around them. Uncapped exposed ends are an impalement hazard to every worker moving through the reinforcement area.

Field Safety Cue — Control Mesh Spring-Back

Welded-wire reinforcement sheets and rolls can spring back forcefully when unrolled or unfolded. Control the material with both hands and keep other workers clear while flattening or repositioning mesh.

Code Alert — Pre-Pour Inspection Requirements

Verify whether the adopted code or permit conditions require a pre-pour inspection of reinforcement, embeds, and joint materials before concrete placement. Do not place concrete over reinforcement that requires inspection until that inspection has been completed and approved.

FW-043 – FW-048

Reinforcement Installation Qualification

Exterior Flatwork Reinforcement Installation

Difficulty: Beginner to Intermediate

Estimated Time: 2–5 hours

Estimated Cost: Varies with reinforcement type and quantity specified

Finished Work: One correctly sized, lapped, tied, supported reinforcement system, inspected and photographed

A. Finished-Project Specification

Scope: one exterior slab reinforcement system, cut, placed, tied, supported, and inspected according to the approved reinforcement drawings, material specification, bar size, mesh type, spacing, laps, supports, cover, and edge conditions, ready for the required pre-pour inspection.

B. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review reinforcement drawings, material, bar size, mesh type, spacing, laps, supports, cover, and edge conditions. Confirm the drawings apply to this exact project and location.
  2. Confirm forms, base, blockouts, penetrations, and isolation materials are complete. Do not begin cutting or placing reinforcement until this prerequisite work is verified.
  3. Inspect reinforcement for heavy rust scale, oil, mud, damage, and wrong size. Reject and replace any material that fails this inspection.
  4. Stage reinforcement safely with capped exposed ends where required. Keep staged material clear of the access route and finishing path.
  5. Cut welded-wire reinforcement or rebar using approved tools and eye protection. Cut to the dimensions specified by the approved reinforcement drawings.
  6. Control spring-back of welded-wire sheets and rolls. Use both hands and keep other workers clear while flattening or repositioning mesh.
  7. Place reinforcement according to the approved orientation. Confirm orientation against the reinforcement drawings before proceeding to the next step.
  8. Maintain specified edge and opening clearance. Measure clearance at every edge, corner, and opening rather than at a single point.
  9. Lap sheets and bars by the approved amount. Measure each lap; do not estimate lap length by eye.
  10. Tie intersections and laps as specified. Use the tie pattern and tie-wire method specified by the approved reinforcement drawings.
  11. Install chairs, bolsters, dobies, or other supports compatible with the base. Confirm the support type is compatible with the base material before installation.
  12. Space supports closely enough to prevent sagging or displacement. Use the support spacing specified by the approved reinforcement drawings.
  13. Do not use loose stones, wood scraps, broken masonry, or unapproved material as supports. Replace any nonconforming support found during inspection.
  14. Install dowels, sleeves, anchors, thickened-edge reinforcement, or embeds where specified. Confirm each embed location against the approved drawings.
  15. Isolate reinforcement from prohibited contact with soil, forms, or incompatible metals. Correct any contact found during inspection.
  16. Walk only on approved access boards or controlled paths. Do not walk directly on placed reinforcement.
  17. Check reinforcement elevation at multiple points. Use a tape measure or approved gauge referenced to the finished-elevation control from Chapter 7.
  18. Verify concrete cover. Check cover at edges, corners, openings, and embedded items using a cover gauge or spacer reference.
  19. Confirm reinforcement will not interfere with joints, drains, or screeding. Compare reinforcement position against the joint plan and drain locations.
  20. Photograph concealed reinforcement. Photograph the complete system before concrete placement begins.
  21. Obtain required pre-pour inspection. Do not proceed to Chapter 9 or Chapter 10 concrete work until this inspection is completed and approved where required.
  22. Correct displaced, unsupported, wrong-size, short-lap, or contaminated reinforcement. Correct and reinspect before concrete placement.

D. Documentation and Handoff

  • Reinforcement worksheet: the completed record of every check in Steps 1–22, including elevation and cover readings.
  • Pre-pour inspection record: confirmation of the required inspection and its result, retained for reference through Chapter 9 and Chapter 10.
  • Photographs: the complete concealed reinforcement system, referenced in the worksheet before concrete placement begins.
  • Correction log: any displaced, unsupported, wrong-size, short-lap, or contaminated reinforcement identified, corrected, and reinspected.

Common Reinforcement Inspection

Inspect every reinforcement system using the same checks regardless of bar type or mesh type.

  • Material check: confirm reinforcement is undamaged, uncontaminated, and the correct size before placement.
  • Orientation check: confirm placement matches the approved reinforcement drawings.
  • Clearance check: confirm edge and opening clearance is measured and correct.
  • Lap and tie check: confirm laps are measured and intersections tied as specified.
  • Support check: confirm chairs, bolsters, or dobies are compatible, correctly spaced, and free of unapproved substitutes.
  • Elevation and cover check: confirm elevation and cover are measured, not estimated.
  • Inspection check: confirm the reinforcement is photographed and the pre-pour inspection is obtained before concealment.

Complete the Reinforcement Installation Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 9.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Reinforcement Sagging Between Supports

Probable cause: chairs, bolsters, or dobies were spaced too far apart, or an unapproved support material was used and failed under the reinforcement's own weight.
Confirmation: measure support spacing against the approved reinforcement drawings and inspect the support material for compatibility.
Correction: add supports at the specified spacing and remove any unapproved support material.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation check at multiple points before concrete placement.

Cover Measured Short at an Edge or Opening

Probable cause: reinforcement was positioned by eye rather than measured against the specified clearance at every edge, corner, and opening.
Confirmation: measure cover with a cover gauge or spacer at multiple points along the affected edge or opening.
Correction: reposition the reinforcement or adjust supports until cover meets the specified minimum at every measured point.
Reinspection: repeat the cover check across the full reinforcement system before concrete placement.

Lap Length Short at a Sheet or Bar Joint

Probable cause: the lap was estimated visually rather than measured against the approved lap-length specification.
Confirmation: measure the lap at the affected joint with a tape measure.
Correction: reposition the sheet or bar to achieve the specified lap length and retie the joint.
Reinspection: repeat the lap check at every joint before concrete placement.

Reinforcement Displaced by Foot Traffic

Probable cause: workers walked directly on placed reinforcement instead of using approved access boards or controlled paths.
Confirmation: inspect the reinforcement for displaced ties, shifted supports, or altered elevation compared to the worksheet record.
Correction: reposition and retie the displaced reinforcement, reset supports, and install access boards before further work continues.
Reinspection: repeat the orientation, elevation, and cover checks before concrete placement.

Embed or Dowel Location Does Not Match the Reinforcement Drawings

Probable cause: a dowel, sleeve, anchor, or thickened-edge detail was installed from memory or estimate rather than measured against the approved drawings.
Confirmation: compare the installed embed location directly against the reinforcement drawings using the same reference points established for the project.
Correction: reposition the embed to the specified location and reverify surrounding reinforcement cover and clearance.
Reinspection: repeat the embed and cover checks before concrete placement.

Pre-Pour Inspection Skipped Before Concrete Placement

Probable cause: concrete placement was scheduled or begun before the required pre-pour inspection was requested and completed.
Confirmation: review the pre-pour inspection record for a confirmed request and approved result before any concrete work in Chapter 9 or Chapter 10 began.
Correction: stop concrete work, request the pre-pour inspection, and do not resume placement until it is completed and approved.
Reinspection: repeat the inspection check once the pre-pour inspection is confirmed.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a fully documented, inspected reinforcement system, not a verbal impression of the placement.

  • Remove cutting waste, tie-wire scraps, and packaging from the work area without disturbing placed reinforcement
  • Confirm access boards remain in place for any remaining pre-pour activity
  • Store unused reinforcement, chairs, and tie wire away from the placement area for later projects
  • File the completed reinforcement worksheet, pre-pour inspection record, and photographs together
  • Store photographs with clear labels referencing the worksheet entries they support

Performance Check

With the reinforcement installation worksheet fully recorded, review it against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the reinforcement material was inspected before placement, orientation matches the approved drawings, edge and opening clearance was measured, laps and ties meet the specified pattern, supports are compatible and correctly spaced, elevation and cover were verified by measurement rather than estimate, embeds match the approved drawings, and the reinforcement is isolated from prohibited contact. Project Closeout

You have completed the eighth chapter in the concrete flatwork sequence: installing reinforcement for exterior flatwork. This chapter required you to select the approved reinforcement type (FW-043), cut welded-wire mesh using approved tools and eye protection (FW-044), place rebar where required in the approved orientation, lap, and spacing (FW-045), support reinforcement at the specified elevation (FW-046), maintain specified concrete cover (FW-047), and inspect and photograph the placement before obtaining the required pre-pour inspection (FW-048).

You now have a correctly sized, lapped, tied, supported reinforcement system at the approved elevation and cover, inspected and ready for concrete placement. That system carries directly into the next chapter: calculating, batching, mixing, staging, and inspecting bagged concrete for the placement that will conceal the reinforcement you have just installed.

File your completed reinforcement worksheet, pre-pour inspection record, and photographs together. You will reference these exact findings if any reinforcement-related question arises later in this book.
