Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to build one garage- or shed-slab practice section: a complete demonstration of subgrade preparation, aggregate base, forming, vapor retarder handling where specified, reinforcement, concrete placement, screeding, finishing, flatness verification, and control jointing. The finished work product is a cured slab practice section, fully documented, with its nonstructural training status clearly stated wherever it has not been separately engineered.

You will review the approved practice-section dimensions, loading, edge detail, reinforcement, vapor control, finish, joints, and drainage; excavate and prepare the subgrade; install and compact aggregate base; install a vapor retarder only where specified; build forms or thickened-edge details; set elevation and slope or level requirements; install reinforcement and supports; place, screed, and bull-float concrete; time hand or power finishing; install control joints; cure and protect the slab; check flatness; and document that full structural-slab design remains outside the practice section unless separately approved.

This chapter draws directly on the excavation and compaction skills from Chapter 4, the aggregate-base skills from Chapter 5, the form-building skills from Chapter 6, the elevation and slope skills from Chapter 7, the reinforcement skills from Chapter 8, the mixing skills from Chapter 9, the placement and finishing skills from Chapters 10 and 11, and the curing skills from Chapter 12. It applies all of them together on a single section, and it prepares you for the joint-focused work in Chapter 17 and the capstone integration in Chapter 20.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FW-091 — Prepare the slab base, including subgrade excavation, compaction, and aggregate base placement to the approved thickness and elevation.
  • FW-092 — Set slab forms, including edge details, elevation, slope or level requirements, and bracing appropriate to the practice-section specification.
  • FW-093 — Install reinforcement, including supports, cover, and any specified sleeves, anchor templates, or blockouts.
  • FW-094 — Place and screed concrete to the approved plane, consolidating edges and correcting low and high areas before finishing begins.
  • FW-095 — Finish the slab surface to the specified texture and install tooled or saw-cut control joints within the approved timing window.
  • FW-096 — Inspect flatness and control joints, and document that full structural-slab design remains outside the practice section unless separately approved.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter integrates the base, form, reinforcement, placement, and finishing skills built across Chapters 4 through 12 into a single slab section that mimics a garage or shed floor without being presented as one. Chapter 13's utility pad and Chapter 14's sidewalk section each demonstrated pieces of this same sequence for a specific finished use; this chapter demonstrates the same sequence at slab scale while holding the line on what a nonstructural practice section may and may not represent.

Chapter 17 will return to this section's joints in more depth, and the flatness and joint records produced here carry forward into the Chapter 20 capstone's own inspection sequence. Nothing in this chapter authorizes substituting the practice detail for an engineered garage or building slab.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved practice-section dimensions, loading, edge detail, reinforcement, vapor control, finish, joints, and drainage
  • excavating and compacting the subgrade and installing compacted aggregate base
  • installing a vapor retarder only where the specification requires one, protected from puncture
  • building and bracing forms or thickened-edge practice details
  • setting finished elevation and the specified slope or level requirement
  • installing reinforcement, supports, sleeves, anchor templates, or blockouts as specified
  • placing, consolidating, screeding, and bull-floating concrete
  • timing hand or power finishing to the specified surface without over-troweling
  • installing tooled or saw-cut control joints, curing, and checking flatness

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • present this practice section as a substitute for an engineered garage or building slab
  • invent slab thickness, edge thickness, reinforcement schedule, concrete strength, vapor-retarder specification, joint spacing, joint depth, curing period, or flatness tolerance not supplied by the approved practice-section specification, adopted code, or manufacturer instructions
  • place concrete before the pre-pour inspection confirms base, forms, vapor retarder, reinforcement, and embedded items are complete
  • over-trowel an exterior or slip-sensitive surface, or apply a finish inconsistent with the specified surface
  • skip the documentation step stating that full structural-slab design remains outside the practice section unless separately approved

Any condition suggesting this section is intended to bear a structure, vehicle loading, or other engineered use must be referred to a qualified design professional rather than resolved by extending the practice-section procedure.

Trade Note

A slab practice section teaches the same base, form, reinforcement, placement, and finishing sequence a real garage or shed slab requires, but it is not, by itself, evidence of structural adequacy. State the nonstructural status of this section clearly in every document produced in this chapter, not only in the closeout record.

Setup Standard

Do not place concrete until the pre-pour inspection confirms subgrade, aggregate base, vapor retarder where specified, forms, reinforcement, supports, and embedded items are complete and match the approved practice-section specification. A pour that begins before this inspection is complete must be treated as unauthorized.

Shop Tip

Check flatness with a straightedge or the approved method immediately after finishing, while the surface is still workable enough to correct minor high and low areas. Waiting until the slab has cured removes your ability to correct anything found.

Common Field Fundamentals

A garage or shed slab practice section combines every system taught earlier in this book — subgrade, base, forms, elevation, reinforcement, concrete, finish, joints, and cure — into one section built at slab scale. Treating any one of these as optional, or treating the finished section as ready for vehicle or structural loading without separate engineering, undermines the purpose of the exercise.

Nonstructural Scope as a Continuous Discipline

State the nonstructural status of this practice section at the start of work, restate it at the pre-pour inspection, and restate it again at final documentation. This is not a one-time disclaimer. A practice section that quietly becomes "the shed floor" without separate engineering review has exceeded this chapter's scope.

Code Alert — Practice Section Is Not an Engineered Slab

Do not use this practice detail as a substitute for an engineered garage or building slab. If the finished section is intended to support an actual garage, shed, vehicle loading, or other structure, refer the project for structural design, permit, and inspection before any loading is applied.

Critical Safety Cue — Wet Concrete and Reinforcement Handling

Wear waterproof gloves, boots, and eye protection throughout mixing, placement, and finishing. Wear eye protection while cutting welded-wire reinforcement or rebar and control spring-back of cut sheets and rolls. Do not kneel directly in fresh concrete without approved protection.

Measurement Cue — One Elevation Reference Through Placement

Use the same elevation reference established during layout for every base, form, reinforcement, and finished-surface elevation check in this chapter. Verify base and form elevations again immediately before the pre-pour inspection.

Field Safety Cue — Screed and Finishing-Stage Footing

Wet aggregate base, freshly placed concrete, and standing water at low points create slip hazards during screeding and finishing. Keep footing controlled and avoid walking directly on reinforcement or the fresh concrete plane except from approved access boards.

A. Finished-Project Specification

Scope: one garage- or shed-slab practice section built from the approved practice-section dimensions, loading, edge detail, reinforcement, vapor control, finish, and joint specification, demonstrating base preparation, forming, reinforcement, placement, finishing, flatness, and jointing, and documented as nonstructural training work unless separately engineered.

B. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review the approved practice-section dimensions, loading, edge detail, reinforcement, vapor control, finish, joints, and drainage. Confirm the specification is current and complete before beginning.
  2. State clearly whether the section is nonstructural training work or part of an engineered slab. Record this determination on the practice-section worksheet before excavation begins.
  3. Do not use the practice detail as a substitute for an engineered garage or building slab. Refer any intended structural or vehicle-loading use for separate design review.
  4. Excavate and compact the subgrade. Follow the Chapter 4 excavation and compaction sequence to the approved depth and slope.
  5. Install and compact aggregate base. Follow the Chapter 5 sequence to the specified thickness, lift count, and elevation.
  6. Install vapor retarder only where specified and protect it from puncture. Lap and seal according to the manufacturer instructions where a vapor retarder is required.
  7. Build and brace forms or thickened-edge practice details. Use the approved edge detail rather than an improvised form condition.
  8. Set finished elevation and slope or level requirement. Transfer elevations from the single project reference established during layout.
  9. Install reinforcement and supports. Place reinforcement at the approved elevation and cover using chairs, bolsters, or other approved supports.
  10. Install sleeves, anchor templates, or blockouts where specified. Verify locations against the approved practice-section specification before concrete conceals them.
  11. Complete pre-pour inspection. Confirm subgrade, base, vapor retarder, forms, reinforcement, and embedded items match the specification.
  12. Calculate concrete quantity and placement sequence. Use only figures supplied by the approved specification or documented site measurements.
  13. Place and consolidate concrete. Follow the Chapter 10 placement sequence, consolidating along forms, corners, and embedded items.
  14. Screed to approved rails or forms. Strike concrete to the finished elevation and correct low and high areas before bull-floating.
  15. Bull float the surface. Embed aggregate and close the surface without overworking it.
  16. Time hand or power finishing according to the specified surface. Wait for bleed water to leave the surface before finishing begins.
  17. Do not over-trowel exterior or slip-sensitive surfaces. Match the finishing method to the specified texture, not to a smoother unspecified alternative.
  18. Install tooled or saw-cut control joints. Follow the approved joint plan for spacing, depth, and timing.
  19. Begin curing and protect from traffic. Start curing as soon as the finish permits and barricade the section for the approved no-traffic period.
  20. Check surface flatness using the approved method. Use a straightedge or the specified flatness-checking method and compare results to the specified tolerance.
  21. Inspect edge thickness, reinforcement record, finish, joints, cracks, and elevation. Compare every result against the approved practice-section specification.
  22. Document that full structural-slab design remains outside the practice section unless separately approved. Restate this status clearly on the closeout record.

D. Documentation and Handoff

  • Practice-section worksheet: the completed record of every finding in Steps 1–22, including the structural-status statement and final acceptance.
  • Reinforcement and embed photographs: images taken before concrete placement conceals reinforcement, supports, and embedded items.

  • Flatness and joint inspection record: straightedge or specified-method readings and joint location, depth, and timing notes.

Common Site-Readiness Inspection

Inspect this practice section using the same checks regardless of whether it is built as a shed-scale or garage-scale section.

  • Status check: confirm the nonstructural or separately engineered status is recorded before excavation and restated at closeout.
  • Base check: confirm subgrade and aggregate base meet the approved elevation, slope, and thickness before forms are accepted.
  • Form and edge check: confirm forms or thickened-edge details are set to approved elevation, slope or level, and edge detail, and are adequately braced.
  • Reinforcement check: confirm reinforcement, supports, and embedded items are placed, photographed, and inspected before concrete placement.
  • Placement and finish check: confirm concrete is consolidated, screeded, bull-floated, and finished to the specified texture without over-troweling.
  • Joint and flatness check: confirm control joints are installed within the approved timing window and that flatness meets the specified tolerance.
  • Curing check: confirm curing begins as soon as the finish permits and the no-traffic period is observed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Practice Section Treated as an Engineered Slab

Probable cause: the nonstructural-status statement was skipped or the section was described in project records as a finished garage or shed floor without separate engineering review.
Confirmation: review the practice-section worksheet for the required structural-status statement at both the start and closeout of the project.
Correction: add the missing statement, and refer the project for structural design review before any loading beyond foot traffic is applied.
Reinspection: repeat the status check before filing the closeout record.

Base Elevation or Thickness Out of Tolerance

Probable cause: aggregate base was placed in a single uncompacted lift or checked at too few locations before forms were accepted.
Confirmation: re-check base elevation and thickness at a grid of locations against the approved specification.
Correction: remove and replace deficient base material in properly compacted lifts before proceeding to forms.
Reinspection: repeat the base check before the pre-pour inspection.

Reinforcement Displaced During Placement

Probable cause: reinforcement was placed on the base without adequate chairs or supports, or workers walked directly on unsupported reinforcement during placement.
Confirmation: compare reinforcement photographs taken before placement to its position after concrete is struck off.
Correction: where accessible, lift and support reinforcement to the specified elevation before the concrete sets; document any area where correction was not possible.
Reinspection: repeat the reinforcement-cover check on the corrected area.

Surface Over-Troweled for an Exterior or Slip-Sensitive Use

Probable cause: steel troweling was applied to a surface specified for a broom or other slip-resistant texture.
Confirmation: compare the finished texture to the specified finish in the practice-section specification.
Correction: where the surface remains workable, apply the specified texture; where the concrete has set, document the deviation and evaluate whether an approved corrective texture can be applied.
Reinspection: repeat the finish check once corrected.

Control Joints Cut or Tooled Outside the Timing Window

Probable cause: joints were tooled or saw-cut before the concrete reached sufficient strength, causing raveling, or after random cracking had already begun.
Confirmation: inspect joint edges for raveling and inspect the surrounding slab for uncontrolled cracks.
Correction: document any raveled joint or uncontrolled crack; do not attempt to re-cut a joint that has already raveled without evaluating repair options from Chapter 18's scope.
Reinspection: repeat the joint check before curing is considered complete.

Flatness Out of Tolerance After Finishing

Probable cause: screeding left high or low areas that were not corrected before bull-floating and finishing proceeded.
Confirmation: check flatness with a straightedge or the specified method at multiple locations across the section.
Correction: where the concrete remains workable, correct high and low areas and refinish; where the concrete has set, document the deviation and evaluate whether it requires referral.
Reinspection: repeat the flatness check across the full section.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a cured, inspected, documented practice section, not an unmarked slab whose status will be forgotten later.

  • Clean placement, finishing, and jointing tools in the contained washout area before concrete sets
  • Remove excess form material, cut reinforcement scraps, and joint-cutting slurry from the work area
  • Store curing materials, barricades, and no-traffic signage in place for the full approved curing period
  • File the completed practice-section worksheet, reinforcement photographs, batch log, and flatness and joint inspection record together
  • Confirm the structural-status statement is visible on every retained document, not only the closeout record

Field Terms

Practice-Section Specification
The approved dimensions, loading, edge detail, reinforcement, vapor control, finish, joints, and drainage requirements controlling this section.
Nonstructural Training Work
Work built to demonstrate technique and procedure, not certified or designed to bear a structure or defined structural loading.
Thickened-Edge Detail
An edge condition with additional depth specified in place of a conventional form, used only where the approved detail calls for it.
Vapor Retarder
A sheet material installed beneath a slab only where specified, protected from puncture through placement.
Finished Elevation
The top-of-slab elevation established from the project's single elevation reference.
Flatness Tolerance
The maximum permitted deviation from a true plane, checked with a straightedge or the specified method.
Control Joint
A tooled or saw-cut joint intended to control the location of shrinkage cracking.
Timing Window
The period during which a joint must be tooled or saw-cut to avoid raveling or uncontrolled cracking.
Pre-Pour Inspection
The confirmation that subgrade, base, vapor retarder, forms, reinforcement, and embedded items match the specification before concrete is placed.
Structural-Status Statement
The documented declaration of whether a slab is nonstructural training work or part of a separately engineered design.
