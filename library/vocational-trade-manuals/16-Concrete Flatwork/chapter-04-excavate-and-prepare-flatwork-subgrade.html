Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to excavate and compact one small flatwork area to the approved subgrade elevation, slope, and support condition. The finished work product is a clean, stable, compacted subgrade with unsuitable material removed, verified elevation and slope at a grid of locations, and a documented excavation and compaction record.

You will confirm the approved layout and excavation depth from Chapter 3, establish excavation limits, strip vegetation and organic material, excavate in controlled passes while protecting foundations and utilities, remove unsuitable soil, shape and compact the subgrade to the approved slope, verify elevation and drainage at a grid of locations, and correct any soft, high, low, loose, or unstable areas before documenting final acceptance.

This chapter depends directly on the layout, excavation limits, and finished-elevation references established in Chapter 3. It does not repeat or revise those references; it excavates to them. The compacted subgrade produced here becomes the surface Chapter 5 places and compacts aggregate base on. Nothing in this chapter invents an excavation depth, compaction target, or slope that Chapter 3's layout and the approved site plan do not already supply.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 1 identified the soil, drainage, and access conditions for this project. Chapter 3 established the layout, excavation limits, and finished-elevation references. This chapter excavates and compacts the subgrade those chapters point to. Chapter 5 cannot place and compact an aggregate base until this chapter has produced an accepted subgrade at the correct elevation, slope, and support condition. Chapter 6 cannot rely on a stable form-staking surface until this chapter's compaction is complete and documented.

The stop-work discipline carried from Chapter 1 continues here: unidentified utilities, unstable excavation edges, contaminated soil, buried tanks, structural foundations, severe erosion, groundwater, unsuitable fill, and expansive soil all require the learner to stop and refer the project rather than excavate through the condition.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • verifying the approved layout, excavation depth, utility markings, and spoil location before excavation begins
  • establishing machine and hand-excavation limits and barricading the work area
  • stripping vegetation, sod, roots, topsoil, and organic material
  • excavating in controlled passes while maintaining required separation from foundations, utilities, trees, and property lines
  • identifying and removing unsuitable material to the approved limit
  • shaping and compacting the subgrade to the required slope
  • verifying elevation, slope, and drainage direction at a grid of locations
  • performing acceptance testing where specified and documenting final acceptance

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • excavate, hand-expose utilities, or drive equipment without confirmed, valid utility-locate markings still visible from Chapter 1 and Chapter 2
  • invent an excavation depth, compaction target, moisture-conditioning method, slope, or elevation that is not supplied by the approved site plan, Chapter 3 layout, or project specification
  • undermine footings, slabs, retaining systems, posts, or adjacent paving to reach a planned depth
  • continue excavation past unidentified utilities, unstable excavation edges, contaminated soil, buried tanks, structural foundations, severe erosion, groundwater, unsuitable fill, or expansive soil
  • accept a subgrade without checking elevation, slope, and compaction at a documented grid of locations

Any condition that requires engineering, geotechnical, or professional review must be documented and referred, not resolved by the learner.

Trade Note

The subgrade elevation and slope recorded in this chapter carry forward without modification into Chapter 5's aggregate base and Chapter 7's finished-elevation verification. Recording an estimated compaction result, or accepting a subgrade without grid verification, is the single most common source of the base-thickness and slope errors diagnosed in later chapters.

Setup Standard

Do not begin excavation until the Chapter 1 utility-locate markings remain visible and valid, the Chapter 3 layout and excavation limits are confirmed, and the work area is barricaded. Hand-expose utilities before machine excavation reaches any location where the utility-locate plan requires it.

Shop Tip

Work excavation in controlled passes rather than digging to full depth in one pass. Shallow, controlled passes make it easier to recognize a soft spot, root mass, or buried obstruction before it becomes a stop-work condition discovered too late.

Common Field Fundamentals

A subgrade is not simply "dirt dug to depth." It is the load-support layer for everything placed above it: aggregate base, forms, reinforcement, and concrete. A subgrade that is at the correct depth but left loose, contaminated with organic material, or out of slope will telegraph those defects into every later chapter, regardless of how carefully the base and concrete are handled afterward.

Excavation, Support, and Slope as One System

Excavation depth, soil suitability, and compacted slope must be evaluated together. A subgrade excavated to the exact planned depth but left with a soft spot underneath will still fail under load. A subgrade compacted firmly but out of slope will still trap water beneath the aggregate base. Every finding recorded in this chapter must be checked against how it will affect the aggregate base in Chapter 5 and the finished elevation verified in Chapter 7.

Critical Safety Cue — Utility and Excavation-Edge Hazards

Hand-expose utilities where required by the utility-locate and excavation plan before machine excavation reaches that location. Do not rely solely on surface markings when exact utility depth or path is uncertain. Keep equipment and personnel clear of unstable excavation edges, and stop work immediately for any unidentified utility, unstable edge, contaminated soil, buried tank, or exposed structural foundation.

Field Safety Cue — Spoil Piles and Equipment Movement

Stockpile spoil away from the excavation edge and clear of drainage paths and access routes. Use spotters and controlled communication around excavation machinery, compactors, and wheelbarrow traffic. Keep footing stable when working near excavation margins.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Compaction Equipment

Operate the plate compactor or rammer according to its manufacturer instructions for the soil type present. Overlap compactor passes as specified by the equipment manufacturer, and do not exceed the lift thickness the equipment is rated to compact effectively.

Measurement Cue — Grid Verification, Not a Single Reading

Check subgrade elevation and slope at a grid of locations across the full excavated area, using the same elevation reference established in Chapter 3. A single center-point reading cannot reveal a localized high spot, low spot, or drainage reversal.

Code Alert — Excavation Depth Near Structures and Property Lines

Maintain the required separation from foundations, utilities, trees, and property lines specified by the approved site plan and adopted code. Excavation that approaches an adjacent footing or structure may require additional shoring, engineering review, or a revised excavation method not covered by this chapter.

Excavation / Subgrade Preparation Qualification

Concrete Flatwork Subgrade Excavation and Preparation

Difficulty: Beginner to Intermediate

Estimated Time: 4–8 hours

Estimated Cost: Equipment cost only

Finished Work: One clean, compacted subgrade at approved elevation and slope, documented

A. Finished-Project Specification

Scope: one small flatwork excavation area, excavated to the approved depth, cleared of unsuitable material, compacted to the approved condition, and verified at a grid of locations for elevation, slope, and drainage direction.

B. Required Project Sequence

  1. Verify approved layout, excavation depth, utility markings, and spoil location. Confirm the Chapter 3 layout and utility-locate markings remain valid before any excavation begins.
  2. Establish machine and hand-excavation limits. Mark the boundary within which machine excavation is authorized and where hand excavation is required instead.
  3. Barricade the work area. Install barricades and signage around the full excavation limit before work begins.
  4. Strip vegetation, sod, roots, topsoil, and organic material. Remove all organic material from within the excavation limits before excavating to depth.
  5. Excavate in controlled passes. Remove soil in shallow, controlled passes rather than digging to full depth in a single pass.
  6. Maintain required separation from foundations, utilities, trees, and property lines. Confirm separation distances against the approved site plan throughout excavation.
  7. Hand expose utilities where required by the utility-locate and excavation plan. Switch to hand excavation at any location the utility-locate plan requires.
  8. Do not undermine footings, slabs, retaining systems, posts, or adjacent paving. Stop and adjust the excavation method if a planned depth would undermine an adjacent structure.
  9. Stockpile or remove spoil without blocking drainage or access. Place spoil clear of the excavation edge, drainage paths, and established access routes from Chapter 2.
  10. Inspect exposed soil for soft spots, pumping, roots, debris, fill, or contamination. Probe and visually inspect the exposed subgrade across its full area.
  11. Remove unsuitable material to the approved limit. Excavate out any organic material, soft spot, debris, or uncontrolled fill identified during inspection.
  12. Stop work when excavation exceeds the planned condition or requires engineering. Document and refer any condition beyond this chapter's scope rather than continuing.
  13. Shape subgrade to the required slope. Grade the exposed subgrade to match the drainage direction established in Chapter 3.
  14. Moisture-condition soil only as specified. Add or allow moisture only according to the approved compaction specification.
  15. Compact using equipment appropriate to soil type and access. Select the plate compactor, rammer, or other equipment matched to the soil type present.
  16. Work from stable edges without overloading excavation margins. Keep compaction equipment clear of unstable excavation edges.
  17. Refill overexcavated areas only with approved material and compacted lifts. Correct any area excavated below the approved elevation using only authorized fill material, compacted in lifts.
  18. Check subgrade elevation at a grid of locations. Measure elevation at multiple points across the excavated area from the Chapter 3 elevation reference.
  19. Check slope and drainage direction. Confirm the grid readings show consistent slope toward the approved discharge path.
  20. Perform proof-rolling, probe, density, or other acceptance testing where specified. Use the acceptance method identified in the project specification.
  21. Correct soft, high, low, loose, or unstable areas. Rework and recompact any area that fails the grid check or acceptance test.
  22. Document excavation depth, soil conditions, corrections, and final acceptance. Complete the subgrade log before authorizing Chapter 5 to begin.

D. Documentation and Handoff

  • Subgrade log: the completed record of excavation depth, soil conditions, corrections, and final acceptance from Steps 1–22.
  • Grid elevation record: the documented elevation and slope readings taken across the subgrade, referenced to the Chapter 3 elevation reference.
  • Acceptance test record: the results of any proof-roll, probe, or density test performed, retained for reference through Chapter 5.
  • Photographs: exposed subgrade condition, corrected areas, and grid-check locations, referenced in the log.

Common Subgrade Inspection

Inspect every excavated subgrade using the same checks regardless of slab type.

  • Utility and layout check: confirm utility-locate markings and the Chapter 3 layout remain valid before excavation begins.
  • Stripping check: confirm all organic material has been removed from within the excavation limits.
  • Protection check: confirm no footing, slab, retaining system, utility, or adjacent paving has been undermined.
  • Suitability check: confirm soft spots, debris, uncontrolled fill, and contamination have been identified and removed.
  • Slope check: confirm the compacted subgrade shows consistent slope toward the approved discharge path.
  • Compaction check: confirm compaction equipment matched soil type and passes were overlapped with no loose or spongy areas remaining.
  • Grid verification check: confirm elevation and slope were checked at a grid of locations, not a single point.
  • Acceptance check: confirm any specified proof-roll, probe, or density test was performed and passed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Soft Spot Compacted Over Instead of Removed

Probable cause: a soft or spongy area was compacted in place rather than excavated out and replaced with approved material.
Confirmation: probe the area again after compaction and compare firmness to surrounding subgrade.
Correction: excavate the soft area to sound soil, refill with approved material in compacted lifts, and recompact.
Reinspection: repeat the probe and compaction check on the corrected area before proceeding.

Subgrade Elevation Checked at Only One Point

Probable cause: a single center-point reading was taken and assumed to represent the entire subgrade.
Confirmation: take additional grid readings across the full excavated area and compare to the single reading.
Correction: correct any location that deviates from the approved elevation or slope, then re-check the full grid.
Reinspection: repeat the grid elevation check before documenting acceptance.

Excavation Undermines an Adjacent Footing or Utility

Probable cause: excavation continued to the planned depth without confirming the required separation distance from an adjacent structure or utility.
Confirmation: measure the actual separation achieved against the approved site plan requirement.
Correction: stop excavation in the affected area, document the condition, and refer it for engineering or structural review before continuing.
Reinspection: do not resume excavation in the affected area until the required review authorizes a revised method.

Subgrade Compacted Out of Slope

Probable cause: the subgrade was compacted level or to an unintended slope rather than the drainage direction established in Chapter 3.
Confirmation: check grid elevation readings against the approved slope direction and discharge path.
Correction: rework and recompact the subgrade to the correct slope using the same elevation reference.
Reinspection: repeat the slope and grid elevation check across the full area.

Overexcavated Area Refilled With Unapproved Material

Probable cause: a location excavated below the approved elevation was refilled with loose spoil, debris, or unapproved fill instead of approved material placed in compacted lifts.
Confirmation: inspect the refilled area for material type, lift thickness, and compaction condition.
Correction: remove the unapproved fill and refill with approved material in properly compacted lifts.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation and compaction check on the corrected area.

Subgrade Accepted Without Required Acceptance Testing

Probable cause: the subgrade log was completed and acceptance recorded without performing the proof-roll, probe, or density test the project specification required.
Confirmation: review the project specification against the subgrade log for the required test method.
Correction: perform the required test before finalizing acceptance, and correct any area that fails.
Reinspection: repeat the acceptance check once the required test is complete and passed.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a compacted, verified subgrade and a completed log, not a visual impression of "dug to depth."

  • Remove or relocate spoil piles that are not needed for the next chapter's grading
  • Confirm utility-locate markings intended to remain visible are left undisturbed for Chapter 5 and later chapters
  • Clean and store excavation and compaction equipment according to manufacturer instructions
  • File the completed subgrade log, grid elevation record, and any acceptance test record together
  • Store photographs with clear labels referencing the subgrade log entries they support
  • Barricade the accepted subgrade to protect it from rain, traffic, and disturbance until Chapter 5 begins

Moisture-Conditioned Soil

A shallow excavation pass used instead of digging to full depth at once, to reveal obstructions and soft spots early.
Spoil
Excavated soil not reused on site, stockpiled or removed without blocking drainage or access.
Subgrade Log
The document recording excavation depth, soil conditions, corrections, and final acceptance for this chapter.
