Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to set and verify the final form elevations and drainage slope for one exterior slab or walkway section. The finished work product is a form system with every form top adjusted to the approved finished elevation and slope, checked at a grid or edge pattern of readings, and approved before concrete placement.

You will review the approved benchmark, high edge, low edge, slope direction, and drainage destination established in Chapter 1; calculate the total fall across the slab; transfer elevations to the form staked and braced in Chapter 6; raise or lower form sections to match; and verify that water will move away from the structure rather than toward it.

This chapter depends directly on the site-readiness plan from Chapter 1 and the squared, staked, braced forms from Chapter 6. It does not repeat the layout or form-building work; it takes the existing form system and sets it to the correct elevations and slope. Chapter 8 cannot correctly place and support reinforcement until the elevations set here are finalized, because reinforcement cover and screed elevation depend on this chapter's grade.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FW-037 — Establish the finished elevation at the controlling high point from the approved benchmark and site plan.
  • FW-038 — Calculate slope and total fall from the approved slope ratio and run.
  • FW-039 — Set form height by transferring calculated elevations to form stakes and fastening the adjustment.
  • FW-040 — Verify drainage direction across the full slab, including cross slope where specified.
  • FW-041 — Check for low points at joints, drains, thresholds, and transitions before pre-pour approval.
  • FW-042 — Inspect and document grade control at a grid or edge-reading pattern before concrete placement.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter sits between Chapter 6, which built and braced the form system, and Chapter 8, which installs reinforcement inside that form system. The elevations and slope set here directly control reinforcement cover, screed elevation in Chapter 10, and the finished drainage behavior of the slab for the rest of its service life.

The elevation reference and drainage findings recorded in Chapter 1 control every calculation in this chapter. A different reference point, high edge, or drainage destination is not available here; if the Chapter 1 findings are incomplete or in question, that deficiency must be resolved before this chapter proceeds.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved benchmark, high edge, low edge, slope direction, drainage destination, and interface elevations from Chapter 1
  • calculating total fall from the approved slope ratio and run
  • transferring calculated elevations to the existing form stakes
  • raising, lowering, and refastening form sections to match calculated elevations
  • verifying cross slope and longitudinal slope where both apply
  • checking for trapped water at structures, transitions, joints, and drains
  • recording grid or edge elevation readings and obtaining pre-pour grade approval

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • invent a slope, fall, finished elevation, or drainage destination that is not supplied by the approved site plan, grading plan, or Chapter 1 findings
  • direct drainage toward the building, a neighboring property, a public hazard, or a prohibited discharge point
  • set an elevation from a different reference point than the single elevation reference established in Chapter 1
  • proceed to Chapter 8 reinforcement installation before grade approval is obtained and documented

Any elevation conflict, unresolved drainage destination, or slope that cannot meet the approved plan must be documented and referred, not resolved by guessing.

Trade Note

Every elevation set in this chapter becomes the screed reference in Chapter 10. An error introduced here is not visible until concrete is struck off against the form tops, at which point it can only be corrected by demolition. Recheck every reading against the single elevation reference before fastening any form adjustment.

Setup Standard

Do not begin transferring elevations until the Chapter 6 form-inspection record confirms the form system is square, braced, and stable. A form that moves during elevation adjustment invalidates every reading taken afterward.

Shop Tip

Take every elevation reading twice, once during initial transfer and once again after the form section is fastened. Fastening a form can shift it slightly from the exact height it was held at when first checked.

Common Field Fundamentals

Slope and finished elevation are not adjustments made by eye. They are calculated from the approved slope ratio and the measured run, then transferred to the form system using a level instrument, and finally verified by direct reading, not by visual impression of the form tops.

Elevation, Slope, and Drainage as One Calculation

The high-point elevation, the slope ratio, and the run together determine the low-edge elevation. Changing any one without recalculating the others produces a form system that does not match the approved drainage plan, even if every individual reading looks correct in isolation. Complete the calculation before touching any form stake.

Measurement Cue — Recalculate Before Adjusting

Calculate the total fall and the low-edge elevation on the grade-elevation worksheet before transferring any reading to a form stake. Do not adjust a form section and then work out afterward whether the result matches the approved slope.

Critical Safety Cue — Water Must Not Be Directed Toward the Building

Verify at every stage of this chapter that the calculated slope moves water away from foundations, siding, doors, and structures, and toward the approved discharge path only. Do not finalize a form elevation that traps water against a structure or directs it toward a neighboring property or prohibited discharge point.

Field Safety Cue — Working Around Staked and Braced Forms

Form stakes, braces, and kickers present trip and puncture hazards while adjusting elevations. Watch footing near form corners and braced runs, and avoid leaning on braces while taking readings.

Code Alert — Threshold and Drainage Clearance

Verify the required clearance between the finished slab elevation and door thresholds, siding, and weep screeds against adopted code and manufacturer requirements. Clearance requirements vary by jurisdiction and construction type.

FW-037 – FW-042

Grade-Setting / Drainage-Verification Qualification

Concrete Flatwork Slope, Drainage, and Finished-Elevation Control

Difficulty: Beginner to Intermediate

Estimated Time: 2–3 hours

Estimated Cost: No additional material cost

Finished Work: One form system set to approved finished elevation and drainage slope, inspected and documented

A. Finished-Project Specification

Scope: one existing, staked, and braced form system from Chapter 6, adjusted to the approved finished elevation and drainage slope from the approved site plan and Chapter 1 findings, verified at a grid or edge-reading pattern, and approved before concrete placement.

B. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review the approved benchmark, high edge, low edge, slope direction, drainage destination, and interface elevations. Confirm these match the Chapter 1 site-readiness findings exactly.
  2. Verify door thresholds, foundations, siding clearances, adjacent pavement, drains, and landscaping. Record each interface elevation relative to the single elevation reference.
  3. Establish the controlling finished elevation at the high point. Mark this elevation on the grade-elevation worksheet before any calculation.
  4. Calculate total fall from the approved slope and run. Use only the slope ratio and run supplied by the approved plan or measured layout.
  5. Record the calculated low-edge elevation. Subtract the total fall from the high-point elevation and record the result.
  6. Transfer elevations to form stakes using a laser, builder's level, water level, or approved method. Follow the level instrument's manufacturer setup and calibration instructions.
  7. Raise or lower form sections to the marks. Adjust one section at a time and recheck the reading before moving to the next.
  8. Fasten forms after each adjustment. Do not leave an adjusted form section unfastened while moving to the next section.
  9. Check cross slope and longitudinal slope where both apply. Verify each slope direction independently against the approved plan.
  10. Verify that water will not be trapped against structures or at transitions. Check thresholds, foundation clearances, and paving transitions specifically.
  11. Check planned joint, drain, and threshold elevations. Confirm these match the interface elevations recorded in Step 2.
  12. Stretch stringlines or use a straightedge across form tops. Check the full length and width of the form system.
  13. Identify local high and low points. Mark any point where the stringline or straightedge reveals a deviation.
  14. Correct sagging, crowned, twisted, or displaced forms. Readjust and refasten any form section that does not meet the calculated elevation.
  15. Check screed-board bearing surfaces. Confirm form tops present a continuous, stable bearing surface for the screed operation planned in Chapter 10.
  16. Confirm base elevation remains compatible with slab thickness. Verify the approved base elevation from Chapter 5 still supports the specified slab thickness beneath the adjusted form elevations.
  17. Recheck all elevations after bracing. Confirm no form section shifted during final bracing.
  18. Record grid or edge readings. Record elevation readings at the pattern specified for this project size.
  19. Obtain pre-pour grade approval. Confirm every reading meets the acceptance standards before proceeding to Chapter 8.
  20. Protect forms from disturbance. Barricade or flag the adjusted form system until reinforcement installation begins.

  • Slope calculation record: the total fall and low-edge elevation calculation, retained for comparison during Chapter 10 screeding.

Common Grade-Control Inspection

Inspect every adjusted form section using the same checks regardless of slab type.

  • Reference check: confirm every reading in this chapter uses the single elevation reference from Chapter 1.
  • Calculation check: confirm total fall and low-edge elevation were calculated before any form adjustment.
  • Form-top check: confirm every form-top reading matches its calculated elevation within tolerance.
  • Drainage check: confirm water moves away from structures toward the approved discharge path with no trapped low point.
  • Fastening check: confirm every adjusted section is refastened and rechecked.
  • Grid-reading check: confirm elevation readings are recorded at the specified pattern and compared to calculated values.
  • Approval check: confirm grade approval is documented before Chapter 8 begins.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Low-Edge Elevation Calculated from the Wrong Reference

Probable cause: a reference point other than the single elevation reference established in Chapter 1 was used for the high-point or low-edge calculation.
Confirmation: compare the recorded high-point elevation directly against the Chapter 1 site-readiness worksheet's elevation reference.
Correction: recalculate total fall and low-edge elevation from the correct reference and re-transfer every affected form-top reading.
Reinspection: repeat the form-top accuracy check at every affected location.

Water Trapped at a Threshold or Transition

Probable cause: the slope calculation addressed the overall high point and low edge but did not verify the elevation at a specific threshold, joint, or paving transition.
Confirmation: recheck the elevation at every recorded interface against the approved interface elevations from Step 2.
Correction: readjust the affected form section so the transition drains toward the approved discharge path.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage check at every transition before grade approval.

Form Section Shifted After Fastening

Probable cause: the elevation reading was taken while holding the form section by hand, and the section moved slightly when fasteners were driven.
Confirmation: recheck the reading at the fastened location against the calculated elevation.
Correction: loosen, re-set, and refasten the section, then recheck immediately after fastening rather than before.
Reinspection: repeat the form-top accuracy check at the corrected location and at adjacent sections.

Cross Slope Overlooked

Probable cause: only the primary longitudinal slope was checked, and the perpendicular cross slope specified in the approved plan was not independently verified.
Confirmation: check cross slope readings at multiple points along the slab's width, separate from the longitudinal readings.
Correction: readjust the affected form sections to restore the specified cross slope without disturbing the verified longitudinal slope.
Reinspection: repeat both slope checks independently before grade approval.

Grade Approval Issued Before Grid Readings Were Complete

Probable cause: a visual check of the form tops was treated as sufficient, and the full grid or edge-reading pattern was not completed and recorded.
Confirmation: compare the grade-elevation worksheet's recorded readings against the specified reading pattern for this project size.
Correction: complete every missing reading before finalizing the approval, and correct any location that does not meet the calculated elevation.
Reinspection: repeat the grid-reading check and reissue grade approval only once every reading is recorded.

Base Elevation No Longer Compatible with Adjusted Slab Thickness

Probable cause: form-top elevations were adjusted without rechecking whether the approved base elevation from Chapter 5 still supports the specified slab thickness beneath the new form elevation.
Confirmation: measure the distance from the adjusted form-top elevation down to the compacted base at multiple points.
Correction: refer the discrepancy for correction of the base elevation or the form elevation before proceeding, rather than accepting a nonstandard slab thickness.
Reinspection: repeat the base-compatibility check at every corrected location before grade approval.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with an approved, documented grade record, not a form system that merely looks level by eye.

  • Remove any loose fasteners, offcuts, or adjustment scrap from around the form system
  • Confirm every stringline, line block, and level instrument used for verification is removed and stored
  • Leave form stakes, braces, and marked elevation points undisturbed for Chapter 8
  • File the completed grade-elevation worksheet and slope calculation record together
  • Store photographs with clear labels referencing the worksheet entries they support
