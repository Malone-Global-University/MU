Job Overview

This project qualifies you to remove an existing floor-mounted toilet and install a replacement on an approved, structurally sound closet flange. The finished work product is one set, sealed, and tested toilet: a stable bowl and tank secured to the flange with a verified wax ring or alternative seal, a new supply connector, and a documented pass on repeated flush and leak testing.

You will shut off and drain the existing toilet, disconnect and remove it while protecting the open drain, inspect the flange for the conditions that determine whether replacement may proceed, install new closet bolts and the manufacturer-approved seal, set and secure the new bowl, reconnect the tank and supply, and verify performance through repeated flush cycles and a delayed reinspection.

This project depends on the isolation and work-area protection sequence from Project 01 and the fixture-stop and supply-connector skills from Project 07. It feeds directly into the Chapter 20 capstone, which uses this exact toilet-setting sequence to complete a small bathroom installation.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • PL-061 — Remove an existing floor-mounted toilet while protecting the open drain and controlling residual water.
  • PL-062 — Inspect the closet flange for elevation, attachment, condition, and drain security before proceeding.
  • PL-063 — Install the manufacturer-approved wax ring or alternative seal on the flange or toilet as directed.
  • PL-064 — Set the replacement toilet on new closet bolts and secure it without cracking the fixture.
  • PL-065 — Connect a new supply connector to the toilet stop and inspect it under static pressure.
  • PL-066 — Test flush and leak performance through repeated cycles and a delayed reinspection.

Where This Skill Fits

Toilet replacement combines the isolation and drain-down discipline from Project 01, the fixture-stop and flexible-connector skills from Project 07, and a seal-and-set procedure that is unique to this fixture. The flange inspection taught here — checking elevation, attachment, cracks, corrosion, and subfloor condition — is a required checkpoint before the seal is placed and before any weight is set on the connection.

The Chapter 20 capstone reuses this exact sequence to set the toilet in a small bathroom rough-in, following the closet-flange installation completed in that chapter's DWV rough-in steps.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • removing an existing floor-mounted toilet on an approved closet flange
  • inspecting the flange for elevation, attachment, cracks, corrosion, slots, subfloor condition, floor level, and drain security
  • installing new closet bolts and a manufacturer-approved wax ring or alternative seal
  • setting, seating, and securing the replacement toilet without cracking the bowl or overtightening the closet bolts
  • reconnecting or installing the tank, a new supply connector, and testing fill, flush, and leak performance

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • repair or replace a broken, unsupported, severely corroded, or incorrectly elevated closet flange beyond an approved adapter method
  • alter the DWV rough-in, trap arrangement, vent connection, or drain piping beneath the flange
  • proceed with the installation when the flange is surrounded by structural water damage or when the subfloor is soft, delaminated, or unsupported
  • reuse a compressed wax ring or a seal that has already been seated once

Any flange, subfloor, or drain condition that fails inspection in this chapter must be corrected or escalated to a qualified professional before the toilet is set.

Trade Note

The seal beneath a toilet only works if the bowl seats evenly, once, without twisting. Every step in this chapter's set-and-secure sequence exists to protect that single condition: the bowl goes down straight, seats fully under controlled pressure, and is never lifted and reseated on the same seal.

Setup Standard

Do not place the wax ring or lower the new toilet until the flange has passed inspection under Step F below and the drain has been covered since the moment the old toilet was lifted. A flange that fails inspection is a stop-work condition for this project until it is corrected or escalated.

Shop Tip

Set the removed toilet on protected material immediately and cover the open drain in the same motion. An uncovered drain, even briefly, is the most common source of sewer-gas odor and dropped-debris callbacks on this project.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every toilet-setting project follows the same four-part sequence: remove and protect, inspect the flange, seal and set, then test and confirm. Skipping the flange inspection to save time is the single most common cause of a toilet that rocks, leaks at the base, or fails inspection after it has already been sealed with base caulk.

Flange Condition and the Stop-Work Boundary

The flange inspection in this chapter is the single checkpoint that determines whether the project may proceed with a standard seal and closet-bolt installation. A flange that is broken, unsupported, severely corroded, incorrectly elevated beyond an approved adapter method, or surrounded by structural water damage is outside this project's scope and must be escalated before the new toilet is set.

Critical Safety Cue — Open Drain and Sewer Gas

Cover the open drain with a removable gas-resistant plug or cover the instant the old toilet is lifted clear. An uncovered drain can release sewer gas into the work area and allow dropped tools or debris to fall into the drain line. Never leave the drain open and unattended.

Safety Cue — Lifting the Toilet

A floor-mounted toilet is heavy and awkward to lift from a low, twisted position. Break the wax seal by controlled rocking before lifting, and use a helper or an approved lifting method rather than lifting alone from a bent-and-twisted position.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Rough-In Before Setting

Verify the rough-in distance and bowl configuration against the new toilet's specification before the seal is placed. A mismatch discovered after the seal is compressed requires a full seal replacement rather than a minor adjustment.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Seal Selection and Orientation

Select the wax ring or alternative seal specified for the flange elevation and toilet condition, and place it on the flange or toilet exactly as the manufacturer directs. A seal placed in the wrong orientation, or a seal type mismatched to the flange elevation, will not compress correctly and will leak.

F. Removal and Flange Inspection

  1. Verify toilet rough-in, bowl configuration, flange type, floor condition, supply location, clearance, and manufacturer requirements. Confirm the replacement toilet matches the measured rough-in distance before removal begins.
  2. Shut off the toilet stop, flush, hold the lever to drain the tank, and remove remaining water. Use a sponge and bucket to remove residual water from the tank and bowl.
  3. Disconnect the supply connector and cap or contain it. Control any residual water released during disconnection.
  4. Remove tank hardware separately where required for safe lifting. Set the tank on protected material if it is removed independently of the bowl.
  5. Remove closet-bolt nuts and cut only approved corroded hardware. Use the closet-bolt wrench; cut a seized nut only when normal removal is not possible.
  6. Break the existing seal by controlled rocking and lift the toilet with the required helper or lifting method. Do not twist or drag the bowl across the flange.
  7. Set the removed toilet on protected material and immediately cover the open drain with a removable gas-resistant plug or cover. Confirm the cover is seated before proceeding to the next step.
  8. Remove the old seal without dropping debris into the drain. Use the putty knife to lift the compressed wax cleanly from the flange.
  9. Inspect flange elevation, attachment, cracks, corrosion, slots, subfloor condition, floor level, and drain security. Record each observed condition on the worksheet before proceeding.
  10. Stop work if the flange is broken, unsupported, severely corroded, incorrectly elevated beyond the approved adapter method, or surrounded by structural water damage. Escalate per the Failure Points section before continuing.

G. Seal Installation and Bowl Setting

  1. Install approved new closet bolts in the correct orientation. Confirm both bolts stand vertically and are seated in the flange slots.
  2. Select the manufacturer-approved wax ring or alternative seal for the flange elevation and toilet condition. Confirm the seal profile matches the flange condition recorded in Step F9.
  3. Place the seal on the flange or toilet as directed. Follow the manufacturer's specified orientation exactly.
  4. Lower the bowl vertically over the bolts without twisting or reusing a compressed seal. Align the bolt holes with the closet bolts before any downward pressure is applied.
  5. Seat the toilet evenly using controlled downward pressure. Apply pressure at the center of the bowl, not at one side, to compress the seal uniformly in a single motion.

H. Securing and Tank Installation

  1. Install washers and nuts and tighten alternately only enough to secure the bowl without cracking it. Check bowl stability after each partial turn.
  2. Shim with approved noncompressible shims if required, then trim and seal only as specified. Verify the bowl sits level front-to-back and side-to-side before trimming shims.
  3. Install or reconnect the tank using the manufacturer's gasket and hardware sequence. Tighten tank bolts evenly to avoid distorting the tank or bowl.
  4. Install a new compatible supply connector. Route it without kinks, twists, or contact with sharp edges.

I. Restoration and Testing

  1. Restore water slowly and inspect the stop, connector, fill valve, tank bolts, and tank-to-bowl connection. Confirm no leakage appears as the tank fills.
  2. Adjust tank water level and verify refill shutoff. Confirm the fill valve shuts off at the manufacturer-specified level.
  3. Perform repeated flush tests using paper and the fixture manufacturer's normal operating condition. Observe at least three complete flush-and-refill cycles.
  4. Inspect the base, ceiling below, surrounding floor, and all supply connections for leakage. Check the ceiling below whenever access is available.
  5. Confirm stability without overtightening the closet bolts. Recheck for rock at the base after the final flush cycle.
  6. Apply base sealant only as permitted while preserving any required leak-indication opening. Do not seal the entire base perimeter if local practice requires an open gap for leak detection.
  7. Complete a delayed reinspection and document the installation. Recheck the base, connections, and floor after normal use has resumed.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed toilet installation using the same checks regardless of the specific model installed.

  • Flange check: confirm the flange was inspected and passed before the seal was placed.
  • Seal check: confirm the correct seal type and orientation were used and were placed only once.
  • Stability check: confirm no rock or movement at the base after securing and after the flush test.
  • Supply check: confirm no leakage at the stop, connector, or tank connection under static and fill pressure.
  • Flush check: confirm normal fill, flush, and refill through repeated cycles.
  • Documentation check: confirm the Toilet Replacement Worksheet is fully completed and signed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Toilet Rocks After Securing

Probable cause: an uneven floor beneath the base, a warped or damaged bowl base, or closet bolts tightened unevenly.
Confirmation: check for rock at each corner of the bowl and inspect the gap between the base and the floor with a straightedge.
Correction: loosen the nuts, install approved noncompressible shims where the gap is confirmed, and retighten alternately to the stable condition.
Reinspection: repeat the stability check at all four points of contact.

Water Leaks at the Base After Flushing

Probable cause: the seal did not compress evenly, the bowl was twisted during setting, or the seal was reused after an initial compression.
Confirmation: isolate and drain the toilet, lift the bowl, and inspect the seal for uneven compression or an incomplete ring around the flange.
Correction: replace the seal with a new manufacturer-approved unit and reset the bowl vertically without twisting.
Reinspection: repeat the flush and base-leak test through at least three cycles.

The Bowl Cracks or Chips During Securing

Probable cause: closet-bolt nuts were overtightened, or the bowl was seated on debris or an uneven flange surface.
Confirmation: inspect the crack location relative to the closet bolts and the flange surface.
Correction: stop work, do not attempt to use a cracked bowl, and replace it with a new unit following the full seal-and-set sequence.
Reinspection: repeat the flange inspection before setting the replacement bowl.

The Supply Connector Leaks Under Pressure

Probable cause: the connector was cross-threaded, overtightened, undertightened, or twisted during routing.
Confirmation: isolate and depressurize, then inspect both ends of the connector and the sealing washer or gasket.
Correction: replace the connector rather than overtightening an existing leak, and reroute it without twisting.
Reinspection: restore water slowly and recheck under static and fill-cycle pressure.

The Flange Fails Inspection Before the Seal Is Placed

Probable cause: corrosion, a cracked flange, an unsupported subfloor, or an elevation outside the range of a standard seal.
Confirmation: re-inspect the flange attachment, subfloor condition, and elevation relative to the finished floor.
Correction: stop work at this flange; escalate flange repair, subfloor repair, or an approved elevation adapter to the required qualified professional before setting any toilet.
Reinspection: repeat the full flange inspection once the corrective work is complete and documented.

The Tank Refills Continuously or Will Not Shut Off

Probable cause: the fill valve was not adjusted to the manufacturer-specified level, or a component was disturbed during tank installation.
Confirmation: observe the fill valve and float during a complete fill cycle and compare the water level to the manufacturer's marked level.
Correction: adjust the fill valve per the manufacturer's instructions; if the valve does not shut off correctly, replace it following the tank-hardware sequence.
Reinspection: repeat the flush and refill test to confirm correct shutoff.

Cleanup

  • Return tools to clean storage after separating any tools used near the open drain
