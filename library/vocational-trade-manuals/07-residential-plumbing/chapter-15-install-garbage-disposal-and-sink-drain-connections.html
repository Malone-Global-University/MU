Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install one listed residential food-waste disposer and its approved sink, discharge, dishwasher, and electrical connections. The finished work product is one securely mounted disposer that fills, drains, and operates without leakage, excess vibration, or an unsafe electrical connection.

You will confirm the electrical circuit is de-energized, remove the existing drain assembly, mount the disposer sink flange, prepare the electrical connection, lift and lock the disposer onto the mounting ring, connect the discharge tube and dishwasher hose, and complete a fill-and-drain and operational test before documenting the finished installation.

This project builds directly on the sink drain trap skills from Chapter 9 and the dishwasher connection skills from Chapter 14. It assumes the fixture, branch, and work area have already been isolated and prepared using the Chapter 1 shutdown and protection sequence.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • PL-085 — Remove the old drain basket, disposer, or branch components included in the project.
  • PL-086 — Mount the disposal unit to the sink using the specified flange, gasket, and mounting-ring assembly.
  • PL-087 — Connect the discharge pipe to an approved trap and branch arrangement.
  • PL-088 — Connect the dishwasher inlet where applicable using the approved high-loop or air-gap arrangement.
  • PL-089 — Test the disposal for leakage, vibration, mounting security, and correct operation.
  • PL-090 — Inspect the finished installation for leaks, drainage performance, and electrical safety.

Where This Skill Fits

The disposer installation combines the trap and drain skills from Chapter 9, the dishwasher connection skills from Chapter 14, and coordination with an electrical circuit that must be verified de-energized before any mechanical work begins. This project is the first in the book that requires the learner to coordinate a plumbing connection directly against an electrical hazard at the same fixture.

The discharge, trap, and dishwasher-connection sequence taught here recurs in the Chapter 20 capstone whenever a kitchen or utility disposer branch is included in the approved plan.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • removing an existing drain basket, disposer, or included branch components
  • mounting a listed disposer using its specified sink flange, gasket or sealant, backup ring, mounting ring, and fasteners
  • preparing the disposer electrical connection using the listed cord kit or hardwired method assigned by the electrical specification
  • connecting the disposer discharge to an approved trap and branch arrangement
  • connecting a dishwasher hose using the approved high-loop or air-gap arrangement
  • testing the disposer for leakage, vibration, mounting security, and correct operation

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • energize, modify, or troubleshoot the branch circuit, switch, or receptacle serving the disposer beyond confirming de-energization with a non-contact voltage tester — wiring work is assigned to the qualified electrical installer identified in the project specification
  • alter the DWV branch, trap arrangement, or vent configuration beyond the approved connection specified in the project plan
  • continue work when the disposer circuit cannot be confirmed de-energized, when the sink is cracked or unsupported, or when a stop-work condition identified in this chapter is present and uncorrected

Trade Note

A disposer installation is judged on three independent conditions: a secure, leak-free sink-flange mount; a trap and discharge arrangement that drains without back-pitch, S-trap, or double trap; and a verified de-energized-to-energized electrical handoff. A disposer that passes a fill test but was wired without verified de-energization has not passed this project.

Setup Standard

Do not begin mechanical work on the disposer, its mounting ring, or its wiring compartment until electrical power to the disposer circuit has been confirmed disconnected with a non-contact voltage tester at the disposer's own connection point. Do not restore electrical power until the mechanical installation is complete, the mounting ring is fully locked, and all rotating components move freely with power still disconnected.

Shop Tip

Remove the dishwasher-inlet knockout on the disposer before the disposer is lifted into the mounting ring, not after. Reaching inside a mounted disposer to knock out the plug is awkward and risks damaging the mounting-ring seal; doing it on the bench first also lets you retrieve the plug reliably instead of losing it inside the unit.

Common Field Fundamentals

A disposer installation succeeds only when three separate systems are each verified independently: the mechanical mount to the sink, the drainage path to the trap and branch, and the electrical handoff. Treating the fill test as proof that all three are correct skips the electrical and mounting verification this chapter requires.

Mounting, Drainage, and Electrical Verification

Mount the disposer only after the sink opening is clean and inspected for cracks or unsupported material. Tighten the mounting assembly evenly, alternating across fasteners rather than fully tightening one side first, to avoid distorting the sink. Route the discharge to an approved trap and branch arrangement without creating an S-trap, double trap, back-pitch, or cross-connection. Confirm the disposer circuit de-energized with a non-contact voltage tester before any wiring or mounting-ring work, and confirm free rotation of the flywheel with the supplied wrench, power still disconnected, before restoring power.

Critical Safety Cue — Verified De-Energization Before Any Mechanical or Electrical Work

Confirm electrical power to the disposer circuit is disconnected using a non-contact voltage tester at the disposer's own connection point before removing the old assembly, mounting the new disposer, or working inside the wiring compartment. Never rely on a switch position, a breaker label, or the fact that the unit is unplugged as confirmation. A disposer circuit that cannot be confirmed de-energized is a stop-work condition.

Safety Cue — Flywheel and Grind-Chamber Contact

Wear cut-resistant gloves any time a hand is placed near the flywheel, grind chamber, or discharge opening, even with power confirmed disconnected. Use the manufacturer-supplied disposer wrench, inserted through the bottom access opening, to manually free a jammed flywheel rather than reaching into the grind chamber directly.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Mounting Ring and Cord Kit

Assemble the sink flange, gasket or sealant, backup ring, and mounting ring in the exact sequence specified by the disposer manufacturer. Install the listed cord kit or hardwired connection using the manufacturer's strain-relief and grounding method. A mounting ring that is not fully locked or a cord connection missing the required strain relief is a stop-work condition.

Code Alert — S-Traps, Double Traps, and Dishwasher Backflow

Connect the disposer discharge to an approved trap and branch arrangement only. Do not create an S-trap, double trap, back-pitched branch, or a dishwasher connection that lacks the required high loop or air gap. Verify the exact configuration required against the adopted code and the approved project plan before assembling the discharge and dishwasher connections.

E. Preparation and Electrical Isolation

  1. Review disposer instructions, sink compatibility, drain configuration, dishwasher connection, switch or cord requirements, and local restrictions. Confirm the disposer model and required hardware match the approved project plan.
  2. Confirm that electrical power is disconnected and verified at the disposer circuit. Use a non-contact voltage tester at the disposer's own connection point; do not rely on a switch position or breaker label alone.
  3. Shut off water where necessary and protect the cabinet. Apply the Chapter 1 shutdown and protection sequence to the affected fixture and cabinet.

F. Removal and Sink Preparation

  1. Disconnect and remove the existing drain basket, disposer, or branch components included in the project. Control retained water and support the assembly while removing it.
  2. Clean the sink opening and inspect for cracks, corrosion, distortion, and unsupported material. Stop work if the sink cannot support the mounting assembly.

G. Sink Flange Mounting

  1. Assemble the disposer sink flange, gasket or sealant, backup ring, mounting ring, and fasteners in the specified sequence. Follow the manufacturer's exact assembly order.
  2. Tighten the mounting assembly evenly without distorting the sink. Alternate across fasteners rather than fully tightening one side first.

H. Electrical Preparation

  1. Remove the dishwasher-inlet knockout only when a dishwasher hose will be connected and retrieve the plug from the disposer. Confirm the plug is fully removed and accounted for.
  2. Prepare the disposer electrical connection using the listed cord kit or hardwired method assigned by the electrical specification. Route conductors without strain, kinks, or contact with moving components.
  3. Install the strain relief and grounding connection according to the electrical instructions. Confirm the grounding conductor is securely terminated before proceeding.

I. Mounting the Disposer

  1. Lift and lock the disposer onto the mounting ring using the required support or helper. Confirm the mounting ring locks fully with an audible or visible engagement per manufacturer instructions.
  2. Rotate the disposer to align the discharge outlet without placing stress on the sink flange. Support the unit while rotating; do not let its weight hang on the flange during alignment.

J. Discharge and Dishwasher Connections

  1. Install the supplied discharge tube, gasket, flange, and fasteners. Confirm the gasket seats fully before tightening.
  2. Connect the discharge to an approved trap and branch arrangement without creating an S-trap, double trap, back-pitch, or cross-connection. Verify the configuration against the approved project plan.
  3. Connect the dishwasher hose using the approved high loop or air-gap arrangement. Secure the hose at the controlled height specified by the project plan.

K. Free-Rotation Check and Water Restoration

  1. Confirm that all rotating components move freely using the supplied wrench with power disconnected. Do not proceed to restore power if the flywheel binds or will not turn.
  2. Restore water and fill the sink to inspect the sink flange. Observe the flange directly while the basin fills.
  3. Release water and inspect the discharge, trap, and dishwasher connection. Observe every disturbed joint during the full-basin release.

L. Electrical Restoration and Operational Test

  1. Restore electrical power through the approved method. Confirm the connection was completed by the assigned qualified installer where required.
  2. Run cold water and operate the disposer briefly according to the manufacturer's procedure. Observe startup, operation, and shutdown.
  3. Inspect vibration, sound, mounting security, drainage, electrical cord clearance, and leakage. Confirm every condition against the Acceptance Standards table.
  4. Stop work for breaker trips, grinding contact, severe vibration, overheating, or leakage at the sink flange. Treat any of these as a stop-work condition.

M. Correction and Documentation

  1. Isolate power and water before correction. Confirm de-energization again with the non-contact voltage tester before touching any disturbed connection.
  2. Repeat the operational test and complete a delayed reinspection. Allow the assembly to sit undisturbed, then recheck every connection for leakage or movement.
  3. Document disposer model, drain arrangement, electrical handoff, and test result. Complete every field on the Disposer Installation Worksheet.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed disposer installation using the same checks regardless of sink type or dishwasher configuration.

  • Electrical check: confirm the disposer circuit was verified de-energized before mechanical work and confirm the final connection under power without exposed conductors.
  • Mounting check: confirm the mounting ring is fully locked and the disposer does not shift under hand pressure.
  • Drainage check: confirm the discharge and trap arrangement matches the approved plan with no S-trap, double trap, or back-pitch.
  • Dishwasher check: confirm the high loop or air gap is installed at the controlled height where a dishwasher connection is included.
  • Leak check: confirm no leakage at the flange, discharge, trap, or dishwasher connection during and after the fill-and-drain test.
  • Operational check: confirm no excessive vibration, abnormal sound, breaker trip, or overheating during the operational test.
  • Documentation check: confirm the Disposer Installation Worksheet is fully completed and signed.

Complete the Disposer Installation Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 16.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Disposer Leaks at the Sink Flange

Probable cause: uneven mounting-ring tightening, an improperly seated gasket, or insufficient sealant coverage on the flange.
Confirmation: fill the sink and observe the flange directly during the full-basin release; inspect the gasket seating from below with power disconnected.
Correction: isolate power and water, loosen the mounting ring, correct the gasket or sealant application, and retighten evenly.
Reinspection: repeat the fill-and-drain test and inspect the flange again.

Disposer Vibrates Excessively or Contacts the Sink Bowl

Probable cause: the mounting ring is not fully locked, the discharge connection is placing side load on the unit, or an object is lodged in the grind chamber.
Confirmation: with power confirmed disconnected, inspect the mounting-ring lock and check the discharge alignment for stress against the flange.
Correction: isolate power, correct the mounting-ring engagement or discharge alignment, and clear any lodged object using the disposer wrench through the bottom access opening.
Reinspection: repeat the free-rotation check and the operational test.

Disposer Does Not Start or Trips the Breaker

Probable cause: a seized flywheel, an incomplete electrical connection, or a wiring fault beyond the scope of this project.
Confirmation: confirm de-energization, use the disposer wrench to check for a seized flywheel, and inspect the electrical connection for looseness or damage.
Correction: free a seized flywheel per manufacturer instructions; refer any wiring fault to the assigned qualified electrical installer rather than continuing independent troubleshooting.
Reinspection: repeat the free-rotation check, then the operational test after the qualified installer confirms the circuit.

Drainage Backs Up or Drains Slowly

Probable cause: an S-trap, double trap, back-pitched branch, or an obstruction in the discharge or trap.
Confirmation: inspect the discharge and trap arrangement against the approved plan and check for slope and obstruction.
Correction: isolate water, correct the trap and branch arrangement to the approved configuration, and clear any obstruction.
Reinspection: repeat the fill-and-drain test and observe flow under full-basin release.

Dishwasher Water Backs Up Into the Dishwasher

Probable cause: a missing or incorrectly installed high loop or air gap, or a dishwasher-inlet knockout that was not removed.
Confirmation: inspect the dishwasher hose routing and height, and confirm the knockout was removed if a hose is connected.
Correction: isolate water and power, correct the high-loop or air-gap installation to the controlled height, and remove the knockout if missed.
Reinspection: repeat the dishwasher connection check and the fill-and-drain test.

Cleanup

This project ends with the disposer mounted, wired, tested, and documented, ready for normal service.

  • Remove and properly dispose of old drain-basket or disposer components
  • Wipe down the cabinet interior and confirm no residual water remains
  • Confirm the mounting ring, discharge, and dishwasher connections remain undisturbed after testing
  • Confirm the disposer circuit is left in its normal operating condition, correctly labeled
  • Store the Disposer Installation Worksheet with the project records for reference by later projects
