Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to rough-in, inspect, finish, test, and document a small bathroom plumbing system serving one lavatory and one toilet. The finished work product is one inspected, functional small-bathroom plumbing system: tested water-supply piping, an approved DWV and venting arrangement, accessible shutoffs, correctly installed fixtures, a complete rough-in inspection record, a complete finish inspection record, and a formal closeout package.

You will obtain and confirm the approved bathroom plan; mark fixture and piping references; install the toilet and lavatory drain, trap, and vent rough-in; install the hot and cold supply branches; cap and test the rough-in; pass a rough-in inspection before wall closure; protect the system during finish work; set the closet flange and fixture stops at finished-floor height; install the toilet, faucet, pop-up drain, and P-trap using the exact procedures from Chapters 9, 10, and 11; restore water and test the completed system; pass a finish inspection; and complete the documentation and handoff required to close out the project.

This is the last project in the book. It does not introduce a new joining method, fixture type, or test procedure. It integrates the shutoff and work-area discipline of Chapter 1, the system-mapping habit of Chapter 2, the pipe-preparation standard of Chapter 3, the supply-piping methods of Chapters 4 through 7, the DWV assembly standard of Chapter 8, the trap-installation procedure of Chapter 9, the faucet and pop-up procedure of Chapter 10, and the toilet-setting procedure of Chapter 11 into one continuous, inspected installation.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • PL-115 — Integrate the water-supply layout with the approved bathroom plan and fixture schedule.
  • PL-116 — Integrate the DWV layout, including drain, trap, vent, and cleanout arrangement, with the approved plan.
  • PL-117 — Install and verify fixture shutoffs serving the lavatory and toilet.
  • PL-118 — Install the sink drain, toilet connection, and faucet using the exact procedures established in Chapters 9, 10, and 11.
  • PL-119 — Complete rough-in and finish leak testing and pass the required inspections.
  • PL-120 — Complete plumbing closeout documentation for the finished bathroom system.

Where This Skill Fits

Every project in this book has been building toward this integration. Chapter 1 supplied the isolation and work-area discipline used at every stage of this project. Chapter 2 supplied the system-mapping habit used to confirm the approved plan against field conditions. Chapters 3 through 7 supplied the pipe-preparation and supply-piping methods used for the hot and cold branches. Chapter 8 supplied the PVC DWV assembly standard used for the drain, trap arm, and vent. Chapter 9 supplied the P-trap installation procedure. Chapter 10 supplied the faucet and pop-up drain procedure. Chapter 11 supplied the toilet-setting procedure. This capstone does not replace any of those procedures — it sequences them into one inspected system.

The rough-in inspection and finish inspection completed in this project are the same two-stage inspection discipline used on any real bathroom plumbing installation: concealed work is verified before it is covered, and completed work is verified before it is handed off.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • rough-in and finish of one small bathroom plumbing system serving one lavatory and one toilet
  • integration of water-supply, DWV, vent, shutoff, and fixture installation using the approved plan and the methods taught in Chapters 1 through 11
  • a rough-in pressure and DWV test and a required rough-in inspection before wall closure
  • a finish installation of the toilet, lavatory faucet, pop-up drain, and P-trap
  • a finish leak test and a required finish inspection before closeout
  • complete documentation, punch-list correction, and handoff of the functional bathroom plumbing system

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • invent standpipe height, trap size, trap-arm length, vent arrangement, branch size, pipe size, fitting type, support spacing, fixture clearance, test pressure, test duration, or code provision not supplied by the approved bathroom plan, fixture schedule, manufacturer instructions, or adopted code
  • cut structural framing members without an approved framing plan and confirmed structural authorization
  • conceal any rough-in piping before the required rough-in inspection has passed
  • proceed to finish work when a stop-work condition identified in this chapter or any prior chapter is present and uncorrected
  • perform work reserved for a licensed plumber, electrician, or other qualified professional under the project's permit conditions

This capstone must be installed inside a permitted small bathroom installation, a code-compliant training structure, or a professionally supervised plumbing practice wall designed for the specified fixtures, and only where the approved plans provide the required framing, fixture clearances, water supply, DWV route, venting, and inspection access.

Trade Note

This chapter does not restate the detailed procedures for PEX or copper joining, PVC DWV assembly, trap installation, faucet and pop-up installation, or toilet setting. Those procedures are controlled by Chapters 4 through 11 and are referenced here by step. Do not substitute a different method for any referenced step without documenting the controlling reason.

Two-Stage Inspection Discipline

This project requires two separate inspections at two separate stages. The rough-in inspection verifies concealed piping, supports, and test results before any wall material is installed over the work. The finish inspection verifies completed fixture operation, drainage performance, and absence of leakage before the project is closed out. Do not combine, skip, or reorder these two inspections.

Measurement Cue — Verify the Plan Before Cutting Framing or Pipe

Confirm every pipe size, fitting type, slope, vent arrangement, and fixture clearance against the approved bathroom plan and fixture schedule before cutting any framing member or pipe. Do not infer a dimension or arrangement that the plan does not state.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Conceal Untested Work

Do not conceal supply, drain, vent, or fixture connections until the rough-in pressure test, DWV test, and required rough-in inspection have passed. Concealing untested work risks undetected leakage, structural water damage, and failed finish inspection.

Field Safety Cue — Coordinate Framing, Electrical, and Structural Work

Coordinate framing holes, backing, and protection plates with the approved framing plan. Do not cut, notch, or drill a structural member beyond what the framing plan authorizes. Verify electrical circuits and other utilities in the work area before drilling.

Code Alert — Rough-In and Finish Inspections Are Both Required

Local jurisdictions vary in scheduling and documentation requirements for rough-in and finish plumbing inspections. Confirm the required inspection sequence, notice period, and documentation with the authority having jurisdiction before wall closure and before fixture handoff.

E. Plan Confirmation and Project Preparation

  1. Obtain the approved bathroom plan, fixture schedule, water-supply design, DWV and vent design, framing plan, finish dimensions, permit requirements, and inspection sequence. Confirm every document is current and applies to the assigned installation.
  2. Confirm the project location, fixture clearances, accessibility requirements, structural conditions, and route availability. Verify the location matches the approved plan.
  3. Define work assigned to the learner and work reserved for licensed or qualified professionals. Record the split on the capstone worksheet before proceeding.
  4. Prepare the plumbing safety, shutoff, sanitation, material-control, testing, and documentation plan. Reference the Chapter 1 work-area readiness procedure for every shutdown required during this project.
  5. Verify the building water-supply connection, approved test source, sanitary connection, vent route, and cleanout access. Confirm each connection point is accessible and matches the approved plan.

F. Layout and Framing Coordination

  1. Mark lavatory centerline, toilet centerline, finished-floor reference, supply stub-outs, drain outlets, vent route, shutoffs, and access points. Use the approved plan dimensions; do not estimate a reference point.
  2. Coordinate framing holes, backing, protection plates, and fixture supports without unauthorized structural cutting. Confirm every framing modification is authorized by the framing plan before cutting.

G. DWV Rough-In

  1. Install the approved toilet drain, closet bend, flange riser or protected rough-in, and vented branch arrangement. Follow the Chapter 8 DWV assembly procedure for every joint.
  2. Install the approved lavatory drain, trap adapter rough-in, trap arm, vent connection, and cleanout provisions. Maintain the exact fitting pattern specified in the plan.
  3. Maintain the specified DWV fitting orientation, slope, support, and pipe protection. Verify slope using the method taught in Chapter 8.

H. Supply Rough-In

  1. Install hot- and cold-water branches using the approved PEX, copper, or other specified system. Follow the exact joining procedure from the applicable chapter — do not blend methods.
  2. Install fixture shutoffs or protected stub-outs in the planned locations. Confirm each shutoff is accessible after finish work.
  3. Support supply piping, protect framing penetrations, and provide required movement or hammer control. Install protection plates at every penetration within the prohibited fastener zone.

I. Rough-In Testing and Inspection

  1. Cap, plug, and label all rough-in openings. Confirm every cap and plug is secure before pressurizing the system.
  2. Complete the approved water-supply pressure test. Apply the specified test pressure and duration and inspect every joint for leakage.
  3. Complete the approved DWV test. Verify no leakage at any DWV joint under the required test method.
  4. Correct failed joints or layout conditions using the controlled repair method. Depressurize before correcting any failed connection and retest after correction.
  5. Obtain the required rough-in inspection before concealment. Do not install any wall material over the work until the inspection is approved.
  6. Photograph and record all concealed piping, dimensions, supports, plates, and inspection approvals. Complete every field on the Rough-In Inspection Record before wall closure.

J. Finish Preparation

  1. Protect plumbing during insulation, wall finish, flooring, and painting work. Confirm capped openings and protection plates remain undisturbed through finish work.
  2. Verify final wall and floor thickness before trimming stub-outs and setting fixtures. Confirm the finished-floor and finished-wall dimensions match the approved plan before cutting any stub-out.
  3. Install the closet flange at the approved finished-floor relationship. Confirm flange elevation against the finished-floor reference before securing it.
  4. Install the toilet stop and lavatory hot and cold stops. Confirm each stop is accessible and operates through its full range.
  5. Flush supply lines before fixture connection. Direct flushed water into approved containment.

K. Fixture Installation

  1. Set and connect the toilet using the Chapter 11 sequence. Follow every step of the Chapter 11 work order without omission.
  2. Install the lavatory faucet and pop-up drain using the Chapter 10 sequence. Follow every step of the Chapter 10 work order without omission.
  3. Install and align the lavatory P-trap using the Chapter 9 sequence. Follow every step of the Chapter 9 work order without omission.

L. Finish Testing, Inspection, and Closeout

  1. Restore water slowly and purge air. Confirm steady flow at both fixture stops before proceeding.
  2. Inspect all supply connections under static and operating conditions. Check every connection disturbed during finish installation.
  3. Fill and release the lavatory basin and operate repeated toilet flush cycles. Observe drainage performance under full-flow conditions.
  4. Inspect drain connections, fixture seals, closet-flange area, surrounding floor, wall cavities where accessible, and the level below. Confirm no leakage at any point.
  5. Verify fixture operation, shutoff access, drainage, vent performance, trap seals, and absence of cross-connections. Confirm every item against the approved plan.
  6. Complete required finish inspection and correct all deficiencies. Do not close out the project with an open deficiency.
  7. Label shutoffs and prepare the final system map. Use the Chapter 2 mapping method for the completed bathroom system.
  8. Compile material records, photographs, pressure and DWV test records, inspection approvals, corrections, and maintenance information. Assemble the complete closeout package.
  9. Complete the plumbing punch list and delayed leak reinspection. Confirm no leakage appears after the delayed observation period.
  10. Hand off the functional bathroom plumbing system with documented operating and emergency-shutoff instructions. Provide the owner or responsible party with the completed closeout package.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Rough-In Pressure Test Fails at One or More Joints

Probable cause: an incomplete crimp, clamp, solder joint, push-fit engagement, or compression connection made during supply rough-in.
Confirmation: depressurize, inspect each connection using the method specified in the applicable supply chapter, and isolate the failed joint.
Correction: remake or replace the failed connection using the exact correction method specified in Chapters 4 through 7 for that joining system.
Reinspection: repressurize and repeat the full rough-in pressure test before proceeding to the DWV test.

DWV Test Reveals Leakage or Slow Drainage at a Rough-In Joint

Probable cause: an incompletely cemented PVC joint, incorrect slope, or a fitting rotated out of the approved direction.
Confirmation: inspect the joint, slope, and fitting orientation against the approved plan using the Chapter 8 inspection method.
Correction: cut out and rebuild the failed joint following the Chapter 8 procedure; do not coat a leaking joint externally with cement or sealant.
Reinspection: repeat the DWV test after the joint has cured for the manufacturer-controlled time.

Rough-In Inspection Identifies a Deficiency

Probable cause: a support spacing, fitting pattern, slope, or clearance that does not match the approved plan or adopted code.
Confirmation: compare the identified deficiency against the approved plan and the inspector's written finding.
Correction: correct the deficiency using the controlled repair method for the affected system before requesting reinspection.
Reinspection: obtain a new rough-in inspection approval before any wall material is installed.

A Finish-Stage Fixture Fails Its Individual Test

Probable cause: the toilet, faucet, pop-up drain, or P-trap was not installed to the acceptance standard defined in its controlling chapter.
Confirmation: re-run the exact test specified in the Chapter 9, 10, or 11 acceptance standards for the affected fixture.
Correction: apply the correction procedure from the controlling chapter for that fixture; do not invent a substitute correction.
Reinspection: repeat the fixture-specific test and the overall finish leak test before requesting the finish inspection.

Finish Inspection Identifies a Deficiency

Probable cause: a fixture connection, drainage condition, or documentation item that does not meet the finish acceptance standards.
Confirmation: compare the identified deficiency against the Acceptance Standards table and the inspector's written finding.
Correction: correct the deficiency, complete the punch list entry, and retest the affected connection.
Reinspection: obtain a new finish inspection approval before closeout.

Documentation Is Incomplete at Closeout

Probable cause: a required photograph, test record, or inspection approval was not compiled during rough-in or finish stage.
Confirmation: review the Rough-In and Finish Inspection Record set against the Material Takeoff and Acceptance Standards requirements.
Correction: locate or reconstruct the missing record; if concealed piping cannot be verified, escalate to the authority having jurisdiction before closeout.
Reinspection: confirm the completed record set before handing off the project.
