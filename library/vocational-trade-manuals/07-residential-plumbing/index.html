07-Residential Plumbing

This field manual provides practical, project-based instruction for installing, repairing, and servicing residential plumbing systems. Students develop hands-on skills in water supply, drain-waste-vent systems, fixture installation, leak repair, and plumbing maintenance while completing real-world residential projects.

Overview

Residential Plumbing develops the practical skills required to work safely on residential water supply and drain systems. Students learn pipe preparation, PEX, copper, and PVC installation, fixture replacement, water heater connections, plumbing repairs, and troubleshooting before completing a capstone bathroom plumbing project representative of common residential work.

Learning Outcomes

  • Safely isolate and prepare residential plumbing systems for service.
  • Identify residential water supply, drain, waste, and vent components.
  • Install and repair PEX, copper, PVC, and common plumbing fittings.
  • Install residential plumbing fixtures, valves, and appliance connections.
  • Diagnose and repair common plumbing leaks and fixture problems.
  • Apply accepted plumbing practices for residential maintenance and repair.
  • Complete a functional residential bathroom plumbing installation.
