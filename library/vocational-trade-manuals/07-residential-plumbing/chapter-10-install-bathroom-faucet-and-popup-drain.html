Job Overview

This project qualifies you to remove a worn bathroom lavatory faucet and pop-up drain, prepare the sink surface, and install a compatible replacement faucet, supply connectors, and pop-up drain assembly that operate correctly and remain leak-free. The finished work product is one installed and tested bathroom faucet and pop-up drain assembly on one lavatory.

You will verify the faucet-hole pattern, sink material, and manufacturer requirements before purchase or installation; shut off and drain the fixture supply using the Chapter 1 sequence; remove the old faucet, pop-up linkage, and drain body; clean and inspect the sink; install the new faucet and drain hardware; connect the hot and cold supplies; assemble and adjust the pop-up linkage; and complete static and operating leak tests before final documentation.

This project builds directly on Chapter 1 (work-area shutoff and drain-down), Chapter 7 (fixture stop and flexible-connector installation), and Chapter 9 (P-trap alignment and slip-joint technique). It is a required sequence for the Chapter 20 capstone bathroom installation.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • PL-055 — Remove the old faucet and pop-up drain assembly without damaging the sink or connected piping.
  • PL-056 — Clean the fixture mounting and sealing surfaces before installing the replacement faucet and drain.
  • PL-057 — Install the faucet body square to the basin using the specified gasket, seal, or sealant and mounting hardware.
  • PL-058 — Connect the hot and cold supply connectors to the faucet and fixture stops without crossing, twisting, or kinking.
  • PL-059 — Install the pop-up drain assembly, including the drain body, linkage, and trap reconnection.
  • PL-060 — Test faucet and drain operation under static and flowing conditions and correct any leakage found.

Where This Skill Fits

Faucet and pop-up drain replacement is one of the most common residential service calls, and it depends directly on the shutoff and drain-down sequence from Chapter 1 and the flexible-connector technique from Chapter 7. The trap-alignment and slip-joint skills from Chapter 9 apply again here when the drain body is reconnected to the existing trap.

The sink-surface preparation, gasket selection, and linkage-adjustment skills taught here also apply directly to the Chapter 20 capstone, which requires an installed and tested lavatory faucet and pop-up drain as part of the finished small-bathroom system.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • verifying faucet-hole pattern, sink material, drain opening, overflow configuration, supply sizes, and deck thickness against the new faucet and drain
  • shutting off, draining, and protecting the work area using the Chapter 1 sequence
  • removing the existing faucet, supply connectors, pop-up linkage, trap connection, drain flange, and drain body
  • cleaning and inspecting the sink mounting and sealing surfaces
  • installing the new faucet, supply connectors, drain flange, drain body, and pop-up linkage
  • restoring water, flushing the faucet, and testing under static and flowing conditions

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • alter the sink material, enlarge or relocate faucet holes, or install a faucet with an incompatible hole pattern
  • replace or relocate the fixture stop or supply piping — that work follows the Chapter 7 sequence and must be completed first if the existing stop is not serviceable
  • replace or reroute the P-trap or wall drain connection beyond reconnecting the existing trap to the new drain body — trap replacement follows the Chapter 9 sequence
  • proceed with installation when the sink is cracked, delaminated, or has unsupported mounting holes

Any condition that fails verification in this chapter must be corrected, repaired, or escalated to a qualified professional before work continues.

Trade Note

Confirm the faucet-hole pattern, sink material, and manufacturer clearance requirements before removing the old faucet. A new faucet purchased without confirming hole spacing, deck thickness, or overflow compatibility with the existing sink is the most common cause of a stalled installation documented in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not disconnect any supply connector, trap nut, or mounting hardware until the fixture supply has been shut off, drained, and verified isolated using the Chapter 1 sequence, and the cabinet floor has been protected. Work started before isolation is verified must stop immediately.

Shop Tip

Photograph the underside of the sink, the supply routing, and the pop-up linkage before disconnecting anything. The photograph is the fastest reference for correct hot-cold orientation and linkage geometry when the new faucet is installed.

Common Field Fundamentals

A bathroom faucet and pop-up drain installation succeeds or fails on three points: whether the faucet actually matches the sink's hole pattern and deck thickness, whether the base and drain-flange seals are made on clean surfaces with the specified sealant, and whether the pop-up linkage is adjusted so the stopper seats and releases without binding or leaking at the pivot rod.

Sink and Mounting Surface Condition

Before installing the new faucet or drain body, inspect the sink for cracks, damaged mounting holes, delamination, or unsupported areas around the faucet or drain openings. A sink with a compromised mounting surface must not receive a new faucet or drain body until the sink condition is corrected or the sink is replaced.

Measurement Cue — Confirm the Hole Pattern Before Purchase or Removal

Measure the sink's hole spacing and count, and confirm the deck thickness at the mounting location, before assuming a replacement faucet will fit. A single-hole, three-hole 4-inch centerset, and widespread faucet are not interchangeable without matching the sink's existing hole pattern.

Critical Safety Cue — Verified Isolation Before Disconnection

Do not disconnect any supply connector until the fixture supply has been shut off, drained, and verified isolated per the Chapter 1 sequence. Confirm hot-water temperature and allow piping to cool before opening any connection. Treat a stop valve that will not close or leaks at the stem or body as a stop-work condition.

Field Safety Cue — Cabinet Protection and Working Position

Protect the cabinet floor and interior with towels or floor protection before beginning removal. Support your working position when using a basin wrench in a confined cabinet space, and keep the flashlight positioned so you can see the connection being loosened before applying force.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Gasket, Seal, and Sealant Selection

Use the exact gasket, foam seal, or plumber's putty specified by the faucet and drain manufacturer. Some faucet bases use a compression gasket that requires no additional sealant, while others require plumber's putty or a specified adhesive sealant. Using the wrong sealing method can void the warranty or cause a persistent leak at the base.

E. Compatibility Verification and Isolation

  1. Verify faucet-hole pattern, sink material, drain opening, overflow configuration, supply sizes, deck thickness, and manufacturer requirements. Record the confirmed specification on the worksheet before proceeding.
  2. Confirm that the new faucet and drain are compatible with the fixture and accessible for service. Do not proceed if the hole pattern, deck thickness, or overflow configuration does not match.
  3. Shut off, drain, and protect the work area. Complete the Chapter 1 sequence and verify isolation at the fixture before disconnecting anything.

F. Removal of the Existing Faucet and Drain

  1. Disconnect existing supply connectors and contain residual water. Direct released water into a bucket or towel-protected area.
  2. Disconnect the pop-up linkage and trap connection. Note the linkage geometry before removal, using a photograph as reference.
  3. Remove faucet mounting hardware and lift out the old faucet. Support the faucet body while loosening the final mounting nut.
  4. Remove the drain flange and drain body without damaging the basin. Use the putty knife or scraper to loosen hardened sealant before removal.

G. Surface Preparation and Inspection

  1. Clean mounting and sealing surfaces of old sealant, corrosion, and debris. Confirm the surface is dry before applying new sealant.
  2. Inspect the sink for cracks, damaged holes, delamination, or unsupported areas. Stop work and correct or replace the sink before continuing if any of these conditions are found.

H. Faucet Installation and Supply Connection

  1. Assemble and place the new faucet gasket, seal, or approved sealant as specified. Follow the exact manufacturer sequence for the selected faucet.
  2. Set the faucet square to the basin and install mounting hardware. Confirm alignment before final tightening.
  3. Tighten mounting hardware evenly without distorting or cracking the fixture. Alternate tightening across multiple fasteners where the faucet uses more than one.
  4. Install the hot and cold supply connectors without crossing, twisting, or kinking. Confirm hot-left, cold-right orientation unless the manufacturer specifies otherwise.

I. Drain Body and Pop-Up Linkage Installation

  1. Prepare the drain flange and body using the specified gasket and sealant arrangement. Confirm the gasket or sealant matches the drain manufacturer's instructions.
  2. Install and align the drain body so the pop-up opening faces the linkage. Confirm the drain body does not rotate once seated.
  3. Reconnect the trap without placing stress on the drain body. Support the trap while tightening the slip-joint nut.
  4. Assemble and adjust the lift rod, clevis, pivot rod, and stopper. Confirm the stopper seats fully in the closed position and lifts fully in the open position.

J. Restoration, Testing, and Correction

  1. Restore water slowly and flush the faucet aerator path as required. Direct the first flow through the aerator to clear installation debris.
  2. Inspect faucet-body, supply, and shutoff connections under static and flowing conditions. Observe each connection directly rather than assuming a dry fitting is sealed.
  3. Fill the basin to the overflow level and inspect the drain flange. Confirm no leakage appears at the flange under standing water.
  4. Operate the pop-up and release a full basin while inspecting the drain body and trap. Observe the connection through the complete drain cycle.
  5. Adjust stopper travel and linkage without creating leakage. Reset the clevis position if the stopper does not seat or release fully.
  6. Reinstall the aerator, clean the fixture, and perform a delayed reinspection. Check all disturbed connections again after the system has stood under pressure.
  7. Document model, connection types, and final test results. Complete every field on the Faucet and Drain Installation Worksheet.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed faucet and drain installation using the same checks regardless of the specific faucet or drain model installed.

  • Mounting check: confirm the faucet is square to the basin and mounting hardware is evenly tightened without fixture distortion.
  • Supply check: confirm hot and cold connectors are correctly oriented, untwisted, and leak-free under static pressure.
  • Drain-body check: confirm the drain body is aligned, sealed at the flange, and free of stress at the trap connection.
  • Linkage check: confirm the stopper opens and closes fully across its designed travel.
  • Flowing-test check: confirm no leakage appears at the flange, drain body, or trap during a full-basin release.
  • Documentation check: confirm the Faucet and Drain Installation Worksheet is fully completed and signed.

Complete the Faucet and Drain Installation Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 11.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Leak at the Faucet Base After Restoring Water

Probable cause: insufficient or incorrectly applied sealant or gasket, or mounting hardware that is not evenly tightened.
Confirmation: dry the base and surrounding deck, restore pressure, and observe the base directly under static conditions.
Correction: shut off and drain the supply, remove the faucet, clean the surfaces, and reapply the specified gasket or sealant before reinstalling.
Reinspection: repeat the static leak test at the faucet base.

Supply Connector Leaks at the Faucet Inlet or Fixture Stop

Probable cause: a twisted or kinked connector, an incorrect connector size, or a connection that was not fully seated before tightening.
Confirmation: inspect the connector routing and both end connections directly under static pressure.
Correction: shut off and drain the supply, straighten or replace the connector, and reconnect using backup support at the fitting.
Reinspection: repeat the static leak test at both connector ends.

Pop-Up Stopper Does Not Seat or Release Fully

Probable cause: incorrect clevis position on the pivot rod, a lift rod set to the wrong length, or a linkage component installed in the wrong orientation.
Confirmation: operate the lift rod through its full range and observe the stopper's travel against the drain-body seat.
Correction: reposition the clevis on the pivot rod and adjust the lift-rod length until the stopper seats fully closed and lifts fully open.
Reinspection: repeat the linkage operation check through several full cycles.

Drain Flange Leaks During the Full-Basin Test

Probable cause: insufficient sealant at the flange, an uneven flange seating, or a drain body that shifted during trap reconnection.
Confirmation: drain the basin, dry the underside of the sink, refill to the overflow level, and observe the flange directly.
Correction: remove the drain assembly, clean the flange and sink opening, reapply the specified sealant, and reinstall without disturbing alignment while tightening.
Reinspection: repeat the full-basin fill and release test.

Trap Connection Leaks After Drain-Body Reconnection

Probable cause: the trap arm was not fully seated in the new drain-body tailpiece, or the slip-joint nut was tightened without correct washer orientation.
Confirmation: inspect the slip-joint connection during the full-basin release and check for movement or gapping at the joint.
Correction: drain the assembly, verify washer orientation, reseat the tailpiece, and retighten the slip-joint nut within the permitted range.
Reinspection: repeat the full-basin release test and inspect the joint again after the delayed reinspection period.

New Faucet Does Not Match the Existing Hole Pattern

Probable cause: the hole pattern, spacing, or deck thickness was not verified against the new faucet before purchase.
Confirmation: re-measure the sink's hole spacing, count, and deck thickness, and compare directly against the new faucet's mounting specification.
Correction: select a faucet matched to the confirmed hole pattern and deck thickness rather than altering the sink.
Reinspection: repeat the compatibility verification before reattempting installation.

Field Terms

Deck Thickness
The measured thickness of the sink material at the mounting location, used to confirm the faucet's mounting-hardware range.
Pop-Up Opening
The position on the drain body where the pop-up linkage engages the stopper.
Clevis
The adjustable connector joining the lift rod to the pivot rod, used to set stopper travel.
Overflow Level
The reference fill height used during the basin-fill test to confirm the flange and drain-body seal under standing water.
Hot-Left, Cold-Right Orientation
The standard connection convention unless the faucet manufacturer specifies a different orientation.
Static Leak Test
A leak check performed under standing supply pressure with no fixture in operation.
Operating Leak Test
A leak check performed while water is actively flowing or draining through the fixture.
