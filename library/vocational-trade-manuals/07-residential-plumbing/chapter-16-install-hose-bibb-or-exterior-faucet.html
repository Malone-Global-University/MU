Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install one approved exterior hose connection complete with wall flashing, an interior shutoff, freeze-protection orientation where specified, and the required backflow-prevention device. The finished work product is one securely mounted, flashed, and tested hose bibb or exterior faucet that delivers water, shuts off fully, drains as designed, and passes its backflow-device check.

You will review the approved location and wall assembly; select the specified hose-bibb type without substitution; verify the penetration path is clear of structural members and other utilities; create and flash the wall opening; secure the faucet body; connect the interior supply using the joining method specified for the project; install the interior shutoff and backflow device; seal only the designated exterior joints while preserving drainage; and test the completed connection for flow, shutoff, drainage, and backflow performance.

This project depends on the shutoff, isolation, and work-area preparation skills from Chapter 1; the PEX, copper, push-fit, or compression joining methods from Chapters 4 through 6, depending on which supply method the project specifies; and the fixture-stop and connector skills from Chapter 7. It prepares you for the laundry-box rough-in in Chapter 17 and the water-heater connector work in Chapter 18, both of which depend on the same principle of connecting a fixture to an existing supply system without disturbing unrelated building systems.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • PL-091 — Select the hose-bibb location and confirm the wall assembly, climate requirement, and clearance conditions support the specified installation.
  • PL-092 — Prepare the wall penetration, including structural, utility, and flashing verification.
  • PL-093 — Connect the interior supply line to the hose bibb using the specified joining method.
  • PL-094 — Secure the exterior faucet to structural backing and integrate it with the wall flashing.
  • PL-095 — Seal the wall opening at the designated exterior joints while preserving required drainage paths.
  • PL-096 — Test exterior water flow, shutoff, drainage, and backflow-device performance.

Where This Skill Fits

An exterior hose connection is one of the few residential fixtures that combines a pressurized water connection with a building-envelope penetration. It draws directly on the wall-penetration and flashing discipline used throughout the framing and siding trades, the pipe-joining methods taught in Chapters 4 through 6, and the backflow-prevention principle that recurs at the dishwasher connection in Chapter 14 and the water-heater relief system referenced in Chapter 18.

The same sequence — verify the wall assembly, protect the penetration, flash before fastening, connect using an approved method, and test flow and backflow performance together — applies whenever a fixture must pass through an exterior wall, including future irrigation, exterior sink, or utility-sink connections outside the scope of this book.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved location, wall assembly, supply route, climate requirement, backflow requirement, shutoff, drainage, and exterior-clearance conditions
  • selecting the specified standard, frost-resistant, or other listed hose bibb without substituting a different type
  • verifying that the planned penetration avoids structural members, electrical wiring, gas piping, ducts, and other utilities
  • creating and flashing the wall penetration in shingle-lap order with the water-resistive barrier
  • connecting the interior supply using the specified PEX, copper, threaded, push-fit, or other approved method
  • installing an accessible interior shutoff and drain where required, and the required vacuum breaker or integral backflow device
  • sealing only the designated exterior perimeter joints while leaving drainage paths open
  • testing flow, shutoff, drainage, and backflow-device performance

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • substitute a different hose-bibb type, freeze-protection design, or backflow device than the one specified by the approved project
  • cut or notch a structural member, or route the penetration through electrical wiring, gas piping, or ductwork
  • seal the exterior perimeter in a way that blocks a designed drainage path for a frost-resistant unit
  • proceed with any project when a stop-work condition identified in this chapter is present and uncorrected

Any wall condition, backflow requirement, or freeze-protection detail not resolved by the approved project design must be corrected, clarified, or escalated to a qualified professional before work continues.

Trade Note

The interior shutoff and drainage arrangement for a frost-resistant hose bibb only protects the piping if the barrel is installed at the correct slight downward orientation toward the exterior and the connected hose is removed after use. A frost-resistant unit installed level or back-pitched, or left with a hose attached through cold weather, can still freeze and split even though the fixture is correctly selected.

Setup Standard

Do not permanently set the faucet body until the flashing has been integrated with the water-resistive barrier in shingle-lap order and the penetration has been confirmed clear of structural members, electrical wiring, gas piping, ducts, and other utilities. Do not restore water until the concealed-side connection has been inspected for leakage with the wall access still open.

Shop Tip

Dry-fit the faucet body and flashing together before drilling any fastener holes. Confirming the flange sits flat against the flashing and the barrel achieves its specified slope before a single screw is driven prevents a re-drilled penetration later.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every exterior fixture connection in this book follows the same four-part sequence: verify the wall and utility path, penetrate and flash before fastening, connect the supply using the specified joining method, and test flow together with the fixture's freeze-protection and backflow performance. Skipping the flashing step or sealing a drainage path closed is the most common cause of concealed water intrusion or freeze failure in an otherwise correctly connected hose bibb.

Flashing Integration and Drainage Path

Integrate the flashing with the water-resistive barrier in shingle-lap order before the faucet is permanently set, so each higher course overlaps the one below it and sheds water outward over the penetration. A frost-resistant barrel's designed drainage path — the internal slope that allows the barrel to empty after the interior shutoff closes — is separate from the exterior perimeter seal and must remain open even after the perimeter joint is sealed.

Measurement Cue — Confirm the Specified Slope Before Fastening

Use a level to confirm the frost-resistant barrel is installed at the slight downward orientation specified by the manufacturer before driving the final fasteners. A barrel installed level or back-pitched will not drain after the interior shutoff is closed, even though the correct fixture was selected.

Critical Safety Cue — Utility Conflicts at the Penetration

Verify that the planned penetration avoids structural members, electrical wiring, gas piping, ducts, and other utilities before drilling. Treat any uncertain or partially visible utility in the penetration path as a stop-work condition until it is confirmed clear by a non-destructive method.

Field Safety Cue — Ladder and Exterior Work-Area Control

Use a stable ladder or work platform, maintain three points of contact, and keep the drilling and fastening tools controlled at height. Protect the interior wall surface and any interior finish materials from drilling debris on the opposite side of the penetration.

Code Alert — Backflow and Freeze-Protection Requirements Vary

Confirm the vacuum-breaker or integral backflow-device requirement, the frost-resistant fixture requirement, and any permit or inspection condition with the authority having jurisdiction before installation. Do not assume a requirement from a different climate or jurisdiction applies to this project.

F. Location, Wall, and Utility Verification

  1. Review the approved location, wall assembly, supply route, climate requirement, backflow requirement, shutoff, drainage, and exterior-clearance conditions. Record each item on the Hose Bibb Installation Worksheet before proceeding.
  2. Select the specified standard, frost-resistant, or other listed hose bibb without substituting a different type. Confirm the model matches the approved project specification.
  3. Verify that the planned penetration avoids structural members, electrical wiring, gas piping, ducts, and other utilities. Use non-destructive verification methods before drilling.
  4. Confirm the required slight drainage orientation for a frost-resistant unit. Identify the exterior direction the barrel must slope toward before marking the penetration.

G. Work-Area Preparation

  1. Shut off, drain, and prepare the supply system. Follow the isolation, drain-down, and protection sequence established in Chapter 1 for the branch serving this connection.

H. Penetration and Flashing

  1. Mark and create the wall penetration using the approved method for the cladding, WRB, sheathing, and framing. Confirm the opening size matches the faucet body or mounting sleeve specification.
  2. Install a sleeve, mounting block, or backing where required. Set it square to the wall plane before proceeding.
  3. Integrate flashing with the WRB in shingle-lap order before the faucet is permanently set. Confirm each higher course overlaps the one below it.

I. Setting and Securing the Faucet

  1. Insert the faucet without damaging flashing or wall components. Confirm the flange or body seats flat against the integrated flashing.
  2. Secure the flange or body to structural backing using approved corrosion-resistant fasteners. Verify the specified slight drainage orientation with a level for a frost-resistant unit before final tightening.

J. Interior Supply Connection and Backflow Protection

  1. Connect the interior supply using the specified PEX, copper, threaded, push-fit, or other approved method. Follow the exact joining procedure from the corresponding chapter without blending methods.
  2. Install an accessible interior shutoff and drain where required. Confirm the shutoff is reachable without special tools.
  3. Install the required vacuum breaker, integral backflow device, or listed assembly. Confirm it matches the device specified by the approved project.

K. Sealing and Restoring Water

  1. Seal only the designated exterior perimeter joints while leaving drainage paths open. Do not seal a joint or opening that the frost-resistant barrel relies on to drain.
  2. Restore water slowly and inspect the concealed-side connection before closure. Keep the wall access open until the connection is confirmed dry under static pressure.

L. Testing and Documentation

  1. Operate the faucet through full flow and shutoff. Confirm the valve opens, delivers flow, and shuts off completely.
  2. Test the backflow device according to its instructions. Record the result on the worksheet.
  3. Verify drainage from a frost-resistant barrel after hose removal. Confirm the barrel empties within the manufacturer's expected time.
  4. Inspect wall flashing, exterior seal, mounting stability, supply connection, valve stem, and spout. Confirm no leakage or movement at any inspected point.
  5. Correct leakage or reverse slope only after re-isolating the system. Do not attempt a correction under pressure.
  6. Document valve type, backflow method, shutoff location, and test result. Complete every field on the Hose Bibb Installation Worksheet.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed hose-bibb installation using the same checks regardless of climate or fixture type.

  • Location check: confirm the penetration matches the approved location and is clear of structural members and other utilities.
  • Flashing check: confirm flashing is integrated with the WRB in shingle-lap order and the faucet flange seats flat against it.
  • Slope check: confirm the frost-resistant barrel is installed at the specified slight downward orientation.
  • Connection check: confirm the interior supply connection passes the leak test specific to its joining method.
  • Backflow check: confirm the listed backflow device is installed correctly and passes its functional test.
  • Seal check: confirm sealant is applied only at designated joints and drainage paths remain open.

Complete the Hose Bibb Installation Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 17.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Water Enters the Wall Cavity Near the Penetration

Probable cause: flashing was not integrated with the WRB in shingle-lap order, or sealant was applied over a joint that should have shed water by lap order instead.
Confirmation: inspect the flashing sequence at the penetration and check for water staining on the interior side of the wall.
Correction: remove the faucet, re-integrate the flashing in the correct shingle-lap order, and reset the faucet before resealing only the designated joints.
Reinspection: repeat the concealed-side inspection under static pressure before closing the wall.

A Frost-Resistant Barrel Does Not Drain After Shutoff

Probable cause: the barrel was installed level or back-pitched, or a hose was left connected, defeating the drainage path.
Confirmation: check the barrel orientation with a level and confirm no hose or shutoff-bypass valve is attached.
Correction: re-isolate the system, correct the mounting orientation to the specified slight downward slope, and remove any attached hose.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage test after hose removal.

The Backflow Device Fails Its Functional Test

Probable cause: an incompatible or incorrectly installed device, or a device installed with an unintended bypass around it.
Confirmation: inspect the device installation against its manufacturer instructions and check for any bypass path.
Correction: re-isolate the system, correct the installation or replace the device with the specified listed unit, and eliminate any bypass.
Reinspection: repeat the backflow-device functional test.

The Interior Supply Connection Leaks Under Static Pressure

Probable cause: a connection defect specific to the joining method used — an under-crimped PEX fitting, an incomplete solder joint, or an under-tightened compression nut.
Confirmation: isolate and depressurize, then inspect the specific connection using the failure-point guidance from the chapter governing that joining method.
Correction: follow that chapter's approved correction procedure for the specific method; do not coat the leak externally with sealant.
Reinspection: repeat the leak test for that connection before restoring water to the faucet.

The Faucet Body Moves Under Normal Operating Force

Probable cause: the flange or body was not fastened to structural backing, or the mounting sleeve was not seated square to the wall plane.
Confirmation: inspect the fastener engagement into structural backing and check the sleeve or backing-block alignment.
Correction: re-isolate the system, add or correct the backing, and refasten the flange to the corrected structural connection.
Reinspection: repeat the mounting-stability check under normal operating force.
