Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to integrate the complete residential framing sequence into one finished structure. Starting from the inspected 8-foot by 10-foot garden-storage shed floor platform completed in Book 1, you will lay out, assemble, raise, plumb, brace, and sheath every wall; frame and connect the door and window rough openings; frame the approved gable roof using either the site-built rafter method from Chapter 14 or the truss method from Chapter 15; install roof sheathing; and complete a full structural inspection and closeout package before handoff to Book 5 roofing work.

The finished work product is a complete, sheathed, inspected small shed frame — floor platform verified, walls framed and connected, rough openings correct, wall sheathing and structural connectors installed, roof framing and roof deck complete, punch list closed, and formal framing closeout documentation signed.

This project sits at the end of Book 4. It does not teach new individual skills; it requires you to apply Chapters 1 through 19 in the correct order on one real structure, under the same acceptance standards used in each of those chapters, without a separate design file beyond the approved shed framing plan identified in the outline.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FR-115 — Verify the existing Book 1 floor platform against the approved capstone specification and establish exterior wall lines, reference corners, and door and window locations before wall framing begins.
  • FR-116 — Lay out, assemble flat, square, raise, plumb, and permanently connect each wall section, including the plan-specified corners, intersections, and rough openings.
  • FR-117 — Install wall sheathing and structural connectors — hold-downs, straps, and ties — according to the approved fastening and connector schedule.
  • FR-118 — Frame the selected roof system, either site-built rafters or prefabricated trusses, without combining the two methods, and install roof sheathing to the approved orientation and fastening schedule.
  • FR-119 — Verify the continuous load path from roof framing through the walls to the floor platform, complete a structural punch list, and correct every authorized deficiency.
  • FR-120 — Complete required framing inspections, compile photographic and written closeout records, and execute the formal framing closeout and handoff to Residential Roofing and Building Envelopes.

Where This Skill Fits

Every chapter in this book feeds this project. The reference-line method from Chapter 1 establishes the shed's wall lines. The wall-framing, corner, opening, and header methods from Chapters 6 through 10 build each wall. The raising, bracing, and sheathing methods from Chapters 11 and 12 stabilize and connect the wall system. The ceiling-joist, rafter or truss, and roof-sheathing methods from Chapters 13 through 16 complete the roof. No new technique is introduced here; the project exists to prove that you can sequence and integrate these techniques correctly on a single structure without supervision.

The completed shed frame becomes the starting condition for Book 5, Residential Roofing and Building Envelopes, which begins with the roof deck and wall sheathing produced in this project.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • verifying the existing Book 1 floor platform against the approved capstone specification and correcting only authorized deficiencies
  • laying out, framing, raising, plumbing, bracing, and permanently connecting all exterior walls of the shed
  • framing and verifying the plan-specified door and window rough openings
  • installing wall sheathing and the required structural connectors
  • framing the selected roof system and installing roof sheathing
  • completing a full punch list, structural inspection, and formal framing closeout

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • rebuild, redesign, or relocate the Book 1 floor platform; the platform is verified and used, not reconstructed
  • combine the site-built rafter method and the prefabricated truss method on the same roof without an engineered transition detail
  • modify or remove temporary bracing before the permanent wall and roof load path is complete and verified
  • conceal any wall, opening, sheathing, or roof-framing work before the required structural inspection is complete
  • proceed past a stop-work condition — foundation or platform movement, missing bearing, damaged connectors, unapproved field changes, unstable assemblies, excessive wind, or unsafe roof conditions — without documented direction

Any condition on the existing floor platform or in the approved shed framing plan that cannot be resolved within this project's authorized corrections must stop work in the affected area until direction is obtained from the responsible design professional or authority having jurisdiction.

Trade Note

This capstone does not rebuild the Book 1 floor platform. Treat the platform the way Chapter 1 treated the foundation: inspect it, verify it against the plan, correct only what you are authorized to correct, and use it as the fixed starting condition for every wall line and dimension in this project.

Setup Standard

Do not begin wall layout until the existing floor platform is inspected against Step 3 and any authorized correction from Step 4 is complete. Do not raise any wall until it has been assembled, squared, and temporarily restrained per Steps 8–14. Do not remove temporary wall or roof bracing until the permanent load path is verified per Step 24 and Step 33.

Shop Tip

Build and label all four walls flat on the platform before raising any of them. Squaring and marking every wall while it is flat, using the same diagonal-check method from Chapter 1, is faster and more accurate than correcting a wall after it has been raised and braced.

Common Field Fundamentals

This capstone follows the same sequence used throughout the book: verify the starting condition and documents, establish and mark reference lines, build and square components, raise and connect the assembly, sheath and connect the structural system, and inspect and document before moving to the next stage. The only difference here is scale — the same sequence now covers an entire small structure rather than one component.

Load-Path Continuity

The single technical thread through this project is load-path continuity: every connector, strap, and fastening schedule installed in Chapters 7 through 16 exists to carry roof load through the walls to the floor platform verified in Step 3. Verify this continuity at each stage rather than only at the end, so a missing connector is caught while the framing is still open rather than after sheathing conceals it.

Measurement Cue — One Controlling Corner

Lay out every exterior wall line, door and window location, and roof reference from the single controlling corner identified in Step 5. Do not re-establish a new reference corner partway through the project. Re-striking a line from a different corner is the same cumulative-error risk identified in Chapter 1 and is the most common cause of an out-of-square capstone shed.

Critical Safety Cue — Bracing Before Load Path Is Verified

Do not remove or modify temporary wall bracing before Step 24 confirms the permanent wall system is complete, and do not remove temporary roof bracing before Step 33 confirms the continuous load path from roof to floor platform. Removing bracing early can allow a partially connected wall or roof assembly to shift or collapse.

Field Safety Cue — Raising, Roof, and Sheathing Work

Maintain cleared lift paths, guarded floor and roof openings, and controlled exclusion zones during wall raising, roof framing, and sheathing, per the requirements taught in Chapters 11, 15, and 16. Stop work for excessive wind, unsafe roof conditions, or loss of control of any raised or lifted assembly.

Code Alert — Permit and Inspection Requirements Vary

Accessory-structure permit thresholds, required inspections, and setback rules vary by jurisdiction and by shed size and use. Verify the current permit and inspection requirements with the authority having jurisdiction before beginning wall framing, and again before concealing any wall or roof framing.

E. Platform Verification and Wall Layout (Steps 1–9)

  1. Obtain the approved shed framing plan, elevations, wall schedules, opening schedule, roof design, connector schedule, sheathing schedule, and local permit requirements. Confirm the revision date of each document against the project's document-control log.
  2. Confirm that the existing floor platform matches the approved capstone dimensions and support conditions. Compare the platform's recorded dimensions and diagonal check against the current plan.
  3. Inspect skids or supports, floor framing, blocking, subfloor, fasteners, square, level, damage, moisture condition, and site drainage. Log every observed condition with its location referenced to the plan.
  4. Correct only authorized floor-platform deficiencies and obtain approval for structural conflicts. Do not rebuild or relocate the platform; refer any deficiency outside this project's authorized corrections to the responsible design professional.
  5. Establish and mark exterior wall lines, reference corners, door and window locations, and roof orientation. Strike every line from the single controlling corner identified in the approved plan, using the baseline method taught in Chapter 1.
  6. Prepare the project material takeoff, cutting sequence, staging plan, lifting plan, bracing plan, fall-protection plan, and inspection schedule. Confirm each plan is specific to this structure and site.
  7. Select, inspect, label, and stage wall, sheathing, connector, and roof-framing materials. Reject or quarantine damaged, warped, or incorrectly identified members using the inspection standard from Chapter 2.
  8. Lay out the first wall plates from the controlling corner. Mark reference ends and transfer them square across both plates.
  9. Mark studs, corners, intersections, door openings, window openings, header locations, and hold-down or connector framing. Label each position using the layout method from Chapters 6 through 9.

F. Wall Assembly and Raising (Steps 10–20)

  1. Cut and label wall components using controlled references. Cut plates, studs, and header components to the lengths specified by the approved plan.
  2. Assemble the first wall flat on the platform. Position plates, studs, and opening members per the marked layout.
  3. Install the plan-specified corner, opening, backing, and connector components. Use the corner and opening methods from Chapters 8 and 9 and the header method from Chapter 10.
  4. Square the wall and install temporary restraint. Measure diagonals and adjust the frame square before fastening is finalized; install temporary diagonal bracing to hold square.
  5. Repeat the controlled assembly process for each remaining wall. Label each completed wall by its plan position before staging it for raising.
  6. Prepare the raising area, workers, equipment, braces, and exclusion zones. Confirm the lift plan from Step 6 is staged and the area is cleared per Chapter 11.
  7. Raise the first wall and secure it immediately with approved temporary bracing. Use the approved worker count and lifting method; do not release the wall until temporary restraint is installed.
  8. Raise adjacent walls in the planned sequence. Bring each new wall into position without disturbing the bracing on previously raised walls.
  9. Connect corners and intersections, align bottom plates, and install approved floor-to-wall connections. Fasten intersections and complete the platform connection per the approved schedule.
  10. Plumb and straighten the complete wall system. Check plumb at ends, openings, and intersections; adjust bracing as needed.
  11. Install top-plate laps, straps, ties, hold-downs, and other structural connectors. Complete every connector shown on the connector schedule before proceeding to sheathing.

G. Openings, Sheathing, and Wall Closeout (Steps 21–24)

  1. Verify door and window rough openings for size, square, plumb, level, and scheduled location. Measure each opening at top, middle, bottom, and both sides per the method taught in Chapter 9.
  2. Install wall sheathing using the approved orientation, gaps, and fastening schedule. Follow the panel-layout and fastening method from Chapter 12.
  3. Complete sheathing around openings without cutting required structural corners or boundary elements. Preserve every shear-wall boundary and connector location identified on the plan.
  4. Verify that the permanent wall system is complete before modifying temporary bracing. Confirm every connector, strap, and sheathing panel required by the plan is installed before any temporary brace is adjusted or removed.

H. Roof Framing and Roof Sheathing (Steps 25–31)

  1. Install ceiling joists, rafter ties, or other roof-support framing required by the selected roof design. Follow the layout and fastening method from Chapter 13.
  2. Frame the approved simple gable roof using either the supplied site-built rafter design or the supplied truss design. Confirm which system the approved plan specifies before staging roof materials.
  3. Do not combine the rafter and truss methods or substitute one for the other. If the plan does not clearly specify one system, stop work and obtain direction before proceeding.
  4. Install ridge, rafters, trusses, gable framing, blocking, outlookers, clips, ties, and permanent bracing required by the selected system. Follow Chapter 14 for a rafter roof or Chapter 15 for a truss roof.
  5. Check roof pitch, ridge line, eave line, bearing, spacing, plumb, and symmetry. Sight and string the ridge and eave lines per the method taught in the selected roof chapter.
  6. Install roof sheathing with the required orientation, gaps, edge support, and fastening schedule. Follow the panel-layout and fastening method from Chapter 16.
  7. Cut only the approved roof openings and preserve required ventilation and structural clearances. Confirm each opening location against the plan before cutting.

I. Load-Path Verification, Punch List, and Structural Closeout (Steps 32–40)

  1. Inspect all wall, opening, sheathing, roof, and connector work against the plans. Compare every installed component against its schedule.
  2. Verify the continuous load path from roof framing through walls to the floor platform and supports. Confirm every connector identified on the connector schedule is installed and seated at each stage of the load path.
  3. Prepare a punch list covering dimensions, plumb, level, square, alignment, panel gaps, fasteners, connectors, bracing, damage, and housekeeping. Reference each item to its location and the responsible correction.
  4. Correct every authorized deficiency and obtain design direction for all structural conflicts. Re-verify each corrected item against the applicable acceptance standard.
  5. Complete required framing and structural inspections before concealment. Confirm inspection approval is recorded before any wall or roof framing is enclosed.
  6. Photograph the floor platform, walls, openings, connectors, sheathing, roof framing, and completed structure. Reference each photograph to its plan location on the closeout record.
  7. Compile material records, field measurements, connector records, approved changes, inspection approvals, and correction records. Attach all Chapter 1 through Chapter 19 records used to complete this project where applicable.
  8. Protect the roof deck and exposed framing from weather until Book 5 roofing work begins. Install or confirm weather protection per the approved sequence.
  9. Complete the formal framing closeout and handoff to Residential Roofing and Building Envelopes. Sign the framing-closeout record and confirm every required entry from Sections E through I is complete.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed shed frame using the same checks regardless of which wall or roof area they cover.

  • Platform check: confirm the verified floor platform matches the approved capstone dimensions and support conditions, with every authorized correction documented.
  • Wall check: confirm every wall is plumb, straight, square, connected, and matches the approved dimensions, corners, and intersections.
  • Opening check: confirm every door and window rough opening matches its scheduled dimensions, square, plumb, and level.
  • Sheathing and connector check: confirm wall and roof sheathing and all structural connectors match the approved orientation, gap, fastening, and schedule.
  • Roof check: confirm the selected roof system matches the approved pitch, ridge height, spacing, bearing, and sheathing requirements.
  • Load-path check: confirm a continuous, unbroken connection from roof framing through wall connectors to the floor platform.
  • Closeout check: confirm the punch list is closed and the framing-closeout record is signed before handoff to Book 5.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Wall System Is Out of Square After Raising

Probable cause: a wall was raised or braced before it was squared flat, or bracing was disturbed during an adjacent wall's raising.
Confirmation: re-measure diagonals on each wall section and check bracing anchorage and angle against the approved bracing plan.
Correction: loosen the affected bracing, re-square the wall using the diagonal method from Chapter 1, and reinstall bracing before continuing.
Reinspection: repeat the wall-system plumb, straightness, and square check across all connected walls.

Roof Ridge or Bearing Does Not Match the Selected Design

Probable cause: the pattern rafter or truss placement was not verified before additional members were set, or wall-top alignment shifted after ceiling framing began.
Confirmation: re-check wall-top alignment against Step 19, and re-verify the pattern rafter or first truss against the approved roof design.
Correction: correct the pattern or first-set member before proceeding, per the correction method in Chapter 14 or Chapter 15; do not field-modify truss members.
Reinspection: repeat the ridge, bearing, spacing, and symmetry check across the full roof system.

A Load-Path Connector Is Missing or Unverified

Probable cause: a connector scheduled at a wall, opening, or roof-to-wall junction was not installed before that area was sheathed or concealed.
Confirmation: compare the installed connector schedule against the approved schedule at each load-path stage identified in Step 33.
Correction: expose the affected area only as required, install the missing connector per the approved schedule, and re-close the affected sheathing per the approved repair method.
Reinspection: repeat the load-path check for the full structure before completing the punch list.

Rough Opening Dimensions Do Not Match the Schedule

Probable cause: king stud, jack stud, or header placement was measured from an inconsistent reference, or the wall shifted after the opening was framed but before it was measured.
Confirmation: re-measure the opening at top, middle, bottom, and both sides per the method in Chapter 9, and confirm the wall remains plumb and square at that location.
Correction: correct authorized dimensional or alignment errors before sheathing; do not conceal an opening outside its scheduled tolerance without a documented decision.
Reinspection: repeat the opening-dimension check after correction and before sheathing that wall section.

Punch List Item Recurs After Correction

Probable cause: the correction addressed the visible symptom but not the underlying cause, such as a loose brace, unverified bearing, or an unresolved wall-square condition upstream of the item.
Confirmation: trace the deficiency to its source using the acceptance standards in Section D rather than re-correcting only the reported symptom.
Correction: correct the underlying cause and re-verify the originally reported item along with any related items sharing the same cause.
Reinspection: close the punch-list item only after the underlying cause and the reported symptom both pass reinspection.

Cleanup

This project ends with a complete, sheathed, inspected shed frame protected from weather and a signed framing-closeout record, not with unresolved punch-list items or exposed framing.

  • Remove offcuts, sawdust, loose fasteners, and packaging from the wall and roof work areas
  • Confirm all temporary bracing that remains in place is labeled and scheduled for removal only after Book 5 permanent bracing or sheathing requirements are met
  • Confirm the roof deck and exposed framing are protected from weather per Step 39
  • Store the signed framing-closeout record, punch list, and supporting photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open item and its required decision to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Book 5 roofing work begins

Field Terms

Controlling Corner
The single platform corner all exterior wall lines and diagonal checks are taken from in this project.
Selected Roof System
Either the site-built rafter design or the prefabricated truss design specified by the approved plan; the two are not combined.
Load Path
The continuous structural connection from roof framing, through wall framing and connectors, to the floor platform.
Punch List
The itemized list of deficiencies identified during final inspection, each with a required decision and correction.
Framing Closeout
The signed record confirming all required inspections, corrections, and documentation are complete before handoff.
Temporary Bracing
Bracing installed to hold a wall or roof assembly stable until the permanent structural system is complete.
Structural Connector
A hold-down, strap, tie, clip, or hanger that transfers load between framing members per the approved schedule.
Rough Opening
The framed opening dimension for a door or window unit, as distinct from the unit's nominal size.
Diaphragm
A sheathed floor, wall, or roof assembly that resists lateral load as a structural unit.
Handoff
The documented transfer of a completed, inspected framing structure to the next stage of work, here to Book 5 roofing.
