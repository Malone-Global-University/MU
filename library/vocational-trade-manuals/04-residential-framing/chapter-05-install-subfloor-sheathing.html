Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install the approved structural subfloor panels over a completed and inspected floor-joist system, producing a flat, bonded, fastened, and gapped floor diaphragm ready for wall layout. The finished work product is a complete subfloor deck installed to the approved panel type, orientation, adhesive, and fastening schedule, with every panel edge supported, every joint staggered, and every fastener seated correctly.

You will review the subfloor-panel and fastening specification, confirm the framing below has passed inspection, clean and verify the joist tops, plan panel courses and joints, cut and dry-fit panels, apply approved adhesive within its working time, fasten each panel to the specified edge and field schedule, frame required openings, and inspect the completed deck before it carries wall-framing loads.

This project follows directly from Project 04, which installed the blocking, bridging, and reinforcement that restrain the joist system. Every wall framed in Chapters 6 through 12 is built and raised on the subfloor installed here, so panel flatness, fastening, and diaphragm continuity established in this project carry forward into every later wall.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FR-025 — Lay out subfloor panel courses, joints, and openings from the approved orientation and reference line.
  • FR-026 — Apply approved construction adhesive within its temperature, open-time, and bead requirements.
  • FR-027 — Fasten subfloor panels using the approved edge and field fastening schedule.
  • FR-028 — Stagger panel end joints and maintain supported edges across courses.
  • FR-029 — Maintain the required perimeter and panel-edge expansion gaps.
  • FR-030 — Inspect the completed subfloor for fastening, gaps, damage, and flatness, and correct authorized deficiencies.

Where This Skill Fits

The subfloor deck installed here is the working platform for every wall-framing project in this book and the structural floor diaphragm that transfers lateral loads from the walls above to the floor framing below. A missed adhesive bead, an unsupported panel edge, or a closed expansion gap installed here can surface later as a squeak, a soft spot, or a panel that buckles once the structure is enclosed.

The panel-layout and fastening discipline taught here — staggered joints, supported edges, and a consistent fastening schedule — is the same discipline used again for wall sheathing in Chapter 12 and roof sheathing in Chapter 16.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved subfloor-panel type, thickness, span rating, orientation, adhesive, and fastening schedule
  • cleaning and verifying the joist framing below before panels are installed
  • planning panel courses, staggered joints, supported edges, and openings
  • cutting, dry-fitting, adhering, and fastening subfloor panels to the approved schedule
  • framing approved floor openings for stairs, ducts, plumbing, chimneys, and access
  • inspecting and correcting the completed subfloor before wall layout begins

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select subfloor panel type, thickness, span rating, adhesive, or fastening schedule without the approved project specification
  • cut a floor opening at a location or dimension not shown on the approved plan
  • leave an unsupported partial panel or narrow strip outside the approved layout
  • begin wall layout or apply framing loads to the subfloor before the completed-deck inspection is passed

Any condition suggesting damaged joists, unsupported panel edges, or a subfloor product not matching the approved specification must stop work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Every subfloor panel edge must land on framing or an approved edge-support detail. Marking the full joist and blocking layout on the first course before cutting any panel is the fastest way to confirm every edge in the deck will be supported before adhesive is applied.

Setup Standard

Do not install any subfloor panel until joist spacing, blocking, rim restraint, and the floor-framing inspection required after Project 04 are confirmed complete (Step 2). Panels installed before that confirmation must be treated as unverified and the framing beneath them re-checked once the inspection is confirmed.

Shop Tip

Dry-fit the first full course of panels before applying any adhesive. Confirming panel breaks, opening locations, and edge support across the entire first course avoids cutting a panel to fit an adhesive bead that has already begun to skin.

Panel Orientation, Staggering, and Gaps

Subfloor panels are installed with their long dimension perpendicular to the joists unless the approved panel system specifies another orientation. Staggering end joints between courses distributes panel breaks across the deck so no long continuous joint line forms, and keeping every edge on framing prevents deflection or damage at unsupported joints. The required perimeter and end-joint gap accommodates panel expansion; closing that gap during installation can cause the deck to buckle after the panels absorb moisture.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Edge Support Before Cutting

Before cutting any panel, confirm against the joist and blocking layout that every edge of the panel, once placed, will land on a joist, rim board, or approved blocking. A panel edge that falls between framing members must be relocated by adjusting the course layout, not left unsupported.

Critical Safety Cue — Guard Every Floor Opening

Guard or cover every floor opening, including stair, duct, plumbing, and chimney openings, continuously during subfloor installation. Do not leave an opening unguarded during a work break, and do not remove a guard to fit a panel without immediately restoring protection before leaving the area.

Field Safety Cue — Adhesive and Open-Joist Footing

Wet construction adhesive on joist tops or subfloor panels is a slip hazard. Keep foot traffic off freshly adhered panels until they are fastened, and maintain controlled footing on open joist bays until panels are placed and secured.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Adhesive Open Time

Apply construction adhesive only within the temperature range, open-time, and bead-size requirements stated in the manufacturer's instructions. Adhesive that has begun to skin before the panel is placed does not provide the bond required for the diaphragm; discard and reapply rather than placing a panel over skinned adhesive.

E. Framing Verification and Course Planning (Steps 1–7)

  1. Review the subfloor-panel type, thickness, span rating, orientation, edge profile, adhesive, fastening schedule, panel-gap, and diaphragm requirements. Record each value on the subfloor installation record before staging panels.
  2. Confirm joist spacing, blocking, rim restraint, openings, and framing inspection are complete. Verify the Project 04 inspection sign-off before placing any panel.
  3. Clean joist tops of mud, ice, standing water, debris, protruding fasteners, and incompatible coatings. Sweep and inspect the full joist run before staging panels over it.
  4. Verify joist crowns, elevations, and alignment before panels conceal the framing. Sight the joist tops with a straightedge and correct any deviation authorized under Project 04's acceptance standards before proceeding.
  5. Stage panels flat, dry, supported, and close enough for controlled handling. Keep staged panels off the ground on dunnage and covered from weather until placed.
  6. Establish the first panel reference line perpendicular to the joists unless the approved system requires another orientation. Strike the reference line with the chalk line from the building's primary reference corner.
  7. Plan full-panel placement, staggered end joints, supported edges, openings, and final-row cuts. Sketch the course layout on the installation record before cutting the first panel.

F. First Panel Placement and Adhesive Bonding (Steps 8–14)

  1. Confirm that every panel edge will bear on framing or approved edge support. Check the planned course layout from Step 7 against the joist and blocking layout before cutting.
  2. Measure and mark the first panel from a defined factory edge. Use the same factory edge as the reference for every subsequent measurement on that panel.
  3. Cut panels using supported work methods and protect the finished face and tongue-and-groove edges. Support the panel fully during cutting to prevent breakout at the cut edge.
  4. Dry-fit the first panel and verify square to the floor framing. Check the panel against the framing square before applying adhesive.
  5. Apply only the approved construction adhesive within its temperature, open-time, and bead requirements. Apply the bead to the joist tops the panel will bear on, following the manufacturer's placement pattern.
  6. Place the panel before the adhesive skins and seat its edges without damaging the tongue. Use a panel-edge pry bar or clamps to close tongue-and-groove joints without forcing the required gap closed.
  7. Maintain the required perimeter and end-joint gaps. Use gap spacers or the manufacturer's stated gap method at every panel end and edge.

G. Fastening, Subsequent Courses, and Openings (Steps 15–19)

  1. Fasten the first panel using the approved edge and field schedule. Drive fasteners in the sequence and spacing recorded on the installation record.
  2. Install subsequent panels in staggered courses and maintain joint support. Confirm each new panel's end joint is offset from the adjacent course by the approved distance before fastening.
  3. Use approved panel-edge tools or blocking to close tongue-and-groove joints without over-tightening required gaps. Recheck the gap after each panel is seated.
  4. Cut and frame openings for stairs, ducts, plumbing, chimneys, and access only from approved dimensions. Mark each opening from the plan before cutting and guard it immediately after the cut is made.
  5. Do not leave unsupported partial panels or narrow strips outside the approved layout. Adjust the course layout so every final-row cut still lands on supported framing.

H. Inspection, Correction, and Closeout (Steps 20–24)

  1. Inspect fastener type, spacing, seating, missed framing, overdriving, and panel damage during installation. Check each course as it is completed rather than waiting until the full deck is installed.
  2. Check the completed surface for high joints, low areas, squeaks, delamination, damage, and unsafe openings. Walk the full deck and check suspect joints with a straightedge.
  3. Correct failed fasteners, unsupported edges, damaged panels, adhesive gaps, and layout errors before wall framing. Use the approved repair method for the specific defect found; do not conceal a defect under later framing.
  4. Cover or guard all floor openings. Confirm every opening guard is complete and secure before leaving the area.
  5. Record panel product, orientation, adhesive, fastening, gaps, repairs, and inspection results. Complete the subfloor installation record before wall layout begins.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed subfloor using the same checks regardless of which section of the floor it covers.

  • Documentation check: confirm panel product, adhesive, and fastening schedule match the approved specification and are recorded on the installation record.
  • Support check: confirm every panel edge bears on framing or approved blocking with no unsupported edges or narrow strips.
  • Joint check: confirm end joints are staggered by the approved offset and no continuous joint line runs across multiple courses.
  • Gap check: confirm perimeter and end-joint gaps match the manufacturer's requirement at every panel.
  • Fastening check: confirm fastener type, spacing, and seating match the approved edge and field schedule.
  • Surface check: confirm the deck is free of high or low joints, damage, and unsafe or unguarded openings.

Complete the Subfloor Installation Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning wall layout in Chapter 6.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Panel Edge Deflects Underfoot

Probable cause: the panel edge was not supported by a joist, rim board, or approved blocking, or the panel was not fully seated before fastening.
Confirmation: locate the deflecting edge against the joist and blocking layout and confirm whether framing is present beneath it.
Correction: if framing is missing, install approved blocking beneath the edge before continuing; if framing is present but the panel is unseated, remove the affected fasteners, reseat the panel, and refasten to the approved schedule.
Reinspection: repeat the support check and walk the deck again to confirm the deflection is corrected.

Panel Joint Buckles or Peaks After Installation

Probable cause: the required perimeter or end-joint gap was closed during installation, not leaving room for panel expansion.
Confirmation: measure the gap at the affected joint and compare it with the manufacturer's stated requirement.
Correction: log the discrepancy and obtain direction from the design professional or panel manufacturer; do not force the joint flat by adding fasteners without addressing the closed gap.
Reinspection: repeat the gap check across the full deck once the correction is applied.

Deck Squeaks Under Foot Traffic

Probable cause: a fastener missed the framing member, backed out, or the adhesive bond failed at that location.
Confirmation: walk the deck to locate the squeak, then inspect the nearest fasteners for missed framing, backing out, or overdriving, and check for a gap between the panel and joist.
Correction: drive an additional approved fastener into solid framing near the affected location, following the approved schedule; do not add adhesive from above an installed panel unless the manufacturer's repair instructions permit it.
Reinspection: retest the location under foot traffic after the correction.

Adhesive Did Not Bond

Probable cause: the adhesive skinned before the panel was placed, the bead size or placement did not match the manufacturer's instructions, or the application temperature was outside the approved range.
Confirmation: inspect the adhesive condition at the affected joint and compare the application conditions against the manufacturer's data.
Correction: log the discrepancy and obtain direction from the design professional or manufacturer; do not conceal an unbonded panel without an approved correction, which may include additional fastening or panel replacement.
Reinspection: repeat the adhesive-bond check at the corrected location.

Opening Was Cut in the Wrong Location or Size

Probable cause: the opening was marked from an incorrect reference point or measured before verifying the approved plan dimensions.
Confirmation: re-measure the opening against the approved plan and its stated reference point.
Correction: log the discrepancy and obtain direction from the design professional; do not enlarge or relocate a structural opening without an approved revision.
Reinspection: repeat the opening-dimension check once the documented decision is incorporated, and confirm the opening guard remains in place.

Final-Row Panel Leaves an Unsupported Strip

Probable cause: the course layout was not planned before cutting, leaving a narrow strip whose edge falls between framing members.
Confirmation: check the final-row strip against the joist and blocking layout to confirm whether its edges land on framing.
Correction: log the discrepancy and adjust the course layout, which may require re-planning panel breaks from an earlier course, or install approved blocking beneath the unsupported edge.
Reinspection: repeat the support check for the corrected row before fastening.

Cleanup

This project ends with a complete, inspected subfloor deck, not with adhesive, offcuts, or unguarded openings left in place.

  • Clear sawdust, offcuts, fastener debris, and unused adhesive containers from the completed deck
  • Remove excess adhesive from the deck surface before it cures
  • Confirm every floor opening guard is complete, secure, and clearly marked before leaving the site
  • Store the subfloor installation record and supporting photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open discrepancy and its required decision to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Chapter 6 begins

Field Terms

The line struck perpendicular to the joists that the first subfloor panel course is aligned to.
Panel Course
One row of subfloor panels installed end to end across the floor framing.
Staggered End Joint
A panel end joint offset from the end joints in the adjacent course by the approved offset distance.
Supported Edge
A panel edge that bears fully on a joist, rim board, or approved blocking.
Panel Gap
The specified space left at panel ends and edges to allow for moisture-related panel movement.
Edge Fastener Zone
The panel-perimeter fastening zone governed by the approved edge spacing.
Field Fastener Zone
The panel-interior fastening zone governed by the approved field spacing.
Floor Diaphragm
The structural assembly formed by the fastened, bonded subfloor deck that transfers lateral loads to the framing below.
Subfloor Installation Record
The document confirming panel product, orientation, adhesive, fastening, gaps, and inspection results.
Stop-Work Condition
A defined condition, such as an unsupported edge or an unbonded joint, that requires work to stop until direction is obtained.
