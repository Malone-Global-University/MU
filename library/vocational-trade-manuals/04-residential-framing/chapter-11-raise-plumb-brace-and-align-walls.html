Job Overview

This project qualifies you to raise the framed wall sections produced in Chapters 6 through 9, secure each wall the moment it is upright, plumb and align the completed wall system along its top plates, connect corners and intersections, and install the temporary bracing that carries every wall safely to the point where permanent sheathing and connectors take over. The finished work product is a connected, plumb, straight, and safely braced wall system, documented on a wall-raising and bracing record.

You will review the wall-raising sequence and lift plan, inspect each wall section before it leaves the deck, clear the lift path and guard floor openings, raise walls in the planned order with immediate temporary restraint, align the bottom plates and complete base connections, plumb each wall at controlled reference points, install diagonal bracing to approved anchors, connect corners and intersections without removing primary bracing, establish a string-line reference along the wall tops, straighten the system to that reference, and inspect and document brace locations, measurements, and corrections.

This project sits immediately after the individual wall sections in Chapters 6 through 9 are assembled, squared, and ready to raise, and immediately before wall sheathing and structural connectors are installed in Chapter 12. Every hold-down, strap, and sheathing panel installed afterward depends on the plumb, aligned, and stable wall system produced here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FR-061 — Raise framed wall sections using the approved worker count, lifting method, and controlled lift path.
  • FR-062 — Plumb wall sections at the approved reference points and confirm plumb before removing hold restraint.
  • FR-063 — Install temporary diagonal bracing from specified wall points to approved anchors and maintain it until permanent stability is achieved.
  • FR-064 — Align wall tops to a common string-line or approved reference and adjust bracing to straighten the wall system.
  • FR-065 — Fasten intersecting walls, corner studs, and plate joints according to the approved connection schedule.
  • FR-066 — Inspect wall stability, plumb, alignment, and bracing, and document measurements, corrections, and release conditions.

Where This Skill Fits

The wall sections built flat in Chapters 6 through 9 are only as good as the raising, plumbing, and bracing work that turns them into a stable system. A wall raised without immediate restraint, plumbed at the wrong reference points, or braced to an unanchored point carries risk into every later step: sheathing fastened to an out-of-plumb wall in Chapter 12, ceiling joists seated on an unaligned top plate in Chapter 13, and roof framing set on a wall line that was never straightened in Chapters 14 through 16.

The string-line and diagonal-brace methods used here to align and stabilize the wall system are the same methods used again in Chapter 20 when the capstone shed walls are raised on the verified Book 1 floor platform.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved wall-raising sequence, wall weights, crew assignments, lifting equipment, wind limits, and temporary-bracing plan
  • inspecting each wall section for completed framing, square, fasteners, openings, connectors, and temporary lifting restraint before it is raised
  • clearing the lift path, guarding floor openings, and establishing exclusion zones
  • raising walls in the planned sequence and installing immediate temporary restraint
  • aligning bottom plates, completing base connections, and plumbing each wall at controlled reference points
  • installing diagonal bracing to specified anchors, connecting intersections, establishing a string-line reference, and straightening the wall system
  • inspecting and documenting brace locations, wall measurements, corrections, and release conditions

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • raise a wall section that has not passed the pre-raise inspection required in this project's procedure
  • remove primary temporary bracing before the permanent load path and required sheathing or connectors are complete
  • raise walls, or work beneath a raised and unrestrained wall, without the required worker count, equipment, and access controls
  • continue raising or bracing operations during wind conditions or other site conditions that exceed the approved lift plan's limits

Any wall section that fails the pre-raise inspection, or any condition suggesting an unstable lift, must stop work until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Every wall raised in this project is restrained the instant it is vertical, before any worker releases it and before the crew moves to the next section. Treating temporary bracing as an optional finishing step, rather than a required part of the raise itself, is the single most common cause of the overturning incidents this book's safety cues are written to prevent.

Setup Standard

Do not raise any wall section until it has passed the pre-raise inspection required in Step 2 below, and do not release a raised wall from the raising crew's control until the immediate temporary restraint required in Step 9 is installed and confirmed. A wall that is upright but not yet restrained must be treated as unsupported.

Shop Tip

Mark each brace's stake or anchor location with a temporary flag before raising begins. Locating and pre-staging every brace anchor point ahead of time keeps the crew from holding a raised wall by hand while someone searches for a stake or a drill.

Common Field Fundamentals

Immediate Restraint and the String-Line Reference

A wall is not considered stable the moment it reaches vertical. It is stable only after the immediate temporary restraint required in Step 9 of the procedure below is installed and confirmed. The top-plate string line established later in the sequence does not replace that immediate restraint; it is a separate, later reference used to straighten a wall system that is already individually braced and connected at its base.

Critical Safety Cue — Overturning Hazard

Do not allow any worker to release manual control of a raised wall section until its immediate temporary restraint is installed and confirmed stable. A raised, unrestrained wall can overturn from wind, contact, or an uneven release and cause serious injury or death. Stop the lift and reset restraint if control of the wall is lost at any point.

Safety Cue — Wind and Weather Limits

Do not raise, brace, or leave partially restrained wall sections outside the wind limit stated in the approved lift plan. Reassess exposed, partially braced walls if wind conditions change during the work.

Field Safety Cue — Lift Path and Floor Openings

Clear the lift path, wall line, and bracing locations of tools, material, and debris before raising begins. Guard every floor opening and establish an exclusion zone around the raising area before the first wall leaves the deck.

Measurement Cue — Plumb at Every Specified Reference Point

Check plumb at the reference points specified by the approved plan or this project's procedure, not at a single convenient location. A wall can read plumb at one point and be out of plumb at an end, an opening, or an intersection if only one point is checked.

Code Alert — Bracing Requirements Vary by Jurisdiction

Required temporary-bracing spacing, anchor type, and wind-exposure provisions vary by jurisdiction and the approved lift plan. Verify current bracing requirements with the authority having jurisdiction or the responsible design professional before finalizing the bracing layout.

E. Pre-Raise Preparation and Site Control (Steps 1–5)

  1. Review the wall-raising sequence, wall weights, crew assignments, lifting equipment, wind limits, temporary-bracing plan, and permanent-connection schedule. Confirm the revision date of each document against the project's document-control log before using it.
  2. Inspect each wall section for completed framing, square, fasteners, openings, connectors, and temporary lifting restraint. Reject or hold any section that does not pass this inspection; do not raise it until the deficiency is corrected and reinspected.
  3. Clear the lift path, floor area, wall line, bracing locations, and emergency access. Remove tools, material, and debris from every area the wall or crew will pass through.
  4. Guard floor openings and establish exclusion zones around the lift. Confirm guards are secure and exclusion-zone boundaries are visible before raising begins.
  5. Stage braces, anchors, fasteners, ladders, lifts, and fall-protection equipment before raising begins. Confirm every item required for the first wall's immediate restraint is within reach of its assigned worker.

F. Raising and Immediate Restraint (Steps 6–10)

  1. Identify the first wall and verify its controlling corner and layout line. Confirm the wall line matches the reference corner and baseline established in Chapter 1.
  2. Assign one person to direct the lift and stop the operation for loss of control or changing conditions. Confirm this person has a clear view of the entire wall and crew before the lift begins.
  3. Raise the first wall using the approved manual or mechanical method. Follow the worker count and technique specified in the lift plan.
  4. Hold the wall in position and install immediate temporary restraint before workers release it. Confirm the brace is fastened and stable before any worker steps away from the wall.
  5. Align the bottom plate with the framing line and complete the approved base connection. Confirm the plate sits on the marked wall line for its full length before fastening.

G. Plumbing, Bracing, and Sequential Raising (Steps 11–16)

  1. Plumb the wall at controlled reference points. Check plumb at each point specified by the approved plan or this project's procedure, not at a single location.
  2. Install diagonal braces from specified wall points to approved floor, ground, or weighted anchors. Confirm brace angle and anchor type match the approved bracing plan.
  3. Raise adjacent wall sections in the planned sequence. Repeat Steps 6 through 12 for each section in the order specified by the raising sequence.
  4. Bring corners and intersections together without removing primary bracing. Confirm each wall's original bracing remains in place while adjoining sections are brought into contact.
  5. Fasten wall intersections, corner studs, and plate joints according to the approved schedule. Verify faces remain flush and no wall is forced out of plumb during fastening.
  6. Lap or strap top plates as specified. Confirm lap length or strap placement matches the approved connection schedule before fastening.

H. Alignment, Final Bracing, and Documentation (Steps 17–24)

  1. Establish a string line or other approved reference along the wall tops. Set line blocks at each end of the wall run and pull the string taut along the top plate.
  2. Adjust braces to straighten the wall system. Loosen and reset individual braces as needed to bring the wall tops to the string-line reference.
  3. Recheck plumb at ends, openings, intersections, and specified intervals. Confirm straightening adjustments have not moved any wall section out of plumb.
  4. Install additional braces where required by wall length, wind, height, openings, or the approved plan. Add bracing at any point the approved plan requires beyond the initial installation.
  5. Inspect brace angle, anchorage, fasteners, splitting, interference, and trip hazards. Correct or replace any brace that does not meet these conditions before continuing.
  6. Mark temporary braces so they are not removed before permanent stability is achieved. Use a consistent, visible marking method identified in the site's bracing plan.
  7. Verify wall location, plumb, straightness, corner connection, top alignment, and overall stability. Confirm every check against the Acceptance Standards table before documenting the wall as complete.
  8. Document brace locations, wall measurements, corrections, and release conditions. Enter every reading, correction, and brace location on the wall-raising and bracing record.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed wall system using the same checks regardless of which wall or intersection it covers.

  • Documentation check: confirm every required lift plan, bracing plan, and connection schedule is current and referenced on the wall-raising and bracing record.
  • Plumb check: confirm every wall reads plumb at each specified reference point within the approved tolerance.
  • Alignment check: confirm wall tops fall within the approved tolerance of the established string-line reference.
  • Connection check: confirm base connections, intersections, corners, and top-plate laps are fastened according to the approved schedule.
  • Bracing check: confirm every wall retains adequate, correctly anchored temporary bracing and that no brace has been removed before the permanent system is complete.

Complete the Wall-Raising and Bracing Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 12.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Wall Will Not Read Plumb at All Specified Points

Probable cause: the wall was framed out of square, the base connection was completed before plumb was confirmed, or a brace anchor point shifted during fastening.
Confirmation: recheck plumb at every specified reference point, inspect the base connection for full contact with the marked wall line, and inspect brace anchors for movement.
Correction: loosen the affected brace, replumb the wall at the specified points, and refasten the brace to a confirmed anchor; if the wall was framed out of square, log the discrepancy and obtain direction before proceeding.
Reinspection: repeat the plumb check at every specified point after correction.

Wall Tops Do Not Align to the String-Line Reference

Probable cause: individual walls were plumbed and braced correctly but not straightened as a system, or the string line was not pulled from a verified reference.
Confirmation: re-verify the string-line reference points, then measure the gap between the string and the wall top at each brace location.
Correction: adjust braces incrementally to bring the wall top to the string-line reference, then recheck plumb at that wall before moving to the next.
Reinspection: repeat the alignment check along the full wall run after all adjustments are complete.

Intersection or Corner Will Not Close Without Forcing a Wall Out of Plumb

Probable cause: one of the adjoining wall sections is out of length, out of square, or was plumbed from an incorrect reference point.
Confirmation: re-measure both adjoining wall sections against their approved dimensions and re-verify plumb on each before attempting the connection again.
Correction: correct the out-of-tolerance wall section per its approved detail before fastening the intersection; do not fasten a corner or intersection by forcing a plumb wall out of plumb to close a gap.
Reinspection: repeat the plumb and square checks on both walls after the correction and before fastening.

A Brace Anchor Pulls Loose or Shows Movement

Probable cause: the anchor was undersized for the load, driven into unsuitable material, or installed at an angle that does not match the approved bracing plan.
Confirmation: inspect the anchor type, depth, and angle against the approved bracing plan, and inspect the surrounding material for the condition that allowed movement.
Correction: relocate the anchor to suitable material or install the anchor type specified by the bracing plan, and reinstall the brace at the correct angle.
Reinspection: repeat the bracing check and confirm the wall remains plumb after the anchor is corrected.

Wall Loses Restraint During Fastening or Adjacent Work

Probable cause: a brace was disturbed, loosened, or removed prematurely by adjacent work, or contact from raising the next wall section shifted an already-braced wall.
Confirmation: inspect all bracing on the affected wall for looseness, damage, or disconnection, and recheck plumb.
Correction: stop work in the affected area, reinstall or tighten the disturbed bracing, and replumb the wall before resuming adjacent work.
Reinspection: repeat the stability and plumb checks on the affected wall and any wall connected to it.

Primary Bracing Removed Before the Permanent System Is Complete

Probable cause: bracing was not marked as required by Step 22, or a later trade removed bracing without confirming the permanent load path was complete.
Confirmation: inspect the affected wall for plumb, alignment, and any visible movement, and confirm whether the approved permanent sheathing and connectors are actually complete.
Correction: reinstall temporary bracing immediately if the permanent system is not complete, and replumb and realign the wall before continuing; document the premature removal on the wall-raising and bracing record.
Reinspection: repeat the full plumb, alignment, and stability check on the affected wall before permanent bracing is finally released.

Cleanup

This project ends with a connected, plumb, aligned, and safely braced wall system, not with bracing removed or the raising area cleared of guards.

  • Remove tools, unused brace stock, fasteners, and debris from the raising area without disturbing installed bracing
  • Confirm every required brace remains fastened, marked, and undisturbed before leaving the site
  • Confirm floor-opening guards and exclusion-zone markers remain in place where openings are still exposed
  • Store the signed wall-raising and bracing record and supporting photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open discrepancy or pending correction to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Project 12 begins

Field Terms

Immediate Temporary Restraint
The brace or hold installed on a wall the instant it reaches vertical, before workers release manual control.
Plumb Reference Point
A labeled location on a wall where plumb is measured and confirmed per the approved plan or procedure.
Base Connection
The approved fastening of the bottom plate to the subfloor, sill plate, or foundation along the marked wall line.
Top-Plate String Line
A single reference line run along the tops of raised walls, used to straighten the entire wall system.
Primary Bracing
The temporary diagonal bracing required to remain in place until the permanent load path and diaphragm are complete.
Exclusion Zone
A marked boundary around a raising operation that restricts unauthorized entry during the lift.
Lift Director
The person assigned to direct a wall-raising operation and stop it for loss of control or changing conditions.
Top-Plate Lap
The overlap of a second top plate across a wall intersection or corner, fastened per the approved connection schedule.
Wall-Raising and Bracing Record
The signed document confirming plumb, alignment, connections, bracing, and inspection results for a raised wall system.
Stop-Work Condition
A defined condition, such as loss of wall control or wind exceeding the approved limit, that requires work to stop until direction is obtained.
