Job Overview

This project qualifies you to build and raise one approved exterior wall section on the completed subfloor established in Project 05. The finished work product is a single stable exterior wall section, framed to the plan-specified length, height, stud spacing, and corner configuration, plumbed and squared, connected to the floor system, and temporarily braced against overturning until adjacent walls and permanent connectors are complete.

You will review the exterior wall plan and its load-bearing designation, select and prepare plate and stud material, lay out both plates together, assemble the plan-specified corner configuration and any intersecting-wall backing, build the wall flat on the subfloor, square it, raise it into position, and verify its dimensions, plumb, and connections before signing off the wall for the next stage of framing.

This project follows Project 06, where you framed a non-load-bearing interior partition. That project introduced plate layout, stud spacing, squaring, and raising on a wall with no structural load assigned to it. This project applies the same sequence to a wall that carries structural load, and introduces the corner assembly, hold-down framing, and connection requirements that a load-bearing exterior wall demands. The wall section completed here becomes the platform for the rough openings in Project 09, the structural headers in Project 10, and the coordinated raising, bracing, and sheathing work in Projects 11 and 12.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FR-037 — Lay out exterior wall plates from the approved wall plan, marking stud, corner, intersection, opening, and hold-down locations on both top and bottom plates.
  • FR-038 — Select, prepare, and install studs to the specified wall height, spacing, and crown orientation.
  • FR-039 — Build the plan-specified corner configuration without substituting another corner type.
  • FR-040 — Install the bottom plate, top plate, and required second top plate in the approved sequence and fastening schedule.
  • FR-041 — Square, raise, plumb, and temporarily brace the assembled wall section, and align it with the established wall line.
  • FR-042 — Inspect the completed exterior wall assembly for length, height, stud spacing, corner geometry, plate alignment, plumb, straightness, and connection completeness.

Where This Skill Fits

The exterior wall section built here carries the structural load path established by the floor framing in Projects 02 through 05 up to the ceiling and roof framing built in Projects 13 through 16. A wall built off its layout line, framed with the wrong corner configuration, or raised without the specified hold-down studs, carries that error into the rough openings, headers, sheathing, and roof bearing that depend on it.

The corner configuration built in this project is compared against an alternate corner type and intersecting-wall backing in Project 08. The rough openings framed into a wall of this type are addressed in Project 09, and the structural headers that span those openings are addressed in Project 10. This project does not frame openings; it produces the plate layout and stud framework that Project 09 will modify.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved exterior wall plan, including load-bearing designation, wall height, stud size and spacing, corner type, and connection schedule
  • laying out and marking both plates from the approved reference end
  • preparing, crowning, and installing studs, corner assemblies, and intersecting-wall backing at their labeled locations
  • assembling, squaring, raising, plumbing, and temporarily bracing the completed wall section
  • aligning the wall with the established floor wall line and completing the approved foundation or floor connection
  • inspecting and recording wall dimensions, corner geometry, plumb, and connection completeness

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select stud size, spacing, corner type, or hold-down requirements without the approved exterior wall plan; these values must come from the plan, not from field judgment
  • frame or cut a door or window rough opening; rough openings are framed under the supplied opening schedule in Project 09
  • size or install a structural header; header sizing and installation are covered in Project 10
  • remove temporary bracing installed in this project before the permanent connections, adjacent walls, and sheathing required by later chapters are complete
  • substitute a corner configuration, stud grade, or connector type not shown on the approved plan

Any wall section whose plan-specified dimensions, load-bearing designation, or connection schedule cannot be confirmed on site must stop for direction from the responsible design professional before framing begins.

Trade Note

This project frames one exterior wall section as a controlled learning unit. On an actual structure, every exterior wall section, along with the corners and intersections that join them, is typically laid out and built before any wall is raised, so that the full building's stud, corner, and opening layout is coordinated before the first wall leaves the deck. Coordinate this project's wall section with the corner and intersection work in Project 08 before raising if more than one wall section is being built on the same structure.

Setup Standard

Do not raise any wall section until the subfloor has passed the inspection required in Project 05, the wall's controlling floor line is marked and verified, and the required worker count, lifting method, and temporary-bracing materials for that specific wall are staged and confirmed (Step 19). A wall raised without confirmed bracing materials on hand must not be released by workers until temporary restraint is installed.

Shop Tip

Lay both plates side by side with their reference ends aligned before marking any layout line. Marking both plates together in a single pass, rather than marking one plate and then copying the marks to the second, is the most reliable way to keep stud locations identical on the top and bottom plate.

Load-Bearing Designation and Corner Configuration

An exterior wall carries structural load from the floor, ceiling, or roof framing above it unless the approved plan specifically designates it otherwise. Confirm the load-bearing designation on the plan before framing, because it governs stud size, spacing, corner configuration, and connection requirements. The corner configuration specified for this wall — for example, a three-stud corner or a California corner — must be built exactly as shown on the plan; Project 08 compares corner types side by side, but this project builds only the type the plan specifies for this wall's actual corners.

Measurement Cue — Lay Out Both Plates Together

Place the top and bottom plates edge to edge with their reference ends aligned before marking any stud, corner, or opening location. Mark both plates in the same pass using the framing square. Do not mark one plate first and transfer marks to the second plate separately; doing so is a common source of studs that are out of alignment between the top and bottom of the wall.

Critical Safety Cue — Wall Raising and Temporary Restraint

Do not release a raised wall section until temporary restraint is installed and confirmed stable. Plan the wall lift with the required worker count, equipment, and access controls identified in Step 19 before raising begins. Stop the lift immediately for loss of control, an obstruction in the raising path, or changing wind conditions, and do not resume until the condition is corrected.

Field Safety Cue — Raising Path and Exclusion Zone

Clear the raising path of tools, material, and debris, and keep workers not assigned to the lift outside the exclusion zone until the wall is secured. Guard any floor opening near the raising path before the lift begins.

Code Alert — Hold-Down and Connector Requirements Vary

Hold-down type, location, and installation torque or fastening requirements vary by jurisdiction, wind or seismic design category, and manufacturer. Verify the approved connection schedule and manufacturer instructions for every hold-down, strap, or connector before installing it, and do not substitute a generic fastener for a listed connector fastener.

E. Document Review and Preparation (Steps 1–5)

  1. Review the exterior wall plan, load-bearing designation, wall height, stud size and spacing, openings, corners, sheathing, hold-downs, and connection schedule. Confirm the revision date against the project's document-control log and record it on the wall-framing record.
  2. Verify the subfloor, wall lines, edge protection, material staging, and raising area. Confirm the subfloor has passed the inspection required in Project 05 and that the wall line for this section is marked and legible.
  3. Identify the controlling wall end and direction of layout. Mark the controlling end on the plan copy used for field reference.
  4. Select plate and stud material matching the approved grade, species or category, treatment, and moisture requirements. Reject stock that does not match the specification or shows defects prohibited by the project specification.
  5. Align the top and bottom plates and mark reference ends. Place both plates together with their reference ends flush before any layout mark is made.

F. Plate Layout and Stud Preparation (Steps 6–9)

  1. Lay out end studs, common studs, corners, intersecting walls, openings, hold-down studs, and concentrated-load locations. Mark every location on both plates in the same pass using the approved spacing.
  2. Transfer layout marks across both plates and label each location. Label each mark with its component type (common stud, king stud, corner, hold-down) as shown on the plan.
  3. Measure and prepare studs to the specified wall height. Cut every stud square from a consistent reference end at the height confirmed in Step 1.
  4. Crown and orient solid-sawn studs consistently. Sight each stud, mark the crown, and orient all crowns in the same direction per the project specification.

G. Corner, Backing, and Wall Assembly (Steps 10–15)

  1. Assemble the approved corner configuration without substituting another corner type. Build the corner exactly as detailed on the plan; do not select a different corner type for convenience.
  2. Assemble intersecting-wall backing or ladder backing where shown. Build backing components per the plan detail before installing them in the wall assembly.
  3. Place common studs and special studs at their labeled locations. Position every stud at its marked location with crowns oriented per Step 9.
  4. Install the bottom plate, top plate, and required second top plate in the approved sequence. Install the second top plate only after the wall is raised and aligned, unless the approved sequence specifies otherwise.
  5. Install straps, hold-down studs, post assemblies, or connector framing that must be built into the wall. Position and label each per the connection schedule before fastening.
  6. Fasten joints according to the approved wall schedule. Use the fastener type, size, and pattern confirmed in Section C for every joint.

H. Squaring and Raising (Steps 16–22)

  1. Verify that studs remain on layout and faces remain flush. Check every stud location against the plate layout marks before squaring.
  2. Measure diagonals and square the wall section. Adjust the assembly until both diagonals match within the tolerance confirmed in Section D.
  3. Install temporary restraint to preserve square during raising. Fasten a diagonal brace across the flat assembly before lifting.
  4. Plan the wall lift, worker positions, equipment, and temporary bracing. Confirm the required worker count, lifting method, and bracing materials are staged before raising begins.
  5. Raise the wall and secure it immediately against overturning. Lift the wall using the planned method and install temporary restraint before releasing the lift.
  6. Align the bottom plate with the wall line and install approved foundation or floor connections. Confirm the bottom plate matches the wall line for its full length before fastening.
  7. Plumb the wall and install temporary braces. Plumb at the ends, openings, and specified intervals, and install braces sufficient to hold the wall stable until permanent connections are complete.

I. Verification and Documentation (Steps 23–25)

  1. Verify wall length, height, stud spacing, corner geometry, plate alignment, plumb, straightness, and connection completeness. Check every item against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Correct authorized deficiencies before adjacent walls or sheathing are installed. Re-verify any corrected item before proceeding.
  3. Record wall identification, dimensions, connectors, temporary bracing, and inspection results. Complete the wall-framing record before releasing the wall for the next project.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed wall section using the same checks regardless of wall length or corner type.

  • Documentation check: confirm the wall plan, load-bearing designation, and connection schedule referenced are current and match the wall-framing record.
  • Layout check: confirm stud, corner, intersection, and hold-down locations match the approved plan spacing on both plates.
  • Assembly check: confirm the corner configuration matches the plan-specified type and every joint is fastened per the approved schedule.
  • Dimensional check: confirm wall length, height, and flat-assembly diagonals are within the approved plan tolerance.
  • Raised-wall check: confirm bottom-plate alignment with the wall line, plumb at required locations, and installed temporary bracing.
  • Connection check: confirm every hold-down, strap, and foundation or floor connection is installed and seated per manufacturer instructions.

Complete the Wall-Framing Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 08.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Studs Are Out of Alignment Between Top and Bottom Plate

Probable cause: the plates were marked separately rather than together, or one plate shifted before fastening.
Confirmation: lay a framing square or straightedge across a suspect stud location on both plates and compare the marks.
Correction: if the wall is not yet fastened, re-mark both plates together from the reference end; if already fastened, remove and reposition the affected stud at the corrected layout mark.
Reinspection: re-check stud spacing across the full wall length before proceeding to squaring.

Flat-Assembly Diagonals Do Not Match Within Tolerance

Probable cause: a corner or intersection joint shifted during assembly, or the wall was measured from inconsistent reference faces.
Confirmation: re-measure both diagonals from the same reference corners, and inspect each joint for full seating and correct fastening.
Correction: loosen the affected joint, realign the wall to the correct diagonal measurement, and re-fasten; install or adjust the temporary diagonal brace to hold the corrected position.
Reinspection: repeat the diagonal check before installing the temporary brace for raising.

Bottom Plate Does Not Align With the Wall Line After Raising

Probable cause: the wall was raised and secured before the bottom plate was checked against the wall line, or the wall line itself was disturbed.
Confirmation: compare the bottom-plate location against the protected wall line established in Project 01 at both ends and the midpoint.
Correction: loosen temporary restraint only as needed to reposition the bottom plate to the wall line, then reinstall temporary restraint before proceeding.
Reinspection: re-verify bottom-plate alignment for the full wall length before installing the foundation or floor connection.

Wall Will Not Plumb at One End

Probable cause: the subfloor is not level at that location, a stud is crowned opposite the rest of the wall, or the bottom plate is not fully seated on the wall line.
Confirmation: check subfloor level at the affected end, inspect the suspect stud for reversed crown, and confirm full bottom-plate bearing.
Correction: shim only where the approved detail permits shimming; correct a reversed-crown stud per the approved method; do not force a wall plumb by racking a stud out of its fastened position.
Reinspection: repeat the plumb check at that end and at the adjacent brace location.

A Hold-Down or Connector Does Not Match the Connection Schedule

Probable cause: the wrong hardware was staged for this wall, or the connector was installed at the wrong stud location.
Confirmation: compare the installed hardware type, location, and fasteners against the approved connection schedule.
Correction: remove and replace with the specified hardware and fasteners; do not substitute a generic fastener for a listed connector fastener.
Reinspection: repeat the connection check for that location per manufacturer instructions.

Wall Racks or Loses Square After Raising

Probable cause: insufficient or improperly angled temporary bracing, or bracing anchored to an unstable point.
Confirmation: inspect brace angle, anchorage, and fastening at each brace location.
Correction: add braces or correct brace angle and anchorage per the approved temporary-bracing plan; re-plumb and re-square the wall before re-tightening braces.
Reinspection: repeat the plumb and straightness check at all required locations after correction.

Cleanup

This project ends with a raised, plumbed, and temporarily braced exterior wall section, not with permanent bracing removed or sheathing installed.

  • Clear offcuts, packaging, and loose fasteners from the subfloor around the completed wall
  • Confirm every temporary brace is fastened, angled, and anchored per the approved temporary-bracing plan
  • Mark temporary braces so they are not mistaken for permanent bracing or removed prematurely
  • Store unused plate, stud, and connector material clear of the raising area for the next wall section
  • Store the completed wall-framing record with the project's permanent documentation

Field Terms
