Job Overview

This project qualifies you to build, square, raise, locate, and secure one non-load-bearing interior partition wall using the approved partition plan and the wall lines established on the subfloor in Project 01. The finished work product is a plumb, straight, correctly located partition wall, built and assembled on the deck, raised into position, and fastened top and bottom according to the approved connection detail.

You will lay out matching top and bottom plates together, mark stud and opening locations on both plates at once, cut and crown studs to the required length, assemble the wall flat on the subfloor, square the assembly using the diagonal method from Project 01, raise the wall into position with temporary restraint, and plumb and fasten it according to the approved detail.

This project follows directly from Project 05, which delivered a complete, inspected subfloor marked with the controlling wall lines from Project 01. It is the first wall-framing project in this book and establishes the plate-and-stud layout method used again in Project 07 for exterior walls and throughout the remaining wall-framing chapters.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FR-031 — Lay out matching top and bottom wall plates from the approved partition plan and the marked subfloor wall line.
  • FR-032 — Mark stud spacing, openings, backing, and intersection locations transferred square across both plates.
  • FR-033 — Measure, crown, and cut studs to the required wall height from a consistent reference method.
  • FR-034 — Assemble the wall frame flat on the subfloor using the approved fastening schedule.
  • FR-035 — Square the assembled wall frame using the diagonal method and install temporary restraint to hold square.
  • FR-036 — Raise, plumb, and secure the wall on its layout line using the approved worker count, lift procedure, and connection detail.

Where This Skill Fits

Every exterior and interior wall framed later in this book uses the same plate-layout, stud-cutting, flat-assembly, squaring, and raising sequence taught here. Project 07 repeats this sequence for a load-bearing exterior wall with corner and connection requirements added. Project 11 repeats it again at the scale of a complete multi-wall system, and the Chapter 20 capstone repeats it to frame every wall of the shed structure.

Because this project uses a non-load-bearing wall, it isolates the plate-layout, stud-cutting, assembly, squaring, and raising skills from the corner, header, and structural-connector requirements introduced in later chapters. Mastering the sequence here, on a wall with no structural load assigned to it, protects the learner from combining a new layout skill with a new structural requirement at the same time.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved partition plan, wall length, height, stud spacing, door openings, backing, fire-blocking, top connection, and fastening details
  • confirming the wall is non-load-bearing and carries no structural load assigned by the approved plan
  • laying out, marking, cutting, and assembling the wall frame flat on the subfloor
  • squaring the assembled frame and installing temporary restraint
  • raising, plumbing, and fastening the wall to its approved top and bottom connections
  • verifying and documenting wall location, length, height, stud spacing, plumb, straightness, square, and stability

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • frame a load-bearing wall, an exterior wall, or a wall carrying any structural connector, header, or hold-down using this project's method alone; those requirements are covered beginning in Project 07
  • relocate a wall from the line marked in Project 01 without an approved plan revision
  • select stud spacing, plate material, or fastening schedule not specified by the approved partition plan
  • raise the wall without the approved worker count, lift procedure, and temporary restraint described in this project

Any wall that the approved plan designates as load-bearing, or that supports a structural connector, header, or hold-down, must be framed using the exterior wall and structural header methods in Projects 07, 9, and 10, not this project alone.

Trade Note

Marking the top and bottom plates together, rather than separately, is what keeps every stud, opening, and backing member square across the finished wall. A plate marked alone, then matched to its partner by eye during assembly, is the most common source of studs that are off layout by the time the wall is raised.

Setup Standard

Do not raise the wall until the assembled frame has been squared by the diagonal method and temporary restraint has been installed to hold that square (Steps 16–17). A wall raised out of square will not plumb or fasten correctly at its top connection, and forcing it into position risks racking the frame or pulling a fastened joint loose.

Shop Tip

Cut and label every stud from the same reference end before assembly begins. Studs cut from mixed reference ends can be the same length yet still cause the top plate to bow if one crown faces a different direction than its neighbors.

Common Field Fundamentals

This project introduces the plate-and-stud layout method used for every wall framed in this book. Marking both plates together, cutting studs from a common reference, assembling flat, squaring by the diagonal method, and raising with controlled temporary restraint is the same sequence used in Project 07 for exterior walls, Project 11 for a full wall system, and Chapter 20 for the capstone shed.

Matched-Plate Layout and the Diagonal Check

Placing the top and bottom plates edge to edge with their reference ends aligned, then marking both at once, guarantees that every stud, opening, and backing location lands in the same position on both plates. The diagonal check used to square the assembled frame is the same corner-to-corner comparison method taught in Project 01: when both diagonals of the rectangular wall frame measure the same length within the approved tolerance, the frame is square.

Measurement Cue — Mark Both Plates From the Same Reference End

Identify one reference end on the plate pair before making any layout mark, and measure every stud, opening, and backing location from that end on both plates. Marking the two plates independently, even from what appears to be the same end, is a common source of studs that are square to one plate but out of position on the other.

Critical Safety Cue — Controlled Wall Raising

Do not raise the wall until the raising path is cleared, the approved worker count is present, and one person is assigned to direct the lift. Stop the lift immediately for loss of control, an obstruction in the path, or a worker out of position. Install temporary restraint before releasing manual support of the raised wall.

Field Safety Cue — Cutting and Assembly Area

Keep the cutting and assembly area clear of offcuts, loose fasteners, and cords along the path where the wall will be flipped or lifted. A tripping hazard at the moment of the lift is a fall hazard for every worker assigned to that wall.

Code Alert — Fire-Blocking and Backing Requirements Vary

Required fire-blocking height, drywall-backing locations, and interior partition fastening schedules vary by jurisdiction and by the approved plan. Verify these requirements against the current adopted code and the approved partition plan before finalizing the stud and backing layout.

FR-031 – FR-036

Layout / Assembly / Raising Qualification

Frame, Square, Raise, and Secure a Non-Load-Bearing Interior Partition Wall

Difficulty: Beginner–Intermediate

Estimated Time: 4–6 hours

Workers Required: Minimum 2 for the raising step; layout and assembly may be completed by 1

Finished Work: One plumb, straight, correctly located non-load-bearing partition wall secured to its approved top and bottom connections

A. Layout Preparation and Plate Marking (Steps 1–7)

  1. Review the partition plan, wall length, height, stud spacing, door openings, backing, fire-blocking, top connection, and fastening details. Confirm the current plan revision against the project's document-control log before marking any material.
  2. Verify that the subfloor is clean, safe, and marked with controlling wall lines. Confirm the wall line from Project 01 is still legible and unobstructed along its full length before proceeding.
  3. Identify the wall as non-load-bearing and confirm that no structural load is assigned to it. Stop and obtain the design professional's direction if the plan designation is unclear or if this wall intersects a bearing line identified in Project 01.
  4. Select straight plate stock and serviceable studs of the specified grade and size. Reject any piece with a defect that falls within a cut zone identified in Step 8 below or that is prohibited by the project specification.
  5. Place the top and bottom plates together with their reference ends aligned. Identify and mark the reference end on both plates before making any layout mark.
  6. Mark end studs, intermediate studs, openings, intersections, backing, and service zones on both plates. Use the stud spacing stated in the approved partition plan, measured from the reference end.
  7. Transfer layout marks square across both plates and label each component position. Use the framing square to carry every mark across the plate width and label each stud, opening, and backing location with its plan designation.

B. Stud Preparation (Steps 8–11)

  1. Measure the actual floor-to-ceiling or floor-to-framing height at the wall location. Take this measurement at both ends of the wall and at any point where the plan indicates the height may vary.
  2. Calculate or obtain the approved stud length from the project specification. Confirm the calculation accounts for the plate thicknesses specified in the partition plan; do not estimate stud length from the floor-to-ceiling measurement alone.
  3. Cut and label studs from a consistent reference method. Cut all common studs to the length determined in Step 9, squared at both ends, and label each with its layout position from Step 6.
  4. Crown solid-sawn studs and orient crowns consistently where required. Sight each stud along its length, mark the crown direction, and orient all crowns the same direction specified by the project specification.

C. Flat Assembly (Steps 12–15)

  1. Lay the wall components flat in assembly position. Position the top plate, bottom plate, and all studs on the subfloor in their marked layout positions before fastening any joint.
  2. Install end studs, intermediate studs, opening members, and backing in the specified sequence. Follow the sequence stated in the approved partition plan; do not fasten intermediate studs before end studs are confirmed on layout.
  3. Verify plate alignment and stud orientation before fastening. Confirm every stud is on its marked layout line, crown oriented correctly, and both plate ends flush with the end studs.
  4. Fasten each joint using the approved nailing or screw schedule. Use the fastener type, size, and pattern specified in the Fastener and Hardware Schedule for each joint type.

D. Squaring and Temporary Restraint (Steps 16–17)

  1. Measure wall diagonals and adjust the frame square. Measure both corner-to-corner diagonals of the assembled frame and compare them using the same method taught in Project 01; adjust the frame until both diagonals are equal within the approved tolerance.
  2. Install temporary diagonal restraint when needed to hold square. Fasten a temporary diagonal brace across the assembled frame to preserve the verified square condition through the raising step.

E. Raising, Plumbing, and Securing (Steps 18–22)

  1. Clear the raising path and assign workers to controlled lift positions. Remove tools, offcuts, and obstructions from the wall's swing path and assign the minimum worker count specified for this wall's length and weight.
  2. Raise the wall using the approved worker count and lifting procedure. Direct the lift from one assigned position and stop immediately for loss of control or an obstruction.
  3. Position the wall on its layout line and install temporary restraint. Align the bottom plate edge with the marked subfloor wall line from Project 01 and install temporary braces before releasing manual support.
  4. Plumb the wall and fasten the bottom and top connections according to the approved detail. Check plumb at both ends and at each opening using the level, then fasten the bottom plate to the subfloor and the top plate to the overhead framing per the approved connection detail.
  5. Connect intersecting partitions or backing where included. Fasten any intersecting wall or backing member shown on the approved plan using its specified connection.

F. Verification and Documentation (Steps 23–25)

  1. Verify wall location, length, height, stud spacing, plumb, straightness, square, and stability. Compare every measured value against the Acceptance Standards in the Project Specification Sheet.
  2. Correct authorized layout or alignment errors before adjacent framing proceeds. Re-check plumb, straightness, and square after any correction.
  3. Record dimensions, connections, deviations, and inspection results. Enter every measurement and correction on the Inspection Checklist below before the wall is released for adjacent framing.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed partition wall using the same checks regardless of wall length or opening count.

  • Layout check: confirm stud spacing, opening locations, and backing positions match the approved partition plan and were transferred square across both plates.
  • Dimensional check: confirm wall length and height are within the approved plan tolerance.
  • Square check: confirm the assembled frame's diagonals were equal within tolerance before raising.
  • Plumb and straightness check: confirm the raised wall is plumb at both ends and each opening, and straight along its length.
  • Connection check: confirm the bottom and top connections match the approved detail and every fastener follows the specified schedule.
  • Stability check: confirm the wall does not move under a hand-pressure racking check once bracing is installed.

Complete the Inspection Checklist below before proceeding to Project 07.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Studs Are Off Layout After Assembly

Probable cause: the top and bottom plates were marked separately rather than together, or a layout mark was transferred without using the framing square to keep it perpendicular to the plate edge.
Confirmation: measure each stud's position on both plates from the same reference end and compare the two readings.
Correction: loosen the affected fasteners, realign the stud to the correct layout mark on both plates, and refasten using the approved schedule.
Reinspection: repeat the layout check across the full wall before proceeding to squaring.

Diagonals Do Not Match Within Tolerance

Probable cause: the frame racked during assembly, a stud was fastened before the plates were confirmed square, or a measurement was taken from inconsistent corners.
Confirmation: re-measure both diagonals from the same corner references and confirm the tape is fully extended and unobstructed.
Correction: apply pressure at the long diagonal's corners to bring the frame into square, then install the temporary diagonal brace before re-measuring.
Reinspection: repeat the diagonal check after the brace is installed and before raising.

Wall Will Not Plumb After Raising

Probable cause: the assembled frame was not square before raising, the bottom plate is not aligned with the marked wall line, or the floor-to-ceiling height varies along the wall length.
Confirmation: re-check the bottom plate position against the Project 01 wall line, and re-measure the floor-to-ceiling height at the point where plumb is out of tolerance.
Correction: if the frame was not square, lower the wall and correct square before re-raising; if the height varies, obtain direction on trimming or shimming from the approved detail rather than forcing the frame.
Reinspection: repeat the plumb check at both ends and every opening after correction.

Wall Rocks or Flexes Under the Stability Check

Probable cause: a stud-to-plate joint was not fully fastened, the bottom-plate-to-subfloor connection is incomplete, or temporary bracing was removed too early.
Confirmation: inspect every joint along the wall for missing or partially seated fasteners, and confirm the specified bottom-plate fastening schedule was completed.
Correction: install the missing fasteners per the approved schedule and do not remove temporary bracing until the stability check passes.
Reinspection: repeat the stability check after every corrected joint.

Wall Length Does Not Match the Plan

Probable cause: the plate stock was cut from the wrong reference end, or a layout mark was measured from the wrong plan dimension.
Confirmation: re-measure the assembled wall length against the approved plan dimension and confirm which reference end was used for the original layout.
Correction: if within the correctable range specified by the project, re-cut the plate from the correct reference; if not, obtain direction from the design professional before continuing.
Reinspection: repeat the dimensional check once the correction or design direction is applied.

Cleanup

This project ends with the partition wall raised, plumbed, and fastened, and the assembly area cleared for the next wall.

  • Remove offcuts, loose fasteners, and layout material from the assembly and raising area
  • Confirm temporary bracing is clearly marked and left in place until the wall system is released per the approved sequence
  • Store unused plate and stud stock protected from moisture for the next partition wall
  • Store the completed Inspection Checklist with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open deviation and its required decision to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Project 07 begins
