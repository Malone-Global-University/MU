Job Overview

This project qualifies you to frame door and window rough openings in walls already erected under Chapters 6 and 7, using the door and window schedule and rough-opening dimensions supplied by the project specification. The finished work product is a set of completed rough openings — each with king studs, jack studs, a header, a sill where applicable, and cripple studs — installed to the scheduled dimensions, plumb, level, square, and ready for structural header installation and sheathing.

You will review the door and window schedule, transfer opening locations and layout marks to the wall plates, prepare and install king studs and jack studs, prepare the header from the supplied specification, set the header at the required elevation, install sill and cripple members, and measure every completed opening at multiple points to confirm it matches the scheduled rough-opening dimensions before sheathing conceals the work.

This project follows Chapter 7, Frame an Exterior Wall Section, and Chapter 8, Frame Wall Corners and Intersecting Walls. It uses the wall framing completed in those chapters as its starting condition and prepares the wall for Chapter 10, Install Structural Headers, which addresses headers with load-bearing spans, ply counts, and connector schedules beyond the scope of this project.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FR-049 — Lay out door and window rough openings on the wall plates from the approved schedule, including centerlines, edge locations, and member positions.
  • FR-050 — Install king studs at their plate-layout locations, full length, plumb, and fastened to the top and bottom plates.
  • FR-051 — Install jack (trimmer) studs with full bearing under each header end, fastened to the adjacent king stud.
  • FR-052 — Build the header from the supplied header specification and install it level at the required elevation.
  • FR-053 — Install upper and lower cripple studs at the required spacing, maintaining layout and bearing.
  • FR-054 — Verify completed rough-opening width, height, plumb, level, and square against the scheduled dimensions.

Where This Skill Fits

A rough opening that is framed even a half inch off its scheduled width or height can prevent a door or window unit from being installed to the manufacturer's instructions, or can force field trimming that voids a warranty or compromises the water-management detail around the unit. This project exists to catch dimensional and alignment errors while the opening is still open framing, rather than after the wall is sheathed.

The king-stud, jack-stud, header, and cripple-stud sequence taught here is the same load-path sequence expanded in Chapter 10 for headers carrying larger structural spans, and it depends directly on the wall plates and stud layout established in Chapters 6 and 7.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the door and window schedule, rough-opening dimensions, sill heights, header references, and manufacturer information
  • laying out king studs, jack studs, header limits, sill members, and cripple-stud locations on the wall plates
  • preparing and installing king studs, jack studs, headers, sills, and cripple studs to the supplied specification
  • measuring and verifying completed rough-opening dimensions, plumb, level, and square

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select a structural header size, span, or ply count from guesswork; header sizing is addressed under the supplied specification and expanded in Chapter 10
  • frame an opening that conflicts with a corner, intersection, hold-down, utility, or concentrated-load location without a documented decision
  • install a door or window unit itself; this project frames the rough opening only
  • alter a scheduled rough-opening dimension to match a unit on hand that does not match the schedule

Any opening that conflicts with a corner, hold-down, utility penetration, or concentrated-load location identified in Chapters 6 through 8 must stop layout in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Rough-opening dimensions in the door and window schedule are sized to the unit and its installation method, not to the nominal door or window size. A "3068" door schedule callout does not by itself tell you the rough-opening size; the schedule's rough-opening dimension controls, and the manufacturer's installation instructions control any unit-specific clearance.

Setup Standard

Do not cut or install any king stud, jack stud, header, sill, or cripple stud until the door and window schedule for the wall section in question has been reviewed and the opening's rough-opening dimensions, sill height, and header reference are confirmed against the approved plan (Step 1).

Shop Tip

Label each king stud and jack stud pair with the opening number as it is cut. Jack studs in particular look identical once cut, and installing one at the wrong opening produces a header bearing surface that is the wrong height for that opening's schedule.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every rough opening in this project, whether for a door or a window, follows the same sequence: transfer the opening location to the plates, install king studs, install jack studs at full bearing under the header, build and set the header at the correct elevation, install the sill and cripple studs where applicable, and verify the finished dimensions. Learning that sequence here supports the larger structural headers addressed in Chapter 10.

Rough-Opening Width and the King-Stud/Jack-Stud Relationship

The scheduled rough-opening width is the clear dimension between the two jack studs, not between the two king studs. King studs are positioned so that, once the jack stud is fastened to the inside face of each king stud, the remaining clear distance between the jack studs equals the scheduled rough-opening width. Confirm this relationship on the plate layout before cutting any king stud, since an error here shifts every jack stud, header, and cripple location that follows.

Measurement Cue — Verify Width Between Jack Stud Faces

After installing jack studs and before setting the header, measure the rough-opening width between the inside faces of the two jack studs at the top, middle, and bottom of the opening. Compare each measurement with the scheduled rough-opening width before proceeding to the header.

Critical Safety Cue — Header Bearing

Do not set or fasten a header until both jack studs are installed, plumb, and fully seated on the bottom plate. A header resting on an unseated or out-of-plumb jack stud can shift or drop once loaded, and must not be relied upon to carry load until corrected.

Field Safety Cue — Cutting and Staging Header Stock

Support header stock fully during cutting and keep the cutting area clear of other workers. Stage built-up header components flat and supported until they are assembled, and keep completed headers from leaning unsupported against the wall framing.

Code Alert — Egress and Emergency Escape Openings

Bedroom and other emergency-escape openings are subject to minimum clear-opening width, height, and sill-height requirements that vary by jurisdiction. Verify the rough-opening dimensions for any egress-designated opening against the approved schedule and the authority having jurisdiction before framing.

Manufacturer Cue — Unit-Specific Rough-Opening Tolerance

Some door and window manufacturers specify a rough-opening dimension that includes a defined installation clearance beyond the unit's frame size. Use the rough-opening dimension stated on the door and window schedule, and confirm any manufacturer-specific clearance requirement before framing an opening for a specific product line.

E. Schedule Review and Opening Layout (Steps 1–6)

  1. Review the door and window schedule, rough-opening dimensions, sill heights, header references, bearing requirements, and manufacturer information. Record each opening's schedule data on the field copy before layout begins.
  2. Confirm that opening dimensions are based on the selected unit and approved plan rather than nominal door or window size alone. Use the rough-opening dimension stated on the schedule, not a size inferred from the unit's marketed name.
  3. Identify each opening by wall, type, size, and orientation. Label each opening on the field plan with its schedule designation before marking the plates.
  4. Transfer opening centerlines and edge locations to the top and bottom plates. Measure from the established wall reference corner and mark both plates identically.
  5. Mark king studs, jack or trimmer studs, header limits, sill members, and cripple-stud locations on the plates. Label each mark with its member designation as it is struck.
  6. Verify that openings do not conflict with corners, intersections, hold-downs, utilities, or concentrated loads. Compare each marked opening against the corner and intersection locations established in Chapters 7 and 8 before cutting any member.

F. King Stud, Jack Stud, and Header Preparation (Steps 7–11)

  1. Select and prepare king and jack studs to the approved lengths. Cut king studs to full wall height and jack studs to the height specified by the header elevation and rough sill height.
  2. Assemble paired or built-up jamb members in the specified orientation. Fasten jack studs to the inside face of each king stud per the wall fastening schedule where the design calls for a fastened pair before installation.
  3. Prepare the header only from the supplied header specification; do not select structural header size from guesswork. Confirm ply count, material, and dimensions against the header reference recorded in Section E before cutting.
  4. Install spacers, insulation, plywood, or built-up header components only when specified. Use the exact spacer material and thickness stated in the header specification.
  5. Assemble the wall opening flat when practical. Where the wall section allows flat assembly, lay out and connect king studs, jack studs, and header in position before standing the assembly.

G. Framing the Opening (Steps 12–18)

  1. Install king studs and verify their plate layout. Fasten each king stud to the top and bottom plates at its marked location and confirm it matches the layout mark on both plates.
  2. Install jack studs with full bearing under the header ends. Confirm the jack stud is seated fully on the bottom plate and plumb before fastening it to the king stud.
  3. Set and fasten the header at the approved elevation. Measure the header's underside elevation from the bottom plate and confirm it matches the schedule before final fastening.
  4. Install window sill members level and at the specified rough sill height. Measure the sill's top edge from the bottom plate and confirm it matches the scheduled rough sill height.
  5. Install upper and lower cripple studs at the required spacing. Maintain the wall's common stud spacing through the cripple-stud layout above the header and below the sill.
  6. Maintain required backing and sheathing-edge support. Confirm every sheathing panel edge at the opening will land on a king stud, jack stud, header, sill, or cripple stud.
  7. Fasten all opening members according to the approved schedule. Complete every king-stud, jack-stud, header, sill, and cripple-stud connection per the wall fastening schedule.

H. Verification and Documentation (Steps 19–24)

  1. Measure rough-opening width at top, middle, and bottom. Record each measurement and compare it with the scheduled rough-opening width.
  2. Measure rough-opening height at both sides and center where required. Record each measurement and compare it with the scheduled rough-opening height.
  3. Check jambs for plumb, sill and header for level, corners for square, and opening plane for twist. Use the level and diagonal measurements to confirm each condition against the approved tolerance.
  4. Correct authorized dimensional or alignment errors before sheathing. Re-verify the opening after any correction is made.
  5. Label each completed opening with its scheduled unit designation. Mark the label where it will remain visible until the unit is installed.
  6. Record opening dimensions, header reference, fastening, and inspection results. Enter every measurement and confirmation on the framing-readiness record before proceeding to sheathing or Chapter 10 header work.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect every completed opening using the same checks regardless of whether it is a door or window opening.

  • Layout check: confirm king-stud, jack-stud, header, sill, and cripple-stud marks on both plates match the door and window schedule.
  • Dimensional check: confirm rough-opening width and height at multiple points match the scheduled dimensions within tolerance.
  • Alignment check: confirm jamb plumb, header and sill level, and opening square within tolerance.
  • Fastening check: confirm every king-stud, jack-stud, header, sill, and cripple-stud connection matches the approved wall fastening schedule.
  • Conflict check: confirm the opening does not conflict with a corner, hold-down, utility, or concentrated-load location.

Complete the Inspection Checklist below for each opening before proceeding to Chapter 10 or wall sheathing.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Rough-Opening Width Does Not Match the Schedule

Probable cause: king studs were positioned from the opening centerline without accounting for jack-stud thickness, or the jack stud was not fully seated against the king stud before measuring.
Confirmation: re-measure the distance between the inside faces of the jack studs at top, middle, and bottom, and confirm the king-stud layout marks against the plate layout in Section E.
Correction: if a king stud is mislocated, remove and reset it at the corrected layout mark before refastening the jack stud; do not force a jack stud out of plumb to correct a width error.
Reinspection: repeat the width measurement at all three points once the correction is complete.

Header Elevation Does Not Match the Schedule

Probable cause: the jack-stud length was cut incorrectly, or the header was measured from the wrong reference before fastening.
Confirmation: measure the jack-stud length against the value calculated from the header elevation and rough sill height, and measure the installed header's underside elevation from the bottom plate.
Correction: remove and refasten the header at the corrected elevation, replacing any jack stud cut to an incorrect length; do not shim a header to the correct elevation in place of correcting the jack-stud length.
Reinspection: repeat the header elevation and level check after correction.

Jamb Is Out of Plumb

Probable cause: the jack stud was not held plumb while it was fastened to the king stud, or the king stud itself is bowed or out of plumb.
Confirmation: check the king stud independently for plumb before checking the jack stud, since a plumb jack stud fastened to an out-of-plumb king stud will still read out of plumb.
Correction: correct the king stud first if it is the source of the error; if the king stud is plumb, loosen and reset the jack stud plumb before final fastening.
Reinspection: repeat the plumb check on both king stud and jack stud after correction.

Cripple Studs Are Off the Wall's Common Layout

Probable cause: cripple-stud spacing was measured from the opening edge instead of continuing the wall's established common stud layout.
Confirmation: compare the cripple-stud marks against the common stud layout marked on the plates in Chapter 7.
Correction: re-mark cripple-stud locations from the established common layout, not from the opening edge, and reposition any cripple stud installed off that layout.
Reinspection: confirm cripple-stud spacing matches the common layout before sheathing.

Opening Conflicts With a Corner, Hold-Down, or Utility

Probable cause: the opening location was transferred without checking it against corner, intersection, hold-down, or utility locations established in earlier chapters.
Confirmation: compare the marked opening location against the corner and hold-down locations from Chapters 7 and 8 and any utility layout on the plans.
Correction: stop framing that opening and obtain a documented decision from the design professional; do not relocate a scheduled opening without an approved revision.
Reinspection: re-verify the opening location against the approved revision before resuming framing.

Cleanup

This project ends with completed, verified rough openings ready for Chapter 10 header work or wall sheathing, not with door or window units installed.

  • Remove offcuts, header spacer scrap, and packaging from the work area
  • Confirm every opening label remains legible and visible
  • Store any unused header, king-stud, jack-stud, sill, or cripple stock for the next scheduled opening
  • Store the completed framing-readiness record entries and photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open opening conflict or dimensional discrepancy to the responsible design professional before sheathing proceeds

Field Terms

Rough Opening
The framed opening dimension specified by the door and window schedule for a given unit.
King Stud
The full-height stud fastened to the top and bottom plates at each side of an opening.
Jack Stud (Trimmer)
The shorter stud fastened to the king stud that carries the header's bearing load.
Header
The horizontal structural member spanning an opening, bearing on the jack studs.
Rough Sill
The horizontal member forming the bottom of a window rough opening at the scheduled sill height.
Cripple Stud
A shortened stud installed above a header or below a sill, on the wall's common stud spacing.
Opening Centerline
The plan-dimensioned centerline used to transfer an opening's location to the wall plates.
Egress Opening
An opening subject to minimum clear-opening and sill-height requirements for emergency escape.
Door and Window Schedule
The approved document listing each opening's unit type, size, rough-opening dimensions, and sill height.
Header Reference
The approved specification controlling header material, ply count, dimensions, and bearing for a given opening.
