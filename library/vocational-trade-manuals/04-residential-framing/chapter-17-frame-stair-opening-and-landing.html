Job Overview

This project qualifies you to frame an approved floor opening and structural landing for a basic residential stair. The finished work product is a framed, headed, trimmed, and supported stair opening with an installed landing frame, guarded on every open edge, and documented on a signed stair-opening framing record.

You will review the approved stair plan, confirm the opening and landing have been designed and approved before any floor framing is cut, mark the opening on the existing subfloor, install temporary support below affected joists, remove only the subfloor area required, double the trimmer joists before cutting interrupted joists, set headers with the specified hangers and connections, frame the landing, verify clearances and headroom, and guard every open edge until the stringers in Project 18 are installed.

This project follows the completed floor, wall, ceiling, and roof framing chapters and uses the reference lines, floor framing, and structural connections established in Projects 1 through 16. It sits immediately before Project 18, which builds and installs the stair stringers that will bear on the opening and landing framed here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FR-097 — Lay out the stair opening on the subfloor from controlling wall and floor references and the approved stair plan.
  • FR-098 — Frame the floor opening, including temporary support, joist removal, and doubled trimmer installation.
  • FR-099 — Install opening headers with the specified hangers, bearing, and connections.
  • FR-100 — Build the landing frame according to the approved plan, including perimeter, supports, posts, beams, and joists.
  • FR-101 — Verify opening and landing clearances, headroom, and dimensions against the approved plan.
  • FR-102 — Inspect stair-framing support and document the completed opening, landing, and load path.

Where This Skill Fits

The stair opening interrupts completed floor framing, so it carries every requirement from Project 3's joist installation and Project 4's blocking and bridging into a new structural condition: cut joists that no longer carry load directly but instead transfer it through headers into doubled trimmers. An opening framed without full trimmer support, or a header hung without the specified connector, transfers that missing capacity into whatever loads the stair, landing, and any framing above it during Project 18 and beyond.

The temporary-support and controlled-cutting sequence used here is the same sequence required in Chapter 19 for structural repairs: support first, expose only what is needed, connect before releasing, and inspect before concealment.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved stair plan for floor-to-floor height, opening size, stair width, headroom, landing size, joist direction, headers, trimmers, posts, and guard requirements
  • marking the finished opening perimeter, rough framing limits, header locations, and landing position on the existing subfloor
  • installing temporary support, removing only the required subfloor area, and doubling trimmer joists before cutting interrupted joists
  • installing headers with the specified hangers, bearing, and connections and framing the landing perimeter, supports, posts, beams, and joists
  • verifying opening and landing dimensions, square, elevation, clearances, and headroom
  • installing temporary guards and covers around every open edge and documenting the completed work

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • cut any floor joist before the stair opening and structural landing have been designed or approved and temporary support is installed
  • cut a joist before its adjacent doubled trimmer is installed and fastened
  • select header size, span, or bearing without the approved stair plan; header sizing is not a field decision in this project
  • install treads, risers, or a walking surface across the opening or landing before the framing and structural inspection required by Section H are complete
  • remove temporary guards or covers from an open floor edge before the stair stringers are installed in Project 18

Any condition suggesting a conflict between the opening and a beam, bearing wall, or utility must stop work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Every doubled trimmer in this project carries the full reaction from the header it supports, plus whatever load the cut joists previously carried directly to the sill or beam. Install and fasten the trimmer completely before cutting the joist it will support. Cutting first and doubling second leaves the floor unsupported at the exact moment the opening is most vulnerable.

Setup Standard

Do not cut any joist until the stair geometry and structural opening have been designed or approved (Step 2) and temporary support is installed below the affected floor members (Step 6). A joist cut without confirmed approval and confirmed temporary support must be treated as an unauthorized structural alteration and reported.

Shop Tip

Mark "cut" and "keep" directly on each joist top before removing any subfloor. Once the subfloor is open, it is easy to lose track of which joist was scheduled for removal, especially in a landing area where trimmers, headers, and full-length joists sit close together.

Common Field Fundamentals

Framing a stair opening interrupts a completed structural system rather than building a new one from unbroken bearing. Every step in this project follows the same discipline used for any interruption of completed structure: verify the design and approval, support before cutting, expose only what is needed, connect the new load path before releasing temporary support, and inspect before concealment.

LayoutOpening perimeter, header locations, and landing position marked from controlling references
Support and cuttingTemporary shoring, subfloor removal, and controlled joist cutting
Headers and trimmersDoubled trimmers installed first, then headers set with specified hangers
LandingPerimeter, supports, posts, beams, and joists framed to the plan

Load Path Through the Opening

Every joist cut for the opening transfers its load into a header instead of directly to its original bearing point. Each header transfers that combined load into the trimmer joists at its ends. Each trimmer transfers the combined load of its header reactions, plus its own tributary floor load, to its own bearing point. This project does not proceed past joist cutting until the doubled trimmer that will carry that new load is installed and fastered at both ends.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Rough Framing Limits Before Cutting

Mark the finished opening perimeter first, then calculate and mark the rough framing limits from the finished-opening dimension plus the finish-material allowance stated on the approved plan. Do not cut the subfloor or joists to the finished-opening dimension; a rough opening cut to finished size will not accept the specified finish material.

Critical Safety Cue — Support Before You Cut

Do not cut any joist scheduled for removal until temporary support is installed below the affected floor members and the doubled trimmer joist that will carry the new load is installed and fully fastened. Cutting a loaded joist before its replacement load path is complete can cause sudden local floor failure.

Field Safety Cue — Open Floor Edges

Install temporary guards or covers around every open edge created by subfloor removal before leaving the area unattended at any point in this project, including overnight and during breaks. An open stair opening is a fall hazard from the moment the subfloor is removed until the stringers, treads, and permanent guards are installed.

Code Alert — Stair Geometry and Headroom Requirements Vary

Minimum headroom, landing size, and opening dimensions are set by the approved stair plan and the adopted code in effect for the jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction before treating any plan dimension as final.

E. Layout and Verification (Steps 1–5)

  1. Review the approved stair plan, floor-to-floor height, opening size, stair width, headroom, landing size, joist direction, headers, trimmers, posts, and guard requirements. Confirm the plan revision date against the project's document-control log.
  2. Confirm that the stair geometry and structural opening have been designed or approved before removing or cutting floor framing. Do not proceed to layout without this confirmation recorded on the stair-opening framing record.
  3. Identify the opening location on the subfloor from controlling wall and floor references. Use the reference corners and baseline established in Project 1.
  4. Mark the finished opening perimeter, rough framing limits, header locations, and landing position. Calculate rough framing limits from the finished-opening dimension plus the plan-specified finish-material allowance and mark both the finished and rough lines, labeled separately.
  5. Verify that the opening does not conflict with beams, bearing walls, utilities, ducts, or other structural components. Cross-check the marked opening against the floor framing plan and any installed utility locations; log and refer any conflict before proceeding.

F. Temporary Support and Controlled Cutting (Steps 6–10)

  1. Install temporary support below affected floor members where required by the approved sequence. Position shoring posts and beams per the temporary support plan and verify they are stable before proceeding.
  2. Remove only the subfloor area required to expose the affected joists. Cut along the marked rough framing limits using controlled, supported cutting methods.
  3. Identify and mark joists to remain, joists to be cut, doubled trimmers, and headers. Mark "cut" and "keep" directly on each joist top before removing any additional material.
  4. Install doubled trimmer joists or other side supports before cutting interrupted joists. Fasten each trimmer to its adjacent joist along its full length per the approved schedule before any joist scheduled for removal is cut.
  5. Cut joists only at the approved locations using controlled support. Confirm temporary support is carrying the load at each cut location immediately before cutting.

G. Header Installation and Landing Framing (Steps 11–14)

  1. Install opening headers with the specified hangers, bearing, or connections. Seat each header fully on its trimmer bearing and install the specified hanger before releasing any temporary support at that location.
  2. Connect cut joists to headers using approved hangers and fasteners. Confirm each cut joist end is fully seated in its hanger with the complete specified fastener pattern.
  3. Frame the landing perimeter, supports, posts, beams, and joists according to the plan. Follow the landing framing detail for member size, spacing, and connection.
  4. Install landing subfloor or temporary decking only after framing inspection permits it. Do not install any walking surface across the landing before Section H's verification is complete.

H. Verification, Guarding, and Documentation (Steps 15–20)

  1. Verify opening width, length, square, floor elevation, landing elevation, and clearances. Compare every measurement against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Check the continuous load path from headers and trimmers to supports below. Confirm each trimmer's bearing point and each header's hanger connection matches the approved schedule with no gaps or missed fasteners.
  3. Install temporary guards and covers around all open edges. Guard every edge of the opening and landing before leaving the area unattended.
  4. Verify required headroom, approach, landing dimensions, and future finish allowances. Measure headroom from the plan-specified reference point and compare against the plan minimum.
  5. Correct authorized framing or alignment deficiencies. Re-verify any corrected dimension, connection, or bearing condition before continuing.
  6. Record opening dimensions, structural members, connectors, temporary support, and inspection results. Complete every entry on the stair-opening framing record before signing.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed opening and landing using the same checks regardless of which level or stair run it serves.

  • Documentation check: confirm the approved stair plan, header schedule, landing detail, and temporary support plan are current and referenced on the framing record.
  • Dimensional check: confirm opening width, length, square, and landing dimensions are within the approved plan tolerance.
  • Structural check: confirm every trimmer, header, hanger, and connector matches the approved schedule and shows full bearing and complete fastening.
  • Clearance check: confirm headroom, approach, and landing clearances meet or exceed the plan and adopted code minimums.
  • Guard check: confirm every open edge is guarded or covered.

Complete the Stair-Opening Framing Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 18.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Opening Diagonals Do Not Match Within Tolerance

Probable cause: the header or trimmer was installed off the marked layout line, or one framing member shifted before fastening was complete.
Confirmation: re-measure both diagonals of the opening from the same reference faces used for the perimeter layout, and inspect header and trimmer position against the marked lines.
Correction: loosen and realign the affected member within the approved connection detail, or replace a member that cannot be realigned without exceeding the plan tolerance; do not force the opening square by altering an adjacent joist without approval.
Reinspection: repeat the diagonal check and confirm full bearing and complete fastening after realignment.

Header Does Not Show Full Bearing on the Trimmer

Probable cause: the trimmer was not fully seated or fastened before the header was set, or the hanger was installed with an incorrect fastener or in the wrong location.
Confirmation: inspect the trimmer's full-length fastening to its adjacent joist and the hanger's seating, fastener type, and fastener count against the approved schedule.
Correction: re-fasten the trimmer per the approved schedule, then re-seat and re-fasten the header in its hanger with the complete specified fastener pattern; do not add fasteners not listed for the hanger.
Reinspection: repeat the structural check on the header-trimmer connection.

Headroom Measures Less Than the Plan Minimum

Probable cause: the opening was framed short of the plan-specified rough framing limit, or overhead framing was installed lower than shown on the plan.
Confirmation: re-measure headroom from the plan-specified reference point and compare the opening's actual rough framing limits against the calculated dimension from Step 4.
Correction: log the discrepancy and obtain a documented decision from the design professional; do not enlarge the opening or alter overhead framing without an approved revision.
Reinspection: repeat the headroom check once the documented decision is incorporated.

A Joist Was Cut Before Its Trimmer Was Installed

Probable cause: the cutting sequence in Section F was not followed, or a joist was cut without confirming the adjacent trimmer's fastening was complete.
Confirmation: inspect the affected joist and trimmer for completed fastening and confirm temporary support was in place at the time of the cut.
Correction: install and fully fasten the required doubled trimmer immediately, keeping temporary support in place until fastening is verified complete; report the sequencing error on the framing record.
Reinspection: repeat the structural check on the affected joist, trimmer, and header before removing temporary support.

Landing Elevation Does Not Match the Plan

Probable cause: landing supports were set from an incorrect reference elevation, or a support settled after installation.
Confirmation: re-measure landing elevation from the same benchmark used for the plan dimension and inspect each support for full bearing and stability.
Correction: shim or re-set the affected support to the plan-specified elevation using the approved method; log and refer any condition that cannot be corrected within the approved tolerance.
Reinspection: repeat the landing elevation and level check after correction.

An Open Edge Was Left Unguarded

Probable cause: a guard or cover was removed for access and not reinstalled, or the area was left unattended before guards were installed.
Confirmation: inspect every open edge of the opening and landing for an installed guard or cover.
Correction: install the required guard or cover immediately and restrict access to the area until it is in place.
Reinspection: repeat the guard check before ending work in the area at any point in the project.

Field Terms

Finished Opening
The stair opening dimension shown on the approved plan after all framing and finish material is installed.
Rough Framing Limit
The larger framing-stage dimension that, with finish material added, produces the finished opening.
Trimmer Joist
The doubled member along each long side of the opening that supports the header ends.
Header
The member at each end of the opening that carries the cut joist ends and bears on the trimmers.
Landing
The framed platform at the top or bottom of a stair run, built to the approved perimeter, support, and elevation.
Headroom
The vertical clear distance from the plan-specified reference point to the nearest overhead framing or finish surface.
Temporary Support
Shoring installed below affected floor members before cutting, sized and placed per the approved support plan.
Load Path
The continuous route by which a cut joist's load transfers through a header, into a trimmer, and into its bearing point.
Stair-Opening Framing Record
The signed document confirming dimensions, structural members, connectors, temporary support, and inspection results.
Stop-Work Condition
A defined condition, such as an unapproved opening conflict or missing bearing, that requires work to stop until direction is obtained.
