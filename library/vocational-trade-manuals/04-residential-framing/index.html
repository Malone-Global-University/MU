04-Residential Framing

This field manual provides practical, project-based instruction for residential wood framing. Students build floor systems, walls, roof structures, stair assemblies, and complete structural framing while applying industry-standard layout, fastening, and structural practices.

Overview

Residential Framing develops the skills required to construct the structural framework of a house. Beginning with foundation layout verification, learners progress through floor framing, wall framing, roof framing, stair construction, structural repairs, and a complete capstone framing project. Each chapter centers on a real construction task performed using professional methods and residential building standards.

Learning Outcomes

  • Lay out residential framing accurately from completed foundations.
  • Construct floor framing systems using accepted building practices.
  • Frame interior and exterior walls with properly sized openings.
  • Install structural headers, wall sheathing, and framing connectors.
  • Build ceiling, roof, and stair framing assemblies.
  • Repair damaged framing safely and correctly.
  • Complete a fully framed small residential structure from foundation to roof.
