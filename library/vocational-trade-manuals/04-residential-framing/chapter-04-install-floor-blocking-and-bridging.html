Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install the solid blocking, bridging, squash blocks, web stiffeners, and local joist-bay reinforcement required by the approved floor-framing plan over the joist and rim system completed in Project 03. The finished work product is a fully blocked and bridged floor-joist system, restrained at every required bearing line, braced wall line, opening, and concentrated-load location, and inspected for contact, fastening, and alignment before subfloor sheathing begins.

You will review the blocking and bridging details, verify that joist spacing and bearing are stable, identify every required blocking row and reinforcement location, measure actual joist-bay widths, cut and install solid blocking and bridging in the approved sequence, install manufacturer-specified squash blocks or web stiffeners without damaging engineered-joist flanges, and inspect and correct every block and bridge before the floor is judged ready for the next project.

This project follows directly from Project 03, where the floor joists and rim joists were laid out and installed, and it prepares the floor for Project 05, where structural subfloor sheathing is installed over the completed and restrained joist system.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FR-019 — Identify every required blocking row, bearing line, braced wall line, panel edge, opening, concentrated-load, and cantilever location from the approved floor-framing plan.
  • FR-020 — Measure actual joist-bay widths and cut solid blocking square to fit each bay without forcing joists out of layout.
  • FR-021 — Install solid blocking at every required row, maintaining joist spacing and vertical alignment.
  • FR-022 — Install metal or wood bridging in the required direction, sequence, and stage of loading.
  • FR-023 — Reinforce joist bays with squash blocks, web stiffeners, or approved local reinforcement at concentrated loads, partitions, and openings.
  • FR-024 — Inspect the completed floor-joist system for blocking contact, bridging installation, fastening, and stiffness, and correct deficiencies using the approved method.

Where This Skill Fits

Blocking and bridging restrain the joist system laterally, transfer load at bearing and braced wall lines, and stiffen the floor before it carries subfloor, wall, and live loads. A floor sheathed before its required blocking rows are installed conceals a restraint gap that cannot be corrected without removing the subfloor, and a joist bay reinforced with an improvised block instead of the manufacturer-specified squash block or web stiffener can compromise the engineered joist's rated capacity at that bearing point.

The bay-by-bay measurement discipline used here, rather than assuming every bay is equal, is the same discipline used later in this book at stair openings in Chapter 17 and at every joist-bay reinforcement required in the Chapter 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved blocking, bridging, load-transfer, opening, bearing, and engineered-joist details
  • identifying every required blocking row, bearing line, braced wall line, panel edge, opening, concentrated-load, and cantilever location
  • measuring actual joist-bay widths and cutting solid blocking to fit
  • installing solid blocking, metal or wood bridging, squash blocks, and web stiffeners according to the approved schedule
  • inspecting and correcting blocking and bridging for contact, orientation, fastening, splitting, and utility interference

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • substitute improvised blocking for manufacturer-required squash blocks, web stiffeners, or reinforcement panels
  • cut, notch, or damage engineered-joist flanges to fit a block or reinforcement piece
  • stagger blocking rows or change the fastening method unless the approved plan and fastening method specifically permit it
  • call the approved floor-alignment and stiffness inspection a structural load test
  • proceed to subfloor sheathing before every required blocking row and reinforcement location has been installed and inspected

Any blocking or bridging condition that conflicts with the approved plan, moves joists off layout, or damages an engineered-joist flange must stop work in that bay until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Solid blocking is not interchangeable with manufacturer-specified squash blocks or web stiffeners. A squash block transfers a concentrated vertical load around the engineered joist's web at a bearing point; a web stiffener resists crushing or buckling of the joist web at a bearing or hanger. Installing ordinary blocking stock in place of either component at a location the manufacturer requires it can reduce the load path's capacity even though the floor appears identical from above.

Setup Standard

Do not install blocking or bridging until joist spacing, alignment, and temporary stability from Project 03 are confirmed (Step 2). Blocking installed against joists that are not yet on layout or stable will lock in a layout error that carries into subfloor sheathing.

Shop Tip

Measure every joist bay before cutting blocking for it. Joist spacing accumulates small variations across a floor even when layout is correct, and a blocking piece cut to the plan's nominal spacing can bind two joists together off layout or leave a gap that will not seat fully.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every blocking and bridging installation in this book follows the same sequence: verify the floor is stable and on layout, identify every required row and reinforcement location from the plan, measure actual bay widths, cut and dry-fit, install without forcing joists off layout, and inspect for contact, fastening, and stiffness. Blocking, Bridging, and Reinforcement Discipline

Every blocking row installed in this project must maintain the joist spacing established in Project 03. A blocking piece that is cut long and forced into place will push adjacent joists off layout; a piece cut short will leave a gap that does not transfer load and will not pass inspection. Bridging is installed only after joists are aligned and only at the stage of loading specified by the approved detail, because bridging tensioned too early can be disturbed by later framing work, and bridging installed too late may not fully engage joists that have already begun to twist.

Measurement Cue — Measure Every Bay Before Cutting

Measure the actual width of each joist bay at blocking height before cutting the blocking piece for that bay. Do not cut a full run of blocking to one nominal dimension taken from the plan's joist spacing. Label each cut piece with its bay location so it is installed in the bay it was measured and cut for.

Manufacturer Cue — Engineered-Joist Reinforcement

Install squash blocks, web stiffeners, and other engineered-joist reinforcement only as specified by the joist manufacturer's instructions for the product in use. Do not trim, notch, or damage an engineered joist's flange to fit a reinforcement piece, and do not substitute solid-sawn blocking for a manufacturer-required web stiffener or squash block.

Critical Safety Cue — Open Joist Bays

Do not walk or place material loads across joist bays that are not yet blocked, bridged, or sheathed according to the approved sequence. Restrict access beneath open floor framing until blocking and bridging required at that location are complete and inspected.

Field Safety Cue — Cutting and Fastening Below Joist Level

Support blocking stock fully before cutting, keep hands clear of the cut line, and maintain stable footing when fastening blocking or bridging from beneath the joist system. Clear offcuts and loose fasteners from the work area as blocking rows are completed.

Code Alert — Blocking and Bridging Requirements Vary

Required blocking spacing, bridging type, and reinforcement at braced wall lines and concentrated loads vary by adopted code, jurisdiction, and the specific engineered-joist product in use. Verify the approved plan, engineered-joist manufacturer instructions, and applicable code provisions before treating any blocking or bridging requirement as complete.

F. Review, Verification, and Location Identification (Steps 1–5)

  1. Review the blocking, bridging, load-transfer, opening, bearing, and engineered-joist details. Confirm the revision date of each document against the project's document-control log before using it.
  2. Confirm joist spacing, alignment, and temporary stability before adding restraint. Re-check the spacing and alignment values recorded at the close of Project 03.
  3. Identify every required blocking row, bearing line, braced wall line, panel edge, opening, concentrated load, and cantilever location. Mark each location on the field copy of the plan before cutting any blocking.
  4. Identify manufacturer-required squash blocks, web stiffeners, closure blocks, rim-board details, and reinforcement panels. Cross-reference each location against the engineered-joist manufacturer's instructions.
  5. Select blocking material compatible with the joist system and approved detail. Confirm species, grade, or engineered-product designation before cutting.

G. Measurement, Cutting, and Dry-Fit (Steps 6–8)

  1. Measure actual joist-bay widths rather than assuming all bays are equal. Record each bay's measured width on the field record by its location number.
  2. Cut solid blocking square and label it by location. Cut each piece to its own recorded bay measurement, not to a single nominal dimension.
  3. Dry-fit blocking so it bears fully without forcing joists out of layout. Re-check joist spacing at each bay immediately after dry-fitting the blocking for that bay.

H. Blocking, Bridging, and Reinforcement Installation (Steps 9–16)

  1. Install the first blocking row while maintaining joist spacing and vertical alignment. Fasten from a reference joist and verify spacing before moving to the next bay.
  2. Stagger blocking only when the approved fastening method and plan permit it. Confirm the approved method before offsetting blocking pieces for face-nailing access.
  3. Install additional rows at bearing lines, braced wall lines, openings, and specified intervals. Complete each row across its full length before beginning the next row.
  4. Install metal or wood bridging in the required direction and sequence. Follow the manufacturer or plan-specified diagonal orientation and installation order.
  5. Tension or fasten bridging only after the joists are aligned and at the required stage of loading. Confirm joist alignment immediately before tensioning or final-fastening any bridging run.
  6. Install squash blocks or web stiffeners tight to the specified bearing surfaces without damaging engineered-joist flanges. Verify full contact with the bearing surface before fastening.
  7. Reinforce joist bays at concentrated loads, partitions, plumbing openings, stair openings, or equipment locations only according to approved details. Do not improvise reinforcement at a location the plan does not identify.
  8. Complete required rim-board and end-restraint connections. Confirm every rim-board joint and end-restraint fastener specified by the approved detail is installed.

I. Inspection and Correction (Steps 17–21)

  1. Inspect every block and bridge for contact, orientation, fastening, splitting, and interference with utilities. Record the inspection result for each row or location on the field record.
  2. Verify that blocking has not moved joists off layout or created high points. Re-measure joist spacing and sight the joist tops with a string line or straightedge.
  3. Perform the approved floor-alignment and stiffness inspection without calling it a structural load test. Follow the inspection method stated in the approved detail; do not apply or describe a hand-pressure check as a load test.
  4. Correct loose, undersized, misaligned, or damaged blocking using the approved method. Remove and replace, rather than shim or force, any piece that fails the acceptance standards in Section E.
  5. Record blocking types, locations, fastening, manufacturer details, and corrections. Enter every item from Sections F through I on the field record before proceeding to Project 05.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed blocking and bridging installation using the same checks regardless of which section of the floor it covers.

  • Location check: confirm every plan-required blocking row, bridging run, and reinforcement location is installed and matches the approved plan.
  • Fit check: confirm each blocking piece bears fully in its bay without forcing joists off the recorded layout.
  • Alignment check: confirm joist spacing and vertical alignment after blocking match the values recorded at the close of Project 03.
  • Reinforcement check: confirm squash blocks and web stiffeners are installed per manufacturer instructions with no flange damage.
  • Fastening check: confirm every fastener matches the approved schedule for type, spacing, and pattern, with no splitting, missed framing, or overdriving.
  • Stiffness check: confirm the approved floor-alignment and stiffness inspection is complete and documented without overstating it as a structural load test.

Complete the Blocking and Bridging Inspection Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 05.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Blocking Has Pushed Joists Off Layout

Probable cause: a blocking piece was cut long and forced into its bay, or the bay was not measured before cutting.
Confirmation: re-measure joist spacing on both sides of the affected blocking row and compare it with the values recorded at the close of Project 03.
Correction: remove the oversized blocking piece, re-measure the bay, and cut and install a correctly sized replacement; do not leave forced blocking in place to avoid re-cutting.
Reinspection: repeat the joist-spacing check for the full length of the affected row after correction.

Blocking Row Creates a High or Low Point

Probable cause: blocking stock is not the same actual depth as the joists, or a blocking piece is not seated fully on its bearing.
Confirmation: sight the joist tops with a string line or straightedge across the affected row and measure the actual depth of the blocking stock against the joist depth.
Correction: replace mismatched blocking stock with material matching the joist depth, or reseat and refasten a block that is not bearing fully.
Reinspection: repeat the string-line or straightedge check across the corrected row.

Bridging Will Not Fully Engage or Seat

Probable cause: the joists were not aligned before the bridging was tensioned, or bridging was installed before the required stage of loading.
Confirmation: re-check joist alignment at the affected bay and confirm the installation stage against the approved detail.
Correction: loosen or remove the bridging, re-align the joists, and reinstall the bridging at the correct stage; do not force a bridging member that will not seat.
Reinspection: repeat the joist-alignment check and the bridging installation check for the affected bay.

Squash Block or Web Stiffener Does Not Bear Fully

Probable cause: the reinforcement piece was cut to an incorrect length, or the bearing surface was not confirmed before installation.
Confirmation: re-measure the bearing surface and the installed reinforcement piece against the manufacturer's specified dimension.
Correction: remove and replace the reinforcement piece with one cut to the manufacturer-specified dimension; do not shim a short piece to close the gap.
Reinspection: repeat the manufacturer-instruction check for the corrected location.

Engineered-Joist Flange Is Damaged During Reinforcement Installation

Probable cause: a reinforcement piece was forced into place, or a fastener was driven through the flange rather than the web.
Confirmation: inspect the flange at the affected location for cracking, crushing, or fastener penetration.
Correction: stop work at that joist and obtain direction from the engineered-joist manufacturer or design professional; do not conceal or continue framing over a damaged flange without an approved repair detail.
Reinspection: repeat the manufacturer-instruction check once an approved repair or replacement is complete.

A Required Blocking Row or Reinforcement Location Was Missed

Probable cause: the location was not marked on the field plan copy before installation began, or the plan review in Step 3 did not identify every bearing line, braced wall line, opening, or concentrated-load location.
Confirmation: compare the completed floor against the approved plan location by location and confirm every marked location has installed blocking, bridging, or reinforcement.
Correction: install the missing blocking, bridging, or reinforcement using the same measurement and fitting procedure used for the rest of the floor.
Reinspection: repeat the location check across the full floor after the missing item is installed.

Cleanup

This project ends with a fully blocked, bridged, and reinforced floor-joist system and a completed field record, not with subfloor material staged or installed.

Field Terms

Blocking Row
A line of solid blocking pieces installed between adjacent joists at a bearing line, braced wall line, panel edge, opening, or plan-specified interval.
Bridging
Diagonal metal or wood members installed between joists to resist joist rotation and distribute load between adjacent joists.
Squash Block
A solid vertical block installed tight beneath a concentrated load and beside an engineered joist to carry that load past the joist.
Web Stiffener
A manufacturer-specified reinforcement panel installed against an engineered joist's web at a bearing or hanger location.
Joist Bay
The measured space between the faces of two adjacent joists.
Braced Wall Line
A plan-designated line of framing that provides lateral resistance and requires blocking at the floor level beneath it.
Closure Block
A manufacturer-specified block used to close the end of an engineered-joist cavity at a rim or bearing condition.
Rim Restraint
The connection between the joist ends, rim board, and bearing that resists joist rotation at the floor perimeter.
Cantilever
A joist section that extends beyond its bearing support and requires specified blocking or reinforcement at the bearing line.
Stiffness Inspection
The approved floor-alignment and deflection check performed after blocking and bridging installation, distinct from a structural load test.
