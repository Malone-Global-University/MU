Job Overview

This project qualifies you to lay out, cut, and install a complete floor-joist and rim-joist system over the beams, girders, and sill plates verified and installed in Projects 01 and 02. The finished work product is a fully bearing joist system with rim joists, doubled members, framed openings, and connector installations that match the approved floor-framing plan, ready for the blocking, bridging, and subfloor work covered in Projects 04 and 05.

You will review the floor-framing plan and joist schedule, confirm that beams and sill plates are complete and stable, inspect joist and rim material, stage material in installation order, mark a common layout reference, lay out and mark joist centers and special framing, cut and crown joists, install rim joists and end restraints, set and fasten joists at their marked locations, frame required openings, and verify and document the completed floor-joist system.

This project follows directly from Project 02, which installed the floor girders and beams this joist system bears on. It sits immediately before Project 04, which installs the blocking and bridging that restrains this joist system, and Project 05, which installs the structural subfloor over it.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FR-013 — Lay out joist spacing on opposing sill plates, beams, or rim members using the approved spacing and orientation.
  • FR-014 — Cut floor joists square to the approved length without reducing required bearing, and apply manufacturer field-cutting restrictions to engineered joists.
  • FR-015 — Install rim joists, rim board, or end blocking in the approved sequence and at the correct orientation.
  • FR-016 — Fasten joists to supports using the specified hangers, toe fasteners, clips, straps, or blocking.
  • FR-017 — Maintain and verify a consistent crown direction across all solid-sawn joists in the completed floor.
  • FR-018 — Inspect the completed floor framing for spacing, bearing, crown orientation, end alignment, rim connection, and opening geometry, and document the results.

Where This Skill Fits

The joist layout established here controls every later floor-related project in this book. Blocking rows in Project 04 are located from the same joist-bay dimensions marked here, and every subfloor panel edge in Project 05 depends on joists remaining on the layout marked in this project. A joist installed off spacing, out of crown orientation, or without full bearing carries that error into the finished floor.

The common-reference layout method used here — marking a single layout line on opposing supports and squaring joist centers from it — is the same method used for ceiling joists in Project 13 and, at a larger scale, for wall-stud layout in Projects 06 and 07.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved floor-framing plan, joist schedule, span direction, spacing, opening, bearing, rim-board, hanger, and fastening details
  • inspecting and staging joist and rim material
  • marking a common joist-layout reference and laying out joist centers, doubled members, and special framing
  • crowning, marking, and cutting solid-sawn joists, and observing manufacturer restrictions for engineered joists
  • installing rim joists, end restraints, and intermediate joists at their marked locations
  • installing joist hangers, toe fasteners, bearing restraints, clips, straps, or blocking required at supports
  • framing approved floor openings with headers, trimmers, hangers, and doubled members
  • verifying, correcting, and documenting the completed floor-joist system

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select joist size, species, grade, or spacing independently of the approved floor-framing plan and joist schedule
  • field-cut, notch, drill, or otherwise alter an engineered joist except as explicitly permitted by its manufacturer's instructions
  • install joists before the supporting beams, girders, and sill plates have passed the inspection required by Project 02 and the project's inspection record
  • proceed with subfloor installation before the floor-joist system has passed the inspection required by this project

Any condition that does not match the approved plans, or that suggests damaged, contaminated, or incorrectly identified joist or rim material, must stop work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Every joist in this project is located from the single common layout reference marked in Step 6 of the procedure below. Measuring an individual joist location from the nearest previously installed joist, rather than from the common reference, allows small errors to accumulate across the floor and is the most common cause of subfloor panel edges landing off framing in Project 05.

Setup Standard

Do not begin joist layout until the beams, girders, and sill plates from Project 02 have passed their required inspection and are recorded as stable on the project's inspection record (Step 2). Joists set on unverified bearing must be treated as unverified and re-checked once the supporting members are confirmed.

Shop Tip

Mark the common layout reference on both opposing supports before cutting a single joist. Cutting joists to length before the layout line is struck on both ends risks a mismatch between the plan spacing and the actual bearing-to-bearing dimension measured in Step 7.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every joist-layout sequence in this book, from this chapter's floor joists to the Project 13 ceiling joists, follows the same order: verify the supporting structure and documents, stage and inspect material, mark a common layout reference, lay out and mark special framing, crown and cut members, install end and intermediate members at their marked locations, install required connectors, verify the completed system, and document. Learning that sequence here protects the ceiling-joist and roof-framing projects later in this book.

Common Layout Reference and Crown Orientation

Every joist center in this project is measured from the single common layout reference marked in Step 6. Crown orientation is identified before cutting: sight the narrow edge of each solid-sawn joist, mark the high point of the bow, and install every joist with the marked crown edge up, unless the approved plan specifies a different orientation for a stated reason. A joist installed crown-down settles under load in the direction opposite the rest of the floor and produces a localized low point that the Project 05 subfloor inspection will detect.

Measurement Cue — Lay Out From the Same Reference End

Mark joist centers on both opposing supports from the same reference end identified in Step 6. Do not lay out one end of the floor from one corner and the opposite end from a different corner. Mixing reference ends produces joists that are not square to the rim and bearing lines that do not align across the floor.

Critical Safety Cue — Verify Bearing Before Loading the Floor

Do not walk, stage material on, or apply any load to joists that are not fully seated and fastened at both ends. A joist resting on a bearing surface without hanger, toe-fastener, or blocking connection can roll or shift under a worker's weight. Complete the connection specified for each joist location before treating it as a walking or staging surface.

Field Safety Cue — Open Floor Framing

Treat the area beneath unsheathed floor framing as a fall and impact hazard until the subfloor in Project 05 is installed. Guard or barricade access beneath the joists during cutting and fastening, and do not allow tools, cut-offs, or fasteners to accumulate where they can fall through open joist bays.

Manufacturer Cue — Engineered Joist Restrictions

Do not field-cut, notch, drill, or otherwise alter an engineered floor joist except where the manufacturer's published instructions explicitly permit the specific hole size, location, or cut. Confirm hanger compatibility, minimum bearing length, and web-stiffener requirements against the manufacturer's current installation guide before setting engineered joists.

Code Alert — Fastening and Hanger Schedules Vary by Jurisdiction

Required fastener types, hanger nail patterns, and prescriptive spacing tables vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current fastening and hanger schedule with the approved plan, the connector manufacturer's published load tables, and the authority having jurisdiction before substituting a fastener or connector.

E. Document Review and Material Staging (Steps 1–5)

  1. Review the floor-framing plan, joist schedule, span direction, spacing, openings, bearing, rim-board, hanger, and fastening details. Confirm the revision date of each document against the project's document-control log before using it.
  2. Confirm that beams, sill plates, bearing lines, and framing references are complete and stable. Verify the Project 02 inspection record shows the required approval before proceeding.
  3. Inspect joists and rim material for correct type, grade, length, depth, treatment, damage, and manufacturer identification. Reject or quarantine material that does not match the joist schedule.
  4. Identify engineered joists and confirm all field-cut, hole, notch, and reinforcement restrictions. Record the manufacturer instruction reference used for each engineered-joist location.
  5. Stack and stage joists in installation order with adequate support and weather protection. Support stacked material level and clear of standing water or direct ground contact.

F. Layout and Special Framing (Steps 6–9)

  1. Mark a common joist-layout reference on opposing sill plates, beams, or rim members. Strike the reference line from the reference corner established in Project 01 and label the reference end used.
  2. Lay out joist centers using the approved spacing and maintain the specified orientation at ends and openings. Transfer every joist center square from the common layout reference on both opposing supports.
  3. Mark doubled members, trimmers, headers, cantilevers, stair openings, plumbing zones, and other special framing. Label each special-framing location with its plan designation as it is marked.
  4. Measure each joist location before cutting where field conditions vary. Compare the field-measured bearing-to-bearing dimension against the plan dimension before cutting joists for that location.

G. Crowning and Cutting (Steps 10–12)

  1. Crown solid-sawn joists and mark a consistent crown direction. Sight or string each joist's narrow edge and mark the crown-up direction on the joist before cutting.
  2. Cut solid-sawn joists square to the approved length without reducing required bearing. Cut from the reference end identified in Step 6 and verify the cut is square before installing the joist.
  3. Do not cut engineered joists except as explicitly permitted by their manufacturer. Use only the field-cut, hole, and notch allowances confirmed in Step 4.

H. Setting, Fastening, and Openings (Steps 13–19)

  1. Install rim joists, rim board, or end blocking in the approved sequence. Confirm the rim is straight and plumb before fastening the first joist to it.
  2. Set the first and last joists and temporarily brace them plumb and in line. Verify each is on its marked layout center before fastening.
  3. Install intermediate joists at the marked layout locations. Maintain the common layout reference at every intermediate location; do not measure from the previously installed joist.
  4. Seat joists fully on beams, sill plates, hangers, or ledgers. Confirm full bearing contact at both ends before fastening.
  5. Install joist hangers, toe fasteners, bearing restraints, clips, straps, or blocking required at supports. Use only the fasteners specified for each listed connector.
  6. Frame approved floor openings with headers, trimmers, hangers, and doubled members according to the plans. Install doubled trimmers before cutting or hanging any interrupted joist at the opening.
  7. Maintain required clearance from chimneys, vents, mechanical systems, and other heat or service components. Confirm the clearance dimension against the approved plan or applicable code table at each affected location.

I. Verification and Documentation (Steps 20–24)

  1. Verify joist spacing, bearing, crown orientation, end alignment, rim connection, and opening geometry. Check each item against the Acceptance Standards table before continuing.
  2. Sight the floor framing for high, low, twisted, bowed, or displaced members. Sight along the top edge of the joists from one rim to the other and mark any deviation for correction.
  3. Correct authorized defects using the approved method; do not plane, notch, or sister structural members without permission. Obtain direction from the design professional for any deviation not covered by an approved detail.
  4. Install temporary walking surfaces only where approved and supported. Confirm any temporary decking is fully supported on framing before use.
  5. Record joist layout, field adjustments, connector installation, and inspection results. Complete every entry on the floor-framing inspection record before staging blocking material for Project 04.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed floor-joist system using the same checks regardless of which section of the floor it covers.

  • Documentation check: confirm every required plan, schedule, and manufacturer instruction is current and referenced on the floor-framing inspection record.
  • Layout check: confirm every joist center matches the approved spacing, measured from the common layout reference.
  • Bearing check: confirm every joist is fully seated at both ends with no reduction of required bearing length.
  • Crown check: confirm every solid-sawn joist is installed with a consistent, marked crown orientation.
  • Connection check: confirm hangers, toe fasteners, clips, straps, and blocking match the approved connector schedule and use only listed fasteners.
  • Opening check: confirm every framed opening matches its plan dimensions with doubled trimmers, headers, and hangers installed as specified.

Complete the Floor-Framing Inspection Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 04.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Joist Spacing Drifts Across the Floor

Probable cause: joist centers were measured from the previously installed joist instead of the common layout reference, allowing small errors to accumulate.
Confirmation: measure each questioned joist center directly from the common layout reference, not from the adjacent joist.
Correction: re-mark the affected joist centers from the common layout reference and relocate any joist installed off that reference.
Reinspection: repeat the spacing check across the full floor after correction.

A Joist Is Installed Crown-Down

Probable cause: the joist was not sighted and marked for crown before installation, or the marked crown was not verified before fastening.
Confirmation: sight the installed joist's edge against an adjacent, correctly oriented joist to confirm the direction of bow.
Correction: remove the fasteners, reverse the joist orientation, and re-seat and re-fasten it crown-up before continuing.
Reinspection: repeat the crown check on adjacent joists that may have been installed during the same work session.

A Joist End Lacks Full Bearing

Probable cause: the joist was cut short, the hanger was set at the wrong elevation, or the bearing surface beneath the joist is damaged or out of level.
Confirmation: measure the actual bearing length at the affected end and compare it with the minimum bearing length specified by the plan or manufacturer.
Correction: do not force or shim an undersized bearing condition; replace the joist, reset the hanger, or obtain a design decision if the bearing surface itself is deficient.
Reinspection: repeat the bearing check at the corrected location and at adjacent joists installed from the same reference.

Rim Joist Is Not Straight or Plumb

Probable cause: the rim was fastened before checking straightness, or joist-end thickness variation pushed the rim out of line.
Confirmation: sight the rim along its length and check plumb at several points with a level.
Correction: loosen and realign the rim within the approved connection detail, or replace a warped rim section, before completing the remaining joist-to-rim connections.
Reinspection: repeat the straightness and plumb check after realignment and before installing subfloor.

A Framed Opening Does Not Match Plan Dimensions

Probable cause: the opening was laid out from an incorrect reference, or a trimmer or header was cut to the wrong length.
Confirmation: re-measure the opening width and length from the common layout reference and compare against the approved plan dimension.
Correction: correct the trimmer or header position within the approved detail, or obtain a documented decision if the correction would alter framing outside this project's authorized scope.
Reinspection: repeat the opening-geometry check after correction and before the opening is used as a reference in Project 17.

An Engineered Joist Was Field-Cut or Notched Without Authorization

Probable cause: the joist was treated like a solid-sawn member, or the manufacturer's field-cut restrictions were not reviewed before cutting.
Confirmation: compare the cut or notch against the manufacturer's current installation guide for that product.
Correction: stop work at the affected location and obtain manufacturer or engineer direction; do not conceal the alteration or continue framing over it without a documented repair or replacement decision.
Reinspection: repeat the material and layout check at the affected location once the documented decision is incorporated.

Cleanup

This project ends with a fully bearing, fastened, and inspected floor-joist system, not with blocking, bridging, or subfloor installed.

  • Clear cut-offs, sawdust, and loose fasteners from bearing surfaces and joist bays
  • Remove temporary braces that are not part of an approved temporary-support requirement
  • Confirm every framed opening remains guarded or covered per the project's fall-protection requirements
  • Store the floor-framing inspection record, joist schedule references, and supporting photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open discrepancy and its required decision to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Project 04 begins

Field Terms

Common Layout Reference
The single line marked on opposing supports from which every joist center is measured.
Joist Center
The marked centerline of a joist location at the approved spacing.
Crown
The natural upward bow along a solid-sawn joist's edge, installed facing up unless the plan specifies otherwise.
Rim Joist / Rim Board
The perimeter member that closes joist ends and provides lateral restraint and load transfer.
Doubled Joist
A joist location built from two fastened-together members to carry a concentrated or specified load.
Trimmer Joist
A joist installed alongside a framed opening to support the header at its end.
Bearing Length
The measured length of a joist end actually in contact with its support, beam, hanger, or ledger.
Joist Hanger
A listed metal connector supporting a joist end at a beam, header, or rim, fastened with the connector manufacturer's specified fasteners.
Reference End
The defined end of the bearing span from which joist layout and cut-length measurements are taken.
Floor-Framing Inspection Record
The document recording joist layout, field adjustments, connector installation, and inspection results.
