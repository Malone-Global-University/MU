Job Overview

This project qualifies you to verify that an approved residential foundation or floor platform is ready for framing, and to establish the permanent reference lines that control every wall, beam, and joist placed in later chapters. The finished work product is a documented, corner-marked, and square-verified foundation or platform, with a signed framing-readiness record and a completed discrepancy list where applicable.

You will obtain and review the controlling documents, confirm required inspections are complete, walk and inspect the foundation for defects, verify overall dimensions and diagonals against the approved tolerance, inspect installed anchor hardware, establish and mark the primary framing baseline and all required wall and bearing lines, transfer those lines across corners, and record every measurement and discrepancy before authorizing framing to begin.

This is the first project in this book. It uses foundation and framing plans supplied by the project specification, and it sits immediately after the foundation inspections required by the site's controlling documents. Every beam placed in Project 02, every joist placed in Project 03, and every wall raised in Chapters 6 through 12 is positioned from the reference lines established here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FR-001 — Inspect installed sill plates, anchor bolts, hold-downs, and embedded connectors and verify their layout against the approved anchor schedule.
  • FR-002 — Measure the foundation or platform's overall dimensions and diagonals and confirm them against the approved plan tolerance.
  • FR-003 — Mark exterior wall lines, interior bearing lines, and beam or girder lines from the established framing baseline.
  • FR-004 — Establish and label the primary reference corner and transfer it across all required corners and foundation sections.
  • FR-005 — Check marked framing lines for square using diagonals or the approved layout method and correct authorized errors.
  • FR-006 — Document field dimensions, discrepancies, corrections, and inspection results on the framing-readiness record.

Where This Skill Fits

Every project in this book depends on the reference lines and readiness record produced here. A beam set one inch off the baseline in Project 02, or a wall raised off an unverified corner in Chapter 7, carries that error into every later component. This project exists to catch dimensional, square, and hardware discrepancies while they cost a chalk line and a phone call, rather than after walls are sheathed and trusses are set.

The diagonal-verification method used here to confirm the foundation is square is the same method used in Chapter 6 through Chapter 11 to square walls before they are raised, and again in Chapter 20 to verify the existing floor platform before the capstone shed is framed.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the current approved foundation plan, framing plan, structural details, anchor schedule, survey information, and inspection approvals
  • confirming that required concrete, waterproofing, backfill, and sill-plate inspections are complete before framing loads are applied
  • inspecting the foundation perimeter, sill plates, and embedded hardware for defects or discrepancies
  • measuring overall dimensions and diagonals and comparing them with the approved tolerance
  • establishing, marking, and transferring the primary framing baseline, wall lines, bearing lines, and reference corners
  • preparing a discrepancy list and a signed framing-readiness record

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • place any structural framing member before the framing-readiness record is signed and required inspections are confirmed complete
  • repair foundation cracking, spalling, movement, or missing anchor hardware without direction from the responsible design professional or authority having jurisdiction
  • alter, relocate, or remove embedded anchors, hold-downs, or straps to correct a layout discrepancy
  • proceed with framing when a diagonal, dimension, or hardware discrepancy exceeds the approved plan tolerance, until a documented decision is obtained

Any condition that does not match the approved plans, or that suggests foundation movement or structural distress, must stop work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Every wall line, bearing line, and beam line in this book is transferred from the single primary reference corner established in this chapter. Re-striking a line from a different corner later in the project, rather than transferring it from the recorded baseline, is the single most common source of the cumulative layout error this book's troubleshooting sections describe.

Setup Standard

Do not mark any framing line or authorize framing to begin until the required concrete, waterproofing, backfill, and sill-plate inspections are confirmed complete on the project's inspection record (Step 2). Framing lines marked before that confirmation must be treated as unverified and re-checked once the inspections are confirmed.

Shop Tip

Mark the primary reference corner with a permanent, weather-resistant label before making any other mark. Every wall line, diagonal check, and later framing measurement in this book is transferred from that one corner, so protect it from being scuffed, painted over, or covered by staged material.

Every reference-line layout in this book, from this chapter's foundation baseline to the Chapter 20 capstone wall lines, follows the same sequence: verify documents and inspections, walk and inspect the structure, measure and compare against the plan, establish and transfer references, check square, and document. Learning that sequence here, before any framing member is cut or installed, protects every later project in this book.

Reference Corner, Baseline, and Diagonal Check

Every wall line and bearing line in this project is struck from the single framing baseline, which itself is struck from the primary reference corner. The diagonal check confirms that a rectangular layout is square: when both diagonals of a rectangle measure the same length within the approved tolerance, the corners are square. A layout that measures correctly on both diagonals but shows a length or width dimension outside tolerance is a plan-dimension discrepancy, not a square discrepancy, and must be logged separately.

Measurement Cue — Read Every Dimension From the Same Reference

Measure overall length, width, and diagonals from the same reference faces identified in Step 6 of the procedure below. Do not measure one dimension from the outside face of the foundation and another from the inside face. Mixing reference faces produces a false dimensional or square discrepancy that has no relationship to the actual condition of the foundation.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work for Structural Distress

Stop work in the affected area immediately and notify the responsible design professional or authority having jurisdiction if you observe foundation movement, structural cracking through a footing or wall, misplaced or missing anchor hardware, missing bearing, or dimensional error beyond the approved tolerance. Do not stage material, apply framing loads, or continue layout in the affected area until direction is received.

Field Safety Cue — Perimeter and Access Hazards

Walk the foundation perimeter before working in any area. Identify standing water, debris, uneven or out-of-level bearing surfaces, exposed anchor bolts, and unsafe or unguarded access points such as unbacked steps or open crawlspace access. Correct or flag hazards before beginning measurement and layout work in that area.

Code Alert — Inspection Sequencing Varies by Jurisdiction

The required sequence and documentation for concrete, waterproofing, backfill, and sill-plate inspections vary by jurisdiction and permit condition. Verify the current inspection record and required approvals with the authority having jurisdiction before treating any inspection as complete.

E. Document Review and Site Walk (Steps 1–4)

  1. Obtain the current approved foundation plan, framing plan, structural details, anchor schedule, survey information, inspection approvals, and change documentation. Confirm the revision date of each document against the project's document-control log before using it.
  2. Confirm that required concrete strength, curing, waterproofing, backfill, and sill-plate inspections are complete before framing loads are applied. Record the inspection date, inspector, and approval reference for each on the framing-readiness record.
  3. Walk the foundation perimeter and identify cracks, spalls, missing anchor hardware, damaged sill plates, standing water, debris, out-of-level bearing surfaces, and unsafe access. Photograph and log each condition found, with its location referenced to the plan.
  4. Stop work and obtain direction for foundation movement, structural cracking, misplaced anchors, missing bearing, excessive dimensional error, or unapproved repair work. Log the condition, the date reported, and the party contacted; do not proceed with layout in the affected area until direction is received.

F. Dimensional and Hardware Verification (Steps 5–10)

  1. Confirm the building orientation and identify the controlling corner, baseline, benchmark, and plan dimensions. Mark the controlling corner location on the plan copy used for field reference.
  2. Measure the overall foundation or platform length and width from consistent reference faces. Use the same face — for example, the outside face of the foundation wall — for every length and width measurement, and record the reference face used.
  3. Measure both diagonals and compare them with the approved tolerance. Record both diagonal measurements and their difference on the framing-readiness record.
  4. Verify step locations, beam pockets, pier locations, bearing ledges, openings, and other framing interfaces. Confirm each against its plan-specified location and elevation.
  5. Inspect sill sealer, sill plates, anchor bolts, washers, nuts, hold-downs, straps, and embedded connectors already installed. Confirm type, spacing, embedment, and condition against the anchor schedule.
  6. Verify sill-plate location, projection, straightness, joints, treatment requirements, and bearing contact. Confirm the sill plate is the treatment category required by the anchor schedule and bears fully on the foundation.

G. Establishing and Marking Reference Lines (Steps 11–16)

  1. Establish the primary framing baseline from the approved reference corner. Strike the baseline with the chalk line along the controlling exterior wall face identified in Step 5.
  2. Mark exterior wall lines, interior bearing lines, beam lines, and other required framing references. Label each line with its plan designation as it is struck.
  3. Transfer reference lines across corners and intersecting foundation sections. Use the framing square or plumb bob to carry each line accurately through every corner it passes.
  4. Verify perpendicular lines using diagonals or the approved layout method. Re-check the diagonal comparison from Step 7 at each rectangular section formed by the marked lines.
  5. Check that framing lines agree with anchor, hold-down, beam-pocket, and utility-penetration locations. Confirm no marked wall or bearing line conflicts with an inspected hardware location from Step 9.
  6. Mark and label reference corners, wall faces, centerlines, floor elevations, and control points that must remain visible. Use a permanent, weather-resistant marking method and protect each control point from later staging or trade work.

H. Discrepancy Resolution and Readiness Documentation (Steps 17–20)

  1. Record all field dimensions and compare them with the approved plans. Enter every measurement from Sections F and G on the framing-readiness record.
  2. Prepare a discrepancy list identifying each variance, its location, required decision, and responsible authority. Include every condition logged in Steps 3, 4, 9, and 15 that has not already been resolved.
  3. Correct authorized layout errors and obtain approved revisions for design conflicts. Re-strike and re-verify any line corrected under this step before proceeding.
  4. Complete and sign the framing-readiness record before material is staged or cut. Confirm every required entry from Sections E through H is complete before signing.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed layout using the same checks regardless of which section of the foundation or platform it covers.

  • Documentation check: confirm every required plan, schedule, survey document, and inspection approval is current and referenced on the framing-readiness record.
  • Dimensional check: confirm overall length, width, and diagonals are within the approved plan tolerance, measured from consistent reference faces.
  • Hardware check: confirm sill plates, anchor bolts, hold-downs, straps, and embedded connectors match the approved anchor schedule.
  • Reference-line check: confirm every wall line, bearing line, beam line, and control point is marked, labeled, square, and free of conflict with installed hardware.
  • Discrepancy check: confirm every logged variance has an assigned decision, responsible authority, and, where authorized, a completed correction.

Complete the Framing-Readiness Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 02.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Diagonals Do Not Match Within Tolerance

Probable cause: the foundation or platform corners are out of square, or one or both diagonal measurements were taken from inconsistent reference faces.
Confirmation: re-measure both diagonals from the same reference faces used for the overall length and width, and confirm the tape is fully extended and unobstructed along its full length.
Correction: if the reference faces were inconsistent, re-measure correctly before concluding the foundation is out of square; if the foundation is genuinely out of square beyond the approved tolerance, log the discrepancy and obtain direction from the design professional before marking framing lines from that section.
Reinspection: repeat the diagonal check after any correction or design direction is applied.

Overall Dimension Does Not Match the Plan

Probable cause: a construction tolerance was exceeded during foundation work, or the field measurement used a different reference face than the plan dimension.
Confirmation: re-measure using the exact reference faces called out on the plan, and compare the result against the plan dimension and its stated tolerance.
Correction: log the discrepancy with its exact location and magnitude, and obtain a documented decision from the design professional; do not adjust framing lines to a location not shown on the approved plan or revision.
Reinspection: repeat the dimensional check once the documented decision is incorporated.

Anchor Hardware Does Not Match the Schedule

Probable cause: incorrect anchor type, spacing, or embedment was installed, or the anchor schedule referenced is not the current approved revision.
Confirmation: confirm the anchor schedule revision date, then re-inspect the installed hardware for type, diameter, spacing, and embedment against that current schedule.
Correction: log the discrepancy and obtain direction from the design professional; do not add, relocate, or substitute anchor hardware without an approved detail.
Reinspection: repeat the hardware check once the documented decision is incorporated.

A Reference Line Conflicts With an Anchor or Beam Pocket

Probable cause: the line was struck from an incorrect reference face or corner, or the anchor or beam pocket was installed off its plan-specified location.
Confirmation: re-verify the reference corner and baseline used to strike the line, and re-measure the anchor or pocket location against the plan.
Correction: if the line was struck incorrectly, erase and re-strike it from the verified baseline; if the anchor or pocket is off location, log the discrepancy and obtain direction before marking that section's framing lines as final.
Reinspection: repeat the reference-line check and the hardware check for the affected section.

The Primary Reference Corner Was Disturbed or Lost

Probable cause: the corner mark was not protected before staging material, or later work covered or removed the mark without transferring it first.
Confirmation: inspect the recorded corner location for a legible mark; if absent or ambiguous, the reference cannot be confirmed.
Correction: re-establish the primary reference corner from the approved plan and permanent site benchmarks, re-mark it using a protected method, and re-verify any line struck from the corner while it was ambiguous.
Reinspection: repeat the dimensional and square checks on any line struck without a confirmed reference corner.

Required Inspections Were Not Confirmed Before Layout Began

Probable cause: the site inspection record was not checked before work began, or an inspection was scheduled but not yet completed.
Confirmation: review the site inspection record for the concrete, waterproofing, backfill, and sill-plate inspections and confirm each approval date and inspector.
Correction: stop layout work in the affected area until the missing inspection is confirmed complete; treat any lines already marked as unverified until the inspection record is confirmed.
Reinspection: repeat the full document-review step and re-verify all previously marked lines once the inspection is confirmed.

Cleanup

This project ends with a documented, corner-marked foundation or platform and a signed readiness record, not with structural material staged or installed.

  • Remove layout stakes, flags, and temporary marking materials that are not part of a protected control point
  • Clear debris, chalk residue, and marking materials from the foundation perimeter and access points
  • Confirm every protected control point remains legible and undisturbed before leaving the site
  • Store the signed framing-readiness record, discrepancy list, and supporting photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open discrepancy and its required decision to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Project 02 begins

Field Terms

Primary Reference Corner
The one foundation or platform corner all baseline and diagonal measurements are taken from.
Framing Baseline
The single straight reference line struck from the primary reference corner along the controlling exterior wall face.
Wall Line
A marked line representing the exterior or interior face of a plan-specified wall.
Bearing Line
A marked line representing the centerline or face of a plan-specified interior bearing wall, beam, or girder.
Control Point
A labeled, protected mark identifying a reference corner, wall face, centerline, or elevation that must not be disturbed.
Diagonal Check
A comparison of the two corner-to-corner measurements of a rectangular layout used to confirm square.
Anchor Schedule
The approved document specifying anchor type, diameter, spacing, and embedment for sill-plate connections.
Discrepancy List
The record identifying each variance from the approved plan, its location, required decision, and responsible authority.
Framing-Readiness Record
The signed document confirming inspections, dimensions, hardware, reference lines, and discrepancies before framing begins.
Stop-Work Condition
A defined condition, such as foundation movement or missing bearing, that requires work to stop until direction is obtained.
