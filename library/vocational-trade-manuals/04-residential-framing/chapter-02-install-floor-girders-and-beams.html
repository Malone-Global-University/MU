Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare and install the approved floor girders or beams that carry the floor-joist system down through posts, pockets, or piers to the foundation. The finished work product is a set of beams identified, cut, seated, connected, and verified against the approved beam schedule, with a completed beam-installation record.

You will review the beam plan and connector schedule, confirm that bearing surfaces have passed inspection, inspect and reject unsuitable beam stock, cut solid-sawn members only where the plan permits field cutting, lift and seat each beam section on its specified bearing points, install the specified post caps, straps, and connectors, and verify elevation, level, line, splice position, and load path before the beam system is loaded.

This project follows the reference lines and readiness record established in Project 01 and sits immediately before the floor joists in Project 03. Every joist placed in Project 03 bears directly on the beams installed here, so an error in beam elevation, bearing, or connection carries forward into the entire floor system.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FR-007 — Identify beam locations, orientation, and bearing points from the approved beam plan and schedule.
  • FR-008 — Cut beam members to the approved length and end condition using the method permitted for the specified material.
  • FR-009 — Set beams on their specified bearing points, posts, pockets, or piers using the approved lifting and temporary-restraint method.
  • FR-010 — Support and verify bearing points, including post caps, bearing plates, and shims specified by the design.
  • FR-011 — Fasten beam connections using the approved connector and fastener schedule.
  • FR-012 — Inspect beam alignment, elevation, level, line, splice position, and connector seating and correct authorized deficiencies.

Where This Skill Fits

The beam system installed in this project is the load path every floor joist in Project 03 depends on, and it is the first structural member-sizing decision in this book that must come directly from the approved beam schedule rather than field judgment. The bearing, splice, and connector discipline taught here — full seating at every support, splices only over approved bearing, and listed fasteners for listed connectors — repeats at every later beam, header, and ridge installation in this book.

The temporary-restraint and controlled-lift procedures introduced here are the same procedures expanded in Chapter 11 for wall raising and in Chapter 15 for truss setting.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved beam plan, beam schedule, bearing details, post locations, connector schedule, and elevations
  • inspecting, rejecting, or quarantining beam material that does not meet the approved specification
  • marking, cutting, and preparing solid-sawn beam members only where the approved plan permits field cutting
  • lifting, placing, and seating beams on their specified bearing points using the approved worker count, rigging, and access controls
  • installing post caps, straps, bolts, structural screws, hangers, and other connectors according to the approved schedule
  • verifying bearing, elevation, alignment, splice position, and connector seating and documenting the completed installation

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select beam size, species, grade, ply count, or span rating without the approved beam schedule, prescriptive table, or a qualified designer's direction
  • field-cut, drill, notch, or otherwise alter an engineered beam member except as explicitly permitted by the manufacturer
  • splice a beam at any location other than a specified support
  • substitute a generic fastener for a connector's listed fastener, or release lifting equipment or temporary restraint before the beam is fully seated and secured

Any beam material that arrives damaged, delaminated, incorrectly identified, or excessively warped must be quarantined and referred, not installed with a field-improvised correction.

Trade Note

A beam that is fully seated but out of level, or seated level but short of full bearing at one end, both fail this project's acceptance standard. Bearing and level are checked separately in the procedure below because a beam can pass one check and fail the other.

Setup Standard

Do not lift or set any beam section until the foundation, sill plates, pockets, piers, and posts at every bearing point for that section have passed the inspection confirmed in Project 01 (Step 2 of this project). A bearing surface that has not passed inspection must be treated as unverified.

Shop Tip

Mark each beam section with its plan location and orientation before it leaves the staging area. A beam that is correctly built but installed at the wrong plan location, or installed end-for-end, produces the same rework as an incorrectly cut beam.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every beam installed in this project is placed, seated, and connected using the same sequence: verify bearing readiness, inspect and stage material, measure before cutting, lift and seat under controlled restraint, connect to the approved schedule, and verify the completed load path. Applying that sequence consistently, section by section, is what keeps a multi-section beam run level and in line.

Bearing, Splices, and the Load Path

A beam section that is level and correctly cut but seated with less than the specified minimum bearing length at one end does not meet this project's acceptance standard, even though it may appear stable. Splices are permitted only directly over an approved bearing point; a splice located between supports is a different structural condition than the approved design and must not be created in the field to use shorter stock or avoid a difficult lift.

Measurement Cue — Measure Bearing-to-Bearing Before Cutting

Measure the actual bearing-to-bearing dimension in the field before cutting any beam section, and compare it with the plan dimension from Step 7 of the procedure below. Do not cut a beam to the plan dimension alone if the field measurement differs; log the difference and confirm the correct length before cutting.

Critical Safety Cue — Controlled Lift and Immediate Restraint

Lift and place each beam section using the approved worker count, rigging method, and access controls identified in the beam plan. Install temporary restraint immediately after the beam is seated and before any worker releases the lifting equipment or steps away. Do not work beneath a beam that is supported only by lifting equipment or unsecured temporary props.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Engineered Beam Members

Do not cut, drill, notch, or otherwise alter an engineered wood beam except as explicitly permitted by the manufacturer's published instructions for that product. Seal treated or engineered-member cut ends when the manufacturer's instructions require it, using the specified sealant.

Code Alert — Beam Sizing and Bearing Requirements Vary

Minimum bearing length, connector requirements, and permitted field-cutting practices vary by jurisdiction, product listing, and the approved design. Verify the current beam schedule and connector schedule with the responsible design professional or authority having jurisdiction before treating any bearing or connection as complete.

E. Document Review and Material Verification (Steps 1–5)

  1. Review the approved beam plan, beam schedule, bearing details, post locations, connector schedule, elevations, and temporary-support requirements. Confirm the revision date against the project's document-control log before using it.
  2. Verify that the foundation, sill plates, pockets, piers, posts, and bearing surfaces have passed the required inspection. Confirm the inspection date, inspector, and approval reference for each bearing point against Project 01's framing-readiness record.
  3. Confirm beam material, grade, treatment, engineered-product designation, moisture condition, and delivery documentation. Compare delivered material against the approved beam schedule before staging.
  4. Reject or quarantine damaged, split, delaminated, excessively warped, contaminated, or incorrectly identified members. Tag and separate rejected material and record the condition and quantity on the beam-installation record.
  5. Establish a controlled staging and lifting area that keeps beams dry, supported, and clear of traffic. Confirm the staging area matches the access and worker-count requirements of the lift plan.

F. Marking and Cutting (Steps 6–10)

  1. Mark each beam by plan location and required orientation. Label the reference end identified on the approved schedule before measuring.
  2. Measure bearing-to-bearing dimensions and compare them with the plan before cutting. Log any difference between the field measurement and the plan dimension before proceeding.
  3. Mark cut lines from a defined reference end and face. Use the framing square to strike the cut line square to the reference face.
  4. Cut only solid-sawn members when the approved plan permits field cutting; use manufacturer-controlled procedures for engineered members. Confirm the plan's field-cutting permission before cutting any member.
  5. Seal treated or engineered-member cut ends when required by the product instructions. Apply the specified sealant to the full cut-end surface.

G. Bearing Preparation and Lift (Steps 11–15)

  1. Prepare beam pockets, post caps, hangers, bearing plates, shims, or other supports specified by the design. Install each bearing component at its plan-specified location before the beam is lifted.
  2. Confirm that temporary supports and lifting equipment are stable and rated for the operation. Verify the rated capacity against the beam section's weight before lifting.
  3. Lift and place the first beam using the approved worker count, rigging method, and access controls. Follow the lift plan's assigned worker positions.
  4. Seat the beam fully on each bearing point without damaging anchors, connectors, masonry, or treated surfaces. Confirm full contact at every bearing point before releasing the lift.
  5. Install temporary restraint before releasing lifting equipment. Confirm the restraint is secured at both ends before any worker releases the rigging.

H. Remaining Sections and Connections (Steps 16–20)

  1. Set remaining beam sections in the approved sequence. Follow the sequence shown on the beam plan; do not reorder sections without a documented reason.
  2. Complete approved splices only over specified supports and in the required orientation. Verify the splice location against the bearing point identified on the plan before fastening.
  3. Install post caps, straps, bolts, structural screws, hangers, or other connectors according to the approved schedule. Use only the listed fastener for each listed connector.
  4. Verify minimum bearing, member elevation, straightness, level, line, splice position, and connector seating. Check each item against the Acceptance Standards table in Section D.
  5. Check beam-to-sill, beam-to-post, and beam-to-foundation load paths. Confirm no bearing point has an unsupported gap between the beam and the structure below.

I. Correction and Documentation (Steps 21–23)

  1. Correct authorized alignment or seating deficiencies without reducing bearing or altering the member. Use shims or approved bearing adjustments; do not trim a beam to correct a bearing discrepancy without design direction.
  2. Record beam identification, dimensions, elevations, connections, field cuts, and inspection results. Complete every entry on the beam-installation record before proceeding to Project 03.
  3. Protect the installed beam system from displacement and unapproved loading until floor joists are installed. Mark the beam system as not-for-loading until the Project 03 floor-joist inspection confirms it is ready.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed beam system using the same checks regardless of which section it covers.

  • Material check: confirm every installed beam section matches the approved beam schedule for size, species/grade or product designation, and condition.
  • Bearing check: confirm full bearing length at every support with no gap, and confirm post caps, bearing plates, and shims match the approved detail.
  • Elevation and level check: confirm beam elevation and level are within the approved tolerance at every bearing point.
  • Splice check: confirm every splice occurs directly over an approved bearing point in the required orientation.
  • Connector check: confirm post caps, hangers, straps, bolts, and structural screws match the approved connector schedule and use only listed fasteners.
  • Documentation check: confirm beam identification, dimensions, elevations, connections, field cuts, and inspection results are complete on the beam-installation record.

Complete the Beam-Installation Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 03.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Beam Is Seated but Out of Level

Probable cause: an uneven bearing surface, a missing or incorrect shim, or a post cap installed at the wrong elevation.
Confirmation: check level at each bearing point individually rather than only at midspan, and measure each bearing surface's elevation against the plan.
Correction: install or adjust shims specified for that bearing condition at the low point; do not correct an elevation error by trimming the beam.
Reinspection: repeat the level check across the full beam length and at every bearing point after shimming.

Bearing Length Is Short at One End

Probable cause: the beam was cut to an incorrect length, the bearing point was built at the wrong location, or the beam shifted during placement.
Confirmation: measure actual bearing length at the affected end and compare it with the minimum stated on the approved plan or connector documentation.
Correction: reposition the beam if shifting caused the shortfall; if the bearing point or beam length is genuinely incorrect, log the discrepancy and obtain direction from the design professional before loading that section.
Reinspection: repeat the bearing-length check at the corrected location before proceeding.

Splice Does Not Align With an Approved Bearing Point

Probable cause: beam sections were ordered or cut to lengths that do not match the plan's bearing spacing, or a section was installed out of sequence.
Confirmation: compare the as-built splice location against the bearing points marked on the approved plan.
Correction: do not fasten a splice that falls between supports; obtain a corrected member length or a design decision before completing that section.
Reinspection: repeat the splice-location check once the corrected member or design decision is installed.

Connector Does Not Match the Approved Schedule

Probable cause: an incorrect connector model was staged, or a generic fastener was substituted for a connector's listed fastener.
Confirmation: compare the installed connector and fastener against the approved connector schedule for model, size, and fastener type.
Correction: remove and replace the incorrect connector or fastener with the specified item; do not leave a substituted fastener in place with the reasoning that it appears equivalent.
Reinspection: repeat the connector check after the correct connector and fastener are installed.

Beam Material Was Rejected After Staging Began

Probable cause: damage, delamination, or excessive warp was not identified during initial delivery inspection.
Confirmation: re-inspect the remaining staged material using the same criteria applied in Step 4, and confirm the extent of the affected material.
Correction: quarantine the rejected material, log the quantity and condition, and obtain replacement material matching the approved beam schedule before continuing that section's installation.
Reinspection: repeat the material-verification step on replacement material before it is staged for lifting.

Cleanup

This project ends with an installed, connected, and documented beam system, protected from loading until the floor joists in Project 03 are ready.

  • Remove offcuts, packaging, and rejected material from the staging and lift area
  • Store unused connectors, fasteners, and shims by type for use in later beam or header installations
  • Confirm every temporary restraint remains installed and secured before leaving the site
  • Store the completed beam-installation record with the project's permanent documentation alongside Project 01's framing-readiness record
  • Communicate any open discrepancy and its required decision to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Project 03 begins

Field Terms

Bearing Point
A support surface where a beam section is required to seat, as identified on the approved beam plan.
Full Bearing
Contact between the beam and its support across the minimum bearing length with no gap.
Reference End
The beam end from which cut-line measurements are taken, as identified on the plan or schedule.
Splice
A joint between two beam sections, permitted only directly over an approved bearing point.
Temporary Restraint
Bracing or strapping installed immediately after a beam is seated and before lifting equipment is released.
Load Path
The continuous connection from beam to post, pier, or foundation that transfers floor loads downward.
Post Cap
A connector installed at the top of a post to seat and secure a beam bearing on that post.
Beam Schedule
The approved document specifying beam size, material, span, and bearing requirements for each beam location.
Connector Schedule
The approved document specifying connector type, fastener, and installation pattern for each beam connection.
Beam-Installation Record
The signed document confirming beam identification, dimensions, elevations, connections, and inspection results.

You have completed the second project in the residential framing sequence: installing floor girders and beams. This project required you to identify beam locations and bearing points from the approved plan (FR-007), cut beam members to the approved length and end condition (FR-008), set beams on their specified bearing points using controlled lifting and temporary restraint (FR-009), support and verify bearing points including post caps and shims (FR-010), fasten beam connections to the approved schedule (FR-011), and inspect beam alignment, elevation, level, splice position, and connector seating (FR-012).
