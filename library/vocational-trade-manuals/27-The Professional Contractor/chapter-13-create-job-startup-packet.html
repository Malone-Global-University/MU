Job Overview

This project produces one written, approved Job Startup Packet for a single signed and accepted residential project. The finished work product is a controlled packet that verifies the contract and required notices, deposit or payment conditions, license and insurance status, permits and approvals, final scope and selections, procurement and subcontractor readiness, staffing and equipment, site logistics, customer communication setup, billing setup, and a signed mobilization authorization.

You will confirm the correct legal entity and parties on the agreement, verify payment and financing conditions have been lawfully satisfied, confirm license, insurance, bond, and permit-holder status remain active, confirm the final scope and pending selections, confirm procurement and subcontractor readiness, confirm staffing and site logistics, conduct an internal startup meeting and, where appropriate, a customer preconstruction meeting, and obtain formal mobilization approval from an authorized manager.

This project sits directly downstream of the accepted proposal (Chapter 8), the contract-ready scope of work (Chapter 9), and the change-order and allowance system (Chapter 10), and it depends on active licensing (Chapter 3), insurance and bonding (Chapter 4), and banking and accounting (Chapter 5) already being in place. No project may mobilize on the strength of this chapter's packet alone if any of those upstream systems are incomplete.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • PC-073 — Confirm that a signed, delivered, and retained agreement authorizes the project before any startup activity begins.
  • PC-074 — Confirm deposit or payment terms have been lawfully satisfied before scheduling mobilization.
  • PC-075 — Collect and confirm all required customer and material selections before startup.
  • PC-076 — Confirm the project schedule, procurement, and subcontractor readiness against the startup date.
  • PC-077 — Organize permits, licenses, insurance, and required project documents into one controlled packet.
  • PC-078 — Prepare a job-start checklist and obtain a formal, signed mobilization approval.

Where This Skill Fits

This project converts an accepted contract into an authorized field mobilization. It draws on the accepted proposal and pricing worksheet (Chapters 7–8), the contract-ready scope of work (Chapter 9), the change-order and allowance system (Chapter 10), the customer intake and site-visit file (Chapter 11), and the sales pipeline handoff (Chapter 12). It also depends on active licensing (Chapter 3), current insurance and bonds (Chapter 4), and functioning banking and accounting controls (Chapter 5), and it references the Book 24 estimate, Book 25 permit and compliance file, and Book 26 project-management plan rather than creating disconnected duplicate systems.

The completed startup packet becomes the reference document for Chapter 14's customer-communication process during construction, and it is the first entry in the project record that Chapter 18's job-cost review and Chapter 19's closeout will later reconcile against.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • verifying the correct legal entity, customer parties, and signed, delivered agreement
  • confirming deposit, financing, escrow, trust, or payment conditions have been lawfully satisfied
  • confirming license, registration, insurance, bond, and permit-holder status remain active
  • confirming permits, approvals, final scope, plans, allowances, and approved changes
  • confirming customer and contractor responsibilities, selections, procurement, and subcontractor readiness
  • confirming staffing, site logistics, occupied-home rules, and preconstruction documentation
  • confirming customer communication, billing setup, and document responsibilities
  • producing the job-start checklist, conducting the startup meeting, and obtaining formal mobilization approval

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • schedule or begin mobilization based on an unsigned proposal, verbal acceptance, text message, handshake, or unverified deposit
  • treat a certificate of insurance, a tax form, or a verbal assurance as complete verification of coverage, licensing, or subcontractor qualification
  • authorize field work while a required legal, payment, permit, insurance, design, safety, or procurement condition remains unresolved
  • insert unreviewed legal, payment, or warranty language into the packet; jurisdiction-specific language remains controlled by Chapters 2 through 10 and by counsel

Any condition that cannot be verified through the approved contract, licenses, insurance file, permits, or company records must be resolved before mobilization is authorized.

Trade Note

Every confirmation in this chapter is written into the startup packet, not held as an informal understanding among sales, estimating, and field staff. A mobilization authorized from memory rather than from a completed packet is the most common source of unlicensed exposure, unpermitted work, unpaid deposits, and preventable customer disputes diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not authorize mobilization while a critical legal, payment, permit, insurance, design, safety, or procurement condition remains unresolved. A mobilization authorized before the packet is complete must be treated as unauthorized and halted until the outstanding condition is corrected.

Shop Tip

Build the pending-selections list and the long-lead procurement list at the same time, not in sequence. A missing selection almost always blocks a purchase order, and finding both gaps in the same review avoids a second round of customer contact right before the crew is scheduled to arrive.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every approved project passes through the same four checkpoints before mobilization: is the project legally authorized to proceed, is the company currently licensed, insured, and permitted to perform it, is the scope and procurement chain actually ready, and has the customer and jobsite been prepared to receive the crew. Confirming these checkpoints in writing, before dispatch, keeps the job-start decision auditable and keeps field personnel from absorbing risk that belongs to sales, accounting, or licensing.

Verification Discipline

A condition is only marked complete on the packet after it is confirmed against a primary source — the signed contract, the deposit receipt, the active license record, the current certificate of insurance, the issued permit, or the subcontractor's own current document. A condition is never marked complete on the basis of a verbal assurance, a screenshot without verification, or an assumption that a prior project's status still applies.

Documentation Cue — Record Every Confirmation With Its Source

When a condition on the startup packet is marked confirmed, record what was checked, the source document, the date checked, and who checked it. Do not simply check a box. The source record protects the business if a license lapses, a policy is cancelled, or a deposit is later disputed.

Critical Compliance Cue — No Mobilization on an Incomplete Packet

Do not schedule or authorize mobilization while any critical legal, payment, permit, insurance, design, safety, or procurement condition remains unresolved on the packet. A packet that is mostly complete is not an authorized packet.

Operational Discipline Cue — One Packet, One Copy of Record

Maintain a single controlled copy of the Job Startup Packet in the project file established under Chapter 2 records. Do not allow separate, informal startup notes to circulate among sales, estimating, and field personnel. Every later reference in Chapters 14, 18, and 19 points to this single approved copy.

Professional Advisor Cue — Payment and Cancellation Conditions Require Counsel-Approved Procedure

Deposit, financing, escrow, and cancellation or commencement conditions must be confirmed using the counsel-approved procedure established when the contract templates were reviewed in Chapters 8 and 9. Do not rely on an informal payment confirmation to satisfy a statutory waiting period or right of cancellation.

E. Legal, Payment, and Authority Verification

  1. Confirm the correct legal entity and customer parties are shown on the agreement. Compare the contract signature block against the Chapter 2 company record and the customer's ownership or authority documentation.
  2. Confirm the contract and required notices were signed, dated, delivered, and retained through the approved process. Verify each required notice was delivered by the method the jurisdiction and contract require, and file a copy in the project record.
  3. Confirm cancellation or commencement conditions with counsel-approved procedures. Do not schedule any startup activity before a statutory cancellation period, where one applies, has run.
  4. Confirm deposit, financing, escrow, or trust payment conditions have been satisfied lawfully. Verify the payment against the bank or escrow record established under Chapter 5, not against a customer statement alone.
  5. Do not schedule mobilization based solely on an unverified payment screenshot or verbal assurance. Treat any unconfirmed payment as an open critical condition on the packet.
  6. Confirm license, registration, insurance, bond, and permit-holder status remain active. Check the current record in the Chapter 3 licensing file and the Chapter 4 insurance and bond file, not a prior project's file.
  7. Confirm permits, HOA approvals, utility approvals, design approvals, and lender or insurer conditions required before start. Record the status of each and the source confirming it.
  8. Confirm the final scope, plans, specifications, addenda, clarifications, allowances, and approved changes. Reconcile the current document set against the Chapter 9 scope and the Chapter 10 approved-change log before continuing.

F. Scope, Selections, Procurement, and Subcontractor Readiness

  1. Confirm customer and contractor responsibilities. Verify the responsibility assignments in the Chapter 9 scope of work still match the current, approved project condition.
  2. Confirm owner-furnished items, owner work, separate contractors, utilities, and access. Record the status of each item the customer is responsible for providing before startup.
  3. Confirm all required selections with product, model, finish, quantity, lead time, and approval. Verify each selection against a signed selection form, not an undocumented showroom conversation.
  4. Identify selections still pending and the latest responsible decision date. Assign a named responsible party and a specific date for every outstanding decision.
  5. Confirm long-lead items, purchase orders, deposits, acknowledgments, shipping, and delivery. Record confirmed delivery dates and compare them against the target startup date and schedule.
  6. Confirm subcontractor agreements, licenses, insurance, scope, schedule, and startup documents. Verify each subcontractor against the Chapter 16 qualification file before adding them to the packet.
  7. Confirm employees, supervisors, competent persons, training, payroll, vehicles, tools, equipment, and PPE. Verify staffing assignments against the Chapter 15 onboarding and training records.
  8. Confirm project-management plan, WBS, baseline schedule, lookahead, and procurement log from Book 26. Attach the current versions to the packet rather than recreating them.
  9. Confirm inspection plan and compliance controls from Book 25. Attach the current permit and inspection schedule to the packet.
  10. Confirm estimate, budget, cost codes, allowance log, and change log. Verify the financial basis in the packet matches the current Book 24 estimate and Chapter 10 logs.
  11. Confirm certificates, notices, lien procedures, and document responsibilities with counsel-approved process. Record who is responsible for issuing each required notice or lien document during the project.

G. Site Logistics, Customer Readiness, and Documentation

  1. Confirm site address, access, parking, delivery, storage, sanitation, power, water, security, and waste. Record each logistics item and how it will be managed on the startup date.
  2. Confirm occupied-home rules, pets, children, vulnerable occupants, tenants, neighbors, hours, dust, noise, and daily shutdown. Record the agreed rules in writing and route them to the field team before mobilization.
  3. Confirm utility shutoffs, emergency contacts, fire extinguisher, first aid, and incident procedures. Verify these are in place and known to the assigned crew before the first workday.
  4. Confirm preconstruction photographs, property-condition documentation, and customer valuables or exclusions. Complete or schedule this documentation before any work begins.
  5. Confirm floor, wall, landscape, vehicle, and common-area protection. Record what protection is required and who is responsible for installing it before work starts.
  6. Confirm customer communication channel, decision authority, meeting schedule, and emergency contact. Record these in the project contact and authority directory.
  7. Confirm billing schedule, invoice contacts, purchase authorizations, and accounting setup. Verify the project is set up correctly in the Chapter 5 accounting system before the first invoice is due.
  8. Confirm certificates, notices, lien procedures, and document responsibilities with counsel-approved process. Re-verify this item against the current project condition immediately before the startup meeting.
  9. Create the project contact and authority directory. Compile all contacts gathered in this section into the one-page directory.
  10. Create the current document index and field-issue set. List every current plan, specification, selection, permit, and change order, and assemble the set to be issued to the field.
  11. Create the site-start checklist by first-day sequence. Sequence the checklist in the order the field lead will actually perform first-day tasks.

H. Startup Meeting and Mobilization Approval

  1. Conduct the internal startup meeting with sales, estimating, operations, accounting, safety, and field leadership. Review every section of the packet with each function represented.
  2. Conduct customer preconstruction meeting where appropriate. Confirm schedule, access, disruption, and decision expectations directly with the customer before mobilization.
  3. Record all decisions, missing items, constraints, and assigned actions. Document every open item from the startup meeting with a responsible party and due date.
  4. Do not authorize mobilization while a critical legal, payment, permit, insurance, design, safety, or procurement condition remains unresolved. Hold the mobilization date until every critical item on the packet is confirmed complete.
  5. Obtain startup approval from the authorized manager. Route the completed packet to the designated manager for review and signature.
  6. Issue the packet to controlled recipients and archive the approved version. Distribute the current document index and site-start checklist to the field team and store the approved packet in the company record system.
  7. Record actual mobilization authorization date. Enter the confirmed date on the packet as the record of when the project was authorized to begin.

Common Packet Inspection

Inspect the completed startup packet using the same checks regardless of project size or trade.

  • Legal check: confirm the agreement, notices, and cancellation or commencement conditions are verified against the counsel-approved procedure.
  • Payment check: confirm deposit, financing, or escrow conditions are verified against the bank or escrow record, not a screenshot or verbal statement.
  • Authority check: confirm license, insurance, bond, and permit-holder status are sourced from the current Chapter 3 and Chapter 4 records.
  • Scope and procurement check: confirm pending selections and long-lead items each have a responsible party and date, and subcontractor documents are on file.
  • Site and customer check: confirm logistics, occupied-home rules, and communication channels are documented and issued to the field team.
  • Approval check: confirm the startup meeting occurred and a designated manager signed and dated the mobilization approval before the recorded mobilization date.

Complete the Job Startup Approval Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before mobilization is scheduled.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Crew Is Dispatched Before the Deposit Is Confirmed

Probable cause: scheduling relied on a customer's verbal statement or a payment screenshot rather than a verified bank or escrow record.
Confirmation: compare the scheduled startup date against the Chapter 5 bank or escrow confirmation date for this project.
Correction: pause mobilization, verify the payment through the accounting system, and reschedule only after confirmation is on file.
Reinspection: repeat the payment-verification check on the corrected packet before rescheduling.

A Permit Is Not Yet Issued When the Crew Arrives

Probable cause: the permit status field on the packet was marked complete from an application date rather than an issuance date.
Confirmation: check the permit authority's current record against the Book 25 permit file for this project.
Correction: stop the permitted portion of the work, correct the packet to reflect actual issuance status, and hold mobilization for that scope until the permit is issued.
Reinspection: repeat the authority-verification check before rescheduling the permitted work.

Material Is Not on Site Because a Selection Was Never Finalized

Probable cause: the pending-selections list did not assign a responsible party and decision date, or the deadline passed without escalation.
Confirmation: compare the current selection status against the decision date recorded in Section F.
Correction: escalate the outstanding selection to the customer through the Chapter 14 communication process and adjust the schedule; do not substitute an unapproved product.
Reinspection: repeat the selections check before confirming the next mobilization date.

A Subcontractor Arrives Without Current Insurance on File

Probable cause: the subcontractor's certificate expired between qualification under Chapter 16 and this project's startup date, and the packet was not re-checked before mobilization.
Confirmation: compare the certificate expiration date against the packet's approval date.
Correction: remove the subcontractor from the site until a current certificate is obtained and verified.
Reinspection: repeat the subcontractor-readiness check before allowing site access.

Field Staff Are Working From an Outdated Scope or Drawing

Probable cause: the current document index was not updated after a Chapter 10 approved change, or superseded documents were not removed from the field set.
Confirmation: compare the field-issued documents against the current document index and the Chapter 10 approved-change log.
Correction: reissue the current document set, collect and destroy or clearly mark superseded copies, and confirm the field team acknowledges the update.
Reinspection: repeat the scope and selections check on the corrected document set.

The Packet Was Approved Without a Startup Meeting

Probable cause: scheduling pressure led a manager to sign the mobilization approval before sales, estimating, operations, accounting, safety, and field leadership reviewed the packet together.
Confirmation: check the packet for a recorded startup meeting date and attendee list.
Correction: suspend mobilization, convene the startup meeting, resolve any items it surfaces, and obtain a new, dated mobilization approval.
Reinspection: repeat the approval check before mobilization proceeds.

Cleanup

This project ends with the approved packet filed for reuse throughout construction, not left as informal notes.

  • Store the signed and dated Job Startup Packet in the project file within the company record system established in Chapter 2
  • Store the project contact and authority directory, current document index, and site-start checklist alongside it
  • Record the confirming source and date for every legal, payment, license, insurance, and permit item
  • Record the actual mobilization authorization date and the authorizing manager's name
  • Distribute the current document index and site-start checklist only to persons with field, supervisory, or project-management authority under the Chapter 2 signing-authority matrix
