Job Overview

This project produces one written, verified contractor licensing and registration file for every jurisdiction named on the Chapter 1 service-area and project-fit matrix. The finished work product is a license-requirement matrix cross-referenced by jurisdiction, service, classification, authority, qualification, fee, bond, insurance, and renewal, supported by an application document checklist, an experience and project-history file, an examination-preparation plan where an exam is required, a financial and background submission plan, a renewal calendar, a license-status verification procedure, and a stop-work procedure.

You will identify the official contractor, trade, business, tax, permit, and consumer-protection authorities for each jurisdiction in the approved service area; match the Chapter 1 service menu against available license classifications; identify scope, monetary, employee, and subcontracting limits; identify work that requires a separately licensed trade professional; gather official applications and instructions; submit applications only after ownership, insurance, and bond information are reconciled; and build the renewal and stop-work controls that keep the business operating on active authority.

This project narrows the Chapter 1 business model to only the services and jurisdictions the business is actually authorized to perform. Chapters 4 through 20 — insurance, banking, pricing, contracts, marketing, and the capstone operating system — all reference the active service and jurisdiction list approved at the end of this project.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • PC-013 — Identify the official licensing authorities governing every jurisdiction in the approved service area.
  • PC-014 — Determine the trade-license classification, scope, and qualification requirements that match each service in the Chapter 1 model.
  • PC-015 — Identify local business, registration, and permit-holder rules that apply in addition to state or trade licensing.
  • PC-016 — Identify examination, experience, financial, background, and bond requirements attached to each license classification.
  • PC-017 — Track application, issuance, and renewal deadlines using a controlled calendar with responsible owners.
  • PC-018 — Create and approve a compliance checklist and stop-work procedure covering every active license and registration.

Where This Skill Fits

This project builds directly on the approved Chapter 1 Business Model Statement and the professionally reviewed structure and company record system created in Chapter 2. The jurisdiction list, service menu, and ownership information gathered in those chapters are the direct inputs to the licensing research performed here.

This project sits ahead of every chapter that depends on active legal authority to operate: the insurance and bonding file in Chapter 4 is matched to the classifications confirmed here, the pricing worksheet in Chapter 7 and every proposal in Chapter 8 may reference only license numbers actually issued, the contract scope in Chapter 9 must respect the scope and monetary limits identified here, and no marketing claim in Chapter 17 may state "licensed" for a jurisdiction or service not confirmed active in this file. The capstone operating system in Chapter 20 verifies this file directly before authorizing limited launch.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • identifying the official contractor, trade, business, tax, permit, and consumer-protection authorities for every jurisdiction in the approved service area
  • matching the Chapter 1 service menu to available license classifications and their scope, monetary, and subcontracting limits
  • identifying work that requires a separately licensed electrician, plumber, HVAC technician, engineer, architect, environmental contractor, or other professional
  • identifying application, examination, experience, financial, background, and bond documentation requirements
  • identifying local business-license, zoning, home-occupation, and permit-holder requirements
  • submitting applications only after legal name, entity, ownership, qualifier, insurance, and bond information are reconciled
  • building a renewal calendar, license-status verification procedure, and stop-work procedure

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • advertise, bid, contract, or accept payment for any service or in any jurisdiction until the applicable license, registration, or permit-holder status is confirmed active by the issuing authority
  • rely on this chapter, a third-party summary, or a prior jurisdiction's requirements as a substitute for the current official source
  • submit an application under a legal name, entity, or ownership structure that has not been reconciled with the Chapter 2 formation and registration records
  • treat a pending application, a pending exam date, or an expired license as equivalent to active authority

Any service or jurisdiction that cannot be supported by a currently active license, registration, or permit-holder status must be removed from the active service and jurisdiction list before contracting proceeds.

Trade Note

Licensing rules are set by the authority, not by this chapter. Every classification name, fee, monetary limit, and renewal period in this project must be confirmed directly with the applicable state, county, or municipal authority before it is relied upon. This chapter teaches the research method and the record system, not a specific jurisdiction's current rule.

Setup Standard

Do not advertise, bid, or contract under a pending license unless the issuing authority expressly permits it in writing. A commitment made on an unconfirmed or pending license must be treated as unauthorized and unwound.

Shop Tip

Build the renewal calendar (Section H) at the same time as the license-requirement matrix, not after the first license is issued. A license researched but not calendared for renewal is the most common cause of an unnoticed lapse.

Research Discipline

A candidate service is only marked "supported" on the matrix after the specific classification, scope limit, and qualification requirement have been confirmed directly through the issuing authority's official source. A classification confirmed only through a third-party summary, a competitor's advertisement, or an out-of-date bulletin is marked "unconfirmed" and re-verified before reliance.

Documentation Cue — Record the Official Source for Every Requirement

For every fee, classification, scope limit, exam requirement, and renewal period recorded on the matrix, record the official source (agency name, publication, or statute/rule citation) and the date it was retrieved. Do not record a requirement without its source; an undated, unsourced entry cannot be trusted at renewal time.

Critical Compliance Cue — No Advertising or Bidding on a Pending or Unconfirmed License

Do not advertise, quote, bid, or accept payment for any service until its license or registration is confirmed active by the issuing authority's verification system. A submitted application is not an active license, and a passed examination is not an issued license.

Operational Discipline Cue — One Matrix, One Copy of Record

Maintain a single controlled copy of the license-requirement matrix in the Chapter 2 company record system. Do not allow separate, informal notes about license status to circulate among sales, estimating, or field personnel. Every proposal and contract in later chapters must reference the single approved copy.

Professional Advisor Cue — Ambiguous Classification or Scope Requires Review

When a service could plausibly fall under more than one classification, when a project size approaches a monetary limit, or when a local registration requirement is unclear, route the specific question to a qualified licensing advisor or attorney before marking the matrix "supported." Do not resolve the ambiguity by assumption.

E. Authority and Jurisdiction Identification

  1. List every state, county, city, town, district, or other jurisdiction in the approved service area. Copy the jurisdiction list directly from the Chapter 1 service-area matrix onto the license-requirement matrix.
  2. Identify the official contractor, trade, business, tax, permit, and consumer-protection authorities for each jurisdiction. Record the specific board or agency name for each function; do not use a general "state government" placeholder.
  3. Record official contact information, web resources, office locations, and application channels. Enter the current phone number, web address, physical address, and application portal for each authority.
  4. Identify whether a general contractor, home-improvement, residential, specialty, trade, salesperson, qualifier, or local registration applies. Mark the applicable classification type for each jurisdiction and service.

F. Classification Matching and Scope Limits

  1. Match the service menu from Chapter 1 to the available license classifications. Enter the matching classification name next to each Chapter 1 service on the matrix.
  2. Identify scope limits, monetary limits, project-type limits, employee limits, and subcontracting rules associated with each classification. Record each limit as a specific figure or rule, sourced to the issuing authority.
  3. Identify work that requires a separately licensed electrician, plumber, HVAC technician, engineer, architect, environmental contractor, or other professional. Mark each such task as excluded from self-performed scope and cross-reference the Chapter 6 trade-boundary definitions once available.
  4. Identify whether a business entity license, individual qualifier, responsible managing employee, registered agent, or local representative is required. Record the specific requirement for each classification.
  5. Identify ownership, experience, education, examination, financial, background, fingerprint, reference, or employment documentation requirements. List each requirement on the application document checklist for that classification.
  6. Identify application, examination, license, renewal, continuing-education, recovery-fund, bond, and registration fees. Record each fee amount and its source and date retrieved.
  7. Identify required insurance, workers' compensation, bond, financial statement, or credit evidence. Record each requirement for coordination with the Chapter 4 insurance and bonding project.
  8. Identify local business-license, tax, zoning, home-occupation, signage, vehicle, and contractor-registration requirements. Record each local requirement separately from the state-level classification.
  9. Identify permit-holder restrictions and who may pull permits. Record the specific rule for each jurisdiction.
  10. Identify advertising, proposal, contract, vehicle, website, invoice, and jobsite-display requirements for license numbers or business names. Record each display requirement for use by the Chapter 8 and Chapter 17 templates.
  11. Identify customer notice, cancellation, contract, deposit, payment, lien, warranty, and complaint requirements that affect operations. Record each requirement for reference by Chapter 9's attorney-reviewed contract scope.
  12. Identify disciplinary, reporting, ownership-change, qualifier-change, address-change, and name-change obligations. Record each ongoing obligation and its reporting deadline.

G. Application Preparation and Submission

  1. Obtain official applications, instructions, candidate bulletins, statutes, rules, and local forms. Download or request the current version directly from each authority; do not rely on a cached or third-party copy.
  2. Do not rely on third-party summaries when official sources are available. Replace any third-party-sourced entry on the matrix with the confirmed official-source entry before proceeding.
  3. Create a license-requirement matrix by jurisdiction, service, classification, authority, qualification, fee, bond, insurance, and renewal. Consolidate Sections E and F into the single controlling matrix.
  4. Create an application document checklist. List every required form and supporting document by classification, with a completed/pending status column.
  5. Create an experience and project-history file supporting qualification. Compile the owner's and qualifier's project history in the format the authority requires for experience verification.
  6. Create an examination-preparation plan where an exam is required. Record the exam name, content outline, registration deadline, and study plan.
  7. Create a financial, bond, insurance, background, and reference submission plan. List each required submission, its source, and its responsible preparer.
  8. Submit applications only after legal name, entity, ownership, qualifier, insurance, and bond information are reconciled. Cross-check every application field against the Chapter 2 company record before filing.
  9. Track submission date, receipt, deficiency notice, response, examination, approval, issuance, and expiration. Record each event on the application document checklist as it occurs.
  10. Respond to agency requests accurately and retain all correspondence. File every agency communication in the Chapter 2 company record system.
  11. Do not advertise or contract under a pending license unless the authority expressly permits it. Confirm this rule in writing with the authority before relying on an exception.
  12. Create local registration files for each municipality where required. Build a separate file per municipality with its specific registration, fee, and renewal requirements.

H. Renewal, Verification, and Stop-Work Controls

  1. Create a renewal calendar with advance reminders, responsible person, continuing education, financial documents, insurance, bond, and fee requirements. Enter every license and registration with its renewal date and a reminder set well in advance of expiration.
  2. Create a license-status verification procedure before proposals, contracts, permit applications, and renewals. Write the specific steps used to confirm active status through the authority's official verification system before each commitment.
  3. Create a stop-work procedure for suspension, lapse, qualifier departure, insurance cancellation, bond lapse, or unresolved disciplinary action. Name the trigger conditions, the required immediate response, and the responsible manager.
  4. Review the compliance matrix with qualified counsel or licensing professionals where ambiguity remains. Record the reviewer's name, date, and specific comments addressed.
  5. Approve the active service and jurisdiction list only after the required authority is confirmed. Sign and date the final matrix, removing any service or jurisdiction that could not be confirmed.
  6. Archive official evidence and update the Chapter 1 business model if authority is narrower than planned. File the issued licenses, registrations, and confirmation records, and revise the Chapter 1 service or jurisdiction list to match confirmed authority.

Common Model Inspection

Inspect the completed licensing file using the same checks regardless of which jurisdiction or classification is involved.

  • Authority check: confirm every jurisdiction's contractor, trade, business, tax, and permit authorities are identified with current contact information.
  • Classification check: confirm every Chapter 1 service is matched to a specific classification with sourced scope, monetary, and subcontracting limits.
  • Application check: confirm every application document checklist item is sourced to the official authority, not a third-party summary.
  • Renewal check: confirm every license and registration has a renewal date, responsible person, and advance-notice reminder.
  • Stop-work check: confirm a stop-work procedure names the trigger conditions and required response.
  • Professional-review check: confirm ambiguous items were routed to and resolved by a qualified reviewer.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Proposal Is Issued for a Service Not Yet Licensed

Probable cause: the license-requirement matrix was not checked before the proposal template was populated, or a pending application was treated as active.
Confirmation: compare the proposed service and jurisdiction against the license-requirement matrix's confirmed-active column.
Correction: withdraw or revise the proposal to remove the unlicensed service, and route future proposals through the license-status verification procedure before issuance.
Reinspection: repeat the authority check on the corrected proposal and matrix.

A License or Registration Lapses Without Notice

Probable cause: the renewal calendar was not created at the same time as the matrix, or a reminder was set too close to the expiration date.
Confirmation: compare the license's actual expiration date against the renewal calendar's reminder date and responsible person.
Correction: immediately trigger the stop-work procedure for any lapsed license, renew as soon as legally possible, and rebuild the calendar entry with an earlier reminder.
Reinspection: repeat the renewal check across every license and registration on the calendar.

A Classification Was Matched Incorrectly to a Service

Probable cause: the classification was matched from a third-party summary or a similar business's advertisement rather than the official source.
Confirmation: re-verify the classification and its scope limit directly with the issuing authority.
Correction: correct the matrix entry, remove the service from the active list until the correct classification is held, and apply for the correct classification if it differs from what is currently issued.
Reinspection: repeat the classification-match check on the corrected matrix.

An Application Is Rejected for Incomplete or Mismatched Information

Probable cause: the application was filed before legal name, entity type, ownership, or qualifier information was reconciled with the Chapter 2 company record.
Confirmation: compare every field on the rejected application against the Chapter 2 formation and ownership documents.
Correction: correct the mismatched fields, resubmit with the complete application document checklist attached, and record the deficiency notice and response in the company record system.
Reinspection: repeat the application-completeness check before the next submission.

Work Proceeds in a Jurisdiction Not Named in the Model

Probable cause: a customer, referral, or opportunity in a new jurisdiction was accepted without confirming that jurisdiction's licensing and registration requirements.
Confirmation: compare the project address against the jurisdiction list on the license-requirement matrix.
Correction: stop work in the unlisted jurisdiction under the stop-work procedure until Sections E through H are completed and confirmed for that jurisdiction.
Reinspection: repeat the authority and classification checks on the updated matrix before resuming work.

The Matrix Was Approved Without Resolving a Known Ambiguity

Probable cause: a classification, scope limit, or local-registration question was left unresolved to meet an internal deadline.
Confirmation: check the matrix for any entry marked "unconfirmed" or lacking a sourced answer.
Correction: suspend reliance on the affected service or jurisdiction until a qualified licensing advisor or attorney resolves the question and the matrix is updated.
Reinspection: repeat the professional-review check before re-approving the matrix.
