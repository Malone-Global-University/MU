Job Overview

This project qualifies you to perform an exterior-only shutdown inspection and cleaning of one residential outdoor condenser or heat-pump unit identified in Project 01. The finished work product is one cleaned, unobstructed outdoor unit with restored clearances, an intact cabinet and coil, and a completed Outdoor Unit Inspection and Cleaning Worksheet.

You will shut off the thermostat call and open the outdoor disconnect and branch breaker; verify the unit cannot start; inspect the pad, cabinet, coil, refrigerant-line exterior, and surrounding vegetation; clear the required clearance zone; clean the coil using only the manufacturer-approved method and product; reinstall all guards and panels; and restore power and observe an authorized cooling call from a safe distance.

This project does not authorize opening the refrigerant circuit, working inside an energized electrical compartment, or servicing the compressor, capacitor, contactor, or fan motor. Those repairs remain outside this book's scope and require a licensed HVAC or refrigerant-certified technician.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HV-085 — Shut down the outdoor unit safely.
  • HV-086 — Clear vegetation and debris from the required clearance zone.
  • HV-087 — Clean condenser fins using the manufacturer-approved method.
  • HV-088 — Inspect the coil for damage.
  • HV-089 — Verify clearance around the unit.
  • HV-090 — Restore exterior airflow and observe operation.

Where This Skill Fits

This project uses the equipment identification and shutdown discipline established in Projects 01 and 02, applied to the outdoor unit identified there. It follows the accessible-service boundary set by Project 13's cabinet inspection: this project inspects and cleans only from outside the refrigerant circuit and energized compartment, exactly as Project 13 inspected only the accessible furnace or air-handler cabinet.

A restricted outdoor coil reduces heat transfer, raises operating pressures, and can accelerate compressor wear. Restoring airflow here supports the airflow-balancing work in Project 11 and the comfort-diagnostic work in Project 19, which both assume the outdoor unit is unobstructed.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • shutting off the thermostat call and opening the outdoor disconnect and branch breaker
  • verifying the unit cannot start before any cleaning work begins
  • inspecting the pad, mounting, cabinet, fan guard, coil, refrigerant-line exterior, insulation, disconnect exterior, drainage, and surrounding vegetation
  • removing leaves, mulch, trash, and vegetation from the specified clearance zone
  • removing only exterior guards or panels identified by the manufacturer for routine coil access
  • cleaning the coil using only the manufacturer-approved method and product
  • reinstalling all guards and panels and restoring the disconnect and breaker
  • observing an authorized cooling call from a safe distance

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • remove a cover that exposes energized line-side terminals
  • open, cut, loosen, braze, solder, charge, recover, evacuate, or otherwise alter the refrigerant circuit
  • service the compressor, capacitor, contactor, fan motor, or control board
  • apply high-pressure spray, bend fins, or use an unapproved coil-cleaning product
  • continue work when damaged refrigerant tubing, oil staining, hissing, electrical scorching, unstable mounting, severe corrosion, or damaged fan components are found

Any suspected refrigerant leak, electrical damage, unstable mounting, or fan-component damage identified in this chapter must be recorded and escalated to a licensed HVAC or refrigerant-certified technician; it is not corrected as part of this project.

Trade Note

Everything in this chapter happens from outside the refrigerant circuit and the energized electrical compartment. The unit is de-energized before a single leaf is brushed off the coil, and it stays de-energized until every guard and panel is back in place. A coil that looks clean is not the goal here — a coil that is clean, undamaged, and reassembled with all safeties restored is the goal.

Common Field Fundamentals

An outdoor condenser or heat-pump coil rejects or absorbs heat through direct contact with outdoor air. Leaves, grass clippings, cottonwood seed, mulch, and dirt build up on the fin face and block that airflow. A blocked coil raises head pressure, reduces capacity, and shortens compressor life. This project restores that airflow using only the manufacturer-approved cleaning method, without disturbing the refrigerant circuit or any energized component.

Restricted Covers and the Refrigerant Circuit

Do not remove a cover that exposes energized line-side terminals, and do not open, cut, loosen, braze, solder, charge, recover, evacuate, or otherwise alter the refrigerant circuit at any point in this project. Only the exterior guards or panels the manufacturer identifies for routine coil access may be removed, and only after the disconnect and breaker are open and verified.

Critical Safety Cue — De-Energize Before Removing Any Guard or Panel

Shut off the thermostat call, open the outdoor disconnect and branch breaker, and verify with a non-contact voltage tester that the unit cannot start before removing any guard or panel. Do not remove a cover that exposes energized line-side terminals. Stop work immediately for damaged refrigerant tubing, oil staining, hissing, electrical scorching, unstable mounting, or severe corrosion, and escalate to a licensed HVAC or refrigerant-certified technician.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Cleaning Product, Method, and Rinse Direction

Use only the coil-cleaning product, dilution, application method, and rinse direction specified by the equipment manufacturer. Apply low-pressure water only in the manufacturer-specified direction. Do not use high-pressure spray, and use a fin comb only for minor accessible fin correction and only when the manufacturer instructions specify it.

Field Safety Cue — Water Control and Footing

Cover the electrical enclosure, motor openings, controls, and nearby building surfaces with plastic sheeting before rinsing. Keep footing stable on wet pads and grass. Direct rinse water away from the building foundation and away from any occupied walkway.

E. Shutdown and Exterior Inspection

  1. Review the outdoor-unit manufacturer instructions, required clearances, coil-cleaning method, and electrical shutdown points. Record the approved coil-cleaning method and clearance distance on the worksheet before proceeding.
  2. Shut off the thermostat call. Set the thermostat to the off condition and confirm the outdoor unit is not cycling.
  3. Open the outdoor disconnect and branch breaker as required. Apply lockout or tag-out to prevent unexpected restoration.
  4. Verify the unit cannot start. Use the non-contact voltage tester at accessible exterior test points before proceeding.
  5. Do not remove a cover that exposes energized line-side terminals. Identify and avoid these covers before beginning any inspection or cleaning step.
  6. Inspect the pad, mounting, cabinet, fan guard, coil, refrigerant-line exterior, insulation, disconnect exterior, drainage, and surrounding vegetation. Record the condition of each item on the worksheet.
  7. Stop work for damaged refrigerant tubing, oil staining, hissing, electrical scorching, unstable mounting, severe corrosion, or damaged fan components. Record the condition and location and escalate to a licensed technician; do not proceed with cleaning in the affected area.

F. Clearance and Water Protection

  1. Remove leaves, mulch, trash, and vegetation from the specified clearance zone. Clear to the manufacturer-specified distance on every side requiring clearance and record the measured clearance achieved.
  2. Protect the electrical enclosure, motor openings, controls, and nearby building surfaces from water. Cover these areas with plastic sheeting before any rinsing begins.

G. Coil Access and Cleaning

  1. Remove only exterior guards or panels identified by the manufacturer for routine coil access and only after verifying safe conditions. Confirm the disconnect remains open and the voltage test remains valid before removing any panel.
  2. Vacuum or brush loose surface debris in the permitted direction. Use the soft-bristle brush and shop vacuum on the coil face without forcing debris deeper into the fins.
  3. Apply only the approved coil-cleaning method and product. Follow the manufacturer's specified dilution and application method exactly; do not substitute a different product.
  4. Rinse with low-pressure water in the manufacturer-specified direction. Direct the rinse from the specified side of the coil only.
  5. Do not bend fins with high-pressure spray. Confirm the nozzle setting matches a low-pressure rinse before applying water.
  6. Use an approved fin comb only for minor accessible fin correction and only when specified. Confirm the manufacturer instructions authorize fin-comb use before applying one.
  7. Inspect for large crushed areas, corrosion, impact damage, blocked base openings, and coil separation. Record each condition found and whether it requires licensed evaluation.
  8. Clean debris from the base pan without disturbing refrigerant or electrical components. Remove loose debris from the pan using the shop vacuum, keeping the attachment away from refrigerant tubing and wiring.

H. Reassembly and Restoration

  1. Reinstall all guards and panels. Confirm every fastener is engaged and each panel is seated in its original position.
  2. Verify that required clearances, drainage, and service access are restored. Confirm the base pan drains freely and the clearance zone remains clear.
  3. Restore the disconnect and breaker. Remove lockout or tag-out devices and close the disconnect and breaker.
  4. Initiate an authorized cooling call. Set the thermostat to call for cooling within the system's normal operating range.
  5. Observe exterior fan operation, vibration, noise, and unobstructed discharge from a safe distance. Confirm the fan starts smoothly and discharges without obstruction.
  6. Stop for abnormal noise, fan contact, breaker operation, or failure to start. Shut down and de-energize the unit again before investigating further, and escalate if the cause is not an accessible, correctable condition within this project's scope.
  7. Document cleaning, clearances, observed damage, and licensed-service recommendations. Complete every field on the worksheet before closing out the project.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Fan Does Not Start After Restoration

Probable cause: the disconnect or breaker was not fully restored, a panel is not seated against a door interlock, or an unrelated equipment fault exists.
Confirmation: re-verify the disconnect and breaker positions and confirm every guard and panel is fully seated and secured.
Correction: restore the disconnect and breaker fully and reseat any loose panel; if the fan still does not start, de-energize the unit again and escalate to a licensed technician.
Reinspection: repeat the authorized cooling-call observation after the correction.

The Coil Remains Restricted After Cleaning

Probable cause: debris was pushed deeper into the fin bed rather than removed, or the approved cleaning product was not applied at the correct dilution.
Confirmation: visually inspect the fin face from both sides for remaining debris and compare against the before photograph.
Correction: repeat the approved cleaning method, using the correct dilution and rinse direction; do not increase water pressure beyond the manufacturer's specification.
Reinspection: photograph the coil again and confirm visible improvement before closing out the project.

Fins Are Found Bent or Crushed During Inspection

Probable cause: prior impact damage, high-pressure cleaning by a previous service, or debris impact.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area for the extent of bending or crushing and whether airflow is significantly blocked.
Correction: correct only minor, accessible fin damage using an approved fin comb where the manufacturer instructions specify it; record more extensive damage and refer it to a licensed technician.
Reinspection: confirm the corrected area matches the surrounding fin pattern and airflow is visibly restored.

Water Enters the Electrical Enclosure During Rinsing

Probable cause: the enclosure was not fully covered before rinsing, or the rinse direction was not controlled.
Confirmation: inspect the plastic sheeting placement and the rinse angle used.
Correction: stop rinsing immediately, do not restore power, and allow the enclosure to dry completely; escalate to a licensed technician before restoring power if any water intrusion is confirmed.
Reinspection: confirm the enclosure is dry and cleared by a licensed technician, if escalated, before restoring the disconnect and breaker.

A Stop-Work Condition Is Found During Exterior Inspection

Probable cause: damaged refrigerant tubing, oil staining, hissing, electrical scorching, unstable mounting, or severe corrosion.
Confirmation: do not approach, disturb, or further investigate the condition; confirm only what is visible from a safe distance.
Correction: stop work in the affected area, record the condition and its location, and escalate to a licensed HVAC or refrigerant-certified technician; do not proceed with cleaning until the condition is addressed.
Reinspection: resume the remaining inspection and cleaning steps only after the condition has been corrected or a qualified professional has authorized continued work.

Cleanup

This project ends with the outdoor unit fully reassembled, restored to service, and the surrounding area left clean and unobstructed.

  • Confirm all guards and panels are reinstalled, secured, and matching their original fastener pattern
  • Confirm the disconnect and breaker are restored and lockout/tag-out devices are removed
  • Confirm plastic sheeting is removed from the electrical enclosure and controls
  • Confirm cleared vegetation, mulch, and debris are removed from the site rather than left piled against the unit
  • Store the coil cleaner, brush, and fin comb in a protected, undamaged condition
