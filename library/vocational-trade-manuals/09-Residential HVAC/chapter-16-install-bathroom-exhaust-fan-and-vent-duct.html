Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install one listed bathroom exhaust fan, its dedicated duct, and an approved exterior termination on a residence or training structure using an approved ventilation design. The finished work product is one operating exhaust fan that draws air from the bathroom ceiling location shown on the approved design and discharges it directly outdoors through a sealed, supported, insulated duct and a weatherproof exterior termination.

You will confirm the approved fan selection, duct size, and termination method; verify framing, wiring, and envelope conditions at the planned location; cut the ceiling opening and mount the housing; route and connect the dedicated duct; prepare the exterior opening and install the termination with correct flashing integration; complete the electrical connection under the approved electrical scope; and verify airflow, damper operation, and discharge before closeout.

This project depends on the system map produced in Chapter 1 and the shutdown procedure in Chapter 2. It uses the duct-sealing methods from Chapter 6, the duct-insulation methods from Chapter 7, and produces a dedicated exhaust path that must never be combined with the HVAC return-air work of Chapter 9 or the dryer exhaust work of Chapter 17. This project's finished fan and duct also become one of the ventilation components documented in the indoor-air-quality plan of Chapter 18.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HV-091 — Lay out the fan location from the approved design, confirming framing, wiring, plumbing, duct route, and clearance conditions before cutting.
  • HV-092 — Cut the ceiling opening to the fan housing's rough-opening dimension without damaging structural members or concealed utilities.
  • HV-093 — Mount the fan housing level and secure using the manufacturer-specified support method.
  • HV-094 — Connect the listed exhaust duct to the fan collar using the specified clamp, seal, and support method, minimizing bends and maintaining the required diameter.
  • HV-095 — Terminate the duct outdoors through an approved exterior opening with a listed termination, backdraft damper, and correctly integrated flashing.
  • HV-096 — Inspect and verify exhaust airflow, damper operation, exterior discharge, sound, and vibration under an authorized operating test.

Where This Skill Fits

Bathroom exhaust is one of the ventilation components identified in the Chapter 1 system map and one of the components assessed again in the Chapter 18 indoor-air-quality plan. This project uses the Chapter 2 shutdown and electrical-isolation procedure before any wiring is touched, the Chapter 6 sealing methods at every duct joint, and the Chapter 7 insulation methods where the duct passes through unconditioned space. The dedicated-duct discipline enforced here — one exhaust path, one termination, no shared routing — is the same discipline required for the dryer exhaust installation in Chapter 17.

A bathroom exhaust fan terminated into an attic, soffit, or wall cavity instead of directly outdoors creates a moisture and mold condition that the Chapter 18 indoor-air-quality assessment is specifically built to catch. Installing the termination correctly the first time prevents that condition from ever being created.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • installing one listed bathroom exhaust fan at the location, with the fan rating, sound rating, and duct size shown on the approved ventilation design
  • cutting one ceiling opening and mounting the fan housing to the specified framing or support method
  • routing, connecting, sealing, supporting, and insulating one dedicated exhaust duct from the fan collar to the exterior termination
  • preparing one exterior wall or roof opening and installing a listed termination with a backdraft damper, integrated with the roofing or weather-resistive barrier
  • completing the electrical connection within the approved electrical scope and verifying fan, damper, and airflow operation

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select the fan rating, duct size, sound rating, or termination method independently of the approved ventilation design
  • perform electrical work beyond the approved electrical scope, including panel work, new circuit installation, or work on line-voltage conductors not assigned to this project
  • perform roofing, siding, or structural work beyond the approved envelope procedure identified by the design
  • terminate the duct into an attic, soffit, crawlspace, garage, or wall cavity under any circumstance
  • connect this duct to any other exhaust system, HVAC duct, or the dryer exhaust duct
  • conceal the installation before the required inspection has passed

Confirm before starting whether local code requires a permit, an inspection, a licensed electrician for the circuit connection, or a licensed roofer for the exterior penetration. Stop work and obtain the required professional if the approved design does not clearly assign that scope.

Trade Note

A bathroom exhaust fan only performs its function if the duct actually reaches the outdoors. A fan that runs and sounds correct but discharges into an attic or soffit has not been installed correctly, even though it appears to work from inside the bathroom. Confirm exterior discharge, not just interior fan operation, before closing out this project.

Setup Standard

Do not begin this project until the approved ventilation design specifies the fan rating, sound rating, duct size, route, termination method, and electrical circuit. Do not select or substitute these values independently. Confirm the electrical circuit is de-energized and verified before any wiring is touched.

Shop Tip

Dry-fit the entire duct route from the fan collar to the exterior opening before making any permanent connection. Confirming the route, bend count, and support points first prevents discovering a blocked path after the ceiling is already cut and the housing is already mounted.

Dedicated-Duct and Termination Discipline

This duct serves this fan only. Do not connect it to another bathroom fan, a kitchen exhaust, an HVAC duct, or the dryer exhaust system. Do not terminate it into an attic, soffit, crawlspace, garage, or wall cavity under any circumstance. The duct must discharge directly outdoors through the listed termination specified by the approved design.

Critical Safety Cue — De-Energize and Verify Before Wiring

Do not connect, splice, or terminate a conductor at the fan until the assigned circuit has been shut down, locked out, and verified de-energized using the Chapter 2 procedure. Restore power only after the housing is secured, the wiring connections are complete, and all covers are reinstalled.

Field Safety Cue — Ceiling, Attic, and Roof Access

Verify ladder footing, attic structural support, adequate lighting, and insulation hazards before entering the ceiling cavity. Use fall protection appropriate to roof or elevated exterior work. Stop work and reassess access if the attic or roof condition is unstable, poorly supported, or obstructed.

Code Alert — Permit, Inspection, and Envelope Requirements Vary

Confirm whether local code requires a permit and inspection for this installation, whether the electrical connection requires a licensed electrician, and whether the roof or wall penetration requires a licensed roofer or siding contractor. Do not conceal the installation before the required inspection has passed.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Fan and Duct Ratings Control the Installation

Install the fan, duct diameter, maximum equivalent length, and termination exactly as specified by the manufacturer instructions and the approved ventilation design. Do not substitute a smaller duct diameter, add unaccounted bends, or exceed the specified equivalent length.

F. Preparation and Design Confirmation

  1. Review the approved ventilation design, fan rating, sound rating, duct size, route, termination, electrical circuit, controls, clearances, and permit requirements. Record each value on the Ventilation Design Record before proceeding.
  2. Confirm that required ventilation calculations and fan selection have been completed by the approved design authority. Do not select or substitute the fan rating, duct size, or termination independently.
  3. Verify ceiling framing, insulation, vapor control, roof or wall assembly, and access route. Use the stud finder and visual inspection from both sides of the ceiling where accessible.
  4. Confirm that the fan is listed for the intended location, including tub or shower zones where applicable. Check the fan's listing label against the installation location before mounting.

G. Electrical Isolation and Layout

  1. De-energize the assigned electrical circuit and verify safe conditions. Use the Chapter 2 shutdown, lockout, and voltage-verification procedure at the assigned breaker.
  2. Mark the fan centerline and rough opening from the approved room layout. Use the tape measure and the manufacturer's rough-opening dimension.
  3. Inspect both sides of the ceiling for framing, wiring, plumbing, ducts, and hazards. Confirm the planned opening avoids joists, wiring, and plumbing not part of this project.

H. Housing Installation

  1. Cut the ceiling opening without damaging structural members or utilities. Cut to the exact rough-opening dimension using the drywall saw or hole saw.
  2. Install the housing support brackets or framing in accordance with the fan instructions. Use the fasteners specified in the Fastener and Hardware Schedule.
  3. Set the housing at the correct relationship to the future ceiling finish. Confirm the housing depth matches the manufacturer's finished-ceiling reference.
  4. Secure the housing and verify it is level and stable. Check level in two directions before proceeding to duct connection.

I. Duct Routing and Connection

  1. Route the listed exhaust duct by the shortest approved path. Confirm the route matches the approved design before cutting duct material.
  2. Minimize bends and preserve the required duct diameter. Do not reduce the duct diameter at any point along the route.
  3. Support the duct without sagging, crushing, or contact with damaging surfaces. Install wide approved straps at the manufacturer-specified interval.
  4. Connect the duct to the fan collar using the specified clamp and seal. Seat the clamp fully and seal the joint with mastic or listed tape.
  5. Insulate the duct where required to control condensation. Apply insulation using the Chapter 7 method where the duct passes through unconditioned space.

J. Exterior Termination and Flashing

  1. Prepare the exterior opening using the approved roof or wall procedure. Confirm the opening dimension matches the termination's manufacturer specification.
  2. Install the listed termination with backdraft damper and pest screen only where permitted. Verify the damper moves freely before final fastening.
  3. Integrate the termination flashing with roofing or the weather-resistive barrier in shingle-lap order. Confirm each upper layer overlaps the layer below it.
  4. Do not terminate into an attic, soffit, crawlspace, garage, or wall cavity. Confirm the termination discharges directly to the exterior before sealing the opening.

K. Electrical Completion and Air Sealing

  1. Complete the electrical wiring under the approved electrical scope. Connect conductors per the fan manufacturer's terminal instructions and the approved circuit design.
  2. Air-seal the fan housing to the ceiling air barrier without blocking service or drainage features. Use a compatible sealant that does not obstruct the damper or service access.
  3. Install the grille. Confirm the grille seats fully and does not obstruct airflow.

L. Testing, Inspection, and Documentation

  1. Complete required inspection before concealment and energization. Do not close the ceiling or energize the circuit until the required inspection has passed.
  2. Restore power and operate the fan. Restore the assigned breaker and start the fan using its control.
  3. Verify damper opening, exterior discharge, sound, vibration, and grille draw using the approved test. Confirm the backdraft damper opens fully and air discharges directly outdoors.
  4. Stop for backdraft, duct leakage, water intrusion, excessive noise, or failed operation. De-energize the circuit before making any correction.
  5. Document fan, duct, termination, electrical handoff, and test results. Complete every field on the Inspection Checklist before closeout.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed installation using the same checks regardless of which residence or training structure the project involves.

  • Design-match check: confirm the installed fan rating, sound rating, and duct diameter match the approved ventilation design.
  • Housing check: confirm the housing is level, secure, and supported per manufacturer instructions.
  • Duct check: confirm the duct is dedicated to this fan only, sealed at every joint, supported at the specified interval, and within the equivalent-length limit.
  • Termination check: confirm the termination discharges directly outdoors, the backdraft damper opens freely, and the flashing is integrated in shingle-lap order.
  • Electrical check: confirm the circuit was verified de-energized before work and functions correctly after restoration.
  • Operating check: confirm fan start, damper operation, exterior discharge, and absence of abnormal noise or vibration.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Fan Runs but Little or No Air Is Felt at the Grille

Probable cause: a crushed or kinked duct section, an undersized duct, excessive bends beyond the manufacturer's equivalent-length limit, or a closed backdraft damper.
Confirmation: inspect the accessible duct route for crushing or kinks and check the damper for free movement at the termination.
Correction: de-energize the circuit, correct the crushed or kinked section, or replace the duct if the diameter or length exceeds the approved design.
Reinspection: restore power and repeat the airflow and damper verification test.

Moisture, Frost, or Staining Appears Along the Duct in Unconditioned Space

Probable cause: missing or incomplete duct insulation allowing condensation on the duct exterior.
Confirmation: inspect the insulation coverage and vapor-facing continuity along the affected section.
Correction: complete the insulation coverage using the Chapter 7 method, sealing all vapor-facing seams.
Reinspection: observe the duct during a following operating cycle for continued condensation.

Water Intrusion Appears at the Exterior Termination After Installation

Probable cause: flashing installed out of shingle-lap order, insufficient sealant, or an incompatible termination for the wall or roof assembly.
Confirmation: inspect the flashing lap sequence and sealant continuity at the termination opening.
Correction: stop work, correct the flashing lap order and sealant application per the manufacturer instructions, and do not conceal the correction until reinspected.
Reinspection: confirm no water intrusion under a controlled water test or the next weather event, as required by the approved design.

Fan Fails to Start or Trips the Assigned Breaker

Probable cause: a wiring error, a short circuit at a connection, or a mismatch between the fan's electrical rating and the assigned circuit.
Confirmation: de-energize the circuit, verify absence of voltage, and inspect the wiring connections against the fan manufacturer's terminal diagram.
Correction: correct the wiring error within the approved electrical scope, or stop work and escalate to a licensed electrician if the fault is not resolved by correcting the connection.
Reinspection: restore power and repeat the fan-start verification test.

Excessive Noise or Vibration Is Observed During the Operating Test

Probable cause: the housing is not level or securely mounted, the duct contacts a framing member, or the fan is not rated for the installed sound requirement.
Confirmation: inspect housing mounting security and duct contact points along the accessible route.
Correction: de-energize the circuit and re-secure the housing or duct support at the point of contact or looseness.
Reinspection: restore power and repeat the sound and vibration observation.
