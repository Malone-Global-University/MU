Job Overview

This project qualifies you to receive, place, consolidate, and screed a residential concrete slab to the correct elevation and surface condition. The finished work product of this project alone is a continuously placed, consolidated, and screeded wet slab, at the required thickness and elevation, turned over to the finishing operation at the correct concrete condition. This project does not end with a finished, jointed, or cured slab surface; that work belongs to the next project, which begins the moment this project's screeded slab reaches the correct condition.

You will stage every tool, worker, and material needed for both placement and finishing before concrete discharge begins, receive and verify the delivered concrete, place and consolidate it without displacing reinforcement, embeds, or the vapor barrier beneath it, strike the surface off to the required elevation and profile, and turn the completed placement area over to the finishing crew without allowing the slab to miss its finishing window.

This project depends entirely on the slab forms, control lines, compacted base, vapor barrier, and reinforcement completed and inspected in the two projects before it. No placement may begin until those inspections are complete and the finishing resources required by the next project are staged and ready.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-091 — Plan the slab-pour sequence, including delivery interval, placement route, and crew assignments.
  • FN-092 — Place concrete without displacing reinforcement, embeds, or the vapor barrier.
  • FN-093 — Screed the surface to the required elevation and profile.
  • FN-094 — Maintain slab elevation, thickness, and slope throughout placement.
  • FN-095 — Consolidate edges, corners, thickened areas, and embedded items.
  • FN-096 — Inspect the wet-slab placement continuously and correct deficiencies before the concrete loses workability.

Where This Skill Fits

The slab forms and control lines from Project 14 and the compacted base, vapor barrier, and reinforcement from Project 15 exist to be placed against and around by this project. Every elevation, slope, and thickness verified in this project is measured against the same benchmark, screed reference, and control line established in those two projects.

This project and the next one are a single continuous slab placement divided into two competency groups for instruction and assessment. Finishing must begin at the correct concrete condition during the same operation described here; it may not be postponed merely because it is described in a separate project. The crew that begins this project must be staffed, staged, and equipped to carry the work straight through floating, edging, jointing, and initial curing without a gap.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved slab mixture, volume, delivery interval, placement route, screed method, joint plan, finish specification, weather plan, testing requirements, crew assignments, and contingency procedures
  • confirming that forms, base, vapor barrier, reinforcement, embeds, penetrations, and inspections are complete before discharge begins
  • staging placing, consolidation, screeding, testing, cleanup, curing, lighting, and finishing resources before concrete arrives
  • receiving, testing, placing, and consolidating the specified concrete mixture
  • striking the surface off to the required elevation and profile and turning it over to the finishing crew

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • accept a delivered concrete mixture that does not match the approved specification
  • begin placement before forms, base, vapor barrier, reinforcement, embeds, and required inspections are complete
  • add unauthorized water or admixture to the mixture at the point of placement
  • float, edge, joint, texture, or cure the slab surface; those operations belong to the next project and must follow immediately once the surface reaches the correct condition
  • allow the placement to pause at a point not planned as an approved construction joint

Any form movement, equipment failure, segregation, delayed delivery, surface crusting, displaced embed, or elevation loss identified during placement must be stopped and corrected before placement continues.

Trade Note

Chapters 16 and 17 describe one continuous slab placement. The finishing crew, curing materials, and protection equipment required by the next project must be staged and ready before this project's concrete discharge begins. Treating this project as a stopping point, rather than the first half of one operation, is the most common cause of a slab that misses its finishing window.

Shop Tip

Assign one crew member as spotter for reinforcement and embed position throughout placement. A spotter who is not also operating the pump, vibrator, or screed can catch a displaced dowel, sleeve, or mesh sheet before it is buried under fresh concrete, when correction is still possible.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every concrete placement in this book, from footings to stem walls to this slab, follows the same discipline: confirm the mixture at delivery, keep a continuous workable front, consolidate around every embedded item without displacing it, and monitor forms, reinforcement, and elevation throughout placement rather than only at the end. Applying that discipline here, at the largest single placement in the book, is what keeps the slab within its required thickness and elevation across its full area.

Continuity Between This Project and the Next

This project's placement and screeding steps and the next project's floating, edging, jointing, and finishing steps are one continuous operation performed on the same wet concrete. Do not treat the end of this project's numbered sequence as a stopping point. The crew, tools, and curing materials required for finishing must already be staged before discharge begins, and the finishing crew must take over each completed area at the correct surface condition without delay.

Measurement Cue — Screed From the Established Reference Only

Strike off every area of the slab from the screed rail, wet-screed pin, laser reference, or other control established in Project 14. Do not estimate slab thickness by eye or adjust the screed reference mid-placement to compensate for an unrelated elevation problem; identify and correct the actual cause instead.

Critical Safety Cue — Pump Lines and Placement Equipment

Treat a pressurized concrete pump line as capable of sudden, forceful discharge or blockage release. Keep all workers clear of the discharge end during line priming, blockage clearing, and cleanout. Stop the pump and follow the manufacturer's blockage-clearing procedure before any worker approaches the line to clear an obstruction.

Field Safety Cue — Delivery and Placement Traffic

Confirm a clear, designated path for ready-mix trucks, pump trucks, and buggies before placement begins. Keep workers not directly involved in placement out of the delivery and buggy travel path, and wear the required high-visibility vest whenever this equipment is operating.

Code Alert — Field Testing Requirements

Required field sampling, slump testing, air-content testing, and cylinder-making frequency vary by jurisdiction, project specification, and concrete supplier agreement. Verify the required testing frequency and personnel qualifications with the approved testing agency before delivery, and do not substitute an informal check for a required documented test.

E. Preparation — Planning and Pre-Placement Verification

  1. Review the approved slab mixture, volume, delivery interval, placement route, screed method, joint plan, finish specification, weather plan, testing, crew assignments, and contingency procedures. Confirm every element of the plan is documented before scheduling delivery.
  2. Confirm that forms, base, vapor barrier, reinforcement, embeds, penetrations, inspections, and finishing resources are complete. Verify the Project 14 and Project 15 inspection records before proceeding.
  3. Protect adjacent walls, openings, utilities, access routes, and finished work. Install protective covering or barriers at every location subject to splatter or equipment contact.
  4. Stage pumps, chutes, buggies, rakes, come-alongs, vibrators, screeds, grade tools, testing equipment, cleanup supplies, finishing tools, curing materials, lighting, and backup equipment. Confirm every item is on site and functional before the delivery truck arrives.
  5. Establish communication among the supplier, driver, pump operator, placing crew, screed crew, finishers, testers, and spotters. Confirm radio contact or an equivalent signal system before discharge begins.
  6. Recheck slab dimensions, form elevation, screed references, slopes, depressions, joints, and reinforcement position. Correct any discrepancy before the first delivery arrives.

F. Delivery Verification and Testing

  1. Inspect delivery documentation and verify the specified mixture before discharge. Compare the delivery ticket to the approved mixture specification and resolve any mismatch before accepting the load.
  2. Conduct required sampling and testing with approved personnel. Record slump, air content, temperature, and cylinder identification as required by the project specification.

G. Placement

  1. Begin placement at the planned location and maintain a continuous workable edge. Follow the placement route established in Step 1.
  2. Deposit concrete close to its final location without damaging the vapor barrier or displacing reinforcement. Avoid excessive free fall and direct impact on the vapor barrier or reinforcement.
  3. Use approved placing tools to distribute concrete without adding unauthorized water. Do not add water at the placement location to improve workability.
  4. Consolidate edges, corners, thickened areas, embeds, blockouts, and penetrations. Use the approved vibration method at every embedded item and form edge.
  5. Lift or maintain reinforcement only by the approved support method and verify that it remains at the specified elevation. Do not lift reinforcement with a rake, shovel, or foot; use the method specified in the reinforcement plan.

H. Strike-Off and Elevation Control

  1. Strike off concrete using the planned screed reference and sequence. Work the screed along the established rails, pins, or laser reference.
  2. Fill low areas with fresh concrete from the same placement and rescreed. Do not leave a low area to be corrected during finishing.
  3. Remove excess concrete without contaminating adjacent work or obstructing access. Direct surplus material to an approved location away from finished work and access routes.
  4. Check slab elevation, thickness controls, slopes, depressions, edges, and transitions while correction remains possible. Verify with the grade rod and level at the intervals specified in the placement plan.

I. Continuous Monitoring and Interruption Control

  1. Monitor forms, stakes, braces, joints, embeds, penetrations, vapor barrier, and reinforcement throughout placement. Assign the spotter identified in the Shop Tip to this task continuously.
  2. Stop and correct form movement, equipment failure, segregation, delayed delivery, surface crusting, displaced embeds, or elevation loss before continuing. Do not continue placement past an identified problem without correcting it.
  3. Coordinate wet edges and planned construction joints if placement is interrupted. Use only the construction-joint location and detail established in the placement plan.

J. Turnover and Documentation

  1. Record deliveries, tests, times, weather, placement sequence, interruptions, corrections, and turnover times. Complete every field on the Slab-Placement Record as the placement proceeds.
  2. Continue directly into the finishing project without allowing the slab to miss its finishing window. Turn each completed placement area over to the finishing crew as soon as it reaches the correct surface condition for floating.

Common Placement Inspection

Inspect every placement area using the same checks regardless of location within the slab.

  • Mixture check: confirm the delivered concrete matches the approved specification before discharge.
  • Continuity check: confirm a continuous workable front is maintained and any interruption follows the approved construction-joint plan.
  • Reinforcement and embed check: confirm no reinforcement, dowel, sleeve, or embedded item has moved beyond the tolerance stated in the approved plan.
  • Vapor-barrier check: confirm no puncture or displacement of the vapor barrier occurred during placement.
  • Elevation and thickness check: confirm the struck-off surface matches the screed reference and approved slab plan.
  • Turnover check: confirm each completed area was handed to the finishing crew at the correct surface condition.

Complete the Slab-Placement Record in the Inspection Checklist section below as each area is turned over to finishing.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Delivered Concrete Does Not Match the Specification

Probable cause: a supplier batching or dispatch error, or a load intended for a different project delivered to this site.
Confirmation: compare the delivery ticket to the approved mixture specification before any discharge occurs.
Correction: reject the load and do not place any portion of it; contact the supplier for a corrected delivery.
Reinspection: verify the replacement delivery ticket against the approved specification before discharge begins.

Reinforcement or an Embedded Item Has Shifted

Probable cause: concrete was deposited with excessive free fall directly onto reinforcement, or a worker or equipment contacted an embedded item during placement.
Confirmation: compare the current position of the item to its approved location and elevation using the reinforcement or embed schedule.
Correction: reposition the item while the concrete remains workable, using the approved support method; do not bury a displaced item without correction.
Reinspection: repeat the reinforcement and embed check for the affected area before continuing placement past it.

Slab Surface Is Low or High Relative to the Screed Reference

Probable cause: an inaccurate screed reference, a miscalculated placement volume, or inconsistent screed technique.
Confirmation: re-check the screed rail, pin, or laser reference elevation and re-measure the affected area with the grade rod.
Correction: fill low areas with fresh concrete from the same placement and rescreed; remove excess material from high areas before it sets.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation and thickness check for the corrected area.

Placement Front Stalls or a Cold Joint Risk Develops

Probable cause: a delivery delay, equipment failure, or crew shortage interrupted the continuous workable front.
Confirmation: assess the elapsed time and surface condition of the stalled edge against the concrete's working time.
Correction: if the surface remains workable, resume placement and consolidate thoroughly at the rejoined edge; if the surface has begun to set, stop at the nearest planned construction-joint location and install the approved joint detail.
Reinspection: inspect the rejoined or jointed edge for consolidation and bond before continuing.

Vapor Barrier Is Punctured or Displaced During Placement

Probable cause: equipment contact, a dropped tool, or excessive free fall from height damaged the vapor barrier before or during concrete deposit.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area for a visible tear, puncture, or displaced seam before concrete covers it.
Correction: stop placement at the affected location and repair the vapor barrier using the approved patch method before continuing.
Reinspection: repeat the vapor-barrier check for the repaired area before placement resumes over it.

Finishing Crew Is Not Ready When the Surface Reaches Turnover Condition

Probable cause: finishing tools, curing materials, or the finishing crew were not staged before discharge began.
Confirmation: compare the actual readiness of finishing resources to the staging requirements confirmed in Step 4.
Correction: stop and complete staging immediately; do not allow the surface to sit past its finishing window while resources are gathered.
Reinspection: confirm the finishing crew has taken over the completed area at the correct surface condition before recording turnover.

Cleanup

This project does not end with tool cleanup and demobilization; it ends with turnover to the finishing crew described in the next project. Complete only the cleanup tasks that do not interfere with immediate finishing.

  • Clean placement tools and equipment without discharging cement residue into soil, drains, or waterways, at a location that does not block finishing access
  • Remove surplus concrete, spillage, and placement debris from areas the finishing crew must access
  • Confirm testing samples and cylinders are labeled, protected, and stored as required by the testing agency
  • Return the Slab-Placement Record to the project file and hand testing documentation to the designated recipient
  • Confirm the finishing crew has taken over every completed area before placement equipment is demobilized

Field Terms

Screed Reference
The screed rail, wet-screed pin, laser control, or other approved elevation reference used to strike off the slab.
Placement Route
The approved path concrete travels from delivery to its final location in the slab.
Workable Front
The continuously advancing leading edge of freshly placed, unconsolidated concrete.
Strike-Off
The act of removing excess concrete to bring the surface to the screed reference.
Consolidation
The process of eliminating voids and entrapped air from freshly placed concrete, typically by vibration.
Turnover
The point at which a completed placement area is handed to the finishing crew.
Construction Joint
A planned stopping location in the slab placement, used only if the operation is interrupted.
Cold Joint
An unplanned, poorly bonded joint formed when fresh concrete is placed against concrete that has begun to set.
Slab-Placement Record
The document recording deliveries, tests, placement sequence, interruptions, corrections, and turnover times.

Performance Check
