Job Overview

This project qualifies you to receive, place, consolidate, strike off, finish, protect, and begin curing residential strip-footing concrete. The finished work product is a placed, consolidated, correctly elevated, finished, and protected strip footing with curing initiated at the correct time, supported by delivery tickets, field-test records, and a completed Footing Placement and Curing Record.

You will stage the crew, tools, testing equipment, and curing materials before the truck arrives; verify the delivered concrete against the specified mixture; sample and test the concrete; place it into the forms built in Project 02 around the reinforcement and embedded hardware installed in Projects 03 and 05; consolidate it without disturbing that reinforcement or hardware; strike off and finish the footing top to the condition required for the next stage of work; and begin the specified curing and protection procedure before leaving the site.

This project assumes Projects 02, 03, and 05 have delivered inspected, released footing forms with reinforcement and any footing-stage embedded hardware in place. No concrete may be placed until those inspections are complete and documented.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-019 — Prepare for concrete delivery, including crew staging, communication, and pre-discharge verification.
  • FN-020 — Place concrete into the footing forms without segregation or reinforcement displacement.
  • FN-021 — Consolidate concrete using the approved method around reinforcement, corners, steps, and embedded items.
  • FN-022 — Screed footing tops to the required elevation and profile.
  • FN-023 — Finish footing surfaces to the condition required for the next construction stage.
  • FN-024 — Protect fresh concrete and begin the specified curing procedure at the correct time.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 01 verified the controlling layout and elevations. Project 02 built the footing forms. Project 03 installed the footing reinforcement, and Project 05 places any footing-stage anchor bolts or embedded hardware designated for this placement. This project is the point at which all of that preparatory work becomes a permanent concrete element. Every stem-wall form in Project 06 bears on the footing placed here, and every dowel or construction-joint detail installed here controls how the stem wall bonds to the footing.

The placement, consolidation, and finishing discipline taught here — controlled deposit, approved consolidation around embedded items, and finishing only to the condition the next stage requires — is the same discipline used again for stem-wall concrete in Project 07 and for the continuous slab placement in Projects 16 and 17.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • staging placing, consolidation, screeding, finishing, testing, cleanup, curing, lighting, and emergency equipment before discharge begins
  • verifying delivered concrete against the specified mixture and conducting required field sampling and testing
  • placing, consolidating, striking off, and finishing footing concrete to the elevation and surface condition required for the next stage
  • rechecking dowels and embedded items before the concrete loses workability
  • beginning the specified curing and weather-protection procedure and documenting the placement

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • alter the specified concrete mixture, add unauthorized water, or accept a delivery that does not match the specified mixture without supplier or engineer resolution
  • relocate, cut, bend, or field-modify reinforcement or embedded hardware to accommodate the placement
  • place concrete before form, reinforcement, and embedded-hardware inspections required by Projects 02, 03, and 05 are complete
  • determine curing duration, strength requirements, or early-loading limits independently of the concrete specification and Project 09 curing plan

Any bulging, leaking, movement, blockage, segregation, or equipment failure identified during placement is a stop-work condition requiring correction before placement continues.

Trade Note

A footing pour is a single continuous operation from discharge through curing start, even though this chapter presents it as a numbered sequence. Complete the staging in Steps 1–7 before the truck arrives. Do not begin discharge until every tool, test kit, and curing material identified in Step 5 is on site and ready.

Setup Standard

Do not begin concrete discharge until forms, reinforcement, dowels, sleeves, blockouts, and required inspections from Projects 02, 03, and 05 are complete and documented, and until every placing, consolidation, screeding, finishing, testing, and curing resource identified in Step 5 is staged on site.

Shop Tip

Assign one crew member to watch the forms, stakes, and braces continuously during discharge. A form that begins to bulge or shift is far easier to correct in the first few minutes of loading than after the full footing length has been placed.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every concrete placement in this book, from this footing pour through the stem-wall placement in Project 07 and the slab placement in Projects 16 and 17, follows the same sequence: verify readiness and stage resources, verify the delivered mixture, sample and test, place and consolidate without disturbing embedded items, strike off and finish to the required condition, and begin curing at the correct time. Learning that sequence at the footing stage, where the placement is simplest, prepares you for the more complex wall and slab placements later in this book.

Continuous Workable Front and Embedded-Item Protection

Maintain a continuous workable front throughout the placement so that fresh concrete is always deposited against fresh, still-workable concrete rather than against concrete that has begun to stiffen. Every dowel, anchor bolt, and embedded item placed in Projects 03 and 05 must be rechecked before the surrounding concrete loses workability; an item that has shifted cannot be corrected once the concrete has stiffened around it.

Measurement Cue — Strike Off to the Verified Elevation

Strike off every footing top to the elevation verified in Project 01 and marked on the forms in Project 02. Do not strike off to a convenient or estimated elevation. Recheck the struck-off elevation at the same intervals used to verify form top elevation in Project 02.

Critical Safety Cue — Pump Lines and Stored Pressure

Do not disconnect, reposition, or clear a blocked pump line without confirming the line has been fully depressurized by the pump operator. A pressurized line can discharge concrete with enough force to cause serious injury. Keep all non-essential personnel clear of the discharge end of the line during priming and pumping.

Field Safety Cue — Truck, Pump, and Buggy Traffic

Confirm a spotter is assigned whenever a concrete truck, pump, or buggy is backing, maneuvering, or discharging near the work area. Wear the required high-visibility vest and keep clear of the swing radius of chutes and pump booms.

Code Alert — Field Testing and Sampling Requirements

Required sampling frequency, test methods, and testing-personnel qualifications vary by jurisdiction and by the project specification. Confirm the required testing frequency and personnel qualifications with the specified testing agency before discharge begins, and do not substitute an informal check for a required field test.

E. Preparation — Readiness Review

  1. Review the approved concrete mixture, placement volume, delivery method, access route, weather plan, crew assignments, testing requirements, joint locations, and contingency plan. Confirm the reviewed document set matches the current site conditions before proceeding.
  2. Verify that excavation, forms, reinforcement, dowels, sleeves, blockouts, and inspection approvals are complete. Confirm the Project 02, 03, and 05 inspection records are signed and available on site.
  3. Confirm that forms are clean, braced, dimensionally correct, and capable of resisting the planned placement method. Walk the full form length and recheck bracing before staging equipment.

F. Staging and Communication

  1. Protect adjacent property, utilities, access routes, and finished work from concrete splatter and equipment damage. Install protective covering and barriers before the truck arrives.
  2. Stage placing, consolidation, screeding, testing, cleanup, curing, lighting, and emergency equipment. Confirm every tool listed in the Common Tools and Equipment table is on site and operable.
  3. Establish communication signals among the driver, pump operator, equipment operator, placing crew, and spotters. Confirm every crew member understands the stop-work hand or radio signal before discharge begins.
  4. Reconfirm footing elevations and visible placement references. Recheck the form-top marks established in Project 02 against the Project 01 benchmark.

G. Delivery Verification and Testing

  1. Inspect delivered concrete documentation before discharge and resolve any mismatch with the specified mixture. Compare the delivery ticket to the approved mixture designation before allowing discharge to begin.
  2. Conduct required field sampling and testing using the approved testing personnel and procedures. Record slump, air content, and cast the required number of cylinders before or during the start of discharge.

H. Placement and Consolidation

  1. Begin placement at the planned location and maintain a continuous workable front. Do not allow a section of placed concrete to sit unconsolidated while a distant section is placed.
  2. Deposit concrete close to its final position without excessive free fall, segregation, or displacement of reinforcement. Direct the chute, pump line, or buggy discharge as close to the form as the equipment allows.
  3. Control the placement rate so forms, stakes, and braces remain stable. Reduce the discharge rate if the assigned form-watch crew member reports bulging, leaning, or stake movement.
  4. Consolidate concrete using the approved method, especially around reinforcement, corners, steps, and embedded items. Insert the vibrator vertically at the specified spacing and withdraw slowly.
  5. Avoid over-vibration, contact that moves reinforcement, or vibration against unsupported formwork. Do not use the vibrator to move concrete horizontally along the form.

I. Monitoring and Stop-Work Response

  1. Monitor forms, footing width, reinforcement position, dowels, sleeves, and concrete level throughout placement. Assign a dedicated crew member to this monitoring task for the full duration of discharge.
  2. Stop placement and correct bulging, leaking, movement, blockage, segregation, or equipment failure before continuing. Do not resume discharge until the identified condition is corrected and confirmed safe.

J. Strike-Off and Finishing

  1. Strike off the footing top to the required elevation and profile. Draw the straightedge screed along the form tops established in Project 02.
  2. Finish top surfaces only to the condition required for the next construction stage. Do not apply a smooth trowel finish where the next stage requires a roughened bonding surface, or the reverse.
  3. Recheck dowels and embedded items before the concrete loses workability. Verify projection, spacing, and plumb against the Project 03 and Project 05 records while adjustment remains possible.
  4. Install or finish approved construction-joint details if the planned placement is interrupted. Use only the joint detail specified in the approved plan.

K. Protection, Curing, and Closeout

  1. Begin the specified curing and weather-protection procedure immediately after the surface condition permits. Apply curing compound or place wet coverings per the Project 09 curing plan.
  2. Barricade the area and prevent vibration, impact, contamination, freezing, rapid moisture loss, and early loading. Post barricades and signage before leaving the placement area unattended.
  3. Clean tools and equipment without discharging cement residue into soil, drains, or waterways. Wash out at the designated concrete washout location only.
  4. Record delivery data, test information, placement times, weather, volume, interruptions, corrections, and curing start time. Complete every field on the Footing Placement and Curing Record.
  5. Inspect the finished footing surfaces and protection before leaving the site. Confirm barricades, coverings, and curing materials are correctly placed before the crew departs.

Common Placement Inspection

Inspect every placement using the same checks regardless of footing length or configuration.

  • Delivery check: confirm the delivery ticket matches the specified mixture design before discharge.
  • Test check: confirm slump, air content, and cylinder casting were completed by the approved testing personnel.
  • Consolidation check: confirm no visible entrapped air voids or honeycombing at forms, corners, or embedded items.
  • Elevation check: confirm the struck-off footing top matches the approved plan within stated tolerance, verified from the Project 01 benchmark.
  • Embedded-item check: confirm dowels, anchors, and sleeves remain within tolerance through the end of placement.
  • Curing check: confirm curing began at the correct surface condition and is recorded with a start time.

Complete the Footing Placement and Curing Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the crew from the site.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Delivered Concrete Does Not Match the Specification

Probable cause: a batching, mix-design, or delivery-ticket error at the plant, or the wrong mixture was dispatched for the site.
Confirmation: compare the delivery ticket to the approved mixture designation and contact the supplier before discharge.
Correction: reject the delivery and obtain a correctly batched load, or obtain engineer authorization if the supplier confirms an acceptable substitution.
Reinspection: repeat the delivery check on the replacement load before discharge begins.

Honeycombing or Voids Appear at a Form Face

Probable cause: insufficient consolidation, excessive placement rate, or congested reinforcement that trapped air against the form.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area after form removal in Project 08 and compare its location to the placement and consolidation record.
Correction: classify the defect per Project 08 and repair only within the authorized minor-repair scope; escalate deep honeycombing for structural review.
Reinspection: repeat the consolidation check on subsequent placements, adjusting vibrator spacing or placement rate as needed.

Footing Top Elevation Is Out of Tolerance

Probable cause: the screed reference was not verified against the Project 01 benchmark before strike-off, or the form top settled during placement.
Confirmation: re-verify the form-top elevation against the benchmark and inspect for form movement or settlement.
Correction: re-screed the affected section to the correct elevation while the concrete remains workable; obtain engineer direction if the concrete has already stiffened.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation check across the full footing length.

A Dowel or Anchor Bolt Has Shifted

Probable cause: contact from the vibrator, chute, or placing tool, or inadequate securing of the item before placement.
Confirmation: compare the current position to the Project 03 or Project 05 placement record while the concrete remains workable.
Correction: realign the item within tolerance while the concrete remains workable; do not force, heat, or bend a hardened item, and obtain direction if realignment is not possible.
Reinspection: repeat the embedded-item check immediately after correction and again before curing begins.

Placement Is Interrupted Before Completion

Probable cause: delivery delay, equipment failure, or a weather event during placement.
Confirmation: assess the condition of the wet edge and the elapsed time since the last delivery.
Correction: install the approved construction-joint detail at the wet edge before it stiffens; do not resume placement against a stiffened, untreated edge.
Reinspection: inspect the joint condition before and after the resumed placement is completed.

Curing Does Not Begin at the Correct Time

Probable cause: curing materials were not staged before discharge, or finishing operations were delayed past the point the surface condition allowed curing to start.
Confirmation: compare the recorded curing start time to the recorded finishing completion time and the surface-condition criteria in the Project 09 curing plan.
Correction: begin curing immediately once identified; document the delay and any resulting protection plan required by Project 09.
Reinspection: repeat the curing check per the Project 09 inspection schedule.

Cleanup

This project ends with a protected, curing footing and clean equipment, not a stripped site.

  • Wash out chutes, buggies, vibrators, screeds, and hand tools at the designated washout location only
  • Remove excess concrete, spillage, and debris from adjacent surfaces and access routes
  • Confirm barricades, signage, and curing coverings remain correctly placed before leaving the site
  • Store cylinder molds in the specified initial-curing condition pending pickup or transport by the testing agency
  • Return the Footing Placement and Curing Record, delivery tickets, and test data to the project record

Field Terms

Workable Front
The leading edge of freshly placed, still-workable concrete maintained continuously during placement.
Consolidation
Eliminating entrapped air and voids from placed concrete using the approved vibration method.
Strike-Off
Removing excess concrete to bring the surface to the required elevation and profile.
Slump
A field measurement of concrete workability, tested against the specified range before and during placement.
Cold Joint
An unplanned discontinuity formed when fresh concrete is placed against concrete that has already begun to stiffen.
Construction Joint
An approved, planned detail used to end and later resume a placement.
Curing Start Time
The recorded time at which the specified curing and protection procedure begins.
Honeycombing
Visible voids in hardened concrete caused by inadequate consolidation or excessive placement rate.
Footing Placement and Curing Record
The document recording delivery, test, placement, and curing data for the footing pour.
Barricading
Physical protection installed to prevent traffic, impact, or loading on curing concrete.

Performance Check

With placement, consolidation, strike-off, finishing, and curing start complete, review the finished Footing Placement and Curing Record against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the delivered mixture matched the specification, required field tests were completed by approved personnel, no cold joint formed outside an approved construction-joint location, no visible honeycombing or entrapped air voids remain at forms or embedded items, the footing top elevation matches the approved plan within tolerance, every dowel and embedded item remains within tolerance, the surface finish matches the condition required for the next stage, and curing began at the correct time and is documented. If an item fails, identify whether the error occurred in delivery verification, placement rate, consolidation method, elevation control, or curing timing before recording the correction and re-running the affected check.
