Job Overview

This project qualifies you to lay out, build, brace, and verify residential slab-edge forms and the internal control references that will govern base preparation, vapor-barrier placement, reinforcement, and the continuous slab placement in Projects 15 through 17. The finished work product is a dimensionally correct, stable, and fully referenced slab-form assembly: perimeter forms at the correct line and elevation, established screed references, and a completed Slab Form and Control Line Inspection Record.

You will review the approved slab plan, re-establish the slab benchmark and perimeter control lines from Project 1's protected references, mark slab edges and special conditions, select and prepare form materials, build and brace the perimeter form, establish interior screed references, verify every dimension and elevation, and record and photograph the completed form system before base work begins.

This project follows the completed backfill in Project 13. It assumes the foundation walls, backfill, and finished grade have reached the condition required to support slab-form stakes and interior screed equipment. No base aggregate, vapor barrier, reinforcement, or concrete work may begin until this project's verification and release steps are complete and recorded.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-079 — Lay out slab edges from the re-established benchmark and control references.
  • FN-080 — Install slab-edge forms at the correct line, elevation, and profile.
  • FN-081 — Set finished-slab elevation and establish interior screed references.
  • FN-082 — Brace slab forms to resist placing and screeding forces.
  • FN-083 — Verify square using diagonal measurement and control-line comparison.
  • FN-084 — Inspect form height, alignment, and stability before base work begins.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 1 established the discipline of measuring every dimension and diagonal from a single defined reference corner and confirming elevations from a single protected benchmark. This project applies that same discipline to the slab stage: the perimeter forms and screed references you establish here are the horizontal and vertical controls that Project 15's base and reinforcement work, and Project 16's continuous concrete placement, depend on directly.

The square-verification method used in this chapter — comparing measured diagonals against each other and against the plan, not only checking the plan dimension — is the identical method taught in Project 1 for the building footprint. If that method is unfamiliar, review Project 1 before proceeding.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved slab plan, thickness, finished-floor elevations, slopes, depressions, thickened edges, joints, openings, embeds, and tolerances
  • re-establishing the slab benchmark, reference corner, perimeter lines, and construction offsets from the protected Project 1 controls
  • marking slab edges, thickened sections, openings, blockouts, steps, recesses, and construction-joint locations
  • selecting, cutting, positioning, staking, and bracing slab-edge forms
  • establishing screed rails, grade pins, wet-screed references, or laser controls for interior elevation control
  • installing sleeves, edge isolation, construction joints, or embedded forms included at this stage
  • verifying perimeter dimensions, diagonals, elevations, thickness references, slopes, and control-line locations
  • recording and photographing a completed, inspected slab-form assembly

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • revise the approved slab thickness, finished-floor elevation, slope, or joint layout on their own authority
  • place base aggregate, vapor barrier, reinforcement, or concrete before this project's verification and release steps are complete
  • install plumbing, electrical, or mechanical embeds not detailed on the approved slab plan or coordinated with the responsible trade
  • disturb, remove, or relocate the protected benchmark or building reference corner established in Project 1

Any dimension, diagonal, elevation, or control line that fails verification must be corrected or escalated to the designer or engineer of record before base work continues.

Trade Note

Every slab-form dimension and elevation in this chapter is re-established from the same protected benchmark and building reference corner confirmed in Project 1 — never from a form line left over from stem-wall construction or an assumed offset from the foundation wall. Measuring from an unconfirmed intermediate reference is the same compounding-error risk identified in Project 1's troubleshooting section, applied here to the slab stage.

Setup Standard

Shop Tip

Set and check every screed reference — rail, grade pin, or laser control — only after the perimeter form elevation has passed verification. A screed reference set against an unverified perimeter form will carry that error into every reading taken from it.

Common Field Fundamentals

This project reuses the layout-verification discipline taught in Project 1: re-establish the fixed references, measure from those references only, compare against the approved plan and stated tolerance, and record every result before releasing the work. The slab-form stage adds two elements not present in Project 1: a physical form that must resist placing and screeding pressure, and interior screed references that control elevation across the interior of the slab, not only at its edges.

Reference Corner and Slab Benchmark

Every perimeter dimension and diagonal in this project is measured from the single reference corner re-established in Step 3. Every form-top elevation and screed reference is verified from the same slab benchmark. Do not substitute a different corner or elevation reference partway through the project; doing so produces a form system that cannot be reconciled against the approved plan or against itself.

Measurement Cue — Re-Establish From Protected Controls Every Time

Measure every slab-edge dimension and diagonal from the reference corner re-established in Step 3. Verify every form-top elevation and screed reference from the slab benchmark established in the same step. Do not measure from a stem-wall form line, an assumed offset, or a convenient nearby stake; doing so allows small errors to compound across the slab.

Critical Safety Cue — Stake Driving and Buried Utilities

Confirm the location of buried utilities, drain tile, and waterproofing protection board before driving any form stake. Do not drive a stake in a location that could puncture the drain system, waterproofing, or a utility line. Treat any unexpected resistance, unusual sound, or visible damage during stake driving as a stop-work condition until the area is inspected.

Field Safety Cue — Uneven Subgrade and Material Staging

Inspect the graded slab area for soft spots, standing water, and uneven footing before walking it with form lumber or equipment. Stage form materials, stakes, and tools clear of the screed path and equipment access routes to avoid trip hazards and material conflicts during later stages.

Code Alert — Slope, Thickness, and Joint Requirements

Required slab thickness, thickened-edge dimensions, control-joint spacing, and finished-floor elevation vary by jurisdiction, occupancy, and the approved plan. Never estimate a slab-form dimension or elevation from a similar prior project. Verify every value against the approved slab plan and structural details, and contact the designer or engineer of record if a value is missing or unclear.

E. Preparation — Document Review and Site Inspection

  1. Review the approved slab plan, thickness, dimensions, finished-floor elevations, slopes, depressions, thickened edges, joints, openings, embeds, and tolerances. Confirm the document set is complete before proceeding to Step 2.
  2. Confirm that underground work, subgrade preparation, and required inspections are complete or coordinated. Obtain written confirmation before disturbing the graded surface with stakes or equipment.

F. Establishing References and Layout

  1. Re-establish the slab benchmark, reference corner, perimeter lines, and construction offsets. Confirm each re-established reference against the protected Project 1 controls before marking any edge.
  2. Mark slab edges, thickened sections, openings, blockouts, steps, recesses, and construction-joint locations. Mark every location directly on the ground or on temporary layout stakes before form material is cut.

G. Form Material Selection and Preparation

  1. Select straight form lumber or the specified forming system, stakes, braces, kickers, fasteners, and release agent. Confirm every material matches the project specification before use.
  2. Inspect materials for warp, damage, contamination, and incompatibility. Set aside any material that fails inspection.
  3. Cut and label form members by slab edge and location. Label each piece so it can be matched to its marked location during assembly.

H. Form Construction

  1. Position the first form line to the controlling horizontal and elevation reference. Set the form to the reference corner and slab benchmark established in Step 3.
  2. Stake and fasten the form while maintaining line and top elevation. Check top elevation at each stake before fastening.
  3. Install adjoining form runs, corners, curves, steps, depressions, and blockouts. Verify each corner angle with the framing square before fastening.
  4. Use spreaders, templates, or diagonal controls to establish the required dimensions and square. Confirm both diagonals as each section is completed, not only at the end of the layout.
  5. Install external braces and kickers to resist placing and screeding forces. Space bracing according to the form design and the planned placement method.

I. Interior Control References

  1. Establish screed rails, grade pins, wet-screed references, laser controls, or other approved interior elevation references. Verify every interior reference against the slab benchmark before relying on it.
  2. Mark required slopes and drainage transitions. Record the slope value and direction at each marked transition.
  3. Install sleeves, edge isolation, construction joints, or embedded forms included at this stage. Confirm each item's location against the approved slab plan before securing it.

J. Release Preparation and Protection

  1. Apply form-release agent only to approved contact surfaces. Keep release agent off screed references, embedded items, and construction-joint surfaces.

K. Verification

  1. Verify perimeter dimensions, diagonals, top elevation, thickness references, slopes, openings, and control-line locations. Compare every measured value to the approved slab plan and stated tolerance.
  2. Check stake spacing, brace stability, joint tightness, access, and screed path. Apply hand pressure at the form line to confirm stability at each stake and brace.

L. Cleanup, Correction, and Release

  1. Remove debris and protect the form system from equipment and weather damage. Clear loose material, cutoffs, and fasteners from inside the form area.
  2. Correct every failed dimension or unstable condition. Identify whether the outstanding item is a conflict requiring designer or engineer direction, and stop work on any unresolved conflict until direction is obtained.
  3. Complete the slab-form inspection record before base and reinforcement work proceeds. Photograph every verified form section and screed reference. Sign and date the release section of the record.

Common Layout Inspection

Inspect every verification item using the same checks regardless of which dimension, elevation, or reference it applies to.

  • Dimensional check: confirm each perimeter dimension is within the plan's stated tolerance, measured from the reference corner.
  • Diagonal check: confirm both diagonals match each other and the plan-calculated diagonal within the stated tolerance.
  • Elevation check: confirm form-top elevation and every screed reference match the approved finished-slab elevation, verified from the slab benchmark.
  • Thickness and joint check: confirm thickened edges, steps, depressions, and construction-joint locations match the approved slab plan.
  • Stability check: confirm every stake, brace, and kicker resists hand-applied lateral pressure without movement.

Complete the Slab Form and Control Line Inspection Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the work for Project 15.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Diagonals Do Not Match Each Other

Probable cause: one or more form corners were built out of square, or a diagonal was measured from an unconfirmed intermediate stake instead of the re-established reference corner.
Confirmation: re-measure both diagonals from the confirmed reference corner and re-check each perimeter dimension against the approved plan.
Correction: loosen the affected form section, relocate the out-of-square corner to the position required by the approved plan and stated tolerance, and re-fasten and re-brace it.
Reinspection: repeat the dimensional check and the diagonal check for the full perimeter.

Form-Top Elevation Does Not Match the Slab Benchmark

Probable cause: the form was set from an unverified stake elevation, or the instrument was not level during the reading.
Confirmation: re-verify the slab benchmark elevation, re-check instrument level, and retake the form-top reading at the affected section.
Correction: adjust the form to the correct elevation and re-check adjoining sections for a matching top-of-form line.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation check at the corrected section and at each adjoining section.

A Screed Reference Does Not Match the Approved Finished-Slab Elevation

Probable cause: the screed reference was set before the perimeter form elevation was verified, or the reference was read from an unconfirmed benchmark setup.
Confirmation: re-verify the perimeter form elevation first, then re-check the screed reference against the confirmed slab benchmark.
Correction: reset the screed reference to the correct elevation using the verified benchmark.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation check for every screed reference established during the same setup.

Form Moves Under Hand-Applied Pressure

Probable cause: insufficient stake spacing, an unbraced form run, or a stake driven into unstable or previously disturbed soil.
Confirmation: apply hand pressure at multiple points along the affected run and identify the specific stake, brace, or joint that moves.
Correction: add stakes, kickers, or braces at the required spacing, or relocate the stake to stable soil.
Reinspection: repeat the stability check along the full length of the corrected run.

Thickened Edge, Step, or Joint Location Conflicts With the Approved Plan

Probable cause: the location was marked from an assumed offset rather than the reference corner, or the approved plan was not consulted before marking.
Confirmation: compare the marked location and dimension to the approved slab plan.
Correction: stop work and obtain designer or engineer direction for any conflict involving dimensions, thickness, joint layout, or plan revisions; do not relocate a designed feature on independent judgment.
Reinspection: repeat the thickness and joint check after the conflict is resolved and any authorized correction is made.

Cleanup

This project ends with a released, protected form system, not a torn-down worksite.

  • Confirm every stake, brace, and kicker is stable, labeled, and protected from foot and equipment traffic
  • Remove cutoffs, damaged material, and loose fasteners from inside and around the form
  • Store unused form materials for the next project stage
  • Label and protect the slab benchmark, reference corner, and every screed reference for use in Project 15
  • Return the Slab Form and Control Line Inspection Record and photographs to the project record

Field Terms

Slab Benchmark
The protected fixed-elevation reference from which finished-slab elevation and every screed reference is verified.
Reference Corner
The single corner of the slab perimeter from which principal dimensions and diagonals are measured.
Construction Offset
The fixed horizontal distance from the foundation wall face to the slab-edge form line.
Screed Reference
Any rail, pin, mark, or laser control point that fixes finished-slab elevation at an interior location.
Thickened Edge
A designed increase in slab thickness at the perimeter or an interior location.
Control Joint Location
A planned joint position established in the form-layout stage.
Kicker
An angled brace member that resists lateral movement of a form under placing or screeding pressure.
Spreader
A temporary member that holds opposing form faces or edges at the required dimension until concrete placement.
Slab Form and Control Line Inspection Record
The document recording dimensions, diagonals, elevations, screed references, conflicts, corrections, and release.
Release
The formal, signed authorization that slab-form verification is complete and base work may begin.
