Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare foundation walls and install the moisture-control system specified for each wall area. The finished work product is a continuous, compatible, inspected damp-proofing or waterproofing assembly: cleaned and repaired wall surfaces, primer and coating or membrane applied to the correct zones and thickness, reinforced corners and cracks, sealed penetrations, terminated top and bottom edges, and a completed product and inspection record with photographs taken before the work is concealed.

You will review the approved moisture-control design and groundwater conditions, confirm that curing, tie treatment, and defect repair have reached the required condition, establish damp-proofing and waterproofing zones and their transitions, apply primer and the specified coating or membrane system, reinforce corners and cracks, seal every penetration, inspect coverage and continuity, repair any defect found, and photograph the completed system before it is concealed by insulation, protection board, or backfill.

This project follows directly from Project 8, which stripped forms and repaired minor concrete defects, and Project 9, which established the curing and protection record this project depends on. Its output is required before Project 11 installs the foundation-drain system and Project 12 installs insulation and protection board over the completed moisture-control assembly.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-055 — Clean wall surfaces in preparation for the specified moisture-control system.
  • FN-056 — Identify waterproofing zones and distinguish them from damp-proofing zones.
  • FN-057 — Apply damp-proofing coating to the designated wall areas at the required coverage.
  • FN-058 — Install waterproofing membrane or liquid-applied system in the designated zones.
  • FN-059 — Seal penetrations using compatible system components.
  • FN-060 — Inspect coating and membrane continuity and document the completed system.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 8 stripped the stem-wall forms and repaired minor concrete defects. Project 9 established the curing and early-age protection record that determines whether the concrete has reached the condition required for moisture-control work. This project converts a repaired, cured concrete wall into a continuous moisture-control assembly that the remaining below-grade projects in this book depend on directly: Project 11 cannot install the foundation-drain system, and Project 12 cannot install insulation and protection board, until this project's system has passed inspection and been photographed.

The zone-based discipline used in this chapter — treating damp-proofing and waterproofing as materially different systems rather than interchangeable coatings, and never claiming that one performs the other's function — is the same discipline applied again in the Chapter 20 capstone when the complete foundation moisture-control assembly is reviewed before backfill.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved moisture-control design, groundwater conditions, drainage plan, product system, substrate requirements, terminations, penetrations, transitions, and inspection hold points
  • confirming that concrete curing, form-tie treatment, defect repair, and surface preparation have reached the required condition
  • inspecting and repairing wall surfaces before coating or membrane application
  • establishing damp-proofing zones, waterproofing zones, transitions, and terminations
  • applying primer, damp-proofing coating, and waterproofing membrane or liquid-applied system according to the approved design and manufacturer instructions
  • reinforcing corners, cracks, joints, and changes in plane, and sealing penetrations
  • inspecting coverage, thickness, adhesion, laps, seams, and terminations, and repairing any defect found
  • photographing and documenting the completed system before concealment

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select a damp-proofing or waterproofing system on independent judgment; the approved plans and site conditions control which system each wall area receives
  • apply damp-proofing to an area the approved design designates for waterproofing, or claim that damp-proofing provides hydrostatic waterproofing performance
  • proceed with coating or membrane application before authorized substrate repairs have reached the condition required by the product
  • conceal the completed system with insulation, protection board, or backfill before it has passed inspection and been photographed
  • substitute an incompatible primer, accessory, or repair material for the one identified by the product manufacturer

Any substrate condition, coverage reading, seam, termination, or penetration seal that fails inspection must be corrected or escalated to a qualified professional before the system is concealed.

Trade Note

Damp-proofing and waterproofing are not the same system and do not provide the same protection. Damp-proofing resists soil moisture; it does not resist hydrostatic pressure. Applying a damp-proofing product to an area the design requires to be waterproofed, or describing a damp-proofing coating as waterproofing on the project record, is a documentation and performance failure that this chapter's zone-identification step (Step 6) exists to prevent.

Setup Standard

Do not begin coating or membrane application until concrete curing, form-tie treatment, and Project 8 defect repairs have reached the condition required by the specified product, and do not conceal the completed system with insulation, protection board, or backfill until it has passed inspection and been photographed in Step 19.

Shop Tip

Check wet-film thickness at multiple points across each wall area as you apply the coating, not only after the entire wall is finished. Correcting a thin area while the material is still workable is far faster than removing and reapplying a cured coating that failed the coverage check.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every moisture-control installation in this book follows the same sequence: confirm the substrate has reached the required condition, identify which zone each wall area belongs to, prepare and protect adjacent work, apply primer and the specified system at the controlled coverage, reinforce and seal every transition and penetration, inspect for continuity, and record and photograph the completed assembly before it is concealed.

Substrate Condition and Zone Identification

Do not begin coating or membrane application until the substrate has reached the condition required by the specified product, and do not apply a product to a wall area before confirming which zone that area belongs to. Applying the wrong system to a zone, or applying any system before the substrate is ready, produces a failure that cannot be corrected without removing and replacing the installed material.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Substrate and Coverage Control

Verify product storage conditions, temperature limits, shelf life, mixing ratios, coverage rate, and application-equipment requirements before opening any container. Apply primer, damp-proofing coating, and waterproofing membrane only at the coverage or thickness stated by the manufacturer, and confirm substrate moisture and temperature are within the manufacturer's stated range before application begins.

Code Alert — Damp-Proofing Is Not Waterproofing

Do not apply both systems generically across a wall, and do not represent a damp-proofing coating as providing hydrostatic waterproofing performance in the project record. The approved plans and site groundwater conditions determine whether a wall area receives damp-proofing, waterproofing, or a proprietary combined system rated for both functions by its manufacturer.

Field Safety Cue — Ventilation and Chemical Handling

Confirm ventilation is adequate for the specified product before mixing or applying it in a confined excavation area. Wear the chemical-resistant gloves, eye protection, and respiratory protection identified by the product safety data sheet, and keep ignition sources away from solvent-based products.

Critical Safety Cue — Excavation Edge Exposure

Foundation walls receiving moisture-control work are typically adjacent to an open excavation. Do not stand, kneel, or set up equipment at an unprotected excavation edge to reach a wall area. Identify a safe working position, ladder access, or assistance before working on any wall section that requires approaching the excavation edge.

E. Preparation — Document Review and Substrate Verification

  1. Review the approved moisture-control design, groundwater conditions, drainage plan, product system, substrate requirements, terminations, penetrations, transitions, and inspection hold points. Confirm the document set is complete before proceeding to Step 2.
  2. Confirm that concrete curing, form-tie treatment, defect repair, and surface preparation have reached the required condition. Verify against the Project 9 curing record and the Project 8 repair record before continuing.
  3. Inspect wall surfaces for fins, voids, cracks, tie holes, laitance, dirt, oil, release agent, moisture, frost, and incompatible repair materials. Record every condition found before continuing.

F. Substrate Repair and Protection

  1. Complete all authorized substrate repairs and allow them to reach the required condition. Use only repair materials compatible with the specified moisture-control system.
  2. Protect footing drains, openings, adjacent surfaces, hardware, and areas not receiving the product. Mask or cover every surface identified in the approved design as excluded from application.

G. Zone Identification

  1. Establish damp-proofing zones, waterproofing zones, transitions, top terminations, bottom terminations, corners, and penetrations. Mark every zone boundary on the wall before application begins.
  2. Verify product storage, temperature, shelf life, mixing, coverage, and application equipment. Confirm every product is within its manufacturer-stated limits before opening a container.

H. Application

  1. Apply required primer or conditioner to the prepared substrate. Apply only to the areas and at the coverage specified by the manufacturer.
  2. Apply the specified damp-proofing coating to designated walls at the manufacturer-controlled coverage or thickness. Verify coverage at multiple points as application proceeds.
  3. Install the specified waterproofing membrane or liquid-applied system in designated zones. Follow the manufacturer's application sequence and working-time limits.

I. Reinforcement and Penetration Sealing

  1. Reinforce corners, cracks, joints, changes in plane, and transitions using the approved accessories. Install reinforcing fabric or mesh per the product system before the base coat or membrane cures.
  2. Seal pipe, conduit, sleeve, tie-hole, and other penetrations using compatible system components. Verify no gap remains at any penetration boundary.
  3. Lap, roll, terminate, fasten, or tool membrane seams according to the product system. Set every seam with the specified seam roller or tool before it cures.
  4. Maintain continuity between footing, wall, sill, drain, flashing, and protection details. Confirm no gap exists at any transition between systems or building elements.

J. Inspection, Testing, and Documentation

  1. Inspect wet-film thickness, coverage, adhesion, laps, seams, terminations, and pinholes using the required method. Use the wet-film thickness gauge at the frequency specified by the manufacturer or approved design.
  2. Repair holidays, blisters, fishmouths, thin areas, tears, contamination, and damaged transitions. Use only manufacturer-approved repair procedures and compatible materials.
  3. Allow the system to cure or set for the specified period without rain, backfill, impact, or contamination. Barricade or protect the area for the full cure period.
  4. Conduct any required flood, adhesion, holiday, or visual testing without overstating what the test proves. Record the test method, duration, and result exactly as performed.
  5. Photograph the completed system before insulation or protection board conceals it. Photograph every zone boundary, termination, corner, and penetration seal.
  6. Complete the product, batch, weather, coverage, inspection, and repair record. Sign and date the completed Foundation Moisture-Control Installation Record.

Common Installation Inspection

Inspect every wall area using the same checks regardless of which zone, product, or detail it involves.

  • Substrate check: confirm the surface was free of fins, voids, cracks, tie holes, laitance, dirt, oil, release agent, moisture, and frost before application.
  • Zone check: confirm each wall area received the system designated by the approved design, with no substitution of damp-proofing for waterproofing or the reverse.
  • Coverage check: confirm wet-film thickness or coverage rate met the manufacturer-controlled value at every verified point.
  • Continuity check: confirm corners, cracks, joints, seams, terminations, and penetrations show no holidays, blisters, fishmouths, thin areas, or gaps.
  • Documentation check: confirm product, batch, coverage, inspection, and repair information is recorded and photographs were taken before concealment.

Complete the Foundation Moisture-Control Installation Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the wall for Project 11.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Coating or Membrane Fails the Coverage Check

Probable cause: the product was applied too thin, spread over too large an area from a fixed volume, or applied to a substrate that absorbed material unevenly.
Confirmation: re-check wet-film thickness at multiple points across the affected area using the specified gauge.
Correction: apply an additional compatible coat to bring the area to the manufacturer-controlled coverage before the existing material cures beyond the recoat window.
Reinspection: repeat the coverage check across the corrected area after the additional coat is applied.

Wrong System Applied to a Zone

Probable cause: zone boundaries were not marked before application began, or the applicator confused a damp-proofing area with a waterproofing area.
Confirmation: compare the applied product to the approved moisture-control design for that wall area.
Correction: stop work and obtain engineer or designer direction; do not conceal the area until the correct system has been installed according to approved direction.
Reinspection: repeat the zone-identification check and the coverage check for the corrected area.

Holiday, Pinhole, or Thin Area Found During Inspection

Probable cause: the product was not applied continuously across the surface, trapped air or debris interrupted the film, or the substrate had an unaddressed void.
Confirmation: visually inspect the area against a light source or use the required holiday-detection method, and confirm the location and extent of the defect.
Correction: repair using the manufacturer-approved patch procedure and compatible repair material.
Reinspection: repeat the continuity check for the repaired area and confirm no adjacent holiday remains.

Penetration Seal Shows a Gap

Probable cause: the penetration boot or patch was sized incorrectly for the pipe or sleeve, or the surrounding membrane was not tooled into the transition.
Confirmation: inspect the full perimeter of the penetration for continuous contact between the seal and the surrounding system.
Correction: remove and reinstall the penetration seal using the correct compatible component and tool the transition according to the product system.
Reinspection: repeat the penetration check for the corrected detail.

Substrate Was Not Ready When Application Began

Probable cause: curing, moisture, or temperature conditions had not reached the level required by the product, or an authorized repair had not fully cured before coating began.
Confirmation: compare the Project 9 curing record and repair-cure timeline to the manufacturer's stated substrate requirements.
Correction: stop work, allow the substrate to reach the required condition, and obtain manufacturer or engineer direction if any material has already been applied to an unready substrate.
Reinspection: repeat the substrate check before resuming application.

System Was Disturbed Before the Cure Period Ended

Probable cause: rain, backfill, impact, or foot traffic contacted the system before it reached the specified cure or set period.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area for tears, contamination, embedded debris, or incomplete cure.
Correction: repair or replace the disturbed area using the manufacturer-approved procedure and re-establish the required protection.
Reinspection: repeat the continuity check and restart the cure-period monitoring for the repaired area.

Field Terms

Damp-Proofing
A coating system that resists soil moisture but does not resist hydrostatic pressure.
Waterproofing
A membrane or liquid-applied system designed to resist hydrostatic pressure in the zones the approved design identifies.
Zone
A defined wall area assigned to receive a specific damp-proofing or waterproofing system by the approved design.
Transition
The defined boundary or detail where one system, zone, or building element meets another.
Termination
The defined top or bottom edge where the moisture-control system ends at a specified line or detail.
Wet-Film Thickness
The measured thickness of applied coating or membrane before cure, checked against the manufacturer's coverage rate.
Holiday
A gap, pinhole, or uncoated area in an otherwise continuous coating or membrane.
Penetration
Any pipe, conduit, sleeve, or opening passing through the moisture-control system that requires a compatible seal.
Foundation Moisture-Control Installation Record
The document recording product, batch, coverage, inspection, repairs, and photographs for the completed system.
Concealment
The point at which insulation, protection board, or backfill covers the completed system and further inspection becomes impractical.
