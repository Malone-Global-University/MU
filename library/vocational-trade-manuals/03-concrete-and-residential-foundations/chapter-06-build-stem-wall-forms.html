Job Overview

This project qualifies you to build, brace, and verify two-sided residential stem-wall forms from the footings verified and poured in Projects 01 through 05. The finished work product is a clean, stable, plumb, aligned, and fully braced form assembly at the approved wall thickness, height, and location, with corners, openings, pilasters, keyways, and blockouts formed to the approved detail, and a completed Stem-Wall Form Inspection Record ready to release the work for reinforcement.

You will review the approved wall dimensions and form-tie system, confirm the footing is prepared and ready to receive forms, establish wall faces and elevation references, select and inspect form materials, assemble and plumb the first form face, install the opposing face using the specified ties and spreaders, form corners and openings, install bracing, apply release agent only where approved, install cleanouts, verify every dimension and stability condition, remove debris, and complete the form inspection before reinforcement and concrete placement begin.

This project depends directly on the footing work completed in Projects 02 through 05. Stem-wall forms may not be installed until the footing has reached the required condition and the footing surfaces, keys, dowels, and construction joints are prepared as confirmed in Step 2 below.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-031 — Lay out stem walls from the verified footing and elevation controls.
  • FN-032 — Assemble wall forms to the approved wall thickness, height, corners, openings, and details.
  • FN-033 — Brace forms to resist placement pressure and worker contact.
  • FN-034 — Install form ties according to the approved tie system and spacing.
  • FN-035 — Verify wall thickness against the approved plan using spreaders or gauges.
  • FN-036 — Inspect plumb and alignment of the completed form assembly.

Where This Skill Fits

Projects 02 through 05 built, reinforced, poured, and equipped the footings that this project's forms now rest upon. The wall faces, corners, and openings you establish here directly control the reinforcement installed in Project 07, the concrete placed in that same project, and every wall dimension checked again when the stripped concrete is inspected in Project 08.

The plumb, alignment, and tie-spacing discipline used in this chapter is the same discipline applied later in this book whenever a form must resist fluid concrete pressure without moving: it recurs in the slab-form work of Project 14 and underlies the capstone review in Project 20.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved wall dimensions, height, thickness, steps, pilasters, openings, form-tie system, concrete placement plan, reinforcement details, and pressure limitations
  • confirming footing readiness and cleaning footing surfaces before form assembly
  • establishing wall faces, centerlines, corners, openings, steps, and top elevations from verified controls
  • selecting, inspecting, cutting, and assembling form components
  • plumbing, aligning, staking, and bracing both form faces
  • forming corners, intersections, steps, pilasters, blockouts, keyways, sleeves, and openings
  • tightening ties and supports in a controlled sequence, applying release agent only to approved surfaces, and installing cleanouts
  • verifying thickness, height, elevation, line, plumb, corners, openings, and step dimensions before release

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • redesign the wall thickness, height, tie spacing, or form-tie system specified by the approved documents
  • install reinforcement or place concrete before the form inspection in this project is complete and released
  • substitute a form-tie system, panel product, or bracing method that differs from the approved form design without engineer or designer authorization
  • form or pour a wall on a footing that has not reached the required condition or that has failed inspection

Any dimension, plumb reading, alignment check, or stability condition that fails verification must be corrected before reinforcement or concrete work proceeds.

Trade Note

Every wall-thickness measurement in this chapter is taken with the specified spreader, gauge, or tie length — never estimated by eye against the opposing form face. A form that looks parallel at the top can still be narrower or wider than specified partway down its height, particularly at corners and openings where standard spreaders cannot be used.

Setup Standard

Do not install reinforcement or place concrete until this project's Stem-Wall Form Inspection Record is complete and the form assembly has been formally released in the final step of the work order. Any reinforcement staged before release must be reverified after the form inspection.

Shop Tip

Tighten form ties in the sequence specified by the form design — typically working from the center of a form run outward, or in the pattern shown by the tie manufacturer — rather than tightening each tie fully before moving to the next. Tightening ties out of sequence pulls the form out of line one tie at a time and is difficult to correct once the full run is tight.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every form-building task in this book, from this chapter's stem walls to the slab forms of Project 14, follows the same sequence: confirm the surface the forms will rest on, establish wall faces and elevations from verified controls, assemble and plumb one face at a time, control thickness with the specified ties or gauges, brace for the actual placement pressure, and verify every dimension before concrete is scheduled. Learning that sequence on stem walls, where pressure is highest and the consequence of an unbraced blowout is greatest, prepares you for every later forming task in this book.

Form Pressure and Bracing

Fresh concrete behaves as a fluid at the moment of placement and exerts lateral pressure on the form faces that increases with placement rate, concrete temperature, and wall height. The bracing, tie spacing, and waler schedule specified in the approved form design account for this pressure at the planned placement rate. Do not reduce the specified number of ties, walers, or braces to save time or material, and do not increase the placement rate beyond what the form design allows.

Critical Safety Cue — Form Blowout Risk

An improperly tied or braced form can fail suddenly under the pressure of fresh concrete, releasing a large volume of concrete without warning. Do not begin concrete placement until every tie, waler, brace, and stake has been verified against the approved form design. Treat any sign of form movement, bulging, or leaking during placement as a stop-work condition requiring immediate correction before placement continues.

Field Safety Cue — Working at the Top of Tall Forms

Use a stepladder, scaffold plank, or other approved access when working at the top of a form section that places your feet above stable standing height. Do not stand on walers, braces, or the top edge of an unbraced form panel to reach a fastening point.

Measurement Cue — Verify Thickness at Multiple Points

Check wall thickness with the specified spreader, tie length, or gauge at the top, mid-height, and bottom of each form section, and at both sides of every corner and opening. A form face can be parallel at the top and out of tolerance lower down if a stake or brace was set before the opposing face was positioned.

Manufacturer Cue — Proprietary Tie and Panel Systems

Proprietary form-tie and panel systems specify their own tie spacing, tightening sequence, tightening value, and compatible accessories. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for the specific product installed on this project rather than a generic tie-spacing assumption from a different system.

Code Alert — Inspection Hold Points

Local jurisdictions vary in whether a form inspection is required before reinforcement installation, before concrete placement, or at both points. Confirm the required inspection hold points with the local building department before scheduling concrete delivery.

E. Preparation — Document Review and Footing Confirmation

  1. Review the approved wall dimensions, height, thickness, steps, pilasters, openings, form-tie system, concrete placement plan, reinforcement details, and pressure limitations. Confirm the document set is complete before proceeding to Step 2.
  2. Confirm that footings have reached the required condition and that footing surfaces, keys, dowels, and construction joints are prepared. Obtain written confirmation before disturbing the footing surface.
  3. Clean footing surfaces and remove laitance, debris, standing water, and unauthorized projections. Record the footing condition before form layout begins.

F. Establishing References

  1. Establish wall faces, centerlines, corners, openings, steps, and top elevations from the verified controls. Snap chalk lines on the footing for both wall faces before selecting materials.
  2. Select form panels, studs, walers, strongbacks, ties, spreaders, braces, chamfers, blockouts, fasteners, and release agent according to the form design. Confirm every selected component matches the approved form design.
  3. Inspect form materials and proprietary components for damage, contamination, and compatibility. Set aside any damaged or incompatible component before assembly.

G. Component Preparation and First Face Assembly

  1. Cut, label, and stage form components in installation order. Organize components by wall run and location before assembly begins.
  2. Assemble the first form face and position it to the approved wall line. Set the face directly on the chalk line established in Step 4.
  3. Plumb, align, stake, and temporarily brace the first form face. Check plumb with the level at each corner before final staking.

H. Second Face, Corners, and Openings

  1. Install the opposing form face using approved ties and spreaders to establish wall thickness. Verify thickness with the specified gauge before securing each tie.
  2. Install corners, intersections, steps, pilasters, blockouts, keyways, sleeves, and opening forms. Match each installation to the approved foundation and structural detail.
  3. Install walers, strongbacks, kickers, braces, and anchorage required by the form design. Install every component listed in the approved bracing schedule.

I. Tightening, Release Agent, and Cleanouts

  1. Tighten ties and supports in a controlled sequence to avoid pulling the forms out of line. Follow the tightening sequence specified by the form design or tie manufacturer.
  2. Apply form-release agent only to approved contact surfaces and keep it off reinforcement and construction joints. Confirm no release agent has contacted the footing key, dowels, or construction joint.
  3. Install cleanout openings where required for debris removal and inspection. Locate cleanouts at the base of the form where specified by the form design.

J. Verification

  1. Verify wall thickness, form height, top elevation, line, plumb, corners, openings, and step dimensions. Check thickness at top, mid-height, and bottom of every section.
  2. Check tie spacing, brace attachment, fastener seating, form joints, and resistance to movement. Confirm every tie and brace matches the approved spacing schedule.
  3. Confirm safe access, working platforms, guardrails, ladders, and placement routes where required. Verify access is adequate for the planned placement method before concluding the inspection.

K. Cleanup and Release

  1. Remove debris through the cleanouts and close them using the approved method. Confirm the form interior is free of debris before closing each cleanout.
  2. Mark concrete-placement lifts, top elevation, embedded-item locations, and monitoring points. Mark these references on the outside of the form for use during placement.
  3. Correct all dimensional or stability deficiencies. Re-verify any corrected item before proceeding to Step 22.
  4. Complete the stem-wall form inspection before reinforcement and concrete placement. Sign and date the release section of the Stem-Wall Form Inspection Record.

Common Form Inspection

Inspect every form section using the same checks regardless of which wall run, corner, or opening it applies to.

  • Thickness check: confirm wall thickness is within the plan's stated tolerance at top, mid-height, and bottom of each section, using the specified spreader, tie length, or gauge.
  • Plumb check: confirm both form faces are plumb at each corner and at intervals along the run.
  • Alignment check: confirm the wall line matches the chalk or string line at top and bottom of each section.
  • Elevation check: confirm the top-of-wall elevation matches the approved plan, verified from the protected benchmark.
  • Bracing check: confirm every tie, waler, strongback, and brace listed in the approved form design is installed, seated, and fastened.
  • Cleanliness check: confirm the form interior is free of debris, wood scraps, and standing water before reinforcement installation.

Complete the Stem-Wall Form Inspection Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the work for Project 07.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Wall Thickness Out of Tolerance Partway Down the Form

Probable cause: a stake or brace was set before the opposing face was positioned, or the spreader was removed before the tie was fully tightened.
Confirmation: re-check thickness at top, mid-height, and bottom of the affected section using the specified gauge.
Correction: loosen the affected ties, reposition the form face to the approved thickness, and re-tighten in the specified sequence.
Reinspection: repeat the thickness check at all three heights for the corrected section and the adjacent sections.

Form Face Out of Plumb

Probable cause: the stake or brace was driven before the level check, or the brace shifted during tie installation.
Confirmation: re-check plumb with the level at the corner and at the midpoint of the affected section.
Correction: loosen the brace, replumb the face, and re-secure the brace before re-tightening adjacent ties.
Reinspection: repeat the plumb check for the corrected section and the two adjacent sections.

Form Bulges or Leaks During a Trial Check

Probable cause: insufficient ties or braces for the specified placement rate, a missing waler, or an unsealed panel joint.
Confirmation: compare the installed tie and brace spacing to the approved form design for the affected section.
Correction: install the missing ties, walers, or braces, and seal any open panel joint before concrete placement is scheduled.
Reinspection: repeat the bracing check and confirm the corrected section matches the approved schedule.

Corner or Opening Dimension Does Not Match the Approved Detail

Probable cause: the corner or opening form was built from a generic dimension rather than the specific detail on the approved structural drawing.
Confirmation: compare the installed corner or opening form to the approved foundation and structural detail.
Correction: stop work and obtain engineer or designer direction before modifying a corner, pilaster, or opening that conflicts with the approved detail.
Reinspection: repeat the corner or opening dimensional check after the correction is authorized and made.

Debris or Standing Water Found Inside the Form

Probable cause: a cleanout was closed before final debris removal, or weather introduced water after the last inspection.
Confirmation: reopen the cleanout and visually inspect the full form interior.
Correction: remove all debris and standing water through the cleanout before closing it again.
Reinspection: repeat the cleanliness check immediately before the form inspection is signed off.

Release Agent Contamination Near a Construction Joint

Probable cause: release agent was applied to the form face before the footing key or dowel area was masked or protected.
Confirmation: inspect the footing key, dowels, and construction joint area for visible release-agent contact.
Correction: clean the contaminated area using the method approved for the specified concrete-bonding system before concrete placement.
Reinspection: repeat the construction-joint inspection after cleaning is complete.

Cleanup

This project ends with a released, braced form assembly, not a torn-down worksite.

  • Confirm every cleanout is closed by the approved method and the form interior remains debris-free
  • Remove offcuts, packaging, and unused fasteners from the work area
  • Store unused form components, ties, and hardware for the next wall run or project
  • Protect the completed form assembly from weather and equipment contact before reinforcement installation
  • Return the Stem-Wall Form Inspection Record and photographs to the project record

Field Terms
