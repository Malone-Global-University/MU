Job Overview

This project qualifies you to build the complete slab-support assembly that lies beneath a residential concrete slab: a fine-graded and compacted aggregate base, a continuous sealed vapor barrier, and a properly supported reinforcement layer, with every plumbing sleeve, conduit, column, and embedded item coordinated before concealment. The finished work product is a compacted, sealed, reinforced, inspected slab assembly ready for the continuous concrete placement in Project 16.

You will place and compact approved aggregate in uniform lifts, fine-grade the base to the required plane and slopes, install and seal the vapor barrier with the specified edge extensions, seal every penetration, place welded wire reinforcement or rebar on approved chairs or bolsters at the specified elevation, install thickened-edge reinforcement and joint assemblies included at this stage, and complete a photographed pre-pour inspection record.

This project builds directly on the slab forms and control lines established in Project 14. It assumes those forms, and any underground utility work coordinated beneath the slab, have already passed their required inspections. No concrete may be placed until this project's vapor-barrier, reinforcement, and pre-pour inspection steps are complete and recorded.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-085 — Fine-grade the slab base to the required plane, thickness, and slopes.
  • FN-086 — Compact aggregate in uniform lifts using equipment suitable for the area and embedded systems.
  • FN-087 — Install the vapor barrier in the required orientation with the specified edge extensions.
  • FN-088 — Tape and seal seams, laps, and penetrations using compatible materials.
  • FN-089 — Install welded wire mesh or rebar according to the approved reinforcement schedule.
  • FN-090 — Inspect slab preparation, including base, vapor barrier, and reinforcement, before concrete placement.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 14 delivered dimensionally correct, braced slab-edge forms with elevation and screed references already established. This project builds the assembly those forms will contain: base, vapor barrier, and reinforcement, each installed and inspected in sequence so nothing is concealed before it is verified. Project 16 depends entirely on the elevation, support, and continuity established here — the reinforcement must remain at its specified elevation throughout placement, and the vapor barrier must remain continuous and undamaged beneath it.

The lift-by-lift compaction discipline used here mirrors the balanced-lift approach used later in Project 13's backfill and reappears in the Chapter 20 capstone, where the same base-to-reinforcement sequence is verified across the complete foundation system.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved subgrade and aggregate elevations, base thickness, compaction requirement, vapor-barrier rating, seam treatment, and reinforcement schedule
  • placing and compacting approved aggregate in uniform lifts without damaging underground work
  • fine-grading the base to the required plane, thickness, slopes, and depressions
  • installing and sealing a continuous vapor barrier with the specified edge extensions and penetration details
  • placing and supporting welded wire reinforcement or rebar, including thickened-edge reinforcement, dowels, isolation joints, control-joint assemblies, and embeds included at this stage
  • inspecting and photographing the complete assembly before concrete placement

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • revise the approved base thickness, compaction requirement, vapor-barrier rating, or reinforcement schedule on their own authority
  • install or relocate underground plumbing, electrical, or radiant-tubing systems beneath the slab without the coordinating trade's approved layout
  • place concrete before the vapor barrier, reinforcement, and pre-pour inspection have passed
  • proceed past a soft spot, settlement, or unsuitable subgrade condition without authorized correction

Any base, vapor-barrier, or reinforcement condition that fails inspection must be corrected and reverified before concrete placement continues.

Trade Note

Every layer in this project depends on the one before it: a soft or unevenly graded base telegraphs through the vapor barrier and can leave reinforcement resting on chairs that later settle unevenly. Verify each layer's elevation and condition before covering it with the next, rather than correcting a base problem after the vapor barrier is already sealed over it.

Setup Standard

Do not place concrete until the vapor barrier is continuous and sealed, reinforcement is supported at the specified elevation, all embeds and penetrations are coordinated, and the pre-pour inspection record in Step 23 is complete. Shop Tip

Establish and mark protected walk paths across the vapor barrier before reinforcement work begins. Routing all foot and material traffic along the same marked paths concentrates any accidental punctures where they are easiest to find and repair before concrete placement.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every layer in this project follows the same sequence: verify the surface below is acceptable, place or install the layer to the specified condition, inspect and repair before covering it, and record the result before moving to the next layer. Learning that sequence here, applied across base, barrier, and reinforcement, is the same discipline used to verify the completed slab assembly in the Chapter 20 capstone.

Layer Sequence and Concealment Discipline

Each layer in this project conceals the one beneath it. Verify base elevation and compaction before the vapor barrier goes down; verify barrier continuity and seam integrity before reinforcement is placed on top of it; verify reinforcement position and support before the concrete truck arrives. Do not proceed to the next layer while an unresolved deficiency remains in the layer below it.

Measurement Cue — Verify Elevation From the Slab Benchmark at Every Stage

Verify base elevation, compaction, and final grade from the slab benchmark established in Project 14, not from a convenient nearby point or a previous day's grade stake. Verify reinforcement elevation from the same benchmark using the specified chair or bolster height, not by eye.

Critical Safety Cue — Compaction Equipment and Reinforcement Ends

Keep hands and feet clear of a running plate compactor or vibratory roller's moving base plate or drum. Cap or bend over exposed rebar ends that project into a walk path, and treat any uncapped vertical bar as a stop-work condition until it is protected.

Field Safety Cue — Protected Walk Paths on the Vapor Barrier

Confirm the marked protected walk path before crossing the installed vapor barrier with tools, reinforcement bundles, or foot traffic. Do not drag sharp-edged material across the barrier, and report any suspected puncture immediately so it can be located and repaired before concealment.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Vapor-Barrier Seam and Penetration System

Use only the tape, adhesive, and penetration-boot components identified as compatible with the installed vapor-barrier product. Follow the manufacturer's lap width, seam-sealing method, and penetration-detail instructions; an incompatible sealant or an undersized lap can void the barrier's rating even when the sheeting itself is undamaged.

E. Preparation — Document Review and Subgrade Inspection

  1. Review required subgrade and aggregate elevations, base thickness, compaction, vapor-barrier rating, seam treatment, reinforcement schedule, cover, penetrations, embeds, and inspection requirements. Confirm the document set is complete before proceeding to Step 2.
  2. Verify slab forms, underground utilities, plumbing, sleeves, and subgrade approvals. Confirm Project 14's form inspection and any underground-work inspections are on record before continuing.
  3. Inspect the slab area for soft spots, settlement, mud, standing water, debris, sharp objects, and damaged penetrations. Record every condition found before continuing.
  4. Correct utility-trench settlement and unsuitable areas using approved fill and compaction procedures. Obtain designer or inspector direction for any condition outside the approved plan.

F. Base Placement and Fine Grading

  1. Establish base-elevation controls from the slab benchmark. Mark the required lift and finish-grade elevations before placing aggregate.
  2. Place approved aggregate in uniform lifts without damaging underground work. Control placement rate and equipment routing to protect sleeves and utility lines.
  3. Compact each lift using equipment suitable for the area and embedded systems. Match compactor type and pass count to the specified compaction requirement and any nearby embedded system.
  4. Fine-grade the aggregate to the required plane, thickness, slopes, and depressions. Use the straightedge or laser grade rod referenced from the slab benchmark.
  5. Verify elevation and compaction at perimeter, center, penetrations, thickened areas, and transitions. Record each verification point before covering it.
  6. Smooth or cover sharp aggregate that could puncture the vapor barrier. Remove or bury any protruding sharp material before barrier placement.

G. Vapor-Barrier Installation

  1. Place the vapor barrier in the required orientation with the specified edge extensions. Extend the barrier up the slab-edge form or foundation wall to the specified height.
  2. Lap and seal seams using compatible tape, adhesive, or accessories. Meet the manufacturer's minimum lap width at every seam.
  3. Seal pipe, conduit, sleeve, column, and other penetrations using approved boots or patch details. Verify each penetration is fully sealed before continuing.
  4. Repair punctures, tears, contamination, and failed seams. Patch every defect found before reinforcement traffic begins.
  5. Establish protected walk paths and restrict traffic on the membrane. Mark the approved routes for all remaining foot and material traffic.

H. Reinforcement Placement and Support

  1. Cut, place, lap, tie, or connect welded wire reinforcement or reinforcing bars according to the approved schedule. Match every bar mark, size, spacing, and lap to the reinforcement schedule.
  2. Support reinforcement on approved chairs, bolsters, or supports so it remains at the specified elevation during placement. Space supports according to the approved schedule to prevent sag.
  3. Install thickened-edge reinforcement, dowels, isolation joints, control-joint assemblies, embeds, and hold-down items included at this stage. Coordinate each item's exact location with the approved plan.
  4. Coordinate radiant tubing or other embedded systems without violating reinforcement, cover, or slab details. Resolve any conflict with the coordinating trade before concealment.

I. Final Verification and Pre-Pour Inspection

  1. Verify reinforcement size, spacing, laps, support, cover, continuity, and stability. Walk the full reinforcement layout and correct any deficiency found.
  2. Inspect vapor-barrier continuity after reinforcement installation and repair all damage. Check for punctures caused during reinforcement placement.
  3. Remove loose wire, bar cutoffs, debris, and standing water. Clear the assembly before photographing it.
  4. Photograph the concealed assembly and complete the pre-pour inspection record. Sign and date the release section of the Slab Preparation Inspection Worksheet.

Common Slab-Preparation Inspection

Inspect every layer using the same checks regardless of which layer it applies to.

  • Base check: confirm elevation, compaction, and plane match the approved plan at every verification point.
  • Barrier check: confirm the vapor barrier is continuous, correctly oriented, and free of unrepaired punctures, tears, or failed seams.
  • Penetration check: confirm every pipe, conduit, sleeve, and column penetration is sealed with compatible boots or patch details.
  • Reinforcement check: confirm size, spacing, laps, cover, and continuity match the approved reinforcement schedule.
  • Support check: confirm reinforcement is supported on approved chairs or bolsters at the specified elevation and remains stable underfoot.
  • Cleanliness check: confirm the assembly is free of loose wire, bar cutoffs, debris, and standing water before photographing.

Complete the Slab Preparation Inspection Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the work for Project 16.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Base Fails Elevation or Compaction Verification

Probable cause: an aggregate lift was placed thicker than specified, compaction equipment was mismatched to the lift, or fine grading was performed from an unconfirmed reference instead of the slab benchmark.
Confirmation: re-check lift thickness against the approved plan and re-verify elevation from the slab benchmark at the failed location.
Correction: remove and re-place the affected lift within the specified thickness, then recompact and re-grade to the required plane.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation and compaction check at the corrected location and at adjacent transition points.

Vapor Barrier Is Punctured or Seam Fails

Probable cause: sharp aggregate was not removed before barrier placement, foot or material traffic crossed the membrane outside the protected walk path, or the seam lap was narrower than the manufacturer's minimum.
Confirmation: inspect the surrounding area for the source of the puncture and re-check the lap width against the manufacturer's instructions.
Correction: patch the puncture and reseal the seam using compatible materials and the manufacturer's specified lap width.
Reinspection: repeat the barrier-continuity check for the repaired area and confirm no additional damage exists nearby.

Penetration Is Not Fully Sealed

Probable cause: the penetration boot or patch detail was undersized, misaligned, or installed before the pipe, conduit, sleeve, or column was in its final position.
Confirmation: inspect the penetration for gaps, incomplete adhesion, or misalignment between the boot and the penetrating item.
Correction: remove and reinstall the boot or patch detail using the manufacturer's specified method and compatible materials.
Reinspection: repeat the penetration-seal check for every penetration in the affected area.

Reinforcement Fails Spacing, Cover, or Support Verification

Probable cause: chairs or bolsters were spaced beyond the approved schedule, reinforcement shifted during placement of an adjacent bar, or cover was measured from an incorrect reference surface.
Confirmation: re-measure spacing, lap length, and cover against the approved reinforcement schedule at the failed location.
Correction: add or reposition chairs or bolsters, retie affected intersections, and reposition bars to the specified spacing and cover.
Reinspection: repeat the reinforcement check for the corrected area and confirm adjacent bars were not disturbed.

Embedded System Conflicts With Reinforcement or Cover

Probable cause: radiant tubing, a dowel, or a joint assembly was installed without coordinating its location against the reinforcement layout or the specified cover.
Confirmation: compare the installed embedded-item location to the approved plan and the reinforcement schedule.
Correction: reposition the embedded item or the affected reinforcement to restore the specified cover and spacing; do not field-cut reinforcement to resolve the conflict without authorization.
Reinspection: repeat the reinforcement and embedded-system coordination check for the corrected area.

Cleanup

This project ends with a released, photographed slab assembly, not a torn-down worksite.

  • Remove loose wire, bar cutoffs, aggregate spoil, and debris from the slab area and protected walk paths
  • Confirm every repaired vapor-barrier area and sealed penetration is undisturbed after final cleanup
  • Store unused aggregate, barrier sheeting, reinforcement, and hardware for later placements
  • Protect the completed assembly from equipment traffic, weather, and contamination until Project 16 begins
  • Return the Slab Preparation Inspection Worksheet and photographs to the project record with the Project 14 handoff documentation
