Job Overview

This project qualifies you to implement and document the continued curing, temperature control, access restriction, and early-age protection plan for the footings, stem walls, and repairs already placed in this book. The finished work product is a maintained and documented curing and protection system covering every current placement, and a completed curing log recording temperatures, moisture checks, weather, protection changes, test results, access incidents, and the final release decision.

You will review the concrete-mixture requirements, placement records, and specified curing method for each placement; confirm which curing method already began during placement and identify any disturbed protection; establish restricted-access zones; install and maintain the specified moisture-retention or curing-compound system; monitor temperatures and moisture at the required frequency; protect against freezing, overheating, and rapid drying; coordinate strength-verification testing; and issue a documented curing closeout or continued-protection plan.

This project applies to the footing concrete placed and initially cured in Project 04, the anchor bolts and embedded hardware protected in Project 05, the stem-wall concrete placed in Project 07, and the minor repairs completed in Project 08. No waterproofing, form removal beyond what has already occurred, backfilling, anchor tightening, or loading of the concrete may proceed until the applicable curing period and release criteria in this project have been satisfied.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-049 — Identify the curing requirements specified for each concrete placement.
  • FN-050 — Maintain moisture at the required concrete surfaces for the specified period.
  • FN-051 — Protect concrete from heat, wind, and rapid drying.
  • FN-052 — Protect concrete from freezing during the early-age period.
  • FN-053 — Prevent early loading, impact, and unauthorized access to curing concrete.
  • FN-054 — Document the curing period, monitoring results, and release decision.

Where This Skill Fits

Every placement made so far in this book — the footings in Project 04, the embedded hardware protected through Project 05, and the stem walls in Project 07 — began its specified curing method at the time of placement. Project 08 added the repaired areas from stripped stem walls to that same protection obligation. This project is where that ongoing protection is inspected, maintained, documented, and formally closed out or continued, rather than left to chance between placements.

The monitoring and access-restriction discipline established here also governs Project 10 through Project 13: no waterproofing, drainage, insulation, or backfill work in those projects may proceed until this project's curing release criteria confirm the wall is ready. The same curing and protection method returns without modification for the slab placement in Project 16 and Project 17, and again for the sill-plate handoff documentation in Project 19.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the specified curing method, curing duration, temperature limits, strength requirements, and loading restrictions for each current placement
  • confirming the curing method that began during placement and identifying disturbed or failed protection
  • establishing and posting restricted-access zones with the required load, traffic, and form-removal limitations
  • installing and maintaining wet coverings, curing compound, plastic sheeting, insulated blankets, enclosures, heating, shading, or windbreaks as specified
  • monitoring ambient, enclosure, surface, or concrete temperatures at the required frequency
  • protecting against freezing, overheating, high wind, direct sun, rain impact, and premature drying
  • coordinating test cylinders, maturity data, or field-cured specimens where required
  • documenting the curing period and issuing a release or continued-protection decision

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • remove forms, apply waterproofing, backfill, tighten anchors, or load the concrete without the authorization required in Step 16
  • select a curing method, duration, or temperature limit that differs from the concrete-mixture requirements and placement records without engineer or supplier direction
  • interpret cracking, scaling, discoloration, or incomplete protection discovered during or after curing as automatically acceptable; every such condition must be inspected and documented per Step 18
  • operate temporary heating equipment without the required ventilation and combustion controls

Any placement whose curing protection has been disturbed, whose temperature has exceeded the specified limits, or whose strength verification has failed must be corrected, re-monitored, or escalated to a qualified professional before release.

Trade Note

Curing is not a single action performed once at the end of a pour. It is a maintained condition that must be inspected and, where necessary, restored at every check throughout the specified period. A covering that was correctly installed at placement but has since torn, dried out, or blown open provides no more protection than no covering at all.

Setup Standard

Do not remove forms beyond what has already occurred, apply waterproofing, backfill, tighten anchors, or apply any load to the concrete until this project's authorization step confirms the specified curing period and strength requirement have been satisfied for the applicable placement.

Shop Tip

Check curing protection at the same time each day whenever practical. A consistent monitoring schedule makes it easier to notice a covering that dried out overnight or a heater that failed sometime after the last check, rather than discovering the failure only at the end of the full curing period.

Curing as a Maintained Condition

The curing method identified in Step 2 must remain effective for the full specified duration, not merely be present at the start of monitoring. A covering that has dried, torn, blown open, or separated from the concrete surface no longer provides the specified protection even though it remains physically in place. Every monitoring check in this project must confirm the covering is both present and functioning.

Measurement Cue — Monitor at the Required Frequency and Location

Monitor ambient, enclosure, surface, or concrete temperatures using the instrument and frequency specified in the concrete-mixture requirements or by the engineer or concrete supplier. Take temperature readings at the specific locations identified for each placement, not only at a single convenient point, and record every reading on the Curing and Protection Log at the time it is taken.

Critical Safety Cue — Temporary Heating Equipment

Maintain safe ventilation and combustion controls for any temporary heating equipment used to protect curing concrete. Do not operate fuel-burning heaters in an enclosed space without the ventilation specified by the equipment manufacturer. Treat carbon monoxide accumulation, flame instability, or fuel-supply damage as a stop-work condition requiring immediate shutdown and qualified correction.

Field Safety Cue — Restricted-Access Zones

Post the required load, traffic, and form-removal limitations at every restricted-access zone before leaving a placement unattended. Confirm barricades remain intact and legible at each monitoring check, and restore any barricade or sign that has been displaced, damaged, or removed before continuing.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Curing Compound Application

Apply the specified curing compound at the coverage rate, application timing, and equipment identified by the product manufacturer. Do not substitute a different compound or coverage rate without confirming compatibility with the concrete mixture and any later surface treatment, such as waterproofing or flooring, planned for the same surface.

E. Preparation — Document Review and Placement Confirmation

  1. Review the concrete-mixture requirements, placement records, weather data, specified curing method, curing duration, temperature limits, strength requirements, and loading restrictions. Confirm this information is available for every current placement before continuing.
  2. Confirm the curing method that began during each placement and identify areas where protection has been disturbed. Walk each placement and compare its current condition to the method recorded at the time of placement.
  3. Inspect concrete surfaces for drying, cracking, freezing indicators, contamination, impact, and unauthorized loading. Record every condition found before continuing.

F. Establishing Access Restriction

  1. Establish restricted-access zones and post the required load, traffic, and form-removal limitations. Install barricades and signage at every current placement before leaving the area unattended.

G. Installing and Maintaining the Protection System

  1. Install or maintain wet coverings, curing compound, plastic sheeting, insulated blankets, enclosures, heating, shading, windbreaks, or other specified systems. Use the method identified in Step 1 for each placement.
  2. Maintain full contact and continuity of coverings without staining or damaging the concrete. Correct any gap, tear, or lifted edge found during installation.
  3. Maintain moisture at the required surfaces for the specified period. Re-wet, replace, or reapply the curing method at the frequency required to keep it continuously effective.

H. Temperature Monitoring and Protection

  1. Monitor ambient, enclosure, surface, or concrete temperatures using the required instruments and frequency. Record every reading on the Curing and Protection Log at the time it is taken.
  2. Protect concrete from rapid temperature change when removing heat or insulation. Remove insulated blankets or heating gradually according to the specified schedule.
  3. Protect against freezing, overheating, high wind, direct sun, rain impact, and premature drying. Install shading, windbreaks, or additional insulation as conditions require.
  4. Maintain safe ventilation and combustion controls for temporary heating equipment. Inspect heaters at every monitoring check for fuel-supply damage and flame stability.

I. Access Control and Inspection

  1. Prevent equipment, backfill, framing, stockpiles, anchors, or other loads from being applied before authorization. Confirm no unauthorized load has been placed at each monitoring check.
  2. Inspect curing materials and protection systems at the required intervals. Compare current condition to the condition recorded at the previous check.
  3. Correct displaced blankets, open seams, dry coverings, failed heaters, ponding, damaged barricades, or unauthorized access. Restore every deficient condition before the next scheduled check.

J. Strength Verification and Release

  1. Coordinate test cylinders, maturity data, field-cured specimens, or other strength-verification methods where required. Use the personnel and procedure specified by the engineer, concrete supplier, or testing agency.
  2. Obtain authorization before removing forms, applying waterproofing, backfilling, tightening anchors, or loading the concrete. Do not proceed on independent judgment if strength verification has not confirmed the required result.
  3. Remove curing materials only at the approved time and in the approved sequence. Follow the sequence specified for the applicable curing method.
  4. Inspect the concrete after curing for cracking, scaling, discoloration, surface damage, and incomplete protection. Record every condition found before completing the release decision.

K. Recording and Closeout

  1. Record dates, times, temperatures, moisture checks, weather, protection changes, test results, access incidents, and release decisions. Complete every field on the Curing and Protection Log for each placement.
  2. Issue the documented curing closeout or continued-protection plan. Sign and date the release section for every placement that has met its curing duration and strength requirement, or state the continued-protection period for any placement that has not.

Common Curing Inspection

Inspect every current placement using the same checks regardless of whether it is a footing, a stem wall, or a repaired area.

  • Method check: confirm the curing method in place matches the method specified in the concrete-mixture requirements or placement plan.
  • Moisture check: confirm no drying, staining, or covering damage is present at the required surfaces.
  • Temperature check: confirm readings remain within the specified limits at every required location and interval.
  • Access check: confirm barricades and signage are intact and no unauthorized loading, traffic, or form removal has occurred.
  • Strength-verification check: confirm test cylinders, maturity data, or field-cured specimens meet the required strength before release.

Complete the Curing and Protection Log in the Inspection Checklist section below before issuing the release decision for Project 10.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Covering Has Dried Out or Separated From the Surface

Probable cause: wind, sun exposure, or an insufficient re-wetting schedule allowed the wet covering to dry and lift away from the concrete surface before the specified curing duration was complete.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area for dry, chalky, or discolored concrete and compare the elapsed time to the specified curing duration.
Correction: re-wet the surface and reinstall or replace the covering, extending the monitoring schedule to confirm continuous moisture for the remaining specified period.
Reinspection: repeat the moisture check at the required frequency for the remainder of the curing duration.

Recorded Temperature Falls Outside the Specified Limits

Probable cause: insufficient insulation, a failed heater, an unprotected exposure to direct sun or wind, or an unmonitored overnight temperature drop.
Confirmation: review the temperature log against the specified limits and inspect the insulation, heating, or shading system for damage or failure.
Correction: restore or add the required insulation, heating, or shading; obtain engineer or concrete supplier direction if the exceedance may have affected strength development.
Reinspection: repeat temperature monitoring at the required frequency and coordinate additional strength verification if directed.

Barricade or Restricted-Access Signage Is Missing or Displaced

Probable cause: site traffic, weather, or equipment movement disturbed or removed the barricade or signage.
Confirmation: inspect the curing zone for any unauthorized foot traffic, equipment contact, or load evidence since the last check.
Correction: restore the barricade and signage immediately and inspect the concrete surface for impact or loading damage.
Reinspection: repeat the access-restriction check and the surface inspection for the affected zone.

Strength Verification Fails to Meet the Required Result

Probable cause: a mixture, placement, curing, or temperature deficiency affected strength development, or the test specimen itself was improperly cured or handled.
Confirmation: review the placement, curing, and temperature records for the affected element and confirm the test specimen was cured according to the specified method.
Correction: stop work and obtain engineer or concrete supplier direction before removing forms, waterproofing, backfilling, tightening anchors, or loading the element.
Reinspection: repeat strength verification according to the direction received before authorizing release.

Surface Shows Cracking, Scaling, or Discoloration After Curing

Probable cause: premature drying, freezing, overworking during finishing, or contamination during the curing period.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area and compare the condition to the curing and temperature log for the same period.
Correction: classify the defect and route it to Project 08 for authorized minor repair, or escalate to qualified structural review if the defect exceeds minor-repair scope.
Reinspection: repeat the post-curing surface inspection after any authorized repair is complete.

Cleanup

This project ends with a documented release or continued-protection decision, not a torn-down worksite.

  • Remove curing materials only at the approved time and in the approved sequence for each released placement
  • Store reusable insulated blankets, plastic sheeting, and barricades for use in later curing operations, including Project 16 and Project 17
  • Dispose of dried, torn, or contaminated coverings that cannot be reused
  • Confirm restricted-access zones for any placement remaining in continued protection are still posted and intact
  • Return the Curing and Protection Log and photographs to the project record with the placement records from the applicable earlier project
