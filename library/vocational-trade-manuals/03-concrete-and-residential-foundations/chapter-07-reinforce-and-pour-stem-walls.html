Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install stem-wall reinforcement and approved embedded hardware into the braced forms built in Project 06, then to receive, place, consolidate, finish, protect, and begin curing the stem-wall concrete. The finished work product is a reinforced, placed, consolidated, finished, and protected stem wall with embedded hardware in the approved locations, a completed pre-placement inspection record, and a completed placement and curing record.

You will install vertical and horizontal reinforcement in the approved sequence, maintain required cover, coordinate reinforcement with anchor bolts and other embedded hardware, close the form cleanouts, receive and test the delivered concrete, place it in controlled lifts, consolidate it around bars and embedded items, bring the wall to the required top elevation, recheck anchor bolts while adjustment remains possible, and begin the specified curing and protection procedure.

This project depends directly on the stem-wall forms verified and released in Project 06. No reinforcement or concrete work may begin until those forms have passed inspection.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-037 — Install vertical reinforcement in the required position and orientation.
  • FN-038 — Install horizontal reinforcement in the approved sequence and support it at the required elevations.
  • FN-039 — Maintain specified clear cover from forms, footing surfaces, openings, sleeves, and embedded hardware.
  • FN-040 — Place stem-wall concrete in controlled lifts at a rate compatible with the form design.
  • FN-041 — Consolidate wall concrete around bars, corners, openings, ties, and embedded hardware without over-vibrating or displacing reinforcement.
  • FN-042 — Inspect the finished stem wall for top elevation, hardware position, and protection before the crew leaves the site.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 06 delivered clean, stable, plumb, braced stem-wall forms with verified thickness, height, and cleanouts. This project installs the reinforcement and embedded hardware that give the wall its structural strength and its connection to the framing that will be installed on top of it, then places the concrete that the forms were built to contain.

The reinforcement-and-embed coordination method used here — installing bars first, then anchor bolts and hold-downs on approved templates, then verifying both together before the cleanouts close — is the same discipline used earlier for footing reinforcement in Project 03 and again for slab reinforcement in Project 15.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the wall-reinforcement schedule, laps, dowels, hooks, cover, embedded hardware, concrete mixture, lift plan, placement rate, access, and inspection hold points
  • installing vertical bars, dowels, corner bars, jamb bars, pilaster bars, and horizontal reinforcement in the approved sequence
  • tying intersections and laps, maintaining required cover, and coordinating reinforcement with anchor bolts, hold-downs, straps, sleeves, blockouts, and penetrations
  • installing stem-wall anchor bolts and embedded hardware designated for the top of the wall using approved templates or supports
  • closing cleanouts, completing the pre-placement inspection, receiving and testing the specified concrete, and placing it in controlled lifts
  • consolidating each lift, monitoring the forms and hardware throughout placement, bringing the wall to the required top elevation, and finishing the top surface
  • beginning the specified curing and weather protection and completing the placement record

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • substitute bar size, spacing, laps, or embedded-hardware locations that differ from the approved structural documents
  • place sill-plate anchor bolts intended for a different concrete element into this stem-wall placement, or omit anchor bolts designated for the top of this wall
  • continue concrete placement after observing form deflection, tie failure, leakage, brace movement, or hardware displacement without stopping and correcting the condition first
  • heat, bend, relocate, cut, or field-modify embedded anchors without written authorization
  • strip forms, apply waterproofing, or backfill against the wall before the concrete has reached the required curing condition documented in Project 09

Any reinforcement, embedded-hardware, or concrete-placement condition that fails inspection must be corrected before the placement continues or before curing begins.

Trade Note

Anchor bolts and hold-downs installed at the top of a stem wall remain adjustable only while the concrete is workable. Once the surface begins to stiffen, any correction attempt is more likely to create a void around the hardware than to fix its position. Verify projection, spacing, and plumb while the concrete can still be worked, not after finishing is complete.

Setup Standard

Do not begin concrete placement until the pre-placement inspection in Step 9 (bar size, spacing, laps, cover, projection, templates, and reinforcement stability) has passed and the cleanouts are closed. Any reinforcement or embedded-hardware condition that fails this inspection must be corrected before concrete discharge begins.

Shop Tip

Recheck anchor-bolt spacing, projection, plumb, and thread condition immediately after the wall reaches its top elevation and again after the top surface is finished. Waiting until the concrete has stiffened to make this check removes your only chance to correct a displaced bolt without cutting into hardened concrete.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every reinforced concrete placement in this book, from footings in Project 04 to slabs in Project 16, follows the same sequence: install and verify reinforcement and embedded hardware first, close off the placement area, receive and test the concrete, place it in controlled increments while monitoring the form and hardware continuously, bring the work to the required elevation, finish only to the condition required for the next stage, and begin curing immediately. This project applies that sequence to a vertical wall placement, where consolidation around closely spaced vertical bars and the risk of form deflection under lateral pressure require closer monitoring than a footing or slab placement.

ReinforcementVertical and horizontal bars installed, tied, and supported at required cover
Embedded hardwareAnchor bolts and hold-downs set on approved templates at the wall top
PlacementConcrete placed in controlled lifts and consolidated around all embedded items

Reinforcement Sequence and Hardware Coordination

Install vertical reinforcement first, then horizontal reinforcement, tying each intersection without pulling bars out of their required position. Install anchor bolts and hold-down hardware only after the reinforcement they attach to or pass near is in its final position, using the approved template or support method. Verify both the reinforcement and the embedded hardware together before closing the cleanouts; a cleanout closed before this joint verification removes your last practical opportunity to inspect the assembly before concrete conceals it.

Measurement Cue — Maintain Cover From Every Surface, Not Only the Form Face

Verify clear cover from the form faces, the footing surface, openings, sleeves, and adjacent embedded hardware — not only from the nearest form face. A bar correctly spaced from the form but too close to a sleeve or an anchor bolt still fails the cover requirement at that location.

Critical Safety Cue — Form Pressure and Wall Placement Rate

Concrete placed too quickly into a stem-wall form generates lateral pressure the form and bracing may not be designed to resist. Control the placement rate specified in the lift plan and stop placement immediately if you observe form deflection, tie stress, leakage, or brace movement. Treat any of these observations as a stop-work condition until the form condition is corrected.

Field Safety Cue — Pump Lines, Vibrators, and Crew Coordination

Establish communication signals among the driver, pump operator, placing crew, and spotters before discharge begins. Keep hands and loose clothing clear of the vibrator head during operation, and do not direct a pump line or hose in a way that could strike a worker positioned at the form top.

Code Alert — Anchor-Bolt Spacing and Embedment Requirements

Required anchor-bolt spacing, embedment depth, and edge distance vary by jurisdiction, structural design, and product. Never substitute a spacing or embedment value not shown in the approved embed schedule, and contact the designer or engineer of record if a hold-down location conflicts with reinforcement or a form feature.

D. Preparation — Document Review and Form Verification

  1. Review the wall-reinforcement schedule, laps, dowels, hooks, cover, embedded hardware, concrete mixture, lift plan, placement rate, access, and inspection hold points. Confirm the document set is complete before proceeding to Step 2.
  2. Verify stem-wall forms, cleanouts, braces, ties, footing surfaces, and dowels before reinforcement installation. Confirm the Project 06 form-inspection record has passed before disturbing the forms.

E. Reinforcement Installation

  1. Install vertical bars, dowels, corner bars, jamb bars, pilaster bars, and other required vertical reinforcement. Position each bar at the location and orientation shown in the approved schedule.
  2. Install horizontal reinforcement in the approved sequence and support it at the required elevations. Use chairs, bolsters, or supports set at the required spacing before placing horizontal bars.
  3. Tie intersections and laps without pulling bars out of position. Check bar position immediately after each tie is completed.
  4. Maintain required cover from forms, footing surfaces, openings, sleeves, and embedded hardware. Measure cover at each location identified in the schedule, not only at the form face.

F. Embedded Hardware and Pre-Placement Inspection

  1. Coordinate reinforcement with anchor bolts, hold-downs, straps, sleeves, blockouts, and penetrations. Adjust bar position only within limits authorized by the structural documents.
  2. Install stem-wall anchor bolts and embedded hardware designated for the top of the wall using approved templates or supports. Do not install sill-plate anchor bolts that belong to a different concrete element.
  3. Inspect bar size, spacing, laps, cover, projection, templates, and reinforcement stability. Correct every deficiency found before continuing to Step 10.
  4. Close cleanouts and complete the required pre-placement inspection. Record the inspection result on the Stem-Wall Reinforcement and Placement Record before scheduling concrete delivery.

G. Delivery Review and Testing

  1. Review weather, delivery, pumping, testing, communication, access, lighting, cleanup, and contingency plans. Confirm every plan element is staged before concrete arrives.
  2. Receive and verify the specified concrete before discharge. Resolve any mismatch between the delivery documentation and the specified mixture before discharge begins.
  3. Conduct required sampling and field testing. Use the approved testing personnel and procedure for every required test.

H. Placement and Consolidation

  1. Place concrete in controlled lifts and at a rate compatible with the form design. Do not exceed the placement rate specified in the lift plan.
  2. Deposit concrete close to its final position and avoid free fall, segregation, and reinforcement displacement. Use chutes, pumps, or buggies positioned to minimize drop height.
  3. Consolidate each lift around bars, corners, openings, ties, and embedded hardware. Insert and withdraw the vibrator at the spacing and depth required to consolidate without over-vibration.
  4. Monitor form deflection, tie condition, leakage, brace movement, wall thickness, and hardware position continuously. Assign a spotter to watch the form exterior during placement.
  5. Stop placement and correct any form, reinforcement, hardware, equipment, or concrete problem before continuing. Do not resume placement until the observed problem is corrected.

I. Top Elevation, Hardware Recheck, and Curing Start

  1. Bring the wall to the required top elevation and finish the top surface for sill plates or later work. Verify elevation from the protected benchmark before finishing.
  2. Recheck anchor-bolt spacing, projection, plumb, orientation, and thread condition while adjustment remains possible. Make this check immediately after the wall reaches top elevation and again after top-surface finishing.
  3. Begin the specified curing and weather protection as soon as the concrete condition permits. Install the specified curing method without delay once the surface condition allows it.
  4. Prevent premature loading, impact, vibration, freezing, overheating, contamination, and moisture loss. Barricade the area and post the required restrictions.

J. Recording and Closeout Inspection

  1. Record delivery, testing, placement rate, lift sequence, interruptions, form observations, hardware checks, weather, and curing start. Complete every field on the Stem-Wall Reinforcement and Placement Record.
  2. Inspect the protected wall placement before the crew leaves the site. Confirm curing materials, barricades, and hardware protection are in place before departure.

Common Placement Inspection

Inspect every reinforcement, hardware, and placement item using the same checks regardless of which wall segment it applies to.

  • Reinforcement check: confirm bar size, spacing, laps, hooks, cover, and tie condition match the approved wall-reinforcement schedule.
  • Embedded-hardware check: confirm every anchor bolt and hold-down matches the approved embed schedule for plumb, orientation, elevation, embedment, and projection.
  • Placement check: confirm lift rate, consolidation, and concrete condition remained within the approved lift plan throughout the placement.
  • Elevation and finish check: confirm the wall top matches the required elevation and the finish condition is correct for the next construction stage.
  • Curing and protection check: confirm the specified curing method and barricades are in place before the crew leaves the site.

Complete the Stem-Wall Reinforcement and Placement Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the wall for Project 08.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Reinforcement Cover Fails at a Sleeve or Adjacent Bar

Probable cause: a bar was positioned relative to the form face only, without checking clearance from a nearby sleeve, opening, or adjacent bar.
Confirmation: measure cover at every location identified in the approved schedule, not only at the nearest form face.
Correction: reposition the bar or support within the limits authorized by the structural documents; obtain engineer direction if the schedule does not allow enough room at that location.
Reinspection: repeat the cover check at the corrected location and at all adjacent bars affected by the repositioning.

Anchor Bolt Is Out of Position After the Recheck

Probable cause: the bolt shifted during consolidation, was struck by the vibrator, or was not adequately secured to its template before placement.
Confirmation: re-measure spacing, projection, and plumb against the approved embed schedule while the concrete remains workable.
Correction: adjust the bolt only while the concrete remains workable and without creating a void; do not attempt to heat, bend, or relocate a bolt in concrete that has begun to stiffen without written authorization.
Reinspection: repeat the spacing, projection, and plumb check after adjustment and again after top-surface finishing.

Form Shows Deflection or Leakage During Placement

Probable cause: the placement rate exceeded the rate the form and bracing were designed to resist, or a tie or brace was inadequately tightened.
Confirmation: stop placement immediately and inspect the affected form section, ties, and braces.
Correction: reduce the placement rate, add or tighten braces and ties as needed, and do not resume placement until the form condition is corrected.
Reinspection: monitor the corrected section continuously through the remainder of the placement.

Honeycombing or Voids Appear Near Reinforcement or Hardware

Probable cause: inadequate consolidation around closely spaced bars or embedded hardware, or concrete placed too quickly for the vibrator to reach every location.
Confirmation: this condition is typically identified after form stripping in Project 08; note the location and placement conditions in the placement record for reference during that inspection.
Correction: adjust vibrator insertion spacing, depth, and placement rate for subsequent lifts or placements based on the observed condition.
Reinspection: confirm the corrected consolidation method during the next lift and again during the Project 08 surface inspection.

Wall Top Elevation Is Incorrect After Finishing

Probable cause: the elevation was checked against a convenient nearby reference instead of the protected benchmark, or the top surface was overworked after initial screeding.
Confirmation: re-verify the wall-top elevation from the protected benchmark carried forward from Project 01.
Correction: correct the elevation while the concrete remains workable; if the concrete has stiffened beyond correction, document the deviation and obtain designer or engineer direction before proceeding to sill-plate installation.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation check after correction and record the final verified elevation.

Cleanup

This project ends with a curing, protected wall, not a torn-down worksite.

  • Clean tools, vibrators, and equipment without discharging cement residue into soil, drains, or waterways
  • Remove excess concrete, spillage, and debris from the work area without contaminating adjacent work
  • Confirm curing coverings or curing compound are fully installed and barricades are posted before leaving the site
  • Store unused reinforcement, tie wire, and embedded-hardware stock for the next applicable placement
  • Return the Stem-Wall Reinforcement and Placement Record and photographs to the project record with the Project 06 form-inspection documentation

Field Terms

Wall-Reinforcement Schedule
The approved structural document stating bar size, spacing, laps, hooks, dowels, cover, and embedded-hardware locations for a stem wall.
Embed Schedule
The approved document identifying anchor bolts, hold-downs, straps, sleeves, and plates required in a specific concrete placement.
Lift
A single controlled increment of concrete placed and consolidated before the next increment is placed.
Clear Cover
The minimum required distance between reinforcement and the nearest form face, footing surface, or opening.
Consolidation
The process of working freshly placed concrete to remove entrapped air and voids around reinforcement and embedded items.
Cleanout
An opening at the base of a wall form permitting debris removal and inspection before it is closed for placement.
Hold-Down
Embedded hardware that connects framing above the wall to the concrete to resist uplift or lateral forces.
Wall-Top Elevation
The required finished top-of-wall elevation established from the protected benchmark.
Stop-Work Condition
An observed condition during placement, such as form deflection or hardware displacement, requiring the crew to halt and correct before continuing.
Curing Start
The point at which the specified curing method is applied to freshly placed concrete.
