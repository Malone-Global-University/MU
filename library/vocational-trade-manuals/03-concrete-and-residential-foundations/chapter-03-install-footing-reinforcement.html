Job Overview

This project qualifies you to read a footing reinforcement plan, prepare and place the specified reinforcing steel, support it at the required elevation, tie it correctly, maintain specified concrete cover, and inspect the completed assembly before concrete placement begins. The finished work product is a stable, correctly supported footing-reinforcement assembly installed inside the footing forms verified in Project 02, matched bar-for-bar to the approved reinforcement schedule, and documented on a completed Footing Reinforcement Inspection Record.

You will review the approved reinforcement plan, inspect and prepare the reinforcing steel, establish a controlled cutting and bending area, prepare a bar list, cut and bend only what the schedule authorizes, place chairs and supports, install longitudinal and transverse reinforcement in the correct position, maintain specified cover, tie intersections and laps, coordinate reinforcement around penetrations and embedded items, and inspect and correct the completed assembly.

This project begins only after the footing forms built in Project 02 have passed inspection. No reinforcing steel may be installed until the forms are clean, stable, dimensionally correct, and released for reinforcement work.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-013 — Read the approved rebar layout, including bar marks, sizes, grades, quantities, spacing, laps, hooks, dowels, and cover requirements.
  • FN-014 — Cut rebar to the required length and shape using the specified equipment and method.
  • FN-015 — Tie rebar intersections using the specified tie type and frequency without displacing bars from their designed position.
  • FN-016 — Maintain specified concrete cover from soil, forms, penetrations, and adjacent bars.
  • FN-017 — Install chairs, bolsters, or other supports at the required spacing to hold reinforcement at the specified elevation.
  • FN-018 — Inspect the completed reinforcement placement against the approved schedule before concrete placement.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 01 verified the layout and elevations, and Project 02 built the footing forms that define the footing's width, depth, and location. This project installs the reinforcing steel that gives the footing its designed structural capacity inside those forms. Project 04 then places, consolidates, and finishes the concrete around the reinforcement installed here — the bar position, cover, and support established in this project cannot be corrected once concrete placement begins.

The cover-maintenance and support discipline taught here — chairs at the required spacing, ties that hold position without pulling bars out of place, and verified clear distance from forms and soil — is the same discipline applied to stem-wall reinforcement in Project 07 and to slab reinforcement in Project 15.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved reinforcement plan, bar marks, sizes, grades, quantities, spacing, laps, hooks, dowels, cover, supports, and inspection hold points
  • receiving, inspecting, cutting, bending, and labeling reinforcing steel according to the approved schedule
  • placing chairs, bolsters, or blocks and installing longitudinal, transverse, dowel, corner, and step reinforcement
  • maintaining specified clear cover and tying intersections, laps, hooks, and splices exactly as shown in the controlling documents
  • coordinating reinforcement around sleeves, blockouts, pipes, and embedded items without unauthorized field-cutting
  • inspecting, correcting, and documenting the completed reinforcement assembly before concrete placement

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • substitute a different bar size, grade, spacing, lap length, or splice method than the approved structural schedule specifies
  • field-cut, field-bend, heat, or reposition a bar or dowel beyond the schedule's authorized locations without written authorization
  • begin reinforcement installation before the footing forms have passed the Project 02 inspection
  • begin concrete placement before this project's inspection and release steps are complete

Any bar size, spacing, cover, lap, hook, or support condition that fails inspection must be corrected before release for concrete placement.

Trade Note

Every bar installed in this project must be traceable to a specific line on the approved reinforcement schedule. Labeling prepared bars by footing run, location, length, and shape before installation — rather than sorting bars by eye at the form — is the single most reliable way to prevent an incorrect bar mark from being tied into the footing.

Setup Standard

Do not begin reinforcement installation until the footing forms have passed the Project 02 form inspection and the bearing surface remains clean, stable, and within tolerance. Shop Tip

Cut and bend bars in a controlled staging area away from the form edges, then carry labeled, finished bars to the footing for placement. Cutting or bending bars directly over the forms risks dropping cutoffs, sparks, or tools into the footing excavation.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every reinforcement installation in this book, from this footing to the stem walls in Project 07 and the slab in Project 15, follows the same sequence: review the approved schedule, inspect and prepare the steel, establish supports, place bars in the correct position, maintain cover, tie intersections without displacing bars, and inspect the completed assembly before it is concealed by concrete. Learning that sequence here, on the simplest reinforcement configuration in the book, means every later reinforcement task starts from a method you already trust.

Bar Identification and Cover

Every bar placed in this footing must correspond to a specific bar mark on the approved reinforcement schedule. Every placed bar must maintain the specified clear cover from soil, forms, penetrations, and adjacent bars for its entire length. Do not substitute a bar of a different size or grade because it is available, and do not reduce cover to make a bar fit; either condition is a schedule deviation requiring correction before concrete placement.

Measurement Cue — Verify Cover at Every Location, Not Just at Midspan

Check clear cover at corners, laps, dowel locations, and anywhere a bar changes direction, not only along a straight run. Cover that is correct at midspan can still fail at a corner, a chair location, or a point where a bar has shifted during tying. Measure cover with a cover gauge or ruler at each location identified in Step 20.

Critical Safety Cue — Stored Energy in Cut and Bent Bar

Reinforcing bar under tension from cutting or bending can release stored energy suddenly. Secure bar stock before cutting, keep hands and face clear of the cut line, and never stand in the plane of a bar being bent. Treat any bar that springs or whips during cutting or bending as a signal to stop and re-secure the work before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Sharp Ends and Form-Edge Hazards

Cap or bend over exposed vertical bar ends and dowels that project into a walking or working area. Keep the cutting and bending area clear of the form edges identified in Project 02, and remove cutoffs and scrap immediately so they do not become a trip or puncture hazard.

Code Alert — Reinforcement Inspection Hold Points

Many jurisdictions require a reinforcement inspection before concrete placement begins, separate from the formwork inspection completed in Project 02. Confirm the required inspection hold points, notice period, and documentation format with the local jurisdiction before scheduling concrete delivery in Project 04.

E. Preparation — Document Review and Material Inspection

  1. Review the approved reinforcement plan, bar marks, sizes, grades, quantities, spacing, laps, hooks, dowels, cover, supports, and inspection hold points. Confirm the document set is complete before proceeding to Step 2.
  2. Confirm that footing forms and bearing surfaces remain clean, stable, and within tolerance. Obtain written confirmation from the Project 02 form inspection before placing any reinforcing steel.
  3. Receive and inspect reinforcing steel for correct identification, excessive rust scale, oil, mud, paint, damage, and unauthorized bending. Reject and document any bar that fails inspection.

F. Cutting and Bending

  1. Establish a controlled cutting and bending area away from form edges and pedestrian routes. Confirm the area is clear of trip and puncture hazards before starting equipment.
  2. Prepare a bar list organized by footing run, location, length, shape, and installation sequence. Use the bar list to confirm every cut bar has a designated installation location.
  3. Measure and mark each bar using the approved reference method. Mark cut lines clearly before engaging the cutting tool.
  4. Cut bars with the specified equipment while controlling sparks, sharp ends, and stored energy. Secure the bar before every cut.
  5. Bend only bars and locations permitted by the approved schedule using the required bending method. Do not bend a bar mark the schedule shows as straight.
  6. Label prepared bars so each piece can be matched to the reinforcement plan. Mark each bar with its bar mark and installation location before moving it to the footing.

G. Support and Placement

  1. Place approved chairs, bolsters, blocks, or other supports at the required spacing. Verify spacing against the approved schedule before placing bars on the supports.
  2. Install longitudinal bars in the required position and orientation. Confirm each bar's position against the bar list before tying.
  3. Install transverse bars, cross bars, dowels, corner bars, step bars, and intersection reinforcement. Match each installed bar to its designated location on the reinforcement plan.
  4. Maintain specified clear cover from soil, forms, penetrations, and adjacent bars. Measure cover at each location before tying it in place.

H. Tying, Splicing, and Coordination

  1. Tie intersections using the specified tie type and frequency without pulling bars out of position. Verify bar position immediately after each tie is completed.
  2. Install required laps, hooks, mechanical splices, or couplers exactly as shown in the controlling documents. Measure lap length and hook dimension against the schedule before final tying.
  3. Support vertical dowels so they remain plumb, aligned, and stable during concrete placement. Brace or tie dowels to prevent movement during the Project 04 pour.
  4. Coordinate reinforcement around sleeves, blockouts, pipes, and embedded items without field-cutting bars unless authorized. Contact the engineer or designer before cutting any bar to clear an unanticipated obstruction.

I. Inspection and Correction

  1. Walk the reinforcement path and identify bars that could shift under workers, hose movement, or concrete flow. Note every bar requiring an additional support or tie point.
  2. Add approved supports or tie points where needed to maintain the designed position. Re-verify position after each added support.
  3. Measure bar spacing, cover, lap locations, dowel projection, and clearances. Record every measurement on the Footing Reinforcement Inspection Record.
  4. Remove loose tie wire, cutoffs, mud, standing water, and debris from the forms. Confirm the forms are clean before final inspection.
  5. Correct all deficiencies and complete the reinforcement inspection before concrete placement. Sign and date the release section of the Footing Reinforcement Inspection Record.

Common Reinforcement Inspection

Inspect every reinforcement item using the same checks regardless of which bar, run, or location it applies to.

  • Identification check: confirm each installed bar matches its bar mark, size, and grade on the approved reinforcement schedule.
  • Spacing check: confirm longitudinal, transverse, and dowel spacing matches the schedule within stated tolerance.
  • Cover check: confirm clear cover from soil, forms, penetrations, and adjacent bars meets or exceeds the specified minimum at every measured location.
  • Lap and splice check: confirm lap lengths, hook dimensions, and mechanical splices match the approved schedule.
  • Support check: confirm chairs, bolsters, or blocks are installed at the required spacing and hold reinforcement at the specified elevation without displacement.
  • Cleanliness check: confirm the forms are free of loose tie wire, cutoffs, mud, standing water, and debris.

Complete the Footing Reinforcement Inspection Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the work for Project 04.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Installed Bar Does Not Match the Approved Schedule

Probable cause: bars were sorted by eye rather than matched to their bar-mark label, or a substituted bar was used because the specified bar mark was not available on site.
Confirmation: compare the installed bar's size, grade, and mark against the approved reinforcement schedule and the bar list prepared in Step 5.
Correction: remove the incorrect bar and install the bar mark specified by the schedule; do not conceal a substitution without written engineer authorization.
Reinspection: repeat the identification check for the corrected location and any adjacent bars handled during the correction.

Clear Cover Is Insufficient at a Corner or Splice

Probable cause: a chair was omitted or spaced too far apart, or a bar shifted toward the form face during tying or splicing.
Confirmation: measure clear cover at the affected location with a cover gauge or ruler and compare it to the specified minimum.
Correction: add or reposition chairs to restore the bar to the specified elevation and distance from the form face; re-tie any intersection that shifted during the correction.
Reinspection: repeat the cover check at the corrected location and at every chair position within the same footing run.

Bar Spacing Does Not Match the Schedule

Probable cause: bars were placed by eye without measuring from a defined reference, or ties were completed before spacing was verified.
Confirmation: measure bar-to-bar spacing along the affected run and compare it to the approved schedule.
Correction: loosen the affected ties, reposition the bars to the specified spacing, and re-tie.
Reinspection: repeat the spacing check for the full length of the affected run.

A Dowel Is Out of Plumb or Unstable

Probable cause: the dowel was not braced or tied adequately before concrete placement, or it was disturbed by foot traffic or material handling.
Confirmation: check the dowel for plumb using a level and check its tie or brace connection to the surrounding reinforcement.
Correction: reposition the dowel to plumb and install the required brace or tie point to hold it stable.
Reinspection: repeat the dowel plumb and stability check immediately before concrete placement begins.

Reinforcement Conflicts With a Sleeve, Pipe, or Embedded Item

Probable cause: the reinforcement layout did not anticipate the exact location of a penetration or embedded item, or the item was installed without checking bar positions first.
Confirmation: compare the reinforcement plan and the embedded-item locations to identify the exact conflict.
Correction: reroute the reinforcement around the obstruction within the schedule's authorized tolerance, or contact the engineer or designer for direction before field-cutting any bar.
Reinspection: repeat the identification, spacing, and cover checks for the rerouted reinforcement.

Loose Tie Wire or Debris Remains in the Forms

Probable cause: cutoffs, tie-wire scraps, or tools were not cleared from the forms during or after reinforcement installation.
Confirmation: visually inspect the full length of the forms and the bottom of the bearing surface.
Correction: remove all loose material without disturbing the placed reinforcement.
Reinspection: repeat the cleanliness check immediately before completing the reinforcement inspection record.

Cleanup

This project ends with a released reinforcement assembly and a clean form, not a torn-down worksite.

  • Remove all cutoffs, tie-wire scraps, and packaging from the cutting and bending area
  • Store unused reinforcing steel, chairs, and tie wire away from the forms and protected from weather and contamination
  • Confirm every chair, tie, and dowel brace is secure and undisturbed before leaving the site
  • Clean and store cutting and bending tools according to their manufacturer instructions
  • Return the Footing Reinforcement Inspection Record and photographs to the project record with the Project 02 form-inspection documentation

Field Terms

Bar Mark
The identifying label on the approved reinforcement schedule tying a specific bar shape, size, and length to its installed location.
Clear Cover
The specified minimum distance between the outside surface of reinforcing steel and the nearest face of soil, form, or adjacent surface.
Chair or Bolster
A manufactured support that holds reinforcing steel at the specified height above the bearing surface.
Lap Splice
The specified overlap length between two bars joined by running them parallel and tying them together.
Dowel
A bar embedded in the footing and projecting upward to connect footing reinforcement to a later concrete placement.
Longitudinal Bar
Reinforcing steel running the length of a footing run.
Transverse Bar
Reinforcing steel running across a footing run, perpendicular to the longitudinal bars.
Mechanical Splice or Coupler
A manufactured device joining two bars end-to-end as an alternative to a lap splice.
Footing Reinforcement Inspection Record
The document recording bar sizes, spacing, cover, laps, dowel projection, corrections, and release.
Release
The formal, signed authorization that reinforcement installation is complete and concrete placement may begin.
