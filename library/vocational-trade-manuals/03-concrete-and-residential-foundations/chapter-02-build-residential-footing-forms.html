Job Overview

This project qualifies you to build, align, stake, brace, and inspect residential strip-footing forms so that the footings match the approved foundation dimensions and can safely resist the pressure of concrete placement. The finished work product is a set of stable, clean, dimensionally correct footing forms — established at the correct width, location, and top elevation — along with a completed formwork inspection record.

You will review the approved footing plan and specification, confirm the excavation and bearing surface are ready, select and inspect form materials, lay out footing edges and elevation references from the controls verified in Project 01, build and stake the first form line, set the opposing form line to the specified width, form corners and steps, brace the assembly against placement pressure, and inspect and record the completed forms before reinforcement begins.

This project depends entirely on the horizontal and vertical controls verified and protected in Project 01. No footing form may be built, staked, or braced from an unverified reference point. The formwork built here becomes the mold for every footing dimension checked again in Project 04, and it must remain stable and undisturbed through reinforcement installation in Project 03 and concrete placement in Project 04.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-007 — Select form lumber, stakes, kickers, spreaders, cleats, fasteners, release agent, and accessories that meet the project specification.
  • FN-008 — Lay out footing forms from the verified horizontal and vertical controls.
  • FN-009 — Stake footing forms at the specified spacing and depth without disturbing the bearing surface.
  • FN-010 — Brace footing forms to resist placement pressure and worker contact.
  • FN-011 — Verify footing width, location, and elevation against the approved plan.
  • FN-012 — Inspect form alignment, stability, and cleanliness and complete the formwork inspection record.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 01 delivered a verified foundation layout with protected horizontal and vertical controls. This project turns that verified layout into a physical mold: the footing forms built here fix the exact width, location, and top elevation of every strip footing on the job. Every reinforcement placement in Project 03 and every concrete pour in Project 04 depends on the forms built and inspected here holding their position and dimensions without movement.

The staking, bracing, and inspection discipline used in this chapter — establishing a form line from a verified control, checking it before fastening the opposing line, and bracing before placement — is the same discipline applied again at a larger scale in Project 06 for stem-wall forms and in Project 14 for slab-edge forms.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved footing plan, sections, dimensions, steps, elevations, concrete specification, reinforcement details, and inspection requirements
  • confirming that excavation and bearing-surface inspections have passed before form construction begins
  • selecting and inspecting form lumber, stakes, kickers, spreaders, cleats, fasteners, release agent, and accessories against the project specification
  • laying out, building, staking, and bracing two-sided or one-sided strip-footing forms, or forming against approved earth conditions where the design permits it
  • forming corners, intersections, steps, offsets, pads, and construction-joint locations
  • verifying and inspecting footing width, location, elevation, straightness, and stability, and completing the formwork inspection record

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • revise the approved footing width, depth, step locations, or elevations shown on the foundation plan
  • begin form construction before excavation and bearing-surface inspections have passed
  • install reinforcing steel or place concrete before the formwork inspection record in this project is complete
  • substitute form lumber, stakes, ties, or release agent for a type not permitted by the project specification

Any excavation condition, bearing-surface condition, or form-material defect discovered during this project must be corrected, replaced, or escalated to a qualified professional before form construction continues.

Trade Note

Build and stake the first form line completely, and verify its horizontal and vertical position against the Project 01 controls, before assembling the opposing form line. Setting both form lines before verifying the first line against the controls is the single most common cause of a footing that is dimensionally correct on paper but out of position in the ground.

Shop Tip

Mark the required top-of-form elevation on every stake before fastening the form board to it. Fastening the board first and then hunting for the elevation mark afterward is a common source of an out-of-level footing top.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every form system in this book, from this chapter's footing forms to the stem-wall forms in Project 06 and the slab-edge forms in Project 14, follows the same sequence: confirm the bearing surface is ready, establish the reference line and elevation from a verified control, build and check the first form line before assembling the opposing line, brace for the actual placement pressure, and inspect before concealment. Learning that sequence on footing forms, before wall or slab formwork is introduced, means every later form system starts from a method you already trust.

Width checkDistance between form lines, measured at multiple points along the run
Elevation checkTop-of-form elevation verified from the Project 01 benchmark
Alignment checkStraightness, corner angles, and step geometry against the approved plan
Stability checkStake spacing, brace condition, and resistance to placement pressure

Bearing Surface and Excavation Readiness

Confirm that the excavation and bearing surface have passed the required inspection and remain clean, stable, and undisturbed before driving a single stake. Do not build forms over standing water, loose or disturbed soil, or a bearing surface that has not been released for footing work.

Measurement Cue — Verify the First Form Line Before Setting the Second

Position, stake, and fasten the first form line completely, then measure and verify its position against the footing edge line and its top elevation against the Project 01 benchmark before installing the opposing form line. Do not set both form lines and check width and elevation only afterward; an error in the first line will be built into every measurement that follows.

Critical Safety Cue — Form Pressure and Bracing

Concrete placement exerts substantial lateral pressure on footing forms. Do not rely on stakes alone to resist this pressure. Install the kickers, braces, and external supports required by the form design before concrete placement begins, and do not stand or lean against an unbraced form line during or after placement.

Field Safety Cue — Excavation Edges and Trip Hazards

Identify unstable excavation edges, exposed stakes, and stretched string lines before working in the footing area. Keep the work area clear of loose lumber, cutoffs, and tools that create a trip hazard along the narrow footing trench.

Code Alert — Inspection Hold Points

Local jurisdictions vary in when a footing-form inspection is required before reinforcement or concrete placement may proceed. Confirm the required inspection hold points with the local building department or the project's inspection agency before scheduling reinforcement installation.

E. Preparation — Document Review and Material Selection

  1. Review the approved footing plan, sections, dimensions, steps, elevations, concrete specification, reinforcement details, and inspection requirements. Confirm the document set is complete before proceeding to Step 2.
  2. Confirm that the excavation and bearing surface have passed the required inspection and remain clean, stable, and undisturbed. Obtain written confirmation before disturbing the bearing surface.
  3. Identify whether the footing will be formed on both sides, formed on one side, or placed against approved earth conditions as permitted by the design. Record the selected forming method on the Formwork Inspection Record.
  4. Select form lumber, stakes, kickers, spreaders, cleats, fasteners, release agent, and accessories that meet the project specification. Verify each material against the material takeoff in Section C.
  5. Inspect all form materials for damage, contamination, excessive warp, split ends, and previous concrete buildup. Set aside any material that fails inspection.

F. Layout and Reference Transfer

  1. Establish footing edges, centerlines, corners, steps, and elevation references from the verified controls. Use only the building reference corner, baseline, and benchmark verified and protected in Project 01.
  2. Cut and label form boards by footing run and location. Mark each board with its run and location before moving it to the layout area.
  3. Assemble straight form sections on a controlled surface where practical. Pre-assemble sections away from the excavation edge before final positioning.

G. First Form Line Construction

  1. Position the first form line to the approved horizontal and vertical references. Align the outside face of the board to the footing edge line established in Step 6.
  2. Drive stakes outside the concrete area at the specified spacing and depth without disturbing the bearing surface. Confirm each stake reaches stable bearing before fastening the board to it.
  3. Fasten the form boards to the stakes while maintaining the required top elevation. Check the top-of-form elevation at each stake before driving the final fastener.

H. Opposing Form Line and Corner Construction

  1. Install the opposing form line using specified spreaders or gauges to control footing width. Verify the spreader length against the specified footing width before installation.
  2. Form corners, intersections, steps, offsets, pads, and construction-joint locations according to the approved detail. Check each corner angle with the framing square before fastening.
  3. Install braces, kickers, and external supports required to resist placement pressure and worker contact. Install every brace shown in the form design before proceeding to Step 15.

I. Sealing, Release Agent, and Cleanup

  1. Seal or close gaps that would permit excessive paste loss without trapping debris inside the form. Inspect every joint and seam along the completed run.
  2. Apply form-release material only to approved surfaces and prevent contamination of reinforcement, soil, and construction joints. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for coverage and application method.

J. Verification and Inspection

  1. Verify footing width, location, top elevation, bottom depth, straightness, corner position, and step geometry. Record each measurement against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Check all stakes, braces, fasteners, joints, and form-contact surfaces. Apply a hand-pressure check at each stake and brace location.
  3. Remove loose soil, wood scraps, standing water, and debris from inside the forms. Clear the full length of the footing run before marking reference points.
  4. Mark reference points that must remain visible during reinforcement and concrete placement. Flag or paint each reference point clearly.
  5. Correct every failed dimension or unstable form condition. Re-verify any corrected item before proceeding.
  6. Complete the formwork inspection record before reinforcement installation begins. Sign and date the release section of the record.

Common Formwork Inspection

Inspect every footing form using the same checks regardless of run length, corner count, or step configuration.

  • Width check: confirm the distance between form lines matches the approved plan within tolerance at multiple points along the run.
  • Location check: confirm the form line matches the footing edge line established from the Project 01 controls.
  • Elevation check: confirm the top-of-form elevation matches the elevation transferred from the Project 01 benchmark.
  • Straightness and corner check: confirm no visible bow beyond tolerance and confirm corner angles and step geometry match the approved detail.
  • Stability check: confirm no movement at stakes, braces, or spreaders under a hand-pressure check.
  • Cleanliness check: confirm the form is free of loose soil, wood scraps, standing water, and debris.

Complete the Formwork Inspection Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the work for Project 03.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Footing Width Is Out of Tolerance

Probable cause: the spreader length did not match the specified footing width, or the opposing form line shifted before fasteners were driven.
Confirmation: re-measure the width at multiple points along the run and compare each spreader to the specified width.
Correction: loosen the affected fasteners, reposition the opposing form line to the specified width using a verified spreader, and refasten.
Reinspection: repeat the width check at every point along the corrected section.

Top-of-Form Elevation Does Not Match the Benchmark

Probable cause: the elevation was transferred from an unverified stake, or the form board shifted after fastening.
Confirmation: re-verify the elevation directly from the Project 01 benchmark using the laser level or optical level and rod.
Correction: reset the form board to the correct elevation and refasten to the stake without disturbing the bearing surface.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation check at each stake along the corrected section.

Form Line Shows Movement Under Hand Pressure

Probable cause: insufficient stake spacing, a missing kicker or brace, or a stake that did not reach stable bearing.
Confirmation: apply a hand-pressure check along the full run and identify the specific stake, brace, or spreader that moves.
Correction: add or replace the stake, kicker, or brace required by the form design at the identified location.
Reinspection: repeat the stability check at the corrected location and along the adjacent run.

Corner Angle or Step Geometry Does Not Match the Approved Detail

Probable cause: the corner was assembled before the framing square check, or the step elevation was transferred from an incorrect reference point.
Confirmation: check the corner angle with the framing square and re-verify the step elevation against the approved detail and the Project 01 benchmark.
Correction: loosen and reposition the affected form boards to match the approved corner angle or step geometry, then refasten.
Reinspection: repeat the corner and step check for the corrected location.

Debris or Standing Water Remains Inside the Form

Probable cause: cleanup was performed before the final round of stake driving and fastening, allowing loose soil or wood scraps to re-enter the form.
Confirmation: visually inspect the full length of the footing form interior.
Correction: remove all loose soil, wood scraps, standing water, and debris from inside the form before completing the inspection record.
Reinspection: repeat the cleanliness check immediately before signing the Formwork Inspection Record.

Cleanup

This project ends with released, inspected forms in place, not a torn-down worksite.

  • Confirm every stake, brace, kicker, and spreader is secure and shows no movement under hand pressure
  • Remove damaged form material, cutoffs, and loose fasteners from the work area
  • Store unused form lumber, stakes, and hardware for the next footing run
  • Protect the marked reference points required for reinforcement and concrete placement
  • Return the Formwork Inspection Record and photographs to the project record with the Project 01 layout documentation

Field Terms

Footing Edge Line
The horizontal reference line that defines the outside face of the first form line.
Top-of-Form Elevation
The vertical reference transferred from the benchmark that defines the required top elevation of the form.
First Form Line
The form line positioned and verified first, before the opposing line is assembled.
Opposing Form Line
The second form line, set to the specified footing width from the first form line.
Spreader
A rigid gauge or brace that holds the specified footing width between form lines.
Kicker
An angled brace that resists outward or inward movement of a form line.
Cleat
A short board used to reinforce a corner or joint connection in the form.
Release Agent
A product applied to form-contact surfaces to permit clean form removal after curing.
Formwork Inspection Record
The document recording width, location, elevation, straightness, stability, and cleanliness before reinforcement.
Release
The formal, signed authorization that formwork is complete and reinforcement installation may begin.

Performance Check

With every stake driven, brace installed, and dimension verified, review the finished forms against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the footing width matches the approved plan within tolerance at multiple points, the footing location matches the footing edge line, the top-of-form elevation matches the benchmark within tolerance, the form shows no movement under a hand-pressure check at every stake, brace, and spreader, corner angles and step geometry match the approved detail, and the form interior is free of debris and standing water. Complete the Formwork Inspection Record and resolve any unresolved conflict before releasing the forms for Project 03. If an item fails, identify whether the error occurred in reference transfer, material selection, staking, bracing, or corner construction before recording the correction and re-running the affected check.

Project Closeout

You have completed the second project in the concrete and residential foundations sequence: building residential footing forms. This project required you to select form lumber, stakes, kickers, spreaders, cleats, fasteners, and release agent that meet the project specification (FN-007), lay out the forms from the verified Project 01 controls (FN-008), stake the forms at the specified spacing and depth (FN-009), brace the forms to resist placement pressure (FN-010), verify footing width, location, and elevation (FN-011), and inspect form alignment, stability, and cleanliness while completing the Formwork Inspection Record (FN-012).

You now have stable, clean, dimensionally correct footing forms ready to receive reinforcing steel and embedded items. That completed formwork carries directly into the next project: installing the reinforcing steel that the concrete will be placed around.

Protect the completed forms, their bracing, and every marked reference point. You will rely on these exact forms remaining undisturbed throughout reinforcement installation and concrete placement in the projects that follow.
