Job Overview

This project qualifies you to verify that the horizontal and vertical layout delivered from Book 2 matches the approved foundation documents before any footing form, reinforcement, or concrete work begins. The finished work product is a documented, corrected, and released foundation layout: a completed Foundation Layout Verification Worksheet, a set of protected horizontal and vertical controls, and photographs of every verified control point.

You will assemble the controlling documents, inspect the work area and existing layout controls, identify the reference corner, baseline, and benchmark, measure the principal foundation dimensions and both diagonals, verify footing centerlines and elevation changes, transfer required elevations to protected controls, resolve any conflicts with a qualified professional, and record and photograph the verified layout.

This is the first project in this book. It assumes Book 2 has delivered a prepared and documented building site with an established survey baseline, benchmark, and staked footprint. No formwork, reinforcement, or concrete placement may begin until this project's verification and release steps are complete and recorded.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-001 — Verify the building footprint against the approved foundation plan.
  • FN-002 — Confirm required setbacks from the approved survey or site plan.
  • FN-003 — Check both building diagonals against the approved plan and stated tolerance.
  • FN-004 — Verify footing locations, including centerlines, pads, steps, and changes in elevation.
  • FN-005 — Confirm benchmark and control-point elevations from the protected benchmark.
  • FN-006 — Document foundation-layout readiness and release the work for footing formwork.

Where This Skill Fits

Book 2 delivered a prepared site with an established baseline, benchmark, and staked footprint. This project applies verification discipline to that delivered layout before any concrete-related work begins: every footing form built in Project 02, every reinforcement placement in Project 03, and every concrete pour from Project 04 forward depends on the horizontal and vertical controls confirmed and protected here.

The comparison method used in this chapter — measuring from a single defined corner and confirming diagonals against each other, not only against the plan dimension — is the same discipline used later in this book to confirm slab-form squareness in Project 14 and to compare foundation dimensions during the Chapter 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • assembling and reviewing the approved site plan, foundation plan, structural details, survey information, benchmark record, permit conditions, and prior inspection documentation
  • inspecting the work area and existing layout controls for damage or disturbance
  • identifying the controlling property line, survey monument, baseline, benchmark, and building reference corner
  • measuring principal foundation dimensions, diagonals, footing centerlines, and elevation changes from defined references
  • verifying benchmark and control-point elevations and transferring them to protected controls
  • identifying conflicts and obtaining direction from a surveyor, engineer, inspector, or designer
  • recording, photographing, and releasing a verified foundation layout

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • establish a new property-boundary survey, resolve a boundary dispute, or set a new legal survey monument; these require a licensed surveyor
  • excavate, build formwork, install reinforcement, or place concrete before this project's verification and release steps are complete
  • revise the approved foundation plan, structural details, or setback requirements on their own authority
  • disturb, remove, or relocate a property-line monument, survey control point, or benchmark without documented authorization

Any control point, benchmark, dimension, diagonal, or elevation that fails verification must be corrected, replaced, or escalated to a qualified professional before layout work continues.

Trade Note

Every measurement in this chapter is taken from the defined reference corner, baseline, or benchmark — never from a previously measured or assumed point. Measuring from an unconfirmed intermediate point, rather than from the protected control, is the single most common source of the compounding layout errors diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not begin footing formwork, reinforcement installation, or any excavation beyond what Book 2 has already completed until this project's Foundation Layout Verification Worksheet is complete and the layout has been formally released in Step 19. Any layout work performed before release must be reverified before it is relied upon.

Shop Tip

Photograph every control point immediately after verifying it, before moving to the next point. Waiting until the end of the day to photograph controls risks missing a point that was disturbed by site traffic between verification and documentation.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every layout verification in this book, from this chapter's foundation footprint to the Chapter 20 capstone handoff, follows the same sequence: assemble the controlling documents, confirm the fixed references, measure from those references only, compare against the approved plan and stated tolerance, and record every result before releasing the work. Learning that sequence here, before any concrete work begins, means every later verification task starts from a method you already trust.

Reference Corner, Baseline, and Benchmark

Every principal dimension and diagonal in this verification is measured from the single building reference corner identified in Step 4. Every elevation is verified from the single protected benchmark identified in the same step. Do not substitute a different corner or a different elevation reference partway through the verification; doing so produces measurements that cannot be reconciled against each other or against the approved plan.

Measurement Cue — Verify From the Defined References Every Time

Measure every principal dimension and diagonal from the building reference corner identified in Step 4. Measure every elevation from the protected benchmark identified in the same step. Do not measure a dimension from a previously placed stake or a diagonal from an unconfirmed intermediate corner; doing so allows small errors to compound across the footprint.

Critical Safety Cue — Excavation Edges and Unstable Ground

Do not approach an unprotected excavation edge, disturbed spoil pile, or unstable bearing surface to reach a control point. Identify a safe approach path or obtain assistance to verify a control point located in a hazardous area. Treat any collapsed, undercut, or visibly unstable excavation edge as a stop-work condition until a competent person addresses it.

Field Safety Cue — Site Traffic and Equipment Conflicts

Confirm there is no active equipment operation in the area before working near batter boards, stakes, or string lines. Wear the required high-visibility vest whenever equipment traffic is present, and keep clear of paths used by delivery vehicles, excavators, or material handling equipment.

Code Alert — Setback and Monument Authority

Required setbacks, property-boundary locations, and survey-monument authority vary by jurisdiction and by the approved survey. Never estimate a setback from a fence, an assumed property line, or a prior structure. Verify every setback from the survey monument or baseline identified in the approved documents, and contact the surveyor of record if a monument is missing, disturbed, or in question.

E. Preparation — Document Review and Site Inspection

  1. Assemble the approved site plan, foundation plan, structural details, survey information, benchmark record, permit conditions, and prior inspection documentation. Confirm the document set is complete before proceeding to Step 2.
  2. Confirm that the site is released for foundation work and that required layout controls remain protected and readable. Obtain written confirmation from the Book 2 handoff record before disturbing any control point.
  3. Inspect the work area for standing water, unstable excavation edges, disturbed bearing surfaces, damaged batter boards, missing stakes, and equipment conflicts. Record every condition found before continuing.

F. Establishing References

  1. Identify the controlling property line, survey monument, baseline, benchmark, and building reference corner. Mark the selected building reference corner on the worksheet before any measurement.
  2. Verify the building orientation and required setbacks from the approved survey or site plan. Measure each required setback from the survey monument or baseline identified in Step 4.

G. Dimensional and Diagonal Verification

  1. Measure the principal foundation dimensions from the specified control references. Hook the tape on the building reference corner for every principal dimension.
  2. Measure both building diagonals from the same defined corner references. Record each diagonal measurement immediately after taking it.
  3. Compare measured dimensions and diagonals with the approved plan and stated tolerance. Identify any measurement outside the stated tolerance before continuing.

H. Footing Location and Excavation Verification

  1. Verify footing centerlines, wall faces, pier locations, thickened areas, steps, pads, and changes in elevation. Compare each location to the approved foundation plan.
  2. Confirm that excavation limits provide the required working room without disturbing property or protected site features. Note any excavation limit that conflicts with a property line, easement, or protected feature.

I. Elevation Verification and Control Transfer

  1. Verify footing-bottom, top-of-footing, top-of-wall, slab, and sill elevations from the protected benchmark. Use the calibrated laser level or optical level and rod for every elevation check.
  2. Transfer required elevation references to protected grade stakes, batter boards, form-control stakes, or other approved controls. Mark each transferred elevation clearly and note its location on the worksheet.
  3. Confirm that control points are located outside concrete-placement, form-removal, equipment, and backfill disturbance zones. Relocate any control point found inside a disturbance zone to an approved protected position.

J. Conflict Resolution

  1. Identify conflicts involving dimensions, setbacks, excavation, utilities, soil conditions, or plan revisions. Record each conflict with its measured value and the approved-plan value.
  2. Stop work and obtain surveyor, engineer, inspector, or designer direction for any unresolved conflict. Do not proceed past an unresolved conflict on independent judgment.
  3. Correct authorized layout discrepancies and repeat all affected measurements. Re-verify every dimension, diagonal, setback, and elevation affected by the correction.

K. Recording and Release

  1. Record final dimensions, diagonals, setbacks, footing references, benchmark elevations, and correction history. Complete every field on the Foundation Layout Verification Worksheet.
  2. Photograph the verified controls from fixed reference positions. Photograph the building reference corner, benchmark, and every transferred control point.
  3. Complete the foundation-layout readiness checklist and release the work for Chapter 2 only after all required checks pass. Sign and date the release section of the worksheet.

Common Layout Inspection

Inspect every verification item using the same checks regardless of which dimension, diagonal, setback, or elevation it applies to.

  • Dimensional check: confirm each principal dimension is within the plan's stated tolerance, measured from the building reference corner.
  • Diagonal check: confirm both diagonals match each other and the plan-calculated diagonal within the stated tolerance.
  • Setback check: confirm every required setback matches the approved survey or site plan, measured from the survey monument or baseline.
  • Elevation check: confirm footing-bottom, top-of-footing, top-of-wall, slab, and sill elevations match the approved plan, verified from the protected benchmark.
  • Control-point condition check: confirm every relied-upon control point is undamaged, undisturbed, and located outside disturbance zones.

Complete the Foundation Layout Verification Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the work for Project 02.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Diagonals Do Not Match Each Other

Probable cause: one or more corners were staked out of square, or a diagonal was measured from an unconfirmed intermediate corner instead of the defined building reference corner.
Confirmation: re-measure both diagonals from the confirmed building reference corner and re-check each principal dimension against the approved plan.
Correction: relocate the out-of-square corner stake to the position required by the approved plan and stated tolerance, then re-verify all affected dimensions.
Reinspection: repeat the dimensional check and the diagonal check for the full footprint.

Setback Does Not Match the Approved Survey

Probable cause: the setback was measured from an assumed property line, fence, or prior structure instead of the survey monument or baseline identified in the approved documents.
Confirmation: re-measure the setback from the confirmed survey monument or baseline and compare it to the approved survey or site plan.
Correction: stop work and obtain surveyor or designer direction; do not relocate the footprint on independent judgment.
Reinspection: repeat the setback check after the surveyor or designer issues direction.

An Elevation Does Not Match the Approved Plan

Probable cause: the elevation was read from an unverified or disturbed benchmark, or the instrument was not level during the reading.
Confirmation: re-verify the benchmark elevation and re-check instrument level, then repeat the elevation reading.
Correction: if the benchmark itself is disturbed, obtain surveyor or engineer direction before continuing; if the reading error was instrument-related, retake all elevation readings taken during that setup.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation check for every reading taken during the affected instrument setup.

A Control Point Is Missing or Damaged

Probable cause: equipment traffic, weather, or site activity disturbed or removed a stake, batter board, or flagged point.
Confirmation: inspect the immediate area for a displaced or buried stake and compare its expected location to the layout record from Book 2.
Correction: restore the control point using replacement stakes, batter-board lumber, or flagging, and re-verify its position and elevation against the approved plan before relying on it.
Reinspection: repeat the dimensional, diagonal, or elevation check that depends on the restored control point.

Footing Location Conflicts With Excavation or a Utility

Probable cause: the excavation limits, a utility location, or a soil condition was not accounted for when the footprint was originally staked in Book 2.
Confirmation: compare the verified footing location and excavation limits to the approved foundation plan and any utility locate documentation.
Correction: stop work and obtain engineer or designer direction for any conflict involving dimensions, setbacks, excavation, utilities, soil conditions, or plan revisions.
Reinspection: repeat the footing-location check after the conflict is resolved and any authorized correction is made.

Control Point Falls Inside a Disturbance Zone

Probable cause: the transferred elevation or reference mark was placed on a stake or batter board located within the planned concrete-placement, form-removal, equipment, or backfill disturbance zone.
Confirmation: compare the control-point location to the planned formwork, equipment access, and backfill limits.
Correction: relocate the control point to an approved protected position and re-transfer the elevation or reference mark.
Reinspection: repeat the control-point condition check for the relocated point.

Cleanup

This project ends with a released layout and protected controls, not a torn-down worksite.

  • Confirm every restored or replaced control point is stable, labeled, and protected from foot and equipment traffic
  • Remove damaged stakes, broken batter-board material, and loose flagging from the work area
  • Store replacement materials not used for the next verification cycle
  • Label and protect the benchmark and building reference corner for use in Project 02
  • Return the Foundation Layout Verification Worksheet and photographs to the project record with the Book 2 handoff documentation
