Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to plan, build, protect, inspect, document, and hand off a complete residential foundation system by executing the applicable work from Chapters 1 through 19 as one integrated project rather than as separate, disconnected exercises. The finished work product is a complete residential foundation — verified layout, formed and reinforced footings, placed and cured footing concrete, embedded hardware, formed and reinforced stem walls, placed and cured wall concrete, stripped and repaired concrete surfaces, cured and protected concrete throughout, a waterproofing or damp-proofing system, a tested foundation-drain system, foundation insulation and protection board, controlled and inspected backfill, formed slab controls, a prepared slab base with vapor barrier and reinforcement, a continuously placed and finished slab, documented minor repairs, and installed pressure-treated sill plates — together with the compiled inspection and test record required to hand the foundation to Book 4 framing.

You will assemble every controlling document used across the book, prepare a single integrated safety, formwork, reinforcement, concrete, weather, testing, curing, inspection, access, environmental, and contingency plan, and then execute the numbered sequence below in the order the approved foundation plan and this book's chapters require. Each numbered item in this project corresponds to the completed chapter work it draws on; it does not repeat the full procedure already taught in that chapter, but it does require you to meet every acceptance standard, inspection hold point, and documentation requirement that chapter established.

This is the last project in Book 3. It assumes every applicable chapter from 1 through 19 has been completed to its stated Required Outcome, or will be completed in the sequence below using this project as the controlling work order. No backfill, slab work, or sill-plate installation may proceed ahead of the concrete strength, curing, inspection, and design conditions established in the chapters that govern each stage.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-115 — Integrate the approved layout, structural, reinforcement, concrete-placement, waterproofing, drainage, slab, and sill-plate documents into a single controlling project plan.
  • FN-116 — Build, reinforce, place, consolidate, finish, protect, and cure footing and stem-wall concrete in the sequence and to the standards established in Chapters 2 through 9.
  • FN-117 — Install and test the waterproofing or damp-proofing, drainage, insulation, and protection-board systems to the standards established in Chapters 10 through 12, and backfill only after all release conditions are satisfied.
  • FN-118 — Build slab forms, prepare the slab base, vapor barrier, and reinforcement, and execute the continuous slab placement and finishing operation to the standards established in Chapters 14 through 17.
  • FN-119 — Evaluate, document, and complete only authorized minor concrete repairs, and install sill sealer and pressure-treated sill plates to the standards established in Chapters 18 and 19.
  • FN-120 — Compile the complete foundation-system inspection, test, and as-built record, complete and reinspect the final punch list, and conduct a documented handoff of the foundation to Book 4 framing.

Where This Skill Fits

Every earlier chapter in this book verified, built, reinforced, placed, protected, or finished one component of the residential foundation in isolation. This capstone project is the point at which those components must be executed, sequenced, and documented together as a single system, with the release conditions from one stage controlling whether the next stage may begin. The judgment demonstrated here — recognizing which chapter's acceptance standard governs a given stage, and refusing to proceed past an unmet hold point — is the same judgment a foundation crew leader exercises on an active residential job.

Completing this project releases the foundation for Book 4 residential framing. The sill-plate layout, wall lines, and top-of-wall condition you hand off here become the starting condition for every framing project in that book.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • assembling and cross-referencing the approved site, foundation, structural, reinforcement, slab, drainage, waterproofing, insulation, anchor, and sill-plate documents into one controlling plan
  • executing, in the required sequence, the applicable layout verification, formwork, reinforcement, concrete placement, curing, waterproofing, drainage, insulation, backfill, slab, repair, and sill-plate work established in Chapters 1 through 19
  • confirming every inspection hold point and acceptance standard from those chapters before advancing to the next stage
  • compiling the complete inspection, test, product, and as-built record for the finished foundation
  • completing and reinspecting a final punch list
  • conducting a documented foundation-to-framing handoff

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • omit, reorder for convenience, or merge any stop-work condition, inspection hold point, or curing period established in Chapters 1 through 19
  • backfill, load, or conceal any component before the concrete strength, curing, waterproofing, drainage, and inspection conditions governing that component are satisfied
  • substitute independent judgment for a required surveyor, engineer, inspector, or designer decision on any unresolved conflict carried forward from an earlier chapter
  • treat this capstone as a shortcut that permits skipping a chapter's numbered procedure, acceptance standard, or documentation requirement

Any condition that fails an acceptance standard established in an earlier chapter must be corrected, or escalated to the qualified professional identified in that chapter, before this project's sequence continues.

Sequencing Discipline

Execute the numbered sequence below in order. Do not begin a numbered item that depends on an unmet hold point from an earlier item. Where an item references a Required Outcome from a specific chapter, that Required Outcome — not a summary judgment — is the pass condition for this project's corresponding step.

Measurement Cue — Carry Forward the Same References

Every dimension, elevation, and control point used in this capstone traces back to the building reference corner, baseline, and benchmark established in Chapter 1. Do not establish a new reference corner or benchmark during this project; verify that the original controls remain protected and use them throughout.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop-Work Conditions Carry Forward

Every stop-work condition identified in Chapters 1 through 19 — including structural cracking, major honeycombing, displaced reinforcement, form movement, unsuitable soil, failed testing, and waterproofing damage — applies during this capstone project exactly as written in the governing chapter. Coordinating multiple stages does not reduce or waive any stop-work condition.

Field Safety Cue — Concurrent Trade and Equipment Activity

This capstone project spans excavation, formwork, concrete, waterproofing, drainage, insulation, and backfill activity that may occur close together in schedule. Confirm before each stage that no conflicting equipment operation, delivery, or trade activity is active in the work area, and maintain the task-specific PPE identified in the governing chapter for each stage.

Code Alert — Inspection Sequencing

Local jurisdictions set the required inspection sequence and hold points for footing, wall, waterproofing, drainage, backfill, slab, and framing-handoff inspections. Confirm the required inspection sequence with the authority having jurisdiction before scheduling any concealment or backfill step in this project, and do not conceal work that requires a pending inspection.

E. Planning and Document Integration

  1. Assemble the approved site, foundation, structural, reinforcement, slab, drainage, waterproofing, insulation, anchor, and sill-plate documents. Confirm every document referenced in Chapters 1 through 19 is present and current before proceeding.
  2. Prepare the complete safety, formwork, reinforcement, concrete, weather, testing, curing, inspection, access, environmental, and contingency plans. Cross-reference each chapter's individual plan requirements into this single controlling project plan.
  3. Verify that Book 2 has delivered the required site, excavation, subgrade, access, drainage, and control conditions. Confirm the Book 2 handoff record before disturbing any protected control.

F. Layout and Footing Stage (Chapters 1–5)

  1. Verify the building footprint, setbacks, dimensions, diagonals, footing locations, and elevations. Execute the complete Chapter 1 procedure and confirm the Foundation Layout Verification Worksheet is released.
  2. Build, brace, clean, and inspect footing forms. Execute the complete Chapter 2 procedure and confirm the formwork inspection record passes before reinforcement begins.
  3. Prepare, place, support, tie, and inspect footing reinforcement and footing-stage embedded items. Execute the complete Chapter 3 procedure and, where applicable, the Chapter 5 embedded-hardware procedure for items designated for the footing.
  4. Receive, test, place, consolidate, finish, protect, and begin curing footing concrete. Execute the complete Chapter 4 procedure, including required field sampling and testing.
  5. Verify all footing dimensions, elevations, dowels, and construction-joint conditions. Confirm every Chapter 4 and Chapter 5 acceptance standard passes before advancing to stem-wall formwork.

G. Stem-Wall Stage (Chapters 6–9)

  1. Build, align, plumb, tie, brace, clean, and inspect stem-wall forms. Execute the complete Chapter 6 procedure and confirm footing surfaces meet the required condition first.
  2. Install stem-wall reinforcement, anchor bolts, hold-downs, sleeves, blockouts, and embedded hardware. Execute the complete Chapter 7 reinforcement procedure and the applicable Chapter 5 embedded-hardware procedure for wall-top hardware.
  3. Receive, test, place, consolidate, finish, protect, and begin curing stem-wall concrete. Execute the complete Chapter 7 placement procedure.
  4. Strip forms at the approved time and inspect all exposed concrete. Execute the complete Chapter 8 stripping and inspection procedure.
  5. Document defects and complete only authorized minor repairs. Apply the Chapter 8 Scope Control; escalate any defect exceeding minor-repair limits.
  6. Continue curing and early-age protection until release criteria are met. Execute the complete Chapter 9 procedure and confirm the curing closeout record before moisture-control work begins.

H. Moisture, Drainage, Insulation, and Backfill Stage (Chapters 10–13)

  1. Prepare foundation surfaces and install the specified damp-proofing or waterproofing system. Execute the complete Chapter 10 procedure, applying its Scope Control to distinguish damp-proofing from waterproofing zones.
  2. Install and test the permanent foundation-drain system. Execute the complete Chapter 11 procedure, including the required continuity, flow, or camera test.
  3. Install compatible foundation insulation, drainage mat, termination components, and protection board. Execute the complete Chapter 12 procedure and confirm compatibility with the installed waterproofing system.
  4. Obtain required inspections before concealment. Confirm every jurisdictional inspection hold point for waterproofing, drainage, and insulation is satisfied before backfill begins.
  5. Backfill in balanced controlled lifts while protecting the wall, drainage, waterproofing, insulation, utilities, and required grade. Execute the complete Chapter 13 procedure only after confirming every Chapter 13 release condition in writing.

I. Slab Construction Stage (Chapters 14–17)

  1. Build and verify slab forms and elevation controls. Execute the complete Chapter 14 procedure.
  2. Prepare and compact the slab base. Execute the applicable Chapter 15 base-preparation procedure.
  3. Install and inspect the vapor barrier, reinforcement, joints, sleeves, penetrations, and embedded systems. Execute the complete Chapter 15 procedure and confirm the pre-pour inspection record.
  4. Plan and execute the continuous slab placement, screeding, floating, edging, jointing, finishing, curing, and protection operation. Execute the complete Chapter 16 and Chapter 17 procedures as one uninterrupted operation, per the Chapter 16–17 Continuity Control.

J. Final Repair, Sill-Plate, and Handoff Stage (Chapters 18–20)

  1. Inspect the foundation and slab for dimensions, elevations, alignment, concrete condition, moisture control, drainage, backfill, and defects. Compare current conditions against every governing chapter's acceptance standards.
  2. Complete authorized minor repairs and document all unresolved items. Apply the Chapter 18 Scope Control and escalate any defect outside minor-repair limits.
  3. Prepare, drill, install, align, and fasten sill sealer and pressure-treated sill plates. Execute the complete Chapter 19 procedure.
  4. Mark framing reference lines and verify the foundation is ready for Book 4. Confirm wall lines, splice locations, and framing offsets required by Book 4 are marked and legible.
  5. Compile survey records, dimensions, reinforcement inspections, concrete delivery and test records, curing logs, product records, concealed-work photographs, repair records, drainage tests, compaction results, inspection approvals, and as-built information. Cross-reference every record against the chapter that produced it.
  6. Prepare and complete the final punch list. Consolidate every open item recorded across Chapters 1 through 19 and this project's own inspections into one list.
  7. Reinspect every corrected item. Confirm each punch-list item meets its governing chapter's acceptance standard before closing it.
  8. Conduct the formal foundation-to-framing handoff with continuing protection, maintenance, and restriction requirements. Sign and date the handoff record, and state any continuing protection or access restriction that applies until Book 4 work begins.

Common Capstone Inspection

Inspect every stage using the acceptance standard established by the chapter that governs it; do not substitute a general visual check for a chapter-specific standard.

  • Layout and footing check: confirm Chapter 1 through 5 acceptance standards, using the released Foundation Layout Verification Worksheet as the controlling record.
  • Stem-wall check: confirm Chapter 6 through 9 acceptance standards, including documented minor repairs and completed curing closeout.
  • Moisture and drainage check: confirm Chapter 10 and 11 acceptance standards, including passed continuity or flow testing.
  • Insulation and backfill check: confirm Chapter 12 and 13 acceptance standards, including written confirmation of all Chapter 13 release conditions before backfill.
  • Slab check: confirm Chapter 14 through 17 acceptance standards, including uninterrupted transition from placement to finishing.
  • Repair and sill-plate check: confirm Chapter 18 and 19 acceptance standards, including the Chapter 18 Scope Control.
  • Final documentation check: confirm the compiled record is complete, cross-referenced, and supports the signed handoff.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Later Stage Was Started Before an Earlier Hold Point Passed

Probable cause: schedule pressure led the crew to begin backfill, slab work, or sill-plate installation before the governing chapter's concrete strength, curing, inspection, or test requirement was confirmed.
Confirmation: compare the date and result of the required hold-point inspection or test against the date the later stage began.
Correction: stop the later stage, obtain the required professional's evaluation of any concealed or loaded condition, and do not resume until the original hold point is confirmed satisfied or an authorized remedial plan is issued.
Reinspection: repeat the acceptance checks for both the skipped hold point and every later stage affected by it.

Controlling Documents Conflict Between Chapters

Probable cause: a plan revision issued after an earlier chapter's work was completed was not cross-referenced into this capstone's controlling project plan.
Confirmation: compare the revision date and content of each conflicting document against the version used in the earlier chapter's completed work.
Correction: obtain designer or engineer direction on which document governs and whether completed work must be modified.
Reinspection: repeat the acceptance check for any stage affected by the resolved conflict.

Compiled Documentation Has Gaps

Probable cause: a required photograph, test result, or inspection record from an earlier chapter was not retained or was not cross-referenced when Step 28 was performed.
Confirmation: compare the compiled record against the documentation requirement stated in each governing chapter.
Correction: where the underlying work remains accessible or otherwise verifiable, obtain the missing record or an equivalent verification from the required professional; where concealed work cannot be verified, obtain engineer or inspector direction on the appropriate remedial documentation or investigation.
Reinspection: repeat the final documentation check once the gap is resolved.

Final Punch List Item Fails Reinspection

Probable cause: the correction made did not fully meet the acceptance standard of the chapter that governs the item.
Confirmation: re-measure or re-test the item against the specific acceptance standard cited on the punch list.
Correction: return the item to the responsible trade or professional for correction using the method specified by the governing chapter.
Reinspection: repeat the reinspection for that item before including it in the signed handoff.
