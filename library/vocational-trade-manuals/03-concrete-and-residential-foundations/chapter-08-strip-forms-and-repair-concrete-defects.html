Job Overview

This project qualifies you to remove stem-wall forms at the approved time, inspect the exposed concrete for surface and structural defects, repair only the defects that fall within your authorized scope, and prepare the wall surface for continued curing and the moisture-control work that follows in Project 10. The finished work product is a stripped, inspected, and documented stem wall: recovered form materials sorted for reuse, a completed Form-Strip and Defect Inspection Record, authorized minor repairs completed and curing, and photographs of every defect and repair before concealment.

You will confirm that the concrete has reached the required condition for form removal, strip forms in the approved sequence without shocking the concrete, clean and sort the recovered form materials, inspect the full concrete surface for honeycombing, voids, tie holes, cracks, and other defects, classify every defect, escalate anything beyond minor-repair scope, complete only the authorized repairs, and release the wall for continued curing.

This project depends directly on Project 07, which placed, consolidated, and began curing the stem-wall concrete. It assumes the wall has reached the concrete condition and time required by the project specification before stripping begins. No waterproofing, drainage, insulation, or backfill work may begin until this project's inspection and repair steps are complete and recorded.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-043 — Remove stem-wall forms safely, in the approved sequence, without shocking the concrete or endangering the crew.
  • FN-044 — Clean recovered form materials and sort them by reusable, damaged, and disposal condition.
  • FN-045 — Inspect exposed concrete surfaces for honeycombing, voids, tie holes, cracks, and other defects.
  • FN-046 — Identify honeycombing and other defects and classify each as cosmetic, minor repairable, or requiring qualified structural review.
  • FN-047 — Patch and cure authorized minor defects using a compatible repair material within manufacturer limits.
  • FN-048 — Prepare foundation surfaces for waterproofing by removing fins, sealing tie holes, and reinspecting repaired areas.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 07 placed, consolidated, finished, and began curing the stem-wall concrete while it was still contained by the forms built in Project 06. This project is the first time that concrete becomes visible and inspectable across its full surface. Every defect classification made here controls what happens next: cosmetic conditions are recorded and left alone, minor conditions are repaired under this project's scope, and anything beyond that scope stops the job until a qualified professional reviews it.

The defect-classification discipline used in this chapter — sound, measure, classify, then either repair or escalate — is the same discipline used again in Project 18, which repairs cracks, spalls, and minor foundation damage discovered later in the project. The surface you prepare here is the same surface Project 10 waterproofs or damp-proofs; a fin, void, or unfilled tie hole left behind here becomes a coating or membrane defect there.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming the concrete has reached the required condition and that authorization to strip has been issued
  • removing stem-wall forms, ties, braces, walers, and strongbacks in the approved sequence without shocking the concrete
  • maintaining any shores, braces, or supports that must remain in place after form removal
  • cleaning and sorting recovered form materials into reusable, damaged, and disposal categories
  • inspecting the full exposed concrete surface for honeycombing, voids, fins, tie holes, bugholes, cold joints, cracks, misalignment, spalls, exposed reinforcement, and dimensional problems
  • classifying each defect as cosmetic, minor repairable, or requiring qualified structural review
  • preparing, applying, curing, and inspecting authorized minor repairs
  • removing fins and sealing tie holes to prepare the surface for the next system

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • strip forms before the concrete has reached the required condition or before authorization has been issued
  • repair deep honeycombing, exposed or corroded reinforcement, significant cracks, displaced walls, major voids, or any condition suggesting structural movement, without qualified structural review
  • select a repair material or method outside the manufacturer's stated limits for the substrate, exposure, or thickness involved
  • conceal an unresolved defect by patching over it before it has been classified and, where required, escalated

Any defect that exceeds the authorized minor-repair scope must be marked, reported, and escalated to a qualified professional before the wall is released for continued curing.

Trade Note

Form stripping and defect repair are two separate qualifications performed in the same operation. Stripping tells you nothing about repair scope until the full surface has been inspected and every defect has been classified. Do not begin patching a defect before the full-surface inspection required in Step 9 is complete; an early patch can hide a related defect nearby that changes the classification.

Setup Standard

Do not strip any stem-wall form until the concrete has reached the condition and time required by the project specification and written authorization to strip has been issued. Do not begin any repair until the full-surface inspection in Step 9 is complete and every defect has been classified. Do not backfill, waterproof, or damp-proof any repaired area until it has passed the reinspection required in Step 19.

Shop Tip

Mark every defect with a visible crayon or paint circle and a number as soon as you find it, before classifying or repairing anything. Working from a fully marked surface prevents a defect from being patched over before it has been recorded, measured, and classified against the escalation criteria.

Stripping Sequence and Exclusion Zones

Every panel, waler, and strongback in this project is removed in the sequence specified by the form design, and no worker stands inside the exclusion zone beneath or around a form component being loosened or lowered. Do not remove a support that the form design requires to remain in place after the surrounding panels are stripped; doing so can allow an unsupported form section to fall or the concrete to be damaged before it has reached the condition assumed by the stripping plan.

Critical Safety Cue — Exclusion Zones During Stripping

Establish an exclusion zone beneath and around every form panel, waler, and strongback before loosening it. Do not allow a worker to stand under a panel, waler, or strongback while it is being freed, lowered, or lifted clear. Treat any panel that binds, resists normal prying force, or begins to move unexpectedly as a stop-work condition until the crew re-establishes control.

Safety Cue — Stripping Tools and Sharp Hardware

Wear safety glasses and gloves when using stripping bars, hammers, and tie-removal tools. Handle removed nails, ties, and fasteners as sharp debris and place them directly into a designated container rather than setting them down loose on the work surface.

Code Alert — Minimum Time and Strength Before Stripping

Minimum concrete age, strength, or test result required before form removal varies by jurisdiction, structural design, and the applicable code. Never strip forms based on elapsed time alone unless that is the specific method authorized by the project's concrete mixture and placement plan. Confirm the required condition and authorization before removing any form component.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Repair Material Limits

Apply the selected patching mortar or repair concrete only within the manufacturer's stated substrate condition, thickness, temperature, and cure requirements. Do not thin, extend, or substitute a different product to make it match field conditions; select a product rated for the actual repair depth and exposure instead.

E. Preparation — Authorization and Exclusion Zones

  1. Verify that the concrete has reached the required condition for form removal and that authorization to strip has been issued. Confirm the strength, age, or test result against the Project 07 concrete-mixture and placement plan before proceeding to Step 2.
  2. Review the stripping sequence, remaining support requirements, access controls, lifting plan, and fall or crush hazards. Identify which supports must remain in place after the surrounding panels are removed.
  3. Inspect the area below and around the forms and establish exclusion zones. Confirm no worker will stand beneath a panel, waler, or strongback while it is being freed or lowered.

F. Stripping and Material Recovery

  1. Remove loose hardware, braces, walers, strongbacks, and panels in the approved sequence without shocking the concrete. Use the specified stripping bar and tie-removal tool for each component.
  2. Maintain any shores, braces, ties, or supports required to remain after form removal. Do not remove a support the form design requires to remain in place.
  3. Lower and stack panels and components using controlled handling methods. Stage recovered materials clear of the exclusion zone before continuing.
  4. Remove or treat form ties according to the waterproofing and structural detail. Break back, cut, or plug ties using the specified method for the tie system in use.
  5. Clean form materials before concrete residue hardens and separate reusable, damaged, and disposal components. Sort materials into three labeled areas before storage.

G. Full-Surface Defect Inspection

  1. Inspect the full concrete surface for honeycombing, voids, fins, tie holes, bugholes, cold joints, cracks, misalignment, spalls, exposed reinforcement, and dimensional problems. Work the full wall surface systematically so no area is skipped.
  2. Mark each defect and classify it as cosmetic, minor repairable, or requiring qualified structural review. Number and mark every defect before moving to the next inspection area.

H. Escalation of Structural-Review Defects

  1. Stop work and obtain direction for deep honeycombing, exposed reinforcement, significant cracks, displaced walls, major voids, or other structural concerns. Do not repair or conceal a structural-review defect on independent judgment.

I. Minor Repair Preparation and Application

  1. Remove only loose or unsound material from authorized minor repair areas. Use the wire brush or chipping hammer to expose sound concrete without enlarging the repair beyond what is required.
  2. Prepare repair surfaces to the profile, cleanliness, moisture, and temperature required by the selected repair product. Follow the manufacturer's stated surface-preparation procedure exactly.
  3. Prepare and apply the approved repair material within manufacturer limits. Mix, place, and time the repair according to the manufacturer's instructions.
  4. Consolidate, shape, and finish the repair to the required surface condition. Match the surrounding surface profile required for the next system.
  5. Cure and protect the repair according to the product instructions. Maintain the specified curing method and duration before disturbing the repair.

J. Surface Preparation for the Next System

  1. Remove sharp fins, projections, and surface contamination that would damage waterproofing or insulation. Grind or chip fins flush with the surrounding surface.
  2. Fill approved tie holes and seal penetrations using compatible materials. Confirm compatibility with the waterproofing system planned for Project 10 before selecting a plug or sealant.

K. Reinspection, Recording, and Release

  1. Reinspect repaired areas for bond, profile, cracks, voids, and compatibility with the next system. Sound each repair and compare its profile to the surrounding surface.
  2. Record defects, classifications, repair products, batch information, locations, and approvals. Complete every field on the Form-Strip and Defect Inspection Record.
  3. Release the wall for continued curing and Chapter 10 only after required repairs pass inspection. Sign and date the release section of the record.

Common Defect Inspection

Inspect every defect using the same checks regardless of its type or location on the wall.

  • Sounding check: tap the surrounding concrete with a sounding hammer and listen for a hollow or dull response indicating delamination or a subsurface void.
  • Measurement check: measure the depth, width, and length of every defect before classifying it.
  • Reinforcement check: confirm whether reinforcing steel is exposed, displaced, or corroded at any defect location.
  • Classification check: confirm every defect is marked as cosmetic, minor repairable, or structural-review, with structural-review defects escalated before repair.
  • Repair-bond check: confirm every completed repair is sound, flush with the surrounding profile, and free of cracks, voids, and debonding.

Complete the Form-Strip and Defect Inspection Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the wall for Project 09 continued curing and Project 10 waterproofing.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Panel Resists Normal Prying Force During Stripping

Probable cause: a tie, fastener, or brace was missed during the removal sequence, or the concrete has not reached the condition assumed by the stripping plan.
Confirmation: stop applying force, inspect the panel and surrounding hardware for a missed tie or fastener, and re-verify the concrete condition against the required stripping authorization.
Correction: remove any missed hardware using the approved tool, or delay stripping until the concrete reaches the required condition.
Reinspection: repeat the exclusion-zone check and resume stripping only after the obstruction is cleared or the required condition is confirmed.

Honeycombing Found at a Tie Location or Cold Joint

Probable cause: incomplete consolidation around the tie or at a placement interruption during the Project 07 concrete pour.
Confirmation: sound the surrounding area, measure the depth of the honeycombing, and check for exposed or displaced reinforcement.
Correction: classify shallow, sound-adjacent honeycombing as minor repairable and repair it per Steps 12–16; escalate any honeycombing that exposes reinforcement or extends into the wall thickness per Step 11.
Reinspection: repeat the sounding and reinforcement checks after classification and, if applicable, after repair.

Repair Debonds or Cracks During Curing

Probable cause: inadequate surface preparation, incorrect moisture condition at the time of application, or a repair material applied outside the manufacturer's stated temperature or thickness limits.
Confirmation: sound the repair area and compare the preparation and application record against the manufacturer's instructions.
Correction: remove the failed repair material, re-prepare the surface to the manufacturer's stated condition, and reapply the repair material within its stated limits.
Reinspection: repeat the repair-bond check after the new repair has cured for the specified period.

Exposed Reinforcement Found During Inspection

Probable cause: insufficient concrete cover during placement, reinforcement displacement during consolidation, or a form-tie or spacer failure in Project 07.
Confirmation: measure the extent of exposure and check for corrosion, section loss, or displacement of the affected bar.
Correction: stop work and obtain engineer or designer direction; do not repair exposed or corroded reinforcement without an approved corrosion-repair detail.
Reinspection: repeat the defect inspection for the affected area after the engineer or designer issues direction.

Tie Hole or Patch Incompatible With the Planned Waterproofing System

Probable cause: the tie-hole plug or patching material was selected without checking compatibility against the waterproofing or damp-proofing system planned for Project 10.
Confirmation: compare the selected plug or patching material against the manufacturer compatibility information for the planned moisture-control system.
Correction: remove the incompatible material and reseal the tie hole or patch using a product confirmed compatible with the planned system.
Reinspection: repeat the surface-preparation check for every tie hole and patch affected by the substitution.

Cleanup

This project ends with a released, repaired wall and sorted materials, not a torn-down worksite.

  • Dispose of damaged form materials and repair-mixing debris according to the site waste plan

Field Terms

Stripping Authorization
Written confirmation that the concrete has reached the required strength, age, or test result for form removal.
Exclusion Zone
The area beneath and around a form component in which no worker stands while it is being freed or lowered.
Cosmetic Defect
A surface condition that does not affect strength, cover, or later-system performance and does not require repair.
Minor Repairable Defect
A defect within the authorized repair scope that can be patched using an approved material without structural review.
Structural-Review Defect
A defect indicating possible structural compromise that requires qualified engineering review before any repair.
Sounding
Tapping concrete with a hammer to identify hollow or delaminated areas by the response.
Honeycombing
A surface or subsurface void pattern caused by incomplete consolidation during placement.
Tie Hole
The void left in the wall surface where a form tie passed through the concrete.
Bughole
A small surface void caused by trapped air or water at the form face during placement.
Form-Strip and Defect Inspection Record
The document recording stripping, material recovery, defect classification, repairs, and release.
