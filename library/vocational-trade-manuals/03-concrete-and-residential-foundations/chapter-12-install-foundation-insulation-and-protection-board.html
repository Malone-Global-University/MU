Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install the specified exterior foundation insulation and protection-board assembly over the completed waterproofing or damp-proofing system from Project 10 and the completed drain-tile system from Project 11, without puncturing, bridging, blocking, or chemically damaging either system. The finished work product is a continuous, compatible, secured, and inspected foundation insulation and protection assembly, photographed before backfill and released for Project 13.

You will confirm compatibility among the insulation, adhesive, fasteners, waterproofing, drain mat, and protection board; verify that waterproofing and drainage inspections are complete; repair any damage found in the wall system; lay out, cut, and dry-fit insulation panels; attach panels using an approved method that does not violate the waterproofing; install protection board and accessories; inspect the completed assembly; and release the wall for backfill only after the protection system passes inspection.

This project depends entirely on the work completed in Projects 10 and 11. Any wall area where waterproofing, damp-proofing, or drain-tile installation has not been inspected and released is not ready for this project, and insulation or protection board must not be installed over it.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-067 — Select the insulation type specified by the approved documents for thermal rating, thickness, exposure limit, and fire or termite requirement.
  • FN-068 — Cut insulation panels to fit corners, steps, penetrations, and terminations using the required tool and dust-control method.
  • FN-069 — Fasten insulation panels using the approved adhesive or mechanical attachment without violating the waterproofing system.
  • FN-070 — Install protection board over exposed waterproofing or insulation where required by the approved detail.
  • FN-071 — Protect the waterproofing and drainage systems from puncture, bridging, and chemical incompatibility throughout the installation.
  • FN-072 — Inspect the completed foundation protection system and document it before release for backfill.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 10 installed the moisture-control system and Project 11 installed the permanent drain-tile system that protects the foundation for the life of the building. This project adds the thermal and physical protection layer that must not compromise either system. Every panel cut, every fastener, and every accessory installed here is evaluated first against one question: does it puncture, bridge, block, or chemically damage the waterproofing or drainage work completed in the two prior projects?

The compatibility-first discipline used in this chapter — checking every material against the waterproofing and drainage systems before it touches the wall — is the same discipline applied again in Project 13, where backfill material and equipment must avoid damaging this exact assembly.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved insulation type, thermal rating, thickness, exposure limits, fire or termite requirements, attachment method, joint treatment, and protection-board detail
  • verifying compatibility among the insulation, adhesive, fasteners, waterproofing, drain mat, protection board, and backfill material
  • confirming that waterproofing and drainage inspections are complete and released for covering
  • inspecting the wall system for damage and repairing it before insulation begins
  • laying out, cutting, dry-fitting, and attaching insulation panels
  • installing protection board, termination bars, cap flashing, insect protection, and drainage mat where specified
  • inspecting and documenting the completed assembly before backfill

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select an insulation type, thickness, or thermal rating not specified by the approved documents
  • install insulation or protection board over a wall area where waterproofing or drainage has not passed inspection
  • use an adhesive, fastener, or accessory not confirmed compatible with the installed waterproofing system
  • repair waterproofing damage using a material not approved for that system; waterproofing repairs must use the system identified in Project 10
  • authorize backfill; that release remains governed by Project 13's prerequisites

Any incompatible material, unrepaired waterproofing damage, or blocked drainage path identified during this project must be corrected before the insulation and protection assembly is considered complete.

Trade Note

Compatibility is checked once, in writing, before any material touches the wall — not discovered after a panel is already bonded to the waterproofing. An adhesive or solvent that is safe on one waterproofing product can soften or dissolve another. Confirm compatibility from the manufacturer documentation for the specific waterproofing system installed in Project 10, not from general familiarity with a similar product.

Setup Standard

Do not begin insulation or protection-board installation over any wall area until the waterproofing or damp-proofing inspection and the drain-tile inspection for that area are complete and the surfaces are released for covering. Any insulation installed over an unreleased area must be removed and the underlying system reverified before reinstallation.

Shop Tip

Dry-fit every panel around corners, steps, pipes, sleeves, and window wells before applying adhesive or fasteners. A dry-fit that reveals a gap, overlap, or interference is far easier to correct than a panel already bonded in the wrong position.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every installation in this project follows the same sequence: confirm compatibility and inspection status before touching the wall, repair any damage first, lay out panels from established references, dry-fit before attaching, attach without violating the waterproofing, install protection board and accessories, and inspect and document before backfill. Following that sequence in the same order every time prevents the most common failure in this project: attaching a panel before confirming what is underneath it is sound.

Compatibility, Inspection Status, and Damage Repair

Do not install any insulation, adhesive, fastener, or protection board over a wall area until the compatibility documentation for that exact material combination has been confirmed and the waterproofing and drainage inspections for that area are complete. If a tear, puncture, contamination, or uncured area is found in the wall system during this project's inspection, repair it using the approved waterproofing system before insulation proceeds over that location.

Manufacturer Cue — Confirm Compatibility Before Any Material Touches the Wall

Verify compatibility among the insulation, adhesive, fasteners, waterproofing, drain mat, protection board, and backfill material using the manufacturer documentation for the specific products installed in Projects 10 and 11. Do not assume compatibility based on a similar product used on a different job. An incompatible adhesive or solvent can soften, dissolve, or degrade the waterproofing membrane beneath it.

Critical Safety Cue — Protecting Completed Waterproofing and Drainage Work

Treat every completed waterproofing surface and every drain-tile component as a stop-work zone for careless tool use. A dropped tool, a dragged panel edge, or an improperly supported ladder can puncture a membrane that passed inspection minutes earlier. Inspect the membrane immediately after any incident before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Cutting Dust and Excavation Edges

Follow the manufacturer's dust-control method for the specified insulation product when cutting, and wear the required respiratory protection. Confirm a stable working position at excavation edges before reaching to fit or fasten a panel near grade; do not lean over an unprotected excavation edge to complete a cut or attachment.

Code Alert — Fire, Termite, and Exposure Requirements

Insulation type, exposure limits above grade, fire-protection requirements, and termite-protection requirements vary by jurisdiction and by the approved design. Do not substitute an insulation product with a different fire rating, termite resistance, or exposure rating than the one specified in the approved documents. Verify jurisdictional requirements with the local building department before substitution.

E. Preparation — Document Review and Compatibility Confirmation

  1. Review the approved insulation type, thermal rating, thickness, exposure limits, fire or termite requirements, attachment method, joint treatment, top and bottom terminations, and protection-board detail. Confirm the document set is complete before proceeding to Step 2.
  2. Verify compatibility among the insulation, adhesive, fasteners, waterproofing, drain mat, protection board, and backfill material. Obtain written manufacturer or project-specific confirmation before any material is installed.
  3. Confirm that waterproofing and drainage inspections are complete and that the surfaces are released for covering. Obtain the Project 10 and Project 11 inspection records before proceeding.

F. Substrate Inspection and Repair

  1. Inspect the wall system for tears, punctures, uncured material, contamination, irregular projections, and trapped water. Record every condition found before continuing.
  2. Repair all damaged waterproofing using the approved system before insulation begins. Use only the waterproofing system identified in Project 10 for any repair.

G. Layout and Panel Preparation

  1. Establish panel layout, vertical and horizontal references, joints, corners, penetrations, and terminations. Mark the layout on the wall or on a layout worksheet before cutting any panel.
  2. Measure and cut insulation panels using the required tool and dust-control method. Follow the manufacturer's specified cutting method and required respiratory protection.
  3. Dry-fit panels around corners, steps, pipes, sleeves, window wells, and other interruptions. Correct any gap, overlap, or interference before attaching any panel.

H. Panel Attachment

  1. Apply compatible adhesive or approved mechanical attachment without violating the waterproofing system. Use only the method and coverage confirmed compatible in Step 2.
  2. Install panels tightly to the wall while maintaining required joint treatment and drainage paths. Verify each panel is fully seated before proceeding to the next.
  3. Stagger, tape, seal, or leave joints as required by the approved system. Follow the specified joint pattern exactly; do not substitute a different joint treatment.

I. Protection Board and Accessories

  1. Install termination bars, cap flashing, insect protection, drainage mat, or other specified accessories. Install each accessory at the location and orientation shown in the approved detail.
  2. Install protection board over exposed waterproofing or insulation where required. Confirm full coverage of every area specified to receive protection board.
  3. Protect membrane terminations and panel edges from backfill intrusion. Verify every termination is sealed or capped as specified.
  4. Maintain clear access to cleanouts, drains, outlets, inspection ports, and required service points. Do not cover or obstruct any required access point.

J. Inspection, Documentation, and Release

  1. Inspect panel alignment, attachment, joint condition, continuity, penetrations, terminations, and damage. Record every condition found on the inspection record.
  2. Correct loose panels, open joints, incompatible materials, blocked drainage, punctures, and exposed waterproofing. Re-inspect every corrected item before continuing.
  3. Photograph the completed assembly before backfill. Photograph every wall face, corner, termination, and protection-board area.
  4. Record product identification, thickness, attachment, compatibility documentation, repairs, and inspection results. Complete every field on the Foundation Insulation and Protection Inspection Record.
  5. Release the wall for backfill only after the protection system passes inspection. Sign and date the release section of the record.

Common Installation Inspection

Inspect every installed component using the same checks regardless of which wall face or detail it applies to.

  • Compatibility check: confirm every material in contact with the waterproofing or drainage system was confirmed compatible in writing before installation.
  • Substrate check: confirm no tears, punctures, uncured material, contamination, or trapped water remain under any installed panel.
  • Attachment check: confirm every panel is fully attached using the approved method without puncturing or bridging the waterproofing.
  • Protection-board check: confirm continuous coverage over every area specified to receive protection board.
  • Access check: confirm every cleanout, drain, outlet, and inspection port remains accessible.

Complete the Foundation Insulation and Protection Inspection Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the work for Project 13.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Adhesive Softens or Discolors the Waterproofing Membrane

Probable cause: the adhesive or solvent used was not confirmed compatible with the specific waterproofing product installed in Project 10.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area for softening, discoloration, or loss of adhesion in the membrane, and check the adhesive's compatibility documentation against the installed waterproofing system.
Correction: stop work, remove the incompatible adhesive and any affected panel, obtain engineer or manufacturer direction on membrane repair, and repair the waterproofing using the approved system before reinstalling with a confirmed compatible adhesive.
Reinspection: repeat the substrate inspection and compatibility check for the repaired area before reattaching any panel.

Insulation Panel Punctures the Waterproofing During Fitting

Probable cause: a panel edge, corner, or fastening tool contacted the membrane with excessive force during dry-fitting or attachment.
Confirmation: inspect the membrane at the contact point for a visible puncture, tear, or thinned area.
Correction: repair the puncture using the approved waterproofing system before continuing insulation installation over that area.
Reinspection: repeat the substrate inspection for the repaired area and confirm cure time before reinstalling insulation.

Protection Board Leaves a Gap Exposing Waterproofing or Insulation

Probable cause: protection-board panels were installed with an unintended gap at a joint, corner, or termination.
Confirmation: inspect the full protection-board coverage against the approved detail and identify every exposed area.
Correction: install additional protection board or a specified accessory to close the gap before backfill.
Reinspection: repeat the protection-board check for the full wall face.

Drain Mat or Insulation Blocks a Drainage Path

Probable cause: a panel or drain mat was installed over a drain-tile outlet, cleanout, or drainage path without maintaining the required clearance.
Confirmation: compare the installed insulation and drainage-mat layout to the Project 11 drain-tile record and identify any conflict.
Correction: remove and reposition the conflicting material to restore the required drainage path and clearance.
Reinspection: repeat the access check for the affected drainage component.

Panel Installed Over an Unreleased Waterproofing Area

Probable cause: insulation installation proceeded before the Project 10 waterproofing inspection for that specific wall area was complete.
Confirmation: compare the installed area to the Project 10 inspection record and identify any area lacking a release signature.
Correction: remove the insulation over the unreleased area, complete the required waterproofing inspection, and reinstall only after release.
Reinspection: repeat the substrate and attachment checks for the affected area.

Termination Bar or Cap Flashing Leaves the Top Termination Open

Probable cause: the termination accessory was omitted, misaligned, or installed with an unsealed gap.
Confirmation: inspect the top termination against the approved detail for continuous coverage and seal condition.
Correction: install or reseat the specified termination bar or cap flashing and seal any remaining gap with a compatible sealant.
Reinspection: repeat the terminations and penetrations check for the affected area.

Cleanup

This project ends with a released, protected assembly ready for backfill, not a torn-down worksite.

  • Remove insulation offcuts, packaging, adhesive containers, and cutting debris from the work area
  • Store unused panels, protection board, and accessories away from weather and equipment traffic
  • Confirm every cleanout, drain, outlet, and inspection port remains visible and accessible for Project 13
  • Protect the completed assembly from equipment contact and weather until backfill begins
  • Return the Foundation Insulation and Protection Inspection Record and photographs to the project record with the Project 10 and Project 11 documentation
