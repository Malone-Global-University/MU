Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install a permanent perimeter foundation-drain system at the footing, using the approved drain detail to control pipe material, perforation orientation, elevation, slope, cleanouts, aggregate, filter fabric, and discharge method. The finished work product is a continuous, sloped, tested drain line running the full protected perimeter, connected to the approved gravity outlet, sump, or designed discharge point, with the adjacent waterproofing reinspected and repaired as needed.

You will review the approved drain detail, confirm that waterproofing and wall repairs are complete and protected, establish invert elevations, prepare the drain bed, place and join perforated pipe at the correct slope, install cleanouts and transition fittings, surround the pipe with aggregate and filter fabric, test the completed line, and photograph and document the system before it is concealed by insulation, protection board, and backfill.

This project depends directly on the waterproofing or damp-proofing system completed in Project 10. It also sets the discharge conditions that must be protected through insulation installation in Project 12 and backfill in Project 13. No aggregate, backfill, or insulation work may bury the drain line until this project's continuity test and inspection are complete and recorded.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FN-061 — Establish drain slope from verified invert elevations toward the approved outlet or sump.
  • FN-062 — Place filter fabric at the drain bed and around the aggregate as specified, without puncturing the wall system.
  • FN-063 — Install perforated pipe in the required orientation and location relative to the footing.
  • FN-064 — Surround the installed pipe with drainage aggregate to the specified thickness without displacing it.
  • FN-065 — Route discharge through cleanouts, transitions, and solid outlet pipe to the approved discharge point.
  • FN-066 — Inspect and test drainage continuity and document the completed system before concealment.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 10 established a continuous foundation moisture-control system on the wall surfaces. This project adds the second half of the water-management strategy: a permanent drain line at the footing that intercepts groundwater before it can build hydrostatic pressure against the wall. The invert-elevation and slope-verification method used here — establishing a fixed reference and checking continuity along the full run before covering the work — is the same discipline applied to the vapor-barrier and reinforcement work in Project 15 and to the final drainage grading confirmed during the Chapter 20 capstone.

The drain system installed in this project must remain functional and undamaged through insulation installation, backfill, and the life of the structure. Every aggregate placement, insulation panel, and backfill lift performed in later projects is required to protect, not bury blindly, the pipe, fabric, and outlet components verified here.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved drain detail, pipe material, perforation orientation, elevation, slope, cleanouts, aggregate, filter fabric, and outlet or sump requirements
  • confirming that waterproofing and required wall repairs are complete and protected from damage before drain work begins
  • establishing invert elevations and confirming that the approved discharge point can receive flow
  • preparing the drain bed, placing filter fabric, and installing perforated pipe at the required slope
  • installing corners, tees, cleanouts, risers, sumps, solid outlet pipe, and transition fittings
  • placing and wrapping drainage aggregate around the pipe
  • testing drainage continuity and inspecting adjacent waterproofing for damage
  • photographing and documenting the completed system before concealment

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select a discharge method, sump-pump system, or connection to a public storm system without the approved drain detail and any required permit or jurisdictional approval
  • alter the specified pipe diameter, perforation pattern, aggregate gradation, or slope on independent judgment
  • bury, backfill, or insulate over the drain system before the continuity test and inspection required in this project are complete
  • repair or modify the waterproofing membrane using a different product than the one approved in Project 10 without authorization

Any drain-slope reading, aggregate condition, fabric placement, fitting connection, or continuity test result that fails the approved standard must be corrected and reverified before the drain system is concealed.

Trade Note

Every invert elevation and slope check in this chapter is taken from the same fixed reference established in Step 4. Checking slope only at the two ends of a run, rather than at regular intervals along its length, is the most common cause of an undetected low spot or reverse-slope section diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not place drainage aggregate above the pipe, install insulation or protection board, or begin backfill until this project's continuity test has passed and the Foundation Drain Inspection Record is complete. Any aggregate or fabric placed before invert elevations are verified must be removed and reverified.

Shop Tip

Photograph each cleanout, tee, and outlet transition immediately after it is installed and before the next section of pipe is placed. Waiting until the full run is complete risks losing the exact location of a fitting once it is surrounded by aggregate.

Invert Elevations and Continuous Slope

Every invert elevation in this installation is verified from the benchmark identified in Step 4 of Project 1 or its transferred control. Slope must be continuous toward the outlet or sump for the full length of the run; a flat or reverse-slope section anywhere in the line is a defect even if the average grade of the full run is correct. Do not rely on a single elevation check at each end of a long run without intermediate checks.

Measurement Cue — Verify Slope at Regular Intervals, Not Only at the Ends

Check invert elevation at each corner, each fitting, and at intervals no greater than those specified in the approved drain detail. A run that passes at both ends can still contain a low spot or a short reverse-slope section between them. Correct any interval reading that breaks the continuous fall toward the outlet before continuing installation.

Critical Safety Cue — Trench Walls and Wet Soil

Treat any undercut, saturated, or sloughing trench wall along the footing perimeter as a stop-work condition until a competent person addresses it. Do not enter a trench section that shows signs of collapse, and do not stand beneath an unsupported excavation face to place pipe or aggregate.

Field Safety Cue — Standing Water and Slip Hazards

Remove standing water from the trench before working in it, and use footing appropriate to wet or muddy conditions. Keep aggregate stockpiles and delivery equipment clear of the open trench edge to prevent material sliding into the work area.

Code Alert — Discharge-Point Authority

Connection to a public storm system, an easement, or a neighboring property line varies by jurisdiction and by the approved drain detail. Never route discharge to an unapproved location, a neighboring lot, or an undersized swale. Confirm the approved discharge point with the designer or local authority before connecting the outlet pipe.

E. Preparation — Document Review and Site Inspection

  1. Review the approved drain detail, pipe material, perforation orientation, elevation, slope, cleanouts, aggregate, filter fabric, sump or outlet, and inspection requirements. Confirm the document set is complete before proceeding to Step 2.
  2. Confirm that waterproofing and required wall repairs are complete and protected from damage. Obtain the Project 10 inspection record before disturbing the wall surface.
  3. Inspect the footing perimeter for debris, standing water, irregular surfaces, damaged coating, and outlet conflicts. Record every condition found before continuing.

F. Establishing References and Preparing the Bed

  1. Establish invert elevations and verify that the approved discharge point can receive flow. Mark the selected invert reference at the outlet end before proceeding.
  2. Prepare the drain bed and place filter fabric where required without puncturing the wall system. Fold fabric up the wall face only to the height specified in the approved detail.
  3. Place the initial drainage aggregate to the specified thickness and grade. Check the bed elevation against the invert reference before placing pipe.

G. Pipe Installation

  1. Install perforated pipe in the required orientation and location relative to the footing. Confirm perforation orientation matches the approved detail before joining sections.
  2. Join pipe sections with compatible fittings and maintain smooth internal flow paths. Seat every joint fully before backfilling around it.
  3. Install corners, tees, cleanouts, risers, sumps, solid outlet pipe, and transition fittings. Locate each fitting exactly as shown in the approved detail.
  4. Maintain continuous slope toward the outlet or sump. Check invert elevation at every fitting and at the specified interval.
  5. Protect open pipe ends from soil, concrete, aggregate, insects, and construction debris. Cap or cover every open end at the close of each work session.

H. Verification, Aggregate Placement, and Fabric Wrap

  1. Verify pipe elevation, slope, alignment, and outlet continuity at regular intervals. Record each interval reading on the Foundation Drain Inspection Record.
  2. Place drainage aggregate around and above the pipe without floating, crushing, or displacing it. Place aggregate in controlled lifts working away from the pipe.
  3. Wrap or separate the drainage aggregate with filter fabric where specified. Overlap fabric seams by the amount required in the approved detail.
  4. Install outlet protection, backwater controls, sump components, or daylight termination features required by the design. Confirm each component matches the approved detail before final connection.

I. Testing and Correction

  1. Conduct the approved continuity, flow, camera, or visual test. Perform the test before further aggregate or fabric conceals any section of the line.
  2. Correct reverse slope, low spots, open joints, blocked pipe, damaged fabric, crushed sections, and outlet problems. Reopen and repair any section that fails the test before retesting.

J. Waterproofing Reinspection, Recording, and Release

  1. Inspect the adjacent waterproofing after installation and repair any damage using the approved system. Do not conceal drain work until this reinspection is complete.
  2. Photograph pipe position, slope controls, cleanouts, outlets, aggregate, and fabric before concealment. Photograph every fitting location before it is surrounded by additional aggregate.
  3. Complete the drainage-system inspection and protect it during insulation and backfill. Sign and date the release section of the Foundation Drain Inspection Record.

Common Drainage Inspection

Inspect every drain-system item using the same checks regardless of which section, fitting, or discharge component it applies to.

  • Invert and slope check: confirm continuous fall toward the outlet or sump at every checked interval, verified from the protected benchmark.
  • Orientation check: confirm perforated pipe faces and is located exactly as required by the approved drain detail.
  • Fabric and aggregate check: confirm continuous fabric coverage and specified aggregate thickness without pipe displacement.
  • Fitting check: confirm every corner, tee, cleanout, riser, and transition is installed, seated, and accessible.
  • Continuity check: confirm the approved flow, camera, or visual test passes with no blockage or leakage.
  • Waterproofing check: confirm the adjacent wall system shows no new damage from drain installation.

Complete the Foundation Drain Inspection Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the work for Project 12.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Section of the Line Shows Reverse Slope or a Low Spot

Probable cause: the trench bed was disturbed after the initial elevation check, or invert elevations were checked only at the ends of a long run rather than at regular intervals.
Confirmation: re-check invert elevation at every fitting and at the specified interval along the affected section, from the protected benchmark.
Correction: remove the pipe and aggregate in the affected section, re-grade the bed to the required slope, and reinstall the pipe.
Reinspection: repeat the slope check for the full length of the affected section and the continuity test for the full run.

The Continuity Test Identifies a Blockage or Open Joint

Probable cause: a joint was not fully seated before aggregate placement, or debris entered an unprotected open pipe end during installation.
Confirmation: re-run the approved flow, camera, or visual test and locate the blockage or open joint by section.
Correction: excavate to the affected section, clear debris or reseat the joint using compatible fittings, and confirm the repair before re-covering.
Reinspection: repeat the continuity test for the full run after the repair.

Filter Fabric Is Torn or Missing Coverage

Probable cause: fabric was punctured during aggregate placement, or seams were not lapped to the specified overlap.
Confirmation: inspect the fabric along the full run and compare seam overlap to the approved drain detail.
Correction: remove aggregate over the damaged section, repair or replace the fabric with the specified overlap, and reinstall aggregate without displacing the pipe.
Reinspection: repeat the fabric and aggregate check for the repaired section.

The Approved Discharge Point Cannot Receive Flow

Probable cause: the discharge point is blocked, undersized for the design flow, or was not verified before pipe installation began.
Confirmation: inspect the discharge point, sump, or outlet and compare its condition and capacity to the approved drain detail.
Correction: stop work and obtain engineer or designer direction; do not connect the outlet pipe to an unapproved or nonfunctional discharge point.
Reinspection: repeat the discharge-point verification after direction is issued and any authorized correction is made.

Waterproofing Is Damaged During Drain Installation

Probable cause: a tool, fitting, or aggregate placement contacted the wall system without adequate protection during trench work.
Confirmation: inspect the wall surface adjacent to the drain trench for punctures, tears, or scraped coating.
Correction: repair the damage using the approved waterproofing system from Project 10 before installing aggregate or fabric over the affected area.
Reinspection: repeat the waterproofing reinspection for the repaired area before concealment.

Aggregate Displaces or Floats the Pipe Out of Position

Probable cause: aggregate was placed directly on the pipe from height, or standing water in the trench floated the pipe before aggregate was placed.
Confirmation: re-check pipe elevation and alignment against the invert reference after aggregate placement.
Correction: remove aggregate around the displaced section, reposition the pipe to the required elevation and alignment, and re-place aggregate in controlled lifts.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation and alignment check for the corrected section.

Cleanup

This project ends with a tested, protected drain system and a clean perimeter, not an open trench.

  • Confirm every cleanout and riser is capped, accessible, and protected from equipment traffic
  • Remove excess pipe cutoffs, packaging, and unused fittings from the trench area
  • Store unused aggregate, fabric, and fittings for the next installation cycle
  • Confirm the discharge point or sump connection is protected from debris until final grading
  • Return the Foundation Drain Inspection Record and photographs to the project record with the Project 10 waterproofing documentation

Field Terms

Invert Elevation
The elevation of the interior bottom of the pipe at a given point, used to establish and verify slope.
Approved Discharge Point
The gravity outlet, sump, or designed discharge location identified in the approved drain detail.
Drain Bed
The prepared base beneath the pipe, including filter fabric and initial aggregate, at the required elevation and grade.
Perforated Pipe
Pipe with openings that collect water along the footing perimeter, installed in the required orientation.
Solid Outlet Pipe
Unperforated pipe that conveys collected water to the approved discharge point.
Cleanout
An accessible fitting installed to allow inspection, flushing, or camera access to the drain line.
Filter Fabric
Permeable fabric that separates drainage aggregate from surrounding soil while allowing water to pass.
Continuity Test
The flow, camera, or visual test used to confirm an open, unobstructed drain line before concealment.
Foundation Drain Inspection Record
The document recording invert elevations, slope readings, materials, test results, and release.
Release
The formal, signed authorization that the drain system is complete and insulation or backfill may proceed.
