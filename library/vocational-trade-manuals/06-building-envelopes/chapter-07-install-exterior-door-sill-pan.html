Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare and install a drained sill-pan system in one exterior-door rough opening before the prehung door is set. The finished work product is a continuous, supported, outward-draining sill pan — extending up both jambs, formed and sealed at both corners, and integrated with the surrounding water-resistive barrier (WRB) — ready to receive the specified prehung door.

You will review the door manufacturer's rough-opening and sill-pan requirements, verify the actual door unit and its dimensions, recheck the rough opening for square, level, plumb, and structural support, clean and slope the sill, install and form the sill-pan membrane or preformed components, seal the corners, integrate the exterior edge with the WRB, and inspect the completed pan for continuity and drainage before it is covered.

This project follows the wall-sheathing acceptance completed in Project 01 and the WRB and seam work completed in Projects 02 and 03. It sits immediately before Project 08, which sets and fastens the prehung exterior door into the sill pan installed here, and it parallels the window sill-pan flashing taught in Project 04.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-037 — Prepare the exterior-door rough opening, verifying dimensions, structural support, and threshold-bearing condition against the door manufacturer's requirements.
  • BE-038 — Create or install the required outward sill slope using the approved tapered material, pan system, or liquid-applied method.
  • BE-039 — Install the sill-pan membrane or preformed pan components so the pan extends up both jambs and across the full opening width.
  • BE-040 — Form and seal sill-pan corners using approved end dams, back dams, gussets, boots, or liquid flashing.
  • BE-041 — Integrate the sill pan's exterior edge with the water-resistive barrier so water discharges to the outside without blocking the drainage path.
  • BE-042 — Inspect sill-pan continuity, corner seals, back dam, slope, bearing, and drainage outlet, and document the completed pan before the door is installed.

Where This Skill Fits

The sill pan installed here is the single most consequential water-control detail in the entire door assembly. Every later door project in this book depends on it: the prehung door set in Project 08 relies on the pan's slope and bearing surface, the jamb and head flashing installed in Project 09 relies on the pan's upturned edges to shed water correctly, and the full exterior water-control inspection in Project 19 specifically checks sill-pan drainage as one of its required items. A sill pan that traps water instead of draining it outward will not announce itself until water damage appears at the threshold or subfloor, often long after the siding has concealed the defect.

The sequence used here — verify the opening, slope the sill, install the pan from center or bottom outward, form the corners, and integrate with the WRB — is the same drainage-first method used for the window sill-pan flashing in Project 04. Building that method correctly here will make Project 04's window sill pan, and the full-building review in Project 19, more consistent and reliable.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the door manufacturer's rough-opening dimensions, threshold-support requirements, sill-pan detail, fastening zones, sealant locations, and accessibility or clearance requirements
  • verifying the actual door unit, handing, swing, threshold type, frame dimensions, and installation accessories against the rough opening
  • measuring the rough opening for width, height, square, level, plumb, floor elevation, and required clearances
  • inspecting the rough sill for rot, damage, uneven bearing, protrusions, contamination, and structural deficiency
  • creating the required outward sill slope and installing the sill-pan membrane or preformed components
  • forming and sealing sill-pan corners and integrating the exterior edge with the WRB
  • inspecting and documenting the completed sill pan before the door is installed

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • proceed with sill-pan installation when the opening, floor, framing, or threshold-support condition falls outside the door manufacturer's permitted adjustment range
  • select a sill-pan system, membrane, or slope material independently of the approved door schedule and manufacturer instructions
  • block the sill-pan's drainage outlet with sealant, fill material, or an unapproved back-dam detail
  • set the prehung door before this project's sill-pan inspection is complete and any required correction has been made

Any rough-opening, floor, or structural condition that falls outside the approved adjustment range must stop work in that opening until qualified review and direction are obtained.

Trade Note

Treat the sill pan as a component with its own inspection standard, not as a preliminary step to be rushed before setting the door. Every jamb flashing, head flashing, and threshold seal completed in Project 09 assumes this pan already drains correctly and remains undamaged. A pan that is compressed, punctured, or bridged with debris before the door is set will fail exactly where it is hardest to inspect afterward.

Setup Standard

Do not begin sill-pan installation until the door manufacturer's rough-opening requirements, sill-pan detail, and installation accessories are confirmed against the actual door unit scheduled for this opening (Step 1–2). Do not treat an opening as ready because it appears close by eye; remeasure width, height, square, level, plumb, and floor elevation before beginning (Step 3).

Shop Tip

Dry-fit every pan component before applying primer or peeling release film. Confirming full threshold support and exterior drainage on a dry-fit avoids cutting a second piece of membrane or forming a corner twice, and it catches an out-of-tolerance sill before the manufacturer's open time on primer or adhesive has expired.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every sill-pan detail in this book, whether at a door in this project or a window in Project 04, follows the same drainage logic: the pan must slope outward, its corners must be closed against inward water movement while remaining open at the exterior edge, and its upturned jamb legs must integrate with the WRB above so water sheds down and out rather than behind the pan.

Separating Authorized Correction From Qualified Review

An opening that is out of square, undersized, oversized, or has an unsupported or deteriorated sill beyond the door manufacturer's permitted adjustment range is not something this project authorizes you to correct independently. Mark it, record it, and obtain qualified review before proceeding. A minor sill-slope build-up using the approved tapered material within the permitted range is an authorized part of this project's scope.

Measurement Cue — Recheck the Opening Before Cutting Membrane

Measure the rough-opening width, height, square, level, plumb, and floor elevation and compare each value against the door manufacturer's permitted adjustment range before cutting sill-pan membrane. Do not cut membrane to a remembered or estimated dimension; an undersized or misaligned pan cannot be stretched into position without compromising its drainage slope.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Proceed Over an Unsupported or Deteriorated Sill

Do not install a sill pan or set threshold-support shims over a rough sill showing rot, structural deficiency, or uneven bearing beyond the door manufacturer's permitted adjustment range. A pan installed over a deteriorated sill will not correct the structural condition, and it conceals the deficiency from later inspection. Stop work and obtain qualified review before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Working at the Threshold Opening

Do not stand on the unsupported rough sill or bridge it with unsecured planks. Maintain solid footing on the interior floor platform or exterior grade while reaching into the opening, and keep the opening barricaded or flagged when unattended during multi-day installations.

Manufacturer Cue — Sill-Pan Product Compatibility

Confirm that the selected sill-pan membrane or preformed pan, primer, and sealant are compatible with each other and with the WRB, sheathing, and treated sill material at this opening before beginning installation. Do not substitute a generic membrane, primer, or sealant not identified by the door manufacturer or the approved sill-pan detail.

Code Alert — Accessibility and Threshold Requirements Vary

Required threshold height, accessible-route clearances, and sill-pan inspection hold-points vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction and the general contractor before finalizing threshold-support elevation or concealing the completed pan.

E. Document Review and Opening Verification (Steps 1–3)

  1. Review the door manufacturer's rough-opening dimensions, threshold support requirements, sill-pan detail, fastening zones, sealant locations, and accessibility or clearance requirements. Confirm the current revision against the project's document-control log before using it.
  2. Verify the actual door unit, handing, swing, threshold type, frame dimensions, and installation accessories. Confirm the unit identification matches the door schedule for this opening.
  3. Measure the rough opening for width, height, square, level, plumb, floor elevation, and required interior and exterior clearances. Record each value against the door manufacturer's permitted adjustment range.

F. Sill Inspection and Preparation (Steps 4–8)

  1. Inspect the rough sill for rot, damage, uneven bearing, protrusions, contamination, and structural deficiency. Use a moisture meter where staining or softness is observed.
  2. Stop work if the opening, floor, framing, or threshold support condition falls outside the approved adjustment range. Mark the condition and obtain qualified review before continuing.
  3. Clean and prepare the sill and lower jambs. Remove debris, dust, and contamination using the sill-pan manufacturer's approved method.
  4. Create the required outward slope using the approved tapered material, pan system, or liquid-applied method. Confirm the resulting opening dimensions remain within the permitted range after the slope material is installed.
  5. Install corner guards, back dams, end dams, or preformed sill-pan components required by the selected system. Dry-fit these components before applying primer or adhesive.

G. Pan Installation and Corner Forming (Steps 9–16)

  1. Dry-fit the sill-pan components and confirm full threshold support and exterior drainage. Correct any interference before proceeding to cutting.
  2. Cut membrane or preformed-pan components to the controlled dimensions required for this opening. Reference the door manufacturer's rough-opening dimensions rather than an estimated length.
  3. Apply required primer or compatible sealant at specified joints. Observe the manufacturer's specified open time before installing membrane.
  4. Install the sill pan so it extends up the jambs and across the full opening width. Work from the center outward without stretching the membrane through the corners.
  5. Form and seal corners using approved end dams, back dams, gussets, boots, or liquid flashing. Confirm full contact and no visible gap at each formed corner.
  6. Integrate the exterior edge with the WRB so water discharges to the outside. Lap the pan's exterior edge beneath the surrounding WRB in shingle-lap order.
  7. Maintain an interior back dam where required to prevent inward water movement. Confirm the back dam remains continuous across the full opening width.
  8. Do not block pan outlets or fill the drainage path with sealant. Leave the exterior edge of the pan open to drain.

H. Support, Inspection, and Documentation (Steps 17–20)

  1. Install threshold support shims or bearing components only where required by the door manufacturer. Use only the shim material and locations specified for the scheduled unit.
  2. Inspect pan continuity, corner seals, back dam, end dams, slope, bearing, and drainage outlet. Confirm every item against the Acceptance Standards table before proceeding.
  3. Repair punctures, wrinkles, gaps, and incomplete corners using the approved method. Do not conceal an unresolved defect with sealant alone.
  4. Protect the completed sill pan from foot traffic and door-handling damage. Cover or barricade the opening until the door is ready to be set.
  5. Photograph and document the pan before the door is installed. Label each photograph with the opening ID for later reference.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect every completed sill pan using the same checks regardless of which opening it covers.

  • Documentation check: confirm the door manufacturer's rough-opening and sill-pan requirements and the door schedule are current and referenced on the sill-pan inspection record.
  • Opening check: confirm the rough opening matches its schedule for dimension, square, level, plumb, and floor elevation within the permitted range.
  • Slope check: confirm the sill slopes outward without reducing the required opening dimensions.
  • Pan continuity check: confirm the pan extends up both jambs and across the full opening width without gaps, wrinkles, or punctures.
  • Corner check: confirm both corners are formed and sealed with no visible gap or void.
  • Drainage check: confirm the exterior edge remains open and the back dam remains continuous.
  • WRB integration check: confirm the exterior edge of the pan is integrated with the surrounding WRB in shingle-lap order.

Complete the Sill-Pan Inspection Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 08.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Sill Pan Does Not Drain Outward

Probable cause: the sill slope was not established before membrane installation, or the tapered material was installed level or back-pitched.
Confirmation: check the sill slope with a level and straightedge and compare the reading against the approved slope detail.
Correction: remove the affected membrane, correct the slope using the approved tapered material or liquid-applied method, and reinstall the pan.
Reinspection: repeat the slope check across the full opening width after correction.

A Sill-Pan Corner Shows a Gap or Void

Probable cause: the corner was formed without full contact, or the membrane was stretched through the corner rather than fit with the approved folding, gusset, or liquid-flashing method.
Confirmation: inspect the corner visually and by hand for lift, gaps, or incomplete adhesion.
Correction: remove and reform the corner using the approved method, applying primer again if the manufacturer's open time has expired.
Reinspection: repeat the corner inspection after correction and before the door is set.

The Drainage Outlet Has Been Sealed or Blocked

Probable cause: sealant intended for an adjacent designated joint was applied across the pan's exterior edge, or debris was left in the drainage path.
Confirmation: inspect the full width of the exterior edge for continuous, unobstructed opening.
Correction: remove the sealant or debris without damaging the membrane and confirm the outlet is fully open.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage-outlet check before the door is set and before Project 08 begins.

The Rough Sill Cannot Provide Full Threshold Bearing

Probable cause: the sill framing is uneven, damaged, or was not built to the elevation required by the scheduled door unit.
Confirmation: check bearing with a straightedge across the full sill width and compare against the door manufacturer's threshold-support requirement.
Correction: mark the condition for qualified review if it falls outside the permitted adjustment range; do not attempt an unauthorized framing correction.
Reinspection: repeat the bearing check after the documented correction is complete and before Project 08 begins.

The Pan's Exterior Edge Is Not Integrated With the WRB

Probable cause: the pan was installed after the surrounding WRB without reopening the correct shingle-lap sequence, or the WRB was cut back further than required.
Confirmation: inspect the lap order at the exterior edge and confirm the WRB overlaps the pan rather than tucking behind it.
Correction: lift and correct the WRB lap or install an approved transition patch to restore shingle-lap order.
Reinspection: repeat the lap-order check before the door is set.

Cleanup

This project ends with a continuous, supported, outward-draining sill pan and a documented inspection record, not with the prehung door installed.

  • Clear debris, release-film backing, and trimmed membrane scraps from the opening and surrounding floor or grade
  • Remove masking or temporary marking materials that are not part of an open deficiency reference
  • Cover or otherwise protect the completed sill pan from foot traffic and door-handling damage before leaving the site
  • Store the sill-pan inspection record and supporting photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open discrepancy and its required decision to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Project 08 begins
