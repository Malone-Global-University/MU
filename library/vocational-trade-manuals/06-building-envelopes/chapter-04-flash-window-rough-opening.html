Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare and flash one new-construction window rough opening using the approved sill-pan and water-resistive barrier (WRB) integration method before the window itself is installed. The finished work product is a dimensionally correct, drained, and fully flashed rough opening: a sloped or pan-drained sill, sealed corners, jamb flashing or jamb preparation in place, a prepared head interface, and the surrounding WRB integrated in the correct shingle-lap order, together with photographs documenting the completed condition.

You will review the window manufacturer's rough-opening and flashing requirements, recheck the opening accepted in Project 01 against those requirements, clean and prepare the sill and jambs, create or install the required outward sill slope, install and seal the sill-pan flashing, install jamb flashing or jamb preparation, prepare the head interface, integrate the surrounding WRB using the approved cut pattern, inspect every layer for continuity and drainage, and photograph the completed assembly before the window is set.

This project follows directly from the wall-sheathing readiness decision issued in Project 01 and the continuous WRB installed and seam-treated in Projects 02 and 03. It sits immediately before Project 05, which sets the window into the opening flashed here, and Project 06, which completes the window's exterior flange flashing and interior air seal.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-019 — Prepare a window rough opening for flashing by verifying dimensions, condition, and structural support against the manufacturer's requirements.
  • BE-020 — Install sill-pan flashing that slopes outward, forms sealed corners, and establishes a defined drainage path to the exterior.
  • BE-021 — Flash the jambs or install the required jamb preparation layers in the sequence required by the selected flashing method.
  • BE-022 — Prepare the head opening interface without fastening through prohibited areas or disrupting the required head-flashing sequence.
  • BE-023 — Integrate the surrounding WRB with the rough-opening flashing using the approved cut pattern and shingle-lap order.
  • BE-024 — Inspect the completed water-shedding sequence for continuity, drainage, corner integrity, and dimensional accuracy, and document the result.

Where This Skill Fits

Every window in this book depends on the flashing completed here. The sill pan installed in this project is what allows incidental water that reaches the sill, whether from wind-driven rain, condensation, or a future seal failure, to drain back out of the wall rather than into the framing below. The jamb and head preparation completed here also controls how cleanly the window's own flange flashing integrates in Project 06.

The water-shedding sequence practiced here — sill first, then jambs, then head, each layer overlapping the one before it in the direction water will travel — is the same sequence used at every other opening and penetration in this book, including the Chapter 7 door sill pan, the Chapter 9 door flashing, and the Chapter 16 penetration flashing.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the window manufacturer's rough-opening requirements, installation method, flange details, clearances, sill-pan system, flashing products, and WRB sequence
  • rechecking rough-opening width, height, square, level, plumb, sill slope, header condition, and structural support against the approved schedule
  • cleaning and preparing the sill, jambs, and surrounding WRB
  • creating or installing the approved outward sill slope and installing sill-pan flashing with sealed corners and a defined drainage path
  • installing jamb flashing or jamb preparation layers required before the window is set
  • preparing the head flap or head-flashing interface
  • integrating the surrounding WRB using the approved cut pattern and shingle-lap order
  • inspecting and photographing the completed rough-opening flashing before the window is installed

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • proceed with flashing on an opening that is oversized, undersized, out of square beyond the permitted adjustment, structurally damaged, or missing required support
  • seal, tape, or otherwise close the exterior drainage edge of the sill pan, or create a reverse lap below the sill
  • fasten through the sill-pan drainage path, jamb flashing, or head-flashing interface at prohibited locations
  • install the window itself; the window is set in Project 05 only after this project's flashing has passed inspection

Any rough opening found outside the permitted adjustment range, structurally damaged, or missing required support must stop work at that opening until qualified review or correction is obtained.

Trade Note

The sill-pan drainage path installed in this project is the last line of defense at every window in this structure. A tight, well-taped flange in Project 06 does not compensate for a sill pan that traps water instead of shedding it. Treat the sill as the most important layer in this project, not the fastest one to finish.

Setup Standard

Do not begin flashing an opening until the window manufacturer's rough-opening requirements, flange details, sill-pan system, and flashing sequence are on site and confirmed current (Step 1), and until the actual window unit, its identification, handing, and dimensions have been verified against that opening (Step 2). Do not flash an opening that has not been rechecked for square, level, plumb, sill slope, header condition, and structural support (Step 3).

Shop Tip

Cut sill-pan flashing to its full controlled length before installing any of it. Attempting to extend a sill pan with a second piece after starting risks a seam directly in the drainage path, which the Acceptance Standards in this project do not permit.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every opening flashed in this book, whether a window in this chapter or a door sill pan in Chapter 7, follows the same water-shedding order: sill first, then jambs, then head, with each layer overlapping the one installed before it and the WRB integrated around the assembly in the same direction. Learning that order here protects every later opening in this book.

Maintaining the Sill-Pan Drainage Path

The drainage outlet at the exterior edge of the sill pan must remain open across every step of this project. Do not seal it, tape over it, or create a reverse lap that directs water back into the wall. If a later step in this project or in Project 05 or 06 appears to require covering that edge, stop and confirm the detail against the manufacturer's instructions before proceeding.

Measurement Cue — Recheck the Opening Before Flashing, Not After

Recheck rough-opening width, height, square, level, plumb, sill slope, and header condition immediately before flashing begins, even though the opening passed inspection in Project 01. Framing and sheathing can shift or be disturbed during WRB installation and seam treatment. Do not assume a previously accepted opening remains within tolerance without remeasuring it.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work on a Nonconforming Opening

Do not flash an opening that is oversized, undersized, out of square beyond the permitted adjustment, structurally damaged, or missing required support. Flashing over a nonconforming opening conceals the defect and sets up a water-control failure that will not be visible again until the Chapter 19 full exterior inspection or an active leak. Stop work at that opening and obtain qualified review or correction first.

Field Safety Cue — Protect the Opening During Flashing

Do not leave the flashed opening exposed to rain or wind longer than necessary before the window is set in Project 05. Stage materials and sequence work so that each opening's flashing is completed and protected within the same work session whenever practical.

Manufacturer Cue — Sill-Pan and Primer Compatibility

Confirm compatibility among the sill-pan membrane or preformed pan, primer, corner-sealing method, WRB, and window flange before beginning work. Do not substitute a flashing tape, primer, or liquid flashing not identified as compatible by the manufacturer's current instructions.

Code Alert — Rough-Opening Flashing Inspection Requirements Vary

Required inspection hold-points before a window is set over completed rough-opening flashing vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction and the general contractor before treating this project's inspection as the only required sign-off.

E. Document Review and Opening Verification (Steps 1–4)

  1. Review the window manufacturer's rough-opening requirements, installation method, flange details, required clearances, sill-pan system, flashing products, and WRB sequence. Confirm the instructions match the actual scheduled unit for this opening.
  2. Verify the actual window unit, opening identification, handing, dimensions, and installation accessories. Compare the unit against the window schedule before beginning any preparation.
  3. Recheck rough-opening width, height, square, level, plumb, sill slope, header condition, and structural support. Remeasure even though the opening was accepted in Project 01; framing and sheathing conditions can shift.
  4. Stop work if the opening is oversized, undersized, out of square beyond the permitted adjustment, structurally damaged, or missing required support. Mark the condition and obtain qualified review or correction before flashing.

F. Sill Preparation and Sill-Pan Installation (Steps 5–13)

  1. Clean the sill, jambs, and surrounding WRB of dust, moisture, protrusions, and contaminants. Use a stiff brush and confirm the surface is dry before flashing.
  2. Create or install the approved outward sill slope without reducing required opening dimensions. Verify the resulting slope with a level against the manufacturer's requirement.
  3. Install corner guards, back dams, end dams, or preformed sill-pan components required by the selected system. Confirm each component matches the manufacturer's identified detail for this opening.
  4. Cut sill flashing to the controlled length and shape required to cover the sill and extend up both jambs. Cut the full piece before installing any of it to avoid a seam in the drainage path.
  5. Apply primer where required. Observe the manufacturer's specified open time before installing flashing over primed surfaces.
  6. Install the sill flashing from the center outward without stretching it through corners. Work the membrane into corners by hand or with the specified tool rather than pulling it taut.
  7. Form corners using approved folding, gusset, patch, or liquid-flashing methods. Confirm full coverage and adhesion at each corner before proceeding.
  8. Maintain a defined drainage path from the sill pan to the exterior face of the WRB. Verify the path is unobstructed along its full width.
  9. Do not seal the exterior drainage edge or create a reverse lap below the sill. Confirm the exterior edge remains open before continuing to jamb flashing.

G. Jamb and Head Preparation (Steps 14–16)

  1. Install jamb flashing or jamb preparation layers required before the unit is set. Lap each jamb layer over the sill-pan upturns in shingle-lap order.
  2. Prepare the head flap or head flashing interface without fastening through prohibited areas. Keep the head flap positioned for later integration with head flashing in Project 06.
  3. Integrate the WRB around the opening using the approved cut pattern and shingle-lap order. Confirm no reverse laps are created at the jambs or head.

H. Inspection and Documentation (Steps 17–20)

  1. Protect prepared flashing from damage during window handling. Cover or otherwise shield the completed flashing until Project 05 begins.
  2. Inspect sill continuity, corner seals, jamb coverage, head preparation, drainage outlet, adhesion, and opening dimensions. Confirm every item against the Acceptance Standards table.
  3. Repair wrinkles, voids, fishmouths, punctures, and incomplete corners using the approved method. Reinspect the repaired area before considering the opening complete.
  4. Photograph the completed rough-opening flashing before the window is installed. Label each photograph with the opening ID for later reference in the Chapter 19 full exterior inspection.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the flashed rough opening using the same checks regardless of which elevation or opening it covers.

  • Documentation check: confirm the window manufacturer's rough-opening and flashing requirements and this opening's schedule reference are recorded on the rough-opening flashing record.
  • Dimension check: confirm the opening's width, height, square, level, plumb, and sill slope were rechecked immediately before flashing began.
  • Sill-pan check: confirm one continuous sill-pan layer with sealed corners, an intact back dam and end dams, and an open drainage outlet.
  • Jamb and head check: confirm jamb flashing or preparation and the head-flap interface are complete in the required sequence.
  • WRB integration check: confirm the surrounding WRB is cut and lapped according to the approved pattern with no reverse laps.
  • Photographic check: confirm photographs of the completed assembly are labeled and stored before the window is installed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Sill Pan Has a Seam in the Drainage Path

Probable cause: the sill flashing was cut short of the controlled length and a second piece was added to reach the far jamb.
Confirmation: inspect the sill pan for any horizontal seam within the drainage path between the back dam and the exterior edge.
Correction: remove the sill pan and reinstall it using one continuous piece cut to the correct controlled length.
Reinspection: repeat the sill-pan continuity and drainage-path check across the full width of the opening.

A Corner Shows a Wrinkle, Void, or Incomplete Coverage

Probable cause: the membrane was stretched through the corner instead of worked in by hand, or primer open time was not observed.
Confirmation: inspect the corner visually and by hand for full contact and adhesion across the entire formed area.
Correction: remove the affected section and reform the corner using the approved folding, gusset, patch, or liquid-flashing method.
Reinspection: repeat the corner-integrity check after the repair has reached the required condition.

The Drainage Outlet Has Been Sealed or Reverse-Lapped

Probable cause: the exterior sill-pan edge was taped or lapped under the WRB below it during jamb or WRB integration work.
Confirmation: inspect the exterior edge of the sill pan to confirm it remains open and laps over, not under, the WRB below the opening.
Correction: remove the sealing material or reverse lap and reinstall the edge so it discharges onto the WRB below.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage-outlet check before proceeding to jamb flashing.

The Rough Opening Fails the Pre-Flashing Recheck

Probable cause: framing or sheathing shifted during WRB installation or seam treatment, or the original Project 01 measurement was recorded incorrectly.
Confirmation: remeasure width, height, diagonals, level, plumb, and sill slope and compare against the window schedule and its permitted adjustment range.
Correction: stop flashing at this opening and obtain qualified review or correction before proceeding.
Reinspection: repeat the dimensional recheck after the documented correction is complete.

The Jamb Flashing Does Not Overlap the Sill-Pan Upturn

Probable cause: jamb flashing was measured and cut before the sill-pan height was finalized, or installed out of sequence.
Confirmation: inspect the transition between the sill-pan upturn and the base of the jamb flashing for a continuous, shingle-lapped overlap.
Correction: remove and reinstall the jamb flashing so it laps over the sill-pan upturn by the required amount.
Reinspection: repeat the jamb-to-sill transition check before integrating the surrounding WRB.

The Surrounding WRB Cut Creates a Reverse Lap

Probable cause: the opening flap was cut using the wrong pattern for the selected flashing method, or flaps were folded in the wrong order.
Confirmation: inspect every WRB flap and cut edge at the opening to confirm each higher layer overlaps the layer below it.
Correction: reposition or replace the affected flap using the approved cut pattern for the selected flashing method.
Reinspection: repeat the shingle-lap check around the full perimeter of the opening.

Cleanup

This project ends with a dimensionally correct, drained, and fully flashed rough opening and a documented flashing record, not with the window installed.

  • Remove trimmed flashing scraps, backing paper, and masking material from the sill and surrounding area
  • Clean primer or liquid-flashing residue from tools according to the manufacturer's cleanup instructions
  • Cover or otherwise protect the completed flashing from unexpected rain, wind, or handling damage before leaving the site
  • Store the rough-opening flashing record and supporting photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open discrepancy and its required decision to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Project 05 begins

Field Terms

Sill Pan
The sloped, drained flashing layer installed across the rough-opening sill and up both jambs.
Back Dam
The raised interior edge of the sill pan that prevents inward water movement.
End Dam
The raised or formed edge at each end of the sill pan that keeps water within the drainage path.
Drainage Outlet
The open exterior edge of the sill pan where water discharges onto the WRB below.
Jamb Flashing
The flashing layer or preparation applied to each vertical side of the rough opening.
Head Flap
The temporarily secured upper WRB flap preserved for head-flashing integration in a later project.
Shingle-Lap Order
The water-shedding sequence in which each higher layer overlaps the layer installed below it.
Reverse Lap
A flashing or WRB layer installed so that water is directed behind, rather than over, the layer below it; not permitted.
Rough-Opening Flashing Record
The document recording opening dimensions, flashing products, corner details, drainage-path condition, and the final inspection result.
