Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to dry-in and weatherproof the roofed 8-foot by 10-foot shed structure carried forward from Books 1, 4, and 5 — or an inspected equivalent built to the same approved project specification. The finished work product is a dried-in, clad, flashed, drained, and inspected small structure, together with a complete exterior water-control inspection record and formal handoff, ready for mechanical rough-ins and interior work.

You will obtain the capstone wall plans and schedules, confirm the framing and roofing handoff records, inspect the wall system, install the WRB in shingle-lap order, flash and set one new-construction window and one prehung exterior door, flash all approved penetrations, install the rainscreen and drainage plane, lay out and install the selected siding system, install exterior trim, install vents and utility blocks, apply designated sealant joints, complete the full exterior water-control inspection from Chapter 19, correct every punch-list item, and complete the envelope closeout.

This project does not rebuild the framing or roof system carried forward from Books 1, 4, and 5, except for authorized corrections identified during the envelope-readiness inspection. It draws directly on every competency built in Projects 1 through 19 and integrates them into one continuous, inspected envelope.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-115 — Integrate the water-resistive barrier, seam treatment, and flashing sequence across a complete small structure in bottom-to-top shingle-lap order.
  • BE-116 — Flash and install a new-construction window and a prehung exterior door in the capstone structure, including sill drainage, flange flashing, head flashing, operation checks, and interior air sealing.
  • BE-117 — Install the approved rainscreen, siding system, and exterior trim on each capstone elevation while preserving drainage and ventilation.
  • BE-118 — Flash, install, and seal approved exterior penetrations, vents, and utility blocks without blocking designed drainage or ventilation paths.
  • BE-119 — Conduct the complete exterior water-control inspection, identify and correct every deficiency, and reinspect each correction.
  • BE-120 — Complete the envelope closeout documentation and formally hand the dried-in structure to the mechanical and interior trades.

Where This Skill Fits

This project is the cumulative demonstration of every competency taught in Book 6. The sheathing inspection from Project 1, the WRB courses from Projects 2 and 3, the window and door flashing sequences from Projects 4 through 9, the rainscreen and siding layout from Projects 10 and 11, the horizontal or vertical siding installation from Project 12 or 13, the trim work from Project 14, the sealant joints from Project 15, the penetration flashing from Projects 16 and 17, and the inspection method from Project 19 are all applied here, in the correct system sequence, on one continuous structure.

The document-review, sequencing, and readiness-decision method used throughout this book — reviewing controlling documents, inspecting before covering, coordinating across chapter boundaries, and issuing a documented decision — governs this capstone project from the first document review through the final handoff.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • obtaining and confirming the capstone wall plans, window and door schedules, cladding elevations, WRB system, flashing details, rainscreen design, and penetration schedule
  • confirming the framing and roofing handoff records from Books 4 and 5 and inspecting the wall system for envelope readiness
  • installing the WRB, seam treatment, and base flashing in bottom-to-top shingle-lap order
  • flashing and installing one new-construction window and one prehung exterior door, including sill drainage, flange and head flashing, operation checks, and interior air sealing
  • preparing and flashing all approved service penetrations
  • installing the rainscreen furring, drainage mat, base venting, and insect protection
  • establishing siding control lines and installing the selected horizontal lap or vertical board-and-batten siding system on each approved elevation
  • installing exterior window, door, corner, and transition trim
  • installing exterior vents, utility blocks, and covers over completed penetration flashings
  • applying designated sealant joints while preserving every drainage and ventilation opening
  • completing the full exterior water-control inspection, correcting every punch-list item, and completing the envelope closeout

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • rebuild, reframe, or re-roof the shed structure carried forward from Books 1, 4, and 5, except for authorized corrections identified during the envelope-readiness inspection
  • mix the Chapter 12 horizontal lap siding detail and the Chapter 13 vertical board-and-batten detail on the same elevation unless the approved capstone design includes a documented transition
  • substitute caulk, foam, tape, or membrane for missing flashing, incorrect laps, or unsupported openings anywhere in the assembly
  • conceal any WRB, flashing, window, door, or penetration detail before it has passed the applicable inspection
  • represent a localized water-control observation or test as proof that the entire structure is watertight or airtight

Any condition that does not match the approved capstone plans, or that suggests structural damage, water intrusion, mold, or contamination requiring specialized handling, must stop work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

This capstone does not introduce new flashing geometry, siding details, or sealant joints beyond what Projects 1 through 19 already taught. Its difficulty comes from sequencing and coordination: keeping the WRB, window and door flashing, rainscreen, siding, trim, penetrations, and sealants in the correct system order across a complete structure, rather than as isolated exercises.

Setup Standard

Do not begin any capstone installation step until the capstone wall plans, window and door schedules, cladding elevations, WRB system, flashing details, rainscreen design, penetration schedule, and product instructions are on site and confirmed current (Step 1), and the framing and roofing handoff records from Books 4 and 5 are confirmed complete (Step 2).

Shop Tip

Work one elevation at a time from WRB through trim rather than installing the same layer on every elevation before moving to the next. This limits how much wall area is exposed to weather at once and keeps the shingle-lap sequence easier to verify.

Common Field Fundamentals

The capstone does not introduce new technical content. It requires disciplined sequencing of everything already taught: readiness inspection, WRB installation, window and door flashing, rainscreen and siding installation, trim, sealants, penetration flashing, and the full exterior water-control inspection, completed in the system order established across the book rather than chapter by chapter in isolation.

Coordinating Across Chapter Boundaries

WRB installation, seam treatment, rough-opening flashing, window or door setting, flange flashing, head flashing, and interior air sealing form one continuous system on the capstone structure and must be coordinated exactly as described in the book's System-Sequence Control section. Do not complete an entire elevation's WRB before returning to flash a window rough opening if the selected method requires jamb or head layers to be completed in a specific order relative to the WRB courses above and below the opening.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Conceal an Unpassed Detail

Do not cover any WRB course, seam, window or door flashing detail, penetration flashing, or rainscreen component with the next layer of the assembly until it has passed its applicable inspection. A concealed defect on the capstone structure is exactly the hidden water path the Step 21 exterior water-control inspection exists to find, and finding it after cladding is installed requires the controlled repair procedure from Project 18 rather than a simple correction.

Field Safety Cue — Weather Exposure Across a Multi-Day Project

Do not expose more wall area than can be made weather-resistant before conditions change. On a multi-day capstone, plan each day's work so that any wall area opened that day reaches at least WRB-complete condition, or is otherwise protected, before leaving the site.

Manufacturer Cue — Confirm Compatibility Across the Full Assembly

Before installing any layer, confirm compatibility among the WRB, self-adhered flashing, liquid flashing, sealant, foam, siding, metal flashing, treated lumber, and fasteners specified for the capstone structure. A compatible detail in isolation can still fail if a later chapter's product is incompatible with an earlier chapter's installed material.

Code Alert — Capstone Sign-Off Requirements Vary

Required inspection sign-offs, hold points, and permit requirements for the capstone structure vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction and the general contractor before treating any capstone detail as accepted without a documented inspection.

Measurement Cue — Verify Every Handoff Dimension

Before covering any framing element carried forward from Books 4 and 5, confirm that its dimensions and condition still match the handoff record. Do not assume a dimension is unchanged since the prior book was completed; remeasure rough openings, transitions, and structural connectors as part of Step 3.

E. Document Review, Readiness, and Sequencing Plan (Steps 1–6)

  1. Obtain the capstone wall plans, window and door schedules, cladding elevations, WRB system, flashing details, rainscreen design, penetration schedule, and product instructions. Confirm the revision date of each document against the project's document-control log.
  2. Confirm that the framing and roofing handoff records from Books 4 and 5 are complete. Verify the handoff record shows the required structural and roofing approvals before proceeding.
  3. Inspect wall sheathing, structural connectors, rough openings, roof-to-wall transitions, foundation or floor-platform interface, and exterior access. Apply the Project 1 inspection method and issue an updated envelope-readiness decision for the capstone structure.
  4. Prepare a complete material, tool, safety, weather-protection, access, staging, and inspection plan. Sequence the plan by elevation so each opened wall area reaches WRB-complete condition or is otherwise protected before the end of each work session.
  5. Identify the selected cladding system for each elevation and document any approved transitions. Confirm whether each elevation uses the Chapter 12 horizontal lap system, the Chapter 13 vertical board-and-batten system, or an approved documented transition between them.
  6. Correct authorized sheathing and opening deficiencies before covering the walls. Remeasure any corrected rough opening or wall area before proceeding to WRB installation.

F. Water-Resistive Barrier and Base Flashing (Steps 7–8)

  1. Install base flashing and the WRB in bottom-to-top shingle-lap order. Apply the Project 2 course, corner, lap, and fastening method to every elevation.
  2. Wrap corners, treat transitions, protect openings, and complete WRB seam and damage repairs. Apply the Project 3 seam-taping, penetration-treatment, and repair method before any opening is flashed.

G. Window and Door Installation (Steps 9–12)

  1. Flash the window rough opening and install the specified new-construction window. Apply the Project 4 sill-pan flashing method, then the Project 5 setting, shimming, and fastening method.
  2. Complete window flange flashing, head flashing, sill drainage, operation checks, and interior air sealing. Apply the Project 6 exterior flashing and interior air-seal method without blocking sill-pan drainage.
  3. Install the exterior-door sill pan and set the specified prehung door. Apply the Project 7 sill-pan method, then the Project 8 setting, shimming, and fastening method.
  4. Complete door jamb flashing, head flashing, threshold sealing, operation checks, and interior air sealing. Apply the Project 9 exterior flashing and interior air-seal method without blocking threshold drainage.

H. Penetrations, Rainscreen, and Siding Layout (Steps 13–15)

  1. Prepare and flash all approved pipe, duct, cable, electrical, and fixture penetrations. Apply the Project 16 shingle-lap penetration-flashing method before the associated utility block or fixture is installed.
  2. Install the approved rainscreen furring, drainage mat, base venting, and insect protection. Apply the Project 10 method across every elevation, preserving the drainage gap around openings, corners, and penetrations.
  3. Establish siding starter, corner, opening, and course control lines. Apply the Project 11 layout method, confirming level, plumb, and drainage-opening protection at every reference line.

I. Siding, Trim, and Penetration Finishing (Steps 16–19)

  1. Install the selected horizontal lap or vertical board-and-batten siding system on each approved elevation. Apply the Project 12 method or the Project 13 method as selected for that elevation, without mixing details across an elevation unless an approved transition is documented.
  2. Install exterior window, door, corner, and transition trim in the required sequence. Apply the Project 14 method, preserving head-flashing projection and sill drainage at every opening.
  3. Install exterior vents, utility blocks, and covers over completed penetration flashings. Apply the Project 17 method, confirming free movement of every damper, louver, or cover.
  4. Apply designated sealant joints using approved joint geometry while preserving every drainage and ventilation opening. Apply the Project 15 method, sealing only the joints identified on the approved sealant schedule.

J. Full Inspection, Punch List, and Closeout (Steps 20–27)

  1. Inspect each elevation during installation for laps, fastening, alignment, clearances, damage, and drainage continuity. Correct any deficiency found during installation before proceeding to the next layer on that elevation.
  2. Complete the full exterior water-control inspection from Chapter 19. Apply the Project 19 inspection method to the entire capstone structure, using concealed-work photographs and records to verify covered details.
  3. Prepare a punch list identifying location, condition, required correction, responsible party, and reinspection method. Include every deficiency found during the Step 21 inspection, whether structural, water-control, or documentation-related.
  4. Correct every failed item and repeat the applicable inspection. Do not close out any punch-list item without a documented reinspection result.
  5. Conduct approved localized operation, drainage, or water-control observations after product cure conditions are satisfied. Follow the defined protocol with interior monitoring and stop conditions; do not represent the result as proof the entire structure is watertight or airtight.
  6. Clean the exterior, remove unauthorized temporary materials, and protect the completed envelope. Confirm the site is free of debris, cut-offs, and temporary flashing or covering materials before documentation.
  7. Compile product records, measurements, photographs, concealed-work evidence, inspection results, corrections, maintenance requirements, and warranty information. Assemble the complete capstone documentation package referenced throughout this project.
  8. Complete the envelope closeout and formally hand the dried-in structure to the mechanical and interior trades. Issue a pass, conditional-pass, or failed-envelope decision and record the responsible party for any remaining open item.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed capstone structure using the same checks regardless of which elevation or siding system it uses.

  • Readiness check: confirm the wall sheathing, structural connectors, and rough openings were accepted before WRB installation and that the handoff records from Books 4 and 5 were confirmed complete.
  • WRB and flashing check: confirm continuous shingle-lap coverage, treated seams, and correctly flashed window and door openings across every elevation.
  • Rainscreen and cladding check: confirm the drainage and ventilation path is unbroken behind correctly installed siding and trim.
  • Penetration check: confirm every service penetration, vent, and utility block is flashed, supported, and operational without blocked drainage.
  • Sealant check: confirm sealant is present only at designated joints and that every drainage and ventilation opening remains clear.
  • Closeout check: confirm the punch list is fully corrected and reinspected, and the envelope closeout record is signed before formal handoff.

Complete the Envelope Closeout Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before handing the structure to the mechanical and interior trades.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A WRB Course or Seam Fails Inspection After Rainscreen Furring Is Installed

Probable cause: a seam or lap was covered before it was inspected, or furring fasteners penetrated a seam that had not fully cured.
Confirmation: compare the concealed-work photographs from Step 8 against the current condition; remove furring locally if the record is incomplete.
Correction: apply the Project 18 controlled-repair procedure to expose, repair, and reinspect the affected WRB before reinstalling furring.
Reinspection: repeat the WRB continuity check at the repaired location and confirm the furring reinstallation does not repeat the same fastener conflict.

The Window or Door Binds After Siding and Trim Are Installed

Probable cause: trim or siding fasteners were driven into the frame or shim area, or the frame was compressed during trim installation.
Confirmation: recheck operation against the Project 5 or Project 8 acceptance standards and inspect fastener locations near the frame.
Correction: remove the conflicting fastener, correct the shim or frame condition, and reinstall trim without compressing the rainscreen cavity.
Reinspection: repeat the operation check before proceeding to the Step 21 full inspection.

A Siding Transition Between Elevations Does Not Match an Approved Detail

Probable cause: horizontal lap siding and vertical board-and-batten siding were mixed on adjacent elevations without a documented transition detail.
Confirmation: compare the installed transition against the capstone plans and the System-Sequence Control requirement that mixed details require an approved design.
Correction: remove and correct the unauthorized transition using the approved detail, or separate the two systems onto non-adjacent elevations if no transition is approved.
Reinspection: repeat the layout and drainage check at the corrected transition.

A Penetration Flashing Is Found Blocked or Reverse-Lapped During the Full Inspection

Probable cause: the utility block or vent was installed before its flashing fully cured, or the flashing layers were installed out of shingle-lap order.
Confirmation: inspect visible flashing edges and the Step 13 concealed-work photographs for lap order and drainage direction.
Correction: remove the vent or utility block, correct the flashing in the required bottom-to-top order, and reinstall the component.
Reinspection: repeat the penetration-flashing check and confirm the vent or utility block operates freely afterward.

Sealant Is Found at a Drainage Opening During the Full Inspection

Probable cause: sealant was applied beyond its designated joint, covering a weep, lap, or ventilation opening.
Confirmation: compare the sealed location against the approved sealant schedule from Project 15.
Correction: cut out the unauthorized sealant and confirm the drainage or ventilation opening is fully restored; do not smear additional sealant over the defect.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage and ventilation check at the corrected location.

A Punch-List Item Cannot Be Verified as Corrected

Probable cause: the correction was completed without a documented reinspection, or the original deficiency location was not clearly recorded.
Confirmation: compare the punch list against the available reinspection records and photographs for that location.
Correction: reinspect the location directly before closing the item; do not close a punch-list item on the basis of a verbal report alone.
Reinspection: document the reinspection result on the punch list before proceeding to Step 27.
