Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install horizontal lap siding on one prepared wall elevation, using the starter strip, corner trim, and layout references established in Project 11, over the completed rainscreen, WRB, flashing, and opening trim from earlier projects in this book. The finished work product is a straight, correctly exposed, drained, and securely fastened horizontal lap-siding elevation ready for the exterior sealant and penetration work covered later in this book.

You will review the siding product instructions and confirm that the starter, corners, opening trim, flashing, and rainscreen have passed inspection, then stage and inspect the siding material, measure and install the first course against the layout references, install successive courses at the required exposure and overlap, stagger and support joints, install any required joint accessories, maintain expansion gaps and drainage clearances, install the top termination, and inspect and document the completed elevation.

This project follows Project 11, which established the starter, corner, and layout references this project depends on, and Project 14, which installs exterior trim around windows and doors, coordinates directly with the siding fit completed here. Chapter 13 covers a separate approved cladding system, vertical board-and-batten siding, for a different wall or practice elevation; the two systems are not mixed on the same elevation unless the approved design includes a transition.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-067 — Cut horizontal lap siding boards to the required length, reference face, and edge treatment.
  • BE-068 — Maintain the approved exposure and overlap for every course across the elevation.
  • BE-069 — Fasten siding correctly into approved backing at the specified location, spacing, and seating condition.
  • BE-070 — Stagger butt joints and locate them over approved support across the elevation.
  • BE-071 — Leave required expansion gaps at trim, openings, corners, and transitions.
  • BE-072 — Inspect siding alignment, exposure, fastening, joint distribution, and drainage clearance across the completed elevation.

Where This Skill Fits

The layout references established in Project 11 only produce a level, plumb elevation if every course installed here holds its exposure and stays aligned to the control lines. The clearances, joint distribution, and fastening pattern completed in this project are what the Chapter 19 full exterior water-control inspection checks directly, and any drift, tight joint, or blocked drainage gap introduced here becomes a visible or hidden defect by the time trim, sealant, and penetration work in Projects 14 through 17 are complete.

The method used here — confirming prior inspections, staging material, working from fixed control references, checking alignment at a defined interval, and inspecting before concealment — is the same method used in Chapter 13 for vertical board-and-batten siding and at the Chapter 20 capstone elevation.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the siding product instructions, exposure, overlap, fastening method, joint treatment, end gaps, and storage limits
  • confirming that the starter, corners, opening trim, flashing, and rainscreen have passed inspection
  • inspecting, staging, and selecting siding material to distribute color, texture, and joints
  • measuring, marking, and cutting siding boards from the designated reference face and end
  • installing the first course and successive courses at the required exposure, overlap, and alignment
  • fastening siding into approved backing at the specified location, spacing, and seating condition
  • staggering joints, installing required joint accessories, and maintaining expansion gaps and clearances
  • installing the top termination and inspecting and documenting the completed elevation

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select a different siding product, exposure, or fastening schedule independently of the approved specification
  • begin siding installation before the starter, corners, opening trim, flashing, and rainscreen have passed inspection
  • seal, tape, or foam over a missing joint accessory, blocked drainage gap, or unsupported joint instead of correcting it
  • mix horizontal lap siding details with the Chapter 13 vertical board-and-batten system on the same elevation without an approved transition detail

Any condition that does not match the approved siding specification, or that suggests damaged rainscreen, blocked drainage, or an unsupported layout reference, must stop work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Every course installed in this project depends on the level base reference, starter engagement, and corner projection established in Project 11. Recheck the control lines at the start of this project rather than assuming they remain accurate; a drifted reference discovered halfway up the elevation is far more costly to correct than one caught before the first course is fastened.

Setup Standard

Do not begin siding installation until the siding product instructions, exposure, overlap, fastening method, and joint treatment are confirmed against the approved specification (Step 1), and the starter, corners, opening trim, flashing, and rainscreen inspections from Projects 10 and 11 are confirmed passed (Step 2).

Shop Tip

Select and lay out boards for an entire course, or several courses, along the ground before fastening any of them. Checking color, texture, and joint distribution across the whole elevation at once, rather than board by board as you fasten, avoids a visibly clustered joint pattern that is difficult to correct once courses above it are installed.

Every course installed in this project follows the same order: confirm the control line and prior inspections, stage and select material, measure and cut from the reference face and end, dry-check exposure and joint position, fasten into approved backing, and check alignment before starting the next course. Holding Exposure Across the Elevation

The approved exposure is set once, at the first course, against the control line established in Project 11. Every later course is measured and checked against that same reference, not against the course immediately below it, so that small errors do not accumulate into visible drift by the top of the elevation. Recheck alignment against the control lines at the interval specified in Step 15 rather than only at the start and end of the wall.

Measurement Cue — Recheck Alignment at the Specified Interval

Check each course against the transferred control lines at the interval specified in Step 15, not only at wall corners. A course that drifts gradually across a long wall can appear level at each end while being visibly out of level at the midpoint. Correct drift at the course where it is found rather than allowing it to carry into the next course.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Overdrive or Restrict Fasteners

Do not overdrive siding fasteners, and do not nail through prohibited areas identified by the siding manufacturer. Overdriven fasteners crush the siding face and compromise its ability to move with temperature and moisture change; fasteners placed through prohibited zones can crack the material or block the required movement that keeps the elevation from buckling.

Field Safety Cue — Elevated Course Work

Use only approved ladders, scaffolds, lifts, or work platforms as course height increases up the elevation. Do not stand on siding materials, unsecured platforms, or makeshift supports. Reposition access equipment as work progresses rather than reaching beyond a safe working stance.

Manufacturer Cue — Cutting, Fastening, and Edge Treatment

Confirm the specified blade, cutting tool, dust-control method, fastener type, spacing, and edge-treatment requirement against the siding manufacturer's current installation instructions before cutting or fastening any board. Do not substitute a generic fastener or omit a required edge sealer, primer, or coating on field-cut ends.

Code Alert — Clearances Above Grade, Roofs, and Paving

Required clearances between the bottom of the siding and grade, roofs, decks, and paving vary by adopted code and the siding manufacturer's instructions. Verify the current required clearance with the authority having jurisdiction and the approved specification before establishing the bottom of the elevation.

E. Document Review and Material Preparation (Steps 1–5)

  1. Review the siding product instructions, exposure, overlap, blind- or face-fastening method, joint treatment, end gaps, paint or sealing requirements, and storage limits. Confirm the current manufacturer instructions match the product staged for this elevation.
  2. Confirm the starter, corners, opening trim, flashing, and rainscreen have passed inspection. Verify each prior inspection record shows the required approval before uncovering or disturbing any of those components.
  3. Inspect siding for damage, moisture condition, coating defects, straightness, and product identification. Set aside any board that fails inspection rather than installing it and marking it for later replacement.
  4. Store and stage siding flat, dry, supported, and protected from soil and weather. Confirm staging keeps the material within the manufacturer's exposure limits before installation.
  5. Select boards or courses to distribute color, texture, and joints according to the approved appearance plan. Lay out an entire course, or several courses, before fastening any board to confirm the distribution across the full elevation.

F. First Course and Cutting (Steps 6–9)

  1. Measure the first course from controlled corner and trim references. Use the corner projection and control line established in Project 11, not a field estimate.
  2. Mark cuts using the designated reference face and end. Follow the siding manufacturer's specified reference face and end for every marked cut.
  3. Support the material and cut it using the specified blade, tool, dust control, and edge-treatment method. Support both sides of the cut to prevent binding or splintering.
  4. Seal, prime, or coat field-cut edges where required. Apply the approved edge treatment before the board is installed, not after.

G. Course Installation and Fastening (Steps 10–13)

  1. Install the first course fully engaged with the starter and aligned to the level control line. Confirm full engagement along the entire course length before fastening.
  2. Maintain required clearances at roofs, decks, grade, paving, flashing, and trim. Verify each clearance against the approved specification before proceeding to the next course.
  3. Fasten into the approved backing with the specified corrosion-resistant fasteners, location, spacing, and seating condition. Confirm each fastener penetrates the specified backing, not only the siding and furring.
  4. Do not overdrive fasteners, restrict required movement, or nail through prohibited areas. Adjust tool pressure or depth setting and correct any overdriven fastener immediately.

H. Successive Courses and Joint Management (Steps 14–19)

  1. Install successive courses with the required exposure and overlap. Measure exposure from the control line, not from the course immediately below.
  2. Check alignment against control lines at the specified interval. Correct drift at the course where it is found before continuing upward.
  3. Stagger butt joints and locate them over approved support. Confirm no two adjacent courses share a joint location within the approved minimum offset.
  4. Install joint flashing, splines, sealant, or proprietary joiners only where the siding system requires them. Do not add sealant at a joint the approved system leaves open.
  5. Maintain expansion gaps at trim, openings, corners, and transitions. Verify each gap against the approved specification before fastening the adjacent board.
  6. Fit courses around penetrations and mounting blocks without blocking drainage. Confirm the rainscreen drainage path remains open at every penetration the course passes.

I. Termination, Inspection, and Documentation (Steps 20–23)

  1. Install the top termination using the approved flashing, trim, or concealed fastening method. Confirm the termination detail matches the approved specification for this elevation.
  2. Inspect exposure, level, joint distribution, fastening, edge treatment, clearances, drainage, and surface damage. Complete this inspection across the full elevation before considering it finished.
  3. Correct drift, cracked boards, overdriven fasteners, tight joints, reverse laps, and unsupported ends. Reinspect each corrected location against the Acceptance Standards table.
  4. Clean the elevation and document the completed installation. Photograph the full elevation and label each photograph with its location before closing out this project.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed elevation using the same checks regardless of wall length or number of openings.

  • Exposure check: confirm every course holds the approved exposure against the transferred control lines.
  • Alignment check: confirm no course drifts beyond the approved tolerance at the specified check interval.
  • Fastening check: confirm every fastener is seated at the approved location and depth without overdriving or restricting movement.
  • Joint check: confirm butt joints are staggered per the approved pattern and located over approved support.
  • Clearance and drainage check: confirm required clearances and expansion gaps are maintained and that rainscreen drainage remains unobstructed.
  • Documentation check: confirm the siding installation record, photographs, and any corrections are complete.

Complete the Siding Installation Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 13 or Project 14 on this elevation.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Courses Drift Out of Level Across a Long Wall

Probable cause: exposure was measured from the course below rather than from the transferred control line, allowing small errors to accumulate.
Confirmation: check the affected courses against the original control lines at several points along the wall, not only at the corners.
Correction: remove and reinstall the affected courses, remeasuring exposure from the control line rather than the adjacent course.
Reinspection: recheck alignment across the full elevation at the specified interval after correction.

Butt Joints Cluster Instead of Staggering

Probable cause: boards were installed in the order received rather than selected to distribute joint locations.
Confirmation: map joint locations across several courses and compare the offset against the approved minimum stagger.
Correction: remove and reposition boards at the affected courses so joints meet the approved offset and remain over approved support.
Reinspection: recheck joint distribution across the full elevation after correction.

Fasteners Are Overdriven or Placed Through Prohibited Areas

Probable cause: nailer pressure or depth setting was not adjusted for the siding thickness, or fastener locations were not verified against the manufacturer's prohibited zones.
Confirmation: inspect fastener seating and location across the affected course against the approved fastening schedule.
Correction: remove and replace overdriven or misplaced fasteners at the correct location, spacing, and seating depth.
Reinspection: recheck the full course for seating condition and movement after correction.

A Course Blocks the Rainscreen Drainage Path at a Penetration

Probable cause: the course was fitted tightly around a penetration or mounting block without confirming the drainage gap behind it.
Confirmation: inspect the drainage gap at the penetration and compare it against the rainscreen specification from Project 10.
Correction: remove and refit the affected siding to restore the required drainage gap; do not seal or foam over a blocked gap instead of correcting the fit.
Reinspection: recheck the drainage path at the penetration after correction.

Expansion Gaps Are Missing at Trim, Corners, or Openings

Probable cause: boards were cut tight to trim or corner boards without measuring the required gap.
Confirmation: measure the gap at each affected location and compare against the approved specification.
Correction: remove and recut the affected board to restore the required gap.
Reinspection: recheck gaps at all trim, corner, and opening locations on the elevation after correction.

A Board Cracks or Splits During or After Installation

Probable cause: the board was fastened too close to its end without the required end gap, was overdriven, or was restrained at an expansion gap.
Confirmation: inspect the fastener locations and adjacent clearances at the cracked board.
Correction: remove and replace the cracked board, correcting the fastener location, end gap, or expansion clearance that caused the failure.
Reinspection: recheck the replacement board and adjacent courses after correction.

Cleanup

This project ends with a straight, correctly exposed, drained, and securely fastened horizontal lap-siding elevation, not with exterior trim, sealant joints, or penetration covers installed.

  • Clear cutoffs, packaging, and fastener debris from the base of the elevation and surrounding grade
  • Store unused siding, fasteners, and joint accessories per the manufacturer's requirements for later elevations
  • Confirm expansion gaps, drainage openings, and weep paths remain visibly open before leaving the site
  • Store the siding installation record and supporting photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open discrepancy and its required correction to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Project 14 begins on this elevation
