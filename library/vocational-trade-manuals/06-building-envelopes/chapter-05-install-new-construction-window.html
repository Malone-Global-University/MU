Job Overview

This project qualifies you to set, align, fasten, and function-test one new-construction flanged window in a rough opening that has already been flashed and accepted under Project 04. The finished work product is a securely installed, square, level, plumb, and operational window, together with a completed installation record, ready for the exterior flange flashing, head flashing, and interior air sealing performed in Project 06.

You will review the manufacturer's installation instructions, inspect the window unit for damage, confirm the rough-opening flashing has passed inspection, dry-fit the unit, correct any authorized opening or flashing conditions, apply the specified sealant or bedding material while preserving drainage, set the unit into the opening, check level, plumb, square, and projection, install shims at approved locations, recheck operation, fasten the flange in the required sequence, and complete a documented installation record.

This project follows directly from Project 04, which flashed and drained the rough opening this window now occupies, and it precedes Project 06, which completes the exterior water-control layers and interior air seal around the unit installed here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-025 — Dry-fit a new-construction flanged window in a flashed rough opening and verify perimeter clearance, sill bearing, flange contact, and exterior projection before permanent fastening.
  • BE-026 — Shim a new-construction window at approved structural and fastener locations without bowing, racking, or distorting the frame.
  • BE-027 — Level and plumb a new-construction window frame, confirming sill level, jamb plumb, head level, frame square, and diagonal equality against the manufacturer's tolerances.
  • BE-028 — Fasten a new-construction window's nailing flange in the manufacturer's required sequence, fastener type, embedment, and spacing without fastening through prohibited drainage or movement zones.
  • BE-029 — Check sash or panel operation, weep-opening clearance, and sill-pan drainage before and after final fastening, and adjust the installation if the unit binds, racks, bows, or fails to latch.
  • BE-030 — Inspect and document the completed window installation, including unit identification, fasteners, measurements, adjustments, and photographs.

Where This Skill Fits

The rough-opening flashing installed and inspected in Project 04 only protects the wall if the window set into it remains square, plumb, properly shimmed, and correctly fastened. A frame that is bowed, racked, or fastened through a prohibited zone can compromise the sill-pan drainage and jamb flashing before the exterior water-control layers in Project 06 are ever installed. The alignment and fastening standards demonstrated here are the same standards verified later during the Project 19 full exterior water-control inspection and the Project 20 capstone.

This project also establishes the handling, dry-fit, and correction sequence used again in Project 8 when setting the prehung exterior door, and the shim and fastening discipline used throughout the rest of this book wherever a manufactured unit is set into a flashed opening.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the window manufacturer's installation instructions, fastener type, flange fastening pattern, shim locations, opening clearances, sealant locations, and warranty requirements
  • inspecting the window unit for shipping damage, cracked glass, distorted frame, blocked weeps, missing components, and correct size or handing
  • confirming that the rough-opening flashing from Project 04 has passed inspection and remains undamaged
  • dry-fitting the unit and verifying perimeter clearance, sill bearing, flange contact, exterior projection, and interior alignment
  • applying the specified sealant bead or bedding material at approved flange or sill locations while preserving required drainage gaps
  • setting, leveling, plumbing, squaring, shimming, and permanently fastening the unit
  • checking sash or panel operation before and after final fastening
  • documenting unit identification, fasteners, measurements, adjustments, and photographs

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • install a window unit into a rough opening that has not passed the Project 04 flashing inspection or that shows damaged or incomplete flashing
  • modify the rough opening, framing, headers, or structural components to fit an incorrectly sized window
  • substitute a fastener type, spacing, or embedment other than the one specified by the window manufacturer
  • seal, tape, or otherwise cover the exterior flange, head flashing, or interior air seal, all of which are completed in Project 06
  • proceed with permanent fastening if the unit binds, racks, bows, fails to latch, or shows blocked weeps at any point in the sequence

Any condition that suggests the rough opening or its flashing does not match Project 04's accepted condition must stop work at that opening until the discrepancy is resolved.

Trade Note

A window that is fastened while out of square or without the manufacturer's specified shim pattern can bind or fail to latch years after installation, long after the exterior flashing and siding have concealed the cause. Confirming square, level, plumb, and free operation before and after final fastening in this project is the last opportunity to catch that condition without removing finished cladding.

Setup Standard

Do not remove the window from its packaging or begin dry-fit until the manufacturer's installation instructions, fastener schedule, shim locations, and sealant locations are confirmed on site (Step 1), and until the Project 04 rough-opening flashing inspection record is confirmed passed and undamaged (Step 3).

Shop Tip

Dry-fit the window without sealant first, exactly as Step 5 requires. Committing to a sealant bead before confirming clearance, sill bearing, and flange contact is one of the most common causes of a window that must be pulled and reset, damaging the flashing installed in Project 04 in the process.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every manufactured-unit installation in this book — this project's window, the Project 8 prehung door — follows the same sequence: confirm the opening and its flashing are ready, inspect the unit before handling, dry-fit without sealant, correct only authorized conditions, apply sealant at approved locations only, set and temporarily secure the unit, level and plumb it, shim at approved locations, recheck operation, fasten permanently in the required sequence, recheck the finished condition, and document it. Learning that sequence here protects every later unit installation in this book.

Dry-Fit Before Commitment

The dry-fit in Step 5 is not optional and is not a visual estimate. Set the unit into the opening without sealant, check every clearance and bearing point named in the Acceptance Standards table, and only then remove the unit to apply sealant. A unit that is bedded in sealant before its fit is confirmed cannot be adjusted without damaging the sealant bead and, in some cases, the rough-opening flashing beneath it.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Square by Diagonal Measurement, Not by Eye

Check frame square by comparing both corner-to-corner diagonal measurements. Equal diagonals within the manufacturer's permitted tolerance confirm square; unequal diagonals indicate the frame is out of square even if the sides appear level and plumb individually. Do not rely on a visual check alone.

Critical Safety Cue — Controlled Handling of Glass Units

Move and set the window using the manufacturer-required number of workers and handling points. Do not lift or set a glass unit alone if the manufacturer specifies additional workers, and do not rest a unit on an unsupported edge or corner. A dropped or racked unit can crack under load without immediate visible damage.

Field Safety Cue — Working at the Opening

Confirm safe, guarded access at the opening before handling the unit, and do not stand on the sill, sill-pan flashing, or unsecured platforms while setting or fastening the window. Keep the work area around the opening clear of packaging, tools, and debris that could cause a fall while handling a heavy unit.

Manufacturer Cue — Fastener Type, Spacing, and Embedment

Install only the flange fastener type, length, spacing, and embedment specified by the window manufacturer, in the sequence the manufacturer requires. Do not substitute a longer or shorter fastener, skip a specified location, or overdrive a fastener head into the flange.

Code Alert — Egress, Fall-Prevention, and Energy Requirements Vary

Egress opening size, fall-prevention hardware, and energy-performance requirements for windows vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement for this opening's use and location with the authority having jurisdiction before treating the scheduled unit as compliant.

E. Review and Unit Inspection (Steps 1–2)

  1. Review the window installation instructions, fastener type, flange fastening pattern, shim locations, opening clearances, sealant locations, and warranty requirements. Confirm the instructions match the scheduled unit's model and configuration.
  2. Inspect the window for shipping damage, cracked glass, distorted frame, blocked weeps, missing components, and correct size or handing. Compare the unit's identification label against the window schedule before proceeding.

F. Opening Confirmation and Dry-Fit (Steps 3–6)

  1. Confirm that the rough-opening flashing has passed inspection and remains undamaged. Review the Project 04 inspection record and visually confirm the sill pan, jamb, and head preparation are intact.
  2. Remove temporary packaging only as directed and keep operating units locked or braced during handling. Follow the manufacturer's packaging-removal sequence to avoid dropping braced sash or panels.
  3. Dry-fit the window without sealant and verify perimeter clearance, sill bearing, flange contact, exterior projection, and interior alignment. Record each measurement against the manufacturer's specified values.
  4. Remove the unit and correct only authorized opening or flashing conditions. Do not proceed to sealant application until every dry-fit measurement is within tolerance.

G. Sealant, Setting, and Alignment (Steps 7–11)

  1. Apply the specified sealant bead or bedding material at the approved flange or sill locations while leaving required drainage gaps open. Confirm the bead location matches the manufacturer's diagram before setting the unit.
  2. Set the window into the opening using the required number of workers and handling points. Lift and set the unit using only the manufacturer-designated handling points.
  3. Center the unit and place temporary fasteners at the manufacturer-approved locations. Use temporary fasteners only at the locations specified for holding the unit during alignment.
  4. Check sill level, jamb plumb, head level, frame square, diagonal equality, and specified projection. Record each measurement on the window installation record.
  5. Install shims at approved structural and fastener locations without bowing the frame. Use paired, tapered shims and check that the frame remains straight after each shim is installed.

H. Fastening and Operation Verification (Steps 12–19)

  1. Recheck sash or panel operation before final fastening. Operate the sash or panel through its full range and confirm smooth movement before committing to permanent fasteners.
  2. Install permanent flange fasteners in the required sequence, type, embedment, and spacing. Follow the manufacturer's fastening sequence exactly, working from the location specified as the starting point.
  3. Keep fastener heads seated without crushing, cracking, or distorting the flange. Stop driving each fastener at the manufacturer's specified seating condition.
  4. Do not fasten through prohibited drainage or movement zones. Confirm the fastening pattern against the manufacturer's diagram before driving each fastener.
  5. Recheck frame dimensions, reveal, lock engagement, sash movement, and weather-strip contact after each fastening phase. Confirm the frame has not shifted out of tolerance during fastening.
  6. Adjust shims or fasteners if the unit binds, racks, bows, or fails to latch. Correct the condition before continuing to the next fastening phase.
  7. Confirm that weep openings and sill-pan drainage remain unobstructed. Inspect the sill for shim material, sealant, or debris blocking the drainage path.
  8. Complete the manufacturer-required operation and installation inspection. Perform every operation check the manufacturer specifies before considering the unit installed.

I. Protection and Documentation (Steps 20–21)

  1. Protect the installed unit from impact, contamination, and premature interior finishing. Cover or otherwise shield the unit until Project 06 begins.
  2. Record unit identification, fasteners, measurements, adjustments, and photographs. Complete the window installation record before leaving the opening.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed window installation using the same checks regardless of which opening it covers.

  • Unit check: confirm the installed unit matches the window schedule for size, handing, and configuration, and is free of shipping damage.
  • Alignment check: confirm sill level, jamb plumb, head level, frame square, and diagonal equality are within the manufacturer's tolerance.
  • Shim and fastening check: confirm shims are placed at approved locations without bowing the frame, and flange fasteners are seated per the manufacturer's schedule.
  • Operation check: confirm the sash or panel operates freely, locks engage, and weep openings and sill-pan drainage remain unobstructed.
  • Documentation check: confirm unit identification, fastener record, measurements, adjustments, and photographs are complete on the window installation record.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Frame Diagonals Are Unequal

Probable cause: the frame was fastened before square was confirmed, or a shim was installed unevenly at one corner.
Confirmation: remeasure both diagonals and compare against the manufacturer's permitted tolerance.
Correction: loosen the affected fasteners, adjust shims, and re-square the frame before refastening.
Reinspection: recheck both diagonals and sash operation after correction.

The Sash or Panel Binds After Fastening

Probable cause: an overdriven fastener, an uneven shim, or a frame that bowed during final fastening.
Confirmation: operate the sash or panel through its full range and identify the point of contact or resistance.
Correction: back off the fastener causing the distortion, adjust the shim at that location, and recheck frame dimensions.
Reinspection: repeat the operation check and frame-dimension check after correction.

A Weep Opening or Sill-Pan Drainage Path Is Blocked

Probable cause: sealant, shim material, or debris was left in the sill drainage path during setting or fastening.
Confirmation: inspect the sill weep openings and sill-pan outlet for obstruction.
Correction: remove the obstruction without damaging the sill pan or flange, and confirm the drainage path is fully open.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage check before the unit is considered complete.

The Flange Is Cracked or Distorted at a Fastener

Probable cause: a fastener was overdriven, or the wrong fastener length or type was used at that location.
Confirmation: inspect the flange surface at each fastener location for cracking, crushing, or distortion.
Correction: remove the fastener, inspect the flange for damage extent, and refasten at the manufacturer's specified depth and, if necessary, an approved adjacent location.
Reinspection: recheck flange condition and fastener seating at the corrected location.

The Unit Does Not Match the Dry-Fit Clearance After Sealant Is Applied

Probable cause: the sealant bead location shifted the unit's bearing, or the unit was reset in a different position than during dry-fit.
Confirmation: remeasure perimeter clearance, sill bearing, and flange contact against the dry-fit measurements recorded in Step 5.
Correction: remove and reset the unit using the same reference points established during dry-fit.
Reinspection: repeat the clearance and bearing check before proceeding to permanent fastening.
