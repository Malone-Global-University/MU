Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to build, seal, insulate, support, test, balance, document, and close out one complete practice air-distribution system. The finished work product is one accessible practice duct system consisting of a supply plenum or trunk, takeoff collars, a rigid or flexible supply branch, a supply register boot and register, a return-air path and grille, mechanically fastened and sealed joints, insulated duct sections, supported and balanced branches, and a completed closeout record, connected only to the approved listed blower or qualified training blower specified by the practice-system drawing.

You will stage all required components; assemble the supply plenum or trunk; install and fasten takeoff collars; install the return path and grille; connect and support the supply branch; seal every required joint using Chapter 6 methods; insulate the required duct sections using Chapter 7 methods; install registers and grilles; run a full leakage and airflow test using the listed blower or test-air source; balance the system using Chapter 11 methods; correct any failed item; and compile a complete closeout record.

This project integrates the duct-sealing skill from Chapter 6, the duct-insulation skill from Chapter 7, the register-boot skill from Chapter 8, the return-air-path skill from Chapter 9, the flexible-duct skill from Chapter 10, and the balancing skill from Chapter 11 into one complete, testable air-distribution system. It does not introduce new duct-construction methods beyond what those chapters already establish.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HV-115 — Integrate supply duct, return path, register boot, and flexible-duct components into one complete practice air-distribution assembly built from the approved drawing and duct schedule.
  • HV-116 — Seal every required duct, boot, collar, and penetration joint using the Chapter 6 mastic and listed-tape procedure.
  • HV-117 — Insulate the required duct sections, fittings, and boots using the Chapter 7 insulation and vapor-facing procedure.
  • HV-118 — Balance airflow across the completed system using the Chapter 11 damper and register procedure without exceeding the approved adjustment range.
  • HV-119 — Inspect and test the completed system for leakage, support, insulation continuity, and airflow delivery using the listed blower or test-air source.
  • HV-120 — Complete the HVAC closeout record, punch list, and handoff documentation for the finished practice system.

Where This Skill Fits

This capstone draws directly on the HVAC system map and identification skill from Chapter 1, the shutdown and readiness procedure from Chapter 2, the duct-inspection and diagnosis skill from Chapter 5, the duct-sealing procedure from Chapter 6, the duct-insulation procedure from Chapter 7, the register-boot procedure from Chapter 8, the return-air-path procedure from Chapter 9, the flexible-duct procedure from Chapter 10, and the airflow-balancing procedure from Chapter 11. It is the final project in this book and does not lead into a later chapter within Book 9.

The completed practice system remains a useful reference fixture. It demonstrates a fully sealed, insulated, supported, and balanced duct assembly that can be referenced for training, inspection practice, or future coursework, provided it is never connected to a fuel-fired furnace, heat-pump refrigerant circuit, central air conditioner, or occupied-building air handler without separate professional design, permitting, inspection, and commissioning.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • obtaining and confirming the approved practice-system drawing, duct schedule, register schedule, and blower or test-air specification
  • assembling and fastening the supply plenum or trunk, takeoff collars, return path, register boot, and branch duct exactly as specified by the approved drawing
  • sealing every required joint, seam, boot, collar, and penetration using listed mastic and tape
  • insulating every required duct section, fitting, boot, and transition
  • installing registers and grilles, connecting the listed blower or test-air source, and running the required leakage and airflow tests
  • balancing airflow using small, incremental damper and register adjustments
  • correcting failed joints, supports, insulation, or balance conditions and repeating the affected test
  • compiling the complete closeout record, punch list, and handoff documentation

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • connect the practice system to a gas furnace, oil furnace, heat pump refrigerant circuit, central air conditioner, or any occupied-building air handler
  • design or resize the duct schedule, register schedule, or blower specification independently of the approved practice-system drawing
  • invent duct sizes, airflow targets, support spacing, insulation values, or damper positions not supplied by the approved drawing
  • bypass, remove, or defeat any blower guard, cover, disconnect, or emergency shutdown control
  • conceal any joint, seam, support, or insulation section before the required leakage and airflow tests have passed

The practice system must remain accessible for inspection at all times. Connecting this assembly to a fuel-fired appliance, a refrigerant circuit, or an occupied-building air handler requires separate professional design, permitting, inspection, and commissioning and is outside the scope of this project.

Trade Note

This capstone is an integration project, not an introduction to new duct-construction methods. Every fastening, sealing, insulating, boot, flexible-duct, and balancing technique used here was taught in Chapters 6 through 11. If a step in this chapter conflicts with your memory of an earlier chapter's method, follow the earlier chapter's exact procedure; do not substitute a shortcut because the assembly is a practice system.

Setup Standard

Do not connect this practice system to a gas furnace, oil furnace, heat pump refrigerant circuit, central air conditioner, or occupied-building air handler. Shop Tip

Mechanically fasten every joint before you apply mastic or tape. A fastened joint that later fails a leakage test can be resealed; a joint sealed without fasteners can separate under blower pressure and force you to redo both the fastening and the sealing.

Sequence Discipline and the Capstone Continuity Rule

Complete the fastening and sealing of every joint before curing time begins for that joint, and do not insulate a duct section until its sealing has cured per the manufacturer-controlled time from Chapter 6. Do not connect the blower or test-air source until all pre-operation corrections identified during visual inspection have been completed. The practice system must remain accessible for inspection at every stage and must not be connected to a fuel-fired furnace, heat-pump refrigerant circuit, central air conditioner, or occupied-building air handler unless the work is professionally designed, permitted, inspected, and commissioned.

Critical Safety Cue — Blower and Electrical Control Verification

Before starting the blower or test-air source, verify all guards, covers, and disconnects are in place and that the emergency shutdown control is accessible and functional. Do not operate the blower with a guard removed. Shut down and isolate the blower before making any correction to ducts, joints, or dampers, and confirm the blower cannot start unexpectedly while you are working inside or against the assembly.

Field Safety Cue — Sheet-Metal Edges and Fastening Hazards

Handle cut sheet-metal edges, collars, and fittings with cut-resistant gloves. Support duct-board and flexible-duct sections before fastening to prevent them from shifting or falling during assembly. Keep the work area clear of loose fasteners, sharp trim scrap, and insulation debris between fabrication steps.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Every Dimension Against the Approved Drawing

Confirm branch sizes, support spacing, register locations, and damper positions against the approved practice-system drawing before fastening. Do not invent a duct size, airflow target, support spacing, or insulation value that the drawing does not supply. If a dimension is missing from the drawing, stop and obtain the missing information before proceeding.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Mastic, Tape, and Insulation Cure Conditions

Follow the manufacturer-controlled cure time for duct mastic and the manufacturer-specified overlap and closure requirements for insulation before proceeding to the next assembly step. Do not insulate over an uncured seal, and do not conceal a joint before its required inspection and testing have passed.

F. Preparation and Staging

  1. Obtain the approved practice-system drawing, duct schedule, branch sizes, return design, register schedule, blower or test-air specification, support details, insulation requirements, and test procedure. Confirm the drawing is complete before staging materials.
  2. Confirm that the system is a training assembly or professionally designed permitted installation. Record which condition applies on the worksheet.
  3. Do not connect the capstone to fuel-fired equipment, a refrigerant system, or occupied-building air handler unless separately authorized and commissioned. Record this restriction on the worksheet before beginning fabrication.
  4. Prepare the safety, tool, access, electrical-control, material, inspection, and documentation plan. Confirm PPE, tools, and materials are staged before cutting begins.
  5. Establish the equipment or blower position, supply-plenum reference, return reference, register locations, and support points. Mark each reference point on the training frame or approved practice structure.
  6. Inspect and stage sheet-metal fittings, collars, boots, flexible duct, return components, fasteners, mastic, listed tape, insulation, supports, dampers, grilles, and test instruments. Confirm every component matches the component schedule before assembly.

G. Supply Plenum, Return Path, and Register Boot Assembly

  1. Assemble and secure the supply-plenum or trunk components according to the approved design. Confirm plenum dimensions against the duct schedule before fastening.
  2. Install takeoff collars in the specified locations and orientation. Verify each collar's position against the marked reference points.
  3. Mechanically fasten all required duct joints before sealing. Confirm every joint is fastened per the fastener schedule before any mastic is applied.
  4. Install the return-air path and grille using the approved design. Follow the Chapter 9 procedure for framing, boot, and grille installation.
  5. Seal return joints to prevent drawing air from unintended spaces. Confirm every return-side joint is sealed per Chapter 6.
  6. Install the supply register boot and structural support. Follow the Chapter 8 procedure for boot orientation and framing support.

H. Branch Connection, Support, and Sealing

  1. Connect the specified rigid or flexible branch to the supply boot. Confirm branch diameter and route against the approved drawing.
  2. Connect the inner liner, insulation, and outer jacket using the Chapter 10 sequence. Follow the exact liner-engagement, sealing, and jacket-closure steps from Chapter 10.
  3. Install supports at approved intervals and maintain bend and sag limits. Verify support spacing with the tape measure against the approved specification.
  4. Install balancing dampers and mark their initial positions. Record each initial damper position on the worksheet.
  5. Seal all approved duct joints, seams, boots, collars, and penetrations using Chapter 6. Confirm continuous mastic contact and correctly burnished tape at every joint.
  6. Allow sealants to cure under the required conditions. Do not proceed to insulation until the manufacturer-controlled cure time has elapsed.

I. Insulation, Registers, and Pre-Operation Inspection

  1. Insulate approved duct sections, fittings, boots, and transitions using Chapter 7. Confirm full coverage, correct overlap, and continuous vapor facing.
  2. Preserve access to dampers, test ports, labels, and service components. Confirm insulation does not cover a required access or test point.
  3. Install supply registers and return grilles. Confirm each register and grille is secured and its damper moves freely.
  4. Inspect the full system for open joints, blocked paths, crushed duct, loose supports, exposed metal, damaged vapor facing, and unsafe edges. Record every finding on the worksheet.
  5. Complete all pre-operation corrections. Correct every finding from the previous step before connecting the blower.

J. Blower Connection, Testing, and Balancing

  1. Connect the listed blower or test-air source using the approved electrical and mechanical controls. Confirm the connection matches the approved specification.
  2. Verify guards, covers, disconnects, and emergency shutdown. Confirm the blower cannot start unexpectedly before proceeding.
  3. Start the blower at the designated test condition. Record the test condition on the worksheet before observing the system.
  4. Observe duct movement, joint leakage, vibration, noise, and register delivery. Record each observation at every accessible joint and register.
  5. Perform the approved leakage inspection. Use the smoke pencil or the approved leakage-test method at every sealed joint.
  6. Measure or compare supply airflow and return airflow using the specified instruments and setup. Record baseline readings before any balancing adjustment.
  7. Adjust dampers and registers in small increments. Make one adjustment at a time per the Chapter 11 procedure.
  8. Allow the system to stabilize after each change. Wait for stable conditions before recording the next reading.
  9. Record final airflow observations or measurements and damper positions. Confirm final positions are within the approved adjustment range.
  10. Inspect flexible duct under operation for collapse, excessive sag, or movement. Record any condition requiring correction.
  11. Inspect insulation and vapor facing for separation. Record any condition requiring correction.

K. Correction and Closeout

  1. Shut down and isolate the blower before corrections. Confirm the blower is de-energized and cannot start before touching any joint, support, or damper.
  2. Correct failed joints, supports, seals, insulation, or balance conditions. Use the exact chapter procedure that governs the failed component.
  3. Repeat all affected operational tests. Re-run the leakage or airflow test for every corrected item.
  4. Compile drawings, materials, photographs, support records, sealant records, airflow results, balancing positions, inspection findings, and corrections. Assemble the complete closeout package.
  5. Label supply, return, airflow direction, dampers, test ports, and shutdown control. Confirm every label is legible and matches the closeout package.
  6. Complete the HVAC punch list. Close every item before handoff.
  7. Hand off the practice system with operation, shutdown, maintenance, and inspection documentation. Confirm the receiving party has all required records.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Sealed Joint Fails the Leakage Test

Probable cause: a missed section of mastic, an unfastened joint that shifted before sealing, or tape applied without the required overlap.
Confirmation: re-run the smoke pencil or approved leakage-test method at the failed joint under the specified test condition.
Correction: shut down and isolate the blower, then clean and reseal the joint using the Chapter 6 procedure, mechanically fastening it first if it was not already fastened.
Reinspection: repeat the leakage test at the corrected joint and confirm no leakage remains before proceeding to insulation.

Flexible Duct Sags or Collapses Under Operation

Probable cause: support spacing exceeding the approved interval, an oversized bend, or a support strap that compressed the insulation.
Confirmation: shut down and isolate the blower, then measure the actual support spacing and sag against the approved specification.
Correction: add or reposition supports at the approved spacing and correct any bend exceeding the specified radius.
Reinspection: restart the blower and confirm the branch remains supported and unrestricted under operation.

Register Airflow Is Uneven Across the System

Probable cause: a damper adjusted outside the approved range, an undetected leak upstream of the register, or an obstruction in the branch.
Confirmation: compare current airflow readings against the recorded baseline and re-check upstream joints for leakage.
Correction: return the damper to the last approved position, correct any upstream leak, and repeat the balancing procedure in small increments.
Reinspection: confirm stabilized airflow at each register falls within the balancing plan's target range before recording final positions.

Insulation Vapor Facing Is Separated or Damaged After Testing

Probable cause: a puncture during handling, an unsealed longitudinal seam, or insulation compressed during support installation.
Confirmation: visually inspect the affected section with the blower shut down and isolated.
Correction: patch or replace the damaged insulation and reseal the vapor facing per Chapter 7.
Reinspection: restart the blower and confirm no condensation or facing separation develops under operation.

The Blower Fails to Start or Trips on Startup

Probable cause: a guard or cover not fully engaged, an incomplete electrical connection, or a system restriction beyond the blower's rated capacity.
Confirmation: verify all guards, covers, and connections against the manufacturer instructions before attempting to restart.
Correction: correct the guard, cover, or connection issue, or reduce excessive system restriction identified during inspection.
Reinspection: confirm normal startup and stable operation before resuming testing.
