09-Residential HVAC

This field manual provides practical, project-based instruction for residential heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems. Students learn to inspect, maintain, install, troubleshoot, and improve residential HVAC equipment and air distribution systems while following safe work practices and applicable codes.

Overview

Residential HVAC emphasizes hands-on skill development through real-world maintenance, repair, and installation projects. Beginning with HVAC system identification and safe work preparation, students progress through filtration, airflow improvement, ductwork inspection, duct installation, thermostat wiring, equipment maintenance, ventilation systems, indoor air quality, troubleshooting, and a complete ducted air distribution system capstone project.

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify and document residential HVAC system components.
  • Work safely around residential heating and cooling equipment.
  • Perform HVAC maintenance tasks including filter replacement and airflow cleaning.
  • Inspect, repair, seal, and insulate accessible ductwork.
  • Install supply registers, return air pathways, and flexible duct branches.
  • Install and wire low-voltage thermostat controls.
  • Maintain furnaces, condensate systems, and outdoor condenser units.
  • Install residential ventilation systems including bathroom fans and dryer vents.
  • Diagnose common HVAC comfort and airflow problems.
  • Complete a practice ducted air distribution system installation.
