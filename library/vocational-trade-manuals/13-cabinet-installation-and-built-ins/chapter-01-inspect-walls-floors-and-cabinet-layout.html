Job Overview

This project qualifies you to inspect one kitchen, vanity, closet, or practice installation area for wall, floor, ceiling, and dimensional conditions, locate the studs and concealed services that will control cabinet fastening, verify every cabinet dimension against the approved plan, and issue a documented cabinet-installation readiness and correction plan. The finished work product is a field-verified installation-area survey — a signed cabinet-readiness record accompanied by a field-verified layout sketch and deficiency list — ready to support the reference-line layout completed in Project 03 and the cabinet installation completed in Projects 4 through 6.

You will obtain the controlling documents, confirm the finished-floor condition, inspect walls for plumb and flatness, inspect the floor for level and damage, inspect ceilings and soffits where tall or wall cabinets approach them, verify room and cabinet dimensions against the plan, locate studs and backing, identify plumbing, electrical, gas, and mechanical obstacles, confirm continued utility access after installation, check door swings and accessibility, identify conditions requiring correction, and issue a documented readiness decision.

This project is the first project in Book 13 and assumes the room arrives with framing, drywall, and finished flooring installed, and with rough plumbing and electrical in place, under the applicable framing, drywall, and flooring books in this series. It sits immediately before Project 02, which stages the protected work area that this project's readiness decision authorizes, and before Project 03, which establishes the cabinet reference lines from the floor high point confirmed here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CB-001 — Inspect wall flatness, plumb, and finish condition across the cabinet installation area against the approved cabinet plan.
  • CB-002 — Inspect floor level, high point, and finish condition across the cabinet installation area against the approved finished-floor reference.
  • CB-003 — Locate wall studs, blocking, and backing across the full cabinet installation area using more than one verification method.
  • CB-004 — Identify plumbing, electrical, gas, and mechanical obstacles that affect cabinet layout, fastening, or continued service access.
  • CB-005 — Verify room dimensions and every scheduled cabinet, filler, panel, and appliance-opening dimension against the approved cabinet plan and cabinet schedule.
  • CB-006 — Document cabinet-installation readiness, including deficiencies and corrections, and issue an approved, conditional, or rejected readiness decision.

Where This Skill Fits

Every later project in this book depends on the survey completed here. The floor high point confirmed in this project controls the base-cabinet, countertop, and wall-cabinet reference lines established in Project 03, and the stud and service locations identified here control every structural fastener installed in Projects 4 through 6, 11, 14, 15, 16, 17, and 18. A wall or floor defect, an unmarked stud gap, or an unverified service location that is concealed rather than corrected here becomes a hidden fastening or access failure that Chapter 19 must trace back to its source during repair.

The document-review and readiness-decision method used here — reviewing controlling documents, inspecting before staging materials, separating conditions you may correct from conditions requiring another trade, and issuing a documented decision — is the same method used at the Chapter 20 capstone readiness checkpoint.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the current cabinet plan, interior elevations, cabinet schedule, appliance schedule, countertop plan, plumbing and electrical plans, and finish information
  • confirming finished-floor type, thickness, installation status, and reference elevation
  • inspecting walls for plumb, flatness, bow, humps, low spots, damaged drywall, moisture, and loose finishes
  • inspecting floors for level, high point, low point, deflection, damage, and transition conditions
  • inspecting ceilings and soffits where tall or wall cabinets approach them
  • verifying room dimensions, wall lengths, corners, openings, and ceiling height against the plan
  • measuring and verifying every cabinet width, height, depth, handing, filler, panel, and appliance-opening dimension
  • locating studs, blocking, backing, and structural support using more than one approved method
  • identifying plumbing supplies, drains, vents, shutoffs, cleanouts, appliance connections, receptacles, switches, junction boxes, disconnects, gas piping, ducts, and other service obstacles
  • confirming that required utilities will remain accessible after cabinet installation
  • inspecting door swings, window operation, passage clearances, and accessibility requirements
  • preparing a field-verified layout sketch and a deficiency list, reinspecting corrected items, and issuing a documented readiness decision

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select cabinet dimensions, cabinet quantities, appliance locations, or finish materials independently of the approved cabinet plan and schedules
  • cut, patch, relocate, or otherwise alter framing, drywall, flooring, plumbing, gas piping, or electrical components without an approved repair detail or the assigned licensed trade
  • conceal an active leak, mold, structural damage, damaged drywall, missing backing, unresolved framing defect, loose flooring, or inaccessible utility rather than mark it for correction or a qualified trade
  • proceed with work-area setup or cabinet layout before this project's readiness decision is issued and any required correction is complete

Any condition that does not match the approved plans, or that suggests active leaks, mold, structural damage, hazardous materials, or unsafe wiring, must stop work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Every later layout, fastening, countertop, and built-in project in this book assumes the wall, floor, and service conditions confirmed in this project were accurate. Treating this survey as a formality, rather than as the last opportunity to find a defect before cabinets are staged, is the most common cause of layout rework, fastening failures, and blocked service access traced back to Project 01 during the Chapter 19 repair project.

Setup Standard

Do not begin the wall, floor, or dimensional inspection until the current cabinet plan, interior elevations, cabinet schedule, appliance schedule, countertop plan, plumbing and electrical plans, and finish information are on site and confirmed current (Step 1). Do not treat an unverified finished-floor installation as complete; confirm floor type, thickness, and installation status directly with the general contractor or flooring trade before proceeding (Step 2).

Separating Conditions You May Correct From Conditions Requiring Another Trade

Every deficiency found in this project falls into one of two categories: a condition this project permits you to mark and carry forward as a documented layout note, or a condition requiring a plumber, electrician, structural professional, flooring installer, or other qualified trade. Marking a stud location, recording an out-of-level floor reading, or noting a wall bow within the cabinet plan's scribe allowance is within this project's authority. An active leak, damaged framing, an unsupported wall, or hazardous material is not; mark it, record it on the deficiency list, and obtain direction before continuing work in that area.

Measurement Cue — Verify Every Cabinet Dimension Against the Schedule

Measure every scheduled cabinet width, height, depth, filler, panel, and appliance opening in the field, and compare the measurement against the approved cabinet plan and cabinet schedule. Do not assume a room dimension matches the plan because it appears close by eye. A room or opening dimension that conflicts with the cabinet schedule is a deficiency to resolve before layout begins in Project 03, not a gap to close informally during installation.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Conceal an Active Leak, Mold, or Structural Damage

Do not proceed with cabinet layout, staging, or installation over an active leak, visible mold, structural damage, damaged drywall, missing backing, or an unresolved framing defect. A concealed condition covered by cabinets and countertops becomes a hidden hazard that is far more costly and dangerous to correct later. Mark the condition, stop work in that area, and obtain qualified review or the assigned trade before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Ladder and Step-Stool Footing

Use only a stable ladder or step stool rated for the required height to inspect ceilings, soffits, and upper-wall conditions. Do not stand on countertops, base cabinets, appliances, or stacked materials. Confirm footing on the finished floor before climbing, and keep the ladder clear of door swings and walkways.

Manufacturer Cue — Stud-Finder and Laser-Level Calibration

Calibrate the stud finder on an accessible section of wall with a known stud location before relying on it across the full cabinet area, and confirm the laser level or water level is reading a stable, level reference before recording the floor high point. Do not record a stud or elevation reading from an uncalibrated or unstable instrument.

Code Alert — Accessibility and Utility-Access Requirements Vary by Jurisdiction

Required clearances for accessible routes, appliance service access, and utility shutoff access vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction and the general contractor before confirming that a room layout, cabinet run, or appliance opening meets its intended accessibility or access requirement.

E. Document Review and Site Confirmation (Steps 1–2)

  1. Obtain the approved cabinet plan, interior elevations, cabinet schedule, appliance schedule, countertop plan, plumbing and electrical plans, and finish information. Confirm the revision date of each document against the project's document-control log before using it.
  2. Confirm finished-floor type, thickness, installation status, and reference elevation. Verify the flooring installation record shows the required completion before proceeding.

F. Wall, Floor, and Ceiling Inspection (Steps 3–6)

  1. Inspect walls for plumb, flatness, bow, humps, low spots, damaged drywall, moisture, and loose finishes. Use the 4-foot level and the 6-foot straightedge across each cabinet run, and probe suspect areas with the moisture meter.
  2. Inspect floors for level, high point, low point, deflection, damage, and transition conditions. Walk the full cabinet footprint and note any area with visible movement or damage.
  3. Inspect ceilings and soffits where tall or wall cabinets approach them. Confirm level, plumb, and clearance at each location a cabinet will contact the ceiling or soffit.
  4. Verify room dimensions, wall lengths, corners, openings, and ceiling height against the plan. Record each measurement directly on the field-verified layout sketch.

G. Dimensional and Service Verification (Steps 7–13)

  1. Measure and verify every cabinet width, height, depth, handing, filler, panel, and appliance-opening dimension. Compare each measurement against the approved cabinet schedule and appliance schedule.
  2. Locate studs, blocking, and backing. Mark each location directly on the wall with the marking pencil.
  3. Verify stud locations using more than one approved method where possible. Confirm stud-finder readings with a pilot-hole confirmation only where the wall finish and plan permit it.
  4. Identify plumbing supplies, drains, vents, shutoffs, cleanouts, and appliance connections. Compare each location with the plumbing plan and the cabinet schedule.
  5. Identify receptacles, switches, junction boxes, disconnects, lighting, and dedicated appliance circuits. Compare each location with the electrical plan and the appliance schedule.
  6. Identify gas piping, ducts, exhaust paths, radiators, baseboard heat, and other service obstacles. Mark each obstacle's location and clearance requirement on the layout sketch.
  7. Confirm that required utilities will remain accessible after cabinet installation. Compare each service location against the finished cabinet, filler, and panel positions shown on the plan.

H. Accessibility and Documentation (Steps 14–20)

  1. Inspect door swings, window operation, passage clearances, and accessibility requirements. Confirm each clearance against the approved plan and the applicable accessibility requirement.
  2. Identify conditions requiring shims, scribing, fillers, extended panels, backing, floor correction, or trade relocation. Mark each condition on the layout sketch and note the required correction.
  3. Stop work for active leaks, severe wall or floor defects, unsupported walls, hazardous materials, or conflicting services. Record the condition, the affected area, and the party contacted for direction.
  4. Prepare a field-verified layout sketch. Include room dimensions, floor high point, wall conditions, stud and backing locations, service locations, and cabinet-run boundaries.
  5. Prepare a deficiency list with location, responsible trade, required correction, and reinspection method. Include every condition marked in the prior steps, whether or not it has been corrected.
  6. Reinspect corrected items. Confirm each corrected condition against the Acceptance Standards table before considering it resolved.
  7. Issue an approved, conditional, or rejected cabinet-installation readiness decision. Base the decision on the completed deficiency list and the Acceptance Standards table, and identify the responsible party for any open item.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the surveyed area using the same checks regardless of which room or cabinet run it covers.

  • Documentation check: confirm every required plan, schedule, and finished-floor record is current and referenced on the cabinet-installation readiness record.
  • Wall and floor check: confirm walls are plumb and flat within the cabinet plan's scribe allowance and the floor high point has been confirmed by repeated measurement.
  • Dimension check: confirm room and cabinet dimensions match the approved cabinet plan and schedule.
  • Stud and service check: confirm studs, backing, and every plumbing, electrical, gas, and mechanical service location are identified, marked, and confirmed accessible after installation.
  • Accessibility check: confirm door swings, window operation, and passage clearances meet the approved plan and applicable accessibility requirement.
  • Decision check: confirm a documented approved, conditional, or rejected decision has been issued with a responsible party assigned to any open item.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Wall Section Is Out of Flat Beyond the Scribe Allowance

Probable cause: drywall finishing variation, framing bow, or a prior repair that was not brought flush with the surrounding wall.
Confirmation: hold the 6-foot straightedge against the wall at the affected location and measure the gap at its widest point.
Correction: record the condition on the deficiency list with the required scribe allowance or wall correction; do not proceed to layout without resolving whether the condition fits within the cabinet plan's scribe allowance.
Reinspection: repeat the flatness check at the affected location and adjacent wall sections once a documented decision is incorporated.

The Floor High Point Cannot Be Confirmed Consistently

Probable cause: an uncalibrated or unstable laser or water level, or a floor with deflection under the instrument's support.
Confirmation: repeat the survey from a different benchmark position and confirm the instrument is stable and level before each reading.
Correction: reset the benchmark, recalibrate the instrument, and repeat the survey until two independent readings agree.
Reinspection: confirm the final high point against a third independent reading before recording it on the readiness record.

A Room Dimension Conflicts With the Cabinet Schedule

Probable cause: the room was framed to a dimension that differs from the plan, or the cabinet schedule was prepared from an earlier plan revision.
Confirmation: remeasure the affected wall length and compare it against both the current cabinet plan and the cabinet schedule.
Correction: record the conflict on the deficiency list and obtain a documented decision from the designer or general contractor before layout begins in Project 03; do not adjust a cabinet or filler dimension independently.
Reinspection: repeat the dimensional check after the documented correction or revised schedule is received.

A Stud or Backing Location Cannot Be Confirmed

Probable cause: nonstandard framing spacing, a stud finder reading affected by wiring or plumbing behind the wall, or missing backing at a planned wall-cabinet or floating-shelf location.
Confirmation: recalibrate the stud finder on a known stud location and cross-check with a second method where the wall finish and plan permit it.
Correction: record the location on the deficiency list and identify whether additional backing must be installed before cabinet installation proceeds.
Reinspection: repeat the stud and backing check after any required backing is installed and before Project 03 layout begins.

A Plumbing or Electrical Location Does Not Match the Plan

Probable cause: a rough-in was relocated during framing or trade work without updated plan documentation.
Confirmation: compare the field-verified location against the plumbing or electrical plan and the cabinet schedule's clearance requirement.
Correction: record the conflict on the deficiency list and contact the responsible plumber, electrician, or designer before layout proceeds; do not relocate a service or alter a cabinet dimension independently.
Reinspection: repeat the service-location check once a documented resolution is received.

An Active Leak, Mold, or Structural Damage Is Found During Inspection

Probable cause: a prior water intrusion event, an unresolved plumbing failure, or an undetected framing or drywall defect.
Confirmation: inspect the extent of the condition and check moisture content at the affected wall or floor area.
Correction: stop work in the affected area and obtain qualified review or the assigned licensed trade before continuing; do not proceed with cabinet layout or staging over an unresolved condition.
Reinspection: repeat the condition check once the documented remediation or repair is complete.
