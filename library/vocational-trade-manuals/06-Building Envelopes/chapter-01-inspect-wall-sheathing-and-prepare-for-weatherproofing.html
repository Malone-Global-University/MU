Job Overview

This project qualifies you to inspect the exterior wall sheathing and rough openings of a structurally complete building, complete only the preparation authorized by the approved documents, and issue a documented envelope-readiness decision. The finished work product is a clean, structurally accepted, and dimensionally verified wall system, together with a signed envelope-readiness inspection record, ready to receive the water-resistive barrier (WRB), flashing, windows, exterior doors, and cladding covered in later projects in this book.

You will obtain the controlling documents, confirm that structural framing and prior inspections are complete, establish safe access, inspect the sheathing from both faces, verify panel type and fastening against the structural specification, inspect structural connectors that will be concealed, verify rough-opening dimensions and drainage, identify high-risk transitions and planned penetrations, clean and correct only authorized conditions, prepare a deficiency list for anything outside your authority, and issue and document a proceed, conditional-proceed, or stop-work decision.

This project is the first project in Book 6 and assumes the building arrives with wall framing, sheathing, and roof framing already complete and inspected under the structural books in this series. It sits immediately before Project 02, which installs the water-resistive barrier over the wall system accepted here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-001 — Inspect exterior and interior wall sheathing for damage, defects, and fastening condition against the approved structural specification.
  • BE-002 — Identify damaged, delaminated, or nonconforming sheathing panels and framing conflicts requiring correction or qualified review.
  • BE-003 — Verify sheathing panel type, thickness, span rating, orientation, edge support, and fastening against the approved structural specification.
  • BE-004 — Correct authorized surface defects and reset or replace authorized sheathing fasteners using the approved fastening schedule.
  • BE-005 — Prepare rough openings for each scheduled window and exterior door to the approved dimension, square, level, plumb, and drainage requirements.
  • BE-006 — Document envelope readiness, including deficiencies, corrections, and photographs, and issue a proceed, conditional-proceed, or stop-work decision.

Where This Skill Fits

Every later project in this book depends on the decision made here. The WRB installed in Project 02 is only as reliable as the surface and fastening confirmed in this project, and the rough-opening dimensions verified here control the sill-pan flashing, window setting, and door setting completed in Projects 4 through 9. A sheathing defect, missed fastener, or out-of-tolerance rough opening that is concealed rather than corrected here becomes a hidden water path that later inspections in Project 19 must trace back to its source.

The document-review and readiness-decision method used here — reviewing controlling documents, inspecting before covering, separating authorized correction from qualified review, and issuing a documented decision — is the same method used at the Chapter 19 full exterior water-control inspection and at the Chapter 20 capstone readiness checkpoints.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the current framing plan, wall-sheathing schedule, structural connector details, window and door schedules, envelope specification, and prior inspection records
  • inspecting exterior and interior sheathing faces for damage, defects, and fastening condition
  • verifying panel type, thickness, span rating, orientation, edge support, and fastening against the approved structural specification
  • inspecting structural connectors and shear-wall components that the envelope system will conceal
  • verifying rough-opening dimensions, square, level, plumb, sill condition, and drainage potential for each scheduled window and exterior door
  • identifying high-risk transitions and planned service penetrations for coordination with later projects
  • cleaning the wall surface and completing only authorized fastener correction and minor surface repair
  • preparing a deficiency list and issuing a documented envelope-readiness decision

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select sheathing panel type, thickness, span rating, or fastening schedule independently of the approved structural specification
  • cut, enlarge, or reframe a rough opening, or alter framing, headers, hold-downs, straps, or shear-wall components, without an approved repair or modification detail
  • conceal damaged, delaminated, contaminated, or nonconforming sheathing rather than mark it for correction or qualified review
  • proceed with WRB installation before this project's envelope-readiness decision is issued and any required correction is complete

Any condition that does not match the approved plans, or that suggests structural damage, water intrusion, mold, or contamination requiring specialized handling, must stop work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Every later flashing and WRB detail in this book assumes the wall surface and rough openings passed inspection in this project. Treating this project as a formality, rather than as the last opportunity to inspect the sheathing before it is covered, is the most common cause of water-control defects traced back to Project 01 during the Project 19 full exterior inspection.

Setup Standard

Do not begin sheathing inspection until the current framing plan, wall-sheathing schedule, structural connector details, window and door schedules, and prior structural inspection records are on site and confirmed current (Step 1). Do not treat an unverified framing or sheathing inspection as complete; confirm it directly against the project's inspection record before proceeding (Step 2).

Shop Tip

Inspect the interior face of the sheathing wherever it remains accessible before the exterior face. Water staining, missed fasteners, and daylight gaps are often easier to identify from inside the wall cavity than from outside, and finding them here avoids a repeat trip once the WRB is installed in Project 02.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every readiness inspection in this book, from this chapter's wall-sheathing review to the Chapter 19 full exterior water-control inspection, follows the same order: assemble and confirm the controlling documents, establish safe access, inspect the surface and structure from every accessible face, verify dimensions and conditions against the approved specification, identify conditions outside your authority, correct what is authorized, and issue a documented decision. Learning that sequence here protects every later inspection project in this book.

Separating Authorized Correction From Qualified Review

Every deficiency found in this project falls into one of two categories: an authorized correction this project permits you to complete directly, or a condition requiring qualified review by the design professional, structural engineer, or authority having jurisdiction. Resetting a missed fastener or removing surface contamination is an authorized correction. A delaminated panel, a framing conflict, or an oversized rough opening is not; mark it, record it on the deficiency list, and obtain direction before concealing it.

Measurement Cue — Verify Every Rough Opening Against Its Schedule

Measure each scheduled window and exterior door opening for width, height, square, level, plumb, and sill condition, and compare the field measurement against the approved window and door schedules. Do not assume an opening matches the schedule because it appears close by eye. An opening outside the permitted adjustment range is a deficiency, not a framing detail to correct informally.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Conceal Structural Damage

Do not cover, patch over, or paper over sheathing damage, framing conflicts, or structural connector deficiencies to keep the schedule moving. A damaged or nonconforming structural component concealed by the WRB and cladding becomes a hidden hazard that is far more costly and dangerous to correct later. Mark the condition, stop work in that area, and obtain qualified review before it is covered.

Field Safety Cue — Elevated and Uneven Access

Use only approved ladders, scaffolds, lifts, or work platforms to reach every elevation. Do not stand on window or door rough openings, unsecured platforms, makeshift supports, or stacked materials. Confirm ladder and scaffold footing on uneven or sloped grade before climbing.

Manufacturer Cue — Sheathing Panel Restrictions

Confirm panel type, thickness, span rating, orientation, edge support, and fastening against the sheathing manufacturer's current installation guide before authorizing any repair or fastener replacement. Do not substitute a generic fastener, panel patch material, or repair method not permitted by the manufacturer and the structural documents.

Code Alert — Structural Inspection Sign-Off Varies by Jurisdiction

Required structural inspection sign-offs, hold-points, and permit requirements before concealing framing and sheathing vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction and the general contractor before treating any structural component as accepted.

E. Document Review and Site Access (Steps 1–3)

  1. Obtain the current framing plan, wall-sheathing schedule, structural connector details, window and door schedules, envelope specification, and prior inspection records. Confirm the revision date of each document against the project's document-control log before using it.
  2. Confirm that wall framing, sheathing, hold-downs, straps, headers, rough openings, and required structural inspections are complete. Verify the prior structural inspection record shows the required approval before proceeding.
  3. Establish safe access around every elevation using approved ladders, scaffolds, lifts, or work platforms. Confirm footing, tie-off points, and platform capacity before beginning inspection at height.

F. Sheathing and Structural Inspection (Steps 4–11)

  1. Inspect the interior face of the sheathing where accessible for missed fasteners, unsupported edges, daylight, water staining, mold, framing conflicts, and penetrations. Use a flashlight to check corners, plate lines, and areas hidden by framing members.
  2. Inspect exterior sheathing for damaged edges, delamination, swelling, soft areas, punctures, excessive gaps, protruding fasteners, contamination, and loose panels. Probe suspect areas with a moisture meter where staining or softness is observed.
  3. Verify panel type, thickness, span rating, orientation, edge support, joint layout, and fastening against the approved structural specification. Record the panel and fastening identification used at each elevation.
  4. Inspect structural connectors and shear-wall components that will be concealed by the envelope system. Confirm hold-downs, straps, and connector fasteners are installed and torqued or seated as specified.
  5. Verify rough-opening width, height, square, level, plumb, sill condition, header condition, and required clearances for each scheduled window and exterior door. Compare every measurement against the approved window and door schedules.
  6. Check that rough-opening sills can drain outward or can receive the approved sloped sill-pan system. Confirm the sill framing supports the slope or pan system specified for that opening.
  7. Identify wall-to-roof, wall-to-foundation, deck-to-wall, porch, balcony, and other high-risk transitions. Mark each transition location for coordination with later flashing and WRB projects.
  8. Identify planned pipe, duct, electrical, cable, and fixture penetrations and compare them with the approved plans. Confirm each planned penetration location does not conflict with framing, connectors, or rough openings.

G. Cleanup and Authorized Correction (Steps 12–17)

  1. Remove loose debris, mud, dust, oil, protrusions, and sharp edges that could damage the WRB or flashing. Clean the full wall surface, not only the areas immediately around openings.
  2. Reset or replace authorized sheathing fasteners using the approved schedule. Use only the fastener type, length, and spacing specified for that panel and framing condition.
  3. Complete only minor surface repairs permitted by the sheathing manufacturer and structural documents. Do not extend a repair beyond the manufacturer's permitted patch size or method.
  4. Mark damaged panels, framing conflicts, oversized openings, unsupported edges, and structural conditions requiring qualified review. Flag each location clearly and do not proceed with correction beyond your authorized scope.
  5. Prepare a deficiency list identifying location, condition, required correction, responsible authority, and reinspection method. Include every condition marked in the prior step, whether or not it has been corrected.
  6. Correct authorized deficiencies and remeasure affected openings and wall areas. Confirm the corrected condition against the Acceptance Standards table before considering it resolved.

H. Documentation and Readiness Decision (Steps 18–20)

  1. Photograph and document sheathing condition, rough-opening dimensions, penetrations, transitions, and unresolved items. Label each photograph with its elevation and location for later reference.
  2. Issue a proceed, conditional-proceed, or stop-work envelope-readiness decision. Base the decision on the completed deficiency list and the Acceptance Standards table, and identify the responsible authority for any open item.
  3. Protect prepared walls and openings until WRB installation begins. Cover or otherwise shield exposed sheathing and openings from unexpected rain or wind before leaving the site.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the prepared wall system using the same checks regardless of which elevation it covers.

  • Documentation check: confirm every required plan, schedule, and prior inspection record is current and referenced on the envelope-readiness inspection record.
  • Sheathing check: confirm every panel is free of damage, delamination, contamination, and protruding fasteners, and matches the approved structural specification.
  • Connector check: confirm structural connectors that will be concealed are installed and inspected as specified.
  • Opening check: confirm every rough opening matches its schedule for dimension, square, level, plumb, and sill drainage potential.
  • Transition and penetration check: confirm every high-risk transition and planned penetration is identified and coordinated with later projects.
  • Decision check: confirm a documented proceed, conditional-proceed, or stop-work decision has been issued with responsible authority assigned to any open item.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Sheathing Panel Shows Delamination or Softness

Probable cause: prolonged moisture exposure before the wall was protected, or panel damage during framing or shipping.
Confirmation: probe the affected area with a moisture meter and compare the reading against the sheathing manufacturer's acceptable range.
Correction: mark the panel for qualified review; do not patch over a delaminated or soft panel as an authorized surface repair.
Reinspection: repeat the moisture and condition check at the affected location and adjacent panels once a documented decision is incorporated.

Fasteners Are Missing, Overdriven, or Underdriven

Probable cause: the original fastening pass skipped locations, or fasteners were driven with incorrect tool pressure or depth setting.
Confirmation: compare the fastener pattern and seating condition at the affected panel against the approved fastening schedule.
Correction: reset or replace fasteners at the correct spacing, type, and seating depth specified by the schedule.
Reinspection: repeat the fastening check across the full panel after correction.

A Rough Opening Is Out of Square or Oversized

Probable cause: framing shifted during construction, or the opening was framed to an incorrect dimension.
Confirmation: remeasure the opening's width, height, diagonals, level, and plumb, and compare against the window or door schedule and its permitted adjustment range.
Correction: mark the opening for qualified review if it falls outside the permitted range; do not attempt an unauthorized framing correction.
Reinspection: repeat the dimensional check after the documented correction is complete and before flashing begins in Project 4 or 7.

A Rough-Opening Sill Cannot Drain Outward

Probable cause: the sill framing is level or back-pitched instead of sloped outward, or the approved sill-pan system was not accounted for during framing.
Confirmation: check the sill slope with a level and compare the condition against the approved sill-pan or slope detail for that opening.
Correction: mark the opening for qualified review or correction according to the approved sill-pan detail before Project 4 or 7 begins.
Reinspection: repeat the slope check after correction and before sill-pan flashing is installed.

A Structural Connector Cannot Be Confirmed Before Concealment

Probable cause: the connector was installed without a documented inspection, or access to verify it is now limited.
Confirmation: review the prior structural inspection record for that connector location and inspect any remaining visible access.
Correction: do not conceal the connector with WRB until its inspection status is confirmed; obtain qualified review if the record is incomplete.
Reinspection: repeat the connector check once documentation or qualified review is complete.

Interior Water Staining or Mold Is Found During Inspection

Probable cause: a prior water intrusion event, condensation, or an unprotected wall condition before this inspection.
Confirmation: inspect the extent of staining or growth and check moisture content at the affected framing and sheathing.
Correction: stop work in the affected area and obtain qualified review or specialized handling before continuing; do not cover a contaminated condition with WRB.
Reinspection: repeat the moisture and condition check once the documented remediation decision is incorporated.

Cleanup

This project ends with a clean, structurally accepted, dimensionally verified wall system and a documented readiness decision, not with WRB, flashing, or windows installed.

  • Clear debris, protruding fastener remnants, and loose material from the wall surface and rough openings
  • Remove marking materials that are not part of an open deficiency reference
  • Cover or otherwise protect exposed sheathing and openings from unexpected rain or wind before leaving the site
  • Store the envelope-readiness inspection record, deficiency list, and supporting photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open discrepancy and its required decision to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Project 02 begins
