Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install a continuous, mechanically attached water-resistive barrier over the wall sheathing accepted in Project 01. The finished work product is a fully covered wall system in which every course, corner, seam, transition, and opening preserves the shingle-lap drainage principle: water striking any point on the wall sheds onto the layer below it, all the way to the ground, without reverse laps or blocked drainage paths.

You will review the approved WRB product and its exposure, orientation, fastening, lap, and corner requirements, confirm that the wall-readiness decision from Project 01 remains valid, install required base flashing at the lower wall termination, install the first course from a level reference line, fasten it correctly, wrap corners by the required distance, install successive courses with the required horizontal lap, stagger and lap vertical seams, maintain continuity at high-risk transitions, prepare openings without exposing the wall to uncontrolled rain, repair damage, and inspect and document the completed WRB before seam treatment begins in Project 03.

This project depends directly on the envelope-readiness decision issued in Project 01 and sits immediately before Project 03, which tapes seams, patches damage, and seals approved penetrations to complete the continuity of this WRB layer.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-007 — Review the approved WRB product and roll house wrap in the correct orientation, course direction, and starting position over the accepted wall sheathing.
  • BE-008 — Overlap successive courses by the required horizontal shingle lap and locate vertical seams according to the approved detail.
  • BE-009 — Fasten the WRB using the approved cap fasteners or manufacturer-specified method, spacing, and location, keeping fasteners out of prohibited zones.
  • BE-010 — Wrap outside and inside corners by the required minimum distance or install approved corner pieces.
  • BE-011 — Maintain shingle-lap drainage continuity at transitions, openings, and penetrations while avoiding reverse laps and uncontrolled wall exposure.
  • BE-012 — Inspect completed WRB coverage for laps, corners, terminations, fastening, and transitions, and document the installation before it is concealed.

Where This Skill Fits

The WRB installed here is the drainage plane that every later flashing detail in this book ties into. Rough-opening sill flashing in Project 4, jamb and head flashing in Projects 4, 6, and 9, penetration flashing in Projects 3 and 16, and the rainscreen in Project 10 all depend on this layer being continuous, correctly lapped, and free of reverse laps before they are installed. A course installed upside down, a missed corner wrap, or a vertical seam without the required overlap becomes a hidden water path that the Project 19 full exterior inspection must trace back to this project.

The shingle-lap principle taught here — every upper layer discharges onto the layer below it — is the same principle governing sill-pan flashing, window and door flashing, penetration flashing, and siding coursing throughout the rest of this book.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved WRB product, exposure limits, orientation, fastening schedule, lap requirements, corner details, foundation transition, roof transition, and opening-flap method
  • inspecting WRB rolls for damage, contamination, weather exposure, and correct product identification before installation
  • installing required through-wall or base flashing before covering the lower wall transition
  • installing courses from the lowest elevation upward with the required horizontal shingle lap and vertical seam overlap
  • fastening the WRB using the approved cap fasteners or manufacturer-specified method and spacing
  • wrapping outside and inside corners by the required minimum distance or installing approved corner pieces
  • maintaining continuity at wall-to-foundation, wall-to-roof, porch, deck, and other transitions
  • making only the cuts required by the selected flashing sequence at window and door openings, and temporarily securing flaps without exposing the wall
  • installing WRB around planned penetrations, smoothing wrinkles, repairing tears and punctures, and inspecting and documenting the completed layer

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select the WRB product, exposure rating, fastening schedule, or lap dimensions independently of the approved product instructions and envelope specification
  • begin WRB installation before the Project 01 envelope-readiness decision has been issued and any required correction completed
  • make permanent cuts at window or door openings before the approved flashing sequence for that opening has been reviewed
  • seal, tape, or conceal seams, penetrations, or damage; those tasks belong to Project 03 and must not be performed out of sequence
  • proceed with rough-opening flashing, window setting, or door setting before this project's WRB inspection has passed

Any condition that does not match the approved WRB instructions, or that suggests the Project 01 readiness decision has been invalidated by subsequent damage or exposure, must stop work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Every later flashing detail in this book assumes the WRB installed here is continuous, correctly lapped, and free of reverse laps. Treating a missed corner wrap or an under-lapped seam as something Project 03's seam tape will fix is the most common cause of water-control defects traced back to Project 02 during the Project 19 full exterior inspection. Tape treats seams; it does not correct a reverse lap or a missing shingle-lap relationship.

Shop Tip

Dry-fit and mark the full course layout for one elevation, including corner wrap points and opening locations, before fastening the first course. Confirming the layout on the wall before committing fasteners avoids a short final course, a missed opening allowance, or a corner wrap that runs out of material partway up the wall.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every water-control layer in this book, from this chapter's house wrap to the final siding course in Chapter 12 or 13, follows the same shingle-lap principle: every upper layer discharges its water onto the layer below it, and no layer may be installed in reverse. Learning to identify and maintain that relationship here, at the simplest and largest scale in the book, protects every flashing, window, door, and siding project that follows.

Maintaining Shingle-Lap Drainage at Every Course

Begin at the lowest course on each elevation and work upward. Each successive course must overlap the course below it by the horizontal lap dimension specified in the approved WRB instructions. Do not install a course so that its lower edge sits behind the course below it; that condition is a reverse lap and will direct water behind the WRB rather than over it. Verify the lap direction at every course before fastening, not only at the start of the elevation.

Measurement Cue — Verify Horizontal Lap and Vertical Seam Overlap at Every Course

Measure the horizontal shingle lap at the start, middle, and end of each course against the approved WRB instructions, and measure the vertical seam overlap wherever two roll ends meet. Do not assume a lap is correct because the roll appears aligned by eye. A lap that is out of tolerance at one location on a long elevation is a deficiency at that location, not an average condition across the wall.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Install a Reverse Lap

Do not install any WRB course, corner detail, or opening flap in a reverse lap. A reverse lap installed at any point on the wall directs water behind the WRB rather than over it, and this condition is frequently concealed by the time it causes damage. If a reverse lap is found after installation, correct it before Project 03 begins; do not rely on seam tape to correct a reverse lap.

Field Safety Cue — Wind and Elevated Material Handling

Full WRB rolls and large cut sheets act as sails in wind. Do not unroll or handle large sheets of WRB from an unsecured ladder or platform in gusting conditions. Stop work and secure or store material if wind conditions exceed the levels the site safety plan permits for elevated sheet-material handling.

Manufacturer Cue — Orientation and Exposure Limits

Install the WRB with the printed or designated face outward, exactly as specified by the manufacturer. Confirm the manufacturer's maximum permitted exposure time before the WRB must be covered by flashing, windows, doors, rainscreen, or siding, and plan the day's coverage so no area remains exposed longer than that limit.

Code Alert — Foundation and Roof Transition Requirements Vary

Required through-wall flashing, base-flashing overlap, and roof-to-wall transition details at the lower wall termination vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction and the design professional before covering the lower transition with WRB.

E. Document Review and Layout Planning (Steps 1–5)

  1. Review the approved WRB product, exposure limits, orientation, fastening schedule, lap requirements, corner details, foundation transition, roof transition, and opening-flap method. Confirm the revision date of each document against the project's document-control log before using it.
  2. Confirm that the wall readiness inspection has passed and that required structural components remain visible or documented. Verify the Project 01 envelope-readiness decision shows proceed or conditional-proceed with corrections complete.
  3. Inspect WRB rolls for damage, contamination, weather exposure, and correct product identification. Set aside and do not install any roll showing tears, mold, or exposure beyond the manufacturer's storage limits.
  4. Plan course direction, starting elevation, roll lengths, vertical seams, opening treatment, and temporary terminations. Dry-fit the layout on the wall and mark corner wrap points and opening boundaries before cutting the first roll.
  5. Establish a level reference line at the approved lower-wall termination. Confirm the line is level across the full elevation with the 4-foot level before snapping it.

F. First Course and Base Transition (Steps 6–11)

  1. Install required through-wall or base flashing before covering the lower transition. Confirm the base flashing extends over the foundation or lower termination as required by the approved detail.
  2. Begin at the lowest course and orient the WRB with the printed or designated face outward. Verify orientation before fastening any portion of the course.
  3. Align the first course to the reference line without stretching or distorting the sheet. Work from the center of the roll outward to avoid introducing wrinkles.
  4. Fasten the WRB using approved cap fasteners or the manufacturer-specified method and spacing. Confirm fastener spacing against the fastening schedule at the start, middle, and end of the course.
  5. Keep fasteners out of prohibited flashing, opening, and drainage zones. Mark these zones on the wall before fastening if they are not otherwise visible.
  6. Wrap outside and inside corners by the required minimum distance or install approved corner pieces. Measure the wrap distance at the top, middle, and bottom of each corner.

G. Successive Courses, Seams, and Transitions (Steps 12–17)

  1. Install successive courses above lower courses with the required horizontal shingle lap. Verify the lap dimension before fastening each course, not only at the start of the elevation.
  2. Stagger or locate vertical seams according to the approved detail and provide the required overlap. Confirm no vertical seam falls within the corner wrap distance or opening boundary unless the approved detail permits it.
  3. Maintain continuity at wall-to-foundation, wall-to-roof, porch, deck, and other transitions. Confirm the WRB laps correctly with any adjoining roof or deck flashing at each identified transition.
  4. At window and door openings, make only the cuts required by the selected flashing sequence and preserve head flaps where applicable. Do not cut beyond the opening boundary marked during layout planning.
  5. Temporarily secure opening flaps without reverse lapping or exposing the wall to uncontrolled rain. Fasten flaps only at locations that will not interfere with the flashing sequence in Project 4 or 7.
  6. Install WRB around planned penetrations without oversized cuts. Cut only as large as the penetration requires, consistent with the penetration treatment planned in Project 01.

H. Repair, Inspection, and Documentation (Steps 18–22)

  1. Smooth major wrinkles and folds that could trap water or interfere with cladding. Re-fasten any area where a wrinkle cannot be smoothed by hand.
  2. Repair tears, punctures, and misplaced fasteners using the approved patch method. Extend every patch beyond the defect by the distance specified in the manufacturer's repair instructions.
  3. Inspect every lap, corner, termination, opening, fastener pattern, and transition. Use the Acceptance Standards table to confirm each item before considering the elevation complete.
  4. Photograph and document the completed WRB before seam treatment and flashing conceal critical details. Label each photograph with its elevation and location for later reference.
  5. Protect the WRB from wind damage, prolonged exposure, and unnecessary traffic. Secure any loose flap or edge and note the coverage date on the WRB installation record before leaving the site.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed WRB using the same checks regardless of which elevation it covers.

  • Documentation check: confirm the approved WRB product instructions, envelope specification, and Project 01 readiness decision are current and referenced on the WRB installation record.
  • Orientation and lap check: confirm every course faces outward and every horizontal and vertical lap meets the required dimension with no reverse laps.
  • Fastening check: confirm approved cap fasteners are installed at the specified spacing and are absent from prohibited zones.
  • Corner check: confirm every corner is wrapped by the required distance or fitted with an approved corner piece.
  • Transition and opening check: confirm continuity at every identified transition and that opening flaps are secured without reverse lapping or exposure.
  • Damage check: confirm every tear, puncture, and misplaced fastener has been repaired using the approved patch method.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Course Was Installed With a Reverse Lap

Probable cause: a course was installed out of bottom-to-top sequence, or a repair patch was placed over rather than under the surrounding material.
Confirmation: trace the lap direction at the affected location and compare it against the shingle-lap principle for the full elevation.
Correction: remove and reinstall the affected course or patch in the correct lap order; do not rely on seam tape in Project 03 to correct a reverse lap.
Reinspection: repeat the lap-direction check at the corrected location and at courses immediately above and below it.

Horizontal or Vertical Lap Is Under the Required Dimension

Probable cause: course exposure was not measured before fastening, or roll ends were aligned by eye instead of by measurement.
Confirmation: measure the lap at multiple points along the affected course or seam and compare against the approved WRB instructions.
Correction: add a supplemental strip of WRB extending the required distance beyond the short lap, fastened and lapped in the correct shingle order, or remove and reinstall the course.
Reinspection: repeat the lap measurement at the corrected location and at three additional points along the same course.

A Corner Wrap Falls Short of the Required Distance

Probable cause: the course was cut to the corner without accounting for the wrap distance, or the corner piece was sized incorrectly.
Confirmation: measure the wrap distance at the top, middle, and bottom of the corner and compare against the approved corner detail.
Correction: install a supplemental strip or approved corner piece to achieve the required wrap distance, lapped correctly with the adjoining courses.
Reinspection: repeat the wrap-distance measurement at all three corner heights after correction.

Fasteners Are Found in a Prohibited Zone

Probable cause: the prohibited zone was not marked before fastening, or the fastening pass did not account for an opening or flashing boundary.
Confirmation: compare the fastener locations against the marked prohibited zones and the approved fastening schedule.
Correction: remove the fastener, patch the resulting hole using the approved patch method, and refasten at an approved location.
Reinspection: repeat the fastener-location check across the full course after correction.

An Opening Flap Was Cut Beyond the Required Boundary

Probable cause: the cut was made before the flashing sequence for that opening was reviewed, or the opening boundary was not marked during layout planning.
Confirmation: compare the actual cut against the opening-flap method required by the approved flashing sequence for that window or door.
Correction: patch the oversized cut using the approved patch method and coordinate the corrected flap with the flashing sequence before Project 4 or 7 begins.
Reinspection: repeat the opening-cut check before rough-opening flashing begins.

Wind or Rain Exposure Occurred Before the Elevation Was Covered

Probable cause: the day's coverage plan exceeded what could be completed before conditions changed, or temporary protection was not installed.
Confirmation: inspect the exposed area for wrinkles, fastener pull-through, saturation, or contamination.
Correction: repair or replace damaged WRB material using the approved patch or course-replacement method before continuing.
Reinspection: repeat the surface-condition and lap checks at the affected area after correction.

Cleanup

This project ends with a continuous, correctly lapped and fastened WRB layer, not with seams taped or penetrations sealed.

  • Clear scrap WRB, cut-offs, and used fastener packaging from the work area and from beneath the wall
  • Secure or remove excess rolled material to prevent wind damage overnight
  • Confirm all opening flaps remain secured without reverse lapping before leaving the site
  • Store the WRB installation record and supporting photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open discrepancy, exposure event, or unresolved lap condition to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Project 03 begins

Field Terms

Water-Resistive Barrier (WRB)
The continuous sheet material installed over wall sheathing to form the wall's primary drainage plane.
Shingle Lap
The required overlap in which every upper layer discharges its water onto the layer below it.
Reverse Lap
A lap installed in the opposite order that can direct water behind the WRB instead of over it.
Course
One horizontal run of WRB material installed at a given height on the wall.
Corner Wrap Distance
The minimum distance WRB must extend around an outside or inside corner, or the approved corner piece used in its place.
Opening Flap
WRB material temporarily cut and secured at a window or door opening for later integration with flashing.
Cap Fastener
A fastener with a plastic or metal cap specified to hold WRB against sheathing without tearing through the sheet.
Prohibited Fastening Zone
A location where the approved instructions do not permit a WRB fastener, such as a flashing, opening, or drainage zone.
Base Flashing
Through-wall or base flashing installed at the lower wall termination before WRB coverage begins.
WRB Installation Record
The document recording roll identification, course layout, fastening, inspection results, and photographs.
