Job Overview

This project qualifies you to complete the concealed water-control and air-seal assembly around the prehung exterior door set and function-tested in Project 08. The finished work product is a flashed, drained, air-sealed, and operational exterior-door installation, together with photographs and records of every concealed detail, ready to receive the rainscreen furring, siding, and trim covered in later projects in this book.

You will review the controlling instructions, confirm the door remains plumb, square, and operational, protect the sill-pan drainage path, install jamb flashing tied into the sill pan, install head flashing and integrate the WRB head flap over it, seal only the designated frame, threshold, and trim joints, install approved exterior trim backing, air-seal the interior perimeter gap without disturbing operation, inspect every layer, and photograph the completed assembly before it is concealed by rainscreen and siding.

This project follows directly from Project 08, which set and function-tested the prehung door, and it precedes Project 10, which installs the rainscreen furring and drainage mat over the completed WRB. A door that is flashed but not correctly drained, or sealed in a location that blocks the sill pan or threshold weeps, becomes a concealed defect that the Project 19 full exterior water-control inspection must trace back to this project.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-049 — Flash door jambs from bottom to top with the required overlap onto the sill-pan upturns without bridging gaps or displacing the frame.
  • BE-050 — Install rigid or flexible head flashing with the required end dams, projection, and extension beyond the jambs, integrated with the WRB head flap.
  • BE-051 — Seal designated frame-to-pan, threshold-to-floor, and threshold-end joints with compatible sealant while keeping the sill-pan outlet and threshold weeps open.
  • BE-052 — Integrate the WRB with the jamb and head flashing in correct shingle-lap order and patch any exposed sheathing or discontinuity.
  • BE-053 — Install approved exterior trim backing or furring support without interrupting drainage, and air-seal the interior frame-to-rough-opening gap using the approved backer rod, sealant, or low-expansion foam.
  • BE-054 — Inspect the completed door water-control assembly, correct failed conditions, and document the concealed detail with photographs and records.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 07 built the drained sill pan, and Project 08 set and function-tested the door in that opening. This project completes the water-control loop by tying jamb and head flashing into that sill pan and into the surrounding WRB, so that water striking the door assembly from any direction is shed outward and downward without reaching the rough opening or the interior. The interior air seal completed here also controls air leakage at the frame perimeter, which is separate from — and must not interfere with — the exterior drainage path.

The shingle-lap discipline used here (sill pan, then jambs, then head, then WRB head flap) is the same drainage logic used at every window and penetration flashing detail in this book, including Project 4 through 6 for windows and Project 16 for wall penetrations.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • rechecking door plumb, square, reveal, latch, threshold bearing, and operation before concealing fasteners or shims
  • confirming the sill-pan drainage path remains open before covering it
  • installing jamb flashing from bottom to top with the required overlap onto the sill-pan upturns
  • installing head flashing with the required end dams and projection, integrated with the WRB head flap
  • sealing only the designated frame-to-pan, threshold-to-floor, and threshold-end joints
  • installing approved exterior trim backing without interrupting drainage
  • air-sealing the interior frame-to-rough-opening gap with backer rod, sealant, or low-expansion foam
  • inspecting, correcting, and documenting the completed concealed door-flashing assembly

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • seal, tape, or otherwise block the sill-pan outlet, threshold weeps, or any other designed drainage or ventilation opening
  • substitute sealant, tape, or foam for a missing or incorrectly lapped flashing layer
  • proceed with jamb or head flashing before the door has passed the Project 08 operation and alignment checks
  • install rainscreen furring, siding, or exterior trim over this assembly before it has passed inspection and been photographed

Any condition that shows a reverse lap, blocked drainage, incompatible sealant, bowed jamb, or failed adhesion must be corrected before concealment. Stop work and obtain qualified review for any structural or door-operation issue outside this project's authorized scope.

Setup Standard

Do not begin jamb flashing until the door has passed the Project 08 operation, plumb, square, and reveal checks (Step 2), and until the sill-pan drainage path has been confirmed open (Step 3). Do not treat an unconfirmed door installation as ready for concealment.

Shop Tip

Dry-fit every jamb and head flashing piece before applying adhesive or primer. Confirming overlap, projection, and end-dam position on a dry fit avoids wasting adhered material and prevents a rushed correction after the flashing has already bonded to the wall.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every concealed water-control assembly in this book — window flashing in Projects 4 through 6, door flashing in this project, and wall-penetration flashing in Project 16 — follows the same shingle-lap order: the lowest layer is installed first and every layer above overlaps the layer below, so water always sheds onto a layer that is already prepared to receive it. Learning that order at the door reinforces the same discipline used at every other opening and penetration in this book.

Jamb flashingBottom-to-top layers tied into the sill-pan upturns
Head flashingEnd dams and projection integrated beneath the WRB head flap
Designated sealantFrame-to-pan and threshold joints sealed; drainage left open
Interior air sealPerimeter gap sealed without disturbing exterior drainage

Keeping Drainage Separate From Air Sealing

The exterior flashing and drainage path and the interior air-seal gap serve two different functions and must never be merged. The sill-pan outlet and threshold weeps drain bulk water and must remain open at all times. The interior air-seal gap controls air leakage and is completed only from the interior, after the exterior flashing has passed inspection. Do not use interior foam or sealant as a substitute for a missing or incomplete exterior flashing layer, and do not seal the exterior drainage outlet to reduce air leakage.

Measurement Cue — Head-Flashing Projection and Jamb Overlap

Extend head flashing beyond both jambs by the projection distance specified in the flashing manufacturer's instructions, and overlap jamb flashing onto the sill-pan upturns by the distance specified in the sill-pan and flashing instructions. Do not estimate these dimensions by eye; measure and record each one on the door-flashing inspection record.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Seal the Sill-Pan Outlet or Threshold Weeps

Do not tape, caulk, or otherwise seal across the sill-pan outlet or threshold weeps unless the approved system specifically requires it. Sealing a designed drainage opening traps water at the sill and threshold, creating a concealed water path directly beneath the door that is far more difficult to diagnose once trim and siding are installed.

Field Safety Cue — Elevated Head-Flashing Work

Use approved ladders, scaffolds, or platforms for head-flashing installation above the approved fall-protection threshold. Do not stand on the door threshold, an open door slab, or an unsecured platform while installing head flashing or WRB head-flap integration.

Manufacturer Cue — Flashing, Primer, and Foam Compatibility

Confirm that the selected flashing tape or membrane, primer, sealant, and low-expansion foam are identified as compatible with the door frame material, the WRB, and the sill-pan material by the applicable manufacturer instructions. Do not substitute a generic tape, sealant, or foam not identified as compatible.

Code Alert — Localized Water-Control Observation Requirements Vary

Requirements for a localized water-control observation or test after this assembly is complete vary by jurisdiction and project specification. Verify the current requirement, approved protocol, and required cure conditions with the authority having jurisdiction and the general contractor before conducting any observation or test.

E. Document Review and Pre-Flashing Verification (Steps 1–3)

  1. Review the door, WRB, flashing, head-flashing, threshold-sealant, and trim-system instructions. Confirm the flashing sequence, tape widths, primer requirements, head-flashing detail, interior air-seal method, and prohibited sealant locations before beginning.
  2. Recheck door frame plumb, square, reveal, latch, threshold bearing, and operation before concealing fasteners or shims. Do not proceed if any Project 08 acceptance condition has changed.
  3. Confirm that the sill-pan drainage path remains open. Inspect the sill pan visually for debris, damage, or an unintended seal before covering it with jamb flashing.

F. Jamb and Head Flashing Installation (Steps 4–9)

  1. Clean and prime jamb, flange, frame, sheathing, and WRB surfaces where required. Observe the primer manufacturer's specified open time before applying flashing.
  2. Install jamb flashing from bottom to top with the required overlap onto the sill-pan upturns. Begin at the sill-pan upturn and work upward so each higher piece overlaps the piece below it.
  3. Maintain full contact without bridging gaps or pulling the frame out of alignment. Roll the flashing with a J-roller and recheck frame plumb after each jamb is complete.
  4. Install rigid or flexible head flashing with the required end dams, projection, and extension beyond the jambs. Confirm the head flashing is level before fastening or adhering it.
  5. Integrate the WRB head flap over the head flashing in shingle-lap order. Fold the head flap down over the head flashing so water sheds outward onto the flashing, not behind it.
  6. Tape or patch flap cuts using the approved sequence. Treat every WRB cut made to accommodate the door opening as a discontinuity requiring patching.

G. Sealant, Trim Backing, and Interior Air Sealing (Steps 10–15)

  1. Seal designated frame-to-pan, threshold-to-floor, and threshold-end joints with compatible sealant. Apply sealant only at the joints identified on the sealant schedule.
  2. Keep the sill-pan outlet and any threshold weeps open. Inspect the drainage path immediately after sealant application to confirm it remains clear.
  3. Install approved exterior trim backing, furring, or mounting support without interrupting drainage. Position backing so it does not bridge or block the rainscreen cavity.
  4. Integrate trim backing with the rainscreen or cladding support system where applicable. Confirm the backing aligns with the furring plane established in Project 10.
  5. From the interior, air-seal the frame-to-rough-opening gap using the approved backer rod, sealant, or low-expansion foam. Install backer rod to the controlled depth before applying sealant, or dispense foam in the bead size specified by the manufacturer.
  6. Recheck door operation during and after air-seal installation. Confirm the door swings freely and the latch engages without binding as the foam or sealant cures.

H. Inspection and Documentation (Steps 16–19)

  1. Inspect all jamb laps, head flashing, threshold seals, sill drainage, interior air seal, and trim support. Compare each condition against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Correct reverse laps, open corners, blocked drainage, incompatible sealant, bowed jambs, or failed adhesion. Remove and replace any failed section rather than adding material over a defective layer.
  3. Conduct only the approved localized water-control observation or test after products have reached the required condition. Follow the approved protocol, cure-time requirement, and interior monitoring method for the observation.
  4. Document the completed concealed door-flashing assembly with photographs and records. Label each photograph with the door location and the detail shown before rainscreen installation begins.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed door-flashing assembly using the same checks regardless of door location.

  • Door-condition check: confirm plumb, square, reveal, latch, and threshold bearing remain within the Project 08 acceptance standard.
  • Jamb-flashing check: confirm bottom-to-top order, full contact, and required overlap onto the sill-pan upturns.
  • Head-flashing check: confirm end dams, projection, extension beyond jambs, and WRB head-flap integration.
  • Sealant check: confirm sealant is present only at designated joints and drainage openings remain open.
  • Trim-backing check: confirm backing does not interrupt rainscreen drainage or cladding support continuity.
  • Air-seal check: confirm the interior perimeter gap is sealed without bowing the frame and the door remains operational.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Jamb Flashing Bridges a Gap or Pulls the Frame Out of Alignment

Probable cause: flashing was applied without full contact against an irregular surface, or tension in the tape distorted the jamb during rolling.
Confirmation: visually inspect for voids behind the flashing and recheck jamb plumb against the Project 08 record.
Correction: remove the affected section, correct the substrate condition, and reapply flashing with full contact using a J-roller.
Reinspection: repeat the contact and plumb check at the corrected location.

Head Flashing Lacks Required Projection or End Dams

Probable cause: the head-flashing component was cut to the opening width rather than to the specified projection, or end dams were omitted during fabrication.
Confirmation: measure the installed projection beyond each jamb and inspect for end-dam presence and orientation.
Correction: remove and replace the head flashing with a correctly sized and formed component before installing the WRB head flap.
Reinspection: repeat the projection and end-dam check before proceeding to WRB integration.

Sealant Is Found at the Sill-Pan Outlet or Threshold Weep

Probable cause: sealant was applied along the entire threshold joint rather than only at the designated locations identified on the sealant schedule.
Confirmation: inspect the full threshold length against the sealant schedule and confirm the drainage outlet location.
Correction: remove the sealant from the drainage opening without damaging the sill pan, and confirm the outlet is fully clear.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage-path check after correction and before trim backing is installed.

The Door Binds or Fails to Latch After Interior Air Sealing

Probable cause: low-expansion foam overfilled the perimeter gap and expanded against the frame, or backer rod was overcompressed against the jamb.
Confirmation: operate the door and compare current reveal and latch engagement against the Project 08 acceptance record.
Correction: remove the excess foam or backer rod causing the distortion and reapply using the manufacturer's specified bead size or rod diameter.
Reinspection: repeat the door-operation check immediately after correction and again after full cure.

WRB Head-Flap Integration Shows a Reverse Lap

Probable cause: the head flap was folded under the head flashing instead of over it, or the flap was cut too short to fold down completely.
Confirmation: inspect the lap direction at the head flashing to confirm water sheds outward and onto the flashing rather than behind it.
Correction: remove the reversed flap section, extend or replace the WRB as required, and reinstall the flap in shingle-lap order over the head flashing.
Reinspection: repeat the lap-direction check before trim backing conceals the detail.

Trim Backing Blocks the Rainscreen Drainage Cavity

Probable cause: trim backing was installed flush against the WRB without maintaining the rainscreen gap established in Project 10, or backing was sized wider than the approved detail.
Confirmation: measure the drainage gap behind the trim backing at multiple points and compare against the approved rainscreen detail.
Correction: remove and replace the backing with correctly sized material that preserves the specified gap.
Reinspection: repeat the gap measurement after correction and before Project 10 furring is installed around the opening.
