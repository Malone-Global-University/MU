Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install the approved rainscreen drainage and ventilation layer over the completed WRB from Projects 2 and 3, and over the flashed and integrated window and door openings from Projects 4 through 9. The finished work product is a continuous, securely supported rainscreen cavity — vertical furring, cross-furring, drainage mat, or profiled WRB, as specified by the approved cladding system — that drains, ventilates, and provides fastening backing for the siding installed in Project 12 or 13.

You will confirm the approved cladding system and rainscreen type, verify that WRB and opening preparation have passed inspection, map stud locations and fastening zones, establish the bottom drainage and ventilation termination, install insect screen or a vent strip while keeping the outlet open, cut and install furring strips or drainage mat without damaging the WRB, fasten through the WRB and sheathing into structural backing, continue the drainage gap around every opening and penetration, integrate mounting blocks and trim support, and inspect and document the completed rainscreen before it is concealed by siding.

This project follows Project 9, which completed the flashed and air-sealed door assembly, and follows the window flashing completed in Project 6. It sits immediately before Project 11, which lays out the siding starter courses and corner trim over the rainscreen plane established here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-055 — Identify the rainscreen type, drainage-gap requirement, and fastening pattern required by the approved cladding system.
  • BE-056 — Install furring strips, drainage mat, or a profiled WRB rainscreen product to the approved layout, spacing, and orientation.
  • BE-057 — Maintain the specified ventilation gap and drainage continuity around openings, corners, penetrations, and trim backing.
  • BE-058 — Fasten rainscreen components through the WRB and sheathing into structural backing using the specified corrosion-resistant fasteners and embedment.
  • BE-059 — Protect designed drainage and ventilation paths, including window head flashing, sill-pan outlets, and base drainage terminations.
  • BE-060 — Inspect rainscreen continuity, attachment, and drainage before it is concealed by siding, and document the completed installation.

Where This Skill Fits

The rainscreen cavity installed in this project is the drainage and ventilation gap that allows incidental water reaching the back of the siding to drain out and the wall assembly to dry. The furring or drainage-mat plane established here also becomes the fastening backing for the siding starter, corner trim, and courses installed in Projects 11 through 13, and it must integrate with the mounting blocks, vents, and utility-block flashing completed in Projects 16 and 17.

Every rainscreen detail installed here must preserve, not interrupt, the water-shedding work already completed: window head flashing and sill-pan outlets from Projects 4 through 6, and door head flashing and threshold drainage from Projects 7 through 9. A furring strip, fastener, or drainage-mat lap that blocks one of those outlets creates a concealed defect that the Project 19 full exterior inspection must later trace back to this project.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved cladding system, rainscreen type, drainage-gap requirement, furring material, fastener specification, insect-screen detail, opening treatment, and fire or code restrictions
  • confirming whether the project uses vertical furring, cross-furring, drainage mat, profiled WRB, or another approved system
  • verifying that WRB, window flashing, door flashing, and penetration preparation have passed inspection before rainscreen work begins
  • mapping stud locations, structural fastening zones, openings, corners, penetrations, and siding orientation
  • establishing the bottom drainage and ventilation termination and installing insect screen or a vent strip
  • cutting and installing furring strips or drainage mat without damaging the WRB, and fastening into required structural backing
  • continuing the drainage gap around windows, doors, corners, penetrations, and trim backing, and integrating mounting blocks
  • inspecting and documenting the completed rainscreen before siding conceals it

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select the rainscreen type, drainage-gap dimension, furring spacing, or fastener schedule independently of the approved cladding system and manufacturer instructions
  • begin rainscreen installation before the WRB, window flashing, door flashing, and penetration preparation have passed inspection
  • block or reduce a designed weep path, window head-flashing clearance, sill-pan outlet, base drainage termination, or rainscreen ventilation opening for any reason, including ease of fastening
  • substitute a furring material, fastener, or insect-screen product not permitted by the approved cladding system and manufacturer instructions

Any condition where WRB, flashing, or opening preparation has not passed inspection, or where structural fastening backing cannot be confirmed, must stop rainscreen work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

The rainscreen plane installed in this project is concealed permanently once siding is installed in Project 12 or 13. Treat the inspection and documentation required at the end of this project as the last opportunity to confirm drainage continuity, fastening backing, and opening clearances before they become inaccessible.

Setup Standard

Shop Tip

Map every stud location, opening edge, corner, and planned penetration on the WRB face with a visible marking method before cutting or installing a single furring strip. Locating structural fastening zones after furring is partially installed forces rework and risks fastening into an unsupported location.

Selecting the Approved Rainscreen System

Confirm at the outset whether the project specifies vertical furring, cross-furring, drainage mat, or a profiled WRB product, because each system has a different fastening pattern, drainage-gap dimension, and opening-treatment method. Do not substitute one rainscreen system for another, and do not mix furring and drainage-mat details on the same elevation unless the approved cladding system explicitly permits a transition.

Measurement Cue — Confirm the Drainage-Gap Dimension Before Cutting Furring

Verify the drainage-gap dimension specified by the approved cladding system and rainscreen manufacturer before cutting or installing furring strips or drainage mat. Do not assume a standard furring thickness provides the required gap; confirm the dimension against the manufacturer's current installation instructions for the specified product.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Fasten Into Unconfirmed Backing

Do not install a furring or mounting-block fastener into a location that has not been confirmed as structural backing by the stud finder or plan reference. A fastener driven into unsupported sheathing alone will not hold the rainscreen or the siding load it is designed to carry, and a missed structural connection discovered after siding is installed requires removing completed cladding to correct.

Field Safety Cue — Elevated Access and Cutting Operations

Use only approved ladders, scaffolds, lifts, or work platforms to reach every elevation during furring layout and installation. Support furring strips and drainage-mat rolls securely while cutting, and do not cut material while holding it against your body or an unsecured surface.

Manufacturer Cue — Furring and Drainage-Mat Product Restrictions

Confirm furring material, drainage-mat product, fastener type, embedment, and spacing against the rainscreen manufacturer's current installation guide before beginning work. Do not substitute a furring material, fastener, or insect-screen product not permitted by the manufacturer and the approved cladding system.

Code Alert — Fire and Ventilation Requirements Vary by Jurisdiction

Fire-rated furring restrictions, required ventilation area, and drainage-gap minimums vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction and the general contractor before finalizing the rainscreen layout.

E. System Confirmation and Site Verification (Steps 1–3)

  1. Review the approved cladding system, rainscreen type, drainage-gap requirement, furring material, fastener specification, insect-screen detail, opening treatment, and fire or code restrictions. Confirm the current revision of each document against the project's document-control log before using it.
  2. Confirm whether the project uses vertical furring, cross-furring, drainage mat, profiled WRB, or another approved system. Do not proceed until the specified system for each elevation is identified in writing.
  3. Verify that WRB, window flashing, door flashing, and penetration preparation have passed inspection. Confirm the inspection record for each area before covering it with furring or drainage mat.

F. Layout and Mapping (Steps 4–6)

  1. Map stud locations, structural fastening zones, openings, corners, penetrations, and siding orientation. Use the stud finder and framing plan reference, and mark each confirmed location visibly on the WRB face.
  2. Establish the bottom drainage and ventilation termination above grade, roofs, decks, and horizontal surfaces. Reference the approved clearance for that transition before marking the starting line.
  3. Install approved insect screen, vent strip, or drainage starter while keeping the outlet open. Confirm the screen or strip does not restrict the drainage or ventilation opening once installed.

G. Furring or Drainage-Mat Installation (Steps 7–12)

  1. Cut furring strips or drainage mat to controlled lengths without damaging the WRB. Support the material fully while cutting and inspect the WRB beneath each cut location afterward.
  2. Install vertical furring over framing or other approved structural fastening locations for horizontal siding. Confirm each furring strip aligns with a mapped structural fastening zone before fastening.
  3. Use cross-furring or the approved support pattern where vertical cladding requires horizontal attachment while maintaining drainage. Confirm the cross-furring pattern preserves the vertical drainage path beneath it.
  4. Fasten through the WRB and sheathing into the required backing using the specified corrosion-resistant fasteners and embedment. Confirm fastener seating without overdriving or crushing the furring or mat.
  5. Keep furring aligned, straight, and within the cladding manufacturer's spacing limits. Check alignment against the established control lines at the specified interval.
  6. Shim only with approved durable material and without creating water traps. Confirm each shim location drains and does not collect standing water behind the furring.

H. Drainage Continuity and Trim Integration (Steps 13–16)

  1. Continue the drainage gap around windows, doors, corners, penetrations, and trim backing. Confirm the gap remains open and continuous at every transition, not only across the flat wall field.
  2. Install head and sill venting or drainage closures where required. Confirm the closure matches the approved detail for that opening or termination.
  3. Do not block window head flashing, sill-pan outlets, weep paths, or base drainage. Verify each protected outlet remains fully open after furring or drainage-mat installation.
  4. Integrate mounting blocks and exterior trim support into the rainscreen plane. Confirm blocking is installed into confirmed structural backing before trim or siding installation begins.

I. Inspection and Documentation (Steps 17–19)

  1. Inspect furring attachment, spacing, plane, continuity, drainage outlet, insect protection, and opening details. Complete this inspection across every elevation before siding staging begins.
  2. Correct crushed mat, blocked gaps, loose furring, missed framing, split strips, and incompatible fasteners. Remeasure and reinspect each corrected location before continuing.
  3. Photograph and document the rainscreen before siding conceals it. Label each photograph with its elevation and location for later reference during the Project 19 full exterior inspection.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed rainscreen using the same checks regardless of which elevation or cladding system it supports.

  • System check: confirm the installed rainscreen type matches the approved cladding system for that elevation.
  • Attachment check: confirm every furring strip or mounting block is fastened into confirmed structural backing at the specified spacing and embedment.
  • Alignment check: confirm furring is straight, plumb, and within the cladding manufacturer's spacing limits.
  • Drainage check: confirm the bottom termination, insect screen, and drainage gap remain open and continuous around every opening, corner, and penetration.
  • Protected-outlet check: confirm window head flashing, sill-pan outlets, and weep paths remain unobstructed.
  • Documentation check: confirm photographs and inspection findings are recorded on the rainscreen inspection record before siding begins.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Furring Strip Rocks or Feels Unsupported at a Fastener Location

Probable cause: the fastener missed the intended structural backing, or the mapped stud location was inaccurate.
Confirmation: recheck the stud location with the stud finder and framing plan reference at the affected furring strip.
Correction: remove and relocate the fastener into confirmed structural backing at the correct spacing; do not leave the original fastener in place as a second, unsupported attachment point.
Reinspection: repeat the attachment check at the corrected location and adjacent fasteners on the same strip.

Drainage Mat Is Crushed or Compressed at a Fastener or Shim Location

Probable cause: a fastener was overdriven, or a shim was installed with excessive pressure against the mat.
Confirmation: inspect the mat's designed thickness at the affected location and compare it against adjacent, uncompressed areas.
Correction: replace the crushed section of drainage mat and reset the fastener or shim to the manufacturer's specified depth and pressure.
Reinspection: repeat the thickness and drainage-continuity check at the corrected location.

A Window Head-Flashing Clearance or Sill-Pan Outlet Is Blocked by Furring

Probable cause: furring was installed without confirming the required clearance at the flashing detail, or the furring layout did not account for the opening's projection.
Confirmation: compare the installed clearance at the flashing detail against the approved cladding system's required dimension for that opening.
Correction: remove and reposition the furring to restore the required clearance; do not trim the flashing or force the furring against it.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage-continuity check at the corrected opening before continuing furring installation on that elevation.

The Bottom Drainage Termination Outlet Is Restricted

Probable cause: insect screen or a vent strip was installed too tightly, or debris accumulated in the opening during installation.
Confirmation: inspect the termination visually and confirm the open area matches the approved detail's required dimension.
Correction: reposition or replace the screen or vent strip and remove any accumulated debris from the opening.
Reinspection: repeat the termination check across the full length of the affected elevation.

Furring Drifts Out of Plumb or Alignment Across the Elevation

Probable cause: the control line was not checked at the required interval, or cumulative shim thickness was not tracked during installation.
Confirmation: recheck plumb and alignment against the established control lines at the specified interval.
Correction: loosen and realign the drifted furring section, adjusting shims as required, before continuing installation past that point.
Reinspection: repeat the alignment check across the corrected section and the adjacent completed furring.

A Mounting Block Cannot Be Confirmed Into Structural Backing

Probable cause: the planned penetration location shifted from the approved plans, or the mapped fastening zone was inaccurate at that location.
Confirmation: recheck the mounting-block location against the penetration schedule and confirm structural backing with the stud finder or plan reference.
Correction: relocate the mounting block to confirmed backing within the approved penetration location, or obtain direction if the approved location cannot provide structural support.
Reinspection: repeat the attachment check at the corrected mounting-block location before Project 16 penetration flashing begins.

Field Terms

Rainscreen
The continuous drainage and ventilation cavity between the WRB and the siding.
Vertical Furring
Furring strips installed vertically over structural backing to support horizontal siding.
Cross-Furring
A horizontal furring layer providing fastening backing for vertical cladding while preserving vertical drainage.
Drainage Mat
A manufactured three-dimensional matting product creating a drainage and ventilation gap without furring strips.
Bottom Termination
The base condition where the rainscreen cavity begins and drainage or ventilation is kept open.
Structural Fastening Zone
A stud, header, or other approved backing location required for furring and mounting-block fasteners.
Insect Screen
A mesh component installed at rainscreen terminations to prevent insect entry while maintaining drainage and ventilation.
Drainage-Gap Dimension
The manufacturer-specified depth of the rainscreen cavity required for drainage and ventilation.
Mounting Block
A structural backing component integrated into the rainscreen plane to support a later vent, utility fixture, or trim component.
Rainscreen Inspection Record
The document recording furring or drainage-mat attachment, continuity, drainage condition, and the final inspection findings.
