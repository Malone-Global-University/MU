Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install exterior trim around one window and one exterior door on a wall that has already received its flashing, WRB integration, rainscreen support, and — depending on the selected cladding system — its siding. The finished work product is a plumb, level, correctly reveal-set trim assembly at each opening that preserves the head flashing installed in Projects 6 and 9, keeps every sill and weep drainage path open, and maintains the rainscreen cavity behind it.

You will confirm whether the selected cladding system requires trim-first or trim-after installation, verify that window and door flashing has passed inspection, measure the actual opening and siding plane, cut and install side casing with the required reveal and drainage clearance, cut and install head trim with the required overhang and head-flashing clearance, prepare and install sill trim or apron using the approved sloped and drained detail, fit door trim around the jamb and threshold while preserving threshold drainage, join trim corners using the approved joint method, apply sealant only at designated interfaces, and inspect the finished trim against the acceptance standards before documenting the completed interface.

This project follows Project 11, which established the siding layout, corner, and starter references, and follows whichever of Project 12 (horizontal lap siding) or Project 13 (vertical board-and-batten siding) was selected for the elevation being trimmed. It sits immediately before Project 15, which seals only the designated exterior joints, and before Project 16, which begins flashing wall penetrations.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-079 — Cut exterior trim components to the controlled lengths, reveals, and joint dimensions required by the approved trim schedule.
  • BE-080 — Install side casing plumb, at the required reveal, and without compressing the rainscreen cavity or damaging flashing.
  • BE-081 — Install head trim level, with the required overhang beyond the side casings, while preserving the head-flashing clearance.
  • BE-082 — Install sill trim, apron, or lower casing using the approved sloped and drained detail without blocking weeps or sill-pan outlets.
  • BE-083 — Maintain flashing clearances, rainscreen continuity, and cladding movement gaps at every trim joint and interface.
  • BE-084 — Inspect completed trim for plumb, level, reveal, joint fit, fastener seating, head-flashing projection, sill drainage, and cladding clearance.

Where This Skill Fits

Trim is the last layer installed at a window or door opening before designated sealant joints are completed in Project 15. Every water-control decision made at the opening in Projects 4 through 9 — sill-pan drainage, head-flashing projection, and jamb integration — depends on trim being installed without covering, blocking, or compressing those layers. Trim that is fastened through a flashing clearance zone, sealed across a weep, or set tight against a head flashing converts a correctly built opening into a concealed leak path that the Project 19 full exterior inspection must trace back to this project.

The reveal-setting, clearance-preserving method used here — measuring the actual opening and siding plane before cutting, maintaining designated gaps at every interface, and inspecting before sealing — is the same method applied to every remaining exterior-trim interface in this book, including the corner, roofline, and transition trim inspected in Project 19.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming whether the selected cladding system requires trim-first or trim-after installation for this elevation
  • verifying that window flashing, door flashing, head flashing, sill-pan drainage, and rainscreen support have passed inspection
  • measuring the actual opening, frame projection, siding plane, and required trim reveals
  • cutting and installing side casing, head trim, and sill trim or apron for one window and one exterior door
  • joining trim corners using the approved butt, miter, scarf, or proprietary joint method
  • applying sealant only at the designated trim-to-siding and trim-to-frame interfaces identified by the approved schedule
  • inspecting and documenting the completed trim and flashing interface

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select trim profile, thickness, joint design, or fastening schedule independently of the approved trim material and schedule
  • remove, shorten, or relocate head flashing, jamb flashing, or sill-pan components to make trim fit
  • seal across window weeps, sill-pan outlets, rainscreen ventilation openings, or other designed drainage paths
  • compress the rainscreen cavity or fasten trim into prohibited flashing or drainage zones to achieve a tighter reveal

Any condition where the approved trim reveal, joint dimension, or clearance cannot be achieved without altering flashing, blocking drainage, or compressing the rainscreen cavity must stop work at that opening until direction is obtained from the design professional or general contractor.

Trade Note

Trim is frequently treated as a cosmetic finish step, but at every window and door it is also the last chance to confirm that head flashing, sill drainage, and rainscreen ventilation remain intact before designated sealant joints close the assembly in Project 15. Treating this project as carpentry alone, rather than as a continuation of the water-control system, is a common source of blocked weeps and reverse laps discovered later.

Setup Standard

Do not begin cutting trim stock until the approved trim material, profile, joint design, fastening schedule, head-flashing detail, siding interface, and sealant plan are confirmed on site (Step 1), and until window and door flashing, head flashing, sill-pan drainage, and rainscreen support have passed inspection (Step 3).

Shop Tip

Dry-fit side casing, head trim, and sill trim at one opening before cutting the matching pieces for the second opening. Confirming the reveal, overhang, and clearance dimensions on the first opening prevents repeating a layout error across every remaining window and door on the elevation.

Trim-First Versus Trim-After Sequencing

The selected cladding system determines whether trim is installed before the adjoining siding courses or fitted after siding is complete. Confirm this sequence from the approved siding specification before cutting any component. Installing trim in the wrong order for the selected system disrupts the designed drainage relationship between trim and siding and can require removing completed siding to correct.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Reveal and Overhang Before Cutting

Measure the actual window or door frame, the siding plane, and the approved reveal dimension before cutting any side casing. Measure the required head-trim overhang beyond both side casings before cutting the head piece. Do not cut trim to a length estimated from the rough-opening dimension; use the as-built frame and siding plane measurements taken at this project.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Alter Flashing to Fit Trim

Do not cut, shorten, bend, or remove head flashing, jamb flashing, or sill-pan components to make trim fit tighter or look straighter. Altering flashing already accepted in Projects 4 through 9 reopens a water-control detail that has already passed inspection. If trim cannot be installed to the approved reveal and clearance without altering flashing, stop work at that opening and obtain direction.

Field Safety Cue — Cutting Station and Elevated Access

Set up the miter saw and circular saw on stable, level ground away from foot traffic, and support long trim stock on both sides of the blade. Use only approved ladders, scaffolds, or platforms for trim installation above the fall-protection threshold, and do not support a ladder on siding, trim, or an unsecured surface.

Manufacturer Cue — Trim, Flashing, and Siding Compatibility

Confirm that the selected trim material is compatible with the installed flashing, siding, sealant, and fastener materials before cutting. Follow the trim manufacturer's field-cut edge treatment, coating, and fastening requirements exactly; do not substitute an uncoated cut edge or a fastener type not specified for the trim material.

Code Alert — Clearance and Fire-Separation Requirements Vary

Minimum clearances above roofs, decks, grade, and paving, and any fire-separation requirements affecting trim material or projection near property lines, vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify current requirements with the authority having jurisdiction before finalizing trim clearances.

E. Document Review and Verification (Steps 1–3)

  1. Review the approved trim material, profile, thickness, joint design, fastening schedule, edge treatment, head-flashing detail, siding interface, and sealant plan. Confirm the current revision of the trim schedule against the project's document-control log before cutting any stock.
  2. Confirm whether the selected cladding system requires trim-first or trim-after installation. Record the confirmed sequence on the trim installation record before proceeding.
  3. Verify that window and door flashing, head flashing, sill-pan drainage, and rainscreen support have passed inspection. Do not begin cutting or fastening trim at any opening whose flashing inspection remains open.

F. Layout and Side Casing (Steps 4–9)

  1. Inspect trim stock for damage, moisture condition, straightness, coating, and compatibility with adjacent materials. Set aside any piece with damage, excess moisture, or coating defects before it is cut.
  2. Measure the actual opening, frame projection, siding plane, and required trim reveals. Take all measurements from the as-built condition at each opening rather than from the rough-opening schedule.
  3. Establish reference lines for side, head, and sill trim. Mark the reveal line at the frame and the outer trim edge line at the siding plane before any cut is made.
  4. Cut side casing to controlled lengths and prepare field-cut ends. Cut each piece to the length established from the reference lines and treat field-cut ends according to the trim manufacturer's requirement.
  5. Install side trim plumb with the required reveal and drainage clearance. Check plumb with the level at both the top and bottom of each casing before fastening.
  6. Fasten through the approved support without compressing the rainscreen cavity or damaging flashing. Confirm fastener length and location avoid the furring cavity and any concealed flashing layer.

G. Head Trim (Steps 10–13)

  1. Measure and cut the head trim or head casing to provide the required overhang beyond side trim. Confirm the overhang dimension at both ends before cutting.
  2. Install or preserve the rigid head flashing above the trim in shingle-lap order. Do not remove, shift, or shorten the head flashing installed in Project 6 or Project 9 to accommodate the head trim.
  3. Maintain the required gap between head flashing and siding or trim components. Verify the gap with a measuring tool rather than by visual estimate before fastening the head trim.
  4. Install head trim level and aligned with the side casings. Check level along the full length of the head trim and confirm consistent contact with both side casings.

H. Sill Trim and Door Trim (Steps 14–16)

  1. Prepare and install sill trim, apron, or lower casing using the approved sloped and drained detail. Confirm the outward slope of the sill trim matches the approved detail before fastening.
  2. Do not seal across window weeps, sill-pan outlets, or lower drainage gaps. Identify and mark every weep and outlet location before installing sill trim so it is not inadvertently covered or sealed.
  3. Fit door trim around the jamb and threshold while preserving threshold drainage and required clearance. Confirm threshold weeps or drainage gaps remain open after door trim is installed.

I. Corner Joints and Sealant (Steps 17–18)

  1. Join trim corners using the approved butt, miter, scarf, or proprietary joint method. Do not substitute a different joint method than the one specified in the approved trim schedule.
  2. Apply sealant or joint treatment only at designated interfaces. Confirm each interface against the approved sealant schedule before applying any bead, and leave every identified drainage opening unsealed.

J. Inspection and Documentation (Steps 19–21)

  1. Inspect plumb, level, reveal, joint fit, fastener seating, head-flashing projection, sill drainage, and cladding clearance. Check every measurement against the Acceptance Standards table before considering the opening complete.
  2. Correct tight joints, reverse laps, blocked weeps, split trim, unsupported ends, and misaligned reveals. Remove and replace any component that cannot be corrected within the approved tolerance.
  3. Document the completed trim and flashing interface. Photograph each opening from a distance that shows the full trim assembly and its relationship to head flashing and sill drainage, and record findings on the trim installation record.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed trim assembly at each opening using the same checks regardless of profile or cladding system.

  • Sequence check: confirm the trim-first or trim-after installation matches the selected cladding system for that elevation.
  • Casing check: confirm side casing is plumb, at the approved reveal, and fastened without compressing the rainscreen cavity.
  • Head trim check: confirm head trim is level, provides the required overhang, and preserves the head-flashing clearance.
  • Sill and threshold check: confirm sill trim, apron, and door threshold drainage remain sloped and unobstructed.
  • Joint check: confirm every corner joint matches the approved method and fits without gaps or forced contact.
  • Sealant check: confirm sealant appears only at designated interfaces and every drainage opening remains clear.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Side Casing Is Not Plumb or the Reveal Varies Along Its Length

Probable cause: the casing was fastened to the frame edge by eye rather than to a marked reveal line, or the frame itself is not perfectly plumb.
Confirmation: check plumb and reveal at the top, middle, and bottom of the casing against the marked reference line.
Correction: remove and reset the casing to the marked reveal line, shimming only where the approved detail permits it.
Reinspection: recheck plumb and reveal along the full length after refastening.

Head Trim Contacts or Crowds the Head Flashing

Probable cause: the head trim was cut and installed without first measuring the required clearance beneath the head flashing.
Confirmation: measure the gap between the head flashing and the top edge of the head trim against the approved clearance dimension.
Correction: remove and recut the head trim to restore the required clearance; do not trim, bend, or relocate the head flashing to create clearance.
Reinspection: recheck the clearance dimension and head-trim level after correction.

Sill Trim or Apron Blocks a Weep or Sill-Pan Outlet

Probable cause: the sill trim was measured and installed without first marking the weep and outlet locations.
Confirmation: inspect the sill trim for coverage over any marked weep or outlet location.
Correction: remove and recut the sill trim to clear the drainage opening, or notch the trim according to the approved detail.
Reinspection: confirm the weep or outlet drains freely after correction.

A Mitered or Scarfed Corner Joint Is Open or Forced

Probable cause: the cut angle was not verified against the actual corner condition, or the trim stock shifted during cutting.
Confirmation: dry-fit the joint before fastening and check for a consistent, unforced contact along the full joint line.
Correction: recut the affected piece rather than forcing the joint closed with fasteners or filling the gap with sealant.
Reinspection: recheck the dry-fit and reveal alignment after the corrected piece is cut.

Sealant Appears at a Weep, Vent, or Other Drainage Opening

Probable cause: the sealant bead was applied along the full trim-to-siding joint without first identifying and marking the designated interfaces.
Confirmation: compare the sealed locations against the approved sealant schedule and the marked drainage-opening locations.
Correction: remove the sealant from the drainage opening using the approved method without damaging surrounding trim or siding.
Reinspection: confirm the opening drains freely and that remaining sealant is limited to designated interfaces.

Trim Fasteners Compress the Rainscreen Cavity

Probable cause: fasteners longer than specified were used, or trim was pulled tight against the wall before the furring or drainage mat thickness was accounted for.
Confirmation: inspect the furring or drainage mat behind the trim for crushing or reduced cavity depth at fastener locations.
Correction: remove and refasten with the specified fastener length and seating condition, restoring the designed cavity depth.
Reinspection: recheck cavity depth at each corrected fastener location.

Field Terms

Reveal
The uniform margin left between the trim edge and the window or door frame.
Trim-First System
A cladding system in which trim is installed before the adjoining siding courses.
Trim-After System
A cladding system in which siding is installed first and trim is fitted against it.
Head-Flashing Clearance
The required gap between head flashing and the top edge of the head trim.
Drainage Clearance
The minimum gap maintained so water can discharge outward past trim without obstruction.
Designated Interface
A trim joint or trim-to-siding interface identified by the approved sealant schedule as requiring sealant.
Apron
The horizontal trim piece installed below a window sill trim, often supporting the sill's visual and drainage detail.
Scarf Joint
An angled lap joint between two trim pieces, used where the approved schedule specifies it in place of a butt or miter joint.
