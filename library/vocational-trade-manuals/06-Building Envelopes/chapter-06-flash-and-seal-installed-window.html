Job Overview

This project qualifies you to complete the exterior flashing and interior air-seal layers around one new-construction window that was set, aligned, fastened, and function-tested in Project 05. The finished work product is a fully integrated window installation that sheds exterior water, preserves sill-pan drainage, limits air leakage at the rough-opening gap, and remains fully operational.

You will review the approved flashing sequence, confirm the window remains level, plumb, square, and secure, inspect the sill pan and lower flange, install jamb flashing tape from bottom to top, install head flashing and integrate the WRB head flap over it, and then work from the interior to install backer rod and low-expansion foam or sealant in the perimeter gap without disturbing the shims or blocking exterior drainage. The project ends with a full inspection of every lap, flashing layer, and air seal, and a photographed, documented record of the completed assembly.

This project assumes the Project 05 window installation has passed inspection and remains level, plumb, square, and operational. It sits immediately after Project 05 and before Project 07, which prepares the exterior-door sill pan on a separate opening.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-031 — Apply self-adhered flashing tape to the window flange in the correct shingle-lap sequence.
  • BE-032 — Integrate rigid or flexible head flashing above the window so water from above sheds outward over the assembly.
  • BE-033 — Seal the side flanges with compatible flashing tape while maintaining full contact and avoiding wrinkles or fishmouths.
  • BE-034 — Maintain the window's designed weep paths and sill-pan drainage outlet throughout flashing and sealing.
  • BE-035 — Air-seal the interior perimeter gap using backer rod and low-expansion foam or an approved sealant without bowing the frame.
  • BE-036 — Inspect the completed window water-control and air-seal assembly and correct failed laps, blocked drainage, or bowed frames.

Where This Skill Fits

The rough-opening flashing prepared in Project 04 and the window set and fastened in Project 05 both depend on this project to become a complete, water-shedding assembly. The jamb and head flashing installed here integrate with the WRB courses from Project 02 and 03, and the interior air seal completed here is the last opportunity to control air leakage at this opening before rainscreen furring in Project 10 and siding in Project 12 or 13 conceal the wall.

The shingle-lap sequence used here — sill, then jambs, then head, each layer overlapping the one below it — is the same drainage logic applied at every other opening and penetration in this book, including the door flashing in Project 09 and the penetration flashing in Project 16.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the window and flashing manufacturers' approved flange-flashing sequence, tape widths, primer requirements, head-flashing detail, interior air-seal method, and prohibited sealant locations
  • confirming the window remains level, plumb, square, operational, and securely fastened before covering the flange
  • inspecting the sill pan and lower flange for damage, contamination, and open drainage paths
  • installing jamb flashing tape from bottom to top with the required overlap onto the sill flashing
  • installing head flashing or a rigid head-flashing component and integrating the WRB head flap over it
  • air-sealing the interior perimeter gap with backer rod and low-expansion foam or an approved sealant
  • inspecting the completed assembly and correcting failed laps, blocked drainage, bowed frames, or voids
  • photographing and documenting the completed window water-control and air-seal assembly

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • tape or seal across the exterior sill drainage opening unless the approved system specifically requires it
  • reset, re-shim, or re-fasten the window frame beyond correcting a condition identified during this project's inspection
  • substitute a flashing tape, primer, head-flashing component, or air-seal product not approved by the window or WRB manufacturer
  • proceed to rainscreen or siding work before this project's inspection is complete and documented

Any condition that does not match the approved plans, or that suggests the window has shifted, racked, or lost operation since Project 05, must stop work at that opening until the condition is corrected and reinspected.

Trade Note

Every layer installed in this project is sequenced so that water moving down the wall always sheds onto the layer below it, never behind it. Working out of order — for example, installing head flashing before the jamb tape, or taping the head flap before the head flashing is in place — creates a reverse lap that channels water into the wall instead of out of it.

Setup Standard

Shop Tip

Dry-fit every piece of jamb and head flashing tape before removing the release liner. Confirm the overlap distance onto the sill flashing and the extension beyond the jambs match the approved detail before committing the adhesive, since repositioning pressure-sensitive tape after full contact often tears the backing.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every water-control detail in this book follows the same shingle-lap logic: the lowest layer is installed first, and each layer above it overlaps the one below rather than tucking behind it. This project applies that logic at the window flange and head, continuing the sequence established at the sill pan in Project 04 and carried forward at the door in Project 09 and at penetrations in Project 16.

Keeping Drainage Open While Sealing Air Leakage

This project asks you to do two things that can seem contradictory: seal the wall against air leakage while keeping it open to drainage. The distinction is location. Air sealing happens at the interior perimeter gap, behind the drainage plane. Drainage happens at the exterior sill-pan outlet and any designed weep. Never let an exterior tape, primer, or sealant bridge the sill-pan outlet, and never let the interior air seal extend into a location where it would block water that has already entered the wall from draining back out.

Measurement Cue — Verify Window Condition Before Covering the Flange

Recheck sill level, jamb plumb, head level, and frame square against the Project 05 installation record before installing any tape or flashing. Do not assume the window remains in the same condition it was left in; confirm it directly. Covering a flange over a window that has shifted conceals the problem instead of correcting it.

Critical Safety Cue — Never Bridge the Sill Drainage Opening

Do not tape or seal across the exterior sill drainage opening unless the approved system specifically requires it. Bridging this opening with tape, primer, or sealant traps water inside the sill pan with no path to the exterior, turning a designed drainage feature into a reservoir that will eventually leak into the wall or interior finishes.

Field Safety Cue — Working at the Interior Perimeter Gap

Clear debris from the perimeter gap before installing backer rod or foam, and confirm shims remain in their approved locations. Do not reach into the gap with unprotected hands where shim edges or fastener points are exposed.

Manufacturer Cue — Tape, Primer, and Foam Compatibility

Use only the flashing tape, primer, head-flashing component, and interior air-seal product identified as compatible by the window and WRB manufacturers. Confirm temperature and substrate limits for tape adhesion and primer cure before beginning work in cold or damp conditions.

Code Alert — Air-Sealing Requirements Vary by Jurisdiction

Required air-sealing methods, materials, and inspection sign-offs at window rough openings vary by adopted energy code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction before treating the interior air seal as complete.

E. Review and Pre-Flashing Verification (Steps 1–4)

  1. Review the window and flashing manufacturers' approved flange-flashing sequence, tape widths, primer requirements, head-flashing detail, interior air-seal method, and prohibited sealant locations. Confirm the current revision of each instruction against the project's document-control log.
  2. Confirm that the window remains level, plumb, square, operational, and securely fastened before covering the flange. Recheck sill level, jamb plumb, head level, frame square, and sash operation against the Project 05 installation record.
  3. Inspect the sill pan and lower flange for damage, contamination, and open drainage paths. Clear debris and confirm the sill-pan outlet remains unobstructed.
  4. Do not tape or seal across the exterior sill drainage opening unless the approved system specifically requires it. Mark the outlet location clearly before beginning tape installation nearby.

F. Jamb and Head Flashing (Steps 5–14)

  1. Clean and prime the flange, sheathing, and WRB surfaces as required. Observe the manufacturer's specified open time before applying tape over primed surfaces.
  2. Cut jamb flashing long enough to extend beyond the flange and overlap the sill flashing by the required amount. Confirm the cut length against the approved detail before removing the release liner.
  3. Install the first jamb flashing from bottom to top without wrinkles or fishmouths. Work in short sections, pressing the tape into full contact as you go.
  4. Install the opposite jamb flashing using the same reference and overlap. Confirm both jambs match in overlap distance and coverage.
  5. Roll pressure-sensitive flashing to achieve full contact at flange edges and transitions. Use a seam roller or J-roller and work outward from the center of each strip.
  6. Install head flashing or a rigid head-flashing component in the required position above the window. Confirm the component sits above the flange and window head in the position specified by the approved detail.
  7. Extend the head flashing beyond both jambs by the approved distance. Measure and confirm the extension on each side before fastening or adhering the component.
  8. Integrate the WRB head flap over the head flashing so water from above sheds outward. Lower the head flap down over the head flashing in shingle-lap order; do not tuck it behind the flashing.
  9. Tape diagonal flap cuts and upper flap edges only as required without creating reverse laps. Confirm each taped edge overlaps the layer below it.
  10. Inspect corner transitions and patch any exposed sheathing or discontinuity. Use the approved patch method for any gap found at the head-to-jamb corner.

G. Interior Air Seal (Steps 15–18)

  1. From the interior, remove debris from the perimeter gap and verify that shims remain in the approved locations. Confirm no shim has shifted or fallen since Project 05.
  2. Install backer rod, low-expansion window-and-door foam, sealant, or another approved air-seal system without bowing the frame. Apply foam in controlled passes, allowing expansion room rather than overfilling the gap.
  3. Maintain the required separation between interior air sealing and exterior drainage. Confirm the air seal does not extend through the gap to the exterior drainage plane.
  4. Recheck window operation after foam or sealant installation and during curing where required. Operate the sash or panel at the interval specified by the product instructions to confirm the frame has not bowed.

H. Inspection and Documentation (Steps 19–21)

  1. Inspect exterior laps, flange coverage, head flashing, sill drainage, interior air seal, and frame condition. Compare every element against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Correct failed adhesion, blocked weeps, bowed frames, voids, or reverse laps using the approved repair method. Remove and replace failed sections rather than layering new material over a defective one.
  3. Photograph and document the completed window water-control and air-seal assembly. Label each photograph with the opening ID for later reference at the Project 19 full exterior inspection.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed window flashing and air-seal assembly using the same checks regardless of which opening it covers.

  • Condition check: confirm the window remains level, plumb, square, securely fastened, and operational.
  • Jamb check: confirm jamb flashing is installed bottom to top with full contact and the required overlap onto the sill flashing.
  • Head check: confirm head flashing extends beyond both jambs by the approved distance and the WRB head flap is integrated over it in shingle-lap order.
  • Drainage check: confirm the sill-pan outlet remains open and unobstructed by tape, primer, or sealant.
  • Air-seal check: confirm backer rod and foam or sealant fill the perimeter gap without bowing the frame or blocking exterior drainage.
  • Documentation check: confirm the completed assembly is photographed and recorded on the window flashing and air-seal record.

Complete the Window Flashing and Air-Seal Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 07.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Flashing Tape Shows Wrinkles or Fishmouths

Probable cause: the tape was applied too quickly without working out air pockets, or it was applied outside the manufacturer's temperature range.
Confirmation: inspect the affected section for trapped air or incomplete adhesive contact.
Correction: remove and replace the affected section using the approved patch or reinstallation method; do not smooth a wrinkle over trapped air.
Reinspection: repeat the adhesion and contact check across the full jamb or head section after correction.

Head Flashing Does Not Extend Far Enough Beyond the Jambs

Probable cause: the head-flashing component was cut short, or the required extension distance was measured incorrectly.
Confirmation: measure the installed extension on each side against the approved detail.
Correction: remove and replace the head-flashing component with one cut to the correct length.
Reinspection: repeat the extension measurement on both sides after replacement.

Sealant or Tape Bridges the Sill Drainage Opening

Probable cause: the sill-pan outlet was not clearly marked before nearby tape or sealant work began.
Confirmation: inspect the sill-pan outlet for tape, primer, or sealant residue blocking the opening.
Correction: remove the obstructing material without damaging the sill pan and confirm the outlet drains freely.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage check by confirming water introduced at the sill pan exits through the outlet.

The Frame Bows or Binds After Foam Installation

Probable cause: excessive foam expansion pressure at the perimeter gap, or foam applied faster than the manufacturer's fill rate.
Confirmation: operate the sash or panel and compare resistance against the pre-foam operation check.
Correction: remove the affected foam section and reapply using controlled passes with adequate expansion room, per manufacturer instructions.
Reinspection: recheck frame dimensions and operation after the replacement foam has cured.

A Shim Has Shifted or Is Missing Before Air Sealing

Probable cause: the shim was disturbed during flange-flashing work or during handling between Project 05 and this project.
Confirmation: inspect each approved shim location and compare against the Project 05 installation record.
Correction: reinstall or replace the shim at the approved location before proceeding with the air seal.
Reinspection: recheck frame level, plumb, and square after the shim is corrected.

Cleanup

This project ends with a flashed, drained, air-sealed, and operational window installation, not with rainscreen, siding, or trim installed.

  • Remove masking materials, tape backing scraps, and foam applicator debris from the work area
  • Wipe excess primer, foam, or sealant from the frame and adjacent WRB before it cures
  • Protect the completed flashing and air seal from impact and contamination until rainscreen work begins
  • Store the window flashing and air-seal record and supporting photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open discrepancy and its required correction to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Project 07 begins

Field Terms

Shingle-Lap Order
The bottom-to-top sequence in which each water-control layer overlaps the layer below it.
Flange
The nailing fin around the perimeter of the window unit.
Head Flap
WRB material left unfastened above the opening, integrated over the head flashing.
Reverse Lap
A condition where an upper layer tucks behind a lower layer, directing water inward instead of outward.
Interior Perimeter Gap
The shimmed space between the window frame and the rough-opening framing.
Sill-Pan Outlet
The designed exterior drainage opening at the base of the sill pan.
Backer Rod
Closed-cell foam rod installed to control sealant or foam depth and prevent three-sided adhesion.
Low-Expansion Foam
Window-and-door-rated foam formulated to expand minimally, limiting frame distortion.
Fishmouth
A raised, unadhered edge of flashing tape that traps water instead of shedding it.
Window Flashing and Air-Seal Record
The document recording flashing products, installation conditions, inspection results, and the completed assembly.
