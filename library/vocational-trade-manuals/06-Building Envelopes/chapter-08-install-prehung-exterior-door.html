Job Overview

This project qualifies you to set, align, fasten, and function-test one prehung exterior door in the sill-pan opening prepared in Project 07. The finished work product is a securely installed, square, level, plumb, and fully operational prehung exterior door, ready for the exterior jamb flashing, head flashing, threshold sealing, and interior air sealing completed in Project 09.

You will review the door manufacturer's installation instructions, inspect the unit for transport damage, confirm the sill pan has passed inspection, dry-fit the unit, apply sealant at approved threshold locations, set the frame, plumb and shim the hinge jamb, fasten the frame in the controlled sequence, adjust the latch jamb for a consistent reveal, install security fasteners, verify hardware operation, and document the completed installation.

This project follows directly after Project 07, which installed the drained sill pan this door frame now bears on, and sits immediately before Project 09, which flashes and weatherproofs the installed door without disturbing its square, plumb, and operational condition.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-043 — Set a prehung exterior door into a prepared sill-pan opening using dry-fit verification and the approved handling method.
  • BE-044 — Shim the door jambs at manufacturer-required locations without bowing the frame.
  • BE-045 — Plumb the hinge side of the frame using an approved reference surface before fastening.
  • BE-046 — Fasten the door frame in the controlled sequence, type, and location specified by the manufacturer.
  • BE-047 — Verify and adjust the door reveal, latch engagement, and threshold contact for consistent, binding-free operation.
  • BE-048 — Inspect the completed door installation for fastener seating, shim support, reveal, frame projection, threshold bearing, and operation.

Where This Skill Fits

The sill pan installed in Project 07 only performs correctly if the door bearing on it remains square, level, and free of racking once fastened. A frame that is forced into an opening, fastened out of plumb, or shimmed unevenly will bind, fail to latch, or place uneven load on the threshold and sill-pan components beneath it. The alignment and fastening method used here also controls the reveal that the Project 09 jamb and head flashing must fit against without gaps or excessive compression.

The set-shim-plumb-fasten-verify sequence used here is the same method used to install the new-construction window in Project 05, adapted to a door frame's hinge-driven alignment and its threshold bearing condition. Learning to plumb from the hinge side first, rather than centering the unit and fastening symmetrically, protects every later door installation in this book.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the door manufacturer's installation instructions, frame-fastening schedule, shim locations, threshold support, security fasteners, sealant locations, and warranty requirements
  • inspecting the prehung unit for transport damage and confirming the sill pan remains clean, undamaged, and open to drain
  • dry-fitting the unit and verifying perimeter clearance, threshold bearing, floor transition, exterior projection, and swing clearance
  • applying sealant or bedding material at approved threshold locations while preserving sill-pan drainage
  • setting, plumbing, shimming, and fastening the frame in the required sequence
  • adjusting the latch jamb for a consistent reveal and installing security fasteners at designated locations
  • verifying latch, deadbolt, weather-stripping, sweep, threshold, and closer operation
  • inspecting the completed installation and documenting measurements, adjustments, and fastener locations

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • install a door before the Project 07 sill pan has passed inspection and remains open to drain
  • enlarge, reframe, or otherwise alter the rough opening or sill-pan components to force an out-of-tolerance unit to fit
  • substitute fasteners, shim material, or a fastening sequence not specified by the door manufacturer
  • begin exterior jamb flashing, head flashing, or threshold sealant work covered in Project 09 before this project's operation and inspection checks are complete

Any transport damage, warped jamb, cracked threshold, or sill-pan condition that does not meet the Project 07 acceptance standards must stop work in that opening until it is corrected.

Trade Note

A prehung door frame is manufactured square and plumb at the factory. Every measurement problem found during this project is the fault of the opening, the sill pan, or the installation sequence, not the door unit. Chase discrepancies back to the opening and the sill pan before assuming the unit itself is out of tolerance.

Setup Standard

Do not set the door until the Project 07 sill pan has passed inspection and its drainage outlet remains open (Step 3). Do not remove factory packaging or bracing from an operating unit except as directed by the manufacturer, and keep the door slab locked or braced closed during handling (Step 4).

Shop Tip

Dry-fit the unit completely before applying any sealant. Correcting an opening or sill-pan condition is far easier with the door out of the opening than after bedding material has been applied and the frame has been temporarily fastened.

Reference Conventions

Establish these references before dry-fit begins, and do not change them once the frame is permanently fastened.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every prehung unit installed in this book, whether a window in Project 05 or a door in this project, follows the same underlying method: dry-fit before sealing, plumb from one controlling reference before fastening the rest of the frame, shim only at approved locations, fasten in the manufacturer's controlled sequence, and verify operation before and after each fastening phase. A door frame differs from a window frame in one important respect: it carries a moving slab under continuous use, so its hinge-side plumb and its reveal consistency directly control whether it will operate correctly for the life of the building.

Dry-fitUnit checked for damage and opening fit before any sealant is applied
Set and plumbFrame centered, hinge jamb plumbed, and shims installed at approved locations
FastenHinge jamb fastened first, latch jamb adjusted, then remaining jambs fastened
VerifyReveal, latch, threshold, and operation confirmed before and after fastening

Why the Hinge Jamb Is Plumbed First

The hinge jamb controls the swing arc of the door slab. If it is out of plumb, the slab will swing open or closed under its own weight, bind against the frame, or fail to seat against the weather stripping evenly. Plumbing and fastening the hinge jamb first, then adjusting the latch jamb to match the resulting reveal, is the only sequence that reliably produces a door that neither swings on its own nor binds.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Sill-Pan Drainage Before Setting the Door

Before setting the unit, confirm the Project 07 sill-pan outlet remains open and unobstructed, and confirm the pan surface is clean and dry. Do not proceed if the sill pan shows standing water, debris, or damage; correct the condition and reinspect before continuing.

Critical Safety Cue — Controlled Handling of the Prehung Unit

Set the unit into the opening using the number of workers specified by the manufacturer for its size and weight. Do not attempt to lift, tilt, or maneuver a prehung door unit alone if the manufacturer specifies additional workers. An uncontrolled unit can crush hands, damage the frame, or cause a fall from an elevated threshold.

Field Safety Cue — Door Slab Control During Handling

Keep the door slab locked, braced, or otherwise secured closed throughout handling and setting. An unsecured slab can swing open unexpectedly, striking a worker or damaging the frame before the unit is fastened.

Manufacturer Cue — Frame Fastening Sequence and Security Screws

Fasten the frame in the sequence, fastener type, location, and embedment specified by the door manufacturer. Install long security screws at hinges, strike plates, or other designated locations only where the manufacturer's instructions call for them, using the specified length and drive type.

Code Alert — Threshold Accessibility and Egress Requirements

Threshold height, landing conditions, and egress clearance requirements vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction and the general contractor before finalizing threshold shimming or bedding.

E. Review and Inspection (Steps 1–3)

  1. Review the door manufacturer's installation instructions, frame-fastening schedule, shim locations, threshold support, security fasteners, sealant locations, and warranty requirements. Confirm the edition matches the installed unit's model number.
  2. Inspect the unit for transport damage, warped jambs, loose hinges, damaged weather stripping, cracked threshold, incorrect handing, and missing parts. Compare the unit against the approved door schedule before proceeding.
  3. Confirm that the sill pan has passed inspection and remains clean, undamaged, and open to drain. Do not proceed if the Project 07 record shows an open deficiency at this opening.

F. Dry-Fit and Sealant (Steps 4–7)

  1. Dry-fit the unit and verify perimeter clearance, threshold bearing, interior floor transition, exterior projection, and door swing clearance. Compare each measurement against the manufacturer's specified dimension.
  2. Remove the unit and complete only authorized corrections. Do not enlarge or reframe the opening or alter the sill pan to force an out-of-tolerance fit; mark any condition outside the permitted range for qualified review.
  3. Apply sealant or bedding material at the approved threshold locations while preserving sill-pan drainage. Keep sealant off the designated drainage outlet and any weep path.
  4. Set the unit into the opening using the required workers and handling method. Keep the door slab locked or braced closed throughout the lift and setting.

G. Plumb, Shim, and Fasten (Steps 8–16)

  1. Center the frame and temporarily secure the hinge-side upper corner. Use temporary fasteners at manufacturer-approved locations only.
  2. Plumb the hinge jamb using the approved reference surface. Recheck plumb over the full jamb height before installing shims.
  3. Install shims behind each hinge and at other manufacturer-required fastening points. Seat each shim firmly without bowing the jamb inward or outward.
  4. Fasten the hinge jamb through the frame and shims with the specified fasteners. Follow the manufacturer's fastening sequence and embedment.
  5. Check the door slab for free swing, consistent reveal, hinge binding, and threshold contact. Correct the hinge jamb before proceeding if the slab binds or racks.
  6. Adjust the latch jamb until the head and side reveals are consistent. Use the hinge-jamb reveal as the controlling reference.
  7. Install latch-side and head shims at approved locations. Confirm shim placement matches the manufacturer's shim-location diagram.
  8. Fasten the remaining jambs in the controlled sequence without bowing the frame. Check reveal consistency after each fastener is set.
  9. Install long security screws at hinges, strike plates, or other designated locations. Use only the length and drive type specified by the manufacturer.

H. Adjustment and Verification (Steps 17–23)

  1. Adjust threshold components only by the approved method. Do not shim or force the threshold in a way not permitted by the manufacturer's instructions.
  2. Verify latch, deadbolt, weather stripping, sweep, threshold, and closer operation where applicable. Operate each component through its full range before continuing.
  3. Confirm that the frame remains square and that the door does not move open or closed by itself when partially positioned. A slab that drifts under its own weight indicates an out-of-plumb hinge jamb requiring correction.
  4. Inspect fastener seating, shim support, reveal, frame projection, threshold bearing, and operation. Compare every result against the Acceptance Standards table.
  5. Correct binding, rubbing, uneven reveal, loose threshold, or failed latching before flashing work begins. Do not carry an unresolved operational defect into Project 09.
  6. Protect the installed door from impact, contamination, and unauthorized use. Restrict traffic through the opening until flashing work is scheduled.
  7. Record measurements, adjustments, fastener locations, and photographs. Complete the door installation record before ending the work session.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed door installation using the same checks regardless of which opening it covers.

  • Documentation check: confirm the manufacturer's installation instructions, fastening schedule, and sill-pan inspection record are referenced on the door installation record.
  • Unit condition check: confirm the unit is free of transport damage, warped jambs, and missing components.
  • Alignment check: confirm the hinge jamb is plumb, the frame is square, and the reveal is consistent at head and both jambs.
  • Fastening check: confirm fasteners and security screws are seated per the manufacturer's schedule without bowing the frame.
  • Operation check: confirm free swing, correct latch and deadbolt engagement, and correct weather-strip, sweep, threshold, and closer function.
  • Documentation completeness check: confirm measurements, adjustments, fastener locations, and photographs are recorded before Project 09 begins.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Door Swings Open or Closed Under Its Own Weight

Probable cause: the hinge jamb is out of plumb, or a hinge-location shim is missing or loose.
Confirmation: recheck hinge-jamb plumb over the full height with a 6-foot level and inspect each hinge shim for firm, even contact.
Correction: loosen the affected fasteners, correct the shim placement and jamb plumb, and refasten in the specified sequence.
Reinspection: repeat the free-swing check at several partial-open positions after correction.

The Door Binds or Rubs Against the Frame

Probable cause: the frame was bowed by an overtightened or misplaced shim, or the latch jamb was fastened before the reveal was adjusted to match the hinge jamb.
Confirmation: measure the reveal at the head and both jambs and compare against the manufacturer's specified dimension.
Correction: loosen the affected jamb, correct shim placement and reveal, and refasten.
Reinspection: repeat the reveal measurement and full-swing operation check.

The Latch or Deadbolt Fails to Engage

Probable cause: the latch jamb is out of alignment with the hinge jamb, or the strike plate location was not adjusted to match the final reveal.
Confirmation: check strike-plate alignment against the latch and deadbolt with the door in the closed position.
Correction: adjust the latch jamb or strike-plate position per the manufacturer's instructions and refasten security screws.
Reinspection: operate the latch and deadbolt through their full range after correction.

The Threshold Does Not Bear Evenly on the Sill Pan

Probable cause: the sill pan was not level across its full width, or required threshold support shims were omitted.
Confirmation: check threshold contact with a torpedo level across the full width and inspect for gaps beneath the threshold.
Correction: install threshold support shims only where the manufacturer permits them, without blocking the sill-pan drainage path.
Reinspection: repeat the level and bearing check after correction.

The Frame Is Racked Out of Square After Fastening

Probable cause: fasteners were driven in the wrong sequence, or one jamb was fastened without confirming diagonal measurements first.
Confirmation: measure both diagonals of the frame and compare them for equality within the manufacturer's permitted tolerance.
Correction: loosen the affected fasteners, correct alignment, and refasten in the specified sequence.
Reinspection: repeat the diagonal measurement and full operational check.

  • Store the door installation record, measurements, and photographs with the project's permanent documentation
