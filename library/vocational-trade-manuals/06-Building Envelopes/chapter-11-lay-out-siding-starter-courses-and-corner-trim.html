Job Overview

This project qualifies you to establish the cladding control lines for one prepared wall elevation and install the approved starter and corner components that the siding system depends on. The finished work product is a level, plumb, and documented siding layout, with correctly installed starter strip and corner boards, ready to receive the horizontal lap siding covered in Project 12 or the vertical board-and-batten siding covered in Project 13.

You will review the selected siding system's exposure, starter, and corner requirements, confirm that the WRB, flashing, rainscreen, and opening preparation have passed inspection, measure the wall and calculate course distribution, establish and transfer a level base reference, mark starter and corner locations, install the starter strip and corner components at the required plumb and projection, plan siding joint distribution, and inspect and document the completed layout before siding begins.

This project follows Project 10, which installed the rainscreen furring and drainage mat over the completed WRB, and it sits immediately before Project 12 or Project 13, whichever cladding system the approved wall specification calls for on this elevation.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • BE-061 — Establish a level siding reference line from the approved lowest siding elevation and transfer it around corners and across openings.
  • BE-062 — Install the approved starter strip or first-course support component while maintaining the designed drainage and ventilation opening.
  • BE-063 — Install outside and inside corner boards, posts, or proprietary corner components plumb, straight, and at the required projection from the rainscreen plane.
  • BE-064 — Verify siding layout against the approved exposure or module, distributing course heights and terminal courses according to the specification.
  • BE-065 — Plan siding end-joint and lap locations so they occur over approved support and remain distributed across the elevation.
  • BE-066 — Inspect starter level, corner plumb, projection, fastening, clearances, drainage opening, and course plan, and document the completed layout.

Where This Skill Fits

Every course installed in Project 12 or Project 13 measures itself against the control lines and starter component established here. A base reference that is not level, a starter strip that blocks its designed drainage opening, or a corner board that is not plumb will carry its error into every course above it, and that error compounds as the elevation rises. The corner projection established here also controls whether the rainscreen drainage and ventilation gap installed in Project 10 remains continuous behind the finished cladding.

The document-review, reference-establishment, and inspection method used here — confirming prior inspections, establishing and transferring a control reference, and inspecting before covering it with the next system — is the same method used throughout this book, from the Project 01 envelope-readiness decision through the Project 19 full exterior water-control inspection.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the selected siding system's exposure or module, starter component, corner detail, trim sequence, rainscreen support, fastening schedule, expansion requirements, and minimum clearances
  • confirming that the WRB, flashing, rainscreen, window and door support, and penetration preparation have passed inspection
  • measuring the wall and calculating course distribution to avoid narrow or uneven terminal courses where practical
  • establishing a level base reference and transferring it around corners and across openings
  • marking and installing the starter strip or first-course support component
  • preparing and installing outside and inside corner boards, posts, or proprietary corner components
  • maintaining required clearances above roofs, decks, paving, grade, and horizontal flashing
  • planning siding end-joint and lap distribution across the elevation
  • inspecting and documenting the completed layout before siding installation begins

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select a siding exposure, module, starter component, or corner detail independently of the approved siding specification
  • begin starter or corner installation before the WRB, flashing, rainscreen, and opening preparation have passed inspection
  • block or reduce a designed drainage or ventilation opening at the starter, corner, or base termination to simplify layout
  • mix horizontal lap and vertical board-and-batten layout details on the same elevation unless the approved design includes a transition

Any wall condition that does not match the approved plans, or a corner, opening, or transition that will not accept the specified clearance, must stop layout work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Every course installed in Project 12 or 13 is only as level, plumb, and well-drained as the reference lines and starter and corner components established in this project. Treating layout as a quick preliminary step, rather than as a control reference the entire elevation depends on, is the most common cause of drifting courses traced back to Project 11 during the Project 19 full exterior inspection.

Setup Standard

Do not begin layout until the selected siding system's exposure, starter component, corner detail, fastening schedule, expansion requirements, and minimum clearances are confirmed against the approved siding specification (Step 1). Do not establish a base reference until the WRB, flashing, rainscreen, window and door support, and penetration preparation have passed inspection (Step 2).

Shop Tip

Calculate course distribution for the full elevation before cutting any starter or corner component. A terminal course that is narrower than the siding manufacturer's permitted minimum is far easier to correct on paper than after the starter strip is already fastened at the wrong height.

Trim-First or Trim-After Sequencing

Confirm whether the selected siding system installs corner trim before siding, after siding, or as an integrated proprietary assembly before cutting any corner component. A corner board installed at the wrong sequence relative to the siding can trap water at the joint or leave a gap the siding cannot close. Do not assume a trim-first sequence applies to every product; verify it against the manufacturer's current installation instructions for the selected corner system.

Measurement Cue — Calculate Course Distribution Before Cutting

Measure the full wall height and divide it by the siding manufacturer's approved exposure to identify the number of courses and the height of the terminal course. Where the terminal course falls below the manufacturer's permitted minimum exposure, adjust the calculated exposure across all courses within the product's approved range rather than leaving an undersized top or bottom course.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Layout Over Unverified Rainscreen or Flashing

Do not establish a base reference, install a starter strip, or fasten a corner component until the WRB, flashing, and rainscreen furring or drainage mat for that elevation have passed inspection. Concealing an unverified drainage plane behind a starter or corner component creates a hidden defect that later inspections cannot see without removing the siding.

Field Safety Cue — Elevated and Uneven Access

Use only approved ladders, scaffolds, lifts, or work platforms to reach every point of the elevation during layout and installation. Do not stand on window or door openings, unsecured platforms, makeshift supports, or staged siding material. Confirm ladder and scaffold footing on uneven or sloped grade before climbing.

Manufacturer Cue — Starter and Corner Product Restrictions

Confirm the starter strip's drainage opening, fastening pattern, and required projection, and the corner component's minimum projection from the rainscreen plane, against the siding manufacturer's current installation guide before cutting or fastening. Do not substitute a generic starter or corner material not permitted by the manufacturer and the approved siding specification.

Code Alert — Clearance Requirements Vary by Jurisdiction

Required clearances above grade, roofs, decks, and paving for siding, starter, and corner components vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction and the general contractor before finalizing the base reference height.

E. Document Review and Verification (Steps 1–3)

  1. Review the selected siding system, exposure or module, starter component, corner detail, trim sequence, rainscreen support, fastening schedule, expansion requirements, and minimum clearances. Confirm the current manufacturer instructions against the approved siding specification before beginning layout.
  2. Confirm that the WRB, flashing, rainscreen, window and door support, and penetration preparation have passed inspection. Verify the prior inspection record for this elevation shows the required approval.
  3. Measure the wall height, width, openings, corners, roof intersections, decks, grade, and planned top termination. Record every measurement on the siding layout record before establishing the base reference.

F. Course Distribution and Reference Establishment (Steps 4–7)

  1. Determine whether the approved siding system installs trim before siding, after siding, or as an integrated proprietary assembly. Confirm the sequence against the manufacturer's current installation guide before cutting any corner component.
  2. Calculate course distribution so narrow or uneven terminal courses are avoided where practical. Adjust the calculated exposure within the manufacturer's approved range if the terminal course falls below the permitted minimum.
  3. Establish a level base reference from the approved lowest siding elevation. Use a water level or laser level to set the starting height and mark it with a chalk line.
  4. Transfer the base reference around corners and across openings using the approved level method. Confirm the transferred reference remains level at each corner and opening before marking further layout lines.

G. Starter and Corner Installation (Steps 8–14)

  1. Mark starter location, corner trim width, opening trim boundaries, joint locations, and required clearances. Use the transferred base reference as the controlling line for every mark.
  2. Verify that starter components will maintain the designed drainage and ventilation opening. Confirm the starter profile against the rainscreen manufacturer's required gap before fastening.
  3. Cut and install the starter strip or first-course support in the required orientation. Align the starter to the base reference line without distorting it at corners.
  4. Fasten starter components at approved locations without distorting them or blocking drainage. Use the fastener type, spacing, and embedment specified by the siding manufacturer.
  5. Prepare outside and inside corner boards, posts, or proprietary corner components. Cut components to the controlled length required for the elevation's top and bottom terminations.
  6. Install corner components plumb, straight, and at the required projection from the rainscreen plane. Check plumb at the top, middle, and bottom of each corner component before final fastening.
  7. Maintain specified clearances above roofs, decks, paving, grade, and horizontal flashing. Confirm each clearance against the approved siding specification before final fastening.

H. Joint Planning, Inspection, and Documentation (Steps 15–20)

  1. Plan siding end joints and laps so they occur over approved support and remain distributed across the elevation. Mark planned joint locations on the layout record before siding installation begins.
  2. Mark control lines at intervals required to prevent course drift. Space control lines according to the siding manufacturer's recommended interval for the selected exposure.
  3. Verify layout symmetry at windows, doors, soffits, and wall terminations. Confirm the course plan maintains consistent reveal at each opening and termination.
  4. Inspect starter level, corner plumb, projection, fastening, clearances, drainage opening, and course plan. Compare each condition against the Acceptance Standards table.
  5. Correct cumulative layout error before siding installation begins. Reset the base reference or adjust course distribution if any check fails the Acceptance Standards table.
  6. Photograph and record the final layout references. Label each photograph with its elevation and location, and complete the siding layout record before Project 12 or Project 13 begins.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed layout using the same checks regardless of which cladding system will follow.

  • Documentation check: confirm the siding specification, starter and corner instructions, and prior inspection records are current and referenced on the siding layout record.
  • Reference check: confirm the base reference line is level and was transferred consistently around every corner and opening.
  • Starter check: confirm the starter component is installed in the required orientation and maintains its designed drainage opening.
  • Corner check: confirm every corner component is plumb, straight, and at the required projection from the rainscreen plane.
  • Clearance check: confirm required clearances above grade, roofs, decks, paving, and flashing are met.
  • Course-plan check: confirm course distribution and joint planning avoid narrow terminal courses and unsupported joints.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Base Reference Line Is Not Level at Every Corner

Probable cause: the reference was transferred using a method sensitive to sag or sightline error, such as an unsupported string line over a long span.
Confirmation: recheck the transferred reference at each corner and midpoint using a water level or laser level.
Correction: re-establish the base reference from the original starting point using the approved transfer method and remark the chalk line.
Reinspection: repeat the level check at every corner and opening before installing the starter strip.

The Starter Strip Blocks Its Designed Drainage Opening

Probable cause: the starter was fastened too tightly against the rainscreen furring, or an incompatible starter profile was substituted.
Confirmation: inspect the starter's lower edge for a continuous open gap matching the rainscreen manufacturer's specified dimension.
Correction: remove and reinstall the starter using the approved profile and fastening method, preserving the open gap.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage-opening check along the full length of the starter strip.

A Corner Board Is Not Plumb

Probable cause: the corner was fastened at the top before its plumb was checked at the bottom, or the rainscreen furring behind it is uneven.
Confirmation: check plumb at the top, middle, and bottom of the corner board with a 4-foot level.
Correction: remove and reset the corner board, correcting the furring condition behind it if that is the cause, before refastening.
Reinspection: repeat the plumb check at all three points after correction.

A Terminal Course Falls Below the Manufacturer's Minimum Exposure

Probable cause: course distribution was not calculated before the base reference was set, or the wall height does not divide evenly at the standard exposure.
Confirmation: recalculate the wall height against the number of courses at the standard exposure and compare the terminal course to the manufacturer's permitted minimum.
Correction: adjust the calculated exposure across all courses within the manufacturer's approved range before installing the starter strip.
Reinspection: repeat the course-distribution calculation and confirm the revised plan on the siding layout record.

Corner Projection Does Not Clear the Rainscreen Plane

Probable cause: the corner component was installed directly against the WRB rather than over the completed rainscreen furring, or the wrong corner thickness was used.
Confirmation: measure the corner component's projection from the rainscreen plane and compare it against the siding manufacturer's minimum requirement.
Correction: remove and reinstall the corner component over the correct backing at the required projection.
Reinspection: repeat the projection check at the top, middle, and bottom of the corner.

Layout Symmetry Is Uneven at a Window or Door Opening

Probable cause: course distribution was calculated without accounting for the opening's head and sill heights relative to the control lines.
Confirmation: compare the marked course lines at the opening against the reveal specified in the approved appearance plan.
Correction: adjust control-line spacing within the manufacturer's approved exposure range so the reveal at the opening matches the plan.
Reinspection: repeat the symmetry check at every opening and termination before siding installation begins.

Cleanup

This project ends with a level, plumb, and documented siding layout with installed starter and corner components, not with siding, trim, or sealant installed.

  • Clear cutoffs, packaging, and loose fasteners from the base of the elevation
  • Remove marking materials that are not part of an active control line or reference mark
  • Protect installed starter and corner components from impact and unnecessary traffic before siding begins
  • Store the siding layout record, measurements, and supporting photographs with the project's permanent documentation
  • Communicate any open discrepancy and its required decision to the responsible design professional or general contractor before Project 12 or 13 begins
