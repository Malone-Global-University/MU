Job Overview

This capstone chapter qualifies you to integrate every competency taught in Chapters 1 through 19 into one complete, functioning paver patio or walkway. The finished work product is one complete hardscape installation carried from an approved readiness plan through excavation, base, bedding, paver installation, cutting, edging, joint sand, drainage, final inspection, and formal closeout.

You will begin from the exact readiness plan, work-area setup, and layout controls established in Chapters 1 through 3, carry the excavation, base, and bedding sequence from Chapters 4 through 6, install the paver field and cuts from Chapters 7 through 9, restrain and joint the field from Chapter 10, integrate a surface-drainage component from Chapter 17, and close the project with the compiled records and inspection standard required by Chapter 20's own project sequence.

This is the final chapter in the book. The finished, drained, edged, compacted, and documented patio or walkway produced here becomes the handoff site for Book 19 landscaping coordination, and the complete record package produced here is the model for every future hardscape project you plan, execute, and close out independently.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HC-115 — Integrate a complete capstone readiness, layout, drainage, excavation, base, and bedding plan from approved site documents before construction begins.
  • HC-116 — Excavate, prepare the subgrade, and install and compact an aggregate base to the approved capstone depth, thickness, and slope.
  • HC-117 — Install an approved surface-drainage component, screed bedding sand, and protect the bedding layer before paver placement.
  • HC-118 — Install the paver field, perimeter and obstacle cuts, border, and edge restraint using the approved pattern, bond, and joint-width controls.
  • HC-119 — Compact, joint, and activate the finished paver surface, and verify drainage capture, outlet flow, and overflow function.
  • HC-120 — Conduct the final capstone inspection, correct every deficiency, compile complete project records, and issue a formal hardscape closeout for Book 19 handoff.

Where This Skill Fits

Every prior chapter in this book taught one stage of hardscape construction in isolation: inspection, staging, layout, excavation, base, bedding, paver installation, cutting, edging, gravel paths, retaining walls, drainage, capping, steps, borders, surface drainage, repair, and finishing. This chapter does not teach a new physical skill. It requires you to sequence and execute those stages together, in the correct technical order, on one real site, without the chapter boundaries that separated them during instruction.

The System-Sequence Control established at the start of this book — utilities and drainage resolved before excavation, subgrade accepted before base, base accepted before bedding, bedding undisturbed until covered, edge and drainage interfaces coordinated before final compaction, and joint sand installed only in clean, dry joints — governs this chapter exactly as it governed every chapter before it. This is the chapter where that sequence is proven under real project conditions rather than described in outline form.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • obtaining and confirming the approved capstone site plan, dimensions, elevations, drainage plan, utility information, pattern, border, base, bedding, edge, joint-sand, permit, and inspection documents
  • completing the Chapter 1 readiness inspection and the Chapter 2 work-area setup for the capstone site
  • establishing layout, excavating, preparing subgrade, and installing and compacting base aggregate
  • installing an approved surface-drainage component and testing its outlet before covering it
  • screeding bedding sand and installing the paver field, cuts, border, and edge restraint
  • compacting, jointing, and activating the finished surface, and verifying drainage function
  • performing a full final inspection, correcting every deficiency, and compiling complete project records
  • issuing a formal hardscape closeout and handing the site off for Book 19 landscaping coordination

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin excavation on a site that has not passed the Chapter 1 readiness inspection with an approved or conditionally approved decision, all conditions resolved
  • substitute this chapter's own procedure for the retaining-wall, stair, or gravel-path procedures in Chapters 11 through 15 if the capstone site plan calls for one of those elements; those chapters' procedures govern any such component built as part of a larger capstone site
  • apply sealer as part of this chapter's closeout; sealing follows Chapter 19's cure and compatibility requirements on a separate later visit
  • hand off a site to Book 19 landscaping coordination with any unresolved deficiency, unverified drainage outlet, or incomplete record on the closeout package

Any capstone site condition that fails inspection at any stage of this chapter must be corrected and reinspected before the next stage begins.

Trade Note

The capstone is not a new project. It is the same site-to-closeout sequence you have practiced in isolated stages since Chapter 1, executed here as one continuous, accountable job. Every record you compile in this chapter — layout, excavation, compaction, base, bedding, drainage, paver-lot, cutting, edge, joint-sand, weather, test, inspection, and correction records — is the same documentation standard a professional hardscape crew produces on every real job.

Common Field Fundamentals

The capstone applies the same System-Sequence Control introduced at the start of this book to one continuous project: utilities, drainage planning, property limits, access, permits, and material delivery are resolved before excavation; unsuitable soil is removed before compaction; subgrade elevation and slope are accepted before base; base is accepted before bedding; bedding remains undisturbed until covered; edge cuts, drains, restraints, and fixed interfaces are coordinated before final compaction; and polymeric sand is installed only in clean, dry joints.

Stop-Work Conditions

This chapter requires the same stop-work discipline established in Chapter 1, applied at every capstone stage rather than only at the initial inspection.

Critical Safety Cue — Conditions Discovered During Capstone Excavation

Stop excavation immediately if an unidentified utility, unstable excavation wall, buried tank, contaminated soil, uncontrolled fill, or groundwater is encountered, even though the Chapter 1 readiness inspection did not identify it. Barricade the area, contact the utility-locating service or other required qualified professional, and do not resume until the condition is resolved and documented.

Code Alert — Drainage Outlet and Overflow Verification

Do not cover the surface-drainage component installed in Section G until the outlet has been tested and the overflow route has been confirmed as required by Chapter 17. Verify the discharge point remains the lawful outlet identified on the approved drainage plan before backfilling around the component.

Field Safety Cue — Multi-Day Site Protection

Because the capstone runs across multiple work sessions, protect open excavations, exposed base, and screeded bedding between sessions using barricades, coverings, or other controls appropriate to weather and site traffic. Reinspect protected areas at the start of each session before resuming work.

Measurement Cue — Carry the Same Fixed Reference Through Every Stage

Use the same fixed reference point established during the Chapter 1 readiness inspection and Chapter 3 layout for every elevation check in this chapter, from subgrade acceptance through finished-surface inspection. Do not introduce a new reference point partway through the capstone.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Cross-Product Compatibility

Confirm that the base aggregate, bedding sand, paver units, edge restraint, joint-sand product, and drainage components selected for this capstone are compatible with each other according to each manufacturer's stated instructions, not only individually compatible with the site conditions identified in Chapter 1.

E. Preparation — Governing Documents and Prior-Chapter Acceptance

  1. Obtain the approved capstone site plan, dimensions, elevations, drainage plan, utility information, paver pattern, border, base, bedding, edge, joint-sand, permit, and inspection documents. Confirm every listed document is present and current before proceeding.
  2. Confirm property limits, setbacks, foundation clearances, accessibility, and lawful drainage outlet. Compare the capstone footprint against the property survey and drainage plan.
  3. Complete utility locating. Submit or reconfirm the locate request for the full capstone footprint and drainage-trench route.
  4. Perform the Chapter 1 readiness inspection. Complete a full Hardscape Readiness Worksheet for this site if one is not already approved, or reconfirm an existing approved worksheet remains current.
  5. Prepare the Chapter 2 work area, material staging, equipment access, silica controls, and public protection. Complete the Chapter 2 work-area safety inspection for the capstone site.

F. Layout

  1. Establish baseline, corners, curves, offsets, excavation limits, pattern controls, and finished elevations. Use the layout procedure and tolerances established in Chapter 3.
  2. Square and document the layout. Verify diagonals and record the approved layout on the Capstone Project Record.
  3. Mark drains, edges, transitions, structures, obstacles, and overflow route. Confirm every marked feature matches the capstone site plan and drainage plan.

G. Excavation, Base, and Drainage

  1. Excavate to the approved depth. Follow the excavation procedure and stop-work conditions established in Chapter 4.
  2. Remove organics, roots, soft spots, uncontrolled fill, and unsuitable soil. Correct to the limits established in Chapter 4.
  3. Shape and compact subgrade. Follow the compaction procedure established in Chapter 4.
  4. Verify subgrade elevation and slope. Check on a measurement grid against the fixed reference established in Section E.
  5. Install base aggregate in approved lifts. Follow the lift-thickness and placement procedure established in Chapter 5.
  6. Compact each lift. Follow the compaction pattern and overlap procedure established in Chapter 5.
  7. Verify final base thickness, slope, elevation, and edge extension. Check on a measurement grid before installing any drainage component or bedding sand.
  8. Install channel drain, basin, outlet pipe, or other approved drainage component where specified. Follow the installation procedure established in Chapter 17.
  9. Test the drainage outlet before covering. Conduct the controlled water-flow test required by Chapter 17 and confirm the overflow route.

H. Bedding and Paver Installation

  1. Install screed rails. Set rails to the required bedding elevation per Chapter 6.
  2. Place and screed bedding sand. Follow the screeding procedure established in Chapter 6.
  3. Remove rails and fill voids. Strike voids flush without overcompacting the bedding.
  4. Protect the screeded surface. Do not walk, kneel, wheel equipment, or stockpile material on the screeded bedding.
  5. Establish first paver course and pattern. Follow the pattern-establishment procedure from Chapters 7 and 8.
  6. Lay the field without disturbing bedding. Place units vertically onto the bedding without sliding them.
  7. Maintain joint spacing and bond lines. Check against control strings at regular intervals.
  8. Blend units from multiple pallets where required. Distribute color variation evenly across the field.
  9. Check alignment and slope throughout. Verify against the fixed reference at regular intervals.
  10. Mark and cut perimeter, border, curve, drain, post, and obstacle units. Follow the cutting procedure and silica controls established in Chapter 9.
  11. Replace weak slivers or cracked pieces. Do not install any cut piece below the approved minimum stable size.
  12. Install border or accent band. Follow the border-integration procedure established in Chapter 16 where a border is specified.

I. Edging, Compaction, and Joint Sand

  1. Install edge restraint into the compacted base. Follow the fastening procedure and spacing established in Chapter 10.
  2. Inspect the full field before compaction. Check pattern, joints, cuts, and edge restraint across the entire site.
  3. Perform initial compaction using the approved protective mat. Follow the pass direction and count established in Chapter 10.
  4. Correct broken, high, low, or rocking units. Replace or reset units before jointing.
  5. Install joint or polymeric sand. Sweep into clean, dry joints per Chapter 10.
  6. Compact, refill, and repeat until joints are full. Follow the repeat-compaction procedure established in Chapter 10.
  7. Clean all sand from surfaces. Remove sand from paver faces, edges, and adjacent surfaces before activation.
  8. Activate polymeric sand under approved weather and watering conditions. Follow the exact watering pattern and quantity specified by the manufacturer.
  9. Protect from rain, irrigation, traffic, and disturbance. Maintain protection through the full cure period.

J. Final Inspection, Correction, and Closeout

  1. Perform a controlled drainage-flow observation after the system permits it. Confirm capture, outlet flow, and overflow function under the finished surface.
  2. Inspect dimensions, pattern, joints, cuts, edge security, surface plane, slope, drainage, transitions, slip, and trip hazards. Use the Inspection Checklist below for the full site.
  3. Prepare a final deficiency list. List every unresolved condition found at any stage of Sections E through I.
  4. Correct and reinspect every item. Do not close out the project with any unresolved deficiency remaining.
  5. Apply sealer only after joint, weather, moisture, and cure conditions permit it and only when specified. Follow Chapter 19's compatibility and cure requirements if sealing is included in the capstone site plan.
  6. Compile utility, layout, excavation, compaction, base, bedding, drainage, paver-lot, cutting, edge, joint-sand, weather, test, inspection, and correction records. Assemble the complete Capstone Record Schedule from Section B into one closeout package.
  7. Provide maintenance, cleaning, snow, deicing, joint-sand, drainage, load, and sealer instructions. Record these instructions on the closeout package for the property owner.
  8. Hand off the completed hardscape to Book 19 landscaping coordination. Confirm the site is stable, drained, edged, compacted, and fully documented before handoff.

Common Site Inspection

Review the completed capstone using the same checks regardless of patio or walkway configuration.

  • Document check: confirm every governing document listed in Section C is on file with the closeout package.
  • Sequence check: confirm every stage in Sections E through J was accepted before the next stage began, with no stage skipped or reordered.
  • Drainage check: confirm the drainage component was tested before covering and reconfirmed after the finished surface was complete, with a verified overflow route.
  • Surface check: confirm pattern, bond, joint width, edge restraint, and surface plane meet the approved specification across the full field.
  • Deficiency check: confirm every item on the final deficiency list is corrected and reinspected, with no open item remaining.
  • Closeout check: confirm the complete record package and maintenance instructions are compiled and ready for Book 19 handoff.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Base Accepted at the Wrong Elevation for the Planned Drainage Component

Probable cause: base elevation was verified against the finished-surface target without checking clearance for the drainage component specified in the capstone site plan.
Confirmation: compare the accepted base elevation against the drainage component's required invert and grate elevation from Chapter 17.
Correction: remove and reset base aggregate to provide the required clearance, then reverify base elevation and slope.
Reinspection: repeat the base acceptance check for the affected area before installing the drainage component.

Drainage Component Covered Before Testing

Probable cause: bedding sand was placed over the drainage component before the required flow test was performed.
Confirmation: check the drainage record for a documented test date and result before the bedding installation date.
Correction: remove bedding sand covering the component, perform the flow test, and correct any capture, outlet, or overflow deficiency found.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage test and, once passed, rescreed the affected bedding area per Chapter 6.

Pattern Drift Discovered Late in the Field

Probable cause: control strings were not checked frequently enough during Section H, allowing small joint-width variations to accumulate across the field.
Confirmation: check current unit positions against the original control lines from Section F.
Correction: lift and reset affected units, redistributing joint corrections evenly, before continuing the field.
Reinspection: repeat the alignment check at closer intervals for the remainder of the field.

Edge Restraint Fastened Into Bedding Sand Instead of Base

Probable cause: the compacted base was not fully exposed at the perimeter before edge restraint was positioned and fastened.
Confirmation: inspect fastener penetration at several perimeter points to confirm anchorage into compacted base rather than loose bedding.
Correction: remove and reset the affected restraint section, expose the compacted base, and refasten per Chapter 10.
Reinspection: repeat the full-perimeter restraint inspection before compaction.

Joint Sand Activated Before Full Perimeter Was Secure

Probable cause: activation watering began before edge restraint and border integration were fully inspected and accepted.
Confirmation: check the closeout record for the sequence of edge-restraint acceptance versus activation date.
Correction: where washout or loose units resulted, repair the affected area following Chapter 18 before reactivating joint sand.
Reinspection: repeat the joint-fill, edge-security, and washout checks for the affected area.

Closeout Package Incomplete at Handoff

Probable cause: a record from an earlier stage, such as a compaction log or cutting record, was not carried forward into the final closeout package.
Confirmation: cross-check the Capstone Record Schedule in Section B against the assembled closeout package to confirm every record is present.
Correction: locate or reconstruct the missing record from field notes and photographs, and add it to the package before handoff.
Reinspection: repeat the closeout check before handing the site off to Book 19 landscaping coordination.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a stable, drained, compacted, and fully documented paver patio or walkway, not a verbal report of finished work.

  • Remove all cutting slurry, excess sand, offcuts, and packaging from the site and dispose of them per Chapter 9 and Chapter 2 controls
  • Return excavation, base, and drainage equipment to inspected, cleaned storage condition
  • Remove barricades, cones, and temporary protection once the surface has cured and passed final inspection
  • Store the complete closeout package, including photographs, with the original Chapter 1 readiness worksheet
  • Confirm the property owner has received the maintenance, cleaning, snow, deicing, joint-sand, drainage, load, and sealer instructions
