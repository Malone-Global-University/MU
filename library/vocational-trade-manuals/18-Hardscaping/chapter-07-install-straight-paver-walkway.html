Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install a complete straight paver walkway section over the base and bedding system built in Chapters 4 through 6. The finished work product is one straight paver walkway section, laid in the approved bond pattern, with consistent joint spacing, squared alignment to the Chapter 3 layout lines, correctly cut edge units, an installed edge restraint, and a documented final inspection of line, bond, joints, cut sizes, edge support, transition, slope, and trip hazards.

You will establish the first pattern line and its perpendicular control, set the first paver at the controlling corner, build outward course by course while checking stringlines at regular intervals, correct pattern drift early rather than late, mark and cut the edge units, install edge restraint, and complete a full inspection before the walkway is compacted and jointed in Chapter 10.

This chapter depends directly on the accepted base from Chapter 5 and the undisturbed, screeded bedding from Chapter 6. It also depends on the layout references established in Chapter 3. This chapter does not repeat excavation, base, or bedding work, and it does not perform final compaction or joint-sand activation, which belong to Chapter 10 after the field and edge restraint are complete and accepted.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HC-037 — Lay the first paver course to the controlling layout reference without disturbing the screeded bedding.
  • HC-038 — Maintain joint spacing using built-in spacers or an approved control method throughout the field.
  • HC-039 — Follow layout lines to maintain square, alignment, and bond pattern across the full walkway.
  • HC-040 — Cut edge pavers to fit the approved layout boundary using controlled silica methods.
  • HC-041 — Install edge restraint into the compacted base to secure the finished field.
  • HC-042 — Inspect finished walkway alignment, including line, bond, joints, cut sizes, edge support, transition, slope, and trip hazards.

Where This Skill Fits

Every paver surface in this book — a straight walkway, a full patio field in Chapter 8, a border or accent band in Chapter 16, and the Chapter 20 capstone — is built using the same course-by-course discipline taught here: start from a controlling reference, maintain joint spacing continuously rather than correcting it at the end, and check stringlines often enough to catch drift while it is still a small correction. A walkway laid without frequent stringline checks accumulates pattern drift that cannot be corrected without lifting and relaying units.

This chapter also introduces the edge-restraint sequence that every later paver chapter repeats: expose the compacted base at the perimeter, install and fasten restraint before final compaction, and never rely on bedding sand or joint sand to hold a field in place. Chapter 10 completes the compaction, joint-sand, and activation steps that this chapter's finished, inspected, and edge-restrained field makes possible.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved walkway pattern, paver type, border, joint width, slope, transitions, edge restraint, and cut plan
  • confirming base and bedding acceptance before placing any paver
  • establishing the first pattern line and its perpendicular control
  • placing and building out the first paver course from the controlling corner
  • maintaining joint width, square, and alignment using spacers and stringlines
  • marking, removing, and cutting edge units using the Chapter 9 procedure
  • installing edge restraint according to the Chapter 10 procedure
  • inspecting the finished, uncompacted walkway field before it advances to Chapter 10

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • place pavers on a base or bedding layer that has not been inspected and accepted under Chapters 5 and 6
  • walk, kneel, or wheel equipment on screeded bedding beyond the edge of the units already placed
  • perform final compaction, joint-sand placement, or polymeric-sand activation, which are covered in Chapter 10 and must follow edge-restraint installation
  • substitute polymeric sand, adhesive, or any joint material for a missing or deficient edge restraint
  • cut pavers without following the silica, saw, and splitter controls established in Chapter 9

Any base or bedding deficiency discovered during this chapter must be corrected under the applicable earlier chapter's procedure before paver placement continues in the affected area.

Trade Note

A straight walkway is the simplest paver field in this book, but it is not a simplified procedure. The same stringline discipline, joint control, cutting method, and edge-restraint sequence used here scale directly to the full patio field in Chapter 8 and the Chapter 20 capstone. Learn the checking interval and correction habit on this project, not on a larger one where drift is harder to see and more costly to fix.

Shop Tip

Check the stringline after every few courses, not only when the walkway looks out of alignment to the eye. Pattern drift of a fraction of an inch per course is invisible in a single glance but compounds quickly over a full walkway length.

Common Field Fundamentals

A straight walkway is built from one controlling corner outward, using a squared perpendicular control and a stringline that is checked at a regular interval rather than trusted for its full length. Joint spacing is maintained continuously, not corrected in a batch at the end of a course. Cuts are marked from the final edge line, not estimated, and edge restraint is installed into the compacted base before final compaction, never anchored into bedding sand alone.

Field Safety Cue — Protecting the Screeded Bedding

Do not walk, kneel, wheel equipment, or set material stacks on screeded bedding that has not yet been covered by placed pavers. Work from placed units or knee boards, and place new units by setting them down vertically rather than sliding them into position.

Measurement Cue — Check Stringlines at a Regular Interval

Check the pattern-control stringline and joint width at the interval specified in the approved layout, or at least every four to six courses if no interval is stated in the layout. Record each check on the walkway installation record. Do not wait until visible drift appears before checking.

Safety Cue — Kneeling and Repetitive Placement

Use knee boards or knee pads when placing units for extended periods, and change position or take breaks to avoid repetitive-strain discomfort. Lift and place pavers using a controlled motion rather than twisting while holding a unit.

Code Alert — Accessibility and Trip-Hazard Limits

Where the walkway is subject to an accessibility standard or a local trip-hazard limit on surface height differences, joint width, or slope, verify the exact figures against the adopted code or accessibility requirement before final inspection. Do not assume a standard tolerance applies without checking the governing requirement for this project.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Spacer Lugs and Joint Width

If the specified paver has built-in spacer lugs, place units so the lugs make full contact and do not add separate spacers that would alter the manufacturer's designed joint width. If the paver has no spacer lugs, use spacers or a measured control method to hold the specified joint width from the approved layout.

D. Preparation — Review and Confirm Readiness

  1. Review walkway pattern, paver type, border, joint width, slope, transitions, edge restraint, and cut plan. Complete the Walkway Installation Record above using the approved layout and product specifications.
  2. Confirm base and bedding acceptance. Verify the dated Chapter 5 base acceptance record and Chapter 6 bedding acceptance record are on file for this walkway section before proceeding.

E. Establish Pattern Control and Place the First Course

  1. Establish the first pattern line and perpendicular control. Set string lines from the Chapter 3 layout references, and square the perpendicular control to the pattern line using the framing square at the controlling corner.
  2. Place the first paver at the controlling corner. Set the unit vertically onto the bedding at the intersection of the pattern line and perpendicular control without sliding it into position.
  3. Set additional pavers without sliding them across the bedding. Place each unit vertically, tight to its neighbor's spacer lugs or measured joint control, and seat it with the rubber mallet.
  4. Maintain joint width using built-in spacers or approved controls. Follow the Manufacturer Instruction Cue above for products with built-in spacer lugs; use separate spacers only where the product has none.
  5. Check the first several units for square and alignment. Verify against the framing square and pattern line before continuing beyond the first several units.

F. Build the Field Course by Course

  1. Continue the first course. Extend the first course along the full pattern-line length before beginning the next course, unless the approved bond pattern requires a different build sequence.
  2. Build outward using the approved bond pattern. Follow the running bond, herringbone, basketweave, or other specified pattern exactly as recorded on the Walkway Installation Record.
  3. Check stringlines at regular intervals. Follow the Measurement Cue above and record each check.
  4. Adjust pattern drift early by distributing small joint corrections. Make any needed correction across several joints rather than closing or opening a single joint by a large amount.
  5. Maintain finished slope and avoid rocking units. Check surface plane and cross-slope with the 4-foot level at each stringline-check interval, and reseat or replace any rocking unit before continuing.
  6. Keep cut units away from high-visibility areas where the layout permits. Follow the approved layout's cut-distribution intent; do not concentrate visible cut units at a prominent edge unless the layout specifies it.

G. Mark, Cut, and Install Edge Units

  1. Mark edge cuts after sufficient field area is installed. Mark the boundary line on each edge unit only once enough of the adjoining field is placed to confirm the exact cut line.
  2. Remove edge units for cutting using Chapter 9. Follow the Chapter 9 procedure for saw or splitter selection, silica controls, and PPE.
  3. Install cut units with approved joint spacing. Fit each cut unit to maintain the same joint width used throughout the field.

H. Install Edge Restraint and Complete Final Inspection

  1. Install edge restraint according to Chapter 10. Follow the full Chapter 10 procedure for base exposure, restraint positioning, and fastening before this walkway advances to compaction.
  2. Compact and joint the walkway only after edge controls are complete. Do not proceed to Chapter 10 compaction and joint-sand steps until edge restraint is installed and inspected for the full perimeter.
  3. Inspect line, bond, joints, cut sizes, edge support, transition, slope, and trip hazards. Complete the Acceptance Standards table in Section C and the Walkway Installation Checklist below.
  4. Document finished alignment. Record the completed inspection, any correction made, and the date on the Walkway Installation Record before handing the field off to Chapter 10.

Common Site Inspection

Review every finished walkway section using the same checks regardless of pattern or length.

  • Base and bedding check: confirm the Chapter 5 and Chapter 6 acceptance records are on file and dated before this chapter began.
  • Pattern and square check: confirm the first course was placed to the pattern line and squared at the controlling corner.
  • Joint check: confirm joint width matches the approved specification throughout the field, including at cut units.
  • Bond check: confirm the placed pattern matches the approved bond pattern with no uncorrected drift.
  • Edge and restraint check: confirm every edge unit meets the approved minimum piece size and the edge restraint is fastened into the compacted base for the full perimeter.
  • Surface and hazard check: confirm no rocking units, no slope deviation beyond tolerance, and no trip hazard remains before advancing to Chapter 10.

Complete the Walkway Installation Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 10.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Pattern Drift Across the Field

Probable cause: stringlines were not checked at a regular interval, or small joint variations were allowed to accumulate rather than being corrected as they appeared.
Confirmation: measure joint width and course alignment against the pattern-control stringline at several points across the field.
Correction: lift and relay units back to the last point where alignment was confirmed correct, redistributing the joint correction across several joints rather than one.
Reinspection: repeat the pattern and square check for the corrected section and resume the stringline-check interval going forward.

Rocking or Uneven Units

Probable cause: a unit was placed on a disturbed or contaminated spot in the screeded bedding, or debris was trapped beneath the unit.
Confirmation: lift the unit and inspect the bedding surface beneath it for a void, high spot, or debris.
Correction: correct the bedding at that location following the Chapter 6 screeding method for the affected spot only, then reset the unit.
Reinspection: recheck the unit and its immediate neighbors with the 4-foot level.

Edge Units Below the Minimum Piece Size

Probable cause: the layout's cut line fell too close to a joint, or the field pattern was not adjusted early enough to distribute the resulting cut width.
Confirmation: measure the planned cut width against the approved minimum stable piece size from the Chapter 9 specification.
Correction: shift the field pattern slightly, following the Chapter 9 procedure for redistributing cuts, so the resulting edge piece meets the minimum size.
Reinspection: repeat the edge-cut check for the affected boundary section.

Edge Restraint Anchored Only into Bedding Sand

Probable cause: the compacted base was not fully exposed at the perimeter before the restraint was positioned and fastened.
Confirmation: inspect the restraint fasteners for penetration into the compacted base rather than only into bedding sand.
Correction: remove the affected restraint section, expose the compacted base per the Chapter 10 procedure, and refasten.
Reinspection: repeat the edge and restraint check for the full affected run.

Slope Deviation Discovered at Final Inspection

Probable cause: surface plane and cross-slope were checked only at the start of the field rather than at each stringline-check interval.
Confirmation: check slope across the full field length and width using the 4-foot level and the fixed reference established in the Chapter 3 layout.
Correction: lift the affected units, correct the underlying bedding per Chapter 6, and reset the units to the approved slope.
Reinspection: repeat the surface and hazard check for the corrected area and confirm no rocking units remain.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a completed, inspected, edge-restrained walkway field, not a field that merely looks finished.

  • Remove excess paver material, spacers, and cut offcuts from the finished field
  • Store leftover field, border, and edge-restraint units by lot for future repairs
  • Clean cutting slurry and debris from the field surface per the Chapter 9 procedure
  • File the Walkway Installation Record and Checklist with the Chapter 5 and Chapter 6 acceptance records
  • Protect the finished, uncompacted field from traffic and rain until Chapter 10 compaction begins

Field Terms

Pattern Line
The primary string line controlling the direction and straightness of the first paver course.
Perpendicular Control
The secondary line squared to the pattern line that controls the starting edge at the controlling corner.
Controlling Corner
The corner from which the first paver is placed and the pattern is built outward.
Bond Pattern
The repeating paver arrangement specified for the walkway.
Field Unit
A full, uncut paver placed within the main body of the walkway.
Edge Unit
A paver cut to fit the boundary between the field and the edge-restraint line.
Minimum Piece Size
The smallest stable cut-paver dimension approved for installation, below which a unit must be replaced or the pattern adjusted.
Edge Restraint
The component installed into the compacted base along the field perimeter to prevent lateral movement of the finished pavers.
Trip Hazard
A transition, edge, or joint condition that exceeds the applicable code or accessibility limit for surface height difference.
Stringline Check
The regular-interval verification of pattern-line alignment and joint width during field installation.

Performance Check

With the walkway field placed, cut, edge-restrained, and inspected, review the finished work against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm that base and bedding acceptance records are on file, the first course is squared to the controlling corner, joint width and bond pattern are consistent across the full field, every edge unit meets the approved minimum size, edge restraint is fastened into the compacted base for the full perimeter, and no rocking unit, slope deviation, or trip hazard remains. If any item is incomplete or a deficiency remains unresolved, correct it and reinspect the affected section before advancing to Chapter 10.

Project Closeout

You have completed the seventh chapter in the hardscaping sequence: installing a straight paver walkway. This chapter required you to lay the first paver course to the controlling reference (HC-037), maintain joint spacing throughout the field (HC-038), follow layout lines to maintain square, alignment, and bond pattern (HC-039), cut edge pavers to fit the approved boundary (HC-040), install edge restraint into the compacted base (HC-041), and inspect the finished walkway alignment (HC-042).

You now have a complete, inspected, edge-restrained paver walkway field ready for final compaction, joint-sand placement, and polymeric-sand activation. That field carries directly into the next chapter, where the same course-by-course pattern discipline, cutting method, and edge-restraint sequence are applied to a full paver patio field.

Retain the completed Walkway Installation Record, Walkway Installation Checklist, and any Chapter 9 cutting records. You will reference this exact installation sequence throughout the rest of this book, including the Chapter 20 capstone.
