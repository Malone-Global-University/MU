Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to produce accurately cut and properly fitted paver pieces for every condition a field or border installation encounters: straight perimeter cuts, curves, drain openings, post and column obstacles, and irregular fixed objects. The finished work product is one completed and installed set of straight, curved, drain, post, and obstacle paver cuts, produced under controlled silica exposure, marked from the actual field condition, cut to the correct waste side, dressed where required, test-fit, and installed with consistent joints.

You will set up a contained cutting area, inspect the saw and splitter before use, wear the required protective equipment, transfer field lines directly onto each unit, cut using the method appropriate to the geometry, dress sharp edges, collect slurry and offcuts, test-fit every piece, and inspect the finished cuts for face quality, joint consistency, support, and alignment.

This chapter is called on directly by Chapters 7, 8, 10, 11, 14, 16, 18, and 20 whenever a paver, cap, or border unit must be cut to fit an edge, curve, drain, post, or obstacle. You are not building a new field in this chapter; you are producing the cut pieces that those other chapters' fields, borders, and caps depend on.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HC-049 — Mark paver cuts directly from field, curve, drain, post, or obstacle conditions, accounting for joint and movement space.
  • HC-050 — Cut pavers safely using a mechanical splitter or a wet/extracted saw appropriate to the required geometry.
  • HC-051 — Fit curves using transferred curve lines, relief cuts where the finished geometry permits them, and dressed edges.
  • HC-052 — Fit pavers around posts, drains, and other fixed obstacles while maintaining minimum stable piece size.
  • HC-053 — Maintain joint-width consistency for every cut piece installed adjacent to field, border, curve, or obstacle units.
  • HC-054 — Inspect cut quality, including visible face condition, edge dressing, support, and alignment, before final acceptance.

Where This Skill Fits

Every paver field installed in this book eventually meets an edge, a curve, a drain, a post, or some other object that a full-size unit cannot span. A poorly marked or poorly cut piece creates an oversized joint, an unsupported sliver, a chipped visible face, or a piece that rocks under foot traffic — problems that cosmetic jointing cannot fix afterward. This chapter isolates the cutting and fitting skill so that Chapters 7, 8, 10, 11, 14, 16, 18, and 20 can call on it as a controlled, repeatable procedure rather than reinventing cutting technique in every chapter that needs a cut piece.

The silica-control discipline built here — contained cutting area, wet or extracted cutting, PPE, and slurry containment — is the same discipline every later cutting operation in this book depends on, including cap cutting in Chapter 14 and border cutting in Chapter 16.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing paver material, thickness, cut geometry, minimum piece size, finished-face orientation, saw or splitter method, and joint requirements
  • establishing a contained cutting area and inspecting the saw, splitter, guards, water system, and electrical protection before use
  • marking straight, curved, drain, post, and obstacle cut lines directly from the field condition
  • cutting using a mechanical splitter for suitable straight cuts and a wet or extracted saw for precision cuts
  • dressing sharp cut edges using an approved wet or extracted method
  • test-fitting every cut piece and correcting pieces that crack, rock, or fall below the minimum stable size
  • inspecting visible face, edge quality, joint width, support, and alignment of the finished cut pieces

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • cut without eye, hearing, hand, foot, and respiratory protection appropriate to the method and material
  • dry-cut, dry-grind, or dry-sweep silica dust where a wet or extracted method is required
  • install a cut piece below the project's approved minimum stable size
  • discharge slurry, cementitious residue, or wash water to a storm drain, soil, or waterway
  • substitute a relief cut for a required curve or geometry where the finished appearance or structural fit does not permit it

Any cut piece that cracks, chips on the visible face, rocks after test-fitting, or falls below the approved minimum stable size must be replaced before it is installed in a field, border, or cap covered by any other chapter in this book.

Trade Note

A cut piece is only correct if it matches the actual field condition, not the theoretical layout. Always transfer the cut line from the real edge, curve, drain, post, or obstacle rather than from a calculated dimension, because minor field variation between the layout in Chapter 3 and the installed field in Chapters 7 or 8 will make a calculated cut line wrong.

Setup Standard

Do not begin cutting until the cutting area is contained, the saw or splitter has passed inspection, water flow or extraction is confirmed functional, and required PPE is worn. A saw or splitter that has not been inspected before this session must be inspected before the first cut.

Shop Tip

Dry-fit every cut piece before final installation, even when the cut appears correct. A piece that looks right on the saw table can still be wrong once it meets the actual joint, curve, or obstacle in the field, and catching the error before installation avoids resetting adjacent units.

Stop-Work Conditions

This chapter requires the operator to halt cutting whenever a condition risks injury, uncontrolled silica exposure, or an unstable installed piece.

Critical Safety Cue — Blade Zone and Guard Condition

Do not operate a saw with a missing, damaged, or bypassed guard, and do not position hands, fingers, or loose clothing inside the blade zone at any point during feeding. Use a push stick or feed guide for any piece that would otherwise bring hands within the blade zone. Stop and de-energize the saw before clearing a jam or adjusting the blade.

Safety Cue — Silica Exposure Control

Do not dry-cut, dry-grind, or dry-sweep paver dust. Confirm water flow or local exhaust extraction is functioning before the first cut of the session, and wear the respirator or dust mask specified for this project whenever wet or extracted control is not fully effective for the cut being made.

Field Safety Cue — Slurry and Offcut Housekeeping

Contain slurry and wash water at the cutting station and do not allow it to reach a storm drain, soil, or waterway. Collect offcuts promptly so they do not create a trip hazard or an uncontrolled edge near the cutting area.

Measurement Cue — Joint and Movement Space

Account for the approved joint width on both sides of every cut piece before marking the cut line. A cut piece marked without joint allowance will either bind against adjacent units or leave an oversized joint that cannot be corrected after installation.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Blade and Feed Rate

Use the blade type and feed rate specified by the saw and blade manufacturer for the paver material being cut. A blade or feed rate not matched to the material increases chipping risk on the finished face and reduces cut accuracy.

E. Preparation — Confirm Materials and Set Up the Cutting Area

  1. Review paver material, thickness, cut geometry, minimum piece size, finished-face orientation, saw or splitter method, and joint requirements. Confirm each item against the current field, border, or cap chapter's specification before cutting begins.
  2. Establish a contained cutting area. Locate the station away from finished field work, occupied areas, and drainage inlets, with slurry containment in place.
  3. Inspect saw, blade, guard, water system, table, electrical protection, splitter, and emergency stop. Confirm each component functions correctly before the first cut of the session.
  4. Wear eye, hearing, hand, foot, and respiratory protection. Confirm PPE matches the cutting method and material being cut.

F. Marking — Transfer Field Conditions to the Unit

  1. Place the full unit in position. Set the uncut paver where it will be installed, against its actual adjoining units, edge, curve, drain, post, or obstacle.
  2. Transfer edge, curve, drain, post, or obstacle lines directly to the paver. Use a straightedge, string template, trammel, or direct tracing appropriate to the geometry.
  3. Mark the waste side. Clearly identify which portion of the unit will be removed so the correct side is cut.
  4. Account for required joint and movement space. Offset the marked line by the approved joint width before cutting.

G. Cutting — Splitter and Saw Operation

  1. Use a splitter for suitable straight cuts where approved. Confirm the product and cut geometry permit splitting before selecting this method.
  2. Use wet saw or extracted saw for controlled precision cuts. Select this method for curves, drains, posts, and obstacles, and for any straight cut not suited to splitting.
  3. Feed the unit without twisting or forcing. Maintain a steady feed rate matched to the manufacturer's blade instructions.
  4. Keep hands outside the blade zone. Use a push stick or feed guide for any piece that would otherwise require hands near the blade.
  5. Use relief cuts only where the finished geometry permits them. Do not substitute a relief cut for a required curve or clean obstacle fit where appearance or structural support would be compromised.
  6. Avoid overcutting inside corners. Stop the cut precisely at the marked corner and complete the remaining material with a second pass or hand tool as appropriate.

H. Finishing, Test-Fit, and Cleanup

  1. Dress sharp edges using an approved wet or extracted method. Dress every cut edge exposed at a walking surface or hand-contact location.
  2. Collect slurry and offcuts. Remove both promptly from the cutting area and from any adjacent finished work.
  3. Test-fit the piece. Set the cut piece into its intended location before final installation.
  4. Maintain consistent joints around curves and obstacles. Confirm the test-fit joint matches the approved joint width on every adjoining side.
  5. Replace pieces that crack, rock, or fall below the minimum stable size. Do not install a piece that fails any of these conditions.
  6. Inspect visible face, edge quality, joint width, support, and alignment. Confirm every installed cut piece meets the Acceptance Standards in Section D.
  7. Clean equipment and dispose of slurry properly. Do not discharge slurry or wash water to a storm drain, soil, or waterway.
  8. Document cut method and controls. Record the cut type, method, silica controls used, and inspection result on the cutting log.

Common Site Inspection

Review every cutting session using the same checks regardless of cut type.

  • Setup check: confirm the cutting area is contained, the saw or splitter has passed inspection, and required PPE is worn before the first cut.
  • Marking check: confirm every cut line was transferred from the actual field condition and accounts for the approved joint width.
  • Cutting method check: confirm the method used matches the geometry, and that no dry-cutting occurred.
  • Finish check: confirm every exposed cut edge is dressed and every installed piece meets the minimum stable size.
  • Fit check: confirm every cut piece was test-fit and does not rock, bind, or leave an out-of-tolerance joint.
  • Cleanup check: confirm slurry and offcuts were contained and removed without discharge to a storm drain, soil, or waterway.

Complete the Paver Cutting and Fitting Log in the Inspection Checklist section below before this set of cuts is accepted into the field, border, or cap installation.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Cut Piece Rocks After Installation

Probable cause: the piece was cut with an out-of-square edge, or the underlying bedding was disturbed when the piece was set.
Confirmation: lift the piece and check the cut edge for square against a straightedge, and inspect the bedding beneath it for disturbance.
Correction: recut the piece square or rescreed the disturbed bedding before resetting it.
Reinspection: repeat the test-fit and support check.

Chipped or Spalled Visible Face

Probable cause: the feed rate was too fast for the blade and material, or the blade was not matched to the paver material.
Confirmation: inspect the chip pattern and compare the blade type and feed rate used against the manufacturer's instructions.
Correction: replace the damaged piece, correct the feed rate or blade, and recut.
Reinspection: repeat the visible-face inspection on the replacement piece.

Joint Too Wide or Too Narrow at a Cut Piece

Probable cause: the marked cut line did not account for the approved joint width, or the line was marked from a calculated dimension instead of the actual field condition.
Confirmation: measure the installed joint at the cut piece against the approved joint width on all adjoining sides.
Correction: remark and recut the piece directly from the field condition, allowing for the correct joint width.
Reinspection: repeat the marking check and the test-fit.

Cut Piece Falls Below Minimum Stable Size

Probable cause: the field layout left an undersized remainder at the perimeter, or the cut line was marked without checking the resulting piece size against the project standard.
Confirmation: measure the cut piece against the recorded minimum stable piece size for the project.
Correction: adjust the field or border layout to redistribute the cut, or substitute a border/accent unit sized for the condition, per Chapter 8 or Chapter 16.
Reinspection: repeat the minimum-piece-size check before installation.

Visible Silica Dust During Cutting

Probable cause: water flow or local exhaust extraction was not functioning, or the cutting method used did not match the required silica control.
Confirmation: check water supply and flow rate or extraction system function at the saw.
Correction: stop cutting, restore water flow or extraction, and do not resume until confirmed functional.
Reinspection: repeat the setup check before the next cut.

Curve Does Not Match the Template

Probable cause: the string template or trammel shifted during marking, or the saw path drifted from the marked line during the cut.
Confirmation: compare the cut edge against the original template or trammel setting.
Correction: remark from a freshly set template and recut, feeding the saw more slowly through the curved section.
Reinspection: repeat the marking check and test-fit for the curved section.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a completed, documented set of installed cut pieces and a cleaned cutting station, not a pile of unrecorded offcuts.

  • Remove and properly dispose of collected slurry and offcuts without discharge to storm drains, soil, or waterways
  • Clean the saw, splitter, blade, and work table before storage
  • Store PPE appropriately and inspect it for damage before the next use
  • File the completed cutting log with the field, border, or cap chapter's records that this cutting session supports
  • Retain the cutting log for reference throughout the remaining chapters that call on this procedure

Performance Check
