Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to restrain, compact, joint, activate, and inspect a completed paver field so that it becomes a locked, stable, finished surface rather than a set of loose units resting on bedding sand. The finished work product is one completed paver walkway or patio field — installed in Chapter 7 or Chapter 8 and cut in Chapter 9 — with edge restraint fastened into the compacted base, the field compacted with a protected plate compactor, joints filled with the specified joint or polymeric sand, polymeric sand activated exactly as the product requires, and the finished field inspected for edge security, joint fill, haze, washout, and lock.

You will expose the compacted base at the field perimeter, position and fasten edge restraint, clean and compact the paver surface, sweep sand into clean dry joints, recompact to settle the sand, remove all residue from paver faces, check weather and moisture conditions, activate polymeric sand using the exact watering pattern the manufacturer requires, protect the field through cure, and inspect the finished result.

This chapter depends directly on the paver field completed in Chapter 7 or Chapter 8 and the cut units produced in Chapter 9. It is also the chapter that Chapter 16's border work and Chapter 20's capstone project both depend on for a correctly restrained, jointed, and locked field.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HC-055 — Install edge restraints along the full perimeter of a completed paver field, cut or bent to fit straight runs and curves according to the product.
  • HC-056 — Fasten restraints securely into the compacted base at the specified spacing, including corner reinforcement.
  • HC-057 — Sweep joint sand into clean, dry paver joints using the correct sweeping direction and fill sequence.
  • HC-058 — Compact pavers using the specified plate compactor and protective mat, following the required pass direction and count.
  • HC-059 — Activate polymeric sand using the manufacturer's exact watering pattern, quantity, and weather conditions.
  • HC-060 — Inspect the locked paver field for joint fill, edge security, haze, washout, loose units, and overall field lock.

Where This Skill Fits

A paver field that is laid to pattern but never restrained, compacted, and jointed is not a finished hardscape. Edge restraint keeps the outer courses from creeping outward under traffic and seasonal movement. Compaction seats the pavers into the bedding sand and begins locking the field together. Joint sand, correctly filled and — where specified — chemically activated, completes that lock and resists joint erosion, weed growth, and insect intrusion.

Every stop-work and sequencing discipline you have followed since Chapter 4 — protecting the compacted base, keeping bedding sand clean and undisturbed, controlling silica during cutting — carries directly into this chapter's requirement to fasten restraint into a compacted base rather than loose sand, and to activate polymeric sand only under the exact conditions the manufacturer specifies. A field jointed or activated incorrectly in this chapter can fail even when every earlier chapter was executed correctly.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • exposing the compacted aggregate base at the paver-field perimeter
  • positioning, cutting or bending, and fastening edge restraint into the compacted base
  • cleaning the paver surface and joints before compaction
  • performing initial field compaction using a plate compactor and protective mat
  • correcting broken, high, or low units before jointing
  • spreading and sweeping joint or polymeric sand into clean, dry joints
  • recompacting to settle joint sand and refilling as required
  • removing residual sand from paver faces and adjacent surfaces
  • activating polymeric sand under the manufacturer's specified weather and watering conditions
  • protecting the field through cure and performing the final field-lock inspection

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • fasten edge restraint into loose bedding sand instead of the compacted aggregate base
  • compact a paver field with cracked, chipped, rocking, or incorrectly seated units still in place
  • sweep joint sand or polymeric sand into wet, contaminated, or partially filled joints
  • activate polymeric sand outside the manufacturer's stated temperature, moisture, or weather window
  • open the field to full traffic before the joint material and compaction are confirmed stable and cured

Any field condition that fails inspection in this chapter must be corrected and reinspected before Chapter 16 border integration or Chapter 20 capstone work proceeds on this field.

Trade Note

Edge restraint and jointing are the two operations most often rushed at the end of a paver project, and they are also the two operations most responsible for long-term field failure when rushed. Treat this chapter with the same discipline as the excavation and base chapters — the field is not finished until it is restrained, compacted, jointed, and inspected as one complete system.

Setup Standard

Do not begin compaction or jointing until the full perimeter edge restraint is installed and inspected. A field compacted or jointed before restraint is complete can shift at the edges during the very operation meant to lock it in place.

Shop Tip

Check the extended weather forecast before scheduling polymeric-sand activation, not just the forecast for the activation day itself. Many products require a specified dry period after activation, and rain during that window can wash out sand that has not yet cured.

Common Field Fundamentals

Restraining, compacting, and jointing a paver field is the operation that converts a laid pattern of individual units into one connected, load-sharing surface. Each part of this chapter's sequence depends on the one before it: restraint must be fastened into the compacted base before compaction begins, the field must be clean and free of broken units before jointing, and joints must be clean and dry before sand is activated.

Stop-Work Conditions

This chapter requires the installer to halt restraint, compaction, or jointing work whenever a condition falls outside what this chapter's procedure alone can resolve.

Critical Safety Cue — Restraint Fastened Into Loose Material

Do not fasten edge restraint into loose bedding sand, uncompacted fill, or any material other than the compacted aggregate base identified in Chapter 5. A restraint anchored into loose material will not hold the field perimeter and must be removed and reinstalled once the compacted base is confirmed exposed.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Polymeric Sand Activation Window

Do not activate polymeric sand outside the manufacturer's stated temperature range, moisture condition, or precipitation-free window. Check the forecast for the entire required cure period, not only the activation day, and postpone activation if the forecast does not meet the stated window.

Field Safety Cue — Compactor Operation on Finished Pavers

Operate the plate compactor only with the specified protective mat installed, and keep hands, feet, hoses, and cords clear of the compactor's travel path. Do not run the compactor directly against uncompacted edge restraint, exposed base, or unsupported cut units.

Measurement Cue — Fastener Spacing and Corner Reinforcement

Measure and record fastener spacing along every restraint run and confirm corner reinforcement matches the manufacturer's specification. A restraint run with fasteners spaced beyond the specified maximum is a deficiency even if the restraint appears secure by hand.

Code Alert — Wash Water and Slurry Discharge

Contain any wash water, slurry, or sand residue generated during cleanup. Do not discharge cementitious residue, polymeric-sand haze removal water, or cleaning runoff to storm drains, soil, or waterways. Verify local discharge requirements before cleanup begins.

E. Preparation — Review and Confirm Field Acceptance

  1. Review edge-restraint type, base extension, fasteners, compactor, protective mat, joint-sand product, weather, activation, and cure. Confirm every product and condition against the manufacturer instructions and record them on the Field-Lock Definition Record above.
  2. Confirm all field and cut pavers are accepted. Review the Chapter 7, 8, and 9 acceptance records and reinspect the field visually for any unit that has shifted, cracked, or rocked since those chapters were completed.

F. Edge Restraint Installation

  1. Expose the compacted base at the paver perimeter. Remove loose bedding sand, soil, or debris from the base extension along the full perimeter so the restraint will fasten directly into the compacted aggregate.
  2. Position edge restraint tight to the paver field. Set the restraint against the outer edge of the field with no gap that would allow the outer course to shift before fastening.
  3. Cut or bend restraint for curves according to the product. Use the restraint manufacturer's specified method for curved sections, maintaining continuous contact with the paver edge through the curve.
  4. Fasten into the compacted base using the specified spikes or anchors. Drive each fastener fully seated and perpendicular to the base surface.
  5. Maintain fastener spacing and corner reinforcement. Measure spacing along each run against the manufacturer's maximum and install additional fasteners at corners as specified.
  6. Do not anchor only into loose bedding sand. Reinstall any fastener found to be seated in sand rather than the compacted base.
  7. Inspect full perimeter restraint before compaction. Walk the entire perimeter, checking tightness to the field, fastener seating, spacing, and corner reinforcement before proceeding.

G. Initial Compaction

  1. Clean the paver surface and joints. Remove construction debris, cutting slurry residue, and loose material from the field before compaction.
  2. Perform initial compaction with the specified plate compactor and protective mat. Install the protective mat on the compactor base plate before the first pass.
  3. Follow the required pass direction and count. Compact in the manufacturer-specified pattern, overlapping passes and covering the full field including areas near restraint and cut units.
  4. Replace broken pavers and correct low or high units. Identify any unit that cracked, chipped, or settled unevenly during compaction and replace or reset it before continuing.

H. Joint Sand Installation

  1. Spread approved joint or polymeric sand on a dry surface. Confirm the paver surface and joints are dry using the moisture meter or manufacturer gauge before spreading sand.
  2. Sweep diagonally into clean, dry joints. Use the stiff broom to work sand into every joint across the field, sweeping at a diagonal angle to the joint lines.
  3. Compact again to settle sand. Run the plate compactor with the protective mat over the field to vibrate sand fully into the joints.
  4. Refill joints and repeat as specified. Add additional sand to any joint that has settled below the paver's finished surface and repeat sweeping and compaction until joints are fully filled.
  5. Remove all sand from paver faces, edges, and adjacent surfaces. Use the leaf blower or soft push broom to clear every trace of surface sand before checking weather conditions for activation.

I. Polymeric Sand Activation

  1. Check weather and moisture conditions before activation. Confirm the current temperature, joint dryness, and forecast meet the manufacturer's stated activation window before beginning.
  2. Activate polymeric sand using the exact watering pattern and quantity. Apply water using the specified nozzle setting, pattern, and volume per unit area exactly as the manufacturer instructs.
  3. Prevent runoff, ponding, washout, and residue. Watch for water pooling on the surface or running off the field during application and adjust the watering rate immediately if either occurs.
  4. Protect from rain, irrigation, traffic, and disturbance through cure. Barricade the field and confirm irrigation systems will not run during the manufacturer's stated cure period.

J. Final Field-Lock Inspection

  1. Inspect joint fill, edge security, haze, washout, loose units, and field lock. Walk the full field after the cure period, checking every joint for full depth fill, every restraint run for tightness and fastening, and every paver for rocking or shifting.
  2. Document product, weather, passes, watering, and cure. Complete the Field-Lock Record Schedule with the actual products, conditions, and counts used, and attach photographs of the restrained and jointed field.

Common Site Inspection

Review every restrained and jointed field using the same checks regardless of paver type or pattern.

  • Restraint check: confirm restraint is fastened into the compacted base along the full perimeter, tight to the field, with correct fastener spacing and corner reinforcement.
  • Compaction check: confirm the field was compacted with the protective mat, in the specified pass pattern, with no broken or unevenly seated units remaining.
  • Joint-fill check: confirm every joint is filled to the paver's full depth with no visible gaps after sweeping and recompaction.
  • Cleanliness check: confirm no residual sand remains on paver faces, edges, or adjacent surfaces.
  • Activation check: confirm polymeric sand was activated within the manufacturer's stated weather window, with no runoff, ponding, or washout.
  • Field-lock check: confirm no unit rocks, shifts, or separates under normal foot pressure after cure.

Complete the Field-Lock Inspection Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 11 or continuing to Chapter 16 border work.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Edge Restraint Shifts After Compaction

Probable cause: fasteners were seated in loose bedding sand or shallow, uncompacted material instead of the compacted aggregate base.
Confirmation: remove one fastener from the affected run and inspect the material it was seated in.
Correction: remove and reposition the restraint, expose the compacted base fully along the affected run, and refasten at the specified spacing.
Reinspection: repeat the restraint check and the compaction check for the affected section.

Joints Settle Below the Paver Surface After Sweeping

Probable cause: a single sweep-and-compact cycle was treated as sufficient, or sand was swept into joints that were not clean and dry.
Confirmation: inspect joint depth across the field and compare against the paver thickness.
Correction: clean and dry any contaminated joint, then repeat the sweep-and-compact cycle until every joint holds sand to the paver's full depth.
Reinspection: repeat the joint-fill check across the full field.

Polymeric Sand Haze Appears on Paver Faces

Probable cause: residual surface sand was not fully removed before activation, or the watering pattern applied too much water too quickly, carrying fine material onto the paver faces.
Confirmation: inspect paver faces in daylight for a visible film or discoloration, and compare the watering method used against the manufacturer's specified pattern.
Correction: follow the manufacturer's approved haze-removal method; do not use an unapproved acid or aggressive cleaner that could damage the paver finish or degrade the joint sand.
Reinspection: repeat the activation check and inspect for recurring haze after the corrected cleaning method.

Joint Sand Washes Out After Activation

Probable cause: rain occurred during the manufacturer's stated cure period, or the field was not adequately protected from irrigation or heavy traffic before the sand fully set.
Confirmation: compare the actual weather record during the cure period against the manufacturer's stated dry-period requirement.
Correction: remove washed-out material from the affected joints, allow the surface to dry completely, and repeat sand installation and activation once conditions meet the manufacturer's window.
Reinspection: repeat the joint-fill check and the activation check for the affected area.

Pavers Rock or Shift During Final Inspection

Probable cause: a cracked, chipped, or unevenly bedded unit was not identified and corrected before compaction, or edge restraint failed to hold the perimeter during compaction.
Confirmation: lift the affected unit and inspect the bedding sand beneath it and the nearest restraint run for displacement.
Correction: correct the bedding using the Chapter 18 repair procedure if the base or bedding has failed, replace the unit if damaged, and reinspect the nearest edge restraint before rejointing the area.
Reinspection: repeat the pre-compaction condition check, the compaction check, and the field-lock check for the affected area.

Fastener Spacing Exceeds the Manufacturer's Maximum

Probable cause: fasteners were installed by visual estimate rather than measured spacing, or spacing was not verified before compaction began.
Confirmation: measure fastener-to-fastener spacing along the full restraint run and compare against the manufacturer's stated maximum.
Correction: install additional fasteners at the required spacing, including any missing corner reinforcement.
Reinspection: repeat the restraint check for the full perimeter.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a completed, signed field-lock worksheet and a protected, curing field, not a verbal impression of a finished job.

  • Contain and properly dispose of all wash water, slurry, and sand residue generated during cleanup
  • Store leftover restraint stock, fasteners, and sand by product and lot for future repair work
  • File site photographs of restraint installation, compaction, jointing, and activation alongside their corresponding worksheet entries
  • Maintain barricades or protection through the full manufacturer-stated cure period before removing them
  • Retain the complete field-lock plan for reference throughout Chapter 16 border integration and the Chapter 20 capstone

Field Terms

Base Extension
The width of compacted aggregate base extending beyond the paver-field edge into which edge restraint is fastened.
Fastener Spacing
The maximum center-to-center distance between restraint spikes or anchors, including reduced spacing required at corners.
Pass Direction and Count
The compactor travel pattern and number of overlapping passes required to seat the field.
Clean, Dry Joint
A paver joint free of standing water, surface moisture, dust, or debris, meeting the manufacturer's condition for sweeping.
Activation Window
The temperature, moisture, and precipitation-free period the polymeric-sand manufacturer specifies for watering and initial cure.
Field Lock
The finished condition in which pavers do not rock, shift, or separate under normal foot traffic and the joint material is fully set.
Protective Mat
A compactor attachment that prevents chipping or scuffing of paver faces during compaction.
Haze
A visible film or discoloration on paver faces resulting from residual polymeric-sand fines not removed before or during activation.

Performance Check
