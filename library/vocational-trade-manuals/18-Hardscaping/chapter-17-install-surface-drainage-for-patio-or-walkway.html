Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install a functioning surface-drainage component — a channel drain, catch basin, or other approved surface-drainage device — within a paver or hardscape practice area, and to connect it to a lawful, tested outlet. The finished work product is one installed drainage component set to the approved elevation, connected to outlet piping at the correct slope, surrounded and supported by rebuilt aggregate base and bedding, integrated with the paver or hardscape surface, and verified by a controlled water-flow test with an established emergency overflow path.

You will review drainage calculations or the approved layout, confirm the component is appropriate for expected runoff, identify existing low points and water sources, verify outlet legality, locate utilities, excavate without undermining adjacent structures, set the component to grade, connect and slope the outlet pipe, protect the pipe, rebuild the surrounding base and bedding, reinstall pavers, establish an overflow route, run a controlled flow test, and correct any deficiency the test or inspection reveals.

This chapter depends directly on Chapter 4's excavation and compaction procedure, Chapter 5's aggregate base procedure, Chapter 6's screeded bedding procedure, and Chapter 9's paver-cutting procedure, each of which you apply here to rebuild hardscape around a drain rather than to install it from scratch. The finished drainage installation feeds directly into Chapter 20's capstone patio or walkway, where a drainage component installed under this exact procedure may be required.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HC-097 — Identify low points in a hardscape surface where surface water collects and requires capture.
  • HC-098 — Install a channel drain, catch basin, or other approved surface-drainage component where site conditions require one.
  • HC-099 — Direct captured runoff away from structures using correctly sloped outlet piping.
  • HC-100 — Connect a drainage outlet to a lawful, verified discharge point.
  • HC-101 — Protect the installed drainage path from crushing, blockage, erosion, and future excavation damage.
  • HC-102 — Inspect water flow through the installed system, including capture, discharge, ponding, leakage, and grate stability.

Where This Skill Fits

A paver walkway or patio installed under Chapters 3 through 10 already carries a finished slope toward a low point. That slope is only useful if the water arriving at the low point has somewhere lawful to go. This chapter installs the component that captures that water and the piping that carries it to a legal, tested outlet, without undermining the surrounding paver base or bedding that Chapters 4 through 6 established.

The same drainage discipline used in Chapter 13 for retaining-wall drainage — continuous fall, protected outlet, separation from fine soil, and a documented overflow path — applies here to surface drainage. A drain that captures water but has no lawful, tested outlet, or a drain installed without an overflow path, creates the same kind of failure this book treats as unacceptable in a retaining wall.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing drainage calculations or the approved layout, inlet type, grate, pipe, invert, outlet, cleanout, load rating, and overflow requirements
  • confirming the selected drainage component is appropriate for expected runoff and use
  • identifying existing low points and water sources within the paver or hardscape practice area
  • verifying outlet ownership, permission, and discharge legality before excavation begins
  • locating utilities and laying out the inlet and pipe route
  • excavating, setting, and bracing the drain component to the approved elevation
  • connecting and sloping outlet piping, and protecting it from crushing and future excavation
  • rebuilding aggregate base and bedding around the drain and reinstalling cut pavers
  • establishing an emergency overflow path
  • performing a controlled water-flow test and correcting deficiencies it reveals

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • select a drainage component or pipe size without approved drainage calculations or an approved layout stating the required capacity
  • connect to a storm drain, combined sewer, neighboring property, or any outlet without verified ownership, permission, and confirmed discharge legality
  • excavate before the required utility locate has been completed and confirmed
  • bury or cover the drainage component, pipe, or connections before the outlet has been tested and accepted
  • omit or defer the emergency overflow path required by Section H of the Work Order below

Any drainage installation that cannot confirm a lawful outlet, that requires connection to an unidentified or undocumented discharge point, or that reveals a site condition beyond this chapter's scope during excavation must be referred to the required qualified professional before work continues.

Trade Note

A drain that captures water is only half the system. The outlet pipe, its slope, its protection, and its lawful discharge point are what determine whether the water actually leaves the site. Treat the outlet connection with the same care as the inlet, and do not consider the installation complete until the flow test in Section I below has been run and passed.

Required PPE for this project: closed-toe footwear, eye protection when cutting pipe or pavers, gloves when handling aggregate and drain hardware, and knee protection when working at grade level for extended periods.

Setup Standard

Do not excavate the inlet or pipe route until the required utility locate is confirmed and the outlet's ownership, permission, and discharge legality are verified in writing. A drain installed on an unverified outlet must be disconnected and corrected, not left in place pending confirmation.

Shop Tip

Photograph the pipe route, slope readings, and connections before covering them. A drainage failure discovered after the base is rebuilt and pavers are reinstalled is far more expensive to diagnose and repair than one caught during the flow test in Section I.

Stop-Work Conditions

This chapter requires the installer to halt work, rather than proceed to connection or burial, whenever a condition falls outside what the approved drainage documents can resolve. These conditions must be referred to the required qualified professional before work continues.

Critical Safety Cue — Unverified or Unlawful Outlet

Do not connect outlet piping to any storm drain, combined sewer, ditch, neighboring property, or other discharge point without written confirmation of ownership, permission, and discharge legality. An unverified connection must be disconnected immediately if discovered, and the outlet re-verified before reconnection.

Code Alert — Discharge Point and Permit Requirements

Confirm whether the jurisdiction requires a permit, an approved discharge point, or a specific pipe material or size for surface-drainage connections before excavation begins. Local requirements vary; do not assume the outlet used on a prior project is automatically lawful for this site.

Field Safety Cue — Open Excavation and Trip Hazards

Barricade the drain excavation and pipe trench whenever work is not in progress. Keep the excavation edge stable, and do not leave an open trench unmarked in a pedestrian or vehicle path overnight.

Measurement Cue — Slope and Elevation Relative to the Fixed Reference

Set grate elevation and verify outlet pipe slope relative to the same fixed reference established in the Chapter 1 readiness plan. A slope measured against an unrelated or temporary reference cannot be verified against the approved drainage calculations.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Component Load Rating and Fittings

Install the drain component's grate, frame, and connection fittings exactly as the manufacturer specifies, including the stated load rating for the intended traffic. Do not substitute an unrated grate or an incompatible fitting to complete a connection.

E. Component and Outlet Review

  1. Review drainage calculations or approved layout, inlet type, grate, pipe, invert, outlet, cleanout, load rating, and overflow. Record each value on the Drain Installation Record before proceeding.
  2. Confirm the drainage component is appropriate for the expected runoff and use. Compare the component's stated capacity and load rating against the project area's size, surface type, and expected traffic.
  3. Identify existing low points and water sources. Compare current site conditions against the low points recorded in the Chapter 1 readiness plan and note any change.
  4. Verify outlet ownership, permission, and discharge legality. Obtain written confirmation before proceeding to excavation.

F. Layout, Excavation, and Foundation

  1. Locate utilities. Confirm the utility locate has been completed and confirmed for the full inlet and pipe route before any ground disturbance.
  2. Lay out inlet and pipe route. Mark the drain location and the pipe path to the verified outlet using the fixed reference from the Chapter 1 readiness plan.
  3. Excavate without undermining the paver base or structures. Remove material to the depth required for the component, foundation, and pipe trench, maintaining separation from adjacent footings and the surrounding compacted base.
  4. Prepare and compact the drain foundation. Place and compact approved aggregate to the specified thickness beneath the component location.

G. Component Setting and Outlet Connection

  1. Set the channel or basin to the approved elevation. Verify grate elevation relative to the stated fixed reference before proceeding.
  2. Align grate elevation slightly below or as specified relative to the hardscape surface. Confirm the exact relative elevation against the approved layout.
  3. Brace the drain against movement. Support the component so it does not shift during backfill or pipe connection.
  4. Connect outlet piping using approved fittings. Use only fittings compatible with the specified pipe material.
  5. Maintain required pipe slope. Verify continuous fall from the drain invert to the outlet using the line or laser level.
  6. Provide solid support and compacted haunching or bedding. Support the pipe along its full length according to the approved detail.
  7. Protect pipe from crushing and future excavation. Install protection specified for the pipe's depth and location, such as marking tape or rigid cover, where required.

H. Base Rebuild, Paver Reinstallation, and Overflow

  1. Rebuild aggregate base around the drain. Restore the base to the same thickness, compaction, and slope as the surrounding installed hardscape base from Chapter 5.
  2. Screed bedding and cut pavers to fit. Apply the Chapter 6 screeding procedure and the Chapter 9 cutting procedure to reinstall pavers around the drain frame.
  3. Maintain joint, movement, and edge support. Preserve the approved joint width and provide full support at every cut edge adjacent to the drain frame.
  4. Install grate and verify removability. Confirm the grate seats securely and can be removed for maintenance without tools not specified by the manufacturer.
  5. Establish an emergency overflow path. Confirm and mark the route water will follow on the finished surface if the drain is blocked or overwhelmed, directed away from structures, entrances, and prohibited discharge points.

I. Flow Test and Final Inspection

  1. Conduct a controlled water-flow test. Apply a measured volume of water at the drain's low point using a hose or measured bucket, and observe capture, discharge, and any leakage or ponding for the duration specified in the approved layout or, absent a stated duration, until discharge stabilizes.
  2. Inspect capture, outlet flow, ponding, leakage, settlement, grate stability, and discharge. Record each observation on the drainage log.
  3. Correct low areas, reverse slope, weak support, or blocked outlet. Address every deficiency the flow test or inspection reveals before final acceptance.
  4. Document pipe route, elevations, outlet, and test. Complete the drainage log with photographs of the concealed pipe route taken before backfill, along with final elevations and the flow-test result.

Common Site Inspection

Review every drainage installation using the same checks regardless of component type.

  • Document check: confirm drainage calculations or the approved layout, outlet permission, and utility locate are all obtained and recorded before excavation.
  • Elevation check: confirm grate elevation and pipe slope are recorded relative to the stated fixed reference.
  • Connection check: confirm outlet piping uses approved fittings, maintains continuous slope, and is haunched and protected per the approved detail.
  • Rebuild check: confirm base, bedding, and paver reinstallation around the drain match the surrounding installed hardscape.
  • Overflow check: confirm an emergency overflow path is established and does not discharge toward a structure or prohibited point.
  • Flow-test check: confirm the controlled water-flow test shows full capture, unobstructed discharge, no ponding, no leakage, and a stable grate.

Complete the Drainage Installation Record and the Inspection Checklist below before covering any concealed connection.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Water Ponds at the Grate Instead of Draining

Probable cause: the grate elevation is set higher than the approved relative elevation, or the surrounding hardscape surface does not slope toward the drain.
Confirmation: measure grate elevation against the stated fixed reference and check surface slope on a grid extending from the drain outward.
Correction: remove surrounding pavers, correct base and bedding elevation to restore the approved slope toward the grate, and reinstall pavers and joint material.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation check and the flow test.

Outlet Pipe Shows Reverse Slope or a Low Section

Probable cause: haunching material settled unevenly under the pipe, or the pipe was not verified with a level before backfilling.
Confirmation: excavate the affected section and check slope with the line or laser level against the fixed reference.
Correction: remove and re-haunch the affected pipe section, restoring continuous fall from invert to outlet.
Reinspection: repeat the pipe-slope check and the flow test before backfilling again.

Leakage Observed at a Pipe Connection During the Flow Test

Probable cause: a fitting was not fully seated, an incompatible fitting was used, or the joint was not assembled according to the manufacturer's instructions.
Confirmation: re-run the flow test while observing each connection directly, and check the fitting type against the manufacturer's specification.
Correction: disassemble and reassemble the connection using the specified fitting and method, or replace the fitting if damaged.
Reinspection: repeat the flow test and confirm no leakage at any connection.

Grate Rocks or Is Difficult to Remove

Probable cause: the frame was not braced adequately during backfill, or debris entered the frame seat before the grate was installed.
Confirmation: remove the grate and inspect the frame seat for debris, movement, or an out-of-level condition.
Correction: clean the frame seat, correct any frame movement, and reset the grate according to the manufacturer's instructions.
Reinspection: repeat the grate-stability and removability check.

Discharge at the Outlet Is Weaker Than the Volume Entering the Grate

Probable cause: a blockage exists in the pipe, or the pipe diameter does not match the approved layout.
Confirmation: inspect the pipe route for an undocumented reduction in diameter, a crushed section, or debris, using a cleanout if one is installed.
Correction: clear the blockage or replace the affected pipe section with the specified diameter and material.
Reinspection: repeat the flow test and confirm discharge volume matches the volume entering the grate.

No Overflow Path Established Before Testing

Probable cause: the installer proceeded directly from paver reinstallation to the flow test without confirming where water would go if the drain failed.
Confirmation: review the drainage log for a recorded overflow path; if none is recorded, the installation does not meet the Acceptance Standards in Section D.
Correction: identify and mark a route on the finished surface that directs excess water away from structures, entrances, and prohibited discharge points, and record it on the drainage log.
Reinspection: repeat the overflow check before final acceptance.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a tested, documented, and fully reinstated hardscape surface around the drain, not an open excavation or an unverified connection.

  • Remove excavation spoil, unused fittings, and packaging from the work area
  • Confirm no bedding sand, joint material, or debris remains on the finished surface or grate
  • Store the drainage log, outlet permission record, and pipe-route photographs with the project's Chapter 1 readiness plan
  • Confirm the overflow path remains clear of stored material or equipment
  • Retain the complete drainage record for reference in Chapter 18 repairs and the Chapter 20 capstone

Field Terms

Invert
The lowest interior elevation of a pipe or drain component at a given point, controlling the direction and rate of flow.
Grate Elevation
The finished elevation of the drain grate relative to the surrounding hardscape surface.
Outlet
The lawful point where captured water is discharged from the drainage system.
Overflow Path
The route surface water follows if the drainage component is blocked, overwhelmed, or not functioning.
Haunching
Compacted aggregate or bedding placed under and around a pipe to provide continuous support.
Cleanout
An accessible point in the piping system allowing inspection or clearing of a blockage.
Flow Test
A controlled application of water used to verify capture, discharge, ponding, and leakage before final acceptance.
Load Rating
The manufacturer-stated traffic or load capacity of a drain grate or frame.
Fixed Reference
The single permanent point from which grate elevation and pipe slope are measured and verified.
Discharge Legality
Written confirmation that an outlet connection is owned, permitted, or otherwise lawful to receive the drainage system's discharge.

Performance Check

With the drain installed, connected, rebuilt, and tested, review the finished installation against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm that the component matches the approved calculations, the outlet is verified lawful, grate elevation and pipe slope are correct relative to the stated fixed reference, the pipe is protected, base and bedding around the drain match the surrounding hardscape, the overflow path is established, and the flow test shows full capture, unobstructed discharge, no ponding, no leakage, and a stable grate. If any item is incomplete or a deficiency remains, correct it and repeat the affected check before final acceptance.
