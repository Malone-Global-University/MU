Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to excavate and compact one hardscape area to the depth, slope, and support conditions established in the approved plan. The finished work product is one excavated and compacted subgrade, at the approved depth and slope, with unsuitable soil removed, moisture and compaction verified on a grid, and the excavation documented and accepted before aggregate-base placement begins.

You will verify the layout, excavation depth, utility markings, spoil location, and equipment limits; barricade the area; strip vegetation and organic material; excavate in controlled passes while protecting foundations, utilities, and adjacent property; hand-expose utilities where required; inspect exposed soil for unsuitable conditions; remove and correct unsuitable material; shape the subgrade to slope; compact using equipment appropriate to soil type; check elevation on a grid; verify slope and drainage direction; and document the finished, accepted excavation.

This chapter depends directly on the layout and reference points established in Chapter 3. It produces the accepted subgrade that Chapter 5 builds the compacted aggregate base on top of. No aggregate base, bedding sand, or paver work in this book may proceed over an excavation that has not passed this chapter's acceptance standards.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HC-019 — Excavate a hardscape area to the design depth established by the approved layout and plan.
  • HC-020 — Identify and remove unsuitable soil, including organic material, roots, soft spots, debris, and uncontrolled fill.
  • HC-021 — Establish the required subgrade slope across the excavated area.
  • HC-022 — Compact the subgrade using equipment and passes appropriate to the soil type and area.
  • HC-023 — Verify finished subgrade elevation on a grid relative to the fixed reference.
  • HC-024 — Inspect the finished excavation and document its acceptance before base placement.

Where This Skill Fits

Every paver walkway, patio, gravel path, retaining wall, and step assembly in this book depends on a subgrade prepared to this chapter's standard. A subgrade excavated to the wrong depth, left with unsuitable soil in place, compacted with the wrong equipment, or graded to the wrong slope cannot be corrected by a thicker aggregate base or a thicker bedding layer. Chapter 5's aggregate base is placed directly on the subgrade this chapter produces, and any deficiency carried forward from this chapter becomes a hidden defect in every layer above it.

This chapter also carries forward the stop-work discipline from Chapter 1: excavation must stop, not proceed on assumption, whenever conditions differ materially from the plan, whenever an unidentified utility is found, or whenever soil conditions require correction beyond the scope of ordinary subgrade preparation.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • verifying the approved layout, excavation depth, utility markings, spoil location, and equipment limits before excavation begins
  • barricading the area and establishing machine and hand-excavation zones
  • stripping vegetation, sod, topsoil, roots, and organic material
  • excavating in controlled passes while maintaining separation from foundations, utilities, trees, irrigation, and property lines
  • hand-exposing utilities where required
  • stockpiling or removing spoil without blocking drainage
  • inspecting exposed soil for soft spots, pumping, organic material, debris, uncontrolled fill, or contamination
  • removing unsuitable material to approved limits and refilling overexcavated areas with approved material in compacted lifts
  • shaping the subgrade to the required slope, moisture-conditioning soil only as specified, and compacting with equipment appropriate to soil type and area
  • checking elevation on a grid, verifying slope and drainage direction, and performing proof-rolling, probing, or density testing where specified
  • correcting soft, loose, high, low, or unstable areas and documenting excavation depth, soil conditions, corrections, and acceptance

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • excavate, hand-expose, or probe near a utility that has not been located and marked, or in any area where a locate response is missing, ambiguous, or contradicted by visible evidence
  • undermine a foundation, footing, slab, wall, fence, post, or adjacent paving to reach design depth
  • continue excavation when conditions differ materially from the approved plan, including unidentified utilities, unstable excavations, buried tanks, contaminated soil, uncontrolled fill, groundwater, or expansive soil, without referring the condition to the required qualified professional
  • place aggregate base over a subgrade that has not passed this chapter's elevation, slope, and compaction acceptance standards
  • substitute a thicker aggregate base or bedding layer for correcting a soft, contaminated, or improperly sloped subgrade

Any condition that fails inspection in this chapter must be corrected, or referred to the required qualified professional, before Chapter 5 aggregate-base work begins.

Trade Note

The subgrade is the least visible part of a finished hardscape and the one most responsible for its long-term performance. Every excavation and compaction record produced in this chapter should be treated as a permanent part of the project file, not a working note that can be discarded once pavers are installed.

Setup Standard

Do not begin machine or hand excavation until the utility-locate response covering the full excavation area has been confirmed and is still valid. Reconfirm markings before excavation if more than the locate service's stated validity period has passed since the response.

Shop Tip

Check elevation on the same grid pattern you plan to use for the aggregate base and bedding layers in Chapters 5 and 6. Using a consistent grid across all three chapters makes it far easier to trace a finished surface defect back to the layer that caused it.

Stop-Work Conditions

This chapter requires the excavator to halt work, rather than continue on assumption, whenever a condition falls outside what the approved plan alone can resolve. These conditions must be referred to the required qualified professional before excavation continues in the affected area.

Critical Safety Cue — Unidentified Utilities and Unstable Excavations

Stop excavation immediately if an unmarked or unidentified utility, buried tank, or unstable excavation wall is encountered. Do not probe, dig, or drive equipment closer to the condition. Contact the utility-locating service or required qualified professional and do not resume in that area until the condition is resolved and documented.

Code Alert — Contaminated Soil and Groundwater

Stop excavation and do not handle, stockpile, or dispose of soil that shows discoloration, odor, debris consistent with prior dumping, or unexpected groundwater. Refer the condition to the required qualified professional and document the referral before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Excavation Walls and Spoil Placement

Keep workers clear of unsupported excavation walls deeper than the approved plan's stated safe limit. Place spoil away from the excavation edge at a distance sufficient to prevent wall collapse, and never so that it blocks an existing drainage path.

Measurement Cue — Elevation Grid Spacing

Check subgrade elevation at a grid spacing no wider than the approved plan specifies, and always at every corner, edge, and drain location. Record every grid-point elevation relative to the same fixed reference used in Chapters 1 and 3.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Compaction Equipment Selection

Match compactor type, weight, and lift thickness to the soil type and area size specified by the approved plan or geotechnical information. Do not select compaction equipment based on availability alone when the plan specifies a required type.

E. Preparation — Verify Plan and Set Up the Work Area

  1. Verify layout, excavation depth, utility markings, spoil location, and equipment limits. Compare the Chapter 3 layout and approved plan against current utility markings and confirm they remain valid before excavation begins.
  2. Barricade the area and establish machine and hand-excavation zones. Mark the boundary between areas approved for machine excavation and areas requiring hand excavation near utilities, foundations, or other sensitive features.
  3. Strip vegetation, sod, topsoil, roots, and organic material. Remove all organic material across the full excavation footprint and place it in the approved spoil location, separate from reusable subgrade or base material.

F. Excavation — Controlled Passes and Utility Protection

  1. Excavate in controlled passes. Remove soil in limited-depth cuts, observing continuously for utilities, soil change, and proximity to foundations or other structures.
  2. Maintain separation from foundations, utilities, trees, irrigation, and property lines. Do not excavate closer than the approved plan's stated clearance to any of these features.
  3. Hand expose utilities where required. Use hand tools only within the clearance zone around any marked utility, and confirm exact utility position and depth before continuing machine excavation nearby.
  4. Do not undermine foundations, footings, slabs, walls, fences, posts, or adjacent paving. Stop excavation at the approved limit next to any of these features and refer the condition if the design requires closer work.
  5. Stockpile or remove spoil without blocking drainage. Place spoil only in the approved location, clear of existing swales, catch basins, and discharge paths.

G. Soil Inspection and Correction

  1. Inspect exposed soil for soft spots, pumping, organic material, debris, uncontrolled fill, or contamination. Probe at a regular interval across the excavated area and record every location where a deficiency is found.
  2. Remove unsuitable material to approved limits. Excavate deficient soil to the depth and extent required to reach stable, suitable material, as confirmed by inspection or probing.
  3. Stop work when conditions differ materially from the plan. Halt excavation in the affected area and refer the condition to the required qualified professional before continuing.

H. Shaping and Compaction

  1. Shape subgrade to the required slope. Grade the exposed subgrade to the slope and drainage direction stated on the approved plan.
  2. Moisture-condition soil only as specified. Add or dry soil only to the moisture condition stated by the approved plan or geotechnical information, and do not compact soil outside that stated range.
  3. Compact with equipment appropriate to soil type and area. Select compactor type and lift thickness matching the soil type and area size specified.
  4. Overlap compactor passes. Maintain the overlap pattern specified by the approved plan or, absent a stated pattern, an overlap sufficient to compact the full area with no untreated strip.
  5. Compact corners and confined areas using suitable equipment. Use hand-operated compaction equipment sized for corners, edges, and areas a plate compactor cannot reach.
  6. Refill overexcavated areas only with approved material in compacted lifts. Place refill material in lifts no thicker than specified and compact each lift before placing the next.

I. Verification and Acceptance

  1. Check elevation on a grid. Measure subgrade elevation at every grid point, corner, edge, and drain location relative to the fixed reference, and record each reading.
  2. Verify slope and drainage direction. Confirm the recorded grid elevations produce the slope and drainage direction stated on the approved plan.
  3. Perform proof-rolling, probing, or density testing where specified. Conduct the test method stated by the approved plan or geotechnical information and record the results.
  4. Correct soft, loose, high, low, or unstable areas. Rework any area that fails the elevation, slope, compaction, or test check, then repeat the affected verification step.
  5. Document excavation depth, soil conditions, corrections, and acceptance. Complete the excavation record with final elevations, soil conditions found and corrected, test results, and a signed acceptance before Chapter 5 begins.

Common Site Inspection

Review every finished subgrade using the same checks regardless of project type.

  • Depth check: confirm finished subgrade elevation matches the design depth within the stated tolerance at every grid point.
  • Soil check: confirm no organic material, roots, debris, uncontrolled fill, soft spot, or contamination remains within the excavation limits.
  • Slope check: confirm the subgrade slope and drainage direction match the approved plan at every grid point.
  • Compaction check: confirm equipment, lift thickness, moisture condition, and pass count match the approved plan or geotechnical information.
  • Utility and clearance check: confirm no foundation, utility, tree, or property-line clearance was violated during excavation.
  • Documentation check: confirm the excavation record is complete and signed before aggregate-base work begins.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Subgrade Excavated Below or Above Design Depth

Probable cause: grade stakes were not checked against the fixed reference during excavation, or excavation continued past the design depth without intermediate elevation checks.
Confirmation: measure elevation at multiple grid points relative to the fixed reference and compare against the design depth stated on the approved plan.
Correction: if excavated too deep, refill with approved material in compacted lifts to the correct elevation; if not deep enough, excavate the remaining depth in a controlled pass.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation grid check across the corrected area.

Soft Spot Discovered After Initial Compaction

Probable cause: probing was performed at too wide an interval, or a soft area was masked by surface soil that appeared stable before compaction.
Confirmation: re-probe the affected area at a closer interval and compare compactor response against surrounding stable subgrade.
Correction: remove the soft material to stable support, refill with approved material in compacted lifts, and recompact the corrected area.
Reinspection: repeat the soil inspection and compaction check for the affected area.

Subgrade Slope Does Not Match the Approved Drainage Direction

Probable cause: elevation was checked at too few grid points, or shaping followed visible ground contours instead of the reference lines and elevations established in Chapter 3.
Confirmation: measure elevation at a full grid across the project area and compare the calculated slope and direction against the approved plan.
Correction: reshape the subgrade to the correct slope using the Chapter 3 reference lines and elevations, then recompact the reshaped area.
Reinspection: repeat the slope check across the full project area.

Compaction Equipment Did Not Match Soil Type or Lift Thickness

Probable cause: equipment was selected based on availability rather than the soil type and lift thickness stated in the approved plan or geotechnical information.
Confirmation: compare the equipment and lift thickness actually used against the approved plan's specification for the soil type present.
Correction: remove and recompact the affected lift using the specified equipment and lift thickness, or perform the specified density test to confirm the existing compaction is adequate before proceeding.
Reinspection: repeat the compaction record review and, if recompacted, the elevation grid check.

Excavation Approached a Foundation or Utility Without Required Clearance

Probable cause: machine excavation continued past the marked hand-excavation boundary, or the clearance zone around a utility or foundation was not verified before excavating nearby.
Confirmation: measure the actual clearance achieved against the approved plan's stated clearance requirement.
Correction: stop excavation in the affected area, hand-expose the feature to confirm its exact position, and refer the condition to the required qualified professional if the design cannot be met without closer work.
Reinspection: repeat the utility and clearance check for the affected area before resuming.

Excavation and Compaction Record Incomplete at Time of Acceptance

Probable cause: a finding recorded during excavation, such as a corrected soft spot or an overexcavated area, was not carried forward to the final excavation record.
Confirmation: cross-check every recorded field observation against the excavation and compaction record schedule to confirm each item appears there.
Correction: add the missing item to the record with its correction and result, and revise the acceptance decision if the missing item would have changed it.
Reinspection: repeat the documentation check before reissuing the acceptance decision.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a compacted, verified subgrade and a completed excavation record, not an excavation left open on the assumption that the base layer will correct any remaining deficiency.

  • Remove excavation equipment, hand tools, and grade stakes not required for Chapter 5 layout reference
  • Confirm the approved spoil location remains clear of drainage paths and access routes
  • Protect the accepted subgrade from traffic, rain, and contamination until aggregate-base placement begins
  • Store the excavation and compaction record, proof-roll or density test results, and any soil-correction photographs with the Chapter 1 readiness plan and Chapter 3 layout
  • Retain the complete excavation record for reference throughout Chapters 5 through 20

Field Terms

Design Depth
The vertical distance from the finished surface down to the accepted subgrade elevation, as stated on the approved plan.
Unsuitable Material
Organic matter, roots, debris, uncontrolled fill, contaminated soil, or soil that fails to support compaction.
Controlled Pass
One excavation cut of limited depth performed with continuous observation for utilities and soil change.
Overexcavation
Removal of subgrade material beyond design depth, requiring approved refill in compacted lifts.
Proof-Roll
A controlled pass of loaded or compaction equipment used to detect soft or yielding subgrade areas.
Fixed Reference
The permanent point from which every excavation depth and subgrade elevation is measured.
Lift
One layer of soil or fill placed and compacted before the next layer is added.
Grid Elevation Check
Measurement of finished subgrade elevation at regularly spaced points across the project area.
Hand-Excavation Zone
An area near utilities, foundations, or other sensitive features where only hand tools are approved for excavation.
Density Test
A specified test method used to confirm compacted soil meets the required density or support standard.

Performance Check

With the subgrade excavated, corrected, and compacted, review the finished excavation against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm that finished elevation matches the design depth at every grid point, no unsuitable material remains within the excavation limits, subgrade slope and drainage direction match the approved plan, compaction equipment and method match the soil type present, any specified proof-roll or density test has been performed and passed, and the excavation record is complete and signed. If any item is incomplete or a deficiency remains unresolved, correct it and reinspect the affected record before issuing final acceptance and beginning Chapter 5.
