Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to cut, fit, bond, align, and inspect cap units on one completed retaining-wall or seat-wall practice section. The finished work product is a straight or smoothly curved wall cap installed on the top course of a wall accepted under Chapter 12, with drainage and backfill accepted under Chapter 13, showing consistent overhang, symmetrically distributed joints and cuts, secure adhesion, and a cure protected from movement and weather.

You will confirm the wall is ready to receive a cap, dry-lay every cap unit before committing any adhesive, distribute cuts and joints for a symmetrical result, mark and cut corner and curve pieces using the silica controls taught in Chapter 9, apply masonry adhesive in a controlled bead pattern, reset caps in sequence with correct alignment and overhang, and inspect the finished cap before the wall is opened to use.

This chapter depends directly on Chapter 12, which built the wall's base, courses, bond, and alignment, and Chapter 13, which installed the wall's drainage system and backfill. A cap cannot be installed on a wall that has not been accepted under both of those chapters. The skills in this chapter also draw on Chapter 9's cutting and silica-control procedure, which governs every cap cut in this project.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HC-079 — Clean the wall top of dust, sand, moisture, and debris before dry-laying or bonding any cap unit.
  • HC-080 — Dry-fit cap units across the full wall length, distributing cuts and joints symmetrically before any unit is bonded.
  • HC-081 — Cut cap blocks for corners, curves, and ends using the Chapter 9 silica-control procedure.
  • HC-082 — Apply compatible exterior masonry adhesive in the specified bead pattern, keeping adhesive clear of drainage paths and visible edges.
  • HC-083 — Align cap overhang, joints, curves, and ends consistently across the full wall length.
  • HC-084 — Inspect the finished wall cap for alignment, joint consistency, adhesion, overhang, rocking, and sharp edges before the wall is opened to use.

Where This Skill Fits

A retaining wall or seat wall is not finished work until it is capped. The cap protects the top course from water intrusion, gives the wall a finished visible edge, and, on a seat wall, becomes the surface people sit on and lean against. A cap installed before the wall and its drainage are accepted, or installed without a dry layout, produces uneven overhang, mismatched joints at corners, and cap units that debond or rock loose within a season.

This chapter sits between the structural and drainage work of Chapters 12 and 13 and the surface work of Chapters 16 through 19. A wall is not ready for a patio border to tie into it, and a seat wall is not ready for use, until this chapter's cap is bonded, aligned, and cured.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • confirming wall alignment, final height, drainage, backfill, and top-course acceptance before cap work begins
  • reviewing cap type, overhang, joint pattern, corner and curve treatment, adhesive, temperature limits, and cure time
  • cleaning the wall top and dry-laying all cap units before bonding any unit
  • distributing cuts and joints symmetrically and checking overhang on both wall faces
  • marking, cutting, and dressing corner, curve, and end pieces under Chapter 9 silica controls
  • applying compatible exterior masonry adhesive in the specified bead pattern
  • resetting caps in sequence, aligning overhang, joints, curves, and ends
  • protecting the cap from movement and weather through cure
  • inspecting the finished cap and correcting loose or misaligned units before the wall is opened to use

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • cap a wall whose alignment, height, drainage, or backfill has not been accepted under Chapters 12 and 13
  • select or substitute an adhesive not identified as compatible with the specific cap and wall-block material by the manufacturer
  • bond a single cap unit before the full dry layout is complete and cuts are distributed across the wall length
  • open the wall to foot traffic, seating, or any load before the adhesive manufacturer's stated cure time has elapsed

Any wall that fails the Section D acceptance standards below must be corrected, or referred to the qualified professional identified in Chapter 12, before this chapter's cap work continues.

Trade Note

A dry layout is not optional preparation, it is the step that prevents a wall from ending in a narrow sliver of cap at one end or an unbalanced joint pattern at a visible corner. Every cap in this chapter is dry-fit across the entire wall length before the first bead of adhesive is applied.

Setup Standard

Do not apply adhesive to any cap unit until the full dry layout is complete, cuts are marked and distributed, and the wall top is clean, dry, and free of dust, sand, and debris. A cap bonded onto a dusty or damp surface will debond.

Shop Tip

Dry-lay the entire cap run before cutting anything. Walking the full length first shows you where the natural cut falls, so you can shift the starting joint to avoid a narrow sliver at a visible end or corner.

Prerequisites

  • A documented Chapter 12 acceptance record confirming wall alignment, first-course level, bond, setback, backfill, and final height
  • A documented Chapter 13 acceptance record confirming drainage aggregate, pipe, fabric, outlet, and overflow route are complete and protected
  • Identified cap-unit product and manufacturer instructions stating specified overhang and joint pattern
  • Identified adhesive product confirmed compatible with both the cap-unit material and the wall-block material by the manufacturer
  • Weather conditions within the adhesive manufacturer's stated application and cure temperature range
  • A cutting station meeting the Chapter 9 silica-control requirements for any corner, curve, or end cuts

Common Field Fundamentals

Every wall cap in this chapter follows the same sequence: confirm the wall is ready, clean the top, dry-lay every unit, distribute cuts, cut under silica control, bond in sequence, align overhang and joints, protect through cure, and inspect before use. That sequence does not change between a straight retaining-wall cap and a curved seat-wall cap; only the cut geometry changes.

Stop-Work Conditions

This chapter requires the installer to halt cap work whenever a condition falls outside what the wall's accepted records or the manufacturer instructions can resolve.

Critical Safety Cue — Uncapped or Unaccepted Wall

Do not begin cap work on a wall that has not received a documented Chapter 12 acceptance for alignment and height or a documented Chapter 13 acceptance for drainage and backfill. Bonding a cap to an unaccepted wall conceals a defect that cutting into the cap later will not safely correct.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Adhesive Compatibility and Temperature

Use only an adhesive identified by its manufacturer as compatible with both the cap-unit material and the wall-block material. Do not apply adhesive below the manufacturer's stated minimum application temperature, and do not assume an adhesive cures normally in conditions outside its stated range.

Safety Cue — Cutting Silica Controls

Cut all corner, curve, and end cap pieces using the contained cutting area, wet saw or extracted saw, and personal protective equipment specified in Chapter 9. Do not dry-cut cap units at the wall.

Measurement Cue — Check Overhang on Both Wall Faces

Verify the specified overhang on the wall's exterior face and, on a double-face seat wall, on the interior face as well. An overhang checked on only one face can leave the opposite face short of the specified dimension or projecting beyond it.

Field Safety Cue — Protecting the Cure

Barricade or otherwise restrict access to a freshly bonded cap. Do not permit foot traffic, seating, leaning, or material staging on the cap until the adhesive manufacturer's stated cure time has fully elapsed.

E. Preparation — Confirm Readiness and Review Cap Requirements

  1. Confirm wall alignment, final height, drainage, backfill, and top-course acceptance. Review the Chapter 12 and Chapter 13 acceptance records for the specific wall section before proceeding. Do not continue if either record is missing or shows an unresolved deficiency.
  2. Review cap type, overhang, joint pattern, corners, curves, adhesive, temperature, and cure. Record each value on the Cap Work Order Record above using the manufacturer instructions for the identified cap and adhesive products.
  3. Clean dust, sand, moisture, and debris from the wall top. Sweep and, if the adhesive manufacturer requires a dry surface, allow the wall top to dry fully before proceeding.

F. Dry Layout and Cut Planning

  1. Dry-lay all cap units. Place every full and anticipated cut unit across the entire wall length without adhesive, following the specified joint pattern.
  2. Distribute cuts and joints symmetrically. Adjust the starting position of the layout so that cut pieces at each end or corner are balanced in size rather than leaving a narrow sliver at one end.
  3. Check overhang on both wall faces. Measure the specified overhang at several points along the exterior face and, on a double-face seat wall, the interior face, and record the results.
  4. Mark corner, curve, and end cuts. Transfer the required cut lines directly onto each unit identified in the dry layout, accounting for joint width.

G. Cutting

  1. Cut caps using Chapter 9 silica controls. Perform every corner, curve, and end cut in the contained cutting area using the wet saw, extracted saw, or splitter and personal protective equipment specified in Chapter 9.
  2. Dress sharp edges where required. Use the approved wet or extracted method to dress any cut edge that will remain visible or within reach on the finished cap.
  3. Dry-fit cut pieces. Return each cut piece to its marked position in the dry layout and confirm fit, joint width, and overhang before removing it for bonding.

H. Bonding and Alignment

  1. Mark each cap location. Number or otherwise mark each unit's position in the confirmed dry layout so the bonding sequence matches the planned layout exactly.
  2. Remove caps in sequence. Lift units one at a time or in small groups, keeping the marked sequence intact.
  3. Apply compatible exterior masonry adhesive in the specified bead pattern. Apply adhesive to the wall top only, following the manufacturer's stated bead width, spacing, and coverage.
  4. Keep adhesive away from drainage paths and visible edges. Do not allow adhesive to enter any wall drainage opening or extend beyond the cap footprint onto a visible face.
  5. Reset caps in order. Place each unit back into its marked position immediately after applying adhesive to that section.
  6. Align overhang, joints, curves, and ends. Check each unit against the string line and the overhang measurements recorded in Section F as it is set.
  7. Seat caps without squeezing out excessive adhesive. Use the rubber mallet to seat each unit evenly, checking that adhesive coverage remains within the specified bead pattern.
  8. Clean adhesive promptly. Remove any adhesive that reaches a visible surface before it cures, using the method specified by the adhesive manufacturer.

I. Cure Protection and Final Inspection

  1. Protect the cap from movement and weather through cure. Barricade or otherwise restrict access to the wall for the full duration of the adhesive manufacturer's stated cure time, and protect the fresh adhesive from rain if the manufacturer requires it.
  2. Inspect alignment, joint consistency, adhesion, overhang, rocking, and sharp edges. Check every unit along the wall for consistent overhang and reveal, full adhesion with no visible gap, no rocking under hand pressure, and no unresolved sharp edge.
  3. Correct loose or misaligned caps before opening the wall to use. Lift, clean, re-bond, and realign any unit that fails inspection, and restart that unit's cure period from the time of correction.
  4. Document adhesive and cure. Record the adhesive lot, application temperature, cure start and end times, and final inspection result on the cap work order.

Common Site Inspection

Review every cap installation using the same checks regardless of wall type.

  • Readiness check: confirm Chapter 12 and Chapter 13 acceptance records are on file before any cap work is credited toward this chapter.
  • Layout check: confirm the dry layout covered the full wall length and cuts are distributed symmetrically before any adhesive was applied.
  • Cutting check: confirm every corner, curve, and end cut was produced under Chapter 9 silica controls.
  • Adhesion check: confirm the adhesive product, bead pattern, and application temperature match the manufacturer instructions recorded in Section E.
  • Alignment check: confirm overhang, joints, curves, and ends are consistent along the full wall length with no rocking unit.
  • Cure check: confirm the wall was protected from movement, traffic, and weather for the full stated cure time before being opened to use.

Complete the Cap Installation Inspection Checklist below before the wall is opened to use or handed off to a later chapter.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Narrow Sliver of Cap at One End of the Wall

Probable cause: the dry layout was started from a fixed end without checking how the cut fell at the opposite end or corner.
Confirmation: re-lay the cap run dry from both directions and compare the resulting cut sizes at each end.
Correction: shift the starting joint position so the cut pieces at each end are balanced and no piece falls below the cap manufacturer's minimum stable size.
Reinspection: repeat the dry-layout check before cutting or bonding any unit.

Inconsistent Overhang Along the Wall

Probable cause: overhang was checked at only one or two points, or cap units drifted from the string line during bonding.
Confirmation: measure overhang at regular intervals along the full wall length and compare each reading against the manufacturer's specified dimension.
Correction: lift and reset any unit outside the specified tolerance while the adhesive remains workable, or remove and re-bond a cured unit that is out of tolerance.
Reinspection: repeat the alignment check along the full wall length.

Cap Unit Rocks or Debonds After Cure

Probable cause: the wall top was not clean and dry when adhesive was applied, the adhesive was applied outside its stated temperature range, or the unit was disturbed before the cure time elapsed.
Confirmation: inspect the debonded surface for dust, moisture, or incomplete adhesive transfer, and review the recorded application temperature and cure protection log.
Correction: remove the unit, clean both bonding surfaces, confirm conditions are within the adhesive manufacturer's stated range, and re-bond using a fresh adhesive application.
Reinspection: repeat the adhesion check after the full cure time has elapsed again.

Adhesive Visible on the Finished Face or in a Drainage Path

Probable cause: the bead pattern was applied too close to the wall face edge, or excess adhesive was not cleaned before it cured.
Confirmation: inspect the visible wall face and any drainage opening beneath the cap for adhesive residue or blockage.
Correction: remove cured adhesive using the method specified by the manufacturer without damaging the finished surface, and confirm the drainage path is clear.
Reinspection: repeat the adhesive-application check and, if drainage was affected, the Chapter 13 drainage continuity check.

Cap Cracked or Chipped at a Cut Edge

Probable cause: the unit was fed into the saw with excessive force, cut without adequate support, or seated with the mallet without dressing a sharp or thin cut edge first.
Confirmation: inspect the damaged unit against the Chapter 9 cutting procedure and the cap manufacturer's minimum stable cut-piece size.
Correction: replace the damaged unit with a new cut piece produced under full Chapter 9 controls, and reset it in the confirmed dry-layout position.
Reinspection: repeat the cutting check and the alignment check for the replaced unit.

Wall Opened to Use Before Cure Time Elapsed

Probable cause: the barricade or restriction was removed, or the wall was used for seating or material staging, before the adhesive manufacturer's stated cure time was complete.
Confirmation: compare the recorded cure start time and the time the wall was accessed against the manufacturer's stated cure duration.
Correction: re-inspect every cap unit for movement or debonding, correct any affected unit, and restart the cure protection period for the full stated duration.
Reinspection: repeat the full final inspection in Section I before opening the wall to use again.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a cured, inspected, documented wall cap, not a visually finished surface that has not been checked for adhesion or alignment.

  • Remove barricades or restrictions only after the full stated cure time has elapsed and the final inspection has passed
  • Collect and dispose of cutting slurry and offcuts per Chapter 9 requirements
  • Store unused cap units and adhesive per the manufacturer's storage instructions
  • File the cap work order, cutting record, and inspection results with the Chapter 12 and Chapter 13 acceptance records for the same wall
  • Retain the complete cap record for reference in Chapters 16 through 20, where the wall may tie into a patio border, drainage system, or capstone project

Bead Pattern
The adhesive application pattern specified by the adhesive manufacturer for the identified materials.

The time period during which a bonded cap must be protected from movement, traffic, and weather.

Seat Wall
A wall cap designed and finished to serve as a usable seating surface.
Relief Cut
A cut made only where the finished cap geometry permits it, used to ease fitting around a curve or corner.
