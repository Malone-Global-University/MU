Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install a continuous drainage system behind a segmental retaining wall, using the leveling pad and first course accepted in Chapter 12. The finished work product is one completed retaining-wall drainage installation covering drainage aggregate, perforated drain pipe, filter fabric where specified, a protected discharge outlet, cleanouts where specified, and backfill placed in compacted lifts without displacing the pipe, fabric, geogrid, or wall units.

You will confirm the outlet location and lawful discharge, prepare the drainage zone behind the wall, place drainage aggregate and perforated pipe at the specified elevation and orientation, connect solid outlet piping, protect the outlet, install cleanouts where specified, extend drainage aggregate upward as wall courses rise, place and compact retained-soil backfill without displacing buried components, verify continuous outlet flow with no reverse slope, and document the concealed system with photographs before it is buried under later courses.

This chapter depends directly on Chapter 12: the leveling pad and first course must already be accepted before drainage work begins. It also feeds every remaining wall chapter: Chapter 14 caps the wall only after the drainage zone is complete and backfilled to the top of the accepted courses, and any wall inspected in Chapter 20's capstone drainage review must trace back to a drainage installation documented with this exact procedure.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HC-073 — Install drainage aggregate behind a retaining wall at the specified gradation, layer thickness, and extent.
  • HC-074 — Place perforated drain pipe at the specified elevation, orientation, and perforation direction.
  • HC-075 — Wrap or place filter fabric at the soil-to-drainage-aggregate interface where the design specifies it.
  • HC-076 — Maintain a continuous outlet path with no reverse slope, from the pipe to a lawful discharge point.
  • HC-077 — Protect the wall backfill zone by placing and compacting retained-soil backfill without displacing pipe, fabric, geogrid, or wall units.
  • HC-078 — Inspect drainage continuity, including pipe, aggregate, fabric, outlet, and overflow route, and document the concealed system.

Where This Skill Fits

A segmental retaining wall fails from water pressure behind it far more often than from the visible face. Drainage aggregate and perforated pipe relieve hydrostatic pressure that would otherwise push against the wall units and their connections. A wall built without a continuous, protected drain path is not a lesser version of the wall built in this chapter — it is a different, unapproved system, regardless of how well the visible courses are set.

This chapter's discipline — verifying outlet legality before covering anything, keeping fines and native soil out of the drainage zone, and photographing every concealed component — is the same discipline the Chapter 20 capstone inspection expects when it reviews drainage continuity on a completed wall. Learn it here, on one wall's drainage zone, before it becomes one buried system among many chapters you can no longer see.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the wall design, drainage aggregate, pipe type, invert, outlet, cleanout, fabric, backfill, and overflow path before drainage work begins
  • confirming the leveling pad and first course from Chapter 12 are accepted
  • establishing the drain-pipe outlet location and confirming lawful discharge
  • preparing the trench or drainage zone behind the wall and placing filter fabric where specified
  • placing drainage aggregate and perforated drain pipe at the specified elevation and orientation
  • connecting solid outlet piping, protecting the outlet, and installing cleanouts where specified
  • extending drainage aggregate as wall courses rise and placing retained-soil backfill in controlled, compacted lifts
  • verifying continuous outlet flow and inspecting and documenting the completed drainage system

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin drainage work on a leveling pad or first course that has not been accepted under Chapter 12
  • select an outlet location or discharge point without confirming ownership, permission, and discharge legality
  • wrap or bury perforated pipe or drainage aggregate in a configuration that blocks flow or departs from the approved design
  • compact backfill using equipment or a method that displaces the pipe, fabric, geogrid, or wall units
  • continue wall construction past this chapter's drainage zone without photographing the concealed pipe, aggregate, fabric, and outlet

Any drainage condition that fails inspection in this chapter must be corrected and reinspected before Chapter 14 wall capping begins.

Trade Note

Drainage aggregate and pipe are a system, not two separate materials placed near each other. Fines, native soil, mortar, or debris that enter the drainage zone clog the aggregate's pore space and defeat the system even if the pipe itself is correctly placed. Treat contamination control as part of installing the pipe, not a separate cleanup task.

Setup Standard

Do not place drainage aggregate, pipe, or fabric behind a leveling pad or first course that has not been accepted under Chapter 12. A drainage zone built against an unaccepted base carries that base's deficiencies forward into a system you will not be able to inspect again once it is backfilled.

Shop Tip

Photograph the pipe, aggregate, and fabric at each stage, not only at final acceptance. A single closing photograph cannot show invert elevation, perforation orientation, or fabric lap that a mid-installation photograph documents clearly.

Common Field Fundamentals

A retaining-wall drainage system relies on four things working together: aggregate with enough pore space to move water, a pipe that carries collected water to a lawful outlet without a reverse slope, separation from fine soil so the pore space does not clog, and backfill compaction that does not disturb any of the three. Losing any one of the four defeats the system even if the other three are correctly installed.

Drainage aggregateFree-draining aggregate placed around and above the pipe, kept free of fines
Perforated pipeSet at the specified invert and orientation, connected to solid outlet piping
Filter fabricSeparation at the soil interface where the design specifies it, without blocking flow
Protected backfillRetained-soil backfill compacted in lifts without displacing buried components

Stop-Work Conditions

This chapter requires the installer to halt drainage work, rather than continue toward backfill, whenever a condition cannot be resolved with the documents and materials on hand. These conditions must be referred to the required qualified professional or corrected before drainage work continues.

Critical Safety Cue — Unconfirmed Outlet Legality

Do not connect or open a drain-pipe outlet to a discharge point whose ownership, permission, or discharge legality has not been confirmed. An unauthorized discharge can create a drainage dispute, a code violation, or a hazard on adjacent property. Confirm the outlet in writing before pipe is connected.

Code Alert — Reverse Slope or Broken Fall

Do not backfill over any section of pipe or outlet path showing a reverse slope or an interrupted fall toward the outlet. Verify continuous fall with a level before covering any section, and correct the invert before proceeding.

Field Safety Cue — Confined Backfill Compaction

Do not operate a full-size plate compactor directly against the wall face or over unburied pipe, fabric, or geogrid. Use equipment and a compaction distance appropriate to the wall design, and hand-compact the zone immediately adjacent to the wall where the design requires it.

Measurement Cue — Record Invert Relative to a Fixed Reference

Record the pipe invert elevation at its starting point, at each cleanout, and at the outlet, relative to the same fixed reference used for the wall's leveling pad. Invert elevations recorded without a stated fixed reference cannot be verified for continuous fall.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Pipe and Fabric Compatibility

Install perforated pipe with perforations oriented as the manufacturer specifies, and install filter fabric with the overlap, fastening, and compatible-aggregate requirements stated in the product instructions. Do not assume a generic orientation or overlap is acceptable without checking the stated requirement.

E. Preparation — Confirm Base, Design, and Outlet

  1. Review wall design, drainage aggregate, pipe type, invert, outlet, cleanout, fabric, backfill, and overflow path. Record each specified value on the drainage installation record before staging materials.
  2. Confirm the leveling pad and first course are accepted. Verify the Chapter 12 acceptance record is complete and signed before placing any drainage material.
  3. Establish drain-pipe outlet location and lawful discharge. Confirm ownership, permission, and discharge legality in writing, and record the confirmation on the drainage installation record.
  4. Verify continuous fall where required. Confirm the design's stated invert values will produce continuous fall from the pipe's starting point to the outlet before excavating the drainage zone.

F. Zone Preparation and Fabric

  1. Prepare the trench or drainage zone behind the wall. Excavate or shape the zone to the dimensions specified in the wall design, maintaining separation from the wall units and any geogrid already placed.
  2. Install filter fabric at the soil-to-drainage-aggregate interface where specified. Place fabric with the overlap and fastening specified by the manufacturer, and extend it to cover the full soil-facing side of the drainage zone.
  3. Do not wrap pipe or aggregate in a way that blocks flow or violates the design. Confirm the fabric configuration matches the design detail before placing aggregate.

G. Pipe and Aggregate Placement

  1. Place the first drainage-aggregate layer. Spread aggregate to the thickness specified in the wall design across the full width of the drainage zone.
  2. Position perforated drain pipe at the specified elevation and orientation. Set the pipe on the first aggregate layer at the recorded invert elevation, with perforations oriented as the manufacturer specifies.
  3. Connect solid outlet pipe where required. Join solid pipe to the perforated run using the fittings specified in the wall design, maintaining the recorded invert through the connection.
  4. Maintain outlet protection against erosion, pests, freezing, blockage, and damage. Install the specified outlet protection, such as a rodent guard or splash pad, at the discharge point.
  5. Install cleanouts or inspection points where specified. Set cleanouts at the locations recorded on the drainage installation record, accessible without excavating the full pipe run.

H. Aggregate Extension and Backfill

  1. Place drainage aggregate around and above the pipe. Cover the pipe fully with aggregate before placing retained-soil backfill in the same zone.
  2. Keep fines, native soil, mortar, and debris out of the drainage zone. Inspect the aggregate surface before each subsequent lift and remove any contamination found.
  3. Extend drainage aggregate upward as wall courses rise. Coordinate aggregate placement with each new course installed under Chapter 12's continuing sequence, maintaining the zone width specified in the wall design.
  4. Fold or terminate fabric according to the design. Complete the fabric detail at the top of the drainage zone or at each lift as specified.
  5. Place retained-soil backfill in controlled lifts. Place backfill in the lift thickness specified for the retained-soil zone, working away from the drainage aggregate.
  6. Compact without displacing pipe, fabric, geogrid, or wall units. Use the equipment and compaction distance specified for backfill near the wall, and inspect each lift for displacement before placing the next.

I. Flow Verification and Inspection

  1. Verify continuous outlet and no reverse slope. Check the pipe invert at the start, each cleanout, and the outlet with a level, confirming unbroken fall before any section is covered by the next lift.
  2. Perform a limited flow observation where approved. Introduce a controlled volume of water at the system's high end where the wall design permits this test, and confirm it reaches the outlet without ponding or backflow.
  3. Inspect pipe, aggregate, fabric, outlet, and overflow route. Confirm every component matches the drainage installation record before the zone is fully backfilled.
  4. Photograph concealed drainage. Photograph the pipe, aggregate, fabric, and outlet at each stage before it is covered, and file the photographs with the drainage installation record.

Common Site Inspection

Review every drainage installation using the same checks regardless of wall length or configuration.

  • Base check: confirm the Chapter 12 leveling pad and first course were accepted before any drainage material was placed.
  • Outlet check: confirm outlet ownership, permission, and discharge legality were confirmed in writing before the outlet was connected.
  • Invert and fall check: confirm invert elevations at the start, each cleanout, and the outlet are recorded relative to a fixed reference and show continuous fall with no reverse slope.
  • Contamination check: confirm the drainage aggregate is free of fines, native soil, mortar, and debris at every stage represented in the photographic record.
  • Backfill check: confirm each backfill lift was inspected for pipe, fabric, geogrid, or wall-unit displacement before the next lift was placed.
  • Photographic-record check: confirm pipe, aggregate, fabric, and outlet were photographed before each was covered.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Drainage Backfilled Over an Unconfirmed Outlet

Probable cause: pipe was connected and covered before written confirmation of outlet ownership, permission, and discharge legality was obtained.
Confirmation: review the drainage installation record for a signed outlet confirmation; if none is recorded, the outlet has not been verified.
Correction: excavate to expose the outlet connection, obtain written confirmation or relocate the outlet to a confirmed lawful discharge point, and reinstall outlet protection.
Reinspection: repeat the outlet check and the photographic-record check for the affected section.

Reverse Slope Discovered After Backfill

Probable cause: pipe invert was set by eye rather than checked with a level at the start, each cleanout, and the outlet before backfill covered the run.
Confirmation: excavate to expose the suspect section and check invert elevation with a level against the fixed reference recorded on the drainage installation record.
Correction: remove backfill and aggregate down to the pipe, reset the pipe to the correct invert, and reconfirm continuous fall before recovering the section.
Reinspection: repeat the invert and fall check for the full pipe run.

Fines Found in the Drainage Aggregate

Probable cause: native soil, mortar debris, or fine material entered the drainage zone during backfill placement or aggregate delivery, and was not removed before covering.
Confirmation: probe or excavate the drainage aggregate at the suspect location and inspect for visible fine-soil contamination.
Correction: remove the contaminated aggregate, restore filter fabric if it was compromised, and replace with clean, approved drainage aggregate.
Reinspection: repeat the contamination check for the full drainage zone.

Pipe or Fabric Displaced During Backfill Compaction

Probable cause: backfill was compacted using equipment or a compaction distance not appropriate for the confined zone near the wall, or lifts were not inspected before the next lift was placed.
Confirmation: excavate the affected lift and inspect pipe position, fabric position, and wall-unit alignment against the drainage installation record.
Correction: reset the displaced component to its specified position, and compact the corrected lift using the equipment and method specified for the confined zone.
Reinspection: repeat the backfill check for the affected lift and the lift above it.

No Photographic Record Before Covering

Probable cause: pipe, aggregate, fabric, or the outlet was covered by the next lift or course before it was photographed.
Confirmation: review the photographic record against the drainage record schedule for any stage missing documentation.
Correction: where the component remains accessible, excavate and photograph it; where it cannot be safely re-exposed, document the omission and note it on the drainage installation record for future reference.
Reinspection: repeat the photographic-record check for the full installation.

Cleanout Not Accessible After Backfill

Probable cause: the cleanout location was covered by backfill or aggregate without preserving surface access.
Confirmation: attempt to locate and access each cleanout recorded on the drainage installation record.
Correction: excavate to the cleanout and restore surface access according to the wall design's cleanout detail.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage record schedule check for cleanout locations.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a backfilled, documented drainage system and organized site records, not an assumption that the pipe was installed correctly because the trench is closed.

  • Remove excess aggregate, fabric offcuts, and pipe scrap from the work area
  • Store the drainage installation record, outlet confirmation, and photographic record with the Chapter 12 acceptance record
  • File site photographs alongside their corresponding drainage record schedule entries
  • Confirm the drainage zone surface is protected from rain and traffic until Chapter 14 wall capping begins
  • Retain the complete drainage record for reference throughout the remaining wall chapters and the Chapter 20 capstone

Field Terms

Invert
The lowest interior point of the pipe at a given cross-section, used as the controlling elevation reference for pipe slope.
Continuous Fall
An unbroken downward slope from the pipe's starting point to its outlet, with no low point capable of trapping water or sediment.
Reverse Slope
A section of pipe or outlet path that rises instead of continuing to fall toward the outlet, defeating gravity flow.
Drainage Zone
The area behind the wall reserved for drainage aggregate and pipe, kept free of fine-soil contamination.
Filter Fabric
Separation fabric placed at the soil-to-drainage-aggregate interface to prevent fine soil migration into the drainage zone without blocking water flow.
Cleanout
An accessible point in the drain-pipe run permitting inspection or maintenance without excavating the full run.
Outlet Protection
A device such as a rodent guard or splash pad installed at the discharge point to prevent erosion, pest entry, freezing, or blockage.
Overflow Route
The path water follows if the primary drainage system's capacity is exceeded, separate from the pipe's normal discharge path.
Fixed Reference
The single permanent point from which every invert elevation and drainage measurement in this chapter is recorded.
Drainage Installation Record
The written record of every finding, elevation, and confirmation required to document the completed drainage system.
