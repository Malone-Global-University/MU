Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install a complete paver field over an accepted base and bedding layer, using a controlled pattern, bond lines, and perimeter cuts, and to inspect the finished surface before it is compacted and jointed. The finished work product is one paver patio field, installed within a defined practice area, with the field laid to a consistent bond, blended for color variation, cut to the planned perimeter, and inspected for plane, pattern, joint consistency, and drainage before Chapter 9 cutting is finalized and Chapter 10 compaction and joint-sand work begins.

You will establish perpendicular control lines through the field, dry-lay a sample of the pattern, confirm module and joint width against the border, place field units without disturbing the bedding, maintain the bond pattern against control strings, blend pavers from multiple pallets, preserve openings for drains and fixed objects, mark and prepare the perimeter for cutting, install a border where specified, install edge restraint, and inspect the completed field before compaction.

This chapter depends directly on Chapter 6, which produced the accepted, undisturbed bedding layer this chapter's pattern is built on, and on Chapter 3's layout, which set the pattern-control lines and border relationship this chapter follows. This chapter in turn feeds Chapter 9's cutting procedure, Chapter 10's edge restraint and compaction sequence, and the Chapter 20 capstone, which repeats this exact field-laying sequence at full scale.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HC-043 — Establish a paver pattern, including perpendicular control lines, module confirmation, and joint-width verification against the approved layout.
  • HC-044 — Lay the paver field, placing units vertically onto the bedding without disturbing it and without sliding units across the surface.
  • HC-045 — Maintain bond lines, checking pattern position against control strings at a regular interval and correcting drift gradually.
  • HC-046 — Control joint spacing, using spacers or built-in lugs consistently across the field and around blended units.
  • HC-047 — Cut perimeter pavers, marking and preparing border, curve, and obstacle cuts for installation using Chapter 9 methods.
  • HC-048 — Inspect the patio surface, checking plane, pattern, bond lines, joint consistency, slope, cut quality, and drainage before compaction.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 7 taught paver installation on a straight, single-direction walkway. This chapter extends that skill into a two-dimensional field, where bond-line drift, color blending, and perimeter geometry are harder to control because errors can compound in more than one direction at once. The pattern-control lines established here, and the discipline of checking against them frequently rather than only at the end, are the same discipline the Chapter 16 border installation and the Chapter 20 capstone depend on.

A patio field laid without frequent control-line checks can appear acceptable at close range while running visibly out of square by the far edge. Correcting that drift after the field is fully laid means lifting and relaying units. Learning to check early and correct in small increments is the core skill this chapter builds.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved patio pattern, border, focal lines, feature locations, drains, slope, and cut plan
  • establishing perpendicular control lines through the field and dry-laying a sample pattern
  • confirming module, joint width, and border relationship before placing units permanently
  • placing field units vertically onto accepted, undisturbed bedding in the selected bond pattern
  • blending units from multiple pallets, checking bond lines against control strings, and correcting drift gradually
  • maintaining openings for drains, sleeves, posts, and fixed objects
  • marking and preparing perimeter cuts, installing a border where specified, and installing edge restraint
  • inspecting the completed field for plane, pattern, joints, cut quality, slope, and drainage before compaction

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin field installation on bedding that has not been accepted under Chapter 6, or on bedding that shows footprints, ridges, hollows, or other disturbance
  • compact the field, install joint sand, or activate polymeric sand before Chapter 10's edge-restraint and compaction sequence
  • cut pavers using a saw or splitter without following the silica and equipment controls in Chapter 9
  • finalize a pattern, bond, or border relationship that differs from the approved layout without documenting the controlling reason

Any field section that fails inspection in this chapter must be corrected before Chapter 9 perimeter cutting is finalized and before Chapter 10 compaction begins.

Trade Note

Bedding sand is a final setting layer, not a correction layer. If a unit sits high or low once placed, lift it and adjust the bedding beneath it rather than tapping it into place with extra force; excessive mallet force disturbs adjacent bedding and creates a hidden low spot that will surface after compaction.

Setup Standard

Do not begin placing field pavers until the bedding layer is confirmed accepted, dry, clean, and undisturbed under Chapter 6, and until the pattern-control lines from Chapter 3 are verified still in place.

Shop Tip

Dry-lay a sample section of the pattern before placing a single unit permanently. A dry-laid sample reveals module mismatches and joint-width problems while they cost nothing to correct.

Reference Conventions

Establish these references before any field installation begins, and record them on the patio field record.

TermDefinition
Control lineA perpendicular reference string set through the field, independent of any irregular wall, fence, or slab, used to check bond-line position throughout installation
ModuleThe combined dimension of one paver plus one joint width, used to verify that the pattern divides evenly across the field or border
Bond lineThe visible line formed by paver joints in the selected pattern, checked against the control line to detect drift
DriftThe gradual, cumulative misalignment of a bond line away from the control line, corrected in small increments rather than in one large adjustment
Cut zoneThe planned perimeter band where field units will be replaced by cut pieces to meet the border, curve, or obstacle line
BlendThe practice of drawing pavers from more than one pallet at a time to distribute natural color and texture variation evenly across the field

Common Field Fundamentals

Every paver field in this book, from the Chapter 7 walkway to the Chapter 20 capstone, is built from the same sequence: establish control lines independent of irregular boundaries, confirm module and joint width with a dry-laid sample, place units without disturbing the bedding, check bond lines frequently, blend for color, preserve fixed-feature openings, and cut the perimeter only after enough field area is established to confirm the true cut line.

Stop-Work Conditions

This chapter requires the installer to halt field installation, rather than continue placing units, whenever a condition compromises the bedding, the pattern control, or the finished surface.

Critical Safety Cue — Disturbed or Contaminated Bedding

Do not lay pavers over bedding sand that shows footprints, wheel ruts, ridges, hollows, standing water, or visible contamination. Stop, rescreed the affected section under Chapter 6 methods, and confirm reacceptance before resuming.

Field Safety Cue — Kneeling and Repetitive Placement

Use knee protection and change position regularly during extended field installation. Distribute weight using knee boards or plywood panels on freshly laid, uncompacted pavers rather than kneeling directly on units that have not yet been checked for full bedding contact.

Measurement Cue — Check Bond Lines at a Regular Interval

Check bond-line position against the control strings at a regular interval as the field progresses, not only when the field is complete. Correct drift by distributing small joint-width adjustments across several courses rather than making one large correction at the perimeter.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Pallet Blending

Where the paver manufacturer specifies a blending method, such as drawing from three or more pallets simultaneously, follow that method exactly. Do not install one pallet at a time when the manufacturer requires blending to control visible color variation.

Code Alert — Openings for Drains, Posts, and Sleeves

Confirm the required clearance and finished elevation for every drain, post, or sleeve opening against the governing plan before laying pavers around it. Do not cover, narrow, or block an opening that a later chapter's drainage or structural work depends on.

E. Preparation — Confirm References and Pattern

  1. Review patio pattern, border, focal lines, furniture or feature locations, drains, slope, and cut plan. Confirm the approved layout against the Patio Field Record before installation begins.
  2. Confirm accepted base and bedding. Verify the Chapter 6 bedding acceptance record and inspect the bedding surface for disturbance immediately before placing the first unit.
  3. Establish perpendicular control lines through the field. Reverify the Chapter 3 control-line locations and reset stringlines and pins as needed.
  4. Dry-lay a sample pattern. Place a representative section of field units, without bedding contact where practical, to confirm the pattern's visual and dimensional result.
  5. Confirm module, joint width, and border relationship. Measure the dry-laid sample against the approved module and border detail, and correct the layout before permanent placement if the module does not divide evenly.

F. Field Installation

  1. Begin at the approved corner, centerline, or fixed feature. Confirm the starting reference against the control lines before placing the first permanent unit.
  2. Place units vertically onto the bedding. Lower each paver straight down onto the bedding surface without sliding it, to avoid displacing sand ahead of the joint.
  3. Maintain the selected bond or repeating pattern. Follow the confirmed pattern continuously across the field, using spacers or built-in lugs for consistent joint width.
  4. Blend units from multiple pallets where color variation requires it. Follow the manufacturer's specified blending method exactly.
  5. Check bond lines against control strings frequently. Stop and measure at the interval specified in the Measurement Cue above.
  6. Correct drift gradually. Distribute small joint-width adjustments across several courses rather than forcing a single large correction.
  7. Keep bedding clean and undisturbed. Use knee boards or plywood panels on freshly laid sections, and avoid stepping directly on uncompacted units where the bedding beneath them has not been confirmed.
  8. Maintain openings for drains, sleeves, posts, and fixed objects. Verify each opening's clearance and elevation against the governing plan as the field reaches it.
  9. Install field area to within the planned cut zone. Continue full-unit placement until reaching the perimeter band identified in the cut plan.

G. Perimeter, Border, and Edge Preparation

  1. Mark perimeter cuts from the final edge or border line. Transfer the true cut line onto the last full field course using chalk or a paint marker.
  2. Cut and fit perimeter units using Chapter 9. Follow the Chapter 9 cutting procedure and silica controls for every marked piece.
  3. Avoid small unstable slivers below the approved minimum. Adjust the field layout or joint spacing to eliminate any cut piece that would fall below the minimum stable size.
  4. Install border or edge course where specified. Place border or accent-band units to the confirmed joint spacing and alignment.
  5. Install edge restraint. Follow the Chapter 10 edge-restraint procedure at the full field perimeter.

H. Inspection and Handoff

  1. Inspect surface plane, pattern, bond lines, joint consistency, slope, cut quality, and drainage. Use a straightedge and level across representative sections and check every finding against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Correct rocking, chipped, high, low, or misaligned units before compaction. Lift and reset any unit that fails inspection, correcting the bedding beneath it rather than forcing the unit into place.
  3. Document patio field acceptance. Complete the Field Inspection Record and confirm the field is ready for Chapter 10 compaction and joint-sand installation.

Common Site Inspection

Review every completed patio field using the same checks regardless of pattern or border selection.

  • Bedding check: confirm no section of the field was placed over disturbed, contaminated, or unaccepted bedding.
  • Pattern check: confirm the installed bond matches the approved pattern and that the dry-laid module confirmation was completed before permanent placement.
  • Bond-line check: confirm bond lines were checked against control strings at a regular interval and remain within tolerance across the full field.
  • Fixed-feature check: confirm every drain, post, and sleeve opening matches the governing plan's clearance and elevation.
  • Perimeter and border check: confirm every cut piece meets the minimum stable size and the border, where specified, is integrated without trapped or unsupported edges.
  • Surface check: confirm the field is free of rocking, high, or low units and the specified drainage slope is maintained before compaction.

Complete the Field Installation Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 9 perimeter cutting and Chapter 10 compaction.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Bond Line Drifts Out of Square Across the Field

Probable cause: bond-line position was not checked against the control strings at a regular interval, allowing small joint-width variations to accumulate uncorrected.
Confirmation: measure the installed bond line against the control string at several points across the field and compare the deviation to the approved tolerance.
Correction: lift and reset units back to the last section that measured within tolerance, then continue installation while checking the bond line at a closer interval.
Reinspection: repeat the bond-line check across the corrected section and the adjoining field.

Visible Color Blotching Across the Field

Probable cause: pavers were installed one pallet at a time instead of following the manufacturer's required blending method.
Confirmation: compare the installed field against the manufacturer's blending instructions and identify the boundary where the single-pallet sections begin.
Correction: lift affected units and reinstall using the specified multi-pallet blending method, redistributing units across the visible transition.
Reinspection: repeat the pattern check across the corrected area under normal daylight conditions.

Rocking Unit Discovered During Inspection

Probable cause: a low spot, foreign object, or inconsistent bedding depth beneath the unit was not detected before the paver was seated.
Confirmation: lift the unit and inspect the exposed bedding for a depression, debris, or an inconsistent surface.
Correction: correct the bedding depth using Chapter 6 methods, reseat the unit vertically, and confirm full contact before continuing.
Reinspection: repeat the surface-plane check across the corrected unit and its immediate neighbors.

Cut Piece Falls Below the Minimum Stable Size

Probable cause: the field layout was not adjusted before reaching the perimeter, leaving a narrow sliver at the cut zone.
Confirmation: measure the marked cut piece against the approved minimum stable size before cutting.
Correction: shift the field layout, adjust joint spacing across the last several courses, or introduce a border course to eliminate the undersized piece.
Reinspection: repeat the perimeter-cut check across the revised layout before cutting proceeds under Chapter 9.

Drain or Post Opening Covered or Undersized

Probable cause: the field pattern was carried across the fixed-feature location without confirming its required clearance against the governing plan.
Confirmation: compare the installed opening's dimensions and elevation to the governing drainage or structural plan.
Correction: remove and reset the units around the feature to restore the required clearance and elevation.
Reinspection: repeat the fixed-feature check for the corrected opening and confirm it against the governing plan.

Bedding Disturbed by Foot Traffic During Installation

Probable cause: workers stepped directly on freshly laid, uncompacted pavers or on unlaid bedding without using knee boards or plywood panels.
Confirmation: inspect the affected units for rocking or the adjacent bedding for footprints or displacement.
Correction: lift the affected units, rescreed the disturbed bedding under Chapter 6 methods, and reinstall.
Reinspection: repeat the surface-plane check for the affected section before continuing the field.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a completed, inspected paver field and organized site records, not an assumption that the field is ready for compaction.

  • Remove offcuts, packaging, and pallet strapping from the completed field area
  • Store unused field, border, and cut units by lot for use in Chapter 9 and Chapter 16
  • Protect the completed, uncompacted field from rain, irrigation, and unnecessary foot traffic until Chapter 10 begins
  • File the Field Installation Record and any inspection photographs with the project's readiness plan
  • Retain the confirmed control-line and module records for reference during Chapter 9 and Chapter 10

Field Terms

Control Line
A perpendicular reference string set through the field, used to check bond-line position throughout installation.
Module
The combined dimension of one paver plus one joint width, used to verify the pattern divides evenly across the field.
Bond Line
The visible line formed by paver joints in the selected pattern.
Drift
The gradual, cumulative misalignment of a bond line away from the control line.
Cut Zone
The planned perimeter band where field units are replaced by cut pieces to meet the border, curve, or obstacle line.
Blend
Drawing pavers from more than one pallet at a time to distribute color and texture variation evenly.
Knee Board
A plywood or rigid panel used to distribute worker weight across freshly laid, uncompacted pavers.
Minimum Stable Size
The smallest cut-piece dimension approved for installation without risk of cracking or displacement.

Performance Check

With the patio field installed and every record on the Field Installation Record completed, review the finished field against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm that the bedding was accepted and undisturbed before installation, the pattern and module were confirmed by dry-lay, bond lines remain within tolerance across the full field, joint spacing is consistent, fixed-feature openings match the governing plan, no cut piece falls below the minimum stable size, and the surface is free of rocking, high, or low units. If any item is incomplete or a deficiency remains unresolved, correct it and reinspect the affected section before proceeding to Chapter 9 perimeter cutting.
