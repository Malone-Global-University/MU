Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install and compact the aggregate base course that carries the paver surface above it. The finished work product is one compacted aggregate base, placed in approved lifts, over an accepted subgrade, at the specified compacted thickness, slope, elevation, and edge extension, with density or proof checks completed and documented.

You will confirm the subgrade acceptance and base specification, inspect delivered aggregate, place and compact the base in controlled lifts, verify thickness, elevation, and slope on a grid, correct deficient areas, protect the accepted base from traffic and weather, and document the completed installation.

This chapter depends directly on the accepted, compacted subgrade produced in Chapter 4. Every later paver, wall, path, and drainage chapter in this book depends on the base you install here: Chapter 6 screeds bedding sand directly from this base's accepted elevation, and no paver, cap, or drainage component installed later in this book may rest on a base that has not passed the acceptance standards in this chapter.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HC-025 — Select base aggregate compatible with the project specification, load, and drainage requirements.
  • HC-026 — Place gravel in controlled lifts not exceeding the specified maximum lift thickness.
  • HC-027 — Compact the base using equipment, pattern, and pass count appropriate to the specified aggregate.
  • HC-028 — Maintain the specified compacted base thickness across the project area.
  • HC-029 — Verify base slope against the approved drainage direction and value.
  • HC-030 — Inspect base stability, including density, proof, or deflection checks where specified, and document acceptance.

Where This Skill Fits

The aggregate base is the structural layer of a hardscape system. Bedding sand is a thin setting layer, not a substitute for a properly compacted base; a base that is thin, loosely compacted, out of slope, or contaminated with fines will settle, rut, or pond regardless of how carefully the pavers above it are installed. This chapter is where the base's thickness, compaction, and slope are locked in before any bedding sand is placed.

The same lift-thickness, compaction, and elevation-verification discipline taught here recurs in the gravel-path base of Chapter 11, the wall leveling pad of Chapter 12, and the base repair work of Chapter 18. Learning the base-installation sequence correctly here prevents the most common cause of hardscape failure addressed later in this book.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • confirming subgrade acceptance and the approved base-aggregate specification, including required compacted thickness and maximum lift thickness
  • inspecting delivered aggregate for gradation, contamination, segregation, and moisture
  • protecting the subgrade from rutting during delivery and placement
  • placing and moisture-conditioning aggregate in controlled lifts
  • compacting each lift using the specified equipment, pattern, and pass count
  • checking lift thickness, density, and elevation after each lift
  • correcting low, high, loose, pumping, or segregated areas
  • verifying final elevation, slope, and edge extension on a grid
  • performing density, proof, or deflection checks where specified
  • protecting the accepted base from traffic, rain, and contamination
  • documenting aggregate, lift count, moisture, passes, tests, and final readings

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • place base aggregate over a subgrade that has not passed the Chapter 4 excavation and subgrade acceptance standards
  • substitute a different aggregate gradation, thickness, or compaction target than the one stated in the approved base-aggregate specification
  • place bedding sand or pavers over a base that has not passed this chapter's thickness, elevation, slope, and stability checks
  • operate compaction equipment near foundations or fragile structures without observing the vibration limits and separation distances identified in this chapter

Any base condition that fails inspection in this chapter must be corrected and reinspected before Chapter 6 bedding-sand work begins.

Trade Note

Flat is not the same as level, and level is not the same as compacted. A base can be raked to a smooth, even-looking surface and still be loose, under-thick, or off-slope beneath that surface. Verify thickness, density, and slope by measurement at every lift, not by visual appearance alone.

Required PPE for this project: hearing protection and safety glasses while operating the compactor; steel- or composite-toe footwear; gloves when handling aggregate; high-visibility clothing where delivery vehicles or equipment are moving on site.

Setup Standard

Do not place aggregate on a subgrade that has not been accepted under Chapter 4. If the subgrade has been exposed to rain, traffic, or standing water since acceptance, reinspect it for softness before placing the first lift.

Shop Tip

Check lift thickness before compaction, not after. A lift that measures correctly loose but compacts thinner than the specified thickness signals either an undersized loose lift or an aggregate gradation that compacts more than expected; verify against the specification before continuing to the next lift.

Prerequisites

  • A subgrade that has passed the Chapter 4 excavation and subgrade acceptance standards, with the acceptance record available
  • An approved base-aggregate specification stating gradation, compacted thickness, maximum loose lift thickness, and edge extension
  • An approved drainage plan or layout stating the required base slope and drainage direction
  • A plate compactor or roller matched to the specified aggregate, inspected and in working order, with manufacturer instructions on hand
  • Delivered aggregate on site, inspected and accepted for gradation, contamination, segregation, and moisture before placement begins

Common Field Fundamentals

Every aggregate base in this book, from a straight walkway base to the Chapter 20 capstone patio base, is built using the same lift-and-compact sequence: confirm the subgrade and specification, place a controlled lift, compact it, verify thickness and elevation, and repeat until the total specified thickness is reached. Skipping a lift check or compacting an oversized loose lift is the most common cause of a base that looks finished but is not structurally sound.

Stop-Work Conditions

This chapter requires the installer to halt base placement, rather than continue to the next lift, whenever a condition falls outside what the approved specification and this chapter's checks can resolve.

Critical Safety Cue — Compaction Near Foundations and Fragile Structures

Do not operate a plate compactor or roller directly against a foundation, retaining structure, or fragile adjacent paving without observing the separation distance and vibration limit stated in the manufacturer's instructions or the project specification. Compact corner and confined areas using hand-operated equipment sized for that purpose instead.

Code Alert — Density and Proof Testing Requirements

Where the project specification, permit conditions, or adopted code require density testing, proof-rolling, or deflection testing, do not substitute a visual inspection for the required test. Confirm the required test method, frequency, and pass/fail standard before placing the final lift.

Field Safety Cue — Aggregate Handling and Compactor Operation

Keep hands and feet clear of the compactor's travel path and vibrating plate at all times. Use a wheelbarrow or cart on planked or protected routes across the subgrade to avoid rutting, and do not overload the cart beyond its rated capacity when moving aggregate.

Measurement Cue — Grid Elevation Checks

Check base elevation on a grid at intervals stated in the project specification, or at a maximum spacing sufficient to detect a low or high area within the finished paver footprint, and record every reading relative to the same fixed reference used in Chapters 3 and 4.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Compactor Pattern and Pass Count

Use the compactor pattern, direction, overlap, and pass count stated in the manufacturer's instructions for the specific aggregate and lift thickness being compacted. Do not assume a higher pass count always produces better compaction; follow the stated pattern instead.

E. Preparation — Confirm Specification and Inspect Aggregate

  1. Confirm subgrade approval and base-aggregate specification. Review the Chapter 4 acceptance record and the approved base-aggregate specification, and record the compacted thickness, maximum loose lift thickness, slope, and edge extension on the base installation record.
  2. Verify required compacted thickness and maximum lift thickness. Confirm the number of lifts needed to reach the total specified compacted thickness without exceeding the maximum loose lift thickness in any single lift.
  3. Inspect delivered aggregate for gradation, contamination, segregation, and moisture. Reject and document any delivered load that does not match the specified gradation or shows visible contamination or segregation.
  4. Protect subgrade from rutting during delivery. Route delivery vehicles, carts, and wheelbarrows on planked or protected paths across the subgrade, and stage aggregate away from the excavation edge.

F. First Lift — Placement and Compaction

  1. Place aggregate in controlled quantities. Dump and spread aggregate in measured quantities across the project area rather than in a single uncontrolled pile.
  2. Spread the first lift evenly beyond the paver edge as specified. Rake the first lift to the specified loose thickness, extending to the required edge-extension distance beyond the finished paver edge.
  3. Maintain the approved slope while spreading. Use string lines or a laser level referenced to the fixed reference point to guide the rake or screed to the approved slope.
  4. Moisture-condition aggregate where required. Add or allow moisture only as stated in the specification or manufacturer instructions, and record the moisture condition before compaction.
  5. Compact using the specified plate compactor, roller, or rammer. Operate the equipment stated in the specification, sized to the aggregate and lift thickness.
  6. Overlap passes and follow the approved pattern. Overlap each compactor pass according to the manufacturer's stated overlap distance and direction.
  7. Keep compaction equipment clear of foundations and fragile structures where vibration limits apply. Use hand-operated equipment in place of the plate compactor within the stated separation distance.
  8. Check lift thickness, density, and elevation. Measure the compacted lift thickness at spot-check locations and record the result on the lift record before placing the next lift.

G. Subsequent Lifts and Final Elevation

  1. Add subsequent lifts. Repeat placement and compaction in additional lifts, each not exceeding the maximum loose lift thickness, until the total specified compacted thickness is reached.
  2. Do not place overly thick loose layers. Split any lift that would exceed the maximum loose lift thickness into two or more compacted lifts.
  3. Fill low spots with approved aggregate. Add specified aggregate to any area found low on the elevation grid, and recompact before continuing.
  4. Trim high spots rather than covering them with bedding sand. Remove excess compacted material from any high area rather than allowing bedding sand in Chapter 6 to compensate for it.
  5. Compact edge, corner, drain, and utility areas. Use equipment appropriate for confined areas to achieve the same specified compaction at edges, corners, drain openings, and utility penetrations.
  6. Check final elevation on a grid. Measure elevation at every grid point stated in the project specification, relative to the fixed reference used throughout the project.
  7. Verify final slope and transitions. Compare the measured slope against the approved drainage plan and confirm smooth transitions at every edge and interface.
  8. Perform density, proof, or deflection checks where specified. Conduct the required test using the stated method and record the result against the stated pass/fail standard.

H. Correction and Protection

  1. Correct loose, pumping, segregated, high, or low areas. Remove and replace or recompact any area failing the elevation, slope, or stability checks, and reinspect the corrected area before continuing.
  2. Protect the accepted base from traffic, rain, and contamination. Cover, barricade, or otherwise protect the accepted base until Chapter 6 bedding-sand work begins.
  3. Document aggregate, lift count, moisture, passes, tests, and final readings. Complete the base installation record with every lift's thickness and moisture, the final elevation grid, slope verification, and stability-check results.

Common Site Inspection

Review every base installation using the same checks regardless of project type.

  • Subgrade check: confirm the Chapter 4 acceptance record is on file and the subgrade was reinspected if exposed to rain or traffic since acceptance.
  • Aggregate check: confirm delivered aggregate matches the specified gradation and shows no contamination, segregation, or unacceptable moisture.
  • Lift check: confirm no loose lift exceeded the maximum loose lift thickness and every lift's compacted thickness was measured.
  • Elevation and slope check: confirm final elevation at every grid point and the measured slope match the approved drainage plan.
  • Stability check: confirm density, proof, or deflection checks were performed where specified and met the stated standard.
  • Protection check: confirm the accepted base is protected from traffic, rain, and contamination before Chapter 6 begins.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Base Compacts Thinner Than the Specified Thickness

Probable cause: the loose lift was placed too thin, or the aggregate gradation compacts more than the amount allowed for in the loose-lift calculation.
Confirmation: measure the loose thickness of the next lift before compaction and compare the compacted result against the specified thickness.
Correction: add an additional lift of the specified aggregate to reach the required total compacted thickness, and recompact.
Reinspection: repeat the lift-thickness check and the final elevation grid check.

Base Surface Pumps or Deflects Under Foot Traffic

Probable cause: the subgrade was soft, saturated, or contaminated with fines before or during base placement, or a lift was compacted at the wrong moisture content.
Confirmation: probe the pumping area to the subgrade and inspect for softness, contamination, or standing water.
Correction: remove the affected base and subgrade material to stable support, correct the underlying cause, and reinstall base aggregate in approved lifts.
Reinspection: repeat the subgrade check, the lift record, and the stability check for the corrected area.

Elevation Grid Shows a Low Area Within the Paver Footprint

Probable cause: aggregate was not spread evenly during placement, or a low area in the subgrade was not fully filled during the first lift.
Confirmation: re-measure the low area against the elevation grid and the fixed reference to confirm the deficiency and its extent.
Correction: add the specified aggregate to the low area in a controlled lift and recompact before rechecking the grid.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation-grid check for the corrected area and its surrounding points.

Measured Slope Does Not Match the Drainage Plan

Probable cause: string lines or the laser level were not referenced correctly to the fixed reference point, or aggregate was not spread consistently along the slope direction.
Confirmation: re-check the string-line or laser-level setup against the fixed reference and re-measure slope at multiple points across the base.
Correction: correct the reference setup, then trim high areas or add aggregate to low areas along the slope direction and recompact.
Reinspection: repeat the slope-verification check across the full base area.

Contaminated or Segregated Aggregate Discovered After Placement

Probable cause: delivered aggregate was not inspected before placement, or a load was mixed with fines, debris, or a different gradation during handling.
Confirmation: inspect the placed material for visible fines, debris, or gradation inconsistent with the specification.
Correction: remove the affected lift and replace it with aggregate matching the approved specification, then recompact.
Reinspection: repeat the aggregate-inspection check and the lift record for the corrected area.

Density, Proof, or Deflection Check Fails the Stated Standard

Probable cause: the base was under-compacted, placed too wet or too dry, or built on an unstable subgrade area that was not detected during Chapter 4.
Confirmation: review the lift record for moisture and pass-count deviations, and probe the subgrade beneath the failing area.
Correction: remove and rebuild the base in the failing area following the specified moisture, lift thickness, and pass count, correcting any subgrade deficiency found.
Reinspection: repeat the stability check for the corrected area before proceeding to Chapter 6.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a completed, documented base installation record and a protected, accepted base, not a visually finished surface alone.

  • Remove excess aggregate, spillage, and debris from adjacent surfaces, drains, and vegetation
  • Store the base installation record with the Chapter 4 subgrade acceptance record and the approved base-aggregate specification
  • File lift-record photographs and elevation-grid photographs alongside their corresponding worksheet entries
  • Cover or barricade the accepted base to prevent traffic, rain intrusion, or contamination before Chapter 6 begins
  • Retain the complete base installation record for reference throughout the remainder of this book, including the Chapter 20 capstone

Field Terms

Compacted Lift Thickness
The finished thickness of one aggregate layer after compaction, as stated in the approved specification.
Maximum Loose Lift Thickness
The maximum thickness of uncompacted aggregate that may be placed before compaction.
Total Compacted Base Thickness
The full specified thickness of the finished, compacted base measured from the accepted subgrade.
Edge Extension
The distance the base extends beyond the finished paver edge, as stated in the approved specification.
Base Slope
The finished slope of the top of the base, matching the approved drainage direction and value.
Proof-Rolling
A visual or instrumented check for pumping, rutting, or deflection under a moving load, performed only where specified.
Segregation
Separation of coarse and fine particles within an aggregate load, resulting in inconsistent gradation.
Grid Elevation Check
A series of elevation measurements taken at regular intervals across the project area relative to a fixed reference.
Fixed Reference
The single permanent point, established in Chapter 1, from which every elevation and slope measurement is recorded.
Density Test
An instrumented measurement of compacted soil or aggregate density compared against a stated pass/fail standard.

Performance Check

With the base placed, compacted, and every record on the Aggregate Base Installation Worksheet completed, review the finished base against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm that the subgrade acceptance is on file, delivered aggregate passed inspection, no lift exceeded the maximum loose lift thickness, total compacted thickness and elevation meet the specification at every grid point, slope matches the drainage plan, edge extension is maintained, required stability checks pass the stated standard, and the base is protected from traffic, rain, and contamination. If any item is incomplete or a deficiency remains unresolved, correct it and reinspect the affected area before beginning Chapter 6.
