Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to build one small segmental gravity retaining-wall practice section that remains within the approved nonengineered height, soil, surcharge, and setback limits stated by the wall design or the manufacturer's published height table. The finished work product is one wall section with an accepted leveling pad, a level and buried first course, correctly staggered and set-back courses, drainage aggregate placed behind the wall as it rises, controlled and compacted backfill, geogrid installed exactly where the design specifies it, and a documented final height and alignment.

You will confirm the wall qualifies for nonengineered construction, excavate and compact a leveling pad, set and level the first course, build successive courses with the required setback and bond, place drainage aggregate and backfill in controlled lifts, install geogrid where required, and inspect the completed wall before it is capped in Chapter 14.

This chapter depends directly on the site readiness and drainage findings from Chapters 1 and 4. Chapter 13 installs the wall's continuous drainage pipe and outlet system into the drainage aggregate you place here, and Chapter 14 caps the wall you build in this chapter. A retaining wall built without a level first course, correct setback, and controlled backfill cannot be corrected by drainage work or a cap installed afterward.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • HC-067 — Prepare the wall base, including excavation, subgrade compaction, and a compacted leveling-pad aggregate lift at the approved elevation and slope.
  • HC-068 — Set the first block course to line, level, and required buried depth at the controlling low point of the wall.
  • HC-069 — Maintain level across each course using a 4-foot level and correct high or low units before proceeding to the next course.
  • HC-070 — Stagger wall units so vertical joints do not align between adjacent courses, maintaining the manufacturer's required setback and bond pattern.
  • HC-071 — Backfill behind the wall with drainage aggregate and retained-soil backfill in compacted lifts, without displacing wall units, geogrid, or fabric.
  • HC-072 — Inspect wall alignment, including line, curve, level, setback, bond, drainage continuity, and stability, and document the results.

Where This Skill Fits

A segmental gravity retaining wall holds back soil using the weight, setback, and interlock of the units themselves rather than embedded reinforcement, except where geogrid is specifically required by the design. Every requirement in this chapter — a level first course buried to the specified depth, consistent setback between courses, staggered vertical joints, and controlled, compacted backfill — exists because a small error at the base of the wall is magnified by every course built on top of it, and because uncontrolled or uncompacted backfill places pressure the gravity system was not designed to resist.

This chapter also teaches the stop-work discipline that separates a legitimate small gravity wall from a wall that requires engineering: a wall supporting a structure, driveway, steep slope, fence, pool, or road, or a wall exceeding the manufacturer's nonengineered height limit, is not built under this chapter's procedure.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • confirming the wall height, retained height, slope, surcharge, soil, foundation, setback, and drainage conditions qualify for nonengineered construction
  • locating utilities and laying out the wall face, ends, curves, and excavation limits
  • excavating and compacting the leveling-pad subgrade and installing a compacted aggregate leveling pad
  • setting, leveling, and burying the first block course
  • installing successive courses with the manufacturer's required setback, bond, and staggered vertical joints
  • placing drainage aggregate behind the wall as it rises
  • compacting reinforced and retained backfill in approved lifts using equipment appropriate to distance from the wall
  • installing geogrid where the design specifies elevation and orientation
  • inspecting and documenting base, first course, bond, setback, drainage, backfill, geogrid, alignment, and stability

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • build a wall supporting a structure, driveway, steep slope, fence, pool, road, or other surcharge not identified and approved on the wall design
  • exceed the manufacturer's published nonengineered gravity-wall height limit for the block system in use
  • omit geogrid where the design or manufacturer height table requires it, or substitute fabric, mesh, or an unapproved reinforcement material
  • cap the wall or open it to backfill loading beyond the approved lift sequence before Chapter 13 drainage and Chapter 14 capping are complete

Any wall condition identified during this chapter as exceeding nonengineered limits must be referred to an engineer before construction proceeds.

Trade Note

The first course of a segmental gravity wall controls the accuracy of every course built above it. Time spent leveling and aligning the first course is never wasted; an error left uncorrected at the base is compounded, not diluted, by the courses that follow.

Required PPE for this project: gloves, closed-toe footwear, eye protection during cutting and compaction, hearing protection near the plate compactor, and back-safe lifting technique or a lifting aid for block units.

Setup Standard

Do not begin excavation until the required utility locate has responded and the wall has been confirmed to fall within the approved nonengineered scope. A wall supporting an unidentified surcharge must be stopped and referred before the leveling pad is excavated.

Shop Tip

Check the first course for level after every two or three units, not only at the end of the course. Correcting one unit is fast; correcting an entire course after the mason's line has already been moved is not.

Reference Conventions

Establish these references before construction begins, and record them on the wall construction record.

Prerequisites

  • A completed Chapter 1 hardscape-readiness plan and Chapter 4 excavation covering the wall location, or equivalent documented site and subgrade findings
  • A wall design or manufacturer nonengineered height table confirming the wall qualifies for construction under this chapter without an engineer
  • A submitted and confirmed utility-locating response for the full wall excavation area
  • Property owner or authorized-representative permission to excavate, place backfill, and operate a plate compactor on site
  • Identification of the contact for an engineer, in case the wall is found during layout or excavation to exceed nonengineered limits

Common Field Fundamentals

Every segmental gravity wall in this book depends on the same sequence: confirm nonengineered scope, prepare an accepted base, set and level the first course, build successive courses with correct setback and stagger, place drainage aggregate and controlled backfill as the wall rises, install geogrid exactly where specified, and inspect the finished wall as one system rather than as blocks stacked on soil.

Stop-Work Conditions

This chapter requires the builder to halt construction, rather than continue building, whenever a condition exceeds the approved nonengineered scope. These conditions must be referred to an engineer before wall construction resumes.

Critical Safety Cue — Walls Supporting Surcharge or Exceeding Height Limits

Do not build a wall under this chapter's procedure if it supports a structure, driveway, steep slope, fence, pool, road, or other surcharge, or if the design wall height or retained height exceeds the manufacturer's published nonengineered limit for the block system in use. Stop work and refer the wall to an engineer before excavation begins.

Critical Safety Cue — Compaction Near the Wall Face

Do not push heavy compaction equipment directly against the wall. Compact backfill within the distance specified by the manufacturer using a hand tamper or lightweight plate compactor, and use standard compaction equipment only beyond that distance, to avoid displacing or bulging wall units.

Code Alert — Setback and Permit Requirements

Confirm the wall's distance from property lines, easements, and structures against the site plan and adopted setback requirements before excavation, and confirm whether the jurisdiction requires a permit or inspection for the wall's design height. Do not assume a small wall is exempt from permit requirements without verification.

Measurement Cue — Level Every Course, Not Only the First

Check level front-to-back and side-to-side on every unit in the first course, and check level and setback again after every course is placed. A level check performed only at the first course does not confirm that stagger and setback were maintained correctly through the remaining courses.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Setback, Buried Depth, and Geogrid

Use the manufacturer's stated setback per course, minimum buried depth for the first course, and geogrid elevation, orientation, and embedment length exactly as published for the block system in use. Do not estimate these values from a similar-looking product.

E. Preparation — Confirm Scope and Locate Utilities

  1. Review wall height, retained height, slope, surcharge, soil, foundation, setback, curve, drainage, reinforcement, property-line, and permit limits. Record each value from the wall design or manufacturer height table on the Wall Scope Record.
  2. Confirm the wall qualifies for the approved nonengineered practice scope. Compare the recorded design height and retained height against the manufacturer's published nonengineered limit for the block system in use.
  3. Stop work for walls supporting structures, driveways, steep slopes, fences, pools, roads, or unknown surcharge without design. Refer any such condition to an engineer and record the referral before proceeding.
  4. Locate utilities. Submit or confirm the utility-locating request for the full wall and backfill excavation area.

F. Layout and Base Excavation

  1. Lay out wall face, ends, curves, and excavation limits. Mark the controlling wall face line, end conditions, and any curves using the layout methods from Chapter 3, and mark excavation limits that extend beyond the wall face to accommodate the leveling pad and drainage zone.
  2. Excavate for leveling pad and drainage zone. Excavate to the depth specified by the wall design, extending behind the wall face to the width of the specified drainage zone.
  3. Remove organic and unsuitable soil. Remove roots, organic material, debris, and any soft or previously disturbed soil found in the excavation.
  4. Compact the subgrade. Compact the exposed subgrade using the plate compactor before placing leveling-pad aggregate.

G. Leveling Pad and First Course

  1. Install approved base aggregate in compacted lifts. Place the leveling-pad aggregate specified by the wall design in lifts not exceeding the specified maximum lift thickness.
  2. Screed and compact the leveling pad. Screed the aggregate to the elevation and slope specified by the design, then compact with the plate compactor.
  3. Set the first block at the controlling low point. Place the first unit at the wall's lowest finished-grade point, so remaining units step up from a confirmed low reference.
  4. Level it front-to-back and side-to-side. Check with the 4-foot level in both directions and adjust the leveling-pad aggregate beneath the unit until level is achieved.
  5. Set additional first-course units tightly or at specified joints. Continue along the layout line, maintaining the manufacturer's specified joint condition between units.
  6. Check line, curve, and level after every few units. Use the mason's line and 4-foot level at regular intervals rather than only at the end of the course.
  7. Bury the required portion of the first course. Confirm the buried depth in front of the wall matches the manufacturer's specified minimum before backfilling in front of the units.
  8. Sweep unit tops clean. Remove aggregate, soil, and debris from the top of the first course before placing the next course.

H. Successive Courses, Drainage, and Backfill

  1. Install the next course with required setback and bond. Set each unit back from the course below by the manufacturer's specified setback distance, following the specified bond pattern.
  2. Stagger vertical joints. Offset each course so vertical joints do not align with the course directly below.
  3. Place drainage aggregate behind the wall as it rises. Add drainage aggregate to the reinforced backfill zone after each course is set, keeping the aggregate free of fine-soil contamination.
  4. Compact reinforced or retained backfill in approved lifts using equipment appropriate to distance from the wall. Compact each lift before placing the next, using a hand tamper or lightweight compactor near the wall face.
  5. Do not push heavy compaction equipment directly against the wall. Maintain the manufacturer's specified minimum distance for standard compaction equipment.
  6. Install geogrid where specified, at the required elevation and orientation. Place geogrid at the exact course elevation and orientation shown in the wall design, extending it to the specified embedment length into the backfill.
  7. Maintain wall batter, alignment, and course level. Recheck the mason's line and 4-foot level after each course and correct any drift before continuing.
  8. Stop at the approved height. Complete the final course at the design wall height and confirm it does not exceed the manufacturer's nonengineered limit.

I. Final Inspection and Documentation

  1. Inspect base, first course, bond, setback, drainage, backfill, geogrid, alignment, and stability. Walk the full wall length and confirm each item against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Document wall limitations and inspections. Record the final wall height, retained height, setback measurements, geogrid record, and inspection findings on the Wall Construction Record.

Common Site Inspection

Review every completed wall section using the same checks regardless of wall length or curve.

  • Scope check: confirm the wall's design height and retained height remain within the manufacturer's nonengineered limit and no unapproved surcharge is present.
  • Base check: confirm the leveling pad is compacted at the correct elevation and slope.
  • First-course check: confirm every unit is level, correctly buried, and aligned to the layout line or curve.
  • Course check: confirm setback, bond, and staggered vertical joints are consistent through every course.
  • Drainage and backfill check: confirm drainage aggregate is continuous and backfill is compacted in lifts without displacing wall units or geogrid.
  • Stability check: confirm the finished wall shows no rocking, bulging, or displaced units before Chapter 13 drainage work begins.

Complete the Wall Construction Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 13.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

First Course Out of Level

Probable cause: the leveling-pad aggregate was not compacted evenly, or a unit was set without checking level in both directions.
Confirmation: recheck level front-to-back and side-to-side across the affected units using the 4-foot level.
Correction: remove the affected units, adjust and recompact the leveling-pad aggregate beneath them, and reset and relevel the units before continuing the course.
Reinspection: repeat the first-course check across the full course before placing the next course.

Wall Bulging or Leaning Outward

Probable cause: backfill was placed in lifts too thick, compacted with equipment too heavy or too close to the wall face, or geogrid was omitted or misplaced where required.
Confirmation: check the wall face against a plumb line or straightedge at several points, and review the backfill and geogrid records against the design.
Correction: stop backfilling, and if displacement is significant, remove affected backfill and courses to a stable point, correct the lift thickness, compaction equipment, or geogrid placement, and rebuild from that point.
Reinspection: repeat the alignment and stability check after correction and before continuing backfill.

Vertical Joints Aligning Between Courses

Probable cause: units were placed directly above the joints of the course below instead of staggered as required.
Confirmation: visually inspect the wall face for a continuous vertical joint running through two or more courses.
Correction: remove and reset the affected units to restore the required stagger before adding further courses above the error.
Reinspection: repeat the course-by-course bond check for the corrected section.

Drainage Aggregate Contaminated with Fine Soil

Probable cause: native soil was allowed to mix into the drainage aggregate during placement or backfilling, or fabric separation was omitted where required.
Confirmation: inspect the drainage aggregate for visible soil intrusion or discoloration.
Correction: remove the contaminated aggregate, restore fabric separation if required by the design, and replace with clean drainage aggregate.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage and backfill check before continuing to the next course.

Wall Height Approaching or Exceeding Nonengineered Limit

Probable cause: the design or field conditions require a wall height at or beyond the manufacturer's published nonengineered limit.
Confirmation: measure the actual and planned final wall height against the manufacturer's height table.
Correction: stop construction at the approved height, and refer any requirement for additional height to an engineer before proceeding further.
Reinspection: repeat the scope check before resuming construction under a revised, engineered design.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a stable, level, correctly backfilled wall section and a completed construction record, not a wall built from memory.

  • Remove excess aggregate, soil, and debris from the work area and adjacent surfaces
  • Store unused block units, drainage aggregate, and geogrid protected from weather and contamination for use in later wall sections
  • Photograph the completed wall face, drainage aggregate, and any geogrid layers before they are concealed by further backfill or capping
  • File the Wall Construction Record with the wall design, manufacturer height table, and utility locate ticket
  • Retain the completed wall record for reference in Chapter 13 drainage installation and Chapter 14 capping

Field Terms

Wall Height
The vertical distance from the top of the leveling pad to the top of the last block course, excluding the cap.
Retained Height
The vertical distance from the top of the leveling pad to the finished grade behind the wall.
Setback
The horizontal distance each course is placed behind the course below it, giving the wall its batter.
Buried Depth
The portion of the first course placed below finished grade in front of the wall.
Surcharge
Any load behind the wall beyond the retained soil itself.
Reinforced Backfill Zone
The backfill zone directly behind the wall where drainage aggregate and geogrid are placed.
Leveling Pad
The compacted aggregate base beneath the first course that establishes the wall's starting elevation and level.
Gravity Wall
A retaining wall that resists soil pressure through the weight and setback of its own units rather than embedded reinforcement.
Geogrid
A specified soil-reinforcement material installed at a defined elevation and orientation to extend the wall's effective mass into the backfill.
Bond Pattern
The staggered arrangement of vertical joints between courses that prevents a continuous joint line from running through the wall.

Performance Check

With the wall constructed and every record on the Wall Construction Record completed, review the finished wall against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm that the wall remains within nonengineered scope, the leveling pad and first course are level and correctly buried, setback and bond are consistent through every course, drainage aggregate is continuous, geogrid is installed exactly as specified where required, backfill is compacted in controlled lifts, and the finished wall shows no rocking, bulging, or displaced units. If any item is incomplete or a deficiency remains unresolved, correct it and reinspect the affected record before beginning Chapter 13.
