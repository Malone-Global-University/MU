Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to carry one small residential room from an approved plan through demolition, concealed repair, finish restoration, fixture and storage installation, operational testing, punch-list inspection, and a documented owner handoff. It is the single project every prior chapter in this book has been building toward, and it does not teach any new hand skill; it integrates the skills you already demonstrated in Chapters 1 through 19 into one continuous, correctly sequenced project.

You will select one approved capstone design — a small laundry or utility room remodel, or the fully documented alternate powder-room refresh — and carry it through the exact investigation-to-closeout sequence defined in the Book-Level Authoring Rule: readiness decision, work-area protection, selective demolition, concealed-condition inspection, cause correction, rough work, concealed-work inspection, drywall and paint restoration, flooring and trim installation, fixture and storage installation, cabinet hardware, operational testing, punch-list inspection, cleanup, and a documented, signed owner handoff.

This is the last chapter in Book 21. The completed, accepted, and documented room is the record that Book 22's property-maintenance and building-inspection practices assume is already in hand.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RR-115 — Integrate project scoping, work-area protection, and selective demolition into one continuous capstone project sequence.
  • RR-116 — Integrate surface repair, trim repair, and finish painting over accepted substrates within the capstone room.
  • RR-117 — Integrate fixture, storage, and cabinet-hardware updates appropriate to the selected capstone design.
  • RR-118 — Perform the required operational and leak tests for every reconnected fixture, appliance, and utility affected by the capstone project.
  • RR-119 — Perform a punch-list inspection of the completed capstone room and correct every identified deficiency.
  • RR-120 — Complete final cleanup, compile the closeout record, and obtain documented owner acceptance of the capstone project.

Where This Skill Fits

Every prior chapter produced one piece of this project: Chapter 1 the readiness decision, Chapter 2 the protection plan, Chapter 3 the demolition method, Chapters 4 through 10 the trim, drywall, flooring, door, casing, and paint restoration methods, Chapters 11 through 15 the plumbing-fixture and cabinet-hardware methods, Chapters 16 and 17 the non-structural opening and clean-water-damage methods, Chapter 18 the room-level sequencing method, and Chapter 19 the punch-list and closeout method. This chapter does not repeat those methods in full; it applies them, in the correct order, to one real room, and directs you back to the controlling chapter for any technique this chapter does not restate.

The completed capstone room becomes the documented starting condition for Book 22, where the same room enters a property-maintenance and periodic-inspection cycle.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • selecting and documenting one approved capstone design before procurement or demolition
  • completing the Chapter 1 readiness inspection and the Chapter 2 work-area protection and dust-control setup for the selected room
  • performing Chapter 3 selective demolition limited to the approved scope, and stopping for every concealed condition that differs materially from that scope
  • correcting the cause of any active water, condensation, drainage, vibration, attachment, or ventilation problem before restoring finishes
  • completing approved minor framing, backing, subfloor, wall-edge, and substrate repairs, and the required concealed-work and rough-in inspections before closure
  • restoring drywall, paint, flooring, and trim using the methods taught in Chapters 4, 5, 7, 9, and 10
  • installing the approved storage system for a laundry or utility room, or the approved vanity, faucet, drain, and toilet for the alternate powder room, using the methods taught in Chapters 11, 12, 14, and 15
  • reconnecting appliances, fixtures, and utilities and performing the required operational and leak tests
  • completing the Chapter 19 punch-list inspection, correcting every deficiency, and reinspecting each corrected item
  • completing final cleanup and a documented, signed owner handoff

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select or substitute a kitchen gut remodel, full bathroom remodel, load-bearing wall removal, structural opening, major water-damage restoration, whole-room rewiring, service or panel work, plumbing-stack relocation, gas work, HVAC redesign, or exterior-envelope window replacement as the capstone; these remain outside the Capstone Design Control regardless of the learner's confidence or prior chapter results
  • proceed past any concealed condition that differs materially from the approved capstone scope without completing the change-control process defined in Chapter 1
  • conceal drywall, flooring, or trim over an unresolved plumbing, electrical, structural, or moisture condition
  • issue written owner acceptance while any safety, code, water, structural, utility, fire, permit, or required-inspection item remains open on the punch list

Any condition identified during this project as requiring a permit, inspection, engineer, or licensed trade must be resolved by that referral before the affected work is concealed or the project is closed out.

Trade Note

This chapter is a sequencing and integration project, not a new-technique project. Where a step references an earlier chapter's controls — for example, "using Chapter 3 controls" — that earlier chapter's full procedure, acceptance standards, and troubleshooting apply in full. This chapter states the order of operations and the capstone-specific decisions; it does not repeat every prior chapter's detail.

Prerequisites

  • Independent demonstration of the competencies in Chapters 1 through 19, including RR-001 through RR-114
  • An approved capstone design meeting the Capstone Design Control: a small laundry or utility room remodel, or a fully documented alternate powder-room refresh with no structural alteration, shower or tub waterproofing, major drain relocation, service upgrade, or uncontrolled hazardous-material disturbance

Common Field Fundamentals

The capstone succeeds or fails on sequencing discipline, not on any single technique. The Book-Level System-Sequence Control governs this chapter: existing conditions and readiness are resolved before demolition; containment and protection are complete before disturbance; demolition remains selective and stops at every concealed condition that differs from the approved scope; the cause of any moisture, movement, or failure is corrected before cosmetic restoration; structural, plumbing, electrical, and fire-rated work is inspected before concealment; finishes are installed only over accepted substrates; and the project closes only after readiness, protection, demolition, concealed-condition, substrate, installation, operational, finish, cleanup, documentation, and owner-acceptance inspections have all passed.

Choosing Between the Two Approved Designs

Select the small laundry or utility room remodel unless the alternate powder-room refresh is fully documented, including vanity, faucet, toilet, flooring, wall repair, trim, paint, ventilation, electrical, and plumbing scopes, and the room requires none of the structural, waterproofing, drain-relocation, service-upgrade, or hazardous-material work excluded by the Capstone Design Control. Do not combine components of both designs into a single undocumented plan, and do not begin demolition until one design is selected and recorded on the Capstone Selection and Boundary Record.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop at Every Concealed Condition

The concealed-condition stop-work rule from Chapter 1 and Chapter 3 applies without exception in the capstone. Do not proceed past a concealed structural, electrical, plumbing, moisture, or hazardous-material condition that differs materially from the approved scope. Complete the change-control process before continuing, even if doing so delays the remaining phases.

Code Alert — Permits and Inspections Carry Through Every Phase

Every permit, inspection, and licensed-trade requirement identified in Step 8 remains in force through demolition, rough work, and closure. Do not conceal plumbing, electrical, or structural work behind drywall or flooring until the required inspection for that work has passed.

Measurement Cue — Carry the Same Reference Points Through Every Phase

Use the same reference points established during the Step 15 pre-demolition photographs and measurements throughout rough work, finish installation, and the Step 49 punch-list inspection. A measurement or photograph taken from an inconsistent reference point cannot be reliably compared across phases.

E. Planning and Readiness (Steps 1–17)

  1. Select the approved capstone room and design before procurement or demolition. Record the room and design on the Capstone Selection and Boundary Record.
  2. Use the preferred small laundry or utility room design, or use the fully documented alternate powder-room refresh. Confirm the selected design meets every condition of the Capstone Design Control before proceeding.
  3. Obtain owner authorization, existing-condition report, measured plan, final layout, finish schedule, fixture schedule, storage design, product data, budget, permit requirements, trade assignments, and inspection sequence. Apply Chapter 1's authorization and existing-condition method to the selected room, crediting any available continuity records.
  4. Define the exact demolition, repair, fixture, flooring, trim, paint, storage, utility, and finish limits. State the boundary in writing on the approved scope, using Chapter 1's repair-limit and exclusion format.
  5. Confirm that structural alteration, major utility relocation, uncontrolled hazardous materials, extensive mold, sewage, fire damage, and other excluded work are not present. Reject or reclassify the capstone selection if any excluded condition is found.
  6. Complete required lead, asbestos, mold, moisture, or other environmental assessment. Apply the hazardous-material assessment method from Chapter 1 and Chapter 3 before any disturbance is planned.
  7. Verify plumbing, electrical, gas, HVAC, fire, accessibility, egress, ventilation, and equipment-clearance requirements. Record each verified requirement on the approved scope.
  8. Identify work requiring licensed trades, design professionals, specialty contractors, permit, or inspection. List every referral using the Chapter 1 permit-and-referral method.
  9. Prepare the project schedule in investigation, protection, demolition, concealed repair, rough work, inspection, closure, finish, installation, test, and closeout order. Sequence the schedule to match this work order's section order.
  10. Prepare the material and equipment list and verify compatibility before procurement. Apply the material-takeoff and compatibility-verification method from the controlling chapter for each material category before ordering.
  11. Establish the change-control, concealed-condition, owner-communication, utility-shutdown, and emergency procedures. Record each procedure using the Chapter 1 and Chapter 2 formats.
  12. Perform the Chapter 1 readiness inspection and issue the approved start decision. Do not proceed to Step 13 until this decision is recorded as approved.
  13. Remove or protect room contents and coordinate occupant access. Apply the Chapter 2 contents-removal and access-coordination method.
  14. Set up the Chapter 2 containment, floor protection, dust control, cutting station, tool staging, debris path, lighting, and public controls. Test and accept the setup using the Chapter 2 acceptance standards before demolition begins.
  15. Photograph existing conditions and verify final dimensions before demolition. Use the same reference points established for the room's existing-condition report.
  16. Label items for salvage, reuse, disposal, trade disconnection, and owner retention. Record each labeled item on the material and equipment list.
  17. Complete required utility shutdowns and disconnections through qualified persons. Verify and document that each shutdown is confirmed before demolition begins.

F. Selective Demolition and Concealed-Condition Investigation (Steps 18–24)

  1. Perform selective removal of approved trim, cabinets, shelves, fixtures, flooring, drywall, hardware, or accessories using Chapter 3 controls. Apply the full Chapter 3 demolition procedure and acceptance standards within the approved boundary.
  2. Stop for every concealed condition that differs materially from the approved scope. Apply the Chapter 1 and Chapter 3 stop-work standard without exception.
  3. Inspect and document exposed framing, subfloor, wall backing, utilities, moisture, fasteners, insulation, and previous repairs. Photograph and measure every concealed condition before any repair or closure.
  4. Process required change orders and obtain approval before expanding the work. Apply the change-control procedure established in Step 11.
  5. Correct active water, condensation, drainage, vibration, attachment, or ventilation causes. Apply the cause-correction method from Chapter 6, 17, or 18 as applicable to the specific condition found.
  6. Complete required plumbing, electrical, HVAC, or other rough work through the approved trade scope. Coordinate qualified trades as identified in Step 8.
  7. Complete approved minor framing, backing, subfloor, wall-edge, or substrate repairs. Apply the substrate-repair methods from Chapters 4, 5, 6, and 7.

G. Rough Work, Backing, and Concealed-Work Inspection (Steps 25–28)

  1. Install backing for vanities, cabinets, shelves, trim, accessories, and storage where required. Verify backing width, attachment, plane, and utility clearance using the Chapter 11 and Chapter 15 methods.
  2. Restore fire blocking, draft stopping, rated layers, insulation, air sealing, vapor control, and acoustic components affected by the work. Match the restored assembly to the approved design rather than to appearance alone.
  3. Complete concealed-work, rough-in, moisture, and pre-closure inspections. Do not proceed to Section H until every required inspection has passed.
  4. Photograph all concealed repairs and utility work before closure. File these photographs with the Concealed-Condition and Change-Order Record.

H. Drywall, Paint, Flooring, Trim, and Fixture Installation (Steps 29–41)

  1. Install matching drywall or approved wall panels with supported edges and complete tape, compound, sanding, and texture work. Apply the full Chapter 5 procedure and acceptance standards.
  2. Prime repaired and porous surfaces using compatible products. Apply the Chapter 10 priming method.
  3. Complete ceiling and wall painting in the approved sequence. Apply the full Chapter 10 procedure, including cut-in, rolling, and blending to natural breaks.
  4. Prepare the floor substrate and verify flatness, strength, cleanliness, dryness, and product compatibility. Apply the Chapter 7 substrate-verification method.
  5. Install approved flooring or localized flooring repair with required movement, transitions, and cure protection. Apply the full Chapter 7 procedure and acceptance standards.
  6. Install or replace baseboard, casing, shoe molding, or other trim with matched profiles and controlled joints. Apply the Chapter 4 and Chapter 9 procedures as applicable.
  7. For a laundry or utility room, install the approved storage, shelving, cabinets, work surface, hooks, or organizers without blocking service access. Apply the Chapter 18 storage-installation method and load-rated fastening requirements.
  8. For the alternate powder room, install the approved vanity, top, faucet, drain, and toilet using Chapters 11 and 12 controls. Apply both chapters' full procedures, including leak and flush testing.
  9. Install or update approved cabinet pulls, hinges, drawer slides, accessories, and door hardware. Apply the full Chapter 15 procedure.
  10. Install the approved backsplash section where included using Chapter 14 controls. Apply the full Chapter 14 procedure and cure requirements.
  11. Reinstall devices, plates, fixtures, registers, alarms, accessories, and trim through the approved trade and finish procedures. Coordinate qualified trades for any device requiring de-energization or reconnection.
  12. Reconnect appliances or equipment through qualified persons and approved manufacturer procedures. Verify anti-tip, restraint, and clearance requirements before operation.
  13. Restore utilities progressively and inspect each system before full operation. Restore one utility at a time and inspect its connections before restoring the next.

I. Operational Testing and Final Sealant (Steps 42–48)

  1. Perform supply-pressure, drain, full-bowl, toilet-flush, appliance, ventilation, electrical, door, drawer, hardware, and other required operational tests. Apply each controlling chapter's specific test procedure and log the result on the Operational Test Log.
  2. Inspect every accessible connection for leakage, strain, clearance, support, and serviceability. Correct and retest any connection that fails.
  3. Install final sealant, caulk, grout treatment, touch-up finish, and labels only after fit and operational testing are accepted. Apply the sealant methods from Chapters 11, 12, and 14.
  4. Allow required cure before loading cabinets, using fixtures, moving appliances, washing surfaces, or opening the room to occupants. Observe each product's stated cure time before returning the room to use.
  5. Remove containment in a controlled clean-to-dirty sequence without spreading residual dust. Apply the Chapter 2 containment-removal method.
  6. Clean the room, access path, adjacent areas, exterior route, tools, and waste location. Apply the Chapter 2 and Chapter 19 cleanup methods.
  7. Restore furniture, contents, window coverings, doors, locks, alarms, and occupant functions included in the scope. Confirm every restored item operates correctly before proceeding to Section J.

J. Punch List, Final Inspection, and Owner Handoff (Steps 49–58)

  1. Perform the Chapter 19 inspection of ceilings, walls, doors, trim, cabinets, storage, flooring, backsplash, plumbing, electrical, ventilation, equipment, finish, and cleanup. Apply the full Chapter 19 inspection sequence and zones.
  2. Prepare a categorized punch list with photographs and acceptance criteria. Separate safety, code, water, structural, operational, finish, documentation, and owner-preference items.
  3. Correct safety, water, structural, utility, operational, code, finish, and documentation deficiencies. Assign and complete corrections in an order that prevents damage to accepted work.
  4. Reinspect and close every punch-list item. Apply the same acceptance criterion used during the original inspection.
  5. Complete required final permit and trade inspections. Confirm every permit identified in Step 8 has received its final inspection approval.
  6. Compile the approved scope, changes, permits, inspections, product data, warranties, color formulas, finish schedule, moisture records, tests, concealed photographs, fixture information, spare materials, and maintenance instructions. Assemble the complete closeout record.
  7. Conduct the owner walkthrough and demonstrate shutoffs, fixtures, storage, doors, drawers, appliances, ventilation, cleaning, and maintenance. Confirm the owner operates each item during the walkthrough.
  8. Document any remaining limitation, excluded condition, future maintenance item, or professional referral. State the specific condition and the responsible party for each deferred item.
  9. Obtain written acceptance and hand off the room as clean, safe, operational, documented, and ready for its defined use. Do not obtain acceptance while any safety, code, water, structural, utility, fire, permit, or required-inspection item remains open.
  10. Document the formal transition to Book 22 property maintenance and building inspection. File the complete closeout record where it will be available to the periodic-inspection and maintenance process.

Common Inspection Review

Review the completed capstone project using the same checks regardless of which approved design was selected.

  • Design-selection check: confirm one approved capstone design was selected and recorded before procurement or demolition, with no mixing of the two designs.
  • Sequence check: confirm readiness, protection, demolition, concealed-condition, rough-work, substrate, installation, operational, finish, cleanup, and documentation phases occurred in the required order.
  • Change-control check: confirm every concealed condition that differed materially from the approved scope was processed through change control before work continued past it.
  • Concealed-work check: confirm every plumbing, electrical, structural, and fire-rated item was inspected and accepted before enclosure.
  • Operational-test check: confirm every reconnected fixture, appliance, and utility was tested with a logged pass result and no leakage, strain, or malfunction.
  • Punch-list check: confirm every deficiency was corrected and reinspected, and that no safety, code, water, structural, utility, fire, permit, or required-inspection item remained open at closeout.
  • Acceptance check: confirm written owner acceptance was obtained, or every deferred item was documented with responsibility and due condition.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Demolition Proceeded Past a Concealed Condition Without Change Control

Probable cause: Section F's stop-work standard was not applied when a concealed structural, utility, or moisture condition differing from the approved scope was uncovered.
Confirmation: compare the exposed-condition photographs and inspection notes against the approved scope to identify the point of divergence.
Correction: stop further work in the affected area, prepare and obtain approval for the required change order, and reinspect the condition before continuing.
Reinspection: repeat the change-control check across the full demolition record.

Drywall or Flooring Was Installed Over an Uninspected Rough-In

Probable cause: Section G's concealed-work inspection was skipped or completed after enclosure began.
Confirmation: compare the concealed-work inspection date against the drywall or flooring installation date on the project schedule.
Correction: where required, expose the enclosed work for inspection before proceeding further, and document the correction.
Reinspection: repeat the concealed-work check and confirm the inspection precedes every subsequent enclosure step.

An Operational Test Was Skipped or Logged Without a Pass Result

Probable cause: Section I's testing steps were treated as optional for a fixture or appliance that appeared to be working by observation alone.
Confirmation: compare the Operational Test Log against the full list of reconnected fixtures, appliances, and utilities from Sections H and I.
Correction: perform the missing test using the controlling chapter's procedure and log the result before proceeding to Section J.
Reinspection: repeat the operational-test check across the full log.

The Punch List Was Closed With an Open Safety or Code Item

Probable cause: Step 51's correction order did not prioritize safety, water, structural, utility, fire, permit, and required-inspection items ahead of finish and documentation items.
Confirmation: cross-check every punch-list category against its reinspection status before Step 57.
Correction: correct and reinspect every open safety, code, water, structural, utility, fire, permit, or required-inspection item before proceeding.
Reinspection: repeat the punch-list check.

Owner Acceptance Was Obtained Before the Closeout Record Was Complete

Probable cause: Step 57's written acceptance was requested before Step 54's compiled closeout record, including permits, inspections, product data, and maintenance instructions, was assembled.
Confirmation: compare the acceptance date against the closeout-record compilation date.
Correction: complete the closeout record, review it with the owner, and obtain a corrected acceptance signature dated after the record is complete.
Reinspection: repeat the acceptance check.

Cleanup

This project ends with a clean, fully restored room and a complete closeout record, not with the last installed component.

  • Remove containment in the controlled clean-to-dirty sequence and confirm no residual dust remains on finished surfaces
  • Clean the room, access path, adjacent areas, exterior route, tools, and waste location
  • Confirm every protruding fastener, sharp debris, trip hazard, wet compound, open penetration, exposed utility, and unsecured material has been removed from the work area
  • Restore furniture, contents, window coverings, doors, locks, alarms, and occupant functions included in the scope
  • File the complete closeout record where it will be available for Book 22 property-maintenance and periodic-inspection use
