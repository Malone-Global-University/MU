26-Construction Project Management

This field manual provides practical instruction in managing residential construction projects from planning through completion. Students learn to organize project information, schedule construction activities, coordinate trades, manage materials, track changes, and maintain quality and safety standards.

Overview

Construction Project Management develops the organizational and leadership skills required to successfully manage residential construction operations. Beginning with project scope and documentation systems, students progress through scheduling, procurement, subcontractor coordination, field communication, change management, safety planning, and quality control procedures.

Learning Outcomes

  • Define residential construction project scope and objectives.
  • Organize project files and construction documentation.
  • Create work breakdown structures and construction sequences.
  • Develop residential construction schedules.
  • Create short-term lookahead schedules for field operations.
  • Coordinate material procurement and deliveries.
  • Manage subcontractor communication and trade handoffs.
  • Conduct effective jobsite coordination meetings.
  • Manage RFIs, changes, allowances, and change orders.
  • Create safety and quality control management systems.
