28-Restoration and Disaster Recovery

This field manual provides practical instruction for residential restoration after water damage, moisture intrusion, mold risks, and minor smoke impacts. Students learn safe response procedures, damage assessment, drying methods, repair preparation, and recovery planning for residential environments.

Overview

Restoration and Disaster Recovery develops the skills needed to respond to common residential damage events safely and systematically. Beginning with damage inspection and documentation, students progress through emergency stabilization, water removal, drying procedures, repair preparation, moisture control, mold-safe practices, and smoke-affected surface cleaning.

Learning Outcomes

  • Inspect and document residential damage conditions.
  • Establish safe restoration work areas.
  • Stabilize properties after minor water damage events.
  • Remove damaged materials using proper procedures.
  • Dry affected rooms and control moisture conditions.
  • Prepare damaged areas for repair and reconstruction.
  • Identify mold risks and apply moisture control practices.
  • Follow basic mold-safe work procedures.
  • Clean and deodorize smoke-affected surfaces.
  • Apply organized restoration response methods.
