Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to perform one final restoration quality and safety inspection of a completed project and close it with a formal, categorized punch list. The finished work product is a completed inspection covering moisture correction, repaired surfaces, odor and staining, exterior weatherproofing, life-safety and utility function, and documentation completeness, together with a punch list that assigns responsibility, acceptance criteria, and photographs to every open item, and a record of every item reinspected and closed.

You will verify that the original source is permanently corrected, that every required inspection and approval is on file, that concealed work was documented before closure, that moisture, odor, and surface conditions meet the project's stated goals, that exterior and life-safety systems are restored and functioning, and that every incomplete or unverified condition is captured as a punch item rather than assumed acceptable. You will then correct, reinspect, and close every punch item, prepare owner maintenance instructions, and obtain written acceptance.

This chapter uses the damage log, moisture logs, photographs, permits, inspections, and repair records built in Chapters 1 through 18. It supplies the final acceptance gate that the Chapter 20 capstone must pass before the restoration file can be archived as complete.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RD-109 — Inspect moisture correction against the project's documented drying goals and reference readings.
  • RD-110 — Inspect repaired surfaces for plane, joint, texture, color, sheen, and attachment.
  • RD-111 — Inspect for odor or staining after normal ventilation and without active masking products.
  • RD-112 — Inspect exterior weatherproofing, drainage, and permanent-repair status.
  • RD-113 — Document remaining defects as classified punch items with responsibility and acceptance criteria.
  • RD-114 — Approve restoration closeout only after every required item is corrected, reinspected, and closed.

Where This Skill Fits

Every prior chapter in this book produced part of the restoration record: the damage log from Chapter 1, the source-control record from Chapter 3, the moisture logs from Chapter 5, the repair and rebuild records from Chapters 6 and 18, and the insurance-support file from Chapter 17. This chapter is the point where all of that evidence is checked against the finished physical condition of the property, not just filed. The habit built here — treat every unverified condition as an open item, not an assumption — is the exact discipline the Chapter 20 capstone applies to close its own restoration file.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the complete restoration file before beginning the physical inspection
  • verifying source correction, temporary-control removal, and required inspections
  • inspecting moisture, surfaces, odor, staining, and exterior weatherproofing against documented goals
  • inspecting life-safety, electrical, plumbing, and HVAC functions disturbed by the restoration work
  • creating a categorized, photographed punch list and closing every item
  • preparing owner maintenance instructions and obtaining written acceptance

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • close a project with an open safety, active-water, structural, utility, environmental, permit, or required-inspection item
  • diagnose new cause of loss, assign insurance coverage, or make a coverage determination while conducting the inspection
  • seal, encapsulate, paint over, or otherwise conceal a punch item instead of correcting it
  • substitute a personal impression of dryness or cleanliness for the documented moisture, odor, and surface criteria established earlier in the project

Any condition that cannot be verified against its documented acceptance criterion must be recorded as an open punch item, not assumed acceptable.

Trade Note

This chapter's punch list is a factual record of open conditions, not a negotiation document. An item is closed only when it is corrected and reinspected against the same criterion used to open it. Closing an item on a verbal assurance instead of a reinspection is the most common source of reopened restoration claims after handoff.

Setup Standard

Do not begin the physical inspection until the complete restoration file — incident assessment, scope, authorizations, environmental records, moisture logs, permits, inspections, photographs, estimates, changes, and warranties — has been collected and reviewed. Do not close any punch item without a reinspection against its original acceptance criterion.

Shop Tip

Take final moisture readings at the exact labeled points used in the Chapter 5 or Chapter 18 drying logs, not at new locations. A new reading point cannot be compared to the documented drying goal, and an uncomparable reading is not evidence of dryness.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every closeout in this book, from a single-room repair to the Chapter 20 capstone, follows the same sequence: verify the record, verify the physical condition against that record, capture every gap as a punch item, correct and reinspect, then obtain written acceptance. Learning that sequence here, on a completed project, means the Chapter 20 capstone closes from a method you already trust.

Reading Comparability and Honest Odor Evaluation

A final moisture reading is only meaningful when taken at the same labeled point, with the same instrument type, as the reading it is being compared to. An odor evaluation performed while a deodorizer, fragrance, or fogging product is still active is not a valid final evaluation. Both practices exist to keep this chapter's findings comparable to the project's own documented goals, not to a general impression of the space.

Documentation Cue — Use the Original Reference Points

Take final readings at the exact labeled moisture-map points established in the project's drying logs. Do not create new reference points at final inspection; a new point has no documented drying goal to compare against.

Critical Safety Cue — Open Safety and Utility Items Stop Closeout

Do not close the project with any open safety, active-water, structural, utility, environmental, permit, or required-inspection item. Record the item, notify the responsible party, and hold the closeout until it is corrected and reinspected.

Field Safety Cue — Reinspect Before Reoccupying Corrected Areas

Confirm any equipment, access panel, or temporary protection disturbed during corrective work has been restored before the area is returned to normal use. Do not leave a corrected area in a partially reassembled condition between reinspection visits.

Code Alert — Confirm Required Inspections Before Closeout

Confirm that every structural, electrical, plumbing, mechanical, environmental, and building inspection required for the project's scope is complete and on file before closing the punch list. Do not accept a contractor's verbal assurance in place of the required inspection record.

F. Preparation — Record Review

  1. Collect the incident assessment, scope, authorizations, environmental records, moisture logs, permits, inspections, photographs, estimates, changes, and warranties. Confirm every item is present before scheduling the physical inspection.
  2. Verify that the original source is permanently corrected or formally accepted. Compare the source-repair record against the original Chapter 3 source-control record.
  3. Verify temporary water, weather, electrical, security, and structural controls were removed or converted to approved permanent work. Cross-check against the temporary-protection records from the governing chapter.
  4. Verify environmental, structural, electrical, plumbing, gas, HVAC, roofing, and building inspections are complete where required. List any missing inspection as a punch item before proceeding.
  5. Confirm concealed work was photographed and approved before closure. Cross-check concealed-work photographs against the rebuild records from Chapter 18.

G. Physical Inspection — Moisture, Surfaces, and Odor

  1. Review final moisture readings, reference locations, drying goals, and equipment-removal record. Confirm the readings were taken at the original labeled points.
  2. Inspect the original affected boundary and adjacent areas for renewed moisture. Take current readings and compare directly to the documented drying goal.
  3. Inspect rooms below, above, and beside the restored area where accessible. Record findings even where no damage is expected.
  4. Check for staining, swelling, peeling, corrosion, efflorescence, condensation, or new odor. Log each observed condition with its location and photograph.
  5. Evaluate odor after normal ventilation and without active masking products. Do not evaluate odor while a deodorizer or fogging product is still active.
  6. Inspect walls and ceilings for plane, joints, texture, color, sheen, cracks, fasteners, and stains. Use raking light to check joint and texture quality.
  7. Inspect trim for profile, alignment, joints, fastening, filler, caulk, and finish. Verify against the trim specification used in the governing repair chapter.
  8. Inspect flooring for plane, pattern, movement, transitions, lippage, gaps, adhesion, and damage. Check transitions with a straightedge or level as required.

H. Physical Inspection — Systems, Exterior, and Contents

  1. Inspect cabinets, fixtures, doors, windows, and built-ins affected by the work. Confirm alignment and normal operation.
  2. Inspect exterior temporary and permanent weatherproofing, siding, trim, flashing, roofing, sealant, drainage, and clearances. Confirm temporary protection has been removed or converted per the governing chapter.
  3. Inspect fire blocking, rated assemblies, alarms, egress, guards, handrails, doors, and life-safety functions affected by work. Do not accept a visual assumption in place of a functional check.
  4. Inspect electrical devices, covers, equipment access, and restored service through the authorized scope. Confirm devices are not disturbed, damaged, or missing covers.
  5. Inspect plumbing fixtures, shutoffs, drains, supply connections, and leak-test records. Confirm the source-repair leak test is on file.
  6. Inspect HVAC registers, returns, filters, condensate, ducts, and system status affected by restoration. Confirm any HVAC isolation from earlier chapters has been reversed.
  7. Verify contents and furniture were cleaned, restored, moved, or excluded according to the approved contents plan. Cross-check against the Chapter 2 or Chapter 15 contents records.
  8. Verify debris, contaminated waste, regulated waste, and ordinary waste have documented disposition. Cross-check against the Chapter 15 disposal log.
  9. Verify the work area, access route, exterior, common areas, and neighboring spaces are clean and undamaged. Record any residual cleaning or repair need as a punch item.

I. Punch-List Creation, Correction, and Closeout

  1. Create a punch item for every incomplete, defective, unverified, damaged, unsafe, or undocumented condition. Assign a unique punch-item number to each.
  2. Classify punch items by safety, source, moisture, contamination, structural, utility, weather, finish, documentation, and owner preference. Enter the classification on the punch list for every item.
  3. Do not close the project with open safety, active-water, structural, utility, environmental, permit, or required inspection items. Escalate any such item to the responsible party immediately.
  4. Assign responsible party, due condition, acceptance criterion, and photograph to each item. Confirm every punch item has a named responsible party before work proceeds.
  5. Complete corrections in an order that protects accepted work. Sequence corrections so that finished surfaces or closed systems are not disturbed by unrelated work.
  6. Reinspect every correction against the original criterion. Record the reinspection date, method, and result for each punch item.
  7. Review insurer-support records, owner-upgrade records, invoices, approvals, and outstanding financial documents. Confirm financial records are reconciled before final owner handoff.
  8. Prepare owner maintenance instructions for moisture monitoring, ventilation, exterior checks, coatings, flooring, alarms, and future inspection. Write instructions in plain language tied to the specific project.
  9. Conduct the final walkthrough and explain repaired areas, exclusions, limitations, and monitoring. Walk the same rooms covered in Section G and H with the owner or responsible party.
  10. Deliver permits, approvals, product information, warranties, photographs, readings, and maintenance records. Provide these as a single indexed handoff package.
  11. Obtain written acceptance or document deferred owner items and unresolved insurer matters separately. Record the name, date, and any deferred items on the acceptance record.
  12. Archive the closed punch list and final restoration report. File the complete closeout record with the restoration file built in Chapters 1 through 18.

Common Inspection Verification

Inspect the completed closeout package using the same checks regardless of incident type or property size.

  • File check: confirm the source-repair, moisture, environmental, permit, inspection, photograph, estimate, and warranty records are all present before physical inspection begins.
  • Moisture check: confirm final readings at original labeled points meet the documented drying goals.
  • Surface check: confirm repaired walls, trim, and flooring meet plane, joint, texture, color, sheen, and attachment standards.
  • Odor check: confirm odor was evaluated after normal ventilation and without masking products.
  • System check: confirm electrical, plumbing, HVAC, and life-safety functions disturbed by the work are restored and verified.
  • Punch-list check: confirm every open item is classified, assigned, corrected, reinspected, and photographed before acceptance.

Complete the Final Inspection and Punch-List Review in the Inspection Checklist section below before archiving the restoration file.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Final Moisture Reading Was Taken at a New Location

Probable cause: the inspector selected a convenient surface instead of the original labeled reference point used in the drying log.
Confirmation: compare the final reading location against the labeled moisture map from the governing drying chapter.
Correction: return to the exact original reference point and retake the reading, or document why that point is no longer accessible and select the nearest documented substitute with a stated reason.
Reinspection: repeat the moisture correction check against the documented drying goal.

Odor Was Evaluated While a Deodorizer Was Still Active

Probable cause: the odor check was performed immediately after a deodorizing product was applied, before ventilation and drying were complete.
Confirmation: check the deodorizer application date and ventilation record against the odor-evaluation timestamp.
Correction: ventilate the area under normal conditions for the required period, then reevaluate odor without any active masking product present.
Reinspection: repeat the odor check and record the result with the ventilation period noted.

A Punch Item Was Closed Without a Reinspection Record

Probable cause: the correction was completed and assumed acceptable without a documented reinspection against the original acceptance criterion.
Confirmation: review the correction and reinspection log for a missing reinspection date, method, or result.
Correction: reopen the punch item, perform the reinspection, and record the result before closing it again.
Reinspection: repeat the punch-list closure check across the full list.

A Required Inspection Record Is Missing from the File

Probable cause: a structural, electrical, plumbing, mechanical, or environmental inspection required by the project's scope was never scheduled, or the record was not filed.
Confirmation: cross-check the required-inspection list against the permit and inspection records on file.
Correction: schedule and complete the missing inspection, or obtain and file the existing record, before proceeding to closeout.
Reinspection: repeat the file-completeness review before continuing the physical inspection.

Temporary Protection Was Left in Place Without Conversion to a Permanent Repair

Probable cause: a temporary weatherproofing or structural control from an earlier chapter was never scheduled for permanent replacement.
Confirmation: compare the exterior condition against the temporary-protection monitoring schedule from the governing chapter.
Correction: open a punch item, arrange the qualified permanent repair, and hold closeout until it is complete and reinspected.
Reinspection: repeat the exterior weatherproofing check after the permanent repair is installed.

Written Acceptance Was Not Obtained Before Archiving the File

Probable cause: the final walkthrough occurred, but no signed or dated acceptance record was collected from the owner or responsible party.
Confirmation: check the acceptance record for a name, date, and signature or equivalent documented acknowledgment.
Correction: schedule a follow-up review, present the final findings again, and obtain the required written acceptance or documented deferral.
Reinspection: confirm the acceptance record is complete before the project is archived as closed.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a complete, filed closeout record, not with any physical work area to clean beyond the punch items themselves.

  • Back up all final-inspection photographs and digital records before leaving the property or immediately upon return
  • Return original notes, checklists, and forms to the restoration file without editing the originals
  • Store the file-completeness review, final inspection findings, punch list, correction and reinspection log, maintenance instructions, and acceptance record together as one dated closeout record
  • Remove any inspection equipment, markers, or temporary access aids used solely for the final inspection
  • Deliver the handoff package and obtain acknowledgment as required by Section I

Performance Check

With the physical inspection, punch-list corrections, and reinspections complete, review the finished closeout package against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the restoration file is complete; the source is permanently corrected; moisture readings meet documented goals at original reference points; repaired surfaces meet plane, joint, texture, color, sheen, and attachment standards; odor and staining were evaluated honestly after ventilation; exterior weatherproofing is permanent and drains correctly; life-safety and disturbed systems are functional; every punch item is corrected, reinspected, and photographed; and written acceptance is on file. If an item fails, identify whether the error occurred in file review, moisture comparison, surface inspection, odor evaluation, exterior inspection, or punch-list closure before correcting it.
