Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to prepare, operate, and document one limited mold-safe work setup for a small authorized repair that stays within the approved size, contamination, occupancy, and regulatory threshold established in Chapter 7. The finished work product is a documented work setup: isolated zones, appropriate containment, correctly selected PPE, low-disturbance removal, contained debris, cleaned tools and surfaces, and a complete written record of every control used.

You will confirm the project still qualifies for limited work, define zones and containment, select and stage PPE through an approved hazard assessment, remove and bag affected material using low-disturbance methods, physically clean retained surfaces, decontaminate tools and personnel, and document every control and result. This chapter does not authorize independent judgment about contamination classification, extent, or health risk; those determinations were made in Chapter 7 or belong to a qualified professional.

This chapter builds directly on Chapter 7's mold-risk and moisture-control assessment. Chapter 7 identifies whether a condition qualifies for this chapter's limited scope. This chapter's containment and PPE disciplines also prepare you for Chapter 9's smoke-residue cleaning setup and every later containment-based project in this book.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RD-043 — Select personal protective equipment appropriate to the assessed hazard and approved respiratory-program requirements.
  • RD-044 — Isolate the work area with zones and containment appropriate to the hazard and airflow plan.
  • RD-045 — Reduce dust disturbance using low-disturbance tools and removal methods.
  • RD-046 — Bag contaminated debris at the point of removal using approved containment methods.
  • RD-047 — Clean tools and retained surfaces before they leave the work zone.
  • RD-048 — Document mold-safe work procedures, controls, and results in a traceable record.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 7 tells you whether a condition may be handled as a small repair at all. This chapter is where that determination becomes a controlled physical setup: zones, containment, PPE, low-disturbance removal, bagged debris, cleaned tools, and a documented record. The zone-and-containment discipline built here is reused directly in Chapter 9's smoke-cleaning setup and in every later chapter that isolates a hazard before physical work begins.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • confirming that the affected area, material, contamination history, occupant condition, and scope still qualify for limited work
  • defining clean, transition, work, equipment, and waste zones with appropriate containment
  • selecting PPE and respiratory protection through an approved hazard assessment
  • removing and bagging affected material using low-disturbance methods
  • physically cleaning retained surfaces and tools using the approved method
  • documenting PPE, containment, tools, removal, cleaning, disposal, photographs, and limitations

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • proceed on any area, material, contamination level, HVAC condition, structural condition, sewage exposure, or occupancy situation that exceeds the approved small-work threshold; those conditions must be stopped and referred
  • select or use a respirator without completing the required hazard assessment, medical evaluation, fit testing, and training the respiratory-protection program requires
  • use bleach, antimicrobial, encapsulant, or fogging treatment as a substitute for physical material removal and cleaning
  • sand, grind, dry brush, use high-pressure air, or aim air movers at contaminated surfaces before source removal and cleaning are complete

Any condition discovered during setup or work that exceeds the approved threshold must stop work and be referred, not worked through or minimized.

Trade Note

This chapter's containment and PPE choices are not generic. Every selection must trace back to the Chapter 7 hazard assessment and the approved scope threshold. Treating this chapter's setup as a standard checklist for any mold condition, rather than a boundary-limited procedure, is how small approved work turns into an unauthorized remediation project.

Setup Standard

Do not begin removal until the affected area, material, contamination history, occupant condition, and scope have been confirmed against the Chapter 7 threshold, and zones, containment, and PPE staging (Steps 1–14) are complete. Do not sand, grind, dry brush, use high-pressure air, or direct air movers at contaminated surfaces at any point in this chapter.

Shop Tip

Stage a labeled "clean" bin just outside the transition zone before work begins. Every tool, bag exterior, and piece of replacement PPE that crosses into the clean area should have an obvious, single, repeatable path — deciding that path after debris is already moving is how contamination crosses into unaffected space.

Threshold, Zones, and Low-Disturbance Method

Every step in this chapter depends on the project remaining inside the Chapter 7 threshold. A zone plan built for a qualifying condition is not automatically valid if the removal boundary grows, if HVAC or structural conditions change, or if occupant health concerns arise mid-work. Confirm the threshold at setup, and stop immediately if any condition changes it.

Documentation Cue — Confirm the Boundary Before Containment

Record the exact removal and cleaning boundary from the Chapter 7 assessment before installing any containment. Do not expand the boundary during work without stopping and processing a documented scope change.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop and Refer Outside the Threshold

Stop work immediately if the affected area, contamination level, HVAC involvement, structural condition, sewage exposure, or occupant health concern exceeds the approved small-work threshold. Do not continue removal, cleaning, or containment on any condition outside this chapter's approved scope.

Field Safety Cue — Respiratory Protection Requires the Full Program

Do not select a respirator by appearance or convenience. Confirm hazard assessment, medical evaluation, fit testing, training, and cartridge selection are complete under the written respiratory-protection program before any respirator is worn.

Code Alert — Environmental and Occupant-Health Boundaries

Confirm required environmental and hazardous-material authorizations before beginning containment. Refer occupant health questions to qualified medical professionals and environmental classification questions to qualified consultants; this chapter does not authorize either determination.

  • Containment supplies: sheeting sufficient to cover the work-zone boundary and construct a zipper doorway, containment tape, and support framing where the plan requires rigid barriers.
  • PPE: gloves, eye protection, and protective clothing sufficient for every worker and every entry/exit cycle, plus program-approved respirators and cartridges only where required.

For each zone, record the following: zone name (clean, transition, work, equipment, or waste), physical boundary, containment material used, entry/exit point, and required condition before workers or materials may cross into it. Do not combine the work zone and the clean zone, and do not allow debris to bypass the transition zone.

F. Preparation — Threshold Confirmation and Authorization

  1. Confirm that the affected area, material, contamination history, occupant condition, and project scope qualify for limited work. Compare current conditions against the Chapter 7 assessment reference.
  2. Obtain required environmental, hazardous-material, and owner authorizations. Record each on the threshold confirmation sheet before proceeding.
  3. Stop and refer work exceeding the approved size, contamination, HVAC, structural, or occupancy threshold. Do not proceed to zone setup if any threshold condition is exceeded.
  4. Define the exact removal and cleaning boundary. Record the boundary in measurable terms on the zone-and-containment plan.

G. Zone Setup, Containment, and Airflow

  1. Identify clean, transition, work, equipment, and waste zones. Mark each zone boundary on the zone-and-containment plan before installing any physical barrier.
  2. Remove or protect unaffected contents. Move or cover contents according to the approved contents plan before containment goes up.
  3. Protect floors, walls, openings, fixtures, and adjacent rooms. Install floor and opening protection before work-zone containment.
  4. Isolate HVAC supplies and returns only through the approved plan. Confirm isolation with the responsible party before sealing any HVAC opening.
  5. Install containment appropriate to the work and maintain emergency egress. Confirm at least one unobstructed emergency exit remains available at all times.
  6. Set up approved air filtration or negative pressure where required. Confirm the unit matches the plan's specified airflow direction and capacity.
  7. Verify exhaust location and airflow direction. Confirm exhaust does not discharge into an occupied or clean area.

H. PPE Selection and Staging

  1. Select gloves, eye protection, protective clothing, footwear, and respiratory protection through the approved hazard assessment. Record the selected PPE for each worker on the PPE log.
  2. Confirm respiratory-program requirements before respirator use. Verify medical evaluation, fit testing, training, and cartridge selection are complete and current for each worker who will wear one.
  3. Stage replacement PPE, bags, wipes, filters, and decontamination supplies. Place staged supplies at the transition zone before work begins.

I. Low-Disturbance Removal and Debris Containment

  1. Select hand tools and low-disturbance methods that minimize dust and aerosolization. Confirm the selected method matches the material and the approved plan before starting.
  2. Do not sand, grind, dry brush, or use high-pressure air on affected material. Stop and select an alternative low-disturbance method if a planned tool would violate this limit.
  3. Do not aim air movers at contaminated surfaces before source removal and cleaning. Confirm air-mover placement avoids direct airflow across unremoved contaminated material.
  4. Lightly control dust only through the approved procedure and compatible method. Use the specified dampening or filtration method rather than an improvised substitute.
  5. Remove material in manageable sections without unnecessary breakage. Record each section's size, location, and removal method on the removal log.
  6. Keep contaminated face surfaces inward during handling. Fold or roll each removed section so the contaminated face does not contact clean surfaces or skin.
  7. Bag or wrap debris at the point of removal. Bag each section immediately after removal, before moving it toward the transition zone.
  8. Use double containment or labeled waste handling when required. Apply the approved double-bagging or labeling method for the material and contamination level.
  9. Clean bag exteriors before transfer through the transition zone where required. Wipe down each bag exterior at the transition zone boundary before it crosses into the clean path.

J. Retained-Surface and Tool Cleaning

  1. Use approved HEPA-filtered equipment for fine particulate control. Confirm the HEPA filter is intact and correctly seated before use.
  2. Do not use ordinary household vacuums. Remove any non-HEPA vacuum from the work zone before cleaning begins.
  3. Clean retained surfaces using the approved physical cleaning method. Record the method and product used for each retained surface on the cleaning record.
  4. Do not use bleach, antimicrobial, encapsulant, or fogging as a substitute for material removal and cleaning. Confirm no such product is applied before physical cleaning is complete and accepted.
  5. Clean tools before they leave the work zone. Wipe or vacuum every tool at the work-zone boundary and record the cleaning on the cleaning record.

K. Decontamination, Inspection, and Teardown

  1. Remove disposable PPE using the approved doffing sequence. Follow the sequence exactly, keeping contaminated surfaces away from skin and clean areas.
  2. Wash hands and exposed skin before entering clean areas. Confirm handwashing is complete before any worker exits the transition zone.
  3. Inspect containment, adjacent areas, and debris route for visible dust or damage. Walk the full containment perimeter and debris path before removing any barrier.
  4. Complete the approved post-cleaning inspection or professional verification. Record the inspection result and, where required, the verifying professional's findings.
  5. Document PPE, containment, tools, removal, cleaning, disposal, photographs, and limitations. Compile all logs and photographs into the mold-safe work record.
  6. Remove containment only after approval and in a sequence that prevents dust release. Fold barriers inward and remove from clean zone toward work zone last.
  7. Restore HVAC, alarms, doors, and occupant access affected by setup. Confirm restoration with the responsible party before closing the record.
  8. Record referrals and follow-up moisture-control actions. Carry forward any unresolved moisture-source item identified in Chapter 7.

Common Inspection Verification

Inspect the completed mold-safe work setup and record using the same checks regardless of material or room size.

  • Threshold check: confirm the project matched the Chapter 7 approved small-work threshold throughout, with no unresolved scope change.
  • Zone check: confirm clean, transition, work, equipment, and waste zones remained physically separated for the duration of the work.
  • PPE check: confirm every worker's PPE and any respirator use matches the hazard assessment and respiratory-protection program.
  • Removal check: confirm every removal step used a low-disturbance method with no sanding, grinding, dry brushing, or high-pressure air on contaminated material.
  • Containment check: confirm every removed item was bagged at the point of removal and every bag exterior was cleaned at the transition zone.
  • Cleaning check: confirm every retained surface and every tool was physically cleaned before leaving the work zone.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Debris Crossed the Transition Zone Without a Bag-Exterior Wipe-Down

Probable cause: the worker moved a filled bag directly from the work zone into the clean path without stopping at the transition zone.
Confirmation: review the removal log and observe the debris route for any bag that bypassed the transition-zone cleaning step.
Correction: stop debris movement, return the bag to the transition zone, wipe the exterior using the approved method, and resume the route.
Reinspection: repeat the containment check across the full debris route.

A Worker Wore a Respirator Without Completed Program Requirements

Probable cause: a respirator was selected by convenience or appearance rather than through the hazard assessment and written respiratory-protection program.
Confirmation: check the PPE log against the respiratory-program record for medical evaluation, fit testing, training, and cartridge selection.
Correction: remove the worker from respirator-required tasks until program requirements are met, and reassign PPE appropriate to the confirmed hazard in the meantime.
Reinspection: repeat the PPE check for every worker before further removal work resumes.

Removal Extended Beyond the Approved Boundary

Probable cause: affected material continued past the marked boundary, and the worker kept removing material without stopping for a documented scope change.
Confirmation: compare the removal log's recorded sections against the zone-and-containment plan's marked boundary.
Correction: stop removal at the point the boundary was exceeded, and route the condition back through the Chapter 7 threshold confirmation before any further removal.
Reinspection: repeat the threshold check before resuming work in the expanded area.

A Cleaning Product Was Applied Before Physical Cleaning Was Complete

Probable cause: an antimicrobial, encapsulant, or fogging product was used to address visible residue instead of completing HEPA vacuuming and physical cleaning first.
Confirmation: review the cleaning record for the sequence of methods used on each retained surface.
Correction: complete physical cleaning of the surface first, and only apply an approved product afterward if the plan specifically calls for it.
Reinspection: repeat the cleaning check on the affected surface.

A Tool Left the Work Zone Without Being Cleaned

Probable cause: a tool was carried directly from the work zone to storage or another task without stopping for HEPA vacuuming or wipe-down.
Confirmation: cross-check the tool inventory used in the work zone against the cleaning record.
Correction: return the tool to the work zone, clean it using the approved method, and record the cleaning before further use.
Reinspection: repeat the cleaning check across the full tool inventory.

Containment Was Removed Before Post-Cleaning Inspection Was Complete

Probable cause: the crew began teardown immediately after cleaning without completing the required inspection step.
Confirmation: check the mold-safe work record for a completed post-cleaning inspection entry dated before the containment-removal entry.
Correction: reinstall containment if conditions allow, complete the required inspection, and only then resume teardown.
Reinspection: confirm the inspection and containment-removal sequence is correctly recorded before the project is closed.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a decontaminated work area and a complete documentation set.

  • Confirm every waste bag is labeled, sealed, and routed to the correct disposal category before leaving the property
  • Confirm all containment materials have been removed in the approved clean-to-dirty sequence and disposed of or stored appropriately
  • Confirm HVAC, alarms, doors, and occupant access affected by setup have been restored
  • Store the threshold confirmation sheet, zone plan, PPE log, removal log, cleaning record, waste log, and photographs together as one dated record
  • Deliver referrals and follow-up moisture-control actions to the responsible party as required by the final step

Field Terms

Clean Zone
Unaffected space protected from contact with contaminated material, debris, or unclean tools.
Transition Zone
The controlled space between the clean zone and the work zone where decontamination and bag-exterior cleaning occur.
Work Zone
The contained space where removal, bagging, and initial cleaning occur.
Low-Disturbance Method
A removal or cleaning technique selected specifically to minimize dust and aerosolization.
Approved Small-Work Threshold
The size, contamination, HVAC, structural, and occupancy limit established in Chapter 7 that this chapter's scope may not exceed.
Doffing Sequence
The approved order for removing disposable PPE that keeps contaminated surfaces from contacting skin or clean areas.
Negative Air
Airflow maintained so the work zone remains at lower pressure than surrounding clean areas, drawing air inward rather than outward.
Double Containment
Bagging or wrapping debris in two sealed layers as required by the material or contamination level.
Respiratory-Protection Program
The written program of hazard assessment, medical evaluation, fit testing, training, and cartridge selection required before respirator use.
Post-Cleaning Inspection
The required review confirming retained surfaces, containment, and debris routes are clean before teardown begins.
