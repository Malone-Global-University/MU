Job Overview

This project qualifies you to complete one coordinated, climate-appropriate seasonal exterior maintenance cycle on an accessible residential property. The finished work product is a dated Seasonal Exterior Maintenance record documenting cleared debris and drainage paths, checked hose-bibb connections, minor accessible sealant service, secured loose items, and referrals for work outside the maintenance scope.

You will identify the seasonal risks that apply to the property, review prior seasonal records and open exterior findings, clear debris from walkways and drainage paths, verify downspout extensions and splash blocks remain connected, inspect and service hose bibbs, replace only minor failed sealant at joints designed for it, secure minor loose exterior hardware, and prepare the property for the specific seasonal condition ahead — freeze, storm, heat, or heavy precipitation.

This project draws directly on the roof-drainage inspection in Chapter 3, the gutter and downspout maintenance in Chapter 4, the siding, trim, and sealant inspection in Chapter 5, and the foundation and grading inspection in Chapter 6. It converts those findings into a recurring, season-specific maintenance cycle rather than a one-time inspection. Chapter 18 performs the matching seasonal cycle for the interior. Chapter 19 will fold every completed and deferred item from this cycle into the property's prioritized repair plan.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • MI-097 — Clear exterior debris from walkways, drains, swales, and foundation edges appropriate to the current season.
  • MI-098 — Inspect hose bibbs, vacuum breakers, and seasonal water-line shutdown for freeze and leak conditions.
  • MI-099 — Check and service accessible exterior caulk at joints designed for sealant, without blocking drainage.
  • MI-100 — Maintain drainage paths, including downspout extensions, splash blocks, and area drains.
  • MI-101 — Prepare exterior systems for the identified seasonal change, including freeze, storm, heat, or heavy-precipitation conditions.
  • MI-102 — Document completed seasonal tasks, unresolved defects, and the next seasonal trigger in the property maintenance log.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapters 3 through 6 taught you to inspect roof drainage, gutters, siding, trim, sealant, foundation, and grading in technical depth. This project does not repeat that inspection. Instead, it applies a recurring, season-driven maintenance cycle to those same systems, using the findings already recorded in the property binder from Chapter 2.

The skill you build here — identifying seasonal risk, clearing and verifying drainage, servicing hose bibbs, performing only minor approved sealant work, and documenting the cycle for next season — is the same recurring discipline Chapter 19 relies on when it builds the property's 30-60-90 day and annual maintenance plan. Chapter 20's capstone repeats this exact cycle as part of the full property inspection and maintenance package.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • identifying the upcoming seasonal risks that apply to the specific property and climate
  • clearing debris from approved walkways, stairs, decks, porches, window wells, drains, swales, and foundation edges
  • verifying downspout extensions, splash blocks, sump discharge, and surface drains remain connected and directed through approved paths
  • inspecting and operating hose bibbs only when freeze conditions, interior shutoffs, and leak response permit, and disconnecting hoses and attachments where the seasonal procedure requires it
  • removing and replacing only minor failed sealant at joints identified in Chapter 5 as designed for sealant
  • tightening only minor accessible exterior hardware with verified backing and compatible fasteners
  • securing or storing approved seasonal furniture, grills, hoses, decorations, and tools
  • producing one dated Seasonal Exterior Maintenance record with completed tasks, unresolved defects, and the next seasonal trigger

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • seal weeps, vents, siding laps, drainage gaps, or joints that require broader flashing repair
  • remove vegetation in a way that destabilizes slopes, damages protected landscaping, or exposes utilities
  • enter confined drainage structures or force concealed blockages
  • repair roofing, flashing, structural cladding, foundation, or retaining-wall defects identified in Chapters 3 through 6 as requiring a qualified professional
  • perform tree pruning or arborist work beyond a ground-level visual check

Any condition that appears to be an active freeze failure, structural hazard, uncontrolled water entry, or utility emergency must stop the routine seasonal cycle and be handled per the Critical Safety Cue in this chapter before work continues.

Trade Note

A seasonal maintenance cycle is not a re-inspection. You are not walking the property to discover new defects the way Chapters 1 and 3 through 6 did. You are applying a recurring set of season-specific tasks to systems already documented, and recording what changed since the last cycle. Treat any newly discovered defect as a referral to the appropriate inspection chapter, not as an item to diagnose or repair here.

Setup Standard

Do not begin the seasonal cycle until the applicable seasonal risks, current weather, and the prior seasonal record are reviewed (Step 1). A task performed before the property-specific seasonal shutdown procedure is confirmed must be reverified before it is recorded as complete.

Shop Tip

Photograph the pre-maintenance condition of drainage paths, hose bibbs, and sealant joints before starting work, and photograph the same locations again after the cycle is complete. A matched before-and-after pair makes the next seasonal cycle faster to plan and easier to verify.

Minor Maintenance Versus Referral Work

This chapter authorizes only minor, accessible, non-invasive maintenance. A joint requiring flashing repair, a drain requiring confined-space entry, a retaining wall showing displacement, or a tree limb overhanging the roof are referral items, not seasonal-maintenance tasks. Record referral items on the worksheet and route them to the appropriate chapter or qualified professional rather than attempting the work here.

Documentation Cue — One Task, One Result, One Record

Record every completed task with the product or part used, the observed condition before and after, and the result. Record every unresolved defect as a referral rather than omitting it because the routine cycle is otherwise complete.

Critical Safety Cue — Freeze, Flooding, and Structural Conditions Stop the Routine Cycle

If you encounter active flooding, a burst or actively leaking water line, a displaced or leaning retaining wall, storm-damaged structural components, or a downed utility line, stop the routine seasonal cycle immediately. Secure or isolate the area if it is safe to do so, and notify the owner, building management, the utility, or emergency services as the situation requires before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Ladders, Weather, and Occupants

Use ladders only on firm, level support with the required angle, extension, and three-point-contact practices, and only for tasks within this chapter's approved access. Do not perform this cycle in unsafe wind, ice, lightning, or extreme heat. Confirm pets are controlled and children are excluded from work areas before beginning.

Code Alert — Drainage Openings Must Remain Open

Do not seal weeps, vents, siding laps, drainage gaps, roof ventilation, or any joint identified in Chapter 5 as an intentional opening. Sealant is authorized only at joints specifically designed for it.

E. Preparation — Risk Review and Records

  1. Identify the upcoming seasonal risks for the property. Determine which of freeze, snow, heavy rain, wind, wildfire, heat, humidity, storms, irrigation, or leaf accumulation apply to this cycle.
  2. Review prior seasonal records and open findings. Check prior seasonal records, open exterior findings from Chapters 3 through 6, manufacturer instructions, local utility guidance, and property-specific shutdown procedures.
  3. Check weather and schedule the work. Confirm dry, safe conditions appropriate to the selected tasks before beginning.
  4. Prepare the task list, tools, and PPE. Stage the approved task list, tools, waste containers, ladder controls, personal protective equipment, and emergency plan.
  5. Photograph pre-maintenance conditions. Walk the site and photograph conditions before beginning any task.

F. Debris and Drainage Maintenance

  1. Remove debris from approved surfaces. Clear leaves, branches, litter, vegetation, and debris from walkways, stairs, decks, porches, window wells, drains, swales, and foundation edges.
  2. Preserve slope stability and utilities. Do not remove vegetation in a way that destabilizes slopes, damages protected landscaping, or exposes utilities.
  3. Clear accessible drainage structures. Clear accessible area drains, catch-basin grates, window-well drains, swales, and discharge outlets without entering confined structures or forcing concealed blockages.
  4. Verify downspout and discharge connections. Confirm downspout extensions, splash blocks, sump discharge, and surface drains remain connected and direct water through the approved path.
  5. Correct minor displaced components. Reposition minor displaced extensions or splash blocks within the approved scope.
  6. Inspect grading for new concerns. Identify new low areas, erosion, settlement, animal burrows, and blocked drainage paths, and record them as referrals if outside the maintenance scope.

G. Hose Bibbs, Sealant, and Minor Hardware

  1. Inspect hose bibbs and connections. Check hose bibbs, vacuum breakers, handles, escutcheons, piping support, and wall penetrations.
  2. Operate bibbs only when conditions permit. Operate hose bibbs only when freeze conditions, interior shutoffs, drainage, and leak response permit it.
  3. Disconnect seasonal attachments. Disconnect hoses, timers, splitters, and attachments where the seasonal procedure requires it.
  4. Complete the seasonal water-line shutdown. Shut down and drain seasonal water lines only through the approved property-specific procedure, and do not assume a frost-resistant fixture is protected while hoses or attachments remain connected.
  5. Service only minor failed sealant. Remove and replace failed sealant only at joints identified in Chapter 5 as designed for sealant, confirming substrate, flashing, drainage, weather, and material compatibility first.
  6. Tighten only minor accessible hardware. Tighten stairs, handrails, guards, gates, locks, and lighting hardware only where backing and compatible fasteners are verified.

H. Seasonal Preparation and Closeout

  1. Secure or store seasonal items. Store approved seasonal furniture, grills, hoses, decorations, tools, and loose objects without blocking exits or equipment.
  2. Inspect trees from the ground. Identify obvious storm or contact concerns and refer pruning or arborist work as needed.
  3. Prepare seasonal-response supplies. Stage snow, ice, storm, wildfire, or heat-response supplies appropriate to the property without creating fuel, chemical, or access hazards.
  4. Inspect exterior vents and terminations. Check for debris, nests, snow exposure, or damage without blocking required airflow.
  5. Recheck drainage and access. Confirm drainage paths and exterior access remain clear after all maintenance tasks.
  6. Clean the work area and record results. Remove waste without leaving combustible debris against the building, then record every completed task, product, adjustment, unresolved defect, photograph, and next seasonal trigger.

Common Seasonal Maintenance Review

Review every completed task using the same checks regardless of which segment of the cycle it came from.

  • Scope check: confirm every task performed falls within this chapter's minor-maintenance scope and does not substitute for flashing, structural, or drainage-design repair.
  • Drainage check: confirm no drainage opening, weep, vent, or lap was sealed or blocked.
  • Connection check: confirm every downspout extension, splash block, and hose-bibb connection was verified after the work, not merely assumed.
  • Referral check: confirm every condition outside the maintenance scope was recorded as a referral rather than attempted or omitted.
  • Documentation check: confirm every completed task, unresolved defect, photograph, and the next seasonal trigger are recorded on the worksheet.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Sealant Was Applied to a Drainage Opening

Probable cause: a weep, vent, siding lap, or movement joint was mistaken for a joint designed for sealant.
Confirmation: compare the sealed joint against the Chapter 5 drainage-opening record for that elevation.
Correction: remove the sealant from the intentional opening and restore the original drainage path.
Reinspection: verify water drains freely through the opening before closing out the task.

A Hose Bibb Was Left Connected Through a Freeze Risk

Probable cause: the hose, timer, or splitter was not disconnected, or the seasonal shutdown procedure was skipped.
Confirmation: check the bibb, attached hardware, and interior shutoff against the property-specific seasonal procedure.
Correction: disconnect all attachments and complete the approved shutdown and drain procedure.
Reinspection: confirm the bibb and line are prepared according to the recorded procedure before the cycle is closed.

A Downspout Extension Was Disconnected During Debris Clearing

Probable cause: the extension was moved or knocked loose while clearing nearby debris and not reconnected.
Confirmation: trace each downspout to its discharge point and verify connection and slope.
Correction: reconnect and reposition the extension so discharge is directed away from the foundation through the approved path.
Reinspection: observe flow from the downspout to confirm the corrected connection functions before closeout.

A Referral Item Was Attempted as Routine Maintenance

Probable cause: a structural, flashing, or drainage-design defect from Chapters 3 through 6 was treated as a minor task within this chapter's scope.
Confirmation: compare the attempted task against the Acceptance Standards table and the original chapter finding.
Correction: stop the attempted repair, restore the area to its pre-work condition where possible, and record the item as a referral to the appropriate qualified professional.
Reinspection: confirm the worksheet lists the item as a referral, not as a completed task.

The Seasonal Record Does Not State the Next Trigger

Probable cause: the worksheet was closed out without identifying the date or condition that starts the next seasonal cycle.
Confirmation: review the completed worksheet for a stated next seasonal trigger.
Correction: add the specific date or condition, based on climate, property systems, and manufacturer instructions.
Reinspection: confirm the property calendar reflects the recorded next seasonal trigger.
