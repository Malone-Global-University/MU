Job Overview

This project qualifies you to service one approved door by correcting minor hinge, latch, lock, weatherstripping, sweep, and sealant conditions identified in Chapter 7. The finished work product is a single door that opens and closes smoothly, latches and locks reliably, seals continuously against air and water intrusion at its weatherstripping and threshold, and is fully documented with the products, adjustments, and test results used to complete the work.

You will select a door from the Chapter 7 service list, confirm it falls within the approved minor-maintenance scope, protect the surrounding finishes, support the door before disturbing hardware, tighten or replace hinge hardware, adjust the strike, clean and lubricate the lock, replace failed weatherstripping and the sweep, apply compatible sealant only at joints designed for it, and cycle and test the completed door before recording the results.

This project builds directly on the Chapter 7 opening inspection, which identified the service list this chapter now works from. It does not replace Chapter 7's inspection, and it does not authorize structural frame repair, insulated-glass replacement, fire-rated or egress-door alteration, or any work beyond the approved minor-maintenance scope. Chapter 9 returns to interior finish surfaces once this door service is complete.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • MI-043 — Tighten hinges using sound fasteners, verified alignment, and manufacturer-compatible replacement hardware when required.
  • MI-044 — Adjust latch alignment by repositioning the strike only after hinge and frame alignment are accepted.
  • MI-045 — Lubricate locks using a product-compatible method and the lubricant specified for the lock and environmental condition.
  • MI-046 — Replace weatherstripping to restore continuous compression without stretching, bunching, or leaving corner gaps.
  • MI-047 — Seal minor gaps at joints intended for sealant while preserving required drainage and threshold function.
  • MI-048 — Inspect completed door performance through repeated cycling, latch and lock testing, and documentation of results and unresolved causes.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 7 inspected every accessible opening and produced a service list separating cleaning, lubrication, minor adjustment, weatherstripping, sealant, hardware, frame repair, flashing repair, and replacement. This project performs the minor-maintenance portion of that list on one selected door: hinge tightening, latch and strike adjustment, lock lubrication, weatherstripping and sweep replacement, and approved sealant work.

This project does not perform frame repair, flashing correction, insulated-glass replacement, or work on fire-rated, smoke-control, egress, high-security, or automatic doors, which remain outside this chapter's scope. Findings requiring that work stay on the Chapter 7 service list for referral. The documentation habits built here — recording reveals, engagement, and test results before and after service — carry forward into every later maintenance chapter in this book.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • selecting one door from the Chapter 7 service list that is within the approved minor-maintenance scope
  • confirming the door is not a fire-rated, smoke-control, garage-separation, egress, high-security, automatic, or specialty assembly
  • tightening or replacing hinge screws and hinges within the approved minor-repair limits
  • adjusting the strike only after hinge and frame alignment are accepted
  • cleaning and lubricating lock and latch components with product-compatible methods
  • removing and replacing failed weatherstripping and the door sweep
  • removing and replacing minor failed exterior sealant only at joints intended for sealant
  • cycling and testing the completed door and documenting results

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • service a fire-rated, smoke-control, garage-separation, egress, high-security, automatic, or specialty door assembly under this chapter's procedure
  • repair frame decay, active water entry, failed threshold pans, major distortion, or structural movement as routine maintenance
  • seal window or door weeps, sill drainage, threshold drainage, or intentional ventilation openings
  • force a binding lock, flood a cylinder, or mix incompatible lubricants

If structural movement, active water entry, frame decay, broken glass, a failed threshold pan, or major distortion is identified during preparation, stop the minor-maintenance procedure and return the item to the Chapter 7 service list for the appropriate referral before continuing.

Trade Note

Minor hardware service can make a door feel "fixed" while a larger cause remains active. A door that binds because the frame has racked from foundation movement will feel the same at first touch as a door that simply needs its hinges tightened. Step 6 of this chapter's work order exists specifically to confirm the cause before any hardware is loosened.

Common Field Fundamentals

Door service in this book always follows the same order: confirm the door is in scope, verify the cause before touching hardware, work from the top of the assembly (hinges) down to the bottom (threshold and sweep), and retest the complete door before recording results. That sequence prevents a symptom from being masked by an adjustment that does not address its actual cause.

Hinge Work Before Latch Work

Hinge and frame alignment control everything downstream: a door that is not hanging correctly will never latch or seal correctly no matter how the strike is adjusted. This chapter requires reveal and operation to be rechecked after hinge work and accepted before the strike is touched.

Critical Safety Cue — Support the Door Before Loosening Hinges

An unsupported door can swing, twist, or fall when hinge hardware is loosened. Support the door with a wedge, prop, or a second person before removing or loosening any hinge screw, and do not release that support until the hinge is fully secured or the door is otherwise stabilized.

Field Safety Cue — Protect Finishes and Confirm Occupant Access

Protect the floor, threshold, walls, trim, and adjacent finishes before beginning. Confirm the door is not the sole available emergency exit for the duration of the work, or that an alternate exit remains available and known to occupants.

Code Alert — Non-Rated Doors Only

Do not service a fire-rated, smoke-control, garage-separation, egress-rated, high-security, automatic, or other specialty door assembly under this chapter's minor-maintenance procedure. These assemblies require separate, qualified procedures and, in many cases, licensed or listed-component service.

Manufacturer Cue — Matching Hardware and Compatible Products

Replace a hinge only with a matching approved size, thickness, corner, pin, finish, and rating. Apply only the lubricant specified for the lock and environmental condition. Use only sealant compatible with the substrate and prior coatings, and never as a substitute for failed flashing.

E. Preparation — Selection, Verification, and Setup

  1. Select a door from the Chapter 7 service list. Choose only an item that is documented as within the approved minor-maintenance scope.
  2. Confirm the door type and rating. Verify the door is not a fire-rated, smoke-control, garage-separation, egress, high-security, automatic, or specialty assembly requiring a separate procedure.
  3. Review documented conditions. Review the symptoms, water evidence, frame condition, hardware, weatherstripping profile, and manufacturer information recorded in Chapter 7.
  4. Protect adjacent finishes. Protect the floor, threshold, walls, trim, and adjacent finishes before beginning work.
  5. Record baseline operation. Operate the door and record reveals, latch engagement, threshold contact, sweep contact, and lock function.
  6. Confirm the reported symptom is not caused by a referral condition. Verify structural movement, active water entry, frame decay, broken glass, a failed threshold pan, and major distortion are not the cause before proceeding. If any is found, stop and return the item to the Chapter 7 service list for the appropriate referral.

F. Hinge and Frame Alignment

  1. Support the door. Place a wedge, prop, or second person under the door before loosening any hinge hardware.
  2. Tighten sound hinge screws. Tighten progressively without stripping, overdriving, or distorting the jamb.
  3. Repair minor stripped holes only where approved. Install compatible replacement screws only after verifying concealed conditions and required screw length.
  4. Replace a worn hinge only with a matching approved hinge. Match size, thickness, corner, pin, finish, and rating exactly.
  5. Recheck reveal and operation. Confirm reveal and operation are acceptable before altering latch or strike position.

G. Lock, Latch, and Strike Service

  1. Clean latch, deadbolt, and lock components. Use a method compatible with the lock's finish and mechanism.
  2. Apply the specified lubricant. Use only the lubricant specified for the lock and environmental condition. Do not flood cylinders, mix incompatible lubricants, or force a binding lock.
  3. Adjust the strike only after hinge and frame alignment are accepted. Reposition the strike plate as required by the rechecked reveal.
  4. Verify full latch and deadbolt engagement. Confirm both enter the strike fully without placing the door under excessive preload.

H. Weatherstripping, Sweep, and Sealant

  1. Remove failed weatherstripping. Remove without damaging the kerf, stop, frame, or finish.
  2. Clean and repair the mounting surface. Prepare the approved mounting surface for new weatherstripping.
  3. Cut and install replacement weatherstripping. Fit without stretching, bunching, or leaving gaps at corners, in the correct orientation and depth.
  4. Replace or adjust the door sweep. Preserve threshold drainage and required clearance.
  5. Inspect the threshold. Confirm secure attachment, drainage, absence of trip condition, and seal compatibility.
  6. Remove failed exterior sealant only at joints intended for sealant. Do not disturb weeps, vents, laps, or drainage gaps.
  7. Prepare the joint and install backer material where required. Follow the approved sealant system's backer-rod requirement.
  8. Apply compatible sealant. Do not block drainage or rely on sealant to repair failed flashing.
  9. Allow required cure time. Do not operate or expose the joint before the specified cure period.

I. Testing and Closeout

  1. Cycle the door repeatedly. Test latch, deadbolt, key, handle, closer, sweep, and weatherstripping contact through multiple full cycles.
  2. Verify emergency operation. Confirm the door opens for emergency use, closes without slamming, locks securely, and does not bind.
  3. Inspect the completed work. Check fasteners, reveals, compression, threshold, sealant, finish damage, water-management features, and floor clearance.
  4. Document the completed service. Record parts, products, adjustments, results, unresolved causes, and next inspection condition on the maintenance-event form.

Common Service Inspection

Review the completed door using the same checks regardless of which parts of the procedure were required.

  • Cause check: confirm the reported symptom was verified against structural, water, glass, and threshold causes before hardware was adjusted.
  • Hinge check: confirm hinge screws are sound and any replacement hinge matches the original specification.
  • Latch check: confirm the latch and deadbolt enter the strike fully without excessive force.
  • Weatherstripping check: confirm continuous compression at all edges with no corner gaps.
  • Sealant check: confirm sealant appears only at joints intended for sealant, never at a weep, vent, or drainage gap.
  • Cycle check: confirm the door was cycled repeatedly and opens for emergency use, closes without slamming, and does not bind.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Door Still Binds After Hinge Tightening

Probable cause: the binding is caused by frame racking, structural movement, or a swollen slab rather than loose hinges.
Confirmation: recheck reveal at head, hinge jamb, and lock jamb; inspect for structural movement, water damage, or slab swelling not previously identified.
Correction: stop the minor-maintenance procedure and return the door to the Chapter 7 service list for frame repair or qualified evaluation.
Reinspection: do not reattempt hinge or strike adjustment on this door until the underlying cause is corrected.

The Latch Will Not Engage Fully After Strike Adjustment

Probable cause: the strike was adjusted before hinge and frame alignment were accepted, or the hinge work shifted the reveal after the strike was set.
Confirmation: recheck reveal at all three edges and compare to the baseline recorded in Step 5.
Correction: reaccept hinge alignment first, then readjust the strike position without placing the door under excessive preload.
Reinspection: cycle the latch and deadbolt repeatedly and confirm full engagement without lifting or slamming.

Weatherstripping Shows Gaps at the Corners

Probable cause: the replacement weatherstripping was cut short, stretched during installation, or installed at the wrong depth.
Confirmation: inspect each corner with the door closed and compare compression to the manufacturer's installation depth.
Correction: remove and reinstall the affected section, cutting to the correct length and installing at the specified depth without stretching.
Reinspection: confirm continuous compression at every edge and corner with the door closed and locked.

The Lock Still Binds After Lubrication

Probable cause: an incompatible or excessive lubricant was used, or the binding is caused by strike misalignment rather than the lock mechanism.
Confirmation: recheck strike alignment and latch engagement; inspect the lubricant used against the manufacturer specification.
Correction: correct strike alignment first; if the lock still binds, clean the incompatible lubricant and reapply only the specified product.
Reinspection: cycle the key and lock repeatedly and confirm smooth operation without forcing.

Sealant Was Applied Over a Drainage Path

Probable cause: a threshold weep, sill drainage path, or intentional gap was mistaken for a joint intended for sealant.
Confirmation: compare the sealed location to the door and threshold manufacturer's drainage-path documentation.
Correction: remove the sealant from the drainage path and restore the original opening; reapply sealant only at the correct approved joint.
Reinspection: confirm water can pass through the restored drainage path before closing out the project.

Cleanup

This project ends with a fully tested door and a completed record, not a set of loose adjustments.

  • Confirm all tools, fasteners, and hardware packaging are removed from the work area
  • Confirm the door support wedge or prop is removed only after hinges are fully secured
  • Clean floor, threshold, walls, and trim of any adhesive residue, sealant, or lubricant overspray
  • Confirm the sealant cure period is observed before the door is returned to full use if sealant was applied
  • Store the completed maintenance-event form, before-and-after photographs, and any unresolved referral notes in the property record established in Chapter 2

Field Terms

Reveal
The gap between the door slab and surrounding frame, measured at head, hinge jamb, and lock jamb.
Latch Engagement
Full, unforced entry of the latch or deadbolt into the strike.
Threshold Contact
The point at which the sweep or bottom seal meets the threshold across its full width.
Compression Seal
Weatherstripping designed to compress between the slab and frame when the door is closed and locked.
Minor-Maintenance Scope
This chapter's approved limit: hinge, strike, lock, weatherstripping, sweep, and sealant service on a non-rated, non-specialty door.
Referral Condition
A cause outside this chapter's scope, such as structural movement, active water entry, frame decay, broken glass, or a failed threshold pan.
Strike Plate
The frame-mounted component the latch and deadbolt engage when the door is closed and locked.
Backer Rod
Compressible material installed behind sealant to control joint depth and prevent three-sided adhesion.
Drainage Path
A weep, sill channel, or intentional gap that must remain open and unsealed to allow water to exit.
Cure Period
The manufacturer-specified time a sealant or adhesive must be left undisturbed before the joint is exposed or operated.
