Job Overview

This project qualifies you to wire and test one single-pole switch controlling one listed light fixture on a de-energized, previously verified branch circuit. The finished work product is one operating switch-and-fixture assembly: a correctly identified switch loop, a fixture connected and supported according to its listing, a continuous equipment-grounding path, and a documented functional test confirming the light turns on and off reliably from the single switch location.

You will verify the circuit de-energized, identify the incoming supply, switched leg, grounded conductor, and equipment-grounding conductors at both the switch box and the fixture box, splice the grounded conductor continuously to the fixture without landing it on the switch, connect the switch and fixture terminations, mount the switch and fixture, restore power under the approved sequence, and cycle the switch to confirm correct, flicker-free operation.

This project builds directly on the box installation of Chapters 3 and 4, the cable routing of Chapter 6, the splicing and pigtailing of Chapter 7, and the grounding and bonding work of Chapter 8. It prepares you for the three-way switching circuit in Chapter 12, which extends this same switch-loop and fixture-wiring discipline to two switch locations.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • EL-061 — Wire a switch loop, correctly identifying the incoming supply and switched-leg conductors at the switch box.
  • EL-062 — Connect fixture conductors, splicing the grounded conductor continuously and landing the switched leg on the fixture's ungrounded lead.
  • EL-063 — Mount the light fixture using a box and support listed for the fixture's weight and type.
  • EL-064 — Install the switch device, terminating conductors using listed terminal methods and mounting the switch square to the box.
  • EL-065 — Test switching operation, confirming correct switching, fixture operation, and absence of flicker after restoration of power.
  • EL-066 — Inspect device and fixture boxes for correct grounding, box fill, splice quality, and support before and after energization.

Where This Skill Fits

Every switch-controlled lighting circuit in a home depends on the same switch-loop discipline taught here: identify the supply and switched leg correctly, keep the grounded conductor off the ordinary single-pole switch, and maintain a continuous equipment-grounding path through both boxes. The mistake most often responsible for a switch that appears to work but fails inspection or causes an intermittent fault is confusing the switched-leg conductor with the incoming supply, or landing a neutral on a switch terminal by assumption rather than by verified identification.

The de-energized termination and controlled-restoration sequence used here is the same sequence used for every device chapter in this book, and the switch-loop identification skill carries forward directly into the two-location switching taught in Chapter 12.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • identifying the incoming supply, switched leg, grounded conductor, and equipment-grounding conductors at the switch box and the fixture box
  • splicing the grounded conductor continuously to the fixture without landing it on an ordinary single-pole switch
  • connecting the switch and fixture terminations using listed connectors and terminal methods
  • bonding metal boxes and connecting all equipment-grounding conductors and fixture grounding parts
  • mounting the switch and a listed, adequately supported light fixture
  • restoring power under the approved sequence and testing switching and fixture operation

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • work on the switch loop or fixture conductors before the circuit has been verified de-energized using the live-dead-live procedure from Chapter 1
  • install a fixture heavier than the box and support are listed to carry, or substitute a standard lighting box where a fan-rated box is required
  • energize the circuit before the required inspection stage for this chapter's work has been completed
  • continue past any stop-work condition identified in the Failure Points section without correcting or escalating it

Any switch, fixture, box, or conductor condition that fails verification in this chapter must be corrected, repaired, or escalated to a qualified professional before work continues.

Trade Note

A switch loop is only two conductors doing two different jobs — the incoming supply and the switched leg — plus the grounded and equipment-grounding conductors that pass through untouched by the switch. Confusing which conductor is which, or assuming a white conductor is always neutral, is the single most common source of a switch that appears to work intermittently or fails a later continuity check.

Setup Standard

Do not remove any switch box or fixture box cover, and do not touch any conductor, until the circuit has been verified de-energized using the live-dead-live procedure established in Chapter 1. Do not restore power until all splices, terminations, grounding connections, switch mounting, and fixture mounting are complete, inspected, and the required inspection stage for this chapter's work is passed.

Shop Tip

Label the incoming supply and switched-leg conductors the moment the switch box is opened, before doing anything else. Reidentifying them later, after conductors have been folded into the box, is where switch loops most often get reversed.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every switch-and-fixture project in this book depends on correctly separating two jobs performed by the ungrounded conductors: bringing power to the switch, and carrying power onward to the fixture only when the switch is closed. The grounded conductor and equipment-grounding conductor pass through both boxes without being interrupted by the switch itself.

Conductor Identification and Grounding Continuity

Before splicing or terminating any conductor, identify the incoming supply, switched leg, grounded conductor, and equipment-grounding conductors at both the switch box and the fixture box, using the approved wiring diagram and, where permitted, reidentification methods required by the wiring method. Splice the grounded conductor continuously from source to fixture neutral. Do not place the grounded conductor on an ordinary single-pole switch terminal at any point in this project.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Conductor Identity Before Splicing

Confirm the incoming supply and switched-leg conductors against the approved wiring diagram before making any splice or termination. Do not rely on cable jacket color, conductor position in the box, or assumption from a similar past job as proof of a conductor's function.

Critical Safety Cue — Verify De-Energization Before Any Conductor Is Touched

Do not remove the switch box or fixture box cover, and do not touch any conductor, until the circuit has been verified de-energized using the live-dead-live test established in Chapter 1. Treat every conductor as energized until that test proves otherwise.

Field Safety Cue — Ladder and Fixture-Handling Setup

Establish a stable ladder or platform setup before working at the fixture box, and support the fixture independently of the conductors while making connections. A fixture left hanging by its conductors can stress terminations and create a later intermittent fault.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Fixture Weight and Lamp Rating

Install only the fixture, mounting bracket, and lamp type specified by the fixture's listing, and confirm the box and support are listed for the fixture's weight before mounting. A fixture heavier than its support is listed to carry, or a lamp exceeding the fixture's rated wattage, is a stop-work condition until corrected.

E. Plan Review and De-Energization

  1. Review the approved wiring diagram, cable types, conductor functions, fixture rating, box support, switch rating, grounding, and circuit protection. Confirm the diagram identifies every conductor by function before proceeding.
  2. Verify the circuit is de-energized using the approved live-dead-live procedure. Do not proceed until the tester has been verified, the target conductors tested, and the tester reverified per Chapter 1.

F. Box and Conductor Inspection

  1. Inspect the switch box, fixture box, cable routes, conductors, grounding path, and box fill. Confirm both boxes remain within their listed volume once devices, pigtails, and connectors are accounted for.
  2. Identify the incoming supply, switched leg, grounded conductor, and equipment-grounding conductors. Confirm identification against the approved wiring diagram at both boxes.
  3. Reidentify conductors where permitted and required by the approved wiring method. Mark or label reidentified conductors so their function remains clear through the remainder of the project.

G. Grounding Preparation and Neutral Continuity

  1. Prepare grounding pigtails and bond metal boxes. Confirm each metal box has a listed grounding screw, clip, or bonding device installed per Chapter 8.
  2. Splice grounded conductors continuously to the fixture neutral without placing the neutral on the ordinary single-pole switch. Confirm the splice using a listed connector before proceeding to switch termination.

H. Switch Termination

  1. Connect the incoming ungrounded conductor to one switch terminal. Confirm this is the identified supply conductor, not the switched leg.
  2. Connect the switched-leg conductor to the other switch terminal. Confirm this conductor runs to the fixture box and not to another load.
  3. Connect the switch grounding terminal. Use the device-grounding pigtail prepared for the switch box.

I. Fixture Termination

  1. At the fixture box, connect the switched conductor to the fixture ungrounded lead. Confirm the switched-leg identification matches the conductor landed at the switch.
  2. Connect the grounded conductor to the fixture neutral lead. Confirm this connection completes the continuous neutral splice established in Section G.
  3. Connect all equipment-grounding conductors and fixture grounding parts. Confirm the fixture's grounding screw or lead is bonded to the box grounding system.
  4. Use listed connectors and terminal methods for the conductor types. Do not exceed a connector's listed conductor range or reuse a single-use connector.

J. Pre-Enclosure Inspection

  1. Inspect all splices and terminations before folding conductors into boxes. Confirm no exposed copper, loose strands, or insulation trapped under a terminal at either box.

K. Switch and Fixture Mounting

  1. Mount the switch square to the box and install the cover plate. Confirm the cover plate sits flush with no gap exposing energized parts.
  2. Verify that the fixture box and support are listed for the fixture weight and type. Confirm this before installing the mounting bracket.
  3. Install the fixture mounting bracket using the listed hardware. Do not substitute unlisted screws or brackets.
  4. Mount the fixture without trapping conductors or insulation. Fold conductors so the fixture seats fully against the bracket or ceiling surface.
  5. Install only the approved lamp type and rating. Confirm the lamp does not exceed the fixture's rated wattage.

L. Inspection, Restoration, and Functional Testing

  1. Complete the required inspection before energization. Confirm every item on the Switch and Fixture Wiring Schedule meets its target condition.
  2. Restore power and operate the switch through repeated cycles. Use the approved restoration sequence and cycle the switch on and off at least three times.
  3. Verify correct switching, fixture operation, absence of flicker, and secure mounting. Confirm the switch consistently controls the fixture with no delay, flicker, or unexpected behavior.
  4. De-energize immediately for abnormal heat, arcing, breaker operation, or intermittent function. Treat any of these as a stop-work condition requiring correction before retesting.
  5. Document the circuit diagram and test results. Complete every field on the Switch and Fixture Wiring Worksheet.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed switch-and-fixture assembly using the same checks regardless of the room or fixture type involved.

  • Conductor-identification check: confirm the incoming supply and switched-leg conductors match the approved wiring diagram at both boxes.
  • Grounding check: confirm the grounded conductor is spliced continuously to the fixture neutral and never landed on the switch.
  • Bonding check: confirm metal boxes, the switch yoke, and fixture grounding parts are bonded to a continuous equipment-grounding path.
  • Termination check: confirm no exposed copper, loose strands, or insulation trapped under any terminal.
  • Support check: confirm the fixture box and support are listed for the installed fixture's weight and type.
  • Functional check: confirm the switch reliably turns the fixture on and off with no flicker or abnormal heat.
  • Documentation check: confirm the Switch and Fixture Wiring Worksheet is fully completed and signed.

Complete the Switch and Fixture Wiring Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 12.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Fixture Does Not Light When the Switch Is Closed

Probable cause: the switched-leg and incoming-supply conductors were reversed at the switch, a splice is loose, or the lamp is defective.
Confirmation: de-energize and verify absence of voltage, then inspect the switch terminations against the approved diagram and check the lamp and fixture connections.
Correction: correct the switch termination if conductors were reversed, re-make any loose splice using a listed connector, or replace a defective lamp.
Reinspection: repeat the live-dead-live test, re-terminate as needed, restore power, and retest switching operation.

The Fixture Flickers or Operates Intermittently

Probable cause: a loose terminal, an undersized or damaged splice, or a conductor stressed during folding into the box.
Confirmation: de-energize and verify absence of voltage, then inspect every termination and splice at the switch and fixture boxes for looseness or damage.
Correction: re-terminate or re-splice the affected conductor using the manufacturer-controlled method and torque, and fold conductors into the box without stress.
Reinspection: repeat the live-dead-live test, restore power, and cycle the switch to confirm flicker-free operation.

The Breaker Trips When the Switch Is Operated

Probable cause: the grounded conductor was inadvertently landed on a switch terminal, or an ungrounded and grounded conductor are in unintended contact at one of the boxes.
Confirmation: de-energize and verify absence of voltage, then inspect the switch box and fixture box for a grounded conductor connected to a switch terminal or evidence of contact between conductors.
Correction: correct the wiring error, re-insulate or re-splice any conductor in unintended contact with another, and confirm proper conductor identification before restoring power.
Reinspection: repeat the live-dead-live test, restore power, and retest switching operation before considering the circuit complete.

The Fixture Feels Loose or the Box Shows Signs of Overload

Probable cause: the fixture exceeds the box or support's listed weight rating, or the mounting bracket was not fully engaged.
Confirmation: de-energize, support the fixture independently, and inspect the box, support, and bracket engagement against the fixture's listed weight.
Correction: install a fixture-rated box or support if the current box is inadequate, or correctly engage the mounting bracket per its instructions.
Reinspection: repeat the support verification and functional test before leaving the fixture in service.

Grounding Continuity Fails at the De-Energized Continuity Check

Probable cause: a missing or loose grounding pigtail, an unbonded metal box, or a fixture grounding lead not connected.
Confirmation: de-energize and verify absence of voltage, then trace the equipment-grounding path from the fixture through the switch box to the branch circuit's grounding system.
Correction: install or re-terminate the missing pigtail, bonding screw, or fixture ground connection using listed hardware.
Reinspection: repeat the de-energized continuity check before restoring power.
