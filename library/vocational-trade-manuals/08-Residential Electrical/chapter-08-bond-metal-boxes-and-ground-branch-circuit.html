Job Overview

This project qualifies you to complete and verify the equipment-grounding and metal-box bonding path for one de-energized branch-circuit assembly. The finished work product is one metal box, correctly bonded with a listed grounding screw or clip, containing a joined equipment-grounding splice, a box-bonding pigtail, and a device-grounding pigtail where required, with continuity verified from the device yoke and metal box back to the branch equipment-grounding path by a de-energized continuity test.

You will identify each equipment-grounding conductor and distinguish it from the grounded neutral conductor; install a listed grounding screw, clip, or bonding device in the metal box; join incoming and outgoing equipment-grounding conductors; add box-bonding and device-grounding pigtails; terminate them at the correct points; keep the neutral conductor isolated from the box and grounding path; and verify continuity with an approved meter before reassembling the box.

This project builds directly on the box installation of Chapters 3 and 4, the connectors of Chapter 5, the cable of Chapter 6, and the splicing and pigtailing of Chapter 7. It is a prerequisite for every device installed later in this book — the duplex receptacle of Chapter 9, the GFCI receptacle of Chapter 10, the switches and fixtures of Chapters 11 through 15, and the Chapter 20 capstone all depend on a metal box that has already been correctly bonded and grounded using this exact procedure.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • EL-043 — Identify each equipment-grounding conductor in a branch-circuit assembly and distinguish it from the grounded neutral conductor.
  • EL-044 — Install a listed grounding screw, grounding clip, or approved bonding device in a metal box.
  • EL-045 — Bond a metal box by joining incoming and outgoing equipment-grounding conductors with an approved connector and pigtail.
  • EL-046 — Attach device-grounding pigtails at the green grounding terminal without exceeding the terminal's listed conductor limit.
  • EL-047 — Verify grounding continuity from the device yoke and metal box to the branch equipment-grounding path using a de-energized continuity test.
  • EL-048 — Inspect the completed grounding path for isolation from the neutral conductor, correct termination, and continuity.

Where This Skill Fits

The grounding and bonding work performed here is what makes a metal box, a device, and every later fixture in this book safe to touch under a fault condition. A box with cable connectors seated and conductors spliced, as completed in Chapters 5 through 7, is not yet a safe assembly until its metal parts are bonded and its equipment-grounding path is proven continuous. Every receptacle, switch, fixture, fan, and exterior device installed in Chapters 9 through 15 terminates its grounding conductor at the pigtail and grounding screw installed in this chapter.

The de-energized continuity test taught here is the same verification method used again in the Chapter 17 diagnostic project, where an open or high-resistance grounding path is one of the conditions a learner must be able to recognize and correct.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • identifying equipment-grounding conductors and distinguishing them from grounded neutral conductors in a de-energized assembly
  • installing a listed grounding screw, grounding clip, or bonding device in a metal box
  • joining incoming and outgoing equipment-grounding conductors with an approved connector
  • adding and terminating box-bonding and device-grounding pigtails
  • keeping neutral conductors isolated from the metal box and equipment-grounding path downstream of the service bonding point
  • performing a de-energized continuity test to verify the completed grounding path

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • perform any part of this procedure on an energized circuit
  • use a sheet-metal screw, device mounting screw, or any unlisted fastener as the grounding connection
  • install a neutral-to-ground jumper or bootleg ground under any circumstance
  • energize the assembly before an open, high-resistance, or uncertain grounding path has been investigated and corrected

Any grounding path that fails the continuity test in this chapter must be corrected before the box is reassembled or the assigned device is installed.

Trade Note

A bootleg ground — a jumper connecting the neutral terminal to the grounding terminal or the metal box in place of a real equipment-grounding path — can make a plug-in tester show a false "correctly wired" result while leaving the box and device frame energized under a fault. This chapter's continuity test is designed to catch that condition, which a simple polarity tester cannot detect.

Setup Standard

Do not begin this project until the circuit has been de-energized and verified using the live-dead-live procedure from Chapter 1. Do not install a neutral-to-ground jumper or bootleg ground at any point, and do not use a sheet-metal screw, device mounting screw, or unlisted fastener as the grounding connection. Work started before de-energization is verified must stop immediately.

Shop Tip

Arrange grounding conductors so the box and device remain bonded even when the device is removed from the box, where the design requires it. A grounding path that depends on the device being physically present is not a continuous path — it is a path that disappears the next time someone pulls the device out for service.

Distinguishing Equipment-Grounding Conductors from Neutral Conductors

Identify each conductor from the approved circuit documentation before touching any terminal. The equipment-grounding conductor is bare or green-insulated and carries fault current back to the source; it is never the same conductor as the grounded neutral, and the two must remain isolated from each other downstream of the service bonding point. Confusing the two, or bonding them together at a device box, creates a bootleg ground and a false-safe condition.

Critical Safety Cue — Never Install a Neutral-to-Ground Jumper or Bootleg Ground

Do not connect the neutral conductor to the grounding terminal, the metal box, or the equipment-grounding conductor at any point downstream of the service bonding point. A bootleg ground can allow a plug-in tester to show a false "correctly wired" result while leaving the box and device frame energized under a fault condition. This is a stop-work condition if discovered on existing wiring; correct it before continuing.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Grounding Screws, Clips, and Connectors

Use only a listed green grounding screw, grounding clip, or bonding device manufactured for equipment grounding. Never substitute a sheet-metal screw, a device mounting screw, or any unlisted fastener as the grounding connection. Follow the connector manufacturer's instructions for conductor range, stripping length, and installation method.

Measurement Cue — Verify Continuity, Not Just Connection

A grounding conductor that is connected but not tested has not been verified. Perform the de-energized continuity test using an approved meter and isolated test conditions, confirming continuity from the device yoke and metal box all the way to the branch equipment-grounding path. Investigate and correct any open, high-resistance, or uncertain reading before energization.

Field Safety Cue — Work Only on a Verified De-Energized Circuit

Confirm the circuit is de-energized using the live-dead-live procedure before removing any cover or touching any conductor. Re-verify de-energization if work is interrupted and resumed, or if there is any doubt about which circuit is present at the box.

E. Design Review and Conductor Identification

  1. Review the approved grounding design, wiring method, conductor material and size, box type, device type, and upstream grounding path. Record this information on the Grounding and Bonding Verification Worksheet before proceeding.
  2. Verify the circuit is de-energized using the approved test procedure. Use the live-dead-live sequence from Chapter 1 at the target conductors before touching any splice or terminal.
  3. Identify each equipment-grounding conductor and distinguish it from grounded neutral conductors. Confirm identification against the approved circuit documentation before proceeding.

F. Box Inspection and Grounding Hardware Installation

  1. Inspect metal boxes, connectors, raceways, grounding screws, clips, bushings, and devices for listed grounding provisions. Confirm the box has a designated, undamaged grounding hole or slot.
  2. Remove paint, debris, or corrosion only from designated bonding locations. Do not scrape or damage surfaces outside the designated bonding point.
  3. Install the listed green grounding screw, grounding clip, or approved bonding device in the metal box. Seat it fully per the manufacturer's instructions.
  4. Do not use a sheet-metal screw, device mounting screw, or unlisted fastener as the grounding connection. Verify this before continuing to the next step.

G. Grounding Splice and Pigtail Installation

  1. Join incoming and outgoing equipment-grounding conductors with the approved connector. Confirm the connector is listed for the exact conductor count and material present.
  2. Add a box-bonding pigtail of the correct material and size. Include it in the same grounding splice as the incoming and outgoing conductors.
  3. Add a separate device-grounding pigtail where required. Confirm whether the project design requires a pigtail independent of the box-bonding pigtail before terminating either.

H. Termination and Neutral Isolation

  1. Terminate the box pigtail under the listed grounding screw or device. Confirm the connection is tight and the conductor is fully seated.
  2. Terminate the device pigtail at the green grounding terminal without placing more conductors under the terminal than listed. Check the terminal's listed conductor limit before terminating.
  3. Keep neutral conductors isolated from the metal box and equipment-grounding path downstream of the service bonding point. Confirm no neutral conductor terminates at the grounding screw, box, or grounding splice.
  4. Inspect metal cable connectors and raceway fittings for required bonding continuity. Confirm each connector and fitting maintains a continuous metallic path where the design requires it.
  5. Arrange grounding conductors so they remain connected when the device is removed where required by the design. Confirm the box-bonding pigtail is independent of the device-grounding pigtail where this is required.

I. Continuity Verification and Reassembly

  1. Perform a de-energized continuity test using an approved meter and isolated test conditions. Confirm the circuit remains de-energized and disconnected from other sources before testing.
  2. Confirm continuity from device yoke and metal box to the branch equipment-grounding path. Record the measured result on the worksheet.
  3. Investigate and correct open, high-resistance, or uncertain paths before energization. Do not proceed until the corrected path retests successfully.
  4. Do not install a neutral-to-ground jumper or bootleg ground. Confirm this a second time before closing the box.
  5. Reassemble the box and install the cover or assigned device. Fold conductors into the box without stressing the grounding splice or pigtails.
  6. Document grounding components, continuity results, and corrections. Complete every field on the Grounding and Bonding Verification Worksheet before beginning the next project.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed grounding assembly using the same checks regardless of which box, device, or circuit the project involves.

  • Identification check: confirm every equipment-grounding conductor was correctly identified and kept isolated from grounded neutral conductors.
  • Bonding check: confirm a listed grounding screw, clip, or bonding device is installed in the metal box, with no unlisted fastener substituted.
  • Splice check: confirm incoming, outgoing, and pigtail grounding conductors are joined with a listed connector, correctly seated with no exposed copper beyond the connector.
  • Termination check: confirm the box pigtail is terminated at the grounding screw or device, and the device pigtail is terminated at the green terminal without exceeding its listed conductor limit.
  • Isolation check: confirm no neutral-to-ground jumper or bootleg ground is present anywhere in the assembly.
  • Continuity check: confirm the de-energized continuity test shows an unbroken path from the device yoke and metal box to the branch equipment-grounding path.
  • Documentation check: confirm the Grounding and Bonding Verification Worksheet is fully completed and signed.

Complete the Grounding and Bonding Verification Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 09.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Continuity Test Shows an Open Path

Probable cause: a loose or missing grounding screw, an unjoined equipment-grounding conductor, a broken pigtail, or a connector that failed to seat.
Confirmation: re-verify the circuit is de-energized, then inspect the grounding screw, splice, and each pigtail termination one at a time.
Correction: reseat or replace the grounding screw, re-splice the equipment-grounding conductors with a new listed connector, and reterminate any loose pigtail.
Reinspection: repeat the de-energized continuity test from the device yoke and metal box to the branch equipment-grounding path.

The Continuity Test Shows High Resistance

Probable cause: corrosion or paint at the bonding point, a loosely tightened grounding screw, or a connector rated for a different conductor range than what is installed.
Confirmation: inspect the bonding surface for paint, corrosion, or debris, and check connector listing against the actual conductor count and size.
Correction: clean the designated bonding location, retighten the grounding screw to a firm, fully seated condition, or replace the connector with one listed for the present conductors.
Reinspection: repeat the continuity test and confirm a low-resistance result.

A Bootleg Ground Is Discovered on Existing Wiring

Probable cause: a prior installation connected the neutral terminal to the grounding terminal or metal box instead of running a real equipment-grounding conductor.
Confirmation: trace the neutral and grounding conductors separately back toward the source and confirm whether they are joined at any point downstream of the service bonding point.
Correction: remove the improper jumper immediately and install a correct equipment-grounding conductor, splice, and pigtail per this chapter's procedure; treat the discovery as a stop-work condition until corrected.
Reinspection: repeat the de-energized continuity test and confirm the neutral and grounding paths remain isolated from each other.

The Device Loses Its Ground When Removed from the Box

Probable cause: the device-grounding pigtail was omitted, or the device was wired as the only path between the box bonding and the branch grounding conductor.
Confirmation: inspect whether the box-bonding pigtail is independently connected to the grounding splice or depends on the device being present.
Correction: install a separate box-bonding pigtail terminated at the grounding screw, independent of the device-grounding pigtail, per the approved design.
Reinspection: repeat the continuity test with the device disconnected from the box to confirm the box remains bonded independently.

A Grounding Screw or Clip Will Not Seat Fully

Probable cause: a damaged or wrong-size grounding screw, a stripped hole, or debris in the designated bonding location.
Confirmation: inspect the screw, clip, and bonding hole for damage, incorrect size, or obstruction.
Correction: replace the grounding screw or clip with the correct listed part for the box, and clear any obstruction from the bonding location before reinstalling.
Reinspection: confirm the grounding screw or clip seats fully and repeat the continuity test.

Cleanup

This project ends with the grounding and bonding path complete, tested, and either covered or ready for the assigned device installation.

  • Confirm the metal box remains de-energized until the assigned device or cover installation and required inspection are complete
  • Store the continuity-capable multimeter and hand tools in a protected, undamaged condition
  • Confirm all grounding splices and pigtails remain seated and undisturbed inside the box
  • Store unused grounding screws, clips, pigtail stock, and connectors for the next assigned project
  • Store the Grounding and Bonding Verification Worksheet with the Chapter 1 Electrical Safety-Readiness Worksheet for reference by later projects
