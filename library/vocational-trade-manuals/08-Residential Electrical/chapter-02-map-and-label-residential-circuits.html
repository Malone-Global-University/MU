Job Overview

This project qualifies you to identify and record which branch circuit serves each receptacle, switch, fixture, and fixed load in a selected residential area, using controlled breaker operation and a listed plug-in tester with all covers left installed. The finished work product is one completed room-by-room circuit map, one legible and accurate panel directory, and one written list of any load that could not be confidently assigned to a circuit.

You will label every accessible outlet, switch, lighting point, and fixed load on a floor-plan worksheet; open one candidate breaker at a time while a partner watches a plug-in tester or lamp at the test location; confirm each finding with at least one additional device where practical; note GFCI-protected, AFCI-protected, multiwire, and shared-neutral indications without opening any device; and replace the existing panel directory with an accurate, location-specific one.

This project builds directly on the isolation and verification discipline from Project 01, but its scope is intentionally narrower and stays energized. Chapter 2 permits energized plug-in observation only with intact covers and no exposed conductors anywhere in the sequence. Any panel-cover removal or exposed energized diagnostic work is outside this chapter's scope and belongs to the de-energized procedure taught in Project 01 and used again beginning in Project 3.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • EL-007 — Identify branch circuits serving a selected residential area using controlled breaker operation without exposing energized conductors.
  • EL-008 — Trace receptacles and lighting points to their correct branch circuit using a listed plug-in tester or identification lamp.
  • EL-009 — Use a plug-in tester to test accessible receptacles for polarity, ground, and GFCI indications with covers intact.
  • EL-010 — Label breakers accurately and legibly on the panel directory to match confirmed field findings.
  • EL-011 — Document circuit locations, unresolved conditions, and testing method for the mapped area.
  • EL-012 — Create a complete, accurate circuit directory and room-by-room circuit map for the selected residential area.

Where This Skill Fits

An accurate circuit directory is the reference every later project in this book depends on to identify the correct breaker before applying the Project 01 shutdown-and-verification sequence. A mislabeled or outdated directory is one of the most common causes of opening the wrong breaker, which then leads directly into the failure conditions described in Project 01's troubleshooting section — a tester unexpectedly showing voltage after the identified breaker is opened.

This chapter also introduces the plug-in receptacle tester, which reappears at the end of Project 9 and Project 10 as the final operational test for a newly installed duplex or GFCI receptacle.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • labeling and numbering every accessible outlet, switch, lighting point, and fixed load in the selected area on a floor-plan worksheet
  • opening one candidate breaker at a time, with all device and panel covers left installed, to identify which loads lose power together
  • confirming each finding with at least one additional receptacle, switch, or fixture where practical
  • testing accessible receptacles with a listed plug-in tester while covers remain installed
  • identifying GFCI-protected downstream outlets using the device's own test button and AFCI or dual-function indications from listed breaker markings and test buttons, without removing the panel cover
  • comparing findings with the existing directory and producing an accurate, legible replacement directory and circuit map

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • remove a panel cover, deadfront, device cover, or any other cover to trace a circuit
  • touch, probe, or work on any exposed energized conductor at any point in this project
  • interrupt power to a load identified as critical without following the special procedure for that load
  • leave a breaker in a state other than its original position once each test is complete
  • resolve a multiwire, tied-breaker, or unexpected cross-circuit indication independently — these findings are recorded for qualified review, not corrected in this chapter

Any breaker, receptacle, or circuit condition that cannot be confidently identified using the methods in this chapter must be recorded as unresolved and referred to a qualified electrical professional.

Trade Note

This is the only chapter in the book where the circuit remains energized throughout the procedure. That is permitted only because every test in this chapter is performed with covers installed, using listed plug-in equipment designed for that purpose. The moment a cover would need to come off to answer a question, the question belongs to a de-energized project, not this one.

Setup Standard

Do not remove a panel cover, deadfront, or device cover at any point in this project. Every finding in this chapter is obtained with covers installed, using a listed plug-in tester at the receptacle end and controlled breaker operation at the panel end. If answering a question would require removing a cover, stop and record the condition as unresolved.

Shop Tip

Keep one person permanently stationed at the test location and one person permanently stationed at the panel for the entire mapping session. Switching roles partway through is the most common cause of a mislabeled breaker, because the person at the panel loses track of which breaker was just returned to its original position.

Common Field Fundamentals

Circuit mapping in this chapter always follows the same energized-but-covered pattern: label every load first, then step through breakers one at a time while a partner watches a plug-in device, confirming each result before moving on. The panel directory produced at the end is only as reliable as the discipline used to build it.

Controlled Breaker Operation and Critical Loads

Every breaker operated in this chapter is one candidate breaker at a time, with the test-location observer and panel operator in direct communication. Before any breaker is opened, confirm which loads in the mapping area are critical and must not be interrupted without a special procedure — a refrigerator, freezer, sump pump, medical device, security system, or similar load. Return every non-controlling breaker to its original position before testing the next candidate; leaving multiple breakers open at once destroys the one-variable-at-a-time logic this method depends on.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Every Finding at a Second Point

Confirm each circuit assignment using at least one additional device or outlet where practical before recording it as final. A single plug-in tester result at one receptacle can be misleading if that receptacle is fed through an unusual splice, a shared neutral, or a multiwire branch circuit.

Critical Safety Cue — Covers Stay Installed Throughout This Chapter

Do not remove a panel cover, deadfront, or device cover at any point in this chapter. Chapter 2 permits energized plug-in observation only with intact covers and no exposed conductors. Any question that can only be answered by removing a cover is outside this project's scope and must wait for the de-energized procedure in Project 01.

Field Safety Cue — Communication Between Panel and Test Location

Maintain direct communication between the person at the panel and the person at the test location for the entire session. Confirm out loud, or by radio, which breaker is about to be operated and confirm its return to the original position before moving to the next candidate.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Plug-In Tester and Identification Lamp Use

Use the plug-in circuit-identification tester, identification lamp, and receptacle tester according to their listed instructions. A tester that gives an unclear or inconsistent indication is a stop-work condition for that trial — repeat the test or record the finding as unresolved rather than guessing at the result.

E. Mapping-Area Definition and Documentation Review

  1. Define the rooms, fixtures, receptacles, appliances, and panels included in the mapping project. Record the boundary in writing before proceeding to Step 2.
  2. Obtain owner authorization and review the existing directory and electrical plans. Note any information that suggests likely circuit groupings or flags a prior issue.
  3. Identify critical loads that must not be interrupted without special procedures. Record each one and its required special procedure, such as a temporary generator or a monitored bypass, before testing begins.

F. Load Labeling and Inspection

  1. Inspect receptacles, switches, fixtures, and covers for damage before testing. Record any damaged device and exclude it from energized testing until it is repaired or replaced.
  2. Number or label each accessible outlet, switch, lighting point, and fixed load on a floor-plan worksheet. Complete this labeling for the entire mapping area before any breaker is operated.

G. Controlled Breaker Trials

  1. Plug the listed circuit-identification lamp or plug-in tester into the first receptacle. Confirm the tester indicates power before proceeding.
  2. Have one person remain at the test location and one authorized person operate breakers where practical. Maintain direct communication for the entire session.
  3. Open one candidate breaker at a time and verify the affected load. Confirm the observed result before moving to the next candidate.
  4. Return noncontrolling breakers to their original state before continuing. Confirm each returned breaker before opening the next candidate.
  5. Confirm the circuit using at least one additional device or outlet where practical. Record the confirming location alongside the original test point.
  6. Record all receptacles, lights, smoke alarms, appliances, exterior outlets, and other loads that lose power together. Complete this record for the current candidate breaker before proceeding to the next one.

H. Energized Receptacle and Protection-Device Testing

  1. Test accessible receptacles with a listed plug-in tester while covers remain installed. Do not remove a receptacle or device cover to test it.
  2. Record indicated polarity, ground, or GFCI concerns without opening energized devices. Note the tester's exact indication rather than an assumed cause.
  3. Identify GFCI-protected downstream outlets using the device test procedure where authorized. Use only the device's own test and reset buttons for this check.
  4. Identify AFCI or dual-function protection from listed breaker markings and test buttons without removing the panel cover. Record the marking and test-button result exactly as observed.
  5. Note multiwire, tied-breaker, or unexpected cross-circuit indications for qualified review. Do not attempt to resolve these conditions independently in this chapter.

I. Repetition, Comparison, and Directory Update

  1. Repeat the mapping procedure until every included load is assigned or marked unresolved. Confirm the floor-plan worksheet has no blank entries before continuing.
  2. Compare actual loads with the existing panel directory. Record every discrepancy found.
  3. Prepare a concise, location-specific circuit description for each mapped breaker. Use room and fixture names rather than vague descriptions.
  4. Replace or update the directory using durable, legible labeling without covering required equipment markings. Confirm every required panel marking remains visible after labeling.

J. Closeout Documentation and Restoration

  1. Prepare a room-by-room circuit map and unresolved-condition list. Complete this as a standalone document referencing the floor-plan worksheet.
  2. Restore all intended circuits and verify critical loads. Confirm every critical load identified in Step 3 is operating normally before ending the session.
  3. Document test dates, tester type, directory changes, and conditions requiring electrician review. Complete every field before the project is considered closed.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed mapping session using the same checks regardless of the size of the area mapped.

  • Coverage check: confirm every accessible outlet, switch, lighting point, and fixed load in the mapping area is assigned to a breaker or listed as unresolved.
  • Confirmation check: confirm every assignment was tested at a second device or outlet where practical.
  • Critical-load check: confirm every identified critical load remained protected or was interrupted only under its special procedure.
  • Restoration check: confirm every non-controlling breaker was returned to its original position after each trial.
  • Protection-indication check: confirm GFCI and AFCI indications were recorded using only device test buttons and listed markings.
  • Directory check: confirm the replacement directory is legible, accurate, and does not cover a required equipment marking.
  • Scope check: confirm no panel cover, deadfront, or device cover was removed at any point in the project.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Two Test Locations Give Conflicting Results for the Same Breaker

Probable cause: a multiwire branch circuit, a shared neutral, a tied breaker, or an unrecorded cross-connection between circuits.
Confirmation: repeat the trial at both test locations, confirming each breaker is returned to its original position between trials, and check the breaker for a handle tie or shared-neutral marking.
Correction: do not attempt to resolve the condition independently; record it as an unresolved cross-circuit condition with both test locations and breaker identifiers, and refer it for qualified review.
Reinspection: confirm the unresolved-condition entry is complete on the circuit map before continuing to the next candidate breaker.

A Critical Load Loses Power Unexpectedly During a Trial

Probable cause: the critical load was not correctly identified before testing began, or it shares a breaker with an unrelated area of the mapping project.
Confirmation: confirm which candidate breaker was open at the time and verify the affected load against the Step 3 critical-load list.
Correction: immediately return the breaker to its original position and confirm the critical load has resumed normal operation before continuing; update the critical-load list and apply its special procedure before retesting that breaker.
Reinspection: repeat the trial only after the special procedure for the critical load is in place.

The Plug-In Tester or Identification Lamp Gives an Unclear Indication

Probable cause: a damaged tester, a loose receptacle connection, or a receptacle fed by more than one source.
Confirmation: retest the same location with a second listed tester and inspect the receptacle for looseness or damage using only external observation with the cover intact.
Correction: if the indication remains unclear, do not record a final assignment for that location; mark it unresolved and note the specific inconsistency observed.
Reinspection: repeat the trial once a functioning tester is confirmed, or refer the location for de-energized inspection under Project 01 procedures.

A GFCI or AFCI Indication Cannot Be Obtained Without Removing a Cover

Probable cause: the device's own test button is missing, damaged, or unresponsive, or the breaker marking is worn and illegible.
Confirmation: attempt the device's test and reset buttons again and inspect the breaker marking under adequate light without removing the panel cover.
Correction: do not remove the cover to confirm protection type; record the device or breaker as an unresolved protection-identification condition for qualified review.
Reinspection: confirm the unresolved entry is complete before continuing to the next device.

The Replacement Directory Does Not Match the Circuit Map

Probable cause: a labeling error made while transferring confirmed findings from the floor-plan worksheet to the directory card.
Confirmation: compare the directory card entry for each breaker against the corresponding confirmed entry on the circuit map.
Correction: correct the directory label to match the confirmed field finding; do not adjust the circuit map to match an unconfirmed directory entry.
Reinspection: repeat the side-by-side comparison for the full directory before closing the project.
