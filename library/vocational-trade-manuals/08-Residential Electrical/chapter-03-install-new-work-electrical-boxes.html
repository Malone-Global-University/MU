Job Overview

This project qualifies you to lay out and install new-work device and lighting boxes in open wood framing before any cable is run. The finished work product is one coordinated set of new-work boxes, mounted at the approved heights and centerlines, set to the correct depth relative to the future finished-wall surface, fastened to the framing without distortion, and sized with sufficient marked volume for the conductors, clamps, devices, and grounding they will hold.

You will review the approved electrical plan and select listed boxes for the wiring method, device type, conductor volume, and support requirement; calculate and confirm box volume; establish the finished-floor and finished-wall reference; mark each box centerline and mounting height; position and fasten each box to the stud or approved support; install support bars and fan-rated systems where specified; mark planned cable entries; and inspect every box for height, setback, alignment, support, volume, and damage before rough wiring begins.

This project depends on the isolation and safe-work-area procedures established in Project 01 whenever any nearby circuit must be de-energized during layout or drilling, and it produces the coordinated box set that Projects 5 through 15 will wire, splice, ground, and terminate.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • EL-013 — Select listed new-work boxes appropriate to the wiring method, device type, conductor volume, and support requirement.
  • EL-014 — Lay out box height, centerline, and location according to the approved electrical plan and finished-wall reference.
  • EL-015 — Fasten boxes to studs or approved supports using the box's listed nails, screws, bracket, or support system.
  • EL-016 — Maintain the required box setback relative to the future finished-wall surface.
  • EL-017 — Calculate required box volume using the approved conductor, clamp, device, and grounding allowances.
  • EL-018 — Inspect box alignment, height, setback, support, and volume before rough wiring begins.

Where This Skill Fits

Every new-work box installed here becomes the fixed reference point for the cable runs in Project 6, the splicing and pigtailing work in Project 7, the grounding and bonding work in Project 8, and every device, switch, fixture, and fan installed in Projects 9 through 15. A box that is undersized, set at the wrong depth, or loosely fastened forces rework or a code-deficient installation at every one of those later stages.

The box-fill calculation habit established in this chapter — confirming marked volume against the approved conductor, clamp, device, and grounding allowances before a single cable enters the box — is the same discipline reused in Chapter 4 for old-work boxes and in Chapter 7 when conductors, splices, and pigtails are folded into the finished box.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved electrical plan for device locations, mounting heights, box types, cable count, and grounding provisions
  • selecting listed boxes for the wiring method, device type, conductor volume, and support requirement, including fan-rated and fixture-rated boxes where the plan requires them
  • calculating and confirming box volume using approved conductor, clamp, device, and grounding allowances
  • establishing the finished-floor and finished-wall reference and marking each box centerline and mounting height
  • positioning, aligning, and fastening each box to the stud or approved support at the correct setback
  • installing support bars and fan-rated support systems where the plan specifies them
  • marking planned cable entries and inspecting the completed box set before rough wiring begins

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • run cable, strip conductors, or make any splice, since those tasks belong to Projects 6 and 7
  • install a box in a location, height, or configuration that departs from the approved electrical plan without documenting the required revision
  • substitute an unlisted box, an undersized box, or an unlisted fan-support system for the one specified by the plan and manufacturer instructions
  • proceed to rough wiring when a box fails the inspection in Section K until the deficiency is corrected

Confirm whether the framing area requires a permit, inspection, or licensed electrician review before layout begins, and confirm that any circuit passing through the work area has been isolated using the Project 01 procedure before drilling or fastening near existing conductors.

Trade Note

A box's stamped or molded volume marking, not its outward appearance, is the controlling figure for box-fill calculation. Two boxes that look identical from the front can have different listed volumes depending on depth, integral clamps, and mounting-ear design. Read the marking before calculating fill, and do not estimate volume from the box's outward dimensions alone.

Setup Standard

Do not fasten any box until the finished-floor reference, finished-wall plane, and box centerline are established and marked, and until the selected box has been confirmed to have sufficient listed volume for its planned conductors, clamps, device, and grounding allowance. A box fastened before these references are set cannot be corrected without removing and re-installing it.

Shop Tip

Mark every box centerline and mounting height on the stud before opening the box carton. Confirming the layout against the approved plan first, and only then selecting and fastening the box, prevents an entire run of boxes being installed at a height that does not match the plan.

Box Selection, Volume, and Support Requirements

Select every box in the set from the approved device schedule, not from what is available on hand. Confirm the wiring method, device type, expected conductor count, and any fan, fixture, grounding, vapor-control, fire-rating, or special-cover requirement before choosing the box. A box that is correct for a duplex receptacle location is frequently insufficient for a ceiling-fan or heavy-fixture location, even at the same nominal size.

Measurement Cue — Calculate Box Volume Before Selecting the Box

Calculate required box volume using the approved conductor, clamp, device, and grounding allowances before confirming the box selection. Confirm that the box's stamped or molded marked volume equals or exceeds the calculated requirement. Do not select a box based on outward size alone.

Critical Safety Cue — Verify De-Energization Before Working Near Existing Conductors

If layout, drilling, or fastening in the work area will occur near an existing energized circuit, do not proceed until that circuit has been isolated, locked out, and verified de-energized using the Project 01 live-dead-live procedure. Treat every existing conductor in the framing as energized until that verification is complete.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Fan-Rated and Heavy-Fixture Support

Install fan-rated boxes, braces, and support bars using the exact procedure in the manufacturer's instructions. A standard lighting box or an unlisted support system is not an acceptable substitute for a listed fan-rated support, regardless of outward similarity. Verify fan-rated and heavy-fixture boxes independently of the general box installation check.

Field Safety Cue — Protecting Empty Boxes

Protect empty boxes from debris, joint compound, insulation, and construction traffic without blocking the future cable installation. A box packed with debris at rough-wiring time slows the work and can mask damage to the box or its mounting.

E. Plan Review and Box Selection

  1. Review the electrical plan, device locations, mounting heights, accessibility requirements, framing layout, box types, cable count, grounding provisions, and finish thickness. Confirm the box schedule accounts for every location included in this work area.
  2. Confirm that the framing is complete, dry, accessible, and free of conflicting utilities. Stop work at any location where framing is incomplete, wet, or conflicts with plumbing or mechanical rough-in.
  3. Select listed boxes for the wiring method, device type, conductor volume, and support requirement. Match each box to its scheduled location before opening the carton.
  4. Identify boxes requiring fan support, fixture support, grounding means, vapor control, fire rating, or special covers. Mark these locations separately on the worksheet before continuing.
  5. Calculate required box volume using the approved conductor, clamp, device, and grounding allowances. Complete this calculation for every box before fastening any of them.
  6. Confirm that each selected box has sufficient marked volume. Compare the calculated requirement directly against the box's stamped or molded volume marking.

F. Reference Establishment and Layout

  1. Establish the finished-floor reference and finished-wall plane. Use the same reference for every box in the work area.
  2. Mark each box centerline and mounting height from the approved reference. Record the measurement method used on the worksheet.
  3. Coordinate paired devices, countertops, cabinets, doors, trim, plumbing, and mechanical equipment. Adjust marked heights only where the approved plan permits coordination, and document the reason for any adjustment.

G. Box Positioning, Fastening, and Support

  1. Position each box against the stud or approved support. Confirm the box sits against solid framing or a listed support system before marking fastener locations.
  2. Set the front edge to the required relationship with the future finished-wall surface. Use the finished-wall plane established in Step 7, not an estimate of finish thickness.
  3. Check the box for level or consistent alignment as required by the project. Correct any box that is twisted or out of alignment with adjacent boxes before fastening.
  4. Fasten the box using its listed nails, screws, bracket, or support system. Use only the box's own specified fastening hardware.
  5. Do not drive fasteners through unapproved box locations or distort the box. Stop and replace any box that cracks, splits, or deforms during fastening.
  6. Install listed support bars for ceiling or between-stud boxes where specified. Follow the support bar's own listed span and fastening instructions.
  7. Verify fan-rated and heavy-fixture boxes independently. Perform the manufacturer's specific verification procedure in addition to the general fastening check.

H. Cable-Entry Planning and Protection

  1. Mark planned cable entries and confirm they will not violate the box-fill or bending space. Reference the Step 5 and Step 6 volume calculation before marking any entry.
  2. Protect empty boxes from debris without blocking future cable installation. Use a cover or plug that can be removed without damaging the box before rough wiring begins.

I. Inspection and Documentation

  1. Inspect box height, setback, alignment, support, volume, damage, and intended device use. Complete this inspection for every box in the set before proceeding to rough wiring.
  2. Correct loose, twisted, undersized, overdeep, or improperly supported boxes. Remove and re-install any box that fails the inspection rather than attempting a field modification that is not listed for the box.
  3. Record box type, listed volume, mounting reference, and special support condition. Complete the New-Work Box Installation Worksheet for the full set before closing out the project.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed box using the same checks regardless of which room, device type, or support condition it involves.

  • Selection check: confirm the installed box matches the approved box schedule for wiring method, device type, and support requirement.
  • Volume check: confirm the marked volume equals or exceeds the calculated conductor, clamp, device, and grounding allowance.
  • Height and centerline check: confirm the box is marked and installed at the plan-specified reference within its stated tolerance.
  • Setback check: confirm the front edge sits at the required relationship to the finished-wall plane.
  • Fastening check: confirm the box does not move under firm hand pressure and shows no cracking, splitting, or distortion.
  • Support check: confirm fan-rated and heavy-fixture boxes pass their independent manufacturer verification.
  • Documentation check: confirm the New-Work Box Installation Worksheet is fully completed for every box in the set.

Complete the New-Work Box Installation Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 04.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Box Fails the Volume Calculation After Installation

Probable cause: the box-fill calculation was skipped or performed after fastening, or the installed box's marked volume does not match the box specified on the approved schedule.
Confirmation: re-read the box's stamped or molded volume marking and recalculate the required volume from the actual conductor, clamp, device, and grounding allowance for that location.
Correction: remove the undersized box and install the correct listed box for the calculated volume; do not attempt to increase volume with an unlisted extension or field modification.
Reinspection: repeat the volume calculation and confirm the replacement box's marking meets or exceeds the requirement.

The Box Sits Proud of or Recessed Behind the Finished-Wall Plane

Probable cause: the finished-wall plane was estimated rather than measured, or the box was fastened before the setback reference was established.
Confirmation: measure the actual planned finish thickness and compare it against the box's current front-edge position.
Correction: reposition and refasten the box to the correct setback, or replace it with an adjustable or extension-rated box listed for the actual finish thickness.
Reinspection: repeat the setback measurement against the finished-wall plane for the corrected box and for any adjacent boxes installed using the same estimate.

A Fastened Box Moves Under Hand Pressure

Probable cause: the wrong fastener was used, the fastener missed solid framing, or the box's mounting ear cracked during installation.
Confirmation: inspect the fastener type, location, and the condition of the mounting ear against the stud.
Correction: remove and replace a cracked or split box; refasten using the box's listed nails, screws, or bracket driven into solid framing.
Reinspection: repeat the hand-pressure check and confirm the box no longer moves.

A Fan-Rated Box Does Not Pass Independent Verification

Probable cause: the support does not engage the required framing member, relies on wallboard alone, or was substituted with a standard lighting box or unlisted brace.
Confirmation: compare the installed support directly against its manufacturer's listed mounting procedure and confirm framing engagement.
Correction: remove the unlisted or improperly engaged support and install the listed fan-rated box or brace according to its exact mounting procedure.
Reinspection: repeat the manufacturer's independent verification procedure for the replacement support.

A Row of Boxes Is Inconsistent in Height or Alignment

Probable cause: each box was measured independently from an assumed floor level rather than from the single established finished-floor reference.
Confirmation: re-measure every box in the row from the same finished-floor reference and compare results.
Correction: remove and reposition any box outside the plan-specified tolerance using the established reference.
Reinspection: repeat the height and alignment check across the full row after correction.

Debris or Finish Material Has Entered an Empty Box Before Rough Wiring

Probable cause: the box was left uncovered during framing, insulation, or finish work.
Confirmation: inspect the box interior for insulation, joint compound, sawdust, or other debris.
Correction: remove all debris from the box interior and install a removable cover or plug before continuing other work in the area.
Reinspection: confirm the box interior is clean and the cover or plug is in place before rough wiring begins.

Cleanup

This project ends with a documented, inspected set of boxes ready for rough wiring, not disturbed further until Project 6 begins.

  • Confirm every box in the set is protected from debris with a removable cover or plug
  • Confirm any support bars, braces, or fan-rated systems remain undisturbed and correctly engaged
  • Store unused boxes, support bars, and fasteners in a protected, organized condition for the next work area
  • Confirm the work area is clear of loose fasteners, packaging, and debris before leaving the site
  • Store the New-Work Box Installation Worksheet with the project records for reference by later projects

Performance Check

With every box selected, calculated, positioned, fastened, supported, and inspected, review the finished box set against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm that each box matches its scheduled selection, meets or exceeds its calculated volume, sits at the correct height, centerline, and setback, remains firmly fastened without distortion, and — where applicable — passes its independent fan- or fixture-support verification. If an item fails, identify whether the error occurred in box selection, volume calculation, layout, fastening, or support verification before correcting it and re-running the affected check.
