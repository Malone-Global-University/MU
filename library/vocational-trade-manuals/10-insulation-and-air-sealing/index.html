10-Insulation and Air Sealing

This field manual provides practical, project-based instruction for improving residential energy efficiency through air sealing, insulation installation, moisture control, and building envelope improvements. Students learn to identify energy losses, install insulation systems, seal air leakage pathways, and improve residential comfort and performance.

Overview

Insulation and Air Sealing emphasizes hands-on skill development through residential building envelope improvement projects. Beginning with identifying air leaks and insulation deficiencies, students progress through air sealing techniques, attic improvements, wall and floor insulation installation, vapor control, duct sealing, moisture diagnosis, and a complete practice structure energy upgrade.

Learning Outcomes

  • Identify air leakage pathways and insulation deficiencies in residential buildings.
  • Prepare a safe and organized insulation installation work area.
  • Seal common residential air leakage points and service penetrations.
  • Install attic ventilation components and insulation systems.
  • Install fiberglass, mineral wool, blown-in, and rigid foam insulation.
  • Improve insulation performance around windows, doors, floors, and rim joists.
  • Apply vapor control and moisture management principles.
  • Seal and insulate HVAC duct areas to improve efficiency.
  • Diagnose common moisture, comfort, and insulation problems.
  • Complete a full air sealing and insulation improvement project.
