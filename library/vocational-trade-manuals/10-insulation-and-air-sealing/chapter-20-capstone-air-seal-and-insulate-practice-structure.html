Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to inspect, air-seal, insulate, verify, document, and close out the complete thermal enclosure of a small practice structure in a single continuous project. The finished work product is a fully air-sealed and insulated small structure — framing joints, service penetrations, attic bypasses, ventilation baffles, wall cavities, window and door perimeters, floor or crawlspace areas, rim joists, continuous rigid-foam insulation where specified, vapor-control layers, duct-adjacent enclosure areas, and an insulated attic access cover — with every ventilation path, heat-source clearance, and service point preserved, ready for a formal handoff to Book 11 for drywall installation.

You will use the dried-in, mechanically coordinated small structure completed through Books 1, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, and 9, provided rough plumbing, electrical, and HVAC work has passed the required inspections. You will confirm moisture readiness, inspect the entire structure, establish work zones, mark the thermal and air-control boundaries, complete every air-sealing task from Chapters 3 through 5, install ventilation and cavity insulation from Chapters 6 through 9, complete attic and floor insulation from Chapters 10 through 13, install continuous insulation and vapor control from Chapters 14 through 15, complete duct-adjacent work and the attic hatch from Chapters 16 through 17, perform the Chapter 19 final quality inspection standard against the whole structure, and issue the formal handoff.

This is the final project in Book 10. It does not introduce new techniques; it requires you to sequence and integrate every technique taught in Chapters 1 through 19 into one coherent, inspected, and documented thermal enclosure. The completed structure remains open for inspection until every requirement is verified, then it is handed off to Book 11 for drywall and finishing.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • IN-115 — Integrate air sealing across framing joints, service penetrations, and attic bypasses into one continuous boundary.
  • IN-116 — Install batt insulation, rigid foam, and attic baffles according to the approved capstone schedule.
  • IN-117 — Seal service penetrations and duct-adjacent areas while preserving required clearances and access.
  • IN-118 — Build and install an insulated attic access hatch that maintains the attic air barrier.
  • IN-119 — Complete a moisture-readiness review and final thermal-envelope quality inspection.
  • IN-120 — Compile complete insulation closeout documentation and issue the formal drywall handoff.

Where This Skill Fits

Every prior chapter in this book produced one deficiency category, one air-sealing task, or one insulation installation in isolation. This capstone requires you to hold the entire System-Sequence Control principle in mind at once: air sealing before cavity insulation, moisture correction before any insulation, rough inspections before cavities close, ventilation and heat-source clearances before loose-fill insulation, and vapor-control placement approved for the specific assembly rather than assumed from general climate rules.

The completed capstone becomes the release record that Book 11 depends on. A drywall crew working from this handoff must be able to trust that every air-sealing, fireblocking, vapor-control, and insulation requirement was verified and documented before the structure was closed.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming the structure is dried in with roofing, WRB, windows, doors, and exterior penetrations complete
  • confirming plumbing, electrical, and HVAC rough-ins have passed required inspections
  • completing a moisture-readiness review and correcting all active water sources before any insulation is installed
  • air-sealing framing joints, service penetrations, attic bypasses, chases, and heat-source transitions
  • installing attic baffles, ventilation dams, and preserving continuous soffit-to-attic ventilation
  • insulating window and exterior-door perimeters, wall cavities, floor or crawlspace areas, and rim joists according to the approved schedule
  • installing rigid-foam panels, taped seams, and the approved vapor retarder only at its designated location
  • completing duct-adjacent air-sealing and insulation, and building the insulated attic hatch
  • installing attic depth markers, access dams, and loose-fill or added attic insulation to the approved quantity and depth
  • performing the final thermal-envelope quality inspection, required testing, documentation, and formal handoff to Book 11

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin any insulation or concealment work before plumbing, electrical, HVAC, and moisture-readiness prerequisites are confirmed complete
  • select a vapor-retarder class or location based on general climate assumptions rather than the approved capstone assembly design
  • perform duct-system repairs; any duct defect beyond a minor facing tear is a Book 9 referral
  • conceal any air-sealing, fireblocking, vapor-control, or insulation work until the required inspection and documentation in this chapter are complete

Any condition that meets a stop-work criterion identified in the referenced chapters must halt work in that area and be referred to the appropriate qualified professional before this chapter's work continues in that location.

Trade Note

Treat this capstone as an integration exercise, not a repeat of individual chapters. Where a step below references an earlier chapter's method, that method is the controlling procedure; this chapter does not redefine fastener spacing, sealant joint dimensions, R-values, ventilation ratios, or clearance distances beyond what the referenced chapters and the approved capstone plan already specify.

Setup Standard

Do not begin any capstone insulation task until the moisture-readiness review in Section D is complete and every active water source has been corrected and confirmed dry. Do not close any cavity until the required rough-in inspections for that area are confirmed passed.

Shop Tip

Work the capstone in the same order as the System-Sequence Control principle in the book's front matter: moisture correction, then air sealing, then ventilation and heat-clearance protection, then insulation, then verification and documentation. Do not jump ahead to insulate an area just because materials are staged and ready.

Stop work in any area of the capstone structure where hazardous materials, damaged wiring, active moisture, structural damage, or an energized or unsafe condition is discovered, using the same stop-work criteria established in the originating chapter. Maintain the work-zone, PPE, dust-control, and ventilation setup from Chapter 2 throughout the entire capstone rather than re-establishing it for each phase. Confirm lighting, footing, and emergency-exit procedures remain adequate as attic and cavity conditions change with each completed phase.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Product Compatibility Across Phases

Confirm that sealants, foams, membranes, tapes, and insulation products used in later phases remain compatible with materials already installed in earlier phases, per each product's manufacturer instructions. Do not assume compatibility across different chapters' material choices without checking the approved capstone plan.

D. Preparation — Readiness, Rough-In Verification, and Moisture Review

  1. Obtain the approved capstone energy plan, insulation schedule, air-barrier details, vapor-control strategy, attic-ventilation plan, service-penetration schedule, fire-clearance details, and inspection sequence. Record the source and date of each document reviewed.
  2. Confirm that the structure is dried in and that roofing, WRB, windows, doors, and exterior penetrations are complete. Walk the full exterior and record confirmation before proceeding.
  3. Confirm that plumbing, electrical, and HVAC rough-ins have passed required inspections. Record the inspection dates and inspector or authority for each trade.
  4. Complete the moisture-readiness review and correct all active water sources. Apply the Chapter 18 diagnostic method to the entire structure and confirm drying before continuing to the next phase.

E. Inspection, Hazard Identification, and Work-Zone Setup

  1. Inspect framing, sheathing, cavities, attic, floor, rim joists, services, and access points. Use the Chapter 1 inspection method across the entire structure and record findings by area.
  2. Identify hazardous materials, damaged wiring, non-IC lights, chimneys, flues, exhaust ducts, and other stop-work conditions. Record each condition, exclude the area if required, and refer it to the appropriate qualified professional.
  3. Establish work zones, PPE, dust controls, ventilation, access paths, and material staging. Apply the Chapter 2 work-area setup across the full structure for the duration of the capstone.
  4. Mark the complete thermal and air-control boundaries. Walk the structure and mark the intended boundary line on framing, attic, and floor surfaces before beginning any sealing or insulation work.

F. Air Sealing

  1. Air-seal top plates, bottom plates, corners, doubled framing, sheathing joints, and floor transitions. Apply the Chapter 3 procedure and acceptance standard to every assigned framing joint.
  2. Seal plumbing, electrical, and mechanical penetrations with compatible systems. Apply the Chapter 4 procedure, selecting materials appropriate to each service and fire requirement.
  3. Block and seal attic bypasses, dropped soffits, chases, and partition tops. Apply the Chapter 5 procedure, installing rigid backing where required.
  4. Install noncombustible blocking and clearance dams at heat-producing penetrations. Confirm every chimney, flue, and other heat-producing component maintains its required clearance before continuing.

G. Ventilation and Perimeter Insulation

  1. Install attic baffles and eave insulation dams. Apply the Chapter 6 procedure at every vented rafter or truss bay in the approved design.
  2. Verify continuous soffit-to-attic ventilation. Confirm airflow continuity from each exterior intake through its baffle to the attic air space before installing attic insulation.
  3. Insulate window and exterior-door perimeter gaps without affecting operation or drainage. Apply the Chapter 9 procedure at every window and exterior door included in the capstone scope.

H. Cavity, Floor, and Continuous Insulation

  1. Install fiberglass or mineral-wool batts in wall cavities according to the approved schedule. Apply the Chapter 7 or Chapter 8 procedure as specified for each wall section.
  2. Fit insulation around wiring, boxes, pipes, braces, headers, and corners. Confirm full-depth contact and no compression at every service penetration within each cavity.
  3. Install floor, crawlspace, or rim-joist insulation included in the capstone design. Apply the Chapter 12 and Chapter 13 procedures, confirming the crawlspace moisture strategy is followed.
  4. Install rigid-foam panels or inserts where specified. Apply the Chapter 14 procedure for layout, cutting, and fastening.
  5. Tape seams and seal edges to the adjoining air-control layer. Confirm continuity between the rigid-foam layer and the surrounding air barrier.

I. Vapor Control, Duct Areas, and Attic Access

  1. Install the approved vapor retarder only at its designated location. Apply the Chapter 15 procedure, confirming the location matches the approved capstone assembly design rather than a general climate assumption.
  2. Air-seal and insulate around ducts while preserving access, condensate, and duct insulation. Apply the Chapter 16 procedure at every duct area in the capstone scope, coordinating with Book 9 records.
  3. Build and install the insulated attic hatch or access cover. Apply the Chapter 17 procedure, confirming perimeter weatherstripping compression and stable operation.
  4. Prepare the attic for loose-fill insulation. Confirm baffles, dams, and clearances from Sections F and G remain intact before proceeding to attic insulation.

J. Attic Insulation

  1. Install depth markers, access dams, equipment guards, and service labels. Apply the Chapter 10 preparation procedure across the full attic area.
  2. Install loose-fill or added attic insulation to the approved product quantity and depth. Apply the Chapter 10 or Chapter 11 distribution procedure, tracking bag count against the approved coverage chart.
  3. Keep ventilation paths, heat clearances, access, and equipment platforms open. Confirm no loose-fill material has blocked a baffle, dam, or required access point.

K. Final Inspection, Testing, and Documentation

  1. Inspect all cavities, seams, penetrations, clearances, baffles, ducts, and service points. Apply the Chapter 19 final inspection method to the complete structure.
  2. Correct gaps, compression, blocked ventilation, poor seals, unsupported batts, and damaged vapor layers. Address every deficiency identified during the final inspection.
  3. Perform required air-leakage or thermal testing under the approved plan. Conduct blower-door, infrared, or other specified testing only under the approved test conditions.
  4. Prepare the final deficiency list. Record location, condition, required correction, responsible party, and reinspection method for every remaining item.
  5. Correct and reinspect every item. Confirm each deficiency is resolved before proceeding to documentation.
  6. Photograph all concealed work before drywall. Capture every area that will be hidden once enclosure begins.
  7. Compile product labels, quantities, coverage, depth readings, test results, inspections, and corrections. Assemble the complete capstone record.
  8. Complete the thermal-envelope readiness inspection. Issue an approved, conditional, or rejected decision, with conditions stated for any conditional status.

L. Handoff and Protection

  1. Issue the formal handoff to Book 11 for drywall installation. Deliver the complete capstone record along with the readiness decision.
  2. Protect the completed insulation and air-sealing work until enclosure by the next trade. Prevent disturbance, moisture exposure, or compression of the finished thermal enclosure until drywall installation begins.

Common Inspection Quality Check

Review the completed capstone using the same checks regardless of which phase produced the finding.

  • Sequence check: confirm moisture correction, air sealing, ventilation protection, and insulation occurred in the required order across every area of the structure.
  • Continuity check: confirm the air barrier is continuous across framing joints, penetrations, bypasses, and heat-source transitions with no unsealed gap remaining.
  • Ventilation and clearance check: confirm soffit-to-attic ventilation is unblocked and no insulation or sealant entered a prohibited clearance zone.
  • Insulation fit check: confirm every cavity, floor, rim-joist bay, and continuous-insulation area is filled to full depth without gaps or damaging compression.
  • Vapor-control check: confirm the vapor retarder appears only at its approved location with no unapproved double low-permeance layer.
  • Access check: confirm every junction box, valve, damper, cleanout, filter, and equipment panel remains reachable.
  • Documentation check: confirm the final deficiency list, corrections, reinspections, photographs, and readiness decision are complete before handoff.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

An Area Was Insulated Before Its Rough-In Inspection Was Confirmed

Probable cause: the phase schedule was not followed in order, and insulation began in one area before plumbing, electrical, or HVAC inspection records were confirmed for that same area.
Confirmation: cross-reference the insulated area against the rough-in inspection log for that trade and location.
Correction: if the rough-in inspection has since passed, document it retroactively and proceed; if it has not passed, stop further concealment in that area and refer it to the appropriate trade and inspector before continuing.
Reinspection: repeat the sequence check for that area and confirm the inspection record is on file before final handoff.

The Vapor Retarder Appears at More Than One Location in the Same Assembly

Probable cause: a vapor-control layer was added in a later phase without checking whether an earlier phase, such as a rigid-foam facer or membrane, already created a low-permeance layer in the same assembly.
Confirmation: review the approved capstone assembly design and identify every low-permeance layer installed in each affected assembly.
Correction: remove or relocate the unapproved layer according to the approved design, and confirm the assembly's drying path is restored.
Reinspection: repeat the vapor-control check for the affected assembly.

Loose-Fill Attic Insulation Buried a Baffle, Dam, or Access Point

Probable cause: the attic-preparation phase in Section I was not fully verified before the attic-insulation phase in Section J began.
Confirmation: inspect the attic and locate the buried baffle, dam, or access point.
Correction: remove the loose-fill material from the affected area, restore the baffle, dam, or access point, and reinstall insulation around it correctly.
Reinspection: repeat the ventilation and clearance check for the attic area.

A Duct-System Defect Was Discovered Late in the Capstone and Treated as Building Air Sealing

Probable cause: the boundary between building-envelope work and duct-system work from Chapter 16 was not maintained under time pressure to finish the capstone.
Confirmation: review the material applied at the duct area and determine whether it was applied to the duct system itself rather than the surrounding building assembly.
Correction: remove any material applied to the duct system, document the discovered defect, and refer it to Book 9 duct-sealing procedures or a qualified HVAC contractor.
Reinspection: confirm the referral is documented and that no further duct-system work occurs under this chapter before handoff.

The Final Deficiency List Was Not Fully Corrected Before Photographs Were Taken

Probable cause: photographs were taken to meet a documentation deadline before every listed deficiency was corrected and reinspected.
Confirmation: compare the final deficiency list against the photograph set and identify any deficiency still visible in a photograph.
Correction: correct the remaining deficiency, reinspect it, and retake the photograph showing the corrected condition.
Reinspection: repeat the documentation check across the complete photograph set before issuing the handoff.
