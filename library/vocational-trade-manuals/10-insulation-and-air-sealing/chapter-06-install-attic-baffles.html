Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install manufacturer-approved rafter or truss ventilation baffles and insulation dams at selected eaves so that the soffit-to-ridge or soffit-to-attic ventilation path remains open above the planned insulation level. The finished work product is a set of correctly positioned, fastened, and dammed baffles covering every assigned vented rafter or truss bay, with a documented inspection confirming continuity from the exterior intake to the attic air space.

You will review the roof-ventilation design, confirm that soffit vents are installed and open, inspect each eave for obstructions, position and fasten a baffle in each assigned bay, maintain the required clearance to the roof deck, build an insulation dam at the eave, preserve heat-source clearances, and inspect every bay for continuity before attic insulation is installed.

This project follows directly from Chapter 5, where attic bypasses, chases, and partition tops were sealed and the attic air barrier was made continuous. It must be completed before Chapter 10, where loose-fill attic insulation is installed, because the baffles and dams installed here are what keeps that insulation out of the soffit ventilation path.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • IN-031 — Identify soffit ventilation paths and the rafter or truss bays that require baffles.
  • IN-032 — Install rafter or truss ventilation baffles using the manufacturer-approved fastening method.
  • IN-033 — Maintain a continuous air channel between the baffle and the roof deck.
  • IN-034 — Protect insulation from blocking the ventilation path by building insulation dams at the eave.
  • IN-035 — Verify intake airflow continuity from the soffit through the baffle to the attic air space.
  • IN-036 — Inspect the completed ventilation clearance for crushed baffles, gaps, blocked channels, and short extensions.

Where This Skill Fits

Attic ventilation depends on an unbroken path from the soffit intake, through the baffle, to the attic air space above the insulation. Chapter 5 sealed the attic air barrier without blocking this same ventilation path; this project installs the physical baffles and dams that keep the path open once insulation is added in Chapters 10 and 11.

The insulation dams built in this chapter also protect the heat-source clearances and access points marked in earlier chapters, and they are inspected again in Chapter 19 as part of the final thermal-envelope quality inspection and in the Chapter 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the roof-ventilation design, intake and exhaust locations, and baffle product selection
  • verifying that the roof system is dry and exterior soffit vents are installed and open
  • inspecting eaves for obstructions, debris, wiring, and prior damage
  • positioning, fastening, and extending baffles in each assigned rafter or truss bay
  • maintaining the required clearance between the baffle and the roof deck
  • installing insulation dams at the eave to keep loose-fill material out of the soffit
  • verifying continuity of the ventilation path and inspecting for defects

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • alter the roof-ventilation design, intake sizing, or exhaust locations without approved plan revision
  • seal, block, or otherwise close the ventilation channel itself for any reason
  • work on a wet, leaking, or structurally damaged roof system
  • install loose-fill attic insulation before the baffles and dams in this chapter are complete and inspected

Any condition that meets a stop-work criterion in Step 6 must halt work in that bay and be referred to the appropriate qualified professional before work continues.

Trade Note

A baffle that is installed but crushed against the roof deck, or that stops short of the final insulation level, provides no more ventilation than no baffle at all. Extend every baffle high enough that it remains above the planned insulation depth, and confirm this before moving to the next bay.

Setup Standard

Do not step off a walk board or joist-supported platform when working near the eaves, where ceiling framing is often shallower and less accessible. Confirm safe footing and adequate lighting before reaching into any eave area, and do not lean on or compress existing insulation to reach a bay.

Shop Tip

Work each rafter or truss bay in the same sequence — inspect, measure, position, fasten, dam, verify — rather than installing all baffles first and returning later for dams. This prevents a bay from being buried by staged insulation before its dam is complete.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every baffle installation in this project moves bay by bay: inspect the eave, measure the bay, position and fasten the baffle, build the dam, and verify continuity before moving to the next bay. Completing all baffles before any dams, or all dams before any baffles, increases the risk that a finished bay is disturbed or buried before it is verified.

Baffles Are Not a Substitute for Air Sealing

A baffle maintains an open air channel; it does not air-seal the attic. The attic air barrier established in Chapters 3 through 5 must already be continuous before this project begins. Do not use baffle installation as an opportunity to leave an air-sealing gap unaddressed, and do not seal the ventilation channel itself in an attempt to correct an unrelated air leak.

Measurement Cue — Extend Baffles Above the Final Insulation Level

Measure the planned final insulation depth from the approved insulation schedule before cutting or positioning any baffle. Extend each baffle high enough into the attic that its upper edge remains above this depth once insulation is installed in Chapter 10 or 11.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work for Active Roof Leakage or Damaged Framing

Stop baffle installation in any bay where you find active roof leakage, damaged or deteriorated roof framing, hazardous materials, bird or animal nests with possible droppings, or unsafe attic access. Do not disturb suspected hazardous material. Record the bay as excluded and refer it to the appropriate qualified professional before work continues in that bay.

Field Safety Cue — Eave Footing and Reach

Ceiling framing near the eaves is often shallower and closer to the roof deck than framing near the attic center. Confirm walk-board or joist support before reaching into any eave area, and never place body weight on insulation, ductwork, or ceiling drywall to gain reach.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Baffle Fastening Method

Fasten baffles only using the method specified by the baffle manufacturer for the framing type present, whether staples, adhesive, or a proprietary clip system. Do not substitute a different fastening method because it is faster or more familiar; an improperly fastened baffle can collapse against the roof deck once insulation is installed against it.

D. Preparation — Review and Access

  1. Review the roof-ventilation design, intake locations, exhaust locations, rafter or truss spacing, insulation depth, and baffle product. Confirm the baffle product specified is compatible with the framing type present in the attic being worked.
  2. Verify that the roof system is dry and that exterior soffit vents are installed and open. Check from outside the structure that soffit vent openings are unobstructed by paint, debris, insect nests, or prior construction.
  3. Establish safe attic access and protect ceiling surfaces. Confirm the walk board or joist platform extends close enough to each assigned eave to avoid stepping off supported framing.

E. Bay Inspection

  1. Identify each vented rafter or truss bay included in the design. Mark or list each bay by its location relative to a fixed reference point, such as a gable end or numbered truss.
  2. Inspect eaves for bird nests, debris, wiring, fireblocking, blocked vents, wind washing, and water staining. Record any condition found in each bay before beginning installation.
  3. Stop work for active roof leakage, damaged roof framing, hazardous materials, or unsafe access. Record the affected bay as excluded, state the specific reason, and identify the qualified professional required before work can proceed in that bay.

F. Baffle Installation

  1. Measure the bay and select a baffle compatible with the framing and required air channel. Confirm the baffle width matches the bay and that its channel depth matches the approved roof-ventilation design.
  2. Position the baffle from the soffit intake toward the upper attic without blocking the intake. Confirm the lower edge of the baffle sits at or beyond the intake opening without covering it.
  3. Fasten the baffle using the manufacturer-approved method. Fasten to framing on both sides of the bay at the spacing specified by the baffle manufacturer.
  4. Maintain the required clearance between baffle and roof deck. Confirm the baffle channel is not crushed, twisted, or collapsed anywhere along its length.
  5. Extend the baffle high enough to remain above the final insulation level. Compare the baffle's upper edge against the planned insulation depth recorded in the Baffle Installation Record.
  6. Seal baffle side edges only where required by the approved wind-wash detail. Do not add edge sealing beyond what the approved detail specifies.
  7. Do not seal the ventilation channel itself. Confirm no fastener, sealant, or dam material has entered or blocked the open air channel.

G. Insulation Dam Installation

  1. Install an insulation dam at the eave to keep loose-fill material out of the soffit. Position the dam beyond the baffle's lower edge so it retains insulation without blocking the intake or the baffle opening.
  2. Maintain required clearances from recessed lights, flues, chimneys, fans, and electrical equipment. Confirm the dam does not cover or restrict access to any marked heat-source or electrical clearance zone.
  3. Repeat for all specified bays. Complete inspection, baffle installation, and dam installation for each assigned bay before considering the project complete.

H. Verification and Documentation

  1. Verify continuity from exterior intake through the baffle to the attic air space. Confirm, bay by bay, that an unobstructed path exists from the soffit vent through the baffle channel to the open attic.
  2. Inspect for crushed baffles, gaps, blocked channels, loose fasteners, or short extensions. Check each bay against the Acceptance Standards table.
  3. Correct defects before installing attic insulation. Re-fasten, replace, or reposition any baffle or dam that fails inspection, and repeat the verification step for that bay.
  4. Document baffle locations and ventilation paths. Record the baffle product, bay locations, dam locations, and inspection results on the worksheet, and photograph representative completed bays.

Common Inspection Quality Check

Review the completed baffle and dam installation using the same checks regardless of which bay produced the finding.

  • Coverage check: confirm every assigned bay has an installed baffle and dam, or is recorded as excluded with a stated reason.
  • Intake check: confirm the soffit intake is unobstructed at every assigned bay.
  • Channel check: confirm the air channel between the baffle and roof deck is open and uncrushed along the full bay length.
  • Extension check: confirm every baffle's upper edge remains above the planned final insulation level.
  • Dam check: confirm every assigned eave has an installed insulation dam before insulation staging begins.
  • Clearance check: confirm required heat-source and electrical clearances remain unobstructed at every baffle and dam location.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Baffle Is Crushed Against the Roof Deck

Probable cause: the baffle was oversized for the bay, improperly fastened, or compressed by existing insulation before installation.
Confirmation: visually inspect the baffle channel along its full length for flattened or pinched sections.
Correction: remove and replace the baffle with one matching the bay width, or refasten it to restore the full channel depth specified in the approved roof-ventilation design.
Reinspection: repeat the channel check for the corrected bay.

Baffle Extension Stops Below the Planned Insulation Level

Probable cause: the baffle length was cut to a standard size without comparing it against the planned final insulation depth for this specific project.
Confirmation: measure the baffle's upper edge height and compare it to the recorded final insulation level on the worksheet.
Correction: extend the baffle with an additional approved section or replace it with a longer baffle that clears the planned insulation depth.
Reinspection: repeat the extension check for the corrected bay.

Insulation Dam Is Missing or Undersized

Probable cause: the dam was treated as optional or installed after insulation staging had already begun nearby.
Confirmation: inspect the eave for a continuous dam that fully retains insulation away from the soffit intake and baffle opening.
Correction: install or extend the dam before any insulation is staged or blown in that area.
Reinspection: repeat the dam check for the corrected eave.

Soffit Intake Is Blocked by Baffle Material or Debris

Probable cause: the baffle was positioned too low, covering the intake opening instead of channeling air from it, or debris accumulated during installation.
Confirmation: inspect the intake opening directly from inside the attic and, where accessible, from the exterior soffit.
Correction: reposition the baffle above the intake opening and clear any debris blocking the vent.
Reinspection: repeat the intake check for the corrected bay.

A Heat-Source Clearance Is Obstructed by a Dam or Baffle

Probable cause: the dam or baffle was installed without first confirming the marked clearance zones from Chapter 5 near a recessed light, flue, or chimney.
Confirmation: compare the installed dam and baffle locations against the marked heat-source clearance zones on the attic plan.
Correction: relocate or trim the dam or baffle to restore the required clearance.
Reinspection: repeat the clearance check for the corrected location.
