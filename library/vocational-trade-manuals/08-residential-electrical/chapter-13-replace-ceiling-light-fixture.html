Job Overview

This project qualifies you to remove an existing ceiling-mounted light fixture and install a compatible replacement on the same verified de-energized branch circuit and existing outlet box. The finished work product is one securely supported, correctly grounded, correctly connected ceiling light fixture that has been tested and confirmed to operate normally at every lamp position.

You will verify that the existing box is accessible and adequately supported for the replacement fixture; de-energize the circuit using the Chapter 1 live-dead-live procedure; support and remove the old fixture without stressing conductors; inspect the box, cable entries, insulation, and grounding path; install the new mounting bracket and connect the replacement fixture's leads to the branch conductors; mount the fixture, install the approved lamps, and restore power under a controlled sequence; and confirm normal operation before closing out the project.

This project builds directly on the single-pole switch and fixture wiring taught in Chapter 11 and the shutdown and verification procedure taught in Chapter 1. It prepares you for the fan-rated support work in Chapter 14, where box support requirements become more demanding because of the added weight and vibration of a rotating appliance.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • EL-073 — Remove an existing ceiling light fixture after supporting it and confirming a verified de-energized circuit.
  • EL-074 — Inspect the existing ceiling box and its support for adequacy, damage, and suitability for the replacement fixture.
  • EL-075 — Connect the replacement fixture's grounded, ungrounded, and equipment-grounding conductors according to the fixture's markings.
  • EL-076 — Mount the replacement fixture's hardware and canopy securely to the box or bracket.
  • EL-077 — Install bulbs or lamps within the fixture's rated type and wattage.
  • EL-078 — Test the completed fixture for normal operation and identify abnormal heat, noise, flicker, or breaker operation.

Where This Skill Fits

Replacing a ceiling fixture is one of the most common residential electrical service calls, and it depends entirely on the shutdown and verification discipline established in Chapter 1 and the fixture-connection skills established in Chapter 11. Unlike new-work fixture installation, this project starts with an existing, already-loaded box and an unknown history of prior connections, so inspecting the box, its support, and the conductor condition before connecting a new fixture is as important as the connection itself.

The support-verification habit developed here — confirming the box and its structural attachment before trusting it with a new fixture's weight — is the same habit expanded significantly in Chapter 14, where fan-rated support becomes mandatory rather than optional.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • verifying that the existing outlet box is accessible, securely supported, and listed for the replacement fixture's weight
  • de-energizing the circuit and verifying absence of voltage using the Chapter 1 live-dead-live procedure
  • supporting, lowering, and removing the existing fixture without stressing branch conductors
  • inspecting the box, support, cable entries, conductor insulation, and grounding path
  • installing the new mounting bracket, connecting the replacement fixture's leads, and mounting the fixture body
  • installing lamps within the fixture's rated type and wattage and testing the completed installation

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • install a replacement fixture on a box that is loose, unsupported, damaged, overloaded, or not suitable for the new fixture's weight or type
  • install a fan, heavy pendant, or other fixture requiring fan-rated support in a box that is not listed for that purpose — this condition is addressed in Chapter 14
  • work on exposed energized conductors or equipment at any point in this project
  • energize the completed fixture before the required inspection and restoration sequence is complete

Any box, support condition, or conductor defect that fails inspection in this chapter must be corrected or escalated to a qualified professional before the replacement fixture is installed.

Trade Note

An existing ceiling box that supported a light fixture for years is not automatically suitable for every replacement fixture. Confirm the box's listed support rating against the new fixture's actual weight, and confirm the box remains securely fastened to framing, before connecting a single conductor. A box that already flexes, rotates, or pulls away from its support under hand pressure is a stop-work condition, not a condition to work around with extra fasteners.

Setup Standard

Do not disconnect any conductor until the circuit has been de-energized and verified using the Chapter 1 live-dead-live procedure, and do not trust the old fixture's mounting screws to hold the fixture unsupported while conductors are disconnected. Support the fixture by hand or with a temporary support before removing the last mounting screw.

Shop Tip

Photograph the existing connections before disconnecting anything. If the replacement fixture's wiring diagram differs from the old fixture, the photograph documents how the branch conductors were identified and connected, which protects against confusion if a problem is discovered later.

Support Verification Before Connection

Before connecting a single conductor, confirm that the existing box remains securely fastened to framing and that its listed support rating meets or exceeds the replacement fixture's actual weight. A box that flexes, rotates, or shows signs of prior overloading is a stop-work condition. Correct or replace the box support before proceeding; do not compensate for an inadequate box by adding extra screws to a damaged or undersized support.

Critical Safety Cue — Verify De-Energization Before Touching Any Conductor

Do not disconnect or touch any fixture or branch conductor until the circuit has been de-energized, locked out, and verified using the Chapter 1 live-dead-live procedure. Confirm absence of voltage again at the exposed conductors immediately before disconnecting them, even if the circuit was verified earlier in the project.

Field Safety Cue — Support the Fixture Before Removing the Last Screw

Support the existing fixture by hand or with a temporary support before removing its final mounting screw. An unsupported fixture that drops when the last screw is removed can stress or break the branch conductors and cause a fall hazard for anyone standing below.

Safety Cue — Stable Ladder and Platform Setup

Set up the ladder or platform on a level, stable surface with a helper present for fixtures too heavy or awkward for one person. Do not overreach from the ladder to access the fixture; reposition the ladder instead.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Follow the Replacement Fixture's Exact Markings

Connect the replacement fixture's leads exactly according to its markings and instructions, and install only the lamp type and wattage stated on the fixture's rating label. Do not assume the replacement fixture's wiring matches the fixture being removed.

E. Fixture and Circuit Review

  1. Review the replacement fixture instructions, weight, dimensions, lamp rating, ceiling-box requirements, grounding, and circuit information. Compare the fixture's requirements against the existing box before proceeding.
  2. Verify that the existing box is accessible, securely supported, and listed for the fixture. Treat a box that fails this check as a stop-work condition addressed under the Failure Points section.

F. De-Energization and Setup

  1. De-energize the circuit and verify absence of voltage using the approved procedure. Apply the full Chapter 1 live-dead-live sequence and lockout before proceeding.
  2. Protect the floor and establish a safe ladder or platform setup. Confirm the ladder or platform is stable and level before climbing.

G. Controlled Removal of the Existing Fixture

  1. Support the existing fixture before removing mounting hardware. Use a helper or temporary support so the fixture cannot fall when the last screw is removed.
  2. Lower the fixture enough to access conductors without stressing them. Do not pull on fixture leads or branch conductors to gain access.
  3. Confirm absence of voltage again at the exposed conductors. Retest even if the circuit was verified earlier in the project.
  4. Photograph and label the existing conductor connections. Complete this before disconnecting anything.
  5. Disconnect grounding, grounded, and ungrounded conductors in a controlled order. Keep each conductor identified as it is disconnected.
  6. Remove the old fixture and mounting bracket. Set the removed fixture aside without placing strain on the box.

H. Box Inspection and New Mounting Bracket

  1. Inspect the box, support, cable entries, conductor insulation, and grounding path. Check for corrosion, damaged insulation, loose clamps, and an intact grounding path.
  2. Stop work if the box is loose, unsupported, damaged, overloaded, or not suitable for the new fixture. Escalate per the Failure Points section before continuing.
  3. Install the new mounting bracket using the listed screws and box holes. Use only the fastener holes and hardware specified by the bracket manufacturer.

I. Fixture Connection and Mounting

  1. Prepare fixture leads and branch conductors to the connector requirements. Strip conductors to the length required by the listed connector without nicking copper.
  2. Connect equipment grounding conductors and fixture grounding parts. Confirm a continuous path from the branch equipment-grounding conductor to the fixture's grounding point.
  3. Connect grounded and ungrounded conductors according to the fixture markings. Do not rely on the old fixture's wiring pattern if the replacement fixture's markings differ.
  4. Inspect all connections and fold conductors into the box without pinching. Confirm no exposed copper remains beyond each connector.
  5. Mount the fixture securely to the bracket. Confirm the fixture does not move under hand pressure once mounting hardware is tightened.
  6. Install the approved canopy, diffuser, and lamp type within the fixture rating. Do not install a lamp exceeding the fixture's marked maximum wattage.

J. Restoration and Testing

  1. Complete the required inspection before energization. Confirm every item on the Fixture Replacement Schedule meets its target condition.
  2. Restore power and operate the fixture. Use the controlled restoration sequence established for the circuit.
  3. Inspect for flicker, noise, heat, movement, abnormal odor, or breaker operation. Observe the fixture through a full operating cycle, including any dimmer or multi-level switching.
  4. De-energize before correcting any issue. Do not attempt to adjust or re-tighten a connection while the fixture is energized.
  5. Document the fixture, support condition, lamp rating, and test results. Complete the Fixture Replacement Worksheet in full.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed fixture replacement using the same checks regardless of fixture type or location.

  • Support check: confirm the box remains securely fastened to framing and rated for the installed fixture's actual weight.
  • De-energization check: confirm the live-dead-live test was completed and reverified before conductors were disconnected and connected.
  • Grounding check: confirm a continuous equipment-grounding path from the branch circuit to the fixture.
  • Connection check: confirm grounded and ungrounded conductors are connected exactly per the fixture's markings with no exposed copper.
  • Mounting check: confirm the fixture does not move under hand pressure and no conductors are pinched.
  • Lamping check: confirm the installed lamp type and wattage match the fixture's rated markings.
  • Operation check: confirm the fixture operates normally with no flicker, heat, noise, or breaker operation.

Complete the Fixture Replacement Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 14.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Existing Box Moves, Rotates, or Feels Loose Under Hand Pressure

Probable cause: a box that was never adequately fastened to framing, fasteners that have loosened over time, or a support system not rated for the weight previously installed.
Confirmation: inspect the box's fastening method and framing attachment directly, and compare the box's listed support rating to the replacement fixture's actual weight.
Correction: stop work and correct or replace the box support before installing the replacement fixture; do not compensate with additional screws into a damaged or undersized support.
Reinspection: repeat the support check with hand pressure after the correction, before reconnecting any conductor.

The Replacement Fixture's Wiring Diagram Does Not Match the Old Fixture

Probable cause: different fixture designs use different lead colors, additional switched legs, or different grounding provisions.
Confirmation: compare the replacement fixture's markings and instructions directly against the branch conductors identified in the box, not against the old fixture's photographed connections.
Correction: connect the replacement fixture strictly according to its own markings and instructions; escalate to a qualified electrical professional if the branch conductor identification cannot be confirmed.
Reinspection: repeat conductor identification and connection inspection before mounting the fixture.

The Fixture Flickers, Buzzes, or Runs Hot After Restoration

Probable cause: a loose connection, an incompatible dimmer or control, a lamp exceeding the fixture's rated wattage, or an incompatible lamp type.
Confirmation: de-energize and verify absence of voltage, then inspect all connections, the lamp rating, and any connected dimmer or control device.
Correction: correct loose connections, install the correct lamp type and wattage, or replace an incompatible control device per its manufacturer instructions.
Reinspection: restore power under the controlled sequence and repeat the operational test.

Grounding Continuity Cannot Be Confirmed at the Fixture

Probable cause: a missing or damaged equipment-grounding conductor, a grounding pigtail not connected to the fixture's grounding point, or a metal box without a bonded grounding path.
Confirmation: de-energize and inspect the grounding path from the branch circuit through the box to the fixture's grounding terminal.
Correction: restore a continuous grounding path using listed connectors and grounding hardware; do not energize the fixture with an unresolved grounding defect.
Reinspection: repeat the grounding continuity check before restoring power.

The Fixture Will Not Fit the Existing Box or Bracket

Probable cause: a replacement fixture with a canopy, bracket pattern, or weight outside the existing box's design.
Confirmation: compare the replacement fixture's mounting requirements against the existing box's listed mounting provisions.
Correction: install a compatible mounting bracket or strap supplied or approved by the fixture manufacturer for the existing box, or escalate to a qualified electrical professional if the box itself must be replaced.
Reinspection: repeat the support and mounting check with the corrected bracket before connecting conductors.

Performance Check

With the replacement fixture mounted, connected, lamped, and tested, review the finished installation against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the box support was verified before connection, the live-dead-live test was completed and reverified before and after conductor work, the equipment-grounding path is continuous, the conductor connections match the fixture's markings with no exposed copper, the fixture is secure under hand pressure with no pinched conductors, the lamp installed matches the fixture's rated markings, and the operational test showed no flicker, heat, noise, or breaker operation. Complete the Fixture Replacement Worksheet and correct any failed item before beginning Project 14. If an item fails, identify whether the error occurred in support verification, de-energization, connection, mounting, lamping, or testing before correcting it and re-running the affected check.

Project Closeout

You have completed the replacement of a ceiling light fixture on a verified de-energized branch circuit. This project required you to verify the existing box's support, remove the old fixture under a controlled procedure, inspect the box and conductors, connect and mount the replacement fixture according to its own markings, install the approved lamp, and test the completed installation for normal operation (EL-073 through EL-078).

You now have a repeatable fixture-replacement procedure that starts from support verification rather than assumption, and a documented record you can reference for future service calls. That procedure carries directly into the next project, where box support requirements become mandatory rather than optional because of the added weight and vibration of a ceiling fan.

Retain your Fixture Replacement Worksheet and photographs. You will reuse this exact support-verification, de-energization, and controlled-connection sequence, with added fan-rated support requirements, in Project 14.
