Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install one approved exterior electrical outlet integrated with the building's weather-control system. You will select either the exterior-light option or the weather-resistant-receptacle option required by the approved plan, review the box, cover, device, gasket, cable, GFCI, corrosion, wet-location, mounting-block, flashing, and environmental requirements, coordinate the wall penetration with the building-envelope plan, install a listed weatherproof box at the correct projection and orientation, connect the device on a verified de-energized circuit, install the required cover or fixture, and test the finished work for correct operation, GFCI protection where applicable, and water shedding.

You will begin every step of this project on a circuit isolated, locked out, and verified de-energized using the exact live-dead-live procedure taught in Chapter 1. You will not remove any device cover, connect any conductor, or test any device on this circuit until that verified de-energized condition has been re-established at this location.

This project follows the duplex-receptacle work of Chapter 9 and the GFCI work of Chapter 10, and applies both to an exterior location where weather resistance, drainage, and building-envelope integration are equally part of the finished, inspectable work.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • EL-085 — Select a listed weatherproof box, mounting block, or flashed support correct for the wiring method, device or fixture option, and environmental condition.
  • EL-086 — Seal the exterior wall penetration in coordination with the weather-resistive barrier and flashing plan without blocking drainage.
  • EL-087 — Install a listed extra-duty in-use cover, gasket, or exterior fixture correct for the selected option.
  • EL-088 — Connect the exterior device or fixture conductors according to the approved diagram on a verified de-energized circuit.
  • EL-089 — Verify GFCI protection where the selected option requires it.
  • EL-090 — Inspect the completed weather-resistant installation for mounting, gasket compression, flashing integration, sealant placement, and drainage.

Where This Skill Fits

This project combines device work from Chapters 9 and 10 with the weather-resistive barrier and flashing sequence from Book 6. An exterior electrical penetration is both an electrical installation and a building-envelope penetration; a technically correct receptacle or fixture wired behind a poorly integrated penetration will still allow water into the wall assembly. This chapter treats the electrical box, cover, flashing, gasket, and sealant as one coordinated system rather than as separate trades working past each other.

The verified de-energization habit from Chapter 1, the box-fill and connector practices from Chapters 3 through 5, and the receptacle and GFCI wiring methods from Chapters 9 and 10 all apply directly here. The controlled water-shedding observation taught in this chapter previews the finish-level system checks used again in the Chapter 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • selecting either the exterior-light option or the weather-resistant-receptacle option required by the approved plan and completing that option's full procedure
  • de-energizing and verifying the circuit before any device, box, or cable work begins
  • installing a listed weatherproof box, exterior mounting block, or flashed box support integrated with the wall's weather-resistive barrier and flashing
  • routing cable through a listed wet- or damp-location connector and sealing the designated penetration without blocking drainage
  • connecting the selected device or fixture, including required GFCI protection for the receptacle option
  • installing the listed extra-duty in-use cover or exterior fixture, gasket, and mounting hardware
  • testing device or fixture operation, GFCI protection where applicable, and a controlled water-shedding observation

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • build one of each option or mix the component schedules of the exterior-light option and the weather-resistant-receptacle option
  • work on exposed energized conductors, remove a restricted cover, or bypass the live-dead-live verification required before this circuit is opened
  • direct water into any energized opening during the water-shedding observation
  • substitute a standard interior device, cover, or box for the listed wet- or damp-location component specified for this installation
  • proceed to energization before the required inspection is complete

Any condition identified in this project that conflicts with the approved building-envelope plan, GFCI requirement, or a manufacturer instruction must be corrected or escalated to a qualified professional before work continues.

Trade Note

Choose one option before staging materials: the exterior-light option or the weather-resistant-receptacle option. Complete that option's entire procedure using only its own component schedule. Do not install parts from both options on the same box, and do not build one of each unless the approved plan specifically calls for two separate outlets at two separate locations.

Setup Standard

Select the exterior-light option or the weather-resistant-receptacle option before staging materials. Do not begin cutting the wall opening, drilling, or disturbing the weather-resistive barrier until the circuit has been de-energized and verified using the live-dead-live procedure and the option has been confirmed against the approved plan.

Shop Tip

Integrate flashing with the weather-resistive barrier in shingle-lap order — each higher layer overlapping the layer below — before setting the electrical box. Setting the box first and flashing around it afterward is the most common cause of a leak at an exterior electrical penetration.

Weatherproof Box Selection and Envelope Integration

Select the listed weatherproof box, exterior mounting block, or flashed box support that matches the wiring method, the selected option, and the wet- or damp-location rating of the exact installation point. Coordinate the wall penetration with the approved building-envelope plan and the Book 6 flashing procedure before cutting the opening. Flashing is integrated with the weather-resistive barrier in shingle-lap order — higher layers overlapping lower layers — before the box is set in place, not afterward.

Critical Safety Cue — De-Energize and Verify Before Opening the Wall or Any Device

De-energize the circuit and verify the absence of voltage using the live-dead-live procedure from Chapter 1 before cutting the wall opening, disconnecting any conductor, or removing any device. Treat every conductor as energized until that verification is complete.

Code Alert — Wet- and Damp-Location Listings and GFCI Requirements Vary by Location

Confirm whether the installation location is classified wet or damp, and confirm the exact GFCI requirement, in-use cover requirement, and permitted box or connector listings with the adopted code and the approved plan. Do not assume a damp-location component is acceptable at a location exposed to direct rain.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Box Projection, Gasket Compression, and Cover Fit

Install the weatherproof box at the exact projection and orientation specified by its listing instructions. Compress the gasket and seat the cover according to the manufacturer's procedure. A cover that does not seat flush against a properly compressed gasket is not weather-resistant regardless of how tightly its screws are torqued.

Field Safety Cue — Ladder Setup and Weather Conditions

Set up the ladder on stable, level ground rated for the working height, and confirm weather conditions are suitable for the flashing, sealant, and cladding work before beginning. Stop work and reschedule if wind, rain, or ground conditions make ladder use or exterior sealant application unsafe or ineffective.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Drainage Path Before Sealing

Before applying any sealant, identify every intended drainage opening in the box, cover, or mounting-block instructions. Seal only the joints the manufacturer identifies as sealed joints, and leave every intended drainage opening clear.

F. Option Selection and Preparation

  1. Select either the exterior-light option or the weather-resistant-receptacle option required by the approved plan. Record the selected option on the Weatherproof Installation Worksheet before staging materials.
  2. Review the box, cover, device, gasket, cable, GFCI, corrosion, wet-location, mounting-block, flashing, and environmental requirements. Confirm every component is listed for the exact condition at the installation location.
  3. Coordinate the penetration with the building-envelope plan and Book 6 flashing procedures. Confirm the flashing sequence and materials before cutting the wall opening.
  4. Verify that the location avoids structural conflicts, utilities, door swings, water concentration, and prohibited clearances. Correct the planned location before proceeding if any conflict is found.

G. De-Energization and Wall Opening

  1. De-energize the circuit and verify absence of voltage. Complete the full live-dead-live procedure from Chapter 1 at the target conductors before continuing.
  2. Mark and create the wall opening using the approved method for the cladding, WRB, sheathing, and framing. Confirm the opening matches the selected box or mounting-block template.
  3. Install a listed exterior mounting block or flashed box support where specified. Confirm the support engages the framing according to its listed instructions.

H. Envelope Integration and Box Installation

  1. Integrate flashing with the WRB in shingle-lap order before setting the electrical box. Confirm each higher layer overlaps the layer below.
  2. Install the listed weatherproof box at the required projection and orientation. Confirm the box sits level and at the manufacturer-specified distance from the finished cladding surface.
  3. Route cable through a listed wet- or damp-location connector. Complete the connector installation per the Chapter 5 procedure.
  4. Seal the designated cable or box penetration without blocking drainage paths. Confirm every intended drainage opening remains clear before continuing.
  5. Bond the box and connect equipment-grounding conductors. Complete grounding per the Chapter 8 procedure before device or fixture wiring begins.

I. Device or Fixture Wiring — Weather-Resistant-Receptacle Option

  1. Install a listed weather-resistant receptacle with required GFCI protection. Confirm the receptacle and GFCI device match the approved plan.
  2. Connect line and load conductors only according to the approved GFCI design. Do not interchange line and load conductors.
  3. Install a listed extra-duty in-use cover where required. Confirm the gasket is compressed evenly and the cover closes fully with a cord plugged in where the cover design requires it.

J. Device or Fixture Wiring — Exterior-Light Option

  1. Install the listed exterior fixture, gasket, mounting bracket, and lamps. Confirm the fixture gasket seats flush against the wall or box before the fixture is fastened.
  2. Connect fixture conductors according to the fixture's marked leads and the approved circuit diagram. Complete grounding, ungrounded, and grounded conductor terminations before mounting the fixture body.

K. Sealing, Inspection, and Energization

  1. Apply sealant only at designated joints while leaving intended drainage openings clear. Confirm sealant placement against the manufacturer's identified joint list before it cures.
  2. Complete required inspection before energization. Correct every identified deficiency before requesting reinspection.
  3. Restore power and test device or fixture operation. Confirm normal operation before proceeding to GFCI testing.
  4. Test GFCI protection where applicable. Use the device test button and a listed external tester, and confirm the protected outlet loses power when the GFCI trips.
  5. Conduct the approved controlled water-shedding observation without directing water into energized openings. Apply water only as described in the manufacturer or plan-specified test method.
  6. Inspect mounting, gasket compression, flashing, sealant, cover closure, corrosion protection, and drainage. Correct any deficiency found during this inspection before final documentation.
  7. De-energize before correction. Repeat the live-dead-live verification before touching any conductor to correct a failed item.
  8. Document environmental ratings, protection method, and test results. Complete every field on the Weatherproof Installation Worksheet.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed exterior installation using the same checks regardless of which option was selected.

  • Option check: confirm only one option's component schedule was used throughout the installation.
  • De-energization check: confirm the live-dead-live procedure was completed before the wall was opened and before any correction was made.
  • Envelope check: confirm flashing overlaps the WRB in shingle-lap order and the box sits at the correct projection.
  • Drainage check: confirm sealant is present only at designated joints and every intended drainage opening remains clear.
  • Wiring check: confirm device or fixture conductors are connected exactly per the approved diagram, with GFCI line and load correctly identified where required.
  • Cover or fixture check: confirm the gasket is compressed evenly and the cover or fixture seats flush with no gap exposing energized parts.
  • Test check: confirm device or fixture operation, GFCI trip and reset, and the water-shedding observation all passed.
  • Documentation check: confirm the Weatherproof Installation Worksheet is fully completed and signed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Water Enters the Box or Device Cavity During the Water-Shedding Observation

Probable cause: flashing installed out of shingle-lap order, a gasket that is not fully compressed, a cover that does not seat flush, or sealant applied over an intended drainage opening.
Confirmation: de-energize the circuit, remove the cover, and inspect the flashing lap order, gasket seating, and sealant placement directly.
Correction: re-integrate the flashing in the correct shingle-lap order, replace a damaged or improperly seated gasket, remove sealant blocking any intended drainage opening, and reseal only the designated joints.
Reinspection: repeat the water-shedding observation on the corrected installation.

The Extra-Duty In-Use Cover Will Not Close Fully With a Cord Plugged In

Probable cause: an incompatible cover and box combination, a cover not rated for in-use closure, or conductors folded in a way that obstructs cover closure.
Confirmation: compare the installed cover's listing against the box and the approved plan, and inspect conductor placement inside the box.
Correction: install the correct listed extra-duty in-use cover for the box and refold conductors to clear the cover mechanism.
Reinspection: repeat the cover-closure check with and without a cord plugged in.

The GFCI Device Will Not Trip or Will Not Reset

Probable cause: reversed line and load conductors, a wiring or grounding fault at the device or downstream outlets, or a damaged GFCI device.
Confirmation: de-energize the circuit, verify absence of voltage, and inspect line-load identification and grounding continuity per the Chapter 10 troubleshooting procedure.
Correction: correct line-load reversal, repair the grounding or wiring fault, or replace a damaged GFCI device.
Reinspection: repeat GFCI trip, reset, and downstream protection tests after restoring power.

The Exterior Fixture or Receptacle Shows Corrosion or Discoloration Shortly After Installation

Probable cause: a component not listed for the wet- or damp-location condition present, incompatible metals in contact, or standing water reaching the device from a drainage failure.
Confirmation: de-energize the circuit, inspect the component listing against the actual exposure condition, and inspect for standing water or metal-to-metal contact.
Correction: replace the component with one listed for the actual exposure condition and correct any drainage failure identified.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage and water-shedding checks after replacement.

The Box or Mounting Block Sits Loose or Out of Level After Installation

Probable cause: insufficient framing engagement, an undersized fastener, or a mounting block not seated flush against the cladding.
Confirmation: de-energize the circuit, remove the cover or fixture, and inspect the mounting fasteners and framing engagement directly.
Correction: reset the box or mounting block using the listed fasteners and confirm framing engagement per the manufacturer instructions.
Reinspection: repeat the level and projection check, then repeat the water-shedding observation.
