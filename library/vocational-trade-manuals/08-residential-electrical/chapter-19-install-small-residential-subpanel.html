Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install the cabinet, feeder, isolated neutral system, equipment-grounding system, and branch breakers for one approved small residential subpanel. The finished work product is a securely mounted, correctly segregated, labeled, and inspected subpanel assembly with its neutral isolated from the cabinet, its grounding system bonded to the cabinet, its feeder and branch circuits properly terminated, and its energization controlled by a qualified electrical professional.

You will obtain the approved load calculation, feeder design, and panel schedule; confirm the cabinet and breaker compatibility; establish a fully de-energized work condition upstream of the panel; mount the cabinet; route and terminate the feeder; install and verify the isolated neutral bar and bonded equipment-grounding bar; install branch breakers; perform de-energized continuity and isolation checks; and hand the completed assembly to a qualified electrical professional for inspection and energization.

This project depends directly on the isolation and verified-dead-circuit procedure taught in Chapter 1, the box and connector skills of Chapters 3 through 5, the grounding and bonding procedure of Chapter 8, and the AFCI/GFCI breaker procedure of Chapter 16. It feeds directly into the Chapter 20 capstone, which may draw on a subpanel installed exactly as described here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • EL-109 — Plan the subpanel location, confirming working space, structural support, environmental protection, and cable or raceway routes from the approved feeder design.
  • EL-110 — Mount the panel cabinet plumb, level, and securely using approved fasteners and structural backing.
  • EL-111 — Route feeder conductors to the cabinet without exceeding bend, support, fill, or pulling requirements.
  • EL-112 — Separate neutrals and grounds by installing an isolated neutral bar and a cabinet-bonded equipment-grounding bar and verifying the isolation before termination.
  • EL-113 — Install branch breakers and terminate branch conductors according to the approved panel schedule.
  • EL-114 — Inspect subpanel readiness through de-energized continuity and isolation checks before authorized energization.

Where This Skill Fits

Every branch-circuit project earlier in this book assumed a panel with correctly terminated conductors, an isolated neutral where required, and a bonded equipment-grounding system already in place. This project builds that panel. The isolation discipline from Chapter 1 governs every step here at a larger scale: the feeder termination point and any upstream equipment must be treated as energized until a qualified person establishes and verifies a fully de-energized condition, because operating a main breaker or disconnect does not by itself prove that line-side conductors are dead.

The isolated-neutral and bonded-grounding-bar work performed here is the same principle taught in Chapter 8's box bonding, applied at the subpanel level: neutral and equipment-grounding conductors remain separated downstream of the service disconnect so that normal neutral current cannot use the equipment-grounding path or the metal cabinet as a parallel conductor.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • planning and mounting a listed small residential subpanel cabinet at an approved location
  • routing and terminating one approved feeder to the subpanel
  • installing an isolated neutral bar and a cabinet-bonded equipment-grounding bar and verifying their electrical isolation from each other
  • installing listed branch breakers and terminating branch conductors according to the approved panel schedule
  • performing de-energized continuity, isolation, and neutral-to-ground bonding checks before energization

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • work in this panel except in a fully de-energized training panel disconnected from all power sources, or as permitted work performed under the direct supervision and authority of a licensed or otherwise legally qualified electrical professional
  • remove a service-equipment cover, meter enclosure, or utility seal, or work on any exposed line-side conductor
  • energize the completed subpanel; energization is performed only by the qualified electrical professional using the authorized sequence
  • install a neutral-to-ground bonding jumper, strap, or screw in this subpanel, or otherwise create a second neutral-to-ground bonding point downstream of the service disconnect
  • proceed with feeder termination or breaker installation when a stop-work condition identified in this chapter is present and uncorrected

Any panel, feeder termination point, or upstream equipment condition that fails verification in this chapter must be corrected, repaired, or escalated to a qualified electrical professional before work continues.

Trade Note

A subpanel is not a smaller version of a service panel. Its neutral bar must remain isolated from the cabinet; the equipment-grounding bar is bonded to the cabinet; and neutral-to-ground bonding exists only at the service disconnect upstream. Confusing these two panel types, or copying a service-panel bonding jumper into a subpanel, creates a parallel current path on the equipment-grounding system and is a stop-work condition documented in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not remove the panel cover, and do not touch any bus, terminal, or conductor, until every energy source has been identified, a fully de-energized condition has been established upstream, and absence of voltage has been verified at the bus and terminal locations using the approved rated test method. This project proceeds only in a fully de-energized training panel disconnected from all power sources, or as permitted work under the direct supervision of a licensed or otherwise legally qualified electrical professional. Energization at the end of this project is performed only by that qualified professional using the authorized sequence.

Shop Tip

Mount the isolated neutral bar and confirm its isolation from the cabinet before you land a single conductor. Once feeder and branch neutrals are terminated, a hidden bonding screw or strap left in place from the factory shipping configuration is far harder to see and correct.

Common Field Fundamentals

A subpanel adds a second layer of overcurrent protection, distribution, and grounding downstream of the service disconnect. Every step below either establishes the physical and electrical foundation for that distribution — location, mounting, feeder routing — or protects the isolation between the neutral and the grounding system that keeps normal neutral current off the equipment-grounding path.

Energy Sources, Qualified Authority, and Verification

Before any cover is removed, identify every source that could energize the feeder termination point: the utility service, any generator, photovoltaic system, battery system, or other backfed equipment. A fully de-energized condition is established only when a qualified person confirms all of these sources are controlled and absence of voltage is verified by direct test at every bus and terminal location within the work boundary — not by breaker position or a non-contact detector alone.

Critical Safety Cue — Qualified Authority Governs This Project

This project is performed only in a fully de-energized training panel disconnected from all power sources, or as permitted work under the direction of a licensed or otherwise legally qualified electrical professional. Stop work immediately if the upstream equipment or feeder termination point cannot be made electrically safe, or if line-side parts remain exposed and energized within the work boundary. Never work on exposed energized conductors or equipment.

Measurement Cue — Verify Absence of Voltage at Every Bus and Terminal

Verify the tester on a known energized source, test every bus and terminal location within the work boundary, and reverify the tester on the known energized source. Do not assume the panel is dead because an upstream breaker has been opened.

Field Safety Cue — Structural Support and Working Space

Confirm required working space, headroom, accessibility, structural support, and environmental protection at the selected location before mounting the cabinet. A subpanel installed without adequate working space or structural backing is a stop-work condition regardless of how well the interior is wired.

Code Alert — Isolated Neutral and Bonded Grounding Bar

Confirm the isolated neutral bar has no bonding screw or strap connecting it to the cabinet, and confirm the equipment-grounding bar is bonded to the cabinet using listed hardware. Do not install a neutral-to-ground jumper or bootleg ground in this subpanel. Verify local jurisdictional requirements for subpanel bonding, disconnecting means, and grounding-electrode conductor requirements, which vary by installation.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Panel, Breaker, and Torque Compatibility

Use only breakers listed as compatible with the subpanel bus and breaker family. Tighten every feeder and branch termination to the manufacturer-specified torque using a calibrated method. An incompatible breaker, an unlisted connector, or an untorqued termination is a stop-work condition, not a detail to correct after energization.

E. Design Confirmation and Location Planning

  1. Obtain the approved load calculation, feeder design, panel schedule, fault-current information, grounding design, permit, and inspection requirements. Confirm all values in writing before proceeding.
  2. Confirm the subpanel manufacturer, model, bus rating, breaker family, main-lug or main-breaker configuration, enclosure rating, and installation environment. Reject any cabinet or component that does not match the approved design.
  3. Verify the approved feeder conductor material, size, insulation, raceway or cable, equipment-grounding conductor, overcurrent protection, and neutral requirement. Record each value on the worksheet.
  4. Select a location providing required working space, headroom, accessibility, structural support, environmental protection, and cable routes. Do not select a location that fails any of these requirements.

F. Establishing a Fully De-Energized Condition

  1. Identify all power sources and establish a fully de-energized work condition. Include the utility service, generators, photovoltaic systems, batteries, and any backfed equipment.
  2. Stop work if the upstream equipment or feeder termination point cannot be made electrically safe. Escalate to the qualified electrical professional before proceeding.

G. Cabinet Mounting

  1. Mark the panel height, centerline, fastener points, and finished-wall relationship. Use the location confirmed in Section E.
  2. Install structural backing or listed supports where required. Confirm the backing engages the required framing.
  3. Mount the cabinet plumb, level, and securely using approved fasteners. Verify with a level before final tightening.

H. Feeder and Branch Raceway Preparation

  1. Install listed feeder and branch-circuit raceway or cable connectors. Match each connector to its listed cable, raceway, and enclosure condition.
  2. Close all unused openings with listed closures. Leave no open knockout or unused entry.
  3. Route the approved feeder to the cabinet without exceeding bend, support, fill, or pulling requirements. Confirm the route matches the approved design.
  4. Protect feeder conductors from abrasion and physical damage. Install protection at any location where the feeder could contact a sharp edge or moving component.

I. Isolated Neutral and Bonded Grounding Bar Installation

  1. Prepare feeder conductors to the manufacturer's strip lengths. Avoid nicking or reducing conductor strands.
  2. Install the isolated neutral bar without the bonding screw or strap that would bond it to the cabinet. Confirm the factory shipping hardware, if bonded, has been removed or set to the isolated configuration.
  3. Install the equipment-grounding bar bonded to the cabinet using listed hardware. Confirm the bonding hardware is fully seated.
  4. Verify electrical isolation between the neutral bar and cabinet before termination. Perform a de-energized continuity test and confirm no continuity exists.

J. Feeder Termination

  1. Terminate the feeder equipment-grounding conductor at the grounding bar. Use the listed terminal and torque method.
  2. Terminate the feeder grounded neutral conductor at the isolated neutral terminal. Confirm the neutral terminal is on the isolated bar, not the grounding bar.
  3. Terminate feeder ungrounded conductors at the approved main lugs or breaker. Match each conductor to its designated terminal.
  4. Tighten all feeder terminals to manufacturer-specified torque using a calibrated method. Record each torque value on the worksheet.

K. Branch Circuit Termination

  1. Route and terminate branch-circuit neutrals individually as required. Confirm each neutral lands on the isolated neutral bar in its own listed terminal position.
  2. Route and terminate equipment-grounding conductors at the grounding bar. Confirm each grounding conductor lands on the bonded grounding bar.
  3. Install only listed compatible branch breakers in the scheduled positions. Reject any breaker not listed as compatible with the panel bus.
  4. Terminate branch ungrounded conductors at their assigned breakers. Match each conductor to the position specified in the approved panel schedule.
  5. Maintain conductor identification, bending space, and panel order. Dress conductors so the schedule can be verified visually.

L. Closeout, Verification, and Authorized Energization

  1. Install filler plates, deadfront, labels, and circuit directory. Leave no open breaker position uncovered.
  2. Perform de-energized continuity and isolation checks. Confirm every branch neutral, grounding conductor, and feeder termination matches the approved schedule.
  3. Verify that neutral-to-ground bonding does not exist in the subpanel. Confirm this with a direct continuity test between the isolated neutral bar and the cabinet.
  4. Obtain required inspection before energization. Do not proceed to energization without this approval.
  5. Energize only under the authority and procedure of the qualified electrical professional. The learner does not independently energize this subpanel.
  6. Perform approved voltage, breaker, grounding, and operational checks. Complete these checks with or under the direction of the qualified electrical professional.
  7. Document feeder design, conductor materials, breaker models, torque values, isolation tests, and inspection approval. Complete every field on the Subpanel Readiness Worksheet.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed subpanel using the same checks regardless of the specific feeder design or branch schedule involved.

  • Location check: confirm the cabinet has required working space, headroom, structural support, and environmental protection.
  • Mounting check: confirm the cabinet is plumb, level, and securely fastened.
  • Feeder check: confirm the feeder is routed, protected, and terminated at manufacturer-specified torque.
  • Isolation check: confirm the neutral bar shows no continuity to the cabinet.
  • Bonding check: confirm the equipment-grounding bar shows continuity to the cabinet through listed hardware.
  • Branch schedule check: confirm every branch breaker and conductor matches the approved panel schedule.
  • Documentation check: confirm the Subpanel Readiness Worksheet is fully completed and signed by the qualified electrical professional where required.

Complete the Subpanel Readiness Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 20.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Continuity Exists Between the Neutral Bar and the Cabinet

Probable cause: the factory shipping bonding screw or strap was left in place, or a stray strand or fastener is bridging the neutral bar to the cabinet.
Confirmation: inspect the neutral bar mounting hardware directly and re-test continuity with all branch and feeder neutrals disconnected from the bar.
Correction: remove the bonding screw or strap, or clear the bridging condition, then reinstall the neutral bar per its listed isolated-mounting instructions.
Reinspection: repeat the de-energized continuity test between the neutral bar and the cabinet before landing any conductor.

The Equipment-Grounding Bar Shows No Continuity to the Cabinet

Probable cause: the bonding hardware is missing, loose, painted over, or not listed for the cabinet.
Confirmation: inspect the grounding bar mounting location for paint, corrosion, or an unlisted fastener, and re-test continuity.
Correction: remove paint or debris from the bonding location and install the listed bonding hardware specified by the panel manufacturer.
Reinspection: repeat the continuity test between the grounding bar and the cabinet.

A Branch Breaker Does Not Match the Panel Bus

Probable cause: a breaker from a different manufacturer or product line was substituted for the listed compatible breaker.
Confirmation: compare the breaker model and listing against the panel manufacturer's compatible-breaker documentation.
Correction: remove the incompatible breaker and install the listed compatible breaker specified in the approved panel schedule.
Reinspection: repeat the branch-schedule check for the corrected position.

The Feeder Termination Cannot Reach Manufacturer-Specified Torque

Probable cause: an incorrect conductor size for the terminal, a damaged terminal, or an uncalibrated torque tool.
Confirmation: verify the conductor size against the terminal's listed range and inspect the terminal and torque tool.
Correction: replace an undersized or damaged terminal assembly, or replace the torque tool, then repeat the termination with the correct conductor and tool.
Reinspection: repeat the torque verification and record the final value.

The Upstream Equipment Cannot Be Confirmed De-Energized

Probable cause: an unidentified alternate source, a shared-neutral condition, or line-side equipment that remains energized regardless of breaker position.
Confirmation: re-trace all identified and suspected energy sources with the qualified electrical professional and re-test at the bus and terminal locations.
Correction: control every identified source before continuing; if the upstream equipment cannot be made electrically safe, stop work and escalate to the qualified electrical professional or the utility.
Reinspection: repeat the full de-energized verification at every bus and terminal location before removing the cover further.

The Cabinet Is Not Plumb, Level, or Adequately Supported

Probable cause: insufficient structural backing, an incorrect fastener pattern, or a mounting surface that is out of plane.
Confirmation: re-check the cabinet with a level and inspect the backing and fastener engagement.
Correction: install additional structural backing or correct fastener placement, then remount the cabinet.
Reinspection: repeat the level and structural-support check before continuing to feeder termination.
