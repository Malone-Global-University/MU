Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to integrate every rough-wiring, device, lighting, protection, and testing competency taught in this book into one complete electrical system. The finished work product is one inspected, energized, and documented practice-wall assembly containing a duplex receptacle circuit, a GFCI receptacle with any approved downstream protection, a single-pole switch controlling a light fixture, a two-location three-way switching circuit, continuous equipment grounding, an AFCI, GFCI, or dual-function breaker at the panel, and a complete set of test, inspection, and closeout records.

You will begin from the approved practice-wall electrical plan rather than from your own design. You will lay out and install boxes, route and protect cable, bond and ground every enclosure, splice and pigtail conductors, install each device and switching circuit using the exact procedures from Projects 9 through 12, install the fixture and support system, terminate every conductor, pass a rough inspection, close or finish the wall, install the panel-level circuit protection from Project 16, pass a finish inspection, energize the system under a qualified procedure, test every circuit and device, and complete a documented handoff.

This project does not teach new wiring theory. It requires disciplined, correctly sequenced execution of the competencies already demonstrated in Projects 1 through 19, in the order the approved plan and this book's Energization and Testing Control require.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • EL-115 — Integrate box layout, cable routing, and knockout and connector work into one coordinated practice-wall rough-in.
  • EL-116 — Complete splicing, grounding, and bonding for every enclosure in the assembly and verify a continuous equipment-grounding path.
  • EL-117 — Install and terminate the duplex receptacle, GFCI receptacle, single-pole switch and light, and three-way switching circuits according to their approved diagrams.
  • EL-118 — Install the approved AFCI, GFCI, or dual-function circuit protection and pass rough and finish inspections in sequence.
  • EL-119 — Test every receptacle, switch, and fixture for correct polarity, grounding, protection function, and operation after controlled energization.
  • EL-120 — Label every circuit and device, compile the complete documentation package, and complete a controlled electrical closeout and handoff.

Where This Skill Fits

Every prior project in this book contributes a required piece of this capstone. Project 1 supplies the isolation, lockout, and live-dead-live procedure used before any conductor is touched. Projects 3 through 8 supply box installation, knockouts and connectors, cable routing, splicing, and grounding. Projects 9 through 12 supply the exact receptacle, GFCI, single-pole, and three-way procedures reused here without modification. Projects 13 through 15 supply fixture and support-system practice. Project 16 supplies the panel-level protection procedure. Projects 17 and 18 supply the diagnostic discipline used if a test fails. Project 19 supplies subpanel practice referenced when the approved plan routes the practice wall from a subpanel rather than the main panel.

This project has no later chapter to prepare for. It is the final demonstration that all 120 competencies in this book can be combined into one safe, inspected, and functioning electrical installation.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • obtaining and following the approved practice-wall electrical plan, one-line diagram, circuit schedule, load calculation, box schedule, cable schedule, device schedule, and grounding design
  • laying out, installing, and bonding every box; routing, protecting, and labeling every cable; and completing every splice, pigtail, and grounding connection in the assembly
  • installing the duplex receptacle, GFCI receptacle, single-pole switch and light, three-way switching circuit, and fixture and support system exactly as taught in Projects 9 through 15
  • completing rough inspection, correcting deficiencies, closing or finishing the wall, and installing the approved AFCI, GFCI, or dual-function breaker exactly as taught in Project 16
  • completing finish inspection, controlled energization, full functional testing, and a documented handoff

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • design a different circuit arrangement, conductor size, breaker rating, box fill, or grounding method than the approved practice-wall plan specifies
  • close, finish, or conceal any wiring before the required rough inspection has passed
  • energize the assembly before the required finish inspection and the qualified energization authorization described in the book's Energization and Testing Control section
  • perform any panel-level work covered by Projects 16 or 19 outside a fully de-energized training panel, or outside permitted work under the direction of a licensed or otherwise legally qualified electrical professional

Any deficiency found at rough or finish inspection must be corrected and reinspected before the project continues to the next stage.

Trade Note

This capstone does not compress the book into a shortcut. Every isolation, verification, box-fill calculation, splice, grounding connection, device termination, and test performed here uses the exact procedure taught in its originating project. Treat each embedded procedure — receptacle, GFCI, single-pole, three-way, breaker installation — as fully controlling, and do not substitute a simplified version because the assembly is a practice wall.

Sequencing the Embedded Procedures

Each device and switching circuit in this project uses the exact numbered procedure from its originating chapter: the duplex receptacle from Project 9, the GFCI receptacle from Project 10, the single-pole switch and light from Project 11, and the three-way switching circuit from Project 12. The panel-level protection breaker uses the exact procedure from Project 16. Do not shorten, combine, or reorder these embedded procedures to save time; complete each one in full before moving to the next.

Measurement Cue — Rough and Finish Inspection Are Separate Holds

Complete rough inspection before closing or covering any wiring, and complete finish inspection before energization. Do not treat these as one combined step. A deficiency corrected after wall closure requires reopening the assembly, not a note on the finish-inspection record.

Critical Safety Cue — Energization Requires Full Completion and Authorization

Do not restore or connect power to the practice wall until finish inspection has passed and a qualified person authorizes energization. Treat every conductor as energized until the live-dead-live test proves otherwise at each stage, and keep the assigned breaker locked out and tagged for the entire rough and finish stages.

Field Safety Cue — Multi-Session Work-Area Control

Because this project spans multiple work sessions, re-verify the lockout, tag, and de-energized condition at the start of every session before resuming work, even if the circuit was left controlled at the end of the previous session.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Every Embedded Device and Breaker

Follow the manufacturer's instructions for every box, connector, device, fixture, and breaker used in this assembly, including strip length, terminal method, torque value, and support requirement. Where this project reuses an embedded procedure from an earlier chapter, that chapter's manufacturer-instruction cues remain fully in force here.

  1. Obtain the approved practice-wall electrical plan, one-line diagram, circuit schedule, load calculation, box schedule, cable schedule, device schedule, grounding design, protection requirements, and inspection sequence. Record the plan reference on the Capstone Documentation Package before any layout begins.
  2. Confirm that the practice wall or small structure is structurally complete, dry, accessible, and suitable for electrical work. Stop work and escalate structural deficiencies before proceeding.
  3. Define whether the project will remain a disconnected training assembly or become a permitted energized installation. Record this decision before layout begins, per the Capstone Continuity Rule.
  4. Identify all work reserved for a licensed or qualified electrical professional. Record which steps require that professional's direct performance or supervision.
  5. Prepare the electrical safety, lockout, testing, material-control, inspection, and documentation plan. Confirm the Project 1 isolation and live-dead-live procedure will control every de-energized step in this project.

F. References, Layout, and Box Installation

  1. Establish finished-floor, wall-finish, device-height, and fixture-location references. Use the same reference conventions taught in Project 3.
  2. Lay out receptacle, GFCI, switch, junction, and lighting boxes. Mark each box location from the approved box schedule.
  3. Calculate box fill for every enclosure. Confirm each selected box has sufficient marked volume before installation.
  4. Install new-work boxes at the approved height, setback, alignment, and support condition. Follow the Project 3 procedure exactly.
  5. Install the listed fan- or fixture-support box where required by the plan. Follow the Project 14 procedure exactly if a fan is specified, or the Project 13 support standard for a fixture-only box.

G. Cable Routing, Protection, and Framing Work

  1. Plan cable routes, framing holes, protection plates, and support points. Confirm every route against the approved cable schedule before drilling.
  2. Drill only approved framing locations. Follow the Project 6 drilling-zone and structural-member controls exactly.
  3. Install connectors and close unused enclosure openings. Follow the Project 5 connector-selection and knockout procedure.
  4. Route and secure NM cable without damage, excessive bend, or prohibited proximity. Follow the Project 6 cable-routing and stapling procedure.
  5. Leave the required conductor length and label every cable end. Record each cable's circuit and destination on the Capstone Documentation Package.
  6. Install steel protection plates and approved fireblocking materials after required inspection stages. Do not seal fireblocking penetrations before the applicable inspection has passed.

H. Grounding, Splicing, and Rough Termination

  1. Prepare and bond all metal boxes. Follow the Project 8 grounding-screw and bonding-pigtail procedure exactly.
  2. Create grounding pigtails and verify the continuous equipment-grounding path. Perform the de-energized continuity test at every enclosure before proceeding.
  3. Strip conductors and complete listed splices and pigtails. Follow the Project 7 stripping, splicing, and pigtailing procedure exactly.

I. Device, Switch, and Lighting Circuit Installation

  1. Install the duplex receptacle circuit according to Project 9. Complete the full embedded procedure without modification.
  2. Install the GFCI receptacle and any approved downstream protection according to Project 10. Confirm line and load conductors remain separately identified throughout.
  3. Install the single-pole switch and controlled light according to Project 11. Confirm the switch loop and fixture connections match the approved diagram.
  4. Install the three-way switching circuit according to Project 12. Preserve the approved common and traveler conductor identification throughout.
  5. Install the approved fixture and support system. Confirm the box and support are listed for the fixture type and weight before mounting.
  6. Terminate all grounded, ungrounded, traveler, switched, and equipment-grounding conductors according to the approved diagrams. Inspect every termination before folding conductors into their boxes.

J. Rough Inspection and Closure

  1. Complete rough inspection before closing or covering any wiring. Do not proceed to wall finish until this inspection has passed.
  2. Correct all rough-inspection deficiencies. Reinspect every corrected item before continuing.
  3. Install wall finish or maintain an approved open training assembly. Follow the decision recorded in Step 3.
  4. Install devices, fixtures, cover plates, and required labels. Confirm every cover plate sits flush with no gap exposing energized parts.

K. Circuit Protection, Pre-Energization Checks, and Finish Inspection

  1. Install or connect the approved AFCI, GFCI, or dual-function circuit protection under the Project 16 controls. Complete the full embedded procedure, including torque and neutral-arrangement requirements, without modification.
  2. Update the circuit directory and device-protection labels. Confirm every label matches the as-built condition of the assembly.
  3. Perform de-energized continuity, grounding, neutral-isolation, and short-circuit checks. Record every result on the Capstone Documentation Package before requesting finish inspection.
  4. Obtain required finish inspection and authorization to energize. Do not proceed to Step 34 without this authorization.

L. Energization and Functional Testing

  1. Restore or connect power using the qualified energization procedure. Follow the exact restoration sequence recorded on the readiness worksheet.
  2. Test receptacle polarity and grounding. Use the listed plug-in tester at the duplex and GFCI receptacles.
  3. Test GFCI and AFCI functions using device test controls and listed testers. Confirm trip, reset, and protected-outlet behavior.
  4. Test single-pole and all three-way switch combinations. Confirm correct switching from every location.
  5. Test each light or fixture under normal operation. Confirm secure mounting and absence of flicker.
  6. Inspect for heat, noise, flicker, loose devices, abnormal odor, or breaker operation. Treat any of these as a failed test.
  7. De-energize and correct any failed condition. Return to the de-energized state using the Project 1 procedure before correcting.
  8. Repeat all affected tests. Do not close the punch list on any item until its retest passes.

M. Documentation and Handoff

  1. Compile box-fill calculations, cable records, photographs, grounding checks, torque records, device tests, breaker tests, inspection approvals, and corrections. Assemble the complete Capstone Documentation Package.
  2. Complete the electrical punch list. Confirm every listed deficiency is corrected and retested.
  3. Hand off the system with circuit map, panel directory, test records, shutdown instructions, and maintenance information. Deliver the completed package to the owner, supervisor, or instructor of record.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Assembly Fails Rough Inspection

Probable cause: incorrect box fill, insufficient cable support or protection, missing grounding continuity, or a termination that does not match the approved diagram.
Confirmation: compare the inspector's findings against the box, cable, and grounding schedules and re-inspect the specific enclosure or run identified.
Correction: correct only the identified deficiency using the originating project's procedure; do not close or finish the wall until the correction is verified.
Reinspection: request rough reinspection of the corrected item before proceeding to wall closure.

A Device Circuit Fails Its Functional Test After Energization

Probable cause: reversed line and load conductors, an incorrect common or traveler identification, a loose termination, or a device installed with damaged parts.
Confirmation: apply the Project 17 or Project 18 diagnostic procedure to the specific circuit, using de-energized continuity testing only after the circuit is verified dead.
Correction: correct the identified wiring error using the originating project's procedure; do not substitute a different conductor arrangement than the approved diagram.
Reinspection: repeat the full functional test sequence for the corrected circuit and any circuit sharing the same box or cable run.

The Installed Breaker Trips Immediately or Will Not Reset

Probable cause: a shared-neutral conflict, a wiring error on the protected circuit, or a breaker incompatible with the panel or circuit.
Confirmation: de-energize and verify absence of voltage, then inspect the breaker's neutral connection and the protected circuit's conductors against the approved diagram.
Correction: correct the wiring error or confirm breaker compatibility before attempting to reset; do not repeatedly reset a breaker that trips.
Reinspection: repeat the breaker test-button and functional test sequence after correction.

Finish Inspection Reveals an Item Concealed During Rough Stage

Probable cause: a connection or grounding path was not photographed or verified before the wall was closed or finished.
Confirmation: review the rough-inspection photographs and records for the item in question.
Correction: open the wall at the affected location only as required to verify or correct the concealed item.
Reinspection: repeat both the rough-equivalent inspection of the reopened area and the finish inspection before energization.

The Documentation Package Is Incomplete at Handoff

Probable cause: a record was not completed at the time of the corresponding step, such as a torque value, test result, or inspection approval.
Confirmation: compare the Capstone Documentation Package against the Capstone Readiness Schedule item by item.
Correction: complete the missing record from direct re-inspection or retest where the original condition remains verifiable; do not record a value that was not actually measured.
Reinspection: confirm the completed package against the Readiness Schedule before handoff.
