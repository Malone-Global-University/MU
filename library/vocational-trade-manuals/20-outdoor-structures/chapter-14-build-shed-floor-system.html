Job Overview

This project qualifies you to build one small detached shed floor platform from an approved gravel pad, skid, pier, or footing support system through a square, level, sheathed, and inspected subfloor ready to carry wall framing. The finished work product is a completed shed floor platform: prepared and compacted base, leveled supports on the approved layout, a squared and blocked joist frame secured to its supports, and fastened subfloor sheathing that has passed the required floor inspection.

You will confirm the site is graded and drained away from the platform, prepare the approved gravel pad or set support locations, install and level skids, piers, footings, or bases, frame and square the rim and joists, secure the frame to its supports, install required blocking and anchorage, lay out and fasten the subfloor sheathing, and inspect the finished platform before any wall framing begins.

This project assumes Project 01's site readiness package, Project 02's work-area setup, and Project 03's layout and squaring skills are already complete and available for reference. It establishes the platform every wall, opening, and roof decision in Project 15 and Project 16 depends on. No wall plate is cut and no stud is raised until this project's floor inspection is complete.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • OS-079 — Lay out the shed floor frame from approved dimensions, support locations, and finished-floor elevation.
  • OS-080 — Install skids or supports at the specified spacing, level, and drainage-preserving elevation.
  • OS-081 — Frame joists on approved spacing with required blocking and connectors.
  • OS-082 — Install rim boards and secure the frame square, level, and anchored to its supports.
  • OS-083 — Install subfloor sheathing with the approved fastening schedule, joint pattern, and panel gaps.
  • OS-084 — Inspect the finished shed floor platform for square, level, bearing, ventilation, drainage, and edge condition.

Where This Skill Fits

This project sits at the start of the shed-construction sequence. Project 15's wall layout, Project 16's roof bearing, and Project 17's siding and door clearances all reference the finished-floor elevation, footprint dimensions, and anchorage established here. A floor platform that has not passed its inspection cannot proceed to wall framing in Project 15.

The habit built in this chapter — confirming drainage before the first support is set, leveling every support before the frame is assembled, and squaring the frame before sheathing conceals it — is the same habit that governs every framed structure in this book, from a small deck to the Chapter 20 capstone shed option.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming site grading and drainage away from the proposed platform
  • preparing an approved gravel pad or setting skid, pier, or footing support locations
  • leveling supports while preserving site drainage and required clearance
  • cutting, treating, and assembling the rim and floor-joist frame
  • squaring the frame using diagonal measurements before permanent connection
  • securing the frame to its supports with the specified anchorage or uplift devices
  • laying out and fastening subfloor sheathing on the approved pattern and schedule
  • inspecting the completed platform before wall framing begins

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • frame or raise shed walls before the floor platform has passed its required inspection
  • substitute an unapproved support type, spacing, or footing depth for the one specified in the approved shed plan
  • bury a wood skid or post in soil or concrete without the exposure-rated treatment and support detail the plan specifies
  • fasten sheathing without confirming joist layout, blocking, and required panel gaps first

Any support condition, level reading, or square measurement that cannot be confirmed must be resolved before this project is declared complete.

Trade Note

A shed floor platform carries every load above it for the life of the structure, and every framing decision in Project 15 and Project 16 assumes the platform underneath is square, level, and properly supported. Rushing past the leveling or squaring steps to "catch up" on schedule is the single most common source of walls that will not plumb up correctly later in the sequence.

Setup Standard

Do not set the first support until the site is confirmed graded and draining away from the platform location, and do not fasten sheathing until the frame has passed its dimension, diagonal, level, and blocking check. A frame assembled out of square must be corrected before a single sheet of subfloor is installed.

Shop Tip

Check level at every support before assembling the frame, not just at the corners. A single low or high support point telegraphs through the entire floor plane and is far harder to correct once the frame and sheathing are in place.

Supports, Drainage, and Bearing

Every support is set on the bearing and spacing shown in the approved plan, not adjusted in the field to compensate for an ungraded site. A support set on soft, sloped, or undrained ground risks settlement that telegraphs through the entire floor plane after the platform is loaded.

Code Alert — Support Type, Spacing, and Frost Requirements

Support type, spacing, footing depth, and frost protection vary by jurisdiction and by the approved shed plan. Verify the current requirements for the specific site before setting any support, and do not substitute an unapproved support type for the one specified.

Critical Safety Cue — Ground-Contact Treatment and Material Compatibility

Confirm ground-contact treatment rating and connector compatibility before installing any skid, pier, or footing hardware. Untreated or insufficiently treated wood in ground contact decays and can fail without visible warning, risking sudden settlement or collapse of the platform above.

Field Safety Cue — Cutting and Lifting Frame Members

Support long rim boards and joists during cutting to prevent binding or kickback. Use team lifting or approved equipment for heavy or awkward members, and keep the cutting area clear of tripping hazards and bystanders.

Measurement Cue — Check Level at Every Support Point

Check level at each individual support, not just at the corners of the footprint. A support that is level relative to its neighbor but out of level relative to the finished-floor elevation still produces an unlevel platform once the frame is assembled.

E. Preparation — Review and Base Prep

  1. Review shed dimensions, use, floor loads, supports, skids, piers, anchors, beams, joists, spans, subfloor, ventilation, drainage, and permits. Confirm the approved plan matches the layout established in Project 03.
  2. Confirm the site is graded and drained away from the platform. Do not proceed if standing water, ponding, or reverse slope is present at the footprint.
  3. Establish the shed footprint and finished-floor elevation. Cross-check against the protected offsets and reference elevation from Project 03.
  4. Prepare and compact the approved gravel pad or support locations. Remove organic material, loose fill, and debris before compacting.

F. Support Installation and Leveling

  1. Install skids, piers, footings, or bases at specified spacing. Confirm each support location matches the approved plan before setting.
  2. Verify ground-contact treatment and material compatibility. Confirm connectors and fasteners are compatible with the treated lumber before installation.
  3. Level supports while preserving site drainage. Recheck each individual support point, not only the corners, using a string line or laser reference across the full footprint.

G. Frame Assembly and Squaring

  1. Cut rim and floor-joist members. Confirm each member is cut square and to the length shown in the approved plan.
  2. Treat field cuts. Apply the specified preservative to every cut end and drilled hole in treated lumber before assembly.
  3. Assemble the floor frame. Build the rim perimeter and install joists at the approved spacing on a level surface where possible.
  4. Check dimensions and diagonals. Adjust the frame until both diagonals match within the approved tolerance before securing any permanent connection.
  5. Install joists on approved spacing. Maintain consistent spacing and crown orientation across the full frame.
  6. Install blocking and connectors. Complete every required blocking and connector location shown in the approved plan.

H. Anchorage, Sheathing, and Inspection

  1. Secure the frame to skids or supports. Use the specified connectors and fasteners at every support location.
  2. Install uplift or anchorage devices. Complete every anchorage point shown in the approved plan before proceeding to sheathing.
  3. Verify frame level, square, bearing, and ventilation. Recheck the full frame against the acceptance standards before any panel is installed.
  4. Lay out subfloor sheathing with staggered joints. Confirm panel orientation matches the approved plan.
  5. Maintain specified panel gaps. Use the manufacturer-specified spacing tool or gauge at every joint.
  6. Apply adhesive only where the floor design requires it. Follow the manufacturer's application instructions where adhesive is specified.
  7. Fasten sheathing using the approved schedule. Complete every required fastener location without overdriving or missing framing.
  8. Protect panel edges and underside exposure as specified. Apply edge sealant or protection where the approved plan requires it.
  9. Check platform plane, square, stiffness, anchorage, drainage, and edge condition. Walk the full platform and confirm no soft spots, movement, or drainage obstruction remain.
  10. Complete required floor inspection before wall framing. Record every finding on the shed floor worksheet before Project 15 begins.

I. Recheck and Closeout Procedure

  1. Recheck every incomplete or unclear item. Classify each gap as missing documentation, an unconfirmed support elevation, an unresolved connector location, or an unresolved drainage condition before closing it out.
  2. Document support and anchor locations. Photograph the finished frame and supports before sheathing conceals them, and file the completed records with the project documentation.

Common Inspection Review

Inspect the completed shed floor platform using the same checks regardless of which support type was selected.

  • Base and drainage check: confirm the site is graded and draining away from the platform and the base was compacted before supports were set.
  • Support check: confirm every skid, pier, or footing is level, spaced, and bearing per the approved plan.
  • Frame check: confirm dimensions and diagonals match the approved layout and every required blocking and connector location is complete.
  • Anchorage check: confirm the frame is secured to its supports and every uplift device is installed per the approved detail.
  • Sheathing check: confirm panel layout, gaps, and fastening schedule are complete, with no soft spots or movement across the platform.

Complete the Shed Floor Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 15.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Platform Is Out of Level After Frame Assembly

Probable cause: one or more supports were not individually leveled before the frame was assembled, or a support settled after loading.
Confirmation: check level at every support point against the finished-floor elevation reference.
Correction: shim, re-level, or reset the affected support and recheck the frame before proceeding to sheathing.
Reinspection: repeat the level check across the full platform.

Frame Diagonals Do Not Match

Probable cause: the rim frame was fastened before diagonals were checked, or a corner shifted during assembly.
Confirmation: re-measure both diagonals and compare against the approved tolerance.
Correction: loosen the affected corner connections, adjust the frame until diagonals match, and re-fasten.
Reinspection: repeat the square check before installing blocking or sheathing.

Sheathing Was Fastened Before Blocking Was Complete

Probable cause: the crew proceeded to sheathing before all required blocking and connector locations were installed.
Confirmation: compare the installed blocking against the approved framing plan from below or before further concealment.
Correction: remove sheathing as needed to complete missing blocking and connectors, then re-fasten.
Reinspection: repeat the frame check before continuing sheathing.

Platform Traps Water or Blocks Drainage Beneath the Frame

Probable cause: the base was not properly graded, or soil, mulch, or debris accumulated against the framing after installation.
Confirmation: inspect beneath the platform for standing water, ponding, or blocked drainage paths.
Correction: regrade the base, clear debris, and confirm the required ventilation clearance beneath the frame.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage and ventilation check.

Panel Gaps or Fastening Schedule Were Not Followed

Probable cause: sheathing was installed without a spacing gauge or the fastening schedule was applied inconsistently across the platform.
Confirmation: inspect panel joints and fastener spacing against the approved schedule.
Correction: correct gaps and add missing fasteners without overdriving existing ones.
Reinspection: repeat the sheathing check on the corrected panels.

Floor Inspection Was Skipped Before Wall Framing Began

Probable cause: the crew proceeded directly to wall layout without completing or recording the floor inspection.
Confirmation: review the shed floor worksheet for a completed and signed inspection entry.
Correction: halt wall framing, complete the floor inspection, and correct any incomplete item before resuming.
Reinspection: repeat the full floor inspection before Project 15 begins.

Cleanup

This project ends with the floor platform inspected, documented, and protected for the wall framing that follows, not left exposed and undocumented.

  • Remove cutoffs, packaging, and debris from the platform and surrounding work area
  • Photograph the completed frame and supports before storing tools, since sheathing will conceal the frame permanently
  • Store unused lumber, connectors, and fasteners for reuse in Project 15's wall framing
  • Label and file the shed floor worksheet, support records, and photographs together
  • Cover the finished platform if wall framing will not begin immediately, protecting it from weather exposure

Field Terms

Finished-Floor Elevation
The approved top-of-floor height used to set every support and framing reference.
Skid
A ground-contact-rated timber support running beneath the floor frame.
Pier / Footing
An individual point support carrying the frame at defined spacing.
Rim Board
The perimeter framing member that closes the joist ends and squares the frame.
Blocking
Short framing members maintaining joist spacing and transferring load.
Subfloor Sheathing
The structural panel layer fastened over the joist frame.
Ventilation Clearance
The required open space beneath the floor frame protecting it from soil moisture.
Uplift Device
Hardware resisting upward or lateral forces on the anchored frame.
Floor Inspection
The completed check of plane, square, stiffness, anchorage, drainage, and edge condition before wall framing.
Shed Floor Worksheet
The document recording support locations, frame measurements, anchorage, and inspection results.
