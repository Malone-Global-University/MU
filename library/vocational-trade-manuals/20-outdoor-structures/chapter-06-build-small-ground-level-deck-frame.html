Job Overview

This project qualifies you to build the structural frame of a small detached ground-level deck on the posts and footings accepted in Project 05, and to bring that frame to square, level or approved slope, and full connector completion before any decking is installed over it. The finished work product is a beam-supported, joist-framed deck frame: beams set on accepted bearing, a squared and braced rim-joist perimeter, joists installed on layout with every required hanger fastener, blocking and bridging in place, lateral and uplift connections completed, and the whole assembly inspected and documented before it is concealed by decking.

You will confirm the posts and supports from Project 05 are accepted and ready to carry load, inspect beam and joist material, assemble and set beams on approved bearing, square and brace the rim-joist perimeter, lay out and install joists with the required hangers and blocking, and complete a framing inspection before Project 07 covers the frame with decking boards.

This project depends entirely on Project 05. No beam is set on a post that has not been plumbed, aligned, connected, and released to framing. The frame built here carries every deck board, guard post, and stair connection addressed in the chapters that follow, so square, level, and connector accuracy established now are not corrected later without removing completed decking.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • OS-031 — Install beams on accepted bearing, post caps, or approved connections at the correct level or specified slope.
  • OS-032 — Install rim or end joists and square the perimeter frame using diagonal measurements.
  • OS-033 — Lay out joists from an approved reference and fasten them on bearing or in listed hangers with the full required fastener set.
  • OS-034 — Install blocking, bridging, joist restraints, and lateral or uplift connections as specified.
  • OS-035 — Square, brace, and hold the frame stable through installation without notching or drilling members outside approved limits.
  • OS-036 — Inspect and document the completed deck frame, including dimensions, diagonals, bearing, connectors, and blocking, before decking begins.

Where This Skill Fits

This project follows directly from the accepted, plumb, aligned posts in Project 05 and precedes Project 07's decking installation, which conceals the framing built here. Project 08's stair stringers attach to this frame's rim or approved landing framing. Project 09's guard posts require a verified structural load path into the blocking installed in this project. A frame that is out of square, under-blocked, or missing required hanger fasteners when this project closes carries that error into every later chapter that builds on or conceals it.

The habit built here — square the frame with diagonals before trusting it, drive every required hanger fastener rather than a partial set, and inspect before concealment — is the same habit that governs every framed structure in this book, from a small deck to the Project 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming posts and supports from Project 05 are accepted and ready to carry beam and joist loads
  • inspecting and assembling beams according to the approved built-up fastening schedule, and setting them on approved bearing
  • installing and squaring a rim-joist perimeter using diagonal measurements
  • laying out and installing joists on bearing or in listed hangers with the full required fastener set
  • installing blocking, bridging, joist restraints, and lateral or uplift connections as specified
  • bracing the frame to hold square and level throughout construction
  • inspecting and documenting the completed frame before it is concealed by decking

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • set a beam or joist on a post or support that has not passed Project 05's plumb, alignment, and connector checks
  • assemble a built-up beam using a fastening pattern other than the one specified in the approved plan
  • notch, drill, or cut a beam, joist, or rim member outside the limits approved for that member
  • proceed to Project 07 decking with a frame that has not passed the dimension, diagonal, bearing, connector, and blocking checks in this project

Any frame condition that cannot be brought to square, level, or full connector completion within the approved tolerance must be corrected and rechecked before this project is declared complete.

Trade Note

A deck frame is only as reliable as its weakest hanger. Every joist in this project is treated as unsupported until every required fastener hole in its hanger is filled with the specified nail or screw, regardless of how solid the joist feels once it is toe-nailed or seated by hand. Skipping hanger holes to save time is one of the most common sources of deflection and squeak discovered later, after decking conceals the frame.

Setup Standard

Do not install a single joist until the rim-joist perimeter has been squared by diagonal measurement and braced to hold that square. A joist installed against an unsquared perimeter must be removed and reinstalled after the perimeter is corrected.

Shop Tip

Crown every joist before it goes into a hanger, and install every joist crown-up in the same direction. A frame built from inconsistently crowned joists telegraphs a wavy decking surface that is difficult to correct once decking is fastened down.

Support Confirmation Before Any Beam Is Loaded

No beam is set on a post until Project 05's inspection record confirms that post is plumb, aligned, connected, and released to framing. Loading a beam onto a post that has not been confirmed risks transferring an undetected plumb or connection defect into the entire frame above it.

Code Alert — Hanger and Fastener Schedule

Joist hangers, beam connectors, and their fasteners are controlled by the approved connection detail, not by what happens to be on hand. Verify the exact hanger model, fastener type, size, and quantity specified, and fill every required fastener hole. A hanger with empty holes is not an approved connection.

Critical Safety Cue — Bracing Before Loading the Frame

Never load decking, tools, or materials onto a beam or joist frame that is not fully braced and connected. Keep temporary bracing in place until every joist is fastened on bearing or in an approved hanger, and do not stand or place weight on an unblocked cantilever or an unsupported rim span. An unbraced frame can shift or collapse under load without warning.

Field Safety Cue — Cutting and Handling Framing Members

Support beams and joists securely during cutting so the cut section cannot bind the blade or fall unexpectedly. Keep hands and cords clear of the cutting path, and treat every field cut and drilled hole in pressure-treated material as a required preservative-treatment point before the member is installed.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Square by Diagonals, Not by Eye

A rim-joist perimeter can look square and still be out of tolerance. Measure both corner-to-corner diagonals before installing any joist, and adjust the frame until the two diagonals match within the approved tolerance. Rechecking diagonals after every brace adjustment prevents a small error from compounding across the full frame.

E. Preparation — Review and Material Inspection

  1. Review deck dimensions, freestanding support plan, footing and post locations, beam sizes, joist sizes, spans, cantilevers, blocking, connectors, and finished elevation. Confirm the approved specification before touching any framing member.
  2. Confirm posts, supports, and drainage are accepted. Cross-check against the Project 05 post installation records before proceeding.
  3. Verify required clearance and ventilation between framing, soil, vegetation, and structures. Confirm the approved elevation preserves drainage beneath the frame.
  4. Inspect beams and joists for grade, treatment, crown, warp, damage, and length. Set aside any member that does not meet the specification.

F. Beam Installation Procedure

  1. Assemble built-up beams only according to the approved fastening schedule. Do not substitute a different nail or bolt pattern for the specified schedule.
  2. Set beams on direct bearing or listed post caps. Confirm full approved bearing contact at every post.
  3. Align and temporarily brace beams. Hold each beam in position and orientation before checking level.
  4. Check beam level or approved slope. Adjust bracing and shim only as the approved detail permits before proceeding.

G. Rim Joist and Frame Squaring Procedure

  1. Install end or rim joists. Set the perimeter members to the approved outside dimensions.
  2. Square the perimeter frame using diagonal measurements. Adjust corners until both diagonals match within the approved tolerance.
  3. Brace the frame to hold square. Install temporary bracing sufficient to prevent the perimeter from shifting during joist installation.

H. Joist Layout and Installation Procedure

  1. Mark joist layout from the approved reference. Use a chalk line and tape measure to lay out every joist location along the rim.
  2. Crown joists consistently where required. Sight each joist and mark the crown before installation.
  3. Install joists on bearing or in listed hangers. Seat each joist fully before fastening.
  4. Install every required hanger fastener. Fill every specified fastener hole in every hanger with no exceptions.
  5. Maintain approved cantilever, bearing, and end clearance. Verify each joist end meets the approved dimension before final fastening.
  6. Install blocking, bridging, joist restraints, and rim connections. Complete every location shown on the approved framing plan.
  7. Install lateral and uplift connections where specified. Use the specified hardware at every required location.
  8. Do not notch or drill joists outside approved limits. Verify any required opening against the approved detail before cutting.
  9. Protect field cuts and holes in treated lumber. Apply preservative to every cut end or drilled hole before the member is concealed.
  10. Verify stair, guard-post, border, picture-frame, and decking-support blocking. Confirm every later-chapter connection point has the blocking it will require.

I. Recheck and Closeout Procedure

  1. Check frame dimensions, diagonals, level or slope, beam bearing, joist spacing, connectors, blocking, and bracing. Correct any component that falls outside the approved tolerance.
  2. Complete required framing inspection before decking. Confirm every item on the Assessed Deck Framing Schedule is recorded and accepted.
  3. Photograph concealed framing. Record every hanger, blocking location, and connection that decking will cover, and label and retain the completed record for reference throughout the rest of this book.

Common Inspection Review

Inspect the completed deck frame using the same checks regardless of beam or joist configuration.

  • Support check: confirm every beam bears on a post already accepted, plumb, and connected.
  • Beam check: confirm beams are assembled per the approved schedule, set on full bearing, and level or to the specified slope.
  • Square check: confirm the perimeter diagonals are equal within the approved tolerance.
  • Joist check: confirm layout, crown direction, and full hanger fastener sets at every joist.
  • Blocking check: confirm blocking, bridging, restraints, and lateral or uplift hardware are installed at every specified location.
  • Documentation check: confirm the framing inspection is recorded and photographed before decking begins.

Complete the Deck Frame Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 07.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Frame Diagonals Do Not Match After Joists Are Installed

Probable cause: the perimeter was not squared and braced by diagonal measurement before joist installation began, allowing it to drift out of square as joists were added.
Confirmation: remeasure both diagonals and compare the difference to the approved tolerance.
Correction: loosen affected connections, re-square the perimeter to matching diagonals, re-brace, and reinstall any joist that was fastened out of position.
Reinspection: repeat the perimeter square check on the corrected frame.

Joist Hanger Has Empty Fastener Holes

Probable cause: a hanger was partially nailed to save time, with the expectation of finishing later, and the remaining holes were missed.
Confirmation: inspect every hanger against the specified fastener count and pattern.
Correction: drive the specified fastener into every remaining hole using the correct nail or screw type.
Reinspection: repeat the connector check on every hanger in the frame, not only the one found incomplete.

Beam Reads Out of Level or Off the Specified Slope

Probable cause: the beam was checked for level at only one point instead of along its full length, or bracing shifted after the check.
Confirmation: recheck level or slope at multiple points along the beam with a 4-ft level.
Correction: loosen the beam connection, adjust bearing or shim only as the approved detail permits, and refasten.
Reinspection: repeat the beam level or slope check after refastening.

Joists Were Crowned Inconsistently

Probable cause: joists were installed as they were picked up rather than sighted and marked for crown direction before setting.
Confirmation: sight along the top of the installed frame for reversed or unmarked crowns.
Correction: remove and reinstall any joist installed crown-down or with an inconsistent crown direction.
Reinspection: repeat the joist layout check across the corrected run.

Blocking Was Omitted at a Guard-Post or Stair Connection Point

Probable cause: blocking locations required for later-chapter connections were not cross-referenced against the approved framing plan before decking was scheduled.
Confirmation: compare the installed blocking against every guard-post, stair, border, and picture-frame location on the approved plan.
Correction: install the missing blocking using the specified fasteners before decking conceals the frame.
Reinspection: repeat the blocking check against the full approved plan.

Beam or Joist Was Loaded Before Its Support Was Confirmed Accepted

Probable cause: framing proceeded against schedule pressure before Project 05's post installation record confirmed plumb, alignment, and connector completion.
Confirmation: check the post installation record against the date the beam or joist was loaded.
Correction: remove the affected framing, confirm the post has passed its Project 05 checks, and reset the framing.
Reinspection: repeat the support confirmation check before reloading the affected framing.

Cleanup

  • Store unused hangers, fasteners, and blocking material for reuse in later framing chapters
  • Label and file the completed deck frame worksheet with the Project 05 post installation records
  • Return the documented framing record to the project records for reference throughout the rest of this book

Field Terms

Bearing
The full, approved contact between a beam or joist and the support that carries its load.
Rim Joist
The joist forming the outer edge of the frame, used to reference perimeter square.
Crown
The slight edge-wise curve in a joist, installed consistently crown-up.
Joist Hanger
The specified connector supporting a joist end, filled with every required fastener hole.
Blocking
Short framing members installed between joists to resist rotation and support edges or fixtures.
Bridging
Diagonal or solid members installed between joists to maintain spacing and resist twisting.
Lateral / Uplift Connection
Specified hardware resisting sideways movement or upward force on the frame.
Diagonal Check
Measuring both corner-to-corner diagonals of a rectangular frame to confirm square.
Cantilever
The portion of a joist extending beyond its beam bearing, limited to the approved dimension.
Deck Frame Worksheet
The document recording beam, joist, connector, blocking, and inspection findings for the frame.
