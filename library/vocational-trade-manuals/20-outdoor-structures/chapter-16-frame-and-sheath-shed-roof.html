Job Overview

This project qualifies you to frame, brace, connect, sheath, and inspect one small shed roof using either site-built rafters or prefabricated trusses, exactly as specified by the approved shed plan. The finished work product is a fully framed, permanently braced, uplift-connected, sheathed, and inspected roof structure, protected temporarily and ready for the weatherproof roofing assembly covered by Book 5.

You will select the approved roof-framing option, confirm the walls beneath it are plumb, braced, and accepted, establish safe access and fall-protection controls, cut or receive the rafters or trusses, raise and connect them to the wall plates with listed uplift connectors, install ridge, ties, blocking, and permanent bracing, sheath the roof deck to the approved pattern and fastening schedule, and inspect the complete roof structure before any covering is installed.

This project assumes Project 15, Frame Shed Walls and Openings, is complete and accepted, and it hands off a structurally sound, protected roof deck to Project 17, which installs siding, trim, and the shed door, and to the roofing sequence in Book 5. No sheathing is covered with finish roofing, and no truss is cut, drilled, or notched, until this project's required inspections are complete.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • OS-091 — Lay out roof framing from the approved span, pitch, and framing plan.
  • OS-092 — Cut rafters or install prefabricated trusses according to the approved design and manufacturer instructions.
  • OS-093 — Install roof framing, including ridge, ties, blocking, gable framing, and permanent bracing.
  • OS-094 — Sheath the roof deck to the approved orientation, spacing, and fastening schedule.
  • OS-095 — Maintain approved overhangs, spacing, and alignment throughout framing and sheathing.
  • OS-096 — Inspect the completed roof structure before any weatherproof roofing assembly is installed.

Where This Skill Fits

This project sits between Project 15's wall framing and Project 17's siding, trim, and door work, and it is the structural handoff point to Book 5's roofing sequence. Every eave, rake, and roof-plane condition referenced later in the siding and trim work depends on the framing and sheathing accepted here. A roof that has not passed the framing and sheathing inspection required by this project cannot be covered in Project 17 or roofed under Book 5.

The habit built in this chapter — confirming wall readiness before loading the roof, keeping temporary bracing in place until the permanent load path is complete, and never modifying a prefabricated truss without written engineering authorization — governs every later roofed structure in this book, from a garden shed to the Chapter 20 capstone if the shed option is selected.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • selecting the approved roof-framing option of site-built rafters or prefabricated trusses
  • establishing safe scaffold, platform, ladder, lifting, and roof-edge fall-protection controls before framing begins
  • laying out, cutting, test-fitting, and installing rafters, or receiving, inspecting, and installing trusses without modification
  • connecting rafters or trusses to wall plates with listed uplift connectors
  • installing ridge, rafter ties, ceiling ties, blocking, gable framing, and permanent bracing
  • sheathing the roof deck with approved orientation, spacing, clips, and fastening schedule
  • inspecting the completed roof structure and installing temporary weather protection or coordinating immediate underlayment

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • cut, drill, notch, or otherwise modify a prefabricated truss without written engineering authorization
  • remove temporary bracing before permanent ties, blocking, and bracing establish a complete stable load path
  • load the roof frame or walk on unsupported sheathing edges before framing is inspected
  • treat sheathing as a finished, weatherproof roof, or defer temporary weather protection when rain is possible before roofing begins
  • proceed to Project 17 or to roofing under Book 5 with an incomplete or unsigned framing and sheathing inspection

Any framing member, connector, or sheathing condition that cannot be confirmed against the approved plan must be resolved before this project is declared complete.

Trade Note

A shed roof carries snow, wind, uplift, and occupancy-adjacent loads even though the space below is not habitable. Treat every tie, connector, and brace specified on the approved plan as load-bearing, not decorative, and never substitute a lighter connector or omit a tie because the framing "looks" strong enough by eye.

Setup Standard

Do not raise a single rafter or truss until temporary bracing stock, ladders or scaffold, and the approved lift plan are staged and ready. A rafter or truss set without immediate temporary bracing is an unsupported member and must be stabilized before workers move to the next one.

Shop Tip

Cut, test-fit, and formally approve one rafter pattern before cutting the remainder. A pattern accepted after test-fitting saves far more time than discovering a birdsmouth error after eight rafters are already cut.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every roof framed under this project follows the same sequence: confirm the walls are ready, select and prepare the framing option, raise and immediately brace each member, connect with listed uplift hardware, install ties and permanent bracing, sheath to the approved schedule, and inspect before any covering goes on. Skipping ahead to sheathing before ties and bracing are complete is one of the most common causes of roof-framing rework.

Rafters Versus Trusses

The approved plan specifies one roof-framing option, and that option is not substituted in the field. Site-built rafters require a verified pattern, correct birdsmouth geometry, and full ridge and tie connections. Prefabricated trusses require the exact bracing sequence in the manufacturer's shipping and installation documents and are never cut, drilled, or notched without written engineering authorization.

Critical Safety Cue — Fall Protection and Roof-Edge Work

Establish approved ladders, scaffold, platforms, and fall-protection equipment before any worker goes onto wall plates, rafters, trusses, or sheathing. Never step onto unsupported sheathing edges or unbraced framing. A fall from roof height can cause serious injury or death.

Code Alert — Truss Modification and Uplift Connectors

Local code and the truss manufacturer's engineering govern truss handling, bracing, and connection. Never cut, drill, or notch a truss without written engineering authorization, and install every uplift connector specified by the approved plan; substituting or omitting a connector removes a required part of the load path.

Field Safety Cue — Temporary Bracing

Install temporary bracing immediately after each rafter or truss is raised and connected. Do not leave a member standing unbraced while moving to the next one, and do not remove temporary bracing until permanent ties, blocking, and bracing establish a complete stable load path.

Measurement Cue — Spacing and Overhang

Verify rafter or truss spacing, ridge alignment, and overhang at both eaves and both rakes before sheathing begins. An error carried into sheathing layout is far more difficult to correct once panels are fastened.

E. Preparation — Framing-Option Review and Access Planning

  1. Select the approved roof-framing option. Confirm whether the plan specifies site-built rafters or prefabricated trusses, and do not substitute one for the other.
  2. Review roof span, pitch, snow, wind, uplift, overhangs, ridge, ties, connectors, sheathing, and ventilation requirements. Confirm the fall-protection plan for the height and duration of the work.
  3. Confirm shed walls are plumb, braced, anchored, and accepted. Verify the Project 15 wall-framing inspection is complete before loading the walls with roof framing.
  4. Establish safe scaffold, platform, ladder, lifting, and roof-edge controls. Stage temporary bracing stock and fall-protection equipment before the first member is raised.

F. Rafter Pattern Preparation (Site-Built Rafters)

  1. Prepare a full-size approved pattern. Use the approved span, pitch, and overhang dimensions from the plan.
  2. Mark plumb cut, seat cut, tail, ridge, and overhang. Lay out each cut line using a framing square and stair gauges.
  3. Verify birdsmouth and notch limits. Confirm the remaining rafter depth after the notch meets the approved bearing requirement.
  4. Cut the first rafter without overcutting. Stop each cut at the layout line to preserve full bearing and structural depth.
  5. Test-fit and approve the pattern. Set the first rafter in place at the wall plate and ridge location before cutting any additional rafters.
  6. Cut remaining rafters from the approved pattern. Use the tested rafter as the template only after it has been verified against the approved layout.

G. Truss Delivery and Handling (Prefabricated Trusses)

  1. Inspect delivery documents, orientation, damage, and bracing instructions. Confirm the truss package matches the approved design and span.
  2. Do not cut, drill, notch, or modify trusses. Any field change requires written engineering authorization before installation proceeds.
  3. Follow the manufacturer's temporary and permanent bracing instructions exactly. Truss bracing sequence is engineered and is not a field judgment call.

H. Raising, Connecting, and Bracing the Roof Structure

  1. Raise rafters or trusses using the approved lift plan. Use adequate workers or lifting equipment for the size and weight of each member.
  2. Install temporary bracing immediately. Brace each member before moving to the next so no framing is left unsupported.
  3. Connect rafters or trusses to wall plates with listed uplift connectors. Install every connector specified by the approved plan; do not omit or substitute hardware.
  4. Install ridge, rafter ties, ceiling ties, blocking, gable framing, and permanent bracing as specified. Complete every connection called for in the approved detail.
  5. Maintain spacing and overhang. Check rafter or truss spacing and overhang continuously as each member is set, not only at the end.
  6. Inspect framing before sheathing. Confirm spacing, ties, blocking, connectors, and permanent bracing are complete and correct before any sheathing panel is installed.

I. Roof Sheathing Installation

  1. Lay out roof sheathing with approved orientation and staggered joints. Follow the panel layout specified by the approved plan.
  2. Maintain panel spacing. Leave the specified gap between panels for material movement.
  3. Install clips where specified. Use panel edge clips called for by the approved design between supports.
  4. Fasten panels using the approved schedule. Follow the specified fastener type, size, and spacing pattern exactly.
  5. Keep workers off unsupported panel edges. Work from framing members or established supports, not from an unfastened panel edge.
  6. Complete eave, rake, and opening details. Finish sheathing at all roof edges and any specified openings per the approved detail.

J. Roof Inspection and Temporary Protection

  1. Inspect roof plane, pitch, framing, ties, connectors, bracing, sheathing, overhangs, and fasteners. Confirm every item on the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Install temporary weather protection or coordinate immediate roof underlayment and covering. Do not leave sheathing exposed to weather without protection.
  3. State clearly that sheathing alone is not a finished roof. Confirm the weatherproof roofing assembly under Book 5 is scheduled before considering the roof complete.
  4. Document roof inspection and roofing handoff. Record framing, connector, sheathing, and protection details for the project file.

K. Recheck and Closeout Procedure

  1. Recheck every incomplete or unclear item. Classify each gap as missing documentation, an unconfirmed connection, an unresolved spacing or overhang issue, or an unresolved sheathing condition before closing it out.
  2. Label and retain the completed roof-framing and sheathing package. Store it for reference and reuse throughout the rest of this book.

Common Inspection Review

Inspect the completed roof structure using the same checks regardless of whether rafters or trusses were selected.

  • Framing-option check: confirm the installed structure matches the approved plan and no truss was modified without engineering authorization.
  • Connection check: confirm every listed uplift connector, tie, and blocking piece specified by the plan is installed.
  • Bracing check: confirm permanent bracing is complete before any temporary bracing was removed.
  • Spacing and overhang check: confirm rafter or truss spacing, ridge alignment, and overhangs meet the approved plan at both eaves and both rakes.
  • Sheathing check: confirm panels are installed to the approved orientation, spacing, clips, and fastening schedule.
  • Protection check: confirm temporary weather protection is installed or roofing is scheduled immediately.

Complete the Roof Framing and Sheathing Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 17.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Rafter Birdsmouth Cut Beyond the Approved Notch Limit

Probable cause: the birdsmouth was cut past the layout line, removing more rafter depth than the approved notch limit allows.
Confirmation: measure the remaining rafter depth at the notch and compare it to the approved plan requirement.
Correction: replace the affected rafter with a correctly cut member; do not reinforce an overcut rafter as a substitute for replacement unless an engineered repair is specified.
Reinspection: repeat the framing check on the replaced rafter.

Truss Field-Cut or Modified Without Authorization

Probable cause: a truss member was cut, drilled, or notched in the field to resolve an interference without engineering review.
Confirmation: inspect the truss for unauthorized cuts and compare against the delivered engineering documents.
Correction: stop work and obtain written engineering authorization for a repair or replacement; do not continue framing around an unauthorized modification.
Reinspection: repeat the framing-option check once the engineered correction is complete.

Rafter or Truss Left Unbraced After Raising

Probable cause: temporary bracing was deferred while workers moved on to raise additional members.
Confirmation: inspect the framing for any rafter or truss without temporary or permanent bracing.
Correction: install temporary bracing on the affected member before any further framing or sheathing proceeds.
Reinspection: repeat the bracing check across the full roof structure.

Uplift Connector Omitted or Substituted

Probable cause: a rafter-to-plate or truss-to-plate connection was made with ordinary fasteners instead of the specified listed connector.
Confirmation: review the approved connector schedule against the installed hardware at each connection.
Correction: install the specified listed connector at every affected connection before proceeding to sheathing.
Reinspection: repeat the connection check on the corrected hardware.

Sheathing Installed Before Framing Inspection

Probable cause: sheathing panels were installed before ties, blocking, connectors, and permanent bracing were verified complete.
Confirmation: compare the framing photographs and inspection record against the sheathing installation date.
Correction: remove sheathing as needed to verify concealed framing, or document an approved alternative verification method accepted by the authority having jurisdiction.
Reinspection: repeat the framing check before resuming or completing sheathing.

Sheathing Left Exposed Without Temporary Protection

Probable cause: roofing was delayed after sheathing installation without temporary weather protection in place.
Confirmation: inspect the sheathing for moisture staining, swelling, or delamination.
Correction: install temporary weather protection immediately, and replace any sheathing panel damaged by moisture exposure before roofing proceeds.
Reinspection: repeat the protection check once temporary covering is installed.

Cleanup

This project ends with the roof structure documented, protected, and staged for the next project, not discarded.

  • Leave permanent bracing in place; remove only bracing explicitly designated as temporary once the permanent load path is verified complete
  • Photograph the completed framing before it is concealed by sheathing, and photograph the completed sheathing before roofing
  • Store leftover rafter stock, connectors, and sheathing offcuts for reuse in Project 17 or later trim work where appropriate
  • Label and file the completed framing worksheet, connector schedule, and inspection records together
  • Return the signed roof-framing and sheathing worksheet to the project records for reference throughout the rest of this book

Field Terms

Rafter
A site-built sloped structural member cut to the approved plumb, seat, and tail geometry.
Truss
A prefabricated, engineered roof-framing assembly installed without field modification.
Birdsmouth
The bearing notch cut into a rafter at the wall plate.
Ridge
The horizontal member at the roof peak connecting opposing rafters.
Rafter Tie / Ceiling Tie
A horizontal member resisting outward thrust at the wall plates.
Uplift Connector
A listed metal connector resisting wind uplift at framing connections.
Permanent Bracing
The fixed bracing required to keep framing stable after temporary bracing is removed.
Roof Sheathing
The structural panel deck installed over the roof framing.
Overhang
The horizontal projection of the roof beyond the wall face at the eave or rake.
Framing Inspection
The required checkpoint verifying framing is complete before it is concealed by sheathing.
