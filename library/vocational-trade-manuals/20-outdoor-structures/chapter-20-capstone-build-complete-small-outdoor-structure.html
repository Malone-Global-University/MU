Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to plan, lay out, build, brace, connect, finish, inspect, document, and close out one complete small detached outdoor structure using every competency developed across Chapters 1 through 19. The finished work product is a stable, weather-protected, fully inspected structure — either the preferred small freestanding ground-level deck platform with a pergola frame and one integrated privacy-screen or low-rail section, or the fully designed alternate small utility shed — accompanied by a complete closeout record.

You will select the approved capstone design before procurement, verify site and property conditions, complete utility locating, run the work-area setup, lay out and inspect footings, set and brace posts, frame and inspect the deck or floor platform, install decking or subfloor, build any required stair, install guards, rails, walls, or pergola framing as specified by the chosen design, complete siding and roofing coordination for the shed option, install hardware, apply finish, pass every required inspection, correct every deficiency, and hand the structure off as approved for its defined use.

This project does not introduce new techniques. It requires you to sequence and integrate the techniques from every earlier chapter in the correct technical order, holding each inspection point before concealment and never skipping a step to save time. No inspection is waived, and no structural connection is approximated because the schedule is tight.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • OS-115 — Integrate site readiness, layout, and footing work into one continuous, documented structure sequence.
  • OS-116 — Set posts and frame a deck or floor platform to approved dimensions, bearing, and connector schedules.
  • OS-117 — Install decking or flooring, and the guard, rail, wall, or pergola components specified by the approved capstone design.
  • OS-118 — Install hardware, trim, siding, or roofing coordination required by the selected design option.
  • OS-119 — Pass every required footing, framing, and final inspection, correcting all deficiencies before handoff.
  • OS-120 — Compile a complete closeout record and maintenance plan and formally hand off the structure.

Where This Skill Fits

This project is the integration point for the entire book. It draws directly on the readiness package from Project 01, the work-area setup from Project 02, the layout method from Project 03, the footing and post work from Projects 04 and 05, the framing and decking sequence from Projects 06 through 09, and — depending on the selected design — the fence, pergola, or shed techniques from Projects 10 through 17, plus the repair-avoidance habits from Project 19.

The structure produced here is handed off as the subject of Book 21's repair and remodeling studies. Every record compiled in this project — layout, footings, framing photographs, connector schedules, inspection approvals, and maintenance instructions — becomes the baseline that later repair work is measured against.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • selecting the approved capstone design before any procurement or site work begins
  • verifying property, utility, drainage, and permit conditions for the capstone site
  • laying out, excavating, and inspecting footings, and setting and bracing posts
  • framing and inspecting the deck or shed floor platform before concealment
  • installing decking or subfloor, guards, rails, walls, or pergola members as specified by the chosen design
  • installing siding, roofing coordination, and hardware for the design option selected
  • passing every required inspection and correcting every deficiency before handoff
  • compiling the full closeout record and maintenance plan

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select an attached deck, elevated deck, roofed gazebo, large shed, habitable accessory structure, pool deck, or other structure outside the approved capstone options without the exact professional design, permits, and inspections that option requires
  • skip, merge, or reorder a required inspection hold point to meet a schedule
  • conceal footing, post, or framing work before the required inspection has passed
  • remove temporary bracing before permanent bracing and connections are complete
  • declare the structure closed out with any open deficiency, missing record, or unconfirmed inspection

Any condition discovered during this project that requires engineering, a specialty contractor, or a jurisdiction ruling must be resolved through the proper professional channel before work continues past that point.

Trade Note

A capstone project fails most often not from a single bad cut, but from sequence violations: decking installed before the framing inspection, bracing removed before the roof stabilizes the walls, or a guard post surface-fastened to save an afternoon. This chapter is a rehearsal for exactly the discipline a professional crew is expected to hold under real schedule pressure.

Setup Standard

Do not select final materials or begin procurement until the capstone design option is chosen and the site plan, structural drawings, footing schedule, connection details, and permit conditions are in hand. A design change after footings are placed can invalidate the footing layout.

Shop Tip

Build a single running closeout binder from day one — layout sheet, footing record, framing photos, inspection sign-offs — rather than reconstructing it at the end. A record built as you go is more accurate than one assembled from memory after the structure is finished.

Design Selection Discipline

The capstone design is chosen once, before procurement, and is not redesigned mid-project. Changing structure type, footprint, or roof condition after footings or framing are underway can invalidate inspections already passed and require rework from the affected phase forward.

Code Alert — Capstone Design Boundaries

Only the preferred deck-and-pergola design or the fully designed alternate shed may be used as a default capstone. An attached deck, elevated deck, roofed gazebo, large shed, habitable structure, pool deck, or structure carrying unusual loads requires the exact approved design, permits, inspections, and professional controls — it is not a default option here.

Critical Safety Cue — Hold Points Are Not Optional

Do not conceal footings, post connections, or framing before the corresponding inspection has passed. Do not remove temporary bracing until permanent bracing and connections give the structure a complete, stable load path. A missed hold point can hide a failure that only becomes visible after the structure is loaded or occupied.

Field Safety Cue — Multi-Phase Site Control

Maintain the work-area zones, barricades, and public-exclusion controls established in Project 02 for the full duration of this multi-day build, not just during the busiest phases.

Measurement Cue — Recheck Before Each New Phase

Reverify dimensions, level, plumb, and square at the start of each new phase, not only at the end of the previous one. A small drift in layout, level, or plumb compounds as framing, decking, and rails are added on top of it.

E. Design Selection and Pre-Construction Planning

  1. Select the approved capstone design before procurement. Choose the preferred small freestanding deck-and-pergola design with one privacy-screen or low-rail section, or the fully designed alternate small shed.
  2. Obtain the complete plan set for the selected design. Gather the site plan, structural drawings, footing schedule, connection details, stair or rail details, material schedule, drainage plan, permit conditions, and required inspection sequence.
  3. Verify property lines, setbacks, easements, utilities, height limits, lot coverage, drainage, and HOA conditions. Confirm the selected design fits every recorded restriction before ordering materials.
  4. Complete utility locating for the capstone site. Do not proceed to layout until the locate is confirmed and marked.
  5. Perform the Chapter 1 readiness inspection for this site. Confirm the site conditions record, drainage assessment, and structure-type rationale are current for the selected design.

F. Work Area, Layout, and Footings

  1. Prepare the Chapter 2 work area. Establish storage, cutting, lifting, fall-protection, concrete, weather, and public-control measures for the full build duration.
  2. Inventory and inspect all materials before use. Confirm lumber, decking or floor material, posts, connectors, fasteners, concrete, trim, hardware, and finish products match the approved schedule, and confirm treatment, grade, exterior rating, and corrosion compatibility.
  3. Establish the baseline, corners, structure lines, post centers, footing centers, openings, stairs, screens, walls, and finished elevations. Square and document the completed layout before excavation.
  4. Excavate footings to accepted soil and the approved dimensions. Install reinforcement, forms, anchors, or post bases per the connection details.
  5. Complete the required footing inspection before placing concrete. Place and cure concrete, then verify anchor position and footing elevations before loading.

G. Posts and Structural Framing

  1. Set, brace, plumb, and align posts on the accepted footings. Treat all field cuts and exposed end grain.
  2. Install beams on approved bearing or connectors. For the deck option, install rim members, joists, blocking, ties, and permanent bracing; for the shed option, install skids, supports, floor framing, blocking, anchors, and subfloor.
  3. Verify dimensions, diagonals, support, connectors, lateral resistance, and uplift path. Do not proceed until every connection matches the approved schedule.
  4. Complete the required framing inspection before any concealment. Photograph all framing and connectors before decking, subfloor, or sheathing covers them.

H. Decking, Access, and Vertical Elements

  1. Install decking or floor panels using the approved spacing and fastening system. Preserve drainage and ventilation throughout.
  2. Build the approved short stair only if the design requires one. Verify final stair geometry and landing against the approved rise, run, and code requirements.
  3. Install the guard, handrail, privacy screen, or wall framing specified by the design, with a verified structural load path for every post and rail or wall connection. Do not rely on surface fastening alone for guard-post resistance.
  4. For the pergola option, install beams, rafters, purlins, knee braces, and uplift connectors. For the shed option, frame, brace, and sheath the walls, then frame and sheath the roof without modifying any prefabricated truss, and coordinate the complete weatherproof roofing assembly.

I. Envelope, Hardware, and Finish

  1. Install siding, WRB, flashing, trim, clearances, and the shed door where the shed option is used. Maintain drainage and ventilation at every wall base and penetration.
  2. Install gates, latches, hinges, hold-open devices, stops, and other project hardware. Control pinch, sharp-edge, and slam hazards, and cycle every moving component repeatedly.
  3. Install caps, fascia, trim, and protected terminations. Apply the specified cut-end treatment and exterior finish, keeping drainage paths, utilities, building clearances, and service access open.
  4. Remove temporary bracing only after permanent stability is verified. Confirm every stair, gate, door, and latch operates correctly before proceeding to final inspection.

J. Final Inspection, Deficiency Correction, and Closeout

  1. Inspect footings, posts, beams, joists, rafters, walls, decking or flooring, stairs, guards, handrails, screens, connectors, fasteners, uplift, bracing, drainage, clearances, and surface hazards. Perform the approved controlled load or stability check.
  2. Prepare a final deficiency list, correct every item, and recheck the correction. Complete the required final permit inspection.
  3. Clean the site and restore disturbed hardscape and landscaping. Compile utility records, layout, footing measurements, concrete records, inspections, lumber and treatment labels, connector and fastener records, framing photographs, stair and rail measurements, roofing or envelope records, finish products, corrections, and approvals.
  4. Provide use, load, cleaning, drainage, coating, fastener, rot, roof, gate, stair, guard, and seasonal-maintenance instructions. Hand off the structure as stable, protected, documented, and approved for its defined use, and document the formal transition to Book 21.

Common Inspection Review

Inspect the completed capstone structure using the same checks regardless of which design option was selected.

  • Design and site check: confirm the approved capstone option was selected before procurement and property, utility, and drainage conditions are verified.
  • Footing and post check: confirm accepted bearing, cured and inspected concrete, and plumb, aligned, connected posts.
  • Framing check: confirm the frame is square, connected, braced, and was inspected before concealment.
  • Enclosure and access check: confirm decking, guards, rails, walls, pergola, or roof match the approved geometry and load path.
  • Hardware and finish check: confirm gates, latches, siding, trim, and finish are installed, operable, and free of hazards.
  • Closeout check: confirm every deficiency is corrected, every inspection is signed, and the complete record is compiled.

Complete the Capstone Inspection Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before declaring the project handed off.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Capstone Design Was Changed After Footings Were Placed

Probable cause: a structure dimension, footprint, or roof condition was altered mid-project without reworking the footing layout that supports it.
Confirmation: compare the as-built footing centers against the current design drawings.
Correction: revert to the original approved design, or engage the design and permit process required for the changed structure before continuing.
Reinspection: repeat the site and layout check on the corrected design.

Framing Was Concealed Before the Framing Inspection

Probable cause: decking, subfloor, or sheathing was installed to keep the schedule moving before the required inspection was completed.
Confirmation: check the inspection log against the date decking or sheathing was installed.
Correction: remove decking or sheathing as needed to expose the framing for inspection, or arrange an approved alternate verification method with the permitting authority.
Reinspection: repeat the framing check once framing is exposed and verified.

Guard Post Relies on Surface Fastening Alone

Probable cause: a guard post was fastened only through decking or fascia rather than into a verified structural load path.
Confirmation: inspect the post connection from beneath or behind the finished surface for through-bolts, blocking, or brackets specified by the design.
Correction: remove the surface fastening and install the specified structural connection into framing.
Reinspection: repeat the enclosure and access check on the corrected post.

Temporary Bracing Was Removed Before Permanent Stability

Probable cause: temporary bracing was taken down for convenience before permanent bracing, sheathing, or roof framing gave the structure a complete load path.
Confirmation: compare the completed permanent bracing against the approved bracing schedule for the current construction stage.
Correction: reinstall temporary bracing immediately and complete the permanent bracing before removing it again.
Reinspection: repeat the framing check once permanent bracing is verified complete.

Closeout Record Is Missing Inspection Sign-Offs or Photographs

Probable cause: photographs or inspection confirmations were deferred until the end of the project and some concealed conditions were not documented before being covered.
Confirmation: review the closeout binder against the Assessed Build Schedule for missing components.
Correction: obtain any missing sign-off through the permitting authority's accepted verification process, and note any condition that cannot be reconfirmed.
Reinspection: repeat the closeout check on the completed record.

Cleanup

This project ends with a compiled closeout record and a structure ready for handoff, not with tools left on site.

  • Clean the site and restore any disturbed hardscape or landscaping affected by the capstone build
  • Return excess lumber, connectors, fasteners, and finish products to storage or dispose of scrap per Project 02 waste handling
  • Photograph the completed structure from multiple angles for the closeout record before removing any remaining site controls
  • Label and file the completed layout sheet, footing and framing photographs, inspection sign-offs, and maintenance instructions together
  • Return the signed closeout package to the project records for reference in Book 21
