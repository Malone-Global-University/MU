Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare one residential electrical rough-in for the required inspection. The finished work product is a documented readiness package showing that boxes, wiring methods, supports, protection, grounding and bonding, firestopping, and licensed-trade participation all match the approved plans, panel schedule, and load information before any covering material is installed.

You will confirm the electrical permit, responsible licensed electrician, approved documents, and prior inspections; verify panel, box, cable, and raceway conditions against approved plans; verify grounding, bonding, and identification; coordinate with other trades; complete a room-by-room preinspection; correct deficiencies; and attend the official inspection with the required documentation.

This project depends on the authority register from Project 01, the permit decision from Project 02, and the code-note summary from Project 04. It supports the energy-code matrix in Project 12 and the life-safety checklist in Project 13, and feeds directly into the final building inspection in Project 16 and the Chapter 20 capstone.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CP-055 — Verify box placement, height, orientation, support, and accessibility against approved plans and finish conditions.
  • CP-056 — Inspect cable and raceway protection at framing penetrations and prohibited-damage locations.
  • CP-057 — Verify staple, strap, and support spacing for cables and raceways against applicable requirements.
  • CP-058 — Inspect grounding-electrode conductors, bonding jumpers, and equipment-grounding provisions for visibility and completeness.
  • CP-059 — Identify circuit, panel, feeder, and equipment labeling needs at the rough-in stage.
  • CP-060 — Complete an electrical rough-in inspection checklist and readiness package.

Where This Skill Fits

Framing inspection in Project 07 confirms the structure is ready to receive rough systems. This project confirms the electrical rough-in itself is ready for its own required inspection before insulation, drywall, or other concealment covers boxes, cable, and grounding work. The readiness discipline here — verify against approved documents, photograph before concealment, and correct through the responsible licensed party — is the same discipline used in Project 09's plumbing rough-in and Project 11's HVAC rough-in, and it feeds the correction log built in Project 17.

Nothing in this project authorizes the learner to design circuits, size services, perform licensed electrical work, or open energized equipment. The learner's role is to verify that the completed rough-in matches approved documents and to prepare the readiness package that supports the licensed electrician's work and the inspector's review.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming the electrical permit, responsible licensed electrician, approved plans, panel schedule, and prior inspections
  • verifying panel, disconnect, meter, and major-equipment locations and working clearances without opening energized equipment
  • verifying box, device, cable, and raceway rough-in against approved plans and finish conditions
  • verifying supports, protection, grounding, bonding, and firestopping visibility
  • identifying labeling needs and coordinating with other trades before concealment
  • completing a documented preinspection, correcting deficiencies, and attending the official inspection

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • design, size, or redesign services, feeders, circuits, or grounding and bonding systems
  • open energized panels, remove protective covers, or perform any licensed electrical installation work
  • approve, waive, or self-certify the rough-in as code-compliant; only the authority having jurisdiction can approve the inspection
  • energize, de-energize, or test circuits beyond what the responsible licensed electrician and the jurisdiction's procedure authorize

Any condition that cannot be confirmed from approved documents or the licensed electrician must be logged as unresolved and referred before the inspection is scheduled.

Trade Note

An electrical rough-in readiness review is not a substitute for the licensed electrician's own quality control or for the inspector's authority. The purpose of this project is to catch missing documentation, unverified conditions, and obvious deficiencies before scheduling — not to approve or certify the installation.

Setup Standard

Do not begin the room-by-room preinspection until the approved plans, panel schedule, load calculations, and permit record are confirmed current. A readiness review performed against superseded documents can pass conditions that the actual approved revision would reject.

Shop Tip

Walk the rough-in room by room in the same order the plans are drawn, not in whatever order is convenient on site. Skipping around the building is how a missing box or an unsupported cable run gets overlooked.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every rough-in readiness project in this book — plumbing in Project 09, electrical here in Project 10, and HVAC in Project 11 — follows the same sequence: confirm the permit and approved documents, verify the physical installation against those documents room by room, verify supports and protection, verify what must remain accessible after finishes, document everything before concealment, correct deficiencies through the responsible trade, and schedule only when the work is genuinely ready. Learning that sequence here on the electrical rough-in means every later inspection-readiness project in this book starts from a method you already trust.

Verifying Against Approved Documents, Not Field Habit

Every box location, cable route, support spacing, and grounding detail is checked against the approved plans and panel schedule, not against what "usually" is done or what a prior project used. Where the approved documents and an installer's habitual method differ, the approved documents control, and the difference is logged as a clarification item for the licensed electrician and, where needed, the design professional.

Critical Safety Cue — Never Open Energized Equipment

Do not open panels, remove dead fronts, or access energized equipment as part of this readiness check. If a panel or disconnect must be inspected, confirm with the responsible licensed electrician whether it is de-energized and locked out before any closer inspection, and defer to the electrician for any determination requiring entry into the equipment.

Professional Boundary Cue — Licensed Work Stays With the Licensed Party

Do not redesign circuits, resize conductors, or approve substitute grounding methods. Any condition that appears to depart from approved documents is a clarification item for the responsible licensed electrician and, where the change affects the design, the design professional and the plan-review process.

Code Alert — Box Fill, Support Spacing, and Grounding Vary by Adopted Requirement

Box fill calculations, staple and support spacing, bonding-jumper sizing, and grounding-electrode requirements are governed by the adopted electrical code and local amendments recorded in the Project 01 binder. Verify the current adopted requirement rather than relying on a figure remembered from another jurisdiction or a prior project.

E. Preparation — Confirm Permit, Documents, and Prior Approvals

  1. Confirm the electrical permit, responsible licensed electrician or authorized installer, approved plans, panel schedule, load information, and revisions. Record the permit number, current revision, and responsible-party contact on the cover sheet.
  2. Identify separate service, underground, rough, temporary power, solar, generator, low-voltage, or final inspections. Confirm from the Project 01 binder and Project 02 permit decision which of these apply to this project.
  3. Confirm utility requirements, service-location approvals, meter conditions, and prior inspections. Record confirmation status for each applicable item.
  4. Verify panel, disconnect, meter, transfer equipment, transformer, and major-equipment locations and working clearances. Measure and record clearances against approved plan dimensions.
  5. Do not open energized equipment or remove protective covers as part of the readiness check. Defer any condition requiring equipment access to the responsible licensed electrician.

F. Verify Boxes, Devices, and Wiring Methods

  1. Verify box locations, heights, orientations, support, depth, fill basis, extensions, and accessibility against approved plans and finish conditions. Record each box location by room on the Box and Device Verification Log.
  2. Verify receptacle, switch, lighting, appliance, alarm, control, exterior, garage, bath, kitchen, and specialty-device rough-ins. Confirm each device type matches the approved plan for that location.
  3. Verify cables, conductors, raceways, fittings, boxes, connectors, bushings, and equipment are listed and used as approved. Record product listing information for each wiring method observed.
  4. Verify cable and raceway routing avoids prohibited damage, heat, moisture, sharp edges, and structural conflicts. Note any routing condition that does not match the approved plan or applicable requirement.
  5. Verify supports, staples, straps, securing, protection, bend, pull, and expansion provisions from applicable requirements. Measure and record support spacing at representative locations in each area.

G. Verify Protection, Grounding, Bonding, and Identification

  1. Verify nail plates, sleeves, bushings, guards, and physical protection at framing penetrations. Record each penetration location and its protective measure.
  2. Verify structural members have not been improperly bored, notched, cut, or weakened. Cross-reference any boring or notching against the Project 07 framing inspection record.
  3. Verify grounding-electrode conductors, electrodes, bonding jumpers, service bonding, water-pipe bonding, gas bonding where required, and equipment-grounding provisions remain visible. Photograph each grounding and bonding location before it can be concealed.
  4. Verify panel, feeder, circuit, disconnect, equipment, and junction identification required at the current stage. Record which identification items are complete and which remain outstanding.
  5. Verify dedicated circuits, appliance circuits, lighting, controls, alarms, exterior equipment, HVAC, and other loads shown in the documents. Confirm each load matches the approved panel schedule without independently redesigning circuit assignments.
  6. Verify required receptacle, lighting, and switching layouts without independently redesigning code coverage. Flag any apparent gap as a clarification item for the licensed electrician rather than adding or relocating devices.
  7. Verify smoke, carbon-monoxide, security, doorbell, communications, and low-voltage coordination where included. Record coordination status with the applicable low-voltage installer.
  8. Verify fire blocking, draft stopping, firestopping, rated penetrations, and ceiling or wall assemblies remain visible. Cross-reference with the Project 13 life-safety checklist where rated assemblies are affected.

H. Coordinate, Correct, and Prepare for Scheduling

  1. Verify boxes and raceways will remain accessible after cabinets, mirrors, trim, equipment, insulation, and finishes. Record any conflict with planned finish work in the Trade Coordination Log.
  2. Verify exterior and wet-location penetrations coordinate with flashing and weatherproofing. Cross-reference with the Project 08 envelope inspection record.
  3. Verify temporary wiring and power are safe, protected, permitted, and separate from completed rough work. Record the status and authorization basis for any temporary power in use.
  4. Collect load calculations, service documents, equipment data, product listings, engineered systems, and utility approvals where required. File each document in the readiness package with its verification date.
  5. Photograph grounding, bonding, concealed routing, box layout, protection, and service components. Index each photograph by room, elevation, or system.
  6. Coordinate plumbing, HVAC, framing, insulation, cabinets, and equipment conflicts before inspection. Resolve each logged conflict with the responsible trade before proceeding.
  7. Remove debris and provide lighting, safe access, panel clearance, ladders, and visible labels. Confirm the site is ready for a safe and unobstructed inspector walk-through.
  8. Complete a room-by-room and equipment-by-equipment preinspection with the responsible electrician. Walk every logged item with the electrician present and record agreement or disagreement on each.
  9. Create and correct the rough-in deficiency list. Assign each deficiency to a responsible party with a target completion date.
  10. Confirm whether circuits must be de-energized, energized, tested, or otherwise presented according to the jurisdiction. Record the jurisdiction's stated presentation requirement on the cover sheet.

I. Schedule, Attend, and Record the Inspection

  1. Schedule the correct inspection type and record confirmation. Use the Project 01 binder's inspection-request procedure and record the confirmation number and date.
  2. Attend with approved plans, permit, load documentation, utility information, and responsible trade representative. Bring the complete readiness package to the inspection.
  3. Record approval, partial approval, corrections, and work that must remain exposed. Use the inspector's exact wording on the Inspection Result Record.
  4. Do not insulate, drywall, cover boxes, or energize unapproved work beyond authorized temporary conditions. Confirm this restriction with every trade present before leaving the site.
  5. File checklist, photographs, supporting documents, inspection result, and correction status. Archive the complete readiness package for use in the Project 17 correction log and the Project 16 final inspection.

Common Rough-In Inspection

Inspect the finished readiness package using the same checks regardless of which room or system is being reviewed.

  • Document-currency check: confirm the approved plans, panel schedule, and permit revision used for the review are the current issued revision.
  • Box-and-device check: confirm every box and device location, height, and orientation is recorded and matched to approved plans.
  • Wiring-method check: confirm every cable and raceway is listed, correctly supported, and free of prohibited damage.
  • Grounding-visibility check: confirm every grounding and bonding location is photographed before concealment.
  • Coordination check: confirm no unresolved cross-trade conflict remains at the time of scheduling.
  • Deficiency-closure check: confirm every logged deficiency is corrected and verified before the inspection is attended.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Box or Device Location Does Not Match the Approved Plan

Probable cause: a field adjustment was made during installation without a corresponding plan revision or documented approval.
Confirmation: compare the field location against the current approved plan revision and the panel schedule, and ask the responsible electrician whether a change was authorized.
Correction: relocate the device to the approved location, or process a formal revision through the required RFI or amended-permit procedure if the change is to remain.
Reinspection: repeat the box-and-device check on the corrected location.

Grounding or Bonding Provisions Cannot Be Seen Clearly

Probable cause: the connection is obscured by insulation, debris, or an installed cover before the required photograph or inspection point.
Confirmation: clear the obstruction and confirm the connection is intact and matches the approved grounding and bonding detail.
Correction: remove the obstruction, photograph the exposed connection, and file the photograph in the Grounding and Bonding Log before any further concealment proceeds.
Reinspection: repeat the grounding-visibility check.

Support Spacing Does Not Meet the Applicable Requirement

Probable cause: staples or straps were installed at a spacing that does not match the current adopted requirement or approved detail.
Confirmation: measure spacing at multiple representative locations and compare against the requirement recorded in the Project 01 binder.
Correction: add or reposition supports through the responsible licensed electrician to meet the required spacing.
Reinspection: repeat the support-and-protection check at the corrected locations.

A Cross-Trade Conflict Blocks Box or Raceway Accessibility

Probable cause: cabinet, plumbing, or HVAC installation was coordinated without checking planned box or device locations.
Confirmation: compare the conflicting trade's installation plan against the electrical plan for the same location.
Correction: resolve the conflict with the responsible trades before scheduling, relocating either the device or the conflicting component through the required approval process.
Reinspection: repeat the coordination check for the affected area.

A Logged Deficiency Was Never Actually Corrected

Probable cause: the deficiency was recorded but not assigned to a responsible party, or the correction was performed without verification.
Confirmation: review the Deficiency and Correction Log for items missing an assigned party, correction date, or verification signature.
Correction: assign the item, complete the correction through the responsible licensed party, and verify it before closing the item.
Reinspection: repeat the deficiency-closure check on the completed log.

No Official Inspection Result Was Recorded

Probable cause: the inspection was attended but the result was not written down immediately, or only a verbal comment was retained.
Confirmation: check the Inspection Result Record for inspection type, date, inspector identifier where permitted, and result.
Correction: obtain the official written result from the jurisdiction's record system if it was not recorded onsite, and file it in the readiness package.
Reinspection: repeat the review of the completed Inspection Result Record.

Field Terms

Rough-In Stage
The point at which boxes, cables, raceways, and grounding are installed but not yet concealed.
Working Clearance
The dedicated space required around panels and equipment for safe access.
Listed Equipment
A product bearing a recognized testing-agency listing or label appropriate to its installed use.
Bonding Jumper
A conductor connecting metal parts to maintain electrical continuity and grounding path integrity.
Grounding-Electrode Conductor
The conductor connecting the grounding-electrode system to the service or system grounding point.
Deficiency Item
A condition that does not match approved documents or applicable requirements and must be corrected before scheduling or reinspection.
Concealment
Insulation, drywall, or finish work that covers rough-in components and prevents further visual inspection.
Panel Schedule
The approved document listing each circuit, its load, and its overcurrent protection assignment.
Temporary Power
Authorized, permitted electrical service used during construction, kept separate from completed permanent rough-in work.
Trade Coordination
The process of resolving conflicts between electrical rough-in and other trades before concealment.
