Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare one residential roof and exterior wall assembly for the required dry-in, flashing, weather-resistive-barrier (WRB), opening, lath, and siding-clearance inspections before any of that work is concealed. The finished work product is one documented envelope inspection-readiness package: verified roof deck and underlayment, verified flashing at every transition, verified WRB continuity, verified window and door opening details, verified drainage-plane and clearance conditions, safe inspector access, and a recorded inspection result.

You will confirm every required roof and envelope inspection stage, confirm the structural approvals required before envelope work begins, verify underlayment, flashing, WRB, and opening details against approved documents and manufacturer instructions, verify drainage continuity and required clearances, photograph every condition that will be concealed, provide safe access, correct identified deficiencies, and attend the inspection with the approved documents and a focused question list.

This project follows the framing inspection in Project 07 and precedes the plumbing, electrical, and HVAC rough-in inspections in Projects 09 through 11. Roofing, siding, and other concealing layers must not be installed until the required approval for this stage is documented. Nothing in this project authorizes the learner to approve a flashing detail, substitute a manufacturer instruction, or waive a required inspection.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CP-043 — Verify roof underlayment type, layer, orientation, laps, attachment, and exposure limits against approved documents.
  • CP-044 — Inspect flashing at drip edges, valleys, step conditions, counterflashing, headwalls, kickouts, and penetrations.
  • CP-045 — Verify WRB installation, orientation, laps, fastening, corners, and transition continuity.
  • CP-046 — Verify window and door flashing, sill pans, jamb and head flashing, and WRB integration.
  • CP-047 — Inspect siding, stucco, masonry veneer, and roof clearances against approved details.
  • CP-048 — Complete an envelope inspection checklist and readiness package supporting a documented inspection result.

Where This Skill Fits

The roof and envelope stage sits between the framing inspection in Project 07 and the concealment-sensitive plumbing, electrical, HVAC, insulation, and drywall stages in Projects 09 through 15. Flashing, WRB, and opening details verified here cannot be corrected later without removing finished roofing or siding, so this project's photographic and verification discipline protects every later stage of the building envelope.

The concealment-hold-point discipline used here — verify against approved documents, photograph before covering, and do not conceal before documented approval — is the same discipline the Chapter 20 capstone applies across the entire authority-to-closeout sequence.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • identifying every required roof and envelope inspection stage from the permit, approved documents, and jurisdictional procedure
  • verifying roof deck, underlayment, flashing, and penetration details against approved plans and manufacturer instructions
  • verifying WRB installation, drainage-plane continuity, and window and door opening flashing details
  • verifying siding, stucco, masonry veneer, trim, soffit, fascia, and roof clearances shown in approved details
  • photographing concealed conditions, providing safe access, correcting deficiencies, and recording the inspection result

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • design, approve, or field-modify a flashing detail, WRB assembly, or opening detail; use only the approved design and manufacturer instructions
  • rely on exposed sealant as a substitute for required flashing
  • request roof access in unsafe weather, surface, slope, electrical, or fall conditions
  • install siding, roofing, or other concealing layers before the required inspection approval is documented

Any flashing, WRB, or opening detail that conflicts between the manufacturer instructions and the project's approved documents must be identified and resolved through the design professional or authority before installation continues.

Trade Note

Roof and envelope failures are rarely caused by a single missing component. They are most often caused by an unverified transition — a roof-to-wall intersection, a penetration, or an opening — where two trades or two products meet. Verify every transition individually rather than assuming the field is consistent because the majority of the assembly looks correct.

Setup Standard

Do not mark a roof or envelope condition as ready for inspection until it is compared directly against the approved plan detail or manufacturer instruction for that exact location. A condition that "looks similar" to an approved detail on a different elevation is not verified.

Shop Tip

Photograph every roof-to-wall transition, penetration, and opening before moving to the next elevation. Photographs taken after the whole roof or wall is dried in are far harder to sort by location and are more likely to miss a condition that needed correction.

Approved Documents, Manufacturer Instructions, and Verified Transitions

Every roof and wall condition is verified against the approved plan detail for its exact location and the manufacturer instructions for the products actually installed. A detail approved for one elevation, penetration, or opening type may not apply to a different condition on the same building. Record the plan reference and manufacturer instruction used for every verified item.

Documentation Cue — Photograph Before Concealment, Every Time

Photograph every flashing, WRB lap, penetration, and opening detail by location before roofing or siding covers it. A condition that is not photographed and not still visible at inspection cannot be verified after the fact.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Conceal Before Approval

Do not install roofing, siding, stucco, or other concealing layers over underlayment, flashing, WRB, or opening details before the required inspection approval is documented. Concealing an unapproved condition is a stop-work condition requiring correction and reinspection, not a scheduling convenience.

Field Safety Cue — Roof and Elevated Access

Do not request or perform roof, scaffold, or lift access under unsafe weather, wet or icy surface, excessive slope, overhead electrical, or unstable fall-protection conditions. Provide the same safe access for the inspector that is required for the learner's own verification work.

Professional Boundary Cue — Reserved Flashing and WRB Decisions

Do not create a field flashing detail, substitute a different WRB product, or resolve a conflict between manufacturer instructions and approved plans without design-professional or authority clarification. Exposed sealant is never an acceptable substitute for a missing or incomplete flashing detail.

E. Preparation — Confirm Stage, Documents, and Prior Approvals

  1. Identify every required roof and envelope inspection stage from the permit, approved documents, and jurisdictional procedure. Record each stage and its sequence on the envelope deficiency log before beginning verification.
  2. Confirm approved roof plans, wall sections, opening details, flashing details, product approvals, manufacturer instructions, and revisions are onsite. Replace any superseded sheet with the current approved revision before proceeding.
  3. Confirm structural roof and wall framing approvals required before envelope work. Do not begin envelope verification over framing that has not received its required structural approval.

F. Verify Roof Deck, Underlayment, and Flashing

  1. Verify roof deck type, thickness, fastening, edge support, dryness, and repair status. Record any repaired area and the material used against the approved specification.
  2. Verify roof underlayment type, layer, orientation, laps, attachment, slope suitability, and exposure limits. Measure lap dimensions against the approved detail rather than visual estimation.
  3. Verify eave membrane, valley membrane, roof-wall transitions, penetrations, crickets, and vulnerable locations. Confirm each vulnerable location has the specific membrane or detail called for at that location.
  4. Verify drip edge, valley flashing, step flashing, counterflashing, apron flashing, headwall flashing, kickouts, and compatible metals. Confirm metal types are compatible at every point of contact.
  5. Verify plumbing vents, flues, chimneys, skylights, roof vents, fans, curbs, solar supports, and other penetrations use approved details. Confirm each penetration flashing matches its approved detail rather than a generic boot or collar substitution.
  6. Do not rely on exposed sealant as a substitute for required flashing. Flag any location where sealant appears to be covering a missing or incomplete flashing detail.
  7. Verify temporary dry-in protects the building without trapping water or concealing required inspection items. Confirm temporary protection can be removed or is transparent enough to permit inspection of the covered condition.

G. Verify WRB, Openings, and Wall Penetrations

  1. Verify wall sheathing, structural panel fastening, repair, cleanliness, and dryness before WRB installation. Confirm no unresolved sheathing defect remains before verifying the WRB layer itself.
  2. Verify WRB type, orientation, shingle-lap direction, horizontal and vertical laps, fastening, corners, tears, and transitions. Confirm lap direction sheds water downward and outward at every location.
  3. Verify drainage-plane continuity at roof-wall, foundation, deck, balcony, porch, and material transitions. Trace the drainage plane by hand at each transition to confirm no gap exists.
  4. Verify window and exterior door openings match approved dimensions and installation details. Compare rough-opening dimensions against the approved opening schedule.
  5. Verify sill pan, end dams where required, jamb flashing, head flashing, WRB integration, shims, fasteners, and drainage openings. Confirm each opening component is present in the sequence shown in the approved detail.
  6. Verify manufacturer installation instructions and project flashing details do not conflict; obtain clarification if they do. Do not resolve a conflict by selecting the less costly option; route it to the design professional or authority.
  7. Verify penetrations for lights, receptacles, pipes, ducts, hose bibbs, meters, utilities, and mounting blocks are integrated with the WRB. Confirm each penetration is flashed into, not merely sealed against, the WRB.

H. Verify Siding, Clearances, and Product Documentation

  1. Verify lath, drainage mat, rainscreen, furring, weeps, screeds, flashings, and accessories at the stage required for inspection. Confirm these components are visible and not yet concealed by finish material.
  2. Verify siding, stucco, masonry veneer, trim, soffit, fascia, and roof clearances shown in approved details. Measure each clearance directly rather than estimating by eye.
  3. Do not block weeps, vents, drainage gaps, or required openings with sealant, mortar, trim, insulation, or finishes. Flag and correct any blocked opening before proceeding.
  4. Verify compatible fasteners, connectors, sealants, tapes, primers, and treated-material interfaces. Confirm compatibility against the manufacturer's stated interface requirements.
  5. Verify product labels, listings, evaluation reports, and approvals are available for inspector review where required. Save copies before any label is removed or concealed.
  6. Verify fire separation, property-line wall, soffit, eave, opening, and wildfire requirements applicable to the envelope. Confirm any special-district requirement identified in the Project 05 zoning checklist is reflected in the envelope detail.
  7. Verify insulation and air-sealing items that will become inaccessible after exterior concealment. Confirm these items are complete and photographed before the exterior layer closes access to them.

I. Document, Correct, and Schedule

  1. Photograph flashing, opening, penetration, and transition details before siding or roofing concealment. Label each photograph with elevation, location, and date.
  2. Provide safe roof, scaffold, lift, ladder, and ground access within the approved safety plan. Confirm access equipment matches the roof pitch and wall height being inspected.
  3. Do not request roof access under unsafe weather, surface, slope, electrical, or fall conditions. Reschedule verification and inspection activity until conditions are safe.
  4. Remove packaging, debris, loose materials, and obstructions that hide work. Clear the roof and wall surfaces before the preinspection walk.
  5. Complete an elevation-by-elevation and roof-plane preinspection. Walk every elevation and roof plane individually rather than sampling a subset.
  6. Create and correct an envelope deficiency list. Assign each identified deficiency to a responsible party and a target date.
  7. Confirm inspection type, sequencing, and areas that must remain exposed. Verify with the jurisdiction's procedure from the Project 01 reference binder.
  8. Schedule and attend the inspection with approved documents and product information. Bring the approved plan set, product approvals, and photographs to the inspection.
  9. Record approval, corrections, partial areas, and authorization for concealment. Record the inspector's result exactly as given, including any partial approval or condition.
  10. Do not install concealing roof or wall layers until required approval is documented. Hold all covering work until the recorded result authorizes it.
  11. File checklist, photographs, product records, inspection result, and corrections. Store the completed package with the project's compliance record.

Common Envelope Inspection

Inspect the finished readiness package using the same checks regardless of which elevation or roof plane is being reviewed.

  • Underlayment check: confirm type, layer, orientation, laps, attachment, and exposure limits match approved documents for every roof plane.
  • Flashing check: confirm every transition and penetration uses the approved detail and compatible materials with no exposed-sealant substitution.
  • WRB check: confirm orientation, lap direction, fastening, corners, and transitions provide continuous drainage-plane coverage.
  • Opening check: confirm every window and door has sill pan, jamb and head flashing, and WRB integration matching the approved detail.
  • Clearance check: confirm siding, stucco, masonry veneer, and roof clearances match approved details with no blocked drainage openings.
  • Documentation check: confirm every concealed condition is photographed by location and every product label is saved before covering.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Roof Penetration Relies on Sealant Instead of Flashing

Probable cause: the approved flashing detail for that penetration type was not sourced or installed, and sealant was applied to close the visible gap.
Confirmation: compare the installed condition against the approved penetration-flashing detail and manufacturer instructions for the specific penetration type.
Correction: remove the sealant-only condition, install the approved flashing detail, and reseal only where the approved detail calls for sealant as a secondary measure.
Reinspection: repeat the flashing check at that penetration and photograph the corrected condition.

WRB Laps Are Reversed at a Transition

Probable cause: a lower course of WRB was installed over an upper course at a roof-wall or material transition, directing water into the wall instead of out of it.
Confirmation: trace the drainage plane by hand at the transition and confirm each higher course laps over the course below it.
Correction: remove and reinstall the affected courses in the correct shingle-lap sequence.
Reinspection: repeat the WRB check at the corrected transition and photograph the result.

A Window Opening Is Missing Its Sill Pan

Probable cause: the opening detail calling for a sill pan was not followed, or the installer substituted sealant and shims without the pan component.
Confirmation: compare the installed opening against the approved opening detail and manufacturer installation instructions for the window product.
Correction: remove the window if not yet finished on the interior, install the specified sill pan and end dams, and reinstall the window per the approved sequence.
Reinspection: repeat the opening check at that window and photograph the corrected installation.

Weep Holes or Drainage Gaps Are Blocked

Probable cause: trim, mortar, sealant, or insulation was installed across a required weep, vent, or drainage opening without confirming the approved detail's clearance requirement.
Confirmation: locate every required weep or drainage opening on the approved detail and confirm each is physically open.
Correction: clear the blocking material without damaging the surrounding assembly, and adjust the installation method to avoid recurrence.
Reinspection: repeat the clearance check at the corrected location.

Concealment Occurred Before Documented Approval

Probable cause: siding or roofing installation proceeded on schedule without confirming the inspection result was recorded as an approval.
Confirmation: check the inspection record for a documented approval matching the date concealment began.
Correction: stop further concealment immediately, contact the authority having jurisdiction, and follow the required procedure for inspecting or documenting the concealed condition.
Reinspection: complete the authority's required corrective and inspection procedure before any further concealment.

Product Labels Were Removed Before Photographing

Probable cause: installation crews removed manufacturer labels or packaging before the documentation step was completed.
Confirmation: check the product-document register for a saved label, listing, or evaluation report matching each installed product.
Correction: locate the manufacturer's product data sheet or packaging record as a substitute, and revise the jobsite procedure to photograph labels before removal on the next elevation.
Reinspection: repeat the documentation check against the completed product-document register.

Cleanup

This project ends with a documented, filed inspection-readiness package and recorded result, not an unresolved worksite.

  • Remove packaging, debris, and loose materials from every roof plane and wall elevation before final inspection

Field Terms

Weather-Resistive Barrier (WRB)
The continuous water-shedding layer installed over wall sheathing before siding.
Drainage Plane
The continuous path by which incidental water is directed out of a roof or wall assembly.
Shingle-Lap Orientation
Installation order in which each higher course overlaps the course below it so water sheds downward and outward.
Sill Pan
The flashing component installed at the base of a window or door opening to direct incidental water out of the wall.
Kickout Flashing
Flashing installed at the base of a roof-to-wall intersection to divert water away from the wall and into the gutter or away from the structure.
Concealment
Any siding, roofing, or finish layer that would hide a required condition from a required inspection if installed before approval.
Listed System
A tested and approved assembly of products installed exactly as evaluated, rather than substituted or field-modified.
Envelope Deficiency Log
The document recording each identified roof or wall condition, its responsible party, and its correction and reinspection status.
Concealment Hold Point
The stage at which work must remain visible until a documented inspection approval authorizes covering it.
