Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare one residential footing, foundation, pier, or slab stage for the required inspection before concrete placement or backfill conceals the work. The finished work product is one complete foundation inspection-readiness package: verified excavation and layout, verified footing and form geometry, verified reinforcement and anchor hardware, verified underground utility work, safe and documented access for the inspector, and a recorded inspection result that authorizes or withholds concealment.

You will confirm the exact inspection stage required by the authority having jurisdiction, keep the approved plans and engineering onsite, verify excavation and soil-bearing conditions within your authorized role, verify footing and form dimensions against approved documents, verify reinforcement and anchor hardware, verify required underground work, photograph everything that will be concealed, complete a trade-by-trade preinspection, correct every deficiency, schedule and attend the official inspection, and record the result before allowing any concrete placement or backfill.

This project depends directly on the authority register and verified sources from Project 01, the permit decision from Project 02, and the approved permit application and drawings from Project 03. It also sets the pattern every later concealment-inspection project in this book will repeat: framing in Project 07, roof and envelope in Project 08, and every rough-in inspection that follows. Nothing in this project authorizes the learner to size, redesign, or approve foundation elements; that remains the responsibility of the design professional, geotechnical engineer, and building official.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CP-032 — Verify reinforcement placement, size, spacing, laps, and support against approved structural documents.
  • CP-033 — Verify forms and elevations against approved plans and required benchmarks.
  • CP-034 — Verify anchor hardware location, type, and projection against approved plans.
  • CP-035 — Prepare safe, documented, and obstruction-free inspection access.
  • CP-036 — Complete a foundation inspection checklist and record the official inspection result.

Where This Skill Fits

Every concealment-inspection project in this book follows the same pattern this chapter establishes: confirm the exact stage the authority requires, keep approved documents onsite, verify the installed condition against those documents rather than against memory or convenience, photograph anything about to be concealed, complete an internal preinspection, correct every deficiency before scheduling, and record the official result before concealment proceeds. Project 07 repeats this pattern for framing; Project 08 repeats it for the roof and envelope; Projects 09 through 11 repeat it for plumbing, electrical, and HVAC rough-in.

This chapter also introduces the concealment hold point that governs the rest of the book: concrete, backfill, drywall, insulation, siding, and finishes may not proceed over regulated work until the required inspection is approved and recorded. Project 17's correction log and Project 20's capstone inspection sequence both depend on the discipline built here.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • identifying the exact required foundation inspection stage from the permit and jurisdiction
  • confirming approved plans, engineering, and product approvals are onsite
  • verifying excavation, layout, footing, form, reinforcement, and anchor-hardware conditions against approved documents
  • verifying required underground plumbing, conduit, and utility work before the inspection stage
  • photographing conditions that will be concealed by concrete or backfill
  • completing a trade-by-trade preinspection and correcting every deficiency
  • scheduling, attending, and recording the result of the official inspection

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • accept soil-bearing capacity beyond the qualifications and inspection authority the learner actually holds
  • size, redesign, or field-modify footings, foundations, reinforcement, or anchor hardware without the authorized design basis
  • substitute a field judgment for a required geotechnical, engineering, or special-inspection report
  • place concrete, backfill, or otherwise conceal the work before the required inspection is approved and recorded

Any condition that cannot be confirmed against approved documents must be labeled unresolved and referred to the design professional, geotechnical engineer, or building official before concealment.

Trade Note

A foundation inspection is the first concealment hold point in a residential project. Every dimension, every bar, and every piece of hardware verified here becomes invisible the moment concrete is placed. The habit of checking against the approved document — not against what "looks right" — starts in this chapter and controls every later inspection project in this book.

Setup Standard

Do not schedule the foundation inspection until excavation, forms, reinforcement, anchor hardware, and required underground work all match the approved plans and every internal deficiency has been corrected. An inspection requested before the work is genuinely ready wastes the scheduled slot and delays the authority's response for every other project waiting behind it.

Shop Tip

Photograph every condition you are about to bury before you ask anyone else to look at it. A photograph taken before the inspector arrives protects the record even if a later dispute arises about what was actually in the ground.

Verification Against Approved Documents, Not Field Judgment

Every dimension, bar placement, and hardware location is verified against the approved foundation plan, sections, details, and structural notes — never against what appears adequate in the field. Where the approved documents and field conditions conflict, or where a soil condition differs from the design assumption, stop and obtain written direction from the geotechnical engineer, structural engineer, or building official before proceeding.

Documentation Cue — Photograph Before You Bury It

Photograph every footing, reinforcement layout, anchor-hardware location, and underground utility run before concrete placement or backfill. Label each photograph with location, date, and the approved detail it corresponds to. A photograph is not a substitute for the official inspection, but it is the only record available if a concealed condition is ever questioned later.

Critical Safety Cue — No Concealment Before Approval

Do not place concrete or backfill until the required foundation inspection is approved and the result is documented. This applies even when the schedule is tight, even when a verbal comment from an inspector sounded favorable, and even when the crew believes the work is obviously correct. Concealment before documented approval is a stop-work condition.

Field Safety Cue — Excavation and Trench Access

Confirm trench protection, safe entry and exit, and stable footing before anyone — including the inspector — enters an excavation. Do not require the inspector to enter an unsafe excavation. If safe access cannot be provided, correct the access condition before requesting or proceeding with the inspection.

Professional Boundary Cue — Soil-Bearing Acceptance

Do not make a soil-bearing acceptance beyond the qualifications and inspection authority the learner actually holds. Where the plans or permit conditions require geotechnical or engineering review, arrange that review and obtain the written report before proceeding. A field observation that soil "looks solid" is not a substitute for the required qualified determination.

E. Confirm Stage, Documents, and Site Controls

  1. Identify the exact required inspection stage and whether it occurs before concrete, before backfill, at slab preparation, or at another jurisdiction-defined point. Confirm this against the Project 01 binder's inspection-sequence resources and the Project 03 approved permit.
  2. Confirm permit issuance, inspection authorization, approved plan revision, and required special-inspection or testing agency. Record the permit number, plan revision date, and any assigned special inspector on the Foundation Project Record.
  3. Keep the permit card, approved site plan, foundation plan, sections, details, structural notes, engineering, and product approvals onsite. Store these documents at a fixed, accessible jobsite location for the duration of the inspection.
  4. Confirm survey layout, property lines, setbacks, easements, benchmark, and required elevation controls. Verify the controlling benchmark matches the one identified on the approved site or foundation plan.
  5. Confirm excavation and trench safety, access, weather, groundwater, spoil placement, and site barriers. Correct any unsafe condition before proceeding to the next phase.

F. Verify Excavation, Bearing, and Footing Geometry

  1. Verify excavation reaches approved bearing and required geometry without loose, frozen, organic, disturbed, or water-covered material. Measure depth and plan dimensions against the approved foundation plan at every corner and transition.
  2. Do not make soil-bearing acceptance beyond the qualifications and inspection authority provided. Refer any uncertain bearing condition to the geotechnical engineer or building official.
  3. Arrange geotechnical or engineering review when required by plans, permit conditions, or changed soil conditions. Record the reviewing party, date, and written finding on the Foundation Project Record.
  4. Verify footing width, depth, thickness, steps, keyways, piers, pads, and transitions against approved dimensions. Record each measurement on the inspection checklist with its approved target value.
  5. Verify frost, slope, adjacent-foundation, property-line, and excavation-protection conditions as documented. Confirm every condition shown on the approved plan is physically present at the correct location.

G. Verify Forms, Reinforcement, and Anchor Hardware

  1. Verify forms are aligned, supported, dimensionally correct, clean, and at the approved elevation. Check form elevation against the controlling benchmark at multiple points.
  2. Verify reinforcing size, grade, quantity, spacing, laps, hooks, bends, dowels, supports, and concrete cover against approved documents. Record each value against its approved target on the inspection checklist.
  3. Verify reinforcement is secured and will not be displaced during placement. Confirm chairs, ties, and supports hold the reinforcement at the approved cover distance.
  4. Verify anchor bolts, hold-downs, post bases, straps, embeds, sleeves, blockouts, grounding electrodes, and other hardware by location, type, projection, and support. Check every device against the approved structural and electrical details.
  5. Verify underground plumbing, sleeves, conduits, drains, radon, termite, vapor, capillary, insulation, and utility work required before the inspection stage. Confirm each system is complete and matches its approved detail.

H. Verify Slab Elements and Required Test Documentation

  1. Verify slab base, compaction documentation, vapor retarder, insulation, reinforcement, joints, and penetrations where part of the inspection. Confirm each element matches the approved slab detail and energy documents.
  2. Verify required concrete mix, testing, placement, curing, and cold- or hot-weather documents are available. Collect the mix design and any required test protocol before scheduling.
  3. Confirm required compaction, soil, concrete, reinforcing, welding, or special-inspection reports are current and complete. File each report with the Foundation Project Record.
  4. Identify work that will be concealed by concrete or backfill and photograph it before inspection. Label each photograph with location, date, and the approved detail it documents.
  5. Mark dimensions, elevations, and hardware locations for efficient verification without substituting markings for actual compliance. Use markings only as a visual aid; confirm the underlying measurement independently.

I. Preinspect, Correct, and Prepare Safe Access

  1. Remove mud, debris, standing water, loose materials, and obstructions that prevent inspection. Clear every area the inspector must view or enter.
  2. Provide safe ladder, walkway, lighting, trench access, guard, and weather conditions for the inspector. Confirm access equipment matches the actual excavation depth and site layout.
  3. Do not require the inspector to enter an unsafe excavation. Correct any unsafe access condition before requesting the inspection.
  4. Complete a trade-by-trade preinspection with the concrete, framing, plumbing, electrical, survey, and special-inspection parties as applicable. Walk the site with each responsible party and record findings on the deficiency list.
  5. Create a deficiency list and assign each item. Assign a responsible party and target date to every item identified during the preinspection.
  6. Correct all incomplete work before scheduling the inspection. Verify and close every deficiency before proceeding to Section J.

J. Schedule, Attend, and Record the Inspection

  1. Confirm inspection request type, permit number, address, contact, access, documents, and required tests. Use the request procedure recorded in the Project 01 binder.
  2. Schedule the inspection within the jurisdiction's procedure and record confirmation. Record the confirmation number, date, and time on the Foundation Project Record.
  3. Protect the work from weather, damage, displacement, and unauthorized concrete placement before inspection. Maintain barriers and coverage as needed until the inspector arrives.
  4. Attend the inspection with approved documents and a focused question list. Bring the permit card, approved plans, and any test reports to the inspection.
  5. Record result, approved areas, partial approval, corrections, inspector notes, and next permitted action. Use the official inspection record as the controlling result, not a verbal comment alone.
  6. Do not place concrete or backfill until the required approval is documented. Treat this as a stop-work condition regardless of schedule pressure.
  7. File photographs, checklist, reports, inspection result, and corrections in the compliance record. Store these records where they will be available for the Project 17 correction log and the Project 20 capstone archive.

Common Foundation Inspection

Inspect the finished readiness package using the same checks regardless of which foundation element is being reviewed.

  • Document check: confirm the onsite plans, sections, details, and structural notes match the latest approved revision.
  • Geometry check: confirm footing, form, and elevation measurements match the approved plan within the stated tolerance.
  • Reinforcement and hardware check: confirm every bar, lap, support, and anchor device matches the approved structural notes.
  • Underground-work check: confirm required plumbing, conduit, and protective systems are complete and photographed.
  • Access check: confirm safe, obstruction-free access is available for every inspection area.
  • Result check: confirm the official inspection result is recorded before any concealment occurs.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Footing Dimensions Do Not Match the Approved Plan

Probable cause: excavation or form placement followed a field estimate rather than the approved sections and details, or the approved plan was revised after excavation began.
Confirmation: measure width, depth, and thickness at multiple points and compare each value to the approved plan revision on file.
Correction: adjust the excavation or forms to match the approved dimensions, or obtain a written revision from the design professional if a change is proposed.
Reinspection: repeat the footing-and-form geometry check on the corrected condition.

Reinforcement Cover or Spacing Does Not Match Structural Notes

Probable cause: chairs or supports were not installed or shifted during form closure, or bar spacing was estimated rather than measured against the structural notes.
Confirmation: measure cover distance and spacing at representative points and compare to the approved structural notes.
Correction: reposition or re-support the reinforcement to the approved cover and spacing before concrete placement.
Reinspection: repeat the reinforcement-and-hardware check before scheduling.

Anchor Hardware Is Missing, Mislocated, or Wrong Type

Probable cause: hardware was ordered or installed from memory rather than the approved structural and electrical details, or a substitution was made without written approval.
Confirmation: compare each installed device's location, type, and projection against the approved detail sheet.
Correction: install or relocate the correct hardware, or obtain a written revision before proceeding if a substitution is proposed.
Reinspection: repeat the reinforcement-and-hardware check on every corrected device.

Underground Work Was Concealed Before Verification or Photographs

Probable cause: plumbing, conduit, or drainage work was backfilled or covered before the trade-by-trade preinspection reached that area.
Confirmation: review the photograph log and preinspection record for the affected system and identify any gap in documentation.
Correction: uncover the concealed work if verification cannot otherwise be confirmed, and photograph and verify it before recovering.
Reinspection: repeat the underground-work check with completed photographs on file.

The Inspection Was Requested Before the Work Was Ready

Probable cause: the inspection was scheduled to hold a calendar slot rather than after the preinspection and deficiency list were closed.
Confirmation: review the deficiency list for open items dated at or after the inspection request date.
Correction: complete and close every deficiency, then request a new inspection date through the official procedure.
Reinspection: repeat the full preinspection sequence before the rescheduled inspection.

Concrete Was Placed Before the Official Result Was Recorded

Probable cause: a verbal comment from the inspector during a partial walk-through was treated as a final approval.
Confirmation: check the official inspection record for a documented approval matching the date of concrete placement.
Correction: stop further work immediately, notify the authority having jurisdiction, and follow the required corrective and documentation procedure for unauthorized concealment.
Reinspection: follow the authority's required process for verifying concealed work, which may include exposure, testing, or engineering evaluation.

Field Terms

Controlling Benchmark
The single elevation reference point on the approved plan against which every other elevation is measured.
Approved Bearing
The soil or rock condition accepted by the qualified geotechnical or engineering authority identified in the approved documents.
Concealment Hold Point
The stage after which concrete placement or backfill makes a condition impossible to verify without demolition.
Deficiency
An installed condition that does not match the approved plans, sections, details, or structural notes.
Preinspection
The learner's own trade-by-trade verification completed before the official inspection is requested.
Anchor Hardware
Bolts, hold-downs, post bases, straps, embeds, sleeves, and grounding electrodes installed in or on the foundation.
Cover Distance
The measured distance between reinforcement and the nearest concrete surface, verified against the structural notes.
Special Inspection
Inspection or testing performed by a qualified agency identified in the approved documents, separate from the jurisdiction's building inspector.
Official Inspection Result
The inspector's recorded approval, partial approval, or correction notice; the controlling record regardless of verbal comments.
Stop-Work Condition
A condition, such as unauthorized concealment, that requires work to halt until it is corrected and documented.
