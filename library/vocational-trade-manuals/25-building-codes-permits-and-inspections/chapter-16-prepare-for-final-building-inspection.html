Job Overview

This project qualifies you to take a residential project through its final stretch: confirming that every permitted scope item is complete, every prior inspection and correction is closed, every life-safety and access item is in place, and every required document and test record is assembled — before a final building inspection is ever requested. The finished work product is one complete final-inspection readiness package: a room-by-room and system-by-system verification, a closed internal deficiency list, a compiled document index, and a scheduled and attended final building inspection with a recorded result.

You will confirm the permit is still active and matches the completed work, verify every exterior, structural, plumbing, electrical, mechanical, energy, and life-safety item against the approved documents, remove construction hazards and debris, assemble the full closeout document set, run an internal prefinal inspection with the responsible trades, correct every deficiency found, schedule and attend the official final inspection, and record the result.

This project depends directly on the correction log built in Project 17's predecessor discipline and the inspection records accumulated in Projects 6 through 15. It also feeds directly into Project 17's correction tracking for any items the final inspection does not pass outright, and into Project 18's closeout compliance archive. Nothing in this chapter authorizes occupancy, use, connection, or representation of the project as complete before the required final authorization is actually issued.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CP-091 — Verify completed scope against the approved permitted documents, room by room and system by system.
  • CP-092 — Inspect required safety devices, including alarms, egress openings, guards, and fire-separation components.
  • CP-093 — Confirm fixtures, equipment, and finishes are installed, operable, and do not block required access.
  • CP-094 — Verify access and clearances at panels, equipment, shutoffs, cleanouts, and service locations.
  • CP-095 — Compile inspection documents, test records, and closeout evidence into one final document index.
  • CP-096 — Complete a final inspection readiness checklist, correct deficiencies, and record the official final inspection result.

Where This Skill Fits

Every prior inspection project in this book verified one stage of concealed or in-progress work: foundation, framing, envelope, plumbing rough-in, electrical rough-in, HVAC rough-in, energy compliance, life safety, stairs and guards, and interior concealment. This project is where all of those separately verified stages are checked together, as a finished, operating building, against the same permitted scope that started the process in Project 02 and Project 03.

A final inspection failure is rarely a new problem. It is almost always an earlier stage item that was never fully closed, a correction that was never verified, or a finish that was installed over an item that should have stayed accessible. This project's internal prefinal walk exists to catch those conditions before the official inspector does, using the same document trail built since Project 01.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming the permit is active and the completed work matches the approved plans and any processed revisions
  • verifying exterior, structural, envelope, plumbing, electrical, mechanical, energy, and life-safety scope items are complete and operating
  • confirming required access, clearances, and labeling at panels, equipment, shutoffs, and cleanouts
  • removing temporary wiring, temporary heat, construction barriers, debris, and other hazards
  • compiling the final document index from prior inspection, test, and product records
  • conducting an internal prefinal inspection, correcting deficiencies, and scheduling and attending the official final inspection
  • recording the official result and identifying any remaining agency approvals or occupancy restrictions

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • issue a certificate of occupancy, certificate of completion, or utility release; those are issued by the authority having jurisdiction and the utility, not by this checklist
  • occupy, energize, connect, use, lease, or represent the project as complete before the required final authorization is documented
  • waive, reinterpret, or field-modify a life-safety, structural, energy, or accessibility requirement identified as unresolved during the prefinal walk
  • close any prior correction item without the official acceptance required by Project 17's correction-control procedure

Any item that cannot be verified as complete and approved must be logged as a deficiency and corrected before the final inspection is requested, or clearly identified as a separately scheduled trade final.

Trade Note

The final building inspection is the point where every earlier compliance decision in this project either holds up or fails. A staged or partially completed area presented as finished, or a correction closed without official acceptance, is the single most common source of the final-inspection failures diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Required handling for this project: do not stage or conceal any area to obscure a defect from the inspector, and do not represent the project as complete until the authority's final authorization is documented.

Setup Standard

Do not schedule the final building inspection until every prior required inspection and correction is closed or has a documented, scheduled reinspection, and until the internal prefinal walk in this chapter has been completed and its deficiency list closed.

Shop Tip

Build the final document index before the prefinal walk, not after. Gaps in the document index — a missing test report, an unclosed correction, a revision that was never processed — are usually the same gaps that surface as failed items at the official inspection.

Prerequisites

Common Field Fundamentals

The final building inspection follows the same authority-scope-document-verify-correct-approve sequence used throughout this book, applied for the last time to the entire finished project at once rather than to one stage. Confirm the permit and scope, verify the completed work against approved documents system by system, confirm access and life-safety items, compile the document trail, correct every deficiency found internally, and only then request the official inspection that determines whether the project may be occupied or used.

Trade Finals, Building Final, and Occupancy Restrictions

Identify every required final inspection, agency approval, utility release, special inspection, test, and certificate before scheduling anything. Confirm whether trade finals for plumbing, electrical, mechanical, gas, or fire must occur before, alongside, or after the building final, and confirm the exact sequence the jurisdiction requires.

Documentation Cue — Compare Completed Work to Permitted Scope Item by Item

Compare the completed project with the permitted scope room by room, elevation by elevation, and system by system. Identify any unapproved addition, substitution, relocation, omission, or field change, and process the required revision before requesting final inspection.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Occupy Before Final Authorization

Do not occupy, energize, connect, use, lease, or represent the project as complete before the required final approval, certificate, release, or utility authorization is issued. A favorable prefinal walk is an internal readiness result, not an occupancy authorization.

Field Safety Cue — Inspector Access on Inspection Day

Confirm inspector access, lockbox or contact, occupants, pets, utilities, lighting, and ladders before the scheduled visit. Provide safe, unobstructed access to every area the inspection covers, and do not require the inspector to move stored materials, debris, or furnishings to reach a required item.

Professional Boundary Cue — Reserved Final Determinations

The certificate of occupancy, certificate of completion, utility release, and any conditional or partial final approval are issued by the building official, fire official, utility, or other authorized agency, not by this checklist or its preparer. Do not represent an internal prefinal pass as equivalent to official final approval.

E. Preparation — Confirm Permit, Scope, and Prior Approvals

  1. Identify every required final inspection, agency approval, utility release, special inspection, test, and certificate and closeout condition. Record each requirement and its source on the final-readiness checklist before beginning the walk.
  2. Confirm the permit remains active and the current approved plans and revisions match the completed work. Check the permit expiration, inactivity limits, and any extension already granted.
  3. Confirm all prior building, structural, plumbing, electrical, mechanical, gas, fire, energy, zoning, and special inspections are approved or scheduled. List any inspection that is neither approved nor scheduled as an immediate blocking item.
  4. Confirm all plan-review and field corrections are closed or formally accepted as deferred. Cross-check the correction log from Project 17's procedure for any item still open.
  5. Compare the completed project with the permitted scope room by room, elevation by elevation, and system by system. Use the approved plan set as the sole reference for this comparison.
  6. Identify unapproved additions, substitutions, relocations, omitted work, and field changes. Record each one separately with its location and system.
  7. Process required revisions before requesting final inspection. Do not treat an unapproved change as acceptable because it appears complete or well built.

F. Verify Exterior, Envelope, and Life-Safety Systems

  1. Verify exterior grading, drainage, setbacks, address, access, stairs, landings, guards, handrails, decks, porches, and walking surfaces. Confirm dimensions and conditions against the approved site and structural documents.
  2. Verify roofing, flashing, gutters, downspouts, siding, trim, openings, penetrations, clearances, and exterior finishes are complete. Photograph any transition or termination that could not be confirmed complete.
  3. Verify doors, windows, locks, latches, emergency escape openings, glazing, weatherstripping, and egress paths. Operate every required opening using only its intended hardware.
  4. Verify smoke alarms, carbon-monoxide alarms, fire separation, rated doors, firestopping, addressing, and other life-safety items. Test alarms only through the manufacturer- and authority-approved procedure identified in Project 13.
  5. Verify stairs, guards, handrails, rail openings, lighting, headroom, landings, and final finish geometry. Use the measurement method established in Project 14 and record any variance found.

G. Verify Plumbing, Electrical, HVAC, and Energy Systems

  1. Verify plumbing fixtures, traps, cleanouts, shutoffs, water heating, drainage, backflow, gas, and leak-free operation through responsible trade checks. Coordinate this verification with the licensed plumbing trade rather than performing it independently.
  2. Verify electrical devices, covers, lighting, panel directories, disconnects, grounding documentation, alarms, exterior equipment, and working clearances. Confirm the panel directory matches the installed circuits.
  3. Verify HVAC equipment, filters, registers, returns, controls, ventilation, exhaust, condensate, combustion, and service access. Confirm equipment operates through a normal control cycle where safe to do so.
  4. Verify insulation, attic access, crawlspace access, energy certificates, testing, ventilation, and product documentation. Confirm the energy matrix from Project 12 shows every required test as accepted.
  5. Verify cabinets, appliances, fixtures, counters, flooring, wall finishes, and trim do not block required access or clearances. Identify any finish installed over a required access point and correct it before scheduling.
  6. Verify utility rooms, garages, attics, crawlspaces, panels, shutoffs, equipment, cleanouts, and valves are accessible and labeled where required. Confirm labeling matches the approved documents and any required manufacturer label.

H. Site Cleanup, Testing Confirmation, and Document Index

  1. Verify temporary wiring, temporary heat, construction barriers, unused materials, and construction equipment are removed or properly authorized. Confirm any remaining temporary condition has documented authorization.
  2. Verify construction debris, sharp objects, trip hazards, open excavations, standing water, and unsafe storage are eliminated. Walk the full site, not only the interior, for these conditions.
  3. Verify required commissioning, balancing, blower-door, duct, pressure, electrical, fire, concrete, or other tests are complete and accepted. Confirm the qualified party and test date for each result.
  4. Collect approved plans, permit card, inspection history, test reports, special-inspection final letters, engineering approvals, energy forms, product approvals, and certificates. Assemble these into the final document index.
  5. Collect contractor, subcontractor, design-professional, utility, fire, zoning, septic, well, flood, historic, and other closeout approvals as applicable. Confirm every applicable agency's approval is present or identified as pending.
  6. Prepare a final document index and identify missing items. List any missing item as a blocking condition until it is obtained or explained.

I. Prefinal Walk, Correction, Scheduling, and Official Inspection

  1. Conduct a full internal prefinal inspection with project management, superintendent, and responsible trades. Use the Final-Readiness Zone Schedule as the walk sequence.
  2. Operate doors, windows, fixtures, alarms, equipment, fans, lights, and other approved components through normal final checks. Record any component that does not operate as approved.
  3. Create and assign a final-readiness deficiency list. Assign a responsible party and target date to every item found.
  4. Correct and reinspect every safety, access, documentation, operation, finish, and scope item. Do not close an item on this internal list without a completed reinspection.
  5. Confirm the building is presented in the condition expected for inspection and not staged to conceal defects. Do not cover, disconnect, or temporarily alter any condition to pass the walk or the official inspection.
  6. Confirm inspector access, lockbox or contact, pets, occupants, utilities, lighting, ladders, and approved documents. Arrange these conditions before the scheduled visit, not on the day of inspection.
  7. Schedule all trade finals and the building final in the required order. Confirm the jurisdiction's required sequence before submitting any request.
  8. Attend inspections with responsible parties and documents. Bring the final document index and be prepared to answer focused questions with it.
  9. Record approvals, corrections, partial finals, outstanding agencies, and occupancy restrictions. Record the result exactly as given by the inspector, without softening or reinterpreting it.
  10. Do not occupy or represent the project as complete until required final authorization is issued. Treat a partial or conditional approval as exactly that, not as a completed project.
  11. File the final-readiness checklist, inspection results, and remaining closeout actions. Carry any remaining item forward into Project 17's correction-control procedure and Project 18's closeout archive.

Common Final Inspection Readiness Review

Review the finished readiness package using the same checks regardless of which zone is being reviewed.

  • Scope-match check: confirm the completed project matches the permitted scope and every approved revision, with no unresolved unapproved change.
  • Life-safety check: confirm every required alarm, egress opening, and fire-separation item is installed, operable, and documented.
  • Access check: confirm every required panel, equipment, shutoff, and cleanout location remains accessible and labeled.
  • Prior-approval check: confirm every prior required inspection and correction is officially closed or has a documented, scheduled reinspection.
  • Document check: confirm the final document index is complete, legible, and cross-checked against the project.
  • Result check: confirm the official final-inspection result, corrections, partial approvals, and occupancy restrictions are recorded exactly as issued.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Completed Work Does Not Match the Approved Plans

Probable cause: a field change, substitution, or relocation was made without processing the required revision or amended permit.
Confirmation: compare the completed area against the current approved plan set, room by room, and identify the exact discrepancy.
Correction: submit the required revision or amended-permit request and obtain approval before proceeding to final inspection.
Reinspection: repeat the scope-match check on the corrected area.

A Required Access Point Is Blocked by a Finish

Probable cause: cabinetry, flooring, drywall, or trim was installed over a panel, cleanout, shutoff, or equipment access point without coordinating with the approved documents.
Confirmation: locate the required access point on the approved plans and verify its current condition in the field.
Correction: modify the finish to restore the required access, clearance, or labeling per approved documents.
Reinspection: repeat the access check at the corrected location.

A Prior Correction Was Never Officially Closed

Probable cause: the corrective work was completed but reinspection was never requested, or a verbal comment was treated as closure without official acceptance.
Confirmation: review the correction log for any item lacking a recorded official acceptance date.
Correction: schedule and complete the required reinspection through the official procedure in Project 17.
Reinspection: repeat the prior-approval check once official acceptance is recorded.

A Life-Safety Device Fails to Operate During the Prefinal Walk

Probable cause: a smoke alarm, carbon-monoxide alarm, or interconnection was left unpowered, miswired, or damaged after rough-in approval.
Confirmation: test the device using the manufacturer- and authority-approved procedure and check power, battery, and interconnection wiring.
Correction: repair or replace the device and confirm interconnection with all other required alarms.
Reinspection: repeat the life-safety check for the affected device and its interconnected group.

The Final Document Index Is Missing a Required Test Report or Certificate

Probable cause: a test was performed but the report was never filed, or a certificate was issued to a party who did not forward it to the project file.
Confirmation: compare the document index against the list of required tests, certificates, and approvals identified in Section H.
Correction: obtain the missing report or certificate from the responsible testing agency, trade, or design professional.
Reinspection: repeat the document check once the index is complete.

The Official Final Inspection Result Is Recorded Incompletely

Probable cause: the inspector's comments were paraphrased or summarized rather than recorded exactly, or a partial approval was treated as a full approval.
Confirmation: compare the recorded result against the official inspection record or notice issued by the authority.
Correction: correct the project record to match the official result exactly, including any partial approval or occupancy restriction.
Reinspection: repeat the result check against the corrected record.

Blocking Item
A deficiency or missing document that must be resolved before the final inspection may be scheduled or approved.
