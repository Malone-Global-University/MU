Job Overview

This project qualifies you to convert approved energy documents into one project-specific compliance matrix that a superintendent, trade, or inspector can use in the field. The finished work product is one energy-code compliance matrix covering enclosure assemblies, air sealing, fenestration, ducts, equipment, ventilation, testing, and documentation, with every item assigned to a responsible trade, inspection stage, verification method, and evidence requirement.

You will identify the adopted energy code and compliance path, build an assembly schedule from approved energy documents, create air-sealing and fenestration schedules, identify duct, equipment, and ventilation requirements, identify required testing, convert conflicts into clarification questions, and perform pre-insulation, pre-drywall, and pre-test reviews using the completed matrix before archiving it with test results and final documentation.

This project depends on the Project 01 reference binder for the adopted energy code and local amendments, the Project 04 code-note summary for energy notes already located on the plans, and the Project 03 application packet for the approved energy forms. It feeds directly into Project 15's interior concealment inspections, Project 16's final inspection, and the Chapter 20 capstone's energy-code matrix and testing evidence.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CP-067 — Identify insulation requirements for every enclosure assembly from approved energy documents.
  • CP-068 — Verify air-sealing details and air-barrier continuity against approved plans and inspection stages.
  • CP-069 — Inspect windows and doors for approved fenestration ratings, installation, and flashing coordination.
  • CP-070 — Review duct-sealing, duct-insulation, and duct-leakage-testing requirements.
  • CP-071 — Identify ventilation, equipment, and controls requirements tied to the energy path.
  • CP-072 — Prepare a trade-assigned, inspection-staged energy-code compliance checklist supported by evidence and test results.

Where This Skill Fits

Energy-code requirements touch nearly every trade on a residential project: framing and insulation contractors, window and door installers, duct and equipment installers, and the ventilation and controls trade. Without one coordinated matrix, energy requirements are scattered across separate architectural, structural, MEP, and energy documents, and a single missed duct-sealing note or fenestration rating can fail a blower-door test, a duct-leakage test, or the final energy inspection after the assemblies are already concealed.

The matrix built in this project becomes the pre-insulation and pre-drywall readiness tool used in Project 15, and its testing evidence and product register carry directly into the Project 16 final inspection and the Project 18 closeout compliance file.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • identifying the adopted energy code, local amendments, compliance path, and climate zone for one defined residential project
  • building an assembly schedule for walls, roofs, ceilings, floors, slabs, foundations, crawlspaces, and rim areas from approved energy documents
  • creating air-sealing and fenestration schedules tied to plan details and inspection stages
  • identifying duct, equipment, controls, and ventilation requirements from approved documents
  • identifying required testing, qualified testers, and corrective processes from approved requirements
  • converting conflicts and missing values into formal clarification questions and recording approved responses
  • performing pre-insulation, pre-drywall, and pre-test reviews using the completed matrix
  • archiving the completed matrix with products, photographs, tests, approvals, and revisions

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select, size, or substitute insulation values, equipment efficiencies, window ratings, or duct-sealing methods; those values come only from the approved energy documents, design professional, or manufacturer instructions
  • perform, supervise, or interpret a blower-door, duct-leakage, or other required energy test beyond the qualified tester's role and the learner's assigned documentation task
  • mix requirements from different compliance paths, such as combining prescriptive insulation values with performance-path duct-leakage targets, without design-professional approval
  • treat the completed matrix as a certificate of energy compliance, an inspection approval, or a substitute for the required final energy documentation

Trade Note

An energy-code matrix is only as reliable as the approved documents it is built from. A value copied from a similar project, a manufacturer's marketing sheet, or a prior job's matrix without rechecking this project's approved energy forms is the single most common source of the value-mismatch failures diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not enter an insulation value, fenestration rating, duct-sealing method, or test target into the matrix until its approved-document source, sheet or form reference, and compliance path are confirmed. A value carried forward from a similar project or a generic energy-code summary must be rechecked against this project's approved documents before it is used.

Shop Tip

Build the assembly schedule and the testing-requirement list before touring the site. Knowing which values and tests apply before the walk lets you check the actual installed condition against a stated target instead of guessing what "should" be there.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every energy-code task in this book, from this chapter's assembly schedule to the Chapter 20 capstone's energy-code matrix, follows the same sequence: confirm the adopted code and compliance path, extract every required value from approved documents, assign the value to a trade and inspection stage, verify the installed condition against the value, and record the evidence. Learning that sequence here, on one project's enclosure, fenestration, duct, and equipment scope, means the interior-concealment and final-inspection projects later in this book start from a matrix you already trust.

Approved Documents, Compliance Paths, and Verification Dates

Every value entered in the matrix must trace to an approved energy form, plan sheet, specification, or manufacturer instruction, with the compliance path recorded once at the matrix cover page and never mixed mid-matrix without a documented design-professional response. A matrix row without a recorded approved-document source cannot be treated as controlling.

Documentation Cue — Trace Every Value to Its Approved Source

Do not carry an insulation value, fenestration rating, or duct-sealing method forward from a similar project, a manufacturer marketing sheet, or memory. Confirm it directly against this project's approved energy documents and record the sheet, form, or specification reference before entering it in the matrix.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Conceal Before Required Testing and Approval

Insulation, drywall, exterior finish, and equipment enclosure must not proceed on any matrix item until the required pre-insulation, pre-drywall, or pre-test inspection stage tied to that item is complete and documented. This checklist is an educational and field-coordination tool, not a certificate of energy compliance, an inspection approval, or a substitute for the authority's required final energy documentation.

Field Safety Cue — Coordinating the Pre-Insulation and Pre-Test Walks

Schedule the pre-insulation and pre-drywall walks with the responsible framing, insulation, MEP, and energy trades present. Record findings using the matrix's own verification-method and evidence columns rather than informal notes, and photograph every concealed air, thermal, duct, and flashing detail before it becomes inaccessible.

Professional Boundary Cue — Reserved Determinations

Insulation values, equipment sizing, fenestration performance requirements, duct-sealing methods, and compliance-path selection are set by the design professional, energy professional, or approved energy documents, not by this matrix or its preparer. Blower-door, duct-leakage, and other required tests must be performed by the qualified tester identified in the approved requirements. Where a value or test requirement cannot be confirmed, label it unresolved and record the official source that must answer it.

E. Preparation — Confirm Code, Path, and Approved Documents

  1. Identify the adopted energy code, local amendments, compliance path, climate zone, project type, and authority. Confirm this against the Project 01 reference binder before entering any value into the matrix.
  2. Obtain approved energy forms, calculations, reports, plans, specifications, equipment schedules, and revisions. Record the current revision date for each document on the matrix cover page.
  3. Confirm whether compliance uses prescriptive, performance, rating, modeled, or other approved path. Record the confirmed path and its source document.
  4. Do not mix requirements from different compliance paths without design-professional approval. Flag any apparent mixing as a clarification question rather than resolving it independently.

F. Build the Assembly, Air-Sealing, and Fenestration Schedules

  1. Create an assembly schedule for walls, roofs, ceilings, floors, slabs, foundations, crawlspaces, and rim areas. List every distinct assembly by location using the approved energy form's own assembly identifiers.
  2. Record required insulation type, location, value, depth, density, installation grade, and continuity from approved documents. Enter one complete row per assembly; do not leave any column blank without marking it unresolved.
  3. Identify thermal bridges, narrow cavities, headers, corners, eaves, knee walls, attic access, and difficult transitions requiring field attention. Note each condition against the affected assembly row.
  4. Identify air-barrier materials and continuity at walls, floors, roofs, ceilings, foundations, openings, penetrations, chases, fireplaces, garages, and attics. Reference the controlling detail sheet for each location.
  5. Create an air-sealing checklist tied to plan details and inspection stages. Assign each air-sealing item to the inspection stage at which it must remain visible.
  6. Identify vapor-control and moisture-management requirements without adding an unapproved layer. Record only the layer shown in the approved assembly; flag any apparent omission as a clarification question.
  7. Create a fenestration schedule with window, door, skylight, area, performance rating, orientation, and product documentation. List every opening using the approved window and door schedule's own tag numbers.
  8. Verify product labels, certificates, ratings, and approved substitutions are retained before installation labels are removed. Photograph each label before it is covered or discarded.
  9. Identify window and door installation, flashing, insulation, and air-sealing checkpoints. Cross-reference the Project 08 envelope-inspection checklist for the shared flashing details.

G. Build the Duct, Equipment, and Ventilation Schedules

  1. Identify duct location, insulation, sealing, support, return pathways, access, and leakage testing requirements. Record each duct run and its required sealing method from the approved energy form.
  2. Identify piping insulation, equipment efficiency, controls, thermostats, zoning, heat-recovery, and commissioning requirements. Record equipment identifiers matching the approved equipment schedule.
  3. Identify local exhaust, whole-house ventilation, makeup air, controls, balancing, and verification requirements. Record each ventilation component and its required verification method.
  4. Identify lighting, high-efficacy, control, exterior-lighting, and other electrical energy requirements where applicable. Record only where the approved documents include a lighting energy requirement.
  5. Identify blower-door, duct-leakage, ventilation, balancing, equipment-startup, or other required testing. Record the required test type, target, and timing from approved requirements.
  6. Identify qualified tester, timing, equipment, target, form, and corrective process from approved requirements. Confirm the tester's qualification before scheduling.
  7. Identify inspections that must occur before insulation, drywall, exterior finish, equipment enclosure, or final approval. Assign each matrix item to its controlling concealment stage.

H. Assign Responsibility, Resolve Conflicts, and Verify in the Field

  1. Create installation photo requirements for concealed air, thermal, duct, and flashing details. List the specific location and angle required for each photograph.
  2. Create a product-document register for insulation, windows, doors, HVAC, water heating, ventilation, lighting, and controls. File each product's label, certificate, or rating alongside its matrix row.
  3. Compare architectural, structural, MEP, and energy documents for conflicts. Note every discrepancy found between the assembly schedule and the architectural or structural drawings.
  4. Convert conflicts and missing values into formal clarification questions. Use the clarification-question log rather than resolving the conflict independently.
  5. Record approved responses and revise the matrix. Update the affected row and note the response date and source.
  6. Assign every item to responsible trade, inspection stage, verification method, and evidence. Complete these four columns for every row before the matrix is used in the field.
  7. Perform a pre-insulation walk using the matrix. Verify framing cavities, air-sealing items, and rough utilities against the matrix before insulation begins.
  8. Perform an insulation and air-sealing readiness review before concealment. Confirm every assembly and air-sealing row is installed as specified and photographed.
  9. Perform a pretest review before blower-door or duct testing. Confirm the building is ready for the test conditions specified by the qualified tester.
  10. Record test results, corrective actions, retests, and final acceptance. Enter results directly into the Testing Schedule with the tester's name and date.
  11. Verify final certificates, labels, forms, manuals, and owner information are installed or delivered as required. Confirm this against the approved energy form's closeout requirements.
  12. Archive the completed energy matrix, products, photographs, tests, approvals, and revisions. Record the archive date, version number, and a summary of what was verified.

Common Matrix Inspection

Inspect the finished matrix using the same checks regardless of which section is being reviewed.

  • Sourcing check: confirm every value has a recorded approved-document source and compliance-path reference.
  • Assignment check: confirm every row has a responsible trade, inspection stage, verification method, and evidence requirement.
  • Completeness check: confirm all eight matrix sections from the Component Schedule are present and populated.
  • Field-walk check: confirm the pre-insulation and pre-drywall walks were completed using the matrix, with findings recorded.
  • Testing check: confirm every required test has a qualified tester, date, result, and corrective action where applicable.
  • Revision-control check: confirm the archived version carries a revision date, change log, and assigned reviewer.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Recorded Insulation Value Does Not Match the Approved Energy Form

Probable cause: the value was copied from a similar project, a manufacturer marketing sheet, or a prior matrix instead of this project's approved energy form.
Confirmation: recheck the approved energy form's assembly schedule and compare the stated value and location to the matrix entry.
Correction: replace the entry with the confirmed value and approved-document reference, and mark the superseded entry as corrected with a date.
Reinspection: repeat the sourcing check on the corrected entry.

A Matrix Row Has No Assigned Trade, Stage, or Evidence Requirement

Probable cause: the row was created during document review but never completed during the assignment step.
Confirmation: review every section for blank trade, stage, verification-method, or evidence columns.
Correction: assign the responsible trade, inspection stage, verification method, and evidence requirement using the approved documents and inspection sequence.
Reinspection: repeat the assignment check on the affected section.

Two Compliance Paths Appear Mixed Without Approval

Probable cause: a prescriptive assembly value and a performance-path duct-leakage target were entered together without a documented design-professional response.
Confirmation: compare the recorded compliance path on the cover page against the source of each conflicting value.
Correction: convert the conflict into a formal clarification question, obtain a design-professional response, and record it before revising the matrix.
Reinspection: repeat the compliance-path consistency check on the full matrix.

The Pre-Insulation Walk Finds Concealed Work Already Covered

Probable cause: insulation or drywall proceeded before the matrix-driven readiness review, concealing an air-sealing, duct, or structural item that required inspection.
Confirmation: compare the matrix's required inspection stages against the actual construction sequence on site.
Correction: stop further concealment, notify the responsible parties, and follow the required exposure, documentation, or corrective procedure before requesting inspection.
Reinspection: repeat the field-walk check once the item is exposed or otherwise verified.

A Required Test Was Performed by an Unqualified Party

Probable cause: a blower-door or duct-leakage test was run by a trade representative instead of the qualified tester identified in the approved requirements.
Confirmation: compare the tester recorded in the Testing Schedule against the qualification required by the approved documents.
Correction: arrange for the qualified tester to perform or verify the test and record the corrected result.
Reinspection: repeat the testing check with the corrected record.

No Revision Date or Change Log Was Recorded at Archiving

Probable cause: the matrix was used in the field without formally closing out a controlled version after testing and final documentation were complete.
Confirmation: check the archive record for a version number, date, and change-log summary.
Correction: assign a version number, record the archive date, and summarize what was verified as of that date.
Reinspection: repeat the revision-control check on the archived matrix.
