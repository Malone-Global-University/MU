Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare one residential plumbing rough-in for the required inspection. The finished work product is a documented, inspection-ready plumbing rough-in package: verified fixture locations, drain-waste-vent and water-distribution routing, supports and protection, active and stable required tests, cleared access, and a completed preinspection checklist, ready for the licensed trade and the building official.

You will confirm the permit and approved documents, verify fixture and piping locations against the approved plans, verify pipe support and protection, confirm which tests are required and who may perform them, verify cleanouts and access remain available, coordinate trade conflicts, complete a deficiency list, and attend the scheduled inspection with the responsible licensed trade.

This project depends on the Chapter 1 authority register and inspection-request procedures, the Chapter 2 permit decision, and the Chapter 3 approved application packet. It feeds directly into Chapter 17's correction tracking if any item is rejected, and into Chapter 16's final inspection once every rough-in stage is closed.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CP-049 — Verify fixture locations against approved plans and finish-surface references.
  • CP-050 — Inspect pipe support, hangers, spacing, and protection from damage.
  • CP-051 — Inspect drain-waste-vent layout, slope, fittings, and terminations against approved documents.
  • CP-052 — Identify required pressure, water, air, or other tests and the qualified party for each.
  • CP-053 — Verify cleanouts and required access remain available and unobstructed.
  • CP-054 — Complete a plumbing rough-in inspection-readiness checklist and record the inspection result.

Where This Skill Fits

The rough-in stage is the last point at which drain, waste, vent, water, and gas piping remain visible before wall and floor finishes conceal them permanently. Every later plumbing correction, fixture installation, and final plumbing check in Chapter 16 depends on the accuracy of what is verified and approved here. A missed cleanout, an unsupported run, or an unrecorded test failure at this stage becomes a far more expensive repair once drywall, flooring, or slab work has closed over it.

This project also reinforces the concealment-hold discipline used throughout the book: nothing that must remain visible for inspection is insulated, drywalled, backfilled, or connected until the required approval is documented.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming the plumbing permit, licensed contractor or authorized installer, approved plans, fixture schedule, and revisions
  • verifying fixture, DWV, water-distribution, and gas rough-in locations and routing against approved documents
  • verifying pipe support, protection, penetrations, and firestopping conditions that must remain visible
  • identifying required tests, the qualified party for each, and safe test-equipment handling
  • verifying cleanouts, access, and service space remain available after finish installation
  • completing a preinspection, correcting deficiencies, scheduling, and attending the inspection
  • recording the result and holding concealment until the required approval is documented

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select pipe material, size, or routing, or redesign any portion of the plumbing system beyond what the approved documents show
  • perform or supervise a regulated pressure, water, gas, or other test reserved for the licensed trade or testing agency
  • open, cut, or connect gas piping, backflow assemblies, or other work reserved for a licensed plumber
  • approve the rough-in, waive a correction, or treat a verbal inspector comment as a final determination without required written confirmation

Any item that cannot be verified against approved documents, or that requires a licensed trade's judgment, must be logged as an official question rather than assumed acceptable.

Trade Note

A plumbing rough-in inspection covers work that becomes completely inaccessible once concealed. Treat every verification step in this project as a last chance to confirm the installed system actually matches the approved documents — not as a formality before a routine approval.

Setup Standard

Do not begin the preinspection walk until the licensed trade confirms the rough-in is physically complete for this stage. Verifying an unfinished system wastes the preinspection and risks a scheduled inspection on work that is not actually ready.

Shop Tip

Photograph every cleanout, support, and penetration before the trade leaves the site for the day. If a correction surfaces later, dated photographs of the original condition make the corrective-action record in Chapter 17 far easier to complete accurately.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every rough-in inspection in this book, whether plumbing, electrical, or HVAC, follows the same underlying discipline: confirm the approved documents, verify the installed condition against them, confirm required tests and their qualified performer, clear access and obstructions, correct deficiencies before scheduling, and hold concealment until the official result is recorded. Learning that discipline here, on the plumbing system, means the electrical and HVAC rough-in projects that follow use a method you already trust.

Approved Documents, Licensed-Trade Boundaries, and Tests

Confirm every fixture location, pipe route, support detail, and required test against the approved plans, fixture schedule, and manufacturer instructions before verification begins. Regulated tests must be performed by the licensed trade or testing agency identified in the approved documents or local requirements — never by the learner acting alone.

Documentation Cue — Photograph Before Concealment

Photograph every cleanout, support, protection plate, and test setup by location before any material conceals it. An unphotographed condition that later needs verification cannot be reconstructed once drywall, flooring, or backfill is in place.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Perform or Supervise a Regulated Test

Do not create, initiate, or supervise a pressure, water, air, gas, or other regulated plumbing test beyond your qualification. These tests must be performed and documented by the licensed trade or a certified testing agency. Protect the building and any occupants from water, gas, air-pressure, freezing, or test-release hazards, and do not remain in a test area beyond what the responsible trade directs.

Field Safety Cue — Attending the Inspection

Have the approved plans, permit, and test documentation ready before the inspector arrives. Ask focused questions about any item you could not verify yourself. Record the inspector's result, corrections, and any areas that must remain open exactly as given, and treat verbal comments as field information pending the official written record.

Professional Boundary Cue — Reserved Determinations

Pipe sizing, venting design, backflow-assembly selection, and fixture-schedule changes are determined by the design professional, licensed plumber, or manufacturer instructions, not by this checklist or its preparer. Log an unresolved or ambiguous rough-in condition as an official question rather than selecting the least costly interpretation.

E. Preparation — Confirm Permit, Documents, and Prior Approvals

  1. Confirm the plumbing permit, licensed contractor or authorized installer, approved plans, specifications, fixture schedule, and revisions. Record the permit number and current revision on the Project and Permit Record above.
  2. Identify whether separate underground, rough, gas, water, sewer, backflow, or final inspections are required. Confirm the exact inspection type for this stage from the Chapter 1 binder.
  3. Confirm prior underground or slab plumbing approvals before concealment or concrete. Do not proceed with rough-in verification if a required prior approval is missing.

F. Verify Fixtures, DWV, and Water Distribution

  1. Verify fixture locations, room layouts, wall thicknesses, cabinet dimensions, and finished-surface references from approved documents. Measure and record each rough-in dimension against the approved fixture schedule.
  2. Verify water, drainage, waste, vent, gas, recirculation, specialty, and appliance rough-in locations from approved documents. Confirm each location against the approved plumbing plan.
  3. Verify pipe material, size, joining system, listing, compatibility, and use without redesigning the system. Compare the installed material against the approved specification; do not substitute or approve a substitution yourself.
  4. Verify drainage and vent routing, slope, fittings, directional changes, branch connections, and vent terminations as shown and required. Confirm slope direction and fitting type at every directional change.
  5. Verify water distribution routing, valves, manifolds, hammer control, backflow, thermal expansion, and equipment connections as documented. Confirm every valve and specialty device location against the approved plan.
  6. Verify gas piping, valves, sediment traps, supports, bonding coordination, test conditions, and appliance branches only through approved licensed work. Confirm this scope was performed and will be tested by the licensed trade.

G. Verify Support, Access, and Water-Heater Conditions

  1. Verify pipe support, hanger type, spacing, isolation, expansion allowance, and protection from damage. Measure hanger spacing against the governing requirement at three representative runs.
  2. Verify holes, notches, bored members, sleeves, nail plates, fire blocking, and structural coordination. Confirm every penetration through a structural member is protected as required.
  3. Verify cleanouts are provided, oriented, sized, accessible, and not blocked by cabinets or finishes. Confirm each cleanout location against the approved plan and the planned finish layout.
  4. Verify fixture, trap, valve, equipment, and service access will remain available after finish installation. Confirm planned cabinet, tile, or trim work does not block a required access point.
  5. Verify water-heater location, pan, drain, relief discharge, venting, combustion, seismic, electrical, and access requirements from approved documents. Confirm each item against the manufacturer instructions and approved plan.
  6. Verify tub, shower, receptor, pan, liner, drain, waterproofing, and flood-test inspection sequence where applicable. Confirm the flood test, if required, is scheduled at the correct stage.
  7. Verify firestopping, draft stopping, penetration protection, and rated-assembly conditions remain visible at the correct stage. Confirm none of these items is concealed before this inspection.
  8. Verify exterior penetrations, roof penetrations, sleeves, and flashing coordination. Confirm coordination with the Chapter 8 envelope inspection where penetrations overlap.

H. Confirm Tests, Coordinate Trades, and Complete Preinspection

  1. Confirm required water, air, vacuum, drainage, gas, pan, or other test method, pressure or head, duration, gauge, and responsible party from authoritative requirements. Record each required test on the test documentation form.
  2. Do not create or supervise a regulated test beyond the learner's qualification or the licensed trade's responsibility. Confirm the qualified party performing each test before it begins.
  3. Verify test equipment is suitable, calibrated where required, safely installed, and not overpressurizing components. Confirm with the licensed trade that equipment meets these conditions.
  4. Protect the building and occupants from water, gas, air-pressure, freezing, and test-release hazards. Confirm barriers, signage, or occupant notice are in place during active testing.
  5. Collect plumbing calculations, fixture data, backflow documents, gas sizing, manufacturer instructions, and test records where required. File each document with the readiness package.
  6. Photograph concealed piping, supports, protection plates, cleanouts, and tests. Photograph each item by location before any concealment occurs.
  7. Coordinate framing, electrical, HVAC, cabinets, roofing, and envelope conflicts before inspection. Resolve any conflict through the required RFI or revision process rather than a field improvisation.
  8. Remove debris and provide lighting, safe access, ladders, and visible pipe identification. Confirm the work area is safe and navigable before the trade preinspection.
  9. Complete a fixture-by-fixture and system-by-system preinspection with the responsible licensed trade. Walk every fixture group and system with the trade present.
  10. Create and correct the rough-in deficiency list. Assign each deficiency to a responsible party and confirm correction before scheduling.

I. Schedule, Attend, and Record the Inspection

  1. Confirm all required tests are active and stable for the scheduled inspection. Verify test gauges hold the required pressure or level immediately before the inspection window.
  2. Schedule the correct inspection type and record confirmation. Use the Chapter 1 binder's inspection-request procedure and record the confirmation number.
  3. Attend with approved plans, permit, test documentation, and responsible trade representative. Confirm all required parties and documents are present before the inspector arrives.
  4. Record approval, partial approval, test acceptance, corrections, and areas that must remain open. Record the result exactly as given by the inspector.
  5. Do not insulate, cover, connect, or conceal plumbing before required approval. Hold all concealment work until the official approval is documented.
  6. File the checklist, photographs, test records, inspection result, and correction status. Archive the completed package in the project compliance record.

Common Rough-In Inspection Review

Review the finished readiness package using the same checks regardless of which fixture group or system is being reviewed.

  • Location check: confirm every fixture and pipe location matches the approved fixture schedule and plan.
  • Support check: confirm hanger type, spacing, and protection match the governing requirement at every verified run.
  • Access check: confirm every cleanout and service point remains reachable after planned finishes.
  • Test check: confirm every required test is complete, documented, and performed by the qualified party.
  • Coordination check: confirm no unresolved conflict remains with framing, electrical, HVAC, or envelope work.
  • Concealment-hold check: confirm no rough-in work is concealed before the official approval is recorded.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Fixture Rough-In Does Not Match the Approved Fixture Schedule

Probable cause: the installer used a field default dimension or a different fixture model than the one specified on the approved schedule.
Confirmation: measure the installed rough-in against the approved fixture schedule and compare the specified fixture model to the one staged for installation.
Correction: relocate the rough-in to the approved dimension or process a formal revision if the fixture model has changed.
Reinspection: repeat the location check on the corrected fixture.

A Cleanout Is Blocked by a Planned Cabinet or Finish

Probable cause: the cabinet or finish layout was finalized without checking it against the approved cleanout location.
Confirmation: compare the cabinet or finish plan with the approved plumbing plan's cleanout location.
Correction: relocate the cleanout, the cabinet, or the finish detail through the required revision process; do not simply leave the cleanout inaccessible.
Reinspection: repeat the access check after the correction is installed.

A Required Test Is Not Stable at the Scheduled Inspection Time

Probable cause: a leak, a loose test cap, or an undersized gauge is allowing pressure or water level to drop before the inspector arrives.
Confirmation: have the licensed trade recheck the test setup, gauge reading, and connection points immediately before the inspection window.
Correction: have the licensed trade correct the leak or connection and re-establish the required test condition.
Reinspection: repeat the test check and confirm stability before requesting or continuing the inspection.

Pipe Supports Do Not Meet the Required Spacing

Probable cause: hangers were installed at a convenient stud or joist spacing rather than the governing requirement's maximum interval.
Confirmation: measure hanger spacing along the affected run and compare it with the governing requirement.
Correction: install additional supports at the required interval.
Reinspection: repeat the support check on the corrected run.

A Trade Conflict Damaged or Displaced Plumbing Work

Probable cause: framing, electrical, or HVAC work was installed without checking for an existing plumbing rough-in in the same cavity.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area and compare it with the approved plumbing plan and the other trade's approved plan.
Correction: repair or reroute the affected plumbing through the responsible trade and document the correction.
Reinspection: repeat the DWV or water-distribution conformance check on the corrected area.

Concealment Began Before the Required Approval Was Documented

Probable cause: insulation, drywall, or finish work proceeded on an assumption that the inspection had passed, without confirming the official written result.
Confirmation: check the inspection record for a documented approval before the concealment date.
Correction: stop the concealment work, expose the affected area if required by the authority, and obtain the documented approval before proceeding.
Reinspection: repeat the concealment-hold check on the affected area.

Cleanup

This project ends with a recorded inspection result and a controlled concealment hold, not a system left in an unverified or ambiguous state.

  • Remove test equipment, caps, and gauges once the qualified party confirms the test is officially closed
  • Confirm every deficiency identified during preinspection is either corrected and reinspected or formally logged as open
  • Store photographs, test records, and the completed checklist in the compliance record
  • Confirm the licensed trade has removed debris and restored safe access to the work area
  • Hold concealment materials until the official approval for this stage is documented in the project record

Field Terms

Rough-In Location
A pipe or fixture-connection point set to a finish-surface reference shown on the approved fixture schedule.
DWV
Drain, waste, and vent piping and its required slope, fittings, and terminations.
Cleanout
An access fitting installed to allow future clearing of a drain line, located and sized per approved documents.
Backflow Assembly
A device preventing reverse flow of nonpotable water into the potable system, selected and tested per approved requirements.
Qualified Party
The licensed plumber, testing agency, or other party authorized to perform and certify a required test.
Concealment
Installation of insulation, drywall, flooring, cabinetry, or backfill that would hide rough-in work from further inspection.
Deficiency
A verified condition that does not match approved documents or a governing installation requirement.
Flood Test
A required test filling a drain system with water to a specified level for a specified duration to confirm watertightness.
Preinspection
An internal walk of the completed rough-in performed with the responsible trade before the official inspection is requested.
Concealment Hold
The requirement that no material conceal inspected work until the official approval is documented.
