Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to manage one complete residential permit and inspection process from authority research through final authorization and compliance-file handoff. The finished work product is one auditable permit-and-inspection management record covering the authority register, permit decision, application packet, approved documents and revisions, staged inspection results, closed corrections, final approvals, and closeout archive for one approved capstone residential project.

You will confirm the authority having jurisdiction and every reviewing agency, build or update the Chapter 1 jurisdiction binder, complete the Chapter 2 permit decision for every scope component, prepare and submit the Chapter 3 application packet, track plan-review comments to resolution, conduct the Chapter 4 code-note review, prepare and complete every trade rough-in and concealment-stage inspection in sequence, build and evidence the energy-code and life-safety checklists, use the Chapter 17 correction log for every failed or conditional inspection, complete the Chapter 16 final-inspection readiness process, obtain final authorization, and build the Chapter 18 closeout compliance file.

This is the final project in Book 25. It integrates every earlier project's method — the binder, the permit decision, the application packet, the code-note summary, every trade inspection-readiness checklist, the energy matrix, the life-safety checklist, the stair and deck checklist, the interior concealment checklist, the final-inspection checklist, the correction log, and the closeout archive — into one continuous authority-to-closeout record. Nothing in this project replaces the building official, plans examiner, inspector, or design professional as the final authority.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CP-115 — Integrate permit research and authority verification into one capstone project record.
  • CP-116 — Prepare and track a complete permit application through plan review to issuance.
  • CP-117 — Conduct code-note review and prepare trade inspection checklists across every construction stage.
  • CP-118 — Prepare, schedule, and complete every required trade inspection in the correct sequence.
  • CP-119 — Track corrections and reinspection items to official closure and confirm final-inspection readiness.
  • CP-120 — Build and hand off a complete compliance closeout file with final authorization on record.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • selecting or confirming one approved capstone residential project and its permit-management scope
  • confirming the authority having jurisdiction, every separate reviewing agency, adopted codes, and local amendments
  • completing the permit decision, application packet, and plan-review cycle through issuance
  • conducting the code-note review and preparing the project inspection matrix
  • preparing and completing every required site, structural, envelope, mechanical, energy, life-safety, and interior concealment inspection in sequence
  • tracking every correction and reinspection item to official closure
  • completing final-inspection readiness, obtaining final authorization, and building the indexed closeout compliance file

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • size, redesign, approve, or field-modify any structural member, foundation, truss, MEP system, energy assembly, or life-safety component without the authorized design basis
  • perform licensed-trade work, sign as a licensed contractor, or self-certify any test, inspection, or approval reserved for the building official, inspector, special inspector, or design professional
  • treat a completed checklist, matrix, or archive entry as a permit approval, inspection result, or occupancy authorization in place of the official record
  • select a capstone project outside the outline's approved capstone description without confirming the applicable zoning, structural, MEP, energy, fire, egress, inspection, and closeout requirements are fully documented

Any requirement that cannot be confirmed from the authority having jurisdiction, approved construction documents, or the responsible design professional must be logged as unresolved and referred, not assumed.

Sequence Discipline Across Chapter Boundaries

The chapter order in this book is instructional; the capstone must follow the approved authority-to-closeout sequence even where it crosses chapter boundaries. Governing authorities and permit need are confirmed before work begins. Zoning, site, and utility constraints are resolved before foundation work. Foundation, framing, rough-trade, and envelope work remain visible until their required inspections pass. Energy-code verification is coordinated with framing, insulation, and testing. Life-safety items are completed before final approval. Corrections are converted into controlled work items before reinspection. Final inspection is requested only after every permit, trade final, document, test, correction, access, and cleanup requirement is complete.

Documentation Cue — One Continuous Compliance Matrix

Carry every requirement identified in Projects 01 through 19 into one continuous capstone compliance matrix with requirement, authority, document reference, responsible party, project stage, verification method, status, evidence, and closeout record. Do not maintain separate, disconnected checklists that cannot be reconciled at closeout.

Critical Safety Cue — No Concealment or Occupancy Before Authorization

Do not conceal any regulated work before its required inspection and approval are documented, and do not occupy, energize, connect, use, or represent the capstone project as complete before required final authorization is issued. This applies at every stage of the capstone, not only at the final inspection.

Field Safety Cue — Coordinating Multiple Inspectors and Trades

The capstone involves building, structural, plumbing, electrical, mechanical, gas, fire, energy, and possibly zoning or special-inspection parties across many site visits. Confirm inspection type, request method, cutoff time, and required site contact for each visit, and provide safe access for every inspector without exposing them to uncontrolled hazards.

Professional Boundary Cue — Design and Licensed-Trade Authority

Do not size, redesign, approve, or field-modify any structural member, foundation, truss, MEP system, energy assembly, or life-safety component during the capstone without the authorized design basis, and do not perform or supervise licensed-trade work, tests, or special inspections beyond the learner's qualification. Route field changes through the required revision or amended-permit process before concealment or installation.

E. Authority, Scope, and Permit Stage

  1. Select the approved capstone residential project and define its permit-management scope. Confirm the project matches the outline's approved capstone description before proceeding.
  2. Identify property, parcel, owner, applicant, contractor, design professionals, responsible trades, and project manager. Record this information on the Capstone Project Record above.
  3. Confirm the authority having jurisdiction and every separate reviewing or approving agency. Use the Chapter 1 method to identify every applicable agency for this specific project.
  4. Build or update the Chapter 1 jurisdiction reference binder. Recheck every entry rather than assuming a prior project's binder remains current.
  5. Confirm adopted codes, local amendments, zoning, licensing, owner-builder, permit, inspection, and closeout procedures. Verify directly from the updated binder before relying on any requirement.
  6. Obtain the Book 23 plan-interpretation package and Book 24 estimate package. Confirm the permit scope, approved documents, cost allowances, schedule assumptions, and trade responsibilities remain aligned across all three books.
  7. Create the capstone authority register, contact list, document register, revision log, and compliance matrix. Use these as the controlling records for the remainder of the capstone.
  8. Define the complete project scope, exclusions, alternates, deferred submittals, and owner responsibilities. State this scope in writing before completing the permit decision.
  9. Complete the Chapter 2 permit decision for every scope component. Classify each item and document the permit or exemption basis.
  10. Identify building, zoning, demolition, grading, plumbing, electrical, mechanical, gas, fire, energy, utility, environmental, well, septic, and specialty permits. Confirm which apply to this specific capstone scope.
  11. Identify required architects, engineers, surveyors, energy professionals, geotechnical professionals, special inspectors, testing agencies, and licensed trades. Confirm each is engaged before the corresponding work begins.
  12. Prepare the site, setback, easement, lot-coverage, height, access, utility, and overlay checklist. Use the Chapter 5 method against the capstone parcel.
  13. Obtain survey, zoning, utility, fire, environmental, HOA, and other prerequisite approvals. Complete these before foundation layout or excavation.

F. Application, Plan Review, and Permit Issuance

  1. Prepare the Chapter 3 permit application packet. Assemble every required document using the current jurisdictional submittal checklist.
  2. Cross-check address, parcel, owner, scope, valuation, plans, calculations, energy forms, and contractor information. Resolve any inconsistency before signature.
  3. Obtain authorized signatures, seals, disclosures, and certifications. Confirm every signature is authentic and properly authorized.
  4. Submit the application and record confirmation, fees, review number, and anticipated process. File the submission proof in the document register.
  5. Create the plan-review comment log. Prepare this before the first round of comments is received.
  6. Receive and preserve official review comments without alteration. Keep the original comment record unchanged in the compliance file.
  7. Assign each comment to the responsible designer, contractor, trade, owner, or consultant. Record the assignment and target response date.
  8. Prepare coordinated response letters and revised documents. Route every response through the responsible design professional.
  9. Confirm revision clouds, dates, sheet indexes, calculations, and responses align. Cross-check the revised package before resubmission.
  10. Resubmit and track each review cycle until permit issuance. Repeat Steps 6 through 9 for every additional review cycle.
  11. Verify the issued permit scope, conditions, approved documents, deferred items, expiration, and inspection sequence. Confirm the issued permit matches the intended project scope.
  12. Create the jobsite permit station with approved plans, permit card, contacts, and inspection records. Keep this station current throughout construction.

G. Code-Note Review and Inspection Matrix

  1. Conduct the Chapter 4 code-note review with the issued plan set. Confirm the review uses the exact issued plan revision, not an earlier draft.
  2. Prepare the project inspection matrix by stage, permit, trade, prerequisite, test, document, and concealment hold point. Use this matrix to sequence every remaining inspection in the capstone.
  3. Coordinate schedule, procurement, labor, inspections, testing, and permit conditions with Book 24 estimate assumptions. Flag and resolve any mismatch before construction proceeds.
  4. Confirm site controls, property layout, setbacks, erosion, utilities, and access before field work. Verify these controls directly against the approved site plan.

H. Staged Construction Inspections

  1. Prepare and complete required site, footing, foundation, underground, or slab inspections. Use the Chapter 6 method for this stage.
  2. Record inspection results and prohibit concrete, backfill, or concealment until approval. Do not proceed past this hold point without documented approval.
  3. Prepare and complete framing, shear, connector, truss, or structural inspections. Use the Chapter 7 method for this stage.
  4. Prepare and complete roof dry-in, flashing, WRB, opening, lath, siding, or envelope inspections. Use the Chapter 8 method for this stage.
  5. Prepare and complete plumbing underground, rough, pressure, pan, gas, and other required trade inspections. Use the Chapter 9 method, confirming licensed-trade participation for every regulated test.
  6. Prepare and complete electrical service, underground, rough, temporary-power, or other required inspections. Use the Chapter 10 method for this stage.
  7. Prepare and complete HVAC, duct, ventilation, gas, fireplace, and mechanical rough inspections. Use the Chapter 11 method for this stage.
  8. Prepare the energy-code matrix and collect product, installation, and testing evidence. Use the Chapter 12 method, verifying before insulation and drywall concealment.
  9. Prepare the fire, alarm, carbon-monoxide, egress, rated-assembly, stair, guard, handrail, and deck checklists. Use the Chapter 13 and Chapter 14 methods.
  10. Prepare and complete insulation, air-sealing, fire-blocking, drywall, lath, or interior concealment inspections. Use the Chapter 15 method for this stage.
  11. Maintain a photographic record of every concealed regulated assembly. Index each photograph by location, system, and date.
  12. Maintain current approved documents in field use and archive superseded documents. Remove superseded sheets from active field use immediately after each approved revision.
  13. Submit every field change and substitution through required RFI, design, revision, and amended-permit procedures. Obtain written approval before concealment or installation.
  14. Do not allow unauthorized changes to proceed merely to preserve schedule. Stop work and route the change through the proper procedure instead.
  15. Record all official inspection results, verbal clarifications requiring confirmation, partial approvals, and hold points. Preserve every official record unaltered in the compliance file.

I. Correction Tracking and Ongoing Controls

  1. Use the Chapter 17 correction log for every failed or conditional inspection item. Enter each item using the official wording from the inspection record.
  2. Assign corrections, obtain revised design where required, document work, and verify readiness. Do not close an item until the correction is verified.
  3. Schedule reinspections and close items only after official acceptance. Preserve the official acceptance record for the closeout file.
  4. Monitor permit expiration, inactivity, extension, fee, and agency conditions throughout construction. Address any expiration risk before it stops the project.
  5. Update project cost, schedule, procurement, change orders, and risk when compliance requirements change. Reconcile these updates against the Book 24 estimate.
  6. Collect special-inspection reports, tests, product approvals, certificates, and final letters as the work proceeds. File each record in the document register at the time it is issued.

J. Final Inspection and Authorization

  1. Prepare the Chapter 16 final building inspection checklist. Use the completed capstone scope as the basis for this checklist.
  2. Confirm all prior inspections, corrections, tests, trade finals, agency approvals, utilities, access, cleanup, and documents are complete. Do not schedule the final inspection until every item is confirmed.
  3. Complete the internal prefinal inspection and close the deficiency list. Correct every internally identified deficiency before scheduling.
  4. Schedule trade finals and final building inspection in the required order. Confirm the jurisdiction's required sequence before scheduling.
  5. Attend final inspections with approved documents and responsible parties. Bring the complete jobsite permit station to every final inspection.
  6. Record final approvals, partial approvals, remaining agency items, and occupancy restrictions. Preserve every result unaltered in the compliance file.
  7. Complete any final corrections and reinspections. Repeat the Section I correction-tracking process for any final-stage item.
  8. Obtain certificate of occupancy, certificate of completion, utility release, final permit record, or other required authorization. Confirm the exact document required by the authority having jurisdiction.
  9. Do not allow occupancy or use before required authorization. Treat this as a stop-work condition until the authorization is issued.

K. Closeout, Handoff, and Transition

  1. Build the Chapter 18 closeout compliance file. Use the completed capstone records as the source material.
  2. Index permits, approved plans, revisions, inspections, corrections, tests, products, warranties, photographs, and final approvals. Confirm every record is present and legible.
  3. Prepare the owner or contractor compliance summary and ongoing permit conditions. Distinguish mandatory maintenance from recommendations.
  4. Conduct the compliance-file handoff and document receipt. Obtain signed acknowledgment of file delivery where required.
  5. Archive the complete controlled project record with retention responsibility. Record the responsible custodian and retention period.
  6. Conduct a post-project review of preventable inspection failures, plan issues, correction costs, schedule effects, and process improvements. Record findings for future project use.
  7. Document the formal transition to Book 26 construction project management. Confirm the archive is complete and ready to serve as the starting record for that book's systems.

Common Capstone Review

Review the finished capstone record using the same checks regardless of which stage produced the entry.

  • Authority check: confirm every applicable agency, adopted code, and local amendment is current and sourced.
  • Permit check: confirm the application, plan-review cycle, and issued permit are complete and internally consistent.
  • Inspection-sequence check: confirm every required inspection was completed in the correct order with no unauthorized concealment.
  • Correction check: confirm every correction item was assigned, corrected, reinspected, and closed only upon official acceptance.
  • Final-authorization check: confirm the required certificate, release, or authorization is on file before any representation of project completion.
  • Closeout check: confirm the indexed archive is complete, protected, and formally handed off.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

An Inspection Stage Was Concealed Before Approval

Probable cause: a trade proceeded with insulation, drywall, concrete, or backfill before the required inspection result was documented, or a verbal field comment was mistaken for a final approval.
Confirmation: compare the inspection matrix against the official inspection record for the affected stage and confirm whether a documented approval exists.
Correction: stop further work in the area, contact the authority having jurisdiction, and follow the required procedure for exposing or otherwise verifying the concealed work.
Reinspection: repeat the inspection-sequence check once the authority's required corrective procedure is complete.

A Correction Item Was Closed Without Official Acceptance

Probable cause: the responsible trade completed and photographed the correction but reinspection was never scheduled or the acceptance record was never obtained.
Confirmation: review the correction log for items marked closed without an attached official acceptance record.
Correction: schedule the reinspection through the official procedure and obtain the acceptance record before treating the item as closed.
Reinspection: repeat the correction check on the affected log entries.

The Issued Permit Scope Does Not Match the Constructed Work

Probable cause: a field change, substitution, or scope expansion proceeded without the required revision or amended-permit process.
Confirmation: compare the completed work against the currently approved plans room by room, elevation by elevation, and system by system.
Correction: prepare and submit the required revision or amended permit before requesting final inspection.
Reinspection: repeat the permit check once the revision or amendment is approved.

The Permit Has Expired or Is at Risk of Expiring

Probable cause: inspection activity slowed or stopped without tracking the jurisdiction's inactivity or expiration rules.
Confirmation: check the permit issuance date, required commencement, inactivity limits, and extension rules against the current project schedule.
Correction: submit the required extension or reactivation request before the permit lapses.
Reinspection: repeat the permit-monitoring step on the Book 24 estimate's schedule assumptions.

The Closeout File Is Missing a Required Final Authorization

Probable cause: the project was represented as complete before the certificate of occupancy, certificate of completion, or utility release was actually issued.
Confirmation: compare the closeout file's index against the authority's published final-authorization requirements for this project type.
Correction: obtain the missing authorization before handing off the file or allowing occupancy or use.
Reinspection: repeat the final-authorization check on the completed closeout file.

The Compliance Matrix Does Not Reconcile With the Book 24 Estimate

Probable cause: a permit, testing, licensed-trade, or correction cost was identified during the capstone but never fed back into the estimate or schedule.
Confirmation: compare the compliance matrix's cost- and schedule-relevant items against the current Book 24 estimate package.
Correction: update the estimate and schedule to reflect the actual permit, testing, and correction requirements identified.
Reinspection: repeat the coordination check between the matrix and the estimate package.

Cleanup

This capstone ends with the compliance file formally handed off and archived, not left as a working construction record.

  • Remove superseded plan sheets, expired forms, and duplicate records from the active closeout file
  • Confirm confidential owner, financial, and access information is stored securely and separately where required
  • Close and label every correction-log entry that reached official acceptance
  • Record the responsible custodian and retention period for the archived project record
  • Store the archived capstone record where it can be located for the Book 26 project-management transition

  2. What must be confirmed before the capstone application packet is submitted, and why does a mismatch between plans, calculations, and application data create risk during plan review?
  3. Why must plan-review comments be preserved unaltered even after a response is prepared?
  4. What is a hold point, and why must regulated work remain visible until its required inspection is documented?
  5. Why can a correction item not be closed on the strength of a completed corrective action alone?
  6. What must the learner do when a field change is needed after concealment has already occurred, and why is proceeding without approval a stop-work condition?
  7. Why must the compliance matrix be reconciled against the Book 24 estimate package throughout construction rather than only at closeout?
  8. What must be true of the closeout compliance file before the project may be represented as complete or occupied?
  9. What is the purpose of the post-project review, and what categories of findings does it record?
  10. Why does this capstone conclude with a documented transition to Book 26 rather than simply ending at final approval?

Performance Check

With every capstone stage completed and recorded, review the finished record against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the authority register and permit decision are current, the issued permit matches the constructed work, every required inspection was completed in sequence with documented approval before concealment, every correction item is closed with official acceptance, final authorization is on file, and the closeout compliance file is indexed and formally handed off. Complete the Capstone Compliance Worksheet and correct any failed item before archiving the project record. If an entry fails, identify whether the error occurred in authority verification, permit tracking, inspection sequencing, correction closure, final authorization, or archiving before correcting and re-running the affected check.

Project Closeout

You have completed the capstone project for Book 25: managing permits and inspections for a residential project. This project required you to integrate permit research and authority verification into one capstone project record (CP-115), prepare and track a complete permit application through plan review to issuance (CP-116), conduct code-note review and prepare trade inspection checklists across every construction stage (CP-117), prepare, schedule, and complete every required trade inspection in the correct sequence (CP-118), track corrections and reinspection items to official closure and confirm final-inspection readiness (CP-119), and build and hand off a complete compliance closeout file with final authorization on record (CP-120).

You now have a complete, auditable authority-to-closeout permit and inspection management record for one real residential project, built on the exact authority register, permit decision, application, code-note, inspection-readiness, correction, and closeout methods carried forward from every earlier project in this book.

Store the archived capstone compliance record where it remains available for the transition to Book 26 construction project management. The authority-scope-permit-document-inspect-correct-approve-archive workflow you followed here is the same workflow you will apply to every future residential project.
