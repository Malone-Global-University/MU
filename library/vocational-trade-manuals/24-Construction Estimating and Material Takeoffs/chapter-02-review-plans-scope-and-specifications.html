Job Overview

This project qualifies you to review a residential bidding package before any quantity is measured. The finished work product is one controlled pre-estimate package containing a completed active document register, a verified scale and sheet inventory, a scope map identifying included and excluded work, a list of owner, contractor, subcontractor, supplier, utility, and designer responsibilities, a list of allowances, alternates, unit prices, and provisional work, a prioritized clarification log with responses, and a final pre-takeoff review summary with authorization to proceed.

You will confirm the bidding package is complete and current, review every document category from site through specifications, identify what is shown but not narrated, what is narrated but not shown, and what is required for a complete installation but not assigned to any party, convert every unresolved item into a classified and prioritized clarification, submit and record responses, and update the scope map and document register before takeoff begins.

This is the second project in the book, and it opens the workbook you built in Chapter 1. Every quantity you measure from Chapter 3 through Chapter 16 depends on the scope boundaries, exclusions, and clarification responses recorded in this project. A takeoff built on an unreviewed document set produces a number that cannot be defended later.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • ET-007 — Review plan sheets for completeness, currency, and internal consistency.
  • ET-008 — Identify scope boundaries among owner, contractor, subcontractor, supplier, utility, municipal, and designer responsibilities.
  • ET-009 — Identify excluded work, allowances, alternates, unit prices, and provisional work.
  • ET-010 — Locate and cross-reference specifications with drawings, schedules, and details.
  • ET-011 — Flag missing, conflicting, or ambiguous information before takeoff begins.
  • ET-012 — Create an estimating clarification list, classify each item, and record its resolution.

Where This Skill Fits

Every quantity takeoff chapter that follows — sitework in Chapter 3, concrete in Chapter 4, framing in Chapter 5, and every chapter through exterior work in Chapter 16 — begins by opening the scope map and clarification log built in this project. A takeoff performed against an unreviewed plan set risks measuring superseded geometry, missing an item shown on one sheet but omitted from another, or pricing work that is actually an owner responsibility.

The document-verification and clarification discipline built here — confirming sheet currency, cross-referencing drawings against specifications, and converting every open question into a classified, prioritized, and tracked item — is the same discipline the Chapter 19 proposal depends on when it states exclusions, assumptions, and unresolved clarifications to the client.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming the bidding package is complete, current, and internally consistent
  • reviewing site, architectural, structural, MEP, and specification documents in sequence
  • identifying scope shown but not narrated, narrated but not shown, and required but not assigned
  • defining owner, contractor, subcontractor, supplier, utility, municipal, and designer responsibilities
  • identifying allowances, alternates, unit prices, deferred selections, and provisional work
  • writing, classifying, prioritizing, submitting, and tracking clarification questions
  • converting unresolved items into approved assumptions, allowances, alternates, unit prices, exclusions, or contingency
  • preparing the final pre-takeoff review summary and obtaining authorization to proceed

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin any quantity takeoff before the pre-takeoff review summary is approved
  • resolve a design conflict, code question, or ambiguous specification independently; unresolved items must be converted into an RFI, owner question, supplier or subcontractor question, code or inspector question, or a documented internal assumption
  • proceed on a document set with unverified scale, missing sheets, or unreconciled revisions
  • submit clarification questions after the stated question deadline without company authorization

Any scope item that remains unresolved after the question deadline must be converted into an approved assumption, allowance, alternate, unit price, exclusion, or contingency before takeoff begins.

Trade Note

A missing clarification does not disappear from the project; it becomes an unpriced risk carried silently into the bid. Every ambiguity found during this review must leave a visible trace — a clarification entry, an assumption, an allowance, or an exclusion — so the estimate reflects a decision rather than an oversight.

Setup Standard

Do not begin any quantity takeoff until the pre-takeoff review summary (Step 30) has been prepared and approval to proceed has been obtained. A document set that has not passed this review must be corrected or clarified before it is used for Chapter 3 or any later chapter.

Shop Tip

Read the specifications before the drawings for each system. A specification section often defines a scope boundary or a responsibility split that changes how the corresponding drawing must be interpreted, and reading it first prevents a scope assumption from being made twice.

Reading Drawings and Specifications Together

Never review a drawing in isolation from its corresponding specification section, and never review a specification section without locating the drawings it governs. A door schedule without the specified hardware group, or a hardware specification without the door schedule it applies to, produces an incomplete scope determination even when each document is individually clear.

Traceability Cue — Verify Scale Before Any Scaled Measurement

Verify printed or digital scale against a known written dimension before taking any scaled measurement from a drawing. Do not scale a drawing when a written dimension, schedule, or approved clarification already controls that value.

Critical Control Cue — Confirm the Document Set Is Current Before Reviewing Scope

Confirm every sheet and specification section listed in an index is present, and remove superseded documents from the active set before mapping scope. Reviewing scope against a superseded sheet produces a scope map that must be entirely redone once the correct revision is identified.

Documentation Cue — Record Every Clarification With Its Document Reference

Write each clarification with its specific document reference, the exact issue, the estimating impact, and a specific question. A clarification without a document reference cannot be verified as resolved when a response is received.

Software Cue — Confirm Version Comparison Behavior

Confirm how the markup or PDF-comparison tool identifies revision clouds, layer changes, and superseded content before relying on it to detect differences between document versions. Some tools do not flag every type of revision automatically.

C. Document Review Sequence

  • Site and logistics documents: site plan, grading plan, utility plan, access, property limits, and logistics information.
  • Architectural documents: plans, elevations, sections, schedules, details, and finishes.
  • Structural documents: foundations, framing, connections, and special inspections.
  • MEP and specialty documents: plumbing, electrical, HVAC, fire, low-voltage, and specialty documents where included in the package.
  • Specifications: products, standards, submittals, testing, warranties, installation, and closeout requirements for every system reviewed above.
  • Non-substitution rule: do not review a specification section without locating and reviewing the drawings it governs, and do not review a drawing without locating its corresponding specification section.

E. Preparation — Document Receipt and Verification

  1. Obtain the invitation, bid instructions, scope narrative, plan set, specifications, addenda, schedules, reports, alternates, selections, and submission forms. Confirm every listed component of the bidding package has actually been received.
  2. Record receipt date, bid due date, site-visit date, question deadline, delivery method, required forms, bonds, signatures, and acknowledgments. Enter these on the Bidding Package Control Record in the Review Conventions section above.
  3. Create the active document register. List every sheet or specification section with its title, date, revision, and status.
  4. Confirm all sheets and specification sections listed in indexes are present. Cross-check the plan index and specification table of contents against the actual document set.
  5. Identify duplicate, superseded, missing, unreadable, resized, or conflicting documents. Remove superseded documents from the active set while preserving them in the archive.
  6. Verify drawing scales against written dimensions. Select one known written dimension per drawing type, measure it at the stated scale, and record any discrepancy on the scale and sheet-verification record.
  7. Read title blocks, legends, general notes, abbreviations, revision clouds, and addendum references. Note any abbreviation or symbol that is not defined in the legend.

F. Document Review and Scope Mapping Procedure

  1. Review site, grading, utilities, access, property limits, and logistics. Mark boundaries, easements, and access constraints on the scope map.
  2. Review architectural plans, elevations, sections, schedules, details, and finishes. Cross-reference each schedule against its corresponding plan and detail.
  3. Review structural foundations, framing, connections, and special inspections. Note any special inspection requirement and its responsible party.
  4. Review plumbing, electrical, HVAC, fire, low-voltage, and specialty documents where included. Confirm which MEP systems have complete design documents versus allowance-level information.
  5. Review specifications for products, standards, submittals, testing, warranties, installation, and closeout. Cross-reference each specification section against the drawings it governs.
  6. Identify work shown on drawings but omitted from the narrative. Record each instance on the scope map as shown-but-not-narrated.
  7. Identify work specified but not clearly shown. Record each instance on the scope map as narrated-but-not-shown.
  8. Identify work required for a complete installation but not assigned. Record each instance on the scope map as required-but-not-assigned.

G. Responsibility, Exclusion, and Allowance Procedure

  1. Define owner, contractor, subcontractor, supplier, utility, municipal, and designer responsibilities. Record each on the responsibility list by scope item.
  2. Identify demolition, protection, temporary work, permits, testing, inspections, cleanup, and closeout responsibilities. Assign each to a specific party on the responsibility list.
  3. Identify owner-furnished, contractor-installed and owner-furnished, owner-installed items. Record each with its exact designation.
  4. Identify allowances, alternates, unit prices, deferred selections, and provisional work. Record each on the exclusion and allowance list with its stated basis.
  5. List explicit and proposed exclusions. Record explicit exclusions as stated in the documents and proposed exclusions identified during this review, kept in clearly separate columns.

H. Conflict Identification and Clarification Procedure

  1. Compare dimensions, schedules, notes, sections, and details for inconsistencies. Record each conflict with the specific documents compared.
  2. Flag missing dimensions, undefined assemblies, ambiguous products, incomplete schedules, and uncoordinated systems. Add each to the clarification log.
  3. Identify site conditions requiring a visit, survey, test, investigation, or owner information. Add each to the clarification log with the required action.
  4. Write each clarification with document reference, issue, estimating impact, and specific question. Use the fields defined in Section D.
  5. Classify questions as RFI, owner, supplier, subcontractor, code, inspector, or internal assumption. Assign the classification field for every clarification log entry.
  6. Assign priority based on cost, scope, safety, schedule, and risk. Rank clarifications so the highest-impact items are submitted first.
  7. Submit questions before the required deadline and record responses. Enter the submission date and the response, or its absence, for every clarification log entry.
  8. Convert unresolved items into approved assumptions, allowances, alternates, unit prices, exclusions, or contingency. Record the conversion decision and its basis for every clarification without a response by the question deadline.
  9. Update the document register and scope map after every response. Reflect each resolved clarification in both records before proceeding.

I. Closeout — Pre-Takeoff Review Summary

  1. Prepare the final pre-takeoff review summary and obtain approval to proceed. Confirm the document register, scope map, responsibility list, exclusion and allowance list, and clarification log are complete, and record the name and date of the person authorizing takeoff to begin.

Common Review Inspection

Inspect the finished pre-estimate package using the same checks regardless of which document category or clarification was reviewed.

  • Completeness check: confirm every sheet and specification section listed in an index is present in the active document register.
  • Currency check: confirm no superseded document remains in the active set and every revision reflected on the scope map matches the current document register.
  • Scale check: confirm scale was verified against a known written dimension for every drawing type used during the review.
  • Scope-mapping check: confirm every identified shown-but-not-narrated, narrated-but-not-shown, and required-but-not-assigned item appears on the scope map.
  • Responsibility check: confirm every demolition, protection, temporary work, permit, testing, inspection, cleanup, and closeout item has an assigned party on the responsibility list.
  • Clarification check: confirm every clarification log entry has a document reference, issue, estimating impact, question, classification, priority, and a recorded response or conversion.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Scaled Dimension Does Not Match a Written Dimension on the Same Sheet

Probable cause: the sheet was printed or exported at a scale other than the stated scale, or the digital file was resized during transmission.
Confirmation: measure a second known written dimension at the stated scale and compare the result to the first discrepancy.
Correction: obtain a correctly scaled copy of the sheet from the document source and repeat the scale-verification step before using any scaled measurement from that sheet.
Reinspection: repeat the scale-verification check on the corrected sheet and record the result on the scale and sheet-verification record.

A Superseded Sheet Remains in the Active Document Register

Probable cause: a later revision was received but the earlier revision was not removed from the active set.
Confirmation: compare revision dates and addendum references across all versions of the sheet.
Correction: move the superseded sheet to the archive, update the document register with the current revision, and re-check every scope-map entry that referenced the superseded sheet.
Reinspection: repeat the completeness and currency checks on the corrected register.

A Specification Section References a Drawing That Cannot Be Located

Probable cause: the referenced drawing was omitted from the package, or the specification references a sheet number from a different revision of the plan index.
Confirmation: check the plan index for the referenced sheet number and confirm whether it exists in the current or an earlier revision.
Correction: add a clarification log entry classified as an RFI, requesting the missing drawing or a confirmed cross-reference.
Reinspection: confirm the clarification log entry includes the exact specification section and sheet reference before submission.

A Clarification Question Was Submitted Without a Document Reference

Probable cause: the issue was identified verbally or from memory rather than recorded directly from the document during review.
Confirmation: review the clarification log entry for a blank or vague document-reference field.
Correction: return to the source document, record the exact sheet, section, schedule, or detail, and update the clarification log entry before resubmission.
Reinspection: repeat the clarification check on the corrected entry.

A Scope Item Appears on a Drawing but Has No Assigned Responsibility

Probable cause: the item was reviewed for its physical scope but not cross-checked against the responsibility list during Section G.
Confirmation: compare the scope map entry against the responsibility list for a matching assignment.
Correction: add the item to the responsibility list with its assigned party, or add a clarification log entry if no document assigns responsibility.
Reinspection: repeat the responsibility check after the correction is recorded.

A Clarification Received No Response by the Question Deadline

Probable cause: the recipient did not respond in time, or the question was misrouted to the wrong classification.
Confirmation: check the submission date and classification recorded in the clarification log against the question deadline.
Correction: convert the item into an approved assumption, allowance, alternate, unit price, exclusion, or contingency, and record the conversion decision and its basis in the clarification log.
Reinspection: confirm every unresolved clarification has a recorded conversion before the pre-takeoff review summary is prepared.

The Pre-Takeoff Review Summary Was Prepared With Open Clarifications

Probable cause: Section H, Step 9 was skipped, or a clarification's conversion was not completed before Section I began.
Confirmation: review the clarification log for any entry without a recorded response or conversion.
Correction: complete the conversion for every remaining open item, then re-update the scope map and document register.
Reinspection: repeat the Common Review Inspection section before re-preparing the pre-takeoff review summary.
