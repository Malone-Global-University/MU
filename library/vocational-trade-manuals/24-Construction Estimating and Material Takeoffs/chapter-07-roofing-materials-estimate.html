Job Overview

This project qualifies you to convert an approved roof plan set into a complete, traceable roofing material and labor worksheet inside the Chapter 1 workbook. The finished work product is one roofing cost-code section containing reconciled slope area by plane, field roofing quantity, starter, ridge and hip cap, underlayment, self-adhered membrane, drip edge, valley material, flashing, ventilation, fasteners, sealants and accessories, tear-off and disposal where applicable, access equipment, labor, and a documented pricing and assumption record.

You will calculate actual roof-plane area from the approved plan set rather than footprint area, apply product-specific waste, convert net quantities into supplier selling units, measure and price accessories and flashing separately from field roofing, identify access and disposal requirements, estimate labor by task, price every line from a dated source, and complete an independent review of slope, valleys, penetrations, and waste before the worksheet is used in Chapter 18 pricing.

This project builds directly on the Chapter 1 workbook and the Chapter 2 scope review. It supplies the roofing cost-code section used in the Chapter 20 capstone estimate and feeds directly into the Chapter 8 building-envelope estimate, which begins at the wall-to-roof transition established here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • ET-037 — Calculate roof area from actual slope, not footprint, reconciled against plan geometry.
  • ET-038 — Estimate shingles or the specified field roofing product using actual manufacturer coverage.
  • ET-039 — Estimate underlayment, including self-adhered membrane, by area, laps, and roll coverage.
  • ET-040 — Estimate flashing and drip edge by linear condition and specified assembly.
  • ET-041 — Estimate ridge and hip cap and ventilation materials from specified type and quantity.
  • ET-042 — Prepare a roofing material worksheet with reconciled quantities, waste, labor, access, and pricing.

Where This Skill Fits

This project uses the workbook built in Chapter 1 and the scope boundaries, exclusions, and clarifications established in Chapter 2. The roof-plane geometry and slope information carried here is reconciled against the framing and structural-panel takeoffs from Chapters 5 and 6, since roof sheathing area and roofing area must trace to the same slope planes.

The roofing cost-code section built here supplies quantities directly to Chapter 17 labor estimating, Chapter 18 pricing, and the Chapter 20 capstone estimate. The wall-to-roof transition, flashing compatibility, and drainage assumptions documented here are the starting point for the Chapter 8 building-envelope estimate.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • calculating actual slope area for every roof plane, including dormers, crickets, and irregular geometry
  • estimating field roofing, starter, ridge and hip cap, underlayment, and self-adhered membrane quantities
  • measuring drip edge, valley material, and step, counter, apron, headwall, sidewall, and kickout flashing separately
  • counting penetrations and calculating specified ventilation products without redesigning the ventilation system
  • calculating fasteners, sealants, primers, adhesives, boots, collars, and compatible metals
  • including tear-off, disposal, access equipment, and labor for a complete roofing worksheet

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • redesign the ventilation system, roofing assembly, or flashing details shown or specified in the approved documents
  • select a roofing product, underlayment, or flashing assembly that is not specified or approved
  • invent a coverage rate, waste percentage, or fastener schedule that is not supplied by the manufacturer, company history, or the chapter project specification
  • price the worksheet using an undated or unverified material cost; every unit cost in this chapter must carry a source and a date

Any roofing quantity that cannot be traced to the plan set, a manufacturer coverage table, or an approved assumption must be recorded as an open clarification rather than estimated.

Trade Note

Roofing area is never the same as footprint area except on a flat roof. Every slope factor, plane split, and irregular geometry calculation in this chapter must be shown in the workbook, not carried as a single unlabeled multiplier, so a reviewer can trace the actual squares back to the plan.

Slope Standard

Do not calculate any product quantity from footprint or plan-view area. Convert every plane to actual slope area using the approved slope factor or measured slope before applying coverage or waste. A quantity calculated from footprint area alone must be treated as unverified and corrected before pricing.

Shop Tip

Build the roof-plane schedule before calculating a single square of shingles. Listing every plane by slope and area first, and reconciling the total against the building footprint and geometry, catches a missing or double-counted plane before it becomes a shortage or overage on delivery day.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every roofing quantity in this chapter follows the same conversion path: plan-view area becomes slope area, slope area becomes squares, squares become field product using manufacturer coverage, and waste is applied by cause before the quantity is converted into a supplier order unit. Accessories, flashing, ventilation, and fasteners are each measured and priced against their own schedule rather than folded into the field product line.

Slope Area, Not Footprint Area

Every product quantity in this chapter begins from actual slope area. A plane measured only in plan view and never converted to slope area will understate every downstream quantity, including underlayment, fasteners, and labor. Confirm the slope factor for every plane before entering a single square-foot value into the cost-code section.

Critical Control Cue — Convert Every Plane to Actual Slope Area

Do not calculate field roofing, underlayment, or fastener quantities from plan-view area. Apply the approved slope factor to every plane before calculating squares. A quantity built on footprint area alone must be corrected before it enters pricing, because the error compounds across field product, underlayment, and fasteners simultaneously.

Traceability Cue — Record the Source and Slope Basis for Every Plane

Record the drawing sheet, slope value, and calculation method for every roof plane the moment it is entered. Do not carry a plane forward with an assumed slope that is not confirmed by the section, elevation, or truss information.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Use the Confirmed Coverage Rate

Use the coverage rate stated in the current manufacturer data for the specified product, exposure, and application method. Do not carry forward a coverage rate from a different product line, a different exposure, or a prior project without confirming it against the current manufacturer data.

Documentation Cue — Separate Field Product From Starter, Ridge, and Valley

Enter field roofing, starter, ridge and hip cap, and valley material as separate line items with their own quantity, waste, and coverage basis. Do not combine them into a single blended quantity, since each has a different coverage rate and waste cause.

C. Roof-Plane and Slope Reference

  • Slope factor source: use the slope factor confirmed by the roof plan, elevation, or truss information for each plane; do not apply one slope factor to all planes without confirming each plane's pitch.
  • Irregular planes: calculate dormers, crickets, triangles, trapezoids, and irregular planes as their own line items using the geometry shown in the plan set.
  • Reconciliation requirement: compare total slope area against building footprint and roof geometry before proceeding to product calculations.
  • Non-substitution rule: do not substitute an assumed slope for a plane whose pitch is not confirmed by the documents; record it as an open clarification instead.

E. Preparation — Documents, Systems, and Plane Setup

  1. Review roof plans, elevations, sections, truss information, details, specifications, selections, and manufacturer instructions. Confirm the current revision against the Chapter 2 document register.
  2. Identify covering, slope limits, deck, underlayment, ice barrier, ventilation, flashing, fastening, and warranty system. Record each on the roofing tab's system summary.
  3. Separate roof planes by slope, product, color, exposure, and access. Assign each plane a unique identifier.
  4. Calculate actual roof-plane area from approved dimensions and slope. Apply the confirmed slope factor to each plane's plan-view area.
  5. Calculate dormers, crickets, triangles, trapezoids, and irregular planes separately. Do not fold irregular geometry into an adjacent plane's area.
  6. Reconcile total slope area with footprint and geometry. Compare the summed slope area against an independent footprint-based estimate and resolve any variance before continuing.

F. Product, Accessory, and Flashing Takeoff Procedure

  1. Calculate field roofing using actual product coverage. Divide total squares by the confirmed manufacturer coverage rate for the specified exposure.
  2. Apply waste based on hips, valleys, dormers, cuts, pattern, handling, and attic stock. Record the waste percentage and its cause separately from the coverage calculation.
  3. Calculate starter by eaves and rakes. Measure linear feet at each condition and convert using starter product coverage.
  4. Calculate ridge and hip cap by length and product coverage. Measure ridge and hip lines separately from field roofing.
  5. Calculate underlayment by area, laps, transitions, roll coverage, and waste. Include the full roof area plus required laps and transitions.
  6. Calculate self-adhered membrane by eaves, valleys, walls, penetrations, and details. Price membrane separately from standard underlayment.
  7. Measure drip edge separately at eaves and rakes. Record linear feet by condition and convert to stock length.
  8. Measure valleys by specified assembly. Confirm whether the valley is open metal, closed-cut, or woven, and price the specified assembly only.
  9. Count vents, skylights, chimneys, flues, curbs, fans, solar penetrations, and other openings. Record each by type, size, and location.
  10. Measure step, counter, apron, headwall, sidewall, cricket, and kickout flashing. Record each condition by linear feet or count and specified material.
  11. Calculate ventilation products from specified type and quantity without redesign. Use the ventilation quantity shown or specified in the documents; do not calculate a net-free-area design not provided by the specification.
  12. Count fasteners by product, deck, slope, wind, starter, ridge, and flashing schedule. Apply the manufacturer or specified fastening schedule for each product and wind zone.
  13. Include sealants, primers, adhesives, boots, collars, closures, and compatible metals. Confirm compatibility with the specified roofing and flashing system.
  14. Include deck-repair allowance only when supported by scope. Do not include a deck-repair quantity without a documented basis from Chapter 2 or a field report.
  15. Include tear-off, disposal, dumpsters, protection, and magnetic cleanup for reroofing. Confirm whether the project is new roofing or reroofing before pricing this line.
  16. Include scaffolding, lifts, roof jacks, fall protection, loading, hoisting, tarping, and weather controls. Base access equipment on plane height, pitch, and site access.

G. Labor, Pricing, and Compatibility Review

  1. Estimate labor by tear-off, preparation, membrane, underlayment, roofing, flashing, ridge, ventilation, and cleanup. Assign a production rate and crew to each task.
  2. Adjust production for slope, height, complexity, temperature, wind, occupancy, and material. Record the adjustment basis for each affected task.
  3. Price products from dated quotations confirming system, coverage, warranty, freight, and lead time. Record supplier, quote number, date, and expiration for every priced line.
  4. Include rooftop loading, crane or conveyor, minimum order, and return restrictions. Confirm delivery method and any related charges.
  5. Include permits, inspection, warranty registration, and manufacturer inspection where required. Confirm requirements against the specified warranty system.
  6. Check compatibility among roofing, underlayment, flashing, fasteners, sealants, and treated materials. Confirm no incompatible product combination is priced into the worksheet.
  7. Prepare the worksheet by plane, product, quantity, waste, labor, equipment, and total. Organize the roofing cost-code section so each plane and product line is independently reviewable.
  8. Document deck, weather, ventilation, existing-layer, and access assumptions. Record each assumption in the workbook's assumptions and risk register, linked to the affected line.
  9. Complete independent review of slope, valleys, penetrations, flashing, and waste. Have a second reviewer or an independent method confirm the highest-value and highest-risk quantities.

Common Roofing Takeoff Inspection

Inspect the finished roofing cost-code section using the same checks regardless of which plane or product line is being reviewed.

  • Slope check: confirm every plane's actual slope area is calculated using a confirmed slope factor, not an assumed value.
  • Reconciliation check: confirm total slope area is reconciled against footprint and roof geometry with the variance documented.
  • Coverage check: confirm field roofing, starter, ridge cap, underlayment, and membrane each use the confirmed manufacturer coverage rate for the specified product.
  • Flashing check: confirm every flashing condition shown or specified in the documents has a corresponding line item.
  • Compatibility check: confirm no incompatible roofing, underlayment, flashing, or fastener combination is priced.
  • Pricing check: confirm every unit cost carries a supplier, quote number, date, and expiration.

Complete the Roofing Takeoff Verification Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 08.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Total Roofing Squares Do Not Match the Footprint-Based Check

Probable cause: a plane's slope factor was applied incorrectly, an irregular plane was omitted or double-counted, or the footprint-based check used the wrong building outline.
Confirmation: compare each plane's recorded slope factor and area against the roof plan and re-total the plane schedule.
Correction: correct the affected plane's slope factor or area and re-run the reconciliation.
Reinspection: repeat the total-square reconciliation and confirm the variance is resolved or documented.

Field Roofing Quantity Appears Short at Delivery

Probable cause: the coverage rate used does not match the specified product's actual manufacturer coverage, or waste was not applied to the correct cause.
Confirmation: compare the coverage rate entered in the workbook against the current manufacturer data sheet for the specified product and exposure.
Correction: correct the coverage rate and re-calculate the field roofing quantity and waste.
Reinspection: repeat the coverage check against the corrected manufacturer data.

A Flashing Condition Shown in the Details Has No Priced Line Item

Probable cause: the penetration and flashing log was built from the elevations only and did not cross-reference the roof and wall-section details.
Confirmation: compare the flashing log against every roof detail, section, and elevation in the document set.
Correction: add the missing flashing condition to the log with its linear feet or count and specified material.
Reinspection: repeat the flashing check against the full document set.

Underlayment Quantity Does Not Account for Laps and Membrane Overlap

Probable cause: underlayment area was calculated as net roof area only, without adding lap and transition allowances.
Confirmation: compare the entered underlayment area against net roof area plus the required lap and transition allowance.
Correction: recalculate underlayment area including laps, transitions, and roll coverage, then reapply waste.
Reinspection: repeat the underlayment quantity check against the corrected calculation.

A Specified Roofing Product Is Priced With an Incompatible Fastener or Underlayment

Probable cause: the fastener or underlayment line was carried forward from a prior project or a default assumption without checking manufacturer compatibility for the specified system.
Confirmation: compare the fastener and underlayment product against the manufacturer's compatibility requirements for the specified field roofing and warranty system.
Correction: replace the incompatible product with the manufacturer-approved or specified equivalent and re-price the affected line.
Reinspection: repeat the compatibility check across roofing, underlayment, flashing, fasteners, and sealants.

A Priced Line Has No Dated Source

Probable cause: a unit cost was entered from memory, a prior project, or an unconfirmed catalog price without a current supplier quotation.
Confirmation: the error-check sheet's missing-source flag identifies the affected row.
Correction: obtain a dated supplier quotation for the item and record supplier, quote number, date, and expiration.
Reinspection: repeat the missing-source check on the corrected row.

Field Terms

Square

Field Roofing
The primary roofing product covering the general roof-plane area, exclusive of starter, ridge, hip, and valley material.
Exposure
The portion of each course of roofing left visible to the weather, controlling coverage per unit.
Attic Stock
Extra field roofing material retained for future repair, calculated separately from installation waste.
Self-Adhered Membrane
Waterproofing underlayment applied at eaves, valleys, and specified details, priced separately from standard underlayment.
Kickout Flashing
Flashing that diverts roof runoff away from a wall at the base of a roof-to-wall intersection.
Net-Free Area
The unobstructed ventilation opening area of a specified ventilation product, used only from a supplied specification, not calculated from scratch in this chapter.
Tear-Off
Removal of existing roofing material before installation of a new roofing system.
Warranty Registration
The manufacturer process confirming an installed roofing system qualifies for its stated warranty.
