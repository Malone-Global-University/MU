Job Overview

This project qualifies you to convert the Project 08 capstone specification into a complete, reconciled set of planning documents: a component takeoff, a stock-conversion worksheet, a lumber cut plan, a blocking cut plan, a plywood panel plan, an aggregate calculation, a connector and fastener schedule, a waste and overage calculation, a final purchase list, a cost worksheet where pricing is assigned, and an assumptions and exclusions record.

The finished work product is not a demonstration exercise. It is the controlling estimate that Project 08 must follow. No capstone material may be purchased or cut from an estimate that has not passed the reconciliation checklist in this project.

This is the seventh project in the book. It uses the accurate measuring and marking system from Project 03, the fastener and hardware identification skills from Project 04, and the approved footprint dimensions and skid-line locations recorded in Project 06. Every quantity calculated here is checked, in Project 08, against the material actually delivered and installed.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CF-037 — Extract every component, dimension, material type, and installation requirement from the Project 08 specification.
  • CF-038 — Calculate lumber, panel, gravel, fastener, connector, and consumable quantities.
  • CF-039 — Convert finished component dimensions into purchasable stock lengths and sheet quantities.
  • CF-040 — Prepare an optimized cut plan that accounts for saw kerf, defects, orientation, and usable offcuts.
  • CF-041 — Apply the stated waste and overage rules without inflating the purchase list unnecessarily.
  • CF-042 — Reconcile the final takeoff, purchase list, cut plan, hardware schedule, exclusions, and cost record.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 03 qualified you to measure, mark, and cut lumber to a controlled tolerance. This project applies that same reference discipline to paper and calculator work: every stock conversion, cut plan, and quantity calculated here must be traceable back to a specific finished component in the Project 08 specification, using the exact reference conventions taught in Project 02 and Project 03.

The documents produced in this project become the controlling work documents for Project 08. The learner in Project 08 does not re-derive quantities from the specification; the learner follows the cut plan, purchase list, and hardware schedule approved here.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reading the complete Project 08 specification and building a component list organized by skids, frame, blocking, deck, connectors, fasteners, aggregate, consumables, and closeout materials
  • converting every finished component dimension into a purchasable stock size
  • preparing the lumber cut plan, blocking cut plan, and three-sheet panel plan
  • calculating aggregate volume and converting it to the supplier's purchase unit
  • calculating hardware, connector, and fastener quantities from the identified connector instructions and the stated fastening schedules
  • applying the stated 10 percent fastener overage and aggregate allowance, and applying lumber waste only where defects, selection requirements, or the cut plan justify it
  • reconciling every purchase item to a component and every component to a purchased item before the estimate is approved

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • purchase, deliver, or cut any capstone material from an unreconciled or unapproved takeoff
  • substitute a different connector, fastener, panel thickness, or lumber treatment level than the one identified in the Project 08 specification
  • invent a load rating, structural capacity, or code approval for the platform based on the quantities calculated here
  • skip the reconciliation checklist because the individual worksheets appear complete

Any component, dimension, or fastener requirement that cannot be resolved from the Project 08 specification, the identified connector instructions, or confirmed site information must be recorded as an open item and resolved before Project 08 site work begins.

Trade Note

Estimating errors are cheaper to correct on paper than in the field. A missed joist hanger discovered during Project 07 costs a phone call. The same missed joist hanger discovered mid-assembly in Project 08 costs a delayed schedule and an incomplete connection. Treat every quantity in this project with the same care used when cutting a component to a fraction of an inch.

Measurement Cue — Finished Length vs. Stock Length

Never confuse a finished component length with the purchasable stock length. A 117-inch joist requires a 120-inch (10-foot) stock board; the 3-inch difference is offcut, not error. Confirm every stock conversion in this project produces a stock length that is equal to or greater than the finished length, never shorter.

Critical Safety Cue — No Estimate, No Purchase, No Cut

Do not authorize the purchase or cutting of any Project 08 material from an estimate that has not completed the reconciliation checklist in this project. An unreconciled estimate may omit a required connector, fastener, or blocking piece that is needed for a safe, complete installation.

Manufacturer Cue — Connector Fastener Counts

Obtain the exact fastener count and approved fastener type for each joist hanger and each frame-to-skid connector from the identified connector's manufacturer instructions. Do not estimate a hole count or substitute a generic fastener quantity.

Code Alert — Site-Specific Requirements

This takeoff covers the base platform only. Keep site-specific anchorage, frost-depth, drainage, and foundation requirements separate from the base takeoff, and record them as unresolved items requiring local verification.

Estimating and Planning Procedure

Capstone Material Takeoff, Cut Plan, and Purchase List

Difficulty: Intermediate

Estimated Time: 3–4 hours

Estimated Cost: No purchase in this project

Finished Work: Approved takeoff, purchase list, cut plans, hardware schedule, and cost worksheet controlling Project 08

A. Finished-Deliverable Specification

Scope: one complete, reconciled set of planning documents for the Project 08 garden storage shed floor platform, consisting of a component takeoff worksheet, a stock-conversion worksheet, a lumber cut plan, a blocking cut plan, a plywood panel plan, an aggregate calculation, a connector and fastener schedule, a waste and overage calculation, a final purchase list, a cost worksheet where pricing is assigned, an assumptions and exclusions record, and a reconciliation checklist. This project does not include purchasing, delivering, or cutting any material.

B. Lumber Calculations

Complete the following before preparing the cut plans:

  1. Distinguish finished length from purchasable stock length for every lumber component. Record both values side by side on the stock-conversion worksheet.
  2. Show that each 117-inch joist requires one 120-inch stock board. Confirm this conversion for all seven longitudinal joists.
  3. Identify the seven 3-inch joist offcuts and state whether they have a valid project use. Record the disposition of each offcut on the cut-plan worksheet.
  4. Show that two 96-inch boards are reserved for the end rims. Confirm the two end rim joists are cut from full-length stock with no splice.
  5. Calculate the blocking total of 171 linear inches. Four pieces at 13 3/4 inches plus eight pieces at 14 1/2 inches; show the arithmetic on the worksheet.
  6. Confirm that two 96-inch boards provide 192 linear inches before kerf and defect allowance. Compare this figure to the 171-inch blocking requirement and record the margin.
  7. Prepare the exact block-cut sequence for four 13 3/4-inch pieces and eight 14 1/2-inch pieces. Diagram the sequence across the two 96-inch boards, including kerf allowance between each cut.

C. Panel Calculations

  1. Orient the 96-inch panel strength direction across the 8-foot platform width, perpendicular to the longitudinal joists. Record this orientation on the panel-plan diagram.
  2. Assign two full panels to the first 96 inches of platform length. Label each panel's position on the panel-plan diagram.
  3. Rip one 24-by-96-inch strip from the third panel for the final 24 inches of platform length. Record the rip dimension and the blade-side allowance.
  4. Identify the remaining 24-by-96-inch offcut from the third panel. Record its disposition; do not discard a usable offcut without recording that decision.
  5. Maintain the required panel gaps in the finished layout. Show the 1/8-inch gap at every panel edge and joint on the panel-plan diagram.
  6. Confirm that both panel end joints occur over the blocking rows at 48 and 96 inches. Compare the panel-plan diagram directly against the framing plan.

D. Aggregate Calculation

Each gravel strip is 12 inches wide, 8 feet long, and 4 inches compacted depth.

  1. Calculate the volume of one gravel strip. One strip is approximately 2.67 cu. ft. Show the width, length, and depth conversion used to reach this figure.
  2. Calculate the volume of all three strips. Three strips total approximately 8 cu. ft.
  3. Apply the 15 percent compaction and handling allowance. The allowance brings the total to approximately 9.2 cu. ft.
  4. Convert the allowance-adjusted volume into the supplier's purchase unit. Round to an available quantity such as 0.4 or 0.5 cu. yd. and record the selected purchase quantity.

E. Hardware and Fastener Calculations

  1. Count ten joist hangers. Confirm this matches both ends of the five interior joists in the Project 08 component schedule.
  2. Count nine frame-to-skid connectors. Confirm this matches the two outside side joists and the center joist over each of the three skids.
  3. Obtain the exact manufacturer hole count and approved fastener schedule for each connector. Record the fastener type, size, and quantity per connector from the identified manufacturer instructions.
  4. Calculate the twelve 5-inch corner-joint structural screws before overage. Three screws at each of the four perimeter corners.
  5. Calculate the forty-eight 3-inch blocking screws before overage. Two screws through each joist into each end of twelve blocks.
  6. Calculate the floor-deck screws from the 6-inch edge and 12-inch field schedule. Document the layout count on the deck fastener worksheet using the panel-plan dimensions, and purchase at least 200 screws or the next available package size.
  7. Add the specified 10 percent fastener overage to the base counts. Apply the overage separately to each fastener category and record the adjusted purchase quantity.
  8. Keep site-specific anchorage separate from the base takeoff. Record any required anchorage fasteners as a separate, unresolved line item pending local design confirmation.

F. Sequence and Reconciliation

  1. Read the complete Project 08 specification without beginning calculations. Confirm you have reviewed every phase of the Project 08 sequence before starting the takeoff.
  2. Highlight every finished dimension, material type, exposure requirement, hardware requirement, and site-control condition. Mark each item directly on a working copy of the specification.
  3. Create a component list organized by skids, frame, blocking, deck, connectors, fasteners, aggregate, consumables, and closeout materials. Use these nine categories consistently across every worksheet.
  4. Enter the finished quantity and size of every component. Cross-check each entry against the Project 08 component schedule.
  5. Convert each component into a purchasable stock size. Complete the stock-conversion worksheet described in Section B.
  6. Prepare the seven-board 2×6-by-10-foot joist cut plan. One 120-inch board per 117-inch joist, with the 3-inch offcut recorded.
  7. Reserve two 2×6-by-8-foot boards for the end rims. Confirm no other component is cut from these two boards.
  8. Prepare the two-board blocking cut plan and include saw kerf between pieces. Use the sequence developed in Section B, Step 7.
  9. Prepare the three-sheet panel plan and label the usable offcut. Use the layout developed in Section C.
  10. Calculate aggregate volume and convert it to the supplier purchase unit. Use the calculation developed in Section D.
  11. Count hangers and skid connectors. Confirm the counts developed in Section E, Steps 1–2.
  12. Obtain the exact approved fastener count from the identified connector instructions. Confirm the count developed in Section E, Step 3.
  13. Calculate frame, blocking, and deck fasteners from the supplied schedules. Confirm the counts developed in Section E, Steps 4–6.
  14. Apply 10 percent fastener overage and the stated aggregate allowance. Confirm both adjustments are recorded on their respective worksheets.
  15. Apply lumber waste only where defects, selection requirements, or the cut plan justify it. Do not add a blanket lumber-waste percentage without a stated justification recorded on the worksheet.
  16. Do not add an extra full plywood sheet automatically. The planned third-sheet offcut already provides substantial contingency material; record this decision explicitly.
  17. Separate base-project materials from site-specific permit, anchorage, drainage, frost, and foundation requirements. Record these items on the assumptions and exclusions worksheet.
  18. Add unit prices and calculate the planned project cost where pricing is assigned. Complete the cost worksheet using locally confirmed or assigned unit prices.
  19. Reconcile every purchase item to a component, step, or controlled contingency. Check off each purchase-list line against its source.
  20. Reconcile every component to a purchased item. Check off each Project 08 component schedule line against the purchase list.
  21. Record assumptions, excluded work, unresolved site requirements, and items requiring manufacturer confirmation. Complete the assumptions and exclusions worksheet in full.
  22. Submit the takeoff, purchase list, cut plan, panel plan, hardware schedule, and cost worksheet for approval. Do not release any document to Project 08 until the reconciliation checklist shows no missing or unexplained item.

Common Installation Inspection

Inspect the completed estimate using the same checks regardless of the order in which the categories were calculated.

  • Component check: confirm every Project 08 component appears in the takeoff with the correct quantity, material, and finished size.
  • Stock-sufficiency check: confirm stock lengths and sheet quantities are sufficient for the controlled cut plan with no shortage.
  • Dimension check: confirm no finished dimension has been confused with a nominal material size.
  • Panel-orientation check: confirm panel orientation and seam locations match the Project 08 framing plan.
  • Aggregate check: confirm the aggregate calculation is dimensionally correct and converted to the correct purchase unit.
  • Connector-fastener check: confirm connector fasteners match the identified manufacturer schedule exactly.
  • Waste and overage check: confirm waste and overage are applied consistently and only where justified.
  • Duplicate check: confirm the final list contains no unexplained duplicate or speculative purchase.

Complete the Reconciliation Checklist in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the estimate for Project 08.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Component Is Missing from the Purchase List

Probable cause: the component list was built from memory instead of directly from the Project 08 specification, or a category such as blocking or connectors was skipped during extraction.
Confirmation: compare the Project 08 component schedule line by line against the takeoff worksheet.
Correction: add the missing component to the takeoff, stock-conversion worksheet, cut plan or panel plan as applicable, and purchase list.
Reinspection: repeat the full reconciliation checklist.

A Finished Length Was Treated as a Stock Length

Probable cause: the stock-conversion step was skipped and a finished dimension such as 117 inches was recorded directly as the purchase length.
Confirmation: review the stock-conversion worksheet and confirm every purchase length equals or exceeds its matching finished length.
Correction: correct the stock length on the purchase list and rebuild the affected cut plan.
Reinspection: repeat the stock-sufficiency check for the affected material.

Connector Fastener Counts Do Not Match the Manufacturer Schedule

Probable cause: a generic or estimated fastener count was used instead of the exact count from the identified connector's manufacturer instructions.
Confirmation: compare the fastener schedule worksheet against the manufacturer instructions for the exact connector identified in the Project 08 specification.
Correction: correct the fastener type, size, and quantity per connector, and recalculate the total fastener purchase quantity.
Reinspection: repeat the connector-fastener check across the entire hardware schedule.

The Panel Plan Does Not Align Panel Joints with the Blocking Rows

Probable cause: the panel plan was drawn without cross-checking it against the framing plan's blocking-row locations at 48 and 96 inches.
Confirmation: overlay the panel-plan diagram on the framing plan and confirm both panel end joints fall directly over a blocking row.
Correction: adjust the panel layout or confirm the blocking-row locations, correcting whichever document is inconsistent with the Project 08 specification.
Reinspection: repeat the panel-orientation check.

The Aggregate Purchase Quantity Does Not Match the Calculated Volume

Probable cause: a unit-conversion error occurred when converting cubic feet to the supplier's cubic-yard purchase unit, or the compaction allowance was omitted.
Confirmation: recheck the strip-volume, three-strip total, and 15 percent allowance calculations, then recheck the cubic-foot-to-cubic-yard conversion.
Correction: correct the calculation and update the purchase quantity to the nearest available supplier unit.
Reinspection: repeat the aggregate check.

The Purchase List Contains an Unexplained Duplicate or Speculative Item

Probable cause: a component was entered on the takeoff more than once, or an extra unit was added without a stated waste, defect, or contingency justification.
Confirmation: review the reconciliation checklist and confirm every purchase-list line traces to exactly one component, step, or documented contingency.
Correction: remove the duplicate or unjustified item, or document the specific justification if the item is legitimately required.
Reinspection: repeat the duplicate check across the full purchase list.
