Job Overview

This project qualifies you to establish the exact ground layout of the 8-foot by 10-foot garden storage shed floor platform built in Project 08. The finished work product is a squared, leveled, and fully dimensioned string-line footprint with batter boards set outside the excavation and work zones, plus the three skid-support centerlines and their gravel-strip limits marked on the ground.

You will establish a benchmark, install batter boards, run and tension string lines to the specified 8-foot by 10-foot rectangle, square the footprint using a 6-8-10 triangle and equal-diagonal check, mark the three skid centerlines and their 12-inch-wide gravel strips, and record every measurement on the layout worksheet.

This is the sixth project in the book. It uses the squares, levels, reference-line, and verification methods from Project 02, the accurate measuring and marking system from Project 03, and the sawhorses built in Project 05 to support materials during layout. The completed, approved layout becomes the controlling field reference for the material takeoff in Project 07 and the capstone construction in Project 08.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CF-031 — Establish the capstone baseline, benchmark, orientation, and controlled corner reference.
  • CF-032 — Install batter boards outside the work footprint and set their reference elevation.
  • CF-033 — Run, tension, and adjust string lines to the specified 8-foot by 10-foot dimensions.
  • CF-034 — Square the footprint using a 6-8-10 right-angle check and equal-diagonal verification.
  • CF-035 — Lay out the three skid-support centerlines and associated gravel-strip limits.
  • CF-036 — Record side lengths, diagonals, elevations, offsets, and final layout approval.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 02 qualified you to establish reference faces, edges, and benchmarks and to verify level, plumb, and square with hand tools. This project moves that same verification discipline from a tabletop layout board to a full-scale outdoor footprint, using string lines, batter boards, and a 6-8-10 triangle instead of a framing square.

The footprint you establish here is not a demonstration. It is the exact controlling layout used to build the Project 08 capstone. Project 07 will use the recorded skid-line locations and platform dimensions to calculate materials, and Project 08 will excavate, place aggregate, and build the frame directly on the lines and marks you establish in this project.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming site permission, boundaries, setbacks, utility-locate requirements, and drainage direction before driving any stake
  • establishing a benchmark, baseline, and controlled corner reference for the 8-foot by 10-foot footprint
  • installing batter boards outside the footprint and work zones and setting their reference elevation
  • running, tensioning, and squaring string lines to the finished platform dimensions
  • laying out the three skid-support centerlines and their 12-inch-wide gravel-strip limits
  • recording every measured dimension, diagonal, elevation, and offset, and obtaining layout approval before Project 08 site work begins

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin excavation, grading, utility disturbance, foundation work, or permanent construction
  • proceed without confirming site permission, property boundaries, setbacks, easements, utility-locate requirements, drainage direction, and access restrictions
  • relocate, resize, or reorient the footprint without correcting the controlling layout record first
  • treat an unequal-diagonal or out-of-tolerance layout as acceptable

Any measurement, string-line intersection, or benchmark that fails inspection must be corrected before the layout is approved for Project 08.

Trade Note

This project produces string lines and ground marks, not a permanent structure. Even so, treat every measurement with the same discipline used on the Project 02 qualification board. A footprint that is out of square or off its diagonal tolerance will carry that error into every remaining step of the capstone, including the skid placement, frame assembly, and deck fastening in Project 08.

Common Field Fundamentals

A building footprint is only as reliable as its weakest measurement. Every dimension in this project is taken from the same benchmark and baseline, every corner is checked with both the 6-8-10 method and the equal-diagonal method, and every skid centerline is measured from the same 10-foot end baseline. Losing the benchmark, skipping a diagonal check, or measuring a skid line from an inconsistent reference produces a footprint that must be torn down and relaid before Project 08 can begin.

Controlled Layout Dimensions

  • Finished platform width: 8 ft. or 96 in.
  • Finished platform length: 10 ft. or 120 in.
  • Theoretical corner-to-corner diagonal: approximately 12 ft. 9 11/16 in.
  • Required final condition: both diagonals equal within 1/8 in.
  • Batter-board setback: minimum 24 in. outside the platform corners
  • Work-zone offset: 18 in. outside the finished platform lines
  • Skid-support centerlines measured from the 10-foot end baseline: 12 in., 60 in., and 108 in.
  • Gravel-strip width: 12 in. centered on each skid line

Measurement Cue — One Benchmark and Baseline

Establish the benchmark and the 10-foot baseline once, at the start of the project, and measure every subsequent dimension, elevation, and skid centerline from those same two references. Re-establishing the baseline partway through the layout is the most common cause of an out-of-square or mismatched footprint.

Critical Safety Cue — Utility and Site Verification

Do not drive a stake, install a batter board, or mark a line until site permission, property boundaries, setbacks, easements, utility-locate requirements, drainage direction, and access restrictions have been confirmed. Reject the location and stop work if this information is unavailable or if the proposed footprint conflicts with utilities, access, drainage, or local restrictions.

Field Safety Cue — Stake and String Hazards

Keep the work area free of tripping hazards created by driven stakes, taut string lines, and batter-board rails. Mark or flag string lines that cross a walking route, and keep the material and access route established in Project 01 clear throughout the layout.

Code Alert — Local Requirements Vary

Permit requirements, setback distances, and utility-locate procedures vary by jurisdiction and property. Verify the applicable local requirements before finalizing the footprint location, and do not rely on this chapter as a substitute for that verification.

E. Preconstruction Verification

  1. Confirm site permission, property boundaries, setbacks, easements, utility-locate requirements, drainage direction, and access restrictions before driving stakes. Record each confirmation on the layout worksheet.
  2. Reject the location until every required confirmation is complete. Stop work if the proposed footprint conflicts with utilities, access, drainage, or local restrictions.
  3. Select the 10-foot baseline and identify its relationship to the site, access route, and drainage direction. Confirm the baseline orientation matches the site sketch before marking it.
  4. Establish and label one benchmark that will remain undisturbed throughout Projects 06 and 08. Choose a fixed, protected point unlikely to be disturbed by later excavation or material staging.
  5. Mark the approximate four platform corners. Use temporary stakes or flags at this stage; these are not the final corner points.

F. Batter Boards and Reference Elevation

  1. Install two batter-board stakes at least 24 inches outside each corner and outside the future excavation and work zones. Drive each stake pair so the connecting rail can be fastened square to the corner it protects.
  2. Fasten the batter-board rails and set their top edges to a common reference elevation. Use the line level to transfer the elevation from the benchmark to each batter-board pair.
  3. Run and tension the first 10-foot baseline string. Attach the string to the batter boards at the two corners defining the selected baseline and pull it taut.
  4. Establish a perpendicular line at the first corner using a 6-8-10 triangle. Measure 6 feet along the baseline and 8 feet along the proposed perpendicular side; adjust the perpendicular string until the diagonal between those two points measures exactly 10 feet.

G. String Lines and Squaring

  1. Run the first 8-foot side string. Attach it to the batter boards at the corner established by the 6-8-10 check and pull it taut.
  2. Run the opposite 10-foot and 8-foot strings to form the rectangle. Attach all four strings to their batter boards so the four sides form a closed rectangle.
  3. Use a plumb bob or controlled intersection method to identify the exact four corners. Mark each corner point on the ground directly beneath its string intersection.
  4. Measure both diagonals and adjust the string intersections until they are equal within 1/8 inch. Use both tape measures at once to compare the two diagonals directly.
  5. Recheck both 10-foot sides and both 8-foot sides after every diagonal adjustment. Confirm no side has shifted outside its ±1/8 inch tolerance.
  6. Compare the measured diagonal to the approximate theoretical diagonal as an error check. A large deviation from 12 ft. 9 11/16 in. indicates a side-length or squaring error that must be corrected before continuing.
  7. Verify the string elevations against the benchmark and document any intended grade or slope requirement. Record the elevation at each corner on the layout worksheet.

H. Skid Centerlines and Gravel Strips

  1. Measure from the selected 10-foot end baseline and mark skid centerlines at 12, 60, and 108 inches. Use the same reference end for all three measurements.
  2. Extend each skid centerline across the full 8-foot width. Mark each centerline at both the near and far side strings.
  3. Mark a 6-inch offset on each side of every skid centerline to establish the three 12-inch-wide gravel strips. Confirm each strip is centered on its skid line before marking.
  4. Mark the 18-inch work-zone offset outside the completed platform rectangle. Use this mark to keep stored material and equipment clear of the footprint.
  5. Confirm that the batter boards remain outside all excavation, support-strip, and work-zone limits. Adjust or re-stake any batter board that intrudes on these limits.

I. Recording and Approval

  1. Record final side lengths, diagonal lengths, benchmark elevation, skid-line locations, strip widths, and work-zone offsets. Enter every value on the layout worksheet.
  2. Photograph or diagram the completed layout from at least two directions. Include the benchmark, all four corners, and the skid centerlines in the documentation.
  3. Protect the layout for Project 08 or remove it according to the site-control plan. If the layout will remain in place, flag or otherwise protect the strings and stakes from disturbance.
  4. Complete and sign or otherwise approve the layout worksheet before Project 08 site work begins. Do not begin excavation or construction on an unapproved layout.

Common Installation Inspection

Inspect the entire footprint using the same checks regardless of the order in which the corners were squared.

  • Side-length check: confirm both 10-foot sides and both 8-foot sides are within ±1/8 in. of their specified dimensions.
  • Diagonal check: confirm both diagonals are equal within 1/8 in. of each other.
  • Benchmark and baseline check: confirm the benchmark and baseline are clearly labeled and undisturbed.
  • String-line check: confirm all four strings remain tight with stable intersections.
  • Batter-board check: confirm all batter boards remain outside the controlled work and excavation limits.
  • Skid-line check: confirm skid centerlines fall at 12, 60, and 108 inches within ±1/8 in., with gravel-strip limits 6 inches from each centerline.

Complete the Layout Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing the footprint for Project 08 site work.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Two Diagonals Do Not Match

Probable cause: one corner intersection shifted during string tensioning, or a side length is out of tolerance.
Confirmation: recheck all four side lengths against the 96-inch and 120-inch targets, then remeasure both diagonals with two tape measures at once.
Correction: adjust the string at the corner producing the largest diagonal error, holding the baseline and its adjacent corner fixed.
Reinspection: repeat the side-length and diagonal checks until both diagonals are equal within 1/8 inch.

The Measured Diagonal Does Not Match the Theoretical Diagonal

Probable cause: a side length was measured from the wrong reference point, or the 6-8-10 check was not verified before the first side string was run.
Confirmation: compare the measured diagonal to the approximate 12 ft. 9 11/16 in. theoretical value and recheck each side length against its baseline reference.
Correction: re-run the 6-8-10 check at the affected corner and reset the string before rechecking the diagonal.
Reinspection: repeat the full diagonal and side-length check sequence.

A Batter Board Intrudes on the Excavation or Work-Zone Limits

Probable cause: the batter-board stakes were installed less than 24 inches from the corner, or the work-zone offset was not marked before the batter boards were placed.
Confirmation: measure the distance from each batter board to its nearest platform corner and compare it to the 24-inch minimum setback.
Correction: relocate the affected batter-board stakes and re-tension the connected string lines.
Reinspection: repeat the batter-board setback check and recheck the affected side length and diagonal.

A Skid Centerline Is Out of Tolerance

Probable cause: the skid centerline was measured from the wrong end of the baseline, or the reference end was lost partway through marking the three centerlines.
Confirmation: remeasure all three skid centerlines from the same confirmed reference end and compare each to its 12-, 60-, or 108-inch target.
Correction: remark the affected centerline from the confirmed reference end and re-extend it across the full 8-foot width.
Reinspection: repeat the skid-centerline check for all three lines.

The String-Line Elevation Does Not Match the Benchmark

Probable cause: a batter-board rail was fastened before its elevation was checked with the line level, or the benchmark was disturbed during stake driving.
Confirmation: check the benchmark for disturbance and recheck each batter-board rail elevation with the line level against the benchmark.
Correction: reset the affected batter-board rail to the correct elevation, or reestablish the benchmark if it has been disturbed and recheck every batter board against the new reading.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation check at all four corners.

Field Terms

Baseline
The selected 10-foot side of the footprint from which the perpendicular side and all subsequent layout are established.
Benchmark
A single labeled elevation reference point kept undisturbed throughout the layout and capstone construction.
Batter Board
A horizontal rail fastened across two stakes outside a footprint corner, holding string lines at a fixed reference elevation and position.
6-8-10 Triangle
A right-triangle check for a 90-degree corner using leg lengths of 6 feet and 8 feet and a diagonal of exactly 10 feet.
Equal-Diagonal Check
A squaring method comparing the two corner-to-corner diagonals of a rectangle; the rectangle is square when both diagonals are equal.
Skid Centerline
One of three lines marking the center of each skid-support gravel strip, measured from the 10-foot end baseline.
Gravel-Strip Limit
The 6-inch offset marked on each side of a skid centerline, establishing the 12-inch-wide support-strip boundary.
Work-Zone Offset
An 18-inch clearance maintained outside the finished platform lines, kept free of batter boards and stored material.
Line Level
A small level hung on a tensioned string, used to check the string's elevation along its length.
Layout Worksheet
The document recording benchmark elevation, side lengths, diagonals, skid-line locations, and final layout approval.
