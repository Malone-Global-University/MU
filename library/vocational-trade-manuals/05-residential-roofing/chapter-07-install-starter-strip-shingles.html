Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install the approved starter-strip system that seals and supports the first course of field shingles. The finished work product is a straight, securely fastened starter course along every required eave and, where the approved system calls for it, along every required rake — installed with the correct overhang, adhesive orientation, joint offset from the first field course, and corner transitions, and inspected before the first field course begins.

You will confirm the starter product's compatibility with the field shingles, stage and prepare starter material, mark the required overhang from the drip-edge reference, cut the first section to create the required joint offset, align and fasten successive sections, form eave-to-rake corner transitions, and document the completed starter course.

This chapter follows Chapter 6, which established the roofing layout control grid, and precedes Chapter 8, where the first field course is installed directly onto the starter strip you install here. No field shingle may be installed until the starter course beneath it has passed inspection.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-037 — Select a starter-strip product compatible with the approved field shingles and roof edges being covered.
  • RF-038 — Align the starter course to the drip-edge reference and required overhang.
  • RF-039 — Offset starter joints from the joints of the first field-shingle course by the required distance.
  • RF-040 — Fasten starter shingles using the specified fastener type, quantity, location, and seating condition.
  • RF-041 — Maintain the required starter overhang at eaves and rakes through corner transitions.
  • RF-042 — Inspect the completed starter course for overhang, alignment, fastening, adhesive orientation, joint offset, and corner condition.

Where This Skill Fits

The starter course is the last water-control layer installed before the visible roof-covering field begins, and the first layer that the field shingles depend on for wind resistance along the roof edges. A starter course installed without the manufacturer-required adhesive orientation, overhang, or joint offset compromises the wind-warranty performance of every course installed above it in Chapter 8.

This chapter depends directly on the roof-edge metal from Chapter 3, the underlayment from Chapter 5, and the layout control grid from Chapter 6. It in turn establishes the edge that Chapter 8's first field-shingle course is measured and fastened against.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the roofing-system instructions for starter product, adhesive location, overhang, fastening, joint offset, eave use, rake use, and wind-warranty requirements
  • confirming that roof-edge metal, underlayment, and layout lines are complete before starter work begins
  • verifying starter-product compatibility with the selected field shingles and the roof edges being covered
  • staging, marking, cutting, aligning, and fastening starter strips at eaves and, where required, at rakes
  • forming eave-to-rake corner transitions without doubled humps, gaps, or exposed adhesive
  • inspecting and correcting the completed starter course before the first field course begins

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • install any field shingle course; that work begins in Chapter 8, only after the starter course in this chapter has passed inspection
  • alter the roof-edge metal installed in Chapter 3 or the underlayment installed in Chapter 5, beyond the surface contact required to fasten the starter strip
  • substitute a starter product, adhesive orientation, or fastening schedule not identified as compatible by the roofing-system instructions
  • proceed with starter installation over an eave or rake that has not passed the applicable Chapter 3 and Chapter 5 inspections

Any eave or rake section that fails this chapter's inspection must be corrected under this chapter's procedure, or the affected section must be held open, before it receives a field-shingle course.

Trade Note

Every finding in this chapter is recorded against a fixed reference: the roofing-system instructions and the drip-edge line established in Chapter 3. Do not evaluate starter overhang or joint offset by eye. If the product instructions and the field condition disagree, record the conflict and escalate it rather than resolving it by assumption.

Shop Tip

Dry-lay the first few starter sections along the eave without fastening them before cutting the offset piece. Confirming the overhang and joint spacing on unfastened material is far faster than pulling and replacing a fastened section.

Common Field Fundamentals

The starter course exists to do two things the field shingles cannot do for themselves at the roof edge: seal the underside of the first course tabs against wind lift, and support the exposed cutouts of the first course over a continuous surface rather than a bare edge. Every decision in this chapter — overhang, adhesive orientation, joint offset, and corner treatment — serves one or both of those purposes.

Overhang, Adhesive, and Joint Offset

Mark the required starter overhang from the drip-edge reference before positioning any section. Confirm the adhesive strip's position relative to the roof edge against the manufacturer instructions before fastening the first section, since this position differs among products. Cut the first starter section to create the required joint offset from the first field-shingle course before installing any full section.

Manufacturer Cue — Starter Product Compatibility

Verify that the selected starter product is identified by the roofing-system instructions as compatible with the field shingles and approved for the specific roof edges being covered before staging any material. Do not substitute a starter product not identified in those instructions.

Measurement Cue — Overhang and Joint Offset

Mark the required starter overhang from the drip-edge reference established in Chapter 3, not from the edge of the underlayment or the fascia. Measure the required joint-offset distance between each starter joint and the nearest first-course joint before cutting the offset piece.

Safety Cue — Cutting and Fastening

Direct the utility knife blade away from the supporting hand and body when cutting starter sections. Seat each fastener flush without overdriving or angling it into an adjoining section.

Field Safety Cue — Roof-Edge Work

Maintain the fall-protection provisions established in Chapter 2 while working along eaves and rakes. Keep starter bundles staged clear of the roof edge and secured against sliding or wind while not in use.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review the roofing-system instructions for starter product, adhesive location, overhang, fastening, joint offset, eave use, rake use, and wind-warranty requirements. Record the confirmed values on the starter installation record before staging material.
  2. Confirm that roof-edge metal, underlayment, layout lines, and surface conditions are complete. Verify the Chapter 3, Chapter 5, and Chapter 6 inspection records show no open items on the eaves and rakes receiving starter strip.
  3. Verify that the starter product is compatible with the selected field shingles and approved for the roof edges being covered. Compare the product identified in Step 1 to the field-shingle product before opening any bundle.
  4. Stage starter strips flat and protected from bending, heat distortion, and wind. Keep bundles clear of the roof edge and secured against sliding.
  5. Establish the starting corner and direction based on prevailing installation sequence and water-shedding details. Record the starting corner and direction on the installation record.
  6. Mark the required starter overhang from the drip-edge reference. Measure from the Chapter 3 drip-edge line at intervals sufficient to hold a straight reference along the full eave length.
  7. Cut the first starter section to create the required joint offset from the first field-shingle course. Use the offset distance confirmed in Step 1 and mark the cut before fastening.
  8. Position the adhesive strip in the manufacturer-required location relative to the roof edge. Confirm orientation against the product instructions before fastening the first section.
  9. Align the starter strip to the reference line without waves or accumulated drift. Check alignment against the marked overhang at each section, not only at the starting corner.
  10. Fasten the strip using the specified fastener type, quantity, location, and seating condition. Seat each fastener flush without cutting the material or leaving a raised head.
  11. Install successive eave starter sections with tight or specified joints and without overlapping unless instructed. Maintain the marked overhang line through each new section.
  12. Keep starter joints offset from field-shingle joints by the required distance. Verify the offset against the Chapter 6 layout lines before fastening each new section.
  13. Form eave-to-rake corner transitions without doubled humps, gaps, or exposed adhesive. Trim and fit corner pieces so the adhesive strip remains continuous across the transition.
  14. Install rake starter strips where required, maintaining the approved edge projection and adhesive relationship. Confirm whether the roofing-system instructions require rake starter before beginning this step.
  15. Cut around approved edge conditions without leaving unsupported tabs or exposed deck. Support every cut edge on underlying material before fastening.
  16. Inspect overhang, alignment, fastening, adhesive orientation, joint offset, and corner transitions. Walk the full length of every eave and rake receiving starter strip and record findings on the installation record.
  17. Replace cracked, distorted, misaligned, overdriven, underdriven, or improperly offset sections. Use the flat pry bar to lift and remove defective sections without damaging the underlayment beneath.
  18. Protect installed starter strips from traffic and contamination before the first field course. Limit foot traffic across completed starter sections until Chapter 8 begins.
  19. Record starter product, layout, edge locations, fastener schedule, and corrections. Complete the starter installation record and attach photographs before beginning Chapter 8.

Common Starter Installation Checks

Apply the same checks to every eave and rake receiving starter strip, regardless of roof size or configuration.

  • Compatibility check: confirm the starter product matches the roofing-system instructions for the field shingles installed.
  • Overhang check: confirm overhang is measured from the drip-edge reference and consistent along the full eave or rake.
  • Adhesive check: confirm the adhesive strip is positioned per the manufacturer-required location relative to the roof edge.
  • Fastening check: confirm fastener type, quantity, location, and seating match the specified schedule.
  • Offset check: confirm starter joints are offset from first-course joints by the required distance.
  • Corner check: confirm eave-to-rake transitions are free of doubled humps, gaps, or exposed adhesive.

Complete the Starter Installation Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 8.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Starter Overhang Varies Along the Eave

Probable cause: the overhang was marked at only a few points and the reference drifted between them, or the drip edge itself is not straight.
Confirmation: remeasure overhang at short, consistent intervals along the full eave and compare each measurement to the required distance from the drip-edge reference.
Correction: remove and reposition the affected starter sections to the correct overhang; if the drip edge itself is out of tolerance, record the finding and route it to the Chapter 3 record for resolution.
Reinspection: recheck overhang along the corrected sections before fastening the first field course.

Starter Joint Aligns With a First-Course Joint

Probable cause: the offset cut on the first starter section was skipped, measured incorrectly, or the layout lines from Chapter 6 were not referenced during starter installation.
Confirmation: compare the marked starter joint locations to the Chapter 6 layout lines for the first field course.
Correction: remove and replace the affected starter section with one cut to the required offset before installing the first field course over it.
Reinspection: recheck the offset at each corrected joint before proceeding to Chapter 8.

Adhesive Strip Faces the Wrong Direction

Probable cause: the starter product was installed upside down or reversed relative to the manufacturer's required orientation.
Confirmation: compare the installed adhesive-strip position to the manufacturer instructions confirmed in Step 1 of the sequence.
Correction: remove and reinstall the affected sections in the correct orientation; do not rely on hand-sealing to compensate for a reversed adhesive strip.
Reinspection: recheck adhesive orientation on every corrected section.

Corner Transition Shows a Gap or Exposed Adhesive

Probable cause: the eave and rake starter pieces were not trimmed to fit before fastening, or the corner sequence was installed out of order.
Confirmation: inspect the corner from a safe position for continuous coverage and confirm the adhesive strip is not exposed to weather.
Correction: remove and refit the corner pieces so the starter course remains continuous across the transition without a doubled hump or gap.
Reinspection: recheck the corrected corner before the first field course is installed over it.

Fastener Overdriven, Underdriven, or Misplaced

Probable cause: incorrect nailer pressure setting, an out-of-zone fastening location, or a fastener driven at an angle.
Confirmation: inspect each fastener for flush seating within the specified fastening zone.
Correction: remove and replace any starter section with a cut, torn, or improperly fastened area; reset the nailer pressure before continuing.
Reinspection: recheck fastener seating and zone placement on all corrected sections.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with an inspected starter course and an organized record set, not scattered offcuts and loose fasteners.

  • Remove starter offcuts, release film, and loose fasteners from the roof surface and the ground below
  • Store unused starter material for reuse in later authorized corrections
  • Protect the completed starter course from unnecessary traffic before Chapter 8 begins
  • Compile the starter installation record and photographs into the project record
  • Transfer the completed starter installation record to the location Chapter 8 will reference before installing the first field course

Performance Check

With installation complete, review the starter installation record against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm every required eave and rake has starter strip installed at the correct overhang, the adhesive strip is oriented per the manufacturer instructions, every fastener is seated within the specified zone, every starter joint is offset from the nearest first-course joint by the required distance, every corner transition is continuous without gaps or exposed adhesive, and every correction has been rechecked. Sign and date the starter-course readiness decision before beginning Chapter 8. If any section cannot be verified from an available position, record it as an open item and issue a conditional-proceed decision rather than a full proceed decision.
