Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to measure, cut, and install a continuous eave and rake drip-edge system using the profile, metal type, and lap direction specified in the approved roof-edge detail. The finished work product is a fully fastened eave drip-edge run in the correct relationship to the deck and fascia, and — where the approved system requires it — a rake drip-edge run installed immediately after the applicable Chapter 5 underlayment course, with laps, corners, and fastening documented and photographed before the metal is covered.

You will confirm whether the approved system places eave metal below the underlayment and rake metal above it, measure and prepare a cut and lap plan for each edge, cut and form corner transitions, install the eave metal from the drainage-low or manufacturer-specified end, and either complete the rake metal now or stage and label it for installation immediately after the relevant underlayment course in Chapter 5.

This chapter depends directly on the Chapter 1 readiness decision and the Chapter 2 access and fall-protection system. It does not install membrane, underlayment, or shingles; it establishes the metal edge that those later layers must lap correctly to shed water.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-013 — Measure each eave and rake from defined corner references and prepare a cut and lap plan.
  • RF-014 — Cut drip-edge metal with the specified tool and form corner transitions without reverse laps.
  • RF-015 — Install eave drip edge with the required deck bearing, fascia leg, projection, and fastening.
  • RF-016 — Install rake drip edge in the correct relationship to the underlayment sequence.
  • RF-017 — Lap successive metal sections to shed water and resist wind-driven entry.
  • RF-018 — Inspect and document the completed edge-water-control assembly before it is covered.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 1 accepted the deck and Chapter 2 established safe roof access. This chapter installs the first roofing-system component onto that deck: the metal edge that controls where water first leaves the eave and how it sheds along the rake. Chapter 4 self-adhered membrane and Chapter 5 synthetic underlayment are installed directly against the eave metal completed here, and Chapter 5 also completes any rake metal staged in this chapter. Chapter 15 fascia and soffit work and Chapter 16 gutter installation both depend on the edge-metal projection and fascia relationship established here.

The Chapter 20 capstone repeats this same eave-then-rake sequence on the Book 4 shed structure, installing eave metal before underlayment and rake metal immediately after it, exactly as required in this chapter.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved roof-edge detail, drip-edge profile, metal type, corrosion compatibility, lap direction, fastening schedule, underlayment sequence, fascia condition, and gutter interface
  • confirming the deck and fascia are sound, aligned, clean, and ready to receive edge metal
  • confirming whether the approved system places eave metal below the underlayment and rake metal above it
  • measuring, marking, cutting, and forming eave and rake metal, including corner transitions
  • installing eave metal with the required deck bearing, fascia leg, projection, alignment, and fastening
  • installing rake metal either now or immediately after the applicable Chapter 5 underlayment course, as the approved system requires
  • inspecting and documenting the completed roof-edge assembly before it is covered

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • install self-adhered membrane, synthetic underlayment, or shingles; those tasks begin in Chapter 4 and later chapters
  • install rake metal over underlayment before the applicable Chapter 5 course is complete, when the approved system requires that sequence
  • correct fascia or subfascia decay, misalignment, or structural damage; refer that condition to Chapter 15 or an approved repair detail
  • substitute an incompatible metal, fastener, or profile for the one identified in the approved roof-edge detail

Any fascia or deck condition discovered during this chapter that falls outside minor correction must be recorded and referred to the responsible authority before edge metal is installed over that section.

Trade Note

Every lap in this chapter must shed water downslope onto the piece below it. Before fastening any section, trace the water path across the completed run in your mind — or with a hand pass along the metal — and confirm each upper piece overlaps the lower piece rather than the reverse. A reverse lap is not always visible from the ground, but it fails the first time wind-driven rain reaches that edge.

Sequence Standard

Confirm before cutting any metal whether the approved system requires eave metal below the underlayment and rake metal above it. If the approved system requires that sequence, prepare and label rake pieces now but do not install them until immediately after the applicable Chapter 5 underlayment course. Installing rake metal in the wrong sequence relative to the underlayment is a defect, not a stylistic choice.

Shop Tip

Stage and label every prepared rake piece with its intended location before moving on to eave installation. A piece cut correctly today but installed at the wrong rake tomorrow creates the same rework as a piece cut incorrectly.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every metal edge, flashing, and lap installed in this book follows the same water-shedding pattern established in this chapter: confirm the governing detail, measure from a fixed reference, cut and form without creating a reverse lap, install from the correct starting end, lap each successive piece so the upper section sheds onto the lower, and document the completed assembly before it is covered. Learning that pattern here, on the first metal installed in the book, protects every later flashing detail.

Confirming the Underlayment Relationship

Confirm whether the approved system requires eave metal installed below the underlayment and rake metal installed above it before cutting any stock. This determines whether rake metal is installed now or staged and labeled for installation immediately after the applicable Chapter 5 underlayment course. Do not install rake metal over underlayment that has not yet been placed, and do not delay eave metal that the approved system requires below the underlayment.

Critical Safety Cue — Roof-Edge Work

All cutting, forming, and fastening performed at or near a roof edge requires the fall-protection system accepted in Chapter 2. Confirm harness connection and edge protection before working within reach of an unprotected edge, and do not lean over an edge to reach a cut or fastening location.

Field Safety Cue — Sheet-Metal Handling

Wear cut-resistant gloves and eye protection when cutting, forming, or carrying drip-edge stock. Support long lengths on stands or sawhorses during marking and cutting to prevent bending, and carry sheet metal edge-first in wind to avoid loss of control.

Measurement Cue — Corner References

Measure each eave and rake from the corner references established in the cut-and-lap plan, not from the previously installed piece. Accumulated error from measuring piece-to-piece can drift the final length away from the actual roof dimension.

Manufacturer Cue — Corrosion Compatibility

Use only the metal type and fastener combination identified as corrosion-compatible in the approved roof-edge detail. Do not place incompatible metals or fasteners in contact, and do not substitute a different profile or gauge without an approved revision.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review the approved roof-edge detail, drip-edge profile, metal type, corrosion compatibility, lap direction, fastening schedule, underlayment sequence, fascia condition, and gutter interface. Record the confirmed profile, metal type, and fastening schedule on the roof-edge documentation form before staging any material.
  2. Verify that the roof deck and fascia or subfascia are sound, aligned, clean, and ready. Cross-reference the Chapter 1 roofing-readiness record for this section and perform a direct visual check from the approved access position.
  3. Confirm whether the approved system requires eave metal below the underlayment and rake metal above it. Record the determination on the documentation form; this decision controls the timing of Step 15 later in this sequence.
  4. Measure each eave and rake from defined corner references and prepare a cut and lap plan. Record each measured length, the number of pieces required, and the planned lap location for each joint.
  5. Stage metal lengths on supported surfaces and protect finished coatings from scratching and bending. Use the work-support stands or sawhorses and keep stock clear of foot traffic and material staging paths.
  6. Mark cuts, corners, hems, and lap locations using the approved reference edge. Use the same reference edge for every mark on a given piece to prevent accumulated measurement drift.
  7. Cut metal with the specified snips or cutting tool and remove sharp burrs. Wear cut-resistant gloves during this step and dispose of cutoffs so they do not accumulate in the work area or fall from the roof edge.
  8. Form approved corner transitions without creating reverse laps or open water paths. Use the seaming pliers or hand seamer to form each transition and verify the water path sheds outward before installing the piece.
  9. Begin at the drainage-low or manufacturer-specified end of the first eave. Confirm the starting end against the cut-and-lap plan from Step 4 before fastening the first section.
  10. Position the eave metal with the required deck bearing, fascia leg, projection, and alignment. Align the first section to the chalk-line reference and verify deck bearing, fascia-leg contact, and projection against the approved detail before fastening.
  11. Fasten the eave metal using the specified fastener type, location, and spacing. Follow the fastening schedule exactly; do not add fasteners outside the specified locations.
  12. Install successive eave sections with laps oriented to shed water and resist wind-driven entry. Confirm each new section overlaps the previous one in the downslope water-shedding direction before fastening.
  13. Keep fasteners out of prohibited lap and drainage zones. Review the fastening schedule's exclusion zones before driving each fastener near a lap or corner.
  14. Prepare rake pieces and corner transitions. Cut, form, and label rake sections using the same reference method as the eave, whether they will be installed now or staged for Chapter 5.
  15. If rake metal must cover underlayment, protect and label the prepared pieces and complete rake installation immediately after the relevant Chapter 5 underlayment course. Store labeled rake pieces flat and protected from bending and coating damage until that installation occurs; if the approved system does not require this sequence, proceed directly to Step 16.
  16. Position and fasten rake metal to the approved edge and underlayment relationship. Confirm the correct underlayment relationship is already in place (per Step 3) before fastening any rake section.
  17. Integrate eave-to-rake corners so upper metal sheds onto lower metal without exposed reverse laps. Verify each corner transition sheds water in the correct direction using a visual trace of the water path.
  18. Inspect metal alignment, laps, fastener seating, coating damage, sharp edges, and fascia contact. Walk the entire completed run from the approved access position and check each item against the Acceptance Standards table.
  19. Correct buckled, short, reverse-lapped, loosely fastened, or incompatible sections. Remove and replace any section that fails inspection using the same cut-and-lap plan and fastening schedule.
  20. Photograph and document the completed roof-edge assembly before it is covered. Attach photographs and final measurements to the roof-edge documentation form and record the date of completion for eave metal and, when applicable, rake metal.

Common Edge-Metal Checks

Apply the same checks to every eave and rake section, regardless of roof length or number of pieces.

  • Sequence check: confirm eave-below and rake-above underlayment relationships match the approved system before any metal is fastened.
  • Compatibility check: confirm metal type and fasteners match the corrosion-compatible combination in the approved detail.
  • Alignment check: confirm each section is straight along the reference line with the required deck bearing, fascia leg, and projection.
  • Lap check: confirm every lap and corner transition sheds water onto the piece below without reverse laps.
  • Fastening check: confirm fastener type, location, and spacing match the schedule and avoid prohibited lap zones.
  • Documentation check: confirm photographs and measurements are recorded before the assembly is covered.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Installed Lap Sheds Water the Wrong Direction

Probable cause: the installation direction was reversed from the drainage-low starting end, or a section was installed out of sequence.
Confirmation: trace the water path across the run from the drainage-low end and compare it to the cut-and-lap plan from Step 4.
Correction: remove and reinstall the affected sections in the correct sequence so each upper piece overlaps the lower piece.
Reinspection: retrace the water path across the corrected run before fastening is finalized.

Rake Metal Was Installed Before the Required Underlayment Course

Probable cause: Step 3's underlayment-sequence determination was not confirmed before rake installation began.
Confirmation: compare the installed condition to the approved roof-edge detail's underlayment-sequence requirement.
Correction: remove the rake metal, allow the correct underlayment course to be installed per Chapter 5, and reinstall the rake metal in the correct relationship.
Reinspection: verify the corrected sequence and document it before the section is covered further.

Fascia Contact Is Inconsistent Along a Run

Probable cause: fascia misalignment, warped stock, or inadequate support during fastening.
Confirmation: hold a straightedge along the fascia leg and identify the specific locations where contact is lost.
Correction: correct minor fascia alignment issues authorized under this chapter's scope, or refer fascia decay or structural misalignment to Chapter 15; reset the affected metal sections after the fascia condition is resolved.
Reinspection: recheck fascia contact along the full run after correction.

Corner Transition Leaves an Open Water Path

Probable cause: the corner was cut without a hem or transition fold, or the transition piece was formed incorrectly.
Confirmation: inspect the corner from a safe position and check for a gap where water could enter behind the metal.
Correction: remove and reform the corner transition using the seaming pliers or hand seamer, or replace it with a manufacturer-specified corner piece.
Reinspection: recheck the corner for a continuous, shedding water path before covering it.

Fastener Located Within a Lap or Drainage Exclusion Zone

Probable cause: the fastening schedule's exclusion zones were not reviewed before fastening near a lap or corner.
Confirmation: compare the fastener location to the fastening schedule's diagram or written exclusion-zone description.
Correction: remove the misplaced fastener, patch the hole per the manufacturer's repair method if required, and refasten at an approved location.
Reinspection: recheck all fastener locations along the affected section.

Incompatible Metal or Fastener Discovered After Installation

Probable cause: stock or fasteners from a different product line were substituted without confirming corrosion compatibility.
Confirmation: compare the installed metal and fastener markings to the approved roof-edge detail's compatibility requirement.
Correction: remove the incompatible material and reinstall using the specified compatible combination.
Reinspection: recheck the corrected section and record the confirmed product match on the documentation form.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, photographed roof-edge assembly and an organized material set, not scattered cutoffs and unlabeled stock.

  • Collect and dispose of metal cutoffs and burrs using a method that does not discharge material from the roof edge or the ground below
  • Store unused drip-edge stock flat, supported, and protected from bending and coating damage
  • Label and protect any staged rake pieces intended for installation after Chapter 5 underlayment
  • Compile the cut-and-lap plan, fastening record, and photographs into the roof-edge documentation record
  • Transfer the completed roof-edge documentation to the location Chapter 4 will reference before membrane installation begins

Field Terms

Drip Edge
Formed metal installed at roof eaves and rakes to direct water away from the fascia and into the drainage system.
Deck Bearing
The portion of the drip-edge profile that rests flat against the roof deck.
Fascia Leg
The portion of the drip-edge profile that turns down against the fascia or subfascia.
Projection
The horizontal distance the drip edge extends beyond the fascia face.
Lap
The overlap between two adjoining metal sections, oriented so the upper piece sheds water onto the lower piece.
Reverse Lap
A lap oriented so the lower piece is exposed over the upper piece, creating an open water path.
Drainage-Low End
The end of a given eave or rake run with the lowest elevation, or the manufacturer-specified starting point.
Corrosion Compatibility
The requirement that metal, fasteners, and adjoining materials not create a chemical reaction that accelerates corrosion.

Chapter Closeout

You have completed the third chapter in the roof-installation sequence: installing drip edge and roof-edge protection. This chapter required you to measure eaves and rakes and prepare a cut and lap plan (RF-013), cut and form corner transitions without reverse laps (RF-014), install eave drip edge to the required bearing, leg, and projection (RF-015), install rake drip edge in the correct underlayment relationship (RF-016), lap successive sections to shed water (RF-017), and inspect and document the completed assembly (RF-018).

You now have a documented, corrosion-compatible roof-edge assembly with eave metal complete and, where required, rake metal staged and labeled for installation after Chapter 5 underlayment. That record carries directly into the next chapter: installing self-adhered membrane at eaves, valleys, and vulnerable transitions before the underlayment field is placed.

Store the completed roof-edge documentation, photographs, and any staged rake-piece labels with the project record. Chapter 4 requires this record before membrane installation begins, and Chapter 5 requires it before rake metal is completed.
