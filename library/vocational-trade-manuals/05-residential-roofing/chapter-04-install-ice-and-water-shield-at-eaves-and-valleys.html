Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install self-adhered ice-and-water shield membrane at required eaves, valleys, and vulnerable transitions on a roof deck that has already passed the Chapter 1 readiness inspection and received the eave drip edge required by Chapter 3. The finished work product is a fully adhered membrane system covering the required interior distance at eaves, running the full length of every valley from low point to high point, integrated at corners and plane changes without fishmouths or reverse laps, and documented with product, lot, weather, and inspection records.

You will confirm the substrate is clean and dry, apply primer only where the manufacturer requires it, plan membrane widths and courses so the required coverage is achieved without an unsupported seam, install the membrane from a straight eave reference and from the low point of each valley toward the high point, treat every corner and transition without trapped air or reverse laps, seal approved penetrations and cuts, and inspect the completed membrane for wrinkles, voids, and adhesion failure before it is covered by synthetic underlayment.

This chapter follows Chapter 3 and precedes Chapter 5. The eave metal installed in Chapter 3 must already be in place beneath this membrane. The synthetic underlayment installed in Chapter 5, and the rake drip edge that Chapter 3 completes over that underlayment, both depend on the membrane installed here being continuous, adhered, and free of defects.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-019 — Prepare the deck surface for self-adhered membrane, including cleaning, drying, and primer application where required.
  • RF-020 — Lay ice-and-water membrane at eaves to the required interior coverage distance without stretching or wrinkling the sheet.
  • RF-021 — Install valley membrane from the low point toward the high point with correct positioning and geometry.
  • RF-022 — Overlap membrane courses and end laps by the manufacturer-controlled dimension at eaves, valleys, and transitions.
  • RF-023 — Seal approved penetrations, fastener heads, and membrane cuts using compatible patches or accessories.
  • RF-024 — Inspect installed membrane for wrinkles, voids, fishmouths, contamination, inadequate laps, bridging, and poor adhesion, and correct defects.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter sits between the roof-edge metal installed in Chapter 3 and the synthetic underlayment installed in Chapter 5. Eave drip edge required below the membrane must already be complete and accepted before this chapter begins. The membrane installed here becomes the last line of defense against ice-dam backup at eaves and against concentrated water flow in valleys, positions that ordinary underlayment laps alone do not adequately protect.

Every later chapter that crosses a membrane-protected area — synthetic underlayment in Chapter 5, valley shingles in Chapter 9, and any flashing chapter that meets a membrane-covered eave or valley — must integrate with this membrane in shingle-lap order rather than disturb or bridge it.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing climate requirements, membrane product instructions, required coverage, substrate limits, primer requirements, temperature limits, and lap dimensions
  • confirming that eave drip edge required beneath the membrane is complete and accepted
  • preparing the substrate at eaves and valleys, including cleaning, drying, and primer application where required
  • installing self-adhered membrane at eaves to the required interior coverage distance
  • installing self-adhered membrane in valleys from the low point toward the high point
  • overlapping membrane courses, end laps, and secondary valley strips by the manufacturer-controlled dimension
  • sealing approved penetrations, fastener heads, and membrane cuts with compatible patches or accessories
  • inspecting, repairing, and documenting the completed membrane installation

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • install membrane over a deck that has not passed the Chapter 1 readiness inspection or that lacks required eave drip edge from Chapter 3
  • install synthetic underlayment, roofing layout lines, starter strips, or shingles; those tasks begin in Chapter 5 and later chapters, only after this chapter's membrane installation is inspected and accepted
  • select or substitute a membrane product, primer, or lap dimension different from the approved roofing-system specification and manufacturer instructions
  • install step flashing, headwall flashing, apron flashing, or penetration flashing accessories that belong to Chapters 10 through 12, beyond sealing approved membrane cuts and fastener heads

Any deck area, corner, or transition that cannot be membraned to the manufacturer's required geometry with the tools and access available must be recorded as a deficiency and escalated before it is covered.

Trade Note

Self-adhered membrane bonds permanently once pressed into place. Plan each course, corner, and valley run before removing release film. A membrane sheet repositioned after full contact tears the deck substrate or the sheet itself rather than releasing cleanly.

Sequence Standard

Do not begin membrane installation until eave drip edge required beneath the membrane, from Chapter 3, is complete and accepted. Do not proceed to synthetic underlayment, layout lines, or shingles until this chapter's membrane inspection is complete and any defects are repaired.

Shop Tip

Precut manageable membrane lengths on the ground or a supported staging surface before climbing to the work position. Handling a full uncut roll on a sloped eave increases the chance of an off-square placement that cannot be corrected once contact is made.

Common Field Fundamentals

Self-adhered membrane depends on two things the installer directly controls: a clean, dry substrate and correct sequencing from low point to high point, or from eave to interior, so every lap sheds water onto the layer below it. A membrane installed out of sequence, or onto a contaminated surface, will not achieve the adhesion the manufacturer's coverage and lap dimensions assume, regardless of how carefully it is rolled.

Substrate Preparation and Primer

Inspect the deck for dust, moisture, frost, oil, loose fibers, protrusions, gaps, and damaged sheathing before staging any membrane. Clean and dry the substrate using methods permitted by the membrane manufacturer. Apply compatible primer only where the manufacturer requires it, and allow the primer to reach the specified condition before membrane placement begins.

Manufacturer Cue — Temperature and Substrate Limits

Do not install self-adhered membrane outside the manufacturer's specified deck-surface and ambient-temperature range. If cold-weather application is permitted, follow the manufacturer's warming method and equipment for both the membrane and the substrate. Do not substitute an unapproved heat source or extend application into conditions the manufacturer's instructions exclude.

Critical Safety Cue — Working Near the Roof Edge

Membrane installation at eaves places workers near an unprotected edge while handling a slick, adhesive-backed material. Confirm fall protection accepted under Chapter 2 is in place before working within the fall-protection zone, and keep footing clear of loose release film, which becomes a slip hazard on any roof surface.

Field Safety Cue — Wind and Adhesive Handling

Stop membrane installation in wind conditions that make sheet control unsafe or that could pull an unrolled length toward a roof edge before it is pressed into place. Keep release film contained; do not allow it to blow off the roof or accumulate underfoot.

Measurement Cue — Eave Coverage Distance

Measure the required eave coverage from the exterior wall line or other controlling reference identified in the roofing-system specification, not from the fascia or drip-edge outer edge. Plan membrane widths and course locations so the required interior coverage is achieved without relying on an unsupported seam at the coverage line.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review climate requirements, membrane product instructions, required coverage, substrate limits, primer requirements, temperature limits, lap dimensions, and valley details. Confirm the revision or date on each document before using it, and record confirmation on the governing-documents table above.
  2. Confirm that eave drip edge required below the membrane is complete and accepted. Locate the Chapter 3 acceptance record for the eaves receiving membrane. If the record cannot be located or shows an open item, stop this chapter and route the project back before continuing.
  3. Inspect the deck for dust, moisture, frost, oil, loose fibers, protrusions, gaps, and damaged sheathing. Perform this inspection at every eave and valley area scheduled for membrane, and record any condition requiring correction before cleaning.
  4. Clean and dry the substrate using methods permitted by the membrane manufacturer. Use only the cleaning method identified in the product instructions; do not use a solvent, degreaser, or drying method the manufacturer does not permit.
  5. Apply compatible primer only where required and allow it to reach the specified condition. Apply primer solely to the areas and substrates the manufacturer identifies, and confirm the required flash-off or cure condition before proceeding.
  6. Measure the required eave coverage from the exterior wall line or other controlling reference. Record the measured coverage distance for each eave on the installation record before cutting membrane lengths.
  7. Plan membrane widths and course locations so the required interior coverage is achieved without an unsupported seam. Lay out the course plan on paper or the installation record before beginning, identifying every side lap and end lap location.
  8. Precut manageable membrane lengths and keep adhesive faces protected until placement. Cut lengths on a supported staging surface, and keep release film in place until each length is positioned at its installation location.
  9. Establish a straight eave reference line. Snap or otherwise mark a straight reference parallel to the eave using the drip-edge relationship identified in the roofing-system specification.
  10. Align the first membrane section without stretching it. Position the first length to the eave reference line, confirming square alignment before removing any release film.
  11. Remove release film in controlled increments while pressing the membrane from the center outward. Peel back only the portion of film needed for the next contact area, and press outward from the center of the sheet to avoid trapping air.
  12. Roll or press the membrane using the specified tool and pressure method. Use the membrane roller or hand roller identified in the tools list, working from the center of the sheet toward each edge and end.
  13. Install successive sections with the manufacturer-controlled end and side laps. Measure each lap against the manufacturer's minimum dimension before pressing the lap into contact, and record the lap dimension used.
  14. Treat eave corners, rake transitions, and changes in plane without fishmouths or reverse laps. Form each corner and transition according to the manufacturer's detail, orienting every lap so the upper piece sheds onto the lower piece.
  15. Prepare valleys by confirming deck support, geometry, and cleanliness. Inspect each valley for adequate width, straight framing support, and a clean substrate before staging valley membrane.
  16. Center or position the valley membrane according to the approved detail and install from low point toward high point. Establish the valley centerline reference before removing release film, and begin installation at the identified low point of the valley run.
  17. Add required secondary valley strips or transition patches in shingle-lap order. Where the approved detail calls for a secondary strip or patch, install it so each added piece sheds onto the piece installed before it.
  18. Seal approved penetrations, fastener heads, or membrane cuts using compatible patches or accessories. Use only the patch or accessory identified in the manufacturer's instructions, and seal only at approved penetration locations confirmed against the Chapter 1 readiness record.
  19. Inspect for wrinkles, voids, fishmouths, contamination, inadequate laps, bridging, and poor adhesion. Inspect the full installed area from an approved position, checking every lap, corner, and valley run against the acceptance standards above.
  20. Repair defects using the manufacturer-approved patch dimensions and sequence. Correct each identified defect using the specific patch size and lap sequence the manufacturer requires; do not rely on hand pressure alone to correct a void or fishmouth.
  21. Protect the membrane from unnecessary traffic, prolonged exposure, and incompatible materials. Limit foot traffic on completed membrane to what is required for the next course, and keep incompatible solvents, sealants, or coatings off the installed surface.
  22. Record product, lot information, weather conditions, locations, laps, repairs, and inspection results. Complete the membrane installation record before beginning Chapter 5, including every repair made and its location.

Common Membrane Installation Checks

Apply the same checks to every eave and valley area receiving membrane, regardless of roof size or configuration.

  • Prerequisite check: confirm the Chapter 3 eave drip-edge acceptance record exists for each eave before membrane installation begins there.
  • Substrate check: confirm the deck is clean, dry, and free of frost, oil, loose fibers, protrusions, gaps, and damaged sheathing before membrane placement.
  • Coverage check: confirm eave membrane meets or exceeds the required interior coverage distance measured from the controlling reference.
  • Sequence check: confirm valley membrane was installed from the low point toward the high point, with every lap shedding onto the layer below it.
  • Lap check: confirm every side lap and end lap meets or exceeds the manufacturer-controlled minimum dimension.
  • Surface check: confirm the finished membrane is free of wrinkles, voids, fishmouths, contamination, inadequate laps, bridging, and poor adhesion, or each item is corrected and rechecked.
  • Documentation check: confirm product, lot, weather, locations, laps, repairs, and inspection results are recorded before Chapter 5 begins.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Membrane Will Not Adhere to the Substrate

Probable cause: deck temperature or ambient temperature outside the manufacturer's application range, contaminated or damp substrate, or missing required primer.
Confirmation: check the deck-surface and ambient temperature against the manufacturer's specified range, and reinspect the substrate for moisture, dust, or oil at the affected location.
Correction: remove and discard the affected membrane section per the manufacturer's instructions, re-clean or dry the substrate, apply primer if required, and reinstall using a new section once conditions are within range.
Reinspection: press-test the reinstalled section and confirm full contact before continuing adjoining courses.

Fishmouth or Lifted Edge at a Lap or Corner

Probable cause: the lap was pressed from one direction only, trapping air, or the corner transition was not formed to the manufacturer's detail.
Confirmation: inspect the lap or corner for a visible gap or lifted edge and check the lap dimension against the manufacturer's minimum.
Correction: lift the affected area only as far as necessary, re-press from the center outward, and install a manufacturer-approved patch over the corrected area if the sheet was disturbed beyond simple re-pressing.
Reinspection: recheck the lap or corner for full contact and confirm no remaining gap before covering the area.

Eave Coverage Distance Falls Short of the Required Dimension

Probable cause: the coverage measurement was taken from the wrong reference, or the course plan did not account for the full required interior distance.
Confirmation: remeasure the installed coverage from the exterior wall line or other controlling reference identified in the roofing-system specification.
Correction: install an additional course extending the coverage to the required distance, lapping onto the existing membrane by the manufacturer-controlled minimum.
Reinspection: recheck the extended coverage against the required distance and record the correction.

Valley Membrane Installed Out of Low-to-High Sequence

Probable cause: installation began at the wrong end of the valley, or successive lengths were placed without confirming which piece must shed onto which.
Confirmation: inspect the valley run from the low point to the high point and identify any lap oriented against the water-flow direction.
Correction: remove and reinstall the affected length in the correct sequence per the manufacturer's instructions; do not attempt to correct a reverse lap with sealant alone.
Reinspection: recheck the full valley run for consistent low-to-high lap direction before installing secondary strips or shingles.

Wrinkle or Void Found During Final Inspection

Probable cause: the membrane was stretched during placement, or debris was trapped beneath the sheet before pressing.
Confirmation: inspect the wrinkle or void visually and by hand pressure to confirm the extent of the unbonded area.
Correction: cut and remove the affected area per the manufacturer's repair instructions, remove any trapped debris, and install a patch of the manufacturer-specified dimension and lap sequence.
Reinspection: press-test the patch and confirm full adhesion before the area is covered by underlayment.

Penetration or Cut Location Does Not Match the Chapter 1 Readiness Record

Probable cause: a penetration was relocated or added after the Chapter 1 inspection without an approved change document.
Confirmation: compare the observed penetration location to the Chapter 1 roof sketch and any approved change documents.
Correction: stop sealing the penetration, record the discrepancy as a deficiency, and route it to the responsible authority for an approved change document before continuing.
Reinspection: reseal the penetration once an approved change document is issued.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a fully adhered, inspected membrane system and a complete installation record, not loose release film and unlogged repairs.

  • Collect and dispose of all release film and membrane cutoffs using an approved method that does not discharge material from the roof edge
  • Wipe down tools used for primer or adhesive contact per the product instructions before storage
  • Store unused membrane rolls protected from moisture, sunlight, and temperature extremes per the manufacturer's storage instructions
  • Compile the product, lot, weather, location, lap, repair, and inspection records into the project record
  • Transfer the completed membrane installation record to the location Chapter 5 will reference before beginning synthetic underlayment

Field Terms

Self-Adhered Membrane
A roofing underlayment product with a factory-applied adhesive face that bonds directly to the deck substrate without separate fasteners across its field.
Eave Coverage Distance
The required interior distance membrane must extend from the exterior wall line or other controlling reference.
Side Lap
The overlap between the long edges of two adjoining membrane courses.
End Lap
The overlap between the ends of two membrane lengths within the same course.
Fishmouth
A gap or lifted edge at a lap or corner where the membrane has not fully bonded, creating an open water path.
Bridging
Membrane spanning a plane change, corner, or deck irregularity without full contact, leaving an unsupported void beneath the sheet.
Low Point / High Point
The lower and upper ends of a valley run; installation proceeds from the low point toward the high point.
Primer
A compatible product applied to specific substrates, only where the membrane manufacturer requires it, to improve adhesion.
Shingle-Lap Order
An installation sequence in which each added layer sheds water onto the layer installed before it.
