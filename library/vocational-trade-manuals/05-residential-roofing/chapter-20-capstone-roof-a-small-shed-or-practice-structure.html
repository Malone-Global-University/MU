Job Overview

This chapter is the cumulative capstone for Book 5. The finished work product is a complete, inspected asphalt-shingle roofing and drainage system installed on the framed and roof-sheathed 8-foot by 10-foot shed structure completed in Book 4, or an approved equivalent practice structure built to the same approved project specification.

You will carry forward every competency taught in Chapters 1 through 19: deck inspection and readiness, roof access and fall protection, edge metal, self-adhered membrane, synthetic underlayment, layout, starter strips, field shingles, valley construction where the structure includes a valley, sidewall and headwall flashing where the structure includes those conditions, penetration flashing, ridge-cap shingles, ridge-vent installation, fascia and soffit ventilation, and gutters and downspouts. You will assemble those systems in the actual water-shedding and manufacturer-controlled construction sequence rather than in chapter-number order, complete a full closeout inspection, and issue a formal handoff to the building-envelope work that follows in Book 6.

This is the last chapter in the book. It does not introduce new material systems; it integrates the systems already taught into one complete, inspected roof.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-115 — Integrate deck inspection, edge metal, and self-adhered membrane and underlayment installation into one coordinated capstone sequence.
  • RF-116 — Install starter strips and asphalt-shingle field courses on the capstone roof planes using the approved layout, exposure, and fastening schedule.
  • RF-117 — Integrate required flashing — valley, sidewall, headwall, or penetration — with the shingle field as each condition is encountered on the capstone structure.
  • RF-118 — Install a ridge-vent system and compatible ridge-cap shingles and verify the intake-to-exhaust attic airflow path.
  • RF-119 — Install gutters, downspouts, and an approved discharge extension on the designated capstone eaves.
  • RF-120 — Conduct a full capstone inspection, correct every failed or incomplete item, and complete the project documentation and handoff to Book 6.

Where This Skill Fits

Every chapter in this book fed into this one. Chapter 1 established the deck-readiness discipline used again here. Chapter 2 established the access and fall-protection plan every capstone step depends on. Chapters 3 through 6 established the edge-metal, membrane, underlayment, and layout sequence. Chapters 7 through 9 established starter strips, field shingles, and valley construction. Chapters 10 through 12 established sidewall, headwall, apron, and penetration flashing. Chapters 13 and 14 established ridge-cap and ridge-vent work. Chapter 15 established fascia, soffit, and edge ventilation. Chapter 16 established gutters and downspouts. Chapters 17 through 19 established repair, diagnosis, and deck-replacement skills that inform the correction step of this capstone.

This chapter does not reopen those skills individually. It requires you to sequence and integrate them correctly on one real structure, and it hands the completed, weather-protected shed off to Book 6, which begins the building-envelope work — siding, trim, and related wall systems — on the same structure.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • confirming that the Book 4 shed structure is complete, stable, square, braced, sheathed, and released for roofing work
  • establishing the complete roof-access, fall-protection, material-staging, dropped-object-control, weather-monitoring, and rescue plan for the capstone roof
  • inspecting the deck and correcting only authorized minor deficiencies, escalating anything structural or moisture-related
  • preparing a complete material takeoff, bundle-distribution plan, flashing schedule, fastener schedule, and work sequence before materials are installed
  • installing fascia, soffit, and intake ventilation where those components must precede edge metal
  • installing eave drip edge, self-adhered membrane, synthetic underlayment, and rake drip edge in the required water-shedding order
  • establishing shingle layout controls, installing starter strips, and installing the asphalt-shingle field on both roof planes
  • integrating penetration flashing and any sidewall, headwall, or valley flashing that the approved capstone design actually contains
  • cutting the ridge-vent slot, installing the ridge vent, and installing ridge-cap shingles over it
  • installing gutters, downspouts, and approved discharge extensions
  • conducting the full closeout inspection, completing documentation, and handing the structure off to Book 6

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • rebuild the roof frame or roof deck completed in Book 4, except for authorized corrections identified during the readiness inspection in Step 4 of the required sequence
  • invent a valley, sidewall, headwall, or penetration condition that is not part of the approved capstone structure; install only the flashing types the actual structure requires
  • substitute a hose test or visual check for proof of whole-building watertightness; the closeout inspection uses only the approved local water-flow observation methods established in earlier chapters
  • begin building-envelope work — siding, trim, or wall-cladding systems — covered in Book 6, except where this chapter's handoff explicitly identifies exposed interfaces that must be protected until that work begins

Any deficiency identified during the closeout inspection must be corrected under the authority that governs it — the relevant chapter procedure for a roofing defect, or an escalation to a structural or environmental authority for framing, decay, or hazardous-material findings — before the handoff is issued.

Trade Note

This chapter does not teach a new installation technique for any individual system. Where a step references a skill taught earlier — deck inspection, membrane placement, underlayment lapping, shingle exposure, flashing integration, ridge-vent cutting, or gutter slope — follow the governing chapter's full procedure and acceptance standards. This chapter's numbered sequence controls the order and coordination of that work on the actual capstone structure, not a replacement description of the work itself.

Sequence Standard

Follow the actual water-shedding and manufacturer-controlled construction sequence, even where it crosses this chapter's own step numbers. Eave metal is installed before the eave membrane and underlayment; rake metal is installed immediately after the applicable underlayment course; valley, sidewall, headwall, and penetration flashings are integrated while the surrounding shingle courses are installed, not inserted afterward; and the ridge vent is installed before the ridge-cap shingles that cover it. Do not install any layer out of this order to save time.

Shop Tip

Build the complete material takeoff and flashing schedule (Step 7) before the first bundle reaches the roof. A capstone roof this size has little room to store excess material, and a missed flashing piece discovered mid-course forces you to stop work with an exposed deck.

Common Field Fundamentals

The capstone does not introduce new roofing theory. It requires you to hold the entire book's sequence in view at once: deck readiness and access first, then edge metal and water-control base layers in their required order, then layout and shingle installation, then flashing integrated course-by-course rather than added afterward, then ridge ventilation and cap shingles, then fascia, soffit, and gutters, and finally a closeout inspection that checks every one of those systems together rather than one at a time.

Coordinating the Actual Construction Sequence

Fascia, soffit, ventilation, edge metal, roof covering, and gutters must be coordinated according to the approved assembly rather than installed blindly in chapter-number order. Where the approved system requires underlayment over eave drip edge and beneath rake drip edge, install eave metal before the underlayment course and rake metal immediately after it. Integrate valley, step, headwall, and penetration flashings while the surrounding shingles are installed; do not insert them after the shingle field is complete. Install the ridge vent before the ridge-cap shingles that cover it.

Critical Safety Cue — Full Fall Protection for Every Roof-Surface Step

Do not begin any roof-surface step in this chapter until the complete Chapter 2 access, fall-protection, material-staging, and rescue plan is inspected and accepted for the capstone structure. A short shed roof does not remove the fall hazard; verify anchors, harnesses, and rescue provisions before every work session, not only once at the start of the project.

Field Safety Cue — Multi-Day Weather Exposure

Because the capstone spans several days, reconfirm the weather forecast and stop-work triggers before each session. Limit exposed deck, membrane, and underlayment area to what can be protected before conditions change or the work period ends, and secure staged materials against wind between sessions.

Code Alert — Structure Release and Local Requirements

Do not begin roofing until the Book 4 structural release for the shed is on record. Confirm local permit, inspection, and jurisdictional requirements for the capstone project before starting, since these vary by location and are not fixed by this chapter.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Obtain the approved capstone roof plan, roof slope, deck specification, roofing-system instructions, ventilation design, edge details, penetration details, gutter plan, permit requirements, and inspection criteria. Confirm the revision or date on each document and record the confirmation on the governing-documents table above.
  2. Confirm that the Book 4 structure is complete, stable, square, braced, sheathed, and released for roofing work. Locate the signed Book 4 structural release. If it cannot be located or shows an open item, stop this chapter and route the structure back to the responsible authority before continuing.
  3. Establish the complete roof-access, fall-protection, material-staging, dropped-object-control, weather-monitoring, and rescue plan. Apply the full Chapter 2 procedure to the capstone structure and record the completed daily roof-safety readiness record before any roof-surface step begins.
  4. Inspect the deck from attic, ground, edge, and approved roof positions. Apply the full Chapter 1 procedure to the capstone structure and produce a deficiency list using the same format.
  5. Correct authorized raised fasteners, debris, minor gaps, and surface defects; escalate structural or moisture damage. Complete only corrections authorized under Chapter 1's scope; route anything beyond that scope to Chapter 19 or the applicable structural or environmental authority before proceeding.
  6. Verify roof slope, eave and rake dimensions, ridge condition, fascia, soffit, ventilation openings, and planned penetrations. Compare each measured value to the approved capstone roof plan and record any deviation as a deficiency requiring resolution before the affected system is installed.
  7. Prepare the complete material takeoff, bundle distribution plan, flashing schedule, fastener schedule, and work sequence. Identify every valley, sidewall, headwall, apron, and penetration condition actually present on the capstone structure and assign each to its governing chapter procedure before ordering or staging materials.
  8. Install or verify fascia, soffit, intake ventilation, and other edge components that must precede edge metal. Apply the Chapter 15 procedure and acceptance standards where the capstone design requires new or corrected fascia, soffit, or intake ventilation.
  9. Install eave drip edge according to the approved roof system. Apply the Chapter 3 procedure, positioning eave metal below the underlayment where the approved system requires that relationship.
  10. Install required self-adhered membrane at eaves, penetrations, and other designated vulnerable areas. Apply the Chapter 4 procedure and confirm coverage against the approved climate and product requirements.
  11. Install synthetic underlayment from low point to high point with required laps and fastening. Apply the Chapter 5 procedure, maintaining shingle-lap water flow from deck to discharge.
  12. Install rake drip edge in the required relationship to the underlayment. Complete the Chapter 3 rake-metal installation immediately after the applicable underlayment course, as required by the System-Sequence Control governing this book.
  13. Establish horizontal and vertical shingle layout controls. Apply the Chapter 6 procedure to both roof planes of the capstone structure, verifying symmetry and avoiding narrow final courses.
  14. Install starter strips along required eaves and rakes. Apply the Chapter 7 procedure and confirm joint offset from the first field-shingle course.
  15. Install asphalt-shingle field courses on both roof planes with approved exposure, offset, fastening, edge cuts, and bundle blending. Apply the Chapter 8 procedure, checking alignment against the layout grid at regular intervals.
  16. Integrate one approved plumbing-vent or training penetration flashing when included in the capstone design. Apply the Chapter 12 procedure while the surrounding shingle courses are installed, not after the field is complete.
  17. Install any applicable sidewall, headwall, valley, or other flashing only where the approved capstone structure contains that condition. Apply the governing Chapter 9, 10, or 11 procedure for each condition actually present, integrated course-by-course with the surrounding shingles.
  18. Cut the approved ridge-vent slot without damaging framing. Apply the Chapter 14 procedure, stopping work immediately if the proposed slot would cut trusses, rafters, ridge beams, connectors, wiring, or other structural or service components.
  19. Install the ridge vent and verify the intake-to-exhaust airflow path. Apply the Chapter 14 procedure and confirm that intake vents, baffles, and rafter channels remain unobstructed.
  20. Install compatible ridge-cap shingles over the vent using the required fastening method. Apply the Chapter 13 procedure, confirming the ridge vent is fully installed before capping begins.
  21. Install gutters, outlets, downspouts, and approved discharge extensions on designated eaves. Apply the Chapter 16 procedure, establishing slope from measured high- and low-point references and confirming the discharge route will not create foundation saturation, erosion, icing, or hazards.
  22. Clean roof planes, gutters, attic, work zones, and surrounding property of nails, cutoffs, packaging, and debris. Complete this cleanup before beginning the closeout inspection so debris does not obscure inspection findings.
  23. Inspect deck preparation records, edge metal, membranes, underlayment, layout, starter strips, field shingles, flashings, ridge vent, cap shingles, fascia, soffit, gutters, downspouts, and discharge. Use the Capstone Inspection Checklist below and the acceptance standards from each governing chapter.
  24. Correct every failed or incomplete item within the authorized scope. Route any item beyond this chapter's authorized scope to the applicable chapter procedure or outside authority, and record the referral.
  25. Conduct approved local water-flow observations at gutters, downspouts, flashings, and transitions without overstating the results. Apply the governing chapter's test procedure for each system and record the observed result; do not represent these observations as proof of whole-building watertightness.
  26. Complete the competency assessment, daily safety records, product records, photographs, concealed-work records, inspection documents, and punch list. Assemble the full capstone documentation set referenced throughout this chapter.
  27. Protect unfinished building-envelope interfaces for transfer to Book 6. Identify and weather-protect any wall, trim, or siding interface left incomplete by this book's scope.
  28. Conduct a formal handoff identifying completed roofing, remaining wall-envelope work, maintenance requirements, product limitations, and warranty documentation. Sign and date the handoff record before releasing the structure to Book 6 work.

Common Capstone Checks

Apply these integration checks in addition to each governing chapter's individual acceptance standards.

  • Sequence check: confirm eave metal, membrane, underlayment, and rake metal were installed in the required water-shedding order rather than in chapter-number order.
  • Integration check: confirm every valley, sidewall, headwall, and penetration flashing was installed course-by-course with the surrounding shingles, not inserted after the field was complete.
  • Ridge check: confirm the ridge vent was fully installed and inspected before ridge-cap shingles were placed over it.
  • Coverage check: confirm fascia, soffit, edge metal, roof covering, and gutters were coordinated according to the approved assembly rather than installed independently.
  • Documentation check: confirm every concealed-work stage was photographed before the next layer covered it.
  • Handoff check: confirm the signed handoff record identifies every remaining building-envelope interface for Book 6.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Flashing Condition Discovered After the Shingle Field Is Already Installed

Probable cause: the flashing schedule prepared in Step 7 did not identify every sidewall, headwall, valley, or penetration condition actually present on the structure before field-shingle installation began.
Confirmation: compare the completed roof against the approved capstone roof plan and the flashing schedule to confirm the omission.
Correction: do not insert the missing flashing beneath the completed field unless an approved repair detail specifically permits it; instead, remove the affected shingle courses using the Chapter 17 procedure, install the correct flashing course-by-course, and reinstall the shingles in the original exposure and offset.
Reinspection: recheck the corrected area against the governing chapter's acceptance standards before continuing the closeout inspection.

Ridge Cap Installed Before the Ridge Vent

Probable cause: the work sequence in Step 7 did not correctly order Chapter 14 before Chapter 13, or the crew proceeded to capping before the vent slot was cut.
Confirmation: inspect the ridge from the attic and roof surface to confirm whether a vent slot and vent are present beneath the cap shingles.
Correction: remove the installed cap shingles, cut the ridge-vent slot per the Chapter 14 procedure, install the vent, and reinstall compatible ridge-cap shingles over it.
Reinspection: verify the intake-to-exhaust airflow path and ridge-cap coverage before continuing the closeout inspection.

Gutter Slope Fails the Water-Flow Test

Probable cause: hangers were installed from an unverified reference line, or a section settled after installation.
Confirmation: remeasure the high- and low-point references established in Chapter 16 and check for standing water during a repeat water-flow test.
Correction: loosen and reposition the affected hangers to restore the required slope, per the Chapter 16 procedure.
Reinspection: repeat the water-flow test and confirm continuous flow to the outlet without standing water.

Deck Deficiency Found During Step 4 That Exceeds This Chapter's Authorized Scope

Probable cause: structural damage, decay, or moisture intrusion occurred to the Book 4 structure between structural release and the start of this chapter.
Confirmation: apply the Chapter 1 inspection procedure and confirm the finding exceeds minor surface correction.
Correction: stop work on the affected section, route the finding to the applicable structural or environmental authority, and apply the Chapter 19 procedure only if and when an approved repair scope is issued.
Reinspection: repeat the deck-readiness inspection on the corrected section before roofing resumes over it.

Handoff Record Omits a Remaining Building-Envelope Interface

Probable cause: an interface such as an unfinished fascia return, exposed sheathing edge, or incomplete trim condition was not identified during Step 27.
Confirmation: walk the completed structure against the approved capstone roof plan and identify every interface where roofing work meets an unfinished wall or trim condition.
Correction: add the omitted interface to the handoff record with its protection method and required Book 6 follow-up.
Reinspection: confirm the interface remains weather-protected until Book 6 work begins.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a signed handoff record and an organized project file, not a pile of loose bundles and cutoffs.

  • Remove all cutoffs, loose nails, release film, and packaging from roof planes, gutters, the attic, and the ground below
  • Confirm dropped-object and material-staging areas are fully cleared before removing barricades or signage
  • Store leftover fasteners, flashing stock, and shingle bundles for use in future authorized repairs
  • Compile the deficiency list, competency assessment, safety records, photographs, product records, and inspection documents into the capstone project file
  • Transfer the signed handoff record to the location Book 6 will reference before beginning building-envelope work
