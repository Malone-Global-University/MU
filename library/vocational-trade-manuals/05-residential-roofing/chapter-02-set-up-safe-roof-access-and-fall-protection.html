Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to establish a complete roof-access and fall-protection system and to issue a signed daily roof-safety readiness record before any roofing material is installed. The finished work product is an inspected ladder-access route, an inspected anchor and fall-protection arrangement matched to the roof and the applicable requirements, a defined material-staging and dropped-object-control plan, a reviewed rescue and communication procedure, and a signed readiness record confirming every element passed inspection.

You will review the roof geometry and hazards identified in Chapter 1, determine the required fall-protection system, inspect every piece of access and fall-protection equipment before use, set and secure the ladder, install anchors only at approved structural locations, establish lifeline or restraint geometry, fit and inspect harnesses, establish controlled access zones and material-staging areas, define weather-monitoring and stop-work triggers, review the rescue procedure with the crew, conduct a dry run, and inspect and sign off every element before roofing work begins.

This chapter depends directly on Chapter 1's roofing-readiness decision. It does not repeat the deck inspection, and it does not authorize roofing material installation. It authorizes safe presence on the roof so that Chapter 3 and every later chapter can begin.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-007 — Select a compliant ladder location based on roof geometry, hazards, and ground conditions.
  • RF-008 — Secure ladder access using the manufacturer-controlled angle, extension, and top-and-bottom securing method.
  • RF-009 — Establish roof work zones, including controlled access zones and material-staging areas.
  • RF-010 — Stage materials safely within approved loading limits and clear of access paths and roof edges.
  • RF-011 — Identify fall hazards, including roof edges, openings, skylights, fragile surfaces, and electrical conductors.
  • RF-012 — Inspect the complete fall-protection setup and document a signed daily roof-safety readiness record.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 1 issued the roofing-readiness decision that confirms the deck is safe to receive roofing material. This chapter converts that readiness decision into safe physical access. Every chapter from Chapter 3 forward — edge metal, membrane, underlayment, layout, starter strips, shingle fields, valleys, flashing, ridge work, ventilation, fascia and soffit, and gutters — depends on the access and fall-protection system this chapter establishes and on the daily readiness record it produces.

The Chapter 20 capstone re-establishes this same access and fall-protection sequence on the Book 4 shed structure before any capstone roofing work begins.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing roof geometry, height, slope, access points, work zones, fall-protection plan, rescue plan, weather forecast, and site restrictions
  • identifying roof edges, openings, skylights, fragile surfaces, steep slopes, electrical conductors, vehicle routes, public areas, and ground-level drop zones
  • determining the required fall-protection system from the applicable regulations, project plan, roof conditions, and employer or qualified-person requirements
  • inspecting and removing from service any damaged, contaminated, expired, modified, or unidentified equipment
  • selecting and securing a ladder location, installing anchors only at approved structural locations, and establishing lifeline or restraint geometry
  • fitting and inspecting harness and connection equipment for each worker
  • establishing controlled access zones, material-staging areas, warning signs, barricades, or spotters where required
  • defining weather-monitoring and stop-work triggers and reviewing the rescue procedure and communication method
  • conducting a worker-access dry run and completing a signed daily roof-safety readiness record

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • select or install fall-protection equipment without qualified-person or applicable regulatory guidance where the site or equipment requires it
  • install roof anchors at locations not confirmed as approved structural locations by the manufacturer instructions and the framing plan
  • begin edge metal, membrane, underlayment, or shingle installation; that work begins in Chapter 3 and later chapters, only after this chapter's readiness record is signed
  • carry roofing bundles or other loaded materials during the dry run required by this chapter's sequence

Any access, anchor, or fall-protection element that fails inspection must be corrected and reinspected before it is recorded as complete, and no worker may proceed onto the roof surface using an element that has not passed inspection.

Trade Note

This chapter treats access and fall protection as a system, not a single piece of equipment. A harness inspected as compliant does not make the system compliant if the anchor, lifeline geometry, ladder security, or rescue plan has not also been inspected and accepted. Record every element separately and do not sign the readiness record until every element has passed.

Equipment Standard

Remove from service any ladder, anchor, harness, lanyard, lifeline, rope grab, guardrail, warning-line component, scaffold, lift, roof bracket, or accessory that is damaged, contaminated, expired, modified, or unidentified. Do not return questionable equipment to service based on a visual estimate; verify condition against the manufacturer's inspection criteria before use.

Shop Tip

Conduct the worker-access dry run required in Step 19 before any roofing bundles reach the roof. Problems with ladder angle, anchor geometry, or lifeline travel are far easier to correct while the crew is moving empty-handed than after the roof is loaded with material.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every access and fall-protection setup in this book follows the same pattern established in this chapter: review the hazards, determine the required system, inspect every component before use, install and secure the system at approved locations, fit and inspect it on each worker, define the controlled zones around it, rehearse access before loading the roof, and sign a readiness record before work begins. Determining the Required Fall-Protection System

Determine the required fall-protection system from the applicable regulations, the project plan, the roof conditions documented in Chapter 1, and any employer or qualified-person requirements that apply to this site. Do not select a fall-protection system based on habit or on what equipment happens to be available; confirm the selection against the identified requirement source before proceeding.

Critical Safety Cue — Inspect Before Every Use

Inspect every ladder, anchor, harness, lanyard, lifeline, rope grab, guardrail, warning-line component, scaffold, lift, roof bracket, and accessory before use, every work period. Remove damaged, contaminated, expired, modified, or unidentified equipment from service immediately and tag it as rejected.

Field Safety Cue — Ladder Placement

Select a firm, level ladder location away from doors, traffic, electrical hazards, unstable soil, and roof runoff. Set the ladder at the manufacturer-controlled angle and extension above the landing surface, and secure it at the top and bottom using the approved method before anyone climbs it.

Measurement Cue — Anchor and Lifeline Geometry

Establish lifeline, lanyard, or restraint geometry using the manufacturer's specified anchor spacing, working length, and clearance calculation. Do not estimate fall clearance or swing-fall distance; verify each against the manufacturer instructions for the specific equipment in use.

Code Alert — Regulatory and Qualified-Person Requirements

Fall-protection requirements vary by jurisdiction, roof height, roof slope, and employer program. Confirm the applicable regulatory requirement and any qualified-person determination before finalizing the system for this site, and do not substitute this chapter's general sequence for that determination.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review the roof geometry, height, slope, access points, work zones, fall-protection plan, rescue plan, weather forecast, and site restrictions. Cross-reference this review against the Chapter 1 roof sketch and readiness decision before selecting any equipment.
  2. Identify roof edges, openings, skylights, fragile surfaces, steep slopes, electrical conductors, vehicle routes, public areas, and ground-level drop zones. Mark each hazard on the roof sketch carried forward from Chapter 1 and record its location on the readiness form.
  3. Determine the required fall-protection system from the applicable regulations, project plan, roof conditions, and employer or qualified-person requirements. Record the determination and its source document on the readiness form before selecting equipment.
  4. Inspect ladders, anchors, harnesses, lanyards, lifelines, rope grabs, guardrails, warning-line components, scaffolds, lifts, roof brackets, and accessories before use. Use the manufacturer's inspection criteria for each item and tag each as inspected and accepted.
  5. Remove damaged, contaminated, expired, modified, or unidentified equipment from service. Tag rejected equipment and separate it from equipment accepted for use so it cannot be selected by mistake.
  6. Select a firm, level ladder location away from doors, traffic, electrical hazards, unstable soil, and roof runoff. Record the selected location and the hazards it avoids on the readiness form.
  7. Set the ladder at the manufacturer-controlled angle and extension above the landing surface. Verify the angle and extension with a level or the manufacturer's stated method before securing the ladder.
  8. Secure the ladder at the top and bottom using the approved method and protect the base from displacement. Confirm both the top and bottom securing points independently before anyone climbs the ladder.
  9. Provide a stable transition from ladder to roof without stepping around an unprotected edge or onto damaged decking. Reference the Chapter 1 deck-condition record for the transition area and avoid any location listed as a deficiency.
  10. Install roof anchors only at approved structural locations using the manufacturer's fasteners and installation instructions. Confirm the structural location against the framing plan before installing any anchor, and record each anchor's location on the roof sketch.
  11. Establish lifeline, lanyard, or restraint geometry that limits free fall, swing fall, edge contact, and excessive travel. Calculate clearance and travel limits using the manufacturer instructions for the specific equipment installed.
  12. Fit and inspect each worker's harness and connection equipment. Adjust each harness to the individual worker and confirm proper fit and connection before that worker accesses the roof.
  13. Establish controlled access zones below roof edges and material-handling points. Mark each zone boundary and record its extent on the site sketch.
  14. Provide warning signs, barricades, spotters, or public protection where falling material could reach people or property. Match the specific measure to the site restrictions identified in Step 1.
  15. Identify material-staging areas that keep loads distributed, restrained, and clear of access paths and roof edges. Record each staging area's location and its distance from the nearest roof edge or access path.
  16. Establish tool tethering, debris handling, and fastener-control practices where required. Confirm the specific practice against the site plan and record it on the readiness form.
  17. Define weather-monitoring and stop-work triggers. Record the specific conditions — rain, lightning, frost, snow, ice, excessive heat, unsafe wind, or inadequate daylight — that will stop work for this site and work period.
  18. Review the rescue procedure, communication method, emergency contacts, and suspension-response plan with the crew. Confirm every crew member understands their role and record the review on the readiness form.
  19. Conduct a worker-access dry run without carrying roofing bundles. Have each worker traverse the ladder, transition, and roof-access route empty-handed and confirm the route functions as planned.
  20. Inspect every anchor, connection, ladder tie, work zone, material zone, and rescue provision. Complete this inspection after the dry run and before any roofing material is staged.
  21. Correct failed conditions before roofing work begins. Reinspect any corrected element using the same standard as the original inspection.
  22. Complete and sign the daily roof-safety readiness record. Record every inspected element, every correction, and the crew members present, then sign and date the record before Chapter 3 begins.

Common Access and Fall-Protection Checks

Apply the same checks at the start of every work period this system will support, not only on the day it is first installed.

  • Prerequisite check: confirm the Chapter 1 roofing-readiness decision is proceed or conditional-proceed for the area to be accessed.
  • Equipment condition check: confirm every item in use is inspected and tagged; confirm rejected equipment is separated from accepted equipment.
  • Ladder security check: confirm the ladder angle, extension, and top-and-bottom securing before each use.
  • Anchor and geometry check: confirm anchors are at approved structural locations and lifeline geometry meets the manufacturer's clearance and travel limits.
  • Zone check: confirm controlled access zones and material-staging areas remain marked, clear, and functioning as planned.
  • Documentation check: confirm the daily roof-safety readiness record is signed before work begins each day.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Ladder Shifts or Feels Unstable During the Dry Run

Probable cause: incorrect ladder angle, an unlevel or soft base, or an inadequate top or bottom securing method.
Confirmation: remeasure the ladder angle against the manufacturer specification and inspect the base surface and both securing points.
Correction: reset the ladder at the correct angle on a firm, level surface and resecure both the top and bottom using the approved method.
Reinspection: repeat the dry run before any worker carries material up the ladder.

Anchor Location Does Not Align With Approved Framing

Probable cause: the intended anchor location was selected by visual estimate rather than by confirming the framing plan.
Confirmation: compare the intended location against the framing plan and, where accessible, verify from the attic or interior underside.
Correction: relocate the anchor to a confirmed approved structural location using the manufacturer's fasteners and installation instructions.
Reinspection: recheck the installed anchor against the framing plan and the manufacturer instructions before it is used.

Lifeline or Lanyard Geometry Allows Excessive Swing Fall

Probable cause: the anchor position or lanyard length was not calculated against the manufacturer's clearance and swing-fall method.
Confirmation: recalculate swing-fall distance using the manufacturer instructions for the installed anchor position and lanyard length.
Correction: relocate the anchor, shorten the working length, or install an additional anchor to bring swing fall within the manufacturer's limit.
Reinspection: recheck the geometry and repeat the dry run before roofing work begins.

Equipment Item Fails Inspection After Being Staged for Use

Probable cause: damage, contamination, or an expired inspection date was not identified during initial equipment sorting.
Confirmation: re-inspect the item against the manufacturer's inspection criteria and compare its condition to the tagging record.
Correction: remove the item from service, tag it as rejected, and replace it with an inspected and accepted item before continuing.
Reinspection: confirm the replacement item passes inspection and update the readiness record.

Material-Staging Area Encroaches on an Access Path or Roof Edge

Probable cause: the staging area was identified without measuring its distance from the access path or nearest roof edge.
Confirmation: measure the staging area boundary against the access path and the nearest roof edge.
Correction: relocate the staging area to maintain clearance from access paths and roof edges, and redistribute any staged material accordingly.
Reinspection: recheck the relocated staging area before materials are moved onto the roof.

Crew Member Cannot Describe the Rescue or Communication Plan

Probable cause: the rescue procedure review in Step 18 was incomplete or a crew member arrived after the review.
Confirmation: ask each crew member to state the communication method, emergency contacts, and suspension-response plan.
Correction: repeat the rescue procedure review with the affected crew member before that worker accesses the roof.
Reinspection: confirm understanding again before signing the readiness record.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a signed daily readiness record and an organized equipment set, not loose gear staged around the site.

  • Return rejected equipment to a clearly marked out-of-service location separate from accepted equipment
  • Store harnesses, lanyards, and lifelines according to the manufacturer's storage instructions to protect them for reuse
  • Confirm barricades, warning signs, and controlled access zone markers remain in place and legible for the duration of the work period
  • Compile equipment-inspection tags, anchor locations, and the daily roof-safety readiness record into the project record
  • Transfer the completed access and fall-protection record to the location Chapter 3 will reference before edge-metal installation begins

Field Terms

Daily Roof-Safety Readiness Record
The signed document confirming every access, anchor, fall-protection, staging, and rescue element has been inspected and accepted for the work period.
Anchor
An approved structural attachment point for a lifeline, lanyard, or restraint line, installed only at a location and with fasteners identified by the manufacturer instructions.
Restraint System
A fall-protection arrangement that limits a worker's travel so a fall over an edge cannot occur.
Fall-Arrest System
A fall-protection arrangement that stops a fall already in progress within defined limits.
Controlled Access Zone
A defined, marked boundary limiting who may enter an area below or adjacent to active roof work.
Material-Staging Area
A defined roof or ground location where materials are distributed, restrained, and kept clear of access paths and roof edges.
Swing Fall
Lateral pendulum-style motion during a fall caused by an anchor point that is not directly overhead of the work position.
Drop Zone
The ground-level area below a roof edge or material-handling point where falling objects could land.
Dry Run
A rehearsal of the access route performed without carrying roofing bundles, used to confirm the setup functions as planned.

Chapter Closeout

You have completed the second chapter in the roof-installation sequence: establishing safe roof access and a complete fall-protection system. This chapter required you to select a compliant ladder location (RF-007), secure ladder access (RF-008), establish roof work zones (RF-009), stage materials safely (RF-010), identify fall hazards (RF-011), and inspect the complete fall-protection setup and document the result (RF-012).

You now have a signed daily roof-safety readiness record confirming that access, fall protection, staging, and rescue provisions are inspected and accepted. That record carries directly into the next chapter: measuring, cutting, and installing eave and rake drip edge in the correct relationship to the deck and the underlayment that follows it.

Store the completed equipment-inspection tags and the daily roof-safety readiness record with the project record. Chapter 3 requires this record before any worker accesses the roof to install edge metal.
