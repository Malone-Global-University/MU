Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to fabricate and install apron or headwall flashing at a roof-to-vertical-wall transition and to integrate it with the roof covering and wall-drainage layer in the correct shingle-lap order. The finished work product is one continuous, corrosion-compatible headwall or apron flashing assembly, dry-fitted, fastened, sealed at approved locations only, and photographed before wall finishes conceal it.

You will measure the headwall from fixed references, cut and form the flashing including end dams and corner returns, integrate the lower roof leg with underlayment and shingles in the manufacturer-controlled order, fasten only in approved locations, lap multiple sections correctly, integrate the wall leg behind the WRB, counterflashing, or cladding, and inspect the completed transition before it is covered.

This chapter builds directly on the roof-covering work established in Chapter 8 and the sidewall step-flashing method taught in Chapter 10. A headwall crosses the slope rather than running along it, so the flashing must carry water across the full width of the wall intersection in a single, continuous, laterally lapped assembly rather than as a shingle-by-shingle system.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-061 — Measure headwall flashing length, corners, and end conditions from defined references.
  • RF-062 — Cut metal flashing to the approved profile using approved tools without damaging its coating.
  • RF-063 — Install apron flashing in the correct relationship to the roof covering and wall drainage layer.
  • RF-064 — Lap underlayment and adjoining shingle courses with the flashing in the correct shingle-lap order.
  • RF-065 — Seal exposed transitions only at approved locations using compatible sealant.
  • RF-066 — Inspect the completed headwall assembly for coverage, laps, fastening, and drainage direction.

Where This Skill Fits

Headwall and apron flashing depends on the deck, underlayment, and roof-covering work completed in Chapters 1, 5, and 8, and on the wall-drainage relationship taught alongside step flashing in Chapter 10. It must be integrated while the surrounding shingle courses are installed; it may not be inserted after the shingle field is complete unless an approved repair detail specifically permits it.

This chapter's flashing sequence feeds directly into later ridge, ventilation, and gutter work whenever a headwall condition is present on the Chapter 20 capstone structure, and it establishes the flashing-integration discipline used again in Chapter 12 for penetrations and in Chapter 18 for leak diagnosis.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved headwall detail, flashing profile, wall-leg height, roof-leg width, metal type, end dams, corner transitions, underlayment relationship, counterflashing, and siding clearance
  • measuring the headwall length and identifying inside corners, outside corners, step-flashing transitions, and end conditions
  • cutting and forming apron flashing, end dams, corner returns, hems, and kickout transitions from an approved metal-cut and lap plan
  • dry-fitting the flashing and integrating the lower roof leg with underlayment, membrane, and shingles in the manufacturer-controlled order
  • fastening only in approved locations, lapping multiple sections to shed water, and integrating the wall leg behind WRB, counterflashing, cladding, or trim
  • applying compatible sealant only at approved end joints, reglets, corners, or terminations
  • inspecting and documenting the completed flashing before wall finishes are installed

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • select an unstated flashing profile, wall-leg height, roof-leg width, or metal type; these are set by the approved headwall detail and this chapter does not substitute a different design
  • bridge a designed drainage opening or weep path with sealant, or use sealant as a substitute for required flashing, laps, fastening, or drainage geometry
  • cut, notch, or fasten through siding, trim, or wall-cladding systems beyond the clearance and integration steps this chapter specifically authorizes
  • insert headwall flashing beneath two completed shingle fields unless an approved repair detail specifically permits it; this chapter assumes integration while surrounding courses are being installed

Any headwall condition that does not match the approved detail, or any wall assembly requiring counterflashing beyond this chapter's scope, must be referred to the responsible authority before the flashing is installed.

Trade Note

A headwall carries water sideways across the full width of the wall intersection before it can drop onto the roof below. Every joint in the flashing run is a potential lateral leak path. Treat lap direction and lap length as fixed requirements from the approved detail, not as adjustable field decisions.

Sequence Standard

Headwall and apron flashing must be integrated while surrounding shingle courses are installed. Do not complete the shingle field first and insert this flashing afterward unless an approved repair detail specifically permits it. Coordinate this chapter's work with Chapter 8 field-shingle installation at the point where the roof plane meets the wall.

Shop Tip

Dry-fit the entire flashing run, including corner pieces, before fastening any section. A corner condition that does not fit cleanly in the dry-fit is far easier to correct before shingles and sealant are in place.

Common Field Fundamentals

A headwall is different from a sidewall. A sidewall runs along the slope and is flashed piece-by-piece with step flashing integrated course by course, as taught in Chapter 10. A headwall runs across the slope, so the flashing must be a continuous run (or a run of laterally lapped sections) wide enough to carry the full width of water arriving from above the wall. Getting the roof-leg and wall-leg relationship right, and keeping every lap oriented to shed water downslope and across the run in the approved direction, is the central skill of this chapter.

Wall legTurns up behind WRB, counterflashing, or cladding at the required height
End damsPrevent side-running water at terminations and corners

Multi-section lapsOriented to shed water in the prevailing drainage direction

Roof-Leg and Wall-Leg Integration

Integrate the lower roof leg with underlayment, membrane, and shingles in the manufacturer-controlled order so water from the wall drains onto the roof covering rather than behind it. Integrate the wall leg behind WRB, counterflashing, cladding, or trim components in the same shingle-lap sequence, maintaining the required clearance between roofing and siding or trim.

Critical Safety Cue — Roof-Edge and Wall-Height Fall Hazards

Headwall conditions often occur near dormers, additions, or upper-story wall intersections where the roof plane is narrow and the fall distance from the wall side may be greater than from the open roof edge. Confirm the roof-access and fall-protection system from Chapter 2 covers this specific location before working at the wall intersection.

Manufacturer Cue — Roof-Leg Width and Wall-Leg Height

Roof-leg width, wall-leg height, end-dam dimensions, and required laps are set by the approved headwall detail and flashing manufacturer's instructions. Do not shorten a leg or reduce a lap to fit an unplanned condition; stop and confirm the detail before cutting metal to a dimension not shown in the specification.

Code Alert — Drainage Openings and Weep Paths

Do not bridge a designed drainage opening or weep path with sealant. Sealant is not a substitute for required flashing, laps, fastening, or drainage geometry. Verify which openings in the wall-drainage system are designed to remain open before applying any sealant near them.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review the approved headwall detail, flashing profile, wall-leg height, roof-leg width, metal type, end dams, corner transitions, underlayment relationship, counterflashing, and siding clearance. Confirm the revision or date on each document and record the confirmation on the governing-documents table above.
  2. Confirm that the roof deck, wall surface, membrane, underlayment, and adjoining roofing courses are ready for integrated flashing work. Verify the Chapter 1 readiness decision and Chapter 5 underlayment record cover this location before proceeding.
  3. Measure the headwall length from defined outside references and identify inside corners, outside corners, step-flashing transitions, and end conditions. Record each measurement and feature location against the roof sketch established in Chapter 1.
  4. Prepare a metal cut and lap plan that minimizes joints and directs each lap away from prevailing drainage. Identify the number of sections required, the length of each section, and the lap location and direction between sections before cutting any metal.
  5. Cut and form the apron flashing using approved tools without damaging its coating. Use tin snips or metal shears for cutting and seaming pliers or a metal brake for forming, and remove sharp burrs from every cut edge.
  6. Form end dams, corner returns, hems, or kickout transitions required by the detail. Form each feature to the exact dimension specified in the approved detail; do not approximate an end dam or corner return by eye.
  7. Dry-fit the flashing and verify continuous wall contact, roof bearing, corner fit, and drainage direction. Confirm the dry-fit before fastening any section, and correct any section that does not achieve continuous contact.
  8. Integrate the lower roof leg with underlayment, membrane, and shingles in the manufacturer-controlled order. Follow the exact layering sequence specified in the roofing-system instructions rather than a generalized shingle-lap assumption.
  9. Position the flashing so water from the wall drains onto the roof covering rather than behind it. Verify this relationship at every section before fastening.
  10. Fasten only in approved locations and keep exposed fasteners out of primary water paths. Use the specified fastener type and spacing and confirm each fastener location against the approved detail before driving it.
  11. Install adjoining shingle courses beneath or over the roof leg as required by the approved assembly. Coordinate this step directly with the Chapter 8 field-shingle sequence at this location; do not complete the shingle field first.
  12. Lap multiple flashing sections by the specified amount and orient laps to shed water. Verify each lap meets the minimum dimension from the cut and lap plan established in Step 4.
  13. Integrate the wall leg behind WRB, counterflashing, cladding, or trim components in shingle-lap order. Confirm the wall leg is fully covered by the wall-drainage layer or approved counterflashing before trim or siding is installed.
  14. Maintain required clearance between roofing and siding or trim. Measure the clearance against the approved specification and record any shortfall as a deficiency.
  15. Use compatible sealant only at approved end joints, reglets, corners, or terminations. Confirm sealant compatibility with the flashing metal and wall-drainage materials before application.
  16. Do not bridge designed drainage openings or weep paths with sealant. Identify every designed opening in the wall-drainage system before applying sealant near it.
  17. Inspect roof-leg coverage, wall-leg height, end dams, laps, fasteners, sealant, WRB integration, and water path. Compare each item to the Acceptance Standards table above.
  18. Correct short flashing, reverse laps, open corners, exposed fasteners, blocked drainage, or incompatible materials. Reinspect each corrected item against the same standard used in the original finding.
  19. Photograph concealed transitions before wall finishes are installed. Label each photograph with its location on the roof sketch.
  20. Record flashing material, dimensions, laps, fastening, sealant, and inspection results. Complete the documentation form and attach it to the project record before wall finishes proceed.

Common Headwall Flashing Checks

Apply the same checks to every headwall or apron condition on the roof, regardless of length or corner count.

  • Coverage check: confirm the roof leg and wall leg meet the approved dimensions across the full measured length.
  • Drainage-direction check: confirm every lap and the roof-leg position direct water onto the roof covering, not behind it.
  • Corner and end-dam check: confirm every corner and termination is formed to the approved dimension without an open gap or reverse lap.
  • Fastener-zone check: confirm every fastener is located only in an approved zone, out of primary water paths.
  • Sealant-zone check: confirm sealant appears only at approved terminations and does not bridge a designed drainage opening.
  • Documentation check: confirm dimensions, laps, fastening, sealant, and photographs are complete before wall finishes conceal the work.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Water Stain Appears Below a Headwall After Installation

Probable cause: the roof leg was fastened over the underlayment rather than beneath it in the required order, or a lap between sections was reversed.
Confirmation: remove the minimum surrounding roofing needed to expose the roof-leg integration and lap direction at the suspect section.
Correction: restore the correct layering order and lap direction using the governing Chapter 18 leak-diagnosis procedure and the original headwall detail.
Reinspection: repeat the roof-leg coverage, lap-direction, and drainage-direction checks at the corrected section.

Corner Transition Will Not Fit Cleanly During Dry-Fit

Probable cause: the corner piece was cut to a generic angle rather than the actual measured wall or roof-plane angle at that corner.
Confirmation: remeasure the actual corner angle and compare it to the cut and lap plan established in Step 4.
Correction: recut or reform the corner piece to the measured angle before proceeding to fastening.
Reinspection: repeat the dry-fit check at the corrected corner.

Exposed Fastener Found in the Roof-Leg Water Path

Probable cause: a fastener was driven through the roof leg in a location not identified as an approved fastening zone.
Confirmation: compare the fastener location to the approved fastening-zone diagram in the headwall detail.
Correction: remove the fastener, seal the resulting hole with compatible sealant only where the detail permits sealant at that location, and reset the fastening at an approved zone if additional securement is required.
Reinspection: recheck the corrected location for approved fastener placement and drainage continuity.

Sealant Found Over a Designed Weep or Drainage Opening

Probable cause: sealant was applied generally along the wall-leg termination without first identifying which openings are designed to remain open.
Confirmation: compare the sealed location to the wall-drainage detail and identify whether the opening is a designed weep path.
Correction: remove the sealant from the designed opening using a method that does not damage the wall-drainage component, and reapply sealant only at the approved locations identified in the detail.
Reinspection: confirm the opening is clear and functioning before the wall finish is installed.

Wall Leg Is Not Fully Covered by WRB or Counterflashing

Probable cause: the WRB or counterflashing installation sequence was completed before the wall-leg height was verified, leaving a gap at the top of the wall leg.
Confirmation: measure the installed wall-leg height and compare it to the approved detail and the WRB or counterflashing overlap requirement.
Correction: extend or replace the wall leg, or adjust the WRB or counterflashing lap, so the required overlap is restored before siding or trim is installed.
Reinspection: recheck the wall-leg-to-WRB overlap at the corrected location.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, photographed flashing assembly, not a pile of metal offcuts left on the roof or ground.

  • Remove metal offcuts, sharp trim scraps, and packaging from the roof surface and the ground below
  • Store leftover flashing metal for reuse in later authorized flashing work
  • Remove excess sealant from exposed roofing and wall surfaces before it cures
  • Compile the flashing documentation form and photographs into the project record
  • Confirm the wall finish contractor or crew has the photographs and documentation before covering the wall leg
