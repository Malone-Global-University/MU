Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install asphalt shingles on one unobstructed roof plane, from the first full course above the starter strip to the planned upper termination. The finished work product is a straight, correctly exposed, correctly fastened shingle field with staggered joints, blended color, clean rake and edge cuts, and a documented inspection record.

You will confirm that starter strips, underlayment, edge metal, layout lines, and roof-safety systems are complete, stage and blend shingle bundles, install the first course to the starter edge and control lines, continue successive courses with the approved offset pattern and exposure, check alignment against the layout grid at regular intervals, cut rake shingles cleanly, integrate any flashing encountered in the plane, and stop the field at the planned upper termination.

This chapter builds directly on Chapter 6 (layout lines) and Chapter 7 (starter strips). It assumes a simple, unobstructed roof plane; valleys, sidewalls, headwalls, penetrations, and ridge work are covered in later chapters and must not be installed by improvising this chapter's procedure.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-043 — Place the first shingle course to the starter edge and horizontal control line.
  • RF-044 — Maintain the manufacturer-specified exposure on every course.
  • RF-045 — Follow the approved nail pattern, fastening zone, and fastener count.
  • RF-046 — Stagger joints using the approved offset pattern.
  • RF-047 — Cut shingles safely using the correct face and cutting surface.
  • RF-048 — Inspect the completed shingle field for exposure, alignment, fastening, and sealing readiness.

Where This Skill Fits

The shingle field installed in this chapter is the roof covering that every later flashing, ridge, and ventilation chapter integrates with. Chapter 9 through Chapter 12 install valley, sidewall, headwall, and penetration flashing course-by-course into a field built using this chapter's method. Chapter 13 and Chapter 14 terminate the field at the ridge. Chapter 20's capstone applies this same procedure to both planes of the shed roof.

A shingle field that drifts from its control lines, skips fasteners, or stacks joints creates rework and leak risk in every chapter that follows. The alignment checks in this chapter exist to catch drift before it is concealed by additional courses.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • confirming starter strips, underlayment, edge metal, layout lines, and roof-safety systems are complete before shingle installation begins
  • inspecting, distributing, and blending shingle bundles
  • installing the first full course and successive courses on one unobstructed roof plane
  • maintaining the approved exposure, offset pattern, and fastening zone on every course
  • checking alignment against the Chapter 6 control lines at regular intervals and correcting drift before it is concealed
  • cutting rake shingles from the correct face and maintaining the required projection
  • integrating field shingles with any approved flashing already present in the plane
  • stopping the field at the planned upper termination and inspecting the completed plane

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • install a new valley, sidewall, headwall, or penetration flashing as part of this chapter's procedure; those tasks are covered in Chapter 9 through Chapter 12 and must be integrated course-by-course, not added afterward
  • install ridge-cap shingles or ridge vent components; those tasks begin in Chapter 13 and Chapter 14
  • proceed with shingle installation over a roof plane whose starter strips, underlayment, edge metal, or layout lines have not passed their governing chapter's inspection
  • install shingles below the roof-slope limit stated in the manufacturer's instructions without an approved low-slope detail

Any roof plane that contains a valley, sidewall, headwall, or penetration must have those features integrated during this chapter's course-by-course installation according to the governing chapter's procedure; this chapter's simple-plane sequence applies to the unobstructed portions of that same plane.

Trade Note

Every course installed in this chapter is checked against the Chapter 6 control lines, not against the course below it. Reading only from the course below allows small errors to accumulate into a visible drift by the time the field reaches the ridge. Check the grid, not just the last course.

Sequence Standard

Do not begin this chapter over a roof plane whose starter strips, underlayment, edge metal, or layout lines have not passed inspection. Valley, sidewall, headwall, and penetration flashings must be integrated while surrounding shingles are installed; they may not be inserted after the field is complete unless an approved repair detail specifically permits it.

Shop Tip

Open and blend shingles from at least four bundles before starting a course. Working from a single bundle at a time is the most common cause of visible color banding across a finished roof plane.

Prerequisites

Common Field Fundamentals

A shingle field is a repeated, self-checking pattern: exposure sets the course spacing, the offset pattern keeps joints from stacking, and the fastening zone keeps every course wind-resistant. Every course you install must satisfy all three at once, and every few courses you must recheck the pattern against the Chapter 6 control lines rather than trusting the accumulated result of prior courses.

Bundle Inspection and Staging

Inspect shingle bundles for correct product, lot, color blend, damage, moisture, and storage condition before distributing them on the roof. Distribute bundles within approved roof-load and access limits without placing them over unsupported or damaged areas. Blend shingles from the required number of bundles to reduce visible color concentration.

Critical Safety Cue — Fall Protection During Course Work

Remain connected to the fall-protection system established in Chapter 2 for the entire duration of surface work on this roof plane. Do not disconnect to reach a bundle, retrieve a tool, or reposition for a cut. Reposition anchors or lifelines using the approved method before moving outside your current protected zone.

Field Safety Cue — Loose Material and Cut Edges

Keep loose nails, release film, cutoffs, and packaging out of drainage paths and off the ground below. Do not allow cutoffs or debris to discharge from the roof edge. Keep the ground-level drop zone established in Chapter 2 clear during cutting and course installation.

Measurement Cue — Recheck Alignment Every Few Courses

Check alignment against the horizontal and vertical control lines established in Chapter 6 at regular intervals, not only against the course immediately below. Correct drift before the error becomes concealed by additional courses.

Code Alert — Roof-Slope and Sealing Requirements

Confirm the roof plane's measured slope, recorded in Chapter 1, falls within the shingle manufacturer's approved slope range for standard application. Confirm ambient temperature meets the manufacturer's sealing requirement, or complete the hand-sealing procedure required for cold-weather installation.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review shingle type, roof-slope limits, exposure, offset pattern, fastening zone, fastener quantity, wind requirements, sealing conditions, and temperature limitations. Record the approved exposure and fastener count from the manufacturer instructions on the field installation record before opening any bundle.
  2. Confirm that starter strips, underlayment, edge metal, layout lines, and roof-safety systems are complete. Locate the signed records from Chapters 2, 3, 5, 6, and 7 for this roof plane. If any record is missing or shows an open item, stop this chapter and route the project back to the responsible chapter before continuing.
  3. Inspect shingle bundles for correct product, lot, color blend, damage, moisture, and storage condition. Reject bundles that do not match the approved product or that show damage, moisture, or improper storage, and record any rejected bundles on the field installation record.
  4. Distribute bundles within approved roof-load and access limits without placing them over unsupported or damaged areas. Stage bundles flat, clear of walkways established in Chapter 2, and away from marked weak areas from Chapter 1.
  5. Blend shingles from the required number of bundles to reduce visible color concentration. Open and draw from at least four bundles at a time throughout the installation, not sequentially from single bundles.
  6. Cut the first shingle to establish the required joint offset. Cut the starting piece to the dimension established in the Chapter 6 layout record so the first course's joint falls at the required offset from the starter-strip joint.
  7. Position the first full course to the starter edge and horizontal control line. Align the course butt edge to the first horizontal control line from Chapter 6 and the shingle edge to the starter-strip overhang from Chapter 7.
  8. Fasten each shingle through the manufacturer's designated fastening zone using approved roofing nails. Drive the specified fastener count at the specified locations within the fastening zone for every shingle in the course.
  9. Seat nail heads flush without cutting the shingle surface or leaving raised heads. Check each fastener by hand or visually before covering it with the next course, and reset any overdriven or underdriven fastener immediately.
  10. Continue the first course while maintaining edge alignment and joint spacing. Verify the running course against the horizontal control line at each shingle and correct drift before starting the next course.
  11. Begin the next course with the required offset and maintain the specified exposure. Measure the offset distance from the layout record and verify exposure against the horizontal control line for that course.
  12. Follow the approved stair-step, rack, or other installation pattern without stacking joints. Use the offset sequence established in the manufacturer instructions and the Chapter 6 layout record for every course.
  13. Check alignment against horizontal and vertical control lines at regular intervals. Recheck every three to five courses against both the horizontal and vertical control lines rather than relying on the course below.
  14. Correct drift before the error becomes concealed by additional courses. If a course has drifted from its control line, adjust exposure gradually over the following courses per the manufacturer's allowed adjustment range, and record the correction.
  15. Cut rake shingles from the correct face using the approved cutting surface and tool. Cut from the manufacturer-specified face with the utility knife and hook blade or specified tool, supported on an approved cutting surface that protects the underlying course.
  16. Maintain the required rake projection or trim line without damaging edge metal. Verify each cut against the rake projection established in the Chapter 6 layout record and the Chapter 3 edge-metal installation.
  17. Integrate field shingles with any approved flashing encountered in the roof plane. Where step, headwall, apron, or penetration flashing is staged for this plane, install it course-by-course per the governing chapter's procedure as the field reaches that location; do not continue the field over an unintegrated flashing location.
  18. Hand-seal shingles only where required by the manufacturer, cold-weather procedure, cut-tab condition, or repair detail. Apply the specified hand-seal adhesive at the manufacturer-required locations and press tabs into position without smearing sealant onto exposed surfaces.
  19. Keep loose nails, release film, cutoffs, and packaging out of drainage paths and off the ground below. Clear debris from the plane at regular intervals during installation, not only at the end of the work period.
  20. Stop the field at the planned upper termination, ridge-vent zone, or cap-shingle preparation line. Confirm the stopping point against the ventilation and ridge plan before installing the final course of this chapter's scope.
  21. Inspect exposure, course alignment, joint offsets, nail placement, nail seating, edge cuts, tab damage, and sealing readiness. Walk the completed plane from approved positions and record findings on the inspection checklist below.
  22. Replace damaged or improperly fastened shingles using the approved correction method. Lift surrounding tabs as needed, remove the defective shingle without damaging the underlayment, and install a matching replacement per the fastening schedule.
  23. Clean the roof plane and document product, layout method, fastener schedule, weather, and inspection results. Photograph the completed field, attach photographs to the field installation record, and sign the inspection checklist.

Common Shingle Field Checks

Apply the same checks to every course on the plane, regardless of roof size or configuration.

  • Prerequisite check: confirm starter strips, underlayment, edge metal, layout lines, and roof-safety systems are signed off before the first course is fastened.
  • Exposure check: confirm every course matches the manufacturer-specified exposure against the horizontal control lines.
  • Offset check: confirm joints follow the approved stair-step, rack, or other pattern with no stacked joints.
  • Fastening check: confirm every shingle has the approved fastener count, location, and flush seating.
  • Cut check: confirm rake cuts are made from the correct face, maintain projection, and do not damage edge metal.
  • Flashing integration check: confirm any flashing in the plane was installed course-by-course, not inserted afterward.
  • Documentation check: confirm the field installation record, photographs, and inspection checklist are complete and signed before Chapter 9 or later flashing and ridge work begins.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Courses Have Drifted Off the Horizontal Control Lines

Probable cause: accumulated small exposure errors from measuring off the course below instead of the control lines, or a control line that was disturbed during installation.
Confirmation: measure the current course against the nearest re-snapped horizontal control line and against the roof-plane dimensions recorded in Chapter 6.
Correction: adjust exposure gradually over the following several courses within the manufacturer's allowed adjustment range so the drift is not corrected in a single visible jump; re-snap disturbed control lines before continuing.
Reinspection: recheck alignment against both control lines every three to five courses until the field reaches the upper termination.

Joints Are Stacking Between Courses

Probable cause: the offset pattern was not followed consistently, or a cut piece was reused in the wrong position.
Confirmation: compare the joint locations of the affected courses to the approved offset pattern from the manufacturer instructions and Chapter 6 layout record.
Correction: remove and reposition the misplaced shingle using the correct offset cut; do not leave a stacked joint in place.
Reinspection: recheck the offset pattern on the corrected course and the courses immediately above and below it.

Fastener Heads Are Overdriven or Cutting the Shingle Surface

Probable cause: incorrect nailer pressure or air-pressure setting, or fastening on an uneven deck surface.
Confirmation: inspect the affected fasteners and the surrounding shingle surface for granule displacement or a cut mat.
Correction: remove the overdriven fastener, adjust the tool setting, and drive a new fastener in an approved adjacent location within the fastening zone; replace any shingle whose mat is cut through.
Reinspection: recheck fastener seating on the corrected shingle and on a sample of surrounding shingles installed with the same tool setting.

Rake Cuts Are Uneven or Damaging the Edge Metal

Probable cause: cutting from the wrong face, using an unsupported cutting surface, or cutting without a straightedge reference.
Confirmation: measure the rake projection at several points against the Chapter 6 layout record and inspect the edge metal for scoring or bending.
Correction: recut affected shingles from the correct face using a supported cutting surface and straightedge; replace any shingle with an unacceptable cut; report damaged edge metal to the Chapter 3 record for correction.
Reinspection: recheck rake projection and edge-metal condition after corrections.

A Flashing Location Was Reached Before the Flashing Was Staged

Probable cause: the flashing schedule was not coordinated with the shingle installation sequence, or the required flashing pieces were not available when the course-by-course installation reached that location.
Confirmation: compare the current course location to the flashing schedule and identify the specific governing chapter for the missing flashing.
Correction: stop the field at that location, obtain and stage the required flashing, and complete the governing chapter's course-by-course procedure before continuing the field; do not continue past the location and insert flashing afterward.
Reinspection: verify the completed flashing integration before resuming field-shingle installation past that location.

Ambient Temperature Is Below the Manufacturer's Sealing Requirement

Probable cause: installation is occurring in cold weather where the shingle adhesive strip will not activate promptly.
Confirmation: check current and forecast temperature against the manufacturer's stated sealing-temperature range.
Correction: apply the manufacturer's approved hand-sealing procedure at the required tab locations rather than relying on adhesive-strip activation.
Reinspection: recheck tab adhesion once temperatures reach the manufacturer's sealing range or before the roof is exposed to wind events, whichever the approved procedure requires.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with an inspected shingle field and an organized record set, not a roof plane littered with cutoffs and loose fasteners.

  • Remove cutoffs, release film, loose granules, and packaging from the completed plane and the ground below
  • Collect and account for loose or dropped fasteners before leaving the roof plane
  • Store leftover shingle bundles for reuse in later authorized repairs or adjoining planes
  • Compile the field installation record, photographs, and inspection checklist into the project record
  • Transfer the completed shingle-field record to the location the next governing chapter (valley, flashing, ridge, or ventilation) will reference

Performance Check

With the shingle field complete, review the Shingle Field Inspection Record against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm every course has been checked against the Chapter 6 control lines, exposure and offset pattern match the manufacturer instructions on sampled courses, fasteners are seated flush at the approved count and location, rake cuts are clean and correctly projected, any flashing encountered in the plane was integrated course-by-course, the field stops at the planned upper termination, and the plane has been cleaned of loose fasteners, cutoffs, and packaging. If any item cannot be verified from an available position, record it as an open item and issue a conditional-proceed decision rather than a full proceed decision.
