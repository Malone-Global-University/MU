Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to inspect a framed and sheathed roof and to issue a documented roofing-readiness decision before any roofing work begins. The finished work product is a completed roof-deck inspection record, a deficiency list with authorized corrections completed and rechecked, and a written proceed, conditional-proceed, or stop-work decision supported by photographs and measurements.

You will confirm that framing and sheathing inspections are complete, identify the safe positions from which the roof may be inspected, examine the deck from both underside and surface positions where access allows, verify slope and deck specification against the approved roof plan, identify every drainage and transition feature on the roof, check clearances at penetrations, remove debris, reset or replace authorized deck fasteners, and document every unresolved item.

This is the first chapter in the book. It establishes the readiness decision that every later chapter depends on: no edge metal, membrane, underlayment, shingle, flashing, ridge, or gutter work may proceed over a deck that has not passed this inspection or been corrected to an authorized condition.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-001 — Inspect roof sheathing from safe interior, exterior, and approved surface positions.
  • RF-002 — Identify damaged decking, including loose panels, raised fasteners, delamination, soft areas, and water staining.
  • RF-003 — Verify roof slope on each roof plane using the approved measurement method.
  • RF-004 — Check roof edges, overhangs, and clearances at penetrations against the approved specification.
  • RF-005 — Prepare the surface for underlayment by removing debris and correcting only authorized minor deficiencies.
  • RF-006 — Document roof readiness with a deficiency list, photographs, measurements, and a proceed, conditional-proceed, or stop-work decision.

Where This Skill Fits

Every chapter that follows in this book — roof-access setup, edge metal, self-adhered membrane, underlayment, layout, starter strips, shingle fields, valleys, flashing, ridge work, ventilation, fascia and soffit, and gutters — is installed onto the deck condition documented in this chapter. A deck that has not been inspected and accepted here is not ready to receive any of those systems.

The readiness decision you issue at the end of this chapter is the gate that Chapter 2 checks before establishing roof access, and that the Chapter 20 capstone checks again before roofing the shed structure carried forward from Book 4.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • gathering and confirming the governing roof plan, framing plan, sheathing schedule, roofing-system specification, product instructions, and prior inspection records
  • confirming that required framing, sheathing, and structural inspections are already complete
  • inspecting the roof deck from safe positions, both underside and surface, without stepping onto an unprotected roof
  • verifying deck material, thickness, span rating, panel orientation, edge support, fastening, and roof slope against the approved specification
  • identifying eaves, rakes, ridges, hips, valleys, sidewalls, headwalls, penetrations, crickets, saddles, and drainage transitions
  • checking clearances at chimneys, vents, skylights, flues, electrical conductors, and combustible assemblies
  • removing debris and completing only authorized minor surface corrections
  • documenting deficiencies, corrections, and a final roofing-readiness decision

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • step onto the roof surface unless compliant roof access and fall protection are already in place; Chapter 2 establishes that system
  • replace structural decking, correct framing damage, or perform any repair beyond a minor authorized surface correction; refer structural deck replacement to Chapter 19 or an approved repair detail
  • install any edge metal, membrane, underlayment, or roofing product; those tasks begin in Chapter 3 and later chapters, only after this chapter's readiness decision is proceed or conditional-proceed with corrections completed
  • disturb insulation, wiring, ductwork, or other concealed building systems encountered during the underside inspection without the authority that governs that system

Any deck section that fails this chapter's inspection must be corrected under an authorized repair detail, or the roofing-readiness decision must record it as a stop-work item, before the section receives any roofing material.

Trade Note

Every finding in this chapter is recorded against a fixed reference: the approved roof plan, framing plan, and sheathing schedule. Do not evaluate slope, deck condition, or clearances against what "looks right" from the ground. If the plan and the field condition disagree, record the conflict and escalate it rather than resolving it by assumption.

Access Standard

Do not step onto the roof merely to complete this chapter. Chapter 2 establishes roof access and fall protection; this chapter does not.

Shop Tip

Mark every suspect area with chalk from a safe position before deciding how to reach it. Deciding access after you are already standing near a weak area removes your ability to plan a safe approach.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every readiness decision in this book, from this chapter's deck inspection to the Chapter 20 capstone readiness check, follows the same pattern: confirm the governing documents, inspect from safe positions, compare the field condition to the approved specification, correct only what is authorized, and document what remains. Learning that pattern here, before any material is installed, protects every layer that follows.

Underside and Surface Inspection

Inspect the underside of the deck where accessible for missed fasteners, unsupported edges, excessive gaps, water staining, daylight, delamination, mold, and framing conflicts. Inspect the accessible roof surface for loose panels, damaged edges, raised fasteners, protrusions, debris, contamination, uneven joints, punctures, and soft areas. Mark weak, wet, damaged, or questionable areas without walking on them.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Step Onto the Roof for This Chapter

Do not step onto the roof merely to complete this inspection. If compliant roof access, fall protection, surface condition, weather, and rescue provisions have not already been evaluated and accepted, limit the inspection to ground, ladder, lift, attic, scaffold, or another already-approved position. Chapter 2 establishes the access and fall-protection system required before any surface work.

Field Safety Cue — Weather and Footing

Stop the inspection for rain, frost, snow, ice, or wind conditions that make ladder, attic, or approved surface positions unsafe. Keep ladder feet on firm, level ground and maintain three points of contact while climbing.

Measurement Cue — Verify Slope on Each Plane

Measure roof slope separately on each distinct roof plane using the approved measurement method. Do not assume every plane on the roof shares the same slope, and do not substitute a visual estimate for a measured value.

Code Alert — Structural and Hazardous-Material Findings

Structural deck damage, suspected asbestos-containing material, active mold, or wet insulation found during this inspection are outside this chapter's authorized correction scope. Record the finding, stop work on the affected section, and route it to the applicable structural, environmental, or licensed authority before roofing proceeds over that section.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Obtain the current roof plan, framing plan, sheathing schedule, roofing-system specification, product instructions, prior inspection records, and approved change documents. Confirm the revision or date on each document before using it as a reference, and record the confirmation on the governing-documents table above.
  2. Confirm that roof framing, sheathing, required connectors, and structural inspections are complete before roofing loads are staged. Locate the signed structural or framing inspection record. If it cannot be located or shows an open item, stop this chapter and route the project back to the responsible authority before continuing.
  3. Establish the permitted inspection positions. Use ground, ladder, lift, attic, scaffold, or an already-approved roof-access and fall-protection system as applicable to this site. Record which positions are available and which roof planes each position allows you to inspect.
  4. Do not step onto the roof merely to complete this chapter. If compliant roof access is not already in place, limit the inspection to the safe positions identified in Step 3 until Chapter 2 establishes full roof access.
  5. Inspect the underside of the roof deck where accessible for missed fasteners, unsupported edges, excessive gaps, water staining, daylight, delamination, mold, and framing conflicts. Use the attic or another interior access point and a flashlight, and record the location of every finding using the framing bay or grid reference shown on the framing plan.
  6. Inspect the roof surface for loose panels, damaged edges, raised fasteners, protrusions, debris, contamination, uneven joints, punctures, and soft areas. Perform this inspection from the approved positions established in Step 3, without stepping onto an unprotected roof.
  7. Mark weak, wet, damaged, or questionable areas without walking on them. Use chalk or a marking crayon visible from the approved inspection position, and record each marked location on the deficiency list.
  8. Verify deck material, thickness, span rating, panel orientation, edge support, and fastening against the approved specification. Compare the observed condition on each accessible plane to the sheathing schedule and record any deviation as a deficiency.
  9. Verify roof slope on each plane using the approved measurement method. Measure each distinct roof plane separately and record the measured slope next to the plan slope for that plane.
  10. Identify eaves, rakes, ridges, hips, valleys, sidewalls, headwalls, penetrations, crickets, saddles, and drainage transitions. Mark each on a roof sketch or the roof plan copy attached to the readiness record so later chapters can reference the same feature locations.
  11. Inspect fascia, subfascia, rafter tails, roof edges, overhangs, and sheathing projection for readiness to receive edge metal and gutters. Record alignment, decay, splits, or inadequate bearing as a deficiency requiring Chapter 15 attention where applicable.
  12. Verify required clearances at chimneys, vents, skylights, flues, electrical conductors, and combustible assemblies. Compare each measured clearance to the approved specification and record any shortfall as a deficiency.
  13. Confirm that planned ridge slots, intake vents, penetrations, and roof openings agree with the framing and ventilation plans. Record any planned opening that does not have a corresponding, approved location on the plans as a deficiency requiring resolution before Chapter 14.
  14. Remove loose debris using a method that does not discharge material from the roof edge or damage the deck. Contain and remove debris from the approved inspection positions or a subsequently authorized access, keeping the drop zone below the roof edge clear during removal.
  15. Reset or replace authorized raised or missing deck fasteners using the approved fastening schedule. Use only the fastener type, length, and spacing identified in the sheathing schedule, and record each location corrected.
  16. Complete only minor surface corrections allowed by the plan; refer structural deck replacement to Chapter 19 or an approved repair detail. Do not attempt any correction beyond fastener resetting, minor debris removal, and other corrections explicitly authorized by the roofing-system specification.
  17. Prepare a deficiency list identifying location, condition, required correction, responsible authority, and reinspection method. Enter every unresolved and every corrected item, including items referred to Chapter 19 or another authority.
  18. Correct authorized deficiencies and recheck the affected areas. Complete only the corrections identified as authorized in Step 16, and reinspect each corrected location against the same standard used in the original finding.
  19. Photograph and record roof slope, deck condition, edge condition, openings, penetrations, and unresolved items. Attach photographs to the readiness record and label each with its location on the roof sketch from Step 10.
  20. Issue a proceed, conditional-proceed, or stop-work roofing-readiness decision. Base the decision on the completed deficiency list: proceed only when every item is corrected and rechecked, conditional-proceed when specific listed corrections remain outstanding on specific roof sections, and stop-work when a structural, moisture, or hazardous-material finding requires resolution outside this chapter's scope. Sign and date the decision.

Common Deck Inspection Checks

Apply the same checks to every accessible roof plane, regardless of roof size or configuration.

  • Structural prerequisite check: confirm the signed framing and sheathing inspection record exists before proceeding.
  • Surface condition check: confirm the deck is free of loose panels, damaged edges, raised fasteners, protrusions, debris, contamination, uneven joints, punctures, and soft areas, or each is listed as a deficiency.
  • Underside condition check: confirm the accessible underside is free of missed fasteners, unsupported edges, excessive gaps, water staining, daylight, delamination, mold, and framing conflicts, or each is listed as a deficiency.
  • Slope check: confirm measured slope on each plane matches the roof plan or is recorded as a deviation.
  • Clearance check: confirm penetration and combustible-assembly clearances match the approved specification.
  • Documentation check: confirm the deficiency list, photographs, and readiness decision are complete and signed before Chapter 2 begins.

Complete the Roof-Readiness Documentation Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 2.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Slope Measurement Does Not Match the Roof Plan

Probable cause: the measurement was taken on the wrong plane, taken from an unlevel reference, or the framing was built to a different slope than the approved plan.
Confirmation: repeat the slope measurement on the identified plane using the approved method and a verified level reference.
Correction: if the remeasured slope confirms a deviation from the plan, record it as a deficiency and route it to the responsible authority for resolution before any roofing-system selection depends on the plan slope.
Reinspection: repeat the slope check on all planes after the deviation is resolved.

Water Staining Found on the Deck Underside

Probable cause: a prior roof leak, condensation from inadequate ventilation, or an active leak in progress.
Confirmation: inspect the extent, color, and apparent age of the staining, check for active moisture with an authorized moisture meter, and check attic ventilation conditions.
Correction: record the finding and its location on the deficiency list; do not cover a suspected active leak, wet insulation, or mold with roofing material. Route the finding to the responsible authority for further diagnosis, which may include the Chapter 18 leak-diagnosis procedure.
Reinspection: recheck the affected area only after the underlying moisture source is resolved.

Raised or Missing Deck Fasteners Found

Probable cause: under-driven or missed fasteners from the original sheathing installation, or fastener backout from moisture cycling.
Confirmation: visually and, where an approved surface position allows, physically confirm each raised or missing fastener location against the sheathing fastening schedule.
Correction: reset or replace each fastener using the type, length, and spacing specified in the fastening schedule; do not use a fastener not identified in the schedule.
Reinspection: recheck each corrected location for flush seating before it is recorded as complete.

Planned Penetration Does Not Match the Ventilation or Framing Plan

Probable cause: a field change was made without updating the roof or ventilation plan, or the penetration was mislocated during framing.
Confirmation: compare the observed penetration location and size to the current framing and ventilation plans and to any approved change documents.
Correction: record the discrepancy as a deficiency and route it to the responsible authority for an approved change document before Chapter 12 or Chapter 14 work proceeds at that location.
Reinspection: recheck the location once an approved change document is issued.

Clearance at a Chimney or Combustible Assembly Is Undersized

Probable cause: the assembly was framed without confirming the required clearance, or trim or blocking was added after framing that reduced the clearance.
Confirmation: remeasure the clearance directly against the approved specification from a safe position.
Correction: record the finding as a deficiency; do not proceed with roofing over the affected area until the responsible authority approves a correction or an alternate detail.
Reinspection: recheck the clearance once the correction or alternate detail is complete.

Inspection Positions Cannot Reach an Entire Roof Plane

Probable cause: the available ground, ladder, lift, attic, or scaffold positions do not provide a clear view of every section of a given plane.
Confirmation: review the positions identified in Step 3 against the roof sketch and identify the specific sections that remain unverified.
Correction: record the unverified sections on the deficiency list as an open item and issue a conditional-proceed decision limited to the verified sections, or arrange an additional approved position before issuing a full decision.
Reinspection: complete the inspection of the previously unverified sections once an approved position is available, then update the readiness decision.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a signed readiness decision and an organized record set, not a pile of loose notes.

  • Remove marking chalk residue from any surface where it could interfere with later adhesion, per the roofing-system specification
  • Dispose of removed debris using an approved method that does not discharge material from the roof edge or the ground below
  • Store leftover replacement fasteners for reuse in later authorized deck corrections
  • Compile the deficiency list, photographs, measurements, and readiness decision into the project record
  • Transfer the completed roof-readiness documentation to the location Chapter 2 will reference before establishing roof access

Performance Check

With the inspection complete, review the deficiency list and the Roof-Readiness Documentation Record against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm every accessible roof plane has been inspected from an approved position, deck material and fastening have been compared to the sheathing schedule, slope has been measured on every plane, all penetration and combustible-assembly clearances have been checked, every deficiency has a recorded location, condition, correction, responsible authority, and reinspection method, and authorized corrections have been completed and rechecked. Issue and sign the roofing-readiness decision before beginning Chapter 2. If any item cannot be verified from an available position, record it as an open item and issue a conditional-proceed decision rather than a full proceed decision.

Chapter Closeout

You have completed the first chapter in the roof-installation sequence: inspecting a roof deck and issuing a documented roofing-readiness decision. This chapter required you to inspect roof sheathing from safe positions (RF-001), identify damaged decking (RF-002), verify roof slope on each plane (RF-003), check roof edges and penetration clearances (RF-004), prepare the surface through authorized correction and debris removal (RF-005), and document roof readiness with a deficiency list, photographs, and a final decision (RF-006).

You now have a documented, signed roofing-readiness record that fixes the deck condition, slope, and feature locations every later chapter will build on. That record carries directly into the next chapter: establishing the roof-access and fall-protection system required before anyone steps onto this roof to install edge metal, membrane, underlayment, or shingles.

Store the completed deficiency list, photographs, and roofing-readiness decision with the project record. Chapter 2 requires this record before establishing roof access.
