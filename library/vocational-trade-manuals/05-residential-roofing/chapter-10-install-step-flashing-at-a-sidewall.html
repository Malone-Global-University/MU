Install Step Flashing at a Sidewall

Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to integrate individual step-flashing pieces with asphalt-shingle courses at a roof-to-sidewall intersection. The finished work product is a complete, course-by-course step-flashing assembly that begins at a properly installed kickout flashing, alternates one flashing piece per shingle course up the sidewall, and terminates in the approved headwall, counterflashing, or transition detail at the top of the wall.

You will confirm the roof-to-wall detail, verify that the wall drainage layer or approved counterflashing will protect the upper flashing legs, install or verify kickout flashing at the base of the wall, prepare individual step-flashing pieces to the specified dimensions, and alternate shingle courses and flashing pieces one at a time up the sidewall while keeping every piece within its required laps and fastening zone.

Step flashing cannot be corrected after the shingle field and siding conceal it. Every piece installed in this chapter becomes a permanent, inaccessible part of the roof-to-wall water path, so each piece must be verified before the next shingle course covers it.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-055 — Identify sidewall flashing locations, including the kickout point, wall drainage interface, and upper termination.
  • RF-056 — Install individual step-flashing pieces at the specified dimensions and fastening zone.
  • RF-057 — Lap each flashing piece with the shingle courses above and below it in correct shingle-lap order.
  • RF-058 — Protect the wall transition with correctly installed kickout flashing and upper-leg integration.
  • RF-059 — Integrate the wall drainage layer or approved counterflashing with the step-flashing upper legs.
  • RF-060 — Inspect the completed flashing sequence for laps, fastening, kickout direction, and drainage continuity.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter depends on the roof deck, underlayment, self-adhered membrane, layout, starter strips, and shingle-field work completed in Chapters 1 through 8, and it applies the valley principle from Chapter 9 that flashing must be integrated while the shingle field is installed, never inserted afterward. The corresponding wall-flashing skill for a roof-to-wall intersection that runs across the slope rather than along it is taught separately in Chapter 11, Headwall and Apron Flashing.

The step-flashing sequence you complete here feeds directly into the Chapter 20 capstone, where the same course-by-course method is applied to any sidewall condition included in the approved capstone design.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved roof-to-wall detail, step-flashing dimensions, metal compatibility, wall drainage or counterflashing sequence, kickout flashing, and siding clearance
  • installing or verifying kickout flashing at the base of the sidewall before standard step flashing begins
  • preparing individual step-flashing pieces to the specified length, width, bend, and corrosion resistance
  • alternating shingle courses and step-flashing pieces one course at a time up the sidewall
  • integrating the upper flashing legs with the wall drainage layer, counterflashing, or siding-system components
  • inspecting the completed sequence before it is concealed by siding or counterflashing

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • install headwall or apron flashing at a wall transition that runs across the roof slope; that work is taught in Chapter 11
  • insert step flashing beneath a completed shingle field; step flashing must be integrated course-by-course as the field shingles are installed
  • install or modify siding, trim, or wall-cladding components beyond the minimum integration required to protect the flashing upper legs
  • fasten through both the roof and wall legs of a flashing piece in a way that restricts movement or creates an exposed leakage path, unless the approved detail specifically requires it

Any sidewall condition that does not match the approved detail, including missing kickout provisions or an incompatible wall drainage system, must be recorded and referred to the responsible authority before flashing proceeds at that location.

Trade Note

Step flashing works because every piece is independent. Each piece can move slightly with normal building movement without opening a water path, because it laps freely with the shingle above and below it rather than being bonded into one continuous strip. Do not fasten a piece in a way that removes this independence, and do not substitute a continuous flashing strip for individual pieces unless an approved proprietary detail specifically allows it.

Concealment Standard

Photograph and record every flashing piece, lap, and the kickout condition before the wall drainage layer, counterflashing, siding, or upper shingle courses conceal it. A defect found after concealment requires removing finished work to correct; a defect found before concealment requires only removing the flashing piece.

Shop Tip

Cut and form all step-flashing pieces for the full sidewall run before starting installation. Working from a completed, inspected stack of pieces keeps the course-by-course pace steady and reduces the temptation to fasten a piece before its bend and dimensions are verified.

Common Field Fundamentals

Step flashing depends on maintaining the same shingle-lap water path used throughout this book: every upper layer sheds onto the layer below it. At a sidewall, that path runs from the wall drainage layer, down over the step-flashing wall leg, onto the step-flashing roof leg, and onto the shingle course below. Breaking this order at any single course creates a concealed entry point that will not appear until water finds it.

Kickout pointLower termination; diverts water away from the wall and into the gutter or roof edge
Course-by-course runOne step-flashing piece per shingle course, alternating up the wall
Upper leg integrationWall drainage layer or counterflashing laps over each wall leg
Upper terminationHeadwall, counterflashing, or approved transition at the top of the wall

Kickout Flashing Before Standard Step Flashing

Install or verify the required kickout flashing at the lower roof-to-wall termination before the first standard step-flashing piece is installed. The kickout piece directs both roof runoff and wall drainage away from the wall base and into the gutter or off the roof edge; without it, water is directed behind the gutter and into the wall assembly at the most vulnerable point in the entire sidewall run.

Critical Safety Cue — Falls at Sidewall Transitions

Sidewall flashing work concentrates a worker's attention on a narrow strip next to a vertical wall, often near a roof edge or valley. Maintain the fall-protection connection required for this roof at all times during cutting, forming, and fastening, and do not lean out from the wall to reach a flashing piece without repositioning your anchor point or work platform first.

Safety Cue — Sheet-Metal Edges

Freshly cut step-flashing and kickout pieces have sharp edges and corners. Wear cut-resistant gloves while cutting, forming, staging, and installing metal, and deburr any edge that will remain exposed to bare hands during installation.

Manufacturer Cue — Metal and Fastener Compatibility

Use only the metal type and fastener specified for this project's corrosion-compatibility requirement. Do not place incompatible metals in contact with each other or with dissimilar wall or roofing components, and do not substitute a fastener not identified in the approved fastening schedule.

Code Alert — Kickout and Wall-Drainage Requirements

Kickout-flashing geometry and the required relationship between step flashing and the wall drainage layer vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the specific kickout dimensions, required WRB lap, and any inspection hold point with the approved detail and the authority having jurisdiction before concealing this work.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review the approved roof-to-wall detail, step-flashing dimensions, metal compatibility, wall WRB or counterflashing sequence, kickout flashing, siding clearance, and fastening restrictions. Confirm the revision or date on each document before using it as a reference, and record the confirmation on the governing-documents table above.
  2. Confirm that the sidewall, roof deck, underlayment, membrane, and lower drainage outlet are ready. Verify these items were accepted under their governing chapter before beginning flashing work at this sidewall.
  3. Verify that the wall drainage layer can lap over the upper legs of the step flashing or that approved counterflashing will provide equivalent protection. Identify which method applies to this sidewall and record it on the flashing record before cutting any pieces.
  4. Install or verify the required kickout flashing at the lower roof-to-wall termination before standard step flashing begins. Confirm the kickout piece directs water away from the wall base and into the gutter or off the roof edge, not behind either.
  5. Establish the first shingle course and step-flashing position from the eave reference. Confirm the course position matches the layout lines established in Chapter 6 as they cross the sidewall.
  6. Prepare individual step-flashing pieces to the specified length, width, bend, and corrosion resistance. Cut and form the full run of pieces needed for this sidewall before beginning installation, and reject any piece that does not meet the specified dimensions or bend angle.
  7. Position the first flashing piece so its lower edge drains onto the lower roofing layer or kickout flashing. Confirm the roof leg bears fully on the roof deck and the wall leg sits flat against the sidewall before fastening.
  8. Fasten each step-flashing piece only in the approved upper area and to the permitted substrate. Use the fastener type, quantity, and location specified in the fastening schedule, and keep fasteners out of the lower, exposed portion of each piece.
  9. Do not fasten through both the roof and wall legs in a way that restricts movement or creates exposed leakage paths unless the approved detail requires it. Confirm each piece can move independently with normal building movement before covering it with the next shingle course.
  10. Install the corresponding shingle course over the roof leg of the flashing. Fasten the shingle using the fastening zone and pattern established in Chapter 8, keeping shingle fasteners out of the flashing's prohibited zones identified in Step 13.
  11. Place the next step-flashing piece over the shingle with the required vertical and horizontal lap. Confirm the lap dimension against the approved detail before fastening the piece.
  12. Continue alternating shingle and flashing pieces one course at a time. Do not install two shingle courses before placing the intervening flashing piece, and do not stage multiple flashing pieces ahead of the shingle courses that support them.
  13. Keep shingle joints, nails, and cut edges out of prohibited flashing zones. Verify no shingle fastener penetrates the step-flashing metal and no shingle joint lands directly over a flashing lap.
  14. Fit flashing around wall transitions without flattening the upturned leg or creating reverse laps. Form each piece individually at inside corners, outside corners, or changes in wall plane rather than bending a straight piece to force a fit.
  15. Integrate the upper flashing legs beneath WRB, counterflashing, or siding-system components in the approved shingle-lap sequence. Confirm the wall drainage layer or counterflashing overlaps each wall leg from above, never from below.
  16. Maintain required siding and trim clearance above the roof surface. Measure the clearance at representative points along the sidewall and record any shortfall as a deficiency.
  17. Complete the upper termination with the approved headwall, counterflashing, or transition detail. Confirm the top of the step-flashing run ties into the governing detail for the upper wall or roof condition, coordinating with Chapter 11 where a headwall condition is also present.
  18. Inspect kickout direction, individual laps, flashing height, fastener placement, WRB integration, siding clearance, and drainage continuity. Complete this inspection before any siding, trim, or counterflashing conceals the assembly.
  19. Correct omitted pieces, short laps, exposed fasteners, blocked kickouts, or reverse-lapped wall layers. Remove and replace any piece that fails inspection rather than adding sealant to compensate for a missing or misplaced piece.
  20. Photograph the flashing sequence before siding or counterflashing conceals it. Label each photograph with its location along the sidewall and attach it to the flashing record.

Common Sidewall Flashing Checks

Apply the same checks to every step-flashing piece along the sidewall, regardless of run length or wall configuration.

  • Kickout check: confirm the kickout piece is installed and directs water away from the wall base before any standard step-flashing piece is installed.
  • Piece-dimension check: confirm every step-flashing piece matches the specified length, width, bend, and corrosion resistance.
  • Fastening-zone check: confirm every fastener is placed only in the approved upper area and does not restrict piece movement.
  • Course-alternation check: confirm one flashing piece was installed per shingle course, with no course skipped or doubled.
  • Upper-leg integration check: confirm the wall drainage layer or counterflashing laps over each wall leg from above.
  • Documentation check: confirm photographs of the completed sequence are attached to the flashing record before concealment.

Complete the Sidewall Flashing Inspection Record below before siding, trim, or counterflashing conceals this work.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Water Staining Appears on the Interior Wall Below a Sidewall

Probable cause: a missing or misdirected kickout flashing, a skipped step-flashing piece, an incorrect lap, or a wall drainage layer that does not overlap the flashing upper legs.
Confirmation: inspect the accessible interior wall cavity and trace the stain upslope; if siding or counterflashing already conceals the flashing, remove only the minimum material required to expose the suspected course.
Correction: replace or reposition the failed piece, reestablish the correct lap and fastening, and reinstall disturbed shingles and wall components in the original order.
Reinspection: verify the corrected course under the same acceptance standards used during original installation before re-concealing the work.

A Step-Flashing Piece Will Not Sit Flat Against the Wall

Probable cause: an incorrect bend angle, a wall surface that is not flat or plumb at that location, or debris trapped behind the piece.
Confirmation: remove the piece, check the bend angle against the specified dimension, and inspect the wall surface for irregularities.
Correction: re-form the piece to the correct bend angle or fabricate a replacement piece; do not force a misshapen piece into place with excess fastening or sealant.
Reinspection: confirm the replacement piece sits flat on both legs before installing the corresponding shingle course.

Fastener Penetrates Both the Roof and Wall Legs

Probable cause: a fastener driven at the bend to save time, or a piece positioned incorrectly during installation.
Confirmation: inspect each installed piece for a fastener crossing the bend line, and compare against the approved detail to confirm whether this condition is permitted.
Correction: if not permitted by the approved detail, remove the fastener and the piece, and reinstall using only the approved upper fastening zone.
Reinspection: confirm the corrected piece retains independent movement before the next shingle course is installed.

Wall Drainage Layer Does Not Lap Over the Flashing Upper Legs

Probable cause: the WRB was installed before the flashing sequence without leaving enough material to fold down over the wall legs, or the WRB was tucked behind the flashing instead of over it.
Confirmation: inspect the WRB-to-flashing relationship at representative points along the sidewall.
Correction: extend, patch, or reposition the WRB so it laps over each wall leg from above, or install approved counterflashing to provide equivalent protection, per the approved detail.
Reinspection: recheck the WRB or counterflashing relationship at every corrected location before siding is installed.

Siding Clearance Above the Roof Surface Is Undersized

Probable cause: siding installed without confirming the required clearance, or a flashing piece that projects higher than specified.
Confirmation: measure the clearance at multiple points along the sidewall against the approved specification.
Correction: trim or reposition the siding, or correct the flashing height, per the approved detail; do not close the clearance gap with sealant as a substitute for the specified dimension.
Reinspection: recheck clearance at all corrected points before final documentation.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a photographed, inspected flashing assembly and an organized record set, not loose metal offcuts on the roof or ground.

  • Remove metal offcuts, spent fasteners, and packaging from the roof surface and the ground below using an approved method
  • Store unused step-flashing stock for reuse in later sidewall runs or authorized repairs
  • Remove sharp offcuts from the work area before leaving the site
  • Compile the flashing record, governing-documents table, acceptance-standards results, and photographs into the project record
  • Transfer the completed record to the location Chapter 11 or the Chapter 20 capstone will reference for the remaining wall-flashing work
