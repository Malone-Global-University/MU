Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install a continuous synthetic underlayment system over a deck that has already passed inspection, received required eave drip edge, and received required self-adhered membrane at eaves, valleys, and vulnerable transitions. The finished work product is a fully covered roof plane with correctly shingle-lapped underlayment courses, specified end and side laps, approved fastening, and a documented record of product, course layout, and inspection results.

You will establish a first-course reference parallel to the eave, stage and orient rolls correctly, install the first course and successive upper courses with the specified overlap, increase laps at low slopes and special details, stagger vertical joints where required, cut around penetrations, complete the rake drip-edge installation from Chapter 3 in the correct relationship to the underlayment, repair any damage, and secure the finished surface against the expected exposure period.

This chapter depends directly on Chapter 3's eave metal and Chapter 4's self-adhered membrane, and it produces the surface that Chapter 6 will use to establish shingle-layout control lines. No layout, starter strip, or shingle work may proceed over underlayment that has not passed this chapter's inspection.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-025 — Roll and position synthetic underlayment courses using the approved reference line, orientation, and course direction.
  • RF-026 — Maintain horizontal courses aligned to the eave and to established control references.
  • RF-027 — Overlap seams at the specified end and side lap dimensions, including increased laps at low slopes, valleys, hips, ridges, walls, and penetrations.
  • RF-028 — Fasten underlayment using the approved fastener type, zone, and spacing.
  • RF-029 — Protect underlayment from wrinkles, stretching, trapped debris, and damage during installation.
  • RF-030 — Inspect the completed surface for continuous water-shedding coverage and document product, layout, and repairs.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 1 established a documented, ready deck. Chapter 2 established compliant roof access. Chapter 3 installed eave drip edge and staged rake drip edge. Chapter 4 installed self-adhered membrane at eaves, valleys, and vulnerable transitions. This chapter installs the continuous underlayment field that ties those layers together and completes the rake drip-edge installation in its correct sequence relative to the underlayment.

The underlayment surface you complete here is the reference surface for Chapter 6's layout lines, Chapter 7's starter strips, and every shingle, valley, and flashing chapter that follows. A gap, reverse lap, or unsecured sheet left in this chapter becomes a concealed defect once shingles cover it.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the underlayment manufacturer's instructions, slope limits, fastening requirements, exposure limits, lap dimensions, repair methods, and roof-covering compatibility
  • confirming that deck inspection, required eave metal, self-adhered membrane, and concealed repairs are complete before underlayment begins
  • establishing the first-course reference and installing horizontal courses from the low roof edge upward with the specified end and side laps
  • increasing laps or applying special details at low slopes, valleys, hips, ridges, walls, and penetrations
  • completing the rake drip-edge installation from Chapter 3 where the approved system requires metal over the underlayment
  • repairing tears, punctures, wrinkles, loose edges, and misplaced fasteners with approved patches
  • inspecting the completed surface and securing it for the expected exposure period

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin underlayment over a deck, eave metal, or membrane that has not passed the applicable prior-chapter inspection
  • install shingle-layout lines, starter strips, or field shingles; those tasks begin in Chapter 6 and later chapters
  • install rake drip edge in a location or sequence that conflicts with the approved roof-edge detail established in Chapter 3
  • leave exposed deck area beyond what can be covered and secured within the current work period and forecast weather window

Any underlayment surface that fails this chapter's inspection must be corrected, patched, or re-laid under this chapter's authorized methods before it is recorded complete and before Chapter 6 layout work begins over it.

Trade Note

Underlayment installation is a shingle-lap system in miniature: every upper course must shed water onto the course below it, and every lap direction must be checked against the prevailing water path, not against what is convenient to install. Treat a reverse lap here the same way you would treat one in the finished shingle field — as a defect that must be corrected before the next layer covers it.

Sequence Standard

Do not begin this chapter until Chapter 1 deck inspection, Chapter 2 roof access, Chapter 3 eave drip edge, and Chapter 4 self-adhered membrane are each complete and accepted. Where the approved system requires rake metal over the underlayment, stage the prepared rake pieces now and install them immediately after the applicable underlayment course, not after the entire roof plane is covered.

Shop Tip

Unroll underlayment in controlled lengths and smooth it from the center outward. Chasing wrinkles after a full course is fastened costs far more time than preventing them during placement.

Common Field Fundamentals

Underlayment installation follows the same shingle-lap water-flow principle that governs every layer in this book: each course must shed water onto the course beneath it, from the low roof edge to the ridge. Establishing a straight first-course reference, maintaining consistent laps, and fastening only in approved zones are the fundamentals that keep that principle intact across an entire roof plane.

Course Installation and Lap Control

Begin at the low roof edge and align the first course to the required eave relationship. Unroll a controlled length without walking backward toward an unprotected edge, and smooth the sheet without stretching, wrinkling, or trapping debris. Install upper courses so each upper sheet overlaps the lower sheet by the specified side lap, and increase laps or apply required special details at low slopes, valleys, hips, ridges, walls, and penetrations.

Critical Safety Cue — Fall Protection Remains Connected

Do not disconnect from the fall-protection system established in Chapter 2 while unrolling, smoothing, or fastening underlayment. Do not walk backward toward an unprotected edge while feeding out a roll. Reposition anchorage and lifeline connections as work progresses across the roof plane.

Field Safety Cue — Exposed-Deck Limits

Keep the exposed, uncovered deck area within what can be made weather-resistant before conditions change or the work period ends. Stop work and secure the installed underlayment for rain, wind, or approaching darkness rather than leaving deck sections partially covered.

Manufacturer Cue — Face Orientation and Slope Limits

Install the underlayment with the manufacturer-designated face and course direction. Confirm the product's minimum roof-slope rating before installation; do not install a standard-slope product on a plane below its rated minimum without the manufacturer's low-slope detail.

Code Alert — Fastening and Exposure Limits

Local code may set additional requirements for underlayment fastening density, permitted exposure time before roof covering, or ice-dam protection beyond the self-adhered membrane installed in Chapter 4. Verify the adopted code and permit conditions before finalizing the fastening schedule and work sequence.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review the underlayment manufacturer's instructions, roof-slope limits, fastening requirements, exposure limits, lap dimensions, repair methods, and compatibility with the roof covering. Confirm the product's minimum slope rating against every roof plane measured in Chapter 1, and record the confirmed lap dimensions on the governing-documents table above.
  2. Confirm that deck inspection, required eave metal, self-adhered membrane, and concealed repairs are complete. Locate the Chapter 1, Chapter 3, and Chapter 4 acceptance records. If any is missing or shows an open item, stop this chapter and route the project back to the responsible chapter before continuing.
  3. Verify weather conditions and limit exposed deck area to what can be protected during the work period. Check the forecast against the estimated time to cover and secure each roof plane, and plan the work sequence so no section remains uncovered beyond that window.
  4. Establish the first-course reference parallel to the eave. Measure from the eave edge using the eave relationship specified by the roofing-system specification, and snap a chalk line across the full width of the roof plane.
  5. Stage rolls so they cannot slide, blow away, overload the roof, or block access. Distribute rolls within the approved roof-load limits established for this project and keep them clear of the access path established in Chapter 2.
  6. Orient the underlayment with the manufacturer-designated face and course direction. Confirm orientation on the roll label before cutting or unrolling the first sheet.
  7. Begin at the low roof edge and align the first course to the required eave relationship. Position the sheet edge to the chalked reference line from Step 4 before fastening.
  8. Unroll a controlled length without walking backward toward an unprotected edge. Unroll in sections short enough to control, and maintain a fall-protection connection throughout.
  9. Smooth the sheet without stretching, wrinkling, or trapping debris. Work from the center of the sheet outward toward both edges and both ends.
  10. Install temporary and permanent fasteners only in approved zones and at the required spacing. Use the fastener type and spacing identified in the manufacturer's instructions and confirmed on the governing-documents table.
  11. Continue the first course while maintaining alignment and end laps. Check alignment against the chalked reference line at regular intervals and correct drift immediately.
  12. Install upper courses so each upper sheet overlaps the lower sheet by the specified amount. Measure the side lap at multiple points along each course before fastening.
  13. Increase laps or use required special details at low slopes, valleys, hips, ridges, walls, and penetrations. Apply the manufacturer's special-detail instructions at each identified condition rather than the standard field lap.
  14. Keep vertical end joints staggered where required and oriented to reduce water entry. Offset end joints between adjacent courses according to the manufacturer's stagger requirement.
  15. Cut neatly around penetrations and integrate the sheet with existing membrane in shingle-lap order. Trim underlayment so the upper layer sheds onto the lower membrane and roofing layers at every penetration.
  16. Complete the rake drip-edge installation from Chapter 3 where the approved system requires metal over the underlayment. Install the prepared rake pieces staged in Chapter 3 immediately after the underlayment course that must pass beneath them, maintaining the approved edge and underlayment relationship.
  17. Fold, lap, or terminate underlayment at ridges and hips according to the ventilation and roofing plan. Leave the termination in the condition required for the ridge-vent or ridge-cap work planned in Chapter 13 or Chapter 14.
  18. Repair tears, punctures, wrinkles, loose edges, and misplaced fasteners using approved patches and fasteners. Use the manufacturer-approved patch dimensions and method, and record each repair location.
  19. Inspect the entire surface for exposed deck, reverse laps, insufficient overlap, loose sheets, trip hazards, and drainage interruptions. Walk the full accessible surface using the fall-protection system, checking against the Acceptance Standards table above.
  20. Secure the underlayment for the expected exposure period and forecast conditions. Confirm all fastening is complete and no section remains loose before ending the work period.
  21. Photograph and record product, course layout, fastening, laps, repairs, and completion time. Attach photographs to the installation record and complete the documentation form established in Step 1.

Common Underlayment Installation Checks

Apply the same checks to every roof plane covered in this chapter, regardless of plane size or configuration.

  • Prerequisite check: confirm Chapter 1, Chapter 3, and Chapter 4 acceptance records exist before starting any course.
  • Alignment check: confirm the first course matches the chalked reference line and required eave relationship.
  • Lap check: confirm end laps and side laps meet the specified dimension at every joint, including special-detail zones.
  • Fastening check: confirm fasteners fall only within approved zones at the required spacing, with no missed or loose fasteners.
  • Surface check: confirm the completed surface is free of wrinkles, trapped debris, exposed deck, and reverse laps.
  • Rake drip-edge check: confirm rake metal required over the underlayment was installed immediately after the applicable course, in the correct relationship.
  • Documentation check: confirm product, layout, fastening, laps, repairs, and completion time are recorded and signed before Chapter 6 begins.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Course Drifts Away From the Reference Line

Probable cause: the sheet was not aligned to the chalked reference before fastening, or the roll was unrolled with a slight twist that accumulated over the course length.
Confirmation: measure the offset between the installed sheet edge and the chalked reference line at multiple points along the course.
Correction: remove fasteners over the drifted section, realign the sheet to the reference line, and refasten within the approved zone and spacing.
Reinspection: recheck alignment at the same measurement points after correction.

Side Lap Is Narrower Than Specified

Probable cause: the upper course was positioned by eye rather than measured, or the lower course's exposed edge was not clearly marked before the upper course was placed.
Confirmation: measure the actual side lap at several points along the affected course length.
Correction: lift and reposition the upper course to achieve the specified lap, or install a manufacturer-approved supplemental strip if the sheet cannot be repositioned without damage.
Reinspection: remeasure the lap at the same points and record the corrected dimension.

Wrinkles or Fishmouths Appear After Fastening

Probable cause: the sheet was stretched during placement, smoothed from one end rather than the center outward, or fastened before wrinkles were worked out.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area visually and check whether the wrinkle traps water or debris under the next course.
Correction: release the nearby fasteners, smooth the sheet from the center outward, and refasten; apply a manufacturer-approved patch if the sheet was creased or damaged during correction.
Reinspection: recheck the corrected area under raking light or by hand for remaining wrinkles before the next course is installed over it.

Fastener Found Outside the Approved Zone

Probable cause: the fastening pattern was not marked before installation, or a fastener was placed to control a wrinkle rather than following the specified spacing.
Confirmation: compare the fastener location to the approved fastening-zone diagram in the manufacturer's instructions.
Correction: remove the misplaced fastener, patch the resulting hole per the manufacturer's repair method, and install a replacement fastener within the approved zone.
Reinspection: recheck the patched location and the corrected fastener for proper seating.

Rake Drip Edge Was Not Ready When the Adjacent Course Was Completed

Probable cause: rake metal preparation from Chapter 3 was not staged in time, or the installer proceeded past the applicable course without stopping for the rake installation.
Confirmation: compare the current course progress to the roof-edge detail confirmed in Chapter 3 to identify which course the rake metal was required to follow.
Correction: stop further course installation at the affected rake, install the prepared rake pieces in the correct relationship to the completed underlayment course, and only then resume upper courses.
Reinspection: verify the completed rake-to-underlayment relationship against the Chapter 3 approved detail before continuing.

Exposed Deck Remains at the End of the Work Period

Probable cause: the covered area planned for the work period exceeded what could actually be completed and secured, or weather changed faster than forecast.
Confirmation: inspect the roof plane for any section without complete, fastened underlayment coverage.
Correction: install temporary weather protection over the exposed section using an approved method, or complete and fasten the remaining course before ending the work period if conditions and time allow.
Reinspection: recheck the temporary protection or completed coverage before leaving the site.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a fully secured underlayment surface and an organized installation record, not loose rolls and unlogged repairs.

  • Remove cutoffs, release liner, and packaging from the roof surface and the ground below, keeping the drop zone clear during removal
  • Store unused underlayment rolls and rake drip-edge stock protected from weather for the next work period
  • Return fastening tools to their approved storage and inspect them per the manufacturer's instructions
  • Compile product information, course layout, laps, fastening, repairs, and photographs into the project record
  • Transfer the completed underlayment installation record to the location Chapter 6 will reference before establishing layout lines

Field Terms

First-Course Reference Line
The chalked horizontal line parallel to the eave that controls the alignment of the first underlayment course.
End Lap
The overlap between the end of one underlayment sheet and the beginning of the next sheet in the same course.
Side Lap
The overlap between an upper course and the lower course beneath it.
Manufacturer-Designated Face
The printed or textured side of the underlayment that must face outward per the product instructions.
Special-Detail Lap
An increased or modified lap dimension required at low slopes, valleys, hips, ridges, walls, or penetrations.
Fastening Zone
The approved area of the sheet width where temporary and permanent fasteners may be placed.
Fishmouth
A raised, unsealed gap in the underlayment surface, often at a wrinkle or improperly lapped joint, that can trap water or debris.
Course Direction
The direction in which courses progress up the roof plane, established by the manufacturer instructions and first-course reference.
Rake Drip Edge
Edge metal along the sloped roof edge, installed over the underlayment where the approved roofing system requires that relationship.
Exposure Limit
The maximum time or area an underlayment product may remain exposed to weather before roof covering is installed.
