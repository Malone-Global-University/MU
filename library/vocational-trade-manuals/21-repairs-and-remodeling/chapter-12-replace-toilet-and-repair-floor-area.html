Job Overview

This project qualifies you to remove an existing floor-mounted toilet, inspect the flange and surrounding floor, complete a limited non-structural floor repair, install a new seal, and reinstall or replace the toilet in the same approved location. The finished work is a stable, level, leak-free toilet installation over a sound repaired floor, with the flange condition verified, the seal fresh, and every connection tested before the room returns to service.

You will shut off and drain the fixture, disconnect and remove the toilet using a controlled lifting procedure, inspect the flange and floor for damage, corrosion, or contamination, complete only the floor repair permitted within this chapter's limits, install a new seal and mounting hardware, set and secure the replacement or reinstalled toilet, reconnect the water supply, and perform repeated flush and leak tests before final sealant is applied.

This chapter builds directly on Chapter 1's inspection and readiness process and Chapter 3's selective-demolition controls. It assumes the existing-condition report and approved scope for this bathroom already identify whether the flange is sound, whether a professional flange repair has already occurred, and whether floor damage exceeds this chapter's repair limit. The stable, tested toilet installation completed here becomes one of the fixture updates the Chapter 20 capstone may reuse for an alternate powder-room refresh.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RR-067 — Remove a floor-mounted toilet using a controlled shutoff, drain, disconnect, and lifting procedure.
  • RR-068 — Inspect the flange area for height, material, attachment, corrosion, cracking, and floor-plane condition.
  • RR-069 — Repair minor accepted floor damage around the flange using a matched subfloor and finish-floor detail without cutting or notching joists.
  • RR-070 — Set a new toilet seal centered and undamaged over a verified flange and finished floor.
  • RR-071 — Reinstall or set a replacement toilet with even bowl contact, progressive hardware tightening, and correct tank and supply reconnection.
  • RR-072 — Test flushing, refill, and every connection for leaks, and document the completed installation.

Where This Skill Fits

Toilet removal exposes one of the most consequential concealed conditions in residential repair work: the flange and the floor structure beneath it. A rushed reinstallation over an unverified flange or unresolved floor damage produces a leak that can travel to the room below long before it is noticed. This chapter applies the same investigation-before-repair discipline taught in Chapter 1 and the same selective-demolition and concealed-condition controls taught in Chapter 3, focused on one plumbing fixture and the floor it depends on.

The floor-repair method taught here is deliberately limited. It assumes joists and blocking are sound and does not authorize any structural correction; any broken flange, damaged drain, or floor condition beyond this chapter's limit is referred out before the project continues. The stable, leak-tested installation produced here supplies the plumbing fixture record the Chapter 19 punch-list inspection and the Chapter 20 capstone both depend on.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing toilet type, rough-in, flange position, supply, shutoff, floor finish, and clearances before beginning work
  • protecting the access route, bathroom floor, tub or shower, vanity, walls, and doorway
  • shutting off water, draining the fixture, and disconnecting the supply connector under controlled sanitation procedures
  • removing the toilet using team assistance or a lifting device and placing it on protected containment
  • inspecting the flange height, material, attachment, corrosion, cracking, drain condition, and surrounding floor plane, moisture, and contamination
  • completing a limited floor-repair boundary around a sound or professionally repaired flange, without cutting or notching joists
  • installing a new seal, setting the toilet, reconnecting the water supply, and testing for leaks and proper flush
  • applying approved base sealant only after testing and while preserving any required inspection opening
  • documenting flange condition, floor repair, seal, hardware, tests, products, and final acceptance

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • relocate the drain, alter the vent, or perform structural framing repair; this chapter assumes the flange and drain are sound or have already been professionally repaired
  • cut, notch, or otherwise modify joists or blocking to correct a floor condition
  • continue past a broken or loose flange, damaged drain, widespread rot, structural damage, sewage contamination, or mold without a completed and inspected professional repair first
  • conceal the toilet base or apply final sealant before flush and leak testing is complete
  • remove extensive contaminated material; a contamination condition beyond a limited, contained cleanup is referred to a qualified remediation professional

Any flange or floor condition identified during inspection that exceeds this chapter's repair limit must be referred to a licensed plumber or other qualified professional, repaired, and inspected before the toilet is reinstalled.

Trade Note

A toilet reinstallation is judged by what happens under the base, not by how clean the finished caulk line looks. A bowl that rocks even slightly on a level floor, or a base seal applied before the leak test is complete, hides the exact failure this chapter is built to prevent.

Setup Standard

Do not lift the toilet (Step 10 forward) until the water is shut off, the fixture is drained, and the supply connector is disconnected and contained (Steps 4–6). Do not reinstall the toilet (Step 24 forward) until the flange and floor inspection (Steps 13–14) and any required professional repair are complete and accepted.

Shop Tip

Seal the open drain immediately after lifting the toilet, using an approved removable method such as a rag stuffed loosely in the opening or a test-ball plug. An unsealed drain opening allows sewer gas into the room and lets debris fall into the line while you are inspecting the flange.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every toilet replacement in this chapter follows the same discipline: verify the shutoff before disconnecting, verify the flange before repairing the floor, verify the floor before setting the seal, and verify the seal before applying final sealant. Skipping ahead in this sequence is the most common cause of a leak that is discovered only after the room is closed up and back in use.

Sound Flange vs. Referral Condition

A sound flange sits at or slightly above the finished floor, shows intact slots and unbroken material, and is firmly attached to the drain pipe with no rocking or corrosion at the attachment point. A broken, cracked, corroded-through, or loose flange, a flange set noticeably below the finished floor, or any sign of a damaged drain pipe below it is a referral condition: stop, do not proceed with the floor repair or reinstallation, and have a licensed plumber complete and have inspected the required flange or drain repair before this chapter's work continues.

Measurement Cue — Confirm the Rough-In Before Ordering or Reinstalling

Measure the rough-in dimension from the finished wall to the centerline of the closet bolts before removing the existing toilet, and confirm the replacement toilet is rated for that exact rough-in. Do not estimate the rough-in from the toilet's outer dimensions or from memory of a similar bathroom.

Critical Safety Cue — Sanitary Control at Every Step

Treat all water inside the tank and bowl, and any water or debris near the drain opening, as contaminated. Wear disposable gloves throughout disconnection, lifting, and cleanup, seal the open drain immediately after lifting the toilet, and wash hands and sanitize tools before handling other materials. Stop and contact a licensed plumber for any active sewage backup, standing contaminated water beyond the fixture, or a drain that will not accept a temporary seal.

Field Safety Cue — Lifting and Floor Protection

A toilet is heavy, off-balance, and fragile. Use team lifting or a mechanical lifting aid, lift with the legs rather than the back, and place the removed toilet on protected containment away from traffic paths. Do not set the toilet down directly on an unprotected finished floor or drag it across flooring.

Code Alert — Verify Permit and Referral Requirements Locally

A straightforward like-for-like toilet replacement in the same location typically does not require a permit, but flange repair, drain repair, or a change in fixture type may trigger permit or inspection requirements in some jurisdictions. Verify the local requirement before proceeding, and complete any required plumbing inspection before the base seal is applied.

E. Preparation and Removal (Steps 1–10)

  1. Review toilet type, rough-in, flange position, supply, shutoff, floor finish, subfloor condition, clearances, ventilation, lifting plan, and permit requirements. Record the rough-in dimension and existing toilet model on the Reference and Rough-In Record before disconnecting anything.
  2. Confirm the project does not require unapproved drain relocation, vent alteration, structural framing repair, or extensive contaminated-material removal. Compare the task against the approved scope from Chapter 1 and stop if any of these conditions is suspected.
  3. Protect the access route, bathroom floor, tub or shower, vanity, walls, and doorway. Confirm the Chapter 2 containment and floor protection are in place before disconnecting the supply.
  4. Shut off the water and verify the valve controls flow. Close the shutoff, then operate the fill valve or flush handle to confirm no additional water enters the tank.
  5. Flush and remove remaining tank and bowl water using a controlled sanitation procedure. Flush to empty the tank, then use a sponge and bucket to remove remaining bowl and tank water, wearing disposable gloves throughout.
  6. Disconnect the supply connector and contain residual water. Place a towel or shallow pan beneath the connection before loosening it, and disconnect at the shutoff and at the fill valve.
  7. Disconnect or remove the tank first where the approved lifting method requires it. Support the tank while removing its mounting bolts, and set it on protected containment separate from the bowl.
  8. Remove caps, nuts, and other mounting components while controlling corrosion and sharp edges. Apply penetrating lubricant to corroded nuts if needed and work slowly to avoid cracking the bowl base.
  9. Break the seal without rocking against fragile flooring or damaging the flange. Rock the bowl gently side to side only enough to break the wax seal, supporting its weight throughout.
  10. Lift the toilet with team assistance or a lifting device and place it on protected containment. Lift with the legs, keep the opening upright to avoid spilling residual water, and set it down immediately on the prepared containment area.

F. Drain Protection and Flange Inspection (Steps 11–15)

  1. Seal the open drain temporarily using an approved removable method that prevents gas and debris entry. Install a rag, test-ball plug, or other removable seal immediately after the toilet is lifted.
  2. Remove the old seal and mounting debris without dropping material into the drain. Scrape old wax and debris onto disposable material, working away from the open drain.
  3. Inspect flange height, material, attachment, cracks, corrosion, slots, drain condition, floor plane, finish flooring, underlayment, subfloor, moisture, odor, and contamination. Record each finding using a moisture meter and flashlight, and photograph the exposed flange and floor area.
  4. Stop for broken or loose flange, damaged drain, widespread rot, structural damage, sewage contamination, mold, or floor repair beyond the approved limit. Do not proceed to floor repair or reinstallation until a licensed plumber or other qualified professional resolves the condition.
  5. Have required flange or plumbing repairs completed and inspected before proceeding. Obtain and file the completed repair documentation before continuing to Section G.

G. Limited Floor Repair (Steps 16–21)

  1. Define the limited floor-repair boundary and remove only unsound finish, underlayment, or subfloor through the approved method. Mark the repair boundary centered on the flange and no larger than the area of confirmed unsound material.
  2. Inspect joists and blocking without cutting or notching them. Confirm joists and blocking are sound; if they are not, stop and refer the condition to a qualified professional.
  3. Install approved blocking and matching subfloor patch only where the repair design permits. Cut the patch to the marked boundary and dry-fit it before fastening.
  4. Fasten the patch with controlled length and restore underlayment, waterproofing, or finish-floor layers required by the existing assembly. Use fasteners into verified framing only, at the length and spacing specified for the patch material.
  5. Verify the finished surface is flat, sound, dry, clean, and compatible with the toilet base. Check the patched area with a level and confirm moisture content is within the acceptable range for the finish material.
  6. Confirm the flange position and finished-floor relationship meet the approved installation. Verify the flange remains at or slightly above the new finished floor surface after the patch is installed.

H. Seal, Set, and Reconnect (Steps 22–29)

  1. Inspect or replace mounting hardware using compatible components. Install new closet bolts upright and square in the flange slots.
  2. Prepare the new toilet and verify all manufacturer-supplied parts, tank components, gasket, seat, and mounting instructions. Lay out all hardware before beginning the seal installation.
  3. Install the specified new seal centered and undamaged. Remove the temporary drain plug immediately before setting the seal in place on the flange.
  4. Lower the toilet vertically over the mounting bolts without twisting the seal. Align the bolt holes with the closet bolts and lower the bowl straight down in a single motion.
  5. Seat the bowl evenly and verify full base contact. Press down evenly on the bowl rim, checking that the base contacts the floor on all sides without rocking.
  6. Install and tighten mounting hardware progressively without cracking the porcelain or distorting the flange. Alternate tightening each nut in small increments until snug; do not overtighten.
  7. Shim only with approved noncompressible material where required and do not use fastener force to pull a rocking bowl down. If the bowl rocks after seating, stop, insert an approved shim at the low point, and retighten.
  8. Reconnect the tank, supply connector, and seat according to the product instructions. Install the tank-to-bowl connection first, then connect the new supply connector at the shutoff and fill valve.

I. Testing, Documentation, and Sealing (Steps 30–35)

  1. Restore water slowly and inspect the shutoff, supply, tank, fill valve, bowl connection, and floor. Open the shutoff partially first, checking every connection before opening it fully.
  2. Perform repeated flush, refill, dye or other approved leak tests while observing the base and room below where accessible. Flush the toilet at least three times, checking the base, tank, and bowl connections after each cycle, and use a dye tablet to check for a hidden tank-to-bowl leak.
  3. Verify stable mounting, proper refill, complete flush, shutoff operation, and absence of leakage or movement. Confirm the bowl remains stable under normal use pressure and the tank refills to the marked level without running continuously.
  4. Apply approved base sealant only after testing and while preserving any required inspection or leak-detection opening. Leave the specified gap at the rear of the base if the approved scope requires a leak-detection opening.
  5. Complete required plumbing inspection and sanitation cleanup. Schedule and pass any required inspection, and sanitize all tools and the surrounding floor area.
  6. Document flange condition, floor repair, seal, hardware, tests, products, and final acceptance. Complete the Fixture Replacement Checklist and file it with the photographs taken in Section F.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed installation using the same checks regardless of whether the toilet was reinstalled or replaced.

  • Flange check: confirm the flange was inspected and any deficiency was professionally repaired and inspected before reinstallation.
  • Floor-repair check: confirm the floor patch is within the approved boundary, flush, dry, and fastened only into verified framing.
  • Stability check: confirm the bowl base contacts the floor evenly with no rocking after mounting hardware is tightened.
  • Leak-test check: confirm repeated flush and refill cycles show no leakage at the base, tank, bowl connection, or supply connector.
  • Sealant-timing check: confirm base sealant was applied only after leak testing was accepted.
  • Documentation check: confirm flange condition, floor repair, seal, hardware, tests, and final acceptance are recorded.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Toilet Rocks After Mounting Hardware Is Tightened

Probable cause: an uneven floor plane, an unsquare flange, or a seal that compressed unevenly when the bowl was set.
Confirmation: check the floor plane with a level at each side of the base and inspect the seal compression by carefully lifting the bowl if the seal is not yet fully set.
Correction: insert an approved noncompressible shim at the low point and retighten progressively; do not use fastener force alone to pull the bowl down.
Reinspection: repeat the stability check and confirm even base contact on all sides before proceeding to testing.

Water Appears at the Base After the First Flush Test

Probable cause: the wax or waxless seal did not form a complete connection, the seal was twisted during setting, or the flange is set below the finished floor.
Confirmation: lift the toilet and inspect the seal for an uneven compression pattern or a gap on one side, and remeasure the flange height against the finished floor.
Correction: install a new seal, or an extended seal if the flange sits below the floor, and reset the bowl following the vertical, untwisted lowering procedure.
Reinspection: repeat the flush and leak test before applying any sealant.

The Floor Patch Sits Proud or Below the Surrounding Floor

Probable cause: the patch material thickness did not match the original subfloor or underlayment, or fasteners were not seated flush.
Confirmation: check the patch against the surrounding floor with a straightedge or level and inspect fastener heads for proud seating.
Correction: reset proud fasteners, or remove and replace the patch with correctly matched material thickness.
Reinspection: repeat the floor-repair check before installing the seal.

A Suspect Flange or Floor Condition Was Not Referred Before Reinstallation

Probable cause: the inspection in Section F identified a broken flange, damaged drain, or floor damage beyond the approved limit, but the toilet was reinstalled before a qualified professional completed and an inspection accepted the repair.
Confirmation: review the flange and floor inspection photographs and records against the Section F stop-work conditions.
Correction: remove the toilet again if necessary, obtain the required professional repair and inspection, and only then proceed to Section G.
Reinspection: repeat the flange check and confirm the professional repair documentation is on file before continuing.

Base Sealant Was Applied Before Leak Testing Was Complete

Probable cause: the sequence in Section I was performed out of order, sealing the base before the required flush and dye tests were completed.
Confirmation: review the documentation record for the order in which testing and sealing occurred.
Correction: cut and remove the sealant bead, complete the required leak testing, and reapply sealant only after testing is accepted.
Reinspection: repeat the sealant-timing check before final documentation.

Cleanup

This project ends with a tested, sealed, sanitized installation and a fully documented record, not simply with the toilet back in place.

  • Remove all disposable containment, rags, old wax, and debris from the work area and dispose of them appropriately
  • Sanitize tools, the surrounding floor, and any surfaces contacted during removal or reinstallation
  • Confirm the shutoff, supply connector, tank, and bowl are all in normal operating condition
  • Remove floor protection only after the base sealant has cured according to the product's instructions
  • File the Fixture Replacement Checklist, photographs, and any professional repair documentation together with the room's existing-condition report
