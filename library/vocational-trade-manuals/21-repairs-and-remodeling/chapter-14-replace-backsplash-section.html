Job Overview

This project qualifies you to remove one damaged or outdated kitchen or vanity backsplash section and install a matched replacement using an approved tile, sheet, or panel system. You will remove the existing material without damaging the countertop or concealed systems, inspect and repair the exposed wall substrate, lay out and set the new material, treat the joints, and inspect the completed surface for plane, pattern, coverage, and sealant integration.

You will confirm removal limits, complete a hazardous-material assessment before disturbing older tile or mastic, coordinate any required device de-energization with a qualified person, protect the countertop and adjacent finishes, remove the existing backsplash to a controlled depth, inspect the wall for moisture, mold, and utility conflicts, repair the substrate, lay out and install the replacement material, grout or finish the joints, and install flexible sealant at every change of plane before reinstalling trim, plates, and plumbing accessories.

This chapter builds on the drywall repair skills from Chapter 5 and the substrate-preparation and finish-integration skills from Chapter 10. It applies those skills to a wet-area finish surface and prepares the learner for the kitchen sink and vanity work in Chapters 11 and 13, where backsplash and countertop conditions are frequently encountered together.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RR-079 — Remove an existing backsplash section without damaging the countertop, cabinets, or concealed utilities.
  • RR-080 — Inspect and prepare the exposed wall surface to a sound, dry, and compatible substrate condition.
  • RR-081 — Lay out replacement backsplash material from verified level and plumb reference lines.
  • RR-082 — Install a tile or panel backsplash using the approved adhesive or mortar system with correct coverage and joint spacing.
  • RR-083 — Grout or seal the installed joints using the specified product and technique.
  • RR-084 — Inspect the finished surface for plane, pattern, coverage, joint treatment, device fit, and sealant integration.

Where This Skill Fits

A backsplash sits at the intersection of the countertop, the wall finish, and any electrical devices mounted in the splash area. This chapter teaches the learner to treat that intersection as a controlled boundary: remove only within the approved limit, protect what remains, verify the substrate before covering it, and finish every transition with the correct joint treatment rather than sealant alone.

The removal, substrate-inspection, and sealed-transition principles taught here carry forward into the Chapter 18 laundry or utility room refresh and the Chapter 20 capstone, both of which may include an approved backsplash section using this exact procedure.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • removing one limited kitchen or vanity backsplash section at natural boundaries such as cabinets, countertop, corners, or outlets
  • inspecting and repairing the exposed wall substrate before covering it
  • installing a tile, sheet, or panel backsplash system using the specified adhesive or mortar
  • grouting or finishing joints and installing flexible sealant at approved changes of plane
  • coordinating removal and reinstallation of electrical devices with a qualified person where required
  • inspecting the finished surface for plane, pattern, coverage, joints, sealant, and device fit

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • de-energize, remove, extend, or reinstall an electrical device or box without a qualified person where required by the approved scope
  • disturb suspect lead-containing, asbestos-containing, or mold-contaminated material before the required hazardous-material assessment is complete
  • treat this project as a code-controlled wet-area waterproofing assembly unless the approved scope specifically documents that requirement
  • fill a required movement or countertop joint with rigid grout in place of the specified flexible sealant
  • proceed past a wet, deteriorated, or unsafe substrate condition, or an unapproved wet-area requirement, without a change order

Any location identified as a code-controlled wet-area or fire-protection assembly requires the exact approved design and qualified review before this chapter's procedure applies.

Trade Note

A backsplash is a decorative splash surface in most kitchens and vanities, but tile, grout, caulk, and paint alone are never a substitute for an approved water-management detail in a code-controlled wet area. Confirm which category applies to the project location before beginning removal.

Setup Standard

Do not remove any existing backsplash material until countertop and cabinet protection is in place, the hazardous-material assessment is complete, and any required device de-energization has been confirmed by a qualified person. Do not apply adhesive or mortar to the wall until the substrate has been inspected, repaired if needed, and confirmed dry, flat, and within the selected system's tolerance.

Shop Tip

Dry-lay the full pattern on the countertop or a flat surface before mixing any adhesive. Centering or balancing cut pieces at visible boundaries is far easier to plan on a dry layout than to correct once tile is set in adhesive with a limited working time.

Common Field Fundamentals

A backsplash replacement fails when the removal boundary drifts past what is approved, the substrate is covered before it is confirmed sound and dry, the layout is taken from an irregular countertop edge instead of a true reference line, or a movement joint is filled with rigid grout instead of flexible sealant. Each of these fundamentals controls one of those failure points.

RemovalControlled demolition to the approved boundary and depth without countertop or utility damage
Substrate PrepInspection, repair, and drying of the exposed wall before any adhesive is applied
Layout and SetReference lines, dry layout, and material installation within the working time
Joint FinishGrout at fixed joints and flexible sealant at every movement and countertop joint

Decorative Splash vs. Code-Controlled Wet Area

Most kitchen and vanity backsplash locations are decorative splash areas, where tile, sheet, or panel material protects the wall from routine splashing. A location behind a cooking surface with special fire-protection clearance requirements, or a wall that forms part of an approved shower, tub, or wet-area waterproofing assembly, is a code-controlled wet area and requires the specific approved water-management detail, not this chapter's decorative procedure. Confirm which category applies before removal, and stop for an unapproved wet-area condition.

Measurement Cue — Reference Lines Independent of the Countertop

Establish a level line and a plumb line using a level and layout gauge before laying out the first course. Do not use the countertop or cabinet edge as the sole reference; both are frequently out of level or plumb by a small but visible amount that will telegraph through an entire row of tile or panel joints.

Critical Safety Cue — Confirm De-Energized Devices Before Removal

Do not remove, extend, or reinstall an electrical device, box, or cover plate in the backsplash area until a qualified person has de-energized the circuit and you have confirmed a de-energized condition with a non-contact voltage tester. Treat every device as energized until that confirmation is documented.

Field Safety Cue — Countertop and Cabinet Protection

Cover the countertop, sink, cooktop, and cabinet fronts with protection sheeting before removal begins. Keep pry bars and oscillating-tool blades working away from the countertop surface, and support any loose or cracked tile section before it falls onto a finished surface.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Working Time and Coverage

Apply adhesive or mortar only to an area that can be covered with material within the product's stated working time, and use the notch size the manufacturer specifies for the tile or panel size selected. Setting material into adhesive that has begun to skin over reduces bond strength even when the tile appears to be seated.

D. Preparation — System Selection and Protection (Steps 1–7)

  1. Select the approved backsplash system: tile, sheet, panel, or other specified finish. Record the manufacturer, product, and size on the Removal and Reference Record before ordering material.
  2. Review removal limits, substrate, countertop joint, cabinets, outlets, devices, plumbing, pattern, edge trim, adhesive or mortar, grout, sealant, finish, and cure requirements. Confirm every item is recorded in Section B before removal begins.
  3. Confirm whether the location is a decorative splash area or part of a code-controlled wet-area or fire-protection assembly. Stop and obtain the approved wet-area design before proceeding if the location is code-controlled.
  4. Complete hazardous-material assessment before disturbing old tile, mastic, adhesive, wallboard, texture, coating, or grout. Do not sand, grind, or dry-scrape suspect material until the assessment and required controls are in place.
  5. Coordinate device de-energization, removal, box protection, extension, and reinstallation with a qualified person where required. Confirm de-energization with a non-contact voltage tester before any device is touched.
  6. Protect countertop, sink, faucet, cabinets, appliances, floor, and adjacent finishes. Install protection sheeting over every surface that will remain in place.
  7. Mark the exact demolition boundary at cabinet, corner, countertop, trim, or pattern breaks. Confirm the marked boundary matches the Removal and Reference Record before cutting begins.

E. Removal and Substrate Inspection (Steps 8–15)

  1. Remove accessories, edge trim, and sealant in a controlled sequence. Score sealant and caulk lines at the boundary before releasing any trim piece.
  2. Break or release existing material using methods that limit substrate damage and flying debris. Work from the marked boundary inward using protective blocks against the countertop edge.
  3. Keep tools clear of concealed wires, plumbing, gas lines, and wall framing. Control cutting and prying depth to the known thickness of the existing finish and backing.
  4. Remove adhesive, mortar, fasteners, or backing only to the approved depth. Stop removal at the point specified for the substrate-repair method to be used.
  5. Inspect wallboard, plaster, backer, framing, moisture, mold, utilities, device boxes, and countertop-to-wall movement. Record findings and photograph the exposed wall before any repair begins.
  6. Stop for wet or deteriorated substrate, unsafe electrical condition, active leak, major wall damage, or unapproved wet-area requirement. Issue a change notice and obtain approval before continuing if any of these conditions is found.
  7. Repair the wall with a compatible approved substrate and allow required cure or drying. Use the drywall repair method from Chapter 5 or the manufacturer-specified backer-board method for the selected system.
  8. Verify the surface is clean, dry, flat, sound, and within the selected system's tolerance. Measure moisture at the repaired area and confirm it meets the product's installation requirement before proceeding.

F. Layout and Installation (Steps 16–24)

  1. Establish level and plumb reference lines independent of irregular countertop or cabinet edges where needed. Mark these lines directly on the prepared substrate using the level and layout gauge.
  2. Dry-lay the pattern and center or balance cuts at visible boundaries. Confirm the dry layout matches the approved pattern before mixing any adhesive.
  3. Plan outlets, corners, edge trim, inside corners, countertop joints, and termination pieces. Mark cutout locations on the dry-laid material before cutting.
  4. Cut test pieces and verify blade, edge quality, fit, and material support. Confirm cut edges are clean and free of chipping before cutting the remaining pieces.
  5. Mix or prepare adhesive, mortar, or panel bonding system according to product instructions. Mix only the quantity that can be applied within the stated working time.
  6. Apply only an area that can be covered within the product's working time. Confirm the applied adhesive has not begun to skin over before setting material.
  7. Set tile or panel material from the approved reference while maintaining alignment, coverage, joint width, movement joints, and edge support. Verify each piece against the reference lines as it is set.
  8. Keep electrical boxes, device screws, plumbing escutcheons, and serviceable components accessible. Confirm cutouts align with each device before the adhesive sets.
  9. Do not fill required movement or countertop joints with rigid grout. Mark every movement and countertop joint location for flexible sealant before grouting begins.

G. Joint Finish, Reinstallation, and Cure (Steps 25–29)

  1. Allow the installation to set before grouting, finishing, or loading. Confirm the manufacturer's required set time has elapsed before continuing.
  2. Apply grout, joint treatment, or panel seam finish using the specified product. Pack every fixed joint completely and consistently using the grout float or specified applicator.
  3. Clean the surface without washing out joints, scratching finishes, or contaminating sealant surfaces. Use the product-specified sponge and water-change schedule during cleanup.
  4. Install compatible flexible sealant at approved changes of plane, countertop joints, and material transitions. Tool the sealant bead to a smooth, consistent profile at every marked movement joint.
  5. Reinstall edge trim, devices, plates, accessories, and plumbing trim after required cure and qualified inspection. Confirm the qualified person has approved device reinstallation before restoring power.

H. Inspection and Documentation (Steps 30–31)

  1. Inspect plane, pattern, cuts, coverage, joints, movement accommodation, sealant, device fit, cleanability, surface damage, and finish integration. Complete the Inspection Checklist below before the project is considered complete.
  2. Document substrate repair, products, lot information, cure conditions, electrical coordination, and care instructions. File the complete record with the project package.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed installation using the same checks regardless of the system selected.

  • Boundary check: confirm removal and installation stayed within the approved boundary recorded before demolition.
  • Substrate check: confirm the wall was inspected, repaired where needed, and confirmed dry before adhesive was applied.
  • Layout check: confirm level and plumb reference lines were used rather than the countertop or cabinet edge alone.
  • Joint check: confirm fixed joints are grouted or seam-finished and every movement or countertop joint uses flexible sealant, not rigid grout.
  • Device check: confirm every electrical box, device screw, and plumbing escutcheon remains accessible.
  • Finish check: confirm plane, pattern, coverage, and cleanliness are accepted under normal viewing conditions.

Complete the Inspection Checklist below before considering the project closed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Countertop Chipped or Scratched During Removal

Probable cause: a pry bar or oscillating-tool blade contacted the countertop surface directly, or protection sheeting was missing or displaced during demolition.
Confirmation: inspect the countertop surface along the full removal boundary under raking light.
Correction: document the damage, refer countertop repair to the approved method or fabricator, and reinstall protection before continuing removal on the remaining boundary.
Reinspection: repeat the countertop-condition check after removal is complete and before adhesive application begins.

Tile or Panel Rows Drift Out of Level Across the Wall

Probable cause: the layout was referenced from the countertop or cabinet edge instead of an independent level line.
Confirmation: check the installed material against a level held independently of the countertop.
Correction: remove and reset the affected material within the adhesive's open time, or plan a corrected cut row at the least visible boundary if the adhesive has already set.
Reinspection: repeat the layout check across the full installed area.

Grout Cracks at the Countertop or Corner Joint

Probable cause: rigid grout was used at a movement joint that should have received flexible sealant.
Confirmation: inspect every countertop, corner, and change-of-plane joint for grout where sealant was specified.
Correction: rake out the cracked grout at the movement joint and replace it with the specified flexible sealant.
Reinspection: repeat the joint-treatment check across every movement and countertop joint.

Device Cover Plate No Longer Sits Flush

Probable cause: the cutout for the device box was undersized, or added material thickness was not accounted for in the box extension.
Confirmation: inspect the gap between the finished surface and the device box, and check whether a box extension was installed.
Correction: have the qualified person install the correct box extension or a device that accommodates the finished thickness before restoring power.
Reinspection: repeat the device-access check after the correction is complete.

Substrate Showed Elevated Moisture After Repair

Probable cause: the substrate repair was covered before the material reached the manufacturer's required dry condition, or an active leak source was not fully corrected before repair.
Confirmation: re-measure moisture at the repaired location and review whether the leak source was verified as corrected before this chapter began.
Correction: stop installation, identify and correct the moisture source, and allow the substrate to dry to the required criteria before proceeding.
Reinspection: repeat the substrate-condition check before any adhesive is reapplied.

Cleanup

  • Remove all sharp tile fragments, cut trim, and adhesive debris from the work area
  • Remove protection sheeting from the countertop, cabinets, and appliances without disturbing curing sealant
  • Clean the finished surface using product-compatible methods without disturbing grout or sealant joints
  • Confirm reinstalled devices, plates, and plumbing trim are secure and accessible
  • Store leftover tile, grout, and sealant with labeled product and lot information for future repairs
  • File the removal and installation record, photographs, and care instructions with the project package

Field Terms
