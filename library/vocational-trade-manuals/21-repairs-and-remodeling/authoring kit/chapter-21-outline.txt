# Book 21 Chapter Outline

# Residential Repairs and Remodeling: Demolition, Repairs, Renovations, and Home Improvements

Book 21 should transition from new construction and trade-specific skill building into **real residential repair and remodeling work**. The sequence should move from **assessment and demolition → wall/floor/ceiling repairs → fixture and finish updates → room upgrades → small remodel coordination**, ending with a complete small-room improvement project.

I would use **RR** for this book: **Residential Repairs and Remodeling**.

## Scope Note

Residential remodeling carries more uncertainty than new construction because hidden conditions are common. A wall, floor, ceiling, cabinet, or fixture may conceal wiring, plumbing, rot, mold, structural damage, pests, asbestos, lead paint, or previous unpermitted work.

The standing rule should be:

**Inspect first, protect the home, demolish selectively, expose hidden conditions carefully, repair the cause before repairing the surface, then close out the work cleanly.**

Chapters should flag:

* Lead paint in older homes
* Asbestos-containing flooring, adhesives, ceiling texture, insulation, or siding
* Mold and active moisture damage
* Hidden wiring and plumbing before cutting
* Load-bearing walls and structural framing
* Fire-rated assemblies and garage separation walls
* Wet-area waterproofing requirements
* Permit requirements for structural, plumbing, electrical, HVAC, or layout changes
* When demolition, structural modification, major water damage, hazardous materials, or code-controlled remodeling requires licensed professionals or inspection.
