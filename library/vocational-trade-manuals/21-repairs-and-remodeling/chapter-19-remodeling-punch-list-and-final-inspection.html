Job Overview

This project qualifies you to inspect a completed repair or remodeling project against its approved scope and produce the closeout package every project in this book ends with: a categorized punch list, corrected and reinspected deficiencies, verified concealed-work documentation, tested fixtures and systems, a completed owner walkthrough, and a formal written acceptance.

You will collect the approved scope, drawings, change orders, product information, permits, inspection approvals, test records, photographs, finish schedule, and owner selections; confirm concealed work was inspected before closure; confirm essential systems were restored; divide the project into inspection zones; inspect ceilings, walls, doors, windows, cabinets, flooring, trim, plumbing fixtures, electrical devices, ventilation, fire and life-safety components, and sealant and joint work; compare installed products against approved selections; check for safety hazards and residual debris; record every deficiency with location, responsible trade, priority, and photograph; separate safety and code items from finish and preference items; assign, complete, and reinspect corrections; perform a final cleaning; conduct the owner walkthrough; deliver closeout records; and obtain written acceptance.

This is the nineteenth chapter in the book. It applies to any completed project produced by Chapters 3 through 18 and is the exact inspection process the Chapter 20 capstone remodel must pass before it can be handed off.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RR-109 — Inspect the finish quality of a completed repair or remodeling project against its approved scope, drawings, and product selections.
  • RR-110 — Identify incomplete, deficient, or non-conforming work and record it as a formal punch-list entry.
  • RR-111 — Verify the operation of plumbing, electrical, ventilation, and other affected fixtures and systems through approved tests.
  • RR-112 — Verify that the work area, debris route, and adjacent spaces have been fully cleaned and restored.
  • RR-113 — Document each deficiency, its correction, and its reinspection result.
  • RR-114 — Approve project closeout only after every safety, code, structural, utility, and required-inspection item is resolved.

Where This Skill Fits

Every prior chapter in this book produced a completed repair, replacement, or installation, and every prior chapter's Required Outcome assumed that a later, independent inspection would confirm the work. This chapter is that inspection. It does not repeat the technical procedures taught in Chapters 3 through 18; it verifies their results against the scope, drawings, permits, and product selections those chapters already produced.

The categorized, corrected, and closed punch list this chapter produces is the exact document the Chapter 20 capstone remodel must complete before its own project can be handed off to the owner and transitioned to the property-maintenance and building-inspection practices in Book 22.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • collecting the approved scope, drawings, change orders, permits, inspection approvals, test records, photographs, and owner selections for a completed project
  • confirming concealed work, essential systems, and life-safety components were restored and previously inspected
  • dividing the project into inspection zones and inspecting each finish, fixture, and system in a consistent sequence
  • comparing installed products, colors, sheen, hardware, and dimensions against approved selections
  • identifying safety hazards, debris, and damage from protection removal
  • recording every deficiency with location, description, responsible trade, priority, and photograph
  • separating safety, code, water, structural, operational, finish, documentation, and owner-preference items
  • assigning, completing, and reinspecting corrections in an order that protects already-accepted work
  • performing final cleaning and conducting the owner walkthrough
  • delivering closeout records and obtaining written acceptance

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • approve closeout while any safety, code, water, structural, utility, fire, permit, or required-inspection item remains open
  • perform new construction, demolition, or design work to correct a deficiency; a correction must use the same approved method, product, and trade requirements as the original chapter that produced the work
  • waive a required permit, inspection, or qualified-trade sign-off because the visible finish appears acceptable
  • reclassify a safety or code deficiency as an owner-preference item to close the punch list faster
  • remove protection, restore contents, or release the space to full occupancy before every essential system and life-safety component has been verified operational

Any deficiency this chapter identifies as safety, code, water, structural, or utility related must remain open until corrected, reinspected, and, where required, signed off by the qualified trade or inspector who originally controlled that work.

Trade Note

A punch list is not a subjective list of preferences. Every entry must be checked against a specific standard: the approved scope, the approved drawings, the manufacturer's installation instructions, an adopted code provision, or the acceptance standard stated in the chapter that produced the work. An entry with no stated standard cannot be verified as corrected.

Setup Standard

Do not begin the zone-by-zone inspection (Section G) until the approved scope, drawings, change orders, permits, inspection approvals, and product selections (Section E) have all been collected and confirmed present. An inspection performed without the approved scope in hand cannot verify conformance and must be repeated once the records are assembled.

Shop Tip

Inspect in the same clockwise, top-to-bottom, entrance-to-exit sequence every time. A consistent sequence prevents a skipped wall, missed device, or unchecked cabinet, and it lets a second inspector repeat the same walkthrough later and compare results directly against your punch list.

Prerequisites

  • The approved scope, drawings, change orders, product information, permits, inspection approvals, test records, photographs, finish schedule, and owner selections for the completed project, all collected and available before the inspection begins
  • Confirmation that every required concealed-work, rough-in, and specialty inspection was already completed and documented before the assembly was closed
  • Access to every room, fixture, device, and system affected by the completed scope
  • A functioning camera, tape measure, level, flashlight, moisture meter, and voltage tester available before Section G
  • Confirmation that no active hazard (exposed wiring, active leak, unstable component, gas odor, or similar) is present; if one is suspected, stop and contact the appropriate emergency service or qualified professional before proceeding

Common Field Fundamentals

This chapter applies the same three-part discipline taught throughout the book, now used to verify rather than to investigate: check every observation against a stated standard, separate deficiencies that threaten safety or code compliance from deficiencies that affect only appearance or preference, and do not close an item until it has been reinspected against the same criterion used to flag it.

Priority Categories

Classify every punch-list entry into one of eight priority categories: safety, code, water, structural, operational, finish, documentation, or owner-preference. Safety, code, water, structural, operational, and documentation items involving a required inspection must be corrected and reinspected before closeout is approved. Finish and owner-preference items may be scheduled or deferred only with the owner's written agreement, using the deferred-item process described in Section I.

Safety / Code / StructuralExposed fasteners, unstable cabinets, missing smoke alarm, unresolved rated-assembly gap — never deferred
Water / OperationalActive leak, faucet that will not shut off, disposal that will not run — never deferred
Finish / PreferenceMinor color variation, a scratch on a protected surface, a preferred hardware finish — may be deferred with written agreement

Measurement Cue — Compare Against the Approved Drawing, Not Against Memory

Measure reveals, clearances, and dimensions against the approved scope and drawings collected in Section E, using the same reference points those documents specify. Do not judge a dimension as correct because it looks similar to the surrounding work; record the measured value and compare it to the stated tolerance.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Approve Closeout Over an Open Safety Item

Do not classify a project as closed, or allow the owner to resume full use of a room, while a safety, code, water, structural, or required-inspection item remains open. Restore smoke alarms, carbon-monoxide alarms, fire doors, locks, handrails, and other life-safety components to full operating condition before any other item is closed.

Field Safety Cue — Inspect Before Removing Remaining Protection

Inspect flooring, trim, and finished surfaces for damage before removing any remaining floor or surface protection, and inspect again immediately after removal. Protection removed before the damage inspection is complete can conceal a defect that becomes disputed later.

Code Alert — Confirm Final Permit Sign-Off Separately

A visually complete project is not the same as a permit-closed project. Confirm with the local building department, licensing authority, or qualified professional whether a final inspection or permit closeout is required in addition to this chapter's punch-list process, and do not represent the project as fully closed until that requirement is satisfied.

E. Preparation — Records Collection and System Verification (Steps 1–4)

  1. Collect the approved scope, drawings, change orders, product information, permits, inspection approvals, test records, photographs, finish schedule, and owner selections. List each document collected and note any document that cannot be located; do not proceed to Section G until the approved scope and drawings are confirmed present.
  2. Verify that all concealed work has required documentation and was inspected before closure. Cross-check the concealed-work photographs and inspection approvals from each relevant chapter against the areas that were closed with drywall, flooring, or other finish material.
  3. Confirm utilities, alarms, locks, doors, handrails, lighting, plumbing, ventilation, appliances, and other affected essential systems are restored. Record the current operating condition of each affected essential system before beginning the detailed zone inspection.
  4. Divide the project into inspection zones and prepare a checklist for each trade and finish. List each zone that applies to the completed project — for example ceilings, walls, doors, windows, cabinets, flooring, trim, plumbing, electrical, ventilation, and life safety — and prepare a checklist line for each required check within that zone.

F. Inspection Sequence and Surface Zones (Steps 5–12)

  1. Inspect from the entrance through the room in a consistent clockwise, top-to-bottom sequence. Record the starting point and direction on the zone-inspection form and follow the same sequence for every zone in the project.
  2. Inspect ceilings for plane, texture, coating, cracks, stains, penetrations, devices, and clean transitions. Use raking light and record each finding with its location and photograph reference.
  3. Inspect walls for flatness, patch visibility, paint color, sheen, roller pattern, cut lines, damage, and completed accessories. Compare the installed color, sheen, and texture against the approved finish schedule from Section E.
  4. Inspect doors, jambs, casing, hinges, latches, stops, reveals, clearances, and finish. Cycle each door and record reveal measurements against the acceptance standard from the chapter that produced the work.
  5. Inspect windows, casing, stools, aprons, coverings, locks, and unaffected operation where included. Confirm window operation was not degraded by adjacent trim, casing, or finish work.
  6. Inspect cabinets, vanities, tops, backsplashes, shelves, hardware, anchorage, reveals, doors, drawers, and sealant. Verify anchorage to structural backing and cycle every door and drawer.
  7. Inspect flooring for plane, pattern, transitions, movement, damage, lippage, gaps, edge conditions, and cleanliness. Record any lippage, hollow spot, or transition defect with its location and measurement.
  8. Inspect base, shoe molding, casing, joints, returns, fasteners, filler, caulk, primer, and finish. Confirm trim profile, joint quality, and finish match the approved specification.

G. System, Fixture, and Life-Safety Verification (Steps 13–20)

  1. Inspect plumbing fixtures, shutoffs, supply connections, drains, traps, sealant, support, access, and leakage. Inspect visually and with the moisture meter at every connection point completed in a prior chapter.
  2. Operate faucets, sprayers, stoppers, toilets, drains, disposals, dishwashers, washers, and other included equipment through approved tests. Record the result of each operational test using the same test method described in the chapter that installed the fixture.
  3. Inspect electrical devices, plates, lighting, fans, alarms, panel access, and equipment only within the authorized inspection scope. Use the non-contact voltage tester to confirm normal operating condition without opening any panel or device outside the authorized scope.
  4. Verify ventilation, exhaust, combustion-air, dryer-duct, equipment-clearance, and service-access conditions where affected. Confirm no required clearance was reduced by newly installed storage, trim, or finish work.
  5. Verify fire blocking, rated repairs, access panels, caulked penetrations, and life-safety components required by the scope. Confirm every rated assembly restored in a prior chapter has its required inspection approval on file.
  6. Inspect caulk, grout, sealant, joints, countertop transitions, backsplash terminations, and wet-area details. Confirm flexible sealant was used at movement joints and rigid grout was not used to fill a required movement joint.
  7. Compare installed products, colors, sheen, hardware, fixtures, and dimensions with approved selections. Record any variance between the installed product and the approved selection as a punch-list entry, regardless of visual similarity.
  8. Check for sharp edges, exposed fasteners, unstable components, pinch points, trip hazards, leaks, odors, dust, debris, and blocked exits. Record every safety condition found as a punch-list entry classified in the safety priority category.

H. Punch-List Preparation, Correction, and Reinspection (Steps 21–27)

  1. Verify removal of protection has not damaged flooring, paint, trim, fixtures, or adjacent rooms. Inspect immediately before and immediately after removing any remaining protection and record any damage found.
  2. Inspect the debris route, exterior access, common areas, yard, driveway, and waste location for project damage or residue. Record any damage or residual debris along the path used throughout the project.
  3. Record each deficiency with location, description, responsible trade, priority, acceptance criterion, and photograph. Complete one punch-list line for every deficiency identified in Sections F and G; do not combine unrelated deficiencies into a single entry.
  4. Separate safety, code, water, structural, operational, finish, documentation, and owner-preference items. Assign each entry exactly one priority category using the Priority Categories guidance above.
  5. Do not approve closeout while safety, code, water, structural, utility, fire, permit, or required inspection items remain open. Confirm this rule applies to every entry classified in one of these categories before continuing to Step 26.
  6. Assign and complete corrections in an order that prevents damage to accepted work. Sequence corrections so that a structural, utility, or concealed-work correction is completed before any finish correction that depends on it.
  7. Reinspect every corrected item against the same acceptance criterion. Record the reinspection date, method, and result on the correction and reinspection log for every corrected entry.

I. Final Cleaning, Owner Walkthrough, and Closeout (Steps 28–32)

  1. Perform a final cleaning and remove labels, protective films, dust, debris, and temporary materials as approved. Confirm the cleaning method used is compatible with each finish material and does not damage a newly installed surface.
  2. Conduct the owner or responsible-party walkthrough and demonstrate fixtures, shutoffs, doors, hardware, storage, finishes, and maintenance items. Operate every affected fixture and system in the owner's presence and record their confirmation of each demonstration.
  3. Deliver product data, warranties, color formulas, spare materials, keys, inspection records, photographs, maintenance schedule, and referral items. List each item delivered on the owner closeout record and obtain the owner's acknowledgment of receipt.
  4. Obtain written acceptance or document any agreed deferred item with responsibility and due condition. Record the owner's signature, the date, and the exact condition and responsible party for any item deferred beyond this closeout.
  5. Archive the closed punch list and complete the formal project closeout. File the complete package, including every zone-inspection record, punch-list entry, correction and reinspection log, and the signed owner closeout record.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed closeout package using the same checks regardless of which project or room was inspected.

  • Records check: confirm the approved scope, drawings, change orders, permits, inspection approvals, and product selections were collected before the zone inspection began.
  • Zone-coverage check: confirm every required inspection zone was inspected and recorded with no zone skipped.
  • Deficiency-documentation check: confirm every punch-list entry states location, description, responsible trade, priority category, acceptance criterion, and photograph reference.
  • Priority check: confirm every entry is classified into exactly one priority category and that no safety or code item was reclassified as a preference item.
  • Correction check: confirm every safety, code, water, structural, operational, and required-documentation item was corrected and reinspected against its original criterion.
  • Closeout check: confirm the project was approved for closeout only after every required item was resolved, with any deferred item documented in writing.

Complete the Inspection Checklist in the Final Closeout Checklist section below before releasing the project or, where applicable, before beginning the Chapter 20 capstone closeout.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Deficiency Was Recorded Without a Stated Acceptance Criterion

Probable cause: the inspector flagged an item as a deficiency based on general impression rather than comparing it to the approved scope, drawing, manufacturer instruction, or prior chapter's acceptance standard.
Confirmation: review the punch-list entry and identify whether a specific standard is cited; if none is cited, the entry cannot be verified as corrected.
Correction: locate the applicable acceptance criterion in the approved scope, drawings, or the originating chapter and add it to the entry, or reclassify the entry as an owner-preference item if no objective standard applies.
Reinspection: repeat the deficiency-documentation check across the full punch list.

A Safety or Code Item Was Classified as an Owner-Preference Item

Probable cause: a deficiency affecting safety, code compliance, water intrusion, structural condition, or a required inspection was misclassified to allow closeout to proceed without correction.
Confirmation: review every finish and preference entry against the Priority Categories guidance and identify any item that actually affects safety, code, water, structural condition, or a required inspection.
Correction: reclassify the item into its correct priority category and hold closeout until it is corrected and reinspected.
Reinspection: repeat the priority check and the closeout classification check.

A Corrected Item Was Closed Without Reinspection

Probable cause: the correcting trade reported the item complete, and the entry was marked closed without the inspector reverifying it against the original acceptance criterion.
Confirmation: review the correction and reinspection log for a recorded reinspection date, method, and result for the item in question.
Correction: reopen the entry and perform the reinspection against the original criterion before closing it again.
Reinspection: repeat the correction check across every closed entry on the punch list.

An Inspection Zone Was Skipped

Probable cause: the inspector did not follow a consistent entrance-to-exit sequence, or a zone checklist was not prepared for a trade or finish present in the completed scope.
Confirmation: compare the list of zones inspected against the full list of trades and finishes present in the approved scope.
Correction: prepare and complete the missing zone checklist before the punch list is finalized.
Reinspection: repeat the zone-coverage check.

The Project Was Closed With an Undocumented Deferred Item

Probable cause: the owner verbally agreed to accept an unfinished item, but the agreement was not recorded with a stated responsibility and due condition.
Confirmation: review the owner closeout record for a written entry matching each item the owner is known to have deferred.
Correction: document the deferred item with its responsible party and due condition and obtain the owner's signature before treating closeout as complete.
Reinspection: repeat the closeout classification check.

Cleanup

This project ends with a complete, signed closeout package and a physically clean, fully restored work area.

  • Confirm all remaining protective materials, labels, and temporary markings have been removed without damaging accepted finishes
  • Confirm the debris route, exterior access, and common areas used during the project are clean and undamaged
  • Restore owner contents, furniture, and window coverings included in the scope to their approved final position
  • Store the punch list, correction and reinspection log, photographs, and owner closeout record together as a single dated package for the project
  • Provide the owner with a copy of the closeout record, warranties, product data, and maintenance schedule
