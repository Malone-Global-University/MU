Job Overview

This project qualifies you to diagnose and correct a non-rated interior door that binds, sags, rubs against its jamb or floor, or fails to latch reliably. You will identify the door and hardware, operate the door to record its exact symptoms, measure reveals at every edge, determine whether the cause is loose or worn hinge attachment, hinge wear, paint buildup, frame movement, or latch misalignment, correct hinge attachment or replace hinges as required, rehang and remeasure the door, adjust the strike plate only after the door itself is aligned, and verify reliable latch engagement across repeated cycles.

You will produce a corrected, securely hung door with even reveals, sound hinge attachment, a strike plate matched to the aligned latch bolt, and a documented record of the original cause and the corrective action taken.

This project builds directly on the inspection discipline from Chapter 1 and the finished-surface standards from Chapters 4 and 5. It prepares you for Chapter 9, where you replace a complete window or door casing assembly, and for the Chapter 20 capstone, which reuses these door-repair controls wherever the capstone room includes an interior door.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RR-043 — Diagnose the cause of a binding, sagging, rubbing, or non-latching interior door through operation, measurement, and inspection.
  • RR-044 — Tighten, repair, or replace hinges using matching size, thickness, and hole pattern.
  • RR-045 — Rehang the door and adjust reveals at the hinge jamb, head, latch jamb, and floor.
  • RR-046 — Repair latch-bolt alignment relative to the strike opening.
  • RR-047 — Install a matching strike plate with the correct fastener and full latch-bolt engagement.
  • RR-048 — Inspect and verify complete, safe door operation through repeated cycling.

Where This Skill Fits

Doors are one of the most frequently reported repair items in an occupied home, and a binding or non-latching door is often the first visible sign of frame movement, seasonal humidity change, or a previous poor repair. This chapter teaches you to separate a simple hinge problem from a condition that indicates settlement or unresolved moisture, and to correct only what this chapter's scope allows.

The reveal-measurement and diagnostic method taught here is reused in Chapter 9 when the casing is removed and replaced, and in the Chapter 19 punch-list inspection, which checks every door's reveals, hinge security, and latch engagement against the standard established in this chapter.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • identifying the door, jamb, hinge, and latch hardware and their non-rated, non-specialty status
  • operating and inspecting the door to record binding, sag, rub, latch failure, and hinge condition
  • measuring reveals at the hinge jamb, head, latch jamb, and floor
  • correcting loose, stripped, or worn hinge attachment, or replacing damaged hinges with a matching size and hole pattern
  • rehanging the door and adjusting reveals
  • planing or trimming the slab only after hinge and frame causes are resolved and only within the door manufacturer's allowable limit
  • adjusting the strike position and installing a matching strike plate after the door is aligned
  • cycling the door repeatedly and inspecting the completed repair

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • repair a fire-rated, smoke-control, egress, exterior, garage-separation, security, or other specialty door using this chapter's non-rated procedure
  • adjust a door before active moisture, structural movement, or frame settlement has been corrected or referred
  • plane, sand, or trim a door slab beyond the door manufacturer's allowable limit, or before hinge and frame causes have been resolved
  • drill, mortise, or fasten into a jamb or slab without a verified screw length that will not contact concealed utilities or split the frame
  • adjust a door stop in a way that prevents latch engagement

Any door found to be fire-rated, part of an egress path with a special hardware requirement, exhibiting active moisture or structural movement, or requiring frame replacement must be excluded from this chapter's scope and referred according to the risk register produced in Chapter 1.

Trade Note

Diagnose before you adjust. A door that binds because the jamb has moved will not stay corrected if you plane the slab to fit the current bind; the same door will bind again as humidity or settlement changes. Resolve hinge and frame causes first, and treat planing as a last-resort correction within the manufacturer's allowable limit.

Setup Standard

Do not remove a hinge screw or hinge pin until the door is supported so it cannot fall, swing uncontrolled, or drop onto a foot or finished floor. Confirm at least one person is available to support the slab during removal and rehanging.

Shop Tip

Record every reveal measurement before you touch a hinge screw. A "before" measurement at the hinge jamb, head, latch jamb, and floor tells you whether the door is currently out of alignment with the frame or whether the frame itself has moved — a distinction you cannot make once the door has already been rehung.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every door repair in this chapter follows the same discipline: operate and measure before touching hardware, correct hinge and frame causes before adjusting the slab, and adjust the strike only after the door itself is aligned. Skipping this order produces a door that appears fixed today and binds again as soon as conditions change.

Identifying the Cause of Binding or Sag

A door that binds, sags, rubs, or fails to latch has one or more of these causes: loose or stripped hinge screws, worn hinge pins or leaves, paint buildup on the slab edge or jamb, swollen slab material from moisture, movement of the jamb or surrounding frame, a change in flooring height, or a latch-bolt and strike opening that no longer align. Identify the cause before selecting a correction. Do not plane the slab, add packing behind a hinge, or force the strike into a new position without first ruling out a hinge or frame cause.

Measurement Cue — Reveal Consistency

Measure the reveal at the top, middle, and bottom of both the hinge jamb and the latch jamb, and at the head. A uniform reveal indicates a square, plumb installation. A reveal that narrows toward one corner indicates sag, jamb movement, or an out-of-square frame; record the exact measurement at each location rather than describing the condition as "tight" or "loose."

Critical Safety Cue — Support the Door Before Releasing Hinge Hardware

Do not remove a hinge screw or drive out a hinge pin unless the door is already supported by a second person or a stable prop that prevents it from swinging free or falling. An unsupported door can strike a hand, foot, or finished floor when the last hinge releases.

Field Safety Cue — Stripped and Sharp Hardware

Remove and set aside every stripped screw, bent hinge pin, or damaged strike plate immediately rather than leaving it on the work surface or floor. Inspect the replacement hinge and strike hardware for sharp stamped edges before installing.

Code Alert — Confirm the Door Is Not a Specialty Assembly

Fire-rated, smoke-control, egress, exterior, garage-separation, security, and accessibility-function doors carry hardware, clearance, and closing-action requirements that this chapter's non-rated procedure does not address. Confirm the door's status against the Chapter 1 risk register before beginning any adjustment.

E. Diagnosis (Steps 1–8)

  1. Identify the door type, core, size, swing, jamb, stop, casing, hinges, latch, strike, floor clearance, and any fire, smoke, security, or accessibility function. Record every item on the Door Identification Record before proceeding.
  2. Exclude fire-rated, smoke-control, egress, exterior, garage-separation, security, or specialty doors unless their approved repair requirements are provided. If the door is excluded, stop and refer it per the Chapter 1 risk register; do not proceed with this chapter's procedure.
  3. Operate the door slowly and record binding, sag, rub, latch failure, hinge noise, floor contact, frame movement, and seasonal evidence. Cycle the door through its full swing at least three times and record the exact location of each symptom.
  4. Inspect the slab, jamb, head, threshold or floor, stops, hinges, screws, strike, latch, casing, and surrounding wall for movement or damage. Record each observed condition and its location.
  5. Measure reveals at the hinge jamb, head, latch jamb, and floor. Record each measurement at the top, middle, and bottom of each jamb using the Measurement Cue method.
  6. Check jamb plumb, head level, frame square, hinge alignment, and wall movement. Record each reading and note any deviation exceeding 1/8 inch (3 mm) over the height of the jamb.
  7. Determine whether the cause is loose fasteners, stripped holes, bent hinges, hinge wear, paint buildup, swollen material, frame movement, flooring change, or latch misalignment. State the identified cause in writing before selecting a correction.
  8. Correct active moisture, structural movement, or frame settlement before adjusting the door. If any of these conditions is confirmed or suspected, stop and refer per the Chapter 1 risk register; do not proceed to Section F until the referral is resolved.

F. Hinge Correction and Rehanging (Steps 9–16)

  1. Support the door before removing hinge screws or pins. Confirm a second person or a stable prop is in place before releasing any hinge hardware.
  2. Tighten sound hinge screws without stripping or overdriving. Test each screw for solid engagement; if a screw turns without tightening, treat the hole as stripped and proceed to the next step.
  3. Repair approved stripped screw holes or replace screws only with a verified length that will not contact concealed utilities or distort the frame. Fill an accepted stripped hole with compatible wood filler, allow the manufacturer-specified cure time, and re-drill a pilot hole at the corrected diameter before installing the new screw.
  4. Replace damaged hinges with matching size, thickness, corner shape, pin, rating, and hole pattern. Confirm the replacement hinge's leaf dimensions and hole pattern match the existing mortise before installing.
  5. Mortise or shim hinges only through a controlled adjustment that preserves leaf support and jamb strength. Do not remove more material than needed to seat the hinge leaf flush, and do not shim a hinge in a way that leaves a gap under any screw.
  6. Rehang the door and remeasure reveals. Record the new reveal measurements at the same locations used in Section E and compare them against the acceptance standard in Section H.
  7. Plane, sand, or trim the slab only after hinge and frame causes are resolved and only within the door manufacturer's allowable limits. State the manufacturer's allowable limit for this specific door before removing any material, and remove material in controlled, verified passes rather than a single aggressive cut.
  8. Seal any exposed wood edge as required by the door finish system. Apply the specified sealer or finish to any freshly planed or trimmed edge before closing out the repair.

G. Latch and Strike Correction (Steps 17–22)

  1. Inspect latch-bolt travel and mark its relationship to the strike opening. Close the door slowly and mark exactly where the latch bolt contacts the strike plate or jamb.
  2. Adjust the strike position only after the door is aligned. Confirm the reveal measurements from Section F meet the acceptance standard before adjusting or relocating the strike.
  3. Repair approved strike-screw attachment and install a matching strike plate using suitable fasteners. Install the strike plate specified in the Fastener and Hardware Schedule, verifying full seating and correct orientation.
  4. Verify the latch fully engages without lifting, pushing, or slamming the door. Close the door using normal closing force and confirm the latch bolt seats fully in the strike opening.
  5. Adjust door stops only where required and without preventing latch engagement. Reposition a stop only enough to eliminate rattling contact, and confirm the latch still engages fully after any stop adjustment.
  6. Clean and lubricate hinges or latch components with a compatible product where specified. Apply lubricant only to clean, dry hardware and wipe away any excess before testing.

H. Testing and Documentation (Steps 23–25)

  1. Cycle the door repeatedly from both sides. Open and close the door at least ten times from each side, using normal operating force, and confirm consistent, quiet, unobstructed operation.
  2. Inspect reveal uniformity, hinge security, latch engagement, strike attachment, floor clearance, stop contact, pinch points, sharp hardware, and finish damage. Record the final measurement at every location checked in Section E and compare each against the acceptance standard.
  3. Document the original cause, adjustments, replacement hardware, and any residual building movement requiring referral. Complete the Door Repair Record below and file it with the project documentation established in Chapter 1.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Door Rebinds Shortly After Planing the Slab

Probable cause: the slab was planed to fit a bind caused by loose hinge screws or jamb movement rather than a permanently oversized slab.
Confirmation: remeasure reveals at all four reference locations and recheck hinge-screw seating; compare against the original diagnostic record from Section E.
Correction: correct the hinge or frame cause identified in the original diagnosis; do not plane the slab further until the underlying cause is resolved.
Reinspection: repeat the reveal measurement and ten-cycle test after the correction.

Latch Bolt Will Not Engage After Rehanging

Probable cause: the strike plate was adjusted or installed before the door's reveals met the acceptance standard, so the latch bolt and strike opening no longer align.
Confirmation: remeasure reveals at the latch jamb and mark the latch-bolt contact point again with the door closed slowly.
Correction: reverify door alignment against the acceptance standard, then reposition or reinstall the strike plate to the corrected contact point.
Reinspection: repeat the latch-engagement check and the ten-cycle test.

Door Sags at the Latch Corner Again After Hinge Replacement

Probable cause: a hinge screw was installed into a stripped or previously filled hole without verifying solid engagement, or the replacement hinge does not match the original leaf thickness.
Confirmation: inspect each hinge screw for play when the door is cycled and compare the installed hinge's dimensions against the Component Schedule.
Correction: refill and re-drill any stripped hole per Section F, Step 11, or replace the hinge with one matching the specified dimensions.
Reinspection: repeat the reveal measurement and cycle test.

Door Rubs Only in Certain Weather or Seasons

Probable cause: seasonal humidity is swelling the slab or jamb material, or the condition indicates ongoing frame movement rather than a one-time hinge defect.
Confirmation: review the owner-reported seasonal pattern recorded in Section E, Step 3, and compare current reveal measurements against any prior readiness or inspection records.
Correction: if the pattern indicates recurring seasonal movement rather than a hinge defect, document the condition and refer it per the Chapter 1 risk register rather than planing the slab to fit the current season's condition.
Reinspection: monitor and remeasure at the next seasonal change if the owner elects to defer further correction.

Door Stop Adjustment Prevents the Latch From Engaging

Probable cause: the stop was moved further into the opening than required to stop rattling, holding the door away from the strike plate.
Confirmation: close the door and check whether the latch bolt reaches the strike opening with the stop in its current position.
Correction: reposition the stop to the minimum adjustment needed to eliminate rattling while allowing full latch engagement.
Reinspection: repeat the latch-engagement check and the ten-cycle test.

Cleanup

  • Remove every stripped screw, bent hinge pin, and damaged strike plate from the work area
  • Wipe away excess lubricant from hinges and latch hardware
  • Vacuum or sweep any sanding, planing, or drilling debris from the floor and jamb area
  • Confirm no sharp or protruding hardware remains on the door, jamb, or floor
  • Return any removed door stop, casing, or accessory to its finished position
  • File the completed Door Repair Record with the project documentation established in Chapter 1

Performance Check

With the door rehung and the strike plate installed, review the finished repair against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm every reveal measurement falls within its stated tolerance, every hinge screw is fully seated without play, the latch bolt fully engages the strike opening on normal closing force, the door completes ten consecutive open-close cycles from each side without binding, rubbing, or failing to latch, and no sharp or protruding hardware or unsealed wood edge remains. Complete the Door Repair Record and issue a final result of corrected, corrected with referral, or referred and not corrected before closing out this project.
