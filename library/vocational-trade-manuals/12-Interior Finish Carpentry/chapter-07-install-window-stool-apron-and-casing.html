Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install a complete interior window trim assembly: a stool that spans the opening and returns into the wall on each side, an apron that supports the stool visually and structurally beneath it, and side and head casing that frame the opening with a consistent reveal. The finished work product is one window with a level, securely fastened stool; a centered, securely fastened apron; and side and head casing installed with the reveal established in Project 01 and aligned to the stool horns.

You will verify the window is installed and operating correctly, establish the stool projection, horn length, casing reveal, and apron alignment from the approved trim schedule, cut and fit the stool to the window and side jambs including horns and jamb notches, fasten the stool without affecting window operation, cut and center the apron beneath the stool, mark and cut side and head casing to the established reveal, align the casing with the stool horns, and fasten the complete assembly.

This project depends directly on the Chapter 1 room survey and controlling-line layout, which recorded the casing reveal and the jamb depth needed to plan the stool and apron. It also depends on the window itself passing the Chapter 1 opening inspection for operation, frame alignment, and jamb depth. The techniques used here — establishing a reveal, cutting and fitting miters, and fastening casing to jamb and wall framing — are the same techniques used for door casing in Project 04 and are reused in every later trim chapter.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FC-037 — Measure the window opening, including jamb depth, jamb returns, and reveal-controlling dimensions.
  • FC-038 — Cut the stool, including horn length, jamb notches, and wall scribes.
  • FC-039 — Install the apron, centered beneath the stool and fastened to backing.
  • FC-040 — Install side casing at the established reveal, aligned with the stool horns.
  • FC-041 — Install head casing at the established reveal, with joints aligned to the side casing.
  • FC-042 — Inspect window-trim alignment, including reveal, stool level, apron centering, and window operation.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 01 established the casing reveal for every opening in the room, including this window, and recorded jamb depth on the Opening Inspection Record. Project 03 taught the measuring, marking, and mitering techniques used throughout this project. Project 04 applied those techniques to door casing; this project applies the same reveal-and-miter method to a window opening, with the added steps of fitting a stool with horns and notches and installing an apron beneath it.

The finished window trim assembly in this project is inspected again in Project 18, where nail holes are filled, joints are caulked at designated paint-grade locations, and the assembly is prepared for paint or stain. It is also one of the trim assemblies integrated into the Chapter 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • verifying the window is installed, flashed, insulated, air-sealed, and operating correctly before trim work begins
  • inspecting jamb depth, drywall plane, sill condition, reveal, and moisture at the opening
  • establishing the stool projection, horn length, casing reveal, and apron alignment from the approved trim schedule and the Chapter 1 controlling-line layout
  • measuring the window opening and jamb returns and transferring horn and notch dimensions to the stool blank
  • cutting the stool ends, horns, and jamb notches, and test-fitting the stool against the window and side jambs
  • scribing the stool for wall irregularities and fastening it to backing without affecting window operation
  • measuring, cutting, centering, and fastening the apron beneath the stool
  • marking the approved casing reveal on the side and head jambs, cutting side and head casing, and aligning casing joints with the stool horns
  • fastening the head and side casing using the approved pattern and securing miters or butt joints as specified
  • checking reveals, stool level, apron centering, casing plane, and window operation, and correcting open joints, a rocking stool, uneven horns, or binding

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • trim a window that has not passed the Chapter 1 opening inspection for operation, frame alignment, and jamb depth; refer an unresolved window defect to the responsible trade before proceeding
  • alter window flashing, insulation, air-sealing, operating hardware, or weather seals in the course of installing trim
  • reduce window operation, egress clearance, or accessibility clearance to achieve a planned reveal or stool projection
  • conceal an open joint, rocking stool, or major gap with filler or caulk in place of correcting the fit; see Project 18 for the approved filling and caulking scope

Trade Note

The stool is the first piece installed and it controls every other piece in this assembly: the apron centers beneath it, and the side casing aligns to its horns. A stool cut without accurate horn and notch dimensions, rather than from the actual measured opening, is the most common cause of the corrected-fit problems addressed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not begin stool layout until the window has passed the Chapter 1 opening inspection for operation, frame alignment, and jamb depth, and the Chapter 1 controlling-line layout has recorded the casing reveal for this opening. Trim fit to an unverified window or an assumed reveal must be treated as unverified until both records are confirmed.

Shop Tip

Cut the stool horns slightly long on the first pass and sneak up on the final notch fit with a block plane or sanding block. A horn cut short cannot be corrected without replacing the stool blank; a horn cut long can be trimmed back in small, controlled passes until it seats fully against the jamb and wall.

Common Field Fundamentals

The stool is fitted first because it is the only piece in this assembly that must be notched around the jambs and scribed to the wall; every other piece in the assembly references the stool once it is set. The apron is centered beneath the stool rather than beneath the rough opening, because the stool's horns, not the framed opening, define the visible width of the finished assembly. The side and head casing then follow the reveal established in Project 01 and align their inside edges with the outer edges of the stool horns.

Horn Length and Notch Fit

Horn length is the distance the stool projects beyond the outer edge of the side casing, measured from the approved trim schedule. The jamb notch depth equals the jamb depth measured at the opening plus any scribe allowance needed for wall irregularity identified in the Project 01 survey. Cut the notch after the horn length and overall stool length are established, and test-fit before committing to the final cut.

Measurement Cue — Use the Recorded Reveal, Not a New One

Mark the side and head casing reveal on this window's jambs using the exact value recorded on the Project 01 Controlling-Line Layout for this opening. Do not establish a new reveal by eye at the casing stage; a reveal that differs from the recorded value will misalign with the stool horns and with the door and other window casing in the same room.

Critical Safety Cue — Verify Window Operation Before and After Trim

Confirm the window opens, closes, and latches correctly before starting stool layout. After the stool, apron, and casing are installed, operate the window through its full range again. Stop work and correct any binding, rubbing, or obstruction caused by trim before fastening is finalized; do not force a window to operate against installed trim.

Field Safety Cue — Fastening Near Glass

Keep fastener paths clear of the glazing and sash when nailing the stool, apron, and casing near the window unit. Angle fasteners away from the glass and sash track, and stop immediately if a fastener meets unexpected resistance near the window frame.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Window Warranty Fastening Limits

Where the window manufacturer specifies a maximum fastener length or a no-fastener zone near the frame to preserve flashing, insulation, or warranty coverage, follow that limit exactly. Confirm this requirement before drilling or nailing near the window frame.

F. Window and Opening Verification

  1. Verify the window is installed, flashed, insulated, air-sealed, and operating correctly. Operate the window through its full range and record the result before proceeding.
  2. Inspect jamb depth, drywall plane, sill condition, reveal, and moisture. Confirm these match the Project 01 Opening Inspection Record for this window; note and correct any discrepancy before continuing.

G. Layout Values

  1. Establish the stool projection, horn length, casing reveal, and apron alignment. Take these values from the approved trim schedule and the Project 01 Controlling-Line Layout, and record them on the Window Trim Record.

H. Stool Layout and Cutting

  1. Measure the window opening and jamb returns. Record the opening width, jamb depth at each side, and any wall irregularity noted in the Project 01 survey.
  2. Transfer horn and notch dimensions to the stool blank. Mark the overall length including both horns, and mark each jamb-notch location and depth.
  3. Cut the stool ends, horns, and jamb notches. Use the miter saw for straight cuts and a coping saw or jigsaw for the notch profile as specified.
  4. Test-fit the stool against the window and side jambs. Check notch fit, horn length on both sides, and full contact along the sill or sill nose.
  5. Scribe and adjust for wall irregularities. Use a block plane or sanding block to fit the stool to the actual wall condition without exceeding the scribe allowance recorded on the Window Trim Record.
  6. Fasten the stool to backing without affecting window operation. Operate the window again immediately after fastening the stool and before proceeding to the apron.

I. Apron Installation

  1. Measure and cut the apron. Use the apron alignment dimension recorded on the Window Trim Record.
  2. Center the apron under the stool and fasten it. Verify centering against the stool horns before final fastening, and fasten into the backing identified in the Project 01 Obstacle and Backing Map.

J. Casing Layout and Installation

  1. Mark the approved casing reveal on side and head jambs. Use the exact value recorded on the Project 01 Controlling-Line Layout for this opening.
  2. Measure and cut side casings. Measure each side independently from the reveal line to the stool.
  3. Measure and cut the head casing. Use the joint method specified in the trim schedule.
  4. Test-fit casing joints and align them with stool horns. Confirm the inside edge of each side casing aligns with the outer edge of its corresponding stool horn.
  5. Fasten the head and side casing using the approved pattern. Follow the fastener schedule for spacing and location.
  6. Secure miters or butt joints as specified. Pin or fasten using the approved method while holding the joint closed.

K. Inspection and Correction

  1. Check reveals, stool level, apron centering, casing plane, and window operation. Compare each measurement against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Correct open joints, rocking stool, uneven horns, or binding. Use the troubleshooting procedure in this chapter for any failed check.
  3. Prepare the assembly for filling, caulking, paint, or stain. Record the completed assembly on the Window Trim Record and hold the room for Project 18.

Common Cutting Inspection

Apply the following checks to every stool, apron, and casing piece before it is fastened permanently.

  • Reference check: confirm the reveal marked on the jamb matches the Project 01 recorded value.
  • Notch check: confirm the stool notch depth matches the measured jamb depth and permitted scribe allowance.
  • Horn check: confirm horn length matches the trim schedule on both sides before the stool is fastened.
  • Miter check: confirm side and head casing miters close fully in a dry-fit before any fastener is driven.
  • Operation check: confirm the window operates correctly after the stool is fastened and again after casing is complete.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Stool Rocks After Fastening

Probable cause: the notch or scribe did not fully account for wall or sill irregularity identified in the Project 01 survey.
Confirmation: hold a straightedge under the stool and measure the gap at the rocking point.
Correction: remove the fasteners, re-scribe the affected edge with a block plane or sanding block, and test-fit again before refastening.
Reinspection: repeat the level check across the full stool length.

Horn Lengths Do Not Match on the Two Sides

Probable cause: horn length was marked from an assumed centerline instead of measured independently from each jamb.
Confirmation: measure horn length on each side from the outer edge of the installed side casing to the end of the stool.
Correction: if the stool is long enough, trim the longer horn to match the shorter one within tolerance; if not, replace the stool blank.
Reinspection: remeasure both horns after correction.

Casing Miter Will Not Close

Probable cause: the reveal line was not marked at the recorded Project 01 value on both jambs, producing side casing legs of unequal effective length.
Confirmation: recheck the reveal line on both jambs against the Project 01 recorded value.
Correction: remark the reveal at the correct value, and recut the affected piece using the Project 03 correction methods for minor errors.
Reinspection: dry-fit the joint again before fastening.

Window Binds After Casing Is Installed

Probable cause: a fastener or the casing itself intrudes into the sash or hardware clearance.
Confirmation: operate the window slowly and identify the exact point of contact.
Correction: remove the interfering fastener or trim the casing edge at the contact point without altering the recorded reveal elsewhere on the same piece.
Reinspection: operate the window through its full range again after correction.

Apron Appears Off-Center

Probable cause: the apron was centered on the rough opening or the wall opening instead of on the installed stool and its horns.
Confirmation: measure from each end of the apron to the corresponding stool horn end.
Correction: remove and reposition the apron using the stool horns as the centering reference, then refasten.
Reinspection: remeasure both distances after correction.

Performance Check

With the assembly complete, review every measurement against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the stool is level, both horns match the trim-schedule length within tolerance, the apron is centered beneath the stool, the side and head casing reveal match the Project 01 recorded value, every miter or butt joint closes without a visible gap, all fasteners are set below the surface without profile damage, and the window opens, closes, and latches without binding. Complete the Window Trim Worksheet and record the assembly status before moving to the next window or opening. If any item cannot be closed, record it as a condition and identify the correction required before Project 18 filling and caulking begins on this assembly.
