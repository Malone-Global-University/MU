Job Overview

This project qualifies you to take the preliminary cut list produced in Project 01 and the cutting and assembly station built in Project 02, and turn them into a labeled set of accurately measured, square-cut, and mitered trim components that are test-fitted and ready for installation technique. The finished work product is a set of specimen pieces cut to the approved cut list, each labeled with its assigned location, checked for square-cut and miter accuracy, test-fitted at a jig or practice assembly, and corrected within the stated tolerance.

You will review the cut list and joint types, inspect stock for defects, mark reference faces and cut lines from the correct measurement point, make controlled square and mitered cuts, test-fit the results, correct minor errors, and record recurring measurement or saw-angle errors so they do not carry into later installation chapters.

This project builds directly on Project 01's controlling reference lines and preliminary cut list, and on Project 02's saw setup, stock support, and blade-zone controls. Every later installation chapter in this book, from door casing in Project 04 through crown corners in Project 12 and stair trim in Projects 16 and 17, depends on the measuring, marking, and cutting method established here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FC-013 — Read finish measurements from the approved cut list and reference conventions.
  • FC-014 — Mark trim stock accurately, including reference face, orientation, and cut lines.
  • FC-015 — Cut square ends accurately using the miter saw station.
  • FC-016 — Cut miters accurately while preserving profile orientation.
  • FC-017 — Test-fit trim pieces at the intended assembly or practice jig.
  • FC-018 — Correct minor fit errors without concealing measurement or cutting mistakes.

Where This Skill Fits

Every installation project from Project 04 onward assumes the learner can measure from the correct reference point, mark a reference face and cut line without ambiguity, produce a square cut and an accurate miter, test-fit before permanent fastening, and correct a minor error without resorting to filler. This project isolates those skills on specimen pieces before they are combined with fastening, adhesive, and finish-grade decisions in later chapters.

The measurement references defined here — long point, short point, shoulder, and square end — are used without re-definition in every remaining chapter of this book.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved cut list, profile orientation, reference face, finished dimensions, and joint types
  • inspecting stock for bow, twist, cup, dents, chipped edges, finish defects, and profile variation
  • assigning each piece to a location before cutting
  • marking the reference face, top, bottom, left, right, and waste side on each piece
  • measuring from the correct long point, short point, shoulder, or square end according to the joint
  • making controlled square cuts and mitered cuts at the Project 02 workstation
  • labeling, test-fitting, and correcting minor fit errors within the stated tolerance
  • recording recurring measurement or saw-angle errors and replacing pieces that cannot meet tolerance

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • permanently fasten, glue, or install any specimen piece into a wall, door, window, floor, or ceiling assembly; this project produces test-fitted specimens only
  • fill large gaps caused by incorrect measurement or cutting; a piece with a major gap is recut or replaced, not concealed
  • change the miter saw setup, blade, or stock-support configuration established and verified in Project 02 without reverifying square and common miter-angle accuracy
  • proceed to installation chapters with unresolved recurring measurement or saw-angle errors

Trade Note

A miter that closes on the saw but opens after the piece is moved to the wall is almost always a measurement-reference error, not a saw error. Confirm which point of the cut — long point, short point, or shoulder — the cut list is measured from before blaming the saw's angle accuracy.

Setup Standard

Do not begin cutting until the Project 02 workstation readiness inspection is current and the square and common miter-angle test cuts from that project are within tolerance. A saw that has been moved, bumped, or used by another learner since its last verification must be reverified with a fresh test cut before this project's specimens are cut.

Shop Tip

Cut one piece at the marked length, check it against the cut list and the test-fit jig, and only then use that piece to set a stop block for any remaining identical lengths. Setting a stop block from an unverified first cut repeats the same error on every piece that follows it.

Reference Conventions

Establish these references before any piece in this project is marked or cut, and do not change them mid-project.

TermDefinition

Short pointThe shorter corner of an angled cut, used as the measuring point when the cut list specifies short-point-to-short-point length

Common Field Fundamentals

Three conditions control accuracy in this project: the reference face and orientation marks are established before the first cut, the correct measurement point — long point, short point, shoulder, or square end — is used for every piece according to its joint type, and every completed piece is test-fitted before it is accepted. A cut that is dimensionally correct but taken from the wrong reference point will not close correctly at the wall, even though the saw performed exactly as set.

Measuring From the Correct Point

Confirm on the cut list which measurement point applies to each piece before marking it. A piece measured long-point to long-point and a piece measured shoulder to shoulder are marked and cut differently even when the finished length is the same number on the cut list. Mark the applicable point directly on the stock before cutting, not from memory.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Before Cutting

Confirm every measurement against the cut list before making the cut, not after. A measurement confirmed only by memory or by a single reading is the most common source of a short piece that cannot be corrected by sanding or planing.

Critical Safety Cue — Full Support and Blade-Stop Before Handling

Support stock fully on both sides of the saw before cutting. Allow the blade to come to a complete stop before raising it or moving the workpiece. Do not reach across the blade path, and keep hands outside the marked blade exclusion zone established in Project 02 at all times.

Field Safety Cue — Small-Piece and Offcut Handling

Use a push stick, hold-down, or clamp for any piece too short to safely support by hand near the blade. Clear offcuts from the table before the next cut rather than reaching past the blade path to remove them.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Blade Selection for Profile and Material

Confirm the blade installed at the Project 02 workstation is the blade specified for the current stock's material and required finish quality. Follow the blade manufacturer's instructions for the material and cut type before cutting MDF, composite trim, or hardwood-faced profiles.

E. Cut List and Stock Review

  1. Review the cut list, profile orientation, reference face, finished dimensions, and joint types. Confirm the cut list corresponds to the specimen set being produced and note the measurement point for each piece.
  2. Inspect stock for bow, twist, cup, dents, chipped edges, finish defects, and profile variation. Set aside any length that cannot produce an acceptable piece within the component schedule.
  3. Assign each piece to a location before cutting. Record the assigned location for each piece on the worksheet before marking begins.

F. Marking

  1. Mark the reference face, top, bottom, left, right, and waste side. Use consistent marks across every piece so orientation is unambiguous at the saw.
  2. Measure from the correct long point, short point, shoulder, or square end according to the joint. Confirm the measurement point against the cut list before marking.
  3. Transfer measurements using a sharp pencil, knife mark, or stop block. Use a stop block only after the first piece at that length has been verified.
  4. Confirm each measurement before cutting. Recheck the mark against the cut list a second time before the piece is placed at the saw.

G. Square Cuts

  1. Support stock fully at the saw. Use the left and right material supports established in Project 02 for any piece extending beyond the saw table.
  2. Make controlled square cuts with the reference face against the fence. Confirm the reference face is seated flat and square to the fence before cutting.
  3. Verify square-cut accuracy. Check the completed end against the combination square and record the result on the worksheet.

H. Miter Cuts

  1. Set the specified miter angle. Confirm the saw angle indicator against the cut list before cutting.
  2. Make left- and right-hand miter cuts while preserving profile orientation. Keep the reference face and top/bottom marks against the fence and table exactly as marked.
  3. Allow the blade to stop before raising it. Do not move or reposition the workpiece until the blade has come to a complete stop.
  4. Label each completed piece. Attach the labeling tape or tag with the assigned location immediately after cutting, before the piece is set aside.

I. Test-Fitting and Correction

  1. Test-fit pieces at the intended assembly or practice jig. Position the square-cut and mitered pieces at the jig exactly as they would meet in the field.
  2. Check joint closure, reveal, length, and profile alignment. Measure the gap at the joint face and compare it to the acceptance standard.
  3. Correct minor errors using recutting, shaving, sanding block, plane, or controlled back-beveling. Remove material in small, controlled amounts and retest after each correction.
  4. Do not fill large gaps caused by incorrect measurement or cutting. A piece with a gap beyond the acceptance standard is recut or replaced, not filled.
  5. Record recurring measurement or saw-angle errors. Note any pattern of short pieces, long pieces, or consistent miter gaps and identify the probable cause on the worksheet.
  6. Replace pieces that cannot meet the project tolerance. Cut a new piece from fresh stock rather than continuing to correct a piece that has been cut back beyond its finished dimension.

Common Cutting Inspection

Apply the following checks to every specimen piece produced in this project, regardless of profile or joint type.

  • Reference check: confirm the reference face mark is visible and was against the fence or table for the entire cut.
  • Measurement-point check: confirm the measurement was taken from the point specified for that joint type — long point, short point, shoulder, or square end.
  • Square check: confirm every square-cut end is within 1/32 in. of square using the combination square.
  • Miter check: confirm every mitered pair closes within 1/32 in. at the face when test-fitted.
  • Labeling check: confirm every completed piece is labeled with its assigned location before it leaves the cutting station.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Mitered Pair Is Open at the Face

Probable cause: the saw's miter angle was not verified against a fresh test cut before this project's specimens were cut, or the workpiece shifted during the cut.
Confirmation: test-cut two scrap pieces at the same angle and check the resulting joint at the jig.
Correction: reset and reverify the saw angle, then recut the affected pieces from fresh stock.
Reinspection: repeat the test-fit check on the recut pieces.

A Piece Is Consistently Short

Probable cause: the measurement was taken from the wrong reference point — for example, short point instead of long point — or the pencil line's width was not accounted for on the waste side.
Confirmation: compare the cut list's specified measurement point against the point actually used, and check which side of the marked line the blade removed.
Correction: recut a new piece using the correct reference point and confirm the waste side before cutting.
Reinspection: repeat the length check against the cut list.

A Square-Cut End Is Out of Square

Probable cause: the reference face was not fully seated against the fence, or debris was trapped between the stock and the fence during the cut.
Confirmation: check the fence and stock contact area for debris and recheck the reference-face seating on a scrap piece.
Correction: clean the fence, reseat the stock, and recut the piece.
Reinspection: recheck the new end with the combination square.

Profile Orientation Is Reversed on a Mitered Piece

Probable cause: the top/bottom or left/right marks were not checked against the cut list before the piece was placed at the saw.
Confirmation: compare the completed piece's profile orientation to the marked reference and to the intended location on the cut list.
Correction: recut a new piece with correct orientation; do not attempt to reverse-cut a piece already cut to the wrong hand.
Reinspection: confirm orientation against the cut list before labeling the new piece.

Corrections Have Reduced a Piece Below Its Finished Dimension

Probable cause: repeated sanding, shaving, or back-beveling was used to chase a fit instead of correcting the underlying measurement or angle error.
Confirmation: measure the piece's current length or angle against the cut list and the acceptance standard.
Correction: replace the piece with a new one cut from fresh stock using the corrected reference point or angle.
Reinspection: repeat the length, square, or miter check on the replacement piece.

Cleanup

This project ends with a labeled, test-fitted specimen set and a completed worksheet, not with installed trim.

  • Clear offcuts, sawdust, and small cutoffs from the saw table and surrounding floor area
  • Return the combination square, tape measure, marking tools, and stop block to their storage location
  • Store accepted specimen pieces flat, labeled, and protected from damage for reference in later chapters
  • Set aside rejected pieces for scrap or offcut disposal per the Project 02 workstation procedure
  • File the completed worksheet, including recorded recurring errors, with the room file established in Project 01

Field Terms

Reference Face
The face of the trim stock held against the saw fence or table and used for all orientation marks.
Long Point
The longer corner of an angled cut, used as a measuring point where specified.
Short Point
The shorter corner of an angled cut, used as a measuring point where specified.
Shoulder
The point where an angled cut meets the back or show face of the profile.
Square End
An end cut at 90 degrees to the length of the piece.
Waste Side
The side of a marked cut line removed by the blade.
Stop Block
A fixed reference used to repeat an identical length across multiple pieces.
Back-Bevel
A controlled secondary angle cut into the back of a joint to allow face contact.
Test-Fit
Positioning a completed piece at its intended joint or jig before permanent fastening.
Recurring Error
A measurement or saw-angle mistake that repeats across multiple pieces and must be diagnosed rather than individually corrected.
