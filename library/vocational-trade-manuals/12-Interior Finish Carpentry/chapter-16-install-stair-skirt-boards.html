Job Overview

This project qualifies you to scribe, cut, fit, and install one finish stair skirt board against an existing stair without changing the stair's structural geometry. The finished work product is a securely installed skirt board that follows the tread-and-riser profile of the stair, terminates correctly at the top and bottom of the run, and preserves the accepted rise, run, tread depth, nosing, landing, guard, and handrail conditions established before this project begins.

You will confirm that the stair has already passed structural inspection, inspect the wall and stair surfaces the skirt will contact, scribe the skirt blank directly against the treads and risers, cut the scribed profile with the approved saws and hand tools, test-fit the skirt without forcing it, fasten it to framing or approved backing, and inspect the finished stair-wall transition for gaps, security, and safety.

This project follows Project 15, which completed closet trim and shelving cleats, and precedes Project 17, which installs finish trim directly on the treads, risers, and nosings. The skirt board installed here establishes the wall-side reference that Project 17's tread and riser trim will fit against.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FC-091 — Measure the stair profile, including every tread, riser, nosing, and landing transition the skirt board must follow.
  • FC-092 — Scribe the skirt board directly against the existing stair and wall to capture the exact tread-riser-nosing profile.
  • FC-093 — Cut the skirt board to the scribed line using the approved saws and hand tools without altering stair geometry.
  • FC-094 — Fit the skirt board to the treads and risers, correcting minor contact points without forcing the piece.
  • FC-095 — Fasten the skirt board to framing or approved backing while keeping fasteners clear of nosings and concealed services.
  • FC-096 — Inspect the finished stair-wall transition for gaps, rocking, unsafe projections, and fastener security.

Where This Skill Fits

Every stair-trim project in this book depends on the stair having already passed a structural readiness decision before finish work begins. This project does not create or verify that structural acceptance; it uses the previously accepted rise, run, tread depth, nosing, landing, guard, and handrail conditions as the fixed geometry the skirt board must follow.

The skirt board installed here becomes the wall-side reference for Project 17, which installs tread, riser, and nosing trim. Any scribe error, forced fit, or altered geometry introduced in this project will surface as a fit or safety problem in Project 17.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved stair geometry, finish profile, skirt thickness, wall condition, tread and riser status, and code constraints before starting
  • confirming that rise, run, tread depth, nosing, landings, guards, and handrails have already been accepted through the responsible structural inspection
  • barricading or otherwise controlling stair use during the work
  • inspecting the wall, treads, risers, stringer, and floor transitions the skirt board will contact
  • establishing the top and bottom skirt termination details from the approved finish specification
  • selecting and acclimating a straight skirt blank
  • scribing the blank against the stair and wall, labeling cut lines, and cutting to the scribed profile
  • test-fitting, refining contact points, and installing the skirt board without changing tread width, nosing projection, or walking clearances
  • fastening the skirt board to framing or approved backing and inspecting the finished stair-wall transition

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • correct a noncompliant or structurally defective stair using finish trim; a stair that has not passed structural inspection is referred to the responsible trade or a qualified stair specialist before this project begins
  • alter rise, run, tread depth, nosing, landing, guard, or handrail geometry under this finish-trim chapter
  • leave the stair in unrestricted service with loose nosing, protruding fasteners, or a partially secured skirt board at any point during the work
  • fasten near electrical boxes, plumbing fixtures, service penetrations, or unlocated utility paths without first locating them

Any stair that has not received an approved structural readiness decision must be corrected by the responsible trade and reinspected before this project's skirt-board installation begins.

Trade Note

A skirt board is a finish piece, not a structural one. Every scribe and fit decision in this chapter is made against the stair as it already exists. Reshaping a tread, riser, or nosing to make the skirt board fit better is outside the scope of this project and must be referred back to the responsible trade rather than corrected with trim.

Setup Standard

Do not begin scribing until the stair has an approved structural readiness decision on file and the stair is barricaded or otherwise controlled against unrestricted use. A skirt board scribed or fastened against an unverified stair must be treated as unverified work until the structural acceptance record is confirmed.

Shop Tip

Label every cut line and waste area on the blank before removing it from the stair for cutting. A scribed profile that is clear at the stair can become difficult to interpret once the blank is on the bench, and mislabeling a waste side is a common cause of an unusable skirt blank.

Common Field Fundamentals

A stair skirt board is a scribed finish piece. Its accuracy depends entirely on a careful transfer of the actual tread, riser, nosing, and landing profile onto the blank, not on the nominal dimensions in the stair schedule. Because the stair remains in partial or full use during this project, controlling access and preserving safe walking conditions at every stage of the work is as important as the fit of the finished piece.

GeometryRise, run, tread depth, nosing, landing already accepted and preserved throughout this project
ScribeTread, riser, nosing, and landing profile transferred directly onto the skirt blank
FitTest-fitted without forcing, with contact points refined rather than gaps filled
AccessStair barricaded or controlled throughout scribing, cutting, fitting, and fastening

Scribing Against an Existing Stair

Position the skirt blank against the stair and wall in its approximate final location, supported so it cannot shift. Set the compass or scribing tool to the widest gap between the blank and the stair profile, then run the tool along the full length of the treads, risers, nosings, and landing transitions, transferring the profile onto the blank in one continuous pass. Label the waste side immediately after scribing, before the blank is moved.

Critical Safety Cue — Stair Access Control

Barricade the stair or establish an approved alternate route before scribing, cutting, fitting, or fastening begins. Do not leave the stair in unrestricted use at any point where nosing, treads, or the skirt board are loose, removed, or only partially secured. Restore full access only after the finished installation passes the inspection in this chapter.

Code Alert — Do Not Alter Accepted Stair Geometry

Do not use scribing, cutting, shimming, or fastening in this project to change rise, run, tread depth, nosing projection, landing dimensions, guard geometry, or handrail geometry. A stair that requires a geometry change to accept the skirt board is referred back to the responsible trade or a qualified stair specialist before this project continues.

Safety Cue — Coping Saw and Jigsaw Control

Support the skirt blank fully at the bench when coping or jigsawing the scribed profile. Keep hands clear of the cutting path, follow the waste side of the line, and allow the blade to stop completely before lifting it from the cut.

Measurement Cue — One Scribe, One Blank

Scribe and cut one skirt blank per continuous stair run unless the finish specification calls for a scarf joint at a defined location. Re-scribing a run in sections without a specified joint location introduces cumulative misalignment at each break.

F. Document Review and Stair Confirmation

  1. Review the approved stair geometry, finish profile, skirt thickness, wall condition, tread and riser status, and code constraints. Confirm the documents correspond to the stair being trimmed.
  2. Confirm that rise, run, tread depth, nosing, landings, guards, and handrails have been accepted. Locate the structural readiness decision on file and record its reference on the work order.
  3. Do not use finish trim to correct noncompliant or structurally defective stairs. If no structural readiness decision is on file, or if the stair shows visible defects, stop and refer the stair to the responsible trade before continuing.

G. Access Control and Surface Inspection

  1. Barricade or control stair use during work. Post the barricade and establish an approved alternate route if the stair is the only access to occupied space.
  2. Inspect the wall, treads, risers, stringer, and floor transitions. Note surface condition, moisture, movement, or damage that could affect the skirt fit or fastening.

H. Layout and Blank Preparation

  1. Establish the top and bottom skirt termination details. Confirm the specified termination style against the finish specification before cutting begins.
  2. Select and acclimate a straight skirt blank. Confirm the blank meets the specified material, thickness, and length, and has acclimated according to the finish specification.

I. Scribing

  1. Position the blank against the stair and wall. Support it so it cannot shift during scribing.
  2. Scribe each tread, riser, nosing, and landing transition. Set the scribing tool to the widest gap and run it in one continuous pass along the full profile.
  3. Label all cut lines and waste areas. Mark the waste side immediately, before the blank is removed from the stair.
  4. Remove the blank and verify the scribe sequence. Confirm every tread, riser, nosing, and landing transition is represented on the scribe line before cutting.

J. Cutting and Fitting

  1. Make straight, angled, and curved cuts using the approved saws and jigsaw. Follow the waste side of the scribe line throughout.
  2. Stay outside the final line and refine with plane, rasp, file, or sanding block. Remove material gradually rather than cutting directly to the line.
  3. Test-fit the skirt without forcing it. Position the cut piece against the stair and wall and check contact along the full profile.
  4. Refine contact points while preserving the full profile. Continue removing small amounts of material only at points where contact is incomplete.
  5. Confirm that the skirt does not change tread width, nosing, or walking clearances. Verify with the tape measure at representative points along the run.

K. Fastening and Final Inspection

  1. Apply adhesive only where specified. Use the product and coverage identified in the finish specification.
  2. Fasten to framing or approved backing. Follow the fastener and backing schedule for the field, terminations, and locations near nosings.
  3. Keep fasteners clear of stair nosings and concealed services. Angle or relocate fasteners as needed to avoid these locations.
  4. Inspect the stair-wall transition, top and bottom terminations, gaps, and fastener security. Check the full length of the installed skirt board against the Acceptance Standards table.
  5. Correct rocking, unsafe projections, and loose sections. Refasten, add backing, or replace the affected section as required.
  6. Keep the stair barricaded until the installation is secure. Remove the barricade only after the final inspection confirms a secure, safe installation.

Common Cutting Inspection

Apply the following checks to every cut made on the skirt blank, in addition to the general cutting standards established in Project 3.

  • Waste-side check: confirm the cut removed material from the labeled waste side of the scribe line, not the finish side.
  • Contact check: confirm the cut skirt board contacts every tread, riser, nosing, and landing transition along its length without a forced fit.
  • Geometry check: confirm tread width, nosing projection, and walking clearance are unchanged from the accepted structural readiness decision.
  • Termination check: confirm the top and bottom terminations match the specified detail.
  • Fastener check: confirm every fastener is set into framing or approved backing, clear of nosings and concealed services, and not proud of the surface.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Skirt Board Has Open Gaps at Several Treads

Probable cause: the scribing tool was reset partway through the pass, or the blank shifted during scribing.
Confirmation: hold the skirt board against the stair and measure the gap at each affected tread and riser.
Correction: reposition and reclamp the blank, rescribe the affected section using a consistent tool setting, and recut.
Reinspection: repeat the contact check along the full profile after recutting.

Skirt Board Rocks After Fastening

Probable cause: a high point was not identified during test-fitting, or a fastener missed the intended backing.
Confirmation: check for a high contact point with a straightedge and confirm fastener locations against the stud finder marks.
Correction: remove the affected fastener, refine the high point with a plane or sanding block, relocate the fastener to verified backing, and refasten.
Reinspection: repeat the stability check along the full length of the skirt board.

Skirt Board Appears to Reduce Tread Width

Probable cause: the skirt board was cut oversized or fitted proud of the tread nosing rather than scribed to the existing profile.
Confirmation: measure tread width at the affected location and compare it to the accepted structural readiness decision.
Correction: remove the skirt board, recut to the correct scribe line, and reinstall; do not sand or force the tread to fit around an oversized skirt board.
Reinspection: repeat the geometry check at every tread along the run.

Fastener Is Proud in a Location Near a Nosing

Probable cause: the fastener was driven straight into the skirt board rather than angled away from the nosing, or the fastener length exceeded the specified schedule.
Confirmation: inspect the fastener location and depth against the fastener and backing schedule.
Correction: remove the fastener, set a replacement fastener of the specified length and angle into confirmed backing, and set it below the surface.
Reinspection: repeat the fastener check at that location and at every fastener within the same run.

Stair Was Left Accessible with the Skirt Board Only Partially Secured

Probable cause: the barricade was removed or not maintained during a work break.
Confirmation: inspect the current barricade status and the fastening state of the skirt board.
Correction: restore the barricade immediately, complete fastening before any further access is permitted, and inspect the full installation before removing the barricade.
Reinspection: repeat the final inspection in Section K before restoring stair access.

Cleanup

This project ends with a secure, inspected skirt-board installation and a documented work record, not with removal of the finished piece.

  • Remove offcuts, scribe waste, and sawdust from the stair and landing before restoring access
  • Return saws, hand tools, clamps, and the stud finder to storage in working condition
  • Confirm every fastener is set and no tools or fasteners remain on a tread, riser, or landing
  • Store the compass or scribing tool setting notes with the project record for reference during Project 17
  • Remove the barricade only after the final inspection in Section K confirms a secure installation
  • File the completed work order, fastener and backing schedule, and inspection results with the stair project record

Field Terms

Skirt Board
The finish board installed along the wall side of a stair, following the tread-riser-nosing profile.
Scribe Line
The line transferred from the actual stair profile onto the skirt blank, used to guide cutting.
Waste Side
The side of the scribe line removed during cutting, labeled before the blank leaves the stair.
Top Termination
The specified detail where the skirt board ends at the top of the run or landing.
Bottom Termination
The specified detail where the skirt board ends at the base of the run or floor transition.
Contact Point
Any location where the skirt board touches a tread, riser, nosing, or landing surface.
Structural Readiness Decision
The prior, documented acceptance of stair geometry this project relies on and does not repeat.
Nosing
The projecting front edge of a tread, whose projection must not be altered by the skirt installation.
Stop-Work Condition
A documented hazard, defect, or missing structural acceptance that halts skirt-board work until corrected.
