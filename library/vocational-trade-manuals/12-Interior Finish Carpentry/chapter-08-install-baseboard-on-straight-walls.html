Job Overview

This project qualifies you to lay out, cut, and fasten baseboard along a set of straight wall runs in a room that has already passed the Project 01 readiness inspection and has completed door casing and other fixed trim. The finished work product is a set of securely fastened, correctly aligned straight baseboard runs with consistent top-edge alignment, an appropriate floor relationship, and corner ends prepared for coping and mitering.

You will confirm that door casing and fixed trim are complete, verify the finished-floor reference established in Project 01, locate studs along every run, plan runs to minimize visible joints, measure and cut each straight piece, dry-fit and scribe where the floor varies, fasten baseboard into framing using the approved pattern, and prepare the ends of each run for the corner work in Project 09.

This project depends directly on the room survey and controlling-line layout from Project 01 and on the completed door casing from Project 04. It supplies the straight, securely fastened runs that Project 09 copes and miters at the corners, and it establishes the top-edge line that Project 10 follows when installing shoe molding.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FC-043 — Lay out baseboard runs, including joint locations, from the room survey and controlling-line layout.
  • FC-044 — Cut baseboard to length using correct reference points for square-cut and joint-prepared ends.
  • FC-045 — Locate studs and mark fastening points along each planned run.
  • FC-046 — Fasten baseboard into framing using the approved fastener pattern.
  • FC-047 — Maintain the specified floor clearance or contact condition along each run.
  • FC-048 — Inspect a straight-wall baseboard installation for alignment, floor relationship, and joint fit.

Where This Skill Fits

This project uses the finished-floor reference, level layout lines, and stud and obstacle locations documented in Project 01, and it begins only after the door casing installed in Project 04 is complete. The straight runs and corner ends produced here are the direct input to Project 09, which copes the inside corners and miters the outside corners. The top-edge line established here also controls the shoe-molding installation in Project 10.

Do not begin this project until door casing and any other fixed trim in the room are complete, and do not proceed to Project 09 until every straight run in this project has passed inspection.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved baseboard profile, finish type, floor system, fastening method, joint plan, and corner sequence
  • confirming that door casing and other fixed trim are complete before baseboard layout begins
  • verifying the finished-floor elevation or controlled floor reference recorded in Project 01
  • inspecting walls and floors along each planned run for waves, humps, gaps, moisture, and debris
  • locating and marking studs above the trim line
  • planning runs to minimize visible scarf or butt joints and assigning factory ends and cut ends
  • measuring, cutting, dry-fitting, and scribing each straight run
  • fastening baseboard into framing using the approved fastener pattern, and installing scarf joints only over support or at approved locations
  • inspecting straightness, floor relationship, casing returns, and joint fit, and correcting bowed pieces, loose areas, high spots, and open joints
  • preparing corner ends for the coped and mitered corner work in Project 09

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin baseboard layout before door casing and other fixed trim are complete, or before the Project 01 readiness decision is Approved or its conditions are satisfied
  • cope or miter corner joints; corner fitting is covered in Project 09
  • install shoe molding or quarter round; floor-trim installation is covered in Project 10
  • fasten through floating flooring where the flooring system prohibits it
  • conceal a wave, gap, or floor-relationship problem with filler or excessive scribing rather than correcting or documenting the underlying condition

Trade Note

Baseboard is normally installed after door casing and other fixed trim, and before shoe molding. Installing baseboard before casing risks a visible mismatch at the casing return; installing shoe molding before baseboard removes the reference the shoe is meant to follow. Confirm this sequence before staging material for this project.

Setup Standard

Do not begin baseboard layout until door casing and other fixed trim in the room are complete and the Project 01 readiness decision is Approved, or every condition on a Conditional decision has been corrected and reinspected. Baseboard fastened before the finished-floor reference is confirmed must be treated as unverified until that reference is checked against the current floor condition.

Shop Tip

Lay out the entire room's baseboard runs on paper or the worksheet before cutting the first piece. Assigning factory ends and cut ends for the whole room at once, rather than run by run, produces fewer visible joints and a more efficient material takeoff.

Common Field Fundamentals

Straight-wall baseboard depends on three conditions established before the first cut: a confirmed finished-floor reference and top-edge line carried over from Project 01 without re-derivation at each wall; stud and backing locations marked along the full length of every planned run; and a run plan that assigns factory ends, cut ends, and scarf-joint locations for the whole room before any piece is cut. Skipping any of these three conditions is the most common cause of misaligned top edges, unsupported joints, and inconsistent floor contact in this project.

LayoutRuns planned from the room survey with joint and corner locations assigned before cutting
BackingStuds and approved backing located and marked above the trim line on every run
FitEach run measured, cut, dry-fitted, and scribed to the confirmed floor condition
FasteningBaseboard fastened into framing using the approved pattern, with the top edge held to the reference line

Run Planning and Joint Minimization

Plan every run in the room before cutting the first piece. Assign the longest available stock to the longest visible walls, place scarf joints only where a stud or approved support exists at the joint location, and use factory ends at fixed terminations such as casing returns where the trim schedule specifies a factory end. Record the plan on the Baseboard Run Record for each wall before proceeding to measurement.

Measurement Cue — Measure Every Run Independently

Measure each straight run from the correct corner or casing reference at both ends of the run rather than relying on a single room-length dimension. Walls are rarely perfectly parallel or perfectly square, and a run measured at only one point can be short or long at the opposite end.

Critical Safety Cue — Fastener Path and Concealed Services

Do not fasten baseboard until stud and backing locations are marked along the full length of the run. Do not drive fasteners near marked electrical box, receptacle, or plumbing locations identified in the Project 01 Obstacle and Backing Map. Stop and confirm the fastener path before driving any fastener where the obstacle map shows a nearby service.

Field Safety Cue — Long Stock Handling

Support long baseboard stock at both ends when carrying, staging, and cutting it. Keep the carry and installation path clear of tools, cords, and other trades' material to avoid tripping or dropping long pieces.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Adhesive and Fastener Compatibility

Where the specification calls for adhesive at a joint or substrate, follow the adhesive manufacturer's instructions for surface preparation, open time, and clamping or fastening while the adhesive cures. Use only the fastener type and finish specified for the baseboard material and any moisture exposure at the location.

F. Preparation and Run Planning

  1. Review the baseboard profile, finish type, floor system, fastening method, joint plan, and corner sequence. Confirm these against the approved trim schedule and fastener schedule before staging material.
  2. Confirm door casing and fixed trim are complete. Inspect each door and window casing for finished, accepted status before beginning baseboard layout.
  3. Verify finished-floor elevation or controlled floor reference. Confirm the single finished-floor reference recorded in Project 01 against current floor conditions.
  4. Inspect walls and floors for waves, humps, gaps, moisture, and debris. Record any condition exceeding the trim profile's ability to follow the surface on the Baseboard Run Record.
  5. Locate studs and mark them above the trim line. Mark every stud along each planned run using the stud finder, confirming against the Project 01 Obstacle and Backing Map.
  6. Plan runs to minimize visible scarf or butt joints. Assign factory ends and cut ends for every run in the room before cutting begins.

G. Measuring and Cutting

  1. Measure each straight run from the correct corner or casing reference. Measure both ends of the run independently and record both dimensions.
  2. Cut pieces to length using the Project 03 measuring, marking, and cutting procedure. Maintain the reference face and top-edge marks established in the run plan.

H. Dry-Fit and Floor Relationship

  1. Dry-fit the first run. Position the cut piece against the wall at the marked top-edge line and check fit at both ends.
  2. Scribe the bottom edge where required for major floor variation. Use the straightedge and scribing method appropriate to the recorded floor condition.
  3. Maintain the specified floor clearance or contact condition. Confirm the clearance or contact matches the trim schedule along the full length of the piece.

I. Fastening

  1. Apply adhesive only where specified. Follow the identified manufacturer instruction for surface preparation and open time.
  2. Fasten into framing using the approved fastener pattern. Drive fasteners at every marked stud location along the run.
  3. Keep the top edge aligned to the room reference. Check alignment against the level layout line before setting each fastener.
  4. Install scarf joints over support or at approved locations. Hold the joint closed while fastening both pieces into the shared stud.
  5. Do not fasten through floating flooring where prohibited. Confirm the flooring system and fastening restriction before driving any fastener near the floor edge.

J. Inspection and Corner Preparation

  1. Check straightness, floor relationship, casing returns, and joint fit. Inspect each completed run against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Correct bowed pieces, loose areas, high spots, and open joints. Refasten, reshim, or replace pieces that do not meet the standard.
  3. Prepare corner ends for Chapter 9. Leave each corner end square-cut, dry-fitted, and unfastened as specified in the run plan, ready for coping and mitering.

Common Cutting Inspection

Apply the following checks to every baseboard run before it is considered permanently installed.

  • Reference check: confirm the same finished-floor reference and top-edge line were used for every run in the room.
  • Backing check: confirm every fastener location corresponds to a marked stud or approved backing.
  • Joint check: confirm every scarf joint is located over support and closed without a visible gap.
  • Floor-relationship check: confirm floor clearance or contact matches the trim schedule along the full run.
  • Corner-preparation check: confirm every corner end is square-cut, dry-fitted, and unfastened as specified for Project 09.

Complete the Inspection Checklist in this project before beginning Project 09.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Top Edge Steps Down at a Joint

Probable cause: the piece was aligned to the adjacent piece rather than to the level layout line, allowing a small error to accumulate along the run.
Confirmation: hold the level against the top edge on both sides of the joint and compare to the reference line.
Correction: loosen or remove the fasteners nearest the joint, realign both pieces to the reference line, and refasten.
Reinspection: recheck top-edge alignment across the full run, not just at the corrected joint.

Baseboard Rocks or Will Not Sit Flat Against the Wall

Probable cause: a wall wave or protrusion identified in Project 01 was not accounted for in the run plan, or debris is trapped behind the piece.
Confirmation: hold the straightedge against the wall behind the piece and check for gaps or high spots.
Correction: remove the piece, clear debris, and either scribe the piece to the wall condition or refer the wall condition for correction if it exceeds what the profile can follow.
Reinspection: repeat the dry-fit and floor-relationship checks after correction.

A Scarf Joint Opens After Fastening

Probable cause: the joint was fastened without being held closed, or the joint was not located directly over the supporting stud.
Confirmation: check the joint location against the marked stud line and inspect for movement when light pressure is applied.
Correction: remove the fasteners at the joint, reposition the joint over a stud if it was not supported, hold it closed, and refasten.
Reinspection: repeat the joint-fit check listed in the Acceptance Standards table.

A Fastener Missed the Stud

Probable cause: the stud location was marked approximately rather than confirmed with the stud finder immediately before fastening.
Confirmation: inspect the fastener location against the marked stud line and check for a loose or spinning fastener.
Correction: remove the missed fastener, fill the hole per Project 18 procedures, relocate the fastener to the confirmed stud position, and refasten.
Reinspection: recheck the fastening pattern along the full run against the Fastener and Hardware Schedule.

Floor Clearance Varies Along a Run

Probable cause: the floor was not checked at representative points along the full run before scribing, or the finished-floor reference was not confirmed against current conditions.
Confirmation: measure clearance at several points along the run and compare to the trim schedule and finished-floor reference.
Correction: remove the piece, rescribe to the actual floor condition, and reinstall.
Reinspection: repeat the floor-relationship check at the same representative points after correction.

Cleanup

This project ends with permanently fastened straight runs and prepared corner ends, not with a fully closed-out room.

  • Remove offcuts, sharp fastener scrap, and packaging from the work area and place them in the designated disposal containers established in Project 02
  • Wipe excess adhesive from installed runs before it cures, per the manufacturer instruction identified in the fastener and hardware schedule
  • Return the nail gun, level, stud finder, and other tools to storage in working condition
  • Record the completed run plan, stud locations, fastener record, and any deficiencies on the Baseboard Run Record
  • Store the corner-end preparation notes with the room file for direct use in Project 09

Field Terms

Top-Edge Reference Line
The level layout line every baseboard run's top edge is aligned to around the room.
Factory End
The milled end of a baseboard piece, used at fixed terminations where specified.
Cut End
An end produced at the saw for a joint, corner, or termination not assigned a factory end.
Scarf Joint
A matched, angled joint between two baseboard pieces along a straight run, located over support.
Run Plan
The room-wide assignment of factory ends, cut ends, and joint locations made before cutting begins.
Floor Clearance
The specified gap or contact condition between the bottom edge of the baseboard and the finished floor.
Scribing
Transferring an irregular wall or floor line onto a piece so the piece follows that surface after cutting.
Corner End (Uncoped/Unmitered)
A square-cut, dry-fitted, unfastened end left at a corner for Project 09.
