Chapter 13

Install Ridge Cap Shingles

Prepare a straight, evenly terminated ridge or hip line, select or fabricate compatible cap shingles, and install a fully covered, concealed-fastener ridge-cap system on a non-vented ridge, hip, or separate practice ridge, using the exposure, fastening, and overlap requirements of the approved product instructions.

Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install a complete ridge-cap shingle system on a non-vented ridge, a hip, or a separate practice ridge assembly. The finished work product is a straight, fully covered ridge or hip line with concealed fasteners, manufacturer-compliant overlaps, and a documented inspection confirming alignment, exposure, side coverage, and terminal detail.

You will confirm the adjoining shingle fields terminate evenly and are secure, select or prepare compatible ridge-cap pieces, establish a starting end and centerline reference, fasten each cap through the designated zone so the next cap conceals the fastener, carry the overlap and exposure pattern through hip geometry changes, and complete the terminal detail with concealed or sealed fasteners.

Ridge caps are the final weather-shedding layer on the roof's highest lines. Every course of field shingles installed in Chapters 8 and 9 terminates here, and this chapter's work is the last opportunity to catch an uneven termination or a fastening defect before the ridge is closed.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-073 — Prepare the ridge line by verifying adjoining shingle-field termination and correcting only by approved methods.
  • RF-074 — Cut or select ridge-cap shingles compatible with the field-shingle product and approved for the specific ridge or hip condition.
  • RF-075 — Align cap shingles to centerline and exposure references established from the starting end.
  • RF-076 — Fasten ridge caps through the designated zone using nails of the correct length for the required substrate penetration.
  • RF-077 — Overlap caps by the specified amount so each cap conceals the fasteners of the piece beneath it.
  • RF-078 — Inspect the finished ridge for alignment, exposure, side coverage, fastener concealment, sealing, and damaged tabs.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter follows the asphalt-shingle field installed in Chapter 8 and the valley work in Chapter 9, and it depends on both roof planes terminating evenly at the ridge or hip. It also depends on the ridge-condition decision made before this work begins: a non-vented ridge is capped directly by this chapter, while a ridge carrying an exhaust-vent system is capped only after Chapter 14 installs the vent.

The completed ridge-cap system feeds directly into the Chapter 20 capstone, which installs ridge-cap shingles over a vent using the sequence this chapter and Chapter 14 establish together.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • confirming whether the ridge is non-vented or requires Chapter 14's ridge-vent system before capping
  • inspecting adjoining shingle fields for equal termination, adequate coverage, and secure fastening
  • selecting factory-made ridge caps or preparing cap pieces from approved shingles according to the product instructions
  • establishing the starting end, centerline, and exposure references
  • fastening caps through the designated zone with concealed-fastener overlap
  • adjusting cap pieces gradually at hips or other geometry changes
  • completing the final cap or terminal piece using the approved concealed-fastener or sealed-fastener detail
  • inspecting the finished ridge and documenting the completed work

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • cap over a planned ridge-vent slot before the vent is installed; that sequence is governed by Chapter 14
  • trim or repair field shingles beyond the approved correction method identified in this chapter; broader field-shingle defects are governed by Chapter 8 or Chapter 17
  • cut a ridge-vent slot or perform any ventilation-system work; that work is governed by Chapter 14
  • substitute an incompatible cap product, fastener, or sealant for the one identified by the roofing-system specification

Any ridge or hip section where the adjoining shingle fields fail this chapter's termination check must be corrected under the governing chapter before it receives ridge-cap shingles.

Trade Note

The ridge cap is the roof's most visible line from the ground. A wandering centerline or an inconsistent exposure is far more noticeable here than the same variation would be in a shingle field. Establish the centerline and exposure references before driving the first fastener, and check them at intervals rather than only at the end of the run.

Vented-Ridge Standard

Do not cap over a planned ridge-vent slot before the vent is installed. Confirm the ridge condition before beginning this chapter's work: a non-vented ridge is capped directly here; a ridge with a designed exhaust-vent slot is capped only after Chapter 14 installs the ridge-vent system, using that chapter's fastening and exposure instructions for the cap layer.

Shop Tip

Dry-lay a short run of cap shingles along the ridge before fastening the first piece. Confirming the exposure and overlap pattern on loose pieces is far faster than removing and repositioning fastened caps.

Common Field Fundamentals

The ridge-cap system is the final layer in the shingle-lap water path established across every earlier chapter of the shingle field. Its job is to cover the exposed upper termination of both roof planes while keeping every fastener hidden beneath the next cap. That pattern — establish a reference, fasten in the concealed zone, cover with the next piece, repeat — governs the entire installation.

Field Termination and Cap Preparation

Inspect the adjoining shingle fields for equal termination, adequate coverage, secure fastening, and a straight ridge line before selecting or preparing cap pieces. Trim or correct field shingles only by the approved method identified in Chapter 8 or Chapter 17. Select factory-made ridge caps or prepare cap pieces from approved shingles according to the product instructions, and reject cracked, overbent, cold-damaged, or incompatible cap pieces before installation begins.

Manufacturer Cue — Confirm Non-Vented Status Before Capping

Confirm whether the ridge is non-vented or whether Chapter 14 must first install a ridge-vent system. Do not cap over a planned ridge-vent slot before the vent is installed. If the ridge condition is unclear, stop and confirm it against the approved ventilation plan before cutting or fastening any cap piece.

Field Safety Cue — Ridge and Hip Footing

Ridge and hip lines are the highest and most exposed points on the roof. Maintain the roof-access and fall-protection provisions established in Chapter 2 throughout this work, and stop for wind, rain, frost, or other conditions that make ridge-line footing unsafe.

Measurement Cue — Establish Centerline Before the First Fastener

Mark the cap-shingle centerline and exposure references before fastening the first cap. Do not fasten caps by eye against the ridge line alone; a small starting error compounds visibly over the length of the run.

Code Alert — Wind and Fastening Requirements Vary

Required nail length, fastener quantity, and starting direction for ridge caps vary by wind exposure category and by product. Verify the applicable requirement against the adopted code and the manufacturer's instructions before beginning, and do not substitute a shorter or fewer fasteners than specified.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review the cap-shingle product, approved use, exposure, fastener length, fastening zone, wind direction, seal requirements, and terminal-course detail. Confirm the product instructions are current and record the exposure, fastener length, and fastening zone on the installation record before proceeding.
  2. Confirm whether the ridge is non-vented or whether Chapter 14 must first install a ridge-vent system. Check the ridge condition against the approved ventilation plan and record the confirmed condition on the installation record.
  3. Do not cap over a planned ridge-vent slot before the vent is installed. If the ridge is designed for a vent system, stop this chapter's work at that ridge line and route to Chapter 14 before continuing.
  4. Inspect the adjoining shingle fields for equal termination, adequate coverage, secure fastening, and a straight ridge line. Walk the full length of the ridge or hip from an approved position and record any uneven termination or loose fastening as a deficiency.
  5. Trim or correct field shingles only by the approved method. Refer any correction beyond the approved method to Chapter 8 or Chapter 17 before capping the affected section.
  6. Select factory-made ridge caps or prepare cap pieces from approved shingles according to the product instructions. Use only the cutting method, dimensions, and bend pattern specified by the manufacturer when field-preparing caps.
  7. Reject cracked, overbent, cold-damaged, or incompatible cap pieces. Set aside defective pieces before staging the accepted caps for installation.
  8. Establish the starting end based on prevailing wind or the manufacturer's required direction. Record the confirmed starting end and direction on the installation record.
  9. Mark the cap-shingle centerline and exposure references. Snap the centerline along the full ridge or hip length and mark exposure intervals from the starting end before fastening any cap.
  10. Position the first cap with the specified overhang or end treatment. Align the first cap to the centerline and starting-end reference and verify overhang before fastening.
  11. Fasten through the designated zones with nails long enough to penetrate the required substrate. Use only the fastener length, quantity, and location specified by the product instructions, and seat each fastener flush without cutting the cap surface.
  12. Cover each fastener with the next cap by the specified overlap. Position each successive cap so the overlap fully conceals the fasteners of the piece beneath it, and verify concealment before moving to the next piece.
  13. Continue along the ridge or hip while maintaining centerline, exposure, and side coverage. Check alignment against the centerline and exposure marks at regular intervals rather than only at the end of the run.
  14. Adjust cap pieces gradually at hips or geometry changes without splitting or distorting them. Make small, incremental adjustments across several pieces approaching a hip intersection or ridge-line change rather than forcing a single piece to absorb the full geometry change.
  15. Prepare the final cap or terminal piece using the approved concealed-fastener or sealed-fastener detail. Use only the terminal method specified by the product instructions for the confirmed ridge or hip condition.
  16. Apply hand sealant only where required by the product or weather conditions. Use only the manufacturer-approved adhesive at the specified locations, and do not substitute sealant for a required fastener or overlap.
  17. Inspect alignment, exposure, side coverage, fastener concealment, sealing, end treatment, and damaged tabs. Walk the full completed run from an approved position and record every finding against the acceptance standards above.
  18. Replace defective or misaligned caps before adhesive strips seal. Correct any finding from Step 17 while the affected caps can still be lifted and repositioned without damage.
  19. Clean the ridge and roof planes of cutoffs and loose fasteners. Remove cutoffs, loose nails, and packaging from the ridge, hip, and adjoining roof planes before documenting completion.
  20. Document product, starting direction, exposure, fastening, terminal detail, and inspection results. Photograph the completed ridge or hip and attach the images and measurements to the installation record.

Common Ridge-Cap Installation Checks

Apply the same checks to every ridge and hip line, regardless of length or roof configuration.

  • Ridge-vent status check: confirm the ridge is non-vented, or that Chapter 14's ridge-vent system is installed and accepted, before capping.
  • Field-termination check: confirm both adjoining roof planes terminate evenly with adequate coverage and secure fastening.
  • Centerline and exposure check: confirm centerline and exposure marks are established before the first fastener and held within tolerance along the full run.
  • Fastener-concealment check: confirm every fastener is covered by the overlap of the next cap.
  • Hip-transition check: confirm cap pieces are adjusted gradually at geometry changes without splitting or distortion.
  • Terminal-detail check: confirm the final cap uses the approved concealed-fastener or sealed-fastener detail.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Ridge Line Wanders Off the Centerline Partway Through the Run

Probable cause: the centerline was not marked before fastening began, or cumulative small misalignments were not checked at intervals.
Confirmation: re-snap the centerline over the installed caps and measure the deviation at regular intervals along the run.
Correction: lift and reposition caps back to the centerline starting from the first point of deviation, working forward toward the terminal end.
Reinspection: recheck alignment against the centerline at the same intervals after correction.

Exposed Fastener Found Beneath a Cap

Probable cause: the fastener was driven outside the designated zone, or the overlap on the covering cap was short of the specified amount.
Confirmation: lift the covering cap and measure the fastener location against the designated zone and the actual overlap achieved.
Correction: remove and reposition the fastener within the designated zone, or reposition the covering cap to achieve the specified overlap, whichever the finding requires.
Reinspection: recheck fastener concealment at that location and at adjacent caps before continuing the run.

Cap Shingle Splits or Distorts at a Hip Intersection

Probable cause: a single cap piece was forced to absorb the full geometry change instead of adjusting gradually across several pieces.
Confirmation: inspect the caps approaching the intersection for cracking, overbending, or forced alignment.
Correction: remove the damaged piece and reinstall the transition using gradual adjustment across the pieces approaching the intersection, per the manufacturer's hip-transition instructions.
Reinspection: recheck the transition for cracking, alignment, and coverage after correction.

Ridge-Vent Slot Found Capped Before the Vent Was Installed

Probable cause: the ridge condition was not confirmed against the ventilation plan before capping began.
Confirmation: compare the capped ridge section to the approved ventilation plan and framing plan.
Correction: remove the installed caps over the affected section, route the section to Chapter 14 for ridge-vent slot cutting and vent installation, and recap only after the vent system is accepted.
Reinspection: recheck the corrected section against this chapter's acceptance standards after Chapter 14 work is complete.

Terminal Cap Shows an Exposed or Unsealed Fastener

Probable cause: the terminal piece was installed using the standard mid-run fastening pattern instead of the approved concealed-fastener or sealed-fastener terminal detail.
Confirmation: inspect the terminal piece against the manufacturer's terminal-course detail.
Correction: remove and reinstall the terminal piece using the specified terminal detail, including hand-seal adhesive where required.
Reinspection: recheck the terminal piece for concealment and sealing before documenting completion.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a clean, fully covered ridge and an organized installation record, not loose cutoffs and unresolved fastener findings.

  • Remove cutoffs, loose granules, and rejected cap pieces from the ridge, hip, and adjoining roof planes
  • Collect and dispose of loose fasteners and packaging from the work area
  • Store unused compatible cap pieces for later authorized repair work
  • Compile the installation record, photographs, and inspection results into the project record
  • Confirm the ridge or hip condition (non-vented, or vented per Chapter 14) is recorded before the project moves to the next chapter

Performance Check

With the ridge-cap run complete, review the finished ridge and the Ridge-Cap Inspection Record against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm both adjoining shingle fields terminated evenly before capping, the ridge-vent status was correctly confirmed, every cap piece is free of cracking or distortion, centerline and exposure are maintained within tolerance along the full run, every fastener is concealed by the correct overlap, hip transitions were adjusted gradually, the terminal piece uses the approved detail, and the ridge and roof planes are clean of cutoffs and loose fasteners. If any item cannot be verified, record it as an open item and issue a conditional-complete decision rather than a full completion decision.

Chapter Closeout

You have completed the ridge-cap installation chapter: covering and sealing a ridge or hip line with the roof's final shingle layer. This chapter required you to verify field termination and correct only by approved methods (RF-073), select or prepare compatible cap shingles (RF-074), align caps to centerline and exposure references (RF-075), fasten each cap through the designated zone (RF-076), overlap caps to conceal every fastener (RF-077), and inspect and document the finished ridge (RF-078).

You now have a documented, straight, fully covered ridge or hip cap system that closes the shingle-lap water path established across the entire field-shingle installation. Where this ridge carries a designed exhaust-vent system, this chapter's work coordinates directly with Chapter 14's ridge-vent installation and the required cap-shingle fastening over the vent.

Store the completed installation record, photographs, and inspection results with the project record. The Chapter 20 capstone references this record when capping the shed's ridge over its installed vent system.
