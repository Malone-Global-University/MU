Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install a complete gutter and downspout system on one approved eave and to prove that the system collects and discharges roof runoff correctly. The finished work product is a secured, sloped, jointed gutter run with a fastened downspout that carries water to an approved discharge point, verified by a controlled water-flow test.

You will confirm the fascia, roof edge, drip edge, and roof covering are complete and safe to work beneath; measure the eave and establish the required slope; mark and install hanger, outlet, corner, and end-cap components; lift, fasten, and join gutter sections while maintaining slope through every joint; install the downspout run to an approved discharge; and test the completed system with a controlled flow of water from the high point.

This chapter depends on the completed roof-edge assembly from Chapter 15. It also depends on the roof covering, drip edge, and underlayment installed in earlier chapters, since the gutter system must collect water shed from the finished roof plane without interfering with any of those completed layers.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-091 — Establish gutter slope from measured high- and low-point references.
  • RF-092 — Install gutter hangers at the approved fascia or framing locations and spacing.
  • RF-093 — Join gutter sections using the manufacturer's lap, connector, rivet, screw, gasket, or sealant method while maintaining slope.
  • RF-094 — Install sealed end caps and outlet fittings using compatible components and sealant.
  • RF-095 — Install downspouts, elbows, straps, and discharge fittings to an approved route and discharge location.
  • RF-096 — Test water flow from the gutter high point and correct slope, joint, hanger, outlet, and discharge defects.

Where This Skill Fits

The gutter and downspout system installed in this chapter is the final water-control layer in the roof-drainage path established across this book: deck, edge metal, membrane, underlayment, shingle field, flashing, and ridge ventilation all shed water toward the eave, and this chapter carries that water away from the building.

This chapter depends on the completed fascia and roof-edge assembly from Chapter 15 and on the finished roof covering from the shingle, valley, and flashing chapters. The Chapter 20 capstone installs gutters and downspouts on the practice shed using this same procedure.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing roof-drainage calculations, gutter profile, material, size, hanger type and spacing, outlet size, downspout route, discharge location, and manufacturer instructions
  • confirming that fascia, roof edge, drip edge, roof covering, access, and below-eave work zones are complete and safe
  • measuring the eave and establishing the required gutter slope from measured high- and low-point references
  • marking hanger locations and preparing outlets, corners, end caps, and expansion components
  • lifting, supporting, fastening, and joining gutter sections while maintaining the established slope through joints and corners
  • installing downspout elbows, vertical sections, straps, offsets, and lower discharge fittings
  • connecting downspouts to approved extensions, splash blocks, drains, or underground systems without sealing required cleanouts
  • conducting a controlled water-flow test and correcting slope, leakage, hanger, outlet, or discharge defects

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin gutter installation before the fascia, roof edge, drip edge, and roof covering from earlier chapters are complete and accepted
  • route discharge to a location that creates foundation saturation, erosion, icing, sidewalk hazards, or neighboring-property impacts; confirm the approved discharge route before installation begins
  • represent the controlled water-flow test in this chapter as a whole-building watertightness test; it verifies only the gutter and downspout run installed here
  • seal a required cleanout, overflow provision, or expansion joint in the course of joining or sealing gutter and downspout components

Any eave or downspout condition that cannot meet the acceptance standards in this chapter must be corrected before the system is documented as complete.

Trade Note

Slope is the single most important measurement in this chapter. A gutter that looks straight from the ground can still hold standing water if the slope was not established and checked from fixed high- and low-point references. Set the slope before you fasten anything, and check it again after every joint.

Access Standard

Do not begin gutter installation until fascia, roof edge, drip edge, roof covering, access, and below-eave work zones have been confirmed complete and safe. Use the same roof-access and fall-protection system accepted in Chapter 2, and confirm the below-eave exclusion zone before any material or component is lifted.

Shop Tip

Prepare outlets, end caps, and corner pieces on the ground before lifting long gutter sections. Sealing and fitting these components at height slows the work and increases the chance of a dropped fastener or tool.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every gutter run in this chapter follows the same pattern: confirm the eave is ready, establish slope from fixed high- and low-point references, install supports and terminations before joining sections, maintain slope through every joint and corner, complete the downspout to an approved discharge, and prove the result with a controlled water-flow test rather than a visual assumption.

Slope and Support

Establish the required gutter slope using measured high- and low-point references before marking the hanger reference line. Mark hanger locations according to the product, expected loads, and local conditions, and confirm the gutter is not placed above the roof's effective drainage path.

Critical Safety Cue — Confirm the Roof Edge Before Working Beneath It

Do not begin gutter or downspout installation until the fascia, roof edge, drip edge, and roof covering above the work area are confirmed complete and safe. Establish and maintain the below-eave exclusion zone from Chapter 2 for the entire duration of lifting, fastening, and testing.

Measurement Cue — Set Slope From Fixed References

Establish the gutter slope using measured high- and low-point references, not a visual estimate. Recheck the slope after every hanger is installed and again after every section is joined, since fastening can shift the run out of the established slope.

Code Alert — Discharge Location

Confirm that the planned downspout discharge route will not create foundation saturation, erosion, icing, sidewalk hazards, or neighboring-property impacts before installation begins. Local drainage requirements vary; verify the approved discharge location and any connection to an underground or municipal system before connecting the downspout.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review roof-drainage calculations, gutter profile, material, size, hanger type, hanger spacing, outlet size, downspout route, discharge location, snow or ice requirements, and manufacturer instructions. Confirm the revision or date on each document before using it as a reference, and record the confirmation on the governing-documents table above.
  2. Confirm that fascia, roof edge, drip edge, roof covering, access, and below-eave work zones are complete and safe. Locate the signed acceptance records from Chapters 3, 8, and 15. If any is missing or shows an open item, stop this chapter and route the project back to the responsible authority before continuing.
  3. Inspect fascia for alignment, sound fastening, decay, damage, and adequate support for the approved hanger system. Record any deficiency and refer correction to Chapter 15 before installing hangers over the affected section.
  4. Measure the eave length and identify high point, low point, outlet locations, corners, end caps, expansion joints, and downspout path. Record each location on the installation record using fixed references established from the eave ends.
  5. Confirm that the discharge route will not create foundation saturation, erosion, icing, sidewalk hazards, or neighboring-property impacts. Verify the approved discharge location before proceeding, and record the confirmation on the installation record.
  6. Establish the required gutter slope using measured high- and low-point references. Calculate and record the required drop from the drainage calculation and the measured eave length.
  7. Snap or mark the hanger reference line without placing the gutter above the roof's effective drainage path. Use a chalk line or laser level set to the established slope, and verify the line at both ends and at the midpoint before marking hanger locations.
  8. Mark hanger locations according to the product, expected loads, and local conditions. Use the spacing identified in the hanger schedule and mark each location on the fascia along the reference line.
  9. Cut gutter sections with the approved tool and remove burrs and sharp edges. Cut to the lengths measured in Step 4, and deburr each cut edge before handling or installation.
  10. Prepare outlets, corners, end caps, and expansion components before lifting long sections. Fit and seal these components at a ground-level or bench position where practical, per Manufacturer Instruction Cue requirements.
  11. Install sealed end caps and outlet fittings using compatible components and sealant. Apply sealant only at the manufacturer-specified locations and seat each fitting fully before it is installed on the gutter run.
  12. Lift and support the first gutter section with the required workers or equipment. Confirm the below-eave exclusion zone is active before lifting, and use the number of workers or lifting equipment specified for the gutter length and material.
  13. Fasten hangers into the approved fascia or framing locations without stripping fasteners or deforming the gutter. Drive each fastener to the seating condition specified by the hanger manufacturer, and recheck slope at each hanger before moving to the next.
  14. Join additional sections using the manufacturer's lap, connector, rivet, screw, gasket, or sealant method. Complete each joint fully before loading the joint with additional gutter length, and orient laps to shed water in the direction of flow.
  15. Maintain the established slope through joints and corners. Recheck the slope reference line after each joint and correct any deviation before continuing to the next section.
  16. Install downspout elbows, vertical sections, straps, offsets, and lower discharge fittings. Follow the downspout route identified in Step 4 and fasten straps at the spacing specified by the product instructions.
  17. Keep downspouts plumb or aligned to the approved route and maintain required wall clearance. Verify plumb or approved alignment at the top, middle, and bottom of each vertical run before final fastening.
  18. Connect downspouts to approved extensions, splash blocks, drains, or underground systems without sealing required cleanouts. Confirm the discharge connection matches the approved discharge location identified in Step 5, and leave every required cleanout accessible.
  19. Conduct a controlled water-flow test beginning at the gutter high point. Introduce water at the high point at a rate consistent with the approved test method and observe the full run to the discharge point.
  20. Observe slope, standing water, joint leakage, outlet flow, overflow, downspout movement, and discharge performance. Record each observation against the acceptance standards in Section C above.
  21. Correct reverse slope, low spots, leaking joints, loose hangers, blocked outlets, and unsafe discharge. Complete only the corrections identified as deficiencies during the test, and repeat the controlled water-flow test on the corrected section.
  22. Clean the system and document dimensions, slope, hanger spacing, outlet locations, discharge route, and test results. Remove cutoffs, fasteners, and debris from the gutter run and below-eave area, and complete the installation record before closing out the chapter.

Common Gutter and Downspout Checks

Apply the same checks to every gutter run and downspout, regardless of eave length or configuration.

  • Readiness check: confirm fascia, roof edge, drip edge, and roof covering are complete and accepted before installation begins.
  • Slope check: confirm the measured slope matches the drainage calculation at the high point, low point, and midpoint.
  • Support check: confirm hangers are installed at the specified spacing with fasteners fully seated and undamaged.
  • Joint and termination check: confirm every joint, corner, outlet, and end cap is installed by the specified method and free of leakage during the test.
  • Downspout check: confirm plumb or approved alignment, required wall clearance, and a secure connection to the approved discharge location.
  • Flow-test check: confirm the controlled water-flow test shows no standing water, leakage, overflow, or discharge failure beyond the permitted condition.

Complete the Gutter Installation and Test Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before closing out this chapter.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Standing Water Found Along the Gutter Run

Probable cause: a low spot from an improperly set slope, a shifted hanger, or a joint that settled after fastening.
Confirmation: remeasure the slope at the affected section against the established high- and low-point references and inspect nearby hangers for movement.
Correction: loosen and reset the affected hangers or joint to restore the established slope; do not correct standing water with additional sealant alone.
Reinspection: repeat the controlled water-flow test on the corrected section.

Joint Leaks During the Water-Flow Test

Probable cause: an incomplete lap, connector, or sealant application, or a joint installed out of the manufacturer-specified orientation.
Confirmation: inspect the joint from below during a repeat test and identify the exact point of leakage.
Correction: disassemble and reinstall the joint using the specified method and orientation; do not rely on additional exterior sealant to compensate for an incomplete joint.
Reinspection: repeat the controlled water-flow test on the corrected joint.

Outlet Overflows During the Test

Probable cause: outlet size undersized for the approved drainage calculation, debris in the outlet, or a downspout partially blocked.
Confirmation: compare the installed outlet size to the drainage calculation and inspect the outlet and downspout for obstruction.
Correction: clear any obstruction; if the outlet size does not match the approved calculation, route the finding to the responsible authority for a corrected specification before continuing.
Reinspection: repeat the controlled water-flow test after the obstruction is cleared or the outlet is corrected.

Downspout Moves or Pulls Away From the Wall Under Flow

Probable cause: insufficient strap spacing, a strap fastened into unsupported material, or a downspout section not fully seated at its joint.
Confirmation: inspect strap locations and fastening substrate against the approved spacing schedule and check each downspout joint for full seating.
Correction: add or relocate straps into approved fastening substrate and reseat any incomplete downspout joint.
Reinspection: repeat the controlled water-flow test and confirm the downspout remains stable under flow.

Discharge Creates Standing Water Near the Foundation

Probable cause: a discharge extension that is too short, a splash block or drain connection positioned incorrectly, or ground grading that directs water back toward the foundation.
Confirmation: observe the discharge point during the controlled water-flow test and compare the observed result to the approved discharge location and route.
Correction: extend, redirect, or reconnect the discharge to the approved location; do not leave a discharge condition that creates foundation saturation, erosion, or icing.
Reinspection: repeat the controlled water-flow test and confirm the discharge reaches the approved location without pooling near the foundation.

Gutter Section Deforms or Sags Between Hangers

Probable cause: hanger spacing wider than the approved schedule, or a hanger installed into unsupported fascia.
Confirmation: measure the hanger spacing at the affected section against the approved schedule and inspect the fascia condition behind the sagging section.
Correction: add hangers at the required spacing, or correct the fascia condition per Chapter 15 before adding a hanger.
Reinspection: recheck slope and support at the corrected section and repeat the controlled water-flow test.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a tested, documented gutter and downspout system, not a pile of cutoffs and loose fasteners.

  • Remove gutter and downspout cutoffs, loose fasteners, and packaging from the roof, fascia, and ground below
  • Remove sealant residue from any surface where it could interfere with finish work or later inspection
  • Store leftover hangers, outlets, end caps, and downspout components for reuse in later authorized corrections
  • Compile the installation record, measurements, and controlled water-flow test results into the project record
  • Confirm the below-eave exclusion zone is cleared and restored to normal use before closing out the chapter

Field Terms

High Point / Low Point
The fixed references at the top and bottom of the established gutter slope.
Outlet
The fitting connecting the gutter to the downspout, passing water out of the gutter run.
End Cap
The sealed fitting that closes the non-outlet end of a gutter run.
Hanger
The fastened support carrying the gutter's weight and load and maintaining its slope.
Expansion Joint
A connector allowing gutter sections to move with temperature change without leaking.
Discharge Location
The approved point where downspout water is released, extended, or connected to a drain system.
Controlled Water-Flow Test
Observed water introduced at the gutter high point and tracked through the system to the discharge point.
Splash Block
A discharge component placed beneath a downspout outlet to direct water away from the foundation.

Performance Check
