Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to cut a ridge-vent slot, install a compatible ridge-exhaust vent, and verify the complete intake-to-exhaust attic ventilation path on an approved roof assembly. The finished work product is a straight, correctly dimensioned ridge slot; a fully seated and fastened ridge vent covered by compatible ridge-cap shingles; and a documented verification that intake vents, baffles, rafter channels, and the ridge exhaust remain unobstructed and appropriately balanced.

You will review the approved ventilation design and required net free ventilating area, confirm intake ventilation is present, inspect the attic for obstructions and hazards, mark and cut the slot without damaging structural framing or concealed services, install the vent with the manufacturer's fastening and joining method, install ridge-cap shingles over the vent using the vent manufacturer's instructions, and confirm the airflow path from intake to exhaust before documenting the work.

This chapter depends directly on Chapter 13. If the approved system requires a vented ridge, the ridge in this chapter has not yet received cap shingles; Chapter 13's non-vented cap procedure does not apply here. The cap-shingle work in this chapter follows immediately after the vent is installed, using the vent manufacturer's exposure and fastening instructions rather than the general cap-shingle procedure alone.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RF-079 — Cut the ridge-vent slot to the manufacturer-specified width and depth without cutting structural framing or concealed services.
  • RF-080 — Maintain required structural and service clearances at ridge ends, walls, hips, chimneys, and framing interruptions.
  • RF-081 — Install the ridge vent with correct alignment, joints, and fastening.
  • RF-082 — Fasten vent components using the manufacturer-specified fastener, spacing, and seating condition.
  • RF-083 — Coordinate intake and exhaust ventilation to achieve the required net free ventilating area and balance.
  • RF-084 — Inspect and document the completed airflow path from intake to ridge exhaust.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter sits between Chapter 13's non-vented ridge-cap procedure and the general ridge-cap technique it depends on. When the approved roof system specifies a ridge-vent, this chapter's slot-cutting and vent-installation work must be completed before any ridge cap shingle covers the ridge; capping over an uncut or unvented ridge first would trap the intended exhaust path under sealed shingles.

The airflow path verified at the end of this chapter — intake through soffit or edge vents, through unobstructed rafter bays, and out through this ridge vent — is also checked again in Chapter 15 when fascia, soffit, and roof-edge ventilation are completed, and in the Chapter 20 capstone when the entire roof system is inspected as a whole.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved ventilation design, required net free ventilating area, intake-exhaust balance, ridge-vent product, slot dimensions, end clearances, and framing restrictions
  • confirming that soffit, edge, or other intake ventilation is present or is included in coordinated work
  • inspecting the attic for blocked rafter bays, insulation obstructions, fire-blocking conflicts, moisture, wiring, ducts, and unsuitable exhaust terminations
  • marking and cutting the ridge-vent slot to the manufacturer-specified width and to the roof-deck thickness only
  • installing the ridge vent, joining sections, and fastening components
  • installing compatible ridge-cap shingles over the vent using the vent manufacturer's fastening and exposure instructions
  • verifying the complete intake-to-exhaust airflow path and documenting slot dimensions, product information, and inspection results

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • cut trusses, rafters, ridge beams, connectors, or any structural framing member; if the proposed slot would cut any of these, stop work and obtain an approved framing detail before proceeding
  • cut through wiring, ducts, or other concealed service components; locate and protect these before cutting
  • terminate bathroom, kitchen, dryer, or combustion exhaust into the attic in place of, or in addition to, the ridge-vent exhaust path
  • install ridge-cap shingles over the vent using Chapter 13's non-vented procedure alone; use the vent manufacturer's fastening and exposure instructions for this installation
  • declare the ventilation system complete if intake ventilation has not been verified present or has not been coordinated as part of this chapter's work

Any obstruction, structural conflict, or concealed-service conflict discovered during this chapter must be resolved by the responsible authority before the slot is cut in the affected area.

Trade Note

A ridge vent only performs as designed when it is paired with adequate, unobstructed intake ventilation and an unobstructed path between the two. Cutting a correct slot and installing a correct vent over a starved or blocked intake path produces a ridge vent that looks complete but does not ventilate the attic as intended. Verify the intake side of the system before considering this chapter's work finished.

Structural Standard

Stop work immediately if the proposed slot would cut trusses, rafters, ridge beams, connectors, wiring, or other structural or service components. Mark slot limits only from approved ridge ends, walls, hips, chimneys, and framing interruptions, and set cutting depth to the roof-deck thickness only.

Shop Tip

Inspect the attic before marking the slot, not after. Locating wiring, ducts, and framing conflicts from below lets you adjust slot limits before a single cut is made, rather than discovering a conflict mid-cut.

Reference Conventions

Establish these references before marking the slot, and use them consistently across the slot layout and airflow verification record.

Common Field Fundamentals

A ridge-vent installation is only as good as the airflow path it serves. Every step in this chapter protects one of three things: the structural framing beneath the slot, the concealed services routed through the attic, and the continuity of the intake-to-exhaust airflow path. Confirm each before, during, and after cutting.

Attic Inspection Before Cutting

Inspect the attic for blocked rafter bays, insulation obstructions, fire-blocking conflicts, moisture, wiring, ducts, and unsuitable exhaust terminations before marking the slot. Confirm that bathroom, kitchen, dryer, and combustion exhausts do not terminate into the attic; any exhaust terminating into the attic must be corrected before this chapter's work is considered complete.

Critical Safety Cue — Stop Work at Any Structural or Service Conflict

Stop work if the proposed slot would cut trusses, rafters, ridge beams, connectors, wiring, or other structural or service components. Do not cut around a suspected conflict without confirming its exact location and routing first. Route the conflict to the responsible framing or trade authority before cutting resumes in that section.

Field Safety Cue — Dropped-Object and Attic-Debris Control

Establish dropped-object and attic-debris controls below the cut before starting the saw. Contain sawdust, nails, and deck fragments so they do not fall into insulation, onto stored items, or into occupied space below.

Measurement Cue — Slot Width and Cutting Depth

Snap parallel cut lines using the manufacturer-specified slot width. Set cutting depth to the roof-deck thickness only; do not set the saw deeper than the deck to "be sure" the cut is complete, since this risks cutting into ridge framing.

Code Alert — Net Free Ventilating Area and Intake Balance

Required net free ventilating area and the intake-exhaust balance are set by the approved ventilation design and adopted code, not by the ridge-vent product alone. Confirm both before installation and record the calculation on the ventilation verification record.

D. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review the approved ventilation design, required net free ventilating area, intake and exhaust balance, ridge-vent product, slot dimensions, end clearances, framing restrictions, and cap-shingle requirements. Record the required net free ventilating area and the ridge-vent product on the ventilation verification record before proceeding.
  2. Confirm that soffit, edge, or other intake ventilation is present or included in the coordinated work. If intake ventilation is absent or insufficient, coordinate its installation as part of this chapter's scope rather than proceeding with exhaust-only work.
  3. Inspect the attic for blocked rafter bays, insulation obstructions, fire-blocking conflicts, moisture, wiring, ducts, and unsuitable exhaust terminations. Use a flashlight from the attic access point and record every obstruction found along the ridge line and adjoining rafter bays.
  4. Stop work if the proposed slot would cut trusses, rafters, ridge beams, connectors, wiring, or other structural or service components. Route any such conflict to the responsible framing or trade authority for an approved resolution before marking the slot in that section.
  5. Mark slot limits from approved ridge ends, walls, hips, chimneys, and framing interruptions. Measure end clearances from the references identified in the ventilation design and mark them on the ridge before snapping cut lines.
  6. Snap parallel cut lines using the manufacturer-specified slot width. Use a chalk line referenced from the ridge centerline so both cut lines remain parallel and equidistant from the ridge along the full slot length.
  7. Set cutting depth to the roof-deck thickness only. Confirm the saw depth setting against the measured or specified deck thickness before making the first cut, and recheck it if the deck thickness changes along the ridge.
  8. Establish dropped-object and attic-debris controls below the cut. Position containment material in the attic below the cut path and confirm the roof-access exclusion zone from Chapter 2 remains in effect below the roof edge.
  9. Cut one side of the slot in controlled sections without crossing marked limits. Cut short, controlled sections rather than one continuous pass, and stop at each marked end-clearance limit.
  10. Cut the opposite side and remove deck strips without damaging framing. Use a pry bar to lift the cut strip evenly, working from one end, and confirm the underlying framing remains undamaged as each strip is removed.
  11. Remove sawdust, nails, loose deck pieces, and debris from the attic and roof. Clear the containment material from Step 8 and inspect the immediate area for any debris that missed containment.
  12. Inspect slot width, continuity, end clearances, and exposed framing. Compare the completed slot to the manufacturer-specified width and the marked end clearances, and confirm no framing member was cut or damaged.
  13. Correct only authorized deck-edge defects before installing the vent. Refer any framing damage discovered during this inspection to the responsible structural authority before continuing; do not proceed with vent installation over damaged framing.
  14. Center or align the ridge vent over the slot in the manufacturer-required direction. Confirm the vent's designated orientation before placing the first section, and align it to the ridge centerline for its full length.
  15. Join vent sections using the specified end-lap, connector, or butt-joint method. Use only the joining method identified in the manufacturer's instructions, and confirm each joint remains continuous and unobstructed.
  16. Fasten the vent with the required fasteners, length, spacing, and seating condition. Drive each fastener to the specified seating condition without crushing the vent's internal baffle or airflow channel.
  17. Keep the vent straight, fully supported, and uncrushed. Sight down the ridge after fastening to confirm the vent remains straight and uniformly supported along its length.
  18. Install compatible ridge-cap shingles over the vent using the vent manufacturer's fastening and exposure instructions. Use the vent manufacturer's exposure and fastener-length requirements rather than the general Chapter 13 non-vented schedule, since fastener length and exposure often differ over a vented ridge.
  19. Verify that intake vents, baffles, rafter channels, and ridge exhaust remain unobstructed. Recheck the attic from the access point used in Step 3 and confirm no installation debris or displaced insulation has blocked the airflow path created in this chapter.
  20. Confirm that bathroom, kitchen, dryer, and combustion exhausts do not terminate into the attic. Trace each mechanical exhaust identified during the Step 3 inspection to its actual termination point and record the confirmed exterior termination or the correction required.
  21. Inspect vent alignment, slot coverage, fastener penetration, end plugs, cap-shingle coverage, and airflow continuity. Complete the Ventilation Verification Record below, and record the ventilation calculations, product information, slot dimensions, intake condition, and inspection results.

Common Ridge-Vent Installation Checks

Apply the same checks to every ridge or hip section receiving a vent, regardless of ridge length or roof configuration.

  • Structural prerequisite check: confirm no framing member, connector, or concealed service was cut before installing the vent.
  • Slot check: confirm slot width, continuity, and end clearances match the manufacturer-specified dimensions and the approved ventilation design.
  • Vent installation check: confirm alignment, joints, fastening, and support meet the manufacturer's instructions.
  • Cap-shingle check: confirm ridge-cap shingles are installed over the vent using the vent manufacturer's fastening and exposure instructions, not the general non-vented schedule alone.
  • Airflow check: confirm intake vents, baffles, rafter channels, and ridge exhaust remain unobstructed and that no mechanical exhaust terminates into the attic.
  • Documentation check: confirm the ventilation calculations, product information, slot dimensions, intake condition, and inspection results are complete before closeout.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Saw Cuts Deeper Than the Deck and Nicks the Ridge Board

Probable cause: the depth gauge was not verified against the actual deck thickness, or deck thickness changed along the ridge without resetting the saw.
Confirmation: inspect the cut edge and the exposed ridge board for saw marks or depth beyond the deck thickness.
Correction: stop cutting in that section, reset the depth gauge to the confirmed deck thickness, and route any ridge-board damage to the responsible structural authority before continuing.
Reinspection: recheck the corrected section's depth and the ridge board condition before installing the vent over it.

Wiring or Ductwork Found Crossing the Marked Slot Location

Probable cause: the attic inspection in Step 3 was incomplete, or the service was rerouted after the ventilation design was approved.
Confirmation: trace the wiring or duct from the attic access point to confirm its exact routing relative to the marked slot limits.
Correction: do not cut through the identified service; adjust the slot limits or obtain an approved detail from the responsible trade authority before cutting that section.
Reinspection: recheck the adjusted slot limits against the confirmed service routing before cutting resumes.

Ridge Vent Sits Unevenly or Appears Crushed After Fastening

Probable cause: fasteners were overdriven, the slot width does not match the vent's designed width, or the deck surface beneath the vent is uneven.
Confirmation: sight down the ridge and check fastener seating condition against the manufacturer's specified condition at several points.
Correction: back out and reset overdriven fasteners, or remove and reposition the vent section if the slot width is incorrect.
Reinspection: resight the ridge and recheck fastener seating after correction.

Ridge-Cap Shingles Do Not Seat Correctly Over the Vent

Probable cause: the general Chapter 13 non-vented cap fastening schedule was used instead of the vent manufacturer's specified exposure and fastener length.
Confirmation: compare the installed exposure and fastener length to the vent manufacturer's instructions rather than the general cap-shingle instructions.
Correction: remove and reinstall the affected cap shingles using the vent manufacturer's specified exposure and fastener length.
Reinspection: recheck exposure, alignment, and fastener seating along the corrected section.

Airflow Verification Finds an Obstructed Rafter Bay

Probable cause: insulation was installed or shifted without a baffle maintaining the required rafter channel, or debris from the slot-cutting work entered the bay.
Confirmation: inspect the obstructed bay from the attic and identify whether the obstruction is insulation, debris, or a missing baffle.
Correction: remove debris, install or reposition the required baffle, or correct the insulation encroachment before considering the airflow path complete.
Reinspection: recheck the corrected bay and confirm continuous airflow from intake to the ridge exhaust.

A Mechanical Exhaust Is Found Terminating Into the Attic

Probable cause: a bathroom, kitchen, dryer, or combustion exhaust duct was disconnected, was never extended to an exterior termination, or was installed incorrectly by another trade.
Confirmation: trace the duct from its source appliance or fixture to its actual termination point.
Correction: record the finding and route it to the responsible trade authority for correction to an approved exterior termination; do not rely on the ridge vent to exhaust mechanical systems.
Reinspection: recheck the duct termination once the correction is complete.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, verified airflow path and an organized record set, not loose cutoffs and unswept debris.

  • Remove all sawdust, nails, loose deck strips, and packaging from the attic, roof planes, and ground below the roof edge
  • Dispose of removed deck material and cutoffs using an approved method
  • Store unused ridge-vent sections, connectors, and end plugs for use in later authorized ventilation work
  • Compile the ventilation calculations, product information, slot dimensions, intake condition, photographs, and inspection results into the project record
  • Transfer the completed Ventilation Verification Record to the location Chapter 15 will reference before completing fascia, soffit, and roof-edge ventilation

Performance Check

With the vent installed and capped, review the Ventilation Verification Record against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the slot width, continuity, and end clearances match the manufacturer-specified dimensions, no structural framing or concealed service was cut or damaged, the vent is straight, fully supported, and correctly fastened, ridge-cap shingles cover the vent per the vent manufacturer's instructions, intake vents, baffles, and rafter channels are confirmed unobstructed, and no mechanical exhaust terminates into the attic. Sign and date the ventilation verification decision before beginning Chapter 15. If any airflow-path segment cannot be verified from an available position, record it as an open item and issue a conditional-proceed decision rather than a full proceed decision.

Chapter Closeout

You have completed the ridge-ventilation chapter in the roof-installation sequence: cutting the ridge-vent slot, installing the ridge-exhaust vent, capping it with compatible ridge-cap shingles, and verifying the complete attic airflow path. This chapter required you to cut the slot without damaging framing or services (RF-079), maintain required clearances (RF-080), install the vent correctly (RF-081), fasten it to the manufacturer's schedule (RF-082), coordinate intake and exhaust ventilation (RF-083), and inspect and document the completed airflow path (RF-084).

You now have a documented, verified ridge-ventilation system that Chapter 15 will reference when completing fascia, soffit, and roof-edge ventilation, and that the Chapter 20 capstone will check again when the small-roof system is inspected as a whole.

Store the completed Ventilation Verification Record, photographs, and product information with the project record. Chapter 15 requires this record before completing the roof-edge ventilation assembly.
