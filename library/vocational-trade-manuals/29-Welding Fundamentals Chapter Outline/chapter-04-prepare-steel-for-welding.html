Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to prepare a traceable set of identified mild-steel coupons from raw stock to a weld-ready condition: verified dimensions, clean weld zones, approved edge and bevel preparation where specified, safe clamping surfaces, and a documented preparation record.

You will review the coupon drawing and specification, verify material identity and condition, measure and mark stock, secure it for cleaning and edge preparation, remove contamination and mill scale from the weld zone, prepare bevels where required, deburr and inspect edges, correct minor distortion, and measure and document the finished coupons before they are released to Chapters 7 through 20 for welding.

This chapter does not perform cutting, grinding a completed weld, or striking an arc. It ends with clamped, measured, clean, traceable coupons ready for cutting in Chapter 5 or, where stock is already cut to length, ready for joint fit-up.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • WF-019 — Clean base metal to the condition required for the approved welding process.
  • WF-020 — Remove rust and paint from identified mild-steel stock using an approved method.
  • WF-021 — Mark cut lines and joint-preparation limits from a verified reference edge.
  • WF-022 — Bevel edges where the project specification requires joint preparation.
  • WF-023 — Clamp work securely for cleaning, edge preparation, and inspection.
  • WF-024 — Inspect prepared steel against dimensional, cleanliness, and edge-condition standards.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter depends on the readiness record from Chapter 1, the PPE staged and inspected in Chapter 2, and the equipment identified and inspected in Chapter 3. It produces the coupon stock that Chapter 5 cuts to final blank dimensions and that Chapter 6 grinds, deburrs, and finishes before any arc is struck in Chapter 7.

The material-identification and contamination controls taught here — stopping for unknown, coated, or contaminated metal — apply to every remaining chapter in this book, including the Chapter 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the coupon drawing, material specification, dimensions, quantity, joint type, edge preparation, and tolerance
  • verifying material identity and stopping for unknown or prohibited coatings and contamination
  • measuring, marking, and clamping stock for cleaning and edge preparation
  • removing oil, grease, rust, mill scale, and paint from the weld zone using an approved method
  • preparing bevels and root faces only from the approved project specification
  • deburring, correcting minor distortion, and inspecting finished coupon condition
  • documenting material, dimensions, edge preparation, cleanliness, and readiness

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • cut stock to final length; cutting with hand tools, saws, and abrasive wheels is covered in Chapter 5
  • strike an arc or perform any welding; welding begins in Chapter 7
  • prepare galvanized, cadmium-plated, lead-coated, painted, oily, solvent-contaminated, chemically treated, stainless, aluminum, high-strength, cast, hardened, or unknown metal; stop and establish the correct procedure and fume controls before proceeding
  • invent a bevel angle, root face, or joint dimension not supplied by the approved welding procedure or project specification

Any coupon, edge condition, or dimension that fails the inspection in this chapter must be corrected or the coupon rejected and remade before it is released for cutting or welding.

Trade Note

Steel preparation is not complete because the stock has been wiped down. It is complete only after material identity, dimensions, cleanliness, and edge condition have all been verified and documented against the coupon drawing. Skipping the material-identification step, rather than any single cleaning pass, is the most common cause of contaminated welds traced back to preparation.

Setup Standard

Do not mark, clean, or bevel any stock until its material identity has been verified as mild steel and it has been checked for prohibited coatings and contamination. A coupon begun before material identification is confirmed must stop until identification is complete.

Shop Tip

Mark every coupon identifier before cleaning begins, not after. Cleaning and beveling can remove a faint scribed mark; a coupon that loses its identifier loses its traceability back to the material and dimension record.

Material Identification and Contamination Control

Material identity is confirmed before any layout mark is made. Do not assume unknown stock is mild steel. Stop for galvanized, cadmium-plated, lead-coated, painted, oily, solvent-contaminated, chemically treated, stainless, aluminum, high-strength, cast, hardened, or unidentified metal until the correct procedure and fume controls are established.

Critical Safety Cue — Unidentified or Prohibited Coatings

Do not clean, bevel, or mark stock with an unknown coating, unknown alloy, or suspected galvanized, cadmium, or lead-based finish. Stop work and contact the responsible person before any grinding, cutting, or cleaning method is applied. Coating removal is permitted only through an approved method with hazardous-material controls in place.

Measurement Cue — Mark From a Single Reference Edge

Select one reference edge per piece before marking length, width, or bevel limits. Verify the reference edge is straight and square before use. Do not measure some marks from one edge and other marks from a different edge on the same piece; this produces compounding layout error that is difficult to detect after cutting.

Safety Cue — Cleaner Ventilation and Evaporation

Use approved cleaners only with the ventilation the cleaner's instructions require. Allow flammable cleaner to evaporate fully and remove it from the hot-work area before any grinding, cutting, or welding begins nearby.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Grinding Wheel and Brush Selection

Select grinding wheels and wire brushes rated for the tool speed and approved for the specific task — bevel preparation, deburring, or mill-scale removal. Use dedicated stainless brushes only when the procedure requires them, and do not cross-contaminate brush types between carbon steel and stainless applications.

E. Preparation — Drawing Review and Material Verification

  1. Review the coupon drawing, material specification, dimensions, quantity, joint type, edge preparation, and tolerance. Confirm the drawing matches the chapter or practice sequence this coupon set will support.
  2. Verify the stock is identified mild steel and free from prohibited coatings or contamination. Check the stock against its mill or supplier identification before proceeding.
  3. Stop for unknown, galvanized, plated, lead-coated, heavily painted, chemically treated, or oily material until approved controls are established. Do not proceed with any piece whose identity cannot be confirmed.
  4. Inspect stock for severe rust, pitting, lamination, cracks, bends, sharp edges, and previous welds. Reject pieces with defects beyond what cleaning and preparation can correct.
  5. Select pieces large enough for safe clamping and the required coupon dimensions. Confirm each piece leaves adequate clamping margin beyond the finished coupon size.
  6. Measure stock thickness using an approved tool. Record the measured thickness against the coupon drawing requirement.

F. Layout and Marking

  1. Mark each piece with a coupon identifier that will remain traceable after cutting. Place the identifier where cleaning and beveling will not remove it.
  2. Establish a clean measuring and layout surface. Confirm the table is flat and free of debris before placing stock on it.
  3. Use a steel rule, square, combination square, scriber, soapstone, or approved marker. Select the marking tool appropriate to the stock surface and required precision.
  4. Mark cut lines from a single reference edge. Do not mix reference edges on the same piece.
  5. Verify length, width, squareness, and quantity before cutting. Recheck every mark against the coupon drawing before releasing the piece for cutting.
  6. Mark bevel, root face, or joint-preparation limits only from the approved project specification. Do not mark a bevel angle or root face that is not supplied by the drawing or procedure.

G. Clamping and Cleaning

  1. Secure each piece in a vise, clamp, or fixture before cleaning or edge preparation. Confirm the piece cannot shift during cleaning or grinding.
  2. Remove oil and grease using an approved cleaner and ventilation procedure. Follow the cleaner manufacturer's instructions for application and required ventilation.
  3. Allow flammable cleaner to evaporate fully and remove it from the hot-work area. Confirm no cleaner residue remains before any grinding or cutting begins.
  4. Remove loose rust, mill scale, paint, and contamination from the weld zone using the approved method. Clean to bright metal within the zone specified by the coupon drawing.
  5. Extend cleaning beyond the expected weld and work-clamp area. Confirm the clamp contact area is also clean to bright metal.
  6. Use dedicated stainless brushes only when the procedure requires them; do not cross-contaminate brush types. Store carbon-steel and stainless brushes separately.
  7. Inspect cleaned metal for remaining coating, rust pits, cracks, and contamination. Reclean or reject any area that does not meet the clean-to-bright standard.

H. Bevel Preparation, Deburring, and Distortion Control

  1. Prepare bevels with an approved grinder, file, saw, or machining method. Use the method identified as approved for the specific joint and material thickness.
  2. Maintain the specified bevel angle and root face without thinning the coupon excessively. Check the bevel against the drawing at intervals during preparation.
  3. Do not create deep grinding grooves parallel to the expected stress or outside the repair allowance. Keep grinding marks controlled and within the approved area.
  4. Deburr cut and beveled edges. Remove sharp burrs from every prepared edge.
  5. Break sharp corners only where allowed by the drawing. Do not round a corner the drawing requires to remain square.
  6. Check straightness and flatness on the work table. Place each coupon on the table and verify against a straightedge.
  7. Correct minor distortion using approved cold methods before welding. Do not apply heat to correct distortion in this chapter.

I. Final Cleaning, Fixturing Check, and Documentation

  1. Clean both sides of the joint and the location for the work clamp. Confirm both faces meet the clean-to-bright standard.
  2. Dry the coupons completely. Confirm no cleaner or moisture remains before storage.
  3. Arrange coupons by process, thickness, joint, and practice sequence. Group coupons to match the chapters that will use them.
  4. Clamp a representative joint and verify access for electrode, gun, torch, filler rod, and inspection. Confirm the fixture allows full approach to the joint from the required welding position.
  5. Confirm the fixture will not create an unintended current path or unstable restraint. Check that clamps contact only the intended coupon surfaces.
  6. Measure final coupon dimensions and joint preparation. Record length, width, thickness, bevel angle, and root face against the drawing tolerance.
  7. Photograph and document material, dimensions, edge preparation, cleanliness, and readiness. Complete the coupon preparation record for every accepted piece.
  8. Reject and remake coupons that do not meet preparation requirements. Mark rejected pieces and remove them from the accepted-coupon zone.

Common Coupon Inspection

Inspect every coupon using the same checks regardless of joint type or thickness.

  • Identification check: confirm material identity, freedom from prohibited coatings, and confirmed thickness before any layout mark is trusted.
  • Layout check: confirm every mark on a piece traces back to the same reference edge.
  • Dimension check: confirm length, width, and thickness match the coupon drawing within tolerance.
  • Cleanliness check: confirm the weld zone and clamp contact area are clean to bright metal.
  • Edge check: confirm bevel angle, root face, and deburring match the drawing and are free of deep grinding grooves.
  • Flatness check: confirm each coupon is straight and flat on the work table, with any distortion corrected by an approved cold method.
  • Traceability check: confirm every coupon carries a legible identifier connecting it to its preparation record.

Complete the Coupon Preparation Inspection Checklist in the Inspection Checklist section below before releasing coupons to Chapter 5.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Coupon's Marked Dimensions Do Not Match After Cleaning

Probable cause: the layout marks were measured from two different edges, or the reference edge itself was not straight or square before marking began.
Confirmation: check the reference edge with a square and remeasure every mark on the piece from that single edge.
Correction: remark the piece from a verified reference edge, or reject the piece if the error removes required material.
Reinspection: repeat the layout check and the dimension check.

The Weld Zone Still Shows Discoloration or Residue After Cleaning

Probable cause: cleaning stopped at the visible mill scale without confirming the full weld-zone and clamp-contact area specified by the drawing.
Confirmation: compare the cleaned area against the weld-zone dimension on the coupon drawing and inspect under adequate lighting.
Correction: reclean the full specified area using the approved method until the surface is bright metal.
Reinspection: repeat the cleanliness check.

The Prepared Bevel Angle Does Not Match the Coupon Drawing

Probable cause: the bevel was prepared by eye without checking the angle against a bevel gauge at intervals during grinding.
Confirmation: measure the bevel angle and root face with the approved gauge at multiple points along the edge.
Correction: continue grinding gradually to bring the bevel within tolerance, or reject the coupon if excessive material has already been removed.
Reinspection: repeat the bevel and root-face check.

A Coupon Rocks or Shows Distortion on the Work Table

Probable cause: the stock was distorted before preparation, or clamping during grinding introduced a bend that was not checked before release.
Confirmation: place the coupon on a flat work table and check with a straightedge across multiple directions.
Correction: correct minor distortion using an approved cold method; reject the coupon if distortion cannot be corrected within tolerance.
Reinspection: repeat the flatness and straightness check.

A Coupon's Identifier Is Missing or Illegible After Preparation

Probable cause: the identifier was marked in a location later removed by cleaning or beveling, or was marked with a material not durable enough for the preparation process.
Confirmation: check the coupon against the preparation record for a matching, legible identifier.
Correction: remark the coupon using an approved durable method in a location outside the weld zone and edge-preparation area.
Reinspection: repeat the traceability check.

Unknown or Suspect Coating Is Discovered Mid-Preparation

Probable cause: the material-identification check in Section E did not catch a coating that became visible only after partial cleaning or grinding.
Confirmation: stop work immediately and compare the exposed surface against known galvanized, plated, or painted coating indicators; do not continue grinding or cleaning.
Correction: escalate to the responsible person and establish the correct coating-removal procedure and fume controls before any further work on the piece.
Reinspection: repeat the material-identification check for the corrected procedure before resuming preparation.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with prepared, documented coupon stock, not a completed weld. Housekeeping here protects the traceability record for the chapters that follow.

  • Confirm every accepted coupon is labeled and staged in its process, thickness, and joint grouping
  • Confirm rejected pieces are marked and separated from the accepted-coupon zone
  • Remove grinding dust, filings, and cleaning residue from the layout and clamping surfaces
  • Return steel rules, squares, markers, vises, and gauges to their staged tool zone
  • Store the completed coupon preparation record where it can be referenced before Chapter 5
