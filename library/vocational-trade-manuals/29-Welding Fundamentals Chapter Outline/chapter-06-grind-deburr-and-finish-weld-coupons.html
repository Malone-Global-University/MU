Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to select and mount the correct grinding, flap, wire-brush, or filing tool for a specific finishing task, control sparks and dust while finishing coupon edges and weld zones, remove burrs and sharp corners without exceeding drawing tolerance, prepare bright clean weld zones and bevels, and inspect and measure the finished surface before it moves on to arc practice.

You will inspect the grinding area and tools, select the wheel or brush matched to the task, mount and run each wheel safely, secure the coupon, control the spark path, finish edges and weld zones to the required condition, and complete a dimensional and surface inspection on every coupon produced.

This chapter builds directly on the cut blanks produced in Chapter 5. Every coupon finished here becomes the material used to strike arcs beginning in Chapter 7, so the cleanliness, dimensions, and edge condition established in this chapter directly control the quality of every practice bead that follows.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • WF-031 — Select grinding wheels appropriate to the finishing task and tool.
  • WF-032 — Control sparks generated during grinding and finishing operations.
  • WF-033 — Grind edges safely without damaging required dimensions.
  • WF-034 — Deburr sharp corners on cut and finished stock.
  • WF-035 — Clean finished coupons of contamination before welding.
  • WF-036 — Inspect surface preparation against the coupon specification.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 4 established coupon dimensions and cleaned weld zones by hand and Chapter 5 produced the cut blanks. This chapter is where those blanks reach their final edge condition, bevel, and weld-zone cleanliness. Chapters 7 through 15 strike arcs on coupons finished in this chapter, and Chapters 16 through 20 depend on the same grinding and deburring skills for fit-up, tack correction, and finish work.

The select-mount-run-finish-clean-inspect sequence taught here repeats, in compressed form, whenever a later chapter calls for grinding, beveling, deburring, or weld-zone cleaning.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • identifying the finishing task and selecting the wheel, disc, brush, or file matched to that task and tool
  • inspecting and mounting grinding wheels, flap discs, and wire wheels safely
  • controlling the spark path and protecting people, leads, cylinders, and combustible material during grinding
  • deburring cut edges and preparing bevels within the approved dimensional tolerance
  • grinding weld zones to bright clean metal for the specified distance
  • inspecting and measuring the finished coupon and labeling accepted and rejected pieces

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • invent a bevel angle, root face, or grinding tolerance that is not supplied by the coupon drawing or project specification
  • strike a welding arc; no arc is produced in this chapter
  • service, repair, or open a grinder beyond normal wheel changes and operator-level setup
  • use a wheel, disc, or brush at a speed rating below the tool's rated speed or for an operation the wheel is not approved to perform

Any coupon that fails the dimensional, edge, or weld-zone inspection in this chapter must be corrected or rejected before it is used in a later chapter.

Trade Note

Grinding is a dimension-removing operation. Every pass takes material away and cannot put it back. Check the coupon dimension and bevel angle frequently while grinding rather than grinding to a guess and measuring only at the end.

Setup Standard

Do not mount, adjust, or change any wheel, disc, or brush while the grinder is connected to power. Disconnect or isolate the tool first, install the correct component with the proper flanges, then run it at no load from a protected position before contacting any coupon.

Shop Tip

Check the wheel or disc against the coupon material and task before mounting it, not after. A wheel rated for one operation, such as cutting, is not automatically approved for a different operation, such as side grinding.

Wheel Selection and Spark Control

Select the wheel, disc, or brush specifically approved for the identified task, not the fastest tool on hand. Grinding wheels, flap discs, and wire wheels have different stock-removal rates, finish characteristics, and speed ratings, and using the wrong one can gouge the coupon, damage the tool, or create an unsafe condition.

Measurement Cue — Check Dimensions Before and During Grinding

Measure coupon thickness, width, and bevel before starting and again at intervals while grinding. Grinding removes material permanently. Stop and re-measure before continuing whenever a bevel or edge is approaching its final target dimension.

Critical Safety Cue — Wheel Speed and Mounting

Verify wheel diameter, arbor size, speed rating, and type against the grinder before mounting. Disconnect power before installing or changing any wheel, disc, or brush. Install the wheel with the correct flanges and tightening method, then run it at no load from a protected position. Stop immediately for vibration, wobble, unusual noise, or a cracked or loose-mounted wheel.

Field Safety Cue — Spark Path and Surrounding Hazards

Position the coupon and the grinder so the spark stream reaches only the confirmed spark-collection area. Keep leads, hoses, gas cylinders, windows, vehicles, and other people out of the spark path before starting the grinder.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Wire Wheel Use

Check wire condition, guard position, speed rating, and correct rotation direction before using a wire wheel or wire brush. Follow the wheel manufacturer's mounting and speed instructions exactly; a wire wheel operated above its rated speed can throw wires.

E. Preparation — Review and Area Setup

  1. Review the required coupon finish, edge condition, weld-zone cleanliness, and dimensional tolerance. Confirm the drawing or specification for each coupon before touching a grinder.
  2. Inspect the hot-work and grinding area for combustible material and spark travel. Repeat the relevant portion of the Chapter 1 area check for the grinding location.
  3. Install welding curtains or grinding screens as required. Confirm sightlines toward other occupied areas are blocked.
  4. Select safety glasses, face shield, hearing protection, gloves, clothing, and respiratory controls. Confirm every item is inspected and in place before grinding begins.

F. Task Identification and Wheel Selection

  1. Identify the task as stock removal, beveling, deburring, mill-scale removal, weld cleaning, or surface blending. Record the task for each coupon before selecting a tool.
  2. Select the wheel or tool specifically approved for the task. Do not substitute a faster or more convenient wheel for one matched to the task.
  3. Verify wheel diameter, arbor, speed rating, type, expiration or condition where applicable, and material compatibility. Reject any wheel that fails this check.

G. Grinder Inspection and Wheel Mounting

  1. Inspect the grinder guard, handle, switch, cord, spindle, flange, and body. Remove any grinder with damage from service.
  2. Disconnect power before wheel installation. Confirm the tool is fully isolated before touching the spindle.
  3. Install the wheel with the proper flanges and tightening method. Follow the wheel and grinder manufacturer's mounting instructions exactly.
  4. Position the guard between the wheel and operator. Confirm the guard cannot be bypassed during normal use.
  5. Run the wheel at no load from a protected position. Stand clear of the wheel plane while bringing it to full speed.
  6. Stop for vibration, wobble, unusual noise, cracked wheel, or loose mounting. Correct the condition before any contact with a coupon.

H. Securing the Coupon and Controlling the Spark Path

  1. Secure the coupon in a vise, clamp, or fixture. Do not hold the coupon by hand during grinding.
  2. Position the work so the spark stream reaches only the approved collection area. Confirm the direction of the spark stream before starting the grinder.
  3. Keep leads, hoses, cylinders, windows, vehicles, and people out of the spark path. Recheck the surrounding area immediately before each grinding pass.

I. Grinding, Deburring, and Weld-Zone Preparation

  1. Use both hands on the grinder. Maintain stable two-hand control for the full grinding pass.
  2. Approach the material with the approved wheel angle. Match the angle to the task and the coupon drawing.
  3. Use light controlled pressure rather than forcing the wheel. Let the wheel remove material at its designed rate.
  4. Keep the wheel moving to avoid grooves, overheating, and local thinning. Do not dwell in one location.
  5. Do not grind on the side of a cutting wheel. Use only the wheel type approved for side loading.
  6. Do not use a wire wheel without checking wire condition, guard, speed, and correct direction. Confirm these items every time the wire wheel is mounted.
  7. Remove burrs along all handling edges. Inspect every cut edge for a burr before moving to the next coupon.
  8. Round or break sharp corners only within the drawing tolerance. Do not remove more material than the tolerance allows.
  9. Grind weld zones to bright clean metal for the specified distance. Confirm the cleaned distance matches the procedure before stopping.
  10. Remove rust, scale, paint, oil, and contamination without thinning the base metal. Stop grinding once bright metal is reached; do not continue removing sound base metal.
  11. Prepare bevels gradually and check angle and root face frequently. Re-measure after each pass rather than grinding to a single final pass.

J. Cooling, Inspection, and Final Correction

  1. Allow hot coupons to cool in the designated area. Confirm the cooling location is separated from combustibles and foot traffic.
  2. Do not touch or measure hot metal with bare hands. Confirm the coupon is at a safe handling temperature before continuing.
  3. Inspect finished surfaces for deep grooves, undercut edges, cracks, excessive rounding, embedded abrasive, and remaining contamination. Reject any coupon that fails this check.
  4. Measure coupon width, length, thickness, bevel, and flatness. Compare each measurement against the coupon drawing tolerance.
  5. Use a file or hand abrasive for final controlled correction where appropriate. Make small corrective passes rather than returning to the power grinder for minor adjustments.
  6. Clean dust with approved vacuum or collection equipment. Remove metal dust and abrasive debris from the coupon and the surrounding area.
  7. Inspect the area for sparks and hot particles. Confirm no smoldering material remains before leaving the coupon unattended.
  8. Label accepted and rejected coupons. Mark each coupon clearly so it is not confused with an unfinished or rejected piece.
  9. Record wheel type, grinder, preparation task, dimensions, defects, and final acceptance. Complete the coupon finishing log entry for every coupon produced.

Common Area Inspection

Inspect every finished coupon using the same checks regardless of which grinding, flap, wire-brush, or filing task produced it.

  • Wheel/tool check: confirm the wheel, disc, or brush used was matched to the task, correctly mounted, and run at no load before contact.
  • Spark-control check: confirm the spark stream reached only the approved collection area throughout grinding.
  • Edge and bevel check: confirm ground edges, bevels, and root faces match the coupon drawing within tolerance.
  • Deburr check: confirm every handling edge is free of burrs and unsafe sharp corners.
  • Weld-zone check: confirm the weld zone is bright clean metal for the specified distance.
  • Dimensional check: confirm final width, length, thickness, bevel, and flatness meet the drawing tolerance.

Complete the Coupon Finishing Inspection Checklist in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 7.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Coupon Is Ground Below the Minimum Thickness Tolerance

Probable cause: repeated grinding passes were applied without re-measuring thickness, or the operator continued grinding after reaching bright metal.
Confirmation: measure coupon thickness at multiple points and compare against the drawing tolerance.
Correction: reject the coupon if it is below the minimum tolerance; remake the coupon from new stock following Chapters 4 and 5.
Reinspection: repeat the dimensional check on the replacement coupon before it proceeds.

A Bevel Angle Does Not Match the Coupon Drawing

Probable cause: the bevel was ground in a single pass without checking angle and root face at intervals.
Confirmation: measure the bevel angle and root face with the approved gauge at several points along the joint edge.
Correction: regrind gradually in light passes, rechecking the angle after each pass, or reject the coupon if insufficient material remains.
Reinspection: repeat the bevel and root-face check for the full joint edge.

A Groove or Local Thin Spot Appears in the Weld Zone

Probable cause: the wheel was held stationary in one location or the wheel angle dug into the surface instead of moving steadily across it.
Confirmation: measure thickness at the groove location and compare against the surrounding coupon thickness and drawing tolerance.
Correction: if the groove is within tolerance, blend it with light finishing passes; if it is not, reject the coupon and remake it.
Reinspection: repeat the surface and dimensional check for the affected area.

The Weld Zone Still Shows Rust, Scale, or Discoloration After Grinding

Probable cause: the wrong wheel or brush was used for the contamination present, or grinding stopped before reaching bright metal.
Confirmation: inspect the weld zone under adequate lighting across the full specified cleaning distance.
Correction: continue grinding with the correct wheel until bright clean metal is achieved across the full zone.
Reinspection: repeat the weld-zone cleanliness check before the coupon is released for welding.

A Handling Edge Remains Sharp After the Deburring Step

Probable cause: the deburring pass skipped a section of the edge or used a tool not suited to the remaining burr.
Confirmation: run a gloved hand carefully along the full length of every cut and formed edge, checking for remaining sharp material.
Correction: finish the missed section with a file or the approved grinding wheel, staying within drawing tolerance.
Reinspection: repeat the deburring check for the entire coupon.

A Wheel Vibrates or Makes Unusual Noise After Mounting

Probable cause: the wheel was mounted with incorrect flanges, uneven tightening, or a wheel that is cracked or out of round.
Confirmation: run the wheel at no load from a protected position and observe for wobble, vibration, or noise.
Correction: disconnect power, remove the wheel, inspect it for damage, and remount correctly or replace it.
Reinspection: repeat the no-load run check before contacting any coupon.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a set of finished, inspected coupons ready for the arc-welding chapters that follow. Housekeeping here protects both the coupons and the grinding area for the next task.

  • Disconnect or isolate the grinder before storage
  • Inspect and store wheels, discs, and brushes in their labeled locations
  • Vacuum or sweep metal dust and abrasive debris from the work surface and floor
  • Confirm no hot or smoldering material remains in the finishing area
  • Store finished, labeled coupons and the completed finishing log where they can be referenced before Chapter 7

Root Face

Handling Edge
Any cut or formed edge of the coupon that a worker's hand or glove may contact during normal handling.
Spark Collection Area
The designated location, confirmed clear of people and combustibles, into which the spark stream from grinding is directed.
Flap Disc

No-Load Run

The area of the coupon that will be joined or fused, cleaned to bright metal for the distance specified by the procedure.
