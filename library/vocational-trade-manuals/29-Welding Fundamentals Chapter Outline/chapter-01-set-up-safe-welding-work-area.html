Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to establish one approved welding work area — a permanent booth or a temporary hot-work location — with a controlled boundary, protected surrounding surfaces, functioning ventilation, staged equipment and PPE, fire-watch controls, and a documented readiness record signed by the responsible person.

You will obtain authorization, assess the surrounding area for fire exposure, remove or protect combustible material, position welding screens and barriers, confirm ventilation for the intended process, stage the required PPE and tools, position fire-extinguishing equipment, establish hot-metal and cooling zones, verify lighting and emergency access, and complete a documented walk-through before any arc is struck.

This is the first chapter in the book. No welding, cutting, or grinding takes place in this chapter. Every later chapter in this book — equipment identification, steel preparation, cutting, SMAW, GMAW, FCAW, GTAW, fit-up, and the capstone project — depends on the readiness record produced here and is performed inside the area established by this chapter.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • WF-001 — Identify welding hazards present in a specific work location and task.
  • WF-002 — Establish a controlled hot-work zone with a defined boundary and restricted access.
  • WF-003 — Stage PPE, tools, and consumables appropriate to the approved process.
  • WF-004 — Protect surrounding surfaces, openings, and concealed spaces from fire and spark exposure.
  • WF-005 — Set up fire-prevention controls, including extinguishing equipment and fire-watch coverage.
  • WF-006 — Complete a documented welding-area readiness inspection and obtain authorization.

Where This Skill Fits

Every other project in this book assumes a work area that has already passed this chapter's readiness inspection. Chapter 2 stages PPE inside this area. Chapter 3 inspects equipment staged inside this area. Chapters 4 through 6 cut, grind, and prepare steel inside the cutting and grinding zones established here. Chapters 7 through 20 strike every practice and capstone arc inside the hot-work boundary defined here.

The authorize-protect-identify-prepare sequence taught in this chapter is the same sequence you repeat, in compressed form, at the start of every remaining chapter: confirm authorization, confirm the area, confirm PPE, and confirm equipment before any hot work begins.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • obtaining shop authorization or a hot-work permit before setup begins
  • identifying combustible material, concealed spaces, and spark-travel paths around the intended work location
  • removing, relocating, or protecting combustible material and establishing the restricted hot-work perimeter
  • positioning welding screens and barriers that protect others while preserving ventilation and emergency access
  • identifying and confirming the ventilation method for the intended process and material
  • staging PPE, tools, fire-extinguishing equipment, and material-handling zones
  • completing a documented walk-through and obtaining formal authorization to weld

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • strike an arc, cut, grind, or perform any hot work; this chapter ends before any tool is energized
  • set up a confined-space hot-work location; confined-space welding requires a formal confined-space program, atmospheric testing, an attendant, and rescue planning that are outside this chapter's scope
  • perform hot work in an area with unresolved flammable, combustible, oxygen-enriched, explosive, or unknown-atmosphere conditions
  • authorize hot work on or near fuel, solvents, vapors, batteries, gas cylinders, aerosols, dust clouds, pressurized containers, tanks, drums, piping, or unknown closed vessels

Any area, screen, extinguisher, ventilation path, or access condition that fails the readiness inspection in this chapter must be corrected before the responsible person signs the readiness record.

Trade Note

A welding area is not "ready" because welding equipment is present. It is ready only after the surrounding fire, ventilation, access, and PPE conditions have been verified and documented. Skipping the walk-through in this chapter, rather than any single missing item, is the most common cause of hot-work incidents traced back to setup.

Setup Standard

Do not connect power to any welding machine, stage a compressed-gas cylinder for use, or authorize any hot work in this area until the responsible person has signed the readiness record produced in this chapter. A hot-work task begun before the record is signed must stop until the record is complete.

Shop Tip

Walk the full hot-work boundary from the outside in, checking above, below, behind, and on the opposite side of the intended work location before checking the equipment itself. Fire and spark-travel hazards are easiest to miss when the walk-through starts at the welding table instead of at the perimeter.

Prerequisites

  • Shop authorization or an approved hot-work permit obtained for the intended location and task

Hot-Work Boundary and Concealed-Space Exposure

The hot-work boundary is established before any spark-producing activity begins, not after. It must account for sparks and heat traveling above, below, behind, and to the opposite side of the work, including through floor cracks, drains, wall openings, penetrations, pits, ducts, and any adjoining room that shares a wall, floor, or ceiling with the work location.

Measurement Cue — Mark the Boundary Before Staging Equipment

Measure and mark the hot-work perimeter with barrier tape, cones, or stands before staging welding screens, tools, or PPE inside it. Confirm the marked boundary provides adequate clearance from the combustible material identified during the area assessment. Do not estimate the boundary by eye once equipment is already in place.

Critical Safety Cue — Unresolved Atmosphere or Fuel Hazards

Do not establish a hot-work area near fuel, solvents, vapors, batteries, gas cylinders not staged for this task, aerosols, dust clouds, combustible insulation, pressurized containers, tanks, drums, piping, or unknown closed vessels. Do not proceed in a location with flammable, oxygen-enriched, explosive, or unknown-atmosphere conditions. Stop setup and contact the responsible person if any of these conditions are present.

Field Safety Cue — Screens, Ventilation, and Access Together

Position welding screens to block arc radiation from reaching other workers, but confirm the screens do not block required ventilation, exits, or emergency access. Confirm screens are stable, cannot tip, and cannot ignite or contact hot material or the arc.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Fire Extinguisher and Fire Blanket Selection

Select a fire extinguisher rated for the specific combustible, electrical, and metal hazards present in the area according to the extinguisher manufacturer's rating and the shop's fire-safety guidance. Use fire blankets, spark-containment mats, or water only where the shop's fire-safety guidance approves them for the hazard present.

E. Preparation — Authorization and Task Review

  1. Obtain shop authorization or the required hot-work permit. Confirm the permit or authorization applies to the intended location, date, and duration before proceeding.
  2. Review the planned welding, cutting, grinding, material, process, duration, and location. Confirm the plan matches the chapter or task that will use this area.
  3. Identify the responsible welder, supervisor, fire watch, and emergency contact. Record each name and role on the readiness checklist.

F. Area Assessment and Fire Protection

  1. Inspect the area above, below, behind, beside, and on the opposite side of the intended work. Walk the full perimeter before staging any equipment.
  2. Identify combustible walls, floors, ceilings, insulation, dust, liquids, gases, paper, wood, fabrics, vehicles, and stored materials. Record each item found within the intended boundary.
  3. Remove combustible material beyond the required hot-work boundary where practical. Relocate movable combustibles to a distance confirmed by the responsible person's guidance.
  4. Protect immovable combustibles with approved fire-resistant blankets, curtains, shields, or guards. Confirm coverage extends beyond the expected spark and heat path.
  5. Inspect floor cracks, drains, wall openings, penetrations, pits, ducts, and concealed spaces for spark travel. Identify every opening within range of the intended work.
  6. Cover or isolate openings only through an approved method that does not block required ventilation or drainage. Confirm the covering method itself is non-combustible and stable.

G. Boundary, Screens, and Ventilation

  1. Establish the restricted hot-work perimeter with signs, barriers, and welding screens. Measure and mark the boundary before positioning equipment inside it.
  2. Position screens to protect others from arc radiation while preserving ventilation and emergency access. Confirm sightlines toward occupied areas are blocked.
  3. Confirm that screens cannot ignite, tip, or contact the arc or hot workpiece. Check screen stability and material rating before continuing.
  4. Identify the ventilation method for the specific process and material. Confirm the method matches the process planned for this area.
  5. Position local exhaust to capture fumes without pulling shielding gas away from the weld. Confirm exhaust placement relative to the intended work position.
  6. Confirm make-up air and exhaust do not expose neighboring workers or occupied spaces. Trace the exhaust path to its final discharge point.
  7. Identify whether respiratory protection or specialty ventilation is required. Record the determination and any required follow-up on the checklist.

H. Work Surface, Zones, and Equipment Staging

  1. Inspect the work table, stands, clamps, fixtures, and floor for stability and grounding suitability. Confirm each item is stable and free of damage before use.
  2. Remove oil, grease, solvent, dust, and combustible residue from the work surface. Clean the full surface, not only the immediate work location.
  3. Establish separate zones for clean metal, consumables, hot metal, scrap, gas cylinders, tools, and completed coupons. Mark each zone clearly on the floor or table.
  4. Secure compressed-gas cylinders upright and away from sparks, heat, traffic, and electrical circuits. Confirm cylinders intended for later use are staged, not connected, at this stage.
  5. Protect hoses, leads, cords, and foot controls from sharp edges, hot metal, vehicles, and trip paths. Route staged cabling away from walkways.
  6. Verify the welding machine disconnect, input power, cable routing, and dry location. Confirm the machine remains de-energized until Chapter 3's equipment inspection is complete.
  7. Stage the required welding helmet, safety glasses, face shield, gloves, clothing, hearing protection, and respiratory equipment. Position PPE within reach of the hot-work zone.
  8. Stage approved clamps, pliers, chipping tools, wire brushes, grinders, and measuring tools. Place each item in its designated tool zone.

I. Fire-Watch, Emergency Controls, and Final Authorization

  1. Place an appropriate fire extinguisher within accessible reach. Confirm the extinguisher rating matches the hazards identified during the area assessment.
  2. Stage fire blankets, spark containment, water or other firefighting media only when approved for the hazard. Do not stage a firefighting method not approved for the specific hazard present.
  3. Verify alarm access, emergency exit, first aid, eyewash where required, and communication. Confirm each remains unobstructed by screens, barriers, or staged equipment.
  4. Establish the fire-watch position and post-work monitoring duration. Record the assigned fire watch and the required monitoring period on the checklist.
  5. Mark the hot-metal cooling location. Confirm the location is separated from combustible material and foot traffic.
  6. Establish the method for identifying hot material to others. Record the method — tags, signage, or verbal notice — on the checklist.
  7. Inspect lighting so work, leads, clamps, cylinders, and exits remain visible. Correct any area with inadequate lighting before continuing.
  8. Confirm that flammable gas, oxygen enrichment, confined-space, or explosive dust conditions are not present. Stop and contact the responsible person if any condition cannot be ruled out.
  9. Complete a documented walk-through using the welding-area readiness checklist. Verify every item in Section D against the physical area.
  10. Correct every failed fire, ventilation, access, electrical, cylinder, PPE, screen, support, or housekeeping item. Re-inspect each corrected item before continuing.
  11. Authorize arc initiation only after the responsible person signs the readiness record. Do not proceed to any later chapter's hot work without this signature.

Common Area Inspection

Inspect the area using the same checks regardless of whether it is a permanent booth or a temporary hot-work location.

  • Boundary check: confirm the hot-work perimeter is measured, marked, and provides adequate clearance from every identified combustible.
  • Fire-protection check: confirm immovable combustibles are covered and concealed spaces are protected or isolated by an approved method.
  • Ventilation check: confirm the ventilation method matches the intended process and material and does not disturb intended shielding.
  • Staging check: confirm PPE, tools, and material zones are staged within reach and correctly separated.
  • Emergency check: confirm extinguishing equipment, alarm access, exits, and first aid or eyewash remain unobstructed.
  • Authorization check: confirm the readiness checklist is fully completed and signed before any hot work is authorized.

Complete the Welding Area Readiness Checklist in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 2.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Combustible Material Is Found After Setup Is Already Complete

Probable cause: the area assessment did not extend above, below, behind, or to the opposite side of the intended work location before equipment was staged.
Confirmation: repeat the full perimeter walk-through described in Section F and compare findings against the original readiness checklist.
Correction: remove, relocate, or cover the newly identified combustible material before continuing setup.
Reinspection: repeat the fire-protection check for the full boundary before authorization.

A Welding Screen Blocks a Required Exit or Ventilation Path

Probable cause: screens were positioned to maximize arc-radiation blocking without checking their effect on exits or airflow.
Confirmation: trace each exit path and exhaust path from the work location and confirm no screen or barrier obstructs either.
Correction: reposition the screen or barrier so radiation is still blocked but the exit and ventilation path remain clear.
Reinspection: repeat the screens-and-barriers check and the ventilation check.

Ventilation Is Adequate for the General Area but Not for the Specific Process or Material

Probable cause: general shop ventilation was assumed to be sufficient without confirming the method for the specific process, material, or coating planned for later chapters.
Confirmation: compare the planned process and material against the ventilation method identified in Section G and confirm local exhaust or respiratory protection where required.
Correction: add or reposition local exhaust, or identify and document the respiratory-protection requirement before authorization.
Reinspection: repeat the ventilation check for the corrected setup.

A Concealed Space Was Missed During the Walk-Through

Probable cause: floor cracks, drains, wall openings, or an adjoining room were not inspected because they were not visible from the immediate work position.
Confirmation: re-walk the boundary specifically looking for floor, wall, and ceiling openings, and check adjoining rooms that share a surface with the work location.
Correction: cover or isolate the opening using an approved method that does not block required ventilation or drainage.
Reinspection: repeat the concealed-space check for the full boundary.

The Fire Extinguisher on Hand Is Not Rated for the Area's Hazards

Probable cause: the extinguisher was selected by availability rather than by matching its rating to the combustible, electrical, and metal hazards identified in the area assessment.
Confirmation: compare the extinguisher's rating label against the hazards recorded in Section F.
Correction: replace the extinguisher with one rated for the identified hazards before authorization.
Reinspection: repeat the fire-extinguishing equipment check.

The Readiness Checklist Is Completed but Not Signed

Probable cause: the walk-through was completed, but the responsible person was not available or was not asked to review and sign the record.
Confirmation: check the readiness checklist for a completed authorization signature and date.
Correction: locate the responsible person, review the completed checklist with them, and obtain the signature before any hot work is authorized.
Reinspection: confirm the signed record is stored where it can be produced before later chapters begin.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, authorized area, not a completed weld. Housekeeping here protects the readiness record for the chapters that follow.

  • Confirm all staged PPE, tools, and consumables remain in their labeled zones
  • Confirm compressed-gas cylinders remain upright, secured, and disconnected
  • Sweep or vacuum combustible dust and debris from the marked hot-work zone
  • Confirm barrier tape, signage, and screens remain in position if the area will stay set up between sessions
  • Store the signed welding-area readiness checklist where it can be referenced before every later chapter

Chapter Closeout

You have completed the first chapter in the welding fundamentals sequence: setting up a safe welding work area. This chapter required you to identify welding hazards specific to the intended location and task (WF-001), establish a controlled hot-work zone with a defined boundary and restricted access (WF-002), stage PPE, tools, and consumables appropriate to the approved process (WF-003), protect surrounding surfaces, openings, and concealed spaces from fire and spark exposure (WF-004), set up fire-prevention controls including extinguishing equipment and fire-watch coverage (WF-005), and complete a documented welding-area readiness inspection and obtain authorization (WF-006).

You now have a documented, signed, and authorized welding area. That area, and the readiness record behind it, carries directly into the next chapter: selecting, fitting, inspecting, and staging the welding PPE you will wear every time you work inside this boundary.

Store the signed Welding Area Readiness Checklist with your other project records. You will confirm this area's readiness before every remaining chapter in this book.
