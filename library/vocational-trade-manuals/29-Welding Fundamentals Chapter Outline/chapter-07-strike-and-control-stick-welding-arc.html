Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to set up one SMAW (Shielded Metal Arc Welding) station and produce controlled arc-start and short-bead practice exercises on flat mild-steel coupons. You will connect the electrode lead and work lead to the approved polarity, select and inspect an approved electrode, set the machine to the approved starting amperage, strike the arc using the approved scratch or tapping start, maintain a controlled arc length while moving into a short practice path, terminate the bead using the approved crater-control method, and clean and inspect every practice bead you produce.

You will work only inside the welding area established in Chapter 1, wearing the PPE selected and inspected in Chapter 2, using the station identified and inspected in Chapter 3, on coupons prepared in Chapter 4 and finished in Chapters 5 and 6. This chapter does not introduce continuous stringer beads, joints, or fillet welds; it isolates arc-starting and short-bead control so those skills are solid before Chapter 8 builds on them.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • WF-037 — Set up SMAW equipment with correct polarity, electrode, and work-clamp connection.
  • WF-038 — Select practice electrodes matched to the approved classification, diameter, and condition requirements.
  • WF-039 — Strike an arc using an approved start method without an uncontrolled arc strike.
  • WF-040 — Maintain arc length according to the approved electrode guidance.
  • WF-041 — Control travel speed through a short practice path with a stable puddle.
  • WF-042 — Inspect practice beads for start quality, continuity, profile, and defects.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter is the first hot-work chapter in the book. It depends directly on the readiness record from Chapter 1, the PPE from Chapter 2, the equipment identification and readiness record from Chapter 3, and the prepared coupons from Chapters 4 through 6. Every SMAW chapter that follows — straight stringer beads in Chapter 8 and lap and T-joint fillet welds in Chapter 9 — assumes the arc-starting and short-bead control taught here.

The setup sequence in this chapter — verify machine de-energized, connect polarity, attach the work clamp to clean metal, select and inspect the electrode, set amperage, and record the settings — repeats with process-specific changes in Chapters 10 (GMAW), 13 (FCAW), and 14 (GTAW).

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • connecting SMAW leads to the approved polarity and attaching the work clamp to clean sound metal
  • selecting and inspecting an approved electrode classification and diameter
  • setting the machine to the approved starting amperage and recording the setup
  • striking an arc using an approved scratch or tapping start and clearing a stuck electrode safely
  • maintaining arc length and travel through a short practice path
  • terminating a bead with controlled crater fill and cleaning and inspecting the result

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • run continuous stringer beads of production length; that skill is taught in Chapter 8
  • weld a joint, tack an assembly, or produce a fillet weld; those skills begin in Chapter 9
  • select machine settings, electrode classification, or polarity outside the approved procedure supplied by the instructor, shop, or machine and consumable manufacturer
  • weld on unidentified, coated, painted, galvanized, or contaminated material

Any welding area, PPE, or equipment item that has not passed its Chapter 1, 2, or 3 readiness inspection must be corrected before this chapter's hot work begins.

Trade Note

Arc starting is a distinct skill from bead running. A learner who can start an arc reliably every time, on the first attempt, without sticking the electrode or gouging the plate, has already solved most of the problems that later show up as poor stringer beads. This chapter isolates that skill deliberately.

Setup Standard

Do not connect or change output connections on an energized machine. Isolate the machine before changing polarity, and confirm the work clamp is attached to clean sound metal close to the practice area before the first arc is struck.

Shop Tip

Practice the scratch or tapping start on a marked area of scrap before starting on the graded coupon. A consistent start motion, repeated the same way every time, is more reliable than adjusting amperage to compensate for an inconsistent start technique.

Arc Length and Start Control

Arc length and start technique are controlled together. An arc that is too long produces excessive spatter and poor fusion; an arc that is too short increases the chance of sticking the electrode to the plate. The approved scratch or tapping start is practiced on scrap until it is repeatable before it is used on a coupon that will be inspected and recorded.

Critical Safety Cue — Isolate Power Before Changing Connections

Verify the machine is de-energized before connecting the electrode lead and work lead or changing polarity. Do not change output connections on an energized machine.

Safety Cue — Clearing a Stuck Electrode

Break a stuck electrode using the approved release motion without lifting the helmet. If the electrode cannot be released safely, stop and isolate power at the machine before freeing it by hand.

Field Safety Cue — Holder Placement

Keep the electrode holder insulated from the table and work whenever it is not being used to weld. Place the holder in the insulated rest between beads, not on the coupon, table edge, or floor.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Electrode Storage and Condition

Keep electrodes dry and identified by classification and diameter at all times. Do not use electrodes with moisture damage, cracked or chipped coating, or contamination; follow the electrode manufacturer's storage instructions between practice sessions.

E. Preparation — Setup Review

  1. Review the approved SMAW practice procedure, coupon, electrode, polarity, position, and starting range. Confirm the procedure matches the station and material staged for this session.
  2. Complete the welding-area, fire-watch, ventilation, and PPE checks. Reconfirm the Chapter 1 readiness record and Chapter 2 PPE inspection before proceeding.
  3. Inspect the power source, leads, electrode holder, work clamp, and input connection. Confirm no item shows damage identified in the Chapter 3 equipment-readiness record.

F. Machine and Consumable Setup

  1. Verify the machine is de-energized before changing output connections. Confirm the disconnect or power switch is off before touching output terminals.
  2. Connect the electrode lead and work lead to the approved polarity. Match the connection to the polarity specified in the approved procedure.
  3. Attach the work clamp to clean sound metal close to the practice area. Confirm firm contact on bare, unpainted metal.
  4. Confirm the clamp path does not pass through bearings, hinges, electronics, or unintended components. Trace the clamp path visually before continuing.
  5. Select the approved electrode classification and diameter. Match the electrode to the approved procedure exactly.
  6. Inspect the electrode coating for moisture, cracks, chips, contamination, and damage. Set aside any electrode that fails inspection.
  7. Keep electrodes dry and identified. Return unused electrodes to approved dry storage between uses.
  8. Set the machine to the approved starting amperage. Use the value specified by the approved procedure; do not estimate.
  9. Record machine, polarity, electrode, diameter, and setting. Complete the station and coupon record in the Reference Conventions section before striking an arc.

G. Coupon Positioning and Marking

  1. Position the coupon flat on a stable welding table. Confirm the coupon cannot shift during welding.
  2. Mark short start lines and bead endpoints. Use soapstone or an approved marker to lay out each practice repetition before welding begins.
  3. Assume a stable body position with clear lead movement. Confirm the electrode lead can move through the full practice path without tension or drag.

H. Arc Initiation

  1. Insert the electrode into the holder with adequate exposed length and secure grip. Confirm the electrode is held firmly and cannot rotate or fall from the holder.
  2. Keep the holder insulated from the table and work when not welding. Place the holder in the insulated rest between beads.
  3. Lower the helmet before contacting the electrode to the work. Confirm the helmet is fully lowered and the lens is functioning before arc contact.
  4. Practice the approved scratch or tapping start on a marked area. Repeat the start motion until it is consistent before recording a graded attempt.
  5. Break a stuck electrode by the approved motion without lifting the helmet. Use the release motion specified by the approved procedure or instructor.
  6. Stop and isolate power if the electrode cannot be released safely. Do not force a stuck electrode by hand while the machine is energized.

I. Short-Bead Travel and Termination

  1. Establish the arc and move immediately into the short practice path. Avoid dwelling at the start point once the arc is established.
  2. Maintain arc length according to the approved electrode guidance. Adjust hand position, not amperage, to hold arc length steady.
  3. Observe the arc, puddle, and slag relationship rather than staring only at the bright center. Watch the puddle edges and trailing slag to judge travel speed.
  4. Maintain the approved work angle and travel angle. Hold both angles steady through the short bead.
  5. Move at a steady travel speed. Avoid speeding up or slowing down mid-bead.
  6. Terminate the short bead using the approved crater-control method. Fill the crater before breaking the arc.
  7. Lift the electrode clear and place the holder in the insulated rest. Confirm the holder is fully seated in the rest before releasing it.
  8. Mark the coupon as hot. Use the method established in Chapter 1 for identifying hot material.
  9. Allow the bead to cool enough for safe cleaning. Do not chip or brush a bead that is still hot enough to burn through gloves.

J. Cleaning and Inspection

  1. Remove slag using safety glasses, face protection, and controlled chipping. Direct chips away from bystanders and the fire-watch position.
  2. Wire-brush the bead using the approved brush. Remove remaining oxide and loose material without contaminating the brush with other metal types.
  3. Inspect arc-start quality, bead continuity, width, height, ripples, slag removal, undercut, overlap, porosity, and arc strikes. Compare the bead against Section D.
  4. Compare results from multiple starts. Identify which starts were clean and which showed sticking, excessive spatter, or arc strikes.
  5. Adjust only one variable at a time under instructor direction. Do not change amperage, electrode, and technique simultaneously.
  6. Repeat until consistent starting and short-bead control is demonstrated. Continue practice repetitions until the acceptance standard is met on request, not by chance.
  7. Record settings, observations, defects, corrections, and accepted practice results. Complete the practice-bead log for every recorded attempt.
  8. Shut down the station and complete the fire-watch check. Follow the shutdown sequence in the Cleanup section below.

Common Bead Inspection

Inspect every practice bead using the same checks regardless of which start method was used.

  • Start check: confirm the arc was established inside the marked start area without a stray arc strike elsewhere on the coupon.
  • Arc-length check: confirm the bead shows a consistent, controlled arc length rather than excessive spatter or a stuck-and-restarted pattern.
  • Travel check: confirm the bead width and ripple spacing are reasonably even along its length.
  • Termination check: confirm the crater is filled without a depression, crack, or abrupt break.
  • Cleaning check: confirm slag is fully removed and the bead is brushed before final inspection.
  • Documentation check: confirm settings, electrode, and results are recorded on the practice-bead log.

Complete the SMAW Arc-Start Practice Checklist in the Inspection Checklist section below before proceeding to Chapter 8.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Electrode Sticks to the Plate at the Start

Probable cause: the electrode was brought to the plate too slowly or the arc length collapsed to zero immediately after contact.
Confirmation: compare the amperage setting and start motion against the approved procedure and observe the next start attempt closely.
Correction: use the approved release motion to free the electrode without lifting the helmet; if it will not release, isolate power at the machine before freeing it by hand.
Reinspection: repeat the start on a fresh area of scrap before returning to the graded coupon.

Excessive Spatter Along the Bead

Probable cause: arc length is too long for the electrode and amperage in use.
Confirmation: compare the observed arc length against the approved electrode guidance during the next attempt.
Correction: shorten the arc length by adjusting hand position; do not change the amperage setting without instructor direction.
Reinspection: repeat the short bead and compare spatter levels against the acceptance standard.

Arc Strikes Appear Outside the Marked Start Area

Probable cause: the electrode contacted the plate accidentally while positioning for the start, or the holder was not placed in the insulated rest between attempts.
Confirmation: inspect the coupon surface outside the marked start lines for small arc-strike marks.
Correction: review approved holder placement and start positioning with the instructor before continuing.
Reinspection: confirm no new arc strikes appear outside the marked area on the next attempt.

Bead Width Varies Significantly Along Its Length

Probable cause: travel speed was not held steady, or the work or travel angle drifted during the pass.
Confirmation: compare bead width at the start, middle, and end of the pass using a rule.
Correction: practice maintaining a steady angle and travel speed on scrap before repeating the graded attempt.
Reinspection: measure width consistency on the next bead against the acceptance standard.

Crater Shows a Depression or Crack at the Stop

Probable cause: the arc was broken abruptly without using the approved crater-fill method.
Confirmation: inspect the termination point under adequate lighting for a depression, cavity, or visible crack.
Correction: review and practice the approved crater-fill method separately before the next graded bead.
Reinspection: inspect the next termination for a filled crater without depression or cracking.

Slag Does Not Release Cleanly During Chipping

Probable cause: the bead cooled unevenly, or the wrong electrode was used for the base metal or setup.
Confirmation: verify the electrode classification against the approved procedure and confirm the bead was allowed to cool as specified before chipping.
Correction: allow the bead to reach the specified safe cooling condition before chipping again; if the electrode classification is wrong, replace it before the next attempt.
Reinspection: confirm slag releases cleanly on the next practice bead.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with inspected practice coupons and a completed practice-bead log, not a finished fabrication. Shutdown protects both the station and the readiness of the area for Chapter 8.

  • Isolate the machine and confirm the electrode holder is seated in the insulated rest
  • Remove and store unused electrodes in approved dry storage, separated by classification
  • Coil leads without sharp bends and keep them clear of hot metal and foot traffic
  • Confirm all practice coupons are marked hot or verified cool before handling without gloves
  • Sweep or vacuum slag and spatter debris from the work surface and floor
  • Complete the required fire-watch period before leaving the area
  • Store the completed practice-bead log with the Chapter 1 through 6 project records
