Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to place and compact an approved aggregate base over the accepted subgrade produced in Chapter 4. The finished work product is a uniform, compacted aggregate base at the specified thickness, density, elevation, and slope, ready to receive the forms built in Chapter 6.

You will confirm the subgrade approval and aggregate specification, verify required base and lift thickness, inspect delivered aggregate, spread and moisture-condition it in controlled lifts, compact each lift with the specified equipment, check elevation and slope at a grid of locations, correct any deficient areas, and document the completed base.

This is the fifth chapter in Book 16. It depends directly on the compacted subgrade accepted in Chapter 4 and produces the surface Chapter 6 forms are built and staked upon. Nothing in this chapter invents a base thickness, lift thickness, or compaction target that the approved base specification does not already supply.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FW-025 — Select aggregate base material against the approved specification, including gradation, contamination, and moisture condition.
  • FW-026 — Spread gravel evenly in controlled lifts across the accepted subgrade.
  • FW-027 — Compact the base in lifts using the specified equipment and pass pattern.
  • FW-028 — Maintain base thickness at the approved value, correcting high, low, and segregated areas.
  • FW-029 — Verify finished base slope and drainage direction against the approved plan.
  • FW-030 — Inspect the completed base for elevation, density, and edge condition, and document the acceptance.

Where This Skill Fits

The System-Sequence Control governing this book requires that aggregate base be placed and compacted in approved lifts before forms or reinforcement are accepted. Chapter 6 cannot stake and brace forms on a base that has not been checked for thickness, elevation, and slope, and Chapter 7 cannot set finished elevations against a base that is still loose, segregated, or out of tolerance.

This chapter also carries forward the stop-work discipline established in Chapter 1: an approved subgrade from Chapter 4 is a prerequisite, not a formality, and this chapter must not proceed if that subgrade acceptance has not been documented.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • confirming subgrade approval and the aggregate base specification before any material is delivered
  • inspecting delivered aggregate for gradation, contamination, segregation, and excess moisture
  • spreading and moisture-conditioning aggregate in controlled lifts
  • compacting each lift with the specified equipment and pass pattern
  • checking lift thickness, final elevation, and slope at a grid of locations
  • correcting loose, segregated, soft, high, or low areas
  • protecting the finished base and documenting acceptance

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • place aggregate base over a subgrade that has not received a documented Chapter 4 acceptance
  • invent a base thickness, lift thickness, compaction target, or gradation requirement that is not supplied by the approved aggregate specification, geotechnical information, or project documents
  • substitute a different aggregate gradation or source without confirming compatibility with the approved specification
  • proceed past unstable edges, contaminated material, standing water, or excessive moisture without correcting the condition first
  • accept a base with undocumented density, elevation, or proof-rolling results where testing is specified

Any condition that requires engineering, geotechnical, or testing-laboratory review must be documented and referred, not resolved by the learner.

Trade Note

A base that looks compacted on the surface can still be loose beneath a thin crust, especially at corners and against form-line edges where compactor coverage is easiest to miss. Overlapping every pass and checking lift thickness at a grid of locations, rather than relying on visual impression alone, is what separates a base that performs from one that settles under the finished slab.

Setup Standard

Do not place any aggregate base material until the Chapter 4 subgrade acceptance is documented and on hand. A base placed over an unaccepted subgrade must be removed and the subgrade rechecked before base placement resumes.

Shop Tip

Compact form-edge and corner areas with a hand tamper or small plate attachment before running the main compactor across the field of the lift. Large compactors routinely under-compact the last few inches next to an edge, and that is exactly where form stakes in Chapter 6 will bear the most load.

Common Field Fundamentals

An aggregate base performs as a load-transfer and drainage layer between the subgrade and the slab. A base placed too thin, compacted in a single overthick lift, or left uncompacted at edges and corners will settle unevenly under the finished slab regardless of how well the concrete itself is placed and finished later in this book.

Lifts, Compaction, and Thickness as One System

A single thick, uncompacted layer of aggregate is not equivalent to the same total thickness placed and compacted in approved lifts. Compaction effort only reaches a limited depth per pass; material below that depth in an overthick lift remains loose even after the surface appears firm. Every lift placed in this chapter must be compacted and checked before the next lift is added.

Measurement Cue — Check Thickness at a Grid, Not a Single Point

Check lift thickness and final base elevation at a grid of locations across the project area, not at one convenient spot. A single measurement near the center of a slab can pass while corners and edges remain thin or high, and those are the same locations Chapter 6 will rely on for form bearing.

Critical Safety Cue — Keep Equipment Clear of Unstable Edges

Keep the plate compactor and loaded wheelbarrows clear of unstable excavation edges and structures identified in Chapter 4. A compactor or loaded wheelbarrow that overloads an unsupported edge can collapse the excavation margin and undermine adjacent paving or foundations.

Field Safety Cue — Footing on Loose Aggregate

Freshly spread, uncompacted aggregate shifts underfoot. Watch footing while spreading and while positioning the compactor, especially near form-line edges and sloped transitions.

Code Alert — Base Thickness and Testing Requirements Vary

Required base thickness, gradation, and testing methods vary by jurisdiction, soil condition, and intended slab use. Verify the approved aggregate specification and any required density or proof-rolling testing before assuming a standard thickness applies.

FW-025 – FW-030

Site Preparation Qualification

Aggregate Base Installation and Compaction

Difficulty: Beginner to Intermediate

Estimated Time: 3–6 hours

Estimated Cost: Aggregate by the ton or yard; compactor rental if not owned

Finished Work: One compacted aggregate base at approved thickness, elevation, and slope, inspected and documented

A. Finished-Project Specification

Scope: one aggregate base placed and compacted over the Chapter 4 accepted subgrade, to the approved thickness, lift schedule, density, elevation, and slope, documented as a complete base-installation record including delivered-material inspection, lift-by-lift compaction, final grid checks, and acceptance.

B. Required Project Sequence

  1. Confirm subgrade approval and aggregate specification. Retrieve the Chapter 4 subgrade acceptance record and the approved aggregate base specification before any material is ordered or delivered.
  2. Verify required base thickness and lift thickness. Record both values from the approved specification on the base-installation worksheet before spreading begins.
  3. Inspect delivered aggregate for gradation, contamination, segregation, and excess moisture. Reject any load that does not match the approved gradation or shows contamination, segregation, or excess moisture.
  4. Protect the subgrade from rutting during delivery. Route delivery vehicles and equipment to avoid disturbing the accepted subgrade surface.
  5. Place aggregate in controlled quantities. Deposit material in amounts that can be spread and compacted within the same working period.
  6. Spread the first lift evenly. Use the rake and shovel to distribute aggregate to a uniform, level surface across the project area.
  7. Maintain slope while spreading. Reference the drainage direction and slope established in Chapter 1 and Chapter 3 while spreading each lift.
  8. Moisture-condition the aggregate where required. Add water only according to the aggregate source's specified moisture range; do not saturate the material.
  9. Compact the lift using the specified plate compactor, roller, or other equipment. Operate the equipment according to its manufacturer instructions.
  10. Overlap compactor passes. Overlap each pass with the previous pass across the full lift, including edges and corners.
  11. Keep equipment clear of unstable edges and structures. Maintain the separation identified in Chapter 4 from excavation margins, foundations, and utilities.
  12. Check lift thickness and elevation. Measure at a grid of locations against the elevation reference before placing the next lift.
  13. Add and compact subsequent lifts. Repeat spreading, moisture-conditioning, and compaction for each additional lift required to reach the approved base thickness.
  14. Avoid thick uncompacted layers. Do not place a lift thicker than the approved lift-thickness value regardless of available time or equipment.
  15. Fill low spots with approved aggregate rather than loose fines. Correct low areas using the same specified aggregate, then recompact.
  16. Trim high spots. Remove excess material from high areas and recompact the corrected surface.
  17. Compact form-edge and corner areas using suitable tools. Use a hand tamper or small plate attachment where the main compactor cannot reach.
  18. Check final base elevation at a grid of locations. Compare readings across the full project area to the approved finished-base elevation.
  19. Verify finished slope and drainage direction. Confirm the compacted surface drains in the direction established in Chapter 1 and confirmed in Chapter 3.
  20. Perform density or proof testing where specified. Arrange the required testing-laboratory or proof-rolling procedure and record the result.
  21. Correct loose, segregated, soft, high, or low areas. Rework any area that fails a check before proceeding to Chapter 6.
  22. Protect the base from rain, traffic, contamination, and disturbance. Barricade or cover the finished base as needed until forms are installed.
  23. Document aggregate, lift count, moisture condition, compaction, and final checks. Complete the base-installation worksheet before beginning Chapter 6.

Common Base-Installation Inspection

Inspect every aggregate base installation using the same checks regardless of project size.

  • Prerequisite check: confirm the Chapter 4 subgrade acceptance record is on hand before any aggregate is placed.
  • Material check: confirm delivered aggregate matches the approved gradation and shows no contamination, segregation, or excess moisture.
  • Lift check: confirm each lift was placed and compacted at or below the approved lift-thickness value.
  • Compaction check: confirm compactor passes overlapped across the full lift, including edges and corners.
  • Thickness and elevation check: confirm final readings at a grid of locations match the approved base specification and elevation reference.
  • Slope check: confirm the finished base drains in the approved direction.
  • Testing check: confirm density or proof-rolling results are recorded where specified and any failed area was corrected.

Complete the Base-Installation Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 6.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Base Settles Unevenly After Compaction Appeared Complete

Probable cause: a single lift was placed thicker than the approved lift-thickness value, leaving material below compactor reach loose while the surface appeared firm.
Confirmation: probe or excavate a small test section to compare compacted density near the surface against density near the subgrade.
Correction: remove the affected lift, re-place it in lifts at or below the approved thickness, and recompact each lift individually.
Reinspection: repeat the thickness and grid-elevation check before adding any further material.

Loose or Soft Areas at Edges and Corners

Probable cause: the main compactor could not reach fully into edges and corners, leaving those areas under-compacted.
Confirmation: probe edges and corners directly and compare firmness to the field of the lift.
Correction: recompact edges and corners using a hand tamper or small plate attachment sized for that access.
Reinspection: repeat the compaction check at every edge and corner location before proceeding to Chapter 6.

Segregated or Contaminated Aggregate Discovered After Placement

Probable cause: delivered aggregate was placed without adequate inspection for gradation, segregation, or contamination.
Confirmation: compare the placed material's visible gradation and cleanliness to the approved aggregate specification.
Correction: remove and replace the affected material with aggregate that matches the approved specification, then recompact.
Reinspection: repeat the material and compaction checks on the replaced area.

Finished Base Slope Does Not Match the Approved Drainage Direction

Probable cause: slope was not checked while spreading and compacting each lift, allowing the surface to drift from the approved drainage direction established in Chapter 1.
Confirmation: recheck the finished base with a string line and level against the elevation reference and approved slope.
Correction: trim high areas or add and recompact approved aggregate in low areas until the approved slope is restored.
Reinspection: repeat the slope and grid-elevation check across the full project area.

Aggregate Placed Over an Unaccepted Subgrade

Probable cause: base placement began without confirming the Chapter 4 subgrade acceptance record.
Confirmation: review project records for a documented subgrade acceptance dated before base placement began.
Correction: stop base placement, remove aggregate as needed to inspect the subgrade, obtain or correct the subgrade acceptance, and resume base placement only after that acceptance is documented.
Reinspection: repeat the prerequisite check before continuing.

Excess Moisture Prevents Adequate Compaction

Probable cause: aggregate was moisture-conditioned beyond the specified range, or placed during or shortly after rain without protection.
Confirmation: check the aggregate's moisture condition against the source's specified range before continuing compaction.
Correction: allow the material to dry to the specified range, or remove and replace saturated material, before recompacting.
Reinspection: repeat the compaction and thickness checks once moisture is within the specified range.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a completed, filed base-installation worksheet and a protected, inspected base, not a visual impression of a finished surface.

  • Clean aggregate spillage from adjacent finished surfaces, sidewalks, and driveways
  • Store unused aggregate away from the compacted base to prevent recontamination
  • Return compactors, rakes, shovels, and measuring tools to inspected, ready storage
  • Confirm barricades or coverings protecting the finished base are in place before leaving the site
  • File the completed base-installation worksheet, elevation and slope record, and any testing record together with the Chapter 4 subgrade acceptance record

Field Terms

Aggregate Base
The compacted layer of crushed stone or approved granular material placed between the subgrade and the concrete slab.
Lift
One layer of aggregate spread and compacted before the next layer is placed.
Gradation
The approved distribution of particle sizes within the aggregate base material.
Base Thickness
The total compacted depth of aggregate base from the accepted subgrade to the finished base surface.
Proof-Rolling
A specified acceptance test in which loaded equipment is driven across the compacted base to detect soft or yielding areas.
Segregation
An uneven separation of aggregate particle sizes within a stockpile or placed lift, treated as a material defect.
Grid of Locations
The set of measurement points spaced across the base area, used to verify thickness, elevation, and slope.
Moisture Conditioning
Adding water to aggregate only within the source's specified range to achieve proper compaction.
Density Testing
A specified laboratory or field test confirming the compacted base meets the required compaction target.
Elevation Reference
The same fixed benchmark established in Chapter 1 and used for every later elevation check in this book.
