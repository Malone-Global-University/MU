Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to lay out and document one rectangular small slab or pad from the dimensions, elevation references, slope, and joint information the Chapter 1 site-readiness worksheet already established. The finished work product is a squared, dimensionally verified layout: corner points, offset stakes or batter boards, marked excavation limits, finished-surface reference points, and a documented layout record.

You will confirm the approved dimensions and reference benchmark, establish a controlling baseline, set and square corner points using the diagonal method, transfer the layout to offset stakes or batter boards beyond the excavation limits, mark the perimeter and excavation limits, mark drainage direction and joint locations, and record every dimension and elevation before obtaining layout approval.

This is the third chapter in Book 16. It uses the dimensions and elevation reference Chapter 1 measured and the work-area protections Chapter 2 established, and it produces the exact excavation limits, finished-elevation references, and drainage direction that Chapter 4 will excavate to and Chapter 7 will verify. Nothing in this chapter invents a dimension, elevation, or slope that Chapter 1's approved site plan and worksheet did not already supply.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FW-013 — Establish slab dimensions from the approved site plan and Chapter 1 worksheet.
  • FW-014 — Square the layout using the diagonal measurement method within the approved tolerance.
  • FW-015 — Mark excavation limits accounting for form thickness, base extension, and working room.
  • FW-016 — Set reference lines, offset stakes, and batter boards beyond the excavation limits.
  • FW-017 — Verify slope direction and finished-surface reference points against the single elevation reference.
  • FW-018 — Document the final layout, including dimensions, diagonals, elevations, and reference points.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 1 produced the approved dimensions, elevation reference, and drainage direction. Chapter 2 staged a protected work area around this exact footprint. This chapter converts those approved figures into physical corner points, offset references, and marked excavation limits that Chapter 4 will excavate to without further interpretation. Chapter 7 will later transfer elevations to the forms this layout controls, and Chapter 6 will build those forms to the lines this chapter establishes.

A layout error carried into excavation is far more expensive to correct than a layout error caught here, on paper and with a tape measure, before any soil is disturbed. The squareness and elevation discipline in this chapter is the same discipline every later chapter depends on.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing approved dimensions, finished elevation, slope, isolation, joint, and access requirements from Chapter 1
  • establishing a controlling baseline and setting corner points
  • squaring the layout using the diagonal measurement method
  • setting offset stakes or batter boards beyond excavation limits
  • marking the slab perimeter, excavation limits, and finished-surface reference points
  • marking drainage direction, isolation joints, drains, sleeves, and embedded items
  • recording all dimensions, diagonals, elevations, and reference points

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin excavation before layout approval is obtained
  • invent a dimension, finished elevation, slope, joint location, or setback that is not supplied by the approved site plan, Chapter 1 worksheet, or project specification
  • drive stakes, offsets, or batter boards without confirmed, still-valid utility-locate markings from Chapter 1
  • direct a marked drainage slope toward a building, neighboring property, or prohibited discharge point

Any conflict between the approved dimensions and actual site conditions must be documented and referred, not resolved by the learner.

Trade Note

Use the same elevation reference point selected in Chapter 1 for every elevation note recorded in this chapter. Do not select a new, more convenient reference point at the layout stage. A layout squared correctly but referenced to the wrong benchmark still produces an excavation and drainage error in Chapter 4 and Chapter 7.

Setup Standard

Do not drive layout stakes, offsets, or batter boards until Chapter 1's utility-locate markings are confirmed still visible and valid. If any marking has been disturbed or has expired, stop work and request a new utility locate before continuing this chapter.

Shop Tip

Measure both diagonals before adjusting any corner, and record all four side lengths plus both diagonals as a set. Adjusting one corner in isolation, without rechecking every side and diagonal afterward, is the most common way an apparently squared layout is actually still out of tolerance.

Common Field Fundamentals

A slab layout is not complete once four corners are marked. It is complete once the perimeter is squared within tolerance, referenced to a single elevation benchmark, transferred to offsets that will survive excavation, and marked with the excavation limits, drainage direction, and joint locations the later chapters depend on.

Squaring by the Diagonal Method

A rectangle is square when its two diagonals are equal in length. Set the first corner and the first side from the controlling baseline, establish the adjacent side at the approved angle, set the remaining corners, and then measure both diagonals. Adjust corners in small increments until both diagonals match within the approved squareness tolerance, then recheck all four side lengths, since adjusting one corner changes the adjacent side lengths as well as the diagonals.

Measurement Cue — Diagonals Confirm Square, Not Just Corner Angle

A framing square placed at one corner can look correct while the overall rectangle is still out of square, because a small angular error compounds over the length of the slab. Always confirm final square using both diagonal measurements, not a single framing-square check at one corner.

Critical Safety Cue — Utility Markings Before Any Stake

Do not drive a layout stake, offset stake, or batter board post until Chapter 1's utility-locate markings are confirmed still visible and valid at the exact location of the stake. If a marking is unclear, faded, or has expired, stop work and request a new utility locate before proceeding.

Field Safety Cue — Taut String Lines and Driven Stakes

Mason's string lines under tension can cause lacerations if pulled or released carelessly, and driven stakes create trip hazards for workers and pedestrians. Keep string lines at a consistent height, mark stake locations clearly, and remove or flag any stake left in a walking path.

Code Alert — Setbacks and Right-of-Way

Confirm the marked layout stays within the property lines, setbacks, and right-of-way limits verified in Chapter 1. A layout that is dimensionally correct but positioned incorrectly on the lot is still a deficiency that must be corrected before excavation.

FW-013 – FW-018

Layout Qualification

Small Concrete Slab or Pad Layout

Difficulty: Beginner

Estimated Time: 2–3 hours

Estimated Cost: Low

Finished Work: One squared, referenced, documented slab or pad layout ready for excavation

A. Finished-Project Specification

Scope: one rectangular small slab or pad, laid out from the dimensions, elevation reference, slope, and joint information established in Chapter 1, squared within the approved tolerance, referenced to offsets beyond the excavation limits, and fully documented.

B. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review approved dimensions, finished elevation, slope, isolation, joint, and access requirements. Confirm these match the Chapter 1 worksheet exactly.
  2. Confirm utility markings, property limits, and reference benchmark. Verify Chapter 1's utility-locate markings remain visible and valid, and confirm the single elevation reference point.
  3. Establish the controlling baseline. Select one reference line every subsequent measurement in this chapter will be taken from.
  4. Set the first corner point. Mark the first corner from the controlling baseline at the approved dimension.
  5. Measure and mark the first side. Measure the approved length from the first corner along the baseline.
  6. Establish the adjacent side at the approved angle. Set the second side perpendicular to the first using the framing square as a preliminary check.
  7. Set remaining corner points. Complete the rectangle using the approved width and length.
  8. Measure both diagonals. Measure corner-to-corner across both diagonals of the rectangle.
  9. Adjust corners until the layout meets the approved squareness tolerance. Make small incremental adjustments and remeasure both diagonals after each adjustment.
  10. Recheck all side lengths after diagonal adjustment. Confirm every side still matches the approved dimension after squaring.
  11. Set offset stakes or batter boards beyond excavation limits. Place references far enough outside the excavation area to survive digging.
  12. Transfer corner and edge lines to offsets. Use string lines to project each corner and edge line onto the offset stakes or batter boards.
  13. Mark the slab perimeter. Mark the finished-slab edge line using paint or flags.
  14. Mark excavation limits accounting for form thickness, base extension, and working room. Mark a separate line outside the slab perimeter reflecting these additions.
  15. Establish finished-surface reference points. Mark the approved finished elevation at each corner and at any required intermediate point, using the single elevation reference.
  16. Mark high edge, low edge, and drainage direction. Identify and mark which edge is high, which is low, and the direction water will flow across the finished slab.
  17. Verify slope does not direct water toward prohibited areas. Confirm the marked drainage direction matches the approved discharge path from Chapter 1 and does not direct water toward a building, neighboring property, or prohibited discharge point.
  18. Mark isolation joints, drains, sleeves, and embedded items. Mark each location shown on the approved plan or project specification.
  19. Record all dimensions, diagonals, elevations, and reference points. Complete the layout worksheet with every measurement taken in this sequence.
  20. Obtain layout approval before excavation. Do not proceed to Chapter 4 until the layout record is reviewed and approved.

D. Documentation and Handoff

  • Layout worksheet: the completed record of every dimension, diagonal, elevation, and reference point from Steps 1–20.
  • Offset and batter-board record: location and elevation of every offset reference, retained for Chapter 4 excavation and Chapter 6 form-building.
  • Drainage and slope record: marked high edge, low edge, and drainage direction, retained for Chapter 7 verification.
  • Layout approval record: the documented approval obtained before excavation begins in Chapter 4.

Common Layout Inspection

Inspect every layout using the same checks regardless of slab type.

  • Dimension check: confirm all four sides match the approved dimensions.
  • Square check: confirm both diagonals match within the approved squareness tolerance.
  • Elevation check: confirm every finished-surface reference point is taken from the single stated elevation reference.
  • Offset check: confirm offset stakes or batter boards are set beyond the excavation limit and will survive excavation.
  • Drainage check: confirm marked slope direction matches the approved discharge path and avoids prohibited areas.
  • Excavation-limit check: confirm the excavation limit line is marked separately from the finished-slab perimeter.
  • Documentation check: confirm the layout worksheet is complete before approval is issued.

Complete the Layout Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 4.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Diagonals Do Not Match After Corner Adjustment

Probable cause: only one corner was adjusted without rechecking the adjacent side lengths and both diagonals afterward.
Confirmation: remeasure all four side lengths and both diagonals as a complete set.
Correction: make small incremental adjustments to the corner points, remeasuring the full set after each adjustment, until both diagonals match within the approved squareness tolerance and all side lengths remain correct.
Reinspection: repeat the square check before setting offset stakes or batter boards.

Offset Stakes Set Too Close to the Excavation Limit

Probable cause: offset stakes or batter boards were set without accounting for the full excavation limit, including form thickness, base extension, and working room.
Confirmation: compare the offset stake locations against the marked excavation-limit line.
Correction: reset offset stakes or batter boards farther from the excavation limit and re-transfer the corner and edge lines.
Reinspection: repeat the offset check before excavation begins in Chapter 4.

Elevation Reference Inconsistent With Chapter 1

Probable cause: a new or different reference point was used for finished-surface elevation marks instead of the single elevation reference selected in Chapter 1.
Confirmation: compare every recorded elevation note in this chapter against the Chapter 1 worksheet's stated reference point.
Correction: re-transfer every finished-surface reference point from the original Chapter 1 elevation reference.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation check on the corrected layout worksheet.

Drainage Direction Marked Toward a Prohibited Area

Probable cause: the high edge and low edge were marked without reconfirming the approved discharge path documented in Chapter 1.
Confirmation: compare the marked drainage direction against the Chapter 1 discharge-path finding.
Correction: re-mark the high edge, low edge, and drainage direction to match the approved discharge path, and document the correction.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage check before finishing layout documentation.

Layout Approval Issued With Incomplete Documentation

Probable cause: layout approval was requested before all dimensions, diagonals, elevations, and reference points were recorded on the worksheet.
Confirmation: review the layout worksheet against the full list of required measurements from Steps 1–19.
Correction: complete every missing measurement or reference point on the worksheet before resubmitting for approval.
Reinspection: repeat the documentation check before excavation begins.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a completed, approved layout record, not a set of stakes with no supporting documentation.

  • Confirm offset stakes and batter boards intended to remain are secure and clearly marked for Chapter 4
  • Remove any temporary marking, string, or stakes used only for the diagonal-squaring process and not needed afterward
  • Confirm utility-locate markings remain visible and undisturbed for Chapter 4 excavation
  • Return the approved site plan and any structural or drainage details to secure, organized storage
  • File the completed layout worksheet, offset and batter-board record, and layout approval record together

Field Terms

Controlling Baseline
The single reference line every corner point, offset, and dimension in this chapter is measured from.
Squareness Tolerance
The maximum permitted difference between the two diagonal measurements of the layout, as stated in the approved project specification.
Offset Stake
A stake set beyond the excavation limit so a corner point can be re-established after excavation disturbs the original mark.
Batter Board
A small horizontal frame set on stakes beyond the excavation limit, used to hang string lines that reestablish corner and edge lines.
Excavation Limit
The marked line accounting for slab perimeter, form thickness, base extension, and working room, beyond the finished-slab perimeter.
Finished-Surface Reference Point
A marked point indicating the approved finished elevation of the slab surface at a specific location, taken from the single elevation reference.
High Edge / Low Edge
The edges of the layout representing the highest and lowest points of the approved finished slope.
Discharge Path
The approved route surface water follows away from the slab, confirmed in Chapter 1 and verified again at layout.
Layout Approval
The documented confirmation that dimensions, squareness, elevation references, and drainage direction are correct before excavation begins.
Layout Worksheet
The document recording every dimension, diagonal, elevation, and reference point established during this chapter's sequence.

Performance Check

With the layout worksheet fully recorded, review it against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm all four side lengths match the approved dimensions, both diagonals match within the approved squareness tolerance, every elevation note is taken from the single elevation reference established in Chapter 1, offset stakes or batter boards are set beyond the excavation limit, the marked drainage direction matches the approved discharge path, and the excavation-limit line is marked separately from the finished-slab perimeter. Correct any open item before issuing a final layout approval, and do not begin Chapter 4 excavation until approval is documented.

Project Closeout

You have completed the third chapter in the concrete flatwork sequence: laying out a small concrete slab or pad. This chapter required you to establish slab dimensions from the approved site plan and Chapter 1 worksheet (FW-013), square the layout using the diagonal measurement method (FW-014), mark excavation limits accounting for form thickness, base extension, and working room (FW-015), set reference lines, offset stakes, and batter boards beyond the excavation limits (FW-016), verify slope direction and finished-surface reference points (FW-017), and document the final layout completely (FW-018).

You now have a squared, referenced, documented layout that Chapter 4 will excavate to directly, without reinterpreting any dimension or elevation. The offset stakes and batter boards you set here will reestablish the corner points once excavation removes the original marks, and the drainage direction you confirmed here carries forward through form-setting in Chapter 7.

File your completed layout worksheet, offset and batter-board record, and layout approval record together. Chapter 4 depends on these exact measurements.
