Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to calculate, batch, mix, stage, and inspect bagged concrete for one small pad or practice placement. The finished work product is a documented set of mixed, staged, and inspected concrete batches produced within the approved bag count, water range, and mixing-time controls, ready for placement in Chapter 10.

You will calculate the required concrete volume from approved dimensions, convert that volume to a bag count using the manufacturer's stated yield, inspect bagged material before use, measure and add water within the approved range, mix each batch to a workable and uniform consistency, and record the actual water used, batch times, and weather conditions.

This chapter follows Chapter 8, which installed and inspected the reinforcement system now in place, and precedes Chapter 10, which places, screeds, and bull floats the concrete you mix here. Nothing in this chapter invents a bag yield, water range, mixing time, or slump limit that is not supplied by the manufacturer's product instructions or the approved concrete specification.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FW-049 — Calculate bag quantities from approved project volume and manufacturer yield.
  • FW-050 — Add water correctly within the manufacturer's approved range and mixer instructions.
  • FW-051 — Mix concrete to a workable, uniform consistency for the specified placement.
  • FW-052 — Avoid overwatering and recognize the signs of an overwatered or segregated batch.
  • FW-053 — Stage mixed batches for discharge in coordination with placement and finishing capacity.
  • FW-054 — Inspect mix quality and reject contaminated, overwatered, or inconsistent batches.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter depends directly on the approved dimensions confirmed in Chapter 3 and the completed pre-pour inspection from Chapter 8. It cannot proceed until forms, base, and reinforcement have been accepted, because the volume calculation in this chapter assumes the finished slab dimensions those chapters established.

Every batch produced here carries forward into Chapter 10 exactly as mixed. A batch mixed outside the approved water range, or accepted despite visible segregation or dry pockets, becomes a placement and finishing problem in the next chapter that cannot be corrected after discharge. The stop-work discipline in this chapter — rejecting any batch that fails inspection rather than placing it anyway — protects every remaining step through curing in Chapter 12.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • calculating concrete volume from approved project dimensions
  • converting volume to a required bag count using the manufacturer's stated yield
  • inspecting bagged material for moisture damage, lumps, tears, and expiration before use
  • measuring and adding water within the approved manufacturer range
  • mixing each batch to a workable, uniform consistency
  • staging batches for discharge in coordination with the placement and finishing crew
  • inspecting and documenting mix quality, and rejecting unacceptable batches

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • add water beyond the manufacturer's approved range or site authorization, or add water to the surface of a discharged batch merely to make it easier to place
  • invent a bag yield, water range, mixing time, or slump limit that is not supplied by the manufacturer's product instructions or the approved concrete specification
  • use retempered, partially set, frozen, contaminated, or expired concrete materials
  • mix fresh material into a partially set batch, or place a batch that fails the mix-quality inspection

Any bagged material that appears moisture-damaged, hardened, torn, or expired must be documented and set aside, not mixed and inspected afterward.

Trade Note

The bag count, water range, and mixing time recorded in this chapter carry forward into every batch mixed for this project. Recording an estimated water quantity instead of the actual amount added, rather than measuring and logging it precisely, is the single most common source of inconsistent strength and finishing problems diagnosed later in this book.

Setup Standard

Do not add water beyond the manufacturer's approved range or site authorization. Record any authorized water addition on the batch log. A batch mixed outside the approved water range must be treated as rejected until the manufacturer's tolerance is reviewed and the batch is confirmed acceptable.

Shop Tip

Add the initial measured water to the mixer or tub before the bagged material, following the product instructions. Adding all the dry material first and then trying to work water into it increases dry pockets and encourages overwatering to compensate.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every batch mixed in this chapter must reach the placement crew within its working time, at the approved water range, and free of dry pockets or segregation. A batch that is technically mixed but overwatered, contaminated, or partially set is not a usable batch; it must be rejected and documented, not placed and corrected later.

Volume, Water, and Working Time as One System

A batch that is correctly proportioned but mixed too far ahead of the placement crew will begin to set before it is discharged. A batch that is discharged on schedule but mixed with too much water will segregate and weaken regardless of timing. Every batch in this chapter must be evaluated against volume, water, mixing time, and placement coordination together, not against any single factor alone.

Measurement Cue — Convert Volume to Bags Using Manufacturer Yield Only

Calculate the approved concrete volume from the project dimensions confirmed in Chapter 3, then convert that volume to a bag count using the yield stated on the product packaging or instructions. Do not substitute an estimated yield or round down the required bag count to save material.

Critical Safety Cue — Wet-Cement Burns

Wet concrete and cementitious materials can cause severe skin and eye burns. Wear waterproof gloves, boots, long sleeves, and eye protection continuously while mixing. Remove wet concrete from skin and clothing promptly and use the immediate washing procedure established in Chapter 2.

Field Safety Cue — Mixer Stability and Dust Control

Position the mixer on stable, level ground before starting. Keep hands, tools, and loose clothing clear of the rotating drum or moving mixing paddle. Control dust while pouring dry bagged material by pouring slowly and close to the water surface.

Manufacturer Cue — Water Range and Mixing Time Are Product-Specific

The approved water range and mixing time are stated by the bagged product's manufacturer instructions, not by general trade practice. Confirm the exact product before mixing and follow its stated water range and mixing duration exactly.

Bagged Concrete Batching and Mixing Plan

Difficulty: Beginner

Estimated Time: 2–3 hours

Estimated Cost: Bagged concrete mix per approved bag count and waste allowance

Finished Work: Mixed, staged, and inspected concrete batches ready for Chapter 10 placement

A. Finished-Project Specification

Scope: calculated, batched, mixed, staged, and inspected bagged concrete for one small pad or practice placement, produced within the approved bag count, water range, and mixing-time controls and documented on the batch log.

B. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review project volume, bag yield, waste allowance, mix specification, water range, mixer capacity, and placement sequence. Confirm the exact bagged product and its stated yield before calculating quantities.
  2. Calculate concrete volume from approved dimensions. Use only the dimensions confirmed in Chapter 3 and verified through the Chapter 8 pre-pour inspection.
  3. Convert volume to required bag count using manufacturer yield. Apply the approved waste allowance to the calculated bag count.
  4. Verify enough material is on site before mixing. Confirm the full calculated bag count, plus waste allowance, is staged and available.
  5. Inspect bags for moisture damage, hard lumps, tears, and expiration. Set aside and document any bag that fails inspection.
  6. Stage clean water, measuring containers, mixer, wheelbarrows, tools, and washout. Confirm the washout area established in Chapter 2 is ready for use.
  7. Wear full wet-cement and dust PPE. Confirm gloves, boots, long sleeves, eye protection, and respiratory protection are in place before mixing begins.
  8. Position the mixer on stable ground. Confirm the mixer will not shift or tip during operation.
  9. Add the initial measured water according to the mixer and product instructions. Measure water using the staged containers, not by estimation.
  10. Add bagged material gradually while controlling dust. Pour close to the water surface and at a controlled rate.
  11. Add only the remaining permitted water needed for the approved consistency. Do not exceed the manufacturer's stated maximum water range.
  12. Mix for the specified time. Use the stopwatch or timer to confirm the manufacturer's stated mixing duration is met.
  13. Inspect consistency, aggregate distribution, color, dry pockets, and segregation. Confirm the batch is uniform before discharge.
  14. Do not add water merely to make placement easier. Reject any impulse to loosen the batch beyond the approved range for convenience.
  15. Record actual water used. Log the measured quantity on the batch log immediately after mixing.
  16. Discharge the batch promptly. Move the batch to the placement crew within its working time.
  17. Keep batch size coordinated with placement and finishing crew capacity. Do not mix faster than the crew can place and finish.
  18. Begin the next batch using the same measured procedure. Repeat Steps 9 through 16 for each subsequent batch.
  19. Do not mix fresh material into partially set concrete. Discard any batch that has begun to set before discharge.
  20. Reject contaminated, overwatered, partially set, or inconsistent batches. Document the rejection and the reason on the batch log.
  21. Clean equipment only in the contained washout area. Confirm no wash water or slurry reaches soil, drains, or waterways.
  22. Document bag count, water, batch time, weather, and mix acceptance. Complete the batch log entry for every batch mixed.

D. Documentation and Handoff

  • Batch log: the completed record of bag count, water used, mixing time, consistency inspection, and acceptance or rejection for every batch.
  • Waste and rejection record: every bag or batch set aside or rejected, with the documented reason.
  • Weather record: conditions recorded at the time of mixing, retained for reference through Chapter 10 placement and Chapter 12 curing.
  • Washout record: confirmation that all equipment cleaning occurred in the contained washout area established in Chapter 2.

Common Mixing Inspection

Inspect every batch using the same checks regardless of batch size.

  • Material check: confirm bagged material is free of moisture damage, hard lumps, tears, and expiration before use.
  • Volume check: confirm the bag count was calculated from approved project volume and manufacturer yield.
  • Water check: confirm water was measured and added within the manufacturer's approved range, and that the actual quantity was recorded.
  • Mixing-time check: confirm each batch was mixed for the manufacturer's specified duration.
  • Consistency check: confirm uniform color, aggregate distribution, and the absence of dry pockets or segregation.
  • Coordination check: confirm batch size and timing match placement and finishing crew capacity.
  • Rejection check: confirm any contaminated, overwatered, partially set, or inconsistent batch was rejected and documented, not placed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Batch Overwatered to Ease Placement

Probable cause: water was added beyond the manufacturer's approved range because the batch felt stiff or difficult to move.
Confirmation: compare the recorded water quantity on the batch log against the manufacturer's approved maximum.
Correction: reject the batch, document the reason, and mix a replacement batch within the approved water range.
Reinspection: repeat the water and consistency checks on the replacement batch before discharge.

Dry Pockets or Segregation in a Discharged Batch

Probable cause: bagged material was added too quickly, mixing time was cut short, or the batch was not inspected before discharge.
Confirmation: visually inspect the batch for uneven color, dry material at the bottom or sides, or excess paste separating at the surface.
Correction: reject the batch, document the reason, and remix a replacement batch for the full specified mixing time.
Reinspection: repeat the consistency check on the replacement batch before discharge.

Batch Count Miscalculated From Estimated Volume

Probable cause: the bag count was estimated rather than calculated from the approved project volume and manufacturer yield.
Confirmation: recalculate the required volume from the Chapter 3 dimensions and compare against the bag count actually staged.
Correction: obtain the correct bag count, including the approved waste allowance, before continuing to mix.
Reinspection: repeat the volume and bag-count check before mixing the next batch.

Batch Mixed Faster Than Placement Crew Can Use It

Probable cause: batches were mixed continuously without confirming the placement and finishing crew's actual capacity.
Confirmation: compare batch discharge times on the batch log against the placement crew's confirmed pace.
Correction: pause mixing until the placement crew is ready for the next batch, and discard any batch that has exceeded its working time.
Reinspection: repeat the coordination check before mixing the next batch.

Contaminated or Expired Bagged Material Used Without Inspection

Probable cause: bags were used without inspecting for moisture damage, hard lumps, tears, or expiration.
Confirmation: review the bagged-material inspection record against the bags actually used in the affected batch.
Correction: reject any batch mixed from bags that fail inspection, document the reason, and replace with inspected, acceptable material.
Reinspection: repeat the material check before mixing the next batch.

Water Quantity Not Recorded

Probable cause: the batch was mixed and discharged before the actual water quantity was logged.
Confirmation: review the batch log for a missing or incomplete water entry against the batches discharged.
Correction: document the actual water quantity from direct observation if verifiable, or flag the batch record as incomplete if it cannot be confirmed.
Reinspection: repeat the documentation check before the batch log is finalized.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a completed batch log and a clean, contained mixing area, not a verbal impression of how the mixing went.

  • Clean the mixer, mixing tub, hoe or paddle, and measuring containers only in the contained washout area
  • Remove and dispose of empty and rejected bags without discharging residue to soil, drains, or waterways
  • Confirm no wash water or slurry has reached storm drains, gutters, or landscaping
  • File the completed batch log, waste and rejection record, and weather record together
  • Store remaining unopened bagged material protected from moisture for the next scheduled batch

Performance Check

With the batch log fully recorded, review it against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the bag count was calculated from approved volume and manufacturer yield, every bag was inspected before use, water was measured and recorded within the approved range for every batch, mixing time met the manufacturer's specification, consistency was inspected before discharge, batch size was coordinated with placement capacity, and every rejected batch was documented rather than placed. Complete the waste and rejection record and correct any open item before beginning Chapter 10. If any batch cannot be confirmed acceptable against these standards, reject it and mix a replacement rather than placing it and correcting the result later.
