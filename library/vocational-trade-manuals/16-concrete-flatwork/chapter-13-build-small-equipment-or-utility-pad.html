Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to build one small concrete equipment or utility pad from an approved specification through layout, subgrade preparation, aggregate base, forms, finished elevation, reinforcement, concrete placement, surface finish, curing, and final inspection. The finished work product is a single cured concrete pad, level or sloped exactly as approved, with anchors, sleeves, conduit, or embeds located and ready for equipment installation.

You will review the equipment footprint, dimensions, loading, anchors, service clearances, drainage, and manufacturer requirements supplied by the approved plan; confirm utility locations and equipment-contractor coordination; lay out the pad; prepare the subgrade and base; build and brace forms; set finished elevation and slope; install isolation material, reinforcement, and embeds; complete a pre-pour inspection; place, screed, and float the concrete; finish and joint the surface; cure and protect the pad; strip forms; and inspect the finished pad against dimensions, elevation, surface, anchors, drainage, and edge condition before handoff.

This chapter draws directly on the site inspection and work-area setup from Chapters 1 and 2, the layout method from Chapter 3, the excavation and subgrade work from Chapter 4, the aggregate base work from Chapter 5, the form-building and bracing work from Chapter 6, the slope and elevation control from Chapter 7, the reinforcement work from Chapter 8, the mixing or batching work from Chapter 9, the placement and finishing work from Chapters 10 and 11, and the curing and protection work from Chapter 12. It applies each of those skills to one applied project: a pad built specifically to support fixed equipment.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FW-073 — Lay out a utility pad from the approved equipment footprint, dimensions, and working clearance.
  • FW-074 — Prepare the subgrade and aggregate base to the approved elevation, thickness, and support condition.
  • FW-075 — Build forms to the approved dimensions, elevation, slope, and bracing requirements.
  • FW-076 — Place concrete for the pad, consolidated at edges and around anchors, sleeves, and embeds.
  • FW-077 — Finish the pad surface to the specified texture, edges, and joints.
  • FW-078 — Inspect the completed pad for level or approved slope, drainage, dimensions, and equipment-bearing readiness.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter is the first of the applied-project chapters in this book. It uses every foundational skill built in Chapters 1 through 12 — site inspection, work-area setup, layout, excavation, aggregate base, forms, elevation and slope, reinforcement, mixing, placement, finishing, and curing — and applies them together to one small, complete equipment or utility pad.

A utility pad differs from a sidewalk or patio in one controlling way: it must remain compatible with a specific piece of equipment. Anchor locations, sleeve and conduit positions, service clearances, and the finished elevation are not general-purpose decisions; they are fixed by the equipment manufacturer's requirements and by the equipment-contractor's coordination. Nothing in this chapter authorizes inventing an anchor pattern, sleeve location, or pad dimension that the approved plan and manufacturer instructions do not already supply.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved equipment footprint, pad dimensions, loading, anchors, service clearances, drainage, and manufacturer requirements
  • confirming utility locations and equipment-contractor coordination before layout
  • laying out the pad and its working clearance
  • excavating and compacting the subgrade and installing and compacting the aggregate base
  • building and bracing forms and setting finished elevation and approved slope
  • installing isolation material, reinforcement, embeds, anchors, sleeves, conduit, and drains where specified
  • completing the pre-pour inspection, placing and finishing the concrete, curing and protecting the pad, and stripping forms when permitted
  • inspecting the finished pad and documenting the equipment handoff

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • invent an anchor pattern, sleeve or conduit location, pad dimension, slab thickness, reinforcement schedule, concrete strength, slump limit, joint spacing, curing period, weather limit, slope, elevation, or load rating that is not supplied by the approved plan, equipment manufacturer's instructions, adopted code, or the chapter project specification
  • install equipment before the approved cure period or strength condition is reached
  • proceed past unidentified utilities, unstable soil, or unresolved equipment-contractor coordination
  • treat this pad's procedure as a substitute for an engineered structural foundation where the equipment manufacturer or adopted code requires one

Large or heavily loaded equipment pads, and any pad supporting equipment with structural, seismic, or engineered foundation requirements, may require an engineer, a structural detail, and a separate permit and inspection sequence not covered by this chapter.

Trade Note

Every anchor, sleeve, conduit, and drain location installed in this chapter becomes permanently concealed once concrete is placed. Confirming these locations against the equipment manufacturer's instructions and the equipment contractor's coordination before the pre-pour inspection is the single most reliable way to avoid a pad that cannot receive the intended equipment.

Setup Standard

Do not place concrete until the equipment contractor's anchor, sleeve, conduit, and drain locations are confirmed against the approved plan and the pre-pour inspection is complete. A pad poured before this confirmation must be treated as nonconforming and corrected before equipment installation proceeds.

Shop Tip

Photograph every anchor, sleeve, conduit, and embed location immediately before placement, with a tape measure or reference line visible in the frame. These photographs are the only record of exact position once the pad is poured.

Common Field Fundamentals

A utility or equipment pad succeeds or fails on the same site-and-material system as every other project in this book — drainage, soil support, base, forms, reinforcement, placement, and cure — with one additional requirement layered on top: everything concealed by the concrete must exactly match what the equipment needs once installed.

Equipment Compatibility as a Controlling Requirement

A pad with excellent concrete quality and finish is still a failed project if an anchor is out of position, a sleeve is missing, or the elevation does not match the equipment manufacturer's base. Every step in this chapter that touches an embed, anchor, sleeve, conduit, or elevation must be checked against the approved plan and the equipment contractor's requirements before concrete conceals it.

Manufacturer Cue — Equipment Base Requirements Control

Follow the equipment manufacturer's instructions for anchor pattern, anchor embedment, pad thickness at the bearing zone, service clearance, and any required isolation or vibration-control detail. Where the manufacturer's instructions and the approved pad plan conflict, stop work and resolve the conflict before proceeding.

Critical Safety Cue — Utility Locate Before Layout

Confirm the utility locate remains valid before driving any stake, pin, or probe for this pad's layout. Utility pads are frequently placed near existing electrical, gas, water, or communication services; do not assume prior clearance from earlier chapters remains accurate without reconfirming it for this specific footprint.

Field Safety Cue — Wet Concrete and Embedded Items

Keep hands and skin clear of wet concrete around anchors, sleeves, and conduit during placement and consolidation. Wear waterproof gloves and eye protection, and wash any wet concrete from skin promptly.

Code Alert — Structural and Engineered Pads

Verify whether the specific equipment requires an engineered foundation, seismic anchorage, or structural design rather than a standard utility pad. Do not use this chapter's procedure as a substitute for an engineered detail where one is required.

A. Finished-Project Specification

Scope: one small concrete equipment or utility pad, built to the approved footprint, dimensions, loading, anchors, service clearances, drainage, and finish, and handed off cured and inspected for equipment installation.

B. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review equipment footprint, pad dimensions, loading, anchors, service clearances, drainage, and manufacturer requirements. Confirm the approved plan supplies every dimension and embed location needed.
  2. Confirm utility locations and equipment-contractor coordination. Reconfirm the utility locate for this specific footprint and verify anchor, sleeve, and conduit requirements with the equipment contractor.
  3. Lay out the pad and working clearance. Establish corners, square, and the approved service-clearance boundary using the Chapter 3 layout method.
  4. Excavate and compact the subgrade. Follow the Chapter 4 subgrade procedure to the approved depth and slope.
  5. Install and compact the aggregate base. Follow the Chapter 5 base procedure to the approved thickness and elevation.
  6. Build and brace forms. Follow the Chapter 6 form procedure, squared and braced for the pad's dimensions and pressure.
  7. Set finished elevation and approved slope. Follow the Chapter 7 elevation and slope procedure, referenced to the equipment manufacturer's required bearing elevation.
  8. Install isolation material where the pad meets structures or paving. Place isolation material at every interface specified in the approved plan.
  9. Install reinforcement and embeds where specified. Follow the Chapter 8 reinforcement procedure and place anchors, sleeves, and conduit at the confirmed locations.
  10. Verify anchor, sleeve, conduit, and drain locations. Check every embed against the approved plan and the equipment contractor's confirmed requirements before proceeding.
  11. Complete the pre-pour inspection. Verify forms, base, reinforcement, embeds, isolation material, and elevation before concrete arrives.
  12. Calculate and stage concrete quantity. Confirm volume from the approved pad dimensions and stage materials, tools, and crew before placement.
  13. Mix or receive concrete. Follow the Chapter 9 procedure for bagged concrete or the ready-mix delivery documentation for the approved mix.
  14. Place and consolidate concrete. Follow the Chapter 10 placement procedure, consolidating carefully around every anchor, sleeve, and embed.
  15. Screed and float the pad. Strike off to form elevation and bull-float following the Chapter 10 procedure.
  16. Finish the surface to the specified texture. Follow the Chapter 11 finishing procedure for the texture specified for this pad's use.
  17. Install edges and joints. Edge the perimeter and tool or saw-cut joints as specified.
  18. Verify equipment-bearing zones remain within the approved plane. Check the finished surface at the bearing zone against the approved elevation before curing begins.
  19. Apply curing and protection. Follow the Chapter 12 curing procedure for the specified method and duration.
  20. Strip forms when permitted. Remove forms only after the approved time or strength condition is reached.
  21. Check dimensions, elevation, surface, anchors, level or drainage slope, and edge condition. Inspect the finished pad against every item in the Acceptance Standards table.
  22. Do not install equipment before approved cure or strength. Confirm the approved condition is met before releasing the pad to the equipment contractor.
  23. Document final pad condition and equipment handoff. Complete the pad work record and hand off to the equipment contractor.

D. Documentation and Handoff

  • Pad work record: equipment type, plan reference, dates, crew, and every inspection result from layout through final acceptance.
  • Embed photographs: anchor, sleeve, conduit, and drain locations photographed with a reference measurement before placement.
  • Concrete tickets or batch log: quantity, mix, batch time, and weather at placement.
  • Curing record: method, start time, duration, and inspection intervals through the required curing period.
  • Equipment handoff record: confirmation of cure or strength condition met, and the date the pad was released to the equipment contractor.

Common Site-Readiness Inspection

Inspect every equipment or utility pad using the same checks regardless of the equipment it will support.

  • Plan check: confirm the equipment footprint, dimensions, loading, anchors, clearances, drainage, and manufacturer requirements are complete before layout.
  • Coordination check: confirm utility locations and equipment-contractor requirements are confirmed before layout.
  • Base and form check: confirm subgrade, base, forms, and elevation meet the approved plan before reinforcement is placed.
  • Embed check: confirm every anchor, sleeve, conduit, and drain location before the pre-pour inspection.
  • Placement and finish check: confirm placement, screeding, floating, edging, jointing, and finish meet the specified texture and plane.
  • Cure check: confirm curing method and duration are applied and documented before form stripping.
  • Handoff check: confirm final inspection is complete and the cure or strength condition is met before equipment installation.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Anchor or Sleeve Out of Position

Probable cause: an anchor, sleeve, or conduit shifted during concrete placement or consolidation, or was not verified against the approved plan before placement.
Confirmation: measure the embed's final position against the approved plan and the pre-placement photographs.
Correction: if the pad has not yet cured, correct the embed position immediately; if cured, consult the equipment contractor and manufacturer instructions for an approved core-drill, epoxy-anchor, or other compatible correction method.
Reinspection: repeat the embed check before equipment handoff.

Bearing Zone Out of Plane

Probable cause: forms were not set to the equipment manufacturer's required bearing elevation, or the surface was not checked against the approved plane before curing began.
Confirmation: check the bearing zone with a level or straightedge against the approved elevation.
Correction: where within approved tolerance, correct with an approved leveling or grouting method compatible with the equipment manufacturer's instructions; where out of tolerance, refer to the equipment contractor before installation.
Reinspection: repeat the elevation check before equipment handoff.

Drainage Ponds at the Pad Edge

Probable cause: the approved slope was not maintained through form-setting, placement, or finishing, or a low spot was left unrescreeded.
Confirmation: observe water flow or use a level to check slope direction across the pad.
Correction: document the deficiency; where correction is required, follow an approved method compatible with the equipment's service clearance and drainage requirements.
Reinspection: repeat the drainage check after any correction.

Isolation Joint Missing or Bridged

Probable cause: isolation material was omitted, displaced, or bridged with concrete at a structure or fixed-paving interface.
Confirmation: inspect every interface identified in the approved plan for continuous isolation material.
Correction: saw-cut a full-depth isolation joint at the affected interface following the approved method.
Reinspection: repeat the isolation check before final acceptance.

Equipment Installed Before Approved Cure

Probable cause: the equipment contractor proceeded with installation before the approved cure period or strength condition was confirmed.
Confirmation: review the curing record and compare the installation date to the approved cure or strength condition.
Correction: stop installation immediately if still in progress; document the deviation and inspect the pad for damage before allowing installation to proceed or resume.
Reinspection: repeat the final acceptance inspection before confirming handoff.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a cured, inspected pad and a complete work record, not a poured slab awaiting undocumented equipment installation.

  • Remove stripped forms, stakes, and bracing material from the work area
  • Clean and store reusable tools, screeds, floats, edgers, jointers, and brooms
  • Clean the contained washout area and confirm no wash water reached soil, drains, or waterways
  • File the pad work record, embed photographs, concrete tickets, curing record, and equipment handoff record together
  • Confirm the no-traffic and no-installation period remains marked until the approved cure or strength condition is met

Performance Check

With the pad fully cured and the pad inspection record completed, review it against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the layout matched the approved footprint and clearance, the subgrade and base met the approved elevation and thickness, forms were set to the approved elevation and slope, isolation material and reinforcement were installed and inspected, every anchor, sleeve, conduit, and drain was verified in position before the pre-pour inspection, placement and finishing met the specified texture and plane, and curing was applied and documented for the full approved period. Confirm the approved cure or strength condition is met before releasing the pad to the equipment contractor, and correct any open deficiency before completing the handoff record.
