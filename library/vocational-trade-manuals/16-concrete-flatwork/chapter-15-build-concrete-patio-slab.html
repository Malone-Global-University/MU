Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to build one small exterior patio slab from an approved site plan through cured, protected handoff. The finished work product is one patio slab with approved dimensions, isolation from the structure, finished slope draining away from the building, reinforcement and embeds where specified, the specified surface texture, correct joints, and a completed curing and protection record.

You will lay out and square the patio, confirm utilities and building interfaces, excavate and compact the subgrade, install the aggregate base, build and brace forms, establish the high edge at the structure and the low edge at the approved drainage side, install isolation joint material, install reinforcement and embeds, complete the pre-pour inspection, place and finish the concrete, cure and protect the slab, strip forms, and inspect the completed patio against dimensions, slope, joints, edge condition, finish, and building clearance.

This chapter draws directly on Chapter 1's site-readiness plan, Chapter 3's layout method, Chapter 4's subgrade preparation, Chapter 5's base placement, Chapter 6's form construction, Chapter 7's slope and elevation control, Chapter 8's reinforcement placement, Chapters 9 through 11's mixing, placement, and finishing, and Chapter 12's curing procedures. Nothing in this chapter invents a dimension, slope, thickness, or curing period that the approved plan or concrete specification does not already supply.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FW-085 — Lay out a patio slab from the approved site plan, including squaring and excavation and isolation limits.
  • FW-086 — Establish drainage away from the structure, including the high edge at the building and the low edge at the approved discharge side.
  • FW-087 — Install aggregate base and forms to the approved elevation, slope, and support condition.
  • FW-088 — Place concrete for the patio slab, including consolidation, screeding, and bull floating.
  • FW-089 — Finish the patio surface, including edging, jointing, and the specified texture.
  • FW-090 — Inspect the final slope, joints, edge condition, and building clearance and document project closeout.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter integrates the site-preparation skills from Chapters 1 through 8 and the concrete-production and finishing skills from Chapters 9 through 12 into one complete exterior project immediately adjacent to a structure. The isolation-joint discipline introduced here — separating the patio from the foundation, steps, and posts rather than rigidly connecting it — is the same discipline required at the sidewalk transitions in Chapter 14 and at every fixed-structure interface in the Chapter 20 capstone.

The drainage-away-from-the-structure requirement in this chapter also carries forward directly: any patio that traps water against the building, siding, or foundation is a defect regardless of how well it is finished, jointed, or cured.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • laying out, squaring, and marking excavation and isolation limits for one small exterior patio slab
  • excavating and compacting the subgrade and installing the aggregate base
  • building, staking, and bracing forms and setting the approved finished elevation and slope
  • installing isolation joint material at the foundation, steps, posts, and other fixed structures where required
  • installing reinforcement, embeds, drains, and future-attachment provisions where specified
  • placing, consolidating, screeding, and bull-floating the patio concrete
  • timing and completing edging, jointing, and the specified surface texture
  • curing, protecting, stripping forms, and inspecting the completed patio

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • assume a deck, roof, post, fireplace, hot tub, or other structural load without an approved design supplied by the outline, plan, or manufacturer instructions
  • invent a slab thickness, base thickness, slope, joint spacing, reinforcement schedule, concrete strength, curing period, or isolation detail not supplied by the approved plan, adopted code, or concrete specification
  • rigidly connect the patio to the foundation, steps, or posts where an isolation joint is specified
  • direct finished slope toward the building, doors, siding, or a prohibited discharge point
  • strip forms, saw joints, apply sealer, or load the slab before the approved strength or time condition

Any condition requiring structural, geotechnical, or public-works review must be documented and referred, not resolved by the learner.

Trade Note

A patio slab sits closer to doors, siding, and foundations than most other flatwork in this book. Every measurement in this chapter — the high edge at the structure, the low edge at the drainage side, and the isolation-joint location — must be checked twice against the building interface before concrete is placed, because a drainage or isolation error here cannot be corrected once the slab has cured.

Setup Standard

Do not place concrete until the pre-pour inspection confirms form elevation and slope, isolation joint material, reinforcement position and cover, and drain, sleeve, and embed locations. A patio poured before this inspection is complete cannot have its isolation or drainage corrected without demolition.

Shop Tip

Set the high edge at the structure first and hold it fixed. Calculate the low edge from the approved slope and run before touching any other form section, so every later elevation check is measured against a single confirmed high point rather than adjusted piecemeal.

Common Field Fundamentals

A patio slab is a complete site-and-material system positioned directly against a structure. Its drainage, isolation, reinforcement, and finish must all be evaluated together, because a defect at the building interface — a trapped low point, a rigid connection where isolation is required, or a joint that conflicts with a drain — cannot be corrected once the slab has cured.

LayoutSquared dimensions, excavation and isolation limits from the approved plan
DrainageHigh edge at the structure, low edge at the approved discharge side

IsolationFull-depth separation from foundation, steps, and posts
ReinforcementPlaced and supported at approved position and cover

Drainage and Isolation as One System

The high edge at the structure and the isolation joint at that same edge are not independent details. If the isolation joint is installed but the slope still directs water toward the building, or the slope is correct but the patio is rigidly connected to the foundation, the patio does not meet the required outcome. Verify both together at the building interface before concrete placement, not as separate checks.

Measurement Cue — One Elevation Reference Through the Whole Project

Use the same elevation reference selected in Chapter 1 for the high edge, the low edge, and every intermediate check in this chapter. Recalculating the high edge from a new reference partway through the project is a common source of the trapped-water defects diagnosed in this book.

Critical Safety Cue — No Assumed Structural Load

Do not build this patio to support a deck, roof, post, fireplace, hot tub, or other structural load unless an approved design supplied by the outline, plan, or manufacturer instructions specifically authorizes it. A patio slab built to the sequence in this chapter is not automatically suitable for structural loading.

Field Safety Cue — Working Near the Structure

Protect doors, siding, foundations, and landscaping throughout excavation, form work, and placement. Keep wet concrete and cementitious materials off siding and finished surfaces, and remove any contact promptly.

Code Alert — Isolation and Drainage Requirements

Verify local requirements for isolation joints at structures and for allowable drainage discharge points before finalizing the layout and slope. Some jurisdictions restrict where patio runoff may be directed, particularly near property lines or public storm systems.

Estimated Cost: Per approved plan and concrete specification

Finished Work: One cured, jointed, drained, and inspected patio slab

A. Finished-Project Specification

Scope: one small exterior patio slab built to the approved dimensions, finished elevations, slope, isolation, reinforcement, finish, and joint plan, draining away from the structure and inspected before handoff.

B. Required Project Sequence

  1. Review patio dimensions, door thresholds, foundation, siding clearance, drainage, finish, joints, furniture or load use, and permits. Confirm every figure against the approved site plan and concrete specification.
  2. Confirm no deck, roof, post, fireplace, hot tub, or other structural load is assumed without approved design. Document the confirmation before proceeding.
  3. Locate utilities and verify building interfaces. Confirm the utility locate remains valid and reconfirm door, foundation, and siding clearances.
  4. Lay out and square the patio. Establish the baseline, corners, and diagonals per the Chapter 3 method.
  5. Mark excavation and isolation limits. Include form thickness, base extension, working room, and isolation-joint location at the structure.
  6. Excavate and compact subgrade. Follow the Chapter 4 sequence, maintaining required separation from the foundation.
  7. Install aggregate base in compacted lifts. Follow the Chapter 5 sequence to the approved thickness and elevation.
  8. Build and brace forms. Follow the Chapter 6 sequence, squaring and bracing the assembly against placement pressure.
  9. Establish high edge at the structure and low edge at the approved drainage side. Set the high edge first, then calculate and set the low edge from the approved slope and run.
  10. Verify finished slope away from the building. Confirm water will not be trapped against the structure or at transitions.
  11. Install isolation joint material against foundation, posts, steps, and fixed structures where required. Maintain full depth and continuous separation.
  12. Install reinforcement and embeds. Follow the Chapter 8 sequence for position, support, and cover.
  13. Verify drains, sleeves, conduits, and future attachments. Confirm each location against the approved plan before concrete conceals it.
  14. Complete pre-pour inspection. Confirm forms, isolation material, reinforcement, cover, and joint conflicts before placement.
  15. Place, consolidate, screed, and bull-float concrete. Follow the Chapter 9 through 10 sequence for mixing or receiving, placing, and striking the slab.
  16. Time edging, joints, and surface finish. Follow the Chapter 11 sequence, observing actual concrete behavior rather than a fixed clock time.
  17. Apply the specified broom, float, or other approved texture. Match the finish schedule exactly.
  18. Begin curing promptly. Follow the Chapter 12 sequence as soon as the finish permits.
  19. Protect doors, siding, landscaping, and drainage paths. Maintain protection through the full curing and protection period.
  20. Strip forms when permitted. Confirm the approved strength or time condition before stripping.
  21. Inspect dimensions, slope, low points, joints, edge condition, finish, and building clearance. Compare every result against the acceptance standards below.
  22. Perform a limited water-flow observation only after the surface condition and curing plan permit it. Confirm water moves to the approved discharge path without ponding at the building.
  23. Document final patio acceptance. Complete the closeout record before handoff.

D. Documentation and Handoff

  • Patio work record: completed record of layout, excavation, base, form, reinforcement, placement, finish, curing, and joint steps.
  • Pre-pour inspection record: confirmation of forms, isolation material, reinforcement, and joint conflicts before placement.
  • Concrete tickets or batch log: retained with placement date, weather, and mix information.
  • Final inspection record: dimensions, slope, joints, edge condition, finish, and building clearance, with the closeout decision.

Common Patio Inspection

Inspect every completed patio using the same checks regardless of finish or joint pattern.

  • Dimension check: confirm final dimensions match the approved layout within tolerance.
  • Slope check: confirm the high edge, low edge, and drainage direction match the approved plan with no low points trapping water at the structure.
  • Isolation check: confirm continuous isolation material at every required structure interface.
  • Reinforcement check: confirm reinforcement position and cover match the approved detail from the pre-pour inspection record.
  • Finish and joint check: confirm edges, joints, and texture match the approved finish schedule.
  • Curing check: confirm the curing method, duration, and protection period were completed before any traffic or loading.
  • Clearance check: confirm doors, siding, foundation, and landscaping remain undamaged and properly cleared.

Complete the Patio Inspection Checklist below before issuing the final closeout decision.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Slope Directs Water Toward the Structure

Probable cause: the high edge was set correctly at the structure, but the calculated fall to the low edge was not verified before forms were braced, or a form section settled after bracing.
Confirmation: recheck elevations across the slab against the single stated reference and compare to the calculated fall.
Correction: readjust and rebrace the affected form section before placement; if concrete has already cured, refer the defect to Chapter 18 repair procedures.
Reinspection: repeat the slope check and, where permitted, the limited water-flow observation.

Isolation Joint Missing or Discontinuous at the Structure

Probable cause: isolation material was installed at some structure interfaces but omitted or gapped at a step, post, or transition.
Confirmation: inspect every structure interface for continuous, full-depth isolation material before concrete placement.
Correction: install the missing isolation material before placement; do not place concrete over a gap and rely on later saw cutting to substitute for isolation.
Reinspection: repeat the isolation check at every interface before proceeding to reinforcement inspection.

Reinforcement Displaced Near the Structure

Probable cause: reinforcement was not adequately supported near the building edge, or foot traffic during isolation-material installation displaced it.
Confirmation: check reinforcement elevation and cover at the structure edge before the pre-pour inspection is signed off.
Correction: reset and re-support the displaced reinforcement to the approved elevation and cover.
Reinspection: repeat the reinforcement check before concrete placement begins.

Trip Hazard or Ponding at a Door Threshold

Probable cause: the finished elevation at the threshold was not verified against the approved plan before or immediately after placement.
Confirmation: measure the finished elevation at the threshold against the recorded high-edge reference.
Correction: where concrete remains workable, correct the plane before finishing; where cured, refer the defect to Chapter 18 for repair evaluation.
Reinspection: repeat the building-clearance check before issuing final acceptance.

Early Traffic or Loading Before Approved Cure

Probable cause: furniture, foot traffic, or equipment was placed on the patio before the approved curing period or strength condition was reached.
Confirmation: review the curing record for start date, method, and required duration against the date of the observed traffic.
Correction: remove the load immediately, inspect the surface for indentation, cracking, or displacement, and document any damage.
Reinspection: repeat the surface and finish inspection before issuing final acceptance.

Structural Load Assumed Without Approved Design

Probable cause: a deck post, hot tub, or similar concentrated load was planned for the patio without a supplied structural design.
Confirmation: compare the intended use against the approved plan and confirm whether structural design documentation exists.
Correction: stop work and refer the condition for structural design and review before proceeding; do not place the load on the standard patio slab.
Reinspection: repeat the scope-and-use confirmation once an approved design is obtained.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a cured, inspected patio slab and a complete closeout record, not a verbal impression of the finished work.

  • Clean and store all forming, placement, and finishing tools in the contained washout area established in Chapter 2
  • Remove stripped form lumber, stakes, and bracing not required for later chapters
  • Confirm curing materials are removed only after the approved curing period is complete
  • File the patio work record, pre-pour inspection record, concrete tickets, and final inspection record together
  • Store photographs with clear labels referencing the work record entries they support

Bull Float
The tool used to embed aggregate and close the surface promptly after screeding.
Control Joint
A tooled or saw-cut joint intended to control the location of shrinkage cracking.
Curing Period
The required duration of moisture retention or protection following the approved curing method.
Limited Water-Flow Observation
A controlled check of drainage behavior performed only after the surface and curing plan permit it.
