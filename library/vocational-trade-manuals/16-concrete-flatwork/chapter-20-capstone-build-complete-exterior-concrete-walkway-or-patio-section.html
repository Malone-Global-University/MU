Job Overview

This capstone chapter qualifies you to plan, lay out, excavate, prepare, form, reinforce, place, finish, cure, joint, inspect, document, and close out one complete exterior walkway or patio section. The finished work product is a single cured, protected, documented exterior walkway or patio section that has passed layout, excavation, base, form, reinforcement, pre-pour, placement, finish, cure, joint, and final acceptance inspections.

Every competency demonstrated in Chapters 1 through 19 is integrated here rather than repeated as an isolated exercise. You will carry forward the site-readiness decision from Chapter 1, the work-area setup from Chapter 2, the layout and squaring method from Chapter 3, the excavation and subgrade practice from Chapter 4, the aggregate base practice from Chapter 5, the form-building and bracing practice from Chapter 6, the slope and elevation control from Chapter 7, the reinforcement placement from Chapter 8, the mixing or delivery control from Chapter 9, the placement and screeding practice from Chapter 10, the edging, jointing, and finishing practice from Chapter 11, the curing and protection practice from Chapter 12, and the joint, repair, and sealing practices from Chapters 17 and 19.

This chapter does not introduce a new dimension, depth, slope, reinforcement schedule, concrete strength, slump limit, joint spacing, curing period, or code provision. Every specification used here must already exist in the approved capstone site plan, the adopted code, the manufacturer instructions, or the ready-mix documentation identified for this project.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FW-115 — Integrate layout, excavation, and subgrade preparation for a complete exterior walkway or patio section from the approved capstone site plan.
  • FW-116 — Integrate gravel base, forms, and slope control to the approved finished elevations and drainage.
  • FW-117 — Integrate reinforcement placement and concrete placement within the approved pre-pour and placement sequence.
  • FW-118 — Integrate finishing, curing, and jointing operations timed to actual concrete behavior and the approved joint plan.
  • FW-119 — Perform final inspection across layout, excavation, base, form, reinforcement, pre-pour, placement, finish, cure, and joint checkpoints.
  • FW-120 — Document the complete flatwork closeout and hand off the section for Book 17 masonry and hardscape or landscape coordination.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter is the final project in Book 16. It does not teach a new technique in isolation; it requires you to sequence and integrate every technique taught since Chapter 1 in the exact site-to-cure order defined by the System-Sequence Control in the book outline: utility locating and drainage planning before excavation, unsuitable-soil removal before compaction, subgrade acceptance before base placement, compacted base before forms, verified forms before reinforcement is accepted, inspected reinforcement before concrete conceals it, readiness confirmation before placement begins, screeding after placement and consolidation, floating after screeding at the proper surface condition, edging and jointing and brooming timed from actual concrete behavior, curing as soon as the finish permits, saw-cut joints within the approved time window, and sealer only after the required cure, dryness, and surface-preparation conditions.

The Capstone Continuity Rule in the book outline permits this project to use an approved exterior practice site adjacent to a structure developed through earlier books, provided drainage, property boundaries, utilities, finished elevations, access, permits, and interfaces with foundations and landscaping are fully defined. The completed section is handed off as a cured, protected, documented exterior walkway or patio section ready for Book 17 masonry coordination and later hardscape or landscape work.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • obtaining and confirming the approved capstone site plan, dimensions, elevations, drainage plan, utility information, reinforcement detail, joint plan, finish, concrete specification, permit requirements, and inspection sequence
  • performing the full Chapter 1 site-readiness inspection and the Chapter 2 work-area setup for this specific capstone site
  • laying out, excavating, basing, forming, reinforcing, placing, finishing, curing, jointing, sealing, inspecting, and documenting one complete exterior walkway or patio section
  • completing every required checkpoint inspection in the site-to-cure sequence before concealing or proceeding past it
  • compiling the full flatwork closeout record and handing off the section for later masonry, hardscape, or landscape coordination

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • invent a slab thickness, excavation depth, gravel-base thickness, compaction target, reinforcement schedule, concrete strength, slump limit, water addition, joint spacing, joint depth, curing period, weather limit, slope, elevation, form dimension, load rating, or code provision that is not supplied by the approved capstone site plan, adopted code, manufacturer instructions, ready-mix documentation, or this chapter's project specification
  • proceed past any checkpoint in the site-to-cure sequence — layout, excavation, base, form, reinforcement, pre-pour, placement, finish, cure, or joint — without the required inspection and documented acceptance
  • strip forms, saw-cut joints, apply sealer, load the section, or open it to traffic before the approved strength or time condition
  • substitute this practice-site capstone for an engineered, permitted, municipal, or public-right-of-way flatwork project without the required professional crew, engineered details, testing, permits, and inspection

Any condition that requires engineering, survey, or professional review must be documented and referred, not resolved by the learner.

Trade Note

This chapter is a sequencing and integration exercise, not a new-technique exercise. Every measurement, elevation, and material quantity used here must trace back to a chapter you have already completed and a document you have already confirmed. Recording an estimate where a measured or plan-confirmed figure is required remains the single most common source of error, and it is more consequential here than in any earlier chapter because it now compounds across the full sequence.

Setup Standard

Do not begin any stage of this capstone before the immediately preceding stage has passed its required checkpoint inspection. A stage completed out of the approved site-to-cure sequence must be treated as unverified, regardless of how it appears, until the correct checkpoint inspection is performed and documented.

Shop Tip

Before starting excavation, lay out every checkpoint inspection on a single closeout log with a blank line for date, inspector, and result. Treating the ten checkpoints as one continuous record, rather than ten separate memories, is what keeps the final punch list short.

Ten Checkpoints, One Sequence

The site-to-cure sequence in the book outline is not optional ordering. Utility locating, drainage planning, access planning, and permit verification occur before excavation. Unsuitable soil and uncontrolled fill are removed before compaction. Subgrade elevation and slope are accepted before base placement. Base is placed and compacted in approved lifts before forms or reinforcement are accepted. Reinforcement, embedded items, isolation joints, sleeves, drains, and penetrations are inspected before concrete conceals them. Concrete quantity, delivery method, crew, tools, weather controls, washout, and curing materials are ready before placement begins. Screeding follows placement and consolidation; floating follows screeding at the proper surface condition. Edging, jointing, and brooming are timed from actual concrete behavior. Curing begins as soon as the finish permits. Saw-cut joints are installed within the approved time window and depth. Sealer is applied only after the required curing, drying, moisture, and surface-preparation conditions.

Measurement Cue — One Elevation Reference for the Entire Capstone

Use the same elevation reference selected in Chapter 1 and carried through Chapters 3 and 7 for every elevation check in this chapter, including the final inspection. A capstone that uses a second reference for the final check cannot be reconciled against its own layout and pre-pour records.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Skip a Concealment Checkpoint

Do not place aggregate base over an unaccepted subgrade, do not accept forms over an unaccepted base, and do not place concrete over unaccepted reinforcement. Each of these materials permanently conceals the work beneath it. A missed checkpoint at any of these stages cannot be corrected once concrete has been placed.

Field Safety Cue — Multi-Stage Site Discipline

This project remains an active excavation, delivery, and placement site across multiple work sessions. Re-verify barricades, utility markings, washout containment, and access routes at the start of every session, not only at the start of the project.

Code Alert — Public-Works and Permit Boundaries

If the capstone site plan places any portion of this section in the public right-of-way, or if the approved plan requires municipal inspection at any checkpoint, complete that specific inspection through the identified public-works process before proceeding, in addition to the checkpoint inspections required by this chapter.

A. Finished-Project Specification

Scope: one complete exterior walkway or patio section, planned, laid out, excavated, prepared, formed, reinforced, placed, finished, cured, jointed, sealed, inspected, and documented per the approved capstone site plan, and handed off ready for Book 17 masonry and future hardscape or landscape coordination.

B. Required Project Sequence

  1. Obtain the approved capstone site plan, flatwork dimensions, finished elevations, drainage plan, utility information, reinforcement detail, joint plan, finish, concrete specification, permit requirements, and inspection sequence. Confirm every document is current and applies to the exact capstone location.
  2. Confirm property boundaries, setbacks, right-of-way, accessibility, and drainage discharge. Compare the capstone site plan against visible boundary markers and confirmed discharge points.
  3. Complete required utility locating. Contact the required utility-location service before any marking, probing, staking, or excavation-related activity.
  4. Perform the Chapter 1 site-readiness inspection. Complete the full site-readiness worksheet for this specific capstone site and issue a readiness decision before proceeding.
  5. Prepare the Chapter 2 work area, access, washout, weather protection, and delivery plan. Complete the full work-area readiness checklist for this capstone site.
  6. Establish the baseline, corners, offsets, excavation limits, and finished-elevation references. Use the single elevation reference selected in Chapter 1.
  7. Square and document the layout. Verify diagonals and side lengths meet the approved squareness tolerance and record the layout checkpoint.
  8. Excavate to the approved depth. Excavate in controlled passes, maintaining required separation from foundations, utilities, trees, and property lines.
  9. Remove topsoil, roots, organic material, soft spots, and unsuitable fill. Remove unsuitable material to the approved limit and document the correction.
  10. Shape and compact subgrade. Compact using equipment appropriate to soil type and access, working from stable edges.
  11. Verify subgrade elevation and slope. Check elevation and slope at a grid of locations and complete the excavation checkpoint inspection before base placement.
  12. Install aggregate base in approved lifts. Spread and compact each lift to the specified thickness.
  13. Compact and verify base thickness, elevation, and drainage. Check the final base elevation at a grid of locations and complete the base checkpoint inspection before forms are accepted.
  14. Build, stake, brace, and square forms. Cut, set, stake, and brace the complete form system per the approved dimensions and stripping plan.
  15. Set form tops to the approved finished elevation and slope. Transfer elevations to form stakes and verify cross slope and longitudinal slope.
  16. Install isolation and expansion material at buildings and fixed structures. Secure material so it does not float, fold, or shift during placement.
  17. Install reinforcement, chairs, embeds, sleeves, and blockouts. Place reinforcement at the approved orientation, lap, and support spacing.
  18. Verify concrete cover and joint conflicts. Check reinforcement elevation and cover at multiple points and confirm no interference with joints, drains, or screeding.
  19. Complete required pre-pour inspection. Photograph concealed reinforcement and obtain the pre-pour checkpoint inspection before concrete arrives.
  20. Confirm crew, tools, concrete quantity, delivery, weather controls, finishing tools, curing materials, and backup plan. Verify every item is on site and ready before placement begins.
  21. Receive or mix concrete within approved consistency and timing. Confirm actual water use, batch time, and consistency against the approved specification.
  22. Place concrete close to final position. Spread concrete using tools designed for concrete, avoiding walking directly on reinforcement.
  23. Consolidate edges and around embedded items. Consolidate along forms, corners, blockouts, and embedded items without over-vibrating.
  24. Screed to form elevations. Draw the screed across form tops using the approved sawing motion, maintaining continuous bearing.
  25. Fill low spots and rescreed. Fill low areas with fresh concrete from the same mix and rescreed corrected areas.
  26. Bull float the surface. Bull-float promptly after screeding to embed aggregate and close the surface without overworking it.
  27. Monitor bleed water and setting. Do not begin final finishing while bleed water remains on the surface.
  28. Edge the slab. Run the edger along forms at the proper surface firmness, maintaining consistent edge radius and depth.
  29. Install tooled joints where specified. Tool joints to the specified depth and width at the approved locations.
  30. Apply the approved walking-surface or patio finish. Complete the specified broom, float, or other approved texture.
  31. Protect the finish from rain, wind, debris, footprints, and traffic. Barricade the section immediately after finishing.
  32. Begin curing as soon as the finish permits. Select and apply the approved curing method without delay.
  33. Maintain curing and weather protection through the required period. Inspect curing protection at scheduled intervals and repair displaced or ineffective materials.
  34. Install saw-cut control joints within the approved time window where specified. Wet-cut or use local extraction per the silica-control plan, maintaining specified depth.
  35. Strip forms only after the approved condition. Confirm the approved strength or time condition has been met before stripping.
  36. Backfill form edges with suitable material after concrete and edge conditions permit. Backfill without disturbing the finished edge or drainage.
  37. Inspect dimensions, elevation, slope, drainage, joints, surface texture, edge condition, cracking, and adjacent-property protection. Complete the final acceptance checkpoint inspection.
  38. Perform limited water-flow observation only when permitted by the cure and finish plan. Confirm drainage direction and discharge without disturbing the cured surface prematurely.
  39. Correct accepted minor deficiencies using approved methods. Use only methods and materials approved for the identified defect.
  40. Apply sealer only after the specified cure, dryness, surface preparation, and weather conditions. Follow the manufacturer's specified coverage and recoat window.
  41. Protect the sealed or cured surface through the required traffic restriction. Maintain barricades through the full restriction period.
  42. Prepare a final punch list. List every minor deficiency identified at final inspection.
  43. Correct and reinspect every item. Close every punch-list item with a documented correction and reinspection.
  44. Compile utility records, layout measurements, excavation notes, compaction records, form checks, reinforcement photographs, concrete tickets or batch logs, weather, finish times, curing, joints, sealer, inspections, and corrections. Assemble the complete closeout record.
  45. Provide traffic, loading, cleaning, deicing, sealing, and maintenance instructions. Document instructions consistent with the approved specification and sealer manufacturer's instructions.
  46. Hand off the completed flatwork section for Book 17 masonry and future hardscape or landscape coordination. Deliver the closeout record with the handoff.

Common Site-Readiness Inspection

Before closing out the capstone, confirm every checkpoint independently, in sequence, regardless of how confident the crew is in the overall result.

  • Layout check: confirm the squared layout and elevation references were verified before excavation began.
  • Excavation and base check: confirm subgrade and base elevation, slope, and compaction were verified before forms were accepted.
  • Form and reinforcement check: confirm form stability and reinforcement position and cover were verified before concrete concealed them.
  • Pre-pour check: confirm crew, tools, concrete, weather, and curing materials were confirmed ready before placement began.
  • Placement and finish check: confirm screeding, floating, edging, jointing, and texture were completed at the correct surface condition.
  • Cure and joint check: confirm curing began promptly and was maintained continuously, and that saw-cut joints were installed within the approved time window.
  • Final acceptance check: confirm every dimension, elevation, drainage, joint, and texture item passed, and that the punch list is fully closed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Checkpoint Skipped Under Schedule Pressure

Probable cause: a checkpoint inspection — most often the base, form, or reinforcement checkpoint — was skipped or performed informally to keep the placement schedule on track.
Confirmation: review the checkpoint inspection log for a missing date, signature, or result at the suspected stage.
Correction: if concrete has not yet been placed, stop and perform the missed checkpoint before proceeding; if concrete has already concealed the stage, document the gap, evaluate the visible evidence available, and involve a qualified professional if the condition cannot be confirmed.
Reinspection: repeat every checkpoint from the missed stage forward once the gap is resolved.

Elevation Reference Inconsistent With Chapter 1 or Chapter 7

Probable cause: the capstone crew re-established a new benchmark instead of using the single elevation reference carried forward from Chapter 1.
Confirmation: compare capstone layout and form elevations against the original Chapter 1 and Chapter 7 records for the same reference point.
Correction: re-measure all capstone elevations from the original reference point before forms are finalized.
Reinspection: repeat the layout and form checkpoint inspections using the corrected reference.

Concrete Placed Before Pre-Pour Inspection Completed

Probable cause: concrete delivery arrived before the reinforcement and pre-pour checkpoint was completed and documented.
Confirmation: review the pre-pour inspection record's timestamp against the concrete delivery ticket or batch time.
Correction: if placement has not begun, hold the delivery and complete the pre-pour inspection first; if placement has already begun, document the deviation, inspect the section for reinforcement position and cover using any available evidence, and refer to a qualified professional if position or cover cannot be confirmed.
Reinspection: repeat the pre-pour checkpoint on any future stage of this project before further concrete is placed.

Curing Delayed Past the Point the Finish Permits

Probable cause: curing materials were not staged and ready, so curing began later than the finish condition allowed.
Confirmation: compare the recorded finish-completion time against the recorded curing-start time in the closeout log.
Correction: begin curing immediately once the gap is identified, and document the delay and any visible early-drying condition on the surface.
Reinspection: inspect the surface for crazing, dusting, or scaling once curing is complete, and note any condition on the final acceptance checklist.

Saw-Cut Joint Installed Outside the Approved Time Window

Probable cause: the saw-cutting crew was scheduled without confirming the approved timing window against actual concrete set, resulting in cutting too early or too late.
Confirmation: compare the recorded cutting time against the concrete placement time and the approved timing window for the specified concrete and weather conditions.
Correction: document any raveling from early cutting or random cracking from late cutting, and correct only using methods approved for the observed condition.
Reinspection: inspect joint depth, straightness, and edge condition once cutting is complete, and record the finding on the joint checkpoint.

Final Handoff Issued With Open Punch-List Items

Probable cause: the section was handed off for Book 17 coordination while one or more punch-list items remained uncorrected or unreinspected.
Confirmation: review the punch list against the handoff record for any item without a documented correction and reinspection.
Correction: withdraw the handoff, correct and reinspect every remaining item, and reissue the handoff only once the punch list is fully closed.
Reinspection: repeat the final acceptance checkpoint before reissuing the handoff.

Cleanup

This capstone ends with a complete, filed closeout record, not a verbal impression of a finished slab.

  • Remove barricades, cones, and signage only after the final traffic-restriction period has passed
  • Clean and store all layout, excavation, forming, reinforcement, finishing, curing, jointing, and sealing tools
  • Confirm the washout area has been closed out and waste handled per the approved plan
  • File the complete closeout record, checkpoint inspection log, punch list, and maintenance instructions together
  • Store all photographs with clear labels referencing the closeout record entries they support
  • Confirm the handoff record has been signed and delivered before considering the project complete

This chapter required you to integrate layout, excavation, and subgrade preparation (FW-115), gravel base, forms, and slope control (FW-116), reinforcement and concrete placement (FW-117), finishing, curing, and jointing (FW-118), final inspection across every checkpoint (FW-119), and complete closeout documentation and handoff (FW-120).

The closeout record you compiled here — utility, layout, excavation, compaction, form, reinforcement, concrete, weather, finish, curing, joint, sealer, and inspection documentation — is the reference every later book will build from, in the same way Chapter 1's site-readiness worksheet was the reference every chapter in this book built from.
