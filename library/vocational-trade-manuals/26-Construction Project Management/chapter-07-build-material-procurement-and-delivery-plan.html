Job Overview

This project qualifies you to create and operate a residential material procurement and delivery plan — the controlling record that moves every material item from approved selection through submittal, purchase order, fabrication, shipping, delivery, receiving, storage, and installation readiness, ending in closeout documentation. The finished work product is one live procurement register with backward-calculated release dates, approved purchase orders, tracked supplier commitments, a coordinated delivery and receiving system, and material closeout records.

You will build the procurement register from the approved estimate and takeoffs, identify long-lead and owner-selection items, calculate submittal and release dates backward from required-on-site dates, prepare and issue purchase orders through authorized approval, track supplier acknowledgment and fabrication status, coordinate delivery logistics and storage, receive and inspect material against a receiving checklist, resolve shortages and damage, and reconcile procurement records for project closeout.

This project depends directly on the baseline schedule built in Project 05 and the two-week lookahead maintained in Project 06: procurement release dates are calculated backward from the required-on-site dates those documents establish. It also feeds forward into the trade coordination and handoff work in Project 08, the change and allowance tracking in Project 11, and the closeout package assembled in Project 18.

Where This Skill Fits

The procurement register built here is one of the live systems that keeps the baseline schedule from Project 05 executable. A schedule that identifies an installation date without a procurement plan behind it is not a working schedule; a release date calculated without a required-on-site date behind it is not a working release date. The backward-calculation method taught in this project — installation date, minus unloading, minus delivery, minus fabrication, minus approval, minus preparation — is the same method used later whenever a schedule-critical item threatens to become late.

This project also supplies the material readiness confirmation that the two-week lookahead in Project 06 depends on, and the receiving and storage discipline it establishes protects the trade handoffs coordinated in Project 08. The closeout records this project produces — warranties, certifications, test reports, and reconciled quantities — carry forward directly into the Project 18 closeout package.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • building a procurement register from the approved estimate, takeoffs, specifications, and baseline schedule
  • identifying custom, engineered, fabricated, imported, long-lead, owner-selected, and allowance items
  • calculating submittal, approval, release, and purchase-order dates backward from required-on-site dates
  • preparing, quoting, and issuing purchase orders through authorized approval
  • tracking supplier acknowledgment, fabrication status, shipping, and expected arrival
  • coordinating delivery route, access, sequence, and storage requirements
  • receiving and inspecting material against a documented checklist, and resolving shortages, damage, and rejections
  • protecting stored material and tracking it for payment documentation
  • updating the procurement log at every schedule update and coordination meeting, and escalating forecast late items
  • collecting warranties, certifications, and closeout records, and reconciling final quantities

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • release material from a preliminary, unconfirmed, or superseded selection, drawing, or dimension
  • issue a purchase order or commit project funds without the authority identified in Step 16 of the procedure below
  • accept damaged, incorrect, incomplete, unapproved, or undocumented material merely to protect the schedule
  • install material before required inspection, acclimation, testing, or approval is complete

Any conflict between the estimate quantities, the current approved drawings, and the owner's selections must be identified and resolved in writing before a purchase order is issued.

Trade Note

Every release date in this chapter is calculated backward from a required-on-site date on the baseline schedule — never estimated informally from a general sense of how long an item "usually takes." Releasing material from an outdated selection or an unconfirmed dimension, rather than the current approved documents, is the single most common source of the procurement rework diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not issue a purchase order (Step 16) until the required-on-site date, backward calculation, and current confirmed selection for that item are complete (Steps 6–10). A purchase order issued before these steps are complete must be reissued from confirmed information before fabrication begins.

Shop Tip

Calculate backward from the required-on-site date, not the installation start date shown on a general schedule bar. Add the unloading and staging time and the inspection or acclimation period the material needs before the crew can actually use it.

Long-Lead Identification and Backward Calculation

Every item on the procurement register must be screened for long-lead risk before it is treated as routine. Screen for custom fabrication, engineering review, import shipping, limited domestic availability, and owner-selection dependency. Calculate the release date backward from the required-on-site date by subtracting, in order, unloading and staging time, shipping and delivery duration, fabrication or production time, approval and resubmission time, and the time required to confirm any unresolved dimension, color, or finish. An item screened as routine that later proves to require engineering review becomes a schedule-controlling item with no backward calculation behind it.

Documentation Cue — Track Submittal and Purchase Status Separately

Submittal approval status and purchase-order status are two different conditions and must be tracked as two different fields in the procurement register. An item can be purchased before its submittal is approved only when the purchase order explicitly conditions fabrication on that approval; do not record an item as "on order" in a way that implies fabrication has begun if approval is still pending.

Critical Procurement Cue — Do Not Release From Unconfirmed Information

Do not release material from preliminary, unconfirmed, or superseded drawings, dimensions, colors, finishes, hardware, or equipment specifications. Confirm the current approved document reference for every purchase order before it is issued. Releasing from unconfirmed information is a leading cause of wrong-product delivery, fabrication rework, and schedule-controlling delay.

Field Coordination Cue — Confirm Delivery Access Before Scheduling a Delivery

Confirm delivery route, gate, street restrictions, neighbor notification, crane or forklift need, and unloading responsibility before a delivery date is scheduled with the supplier. A delivery that arrives without confirmed access creates unloading delay, blocked streets, damaged material, or a rejected delivery attempt.

Supplier Instruction Cue — Confirm Quotation Terms Before Purchase-Order Issuance

Confirm quotation validity period, lead time, freight terms, tax treatment, deposit requirement, minimum order quantity, return policy, and price-escalation terms before issuing the purchase order. Do not assume a verbal quotation carries the same terms as the supplier's standard written terms.

E. Preparation — Build the Register and Screen for Long-Lead Items

  1. Collect the approved estimate, material takeoffs, specifications, schedules, selections, submittal requirements, and baseline schedule. Assemble every available document before the register is built.
  2. Create a procurement register by WBS, cost code, trade, material, supplier, and installation area. Populate the identification fields for every item on the estimate.
  3. Identify custom, engineered, fabricated, imported, volatile, limited-availability, and long-lead items. Flag each item's risk classification in the register.
  4. Identify owner-selected, allowance, alternate, owner-furnished, and utility-furnished items. Record the responsible selecting or furnishing party for each.
  5. Identify products requiring samples, mockups, shop drawings, engineering, calculations, product approvals, or authority review. Record the submittal requirement for each flagged item.

F. Backward Calculation and Purchase-Order Preparation

  1. Define the required-on-site date from the schedule. Confirm this date with the current baseline schedule from Project 05, not an informal estimate.
  2. Calculate backward through unloading, delivery, shipping, fabrication, approval, resubmission, preparation, and selection durations. Record each duration and the resulting release date.
  3. Establish submittal, approval, release, and purchase-order dates. Enter each date in the register's schedule-dates field group.
  4. Identify dates by which unresolved dimensions, colors, finishes, hardware, equipment, and openings must be confirmed. Record the confirmation deadline and its owner.
  5. Assign responsibility for submittal preparation, technical review, owner selection, design approval, and purchasing. Name one responsible party for each task.
  6. Prepare purchase scopes that match the approved drawings, specifications, quantity, accessories, delivery, and warranty requirements. Write the scope so it cannot be misread by the supplier.
  7. Check purchase quantities against the Book 24 takeoff and current approved changes. Confirm the quantity reflects current approved scope.
  8. Confirm model, size, grade, treatment, color, finish, voltage, handedness, performance, compatibility, and approved substitution status. Verify each characteristic against the current approved documents.
  9. Do not release material from preliminary or superseded information. Confirm the document reference is current before proceeding.
  10. Obtain supplier quotations with validity, lead time, freight, tax, deposit, minimum order, return, and escalation terms. Record the complete terms in the register.

G. Purchase-Order Issuance and Supplier Tracking

  1. Issue purchase orders through authorized approval. Confirm the approval is from the person or role identified in the register header.
  2. Record purchase-order number, amount, terms, release date, promised date, and responsible buyer. Enter this information in the register at the time of issuance.
  3. Obtain supplier acknowledgment and compare it with the purchase order. Confirm the acknowledgment matches the issued scope, quantity, and terms.
  4. Resolve differences in quantity, product, delivery, price, exclusions, and terms before fabrication. Do not allow fabrication to begin on an unresolved discrepancy.
  5. Track submittal and approval status separately from purchase status. Update both fields independently as each changes.
  6. Track fabrication start, production status, shipping, carrier, tracking, and expected arrival. Update the register at each status change.

H. Delivery Coordination, Receiving, and Storage

  1. Coordinate delivery route, gate, street restrictions, neighbors, permits, traffic, crane, forklift, unloading, and weather. Confirm access before scheduling the delivery date.
  2. Coordinate delivery sequence with site storage and installation sequence. Confirm storage space and installation readiness before the delivery date is confirmed with the supplier.
  3. Identify protected, conditioned, secure, elevated, dry, ventilated, or hazardous-material storage requirements. Assign a storage location that meets the item's requirement.
  4. Prepare a receiving checklist for quantity, model, damage, packaging, documentation, and approval status. Complete the checklist at the time of every delivery.
  5. Assign a person authorized to receive and inspect each delivery. Confirm this person is present or has delegated the task before the delivery arrives.
  6. Photograph major deliveries and record discrepancies at receipt. Photograph before the delivery truck departs whenever a discrepancy is found.
  7. Segregate damaged, incorrect, incomplete, or unapproved materials. Physically separate rejected material from accepted material.
  8. Issue shortage, damage, rejection, replacement, freight-claim, or return records promptly. Do not delay documentation past the same working day.
  9. Do not install material before required inspection, acclimation, testing, or approval. Confirm the hold condition is satisfied before releasing material to the installing trade.
  10. Link received material to inventory and installation area. Update the register to show the material as received and staged.
  11. Protect stored material from weather, theft, contamination, impact, and trade damage. Verify the storage location continues to meet the item's requirement.

I. Tracking, Escalation, and Closeout Reconciliation

  1. Track stored material for payment documentation where applicable. Record stored-material value for the job-cost and payment-application systems.
  2. Update procurement status during every schedule update and coordination meeting. Refresh the register at the same interval as the schedule and lookahead updates.
  3. Escalate forecast late items with impact, alternatives, and decision deadline. Enter the escalation in the constraint log established in Project 06.
  4. Collect warranties, certifications, test reports, care instructions, and spare material for closeout. Record each item as it becomes available rather than reconstructing it later.
  5. Reconcile final ordered, received, installed, returned, and retained quantities. Confirm the reconciled totals for every line item before closing it.
  6. Close purchase records only after delivery, credits, documentation, and warranty obligations are confirmed. Mark the register status as closed only when every condition is satisfied.

Common Procurement Register Inspection

Inspect every procurement register using the same checks regardless of project size or complexity.

  • Completeness check: confirm every estimate line item and takeoff quantity appears in the register individually.
  • Long-lead check: confirm every custom, engineered, imported, and owner-selected item is flagged with a risk classification and a backward-calculated date.
  • Currency check: confirm every purchase order references the current approved drawings, specifications, and selections, not a superseded version.
  • Status check: confirm submittal status and purchase status are tracked as separate fields for every item.
  • Receiving check: confirm every delivery has a completed receiving checklist with quantity, model, and damage recorded at the time of receipt.
  • Reconciliation check: confirm ordered, received, installed, returned, and retained quantities are reconciled before an item is closed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Long-Lead Item Is Discovered Late and Threatens the Schedule

Probable cause: the long-lead screening in Step 3 was not completed for every estimate line item, or an item was screened as routine before its fabrication or engineering requirement was known.
Confirmation: check the register for the item's risk classification and compare the required-on-site date against the actual lead time now quoted by the supplier.
Correction: escalate the item through the constraint log immediately, identify recovery alternatives, and set a decision deadline with the responsible parties.
Reinspection: repeat the long-lead screening across the full register, not only the affected item.

Material Arrives That Does Not Match the Current Approved Selection

Probable cause: the purchase order in Step 12 was issued from a preliminary or superseded selection, or a selection change was approved after the purchase order was already issued and the register was not updated.
Confirmation: compare the purchase order's document reference against the current approved selection record and the register's currency-check field.
Correction: segregate the incorrect material, issue a supplier correction or return record, and reissue the purchase order from the current approved selection.
Reinspection: repeat the currency check on every open purchase order for that trade.

A Delivery Is Rejected or Delayed at the Gate

Probable cause: delivery access in Step 22 was not confirmed before the delivery date was scheduled with the supplier, or a required permit, street restriction, or unloading resource was not arranged in advance.
Confirmation: check the register's delivery and storage field group for a confirmed access record and compare it against the site logistics plan.
Correction: reschedule the delivery only after access, permits, and unloading resources are confirmed, and document the delay's cause and cost.
Reinspection: repeat the access confirmation for every remaining delivery scheduled for that area.

A Discrepancy Is Not Discovered Until After the Delivery Truck Has Left

Probable cause: the receiving checklist in Step 26 was not completed at the time of delivery, or the person assigned in Step 27 was not present or was not authorized to inspect the delivery.
Confirmation: check the register's receiving record for a completed checklist and photographs dated to the delivery.
Correction: document the discrepancy as discovered, notify the supplier promptly, and issue a shortage, damage, or rejection record even though the truck has departed.
Reinspection: confirm a receiving-responsibility assignment is in place for every subsequent delivery before it is scheduled.

Stored Material Is Damaged Before Installation

Probable cause: the storage requirement identified in Step 24 was not matched to an actual available location, or the protection condition was not verified after initial storage.
Confirmation: check the register's storage-requirement field against the actual storage location and inspect the material's current condition.
Correction: relocate or re-protect the material to meet its documented requirement, and record the damage, cause, and corrective action.
Reinspection: repeat the storage-protection check for all material sharing that storage location.

Final Quantities Will Not Reconcile at Closeout

Probable cause: received, installed, returned, or retained quantities were not updated in the register as each event occurred, so the reconciliation in Step 38 has no current record to compare against the purchase order.
Confirmation: check the register's history for the item and identify the point at which updates stopped or were skipped.
Correction: reconstruct the missing quantities from receiving checklists, field verification, and supplier records, and update the register before closing the item.
Reinspection: repeat the reconciliation check for every item closed in the same period.

Cleanup

This project ends with a live, current procurement register, not a one-time document left unmaintained after the initial build.

  • Confirm every open purchase order is current against approved drawings, specifications, and selections
  • Confirm the constraint log reflects every unresolved long-lead or late-delivery escalation with an owner and due date
  • Confirm every completed delivery has a filed receiving checklist and any related damage or shortage records
  • Confirm stored material is protected and its location is recorded in the register
  • Provide the procurement register, purchase orders, and receiving records to Project 08 for use in trade-handoff coordination and to Project 15 for job-cost tracking
