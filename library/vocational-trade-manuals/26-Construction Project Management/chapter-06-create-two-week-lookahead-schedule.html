Job Overview

This project qualifies you to convert the approved baseline schedule from Project 05 into one executable two-week lookahead — the working document field supervision and trades use to commit to specific tasks in a specific area on a specific day. The finished work product is one published, constraint-checked two-week lookahead grid supported by a current constraint log, confirmed labor and material readiness, coordinated access, and scheduled inspections.

You will update the master schedule with actual progress, extract activities planned within the window, break broad schedule activities into daily field tasks, confirm predecessor completion, documents, labor, material, equipment, safety, quality, and inspection readiness for each task, enter unresolved items in a constraint log with an owner and due date, coordinate trade work zones and deliveries, obtain explicit commitments from trade leads, and publish the lookahead to the project team.

This project depends directly on the baseline schedule approved in Project 05 and the WBS built in Project 03. It also draws on the procurement plan built in Project 07 and feeds the coordination meeting run in Project 09, the trade handoff process in Project 08, and the constraint tracking used throughout the rest of the book. A lookahead is not a wish list: a task does not belong on this document until its readiness has actually been checked.

Where This Skill Fits

The baseline schedule approved in Project 05 tells the project what should happen over the life of the job. The lookahead built here tells the field what will actually happen in the next two weeks, and only after every readiness condition has been checked. Every later coordination activity in this book — the procurement release dates in Project 07, the trade handoffs in Project 08, the coordination-meeting agenda in Project 09, and the punch-list sequencing in Project 14 — depends on a lookahead that has not been allowed to drift into an unchecked wish list.

The discipline of confirming readiness before a task appears on the lookahead, taught in this chapter, is the same discipline used later to confirm readiness before a trade is released into an area in Project 08 and before an inspection is requested in Project 16.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • updating the master schedule with actual starts, actual finishes, progress, and remaining duration before building the lookahead
  • extracting and breaking down activities planned to occur within the two-week or approved short-interval window
  • confirming predecessor completion, current documents, labor, material, equipment, access, safety, and quality readiness for each task
  • confirming inspection requests, inspector availability, and required hold points
  • entering unresolved items in a constraint log with one owner and one due date
  • coordinating trade work zones, deliveries, and daily inspections to avoid overcrowding and damage
  • obtaining explicit trade commitments and publishing the lookahead to affected parties
  • tracking daily completion, missed commitments, and reasons to improve the next lookahead

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • place a task on the lookahead before its drawings, decisions, labor, material, equipment, access, predecessor work, safety controls, and inspection requirements have actually been checked
  • rebaseline or overwrite the approved master schedule from Project 05; the lookahead reports against that baseline, it does not replace it
  • commit a subcontractor, supplier, or inspector to a date without confirming that commitment directly with the responsible party
  • remove a required safety control, quality hold point, cure period, or inspection to make the lookahead appear achievable

Any conflict between the master schedule, the field's actual progress, and reported readiness must be identified and resolved in the constraint log before a task is published on the lookahead.

Trade Note

A lookahead is not a copy of the master schedule for the next two weeks. It is a shorter, checked, and more detailed commitment document. A task that appears on the master schedule but has not passed the readiness checks in this chapter does not belong on the lookahead — it belongs in the constraint log until it is ready.

Setup Standard

Do not begin extracting lookahead tasks (Step 3) until the master schedule has been updated with actual progress (Step 2). A lookahead built from a stale master schedule will misstate predecessor completion and must be rebuilt before it is published.

Shop Tip

Write every lookahead task as a measurable commitment tied to an area. "Rough-in plumbing, second-floor bathroom, through DWV testing" is a task. "Plumbing work" is not, because it does not say what area, what scope, or what finish condition marks it complete.

Committed Tasks, Conditional Tasks, and Removed Tasks

Every lookahead must distinguish a task that is genuinely ready from one that remains dependent on an unresolved constraint. Write conditional tasks clearly labeled with the constraint they depend on so a reader cannot mistake a conditional task for a committed one. A task placed on the lookahead without a completed readiness check becomes a missed commitment once the crew arrives and finds the area, material, or information is not actually ready.

Documentation Cue — Record the Reporting Date, Window, and Revision

Every published lookahead must carry a reporting date, the exact window it covers, and a revision number. Do not distribute an undated lookahead or one without a clear window as though it were the current field document. Recording only the current cycle, without a revision history, allows a superseded lookahead to be mistaken for the controlling field document later in the week.

Critical Readiness Cue — Do Not Publish an Unchecked Task

Do not place a task on the lookahead until its drawings, decisions, labor, material, equipment, access, predecessor work, safety controls, and inspection requirements have actually been checked. Remove tasks that are not genuinely ready unless they remain clearly labeled as conditional. Treating the master schedule as automatically executable is a leading cause of missed field commitments.

Field Coordination Cue — Coordinate Trade Work Zones Before Publishing

Coordinate trade work zones, laydown, and shared equipment before the lookahead is published, not after a conflict appears in the field. Avoid trade stacking that creates unsafe congestion, damaged work, or unclear responsibility. A lookahead that schedules two trades into the same limited area without a defined sequence is not a coordinated document.

Contract Instruction Cue — Follow the Executed Agreement's Notice Requirements

Where the executed agreement specifies a particular method or timeframe for schedule notices, delay notices, or access requests, follow that method exactly when the lookahead identifies a schedule change or an access requirement. Do not substitute an informal communication channel for the notice method the contract requires.

E. Preparation — Update the Schedule and Extract the Window

  1. Select the current reporting date and define the two-week or approved short-interval window. Record the exact start and end date the lookahead will cover.
  2. Update the master schedule with actual starts, actual finishes, progress, and remaining duration. Do not extract activities from a schedule that has not been refreshed with current field status.
  3. Extract all activities planned to start, continue, finish, or constrain work during the lookahead window. Pull directly from the updated master schedule.
  4. Include activities just outside the window that require immediate procurement, decisions, or preparation. Record these separately so they are not mistaken for committed near-term tasks.
  5. Break broad schedule activities into daily or area-specific field tasks. Convert each master-schedule activity into one or more measurable daily tasks.
  6. Define the measurable commitment for each task. State the area, scope, and finish condition that marks the task complete.

F. Confirm Readiness for Each Task

  1. Confirm predecessor work is complete or forecast to be complete. Verify against the updated master schedule and field observation.
  2. Confirm current approved drawings, specifications, RFIs, submittals, and selections are available. Check the document register for the applicable task.
  3. Confirm permit, inspection, testing, and hold-point requirements. Check the inspection register and quality plan.
  4. Confirm the responsible trade and committed crew size. Contact the trade lead directly rather than assuming availability.
  5. Confirm required supervision, competent persons, operators, and specialty qualifications. Verify against the safety plan and trade documentation.
  6. Confirm material has been approved, ordered, shipped, received, inspected, and staged as required. Check the procurement log for the current status.
  7. Confirm tools, equipment, lifts, scaffolds, temporary power, water, lighting, and access. Verify availability with the responsible party for shared equipment.
  8. Confirm work area, laydown, parking, delivery, waste, and housekeeping conditions. Check the jobsite logistics plan from the safety and housekeeping plan.
  9. Confirm safety plan, hazard analysis, permits, orientation, and protective measures. Verify the applicable controls are active for the task.
  10. Confirm quality criteria, mockups, checklists, samples, and first-work inspection requirements. Check the quality plan for the applicable acceptance criteria.
  11. Confirm inspection requests and inspector availability. Contact the authority or inspecting party directly for the applicable window.
  12. Confirm owner access, occupancy, shutdown, noise, dust, utility, and security coordination. Verify against the communication plan for occupied-home conditions.

G. Log Constraints and Coordinate the Window

  1. Enter each unresolved item in the constraint log. Record every readiness check from Section F that did not pass.
  2. Assign one owner and due date to each constraint. Do not record a constraint without a specific responsible party and date.
  3. Identify the date after which an unresolved constraint will affect the task. State the exact impact date the constraint must be resolved by.
  4. Remove tasks that are not genuinely ready unless they remain clearly labeled as conditional. Do not publish an unready task as though it were committed.
  5. Identify alternative executable work for foreseeable constraints. Record a backup task for crews affected by a known constraint.
  6. Coordinate trade work zones to avoid overcrowding and damage. Confirm the sequence and boundaries for each shared area.
  7. Coordinate deliveries by date, time, route, unloading, and storage. Confirm delivery windows against site access and laydown capacity.
  8. Coordinate daily inspections, tests, and quality hold points. Schedule inspections against confirmed task readiness, not assumed completion.
  9. Add weather contingencies and protection activities. Identify tasks affected by forecast weather and their protection requirements.

H. Commit, Publish, and Track

  1. Prepare a daily grid showing task, area, trade, crew, material, equipment, predecessor, inspection, and constraint. Assemble the confirmed information from Sections E through G.
  2. Compare the lookahead with the baseline and explain deviations. State the reason for any task moved earlier, later, or off the current window.
  3. Review commitments with field supervision and trade leads. Walk the grid with the responsible parties before publication.
  4. Obtain explicit commitments rather than assuming attendance. Record a direct confirmation from each trade lead for each committed task.
  5. Publish the lookahead to owners, trades, suppliers, inspectors, and other affected parties according to communication rules. Distribute through the defined channel from the project communication plan.
  6. Post or provide the current field version. Confirm the field crew has access to the published version, not a prior draft.
  7. Track daily completion, missed commitments, reasons, and recovered work. Record actual results against each committed task.
  8. Use completion reliability and constraint causes to improve the next lookahead. Identify recurring constraint causes before building the next cycle.
  9. Archive the issued lookahead and preserve revisions. Move the superseded cycle to the archive location before publishing the next one.

Common Lookahead Inspection

Inspect every published lookahead using the same checks regardless of project size or complexity.

  • Currency check: confirm the master schedule was updated with actual progress before the lookahead was extracted.
  • Task check: confirm every daily task states a measurable commitment tied to a specific area and finish condition.
  • Readiness check: confirm predecessor, documents, labor, material, equipment, access, safety, and inspection readiness was checked for every committed task.
  • Constraint check: confirm every unresolved item is logged with one owner, one due date, and an identified impact date.
  • Coordination check: confirm trade zones, deliveries, and shared equipment were coordinated to avoid conflict or unsafe stacking.
  • Commitment check: confirm an explicit trade-lead commitment, not an assumption, was recorded for every committed task.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Crew Arrives to Find the Area or Material Not Ready

Probable cause: the task was published without completing the readiness confirmation in Section F, or a constraint identified during confirmation was not removed from the committed grid.
Confirmation: check the readiness confirmation record for the task and compare it against the constraint log for the same item.
Correction: move the task to conditional status, log the constraint with an owner and due date, and identify alternative executable work for the affected crew.
Reinspection: repeat the readiness check on the affected task before it is republished as committed.

Two Trades Are Scheduled Into the Same Area on the Same Day

Probable cause: the coordination step in Section G was not completed for that area, or the trade work zones were not confirmed with both trade leads before publication.
Confirmation: check the coordination record for the area in question and confirm whether a sequence or boundary was defined.
Correction: revise the daily grid to sequence the trades or divide the area into defined zones, and confirm the revision with both trade leads.
Reinspection: repeat the coordination check across the full lookahead window, not only the conflicting item.

A Trade Does Not Show Up for a Committed Task

Probable cause: the commitment recorded in Step 31 was an assumed attendance rather than an explicit confirmation obtained directly from the trade lead.
Confirmation: check the commitment record for the task and confirm whether a direct confirmation exists with a name and date.
Correction: contact the trade lead, document the reason, update the constraint log if a new constraint caused the miss, and record the result in the completion tracking.
Reinspection: confirm the commitment process for the next cycle requires a direct confirmation from every trade lead before publication.

An Inspection Is Requested but the Inspector Is Not Available

Probable cause: inspector availability was not confirmed in Step 17, or the inspection request lead time from the Project 16 inspection plan was not observed.
Confirmation: check the inspection request record against the required lead time and confirm whether availability was verified before the task was committed.
Correction: reschedule the inspection through the approved channel, log the delay as a constraint with an impact date, and identify whether dependent tasks must be moved.
Reinspection: repeat the inspection-readiness check for the rescheduled item and confirm the constraint log is updated.

The Lookahead Repeatedly Shows Low Completion Reliability

Probable cause: constraints are being identified but not resolved by their due dates, or tasks are being placed on the lookahead without a completed readiness check in Section F.
Confirmation: review the completion-tracking record across the last several cycles and identify the most frequent constraint cause and readiness-check failure.
Correction: escalate recurring overdue constraints through the approved notice process and tighten the readiness confirmation before the next task list is committed.
Reinspection: track completion reliability for the next two cycles and confirm improvement against the identified cause.

The Lookahead Conflicts With the Approved Baseline Schedule

Probable cause: the master schedule was not updated with actual progress in Step 2 before extraction, or a deviation from Step 29 was not documented.
Confirmation: compare the published lookahead against the current master schedule and check whether every deviation has a recorded explanation.
Correction: update the master schedule with current actual progress, document the deviation, and republish the lookahead once the comparison is current.
Reinspection: repeat the currency check before the next lookahead cycle is extracted.

Field Staff Are Working From a Superseded Lookahead

Probable cause: the prior cycle was not moved to the archive location in Step 36, or the current published version was not distributed to every party identified in the communication plan.
Confirmation: check the archive location for the reporting date and revision currently in active use, and confirm distribution records against the project team list.
Correction: remove the superseded lookahead from active use, redistribute the current published version, and confirm receipt from affected parties.
Reinspection: repeat the publication and distribution check for the current cycle.
