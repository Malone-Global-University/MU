Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install and secure one bathroom vanity cabinet and its compatible top around verified plumbing services. The finished work product is a level, plumb, structurally secured vanity cabinet with a supported top, sealed moisture-prone joints, and preserved access to sink clips, shutoffs, drains, and electrical devices, ready to hand off to the authorized plumbing trade for faucet, supply, drain, and trap connections.

You will review the vanity, top, sink, faucet, backsplash, plumbing, wall, floor, and accessibility requirements, verify plumbing rough-in locations, inspect the wall and floor, establish the vanity centerline and top elevation, cut protected service openings using the Chapter 7 method, position and secure the vanity, dry-fit and set the top, install and seal the backsplash, seal exposed edges against moisture, and inspect the completed installation before coordinating plumbing connections.

This project assumes the Chapter 1 readiness inspection has been completed for the bathroom area, that rough plumbing is installed and accessible, and that the vanity cabinet, top, and sink have been selected and confirmed compatible under the approved plan. It applies the base-cabinet leveling and fastening method from Project 04, the service-cutting method from Project 07, and the countertop and sink-opening methods from Projects 09 and 10 to a single freestanding or wall-adjacent vanity cabinet and top.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CB-061 — Position the vanity cabinet against verified layout references and plumbing locations.
  • CB-062 — Cut protected plumbing openings in the vanity cabinet using field-verified service locations.
  • CB-063 — Level and structurally secure the vanity cabinet to wall framing or approved backing.
  • CB-064 — Install the vanity top with verified support, overhang, and sink-position accuracy.
  • CB-065 — Seal backsplash, splash, and top-edge joints using the specified sanitary sealant and edge treatment.
  • CB-066 — Inspect the completed vanity installation for fastening, support, sealant, and service access before trade handoff.

Where This Skill Fits

This project applies the base-cabinet leveling and structural-fastening method taught in Project 04 to a vanity cabinet installed around plumbing rather than an open base-cabinet run, and it applies the service-opening method taught in Project 07 to a single cabinet back or side panel rather than a full cabinet run. It also applies the countertop-support and edge-sealing principles from Project 09 and the sink-opening method from Project 10 to a compatible vanity top.

The plumbing clearance, cabinet fastening, and moisture-sealing standard established here carries forward into the Chapter 20 capstone, where a vanity cabinet and top may be included in the complete installation, and into Chapter 19, where an improperly sealed or fastened vanity is one of the most common causes of traced water damage and loose-cabinet complaints.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing vanity dimensions, top, sink, faucet, backsplash, plumbing, wall, floor, and accessibility requirements
  • shutting off water and verifying plumbing rough-in locations
  • inspecting the wall and floor for level, plumb, moisture, and structural support
  • establishing the vanity centerline and top elevation
  • measuring and transferring supply, drain, valve, and outlet locations to the cabinet
  • cutting protected service openings using the Project 07 method
  • positioning the vanity and verifying plumbing clearance
  • shimming the vanity level and plumb at approved load points and aligning cabinet faces and side clearances
  • securing the vanity to studs or approved backing
  • installing fillers or finished panels where required
  • dry-fitting the vanity top and verifying wall fit, overhang, sink position, faucet clearance, and cabinet support
  • setting the top, installing backsplash or side splash, and sealing designated joints with compatible sanitary sealant
  • sealing exposed cabinet and countertop edges against moisture
  • inspecting cabinet fastening, top support, sealant, service access, and door or drawer operation before trade handoff

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • connect, disconnect, or modify faucet, supply, drain, trap, or electrical connections; these remain assigned to the authorized plumbing or electrical trade
  • relocate a plumbing or electrical rough-in independently of the approved plan and the assigned licensed trade
  • field-cut stone, quartz, or solid-surface vanity tops without the approved professional fabrication process
  • block sink clips, shutoffs, drain access, or electrical devices with fillers, panels, backsplash, or sealant
  • proceed with plumbing openings or top installation before the water is shut off and the rough-in locations are field verified

Any condition that does not match the approved plan, or that suggests a leaking supply, damaged drain, unsupported wall, or hazardous material, must stop work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

A vanity cabinet installed around plumbing carries a higher moisture-damage risk than an open base-cabinet run because supply lines, drains, and traps continue to move, condense, and occasionally leak after installation. Treating the sealant and edge-treatment steps in this project as optional finishing work, rather than as the barrier that protects the cabinet and top from that ongoing exposure, is the most common cause of vanity delamination and cabinet-bottom failure traced back to this project during the Chapter 19 repair project.

Setup Standard

Do not begin cutting a plumbing opening or dry-fitting the top until the water has been shut off and the rough-in locations have been field verified against the plumbing plan (Steps 2 and 5). Do not proceed with fastening or sealant application until the wall and floor inspection required in Step 3 confirms adequate support and no unresolved moisture condition.

Shop Tip

Establish the vanity centerline and top elevation before measuring a single plumbing location. Every supply, drain, and outlet transfer in this project is referenced from that centerline, and confirming it first prevents a repeat layout once the cabinet is positioned.

Common Field Fundamentals

Installing a vanity cabinet and top follows the same order as any other cabinet and countertop system in this book: verify references and services, position and secure the cabinet, fit and secure the top, seal every moisture-exposed joint, and inspect before handing off connections to another trade. What differs here is the concentration of plumbing services directly behind and beneath the cabinet, which requires the service-opening and clearance discipline taught in Project 07 applied to a single, tightly confined cabinet interior.

Separating This Project's Scope From the Plumbing Trade's Scope

This project authorizes positioning, cutting, fastening, and sealing the vanity cabinet and top around the plumbing services already roughed in. It does not authorize connecting, disconnecting, or altering the faucet, supply, drain, trap, or any electrical device. Where a rough-in location conflicts with the cabinet or top, or where a shutoff, drain, or device would be blocked by a filler, panel, or sealant bead, record the conflict and obtain a documented resolution from the responsible plumber, electrician, or designer before continuing.

Critical Safety Cue — Shut Off Water Before Cutting or Fastening Near Supply Lines

Shut off water to the affected supply lines before drilling, cutting, or fastening within reach of a supply, valve, or drain connection. Do not rely on a visual estimate of pipe location; confirm the shutoff is closed and the line is free of pressure before beginning any cutting step in this project.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Sink Centerline Against the Cabinet and Plumbing

Confirm the sink centerline against both the vanity cabinet centerline and the plumbing rough-in location before cutting any opening or drilling any hole. A centerline that is correct against the cabinet but not against the plumbing, or correct against the plumbing but not against the cabinet, produces a conflict that is far more difficult to correct after the top is set.

Field Safety Cue — Confined-Space Cutting Behind the Vanity

Cutting plumbing openings in a vanity cabinet back or side panel is confined, close-quarters work. Support the cutout waste from below or behind before completing a cut, keep hands clear of the cutting path, and use adequate lighting to confirm the exact pipe or conduit location before starting each cut.

Manufacturer Cue — Sanitary Sealant Cure Time

Follow the manufacturer's specified cure time for the sanitary sealant before exposing the joint to water, cleaning products, or normal use. Do not connect plumbing, use the sink, or apply water to a sealed joint before the specified cure time has elapsed.

Code Alert — Accessibility and Shutoff-Access Requirements Vary by Jurisdiction

Required clearances for accessible vanity height, knee space, and shutoff-valve access vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction and the general contractor before confirming that the vanity installation meets its intended accessibility or access requirement.

E. Document Review and Rough-In Verification (Steps 1–2)

  1. Review vanity dimensions, top, sink, faucet, backsplash, plumbing, wall, floor, and accessibility requirements. Confirm the vanity, top, and sink models against the approved cabinet schedule and countertop plan before proceeding.
  2. Shut off water and verify plumbing rough-in locations. Confirm the shutoff is closed and compare the supply, drain, and valve positions against the plumbing plan.

F. Wall, Floor, and Reference Verification (Steps 3–5)

  1. Inspect the wall and floor for level, plumb, moisture, and structural support. Use the 4-foot level and confirm no active leak, mold, or damaged framing is present before continuing.
  2. Establish the vanity centerline and top elevation. Reference both from the approved plan and the room's fixed dimensions.
  3. Measure and transfer supply, drain, valve, and outlet locations to the cabinet. Mark each location on the cabinet interior using the vanity centerline as the controlling reference.

G. Cutting and Cabinet Installation (Steps 6–11)

  1. Cut protected service openings using Chapter 7. Mark the waste side, protect finished surfaces, and support cutout waste before cutting.
  2. Position the vanity and verify plumbing clearance. Confirm each service opening aligns with its corresponding supply, drain, or outlet before fastening.
  3. Shim the vanity level and plumb at approved load points. Use noncompressible shims under cabinet sides or rails only.
  4. Align cabinet faces and side clearances. Confirm the cabinet matches the vanity centerline and any adjacent wall or fixture clearance.
  5. Secure the vanity to studs or approved backing. Pre-drill and install structural cabinet screws through the hanging rail or back into verified stud or backing locations.
  6. Install fillers or finished panels where required. Scribe and fasten per the Project 08 method.

H. Top Installation and Sealing (Steps 12–20)

  1. Dry-fit the vanity top. Set the top in position without adhesive or fasteners to confirm fit before proceeding.
  2. Verify wall fit, overhang, sink position, faucet clearance, and cabinet support. Compare each dimension against the approved countertop plan.
  3. Scribe or adjust only within the product limits. Do not remove material beyond the top manufacturer's specified scribe allowance.
  4. Apply the specified adhesive or setting material to cabinet support points. Follow the top manufacturer's application pattern and coverage.
  5. Set the top and verify level. Confirm level across the full top surface with the torpedo level before the setting material cures.
  6. Install separate backsplash or side splash where specified. Dry-fit before fastening or sealing.
  7. Seal the designated wall-to-splash and top joints using compatible sanitary sealant. Apply a continuous bead and tool it to a smooth, gap-free profile.
  8. Seal exposed cabinet and countertop edges against moisture. Use the edge treatment specified for the top and cabinet material.
  9. Do not block sink clips, shutoffs, drain access, or electrical devices. Confirm each remains reachable after fillers, panels, and sealant are complete.

I. Trade Coordination and Inspection (Steps 21–23)

  1. Coordinate faucet, supply, drain, and trap connections with the authorized plumbing scope. Do not connect, disconnect, or modify these components as part of this project.
  2. Inspect cabinet fastening, top support, sealant, service access, and door or drawer operation. Confirm each item against the Acceptance Standards table.
  3. Document installation and trade handoff. Record the completed vanity installation record and identify the responsible trade for remaining connections.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed vanity installation using the same checks regardless of vanity size or top material.

  • Documentation check: confirm the vanity model, top material, sink model, and plumbing plan reference are recorded on the vanity installation record.
  • Level and plumb check: confirm the cabinet is level front-to-back and side-to-side and plumb on both side planes.
  • Fastening check: confirm the cabinet is secured to studs or approved backing with structural cabinet screws.
  • Opening and clearance check: confirm every plumbing opening aligns with its verified rough-in location and preserves required clearance.
  • Top and sealant check: confirm the top is fully supported, correctly positioned, and every designated joint is sealed with the specified sanitary sealant.
  • Access check: confirm sink clips, shutoffs, drain access, and electrical devices remain unobstructed.

Complete the Vanity Installation Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before coordinating plumbing connections.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Vanity Rocks After Shimming and Fastening

Probable cause: a shim placed under an unsupported cabinet bottom rather than a side or rail, or a fastener that was not driven into a verified stud or backing location.
Confirmation: check shim placement against approved load points and confirm each structural screw location against the marked stud or backing.
Correction: remove and reposition shims at approved load points, and relocate any fastener that missed structural backing.
Reinspection: repeat the level, plumb, and hand-check stability test before proceeding to top installation.

A Plumbing Opening Does Not Align With the Rough-In

Probable cause: the centerline transfer step was skipped or measured from the wrong reference, or the rough-in was relocated without updated documentation.
Confirmation: remeasure the rough-in location against the vanity centerline and compare with the plumbing plan.
Correction: record the conflict on the vanity installation record and obtain a documented resolution from the responsible plumber or designer before enlarging or relocating the opening; do not enlarge an opening beyond the cabinet manufacturer's approved trim method.
Reinspection: repeat the clearance check once the documented resolution is implemented.

The Top Does Not Sit Level After Setting

Probable cause: uneven cabinet support points, setting material applied inconsistently, or the cabinet itself was not level before the top was set.
Confirmation: check cabinet level again with the 4-foot level and check the top surface with the torpedo level at multiple points.
Correction: relevel the cabinet before the setting material fully cures, or remove and reset the top if the material has already cured.
Reinspection: repeat the level check across the full top surface before installing the backsplash or sealant.

A Sealant Joint Shows Gaps or Fails to Bond

Probable cause: an incompatible sealant, a joint surface that was not clean and dry before application, or a bead applied too thin to bridge the joint width.
Confirmation: inspect the joint for adhesion failure, gaps, or incomplete coverage, and confirm the sealant matches the manufacturer specification for the joint's exposure condition.
Correction: remove the failed sealant, clean and dry the joint, and reapply the specified sanitary sealant using a continuous, correctly sized bead.
Reinspection: reinspect the joint after the manufacturer's specified cure time before exposing it to water.

A Sink Clip, Shutoff, or Electrical Device Is Blocked After Installation

Probable cause: a filler, panel, or sealant bead installed without confirming the service-access clearance beneath it.
Confirmation: compare the finished filler, panel, or sealant location against the marked service-access clearance from the layout step.
Correction: remove and reposition the obstructing component, or correct the sealant bead, to restore the required access.
Reinspection: repeat the service-access check before documenting installation and trade handoff.

Cleanup

This project ends with a documented, sealed, and inspected vanity installation ready for plumbing connection, not with the sink, faucet, or trap connected.

  • Remove excess sealant, adhesive, and masking tape from the cabinet, top, and adjacent finishes
  • Store remaining fasteners, shims, and sealant with the project's materials for later touch-up
  • Confirm the vanity area remains accessible and undamaged before leaving the site
  • Communicate any open discrepancy and the responsible trade for remaining connections before plumbing work begins
