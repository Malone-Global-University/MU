Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install and align a controlled set of cabinet doors, drawer fronts, hinges, and drawer slides on cabinet boxes that were leveled, plumbed, joined, and structurally secured in Projects 4 through 6, and finished with fillers, end panels, and toe kicks in Project 8. The finished work product is a set of hung doors and installed drawer fronts with consistent top, bottom, and side reveals, hardware that is securely fastened and correctly adjusted, full opening clearance against walls, fillers, appliances, and adjacent doors, and smooth, repeatable, self-closing operation confirmed by repeated testing.

You will confirm the cabinet boxes are ready to receive doors and drawers, review the door and drawer design and hardware schedule, match every door, drawer front, hinge, and slide to its assigned cabinet, install hinge cups and mounting plates, hang and align doors using a controlled reveal sequence, install and align drawer boxes and fronts, adjust hardware in the specified order, install soft-close devices, and inspect the finished set for reveal consistency, security, and operation.

This project assumes the cabinet boxes have already passed the Project 4, 5, and 6 alignment and structural-fastening inspections, and that fillers, end panels, and toe kicks from Project 8 are in place. It sits immediately before Project 13, which lays out and drills pulls and knobs only after every door and drawer front installed here is fully aligned.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CB-067 — Install concealed hinges to the specified boring depth, screw type, and mounting-plate configuration for the assigned door and cabinet.
  • CB-068 — Hang cabinet doors in the correct cabinet, orientation, and sequence without collision with walls, fillers, appliances, or adjacent doors.
  • CB-069 — Align door reveals to the specified top, bottom, and side tolerances using the manufacturer's hinge-adjustment sequence.
  • CB-070 — Install drawer fronts aligned with adjacent doors and cabinets using the specified spacer or clamping method.
  • CB-071 — Adjust hinge and slide hardware for height, side, tilt, and closing action, including soft-close devices where specified.
  • CB-072 — Inspect and document door and drawer operation, reveal consistency, hinge security, and slide engagement.

Where This Skill Fits

Every door, drawer front, hinge, and slide installed in this project depends on the cabinet-box alignment completed in Projects 4 through 6. A cabinet box that is out of level, out of plumb, or racked will not accept a consistent reveal no matter how carefully the hinge is adjusted here; that condition must be corrected in the cabinet box before this project proceeds. The reveal, hardware-adjustment, and testing method used in this project is the same method the Chapter 20 capstone inspection applies to the complete installation, and the diagnostic pattern taught here — probable cause, confirmation, correction, reinspection — is the same pattern Chapter 19 uses to repair hinge, slide, and joint defects found after occupancy.

The hardware template established for pulls and knobs in Project 13 is measured from the door and drawer-front reveals confirmed here. A reveal that is corrected informally after Project 13 hardware is drilled will misalign the finished hardware.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming cabinet boxes are level, plumb, square, joined, and permanently secured before doors and drawers are installed
  • reviewing door and drawer design, overlay, inset, reveal, handing, hinge model, slide type, and manufacturer settings against the approved schedule
  • matching every door, drawer, hinge, and front to its assigned cabinet designation
  • inspecting components for warp, damage, finish defects, and correct factory drilling
  • installing hinge cups, hinge plates, and mounting plates using the specified screws and boring or mounting depth
  • hanging doors and establishing controlling top, bottom, and side reveals
  • installing or verifying drawer boxes and slides, and positioning and permanently fastening drawer fronts
  • adjusting hinge height, side, and depth, and slide height, side, tilt, and closing action, in the specified order
  • installing soft-close devices or stops where specified
  • operating every door and drawer repeatedly and inspecting reveal consistency, hinge security, slide engagement, opening clearance, and self-closing action

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • install doors, drawer fronts, hinges, or slides on a cabinet box that has not passed the Project 4, 5, or 6 structural-fastening and alignment inspection
  • substitute a longer, shorter, or different-drive hardware screw than the one specified for the hinge, mounting plate, or slide system
  • drill or bore a door or drawer front for hardware position not covered by this project's hinge and reveal specification; hardware location for pulls and knobs is covered in Project 13
  • conceal a warped door, damaged hinge-boring, a racked drawer box, or a cabinet that will not hold a level reveal rather than mark it for correction

Any door, drawer, or hinge condition that will not hold the specified reveal or adjustment after the full adjustment sequence is applied must be identified and corrected using the Failure Points and Troubleshooting section before continuing to Project 13.

Trade Note

The hardware template in Project 13 is built from the reveals and alignment confirmed here. Treating a slightly uneven reveal as close enough, rather than correcting it through the specified hinge-adjustment sequence, is the most common cause of crooked-looking pull and knob rows traced back to this project during the Chapter 19 repair project.

Setup Standard

Do not begin hinge or hardware installation until the cabinet boxes have passed the Project 4, 5, and 6 alignment and structural-fastening inspection and the fillers, end panels, and toe kicks from Project 8 are installed (Step 1). Do not hang a door or install a drawer front until every component has been matched to its cabinet designation and inspected for warp, damage, and correct factory drilling (Steps 3–4).

Shop Tip

Hang and align the first door of a run completely, including final reveal adjustment, before hanging the second door. Using the first door's confirmed reveal as the working reference for every adjacent door prevents small errors from compounding across a long run.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every hardware-installation project in this book, from this chapter's door and drawer hanging to Project 13's pull and knob installation, follows the same order: confirm the structure is ready to receive the component, match every component to its designation, install using the specified fastener and depth, align to a controlling reference, adjust in the specified order, and inspect operation before final acceptance. Separating Adjustment From Replacement

Every reveal or operation defect found during this project falls into one of two categories: a condition correctable through the manufacturer's specified hinge or slide adjustment sequence, or a condition requiring component replacement. A door that will not hold level within the adjustment range of its hinges, a warped door, or a drawer box damaged during installation is not correctable through adjustment; replace the component rather than forcing an adjustment beyond its designed range.

Measurement Cue — Establish the Controlling Reveal Before Adjusting Adjacent Doors

Set and confirm the first door's top, bottom, and side reveal against the approved door and drawer design before hanging the next door in the run. Use that confirmed reveal, not the cabinet opening alone, as the working reference for every adjacent door and drawer front. Adjusting each door independently against the cabinet opening, rather than against the confirmed controlling reveal, produces a visibly inconsistent run even when each individual door measures within tolerance.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Substitute a Longer Hardware Screw

Do not install a longer screw than the one specified for a hinge cup, mounting plate, or slide, even if a longer screw appears to hold more securely. A screw that penetrates through a door or drawer front damages the finished face and cannot be corrected without replacing the component. Confirm screw length against the specified hardware schedule before installing any hinge, plate, or slide fastener.

Field Safety Cue — Support Removed Doors and Drawer Fronts

Place every door, drawer front, and drawer box removed for hinge or hardware work on a padded support surface, finished face up unless the specified operation requires otherwise. Do not lean removed components against unprotected surfaces or stack them in a way that can chip edges or scratch finishes.

Manufacturer Cue — Follow the Specified Hinge-Adjustment Order

Adjust concealed hinges in the order specified by the hinge manufacturer — typically depth, then side, then height — rather than in an order of convenience. Adjusting the axes out of the specified order can cause a later adjustment to undo an earlier one, requiring the full sequence to be repeated.

Code Alert — Accessibility Requirements for Operable Hardware Vary by Jurisdiction

Required operating force, reach range, and clearance for cabinet doors and drawers in accessible installations vary by adopted code and jurisdiction. Verify the current requirement with the authority having jurisdiction and the general contractor before confirming that installed doors, drawers, and hardware meet an intended accessibility requirement.

E. Confirmation and Component Matching (Steps 1–4)

  1. Confirm cabinet boxes are level, plumb, square, joined, and permanently secured. Verify the Project 4, 5, and 6 alignment and structural-fastening inspection record before proceeding.
  2. Review door and drawer design, overlay, inset, reveal, handing, hinge model, slide type, and manufacturer settings. Confirm the current revision of the door, drawer, and hardware schedule against the document-control log.
  3. Match each door, drawer, hinge, and front to its cabinet designation. Verify the designation against the cabinet schedule before removing any packaging or protective film.
  4. Inspect components for warp, damage, finish defects, and correct drilling. Set aside any component that does not meet the acceptance standard and record it on the adjustment log.

F. Hinge and Plate Installation (Steps 5–7)

  1. Install hinge cups or hinge plates using the specified screws and depth. Set the drill clutch to seat each screw fully without overdriving.
  2. Do not substitute longer screws that can penetrate finished faces. Confirm screw length against the hardware schedule before installing.
  3. Attach hinges to doors and mounting plates to cabinets. Verify each hinge arm and mounting plate is seated fully before hanging the door.

G. Door Hanging and Reveal Alignment (Steps 8–12)

  1. Hang the first door. Engage the hinge arm on the mounting plate and confirm the door is fully seated before releasing support.
  2. Establish the controlling top, bottom, and side reveals. Measure against the approved door and drawer design and record the confirmed reveal on the adjustment log.
  3. Use hinge height, side, and depth adjustments in the specified order. Follow the manufacturer's specified adjustment sequence rather than an order of convenience.
  4. Install adjacent doors and align pairs. Use the confirmed controlling reveal, not the cabinet opening alone, as the working reference.
  5. Confirm doors do not collide with walls, fillers, handles, appliances, or each other. Operate each door through its full swing before continuing to the next.

H. Drawer Installation and Final Adjustment (Steps 13–22)

  1. Install or verify drawer boxes and slides. Confirm each slide is level and fully engaged before positioning the drawer front.
  2. Position drawer fronts using spacers, clamps, or temporary screws. Confirm alignment against the confirmed controlling reveal before permanent fastening.
  3. Align drawer-front reveals with adjacent doors and cabinets. Verify top, bottom, and side reveals match the approved design.
  4. Permanently fasten drawer fronts from approved concealed locations. Use the specified fastener and confirm full seating without distorting the front.
  5. Adjust slide height, side, tilt, and closing action. Follow the manufacturer's specified adjustment sequence.
  6. Install soft-close devices or stops where specified. Confirm the device engages consistently across repeated closings.
  7. Operate every door and drawer repeatedly. Confirm reveal, clearance, and closing action hold consistent across at least five open-close cycles.
  8. Inspect reveal consistency, hinge security, slide engagement, opening clearance, and self-closing action. Compare the finished run against the Acceptance Standards table.
  9. Correct racking, binding, sag, collision, and loose hardware. Use the Failure Points and Troubleshooting section to diagnose and correct any defect found.
  10. Document final adjustment settings and damaged components. Record every hinge and slide setting and any component set aside for replacement on the adjustment log.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect every door and drawer front using the same checks regardless of which cabinet run it covers.

  • Documentation check: confirm the cabinet, door, drawer, hinge, and slide schedule is current and referenced on the adjustment log.
  • Component-matching check: confirm every door, drawer front, hinge, and slide is matched to its assigned cabinet designation and free of warp, damage, or finish defect.
  • Hardware-security check: confirm every hinge cup, mounting plate, and slide screw is fully seated using the specified fastener.
  • Reveal check: confirm top, bottom, and side reveals match the approved door and drawer design within the specified tolerance across the full run.
  • Clearance check: confirm every door and drawer clears walls, fillers, appliances, handles, and adjacent doors through its full range of motion.
  • Operation check: confirm every door and drawer with specified soft-close hardware closes fully and smoothly without slamming or rebound across repeated cycles.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Door Reveal Is Uneven Along Its Height

Probable cause: a hinge-side adjustment applied out of the manufacturer's specified sequence, or a cabinet box that is not plumb.
Confirmation: check the cabinet side for plumb with the 4-foot level and measure the reveal at the top, middle, and bottom of the door.
Correction: if the cabinet is plumb, repeat the hinge adjustment in the specified depth-then-side-then-height sequence; if the cabinet is not plumb, correct the cabinet box under Project 4, 5, or 6 methods before continuing.
Reinspection: repeat the reveal measurement at the top, middle, and bottom of the door once the adjustment or cabinet correction is complete.

A Door Sags or Drops Over Time

Probable cause: a hinge-plate screw that was not fully seated, an oversized or stripped pilot hole, or a hinge overloaded beyond its rated door weight.
Confirmation: inspect each mounting-plate screw for full seating and check the pilot-hole condition; compare the door weight against the hinge's rated capacity.
Correction: reinstall the mounting plate using the specified screw in a sound pilot hole, or an approved fastener repair where the original hole is stripped; replace the hinge if it is undersized for the door weight.
Reinspection: operate the door through five open-close cycles and recheck the reveal after the repair.

Adjacent Doors Collide When Opened

Probable cause: insufficient filler or clearance between cabinets, or a door hung with the incorrect handing.
Confirmation: compare the installed handing against the door and cabinet schedule and measure the clearance between adjacent doors at full swing.
Correction: rehang the door with the correct handing where the schedule was misread, or record the clearance conflict on the deficiency list and refer it to the designer where the filler width itself is insufficient; do not trim a door or filler beyond the specified allowance without approval.
Reinspection: operate both doors through full swing after the correction and confirm clearance against the schedule.

A Drawer Binds or Does Not Extend Fully

Probable cause: a slide that is not level, a slide member not fully engaged, or debris in the slide track.
Confirmation: check the slide for level with the 4-foot level and inspect the track for obstruction or misengagement.
Correction: relevel and reseat the slide, clean the track, and reengage the slide members per the manufacturer's instructions.
Reinspection: operate the drawer through its full extension at least five times and confirm smooth, level travel.

A Soft-Close Device Slams or Fails to Engage

Probable cause: a soft-close device installed at the wrong location or engagement point, or a device not compatible with the installed slide or hinge.
Confirmation: compare the installed device and location against the manufacturer's specified engagement point and compatibility list.
Correction: reposition or replace the soft-close device per the manufacturer's instructions.
Reinspection: operate the door or drawer through at least five closings and confirm consistent, smooth engagement.

A Drawer Front Is Not Aligned With Adjacent Doors

Probable cause: the drawer front was permanently fastened before the confirmed controlling reveal was verified against adjacent components.
Confirmation: measure the drawer-front reveal against the confirmed controlling reveal and the adjacent door reveals.
Correction: loosen the concealed fasteners, reposition the drawer front using spacers or clamps against the controlling reveal, and refasten.
Reinspection: recheck the drawer-front reveal against adjacent doors and cabinets after refastening.
