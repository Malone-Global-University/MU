Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to integrate every competency taught in Projects 1 through 19 into one complete cabinet and built-in storage installation. The finished work product is a fully installed, structurally fastened, service-accessible, moisture-protected, and functionally tested cabinet system — including base, wall, and tall cabinets; fillers, panels, and toe kicks; a laminate or butcher-block countertop with sealed edges and, where included in the approved capstone plan, a sink opening and a vanity cabinet and top; doors, drawers, hinges, slides, and hardware; open utility shelving; a closet organizer or alternate storage system; a built-in bookcase; a bench or window seat where included; and floating shelves or wall-mounted storage — closed out with complete records and a formal handoff.

You will perform the Project 01 readiness inspection and correct deficiencies, stage the Project 02 work area, establish the Project 03 reference lines, install and structurally secure base, wall, and tall cabinets per Projects 4 through 6, cut protected service openings per Project 07, install fillers, panels, and toe kicks per Project 08, template and install the countertop per Project 09, cut and prepare a sink opening per Project 10, install a vanity where included per Project 11, install doors, drawer fronts, hinges, and slides per Project 12, install pulls and knobs per Project 13, build and install open shelving per Project 14, install a closet organizer per Project 15, build a built-in bookcase per Project 16, build a bench or window seat where included per Project 17, install floating shelves or wall-mounted storage per Project 18, apply the specified proof loads, inspect and correct the complete installation, and hand off the finished system with cabinet-care, countertop-care, load-limit, shutoff-access, and adjustment information.

This project is the final project in Book 13 and assumes every project from Project 01 through Project 19 has been completed, or that the equivalent competency has been independently demonstrated, before the capstone begins. It sits at the end of the cabinet-installation and built-ins sequence and hands the finished, trimmed practice room or approved training space off to Book 14, which addresses flooring coordination, and to the licensed trades responsible for final appliance, fixture, and utility connections.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CB-115 — Integrate the cabinet-installation readiness inspection, reference-line layout, and base-cabinet installation into one field-verified sequence, structurally securing every base cabinet before fillers, panels, or countertops conceal the adjustment.
  • CB-116 — Install and structurally secure the wall-cabinet run using a verified support and lifting plan, and integrate service-opening cutting with confirmed plumbing and electrical locations.
  • CB-117 — Fit and install fillers, end panels, and toe kicks, then template, fit, secure, and edge-seal the approved countertop and, where included, cut, seal, and dry-set the sink opening.
  • CB-118 — Install and align cabinet doors, drawer fronts, hinges, slides, pulls, and knobs on the secured cabinet installation, and verify consistent reveals and smooth operation across the complete run.
  • CB-119 — Build and install open utility shelving, a closet organizer or alternate storage system, a built-in bookcase, a bench or window seat where included, and floating shelves or wall-mounted storage, each secured to verified structural support and tested under the specified proof load.
  • CB-120 — Inspect, correct, document, and formally hand off a complete cabinet and built-in storage installation, including a final punch list, complete records, and cabinet-care and load-limit information for the receiving party.

Where This Skill Fits

This capstone is the point at which every isolated project in Book 13 is proven as one integrated cabinet and built-in system rather than as separate exercises. The readiness inspection, reference-line, and structural-fastening discipline from Projects 1 through 6 controls whether the countertop, sink, and vanity work in Projects 9 through 11 can proceed on a secure base. The service-opening and clearance discipline from Project 07 controls whether utilities remain accessible once fillers, panels, and countertops in Projects 08 and 09 conceal the cabinet interior. The door, drawer, and hardware alignment discipline from Projects 12 and 13 depends entirely on the box-level, plumb, and square work completed earlier. The shelving, closet, built-in, and floating-shelf load and anchoring discipline from Projects 14 through 18 is tested here under the same proof-load method used in each of those projects. Every deficiency found and corrected at this capstone follows the same troubleshooting pattern used throughout the book, and every documentation habit established in Project 01 culminates in the final closeout records and trade handoff produced here.

Completing this capstone confirms readiness to perform unsupervised cabinet and built-in installation work within the scope defined by this book, and to correctly identify the point at which plumbing, electrical, appliance, structural, or specialty-countertop work must be assigned to another licensed or qualified trade.

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select cabinet dimensions, countertop material, storage components, or finish materials independently of the approved capstone plan and schedules
  • connect plumbing, electrical, gas, or appliance services, or field-cut stone, quartz, solid-surface, or other specialty countertop material outside the authorized scope of Projects 9 and 10

Any condition that does not match the approved capstone plan, or that suggests active leaks, mold, structural damage, hazardous materials, or unsafe wiring, must stop work in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

This capstone does not introduce new physical techniques. It integrates the readiness, layout, structural-fastening, service-access, countertop, moving-component, storage, and closeout methods taught in Projects 1 through 19 into one continuous field sequence, in the exact order established by the System-Sequence Control governing this book. Treating any earlier project's method as optional at capstone scale — skipping a reinspection, substituting a drywall screw for a structural cabinet screw, or concealing a service location behind a panel — reproduces the same failures Project 19 was built to repair, at full installation scale.

Shop Tip

Work the capstone in the exact stage order given in the Required Project Sequence. Every later stage — countertop templating, sink cutting, door and hardware alignment, shelf and built-in anchoring — depends on the structural fastening and service verification completed in the stage before it. Skipping ahead to a visually satisfying stage, such as hardware or trim, before the structural stages are inspected is the most common source of capstone rework.

Maintaining the Approved Installation Sequence Across Stages

The System-Sequence Control governing this book requires that base, wall, and tall cabinets be leveled, plumbed, joined, and secured before fillers, panels, toe kicks, or countertops conceal the adjustment; that plumbing and electrical cutouts be made only after cabinet position and service locations are field verified; that countertops be templated only after cabinet boxes are secured in final position; and that doors, drawers, hinges, and pulls be installed or finally adjusted only after cabinet boxes are secure and aligned. The capstone sequence in this project follows that order exactly. Do not reorder a stage to save time; document the controlling reason if a genuine site condition requires a deviation, and obtain direction before proceeding out of sequence.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Stage Acceptance Before Proceeding

Before beginning any capstone stage, confirm the prior stage's acceptance standard has been met and recorded on the capstone installation record. Do not proceed to countertop templating on an unconfirmed base-cabinet run, to door and hardware installation on an unconfirmed cabinet box, or to proof-load testing on an unconfirmed anchor installation.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Conceal a Deficiency to Meet Capstone Schedule

Do not conceal an active leak, mold, structural damage, missing backing, unresolved framing defect, blocked service access, or unsafe wiring behind a panel, countertop, or built-in to complete the capstone on schedule. A concealed condition becomes a hidden hazard traced back to this project during a future repair. Mark the condition, stop work in that area, and obtain qualified review or the assigned trade before continuing.

Field Safety Cue — Lifting and Ladder Discipline Across Every Stage

Use cabinet jacks, support rails, lifts, or adequate helpers for every wall and tall cabinet lift, and never stand beneath an unsupported wall cabinet. Confirm stable ladder or platform footing before every wall-cabinet, ceiling, soffit, or upper-shelf task, and keep ladders clear of door swings and walkways throughout the capstone.

Manufacturer Cue — Follow the Identified Product Instructions at Every Stage

Cabinet hanging systems, countertop seam and fastening systems, sink clip systems, hinge and slide adjustment sequences, and floating-shelf bracket systems each carry manufacturer-specific instructions. Follow the identified manufacturer instruction for each product at the stage where it applies; do not substitute a generic method across different products.

Code Alert — Confirm Trade Boundaries and Inspection Requirements Before Handoff

Permit, inspection, and licensing requirements for plumbing, electrical, gas, structural, and specialty-countertop work vary by jurisdiction. Confirm which capstone stages require inspection sign-off and which connections must be coordinated with a licensed plumber, electrician, or other qualified trade before issuing the final handoff.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Countertop Section Rocks or Shows a Gap After Installation

Probable cause: a base cabinet that was not fully leveled or secured before templating, or support that was not confirmed at a seam or unsupported span.
Confirmation: check the base-cabinet top plane with a long straightedge and inspect support at every corner, seam, and appliance opening per Project 09.
Correction: correct the cabinet level and fastening before re-securing the countertop; add support blocking where the original span was inadequate.
Reinspection: repeat the countertop-plane and support check across the complete run.

A Door or Drawer Reveal Is Inconsistent Across the Run

Probable cause: a cabinet box that shifted during fastening, an unadjusted hinge, or a face frame that was not aligned before joining per Project 04.
Confirmation: check cabinet-box level, plumb, and face alignment, then check hinge height, side, and depth adjustment per Project 12.
Correction: correct the cabinet-box alignment first, then re-adjust hinges in the manufacturer-specified sequence.
Reinspection: operate the door or drawer repeatedly and recheck reveal consistency against adjacent components.

A Shelf, Bench, or Floating-Shelf Bracket Fails the Proof Load

Probable cause: a fastener that missed structural framing, an anchor system not rated for the applied load, or a bracket that was not fully seated against the wall.
Confirmation: remove the load, inspect fastener engagement and bracket contact, and confirm the fastener location against the marked stud or backing.
Correction: reinstall using structural framing or the exact approved anchor system rated for the design load; do not substitute a lighter-duty fastener to pass the test.
Reinspection: reapply the proof load gradually and confirm no deflection, fastener movement, or wall damage before release.

A Service Location Is Blocked After Fillers, Panels, or the Countertop Are Installed

Probable cause: a filler, panel, or countertop support that was installed without confirming the Project 07 service-opening clearance.
Confirmation: compare the as-built condition against the service plans and the Project 07 clearance records.
Correction: remove or modify the concealing component using the approved trim or access method; do not leave a shutoff, disconnect, or cleanout inaccessible.
Reinspection: confirm access is restored and document the correction on the capstone installation record.

A Tall Cabinet or Built-In Shows Movement During the Stability Check

Probable cause: a missing or improperly installed anti-tip restraint, or a structural fastener that did not engage framing.
Confirmation: inspect the anti-tip bracket or restraint and confirm structural fastener locations against marked studs or backing per Projects 6 and 16.
Correction: install or correct the anti-tip restraint and re-secure structural fasteners into confirmed framing.
Reinspection: repeat the controlled stability check before releasing the cabinet or built-in for use.

A Punch-List Item Remains Open at the Scheduled Handoff

Probable cause: a correction that requires another trade, a material on order, or a condition discovered late in the final inspection.
Confirmation: review the punch list against the Step 39–43 inspection and confirm the current status of each open item.
Correction: document the open item, the responsible party, and the required completion date on the capstone installation record before proceeding.
Reinspection: reinspect and close the item before final sign-off; do not issue an unqualified handoff with an open structural, service-access, or safety item.

The highest elevation of the finished floor within the cabinet footprint, used as the base reference for all cabinet elevations.

Framing, blocking, or an approved engineered product installed to receive a structural cabinet, shelf, or built-in fastener.

The Project 01 approved, conditional, or rejected determination that authorizes work to proceed.
