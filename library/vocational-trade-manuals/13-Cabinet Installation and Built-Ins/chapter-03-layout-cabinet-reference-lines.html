Job Overview

This project qualifies you to establish and verify every controlling reference line needed to install one cabinet run: the floor high point, the base-cabinet and countertop elevations, the wall-cabinet elevations, plumb references at run ends and tall-cabinet locations, stud and backing centerlines, and marked service and no-fastener zones. The finished work product is a complete, field-verified cabinet layout — floor high point, cabinet elevations, run limits, studs, services, fillers, and appliance openings marked directly on the wall and floor and recorded on the layout documentation — ready to support the base-cabinet installation completed in Project 04.

You will review the field-verified cabinet plan and installation sequence accepted in Project 01, establish a stable benchmark, survey the floor for its high point, mark the base-cabinet and countertop elevations from that high point, establish the wall-cabinet elevations, transfer level lines around corners, mark plumb references, locate and mark studs and backing, mark service and no-fastener zones, dry-lay cabinet widths on the wall, verify fillers and clearances, compare the total run dimension with the actual wall, resolve any dimensional conflict, and photograph and document the completed layout.

This project follows directly from the readiness decision issued in Project 01 and the protected work area staged in Project 02. It sits immediately before Project 04, which installs the base-cabinet run from the floor high point and reference lines established here, and it controls every wall-cabinet, tall-cabinet, filler, countertop, and built-in reference used through the remainder of this book.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • CB-013 — Establish the high point of the finished floor within the cabinet footprint and confirm it by repeated measurement.
  • CB-014 — Mark the base-cabinet and countertop reference lines from the confirmed floor high point.
  • CB-015 — Mark the wall-cabinet reference lines from the approved elevation and coordinate them with appliances, backsplash, windows, soffits, and ceilings.
  • CB-016 — Transfer level references around inside and outside corners and mark plumb references at run ends and tall-cabinet locations.
  • CB-017 — Locate and mark stud or backing centerlines and service locations across the full cabinet area using an approved verification method.
  • CB-018 — Verify layout accuracy by dry-laying cabinet widths, comparing the total run dimension with the actual wall, and resolving any dimensional conflict before installation.

Where This Skill Fits

Every reference line marked in this project becomes a controlling dimension for the rest of the book. The floor high point confirmed here sets the base-cabinet, countertop, and wall-cabinet elevations installed in Projects 4 through 6; the stud and backing centerlines marked here control every structural fastener driven in Projects 4, 5, 6, 11, 14, 15, 16, 17, and 18; and the service and no-fastener zones marked here are what Project 7 verifies again in the field before any cabinet opening is cut. An inaccurate reference line is not a cosmetic error — it becomes a cabinet run that is out of level, a fastener that misses backing, or a service opening cut in the wrong location.

The layout method used here — surveying from a single confirmed benchmark, transferring that benchmark around the full run, and verifying the layout against the actual wall before committing to installation — is the same method the Chapter 20 capstone uses to establish its own reference lines before cabinet installation begins.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the field-verified cabinet plan and installation sequence accepted in Project 01
  • establishing a stable benchmark and surveying the finished floor for its high point using a laser, water level, or long level
  • marking and confirming the floor high point by repeated measurement
  • establishing the base-cabinet top or cabinet-support reference and marking the base-cabinet run, end limits, corners, appliance openings, and tall-cabinet positions
  • marking the finished countertop elevation where specified
  • establishing the wall-cabinet bottom and top reference lines from the approved elevation and coordinating them with appliances, backsplash, windows, soffits, and ceilings
  • transferring level lines around inside and outside corners and marking vertical plumb references at run ends and tall cabinets
  • locating and marking stud or backing centerlines and plumbing, electrical, vent, gas, and appliance service locations across the full cabinet area
  • marking no-fastener and no-cut zones
  • dry-laying cabinet widths on the wall using tape or story poles and verifying fillers, end panels, corner clearances, door swing, drawer pullout, and appliance gaps
  • comparing the total cabinet run dimension with the actual wall, resolving any dimensional conflict, and photographing and documenting the final reference lines

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin base-, wall-, or tall-cabinet installation before the reference lines in this project are complete and any dimensional conflict is resolved
  • cut cabinet openings, drill fastener holes, or otherwise alter the wall, floor, or cabinet components based on this project's marked references alone; Project 7 field-verifies service locations again before any opening is cut
  • revise a cabinet, filler, or appliance-opening dimension independently to resolve a layout conflict; an unresolved conflict between the cabinet schedule and the actual wall must be referred to the designer or general contractor
  • proceed with layout over a wall, floor, or service condition that was not accepted in the Project 01 readiness decision

Any dimensional conflict that cannot be resolved within the cabinet plan's stated filler or scribe allowance, or any wall, floor, or service condition that was not accepted in the Project 01 readiness decision, must stop layout in that area until direction is obtained.

Trade Note

Every base-cabinet, wall-cabinet, tall-cabinet, filler, and countertop project in this book assumes the reference lines marked in this project were accurate. A benchmark that was not verified by repeated measurement, or a stud location marked from a single unconfirmed reading, is the most common cause of an out-of-level cabinet run or a missed structural fastener traced back to this project during the Chapter 19 repair project.

Shop Tip

Confirm the floor high point from at least two independent benchmark positions before marking any base-cabinet or countertop reference line. A high point that is off by even a small amount at this stage compounds across the length of the run and reappears as a fastening or countertop-support problem in Project 4 or Project 9.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every reference-line layout in this book, from this project's floor-high-point survey to the Chapter 20 capstone layout, follows the same order: confirm the accepted readiness decision, establish a single stable benchmark, survey and confirm the controlling elevation, transfer that elevation into every dependent reference line, locate and mark concealed services and structural support, verify the layout against the actual room before committing to it, and resolve any conflict before installation begins. Learning that sequence here protects every later layout and installation project in this book.

Separating a Conflict This Project Permits You to Resolve From One Requiring the Designer

A dimensional conflict found during layout falls into one of two categories: a gap within the cabinet plan's stated filler or scribe allowance, which this project permits you to close by marking the required filler or scribe width, or a conflict that exceeds that allowance, which requires a documented decision from the designer or general contractor. Marking a wider filler within the plan's allowance is within this project's authority. Resizing a cabinet, omitting a filler, or altering an appliance opening independently is not; record the conflict on the layout record and obtain direction before marking a final reference line at that location.

Measurement Cue — Confirm the Floor High Point by Repeated Measurement

Confirm the floor high point from at least two independent benchmark positions before marking any base-cabinet, countertop, or wall-cabinet reference line. Do not mark a reference line from a single reading. Every elevation in this project is transferred from that one point, and an unconfirmed high point compounds into a cabinet run that is out of level across its full length.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Mark a Fastener Line Over an Unmarked Service

Do not mark a stud, hanging-rail, or fastener reference line over a plumbing, electrical, gas, or mechanical service location until that service has been identified, confirmed against the plumbing and electrical plans, and marked as a no-fastener zone. A fastener line marked without confirming the service location behind it becomes a struck pipe, conduit, or gas line during Project 4, 5, or 6 installation.

Field Safety Cue — Laser Level and Chalk-Line Path

Confirm the layout path is clear of staged cabinets, tools, cords, and other trip hazards before operating the laser level or snapping a chalk line. Do not stand on staged cabinets, ladders positioned in a door swing, or unstable surfaces while sighting or marking a reference line.

Manufacturer Cue — Laser-Level and Stud-Finder Calibration

Confirm the laser level or water level is reading a stable, level reference and recalibrate the stud finder on an accessible section of wall with a known stud location before relying on either instrument across the full cabinet run. Do not mark a reference line or a stud location from an uncalibrated or unstable instrument reading.

Code Alert — Appliance and Backsplash Clearances Vary by Jurisdiction and Manufacturer

Required clearances between wall-cabinet reference lines and appliances, backsplash height, and ventilation openings vary by adopted code, jurisdiction, and appliance manufacturer instructions. Verify the current clearance requirement with the appliance manufacturer instructions, the approved cabinet plan, and the authority having jurisdiction before marking a wall-cabinet reference line near an appliance location.

E. Benchmark and Floor Survey (Steps 1–5)

  1. Review the field-verified cabinet plan and installation sequence. Confirm the Project 01 readiness decision and cabinet schedule match the run being laid out.
  2. Establish a stable benchmark in the room. Select a fixed, undisturbed location that will remain accessible throughout the layout and installation sequence, and record its position on the layout record.
  3. Use a laser, water level, or long level to survey the finished floor. Survey the full cabinet footprint from the established benchmark.
  4. Mark and record the highest floor point within the cabinet footprint. Mark the location directly on the floor and record the elevation on the layout record.
  5. Confirm the high point by repeating measurements. Survey from a second independent benchmark position and confirm both readings agree before proceeding.

F. Cabinet Elevation Layout (Steps 6–10)

  1. Establish the base-cabinet top or cabinet-support reference from the high point. Transfer the confirmed elevation using the laser level or a story pole.
  2. Mark the base-cabinet run, end limits, corners, appliance openings, and tall-cabinet positions. Use the chalk line to mark a continuous, level reference across the full run.
  3. Mark the finished countertop elevation where specified. Transfer the countertop elevation from the confirmed base-cabinet reference line.
  4. Establish the wall-cabinet bottom and top reference lines from the approved elevation. Confirm the elevation against the cabinet plan before marking.
  5. Coordinate wall-cabinet lines with appliances, backsplash, windows, soffits, and ceilings. Confirm each clearance against the appliance schedule and manufacturer instructions before finalizing the line.

G. Corner, Plumb, and Structural Layout (Steps 11–15)

  1. Transfer level lines around inside and outside corners. Confirm the transferred line remains level and continuous at each corner.
  2. Mark vertical plumb references at run ends and tall cabinets. Use a level or plumb bob to confirm true plumb at each marked location.
  3. Locate and mark stud or backing centerlines across the full cabinet area. Confirm stud-finder readings using a second verification method where the wall finish and plan permit it.
  4. Mark plumbing, electrical, vent, gas, and appliance service locations. Compare each location against the plumbing and electrical plans before marking.
  5. Mark no-fastener and no-cut zones. Clearly identify each zone at every marked service location.

H. Verification and Documentation (Steps 16–20)

  1. Dry-lay cabinet widths on the wall using tape or story poles. Lay out every cabinet, filler, and panel width in sequence along the marked base-cabinet reference line.
  2. Verify fillers, end panels, corner clearances, door swing, drawer pullout, and appliance gaps. Confirm each clearance against the cabinet schedule and appliance schedule.
  3. Compare total cabinet run dimension with the actual wall. Measure the full dry-laid run and compare it against the actual wall length.
  4. Resolve accumulated dimensional conflict before cabinet installation. Close any gap within the cabinet plan's stated filler or scribe allowance; refer any conflict exceeding that allowance to the designer or general contractor and record it on the layout record.
  5. Photograph and document final reference lines. Photograph the floor high point, base-cabinet and wall-cabinet reference lines, plumb references, stud and service marks, and the dry-lay result, and complete the cabinet reference-line layout record.

Common Verification Inspection

Inspect the completed layout using the same checks regardless of which cabinet run it covers.

  • Benchmark check: confirm the benchmark is stable, undisturbed, and recorded on the layout record.
  • Floor high point check: confirm the high point was confirmed from at least two independent benchmark positions.
  • Elevation check: confirm base-cabinet, countertop, and wall-cabinet reference lines are level and transferred correctly from the confirmed high point and approved elevation.
  • Plumb and corner check: confirm plumb references are marked at run ends and tall-cabinet locations and level lines are continuous around every corner.
  • Structural and service check: confirm studs, backing, and every service location are marked using more than one verification method, with no-fastener zones clearly identified.
  • Dimensional check: confirm the dry-lay result matches the actual wall within the cabinet plan's stated allowance and any conflict has been resolved and documented.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Floor High Point Does Not Agree Between Benchmark Positions

Probable cause: an uncalibrated or unstable laser or water level, a disturbed benchmark, or a floor with deflection under the instrument's support.
Confirmation: reset the benchmark on a confirmed stable location, recalibrate the instrument, and repeat the survey from two new positions.
Correction: discard the disagreeing readings and repeat the full survey until two independent readings agree within the cabinet plan's stated tolerance.
Reinspection: confirm the final high point against a third independent reading before marking any dependent reference line.

A Wall-Cabinet Reference Line Conflicts With an Appliance or Backsplash Clearance

Probable cause: the wall-cabinet elevation was transferred from the plan without confirming the appliance manufacturer's required clearance at the actual site.
Confirmation: compare the marked line against the appliance schedule and manufacturer instructions at the affected location.
Correction: adjust the marked line to the manufacturer-required clearance within the cabinet plan's allowance, or refer the conflict to the designer if no allowance covers it.
Reinspection: repeat the clearance check at the corrected line before proceeding to the next marked location.

A Level Line Does Not Remain Level Across a Corner

Probable cause: the level was not transferred using a calibrated instrument, or the corner itself is out of square beyond the cabinet plan's scribe allowance.
Confirmation: re-transfer the line using the laser level and compare the corner condition against the Project 01 wall-inspection findings.
Correction: re-mark the line from the confirmed benchmark; if the corner condition exceeds the scribe allowance, record it on the layout record and obtain a documented decision before proceeding.
Reinspection: confirm the corrected line is level and continuous around the full corner.

A Stud or Backing Centerline Cannot Be Confirmed

Probable cause: nonstandard framing spacing, a stud finder reading affected by wiring or plumbing behind the wall, or missing backing at a planned fastener location.
Confirmation: recalibrate the stud finder on a known stud location and cross-check with a second method where the wall finish and plan permit it.
Correction: record the location on the layout record and identify whether additional backing must be installed before base-cabinet installation proceeds in Project 4.
Reinspection: repeat the stud and backing check after any required backing is installed and before final layout lines are photographed.

The Dry-Laid Cabinet Run Does Not Match the Actual Wall Length

Probable cause: an accumulated dimensional error across multiple cabinet, filler, and panel widths, or a wall length that differs from the cabinet schedule.
Confirmation: remeasure the dry-laid run in sections and compare each section against the cabinet schedule and the actual wall.
Correction: close the gap using the cabinet plan's stated filler or scribe allowance; if the gap exceeds that allowance, record the conflict on the layout record and obtain a documented decision from the designer or general contractor before installation begins.
Reinspection: repeat the dry-lay and dimensional comparison after the documented correction or revised schedule is received.

A Marked Service Location Conflicts With a Planned Fastener Line

Probable cause: the fastener reference line was marked before the plumbing or electrical plan was compared against the actual field location of the service.
Confirmation: compare the marked fastener line against the plumbing and electrical plans and the field-marked service location.
Correction: mark the affected area as a no-fastener zone and adjust the fastener line to the nearest confirmed stud or backing location clear of the service; do not relocate the service independently.
Reinspection: repeat the service and fastener-line check before base-cabinet installation begins in Project 4.

Field Terms

The highest elevation of the finished floor within the cabinet footprint, used as the base reference for all later cabinet elevations.
Benchmark
A single stable, repeatable elevation reference from which the floor survey and all later transfers are taken.
Base-Cabinet Reference Line
The level line marking the top of the base-cabinet run, established from the confirmed floor high point.
Wall-Cabinet Reference Lines
The level lines marking the bottom and top of the wall-cabinet run.
Plumb Reference
A vertical line marking true plumb at a run end or tall-cabinet location.
No-Fastener Zone
A marked area where a service prohibits driving a fastener or cutting into the wall or floor.
Story Pole
A rigid, marked layout tool used to dry-lay cabinet widths before final reference lines are committed.
Scribe Allowance
The permitted gap between a cabinet or panel and an irregular wall or floor surface, closed by scribing a filler or panel to the actual condition.

The document recording the benchmark, floor high point, reference lines, structural and service marks, dimensional verification, and photographed layout.
