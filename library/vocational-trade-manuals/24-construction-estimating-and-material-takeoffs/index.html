24-Construction Estimating and Material Takeoffs

This field manual provides practical instruction in residential construction estimating, quantity calculations, and material takeoff preparation. Students learn to analyze plans, measure building components, calculate quantities, organize estimates, and prepare professional project cost documentation.

Overview

Construction Estimating and Material Takeoffs develops the skills needed to accurately quantify materials and prepare residential construction estimates. Beginning with estimating systems and plan review, students progress through sitework, foundations, framing, building envelope systems, interior finishes, mechanical allowances, exterior work, and complete project material summaries.

Learning Outcomes

  • Set up organized residential estimating systems.
  • Review plans, specifications, and project scope documents.
  • Perform quantity takeoffs from construction drawings.
  • Estimate sitework, excavation, concrete, and foundation materials.
  • Calculate framing lumber, sheathing, and structural material quantities.
  • Estimate roofing, envelope, insulation, and drywall materials.
  • Calculate interior finish, cabinetry, flooring, and paint quantities.
  • Develop allowances for plumbing, electrical, and HVAC systems.
  • Estimate exterior construction materials and site improvements.
  • Prepare professional residential construction material estimates.
