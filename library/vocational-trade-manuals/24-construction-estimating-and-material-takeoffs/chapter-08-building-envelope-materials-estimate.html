Job Overview

This project qualifies you to produce a complete exterior-envelope material takeoff for one residential project using the workbook built in Project 01. The finished work product is one coordinated envelope estimate covering the water-resistive barrier, flashing at every penetration and transition, windows and exterior doors, siding, trim, soffit, fascia, accessories, sealants, fasteners, access, and labor, reconciled against the wall area and opening schedule and priced from dated quotations.

You will measure gross wall area by elevation, calculate WRB and flashing quantities from wall area and opening perimeter, count and specify windows and exterior doors by tag, calculate siding by the approved net or gross method, measure trim by opening and elevation, calculate fasteners and sealant from approved schedules and joint geometry, price every material from dated quotations, reconcile the takeoff against wall area and opening counts, and document trade boundaries and unresolved compatibility questions before the estimate advances to Chapter 9.

This project follows the roofing estimate in Chapter 7 and precedes the insulation and air-sealing estimate in Chapter 9. The wall area, opening schedule, and flashing details established here carry directly into the insulation, drywall, and interior finish quantities developed later in the book, and the water-management scope documented here becomes part of the risk register carried into Chapter 18 pricing and Chapter 20 capstone review.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • ET-043 — Estimate house wrap from wall area, laps, corners, transitions, roll size, and waste.
  • ET-044 — Estimate flashing tape and related flashing products from required width, application, and joint geometry.
  • ET-045 — Estimate windows and exterior doors by tag, type, size, and confirmed pricing scope.
  • ET-046 — Estimate siding by the approved net or gross method with product-specific waste.
  • ET-047 — Estimate exterior trim, soffit, fascia, and accessories by elevation and stock length.
  • ET-048 — Prepare an exterior envelope takeoff reconciled against wall area, openings, and trim length, with labor, access, and total cost.

Where This Skill Fits

The roofing estimate in Chapter 7 establishes the roof-to-wall interface; this project extends that water-management scope down every elevation. The wall area and opening perimeter measured here feed directly into the insulation and air-sealing estimate in Chapter 9, the exterior WRB and flashing quantities inform the drywall and interior finish coordination in later chapters, and the window and door schedule confirmed here is the same schedule referenced by the finish-carpentry estimate in Chapter 11.

The compatibility and trade-boundary discipline built into this chapter — confirming WRB, flashing, siding, fasteners, and sealants work together as one system rather than as unrelated line items — is the same discipline the estimate depends on in Chapter 18, where waste, freight, tax, and markup are applied to a cost basis that must already be internally consistent.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • measuring gross wall area by elevation, including gables, bump-outs, dormers, and irregular elevations
  • calculating WRB, flashing tape, and related flashing products from wall area, opening perimeter, and joint geometry
  • counting and specifying windows and exterior doors by tag, type, size, glazing, operation, rating, frame, finish, and hardware
  • calculating siding, corners, starter, channels, and rainscreen or furring components by the approved method
  • measuring trim, soffit, fascia, and accessories by elevation and stock length
  • calculating fasteners and sealant from approved schedules and joint geometry
  • pricing every envelope material and confirming scope from dated quotations
  • reconciling the takeoff against wall area, openings, and trim length, and documenting trade boundaries

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select a wall assembly, WRB system, flashing detail, or siding product not shown on the approved elevations, wall sections, or specifications
  • invent window or door sizes, ratings, or hardware groups not shown on the approved opening schedule
  • combine incompatible WRB, flashing, sealant, and fastener products without a manufacturer compatibility confirmation
  • price rough-opening framing, structural sheathing, or roofing scope, which are covered in Chapters 5, 6, and 7

Any unresolved flashing, compatibility, or trade-boundary question must be recorded and resolved before this estimate advances to pricing in Chapter 18.

Trade Note

An envelope estimate that prices WRB, flashing, siding, and trim as unrelated line items without confirming they form one compatible water-management system is the most common source of a callback that the original estimate never priced. Compatibility confirmation is not optional and belongs in the workbook's assumptions field, not only in the estimator's memory.

Setup Standard

Do not begin siding or trim quantity calculations until gross wall area, opening count, and opening perimeter have been measured and reconciled against the elevations. A siding quantity built on an unreconciled wall area must be corrected before pricing begins.

Shop Tip

Measure WRB and flashing quantities from the same wall-area and opening-perimeter figures used for siding, rather than remeasuring each product separately. A second independent measurement is for reconciliation, not for building the primary takeoff twice from different sources.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every envelope estimate is built from the same four measured quantities: gross wall area by elevation, opening count and perimeter, siding and trim linear or area quantities converted to purchase units, and a reconciliation that ties all three together before pricing. Building the takeoff in this order prevents a siding or WRB quantity from being calculated before the opening schedule that must reduce or inform it has been confirmed.

Measuring Gross Wall Area and Opening Perimeter

Measure gross wall area by elevation before applying any opening deduction. Include gables, bump-outs, dormers, and irregular elevations as separate measured areas rather than estimating them as a percentage addition to a simpler elevation. Record the opening perimeter for every window and door at the same time the opening is counted, since the same perimeter figure supports flashing tape, casing, and trim quantities later in the takeoff.

Traceability Cue — Record Elevation and Revision With Every Area

Record the elevation identifier and the document date and revision with every gross wall area entered. A wall area with no recorded elevation or revision source cannot be reconciled against a later elevation revision and must be treated as unverified until the source is added.

Critical Control Cue — Confirm the Deduction Rule Before Calculating Siding

Confirm whether the approved method deducts openings from siding and WRB quantities, and apply that rule consistently across every elevation. Switching between net and gross methods within the same takeoff will silently produce an incorrect total that the reconciliation step in Section H is specifically designed to catch, but only if the rule was documented at the start.

Documentation Cue — Record Every Trade Boundary

Record whether window and door pricing includes field glazing, installation, trim, and warranty service, and record which trade installs siding, trim, and flashing where more than one trade is involved. An undocumented trade boundary is the most common source of a duplicated or missing cost when the estimate is priced in Chapter 18.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Confirm Compatibility Before Pricing

Confirm WRB, flashing tape, liquid flashing, sealant, and fastener compatibility with the manufacturer's published system before pricing any of these products. Do not assume a lower-priced substitute is compatible without a manufacturer confirmation in the document register.

C. Product Categories and Waste Basis

  • WRB: calculated from wall area, laps, corners, and transitions, converted to roll count, with waste based on roll width, wall height, and cut condition.
  • Flashing tape and liquid flashing: calculated from required width and application at sill, jamb, head, corner, band, penetration, and roof-to-wall conditions; not substituted with a generic tape width without confirming the specified width.
  • Windows and exterior doors: priced by tag as scheduled; do not aggregate dissimilar units into a single average unit price.
  • Siding: calculated by the approved net or gross method, converted into courses, panels, boards, cartons, or squares using actual product coverage, with waste based on length, panel layout, pattern, openings, color, damage, and attic stock.
  • Trim, soffit, and fascia: calculated by width, thickness, material, stock length, coating, and exposure, with waste based on stock length and cut condition.
  • Fasteners and sealant: calculated from the manufacturer's fastener schedule and approved joint geometry; sealant is not applied at intentional drainage openings or laps.

E. Preparation — Documents and Assembly Confirmation

  1. Review elevations, wall sections, opening schedules, envelope details, siding details, specifications, and manufacturer instructions. Confirm the current revision of each against the document register built in Chapter 2.
  2. Identify wall assembly, WRB, rainscreen, flashing, siding, trim, soffit, fascia, sealant, and coating systems. Record the confirmed product for each without substituting an unspecified alternative.
  3. Separate materials by elevation, exposure, product, color, profile, and phase. Create one takeoff row group per elevation before measuring any quantity.

F. Wall Area, Openings, and Water-Management Measurement

  1. Measure gross wall area including gables, bump-outs, dormers, and irregular elevations. Record the source elevation and revision for each measured area.
  2. Track openings by tag, size, type, and perimeter. Reconcile the opening count against the approved opening schedule before continuing.
  3. Calculate WRB from wall area, laps, corners, transitions, roll size, and waste. Convert to whole roll counts.
  4. Include compatible tape, cap fasteners, liquid flashing, primers, sealants, and membranes. Confirm each product's manufacturer compatibility before adding it to the takeoff.
  5. Measure sill, jamb, head, corner, band, penetration, and roof-to-wall flashing. Record each flashing condition separately by opening or transition.
  6. Calculate flashing products by required width and application. Do not substitute a generic width without confirming the specified detail.
  7. Count windows and doors by tag, type, size, glazing, operation, rating, frame, finish, and hardware. Reconcile the count against the opening schedule from Step 2.
  8. Identify mulled units, transoms, sidelights, specialty glass, screens, jamb extensions, and accessories. Add each as a separate line where priced separately by the supplier.
  9. Confirm whether pricing includes freight, field glazing, installation, trim, and warranty service. Record the confirmed scope on the takeoff sheet and in the assumptions field.

G. Siding, Trim, and Accessory Measurement

  1. Measure siding by the approved net or gross method. Apply the deduction rule documented in the header record consistently across every elevation.
  2. Convert area into courses, panels, boards, cartons, or squares using actual coverage. Round to whole purchase units at the final conversion step only.
  3. Apply waste based on length, panel layout, pattern, openings, color, damage, and attic stock. Record the waste basis used for each siding product.
  4. Measure corners, starter, channels, utility trim, furring, rainscreen, and mounting blocks. Count each as a separate line item with its own unit and source.
  5. Measure trim at openings, corners, bands, frieze, water table, soffit, fascia, rake, columns, and transitions. Use the opening perimeter recorded in Section F for opening-related trim.
  6. Separate trim by width, thickness, material, stock length, coating, and exposure. Convert linear quantities into stock lengths with cut optimization.
  7. Calculate soffit and fascia with ventilation accessories. Include net free-area requirements only where specified.
  8. Count vents, louvers, blocks, meter mounts, light blocks, receptacle blocks, and penetration accessories. Record each by elevation and quantity.
  9. Calculate fasteners by material, substrate, wind, corrosion, and manufacturer schedule. Do not use a generic fastener count without the manufacturer's spacing schedule.
  10. Calculate sealant by approved joint geometry and cartridge yield. Do not include sealant at intentional drainage openings or laps.
  11. Include factory finish, field primer, cut-edge treatment, paint, stain, and touch-up. Record only where included in the specification or an approved quotation.

H. Access, Labor, Pricing, and Reconciliation

  1. Include scaffolds, lifts, pump jacks, fall protection, weather protection, and public protection. Base access equipment on wall height and site conditions.
  2. Estimate labor by WRB, flashing, openings, siding, trim, soffit, fascia, sealant, and coating. Enter production rates only from company history, verified references, or documented judgment.
  3. Adjust production for height, openings, material weight, cutting, complexity, and weather. Record the adjustment basis for each affected task.
  4. Price materials from dated quotations confirming product, color, profile, warranty, lead time, and freight. Log every quotation in the workbook's quotation fields.
  5. Reconcile wall area, openings, siding quantity, and trim length. Compare the siding order quantity against wall area independently before accepting the takeoff.
  6. Prepare the takeoff by elevation, layer, product, quantity, waste, labor, access, and total. Confirm every row links to the correct workbook cost-code section.
  7. Document trade boundaries and unresolved flashing or compatibility questions. Enter each in the assumptions, exclusions, and risk register built in Chapter 1.
  8. Complete independent review of openings, transitions, and accessories. Have a second reviewer confirm the opening count, flashing conditions, and accessory quantities against the source documents.

Common Envelope Inspection

Inspect the finished envelope takeoff using the same checks regardless of which elevation or product category is being reviewed.

  • Wall area check: confirm gross wall area for every elevation traces to the current elevation revision and includes gables, bump-outs, and dormers.
  • Opening check: confirm the opening count and tags match the approved opening schedule exactly.
  • Deduction-rule check: confirm the same net or gross deduction rule was applied to every elevation.
  • Compatibility check: confirm WRB, flashing, sealant, and fastener products are confirmed compatible by the manufacturer.
  • Reconciliation check: confirm siding order quantity and trim length were independently reconciled against wall area and opening perimeter.
  • Pricing check: confirm every priced item has a dated quotation recorded in the workbook.
  • Documentation check: confirm trade boundaries and unresolved questions are recorded in the assumptions and risk register.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Siding Order Quantity Does Not Reconcile With Wall Area

Probable cause: the net or gross deduction rule was applied inconsistently across elevations, or a gable, bump-out, or dormer area was omitted from the gross wall area.
Confirmation: recalculate wall area per elevation using the documented deduction rule and compare against the siding order quantity's source calculation.
Correction: reapply the documented deduction rule consistently and add any omitted elevation area.
Reinspection: repeat the Section H, Step 5 reconciliation after correction.

Opening Count Does Not Match the Approved Opening Schedule

Probable cause: the takeoff was built from an elevation drawing without cross-checking the opening schedule, or a schedule revision was issued after the takeoff began.
Confirmation: compare the takeoff's opening tags one-by-one against the current opening schedule revision recorded in the document register.
Correction: update the opening count, tags, and related flashing and casing quantities to match the current schedule.
Reinspection: repeat the opening check and confirm the document register reflects the schedule revision used.

Flashing Tape Width Does Not Match the Specified Detail

Probable cause: a generic or previously used tape width was applied without confirming the current wall-section detail.
Confirmation: compare the specified flashing width in the wall-section detail against the width entered in the takeoff.
Correction: correct the tape width and recalculate the flashing tape length and cost using the specified width.
Reinspection: repeat the flashing quantity check against the wall-section detail.

A Product Substitution Was Priced Without a Compatibility Confirmation

Probable cause: a lower-priced WRB, flashing, sealant, or fastener product was substituted based on price alone, without checking manufacturer compatibility documentation.
Confirmation: review the document register for a manufacturer compatibility confirmation covering the substituted product; absence of a recorded confirmation confirms the error.
Correction: obtain and record a manufacturer compatibility confirmation, or revert to the specified product.
Reinspection: repeat the compatibility check for every substituted product before pricing is finalized.

Window or Door Pricing Scope Is Unclear

Probable cause: the quotation did not clearly state whether freight, field glazing, installation, trim, and warranty service are included.
Confirmation: review the quotation for explicit statements on each scope item; a quotation silent on any item confirms the gap.
Correction: request written confirmation from the supplier for every unclear scope item and record the response in the quotation log.
Reinspection: repeat the Section F, Step 9 scope-confirmation check before the quotation is used for pricing.

Trim Linear Quantity Does Not Account for Opening Perimeter

Probable cause: trim was measured by elevation run length only, without adding the casing length required at each opening perimeter.
Confirmation: compare the trim linear quantity against the sum of elevation runs plus the opening perimeters recorded in Section F, Step 2.
Correction: add the missing opening-perimeter trim length and recalculate stock-length conversion and waste.
Reinspection: repeat the trim reconciliation in Section H, Step 5.

Field Terms

WRB
Water-resistive barrier; the primary drainage-plane material applied over wall sheathing.
Gross Wall Area
Total wall area by elevation before any opening deduction is applied.
Net Wall Area
Gross wall area after openings are deducted according to the approved method.
Opening Perimeter
The total linear measurement around a window or door rough opening.
Rainscreen
A furred air gap between the WRB and the siding that allows drainage and drying.
Compatible System
WRB, flashing, sealant, and fastener products confirmed by the manufacturer to work together as installed.
Trade Boundary
The documented line separating scope priced in one trade or chapter from scope priced elsewhere.
Cartridge Yield
The linear coverage a single sealant or adhesive cartridge provides at a specified bead size.
Attic Stock
Extra material set aside for future repair, included as part of approved waste where specified.
Reconciliation
Comparing a major quantity by an independent method or reviewer to confirm accuracy.
