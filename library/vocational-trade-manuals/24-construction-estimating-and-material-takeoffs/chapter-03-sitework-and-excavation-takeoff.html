Job Overview

This project qualifies you to produce the sitework and excavation takeoff for a residential estimate. The finished work product is one documented sitework takeoff entered into the Chapter 1 workbook, covering clearing and grubbing, topsoil stripping, cut and fill, building excavation, utility and drainage trenching, structural and common fill, aggregate, haul-out and import loads, temporary access and erosion control, final grading and restoration, and the equipment and labor supporting that scope.

You will confirm datum, contours, benchmark, and scale; define takeoff zones; measure disturbed, clearing, and stripping areas; calculate cut and fill by an approved method; calculate building excavation and trench volumes; separate fill and aggregate types; convert compacted volume into purchase quantity; calculate haul-out and import loads; measure temporary access and erosion-control devices; identify equipment and price sources; and reconcile the completed takeoff by an independent method before pricing begins.

This project opens the Chapter 1 master workbook copy created for this project and enters the first real quantities of the book into the sitework cost-code section. Every later chapter, through the Chapter 20 capstone, follows this same measure-document-reconcile pattern for its own trade.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • ET-013 — Measure disturbed areas by zone with a recorded datum, scale, and source.
  • ET-014 — Calculate excavation quantities for building, utility, and drainage scope using an approved method.
  • ET-015 — Estimate fill requirements, separating structural, common, select, and topsoil fill.
  • ET-016 — Calculate gravel base and aggregate quantities converted to purchase units.
  • ET-017 — Identify hauling needs, including haul-out, import, and load-count calculations.
  • ET-018 — Prepare a sitework takeoff summary with reconciled quantities, assumptions, risks, and pricing sources.

Where This Skill Fits

This is the first quantity takeoff chapter in the book and the first chapter to enter real data into the Chapter 1 workbook. The document register, scope map, and clarification log from Chapter 2 identify exactly which site, grading, utility, and geotechnical documents control this takeoff and which excavation, hauling, or restoration items remain unresolved. The reconciliation method built here — an independent cut, fill, and haul check — is the same method every later takeoff chapter repeats for its own trade, and the sitework cost-code section completed here becomes one input to the Chapter 17 labor estimate and the Chapter 18 pricing worksheet.

Concrete quantities in Chapter 4 depend on the building-pad elevations and excavation established here. Exterior-work quantities in Chapter 16 depend on the grading and drainage assumptions recorded here.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing site, grading, utility, drainage, landscape, erosion-control, and geotechnical documents
  • measuring disturbed, clearing, grubbing, and topsoil-stripping areas
  • calculating cut, fill, building excavation, and trench volumes by an approved method
  • calculating structural fill, common fill, select fill, aggregate, and topsoil separately
  • converting compacted volume into purchase quantity and calculating haul-out and import loads
  • measuring temporary access, erosion-control devices, and final grading and restoration where assigned
  • identifying equipment, labor, and pricing sources and reconciling the completed takeoff

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • perform actual excavation, grading, hauling, or erosion-control installation; this chapter is a takeoff and cost estimate only
  • assume shrink, swell, compaction, moisture, or unsuitable-soil values that are not supplied by an approved geotechnical report or documented company history
  • design drainage, grading, or utility routing not shown on the approved documents
  • finalize hauling, disposal, or import pricing without a dated, sourced quotation

Any takeoff quantity that cannot trace to a document, field condition, approved assumption, or quotation must be flagged in the clarification log carried forward from Chapter 2 rather than estimated by guess.

Trade Note

Sitework quantities set the shape of every excavation-dependent quantity later in the estimate. A cut-and-fill volume that does not reconcile with an independent check at this stage is far cheaper to correct here than after concrete, framing, and drainage quantities have been built on top of it.

Takeoff Standard

Do not enter a cut, fill, or haul quantity into the workbook until its source document, method, and datum are recorded. A quantity with no recorded method cannot be reconciled and must be treated as unverified until the method is added.

Shop Tip

Measure disturbed area first, before calculating any volume. Every stripping, cut, fill, and haul quantity in this chapter is a subset of the disturbed area, and an area measured too small or too large will propagate into every volume calculated from it.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every volume calculated in this chapter follows the same pattern: measure a defined zone against a verified datum and scale, apply the approved calculation method, document shrink, swell, compaction, and moisture assumptions from the geotechnical report, convert to a purchase or haul unit, and reconcile the result against an independent method before it is priced.

Building padFooting, wall, slab, crawlspace, basement, and overdig excavation
Driveway and walksSubgrade, base, and drainage excavation and fill
Utilities and drainageTrench length, width, depth, bedding, and backfill

Cut, Fill, and Volume Method Selection

Select one approved volume method for the entire takeoff — grid method, surface model, cross-section method, or average-end-area method — and record which method was used and why. Do not switch methods between zones without documenting the reason, and do not average results from two different methods together as if they were the same measurement.

Traceability Cue — Record Datum, Scale, and Method With Every Volume

Record the datum, verified scale, and calculation method used for every area and volume the moment it is calculated, not afterward. A volume without a recorded method cannot be reconciled by an independent reviewer and must be treated as unverified until the method is added.

Critical Control Cue — Do Not Combine Cut, Fill, Import, and Export Without Reconciliation

Cut, fill, stockpile, import, and export quantities must reconcile to the same site balance before pricing. A cut-and-fill takeoff that has not been reconciled by an independent method can silently overstate or understate hauling, base, and structural-fill costs by a wide margin without producing any visible error in the workbook.

Geotechnical Cue — Use Only Reported Shrink, Swell, and Compaction Values

Apply shrink, swell, compaction, moisture, and unsuitable-soil assumptions only from the identified geotechnical report or documented company history for comparable soil conditions. Do not invent a shrink or swell percentage when no geotechnical report is available; flag the gap as an unresolved clarification instead.

Documentation Cue — Separate Rock, Groundwater, and Unsuitable Soil as Risk Items

Identify rock, groundwater, contamination, buried debris, and unsuitable soil as risk items or unit-price items rather than folding an assumed allowance into the base excavation quantity. Record each as a separate line with its own basis and pricing treatment.

D. Preparation — Documents, Datum, and Zones

  1. Review site, grading, utility, foundation, drainage, landscape, erosion-control, and geotechnical information. Confirm each document is the current revision per the Chapter 2 document register.
  2. Confirm datum, existing and proposed contours, benchmark, finished-floor elevation, and scale. Record these on the site reference and datum record before measuring any zone.
  3. Identify property, easement, utility, septic, well, tree-protection, and no-disturbance limits. Mark these boundaries on the working takeoff document so no measured zone crosses them.
  4. Define inclusions and exclusions for demolition, clearing, stripping, excavation, trenching, grading, hauling, and restoration. Cross-reference the Chapter 2 scope map and carry forward any unresolved clarification.
  5. Create takeoff zones for building pad, driveway, walks, patios, utilities, drainage, landscaping, and stockpiles. Use the zones listed in Section B.

E. Area, Stripping, and Cut/Fill Calculation

  1. Measure total disturbed area and record sources. Enter the source document, page or sheet reference, and scale used for each zone.
  2. Measure clearing and grubbing areas by vegetation and access condition. Separate light, moderate, and heavy clearing where the documents distinguish them.
  3. Measure topsoil stripping area and apply the approved depth. Use the depth stated in the documents or geotechnical report; do not assume a depth.
  4. Calculate stripped volume and identify reuse, stockpile, export, or disposal. Record the destination for every cubic yard of stripped topsoil.
  5. Measure cut and fill using the approved grid, surface, section, or average-end-area method. Use the single method selected in the Common Field Fundamentals section.
  6. Document shrink, swell, compaction, moisture, and unsuitable-soil assumptions. Cite the geotechnical report section and date for each value used.

F. Excavation, Trenching, and Fill Quantities

  1. Calculate building excavation by footing, wall, slab, crawlspace, basement, and overdig geometry. Reference the approved foundation plan and section details.
  2. Calculate utility and drainage trenches by length, width, depth, bedding, and backfill zone. Separate trench excavation from bedding and backfill material.
  3. Calculate structural fill, common fill, select fill, aggregate, and topsoil separately. Do not combine fill types into a single quantity line.
  4. Convert compacted volume to purchase quantity using the approved basis. Apply the compaction factor documented in Step 6 and record the resulting purchase unit.
  5. Identify geotextile, stabilization, dewatering, shoring, testing, and trench-protection scope. Record each as its own line with its own basis.
  6. Identify rock, groundwater, contamination, buried debris, and unsuitable soil as risks or unit-price items. Follow the Critical Control Cue and Geotechnical Cue above.

G. Hauling, Access, and Restoration

  1. Calculate haul-out volume and load count using approved truck capacities. Convert loose (haul) volume into load count using the truck-capacity reference.
  2. Calculate import loads for fill, aggregate, and topsoil. Use the same truck-capacity reference and purchase-unit conversions.
  3. Include loading, travel, queue, disposal, tipping, washout, and return assumptions. Record the source and date for each assumption.
  4. Measure temporary access, stabilized entrance, tracking control, fencing, signage, and erosion-control devices. Use the erosion-control plan as the controlling document.
  5. Measure final grading, topsoil, seed, sod, mulch, and landscape restoration where assigned. Confirm assignment against the Chapter 2 scope map before including this quantity.
  6. Identify equipment, attachments, mobilization, standby, fuel, operators, and minimum rentals. Record equipment type, source, and rate basis without inventing a rental rate.
  7. Include survey, layout, testing, permits, utility locating, and traffic control. Record each as its own workbook line.

H. Pricing, Reconciliation, and Review

  1. Price materials, hauling, disposal, equipment, labor, and subcontract work from dated sources. Record the source, date, and expiration for each price entered.
  2. Reconcile cut, fill, stockpile, import, and export quantities. Confirm the site material balance closes; investigate and document any unresolved variance.
  3. Review access, weather, working room, neighboring property, and schedule effects. Record any assumption affecting productivity or sequencing.
  4. Prepare the takeoff summary with quantities, assumptions, risks, pricing sources, and exclusions. Use the sitework cost-code section of the Chapter 1 workbook as the summary location.
  5. Complete an independent volume and haul review. Have a second method or reviewer confirm the cut, fill, and haul totals before the takeoff is closed out.

Common Takeoff Inspection

Inspect the finished sitework takeoff using the same checks regardless of which zone or fill type is being reviewed.

  • Datum check: confirm every measured area and volume references the same recorded benchmark, scale, and datum.
  • Method check: confirm the same approved volume method was used throughout, or that any deviation is documented and justified.
  • Fill-separation check: confirm structural fill, common fill, select fill, aggregate, and topsoil remain in separate quantity lines.
  • Balance check: confirm cut, fill, stockpile, import, and export quantities reconcile to a documented site balance.
  • Source check: confirm every quantity, assumption, and price has a recorded source and date.
  • Risk check: confirm rock, groundwater, unsuitable soil, and contamination are recorded as separate risk or unit-price items, not buried in base excavation.

Complete the Sitework Takeoff Verification Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 04.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Cut and Fill Do Not Reconcile With the Independent Check

Probable cause: two different volume methods were mixed between zones, the datum shifted between measurements, or a zone was measured twice or omitted.
Confirmation: recheck each zone's recorded datum, scale, and method against the Section E and F entries and compare zone boundaries for overlap or gaps.
Correction: remeasure the affected zones using the single approved method and datum, and remove any duplicated or missing zone.
Reinspection: repeat the Section H, Step 2 reconciliation and confirm the site balance closes.

Haul-Out Load Count Does Not Match the Excavation Volume

Probable cause: the conversion from in-place (bank) volume to loose (haul) volume was skipped, or the wrong truck capacity was applied.
Confirmation: trace the haul calculation back to the excavation volume and confirm the loose-volume conversion factor was applied before dividing by truck capacity.
Correction: reapply the correct loose-volume conversion and recalculate the load count using the documented truck-capacity reference.
Reinspection: repeat the haul-load calculation and compare it against the reconciled excavation volume.

A Fill Quantity Combines Two Fill Types Into One Line

Probable cause: structural fill and common fill were measured as a single zone without separating the specified fill type by location.
Confirmation: compare the workbook fill line against the foundation and grading plan to confirm which areas require structural versus common fill.
Correction: split the combined line into separate structural-fill and common-fill lines with their own areas, depths, and compaction factors.
Reinspection: repeat the fill-separation check from the Common Takeoff Inspection section.

No Shrink or Swell Factor Is Documented for a Fill Quantity

Probable cause: the geotechnical report was not consulted before the compacted-to-purchase conversion was built, or no geotechnical report is available for the project.
Confirmation: check whether the fill line cites a geotechnical report section and date; absence of a citation confirms the error.
Correction: locate and cite the correct geotechnical report value, or, if none exists, flag the fill quantity as an unresolved clarification rather than assuming a value.
Reinspection: repeat the source check on the corrected fill line.

Rock or Unsuitable Soil Cost Is Buried Inside the Base Excavation Price

Probable cause: an allowance for difficult excavation conditions was added directly to the unit price instead of being separated as its own risk or unit-price line.
Confirmation: compare the base excavation unit price against a documented baseline rate for normal soil conditions; an inflated rate with no separate risk line confirms the error.
Correction: remove the hidden allowance from the base unit price and create a separate rock, groundwater, or unsuitable-soil risk or unit-price line.
Reinspection: repeat the risk check from the Common Takeoff Inspection section.

Field Terms

Datum
The fixed elevation reference point all cut, fill, and grading elevations are measured against.
Bank Volume
The volume of material measured in its original, undisturbed (in-place) condition.
Loose Volume
The volume of material after excavation, expanded from its bank state, used for hauling calculations.
Compacted Volume
The volume of material after placement and compaction, used for structural and common fill design.
Shrink/Swell Factor
The documented ratio converting between bank, loose, and compacted volume states.
Overdig
Additional excavation width or depth beyond the neat structural line required for forming, working room, or drainage.
Haul Load
One truck's capacity of hauled material, used to convert volume into a load count.
Structural Fill
Engineered fill placed and compacted to a specified density under structures or pavement.
Stockpile
Material temporarily stored on site for reuse, pending disposal, or pending haul-out.
Site Balance
The reconciliation of cut, fill, stockpile, import, and export quantities to confirm the takeoff is complete and consistent.
