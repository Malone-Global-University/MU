Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare one complete residential exterior-work estimate covering decks, stairs, guards, fences, gates, pavers, walls, walks, patios, drainage, planting, soil, mulch, irrigation changes, and restoration. The finished work product is a reconciled exterior-work section of the Chapter 1 workbook containing measured quantities, purchase-unit conversions, waste, equipment, labor, and pricing for every zone identified on the site and landscape documents, plus a risk register addressing concealed and seasonal conditions.

You will review site, landscape, grading, drainage, structural, deck, fence, planting, and irrigation documents, confirm property and regulatory constraints, measure and calculate quantities for decks, fences, hardscape, planting, soil, and mulch, price equipment and labor, and reconcile every zone before completing the estimate.

Exterior work is priced last among the takeoff chapters because it depends on site conditions, drainage decisions, and finish selections that are often confirmed only after the building shell and interior scope are settled. This chapter enters its quantities into the exterior-work cost-code section of the same workbook built in Chapter 1 and feeds directly into the Chapter 17 labor estimate and the Chapter 18 pricing summary.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • ET-091 — Estimate deck materials, including framing, footings, connectors, decking, guards, and stairs.
  • ET-092 — Estimate fence materials, including posts, rails, panels, pickets, gates, and hardware.
  • ET-093 — Estimate pavers and base materials, including excavation, geotextile, base, bedding, and joint material.
  • ET-094 — Estimate planting materials, including plant counts, substitution assumptions, and establishment requirements.
  • ET-095 — Estimate mulch and soil, including area, depth, settlement, and supplier unit conversion.
  • ET-096 — Prepare an exterior work estimate integrating decks, fences, hardscape, planting, soil, mulch, equipment, labor, and site-risk controls.

Where This Skill Fits

This project uses the workbook structure from Chapter 1, the plan-review discipline from Chapter 2, and the sitework and grading quantities from Chapter 3. It applies the same waste, conversion, and traceability controls used in Chapters 4 through 15 to a different set of assemblies: decks, fences, hardscape, and planting.

The exterior-work quantities produced here feed Chapter 17's labor estimate, Chapter 18's pricing and indirect-cost calculation, and, where exterior work is included in the capstone scope, the Chapter 20 integrated estimate. Any allowance, unit price, or exclusion created here must appear in the Chapter 19 bid proposal without unexplained differences.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing site, landscape, grading, drainage, structural, deck, fence, planting, and irrigation documents and confirming regulatory constraints
  • measuring and calculating deck, stair, guard, and fence quantities including footings, connectors, and hardware
  • measuring and calculating paver, slab, stone, and hardscape area, base, bedding, and joint material
  • counting plants and calculating mulch, soil, and amendment quantities
  • pricing equipment, labor, and materials from dated local sources and leveled subcontractor proposals
  • reconciling areas, linear quantities, volumes, and plant counts and documenting seasonal and hidden-condition assumptions

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • design deck framing, footing sizes, or guard systems beyond what the structural documents and manufacturer span tables specify
  • substitute plant species, hardscape materials, or fence products without a documented approved substitution
  • price irrigation, retaining-wall engineering, or drainage redesign scope that has not been assigned in the documents
  • issue this workbook section as a client-facing proposal; that document is prepared separately in Chapter 19

Any zone left unresolved after the document review in Section E must be converted into an approved assumption, allowance, unit price, or exclusion before pricing continues.

Trade Note

Exterior work is the estimate section most often distorted by hidden site conditions: rock, roots, unsuitable soil, and utility conflicts. This chapter does not authorize guessing at those conditions. Convert every unresolved site condition into a documented allowance, unit price, or contingency line rather than a silent assumption buried in a waste factor.

Reconciliation Standard

Do not price any exterior-work zone until its area, linear quantity, volume, or plant count has been reconciled against the site and landscape plans by an independent method. A zone that has not been reconciled must be flagged and corrected before pricing continues.

Shop Tip

Price aggregate, soil, and mulch only after confirming the supplier's coverage basis for the stated compacted or settled depth. Loose-volume and compacted-volume coverage figures are not interchangeable, and mixing them silently understates the order quantity.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every exterior-work zone in this chapter follows the same sequence: confirm constraints, measure or count, convert to purchase units, apply zone-specific waste, price from dated sources, and reconcile. Applying this sequence consistently across decks, fences, hardscape, and planting prevents one zone's assumptions from silently contaminating another.

Zone Separation and Cross-Contamination

Keep deck, fence, hardscape, planting, soil, and mulch quantities in separate zones within the exterior-work cost-code section, even where they share a common material such as concrete or aggregate. A shared material used across zones must still be tracked separately by zone so that a change to one zone's scope does not silently change another zone's quantity.

Traceability Cue — Record the Source for Every Zone Quantity

Enter a source for every deck, fence, hardscape, and planting quantity at the moment it is measured or counted. A quantity without a recorded plan sheet, schedule, or field measurement is unverified and must not be used for pricing until a source is added.

Critical Control Cue — Do Not Interchange Loose and Compacted Volumes

Confirm whether a supplier's coverage figure for aggregate, soil, or mulch is stated as loose volume or compacted or settled volume before calculating order quantity. Using a loose-volume coverage rate against a compacted-depth requirement will understate the order quantity and cause a mid-project shortage.

Regulatory Alert — Confirm Setback and Permit Status Before Pricing

Confirm setback, easement, HOA, and permit requirements for decks, fences, and hardscape before finalizing quantities. A structure shown on the plans that does not meet a locally verified setback or permit requirement must be flagged as a clarification, not priced as shown.

Supplier Cue — Confirm Plant Substitution and Availability Terms

Confirm the nursery's substitution, availability, warranty, and mortality-replacement terms before pricing the planting schedule. Do not assume a scheduled species, size, or container will be available at the pricing date without a dated confirmation.

C. Waste and Conversion Basis

  • Deck and fence lumber: convert net lengths to stock lengths and apply waste based on stock length, defects, and framing complexity, consistent with the Chapter 5 framing conventions.
  • Hardscape units: apply waste based on pattern, border, curve, and cut complexity; do not apply a single waste rate to both rectangular and curved layouts.
  • Aggregate and soil: convert compacted or settled depth into purchase volume using the supplier's stated compaction or settlement factor; never apply a loose-volume factor to a compacted-depth requirement.
  • Plants: apply no waste factor to plant counts; instead document a separate mortality-replacement assumption from the nursery's stated terms.
  • Mulch: apply waste based on bed shape, edging condition, and supplier bag or bulk unit.

E. Preparation — Documents and Constraints

  1. Review site, landscape, grading, drainage, structural, deck, fence, planting, and irrigation documents. Confirm the controlling revision for each before measuring any zone.
  2. Confirm property, setback, easement, utility, access, HOA, permit, and environmental constraints. Record each confirmed constraint on the exterior-work scope record.
  3. Separate deck, stair, guard, fence, gate, paver, wall, walk, patio, drainage, planting, soil, mulch, irrigation, and restoration into distinct zones. Do not combine zones that will be priced, waste-adjusted, or scheduled differently.

F. Deck and Fence Takeoff

  1. Measure deck footprint, framing grid, beams, joists, posts, stairs, guards, screens, and decking. Record the source plan sheet or detail for each measured element.
  2. Count footings, bases, connectors, fasteners, fascia, rails, and finishes. Confirm footing size and depth against the structural detail rather than assuming a standard footing.
  3. Include treatment compatibility, field treatment, delivery, lifting, and waste. Confirm fastener and connector compatibility with the specified treated-lumber chemical.
  4. Measure fence length by type, height, material, grade, and wind exposure. Separate each fence type and height into its own line.
  5. Count posts, rails, panels, pickets, caps, concrete, gates, hinges, latches, braces, and hardware. Calculate post count from length divided by approved spacing per Section D, adding corner and gate posts separately.

G. Hardscape Takeoff

  1. Measure paver, slab, stone, and hardscape area by pattern, border, curve, cut, and waste. Record the source dimension or verified scale for each area.
  2. Calculate excavation, geotextile, base, bedding, pavers, joint material, edging, and compaction. Confirm each layer's specified thickness before calculating volume.
  3. Calculate aggregate and soil by compacted depth and approved conversion. Apply the Section C conversion basis and confirm it against the supplier's stated coverage.
  4. Identify drainage, slope, step, retaining, utility, root, and accessibility conditions. Flag any condition not clearly assigned in the documents as a clarification.

H. Planting, Soil, and Mulch Takeoff

  1. Count plants by schedule, size, container, species, and location. Reconcile the plant count against the planting plan symbol legend.
  2. Identify substitution, availability, warranty, establishment, and mortality assumptions. Record the nursery's dated terms for each on the scope record.
  3. Measure beds, lawn, seed, sod, mulch, topsoil, compost, amendment, and edging. Record area and the source plan sheet for each.
  4. Calculate mulch and soil by area, depth, settlement, and supplier unit. Apply the Section C conversion basis and confirm settled versus as-placed depth.
  5. Include tree protection, clearing, demolition, hauling, excavation, backfill, grading, and restoration. Confirm these are assigned to this chapter's scope and not carried in the Chapter 3 sitework takeoff.
  6. Include irrigation changes only where assigned. Do not price irrigation scope that has not been explicitly assigned in the documents; flag unassigned irrigation work as a clarification.

I. Equipment, Labor, and Pricing

  1. Identify augers, compactors, excavators, skid steers, trenchers, saws, mixers, lifts, and trucks. Match each piece of equipment to the zone and task requiring it.
  2. Include equipment mobilization, attachments, fuel, operator, standby, and minimum rental. Record the rental source and date for each rate.
  3. Include traffic, pedestrian, neighbor, weather, heat, cold, and site-protection controls. Identify which controls apply to which zone.
  4. Estimate labor by layout, excavation, footing, framing, fence, hardscape, planting, irrigation, cleanup, and maintenance. Assign each task to a zone and crew consistent with the Chapter 17 labor conventions.
  5. Adjust production for slope, soil, access, roots, weather, occupancy, handling, and layout complexity. Record the adjustment basis for each affected task.
  6. Include water, temporary irrigation, establishment, cleanup, and maintenance where required. Confirm the duration and responsibility for any establishment period.
  7. Price materials, plants, delivery, and equipment from dated local sources. Record supplier, date, and quotation number for each priced item.
  8. Confirm truck access, minimum loads, dumping, pallet deposits, returns, and unloading. Record each supplier's terms on the pricing log.
  9. Obtain and level specialty subcontractor proposals where needed. Level each proposal to the same zone scope before comparison.

J. Reconciliation and Closeout

  1. Reconcile areas, linear quantities, volumes, plant counts, and loads. Use an independent measurement or count method for at least the two highest-value zones.
  2. Prepare the estimate by zone, system, material, labor, equipment, subcontract, and total. Enter each zone's totals into the exterior-work cost-code section of the master workbook.
  3. Document seasonal, soil, utility, drainage, boundary, and hidden-condition assumptions. Link each assumption to the affected zone.
  4. Create allowances or unit prices for rock, unsuitable soil, roots, contamination, irrigation conflicts, and concealed utilities. State exactly what each allowance or unit price includes and excludes.
  5. Complete independent review of foundations, base depths, drainage, conversions, and logistics. Record the reviewer, date, and disposition of any correction.

Common Exterior-Work Inspection

Inspect the finished exterior-work section using the same checks regardless of which zone is being reviewed.

  • Zone-separation check: confirm deck, fence, hardscape, planting, soil, and mulch quantities remain in separate zones with no shared, unallocated totals.
  • Conversion check: confirm every aggregate, soil, and mulch quantity uses the compacted or settled conversion factor, not a loose-volume figure.
  • Constraint check: confirm every deck, fence, and hardscape zone has a recorded setback, easement, HOA, or permit confirmation.
  • Substitution check: confirm the planting zone has a recorded substitution, availability, warranty, and mortality-replacement basis.
  • Pricing check: confirm every material, plant, and equipment price carries a supplier, date, and quotation reference.
  • Reconciliation check: confirm the two highest-value zones were reconciled by an independent method.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Aggregate or Soil Order Quantity Falls Short on Delivery

Probable cause: the takeoff applied a loose-volume coverage rate to a compacted-depth requirement, understating the purchase quantity.
Confirmation: compare the supplier's stated coverage basis against the conversion factor used in the takeoff for the affected zone.
Correction: recalculate the order quantity using the supplier's compacted or settled coverage basis and update the pricing log.
Reinspection: repeat the conversion check across all aggregate, soil, and mulch zones.

Fence or Deck Quantity Does Not Match a Later Field Measurement

Probable cause: the original measurement scaled a drawing instead of using a written dimension, or used an outdated plan revision.
Confirmation: compare the takeoff source citation against the confirmed controlling plan revision from Section E, Step 1.
Correction: remeasure from the current written dimension or verified scale and update the zone quantity and source.
Reinspection: repeat the reconciliation check for the corrected zone.

Plant Count Does Not Match the Planting Plan Symbol Legend

Probable cause: a symbol was miscounted, or a substitution changed the count without updating the schedule.
Confirmation: recount the plan symbols by tag and compare against the planting schedule and any recorded substitution.
Correction: correct the plant count and document the substitution basis if one applies.
Reinspection: repeat the substitution check on the corrected planting zone.

A Concealed Site Condition Appears After Pricing Is Complete

Probable cause: rock, roots, unsuitable soil, or a utility conflict was not addressed with an allowance or unit price during takeoff.
Confirmation: check the exterior-work risk register for an existing allowance or unit price covering the discovered condition.
Correction: if no allowance or unit price exists, create one using the Section J, Step 4 procedure and document the condition, source, and pricing basis.
Reinspection: repeat the independent review of the affected zone after the allowance or unit price is added.

A Deck or Fence Location Does Not Meet a Verified Setback

Probable cause: the constraint confirmation in Section E, Step 2 was not completed before the zone was measured and priced.
Confirmation: compare the measured location against the confirmed setback, easement, or HOA limit on the exterior-work scope record.
Correction: convert the discrepancy into a clarification, and do not finalize pricing for the affected zone until a response is recorded.
Reinspection: repeat the constraint check for the corrected zone once the clarification is resolved.
