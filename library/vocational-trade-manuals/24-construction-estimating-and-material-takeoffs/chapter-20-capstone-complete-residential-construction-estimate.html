Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to run one complete residential estimate from document intake through issued proposal and closed estimating record. The finished work product is one complete, reviewed, issued, and archived residential estimate built in a fresh copy of the Chapter 1 master workbook, covering sitework, concrete, framing, structural panels, roofing, envelope, insulation, drywall, interior trim, cabinets, flooring, paint, MEP allowances or qualified trade proposals, limited exterior work, self-performed labor, priced materials and subcontractors, indirect costs, a risk register, a bid proposal, a sample change order, and a final operations handoff.

You will begin with the exact document package prepared in Book 23 — its document register, revision log, marked-up plan set, scope checklist, conflict list, and approved clarifications — and carry that package through every trade takeoff built in Chapters 3 through 16, the labor estimate built in Chapter 17, the pricing and indirect-cost controls built in Chapter 18, and the proposal and change-order procedure built in Chapter 19. This project does not introduce a new takeoff method. It integrates every method already learned into one governed estimate.

This is the final project in Book 24. The completed estimate workbook, takeoff archive, pricing record, labor plan, risk register, bid proposal, change-order template, and final review package are handed off as the starting record for Book 25.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • ET-115 — Integrate plan review, sitework takeoff, concrete estimate, framing takeoff, and envelope estimate into one governed workbook.
  • ET-116 — Integrate the interior finish estimate, MEP allowances or qualified trade pricing, and exterior-work estimate with the workbook's cost-code structure.
  • ET-117 — Build labor pricing from the completed takeoff and apply overhead, profit, escalation, and contingency on an approved basis.
  • ET-118 — Assemble a bid proposal and a sample change order that reconcile line-for-line with the frozen estimate.
  • ET-119 — Complete a final independent review covering documents, takeoff, pricing, labor, subcontractors, indirect costs, and risk.
  • ET-120 — Archive the estimating record and prepare an operations handoff to Book 25.

Where This Skill Fits

Every prior chapter in this book built one piece of this estimate. Chapter 1 built the workbook this project opens a fresh copy of. Chapter 2 built the scope-review method this project repeats against the Book 23 package. Chapters 3 through 16 built every trade takeoff this project reconciles into the workbook's cost-code sections. Chapter 17 built the labor method this project applies to the completed scope. Chapter 18 built the pricing, waste, indirect-cost, and markup controls this project applies to reach a total bid. Chapter 19 built the proposal and change-order procedure this project uses to issue the estimate.

This project is not a new skill. It is the governed integration of every skill in Book 24, performed once, completely, on one real document package, so that the resulting estimate, proposal, and archive can be handed to Book 25 without gaps.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • selecting one approved capstone document package and confirming the controlling plan and specification revision
  • opening a fresh copy of the Chapter 1 master workbook and importing the Book 23 document register
  • performing the Chapter 2 scope and specification review against the capstone package
  • completing and reconciling every trade takeoff built in Chapters 3 through 16 that applies to the capstone project
  • obtaining and leveling supplier and subcontractor quotations
  • completing the Chapter 17 labor estimate for self-performed work
  • applying the Chapter 18 pricing, waste, indirect-cost, escalation, contingency, overhead, and profit controls
  • building and resolving a risk register
  • completing an independent multi-role review and freezing the estimate
  • preparing and issuing the Chapter 19 bid proposal and one sample change order
  • archiving the complete estimating record and preparing the Book 25 operations handoff

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • select a major commercial project, high-rise, complex multifamily building, hospital, industrial project, heavy civil project, or any project requiring specialized systems beyond the estimator's qualified scope as the capstone package
  • use a conceptual sketch without dimensions, or an incomplete plan set represented as construction-ready, as the capstone package
  • invent any dimension, quantity, production rate, labor burden, unit price, waste percentage, tax rate, delivery charge, equipment cost, permit fee, overhead percentage, profit target, escalation, contingency, or contract term not supplied by the approved documents, current market information, company policy, or a qualified quotation
  • present a conceptual or preliminary figure as a fixed construction price at any stage of this project
  • issue the proposal without completing the independent review and freezing the estimate version first

An addition, detached garage, or small residential renovation may be used as the capstone project only when existing-condition assumptions, demolition, protection, temporary work, unknown conditions, and occupied-building constraints are explicitly documented in the workbook before takeoff begins.

Trade Note

Every quantity, price, and rate carried into this capstone must already trace to a drawing, specification, field condition, approved assumption, or quotation from its originating chapter. This project does not re-derive that traceability; it verifies it. A figure that cannot be traced back to its source chapter must be treated as unverified and corrected before the estimate is frozen.

Capstone Standard

Do not issue the proposal in Section H until the workbook checks in Section G have passed, the highest-value and highest-risk quantities have been independently reconciled, and every required reviewer has recorded disposition on the risk register. A proposal issued before the estimate is frozen must be withdrawn and reissued from the corrected, frozen version.

Shop Tip

Work the capstone in the same order the chapters were taught: documents, sitework, concrete, framing, panels, roofing, envelope, insulation, drywall, interior trim, cabinets, flooring, paint, MEP, exterior work, labor, pricing, risk, review, proposal, archive. Skipping ahead to pricing before every trade takeoff is reconciled is the most common source of an unsupported total bid.

Common Field Fundamentals

The capstone does not introduce new takeoff mathematics. It requires disciplined sequencing: every trade section must be complete and reconciled before pricing begins, every price must carry a date and source before it is entered, and every risk must carry a disposition before the estimate is frozen. The zones below summarize the governed build order.

Reconciliation Before Pricing

Every cost-code section entered into the fresh workbook copy must show a completed reconciliation from its originating chapter before any unit price is attached to it. Pricing an unreconciled quantity produces a total bid that cannot be defended if challenged during review or negotiation.

Traceability Cue — Carry the Source Forward

When a quantity is copied from a Chapter 3–16 worksheet into the capstone workbook, carry its recorded source with it. A quantity that arrives in the capstone workbook without its original source must be treated as unverified until the source is located and re-attached.

Critical Control Cue — Freeze Before Proposing

Do not begin Section H proposal assembly until Section G's independent review is complete and the workbook version is frozen. A proposal built from an unfrozen workbook can be issued with numbers that no longer match the underlying estimate after a late correction.

Documentation Cue — Log Every Capstone Revision

Record workbook version, date, person, reason for change, and approval status in the revision log for every correction made during the capstone build, including corrections found during the Section G review.

Quotation Cue — Verify Scope Before Using a Price

Confirm every supplier and subcontractor quotation matches the documents and approved alternates before its price is entered. A quotation for a substituted product or a different scope must not be used until it is confirmed compliant or the substitution is approved in writing.

E. Preparation — Capstone Package, Documents, and Scope

  1. Select the approved capstone project and define the estimate purpose. Confirm the package meets the book outline's Capstone Estimate Control before proceeding, and record the estimate purpose on the project-information sheet.
  2. Obtain the Book 23 document register, revision log, marked-up set, scope checklist, conflict list, and clarifications. Confirm this is the complete Book 23 output for the selected project.
  3. Collect current plans, specifications, addenda, reports, schedules, selections, instructions, and forms. Add any documents issued after Book 23 was completed to the active register.
  4. Confirm the controlling plan and specification revision. Remove superseded documents from the active set while preserving them in the archive.
  5. Create the estimate number, pricing date, validity, market, currency, estimator, reviewer, and approval route. Record all fields on the project-information sheet.
  6. Create the Chapter 1 workbook copy and verify formulas, codes, summaries, checks, and versioning. Run the Chapter 1 test-item and error-check procedure on the fresh copy before importing any capstone data.
  7. Import the document register and create the active estimating file structure. Confirm every capstone cost-code section from Section B has a labeled place in the workbook.
  8. Perform the Chapter 2 scope and specification review. Repeat the full Chapter 2 procedure against the capstone package rather than assuming the Book 23 review remains complete without verification.
  9. Define inclusions, exclusions, allowances, alternates, unit prices, owner work, and responsibility boundaries. Record each on the workbook's assumptions and exclusions fields.
  10. Create and submit clarification questions and record responses. Use the Chapter 2 clarification-log format and the current bid instructions' question deadline.
  11. Convert unanswered items into approved assumptions, allowances, alternates, unit prices, exclusions, or contingency. Do not leave an unanswered item unresolved in the workbook.
  12. Prepare site logistics, access, phasing, protection, temporary work, weather, and schedule assumptions. Record these on the project-information sheet or a linked logistics field.

F. Trade Takeoff Integration and Procurement

  1. Complete and reconcile the sitework and excavation takeoff. Use the Chapter 3 method and enter the reconciled quantities into the workbook's Sitework cost-code section.
  2. Complete and reconcile the concrete estimate. Use the Chapter 4 method and enter reconciled quantities into the Concrete section.
  3. Complete and reconcile the framing lumber takeoff. Use the Chapter 5 method and enter reconciled quantities into the Framing section.
  4. Complete and reconcile the structural-panel takeoff. Use the Chapter 6 method and enter reconciled quantities into the Framing or a linked panel section.
  5. Complete and reconcile the roofing estimate. Use the Chapter 7 method and enter reconciled quantities into the Roofing section.
  6. Complete and reconcile the building-envelope estimate. Use the Chapter 8 method and enter reconciled quantities into the Envelope section.
  7. Complete and reconcile the insulation and air-sealing estimate. Use the Chapter 9 method and enter reconciled quantities into the Insulation section.
  8. Complete and reconcile the drywall estimate. Use the Chapter 10 method and enter reconciled quantities into the Drywall section.
  9. Complete and reconcile the interior trim and door estimate. Use the Chapter 11 method and enter reconciled quantities into the Finishes section.
  10. Complete and reconcile the cabinet, countertop, and built-in estimate. Use the Chapter 12 method and enter reconciled quantities into the Finishes section.
  11. Complete and reconcile the room-by-room flooring estimate. Use the Chapter 13 method and enter reconciled quantities into the Finishes section.
  12. Complete and reconcile the paint and surface-finish estimate. Use the Chapter 14 method and enter reconciled quantities into the Finishes section.
  13. Complete and reconcile the MEP allowance or subcontractor worksheet. Use the Chapter 15 method and enter counts, allowance basis, or leveled subcontract price into the MEP section.
  14. Complete and reconcile exterior work where included. Use the Chapter 16 method and enter reconciled quantities into the Exterior Work section only if the capstone scope includes decks, fences, hardscape, or landscaping.
  15. Create the material-purchase summary by supplier, phase, lead time, freight, and tax. Link each purchase-summary row to its originating cost-code section.
  16. Obtain dated quotations for major and long-lead materials. Record supplier, quote number, date, expiration, freight, lead time, minimum order, and qualifications for each.
  17. Request and level qualified subcontractor proposals. Confirm each proposal matches the same documents, scope, and revision before comparison.
  18. Resolve scope gaps, duplication, and exclusions among trade proposals. Do not select the lowest proposal until every gap and qualification is resolved.

G. Labor, Pricing, Risk, and Independent Review

  1. Complete the labor estimate for self-performed work. Use the Chapter 17 method against the reconciled takeoff quantities from Section F.
  2. Review crew, production, setup, cleanup, supervision, punch, and duration with field leadership. Record the reviewer's name and date on the labor worksheet.
  3. Price equipment, temporary facilities, protection, testing, permits, fees, insurance, bonds, and general conditions. Use dated sources for each and record them in the pricing log.
  4. Apply material-specific waste and verify no double-counting. Confirm waste is not already embedded in a unit price carried from a Chapter 3–16 worksheet before adding a separate waste line.
  5. Apply current prices, freight, tax, minimum orders, and expiration. Confirm every price entered in Section F carries a date and source.
  6. Create the risk register for scope, quantity, site, labor, schedule, procurement, market, and subcontractors. Use the Section D categories and enter every unresolved item identified during Sections E and F.
  7. Resolve each risk through clarification, allowance, alternate, unit price, exclusion, contingency, or acceptance. Record the disposition and the person who approved it for every entry.
  8. Apply approved escalation, contingency, overhead, and profit. Use the Chapter 18 method and the company's approved calculation basis.
  9. Verify markup and margin calculations. Confirm the labeled calculation basis from Chapter 1 and Chapter 18 was applied consistently.
  10. Run sensitivity scenarios on prices, productivity, unresolved scope, and duration. Record the scenario basis and result for each high-risk item.
  11. Compare totals with relevant historical benchmarks and investigate variance. Treat a benchmark comparison as a secondary check, never a substitute for the measured scope.
  12. Run workbook checks for missing quantities, units, prices, sources, formulas, duplicates, hidden rows, and broken links. Use the Chapter 1 error-check sheet across every capstone cost-code section.
  13. Independently reconcile the highest-value and highest-risk quantities. Use a second method or a second reviewer for each.
  14. Conduct estimator, peer, project-management, field, procurement, accounting, and management review as required. Confirm each required role has reviewed the estimate before it is frozen.
  15. Record review comments and disposition. Log every comment and its resolution in the workbook's revision log or a linked review log.
  16. Freeze the approved estimate version. Lock the workbook version and record the freeze date, version number, and approving person.

H. Proposal, Change Order, and Issue

  1. Prepare the Chapter 19 bid proposal from the approved estimate. Use only the frozen version identified in Section G, Step 16.
  2. State scope, exclusions, assumptions, allowances, alternates, unit prices, tax, schedule, validity, and total. Follow the Chapter 19 proposal-assembly procedure in full.
  3. Reconcile proposal values with the frozen workbook. Confirm every proposal figure traces to the corresponding frozen workbook value with no unexplained difference.
  4. Complete required forms, acknowledgments, signatures, bonds, and attachments. Use the bid instructions confirmed in Section E.
  5. Prepare a project-specific change-order template and sample hidden-condition change. Use the Chapter 19 change-order procedure and one documented hidden-condition scenario appropriate to the capstone project.
  6. Verify delivery instructions, recipient, deadline, format, and authorization. Confirm against the bid instructions before transmission.
  7. Issue the proposal and save proof of submission. Retain the submission record in the estimating archive.

I. Closeout — Archive and Operations Handoff

  1. Archive documents, takeoffs, quotations, subcontract bids, workbook versions, reviews, proposal, and change-order records. Store the complete estimating record together.
  2. Prepare an operations handoff covering scope, risks, assumptions, procurement, labor, schedule, and unresolved items. Write it for a reader who did not participate in the estimate.
  3. Conduct post-bid or post-award review when results are available. Record the outcome and any client or reviewer feedback received.
  4. Record lessons learned, quantity differences, pricing feedback, and improvements. Add these to the estimating record for use on future capstone-type projects.
  5. Close the estimate as issued, revised, withdrawn, lost, awarded, or converted to contract. Record the final status and date on the project-information sheet.
  6. Document the formal transition to Book 25 building codes, permits, and inspections. Confirm the archive and handoff are ready to be opened as the starting record for Book 25.

Common Workbook Inspection

Inspect the finished capstone estimate using the same checks regardless of which trade section or summary sheet is being reviewed.

  • Document check: confirm the controlling plan and specification revision matches the Book 23 handoff plus any documents added in Section E.
  • Takeoff check: confirm every applicable Chapter 3–16 trade section is reconciled and entered into its corresponding capstone cost-code section.
  • Procurement check: confirm every material and subcontractor price carries a date, source, and confirmed scope match.
  • Labor and pricing check: confirm labor hours trace to the Chapter 17 method and indirect costs follow the Chapter 18 labeled calculation basis.
  • Risk check: confirm every risk-register entry has a recorded disposition and approving person.
  • Review check: confirm every required reviewer role has recorded comments and disposition before the freeze.
  • Proposal check: confirm the issued proposal reconciles line-for-line with the frozen workbook version.

Complete the Capstone Verification Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before closing the estimate.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Trade Section's Capstone Total Does Not Match Its Originating Chapter Worksheet

Probable cause: a quantity was transcribed manually into the capstone workbook instead of linked, or the originating worksheet was revised after the capstone entry was made.
Confirmation: compare the capstone cost-code section total to the current version of the originating Chapter 3–16 worksheet.
Correction: re-enter or re-link the current reconciled quantity and record the correction in the revision log.
Reinspection: repeat the trade-summary comparison after correction.

A Subcontractor Proposal Was Selected Before Scope Leveling Was Complete

Probable cause: a low price was entered into the workbook before its scope gaps were resolved against the documents.
Confirmation: compare the selected proposal's stated scope to the documents and to the other proposals collected for the same trade.
Correction: complete leveling before re-selecting, and record the leveling basis in the pricing log.
Reinspection: repeat the procurement check on the corrected selection.

The Proposal Total Does Not Match the Frozen Workbook

Probable cause: the proposal was assembled from a workbook version that was later corrected, or a manual transcription error occurred during proposal assembly.
Confirmation: compare the proposal's stated total, alternates, and allowances line-by-line against the frozen workbook version identified in Section G, Step 16.
Correction: withdraw the proposal if issued, correct the discrepancy, and reissue from the verified frozen version.
Reinspection: repeat the full reconciliation described in Section H, Step 3.

A Risk-Register Entry Has No Recorded Disposition

Probable cause: the risk was logged during takeoff or pricing but not carried through to the Section G review.
Confirmation: review the risk register for any entry without a disposition value or approving person.
Correction: assign a disposition from the Section D list and record the approving person before the estimate is frozen.
Reinspection: repeat the risk check on the full register before proceeding to Section H.

A Required Reviewer Role Did Not Record Comments Before the Freeze

Probable cause: the review was scheduled but not completed, or comments were given verbally and not recorded.
Confirmation: compare the list of required reviewer roles in Section G, Step 14 to the recorded review log.
Correction: obtain and record the missing review before freezing the estimate; do not freeze with an incomplete review log.
Reinspection: repeat the review check before Section G, Step 16.

The Estimate Was Frozen With an Open Error-Check Flag

Probable cause: the Chapter 1 error-check sheet was not re-run across all capstone sections after the final data entry.
Confirmation: re-run the full error-check sheet against the current workbook state.
Correction: unfreeze the version only through a logged revision, correct the flagged item, and re-freeze with a new version number.
Reinspection: repeat the full Common Workbook Inspection section before reissuing any proposal built from the corrected version.

Cleanup

This project ends with an archived estimating record and a documented operations handoff, not an open workbook awaiting further edits.

  • Confirm the workbook is frozen at its final approved version with no open error-check flags
  • Confirm the risk register shows a disposition for every entry
  • Confirm the issued proposal and sample change order reconcile with the frozen workbook
  • Store the complete estimating record — documents, takeoffs, quotations, reviews, proposal, and change order — together in the archive
  • Complete and file the operations handoff summary for Book 25
