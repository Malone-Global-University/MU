Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install a complete section of nail-down solid-hardwood flooring over an approved wood subfloor. The finished work product is one installed hardwood floor area with a straight starter course, staggered end joints, fully engaged tongue-and-groove connections, correctly seated fasteners, preserved perimeter expansion space, and a documented installation record.

You will confirm the wood-to-subfloor moisture relationship, verify subfloor thickness and fastening, establish board direction and a straight starter line, lay out dry courses for color and joint distribution, set and operate a flooring nailer, maintain fastener spacing and stagger requirements, cut and fit boards around obstacles, face-fasten and fill starter and final rows, and inspect the completed floor for alignment, seating, and squeaks.

This project depends directly on the substrate, moisture, and flatness findings from Chapter 1, the subfloor repairs from Chapter 3, the flatness correction from Chapter 4, and any underlayment or moisture-control layer installed in Chapter 5. The floor you install here is inspected using the methods taught in Chapter 19 and may be referenced by the Chapter 20 capstone.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FL-049 — Lay out hardwood courses, including board direction, starter reference line, and joint staggering.
  • FL-050 — Install starter boards using the approved face-fastening method and perimeter expansion space.
  • FL-051 — Use a flooring nailer to fasten boards through the tongue at the specified spacing and angle.
  • FL-052 — Maintain expansion space at walls, columns, and fixed objects throughout the installation.
  • FL-053 — Stagger end joints according to the product requirement while installing successive courses.
  • FL-054 — Inspect hardwood alignment, fastener seating, joint engagement, and surface condition.

Where This Skill Fits

This project builds directly on the substrate inspection in Chapter 1, the squeak and damage repair in Chapter 3, the flatness correction in Chapter 4, and the underlayment or moisture-barrier work in Chapter 5. The moisture-relationship and subfloor-fastening verification performed here is the same discipline used again for the floating engineered hardwood installed in Chapter 10, and the doorway-fitting method used in Step 18 depends directly on the jamb-undercutting procedure taught in Chapter 16.

The joint-stagger, expansion, and fastening records produced in this project are reused during the Chapter 19 final quality inspection and may become part of the completed room handed off in the Chapter 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • confirming wood and subfloor moisture readings are within the approved relationship stated in the manufacturer instructions
  • confirming subfloor thickness, panel orientation, fastening, and concealed-service conditions before fastening begins
  • establishing board direction, a straight starter reference line, and calculated first- and last-row widths
  • laying out dry courses to plan color distribution and end-joint stagger
  • face-fastening starter and final rows and using a flooring nailer for the field of the room
  • maintaining fastener spacing, end distance, and the specified end-joint stagger
  • cutting and fitting boards around vents, pipes, jambs, and obstacles while preserving expansion clearance
  • inspecting joints, alignment, surface condition, fastener seating, squeaks, and expansion

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin installation before the Chapter 1 substrate and moisture findings and any Chapter 3, 4, or 5 corrections have been completed and reinspected
  • fasten into radiant-heat tubing, wiring, plumbing, or any other concealed system whose location has not been verified
  • substitute an unapproved fastener, spacing, or nailer setting for the one specified by the flooring manufacturer
  • trap the floor against a wall, cabinet, or fixed object by omitting the required perimeter expansion space
  • undercut a door jamb or install a transition — that work is covered in Chapters 15 and 16

Any moisture, subfloor, or concealed-service condition that fails verification in this chapter must be corrected or escalated before fastening begins.

Trade Note

The wood-to-subfloor moisture relationship confirmed in Step 2 is not the same check performed in Chapter 1. Chapter 1 confirms the subfloor is dry enough to build on; this project confirms the delivered hardwood's moisture content is within the specific range the manufacturer allows relative to the subfloor reading on the day of installation. Skipping this second check is a leading cause of gapping and cupping documented in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not begin fastening any course until wood and subfloor moisture readings have been confirmed within the manufacturer's approved relationship, subfloor thickness and fastening have been verified, and the starter reference line has been checked for straightness. A course fastened before every check is complete must be treated as unverified and corrected before continuing.

Shop Tip

Lay out at least three or four rows of boards dry, unfastened, across the room before driving a single fastener. This is the only reliable way to catch a bad color match, a short board run, or an unacceptable end-joint cluster before it is permanently fastened into the floor.

Moisture Relationship, Subfloor Verification, and Nailer Setup

Confirm the wood-to-subfloor moisture relationship and subfloor condition specified by the flooring manufacturer before any board is fastened. Set the flooring nailer for the exact board thickness and fastener specified, and test it on scrap material before fastening a single field board.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Moisture Relationship and Fastening Schedule

Use the exact wood-to-subfloor moisture relationship, fastener type and spacing, and nailer setting specified by the flooring manufacturer for the identified species, width, and subfloor. Do not substitute a fastener spacing or nailer pressure that is not stated in the manufacturer's instructions or the approved chapter project specification.

Critical Safety Cue — Concealed Services Beneath the Subfloor

Do not fasten into radiant-heat tubing, wiring, plumbing, or any other concealed system whose location has not been verified. Confirm concealed-service locations before fastening begins and mark them so fastener placement avoids them throughout the installation.

Field Safety Cue — Nailer and Saw Operation

Keep hands clear of the striking mechanism when operating a pneumatic or manual flooring nailer. Disconnect the air supply or remove the striker before clearing a jam or adjusting the tool. Wear hearing and eye protection during all nailing and cutting operations.

Measurement Cue — Verify the Starter Line Before Fastening

Check the starter reference line for straightness and correct expansion offset at multiple points along its length before fastening the first board. A starter row fastened off a line that is not straight forces every later course out of alignment and cannot be corrected without removing boards.

E. Moisture, Subfloor, and Layout Verification

  1. Review wood species, grade, width, moisture requirements, room environment, subfloor, fastening schedule, and finish condition. Confirm this information matches the approved plan before proceeding.
  2. Confirm wood and subfloor moisture readings are within the approved relationship. Take readings at representative locations using the calibrated moisture meter and record every result.
  3. Confirm the subfloor thickness, panel orientation, fastening, and concealed-service conditions. Verify against the Chapter 1 Flooring-Readiness Plan and mark any concealed-service location.
  4. Identify joist direction and approved board direction. Record the specified board direction on the installation record before establishing the starter line.

F. Board Preparation and Dry-Lay

  1. Mix boards from multiple bundles for color and length distribution. Draw boards from at least three bundles as the installation proceeds.
  2. Inspect boards for defects and set aside unsuitable pieces. Reject boards with damaged tongues, grooves, or faces before they reach the layout.
  3. Establish a straight starter reference line. Snap a chalk line offset from the starting wall by the specified expansion space.
  4. Calculate first- and last-row widths. Measure the room perpendicular to board direction and adjust the starter-row width to avoid an unacceptably narrow final row.
  5. Provide the specified perimeter expansion space. Place spacers at the starting wall and every fixed object before fastening begins.
  6. Lay out several dry courses to plan joint staggering and color. Arrange at least three or four rows unfastened before driving any fastener.

G. Starter Row Installation

  1. Face-nail or screw the starter rows using the approved concealed or visible method. Align the starter row to the chalk line and confirm straightness before fastening.
  2. Pre-drill near ends where required. Use the pilot-hole size specified by the manufacturer or fastener product.

H. Nailer Setup and Field Fastening

  1. Set the flooring nailer for the board thickness and fastener. Match the nailer's base plate, foot, and driving-force setting to the specified fastener and board thickness.
  2. Test the nailer on scrap. Confirm the fastener seats fully without splitting the tongue or crushing the board face.
  3. Engage each board tongue and groove fully. Seat every board against the previous course before striking the nailer.
  4. Strike the nailer only after confirming board seating. Do not fasten a board that is not fully seated tongue-to-groove.
  5. Maintain fastener spacing and end distance. Follow the manufacturer's specified spacing along the length of each board and at board ends.
  6. Avoid fastening into radiant heat, wiring, pipes, or other concealed systems. Check the marked concealed-service locations before driving each fastener near them.
  7. Stagger end joints according to the product requirement. Confirm each end joint meets the manufacturer's minimum offset from adjacent courses.
  8. Reject severely bowed boards or use only approved straightening methods. Do not force a bowed board into alignment by overdriving fasteners.
  9. Continue while checking alignment against reference lines. Recheck straightness against the starter line every several courses.

I. Obstacles, Doorways, and Final Row

  1. Cut around vents, pipes, jambs, and obstacles while preserving expansion clearance. Maintain the specified gap at every obstacle.
  2. Undercut jambs using Chapter 16 before installing doorway pieces. Do not proceed with a doorway piece until the jamb has been undercut per the Chapter 16 procedure.
  3. Do not fasten through the field or trap the floor beneath fixed cabinets. Confirm cabinets and built-ins do not pin the flooring field.
  4. Install final rows using approved face-fastening and pull-bar methods. Rip the final row to width and use a pull bar to seat it where a nailer cannot be operated.
  5. Fill only designated face-fastener holes with compatible material. Do not fill tongue-side fastener locations, which remain concealed.

J. Inspection and Protection

  1. Inspect joints, alignment, surface damage, fastener seating, squeaks, and expansion. Walk the completed floor and confirm every item against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Protect the floor and record moisture and installation conditions. Complete the installation record before opening the room to traffic.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed installation area using the same checks regardless of species, width, or room shape.

  • Moisture check: confirm wood and subfloor readings were within the approved relationship before fastening began.
  • Alignment check: confirm the starter row and every subsequent course remain straight against the reference line.
  • Fastening check: confirm every tongue-side and face fastener is seated at the specified spacing with none proud or missed.
  • Stagger check: confirm every end joint meets the manufacturer's minimum offset from adjacent courses.
  • Expansion check: confirm perimeter expansion space is preserved at every wall, column, pipe, cabinet, and fixed object.
  • Surface check: confirm no unaddressed gaps, squeaks, damaged boards, or unfilled designated fastener holes remain.
  • Documentation check: confirm the installation record is fully completed and signed.

Complete the installation record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 10.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Boards Cup or Gap After Installation

Probable cause: the wood-to-subfloor moisture relationship was not within the approved range at the time of installation, or the room's humidity changed significantly after fastening.
Confirmation: re-test wood and subfloor moisture at multiple locations and compare against the manufacturer's approved relationship and the readings recorded before installation.
Correction: stabilize room humidity per manufacturer instructions and allow the floor to acclimate to the corrected environment before evaluating whether individual boards require replacement.
Reinspection: re-measure moisture and surface condition after the environment has stabilized for the period specified by the manufacturer.

A Course Drifts Out of Alignment with the Starter Line

Probable cause: the starter row was not straight, a board was not fully seated before fastening, or alignment was not rechecked frequently enough during installation.
Confirmation: measure the current course against the starter reference line at multiple points along its length.
Correction: stop installation, pull the affected boards back to the point of drift, reseat them against the reference line, and refasten.
Reinspection: recheck alignment against the reference line every few courses for the remainder of the installation.

A Board Squeaks or Moves After Fastening

Probable cause: a tongue-side fastener missed the subfloor framing pattern, was underdriven, or the board was not fully seated before the nailer was struck.
Confirmation: walk and load the affected area to confirm the exact location and extent of the movement.
Correction: add a fastener at an approved location per manufacturer instructions, or replace the affected board if the fastener cannot correct the movement.
Reinspection: rewalk the corrected area to confirm the squeak or movement is eliminated.

End Joints Cluster in the Same Area of the Room

Probable cause: boards were fastened directly from the bundle without a dry-lay step, or the required end-joint stagger was not checked before fastening.
Confirmation: measure the offset between end joints in adjacent courses across the affected area.
Correction: remove and replace boards in the affected courses to restore the manufacturer-specified stagger.
Reinspection: re-measure end-joint offsets across the corrected area before continuing installation.

The Floor Is Trapped at a Wall, Cabinet, or Fixed Object

Probable cause: spacers were removed too early, a fastener was driven too close to a fixed object, or trim was installed through the flooring field.
Confirmation: inspect the affected perimeter for missing expansion space or a fastener located within the required clearance.
Correction: remove the offending fastener or trim, restore the specified expansion space, and reinstall trim without penetrating the flooring field.
Reinspection: confirm expansion space is restored at every wall, column, pipe, cabinet, and fixed object in the affected area.

A Fastener Strikes a Concealed Service

Probable cause: a concealed-service location was not verified or marked before fastening reached that area.
Confirmation: stop work immediately and inspect for damage to wiring, plumbing, or radiant-heat tubing at the affected location.
Correction: de-energize or isolate the affected system as required, and escalate to a qualified plumber, electrician, or radiant-heat professional for repair before continuing installation.
Reinspection: confirm the concealed system has been repaired and cleared before resuming fastening in the affected area.

Cleanup

This project ends with the installed floor protected and the work area cleared of scrap, fasteners, and dust before the room is opened to the next chapter's work.

  • Remove and properly store the flooring nailer, saws, and hand tools
  • Sweep and vacuum sawdust, packaging, and fastener waste from the installed floor and work area
  • Cover the completed floor with the specified protection material through the required cure period

Field Terms

Starter Reference Line
The straight chalk line, offset from the starting wall by the specified expansion space, that the first course is aligned against.
Board Direction
The direction the long axis of the flooring runs relative to joist direction, room shape, or light source, as specified by the approved plan.
End-Joint Stagger
The minimum offset required between end joints in adjacent courses, stated by the flooring manufacturer.
Perimeter Expansion Space
The gap maintained between the flooring field and any wall, column, pipe, cabinet, or fixed object.
Tongue-Side Fastening
Driving a fastener through the tongue of each board at the angle and spacing specified for the nailer and board thickness.
Face-Fastening
Fastening a board through its top face, used only on starter and final rows where a nailer cannot be operated.
Dry-Lay
Arranging multiple unfastened courses to plan color distribution and end-joint stagger before any fastener is driven.
Pull Bar
A tool used to engage and seat final-row boards against the previous course where a flooring nailer cannot be operated.
Moisture Relationship
The manufacturer-specified allowable difference between wood moisture content and subfloor moisture content at the time of installation.
