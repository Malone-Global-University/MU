Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install one approved underlayment and moisture-control system over a prepared substrate. The finished work product is a continuous underlayment and moisture-barrier layer, correctly seamed and sealed, free of wrinkles, punctures, and open edges, with penetrations addressed and movement details preserved, ready for the finish flooring specified in a later chapter.

You will confirm the substrate has passed the repair, flatness, cleanliness, and moisture inspections completed in Chapters 1 through 4; select the correct underlayment or moisture-barrier system for the flooring type; establish the starting wall and course direction; place and seam the material without wrinkles; treat penetrations and perimeter details; and inspect the completed layer before it is covered by finish flooring.

This is the last preparation project before finish flooring begins. Chapters 6 through 10 — laminate, luxury vinyl plank, sheet vinyl, nail-down hardwood, and engineered floating hardwood — all depend on the continuous, compatible, uncompromised underlayment and moisture-control system installed here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FL-025 — Select underlayment appropriate to the substrate, flooring type, and moisture condition.
  • FL-026 — Roll or place underlayment without wrinkles.
  • FL-027 — Overlap or butt seams according to product instructions.
  • FL-028 — Tape or seal joints with the specified compatible tape.
  • FL-029 — Install a moisture barrier compatible with the substrate and flooring system.
  • FL-030 — Inspect the installed layer for continuous, unbroken coverage.

Where This Skill Fits

The underlayment and moisture-control layer installed in this chapter sits between every substrate condition documented in Chapters 1 through 4 and every finish flooring installed in Chapters 6 through 17. A wrinkled underlayment telegraphs through resilient flooring, a punctured moisture barrier undermines the entire moisture-control strategy, and a duplicated or incompatible layer can void a manufacturer warranty even when the finish flooring itself is installed correctly.

The seam, coverage, and penetration-treatment discipline established here is the same discipline reused for the sheet-vinyl seam work in Chapter 8 and the tile-backer-board seam treatment in Chapter 11.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the flooring type, substrate, moisture-test results, underlayment requirement, vapor-control strategy, radiant-heat condition, and product compatibility
  • confirming the substrate has passed the repair, flatness, cleanliness, and moisture inspections required before underlayment installation
  • determining whether the system requires separate underlayment, attached pad, moisture barrier, liquid membrane, sheet membrane, or no additional layer
  • rolling or placing underlayment courses without wrinkles, in the correct direction, from the correct starting wall
  • butting or overlapping seams and taping or sealing them with the specified compatible tape
  • installing sheet or liquid moisture control at the coverage, thickness, lap, and cure requirements specified
  • sealing pipe and column penetrations and preserving expansion joints, floor drains, and vents
  • inspecting the full area for open seams, wrinkles, contamination, punctures, and missed edges before it is covered

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • install underlayment or a moisture barrier over a substrate that has not passed the Chapter 1 moisture inspection, the Chapter 3 structural repair, or the Chapter 4 flatness correction
  • install duplicate underlayment, vapor layers, or membrane systems that the flooring manufacturer does not permit
  • cover unresolved moisture or active slab emissions beyond the product's stated limits
  • install finish flooring — that work is covered in Chapters 6 through 17
  • proceed when a puncture, open seam, or missed area cannot be corrected using the approved patch method

Any substrate condition that has not passed the required prior inspections must be corrected before this project begins.

Trade Note

Underlayment and moisture-barrier selection is controlled entirely by the flooring manufacturer's instructions for the product being installed above it. Do not substitute a different underlayment thickness, a different moisture-barrier permeability rating, or a different seam-tape product because it is available on hand. An incompatible layer can trap moisture against the finish flooring or defeat the flooring's own warranty terms even when every other step in this chapter is performed correctly.

Setup Standard

Do not begin placing underlayment or moisture-barrier material until the substrate has passed the Chapter 1 moisture inspection, the Chapter 3 structural repair, and the Chapter 4 flatness correction, and the cure time for any patch or leveling material has fully elapsed. A layer installed over an unverified substrate must be treated as incomplete and removed before finish flooring begins.

Shop Tip

Dry-lay the first course before committing to adhesive, tape, or a troweled membrane. Confirm the course direction, starting wall, and seam location match the manufacturer's layout requirement and the finish-flooring layout planned for the room. Repositioning underlayment after seams are taped or membrane is troweled is far more disruptive than confirming layout the first time.

System Selection and Compatibility

Every underlayment or moisture-barrier decision in this chapter is based on the flooring manufacturer's written instructions for the specific product being installed above it, not on a general assumption about what "most floors" require. Where the flooring manufacturer requires a specific underlayment thickness, a specific moisture-barrier permeability rating, or prohibits a duplicate layer, follow that requirement exactly and record it on the Installation Record.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Do Not Duplicate Moisture-Control Layers

Install only the underlayment and moisture-control layers specified by the flooring manufacturer for the exact product being installed. Do not add a second vapor layer, a second underlayment, or a substitute product because it seems like extra protection. A duplicated or incompatible layer can trap moisture against the finish flooring, prevent required adhesive bond, or void the flooring manufacturer's warranty.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Lap and Seam Distances Before Sealing

Measure every overlap and lap distance against the manufacturer's stated minimum before applying seam tape or sealing a lap. A seam sealed at an insufficient lap distance is a defect even if the tape itself appears fully adhered.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Cover Unresolved Moisture

Do not install underlayment or a moisture barrier over a substrate with an unresolved moisture reading, an active leak, or slab emissions beyond the product's stated limit. Covering an unresolved moisture condition conceals it from later inspection and can lead to trapped moisture, mold, adhesive failure, or structural damage beneath finish flooring that has already been installed.

Field Safety Cue — Ventilation During Liquid Membrane Application

Provide the ventilation specified by the product's safety data sheet whenever mixing or applying a liquid or troweled moisture-control membrane. Keep the work area accessible to an exit at all times during application and cure.

E. System Review and Substrate Confirmation

  1. Review flooring type, substrate, moisture test results, underlayment requirement, vapor-control strategy, radiant-heat condition, and product compatibility. Confirm the identified underlayment and moisture-barrier products match the flooring manufacturer's written instructions before opening any packaging.
  2. Confirm the substrate has passed repair, flatness, cleanliness, and moisture inspections. Review the Chapter 1, Chapter 3, and Chapter 4 records for the inspection area and re-test moisture at a representative location if the prior test is not current.
  3. Determine whether the system requires separate underlayment, attached pad, moisture barrier, liquid membrane, sheet membrane, or no additional layer. Record the determination and the controlling manufacturer instruction on the Installation Record.
  4. Do not install duplicate underlayment or vapor layers unless the flooring manufacturer permits them. Confirm no existing underlayment or vapor layer remains from a prior installation before proceeding.

F. Course Layout and Placement

  1. Establish the direction and starting wall. Mark the starting wall and course direction with chalk or marking tape, matching the manufacturer's layout requirement and the planned finish-flooring layout.
  2. Roll or place the first underlayment course without wrinkles. Sweep the substrate immediately before placement and smooth the course from the starting wall outward.
  3. Maintain the required wall upturn or perimeter treatment. Turn moisture-barrier sheet up the wall to the specified height where the manufacturer requires a perimeter upturn.

G. Seaming and Sealing

  1. Butt or overlap seams according to the exact product instructions. Measure each seam against the specified minimum lap or butt condition before proceeding.
  2. Tape or seal seams with the specified compatible tape. Press each seam flat with the seam roller immediately after taping.
  3. Keep underlayment flat at transitions and doorways. Trim excess material at transitions without leaving a raised edge or unsupported flap.
  4. Avoid puncturing the moisture barrier. Set tools and materials on protective board or scrap material when working on top of the placed barrier.
  5. Patch tears and holes using the approved method. Repair any puncture found during placement before continuing to the next course.

H. Course Continuation and Penetrations

  1. Place subsequent courses while preserving seam orientation. Continue in the established course direction, checking each seam against the same lap or butt standard.
  2. Do not create thick overlaps beneath floating flooring. Trim excess material at overlaps to the specified single-thickness condition where a floating floor will be installed above.
  3. Seal pipe and column penetrations where required. Fit a boot, collar, or sealant bead at every identified penetration before the surrounding course is completed.
  4. Preserve expansion joints, floor drains, vents, and movement details. Mark and protect each detail so it is not covered or blocked by the underlayment or moisture-barrier material.

I. Moisture-Control Membrane Installation

  1. Install sheet or liquid moisture control according to coverage, thickness, lap, and cure requirements. Mix liquid or troweled membrane products using the specified ratio and application rate, and apply at the specified thickness with the specified trowel or roller.
  2. Do not cover unresolved moisture or active slab emissions beyond the product limits. Stop work and escalate per the Failure Points section if a moisture reading exceeds the product's stated limit at this stage.

J. Final Inspection and Protection

  1. Inspect the full area for open seams, wrinkles, contamination, punctures, and missed edges. Walk the entire inspection area under adequate light and mark any deficiency found.
  2. Protect the installed system until flooring begins. Restrict foot traffic, tool storage, and following-trade access to the protected areas only.
  3. Document product, lot, coverage, seams, repairs, and moisture-control details. Complete the Installation Record and attach photographs of seams, laps, penetrations, and full coverage before the layer is scheduled to be covered.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed inspection area using the same checks regardless of which underlayment or moisture-barrier system the project involves.

  • Compatibility check: confirm the installed product matches the flooring manufacturer's written instructions and no duplicate layer is present.
  • Coverage check: confirm continuous coverage across the full area with no open seams, wrinkles, or missed edges.
  • Seam check: confirm every seam meets the specified lap or butt condition and is fully taped or sealed.
  • Penetration check: confirm every pipe, column, and fixed-object penetration is sealed per the approved method.
  • Movement-detail check: confirm expansion joints, floor drains, and vents remain unblocked.
  • Repair check: confirm every identified tear or hole has been patched using the approved method.
  • Documentation check: confirm the Installation Record is fully completed and signed.

Complete the Underlayment and Moisture-Barrier Installation Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 06.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Seam or Overlap Falls Short of the Specified Lap Distance

Probable cause: a course was placed without measuring against the reference line, or material shifted during placement before taping.
Confirmation: measure the seam or overlap against the manufacturer's stated minimum at multiple points along its length.
Correction: reposition the course to achieve the specified lap, or install an additional strip of compatible material and tape to bring the seam into compliance.
Reinspection: remeasure the corrected seam and confirm it is fully taped or sealed before continuing.

A Wrinkle or Bubble Appears in a Placed Course

Probable cause: the material was not smoothed from the starting wall outward, the substrate was not swept clean before placement, or the product was placed outside its acclimation or temperature range.
Confirmation: lift and reinspect the affected section and check the substrate beneath it for debris or an unresolved flatness deviation.
Correction: remove and reposition the affected course, cleaning the substrate before replacing the material.
Reinspection: confirm the corrected section lies flat under normal lighting from multiple viewing angles.

A Puncture or Tear Is Found in the Moisture Barrier

Probable cause: a tool, fastener, or sharp material edge contacted the placed barrier during installation.
Confirmation: inspect the full extent of the tear and confirm whether the substrate beneath it was also affected.
Correction: patch the tear using the manufacturer's approved patch method and material, extending the patch beyond the damaged area by the specified minimum overlap.
Reinspection: confirm the patch is fully sealed and reinspect the surrounding area for additional damage before covering the layer.

A Moisture Reading Exceeds the Product Limit Immediately Before Covering

Probable cause: a moisture condition present at the Chapter 1 inspection has returned or worsened, or an active source was not fully resolved.
Confirmation: re-test at the same representative locations used in the Chapter 1 inspection and compare results.
Correction: stop work, do not install the moisture-control layer over the affected area, and escalate to a qualified professional to resolve the moisture source before proceeding.
Reinspection: repeat the moisture test at the same locations after the source is corrected.

An Expansion Joint, Drain, or Vent Is Found Covered or Blocked

Probable cause: the detail was not marked before placement, or a course was extended across it without notice.
Confirmation: compare the placed layer against the marked locations recorded during Chapter 1 inspection.
Correction: cut back the underlayment or moisture-barrier material to expose and preserve the detail, and reseal the cut edge per the approved method.
Reinspection: confirm the detail is fully exposed and functional and that the surrounding seam remains sealed.

A Duplicate or Incompatible Layer Is Discovered During Review

Probable cause: an existing underlayment or vapor layer from a prior installation was not identified during the system-review step.
Confirmation: compare the existing layer against the flooring manufacturer's written instructions for permitted underlayment and vapor-control systems.
Correction: remove the incompatible or duplicate layer and reinspect the exposed substrate before installing the approved system.
Reinspection: repeat the substrate confirmation step before placing the approved underlayment or moisture barrier.

Cleanup

This project ends with the installed underlayment and moisture-control layer fully protected and undisturbed until finish flooring begins.

  • Remove and properly store cutting tools, seam rollers, mixing equipment, and unused material
  • Dispose of membrane mixing containers and consumables according to the product's safety data sheet
  • Confirm no tools, fasteners, or debris remain on the installed layer
  • Store the completed Installation Record with the Flooring-Readiness Plan for reference by later projects
