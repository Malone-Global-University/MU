Job Overview

This capstone project requires you to complete a full flooring system in one practice room, from substrate inspection through final protected handoff, using the approved capstone floor plan, product schedule, substrate details, moisture limits, transition plan, trim plan, door details, and inspection sequence. The finished work product is one complete practice-room flooring installation: an accepted substrate, an installed moisture-control and underlayment system, an installed primary finish-flooring system, an installed secondary practice flooring zone demonstrating a second material system, transitions and thresholds between flooring types, coordinated baseboard and shoe molding, a completed repair demonstration, a passed final inspection, and a documented, protected handoff.

You will re-perform the Chapter 1 substrate and moisture inspection on the assigned room, correct any squeaks, movement, damage, or flatness deviation found, prepare the work area under Chapter 2 controls, install underlayment and moisture control, install the primary and secondary flooring systems specified by the capstone plan, undercut doorways, install transitions, coordinate trim, complete a repair demonstration, and carry the finished room through the Chapter 19 final quality inspection before handoff.

This is the last project in Book 14. It draws directly on every earlier chapter — substrate inspection and moisture testing (Chapter 1), work-area preparation (Chapter 2), subfloor repair (Chapter 3), flatness correction (Chapter 4), underlayment and moisture barriers (Chapter 5), the primary and secondary finish-flooring systems selected from Chapters 6 through 14, transitions and thresholds (Chapter 15), doorway undercutting (Chapter 16), stair or landing interfaces where included (Chapter 17), material-specific repair (Chapter 18), and final quality inspection (Chapter 19) — and hands the completed room off ready for Book 15 painting and surface finishes.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FL-115 — Integrate subfloor preparation, moisture control, and flatness correction into one accepted substrate.
  • FL-116 — Install underlayment, primary finish flooring, and a secondary practice flooring zone as one coordinated system.
  • FL-117 — Install transition strips and thresholds between flooring types without trapping floating floors.
  • FL-118 — Undercut doorways and coordinate baseboard, shoe molding, and toe kicks with the finished floor.
  • FL-119 — Complete a flooring-repair demonstration and a full final quality inspection of the finished room.
  • FL-120 — Document the complete flooring closeout and hand off the room ready for Book 15 finishes.

Where This Skill Fits

This capstone is the point in the book where every previously isolated skill — substrate inspection, moisture testing, flatness correction, underlayment, a primary finish-flooring system, a secondary finish-flooring system, transitions, doorway undercutting, trim coordination, repair, and final inspection — must function together as one uninterrupted project, in the same sequence a working flooring installer follows on an occupied job site.

The documented Flooring-Readiness Plan, work-area readiness record, repair records, underlayment record, installation records, transition records, and final quality inspection completed in this chapter form the complete project file that is handed off to the property owner, general contractor, or Book 15 finish crew.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • re-performing the Chapter 1 substrate and moisture inspection and the Chapter 2 work-area preparation on the assigned capstone room
  • correcting squeaks, movement, damaged subfloor, proud fasteners, unsupported edges, and flatness deviations under Chapters 3 and 4
  • installing the approved underlayment and moisture-control system under Chapter 5
  • establishing room layout and reference lines and installing the primary plank-flooring or resilient-flooring system specified by the capstone plan under the applicable chapter from 6 through 10
  • installing an approved secondary practice flooring panel or zone demonstrating another material system where the capstone plan requires it, which may draw on Chapters 11 through 14 for tile or carpet
  • undercutting door jambs and casing under Chapter 16 and installing transition strips and thresholds under Chapter 15
  • coordinating baseboard, shoe molding, toe kicks, and cabinet clearances without fastening trim through floating flooring
  • completing the designated flooring-repair demonstration under Chapter 18
  • performing the Chapter 19 final quality inspection, correcting every punch-list item, and completing formal documentation and handoff

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • alter stair rise, run, tread depth, nosing, landing geometry, guard, or handrail conditions
  • disturb suspect legacy materials before hazardous-material clearance
  • proceed past any stop-work condition identified during the Chapter 1 re-inspection, structural correction, moisture testing, or hazardous-material screening
  • invent moisture limits, flatness tolerances, acclimation periods, expansion gaps, underlayment types, adhesive coverage, fastener spacing, trowel sizes, grout-joint widths, transition clearances, cure times, or rolling requirements not supplied by the capstone plan, adopted code, or manufacturer instructions

Any substrate, moisture, structural, or hazardous-material condition that fails verification during this project must be corrected, tested by a qualified professional, or escalated before installation continues.

Trade Note

This capstone is not a new procedure. It is the same substrate-to-finish sequence taught in Chapters 1 through 19, performed once from beginning to end without chapter breaks. Every deficiency, moisture reading, flatness measurement, and installation record from the earlier chapters in this room carries forward into this project's documentation rather than being repeated from scratch.

Setup Standard

Do not begin underlayment or finish-flooring installation until the Chapter 1 substrate and moisture re-inspection is complete, every structural deficiency has been corrected under Chapter 3, and flatness has been re-verified under Chapter 4. A capstone installation begun before every prior-chapter condition is confirmed must be treated as incomplete and corrected before continuing.

Sequence Continuity Across Chapter Boundaries

A real flooring project follows the approved substrate-to-finish sequence even when that sequence crosses chapter boundaries. Structural correction always precedes flatness correction. Flatness correction is completed and cured before underlayment or finish flooring begins. Floating floors remain free to move and are never trapped by cabinets, partitions, door jambs, or trim. Door jambs are undercut before the final flooring pieces are installed through the opening. The final quality inspection is always the last step before handoff.

Critical Safety Cue — Re-Screen Before Disturbing Any Existing Finish

If the capstone room contains any existing resilient flooring, felt backing, black mastic, or other suspect legacy material not already cleared in an earlier chapter, stop work and escalate to a qualified hazardous-material professional before disturbing it. A prior clearance from an earlier chapter does not extend to material that was not inspected at that time.

Measurement Cue — Re-Verify, Do Not Assume

Re-perform the moisture and flatness measurements required by Chapter 1 and Chapter 4 for the assigned capstone room rather than relying solely on records from an earlier chapter. Conditions can change between chapters, particularly moisture content and flatness near exterior walls, plumbing, and previously repaired areas.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Two Systems, Two Sets of Limits

The primary and secondary flooring systems specified by the capstone plan may carry different moisture limits, expansion requirements, underlayment compatibility, and fastening schedules. Apply each product's own manufacturer instructions at the transition line rather than assuming one system's limits apply to both.

Code Alert — Confirm Before Final Documentation

Confirm whether the capstone room requires a permit, inspection, or licensed-trade sign-off before it is treated as complete under local jurisdiction. Practice-room status does not exempt work from applicable adopted code where the capstone plan represents a real occupied space.

E. Plan Review and Substrate Re-Inspection

  1. Obtain the approved capstone floor plan, product schedule, substrate details, moisture limits, transition plan, trim plan, door details, and inspection sequence. Record each reference document on the capstone project file before beginning any physical work.
  2. Perform the Chapter 1 substrate and moisture inspection. Re-identify the substrate, re-test moisture at representative locations, and compare results against any existing Chapter 1 record for this room.
  3. Identify hazardous legacy materials before disturbing existing finishes. Stop work and escalate to a qualified hazardous-material professional if any suspect material is found that was not previously cleared.
  4. Correct squeaks, movement, damaged subfloor, proud fasteners, and unsupported edges. Apply the Chapter 3 repair procedure to every deficiency found during the re-inspection.
  5. Measure and correct floor flatness. Apply the Chapter 4 procedure using the straightedge and tolerance specified for the primary flooring system.
  6. Allow patching or leveling materials to cure. Observe the exact cure time specified by the patch or leveling-compound manufacturer before continuing.
  7. Recheck flatness and moisture. Confirm both readings meet the manufacturer's requirement for the primary flooring system before proceeding to work-area preparation.

F. Work-Area Preparation and Staging

  1. Prepare the Chapter 2 work area and environmental controls. Confirm the room is enclosed, dry, and within the temperature and humidity range required by every flooring product specified in the capstone plan.
  2. Stage and condition flooring according to the exact product requirements. Acclimate or condition the primary and secondary flooring materials per their individual manufacturer instructions.
  3. Confirm wall, cabinet, door, appliance, stair, and adjacent-floor relationships. Record every fixed object and adjacent floor elevation that will affect layout, expansion, or transitions.

G. Underlayment and Primary Flooring Installation

  1. Install the approved underlayment and moisture-control system. Apply the Chapter 5 procedure for the product specified in the capstone plan, maintaining continuous coverage without wrinkles or open seams.
  2. Establish the room layout and reference lines. Set the starting wall and direction specified by the capstone plan for the primary flooring system.
  3. Calculate starter and final row widths. Adjust the layout to avoid an unacceptably narrow final row, per the applicable chapter's method.
  4. Undercut door jambs and casing. Apply the Chapter 16 procedure before installing any flooring piece that passes beneath a jamb or casing.
  5. Install the primary plank-flooring system specified for the room. Apply the complete installation procedure from the applicable Chapter 6 through 10 for the specified product.
  6. Maintain expansion at all walls, columns, cabinets, pipes, and fixed objects. Use the spacer and clearance requirements specified by the primary flooring manufacturer.
  7. Maintain required joint stagger and pattern. Follow the exact stagger and pattern requirement stated in the primary flooring manufacturer's instructions.
  8. Fit around doorways, vents, cabinets, and obstacles. Cut and fit each piece while preserving the required expansion clearance.

H. Secondary Flooring Zone and Transitions

  1. Install an approved secondary practice flooring panel or zone demonstrating another material system where required by the capstone. Apply the complete installation procedure from the applicable chapter for the specified secondary system, including its own layout, fastening, and expansion requirements.
  2. Install transition strips and thresholds between flooring types. Apply the Chapter 15 procedure at every line where the primary system, secondary system, or an adjacent existing floor meet.
  3. Verify transitions do not trap floating floors. Confirm the fastening method used at each transition preserves the required movement space for any floating flooring system installed in the room.

I. Trim Coordination and Repair Demonstration

  1. Coordinate baseboard, shoe molding, toe kicks, and cabinet clearances. Confirm trim covers the required expansion gap without restricting floor movement.
  2. Do not fasten trim through floating flooring. Fasten shoe molding and baseboard into the wall framing or wall base, not through the floating floor field, unless the approved system explicitly permits it.
  3. Complete the designated flooring-repair demonstration. Apply the Chapter 18 procedure for the material and damage type assigned by the capstone plan.

J. Cleanup, Final Inspection, and Handoff

  1. Clean adhesive, mortar, grout, dust, and installation residue. Use the cleaning method approved for each material before it cures beyond removal.
  2. Protect the completed floor through required cure and following-trade periods. Install rosin paper, floor protection film, or other approved protection immediately after each system reaches its specified set or cure point.
  3. Inspect joints, seams, expansion, flatness, lippage, movement, squeaks, transitions, doorway fit, and surface damage. Apply the complete Chapter 19 final quality inspection procedure to the entire room.
  4. Inspect stair or landing interfaces where included. Apply the Chapter 17 inspection criteria to any stair or landing finish connected to this room.
  5. Operate all doors and drawers crossing the finished floor. Confirm normal operation with no binding, dragging, or trip hazard at any threshold.
  6. Prepare a final deficiency list. Record every remaining item by location and assign a correction method and responsible trade.
  7. Correct and reinspect every item. Repeat the applicable chapter's inspection procedure at each corrected location.
  8. Compile moisture, flatness, acclimation, product, underlayment, adhesive, fastener, mortar, grout, transition, repair, photograph, and inspection records. Assemble the complete capstone project file in the order the work was performed.
  9. Provide cleaning, maintenance, protection, humidity, and traffic instructions. Record the manufacturer-specified instructions for each installed flooring system in the project file.
  10. Hand off the room as complete, protected, and ready for Book 15 painting and surface finishes. Confirm the room meets the Capstone Continuity Rule before handoff.
  11. Document remaining baseboard, shoe, threshold, appliance, plumbing, or finish-trade coordination. Record any item that must be completed by another trade before the room is fully finished.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect the completed capstone room using the same checks regardless of which primary and secondary flooring systems the plan specified.

  • Substrate and moisture check: confirm the re-inspection findings match or exceed the original Chapter 1 record, with any deviation documented and corrected.
  • Structural check: confirm every squeak, loose panel, and damaged subfloor area identified during re-inspection was corrected and reinspected.
  • Flatness check: confirm the final flatness reading meets the tolerance required by every installed flooring system.
  • Installation check: confirm the primary and secondary flooring systems each pass their own chapter's acceptance standards.
  • Transition and trim check: confirm every transition is seated and every trim piece is fastened without trapping a floating floor.
  • Repair check: confirm the repair demonstration blends in color, pattern, and plane.
  • Final inspection check: confirm every Chapter 19 item passed and every punch-list item was corrected and reinspected.
  • Documentation check: confirm the capstone project file is fully compiled and signed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Re-Inspection Finds a Moisture or Flatness Reading Outside the Original Chapter 1 Record

Probable cause: a new moisture source, seasonal humidity change, or a structural condition that developed since the original inspection.
Confirmation: re-test at the same representative locations used in the original Chapter 1 inspection and compare results directly.
Correction: apply the applicable Chapter 3, Chapter 4, or moisture-source correction before continuing to underlayment or finish-flooring installation.
Reinspection: repeat the moisture or flatness test at the corrected location before proceeding.

The Secondary Flooring Zone Conflicts With the Primary System's Finished Height

Probable cause: the two specified flooring systems, including underlayment, produce different finished heights at the transition line.
Confirmation: measure the finished height of each system at the transition line and compare against the approved transition plan.
Correction: install the transition profile specified in the capstone plan for the measured height difference, or adjust underlayment thickness where the plan permits it.
Reinspection: re-measure the transition fit and confirm accessibility and trip-hazard limits are met.

A Floating Floor Is Found Trapped by Trim or a Transition

Probable cause: baseboard, shoe molding, or a transition strip was fastened through the field of a floating floor rather than into the wall or subfloor beyond the floor's edge.
Confirmation: inspect the fastener path at the affected trim or transition and check for restricted movement at the perimeter.
Correction: remove the offending fastener, reposition the trim or transition per the approved method, and confirm the floor can move freely at that location.
Reinspection: walk the perimeter and confirm free movement at every wall, transition, and trim line.

The Repair Demonstration Does Not Blend With the Surrounding Floor

Probable cause: a mismatched dye lot, incorrect pile or pattern orientation, or an uncured patch material affecting plane or color.
Confirmation: compare the repair against the Chapter 18 acceptance standards for color, pattern, plane, and seam.
Correction: replace the repair material with a correctly matched piece or allow the patch material to fully cure before final assessment.
Reinspection: reinspect the repair under normal and raking light per the Chapter 19 inspection method.

A Final Inspection Item Fails After Every Chapter Was Individually Completed

Probable cause: a condition changed between chapters, such as a transition installed after trim that reintroduced a trapped floating floor, or a doorway cut before final flooring thickness was confirmed.
Confirmation: trace the failed item back to the specific chapter procedure responsible and re-check that chapter's acceptance standard at the failed location.
Correction: correct the condition using the responsible chapter's procedure without disturbing unrelated, already-accepted work.
Reinspection: repeat the full Chapter 19 final quality inspection at the corrected item and confirm no new deficiency was introduced.
