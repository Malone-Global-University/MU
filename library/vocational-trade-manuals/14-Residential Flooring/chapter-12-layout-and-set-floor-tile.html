Job Overview

This project qualifies you to lay out and set a controlled field of ceramic or porcelain floor tile on an accepted backer-board substrate. The finished work product is one completed tile field: a verified dry layout with perpendicular control lines, a documented movement-joint plan, a mixed and applied thinset mortar bed with verified coverage, a set tile field with maintained joint width and controlled lippage, and accurately cut perimeter, doorway, pipe, and obstacle tiles.

You will establish reference lines from a dry layout; plan movement and perimeter joints; mix mortar to the specified ratio; spread and comb mortar within its open time; set tile while collapsing mortar ridges; maintain joint width and pattern with spacers or leveling devices; verify mortar coverage by lifting representative tiles; and inspect the completed field for layout accuracy, coverage, lippage, and clean joints before grouting begins in Chapter 13.

This project depends on the tile-backer substrate accepted in Chapter 11. The grouting, cleaning, and sealing work that finishes this floor is covered in Chapter 13 and must not begin until the mortar set in this project has cured for the required period.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FL-067 — Establish tile layout using dry-laid reference rows and perpendicular control lines.
  • FL-068 — Mix thinset mortar using the specified water, mixing, slake, and remix procedure.
  • FL-069 — Spread mortar with the specified trowel and maintain coverage within the working time.
  • FL-070 — Set tile on the reference lines while collapsing mortar ridges and verifying coverage.
  • FL-071 — Maintain joint width, pattern, and lippage tolerance across the tile field.
  • FL-072 — Inspect tile alignment, coverage, lippage, cuts, and movement details.

Where This Skill Fits

This project follows directly from the tile-backer substrate installed and inspected in Chapter 11. It establishes every condition that Chapter 13 grouting depends on: joint width and depth, tile plane, preserved movement joints, and mortar coverage beneath every tile. A poorly planned layout, an underfilled mortar bed, or an uncontrolled lippage condition set in this project cannot be corrected by grouting and is the leading cause of cracked tile, hollow-sounding tile, and rejected tile-floor inspections.

The documentation habit established here — recording mortar batch, coverage checks, and cure conditions — is reused in Chapter 13's grouting record and in the Chapter 19 final quality inspection.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • establishing dry-laid reference rows and perpendicular control lines for a ceramic or porcelain tile field
  • planning movement and perimeter joints before mortar is mixed
  • mixing thinset mortar using the specified water ratio, mixing time, slake period, and remix procedure
  • spreading and combing mortar within its open time and back-buttering tile where required
  • setting tile on the established reference lines, maintaining joint width, pattern, plane, and lippage tolerance
  • verifying mortar coverage by lifting representative tiles and correcting low coverage before it hardens
  • cutting and setting perimeter, doorway, pipe, and obstacle tiles with required clearance
  • cleaning mortar from tile faces and joints before it hardens and protecting the field through cure

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • set tile on a substrate that has not passed the Chapter 11 backer-board inspection
  • fill designated movement joints with rigid grout or mortar
  • use mortar as a leveling layer to compensate for an unresolved flatness deficiency
  • grout, clean, or seal the completed tile field — that work is covered in Chapter 13 and may not begin before the specified mortar cure period has elapsed
  • proceed with tile setting when a stop-work condition identified in this chapter is present and uncorrected

Any layout, coverage, plane, or movement-joint condition that fails verification in this chapter must be corrected before grouting begins.

Trade Note

Every dry layout in this chapter is completed and adjusted before any mortar is mixed. A tile field set directly against a wall without a dry-laid check for narrow cuts, doorway alignment, or pattern drift is the most common cause of an avoidable rework documented in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not mix mortar until the dry layout has been checked, control lines are established, movement joints are planned, and the backer-board substrate has passed the Chapter 11 inspection. A tile field started before the layout is confirmed must be treated as incomplete and corrected before mortar is mixed.

Shop Tip

Lift a representative tile from the first few square feet set each session and check the mortar coverage on both the tile back and the substrate. Confirming coverage early in the session, while mortar is still workable, allows immediate correction; discovering low coverage after the field has hardened requires removing and resetting the affected tile.

Layout, Mortar Mixing, and Coverage Verification

Every control line established in this chapter is derived from a dry-laid reference row checked against room dimensions, doorway alignment, and cut sizes at every wall. Do not chalk a control line directly from a wall measurement without first confirming it against a dry layout. Where the mortar manufacturer specifies a water ratio, mixing time, slake period, remix procedure, open time, or trowel size, follow that requirement exactly and record it on the Tile-Setting Record.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Layout Before Mixing Mortar

Dry-lay reference rows in both directions and adjust the layout to avoid tile cuts narrower than the manufacturer-approved minimum before mixing any mortar. A layout adjusted after mortar has been spread wastes material and risks tile being set into mortar that has exceeded its open time.

Critical Safety Cue — Wet-Saw Cutting and Silica Dust

Cut tile only with a wet saw or method that controls silica dust. Do not dry-cut tile with an abrasive blade in a manner that generates airborne silica dust. Use eye protection and keep the cutting area separated from the mortar-mixing and setting zones as established in Chapter 2.

Field Safety Cue — Extended Kneeling and Repetitive Work

Use knee pads and change position regularly during extended tile-setting work. Keep the mixing and cutting areas clear of trip hazards, hoses, and standing water that could cause a slip while carrying mortar or tile.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Mortar Mixing and Open Time

Mix mortar using the exact water ratio, mixing time, slake period, and remix procedure specified by the mortar manufacturer. Do not add water after the permitted mixing sequence. Spread only the area that can be covered with tile within the mortar's specified open time.

E. Layout and Reference-Line Establishment

  1. Review tile size, pattern, room dimensions, joint width, movement-joint plan, mortar, trowel, coverage requirement, and cut locations. Confirm every value against the approved tile assembly specification before proceeding.
  2. Confirm the substrate has passed inspection. Verify the Chapter 11 backer-board inspection record shows an approved plane, support, fastening, and seam condition for the inspection area.
  3. Dry-lay reference rows. Place tile without mortar in both directions across the inspection area using the specified joint width.
  4. Establish perpendicular control lines. Chalk two lines perpendicular to each other from the confirmed dry layout and verify the intersection is square using a measured diagonal check.
  5. Adjust layout to avoid unacceptable narrow cuts and poor doorway alignment. Shift the control lines and re-check the dry layout until every perimeter cut meets the minimum width and the doorway alignment is acceptable.
  6. Plan movement and perimeter joints. Mark the location of every required movement joint at the perimeter, at changes in plane, and within the field before mortar is mixed.

F. Tile Sorting and Cutting Setup

  1. Sort tile for color, size, pattern, and damage. Set aside any tile with visible defects before it enters the field.
  2. Cut sample pieces and verify wet-saw or cutter setup. Confirm the cutting equipment produces a clean, accurate edge before cutting field quantities.

G. Mortar Mixing

  1. Mix mortar using the specified water, mixing, slake, and remix procedure. Measure water to the specified ratio and mix for the specified time.
  2. Do not add water after the permitted mixing sequence. Discard and remix a batch that has begun to stiffen rather than adding water to restore workability.

H. Mortar Spreading and Tile Setting

  1. Spread only the area that can be covered within open time. Limit each spread section to the area the crew can set with tile before the mortar skins over.
  2. Comb mortar in one consistent direction. Use the specified notched trowel to comb ridges in a single direction across the spread area.
  3. Back-butter tile where required. Apply mortar to the tile back in addition to the substrate when the tile size or mortar manufacturer requires it.
  4. Set the first tile on the reference lines. Align the first tile precisely to the established control lines before continuing the course.
  5. Move the tile perpendicular to mortar ridges to collapse them. Use a slight perpendicular motion when setting each tile to fully collapse the combed ridges into solid contact.
  6. Install spacers or leveling devices where approved. Place the specified spacer or leveling-device system at every joint as tile is set.
  7. Maintain joint width and pattern. Check joint width against the specification at regular intervals across the field.
  8. Check tile plane and lippage frequently. Run a straightedge across adjoining tiles at regular intervals and correct any tile exceeding the lippage tolerance before the mortar sets.

I. Coverage Verification and Correction

  1. Lift representative tiles to verify mortar coverage. Remove at least one tile from each spread section while the mortar is still workable and inspect the back of the tile and the corresponding substrate area.
  2. Correct low coverage, skinned mortar, or hollow areas immediately. Re-comb or re-spread mortar and reset the tile before continuing to the next section.
  3. Keep mortar out of movement joints and excessive joint depth. Tool or trim mortar away from every marked movement joint before it hardens.

J. Perimeter, Cleanup, and Cure Protection

  1. Cut perimeter, doorway, pipe, and obstacle tiles accurately. Measure each location directly and cut the tile to the actual dimension.
  2. Maintain required perimeter clearance. Preserve the specified gap at every wall, column, and fixed object.
  3. Clean mortar from tile faces and joints before it hardens. Wipe excess mortar from the tile surface and from within the joints using a damp sponge before it sets.
  4. Protect the installation from traffic and vibration through cure. Restrict foot traffic and following-trade work for the mortar manufacturer's specified cure period.
  5. Inspect layout, joints, coverage, lippage, cuts, and movement details. Complete the full field inspection described in the Common Readiness Inspection section before proceeding to Chapter 13.
  6. Document mortar, trowel, batch, coverage checks, and cure. Record every required entry on the Tile-Setting Record.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed tile field using the same checks regardless of tile size, pattern, or room shape.

  • Layout check: confirm control lines are square and match the confirmed dry layout with no unacceptable narrow cuts.
  • Coverage check: confirm every lifted representative tile showed full mortar contact with no low coverage left uncorrected.
  • Joint check: confirm joint width and pattern are consistent across the entire field.
  • Lippage check: confirm every checked joint falls within the lippage tolerance.
  • Movement-joint check: confirm every designated movement joint is free of mortar intrusion.
  • Cut check: confirm every perimeter, doorway, pipe, and obstacle tile is accurately cut with required clearance.
  • Cleanliness check: confirm tile faces and joints are free of hardened mortar residue.
  • Documentation check: confirm the Tile-Setting Record is fully completed.

Complete the Tile-Setting Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 13.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Lifted Representative Tile Shows Low or Skinned Mortar Coverage

Probable cause: the mortar skinned over before the tile was set, the trowel notch size did not match the tile and substrate requirement, or the tile was not moved perpendicular to the ridges to collapse them.
Confirmation: lift additional tiles from the same spread section and compare coverage against the required percentage.
Correction: remove the affected tile, re-comb or re-spread fresh mortar within its open time, back-butter the tile if required, and reset it on the reference lines.
Reinspection: lift the corrected tile again to confirm full coverage before proceeding to the next section.

Lippage at a Joint Exceeds the Specified Tolerance

Probable cause: an uncorrected minor substrate deviation, an inconsistent mortar bed thickness, or a leveling device not fully engaged before the mortar set.
Confirmation: re-check the joint with a straightedge across both directions and compare against the specified tolerance.
Correction: while the mortar remains workable, lift and reset the high tile with corrected mortar thickness; if the mortar has hardened, remove the tile per the Chapter 18 repair procedure.
Reinspection: recheck the corrected joint and the adjoining joints for consistent plane.

A Perimeter Cut Is Narrower Than the Approved Minimum

Probable cause: the control lines were established directly from a wall measurement without a confirmed dry layout, or the layout was not adjusted before mortar was spread.
Confirmation: re-measure the affected cut against the minimum width specified in the tile assembly specification.
Correction: where mortar has not yet been spread in the affected area, shift the control lines and re-lay the dry reference row; where tile has already been set, document the condition and correct before grouting.
Reinspection: re-verify the dry layout across the full width of the affected wall before resuming mortar spreading.

Mortar Has Intruded Into a Designated Movement Joint

Probable cause: the movement-joint location was not marked before mortar was spread, or mortar was not trimmed away from the joint before it hardened.
Confirmation: inspect the full length of every marked movement joint for mortar contamination.
Correction: remove intruded mortar from the joint while it remains workable using a margin trowel or scoring tool; where the mortar has hardened, remove it mechanically without damaging adjoining tile.
Reinspection: confirm the joint is fully clear and ready for the flexible sealant installed in Chapter 13.

Mortar Batch Stiffened Before the Spread Area Was Fully Set

Probable cause: too large an area was spread relative to the mortar's open time, or ambient temperature and humidity shortened the working time.
Confirmation: check the mortar surface for skinning by lightly touching an unset area with a gloved finger.
Correction: remove the skinned mortar, discard it, and re-spread a smaller section sized to the crew's actual setting pace.
Reinspection: verify coverage on the first tile set into the re-spread section before continuing.

Hardened Mortar Residue Remains on the Tile Face After Setting

Probable cause: mortar was not cleaned from the tile surface before it began to set.
Confirmation: inspect the tile face under raking light for residue and haze.
Correction: remove hardened residue using the method approved for the tile finish without scratching the surface; consult the tile manufacturer's cleaning instructions before using any abrasive method.
Reinspection: re-inspect the cleaned area under the same lighting condition.

Cleanup

This project ends with the tile field fully set, cleaned, and protected through the specified cure period, not yet grouted.

  • Clean mixing tools, trowels, and buckets before mortar residue hardens on them
  • Dispose of mortar waste and rinse water according to local requirements; do not rinse mortar residue into a floor drain not approved for that purpose
  • Leave spacers or leveling devices in place until the mortar has cured sufficiently for their removal per manufacturer instructions
  • Barricade or restrict traffic across the field for the specified cure period
  • Store the Tile-Setting Record with the PPE and documentation station for reference by Chapter 13

Field Terms

Control Line
A chalk or marked reference line, established perpendicular to a second control line, used to align every course of tile in the field.
Dry-Laid Reference Row
A row of tile placed without mortar to verify layout, joint width, and cut sizes before setting begins.
Open Time
The period after mortar is spread during which it remains workable enough to properly bond tile, as specified by the mortar manufacturer.
Mortar Coverage
The percentage of a tile's back surface, and the corresponding substrate area, in full contact with mortar after the tile is set and pressed into place.
Lippage
A vertical difference in height between the edges of two adjoining tiles, measured at the joint.
Movement Joint
A joint at the perimeter, at changes in plane, or within a large field that must remain flexible and free of rigid mortar or grout to accommodate structural and thermal movement.
Back-Buttering
Applying mortar directly to the back of a tile in addition to the substrate to achieve required coverage.
Leveling Device
A spacer-and-clip or strap-and-wedge system used to hold adjoining tile edges in a common plane while mortar cures.
Tile-Setting Record
The document recording layout confirmation, mortar product and batch, coverage checks, joint and lippage verification, and cure conditions.
