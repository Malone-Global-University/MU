Job Overview

This project qualifies you to measure a floor area against the flatness tolerance required by the selected flooring system and to correct that area to within tolerance using the approved patching, planing, sanding, or grinding method for the identified substrate. The finished work product is one flatness-corrected, cleaned, and reverified floor area, ready for the underlayment or moisture-barrier work covered in Chapter 5.

You will confirm the manufacturer's flatness requirement, measure the existing surface with a straightedge across multiple directions, mark high spots and low areas, correct minor wood ridges by planing or sanding, correct concrete surface irregularities by approved grinding or patching, mix and apply floor patch or self-leveling compound within its working time, protect the repair through its cure period, and reverify flatness using the same measuring method before the area is released for the next chapter.

This project depends on the substrate, moisture, and structural findings from Project 01 and the structural repairs completed in Project 03. It supplies the flat, cleaned surface that Project 05 depends on for underlayment and moisture-barrier installation.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FL-019 — Check floor flatness against the manufacturer-specified tolerance using the specified straightedge or survey method.
  • FL-020 — Identify high spots requiring planing, sanding, or grinding correction.
  • FL-021 — Fill low areas using the approved floor patch or self-leveling compound within its working time.
  • FL-022 — Apply floor patch using the specified primer, mixing ratio, trowel, and cure procedure.
  • FL-023 — Sand or plane minor ridges using dust extraction and approved depth control.
  • FL-024 — Inspect the corrected surface against the manufacturer's flatness tolerance.

Where This Skill Fits

Flatness correction sits between subfloor repair and underlayment installation in every chapter that follows. Chapter 5's moisture barrier and underlayment, Chapters 6 through 10's plank and resilient flooring, and Chapters 11 through 13's tile backer board and mortar coverage all depend on the flatness deviation being corrected and cured before that later work begins. An uncorrected high spot telegraphs through resilient flooring, causes hollow spots and cracked grout under tile, and prevents plank locking systems from engaging fully.

The measurement discipline established here — placing the straightedge across multiple directions, marking every deviation, and recording the largest measured gap — is the same method reused for the final flatness check in Chapter 19 and the capstone flatness recheck in Chapter 20.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • measuring floor flatness using the manufacturer-specified straightedge length and tolerance
  • marking high spots, low areas, abrupt ridges, seams, and transitions
  • planing or sanding minor wood-substrate ridges using dust extraction and approved depth control
  • correcting concrete-substrate irregularities using approved grinding or scarifying after hazardous-material and silica controls are established
  • mixing and applying floor patch or self-leveling compound within its specified working time and depth
  • protecting the correction through its specified cure time and reverifying flatness with the same measuring method

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • correct structural movement, loose panels, unrepaired squeaks, or unresolved cracks requiring engineering evaluation — that work belongs to Chapter 3 or a structural professional and must be complete before this project begins
  • use patch, self-leveling material, or grinding to bridge active cracks, movement joints, unsupported seams, or structural deflection
  • reduce a structural wood panel's thickness below the manufacturer- or code-permitted limit through sanding or planing
  • grind or scarify concrete before hazardous-material screening and silica dust controls required by Project 01 and Project 02 are in place
  • install underlayment, moisture barrier, or finish flooring over an uncured patch or an unverified flatness reading — that work is covered in Chapters 5 through 17

Any structural movement, active crack, or hazardous-material condition discovered during flatness measurement must stop this project and be escalated per the Failure Points section before correction continues.

Trade Note

Flat is not the same as level. A floor may be intentionally pitched or sloped for drainage or design and still meet the manufacturer's flatness tolerance. Record every flatness deviation against the straightedge independent of whether the floor is sloped, and never correct an intentional slope by adding patch across the entire area.

Setup Standard

Do not begin flatness correction until Project 03 structural repairs are complete and reinspected, the substrate is clean, and any hazardous legacy material identified in Project 01 has been cleared. A flatness measurement taken over an uncleaned or unrepaired surface must be treated as unreliable and repeated after cleaning or repair.

Shop Tip

Record the largest measured deviation and its exact location before starting any correction. Re-measuring from the same marked points after patching, sanding, or grinding is the only way to confirm the correction actually closed the gap rather than shifted it to an adjacent point.

Flatness Tolerance and Measurement Verification

Confirm the flooring manufacturer's flatness tolerance and required straightedge length before any measurement begins. Use the exact straightedge length specified for the selected flooring system; a shorter or longer straightedge than specified produces a reading that cannot be compared against the stated tolerance. Record the largest measured deviation and its location before beginning any correction.

Measurement Cue — Use the Specified Straightedge Length

Use only the straightedge length specified by the flooring manufacturer for the selected flooring system. A 6-foot and a 10-foot straightedge measure different flatness conditions and are not interchangeable. Record which length was used at each marked point.

Critical Safety Cue — Silica Dust from Concrete Grinding

Do not grind, scarify, or mechanically abrade concrete without the wet-cutting, local dust extraction, HEPA vacuuming, and respiratory protection required for silica dust control. Confirm hazardous-material and silica controls from Project 01 and Project 02 are in place before any concrete correction begins. Do not dry-sweep fine dust.

Field Safety Cue — Sanding and Planing Dust

Operate sanders and planes with dust extraction connected and running. Ventilate the work area and wear a respirator or dust mask rated for wood dust. Do not sand or plane legacy flooring or adhesive that has not been cleared as free of hazardous material.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Mixing, Working Time, and Cure

Mix floor patch or self-leveling compound using the exact water ratio and mixing time specified by the manufacturer. Apply within the stated working time and protect the repair through the full specified cure period before rechecking flatness or proceeding to underlayment. Do not add unapproved water, solvents, or accelerators.

F. Confirm Requirements and Prior Conditions

  1. Review the flooring manufacturer's flatness requirement and approved patching or grinding products. Record the required straightedge length, permitted deviation, and identified product names on the Flatness Correction Record.
  2. Confirm that structural movement, loose panels, cracks requiring engineering, and moisture problems are resolved. Verify against the Project 01 Flooring-Readiness Plan and the Project 03 structural-repair record before proceeding.

G. Clean and Survey the Substrate

  1. Clean the substrate completely. Remove debris, dust, adhesive residue, and loose material before any measurement begins.
  2. Place the specified straightedge or use the approved survey method across multiple directions. Cover the full correction area, including areas near walls and transitions.
  3. Mark high spots, low areas, abrupt ridges, seams, and transitions. Use chalk or marking tape at every point where the measured gap approaches or exceeds the manufacturer's tolerance.
  4. Record the largest measured deviation. State its exact location on the Flatness Correction Record before beginning any correction.
  5. Distinguish isolated ridges from broad elevation changes. Identify whether each marked point requires sanding or planing, patch, self-leveling compound, or grinding based on its shape and extent.

H. Correct Wood-Substrate High Spots

  1. For wood substrates, tighten or repair panels before surface correction. Confirm any remaining fastening or panel-seam issue is corrected under Project 03 methods before sanding or planing.
  2. Plane or sand minor wood ridges using dust extraction and approved depth control. Operate the sander or plane with dust extraction connected and check depth against the marked deviation as work proceeds.
  3. Do not reduce structural panel thickness below the permitted limit. Stop correction at a marked point if continued removal would reduce the panel below the manufacturer- or code-permitted thickness, and escalate per the Failure Points section.

I. Correct Concrete-Substrate Irregularities

  1. For concrete, use approved grinding or scarifying only after hazardous-material and silica controls are established. Confirm wet-cutting, local extraction, HEPA vacuuming, and respiratory protection are in place before starting.
  2. Vacuum all dust with HEPA equipment. Clear the corrected area completely before priming.

J. Prime and Apply Patch or Leveling Compound

  1. Apply primer where required by the patch product. Follow the manufacturer's stated coverage rate and drying time before mixing patch.
  2. Mix floor patch or leveling material using the specified water ratio and mixing time. Record the product name and batch on the Flatness Correction Record.
  3. Apply patch within its working time. Spread and screed to the depth marked from the flatness survey.
  4. Feather edges only where the product permits it. Confirm the manufacturer's instructions allow feathering before tapering any edge.
  5. Do not use patch to bridge active cracks, movement joints, unsupported seams, or structural deflection. Stop and escalate per the Failure Points section if any such condition is found beneath a marked low area.

K. Cure, Reverify, and Release

  1. Protect the repair through the specified cure time. Restrict traffic, moisture exposure, and further application until the manufacturer's stated cure period has elapsed.
  2. Recheck flatness with the same measuring method. Use the same straightedge length at the same marked points recorded in Step G.
  3. Mark remaining deficiencies. Identify any point that still exceeds the manufacturer's tolerance after the first correction.
  4. Apply additional compatible correction only after the prior layer permits it. Confirm the manufacturer's recoat or build-up instructions before adding a second application.
  5. Confirm transitions and finished-floor height. Recheck the finished-floor height at each transition point identified in Project 01 against the corrected surface.
  6. Document product, batch, application depth, cure, and final flatness readings. Complete the Flatness Correction Record before releasing the area to Project 05.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every completed correction area using the same checks regardless of substrate type or flooring system.

  • Tolerance check: confirm the manufacturer's flatness tolerance and required straightedge length were recorded before measuring.
  • Survey check: confirm every marked high spot and low area has a recorded deviation measurement.
  • Correction check: confirm every marked point was corrected using the method appropriate to its substrate and cause.
  • Dust-control check: confirm HEPA vacuuming and silica or wood-dust controls were used for every sanding, planing, or grinding operation.
  • Cure check: confirm the full manufacturer-specified cure time elapsed before reverification.
  • Reverification check: confirm every originally marked point was rechecked with the same straightedge and method.
  • Documentation check: confirm the Flatness Correction Record is fully completed.

Complete the Flatness Correction Record in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 05.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Flatness Deviation Still Exceeds Tolerance After the First Correction

Probable cause: insufficient patch depth, shrinkage during cure, an underestimated deviation, or a high spot not fully corrected before patching the surrounding low area.
Confirmation: re-measure at the same marked points with the same straightedge length and compare against the original recorded deviation.
Correction: apply an additional compatible layer only after the manufacturer's recoat instructions permit it, or complete additional sanding, planing, or grinding at the remaining high spot.
Reinspection: repeat the flatness survey at the corrected points after the additional layer or correction cures.

An Active Crack or Unsupported Seam Is Found Beneath a Marked Low Area

Probable cause: a structural condition was not fully resolved in Project 03, or a new condition developed since the last inspection.
Confirmation: inspect the crack or seam for movement and compare against the Project 03 structural-repair record.
Correction: stop patching at the affected location and escalate to Project 03 repair or a structural professional before continuing flatness correction.
Reinspection: resume flatness correction at the location only after the structural condition is corrected and reinspected.

Sanding or Planing Approaches the Permitted Panel-Thickness Limit

Probable cause: a high spot is deeper than initially estimated, or repeated passes have removed more material than planned.
Confirmation: measure remaining panel thickness at the location and compare against the manufacturer- or code-permitted minimum.
Correction: stop mechanical removal at the location and correct the remaining deviation using an approved patch or leveling compound instead of further sanding or planing.
Reinspection: recheck flatness and panel thickness at the location after the alternate correction cures.

Patch or Leveling Compound Cracks, Debonds, or Shows Pinholes After Cure

Probable cause: incorrect mixing ratio, application beyond the working time, inadequate primer, insufficient substrate cleaning, or curing under an unapproved temperature or humidity condition.
Confirmation: inspect the affected area for bond failure, cracking pattern, and pinhole depth, and compare mixing and application records against manufacturer instructions.
Correction: remove the defective patch to sound substrate, reprime, and reapply using the corrected mixing ratio and working-time procedure.
Reinspection: reverify flatness and bond at the corrected location after the new application cures.

Silica or Wood Dust Is Found Outside the Controlled Work Area

Probable cause: inadequate local extraction, a disconnected dust-collection hose, or dry-sweeping instead of HEPA vacuuming.
Confirmation: inspect the dust-extraction setup and the boundary of the controlled work area established in Project 02.
Correction: stop grinding, sanding, or planing until dust extraction and containment are restored, and HEPA-vacuum the affected area.
Reinspection: confirm dust containment before resuming any mechanical surface correction.

Finished-Floor Height Conflict Appears After Patch Application

Probable cause: the applied patch depth raised the finished surface beyond the height anticipated at a doorway or transition identified in Project 01.
Confirmation: re-measure the finished-floor height at the affected transition and compare against the Project 01 findings.
Correction: record the conflict for resolution in Chapter 15 transition planning, or adjust the correction depth in adjacent areas within the manufacturer's permitted range.
Reinspection: confirm the revised height calculation before releasing the area to Project 05.

Cleanup

This project ends with a clean, cured, and flatness-verified surface, free of dust, debris, and patch residue outside the corrected areas.

  • HEPA-vacuum all sanding, planing, and grinding dust from the work area and adjacent surfaces
  • Clean mixing tools, trowels, and gauge rakes before patch material cures on them
  • Store unused patch, primer, and abrasive materials per the manufacturer's shelf-life instructions
  • Store the Flatness Correction Record with the Project 01 Flooring-Readiness Plan for reference by later projects

Field Terms

Flatness Deviation
The measured gap between the underside of the specified straightedge and the floor surface at any point along its length.
High Spot
A location where the floor surface contacts or nearly contacts the straightedge while adjacent areas show a measurable gap.
Low Area
A location where the measured gap beneath the straightedge exceeds the manufacturer's permitted tolerance.
Working Time
The period after mixing during which floor patch or self-leveling compound remains workable, stated by the manufacturer.
Feathering
Tapering the edge of an applied patch to a thin, smooth transition, permitted only where the product instructions allow it.
Cure Time
The manufacturer-specified period the patch or leveling compound must be protected from traffic, moisture, or further application before proceeding.
Scarifying
Mechanically removing a thin layer of concrete surface to correct irregularities, performed only under approved silica-dust controls.
Flatness Correction Record
The document recording the manufacturer's tolerance, survey readings, correction method, product and batch data, cure time, and final reverification readings.
