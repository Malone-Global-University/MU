Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install finish trim on stair treads, risers, and nosings on one controlled stair section while preserving the rise, run, tread depth, and nosing geometry the structural stair has already been inspected and accepted with. The finished work product is a securely installed set of riser and tread finish pieces with supported nosing transitions, a documented set of final stair measurements, and a completed stair-finish safety inspection.

You will verify that the structural stair has already passed inspection, measure every tread and riser independently, inspect for movement and protruding fasteners, correct structural or fastening defects through the responsible trade before finish work begins, barricade the stair and establish safe alternate access, install riser and tread trim in the approved sequence, fit nosing or return trim while maintaining uniform projection, fasten and adhere trim without leaving proud fasteners in the walking surface, and hold the stair out of service through the required cure period.

This project depends directly on Project 16. The skirt board installed there establishes the wall-side termination that riser and tread trim in this project must fit against. This project also depends on the Chapter 1 readiness survey and on the structural stair inspection referenced throughout this chapter; neither this project nor Project 16 authorizes any change to rise, run, tread depth, nosing, guard, or handrail geometry.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FC-097 — Inspect stair surfaces for movement, damage, protruding fasteners, and moisture before finish trim is installed.
  • FC-098 — Cut riser trim to fit between skirts or walls in the approved sequence.
  • FC-099 — Install tread trim without reducing required tread depth.
  • FC-100 — Fit nosing transitions using the specified profile, support, and uniform projection.
  • FC-101 — Secure stair trim with adhesive and fasteners that leave no exposed or proud heads in the walking surface.
  • FC-102 — Inspect completed stair-finish work for trip, movement, projection, and geometry hazards.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 16 established the finish skirt board fitted to the existing tread-and-riser profile without altering stair geometry. This project installs the riser, tread, and nosing trim that completes the finished walking surface against that skirt. The final stair measurements and safety inspection produced here become the controlling stair-trim record referenced in the Chapter 20 capstone, which repeats this inspection whenever the capstone room includes an inspected stair assembly.

The stop-work and barricade discipline used in this project also controls Chapter 20 Step 21 and Step 34, which reference this chapter directly for any capstone room containing stair trim.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved tread, riser, nosing, landing, skirt, adhesive, fastener, and finish details for the stair being trimmed
  • verifying that the structural stair and its geometry have already passed inspection
  • measuring every tread and riser independently and inspecting for squeaks, movement, damage, protruding fasteners, and moisture
  • referring structural or fastening defects to the responsible trade for correction before finish installation continues
  • barricading the stair and establishing safe alternate access for the duration of the work
  • cutting and dry-fitting riser trim and installing it in the approved sequence
  • measuring, cutting, and fitting tread finish pieces without reducing required tread depth
  • installing nosing or return trim using the specified profile and support while maintaining uniform nosing projection
  • applying adhesive at the specified coverage and fastening trim without proud or exposed heads in the walking surface
  • verifying rise, run, tread depth, nosing, level, and movement at every completed step, checking landing and floor transitions, correcting hazards, and holding the stair out of service through the required cure period

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • alter stair structure, rise, run, tread depth, nosing, guard, or handrail geometry; this is finish trim over an already-accepted structural stair
  • proceed with finish installation over a structural or fastening defect; such defects are referred to the responsible trade and reinspected before finish work continues
  • return the stair to service before the required cure period has elapsed and the final safety inspection has been completed
  • use finish trim, filler, or adhesive buildup to conceal an out-of-tolerance tread depth, riser height, or nosing projection

Any tread, riser, or nosing condition that fails this project's inspection standards must be corrected, reinspected, and documented before the stair is returned to service.

Trade Note

Stair-finish trim is a walking-surface installation, not a decorative assembly. A riser or tread piece installed slightly out of the recorded structural dimension changes the rise, run, or tread depth a person's foot expects at that step, which is a documented cause of stair falls even when the difference is within what looks acceptable by eye. Every dimension in this chapter is checked against the structural measurement recorded before finish trim begins, not against the adjacent finished step.

Setup Standard

Do not begin any step in Section F until the structural stair and its geometry have been confirmed as accepted and the stair has been barricaded with safe alternate access established. A stair that has not been formally accepted for finish work must be treated as unverified until the responsible trade or a qualified professional confirms it.

Shop Tip

Measure and record every tread and riser before cutting the first piece of trim. Treads and risers on the same flight commonly vary by small amounts even on an accepted stair, and cutting each piece to its own measured step rather than to a single averaged dimension is what keeps the finished nosing projection uniform from step to step.

Reference Conventions

Common Field Fundamentals

Stair-finish trim differs from every other trim category in this book in one controlling respect: the completed work is a walking surface with fall and trip consequences, not a decorative or wall-mounted assembly. Every dimension installed in this chapter is checked against the structural reference measurement recorded before finish work began, not against the adjacent finished step or the learner's visual judgment. The stair remains barricaded and out of service from the start of Section E through the completion of the cure period in Section K.

Measuring Every Tread and Riser Independently

Do not assume every tread and riser in a flight matches the drawn dimension exactly. Measure and record the rise, run, and tread depth of every individual step against the structural reference before cutting any finish piece, and cut each riser and tread to its own measured step rather than to one averaged dimension for the flight.

Critical Safety Cue — Stair Remains Barricaded Throughout This Project

Barricade the stair and establish safe alternate access before removing any tread or riser covering, before dry-fitting any piece, and before beginning any cut, fastening, or adhesive step described in this chapter. Do not allow foot traffic on the stair while trim is loose, adhesive is curing, or fasteners have not been fully set. Keep the barricade in place through the required cure period specified in Section K.

Code Alert — Finish Trim Does Not Change Accepted Stair Geometry

This project may not alter rise, run, tread depth, nosing projection, guard, or handrail geometry established by the accepted structural stair. Verify local code requirements for maximum rise and run variation within a flight before beginning finish installation, and confirm that the finished nosing projection matches the value in the approved tread, riser, and nosing detail.

Measurement Cue — Uniform Nosing Projection

Measure and record the nosing projection at every tread against the structural reference measurement. A nosing that projects more or less than the adjacent steps by more than the tolerance stated in the approved detail is a trip hazard even where each individual tread depth otherwise passes inspection.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Adhesive Coverage and Cure

Apply adhesive at the coverage rate stated in the manufacturer's instructions for the tread, riser, or nosing product being installed, and hold the stair out of service for the full cure period stated by the manufacturer before foot traffic resumes.

C. Material Takeoff

  • Controlling documents: approved tread, riser, nosing, landing, skirt, adhesive, fastener, and finish details for the stair being trimmed.
  • Riser, tread, and nosing stock: quantity and stock size per the approved detail, acclimated per the finish specification before cutting.

  • Fasteners: type, size, and pattern per the approved fastener schedule; do not substitute a fastener not identified in the schedule.

D. Fastener and Hardware Schedule

Use the fastener type, size, spacing, and installation direction stated in the approved stair-finish specification for each of the following joints: riser-to-stringer or riser-to-skirt, tread-to-stringer or tread-to-subtread, and nosing-to-tread. Set every fastener below the walking surface without crushing the profile. Do not use a fastener not identified in the approved schedule.

F. Document Review and Structural Verification

  1. Review the approved tread, riser, nosing, landing, skirt, adhesive, fastener, and finish details. Confirm the documents correspond to the stair section being trimmed and record the references on the stair-trim worksheet.
  2. Verify the structural stair and geometry have passed inspection. Obtain and record the structural inspection reference before proceeding; do not begin Section G without this confirmation.

G. Pre-Installation Inspection

  1. Measure every tread and riser independently. Record rise, run, and tread depth at each step against the structural reference measurement before cutting any finish piece.
  2. Inspect for squeaks, movement, damage, protruding fasteners, and moisture. Walk each step under load, check for deflection, and use the moisture meter at any suspect area.
  3. Correct structural or fastening defects through the responsible trade before finish installation. Document each defect, refer it to the responsible trade, and reinspect the corrected step before continuing.

H. Barricade and Access Control

  1. Barricade the stair and establish safe alternate access. Install physical barriers and signage at both ends of the affected flight, and confirm and communicate the alternate access route before any tread covering is removed.

I. Riser Installation

  1. Cut riser trim to fit between skirts or walls. Cut each riser piece to the width measured at that specific riser location, not to an averaged flight dimension.
  2. Dry-fit each riser and verify height relationships. Confirm the dry-fit piece preserves the structural reference rise before proceeding to the next riser.
  3. Install risers in the approved sequence. Follow the sequence stated in the approved stair-finish specification, coordinating each riser with the skirt board terminations from Project 16.

J. Tread and Nosing Installation

  1. Measure and cut tread finish pieces. Cut each tread piece to the depth and width measured at that specific step.
  2. Fit tread pieces without reducing required tread depth. Confirm the finished tread depth meets or exceeds the structural reference measurement before fastening.
  3. Install nosing or return trim using the specified profile and support. Fit each nosing piece per the approved detail, coping or mitering returns as specified.
  4. Maintain uniform nosing projection. Measure the projection at each tread against the structural reference measurement and against the adjacent installed treads.
  5. Apply adhesive at the specified coverage. Use the trowel or notched applicator to achieve the coverage rate stated in the manufacturer's instructions.
  6. Fasten trim without leaving exposed or proud heads in walking surfaces. Set every fastener below the surface without crushing the profile, using the pattern in the Fastener and Hardware Schedule.
  7. Clamp or weight components only as permitted. Use only the clamping or weighting method stated in the manufacturer's instructions for the adhesive and trim product in use.
  8. Remove excess adhesive before cure. Clean squeeze-out from the walking surface and joint lines before the adhesive skins over.

K. Verification, Correction, and Cure

  1. Verify each completed step for rise, run, tread depth, nosing, level, and movement. Record each measurement against the structural reference on the stair-trim worksheet before moving to the next step.
  2. Check transitions at the top landing and bottom floor. Confirm the finished trim integrates cleanly with the landing and floor references without creating a trip edge.
  3. Correct loose, proud, slippery, uneven, or trip-producing conditions. Identify the affected step, correct the condition, and reinspect before continuing.
  4. Keep the stair out of service through the required cure period. Maintain the barricade and alternate access until the manufacturer-specified cure period has fully elapsed.
  5. Document final stair measurements and safety inspection. Record final rise, run, tread depth, and nosing projection for every step, along with the completed safety inspection and the date the stair was returned to service.

Common Cutting Inspection

Apply the following checks to every riser, tread, and nosing piece before it is fastened or adhered permanently.

  • Square-cut check: confirm each riser and tread end is square to the reference face before dry-fitting.
  • Dimension check: confirm each cut piece matches the individually measured rise, run, or tread depth for that specific step, not an averaged flight dimension.
  • Nosing check: confirm each nosing or return piece maintains uniform projection matching the structural reference measurement.
  • Fastener check: confirm every fastener is set below the walking surface without crushing the profile before moving to the next step.
  • Waste check: do not fasten or adhere a piece that fails the dimension or nosing check; recut or replace it.

Complete the Stair-Finish Safety Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before removing the barricade and returning the stair to service.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Finished Tread Depth Reads Shorter Than the Structural Reference

Probable cause: the tread trim or nosing was cut using an averaged flight dimension rather than the measurement recorded for that specific step, or the riser trim above was installed proud of its reference line.
Confirmation: re-measure the finished tread depth at that step and compare it to the recorded structural reference measurement.
Correction: remove and recut the tread or nosing piece, or correct the riser installation, so the finished tread depth meets or exceeds the structural reference measurement.
Reinspection: repeat the tread-depth measurement at the corrected step and at the two adjacent steps.

Nosing Projection Varies Between Adjacent Treads

Probable cause: nosing pieces were fit to the adjacent installed tread by eye instead of to the structural reference measurement for each step.
Confirmation: measure nosing projection at every tread in the flight and compare each to the structural reference measurement.
Correction: remove and refit the out-of-tolerance nosing piece to match the structural reference measurement.
Reinspection: repeat the nosing-projection measurement across the full flight.

A Tread Deflects or Squeaks After Installation

Probable cause: inadequate adhesive coverage, a missed fastener location, or an unresolved structural defect that was not corrected before finish installation.
Confirmation: walk the affected tread under load and inspect the underside and fastening pattern where accessible.
Correction: refer an unresolved structural cause to the responsible trade; otherwise remove and reinstall the tread with the specified adhesive coverage and fastener pattern.
Reinspection: repeat the load check and confirm no deflection or squeak remains.

A Fastener Head Is Proud in the Walking Surface

Probable cause: the fastener was not set below the surface, or was driven at an angle that raised the head on one side.
Confirmation: run a hand across the surface and inspect under raking light for a raised head.
Correction: set the fastener flush below the surface without crushing the profile, or remove and replace it with a properly seated fastener.
Reinspection: repeat the hand and raking-light check across the full tread and nosing.

The Stair Was Returned to Service Before the Cure Period Elapsed

Probable cause: the barricade was removed based on the fastening schedule alone without confirming the manufacturer's adhesive cure period.
Confirmation: compare the installation date and time against the manufacturer's stated cure period for the adhesive used.
Correction: reinstall the barricade immediately and hold the stair out of service until the full cure period has elapsed.
Reinspection: repeat the movement and load check at every tread before removing the barricade again.

Cleanup

This project does not end until the required cure period has elapsed and the final safety inspection is complete.

  • Remove adhesive residue, dust, and cut offcuts from every tread, riser, and landing before the cure period begins
  • Return saws, jigsaws, coping saws, and adhesive tools to storage in working condition
  • Keep the barricade and alternate access in place and clearly signed through the full cure period
  • Compile the stair-trim worksheet, structural inspection reference, final stair measurements, and safety inspection into the stair-trim project record
  • Store photographs of structural conditions, defects, and completed work with the project record
  • Remove the barricade only after the cure period has elapsed and the final safety inspection has been completed and signed

Performance Check

With the stair-trim installation complete, review every step against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm every rise, run, and tread depth was measured and recorded against the structural reference, nosing projection is uniform across the flight within the stated tolerance, no fastener is proud or exposed in the walking surface, no tread or riser shows movement or squeak under load, the top landing and bottom floor transitions are free of trip conditions, and the stair has been held out of service through the full cure period. If any item cannot be closed, keep the barricade in place, record the item as an open condition, and identify the trade or professional responsible for correction before the stair is returned to service.
