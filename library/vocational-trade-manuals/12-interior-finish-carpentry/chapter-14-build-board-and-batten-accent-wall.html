Job Overview

This project qualifies you to lay out and install a complete board-and-batten trim system on one approved wall. The finished work product is a level, plumb, and symmetrical accent wall with horizontal rails, evenly spaced vertical battens, and reveals that are coordinated around every outlet, switch, window, door, cabinet, or other interruption on the wall.

You will measure the wall, record every interruption, calculate the approved number and spacing of battens, mark level reference lines for the bottom, intermediate, and top rails, install the rails, measure and cut each vertical batten individually, dry-fit and verify spacing before permanent fastening, install the battens plumb, and fasten the completed system without splitting edges or missing backing.

This project depends on the wall inspection and controlling-line work established in Project 01 and on the cutting and test-fitting practice built in Project 03. It uses the layout discipline from Chapter 13's chair-rail and picture-rail work, and it prepares the wall for the fill, caulk, and paint-readiness work in Project 18.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FC-079 — Lay out batten spacing, including centerline verification and equal field-width calculation.
  • FC-080 — Install horizontal rails level and aligned to the approved design dimensions.
  • FC-081 — Install vertical battens plumb and to the verified spacing.
  • FC-082 — Maintain consistent reveals around outlets, switches, and other wall openings.
  • FC-083 — Fasten trim securely into framing or approved backing without splitting edges.
  • FC-084 — Inspect the completed accent wall for symmetry, level, plumb, and surface plane.

Where This Skill Fits

This project applies the reveal, height, and layout-line discipline established in Project 01 to a wall-covering trim system rather than a single opening or a continuous rail. It follows the chair-rail and picture-rail layout method taught in Chapter 13 and extends it to a two-axis grid of horizontal rails and vertical battens that must remain visually balanced across the entire wall.

The finished wall carries forward into Project 18, where fasteners are set, nail holes are filled, and paint-grade joints are caulked. If the capstone room in Chapter 20 includes a board-and-batten feature, it is built using the exact layout and fastening method demonstrated here.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the approved wall elevation, batten profile, rail profile, panel or wall backing, finish type, outlet locations, and design dimensions
  • inspecting the wall for plane variation, damage, moisture, and unsuitable coatings
  • measuring total wall width and height and recording every door, window, switch, receptacle, vent, and other interruption
  • determining the approved number and spacing of battens and calculating equal field widths and edge conditions
  • marking centerlines, verifying visual symmetry, and establishing level lines for bottom, intermediate, and top rails
  • locating framing or identifying the approved adhesive-and-fastener method
  • cutting, dry-fitting, and installing horizontal rails level and aligned
  • measuring, cutting, dry-fitting, and installing vertical battens plumb and to the verified spacing
  • maintaining consistent reveals around outlets and openings, including approved box extensions or device coordination through the electrical trade
  • fastening trim without splitting edges or missing backing, and inspecting symmetry, spacing, level, plumb, intersections, and surface plane

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • relocate, extend, or modify any electrical box, switch, receptacle, or device; box extensions or device coordination beyond simple trim clearance must be performed by the electrical trade
  • fasten into a wall without first confirming framing, blocking, or an approved anchor method for the specified shelf, rail, or batten load
  • proceed with layout on a wall with unresolved plane variation, damage, moisture, or unsuitable coatings; these conditions are documented and referred for correction before layout begins
  • fill, caulk, prime, or paint the completed accent wall; that work is performed in Project 18 after fasteners are inspected and set

Any wall condition, framing deficiency, or electrical conflict discovered during layout must be corrected, reinspected, and documented before rails or battens are permanently fastened.

Trade Note

A board-and-batten wall reads as symmetrical or uneven from across the room long before anyone notices a level bubble. Establishing the centerline and equal field widths before any rail or batten is cut, rather than adjusting spacing piece by piece as the run proceeds, is the single largest factor in avoiding a visibly uneven wall.

Setup Standard

Do not cut a single rail or batten until the wall interruption survey is complete, the batten count and spacing are calculated and verified for visual symmetry, and level and centerline references are marked on the wall. Layout errors made before cutting compound across every batten on the wall and cannot be corrected with filler.

Shop Tip

Dry-fit every batten across the full wall before fastening any of them. Standing back from the wall to view the dry-fit layout under normal room lighting reveals spacing drift and asymmetry that is difficult to see while working close to the wall.

Reference Conventions

Establish these references before any layout mark is made, and do not change them mid-wall.

TermDefinition
Field widthThe distance between the faces of two adjacent battens, or between a batten and the wall edge or corner, measured along the wall
Bottom railThe lowest horizontal rail in the design, typically aligned above the baseboard or at the specified height from the finished-floor reference
Top railThe uppermost horizontal rail in the design, set at the height specified in the wall elevation
Intermediate railAny horizontal rail between the bottom and top rail, where the design includes one
BattenA vertical trim member installed between rails or terminations at the spacing calculated during layout
Wall centerlineThe vertical line marking the mathematical center of the wall, used to verify that the batten layout is visually balanced left to right
Edge conditionThe field width and batten placement at the wall's left and right ends, including any adjustment made to keep the layout symmetrical

Common Field Fundamentals

Every board-and-batten wall depends on three conditions established before any rail or batten is cut: the wall width, height, and every interruption are measured and recorded; the number and spacing of battens are calculated from the wall centerline so the layout is visually balanced rather than simply divided by a fixed spacing dimension; and level, plumb, and framing references are marked on the wall before layout proceeds. Confirming these three conditions before cutting is the largest factor in avoiding visible spacing drift across the finished wall.

SurveyWall width, height, and every door, window, switch, receptacle, and vent recorded
LayoutCenterline, batten count, field widths, and edge conditions calculated for symmetry
RailsBottom, intermediate, and top rails installed level and aligned to the design
BattensVertical battens cut individually, dry-fit, and installed plumb at verified spacing

Calculating Batten Spacing for Visual Symmetry

Determine the approved number of battens from the wall elevation, then divide the field between wall edges, corners, or terminations into equal widths. Mark the wall centerline first and verify that the layout is symmetrical about it before marking individual batten locations. A design with an even number of equal fields places a field, not a batten, on the wall centerline; a design with an odd number of equal fields places a batten on the centerline. Confirm which condition the approved design specifies before marking layout lines.

Measurement Cue — Record Interruptions Before Calculating Spacing

Measure and record the exact location of every switch, receptacle, window, door, cabinet edge, and other wall interruption before calculating batten spacing. A batten centerline calculated without this record can land directly on a device or window casing, forcing a rework of the entire layout after cutting has begun.

Critical Safety Cue — Confirm Framing Before Fastening

Locate studs, blocking, or the approved backing method before fastening any rail or batten. Do not fasten blindly near electrical boxes, switches, receptacles, or other concealed services. Stop work and refer the condition to the electrical trade if a planned fastener location conflicts with a device or concealed wiring path.

Field Safety Cue — Long Stock and Saw Support

Support rail and batten stock fully on both sides of the miter saw before cutting. Keep the marked blade exclusion zone clear and allow the blade to stop completely before lifting or moving any cut piece.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — MDF and Composite Batten Material

Where MDF or composite batten material is specified, follow the manufacturer's instructions for blade selection, dust control, fastener type, and edge sealing before installation.

E. Document Review and Wall Preparation

  1. Review the wall elevation, batten profile, rail profile, panel or wall backing, finish type, outlet locations, and design dimensions. Confirm the documents correspond to the wall being trimmed and match the Project 01 controlling-line layout for that wall.
  2. Inspect the wall for plane variation, damage, moisture, and unsuitable coatings. Stop work and refer any active hazard or unresolved defect for correction before proceeding.

F. Wall Survey and Interruption Record

  1. Measure total wall width and height. Record both dimensions on the Wall Layout Record.
  2. Record doors, windows, switches, receptacles, vents, and other interruptions. Measure and record the exact horizontal and vertical location of each on the Interruption Survey.
  3. Determine the approved number and spacing of battens. Use the batten count specified in the wall elevation, or the approved calculation method if the count is design-derived.
  4. Calculate equal field widths and edge conditions. Divide the field between wall edges, corners, or terminations into equal widths per the approved design condition.
  5. Mark centerlines and verify visual symmetry. Mark the wall centerline first, then verify the calculated batten layout is balanced about it before marking individual locations.

G. Rail Layout and Installation

  1. Establish level lines for bottom, intermediate, and top rails. Use the finished-floor reference from Project 01 and the heights specified in the wall elevation.
  2. Locate framing or identify the approved adhesive-and-fastener method. Mark stud, blocking, or approved anchor locations on the Framing and Backing Map.
  3. Cut and dry-fit horizontal rails. Cut each rail to the length recorded on the Rail and Batten Cut List and dry-fit it to the marked level line.
  4. Install rails level and aligned. Fasten each rail into framing or approved backing per the fastener schedule, checking level across the full length before setting all fasteners.

H. Batten Layout and Installation

  1. Measure each vertical batten individually between rails or terminations. Do not assume a single uniform batten length; verify each location independently.
  2. Cut battens square and label locations. Label each cut batten with its wall position matching the Rail and Batten Cut List.
  3. Dry-fit battens and verify spacing. Position all battens at their calculated locations without fastening and confirm field widths and symmetry across the full wall.
  4. Install battens plumb. Fasten each batten into the rails and approved backing per the fastener schedule, checking plumb before setting all fasteners.
  5. Maintain consistent reveals around outlets and openings. Adjust batten position within the approved tolerance where required to preserve a consistent reveal at each device or opening.
  6. Add approved box extensions or device coordination through the electrical trade where required. Do not extend or modify a box or device directly; refer this work to the electrical trade.

I. Fastening and Final Inspection

  1. Fasten trim without splitting edges or missing backing. Confirm every fastener lands in framing or approved backing per the Framing and Backing Map.
  2. Inspect symmetry, spacing, level, plumb, intersections, and surface plane. Record results on the Installation Inspection Record against the Acceptance Standards table.
  3. Correct visible spacing drift and joint misalignment. Reset and refasten any rail or batten that fails the recorded standard.
  4. Prepare the wall for Chapter 18. Confirm all fasteners are set and the wall is ready for fill, caulk, and paint-readiness preparation.

Common Cutting Inspection

Apply the following checks to every rail and batten cut for this project, in addition to the general cutting standards from Project 03.

  • Length check: confirm each batten was measured individually at its installed location rather than assumed uniform.
  • Square-cut check: verify both ends of each rail and batten are cut square unless the design specifies an angled termination.
  • Label check: confirm every cut piece is labeled with its wall position before leaving the cutting station.
  • Profile orientation check: confirm the reference face and orientation match the wall elevation before installation.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Batten Spacing Appears to Drift Across the Wall

Probable cause: field widths were measured and cut sequentially from one wall edge instead of calculated from the verified centerline before cutting began.
Confirmation: measure the installed field widths against the recorded Batten Spacing Calculation.
Correction: remove and reposition the affected battens using the recorded calculation, working outward from the centerline.
Reinspection: repeat the spacing check across the full wall after correction.

A Batten Lands Directly Over a Switch or Receptacle

Probable cause: the Interruption Survey was incomplete or was not consulted before the Batten Spacing Calculation was finalized.
Confirmation: compare the batten layout against the recorded device locations on the Interruption Survey.
Correction: adjust the field widths within the approved tolerance, or revise the batten count with the wall elevation's approval process, before cutting proceeds.
Reinspection: repeat the dry-fit and symmetry check with the revised layout.

A Rail Reads Level at the Ends but Sags in the Middle

Probable cause: the rail was fastened only at the ends before the level was checked across the full length, or the wall plane varies at an unrecorded midpoint.
Confirmation: hold the straightedge and level across the full rail length at multiple points.
Correction: remove the rail, correct or shim the wall condition per the deficiency process, and refasten while checking level continuously along the length before setting all fasteners.
Reinspection: repeat the level check across the full rail after correction.

A Batten Splits Near a Fastener

Probable cause: the fastener was driven too close to the batten edge, or without a pilot hole where the specified fastener and material require one.
Confirmation: inspect the split and compare the fastener location and type against the approved fastener schedule.
Correction: remove the damaged batten section, replace it with a new piece cut to the same recorded length, and refasten using the correct edge distance and pilot-hole method.
Reinspection: repeat the fastening inspection on the replaced piece.

The Completed Wall Looks Symmetrical Up Close but Uneven from Across the Room

Probable cause: the centerline symmetry check was performed only at close range during dry-fit, without stepping back to view the full wall under normal lighting.
Confirmation: view the completed wall from the far side of the room and compare the visual field widths to the recorded calculation.
Correction: document the specific location of the visible imbalance and correct the affected battens per the spacing-drift procedure above.
Reinspection: repeat the full-wall visual check from across the room after correction.

Field Terms

Field Width
The distance between the faces of two adjacent battens, or between a batten and the wall edge or corner.
Bottom Rail
The lowest horizontal rail in the design, typically set above the baseboard.
Top Rail
The uppermost horizontal rail in the design, set at the specified height.
Intermediate Rail
Any horizontal rail between the bottom and top rail, where included.
Batten
A vertical trim member installed between rails or terminations at calculated spacing.
Wall Centerline
The vertical line marking the mathematical center of the wall, used to verify layout symmetry.
Edge Condition
The field width and batten placement at the wall's left and right ends.
Interruption Survey
The recorded location of every door, window, switch, receptacle, and vent on the wall.
Batten Spacing Calculation
The recorded batten count, field width, and centerline verification used to control cutting.
Reveal
The consistent gap maintained between trim and an adjacent device, opening, or edge.
