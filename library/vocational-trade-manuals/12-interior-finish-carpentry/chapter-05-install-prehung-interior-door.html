Job Overview

This project qualifies you to prepare a rough opening, set and adjust a compatible prehung interior door unit, and fasten the frame so the door swings smoothly with consistent reveals and correct floor clearance. The finished work product is one plumb, square, securely fastened interior door frame with a hung door that operates through its full swing without binding, rubbing, or drifting open or closed on its own.

You will inspect the rough opening and door unit, place the unit in the opening, shim and fasten the hinge jamb first, check hinge-jamb straightness, close the door to check head and strike-side reveals, shim and fasten the strike jamb, operate the door repeatedly, and document final clearances, reveal, and fastening.

This project depends on the readiness decision from Project 01 and the opening inspection completed there. It sets up Project 06, which installs the latch set and adjusts final door operation, and Project 04, which installs casing over the reveal established here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FC-025 — Prepare the rough opening for a prehung interior door, verifying size, plumb, level, square, backing, and floor elevation.
  • FC-026 — Set the prehung door unit in the rough opening and center it before shimming.
  • FC-027 — Shim the hinge side of the frame and fasten it in a straight, plumb line.
  • FC-028 — Plumb the jambs in both directions and correct any twist before final fastening.
  • FC-029 — Fasten the door frame using the approved fastener pattern without distorting the jamb.
  • FC-030 — Inspect final reveal, swing, floor clearance, and jamb security, and document the result.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 01 verified the rough opening and finished-floor reference this project depends on. Project 04 installs casing over the reveal this project establishes, so a plumb, correctly reveal-adjusted door frame here prevents a casing rework in Project 04. Project 06 completes the latch set and hardware adjustment on the door this project hangs. A door installed out of plumb, with an inconsistent reveal, or with the wrong floor clearance carries that defect into casing, hardware, and final inspection.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • verifying door size, handing, swing, jamb width, wall thickness, floor condition, and hardware preparation against the approved door schedule
  • inspecting the rough opening for size, plumb, level, square, backing, and floor elevation
  • confirming the opening is free of wiring, plumbing, and protruding fasteners before the unit is placed
  • removing packaging while retaining shipping braces until the manufacturer permits their removal
  • placing, centering, shimming, plumbing, and fastening the hinge jamb, then the strike jamb and head jamb
  • checking and adjusting head and strike-side reveals for consistency
  • trimming shims, operating the door repeatedly, and inspecting swing, latch alignment, floor clearance, and jamb security
  • documenting final clearances, reveal, and fastening

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • enlarge, reframe, or structurally modify a rough opening that does not meet the approved size, plumb, level, or square tolerance; such an opening is referred back to the framing trade before this project continues
  • install a fire-rated door, self-closing device, labeled frame, or smoke-seal assembly using this general procedure; those units require the approved listed installation details
  • install long hinge or strike screws into framing beyond what the door schedule specifies, or in a way that distorts the jamb
  • proceed with installation where the opening or wall condition prevents safe adjustment; such a condition is documented and referred to a qualified professional

Any rough opening that fails inspection in Section F must be corrected, reinspected, and documented before the door unit is placed.

Trade Note

The hinge jamb controls the entire installation. Every reveal, swing, and clearance decision in this project is made relative to a hinge jamb that is already plumb and fastened in a straight line. Adjusting the strike jamb cannot correct a hinge jamb that is out of plumb; it can only mask the defect until the door is operated.

Setup Standard

Do not place the door unit in the opening until the rough opening has passed the Section F inspection. A unit set into an undersized, out-of-plumb, or out-of-square opening will require correction after the jamb is fastened, which is far more difficult than correcting the framing before the unit is placed.

Shop Tip

Shim directly behind each hinge location first, not at random points along the jamb. The hinge shims carry the weight of the door and control hinge-jamb straightness; shims placed only between hinges will not prevent the jamb from bowing under the door's weight.

Common Field Fundamentals

A prehung interior door installation succeeds or fails at the hinge jamb. Every later adjustment, including the reveal check, strike-side shimming, and final operation test, depends on a hinge jamb that is plumb in both directions and fastened in a straight line without bowing. Reveal is a symptom of hinge-jamb accuracy, not a separate correction to be chased independently at the head and strike side.

Shimming at Fastening Points

Install shims in pairs from opposite directions to form a flat, parallel surface behind the jamb at each fastening point. Drive the fastener through the jamb and the shimmed area into the framing, then trim the protruding shim flush with the wall surface before installing the next piece.

Measurement Cue — Reveal and Floor Clearance

Check reveal at the head and at both side jambs with the door closed. Confirm floor clearance against the finished-floor reference recorded in Project 01, not against the current subfloor or temporary floor covering, unless the door schedule specifically directs clearance from the subfloor.

Critical Safety Cue — Door Unit Handling

A prehung door unit is heavy and awkward to carry alone. Use a second person or an approved door cart when moving the unit to the opening. Do not lean an unsupported unit against a wall or opening where it can fall.

Field Safety Cue — Fastener and Tool Control

Keep hands clear of the jamb edge when driving fasteners near the hinge or strike locations. Support the unit with temporary wedges or a helper before releasing hand pressure to drive fasteners, so the unit cannot shift out of position.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Shipping Braces and Fastener Locations

Follow the door manufacturer's instructions for when shipping braces may be removed and which fastener locations, types, and lengths are approved for the jamb material supplied. Do not remove a shipping brace before the manufacturer permits it, and do not substitute a fastener length or type not identified by the door schedule or manufacturer instructions.

F. Rough-Opening and Unit Verification

  1. Verify door size, handing, swing, jamb width, wall thickness, and floor condition against the approved door schedule. Confirm hardware preparation matches the specified latch set before proceeding.
  2. Inspect the rough opening for size, plumb, level, square, backing, and floor elevation. Measure opening width and height at multiple points and check diagonals for square.
  3. Confirm the opening is free of wiring, plumbing, and protruding fasteners. Correct any conflict before placing the unit.
  4. Remove packaging while keeping shipping braces until the manufacturer permits removal. Retain braces that hold the door in the closed position during handling.
  5. Inspect the unit for twist, damage, hinge security, and matching components. Sight down each jamb leg for twist and confirm hinge screws are tight.
  6. Establish finished-floor clearance using the approved reference. Record the target clearance from the Project 01 finished-floor reference on the Door Installation Record.

G. Placing and Shimming the Hinge Jamb

  1. Place the unit in the opening and center it. Confirm equal reveal margins at both side jambs before shimming begins.
  2. Shim behind the top hinge location. Install shims from both directions to form a flat surface directly behind the top hinge.
  3. Plumb the hinge jamb in both directions. Check plumb with the level held against the jamb face and against the jamb edge.
  4. Install the first approved fastener through the jamb or hinge location. Drive the fastener specified by the door schedule at the top hinge shim point only.
  5. Shim and fasten behind each remaining hinge. Repeat the shim-and-check sequence at every hinge location down the jamb.
  6. Check hinge-jamb straightness with a long level or straightedge. Confirm the jamb is straight over its full height with no bow between hinge points.

H. Reveal Check and Strike-Jamb Fastening

  1. Close the door and inspect the head and strike-side reveals. Measure the gap at the head and at multiple points along the strike side.
  2. Shim the strike jamb at the latch and other specified points. Install shims in pairs to bring the strike jamb into a position that produces a consistent reveal.
  3. Adjust the frame until reveals are consistent and the door remains stationary at partial openings where appropriate. Recheck the door at several partial-open positions.
  4. Fasten the strike jamb and head jamb according to the approved method. Drive fasteners at each specified shim point once the reveal is confirmed correct.
  5. Install long hinge or strike screws only where specified and without distorting the jamb. Confirm the jamb remains straight after installing any longer screws into framing.

I. Finish Adjustments and Verification

  1. Trim shims without damaging the wall. Score each shim with the utility knife at the wall surface and snap or cut it flush.
  2. Operate the door repeatedly. Swing the door through its full range at least five times, checking for binding, rubbing, or unintended movement.
  3. Check swing, latch alignment, floor clearance, jamb twist, and frame security. Confirm floor clearance against the finished-floor reference and confirm the frame does not move when pressure is applied at each jamb.
  4. Stop work if the opening or wall condition prevents safe adjustment. Document the condition and refer it to a qualified professional before continuing.
  5. Document final clearances, reveal, and fastening. Record measured reveal, floor clearance, and confirmation of every specified fastener location on the Door Installation Record.

Common Cutting Inspection

This project does not cut trim material, but shim material must be cut cleanly at every fastening point. Apply the following checks to every shim cut and every fastener location.

  • Shim-pair check: confirm shims were installed in opposing pairs at every fastening point rather than as a single wedge.
  • Flush-trim check: confirm every shim is trimmed flush with the wall surface without damaging the drywall or jamb.
  • Fastener-location check: confirm every fastener matches the location, type, and length specified by the door schedule.
  • Plumb-sequence check: confirm the hinge jamb was plumbed and fastened before the strike jamb was shimmed.
  • Reveal check: confirm the reveal was measured at the head and both side jambs with the door closed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Door Swings Open or Closed on Its Own

Probable cause: the hinge jamb is out of plumb, causing the door to move under its own weight.
Confirmation: check the hinge jamb plumb in both directions with the long level.
Correction: loosen the hinge-jamb fasteners nearest the error, reshim to correct plumb, and refasten.
Reinspection: repeat the plumb check and operate the door through its full swing.

Door Binds Near the Latch Side When Closing

Probable cause: the strike jamb was shimmed to a position that narrows the reveal at the latch side, or the hinge jamb has a slight twist.
Confirmation: measure reveal along the full strike side with the door closed and sight down the hinge jamb for twist.
Correction: reshim the strike jamb for a consistent reveal, or correct hinge-jamb twist before readjusting the strike side.
Reinspection: recheck reveal and operate the door through its full swing.

Reveal Is Inconsistent Along the Head Jamb

Probable cause: the head jamb was fastened before confirming both side jambs were plumb and at the correct reveal.
Confirmation: measure the head reveal at both corners and the center with the door closed.
Correction: loosen the head-jamb fasteners nearest the inconsistent area, reshim, and refasten.
Reinspection: recheck the head reveal across its full width.

Frame Moves When Pressure Is Applied at the Jamb

Probable cause: a specified fastening point was skipped, or a fastener missed the framing behind the shim.
Confirmation: compare installed fastener locations against the Fastener and Hardware Schedule and probe suspect locations.
Correction: install the missing fastener at the correct location and depth into framing.
Reinspection: repeat the frame-security check at every jamb.

Floor Clearance Does Not Match the Recorded Reference

Probable cause: clearance was measured from the current subfloor or temporary covering instead of the finished-floor reference recorded in Project 01.
Confirmation: compare the measured clearance against the Project 01 finished-floor reference value.
Correction: document the discrepancy; correct only within the adjustment method permitted by the door schedule, and refer to a qualified professional if correction requires reframing.
Reinspection: remeasure clearance against the correct reference after correction.

Cleanup

This project ends with a securely installed, fully operable door and a complete installation record.

  • Remove all shim offcuts, packaging, and shipping braces once the manufacturer permits their removal
  • Return levels, squares, pry bars, and fastening tools to storage in working condition
  • Sweep and remove sawdust and shim debris from the opening and adjacent floor area
  • Compile the Door Installation Record with final clearance, reveal, and fastening documentation into the room file established in Project 01
  • Confirm the door is left in a closed and latched or blocked position appropriate for ongoing work in the room

The uniform gap between the door face and the jamb, checked with the door closed.

The gap between the bottom of the door and the finished-floor reference.

The framed opening sized to receive the prehung door unit.
