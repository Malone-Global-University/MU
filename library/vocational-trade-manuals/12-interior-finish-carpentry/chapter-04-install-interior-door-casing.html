Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install a complete three-piece interior door-casing set: head casing and two side casings, mitered at the corners, fastened to jamb and wall framing, and held to a consistent reveal along both jamb legs and the head. The finished work product is one fully installed, permanently fastened door-casing assembly with tight, aligned miter joints and unimpaired door operation.

You will verify that the jamb is secure and operating correctly, establish the casing reveal from the Project 01 survey, measure and cut head and side casing to the required miters, dry-fit and adjust the assembly, fasten it to jamb and wall framing using the approved fastener schedule, and inspect the finished casing for reveal, plane, joint alignment, and door operation.

This project depends directly on Project 01's controlling reveal line and Project 05's prehung door jamb, and on the measuring, marking, and mitering skills developed in Project 03. The casing installed here is left with fasteners set but not yet filled or caulked; Project 18 completes that surface preparation after every trim assembly in the room is installed.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FC-019 — Establish the casing reveal on both jamb legs and the head from the approved controlling-line layout.
  • FC-020 — Cut head casing to the required length and miter angles while preserving profile orientation.
  • FC-021 — Cut side casing to the required length and miter angles, independently measured for each leg.
  • FC-022 — Fasten casing to jamb and wall framing using the approved fastener pattern without damaging the profile.
  • FC-023 — Align and pin miter joints at the correct reveal without distorting the assembly.
  • FC-024 — Inspect door-trim fit for reveal, joint alignment, casing plane, and unimpaired door operation.

Where This Skill Fits

This project applies the Project 01 controlling reveal line to the first permanently installed trim assembly in the room and applies the measuring, marking, and mitering procedure from Project 03 to a finished, visible joint. It requires a jamb installed and adjusted under Project 05 and hardware operating correctly under Project 06 before the reveal is finalized against the door.

The casing method used here — establishing reveal, cutting to long point or short point as required by the miter, dry-fitting before permanent fastening, and pinning the miter without distorting the reveal — is reused directly in Project 07 for window casing and referenced again in Project 13 for rail returns and Project 14 for batten terminations.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • verifying that the door jamb is secure, plumb, adjusted, and operating correctly before casing is planned
  • establishing and marking the approved casing reveal on both jamb legs and the head
  • measuring, cutting, and test-fitting head and side casing pieces
  • adjusting miters without changing the established reveal
  • fastening casing to jamb and wall framing using the approved fastener pattern
  • pinning or fastening miter joints using the approved method
  • inspecting casing plane, reveal, joint alignment, and door operation
  • correcting proud miters, open joints, bowed casing, and inconsistent reveals
  • documenting jamb or wall irregularities that affected the installation

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • install or adjust the prehung door unit itself; the jamb must already be set, plumb, and operating correctly under Project 05 before this project begins
  • fill, caulk, sand, or prime the finished casing; this occurs in Project 18 after every trim assembly in the room is complete
  • alter fire-rated door assemblies, labeled frames, self-closing devices, or smoke seals beyond the approved listed casing detail
  • conceal jamb or wall irregularities with filler, caulk, or excessive compound instead of documenting and correcting them

Any door opening with an unresolved jamb, hardware, or wall-plane defect must be corrected before casing begins on that opening.

Trade Note

The reveal established in this project is not a decorative choice made at the saw. It is transferred directly from the Project 01 controlling-line layout and marked on the actual jamb before any casing is cut. Casing measured from an assumed reveal rather than the marked line is the most common cause of the mismatched reveals addressed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not cut casing for an opening until the jamb has passed the Project 05 and Project 06 inspections and the Project 01 controlling reveal line for that opening is confirmed still valid against the current jamb condition. A jamb adjusted after the room survey must be re-verified against the finished-floor reference before casing is measured.

Shop Tip

Dry-fit all three casing pieces together, off the wall, before any piece is permanently fastened. Checking miter closure and reveal position on the bench first, then again against the jamb, catches a mismeasured piece before it is nailed and before the error is harder to see.

Field Fundamentals

Door casing conceals the gap between the jamb and the rough opening, but it does not correct jamb or wall defects. The reveal marked on the jamb face is the single controlling reference for every measurement in this project: head length, side-casing length, and miter position are all taken from that line, not from the wall opening or the jamb edge itself. Establishing the reveal accurately, on all three jamb faces, before any casing is cut is the largest single factor in avoiding mismatched joints.

Head CasingCut to the marked reveal with correct left and right miters
Side CasingMeasured independently per leg and mitered to match the head
FasteningSet to jamb and framing per the approved fastener schedule

Reveal, Miter, and Reference Face

Mark the reveal with a combination square held against the jamb edge, scribing a light pencil line the reveal distance in from the edge on both jamb legs and the head. Measure and cut with the casing's reference face up and the profile orientation consistent on every piece; a piece cut with the profile reversed will not match its mate at the miter even if the angle and length are correct.

Measurement Cue — Measure Each Side Casing Independently

Do not assume both side-casing pieces are the same length. Measure each leg from its own reveal marks at the head and at the floor or baseboard reference, because floor and jamb variation identified in Project 01 can make the two legs different lengths even on the same opening.

Critical Safety Cue — Miter Saw Operation

Support casing stock fully on both sides of the saw table before cutting. Keep hands outside the marked blade exclusion zone established in Project 02, and allow the blade to come to a complete stop before lifting it or removing the cut piece.

Field Safety Cue — Nailer Use Near the Jamb

Confirm the finish nailer's depth setting on scrap stock of the same casing profile before fastening near the finished jamb. Keep the nailer's safety tip fully depressed against the work surface and keep free hands clear of the fastening path.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Adhesive and Fastener Products

Where the fastener and hardware schedule specifies an adhesive or a proprietary fastener product, follow that product's manufacturer instructions for surface preparation, open time, clamping or fastening while curing, and cure time before the joint is loaded or sanded.

FC-019 – FC-024

Installation Qualification

Interior Door Casing Installation

Difficulty: Beginner to Intermediate

Estimated Time: 2–4 hours per opening

Estimated Cost: Per approved trim schedule

Finished Work: One permanently installed, mitered, fastened door-casing assembly with consistent reveal

A. Jamb and Opening Verification

  1. Verify the door jamb is secure, plumb, adjusted, and operating correctly. Confirm the Project 05 and Project 06 inspection records for this opening show a passed status before proceeding.
  2. Confirm finished floor and jamb projection. Check the jamb reveal at the floor against the finished-floor reference recorded in Project 01.
  3. Inspect surrounding drywall for damage or excessive plane variation. Use a straightedge across the drywall adjacent to the jamb; if variation exceeds what the casing profile can follow, stop and refer the condition per Section E below before continuing.

B. Reveal Layout

  1. Establish and mark the approved casing reveal on both jamb legs and the head. Use the combination square to scribe the reveal line the distance set by the finish specification, transferred from the Project 01 controlling reveal line.

C. Head Casing

  1. Measure the head casing between the required long points. Take the measurement between the outside corners of the reveal marks on the two jamb legs.
  2. Cut the head casing with the correct left and right miters. Cut with the reference face up and the profile orientation consistent with the component schedule.
  3. Dry-fit the head and check reveal and joint position. Hold the head casing to its reveal marks and confirm both miter faces align with where the side casings will meet it.

D. Side Casing

  1. Measure each side casing independently. Measure from the head reveal mark to the floor or baseboard reference on each leg separately.
  2. Cut side-casing miters and square or profiled bottom ends as specified. Cut the top miter to match the head casing angle and the bottom end per the finish specification.
  3. Test-fit all three pieces together. Assemble the head and both side casings against the jamb without fastening and check both miter joints for closure.
  4. Adjust miters without changing the reveal. Use the block plane or sanding block for minor correction; recut any piece with an error the tolerance in Section F cannot absorb.

E. Fastening

  1. Apply adhesive only where specified. Follow the fastener and hardware schedule and any identified manufacturer instructions for the adhesive product.
  2. Position the head casing and secure it with initial fasteners. Hold the head to its reveal marks and drive the first fasteners per the fastener schedule, leaving room to adjust before final fastening.
  3. Install side casings while holding the reveal. Position each side casing to its reveal marks and confirm miter closure against the head before driving initial fasteners.
  4. Pin or fasten miter joints using the approved method. Drive the miter-joint fastener per the fastener schedule while holding the joint faces flush.
  5. Fasten the casing to jamb and wall framing with the specified fastener pattern. Complete the full fastener schedule from Section D at both the jamb leg and the wall-framing edge.
  6. Keep fasteners away from fragile profile edges. Follow the edge-distance guidance in the fastener schedule to avoid splitting the profile.

F. Inspection and Correction

  1. Check casing plane, reveal, joint alignment, and door operation. Apply the Acceptance Standards table in Section F of the Project Specification Sheet to every joint and reveal line.
  2. Correct proud miters, open joints, bowed casing, and inconsistent reveals. Recut or replace pieces that fail the acceptance standard; do not conceal the defect with filler.
  3. Set fasteners and prepare the casing for Chapter 18. Set all fastener heads below the surface without crushing the profile; do not fill or caulk at this stage.
  4. Document any jamb or wall irregularities that affected the installation. Record the condition, its location, and how it was accommodated or referred for correction.

Common Cutting Inspection

Apply the following checks to every casing piece before it is permanently fastened.

  • Reference check: confirm every measurement was taken from the marked reveal line, not from the jamb edge or the rough opening.
  • Orientation check: confirm the reference face and profile orientation are consistent across all three pieces before cutting.
  • Miter accuracy check: confirm each miter angle against the combination square before the piece leaves the saw station.
  • Independent-length check: confirm each side casing was measured on its own leg and not assumed equal to the other side.
  • Dry-fit check: confirm all three pieces were test-fitted together before any piece was permanently fastened.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Miter Joint Has a Visible Gap at the Face

Probable cause: the head or side-casing miter angle was cut inaccurately, or the reveal marks on the two legs are not at the same distance from the jamb edge.
Confirmation: check both miter angles with the combination square and re-measure the reveal marks on both legs against the Project 01 controlling line.
Correction: recut the piece with the inaccurate angle, or remark the reveal from the controlling line and recut both affected pieces.
Reinspection: repeat the dry-fit and check joint closure under normal room light.

Reveal Is Inconsistent Along One Jamb Leg

Probable cause: the side casing was fastened without holding it to the reveal marks along its full length, allowing it to drift from the line.
Confirmation: measure the reveal at the top, middle, and bottom of the affected leg and compare to the marked line.
Correction: remove the fasteners nearest the deviation, reposition the casing to the reveal marks, and refasten.
Reinspection: repeat the reveal measurement at the same three points after refastening.

Casing Rocks or Will Not Sit Flat Against the Wall

Probable cause: a wall-plane irregularity identified in Project 01 was not accommodated, or the casing itself is bowed.
Confirmation: hold the straightedge across the casing at the high point and measure the gap; compare to the Project 01 surface inspection record for that wall.
Correction: for a documented wall irregularity within tolerance, scribe or shim per the finish specification; for a bowed piece, replace it.
Reinspection: repeat the straightedge check after correction and confirm the reveal is still consistent.

Door Contacts the Casing During Its Swing

Probable cause: the reveal was set too close to the jamb edge on the hinge or strike side, or the casing projects beyond the jamb face into the door's travel path.
Confirmation: operate the door through its full swing and mark the point of contact.
Correction: remove and reposition the affected casing to the correct reveal, or refer the condition to Project 06 if the contact indicates a hinge or strike adjustment problem rather than a casing position problem.
Reinspection: operate the door through its full swing again and confirm no contact at any point.

Fastener Head Crushes or Splits the Profile

Probable cause: the nail set was misaligned, the fastener was driven too close to a profile edge, or the fastener schedule's edge-distance guidance was not followed.
Confirmation: inspect the fastener location against the schedule's edge-distance requirement.
Correction: remove the damaged fastener, fill the resulting void per Project 18 procedures, and drive a replacement fastener at the correct location.
Reinspection: inspect the corrected fastener location and surrounding profile under normal light.

Cleanup

  • Remove offcuts and waste casing stock and sort reusable lengths per Project 02 staging procedures
  • Return the finish nailer, nail set, block plane, and squares to their storage locations in working condition
  • Vacuum or sweep saw and assembly areas of dust and small fasteners
  • Store any documented jamb or wall irregularity notes with the room file established in Project 01
  • Confirm the door operates freely through its full swing before leaving the opening in service

Field Terms

Reveal
The set-back distance between the casing's inside edge and the jamb edge, marked before any casing is cut.
Head Casing
The horizontal casing piece installed across the top of a door or window opening.
Side Casing
The vertical casing piece installed along a jamb leg.
Long Point
The longer corner of a mitered end, used as the standard measuring reference for miter cuts.
Short Point
The shorter corner of a mitered end.
Reference Face
The finished front face of a trim profile, kept consistent through marking, cutting, and installation.
Dry-Fit
Temporarily assembling trim pieces without permanent fasteners to check fit before installation.
Pinning
Driving a small-diameter fastener through a joint face to hold it flush and closed.
Edge Distance
The minimum distance from a profile edge at which a fastener may be driven without splitting the material.
Proud Fastener
A fastener head that sits above the trim surface instead of flush or slightly below it.

Performance Check

With the casing installed, review the assembly against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm the reveal is consistent along both jamb legs and the head, both miter joints are closed with no visible gap under normal room light, no proud corner or twisted piece is detectable with a straightedge, the fastener pattern matches the approved schedule with all heads set without crushing the profile, and the door swings, latches, and closes without contacting the casing at any point in its travel. Complete the Readiness Worksheet and document any jamb or wall irregularities before beginning Project 05 on the next opening or proceeding to Project 07.
