Job Overview

This capstone project qualifies you to plan, cut, install, adjust, prepare, inspect, and close out the complete finish-carpentry package for a small practice room. The finished work product is a fully trimmed room: an operating prehung interior door with casing, a complete window stool-apron-casing assembly, straight and cornered baseboard, shoe molding or quarter round, crown molding with fitted corners, a chair rail, picture rail, or board-and-batten feature, closet trim and shelving cleats or an approved alternate feature, stair skirt or tread-and-riser trim where the capstone room includes an inspected stair assembly, complete fastener setting, filling, sanding, and designated caulking, and a full closeout record documenting the room as ready for paint or stain.

You will begin with the Project 01 readiness inspection, correct every listed deficiency, prepare the Project 02 workstation, and then carry out the complete installation sequence in the order required by the System-Sequence Control: door before casing, casing before baseboard, baseboard before shoe, crown layout before crown corners, wall-trim features coordinated with fixed obstacles, closet and stair trim fitted without altering governed geometry, and all filling, caulking, and sanding performed only after every piece is permanently fastened and adjusted.

This is the final project in Book 12. It draws directly on every competency demonstrated in Projects 1 through 19 and produces the single room handed off to Book 13 for cabinet and built-in installation.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FC-115 — Integrate door casing, window casing, baseboard, and floor trim into one coordinated room installation sequence.
  • FC-116 — Integrate crown molding and wall-trim features, including corner fitting, with the room's controlling reference lines.
  • FC-117 — Integrate closet trim, shelving cleats, and stair trim where included, without altering governed clearances or geometry.
  • FC-118 — Perform final filling, caulking, and surface preparation across the complete trim package according to paint-grade and stain-grade requirements.
  • FC-119 — Inspect the complete room under normal and raking light and correct every item on a final deficiency list.
  • FC-120 — Compile complete finish-carpentry closeout records and document the formal handoff to Book 13.

Where This Skill Fits

Every project in this book contributes one component or one technique to this capstone. Project 01 supplies the readiness inspection and controlling reference lines. Project 02 supplies the workstation. Projects 3 through 17 supply every cutting, fitting, and installation technique used here. Projects 18 and 19 supply the fill, caulk, and repair techniques used to bring the finished package to paint- or stain-ready condition.

The completed, closed-out room produced here is the controlling handoff record for Book 13, which begins cabinet and built-in installation against the finished surfaces, trim lines, and clearances documented in this project.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • repeating the Project 01 readiness inspection on the capstone room and correcting every listed deficiency before layout
  • preparing or verifying the cutting, assembly, dust-control, and staging station from Project 02
  • inventorying and acclimating trim stock, and separating paint-grade from stain-grade material
  • establishing casing reveals, baseboard height, crown drop and projection, rail heights, batten spacing, and other controlling lines for the room
  • installing or verifying the prehung interior door and adjusting swing, reveal, hinges, latch, and strike
  • installing interior door casing, window stool, apron, and casing
  • installing straight baseboard runs and completing coped inside and mitered outside corners
  • installing shoe molding or quarter round after the flooring relationship is confirmed
  • laying out and installing straight crown-molding runs and fitting crown inside and outside corners
  • installing chair rail, picture rail, or the approved board-and-batten wall-trim feature
  • installing closet trim and shelving cleats, or the approved alternate trim feature
  • completing stair skirt or tread-and-riser trim only when the capstone room includes an inspected stair assembly
  • verifying all clearances, correcting defects, setting fasteners, filling, sanding, and caulking designated joints
  • final inspection, deficiency correction, closeout documentation, and formal handoff to Book 13

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • alter stair rise, run, tread depth, nosing, guard, or handrail geometry; stair trim in this project is limited to skirt, tread, riser, and nosing finish work on an already-accepted stair structure
  • alter fire-rated doors, self-closing devices, labeled frames, or smoke seals without the approved listed details
  • caulk stain-grade joints or moving joints, drainage gaps, or door clearances unless the finish specification permits it
  • issue a final handoff on a room with an open safety, code, or structural deficiency; such a room is held at Conditional or Rejected status until every condition is corrected and reinspected

The capstone room must have finished drywall, installed windows, an installed or install-ready interior door, completed flooring references, and verified backing before this project begins, consistent with the Capstone Continuity Rule.

Trade Note

This project does not introduce new cutting or fitting techniques. Every method used here was taught and demonstrated in a specific earlier chapter. Where this chapter's procedure references a technique without repeating its full instruction, follow the numbered procedure in the chapter identified in the cross-reference rather than improvising a shortcut.

Common Field Fundamentals

This capstone does not introduce new field fundamentals. It requires disciplined sequencing of everything taught earlier in the book. The System-Sequence Control governs the order of every installation step: surface and opening defects are corrected before layout; the door and window operate correctly before casing conceals installation gaps; casing is installed before baseboard; baseboard is installed before shoe; crown layout is established before crown corners are cut; wall-trim layouts are coordinated with outlets, switches, and fixed obstacles; closet cleats follow the approved load design; stair trim preserves code-compliant geometry; and filling, caulking, and sanding follow final fastening and adjustment.

Door & WindowPrehung door operation and casing, window stool, apron, and casing
Floor TrimStraight and cornered baseboard, shoe molding or quarter round
Crown & Wall TrimCrown runs and corners, chair rail, picture rail, or board-and-batten
Closet & StairCloset trim and cleats, stair skirt or tread-and-riser trim where included

Sequencing Discipline

Complete each installation category across the entire room before beginning the next category unless a specific step directs otherwise. Do not fill or caulk any joint until every piece in the room is permanently fastened, adjusted, and has passed its category-specific inspection from the chapter where that technique was taught.

Measurement Cue — One Set of Controlling Lines for the Whole Project

Use the controlling reveal, height, drop, and spacing values recorded in the Project 01 room file for every installation step in this project. Do not re-derive a controlling line locally at any single wall; a locally re-derived line is the most common source of visible misalignment at the point where two categories of trim meet, such as a baseboard-to-casing return or a crown-to-wall-trim intersection.

Critical Safety Cue — Stair and Egress Conditions

If the capstone room includes a stair assembly, keep the stairway usable or fully barricaded throughout this project. Do not leave loose nosing, protruding fasteners, or partially secured stair trim in service at the end of any work session. Do not alter rise, run, tread depth, nosing, guard, or handrail geometry under this chapter.

Field Safety Cue — Long-Duration, Multi-Category Work

This project runs across multiple work sessions and multiple trim categories. Re-inspect saw guards, dust collection, stock support, and electrical safety at the start of every session rather than relying on the setup check performed at the beginning of the project.

Code Alert — Fire-Rated and Labeled Assemblies

If the capstone door, frame, or assembly is fire-rated or labeled, do not alter it beyond the approved listed details. Verify labeled-assembly status before adjusting hinges, strikes, or self-closing devices in this project.

E. Room Readiness and Workstation Preparation

  1. Obtain the approved room elevations, trim schedule, door and window schedules, floor information, finish specification, and inspection sequence. Confirm all documents correspond to the capstone room.
  2. Perform the Project 01 readiness inspection on the capstone room. Repeat the full surface, transition, opening, and obstacle inspection using the Project 01 procedure.
  3. Correct drywall, opening, backing, floor-reference, moisture, and access deficiencies. Refer any deficiency outside this project's scope to the responsible trade or a qualified professional and reinspect after correction.
  4. Prepare the cutting, assembly, dust-control, and staging station. Use the Project 02 procedure and complete the workstation readiness inspection before cutting any trim.
  5. Inventory and acclimate trim stock. Confirm quantities against the trim schedule and acclimate wood and engineered trim according to the manufacturer's requirement before cutting.
  6. Separate paint-grade and stain-grade material. Stage each category separately and mark the finish grade on each piece or bundle.

F. Controlling Lines and Cut List

  1. Establish casing reveals, baseboard height, crown drop and projection, rail heights, batten spacing, and other controlling lines. Transfer these values from the Project 01 room file and mark them on the walls of the capstone room.
  2. Prepare the complete cut list and installation sequence. Expand the Project 01 preliminary cut list into a full cut list covering every component in Section B, sequenced according to Section I of this work order.

G. Door, Window, and Casing Integration

  1. Install or verify the prehung interior door. Follow the complete Project 05 procedure if the door is not yet installed; verify all Project 05 acceptance standards if it is.
  2. Adjust door swing, reveal, hinges, latch, and strike. Follow the Project 06 procedure and confirm smooth, reliable operation before casing is planned.
  3. Install interior door casing. Follow the Project 04 procedure using the controlling reveal established in Section F.
  4. Install window stool, apron, and casing. Follow the Project 07 procedure for every window in the capstone room.

H. Floor Trim Integration

  1. Install straight baseboard runs. Follow the Project 08 procedure using the baseboard height established in Section F.
  2. Complete coped inside and mitered outside baseboard corners. Follow the Project 09 procedure at every inside and outside corner in the room.
  3. Install shoe molding or quarter round after the flooring relationship is confirmed. Follow the Project 10 procedure, including the fastening restriction for floating flooring.

I. Crown and Wall-Trim Integration

  1. Lay out crown molding and install straight runs. Follow the Project 11 procedure, confirming the spring angle with a test piece before cutting long runs.
  2. Cut and fit crown inside and outside corners. Follow the Project 12 procedure at every crown corner in the room.
  3. Install chair rail, picture rail, or the approved board-and-batten wall-trim feature. Follow the Project 13 or Project 14 procedure as specified for the capstone room, coordinating layout with outlets, switches, windows, doors, cabinets, and furniture.

J. Closet and Stair Integration

  1. Install closet trim and shelving cleats, or the approved alternate trim feature. Follow the Project 15 procedure, confirming the cleat design and load criteria before fastening.
  2. Complete stair skirt or tread-and-riser trim only when the capstone includes an inspected stair assembly. Follow the Project 16 and Project 17 procedures, keeping the stair barricaded or in controlled use throughout. Skip this step and record the omission if the capstone room has no stair assembly.

K. Clearance Verification and Defect Correction

  1. Verify all electrical, plumbing, HVAC, door, window, and access clearances. Confirm no installed trim obstructs a device, fixture, panel, shutoff, or access point identified in the Project 01 Obstacle and Backing Map.
  2. Check all reveals, alignments, corners, scarf joints, returns, and terminations. Compare each against the controlling lines established in Section F and the acceptance standards of the source chapter listed in Section B.
  3. Correct loose, bowed, split, short, damaged, or misaligned trim. Recut, refasten, or replace any piece that does not meet its source-chapter acceptance standard.

L. Surface Preparation

  1. Set proud fasteners. Follow the Project 18 procedure across every installed trim category.
  2. Fill approved nail holes and minor defects. Use filler compatible with the trim material and finish system for each piece.
  3. Sand filler and trim surfaces without damaging profiles. Sand flush and remove dust by HEPA vacuum before caulking.
  4. Caulk designated paint-grade joints. Apply and tool caulk only at joints identified in the finish specification.
  5. Leave stain-grade joints uncaulked unless the finish plan specifies otherwise. Verify joint fit meets the stain-grade standard from the relevant source chapter instead of relying on caulk.
  6. Clean dust, adhesive, filler, and caulk residue. Clean every surface before the final inspection.
  7. Spot-prime or prepare end grain, MDF edges, and repairs. Apply spot preparation where the finish specification requires it.

M. Functional Verification

  1. Operate all doors and windows. Confirm full, smooth operation with all trim installed.
  2. Test closet shelf support only to the specified load. Apply the test load identified in the Project 15 design; do not exceed it.
  3. Inspect stair trim for trip, movement, projection, and geometry hazards where included. Confirm the stair remains safe for return to service.

N. Final Inspection and Closeout

  1. Inspect the room under normal and raking light. Repeat the Project 01 surface-inspection method across the completed trim package.
  2. Prepare a final deficiency list. Consolidate every unresolved item from Sections K through M and any item carried forward from earlier chapters.
  3. Correct and reinspect every item. Close each deficiency using the correction method specified in its source chapter.
  4. Compile cut lists, profile records, layout measurements, photographs, finish-preparation records, repairs, and inspection results. Assemble the complete closeout package for the room.
  5. Hand off the room as complete, clean, and ready for paint or stain. Record the handoff date and the name of the party receiving the room.
  6. Document the formal transition to Book 13 cabinet and built-in work. Record any clearance, backing, or trim-line information Book 13 will need for cabinet and built-in installation against this room's finished surfaces.

Common Cutting Inspection

Apply the following cross-category checks after every installation category in Sections G through J, in addition to the category-specific inspection required by that category's source chapter.

  • Reference check: confirm the category's reveal, height, drop, or spacing matches the single Project 01 controlling-line record.
  • Sequence check: confirm no later category was started before its prerequisite category passed inspection.
  • Obstacle check: confirm the installed trim does not obstruct any item on the Project 01 Obstacle and Backing Map.
  • Finish-grade check: confirm paint-grade and stain-grade pieces were not mixed within a single run or joint.
  • Corner check: confirm every coped and mitered corner meets its source-chapter face-contact and profile-continuity standard.

Complete the Final Inspection Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before compiling the closeout package.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Crown Drop Does Not Match Baseboard Height at a Return

Probable cause: the crown drop or baseboard height was measured locally at that wall instead of from the Project 01 controlling-line record.
Confirmation: compare both measurements against the single recorded controlling-line values.
Correction: remark and, where already fastened, recut or reinstall the affected run from the correct controlling value.
Reinspection: repeat the reveal and height check at the return and at the adjacent corners.

A Joint Was Caulked That Should Have Remained Stain-Grade

Probable cause: paint-grade and stain-grade material or joints were not separated or marked before the filling and caulking phase.
Confirmation: compare the caulked joint against the finish specification's stain-grade joint list.
Correction: remove the caulk without damaging the profile, refit the joint to the stain-grade standard, and finish according to the approved color-matched repair method.
Reinspection: repeat the finish-grade check across all remaining stain-grade joints in the room.

Shoe Molding Was Fastened Through Floating Flooring

Probable cause: the flooring system was not confirmed before fastening, or the fastening restriction from Project 10 was overlooked during the integrated sequence.
Confirmation: check the flooring manufacturer's instructions and the fastener location against the baseboard or wall substrate.
Correction: remove the incorrect fastener, refasten to the baseboard or approved substrate, and repair any resulting flooring damage through the responsible trade.
Reinspection: repeat the fastening-method check along the full shoe-molding run.

A Later Category Was Installed Before an Earlier Category Passed Inspection

Probable cause: the System-Sequence Control order in Sections G through J was not followed, or a category's inspection was skipped to save time.
Confirmation: compare the installation log against the required sequence in Section B.
Correction: inspect the skipped category against its source-chapter acceptance standard; where installed trim now conceals a deficiency, remove enough trim to inspect and correct the concealed condition.
Reinspection: repeat the category-specific inspection for both the concealed category and the category that was installed over it.

The Room Was Handed Off with an Open Deficiency

Probable cause: a deficiency was corrected but not reinspected, or an item was left off the final deficiency list during consolidation.
Confirmation: compare the final deficiency list against the reinspection record for each item and against the Sections K through M verification steps.
Correction: withhold or revise the handoff status until every deficiency is reinspected and closed, consistent with the Project 01 readiness-decision standard.
Reinspection: repeat the full final-inspection pass under normal and raking light before reissuing the handoff record.

Cleanup

This project ends with a fully documented, clean, paint- or stain-ready room and a complete closeout package, not with any further material work.

  • Remove all scrap, offcuts, and packaging from the room and the workstation
  • HEPA-vacuum the room, including sills, floor perimeter, and any surface where sanding or filling occurred
  • Return all saws, fasteners, adhesives, fillers, and caulk to controlled storage, disposing of empty containers and sharp-metal waste appropriately
  • Compile the closeout package: cut lists, profile records, layout measurements, photographs, finish-preparation records, repair records, the final deficiency list, and the final inspection results
  • File the closeout package with the Project 01 room file for the same room
  • Confirm the room is barricaded-free, clean, and left in a condition suitable for the Book 13 cabinet and built-in trade to begin work
