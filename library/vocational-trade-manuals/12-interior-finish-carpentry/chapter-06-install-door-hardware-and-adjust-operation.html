Job Overview

This project qualifies you to install a complete interior latch set into a previously bored and mortised door and to adjust the door's hinges, latch, and strike until the door operates smoothly and latches reliably. The finished work product is one operating interior door with an installed latch or lockset, a properly positioned and fastened strike plate, and a documented record of every adjustment made to correct binding, rubbing, or unreliable latching.

You will review the hardware manufacturer's instructions, confirm the door and frame are securely installed, install the latch and handles, mark and install the strike plate, diagnose and correct hinge binding and other contact problems within the permitted correction scope, adjust the strike for reliable engagement, and inspect the finished door for reveal, swing, latch function, handle return, strike security, and floor clearance.

This project depends directly on Project 05, which installed and plumbed the prehung door and frame this hardware is installed into. It also depends on the casing reveal established in Project 01 and applied in Project 04, since casing must remain in place and undisturbed while this hardware is installed. The adjusted, latching door produced here is the door that Project 18 later prepares for paint or stain finishing.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • FC-031 — Install the latch set in the correct bevel orientation and secure the faceplate and handles without stripping screws.
  • FC-032 — Mark and install the strike plate at the correct location on the strike jamb using the specified screws.
  • FC-033 — Adjust the door reveal at the head, hinge, and strike jambs within the permitted correction scope.
  • FC-034 — Diagnose and correct hinge binding, hinge-side rubbing, and other contact problems using approved correction methods only.
  • FC-035 — Align latch operation so the latch engages the strike without excessive force or rattle, for both privacy and passage function as applicable.
  • FC-036 — Inspect finished door function, including reveal, swing, latch engagement, handle return, strike security, and floor clearance, and document adjustments and any unresolved opening defects.

Where This Skill Fits

Project 05 set the door plumb in its frame with consistent reveals but installed no operating hardware. This project completes that door by installing the latch set that lets it actually be used, and by correcting the small alignment problems — binding, rubbing, uneven contact — that only become visible once the door is repeatedly operated under load.

The reveal and swing standards you verify here are the same standards the Chapter 20 capstone re-checks before a room is handed off for paint or stain. A door that passes this project's inspection should not need hinge or strike correction again during the capstone walk-through.

Scope and Intended Use

This project covers:

  • reviewing the hardware manufacturer's instructions for backset, handing, latch orientation, and strike location
  • confirming the door and frame are securely installed before hardware work begins
  • installing the latch, faceplate, and inside and outside handles or knobs
  • marking, positioning, and fastening the strike plate
  • testing latch engagement and door closure
  • diagnosing hinge binding, rubbing, and contact problems and correcting them using hinge adjustment, shimming, planing, or strike adjustment only, within the permitted correction scope
  • adjusting the strike so the latch engages without excessive force or rattle
  • testing privacy or passage function as applicable to the hardware installed
  • inspecting reveal, swing, latch, handle return, strike security, and floor clearance
  • documenting adjustments made and any unresolved opening defects

This project does not authorize the learner to:

  • rehang, replumb, or reset a door or frame that is not already securely installed; unresolved installation defects are referred back to Project 05 or to a qualified professional
  • alter fire-rated doors, self-closing devices, labeled frames, or smoke seals beyond the approved listed hardware and installation details
  • enlarge a bore, mortise, or strike pocket beyond the correction limits stated in Section J of this project's work order
  • substitute hardware, backset, or fastener specifications different from the approved hardware schedule without documenting the change and the reason

Any door that cannot be brought to a reliable latching, smooth-swinging condition within this project's permitted correction scope must be documented as an unresolved opening defect and referred to a qualified professional before the room proceeds to Project 18.

Trade Note

Hardware installation is where small framing and jamb-setting errors from Project 05 finally show up. A door that looked plumb and reveal-consistent when first hung can still bind or rub once the latch is engaged and the door is closed repeatedly under its own weight. Treat every binding or rubbing symptom as a diagnostic problem with a specific cause, not as a condition to be forced past with a longer screw or a heavier push.

Setup Standard

Do not begin hardware installation until Project 05's readiness condition is confirmed: the door and frame are plumb, securely fastened, and swinging freely with no hardware installed. A door that is still shimmed, unfastened, or out of adjustment at the frame must be corrected in Project 05 before this project begins.

Shop Tip

Operate the latch by hand, without the strike plate installed, before mounting the strike. Confirm the latch bolt extends and retracts smoothly and the bevel faces the correct direction. Diagnosing a stiff or reversed latch before the strike is installed is faster than diagnosing it after the door will not close.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every latch installation in this project depends on three conditions: the door and frame are already correctly hung, so hardware work never substitutes for frame correction; the latch is installed in the correct bevel orientation so it retracts and extends properly as the door closes; and any binding or rubbing found during testing is corrected using only the permitted methods, applied at the specific point of contact identified by diagnosis rather than by forcing the door or over-tightening hardware.

LatchBore, faceplate, and handles installed in correct orientation with even screw tension
StrikeLocation transferred from the closed latch center and mortised to the specified depth
Contact pointsHinge, head-jamb, and strike-side contact diagnosed and corrected within the permitted scope
FunctionLatch engagement, handle return, and privacy or passage operation tested and confirmed

Diagnosing Binding Before Adjusting Anything

Close the door slowly and watch where it makes contact before it reaches its final position. Binding at the top corner of the hinge side points to a different correction than binding at the bottom of the strike side. Identify the exact contact point first; do not begin tightening hinge screws or planing an edge until the contact point is confirmed.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Backset Before Boring Assumptions

Confirm the door's existing bore matches the backset of the specified latch set before attempting installation. Do not assume a standard backset; verify it against the hardware manufacturer's instructions and the door schedule for this opening.

Critical Safety Cue — Preserve Fire-Rated and Labeled Assemblies

Do not install hardware, strike plates, or fasteners on a fire-rated or labeled door assembly that are different from the approved listed hardware for that assembly. Do not remove, defeat, or block a self-closing device or smoke seal to ease hardware installation. Stop work and refer the opening to a qualified professional if the specified hardware does not match the labeled assembly's listing.

Field Safety Cue — Working Around a Swinging Door

Keep the swing path clear of tools, cords, and materials while testing door operation. Support the door against uncontrolled swinging on sloped or uneven floors, and keep fingers clear of the hinge barrel and door edge during repeated open-close testing.

Manufacturer Instruction Cue — Follow the Hardware Template

Use the manufacturer's paper template or stated dimensions to locate the strike-plate mortise and faceplate screws. Do not estimate these locations by eye; a strike positioned even 1/16 in. off the transferred centerline can prevent reliable latching.

F. Document Review and Confirmation of Readiness

  1. Review the hardware instructions, door preparation, backset, handing, latch orientation, and strike location. Confirm the specified latch set matches the door schedule for this opening before opening the hardware packaging.
  2. Confirm the door and frame are securely installed. Verify against the Project 05 record that the door is plumb, fastened, and swinging freely with no open shims or unresolved reveal issues.
  3. Inspect the bore, edge mortise, hinge screws, and jamb for damage. Record any damage found before installing hardware.

G. Latch and Handle Installation

  1. Install the latch in the correct bevel orientation. Insert the latch body into the edge bore with the bevel facing the direction specified by the manufacturer's instructions for the door's swing direction.
  2. Secure the latch faceplate without stripping screws. Drive faceplate screws evenly and stop as soon as the faceplate seats flush; do not continue driving once resistance increases sharply.
  3. Install the inside and outside handles or knobs. Align the spindle and rose or escutcheon before engaging the mounting screws.
  4. Tighten mounting screws evenly. Alternate between screws on opposite sides of the assembly to avoid distorting the escutcheon or binding the spindle.
  5. Operate the latch before installing the strike. With the door open, press and release the latch bolt by hand to confirm smooth retraction and extension.

H. Strike Plate Location and Installation

  1. Mark the latch center on the strike jamb. Close the door to its resting position and mark the latch bolt's vertical center on the strike jamb using the manufacturer's transfer method.
  2. Position and fasten the strike plate using the specified screws. Center the strike-plate opening on the transferred mark, confirm the mortise depth matches the manufacturer's specification, and drive the specified screws.
  3. Test latch engagement and door closure. Close the door under normal closing effort and confirm the latch bolt enters and seats in the strike without forcing.

I. Diagnosing Binding and Contact Problems

  1. Diagnose hinge binding, hinge-side rubbing, head-jamb contact, strike-side contact, or floor drag. Close the door slowly through its full arc and identify the exact location and point in the swing where contact occurs.
  2. Tighten loose hinge screws. Check every hinge screw for looseness before considering any other correction method.

J. Permitted Correction Scope

  1. Install approved longer screws into framing where required. Use this correction only where a hinge has worked loose from the jamb framing behind the shim, and only with the screw length and location specified for this correction.
  2. Use hinge adjustment, shimming, planing, or strike adjustment only within the permitted correction scope. Shim behind a specific hinge leaf to correct localized binding; plane a door edge only at the identified contact point and only removing the minimum material needed to clear the contact; adjust the strike position only within the strike plate's mortise tolerance.
  3. Preserve required fire-door and labeled-frame conditions. Do not plane, shim, or otherwise alter a labeled fire-rated door or frame assembly outside the approved listed correction methods for that assembly; refer such conditions to a qualified professional.

K. Strike Adjustment and Function Testing

  1. Adjust the strike so the latch engages without excessive force or rattle. Reposition or re-mortise the strike within its plate tolerance until the door closes with normal effort and does not rattle when closed.
  2. Test privacy or passage function. Operate the locking function, if installed, from both sides according to the hardware's specified function, confirming it locks, unlocks, and releases as intended.

L. Final Inspection and Documentation

  1. Inspect reveal, swing, latch, handle return, strike security, and floor clearance. Confirm every item against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Document adjustments and unresolved opening defects. Record every correction made under Section J and any condition that could not be resolved within the permitted correction scope, and identify the professional responsible for further correction where required.

Common Cutting Inspection

This project performs no primary trim cutting. Apply the following checks to any material removed under the permitted correction scope, such as planing at a contact point.

  • Minimum-removal check: confirm only the material needed to clear the identified contact point was removed.
  • Reveal-preservation check: confirm the reveal established in Project 05 was not altered outside its stated tolerance by the correction.
  • Edge-finish check: confirm a planed edge is smooth and does not leave a visible step or tool mark at the door face.
  • Reassembly check: confirm any hardware removed for access during correction was reinstalled and retested.

Complete the Function and Adjustment Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Project 07.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Latch Bolt Does Not Retract Smoothly

Probable cause: the latch was installed with the bevel facing the wrong direction for the door's swing, or the faceplate screws were overtightened and are binding the mechanism.
Confirmation: operate the latch bolt by hand with the door open and compare the bevel orientation to the manufacturer's instructions for this door's handing.
Correction: back off overtightened faceplate screws to the specified seating tension, or remove and reinstall the latch with the correct bevel orientation.
Reinspection: repeat the hand-operated latch test before installing the strike.

Door Will Not Latch After Strike Installation

Probable cause: the strike-plate opening was positioned off the transferred latch centerline, or the mortise depth does not match the manufacturer's specification.
Confirmation: close the door slowly and observe where the latch bolt contacts the strike plate relative to its opening.
Correction: adjust the strike position within its mortise tolerance under Section J, or remove and reposition the strike plate from a corrected centerline transfer.
Reinspection: repeat the latch engagement and closure test.

Door Binds at the Top Corner of the Hinge Side

Probable cause: the top hinge has worked loose from its jamb framing, or a hinge leaf shim shifted during Project 05.
Confirmation: check the top hinge screws for looseness and inspect the shim behind the hinge leaf.
Correction: tighten loose screws first; if the screws will not tighten because the framing is stripped, install the approved longer hinge screw specified for this correction.
Reinspection: repeat the slow-close swing test and check the reveal at the hinge jamb.

Door Rattles When Closed Even Though It Latches

Probable cause: the strike opening is oversized relative to the latch bolt, or the strike was mortised too deep, allowing the bolt excess travel before seating.
Confirmation: close the door and gently push and pull on the handle to check for play between the latch bolt and strike.
Correction: adjust the strike position within its mortise tolerance under Section J to reduce bolt travel before seating.
Reinspection: repeat the rattle check and the normal-effort closing test.

Handle Does Not Return Fully After Operation

Probable cause: the mounting screws were tightened unevenly, distorting the escutcheon and binding the spindle.
Confirmation: loosen the mounting screws slightly and check whether the handle returns freely.
Correction: retighten the mounting screws evenly, alternating sides, stopping as soon as the escutcheon seats without distortion.
Reinspection: repeat the handle-return check through several full operation cycles.

Cleanup

This project ends with a fully operating door and a filed adjustment record, not with material disposal.

  • Remove hardware packaging, template sheets, and any masking or protective film from the installed hardware
  • Return chisels, punches, and drivers to storage in working condition
  • Sweep any wood shavings or debris from planing corrections
  • Compile the Hardware Installation Record, adjustment notes, and Function and Adjustment Worksheet into the room file established in Project 01
  • Store any removed shims, spare hardware screws, or documentation with the room file for reference during later chapters

Field Terms

Backset
The horizontal distance from the door edge to the center of the bore, matched to the specified latch set.
Latch Bevel
The angled face of the latch bolt, oriented so it retracts on closing and extends past the strike opening.
Strike Center
The point on the strike jamb transferred from the latch centerline with the door closed.
Permitted Correction Scope
The approved adjustment methods for this project: hinge adjustment, shimming, planing, and strike adjustment.
Passage Function
Latch hardware that operates without a locking mechanism.
Privacy Function
Latch hardware with a room-side locking mechanism and an outside emergency release.
Reveal
The consistent gap between the door face and the jamb at the head, hinge, and strike sides.
Faceplate
The plate on the latch body that seats flush with the door edge and is fastened to hold the latch in the bore.
Escutcheon
The decorative plate or rose surrounding a handle or knob at its mounting point.
Unresolved Opening Defect
A binding, latching, or alignment problem that cannot be corrected within the permitted correction scope and must be referred to a qualified professional.
