Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to inspect a completed painting and finishing project and issue a documented acceptance, conditional acceptance, or rejection decision. The finished work product is a completed Final Quality Inspection Worksheet covering wall and ceiling coverage, cut lines, trim and cabinet finishes, stain and clear-coat consistency, exterior work, masonry and floor coatings, sprayed finishes, hardware and component operation, protection removal, and cleanup, together with a written punch list of every deficiency, its assigned correction, and its reinspection result.

You will assemble the approved color and finish schedules and every prior chapter's product, environmental, and coat records; inspect the completed work under normal and raking light; check coverage, cut lines, sheen, drips, and sags; verify hardware and component operation; confirm protection and temporary coverings have been removed; compare finished color and sheen against approved samples; record every deficiency by location and type; assign a correction method, product, responsible person, and reinspection condition; correct and reinspect every item; and issue the final documented finish status.

This chapter depends on every preceding chapter's records. It cannot be performed against a project with no color schedule, no product records, and no prior inspection history. Chapter 20 uses the inspection method taught here as its own closeout step, applied to the complete capstone room.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • PF-109 — Inspect wall and ceiling coverage for holidays, flashing, thin areas, lap marks, and roller texture.
  • PF-110 — Inspect cut lines at ceilings, trim, corners, windows, doors, fixtures, and color transitions.
  • PF-111 — Inspect finished surfaces for sheen consistency against approved samples.
  • PF-112 — Inspect finished surfaces for drips, sags, brush marks, dust nibs, and adhesion defects.
  • PF-113 — Document punch-list corrections by location, type, correction method, and reinspection result.
  • PF-114 — Approve, conditionally approve, or reject finish readiness based on documented inspection results.

Where This Skill Fits

Every chapter from Chapter 3 through Chapter 18 produces work that this chapter verifies. This chapter does not repeat preparation, priming, or application work; it inspects the finished result against the standards those chapters already established and against the readiness decision issued in Chapter 1. A defect found here is corrected using the specific method taught in the chapter that produced it — a coverage gap returns to Chapter 9's rolling standard, a failed cut line returns to Chapter 8, a coating failure returns to Chapter 18's cause-based repair method.

The formal acceptance, conditional acceptance, or rejection decision issued here is the same type of documented outcome introduced in Chapter 1's readiness decision. Chapter 20's capstone room closes out using this exact inspection sequence, so the discipline of recording every finding by location, assigning a specific correction, and reinspecting before final sign-off carries forward unchanged.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • assembling approved schedules, product records, sample approvals, environmental records, coat records, and prior inspection reports before inspection begins
  • inspecting wall and ceiling coverage, cut lines, trim and cabinet finishes, stain and clear-coat consistency, exterior work, masonry and floor coatings, and sprayed finishes under normal and raking light
  • verifying hardware, doors, drawers, windows, vents, and access panel operation
  • verifying removal of tape, protection, labels, and temporary coverings
  • comparing finished color and sheen with approved samples
  • recording deficiencies by location and type
  • assigning correction method, product, responsible person, and reinspection condition
  • protecting accepted work during correction, correcting and reinspecting every item, verifying cleanup and waste removal, and issuing a documented approved, conditional, or rejected finish status
  • preparing maintenance, cure, cleaning, and touch-up information for handoff

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • perform the original preparation, priming, application, or repair work; correction of an identified deficiency must follow the specific method taught in the chapter that governs that surface or defect type, most commonly Chapter 18
  • issue a final approved status for any item with an incomplete correction or an unrecorded reinspection result
  • approve sprayed, masonry, or exterior work that has not met the environmental, cure, or coverage requirements established in its governing chapter
  • waive a documented color or sheen mismatch against an approved sample without a written justification and sign-off from the party who approved the original sample

Any item that fails inspection must be corrected using its governing chapter's method and reinspected before this chapter's finish status may be approved.

Trade Note

This inspection is comparative, not opinion-based. Every finding is checked against a specific reference: the approved color and finish schedule, an approved sample, a prior chapter's coverage or cut-line standard, or a manufacturer's cure requirement. A finding with no reference to compare against cannot be recorded as a deficiency; it must be resolved by locating or reconstructing the missing reference before the item is judged.

Setup Standard

Do not begin the final inspection until the specified dry or cure time for every coating system has passed (Step 2) and every schedule, sample approval, and prior inspection record has been assembled (Step 1). Do not issue a final finish status (Step 21) until every recorded deficiency has a documented correction and reinspection result.

Shop Tip

Inspect in the same room sequence used for application — ceiling, walls, trim, doors, cabinets — rather than jumping between areas. A consistent sequence keeps the worksheet organized and makes it easier to confirm nothing was skipped when you reach the final punch-list review.

Prerequisites

  • Approved color and finish schedules, product records, sample approvals, environmental records, coat records, and prior inspection reports obtained and assembled in one location
  • Specified dry or cure time confirmed complete for every coating system present, including primer, wall and ceiling finish, trim enamel, stain and clear coat, exterior coatings, masonry or floor coatings, and any sprayed finish
  • Access to every surface, hardware component, and protected area within the scope of the completed project
  • Approved color and sheen samples available for direct comparison
  • Authorization to assign corrections, involve the responsible party, and withhold final approval until every punch-list item is reinspected and closed

Common Field Fundamentals

This chapter applies one inspection method across every surface type produced in Chapters 3 through 18: compare the finished condition against its governing standard, record any deviation by location and type, assign a specific correction referencing the chapter method that applies, correct the item, and reinspect before closing it. The same method scales from a single touched-up wall to the full capstone room in Chapter 20.

Normal Light and Raking Light

A normal-light pass identifies obvious coverage, color, and sheen problems visible during ordinary use of the space. A raking-light pass — the same technique introduced in Chapter 1 — reveals roller texture, holidays, lap marks, and film-thickness irregularities that normal lighting conceals. Every wall and ceiling receiving final inspection under this chapter must be checked under both conditions.

Measurement Cue — Record Every Finding at the Point of Inspection

Write every deficiency directly on the worksheet at the moment you observe it, with its exact location, type, and the reference standard it fails against. Do not rely on memory to reconstruct the punch list after leaving the room. An unrecorded finding cannot be assigned a correction or reinspected.

Critical Safety Cue — Cure Time Before Inspection

Do not begin final inspection, and do not perform adhesion or operational checks that stress the coating, until the specified dry or cure time has passed for every coating system present. Inspecting or testing an undercured finish can produce false defect readings and can damage a coating that would otherwise pass.

Critical Safety Cue — Suspect Coating Failures

If a coating failure discovered during this inspection suggests an uncorrected moisture, contamination, or hazardous-coating cause, do not proceed directly to touch-up. Route the finding to Chapter 18's cause-based diagnosis before any correction is applied.

Field Safety Cue — Ladders, Lighting, and Footing

Use a stable, rated ladder or platform for ceiling and upper-wall inspection. Confirm adequate lighting before inspecting closets, corners, cabinet interiors, and areas behind furniture. Keep the inspection path clear of tools and loose materials.

Code Alert — Slip Resistance, Fire Rating, and Exposure Approvals

Where the completed project includes a slip-resistant floor coating, a rated assembly, or an exterior exposure rating, verify that the finished surface still meets its required classification before issuing final approval. A coating defect that compromises a required rating must be corrected before the item is closed, regardless of its cosmetic appearance.

E. Document Assembly and Cure Verification

  1. Assemble approved color and finish schedules, product records, sample approvals, environmental records, coat records, and prior inspection reports. Record which documents were located and which are missing before proceeding.
  2. Confirm specified dry or cure time has passed for inspection. Check the coat record and product data sheet for every coating system present and record the confirmed cure status for each.

F. Coverage and Cut-Line Inspection

  1. Inspect surfaces under normal room lighting. Walk the full project scope once under ordinary lighting conditions and record obvious defects.
  2. Inspect critical surfaces under raking light. Repeat the pass on every wall and ceiling using a handheld light held low and to the side.
  3. Check wall and ceiling coverage for holidays, flashing, thin areas, lap marks, and roller texture. Record each finding by location.
  4. Inspect cut lines at ceilings, trim, corners, windows, doors, fixtures, and color transitions. Record any crooked, uneven-width, or bled cut line by location.

G. Trim, Cabinet, Stain, and Exterior Inspection

  1. Inspect trim, doors, cabinets, built-ins, and wood finishes for runs, sags, brush marks, dust nibs, blocking, and adhesion. Record each finding with its location and defect type.
  2. Inspect stain color, grain, overlap, and sheen consistency. Compare against the approved sample directly, side by side, under consistent lighting.
  3. Inspect exterior work for edge coverage, drainage, weather seals, and coating continuity. Confirm no drainage gap, weep, or weather seal was coated over.

H. Masonry, Floor, and Sprayed-Finish Inspection

  1. Inspect masonry and floor coatings for adhesion, bubbles, pinholes, contamination, profile, and slip condition. Record findings against the standard established in Chapter 16.
  2. Inspect sprayed finishes for orange peel, dry spray, runs, overspray, and pattern inconsistency. Record findings against the standard established in Chapter 17.

I. Function, Protection, and Comparison Checks

  1. Verify hardware, doors, drawers, windows, vents, and access panels operate correctly. Operate each component through its full range and record any sticking, blocking, or misalignment.
  2. Verify that tape, protection, labels, and temporary coverings have been removed as required. Walk every room and confirm no protective material remains in place.
  3. Check floors, cabinets, glass, fixtures, and adjacent property for spatter or damage. Record any damage requiring correction outside the coating scope.
  4. Compare colors and sheens with approved samples. Confirm every finished surface matches its assigned sample within the schedule's stated tolerance.

J. Punch List, Correction, and Finish Status

  1. Record deficiencies by location and type. Enter every finding from Sections F through I onto the punch list with a unique location reference.
  2. Assign correction method, product, responsible person, and reinspection condition. Route each item to its governing chapter method — most commonly Chapter 18 for coating failures and touch-ups — and record who will perform the correction and how it will be reinspected.
  3. Protect accepted work during corrections. Mask or otherwise protect surrounding surfaces that already passed inspection before correction work begins nearby.
  4. Correct and reinspect every item. Complete each assigned correction and record the reinspection result directly beside the original finding.
  5. Verify cleanup, waste removal, and safe storage. Confirm spatter, dust, tools, and protection materials have been removed and that paint, solvent, and rag waste has been disposed of using approved methods.
  6. Issue approved, conditional, or rejected finish status. Approve only when every punch-list item is closed; issue a conditional status listing exact open items and required timeline if minor items remain; reject the project if deficiencies are broad or a governing standard was not met.
  7. Prepare maintenance, cure, cleaning, and touch-up information. Compile the information the occupant or next trade will need to protect and maintain the finished work.

Common Inspection Points

Apply the same checks across every surface category regardless of which chapter produced it.

  • Cure check: confirm the specified dry or cure time was verified for every coating system before inspection began.
  • Raking-light check: confirm every wall and ceiling was inspected under raking light, not normal light alone.
  • Reference check: confirm every recorded finding cites the specific standard or sample it was judged against.
  • Function check: confirm every hardware component and moving part was operated through its full range during inspection.
  • Punch-list closure check: confirm every recorded deficiency has a completed correction and a documented reinspection result before the finish status is issued.
  • Status check: confirm the finish status states approved, conditional, or rejected, with open items or reasons listed for any conditional or rejected decision.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Sheen Mismatch Appears Only Under Raking or Side Light

Probable cause: inconsistent film build between the original coat and a later touch-up, or two different product batches used on the same plane.
Confirmation: compare the affected area with the approved sample under both normal and raking light, and check the coat record for batch or lot differences.
Correction: recoat the full plane rather than spot-touching, per the Chapter 18 standard for sheen mismatches that local touch-up cannot resolve.
Reinspection: reinspect the recoated plane under raking light and compare it against the approved sample before closing the item.

A Cabinet Door Sticks After Reinstallation

Probable cause: coating applied inside a hinge cup or against an adjoining surface before full cure, or hinges reinstalled before the specified cure time elapsed.
Confirmation: operate the door through its full range and inspect the hinge cup, edge, and adjoining surface for paint bridging or incomplete cure.
Correction: remove the door, correct the coating buildup per the Chapter 11 standard, allow full cure, and reinstall and adjust the hinges.
Reinspection: operate the door through its full range again and confirm smooth movement with no contact or drag.

A Punch-List Item Is Marked Closed With No Reinspection Result Recorded

Probable cause: the correction was completed but the worksheet was not updated with a reinspection entry before moving to the next item.
Confirmation: review the punch list for any item marked corrected with a blank reinspection field.
Correction: return to the affected location, perform the reinspection, and record the result before treating the item as closed.
Reinspection: confirm every closed item on the punch list has both a correction entry and a reinspection entry before the finish status is issued.

A Drainage Weep or Movement Joint Is Found Coated Over

Probable cause: masking or cut-in work in Chapter 14 or 15 did not exclude a required drainage or movement detail.
Confirmation: inspect the affected detail and compare it against the exterior schedule's list of excluded joints and weeps.
Correction: reopen the coated-over detail using a method that does not damage the surrounding finish, per the governing exterior chapter.
Reinspection: confirm the detail is fully open and functional, and inspect surrounding coating for damage from the correction.

A Floor Coating Shows Pinholes After Full Cure

Probable cause: off-gassing from the concrete substrate during application, inadequate induction time, or moisture in the slab at the time of coating.
Confirmation: inspect the pattern and density of pinholes and review the Chapter 16 moisture-test and induction-time records.
Correction: correct per the Chapter 16 and Chapter 18 standards, which may require grinding, moisture retesting, and recoating the affected area.
Reinspection: reinspect the corrected area for adhesion, pinholes, and slip condition before closing the item.

Finish Status Is Approved With Open Punch-List Items

Probable cause: a decision to approve was made before every recorded deficiency reached a documented reinspection result.
Confirmation: review the complete punch list against the finish status entry for any unresolved item.
Correction: revise the finish status to conditional, list the open items and required timeline, and complete the outstanding corrections and reinspections.
Reinspection: confirm every item is closed before reissuing the finish status as approved.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, closed inspection record retained for handoff, not a physical work area to restore beyond what is verified in Step 20.

  • Return the handheld light, camera, sample cards, and clipboard to storage
  • Confirm all photographs are labeled with their corresponding punch-list entries
  • File the completed Final Quality Inspection Worksheet with the approved color and finish schedule, product records, and prior chapter reports
  • Confirm all correction waste, spent materials, and protection debris have been removed and disposed of using approved methods
  • Store the finish status and maintenance information where it will be available for handoff or for the Chapter 20 capstone closeout

Field Terms

Holiday
A thin or missed spot in a coating film where the substrate or a prior coat shows through.
Flashing
An uneven sheen pattern caused by inconsistent film build or absorption, typically at touch-up areas.
Cut Line
The painted edge where a brushed or cut-in area meets an adjoining color, trim boundary, or ceiling line.
Blocking
Coating tackiness that causes a painted component to stick to an adjoining surface.
Orange Peel
A textured, dimpled sprayed-finish surface caused by improper atomization, pressure, or distance.
Punch List
The documented list of deficiencies identified during final inspection, each with a location, type, correction method, and reinspection result.
Finish Status
The documented approved, conditional, or rejected outcome of this chapter's inspection process.
Reinspection
The recorded verification that a corrected deficiency now meets its governing standard.
Governing Chapter
The earlier chapter whose preparation, application, or repair standard controls how a given deficiency must be corrected.
Final Quality Inspection Worksheet
The document recording every inspected surface, finding, correction, and final finish status for the project.
