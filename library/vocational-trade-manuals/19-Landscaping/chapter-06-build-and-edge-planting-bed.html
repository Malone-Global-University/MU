Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to lay out, shape, prepare, and edge one planting bed so that it is clearly defined against adjacent lawn or hardscape, holds prepared soil at the correct grade, and provides stable, safe, mower-clear edging. The finished work product is one bed with a marked and approved perimeter, prepared soil, and installed edging that passes inspection for width, curve quality, drainage, edge security, and mowing clearance.

You will review the approved bed shape, plant layout, mature plant size, drainage, edging, and maintenance access; mark and inspect the bed perimeter from primary viewing points; verify clearance from fixed features; cut the bed edge; remove sod and unwanted vegetation within the bed; prepare soil using the methods taught in Chapters 3 through 5; install the specified edging; and inspect and document the completed bed.

This chapter uses the readiness plan from Chapter 1, the site protections from Chapter 2, the tested and amended soil from Chapter 3, the vegetation-clearing methods from Chapter 4, and the grading and drainage work from Chapter 5. The bed produced here becomes the required surface for the fabric-and-mulch work in Chapter 7 and every planting chapter that follows through Chapter 10.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • LS-031 — Lay out the bed shape from the approved plan and verify it against fixed features and viewing points.
  • LS-032 — Cut clean bed edges at the approved depth and angle.
  • LS-033 — Prepare planting soil within the laid-out bed using the methods taught in Chapters 3 through 5.
  • LS-034 — Install the specified edging material on a stable base, fastened or staked clear of utilities.
  • LS-035 — Separate turf from the bed with a clean, maintained edge relationship.
  • LS-036 — Inspect bed definition, correcting loose, high, low, or jagged edging before documentation.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter depends directly on the readiness plan from Chapter 1, the protected work area from Chapter 2, the tested and amended soil from Chapter 3, the cleared vegetation from Chapter 4, and the graded, positively drained surface from Chapter 5. It applies those results to a specific, permanently defined bed shape rather than treating the bed as an assumed given.

The completed, edged bed produced in this chapter is the required surface for Chapter 7's fabric and mulch installation and for the shrub, tree, and perennial planting taught in Chapters 8 through 10. No later planting chapter authorizes work in a bed that has not been laid out, cleared, prepared, and edged under this chapter's standards.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved bed shape, plant layout, mature plant size, drainage, edging, and maintenance-access plan
  • marking the bed perimeter and inspecting curves from primary viewing points
  • verifying clearance from foundations, utilities, drains, paths, doors, and equipment
  • adjusting curves to avoid narrow mowing strips and inaccessible pockets
  • cutting the bed edge at the approved depth and angle
  • removing sod and unwanted vegetation within the bed using Chapter 4 methods
  • preparing soil within the bed using Chapters 3 through 5
  • installing metal, plastic, wood, masonry, stone, or other approved edging on a stable, fastened base
  • maintaining the specified relationship between edging, turf, and mulch
  • inspecting bed width, curve quality, drainage, soil condition, edge security, and mowing clearance

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • mark, cut, stake, or install edging in an area that has not received an approved or conditional readiness decision under Chapter 1, or that has unresolved utility-locating deficiencies
  • select a bed shape, edging material, or edging depth that departs from the approved plan without documenting the controlling reason
  • fasten or stake edging without confirming utility clearance at the specific fastening or staking location
  • proceed to Chapter 7 fabric and mulch work, or any Chapter 8 through 10 planting work, in a bed that has not passed this chapter's inspection

Any bed area showing an unresolved boundary, drainage, or utility deficiency from Chapters 1 through 5 must be corrected and reinspected under the controlling chapter before this chapter's layout and edging work begins.

Trade Note

Every curve, clearance measurement, and edge depth is recorded on the Landscape Readiness Worksheet at the time it is marked, not reconstructed from memory after the edging is installed. A curve approved from only one viewing point, or an edge depth not recorded before backfilling, is the most common source of the bed-definition errors diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not cut the bed edge until the perimeter has been marked, reviewed from primary viewing points, and verified for clearance from foundations, utilities, drains, paths, doors, and equipment. Do not fasten or stake edging at any point whose utility clearance has not been confirmed.

Shop Tip

Review the marked perimeter from every primary viewing point before cutting — typically the main approach to the house, the street or driveway view, and the principal window or patio view. A curve that looks correct from one direction often reads as uneven or awkward from another, and this is the most common cause of the curve-quality corrections diagnosed later in this chapter.

Layout, Clearance, and Stop-Work Conditions

Mark the bed perimeter using the approved bed shape and plant layout before any cutting begins. Verify clearance from foundations, utilities, drains, paths, doors, and equipment at every point along the perimeter, and adjust curves to avoid narrow mowing strips and inaccessible pockets. Confirm utility clearance again at each specific stake or fastener location before driving any stake or fastener for the edging.

Measurement Cue — Review Curves From Every Primary Viewing Point

Mark the perimeter, then walk to each primary viewing point identified for the property and review the curve from that position before cutting. A curve reviewed from only one direction can appear uneven, pinched, or awkward from another common viewing angle.

Critical Safety Cue — Confirm Utility Clearance Before Cutting or Staking

Do not cut the bed edge, drive a stake, or install a fastener at any point whose utility clearance has not been confirmed under the current, valid utility-locating markings. Contact with an unmarked utility line can cause serious injury, fire, explosion, or service disruption.

Field Safety Cue — Powered Edger and Cutting-Tool Handling

Inspect the powered edger before use, keep hands and feet clear of the blade, and maintain firm footing on sloped or uneven ground while cutting. Wear eye protection during operation and stop work if the blade strikes buried debris, root material, or an unmarked object.

Code Alert — Property Line and Setback Verification

Confirm the bed perimeter does not encroach on a verified property line, easement, or setback established in Chapter 1. These boundaries vary by jurisdiction and property and must be verified rather than assumed from the plan alone.

E. Plan Review and Perimeter Layout

  1. Review the approved bed shape, plant layout, mature plant size, drainage, edging, maintenance access, and lawn relationship. Confirm the current Chapter 1 readiness decision and Chapter 3 through 5 results remain valid for the area.
  2. Mark the bed perimeter with paint, rope, hose, or stakes. Follow the approved bed shape and plant layout exactly.
  3. Inspect curves from primary viewing points. Walk to each fixed viewing location identified for the property and review the marked line before cutting.
  4. Verify clearance from foundations, utilities, drains, paths, doors, and equipment. Measure and record the clearance distance at each point of concern.
  5. Adjust curves to avoid narrow mowing strips and inaccessible pockets. Re-mark and re-review any adjusted section from the same primary viewing points.

F. Edge Cutting and Soil Preparation

  1. Cut the bed edge using a spade, edging tool, or powered edger. Follow the reviewed and approved perimeter mark exactly.
  2. Maintain the approved edge depth and angle. Check depth at intervals along the perimeter and correct any section that departs from the specification.
  3. Remove sod and unwanted vegetation within the bed. Use the methods taught in Chapter 4, protecting any desirable plant marked for retention.
  4. Prepare soil using Chapters 3 through 5. Apply the sampling, amendment, and grading methods taught in those chapters within the laid-out bed.
  5. Rake the bed to the planned grade. Confirm the graded surface maintains the positive drainage established in Chapter 5.

G. Edging Installation

  1. Install metal, plastic, wood, masonry, stone, or other approved edging where specified. Use only the edging material identified in the approved plan.
  2. Place edging on stable support. Correct any soft, loose, or uneven base material before setting the edging.
  3. Fasten or stake edging clear of utilities. Confirm utility clearance at each specific fastener or stake location before driving it.
  4. Maintain smooth curves and straight runs. Sight along installed sections and correct any visible deviation before proceeding to the next section.

H. Final Placement, Inspection, and Documentation

  1. Keep edging at the specified relationship to turf and mulch. Confirm the installed height against the approved plan using a level or straightedge.
  2. Avoid sharp exposed edges and trip hazards. Inspect every joint, corner, and cut end for a hazard before continuing.
  3. Blend ends into hardscape or structures cleanly. Finish transitions so no edging end remains exposed or unsecured.
  4. Inspect bed width, curve quality, drainage, soil condition, edge security, and mowing clearance. Compare the completed bed against the Acceptance Standards table.
  5. Correct loose, high, low, or jagged edging. Reset, refasten, or replace any section that fails inspection, then reinspect the corrected section.
  6. Document the completed bed. Record final dimensions, edging material and quantities, clearance verification, and inspection results on the Landscape Readiness Worksheet.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed bed using the same checks regardless of the bed's size, shape, or edging material.

  • Perimeter check: confirm the cut edge follows the approved bed shape and every clearance distance is verified and recorded.
  • Curve-quality check: confirm curves were reviewed and approved from every primary viewing point.
  • Edge-depth check: confirm uniform depth and angle along the full perimeter.
  • Soil-condition check: confirm soil within the bed meets the standards established in Chapters 3 through 5.
  • Edging-security check: confirm edging is set on stable support and fastened or staked clear of utilities, with no loose, high, low, or jagged sections.
  • Mowing-clearance check: confirm sufficient space is maintained between edging and adjacent turf for mower and trimmer access.

Complete the Inspection Checklist in the section below before beginning Chapter 7.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Curve Reads Uneven From an Unreviewed Viewing Point

Probable cause: the perimeter was reviewed from only one viewing point before cutting, missing how the curve appears from another common approach or window.
Confirmation: walk to each primary viewing point after cutting and compare the curve against the approved bed shape.
Correction: re-mark and recut the affected section to match the approved shape, reviewing from all primary viewing points before the final cut.
Reinspection: repeat the curve-quality check from every primary viewing point.

Edging Loosens or Shifts After Installation

Probable cause: the edging was set on a soft, loose, or uneven base, or fasteners or stakes were not seated fully at installation.
Confirmation: inspect the base beneath the affected section and check fastener or stake seating against the specified installation method.
Correction: remove the affected section, stabilize the base, and reinstall the edging with fasteners or stakes fully seated.
Reinspection: repeat the edging-security check on the corrected section.

Bed Edge Holds Water or Shows a Reversed Slope

Probable cause: soil preparation within the bed did not maintain the positive drainage established in Chapter 5, or grading was disturbed during edge cutting or edging installation.
Confirmation: conduct a controlled water observation where permitted, or check the graded surface with a level or straightedge against the Chapter 5 drainage record.
Correction: regrade the affected area following Chapter 5 methods before reinstalling or adjusting edging.
Reinspection: repeat the soil-condition check and the drainage portion of the bed-definition inspection.

Mowing Strip Is Too Narrow for Safe Equipment Access

Probable cause: the marked curve was cut without confirming mower and trimmer clearance against the adjacent turf area, or a tight curve created an inaccessible pocket.
Confirmation: measure the mowing strip width at the narrowest point and compare it against the maintenance-access plan.
Correction: adjust the affected curve to restore the specified mowing clearance, recutting and reinstalling edging as required.
Reinspection: repeat the mowing-clearance check on the corrected section.

A Fastener or Stake Was Driven Without Confirmed Utility Clearance

Probable cause: utility clearance was verified for the general perimeter but not rechecked at the specific fastener or stake location before driving.
Confirmation: compare the fastener or stake location against the current utility-locating markings and the recorded clearance verification.
Correction: stop work at the affected location, confirm clearance before proceeding, and remove and relocate the fastener or stake if clearance cannot be confirmed.
Reinspection: repeat the clearance-verification check at every fastener or stake location before continuing installation.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a completed, inspected bed ready for Chapter 7's fabric and mulch work, not left with loose material or unresolved deficiencies.

  • Remove excess cut sod, soil, and vegetation debris from the bed and adjacent turf
  • Return unused edging material, fasteners, and stakes to staging for inventory
  • Sweep or rake loose soil from adjacent hardscape without discharging it to a drain
  • Store the completed bed's dimensions, edging record, and inspection results with the Landscape Readiness Worksheet
  • Confirm utility-locating markings remain visible and within their valid period before Chapter 7 begins

Field Terms

Bed Perimeter
The marked line defining the outer edge of the planting bed, established from the approved bed shape and plant layout.
Primary Viewing Point
A fixed location from which the bed is regularly seen, used to review curve quality before cutting.
Edge Depth
The vertical depth of the cut bed edge, maintained uniformly along the perimeter.
Mowing Clearance
The space between the bed edge or edging and adjacent turf sufficient for mower and trimmer access.
Turf-to-Mulch Relationship
The specified height of installed edging relative to the adjacent turf surface and the finished mulch surface within the bed.
Stable Support
A base beneath edging that has been inspected and corrected so it will not settle, shift, or loosen the edging after installation.
