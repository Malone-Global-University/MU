Job Overview

This capstone chapter qualifies you to carry one residential landscape bed through the complete Book 19 sequence in a single, continuous, documented project. The finished work product is a planted, irrigated, mulched, and documented landscape bed that has passed a site, soil, grade, plant-layout, planting-depth, irrigation, mulch, drainage, cleanup, and establishment inspection.

You will obtain the approved capstone plans and schedules, confirm boundaries and utilities, perform the Chapter 1 readiness inspection, set up the Chapter 2 work area, prepare and amend soil, clear vegetation, grade for positive drainage, lay out and edge the bed, install irrigation and drainage components below grade, plant trees, shrubs, and perennials at the correct root-flare and crown depths, install fabric only where justified and mulch to the specified depth, restore disturbed areas, clean the site, complete a full inspection with a deficiency list, and hand off a closeout package.

This is the last chapter in the book. It does not introduce new technique. It requires you to sequence and integrate every technique taught in Chapters 1 through 19 into one bed, in the correct order, without omitting or reordering the controlling site-to-establishment sequence described in the book's System-Sequence Control.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • LS-115 — Integrate site inspection, soil preparation, and bed layout into one controlled sequence for a complete residential landscape bed.
  • LS-116 — Integrate drainage review, drip irrigation installation, and backflow-protected water-source connection into the same bed.
  • LS-117 — Plant shrubs, a small tree where included, and perennials at the approved root-flare and crown depths within the same bed.
  • LS-118 — Install mulch and, where justified, landscape fabric to the specified depth while protecting plant crowns and building clearances.
  • LS-119 — Restore disturbed areas, clean the site, and complete cleanup to the Chapter 19 restoration standard.
  • LS-120 — Conduct a full site, soil, grade, plant-layout, planting-depth, irrigation, mulch, drainage, and cleanup inspection, and issue a documented landscape closeout.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter is the cumulative outcome of the entire book. Chapter 1 through Chapter 19 each taught one stage of the site-to-establishment sequence: inspection and planning, work-area setup, soil testing and amendment, vegetation removal, grading, bed edging, fabric and mulch, shrub planting, tree planting, perennial and seasonal-color planting, lawn seeding, sod installation, turf repair, drip irrigation, sprinkler or hose-end irrigation, wet-area drainage, pruning, fertilizing and maintenance, and restoration and cleanup.

This chapter requires you to apply those stages, in the controlling order, to one bed. The bed is handed off at the end of this chapter as a planted, irrigated, mulched, documented landscape ready for ongoing maintenance and coordination with Book 20 outdoor structures, as described in the book's Capstone Continuity Rule.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • obtaining and confirming the approved capstone site plan, planting plan, plant schedule, soil results, grading and drainage details, irrigation design, mulch specification, permit conditions, and inspection sequence
  • completing utility locating and the Chapter 1 readiness inspection before any digging, trenching, or planting
  • preparing the work area, protecting existing plants and hardscape, and staging materials under the Chapter 2 standard
  • collecting or confirming soil-test results and applying only approved amendments
  • removing weeds, sod, roots, debris, and unwanted vegetation and grading the bed for positive drainage
  • laying out and edging the bed boundary and placing trees, shrubs, perennials, and seasonal plants for layout approval before digging
  • installing below-grade irrigation and drainage components, including backflow protection, filtration, and pressure control, before planting where the design requires it
  • digging planting holes, correcting root systems, and planting at the approved root-flare and crown depths
  • installing emitters or dripline, pressurizing and testing the irrigation system, and correcting leaks or uneven distribution
  • installing fabric only where justified and applying mulch to the specified depth while protecting crowns, trunks, and building clearances
  • restoring disturbed lawn and soil, cleaning the site, and completing a full inspection, deficiency list, and documented closeout

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin any digging, trenching, staking, or planting before utility locating is complete and confirmed, and before the Chapter 1 readiness inspection has issued an approved or conditional decision for the capstone area
  • install below-grade irrigation, drainage, or planting work in an area with unidentified utilities, unstable slopes, contaminated soil, buried tanks, sewage, major erosion, foundation conflicts, groundwater, hazardous materials, or an unknown drainage outlet
  • connect irrigation to a potable water source without the required approved backflow device, or make final design decisions where an arborist, landscape architect, irrigation contractor, backflow tester, or drainage contractor is required by conditions found
  • substitute a design, plant species, spacing, irrigation component, or mulch depth not supplied by the approved capstone plans, product labels, or nursery instructions

Any deficiency found during the Chapter 20 inspection must be corrected, or referred to the required qualified professional, and reinspected before the landscape closeout is issued.

Trade Note

This chapter does not relax any control taught in Chapters 1 through 19. Every root-flare depth, backflow requirement, mulch clearance, and stop-work condition from those chapters remains fully in force here. The capstone is graded on correct sequencing and integration, not on completing the work faster by skipping a stage.

Boundaries, Utilities, and Stop-Work Conditions

Confirm property limits, utilities, foundation clearances, mature plant sizes, and lawful drainage before any capstone work begins. Complete utility locating before digging, trenching, auguring, driving stakes, installing edging, or planting. Stop work for unidentified utilities, unstable slopes, contaminated soil, buried tanks, sewage, major erosion, foundation conflicts, groundwater, hazardous materials, or unknown drainage outlets, consistent with the book's universal stop-work conditions.

Critical Safety Cue — Backflow Protection Is Not Optional

Do not connect irrigation directly to potable water without the required approved backflow device. Do not create cross-connections, submerged outlets, or contaminated water paths at any point in the capstone irrigation installation.

Measurement Cue — Root Flare Controls Planting Depth

Set every shrub and tree so the root flare sits at or slightly above the approved finished grade. Do not assume the top of the nursery container is the correct planting depth. Locate the true root flare before setting final grade for each plant.

Code Alert — Permit, Backflow, and Water-Use Requirements

Confirm adopted zoning, stormwater, irrigation, backflow, and water-use requirements for the jurisdiction before pressurizing the capstone irrigation system. These requirements vary by location and must be verified rather than assumed.

Field Safety Cue — Multi-Day Site Discipline

Re-verify utility markings, plant protection, and staging at the start of each work session on a multi-day capstone. Watch footing near open planting holes, trenches, and staged materials, and maintain hydration, shade, and weather monitoring throughout.

E. Plans, Readiness, and Work Area

  1. Obtain the approved capstone site plan, planting plan, plant schedule, soil results, grading and drainage details, irrigation design, mulch specification, permit conditions, and inspection sequence. Confirm each document is current before relying on it.
  2. Confirm property limits, utilities, foundation clearances, mature plant sizes, and lawful drainage. Compare the capstone plans against observed site conditions.
  3. Complete utility locating. Do not proceed to digging, trenching, driving stakes, installing edging, or planting until markings are placed and confirmed valid.
  4. Perform the Chapter 1 readiness inspection. Issue an approved or conditional decision for the capstone area before continuing; correct and reinspect any deficiency before proceeding.
  5. Prepare the Chapter 2 work area, plant protection, equipment routes, chemical controls, hydration, and material staging. Protect existing hardscape, structures, and any retained plants.
  6. Inventory plants, soil, amendments, edging, tubing, emitters, backflow components, mulch, and protection materials. Confirm quantities against the approved plant schedule and irrigation design before starting soil work.

F. Soil, Vegetation Removal, and Grading

  1. Collect and interpret soil samples where current results are not available. Follow the Chapter 3 sampling and interpretation sequence.
  2. Apply only approved amendments. Calculate and spread amendments strictly from the soil test and capstone design; do not amend by assumption.
  3. Remove weeds, sod, roots, debris, and unwanted vegetation. Protect any retained plants and utilities during removal.
  4. Shape the bed for positive drainage. Establish the approved fall away from structures and eliminate low spots that would hold water.
  5. Protect siding, weeps, vents, thresholds, foundations, and tree root flares. Mark no-fill zones before placing any additional soil.

G. Layout, Edging, and Approval

  1. Lay out the bed boundary. Mark the perimeter and verify curves and clearances from multiple viewing points.
  2. Cut and install edging. Follow the Chapter 6 edge-depth, fastening, and clearance requirements.
  3. Lay out trees, shrubs, perennials, and seasonal plants in containers. Place every plant at its plan location before any digging begins.
  4. Verify mature spacing, utilities, structure clearance, sun, and irrigation zones. Confirm every plant against its mature size and the irrigation design.
  5. Obtain layout approval before digging. Do not dig any planting hole until layout approval is confirmed and recorded.

H. Irrigation and Drainage Rough-In

  1. Install drainage components before planting where specified. Follow the Chapter 16 trench, slope, and outlet requirements for any drainage work the design includes.
  2. Install irrigation mainline, laterals, backflow, filter, regulator, and shutoff as assigned. Install components in the sequence specified by the Chapter 14 procedure.
  3. Flush tubing before final emitter installation. Confirm the line is clear before installing dripline or individual emitters.

I. Planting

  1. Dig planting holes to approved width and root-flare depth. Keep excavated soil separate from mulch and debris.
  2. Inspect and correct root systems. Remove circling, kinked, girdling, dead, or compacted roots using the approved method for each plant type.
  3. Plant shrubs at the approved root-flare elevation. Follow the Chapter 8 backfill and watering-basin sequence.
  4. Plant the approved small tree where included. Follow the Chapter 9 root-flare, orientation, and backfill sequence.
  5. Stake only if justified and record removal timing. Use flexible ties and prevent bark damage where staking is used.
  6. Plant perennials and seasonal color at correct crown depth. Follow the Chapter 10 spacing and crown-depth sequence.
  7. Backfill, firm lightly, and water each plant. Eliminate large air pockets without overcompaction.

J. Irrigation Finish, Fabric, Mulch, and Establishment

  1. Install emitters or dripline according to plant water needs. Keep emitters clear of trunks and crowns.
  2. Pressurize and inspect the irrigation system. Check every fitting and emitter for leakage.
  3. Correct leaks and uneven distribution. Adjust emitter placement or zoning and retest as needed.
  4. Install landscape fabric only in areas where the approved design justifies it. Follow the Chapter 7 seam, fastening, and clearance requirements.
  5. Apply mulch to the specified depth. Calculate quantity from area and specified depth before spreading.
  6. Keep mulch away from trunks, stems, crowns, siding, vents, and weeps. Avoid mulch volcanoes.
  7. Program the establishment watering schedule. Base the schedule on soil, plant, season, and local restrictions.

K. Restoration, Final Inspection, and Closeout

  1. Restore disturbed lawn and soil. Follow the Chapter 11 or 12 method matching the disturbed area.
  2. Clean hardscape, drains, edging, windows, siding, and work areas. Follow the Chapter 19 restoration and cleanup standard.
  3. Inspect plant depth, root flare, spacing, stability, mulch, edging, soil, drainage, irrigation, overspray, backflow, and building clearances. Use the full inspection checklist in Section K below.
  4. Conduct a controlled irrigation and drainage observation. Run the system and observe distribution, ponding, and outlet performance.
  5. Prepare a final deficiency list. List every condition that must be corrected or referred before closeout.
  6. Correct and reinspect every item. Do not carry an unresolved deficiency into the closeout decision.
  7. Compile soil tests, amendments, plant tags, nursery records, layout, irrigation components, backflow information, watering schedule, photographs, inspections, and corrections. Assemble the complete closeout package.
  8. Provide establishment, watering, pruning, fertilizer, mulch, weed, irrigation, and seasonal-maintenance instructions. Write instructions specific to the species and irrigation zone installed.
  9. Hand off the completed landscape bed for ongoing maintenance and Book 20 outdoor-structure coordination. Record the handoff date and recipient.

Common Inspection Review

Review the completed capstone bed using the same checks regardless of bed size or plant schedule.

  • Sequence check: confirm utility locating, the Chapter 1 readiness decision, layout approval, and below-grade irrigation and drainage all preceded the work that depended on them.
  • Planting-depth check: confirm every shrub, tree, and perennial is at the approved root-flare or crown-depth elevation.
  • Irrigation check: confirm backflow protection, pressure control, leak-free fittings, and verified distribution.
  • Mulch and fabric check: confirm specified mulch depth, protected crowns and clearances, and fabric present only where justified.
  • Drainage check: confirm positive drainage with no ponding, reverse slope, or blocked outlet.
  • Closeout check: confirm every deficiency is corrected, referred, or documented, and the closeout decision is signed and dated with a complete record package.

Complete the Final Inspection Checklist in the section below before issuing the landscape closeout.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Planting Began Before Layout Approval Was Recorded

Probable cause: a plant was dug in and backfilled before the layout-approval step was confirmed and photographed.
Confirmation: compare the planting-hole locations against the approved plant schedule and layout photograph.
Correction: if the plant is out of position, relocate it following the correct root-flare planting sequence; if in position, document the approval retroactively only if spacing and clearance are verified correct.
Reinspection: repeat the sequence check and the planting-depth check before continuing.

Irrigation Was Pressurized Before Backflow Protection Was Installed

Probable cause: the installation sequence in Section H was not followed in order, or a component was installed out of the specified sequence.
Confirmation: inspect the water-source connection for the presence and correct order of backflow device, filter, regulator, and shutoff.
Correction: depressurize the system immediately, install the missing or incorrectly sequenced component, and do not repressurize until the sequence is correct.
Reinspection: repeat the irrigation check before continuing to Section J.

Mulch Was Applied Before Irrigation Rough-In Was Complete

Probable cause: Section J was started before Section H's below-grade irrigation and drainage components were fully installed and flushed.
Confirmation: check the irrigation design against the installed components and inspect for tubing that would require disturbing finished mulch to access.
Correction: pull back mulch as needed, complete the remaining irrigation or drainage installation, then reapply mulch to the specified depth.
Reinspection: repeat the mulch and fabric check and the irrigation check.

A Root Flare Was Buried During Backfill

Probable cause: the planting hole depth was set from the container height rather than the located root flare.
Confirmation: probe at the base of the trunk or main stem to locate the actual root flare relative to the surrounding grade.
Correction: excavate around the base to expose the root flare to the approved finished-grade elevation, or reset the plant if the flare is buried more than the specified tolerance.
Reinspection: repeat the planting-depth check for the corrected plant and any plant installed using the same method.

The Closeout Package Is Missing an Earlier-Chapter Record

Probable cause: a soil test, work-area inspection, or maintenance record from an earlier chapter was not carried forward into the capstone closeout folder.
Confirmation: compare the compiled closeout package against the Record and Reference Takeoff in Section C and identify the missing item.
Correction: locate the original record or, if unavailable, document the gap and perform the minimum reinspection needed to confirm the underlying condition is still acceptable.
Reinspection: repeat the closeout check before issuing the final decision.

Cleanup

  • Store the completed closeout package where ongoing maintenance and Book 20 coordination can reference it
