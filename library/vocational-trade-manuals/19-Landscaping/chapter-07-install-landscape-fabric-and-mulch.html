Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to decide whether landscape fabric is appropriate for a specific planting bed, install it correctly where justified, and apply a uniform mulch layer over the bed while protecting plant crowns, trunks, stems, soil health, drainage, and building clearances. The finished work product is one bed with fabric installed only where the design justifies it, and mulch applied at the specified depth across the full bed area.

You will review the bed's plant type, weed pressure, soil-amendment needs, and future maintenance requirements; decide whether fabric belongs in this bed at all; install fabric with correct orientation, overlap, and fastening where it is used; cut controlled openings for existing or planned plants; install drip tubing according to the approved irrigation detail; calculate and place mulch at the correct depth; and inspect the finished bed for fabric exposure, mulch depth, plant clearances, and drainage.

This chapter builds directly on the bed shaping in Chapter 5 and the edging and soil preparation in Chapter 6. It also coordinates with the drip-irrigation installation taught in Chapter 14 where the bed design calls for below-grade or above-fabric tubing. The fabric and mulch installed here become the surface every planting chapter from Chapter 8 through Chapter 10 must work around without disturbing.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • LS-037 — Select fabric where appropriate, based on plant type, weed pressure, soil-amendment needs, and future maintenance.
  • LS-038 — Install fabric around existing or planned plants without wrapping trunks or stems.
  • LS-039 — Overlap fabric seams by the approved amount and in the drainage-compatible direction.
  • LS-040 — Cut planting openings that fit existing or planned plants without excess exposed fabric.
  • LS-041 — Apply mulch at the proper depth without burying crowns, trunks, or building components.
  • LS-042 — Inspect mulch coverage, fabric exposure, seams, fasteners, and clearances.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter depends on the cleared and graded bed from Chapters 4 and 5, the prepared soil from Chapter 3, and the edged bed boundary from Chapter 6. It also depends on the sun, shade, drainage, and irrigation findings recorded on the Landscape Readiness Worksheet in Chapter 1.

The fabric-and-mulch decision made here directly constrains every planting chapter that follows. A bed with fabric installed where it should not have been used will restrict the perennial division and groundcover spread taught in Chapter 10, and will interfere with the tree-root access needed by Chapter 9. The mulch depth and clearance standards set here also carry into the fertilizing and mulch-maintenance work in Chapter 18 and the mulch-refresh work in the Chapter 19 restoration.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing bed use, plant type, weed pressure, soil-amendment needs, fabric product, mulch, irrigation, and future maintenance before selecting fabric
  • deciding whether fabric is appropriate for the specific bed, and declining to use fabric where it is not justified
  • confirming weed removal, soil preparation, grading, and irrigation trenching are complete before fabric or mulch is placed
  • installing fabric with correct orientation, seam overlap, and fastening
  • cutting controlled openings for existing or planned plants without wrapping trunks or stems
  • installing drip tubing above or below fabric according to the approved irrigation detail
  • calculating and placing mulch at the specified depth without burying crowns, trunks, or building components
  • inspecting fabric exposure, seams, fasteners, mulch depth, plant clearances, and drainage

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • install fabric in a bed requiring frequent division, soil amendment, groundcover spread, tree-root access, or natural reseeding unless the approved bed design specifies fabric for that bed
  • begin fabric or mulch work before weed removal, soil preparation, grading, and any required irrigation trenching are confirmed complete
  • wrap fabric tightly around trunks or stems, or place fasteners over utilities or irrigation tubing
  • place mulch against trunks, stems, crowns, siding, foundations, vents, or masonry weeps, or build a mulch volcano at any plant
  • bury the root flare of an existing or newly planted woody plant under fabric or mulch

Any bed where weed removal, soil preparation, grading, or irrigation trenching has not been confirmed complete must be referred back to the applicable earlier chapter before this chapter's work begins.

Trade Note

Fabric is a design decision, not a default. A bed planted with perennials that will be divided, groundcovers meant to spread, or trees whose roots need long-term access is a bed where fabric works against the planting plan rather than for it. Decide fabric use bed by bed, not for the whole property at once.

Setup Standard

Do not place fabric or mulch until weed removal, soil preparation, grading, and any required irrigation trenching are confirmed complete on the Landscape Readiness Worksheet or bed-preparation record. Do not install drip tubing after fabric or mulch placement unless the approved irrigation detail specifies above-fabric or above-mulch installation.

Shop Tip

Dry-lay a full fabric roll across the bed and mark plant locations with flags before making any cuts. Cutting openings from memory after the fabric is fastened is the most common cause of oversized or misplaced openings diagnosed later in this chapter.

Fabric Selection and Preparation Confirmation

Review bed use, plant type, weed pressure, soil-amendment needs, fabric product, mulch, irrigation, and future maintenance before deciding whether fabric belongs in this bed. Do not use fabric in beds requiring frequent division, soil amendment, groundcover spread, tree-root access, or natural reseeding unless the approved design specifies otherwise. Confirm weed removal, soil preparation, grading, and irrigation trenching are complete before placing fabric or mulch.

Measurement Cue — Verify Seam Overlap at Installation

Measure seam overlap with a tape measure at the time of installation rather than estimating by eye. Confirm the overlap direction sheds water toward the bed rather than trapping it at the seam.

Critical Safety Cue — Never Wrap Fabric Around Trunks or Stems

Do not wrap fabric tightly around a trunk or stem, and do not leave a planting opening undersized. A tightly wrapped opening will girdle the plant as it grows, cutting off water and nutrient flow and eventually killing the plant.

Field Safety Cue — Watch Footing While Moving Mulch

Watch footing on uneven or wet ground while transporting mulch with a wheelbarrow or cart. Use proper lifting technique when loading and unloading mulch, and take a rest and hydration break during hot-weather work.

Code Alert — Confirm Drainage and Setback Requirements Remain Met

Confirm that fabric and mulch placement does not alter the accepted drainage grade or violate any adopted setback, stormwater, or water-use requirement identified in the Chapter 1 readiness inspection.

E. Preparation — Review, Decision, and Bed Smoothing

  1. Review bed use, plant type, weed pressure, soil-amendment needs, fabric product, mulch, irrigation, and future maintenance. Compare each factor against the approved bed design before proceeding.
  2. Decide whether fabric is appropriate for the specific bed. Record the decision and its justification on the bed-preparation record.
  3. Do not use fabric in beds requiring frequent division, soil amendment, groundcover spread, tree-root access, or natural reseeding unless specified. Confirm this determination against the plant schedule for the bed.
  4. Confirm weed removal, soil preparation, grading, and irrigation trenching are complete. Verify each item against the Chapter 4, 5, and 6 records before continuing.
  5. Rake the bed smooth. Remove any remaining stones, roots, or debris exposed by raking.

F. Fabric Installation and Plant Openings

  1. Roll fabric with the specified side orientation. Confirm the manufacturer's stated face-up or face-down orientation before unrolling.
  2. Fit fabric to curves and fixed edges without tension or wrinkles. Work slowly along the bed edge to avoid stretching the material.
  3. Overlap seams by the approved amount and in the drainage-compatible direction. Measure the overlap with a tape measure at each seam.
  4. Secure fabric with the specified staples or pins. Place fasteners at the manufacturer's specified spacing along seams and edges.
  5. Keep fasteners clear of utilities and irrigation tubing. Confirm marked utility and tubing locations before driving each fastener.
  6. Cut X-shaped or controlled openings for existing or planned plants. Size each opening to the plant's current or planned stem or trunk diameter.
  7. Do not wrap fabric tightly around trunks or stems. Leave clearance sufficient for the plant's expected growth before the next scheduled maintenance cycle.
  8. Keep fabric clear of drains, weeps, foundation clearances, and tree root flares. Trim fabric back from each of these features rather than tucking it underneath.

G. Irrigation Sequencing and Mulch Application

  1. Install drip tubing above or below fabric according to the approved irrigation detail. Confirm the specified sequence before placing tubing; do not default to one sequence without checking the detail.
  2. Calculate mulch quantity from area and specified depth. Use the bed's measured area from Chapter 1 and the specified depth from the approved design.
  3. Place mulch evenly without dragging or tearing fabric. Distribute mulch by hand or rake rather than dumping and spreading with equipment that could tear the fabric.
  4. Keep mulch away from trunks, stems, crowns, siding, vents, and masonry weeps. Maintain the specified clearance at every plant and building feature.
  5. Avoid mulch volcanoes. Taper mulch depth down near each trunk or stem rather than mounding it against the plant.
  6. Maintain the specified depth without burying plant crowns. Check depth at representative points across the bed as work proceeds.
  7. Rake mulch to a uniform finish. Smooth the surface and correct any uneven or thin areas.

H. Final Inspection and Documentation

  1. Inspect fabric exposure, seams, fasteners, mulch depth, plant clearances, and drainage. Check each item against the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Document product, area, depth, and maintenance needs. Record the fabric decision, product names, bed area, finished mulch depth, and any follow-up maintenance requirement.

Common Inspection Review

Review every fabric-and-mulch installation using the same checks regardless of the bed's size or plant type.

  • Fabric justification check: confirm fabric is present only where the bed design identifies it as appropriate.
  • Seam and fastener check: confirm every seam meets the specified overlap and every fastener is seated and clear of utilities.
  • Plant opening check: confirm every opening fits the plant without tight wrapping.
  • Mulch depth check: confirm depth matches the specification at representative points across the full bed.
  • Clearance check: confirm mulch and fabric are clear of trunks, stems, crowns, siding, vents, and masonry weeps.
  • Drainage check: confirm the accepted grade from Chapter 5 has not been altered.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Fabric Was Installed in a Bed That Needed Root Access or Division

Probable cause: the fabric decision was made without checking the plant schedule for perennials requiring division, groundcovers meant to spread, or trees needing long-term root access.
Confirmation: compare the installed fabric location against the plant schedule and the Chapter 1 readiness plan.
Correction: remove fabric from the affected area and restore the bed to an unfabricated planting surface before proceeding.
Reinspection: repeat the fabric justification check on the corrected area.

A Seam Failed to Shed Water and Trapped Moisture Beneath the Fabric

Probable cause: the seam overlap was installed in the wrong direction relative to the bed's slope, or the overlap was narrower than specified.
Confirmation: lift the fabric at the affected seam and check the overlap direction and width against the specification.
Correction: reposition the seam with the correct overlap direction and width, and refasten.
Reinspection: repeat the seam and fastener check across the corrected section.

A Plant Opening Was Cut Too Small and Is Girdling the Stem

Probable cause: the opening was cut to the plant's current diameter without allowing for growth before the next scheduled maintenance cycle.
Confirmation: inspect the stem or trunk at the fabric opening for compression marks or restricted growth.
Correction: enlarge the opening immediately to the required clearance.
Reinspection: repeat the plant opening check at every affected location in the bed.

Mulch Depth Is Uneven or Exceeds the Specification

Probable cause: mulch was dumped in piles and roughly spread rather than measured and raked to a uniform finish.
Confirmation: measure depth at multiple representative points across the bed and compare to the specified depth.
Correction: remove excess mulch from over-depth areas and add mulch to under-depth areas until the full bed matches the specification.
Reinspection: repeat the mulch depth check across the corrected bed.

A Mulch Volcano Formed Against a Trunk

Probable cause: mulch was mounded against the trunk for a uniform appearance rather than tapered to maintain crown clearance.
Confirmation: inspect the base of the affected plant for mulch contact with the trunk or bark.
Correction: pull mulch back from the trunk to the specified clearance and taper the remaining mulch outward.
Reinspection: repeat the crown and building clearance check at every plant in the bed.

Drip Tubing Was Installed in the Wrong Sequence Relative to Fabric

Probable cause: tubing was installed by default habit rather than checking the approved irrigation detail for this specific bed.
Confirmation: compare the installed tubing position against the approved irrigation detail.
Correction: remove the affected fabric or mulch section, reposition the tubing to the specified sequence, and reinstall.
Reinspection: repeat the irrigation sequencing check on the corrected section before final mulch placement.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, inspected bed surface ready for planting, not an installation left for later verification.

  • Remove excess fabric scraps, staple packaging, and mulch bag or bulk-delivery debris from the bed and surrounding hardscape
  • Sweep loose mulch fragments from adjacent walkways, patios, and lawn areas
  • Store unused fabric, staples, and mulch under cover and away from moisture for use in later beds
  • Confirm marking flags for utilities, tubing, and plant locations remain in place where later chapters still reference them
  • Store the completed bed-preparation record with the fabric decision, mulch depth, and photographs where Chapters 8 through 10 can reference them
