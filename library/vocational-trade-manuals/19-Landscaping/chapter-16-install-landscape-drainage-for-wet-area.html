Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install one approved landscape-drainage trench or collection system for a wet residential area. The finished work product is a supported, accessible drainage system that captures water at the identified collection point, carries it along a continuous fall to a lawful outlet, protects that outlet from erosion and blockage, provides an emergency overflow route, and passes a controlled flow test.

You will confirm the wet-area cause is suitable for a landscape drain, verify property and discharge requirements, locate utilities, lay out the drainage path and invert elevations, excavate a stable trench, install separation fabric and aggregate, install pipe and fittings at the correct slope, connect to the lawful outlet, protect that outlet, establish an overflow path, backfill without creating settlement, and inspect and test the finished system.

This project depends directly on the Chapter 1 readiness inspection and the drainage findings recorded there. It follows the System-Sequence Control rule in the book outline: positive drainage is established before edging, fabric, mulch, planting, turf, or irrigation conceal grading defects, and any below-grade drainage work required by the design is installed before final mulch or turf.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • LS-091 — Identify water-collection points from the drainage plan and site observation.
  • LS-092 — Lay out the drainage path, including invert elevations and required fall.
  • LS-093 — Install a gravel trench with separation fabric and a stable trench bottom.
  • LS-094 — Install perforated drain pipe where appropriate, with correct orientation and fittings.
  • LS-095 — Route discharge safely to a lawful outlet with erosion protection and overflow provision.
  • LS-096 — Inspect drainage performance through a controlled flow test and documented findings.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter uses the wet-area finding recorded in the Chapter 1 Landscape Readiness Worksheet and the grading work performed in Chapter 5. It applies before Chapter 7 fabric and mulch conceal grade, before Chapter 8 through Chapter 10 planting occupies the bed, and before Chapter 11 through Chapter 13 turf work is placed over the drained area. The completed drainage record from this chapter feeds directly into Chapter 19 restoration, where drainage function is reverified, and into the Chapter 20 capstone, where drainage installed under this chapter's method may be part of the final integrated project.

A landscape drain installed under this chapter is not a substitute for a plumbing, foundation, sewer, or structural repair. Where the wet-area cause is one of those conditions, this chapter requires referral rather than installation.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • confirming that a landscape drain is the correct response to the identified wet-area cause
  • verifying property, easement, and lawful-discharge requirements before excavation
  • locating utilities before trenching
  • laying out the inlet, trench, pipe, outlet, and overflow route with invert elevations and required fall
  • excavating a stable trench without undermining structures, hardscape, roots, or utilities
  • installing separation fabric, gravel bedding, perforated or solid pipe, fittings, and cleanouts
  • connecting to a lawful outlet and protecting that outlet from erosion, pests, blockage, and freezing
  • establishing an emergency overflow route
  • backfilling and restoring grade without creating settlement
  • performing a controlled flow test and inspecting collection, pipe flow, leaks, ponding, outlet, erosion, and overflow

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • excavate before utility locating is complete and confirmed
  • install a landscape drain where the wet-area cause is a plumbing, foundation, sewer, or structural defect; refer these conditions to the required qualified professional
  • discharge water toward foundations, entrances, neighboring property, sidewalks, roads, public hazards, or any prohibited discharge point
  • connect a landscape drain to a sanitary sewer, septic system, or any cross-connection with potable water
  • conceal an unresolved drainage failure with mulch, soil, or turf rather than correcting it

Any wet area that is not suitable for a landscape drain, or any discharge point that is not confirmed lawful, must be referred to the required qualified professional and resolved before this chapter's work proceeds.

Trade Note

A landscape drain only works if water can enter it, flow through it on a continuous fall, and leave it at a point where discharge is lawful and will not create a new problem downstream. Every step in this chapter serves one of those three requirements. A drain that fails any one of them is not complete, regardless of how well the trench and pipe were installed.

Setup Standard

Do not excavate any part of the trench path until utility locating is complete and confirmed valid. Do not connect to any outlet until the outlet has been confirmed lawful under the approved drainage plan and applicable local discharge requirements.

Shop Tip

Set stakes at the inlet and outlet first and stretch a string line between them before digging. Marking the intended fall on the string line with a line level before excavation begins makes it far easier to dig a trench bottom that already follows the required slope, rather than digging first and correcting elevation errors afterward.

Diagnosis, Utilities, and Stop-Work Conditions

Confirm the wet-area cause is suitable for a landscape drain rather than a plumbing, foundation, sewer, or structural repair before any excavation begins. Verify property, easement, and discharge requirements for the intended outlet. Locate utilities before marking the trench path. Do not excavate, install pipe, or connect to an outlet until these three conditions are confirmed.

Measurement Cue — Verify Invert Elevations Along the Full Run

Check invert elevation at the inlet, at each fitting, and at the outlet, not only at the two ends of the trench. A trench that appears to fall correctly end to end can still contain a reverse-sloped or flat section that will collect sediment and block flow.

Critical Safety Cue — Confirm Utilities and Trench Stability Before Entering

Do not excavate any part of the trench path until utility locating is complete and confirmed valid. Do not enter a trench with unstable walls, standing water, or signs of collapse. Keep excavated soil and materials back from the trench edge to reduce wall loading, and stop work if groundwater, an unidentified utility, or an unstable wall is encountered.

Field Safety Cue — Wet Soil and Slippery Footing

Wet-area excavation often involves saturated or slippery soil. Watch footing at the trench edge and inside the trench, use stable footing when lifting pipe or aggregate, and stop work during heavy rain or lightning.

Code Alert — Lawful Discharge Point

Confirm the intended outlet is an approved discharge point under the applicable local stormwater or drainage requirements before connecting the system. Discharge to a neighboring property, public right-of-way, or a prohibited point is not permitted regardless of how effectively the drain performs.

E. Preparation — Diagnosis, Verification, and Layout

  1. Review the drainage plan, water source, collection point, soil, slope, pipe, aggregate, fabric, outlet, cleanout, and overflow. Confirm the plan is current and matches the wet-area condition observed on site.
  2. Confirm the wet-area cause is suitable for a landscape drain rather than a plumbing, foundation, sewer, or structural repair. Refer any condition outside this scope to the required qualified professional before continuing.
  3. Verify property, easement, and discharge requirements. Confirm the intended outlet is an approved discharge point.
  4. Locate utilities. Do not mark the trench path with stakes driven below the surface, or begin excavation, until utility locating is complete and confirmed valid.
  5. Mark the inlet, trench, pipe, outlet, and overflow route. Use stakes and a string line to define the full path before digging.
  6. Establish invert elevations and required fall. Record the target elevation at the inlet, at each fitting location, and at the outlet using the line level or laser level.

F. Excavation and Trench Preparation

  1. Excavate the trench without undermining structures, hardscape, roots, or utilities. Keep excavated soil and materials clear of the trench edge.
  2. Shape a stable trench bottom. Remove soft spots, organic pockets, and loose debris that would allow settlement under the pipe.
  3. Install separation fabric where specified. Fit the fabric to the trench dimensions with the overlap stated in the approved plan.
  4. Place the initial gravel bed. Spread the bedding layer to the depth specified in the approved plan before setting pipe.

G. Pipe, Fittings, and Aggregate

  1. Install perforated or solid pipe in the specified orientation. Confirm perforation-down or perforation-up orientation matches the approved plan before backfilling any section.
  2. Connect fittings and cleanouts. Seat each fitting fully and position cleanouts for future access.
  3. Maintain continuous slope. Recheck invert elevation at each fitting against the Step 6 targets before proceeding to the next section.
  4. Place drainage aggregate around and above pipe. Place aggregate in the lifts specified in the approved plan without displacing the pipe.
  5. Fold or terminate fabric according to the design. Close the fabric over the aggregate as specified to maintain separation from backfill soil.
  6. Install inlet basin, surface grate, or collection stone where specified. Set the component level and connected to the pipe run at the approved elevation.

H. Outlet, Overflow, and Backfill

  1. Connect to the lawful outlet. Complete the final connection only after the outlet has been confirmed under Step 3.
  2. Protect the outlet from erosion, pests, blockage, and freezing. Install the erosion protection, screen, or other component specified in the approved plan.
  3. Establish emergency overflow. Confirm the overflow route directs excess water away from foundations, entrances, neighboring property, and any prohibited discharge point.
  4. Backfill and restore grade without creating settlement. Backfill in lifts, firming lightly, and match the surrounding grade.

I. Testing and Inspection

  1. Perform a controlled flow test. Introduce water at the inlet using the method specified in the approved plan and observe flow through the full system to the outlet.
  2. Inspect collection, pipe flow, leaks, ponding, outlet, erosion, and overflow. Confirm water enters at the inlet, flows continuously to the outlet, and discharges without leaks, unexpected ponding, or erosion.
  3. Document route, elevations, outlet, and test. Record the trench path, invert elevations, outlet location and condition, and flow-test results on the Drainage Installation Record.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed drainage installation using the same checks regardless of trench length or wet-area cause.

  • Cause check: confirm the wet-area cause was verified suitable for a landscape drain before excavation began.
  • Utility and boundary check: confirm utility locating and discharge verification were completed before excavation.
  • Fall check: confirm invert elevation decreases continuously from inlet to outlet with no flat or reverse-sloped section.
  • Outlet check: confirm the outlet is lawful, connected, and protected from erosion, pests, blockage, and freezing.
  • Overflow check: confirm the emergency overflow route does not direct water toward a prohibited point.
  • Flow-test check: confirm the controlled flow test passed with no leaks and no unexpected ponding.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Water Pools Partway Along the Trench During the Flow Test

Probable cause: a flat or reverse-sloped section of trench bottom or pipe, often caused by digging to a marked depth rather than to a verified elevation.
Confirmation: check invert elevation at the section before and after the pooling point using the line level or laser level.
Correction: excavate the affected section deeper at the low end, or raise the high end, until continuous fall is restored, then reset the pipe and aggregate.
Reinspection: repeat the fall check and the flow test for the corrected section.

Water Exits the Pipe Before Reaching the Outlet

Probable cause: a fitting was not fully seated, or a coupling was displaced during aggregate placement or backfill.
Confirmation: excavate to expose the suspected connection and inspect the fitting for full engagement and displacement.
Correction: reseat or replace the fitting according to the manufacturer's instructions and reconfirm alignment before backfilling.
Reinspection: repeat the flow test for the full run after correction.

The Outlet Discharges Onto a Neighboring Property or Prohibited Area

Probable cause: the outlet location was not verified against the approved drainage plan and applicable local discharge requirements before connection.
Confirmation: compare the as-built outlet location against the approved plan and confirm lawful-discharge status with the applicable local requirement.
Correction: disconnect the discharge, identify a confirmed lawful outlet, and reroute the pipe run to that location before resuming the flow test.
Reinspection: repeat the outlet check and the flow test after rerouting.

Backfilled Trench Settles After a Rain Event

Probable cause: backfill was placed in an oversized lift without adequate firming, or organic material was left in the trench bottom before pipe installation.
Confirmation: inspect the settled area for a depression and probe for loose or organic material beneath the surface.
Correction: remove settled backfill, correct the underlying condition, and rebackfill in the specified lifts with light firming.
Reinspection: repeat the backfill and grade check after correction and monitor through the next rain event where practical.

The Overflow Route Directs Water Toward a Foundation

Probable cause: the overflow path was assumed rather than traced on site, or a later grading change altered the path after the drainage plan was approved.
Confirmation: trace the overflow route from the inlet at capacity to its final discharge point and compare against foundation, entrance, and property-line locations.
Correction: regrade or redirect the overflow route so it discharges away from the foundation and other prohibited points.
Reinspection: repeat the overflow check and document the corrected route.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a tested, documented drainage system, not an unverified trench closed and left for later chapters to discover problems in.

  • Remove excess excavated soil, unused aggregate, pipe offcuts, and fabric scraps from the work area
  • Return borrowed plans and utility records to their source or designated storage
  • Rinse and store trenching tools, the pipe cutter, and the level equipment used for this installation
  • Organize invert-elevation records and flow-test photographs by section to match the Drainage Installation Record
  • Confirm the cleanout locations remain accessible and are not buried beyond the approved access depth

Field Terms

Inlet
The point where surface or subsurface water enters the drainage system.
Invert Elevation
The elevation of the interior bottom of the pipe at a given point along its length.
Fall
The continuous downward slope of the pipe and trench bottom from inlet to outlet.
Lawful Outlet
The confirmed, approved discharge point for the collected water.
Overflow Route
The path collected water follows if the drainage system's capacity is exceeded.
Cleanout
An accessible fitting installed to allow inspection and clearing of blockages.
Separation Fabric
Fabric placed between soil and drainage aggregate to preserve aggregate porosity.
Controlled Flow Test
A test introducing a known volume of water at the inlet to verify continuous flow, leak-free connections, and lawful discharge.

Performance Check
