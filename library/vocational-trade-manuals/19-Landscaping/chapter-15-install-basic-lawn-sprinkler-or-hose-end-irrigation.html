Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to lay out, install or position, adjust, test, and document one basic lawn-watering zone using either an approved fixed-head sprinkler system or a hose-end sprinkler layout. The finished work product is one functioning watering zone with confirmed backflow protection, marked sprinkler positions, controlled coverage, a completed catch-cup or approved coverage test, and a documented watering schedule.

You will select the approved project option, review the water source and pressure, confirm required backflow protection, measure the lawn area and identify obstacles, establish head-to-head or manufacturer-specified coverage, mark sprinkler positions, install or position the sprinklers, pressurize the zone, adjust arcs and nozzles, run a coverage test, record distribution, make final adjustments, establish a watering schedule, and inspect the finished zone for leaks, runoff, misting, and unsafe conditions.

This chapter builds directly on the Landscape Readiness Worksheet from Chapter 1 and the drip-irrigation practices from Chapter 14. It does not replace a full multi-zone irrigation design; it qualifies one basic zone using the water source, pressure, and backflow protection already confirmed as available.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • LS-085 — Determine sprinkler coverage using head-to-head or manufacturer-specified spacing for the selected option.
  • LS-086 — Lay out heads or hose-end sprinklers at marked positions that achieve the required coverage.
  • LS-087 — Reduce overspray onto siding, windows, foundations, streets, sidewalks, fences, and planting beds.
  • LS-088 — Adjust spray patterns, arcs, radii, and nozzles to match the marked coverage layout.
  • LS-089 — Test coverage using a catch-cup or approved method and record distribution results.
  • LS-090 — Document watering zones, schedule, pressure, and coverage results on the irrigation-layout worksheet.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • selecting one project option: a fixed basic sprinkler zone or a hose-end irrigation layout
  • reviewing water source, backflow, pressure, flow, head or sprinkler type, spacing, wind, slope, controller, and local watering restrictions
  • confirming required backflow protection before connecting to the water source
  • measuring the lawn area and identifying corners, curves, narrow strips, obstacles, sidewalks, buildings, and slopes
  • establishing head-to-head or manufacturer-specified coverage and marking sprinkler positions
  • installing pipe, swing joints, valves, and heads only under an approved irrigation scope, or routing hoses safely for the hose-end option
  • pressurizing the zone, adjusting arcs, radii, and nozzles, and reducing overspray
  • performing a catch-cup or approved coverage test and recording distribution
  • establishing a watering schedule and inspecting for leaks, runoff, misting, low pressure, blocked heads, and unsafe hoses

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • connect to a potable water source without the required approved backflow device confirmed in Chapter 1 or reverified in this chapter
  • trench, install pipe, or set valves for a fixed system before utility locating has been reconfirmed for the specific trench path
  • design or install a complete multi-zone irrigation system, main-line sizing, or controller wiring beyond the one basic zone assigned in this chapter
  • direct spray, runoff, or overspray onto neighboring property, public sidewalks, roads, or prohibited discharge points
  • ignore local watering restrictions, permit requirements, or backflow-testing requirements that apply to the jurisdiction

Any zone showing an unresolved backflow, pressure, or utility deficiency must be corrected, or referred to the required irrigation contractor or backflow tester, before the coverage test is finalized.

Trade Note

A basic lawn zone is only as reliable as its weakest measurement: pressure, spacing, or coverage. Recording an assumed pressure instead of a measured one, or spacing heads by eye instead of by the manufacturer's stated radius, is the single most common cause of the dry-strip and overspray failures diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not connect any sprinkler or hose layout to the water source until required backflow protection is confirmed present and functional. Do not begin trenching for the fixed option until utility locating has been reconfirmed for the specific trench path marked in this chapter.

Shop Tip

Mark every sprinkler position with a flag before installing or placing a single head. Reviewing the full marked layout from the primary viewing points catches spacing gaps and overspray risks while correction is still easy, rather than after pipe is cut or hoses are staged.

Project Option Selection

Select one approved project option before beginning layout: a fixed basic sprinkler zone using buried pipe, swing joints, valves, and heads, or a hose-end sprinkler layout using positioned hose-end sprinklers and routed hoses. Complete the full project sequence for the selected option. Do not install one of each option and do not mix fixed-system components with a hose-end layout.

Critical Safety Cue — Confirm Backflow Protection Before Any Connection

Do not connect the fixed system or a hose-end sprinkler to a potable water source until the required backflow device is confirmed present and functioning. An unprotected connection can allow contaminated water to enter the drinking-water supply.

Code Alert — Local Watering Restrictions and Permits

Confirm local watering-day restrictions, runtime limits, backflow-testing requirements, and any permit required for fixed sprinkler installation before finalizing the schedule. These requirements vary by jurisdiction and must be verified rather than assumed.

Field Safety Cue — Pressurized Lines and Trenching

Keep hands and tools clear of a pressurized line while testing for leaks. Watch footing near open trenches, wet turf, and hose runs. Locate utilities again for the specific trench path before excavating for the fixed option.

E. Preparation — Option, Water Source, and Area

  1. Select the approved project option: fixed basic zone or hose-end layout. Record the selection on the irrigation-layout worksheet before proceeding.
  2. Review water source, backflow, pressure, flow, head or sprinkler type, spacing, wind, slope, controller, and local watering restrictions. Compare against the Chapter 1 Landscape Readiness Worksheet.
  3. Confirm required backflow protection. Do not proceed to any connection until the device is confirmed present and functioning.
  4. Measure the lawn area and identify corners, curves, narrow strips, obstacles, sidewalks, buildings, and slopes. Record each dimension and feature on the worksheet.

F. Coverage Layout

  1. Establish head-to-head or manufacturer-specified coverage. Use the stated radius for the selected head, nozzle, or hose-end sprinkler.
  2. Mark sprinkler positions. Place a flag or stake at every position before installing or placing a single unit.
  3. Group sprinklers with compatible precipitation rates and pressure needs. Do not mix incompatible nozzle types within the same zone.

G. Installation — Fixed Option

  1. For fixed systems, locate utilities before trenching. Reconfirm markings for the specific trench path.
  2. Install pipe, swing joints, risers, valves, and heads only under the approved irrigation scope. Follow the specified pipe size, fitting type, and joint method.
  3. Set heads to finished grade. Verify each head sits flush with the surrounding turf surface after backfilling.

H. Installation — Hose-End Option

  1. For hose-end systems, route hoses to avoid trip, mower, door, and vehicle hazards. Keep hose runs clear of walkways and driveways where practical.
  2. Install sprinklers on stable bases. Confirm each sprinkler will not tip or shift once pressurized.

I. Pressurizing, Adjustment, and Testing

  1. Pressurize the zone. Open the water source slowly and observe the full zone for immediate leaks.
  2. Adjust arcs, radii, nozzles, and rotation. Match each unit to the marked coverage layout.
  3. Reduce overspray onto siding, windows, foundations, streets, sidewalks, fences, and planting beds. Readjust any unit spraying beyond the intended zone.
  4. Perform a catch-cup or approved coverage test. Place catch cups across the zone and run the system for the specified test duration.
  5. Record distribution and dry or wet areas. Log each catch-cup reading and mark any dry strip or overwatered area on the layout.

J. Final Adjustment, Schedule, and Inspection

  1. Adjust positions, nozzles, pressure, or runtime. Correct every dry strip or overwatered area identified in the coverage test.
  2. Establish watering zones and schedule based on soil, turf, season, slope, and restrictions. Record the runtime and frequency on the irrigation-layout worksheet.
  3. Inspect leaks, runoff, misting, low pressure, blocked heads, and unsafe hoses. Correct every condition found before closeout.
  4. Document layout, pressure, heads, coverage, and schedule. Complete the irrigation-layout worksheet and attach the coverage-test log and photographs.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed zone using the same checks regardless of the selected project option.

  • Backflow check: confirm the required device is present and was functioning before pressurizing.
  • Coverage check: confirm marked spacing was achieved across the full zone with no unmarked gaps.
  • Overspray check: confirm no spray reaches hardscape, structures, or neighboring property during the coverage test.
  • Distribution check: confirm the catch-cup or approved test was completed and recorded for the full zone.
  • Leak and hazard check: confirm no leaks, runoff, misting, low pressure, blocked heads, or unsafe hose routing remain.
  • Documentation check: confirm layout, pressure, heads or sprinklers, coverage results, and schedule are fully recorded.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Dry Strip Appears Between Two Sprinkler Positions

Probable cause: spacing exceeded head-to-head or manufacturer-specified coverage, or a nozzle radius was smaller than assumed.
Confirmation: measure the actual spacing between the two positions and compare it to the manufacturer's stated radius for the installed nozzle.
Correction: add an intermediate position, change to a longer-radius nozzle, or reposition the affected units to restore head-to-head coverage.
Reinspection: repeat the coverage test on the corrected area.

Overspray Lands on Siding, Sidewalk, or Neighboring Property

Probable cause: arc, radius, or rotation was not adjusted after installation, or a unit was positioned too close to the boundary or structure.
Confirmation: run the zone and observe spray landing beyond the marked coverage area.
Correction: adjust the arc, radius, or nozzle, or reposition the unit to keep spray within the intended zone.
Reinspection: repeat the overspray check during the next coverage test.

Catch-Cup Readings Are Highly Uneven Across the Zone

Probable cause: mismatched precipitation rates among grouped sprinklers, low or fluctuating operating pressure, or wind during the test.
Confirmation: compare the precipitation rate of each installed nozzle and measure operating pressure during a repeat test on a calm day.
Correction: regroup sprinklers by matching precipitation rate, adjust the pressure regulator, or reschedule the test for calmer conditions.
Reinspection: repeat the distribution check with the corrected grouping or conditions.

Leak Appears at a Fitting or Hose Connection After Pressurizing

Probable cause: a fitting was not fully seated, a hose-thread connection was not tightened to a leak-free seal, or a component was damaged during installation.
Confirmation: depressurize the zone, inspect the specific fitting or connection, and reseat or retighten it.
Correction: replace a damaged component before repressurizing.
Reinspection: repeat the leak and hazard check after the repair.

Watering Schedule Does Not Account for Local Restrictions

Probable cause: the schedule was set from soil and turf needs alone without checking local watering-day or runtime restrictions.
Confirmation: compare the recorded schedule against the current local watering restrictions identified in Step 2.
Correction: revise the schedule to comply with the restriction while meeting the turf's watering need within the allowed days and runtime.
Reinspection: repeat the documentation check with the corrected schedule.

Backflow Device Was Not Verified Before the Zone Was Pressurized

Probable cause: the installer assumed the device confirmed in Chapter 1 remained installed and functional without a direct check at the time of this project.
Confirmation: inspect the backflow device directly and confirm it is installed, undamaged, and functioning before any further work.
Correction: stop work immediately, install or repair the required device, and confirm function before repressurizing.
Reinspection: repeat the backflow check before reissuing any coverage-test result obtained before the correction.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a functioning, tested, and documented zone, not an installation left unverified.

  • Coil and store hoses used for the hose-end option away from vehicle and mower traffic
