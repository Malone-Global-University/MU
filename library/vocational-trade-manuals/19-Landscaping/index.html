19-Landscaping

This field manual provides practical, project-based instruction for residential landscaping design, installation, maintenance, and improvement. Students learn to prepare soil, install planting areas, establish lawns, install irrigation, manage drainage, and maintain residential landscapes.

Overview

Landscaping emphasizes hands-on skill development through complete residential landscape projects. Beginning with site evaluation and soil preparation, students progress through grading, planting beds, landscape fabric, shrubs, trees, lawns, irrigation systems, drainage improvements, maintenance practices, restoration, and a complete residential landscape installation capstone project.

Learning Outcomes

  • Inspect residential sites and plan landscape projects.
  • Prepare safe and organized landscaping work areas.
  • Test, improve, and prepare residential soil conditions.
  • Remove unwanted vegetation and establish planting areas.
  • Grade landscape areas for proper drainage.
  • Install planting beds, shrubs, trees, and seasonal plantings.
  • Establish residential lawns using seed and sod methods.
  • Install basic irrigation and landscape drainage systems.
  • Maintain, prune, fertilize, and restore residential landscapes.
  • Complete a full residential landscape installation project.
