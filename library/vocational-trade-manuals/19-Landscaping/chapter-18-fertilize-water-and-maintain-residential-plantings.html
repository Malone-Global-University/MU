Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to inspect and complete one documented maintenance cycle for established and newly planted residential beds. The finished work product is a completed Planting Maintenance Log recording soil-moisture findings, plant-condition observations, fertilizer application, mulch correction, irrigation adjustment, and a follow-up date.

You will review plant species, establishment stage, soil-test results, weather, season, irrigation, mulch, and local restrictions; inspect soil moisture at representative depths; inspect plants for wilting, yellowing, scorch, pests, disease, overwatering, drought, and nutrient symptoms; distinguish moisture stress from nutrient deficiency before fertilizing; select and calibrate the labeled fertilizer product at the approved rate; apply fertilizer within label and local limits while protecting drains, hardscape, water, pets, children, and neighboring property; inspect and restore mulch depth; inspect and adjust drip and sprinkler coverage; remove weeds and minor debris; and record the completed cycle.

This chapter builds directly on the shrub, tree, and perennial plantings established in Chapters 8 through 10, the drip and sprinkler systems installed in Chapters 14 and 15, and the pruning performed in Chapter 17. It applies to both newly installed plantings still in their establishment period and established plantings receiving routine seasonal maintenance. The maintenance cycle documented here feeds directly into the restoration and cleanup work of Chapter 19 and the capstone maintenance handoff in Chapter 20.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • LS-103 — Identify watering needs by inspecting soil moisture at representative depths and distinguishing moisture stress from other plant-condition symptoms.
  • LS-104 — Apply fertilizer responsibly, selecting the labeled product and rate matched to soil-test or plant-specific recommendations and applying only within label and local limits.
  • LS-105 — Maintain mulch depth by inspecting existing coverage, pulling mulch clear of trunks and structures, and restoring only to the approved total depth.
  • LS-106 — Monitor plant stress, inspecting for wilting, yellowing, scorch, pests, disease, and nutrient symptoms and distinguishing among probable causes.
  • LS-107 — Adjust the irrigation schedule, inspecting drip emitters and sprinkler coverage and adjusting runtime, frequency, or emitter placement based on season, soil, plant, and observed moisture.
  • LS-108 — Document the maintenance cycle, recording fertilizer, rate, weather, watering, irrigation changes, plant observations, and a follow-up date.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter is the routine-maintenance link between the installation chapters and the restoration and closeout chapters that follow. The plant species, establishment stage, soil-test results, and irrigation zones documented in Chapters 1, 3, 8 through 10, 14, and 15 are the reference data this chapter's inspection depends on rather than re-derives.

The maintenance-log entries produced here — moisture findings, fertilizer applications, mulch corrections, and irrigation adjustments — become the reference record for Chapter 19's restoration and cleanup and for the ongoing maintenance instructions handed off at the Chapter 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing plant species, establishment stage, soil-test results, weather, season, irrigation, mulch, and local restrictions before any application
  • inspecting soil moisture at representative depths using a soil probe, trowel, or moisture meter
  • inspecting plants for wilting, yellowing, scorch, pests, disease, overwatering, drought, and nutrient symptoms
  • distinguishing moisture stress from nutrient deficiency before selecting a correction
  • selecting a labeled fertilizer product and rate matched to a soil-test or plant-specific recommendation
  • calibrating the applicator and applying fertilizer evenly within label and local limits
  • protecting drains, hardscape, water, pets, children, and neighboring property during application
  • inspecting and restoring mulch depth without burying trunks, stems, crowns, siding, or weeps
  • inspecting drip emitters and sprinkler coverage and adjusting runtime, frequency, or placement
  • removing weeds and minor debris encountered during the maintenance walk
  • documenting the completed cycle and referring conditions that require diagnosis

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • apply fertilizer or any chemical outside the product label, local restrictions, or applicator requirements
  • fertilize before heavy rain, on saturated, frozen, or drought-stressed soil, or near a prohibited water zone
  • diagnose or treat disease, major pest infestation, or plant decline that requires specialist review
  • redesign, relocate, replace, or retrench an existing irrigation zone beyond runtime, frequency, and emitter-placement adjustment authorized under Chapters 14 and 15
  • substitute a fertilizer product or rate not supported by the soil test, plant-specific recommendation, or product label

Any condition identified during this chapter that requires diagnosis, specialist review, or correction beyond this chapter's scope must be referred and recorded on the maintenance log rather than treated informally.

Trade Note

Every finding in this chapter is recorded on the Planting Maintenance Log at the time it is observed. A wilting symptom, a moisture reading, or a mulch depth noted from memory after the visit is the most common source of the fertilizer-timing and overwatering errors diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not select or apply any fertilizer product until soil-moisture and plant-condition inspection under Steps 2 through 4 is complete and moisture stress has been distinguished from nutrient deficiency. Do not apply fertilizer before heavy rain, or on saturated, frozen, or drought-stressed soil.

Shop Tip

Check soil moisture at more than one depth and more than one location within the bed before concluding a plant is drought-stressed. A dry surface over moist subsoil, or a moist surface over compacted dry subsoil, produces very different corrections and is the most common cause of the watering-versus-fertilizing errors diagnosed later in this chapter.

Common Field Fundamentals

Every maintenance cycle in this chapter depends on separating four questions in the correct order: is the plant dry or wet, is the plant nutrient-deficient, is the mulch at the correct depth, and is the irrigation zone delivering the water the plant needs. Reversing this order is the source of most fertilizer-timing and overwatering failures.

Moisture, Nutrient, and Application Fundamentals

Inspect soil moisture at representative depths before drawing any conclusion about plant stress. Distinguish moisture stress from nutrient deficiency before selecting a correction. Select only the labeled fertilizer product and rate supported by the soil test or plant-specific recommendation, and apply it only within label and local limits. Do not fertilize before heavy rain or on saturated, frozen, or drought-stressed soil.

Measurement Cue — Check Moisture at the Representative Depth

Probe soil moisture at the depth specified for the plant type being checked, not only at the surface. Check more than one location within the maintenance area and record each reading before concluding the plant is under- or overwatered.

Critical Safety Cue — The Product Label Is Controlling

Do not apply fertilizer or any chemical outside the product label, local restrictions, or applicator requirements. More product is not better. Protect wells, drains, storm systems, streams, ponds, soil, pets, children, pollinators, food gardens, and adjacent property from chemical drift, runoff, spills, and overapplication.

Field Safety Cue — Weather and Application Timing

Do not apply fertilizer before heavy rain, on frozen ground, on saturated soil, or near prohibited water zones. Wear sun, insect, and weather protection appropriate to the season, and stop the visit during lightning or other hazardous weather.

Code Alert — Local Fertilizer and Water-Use Restrictions

Confirm adopted local restrictions on fertilizer timing, fertilizer-free zones near water, and watering schedules before applying fertilizer or adjusting irrigation. These requirements vary by location and must be verified rather than assumed.

E. Reference Review and Moisture Inspection

  1. Review plant species, establishment stage, soil test, weather, season, irrigation, mulch, and local restrictions. Confirm each source document is current before relying on it.
  2. Inspect soil moisture at representative depths. Probe at more than one location within the maintenance area and record each reading on the Planting Maintenance Log.
  3. Inspect plants for wilting, yellowing, scorch, pests, disease, overwatering, drought, and nutrient symptoms. Record the pattern, location, and affected plants for each symptom observed.
  4. Distinguish moisture stress from nutrient deficiency before fertilizing. Compare the moisture findings from Step 2 against the symptom pattern from Step 3 before proceeding to a fertilizer decision.

F. Fertilizer Selection and Application

  1. Review soil-test or plant-specific fertilizer recommendation. Confirm the recommendation applies to the current maintenance area and season.
  2. Select the labeled product and approved rate. Read the full label before opening the container.
  3. Calibrate the applicator. Verify the spreader or measuring container delivers the calculated rate before beginning application.
  4. Protect drains, hardscape, water, pets, children, and neighboring property. Identify and mark any nearby drain, water feature, or restricted zone before applying.
  5. Apply fertilizer evenly and only within label and local limits. Do not exceed the label rate under any circumstance.
  6. Sweep granules from hardscape back into the bed. Do not allow granules to remain on paved surfaces or enter drains.
  7. Water in fertilizer only as the label requires. Do not water in a product whose label does not call for it.
  8. Do not fertilize before heavy rain or on saturated, frozen, or drought-stressed soil. Reschedule the application if current conditions do not meet this standard.

G. Mulch, Irrigation, and Documentation

  1. Inspect mulch depth and distribution. Measure existing depth at multiple points across the maintenance area.
  2. Pull mulch away from trunks, stems, crowns, siding, and weeps. Clear the specified distance before adding any material.
  3. Add mulch only to restore the approved total depth. Do not add mulch on top of existing coverage that already meets the approved depth.
  4. Inspect drip emitters and sprinkler coverage. Confirm every emitter and head serving the maintenance area is functioning.
  5. Adjust runtime, frequency, or emitter placement based on season, soil, plant, and observed moisture. Base every adjustment on the findings recorded in Section E.
  6. Check for leaks, overspray, runoff, and clogged emitters. Correct or flag each condition found.
  7. Remove weeds and minor debris. Clear the maintenance area without disturbing mulch depth or irrigation components.
  8. Record fertilizer, rate, weather, watering, irrigation changes, plant observations, and follow-up date. Complete every field on the Planting Maintenance Log before ending the visit.
  9. Refer disease, pest, or decline conditions requiring diagnosis. Document the referral and the condition on the maintenance log rather than treating it outside this chapter's scope.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed Planting Maintenance Log using the same checks regardless of bed size or plant type.

  • Completeness check: confirm all ten sections of the Maintenance Log Section Schedule are completed with the required content.
  • Sequence check: confirm moisture inspection and stress determination were completed before any fertilizer decision.
  • Label check: confirm the applied product, rate, timing, and method match the label exactly.
  • Weather and surface check: confirm no application occurred before heavy rain or on saturated, frozen, or drought-stressed soil.
  • Mulch and irrigation check: confirm restored mulch depth and every irrigation adjustment are recorded with the supporting observation.
  • Documentation check: confirm fertilizer, rate, weather, watering, irrigation changes, plant observations, and a follow-up date are recorded.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Plant Was Fertilized That Was Actually Moisture-Stressed

Probable cause: fertilizer was applied based on visible symptoms alone without probing soil moisture at a representative depth first.
Confirmation: probe soil moisture at the representative depth now and compare the reading to the symptom pattern recorded at the time of application.
Correction: correct the watering schedule to address the confirmed moisture condition, and do not apply additional fertilizer until a new soil test or plant-specific recommendation supports it.
Reinspection: repeat the moisture-before-fertilizer sequence check on the affected planting.

Fertilizer Granules Were Found on Hardscape or Near a Drain

Probable cause: the applicator was not calibrated correctly, or the bed edge was not identified before spreading.
Confirmation: inspect the application boundary against the bed edge and check the applicator's calibration against the label rate.
Correction: sweep all granules from hardscape back into the bed immediately and recalibrate the applicator before the next application.
Reinspection: repeat the label check and the protection check on the corrected boundary.

Mulch Depth Exceeds the Approved Total Depth

Probable cause: mulch was added on top of existing coverage without first measuring the depth already present.
Confirmation: measure current mulch depth at multiple points and compare to the approved total depth from the Chapter 7 specification.
Correction: remove excess mulch down to the approved total depth and pull material clear of trunks, stems, and crowns.
Reinspection: repeat the mulch and irrigation check on the corrected area.

An Irrigation Adjustment Did Not Match the Recorded Moisture Finding

Probable cause: the runtime or frequency change was made from general assumption rather than the moisture readings recorded in Section E.
Confirmation: compare the adjustment recorded on the maintenance log against the moisture findings from the same visit.
Correction: reset the adjustment to match the documented finding, or record a new moisture check to support the intended change.
Reinspection: repeat the irrigation adjustment check before closing the log entry.

A Maintenance Cycle Was Logged Without a Follow-Up Date

Probable cause: the documentation step was completed for fertilizer and irrigation but the follow-up date field was left blank.
Confirmation: review the completed log entry against the Maintenance Log Section Schedule.
Correction: assign a specific follow-up date based on the plant's establishment stage, season, and any condition still under observation.
Reinspection: repeat the documentation check before filing the completed log entry.

A Disease or Pest Symptom Was Treated Without Referral

Probable cause: a symptom outside this chapter's scope was addressed informally with fertilizer or watering changes instead of being referred.
Confirmation: compare the observed symptom against the moisture and nutrient patterns this chapter authorizes correcting.
Correction: stop informal treatment, document the condition, and refer it to the required qualified professional.
Reinspection: repeat the plant-condition section review once the referral has been completed.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a completed, signed maintenance log retained for the next scheduled cycle, not filed away unused.

  • Return unused fertilizer product to its labeled, sealed container and approved storage location
  • Clean the applicator, rake, and mulch fork before storage
  • Confirm no fertilizer granules remain on hardscape, in drains, or near restricted water zones
  • Organize maintenance-visit photographs by section to match the Maintenance Log Section Schedule
  • Store the completed Planting Maintenance Log where the next scheduled visit and Chapter 19 restoration work can reference it

Field Terms

The specific bed, planting group, or zone defined for this maintenance cycle.
