Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to prepare soil and seed one small residential lawn area from accepted grade through germination watering. The finished work product is a uniformly seeded, protected lawn area with a documented Seeding and Establishment Record showing seed species, rate, weather conditions, watering schedule, and follow-up inspection.

You will review the seed species, season, sun exposure, soil-test results, irrigation capacity, slope, and expected use recorded in earlier chapters; confirm that drainage and rough grade are already accepted; remove stones, roots, debris, weeds, and clods; correct low spots; spread approved topsoil or compost only where specified; incorporate amendments; establish final grade relative to adjacent hardscape; rake to a fine, firm seedbed; calculate and apply seed at the labeled rate in two directions; rake seed lightly into the surface; apply cover material where specified; begin gentle irrigation; establish a germination watering schedule; protect the area from traffic and erosion; and document the completed work.

This chapter does not repeat soil testing, grading, or drainage work. Those competencies were established in Chapter 1, Chapter 3, and Chapter 5, and this chapter proceeds only from an area where that work has already been accepted.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • LS-061 — Grade lawn soil to the specified final elevation relative to adjacent hardscape.
  • LS-062 — Remove debris, stones, roots, weeds, and clods from the seedbed area.
  • LS-063 — Spread topsoil or compost only where specified and incorporate it uniformly.
  • LS-064 — Apply grass seed at the labeled rate using a calibrated spreader in a two-direction pattern.
  • LS-065 — Cover seed lightly without exceeding the permitted burial depth.
  • LS-066 — Establish and document a germination-stage watering schedule and transition to deeper watering as roots develop.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter depends directly on the Landscape Readiness Worksheet from Chapter 1, the soil-test results and amendment record from Chapter 3, the vegetation-removal record from Chapter 4, and the accepted grade and drainage record from Chapter 5. No seeding work in this chapter begins until drainage and rough grade for the specific lawn area are already accepted.

The seeded lawn area produced here becomes the reference record for Chapter 13, which diagnoses and repairs bare or damaged turf, and it establishes the watering-schedule pattern extended in Chapter 18's ongoing maintenance cycle. Where the project instead calls for sod rather than seed, Chapter 12 uses this same accepted grade and soil preparation as its starting point.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing seed species, season, sun exposure, soil test, irrigation, slope, erosion, and expected use before starting
  • confirming that drainage and rough grade are already accepted from Chapter 5
  • removing stones, roots, debris, weeds, and clods from the seedbed
  • correcting low spots and settlement within the seedbed area
  • spreading approved topsoil or compost only where the project specification requires it
  • establishing final grade below adjacent hardscape by the specified allowance
  • calculating seed quantity from area and the seed label's rate
  • calibrating a spreader and applying seed in a two-direction pattern
  • covering seed lightly and applying erosion-control cover where specified
  • beginning gentle irrigation and establishing a germination watering schedule
  • protecting the seeded area from traffic, pets, and erosion
  • inspecting coverage, washout, puddling, bare areas, and germination and documenting the result

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • begin seeding an area where drainage and rough grade have not received an approved or conditional readiness decision under Chapter 1 and Chapter 5
  • select amendments, rates, or seed varieties that are not supported by the soil test, seed label, or project specification
  • bury seed deeper than the seed label permits or apply seed at a rate other than the labeled rate
  • apply fertilizer, starter material, or chemicals outside label directions, local water-use restrictions, or the project specification

Any lawn area showing unresolved drainage failure, contaminated soil, or an unaccepted grade must be referred back to Chapter 5 or Chapter 3 for correction and reinspection before seeding proceeds.

Trade Note

The seed label is the controlling document for rate, depth, and season. A denser stand does not come from doubling the label rate; overseeding at more than the labeled rate causes overcrowded, weak seedlings that compete for the same limited moisture and nutrients. Follow the rate printed on the specific product being used.

Setup Standard

Do not begin seedbed preparation until the Chapter 5 grading record confirms drainage and rough grade are accepted for this specific lawn area. Do not apply seed until the seedbed has been raked to a fine, firm surface free of stones, roots, debris, and clods.

Shop Tip

Divide the calculated seed quantity into two equal portions and walk the spreader in perpendicular passes — one set of passes north-south and the second set east-west. This two-direction pattern is the most reliable way to avoid the light and heavy streaks that a single-direction pass produces.

Common Field Fundamentals

This chapter depends on four fundamentals already established elsewhere in this book: accepted grade and drainage, tested and amended soil, a fine and firm seedbed surface, and a correctly calculated seed rate. Seeding work does not correct a grading or drainage defect; it conceals one if performed before that defect is corrected.

Grade and Drainage Confirmation

Confirm the Chapter 5 grading record for this lawn area before any seedbed preparation begins. Do not use seedbed raking, topsoil addition, or seed application to correct a low spot, reverse slope, or blocked outlet; refer any unresolved drainage condition back to Chapter 5.

Measurement Cue — Confirm the Labeled Rate Before Calculating Seed Quantity

Read the specific seed product's label rate before calculating quantity for the measured area. Rates vary by species, blend, and manufacturer, and a rate carried over from a different product will produce an incorrect quantity.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Seed Over an Unresolved Drainage Defect

Do not proceed with seedbed preparation or seeding over a low spot, reverse slope, or blocked outlet identified in the Chapter 5 grading record. Seeding over an unresolved drainage defect conceals the problem, leads to washout or ponding under the new turf, and requires the repair work of Chapter 13.

Field Safety Cue — Footing on Loosened Soil

Watch footing on freshly tilled or raked soil, which shifts underfoot differently than firm turf. Wear closed-toe footwear at all times, and stop work during lightning, extreme heat, or other hazardous weather.

Code Alert — Local Watering Restrictions

Confirm local water-use restrictions before establishing the germination watering schedule. Many jurisdictions limit watering days, times, or methods, and germination-stage watering must be scheduled within those limits.

E. Preparation — Review, Confirmation, and Seedbed Clearing

  1. Review seed species, planting season, sun, soil test, irrigation, slope, erosion, and expected use. Confirm the seed product's label rate, depth, and season match current conditions.
  2. Confirm drainage and rough grade are accepted. Check the Chapter 5 grading record for this specific lawn area before proceeding.
  3. Remove stones, roots, debris, weeds, and clods. Clear the full seedbed area by hand or rake.
  4. Correct low spots and settlement. Fill and level any low spot identified within the seedbed area using approved topsoil.

F. Amendment and Grade Finishing

  1. Spread approved topsoil or compost only where specified. Follow the amendment depth stated in the project specification.
  2. Incorporate amendments uniformly. Mix into the existing soil surface without leaving pockets of unmixed material.
  3. Establish final grade below adjacent hardscape by the specified allowance. Check the elevation against walks, driveways, and patios at multiple points.
  4. Rake to a fine, firm seedbed. Break up remaining clods and produce a level, uniform surface.
  5. Lightly firm the surface. Use a roller or hand tamper only as the project specification requires.

G. Seed Application and Coverage

  1. Calculate seed quantity from area and label rate. Multiply the measured area by the specific product's labeled rate.
  2. Calibrate the spreader. Set the spreader according to the manufacturer's calibration chart for the specific seed product.
  3. Divide seed for two-direction application. Split the calculated quantity into two equal portions.
  4. Spread the first half in one direction. Walk the spreader in parallel passes across the full seedbed.
  5. Spread the second half perpendicular to the first. Walk the spreader in parallel passes across the seedbed at a right angle to the first pass.
  6. Rake seed lightly into the surface. Use a light touch that settles seed into contact with soil.
  7. Do not bury seed deeper than permitted. Confirm the resulting coverage does not exceed the seed label's stated depth.

H. Cover, Watering, and Protection

  1. Apply straw, erosion blanket, starter material, or cover only where specified. Use the cover material and rate stated in the project specification.
  2. Roll or press seedbed lightly where required. Use only the method and equipment specified for the project.
  3. Begin gentle irrigation without washing seed. Use a fine spray setting or sprinkler pattern that does not displace seed or cause runoff.
  4. Establish frequent light watering for germination and transition to deeper watering as roots develop. Record the schedule on the Seeding and Establishment Record.
  5. Protect from traffic, pets, erosion, and mowing. Mark or fence the area as needed until germination and initial rooting are established.
  6. Inspect coverage, washout, puddling, bare areas, and germination. Walk the full area and note any deficiency.
  7. Document seed, rate, weather, watering, and follow-up. Complete the Seeding and Establishment Record before closing the project.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed Seeding and Establishment Record using the same checks regardless of the lawn area's size or seed species.

  • Grade and drainage check: confirm the Chapter 5 grading record was accepted before seedbed preparation began.
  • Seedbed check: confirm the seedbed was cleared of debris and raked to a fine, firm surface.
  • Rate check: confirm the applied seed quantity matches the measured area and the specific product's labeled rate.
  • Coverage check: confirm seed was raked in without exceeding the labeled burial depth.
  • Watering check: confirm a germination watering schedule was established and recorded.
  • Uniformity check: confirm no bare or washed-out area exceeds the project specification's tolerance.

Complete the Inspection Checklist in the section below before beginning Chapter 12 or Chapter 13.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Seed Washes Into Streaks or Piles After the First Watering

Probable cause: irrigation was applied at too high a flow or pressure for the germination stage, or the seedbed retained a slope steeper than the specification permits.
Confirmation: inspect the area immediately after watering for visible streaking, pooling, or bare patches where seed has moved.
Correction: reduce flow to a fine spray or mist setting, redistribute displaced seed by hand where practical, and reseed bare patches at the labeled rate.
Reinspection: repeat the seedbed and watering check after the next irrigation cycle.

Germination Is Patchy or Delayed Across Part of the Area

Probable cause: uneven seed distribution from a single-direction application, an uncalibrated spreader, or inconsistent watering across the area.
Confirmation: compare germination density across different sections of the lawn area and check the spreader's calibration record against the seed product's chart.
Correction: reseed thin or bare sections at the labeled rate using a properly calibrated spreader, and verify irrigation coverage reaches all sections evenly.
Reinspection: repeat the coverage and germination check after the correction is watered in.

The Seedbed Retains a Low Spot That Ponds Water

Probable cause: a low spot present in the Chapter 5 grading record was not corrected before seeding, or settlement occurred after topsoil placement.
Confirmation: check the area for ponding during or immediately after irrigation, and compare the current elevation to the accepted grading record.
Correction: refer the area back to Chapter 5 for grade correction before reseeding the affected section.
Reinspection: repeat the grade and drainage confirmation before reseeding.

Seedlings Emerge Then Wilt Within the First Two Weeks

Probable cause: the germination watering schedule was interrupted, allowing the seedbed surface to dry out during the critical early-root period.
Confirmation: check soil moisture at the surface and compare the recorded watering schedule against actual watering performed.
Correction: resume frequent light watering immediately and adjust the schedule to match the recorded plan; reseed any section that did not survive.
Reinspection: repeat the watering check daily until consistent germination resumes.

Final Grade Does Not Match the Specified Allowance Below Hardscape

Probable cause: topsoil or amendment was added without rechecking elevation against adjacent walks, driveways, or patios.
Confirmation: measure the finished seedbed elevation at multiple points against the adjacent hardscape.
Correction: remove or add material as needed to restore the specified allowance before reseeding the affected section.
Reinspection: repeat the final grade check across the full measured area.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, watered, and protected seeded lawn area, not a finished-looking surface left unprotected.

  • Remove excess topsoil, compost, or cover material staged for the project and return unused seed to sealed, labeled storage
  • Clean and store the spreader, rake, and roller according to manufacturer instructions
  • Sweep or blow spilled seed off hardscape back onto the seedbed rather than into storm drains
  • Install or verify protective markers, flags, or barriers around the seeded area before leaving the site
  • Store the completed Seeding and Establishment Record where Chapter 13 and Chapter 18 can reference it
