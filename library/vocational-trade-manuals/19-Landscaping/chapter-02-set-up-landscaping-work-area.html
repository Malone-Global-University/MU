Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to prepare a controlled landscaping work site directly from the conditions and constraints documented in the Chapter 1 Landscape Readiness Worksheet. The finished work product is a protected, organized landscape site with established work zones, protected existing plants and hardscape, stable material staging, safe equipment and delivery routes, controlled chemical storage, waste handling, and a completed work-area safety inspection.

You will review the approved sequence, materials, equipment, deliveries, weather, and occupancy conditions; confirm utility markings remain valid; establish soil, mulch, plant, turf, irrigation, cutting, chemical, and waste zones; establish equipment routes; protect hardscape and existing plants; stage materials on stable surfaces; inspect tools; establish PPE and weather procedures; establish chemical and waste handling; and complete a documented work-area safety inspection.

This chapter builds directly on Chapter 1. No zone, route, or staging area may be established in a location the Chapter 1 worksheet identified as a deficiency, a utility-locating area not yet confirmed, or a stop-work condition. Every later chapter — soil amendment, vegetation removal, grading, bed edging, planting, turf work, irrigation, drainage, pruning, fertilizing, and restoration — depends on the protected, organized site established here.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • LS-007 — Establish work zones for soil, mulch, plants, turf, irrigation, cutting, chemicals, and waste.
  • LS-008 — Stage soil and mulch on stable surfaces without damaging protected root zones or drainage structures.
  • LS-009 — Protect hardscape surfaces, including pavers, concrete, masonry, siding, doors, windows, and vehicles.
  • LS-010 — Protect existing plants using trunk guards, root-zone protection, fencing, or no-entry zones.
  • LS-011 — Plan tool and equipment access routes for trucks, wheelbarrows, carts, sod cutters, tillers, and trenchers.
  • LS-012 — Complete the work-area safety inspection, including PPE, hydration, chemical control, and stop-work review.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter converts the Chapter 1 Landscape Readiness Worksheet into a physically protected, organized work site. The zones, routes, and protections established here remain in place through soil testing and amendment in Chapter 3, vegetation removal in Chapter 4, grading in Chapter 5, bed layout in Chapter 6, every planting chapter from Chapter 8 through Chapter 10, turf work in Chapters 11 through 13, irrigation and drainage in Chapters 14 through 16, ongoing maintenance in Chapters 17 and 18, restoration in Chapter 19, and the Chapter 20 capstone.

The zone boundaries, protection locations, and equipment routes documented in this chapter are not temporary conveniences. They are the reference layout every later chapter is required to maintain rather than redefine.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved project sequence, material quantities, equipment, deliveries, weather, and occupancy
  • confirming utility markings from Chapter 1 remain visible and valid
  • establishing soil, compost, mulch, plant, turf, irrigation, cutting, chemical, waste, and restricted zones
  • establishing equipment and delivery routes for trucks, wheelbarrows, carts, sod cutters, tillers, and trenchers
  • protecting pavers, concrete, masonry, siding, doors, windows, vehicles, and neighboring property
  • installing trunk guards, root-zone protection, fencing, or no-entry zones around existing plants
  • staging soil, compost, mulch, gravel, sod, and plants on stable surfaces, shaded and protected from wind
  • inspecting hand tools and powered equipment before use
  • establishing PPE, hydration, shade, first-aid, heat-stress, and weather procedures
  • establishing chemical storage, mixing, spill control, and restricted-access zones where applicable
  • establishing waste handling for plant, soil, sod, plastic-pot, pallet, and hazardous material
  • completing a documented work-area safety inspection

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • establish any zone, route, or staging area in a location the Chapter 1 worksheet lists as an unresolved deficiency or stop-work condition
  • stage soil, mulch, equipment, or pallets over protected root zones or drainage structures identified in Chapter 1
  • begin soil amendment, vegetation removal, grading, planting, turf, or irrigation work authorized by a later chapter before the work-area safety inspection in this chapter is complete
  • store or mix chemicals without confirming label directions, local restrictions, and applicator requirements

Any zone, route, or protection measure found inadequate during the work-area safety inspection must be corrected and reinspected before later chapters' work begins.

Trade Note

The zones established in this chapter are not marked once and forgotten. A soil zone that drifts into a marked no-dig zone, or a cutting zone that migrates next to a protected root zone, is the single most common source of the plant and utility damage diagnosed in this chapter's troubleshooting section.

Setup Standard

Do not establish any zone or route until Step 2 confirms utility markings from Chapter 1 remain visible and valid. Do not stage soil, mulch, equipment, or pallets over any protected root zone or drainage structure identified in Chapter 1.

Shop Tip

Walk the planned equipment route with an empty wheelbarrow or cart before any delivery arrives. A route that looks wide enough on paper often narrows at a gate, a downspout, or a low tree limb, and this is the most common cause of the route failures diagnosed later in this chapter.

Zones, Protection, and Stop-Work Conditions

Establish soil, compost, mulch, plant, turf, irrigation, cutting, chemical, waste, and restricted zones before any material arrives on site. Do not stage soil, mulch, equipment, or pallets over protected root zones or drainage structures identified in Chapter 1. Stop work if access, staging, plant protection, chemical control, hydration, weather, or public protection is inadequate.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Route Width Before Deliveries

Measure the narrowest point of every planned equipment route, including gates, side-yard passages, and turns, and compare it against the width of the largest planned delivery vehicle, wheelbarrow, cart, or powered equipment. Record the confirmed minimum width on the Work-Area Safety Inspection form.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Stage Over Utility Markings or Protected Roots

Do not place soil, mulch, gravel, pallets, or equipment over utility markings, protected root zones, or drainage structures identified in Chapter 1. Loss of utility markings under staged material can lead to an unmarked strike during later digging, and staging over root zones can compact soil and kill established roots.

Field Safety Cue — Set Up Hydration and Shade Before Work Begins

Establish a shaded rest area and clean drinking water before setup work begins, and monitor weather conditions throughout the day. Stop setup work during lightning, extreme heat, or other hazardous weather.

Code Alert — Chemical Storage and Local Restrictions

Confirm local restrictions on chemical storage, mixing, and disposal before establishing a chemical zone. Product labels and local rules govern container type, secondary containment, and distance from wells, drains, and water features.

E. Preparation — Review and Zone Establishment

  1. Review the approved project sequence, material quantities, equipment, deliveries, weather, and occupancy. Compare against the Chapter 1 Landscape Readiness Worksheet.
  2. Confirm utility markings remain visible and valid. Do not proceed to zone establishment over marked ground until this is confirmed.
  3. Establish soil, compost, mulch, plant, turf, irrigation, cutting, chemical, waste, and restricted zones. Mark each zone with paint, flags, or cord and record its boundary on the Work-Area Safety Inspection form.
  4. Establish routes for trucks, wheelbarrows, carts, sod cutters, tillers, trenchers, and other equipment. Measure the narrowest point of each route before confirming it.

F. Protection and Staging

  1. Protect pavers, concrete, masonry, siding, doors, windows, vehicles, and neighboring property. Install plywood, protection boards, or tarps along every confirmed equipment route and staging area.
  2. Install trunk guards, root-zone protection, fencing, or no-entry zones around existing plants. Reference the Chapter 1 existing-plant assessment for each plant requiring protection.
  3. Do not stage soil, mulch, equipment, or pallets over protected root zones or drainage structures. Verify staging locations against the Chapter 1 drainage and root-zone findings before placing any material.
  4. Stage soil, compost, mulch, gravel, sod, and plants on stable surfaces. Confirm the staging surface will not settle, shift, or drain material into a restricted zone.
  5. Keep plant roots and containers shaded, moist, upright, and protected from wind. Check moisture and shade coverage at intervals appropriate to the weather.
  6. Separate plants by location and label. Mark each group with its intended planting zone from the project plan.

G. Tools, Worker Protection, Chemicals, and Final Inspection

  1. Inspect shovels, rakes, mattocks, augers, tillers, pruners, saws, spreaders, hoses, pumps, and irrigation tools. Remove or tag out any tool with a damaged handle, guard, or cutting edge.
  2. Establish PPE, hydration, shade, first-aid, heat-stress, and weather procedures. Confirm a shaded rest area and clean drinking water are available before setup work continues.
  3. Establish chemical storage, mixing, spill control, and restricted-access zones where applicable. Confirm container type, secondary containment, and distance from wells, drains, and water features against the product label and local restrictions.
  4. Establish clean-water access and equipment-cleaning procedures. Locate this area away from storm drains and planting beds.
  5. Protect storm drains and hardscape from soil, mulch, fertilizer, and chemical runoff. Install barriers or covers at any drain within or adjacent to a work zone.
  6. Establish plant-waste, soil, sod, plastic-pot, pallet, and hazardous-waste handling. Assign a labeled container or area for each waste category.
  7. Mark no-dig, no-chemical, no-storage, utility, and emergency zones. Confirm these marks are distinct from the functional work-zone marks established in Step 3.
  8. Confirm lighting and communication where work may extend. Verify a working communication method and adequate lighting for any period extending beyond daylight.
  9. Complete the work-area safety inspection. Record every zone, route, protection measure, and control on the Work-Area Safety Inspection form.
  10. Stop work if access, staging, plant protection, chemical control, hydration, weather, or public protection is inadequate. Correct and reinspect before Chapter 3 begins.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed Work-Area Safety Inspection using the same checks regardless of the project area's size or intended use.

  • Utility marking check: confirm markings from Chapter 1 remain visible and within their valid period.
  • Zone check: confirm every zone is marked, functionally separated, and clear of restricted areas.
  • Route check: confirm every equipment route is measured, wide enough, and surface-protected where required.
  • Protection check: confirm trunk guards, root-zone fencing, and hardscape protection are installed at every location identified in Chapter 1.
  • Chemical and waste check: confirm storage, mixing, spill control, and waste handling match label directions and local restrictions.
  • Final inspection check: confirm the Work-Area Safety Inspection is signed, dated, and free of unresolved deficiencies.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Equipment Route Fails Once Material Deliveries Begin

Probable cause: the route was confirmed by eye rather than measured at its narrowest point, or a gate, downspout, or low branch was not accounted for.
Confirmation: remeasure the route at every constriction point and compare against the width of the actual delivery vehicle, cart, or equipment.
Correction: reroute around the constriction, remove or trim the obstruction only within the approved scope, or reduce the equipment size used on that route.
Reinspection: repeat the route check before the next delivery.

Staged Material Was Placed Over a Protected Root Zone or Drainage Structure

Probable cause: the staging location was chosen for convenience without checking it against the Chapter 1 root-zone or drainage findings.
Confirmation: compare the staging location against the Chapter 1 Landscape Readiness Worksheet and the marked no-storage zones.
Correction: remove the staged material immediately, inspect the affected root zone or drainage structure for compaction or damage, and relocate staging to an approved zone.
Reinspection: repeat the zone check and the plant-protection check before resuming staging.

A Trunk Guard or Root-Zone Fence Was Installed After Equipment Already Entered the Area

Probable cause: protection was scheduled after material staging or route establishment instead of before.
Confirmation: inspect the trunk, bark, and surrounding soil for fresh scarring, compaction, or root exposure.
Correction: install the required protection immediately, restrict further access to the area, and document any observed plant damage on the Work-Area Safety Inspection form.
Reinspection: repeat the existing-plant protection check before permitting further equipment or staging use in the area.

Chemical Storage Does Not Match Label or Local Restrictions

Probable cause: a chemical zone was established using a general storage practice rather than the specific product label and local restriction.
Confirmation: compare the storage container, containment, and zone distance from wells, drains, and water features against the label and local restriction.
Correction: relocate or reconfigure the chemical zone to match the label and restriction before any chemical is stored or mixed on site.
Reinspection: repeat the chemical and waste check before authorizing chemical use in later chapters.

A Zone Boundary Drifted During Setup

Probable cause: zone marks were placed loosely with cord or flags that shifted, or a second zone was staged without remarking a boundary that had faded.
Confirmation: walk every zone boundary and compare its current location against the recorded boundary on the Work-Area Safety Inspection form.
Correction: remark the boundary, move any material or equipment that has crossed into an adjacent zone, and confirm no restricted zone has been encroached.
Reinspection: repeat the zone check before continuing setup or beginning later chapter work.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a signed Work-Area Safety Inspection retained for use by every later chapter, not a temporary layout that gets abandoned once work begins.

  • Confirm all zone markings, fencing, trunk guards, and protection boards remain in place and are not scheduled for removal before later chapters begin
  • Store unused fencing, marking materials, and protection boards where they remain available for reuse as zones are adjusted in later chapters
  • Photograph the completed zone layout, protection measures, and staging areas
  • Store the completed Work-Area Safety Inspection form alongside the Chapter 1 Landscape Readiness Worksheet
  • Confirm chemical storage remains locked or restricted and waste containers remain labeled before leaving the site

Field Terms

Work Zone
A marked area assigned to one function and not used for another without remarking.
No-Entry Zone
A fenced or barricaded area around an existing plant, utility, or drainage structure that must not be entered.
Equipment Route
The confirmed path for trucks, wheelbarrows, carts, sod cutters, tillers, and trenchers between the delivery point and each work zone.
Restricted Zone
An area marked no-dig, no-chemical, or no-storage because of a utility, drainage structure, or protected root zone.
Chemical Zone
A designated area for chemical storage, mixing, and spill control established only where the project requires on-site chemicals.
Work-Area Deficiency
Any zone, route, or protection measure found inadequate during the safety inspection that must be corrected and reinspected.
Work-Area Safety Inspection
The document recording every zone, route, protection measure, and control established for the site.
Trunk Guard
Padding or barrier material installed around an existing tree trunk to prevent contact damage from equipment or staged material.
