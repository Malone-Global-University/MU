Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to plant one small residential tree at a location verified during the Chapter 1 readiness inspection, using the mature-size, utility, structure, drainage, and clearance information already recorded on the Landscape Readiness Worksheet. The finished work product is one correctly located, correctly planted small tree with the root flare exposed at the approved finished grade, corrected roots, stable backfill, appropriate mulch and initial watering, and staking installed only where justified by site conditions.

You will review the tree species and its mature behavior, confirm the tree is not invasive, prohibited, or unsuitable, inspect the nursery stock, verify the proposed location against every required clearance, confirm utility clearance, dig a properly sized hole, locate and correct the root flare and root system, set and orient the tree, backfill and water it in stages, mulch it correctly, stake it only if justified, and inspect and document the completed planting.

This chapter depends directly on the site data recorded in Chapter 1 and the prepared soil and drainage conditions established in Chapters 3 through 5. A tree may not be planted in a location that has not passed the Chapter 1 readiness inspection or that shows an unresolved mature-size conflict identified there.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • LS-049 — Select the planting location, verifying mature size, root behavior, sun, soil, drainage, water, utilities, structures, pavement, and overhead clearance.
  • LS-050 — Dig the tree hole to the approved width and root-flare elevation on a firm, undisturbed base.
  • LS-051 — Inspect the root flare, correcting circling, girdling, or excess soil above the true flare.
  • LS-052 — Position the tree upright with the root flare at the approved finished grade and the trunk and canopy correctly oriented.
  • LS-053 — Backfill and water the tree in stages without creating a bathtub effect or burying the root flare.
  • LS-054 — Stake the tree only where justified by wind, root-ball instability, or site conditions, and document the removal date.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter follows the shrub-planting work of Chapter 8 and applies the same root-flare and backfill discipline to a larger, longer-lived plant with greater consequences for building and utility conflicts. The location verified here must already satisfy the mature-clearance findings recorded in Chapter 1 and must not conflict with irrigation zones planned in Chapters 14 through 16 or drainage work completed in Chapter 5 and Chapter 16.

The planted tree, its documented species, source, planting date, and establishment plan carry forward into Chapter 17 pruning, Chapter 18 fertilizing and watering, Chapter 19 restoration, and the Chapter 20 capstone, where a small tree may be included in the complete planting plan.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing tree species, mature height and spread, root behavior, sun, soil, drainage, water, utility, structure, pavement, and overhead-clearance requirements
  • confirming the tree is not invasive, prohibited, or unsuitable for the site
  • inspecting the root ball, trunk, leaders, branches, root flare, container or burlap, basket, and moisture condition of nursery stock
  • verifying mature clearance from foundations, sidewalks, driveways, roofs, utilities, sight lines, septic systems, and neighboring property
  • confirming utility clearance before digging
  • digging a wide hole with a firm, undisturbed base at the correct root-flare elevation
  • locating the true root flare and correcting circling, girdling, and excess soil above it
  • setting, orienting, backfilling, and watering the tree
  • mulching correctly and staking only where justified
  • inspecting and documenting the completed planting

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • plant a tree in a location that has not received an approved or conditional readiness decision under Chapter 1, or that shows an unresolved mature-size conflict
  • dig, auger, or drive stakes before utility locating has been confirmed for the specific planting location
  • plant a species identified as invasive, prohibited, or locally restricted
  • bury the root flare, create a water-holding basin around the root ball, or leave circling or girdling roots uncorrected
  • stake a tree automatically without a documented justification, or leave staking installed beyond the specified removal period
  • prune, remove major roots, or perform structural corrective work beyond the scope defined by this chapter without qualified arborist review

Any location showing unidentified utilities, unstable slopes, contaminated soil, a disputed boundary, or an unresolved drainage defect must be corrected or referred to a qualified professional and reinspected before planting proceeds.

Trade Note

The single most consequential decision in this chapter is locating the true root flare before the tree is set in the hole. A tree planted even a few inches too deep, or left with excess nursery soil covering the flare, can decline and fail years after planting when the cause is no longer obvious. Locate and expose the flare before judging planting depth by any other reference.

Setup Standard

Do not dig the planting hole until utility clearance for this specific location is confirmed and the location has been verified against every mature-clearance requirement recorded in Chapter 1. Do not set the tree in the hole until the true root flare has been located and every circling or girdling root has been corrected.

Shop Tip

View the tree from the primary viewing direction — typically the street, entry walk, or main window — before deciding final trunk and canopy orientation. Rotate the tree in the hole, not after backfilling begins.

Location, Utilities, and Stop-Work Conditions

Verify mature clearance from foundations, sidewalks, driveways, roofs, utilities, sight lines, septic systems, and neighboring property before digging. Confirm utility locating for the specific planting location. Stop work for unidentified utilities, unstable slopes, contaminated soil, or an unresolved mature-size conflict already flagged on the Chapter 1 deficiency list.

Measurement Cue — Locate the Root Flare Before Setting Depth

Remove excess soil from the top of the root ball until the true root flare is visible. Set the hole depth, and later the tree, from this located flare, not from the top of the container or burlap ball, which frequently sits above the true flare on nursery stock.

Critical Safety Cue — Confirm Utility Clearance Before Digging

Do not dig, auger, or drive stakes at this location until utility locating has been requested, markings placed, and those markings confirmed valid for the specific planting location. Contact with an unmarked utility line can cause serious injury, fire, explosion, or service disruption.

Field Safety Cue — Lift and Handle the Root Ball Safely

Use safe lifting methods, a cart, dolly, or team lift for balled-and-burlapped or large container stock. Do not lift or drag the tree by the trunk or stem. Keep hands and feet clear when cutting wire baskets under tension, and wear eye protection during wire cutting.

Code Alert — Setback and Easement Restrictions

Confirm local zoning, easement, sight-line, and utility-clearance requirements for tree placement before finalizing the location. These requirements vary by jurisdiction and must be verified rather than assumed.

E. Species Review, Stock Inspection, and Location

  1. Review tree species, mature height and spread, root behavior, sun, soil, drainage, water, utilities, structures, pavement, and overhead clearance. Use the tree schedule and planting plan; do not rely on general impressions of the species.
  2. Confirm the tree is not invasive, prohibited, or unsuitable. Check the species against local restricted-plant lists before proceeding.
  3. Inspect the root ball, trunk, leaders, branches, root flare, container, burlap, basket, and moisture. Record condition on the Tree Planting Record.
  4. Reject severely girdled, damaged, unstable, diseased, or dried stock. Do not attempt to correct stock that fails this inspection; obtain replacement stock instead.
  5. Place the tree at the proposed location. Set the tree, still in its container or burlap, at the marked planting point.
  6. Verify mature clearance from foundations, sidewalks, driveways, roofs, utilities, sight lines, septic systems, and neighboring property. Compare the placement against the mature-size figures reviewed in Step 1 and the Chapter 1 findings.
  7. Confirm utility clearance. Verify utility-locating markings are present and valid for this specific location before digging.

F. Hole Preparation and Root Correction

  1. Dig a wide planting hole with a firm undisturbed base. Widen the hole well beyond the root ball while leaving the base soil uncompacted by digging but not loosened.
  2. Do not dig deeper than the root-flare elevation requires. Check hole depth against the located flare, not against the container height.
  3. Remove the tree from the container or prepare balled-and-burlapped material using the approved sequence. Support the root ball fully during removal; do not pull or lift by the trunk.
  4. Locate the true root flare. Probe or brush away surface soil at the base of the trunk until the flare is visible.
  5. Remove excess soil from the top of the root ball where needed. Continue until the flare is exposed at the surface of the root ball.
  6. Correct circling and girdling roots using the approved method. Cut, straighten, or remove roots that circle the root ball or cross and constrict other roots or the trunk.
  7. Remove or fold back burlap, twine, and basket material as specified. Cut and remove wire baskets and synthetic burlap in full; fold back natural burlap from the top and sides of the root ball as specified for the planting method.

G. Setting, Backfill, Watering, Mulch, and Staking

  1. Set the tree upright with the root flare at or slightly above the approved finished grade. Check elevation against the hole edge and the surrounding grade using a level or straightedge.
  2. Orient trunk and canopy. View the tree from the primary viewing direction and rotate it in the hole before backfilling begins.
  3. Backfill with approved site soil. Add soil in stages around the root ball, working it into voids without dropping large clods against the trunk.
  4. Firm lightly and water in stages. Firm each backfill lift only enough to eliminate large air pockets, then water to settle the soil before adding the next lift.
  5. Avoid creating a bathtub effect with overamended soil. Backfill with the native site soil removed from the hole rather than heavily amended soil unless the approved plan specifies otherwise.
  6. Apply mulch in a broad ring while keeping it away from the trunk. Maintain the specified depth and keep mulch off the trunk and root flare.
  7. Stake only if wind, root-ball instability, or site conditions justify it. Do not stake automatically; document the specific condition that justifies staking when it is used.
  8. Use flexible ties and prevent bark damage. Attach ties loosely enough to allow trunk movement and growth without cutting into bark.
  9. Record stake-removal date. Enter the planned removal date on the Tree Planting Record at the time staking is installed.

H. Inspection and Documentation

  1. Inspect root flare, trunk stability, vertical alignment, mulch, water, stakes, and clearances. Confirm every acceptance standard in Section D before closing out the project.
  2. Document species, nursery source, planting date, and establishment plan. Complete the Tree Planting Record and retain it for use by Chapter 17, Chapter 18, and Chapter 19.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed tree planting using the same checks regardless of species or hole conditions.

  • Location check: confirm every mature-clearance and utility-clearance requirement was verified before digging.
  • Root-flare check: confirm the flare is exposed and set at or slightly above the approved finished grade.
  • Root-condition check: confirm no circling, girdling, or kinked roots remain uncorrected.
  • Stability check: confirm the trunk is plumb and stable, with staking present only where justified.
  • Mulch check: confirm mulch is at the specified depth and clear of the trunk.
  • Documentation check: confirm species, source, planting date, and establishment plan are recorded.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

The Root Flare Is Buried After Planting

Probable cause: planting depth was judged from the top of the container or burlap ball rather than from the located true root flare.
Confirmation: probe the soil at the base of the trunk and compare the depth of the flare to the surrounding finished grade.
Correction: remove backfill and mulch around the trunk until the flare is exposed at or slightly above grade; do not leave excess soil covering the flare.
Reinspection: repeat the root-flare check after correction.

The Tree Leans After Backfilling

Probable cause: the hole base was not firm and undisturbed, allowing the root ball to settle unevenly, or backfill was firmed unevenly around the root ball.
Confirmation: check trunk plumb from two directions at ninety degrees to each other.
Correction: excavate around the root ball, reset the tree plumb on a firm base, and rebackfill evenly in stages.
Reinspection: repeat the stability check after the correction and again after the next watering cycle.

Water Pools Around the Root Ball After Watering

Probable cause: a bathtub effect from an overexcavated or heavily amended hole that drains more slowly than the surrounding native soil.
Confirmation: observe drainage after a thorough watering and compare the drainage rate inside the hole to the surrounding soil.
Correction: where practical, break through the compacted or glazed hole sidewall to restore drainage continuity with the surrounding soil; consult the drainage findings from Chapter 5 for the specific location.
Reinspection: repeat a controlled water observation on the corrected hole.

A Circling Root Was Missed Before Backfilling

Probable cause: the root system was inspected only at the outer surface of the root ball without checking beneath the surface for roots circling deeper in the container.
Confirmation: if discovered after backfilling has begun, remove backfill and reinspect the root ball surface at multiple depths.
Correction: cut and correct the circling root using the approved method before continuing backfill.
Reinspection: repeat the root-condition check before completing backfill.

Staking Was Installed Without a Documented Justification

Probable cause: staking was applied automatically as routine practice rather than in response to an observed wind, root-ball, or site condition.
Confirmation: review the Tree Planting Record for a stated justification and compare it against the actual site conditions observed at planting.
Correction: if no justification exists and the tree is stable without staking, remove the stakes and ties; if a justification exists but was not recorded, document it and the planned removal date.
Reinspection: repeat the stability check with staking removed or the documentation check with staking retained.

Mulch Is Piled Against the Trunk

Probable cause: mulch was applied in a mound against the trunk rather than in a broad, flat ring kept clear of the bark.
Confirmation: inspect the base of the trunk for mulch contact and bark moisture or softening beneath the mulch.
Correction: pull mulch back from the trunk to expose the root flare and reshape the mulch ring to the specified depth.
Reinspection: repeat the mulch check after correction.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a stable, correctly planted tree and a completed record retained for use by every later chapter.

  • Remove and dispose of the container, burlap remnants, wire, twine, and tags according to the applicable waste-handling procedure
