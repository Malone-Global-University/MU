Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install and test one simple drip-irrigation zone for a planting bed. The finished work product is a leak-free, backflow-protected, pressure-controlled, filtered drip zone with emitters or dripline placed according to plant water needs, verified distribution, and a documented layout, component list, and initial watering schedule.

You will review the irrigation plan and water source, confirm approved backflow protection, verify pressure and flow, select a compatible filter and regulator, lay out mainline and lateral tubing, cut and connect tubing, install the backflow, filter, regulator, shutoff, and timer in the specified order, flush the system, install emitters or dripline, secure tubing, pressurize and inspect for leaks, test distribution, adjust placement or zoning, program an initial schedule, and mulch without burying serviceable fittings.

This chapter follows bed preparation in Chapters 3 through 7 and planting in Chapters 8 through 10. It precedes final mulching where drip tubing is installed beneath or above mulch according to the approved irrigation detail, and it supplies the irrigation record every later maintenance chapter and the Chapter 20 capstone rely on.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • LS-079 — Lay out drip tubing mainline and lateral runs around plants according to the approved irrigation plan.
  • LS-080 — Connect the system to the water source with the required backflow protection, filter, regulator, and shutoff.
  • LS-081 — Install emitters or dripline according to plant water needs and product limits.
  • LS-082 — Secure tubing without pinching it and keep emitters clear of trunks and crowns.
  • LS-083 — Test water distribution at representative locations and adjust placement or zoning.
  • LS-084 — Inspect irrigation coverage, leaks, and fittings, and document the completed installation.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter depends on the readiness plan from Chapter 1, the prepared soil and bed work from Chapters 3 through 7, and the plant placement completed in Chapters 8 through 10. The irrigation source, pressure, backflow protection, and watering restrictions recorded in Chapter 1 are the controlling reference for this installation; this chapter does not re-derive them.

The completed drip zone becomes the reference every later maintenance chapter depends on. Chapter 18 adjusts this system's runtime and inspects it for leaks and clogged emitters. Chapter 19 cleans irrigation heads, drains, and access points during restoration. The Chapter 20 capstone assigns and integrates this exact irrigation scope into the complete planted bed.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the irrigation plan, water source, backflow requirement, pressure, flow, filter, regulator, tubing, emitters, zones, controller, and winterization
  • verifying available pressure and flow using the approved method
  • selecting a filter and pressure regulator compatible with the system
  • laying out mainline and lateral tubing around plants
  • cutting tubing, installing fittings, and connecting to the approved supply assembly
  • installing backflow, filter, regulator, shutoff, and timer in the specified order
  • flushing tubing before installing final emitters
  • installing dripline or individual emitters according to plant water needs and product limits
  • securing tubing, pressurizing the system, and inspecting every fitting and emitter for leakage
  • testing distribution, adjusting placement or zoning, and programming an initial watering schedule
  • mulching without burying service fittings beyond access, and documenting the completed installation

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • connect any irrigation tubing directly to potable water without the required approved backflow device
  • create cross-connections, submerged outlets, or contaminated water paths
  • design a multi-zone irrigation system, size a mainline for a whole property, or select a backflow assembly without an approved irrigation plan or licensed irrigation contractor where required
  • bury service fittings, valves, or the backflow assembly beyond specified access
  • proceed with installation where the readiness plan, water-source approval, or bed preparation from earlier chapters has not been completed

Any condition beyond this scope must be referred to the required irrigation contractor, backflow tester, or other qualified person identified by the readiness plan or local code.

Trade Note

The backflow device is not optional hardware and is not a step to be completed later. Every connection to a potable water source in this chapter is sequenced after backflow protection is installed and confirmed, not before. A drip system pressurized before backflow protection is in place is a stop-work condition, not a shortcut.

Setup Standard

Do not connect to the water source until backflow protection, filter, regulator, and shutoff are installed in the specified order and confirmed present. Do not install final emitters until the tubing has been flushed. Do not pressurize the system until every fitting is fully seated and inspected.

Shop Tip

Flush tubing before installing final emitters, not after. Debris left inside the tubing from cutting and fitting installation is the most common cause of clogged emitters discovered during the coverage test later in this chapter.

Backflow, Pressure, and Stop-Work Conditions

Do not connect to potable water without the required backflow protection. Verify available pressure and flow using the approved method before selecting the filter and pressure regulator. Do not create cross-connections, submerged outlets, or contaminated water paths at any point in the installation.

Measurement Cue — Verify Pressure and Flow Before Component Selection

Measure available pressure with a pressure gauge at the approved supply assembly and confirm flow using the method identified in the irrigation plan before selecting the filter and regulator. Selecting components before verifying these values is a common cause of undersized filtration and inconsistent emitter output.

Critical Safety Cue — Backflow Protection Before Connection

Do not connect drip tubing to a hose bibb, valve, or irrigation supply assembly until the approved backflow device is installed and confirmed present. Connecting irrigation tubing to potable water without approved backflow protection can allow contaminated water to siphon back into the drinking-water system.

Field Safety Cue — Working Around Pressurized Lines

Depressurize the system before adjusting fittings or emitters that have already been pressurized. Watch footing in the planting bed, keep the punch tool and cutter pointed away from hands and other workers, and stop work during lightning or hazardous weather.

Code Alert — Backflow and Water-Use Requirements

Confirm the locally adopted backflow-prevention and water-use requirements for the jurisdiction, and confirm whether a licensed backflow tester or irrigation contractor is required for this specific connection, before proceeding past Step 2.

E. Preparation — Plan Review, Water Source, and Component Selection

  1. Review the irrigation plan, water source, backflow requirement, pressure, flow, filter, regulator, tubing, emitters, zones, controller, and winterization. Confirm each specified component and value before beginning.
  2. Confirm the water-source connection is approved. Verify the supply assembly identified in the irrigation plan is the lawful connection point.
  3. Do not connect to potable water without required backflow protection. Confirm the specified backflow device is on site and matches the irrigation plan.
  4. Verify available pressure and flow using the approved method. Record both values on the irrigation record form.
  5. Select filter and pressure regulator compatible with the system. Match the selection to the verified pressure and flow and to the manufacturer instructions for the selected emitters or dripline.

F. Layout and Tubing Installation

  1. Lay out mainline and lateral tubing around plants. Follow the approved irrigation plan layout without deviating from specified routing.
  2. Keep tubing clear of heat, sharp edges, mowing, digging, and building drainage. Route around hardscape edges, mower paths, and known digging zones.
  3. Cut tubing square using the approved cutter. Inspect each cut end before fitting installation.
  4. Install fittings fully. Seat each fitting completely per manufacturer instructions before proceeding to the next connection.
  5. Connect to the approved hose bibb, valve, or irrigation supply assembly. Confirm the connection point matches the irrigation plan.
  6. Install backflow, filter, regulator, shutoff, and timer in the specified order. Do not change the installation order specified by the irrigation plan or manufacturer instructions.

G. Flushing and Emitter Installation

  1. Flush tubing before installing final emitters. Open the line end and run water until the discharge is clear of debris.
  2. Install dripline or individual emitters according to plant water needs and product limits. Match emitter flow rate and spacing to the plant list and irrigation plan.
  3. Use approved punch tools and plugs. Plug unused punched openings before pressurizing the system.
  4. Secure tubing with stakes without pinching it. Space stakes to hold tubing at grade without restricting flow.
  5. Keep emitters away from trunks and crowns. Position each emitter at the distance from the plant base specified by the irrigation plan.
  6. Cap or flush the line end. Install the specified end cap or flush-cap fitting at each lateral and mainline termination.

H. Pressurizing, Inspection, and Distribution Testing

  1. Pressurize the system. Open the shutoff slowly and bring the zone to the regulated operating pressure.
  2. Inspect every fitting and emitter for leakage. Walk the full tubing run and check each connection and emitter under pressure.
  3. Measure or compare distribution at representative locations. Use the method identified in the irrigation plan to confirm delivery at each representative point.
  4. Adjust emitter placement or zoning. Correct any location showing inadequate, excessive, or absent distribution, then retest the corrected location.
  5. Program an initial watering schedule based on soil, plant, season, and local restrictions. Record the programmed schedule on the irrigation record form.
  6. Mulch without burying service fittings beyond access. Keep the backflow device, filter, regulator, shutoff, and timer accessible after mulching.
  7. Document layout, components, backflow, pressure, flow, zones, and schedule. Complete the irrigation record form and attach the photographic record.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed drip-irrigation zone using the same checks regardless of bed size or plant type.

  • Backflow and connection-order check: confirm backflow, filter, regulator, and shutoff were installed in the specified order before pressurization.
  • Pressure and flow check: confirm verified values are recorded and match the selected filter and regulator.
  • Leak check: confirm every fitting and emitter was inspected under pressure with no leakage observed.
  • Emitter-placement check: confirm emitters match plant water needs and are clear of trunks and crowns.
  • Distribution check: confirm representative locations were tested and any deficiency was corrected and retested.
  • Documentation check: confirm the irrigation record form is complete with layout, components, pressure, flow, zones, and schedule.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Emitters Show No Flow or Weak Flow During the Distribution Test

Probable cause: tubing was not flushed before final emitters were installed, leaving debris lodged in the emitter, or the filter is undersized for the verified flow.
Confirmation: depressurize the affected lateral, remove and inspect the emitter, and check the filter against the verified flow value on the irrigation record form.
Correction: flush the affected lateral again, clean or replace the emitter, and replace the filter if it does not match the verified flow.
Reinspection: repeat the leak check and distribution check for the corrected lateral.

A Fitting Leaks When the System Is Pressurized

Probable cause: the fitting was not fully seated during installation, or the tubing was not cut square before the fitting was installed.
Confirmation: depressurize the system, inspect the fitting and the cut end of the tubing for a square, fully seated connection.
Correction: cut a fresh square end if needed, reseat or replace the fitting, and reconnect per manufacturer instructions.
Reinspection: pressurize the system again and repeat the leak check at the corrected fitting.

The System Was Pressurized Before Backflow Protection Was Confirmed

Probable cause: the installation order in Section F, Step 6 was not followed, or the backflow device was staged but not actually installed before connection.
Confirmation: depressurize and inspect the supply-assembly connection to confirm whether the backflow device is physically installed and correctly oriented.
Correction: stop use of the system immediately, install the backflow device in the specified order, and confirm it before repressurizing.
Reinspection: repeat the backflow and connection-order check before continuing to Section G.

An Emitter Is Placed Too Close to a Trunk or Crown

Probable cause: emitter spacing was estimated by eye rather than measured against the specified clearance in the irrigation plan.
Confirmation: measure the distance from the emitter to the trunk or crown and compare it against the irrigation plan's specified clearance.
Correction: relocate the emitter to the specified clearance and plug the original punched opening.
Reinspection: repeat the emitter-placement check for the corrected location.

One Zone Shows Inconsistent Distribution Across the Bed

Probable cause: emitters with mismatched flow rates were installed on the same lateral, or the lateral exceeds the pressure or flow capacity verified in Section E.
Confirmation: compare installed emitter flow rates against the irrigation plan and recheck pressure at the affected lateral.
Correction: replace mismatched emitters with the specified type, or split the lateral into additional zones per the irrigation plan.
Reinspection: repeat the distribution check at representative locations on the corrected lateral.

A Service Fitting Was Buried During Mulching

Probable cause: mulch was applied without confirming the location of the backflow device, filter, regulator, shutoff, or timer.
Confirmation: locate each service fitting under the mulch layer and compare its accessibility against the irrigation plan.
Correction: remove mulch from around the buried fitting to restore the specified access clearance.
Reinspection: repeat the documentation check to confirm all service fittings remain accessible.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a tested, documented drip zone left in an accessible, serviceable condition, not with tubing and fittings left loose in the bed.

  • Remove tubing scraps, fitting packaging, and punch-tool plugs not installed in the system
  • Confirm the backflow device, filter, regulator, shutoff, and timer remain accessible after mulching
  • Coil and label any unused tubing or spare fittings retained for future repair
  • Store the completed irrigation record form and photographic record where later maintenance chapters can reference them
  • Confirm the shutoff is left in the position specified for handoff before leaving the site
