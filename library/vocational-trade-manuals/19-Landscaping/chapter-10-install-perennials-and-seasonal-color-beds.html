Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to lay out, install, water, and inspect one mixed perennial and seasonal-color planting bed. The finished work product is a planted bed with plants set at the approved spacing and crown depth, correct root condition, initial watering completed, mulch applied without burying crowns, and a documented planting and maintenance record.

You will review the plant schedule and bloom pattern, inspect incoming plants for vigor and disease, place plants in their containers to check the layout before digging, mark each location, dig holes suited to each root ball, correct compacted or circling roots, set perennials and annual color plants at the approved crown depth, backfill and water each plant, install or verify drip coverage where specified, mulch without covering crowns, and inspect the completed bed for spacing, pattern, depth, crown exposure, moisture, and visual uniformity.

This chapter builds directly on the readiness plan from Chapter 1, the protected work area from Chapter 2, the tested and amended soil from Chapter 3, the cleared bed from Chapter 4, the graded and drained bed from Chapter 5, the edged bed from Chapter 6, and the fabric and mulch decisions from Chapter 7. Do not install perennials or seasonal color in a bed that has not completed those prior chapters.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • LS-055 — Lay out plant spacing according to the approved plant schedule and mature spread.
  • LS-056 — Prepare planting holes suited to each root ball without damaging the prepared bed soil.
  • LS-057 — Install perennials at the approved crown depth with corrected roots.
  • LS-058 — Install annual color plants at the specified depth without burying crowns or stems.
  • LS-059 — Water new plantings during installation and verify irrigation coverage where specified.
  • LS-060 — Inspect bed uniformity, including spacing, pattern, depth, crown exposure, and moisture.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter follows the shrub and foundation-plant installation of Chapter 8 and the small-tree installation of Chapter 9, and it completes the plant-installation stage of the book before turf work begins in Chapter 11. The bed prepared through Chapters 3 through 7 and the shrub and tree placements from Chapters 8 and 9 control the remaining spacing available for perennials and seasonal color.

The completed, planted, watered, and mulched bed produced in this chapter becomes part of the base condition referenced by Chapter 14 for drip-irrigation installation where not already completed, Chapter 17 for pruning, Chapter 18 for fertilizing and maintenance, Chapter 19 for restoration, and the Chapter 20 capstone.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the approved plant schedule, mature spread, height layers, bloom sequence, sun, soil, water, color pattern, and maintenance requirements
  • inspecting perennial and annual plant stock for vigor, roots, pests, disease, labeling, and moisture
  • placing plants in containers to confirm the layout before any digging occurs
  • digging holes suited to each root ball and correcting compacted or circling roots
  • setting perennials at the approved crown depth and annual color plants at the specified depth
  • backfilling, firming, and watering each plant during installation
  • installing drip emitters or verifying irrigation coverage where specified by the approved design
  • applying mulch without covering crowns
  • documenting species, locations, and maintenance schedule
  • inspecting the finished bed for spacing, pattern, depth, crown exposure, moisture, and visual uniformity

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • install plants in a bed that has not passed the Chapter 1 readiness inspection, or the soil, vegetation-removal, grading, edging, and fabric or mulch preparation of Chapters 3 through 7
  • invent plant spacing, crown depth, or watering rates that are not supplied by the approved plant schedule, nursery tags, or irrigation design
  • install plants listed as invasive, prohibited, diseased, or unsuitable for the site
  • design or size a new drip-irrigation zone; this chapter only installs emitters or verifies coverage under an irrigation design supplied by Chapter 14 or the project's approved irrigation plan

Any plant rejected for disease, severe root-binding, or damage must be replaced before the bed passes the uniformity inspection in this chapter.

Trade Note

Perennial crowns and annual color-plant stems are more easily buried by careless backfilling or overzealous mulching than shrub or tree root flares are. A crown set even slightly below the surrounding grade collects water and mulch against the stem base, which is the most common cause of the bed-uniformity failures diagnosed later in this chapter.

Setup Standard

Do not dig a single planting hole until every plant in the group has been placed in its container at its planned location and the layout has been viewed from the primary viewing directions in Step 4. Do not backfill any hole until the crown or stem base of that plant has been verified against the approved depth in Step 11 or Step 12.

Shop Tip

Keep the nursery tag with each plant, or in the labeled bucket, until the planting and maintenance schedule in Step 21 is documented. A tag removed early is the most common cause of an undocumented species entry on the finished planting record.

Plant Selection, Layout, and Stop Conditions

Review the plant schedule, mature spread, height layers, bloom sequence, sun, soil, water, color pattern, and maintenance requirements before placing any plant. Inspect every plant for vigor, roots, pests, disease, labeling, and moisture before installation. Place all plants in containers according to the plan and view the layout from primary directions before digging any hole.

Measurement Cue — Confirm Spacing Before Digging

Measure spacing between container placements against the approved plant schedule before marking any hole. Adjust spacing to maintain mature coverage and required access to paths, drains, structures, and irrigation hardware.

Critical Safety Cue — Do Not Bury Crowns or Stems

Do not set a perennial crown or an annual stem base below the approved finished grade. A buried crown or stem collects moisture against living tissue and is a leading cause of rot and plant loss in the establishment period.

Field Safety Cue — Protect Knees, Hands, and Hydration

Use knee protection during sustained kneeling work and gloves when handling root balls and amended soil. Maintain hydration, shade, and rest appropriate to the season during extended planting sessions.

Code Alert — Invasive and Prohibited Plants

Confirm that no plant on the approved schedule is listed as invasive, prohibited, or locally restricted before installation. Local restrictions vary and must be verified rather than assumed.

E. Preparation — Schedule Review and Plant Inspection

  1. Review plant schedule, mature spread, height layers, bloom sequence, sun, soil, water, color pattern, and maintenance. Confirm the schedule matches the bed prepared through Chapters 3 through 7.
  2. Inspect plants for vigor, roots, pests, disease, labeling, and moisture. Set aside any plant showing wilting, breakage, severe root-binding, disease symptoms, or missing labeling for review before installation.

F. Layout Confirmation

  1. Place all plants in containers according to the plan. Position each container at its planned location without removing the plant from its pot.
  2. View the layout from primary directions. Check the arrangement from the main viewing points identified in the plant schedule or site plan.
  3. Adjust spacing to maintain mature coverage and access. Correct any placement that would crowd mature spread or block required access once plants reach full size.
  4. Keep plants clear of paths, drains, structures, and irrigation hardware. Reposition any container placed over a fixed feature or irrigation component.

G. Hole Preparation and Root Correction

  1. Mark each location. Use a flag, stake, or marking paint at each confirmed container position before removing any plant from its pot.
  2. Dig holes suited to the root ball. Size each hole to the width and depth of the individual root ball rather than a uniform hole size for all plants.
  3. Remove containers carefully. Support the base of the root ball and avoid pulling on stems or foliage during removal.
  4. Loosen or correct compacted roots using the approved method. Score, tease, or trim circling and compacted roots as appropriate to the species and root condition observed.

H. Setting Depth, Backfill, and Watering

  1. Set perennials at the approved crown depth. Position the crown at or slightly above the finished grade specified by the plant schedule or nursery tag.
  2. Set annual color plants at the specified depth. Match the container's original soil line to the surrounding finished grade unless the schedule specifies otherwise.
  3. Do not bury crowns or stems. Recheck depth immediately before backfilling each plant.
  4. Backfill and firm lightly. Fill around the root ball with prepared bed soil and firm gently without compacting the surrounding soil structure.
  5. Water each plant during installation. Apply water slowly at the base of each plant until the root zone is thoroughly moistened.

I. Irrigation, Mulch, and Documentation

  1. Install drip emitters or verify irrigation coverage where specified. Use the approved irrigation design; do not size or design a new zone as part of this chapter.
  2. Apply mulch without covering crowns. Spread mulch to the specified depth while keeping it clear of every crown and stem base.
  3. Remove tags only after documenting species and locations. Record each plant's species and location on the planting record before discarding or storing the tag.

J. Inspection and Closeout

  1. Inspect spacing, pattern, depth, crown exposure, moisture, and visual uniformity. Compare the completed bed against the approved plant schedule and the layout confirmed in Step 4.
  2. Replace weak or damaged plants. Remove and replace any plant showing wilting, breakage, or failure to establish, and repeat the applicable installation steps for the replacement.
  3. Document planting and maintenance schedule. Record species, quantity, locations, installation date, watering performed, and the follow-up maintenance schedule.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed perennial and seasonal-color bed using the same checks regardless of bed size or plant count.

  • Spacing check: confirm every plant matches the approved schedule's spacing before and after installation.
  • Depth check: confirm every crown and stem base sits at or above the approved finished grade with no burial.
  • Root check: confirm compacted or circling roots were corrected before backfilling.
  • Watering check: confirm every plant received thorough watering during installation.
  • Mulch check: confirm mulch depth is correct and clear of every crown and stem.
  • Uniformity check: confirm pattern, color grouping, and height layering match the approved schedule from primary viewing directions.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Perennial Crowns Show Rot Within Weeks of Installation

Probable cause: the crown was set below the approved finished grade during backfilling, or mulch was later pulled against the crown.
Confirmation: excavate lightly around the affected plant and compare the crown elevation to the surrounding finished grade.
Correction: lift the plant, rebuild the base with prepared soil to the correct elevation, reset the crown at the approved depth, and pull mulch back from the crown.
Reinspection: repeat the depth check and mulch check on the corrected plant and any neighboring plants installed the same day.

Bed Pattern Does Not Match the Approved Color or Height Layout

Probable cause: plants were dug and set without a final layout check from the primary viewing directions, or spacing drifted during digging.
Confirmation: compare the installed bed against the approved plant schedule and photograph of the confirmed layout from Step 4.
Correction: relocate misplaced plants while root balls remain intact, or replace plants that were damaged during relocation.
Reinspection: repeat the uniformity check from all primary viewing directions.

Newly Installed Plants Wilt Within the First Days

Probable cause: installation watering was insufficient to reach the full root zone, or root balls were allowed to dry out before planting.
Confirmation: check soil moisture at root-ball depth for the affected plants and compare against unaffected plants in the same bed.
Correction: water thoroughly and slowly at the base of each affected plant; replace any plant that does not recover.
Reinspection: repeat the watering check across the full bed and confirm irrigation coverage where drip emitters are installed.

Drip Coverage Does Not Reach Every Plant Group

Probable cause: emitters were installed by estimate rather than against the approved irrigation design, or plant spacing changed after the irrigation layout was set.
Confirmation: compare emitter locations against the approved irrigation design and observe distribution during a test run.
Correction: reposition or add emitters only within the scope of the approved design; refer any needed redesign to Chapter 14 or the irrigation contractor.
Reinspection: repeat the irrigation-coverage check for the corrected zone.

A Plant Species Is Missing from the Planting Record

Probable cause: a nursery tag was discarded or misplaced before the species and location were documented in Step 21.
Confirmation: compare the installed bed to the approved plant schedule and identify any plant without a matching record entry.
Correction: identify the species from remaining tags, labels, or the plant schedule and complete the missing record entry.
Reinspection: repeat the documentation review against the full plant schedule before closing out the bed.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a watered, mulched, documented bed ready for ongoing maintenance, not a site left with loose tags and empty containers.

  • Remove empty containers, flats, tags no longer needed for documentation, plastic, and packaging from the bed
  • Return unused plant stock to shaded, protected staging or to the nursery source as applicable
  • Store retained nursery tags with the completed planting record
  • Organize installation photographs to match the Plant Group Schedule
  • Confirm the bed drains away from the plants and that no tools or hoses remain in walkways

Performance Check
