Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to lay out and plant a controlled group of shrubs or foundation plants at the mature spacing, root-flare depth, and building clearance approved in the plant schedule. The finished work product is one planted shrub group with the root flare exposed at approved grade, corrected root systems, stable backfill, an initial watering basin only where specified, mulch clear of stems, and a completed planting record.

You will review the plant schedule and mature-size data, inspect incoming plants for tags, root balls, pests, and moisture, place plants in their containers at planned locations before digging, verify mature clearance from buildings and utilities, mark and dig each hole, inspect and correct roots, set the root flare at approved grade, backfill, water, mulch, and document the finished planting.

This chapter builds directly on the readiness plan from Chapter 1, the bed preparation from Chapters 3 through 6, and the fabric and mulch decisions from Chapter 7. It supplies the shrub-planting competencies required for the Chapter 20 capstone.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • LS-043 — Lay out shrub spacing according to the approved plant schedule and mature-size data.
  • LS-044 — Dig planting holes to the approved width and root-flare depth.
  • LS-045 — Correct root-bound, circling, kinked, or girdling roots using the approved method.
  • LS-046 — Set the planting depth so the root flare sits at approved finished grade.
  • LS-047 — Backfill properly in controlled lifts without burying the root flare.
  • LS-048 — Water in new shrubs thoroughly and document the planting.

Where This Skill Fits

This chapter depends on the readiness plan from Chapter 1, the prepared and amended soil from Chapters 3 and 5, the defined and edged bed from Chapter 6, and the fabric and mulch decisions from Chapter 7. The bed you plant in this chapter must already carry positive drainage and an accepted grade before any hole is dug.

The planted shrub group produced here becomes part of the planting record referenced in Chapter 17 pruning, Chapter 18 fertilizing and maintenance, Chapter 19 restoration, and the Chapter 20 capstone, which repeats this exact sequence at full project scale.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • reviewing the plant schedule, mature size, sun, soil, water, and clearance requirements before planting
  • inspecting incoming plant tags, root balls, stems, leaves, pests, damage, and moisture
  • laying out plant locations in their containers and verifying spacing and clearance before digging
  • digging holes to the approved width and no deeper than required for root-flare placement
  • inspecting and correcting root-bound, circling, kinked, or girdling roots using the approved method
  • setting the root flare at approved finished grade and backfilling in controlled lifts
  • watering in new shrubs and applying mulch clear of stems
  • documenting species, quantity, location, and initial maintenance

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • dig any planting hole before utility locating from Chapter 1 is confirmed valid for the bed area
  • plant a shrub whose mature size the plant schedule has not verified against building, utility, path, or sight-line clearance
  • invent a planting depth, spacing dimension, or backfill soil mix not supplied by the approved plant schedule, nursery tag, or bed soil design
  • plant species listed as invasive, prohibited, or locally restricted, regardless of availability or price
  • proceed with diseased, severely root-bound beyond correction, broken, dried, or mislabeled stock

Any plant or planting location that fails inspection under this chapter must be corrected, replaced, or referred before backfilling proceeds.

Trade Note

The single most common cause of shrub failure documented in this chapter's troubleshooting section is planting too deep. The top of the soil inside a nursery container is frequently above the true root flare. Locate the actual root flare on every plant before setting depth; never assume the container soil line is correct.

Setup Standard

Do not dig any planting hole until utility locating from Chapter 1 is confirmed valid for the bed area and every plant has been placed in its container at the planned location and reviewed from the primary viewing points. Do not backfill any hole until the root flare has been located and any circling, kinked, or girdling roots have been corrected.

Shop Tip

Place every plant, still in its container, at its planned location before digging any hole. Reviewing the full layout from the street, walkway, and window views first prevents the wasted labor of digging a hole and then discovering the spacing or clearance is wrong.

Layout Before Digging

Place every plant in its container at the planned location before digging any hole. Review spacing from multiple viewing points and verify mature clearance from buildings, windows, paths, utilities, and other plants against the plant schedule. Confirm utility clearance before digging any hole.

Measurement Cue — Verify Spacing Against the Plant Schedule, Not by Eye

Measure spacing between planned plant centers against the plant schedule's mature-spread figures. A layout that looks correct at the current container size is frequently too tight once the plants reach mature spread.

Critical Safety Cue — Confirm Utility Clearance Before Digging Any Hole

Do not dig any planting hole until utility locating from Chapter 1 is confirmed valid for the specific location. Contact with an unmarked utility line can cause serious injury, fire, explosion, or service disruption.

Field Safety Cue — Lift Root Balls With Your Legs, Not Your Back

Lift balled or containerized shrubs by bending at the knees and keeping the load close to the body. Use two-person lifting or a cart for larger root balls rather than dragging by the stem or trunk.

Code Alert — Confirm the Species Is Not Invasive or Restricted

Verify the plant schedule's species list against local invasive-species and planting restrictions before installation. Requirements vary by jurisdiction and must be verified rather than assumed.

E. Plant Review and Layout

  1. Review plant schedule, mature size, sun, soil, water, foundation clearance, utility clearance, and maintenance access. Confirm the schedule is the approved, current version.
  2. Inspect plant tags, root balls, stems, leaves, pests, damage, and moisture. Record condition of each plant on the Shrub Planting Record.
  3. Reject diseased, severely root-bound, broken, dried, or mislabeled plants. Set rejected plants aside and note the reason and replacement plan.
  4. Place plants in containers at planned locations. Do not dig any hole before this step is complete.
  5. Review spacing from multiple viewing points. Walk the primary viewing directions, including the street, entrance, and windows.
  6. Verify mature clearance from buildings, windows, paths, utilities, and other plants. Compare measured distances against the plant schedule's mature-size figures.
  7. Mark each planting hole. Use a flag or paint mark at the center of each planned hole.

F. Excavation and Root Correction

  1. Confirm utility clearance before digging. Verify locating markings remain valid for the marked hole locations.
  2. Dig holes to the approved width and no deeper than required for root-flare placement. Keep excavated soil separate from mulch and debris.
  3. Keep excavated soil separate from mulch and debris. Stage excavated soil on a tarp or in a wheelbarrow beside the hole.
  4. Remove the plant from its container without pulling the stem. Support the root ball from underneath while removing the container.
  5. Inspect root flare and root system. Remove excess container soil from the top of the root ball if it obscures the true root flare.
  6. Remove circling, kinked, girdling, dead, or compacted outer roots using the approved method. Cut or tease roots according to the nursery tag or plant-specific guidance supplied with the plant schedule.
  7. Remove wire, twine, tags, and container material. Confirm no wire basket, string, or plastic remains against the trunk or roots.

G. Setting, Backfilling, Watering, and Documentation

  1. Set the plant so the root flare is at the approved finished grade. Check the flare elevation against the surrounding prepared bed soil, not against the hole's excavated depth.
  2. Rotate the best face toward the viewing direction. Confirm the orientation before backfilling begins.
  3. Backfill with approved soil in controlled lifts. Add soil in layers, lightly firming each layer before adding the next.
  4. Eliminate large air pockets without overcompaction. Work backfill soil gently around the root ball rather than tamping heavily.
  5. Do not bury the root flare or stem. Recheck flare elevation after each backfill lift.
  6. Build only the specified watering basin. Construct a basin only where the plant schedule calls for one.
  7. Water slowly and thoroughly. Allow water to soak in before adding more, and confirm water reaches the root ball rather than running off.
  8. Add mulch while keeping it clear of stems. Apply mulch to the Chapter 7 specified depth, leaving a clear ring at the stem.
  9. Inspect plant depth, spacing, vertical alignment, root flare, watering, and building clearance. Confirm every acceptance standard in Section D is met for each plant.
  10. Document species, quantity, location, and initial maintenance. Complete the Shrub Planting Record with planting date and any follow-up watering or inspection schedule.

Common Inspection Review

Review every completed shrub planting using the same checks regardless of species or bed location.

  • Spacing and clearance check: confirm every plant location matches the approved plant schedule's mature spacing and clearance.
  • Root-flare check: confirm every root flare is exposed and set at approved finished grade.
  • Root-correction check: confirm every identified circling, kinked, or girdling root has been corrected.
  • Backfill check: confirm controlled lifts were used and no large air pockets or overcompaction remain.
  • Watering and mulch check: confirm every plant was watered thoroughly and mulch is clear of stems.
  • Documentation check: confirm the Shrub Planting Record is complete for every plant installed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Shrub Shows Poor Growth or Dieback Weeks After Planting

Probable cause: the root flare was buried during backfilling because the container soil line was used as the depth reference instead of the true root flare.
Confirmation: excavate soil away from the base of the plant and check whether the root flare is below the surrounding grade.
Correction: carefully remove soil down to the true root flare, replant at the corrected depth if the flare is significantly buried, and reapply mulch clear of the stem.
Reinspection: repeat the root-flare check on the corrected plant and on any other plant installed in the same batch.

A Shrub Rocks Loosely or Leans After Watering

Probable cause: backfill was placed in a single lift without firming, leaving large air pockets around the root ball.
Confirmation: gently probe the backfill around the base of the plant to check for voids.
Correction: add backfill in controlled lifts, lightly firming each layer, without overcompacting the soil.
Reinspection: repeat the backfill check and confirm the plant stands upright without support.

Mature Shrubs Crowd Each Other or the Building Sooner Than Expected

Probable cause: spacing was set by eye against the current container size rather than measured against the plant schedule's mature-spread figures.
Confirmation: measure current plant-to-plant and plant-to-building distances and compare them to the mature-spread values in the plant schedule.
Correction: relocate or remove plants that will conflict at maturity, and update the Shrub Planting Record with the corrected layout.
Reinspection: repeat the spacing and clearance check for the entire planted group.

A Plant Declines Despite Correct Depth and Watering

Probable cause: a circling or girdling root was not corrected before backfilling and is now constricting the plant's vascular tissue as it grows.
Confirmation: carefully excavate around the root ball and inspect the root structure for uncorrected circling or girdling roots.
Correction: correct the identified root using the approved method if the plant can be safely excavated, or refer the plant for removal and replacement if correction is not practical at this stage.
Reinspection: repeat the root-correction check on remaining plants from the same nursery batch.

Mulch Volcano Forms Against the Stem

Probable cause: mulch was applied without maintaining the clear ring around the stem specified in Chapter 7.
Confirmation: inspect mulch depth directly against the stem and compare it to the surrounding bed depth.
Correction: pull mulch back from the stem to restore the specified clearance without exposing the root flare.
Reinspection: repeat the watering and mulch check for the corrected plant and any others in the same bed.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a watered, mulched planting and a completed record retained for later maintenance chapters, not filed away unused.

  • Remove empty containers, tags no longer needed for reference, twine, wire, and packaging from the bed
  • Return unused backfill soil and mulch to designated staging per Chapter 2
  • Organize planting photographs by plant location to match the Shrub Planting Record
  • Store the completed Shrub Planting Record where Chapters 17, 18, 19, and 20 can reference it
  • Confirm the bed remains protected from traffic while plants establish

Field Terms

Root Flare
The point where the trunk or main stem widens into the top of the root system; the reference point for finished planting depth.
Finished Grade
The elevation of the surrounding prepared bed soil at the planting hole.
Mature Clearance
The verified distance between a plant's mature spread or height and the nearest building, window, path, utility, or other plant.
Girdling Root
A root that circles the trunk or crosses other roots and will constrict the plant's vascular tissue as it grows if uncorrected.
Watering Basin
A shallow ring of soil built around a newly planted shrub only where specified, used to direct water to the root zone.
Backfill Lift
One controlled layer of backfill soil placed and lightly firmed before the next layer is added.
Shrub Planting Record
The document recording species, quantity, location, planting date, and initial maintenance for the planted group.
