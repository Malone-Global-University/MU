# Book 22 Chapter Outline

# Property Maintenance and Building Inspection: Preventive Maintenance, Condition Assessment, and Repair Planning

Book 22 should transition from repair/remodeling into the recurring work of **keeping a property functional, safe, dry, efficient, and well-documented**. The sequence should move from **inspection setup → exterior systems → interior systems → mechanical checks → safety systems → seasonal maintenance → repair planning**, ending with a complete property inspection and maintenance plan.

I would use **MI** for this book: **Maintenance and Inspection**.

## Scope Note

Book 22 is not just paperwork. It should teach the reader how to **walk a property, find developing problems early, perform basic preventive maintenance, and convert findings into an actionable repair plan**.

The standing rule should be:

**Inspect regularly, document clearly, correct small issues early, prioritize safety and water control first, then maintain the property on a schedule.**

Chapters should flag:

* Ladder safety during roof, gutter, and exterior inspection
* Electrical hazards around panels, outlets, fixtures, and damaged wiring
* Plumbing leaks, shutoff failures, and water-damage indicators
* Foundation cracks, movement, and drainage failures
* Mold, pest activity, rot, and concealed moisture
* Combustion appliance safety and carbon monoxide risk
* Dryer vent lint and fire risk
* Smoke alarm and CO alarm replacement intervals
* Lead, asbestos, and other older-home hazards
* When inspection findings require a licensed contractor, structural engineer, electrician, plumber, HVAC technician, pest-control professional, or environmental specialist.
