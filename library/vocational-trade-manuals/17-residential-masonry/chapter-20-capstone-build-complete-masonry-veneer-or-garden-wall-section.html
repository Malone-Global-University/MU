Job Overview

This capstone chapter integrates every competency taught in Chapters 1 through 19 into one closed-out masonry section. The finished work product is one complete, cured, drained, aligned, and documented masonry veneer practice section or nonretaining garden-wall section, selected under the Capstone Continuity Rule established in the book outline.

You will select one approved capstone option — a drained masonry-veneer practice section built on an approved practice foundation, engineered veneer practice wall, or slab section completed through Book 16, or a small nonretaining garden-wall section built on an approved footing — and carry it through readiness inspection, work-area staging, material inventory, layout, base preparation, water-management installation where applicable, mortar mixing, unit placement, cutting, reinforcement or tie placement, joint tooling, cleaning, curing, final inspection, and closeout.

This is the last chapter in Book 17. Nothing in this chapter introduces a new structural design, a new water-management detail, or a new material not already specified by the approved capstone documents identified in Step 2. This chapter does not authorize retaining-wall, load-bearing, chimney, fireplace, or structural-pier work beyond what has already been taught and approved in earlier chapters.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RM-115 — Integrate layout, establishing the wall baseline, ends, corners, openings, movement joints, finished elevations, story poles, and course marks for the selected capstone option.
  • RM-116 — Integrate base preparation, mortar mixing, and unit placement, accepting the base or support, mixing mortar to the specified consistency, and laying units in the specified bond with full bed and head joints.
  • RM-117 — Integrate cutting, reinforcement or ties, and flashing and weeps, cutting units using Chapter 10 controls and installing reinforcement, ties, flashing, end dams, and weeps exactly as specified for the selected option.
  • RM-118 — Integrate joint tooling and cleaning, tooling joints at the correct firmness and cleaning faces without smearing or aggressive chemicals.
  • RM-119 — Perform final inspection, verifying support, line, level, plumb, bond, joint fullness, tooling, cuts, reinforcement or ties, flashing, weeps, cavity or drainage condition, cap, and a completed deficiency and reinspection record.
  • RM-120 — Complete masonry closeout, compiling layout, batch, mortar, grout, reinforcement, tie, flashing, weep, weather, cleaning, photograph, and inspection records and handing off the completed section for Book 18 hardscaping coordination.

Where This Skill Fits

Every chapter in this book contributed one piece of the sequence this chapter now integrates: Chapter 1 readiness inspection, Chapter 2 work-area staging, Chapter 3 mortar mixing, Chapter 4 layout, Chapters 5 through 7 block laying and reinforcement, Chapters 8 through 10 brick laying and cutting, Chapters 11 through 13 veneer and water management, Chapters 14 and 15 repair judgment, Chapters 16 and 17 garden-wall and step geometry, Chapter 18 chimney inspection boundaries, and Chapter 19 cleaning and sealing.

The completed, documented section produced in this chapter is handed off directly to Book 18 for hardscaping coordination. The support, drainage, reinforcement or tie design, movement strategy, and inspection sequence you document here become the starting condition for that later work.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • selecting one approved capstone option: a drained masonry-veneer practice section or a small nonretaining garden-wall section
  • integrating the readiness inspection, work-area staging, and material inventory sequence from Chapters 1 and 2
  • establishing layout, story poles, and course marks using Chapter 4 methods
  • preparing or accepting the base, footing, or support and installing water-management details for the veneer option using Chapters 11 through 13 methods
  • mixing mortar using Chapter 3 methods and laying units using Chapters 5, 6, 8, and 9 methods as applicable to the selected option
  • cutting units using Chapter 10 controls and installing reinforcement, ties, flashing, and weeps at the specified stages
  • tooling joints, cleaning faces, and protecting and curing the completed section using Chapters 14 and 19 methods
  • performing a complete final inspection and preparing a deficiency list, reinspection record, and closeout package

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • design a new structural, reinforcement, flashing, or drainage detail not already supplied by the approved capstone documents identified in Step 2
  • proceed on a retaining wall, load-bearing wall, foundation wall, chimney, fireplace, or structural pier under this chapter
  • select the capstone option without confirming the exact support, drainage, reinforcement or tie design, movement strategy, and inspection sequence required by the Capstone Continuity Rule
  • skip any earlier-chapter inspection hold point because this is the final project in the book

Any base, support, or prior condition that fails the readiness inspection in this chapter must be documented, corrected by a qualified party where required, and reinspected before capstone work begins.

Trade Note

This chapter does not teach any new laying, mixing, cutting, reinforcement, flashing, or cleaning technique. Every technique used here was already taught in an earlier chapter. Where this chapter says "using Chapter 3 methods" or "using Chapter 11 methods," return to that chapter's numbered procedure, acceptance standards, and troubleshooting rather than improvising a shortcut because the capstone project feels familiar.

Common Field Fundamentals

The capstone project follows the same support-to-closeout sequence established in the book outline's System-Sequence Control: base, drainage, movement, structural role, and weather exposure are resolved before mortar or units are staged; layout, corners, leads, story poles, course heights, openings, reinforcement, flashing, and joints are established before production wall work; reinforcement, ties, lintels, flashing, and grout or cavity spaces are inspected before concealment; and cleaning follows sufficient cure using the least aggressive compatible method.

Capstone Option Selection and Stop-Work Judgment

The capstone option recorded in Step 1 controls every later step in this chapter. Do not mix the component schedule, reinforcement design, or water-management system of one option with the other. A veneer practice section and a garden-wall section are built, inspected, and closed out using separate sequences within this chapter, and the learner completes one option, not a combination of both, unless the book outline for a specific program explicitly authorizes both.

Measurement Cue — Reissue the Story Pole for This Project

Build a new story pole for the capstone section using the actual unit height and specified bed-joint thickness for the selected option's units. Do not reuse a story pole built for a different chapter's practice wall, even if the unit type appears similar.

Critical Safety Cue — Unresolved Readiness Findings

Do not begin base preparation or mortar mixing if the reissued Chapter 1 readiness decision for this capstone project is Rejected, or if any stop-work condition identified in that inspection remains unresolved. Stop work immediately if settlement, active movement, or an unidentified structural condition appears in the practice foundation or base during capstone work, and refer it to the responsible design or inspection authority.

Field Safety Cue — Multi-Day Protection

Because this project spans multiple working days, protect uncured mortar, exposed reinforcement, open cavities, and unfinished flashing at the end of every work session using the specified covers, and reinspect protection at the start of the next session before resuming work.

Code Alert — Confirm Professional Design and Inspection Responsibilities

Confirm the professional design and inspection responsibilities identified in Step 3 before proceeding. Even a practice-scale capstone section follows the reinforcement, flashing, and inspection requirements of its controlling documents; a project labeled "practice" does not exempt it from the acceptance standards in this chapter.

E. Selection, Readiness, and Staging

  1. Select the approved capstone option: drained masonry veneer or small nonretaining garden wall. Record the selection on the Capstone Identification Record before proceeding.
  2. Obtain the complete support, drainage, reinforcement or tie, flashing, joint, finish, weather, permit, and inspection documents. Confirm every document is the current approved version for the selected option.
  3. Confirm professional design and inspection responsibilities. Record the name and contact information of the engineer, architect, or building department of record.
  4. Perform the Chapter 1 readiness inspection. Reissue the Masonry-Readiness Worksheet specifically for this capstone project and confirm an Approved or Conditional decision before staging.
  5. Prepare the Chapter 2 work area, staging, scaffold, mixing, silica, washout, and weather controls. Confirm every Chapter 2 acceptance standard is met before materials are unloaded.
  6. Inspect and inventory units, mortar, grout, reinforcement, ties, flashing, weeps, caps, cleaners, and protection. Cross-reference every item against the Component Schedule in Section A.

F. Layout and Base Preparation

  1. Establish wall baseline, ends, corners, openings, movement joints, and finished elevations. Use Chapter 4 baseline and reference methods.
  2. Dry-lay units and verify module. Adjust layout to minimize small cuts while preserving the specified joint width.
  3. Create story poles and course marks. Build a new story pole using the actual unit height and specified bed-joint thickness for this project.
  4. Prepare and accept the base, ledge, footing, or support. Confirm the base or footing acceptance hold point in Section D before proceeding.
  5. For the veneer option, inspect and repair the WRB. Confirm continuity across sheathing, penetrations, and transitions using Chapter 11 methods.
  6. Install base and through-wall flashing. Follow the shingle-lap sequence specified in the controlling documents.
  7. Form end dams and exterior outlets. Extend flashing to the required outlet using Chapter 13 methods.
  8. Install cavity drainage material and weeps. Keep the cavity free of mortar bridging.
  9. Install veneer ties at approved supports. Maintain the specified spacing, embedment, and slope.
  10. For the garden-wall option, prepare and compact the base or footing. Verify base width, level, and elevation against the controlling documents.
  11. Install reinforcement or connectors where specified. Install only the reinforcement, pins, adhesive, or connectors named in the controlling documents.
  12. Complete required pre-lay or pre-grout inspections. Confirm the pre-lay and applicable pre-concealment hold points in Section D before mixing mortar.

G. Mortar, Placement, Cutting, and Concealed Details

  1. Mix mortar using Chapter 3. Batch, test, and document mortar consistency before laying.
  2. Build corner leads or end controls. Use Chapter 6 or Chapter 9 methods as applicable to the unit type.
  3. Set the mason's line. Use Chapter 4 line-setting methods for each course.
  4. Lay the first course to line, level, elevation, and full bearing. Confirm bearing on the accepted base or footing.
  5. Lay subsequent courses in the specified bond. Maintain the bond pattern recorded in the controlling documents.
  6. Maintain full bed and head joints. Avoid excessive tapping that squeezes out the bed.
  7. Keep courses level and the wall plumb. Check at the intervals specified in Chapters 5, 6, 8, and 9.
  8. Maintain the veneer cavity or garden-wall drainage condition. Keep the cavity or drainage path clear of mortar droppings throughout laying.
  9. Install ties, joint reinforcement, vertical reinforcement, grout, embeds, or caps at the specified stages. Do not conceal any of these items until the applicable pre-concealment inspection has passed.
  10. Cut units using Chapter 10 controls. Maintain wet-cutting or extraction controls for every cut.
  11. Maintain movement joints and isolation. Do not rigidly bond across a movement joint identified in the controlling documents.

H. Tooling, Cleaning, and Protection

  1. Tool joints at the correct firmness. Tool when thumbprint hard or at the condition specified for the unit type.
  2. Clean faces without smearing or aggressive chemicals. Use only the cleaning method approved in Chapter 19 for the cured mortar age.
  3. Protect the work from rain, freezing, rapid drying, heat, and impact. Apply covers at the end of every work session.
  4. Maintain curing protection. Reinspect protection at the start of each subsequent work session.

I. Final Inspection, Correction, and Closeout

  1. Inspect support, line, level, plumb, bond, joint fullness, tooling, cuts, reinforcement or ties, flashing, weeps, cavity, cap, and drainage. Use the Final Inspection standard in Section D.
  2. Perform limited drainage observation where the veneer mockup plan permits it. Record the method and result.
  3. Prepare a deficiency list. Compile every item that does not meet the acceptance standards into a single written list with location, description, and required correction.
  4. Correct and reinspect every item. Return to each corrected deficiency and verify the correction using the same tool or method used in the original inspection.
  5. Complete final compatible cleaning after sufficient cure. Confirm the cure period required before cleaning has elapsed.
  6. Apply sealer only when specifically approved. Do not apply sealer to correct a missing flashing, drainage, or structural deficiency.
  7. Compile layout, batch, mortar, grout, reinforcement, tie, flashing, weep, weather, cleaning, photographs, inspections, and corrections. Assemble the complete closeout package.
  8. Provide maintenance, cleaning, drainage, crack-monitoring, and sealant information. Record recommended monitoring intervals and referral conditions.
  9. Hand off the completed section for Book 18 hardscaping coordination. Confirm receipt of the closeout package by the responsible party.

Common Final Inspection

Inspect every completed capstone section using the same checks regardless of the selected option.

  • Option check: confirm the selected capstone option was recorded before staging began and that no component, reinforcement, or water-management detail from the other option was substituted.
  • Base and layout check: confirm base acceptance, story pole, course marks, and mason's-line records are complete.
  • Placement check: confirm bond, full joints, level, plumb, and alignment meet the acceptance standards.
  • Concealed-detail check: confirm every reinforcement, tie, flashing, weep, or connector was photographed and inspected before concealment.
  • Closeout check: confirm the deficiency list, reinspection record, and compiled closeout package are complete and handed off.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Concealed Detail Was Covered Before Inspection

Probable cause: laying continued past the pre-concealment hold point without stopping for inspection and photographs.
Confirmation: compare the closeout package against the Section D hold-point list; a missing pre-concealment photograph or inspection record confirms the condition.
Correction: where physically possible, expose the concealed detail for inspection and photography; where exposure would damage the completed work, document the omission as an unresolved deficiency and obtain professional evaluation before final closeout.
Reinspection: repeat the affected hold-point inspection and update the closeout package.

Components From Both Capstone Options Were Mixed

Probable cause: a reinforcement, tie, or water-management detail from the unselected option was installed alongside the selected option's components.
Confirmation: compare the as-built section against the Component Schedule for the selected option only.
Correction: remove or correct the mismatched component so the section matches a single option's controlling documents.
Reinspection: repeat the affected placement and pre-concealment inspections.

The Story Pole or Layout Was Reused From an Earlier Chapter's Practice Wall

Probable cause: an existing story pole or layout mark was reused without verifying unit height and joint thickness for this specific capstone project.
Confirmation: measure actual course heights against the reused story pole; a mismatch confirms the condition.
Correction: build a new story pole using the actual units and specified joint thickness for this project and remark course elevations.
Reinspection: repeat the pre-lay layout inspection using the corrected story pole.

The Deficiency List Was Closed Without Reinspection

Probable cause: a corrected item was marked resolved based on visual impression rather than repeating the original inspection tool or method.
Confirmation: compare the deficiency list's correction entries against reinspection dates and methods; a missing reinspection date confirms the condition.
Correction: reinspect every corrected item using the same tool or method as the original inspection before closeout.
Reinspection: record the completed reinspection date and result for each item.

Sealer Was Applied to Mask a Drainage or Structural Deficiency

Probable cause: a sealer was applied in Step 39 before the underlying flashing, drainage, or structural deficiency identified in the final inspection was corrected.
Confirmation: compare the deficiency list against the sealer application date; a sealer applied before a related deficiency's reinspection date confirms the condition.
Correction: remove the sealer if compatible removal is possible, correct the underlying deficiency, reinspect, and reapply sealer only if it remains specifically approved.
Reinspection: confirm the underlying deficiency passes reinspection before any sealer is reapplied.

  • Store professional referral correspondence and hold-point inspection records with the package

  • Retain the closeout package and photographs for handoff to Book 18 hardscaping coordination

Pre-Concealment Inspection

Closeout Package
The compiled layout, batch, mortar, grout, reinforcement, tie, flashing, weep, weather, cleaning, photograph, and inspection record produced at the end of this chapter.
End Dam
A formed termination in flashing that prevents water from flowing off the end of the flashing run.
Rack-Back
A stepped reduction in a corner lead used to control course elevation while the lead is built ahead of adjacent work.
Drainage Cavity
The air space behind masonry veneer that carries incidental water to weeps and flashing.
Movement Joint
A designed gap that allows masonry to move without cracking, which must not be rigidly bonded across.
Handoff
The documented transfer of the completed, closed-out section to the responsible party for Book 18 hardscaping coordination.
