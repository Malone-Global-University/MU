Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install reinforcement, cleanouts, and grout in one approved concrete-masonry practice wall section and to inspect the completed assembly before and after grouting. The finished work product is one grouted, reinforced block-wall practice section with vertical and, where specified, horizontal or bond-beam reinforcement correctly positioned, clean unobstructed cells, closed cleanouts, fully consolidated grout at the specified lift height, and a completed reinforcement-and-grout inspection record.

This project depends on the block wall you built and inspected in Chapter 5 and the plumb, bonded corner or intersection you built in Chapter 6. You will use the same base, layout, and wall you have already accepted, and you will add the reinforcement and grout that the approved design or the training assembly specifies.

This chapter draws a firm line between structural practice and nonstructural training, and requires you to state which one applies before any bar is placed. Every step in this chapter — bar placement, cleanout inspection, grout mixing, lift-height control, and consolidation — must be documented as it happens, because reinforcement and grout fill cannot be reinspected once the wall is closed and cured.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RM-037 — Place vertical reinforcement, including bars, dowels, laps, cover, positioners, and cell alignment as specified.
  • RM-038 — Place horizontal reinforcement where required, including joint reinforcement and bond-beam reinforcement.
  • RM-039 — Clean block cells, installing cleanouts where required and removing mortar droppings and debris before grouting.
  • RM-040 — Mix grout to the approved consistency and confirm it is compatible with the specified assembly.
  • RM-041 — Fill cells, placing and consolidating grout within the permitted lift height without displacing reinforcement or blowing out block shells.
  • RM-042 — Inspect the reinforced wall section, confirming fill completeness, reinforcement position, embeds, absence of blowouts, and wall alignment.

Where This Skill Fits

Chapter 5 taught you to build a plumb, bonded block wall, and Chapter 6 taught you to build a plumb, bonded corner or intersection. This chapter adds the reinforcement and grout that turn a stacked block assembly into a reinforced masonry section — the same sequence used later in Chapter 11 and Chapter 12 veneer ties and embeds, and in the Chapter 20 capstone reinforcement and grout steps.

The cleanout, inspection-before-concealment, and lift-height discipline you practice here is the same discipline required anywhere reinforcement, ties, anchors, flashing, or embedded items must be inspected before they are hidden by grout, mortar, or veneer.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • placing vertical bars, dowels, laps, cover, and positioners in an approved or training block-wall section
  • placing horizontal joint reinforcement or bond-beam reinforcement where the wall design specifies it
  • installing cleanout openings, removing mortar droppings and debris, and verifying unobstructed cells
  • installing anchor bolts, embeds, sleeves, or connectors at specified locations
  • closing cleanouts using the approved method after inspection
  • mixing grout to the approved consistency and confirming compatibility with the assembly
  • placing grout within the permitted lift height and consolidating it by the specified method
  • inspecting fill completeness, reinforcement position, embeds, blowouts, and wall alignment

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • design a reinforcement schedule, bar size, lap length, grout strength, or cell-fill requirement; every value used in this chapter must come from an approved engineered design, adopted code, manufacturer instruction, or the training-assembly specification supplied with this chapter
  • treat this practice or training assembly as load-bearing or structural unless it is expressly identified as such, with the exact engineered design and inspection sequence supplied
  • substitute mortar for grout, or substitute an unspecified bar size, grout strength, or admixture for the one specified
  • close a cleanout, conceal reinforcement, or place grout before the required inspection of that item is complete

Any reinforcement, cleanout, or embed condition that fails inspection in this chapter must be corrected and reinspected before the cleanout is closed or grout is placed.

Trade Note

Reinforcement and grout are the two elements of this chapter that cannot be inspected again once the wall is closed. A bar out of position, a blocked cell, or a mortar dropping bridging the grout space is invisible in the finished wall and can only be found by inspecting before concealment. This is why this chapter requires photographs and a written inspection record before every cleanout is closed and before every lift is placed.

Setup Standard

Do not close a cleanout or place grout until every vertical bar, horizontal reinforcement item, positioner, embed, and cell has passed the pre-grout inspection in Step 18 and that inspection has been recorded with photographs. A cell that has not passed inspection must not be grouted.

Shop Tip

Photograph the full depth of every cell before closing its cleanout, not just the bar at the opening. A bar that looks correctly placed at the cleanout can still be out of position, missing its lap, or obstructed higher in the cell.

Structural Practice vs. Nonstructural Training

Record whether the selected wall section is built to an engineered structural design or is a nonstructural training assembly before any bar is placed. Do not treat a training assembly as load-bearing or structural, and do not proceed on a structural section without the exact engineered design and inspection sequence supplied. This classification controls the reinforcement schedule, grout strength, lap lengths, and inspection sequence used for the remainder of this chapter.

Measurement Cue — Record Bar Position Before Concealment

Record cover, lap length, positioner spacing, and cell alignment for every bar directly on the inspection record at the point it is placed. A bar recorded from memory after cleanouts are closed cannot be verified again.

Critical Safety Cue — Concealment Before Inspection

Do not close a cleanout, install a closure, or place grout over any reinforcement, embed, or cell that has not passed the required pre-grout inspection. Do not exceed the permitted lift height. Excessive lift height increases grout pressure inside the cell and can blow out block shells, displace reinforcement, or cause personal injury from failed formwork or bulging cells.

Field Safety Cue — Grout Handling and Consolidation

Wear waterproof gloves and eye protection whenever handling wet grout. Keep hands and body clear of the grout stream during pumped or chuted placement. Do not stand directly over a cell being vibrated or consolidated. Wash grout from skin immediately.

Code Alert — Reinforcement and Grout Inspection Requirements

Confirm whether the selected wall section requires a permit, engineer review, or inspector approval of reinforcement and cleanouts before grouting. Requirements vary by jurisdiction and by whether the assembly is structural or a training exercise; verify locally before proceeding.

E. Preparation — Documents and Classification

  1. Review engineered or approved reinforcement, grout, cell, lap, cleanout, lift, inspection, and consolidation requirements. Confirm every document is the current approved version before reinforcement placement begins.
  2. State whether the assembly is structural practice or nonstructural training. Record this classification on the Project Identification Record before proceeding.
  3. Confirm the wall and base have passed inspection. Verify the Chapter 5 or Chapter 6 acceptance record for the selected wall section before adding reinforcement.

F. Reinforcement Placement

  1. Verify vertical bars, dowels, laps, cover, positioners, and cell alignment. Cut or confirm bar length, install positioners at the specified spacing, and secure laps with tie wire.
  2. Install horizontal joint reinforcement or bond-beam reinforcement where specified. Place at the specified course or elevation and secure against displacement.
  3. Maintain clearance from shell faces and mortar droppings. Check cover on every face of every bar before proceeding.
  4. Prevent excessive mortar projections into grout spaces during laying. Trim or remove any mortar protruding into the cell as courses are completed.

G. Cleanouts, Cell Inspection, and Embeds

  1. Install cleanout openings at required locations. Cut or remove the specified face shell at the base of each cell to be grouted.
  2. Remove mortar droppings and debris from cells. Use hand tools, a shop vacuum, or a water flush as specified, working from the cleanout.
  3. Verify cells are unobstructed from cleanout to grout height. Inspect with a flashlight and mirror, or by direct sightline, and photograph each verified cell.
  4. Install anchor bolts, embeds, sleeves, or connectors where specified. Secure each item at its specified location and elevation before grouting.
  5. Close cleanouts using the approved method after inspection. Do not close any cleanout until Step 18 is complete for that cell.

H. Grout Mixing and Placement

  1. Inspect grout ingredients, packaged grout, water, mixer, pump, chute, and placement tools. Confirm materials are free of contamination and equipment is clean and functional.
  2. Mix grout to the approved consistency. Follow the specified proportions or packaged-product instructions and record the batch.
  3. Do not substitute mortar for grout. Confirm the product identification before mixing or placing.
  4. Place grout within the permitted lift height. Measure and record the lift height as it is placed.
  5. Consolidate using the specified mechanical or reconsolidation method. Vibrate or rod each lift as specified, and reconsolidate after initial water loss where required.
  6. Avoid displacing reinforcement or blowing out block shells. Watch for shell bulging or bar movement during placement and stop immediately if either occurs.
  7. Refill settlement at the top of the lift as required. Add grout to compensate for consolidation settlement before the surface sets.
  8. Finish grout at bond beams or top cells to the specified elevation. Strike off or finish the exposed grout surface as specified.

I. Cleanup, Cure Protection, and Final Inspection

  1. Clean spills from faces. Remove grout smears from block faces before they harden.
  2. Protect the grouted wall during cure. Cover or shield the section from rain, freezing, rapid drying, and impact for the specified cure period.
  3. Inspect fill completeness, reinforcement position, embeds, blowouts, and wall alignment. Compare the finished section against the Acceptance Standards table and record the result.
  4. Document grout batch, placement time, lift, consolidation, and inspection. Complete the Reinforcement-and-Grout Inspection Record and file it with the wall's other project records.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every reinforced and grouted wall section using the same checks regardless of whether it is structural practice or nonstructural training.

  • Classification check: confirm the structural-practice-or-training classification was recorded before reinforcement was placed and was not changed afterward.
  • Reinforcement check: confirm bar size, position, cover, lap, and positioner engagement match the approved design or training specification and are photographed before concealment.
  • Cell check: confirm every cell to be grouted was verified clean and unobstructed from cleanout to grout height before that cleanout was closed.
  • Grout check: confirm the grout batch record shows the approved mix, and that mortar was never substituted for grout.
  • Placement check: confirm every lift stayed within the permitted height and was consolidated by the specified method.
  • Final check: confirm fill completeness, absence of blowouts, and wall alignment are recorded, with any deficiency corrected and reinspected before the record is closed.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Cleanout Was Closed Before the Cell Was Verified Clean

Probable cause: the cell was assumed clean based on the cleanout opening view rather than a full-depth inspection with a flashlight and mirror.
Confirmation: compare the inspection record for a photograph and description of the full cell depth; a closed cleanout with no full-depth photograph indicates an incomplete inspection.
Correction: reopen the cleanout, remove debris, and reinspect the full cell depth before closing it again.
Reinspection: repeat the cell-cleanliness check and photograph the result before grouting.

A Block Shell Blew Out During Grout Placement

Probable cause: the lift height exceeded the permitted maximum, the block was undersupported, or consolidation pressure was applied to an already-full cell.
Confirmation: measure the lift height at the time of failure against the permitted maximum and inspect adjacent cells for similar overfilling.
Correction: stop grouting, remove displaced grout and damaged block, replace the block, and reduce lift height for subsequent placements.
Reinspection: reinspect the repaired cell and adjacent cells for reinforcement position and cell cleanliness before regrouting.

Reinforcement Shifted Out of Position During Grouting

Probable cause: positioners were missing, inadequately spaced, or displaced by grout pressure or consolidation.
Confirmation: compare the pre-grout inspection photograph against the bar's position visible at the top of the cell or through a cleanout, where accessible.
Correction: where the cell has not yet cured, correct bar position and add positioners before continuing; where grout has cured with a bar out of tolerance, document the condition and refer it to the responsible design authority for a structural assembly, or record it as a training deficiency for a nonstructural assembly.
Reinspection: verify positioner spacing on all remaining ungrouted cells before placing further lifts.

Grout Consistency Was Rejected After Mixing

Probable cause: incorrect water content, segregated aggregate, or contaminated materials.
Confirmation: compare the batch record against the approved mix design or packaged-product instructions and perform the specified consistency check.
Correction: discard the rejected batch and remix using correct proportions and clean materials; do not add water to a rejected batch to correct consistency.
Reinspection: perform the consistency check again on the new batch before placement.

Mortar Was Placed Where Grout Was Specified

Probable cause: the crew used mortar remaining from wall construction to fill a cell instead of mixing the specified grout.
Confirmation: compare the material used against the batch record and the approved grout specification; mortar and grout have different aggregate and strength characteristics and must not be confused.
Correction: where possible before cure, remove the mortar and refill with the specified grout; where cured, document the condition and refer it to the responsible design authority.
Reinspection: verify the correct material is identified and used for all remaining cells.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a cured, inspected reinforced wall section and a complete written record, not a verbal description of what was placed.

  • Confirm every photograph of reinforcement, embeds, and cleanouts is labeled with the item, location, and date it documents
  • File the completed Reinforcement-and-Grout Inspection Record with the batch records and placement log it references
  • Clean mixers, pumps, chutes, and consolidation equipment before storage
  • Dispose of grout washout without drain discharge, following the Chapter 2 washout controls
  • Retain the record and photographs for use in later chapters that build on this wall section

Field Terms

Grout
A fluid cementitious material placed in masonry cells to bond reinforcement to the surrounding block, distinct from mortar used to bed and joint units.
Cleanout
An opening at the base of a grout space allowing removal of debris and inspection of the cell before grouting.
Lift
A single placement of grout to a specified maximum height, followed by consolidation before the next lift.
Consolidation
Mechanical vibration or rodding of placed grout to eliminate voids and ensure full contact with reinforcement and block shells.
Positioner
A device that holds a vertical reinforcing bar centered and at the specified cover within a cell until grout is placed.
Cover
The clear distance between a reinforcing bar and the nearest face of the surrounding block shell.
Lap
The length over which two reinforcing bars overlap to transfer load between them.
Blowout
A rupture of a block shell caused by excessive grout pressure, improper consolidation, or an unsupported cell.
Bond Beam
A horizontal course of specially shaped block filled with grout and reinforcement to distribute load or tie a wall together.
Embed
An anchor bolt, sleeve, or connector installed in a cell before grouting so it becomes fixed in the finished assembly.
