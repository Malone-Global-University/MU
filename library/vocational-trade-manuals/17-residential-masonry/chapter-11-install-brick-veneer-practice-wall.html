Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to install a complete brick-veneer practice section as one connected water-management assembly rather than as brick stacked on a wall. The finished work product is one practice wall section with an inspected support ledge, an inspected and repaired weather-resistive barrier (WRB), lapped base and through-wall flashing with formed end dams, a clean drained air space, corrosion-resistant veneer ties engaged at the specified embedment and spacing, weeps aligned with the flashing, brick laid in running bond or the specified bond with full head joints, and tooled joints.

You will work from the approved veneer wall section documented in your Chapter 1 readiness plan and the flashing, tie, and weep details it identifies. Nothing in this chapter authorizes you to design the flashing system, alter the structural backup wall, or install veneer on unverified support; those decisions come from the approved drawings, the manufacturer instructions for the specified products, and the inspections completed in Chapters 1 and 4.

This chapter builds directly on the layout skills from Chapter 4, the mortar skills from Chapter 3, the corner and bond skills from Chapters 8 and 9, and the cutting skills from Chapter 10. It also prepares you for Chapter 12 (stone veneer), Chapter 13 (flashing and weep details in isolation), and the Chapter 20 capstone, which may use this same water-management sequence for its veneer option.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RM-061 — Prepare the veneer substrate, confirming the structural backup and support ledge have passed inspection and the WRB is continuous.
  • RM-062 — Install weather barrier where required, repairing WRB damage and installing base flashing in the correct shingle-lap sequence with formed end dams.
  • RM-063 — Install wall ties, placing corrosion-resistant veneer ties at the specified rows, spacing, embedment, and slope.
  • RM-064 — Lay veneer courses, building corner leads and laying running bond or the specified bond to line, level, and plumb.
  • RM-065 — Maintain air space, keeping the drainage cavity clean and free of mortar bridging throughout construction.
  • RM-066 — Inspect veneer drainage, verifying tie spacing, embedment, cavity condition, flashing, and weeps before the section is closed out.

Where This Skill Fits

Brick veneer is not a decorative skin. It is a rain-screen system: the structural backup and WRB keep the building dry, the cavity and flashing collect and direct any water that gets past the brick, and the weeps let that water out. If any one part of that chain is missing or damaged, the veneer will eventually leak regardless of how well the brick itself is laid.

The support-ledge inspection, WRB continuity check, and flashing sequence you perform here use the same base-and-drainage judgment you practiced in Chapter 1 and the same story-pole and bond-layout skills from Chapters 4, 8, and 9. The tie spacing, weep alignment, and cavity-cleanliness habits you build in this chapter carry forward unchanged into Chapter 12's stone veneer and Chapter 13's dedicated flashing work.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • confirming the structural backup and veneer support ledge have passed the required inspection before any WRB, flashing, or brick work begins
  • inspecting sheathing, WRB, penetrations, windows, doors, and transitions for continuity, and repairing WRB damage using the approved building-envelope method
  • installing base or shelf-angle flashing in shingle-lap sequence with end dams and an exterior outlet
  • installing mortar-dropping control or cavity drainage material where specified
  • installing corrosion-resistant veneer ties at the specified rows, spacing, embedment, and slope
  • laying the first brick course on the approved ledge, maintaining air space, and installing weeps at the specified locations
  • building corner leads, using a mason's line, and laying running bond or the specified bond with ties engaged in the mortar joints
  • installing head flashing, lintels, shelf angles, and movement details where included in the approved section
  • tooling joints, cleaning mortar smears without flooding the cavity, and inspecting the completed section

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • design the flashing, tie, cavity, or weep system; every dimension, spacing, and material in this chapter must come from the approved veneer wall section, manufacturer instructions, or adopted code, never from the installer's estimate
  • proceed with veneer installation on a structural backup or support ledge that has not passed the Chapter 1 or Chapter 4 inspection
  • alter the structural backup wall, window and door assemblies, or roof-to-wall interfaces beyond the WRB repair and flashing integration specified here
  • conceal ties, flashing, weeps, or cavity condition before the required inspection and photograph documentation is complete

Any support, WRB continuity, or flashing condition that fails inspection in this chapter must be corrected and reinspected before brick is laid over it.

Trade Note

Every concealed water-management detail in this chapter — flashing laps, end dams, tie embedment, and weep alignment — is photographed at the point of installation, before the next course of brick covers it. A photograph taken after the wall is closed cannot verify what is behind the brick; this is the same point-of-observation discipline you practiced in Chapter 1.

Setup Standard

Do not lay the first brick course until the structural backup, support ledge, WRB continuity, base flashing, end dams, and first row of ties have been installed and inspected. A cavity that is closed before this inspection is complete must be reopened, not assumed acceptable.

Shop Tip

Dry-fit and photograph the flashing end dams and lap sequence before applying any sealant. Sealant applied to correct a flashing lap that was installed backward hides the defect instead of fixing it.

Common Field Fundamentals

Brick veneer succeeds or fails as one system: support, water-resistive barrier, flashing, ties, air space, and weeps. Skipping the inspection of any one part to save time on this practice section teaches a habit that produces real leaks in real work.

Water-Management Sequence Discipline

Install this veneer section in the order the master instructions require: support and WRB first, then flashing and end dams, then cavity drainage material, then the first tie row and first brick course, then subsequent courses with ties engaged at every specified row, then head flashing and terminations, then final cleaning. Do not lay brick ahead of the tie schedule to save time; a tie row skipped now cannot be added later without removing brick.

Measurement Cue — Air Space and Tie Spacing

Verify the air-space width with a tape measure at multiple points as you build, not only at the start. Verify tie spacing and embedment against the approved wall section or tie manufacturer's instructions at every row; do not estimate spacing by eye.

Critical Safety Cue — Unverified Support

Do not place veneer brick on an unsupported ledge, unverified shelf angle, damaged sheathing, or any support condition that has not passed the Chapter 1 or Chapter 4 inspection. Stop work and refer the condition to the responsible design or inspection authority identified in your Project Identification Record.

Field Safety Cue — Silica and Wet Mortar

Wear waterproof gloves and eye protection while mixing mortar and buttering brick. Use wet cutting or approved local exhaust if any brick must be cut per Chapter 10. Do not kneel in wet mortar without approved protection.

Manufacturer Cue — WRB Tape and Flashing Compatibility

Confirm WRB repair tape, flashing, and sealant are chemically compatible with the installed WRB and with each other before application. Follow the manufacturer's stated lap dimension, cure time, and application-temperature range; do not substitute an incompatible product to finish the job faster.

Code Alert — Weep Spacing and Air-Space Width

Weep spacing and minimum air-space width vary by jurisdiction and by the specific veneer system. Use the value stated in the approved veneer wall section; where the section defers to adopted code, verify the current local requirement before installing weeps or setting the cavity width.

E. Substrate and Water-Barrier Preparation

  1. Review the approved veneer wall section, support ledge, WRB, flashing, air space, ties, weeps, openings, lintels, and movement joints. Confirm this is the current approved version before any material is opened.
  2. Confirm the structural backup and veneer support have passed inspection. Verify the Chapter 1 and Chapter 4 records are on file and cleared before proceeding.
  3. Inspect sheathing, WRB, penetrations, windows, doors, and transitions for continuity. Walk the full section and mark any gap, tear, or unlapped seam.
  4. Repair WRB damage using the approved building-envelope method. Use compatible repair tape or patch material per manufacturer instructions and photograph the repair before covering it.

F. Flashing and Drainage Cavity Installation

  1. Install base or shelf-angle flashing in shingle-lap sequence. Begin at one end and work toward the other so each higher piece overlaps the piece below it.
  2. Extend flashing to the required outlet and end dams. Form end dams at every termination and confirm the flashing reaches the exterior face or drip as specified.
  3. Install mortar-dropping control or cavity drainage material where specified. Cut material to the cavity width and height without bridging the air space.
  4. Mark tie rows and stud or backup attachment points. Transfer the specified tie-row elevations from the story pole to the backup wall.

G. Tie Installation and First Course

  1. Install corrosion-resistant veneer ties using listed fasteners. Fasten at the marked rows and specified horizontal spacing.
  2. Maintain tie orientation, embedment, and slope so water is not directed inward. Verify slope with a level before the tie is engaged in mortar.
  3. Lay the first brick course on the approved ledge. Set to the story-pole elevation and the mason's line established in Chapter 4.
  4. Maintain air space and prevent mortar bridging. Check cavity width with a tape measure as each section of the first course is laid.
  5. Install weeps at the specified locations. Place weeps in the head joints directly above the flashing at the specified spacing.

H. Corner Leads, Bond, and Subsequent Courses

  1. Build corner leads and use a mason's line. Follow the corner and lead procedure established in Chapters 4, 8, and 9.
  2. Lay running bond or the specified bond. Maintain full head joints and the story-pole course height at every lift.
  3. Engage ties in mortar joints at the approved depth. Confirm each tie is embedded to the specified depth as its course is laid, not adjusted after the mortar sets.
  4. Keep flashing and cavity clean. Remove any mortar droppings from the cavity before they bridge to the backup wall.
  5. Install head flashing, lintels, shelf angles, and movement details where included. Integrate each with the WRB and cavity system per the approved section.
  6. Maintain clearance below siding, trim, roofing, and wood components. Verify the specified clearance at every interface as the veneer approaches it.

I. Tooling, Cleaning, and Final Inspection

  1. Tool joints at the proper time. Tool when the mortar is thumbprint hard, matching the profile specified for this project.
  2. Clean mortar smears without flooding the cavity. Use a dry brush first; use water sparingly and never direct it into the weeps or cavity.
  3. Inspect tie spacing, embedment, cavity, flashing, weeps, line, level, bond, and joints. Compare every item against the Acceptance Standards table before declaring the section complete.
  4. Photograph concealed water-management details. Confirm photographs exist for every tie row, flashing lap, end dam, and weep before the section is closed out.
  5. Protect and cure the veneer. Cover the fresh work per the weather controls established in your Chapter 1 plan.

Common Installation Inspection

Inspect every veneer section using the same checks regardless of brick type or bond pattern.

  • Support check: confirm the ledge, WRB, and backup passed inspection before brick was placed.
  • Water-management check: confirm flashing laps, end dams, cavity cleanliness, tie embedment and slope, and weep alignment are documented with photographs.
  • Bond and joint check: confirm the specified bond, full head joints, and consistent tooling profile throughout the section.
  • Alignment check: confirm line, level, and plumb are within the tolerance stated in the approved wall section.
  • Closeout check: confirm the section is protected through cure and all inspection records are filed before the next chapter begins.

Complete the Veneer Installation Worksheet in the Inspection Checklist section below before beginning Chapter 12 or the veneer option of Chapter 20.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

Mortar Bridges the Air Space

Probable cause: excess mortar was allowed to fall or squeeze into the cavity during bed-joint spreading or head-joint buttering and was not removed before the next course was laid.
Confirmation: inspect the cavity with a flashlight at intervals during construction; look for mortar droppings bridging from the brick to the backup wall.
Correction: remove the bridging mortar with a trowel or wire before continuing; do not lay additional courses over a bridged cavity.
Reinspection: recheck the cavity at the same location after the correction and before the next course is laid.

A Tie Is Missing or Under-Embedded at a Specified Row

Probable cause: the course was laid ahead of the tie schedule, or a tie was set but not fully engaged in the bed joint before the brick was placed.
Confirmation: compare the completed tie rows against the story-pole marks and the specified horizontal spacing; check embedment against the manufacturer's minimum.
Correction: where the mortar has not yet set, remove the affected brick, reset the tie to the specified embedment, and relay the unit; where mortar has set, consult the approved wall section for the correction method rather than concealing the deficiency.
Reinspection: verify tie embedment and spacing at the corrected row before laying further courses.

A Weep Is Blocked or Missing

Probable cause: mortar was allowed to fill the head joint at the weep location, or the weep spacing was not marked before laying the course.
Confirmation: sight along the flashing line for open weeps at the specified spacing; probe suspect head joints for obstruction.
Correction: clear the obstruction or install the missing weep using the approved method; do not substitute a drilled hole through solid brick for a properly detailed weep.
Reinspection: confirm every weep at the flashing line is open and correctly spaced before final cleaning.

The Flashing End Dam Was Omitted or Reversed

Probable cause: the flashing was cut and installed without forming the required upturn at the termination, or the lap sequence was installed shingle-reversed.
Confirmation: inspect the termination before brick covers it; a missing or reversed end dam allows water to run off the end of the flashing into the wall.
Correction: remove and reinstall the flashing section with a correctly formed end dam and correct lap direction before proceeding.
Reinspection: photograph the corrected end dam and lap before the first brick course is laid over it.

The Wall Face Drifts Out of Plumb

Probable cause: the mason's line was not rechecked at intervals, or course thickness was adjusted inconsistently to compensate for a layout error.
Confirmation: check plumb at multiple points with the 4-foot level and compare against the corner leads.
Correction: correct drift before the mortar hardens by resetting the affected units to the mason's line and story-pole elevation.
Reinspection: recheck plumb and course height at the corrected section and at the adjacent leads.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a documented, protected, curing veneer section, not an unrecorded finished-looking wall.

  • Confirm every concealed-detail photograph is labeled with the item, location, and date it documents
  • File the completed Veneer Installation Worksheet with the approved wall section and the Chapter 1 and Chapter 4 records it references
  • Clean and store trowels, jointers, the mason's line, and line blocks
  • Dispose of mortar waste and washout per the Chapter 2 site controls
  • Confirm weather protection is in place over the curing section before leaving the site

Field Terms

Support Ledge
The shelf angle, foundation ledge, or other approved bearing surface the veneer's first course rests on.
Weather-Resistive Barrier (WRB)
The continuous water-shedding layer installed over sheathing beneath the veneer cavity.
Shingle Lap
An overlapping installation sequence, worked from bottom to top or low end to high end, so each higher piece sheds water onto the piece below it.
End Dam
A formed upturn at the termination of a flashing run that prevents water from running off the flashing's end.
Air Space / Cavity
The clear gap between the back of the veneer and the WRB or drainage material, kept free of mortar bridging.
Veneer Tie
A corrosion-resistant connector linking the veneer to the structural backup, engaged in a mortar bed joint and sloped to shed water outward.
Weep
An opening at flashing level allowing collected cavity water to drain to the exterior.
Running Bond
A bond pattern in which each course is offset by a half unit from the course below it.
Corner Lead
A stepped, racked-back reference assembly built at a wall corner to control line and course elevation for the intervening wall.
Veneer Installation Worksheet
The document recording every inspection finding, photograph reference, deficiency, and closeout result for this chapter's project.
