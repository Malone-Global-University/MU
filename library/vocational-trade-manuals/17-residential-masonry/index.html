17-Residential Masonry

This field manual provides practical, project-based instruction for residential masonry construction. Students learn to prepare materials, mix mortar, build masonry assemblies, install veneer systems, perform repairs, and maintain brick, block, and stone surfaces.

Overview

Residential Masonry emphasizes hands-on skill development through complete masonry construction projects. Beginning with jobsite preparation and material handling, students progress through mortar preparation, concrete block walls, brick construction, stone veneer, masonry detailing, repairs, maintenance, and a complete masonry veneer or garden wall capstone project.

Learning Outcomes

  • Inspect and plan residential masonry projects.
  • Prepare safe and organized masonry work areas.
  • Mix mortar and prepare masonry materials correctly.
  • Layout masonry walls using accurate measurements and reference points.
  • Build concrete block walls, corners, and reinforced masonry sections.
  • Construct brick walls, corners, piers, and veneer systems.
  • Install masonry flashing and drainage details.
  • Cut brick, block, and stone safely for installation.
  • Repair, repoint, clean, and maintain masonry surfaces.
  • Complete a residential masonry construction project.
