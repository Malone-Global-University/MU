Job Overview

This chapter qualifies you to perform a ground-based exterior chimney inspection and to complete only one approved, low-risk mortar maintenance area, using safe vantage points rather than roof or chimney-top access. The finished work product is a completed Chimney Condition Report documenting the plumb, joint, crown, cap, flashing, and staining condition of the chimney, classifying every finding as monitor, minor maintenance, flashing repair, crown or cap repair, flue inspection, structural repair, or immediate professional action, and, where an area qualifies, one completed minor repointing repair with its own bond, depth, compaction, and cure record.

This chapter draws directly on the mortar-mixing skills from Chapter 3 and the repointing skills from Chapter 14, applied here under stricter access and scope limits because chimneys combine height, fire exposure, and flue conditions that are outside general masonry practice. Nothing in this chapter authorizes flue, liner, smoke chamber, fireplace, damper, combustion-air, vent-connector, or gas-appliance work, and nothing in this chapter authorizes roof or chimney-top access without the fall-protection and access system that this chapter does not provide.

This is the eighteenth chapter in the book. It depends on the mortar-mixing procedure from Chapter 3 and the repointing procedure from Chapter 14, and it produces the documented chimney condition record referenced by Chapter 19 cleaning and sealing decisions and by the Chapter 20 capstone closeout.

Skills You Will Demonstrate

  • RM-103 — Inspect the chimney exterior from safe ground-based vantage points, recording plumb, alignment, cracking, spalling, missing units, vegetation, staining, and efflorescence.
  • RM-104 — Identify deteriorated mortar, distinguishing joints that qualify for minor maintenance from conditions that require professional repair.
  • RM-105 — Identify damaged caps or crowns, recording cracking, separation, poor slope, and missing overhang without attempting repair in this chapter.
  • RM-106 — Identify flashing concerns, recording gaps, corrosion, sealant-only repairs, and water staining visible from safe vantage points.
  • RM-107 — Perform minor repointing where appropriate, using compatible mortar in one accessible area that does not require roof-edge exposure.
  • RM-108 — Document chimney repair needs, producing a written condition report that classifies every finding and states the required professional referral.

Where This Skill Fits

Every chimney in this book is treated as an inspection and minor-maintenance subject only, never as an ordinary masonry-repair project. The classification skill practiced here — separating a joint that a mason may repoint from a crown, cap, flashing, or flue condition that requires a chimney specialist — is the same judgment introduced in Chapter 1's stop-work criteria and reused in Chapter 15's crack-diagnosis procedure.

The Chimney Condition Report you produce here carries forward into Chapter 19, where cleaning and sealing decisions must account for any open joint, crack, or moisture source you document, and into the Chapter 20 capstone closeout, where any chimney referenced by the capstone project must show a current condition report on file.

Scope and Intended Use

This chapter covers:

  • ground-based visual inspection of chimney plumb, alignment, bulging, cracking, missing units, spalls, open joints, vegetation, staining, and efflorescence, using binoculars or approved imaging
  • ground-based visual inspection of the visible crown or cap for cracking, separation, poor slope, missing overhang, and damaged termination
  • inspection of roof flashing and counterflashing from safe vantage points, identifying gaps, corrosion, sealant-only repairs, and water staining
  • inspection of attic or interior areas for leakage only when safely accessible without specialized access equipment
  • minor repointing of accessible mortar joints that do not require roof-edge exposure, using compatible mortar and the repointing procedure from Chapter 14
  • classification of every finding as monitor, minor maintenance, flashing repair, crown or cap repair, flue inspection, structural repair, or immediate professional action
  • preparation of a written Chimney Condition Report with photographs, classifications, and professional referrals

This chapter does not authorize the learner to:

  • enter or climb onto the roof, chimney top, or any improvised platform without the required access and fall-protection plan, which this chapter does not supply
  • alter, repair, or seal chimney flues, liners, smoke chambers, fireplaces, dampers, combustion-air systems, vent connectors, or gas appliances under this chapter
  • repair or seal cracks in crowns, caps, or flashing, or substitute ordinary mortar or caulk for a proper crown, cap, or flashing repair
  • proceed with any repointing on a chimney showing loose masonry, structural cracking, damaged flue indications, active combustion concern, unsafe roof condition, or severe deterioration; those conditions are documented and referred, not corrected, in this chapter

Any chimney condition beyond the minor-maintenance scope defined in this chapter must be documented, referred to a qualified chimney professional, and reinspected only after that professional's work is complete.

Trade Note

A chimney inspection in this chapter is a ground-based inspection. Every finding above what binoculars, ground-level attic access, or approved imaging can safely show is recorded as requiring further professional evaluation, not estimated from a distance and then repaired.

Setup Standard

Do not begin any repointing until the ground-based inspection in Section F is complete and the target joint has been classified as Minor Maintenance under the Acceptance Standards table. A chimney showing any Stop-Work condition must not receive repointing work in this chapter; document and refer it instead.

Shop Tip

Photograph every side of the chimney and every visible termination before beginning any repair. A single accessible joint on the shaded side of a chimney can look like the whole story; the photographs are what let a chimney professional see the rest.

Prerequisites

  • Completion of Chapter 3 (mixing mortar) and Chapter 14 (repointing brick or block mortar joints); this chapter applies those procedures under stricter chimney-specific access and scope limits
  • A completed Chapter 1 readiness determination for this chimney, confirming the project is classified chimney-related and identifying the responsible design or inspection authority where applicable
  • Safe ground-level or interior vantage points from which the full chimney exterior, or as much of it as is safely visible, can be observed

  • Confirmation that no fall-protection, scaffold, or roof-access system is required for this inspection; if the chimney cannot be adequately inspected from safe ground-based or interior vantage points, the inspection is limited accordingly and documented as such

Common Field Fundamentals

Chimney work in this book follows a narrower rule than the rest of the masonry chapters: inspect from safe vantage points, repair only the smallest, clearly nonstructural mortar condition, and refer everything else. The classification made in this chapter controls whether Chapter 19 cleaning proceeds on this chimney and whether the Chapter 20 capstone may reference it.

Access Judgment and Scope Discipline

The single most important judgment in this chapter is recognizing what ground-based inspection can and cannot tell you. Binoculars can show cracking, spalling, missing units, and gross crown or flashing damage. They cannot substitute for a hands-on inspection of a crown, cap, liner, or flue condition. Record what the ground-based method shows, and classify anything it cannot fully resolve as requiring further professional evaluation rather than guessing at its severity.

Measurement Cue — Record Findings at the Point of Observation

Record every crack width, joint depth, and moisture reading directly on the Chimney Condition Report at the location and vantage point from which it was observed. Note the vantage point used (ground level, attic, upper-floor window, binoculars) for every recorded finding.

Critical Safety Cue — Roof and Chimney-Top Access

Do not enter or climb onto the roof, chimney top, or any improvised platform during this inspection or repair. This chapter does not supply the fall-protection and access system required for roof-level work. Inspect chimney tops, crowns, caps, and roof-level flashing from the ground or from a safe interior vantage point using binoculars or approved imaging only.

Field Safety Cue — Attic and Interior Access

Inspect attic or interior areas for leakage only when access is safe, unobstructed, and does not require specialized equipment. Use a flashlight and avoid stepping off framing members. Stop the interior inspection and report the condition if attic access is unsafe, obstructed, or requires equipment beyond a stepladder.

Code Alert — Chimney Professional Scope

Flue, liner, smoke chamber, fireplace, damper, combustion-air, vent-connector, and gas-appliance work is outside the scope of this chapter and this book. Crown, cap, and flashing repairs beyond the minor mortar maintenance defined here also require a qualified chimney professional. Identify and record the required referral rather than substituting mortar, caulk, or sealant for a proper repair.

C. Documentation Requirements

  • Photographs: all visible sides of the chimney, all visible terminations, the crown or cap, visible flashing, and every recorded finding.
  • Vantage-point record: the method used for every finding — ground level, attic, interior window, or binoculars — so a reviewing professional understands the limits of the inspection.
  • Classification record: a single classification for every inspected item, drawn only from the seven categories in the Acceptance Standards table.
  • Minor-maintenance record: mortar type, batch record, joint depth, compaction, tooling, and cure protection for any repointing repair performed.
  • Referral record: every professional referral made, the urgency recommendation, and the limits of this inspection stated in writing.

E. Preparation — Records and Ground-Based Setup

  1. Review chimney location, height, roof access, flue use, appliance type, service history, and inspection limits. Record this information on the Chimney Identification Record before beginning the exterior inspection.
  2. State clearly that flue, liner, smoke chamber, fireplace, combustion, cap, crown, and structural repairs may require a qualified chimney professional. Record this scope limitation on the Chimney Condition Report before recording any findings.

F. Ground-Based Exterior Inspection

  1. Begin with ground-based visual inspection using binoculars or approved imaging. Select vantage points that show all visible sides of the chimney.
  2. Inspect overall plumb, leaning, bulging, cracking, missing units, spalls, open joints, vegetation, staining, and efflorescence. Record each finding on the Inspection Item Schedule with a photo reference.
  3. Inspect visible crown or cap for cracking, separation, poor slope, missing overhang, and damaged termination. Record findings without attempting repair.
  4. Inspect roof flashing and counterflashing from safe vantage points. Use binoculars to observe laps, corrosion, and staining without approaching the roof edge.
  5. Look for gaps, corrosion, sealant-only repairs, and water staining. Record the location of each condition relative to the chimney sides and roof plane.

G. Interior Inspection and Stop-Work Determination

  1. Inspect attic or interior areas for leakage only when safely accessible. Use the flashlight and moisture meter; record findings or mark the area Not Inspected — Access Unsafe.
  2. Do not enter or climb onto the roof without the required access and fall-protection plan. Confirm this restriction was followed before proceeding to classification.
  3. Stop work for loose masonry, structural cracking, damaged flue, active combustion concern, unsafe roof, or severe deterioration. Classify the finding as Immediate Professional Action and record the referral before continuing any further work on this chimney.

H. Minor Maintenance and Reporting

  1. Identify minor repointing areas accessible without roof-edge exposure. Confirm the area meets the Minor Maintenance standard in the Acceptance Standards table.
  2. Verify mortar compatibility. Confirm the repointing mortar is no harder or less permeable than the existing chimney mortar, per Chapter 14.
  3. Remove loose mortar by hand or controlled methods. Rake or chisel to the joint depth specified in Chapter 14 without widening the joint or damaging unit edges.
  4. Clean, dampen where required, repack, tool, and cure the minor repair. Follow the Chapter 14 repointing procedure in full for this area.
  5. Do not seal cracks in crowns, caps, flashing, liners, or flues with ordinary mortar or caulk as a substitute for proper repair. Confirm no such substitution has occurred before closing out the repair.
  6. Photograph all sides and terminations visible. Confirm every side of the chimney and every visible termination has at least one photograph on file.
  7. Classify findings as monitor, minor maintenance, flashing repair, crown or cap repair, flue inspection, structural repair, or immediate professional action. Assign exactly one classification to every inspected item.
  8. Prepare a written chimney condition report. Compile every finding, classification, photograph reference, and the minor-maintenance record into the Chimney Condition Report.
  9. Document the limits of the inspection. State every area that could not be safely inspected and why.
  10. Provide referral and urgency recommendations. For every classification other than Monitor or completed Minor Maintenance, state the required professional type and an urgency recommendation.

Common Readiness Inspection

Inspect every Chimney Condition Report using the same checks regardless of the chimney's construction or flue type.

  • Access check: confirm every finding above ground-level or safe-interior vantage points is marked with the vantage-point method used, and confirm no roof or chimney-top access occurred.
  • Classification check: confirm every inspected item carries exactly one of the seven defined classifications and that no finding was minimized to avoid a referral.
  • Minor-maintenance check: confirm any repointing performed meets the Minor Maintenance standard and was not performed on an item that should have been classified Structural Repair, Crown or Cap Repair, Flashing Repair, or Immediate Professional Action.
  • Photograph check: confirm all visible sides and terminations are photographed and referenced in the report.
  • Referral check: confirm every classification other than Monitor or completed Minor Maintenance carries a written professional referral and urgency recommendation.

Failure Points and Troubleshooting

A Crown or Flashing Condition Was Sealed With Mortar or Caulk Instead of Referred

Probable cause: the inspector treated a crown crack or flashing gap as an ordinary mortar joint rather than classifying it under Crown or Cap Repair or Flashing Repair.
Confirmation: compare the repaired location against the Inspection Item Schedule; any sealant or mortar applied to a crown, cap, flashing, liner, or flue condition is out of scope regardless of the material used.
Correction: document the improper repair, reclassify the underlying condition correctly, and refer it to a qualified chimney professional.
Reinspection: confirm the referral was made and do not close out the report until the correct classification is recorded.

An Inaccessible Condition Was Estimated Rather Than Marked Not Inspected

Probable cause: the inspector attempted to judge a roof-level or flue condition from an inadequate vantage point and recorded a finding without noting the limitation.
Confirmation: review the vantage-point record for every finding; any finding without a stated vantage point should be treated as unverified.
Correction: reclassify the finding as Not Inspected — Access Unsafe or obtain a better vantage point, approved imaging, or a professional inspection before finalizing the report.
Reinspection: repeat the affected inspection item using a documented, safe vantage point.

Minor Repointing Was Performed on a Condition That Should Have Been Referred

Probable cause: the joint condition was not fully compared against the Minor Maintenance standard before repair began, and an accompanying structural or flue indicator was missed.
Confirmation: re-review the repaired area and the surrounding chimney for signs of leaning, structural cracking, or interior leakage that should have triggered a different classification.
Correction: document the repair performed, note any accompanying condition discovered, and add the required professional referral to the Chimney Condition Report.
Reinspection: monitor the repointed joint at the next inspection cycle and confirm the referral is tracked separately.

Roof or Chimney-Top Access Was Used to Complete the Inspection

Probable cause: a vantage point could not be found at ground level or from a safe interior location, and the inspector climbed onto the roof or chimney top without the required fall-protection and access system.
Confirmation: review the vantage-point record and photograph metadata or description for any indication of roof-level photography.
Correction: stop this practice immediately; mark the affected findings as Not Inspected — Access Unsafe and arrange a professional inspection with the required access equipment.
Reinspection: confirm no further roof or chimney-top access occurs under this chapter's scope.

The Chimney Condition Report Omits a Required Classification or Referral

Probable cause: a finding was recorded on the Inspection Item Schedule but never assigned one of the seven required classifications, or a required referral was left blank.
Confirmation: compare every row of the Inspection Item Schedule against the final report; every row must carry exactly one classification and, where required, a referral and urgency recommendation.
Correction: complete the missing classification and referral before filing the report.
Reinspection: confirm the completed report is internally consistent before proceeding to Chapter 19.

Cleanup

This chapter ends with a filed condition report and, where applicable, a cured minor repair — not a verbal impression of the chimney's condition.

  • Confirm every photograph is labeled with the item, location, vantage point, and date it documents
  • Protect the completed minor repointing repair from rain, freezing, and impact through its cure period
  • File the completed Chimney Condition Report with the property's masonry records
  • Store professional referral correspondence with the report
  • Return borrowed tools (binoculars, moisture meter, camera) to storage
  • Retain the report and photographs for use in Chapter 19 and any future chimney reinspection

Field Terms

Ground-Based Inspection
Visual chimney inspection performed from the ground, an attic, or another safe interior vantage point using binoculars or approved imaging for elevated conditions.
Minor Maintenance Area
An accessible mortar joint showing only isolated deterioration with no accompanying structural, flue, crown, cap, or flashing concern.
Crown
The sloped concrete or masonry cap at the very top of a chimney that sheds water away from the flue and masonry below.
Counterflashing
The upper flashing component set into or against the chimney masonry that overlaps the base flashing at the roofline.
Smoke Chamber
The transitional interior space above a fireplace firebox and below the flue; alteration of this component is outside this chapter's scope.
Classification
The single controlling finding category assigned to each inspected chimney item under this chapter's Acceptance Standards table.
Stop-Work Condition
A finding serious enough to halt further work on the chimney until a qualified chimney professional evaluates it.
Chimney Condition Report
The document recording every inspection finding, classification, minor-maintenance record, photograph reference, and professional referral for the inspected chimney.
Immediate Professional Action
The classification assigned to any finding presenting an immediate hazard, requiring the referral to be made without delay.
Professional Referral
The documented act of directing a chimney finding to a qualified chimney, flue, structural, or other professional rather than resolving it in this chapter.

Performance Check

With the inspection complete, review the Chimney Condition Report against the Acceptance Standards table and confirm every exterior, crown or cap, flashing, interior, and mortar-joint item has been recorded with a photograph and a vantage-point reference. Confirm every item carries exactly one of the seven required classifications, that every classification other than Monitor or completed Minor Maintenance carries a written referral and urgency recommendation, and that any minor repointing performed meets the Chapter 14 bond, depth, compaction, and cure standards. Do not close out the report until every referral is documented and no crown, cap, flashing, liner, or flue condition has been sealed with ordinary mortar or caulk.
