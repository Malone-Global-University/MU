12-Probability and Statistics

Probability and statistics provide the mathematical framework for reasoning under uncertainty, analyzing data, and making informed decisions across science, engineering, economics, and social systems.

Overview

This textbook develops foundational and advanced methods in probability theory and statistical inference. Topics include random variables, distributions, expectation, estimation, hypothesis testing, regression, and stochastic processes, with emphasis on real-world data modeling.

Learning Outcomes

  • Apply probability rules to compute and interpret uncertainties.
  • Analyze discrete and continuous random variables and distributions.
  • Compute expectations, variances, and higher moments.
  • Perform statistical inference using estimation and hypothesis testing.
  • Apply regression models to real-world data.
  • Use Bayesian and likelihood-based reasoning for inference.
  • Understand stochastic processes and time-dependent randomness.
