10-Combinatorics

Combinatorics is the study of counting, arrangement, structure, and discrete selection processes. It provides the mathematical foundation for probability theory, algorithm design, cryptography, optimization, and discrete structures in computer science and modern mathematics.

Overview

This textbook introduces core principles of combinatorics including counting techniques, permutations and combinations, binomial reasoning, discrete probability foundations, and structured problem-solving strategies used across mathematics and computer science. Students develop both theoretical understanding and applied problem-solving skills.

Learning Outcomes

  • Apply fundamental counting principles in structured problem solving.
  • Compute permutations and combinations in complex scenarios.
  • Use inclusion and exclusion principles to resolve overlapping sets.
  • Analyze discrete probability models using combinatorial methods.
  • Work with binomial expansions and coefficient structures.
  • Construct and solve recurrence relations.
  • Use generating functions to model and solve counting problems.
  • Interpret and apply basic graph theory concepts.
  • Develop advanced reasoning skills in discrete mathematical systems.
