03-Algebra II

Algebra II extends the concepts introduced in Algebra I through advanced equations, functions, polynomials, rational expressions, radicals, exponential models, logarithms, sequences, and introductory mathematical analysis.

Overview

Algebra II develops higher-level symbolic reasoning and prepares students for precalculus, calculus, statistics, computer science, economics, engineering, and the physical sciences. Students explore increasingly complex algebraic structures, multiple function families, and mathematical modeling techniques used throughout modern quantitative disciplines.

Learning Outcomes

  • Analyze and manipulate polynomial, rational, radical, exponential, and logarithmic expressions.
  • Solve advanced algebraic equations and systems of equations.
  • Interpret and graph a wide variety of functions.
  • Model real-world phenomena using algebraic functions and equations.
  • Apply algebraic reasoning to scientific, engineering, and quantitative problems.
  • Develop readiness for precalculus and calculus.
