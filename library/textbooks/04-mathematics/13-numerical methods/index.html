Numerical Methods

Numerical methods develop practical techniques for approximating solutions to mathematical problems that cannot always be solved exactly. Students learn how computation, error analysis, approximation, and algorithmic thinking support modern science and engineering.

Overview

This textbook introduces numerical analysis and computational methods for solving equations, approximating functions, differentiating and integrating numerically, working with linear algebra, optimizing functions, and solving ordinary and partial differential equations. The book emphasizes accuracy, stability, and the practical use of algorithms in scientific computing.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain how numerical methods are used to approximate mathematical solutions.
  • Analyze error, stability, and floating-point limitations in computation.
  • Use interpolation, splines, and curve fitting to model data.
  • Apply numerical techniques for differentiation and integration.
  • Work with Taylor series and function approximation methods.
  • Solve linear systems and eigenvalue problems numerically.
  • Use optimization methods to locate extrema of functions.
  • Approximate solutions to ordinary and partial differential equations.
  • Interpret computational results with attention to accuracy and efficiency.
  • Apply numerical thinking to scientific and engineering problems.
