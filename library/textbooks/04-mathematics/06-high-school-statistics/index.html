06-High School Statistics

Statistics is the science of collecting, organizing, analyzing, interpreting, and communicating data. Students learn how data reveals patterns, supports decision-making, and informs research across science, business, government, and everyday life.

Overview

This textbook introduces descriptive statistics, probability, data visualization, sampling methods, statistical modeling, inference, and hypothesis testing. Students develop the ability to critically evaluate data, identify trends, and make informed conclusions from quantitative evidence.

Learning Outcomes

  • Collect, organize, and summarize quantitative and categorical data.
  • Create and interpret graphs, charts, and statistical displays.
  • Analyze relationships using correlation and regression techniques.
  • Apply probability principles to uncertain events.
  • Interpret random variables and probability distributions.
  • Use sampling methods to draw conclusions about populations.
  • Conduct and evaluate hypothesis tests.
  • Communicate statistical findings clearly and accurately.
  • Apply statistical reasoning to real-world problems and decision-making.
