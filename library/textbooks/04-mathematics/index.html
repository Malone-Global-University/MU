Mathematics Shelf

The Mathematics Shelf contains a complete structured learning sequence that begins with foundational arithmetic and progresses through algebra, geometry, trigonometry, precalculus, statistics, linear algebra, calculus, discrete mathematics, combinatorics, differential equations, probability, and numerical methods. Each textbook is designed for progressive mastery, conceptual depth, and long-form academic study.

Overview

The Mathematics Shelf represents a full academic pathway from early numerical literacy to advanced university-level mathematics. The sequence is designed so that each textbook builds directly on prior knowledge, ensuring continuity from basic computation to abstract reasoning, modeling, and applied mathematical systems.

These textbooks emphasize problem solving, conceptual understanding, formal reasoning, and mathematical communication. Together they form a unified curriculum covering both pure and applied mathematics across secondary and early university levels.

Mathematics

This sequence provides a complete mathematical education pathway from foundational arithmetic through advanced topics in analysis, algebra, geometry, probability, and computational mathematics.

  • 01-Arithmetic

    A foundational mathematics textbook covering number sense, operations, fractions, decimals, ratios, percentages, measurement, word problems, and early algebra readiness.

  • 02-Algebra 1

    An introductory algebra textbook covering expressions, equations, inequalities, functions, graphing, linear relationships, systems of equations, exponents, polynomials, factoring, and quadratic foundations.

  • 03-Algebra 2

    An advanced algebra textbook covering polynomial, rational, radical, exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric functions with deeper modeling and equation-solving techniques.

  • 04-Geometry

    A geometry textbook covering points, lines, angles, polygons, circles, congruence, similarity, geometric proof, coordinate geometry, transformations, area, volume, and spatial reasoning.

  • 05-Trigonometry and Precalculus

    A precalculus textbook covering trigonometric functions, identities, vectors, complex numbers, conic sections, sequences, series, limits, and preparation for calculus.

  • 06-High School Statistics

    A statistics textbook covering data analysis, variation, distributions, sampling, probability, correlation, regression, inference, and statistical reasoning.

  • 07-Linear Algebra

    A linear algebra textbook covering vectors, matrices, systems of equations, linear transformations, determinants, vector spaces, eigenvalues, eigenvectors, orthogonality, and applications.

  • 08-Calculus

    A calculus textbook covering limits, derivatives, integrals, applications, sequences, series, and the mathematical study of continuous change.

  • 09-Discrete Mathematics

    A discrete mathematics textbook covering logic, sets, relations, functions, combinatorial reasoning, graph theory, recursion, algorithms, and proof techniques.

  • 10-Combinatorics

    A combinatorics textbook covering counting principles, permutations, combinations, binomial coefficients, recurrence relations, generating functions, and combinatorial proofs.

  • 11-Differential Equations

    A differential equations textbook covering first-order equations, systems of equations, modeling, linear differential equations, Laplace transforms, numerical methods, and qualitative behavior of solutions.

  • 12-Probability and Statistics

    A probability and statistics textbook covering random variables, probability distributions, expectation, variance, estimation, hypothesis testing, regression, and statistical inference.

  • 13-Numerical Methods

    A numerical methods textbook covering approximation techniques, error analysis, root-finding algorithms, interpolation, numerical differentiation, numerical integration, linear systems, and computational modeling.
