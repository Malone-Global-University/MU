11-Differential Equations

Differential equations describe how quantities change in relation to one another and form the mathematical backbone of modeling in physics, engineering, biology, economics, and applied sciences.

Overview

This textbook introduces the theory and applications of differential equations, beginning with first-order methods and progressing through higher-order equations, systems, Laplace transforms, numerical methods, and partial differential equations. Students learn both analytical and computational techniques for solving dynamic systems.

Learning Outcomes

  • Classify and model real-world problems using differential equations.
  • Solve first-order and higher-order differential equations using standard techniques.
  • Analyze physical systems such as oscillations and mechanical vibrations.
  • Apply Laplace transforms to solve linear differential equations.
  • Use numerical methods for approximate solutions.
  • Analyze systems of differential equations and dynamic interactions.
  • Understand introductory partial differential equations.
