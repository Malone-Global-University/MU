08-Calculus

Calculus is the mathematics of change, motion, accumulation, and optimization. Students learn how limits, derivatives, and integrals provide powerful tools for analyzing natural phenomena, engineering systems, economics, and scientific models.

Overview

This textbook introduces differential and integral calculus through conceptual understanding, computational techniques, and real-world applications. Students develop the mathematical foundations necessary for advanced study in mathematics, science, engineering, economics, computer science, and related disciplines.

Learning Outcomes

  • Evaluate limits and analyze continuity.
  • Compute derivatives using multiple differentiation techniques.
  • Apply derivatives to optimization, rates of change, and curve analysis.
  • Evaluate definite and indefinite integrals.
  • Apply integration to area, volume, accumulation, and physical modeling.
  • Solve and interpret basic differential equations.
  • Analyze infinite sequences and series.
  • Work with parametric equations, polar coordinates, and vector-valued functions.
  • Use calculus to model real-world phenomena in science, engineering, economics, and technology.
