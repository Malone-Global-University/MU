09-Discrete Mathematics

Discrete mathematics is the study of structures that are fundamentally countable and separate, forming the mathematical foundation of logic, computation, algorithms, and modern information systems.

Overview

This textbook develops the core language of mathematical reasoning used in computer science and modern mathematics. Topics include logic, proof techniques, sets, combinatorics, number theory, algorithms, and Boolean algebra. Students learn to model discrete systems and construct rigorous proofs.

Learning Outcomes

  • Use formal logic to analyze and construct mathematical arguments.
  • Apply set theory and relations in structured problem solving.
  • Construct proofs using direct, contrapositive, contradiction, and induction methods.
  • Analyze recursive definitions and sequences.
  • Apply modular arithmetic in number theory and computation.
  • Solve combinatorial and probability-based counting problems.
  • Model networks and optimization problems using graph structures.
  • Understand algorithmic thinking and computational processes.
  • Work with Boolean algebra in digital logic systems.
