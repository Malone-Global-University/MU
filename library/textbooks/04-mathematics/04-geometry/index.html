04-Geometry

Geometry explores the properties of shapes, space, measurement, proof, and logical reasoning. Students investigate points, lines, angles, polygons, circles, transformations, similarity, congruence, and geometric relationships that form the foundation of mathematics, engineering, architecture, and science.

Overview

Geometry develops spatial reasoning and deductive thinking through the study of two-dimensional and three-dimensional figures. Students learn how geometric systems are constructed, how proofs establish mathematical truth, and how geometric principles apply to real-world design, measurement, construction, navigation, and technology.

Learning Outcomes

  • Apply geometric vocabulary, notation, and reasoning.
  • Analyze properties of points, lines, angles, triangles, polygons, and circles.
  • Construct logical geometric proofs and mathematical arguments.
  • Use congruence, similarity, and transformational geometry to solve problems.
  • Calculate perimeter, area, surface area, and volume.
  • Apply coordinate geometry and trigonometric relationships.
  • Model real-world structures and spatial relationships using geometric principles.
