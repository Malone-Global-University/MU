Textbook Shelves

The Textbook Shelves organize complete course-style learning PDF formatted ebooks across the major MGU Library subject areas. Each textbook is designed for sustained study, chapter-by-chapter instruction, academic development, and long-term reference.

Overview

Theses shelves gather MGU textbook projects into a single library index. The collection includes foundational humanities texts, history and social science surveys, business and economics texts, a full mathematics sequence, core science books, and computer science and technology books.

Textbooks differ from dictionary, encyclopedia, reference, and tutorial pages because they are designed for structured learning. They organize concepts into chapters, sections, examples, reviews, and structured explanations so that students can move from introductory knowledge toward deeper understanding. They are complete PDF textbooks.

Humanities

Textbooks in this shelf strengthen reading, writing, research, interpretation, and academic communication. These books support the skills students use across the entire MGU Library.

  • English Composition and Academic Writing

    A core writing textbook covering sentences, paragraphs, essays, research, citation, revision, rhetoric, and academic tone.

  • Literature Reading Interpretation and World Texts

    A literature textbook focused on close reading, literary forms, poetry, drama, fiction, symbolism, theme, genre, and historical context.

  • Research Methods and Information Literacy

    A research and library skills textbook covering research questions, source evaluation, evidence, misinformation, note organization, databases, and citation.

History and Social Science

Textbooks in this shelf examine societies, governments, civic life, historical change, institutions, rights, conflict, reform, and global development across time.

  • Government Civics and Political Systems

    A civics and political systems textbook covering constitutions, branches of government, federalism, elections, courts, rights, public policy, and comparative government.

  • United States History

    A United States history textbook covering Indigenous history, colonization, founding, slavery, the Civil War, Reconstruction, industrialization, world wars, civil rights, and contemporary America.

  • World History Civilizations Empires and Global Change

    A world history textbook organized around civilizations, empires, regions, exchanges, belief systems, revolutions, modernization, and global connections.

Mathematics

Textbooks in this shelf build a full mathematics pathway from arithmetic through algebra, geometry, trigonometry, calculus, statistics, discrete mathematics, linear algebra, differential equations, probability, and numerical methods.

  • 01-Arithmetic

    A foundational mathematics textbook covering number sense, operations, fractions, decimals, ratios, percents, measurement, word problems, and early algebra readiness.

  • 02-Algebra 1

    An algebra textbook covering expressions, equations, inequalities, functions, graphing, linear relationships, systems, exponents, polynomials, factoring, and quadratic foundations.

  • 03-Algebra 2

    An advanced algebra textbook covering polynomial, rational, radical, exponential, logarithmic, and trigonometric functions with deeper modeling and equation solving.

  • 04-Geometry

    A geometry textbook covering points, lines, angles, polygons, circles, congruence, similarity, proof, coordinate geometry, transformations, area, volume, and geometric reasoning.

  • 05-Trigonometry and Precalculus

    A precalculus textbook covering trigonometric functions, identities, vectors, complex numbers, conic sections, sequences, limits, and preparation for calculus.

  • 06-High School Statistics

    A high school statistics textbook covering data, variation, distributions, sampling, probability, correlation, regression, inference, and statistical reasoning.

  • 07-Linear Algebra

    A linear algebra textbook covering vectors, matrices, systems, transformations, determinants, vector spaces, eigenvalues, eigenvectors, orthogonality, and applications.

  • 08-Calculus

    A calculus textbook covering limits, derivatives, integrals, applications, differential equations, sequences, series, and the mathematical study of change.

  • 09-Discrete Mathematics

    A discrete mathematics textbook covering logic, sets, functions, relations, counting, graph theory, recursion, algorithms, proof methods, and finite structures.

  • 10-Combinatorics

    A combinatorics textbook covering counting principles, permutations, combinations, binomial coefficients, recurrence relations, generating functions, and combinatorial proof.

  • 11-Differential Equations

    A differential equations textbook covering first-order equations, systems, modeling, linear equations, Laplace transforms, numerical methods, and qualitative behavior.

  • 12-Probability and Statistics

    A calculus-based probability and statistics textbook covering random variables, distributions, expectation, estimation, hypothesis testing, regression, and statistical inference.

  • 13-Numerical Methods

    A numerical methods textbook covering approximation, error, root finding, interpolation, numerical differentiation, numerical integration, linear systems, and computational modeling.
