Humanities

The Humanities shelf gathers core textbooks in reading, writing, interpretation, and research. These books build the intellectual foundation students use across every other academic field in the MGU Library.

Overview

This shelf contains the humanities textbooks that support close reading, academic writing, source evaluation, and structured research. The collection is designed to move students from foundational literacy skills into stronger interpretation, argumentation, and evidence-based thinking.

Humanities textbooks differ from reference pages and quick-study tutorials because they are built for sustained learning. They organize concepts into chapters, examples, exercises, and review material so students can build durable academic skill over time.

Humanities Textbooks

These books strengthen communication, interpretation, and research fluency across the full academic program.

  • English Composition and Academic Writing

    A core writing textbook covering sentences, paragraphs, essays, research, citation, revision, rhetoric, and academic tone.

  • Literature Reading Interpretation and World Texts

    A literature textbook focused on close reading, literary forms, poetry, drama, fiction, symbolism, theme, genre, and historical context.

  • Research Methods and Information Literacy

    A research and library skills textbook covering research questions, source evaluation, evidence, misinformation, note organization, databases, and citation.

  • Practical Phonetics and the International Phonetic Alphabet

    An introduction to phonetics and the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA), covering speech production, consonants, vowels, suprasegmentals, transcription, acoustic phonetics, and phonetic analysis across the world's languages.
