English Composition

English Composition develops the skills necessary to communicate clearly, effectively, and persuasively through writing. Students learn the writing process, rhetorical strategies, research methods, critical reading, and academic communication.

Overview

This textbook introduces students to the principles of effective writing across academic, professional, and public contexts. Through reading, analysis, drafting, revision, and research, students learn how to construct clear arguments, organize information, support claims with evidence, and communicate with diverse audiences.

Learning Outcomes

  • Analyze audience, purpose, and rhetorical context.
  • Apply the writing process from planning through revision.
  • Develop clear thesis statements and organized arguments.
  • Use evidence effectively to support claims.
  • Write with clarity, coherence, and appropriate style.
  • Demonstrate proficiency in grammar, usage, and mechanics.
  • Conduct research using credible academic sources.
  • Integrate and cite sources using standard documentation practices.
  • Compose narrative, expository, analytical, and argumentative essays.
  • Build a portfolio demonstrating effective written communication.
