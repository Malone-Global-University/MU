MGU IPA

Practical Phonetics and the International Phonetic Alphabet introduces the scientific study of speech sounds and teaches students how to read, write, analyze, and interpret phonetic transcriptions using the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA). The textbook combines articulatory phonetics, acoustic evidence, and practical transcription skills to build a strong foundation for language study, linguistics, language teaching, speech sciences, and pronunciation.

Overview

MGU IPA provides a comprehensive introduction to phonetics and the International Phonetic Alphabet. Beginning with the nature of speech sounds and the anatomy of human speech production, students learn how consonants, vowels, suprasegmental features, and connected speech are represented using standardized phonetic notation. The text emphasizes practical transcription, careful observation, and scientific analysis, enabling learners to accurately describe pronunciation across languages. By the end of the book, students will be able to produce broad and narrow transcriptions, interpret acoustic evidence, analyze phonological systems, and construct a complete phonetic profile of a language or speaker.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain the goals, methods, and major branches of phonetics.
  • Understand how the human speech system produces spoken language.
  • Recognize and classify consonants and vowels using IPA conventions.
  • Identify places and manners of articulation.
  • Describe voicing, phonation, and airstream mechanisms.
  • Read and write broad and narrow phonetic transcriptions.
  • Apply IPA diacritics and suprasegmental notation accurately.
  • Analyze syllable structure, stress, rhythm, tone, and intonation.
  • Interpret acoustic evidence in phonetic analysis.
  • Investigate the sound systems of natural languages using scientific methods.
  • Create phonetic inventories and analyze phonological patterns.
  • Produce a comprehensive phonetic profile demonstrating mastery of IPA transcription and analysis.
