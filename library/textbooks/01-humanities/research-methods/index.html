Research Methods

Research methods is the study of how information is found, evaluated, organized, interpreted, cited, and used to build knowledge. It gives students the tools to ask good questions, locate reliable sources, and construct evidence-based understanding.

Overview

This textbook introduces research as a disciplined process of inquiry. Students learn how to identify information needs, search effectively, evaluate evidence, track sources, avoid plagiarism, use citation systems, and communicate findings responsibly across academic and professional contexts.

Learning Outcomes

  • Formulate research questions and plan research projects effectively.
  • Find, evaluate, and organize sources using libraries, databases, and the open web.
  • Distinguish between evidence, opinion, bias, and misinformation.
  • Track notes, quotations, paraphrases, and source information accurately.
  • Use citation systems responsibly to support academic integrity.
  • Write research papers and reports that synthesize multiple sources.
  • Apply research methods across different academic disciplines.
  • Use digital tools, including AI, ethically and responsibly.
  • Communicate research findings clearly in papers, posters, and presentations.
  • Develop lifelong information literacy and independent research skills.
