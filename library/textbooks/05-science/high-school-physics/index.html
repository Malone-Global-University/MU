High School Physics

Physics is the study of matter, energy, motion, forces, fields, and the laws that govern the physical universe. This comprehensive textbook develops a conceptual and mathematical understanding of nature from everyday motion to modern physics.

Overview

This textbook provides a complete introduction to high school and introductory college physics. Topics include mechanics, energy, waves, thermodynamics, electricity, magnetism, optics, modern physics, and applied scientific modeling. Students develop problem-solving skills, quantitative reasoning, and scientific literacy while learning how physical principles explain the natural world.

Learning Outcomes

  • Apply scientific reasoning to physical systems and phenomena.
  • Analyze motion, forces, momentum, and energy using mathematical models.
  • Explain waves, sound, light, and electromagnetic phenomena.
  • Understand thermodynamic processes and energy transfer.
  • Apply principles of electricity and magnetism.
  • Interpret physical data using graphs, equations, and experiments.
  • Understand the foundations of modern physics, including relativity and quantum theory.
  • Develop quantitative problem-solving skills used in science and engineering.
  • Connect physical principles to technology and everyday life.
