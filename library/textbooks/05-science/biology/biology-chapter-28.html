Biology

Biology is the scientific study of life. It explores living organisms, their structures, functions, origins, interactions, and the processes that sustain life from the molecular level to entire ecosystems.

Overview

This textbook introduces the major concepts of modern biology, including cell structure, genetics, evolution, ecology, physiology, biodiversity, and biotechnology. Students develop an understanding of life as an integrated system governed by chemical, genetic, and ecological processes.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain the fundamental characteristics shared by living organisms.
  • Describe the chemical and cellular foundations of life.
  • Analyze the processes of energy transformation in living systems.
  • Understand heredity, genetics, and molecular biology.
  • Evaluate the mechanisms and evidence for biological evolution.
  • Classify organisms and understand biodiversity.
  • Explain the structure and function of major biological systems.
  • Analyze ecological relationships and environmental challenges.
  • Interpret biological data and scientific evidence.
  • Apply biological principles to health, biotechnology, conservation, and society.
