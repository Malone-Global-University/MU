Environmental Science

Environmental science examines the interactions between humans, living organisms, Earth systems, and the natural environment. Students explore ecological principles, environmental challenges, sustainability, conservation, and evidence-based solutions for managing natural resources.

Overview

This textbook provides a comprehensive introduction to environmental science by integrating biology, chemistry, geology, geography, climatology, economics, and public policy. Students learn how Earth's systems function, how human activities affect those systems, and how scientific knowledge can be applied to environmental stewardship and sustainable development.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain the major components and processes of Earth's environmental systems.
  • Analyze ecosystem structure, biodiversity, and ecological relationships.
  • Evaluate human impacts on land, water, atmosphere, and biological systems.
  • Assess environmental issues using scientific evidence and data.
  • Understand pollution sources, environmental risks, and mitigation strategies.
  • Compare renewable and nonrenewable energy resources.
  • Analyze environmental policies, laws, and sustainability initiatives.
  • Apply systems thinking to complex environmental challenges.
  • Interpret environmental data and scientific studies.
  • Develop informed approaches to environmental stewardship and sustainable development.
