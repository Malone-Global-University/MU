Chemistry

Chemistry is the study of matter, its properties, its composition, and the transformations it undergoes. It connects the microscopic world of atoms and molecules to the observable world around us.

Overview

This textbook introduces the foundations of modern chemistry, including atomic theory, chemical bonding, reactions, stoichiometry, thermodynamics, equilibrium, acids and bases, electrochemistry, organic chemistry, and biochemistry. Students learn how chemical principles explain natural phenomena and support advances in science, medicine, engineering, and industry.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain the structure and behavior of matter at atomic and molecular scales.
  • Use the periodic table to predict chemical properties and relationships.
  • Analyze chemical bonding, molecular structure, and chemical reactions.
  • Perform stoichiometric calculations and quantitative chemical analysis.
  • Apply principles of thermodynamics, kinetics, and equilibrium.
  • Understand acids, bases, electrochemistry, and nuclear processes.
  • Describe the foundations of organic chemistry and biochemistry.
  • Evaluate environmental and industrial applications of chemistry.
  • Interpret chemical data using scientific reasoning and evidence.
  • Apply chemistry concepts to real-world scientific and technological problems.
