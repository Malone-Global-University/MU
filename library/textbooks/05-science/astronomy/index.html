Astronomy and Cosmology Foundations

Astronomy is the scientific study of celestial objects, space, and the universe as a whole. Cosmology extends this study to the origin, structure, evolution, and ultimate fate of the cosmos.

Overview

This textbook introduces the physical principles governing the universe, from observations of the night sky to the formation of stars, galaxies, black holes, and the large-scale structure of the cosmos. Students develop an understanding of modern astronomy, astrophysics, planetary science, and cosmology through scientific reasoning and observation.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain the fundamental objects and structures studied in astronomy.
  • Understand how astronomers observe and measure the universe.
  • Describe the formation and evolution of stars, planets, and galaxies.
  • Apply principles of gravity, motion, and light to astronomical phenomena.
  • Analyze evidence supporting modern cosmological theories.
  • Evaluate the roles of dark matter and dark energy in the universe.
  • Understand current research on exoplanets and astrobiology.
  • Interpret scientific evidence concerning the origin and evolution of the cosmos.
  • Develop scientific literacy regarding major discoveries and unanswered questions in astronomy.
