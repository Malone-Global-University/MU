Planos, possibilidades e condições

Unit IV, Chapter 1 · Speak and Write Portuguese: Independent Everyday Portuguese

Chapter Overview

Chapter 16 opens Unit IV and moves the course from narrating the past into talking about what comes next. You will learn to refer to future events with the simple future, offer polite suggestions and soft requests with formulaic conditional expressions such as gostaria and poderia, and state realistic conditions with controlled se clauses like se eu tiver tempo, eu vou. Building directly on the participation language from Chapter 14 and 15, this chapter keeps every hypothetical grounded in ordinary, practical possibilities — not abstract speculation.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Refer to future events using the simple future tense of regular and high-frequency irregular verbs.
  2. Make polite suggestions and soft requests using the formulaic conditional forms gostaria, poderia, and seria.
  3. State a realistic condition and its result using controlled se clauses with the present indicative.
  4. Present two or three alternatives and explain the conditions under which you would choose each one.
  5. Ask for clarification about someone else's plans or conditions.

Communication Scenario

A friend is helping you decide between two or three options for an upcoming weekend, trip, or event — real or invented. For each option, you explain what you would do, under what conditions you would choose it, and what you will do if things go a certain way. Your friend asks follow-up questions and offers their own suggestions.

  1. The productive target is a structured comparison of alternatives that states plans, polite suggestions, and realistic conditions.
  2. Only the simple future, formulaic conditional expressions, and controlled se clauses with the present indicative are required; full subjunctive paradigms are not introduced here.
  3. You may invent the options, the event, and your own preferences.
  4. Brazilian Portuguese remains the declared production model for all required production in this chapter.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Future-tense endings and the linked -ia of the conditional

The simple future is built by adding a stressed ending directly onto the infinitive: falará, comerá, decidirá. Make sure the final syllable carries the stress and the written accent is not dropped. The formulaic conditional forms gostaria, poderia, and seria all end in an unstressed -ia; in natural speech this glides quickly, closer to two syllables than three. Practice this sentence aloud: Se eu tiver tempo, eu vou viajar no sábado, mas talvez eu prefira ficar em casa; de qualquer jeito, seria bom descansar um pouco.

If I have time, I'm going to travel on Saturday, but maybe I'll prefer to stay home; either way, it would be good to rest a little.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces three new systems for talking about the future and hypothetical plans: the simple future, formulaic conditional expressions, and controlled se clauses. Each is staged carefully so that it supports realistic planning language without requiring the full subjunctive system, which begins in Chapter 17.

Simple Future for Plans and Predictions

The simple future adds one set of endings directly to the infinitive of all three verb classes: -ei, -ás, -á, -emos, -ão. A small number of high-frequency verbs shorten the infinitive before adding these endings.

  • Eu viajarei no fim de semana. I will travel on the weekend. viajar + -ei
  • Nós faremos uma reserva amanhã. We will make a reservation tomorrow. fazer → far- + -emos (shortened stem)

In everyday Brazilian speech, ir + infinitivo (introduced in Volume I) remains far more common than the simple future for near plans. The simple future is developed here mainly for predictions, written plans, and slightly more formal statements; both forms are acceptable production in this chapter.

Formulaic Conditional: Gostaria, Poderia, Seria

Three conditional forms are introduced here as fixed, memorized expressions rather than a full paradigm. They soften requests, suggestions, and hypothetical statements.

  • Eu gostaria de viajar para o litoral. I would like to travel to the coast.
  • Você poderia me ajudar a escolher? Could you help me choose?
  • Seria mais barato viajar de ônibus. It would be cheaper to travel by bus.

Only these three forms are productive in this chapter. Do not extend the conditional to other verbs or persons yet; that full development belongs to a later volume.

Controlled Se Clauses: Real Conditions

A controlled se clause states a realistic, likely condition. The se clause uses the present indicative; the result clause uses the present indicative or the simple future (or ir + infinitivo).

  • Se eu tiver tempo, eu viajo no sábado. If I have time, I travel on Saturday. se + present (tiver) → present (viajo)
  • Se o orçamento permitir, nós vamos ficar em um hotel melhor. If the budget allows, we're going to stay in a better hotel.

Tiver and quiser, already used formulaically since Chapter 14, are the two most common present-subjunctive forms that appear inside these se clauses. Treat them here as fixed patterns tied to se and quando; their fuller subjunctive analysis begins in Chapter 17. Do not introduce hypothetical or contrary-to-fact se clauses (with the imperfect subjunctive) in this chapter.

Model Dialogue

Context: Two friends compare weekend options. Everyday spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Rafael Eu estou pensando em duas opções para o fim de semana: a praia ou a montanha. Você poderia me ajudar a decidir? I'm thinking about two options for the weekend: the beach or the mountains. Could you help me decide?
Bianca Claro! Depende do orçamento. Se o orçamento permitir, eu escolheria a praia; é mais divertido. Sure! It depends on the budget. If the budget allows, I would choose the beach; it's more fun.
Rafael Se eu tiver dinheiro suficiente, eu irei para a praia. Se não, eu ficarei por aqui mesmo. If I have enough money, I'll go to the beach. If not, I'll just stay around here.
Bianca Seria bom de qualquer jeito. Eu gostaria de ir junto, se você não se importar. It would be good either way. I would like to go along, if you don't mind.
Rafael Perfeito. Quando eu souber o valor da viagem, eu te aviso. Perfect. When I find out the cost of the trip, I'll let you know.
  1. What two options is Rafael considering?
  2. What does the final decision depend on?
  3. Find one simple future verb, one formulaic conditional expression, and one controlled se clause in the exchange.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script — Choosing a weekend plan
Eu tenho duas opções para o próximo fim de semana: ficar em casa e descansar, ou viajar para a casa dos meus pais. Se eu tiver energia depois do trabalho, eu viajarei na sexta à noite. Se não, eu ficarei em casa e talvez eu convide alguns amigos. Seria bom rever minha família, mas também gostaria de descansar um pouco. De qualquer jeito, eu decidirei até quinta-feira.
I have two options for next weekend: staying home and resting, or traveling to my parents' house. If I have energy after work, I'll travel on Friday night. If not, I'll stay home and maybe invite some friends. It would be good to see my family again, but I would also like to rest a little. Either way, I'll decide by Thursday.
  1. What are the speaker's two options for the weekend?
  2. Under what condition will the speaker travel on Friday night?
  3. By when will the speaker decide?

Reading Practice

Dois planos para as férias

Minha irmã e eu estamos planejando as férias de julho. Temos duas opções: visitar uma cidade histórica ou ficar em uma praia tranquila. Se o preço das passagens baixar, nós viajaremos para a cidade histórica, porque gostaríamos de conhecer os museus e os prédios antigos. Se não, provavelmente ficaremos na praia, que também seria uma boa opção. De qualquer jeito, nós decidiremos até o fim do mês, porque precisamos reservar o hotel com antecedência.

  1. What two vacation options are being considered?
  2. Under what condition will they travel to the historic city?
  3. What formulaic conditional expression describes the beach option?
  4. By when do they need to decide, and why?

Writing Workshop

Write a short paragraph (7–9 sentences) presenting two or three options for a plan, event, or trip, stating a realistic condition for each, and closing with a polite suggestion.

  1. Name the plan, event, or trip and the options you are considering.
  2. State a realistic condition and result for the first option using a controlled se clause.
  3. State a realistic condition and result for a second option, using the simple future or ir + infinitivo.
  4. Offer a polite suggestion or preference using gostaria, poderia, or seria.
  5. Close with when you will decide, using quando or a time expression.

Revision checklist

  • I stated at least one realistic condition using a controlled se clause with the present indicative.
  • I used at least one simple future verb correctly formed and accented.
  • I used at least one formulaic conditional expression (gostaria, poderia, or seria).
  • I did not extend the conditional beyond these three fixed forms.
  • I preserved all diacritics, including in condição, possibilidade, and orçamento.
  • I did not introduce hypothetical or contrary-to-fact se clauses or full subjunctive paradigms.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Keeping plans open and practical

In many everyday Brazilian contexts, plans are often stated with some built-in flexibility — de qualquer jeito, vamos ver, depende — rather than as fixed commitments, especially among friends and family. This does not mean plans are unimportant; it reflects a common conversational habit of naming the condition that will determine the outcome, which is exactly the function of the se clauses in this chapter. Formality and firmness of commitment still vary by relationship, setting, and region, so this pattern should not be treated as a fixed national trait.

As in earlier chapters, personal disclosure about your own real plans, budget, or family remains entirely optional. You may describe a real situation or invent one; what matters is combining future reference, formulaic conditional expressions, and realistic conditions accurately.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Treating the se clause as hypothetical rather than real

Error: Using a controlled se clause for something clearly imaginary or contrary to fact, such as Se eu fosse rico, eu viajaria para todos os países.
Better: Reserve se clauses in this chapter for realistic, likely conditions: Se eu tiver dinheiro suficiente, eu viajarei para a praia.

The imperfect subjunctive and the hypothetical se pattern are not active in this chapter. Keep every condition grounded in something that could plausibly happen.

Common Mistake — Conjugating gostaria, poderia, or seria for other persons

Error: Eu poderia, ela poderias...
Better: Use gostaria, poderia, and seria only in the fixed forms taught here; do not attempt to build a full conditional paradigm yet.

These forms are formulaic at this stage. A fuller conditional system is not part of this chapter's scope.
