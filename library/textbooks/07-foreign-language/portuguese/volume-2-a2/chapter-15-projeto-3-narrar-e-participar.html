Projeto 3: Narrar e participar

Unit III Assessment · Speak and Write Portuguese: Independent Everyday Portuguese

Chapter Overview

Chapter 15 is the Unit III assessment. It does not introduce new grammar, new vocabulary domains, or new pronunciation targets. Instead, it asks you to bring together everything from Chapters 11 through 14 into a single connected scenario: telling a sequence of recent events, describing past routines and background, supporting an opinion, and discussing community participation with quando and se clauses. The task in this chapter is a community scenario in which you narrate what happened, explain how things used to be, give your opinion, and describe your own conditions for getting involved — all in one coherent piece of speaking and writing.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Narrate a short sequence of recent events using the preterite and connectors such as primeiro, depois, então, and porque.
  2. Describe past routines, habits, and background using the imperfect, contrasted with completed events in the preterite.
  3. Support an opinion about a community event or issue using achar que, preferir, and comparative or superlative forms.
  4. Discuss civic participation using quando and se clauses with the formulaic patterns tiver and quiser.
  5. Combine narration, opinion, and participation into a single organized spoken and written performance.
  6. Ask for clarification, repetition, or confirmation when participating in a community-based exchange.

Communication Scenario

Your neighborhood association held a community event last month — a mutirão, a fundraiser, or a public meeting, real or invented. A friend who could not attend asks you what happened, what the neighborhood used to be like before, what you thought of the event, and whether you plan to get involved in the next one. You respond with a connected account that narrates, reflects on the past, gives an opinion, and explains your future participation.

  1. The productive target is an integrated narration-and-participation task that draws only on systems active through Chapter 14.
  2. No new grammar, vocabulary domain, or pronunciation feature is introduced in this chapter.
  3. You may invent the event, the neighborhood, and your own level of involvement.
  4. Brazilian Portuguese remains the declared production model for all required production in this chapter.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review — Linking narration and condition

Review the unstressed linking of quando and se from Chapter 14, along with the preterite/imperfect contrast in connected speech from Chapters 11–12. Practice this review sentence aloud, noting where stress falls: Antigamente, a gente não tinha um centro comunitário, mas ano passado a associação organizou um mutirão, e quando eu tiver tempo, eu vou ajudar de novo.

In old days, we didn't have a community center, but last year the association organized a work party, and when I have time, I'm going to help again.

Grammar Review

No new grammar is introduced in this chapter. The following review consolidates the four systems this project draws on.

Narration: Preterite and Sequencing

  • Primeiro, a associação anunciou o mutirão; depois, os moradores se organizaram. First, the association announced the work party; afterward, the residents organized themselves.

Background: Imperfect and Preterite/Imperfect Contrast

  • Antigamente, o bairro não tinha muitos eventos, mas o ano passado mudou tudo. In the old days, the neighborhood didn't have many events, but last year changed everything.

Opinion: Achar que and Comparison

  • Eu acho que o mutirão foi mais organizado do que a reunião anterior. I think the work party was more organized than the previous meeting.

Participation: Quando and Se Clauses

  • Quando eu tiver tempo, eu ajudo; se a associação precisar de voluntários, eu vou participar. When I have time, I help; if the association needs volunteers, I'm going to participate.
Building Block — Narrate, reflect, opine, commit
[Narração no pretérito] + [Contraste com o imperfeito] + [Achar que + opinião] + [Quando/se + participação]
the four systems combine in one connected account: what happened, how things used to be, what you think, and what you will do
No mês passado, a associação organizou um mutirão no parque. Antigamente, ninguém cuidava daquele espaço, mas agora está diferente. Eu acho que foi um dos melhores eventos do bairro. Quando tiver outro mutirão, eu vou participar de novo.
Last month, the association organized a work party in the park. In the old days, nobody took care of that space, but now it's different. I think it was one of the best events in the neighborhood. When there is another work party, I'm going to participate again.

Prática guiada — Guided practice: Complete each sentence using only systems active through Chapter 14.

  1. No sábado passado, a associação ___ (organizar) um mutirão no parque.
  2. Antigamente, o bairro ___ (não ter) muitos eventos.
  3. Eu acho que o evento foi ___ (mais / interessante) do que eu esperava.
  4. ___ (Quando / eu / ter) tempo, eu vou ajudar na próxima reunião.

Model Exchange

Context: A friend who missed the community event asks about it. Everyday spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Camila E aí, como foi o mutirão da associação? So, how was the association's work party?
Diego Foi ótimo! Primeiro, a gente limpou o parque; depois, todo mundo plantou árvores. Antigamente, ninguém se importava com aquele espaço. It was great! First, we cleaned the park; afterward, everyone planted trees. In the old days, nobody cared about that space.
Camila Que legal. E o que você achou? Nice. And what did you think of it?
Diego Eu acho que foi mais organizado do que o do ano passado. Se a associação organizar outro, eu vou participar de novo. I think it was more organized than last year's. If the association organizes another one, I'm going to participate again.
Camila Quando tiver a próxima data, me avisa! When you have the next date, let me know!
  1. What two things did the group do at the work party, in order?
  2. What comparison does Diego make about this year's event?
  3. Find one preterite verb, one imperfect verb, one opinion expression, and one se clause in the exchange.

Listening Task

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script — A resident reflects on the mutirão
No mês passado, a associação organizou um mutirão no parque perto da minha casa. Primeiro, todo mundo se encontrou de manhã; depois, a gente limpou o parque e plantou árvores novas. Antigamente, esse parque estava sempre sujo e ninguém cuidava dele. Eu acho que o mutirão foi uma das melhores iniciativas do bairro este ano. Quando a associação organizar outro, eu vou participar de novo, e se meus vizinhos quiserem vir, eu vou chamar todos eles.
Last month, the association organized a work party in the park near my house. First, everyone met in the morning; afterward, we cleaned the park and planted new trees. In the old days, this park was always dirty and nobody took care of it. I think the work party was one of the best initiatives in the neighborhood this year. When the association organizes another one, I'm going to participate again, and if my neighbors want to come, I'm going to invite them all.
  1. What two things did the group do at the work party, in order?
  2. How does the speaker describe the park in the past, before the event?
  3. Under what two conditions does the speaker plan to get involved again?

Reading Task

Um bairro que mudou

Antigamente, a praça do bairro não tinha bancos nem árvores, e poucas pessoas passavam por lá. No ano passado, a associação de moradores organizou um mutirão para transformar o espaço. Primeiro, os voluntários limpararam a praça; depois, plantaram árvores e pintaram os bancos. Muitos moradores acham que a praça ficou mais bonita e mais útil do que antes. Se a associação organizar outro mutirão este ano, muita gente pretende participar de novo, e quando o próximo evento acontecer, a prefeitura vai ajudar com mais recursos.

  1. What did the square lack in the old days?
  2. What two things did the volunteers do, in order?
  3. What do many residents think of the square now, compared with before?
  4. What will happen when the next work party takes place?

Writing Task

Write a short paragraph (7–9 sentences) narrating a community event, contrasting it with the past, giving your opinion, and describing your future participation.

  1. Name the community event, service, or organization involved.
  2. Narrate what happened, in order, using the preterite and connectors such as primeiro and depois.
  3. Describe how things used to be before, using the imperfect.
  4. Give your opinion of the event using achar que and a comparative or superlative form.
  5. State the conditions under which you would participate again, using a quando clause and a se clause.

Revision checklist

  • I narrated events in a clear order using the preterite.
  • I described past background or habits using the imperfect.
  • I gave an opinion using achar que and a comparative or superlative form.
  • I used at least one quando clause and one se clause correctly.
  • I preserved all diacritics, including in associação, reunião, and antigamente.
  • I did not introduce grammar beyond Chapter 14, such as full future subjunctive paradigms or hypothetical se.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Telling the story of a changing neighborhood

Talking about how a neighborhood, community group, or public space has changed over time is a common and natural way to connect narration to community life. This kind of account, contrasting antigamente with recent events, appears in everyday conversation across many Brazilian communities, though the specific practices, organizations, and pace of change vary a great deal by city, region, and social context.

As in Chapter 14, personal disclosure about your own neighborhood remains entirely optional. You may describe a real place or invent one; what matters is combining narration, contrast, opinion, and conditional participation accurately, not revealing details about your actual community.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Narrating background events in the preterite

Error: Antigamente, o bairro não teve muitos eventos.
Better: Antigamente, o bairro não tinha muitos eventos.

A repeated or ongoing state in the past, marked by antigamente, calls for the imperfect, not the preterite. Reserve the preterite for the completed action or event itself.

Common Mistake — Losing the opinion or condition in a long narration

Error: A response that narrates events in detail but never states an opinion or a condition for future participation.
Better: Structure the response in four clear moves: what happened, how it used to be, what you think, and what you will do.

This chapter's task specifically requires all four elements together; a narration alone, however accurate, does not meet the performance criteria.
