Projeto final: Agir com independência

Unit IV, Chapter 5 (Final B1 Assessment) · Speak and Write Portuguese: Independent Everyday Portuguese

Chapter Overview

Chapter 20 is the final assessment of Volume II. It does not introduce new productive grammar, new vocabulary domains, new pronunciation targets, or new orthographic burdens. Instead, it asks you to bring together everything you have built since Chapter 1 — housing and moving, study and career, health, media and technology, food and market habits, service complaints, travel, recent events, memories and past habits, opinions, community participation, plans and conditions, wishes and advice, public texts, and Lusophone awareness — into one independent, real-world scenario portfolio. You will move through a connected situation as a single storyline: settling into a new city, handling a problem, reflecting on how things have gone, and making plans for what comes next, entirely in your declared Brazilian Portuguese production model.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Narrate a connected sequence of recent events and describe relevant past habits and background using preterite and imperfect together.
  2. Handle a housing, service, or travel problem from start to finish, including a polite complaint and a resolution.
  3. Give and support an opinion, and offer advice or a recommendation using scoped subjunctive and evaluative expressions.
  4. Discuss possibilities and conditions for future plans using future reference and controlled conditional and se-clause patterns.
  5. Read and summarize a public or informational text and respond to it appropriately.
  6. Produce an extended, organized piece of writing and a sustained independent oral account that integrate multiple Volume II systems.
  7. Maintain consistent Brazilian Portuguese production while labeling any Lusophone variation mentioned along the way.

Communication Scenario

You have recently moved to a new city for study or work. Over the past few months you found housing, settled into a routine, dealt with a service problem, traveled somewhere in the Lusophone world, and formed opinions about your new life. A friend, a classmate, or a new acquaintance asks you to tell the whole story: how it started, what happened, what you think about it now, and what you plan to do next. You respond with a connected account and a follow-up written message, drawing on the full range of systems developed across this volume.

  1. The productive target is an integrated spoken and written account, produced entirely in Brazilian Portuguese.
  2. You may invent every detail of the scenario or adapt it from your own experience.
  3. No new grammar, vocabulary domain, or pronunciation feature is introduced; every tool you need was activated in Chapters 1–19.
  4. Any Lusophone variation you choose to mention must remain clearly labeled and must not be produced as an unmarked mix with Brazilian Portuguese.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review — Consolidating Brazilian Portuguese production

No new pronunciation feature is introduced in this chapter. As you prepare your speaking task, review the features that have been building across the volume: full vowel articulation, nasal vowels and nasal diphthongs in words like não, também, and informação, stress placement in past-tense verb forms such as morei versus morou, and natural rhythm across longer connected sentences. Read your written response aloud slowly before recording your spoken task, paying attention to any word where you are unsure of the stressed syllable.

Grammar Review and Integration

This chapter introduces no new productive grammar. It requires you to combine, in a single connected account, the major systems consolidated across Volume II: preterite/imperfect narration, comparison and opinion structures, future and conditional plans, controlled se-clauses, and the limited productive subjunctive frames from Chapter 17.

Combining Past Narration with Present Opinion

  • Quando eu cheguei à cidade, eu não conhecia ninguém, mas agora acho que fiz a escolha certa. When I arrived in the city, I didn't know anyone, but now I think I made the right choice. preterite + imperfect + achar que, active since Ch. 11-13
  • No começo, tudo parecia difícil, mas com o tempo tudo ficou mais fácil. At first, everything seemed difficult, but over time everything became easier. imperfect background + preterite resolution

Combining Plans, Conditions, and Advice

  • Se tudo der certo, vou continuar morando aqui e talvez comece um curso novo. If everything works out, I'm going to keep living here and maybe start a new course. se-clause + future/ir + infinitive + subjunctive frame, active since Ch. 16-17
  • Um amigo me recomendou que eu procurasse ajuda antes de decidir sozinho. A friend recommended that I look for help before deciding on my own. formulaic recommendation pattern from Ch. 17, recognition-level past subjunctive

The second example uses a formulaic recommendation frame already modeled in Chapter 17; you are not expected to generate new subjunctive paradigms independently.

Building Block — The full-story frame
Quando eu [pretérito]... eu [imperfeito]. Depois, [pretérito/sequência]. Hoje, eu acho que... e, se der certo, eu vou...
a scaffold combining narration, opinion, and future planning without any new grammar
Quando eu me mudei para cá, eu não tinha muitos amigos. Depois, comecei a participar de eventos no bairro e conheci pessoas legais. Hoje, eu acho que valeu a pena, e, se tudo der certo, vou ficar mais um ano.
When I moved here, I didn't have many friends. Then, I started participating in neighborhood events and met nice people. Today, I think it was worth it, and, if everything works out, I'm going to stay another year.

Model Dialogue

Context: A new acquaintance asks about the learner's first months in the city. Everyday spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Bianca Me conta, como foi quando você chegou aqui? Tell me, what was it like when you arrived here?
Marcos No começo foi difícil. Eu não conhecia ninguém e tive um problema sério com o aluguel do apartamento. Reclamei com o proprietário várias vezes até resolver. At first it was hard. I didn't know anyone and I had a serious problem with the apartment rent. I complained to the landlord several times until it got resolved.
Bianca Nossa, que chato. E depois melhorou? Wow, how annoying. And did it get better afterward?
Marcos Melhorou bastante. Comecei a participar de eventos da comunidade, viajei para Cabo Verde nas férias e conheci gente incrível. Hoje eu acho que valeu muito a pena. It got much better. I started participating in community events, I traveled to Cape Verde during vacation, and I met incredible people. Today I think it was really worth it.
Bianca E o que você recomenda para quem está chegando agora? And what do you recommend for someone who is arriving now?
Marcos Eu recomendo que a pessoa participe da comunidade desde o início. É importante que a gente não fique isolado. E, se der certo, quero ficar mais alguns anos aqui. I recommend that the person get involved in the community from the start. It's important that we don't stay isolated. And, if it works out, I want to stay here a few more years.
  1. What problem did Marcos have when he first arrived?
  2. Name two things that improved his experience over time.
  3. What does Marcos recommend for a newcomer, and what future plan does he mention?

Writing Workshop

Write an extended message or short account (10–14 sentences) to a friend telling the full story of your independent scenario: your arrival, one problem and how you resolved it, how your routine and opinions developed, and your plans if things go well.

  1. Open by describing your arrival and initial situation using the imperfect for background.
  2. Narrate one specific problem and its resolution using the preterite.
  3. Describe how your routine, food habits, or community involvement changed over time.
  4. Give your current opinion using an evaluative or opinion expression from Chapters 13 or 17.
  5. Close with a plan using a future or se-clause structure from Chapter 16.

Revision checklist

  • I distinguished preterite and imperfect correctly throughout the narration.
  • I included at least one opinion and one recommendation or wish.
  • I included at least one future or conditional plan.
  • I labeled any non-Brazilian Portuguese form I mentioned.
  • I preserved all diacritics, agreement, and punctuation conventions from earlier chapters.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Independent participation in Lusophone life

Independent communicators do more than exchange information: they participate in the life of a community, ask for help when needed, offer their own opinions and advice, and adapt to unfamiliar situations with confidence. Across this volume you have practiced these behaviors in the context of Brazilian Portuguese while learning to recognize and respect variation across Portugal, Angola, Mozambique, Cape Verde, Guinea-Bissau, São Tomé and Príncipe, Timor-Leste, and diaspora communities. This chapter asks you to demonstrate that independence by combining narration, complaint and resolution, opinion, advice, and future planning into a single coherent story, entirely in your declared production model.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Losing preterite/imperfect contrast in longer narration

Error: Quando eu chegava, eu não conheci ninguém. (imperfect and preterite reversed)
Better: Quando eu cheguei, eu não conhecia ninguém. (preterite for the arrival event, imperfect for the ongoing state)

In a long connected account, it is easy to lose track of which verb marks a completed event and which marks background. Reread your narration and check each verb individually.

Common Mistake — Mixing Lusophone forms without labels in an integrated account

Error: Eu fui apanhar o ônibus e depois fui para casa. (unlabeled European verb inside a Brazilian sentence)
Better: Eu fui pegar o ônibus e depois fui para casa.

Even in a long, multi-topic account, keep your own production consistently Brazilian Portuguese. Mention other varieties only with clear labels, as practiced in Chapter 19.

Guided Practice — Exercício A (sequence)

  1. Correct order: 2, 3, 1, 4 (arrival background → developing routine → present opinion → future plan).

Guided Practice — Exercício B (correction)

  1. Correct as written: "Quando eu era criança, eu morava em uma casa pequena" is the natural habitual form; "morei" would imply a single completed stay rather than a repeated childhood state.
  2. "Se eu tiver tempo amanhã, eu vou viajar." (present subjunctive required after "se" referring to a future condition)
  3. "É importante que a gente participe da comunidade." (subjunctive required after the evaluative "é importante que" frame)
