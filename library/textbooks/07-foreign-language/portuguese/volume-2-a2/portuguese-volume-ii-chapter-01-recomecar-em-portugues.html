Recomeçar em português

Reconnecting and Review · Speak and Write Portuguese: Independent Everyday Portuguese

Chapter Overview

Welcome back to Portuguese. In Volume I, you built the foundations of practical communication: greetings, identity, routines, the past, and simple opinions. Volume II begins by reconnecting with everything you already know and then stretching it further. In this chapter, you will reintroduce yourself with more depth than before — not just who you are, but your background, your language-learning story, and your goals — and you will practice sustaining a longer, more natural exchange instead of a short exercise. You will also begin actively managing your own conversations: asking someone to repeat, confirm, or slow down when communication breaks down. This chapter activates no unfamiliar grammar; it strengthens agreement, pronunciation, and confidence in systems you already control, while opening the door to the independent, connected communication that defines this volume.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Reintroduce yourself with expanded detail about your background, interests, and goals.
  2. Explain your personal history with the Portuguese language and why you are continuing to study it.
  3. Sustain a longer conversational exchange by linking several related ideas together.
  4. Ask for clarification, repetition, or confirmation when you do not understand something.
  5. Recognize and correct common fossilized errors from earlier study.
  6. Read and write a short, connected personal profile appropriate to independent-level communication.

Communication Scenario

You have joined a Portuguese conversation group that meets for the first time this week. Before the first meeting, each member records a short audio introduction and writes a short welcome message so that the group can get to know each other. You want to say more than a beginner "hello" — you want to explain who you are, your connection to Portuguese, and what you hope to get out of the group.

  1. This chapter reviews and strengthens Volume I systems; it does not introduce major new grammar.
  2. The productive target is one sustained oral introduction and one written welcome message that connect several ideas naturally.
  3. You may use fictional or generalized personal information at any point.
  4. Only systems marked active, developed, or consolidated through Volume I, Chapter 20 are required for independent production.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Note — Rhythm across a longer turn

Volume I focused on individual sentences. In Volume II, you will begin producing longer, connected turns of speech, so pronunciation now includes managing rhythm and pausing across several linked sentences, not only pronouncing single words correctly. Pay attention to nasal vowels in words you already know, such as então, também, and não, since they tend to weaken when speakers rush through a longer account. Pause briefly at connectors such as além disso and por isso rather than running every idea together without a break.

Intelligibility across a sustained turn, not native-like accent, is the goal of this chapter.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This section reviews and strengthens grammar activated in Volume I. It does not introduce new productive systems; the goal is accuracy, agreement, and confident cumulative control as you move toward independent communication.

Present Tense and Duration with faz

  • Eu estudo português há dois anos. I have been studying Portuguese for two years. present tense + duration expression
  • Faz um tempo que eu não pratico, mas quero continuar. It's been a while since I've practiced, but I want to continue. faz + time + que, review of duration

Past Review: Preterite and Imperfect Together

  • Eu comecei a estudar português no ano passado porque queria viajar para o Brasil. I started studying Portuguese last year because I wanted to travel to Brazil. preterite (comecei) + imperfect (queria)

Goals and Intentions

  • O meu objetivo este ano é falar com mais confiança e entender filmes sem legenda. My goal this year is to speak with more confidence and understand movies without subtitles. ser + infinitive complement, expanded goal statement

Repair and Clarification Formulas

  • Desculpa, pode repetir? Eu não entendi bem essa parte. Sorry, could you repeat that? I didn't quite understand that part. formulaic repair request, imperative + question
Building Block — An expanded self-introduction
identity + duration with the language + a past reason + a present goal, connected with porque, mas, além disso, por isso
the chapter task links present, past, and future goals into one sustained introduction, reusing only Volume I systems
Oi, eu me chamo Marina. Eu estudo português há três anos. Comecei a estudar porque tenho amigos brasileiros, mas parei de praticar por um tempo. Agora eu voltei porque quero conversar com mais confiança. Além disso, o meu objetivo é ler livros em português até o fim do ano.
Hi, my name is Marina. I've been studying Portuguese for three years. I started studying because I have Brazilian friends, but I stopped practicing for a while. Now I'm back because I want to speak with more confidence. Besides that, my goal is to read books in Portuguese by the end of the year.
Language Note — What is not assessed in this chapter

This chapter does not assess productive subjunctive conjugation, formal clitic placement, or unscheduled Lusophone variation. It reviews and consolidates present, preterite, imperfect, and goal expressions with ir + infinitivo and querer/esperar/pretender from Volume I.

Model Dialogue

Context: Two members of a conversation group meet for the first time and reintroduce themselves. Informal-neutral register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Camila Oi! Eu sou a Camila. E você, como se chama? Hi! I'm Camila. And you, what's your name?
Rafael Eu me chamo Rafael. Prazer! Faz quanto tempo que você estuda português? My name is Rafael. Nice to meet you! How long have you been studying Portuguese?
Camila Eu estudo há quase três anos. Comecei porque queria morar no Brasil um dia. E você? I've been studying for almost three years. I started because I wanted to live in Brazil one day. And you?
Rafael Desculpa, pode repetir? Eu não entendi a última parte. Sorry, could you repeat that? I didn't understand the last part.
Camila Claro! Eu disse que comecei a estudar porque queria morar no Brasil. Sure! I said that I started studying because I wanted to live in Brazil.
Rafael Ah, entendi! Eu comecei há um ano, mas parei de praticar. Agora voltei porque quero falar com mais confiança. Ah, I understand! I started a year ago, but I stopped practicing. Now I'm back because I want to speak with more confidence.
Camila Que legal. Então, qual é o seu objetivo para este grupo? How nice. So, what's your goal for this group?
Rafael O meu objetivo é praticar toda semana e, além disso, aprender mais sobre a cultura brasileira. My goal is to practice every week and, besides that, learn more about Brazilian culture.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script
Oi, pessoal! Eu me chamo Diego e estudo português há quatro anos. Comecei a estudar na universidade porque precisava de uma língua estrangeira, mas depois comecei a gostar de verdade. No início, eu tinha muita dificuldade com os verbos, mas agora entendo melhor. Parei de praticar durante um ano por causa do trabalho, e por isso esqueci bastante vocabulário. Agora voltei porque quero viajar para Portugal no ano que vem. O meu objetivo é entender uma conversa real sem pedir para repetir toda hora.
Hi, everyone! My name is Diego and I've been studying Portuguese for four years. I started studying in college because I needed a foreign language, but afterward I started to really enjoy it. At first, I had a lot of difficulty with verbs, but now I understand better. I stopped practicing for a year because of work, and because of that I forgot a lot of vocabulary. Now I'm back because I want to travel to Portugal next year. My goal is to understand a real conversation without asking for repetition all the time.

Reading Practice

Bem-vindos ao grupo — Mensagem de apresentação

Oi, pessoal! Eu me chamo Ana Paula e sou de Recife, mas moro em São Paulo há cinco anos. Eu ajudo a organizar este grupo de conversação porque acho importante praticar português com pessoas de fora do Brasil também. Faz muito tempo que eu não ensino, mas sempre gostei de conversar sobre a cultura brasileira. No início deste projeto, eu tinha um pouco de medo de não saber explicar a gramática direito, mas agora sinto mais confiança. O meu objetivo com este grupo é criar um espaço tranquilo onde todo mundo possa praticar sem medo de errar. Se você não entender alguma coisa, pode pedir para repetir ou explicar de outro jeito — isso é normal e faz parte do processo.

Notice how this welcome message moves from identity to a current situation, to a past feeling, and finally to a present goal — the same structure your own introduction should follow.

Tarefa de leitura — Reading task:

  1. Where is Ana Paula from, and where does she live now?
  2. What did she feel at the beginning of this project, and how does she feel now?
  3. What is her goal for the group, and what does she tell members to do if they don't understand something?

Writing Workshop

Write your own welcome message for the conversation group, of six to ten sentences, following the model above.

  1. Introduce yourself. State your name and, if you like, your origin or current city.
  2. Explain your history with Portuguese. Include how long you have studied and why you started, using há and the preterite.
  3. Mention a difficulty or a break. Use the imperfect or preterite to describe a past challenge.
  4. State your current goal. Use o meu objetivo é, quero, or espero.
  5. Connect your ideas with at least two connectors such as porque, mas, além disso, or por isso.
  6. Check diacritics, agreement, and verb endings throughout, using the checklist below.

Lista de revisão — Editing checklist:

  • The message introduces identity, history with Portuguese, a past difficulty, and a present goal, in a connected way.
  • The duration expression with há is used correctly.
  • At least one preterite and one imperfect form appear with a clear function each.
  • At least two connectors link ideas across sentences.
  • Articles, gender, and adjective agreement are controlled throughout.
  • All diacritics are preserved, including é, história, and objetivo.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Conversation groups and language exchange

Portuguese conversation groups, in person or online, are common ways for learners to practice with native speakers and with each other, and they exist across Brazil, Portugal, and other Lusophone communities and their diasporas. Introducing your own history with the language — why you started, what has been hard, and what you hope to achieve — is a normal and welcome part of joining such a group, not oversharing.

Regional Variation — A reminder about the declared production model

This volume continues to use contemporary Brazilian Portuguese as your default production model, as in Volume I. Labeled European, African, and other Lusophone forms remain recognition-level material until a later chapter schedules them productively. Continue producing your declared variety consistently in this chapter's tasks.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing duration há with the preterite

Error: Saying Eu estudei português há dois anos to mean "I have been studying Portuguese for two years."

Correction: Use the present tense with há for an action that continues: Eu estudo português há dois anos. Reserve the preterite for a single completed action: Eu comecei a estudar há dois anos (I started two years ago).

Common Mistake — Avoiding repair language out of politeness

Error: Nodding along without understanding instead of asking for repetition or clarification.

Correction: Use formulas such as Desculpa, pode repetir? or O que significa isso? These are normal, polite, and expected in real conversation, especially at this stage of learning.

Common Mistake — Producing disconnected sentences instead of a sustained account

Error: Listing facts without connection: Eu estudo português. Eu comecei há dois anos. Eu quero praticar mais.

Correction: Use connectors to build one sustained account: Eu estudo português há dois anos e comecei porque queria viajar, mas agora quero praticar mais.
