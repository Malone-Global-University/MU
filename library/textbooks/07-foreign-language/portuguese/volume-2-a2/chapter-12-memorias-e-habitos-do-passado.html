Memórias e hábitos do passado

Memories and Past Habits · Speak and Write Portuguese: Independent Everyday Portuguese

Chapter Overview

Chapter 11 gave you the tools to narrate a sequence of completed recent events. Chapter 12 adds a different way of looking at the past: describing what things used to be like, rather than what happened at one specific moment. You will learn the imperfect tense to describe childhood routines, former homes, family traditions, and background circumstances — the kind of past that does not move a story forward but instead paints the scene around it. This chapter also opens the preterite/imperfect contrast that will continue developing through the rest of Unit III, so you can begin choosing between the two pasts based on what you actually mean to say.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Form the imperfect of regular -ar, -er, and -ir verbs, and of the irregulars ser, ter, and ir.
  2. Use the imperfect to describe childhood routines, habitual past actions, and ongoing background states.
  3. Describe a former home, neighborhood, or family tradition using the imperfect.
  4. Begin to distinguish the imperfect (background, habit) from the preterite (a completed, bounded event) in simple contexts.
  5. Read a short passage about someone's childhood and identify imperfect verb forms and their function.
  6. Listen to a short account of past habits and identify what used to happen regularly.
  7. Write a short paragraph describing a childhood routine, former home, or family tradition.

Communication Scenario

Someone asks you about your childhood, an earlier stage of your life, or a family tradition: Where did you use to live? What did you use to do every summer? What was your grandmother's house like? This chapter asks you to step back from single completed events and describe what life was generally like — the routines, the settings, and the recurring traditions that shaped an earlier time.

  1. The productive target is a short spoken or written description of childhood, a former home, or a family tradition, built mainly with the imperfect.
  2. Only the imperfect of regular verbs and of ser, ter, and ir, together with previously activated preterite forms, are required; full preterite/imperfect contrast in complex narratives continues to develop through Chapter 13 and beyond.
  3. You may invent a fictional childhood, home, or family tradition for any task in this chapter.
  4. Brazilian Portuguese remains the declared production model for all required production in this chapter.

Pronunciation or Writing-System Focus

Pronunciation Focus — The imperfect endings -va and -ia

The imperfect introduces two new stressed endings that you will hear constantly in this chapter: -ava for -ar verbs (morava, brincava) and -ia for -er and -ir verbs (vivia, saía). Stress falls on the syllable just before these endings, not on the ending itself: mo-RA-va, not mo-ra-VA.

Listen for the contrast between the imperfect morava ("used to live") and the preterite morou ("lived, for a bounded period"): the vowel quality and stress placement differ clearly, and mixing them up changes meaning, not just spelling. Practice this pair aloud: Eu morava em Salvador quando era criança. I used to live in Salvador when I was a child.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces the imperfect tense for describing habitual past actions and background states, and begins a functional contrast with the preterite you already control.

Era, tinha, and ia are extremely frequent in childhood description: Eu era muito tímido ("I was very shy"), Nós tínhamos um cachorro ("We had a dog"), Eu ia à escola a pé ("I used to walk to school").

What the Imperfect Describes

  • Quando eu era criança, morava numa casa perto da praia. When I was a child, I lived in a house near the beach. background state, ongoing situation
  • Todo verão, a gente ia para a casa dos meus avós. Every summer, we used to go to my grandparents' house. repeated, habitual action
  • Meus avós costumavam contar histórias depois do jantar. My grandparents used to tell stories after dinner. costumar + infinitive frames a habit explicitly
Language Note — A first look at preterite vs. imperfect

The imperfect describes a background, a habit, or something that was ongoing without a clear beginning or end in the story. The preterite, which you have used since earlier chapters, describes a specific event that happened and finished. Compare: Eu morava em Salvador ("I used to live in Salvador" — background, no specific endpoint in view) versus Eu morei em Salvador por dois anos ("I lived in Salvador for two years" — a bounded period treated as a completed whole). At this stage, focus on recognizing the difference in simple sentences; combining both tenses fluently in one narrative is the work of the next several chapters.

Building Block — Describing a childhood routine
quando eu era criança + [imperfeito de estado] + [imperfeito de hábito] + todo(s)/de vez em quando + [imperfeito]
era sets the frame; a state verb describes background; a second imperfect verb, often with a frequency expression, adds a recurring habit
Quando eu era criança, morava numa casa pequena com um quintal grande. De vez em quando, meus primos vinham passar o fim de semana, e a gente brincava no quintal até escurecer.
When I was a child, I lived in a small house with a big backyard. From time to time, my cousins would come spend the weekend, and we would play in the backyard until dark.

Model Dialogue

Context: Two friends talk about their childhood neighborhoods. Everyday spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Renata Onde você morava quando era criança? Where did you live when you were a child?
Diego Eu morava numa casa antiga no interior de Minas. Tinha um quintal enorme, e eu brincava lá o dia inteiro. I lived in an old house in the countryside of Minas. It had a huge backyard, and I used to play there all day.
Renata Que legal. E a sua família tinha alguma tradição especial? That's nice. Did your family have any special tradition?
Diego Tinha, sim. Todo mês de junho, a gente ia para a festa junina da cidade. Meus avós sempre faziam comida típica, e eu adorava aquela época do ano. We did. Every June, we would go to the town's June festival. My grandparents always made traditional food, and I loved that time of year.
Renata Que bonito. Aqui em casa era mais simples: a gente só costumava fazer um almoço grande no domingo. That's beautiful. At my house it was simpler: we just used to have a big lunch on Sundays.
  1. Where did Diego live as a child, and what was his backyard like?
  2. What tradition does Diego describe?
  3. Find two imperfect verb forms in the dialogue and identify whether each describes a background state or a habit.

Listening Task

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script — A grandmother's house
A casa da minha avó ficava numa rua tranquila, perto de um mercado pequeno. Ela morava sozinha, mas todo domingo a família inteira ia até lá para almoçar. Minha avó cozinhava desde cedo, e a casa sempre cheirava a comida boa. Eu e meus primos costumávamos brincar no quintal enquanto os adultos conversavam. De vez em quando, ela contava histórias antigas da família, e todo mundo ficava em silêncio para escutar.
My grandmother's house was on a quiet street, near a small market. She lived alone, but every Sunday the whole family would go there for lunch. My grandmother cooked from early morning, and the house always smelled of good food. My cousins and I used to play in the backyard while the adults talked. From time to time, she would tell old family stories, and everyone would fall silent to listen.
  1. Where was the grandmother's house located?
  2. What did the family do every Sunday?
  3. What did the children do while the adults talked?

Reading Task

A vizinhança da minha infância

Quando eu era criança, minha família morava numa vizinhança pequena onde todo mundo se conhecia. As ruas não tinham muito movimento, e as crianças brincavam ao ar livre até o fim da tarde. Todo verão, os vizinhos organizavam uma festa na rua, com comida e música. Eu adorava aquela época do ano, porque todos os amigos da rua ficavam juntos até tarde. Hoje, a vizinhança mudou bastante, mas eu ainda me lembro com carinho daqueles dias.

  1. What was the neighborhood like when the narrator was a child?
  2. What did the neighbors organize every summer?
  3. How does the narrator feel about that time now?

Writing Task

Write a short paragraph (5–7 sentences) describing a childhood routine, a former home, or a family tradition.

  1. State where you lived and what the place was like, using the imperfect.
  2. Describe a routine or habit you had, using the imperfect or costumar + infinitive.
  3. Include at least one frequency expression such as todo verão or de vez em quando.
  4. Describe one person who was part of this memory, using ser or ter in the imperfect.
  5. Close with a brief final sentence about how you feel about that memory now.

Revision checklist

  • I used at least three correct imperfect forms.
  • I did not use the preterite for background description or ongoing habits.
  • I included at least one frequency expression.
  • I preserved all diacritics, including the accent in forms like saía and tínhamos.
  • I did not introduce grammar beyond Chapter 12.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Sharing memories across generations

In many Brazilian families, talking about childhood, former homes, and family traditions is a common way of connecting across generations, whether at a Sunday lunch, a holiday gathering, or a casual conversation between friends. Grandparents in particular are often associated with storytelling about "the old days" (antigamente), and younger family members are often expected to listen attentively, though the exact customs and level of formality vary by family, region, and social context.

Personal disclosure in this chapter remains entirely optional. You may describe your own childhood, a relative's, or an invented one; what matters is producing the imperfect accurately and describing background and habit clearly, not revealing personal history you would rather keep private.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using the preterite for background description

Error: Quando eu fui criança, eu morei numa casa pequena.
Better: Quando eu era criança, eu morava numa casa pequena.

Ser and morar here describe an ongoing state without a specific beginning or end in the story, so they belong in the imperfect, not the preterite.

Common Mistake — Confusing morava and morou

Error: Eu morou em São Paulo quando era criança. (mixed subject-verb agreement and tense)
Better: Eu morava em São Paulo quando era criança.

Keep subject and verb ending consistent, and choose morava (imperfect, background) rather than morou (preterite, bounded period) when describing an ongoing childhood situation without a stated endpoint.
