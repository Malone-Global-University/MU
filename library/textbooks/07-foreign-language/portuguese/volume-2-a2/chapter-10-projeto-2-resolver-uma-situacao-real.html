Projeto 2: Resolver uma situação real

Unit II Assessment: Solving a Real Situation · Speak and Write Portuguese: Independent Everyday Portuguese

Chapter Overview

Chapter 10 is the Unit II assessment. It does not introduce new grammar, new vocabulary domains, or new pronunciation targets. Instead, it asks you to combine everything you built in Chapters 6 through 9 — media and technology contact, food and market habits, service encounters and complaints, and travel planning — into a single integrated task. You will resolve a realistic problem that mixes a service issue with travel logistics, using only the systems that are active through Chapter 9. This chapter also recycles foundational material from Unit I: diacritics, articles, agreement, the preterite, and everyday routines. Completing this project demonstrates that you can function independently in the kinds of situations a traveler or new resident regularly faces.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Explain a real-world problem that combines a service issue and a travel complication.
  2. Use the preterite to narrate what already happened, and ir + infinitivo to state what you plan to do next.
  3. Use polite request forms such as queria and gostaria, together with me/lhe, to ask for help and describe a complaint.
  4. Use por and para correctly in their travel-scope meanings.
  5. Read a short service or travel notice and identify the information needed to solve a problem.
  6. Write a message that explains a problem, requests a solution, and proposes next steps.
  7. Sustain a supported spoken interaction that resolves a service/travel scenario from beginning to end.

Communication Scenario

Something has gone wrong with your travel plans. Maybe your flight was changed, your reservation disappeared, or a service you booked did not work out the way you expected. You need to explain what happened, request help politely, and figure out what to do next. This project asks you to handle the entire situation — from explaining the problem to arriving at a solution — using only language you have already studied.

  1. The productive target is a complete problem-solving exchange: narrate what happened, explain the problem, make a polite request, and state a revised plan.
  2. Only systems marked active, developed, or consolidated through Chapter 9 are required; the full subjunctive and the complete por/para contrast remain outside this project's requirements.
  3. You may invent a fictional airline, hotel, service provider, or travel companion for any task in this chapter.
  4. Brazilian Portuguese remains the declared production model for all required production in this chapter.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review — No new sounds this chapter

Chapter 10 introduces no new pronunciation system. Before completing the speaking task, review the nasal diphthongs in viagem, passagem, and reclamação, and the open/closed vowel contrast in pairs like avó/avô. Pay attention to sentence rhythm when you combine a past narration with a polite request in the same turn; the request typically softens in tone even when the narration that precedes it is direct.

Grammar Review

This project consolidates rather than introduces. It combines the preterite and ir + infinitivo from Chapter 9, the polite request forms and indirect object pronouns from Chapter 8, and travel-scope por/para from Chapter 9. No new productive grammar appears in this chapter.

Combining a Problem Narration with a Request

A complete problem-solving turn typically narrates what happened, explains the problem, and then makes a polite request, in that order.

  • Eu reservei o hotel pelo aplicativo, mas a reserva não apareceu no sistema. I booked the hotel through the app, but the reservation didn't appear in the system. reservar, aparecer, preterite, 1sg/3sg
  • Eu perdi o voo porque o ônibus para o aeroporto atrasou. I missed the flight because the bus to the airport was late. perder, atrasar, preterite + porque
  • Eu queria remarcar a passagem para amanhã, se possível. I would like to rebook the ticket for tomorrow, if possible. queria + infinitive, softened request
  • Você pode me ajudar a resolver isso, por favor? Can you help me resolve this, please? indirect object me + service register
Language Note — Sequencing a problem-solving exchange

A well-organized complaint or problem-solving exchange usually follows four steps, all built from systems you already control:

  1. Narrate: preterite verbs state what happened. Eu comprei a passagem online.
  2. Explain the problem: connectors like mas and porque introduce the complication. …mas o voo foi cancelado.
  3. Request: queria/gostaria plus an infinitive, softened with por favor or se possível. Eu gostaria de remarcar o voo.
  4. State the next plan: ir + infinitivo confirms what will happen next. Eu vou viajar no dia seguinte.

None of these four steps requires a new grammar system. The project simply asks you to sequence them together clearly.

Building Block — The full problem-solving sequence
narração (pretérito) + mas/porque + queria/gostaria + infinitivo + vou + infinitivo
preterite narrates; mas/porque explains; queria/gostaria softens the request; ir + infinitive states the resolution
Eu reservei a pousada pelo aplicativo, mas a reserva não apareceu no sistema. Eu gostaria de falar com um atendente, por favor. Se não for possível resolver hoje, eu vou procurar outra pousada perto do centro.
I booked the guesthouse through the app, but the reservation didn't appear in the system. I would like to speak with a representative, please. If it isn't possible to resolve today, I'm going to look for another guesthouse near downtown.

Prática guiada — Guided practice: Complete each sentence using only systems active through Chapter 9.

  1. Eu ___ (comprar, preterite) a passagem online, mas o voo ___ (ser, preterite) cancelado.
  2. Eu ___ (querer, past/softened form) falar com um atendente, por favor.
  3. Você pode ___ (me/lhe) ajudar com essa reclamação?
  4. Se não for possível hoje, eu ___ (ir + infinitive) voltar amanhã.

Model Dialogue

Context: A traveler speaks with an airline representative after a missed connection. Polite service register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Atendente Boa tarde. Como posso ajudar? Good afternoon. How can I help?
Renata Boa tarde. Eu comprei uma passagem para Maputo com conexão em Lisboa, mas o voo de conexão atrasou e eu perdi o segundo voo. Good afternoon. I bought a ticket to Maputo with a connection in Lisbon, but the connecting flight was delayed and I missed the second flight.
Atendente Sinto muito. Vou verificar o próximo voo disponível para Maputo. I'm sorry. I'll check the next available flight to Maputo.
Renata Eu gostaria de remarcar para amanhã, se possível. Também preciso de um hotel para hoje à noite. I would like to rebook for tomorrow, if possible. I also need a hotel for tonight.
Atendente Sem problema. Vou remarcar o voo para amanhã de manhã e vou lhe dar um voucher para o hotel. No problem. I'll rebook the flight for tomorrow morning and give you a hotel voucher.
Renata Muito obrigada pela ajuda. Thank you very much for the help.
  1. What went wrong with Renata's trip, and why did she miss her connecting flight?
  2. What two things does Renata request?
  3. Find one preterite verb and one ir + infinitivo construction in the dialogue.

Listening Task

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script — A traveler reports a problem
Eu reservei uma pousada pelo aplicativo, mas quando cheguei, o quarto já estava ocupado. Eu liguei para o atendente e expliquei o problema. Ele disse que sentia muito e me ofereceu outro quarto em uma pousada perto dali. Eu aceitei, mas pedi um desconto porque perdi tempo esperando. I booked a guesthouse through the app, but when I arrived, the room was already occupied. I called the representative and explained the problem. He said he was very sorry and offered me another room at a guesthouse nearby. I accepted, but I asked for a discount because I lost time waiting. In the end, everything got resolved and I stayed at the new guesthouse for three nights.
  1. What problem did the traveler find when they arrived?
  2. What did the traveler request in addition to a new room?
  3. How many nights did the traveler stay at the new guesthouse?

Writing Task

Write a short message (6–8 sentences) to a service provider explaining a travel or service problem and requesting a solution.

  1. Open with a greeting and briefly state your name or booking reference.
  2. Narrate what happened, using the preterite.
  3. Explain the problem clearly, using mas or porque.
  4. Make a polite request using queria or gostaria.
  5. State your revised plan using ir + infinitivo.
  6. Close politely, thanking the reader for their help.

Revision checklist

  • I narrated the problem using the preterite correctly.
  • I made a polite request using queria or gostaria.
  • I stated a next step using ir + infinitivo.
  • I used por or para correctly at least once.
  • I preserved all diacritics and did not introduce grammar beyond Chapter 9.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Repair and clarification across contexts

Asking for repair, clarification, and repeated confirmation is a normal, expected part of communication in Portuguese, not a sign of failure. Phrases such as Desculpe, pode repetir? and Não entendi bem, pode explicar de novo? remain appropriate at every proficiency level, including for independent B1 speakers handling unfamiliar service situations.

Service culture varies across Lusophone contexts in pace, formality, and directness, though the core structure of narrating a problem and requesting help remains broadly similar. This project keeps the interaction in the declared Brazilian production model rather than requiring learners to adapt register across multiple varieties at once.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Skipping the request and going straight to demands

Error: Quero um hotel agora. as an opening line to a representative.
Better: Eu gostaria de saber se é possível conseguir um hotel, por favor.

A softened request with gostaria or queria, together with por favor, fits the service register from Chapter 8 far better than a bare command, even when the problem is frustrating.

Common Mistake — Losing the time sequence between problem and plan

Error: Eu vou perder o voo ontem.
Better: Eu perdi o voo ontem. Agora eu vou remarcar para amanhã.

Keep the preterite for the completed problem and ir + infinitivo for the plan that follows it. Mixing the two tenses within the same event confuses the sequence of what already happened and what is still to come.
