Saúde e bem-estar

Health and Well-Being · Speak and Write Portuguese: Independent Everyday Portuguese

Chapter Overview

Talking about how you feel, describing a symptom, and getting or giving practical advice are essential everyday skills. This chapter builds your ability to describe symptoms, make and discuss appointments, talk about habits that affect well-being, and give or receive simple advice. Along the way, you will practice imperative and request formulas for common situations such as clinics and pharmacies, and expand your modal expressions with dever alongside the ter que and precisar you developed in Chapter 3. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to describe how you feel, schedule an appointment, and give or receive basic health-related advice in Portuguese.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe common symptoms and how you are feeling.
  2. Make, change, or discuss a medical or dental appointment.
  3. Talk about habits that support or affect well-being.
  4. Give and receive simple advice using dever, ter que, and precisar.
  5. Use imperative and request formulas appropriate to clinic and pharmacy settings.
  6. Read and write a short text describing symptoms, a habit, or a piece of advice.

Communication Scenario

A member of your conversation group is not feeling well and needs to call a clinic to make an appointment. Another member wants advice about starting a healthier routine. You practice both roles: describing symptoms and scheduling an appointment, and giving simple, supportive advice about habits.

  1. This chapter develops imperative and request formulas, and the modal expressions dever, ter que, and precisar; it recycles Chapter 3's reflexive verbs, routine vocabulary, and modal frames.
  2. The productive target is a short clinic exchange and a short piece of written or spoken advice about a habit.
  3. You may use a fictional or generalized symptom, appointment, or habit at any point.
  4. Only systems marked active, developed, or consolidated through this chapter are required for independent production.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Note — Imperative intonation and polite requests

Imperative forms such as tome, descreva, and descanse should be produced with a falling, matter-of-fact intonation rather than a sharp or commanding tone, especially in clinic and pharmacy contexts. Practice pairing imperatives with por favor to keep requests polite: Descreva os sintomas, por favor. Continue watching stress in words such as médico, which is stressed on the antepenultimate syllable, and também, which carries a nasal diphthong on the final syllable.

Intelligibility in short clinic exchanges, not native-like accent, remains the goal of this chapter.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This section develops imperative and request formulas for clinic and pharmacy contexts and expands the modal system with dever for advice, alongside ter que and precisar from Chapter 3.

Imperative and Request Formulas

  • Descreva os sintomas, por favor. Describe the symptoms, please. formal imperative (você/formal) + por favor
  • Tome esse remédio duas vezes por dia. Take this medicine twice a day. formal imperative — instruction from a doctor or pharmacist
  • Descanse bastante e beba bastante água. Rest a lot and drink plenty of water. two coordinated formal imperatives — common advice pattern

Imperative forms in this chapter are practiced in fixed, high-frequency formulas tied to clinic and pharmacy advice; broader imperative paradigms and informal-tu imperatives are not required for independent production at this point.

Dever for Advice

  • Você deve descansar mais esta semana. You should rest more this week. dever + infinitive — advice, softer than obligation
  • A gente deve comer melhor durante a semana de trabalho. We should eat better during the work week. a gente + dever (third-person-singular) + infinitive
Language Note — Dever, ter que, and precisar together

Dever expresses advice or a recommendation and is softer than obligation: it suggests what someone should do, not what they are required to do. Ter que (and its more formal written counterpart ter de) expresses a firmer obligation. Precisar expresses necessity and often sits between the two in strength, depending on context. Compare: Você deve dormir mais (advice), Você precisa dormir mais (necessity), and Você tem que ir à consulta amanhã (firm obligation). All three take the infinitive and are productive in this chapter.

Language Note — What is not assessed in this chapter

Model Dialogue

Context: A patient calling a clinic to describe symptoms and schedule an appointment. Polite-neutral register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Recepcionista Clínica Bem-Estar, bom dia. Como posso ajudar? Bem-Estar Clinic, good morning. How can I help?
Mariana Bom dia. Eu estou com dor de garganta e um pouco de febre. Eu preciso marcar uma consulta. Good morning. I have a sore throat and a slight fever. I need to schedule an appointment.
Recepcionista Descreva os sintomas, por favor. Desde quando você está assim? Describe the symptoms, please. Since when have you felt this way?
Mariana Desde ontem à noite. Eu também estou muito cansada. Since last night. I'm also very tired.
Recepcionista Entendo. Você tem horário livre às três da tarde? I understand. Do you have free time at three in the afternoon?
Mariana Tenho, sim. Eu devo levar algum documento? Yes, I do. Should I bring any document?
Recepcionista Traga um documento com foto, por favor. Até às três, então. Bring a photo ID, please. See you at three, then.
  1. List Mariana's symptoms, in the order she mentions them.
  2. Find every imperative form used in this dialogue.
  3. Find the sentence that uses dever and explain what it expresses.

I think I need to schedule an appointment with the doctor. He will probably say that I should rest more and eat better. We always have to take care of our health, even when the routine is very busy. Reading Practice

Conselhos simples para uma rotina mais saudável

Muitas pessoas sentem cansaço e estresse durante a semana de trabalho ou de estudo. Os médicos costumam recomendar algumas mudanças simples. Primeiro, a gente deve dormir pelo menos sete ou oito horas por noite. Segundo, é importante comer bem e não pular refeições, mesmo em dias corridos. Terceiro, quem passa muitas horas sentado deve se exercitar, mesmo que seja uma caminhada curta todos os dias. Por fim, se os sintomas de cansaço ou estresse continuarem, a pessoa precisa marcar uma consulta com um médico, em vez de ignorar o problema. Pequenas mudanças na rotina podem fazer uma grande diferença no bem-estar geral.

Notice how this passage moves from a common problem, to three pieces of general advice, to a final recommendation — a structure you can reuse in your own writing.

  2. What should a person do if symptoms continue?

Writing Workshop

Write a short text describing a symptom or a habit affecting your well-being and offering yourself or someone else practical advice, of six to ten sentences, following the model above.

  1. State the symptom or habit. Describe it in one or two sentences.
  2. Explain the context. Say since when, how often, or why it happens.
  3. Give at least one piece of advice with dever. Keep it practical and non-diagnostic.
  4. Express at least one necessity or obligation using precisar or ter que.
  5. Include at least one imperative or request formula, such as advice given by a doctor or friend.
  6. Check imperative forms, modal expressions, and diacritics throughout, using the checklist below.

Lista de revisão — Editing checklist:

  • The text describes a symptom or habit, its context, and at least one piece of advice in a connected way.
  • Formal imperatives such as descanse, tome, and descreva are used correctly.
  • Dever, ter que, and precisar are each used appropriately and take the infinitive.
  • The content stays practical and non-diagnostic, without acting as medical advice.
  • Articles, gender, and adjective agreement are controlled throughout.
  • All diacritics are preserved, including médico, saúde, and também.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Clinics, pharmacies, and everyday health talk

In many Brazilian communities, pharmacists play an active role in everyday health guidance, and it is common to describe minor symptoms directly at a pharmacy counter before deciding whether to see a doctor. Public and private healthcare systems coexist in Brazil, and the specific process for scheduling a consultation can vary by city, clinic, and type of coverage. Health-related conversations in Lusophone contexts generally remain practical and matter-of-fact rather than highly personal, though the level of detail shared can depend on the relationship between speakers.

Regional Variation — A reminder about the declared production model

This chapter continues to use contemporary Brazilian Portuguese as your default production model. Clinic and pharmacy vocabulary, as well as healthcare systems and appointment procedures, can differ noticeably across Lusophone regions; labeled European, African, and other Lusophone terms remain recognition-level material until a later chapter schedules them productively. Continue producing your declared variety consistently in this chapter's tasks.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing dever and ter que

Error: Saying Eu tenho que descansar mais when the intended meaning is gentle advice rather than firm obligation.

Correction: Use dever for advice or recommendation: Eu devo descansar mais. Reserve ter que for firmer obligations, such as a scheduled appointment: Eu tenho que ir à consulta amanhã.

Common Mistake — Using the infinitive instead of the imperative in instructions

Error: Saying Descansar bastante as a direct instruction to someone, using the infinitive instead of the imperative.

Correction: Use the formal imperative for direct instructions: Descanse bastante. The infinitive alone does not function as a command directed at a specific listener.

Common Mistake — Presenting advice as a diagnosis

Error: Writing a text that names a specific medical condition or diagnosis based on described symptoms.

Correction: Keep chapter language practical and non-diagnostic. Describe symptoms and general advice, such as resting or scheduling an appointment, rather than naming or guessing a specific illness: Eu acho que preciso marcar uma consulta, not a stated diagnosis.
