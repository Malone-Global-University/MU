Casa, bairro e mudança

Housing and Moving · Speak and Write Portuguese: Independent Everyday Portuguese

Chapter Overview

Where you live shapes daily life, so this chapter gives you the language to describe your home, your neighborhood, and the process of moving from one place to another. You will learn to talk about apartments and houses, rooms and features, rent and utilities, and the qualities that make a neighborhood convenient or difficult. This chapter also develops your control of Portuguese prepositions and contractions — small words that carry a great deal of meaning — and sharpens the distinction between ficar and estar for describing where something is located. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to describe your current home, compare it to another place you have lived, and explain what you are looking for in a new home or neighborhood.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe an apartment or house, including its rooms, size, and features.
  2. Use prepositions and contractions to describe location within and around a home or neighborhood.
  3. Distinguish ficar and estar when talking about where places and things are located.
  4. Discuss rent, utilities, and the practical details of moving.
  5. Compare two homes or neighborhoods using simple comparative language.
  6. Read and write a short message about housing needs or a moving situation.

Communication Scenario

A friend from your conversation group is looking for a new apartment and has asked members of the group to describe their own homes and neighborhoods so she can compare options. You decide to write a short message describing where you live now, and you also prepare to talk with her by phone about what to look for in a good neighborhood.

  1. This chapter develops prepositions, contractions, and the ficar/estar distinction; it recycles Volume I housing vocabulary and expands it.
  2. The productive target is one description of a home and one spoken exchange comparing housing options.
  3. You may use a fictional or generalized home and neighborhood at any point.
  4. Only systems marked active, developed, or consolidated through this chapter are required for independent production.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Note — Contractions in connected speech

Contractions such as no, na, do, and da are unstressed and blend tightly into the words around them in natural speech. Practice saying no quarto, na cozinha, and perto do prédio as single rhythmic units rather than pausing before or after the contraction. Also continue watching stress in words such as aluguel, which carries stress on the final syllable, unlike most Portuguese words ending in a consonant.

Intelligibility in connected phrases, not isolated word pronunciation, is the goal of this chapter.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This section develops prepositions and contractions for describing location, strengthens the distinction between ficar and estar, and introduces simple comparative expressions as formulaic patterns tied to housing description.

Prepositions and Contractions of Location

  • O apartamento fica perto do centro, na Rua das Flores. The apartment is located close to downtown, on Flowers Street. perto + de + o = perto do; em + a = na
  • A cozinha fica ao lado da sala, e o banheiro fica entre os dois quartos. The kitchen is next to the living room, and the bathroom is between the two bedrooms. a + a = à (ao lado da); entre + plural noun
  • Eu moro no terceiro andar, em cima da padaria. I live on the third floor, above the bakery. em + o = no; em cima + de + a = em cima da

Ficar and estar for Location

  • O prédio fica na esquina, perto do mercado. The building is on the corner, close to the market. ficar for the fixed location of a place
  • Hoje as minhas chaves estão em cima da mesa, mas normalmente ficam na bolsa. Today my keys are on the table, but they're normally in my bag. estar for a temporary position; ficar for a habitual or default location
Language Note — Choosing between ficar and estar

Both ficar and estar can describe where something is, but they are not interchangeable. Use ficar for the fixed, defining location of a place, such as a building, a neighborhood, or a city — information that does not usually change. Use estar for the current, possibly temporary position of a person or object, such as where your keys happen to be right now. When in doubt about a building or a place, ficar is usually the safer choice.

Comparing Homes and Neighborhoods

  • Este bairro é mais tranquilo do que o meu bairro antigo. This neighborhood is quieter than my old neighborhood. mais + adjective + do que, formulaic comparison
  • O aluguel aqui é menos caro do que no centro. The rent here is less expensive than downtown. menos + adjective + do que

Comparative patterns in this chapter are practiced as fixed formulas (mais... do que, menos... do que) rather than as a fully productive grammar system; broader comparative and superlative structures develop later in the volume.

Building Block — Describing a home
home type + fica + preposition/contraction + neighborhood quality + comparison with a previous home
the chapter task links location, description, and comparison into one connected paragraph about housing
Eu moro em um apartamento pequeno que fica no segundo andar de um prédio antigo. Fica perto do metrô, na Rua Almirante Barroso. O bairro é tranquilo e seguro, e o aluguel é mais barato do que no meu bairro antigo. A única coisa negativa é que não tem elevador.
I live in a small apartment that is on the second floor of an old building. It's close to the subway, on Almirante Barroso Street. The neighborhood is quiet and safe, and the rent is cheaper than in my old neighborhood. The only downside is that there's no elevator.
Language Note — What is not assessed in this chapter

This chapter does not assess a full comparative and superlative paradigm, formal clitic placement, or unscheduled Lusophone variation. It develops prepositions, contractions, ficar versus estar, and the formulaic comparative patterns mais... do que and menos... do que.

Model Dialogue

Context: A phone call between two friends from the conversation group. One is looking for a new apartment. Informal-neutral register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Juliana Oi, Marcos! Estou procurando um apartamento novo. Como é o seu bairro? Hi, Marcos! I'm looking for a new apartment. What's your neighborhood like?
Marcos O meu bairro é bem tranquilo. O apartamento fica no quarto andar, perto de uma praça. My neighborhood is quite quiet. The apartment is on the fourth floor, close to a square.
Juliana Que legal! E o aluguel, é caro? Nice! And the rent, is it expensive?
Marcos Não muito. É mais barato do que no centro, mas as contas de água e luz não estão incluídas. Not too much. It's cheaper than downtown, but the water and electricity bills aren't included.
Juliana Entendi. E o prédio tem elevador? I see. And does the building have an elevator?
Marcos Tem, sim. E o mercado fica bem perto, na esquina. Yes, it does. And the market is very close, on the corner.
Juliana Parece ótimo. Vou procurar um apartamento parecido perto daí. Sounds great. I'm going to look for a similar apartment near there.
  1. List the details Marcos gives about his apartment, in order.
  2. Find every preposition or contraction used to describe location in this dialogue.
  3. Find the sentence that uses a comparative pattern, and explain what is being compared.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script
Oi! Eu me chamo Bianca e quero falar um pouco sobre o meu apartamento. Eu moro em um apartamento de dois quartos que fica no sexto andar de um prédio perto da universidade. A cozinha é pequena, mas a sala é bem grande, e tem uma varanda que fica de frente para o parque. O aluguel não é barato, porque o bairro é muito central, mas é mais tranquilo do que eu esperava. As contas de água e luz ficam por minha conta, mas a internet já está incluída no contrato. Eu gosto muito daqui, principalmente porque o mercado e a padaria ficam bem perto, a poucos minutos a pé.
Hi! My name is Bianca and I want to talk a bit about my apartment. I live in a two-bedroom apartment on the sixth floor of a building close to the university. The kitchen is small, but the living room is quite big, and there's a balcony that faces the park. The rent isn't cheap, because the neighborhood is very central, but it's quieter than I expected. The water and electricity bills are my responsibility, but internet is already included in the lease. I like it a lot here, mainly because the market and the bakery are very close, just a few minutes on foot.
  1. Where is Bianca's apartment located, and on which floor?
  2. What does she say about the rent and the utilities?
  3. Why does she like living there?

Writing Workshop

Write a short message to a friend describing your home and neighborhood, of six to ten sentences, following the model above.

  1. State where you live. Use ficar with a preposition or contraction to describe the location.
  2. Describe your home. Mention the type of home, the number of rooms, and one notable feature.
  3. Describe the rent and utilities. State whether utilities are included, using vocabulary from this chapter.
  4. Describe the neighborhood. Mention at least two qualities, such as tranquilo, central, or seguro.
  5. Add one comparison to a previous home or neighborhood, using mais... do que or menos... do que.
  6. Check prepositions, contractions, and diacritics throughout, using the checklist below.

Lista de revisão — Editing checklist:

  • The message describes the home's location, rooms, rent, and neighborhood in a connected way.
  • Contractions such as no, na, do, and da are used correctly.
  • Ficar and estar are each used appropriately at least once.
  • At least one comparison uses mais... do que or menos... do que.
  • Articles, gender, and adjective agreement are controlled throughout.
  • All diacritics are preserved, including água, aluguel, and próximo.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Housing across Lusophone cities

Housing arrangements vary widely across Lusophone cities and are shaped by cost of living, family structure, and local custom. In many Brazilian cities, renters commonly share the cost of a condomínio (building maintenance fee) in addition to rent and utilities, and shared housing among students or young professionals is common in expensive central neighborhoods. Housing customs in Portugal, Angola, Mozambique, and other Lusophone contexts differ in their own ways and are not identical to the Brazilian pattern described here.

Regional Variation — A reminder about the declared production model

This chapter continues to use contemporary Brazilian Portuguese as your default production model. Housing vocabulary can differ noticeably across Lusophone regions; labeled European, African, and other Lusophone terms remain recognition-level material until a later chapter schedules them productively. Continue producing your declared variety consistently in this chapter's tasks.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using estar for a building's fixed location

Error: Saying O prédio está perto do metrô to describe where a building permanently is.

Correction: Use ficar for the fixed location of a place: O prédio fica perto do metrô. Reserve estar for the current, changeable position of a person or object.

Common Mistake — Forgetting to contract prepositions with articles

Error: Saying A cozinha fica perto de a sala instead of contracting de and a.

Correction: Portuguese requires the contraction: A cozinha fica perto da sala. Contractions such as do, da, no, and na are required, not optional, whenever the preposition and article combine.

Common Mistake — Comparing without do que

Error: Saying Este bairro é mais tranquilo o meu bairro antigo, omitting the connector.

Correction: The comparison requires do que: Este bairro é mais tranquilo do que o meu bairro antigo.
