Opiniões e preferências

Opinions and Preferences · Speak and Write Portuguese: Independent Everyday Portuguese

Chapter Overview

Chapter 12 gave you the imperfect for describing background and habit. Chapter 13 turns from the past toward the present moment and toward judgment: what you think, what you like better, and why. You will learn achar que for stating an opinion, preferir for expressing preference, the comparative and superlative system for ranking options, and the first que clauses that let you attach a reason or a description to an opinion. Together these tools let you talk about movies, music, restaurants, neighborhoods, and everyday choices the way you would with a friend — and to disagree politely when your opinion differs from someone else's.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use achar que to introduce an opinion about media, places, or activities.
  2. Use preferir to state a preference between two or more options.
  3. Form and use comparatives with mais... (do) que, menos... (do) que, and tão... quanto.
  4. Use common irregular comparatives, including melhor, pior, maior, and menor.
  5. Form simple superlatives with o/a mais and o/a melhor.
  6. Attach a simple que clause to describe a preference, such as um filme que eu adoro.
  7. Agree and disagree politely, and support an opinion with a short reason.

Communication Scenario

A friend asks what you think of a movie, a restaurant, a neighborhood, or a way of spending free time. You are expected not only to answer, but to compare it with another option and to explain briefly why you feel that way. Someone may disagree with you — and you need the language to accept that disagreement gracefully rather than treat it as a problem to fix.

  1. The productive target is a short spoken or written exchange giving an opinion, comparing two options, and giving one simple supporting reason.
  2. Only achar que, preferir, comparative and superlative forms already presented in this chapter, and early descriptive que clauses are required; fuller subordination and the subjunctive continue to develop in later chapters.
  3. You may invent your opinions and preferences for any task in this chapter if you would rather not share your own.
  4. Brazilian Portuguese remains the declared production model for all required production in this chapter.

Pronunciation or Writing-System Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Stress in comparative and superlative phrases

Comparative and superlative phrases are usually spoken as a single rhythmic unit rather than word by word. In mais interessante do que ("more interesting than"), the strongest stress falls on interessante, while mais and do que are spoken more quickly and lightly around it.

Listen also for the difference between gosto (I like, from gostar) and o gosto (the taste, a noun): stress and vowel quality distinguish them, even though the spelling overlaps closely. Practice this phrase aloud: Eu acho que esse restaurante é melhor do que aquele. I think this restaurant is better than that one.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces language for stating and supporting opinions, expressing preferences, and comparing options.

Stating an Opinion: achar que

Achar que ("to think that") is the everyday way to introduce an opinion in Brazilian Portuguese. It is followed directly by a clause, without a comma before que.

  • Eu acho que esse filme é ótimo. I think this movie is great.
  • Nós achamos que o bairro é tranquilo. We think the neighborhood is quiet.
  • Você acha que essa série é interessante? Do you think this series is interesting?

Achar also means "to find" in a physical sense (Achei minhas chaves, "I found my keys"); the opinion meaning is limited to the achar que pattern taught here.

Expressing Preference: preferir

Preferir changes its stem vowel in the eu form only (prefiro, not prefero). Use it with a noun or with another verb in the infinitive:

  • Eu prefiro filmes de comédia. I prefer comedy movies.
  • Ela prefere morar num bairro mais calmo. She prefers to live in a quieter neighborhood.

Comparatives: mais, menos, tão

Comparative patterns
PatternMeaningExample
mais ... (do) quemore ... thanEsse filme é mais interessante do que aquele.
menos ... (do) queless ... thanEste bairro é menos barulhento do que o centro.
tão ... quantoas ... asA série é tão boa quanto o livro.

Do in do que is optional in everyday speech (mais interessante que aquele is also common), but written Portuguese typically keeps it.

Irregular Comparatives

Common irregular comparatives
AdjectiveRegular expectationActual comparative

Melhor, pior, maior, and menor do not change for gender, only for number (melhores, piores, maiores, menores).

Simple Superlatives

  • Esse é o melhor restaurante do bairro. That is the best restaurant in the neighborhood.
  • Essa é a série mais interessante da temporada. That is the most interesting series of the season.

The superlative pattern uses the definite article (o, a) plus the comparative, followed by de to introduce the group being compared (do bairro, da temporada).

Early que Clauses

A simple que clause lets you attach a description directly to a noun, without a separate sentence:

  • É um filme que eu adoro. It's a movie that I love. que + subject + verb, describing "filme"
  • Prefiro lugares que são tranquilos. I prefer places that are quiet. que + ser, describing "lugares"

This chapter limits que clauses to simple present-tense description; more complex relative clauses continue to develop in later chapters.

Language Note — Opinion is not fact

Achar que softens a statement into a personal opinion rather than a flat claim. Compare Esse filme é chato ("This movie is boring" — stated as fact) with Eu acho que esse filme é meio chato ("I think this movie is kind of boring" — framed as opinion). In everyday conversation about taste, Brazilian speakers very commonly use achar que even when they are quite confident, because it leaves room for someone else to disagree without direct conflict.

Building Block — Opinion, comparison, reason
Eu acho que + [opinion] + porque + [reason], mas prefiro + [alternative]
achar que introduces the opinion; porque supports it briefly; preferir introduces a ranked alternative
Eu acho que essa série é boa, porque a história é interessante, mas prefiro o filme, porque é mais curto e mais fácil de assistir num dia só.
I think this series is good, because the story is interesting, but I prefer the movie, because it's shorter and easier to watch in a single day.

Model Dialogue

Context: Two friends compare two restaurants they both know. Everyday spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Bruno O que você acha do restaurante novo perto da praça? What do you think of the new restaurant near the square?
Camila Eu acho que a comida é boa, mas é mais caro do que o restaurante da esquina. I think the food is good, but it's more expensive than the one on the corner.
Bruno Verdade. Mas o serviço lá é melhor, não acha? True. But the service there is better, don't you think?
Camila Concordo. Eu prefiro pagar um pouco mais por um lugar que tem um serviço mais atencioso. I agree. I'd rather pay a bit more for a place that has more attentive service.
Bruno Eu discordo um pouco. Na minha opinião, o restaurante da esquina é tão bom quanto, e é bem mais barato. I disagree a bit. In my opinion, the corner restaurant is just as good, and it's a lot cheaper.
  1. What does Camila think of the new restaurant's food and price?
  2. Why does Camila prefer the new restaurant despite the price?
  3. Find one comparative and one superlative-style phrase in the dialogue.

Listening Task

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script — Two neighborhoods
Eu moro no centro, mas acho que o bairro onde minha irmã mora é mais tranquilo do que o meu. Lá tem menos barulho e mais árvores. Por outro lado, o centro é mais prático, porque tudo fica mais perto. Na minha opinião, o bairro da minha irmã é melhor para morar, mas o centro é melhor para quem trabalha fora de casa. Cada lugar tem suas vantagens.
I live downtown, but I think the neighborhood where my sister lives is quieter than mine. There's less noise there and more trees. On the other hand, downtown is more practical, because everything is closer. In my opinion, my sister's neighborhood is better to live in, but downtown is better for people who work away from home. Each place has its advantages.
  1. According to the speaker, which neighborhood is quieter?
  2. What advantage does downtown have?
  3. Does the speaker think one neighborhood is better for everyone, or does it depend?

Reading Task

Duas séries, duas opiniões

Muita gente pergunta qual série é melhor: a nova ou a antiga. Na minha opinião, a série nova é mais rápida e tem uma história mais interessante, mas a série antiga tem personagens que eu acho mais interessantes. A série nova é tão popular quanto a antiga, mas por razões diferentes. Eu prefiro a antiga, porque conheço os personagens há mais tempo, mas entendo por que muita gente prefere a nova.

  1. According to the writer, what is the new series better at?
  2. What does the writer prefer about the old series?
  3. Does the writer think everyone should agree with their opinion?

Writing Task

Write a short paragraph (5–7 sentences) comparing two movies, restaurants, or neighborhoods and giving your opinion.

  1. Name the two things you are comparing.
  2. State your opinion of each one using achar que.
  3. Compare them using at least one comparative and one superlative form.
  4. State a preference using preferir, and support it with a reason introduced by porque.
  5. Close with a sentence acknowledging that others might disagree.

Revision checklist

  • I used at least one comparative and one superlative form correctly.
  • I used preferir with correct present-tense agreement.
  • I preserved all diacritics, including the tilde in opinião.
  • I did not introduce grammar beyond Chapter 13.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Disagreeing without conflict

In many everyday Brazilian conversations, disagreement about taste — a movie, a restaurant, a neighborhood — is common and generally treated as light and unremarkable, though the tone and directness can vary by relationship, region, and setting. Framing a claim with eu acho que or na minha opinião signals that the speaker knows the statement is a personal view rather than a settled fact, which helps keep everyday disagreements friendly.

Personal disclosure in this chapter remains entirely optional. You may share your own opinions and preferences or invent them; what matters is producing opinion, comparison, and preference language accurately, not revealing tastes you would rather keep private.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Regular comparatives for bom and ruim

Error: Esse restaurante é mais bom do que aquele.
Better: Esse restaurante é melhor do que aquele.

Bom and ruim do not take mais; use the irregular forms melhor and pior instead.

Common Mistake — Comma before que

Error: Eu acho, que esse filme é ótimo.
Better: Eu acho que esse filme é ótimo.

Unlike some English sentence patterns, Portuguese achar que is not separated by a comma; the clause follows que directly.
