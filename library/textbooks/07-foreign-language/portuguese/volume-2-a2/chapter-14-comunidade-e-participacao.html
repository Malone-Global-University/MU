Comunidade e participação

Community Participation · Speak and Write Portuguese: Independent Everyday Portuguese

Chapter Overview

Chapter 13 gave you the language of opinion, comparison, and preference. Chapter 14 turns those tools outward, from personal taste toward public life. You will talk about the services, events, and organizations that make up a neighborhood or town: the health clinic, the community center, the neighborhood association, the volunteer group, the local festival. Along the way, two small words that you have already met in a limited, recognition-only way — quando and se — begin their transition into active, productive use, letting you link a time or a condition directly to an action: quando eu tiver tempo, se você quiser. By the end of the chapter you will be able to describe community involvement, explain how and when you participate, and talk about civic life without relying only on isolated sentences.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Name and describe common community services, spaces, and organizations.
  2. Discuss local events, meetings, and volunteer opportunities.
  3. Use quando clauses to connect a time or circumstance to an action.
  4. Use se clauses to express simple real conditions in present or near-future time.
  5. Combine quando and se clauses with vocabulary and structures from earlier chapters, including achar que and comparatives.
  6. Ask about and explain civic participation, such as volunteering or attending a community meeting.
  7. Read and summarize a short notice about a community event.

Communication Scenario

A neighbor, a community bulletin board, or a local association mentions an upcoming event, a volunteer opportunity, or a new service in the area. You are expected to understand the basic details, ask follow-up questions, and explain your own level of involvement — including conditions under which you would or would not participate, and when you typically have time to do so.

  1. The productive target is a short spoken or written exchange describing community participation, using at least one quando clause and one se clause.
  2. Only quando and se clauses with present or near-future reference are required; hypothetical and subjunctive uses of se and quando continue to develop in later chapters.
  3. You may invent your community, your schedule, and your level of involvement for any task in this chapter.
  4. Brazilian Portuguese remains the declared production model for all required production in this chapter.

Pronunciation or Writing-System Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Linking quando and se into the sentence

Quando and se are short, unstressed words that lean onto the words around them rather than carrying their own strong stress. In quando eu tiver tempo, the stress falls on tempo, not on quando; in se você quiser, the stress falls on quiser.

Listen also for the difference between se ("if," an unstressed conjunction) and sé (an accented word meaning "cathedral," unrelated here but a useful reminder that the acute accent changes both sound and meaning). Practice this phrase aloud: Eu ajudo, se tiver tempo, quando o mutirão acontecer no bairro. I help, if I have time, when the community work party happens in the neighborhood.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter moves quando and se from recognition to active, controlled production, and applies them to community participation.

Quando Clauses: Linking Time and Action

Quando ("when") joins a time clause to a main clause. With present-tense or habitual reference, both clauses use the present tense you already control:

  • Quando eu tenho tempo, eu ajudo no centro comunitário. When I have time, I help at the community center.
  • Eu participo da reunião quando ela é à noite. I take part in the meeting when it is at night.

For a future or not-yet-real time reference (something that has not happened yet), Brazilian Portuguese uses the future subjunctive after quando. At this stage, only the high-frequency formulaic pattern quando + tiver (from ter) is active for production; the full future subjunctive paradigm remains receptive until a later chapter.

  • Quando eu tiver tempo, eu vou ajudar no mutirão. When I have time (in the future), I'm going to help with the work party. quando + tiver (formulaic future reference); main clause in present or ir + infinitive

Do not attempt other future subjunctive forms independently in this chapter; quando tiver is taught and practiced as a fixed, meaningful pattern.

Se Clauses: Simple Real Conditions

Se ("if") introduces a real, present-or-future condition. With present-tense reference, both clauses use the present tense:

  • Se eu tenho tempo, eu participo do evento. If I have time, I take part in the event.

For a condition about the near future, use se with the present tense in the se clause and ir + infinitivo, the simple future, or an imperative in the main clause:

  • Se você quiser ajudar, a reunião é sábado. If you want to help, the meeting is on Saturday. se + quiser (formulaic future reference, from querer)
  • Se precisar de voluntários, eu vou participar. If they need volunteers, I'm going to participate.

Quiser and precisar in these examples are taught as formulaic future-reference patterns, parallel to quando tiver. Hypothetical se clauses with the imperfect subjunctive (equivalent to English "if I were...") are not introduced until a later chapter.

Language Note — Quando and se are not interchangeable

Quando assumes the event will happen and asks only about timing: Quando eu tiver tempo, eu ajudo assumes the speaker will eventually have time. Se leaves the condition genuinely open: Se eu tiver tempo, eu ajudo does not assume the speaker will have time at all. Choosing the right word changes the meaning of the sentence, not just its style.

Building Block — Time, condition, participation
Quando + [tempo], eu + [ação]; se + [condição], eu + [ação futura]
quando situates the action in time; se attaches a condition; both connect directly to community participation vocabulary
Quando eu tenho um fim de semana livre, eu gosto de participar do mutirão do bairro; e se a associação de moradores precisar de mais gente, eu vou chamar meus vizinhos também.
When I have a free weekend, I like to take part in the neighborhood work party; and if the neighborhood association needs more people, I'm going to invite my neighbors too.

Prática guiada — Guided practice: Complete each sentence using only systems active through Chapter 14.

  1. ___ (Quando / eu / ter) tempo, eu ajudo no centro comunitário.
  2. Se você ___ (querer) participar, a reunião é na terça.

Model Dialogue

Context: Two neighbors discuss an upcoming community work party. Everyday spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Rafael Você soube do mutirão na associação de moradores? Did you hear about the work party at the neighborhood association?
Luciana Soube. Quando é? I heard. When is it?
Rafael Sábado de manhã, no centro comunitário. Se você quiser ajudar, eles precisam de voluntários para o jardim. Saturday morning, at the community center. If you want to help, they need volunteers for the garden.
Luciana Quando eu tiver um sábado livre, eu adoro participar. Acho que é mais interessante do que ficar em casa. When I have a free Saturday, I love to participate. I think it's more interesting than staying home.
Rafael Perfeito. Se a gente for cedo, ainda dá tempo de tomar um café antes. Perfect. If we go early, there's still time to grab a coffee before.
  1. What kind of help do they need volunteers for, and when is the work party?
  2. Under what condition does Luciana say she loves to participate?
  3. Find one quando clause and one se clause in the dialogue.

Listening Task

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script — A community announcement
Boa tarde, moradores. Na próxima semana, a associação vai organizar um mutirão no parque perto da praça. Se você quiser participar, é só chegar no sábado, às oito da manhã. Quando o mutirão terminar, vamos ter um pequeno almoço para todos os voluntários. Achamos que essa é uma boa oportunidade para conhecer os vizinhos e cuidar do bairro juntos.
Good afternoon, residents. Next week, the association is going to organize a work party in the park near the square. If you want to participate, just show up on Saturday, at eight in the morning. When the work party ends, we're going to have a small lunch for all the volunteers. We think this is a good opportunity to meet the neighbors and take care of the neighborhood together.
  1. What time and day should volunteers arrive?
  2. What happens when the work party ends?
  3. According to the speaker, what is one benefit of participating besides helping the park?

Reading Task

Um novo posto de saúde no bairro

A prefeitura anunciou um novo posto de saúde para o bairro. Quando o posto abrir, os moradores vão ter consultas mais perto de casa. Se você mora longe do centro, essa mudança pode ser muito útil. A associação de moradores acha que o novo posto vai melhorar a vida de muita gente, mas alguns moradores acham que ainda faltam mais médicos.   2. What concern do some residents have?
  3. What will happen when the health post starts operating?

Writing Task

Write a short paragraph (5–7 sentences) describing a community event or service and your participation in it.

  1. Name the community event, service, or organization.
  2. State your opinion of it using achar que.
  3. Describe when you typically participate, using a quando clause.
  4. State a condition for your participation, using a se clause.
  5. Close with a sentence comparing this event or service to another one, using a comparative or superlative form from Chapter 13.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Community participation in Brazil

Community participation in Brazil takes many forms, from formal neighborhood associations to informal cooperation among neighbors, and its shape varies a great deal by city, region, and social context. The mutirão, a cooperative work effort in which neighbors join together to complete a shared task, is one well-known example, but it is not the only way people organize collectively, and not every neighborhood or community uses this term or this exact practice.

As in earlier chapters, personal disclosure about your own community involvement remains entirely optional. You may describe your real neighborhood or invent one; what matters is producing quando and se clauses accurately in a community context, not revealing your actual level of civic participation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing quando and se

Error: Quando eu tiver tempo, talvez eu ajude. (used to express real uncertainty)
Better: Se eu tiver tempo, eu ajudo.

Quando assumes the event will happen; if the condition is genuinely uncertain, se is the appropriate choice, not quando softened with words like talvez.

Common Mistake — Overextending tiver and quiser

Error: Quando eu falar com ela, eu vou explicar. (attempting to generate other future subjunctive forms independently at this stage)
Better: Limit independent production to the formulaic patterns quando/se + tiver and se + quiser taught in this chapter.

The full future subjunctive system remains receptive until a later chapter; treat tiver and quiser as fixed patterns rather than as a general rule to extend to every verb.
