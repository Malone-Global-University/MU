Histórias recentes

Recent Events · Speak and Write Portuguese: Independent Everyday Portuguese

Chapter Overview

Chapter 11 opens Unit III and moves your storytelling forward. You already narrate single completed events with the preterite; now you will connect several recent events into one coherent account, using a wider set of irregular preterite verbs and the connectors porque, então, and depois to show sequence and reason. You will also begin reporting what someone else said, using simple forms such as disse que. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to tell a short, well-organized story about something that recently happened to you — what happened first, what happened because of it, and what happened next.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Narrate a sequence of recent events using an expanded range of preterite verbs, including common irregulars.
  2. Connect events and reasons using porque, então, and depois.
  3. Report what someone said using a simple formulaic pattern with disse que.
  4. Track who or what a story is about across several connected sentences without repeating the noun in every sentence.
  5. Read a short narrative about a recent incident and identify the sequence of events.
  6. Listen to a short account of something that recently happened and identify the order and the reasons.
  7. Write a short paragraph narrating a recent event with a clear beginning, complication, and result.

Communication Scenario

Something happened recently — a small mishap, an unexpected change of plans, a piece of good news, or an ordinary day that took an interesting turn. A friend or colleague wants to know what happened. This chapter asks you to tell that story clearly: what happened first, why it happened, what happened as a result, and what happened after that.

  1. The productive target is a short spoken or written narrative of three to five connected recent events, organized with sequencing and reason connectors.
  2. Only preterite forms, near-future ir + infinitivo, and the connectors introduced in this chapter are required; the imperfect and the preterite/imperfect contrast remain outside this chapter's requirements and begin in Chapter 12.
  3. You may invent a fictional recent event, person, or place for any task in this chapter.
  4. Brazilian Portuguese remains the declared production model for all required production in this chapter.

Pronunciation or Writing-System Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Sequencing rhythm and sentence-final falling intonation

Chapter 11 introduces no new sound. Instead, it develops presentation-ready pronunciation: the rhythm of connected narration. When a story moves from one event to the next, Brazilian Portuguese speakers typically use a falling pitch at the end of each completed event and a brief pause before então or depois, which signals to the listener that a new step in the story is beginning.

Practice reading this short chain aloud, pausing briefly at each comma: Eu recebi um e-mail, então liguei para o colega, depois marcamos uma nova reunião. I received an email, so I called my colleague, and afterward we scheduled a new meeting.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter expands the preterite to a wider set of irregular verbs, introduces porque, então, and depois as sequencing tools, and introduces a simple formulaic pattern for reporting what someone said.

Expanded Preterite: More Irregular Verbs

You already know the preterite of high-frequency verbs like ser, ir, and regular -ar/-er/-ir verbs. This chapter adds several more irregulars that are essential for everyday storytelling.

Connecting Events: porque, então, depois

  • Eu não fui à reunião porque perdi o ônibus. I didn't go to the meeting because I missed the bus. porque = reason, introduces cause
  • Perdi o ônibus, então cheguei atrasado ao trabalho. I missed the bus, so I arrived late to work. então = result, introduces consequence
  • Cheguei atrasado, depois expliquei o que aconteceu para o chefe. I arrived late, and afterward I explained what happened to the boss. depois = sequence, introduces the next step
Language Note — porque vs. então: cause and result are not the same connector

Porque answers the question "why?" and looks backward to a cause. Então looks forward from a cause to its result. The two are not interchangeable: Perdi o ônibus porque cheguei atrasado reverses the real cause and effect and creates a confusing sentence. Keep the cause before porque and the result after então.

Reporting What Someone Said: disse que

To report someone else's words, Brazilian Portuguese commonly uses disse que ("said that") followed by a statement. At this stage, treat this as a formulaic pattern rather than a full reported-speech system; the verb inside the que clause usually stays in a tense you already control.

  • Ela disse que ia chegar mais tarde. She said (that) she was going to arrive later. disse que + ia + infinitive (formulaic)
  • O colega disse que não recebeu o e-mail. My colleague said (that) he didn't receive the email. disse que + preterite
Building Block — The recent-events narrative chain
evento 1 (pretérito) + porque + causa  |  então + resultado  |  depois + próximo evento
preterite states each event; porque explains a cause; então marks a result; depois marks the next step in sequence
Ontem eu recebi uma notícia inesperada: o voo da minha viagem foi cancelado porque houve um problema técnico. Então liguei para a companhia aérea. Depois, um atendente me disse que havia um voo alternativo à noite.
Yesterday I received unexpected news: my flight was cancelled because there was a technical problem. So I called the airline. Afterward, a representative told me there was an alternative flight that night.

Model Dialogue

Context: Two colleagues catch up after a missed meeting. Everyday spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Bruno Oi, Camila! O que aconteceu ontem? Você não veio à reunião. Hi, Camila! What happened yesterday? You didn't come to the meeting.
Camila Ai, foi um dia complicado. Eu recebi uma ligação de última hora porque um cliente teve um problema urgente. Oh, it was a complicated day. I got a last-minute call because a client had an urgent problem.
Bruno Nossa. E o que você fez? Wow. So what did you do?
Camila Eu não pude ir à reunião, então avisei a Marta por e-mail. Ela disse que ia explicar a situação para o resto da equipe. I couldn't go to the meeting, so I let Marta know by email. She said she was going to explain the situation to the rest of the team.
Bruno Entendi. E o cliente, deu tudo certo? I understand. And the client, did everything work out?
Camila Deu. Resolvi o problema, depois liguei para a Marta para contar o resultado. It did. I solved the problem, and afterward I called Marta to tell her the result.
  1. Why didn't Camila go to the meeting?
  2. What did Camila do instead of attending?
  3. Find one example of disse que and one irregular preterite verb in the dialogue.

Listening Task

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script — An unexpected day at work
Ontem foi um dia estranho no trabalho. De manhã, eu recebi um e-mail importante, mas o sistema caiu e eu não pude abrir o arquivo. Então liguei para o suporte técnico. Um técnico veio até minha mesa e resolveu o problema em poucos minutos. Depois, minha colega me disse que ela teve o mesmo problema na semana passada. No fim, consegui terminar o trabalho, mas cheguei em casa mais tarde do que o normal.
Yesterday was a strange day at work. In the morning, I received an important email, but the system crashed and I couldn't open the file. So I called technical support. A technician came to my desk and solved the problem in a few minutes. Afterward, my colleague told me she had had the same problem the week before. In the end, I managed to finish the work, but I got home later than usual.
  1. What problem did the speaker have in the morning?
  2. What did the speaker do about it, and who helped?
  3. What did the colleague say had happened to her?

Reading Task

Um imprevisto no fim de semana

No sábado, meus amigos e eu planejamos um piquenique no parque. De manhã, começou a chover forte, então mudamos de planos rapidamente. Eu liguei para todo mundo e disse que íamos nos encontrar em um café perto do parque. Depois, passamos a tarde conversando e jogando cartas. No fim, todos disseram que gostaram mais do café do que do piquenique original.

  1. What was the original plan, and why did it change?
  2. What did the narrator do after the plan changed?
  3. What did everyone say at the end of the story?

Writing Task

Write a short paragraph (5–7 sentences) narrating a recent event with a clear beginning, a complication, a result, and a next step.

  1. State what happened first, using the preterite.
  2. Explain the cause of a complication using porque.
  3. State the result using então.
  4. State what happened next using depois.
  5. Include at least one reported statement using disse que.
  6. Close with a brief final sentence summarizing how the situation ended.

Revision checklist

  • I used at least two irregular preterite verbs correctly.
  • I used porque to explain a cause and então to state a result.
  • I used depois to mark a following step.
  • I included one example of disse que.
  • I preserved all diacritics, including the circumflex in pôde where relevant, and did not introduce grammar beyond Chapter 11.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Telling a concrete, time-bounded story

In everyday Brazilian Portuguese conversation, recounting what happened recently is a common way to catch up with friends, family, and colleagues. Stories at this stage remain concrete and time-bounded — anchored to a specific day, event, or short span of time — rather than reflecting on habits or general background, which begins in Chapter 12 with the imperfect.

Sharing a recent mishap or piece of news is a normal, low-stakes way to build rapport in many everyday settings, though the level of detail shared and the directness of the account can vary by relationship and context. This chapter keeps narration in the declared Brazilian production model and in everyday spoken register.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Reversing porque and então

Error: Cheguei atrasado porque então perdi o ônibus.
Better: Perdi o ônibus, então cheguei atrasado.

Keep the cause before então and never combine porque and então in the same clause; each connector marks a different logical direction.

Common Mistake — Losing track of who the story is about

Error: A Marta ligou. A Marta disse que a Marta ia chegar tarde.
Better: A Marta ligou. Ela disse que ia chegar tarde.

Once a person has been named, use a subject pronoun, subject omission, or a natural reference rather than repeating the full name in every following sentence. Repeating the noun in every clause sounds unnatural and makes a short narrative harder to follow.
