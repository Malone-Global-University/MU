17

Desejos, conselhos e recomendações

Unit IV, Chapter 2 · Speak and Write Portuguese: Independent Everyday Portuguese

Chapter Overview

Chapter 17 builds directly on the tiver and quiser forms you have already been using formulaically inside quando and se clauses since Chapter 14. Here those forms become the entry point into the present subjunctive, developed through a small, controlled set of meaning-based frames: wishing something for someone else with espero que, giving advice with recomendo que and sugiro que, and evaluating a situation with é importante que and é bom que. You will learn to form the present subjunctive for regular verbs and a handful of high-frequency irregulars, and to recognize the key structural signal that triggers it: a change of subject between the main clause and the que clause.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Form the present subjunctive of regular -ar, -er, and -ir verbs and recognize the high-frequency irregular forms already familiar from quando/se clauses.
  2. Express a wish for someone else using espero que and quero que.
  3. Give advice and recommendations using recomendo que, sugiro que, and aconselho que.
  4. Evaluate a situation using é importante que and é bom que.
  5. Distinguish when a que clause requires the subjunctive from when an infinitive is used instead.

Communication Scenario

A friend or family member is facing a decision about school, work, health, or travel — real or invented. You offer a wish for how things turn out, give two or three pieces of advice, and explain why each piece of advice matters. Your friend responds with their own wish or plan.

  1. The productive target is a short exchange of wishes, advice, and evaluative comments about someone else's situation.
  2. Only the present subjunctive triggered by espero que, quero que, recomendo que, sugiro que, aconselho que, é importante que, and é bom que is required; other subjunctive triggers, such as doubt or emotion, are not introduced here.
  3. You may invent the situation, the advice, and the outcome.
  4. Brazilian Portuguese remains the declared production model for all required production in this chapter.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — The vowel switch of the present subjunctive

The present subjunctive is easiest to hear as a vowel switch: -ar verbs move to an -e ending (fale, not fala), while -er and -ir verbs move to an -a ending (coma, decida). Listen for this switch, since it is often the only signal that distinguishes a statement from a wish or recommendation. Practice this sentence aloud: Espero que você fale com o professor e que ele explique tudo com calma; recomendo que vocês decidam logo.

I hope you talk to the professor and that he explains everything calmly; I recommend that you all decide soon.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter develops the present subjunctive through a controlled set of meaning-based frames: wishing, recommending, and evaluating. The forms tiver and quiser, already familiar from quando/se clauses since Chapter 14, are part of this same system; here the pattern expands to regular verbs and a few additional irregulars, but only inside the trigger expressions taught below.

Forming the Present Subjunctive

For regular verbs, start from the eu form of the present indicative, drop the final -o, and add the "opposite vowel" ending.

  • falar → falo → fale, fales, fale, falemos, falem to speak → subjunctive stem -ar verbs take -e endings
  • comer → como → coma, comas, coma, comamos, comam to eat → subjunctive stem -er verbs take -a endings
  • decidir → decido → decida, decidas, decida, decidamos, decidam to decide → subjunctive stem -ir verbs also take -a endings

A small set of high-frequency irregular verbs already used formulaically in quando/se clauses recur here: tenha (ter), seja (ser), esteja (estar), vá (ir), saiba (saber), queira (querer), possa (poder), faça (fazer), diga (dizer), and dê (dar). Treat these as a short reference list rather than a full irregular paradigm to memorize all at once.

Wishing for Someone Else: Espero que, Quero que

When the subject of the main verb differs from the subject of the following clause, Portuguese requires que plus the subjunctive. When the subject stays the same, an infinitive is used instead — no que clause is needed.

  • Eu espero que você tenha sucesso na entrevista. I hope (that) you have success in the interview. different subjects (eu / você) → que + subjunctive
  • Eu espero ter sucesso na entrevista. I hope to have success in the interview. same subject (eu / eu) → infinitive, no que clause

This same-subject-versus-different-subject test is the single most useful rule in this chapter. Apply it before choosing between an infinitive and a subjunctive clause.

Advice and Recommendations

Recomendar que, sugerir que, and aconselhar que all trigger the subjunctive in the following clause.

  • Eu recomendo que você fale com o médico antes da viagem. I recommend that you talk to the doctor before the trip.
  • Nós sugerimos que vocês cheguem mais cedo. We suggest that you all arrive earlier.

Evaluating a Situation: É importante que, É bom que

Impersonal evaluative expressions also trigger the subjunctive when followed by que and a named subject.

  • É importante que você descanse antes da prova. It is important that you rest before the exam.
  • É bom que ela saiba a resposta com antecedência. It is good that she knows the answer ahead of time.

Only these seven trigger expressions are productive in this chapter: espero que, quero que, recomendo que, sugiro que, aconselho que, é importante que, and é bom que. Subjunctive triggers based on doubt, denial, or emotion (such as duvido que or tenho medo que) are not introduced here.

Building Block — Wishing, advising, and evaluating
[Espero / Recomendo / É importante] que + [sujeito diferente] + [verbo no subjuntivo].
a trigger expression plus a changed subject calls for the present subjunctive
Espero que você tenha uma boa entrevista amanhã. Recomendo que você chegue quinze minutos antes e que leve o currículo impresso. É importante que você fale com calma.
I hope you have a good interview tomorrow. I recommend that you arrive fifteen minutes early and that you bring a printed résumé. It's important that you speak calmly.

Prática guiada — Guided practice: Complete each sentence using only the systems introduced above.

  1. Espero que você ___ (ter) uma boa viagem.
  2. Recomendo que vocês ___ (chegar) mais cedo.
  3. É importante que ela ___ (saber) a verdade.
  4. Eu quero ___ (descansar) hoje. [same subject — infinitive]

Model Dialogue

Context: A friend prepares for a job interview. Everyday spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Marina Amanhã é a minha entrevista para o novo emprego. Estou um pouco nervosa. Tomorrow is my interview for the new job. I'm a little nervous.
Diego Espero que tudo dê certo! Recomendo que você chegue uns quinze minutos antes. I hope everything works out! I recommend that you arrive about fifteen minutes early.
Marina Boa ideia. O que mais você sugere? Good idea. What else do you suggest?
Diego Sugiro que você leve o currículo impresso e que descanse bem esta noite. É importante que você fale com calma e que responda com confiança. I suggest that you bring your printed résumé and that you rest well tonight. It's important that you speak calmly and answer with confidence.
Marina Obrigada! Espero que dê tudo certo mesmo. Thank you! I really hope everything works out.
  1. Why is Marina nervous?
  2. List two pieces of advice Diego gives.
  3. Find one wish, one recommendation, and one evaluative expression in the exchange.

Guided Practice

Exercício A — Infinitive or subjunctive? Decide whether the subject stays the same or changes, then choose the correct form.

  1. Eu quero ___ (viajar) no verão. / Eu quero que você ___ (viajar) comigo.
  2. Nós esperamos ___ (chegar) cedo. / Nós esperamos que eles ___ (chegar) cedo.
  3. Ela espera ___ (passar) na prova. / Ela espera que nós ___ (passar) na prova.

Exercício B — Give advice. Complete each recommendation with the correct subjunctive form.

  1. Recomendo que você ___ (descansar) antes da viagem.
  2. Sugiro que vocês ___ (falar) com o professor.
  3. Aconselho que ela ___ (ter) mais paciência.

Writing Workshop

Write a short message (7–9 sentences) to a friend or family member facing a real or invented decision, expressing a wish for them and giving advice.

  1. Name the situation the person is facing.
  2. Express a wish for them using espero que or quero que.
  3. Give two pieces of advice using recomendo que, sugiro que, or aconselho que.
  4. Add one evaluative comment using é importante que or é bom que.
  5. Close with a final wish or encouraging comment.

Revision checklist

  • I used at least one wish expression with the subjunctive correctly formed.
  • I used at least two advice or recommendation expressions with the subjunctive.
  • I used at least one evaluative expression (é importante que or é bom que).
  • I checked whether the subject stayed the same (infinitive) or changed (subjunctive) in each sentence.
  • I did not use subjunctive triggers based on doubt or emotion that are not part of this chapter.
  • I preserved all diacritics, including in recomendação, é, and está.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Giving advice without overstepping

Among friends and family in many Brazilian everyday contexts, advice is often offered directly and warmly, especially in close relationships, but the degree of directness still depends on the relationship, the topic, and the setting. Softening an opinion with eu acho que or framing it as a wish with espero que can make advice feel supportive rather than intrusive, particularly with someone who has not asked for an opinion. This tendency should not be treated as a fixed national trait, since individual, regional, and situational variation is considerable across the Lusophone world.

As in earlier chapters, personal disclosure about your own real relationships, health, or decisions remains entirely optional. You may describe a real situation or invent one; what matters is combining wishes, recommendations, and evaluative comments accurately.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using the indicative after a subjunctive trigger

Error: Espero que você tem sucesso.
Better: Espero que você tenha sucesso.

Once a trigger expression like espero que, recomendo que, or é importante que introduces a clause with a different subject, the following verb must be in the subjunctive, not the ordinary present indicative.

Common Mistake — Using que + subjunctive when the subject has not changed

Error: Eu espero que eu tenha sucesso.
Better: Eu espero ter sucesso.

When the subject of the main verb and the following verb are the same, Portuguese normally uses an infinitive, not a que clause with the subjunctive.
