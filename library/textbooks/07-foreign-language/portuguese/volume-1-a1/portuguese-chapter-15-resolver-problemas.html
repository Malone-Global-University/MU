Projeto 3: Resolver problemas

Unit III Integrated Assessment · Speak and Write Portuguese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview

This chapter is the Unit III integrated assessment. It does not introduce new productive grammar. Instead, it brings together everything you built in Chapters 11 through 14 — making plans, asking and giving directions, asking for help, describing ability, and talking about health, needs, and obligations — into a single set of connected, practical problem-solving tasks. You will move through a short story of a day that does not go as planned: you need directions, then you need help, and then you start to feel unwell. Completing this chapter well means using your existing systems clearly and confidently, not stretching beyond them.

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Ask for and give simple directions using por/para, até, and sequence connectors.
  2. Ask for help and describe ability using poder, saber, and conseguir.
  3. Describe basic symptoms, needs, and obligations using estar com, precisar, ter que, and dever.
  4. Combine movement, service, and health language into one coherent problem-solving narrative.
  5. Complete a listening, reading, writing, and speaking task that draws only on material already activated through Chapter 14.

Communication Scenario

Your day is not going smoothly. First you need directions to get somewhere. Then you run into a problem and need someone's help. Finally, you start to feel unwell and have to decide what you need to do about it. You will narrate this chain of events, respond to a listening passage, read a short account of someone else's difficult day, and write your own connected account.

  1. This chapter is assessment only. No new productive grammar, vocabulary domain, pronunciation system, or orthographic burden is introduced.
  2. Every task below draws only on systems activated in Chapters 11–14.
  3. You may invent your own version of the day's events if you prefer not to describe a real one.

imperative/request form + até + place; sequence connector + direction

Task 1: Como eu chego lá?

Uma manhã complicada

Hoje eu preciso ir à farmácia antes do trabalho, mas não sei bem o caminho. Com licença, como eu chego à farmácia daqui? — Vá até a esquina e vire à direita. Depois, siga em frente até a Rua das Flores. A farmácia fica entre o banco e o mercado, perto da praça. — Muito obrigado! É longe daqui? — Não, é perto. Você pode ir a pé em dez minutos.

Notice como eu chego, vá até a esquina, vire à direita, and siga em frente recycled directly from Capítulo 12.

  1. Where does the speaker need to go, and why?
  2. Give the directions in your own words, in Portuguese, using at least two sequence connectors.
  3. Is the pharmacy close or far? How does the speaker know?

Task 2: Você pode me ajudar?

Context: At the pharmacy counter, customer to employee, neutral polite register.
Cliente Com licença, você pode me ajudar? Eu preciso de um remédio, mas não sei o nome em português. Excuse me, can you help me? I need a medicine, but I don't know the name in Portuguese.
Funcionário Claro. Você sabe descrever o problema? Você está com dor de cabeça, febre, ou dor de garganta? Of course. Can you describe the problem? Do you have a headache, a fever, or a sore throat?
Cliente Estou com dor de cabeça e um pouco de febre. Eu acho que preciso descansar também. I have a headache and a bit of a fever. I think I need to rest too.
Funcionário Entendo. Eu consigo recomendar um remédio simples, mas se a febre não melhorar, você tem que ir ao médico. I understand. I can recommend a simple medicine, but if the fever doesn't improve, you have to go to the doctor.
  1. Why does the customer ask for help?
  2. What ability verb does the employee use to say what he can recommend?
  3. What must the customer do if the fever does not improve?

Task 3: Estou doente

Continue the story from Tasks 1 and 2. After leaving the pharmacy, the customer still does not feel well and has to decide what to do next.

Fill in the blank
Depois da farmácia, eu ainda não estou me sentindo bem. Estou com ______. Eu preciso ______. Acho que eu tenho que ______, mas talvez eu deva ______ primeiro.
After the pharmacy, I still am not feeling well. I have ______. I need ______. I think I have to ______, but maybe I should ______ first.
  1. Complete the message above with your own natural choices.
  2. Read your completed message aloud, paying attention to estar com versus ter.

Listening Task

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script
Com licença, você pode me ajudar? Eu preciso chegar ao hospital, mas não sei o caminho e não estou me sentindo bem. — Claro. Primeiro, vá até a esquina e vire à esquerda. Depois, siga até a Avenida Central. O hospital fica entre a farmácia e a escola. — Muito obrigado. Estou com dor de cabeça e um pouco de febre; acho que tenho que ir agora. — Você consegue ir a pé, ou prefere pegar um táxi? Não é longe, mas se você está com febre, talvez seja melhor pegar um táxi. — Boa ideia. Eu devo chegar em quinze minutos assim.
Excuse me, can you help me? I need to get to the hospital, but I don't know the way and I'm not feeling well. — Of course. First, go to the corner and turn left. Then, continue to Central Avenue. The hospital is between the pharmacy and the school. — Thank you very much. I have a headache and a bit of a fever; I think I have to go now. — Can you walk, or would you prefer to take a taxi? It's not far, but if you have a fever, maybe it's better to take a taxi. — Good idea. I should arrive in fifteen minutes that way.
  1. Where does the speaker need to go, and why?
  2. What directions are given?
  3. What symptoms does the speaker describe, and what transportation decision is made?

Reading Task

O dia difícil de Camila

Camila precisava ir ao banco, mas não sabia o caminho. Ela perguntou a um vizinho: "Você pode me ajudar? Como eu chego ao banco daqui?" O vizinho disse: "Vá até a esquina, vire à direita e siga até a praça. O banco fica perto da farmácia." Camila agradeceu e foi. No caminho, ela começou a sentir dor de cabeça e um pouco de febre. Ela pensou: "Eu preciso descansar, mas primeiro tenho que resolver o problema no banco. Depois, devo ir para casa e talvez ligar para o médico."

Notice você pode me ajudar, vá até a esquina, estou com dor de cabeça, eu preciso descansar, tenho que resolver, and devo ir para casa all recycled together in one short narrative.

Tarefa de leitura — Reading task:

  1. Where did Camila need to go, and what directions did she receive?
  2. What symptoms did Camila begin to feel?
  3. In what order does Camila plan to handle her problems?

Writing Task

Write your own connected account of a difficult day, combining directions, a request for help, and a health-related need or obligation, following the model of Camila's story above.

  1. Plan the sequence of events. Decide where you needed to go, who helped you, and what symptom, need, or obligation appeared.
  2. Draft the directions section. Use at least one sequence connector and one preposition of place or route from Capítulo 12.
  3. Draft the request-for-help section. Use at least one of poder, saber, or conseguir from Capítulo 13.
  4. Draft the health section. Use estar com for a symptom and distinguish ter que from dever from Capítulo 14.
  5. Combine the sections into one short narrative of six to eight sentences, adding a simple opening and closing.
  6. Check diacritics and spelling throughout, including forms such as até, não, and médico.

Lista de revisão — Editing checklist:

  • The narrative includes a direction-asking or direction-giving moment.
  • The narrative includes a clear request for help using poder, saber, or conseguir.
  • The narrative includes estar com for a symptom.
  • ter que and dever are used with their distinct meanings, if both appear.
  • All diacritics are preserved.
  • No new or unscheduled grammar appears.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Asking strangers for help

In many Brazilian cities, it is common and socially expected to ask a stranger on the street for directions or brief assistance, often preceded by com licença or desculpa. This kind of everyday cooperation reflects general social norms and varies by city, neighborhood, and situation.

Regional Variation — Getting around

Transportation choices such as walking, taking a bus, or taking a taxi vary widely across Lusophone cities depending on distance, cost, time of day, and local infrastructure. Decisions like Camila's or the hospital visitor's are practical rather than culturally fixed, and similar choices appear across Brazil, Portugal, and other Portuguese-speaking regions in different forms.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing poder, saber, and conseguir

Error: Saying Eu sei ir ao hospital a pé to mean "I am able to walk to the hospital."

Correction: Use conseguir for physical ability or managing to do something: Eu consigo ir ao hospital a pé. Reserve saber for knowledge or learned skills: Eu sei o caminho para o hospital.

Common Mistake — Mixing up ter que and dever under pressure

Error: Saying Eu devo ir ao médico agora, é urgente! when strong necessity, not advice, is intended.

Correction: Use ter que for urgent or necessary obligation: Eu tenho que ir ao médico agora. Save dever for softer advice: Eu devo descansar mais esta semana.

Common Mistake — Skipping sequence connectors in directions

Error: Listing directions as separate, disconnected commands without linking words.

Correction: Use primeiro, depois, and então to connect steps clearly: Primeiro, vá até a esquina. Depois, vire à direita.
