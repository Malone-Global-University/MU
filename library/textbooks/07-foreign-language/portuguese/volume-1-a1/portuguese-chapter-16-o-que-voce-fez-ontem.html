O que você fez ontem?

Completed Past Events · Speak and Write Portuguese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview

Up to this point, you have spoken about the present and about the near future. This chapter opens Unit IV by giving you a new tool: the preterite, the verb form Portuguese uses to talk about completed actions in the past. You will learn the regular preterite endings for -ar, -er, and -ir verbs, along with several high-frequency irregular preterites you will need right away. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to narrate what you did yesterday, last weekend, or earlier today, and you will start noticing how já and ainda não help organize a story in time.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Form the regular preterite of -ar, -er, and -ir verbs.
  2. Use the irregular preterites of ir, ser, fazer, ter, and estar to narrate completed events.
  3. Ask and answer questions about what happened yesterday or last weekend.
  4. Sequence a short narration using primeiro, depois, and então.
  5. Use já and ainda não to say whether something has already happened.
  6. Read and write a short account of a completed day, preserving all diacritics.

Communication Scenario

A friend asks what you did yesterday. You need to answer with a short, connected account of your day: what you did in the morning, in the afternoon, and in the evening. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to tell this story, understand someone else telling theirs, and write it down clearly.

  1. This chapter activates the regular preterite and a small set of high-frequency irregular preterites.
  2. Present-tense routine language from earlier chapters remains available for context and comparison, but the chapter's new productive target is completed past action.
  3. You may describe a real day or invent one.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Note — Stress in the preterite

In the regular preterite, stress falls on the ending, not on the verb stem. In the eu form of -ar verbs, this produces a closed final vowel written with an acute accent: trabalhei, estudei, cheguei. In the você/ele/ela form of -ar verbs, the stressed vowel is written with a circumflex: trabalhou has no accent mark, but forms like falámos do not apply in Brazilian Portuguese — Brazilian nós preterite forms for -ar verbs are identical in spelling to the present tense (trabalhamos) and are distinguished only by context and stress in speech.

Listen for the contrast between present trabalho (stress on the first syllable) and preterite trabalhei (stress on the final syllable). This stress shift is one of the clearest oral signals that a speaker is talking about the past.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

The preterite narrates a specific action completed at a specific past time. Use it to answer the question "What happened?" rather than "What was going on?"

Building Block — Regular preterite
subject + verb stem + preterite ending
stress falls on the ending, signaling completed past action
Eu trabalhei ontem. Nós assistimos a um filme.
I worked yesterday. We watched a movie.

Building Block — Irregular preterite
subject + irregular preterite stem + irregular ending
no accent marks on these irregular forms; stress patterns must be learned by form
Eu fui ao cinema. Ela fez o trabalho de casa. Nós tivemos um dia difícil.

Já and ainda não

  • Eu já comi. I already ate. já + preterite = action already completed
  • Ela ainda não chegou. She hasn't arrived yet. ainda não + preterite = action not yet completed

Model Dialogue

Context: Two friends catching up on a Monday morning, informal register.
Mariana Oi! Tudo bem? O que você fez ontem? Hi! How's it going? What did you do yesterday?
Rafael Oi, Mariana! Ontem foi um dia bom. De manhã, eu trabalhei um pouco em casa. Depois, eu fui ao mercado e comprei frutas. Hi, Mariana! Yesterday was a good day. In the morning, I worked a little at home. Then, I went to the market and bought fruit.
Mariana E à tarde, o que você fez? And in the afternoon, what did you do?
Rafael À tarde, eu estudei português por uma hora. Então, eu assisti a um filme com minha família. E você, o que você fez? In the afternoon, I studied Portuguese for an hour. Then, I watched a movie with my family. And you, what did you do?
Mariana Eu tive um dia mais corrido. Eu fui ao trabalho, tive uma reunião longa, e não fiz muito depois. Já jantei e já fui dormir cedo! I had a busier day. I went to work, had a long meeting, and didn't do much afterward. I already had dinner and already went to bed early!
  1. List everything Rafael did yesterday, in order.
  2. What did Mariana do at work?
  3. Find one example of já in the dialogue and explain what it signals.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script
Ontem foi um dia cheio para mim. Primeiro, eu acordei cedo e fui trabalhar. Na hora do almoço, eu comi com dois colegas em um restaurante perto do escritório. Depois do trabalho, eu fui à academia e fiz exercício por quarenta minutos. À noite, eu estudei um pouco e então assisti a um programa de televisão. Eu já dormi antes da meia-noite. E você, o que você fez ontem?
Yesterday was a full day for me. First, I woke up early and went to work. At lunchtime, I ate with two colleagues at a restaurant near the office. After work, I went to the gym and exercised for forty minutes. In the evening, I studied a little and then watched a television program. I was already asleep before midnight. And you, what did you do yesterday?
  1. List the speaker's activities in the order they happened.
  2. Where did the speaker eat lunch, and with whom?
  3. What time-related detail tells you the speaker went to bed early?

Reading Practice

O fim de semana de Lucas

No sábado, Lucas acordou tarde e tomou café da manhã com calma. De manhã, ele não fez muito; só descansou e leu um livro. À tarde, ele foi ao parque com dois amigos e eles jogaram futebol por uma hora. À noite, Lucas e os amigos comeram pizza e assistiram a um filme juntos. No domingo, Lucas teve um dia mais tranquilo: ele estudou português por trinta minutos, ligou para a família e depois foi dormir cedo, porque a semana de trabalho já ia começar na segunda-feira.

Notice acordou, tomou, fez, foi, jogaram, comeram, assistiram, teve, estudou, ligou, and foi — a mix of regular and irregular preterites narrating a full weekend.

Tarefa de leitura — Reading task:

  1. What did Lucas do on Saturday morning, afternoon, and evening?
  2. What did Lucas do on Sunday, and why did he go to bed early?
  3. Find three irregular preterite forms in the passage and give their infinitives.

Writing Workshop

Write a short narrative of six to eight sentences describing what you did yesterday, following the model of Lucas's weekend above.

  1. Plan your timeline. Decide what happened in the morning, afternoon, and evening.
  2. Draft the morning section. Use at least one regular preterite verb.
  3. Draft the afternoon section. Use at least one irregular preterite verb (ir, fazer, ter, or estar).
  4. Draft the evening section. Use já or ainda não at least once.
  5. Connect the sections using primeiro, depois, então, and por fim.
  6. Check diacritics and spelling throughout, including forms such as já, até, and é.

Lista de revisão — Editing checklist:

  • The narrative uses at least one regular preterite verb correctly conjugated.
  • The narrative uses at least one irregular preterite verb correctly conjugated.
  • já or ainda não appears at least once with a clear meaning.
  • The events are connected with sequence connectors.
  • All diacritics are preserved.
  • No unscheduled grammar, such as the imperfect, appears.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Talking about the weekend

Asking O que você fez no fim de semana? is a common, friendly way to start a conversation on a Monday in many Brazilian workplaces and schools. It functions similarly to small talk about the weekend in many English-speaking contexts, and the level of detail shared usually depends on the closeness of the relationship.

Regional Variation — Nós preterite forms

In Brazilian Portuguese, the nós preterite form of regular -ar verbs looks identical to the present tense in writing (trabalhamos can mean either "we work" or "we worked"), and context or an added time expression usually resolves any ambiguity. European Portuguese written norms sometimes mark this distinction differently; this is a recognition note only, since the productive model for this course is Brazilian Portuguese.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using present-tense stress in the preterite

Error: Pronouncing trabalhei with stress on the first syllable, as in the present-tense trabalho.

Correction: Stress the final syllable in the preterite eu form: trabalhei. The stress shift is part of what signals the past tense in speech.

Common Mistake — Regularizing ir, ser, fazer, ter, and estar

Error: Saying Eu fazi or Eu tei by applying regular endings to irregular verbs.

Correction: These five verbs have irregular preterite stems that must be memorized: fui, fiz, tive, estive. Practice them as fixed forms before trying to build them from a rule.

Common Mistake — Dropping sequence connectors

Error: Listing several preterite actions in a row with no connecting words, producing a choppy narration.

Correction: Use primeiro, depois, então, and por fim to link events clearly: Primeiro, eu trabalhei. Depois, eu fui ao mercado.
