Projeto final: Minha vida em português

Final A2 Assessment · Speak and Write Portuguese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview

This chapter is the final assessment of Volume I. It does not introduce new grammar, new vocabulary domains, new pronunciation systems, or new orthographic burdens. Instead, it draws together everything you have built since Chapter 1: greetings and identity, family and description, dates and routines, food and shopping, plans and directions, help and health, and — most recently — narrating the past and stating opinions. You will assemble a written portfolio and complete an oral interaction that show what you can do in Portuguese, using only systems already activated in Chapters 1 through 19. This chapter is a demonstration of your beginner Portuguese, not a new lesson.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Introduce yourself and describe your identity, origin, and roles.
  2. Describe your family, friends, and daily routine.
  3. Give dates, times, locations, and simple directions.
  4. Narrate completed past events and describe habitual past conditions.
  5. State and support an opinion using a comparison and a connector.
  6. Combine these systems into one connected oral interview and one connected written portfolio.
  7. Self-assess your Portuguese using the Volume I performance criteria.

Communication Scenario

You have been invited to record a short video profile and write a companion portfolio piece titled "Minha vida em português" (My life in Portuguese) for a class or personal project. A viewer who does not know you should come away understanding who you are, what your daily life looks like, something you did recently or something from your past, and what you think about a topic you care about — food, a city, a show, or a book.

  1. This chapter activates no new productive grammar; it consolidates Chapters 1–19.
  2. The productive target is one connected oral interview and one connected written portfolio piece that move across identity, routine, past, and opinion.
  3. You may use fictional or generalized personal information at any point.
  4. Only systems assigned active, developed, or consolidated status through Chapter 19 are assessed; receptive and formulaic items are not required for independent production.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Note — Reading your own portfolio aloud

Before recording your oral interview, read your written portfolio aloud once for pronunciation rather than content. Pay particular attention to nasal vowels in words such as não, também, and então; to stress in accented words such as é, está, and opinião; and to the reduced, unstressed pronunciation of connector que compared with the more prominent question word o que.

Intelligibility, not native-like accent, is the pronunciation goal for this final assessment.

Grammar Review and Integration

This section reviews, but does not expand, the grammar systems activated across the volume. The goal is to combine them naturally in one connected account rather than to learn anything new.

Present-Time Systems

  • Eu sou estudante e moro em uma cidade pequena. I am a student and I live in a small city. ser + estar/moro, identity and location
  • Todos os dias, eu acordo cedo e tomo café da manhã com minha família. Every day, I wake up early and have breakfast with my family. regular present, routine, ter/família

Past-Time Systems

  • No ano passado, eu viajei para o Brasil e conheci pessoas incríveis. Last year, I traveled to Brazil and met incredible people. preterite, completed events
  • Quando eu era criança, eu morava em outra cidade e brincava muito na rua. When I was a child, I lived in another city and played a lot in the street. imperfect, background and habitual past

Opinion and Comparison

  • Acho que morar em uma cidade pequena é melhor do que morar em uma cidade grande, porque a vida é mais tranquila. I think living in a small city is better than living in a big city, because life is calmer. acho que + comparative + porque
Building Block — A connected life account
identity + routine + past event + past habit + opinion, connected with e, mas, porque, então
the capstone task links present, past, and opinion into one account about the same person
Eu sou professora e moro em São Paulo. Todos os dias eu trabalho e depois estudo português. No ano passado eu viajei para Portugal, mas quando eu era criança eu nunca tinha viajado para lá. Acho que viajar é a melhor forma de aprender um idioma, porque a gente pratica de verdade.
I am a teacher and I live in São Paulo. Every day I work and then I study Portuguese. Last year I traveled to Portugal, but when I was a child I had never traveled there. I think traveling is the best way to learn a language, because you really practice.
Language Note — What is not assessed in this chapter

This chapter does not assess productive subjunctive conjugation, advanced clitic placement, mesoclisis, or unscheduled regional variation. Fixed formulas such as talvez and espero que may appear as whole expressions if you choose to use them, but they are not required and will not be evaluated as independent grammar production.

Model Dialogue

Context: A model interview between an instructor and a student, integrating identity, routine, past, and opinion. Informal-neutral classroom register.
Professora Oi! Para começar, quem é você e onde você mora? Hi! To start, who are you and where do you live?
Aluno Eu me chamo Rafael, sou estudante de engenharia e moro com minha família em Belo Horizonte. My name is Rafael, I'm an engineering student and I live with my family in Belo Horizonte.
Professora Legal! Como é a sua rotina, geralmente? Nice! What's your routine usually like?
Aluno Eu acordo às sete, estudo de manhã e trabalho à tarde. À noite, eu geralmente janto com minha família. I wake up at seven, study in the morning, and work in the afternoon. At night, I usually have dinner with my family.
Professora E o que você fez no fim de semana passado? And what did you do last weekend?
Aluno Eu viajei para a casa dos meus avós e visitei uma cidade que eu nunca tinha visitado. I traveled to my grandparents' house and visited a city that I had never visited.
Professora Que legal. E quando você era criança, como era a sua vida? How nice. And when you were a child, what was your life like?
Aluno Eu morava em uma cidade menor e brincava na rua todo dia com meus primos. I lived in a smaller city and played in the street every day with my cousins.
Professora Para terminar: na sua opinião, qual é a melhor parte de morar no Brasil? To finish: in your opinion, what's the best part of living in Brazil?
Aluno Acho que a melhor parte é a comida, porque tem muita variedade, mas as pessoas também são muito acolhedoras. I think the best part is the food, because there's a lot of variety, but the people are also very welcoming.
  1. List the four topics the interview moves through, in order.
  2. Find one preterite verb and one imperfect verb, and explain the difference in function.
  3. Find the sentence with an opinion, a comparison, and a connector, and identify each part.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script
Oi, eu me chamo Camila e sou de Salvador. Moro sozinha e trabalho em um escritório perto de casa. Todos os dias eu acordo cedo, tomo café e vou para o trabalho de ônibus. No mês passado, eu viajei para o Rio de Janeiro e visitei minha melhor amiga. Quando eu era criança, eu morava perto da praia e nadava todo fim de semana. Hoje eu moro em um apartamento, mas ainda amo o mar. Na minha opinião, Salvador tem a melhor comida do Brasil, mas o Rio tem paisagens tão bonitas quanto as daqui.
Hi, my name is Camila and I'm from Salvador. I live alone and work in an office near home. Every day I wake up early, have coffee, and go to work by bus. Last month, I traveled to Rio de Janeiro and visited my best friend. When I was a child, I lived near the beach and swam every weekend. Today I live in an apartment, but I still love the sea. In my opinion, Salvador has the best food in Brazil, but Rio has landscapes as beautiful as the ones here.
  1. What does Camila do every day?
  2. What happened last month?
  3. What was Camila's life like as a child, and what is one opinion she gives at the end?

Reading Practice

Minha vida em português — Modelo de portfólio

Eu me chamo Bruno, sou brasileiro e moro em Curitiba. Sou professor de história em uma escola pública. Todos os dias eu acordo às seis, tomo café com minha esposa e vou para o trabalho de bicicleta. À noite, eu geralmente leio um pouco antes de dormir. No ano passado, eu viajei para Portugal pela primeira vez e conheci a cidade do Porto, que é linda. Quando eu era criança, eu morava em uma cidade pequena no interior e passava as férias na casa dos meus avós, onde eu ajudava no jardim e ouvia histórias antigas. Hoje, na minha opinião, viver em uma cidade como Curitiba é melhor do que viver em uma cidade muito grande, porque o trânsito é menor e a qualidade de vida é mais tranquila. Mas confesso que sinto saudade da simplicidade da minha infância no interior.

Writing Workshop

Write your own portfolio piece, "Minha vida em português," of eight to twelve sentences, following the model above.

  1. Introduce yourself. State your name, nationality or origin, and role.
  2. Describe your routine. Include at least two daily actions with frequency words.
  3. Narrate a completed past event. Use the preterite for at least one specific event.
  4. Describe a past habit or background condition. Use the imperfect for childhood or past routine.
  5. State an opinion. Use acho que or na minha opinião, with a comparative or superlative and a connector.
  6. Connect at least one idea with relative que.
  7. Check diacritics, agreement, and verb endings throughout, using the checklist below.

Lista de revisão — Editing checklist:

  • The portfolio introduces identity, routine, a past event, a past habit, and an opinion, in a connected way.
  • The preterite and imperfect are each used correctly at least once, with a clear function for each.
  • The opinion includes a comparative or superlative and at least one connector.
  • Relative que connects two ideas at least once.
  • Articles, gender, and adjective agreement are controlled throughout.
  • All diacritics are preserved, including é, história, and opinião.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Telling your story across Lusophone communities

Personal narrative is a common genre across the Portuguese-speaking world, from a spoken interview in Brazil to a written profile in Portugal or a community radio segment in Cape Verde or Mozambique. The specific expressions and pacing may vary, but the underlying task — introducing yourself, describing your life, and sharing an opinion — remains recognizable across these contexts.

Regional Variation — A reminder about the declared production model

Throughout this volume you have produced contemporary Brazilian Portuguese as your default model, while recognizing labeled European, African, and other Lusophone forms receptively. Continue to use your declared production variety consistently in your final portfolio and interview; broader Lusophone comparison remains recognition-level material at this stage.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Mixing preterite and imperfect without a clear function

Error: Using the preterite for a repeated childhood habit, as in Eu brinquei todo dia when describing childhood in general.

Correction: Use the imperfect for repeated or background past actions and the preterite for a single completed event: Eu brincava todo dia (habitual) versus Ontem eu brinquei com meus primos (one specific occasion).

Common Mistake — Stacking unconnected sentences instead of a connected account

Error: Writing a list of disconnected facts: Eu sou estudante. Eu moro em São Paulo. Eu gosto de música.

Correction: Use connectors and relative que to link ideas: Eu sou estudante e moro em São Paulo, uma cidade que eu adoro porque tem muita música.

Common Mistake — Overloading the portfolio with unscheduled grammar

Error: Attempting to use productive subjunctive forms, advanced clitic placement, or unlabeled regional forms to sound more sophisticated.

Correction: Use the systems activated through Chapter 19 with confidence and accuracy. A clear, correct A2 account demonstrates stronger control than an ambitious account full of unscheduled errors.
