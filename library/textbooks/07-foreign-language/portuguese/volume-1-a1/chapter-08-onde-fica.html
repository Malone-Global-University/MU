8

Onde fica?

Places, Location, and Movement · Speak and Write Portuguese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview

Once you can describe your routine, the next natural step is describing where things are and how to get there. This chapter continues Unit II by teaching you to locate places and things with estar and ficar, to talk about going somewhere with ir a and ir para, and to use the contractions no, na, do, and da to talk about neighborhoods, homes, and everyday places. You will also practice the question word onde and continue developing your pronunciation of r and rr, which appear constantly in place names and directions.

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Ask and answer Onde fica? to locate places.
  2. Distinguish estar and ficar when describing location.
  3. Use ir a and ir para to describe movement toward a place.
  4. Use the contractions no, na, do, and da with common places.
  5. Name everyday places in a neighborhood and describe simple movement through it.
  6. Pronounce r and rr accurately in place names and directions.

Communication Scenario

A new member of the conversation group is visiting the neighborhood for the first time and does not know where anything is. Group members need to explain where common places are located and how to get to them.

  1. This chapter activates estar versus ficar, ir a/para, the contractions no/na/do/da, and the question word onde.
  2. You may invent your own neighborhood and places if you prefer not to describe a real one.
  3. Chapters 1–7 systems (greetings, ser, ter, days and times, regular -ar/-er/-ir verbs, gostar de, querer, fazer, ir, frequency words) continue to recycle throughout this chapter.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — r versus rr

A single r between vowels, as in bairro's first syllable or parque, is pronounced as a light tap in this production model. A double rr, as in bairro, and a word-initial r, as in rua and restaurante, are pronounced with a stronger, more guttural sound in contemporary Brazilian Portuguese. Word-final r, as in ficar, is often softened or reduced in casual Brazilian speech. Practice these pairs aloud: rua / hora, bairro / caro, restaurante / apartamento.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Estar versus ficar for location

Portuguese uses both estar and ficar to talk about where something is. Estar describes where something is located right now; ficar is commonly used for the fixed, ongoing location of a place, such as a building or a neighborhood.

Building Block
estar: eu estou, você/ele/ela está, nós estamos, vocês/eles/elas estão
ficar: eu fico, você/ele/ela fica, nós ficamos, vocês/eles/elas ficam
note: ficar is a regular -ar verb; estar is irregular
A farmácia fica perto do banco.
The pharmacy is (located) near the bank.
  • Onde fica o mercado? Where is the market (located)?
  • Eu estou no parque agora. I am at the park right now. estar → current, momentary location
  • A escola fica na Rua das Flores. The school is on Flores Street. ficar → fixed location of a place

This chapter activates ficar specifically for asking and stating the location of places. Other functions of estar and ficar are developed in later chapters and volumes.

Ir a and ir para

Both ir a and ir para mean "to go to," but they differ slightly in focus: ir a often suggests a shorter visit, while ir para often suggests staying longer or heading toward a destination in a more general sense. In everyday Brazilian speech, ir para is extremely common for both meanings.

Building Block
ir a + o/a = ir ao / ir à
ir para + o/a = ir para o / ir para a
note: ir a contracts with the article; ir para does not contract
Eu vou ao mercado.
I'm going to the market.
  • Nós vamos para a escola de manhã. We go to school in the morning.
  • Ela vai à farmácia agora. She's going to the pharmacy now. a + a = à

This chapter activates ir a and ir para with concrete, everyday places. Abstract and idiomatic uses of por/para are scheduled for later chapters.

Contractions: no, na, do, da

The prepositions em and de contract with the definite articles o and a. These contractions are required, everyday Portuguese, not optional shortcuts.

Building Block
em + o = no · em + a = na
de + o = do · de + a = da
note: no/na/do/da are always required in place of em o, em a, de o, de a
O restaurante fica na praça.
The restaurant is on the plaza.
  • Eu estou no restaurante. I am at the restaurant. em + o restaurante = no restaurante
  • A padaria fica perto do parque. The bakery is near the park. de + o parque = do parque
  • Nós moramos na Rua das Flores. We live on Flores Street. em + a Rua = na Rua
Language Focus — Asking with onde

Onde combines with ficar and estar to ask about location: Onde fica a farmácia? / Onde você está? Notice that onde comes first in the question, the same pattern as other question words from Chapter 2.

Model Dialogue: Perdido no bairro

Context: A visitor to the neighborhood asks a group member for help finding places. Friendly, informal register.

Sofia: Oi! Desculpa, você mora aqui no bairro? Hi! Sorry, do you live here in the neighborhood?
Daniel: Oi! Sim, eu moro na Rua das Flores. Você está perdida? Hi! Yes, I live on Flores Street. Are you lost?
Sofia: Um pouco! Onde fica a farmácia? A little! Where is the pharmacy?
Daniel: A farmácia fica perto do banco, na praça. The pharmacy is near the bank, on the plaza.
Sofia: E o mercado? Fica longe daqui? And the market? Is it far from here?
Daniel: Não, fica perto! O mercado fica ao lado da escola. No, it's close! The market is next to the school.
Sofia: Obrigada! E você, para onde vai agora? Thank you! And you, where are you going now?
Daniel: Eu vou para o restaurante em frente do parque. Você quer ir também? I'm going to the restaurant in front of the park. Do you want to go too?

This dialogue combines ficar, estar, ir a/para, contractions, and onde (Ch. 8) with greetings, morar, and querer recycled from earlier chapters.

Guided Practice

  1. Complete each sentence with the correct form of ficar or estar: A escola ______ perto do banco. / Eu ______ no parque agora. / Onde ______ a farmácia?
  2. Complete each sentence with no, na, do, or da: O restaurante fica ______ praça. / Nós moramos ______ Rua das Flores. / A padaria fica perto ______ parque.
  3. Rewrite each sentence using ir a or ir para with the correct contraction: Eu vou (a + o) mercado. / Ela vai (a + a) escola.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script
Oi! Eu sou o Daniel. Eu moro na Rua das Flores, perto da praça. O banco fica na praça, e a farmácia fica ao lado do banco. Eu sempre vou ao mercado de manhã, porque o mercado fica perto da minha casa. À noite, eu vou para o restaurante em frente do parque.
Hi! I'm Daniel. I live on Flores Street, near the plaza. The bank is on the plaza, and the pharmacy is next to the bank. I always go to the market in the morning, because the market is near my house. At night, I go to the restaurant in front of the park.
  1. Where does Daniel live?
  2. Where is the pharmacy located?
  3. Why does Daniel go to the market in the morning?

Reading Practice

O bairro da Marina — Marina's neighborhood

Marina mora em um apartamento na Rua Almirante. O bairro fica perto do centro da cidade. A escola fica ao lado da casa de Marina, e o mercado fica em frente da escola. Todo dia, Marina vai a pé para o mercado. Às vezes, ela vai de bicicleta para o parque, que fica longe do bairro. Ela nunca vai de carro, porque tudo fica perto.

Tarefa de leitura — Reading task:

  1. Where does Marina live?
  2. Where is the market located, in relation to the school?
  3. Why does Marina never go by car?

Writing Workshop

Complete the following linked writing tasks in order to build a short message giving directions to a visiting group member.

Tarefa 1 — My neighborhood: Write one sentence stating where you live, using morar and a contraction (na or no).

Tarefa 2 — Locating places: Write two sentences using ficar to locate two places near you, each with perto de, longe de, ao lado de, or em frente de.

Fill in the blank
Oi! Eu moro ______. Perto daqui, a ______ fica ______. Eu sempre vou ______ a pé. Você quer visitar?
Hi! I live ______. Near here, the ______ is ______. I always go ______ on foot. Do you want to visit?

Tarefa 3 — Going somewhere: Write one sentence with ir a and one sentence with ir para, describing places you go.

Tarefa 4 — Full note: Combine Tarefas 1–3 into one short note of four to five sentences for the visiting group member.

  1. Draft each section separately. Write the neighborhood, location, and movement sentences one at a time before combining them.
  2. Combine the sections into one note. Add a greeting at the start, recycled from Chapter 1.
  3. Check every contraction. Confirm that no/na/do/da and ao/à are used correctly.
  4. Check ficar and estar. Confirm each verb matches its function: fixed location versus current location.
  5. Check diacritics. Confirm that accented forms such as à and praça are written correctly.

Lista de revisão — Editing checklist:

  • The note includes a greeting, a home location, at least two located places, and one movement sentence.
  • ficar and estar are used correctly for their functions.
  • Contractions no/na/do/da and ao/à are used correctly and consistently.
  • All diacritics, including à and praça, are preserved.
  • Onde is used correctly if a question appears in the note.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Neighborhood life in Brazil

Many Brazilian neighborhoods are organized so that small markets, bakeries, and pharmacies are within walking distance of homes, though this varies by city, region, and household income. In large cities, some neighborhoods depend heavily on buses or cars, while in smaller towns, walking remains common. Do not assume every Brazilian neighborhood looks the same.

Regional Variation — Naming everyday places

Words for small neighborhood markets and bakeries can vary somewhat by region within Brazil, and Portuguese-speaking countries outside Brazil may use different everyday terms for some of these places. This course presents contemporary Brazilian vocabulary as the default; other Lusophone terms are recognition only unless a later chapter labels and activates them.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using estar for a place's fixed location

Error: Saying A escola está na Rua das Flores when describing where a building is permanently located.

Correction: Use ficar for the fixed location of places: A escola fica na Rua das Flores.

Common Mistake — Forgetting the contraction

Error: Saying Eu estou em o parque, leaving the preposition and article uncontracted.

Correction: Always contract: Eu estou no parque.

Common Mistake — Mixing up ir a and ir para contractions

Error: Saying Eu vou para ao mercado, combining both patterns.

Correction: Choose one pattern: Eu vou ao mercado or Eu vou para o mercado.
