Vamos sair?

Plans, Invitations, and Future Reference · Speak and Write Portuguese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview

This chapter opens Unit III by giving you the tools to talk about the future: making plans, inviting someone to do something, accepting or declining, and discussing the weather and weekend leisure. The central pattern is ir + infinitivo — the same verb ir you already use for movement (Capítulo 8) now points toward something you are going to do. You will also meet vamos as an invitation form, connect ideas with porque, and learn to recognize the Brazilian progressive (estar + gerúndio) when other people use it to talk about what is happening right now.

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State future plans using ir + infinitivo.
  2. Invite someone to do something using vamos + infinitivo.
  3. Accept and decline invitations appropriately, and give a reason using porque.
  4. Talk about the weather and typical weekend or leisure activities.
  5. Recognize the Brazilian progressive (estar + gerúndio) when it describes an action happening right now.
  6. Combine these systems into a short spoken and written exchange about weekend plans.

Communication Scenario

A friend calls or messages to ask about weekend plans. The two of you decide what to do together, check the weather, and confirm a time and place — recycling the routine, food, location, and shopping language from Unit II.

  1. This chapter activates ir + infinitivo, vamos, future time expressions, and porque for the first time.
  2. The Brazilian progressive (estar + gerúndio) appears only as a recognition item in this chapter; you do not need to produce it yourself yet.
  3. You may invent your own plans, weather, and activities if you prefer not to describe a real weekend.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Ir + infinitivo: talking about the future

Use this pattern to say what you or someone else is going to do, without conjugating a second verb.

Building Block
ir (conjugated) + infinitive verb
The first verb, ir, carries the subject agreement you already know from Capítulo 8. The second verb never changes form.
Eu vou estudar amanhã.
I am going to study tomorrow.
  • Nós vamos sair no sábado. We are going to go out on Saturday. ir (nós) + sair (infinitive)
  • Você vai trabalhar no fim de semana? Are you going to work this weekend? ir (você) + trabalhar (infinitive)
  • Eles vão comer no restaurante novo. They are going to eat at the new restaurant. ir (eles) + comer (infinitive)
Vamos as an invitation

Vamos is the nós form of ir, but on its own it also works as "Let's," inviting someone to join an activity.

  • Vamos ao cinema hoje à noite? Shall we go to the movies tonight?
  • Vamos comer alguma coisa! Let's eat something!

Compare: Nós vamos ao cinema (a plain statement, "We are going to the movies") versus Vamos ao cinema? (an invitation, "Shall we go to the movies?"). Rising intonation or a question mark signals the invitation reading.

Giving a reason with porque

Use porque to explain why you accept, decline, or make a particular plan.

  • Não posso sair hoje porque preciso trabalhar. I can't go out today because I need to work.
  • Vamos ao parque porque faz sol. Let's go to the park because it's sunny.

Distinguish porque (because, joining a reason to a statement) from por que (why, used in questions). Only porque is activated for production in this chapter.

Language Focus — Recognizing the progressive

Brazilian Portuguese has a separate way of saying that something is happening right now: estar + gerúndio (the -ando/-endo/-indo form). You will meet it in this chapter only as a recognition item — for example, in the weather expression está chovendo ("it's raining") or in a friend's message saying Estou fazendo planos ("I'm making plans"). You are not expected to produce these forms independently yet; the progressive becomes an active productive system beginning in Chapter 12.

Model Dialogue

Context: Two friends, informal register, texting about the weekend.
Marina Oi! O que você vai fazer no fim de semana? Hi! What are you going to do this weekend?
Thiago Ainda não sei. Talvez eu vá estudar. E você? I don't know yet. Maybe I'm going to study. And you?
Marina Eu vou à praia com a minha família. Vamos também no domingo? I'm going to the beach with my family. Shall we also go on Sunday?
Thiago Claro que sim! Faz sol no domingo? Of course! Is it sunny on Sunday?
Marina Acho que sim. Mas hoje está chovendo bastante aqui. I think so. But it's raining a lot here today.
Thiago Então, vamos combinar o horário amanhã. Não posso agora porque preciso trabalhar. Then let's settle on a time tomorrow. I can't now because I need to work.
Marina Tudo bem. Até amanhã! Okay. See you tomorrow!

Note está chovendo in Marina's message: a progressive form for recognition only. Everything else in this exchange uses systems activated in this chapter or earlier.

Guided Practice

  1. Reread the model dialogue and underline every example of ir + infinitivo.
  2. Complete each sentence with the correct form of ir: Eu ______ sair no sábado. / Nós ______ ao parque amanhã. / Eles ______ comer no restaurante.
  3. Turn each statement into an invitation using Vamos...?: Nós vamos ao cinema. → ______? / Nós vamos comer pizza. → ______?

Listening Task

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script
Oi, tudo bem? Este fim de semana eu vou fazer muitas coisas. No sábado de manhã, eu vou ao mercado, porque eu preciso comprar frutas. Depois, eu vou à praia com uns amigos, porque faz sol e calor. À noite, nós vamos a um show perto do centro. No domingo, eu vou descansar em casa, porque acho que vai chover. E você, o que você vai fazer?
Hi, how's it going? This weekend I'm going to do a lot of things. On Saturday morning, I'm going to the market, because I need to buy fruit. Then, I'm going to the beach with some friends, because it's sunny and hot. At night, we're going to a show near downtown. On Sunday, I'm going to rest at home, because I think it's going to rain. And you, what are you going to do?
  1. Why is the speaker going to the market on Saturday morning?
  2. Why is the speaker going to the beach?
  3. What is the speaker going to do on Sunday, and why?

Reading Task

Uma mensagem de Rafael

Oi, Camila! Como você está? Estou pensando no fim de semana. Você quer sair no sábado? Podemos ir ao parque, porque faz sol e calor esta semana. Se chover, vamos ao cinema. Eu estou trabalhando muito esta semana, mas no sábado eu estou livre o dia todo. Depois do parque, vamos comer alguma coisa perto da praça? Me avise se você pode!

Notice estou pensando, estou trabalhando, and estou livre in this message. The progressive forms are for recognition only; estou livre uses estar with an adjective, already active since Capítulo 4.

Writing Task

Complete the following linked writing tasks in order to build a short message inviting a friend to do something this weekend.

Tarefa 1 — Plan: Write one sentence stating a plan using ir + infinitivo and a future time expression.

Tarefa 2 — Invitation: Write one invitation using Vamos...?.

Fill in the blank
Oi! Tudo bem? ______ (dia/momento futuro), eu vou ______. Vamos ______ juntos? Faz ______ esta semana, então ______. Se você não pode, tudo bem — talvez ______ semana que vem!
Hi! How's it going? ______ (a future day/time), I'm going to ______. Shall we ______ together? It's ______ this week, so ______. If you can't, that's okay — maybe ______ next week!

Tarefa 3 — Reason and decline: Write one sentence declining an invitation and explaining why with porque.

Tarefa 4 — Full message: Combine Tarefas 1–3 into one short message of five to six sentences inviting a friend to do something this weekend, following the model of Rafael's message in the Reading Task.

  1. Draft each section separately. Write the plan, invitation, and decline-with-reason sentences one at a time before combining them.
  2. Combine the sections into one message. Add a greeting at the start, recycled from Chapter 1.
  3. Check the ir + infinitivo pattern. Confirm that ir agrees with the subject and the second verb stays in the infinitive.
  4. Check porque. Confirm it introduces a reason and is spelled as one word, distinct from por que.
  5. Check diacritics. Confirm that forms such as vamos, às vezes, and até keep their correct spelling.

Lista de revisão — Editing checklist:

  • The message includes a greeting, a stated plan, an invitation, and a decline with a reason.
  • ir + infinitivo is used correctly, with subject agreement only on ir.
  • vamos is used correctly as an invitation where intended.
  • porque is used correctly to give a reason.
  • All diacritics are preserved.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Making plans informally

Among many Brazilian friend groups, weekend plans are often confirmed close to the day itself rather than scheduled far in advance, and messaging apps are a common way to coordinate. Weather frequently shapes plans, since outdoor activities such as the beach or a park are popular whenever the forecast allows, though this varies by city, region, and personal preference.

Regional Variation — Weather and leisure vocabulary

Weather patterns and popular weekend activities differ across Brazil's regions and across other Lusophone countries — for example, beach culture is far more central in coastal cities than in inland ones. This chapter continues the Brazilian production model established earlier in the volume; other regional patterns remain recognition only unless a later chapter labels and activates them.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Conjugating the second verb

Error: Saying Eu vou como pizza, conjugating both verbs.

Correction: Only ir agrees with the subject; the second verb stays in the infinitive: Eu vou comer pizza.

Common Mistake — Confusing porque and por que

Error: Writing Por que preciso trabalhar to mean "because I need to work."

Correction: Use porque (one word) for "because": Não posso sair porque preciso trabalhar. Por que (two words) is reserved for asking "why."

Common Mistake — Trying to produce the progressive too early

Error: Attempting to build new estar + gerúndio sentences from scratch, such as inventing Eu estou planejando um viagem without guidance.

Correction: In this chapter, recognize the progressive when you read or hear it, such as in está chovendo, but rely on ir + infinitivo for your own plans. Independent progressive production begins in Chapter 12.
