Quanto custa?

Shopping, Quantities, and Transactions · Speak and Write Portuguese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview

Now that you can talk about where places are, this chapter teaches you what to do once you get there: shopping. You will learn to point things out with demonstratives such as este and esse, to place descriptive adjectives correctly, to say what you need with precisar de, to ask what is possible with poder, and to handle numbers and prices in everyday transactions. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to shop for clothing and everyday items, ask how much something costs, and compare simple options.

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Ask Quanto custa? and understand answers involving prices.
  2. Point out items using este, esse, and aquele in their basic forms.
  3. Place common adjectives correctly, including color and size adjectives.
  4. State what you need using precisar de.
  5. Ask what is possible or allowed using poder.
  6. Use numbers to state prices, sizes, and quantities.

Communication Scenario

A conversation-group member is shopping for clothes and everyday items at a small shop and needs to ask about prices, sizes, and colors, and decide what to buy.

  1. This chapter activates demonstratives, adjective position, precisar de, poder, and numbers and prices.
  2. You may invent your own shop, items, and prices if you prefer not to describe a real purchase.
  3. Chapters 1–8 systems (greetings, ser, estar, ter, days and times, gostar de, querer, ficar, contractions no/na/do/da) continue to recycle throughout this chapter.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — Stress in numbers and prices

Numbers such as cinquenta, cem, and duzentos follow the stress patterns you have already practiced, with stress usually falling on the second-to-last syllable unless marked otherwise. When saying a price aloud, group the number and the currency word together in one smooth phrase rather than pausing between them: trinta reais, not trinta... reais. Practice these price phrases aloud: dez reais, cinquenta reais, cem reais.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Demonstratives: este, esse, aquele

Portuguese demonstratives point out specific items by distance from the speaker. This chapter activates only their basic singular and plural forms for shopping contexts; the full distance system is developed further in later chapters.

Building Block
este/esta (this, near me) · esse/essa (that, near you) · aquele/aquela (that, far from both)
note: all four forms agree in gender and number with the noun
Eu quero esta camisa azul.
I want this blue shirt.
  • Quanto custa esse vestido? How much does that dress cost?
  • Eu prefiro aqueles sapatos pretos. I prefer those black shoes (over there). aqueles → plural, masculine, far from speaker and listener

This chapter activates demonstratives only as pointing words with concrete, visible items. Their use as pronouns referring to ideas or unstated nouns is not activated yet.

Adjective position: size, color, and description

Descriptive adjectives, including size and color, normally follow the noun in Portuguese and agree with it in gender and number.

Building Block
noun + adjective (agreement in gender and number)
uma camisa grande · um sapato pequeno · uma bolsa vermelha
a large shirt · a small shoe · a red bag
  • Eu preciso de uma calça azul, tamanho médio. I need blue pants, medium size.
  • Estes sapatos são pequenos para mim. These shoes are small for me. pequenos agrees with sapatos: masculine plural

This chapter activates post-nominal adjective position for size, color, and description. Pre-nominal position and meaning shifts by position are introduced beginning this chapter but developed further in Chapters 11–14.

Precisar de and poder

Precisar de expresses need and is followed by a noun with de, or directly by an infinitive without de. Poder expresses possibility, permission, or ability and is followed by an infinitive.

Building Block
precisar de: eu preciso de, você/ele/ela precisa de, nós precisamos de, vocês/eles/elas precisam de
poder: eu posso, você/ele/ela pode, nós podemos, vocês/eles/elas podem
Eu preciso de um vestido para a festa. Você pode me ajudar?
I need a dress for the party. Can you help me?
  • Nós precisamos de sapatos novos. We need new shoes.
  • Eu posso pagar com cartão? Can I pay with a card? poder + infinitive: possibility/permission

This chapter activates poder for simple questions about possibility and permission in shopping contexts. Broader uses of poder, saber, and conseguir together are scheduled for Chapter 13.

Language Focus — Asking with quanto

Quanto custa? is the formulaic core question for prices. With plural items, Brazilian speakers commonly say Quanto custam?, agreeing the verb with the plural item; both forms are acceptable, and Quanto custa? remains fully understandable and common even with plural items in everyday speech.

Model Dialogue: Na loja de roupas

Context: A customer is shopping for a shirt in a small clothing store. Polite, everyday service register.

Vendedor: Boa tarde! Posso ajudar? Good afternoon! Can I help?
Cliente: Boa tarde! Eu preciso de uma camisa para o trabalho. Good afternoon! I need a shirt for work.
Vendedor: Claro! Temos esta camisa branca e essa camisa azul. Qual tamanho você usa? Of course! We have this white shirt and that blue shirt. What size do you wear?
Cliente: Médio, por favor. Quanto custa a azul? Medium, please. How much is the blue one?
Vendedor: Custa oitenta reais. A branca é mais barata: custa sessenta reais. It costs eighty reais. The white one is cheaper: it costs sixty reais.
Cliente: Eu prefiro a azul. Posso pagar com cartão? I prefer the blue one. Can I pay with a card?
Vendedor: Pode sim! Aqui está o seu troco... ah, não precisa, é no cartão. Obrigado! Yes, you can! Here's your change... oh, that's not needed, it's on the card. Thank you!

This dialogue combines demonstratives, adjective position, precisar de, poder, and numbers and prices (Ch. 9) with greetings and preferir-style preference language recycled from earlier chapters.

Listening script
Oi! Eu estou na loja de roupas agora. Eu preciso de uma calça e um vestido para uma festa. A calça preta custa cem reais, e o vestido verde custa cento e vinte reais. Eu prefiro o vestido verde, mas ele é caro. A vendedora pergunta: "Posso ajudar? Qual tamanho você usa?" Eu digo: "Médio, por favor."

Tarefa comunicativa — Communicative task: shop role-play

With a partner, take turns playing customer and salesperson. The customer names an item, a color, and a size, and asks the price; the salesperson answers with a price and offers a second option using mais barato or mais caro.

Prática individual — Solo alternative

Record yourself describing three items you would like to buy, including a demonstrative, a color or size adjective, and a price for each.

Reading Practice

Uma lista de compras — A shopping list

Rafael precisa de roupas novas para o trabalho. Ele vai à loja perto de casa. Primeiro, ele vê uma camisa branca; ela custa cinquenta reais. Depois, ele vê uma calça azul; ela custa noventa reais. Rafael também precisa de sapatos, mas os sapatos pretos são muito caros. Ele pergunta: "O senhor tem sapatos mais baratos?" O vendedor mostra um sapato marrom, que custa setenta reais. Rafael compra a camisa, a calça e o sapato marrom. Ele paga com cartão.

Tarefa de leitura — Reading task:

  1. What two items does Rafael see first, and how much does each cost?
  2. Why doesn't Rafael buy the black shoes?
  3. How does Rafael pay for his purchases?

Writing Workshop

Complete the following linked writing tasks in order to build a short shopping note for a friend.

Tarefa 1 — What you need: Write one sentence using precisar de to state one item you need.

Tarefa 2 — Describing items: Write two sentences describing items using a color or size adjective in correct position and agreement.

Fill in the blank
Oi! Eu estou na loja. Eu preciso de ______. Esta ______ custa ______ reais. Eu posso ______? Você quer ir comigo?
Hi! I'm at the store. I need ______. This ______ costs ______ reais. Can I ______? Do you want to come with me?

Tarefa 3 — Asking and comparing: Write one question with Quanto custa? and one sentence comparing two items with mais caro or mais barato.

Tarefa 4 — Full note: Combine Tarefas 1–3 into one short note of four to five sentences for a friend about your shopping trip.

  1. Draft each section separately. Write the need, description, and comparison sentences one at a time before combining them.
  2. Combine the sections into one note. Add a greeting at the start, recycled from Chapter 1.
  3. Check adjective agreement. Confirm that gender and number match the noun in every description.
  4. Check demonstratives. Confirm that este/esse/aquele forms agree with the noun and fit the context.
  5. Check numbers and diacritics. Confirm that prices are written correctly and that accented forms such as é and está are preserved.

Lista de revisão — Editing checklist:

  • The note includes a greeting, a need, at least two described items, and a price question or comparison.
  • Adjectives agree in gender and number with their nouns.
  • Demonstratives are used correctly and agree with their nouns.
  • precisar de and poder are used correctly for their functions.
  • All diacritics and number spellings are preserved.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Shopping and small talk

In many Brazilian shops, salespeople often greet customers warmly and offer help quickly, and brief small talk before or during a purchase is common and expected rather than rushed past. Bargaining is not generally expected in ordinary retail stores, though it may occur in informal markets or with larger purchases; this varies by setting, city, and store type.

Regional Variation — Currency and clothing terms

This chapter uses the Brazilian real (real, reais) as the default currency term, since it matches the declared Brazilian production model. Other Portuguese-speaking countries use different currencies and, in some cases, different everyday clothing vocabulary. These forms are recognition only unless a later chapter labels and activates them.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Forgetting adjective agreement

Error: Saying uma camisa branco, leaving the adjective in the masculine form with a feminine noun.

Correction: Agree the adjective with the noun: uma camisa branca.

Common Mistake — Dropping de after precisar

Error: Saying Eu preciso um vestido, omitting de before a noun.

Correction: Include de before a noun: Eu preciso de um vestido. (No de is needed before an infinitive: Eu preciso pagar.)

Common Mistake — Confusing demonstrative gender/number

Error: Saying este sapatos, mixing a singular demonstrative with a plural noun.

Correction: Match number and gender: estes sapatos.
