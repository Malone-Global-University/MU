Debate e opinião

Debate and Opinion · Speak and Write Portuguese: Connected Discourse and Public Life

Chapter Overview

Chapter 9 brings together the opinion and evaluation systems developed in Chapter 8 and extends them into fuller argument. You will learn to support a position with more than one reason, connect ideas using argument connectors such as além disso and por um lado / por outro lado, concede a point to an opposing view with expressions such as embora, compare options using tão... quanto and mais... do que, and identify people, things, or ideas more precisely using relative clauses with que and quem. This chapter closes Unit II and prepares you for the Unit II assessment in Chapter 10, so it emphasizes disagreeing respectfully and building a short, organized argument rather than simply reacting to a single cultural item.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State an opinion on a debatable topic and support it with at least two connected reasons.
  2. Link ideas using argument connectors such as além disso, por um lado / por outro lado, and portanto.
  3. Concede a point to an opposing view using embora with the subjunctive, while still maintaining your own position.
  4. Compare two options or positions using comparative and equative structures such as mais... do que, menos... do que, and tão... quanto.
  5. Identify a person, thing, or idea more precisely using a relative clause with que or quem.
  6. Disagree respectfully and invite a partner's view using expressions such as discordo um pouco porque and o que você acha?
  7. Ask for clarification, repetition, confirmation, or repair when communication breaks down.
  8. Maintain consistent Brazilian Portuguese production, with any variation clearly labeled.

Communication Scenario

A small study group meets to discuss a debatable everyday topic — for example, whether working from home is better than working in an office, or whether smartphones help or hurt young people's education. Each participant states a position, supports it with reasons, concedes a point to the other side, and compares the two options before inviting the group's reaction.

  1. The productive target is a short spoken or written argument with a clear position, at least two supporting reasons, one concession, one comparison, and one relative clause used naturally.
  2. Debate topics may be invented, adapted, or drawn from everyday life; specific real-world statistics should not be presented as verified fact.
  3. Systems from Chapters 1–8 remain available and should be recycled where natural, especially the opinion and evaluation frames from Chapter 8.
  4. Formal debate structure (opening statements, rebuttals, closing arguments as a specialized genre) is not required in this chapter; the focus is on everyday reasoned disagreement.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Contrastive stress in agreement and disagreement

When agreeing or disagreeing in Brazilian Portuguese, speakers often place extra stress on the verb itself to signal the stance clearly: Concordo com isso. and Discordo um pouco. When contrasting two sides with por um lado and por outro lado, a small pause and slight pitch reset before por outro lado helps the listener hear that a contrasting idea is beginning. Continue practicing the linking of multi-word connectors from Chapters 7 and 8, since por causa de, apesar de, and the new connectors in this chapter share a similar rhythmic pattern.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter develops argument connectors, concession with embora, comparison, and relative clauses. All Unit I and Chapter 6–8 systems remain consolidated and available.

Building an Argument

Use além disso to add a second reason, por um lado / por outro lado to present two sides of an issue, and portanto to introduce a conclusion drawn from what was just said.

  • Trabalhar em casa economiza tempo. Além disso, permite mais concentração. Working from home saves time. Besides, it allows more concentration. além disso: adds a second reason
  • Por um lado, o trabalho remoto oferece flexibilidade; por outro lado, pode isolar o funcionário da equipe. On one hand, remote work offers flexibility; on the other hand, it can isolate the employee from the team. por um lado / por outro lado: contrasting sides
  • A empresa economiza com escritório menor; portanto, muitos gestores preferem o modelo híbrido. The company saves on a smaller office; therefore, many managers prefer the hybrid model. portanto: introduces a conclusion

Conceding a Point with embora

Use embora followed by the present subjunctive to concede that something is true, while still maintaining your overall position. This functions like apesar de from Chapter 7, but embora introduces a full clause rather than a noun phrase.

  • Embora o trabalho remoto tenha vantagens, muitas pessoas sentem falta do contato direto com os colegas. Although remote work has advantages, many people miss direct contact with colleagues. embora + present subjunctive: concession clause
  • Embora o celular ajude na pesquisa escolar, ele também pode distrair o estudante. Although the phone helps with schoolwork, it can also distract the student. embora + present subjunctive: concession clause

Compare: apesar de + noun/infinitive (Chapter 7) versus embora + subjunctive clause (this chapter). Both introduce a concession, but embora requires a full clause with a conjugated verb.

Comparing Options

Use mais... do que and menos... do que for unequal comparison, and tão... quanto for equal comparison.

  • O ensino a distância pode ser tão eficaz quanto o ensino presencial, dependendo do curso. Distance learning can be as effective as in-person learning, depending on the course. tão + adjective + quanto: equal comparison
  • Para alguns alunos, estudar em grupo é mais produtivo do que estudar sozinho. For some students, studying in a group is more productive than studying alone. mais + adjective + do que: unequal comparison

Identifying with Relative Clauses

Use que to identify a thing or idea and quem (after a preposition) to identify a person, adding a clause that gives more precise information.

  • O argumento que a professora apresentou fez muitos alunos mudarem de opinião. The argument that the teacher presented made many students change their minds. que: relative clause identifying "o argumento"
  • A colega com quem discuti o tema tem um ponto de vista bem diferente do meu. The colleague with whom I discussed the topic has a very different point of view from mine. com quem: relative clause identifying a person
Building Block — Structuring a short argument
Acho que... Além disso,... Por um lado,... por outro lado,... Embora..., portanto...
a scaffold moving from a position, to a first reason, to two contrasting sides, to a concession, to a conclusion
Acho que o trabalho remoto traz mais vantagens do que desvantagens. Além disso, muitos funcionários relatam mais produtividade em casa. Por um lado, o modelo tradicional facilita a comunicação direta; por outro lado, ele exige tempo de deslocamento todos os dias. Embora o trabalho remoto possa isolar alguns funcionários, portanto, acho que as empresas deveriam oferecer essa opção sempre que possível.
I think remote work brings more advantages than disadvantages. Besides, many employees report more productivity at home. On one hand, the traditional model makes direct communication easier; on the other hand, it requires commuting time every day. Although remote work can isolate some employees, therefore, I think companies should offer this option whenever possible.

Model Dialogue

Context: Three coworkers discuss whether working from home is better than working in an office. Neutral spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Renata Acho que o trabalho remoto traz mais vantagens do que desvantagens. Além disso, economizo muito tempo sem o deslocamento. I think remote work brings more advantages than disadvantages. Besides, I save a lot of time without the commute.
Felipe Discordo um pouco. Por um lado, entendo a vantagem do tempo; por outro lado, sinto falta do contato direto com a equipe. I disagree a bit. On one hand, I understand the time advantage; on the other hand, I miss direct contact with the team.
Renata Desculpa, pode repetir a parte sobre o contato com a equipe? Sorry, can you repeat the part about contact with the team?
Felipe Claro. Embora o trabalho remoto ofereça flexibilidade, acho que a comunicação fica menos natural do que presencialmente. Sure. Although remote work offers flexibility, I think communication becomes less natural than in person.
Sofia O argumento que o Felipe apresentou faz sentido. Talvez um modelo híbrido seja tão eficaz quanto os dois extremos. The argument that Felipe presented makes sense. Maybe a hybrid model is as effective as either extreme.
Renata Portanto, talvez o ideal seja deixar cada equipe escolher o que funciona melhor. Therefore, maybe the ideal is to let each team choose what works best.

  2. What clarification does Renata ask for?
  3. What concession does Felipe make, and what conclusion does the group reach?

Writing Workshop

Write a short argumentative paragraph (10–14 sentences) with a position, two supporting reasons, a concession, a comparison, and a conclusion.

  1. Open with a clear position using acho que or na minha opinião.
  2. Add a first reason, then a second reason using além disso.
  3. Present both sides of the issue using por um lado / por outro lado.
  4. Concede a point using embora with the subjunctive.
  5. Include one comparison (mais... do que, tão... quanto) and one relative clause with que or quem.
  6. Close with a conclusion using portanto.

Revision checklist

  • I stated a clear position and supported it with at least two reasons.
  • I used at least one argument connector and one concession with embora + subjunctive.
  • I included one comparison and one relative clause with que or quem.
  • I recycled at least one opinion or evaluation frame from Chapter 8.
  • I preserved all diacritics, agreement, and punctuation conventions.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Disagreeing respectfully

In many everyday Brazilian conversations, disagreement is softened with expressions such as discordo um pouco rather than a blunt você está errado, and speakers often acknowledge part of the other person's point before adding their own view. Inviting a partner's reaction with a question such as o que você acha? is a common way of keeping a conversation collaborative rather than confrontational. These norms vary by setting, relationship, and individual style across Lusophone communities, so treat them as common tendencies rather than fixed rules, and adjust register when the conversation is more formal or professional.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using the indicative after embora

Error: Embora o trabalho remoto tem vantagens, prefiro o escritório. (indicative after embora)
Better: Embora o trabalho remoto tenha vantagens, prefiro o escritório.

Embora always requires the subjunctive in the clause it introduces, unlike some other concession expressions.

Common Mistake — Confusing apesar de and embora

Error: Apesar que o curso seja difícil, gosto muito. (mixing apesar de with a full clause)
Better: Embora o curso seja difícil, gosto muito. or Apesar de o curso ser difícil, gosto muito.

Apesar de (Chapter 7) is normally followed by a noun phrase or an infinitive construction, while embora (this chapter) is followed directly by a conjugated subjunctive clause.
