Projeto 1: Perfil e plano de ação

Unit I Assessment · Speak and Write Portuguese: Connected Discourse and Public Life

Chapter Overview

Chapter 5 is the Unit I assessment. It does not introduce new grammar, vocabulary domains, pronunciation targets, or orthographic burden. Instead, it asks you to combine everything activated in Chapters 1–4 — presenting your trajectory, describing a city and mobility choices, explaining work or study responsibilities, and summarizing public-health information and habits — into a single connected project: a personal profile followed by a practical action plan. The project is designed to show that you can sustain longer, connected Portuguese across several related topics rather than in isolated sentences.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Present a connected personal or professional profile drawing on Chapters 1–4 vocabulary and grammar.
  2. Describe a city or region and a mobility choice relevant to your profile.
  3. Explain a work, study, or project responsibility using subordination and object reference from Chapter 3.
  4. Summarize a health-related habit or recommendation using the impersonal se and formulaic subjunctive frames from Chapter 4.
  5. Organize a profile and action plan into a coherent, multi-paragraph spoken and written text.
  6. Ask for clarification, repetition, confirmation, or repair when communication breaks down.
  7. Maintain consistent Brazilian Portuguese production, with any variation clearly labeled.

Communication Scenario

A community program you are joining asks each participant to submit a short personal profile and a simple action plan for the next year: who you are, where you live and how you get around, what you are working on or studying, and one health or wellbeing habit you want to maintain or improve. You will present this profile and plan aloud to a small group, and then submit a written version.

  1. The productive target is a connected profile-and-plan text, spoken and written, drawing only on systems activated in Chapters 1–4.
  2. You may invent the details of your profile and plan, or adapt them from your own life.
  3. No new grammar, vocabulary domain, or pronunciation feature is introduced in this chapter.
  4. Any health-related content remains language practice only, not medical advice.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review — Nasal vowels and stress across Unit I vocabulary

Before recording or presenting your project, review the nasal diphthongs in words such as trajetória, alimentação, and recomendação, and the hiatus in saúde. No new pronunciation feature is introduced this chapter; the goal is consistent, intelligible production of sounds already practiced in Chapters 1–4, at a pace appropriate for a short spoken presentation.

Grammar Review

This chapter assesses Chapters 1–4 only. No new productive grammar is introduced; the sections below review the systems your project must integrate.

Reviewing Subordination and Object Reference (Chapter 3)

  • Estou trabalhando num projeto que exige bastante organização. I'm working on a project that requires a lot of organization. relative clause with que
  • Envio o relatório para a equipe assim que ele fica pronto. I send the report to the team as soon as it's ready. subordinate clause + object reference

Reviewing the Impersonal Se and Formulaic Subjunctive (Chapter 4)

  • Recomenda-se manter uma rotina de sono regular. It's recommended to keep a regular sleep routine. impersonal se + infinitive
  • É importante que eu beba mais água ao longo do dia. It's important that I drink more water during the day. é importante que + formulaic present subjunctive

Do not attempt to generate new subjunctive forms outside the fixed frames é importante que, é bom que, and recomendar que. The full productive subjunctive system remains scheduled for later chapters.

Building Block — Connecting the four project sections
Eu sou/trabalho/moro... Além disso,... Também é importante que eu... Recomenda-se...

Eu me chamo Camila e estou terminando meus estudos em administração. Moro num bairro tranquilo e vou para o trabalho de bicicleta na maioria dos dias. No momento, trabalho num projeto que exige bastante organização e comunicação com a equipe. Além disso, é importante que eu durma mais e me exercite com mais regularidade; recomenda-se pelo menos sete horas de sono por noite, e eu quero seguir essa recomendação neste próximo ano.
My name is Camila and I'm finishing my studies in administration. I live in a quiet neighborhood and go to work by bike most days. Right now, I'm working on a project that requires a lot of organization and communication with the team. Besides that, it's important that I sleep more and exercise more regularly; at least seven hours of sleep a night is recommended, and I want to follow that recommendation this coming year.

Model Dialogue

Context: Two participants in the community program practice their profile-and-plan presentations with each other before submitting the project. Semi-formal spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Rafael Posso praticar meu perfil com você antes de gravar? Can I practice my profile with you before recording it?
Camila Claro! Pode começar. Sure! Go ahead.
Rafael Eu me chamo Rafael, moro numa cidade pequena e vou para o trabalho de ônibus. Trabalho num projeto que envolve muitas reuniões com a equipe, e às vezes fico até tarde para terminar tudo. My name is Rafael, I live in a small city, and I go to work by bus. I work on a project that involves many meetings with the team, and I sometimes stay late to finish everything.
Camila Desculpa, você disse que fica até tarde todo dia, ou só às vezes? Sorry, did you say you stay late every day, or just sometimes?
Rafael Só às vezes, mas por isso quero mudar um hábito este ano. Only sometimes, but that's why I want to change a habit this year.
Camila E qual é o hábito? And what's the habit?
Rafael É importante que eu durma mais cedo. Recomenda-se sete horas de sono, e eu geralmente durmo menos. It's important that I sleep earlier. Seven hours of sleep is recommended, and I usually sleep less.
Camila Ficou bem claro. Acho que seu perfil está pronto. That was very clear. I think your profile is ready.
  1. What does Rafael say about his city and how he gets to work?
  2. What clarification does Camila ask for, and how does Rafael respond?
  3. What habit does Rafael want to change, and what recommendation does he mention?

Writing Workshop

Write a connected profile and action plan (10–14 sentences) that integrates Chapters 1–4.

  1. Introduce yourself and your current trajectory (Chapter 1).
  2. Describe where you live and a mobility choice (Chapter 2).
  3. Explain a work, study, or project responsibility using at least one subordinate clause (Chapter 3).
  4. State one health or wellbeing habit using the impersonal se and one formulaic subjunctive frame (Chapter 4).
  5. Close with a brief statement about your goals for the coming year.

Revision checklist

  • I used at least one relative clause and one instance of object reference from Chapter 3.
  • I used the impersonal se and one formulaic subjunctive frame from Chapter 4.

  • I preserved all diacritics, agreement, and punctuation conventions.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Personal profiles in community and workplace settings

Community programs, workplaces, and schools across Brazil and other Lusophone contexts often ask participants to introduce themselves through a short profile that combines personal background, daily routine, and a practical goal, whether spoken at an orientation meeting or written for an application. The level of detail, formality, and the specific goals mentioned can vary widely by institution, region, and audience, and a profile written for a job application will typically be more formal than one shared informally with new acquaintances in a community group. This chapter's profile-and-plan task reflects that everyday genre as language practice, not as a template for any single Lusophone country or institution.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Treating the four sections as unconnected sentences

Error: Eu me chamo Ana. Moro em São Paulo. Trabalho num projeto. Durmo pouco. (four disconnected statements)
Better: Eu me chamo Ana e moro em São Paulo, onde trabalho num projeto que exige bastante atenção; por isso, é importante que eu durma mais.

The Unit I project assesses connected discourse, not a list of isolated facts. Use connectors, relative clauses, and the Building Block pattern above to link your four sections into a single coherent text.

Common Mistake — Reaching for new grammar beyond Chapters 1–4

Error: attempting a freely generated present-subjunctive clause outside the fixed frames é importante que, é bom que, or recomendar que.
Better: stay within the formulaic frames already practiced, or use the impersonal se plus an infinitive instead.

This assessment evaluates control of systems already activated. Reaching for unscheduled grammar, even if you have encountered it elsewhere, will not earn additional credit and may introduce avoidable errors.
