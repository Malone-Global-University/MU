Saúde pública e qualidade de vida

Unit I, Chapter 4 · Speak and Write Portuguese: Connected Discourse and Public Life

Chapter Overview

Having explained your responsibilities and collaborations at work or school, you now turn to a different kind of public communication: summarizing health information and discussing everyday habits. This chapter activates the impersonal se for describing general practices and public recommendations, develops recommendation language built on verbs such as recomendar and é importante, and introduces formulaic present-subjunctive patterns tied to advice and recommendation. You will practice summarizing a piece of public health information and discussing your own habits and quality of life, always as language practice rather than as medical guidance.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe everyday habits related to sleep, food, exercise, and rest using vocabulary for wellbeing and public health.
  2. Use the impersonal se to describe general practices, campaigns, and public recommendations.
  3. Make recommendations using é importante que, é bom que, and similar formulaic frames with the present subjunctive.
  4. Summarize the main point of a short public-health notice or campaign message.
  5. Discuss your own habits and quality of life in general, non-clinical terms.
  6. Ask for clarification, repetition, confirmation, or repair when health-related information is unclear.
  7. Maintain consistent Brazilian Portuguese production while labeling any regional variation you introduce.

Communication Scenario

Your community center or workplace has posted a short public-health notice about sleep, hydration, and physical activity, and a friend who missed the announcement asks you to summarize it. You explain the main recommendations in your own words, then compare them with your own daily habits, mentioning what you already do well and what you might change.

  1. The productive target is a connected spoken summary of a public-health notice, followed by a short comparison with your own habits, entirely in Brazilian Portuguese.
  2. You may invent the details of the notice and your own habits, or adapt them from a real routine.
  3. The impersonal se, recommendation language, and formulaic present-subjunctive frames are the new productive tools activated this chapter.
  4. This chapter is a language exercise only. Do not treat any example, notice, or vocabulary item in this chapter as medical advice.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Listening tolerance across Lusophone speakers in public-information speech

This chapter continues the Volume III listening-tolerance target with words common to public-health announcements, such as alimentação, recomendação, and saúde. Notice the nasal diphthong pattern in alimentação and recomendação, which behaves like the nasal endings already familiar from informação and reunião, and the hiatus between a-ú in saúde, marked by the acute accent that breaks what would otherwise be a diphthong. Both careful and increasingly natural speakers may compress this hiatus slightly in fast speech; both realizations are intelligible in the declared Brazilian production model.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter activates the impersonal se for general practices and public recommendations, develops recommendation language, and introduces formulaic present-subjunctive frames tied to advice.

The Impersonal Se

  • Recomenda-se dormir pelo menos sete horas por noite. It is recommended to sleep at least seven hours a night. impersonal se + recommend-type verb
  • Não se deve pular refeições com frequência. One shouldn't skip meals frequently. impersonal se + dever + infinitive
  • Bebe-se água ao longo do dia, e não só quando se sente sede. One drinks water throughout the day, not only when one feels thirsty. impersonal se in coordinated clauses

The impersonal se is productive this chapter for describing general practices, public notices, and campaign language. It is distinct from the reflexive se already seen in verbs such as se exercitar and se hidratar, though the two frequently appear side by side in the same notice.

Recommendation Language

  • É importante beber bastante água durante o dia. It's important to drink plenty of water during the day. é importante + infinitive (general recommendation)
  • Os médicos recomendam que a gente durma pelo menos sete horas. Doctors recommend that people sleep at least seven hours. recomendar que + present subjunctive (formulaic)
  • É bom que a gente reduza o estresse quando possível. It's good for people to reduce stress when possible. é bom que + present subjunctive (formulaic)

The subjunctive forms after recomendar que, é importante que, and é bom que are introduced this chapter only as formulaic patterns tied to these specific frames. The full productive present subjunctive system is not activated until later in this volume; do not attempt to generate new subjunctive forms outside these fixed frames yet.

Discussing Habits

  • Eu tento dormir cedo, mas às vezes fico até tarde trabalhando. I try to sleep early, but sometimes I stay up late working. personal habit statement + concession with mas
  • Eu já bebo bastante água, mas preciso me exercitar mais. I already drink plenty of water, but I need to exercise more. contrast between an existing habit and a habit to change

Model Dialogue

Context: A friend who missed a community-center health notice asks for a summary. Semi-formal spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese, everyday setting.
Marcos Oi, Paula! Vi um cartaz lá no centro comunitário, mas não li tudo. Do que ele falava? Hi, Paula! I saw a poster at the community center, but I didn't read it all. What was it about?
Paula Era uma campanha sobre qualidade de vida. Recomenda-se dormir pelo menos sete horas por noite e beber água ao longo do dia. It was a campaign about quality of life. They recommend sleeping at least seven hours a night and drinking water throughout the day.
Marcos Desculpa, você disse sete horas ou oito horas de sono? Sorry, did you say seven hours or eight hours of sleep?
Paula Sete, pelo menos. E também é importante que a gente reduza o estresse e se exercite algumas vezes por semana. Seven, at least. And it's also important for people to reduce stress and exercise a few times a week.
Marcos Eu já bebo bastante água, mas durmo muito pouco, porque trabalho até tarde. I already drink plenty of water, but I sleep very little, because I work until late.
Paula Entendo. Eu também preciso me exercitar mais; ando muito parada ultimamente. I understand. I also need to exercise more; I've been very sedentary lately.
Marcos Você pode me mandar uma foto do cartaz, para eu ler o resto com calma? Could you send me a photo of the poster, so I can read the rest calmly?
Paula Mando sim, ainda hoje. Yes, I will, still today.
  1. What two recommendations from the poster does Paula mention first?
  2. What clarification does Marcos ask for, and how does Paula respond?
  3. What habit does Marcos say he already has, and what habit does he say he lacks?

Cuidar da saúde não precisa ser complicado. Writing Workshop

Write a short text (8–10 sentences) summarizing a public-health notice (real or invented) and comparing it with your own habits.

  1. State what kind of notice or campaign you are summarizing.
  2. Summarize two or three recommendations using the impersonal se.
  3. Add at least one recommendation using é importante que or é bom que.
  4. Compare the recommendations with two of your own habits, noting one you already follow and one you would like to change.
  5. Close with a brief general statement about quality of life.

Revision checklist

  • I used at least two instances of the impersonal se.
  • I used at least one formulaic subjunctive frame correctly.
  • I compared the recommendations with my own habits.
  • I avoided giving or implying any medical advice beyond language practice.
  • I preserved all diacritics, agreement, and punctuation conventions.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Public health messaging in Brazilian Portuguese

Public-health notices and campaigns in Brazil, whether from a município's health department, a community center, or a school, commonly rely on the impersonal se and formulaic recommendation phrases such as recomenda-se and é importante que, since these forms describe a general practice without addressing any one reader directly. The same strategies appear in campaign posters, pamphlets, and short social-media messages from public institutions. Tone and level of formality can vary by institution, region, and audience, and a campaign aimed at children or teenagers may use simpler or more direct language than one aimed at adults. Do not assume that every Portuguese-speaking country or institution phrases public-health messages identically, and remember that this chapter's language is for communicative practice only, not medical guidance.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Omitting the impersonal se

Error: Recomenda dormir sete horas por noite. (missing se)
Better: Recomenda-se dormir sete horas por noite.

When a recommendation has no specific stated subject (no "doctors," no "the poster," no named agent), Brazilian Portuguese typically marks the impersonal meaning with se attached to the verb. Check whether your sentence expresses a general practice without a clear personal subject before dropping se.

Common Mistake — Using the indicative after é importante que

Error: É importante que a gente bebe bastante água. (indicative instead of subjunctive)
Better: É importante que a gente beba bastante água.

The frames é importante que and é bom que require a present-subjunctive form in the following clause. At this stage, treat this as a fixed formula: memorize the pattern and the specific verb forms it requires, rather than trying to derive the full subjunctive system from it.
