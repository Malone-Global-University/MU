Trabalho, estudo e projetos

Unit I, Chapter 3 · Speak and Write Portuguese: Connected Discourse and Public Life

Chapter Overview

Having described your trajectory and the places connected to it, you will now explain what you actually do at work, in school, or on a project: your responsibilities, the people you collaborate with, and the deadlines you manage. This chapter activates subordinate clauses for independent production, expands object reference beyond full nouns, and introduces the formal email frame as a productive written pattern in Brazilian Portuguese. You will practice explaining responsibilities and collaboration both in conversation and in a written message appropriate for a workplace or academic setting.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe responsibilities, tasks, and ongoing projects using vocabulary for work, study, and collaboration.
  2. Build subordinate clauses with connectors such as que, porque, quando, and para que to explain reasons, timing, and purpose.
  3. Refer to people and things already mentioned using object pronouns instead of repeating full nouns.
  4. Open and close a formal email appropriately for a workplace or academic reader.
  5. Explain collaboration with colleagues or classmates and describe how a project is organized.
  6. Ask for clarification, repetition, confirmation, or repair when a task or deadline is unclear.
  7. Maintain consistent Brazilian Portuguese production while labeling any regional or workplace-specific variation you introduce.

Communication Scenario

A new colleague or classmate has just joined your team and wants to understand how the current project is organized: who is responsible for what, when the next deadline falls, and how the group communicates. You explain your own role and the roles of two or three collaborators, then follow up with a short formal email confirming the details and the next deadline.

  1. The productive target is a connected spoken explanation of responsibilities and collaboration, followed by a short formal email, entirely in Brazilian Portuguese.
  2. You may invent every detail of the project, the team, and the deadlines, or adapt them from a real experience.
  3. Subordinate clauses, object pronouns referring to people or things already mentioned, and the formal email frame are the new productive tools activated this chapter.
  4. Any workplace-specific or regional variation you choose to mention must remain clearly labeled and must not be produced as an unmarked mix with Brazilian Portuguese.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Listening tolerance across Lusophone speakers in workplace speech

This chapter continues the Volume III listening-tolerance target by asking you to follow careful, increasingly natural speech in words common to workplace and academic contexts, such as reunião, responsabilidade, and cronograma. Pay attention to the nasal diphthong in reunião, which behaves like the nasal pattern in informação from earlier chapters, and to the consonant cluster in cronograma, which some speakers realize with a very brief vowel-like glide between g and r. Both realizations are intelligible in the declared Brazilian production model and should be recognized as normal speaker variation rather than corrected as an error.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter activates subordinate clauses of reason, time, and purpose for independent production, develops object pronouns as a productive alternative to repeating full nouns, and introduces the formal email frame as a productive written pattern.

Subordination: Reason, Time, and Purpose

  • Vou adiar a reunião porque ainda não terminei o relatório. I'm going to postpone the meeting because I haven't finished the report yet. porque + reason clause
  • Quando eu entregar a minha parte, você pode revisar o texto inteiro. When I submit my part, you can review the whole text. quando + future-oriented subordinate clause
  • Mandei o cronograma para que todo mundo soubesse o prazo. I sent the schedule so that everyone would know the deadline. para que + purpose clause (formulaic at this stage)

The clauses with porque and quando are productive this chapter. Para que is presented only as a formulaic pattern for now; its full subjunctive system is not activated until later in this volume.

Object Pronouns for People and Things Already Mentioned

  • Já mandei o relatório? Já, eu já o mandei ontem. Did I already send the report? Yes, I already sent it yesterday. direct object clitic o (formal written register)
  • Você conhece a Fernanda? Conheço, sim, trabalho com ela todos os dias. Do you know Fernanda? Yes, I do, I work with her every day. strong pronoun ela after preposition com
  • O gerente me pediu um relatório atualizado. The manager asked me for an updated report. indirect object clitic me

In everyday spoken Brazilian Portuguese, a noun object is often repeated or simply omitted when recoverable from context, rather than replaced by a formal clitic like o/a. This chapter activates common spoken patterns (me, te, strong pronouns after prepositions) for production, while formal written clitics such as o and a remain limited to the email frame introduced below.

The Formal Email Frame

  • Prezado Sr. Almeida, / Prezada Sra. Costa, Dear Mr. Almeida, / Dear Ms. Costa, formal opening with title and last name
  • Escrevo para confirmar o prazo do projeto. I am writing to confirm the project deadline. formulaic opening line stating purpose
  • Fico à disposição para qualquer dúvida. Atenciosamente, I remain available for any questions. Sincerely, formulaic closing pair
Building Block — Explaining responsibilities and collaboration
Eu sou responsável por... Trabalho com [colega], que... Quando eu..., ele/ela... O prazo é..., porque...
a scaffold combining a responsibility statement, a relative clause introducing a collaborator, a time clause, and a reason clause
Eu sou responsável pela parte financeira do projeto. Trabalho com o Rafael, que cuida da parte técnica. Quando eu termino a minha parte, ele revisa tudo antes de entregarmos ao gerente. O prazo é sexta-feira, porque o cliente quer os resultados antes do fim do mês.
I'm responsible for the financial part of the project. I work with Rafael, who handles the technical part. When I finish my part, he reviews everything before we submit it to the manager. The deadline is Friday, because the client wants the results before the end of the month.

Model Dialogue

Context: A new team member asks a colleague about the current project. Semi-formal spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese, workplace setting.
Juliana Oi, Tiago! Você pode me explicar como o projeto está organizado? Hi, Tiago! Can you explain to me how the project is organized?
Tiago Claro! Eu sou responsável pelo cronograma e pela comunicação com o cliente. Trabalho com a Renata, que cuida da parte técnica. Sure! I'm responsible for the schedule and communication with the client. I work with Renata, who handles the technical part.
Juliana Desculpa, você disse que a Renata cuida da parte técnica ou da parte financeira? Sorry, did you say Renata handles the technical part or the financial part?
Tiago Da parte técnica. A parte financeira é com o Marcelo. The technical part. The financial part is with Marcelo.
Juliana Entendi. E qual é o prazo da próxima entrega? Got it. And what's the deadline for the next deliverable?
Tiago É sexta-feira, porque o cliente quer os resultados antes do fim do mês. Quando a Renata terminar os testes, eu envio tudo para você revisar. It's Friday, because the client wants the results before the end of the month. When Renata finishes the tests, I'll send everything for you to review.
Juliana Perfeito. Você pode me mandar um e-mail com esses detalhes, para eu ter tudo por escrito? Perfect. Could you send me an email with these details, so I have everything in writing?
Tiago Mando sim, ainda hoje. Yes, I will, still today.
  1. Who is responsible for the schedule and client communication, and who handles the technical part?
  2. What clarification does Juliana ask for, and how does Tiago respond?
  3. Why is the deadline on Friday, and what will happen when Renata finishes the tests?

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script — Explaining a team project
No nosso projeto atual, cada pessoa da equipe é responsável por uma parte diferente. Eu cuido da comunicação com o cliente, porque conheço bem o histórico do contrato. A Beatriz é encarregada do design, e o Felipe é responsável pela parte técnica. Quando alguém termina uma tarefa, avisa o grupo numa reunião rápida, para que todo mundo saiba o que já foi concluído. O prazo final é no fim do mês, mas tentamos entregar tudo alguns dias antes, para termos tempo de revisar com calma.
In our current project, each person on the team is responsible for a different part. I handle communication with the client, because I know the contract history well. Beatriz is in charge of design, and Felipe is responsible for the technical part. When someone finishes a task, they let the group know in a quick meeting, so that everyone knows what has already been completed. The final deadline is at the end of the month, but we try to deliver everything a few days early, so we have time to review calmly.
  1. What is each team member responsible for?
  2. What happens when someone finishes a task?
  3. Why does the team try to deliver a few days before the final deadline?

Reading Practice

Prezada Sra. Ribeiro, escrevo para confirmar o prazo do projeto que discutimos na reunião de segunda-feira. Fico responsável pela parte de pesquisa, que devo concluir até quinta-feira, para que o Bruno tenha tempo de revisar antes da entrega final na sexta-feira. Writing Workshop

Write a short formal email (8–10 sentences) to a supervisor or instructor confirming your responsibilities on a project, your collaborators, and the next deadline.

  1. Open with a formal greeting using a title and last name.
  2. State the purpose of the email using a formulaic opening line.
  3. Explain your responsibility and at least one collaborator's role, using a relative or subordinate clause.
  4. Give the deadline and a reason for it using porque or quando.
  5. Close with an appropriately formal closing line.

Revision checklist

  • I used at least one subordinate clause with porque and one with quando.
  • I used at least one object pronoun to refer to a person or task already mentioned.
  • I used the formal opening and closing frame correctly.
  • I maintained one consistent formal register from greeting to closing.
  • I preserved all diacritics, agreement, and punctuation conventions.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Workplace politeness across sectors

Workplace politeness in Brazil varies considerably by sector, company culture, region, and generation. In many technology and creative companies, colleagues commonly address one another informally with você and first names from the first meeting, while in law firms, government offices, and many traditional companies, a more formal register with titles and last names may be expected, especially in writing and with senior staff. Email conventions such as Prezado(a) and Atenciosamente remain common in formal written communication across sectors, even where spoken register is informal. Do not assume that one company's norms represent all Brazilian workplaces, and do not treat formal written Portuguese as evidence that spoken interaction in the same workplace is equally formal.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Dropping the object pronoun in formal writing

Error: O gerente pediu um relatório eu ontem. (missing indirect object pronoun)
Better: O gerente me pediu um relatório ontem.

In formal or written Brazilian Portuguese, the indirect object pronoun (me, te, lhe) is required to mark who received the action. Check whether your sentence names a receiver without a pronoun to introduce them.

Common Mistake — Wrong preposition with responsável

Error: Ele é responsável da parte técnica. (wrong preposition)
Better: Ele é responsável pela parte técnica.

Responsável takes the preposition por (contracted as pelo/pela), not de. Learn this verb-preposition frame as a fixed unit rather than translating from English "responsible for."
