Ler, resumir e responder

Read, Summarize, and Respond · Projeto 2 · Speak and Write Portuguese: Connected Discourse and Public Life

Chapter Overview

Chapter 10 is the Unit II assessment, Projeto 2: Ler, resumir e responder. Rather than introducing new grammar, this chapter consolidates everything Unit II has built: the opinion and evaluation frames of Chapters 6–8, and the argument connectors, concession, comparison, and relative clauses of Chapter 9. You will read a short public or media-style text, summarize its main ideas in your own words, and respond to it with a supported opinion of your own, in speaking and in writing. The chapter closes Unit II and prepares you for the freer, more independent communication of Unit III.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Read a short public or media-style text and identify its main idea and key supporting points.
  2. Summarize a text in your own words using vocabulary and structures active through Chapter 9.
  3. Respond to a text with your own opinion, using frames such as acho que and na minha opinião from Chapter 6.
  4. Support your response with at least two reasons, connected with além disso or por um lado / por outro lado.
  5. Concede a point raised by the text or by a different point of view using embora with the subjunctive.
  6. Compare two ideas, options, or positions from the text using comparative and equative structures.
  7. Identify a person, thing, or idea more precisely using a relative clause with que or quem.
  8. Combine reading, summarizing, and responding into one coherent spoken or written performance.
  9. Maintain consistent Brazilian Portuguese production throughout the assessment.

Communication Scenario

A community online forum publishes a short opinion piece on an everyday public topic, and readers respond with their own reactions. You will read the piece, summarize it for someone who has not seen it, and post your own response — agreeing in part, disagreeing in part, or offering a new angle — the way a thoughtful reader in this forum would.

  1. This chapter does not introduce new grammar or vocabulary; it consolidates and assesses systems from Chapters 6–9.
  2. The reading passage and forum topic are invented or adapted for instructional purposes; no real statistics or sources are implied.
  3. Your response should read or sound like one connected passage, not a checklist of grammar points.
  4. Systems from Unit I and earlier chapters remain available and may be recycled naturally.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Reading aloud with connected-speech rhythm

This chapter reinforces rather than introduces pronunciation targets. As you read the passage aloud and present your response, continue applying the contrastive stress on agreement and disagreement verbs from Chapter 9 (concordo, discordo), and the small pause before por outro lado that signals a contrasting idea. When reading a written passage aloud, group words into short, natural phrases rather than pausing after every word, and let your pitch fall at the end of a completed sentence.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces no new grammar. It consolidates the opinion, evaluation, and argument systems of Chapters 6–9 and shows how they combine into one connected performance: reading, summarizing, and responding.

From Reading to Summary

A summary restates the main idea and key points of a text in your own words, using shorter, simpler sentences than the original. Use reporting frames such as o texto diz que and segundo o autor / a autora to distance the summary from your own opinion, which comes afterward.

  • O texto diz que o trabalho remoto vem crescendo nos últimos anos. The text says that remote work has been growing in recent years. o texto diz que: reporting frame, summary voice
  • Segundo a autora, o modelo híbrido combina as vantagens dos dois lados. According to the author, the hybrid model combines the advantages of both sides. segundo + noun: attributes an idea to the source

From Summary to Response

Once the summary is complete, shift clearly into your own opinion using the Chapter 6 frames acho que or na minha opinião, support it with reasons using além disso and por um lado / por outro lado from Chapter 9, concede a point with embora + subjunctive, and compare ideas or conclude with portanto.

  • Na minha opinião, o texto tem razão em destacar a flexibilidade do trabalho remoto. In my opinion, the text is right to highlight the flexibility of remote work. na minha opinião: shift from summary to response
  • Embora o autor reconheça as desvantagens do isolamento, acho que ele subestima esse problema. Although the author acknowledges the disadvantages of isolation, I think he underestimates that problem. embora + subjunctive: concession to the text's own argument
Building Block — The read-summarize-respond scaffold
O texto diz que... Segundo o autor,... Na minha opinião,... Além disso,... Embora..., portanto...
a scaffold moving from reporting the text's content, to your own position, to a supporting reason, to a concession, to a conclusion
O texto diz que cada vez mais pessoas trabalham em casa. Segundo a autora, isso traz mais produtividade e menos tempo perdido no trânsito. Na minha opinião, o texto tem razão em destacar essas vantagens. Além disso, acho que o trabalho remoto também melhora o equilíbrio entre vida pessoal e profissional. Embora a autora reconheça que o contato direto com a equipe diminui, acho que um modelo híbrido resolve boa parte desse problema; portanto, concordo com a conclusão geral do texto.
The text says that more and more people work from home. According to the author, this brings more productivity and less time lost in traffic. In my opinion, the text is right to highlight these advantages. Besides, I think remote work also improves work-life balance. Although the author acknowledges that direct contact with the team decreases, I think a hybrid model solves much of that problem; therefore, I agree with the text's general conclusion.

Prática guiada — Guided practice: Complete each sentence with the reporting, opinion, or connector form indicated.

  1. ___ (segundo) o autor, o uso do celular na escola ___ (crescer, presente) todos os anos.

  3. ___ (embora) o texto ___ (apresentar, subjuntivo) bons argumentos, discordo da conclusão.

Model Dialogue

Context: Two classmates discuss a forum post they both just read, moving from summary to their own responses. Neutral spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Bianca Então, o texto diz que os aplicativos de estudo estão ajudando muito os alunos, né? So, the text says that study apps are helping students a lot, right?
Diego Isso. Segundo o autor, além disso, esses aplicativos também ensinam os alunos a organizar melhor o tempo. Right. According to the author, besides that, these apps also teach students to organize their time better.
Bianca Na minha opinião, o texto tem razão, mas embora os aplicativos ajudem, acho que eles também podem distrair. In my opinion, the text is right, but although the apps help, I think they can also be distracting.
Diego Desculpa, pode repetir? Não entendi bem essa parte sobre distração. Sorry, can you repeat that? I didn't quite understand that part about distraction.
Bianca Claro. Por um lado, o aplicativo organiza o estudo; por outro lado, o aluno também recebe muitas notificações. Sure. On one hand, the app organizes studying; on the other hand, the student also gets a lot of notifications.
Diego Faz sentido. Portanto, talvez o ideal seja usar o aplicativo com um horário bem definido. That makes sense. Therefore, maybe the ideal is to use the app with a well-defined schedule.
  1. What does the text say about study apps, according to Diego's summary?
  2. What clarification does Diego ask Bianca for?
  3. What concession does Bianca make, and what conclusion does Diego reach?

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script — A reader's response on the forum
O texto diz que ler notícias pelo celular está substituindo a leitura de jornais impressos. Segundo o autor, isso acontece porque as notícias digitais são mais rápidas e mais fáceis de compartilhar. Na minha opinião, o autor tem razão, mas embora as notícias digitais sejam mais práticas, acho que elas também podem ser menos confiáveis. Por um lado, qualquer pessoa pode publicar uma notícia online; por outro lado, os jornais impressos passam por mais revisão. Portanto, acho importante que o leitor verifique a fonte antes de acreditar em uma notícia.
The text says that reading news on the phone is replacing reading printed newspapers. According to the author, this happens because digital news is faster and easier to share. In my opinion, the author is right, but although digital news is more practical, I think it can also be less reliable. On one hand, anyone can publish news online; on the other hand, printed newspapers go through more review. Therefore, I think it's important that the reader check the source before believing a piece of news.
  1. What does the text say is replacing printed newspapers, according to the summary?
  2. What contrast does the speaker draw between digital and printed news?
  3. What conclusion does the speaker reach, and what does it recommend to the reader?

Reading Practice

As redes sociais e o debate público

Nos últimos anos, as redes sociais se tornaram um espaço importante para o debate de temas públicos. Cada vez mais pessoas expressam sua opinião sobre política, educação e tecnologia diretamente em suas contas pessoais, sem passar por jornais ou revistas tradicionais. Por um lado, isso dá voz a pessoas que antes não tinham espaço para se manifestar; por outro lado, muitas discussões acabam se tornando agressivas em vez de respeitosas. Além disso, é fácil encontrar informações imprecisas misturadas com opiniões bem fundamentadas, o que dificulta a tarefa do leitor. Embora as redes sociais tenham ampliado o acesso ao debate público, alguns especialistas duvidam que esse tipo de discussão substitua, com a mesma qualidade, o jornalismo tradicional. Portanto, muitos educadores defendem que os jovens aprendam, desde cedo, a avaliar criticamente o que leem online.

  1. What does the text say social media has done for public debate?
  2. What contrast does the text draw between giving people a voice and the quality of discussion?
  3. What concession and conclusion does the text reach about traditional journalism and young readers?

Writing Workshop

Write a short read-summarize-respond passage (12–16 sentences) based on the Reading Practice passage above, or on another debatable public or media topic approved by your instructor.

  1. Open with a one- or two-sentence summary using o texto diz que or segundo o autor / a autora.
  2. Add one more summary sentence covering a second main point of the text.
  3. Shift clearly into your own opinion using na minha opinião or acho que.
  4. Support your opinion with two reasons, connected with além disso and por um lado / por outro lado.
  5. Concede a point from the text or from an opposing view using embora with the subjunctive.
  6. Include one comparison and one relative clause with que or quem.
  7. Close with a conclusion using portanto.

Revision checklist

  • I clearly separated the summary of the text from my own opinion.
  • I supported my opinion with at least two connected reasons.
  • I used at least one concession with embora + subjunctive.
  • I included one comparison and one relative clause with que or quem.
  • I preserved all diacritics, agreement, and punctuation conventions.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Responding to public texts online

In many Brazilian online forums and comment sections, a thoughtful response often begins by briefly acknowledging what the original text said before disagreeing or adding a new point, much like the softened disagreement patterns from Chapter 9. Readers commonly use expressions such as concordo em parte ("I partly agree") to signal a nuanced position rather than a flat agreement or rejection. As with spoken disagreement, these norms vary by platform, community, and individual style across Lusophone contexts, so treat them as common tendencies and adjust register for more formal academic or professional writing contexts.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Blending summary and opinion without a clear shift

Error: O texto diz que as redes sociais são ótimas e concordo totalmente. (summary and opinion collapsed into one unmarked sentence)
Better: O texto diz que as redes sociais ampliaram o debate público. Na minha opinião, essa mudança é positiva.

Keep the summary voice (o texto diz que, segundo o autor) separate from your own opinion voice (acho que, na minha opinião), so the reader or listener can tell which ideas belong to the text and which belong to you.

Common Mistake — Forgetting the subjunctive after embora in a response

Error: Embora o texto apresenta bons argumentos, discordo da conclusão. (indicative after embora)
Better: Embora o texto apresente bons argumentos, discordo da conclusão.

As established in Chapter 9, embora always requires the subjunctive, even when it is conceding a point made by the text you are summarizing rather than by a person in conversation.
