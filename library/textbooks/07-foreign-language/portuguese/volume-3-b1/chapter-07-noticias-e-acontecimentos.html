Notícias e acontecimentos

News and Events · Speak and Write Portuguese: Connected Discourse and Public Life

Chapter Overview

Chapter 7 builds on the narrative control developed in Chapter 6 and turns it toward a new communicative purpose: summarizing news and discussing events with other people. You will learn to connect a series of events with cause-and-concession connectors, talk about what is likely to happen or would happen using future and conditional forms, and use the subjunctive to express doubt or evaluation about a situation. Rather than simply retelling a personal story, you will practice organizing information the way a person does when explaining what happened, why it happened, and what it might mean.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Summarize the main facts of a news item or event in a short connected passage.
  2. Connect causes, effects, and concessions using expressions such as por causa de, já que, and apesar de.
  3. Talk about likely or predicted developments using the future with vai + infinitivo and simple conditional forms such as seria and poderia.
  4. Use the subjunctive after expressions of doubt or evaluation, such as é provável que and talvez, as controlled formulaic frames.
  5. Organize a short passage using a topic sentence, supporting facts, and a connector-based conclusion.
  6. Ask for clarification, repetition, confirmation, or repair when communication breaks down.
  7. Maintain consistent Brazilian Portuguese production, with any variation clearly labeled.

Communication Scenario

Two coworkers catch up before a meeting, discussing something they each read or heard about recently — a local story, a change at work, or an event in the news. Each explains what happened, what caused it, and what might happen next.

  1. The productive target is a short spoken or written summary of an event with cause, concession, and a prediction or evaluation.
  2. News items may be invented or adapted; specific real-world claims should not be asserted as verified fact.
  3. Systems from Chapters 1–6 remain available and should be recycled where natural.
  4. Reported-speech constructions (for example, quoting exactly what someone said) are not required or expected in this chapter.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Linking and stress in connector phrases

Multi-word connectors such as por causa de and apesar de are usually pronounced as a single rhythmic unit in connected Brazilian speech, with the unstressed vowels of de reduced and linked smoothly to the following word: por causa disso, apesar do problema. Practice saying these connectors quickly and smoothly rather than pausing between each word, since a full pause before de or disso can make the phrase sound unnatural. Also continue practicing the preterite/imperfect stress contrast from Chapter 6, since narrating background events remains useful when summarizing news.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter develops cause and concession connectors, future and conditional meanings, and a controlled subjunctive frame for doubt and evaluation. All Unit I and Chapter 6 systems remain consolidated and available.

Cause and Concession

Use por causa de to introduce a cause and apesar de to introduce a concession — something true despite which the main statement still holds.

  • O trânsito aumentou por causa da obra na avenida principal. Traffic increased because of the construction on the main avenue. por causa de + noun: cause
  • Apesar do aviso, muitas pessoas não mudaram de rota. Despite the warning, many people didn't change their route. apesar de + noun: concession
  • A prefeitura anunciou o projeto, já que o problema já durava meses. The city government announced the project, since the problem had already lasted months. já que + clause: cause (review)

Talking About What Will or Would Happen

Use vai + infinitivo for a confident prediction and the simple conditional (seria, poderia, mudaria) for a softer or more hypothetical prediction.

  • Segundo a notícia, a obra vai terminar em dois meses. According to the news, the construction is going to finish in two months. confident future prediction
  • Isso poderia resolver o problema do trânsito na região. That could solve the traffic problem in the area. conditional: softer, more hypothetical prediction

Doubt and Evaluation with the Subjunctive

Use fixed frames such as é provável que and talvez followed by the present subjunctive to express doubt or evaluation about an event. Treat these as controlled formulaic patterns rather than a full subjunctive paradigm.

  • É provável que o projeto comece no mês que vem. It's likely that the project will start next month. é provável que + present subjunctive
  • Talvez a situação melhore depois da mudança. Maybe the situation will improve after the change. talvez + present subjunctive

This chapter does not require producing reported speech (quoting exactly what someone said); focus instead on summarizing facts, causes, and predictions in your own words.

Building Block — Summarizing a news item
Segundo a notícia,... por causa de.../já que... Apesar de..., é provável que...
a scaffold moving from the main fact, to its cause, to a concession, to a prediction or evaluation
Segundo a notícia, o número de ônibus na cidade vai aumentar no próximo ano. A prefeitura decidiu investir mais no transporte público por causa das reclamações constantes dos moradores. Apesar do investimento, alguns especialistas duvidam que o problema do trânsito desapareça completamente. Ainda assim, é provável que a mudança melhore a vida de muitos passageiros.
According to the news, the number of buses in the city is going to increase next year. The city government decided to invest more in public transportation because of residents' constant complaints. Despite the investment, some experts doubt that the traffic problem will disappear completely. Even so, it's likely that the change will improve the lives of many passengers.

Prática guiada — Guided practice: Complete each sentence with the connector or verb form indicated.

  1. O evento foi cancelado ___ (por causa de) da chuva forte.
  2. ___ (apesar de) o aviso, muita gente não ouviu a notícia.
  3. É provável que a situação ___ (melhorar, subjuntivo) logo.

Model Dialogue

Context: Two coworkers talk briefly before a meeting about a local news story. Neutral spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Bruno Você viu a notícia sobre o novo horário dos ônibus? Did you see the news about the new bus schedule?
Larissa Vi sim. Segundo a notícia, vai ter mais ônibus por causa das reclamações dos moradores. Yes, I saw it. According to the news, there's going to be more buses because of residents' complaints.
Bruno Desculpa, você pode repetir? Não entendi a parte sobre o motivo da mudança. Sorry, can you repeat that? I didn't understand the part about the reason for the change.
Larissa Claro. A prefeitura decidiu aumentar o número de ônibus já que muita gente reclamava do transporte. Sure. The city government decided to increase the number of buses since a lot of people were complaining about the transportation.
Bruno Apesar da mudança, acho que o problema não vai acabar de vez. Despite the change, I think the problem isn't going to end for good.
Larissa Talvez você tenha razão, mas é provável que melhore um pouco pelo menos. Maybe you're right, but it's likely to improve a little at least.
  1. What news item does Larissa mention, and what caused the change?
  2. What clarification does Bruno ask for?
  3. What doubt does Bruno express, and how does Larissa respond?

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script — A short news update
Segundo a notícia divulgada hoje, o parque da cidade vai passar por uma reforma completa. A prefeitura decidiu investir no espaço por causa do grande número de visitantes nos últimos anos. Apesar do investimento, alguns moradores estão preocupados com o barulho das obras. Ainda assim, é provável que o parque fique pronto antes do verão.
According to news released today, the city park is going to undergo a complete renovation. The city government decided to invest in the space because of the large number of visitors in recent years. Despite the investment, some residents are worried about the noise from the construction. Even so, it's likely that the park will be ready before summer.
  1. What is going to happen to the park, according to the report?
  2. What caused the city government's decision?
  3. What concession is mentioned, and what is the prediction at the end?

Reading Practice

Uma mudança no bairro

Segundo uma notícia publicada esta semana, a rua principal do bairro vai passar por uma grande reforma. Os moradores pediram a mudança por causa do aumento constante de acidentes na região. Apesar da demora da prefeitura em responder aos pedidos, o projeto finalmente foi aprovado no mês passado. Alguns comerciantes estão preocupados com o barulho e o movimento das obras, já que o período de reforma pode durar vários meses. Mesmo assim, é provável que a rua fique mais segura depois das mudanças, e muitos moradores já demonstram otimismo com o projeto.

  1. What change is described, and what caused residents to request it?
  2. What concession does the passage mention regarding the city government?
  3. What worry do some shop owners have, and what is the overall prediction at the end?

Writing Workshop

Write a short news summary (10–14 sentences) with a main fact, a cause, a concession, and a prediction or evaluation.

  1. Open with the main fact, using an expression such as segundo a notícia or uma notícia recente informou que.
  2. Explain the cause using por causa de or já que.
  3. Add a concession using apesar de.
  4. Close with a prediction using vai + infinitivo or the conditional, or an evaluation using é provável que or talvez.
  5. Keep the topic invented or clearly adapted rather than asserting unverified real-world facts.

Revision checklist

  • I included at least one cause connector and one concession connector.
  • I used a future or conditional form for a prediction.
  • I used the subjunctive correctly within a fixed frame such as é provável que or talvez.
  • I did not attempt to produce reported-speech quotations, since that system is not yet active.
  • I preserved all diacritics, agreement, and punctuation conventions.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Discussing news and public information

Discussing recent news or local happenings is a common way of making everyday conversation across Brazil and other Lusophone communities, whether at work, among neighbors, or with family. Listeners often show engagement by asking about the cause of an event before offering their own prediction or opinion, which is part of why this chapter connects cause, concession, and evaluation together. When discussing real-world events outside the classroom, remember that claims should be checked against a current, reliable source rather than repeated as fact; the invented and adapted items in this chapter are for language practice only.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing por causa de and apesar de

Error: Apesar da chuva forte, o jogo foi adiado. (used as if it were a cause)
Better: O jogo foi adiado por causa da chuva forte. or Apesar da chuva forte, o jogo não foi adiado.

Use por causa de when one thing directly explains another, and reserve apesar de for something true despite which the main statement still holds.

Common Mistake — Using the indicative after é provável que

Error: É provável que a situação melhora logo. (indicative after a doubt frame)
Better: É provável que a situação melhore logo.

Fixed frames of doubt or evaluation such as é provável que require the subjunctive in the following clause, even though the overall meaning is not negative.
