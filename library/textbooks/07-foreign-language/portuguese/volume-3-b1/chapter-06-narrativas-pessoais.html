Narrativas pessoais

Personal Narratives · Speak and Write Portuguese: Connected Discourse and Public Life

Chapter Overview

Chapter 6 opens Unit II by moving from the connected present-time profile of Chapter 5 into connected past-time storytelling. You will learn to tell a coherent personal narrative that establishes background, moves through a sequence of events, and highlights a turning point — the kind of story a person tells about a trip, a decision, a childhood memory, or an unexpected event. The chapter develops the preterite/imperfect contrast beyond isolated sentences into full narrative control, and introduces the pluperfect as a recognition-only form for describing something that had already happened before another past event.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Distinguish completed events (preterite) from background description and habitual past (imperfect) across a connected narrative.
  2. Recognize the pluperfect (tinha + particípio) when it appears in a story, without being required to produce it independently.
  3. Establish narrative background: time, place, weather, age, and ongoing circumstances.
  4. Sequence a series of completed past events using time connectors.
  5. Mark a turning point or unexpected event within a personal narrative.
  6. Ask for clarification, repetition, confirmation, or repair when communication breaks down.
  7. Maintain consistent Brazilian Portuguese production, with any variation clearly labeled.

Communication Scenario

At a small gathering of the same community program from Chapter 5, participants take turns telling a short personal story: a memorable trip, a childhood memory, or a moment that changed their plans. Listeners ask follow-up questions to find out what the situation was like beforehand and what happened next.

  1. The productive target is a connected past narrative with background, a sequence of events, and a turning point.
  2. You may invent your story or adapt it from your own life; narratives may be fictionalized.
  3. The pluperfect appears only for recognition in this chapter; you are not expected to produce it independently.
  4. Systems from Chapters 1–5 remain available and should be recycled where natural.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Stress in preterite and imperfect verb endings

In the preterite, first-person singular -ei and third-person singular -ou endings for -ar verbs carry the stress: morei, morou. Contrast this with the imperfect, where stress falls earlier and endings such as -ava and -ia are unstressed on the final vowel: morava, tinha. Practice pairs such as morou / morava and decidiu / decidia aloud, noticing that the preterite ending is sharper and the imperfect ending trails off. This distinction is often the clearest spoken signal of which past tense a speaker intends.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter develops the preterite/imperfect contrast for full narrative use and introduces the pluperfect as a recognition-only form. All other Unit I systems remain consolidated and available.

Preterite and Imperfect in Narrative

Use the imperfect to set the scene: what things were like, what was happening, or what used to happen. Use the preterite for the specific events that moved the story forward.

  • Naquela época, eu morava numa cidade pequena e trabalhava num escritório perto de casa. Back then, I lived in a small city and worked at an office near home. imperfect: background
  • Um dia, recebi uma ligação inesperada. One day, I got an unexpected call. preterite: single completed event
  • Eu estava tomando café quando o telefone tocou. I was drinking coffee when the phone rang. imperfect (ongoing) + preterite (interrupting event)

Marking the Turning Point

  • De repente, percebi que minha vida ia mudar completamente. Suddenly, I realized my life was going to change completely. de repente + preterite marking the turning point
  • Foi então que decidi aceitar o novo emprego. That's when I decided to accept the new job. foi então que + preterite

Recognizing the Pluperfect

The pluperfect (tinha + past participle) describes something that had already happened before another past event. You only need to recognize it in this chapter; you are not expected to produce it independently.

  • Quando cheguei, a reunião já tinha começado. When I arrived, the meeting had already started. pluperfect (recognition only)

Do not attempt to generate new pluperfect forms in your own speaking or writing this chapter; treat já tinha + particípio as a recognizable pattern in what you read and hear.

Building Block — Structuring a short narrative
Naquela época, eu... Um dia,... De repente,... Foi então que...
a scaffold moving from background (imperfect) to a sequence of events (preterite) to a turning point
Naquela época, eu morava com meus pais e trabalhava meio período numa loja. Um dia, uma amiga me contou sobre uma vaga numa empresa de tecnologia. No começo, eu não tinha muito interesse, mas fui à entrevista mesmo assim. De repente, percebi que aquele trabalho combinava perfeitamente comigo. Foi então que decidi aceitar a proposta, e minha vida mudou bastante depois disso.
Back then, I lived with my parents and worked part-time at a store. One day, a friend told me about an opening at a technology company. At first, I wasn't very interested, but I went to the interview anyway. Suddenly, I realized that job suited me perfectly. That's when I decided to accept the offer, and my life changed a lot after that.

Prática guiada — Guided practice: Choose preterite or imperfect for each blank.

  1. Naquela época, eu ___ (morar) numa cidade grande.
  2. Um dia, eu ___ (receber) uma notícia inesperada.
  3. Eu ___ (estar) trabalhando quando o telefone ___ (tocar).

Model Dialogue

Context: Two participants share short personal stories at the community gathering. Informal spoken register, Brazilian Portuguese.
Camila Conta uma história para mim. Alguma coisa que mudou seus planos. Tell me a story. Something that changed your plans.
Rafael Naquela época, eu morava numa cidade pequena e planejava ficar lá para sempre. Um dia, recebi uma proposta de trabalho em outra cidade. Back then, I lived in a small city and planned to stay there forever. One day, I got a job offer in another city.
Camila Desculpa, você disse que já tinha decidido ficar, certo? O que aconteceu depois? Sorry, you said you had already decided to stay, right? What happened next?
Rafael Isso mesmo. Mas de repente percebi que essa era uma oportunidade única. Foi então que mudei de ideia. That's right. But suddenly I realized this was a unique opportunity. That's when I changed my mind.
Camila Que interessante! E você se arrependeu? How interesting! And did you regret it?
Rafael Não, nunca. Foi a melhor decisão que já tomei. No, never. It was the best decision I've ever made.
  1. What was Rafael's original plan, and what changed it?
  2. What clarification does Camila ask for, and what pluperfect form does she use?
  3. How does Rafael describe the moment he changed his mind?

Listening script — A childhood memory
Quando eu era criança, minha família morava perto de uma praia pequena. Todo verão, íamos lá quase todos os dias. Um ano, porém, uma tempestade forte destruiu parte da praia, e nós não pudemos voltar por muitos meses. De repente, percebi que aquele lugar não era permanente como eu pensava. When I was a child, my family lived near a small beach. Every summer, we went there almost every day. One year, however, a strong storm destroyed part of the beach, and we couldn't go back for many months. Suddenly, I realized that place wasn't as permanent as I thought. That's when I started to value simple moments with my family more.

  2. What single event disrupted that routine?
  3. What did the speaker realize, and when?

Reading Practice

Uma decisão inesperada

Naquela época, Marina trabalhava numa empresa grande e não pensava em mudar de emprego. Ela gostava da rotina e tinha bons colegas de trabalho. Um dia, porém, a empresa anunciou uma reestruturação, e várias pessoas perderam o emprego. Marina não tinha imaginado que isso pudesse acontecer com ela também. De repente, percebeu que precisava repensar seus planos. Foi então que decidiu abrir seu próprio negócio, algo que ela já tinha considerado antes, mas nunca tinha tido coragem de fazer.

  1. What was Marina's situation before the restructuring, according to the imperfect verbs?
  2. What single event changed her situation?
  3. What decision did Marina make, and what pluperfect form describes something she had considered before?

Writing Workshop

Write a connected personal narrative (10–14 sentences) with background, a sequence of events, and a turning point.

  1. Establish the background using the imperfect: where you were, what things were like, what you used to do.
  2. Introduce the story with a time expression such as um dia or certa vez.
  3. Narrate two or three completed events using the preterite.
  4. Mark a clear turning point using de repente or foi então que.
  5. Close with a brief reflection on how the event affected you.

Revision checklist

  • I used the imperfect for background and the preterite for completed events.
  • I included at least one clear turning point expression.
  • If I used the pluperfect, I used it only as a recognized pattern, not a freely generated one.
  • I avoided any grammar or vocabulary not activated through Chapter 6.
  • I preserved all diacritics, agreement, and punctuation conventions.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Personal storytelling in everyday conversation

Sharing short personal stories is a common way of building connection in everyday conversation across Brazil and other Lusophone communities, whether among friends, coworkers, or new acquaintances at a community event. Listeners often show engagement by asking clarifying questions about the background of a story before the main event, which is one reason background information and the turning point both matter for clear narration. Storytelling conventions and how much personal detail is considered appropriate can vary by relationship, setting, and individual preference, so treat the practice tasks in this chapter as one common pattern rather than a universal rule.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using the preterite for background description

Error: Naquela época, eu morei numa cidade pequena. (preterite used for an ongoing state)
Better: Naquela época, eu morava numa cidade pequena.

Use the imperfect for background circumstances that were ongoing or habitual, and reserve the preterite for specific completed events that move the story forward.

Common Mistake — Generating new pluperfect forms freely

Error: attempting to build unfamiliar pluperfect forms beyond the recognized pattern já tinha + particípio in independent speaking or writing.
Better: recognize the pluperfect when reading or listening, but rely on preterite and imperfect for your own narration this chapter.

The pluperfect remains receptive only in Chapter 6. Productive pluperfect use is not assessed at this stage.
