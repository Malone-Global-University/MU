जो लोग, जो बातें

Volume II, Chapter 17: Relative Description · Speak and Write Hindi: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use controlled जो...वह sentences to identify and describe people and things.
  2. Recognize and make limited use of जहाँ...वहाँ for describing places.
  3. Recognize जिस...उस in a small number of formulaic, oblique-context examples.
  4. Track reference across connected sentences using यह/वह/ये/वे, इस/उस, and उन together with relative structures.
  5. Combine relative description with comparison and recommendation from Chapter 14.
  6. Read a short profile or recommendation text that uses relative-correlative description.
  7. Write a connected descriptive-recommendation paragraph in standard Devanagari.

Communication Scenario

The group's project went well after their contingency planning in Chapter 16. Now Priya is helping a new student, Kabir, settle into the neighborhood. He needs to know which shop, which doctor, which tutor, and which café to choose among several options — and he wants to know why. Priya describes people and places by pointing out which one has which quality, rather than just listing names.

  1. Notice how जो introduces a description and वह points back to the person or thing being identified.
  2. Notice how जहाँ...वहाँ works the same way for places.
  3. Notice how Priya keeps track of who or what she is talking about across several sentences without repeating names.
  4. Notice how relative description supports comparison and recommendation, recycling skills from Chapter 14.

Pronunciation and Orthography Focus

Clause grouping in relative-correlative sentences

A जो...वह sentence groups into two parts, just like the अगर...तो sentences from Chapter 16. Group the जो-clause together, pause briefly, and then group the वह-clause together: जो दुकान नज़दीक है, || वह सस्ती भी है. Avoid pausing in the middle of either clause.

Schwa deletion in reference forms

Notice the pronunciation of जिसका (approximately jis-kaa, not ji-sa-kaa) and भरोसेमंद (approximately bha-ro-se-mand). Review the general schwa-deletion pattern from earlier chapters and apply it to these longer reference and quality words.

Avoiding overlong English-style relative clauses

English speakers sometimes try to build one long relative clause the way English does. In Hindi at this level, keep जो-clauses short and let वह clearly point back to what was described. Compare the natural rhythm of जो डॉक्टर पास में हैं, वह बहुत अच्छे हैं with an unnecessarily long, run-on version.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

17.3 जो...वह — Identifying People and Things

Language Building Block
जो दुकान नज़दीक है, वह सस्ती भी है।
The shop that is nearby is also cheap.

Form: जो introduces a clause that describes or identifies a person or thing; वह (or वे for plural or respectful reference) begins the main clause and points back to what जो described. The noun being identified may appear once, in the जो-clause, and is not usually repeated in the वह-clause.

Meaning: The structure narrows a general category ("a shop," "a doctor," "a person") down to one specific example by describing a quality, location, or action associated with it, and then says something further about that specific example.

Use: जो...वह is common in recommendations, comparisons, directions, and everyday description. Learners should keep the जो-clause short and controlled at this level rather than stacking multiple descriptions into one sentence.

Identifying clause (जो...)Main clause (वह...)
जो डॉक्टर पास में हैंवह बहुत अच्छे हैं
जो ट्यूटर हिंदी पढ़ाती हैंवह बहुत अनुभवी हैं
जो लड़का कल आया थावह मेरा नया दोस्त है
जो कैफ़े कोने पर हैवह हमेशा भरा रहता है

17.4 जहाँ...वहाँ — Identifying Places (Receptive, Limited Use)

Language Building Block — Receptive
जहाँ भीड़ कम है, वहाँ माहौल भी अच्छा है।
Where there is less crowding, the atmosphere is also good.

जहाँ...वहाँ works like जो...वह but identifies a place rather than a person or thing: it connects "the place where X" to "there, Y." At this stage, learners should recognize जहाँ...वहाँ in listening and reading and may reuse a small number of formulaic examples such as जहाँ मैं रहता हूँ, वहाँ..., but full productive control of new content is not required until later chapters.

17.5 जिस...उस — Oblique Relative Reference (Recognition Only)

जिस...उस is the oblique form of जो...वह, used before postpositions, as in जिस दुकान के बारे में तुमने बताया, उस दुकान में मैं गया ("I went to the shop you told me about"). At this stage, learners should recognize this pattern in a small number of glossed examples. Full productive use, including choosing the correct oblique form, is developed in later volumes.

17.6 Reference Tracking Across Sentences

Once जो...वह identifies a person, place, or thing, later sentences can refer back to it with वह/वे, इसका/उसका, or वहाँ instead of repeating the full noun:

  • जो डॉक्टर पास में हैं, वह बहुत अच्छे हैं। उनका क्लीनिक सोमवार से शनिवार तक खुलता है।
  • जो कैफ़े कोने पर है, वह हमेशा भरा रहता है। वहाँ की कॉफ़ी बहुत अच्छी है।

Learners should practice continuing a description across two or three sentences using pronoun and demonstrative reference rather than repeating the identified noun each time.

17.7 Combining Relative Description with Comparison and Recommendation

Relative description now supports the comparison and recommendation patterns from Chapter 14:

  • जो ट्यूटर हिंदी पढ़ाती हैं, वह दूसरी ट्यूटर से ज़्यादा अनुभवी हैं, इसलिए मैं उन्हीं का सुझाव देती हूँ।

Learners are not expected to combine every system in one sentence, but should be comfortable identifying a person or thing and then adding one comparison or recommendation.

Boundary

This chapter does not require full mastery of all relative-correlative forms, embedded clauses, formal written relative syntax, or free oblique production with जिस...उस. Those systems develop further in Volume III.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Priya is showing Kabir, a new student in the neighborhood, around and helping him choose a doctor, a tutor, and a café.

कबीर यहाँ इतने डॉक्टर हैं। तुम किसका सुझाव दोगी? There are so many doctors here. Who would you recommend?
प्रिया जो डॉक्टर पास में हैं, वह बहुत अच्छे हैं। उनका क्लीनिक सोमवार से शनिवार तक खुलता है, और वह हमेशा समय पर देखते हैं। The doctor who is nearby is very good. Their clinic is open Monday through Saturday, and they always see patients on time.
कबीर अच्छा। और हिंदी सीखने के लिए, कोई ट्यूटर जानती हो? Good. And for learning Hindi, do you know any tutor?
प्रिया जो ट्यूटर हिंदी पढ़ाती हैं, वह दूसरी ट्यूटर से ज़्यादा अनुभवी हैं, इसलिए मैं उन्हीं का सुझाव देती हूँ। The tutor who teaches Hindi is more experienced than the other tutor, so I recommend her.
कबीर ठीक है। और अगर मुझे पढ़ने के लिए कोई शांत जगह चाहिए? Okay. And if I need a quiet place to study?
प्रिया जो कैफ़े कोने पर है, वह हमेशा भरा रहता है, इसलिए वहाँ पढ़ना मुश्किल है। लेकिन जहाँ भीड़ कम है, वहाँ माहौल भी अच्छा है — मेरा मतलब है, स्टेशन के पास वाला छोटा कैफ़े। The café on the corner is always full, so it's hard to study there. But where there's less crowding, the atmosphere is also good — I mean the small café near the station.
कबीर वहाँ की कॉफ़ी कैसी है? How's the coffee there?
प्रिया बहुत अच्छी है, और सस्ती भी। जिस दुकान के बारे में मैंने पहले बताया था, उसी के बगल में है। Very good, and cheap too. It's right next to the shop I told you about earlier.
कबीर धन्यवाद, प्रिया। तुम्हारी मदद से यहाँ बसना आसान हो रहा है। Thank you, Priya. Settling in here is becoming easier with your help.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. प्रिया किस डॉक्टर का सुझाव देती है, और क्यों? (Which doctor does Priya recommend, and why?)
  2. प्रिया किस ट्यूटर का सुझाव देती है, और वह ट्यूटर दूसरी ट्यूटर से कैसे अलग है? (Which tutor does Priya recommend, and how is that tutor different from the other tutor?)
  3. प्रिया कोने वाले कैफ़े की जगह कौन-सी जगह सुझाती है, और क्यों? (What place does Priya suggest instead of the corner café, and why?)

Writing Workshop

Write a short recommendation paragraph titled मेरे पड़ोस के सुझाव ("My Neighborhood Recommendations") for someone new to your area — real or imagined.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write a connected text of 150-220 words in standard Devanagari, in complete sentences. Your text must include: (1) a brief introduction to the neighborhood or area; (2) at least two जो...वह sentences identifying a person, place, or thing you recommend; (3) at least one comparison or reason connecting to your recommendation; and (4) a closing sentence stating why the recommendations will help.

  1. Introduce the setting. Briefly describe the neighborhood or area.
  2. Identify your first recommendation. Use जो...वह to describe a person or thing and say something about it.
  3. Identify your second recommendation. Use a different noun (a different person, place, or thing) with जो...वह.
  4. Add a comparison or reason. Use से ज़्यादा, से कम, क्योंकि, or इसलिए to support one recommendation.
  5. Close with a summary. Say why these recommendations will be useful.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • Each जो-clause is followed by a clear वह-clause.
  • The identified noun is not repeated unnecessarily in the वह-clause.
  • Pronoun and demonstrative reference is used correctly across sentences.
  • Verb forms agree correctly in gender and number where relevant.
  • Spelling, matras, word spacing, and dandas are correct throughout.

Speaking Practice

With a partner, take turns giving a जो-clause and having the other person supply a logical वह-clause, then switch roles.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Recommending by relationship and trust

In many everyday Hindi-speaking contexts, recommendations for a doctor, tutor, shop, or service often come through personal relationships and word of mouth rather than formal reviews alone. Describing someone as भरोसेमंद ("trustworthy") carries real social weight in this kind of recommendation.

Culture in Context — Describing people respectfully

When identifying and describing people with जो...वह, learners should avoid intrusive personal judgments and focus on relevant, respectful qualities, as Priya does when describing the doctor and tutor by their reliability and experience rather than by unrelated personal details.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Repeating the noun in the वह-clause

Error: जो डॉक्टर पास में हैं, वह डॉक्टर बहुत अच्छे हैं, repeating डॉक्टर unnecessarily.

Correction: जो डॉक्टर पास में हैं, वह बहुत अच्छे हैं। Once जो identifies the noun, वह alone can refer back to it.

Common Mistake — Building one long English-style relative clause

Error: Trying to fit an entire English relative clause with several ideas into one long जो-clause, producing an unclear sentence.

Correction: Keep the जो-clause short and add extra information in a separate sentence, as in जो डॉक्टर पास में हैं, वह बहुत अच्छे हैं। उनका क्लीनिक सोमवार से शनिवार तक खुलता है।

Common Mistake — Using जिस...उस freely in production

Error: Attempting to freely construct new जिस...उस sentences with untaught postpositions or nouns.

Correction: At this level, reuse only the formulaic जिस...उस examples modeled in this chapter. Free oblique relative production is developed in Volume III.
