पढ़ाई, काम, और अनुमति

Volume II, Chapter 12: Study, Work, and Permission · Speak and Write Hindi: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe study and workplace tasks, schedules, deadlines, and meetings in more detail.
  2. Ask for permission or clarification politely in institutional contexts using expanded सकना and polite request structures such as क्या मैं...? and क्या आप...?.
  3. Distinguish ability, permission, obligation, necessity, and preference using सकना, चाहिए, ज़रूरी है, ज़रूरत है, and करना पड़ता है.
  4. Recognize पाना in ability/result contexts.
  5. Use infinitive complements after चाहना, सकना, पड़ना, and चाहिए.
  6. Write a short, polite institutional email or message requesting permission or reporting progress.
  7. Understand announcements and instructions in a school or workplace setting.

Communication Scenario

Now that Arjun's headache has passed and the tap is fixed, he turns back to his class project. The deadline is close, but he needs to leave town for a family matter for two days. He must ask his professor for a short extension, and separately, he needs to ask his manager at his part-time job for permission to change his shift for the same days. Priya, who works with him on the project, agrees to send him a progress update by message.

  1. Notice how Arjun uses polite request forms rather than simply stating what he needs.
  2. Notice how the professor and the manager distinguish what is possible (सकना) from what is necessary (ज़रूरी है, चाहिए).
  3. Notice how formal address and honorific agreement remain consistent throughout an institutional exchange.

Chapter 12 continues Unit III, समस्या, सेवा, और विचार. It builds directly on Chapter 11's advice, softeners, and experiencer patterns, shifting the communicative focus toward institutional permission, ability, and formal requests in study and work settings.

Pronunciation and Schwa Deletion

Focus — Formal speech rhythm

Formal institutional speech is generally slower and more evenly paced than casual conversation. Compare the brisk, casual शिफ़्ट बदल दो with the more measured क्या मैं अपनी शिफ़्ट बदल सकता हूँ?, which is spoken with clear phrase breaks and a polite, level tone.

Focus — Schwa deletion in institutional vocabulary

Notice that समय सीमा is read as approximately sa-may see-ma, with the final inherent vowel of समय retained because it stands as an independent word, while अनुमति is read as approximately a-nu-mati, with the middle inherent vowel retained but the final one dropped in normal speech.

Focus — Contrast between सकता/सकती and चाहता/चाहती

Listen carefully to the difference between मैं जा सकता हूँ (I am able to go / I may go) and मैं जाना चाहता हूँ (I want to go). The gender-marked forms सकता/सकती and चाहता/चाहती sound similar but express very different meanings; slow down and enunciate the verb stem clearly.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

12.5 Expanded सकना for ability and permission

You already know सकना for ability, as in मैं हिंदी बोल सकता हूँ. This chapter extends the same structure to asking for and granting institutional permission.

Language Building Block — Ability and permission with सकना
क्या मैं दो दिन बाद रिपोर्ट जमा कर सकता/सकती हूँ? आप कल छुट्टी ले सकते/सकती हैं।
May I submit the report two days later? You may take leave tomorrow.

Form: [subject] + [verb stem] + सक + [agreement] + [tense auxiliary]. The same form covers both physical/mental ability and social permission; context and question framing distinguish them. A question with क्या मैं...सकता/सकती हूँ? is understood as a request for permission, not a request for information about capability.

पाना appears in some ability/result contexts, often after a verb stem, to mean succeeding in doing something despite difficulty, as in मैं समय पर नहीं आ पाया (I wasn't able to arrive on time). At this stage, treat पाना as recognition vocabulary rather than a fully productive pattern.

12.6 Obligation and necessity: चाहिए, ज़रूरी है, ज़रूरत है, करना पड़ता है

Language Building Block — Degrees of obligation
मुझे यह रिपोर्ट कल जमा करनी चाहिए। यह रिपोर्ट समय पर जमा करना ज़रूरी है। मुझे थोड़ा और समय चाहिए / मुझे थोड़े और समय की ज़रूरत है। कभी-कभी मुझे देर तक काम करना पड़ता है।
I should submit this report tomorrow. It's necessary to submit this report on time. I need a little more time. Sometimes I have to work late.

Form and meaning: चाहिए expresses a personal recommendation or expectation directed at the experiencer with को; ज़रूरी है states a general necessity, often about the situation rather than the person; ज़रूरत है expresses a need for something (a noun or infinitive), again typically with को; करना पड़ता है expresses an obligation imposed by circumstances, and its tense changes to show when the obligation applied (करना पड़ा, करना पड़ेगा).

Do not treat these four expressions as interchangeable. Each carries a slightly different shade of "must" or "need," and choosing the right one is part of natural, polite institutional Hindi.

12.7 Polite request structures

Language Building Block — Formal polite requests
क्या मैं कुछ पूछ सकता/सकती हूँ? क्या आप मुझे थोड़ा समय दे सकते/सकती हैं? कृपया मुझे बताइए कि क्या यह ठीक है। मुझे एक छोटी छुट्टी चाहिए।
May I ask something? Could you give me a little time? Please tell me if this is okay. I need a short leave.

These four frames — क्या मैं...?, क्या आप...?, कृपया..., and मुझे...चाहिए — form the core polite-request toolkit for institutional Hindi. क्या मैं...? asks permission for the speaker's own action; क्या आप...? politely asks the listener to do something; कृपया softens an instruction or request; मुझे...चाहिए states a need directly but politely.

12.8 Infinitive complements

Language Building Block — Verb + infinitive combinations
मैं जल्दी जाना चाहता/चाहती हूँ। मैं कल आ सकता/सकती हूँ। मुझे आज काम करना पड़ता है। हमें यह रिपोर्ट लिखनी चाहिए।
I want to go early. I can come tomorrow. I have to work today. We should write this report.

Form: [infinitive] + चाहना, सकना, पड़ना, or चाहिए. The infinitive typically agrees in gender and number with the object it governs when there is no other agreement trigger, as in यह रिपोर्ट लिखनी चाहिए (feminine singular रिपोर्ट triggers लिखनी), while चाहना and सकना agree with the subject.

12.9 Formal pronoun and honorific agreement

Institutional communication consistently uses आप and honorific plural verb agreement, even when addressing a single person of authority such as a professor, manager, or officer. Maintain this consistently across an entire email or conversation; do not shift to तुम partway through a formal exchange.

Boundary

This chapter does not introduce advanced bureaucratic Hindi, specialized academic writing conventions, or complex indirect request structures. पाना is presented only as recognition vocabulary. Those extensions belong to later chapters and volumes.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Arjun speaks with his professor after class, then messages his manager about his part-time job shift.

अर्जुन नमस्ते सर, क्या मैं आपसे कुछ पूछ सकता हूँ? Hello sir, may I ask you something?
प्रोफ़ेसर हाँ, ज़रूर, बताइए। Yes, of course, tell me.
अर्जुन मुझे अगले हफ़्ते एक पारिवारिक काम से बाहर जाना पड़ रहा है। क्या मैं प्रोजेक्ट रिपोर्ट दो दिन बाद जमा कर सकता हूँ? I have to go out of town next week for a family matter. May I submit the project report two days later?
प्रोफ़ेसर ठीक है, लेकिन आपको मुझे एक लिखित आवेदन देना ज़रूरी है। कृपया आज शाम तक ईमेल भेज दीजिए। Okay, but it's necessary that you give me a written request. Please send an email by this evening.
अर्जुन जी ज़रूर, धन्यवाद। मुझे बस थोड़े और समय की ज़रूरत है। Yes, of course, thank you. I just need a little more time.

Later, a message to his manager:

अर्जुन नमस्ते मैम, क्या मैं अगले हफ़्ते अपनी शिफ़्ट बदल सकता हूँ? मुझे दो दिन के लिए बाहर जाना है। Hello ma'am, may I change my shift next week? I have to go out of town for two days.
मैनेजर यह मुमकिन है, लेकिन आपको कल तक बता देना चाहिए ताकि हम शिफ़्ट का इंतज़ाम कर सकें। This is possible, but you should let us know by tomorrow so we can arrange the shift.
अर्जुन ठीक है, मैं आज ही बता दूँगा। धन्यवाद। Okay, I'll let you know today itself. Thank you.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. अर्जुन प्रोफ़ेसर से क्या अनुमति माँगता है? (What permission does Arjun ask the professor for?)
  2. प्रोफ़ेसर को क्या चाहिए, इससे पहले कि वह अनुमति दें? (What does the professor need before granting permission?)
  3. मैनेजर अर्जुन से क्या करने को कहती है? (What does the manager ask Arjun to do?)

Writing Workshop

Write a short formal email or message titled अनुमति के लिए अनुरोध, requesting permission from a teacher, professor, or manager.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write a connected message of 120-180 words in Devanagari. Your message must include: (1) a formal salutation such as आदरणीय, (2) a clear statement of the situation, (3) a polite request for permission using क्या मैं...? or अनुमति दी जाए, (4) at least one obligation or necessity expression (चाहिए, ज़रूरी है, ज़रूरत है, or करना पड़ता है), (5) an infinitive complement pattern, and (6) a formal closing such as सादर.

  1. Choose your situation. Pick a schedule conflict, a deadline problem, or a shift change.
  2. Open formally. Use आदरणीय and a brief greeting.
  3. State the situation and make your request. Use क्या मैं...? or a formal request frame.
  4. Explain the necessity. Use at least one obligation expression.
  5. Close politely. Use सादर and your name.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • आप and honorific plural agreement are used consistently throughout.
  • The polite request frame is natural and not a direct translation from English.
  • The correct obligation expression is chosen for its intended meaning.
  • Infinitive complements agree correctly where required.
  • Formal salutation and closing are present and appropriate.
  • Spelling, matras, word spacing, and dandas are correct throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Formality in institutional requests

Requests to professors, managers, and officials are typically formal, even when the underlying relationship is friendly. Written requests often include a brief formal salutation and closing, and a clearly stated reason. Learners should default to the formal patterns in this chapter when addressing someone in an institutional role.

Culture in Context

English-origin words such as प्रोजेक्ट, रिपोर्ट, and शिफ़्ट are extremely common in Hindi-speaking study and work settings across many regions and institutions. This reflects real, everyday bilingual usage rather than a weakness in the Hindi sentence, which remains structured entirely by Hindi grammar.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Confusing सकना and चाहना

Error: Writing मैं जाना सकता हूँ to mean "I want to go."

Correction: मैं जाना चाहता हूँ। (I want to go.) versus मैं जा सकता हूँ। (I am able to / may go.) सकना attaches directly to the verb stem, not to the infinitive.

Common Mistake — Using चाहिए where ज़रूरी है fits better

Error: Writing आपको मुझे एक लिखित आवेदन चाहिए to state a general institutional requirement.

Correction: आपको मुझे एक लिखित आवेदन देना ज़रूरी है। ज़रूरी है better expresses a general necessity tied to the situation, not a personal preference.

Common Mistake — Dropping honorific agreement mid-message

Error: Starting with आप बता सकते हैं? and later writing तुम मुझे बताओ in the same formal email.

Correction: Keep आप and honorific plural agreement consistent throughout a formal exchange; do not shift to तुम.

Common Mistake — Treating पाना as fully productive

Error: Freely producing new sentences with पाना in untaught patterns.

Correction: At this stage, recognize पाना in context (for example, मैं समय पर नहीं आ पाया) rather than generating new combinations independently. Full productive control develops in a later volume.

Reading — Comprehension

  1. Arjun requests permission to submit his project report two days late.
  2. Arjun promises to complete his work as soon as he returns.
  3. He uses आदरणीय as a formal salutation and सादर as a formal closing.
