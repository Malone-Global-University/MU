समीक्षा 2: घटना सुनाना

Volume II, Chapter 10: Unit II Assessment — Telling What Happened · Speak and Write Hindi: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Narrate a connected past event using both intransitive and transitive perfective forms.
  2. Use ने accurately with the scheduled transitive verb set, including correct agreement with unmarked objects.
  3. Sequence a narrative using time expressions and connectors such as पहले, फिर, उसके बाद, तभी, and आखिर में.
  4. Describe a trip or visit using source, destination, and time-relation postpositions such as से, तक, से पहले, and के बाद.
  5. Frame a personal experience using कभी, पहली बार, or एक बार.
  6. Use at least one common compound verb from Chapter 9 to add nuance to a narrative.
  7. Read, listen to, and produce a coherent B1-level narrative with background, sequence, completed events, and reaction.
Note on this chapter

This is a review and assessment chapter. It introduces no new grammar systems. It consolidates and integrates the language taught in Chapters 6-9 into one connected narrative performance.

Communication Scenario

The study group's teacher, Kavita Ma'am, asks each student to prepare a short "story of the week" to share with the class — a real event, a trip, or a memorable experience from recent weeks. Sunita decides to combine two things: a short account of a weekend visit to her cousin's house, and one surprising moment from that visit. As she plans what to say, she realizes she needs everything the group has learned since Chapter 6: how to say what happened, how to use ने correctly, how to talk about a trip, and how to shape a story with a beginning, a turning point, and an end.

  1. Notice how Sunita's story moves from background (why she went) to sequence (what she did) to a turning point (something unexpected) to a resolution.
  2. Notice how she recycles ने, travel postpositions, and one compound verb, without needing any new grammar.
  3. Notice how the class listens and asks natural follow-up questions, just as in earlier chapters.

Pronunciation and Schwa Deletion Review

Review Focus — Perfective forms and schwa deletion

Review the pronunciation of perfective forms such as खरीदा, भेजी, and देखा. Notice how the final inherent vowel is not always pronounced the same way it is written, and keep gender agreement audible in your reading.

Review Focus — Compound verb rhythm

As in Chapter 9, read compound verbs such as भूल गई and देख लिया as one connected phrase rather than two separate words, with reduced stress on the light verb.

Review Focus — Narrative rhythm across a full story

Practice reading a full connected narrative aloud, slowing slightly and raising pitch at तभी to mark the turning point, then settling the pace again for आखिर में. Sustaining this rhythm across several sentences is the main new listening/speaking demand in this chapter.

Grammar Review

No new grammar system is introduced in this chapter. The systems below are reactivated together so that learners can combine them fluently in one connected narrative.

10.3 Intransitive and transitive perfective together

Language Building Block — Review: intransitive + transitive perfective
मैं शनिवार को अपनी मौसी के घर गई। वहाँ मैंने बहुत सारा खाना खाया और अपनी मौसी की मदद की।
I went to my aunt's house on Saturday. There I ate a lot of food and helped my aunt.

Reminder: Movement verbs like जाना remain intransitive and never take ने; verbs like खाना and करना take ने in the perfective and agree with an unmarked object.

10.4 Travel and time sequencing together

Language Building Block — Review: source, destination, and sequence
हम सुबह घर से निकले और दोपहर तक वहाँ पहुँच गए। पहुँचने के बाद, हमने चाय पी।
We left home in the morning and arrived there by afternoon. After arriving, we drank tea.

10.5 Experience framing and one compound verb

Language Building Block — Review: experience + compound verb
पहली बार मैं वहाँ अकेले गई थी, इसलिए मैं थोड़ी घबराई हुई थी। तभी मुझे याद आया कि मैं अपना उपहार घर पर ही भूल गई!
It was the first time I had gone there alone, so I was a little nervous. Right then I remembered that I had left my gift at home!
Boundary

This chapter does not extend ने, compound verbs, or experience patterns beyond what was activated in Chapters 6-9. It also does not introduce reported speech with कि, conditionals, or relative-correlative structures; those systems begin in Unit III and Unit IV.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Class has gathered for "story of the week." Sunita shares her story; classmates ask follow-up questions.

कविता मैम सुनीता, इस हफ़्ते तुम्हारी कहानी क्या है? Sunita, what is your story this week?
सुनीता शनिवार को मैं अपनी मौसी के घर गई। पहली बार मैं वहाँ अकेले गई थी। On Saturday I went to my aunt's house. It was the first time I had gone there alone.
अर्जुन तुम वहाँ कैसे पहुँची? कितना समय लगा? How did you get there? How much time did it take?
सुनीता मैं सुबह घर से निकली और दोपहर तक वहाँ पहुँच गई। पहुँचने के बाद, मैंने अपनी मौसी की मदद की और हमने साथ में खाना बनाया। I left home in the morning and arrived there by afternoon. After arriving, I helped my aunt and we cooked together.
प्रिया फिर क्या हुआ? कोई हैरानी वाली बात? Then what happened? Anything surprising?
सुनीता हाँ! तभी मुझे याद आया कि मैं अपना उपहार घर पर ही भूल गई। मैं बहुत शर्मिंदा हुई। Yes! Right then I remembered that I had left my gift at home. I felt very embarrassed.
अर्जुन आखिर में क्या किया? What did you do in the end?
सुनीता आखिर में, मैंने मौसी को सच बता दिया, और उन्होंने कहा कि कोई बात नहीं। अगली बार मैं ज़रूर अपना बैग पहले देख लूँगी! In the end, I told my aunt the truth, and she said it's no problem. Next time I will definitely check my bag first!

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. सुनीता कब और कहाँ गई? (When and where did Sunita go?)
  2. रास्ते में और पहुँचने के बाद उसने क्या किया? (What did she do en route and after arriving?)
  3. कहानी का हैरानी वाला मोड़ क्या था? (What was the story's surprising turning point?)

Reading Practice

Read Priya's message to the class group, written after her own "story of the week," then answer the questions.

प्रिया का संदेश — Priya's Message
पिछले रविवार को मैं अपने भाई के साथ एक नई जगह घूमने गई। हम बस से गए और वहाँ तक पहुँचने में दो घंटे लगे। रास्ते में हमने बहुत सुंदर पहाड़ देखे। पहुँचने के बाद, मेरे भाई ने खाना ऑर्डर किया, लेकिन तभी वेटर ने कहा कि रेस्तराँ बंद होने वाला है। हम थोड़े निराश हुए, लेकिन इसलिए हमने पास की एक छोटी दुकान से कुछ खरीद लिया और बाहर बैठकर खाया। यह मेरी पहली बार थी जब मैंने इतनी दूर की यात्रा की, और आखिर में यह दिन बहुत यादगार रहा।
Last Sunday I went sightseeing to a new place with my brother. We went by bus and it took two hours to get there. On the way we saw very beautiful mountains. After arriving, my brother ordered food, but right then the waiter said the restaurant was about to close. We were a little disappointed, but so we bought something from a small shop nearby and ate sitting outside. This was the first time I had traveled so far, and in the end this day remained very memorable.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. Where did Priya go, with whom, and how did they travel?
  2. What was the turning point in Priya's story, and what did they do about it?
  3. Why does Priya call this day memorable?

Writing Workshop

Write your own "story of the week" titled एक घटना जो मुझे याद है, describing a real or realistic trip, visit, or event from your own life.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write a connected story of 220-300 words in Devanagari. Your story must include: (1) background explaining when and why the event happened, (2) at least three sentences using ने with scheduled transitive verbs and correct object agreement, (3) at least one travel or visit element using से, तक, से पहले, or के बाद, (4) one experience frame with कभी, पहली बार, or एक बार, (5) one compound verb from Chapter 9, (6) a turning point marked with तभी, and (7) a closing with आखिर में that includes one reaction or one lesson.

  1. Choose your event. Pick a trip, visit, or memorable occasion from recent weeks.
  2. Set the background. Say when and why the event happened.
  3. Narrate the sequence. Use पहले, फिर, and उसके बाद to move the story forward.
  4. Mark the turning point. Use तभी to introduce something unexpected.
  5. Close the story. Use आखिर में to resolve the story and add a reaction or lesson.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • The story has clear background, sequence, a turning point, and a closing.
  • Transitive perfective sentences correctly use ने and agree with the unmarked object.
  • Intransitive movement verbs never take ने.
  • At least one travel/visit postposition and one experience frame appear naturally.
  • The compound verb adds nuance appropriately rather than being translated word-for-word.
  • Spelling, matras, word spacing, and dandas are correct throughout.

Speaking Practice

With a partner, take turns telling a one-sentence event (for example, मैं कल बाज़ार गई।) and asking three follow-up questions using कब, कैसे, and फिर क्या हुआ.

संचार कार्य — Communicative task: हफ़्ते की कहानी

Tell a partner or small group your "story of the week," including background, sequence, a turning point marked with तभी, and a closing with आखिर में. Listeners should ask at least two follow-up questions.

अकेले अभ्यास — Solo alternative

Record yourself telling your full "story of the week" in 1-2 minutes, then listen back and check your use of ने, travel postpositions, and one compound verb.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Sharing a "story of the week" in class

Asking students to share a brief personal update is a common classroom routine in many Hindi language-learning contexts, whether in India or in diaspora communities. The tone is usually light and supportive, and listeners are expected to respond with interest rather than correction.

Culture in Context — Family visits and small mishaps

As in Chapter 9, small mishaps during a family visit, such as forgetting a gift, are usually met with reassurance rather than serious concern, especially among close relatives. This kind of warmth is common but not universal, and varies by family and relationship.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Adding ने to a movement verb

Error: Writing मैंने गई.

Correction: मैं गई। जाना is intransitive and never takes ने, even in a longer connected narrative.

Common Mistake — Losing object agreement across a long sentence

Error: Writing मैंने अपनी मौसी की मदद किया.

Correction: मैंने अपनी मौसी की मदद की। The perfective verb agrees with the unmarked feminine object मदद, not with the subject मैं.

Common Mistake — Stacking connectors without variety

Error: Using फिर to link every single sentence in a long story.

Correction: Vary sequencing with पहले, फिर, उसके बाद, तभी, and आखिर में so the story's shape is clear rather than a flat list of events.

Common Mistake — Mixing an experience frame with a specific dated event

Error: Writing कभी पिछले हफ़्ते मैं गई.

Correction: पिछले हफ़्ते मैं गई। or पहली बार पिछले हफ़्ते मैं गई। कभी alone does not combine naturally with a specific dated time expression.
