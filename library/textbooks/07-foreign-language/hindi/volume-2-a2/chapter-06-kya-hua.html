6

क्या हुआ?

Volume II, Chapter 6: What Happened? · Speak and Write Hindi: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Ask and answer क्या हुआ? about a recent event.
  2. Narrate a simple recent event using intransitive perfective verbs such as गया/गई, आया/आई, and हुआ/हुई.
  3. Recognize and reproduce familiar transitive perfective forms such as मैंने खाना खाया.
  4. Sequence events using पहले, फिर, उसके बाद, and अंत में.
  5. Distinguish background information (habitual/progressive) from completed actions in a narrative.
  6. Express a simple reaction to an event using अच्छा लगा, बुरा लगा, and मज़ा आया.
  7. Read and write a short connected account of a recent event in Devanagari.

Communication Scenario

It is Monday morning, and Priya's study group is catching up before class. Aman looks tired and a little distracted, so Sunita asks him what happened over the weekend. As Aman tells his story, he moves between what he usually does, what was happening at a particular moment, and what actually took place — the same three-way contrast Chapter 5 asked learners to control in their own writing. Vikram listens and asks a follow-up question, modeling the kind of question a listener asks to keep a story moving.

  1. Notice that Aman's story opens with a normal weekend routine before shifting into what actually happened — this is the background-versus-event contrast this chapter introduces.
  2. Notice the intransitive perfective forms गया, आया, and हुआ, which do not require ने.
  3. Notice the one transitive perfective sentence, मैंने खाना बनाया, which appears here only as a familiar, formulaic recall from earlier chapters. Chapter 7 will make this pattern fully productive.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 6 opens Unit II, घटना और अनुभव. It shifts the course from description and connected profiles toward narration of completed events. It reviews and expands the intransitive perfective forms and formulaic transitive perfective sentences learners have already met in Volume I and Chapter 5, and it introduces time and sequence markers needed to tell a coherent story.

Coming Into This Chapter

From Volume I and Chapter 5, learners already control habitual and progressive forms, basic postpositions, and a small number of formulaic perfective sentences such as मैंने खाना खाया. Pronoun reference and honorific agreement from Chapter 2 continue to apply across narrative sentences.

New in This Chapter

Productive intransitive perfective narration; time markers (कल, परसों, पिछले हफ्ते); sequencing words (पहले, फिर, उसके बाद, अंत में); the background-versus-completed-action contrast; and simple experiencer reactions.

Looking Forward

Chapter 7, मैंने क्या किया?, makes ने and transitive perfective agreement fully productive, building directly on the intransitive foundation and sequencing tools established here.

Pronunciation and Schwa Deletion

Focus — Schwa deletion in perfective forms

Perfective forms such as हुआ, गया, and आया are short and frequent, but their feminine and plural counterparts change the final syllable in ways that affect rhythm: हुई, गई, आई, गए, आए. Practice these pairs aloud, keeping the vowel length and quality distinct rather than reducing every ending to the same sound.

Focus — Phrase grouping in sequenced narration

When a sentence begins with a sequencing word such as फिर or उसके बाद, a brief pause after the sequencing word helps the listener track the story. Avoid running every clause together without any phrase breaks, and avoid pausing after every single word, which was flagged as a common paragraph-reading habit to avoid in Chapter 5.

Focus — Listening for time markers

कल and परसों can mean either a past or future day depending on the surrounding verb. Listen for the perfective or future verb form nearby to determine direction, rather than relying on the time word alone.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

6.5 Intransitive perfective narration

Form: Many common intransitive verbs form the perfective by adding आ (masculine singular), ई (feminine singular), ए (masculine plural/respectful), or ईं (feminine plural/respectful) to the verb stem. The subject appears in the direct case; no ने is used.

Language Building Block — Intransitive perfective
मैं कल स्कूल गया/गई। वह देर से आया/आई। कुछ अजीब हुआ।
I went to school yesterday. He/she came late. Something strange happened.

Meaning and use: These forms report a single completed event with no object. They contrast directly with the habitual (जाता हूँ) and progressive (जा रहा हूँ) forms already familiar from Chapters 1-5.

जाना is irregular: its perfective stem is ग-, not जा-. This chapter does not require a complete list of irregular perfective stems; only the high-frequency verbs above are productive here.

6.6 Familiar transitive perfective (formulaic recall)

Learners have already met sentences such as मैंने खाना खाया as formulaic material. This chapter allows continued formulaic use of a small set of first-person examples, but it does not require free production with new transitive verbs or explain ने in full. That analysis begins in Chapter 7.

Formulaic Recall — Familiar transitive perfective
मैंने खाना खाया। मैंने चाय पी।
I ate food. I drank tea.

6.7 Sequencing a narrative

Language Building Block — Sequencing events
पहले मैं बाज़ार गया। फिर मैं दोस्त से मिला। उसके बाद हम घर आए। अंत में हम सो गए।
First I went to the market. Then I met a friend. After that we came home. In the end we went to sleep.

Use: Place a sequencing word at the start of the clause it introduces. Do not stack more than one sequencing word in the same clause.

6.8 Background versus completed action

A natural narrative usually opens with background (habitual or progressive) before introducing what actually happened.

Language Building Block — Background + event
मैं आम तौर पर शनिवार को घर पर रहता हूँ, लेकिन कल मैं बाज़ार गया।
I usually stay home on Saturdays, but yesterday I went to the market.

6.9 Simple reactions

Language Building Block — Experiencer reaction
मुझे बहुत मज़ा आया। मुझे थोड़ी हैरानी हुई। मुझे बुरा लगा।
I had a lot of fun. I was a little surprised. I felt bad.

These reaction patterns use the same मुझे experiencer frame reviewed in Chapter 5's obligation patterns, extended here to feelings about a completed event.

Boundary

This chapter does not require full perfective agreement across all noun types, passive or reported narration, or free production with untaught transitive verbs. The target is confident, accurate intransitive narration plus recognizable formulaic transitive recall.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Monday morning, before class. Sunita asks Aman about his weekend.

सुनीता अमन, तुम आज थके हुए लग रहे हो। क्या हुआ? Aman, you look tired today. What happened?
अमन मैं आम तौर पर शनिवार को घर पर आराम करता हूँ, लेकिन कल कुछ अलग हुआ। पहले मैं सुबह बाज़ार गया, क्योंकि हमें कुछ सामान चाहिए था। I usually rest at home on Saturdays, but yesterday something different happened. First I went to the market in the morning, because we needed some things.
अमन फिर मैं वहाँ विक्रम से मिला, और हम दोनों साथ में एक पुराने दोस्त के घर गए। उसके बाद हमने वहाँ खाना खाया, और मुझे बहुत मज़ा आया। Then I met Vikram there, and the two of us went together to an old friend's house. After that we ate food there, and I had a lot of fun.
विक्रम हाँ, लेकिन अंत में हम बहुत देर से घर पहुँचे, है ना? Yes, but in the end we reached home very late, right?
अमन हाँ, बिल्कुल। अचानक बारिश भी हुई, इसलिए हमें रास्ते में रुकना पड़ा। मुझे थोड़ी हैरानी हुई, लेकिन कोई बड़ी बात नहीं थी। Yes, exactly. It suddenly rained too, so we had to stop on the way. I was a little surprised, but it wasn't a big deal.
सुनीता अच्छा हुआ कि तुम्हें मज़ा आया, भले ही देर हो गई। It's good that you had fun, even though it got late.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. अमन आम तौर पर शनिवार को क्या करता है? (What does Aman usually do on Saturdays?)
  2. अमन सबसे पहले कहाँ गया, और क्यों? (Where did Aman go first, and why?)
  3. अमन और विक्रम को घर आने में देर क्यों हुई? (Why were Aman and Vikram late getting home?)

Reading Practice

Read Vikram's diary entry below, which sequences a recent event with background, completed action, and reaction, then answer the questions.

विक्रम की डायरी — Vikram's Diary
परसों मेरे साथ एक दिलचस्प घटना हुई। मैं आम तौर पर रात को जल्दी सो जाता हूँ, लेकिन उस रात मुझे नींद नहीं आ रही थी। पहले मैंने थोड़ी देर किताब पढ़ी, फिर मैं टहलने के लिए बाहर गया। बाहर मेरी मुलाक़ात मेरे पुराने पड़ोसी से हुई, जो कई साल बाद वापस आया था। हम दोनों बहुत देर तक बातें करते रहे। उसके बाद मैं घर वापस आया, और मुझे बहुत अच्छा लगा कि मैंने उससे मुलाक़ात की। अंत में मैं देर से सोया, लेकिन मुझे कोई अफ़सोस नहीं हुआ।
The day before yesterday, an interesting thing happened to me. I usually go to sleep early at night, but that night I wasn't feeling sleepy. First I read a book for a while, then I went outside for a walk. Outside I ran into my old neighbor, who had come back after several years. The two of us kept talking for a long time. After that I came back home, and I felt very good that I met him. In the end I went to sleep late, but I had no regrets.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. Why couldn't Vikram sleep that night?
  2. Whom did Vikram meet outside, and what was notable about that person?
  3. How did Vikram feel about the evening, even though he went to sleep late?

Writing Workshop

Write a short account titled क्या हुआ? describing something that happened to you recently.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write a connected account of 120-180 words in Devanagari. Your account must include: (1) one sentence of background using habitual or progressive aspect, (2) at least three sequenced intransitive perfective sentences using पहले, फिर, उसके बाद, or अंत में, (3) at least one time marker such as कल or पिछले हफ्ते, and (4) one reaction sentence using अच्छा लगा, बुरा लगा, मज़ा आया, or हैरानी हुई.

  1. Set the background. State what you usually do at that time, using habitual or progressive aspect.
  2. Introduce the time. Use a time marker to show when the event occurred.
  3. Sequence the event. Narrate at least three completed actions in order, using sequencing words.
  4. Add a reaction. Close with how you felt about what happened.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • Intransitive perfective forms agree correctly in gender and number with their subjects.
  • Sequencing words appear at the start of the clauses they introduce, without stacking.
  • The background sentence uses habitual or progressive aspect, not perfective.
  • The reaction sentence uses the correct experiencer pattern with मुझे.
  • Any formulaic transitive perfective sentence (such as मैंने खाना खाया) matches the familiar patterns from earlier chapters.
  • Spelling, matras, word spacing, and dandas are correct throughout.

Record yourself narrating a recent event with background, sequence, and reaction. Listen back and check that your intransitive perfective forms agree correctly with their subjects.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Opening a story naturally

In everyday conversation, Hindi speakers often preface a story with a brief greeting or context rather than launching directly into narration, and listeners frequently interject with short reactions or follow-up questions rather than waiting silently for the story to finish. This differs somewhat from a written diary or message, where the narration usually proceeds more directly.

Culture in Context — Sharing minor mishaps

As in Seema's listening passage, minor mishaps such as a small accident are often shared matter-of-factly, with reassurance that the situation was not serious. Sympathy and reassurance both play a role in these exchanges, and their balance varies by relationship, region, and personality rather than following one fixed pattern.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Wrong gender agreement on intransitive perfective

Error: A woman writing मैं कल स्कूल गया, using the masculine form regardless of her own gender.

Correction: मैं कल स्कूल गई। The intransitive perfective agrees with the grammatical subject's gender and number, not with a fixed default form.

Common Mistake — Using perfective for background information

Error: Writing मैं आम तौर पर शनिवार को घर पर रहा when describing a usual routine.

Correction: मैं आम तौर पर शनिवार को घर पर रहता हूँ। Background information uses habitual aspect; only the specific completed event uses the perfective, as reviewed in Chapter 5's aspect-marker matching.

Common Mistake — Adding ने to an intransitive verb

Error: Writing मैंने गया.

Correction: मैं गया। Intransitive verbs such as जाना, आना, and होना never take ने. This marker becomes productive only with transitive verbs, beginning in Chapter 7.
