काम, ज़िम्मेदारियाँ, और आदतें

Volume II, Chapter 4: Work, Responsibilities, and Habits · Speak and Write Hindi: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe work, study, household, and community responsibilities in connected language.
  2. Distinguish routine actions from actions happening right now, using the habitual-progressive contrast.
  3. Explain obligations and needs using चाहिए, ज़रूरत है, and पड़ना.
  4. Use सकना to talk about ability and permission in controlled frames.
  5. Use the experiencer/obligation pattern मुझे ... करना है to say what someone has to do.
  6. Express frequency and duration using से and तक.
  7. Discuss a schedule conflict politely and negotiate a solution.

Communication Scenario

Aman has decided to look at a room near Priya's neighborhood, but before he can move, he and his roommate Vikram need to sort out a scheduling conflict: both of them are usually busy in the evenings, and someone needs to be home when the landlord visits on Thursday. Vikram calls Priya for advice, since she has dealt with the same landlord before. In the conversation, Vikram explains his usual routine, what he is doing this week that is different from normal, and what he and Aman each have to do before Thursday.

  1. Notice how Vikram distinguishes what he usually does (habitual) from what he is doing this particular week (progressive) — the two aspects look different and mean different things.
  2. Notice three different ways of expressing obligation and need: चाहिए, ज़रूरत है, and करना है, and how they are not simply interchangeable.
  3. Notice that Vikram uses से and तक to say how long his workday usually lasts, and सकना to ask whether Priya can help.

Devanagari and Pronunciation Focus

This chapter introduces no new letters or matras. It develops schwa deletion in infinitives and verbal nouns, and continues rhythm practice across longer sentences that combine two clauses, such as a routine statement followed by an obligation.

Focus — Schwa deletion in infinitives and verbal nouns

Read ज़िम्मेदारी, सफ़ाई करना, and ज़रूरत as natural spoken forms. Notice that the inherent vowel after the final consonant of an infinitive stem is often reduced in connected speech, and that ज़रूरत is pronounced with the stress falling naturally on the middle syllable rather than the first.

Focus — Rhythm across routine-plus-obligation sentences

A sentence such as मैं आम तौर पर सुबह काम पर जाता हूँ, लेकिन आज मुझे घर पर रहना है contains two rhythmic units joined by लेकिन. Practice pausing briefly at the comma and keeping each clause's internal rhythm smooth, rather than pausing after every word.

Focus — Distinguishing सकता/सकती from चाहता/चाहती

These two forms are easy to confuse for English speakers because both can precede हूँ. Listen carefully: मैं आ सकता हूँ means "I can come," while मैं आना चाहता हूँ means "I want to come." The vowel and consonant shape of सक- versus चाह- carries the entire meaning difference.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter develops two closely related systems: the contrast between habitual and progressive aspect, and an expanded set of need/obligation/ability expressions built around चाहिए, ज़रूरत है, पड़ना, सकना, and the experiencer pattern मुझे ... करना है.

4.5 Language Focus — Habitual versus progressive

The habitual present describes what someone usually or regularly does. The progressive describes what someone is doing right now, or during a specific, limited period such as "these days." Both use a form of होना as an auxiliary, but the main verb form is different: the habitual uses the -ता/-ती/-ते form, and the progressive uses रहा/रही/रहे after the verb stem.

Language Building Block — Habitual versus progressive contrast
मैं आम तौर पर रोज़ सुबह नौ बजे दफ़्तर जाता हूँ। लेकिन आजकल मैं घर से काम कर रहा हूँ, क्योंकि दफ़्तर में एक नई मीटिंग रूम बन रहा है।
I usually go to the office at nine o'clock every morning. But these days I am working from home, because a new meeting room is being built in the office.

Time markers help signal which aspect is expected: आम तौर पर, रोज़़, and हर हफ़्ते usually accompany the habitual, while आजकल, अभी, and इस हफ़्ते usually accompany the progressive.

4.6 Language Focus — Need and obligation: चाहिए, ज़रूरत है, and पड़ना

चाहिए expresses what someone should do, based on advice, propriety, or general expectation. ज़रूरत है expresses a concrete need for a thing or action. पड़ना, which you will mostly recognize receptively in this chapter, expresses an obligation that arises from circumstances beyond the speaker's control.

Language Building Block — चाहिए and ज़रूरत है in responsibility statements
विक्रम को गुरुवार को घर पर रहना चाहिए, क्योंकि मकान मालिक आएगा। उसे थोड़े समय की ज़रूरत है ताकि वह पहले सफ़ाई कर सके।
Vikram should stay home on Thursday, because the landlord is coming. He needs a little time so that he can clean first.
Recognition only — पड़ना

You may see or hear sentences such as मुझे आज काम पर जल्दी जाना पड़ेगा ("I will have to go to work early today, whether I want to or not"). Recognize the meaning — an obligation forced by circumstance — but you are not expected to produce पड़ना freely yet. Full productive control develops in later chapters.

4.7 Language Focus — सकना for ability and permission

You have already used सकना for physical or learned ability. This chapter extends it to asking and giving permission, a closely related use of the same form.

Language Building Block — Asking permission with सकना
क्या मैं कल छुट्टी ले सकता हूँ? — हाँ, आप ले सकते हैं, लेकिन आपको अपनी रिपोर्ट पहले पूरी करनी चाहिए।
Can I take tomorrow off? — Yes, you can, but you should finish your report first.

4.8 Language Focus — The experiencer/obligation pattern मुझे ... करना है

This pattern, already partly familiar from Volume I, places the person who has an obligation in the को-marked experiencer position rather than as a grammatical subject. The infinitive agrees with the following noun if there is one, or defaults to the masculine singular form if the object is unspecified.

Language Building Block — Obligation with मुझे ... करना है
मुझे आज कपड़े धोने हैं और खाना भी बनाना है। विक्रम को गुरुवार से पहले किराया देना है।
I have to wash clothes today and also cook. Vikram has to pay the rent before Thursday.

Notice that कपड़े धोने हैं shows the infinitive धोना agreeing with the plural masculine noun कपड़े ("clothes"), while खाना बनाना है keeps the default masculine singular form because खाना ("food") is masculine singular. This agreement pattern connects directly to the object-agreement system that becomes fully productive with ने in Chapter 7.

4.9 Language Focus — Frequency and duration with से and तक

Chapter 3 introduced से and तक for physical distance. This chapter reinforces the same postpositions for time duration.

Language Building Block — Duration with से...तक
विक्रम आम तौर पर सुबह नौ बजे से शाम छह बजे तक दफ़्तर में काम करता है, लेकिन इस हफ़्ते वह सोमवार से बुधवार तक घर से काम कर रहा है।
Vikram usually works at the office from nine in the morning until six in the evening, but this week he is working from home from Monday until Wednesday.
Boundary

This chapter does not require productive control of पड़ना, full subjunctive-like conditional politeness, or complex workplace reporting structures. Free production is limited to चाहिए, ज़रूरत है, सकना, and the मुझे ... करना है pattern in familiar responsibility contexts. Full obligation-system flexibility develops further in Chapters 11 and 12.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Vikram calls Priya on the phone to ask for advice about Thursday's landlord visit.

विक्रम प्रिया, मुझे तुमसे एक बात पूछनी है। हमारा मकान मालिक गुरुवार को आ रहा है, और मुझे नहीं पता कि क्या करना है। Priyaa, mujhe tumase ek baat poochhanee hai. Hamaaraa makaan maalik guruvaar ko aa rahaa hai, aur mujhe naheeँ pataa ki kyaa karanaa hai. Priya, I need to ask you something. Our landlord is coming on Thursday, and I don't know what to do.
प्रिया कोई बात नहीं। तुम आम तौर पर गुरुवार को क्या करते हो? Koee baat naheeँ. Tum aam taur par guruvaar ko kyaa karate ho? No problem. What do you usually do on Thursdays?
विक्रम आम तौर पर मैं सुबह नौ बजे से शाम छह बजे तक दफ़्तर में काम करता हूँ। लेकिन इस हफ़्ते मैं घर से काम कर रहा हूँ, तो मुझे लचीलापन है। Aam taur par maiँ subah nau baje se shaam chhah baje tak daftar meँ kaam karataa hooँ. Lekin is hafte maiँ ghar se kaam kar rahaa hooँ, to mujhe lacheelaapan hai. Usually I work at the office from nine in the morning until six in the evening. But this week I am working from home, so I have some flexibility.
प्रिया अच्छा। तुम्हें घर की सफ़ाई भी करनी चाहिए, ख़ासकर रसोई। मकान मालिक हमेशा रसोई देखता है। Achchhaa. Tumheँ ghar kee safaaee bhee karanee chaahiye, khaasakar rasoee. Makaan maalik hameshaa rasoee dekhataa hai. Good. You should also clean the house, especially the kitchen. The landlord always looks at the kitchen.
विक्रम ठीक है। मुझे और अमन को कपड़े भी धोने हैं — पूरा घर थोड़ा अस्त-व्यस्त है। क्या अमन बुधवार को छुट्टी ले सकता है? Theek hai. Mujhe aur Aman ko kapade bhee dhone hain — pooraa ghar thoda ast-vyast hai. Kyaa Aman budhavaar ko chhuttee le sakataa hai? Okay. Aman and I also have to wash clothes — the whole house is a bit disorganized. Can Aman take Wednesday off?
प्रिया यह उसके काम पर निर्भर करता है। लेकिन तुम्हें ज़रूरत है, तो पूछ लो। और एक बात — मकान मालिक को किराया भी याद दिलाना चाहिए, अगर अभी तक नहीं दिया। Yah usake kaam par nirbhar karataa hai. Lekin tumheँ zaroorat hai, to poochh lo. Aur ek baat — makaan maalik ko kiraayaa bhee yaad dilaanaa chaahiye, agar abhee tak naheeँ diyaa. That depends on his work. But you need it, so ask him. And one more thing — you should also remind the landlord about the rent, if it hasn't been paid yet.
विक्रम हमें बुधवार को किराया देना है। तो मुझे सफ़ाई करनी है, कपड़े धोने हैं, और किराया भी तैयार रखना है — बहुत काम है! Hameँ budhavaar ko kiraayaa denaa hai. To mujhe safaaee karanee hai, kapade dhone hain, aur kiraayaa bhee taiyaar rakhanaa hai — bahut kaam hai! We have to pay the rent on Wednesday. So I have to clean, wash clothes, and also keep the rent ready — that's a lot of work!
प्रिया तुम कर सकते हो। बस अमन के साथ काम बाँट लो — दोनों की बारी है, अकेले तुम्हारी नहीं! Tum kar sakate ho. Bas Aman ke saath kaam baँt lo — donoँ kee baaree hai, akele tumhaaree naheeँ! You can do it. Just divide the work with Aman — it's both your turns, not just yours alone!

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. विक्रम आम तौर पर कब से कब तक दफ़्तर में काम करता है? (From when until when does Vikram usually work at the office?)
  2. इस हफ़्ते विक्रम क्यों घर से काम कर रहा है? (Why is Vikram working from home this week?)
  3. विक्रम और अमन को गुरुवार से पहले क्या-क्या करना है? (What all do Vikram and Aman have to do before Thursday?)
  4. प्रिया विक्रम को क्या सलाह देती है? (What advice does Priya give Vikram?)

Reading Practice

Read the short workplace notice below, which brings together routine, obligation, and permission language, then answer the questions.

कार्यालय सूचना — Office Notice
सभी कर्मचारियों के लिए एक सूचना: अगले हफ़्ते की छुट्टियों के बारे में। जिन कर्मचारियों को अगले सोमवार या मंगलवार को छुट्टी चाहिए, उन्हें अपने प्रबंधक को इस शुक्रवार तक बताना है। आम तौर पर छुट्टी की अनुमति मिल जाती है, लेकिन जिन कर्मचारियों की रिपोर्ट अधूरी है, उन्हें पहले अपना काम पूरा करना चाहिए। इस हफ़्ते दफ़्तर में एक नई मीटिंग रूम बन रहा है, इसलिए बुधवार को कुछ कर्मचारियों को घर से काम करना पड़ सकता है। हमें उम्मीद है कि सब लोग एक-दूसरे की मदद कर सकते हैं।

Leave is usually granted, but employees whose reports are incomplete should finish their work first. A new meeting room is being built in the office this week, so some employees may have to work from home on Wednesday. We hope that everyone can help each other.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. What do employees who need Monday or Tuesday off have to do, and by when?
  2. Which employees should finish their work first before requesting leave?
  3. Why might some employees have to work from home on Wednesday?

Writing Workshop

Write a connected paragraph explaining a busy week — your own, a remembered one, or an imagined one.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write eight to ten sentences describing a busy week. Your paragraph must include: (1) at least one habitual sentence describing your usual routine, (2) at least one progressive sentence describing what you are doing this particular week that differs from your routine, (3) at least one sentence with चाहिए or ज़रूरत है, (4) at least one sentence using the pattern मुझे ... करना है, and (5) one duration expression using से...तक.

  1. State your usual routine. Say what you normally do and when, using the habitual.
  2. Say what is different this week. Describe a current activity using the progressive.
  3. Explain a need or obligation. Use चाहिए or ज़रूरत है.
  4. List a task. Use मुझे ... करना है to describe something you have to do.
  5. Give a duration. Say how long something usually takes using से...तक.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • Habitual and progressive forms are used correctly and are clearly distinguished.
  • चाहिए, ज़रूरत है, and मुझे ... करना है are used accurately.
  • The infinitive in मुझे ... करना है agrees correctly with any following noun.
  • से and तक are used correctly for duration.
  • Spelling, matras, and word spacing are correct, and a danda (।) ends each sentence.

Speaking Practice

नियंत्रित अभ्यास — Controlled practice: routine versus this week

Describe five things you usually do, then describe two things you are doing differently this week, using clear habitual and progressive forms out loud.

संचार कार्य — Communicative task: negotiating responsibilities

With a partner, imagine you share a household. Negotiate who will do three tasks this week, using चाहिए, मुझे ... करना है, and at least one question with सकना asking for help or permission.

अकेले अभ्यास — Solo alternative

Record yourself explaining a busy week, including your usual routine, one current change, one obligation, and one duration expression. Listen back and check your habitual/progressive contrast.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Obligation and hierarchy

How directly someone expresses an obligation often depends on hierarchy, family role, and workplace status. A student might explain a deadline to a teacher quite differently than a manager explains one to an employee. चाहिए and ज़रूरत है can both soften a statement of obligation compared to a blunt command, and this softening matters across many Hindi-speaking professional and family contexts.

Culture in Context — Shared households and divided labor

Household responsibilities are divided differently across families, roommates, and generations. Some households follow strict rotations; others divide tasks informally by preference or availability. Do not assume that any single pattern of task-sharing represents all Hindi-speaking households.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Confusing habitual and progressive forms

Error: Writing मैं आजकल दफ़्तर जाता हूँ when the intended meaning is "these days I am working from home" — the habitual form contradicts the time marker आजकल.

Correction: मैं आजकल घर से काम कर रहा हूँ। Match the aspect to the time marker: habitual markers like आम तौर पर need the -ता/-ती/-ते form, while current markers like आजकल usually need the progressive.

Common Mistake — Missing infinitive agreement in मुझे ... करना है

Error: Writing मुझे कपड़े धोना है instead of मुझे कपड़े धोने हैं.

Correction: मुझे कपड़े धोने हैं। When the object is plural masculine (कपड़े), the infinitive and है must agree with it: धोने हैं, not धोना है.

Common Mistake — Treating चाहिए and ज़रूरत है as identical

Error: Writing मुझे पानी चाहिए ज़रूरत है, combining both forms in one sentence.

Correction: Choose one: मुझे पानी चाहिए ("I should have/want water," a general statement) or मुझे पानी की ज़रूरत है ("I need water," a concrete need). Note that ज़रूरत है requires the possessive की before the noun it modifies.
