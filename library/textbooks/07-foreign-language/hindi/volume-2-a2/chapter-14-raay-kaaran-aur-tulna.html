राय, कारण, और तुलना

Volume II, Chapter 14: Opinions, Reasons, and Comparison · Speak and Write Hindi: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Express an opinion politely using frames such as मुझे लगता है कि... and मेरे विचार में....
  2. Compare two or more options, places, people, or services using से ज़्यादा, से कम, and सबसे.
  3. Recognize जितना...उतना comparison patterns in reading and listening.
  4. Give reasons and results using क्योंकि, इसलिए, and इस वजह से.
  5. Agree and disagree with appropriate softening, using expressions such as मैं सहमत हूँ and मुझे ऐसा नहीं लगता.
  6. Make a simple recommendation supported by at least two reasons.
  7. Write a short review or opinion paragraph comparing two options.

Communication Scenario

With the meeting rescheduled to Tuesday, Arjun, Priya, Rohan, and Neha now need to choose where to hold their group project session — the college library, a nearby café, or a friend's apartment. Each person has a different opinion, and the group must weigh convenience, noise, cost, and internet access before agreeing on a recommendation. Priya also wants to know which laptop Neha ended up buying, since Neha compared two options online before deciding.

  1. Notice how each speaker introduces an opinion politely before comparing the options.
  2. Notice how reasons connect directly to the recommendation using क्योंकि and इसलिए.
  3. Notice how disagreement is softened rather than stated bluntly.

Pronunciation and Schwa Deletion

Focus — Listening for stance and reason

In connected opinion speech, listen for the small stance-marking words that come early in the sentence, such as मुझे लगता है or शायद, since these signal that an opinion (not a fact) is about to follow.

Focus — Schwa deletion in evaluative vocabulary

Notice that सुविधाजनक is read as approximately su-vi-dhaa-ja-nak, with the final inherent vowel of नक retained lightly at the end of the word, while महँगा is read as approximately meh-ngaa, with the nasalized vowel carried across the syllable boundary rather than pronounced as a separate n sound.

Focus — Rhythm in clauses with कि, क्योंकि, and इसलिए

Each of these connectors introduces a short pause in natural speech: a brief breath before कि, a slightly longer one before क्योंकि when it begins a new reason, and a falling pitch on इसलिए when it introduces a conclusion.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

14.5 Opinion frames with कि

Chapter 13 introduced कि for reporting what someone said. This chapter uses the same clause pattern to introduce a personal opinion.

Language Building Block — Introducing an opinion
मुझे लगता है कि लाइब्रेरी सबसे सुविधाजनक जगह है। मेरे विचार में, कैफ़े थोड़ा महँगा है। मेरा मानना है कि हमें शांत जगह चुननी चाहिए।
I think that the library is the most convenient place. In my view, the café is a bit expensive. I believe that we should choose a quiet place.

Form: [opinion frame] + कि + [complete clause with subject, verb, and its own agreement]. मुझे लगता है कि... is the everyday default. मेरे विचार में... does not require कि and instead precedes a normal complete sentence. मेरा मानना है कि... is somewhat more formal and works well in writing.

14.6 Comparison with से ज़्यादा, से कम, and सबसे

Language Building Block — Comparing two options
कैफ़े लाइब्रेरी से ज़्यादा महँगा है, लेकिन लाइब्रेरी कैफ़े से ज़्यादा शांत है। सब जगहों में लाइब्रेरी सबसे सुविधाजनक है।
The café is more expensive than the library, but the library is quieter than the café. Among all the places, the library is the most convenient.

Form: [X] + [Y] + से ज़्यादा/कम + [adjective] + है compares X to Y. The item after which you place से is the standard of comparison. For the superlative, use सबसे immediately before the adjective. Variable adjectives such as अच्छा still agree with the noun being described; invariable adjectives such as सुविधाजनक and शांत do not change form.

Recognition note: जितना...उतना ("the more... the more...") appears in some reading and listening passages, as in जितना सस्ता, उतना अच्छा ("the cheaper, the better"). This pattern is recognition-only at this stage.

14.7 Reasons with क्योंकि and results with इसलिए

Language Building Block — Giving a reason and a result
मुझे लाइब्रेरी पसंद है क्योंकि वहाँ इंटरनेट अच्छा है। कैफ़े में बहुत शोर होता है, इसलिए मुझे वहाँ काम करना मुश्किल लगता है।
I like the library because the internet is good there. There's a lot of noise in the café, so I find it difficult to work there.

Form and use: क्योंकि normally follows the main clause and introduces the reason: [result] + क्योंकि + [reason]. इसलिए works in the opposite direction: [reason] + इसलिए + [result]. इस वजह से can replace इसलिए in slightly more formal writing, such as a written recommendation.

14.8 Agreement and disagreement

Language Building Block — Responding to an opinion
मैं सहमत हूँ, लाइब्रेरी अच्छी जगह है। हो सकता है, लेकिन मुझे ऐसा नहीं लगता — वहाँ शाम को बहुत भीड़ होती है। शायद हमें कोई और जगह देखनी चाहिए।
I agree, the library is a good place. That may be, but I don't think so — it gets very crowded there in the evening. Maybe we should look at somewhere else.

Use: Agreement can be direct: मैं सहमत हूँ. Disagreement is normally softened rather than stated as a flat contradiction. A concessive opener such as हो सकता है, लेकिन... acknowledges the other person's point before offering a different view. मुझे ऐसा नहीं लगता is the everyday polite way to disagree; it is gentler than a direct denial.

14.9 Making a recommendation

Language Building Block — Recommending with reasons
मेरा सुझाव है कि हम लाइब्रेरी चुनें, क्योंकि वहाँ इंटरनेट अच्छा है और माहौल शांत है।
My suggestion is that we choose the library, because the internet is good there and the atmosphere is quiet.

Form: मेरा सुझाव है कि... + [subjunctive-like verb form, e.g., चुनें] is a formulaic recommendation frame at this level. Support the recommendation with at least one reason using क्योंकि.

Boundary

This chapter does not introduce advanced argumentation, debate rhetoric, statistical claims, formal academic essay structure, or the full productive range of comparative constructions. Those extensions belong to later volumes.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Arjun, Priya, Rohan, and Neha discuss where to hold their group project session.

प्रिया हमें मंगलवार को मिलना है। मुझे लगता है कि हमें लाइब्रेरी में मिलना चाहिए। We're meeting on Tuesday. I think we should meet in the library.
रोहन हो सकता है, लेकिन मुझे ऐसा नहीं लगता। लाइब्रेरी में बात करना मुश्किल है, क्योंकि वहाँ चुप रहना पड़ता है। That may be, but I don't think so. It's hard to talk in the library, because you have to stay quiet there.
अर्जुन मेरे विचार में, कैफ़े बेहतर है — वहाँ बात भी कर सकते हैं और कॉफ़ी भी मिल जाती है। In my view, the café is better — you can talk there and get coffee too.
नेहा लेकिन कैफ़े लाइब्रेरी से ज़्यादा महँगा है, और वहाँ इंटरनेट भी उतना अच्छा नहीं है। But the café is more expensive than the library, and the internet isn't as good there either.
प्रिया तो फिर मेरा सुझाव है कि हम लाइब्रेरी का एक ग्रुप कमरा लें। वहाँ बात भी कर सकते हैं, और इंटरनेट भी सबसे अच्छा है। Then my suggestion is that we book a group room at the library. We can talk there too, and the internet is the best.
रोहन ठीक है, मैं सहमत हूँ। यह सबसे अच्छा विकल्प लगता है। Okay, I agree. This seems like the best option.
नेहा वैसे, अर्जुन, तुमने कौन-सा लैपटॉप खरीदा? तुम दो लैपटॉप की तुलना कर रहे थे न? By the way, Arjun, which laptop did you buy? You were comparing two laptops, right?
अर्जुन मैंने वही खरीदा जो सस्ता था, क्योंकि दूसरा मेरे बजट से ज़्यादा महँगा था। I bought the one that was cheaper, because the other one was more expensive than my budget.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. रोहन लाइब्रेरी में मिलने से क्यों असहमत है? (Why does Rohan disagree with meeting in the library?)
  2. समूह ने आख़िर में क्या फ़ैसला किया? (What did the group decide in the end?)
  3. अर्जुन ने कौन-सा लैपटॉप खरीदा और क्यों? (Which laptop did Arjun buy, and why?)

Writing Workshop

Write a short recommendation titled मेरी सिफ़ारिश, comparing two options (places, services, products, or activities) and recommending one.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write a connected text of 100-150 words in standard Devanagari, in complete sentences. Your text must include: (1) a clear opinion using an opinion frame such as मुझे लगता है कि..., (2) at least one comparison using से ज़्यादा, से कम, or सबसे, (3) at least two reasons using क्योंकि and/or इसलिए, and (4) a final recommendation.

  1. Choose your topic. Pick two options you can genuinely compare.
  2. State your opinion. Open with a clear opinion frame.
  3. Compare the options. Use at least one comparison structure.
  4. Give your reasons. Support your opinion with क्योंकि and इसलिए.
  5. Close with a recommendation. State clearly which option you recommend.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • The opinion frame and its कि-clause form a complete, natural sentence.
  • The comparison structure is used correctly and agrees where needed.
  • At least two reasons are expressed clearly.
  • The recommendation is stated directly at the end.
  • Spelling, matras, word spacing, and dandas are correct throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Softened disagreement

Direct contradiction is often softened in Hindi conversation, especially among peers who want to preserve a comfortable relationship. Openers such as हो सकता है, लेकिन... acknowledge the other person's view before offering a different one. This is a matter of social ease rather than a fixed rule, and directness varies by relationship, setting, and individual style.

Culture in Context — Group decisions and shared reasoning

In many everyday Hindi-speaking contexts, a group decision — such as where to study or which service to use — is often reached by weighing everyone's stated reasons together rather than by one person deciding alone. Giving a reason, not just an opinion, is a normal and expected part of the conversation.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Reversing the standard of comparison

Error: कैफ़े से ज़्यादा लाइब्रेरी महँगी है when the intended meaning is that the café is more expensive than the library.

Correction: कैफ़े लाइब्रेरी से ज़्यादा महँगा है। The item placed directly before से is the standard being compared to, not the subject of the sentence.

Common Mistake — Dropping कि after an opinion frame

Error: Writing मुझे लगता है लाइब्रेरी अच्छी जगह है without कि.

Correction: मुझे लगता है कि लाइब्रेरी अच्छी जगह है। As with reported speech in Chapter 13, कि is required to introduce the clause in standard written Hindi.

Common Mistake — Disagreeing too bluntly

Error: Responding to an opinion with only नहीं, यह ग़लत है। ("No, that's wrong.")

Correction: हो सकता है, लेकिन मुझे ऐसा नहीं लगता, क्योंकि... Softening the disagreement and giving a reason keeps the exchange polite and constructive.
