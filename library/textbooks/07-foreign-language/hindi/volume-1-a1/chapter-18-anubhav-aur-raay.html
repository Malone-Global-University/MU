अनुभव और राय

Volume I, Chapter 18: Experiences and Opinions · Speak and Write Hindi: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Talk about simple past experiences using a supported मैंने ... किया है-type pattern in limited contexts.
  2. Compare two people, places, or things using से ज़्यादा, से कम, and सबसे.
  3. State an opinion with मुझे लगता है कि... in a controlled frame.
  4. Give a simple reason using क्योंकि.
  5. Make a short recommendation.
  6. Read a short review or opinion message in Devanagari.

Communication Scenario

A few days after Rohan's cousin's birthday party, Priya and Rohan talk about two restaurants they have each tried near their neighborhood. Priya compares the food, the price, and the service, gives her opinion, and recommends one restaurant over the other. Rohan shares a different experience and offers a reason for his preference.

  1. Notice how comparison words come before the adjective: यह जगह उस जगह से ज़्यादा अच्छी है.
  2. Notice how मुझे लगता है कि... introduces a personal opinion, and क्योंकि introduces the reason behind it.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 18 continues Unit IV, अनुभव, योजना और कहानी. Chapter 17 introduced productive future forms, intention with चाहना, and the formulaic वाला है/हैं pattern. Chapter 18 turns to comparison, opinion, and supported experience, drawing together preferences, past narration, and future language from earlier chapters into more evaluative communication.

Coming Into This Chapter

You can make future plans with जाना and आना, express intention with चाहना, describe an already-arranged plan with वाला है/हैं, and invite, accept, decline, and reschedule politely.

New in This Chapter

Comparison becomes productive for the first time, using से ज़्यादा, से कम, and सबसे. You will also state opinions with मुझे लगता है कि..., give reasons with क्योंकि, and use a supported experience pattern in limited contexts.

Looking Forward

Chapter 19, कहानी, कारण और संबंध, connects sentences into fuller explanations and short stories, expanding reason, contrast, and condition into more connected discourse.

Devanagari and Pronunciation Focus

This chapter introduces no new letters or matras. It focuses on the rhythm of comparison phrases and the intonation of opinion and disagreement.

Pronunciation Focus — Schwa deletion in comparison words

Review schwa deletion in महँगा, सस्ता, and बढ़िया. Read each word as a natural spoken form, paying attention to the nasalized vowel in महँगा.

Pronunciation Focus — Phrase grouping in comparison sentences

Practice grouping इस रेस्तराँ से as one phrase before adding ज़्यादा अच्छा है. Comparison sentences read more naturally when the से-phrase is grouped closely with the noun it follows.

Pronunciation Focus — Intonation for opinion and polite disagreement

Listen for the thoughtful, slightly falling tone of मुझे लगता है कि... compared with a more direct statement. Polite disagreement often softens with a pause and a courteous opener rather than a flat contradiction.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

18.4 Language Focus — Comparison with से ज़्यादा, से कम, and सबसे

Hindi comparison uses the postposition से together with ज़्यादा (more) or कम (less) to compare two items. सबसे marks the superlative, meaning "the most" or "the -est," and does not require से.

Language Building Block — Comparing two items
यह रेस्तराँ उस रेस्तराँ से ज़्यादा अच्छा है। वह जगह इस जगह से कम महँगी है।
This restaurant is better than that restaurant. That place is less expensive than this place.
Language Building Block — Superlative with सबसे
यह शहर का सबसे अच्छा रेस्तराँ है।
This is the best restaurant in the city.

This chapter limits productive comparison practice to adjectives already familiar from earlier chapters, such as अच्छा, बढ़िया, सस्ता, and महँगा, in controlled sentences with two or three familiar nouns.

18.5 Language Focus — Opinions with मुझे लगता है कि...

मुझे लगता है कि... introduces a personal opinion in a supported frame, similar in structure to the experiencer patterns already familiar from Chapter 12. The clause after कि follows ordinary sentence order.

Language Building Block — Opinion pattern
मुझे लगता है कि यह रेस्तराँ बहुत अच्छा है।
I think that this restaurant is very good.

18.6 Language Focus — Reasons with क्योंकि

क्योंकि connects a statement to its reason. It is placed between the two clauses, and each clause keeps ordinary Hindi word order.

Language Building Block — Reason pattern
मुझे यह जगह पसंद है क्योंकि खाना सस्ता और बढ़िया है।
I like this place because the food is cheap and excellent.

18.7 Language Focus — Supported experience with मैंने ... किया है

This chapter introduces a limited, controlled experience pattern built on the perfective forms already familiar from Chapters 14 and 16. Learners use it only with a small set of familiar transitive verbs to talk about something they have tried or done.

Language Building Block — Supported experience pattern
मैंने वह फ़िल्म देखी है। मैंने उस रेस्तराँ में खाना खाया है।
I have seen that film. I have eaten at that restaurant.

This pattern is limited, not a full present-perfect system. This chapter restricts productive use to familiar transitive verbs such as देखना and खाना, and does not require independent production of the full range of perfective agreement or object-marking conditions.

Boundary

This chapter does not require advanced subjunctive forms, literary conditionals, full relative-correlative production, or advanced argumentative essays. Productive practice remains limited to comparison with familiar adjectives, the मुझे लगता है कि... opinion frame, क्योंकि reason clauses, and the supported experience pattern with familiar verbs.

Model Dialogue

Setting: A few days after the party, Priya and Rohan compare two restaurants near their neighborhood.

प्रिया रोहन, क्या आपने वह नया रेस्तराँ देखा है, बाज़ार के पास वाला? Rohan, kyaa aapane vah nayaa restaraaँ dekhaa hai, baazaar ke paas vaalaa? Rohan, have you seen that new restaurant, the one near the market?
रोहन हाँ, मैंने वहाँ खाना खाया है। मुझे लगता है कि खाना बढ़िया है, लेकिन थोड़ा महँगा भी है। Haaँ, maiँne vahaँ khaanaa khaayaa hai. Mujhe lagataa hai ki khaanaa baढiyaa hai, lekin thoड़aa mahaँgaa bhee hai. Yes, I have eaten there. I think the food is excellent, but it's also a bit expensive.
प्रिया सच में? मुझे तो पुराना रेस्तराँ ज़्यादा पसंद है। वह इस नए रेस्तराँ से सस्ता है, और सेवा भी अच्छी है। Sach meँ? Mujhe to puraanaa restaraaँ zyaadaa pasand hai. Vah is nae restaraaँ se sastaa hai, aur sevaa bhee achchee hai. Really? I actually like the old restaurant more. It's cheaper than this new restaurant, and the service is good too.
रोहन यह सच है, लेकिन मुझे लगता है कि नए रेस्तराँ का खाना ज़्यादा अच्छा है, क्योंकि वहाँ खाना ताज़ा बनता है। Yah sach hai, lekin mujhe lagataa hai ki nae restaraaँ kaa khaanaa zyaadaa achchaa hai, kyoँki vahaँ khaanaa taazaa banataa hai. That's true, but I think the new restaurant's food is better, because the food is made fresh there.
प्रिया ठीक है, तो मेरी सलाह है कि हम दोनों जगह एक बार फिर जाएँ, और फिर तय करें। Theek hai, to meree salaah hai ki ham donoँ jagah ek baar phir jaaeँ, aur phir tay kareँ. Okay, so my suggestion is that we go to both places once more, and then decide.
रोहन अच्छा विचार है! मुझे लगता है कि पुराना रेस्तराँ सबसे अच्छी जगह है परिवार के साथ जाने के लिए। Achchhaa vichaar hai! Mujhe lagataa hai ki puraanaa restaraaँ sabase achchee jagah hai parivaar ke saath jaane ke lie. Good idea! I think the old restaurant is the best place to go with family.
प्रिया और नया रेस्तराँ शायद दोस्तों के साथ जाने के लिए सबसे अच्छा है! Aur nayaa restaraaँ shaayad dostoँ ke saath jaane ke lie sabase achchaa hai! And the new restaurant is maybe the best for going with friends!

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. रोहन को नए रेस्तराँ के बारे में क्या लगता है? (What does Rohan think about the new restaurant?)
  2. प्रिया को पुराना रेस्तराँ ज़्यादा पसंद क्यों है? (Why does Priya like the old restaurant more?)
  3. वे दोनों आख़िर में क्या करने का फ़ैसला करते हैं? (What do they finally decide to do?)

Reading Practice

रेस्तराँ की समीक्षा — Restaurant Review
मैंने इस रेस्तराँ में कल खाना खाया है। खाना बढ़िया था और सेवा भी अच्छी थी। मुझे लगता है कि यह जगह शहर के सबसे अच्छे रेस्तराँ में से एक है। कीमत थोड़ी ज़्यादा है, लेकिन खाने की गुणवत्ता के कारण यह उचित लगती है। मेरी सलाह है कि आप ज़रूर यहाँ जाएँ।
I ate at this restaurant yesterday. The food was excellent and the service was good too. I think this place is one of the best restaurants in the city. The price is a little high, but it seems reasonable because of the quality of the food. My recommendation is that you should definitely go here.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. What is the reviewer's overall opinion of the restaurant?
  2. What reason does the reviewer give for the higher price seeming reasonable?
  3. What does the reviewer recommend?

Writing Workshop

Write a short comparison and recommendation about two places, activities, or experiences you know.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write six to eight sentences: (1) mention one experience using the supported मैंने ... किया है-type pattern, (2) compare two things using से ज़्यादा, से कम, or सबसे, (3) state your opinion with मुझे लगता है कि..., (4) give one reason with क्योंकि, and (5) end with a short recommendation.

  1. Introduce your experience. Begin with मैंने ... है and name what you saw, ate, or tried.
  2. Compare. Use से ज़्यादा, से कम, or सबसे with a familiar adjective.
  3. State your opinion. Use मुझे लगता है कि....
  4. Give a reason. Use क्योंकि to explain your opinion.
  5. Recommend. Close with मेरी सलाह है कि... or a similar recycled courtesy formula.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • The supported experience pattern is used only with familiar transitive verbs from this chapter or earlier chapters.
  • Comparison words appear in the correct order: noun + से + ज़्यादा/कम + adjective.
  • The opinion clause after मुझे लगता है कि follows ordinary Hindi word order.
  • At least one reason is given with क्योंकि.
  • A danda (।) ends each sentence, and spelling matches this chapter's vocabulary.

Speaking Practice

नियंत्रित अभ्यास — Controlled practice: substitution drill

Practice the pattern यह ___ उस ___ से ज़्यादा अच्छा है by substituting different nouns: रेस्तराँ, फ़िल्म, जगह. Then restate each as an opinion with मुझे लगता है कि....

संचार कार्य — Communicative task: comparing experiences

With a partner, take turns describing an experience you have each had, comparing two options, and giving a reason for your opinion. One partner should politely disagree and offer an alternative view.

अकेले अभ्यास — Solo alternative

Record yourself comparing two places or experiences you know, stating your opinion, and giving a reason. Listen back and check your comparison word order and adjective agreement.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Softening disagreement

Directly contradicting another person's opinion can feel abrupt in many Hindi-speaking social contexts. Framing a differing view with यह सच है, लेकिन... or मुझे लगता है कि... softens disagreement while still stating a clear opinion, though exact expectations vary by relationship and setting.

Culture in Context — Recommendations among friends and family

Recommending restaurants, films, and other experiences is a common everyday activity across Hindi-speaking communities, but preferences and priorities vary widely by family, budget, region, and personal taste. Avoid presenting any single opinion as the universal standard.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Reversing the comparison word order

Error: Saying यह ज़्यादा अच्छा से वह है.

Correction: The से-phrase comes before ज़्यादा/कम and the adjective: यह उससे ज़्यादा अच्छा है.

Common Mistake — Using सबसे with only two items

Error: Saying यह रेस्तराँ उससे सबसे अच्छा है when comparing only two restaurants.

Correction: Use से ज़्यादा for two items: यह रेस्तराँ उससे ज़्यादा अच्छा है. Reserve सबसे for comparing three or more.

Common Mistake

Error: Overusing the supported experience pattern with unfamiliar verbs, such as forcing it onto verbs not yet practiced.

Correction: Keep this pattern to familiar verbs such as देखना and खाना, and use the simple perfective from Chapter 16 for other narration.
