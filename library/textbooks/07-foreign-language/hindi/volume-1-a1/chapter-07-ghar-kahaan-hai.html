घर कहाँ है?

Volume I, Chapter 7: Home and Location · Speak and Write Hindi: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify rooms, household objects, and common belongings in Devanagari.
  2. State where something or someone is using में, पर, and के पास.
  3. Ask and answer simple कहाँ? questions about a home or neighborhood.
  4. Expand मेरे पास ... है to say what you have and what you do not have.
  5. Recognize that some nouns change form before certain postpositions.
  6. Describe a simple neighborhood using familiar location words.
  7. Read a short housing note or neighborhood description in Devanagari.
  8. Write a short description of a home and its location relative to familiar places.

Communication Scenario

Priya is visiting Anil's home for the first time. Anil shows her around, pointing out the living room, the kitchen, and his own room, and mentioning where a few of his things are. Afterward, on the way out, Anil describes the neighborhood: what is near the house, what is across from it, and what is behind it.

  1. This chapter introduces three closely related postpositions of place — में, पर, and के पास — along with के सामने, के पीछे, and के अंदर in controlled, formulaic phrases.
  2. You will also begin noticing that some nouns look slightly different right before these postpositions. This is your first exposure to the oblique form. You are not expected to control the full oblique system yet — only to recognize it in familiar words.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 7 continues Unit II, रोज़मर्रा की ज़िंदगी. It builds on the food and drink language of Chapter 6 and introduces the first postpositions of the volume.

Coming Into This Chapter

You can say what you like, dislike, need, and eat or drink using मुझे ... पसंद है, मुझे ... चाहिए, and a first habitual verb frame. You can also express simple possession with मेरे पास ... है from Chapter 4.

New in This Chapter

Rooms, household objects, and neighborhood places; the postpositions में, पर, के पास, के सामने, के पीछे, and के अंदर; कहाँ? questions; first oblique singular awareness; and expanded मेरे पास ... है/नहीं है.

Looking Forward

Chapter 8, बाज़ार में, introduces shopping, prices, quantities with कितना/कितनी, and your first look at nukta letters.

Devanagari and Pronunciation

Chapter 7 introduces no new independent vowels or matras. It does introduce a small set of high-frequency conjuncts that appear in this chapter's vocabulary, and it continues building schwa deletion awareness.

7.4 Conjuncts in home and location words

स्कूल
बिस्तर
फ़्लैट

Notice the conjunct स्क in स्कूल and स्त-type clusters that you will see more of in later chapters. For now, simply recognize that two consonants can combine into a single visual unit, as introduced in Chapter 4.

Pronunciation Focus — Schwa deletion in home vocabulary

Notice schwa deletion again in familiar-looking words: कमरा is pronounced closer to "kamraa," and घर keeps its final consonant without an extra vowel sound, the same way खाना did in Chapter 6. This remains an awareness system, not yet a complete rule.

Pronunciation Focus — Oblique forms sound slightly different

कमरा becomes कमरे में ("in the room"). The final आ changes to ए before में. This small, regular change is part of the oblique form, introduced formally in the next section. Listen for this shift as you read the examples below.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces location postpositions and a first, limited look at oblique forms. You are not yet expected to control the full oblique paradigm — only to recognize and reproduce it in the controlled patterns below.

7.5 Saying where something is with में and पर

Language Building Block — में and पर
किताब मेज़ पर है। फ़ोन कमरे में है।
The book is on the table. The phone is in the room.
Language Focus — में versus पर

में means "in" or "inside," and पर means "on" or "at." Like all Hindi postpositions, they follow the noun rather than coming before it, unlike English prepositions. Keep your location phrases close to the models in this chapter for now; the full range of meanings for these postpositions is developed further starting in Chapter 11.

7.6 First look at the oblique form

Language Focus — Why कमरा becomes कमरे

Some Hindi nouns change form right before certain postpositions. This changed form is called the oblique form. You already saw a small hint of this with मेरे पास in Chapter 4. Here, notice the same pattern: कमरा ("room") becomes कमरे before में and पर. Feminine nouns like मेज़ ("table") do not change in the singular. For this chapter, simply recognize and reproduce the oblique forms shown in the vocabulary and examples. A fuller explanation of the oblique system continues to develop across Chapters 8–15.

घरघरघर में (no change)
बाज़ारबाज़ारबाज़ार के पास (no change)

7.7 Location with के पास, के सामने, के पीछे, and के अंदर

Language Building Block — Formulaic location phrases with के
पार्क घर के पास है। दुकान स्कूल के सामने है।
The park is near the house. The shop is across from the school.

Learn के पास (near), के सामने (in front of / across from), के पीछे (behind), and के अंदर (inside) as formulaic phrases for now, following the same के pattern as मेरे पास from Chapter 4. Full possessive-agreement analysis of का/की/के continues to develop in later chapters.

7.8 Asking कहाँ?

Language Building Block — कहाँ? questions
घर कहाँ है? किताब कहाँ है?
Where is the house? Where is the book?

Answer कहाँ? questions using the location phrases above: घर पार्क के पास है। ("The house is near the park.")

7.9 Expanding possession with मेरे पास

Language Building Block — मेरे पास ... है / नहीं है
मेरे पास एक बड़ा कमरा है। मेरे पास अलमारी नहीं है।
I have a big room. I don't have a cupboard.

This expands the formulaic pattern from Chapter 4 into both affirmative and negative statements. It remains formulaic; the full existential-possession system continues to develop in later chapters.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Anil shows Priya around his home, then describes the neighborhood outside.

अनिल यह हमारी बैठक है। मेरा कमरा वहाँ है। Yah hamaaree baiṭhak hai. Meraa kamraa vahaaँ hai. This is our living room. My room is there.
प्रिया तुम्हारे कमरे में क्या है? Tumhaare kamre meँ kyaa hai? What is in your room?
अनिल मेरे कमरे में एक बिस्तर, एक मेज़, और एक अलमारी है। मेरी किताबें मेज़ पर हैं। Mere kamre meँ ek bistar, ek mez, aur ek almaaree hai. Meree kitaabeँ mez par haiँ. In my room there is a bed, a table, and a cupboard. My books are on the table.
प्रिया अच्छा। और तुम्हारा घर कहाँ है? Achchhaa. Aur tumhaaraa ghar kahaaँ hai? Nice. And where is your house?
अनिल हमारा घर एक पार्क के पास है। एक दुकान घर के सामने है, और स्कूल घर के पीछे है। Hamaaraa ghar ek paark ke paas hai. Ek dukaan ghar ke saamne hai, aur skool ghar ke peechhe hai. Our house is near a park. A shop is across from the house, and the school is behind the house.
प्रिया बहुत अच्छा पड़ोस है! Bahut achchhaa paḍos hai! It's a very nice neighborhood!

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. अनिल के कमरे में क्या है? (What is in Anil's room?)
  2. अनिल की किताबें कहाँ हैं? (Where are Anil's books?)
  3. घर के सामने क्या है? (What is in front of the house?)

Writing Workshop

Write a short description of a home — real or imagined — combining this chapter's location language with vocabulary and patterns from earlier chapters.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write four to six sentences in Devanagari describing a home, what is in it, and where it is located relative to two neighborhood places.

  1. Name the home and one or two rooms. Use यह ... है or मेरा घर ... है.
  2. State what is in a room. Use ... कमरे में है or ... मेज़ पर है.
  3. State one thing you have and one thing you do not have. Use मेरे पास ... है and मेरे पास ... नहीं है.
  4. Locate the house relative to two places. Use two of के पास, के सामने, or के पीछे.
  5. Check your Devanagari. Compare your spelling of home and location words against the vocabulary tables above, and check any oblique forms like कमरे.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • में or पर appears at least once, correctly.
  • At least one of के पास, के सामने, or के पीछे appears correctly.
  • मेरे पास ... है and मेरे पास ... नहीं है both appear correctly.
  • Home and location words are spelled correctly in Devanagari, without unnecessary romanization.
  • A danda (।) ends each sentence.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Homes vary widely

Housing arrangements among Hindi-speaking communities vary enormously by region, income, city or village setting, and family structure. Some households include extended family across generations; others do not. This chapter's home is one example, not a universal Hindi-speaking home.

Culture in Context — Urban and rural neighborhoods

Neighborhood layout, distances, and nearby places differ between dense urban settings and rural areas. A market, school, or park may be a short walk away in one setting and much farther in another. Treat the vocabulary in this chapter as flexible tools for describing many kinds of neighborhoods.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Putting the postposition before the noun

Error: Following English word order and saying में कमरे for "in the room."

Correction: Hindi postpositions follow the noun: कमरे में. Unlike English prepositions, में, पर, and similar words always come after the noun they relate to.

Common Mistake — Forgetting the oblique change

Error: Saying कमरा में instead of कमरे में.

Correction: Masculine nouns ending in आ, such as कमरा, change to ए before postpositions like में and पर: कमरे में, कमरे पर. Not every noun changes this way — घर stays the same.

Common Mistake — Confusing में and पर

Error: Saying किताब मेज़ में है when the book is on top of the table.

Correction: Use पर for "on" a surface: किताब मेज़ पर है. Reserve में for "in" or "inside": किताब अलमारी में है ("The book is in the cupboard").
