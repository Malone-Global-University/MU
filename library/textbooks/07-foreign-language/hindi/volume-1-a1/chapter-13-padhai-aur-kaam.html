पढ़ाई और काम

Volume I, Chapter 13: Study and Work · Speak and Write Hindi: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe school, study, work, and technology activities.
  2. Say what you and others are doing right now using the progressive रहा/रही/रहे.
  3. Express ability with सकना in controlled frames.
  4. Make polite requests in institutional settings, such as क्या आप ... सकते हैं?
  5. Read a short announcement, notice, or schedule about school or work.
  6. Write a simple, polite request message.

Communication Scenario

Priya is at her office. Her colleague Vikram stops by her desk to ask what she is working on, and she explains a task with a deadline. Later, Priya emails her Hindi instructor to ask politely whether she can reschedule a class.

  1. Notice how Priya and Vikram describe activities in progress using रहा/रही/रहे with हूँ, है, and हैं.
  2. Notice how Priya asks politely for something using सकना in her email.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 13 continues Unit III, शहर, स्वास्थ्य, पढ़ाई और काम. It builds on the polite request formula क्या आप ... सकते हैं? from Chapter 11 and develops the formulaic progressive you met in Chapter 12's feeling expressions into a full productive system.

Coming Into This Chapter

You can make polite requests with क्या आप ... सकते हैं?, describe how you feel using मुझे ... है and the formulaic लग रहा है, and use habitual present verbs for routines.

New in This Chapter

School, work, and technology vocabulary; the progressive रहा/रही/रहे as a full productive system with auxiliary agreement; ability with सकना; infinitive complements such as काम करना and ईमेल लिखना; and polite institutional requests.

Looking Forward

Devanagari and Pronunciation

This chapter introduces no new letters or matras. It develops the rhythm of progressive verb phrases and continues schwa deletion practice with study and work vocabulary.

Pronunciation Focus — Rhythm of रहा/रही/रहे phrases

Progressive phrases such as कर रहा हूँ and लिख रही है are read as smooth, connected units rather than three separate words. Practice linking the main verb, रहा/रही/रहे, and the auxiliary without pausing between them.

Pronunciation Focus — Schwa deletion in study and work verbs

Practice reading पढ़ना, लिखना, and भेजना aloud. Remember that the final अ of these infinitives is not pronounced: पढ़ना is read with two syllables, not three.

Pronunciation Focus — English loanwords in Devanagari

कंप्यूटर, मीटिंग, and प्रोजेक्ट keep Hindi rhythm and vowel quality even though they come from English. Avoid pronouncing them with full English stress patterns; keep syllables even, as in ordinary Hindi words.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

13.5 The progressive: रहा/रही/रहे

Language Building Block — Progressive with auxiliary agreement
मैं काम कर रहा/रही हूँ। वह ईमेल लिख रही है। हम रिपोर्ट पढ़ रहे हैं।
I am working. She is writing an email. We are reading the report.
Language Focus — Building the progressive

The Hindi progressive combines the verb stem, the participle रहा/रही/रहे, and an auxiliary form of होना (हूँ, है, हैं). The participle agrees with the grammatical subject in gender and number: रहा for masculine singular, रही for feminine singular, and रहे for masculine plural or mixed groups. You met this pattern formulaically in Chapter 12 with feeling expressions such as मुझे अच्छा लग रहा है. This chapter analyzes the full pattern and extends it to ordinary action verbs.

13.6 Asking about current activity

Language Building Block — आप क्या कर रहे/रही हैं?
आप क्या कर रहे हैं? मैं एक ईमेल लिख रहा हूँ।
What are you doing? I am writing an email.

Use क्या with the progressive to ask what someone is doing right now. Answer with the same pattern, matching the participle to your own gender.

13.7 Ability with सकना

Language Building Block — Verb stem + सकना
मैं हिंदी बोल सकता/सकती हूँ। क्या आप कल आ सकते हैं?
I can speak Hindi. Can you come tomorrow?
Language Focus — सकना as an ability auxiliary

सकना follows the verb stem directly, without an infinitive ending: बोल सकना, not बोलना सकना. सकना itself conjugates for tense, and its participle agrees with the subject, similarly to other verbs. At this stage, focus on present-time frames such as सकता/सकती हूँ, सकते हैं, and सकते हैं? in questions.

13.8 Infinitive complements

Language Building Block — Infinitive as object
मुझे काम करना है। वह हिंदी पढ़ना चाहती है।
I have to work. She wants to study Hindi.

Infinitives such as काम करना, हिंदी पढ़ना, and ईमेल लिखना can function as the object of another verb or expression, including चाहना and है-based obligation patterns from earlier chapters. The infinitive itself does not change for the subject; only the surrounding auxiliary or verb agrees.

13.9 Polite institutional requests

Language Building Block — Expanded क्या आप ... सकते हैं?
क्या आप कल फिर से आ सकते हैं? क्या आप यह रिपोर्ट कल तक भेज सकते हैं?
Can you come again tomorrow? Can you send this report by tomorrow?

You met क्या आप ... सकते हैं? in Chapter 11 for asking someone to help with directions. This chapter extends the same pattern to school and work requests, such as rescheduling an appointment or asking a colleague to complete a task.

Boundary: This chapter does not introduce a full imperative-level system, advanced honorific verb morphology, compound verbs, or formal bureaucratic Hindi. सकना is developed only in controlled present-time frames.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Vikram stops by Priya's desk at the office.

विक्रम नमस्ते प्रिया जी। आप क्या कर रही हैं? Namaste Priyaa jee. Aap kyaa kar rahee haiँ? Hello Priya ji. What are you doing?
प्रिया नमस्ते विक्रम जी। मैं एक रिपोर्ट लिख रही हूँ। आख़िरी तारीख़ कल है। Namaste Vikram jee. Maiँ ek riporṭ likh rahee hooँ. Aakhiree taareekh kal hai. Hello Vikram ji. I am writing a report. The deadline is tomorrow.
विक्रम अच्छा। क्या मैं कुछ मदद कर सकता हूँ? Achchaa. Kyaa maiँ kuchh madad kar sakataa hooँ? I see. Can I help with something?
प्रिया हाँ, धन्यवाद। क्या आप यह ईमेल कल तक भेज सकते हैं? Haaँ, dhanyavaad. Kyaa aap yah eemel kal tak bhej sakate haiँ? Yes, thank you. Can you send this email by tomorrow?
विक्रम जी हाँ, मैं भेज सकता हूँ। कोई बात नहीं। Jee haaँ, maiँ bhej sakataa hooँ. Koee baat naheeँ. Yes, I can send it. No problem.

Setting: Priya emails her Hindi instructor to ask about rescheduling a class.

प्रिया (ईमेल) नमस्ते सर। मुझे माफ़ कीजिए, पर मैं कल की कक्षा में नहीं आ सकती। क्या हम कक्षा का समय बदल सकते हैं? Namaste sar. Mujhe maaf keejie, par maiँ kal kee kakshaa meँ naheeँ aa sakatee. Kyaa ham kakshaa kaa samay badal sakate haiँ? Hello sir. Please excuse me, but I cannot come to tomorrow's class. Can we change the class time?
शिक्षक (ईमेल) नमस्ते प्रिया जी। कोई बात नहीं। क्या आप शुक्रवार को आ सकती हैं? Namaste Priyaa jee. Koee baat naheeँ. Kyaa aap shukravaar ko aa sakatee haiँ? Hello Priya ji. No problem. Can you come on Friday?
प्रिया (ईमेल) जी हाँ, मैं शुक्रवार को आ सकती हूँ। धन्यवाद। Jee haaँ, maiँ shukravaar ko aa sakatee hooँ. Dhanyavaad. Yes, I can come on Friday. Thank you.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. प्रिया क्या कर रही है? (What is Priya doing?)
  2. विक्रम क्या भेज सकता है? (What can Vikram send?)
  3. प्रिया शुक्रवार को क्या कर सकती है? (What can Priya do on Friday?)

Writing Workshop

Write a short, polite request message in Devanagari to a teacher, classmate, or colleague.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write five to seven sentences describing what you are currently doing (real or imagined), and politely asking for or offering to reschedule, help, or send something.

  1. Greet the reader. Use a courtesy formula from Chapter 1.
  2. Say what you are doing. Use the progressive, such as मैं ... कर रहा/रही हूँ.
  3. Explain the situation. Mention a deadline, task, or class using this chapter's vocabulary.
  4. Make a polite request. Use क्या आप ... सकते हैं?
  5. Offer or confirm ability if appropriate. Use मैं ... सकता/सकती हूँ.
  6. Close politely. Use a closing courtesy formula.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • The progressive रहा/रही/रहे agrees correctly with the subject's gender and number.
  • सकना follows the verb stem directly, without an infinitive ending.
  • At least one item of school or work vocabulary from this chapter appears.
  • A polite request formula from Chapter 11 or this chapter is used correctly.
  • A danda (।) ends each sentence, and spelling matches this chapter's vocabulary.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Institutional politeness depends on relationship and setting

How directly you make a request depends on age, role, hierarchy, familiarity, and setting. A request to a close colleague may be more direct than a request to a teacher or supervisor, where softened forms and courtesy titles such as जी are common.

Culture in Context — Hindi-English terminology in education and work

Words such as मीटिंग, प्रोजेक्ट, and ईमेल are common in many contemporary Hindi-speaking schools and workplaces. This does not mean that Hindi-English mixing replaces Hindi learning; it reflects a real, ordinary feature of modern professional and educational communication that exists alongside a full range of Hindi vocabulary.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Missing auxiliary agreement in the progressive

Error: Saying मैं काम कर रहा है when the speaker is describing their own action.

Correction: Match the auxiliary to the subject: मैं काम कर रहा हूँ (I am working, if masculine), or मैं काम कर रही हूँ (feminine).

Common Mistake — Adding an infinitive ending before सकना

Error: Saying मैं बोलना सकता हूँ, adding -ना to the verb stem before सकना.

Correction: Use the bare verb stem directly before सकना: मैं बोल सकता हूँ.

Common Mistake — Confusing progressive and habitual present

Error: Using the habitual present, such as मैं काम करता हूँ, to describe an action happening right now.

Correction: Use the progressive for a current activity: मैं अभी काम कर रहा हूँ (I am working right now). Reserve the habitual present, from Chapter 9, for routines and general actions.
