कहानी, कारण और संबंध

Volume I, Chapter 19: Story, Reason, and Connection · Speak and Write Hindi: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Connect a statement to its reason using क्योंकि and to a result using इसलिए.
  2. Contrast two ideas using लेकिन within a connected explanation.
  3. State a simple condition using अगर...तो.
  4. Report simple information using कि after verbs such as कहना and लगना.
  5. Recognize the relative-correlative pattern जो...वह in supported reading.
  6. Write a short connected A2 paragraph using sequence, reason, and condition.

Communication Scenario

Priya writes a message to her friend Meera explaining why she missed a get-together last week, and what she plans to do if the weather is good this weekend. Meera replies, reporting what a mutual friend said and offering a small piece of advice. Their exchange moves from a personal explanation into a short connected story with reasons, a condition, and reported information.

  1. Notice how क्योंकi-clauses and इसलिए-clauses link one idea to another across several sentences, not just one.
  2. Notice how अगर मौसम अच्छा हुआ, तो हम पार्क जाएँगे states a condition and its result together.
  3. Notice how उसने कहा कि... reports what someone else said, without quoting their exact words.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 19 continues Unit IV, अनुभव, योजना और कहानी, and is the last regular instructional chapter before the Chapter 20 final assessment. Chapter 18 introduced productive comparison with से ज़्यादा, से कम, and सबसे, opinions with मुझे लगता है कि..., reasons with क्योंकि, and a limited supported experience pattern. Chapter 19 expands reason and contrast into fuller explanation, adds condition and reported information, and gives learners a first receptive look at relative-correlative structure.

Coming Into This Chapter

You can compare two or more things, state an opinion with मुझे लगता है कि..., give a reason with क्योंकि, and describe a limited past experience.

New in This Chapter

Reason and contrast expand into connected explanation with क्योंकि, इसलिए, and लेकिन. You will also state a simple condition with अगर...तो, report information with कि, and recognize जो...वह in reading without needing to produce it independently.

Devanagari and Pronunciation Focus

This chapter introduces no new letters or matras. It focuses on the rhythm of longer connected sentences and the intonation of condition and reported speech.

Pronunciation Focus — Schwa deletion review in connector words

Review schwa deletion in समस्या, कारण, and मौसम. Read each word as a natural spoken form rather than sounding out every written vowel.

Pronunciation Focus — Pausing at clause boundaries

Practice a brief pause after the अगर-clause and before तो: अगर मौसम अच्छा हुआ, [pause] तो हम पार्क जाएँगे. A short pause at the comma helps the listener track the two connected parts of the sentence.

Pronunciation Focus — Rhythm across a connected explanation

Listen for how क्योंकि, इसलिए, and लेकिन each carry their own light stress as they introduce a new part of a longer explanation, rather than being rushed together with the surrounding words.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

19.4 Language Focus — Reason and result with क्योंकि and इसलिए

क्योंकि (because) introduces the reason after the main statement, just as in Chapter 18. इसलिए (therefore, so) works in the opposite direction: the reason comes first, and इसलिए introduces the result that follows from it. Both connectors keep ordinary Hindi word order in each clause.

Language Building Block — Reason with क्योंकि
मैं पार्टी में नहीं आई क्योंकि मुझे बहुत काम था।
I did not come to the party because I had a lot of work.
Language Building Block — Result with इसलिए
मुझे बहुत काम था, इसलिए मैं पार्टी में नहीं आई।
I had a lot of work, so I did not come to the party.

Notice that the two sentences above describe the same situation in opposite order. This chapter moves इसलिए from the receptive status it held in Chapters 14 and 18 into controlled productive use.

19.5 Language Focus — Contrast with लेकिन in longer explanation

लेकिन (but) is already familiar from earlier chapters. This chapter develops it further by placing it inside a longer explanation that also contains a reason or a result, rather than a single short sentence.

Language Building Block — Contrast within an explanation
मुझे यह जगह पसंद है, लेकिन मेरी बहन को यह पसंद नहीं है, क्योंकि उसे शोर पसंद नहीं है।
I like this place, but my sister does not like it, because she does not like noise.

19.6 Language Focus — Condition with अगर...तो

अगर introduces a condition, and तो introduces the result. This chapter limits condition to simple, likely, present-or-future situations using familiar habitual and future forms, not hypothetical or contrary-to-fact conditions.

Language Building Block — Condition and result
अगर मौसम अच्छा हुआ, तो हम पार्क जाएँगे। अगर मुझे समय मिला, तो मैं ज़रूर आऊँगी।
If the weather is good, then we will go to the park. If I get time, I will definitely come.

This chapter restricts productive practice to अगर...तो sentences with future or habitual outcomes and familiar vocabulary. It does not require hypothetical, contrary-to-fact, or literary conditional forms.

19.7 Language Focus — Reported information with कि

कि connects a reporting verb such as कहना or लगना to the information being reported. The clause after कि follows ordinary Hindi word order, just as in the मुझे लगता है कि... pattern from Chapter 18.

Language Building Block — Reported information
मीरा ने कहा कि वह अगले हफ़्ते आएगी। उसने कहा कि उसे यह जगह बहुत पसंद है।
Meera said that she would come next week. She said that she likes this place very much.

This chapter limits reported information to simple, present- or future-oriented statements with कहना and the already-familiar लगना pattern. It does not require full reported-speech tense shifting or quoted speech.

19.8 Language Focus — Recognizing जो...वह

जो...वह is a relative-correlative pattern: जो introduces a description, and a later वह refers back to it. This chapter introduces the pattern receptively only, in supported reading, so that learners can recognize it and understand its meaning.

Language Building Block — Receptive recognition
जो रेस्तराँ बाज़ार के पास है, वह सबसे अच्छा है।
The restaurant that is near the market is the best. (Literally: "Which restaurant is near the market, that one is the best.")

Receptive only. Learners are not required to produce जो...वह sentences independently in this chapter. Recognize the pattern in reading and understand its meaning; full productive control is developed in later volumes.

Boundary

This chapter does not require formal literary Hindi, full relative-correlative production, advanced reported speech with tense shifting, or complex hypothetical conditional systems. Productive practice remains limited to क्योंकि, इसलिए, लेकिन in longer explanations, simple अगर...तो conditions, and simple reported information with कि.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Priya writes a message to her friend Meera about missing last week's get-together and her plans for the weekend.

प्रिया मीरा, माफ़ करना, मैं पिछले हफ़्ते मुलाक़ात में नहीं आई, क्योंकि मुझे अचानक बहुत काम आ गया। Meeraa, maaf karanaa, maiँ pichhale haफ़्ते mulaaqaat meँ naheeँ aaee, kyoँki mujhe achaanak bahut kaam aa gayaa. Meera, sorry, I did not come to the get-together last week, because I suddenly had a lot of work.
मीरा कोई बात नहीं। रोहन ने कहा कि तुम बीमार थीं, इसलिए मुझे थोड़ी चिंता हुई। Koee baat naheeँ. Rohan ne kahaa ki tum beemaar theeँ, isalie mujhe thoड़ee chintaa hueee. No problem. Rohan said that you were sick, so I got a little worried.
प्रिया नहीं, नहीं, मैं बीमार नहीं थी, लेकिन काम बहुत ज़्यादा था। वैसे, इस हफ़्ते के अंत में क्या योजना है? Naheeँ, naheeँ, maiँ beemaar naheeँ thee, lekin kaam bahut zyaadaa thaa. Vaise, is haफ़्ते ke aँt meँ kyaa yojanaa hai? No, no, I wasn't sick, but I had a lot of work. By the way, what's the plan for this weekend?
मीरा अगर मौसम अच्छा हुआ, तो हम पार्क जाएँगे। अगर बारिश हुई, तो हम घर पर ही मिलेंगे। Agar mausam achchhaa huaa, to ham paark jaaeँge. Agar baarish huee, to ham ghar par hee mileँge. If the weather is good, then we will go to the park. If it rains, then we will just meet at home.
प्रिया ठीक है। जो जगह पार्क के पास है, वह मुझे बहुत पसंद है, इसलिए मैं ज़रूर आऊँगी अगर मौसम अच्छा रहा। Theek hai. Jo jagah paark ke paas hai, vah mujhe bahut pasand hai, isalie maiँ zaroor aaoँgee agar mausam achchhaa rahaa. Okay. The place that is near the park, I like it very much, so I will definitely come if the weather stays good.
मीरा बहुत अच्छा! मैं तुम्हें कल फिर से संदेश भेजूँगी, क्योंकि तब तक मौसम के बारे में और पता चलेगा। Bahut achchhaa! Maiँ tumheँ kal phir se sandesh bhejooँgee, kyoँki tab tak mausam ke baare meँ aur pataa chalegaa. Very good! I will send you a message again tomorrow, because by then we will know more about the weather.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. प्रिया पिछले हफ़्ते मुलाक़ात में क्यों नहीं आई? (Why didn't Priya come to last week's get-together?)
  2. रोहन ने मीरा को क्या कहा था? (What had Rohan told Meera?)
  3. अगर मौसम अच्छा हुआ, तो वे क्या करेंगे? (If the weather is good, what will they do?)

Writing Workshop

Write a short connected paragraph explaining a small problem or situation from your own life.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write six to eight sentences: (1) describe a small problem or situation, (2) give a reason using क्योंकि, (3) state a result using इसलिए, (4) add a contrast using लेकिन, (5) state a condition using अगर...तो, and (6) report what someone said using कि.

  1. Introduce the situation. Describe what happened or what is happening, using familiar vocabulary and recycled past narration.
  2. Give a reason. Use क्योंकि to explain why.
  3. State a result. Use इसलिए to connect the reason to what happened next.
  4. Add a contrast. Use लेकिन to introduce a different or unexpected part of the situation.
  5. State a condition. Use अगर...तो to describe what will happen next, depending on something.
  6. Report information. Use कहा कि or लगता है कि to report what someone else said or thought.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • क्योंकि and इसलिए are used in the correct direction: reason-then-क्योंकि or reason-then-इसलिए-result.
  • The अगर-clause and the तो-clause each keep ordinary Hindi word order.
  • Reported information after कि reads as a natural, ordinary Hindi sentence.
  • जो...वह is used only for recognition in reading, not required in the learner's own writing this chapter.
  • A danda (।) ends each sentence, and spelling matches this chapter's vocabulary.

Speaking Practice

नियंत्रित अभ्यास — Controlled practice: chain building

Practice building a chain: state a simple fact, add a reason with क्योंकि, then restate the same information starting with the reason and ending with इसलिए. For example: मैं घर पर हूँ क्योंकि बारिश हो रही है → बारिश हो रही है, इसलिए मैं घर पर हूँ.

संचार कार्य — Communicative task: explaining a small problem

With a partner, take turns explaining a small everyday problem, giving a reason, stating what you will do if a certain condition happens, and reporting what someone else said about the situation.

अकेले अभ्यास — Solo alternative

Record yourself explaining a small problem, its reason, a condition for what happens next, and one piece of reported information. Listen back and check your use of क्योंकि, इसलिए, अगर...तो, and कि.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Explaining an absence

Explaining why you missed a social event is common courtesy in many Hindi-speaking contexts, though the expected level of detail, formality, and timing of the explanation varies by relationship, region, and setting. A brief, sincere reason is usually appreciated more than an elaborate excuse.

Culture in Context — Passing along information carefully

Reporting what someone else said, as in उसने कहा कि..., is a normal part of everyday communication, but it can also shape how a situation is understood by others. Speakers in many settings take care to report information accurately and to avoid exaggerating what was actually said.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Confusing the direction of क्योंकि and इसलिए

Error: Saying मुझे बहुत काम था इसलिए मैं क्योंकि पार्टी में नहीं आई.

Correction: Use only one connector per sentence. Result-first: मुझे बहुत काम था, इसलिए मैं पार्टी में नहीं आई. Statement-first: मैं पार्टी में नहीं आई क्योंकि मुझे बहुत काम था.

Common Mistake — Dropping तो in a condition sentence

Error: Saying अगर मौसम अच्छा हुआ, हम पार्क जाएँगे without तो.

Correction: Include तो before the result clause for clarity at this level: अगर मौसम अच्छा हुआ, तो हम पार्क जाएँगे.

Common Mistake — Trying to produce जो...वह independently

Error: Attempting to build new जो...वह sentences from scratch in speaking or writing tasks.

Correction: This chapter treats जो...वह as receptive only. Recognize it in reading, but use familiar demonstrative and coordination patterns for your own speaking and writing this chapter.
