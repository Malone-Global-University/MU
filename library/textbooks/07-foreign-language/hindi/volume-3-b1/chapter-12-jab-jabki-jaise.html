जब, जबकि, और जैसे

Volume III, Chapter 12: Time, Contrast, and Manner · Speak and Write Hindi: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Connect two events or situations by their time relationship using जब...तब.
  2. Contrast two situations, viewpoints, or facts using जबकि.
  3. Describe manner, process, and similarity using जैसे and वैसे.
  4. Explain what happens when a time frame or background circumstance is present.
  5. Interpret जब, जबकि, and जैसे accurately in connected reading and listening.
  6. Write an explanatory paragraph that uses temporal and contrastive clauses clearly, without overloading a single sentence.

Communication Scenario

The neighborhood directory is finished, and Meera Ji's group meets for a twelfth session to plan a short orientation talk for new residents — explaining how life in the neighborhood has changed, what tends to happen at different times of day and year, and how the neighborhood compares with where some residents lived before. Priya, Aditya, and Kabir quickly find that description alone is not enough; they need language that connects events in time, contrasts one situation with another, and explains how something is done.

  1. Notice how the group moves from separate sentences toward sentences that link a time frame to what happens within it.
  2. Notice how जबकि lets a speaker hold two facts side by side without one canceling the other.
  3. Notice how जैसे and वैसे let the group explain a process or compare one thing to another without a long explanation.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 12 continues Unit III, जटिल वाक्य और स्वतंत्र अभिव्यक्ति. Chapter 11 expanded जो...वह into जिस...उस, जिसका...उसका, and जहाँ...वहाँ for precise description. This chapter uses the same two-clause sentence-linking skill for a different purpose: connecting events by time, holding two situations in contrast, and describing manner or similarity. Chapter 13 will then move from time and contrast to condition and possibility.

Coming Into This Chapter

Learners can already build a two-clause relative-correlative sentence with a clear comma pause and a matching correlative form, and can track reference across that pause without losing clarity.

What This Chapter Does

This chapter applies the same clause-linking logic to three new connectors: जब...तब for time, जबकि for contrast, and जैसे...वैसे for manner and similarity. It stays within explanation and comparison; it does not introduce conditionals, reported speech, or new relative forms.

Looking Forward

Chapter 13, अगर, चाहे, और संभावना, builds on this chapter's clause-linking control to discuss conditions, concessions, and possibilities.

Receptive Vocabulary Note

Forms such as जैसा कि, जिस तरह, जहाँ तक, तभी, and तब तक appear in this chapter's reading passage for recognition only. You are not expected to produce these formal or extended contrast markers independently yet; treat them as glossed items.

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

Schwa deletion in temporal and contrastive connectors

Practice reading these forms aloud, paying attention to schwa deletion: जबकि (JAB-ki, not ja-ba-ki), दरअसल (da-RAS-al), and पृष्ठभूमि (PRISHTH-bhoo-mi). Compare जैसे / वैसे aloud several times so the parallel sound pattern becomes automatic, just as you practiced जहाँ / वहाँ in Chapter 11.

Pausing and intonation in multi-clause sentences

A temporal or contrastive sentence has two natural phrase groups, just like the relative-correlative sentences of Chapter 11. Pause briefly at the comma: जब बारिश होती है, || तब सड़कों पर पानी भर जाता है। For जबकि, use a slightly falling-then-level tone before the connector to signal that a contrast is coming, and keep both halves of the contrast at a similar pace so neither side sounds more important than the other.

Listening for the contrast, not just the fact

Before listening, remind yourself that जबकि signals that two facts are being placed side by side, often to highlight a difference. Listen past the first clause for the word जबकि itself, and then listen for what changes in the second clause. Missing the contrast marker is the most common reason learners hear two facts but miss the comparison being made.

Grammar: Time, Contrast, and Manner

You already know how to build a two-clause sentence with a comma pause and a matching correlative form from Chapter 11's relative-correlative work. This chapter applies that same logic to three new connectors, each suited to a different communicative job: time, contrast, and manner.

12.4 जब...तब — Time Relationships

Building Block — जब...तब
जब बारिश होती है, तब सड़कों पर पानी भर जाता है।
When it rains, water fills up the streets.

Form: जब + clause + comma + (तब) + main clause. Use: connecting a time frame or repeated situation to what happens within it. Meaning: "when X, Y (happens/happened/happens habitually)."

Note: In natural spoken Hindi, तब is very often dropped once the time relationship is clear from जब alone: जब बारिश होती है, सड़कों पर पानी भर जाता है। Both versions are correct; the version with तब is slightly more formal or emphatic.

12.5 जबकि — Contrast

जबकi places two facts side by side, usually to highlight a difference between them. Unlike लेकिन, which simply adds a contrasting idea, जबकि frames the two clauses as a direct comparison.

Building Block — जबकि
पहले यहाँ सिर्फ़ एक छोटी दुकान थी, जबकि आज यहाँ पूरा बाज़ार है।
Before, there was only one small shop here, whereas today there is an entire market here.

Form: clause 1 + comma + जबकि + clause 2. Use: comparing two time periods, two people, two places, or two viewpoints. Meaning: "whereas," "while, in fact," or "even though" in a comparative (not conditional) sense.

Note: जबकि can appear at the start of a sentence or between the two clauses; the meaning does not change: जबकि आज यहाँ पूरा बाज़ार है, पहले यहाँ सिर्फ़ एक छोटी दुकान थी।

12.6 जैसे...वैसे — Manner and Similarity

Building Block — जैसे...वैसे
जैसे मेरी दादी खाना बनाती थीं, वैसे ही मेरी माँ भी बनाती हैं।
Just as my grandmother used to cook, my mother cooks in that same way too.

Form: जैसे + clause + comma + वैसे (+ ही) + main clause. Use: describing that one action, process, or quality resembles another. Meaning: "just as X, (in that same way) Y."

Note: जैसे alone (without a matching वैसे clause) is also common as a simple comparison marker meaning "like" or "such as": यह जगह एक बाज़ार जैसी है। ("This place is like a market.") Distinguish this simpler use from the full जैसे...वैसे two-clause pattern.

12.7 Backgrounding with Past Progressive and Habitual-Past Patterns

जब clauses often set a background situation using past progressive or habitual-past forms, while the main clause reports what happened within that background.

Building Block — Background + Event
जब मैं छोटा था, तब हम इस मोहल्ले में रहते थे।
When I was young, we used to live in this neighborhood.

This pattern recycles the past habitual (रहते थे) you already control from Volume II, now placed inside a जब clause to build extended background description.

12.8 Keeping Complex Sentences Clear

As in Chapter 11, keep each sentence to two clauses at this stage. Natural spoken Hindi sometimes reverses clause order or drops तब, but do not chain a जब clause, a जबकि clause, and a जैसे clause together in one sentence. Use separate sentences instead, and let discourse markers such as इसी दौरान or इसके विपरीत connect them at the paragraph level.

Do not treat जबकि as identical to लेकिन. लेकिन simply adds a contrasting idea anywhere in a conversation; जबकि specifically frames two clauses as a structured comparison, and it sounds more explanatory or written in style.

Model Dialogue

Setting: At the group's twelfth meeting, Meera Ji, Priya, Aditya, and Kabir plan a short orientation talk for new residents about how the neighborhood works and how it has changed.

मीरा जी नए निवासियों को यह बताना ज़रूरी है कि दिन के अलग-अलग समय पर मोहल्ले में क्या होता है। जैसे, जब सुबह होती है, तब सब्ज़ी वाला हमारी गली में आता है। It's important to tell new residents what happens in the neighborhood at different times of day. For example, when it's morning, the vegetable vendor comes to our lane.
प्रिया और जब शाम होती है, तब बच्चे मैदान में खेलने आते हैं। यह पैटर्न लगभग हर दिन एक जैसा रहता है। And when it's evening, children come to play in the field. This pattern stays roughly the same every day.
कबीर लेकिन कुछ चीज़ें बदल गई हैं। दस साल पहले यहाँ बहुत कम गाड़ियाँ थीं, जबकि आजकल सड़क पर हमेशा ट्रैफ़िक रहता है। But some things have changed. Ten years ago there were very few vehicles here, whereas nowadays there's always traffic on the road.
मीरा जी बिल्कुल, यह अच्छा उदाहरण है। जबकि का इस्तेमाल तभी करें जब आप दो स्थितियों की सीधी तुलना कर रहे हों — यहाँ आप दस साल पहले और आज की स्थिति की तुलना कर रहे हो। Exactly, that's a good example. Use jabki only when you're making a direct comparison between two situations — here you're comparing the situation ten years ago and today.
आदित्य एक और बात नए लोगों के लिए उपयोगी होगी: यहाँ त्योहार कैसे मनाए जाते हैं। जैसे लोग दिवाली पर घर सजाते हैं, वैसे ही होली पर पूरी गली मिलकर रंग खेलती है। One more thing would be useful for new people: how festivals are celebrated here. Just as people decorate their homes for Diwali, in that same way the whole lane plays with colors together at Holi.
कबीर यह जैसे...वैसे पैटर्न अच्छा लगा। तो हम इसे तुलना के लिए भी इस्तेमाल कर सकते हैं, है ना — जैसे कहीं और होता है, वैसे यहाँ भी होता है? I liked this jaise...vaise pattern. So we can use it for comparison too, right — the way it happens elsewhere, it happens here too?
प्रिया हाँ, बिल्कुल। और अब मुझे लगता है कि हमारे पास तीन ज़रूरी उपकरण हैं: समय के लिए जब...तब, तुलना के लिए जबकि, और तरीक़े के लिए जैसे...वैसे। Yes, exactly. And now I think we have three essential tools: jab...tab for time, jabki for comparison, and jaise...vaise for manner.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. सुबह होने पर मोहल्ले में क्या होता है? (What happens in the neighborhood when it's morning?)
  2. जबकि का इस्तेमाल कबीर ने किस तुलना के लिए किया? (What comparison did Kabir use jabki for?)
  3. होली और दिवाली की तुलना करने के लिए आदित्य ने कौन-सा पैटर्न इस्तेमाल किया? (Which pattern did Aditya use to compare Holi and Diwali?)

Reading Practice

Read the adapted orientation-talk excerpt below, drafted by Meera Ji's group, and then answer the questions.

नए निवासियों के लिए — For New Residents: हमारा मोहल्ला कैसे चलता है — How Our Neighborhood Runs
जब आप यहाँ रहना शुरू करेंगे, तब आपको धीरे-धीरे यहाँ का रोज़मर्रा का दौर समझ आ जाएगा। सुबह जल्दी सब्ज़ी वाला आता है, जबकि दोपहर में गली लगभग ख़ाली रहती है, क्योंकि ज़्यादातर लोग काम पर होते हैं। शाम को स्थिति फिर बदल जाती है — बच्चे मैदान में खेलते हैं, और बड़े लोग टहलने निकलते हैं। जैसे हर मोहल्ले का अपना तरीक़ा होता है, वैसे ही हमारे मोहल्ले का भी अपना एक ख़ास रिदम है। एक तरफ़ पुराने निवासी परंपरागत तरीक़ों को पसंद करते हैं, जबकि दूसरी तरफ़ नए परिवार अक्सर नई सुविधाओं की माँग करते हैं। इसी दौरान, मोहल्ला समिति दोनों पक्षों की बात सुनने की कोशिश करती है, ताकि बदलाव धीरे-धीरे और सबकी सहमति से हो।
When you start living here, you will gradually come to understand the everyday rhythm of this place. The vegetable vendor comes early in the morning, whereas in the afternoon the lane stays almost empty, because most people are at work. In the evening the situation changes again — children play in the field, and elders go out for walks. Just as every neighborhood has its own way of doing things, our neighborhood too has its own particular rhythm. On one hand, older residents prefer traditional ways, whereas on the other hand, new families often ask for new facilities. During this same time, the neighborhood committee tries to listen to both sides, so that change happens gradually and with everyone's agreement.

Writing Workshop

Write a short explanatory text, हमारे मोहल्ले का रोज़मर्रा का दौर ("The Daily Rhythm of Our Neighborhood"), about a real or invented neighborhood, campus, or community you know, explaining how it changes across the day, the year, or over time.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write a connected 120-160 word text in standard Devanagari that includes:

  1. At least one जब...तब sentence connecting a time frame to what happens within it.
  2. At least one जबकि sentence contrasting two time periods, people, or situations.
  3. At least one जैसे...वैसे sentence describing a manner, process, or similarity.
  4. At least one discourse connector such as इसी दौरान, एक तरफ़...दूसरी तरफ़, or इसके विपरीत linking your ideas at the paragraph level.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • Each sentence stays limited to two clauses; no जब, जबकि, and जैसे clause is chained together in one sentence.
  • जबकि is used only where two situations are being directly compared, not as a general substitute for लेकिन.
  • A comma separates the two clauses in each temporal, contrastive, or manner sentence.
  • Past habitual and past progressive forms are used accurately inside background जब clauses.
  • Gender, number, agreement, and postpositions are applied correctly throughout.
  • Spelling, matras, conjuncts, word spacing, and dandas are correct throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Describing daily and seasonal rhythms

Explaining how a neighborhood, campus, or community changes across the day and across seasons is a common and useful way to orient a newcomer in many Hindi-speaking contexts. This kind of explanation often relies on exactly the temporal and contrastive tools taught in this chapter, rather than on a single static description.

Culture in Context — Softening contrast

As the chapter scope notes, contrast can be made politely without direct confrontation. When comparing an older way of doing things with a newer one — as in the reading passage's contrast between older residents and new families — speakers often add a phrase acknowledging both sides, such as दोनों की अपनी जगह है ("each has its own place"), rather than framing one side as simply right or wrong.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Treating जबकि as identical to लेकिन

Error: Using जबकि anywhere a simple contrast word would work, even when no direct two-situation comparison is being made.

Correction: Reserve जबकि for structured comparisons between two clearly defined situations, such as दस साल पहले यहाँ कम गाड़ियाँ थीं, जबकि आजकल हमेशा ट्रैफ़िक रहता है। Use लेकिन for a simple contrasting remark elsewhere.

Common Mistake — Dropping the comma pause in जब...तब and जैसे...वैसे sentences

Error: जब बारिश होती है सड़कों पर पानी भर जाता है। (no pause, sentence runs together)

Correction: जब बारिश होती है, सड़कों पर पानी भर जाता है। — the comma marks the boundary between the time clause and the main clause and should also be reflected as a brief pause in speech.

Common Mistake — Chaining three connectors into one sentence

Error: जब सुबह होती है, तब सब्ज़ी वाला आता है, जबकि दोपहर में जैसे गली ख़ाली रहती है, वैसे शाम को नहीं रहती। (three connectors chained together, unclear)

Correction: Split into separate sentences: जब सुबह होती है, तब सब्ज़ी वाला आता है। जबकि दोपहर में गली ख़ाली रहती है, शाम को स्थिति बदल जाती है। — clarity matters more than combining every idea into one sentence.

Looking Ahead

अब आप क्या कर सकते हैं — What you can do now

You can connect events by time relationship with जब...तब, contrast two situations with जबकि, and describe manner or similarity with जैसे...वैसे, while keeping each sentence clear and limited to two clauses.
