समीक्षा 2: समझाना और मनाना

Volume III, Chapter 10: Unit II Assessment — Explaining and Persuading · Speak and Write Hindi: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

This review chapter introduces no new grammar. By the end of it, you will be able to:

  1. State and support an opinion with reasons, examples, and appropriate hedging.
  2. Explain a problem, identify its causes and results, and propose a realistic solution.
  3. Summarize a short news item or public-information source and attribute information accurately.
  4. Compare two or three options and make an audience-appropriate recommendation.
  5. Agree, disagree, and partially agree politely while responding to another person's view.
  6. Produce one integrated spoken and written performance that combines opinion, evidence, comparison, and conclusion.

Communication Scenario

The parents' committee has asked the conversation group to turn their Chapter 9 discussion into a single written recommendation, supported by the news report from Chapter 8 and the group's own reasoning from Chapters 6 and 7. At the group's tenth meeting, Priya, Aditya, Kabir, and Meera Ji prepare this combined explanation together, checking that every claim is either their own supported opinion or something clearly attributed to a source.

  1. Notice how the group distinguishes what the report says (Chapter 8) from what they themselves believe (Chapter 6).
  2. Notice how they connect the parking problem's causes and results (Chapter 7) to their final comparison and recommendation (Chapter 9).
  3. Notice how disagreement is handled politely, with partial agreement before a counterpoint.
  4. This chapter's task is integration: combining four chapters' worth of language into one coherent, evidence-based recommendation.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 10 is the Unit II assessment chapter. It does not introduce new grammar, vocabulary domains, or text types. Instead, it asks learners to combine the four systems developed across Chapters 6-9 into one integrated performance.

Coming Into This Chapter

Learners arrive with opinion and hedging language from Chapter 6 (मेरे विचार में, शायद, संभव है कि), cause-result and problem-solution language from Chapter 7 (के कारण, की वजह से, इसलिए), source attribution and reported information from Chapter 8 (के अनुसार, कहा कि, बताया कि), and comparison and recommendation frames from Chapter 9 (की तुलना में, के मुकाबले, मैं सलाह दूँगा/दूँगी कि, बेहतर होगा कि).

What This Chapter Does

This chapter does not add new structures. It reviews all four systems together, gives extended integrated practice in listening, reading, speaking, and writing, and assesses learners' emerging B2 control of explanation, evidence, comparison, and audience-aware persuasion.

Looking Forward

Chapter 11, जो, जिस, जहाँ, opens Unit III by expanding relative-correlative structures for more flexible description and reference tracking.

Pronunciation and Listening Review

Schwa deletion in abstract and media vocabulary

Review schwa deletion across this unit's longer words: प्राथमिकता (praath-a-mik-TAA), व्यावहारिक (vyaa-va-HAA-rik), विश्वसनीय (vish-vas-a-NEEY), and परिणाम (pa-ri-NAAM). Read each aloud in isolation, then inside a full sentence from the Model Dialogue below.

News-style listening and summarizing

Before listening, identify what kind of information you are listening for: an opinion (Chapter 6), a cause-result relationship (Chapter 7), a reported fact with a source (Chapter 8), or a comparison and recommendation (Chapter 9). Naming the type of information first makes it easier to summarize accurately afterward.

Punctuation for quotations and source attribution

Review quotation punctuation from Chapter 8 alongside comparison punctuation from Chapter 9: a source attribution phrase such as रिपोर्ट के अनुसार is normally followed by a comma before the reported clause, while a comparison phrase such as की तुलना में is normally followed by a comma before the main clause it modifies.

Spoken emphasis in argument

Review the emphasis markers ही, भी, and तो from Chapter 9. In argument and persuasion, these markers help a speaker narrow focus (यही सबसे बड़ी समस्या है), add a supporting point (यह महँगा भी है), or introduce a concession before a contrast (यह सस्ता तो है, लेकिन...).

Grammar Review: Unit II Systems

This chapter introduces no new grammar. The four systems below, one from each of Chapters 6-9, must now work together in a single connected performance.

10.3 Opinion and Hedging (Chapter 6)

Review — Claim, Reason, Example, Conclusion
मेरे विचार में, स्कूल के पास पार्किंग की समस्या गंभीर है, क्योंकि हर सुबह सड़क पर बहुत भीड़ होती है। उदाहरण के लिए, पिछले हफ़्ते एक बच्चा लगभग गिर गया। इसलिए, अंत में, मुझे लगता है कि नगर निगम को जल्दी कोई कदम उठाना चाहिए।
In my view, the parking problem near the school is serious, because there is a lot of crowding on the road every morning. For example, last week a child nearly fell. So, in the end, I think the municipal corporation should take some action soon.

10.4 Cause, Result, and Solution (Chapter 7)

Review — Cause-Result Chain
भीड़ के कारण बच्चों को सड़क पार करने में कठिनाई होती है, जिससे अभिभावकों में चिंता बढ़ती है। इसलिए, बेहतर होगा कि नगर निगम एक सुरक्षित जगह जल्दी बनाए।
Because of the crowding, children have difficulty crossing the road, which increases parents' worry. Therefore, it would be better if the municipal corporation built a safe space soon.

10.5 Source Attribution and Neutral Summary (Chapter 8)

Review — Reporting a Source
रिपोर्ट के अनुसार, नगर निगम तीन विकल्पों पर विचार कर रहा है। एक अधिकारी ने बताया कि अंतिम फ़ैसला अगले महीने लिया जाएगा। यह स्पष्ट नहीं है कि इसमें अभिभावकों की राय शामिल की जाएगी या नहीं।
According to the report, the municipal corporation is considering three options. An official reported that the final decision will be made next month. It isn't clear whether parents' opinions will be included in it.

10.6 Comparison and Recommendation (Chapter 9)

Review — Comparison, Concession, Recommendation
शटल सेवा की तुलना में, नई पार्किंग जगह ज़्यादा सुरक्षित है। हालाँकि यह महँगी है, फिर भी मैं सलाह दूँगी कि नगर निगम इसे प्राथमिकता दे।
Compared to the shuttle service, the new parking space is safer. Although it is expensive, I would still advise that the municipal corporation prioritize it.

10.7 Integrating the Four Systems

A complete Unit II performance normally moves through the four systems in this order: state an opinion with a reason (10.3); explain the underlying cause and result (10.4); support the opinion with a source (10.5); and compare options before recommending one (10.6). Not every task requires all four in every paragraph, but a strong argument connects them rather than treating them as separate exercises.

Use: When agreeing, disagreeing, or qualifying another person's argument, connect your response to the specific system they used: challenge a cause-result claim with evidence, not with a bare opinion, and challenge a bare opinion by asking for a reason or source.

Model Dialogue

Setting: At the conversation group's tenth meeting, the group drafts their combined recommendation for the parents' committee, weaving together opinion, cause-result explanation, source attribution, and comparison from the previous four chapters.

मीरा जी इससे पहले कि हम सिफ़ारिश लिखें, हमें साफ़ करना चाहिए कि कौन-सी बात हमारी अपनी राय है और कौन-सी बात रिपोर्ट से आई है। Before we write the recommendation, we should make clear which part is our own opinion and which part comes from the report.
आदित्य ठीक है। रिपोर्ट के अनुसार, नगर निगम तीन विकल्पों पर विचार कर रहा है, और एक अधिकारी ने बताया कि फ़ैसला अगले महीने होगा। यह तथ्य है, राय नहीं। Okay. According to the report, the municipal corporation is considering three options, and an official reported that the decision will happen next month. That's a fact, not an opinion.
प्रिया और मेरे विचार में, भीड़ की वजह से बच्चों के लिए सड़क पार करना खतरनाक होता जा रहा है। यह हमारी राय है, लेकिन यह रिपोर्ट में उठाई गई चिंता से भी मेल खाती है। And in my view, crossing the road is becoming dangerous for children because of the crowding. That's our opinion, but it also matches a concern raised in the report.
कबीर मैं आपसे कुछ हद तक सहमत हूँ, लेकिन क्या हम यह कह सकते हैं कि यही समस्या का इकलौता कारण है? शायद ट्रैफ़िक भी एक कारण है। I agree with you to some extent, but can we say that this is the only cause of the problem? Perhaps traffic is also a cause.
मीरा जी यह एक अच्छा बिंदु है। तो हम लिख सकते हैं: भीड़ और ट्रैफ़िक, दोनों के कारण, सड़क पार करना मुश्किल हो जाता है। That's a good point. So we can write: because of both the crowding and the traffic, crossing the road becomes difficult.
आदित्य अब तीन विकल्पों की तुलना करते हैं। हम पहले ही तय कर चुके हैं कि शटल सेवा की तुलना में और ड्रॉप-ऑफ़ लेन के मुकाबले, नई पार्किंग जगह सबसे सुरक्षित और सबसे व्यावहारिक है। Now let's compare the three options. We've already decided that, compared to the shuttle service and compared to the drop-off lane, the new parking space is the safest and most practical.
प्रिया हालाँकि यह सबसे महँगा विकल्प नहीं है, फिर भी हमें इसकी लागत का ज़िक्र करना चाहिए, ताकि सिफ़ारिश ईमानदार लगे। Although it isn't the most expensive option, we should still mention its cost, so that the recommendation seems honest.
कबीर मुझे लगता है कि यह पूरी तरह सही नहीं है कि हम सिर्फ़ लागत का ज़िक्र करें। हमें यह भी बताना चाहिए कि यह लागत क्यों उचित है। I think it isn't entirely correct for us to just mention the cost. We should also explain why this cost is reasonable.
मीरा जी सही बात है। तो अंत में हम लिखेंगे: इसलिए, मैं सलाह दूँगी कि नगर निगम नई पार्किंग जगह को प्राथमिकता दे, क्योंकि यह भीड़ और ट्रैफ़िक, दोनों की समस्या को सबसे अच्छी तरह हल करती है। That's right. So in the end we'll write: therefore, I would advise that the municipal corporation prioritize the new parking space, because it solves the problem of both the crowding and the traffic best.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. कौन-सी बातें रिपोर्ट से आई हैं, और कौन-सी बातें समूह की अपनी राय हैं? (Which points come from the report, and which points are the group's own opinion?)
  2. कबीर मीरा जी और प्रिया की बातों से कैसे कुछ हद तक असहमत होता है? (How does Kabir partially disagree with Meera Ji and Priya?)
  3. समूह की अंतिम सिफ़ारिश क्या है, और वे इसे किन कारणों से सही ठहराते हैं? (What is the group's final recommendation, and what reasons do they give for it?)

Reading Practice

Read the short adapted opinion-and-recommendation article below, published on a community website, and then answer the questions.

सामुदायिक समाचार वेबसाइट — Community News Website: हमारे पार्क का भविष्य — The Future of Our Park
रिपोर्ट के अनुसार, स्थानीय पार्क के पुराने खेल के उपकरण अब सुरक्षित नहीं माने जाते, और पिछले साल दो बच्चे मामूली रूप से घायल भी हुए। एक अभिभावक ने बताया कि उपकरण की मरम्मत कई बार माँगी गई है, लेकिन अब तक कोई कार्रवाई नहीं हुई। इस वजह से, कई परिवार अब पार्क में जाने से हिचकिचाते हैं, जिससे बच्चों के खेलने की जगह कम हो गई है। नगर निगम के सामने दो विकल्प हैं: पुराने उपकरण की मरम्मत करना, या पूरी तरह नए उपकरण लगाना। मरम्मत की तुलना में, नए उपकरण लगाना महँगा है, लेकिन यह लंबे समय तक चलेगा और आधुनिक सुरक्षा मानकों को पूरा करेगा। हालाँकि नए उपकरण की लागत ज़्यादा है, फिर भी कई अभिभावकों का मानना है कि यह बेहतर विकल्प है, क्योंकि बार-बार मरम्मत की तुलना में यह अंततः सस्ता साबित होगा। मेरे विचार में भी, बच्चों की सुरक्षा को देखते हुए, नगर निगम को नए उपकरण को प्राथमिकता देनी चाहिए।
According to the report, the local park's old play equipment is no longer considered safe, and last year two children were also mildly injured. One parent reported that repairs have been requested several times, but no action has been taken so far. Because of this, many families now hesitate to visit the park, which has reduced children's play space. The municipal corporation has two options: repairing the old equipment, or installing entirely new equipment. Compared to repair, installing new equipment is expensive, but it will last longer and meet modern safety standards. Although the cost of new equipment is higher, many parents believe it is the better option, because compared to repeated repairs, it will ultimately prove cheaper. In my view too, given children's safety, the municipal corporation should prioritize new equipment.

  2. What causes and results does the text describe regarding the old equipment?
  3. Which two options are compared, and what is the outcome of the comparison?

Writing Workshop

Write an integrated Unit II performance, एक समझाने वाला निबंध ("An Explanatory Essay"), on a local or community issue of your choice (or one your instructor assigns).

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write a connected 180-220 word text in standard Devanagari that includes all four of the following, clearly connected to one another:

  1. An opinion with at least one reason and one example (Chapter 6 pattern).
  2. At least one cause-result explanation using के कारण, की वजह से, or इसलिए (Chapter 7 pattern).
  3. At least one piece of attributed information, using के अनुसार or a reporting verb such as बताया कि (Chapter 8 pattern).
  4. A comparison of two options and a final recommendation, including at least one concession with हालाँकि... फिर भी... (Chapter 9 pattern).

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • Opinion, cause-result, source attribution, and comparison-recommendation are all present and clearly distinguishable.
  • Every attributed claim is marked with a source phrase or reporting verb, not stated as if it were the writer's own knowledge.
  • The concession acknowledges a genuine disadvantage rather than a minor detail.
  • The final recommendation is stated clearly and connects back to the earlier cause-result explanation.
  • Gender, number, agreement, and oblique forms are applied correctly throughout.
  • Spelling, matras, conjuncts, word spacing, and dandas are correct throughout.

Speaking Practice

नियंत्रित अभ्यास — Controlled practice: चारों प्रणालियाँ जोड़ना

With a partner, take turns building one connected argument aloud: first state an opinion, then add a cause-result explanation, then attribute one fact to an imagined source, then compare two options and recommend one.

संचार कार्य — Communicative task: सहमति और असहमति

Present your Writing Workshop argument to a partner. Your partner should respond with full agreement, partial agreement (मैं कुछ हद तक सहमत हूँ, लेकिन...), or polite disagreement, and should ask at least one clarification question before responding.

अकेले अभ्यास — Solo alternative

Record a two- to three-minute spoken explanation and recommendation that includes all four Unit II systems, then listen back and check off each system on the editing checklist above.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Separating opinion from source in public discussion

In many public and institutional settings, clearly separating personal opinion from reported fact is considered a mark of credibility, not a formality to skip. As Meera Ji models in the dialogue above, distinguishing रिपोर्ट के अनुसार from मेरे विचार में helps a recommendation sound trustworthy to a committee or audience that does not share the speaker's assumptions.

Culture in Context — Partial agreement as a discussion skill

Kabir's response, मैं आपसे कुछ हद तक सहमत हूँ, लेकिन..., models a common and valued discussion strategy: acknowledging the validity of another person's point before adding a distinct one. This differs from flatly agreeing or flatly disagreeing, and it tends to keep group discussion collaborative rather than adversarial.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Blending opinion and reported fact

Error: Writing पार्किंग की समस्या गंभीर है और नगर निगम तीन विकल्पों पर विचार कर रहा है as one undifferentiated claim, without marking which part is opinion and which part is reported.

Correction: Mark each part separately: मेरे विचार में, पार्किंग की समस्या गंभीर है। रिपोर्ट के अनुसार, नगर निगम तीन विकल्पों पर विचार कर रहा है।

Common Mistake — Comparing without first explaining the underlying problem

Error: Jumping directly to a comparison of options without first explaining what problem the options are meant to solve.

Correction: Establish the cause and result first: भीड़ के कारण सड़क पार करना मुश्किल है। इसलिए, आइए विकल्पों की तुलना करें।

Common Mistake — Disagreeing without acknowledging the other person's point

Error: Responding to another speaker with a flat contradiction such as यह सही नहीं है without acknowledging what was valid in their point.

Correction: Use a softened, partial-agreement frame: यह एक अच्छा बिंदु है, लेकिन मुझे लगता है कि...
