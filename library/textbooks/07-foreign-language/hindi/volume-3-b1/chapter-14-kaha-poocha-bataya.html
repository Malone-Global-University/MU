कहा, पूछा, और बताया

Volume III, Chapter 14: Reporting Speech and Information · Speak and Write Hindi: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Report what someone said using कहा कि and related reporting verbs.
  2. Report questions someone asked using पूछा कि with indirect question words such as क्या, कहाँ, कब, क्यों, and कैसे.
  3. Report explanations, requests, and written messages using बताया कि, समझाया कि, and लिखा कि.
  4. Distinguish direct quotation from reported (indirect) speech, including basic punctuation conventions for each.
  5. Adjust pronouns and time/place references appropriately when reporting what someone said.
  6. Write a concise, neutral report of a conversation, message, or announcement.

Communication Scenario

The orientation talk is now only days away, and Meera Ji's group has been fielding calls, messages, and questions from neighbors who cannot attend the planning meetings in person. At their fourteenth session, Priya, Aditya, and Kabir compare notes: one neighbor called to ask something, another sent a message explaining a concern, and a third stopped Kabir on the street to make a request. Before the group can act on any of this, they need to report accurately to each other — who said what, who asked what, and who wrote what — without turning every report into a word-for-word transcript.

  1. Notice how the group uses कहा कि, पूछा कि, and बताया कि to summarize different kinds of communication.
  2. Notice how a question someone asked out loud becomes a smooth indirect question inside a reporting sentence.
  3. Notice how the group keeps their reports neutral, without turning someone else's words into their own opinion.

Coming Into This Chapter

Learners can already build a clear two-clause sentence with a comma pause and a matching connector, and they already use कि after opinion and belief verbs from Chapter 6 (मुझे लगता है कि...) and after news-reporting frames from Chapter 8 (रिपोर्ट के अनुसार..., उन्होंने कहा कि...).

What This Chapter Does

This chapter expands कहा कि from Chapter 8's news context into a full reporting system: reported statements, reported questions with indirect question words, reported explanations and requests, and the distinction between quoting someone directly and reporting their meaning indirectly. It stays within everyday, workplace-adjacent, and community reporting; it does not introduce full literary narrative style or exhaustive tense-shift rules.

Receptive Vocabulary Note

Formal reporting verbs common in media and institutional writing, such as बयान दिया ("gave a statement"), स्पष्टीकरण दिया ("gave a clarification"), and passive-leaning frames like कहा गया and बताया गया, appear in this chapter's reading passage for recognition only. You are not expected to produce these independently yet; Chapter 8's controlled introduction and Volume IV will expand this system further.

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

Schwa deletion in reporting verbs and formal nouns

Practice reading these forms aloud, paying attention to schwa deletion: समझाया (sam-jhaa-YAA, not sa-ma-jhaa-ya), स्पष्ट (SPASHT, one tight syllable, not spa-sha-ta), and घोषणा (gho-SHA-naa). Notice that पुष्टि ends in a consonant cluster with no vowel at all after ष्ट — resist the urge to add a vowel sound at the end.

Phrase grouping in reporting sentences

A reporting sentence with कि has two natural phrase groups, just like the अगर...तो sentences of Chapter 13: उसने कहा || कि वह कल आएगी। Unlike Chapter 13's connectors, there is normally no comma before कि in everyday writing, but the spoken pause still falls in roughly the same place — right after the reporting verb.

Intonation for quotation versus summary

A direct quotation is often read with a small shift in pitch or pace, as if briefly stepping into the other speaker's voice: उसने कहा, "मैं कल नहीं आ सकता।" A reported summary with कि is usually read in one even, connected tone, without that shift: उसने कहा कि वह कल नहीं आ सकता। Before listening, remind yourself to listen for this difference: is the speaker quoting, or summarizing?

Grammar: Reporting Speech and Information

You already use कि after opinion verbs (Chapter 6) and after news-reporting frames (Chapter 8). This chapter expands that same कि-clause pattern into a full system for reporting statements, questions, explanations, and messages.

14.4 Reported Statements with कहा कि

Building Block — Reported Statement
सुनीता ने कहा कि वह मीटिंग में देर से आएगी।
Sunita said that she would arrive late to the meeting.

Form: subject + ने + कहा + कि + reported clause. Use: summarizing what someone said, without necessarily using their exact words. Meaning: "X said that Y."

Note: The reporting verb कहा takes ने because it is a perfective transitive verb, just as you practiced with other ने-marked verbs in earlier chapters. Pronouns inside the reported clause usually shift to match the reporter's point of view: if Sunita said "I will be late" (मैं देर से आऊँगी), the report becomes कि वह देर से आएगी — "I" becomes "she."

14.5 Reported Questions with पूछा कि

Building Block — Reported Question
उसने पूछा कि कार्यक्रम कहाँ होगा।
He/she asked where the program would be.

Form: subject + ने + पूछा + कि + indirect question word (क्या, कहाँ, कब, क्यों, कैसे) + rest of clause. Use: reporting a question someone asked out loud or in writing. Meaning: "X asked whether/where/when/why/how Y."

Note: For yes-no questions, use क्या inside the reported clause: direct question क्या आप आ रहे हैं? becomes उसने पूछा कि क्या मैं आ रहा हूँ। The word order inside the reported clause stays close to a normal statement — do not keep the inverted or emphatic word order of the original spoken question.

14.6 Reporting Information, Explanation, and Requests

Building Block — Reported Information and Request
उसने बताया कि पार्किंग की जगह कम है। उसने अनुरोध किया कि हम जल्दी पहुँचें।
He/she told us that parking space is limited. He/she requested that we arrive early.

Form: बताया कि... for factual information; समझाया कि... for a fuller explanation; अनुरोध किया कि... for a polite request. Use: distinguishing plain information from explanation from request, so that a listener understands not just what was said but what kind of communication it was. Meaning: each verb signals the communicative purpose of the original message, not only its content.

Note: Do not use कहा कि for everything. Choosing बताया, समझाया, पूछा, or अनुरोध किया tells your listener more precisely what happened in the original conversation.

14.7 Direct Quotation versus Reported Speech

Building Block — Quotation vs. Report
सीधा उद्धरण: कबीर ने कहा, "मैं कल नहीं आ सकता।" अप्रत्यक्ष रिपोर्ट: कबीर ने कहा कि वह कल नहीं आ सकता।
Direct quotation: Kabir said, "I can't come tomorrow." Indirect report: Kabir said that he couldn't come tomorrow.

Form: direct quotation uses a comma, quotation marks, and the speaker's exact words with first-person and present/future forms preserved; indirect report uses कि, no quotation marks, and shifted pronouns and verb forms. Use: choose direct quotation when the exact wording matters (a promise, a claim, an official statement); choose indirect report for ordinary summary. Meaning: both convey the same basic content, but direct quotation claims to preserve the speaker's precise words.

Note: In this chapter, treat pronoun and tense shifts as a practical guideline rather than a rigid formula: change "I/we" to "he/she/they" as needed, and adjust obvious time words (आज → उस दिन, कल → अगले दिन) only when it is necessary for clarity. Full systematic tense-shift rules are not a target of this chapter.

14.8 Neutral Source Attribution

When reporting for a group, a notice, or a summary, keep your report neutral and attribute it clearly to its source, as you practiced with news reporting in Chapter 8: एक पड़ोसी ने बताया कि..., एक व्यक्ति ने पूछा कि.... Avoid presenting someone else's statement as your own opinion, and avoid adding judgment (वह ग़लत था) inside a neutral report unless you are clearly labeling it as your own separate reaction.

14.9 Keeping Reporting Sentences Clear

As in Chapters 11-13, keep each reporting sentence limited to one reporting verb and one कि-clause at this stage. Do not stack multiple reported statements, questions, and requests inside a single sentence. Use separate sentences, and let expressions such as इसके अलावा ("besides this") or फिर ("then") connect them at the paragraph level.

Do not treat every reported sentence as a word-for-word quotation. Reported speech with कि represents meaning, not necessarily the speaker's exact words.

Model Dialogue

Setting: At the group's fourteenth meeting, Priya, Aditya, and Kabir report calls, messages, and requests from neighbors about the upcoming orientation talk.

प्रिया सुनीता जी ने फ़ोन किया था। उन्होंने कहा कि वह कार्यक्रम में देर से आएँगी, क्योंकि उनकी बस लेट है। Sunita Ji called. She said that she would arrive late to the program, because her bus is late.
आदित्य ठीक है, कोई बात नहीं। एक और पड़ोसी ने मुझसे पूछा कि कार्यक्रम कहाँ होगा और क्या पार्किंग है। Okay, no problem. Another neighbor asked me where the program would be and whether there's parking.
कबीर मैंने उन्हें बताया कि पार्किंग की जगह कम है, इसलिए बेहतर होगा कि वे पैदल आएँ। उन्होंने पूछा कि कितने बजे शुरू होगा। I told them that parking space is limited, so it would be better if they came on foot. They asked what time it would start.
प्रिया और मीरा जी को एक संदेश मिला। संदेश में लिखा था कि एक परिवार बीमारी की वजह से नहीं आ पाएगा, लेकिन उन्होंने अनुरोध किया कि हम उन्हें बाद में जानकारी भेजें। And Meera Ji received a message. The message said that a family wouldn't be able to come because of illness, but they requested that we send them information afterward.
आदित्य ठीक-ठीक शब्दों में क्या लिखा था? या तुमने सिर्फ़ मतलब बताया? What did it say in exact words? Or did you just tell us the meaning?
प्रिया संदेश में लिखा था, "हम आना चाहते थे, पर घर में सब बीमार हैं।" मैंने बस संक्षेप में बताया कि क्या हुआ। The message said, "We wanted to come, but everyone at home is sick." I just briefly summarized what happened.
कबीर समझ गया। तो कुल मिलाकर: एक व्यक्ति ने देर से आने की बात कही, दो लोगों ने कार्यक्रम के बारे में पूछा, और एक परिवार ने माफ़ी माँगते हुए बाद में जानकारी माँगी। Got it. So altogether: one person said they'd be late, two people asked about the program, and one family, apologizing, asked for information afterward.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. सुनीता जी ने क्या कहा? (What did Sunita Ji say?)
  2. कबीर ने पड़ोसी को क्या बताया? (What did Kabir tell the neighbor?)
  3. संदेश में क्या लिखा था, और परिवार ने क्या अनुरोध किया? (What did the message say, and what did the family request?)

Writing Workshop

Write a short report, बातचीत का सारांश ("Summary of a Conversation"), summarizing a real or invented conversation, phone call, or set of messages you have received about an event, plan, or request.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write a connected 120-160 word text in standard Devanagari that includes:

  1. At least two reported statements using कहा कि or बताया कि.
  2. At least one reported question using पूछा कि with an indirect question word.
  3. One reported request or explanation using अनुरोध किया कि or समझाया कि.
  4. One short direct quotation with correct punctuation, clearly distinguished from your reported summary.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • Each reporting sentence contains one reporting verb and one कि-clause; reports are not stacked into one long sentence.
  • Pronouns inside reported clauses are shifted appropriately (for example, "I" becomes "he/she" where needed).
  • The direct quotation uses quotation marks and a comma, and is clearly separate from the indirect reports.
  • The report stays neutral; the writer's own opinion, if included at all, is clearly separated from what was reported.
  • Gender, number, agreement, and postpositions are applied correctly throughout.
  • Spelling, matras, conjuncts, word spacing, and dandas are correct throughout.

Speaking Practice

नियंत्रित अभ्यास — Controlled practice: किसने क्या कहा

With a partner, take turns: one partner makes a short statement, a question, or a request out loud, and the other reports it back using कहा कि, पूछा कि, or अनुरोध किया कि. Then switch roles.

संचार कार्य — Communicative task: बातचीत का सारांश

Describe a real or invented phone call, message, or conversation to your partner, reporting at least two statements, one question, and one request. Your partner should ask a follow-up question about who said what.

अकेले अभ्यास — Solo alternative

Record yourself summarizing a short conversation or set of messages, using at least one reported statement, one reported question, one reported request, and one direct quotation. Listen back and check that your reported clauses are clear and that pronouns are shifted correctly.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Reporting with care

As the chapter scope notes, reporting someone else's words requires care, especially in a community, workplace, or formal setting. A neutral report such as उन्होंने बताया कि वे नहीं आ सकते respects the original speaker's words, while adding unasked-for judgment — implying, for instance, that the person is being unreasonable — changes a simple report into something closer to gossip or criticism. Distinguishing exact quotation, paraphrase, and personal inference is a useful habit in any language.

Culture in Context — Honorifics in reported speech

When reporting about someone who is addressed respectfully, such as मीरा जी, honorific agreement often carries over into the report: मीरा जी ने बताया कि वे कल आएँगी uses the respectful plural verb form आएँगी rather than the singular, even though only one person is meant. This reflects the same honorific agreement patterns you practiced in Chapter 2.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Keeping the direct-question word order inside a reported question

Error: उसने पूछा कि क्या है कार्यक्रम कहाँ? (keeps the direct-question form inside the reported clause)

Correction: उसने पूछा कि कार्यक्रम कहाँ है। — inside a reported question, the clause following कि should read like an ordinary statement with the question word in its natural position, not like the original spoken question.

Common Mistake — Forgetting to shift the pronoun

Error: Kabir said, "मैं कल नहीं आ सकता," reported as कबीर ने कहा कि मैं कल नहीं आ सकता। (keeps "I" even though the reporter is not Kabir)

Correction: कबीर ने कहा कि वह कल नहीं आ सकता। — when someone else's first-person statement is reported by another speaker, "I" normally becomes "he/she."

Common Mistake — Using कहा कि for every kind of communication

Error: उसने कहा कि क्या हम जल्दी आ सकते हैं? (uses कहा for what was actually a request)

Correction: उसने अनुरोध किया कि हम जल्दी आएँ। — choosing अनुरोध किया, पूछा, or समझाया instead of a generic कहा tells the listener more precisely what kind of communication took place.

Chapter Review — भाग B

  1. (a) कि वह देर से आएगी।; (b) कि कार्यक्रम कहाँ होगा।; (c) कि हम जल्दी आएँ।; (d) कि बच्चों के लिए जगह होगी।
  2. उसने कहा, "मैं नहीं आ सकता।" is a direct quotation (quotation marks, comma, exact first-person words preserved); उसने कहा कि वह नहीं आ सकता। is a reported statement (no quotation marks, कि-clause, pronoun shifted to third person).

Looking Ahead

अब आप क्या कर सकते हैं — What you can do now

You can report what someone said, asked, explained, or requested using कहा कि, पूछा कि, बताया कि, समझाया कि, and अनुरोध किया कि, distinguish direct quotation from indirect report, and shift pronouns appropriately when summarizing a conversation or message.
