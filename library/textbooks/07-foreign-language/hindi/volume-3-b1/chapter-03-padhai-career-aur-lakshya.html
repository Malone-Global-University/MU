पढ़ाई, करियर, और लक्ष्य

Volume III, Chapter 3: Education, Career, and Goals · Speak and Write Hindi: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Discuss your education, work experience, skills, and professional goals in a connected account.
  2. Describe qualifications and responsibilities clearly and accurately.
  3. Express purpose and motivation using के लिए, ताकि, and infinitive-based patterns such as सीखने के लिए.
  4. State intentions and decisions using का इरादा है, निर्णय लिया, and controlled सोचना patterns.
  5. Express obligation and expectation with चाहिए, पड़ना, and ज़रूरी है.
  6. Compare educational or career options using से बेहतर, से ज़्यादा, कम, and सबसे.
  7. Ask and answer interview-style questions about study, work, and goals.
  8. Write a formal-adjacent statement of goals or professional learner profile.

Communication Scenario

At the conversation group's third meeting, Meera जी introduces a new activity: each member will describe their educational and career path so far, explain one current goal, and answer a partner's interview-style questions about it. Priya is finishing a certificate program and considering two job offers; Kabir is thinking about switching fields and needs to explain his reasoning to skeptical family members.

  1. Notice how Priya uses के लिए and ताकi to explain why she is taking a particular course of action, not just what she is doing.
  2. Notice how Kabir uses से बेहतर and सबसे to compare his two options and justify his decision to people who need convincing.
  3. Notice that both speakers use चाहिए, पड़ना, and ज़रूरी है to distinguish what they personally feel obligated to do from what circumstances require of them.
  4. Notice that reference tracking and honorific agreement from Chapter 2 continue to operate here — this chapter adds purpose, intention, obligation, and comparison on top of that foundation, not in place of it.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 3 continues Unit I, पहचान, अनुभव, और बदलता जीवन, moving from Chapter 2's relationship narratives toward education, work, and personal goals.

Coming Into This Chapter

Learners arrive with expanded paragraph-level reference tracking, honorific agreement, `को` for recipients and experiencers, and causal/relational postpositional frames such as की वजह से and के कारण from Chapter 2. Volume II work and study vocabulary, ability, and obligation are also assumed as available foundations.

New in This Chapter

Purpose expressions with के लिए, ताकि, and infinitive-based patterns; intention and decision language with का इरादा है, निर्णय लिया, and सोचना; obligation and expectation with चाहिए, पड़ना, and ज़रूरी है; and expanded comparison with से बेहतर, से ज़्यादा, कम, and सबसे for evaluating options.

Pronunciation and Orthography Focus

English-origin words in Hindi professional speech

Words such as कोर्स, प्रोजेक्ट, and इंटरव्यू appear naturally in ordinary Hindi professional conversation. Pronounce them with Hindi rhythm and vowel quality rather than switching into an English accent mid-sentence.

Schwa deletion in formal nouns and infinitive-derived phrases

Words such as ज़िम्मेदारी, प्रशिक्षण, and phrases like सीखने के लिए combine familiar schwa-deletion patterns with formal or derived shapes. Read each form slowly first, then in the connected sentences of the dialogue and reading.

Presentation rhythm in interview answers

When answering an interview-style question, group each reason or example into its own breath phrase rather than running everything together. A short pause after इसलिए or क्योंकि helps the listener follow your explanation.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

3.4 Purpose with के लिए, ताकि, and Infinitives

के लिए after an infinitive expresses purpose: मैं यह कोर्स नई नौकरी पाने के लिए कर रहा हूँ ("I'm taking this course in order to get a new job"). ताकि introduces a purpose clause with its own subject and verb, often followed by a subjunctive-like form: मैं मेहनत कर रही हूँ ताकि मुझे बेहतर पद मिल सके ("I'm working hard so that I can get a better position").

PatternFunctionExample
infinitive + के लिएpurpose, same subjectनया कौशल सीखने के लिए मैंने यह कोर्स चुना।
ताकि + subjunctive-like verbpurpose, subject may differमैं जल्दी आया ताकि हम पूरी बात कर सकें।
Language Building Block
मैंने यह प्रमाणपत्र इसलिए लिया ताकि मुझे नए क्षेत्र में काम करने का मौक़ा मिल सके, और अब मुझे लगता है कि यह निर्णय सही था।
I took this certificate so that I could get the chance to work in a new field, and now I feel that this decision was right.

Form: This sentence links a past action (लिया), a purpose clause with ताकि, and a present reflective stance (मुझे लगता है कि...) reactivated from Chapter 1.

Meaning: The purpose clause explains why the action was taken, not merely what was done.

Use: When explaining an educational or career choice, connect the action to its purpose before reflecting on the outcome.

3.5 Intention and Decision

FrameFunctionExample
का इरादा हैhas the intention toमेरा इरादा है कि मैं अगले साल तक यह कोर्स पूरा कर लूँ।
निर्णय लियाmade a decision (completed)उसने नई नौकरी लेने का निर्णय लिया।
सोच रहा/रही हूँam consideringमैं क्षेत्र बदलने के बारे में सोच रहा हूँ।

Use these frames in controlled patterns. Do not treat सोचना here as a general verb "to think" in every context — in this frame, it specifically communicates that a decision is still being weighed.

3.6 Obligation and Expectation

FrameFunctionExample
चाहिएshould, personal/moral obligationमुझे और अभ्यास करना चाहिए।
पड़नाcircumstance forces the actionमुझे हफ़्ते में तीन बार जाना पड़ता है।
ज़रूरी हैnecessary, often impersonalइस पद के लिए अनुभव ज़रूरी है।

Distinguish these carefully: चाहिए expresses what someone feels they should do; पड़ना expresses what circumstances require regardless of preference; ज़रूरी है states a general or institutional requirement without attributing feeling to any one person.

3.7 Comparing Options

FrameFunctionExample
से बेहतरbetter thanयह नौकरी पिछली नौकरी से बेहतर है।
से ज़्यादाmore thanयहाँ वेतन वहाँ से ज़्यादा है।
से कमless thanयह ज़िम्मेदारी पहले से कम है।
सबसेthe most (superlative)यह मेरे लिए सबसे अच्छा विकल्प है।
Boundary

This chapter does not introduce new relative-correlative structures, new aspectual systems, or full subjunctive analysis. ताकि and सकें-type forms appear as controlled formulaic patterns here; broader conditional and hypothetical work begins in Chapter 13.

Model Dialogue

Setting: At the conversation group's third meeting, Priya and Kabir discuss their current education and career decisions with Meera जी.

मीरा जी आज हम अपनी पढ़ाई और करियर के बारे में बात करेंगे। प्रिया, तुम्हारा क्या हाल है? Today we'll talk about our education and career. Priya, how are things with you?
प्रिया मैं एक प्रमाणपत्र कोर्स पूरा कर रही हूँ। मैंने यह कोर्स इसलिए लिया ताकि मुझे नए क्षेत्र में काम करने का मौक़ा मिल सके। I'm completing a certificate course. I took this course so that I could get the chance to work in a new field.
कबीर अच्छा! और अब क्या इरादा है? Good! And what's the plan now?
प्रिया मेरे पास दो नौकरी के प्रस्ताव हैं। एक कंपनी में वेतन ज़्यादा है, लेकिन ज़िम्मेदारी भी ज़्यादा है। दूसरी कंपनी पहली से छोटी है, लेकिन वहाँ काम पहली नौकरी से ज़्यादा दिलचस्प लगता है। I have two job offers. At one company the salary is higher, but the responsibility is also greater. The other company is smaller than the first, but the work there seems more interesting than the first job.
कबीर तो तुम्हारे हिसाब से कौन-सी नौकरी सबसे बेहतर है? So in your opinion, which job is the best?
प्रिया मुझे लगता है कि दूसरी नौकरी मेरे लिए सबसे उपयुक्त है, क्योंकि मुझे सीखने का ज़्यादा मौक़ा मिलेगा। लेकिन मुझे अभी सोचना पड़ेगा — अंतिम निर्णय अगले हफ़्ते लेना है। और तुम्हारा क्या हाल है? I feel that the second job is the most suitable for me, because I'll get more chance to learn. But I still have to think about it — I have to make the final decision next week. And how are things with you?
कबीर मैं अपना क्षेत्र बदलने के बारे में सोच रहा हूँ, लेकिन मेरे परिवार को यह समझाना मुश्किल है। उन्हें लगता है कि मुझे स्थिर नौकरी में ही रहना चाहिए। I'm thinking about changing my field, but it's difficult to explain this to my family. They feel that I should stay in a stable job.
मीरा जी तुम उन्हें क्या समझाओगे? What will you explain to them?
कबीर मैं उन्हें बताऊँगा कि नए क्षेत्र में मौक़े पुराने क्षेत्र से ज़्यादा हैं, और मुझे अभी बदलाव लाना ज़रूरी है, वरना बाद में मुश्किल हो जाएगा। I'll tell them that there are more opportunities in the new field than in the old field, and it's necessary for me to make a change now, otherwise it will become difficult later.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. प्रिया ने प्रमाणपत्र कोर्स क्यों किया? (Why did Priya take the certificate course?)
  2. दोनों नौकरियों में क्या अंतर है? (What is the difference between the two jobs?)
  3. कबीर के परिवार को समझाना मुश्किल क्यों है? (Why is it difficult for Kabir to explain to his family?)

Writing Workshop

Write a short statement of goals, मेरा लक्ष्य ("My Goal"), describing your current or planned education/career path.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write a connected 180-250 word account in standard Devanagari, in complete multi-sentence paragraphs, that includes:

  1. Describe your current education or work situation, using relevant vocabulary from this chapter.
  2. State one goal and its purpose, using के लिए or ताकि.
  3. Explain a decision or intention, using का इरादा है, निर्णय लिया, or सोच रहा/रही हूँ.
  4. Compare at least two options or stages, using से बेहतर, से ज़्यादा, कम, or सबसे.
  5. Mention one obligation or requirement, using चाहिए, पड़ना, or ज़रूरी है.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • Purpose is expressed clearly, not just an action without a reason.
  • Intention/decision language matches whether the decision is made, being made, or still being considered.
  • Obligation frames (`चाहिए`/`पड़ना`/`ज़रूरी है`) are used with the correct distinction between personal, circumstantial, and general necessity.
  • Comparison frames are accurate and support a clear conclusion.
  • Gender, number, agreement, and oblique forms are applied correctly throughout.
  • Spelling, matras, conjuncts, word spacing, and dandas are correct throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — English in professional Hindi

As the course notice and dialogue show, English-origin words such as कोर्स and प्रोजेक्ट are entirely normal in contemporary Hindi professional speech across many contexts. This is not a sign of incomplete Hindi; it reflects real, widespread usage. Core Hindi vocabulary remains equally important and should not be replaced wholesale by English terms.

Culture in Context — Family and career decisions

As Kabir's example shows, career decisions are often discussed with family members, and explaining a change in direction may require patience and clear reasoning rather than a single announcement. This pattern varies widely by family, region, and individual circumstance, and should not be treated as a fixed rule for all Hindi-speaking families.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Confusing चाहिए, पड़ना, and ज़रूरी है

Error: Using चाहिए to describe an external requirement, such as saying इस पद के लिए अनुभव चाहिए when a neutral institutional requirement, not a personal obligation, is meant.

Correction: Use ज़रूरी है for a general or institutional requirement (इस पद के लिए अनुभव ज़रूरी है), and reserve चाहिए for what a specific person feels they personally should do.

Common Mistake — Treating के लिए and ताकि as interchangeable

Error: Writing मैं मेहनत कर रहा हूँ के लिए मुझे बेहतर पद मिल सके, mixing the two purpose patterns.

Correction: Use के लिए directly after an infinitive when the subject stays the same (बेहतर पद पाने के लिए), and use ताकि to introduce a full clause, especially when a different subject or a subjunctive-like verb form is involved (ताकि मुझे बेहतर पद मिल सके).

Common Mistake — Overusing सबसे without a clear comparison set

Error: Saying यह सबसे अच्छा है without having established what is being compared.

Correction: Establish the comparison set first (two or more named options), then use सबसे to single out the best among them, as Priya does when comparing her two job offers before concluding दूसरी नौकरी मेरे लिए सबसे उपयुक्त है.

This chapter extends Chapter 2's connected, reference-aware discourse into education and career, reactivating ability, desire, and formal request language from Volume II, while introducing purpose expressions, intention/decision language, obligation contrasts, and expanded comparison as its main new development. Practical Performance Task

मेरा लक्ष्य — My Goal

Complete the following connected tasks in order.

  1. Listen to Neha's account above and complete the listening table.
  2. Read the course notice and Amit's profile and answer the comprehension questions.
  3. Speak in an interview-style role-play with a partner or instructor, comparing at least two options and explaining one decision.
  4. Write your complete 180-250 word statement of goals, मेरा लक्ष्य, following the Writing Workshop checklist.
  5. Edit your written text for purpose expressions, obligation frames, and comparison accuracy.

Answer Key

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. Priya took the certificate course so that she could get the chance to work in a new field.
  2. One job offers a higher salary but more responsibility; the other job is smaller but seems more interesting, with more chance to learn.
  3. It is difficult for Kabir because his family feels he should stay in a stable job, and he needs to explain why changing fields is necessary now.

Listening

  • Neha took evening classes to learn a new skill, so that she could get the chance to advance in her work.
  • It was difficult at first because she had to study every day after work.
  • The new position is better than the old one, and the responsibility is more interesting.
  • Neha intends to take another course next year so that she can advance further.

Reading — Comprehension

  1. The notice is aimed at people who want to learn data-related work in their field; attendance is necessary to complete the course.
  2. Amit decided to take the course because he wants to work in the technology field instead of history.
  3. After the course, Amit plans to apply for an internship at a small company, so that he can get practical experience.
  4. Sample answer: ताकि मुझे व्यावहारिक अनुभव मिल सके expresses the purpose of applying for the internship — gaining practical experience.
