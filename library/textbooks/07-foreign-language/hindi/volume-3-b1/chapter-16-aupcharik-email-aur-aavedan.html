औपचारिक ईमेल और आवेदन

Volume III, Chapter 16: Formal Email and Applications · Speak and Write Hindi: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Write a formal or semi-formal Hindi email with an appropriate opening, closing, and structure.
  2. Make a polite request, application, complaint, or follow-up message using controlled formal frames such as मैं अनुरोध करता/करती हूँ कि... and कृपया सूचित करें कि....
  3. Explain background, purpose, and desired action clearly in an institutional message.
  4. Recognize and, in controlled contexts, use passive-like institutional forms such as भेजा गया, स्वीकार किया गया, and सूचित किया जाता है.
  5. Revise an informal message into an appropriately formal version, adjusting pronouns, address, and vocabulary.
  6. Respond appropriately to an institutional email, request, or notice.

Communication Scenario

Encouraged by how well the orientation talk went, Meera Ji's group now wants to reserve the community hall again for a follow-up program in three months, and this time they want to do it properly — through a written request to the hall's booking office rather than a phone call. Priya also needs to write a short formal email to her academic advisor about extending a coursework deadline, and Kabir needs to follow up on a job application he submitted last month. All three tasks require the same underlying skill: turning an everyday request into clear, polite, institutional Hindi.

  1. Notice how each writer opens and closes the email according to the relationship and institution.
  2. Notice how background, purpose, and desired action are organized into short, clear paragraphs.
  3. Notice where formal request frames replace everyday phrasing.
  4. Notice where passive-like institutional language appears in the replies the group receives.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 16 opens Unit IV, औपचारिक, सांस्कृतिक, और मध्यस्थ भाषा. It introduces formal correspondence as the first of four new domains — formal writing here, presentation and discussion in Chapter 17, cultural interpretation in Chapter 18, and register mediation in Chapter 19 — before Chapter 20 integrates all of Volume III into a final B2 performance.

Coming Into This Chapter

Learners already control reported speech, quotation, conditions, causes, purposes, and relative-correlative description from Unit III (Chapters 11-15), along with everyday requests and polite language from Volume II. This chapter does not reteach those systems; it applies them inside a new, more formal genre.

What This Chapter Does

This chapter teaches the structure and register of a formal Hindi email: opening, background, purpose, request, closing. It introduces formal request frames, purpose expressions in institutional writing, and controlled recognition of passive-like institutional phrasing, while explicitly contrasting formal register with the everyday register learners already control.

Looking Forward

Chapter 17, प्रस्तुति और चर्चा, extends this formal-register work from written correspondence into spoken presentation and discussion, including responding to audience questions.

Register Note — Everyday vs. Formal

Compare an everyday request with its formal equivalent: मुझे थोड़ा समय चाहिए (everyday) versus मुझे कुछ अतिरिक्त समय की आवश्यकता है (formal). Both are correct Hindi; the choice depends on audience and setting, not on which one is "proper" Hindi.

16.3 Receptive Vocabulary

The following forms should be recognized in institutional emails and notices but are not required for independent production: संदर्भ में ("with reference to"), उपरोक्त ("above-mentioned"), तदनुसार ("accordingly"), and heading labels such as विषय: ("Subject:").

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

Reading formal messages aloud

Formal written Hindi, when read aloud, should sound measured and clear rather than rushed. Practice reading the model email below at a steady pace, pausing slightly at each paragraph break and after कृपया at the start of a request.

Schwa deletion in formal nouns

Practice these forms: अनुरोध (a-nu-RODH, not a-nu-ra-o-dha), आवश्यकता (aa-VASH-yak-taa), संलग्नक (san-LAG-nak), and आदरणीय (aa-dar-a-NEE-ya, with the second schwa retained before the conjunct). These words recur throughout formal Hindi correspondence.

Polite formal intonation

A spoken formal request, such as reading a request aloud to a colleague before sending it, typically uses a level, unhurried tone rather than the rising warmth of a casual favor. Contrast the intonation of थोड़ा समय दे दो! (casual, animated) with क्या आप कुछ अतिरिक्त समय दे सकते हैं? (formal, level).

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

16.4 Formal Request Frames

Language Building Block — Formal Request
मैं अनुरोध करती हूँ कि हमें अगले महीने की पंद्रह तारीख़ के लिए हॉल उपलब्ध कराया जाए। यदि संभव हो तो, कृपया इसकी पुष्टि जल्द से जल्द भेज दें।
I request that the hall be made available to us for the fifteenth of next month. If possible, please send confirmation of this as soon as possible.

Use मैं अनुरोध करता/करती हूँ कि... as the core formal request frame. Combine it with कृपया for a softened follow-up instruction, and with यदि संभव हो तो... when the request involves some flexibility. Note that the clause after कि often uses a controlled passive-like form such as उपलब्ध कराया जाए — this pattern is presented for recognition and limited, formulaic production only.

16.5 Purpose and Reason in Formal Writing

Language Building Block — Purpose in Formal Writing
मैं यह ईमेल इसलिए लिख रही हूँ ताकि आपको हमारी योजना के बारे में सूचित कर सकूँ।
I am writing this email so that I can inform you about our plan.

State the purpose of a formal message early, using मैं यह ईमेल इसलिए लिख रहा/रही हूँ ताकि... or the simpler इस ईमेल का उद्देश्य यह है कि.... This mirrors the purpose expressions from Chapter 3 (के लिए, ताकि) but places them in an institutional frame.

16.6 Passive-Like and Impersonal Forms (Controlled Recognition)

Institutional replies often use passive-like or impersonal forms such as भेजा गया ("was sent"), स्वीकार किया गया ("was accepted"), and सूचित किया जाता है कि... ("it is hereby informed that..."). At B2 level, learners should:

  • recognize these forms when reading institutional emails and notices;
  • understand that they remove the emphasis on who performed the action;
  • use a small number of them in fixed, formulaic patterns, such as आपका आवेदन स्वीकार कर लिया गया है; and
  • continue using ordinary active sentences with a clear subject for most of their own formal writing.

Full productive control of the passive and impersonal system belongs to Volume IV. Do not attempt to build every sentence of a formal email around these forms.

16.7 Register Transformation: Casual to Formal

Language Building Block — Casual → Formal
Casual: मुझे थोड़ा और टाइम चाहिए, यार, असाइनमेंट के लिए। ↔ Formal: महोदया, मैं अनुरोध करती हूँ कि मुझे असाइनमेंट जमा करने के लिए कुछ अतिरिक्त समय दिया जाए।
Casual: I need a bit more time, buddy, for the assignment. ↔ Formal: Madam, I request that I be given some additional time to submit the assignment.

Notice the changes: address shifts from a friend's name to a title; यार and English loanwords like टाइम and असाइनमेंट shift to समय and a more neutral phrase where appropriate; and the request frame becomes explicit rather than implied.

Boundary Reminder

This chapter does not require full mastery of the passive/impersonal system, government-file terminology, or legal petition language. Keep formal writing clear and direct; do not overload sentences with Sanskritic vocabulary merely to sound more official.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Before sending her formal email, Priya reads her draft aloud to Aditya so he can check the tone.

प्रिया सुनो, ज़रा बताओ — क्या यह ईमेल बहुत औपचारिक लग रहा है या ठीक है? "आदरणीय शर्मा जी, मैं यह ईमेल इसलिए लिख रही हूँ ताकि आपको अपने असाइनमेंट की समय-सीमा के बारे में सूचित कर सकूँ।" Listen, tell me — does this email sound too formal, or is it okay? "Respected Sharma Ji, I am writing this email so that I can inform you about my assignment deadline."
आदित्य यह ठीक है, लेकिन थोड़ा और साफ़ करो — यह मत कहो कि तुम "सूचित करना" चाहती हो, बल्कि सीधे बताओ कि तुम क्या चाहती हो। This is fine, but make it a bit clearer — don't just say you want to "inform," but say directly what you want.
प्रिया ठीक है, तो मैं आगे यह लिखूँगी: "मैं अनुरोध करती हूँ कि मुझे असाइनमेंट जमा करने के लिए एक हफ़्ते की अतिरिक्त समय-सीमा दी जाए, क्योंकि पिछले हफ़्ते मुझे तबीयत ख़राब होने के कारण पढ़ाई करने का समय नहीं मिला।" Okay, so I'll write next: "I request that I be given one additional week to submit the assignment, because last week I did not get time to study due to being unwell."
आदित्य अच्छा है। अंत में "सादर" या "धन्यवाद सहित" लिखना मत भूलना, और अपना नाम और कोर्स का नाम भी दे देना। That's good. Don't forget to write "respectfully" or "with thanks" at the end, and also give your name and the course name.
प्रिया हाँ, बिल्कुल। वैसे, कबीर, तुम्हारी नौकरी के आवेदन का क्या हुआ? क्या तुमने अनुवर्ती ईमेल भेजा? Yes, absolutely. By the way, Kabir, what happened with your job application? Did you send a follow-up email?
कबीर अभी तक नहीं, लेकिन आज शाम भेजूँगा। मैं बस यह पूछना चाहता हूँ कि मेरे आवेदन की स्थिति क्या है, बिना यह लगे कि मैं जल्दबाज़ी कर रहा हूँ। Not yet, but I'll send it this evening. I just want to ask what the status of my application is, without it seeming like I'm being impatient.
आदित्य तो "कृपया सूचित करें कि मेरे आवेदन पर अब तक क्या निर्णय लिया गया है" — यह वाक्य विनम्र भी है और सीधा भी। Then "please inform me what decision has been made regarding my application so far" — this sentence is both polite and direct.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. प्रिया अपने ईमेल में क्या अनुरोध कर रही है, और क्यों? (What is Priya requesting in her email, and why?)
  2. आदित्य प्रिया के ईमेल में क्या बदलाव सुझाता है? (What change does Aditya suggest for Priya's email?)
  3. कबीर अपने अनुवर्ती ईमेल में क्या पूछना चाहता है, बिना जल्दबाज़ी दिखाए? (What does Kabir want to ask in his follow-up email, without seeming impatient?)

Reading Practice

Read Meera Ji's formal email to the community hall's booking office, then answer the questions.

ईमेल — Email: हॉल बुकिंग का अनुरोध — Hall Booking Request
विषय: अगले कार्यक्रम के लिए हॉल बुकिंग का अनुरोध

आदरणीय महोदय/महोदया,

मैं यह ईमेल इसलिए लिख रही हूँ ताकि आपको हमारे अगले सामुदायिक कार्यक्रम के बारे में सूचित कर सकूँ। पिछले महीने हमने आपके हॉल में एक सफल कार्यक्रम आयोजित किया था, जिसमें लगभग साठ लोग शामिल हुए थे। कई पड़ोसियों ने कहा है कि वे चाहते हैं कि हम हर तीन महीने में ऐसा कार्यक्रम करें।

इसी सिलसिले में, मैं अनुरोध करती हूँ कि हमें अगली 15 तारीख़ को शाम पाँच से आठ बजे तक हॉल उपलब्ध कराया जाए। हमें लगभग साठ से सत्तर लोगों के लिए जगह चाहिए। यदि संभव हो तो, कृपया मुझे बताएँ कि क्या कुर्सियाँ और माइक्रोफ़ोन भी उपलब्ध होंगे, या हमें स्वयं इनकी व्यवस्था करनी होगी।

कृपया इस अनुरोध की पुष्टि जल्द से जल्द भेज दें, ताकि हम आगे की योजना बना सकें। यदि किसी अतिरिक्त जानकारी या दस्तावेज़ की आवश्यकता हो, तो कृपया मुझे बताएँ।

धन्यवाद सहित,
सादर,
मीरा शर्मा
समुदाय आयोजन समिति
Subject: Request for hall booking for the next event. Respected Sir/Madam, I am writing this email so that I can inform you about our next community event. Last month we organized a successful event in your hall, in which about sixty people took part. Several neighbors have said that they want us to hold such an event every three months. In this connection, I request that the hall be made available to us on the 15th, from five to eight in the evening. We need space for about sixty to seventy people. If possible, please tell me whether chairs and a microphone will also be available, or whether we will have to arrange these ourselves. Please send confirmation of this request as soon as possible, so that we can plan further. If any additional information or documents are needed, please let me know. With thanks, respectfully, Meera Sharma, Community Events Committee.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. Identify the sentence that states the purpose of the email.
  2. What exact request does Meera Ji make, including date, time, and number of people?
  3. What two questions does she ask about equipment?
  4. Identify the opening and closing formulas used in this email.

Writing Workshop

Write a formal email, औपचारिक अनुरोध ("Formal Request"), for one of the following situations: requesting a deadline extension, applying for a course or position, following up on an application, or making a service request or complaint.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write a connected 120-180 word formal email in standard Devanagari that includes:

  1. An appropriate opening (आदरणीय...) matched to the relationship and institution.
  2. A clear statement of purpose using मैं यह ईमेल इसलिए लिख रहा/रही हूँ ताकि... or an equivalent frame.
  3. Background using at least one cause-result or relative-correlative sentence recycled from earlier chapters.
  4. A clear formal request using मैं अनुरोध करता/करती हूँ कि..., with one detail softened by यदि संभव हो तो....
  5. An appropriate closing (सादर / धन्यवाद सहित) with your name.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • The opening and closing match the level of formality required by the situation.
  • The purpose of the email is stated clearly within the first two sentences.
  • The request itself is unambiguous and includes any necessary dates, numbers, or details.
  • Passive-like forms, if used, are limited to fixed, correctly formed patterns rather than freely constructed.
  • Vocabulary avoids unnecessary casual expressions, English loans used casually, or overly decorative Sanskritic language.
  • Gender, number, agreement, oblique forms, and postpositions are applied correctly throughout.
  • Spelling, matras, conjuncts, word spacing, and punctuation are correct throughout.

Speaking Practice

नियंत्रित अभ्यास — Controlled practice: कैज़ुअल से औपचारिक — Casual to Formal

With a partner, take turns: one person states a request casually (e.g., asking a friend for more time, a favor, or information), and the other restates it as a formal request suitable for an email or institutional conversation, using the frames from this chapter.

संचार कार्य — Communicative task: ईमेल पढ़कर सुनाना

Draft your formal email from the Writing Workshop, then read it aloud to a partner as though leaving a formal voicemail or reading it before sending. Your partner should identify the purpose, the specific request, and suggest one place where the tone could be clearer or more polite.

अकेले अभ्यास — Solo alternative

Record yourself reading your formal email aloud at a measured pace. Listen back and check whether your pausing and intonation match the level, unhurried style described in the Pronunciation section.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Formality depends on institution and relationship

The right level of formality in a Hindi email depends on the institution, the relationship, and the purpose of the message — not on a fixed rule that "more Sanskritic" always means "more correct." A message to a close colleague may reasonably mix Hindi and English and use a simpler opening, while a message to an unfamiliar office or senior official typically calls for fuller formal conventions such as आदरणीय and सादर.

Culture in Context — Politeness without over-formality

Excessively Sanskritized or bureaucratic language can sound stiff or even insincere in many everyday institutional contexts. Clear, polite, well-organized Hindi is usually more effective than decorative formality. As Kabir's example shows, a direct but respectful question is often better received than an overly elaborate one.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Casual address in a formal email

Error: हाय सर, मुझे थोड़ा टाइम चाहिए असाइनमेंट के लिए। (casual greeting and vocabulary in an email to an instructor or official)

Correction: आदरणीय महोदय, मैं अनुरोध करता हूँ कि मुझे असाइनमेंट जमा करने के लिए कुछ अतिरिक्त समय दिया जाए। — match the opening, vocabulary, and request frame to the formal context.

Common Mistake — Burying the request

Error: A long email that describes background in detail for several paragraphs without ever clearly stating what is being requested.

Correction: State the purpose early (मैं यह ईमेल इसलिए लिख रहा/रही हूँ ताकि...), then give brief background, then state the specific request clearly using मैं अनुरोध करता/करती हूँ कि....

Common Mistake — Overusing passive-like forms

Error: यह बताया जाता है कि यह अनुरोध किया जा रहा है कि हॉल दिया जाए। (an unnatural chain of passive-like forms that a B2 learner is not yet ready to construct reliably)

Correction: मैं अनुरोध करती हूँ कि हमें हॉल दिया जाए। — use one controlled passive-like clause where natural, and otherwise keep sentences active and direct.

Answer Key

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. Priya is requesting a one-week extension to submit her assignment, because she was unwell last week and did not have time to study.
  2. Aditya suggests she state directly what she wants rather than only saying she wants to "inform" her instructor.
  3. Kabir wants to politely ask what decision has been made about his application, without seeming impatient.

Listening

  • Kabir politely asked what decision had been made about his application so far.
  • He said that if any additional documents were needed, he could send them as soon as possible.
  • The passive-like form used was स्वीकार कर लिया गया है ("has been accepted").

Reading — Comprehension

  1. Purpose sentence: मैं यह ईमेल इसलिए लिख रही हूँ ताकि आपको हमारे अगले सामुदायिक कार्यक्रम के बारे में सूचित कर सकूँ।
  2. She requests the hall on the 15th, from 5 to 8 p.m., for about sixty to seventy people.
  3. She asks whether chairs and a microphone will be available, or whether they must arrange these themselves.
  4. Opening: आदरणीय महोदय/महोदया; closing: धन्यवाद सहित, सादर.

Looking Ahead

अब आप क्या कर सकते हैं — What you can do now

You can write a clear, appropriately formal Hindi email that states its purpose, gives necessary background, makes a specific request, and closes appropriately — and you can revise a casual message into formal register when the situation calls for it.
