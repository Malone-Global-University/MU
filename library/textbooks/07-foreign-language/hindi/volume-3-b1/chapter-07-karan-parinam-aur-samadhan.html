कारण, परिणाम, और समाधान

Volume III, Chapter 7: Causes, Results, and Solutions · Speak and Write Hindi: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Explain a practical problem in organized detail.
  2. Identify one or more causes and one or more results of a problem.
  3. Weigh advantages and disadvantages of a proposed course of action.
  4. Propose a realistic solution using appropriate recommendation language.
  5. Discuss what should be done and why, with audience-appropriate softening.
  6. Write a connected problem-solution paragraph or short report.

Communication Scenario

At the group's seventh meeting, Meera जी shifts the discussion from opinions in general to a specific practical problem: increasing traffic and parking difficulty near Priya's and Kabir's neighborhood school. The group moves beyond stating an opinion, as they did in Chapter 6, to explaining the problem's causes, its effects on the community, and possible solutions.

  1. Notice how each speaker names a specific cause before jumping to a solution.
  2. Notice how इसलिए, के कारण, and की वजह से connect a cause to its result across two or more sentences.
  3. Notice how recommendations use softened forms such as बेहतर होगा कि rather than blunt commands.
  4. This chapter's task is to move from a single supported opinion (Chapter 6) to a fuller problem-solution explanation: problem, cause, result, and realistic next step.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 7 continues Unit II, विचार, प्रमाण, और सार्वजनिक संवाद. It builds directly on Chapter 6's opinion and evidence language by adding cause-result connectors, modal recommendation frames, and problem-solution paragraph organization.

Coming Into This Chapter

Learners arrive from Chapter 6 with stance expressions (मेरे विचार में, मुझे लगता है कि), hedging (शायद, संभव है कि), concession (हालाँकि...फिर भी), and evidence markers (उदाहरण के लिए). They also bring Chapter 4's cause-result vocabulary (के कारण, की वजह से) from community-change description.

What This Chapter Does

This chapter expands cause-result connectors into multi-sentence chains, introduces modal recommendation with चाहिए, ज़रूरी है, and बेहतर होगा कि, and adds controlled पड़ना for necessity and consequence. It also teaches the problem-solution paragraph structure used across the rest of Unit II.

Looking Forward

Chapter 8 builds on this chapter's cause-result and recommendation language to interpret news and public-information texts, including source attribution and reported claims.

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

Phrase grouping in cause-result explanation

Longer cause-result sentences are easier to follow when the cause and result form two distinct phrase groups, with a brief pause at the connector. Practice reading सड़क पर गाड़ियों की संख्या बढ़ गई है, इसलिए स्कूल के पास पार्किंग की समस्या और गंभीर हो गई है aloud, pausing briefly after बढ़ गई है and before इसलिए.

Intonation for recommendation

बेहतर होगा कि and ज़रूरी है कि are usually spoken with a level, measured pitch rather than a sharp or commanding tone. Contrast the flatter, more assertive यह करो (a direct command) with the softer, more deliberative बेहतर होगा कि हम यह करें.

Schwa deletion in longer abstract and formal words

Practice schwa deletion in this chapter's longer vocabulary: ज़िम्मेदारी (zim-me-DAA-ree, not zim-me-daa-REE-a), समाधान (sa-maa-DHAAN), and प्राथमिकता (praath-mik-TAA). Read each aloud in isolation, then inside a full sentence from the Model Dialogue below.

Listening for problem, cause, result, and proposed action

While listening to the Model Dialogue and Listening Practice passage, listen specifically for (1) what the problem is, (2) what caused it, (3) what result or effect it has had, and (4) what the speaker proposes to do about it.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces two closely related systems: cause-result chains that connect a cause to its result across one or more sentences, and modal recommendation language used to propose a realistic solution. Both systems support the problem-solution paragraph structure introduced at the end of this section.

7.4 Cause-Result Chains

Form: A cause-result chain names a cause, then explicitly connects it to a result using इसलिए, इस वजह से, के कारण, की वजह से, or जिससे.

Language Building Block — Cause + इसलिए + Result
पिछले कुछ सालों में स्कूल के पास गाड़ियों की संख्या बहुत बढ़ गई है। इसलिए, सुबह के समय सड़क पर बहुत भीड़ हो जाती है।
Over the past few years, the number of vehicles near the school has increased a lot. Therefore, the road becomes very crowded in the morning.

Meaning: इसलिए and इस वजह से open a new sentence that presents the result of the previously stated cause. के कारण and की वजह से instead attach the cause directly to the result within one sentence, functioning like a postpositional phrase: गाड़ियों की बढ़ती संख्या के कारण, सड़क पर भीड़ हो जाती है।

Use: जिससे links a clause to its consequence more tightly than इसलिए: पार्किंग की जगह कम है, जिससे अभिभावकों को गाड़ी दूर खड़ी करनी पड़ती है। ("Parking space is limited, because of which parents have to park the car far away.") Do not stack more than two cause-result links in a single sentence; split longer explanations into separate sentences for clarity.

7.5 नतीजा यह हुआ कि...

Language Building Block — Formal Result Clause
कई अभिभावकों ने अपनी गाड़ियाँ स्कूल के गेट के ठीक सामने खड़ी करनी शुरू कर दीं। नतीजा यह हुआ कि बच्चों के लिए पैदल चलना मुश्किल हो गया।
Many parents began parking their cars right in front of the school gate. The result was that it became difficult for children to walk.

Form: नतीजा यह हुआ कि opens a full clause describing a consequence, and is slightly more formal than इसलिए.

Use: Reserve नतीजा यह हुआ कि for a clear, notable consequence, especially in writing; इसलिए remains the more common everyday connector in speech.

7.6 Modal Recommendation

Language Building Block — Softened Recommendation
मेरे हिसाब से, बेहतर होगा कि स्कूल गेट के पास एक अलग पार्किंग क्षेत्र बनाए।
In my view, it would be better if the school created a separate parking area near the gate.

Form: चाहिए attaches directly to the infinitive stem (बनाना चाहिए); ज़रूरी है and बेहतर होगा are typically followed by कि + a full clause.

Meaning: चाहिए expresses a general obligation or advisability; ज़रूरी है कि expresses stronger necessity; बेहतर होगा कि is the softest of the three, framing the recommendation as one reasonable option rather than an obligation.

Use: Choose among the three based on how strongly the speaker wants to insist on the recommendation and how the audience might receive directness. बेहतर होगा कि is often the safest default in a mixed group discussing a shared problem.

7.7 Controlled पड़ना for Necessity and Consequence

Language Building Block — Necessity with पड़ना
पार्किंग की जगह न होने के कारण, अभिभावकों को गाड़ी दूर खड़ी करनी पड़ती है।
Because there is no parking space, parents have to park their car far away.

Form: The light verb पड़ना follows an infinitive agreeing with its object, as in करनी पड़ती है or जाना पड़ा.

Meaning: पड़ना expresses that an action becomes necessary because of circumstances beyond the subject's control, distinct from चाहिए, which expresses advice or general obligation rather than forced necessity.

Use: This chapter introduces पड़ना only in familiar, controlled patterns describing consequence (...पड़ती है, ...पड़ा). Full productive control of the compound-verb system continues to develop across later chapters.

7.8 Paragraph Organization: Problem, Cause, Result, Solution

Organize a short spoken or written problem-solution explanation using four parts:

  1. Problem — name the problem clearly: स्कूल के पास पार्किंग एक बड़ी समस्या बन गई है।
  2. Cause — explain why, using क्योंकि or के कारण: ...क्योंकि गाड़ियों की संख्या बढ़ गई है।
  3. Result — connect the cause to its effect, using इसलिए or नतीजा यह हुआ कि: इसलिए, बच्चों के लिए पैदल चलना मुश्किल हो गया है।
  4. Solution — propose a realistic next step: मेरा सुझाव है कि स्कूल एक अलग पार्किंग क्षेत्र बनाए।

Do not introduce policy analysis, legal argument, or specialized economic vocabulary in this chapter; the four-part structure above is the complete Chapter 7 target.

Model Dialogue

Setting: At the conversation group's seventh meeting, Meera जी introduces a specific community problem — traffic and parking near Priya's and Kabir's neighborhood school — and the group works through its causes, results, and possible solutions.

मीरा जी आज हम एक असली समस्या पर बात करेंगे। प्रिया, तुमने पिछली बार अपने स्कूल के पास पार्किंग की समस्या का ज़िक्र किया था। क्या तुम इसे थोड़ा और समझा सकती हो? Today we'll talk about a real problem. Priya, last time you mentioned the parking problem near your school. Can you explain it a bit more?
प्रिया ज़रूर। पिछले कुछ सालों में हमारे इलाक़े में गाड़ियों की संख्या बहुत बढ़ गई है। इसलिए, स्कूल के पास सुबह के समय बहुत भीड़ हो जाती है। Sure. Over the past few years, the number of vehicles in our area has increased a lot. Therefore, there's a lot of crowding near the school in the morning.
कबीर इसका क्या असर पड़ता है? What effect does this have?
प्रिया कई अभिभावकों ने अपनी गाड़ियाँ स्कूल के गेट के ठीक सामने खड़ी करनी शुरू कर दीं, जिससे बच्चों के लिए पैदल चलना मुश्किल हो गया है। नतीजा यह हुआ कि कुछ हफ़्ते पहले एक छोटी-सी दुर्घटना भी हुई। Many parents started parking their cars right in front of the school gate, because of which it has become difficult for children to walk. The result was that a small accident even happened a few weeks ago.
आदित्य यह तो गंभीर बात है। मेरे हिसाब से, ज़रूरी है कि स्कूल इस समस्या को जल्दी हल करे। That's a serious matter. In my view, it's necessary for the school to solve this problem quickly.
कबीर पर समाधान क्या हो सकता है? पार्किंग की जगह न होने के कारण, अभिभावकों को गाड़ी वहीं खड़ी करनी पड़ती है। But what could the solution be? Because there's no parking space, parents have to park the car right there.
प्रिया मेरा सुझाव है कि स्कूल गेट से थोड़ी दूर एक अलग पार्किंग क्षेत्र बनाए, ताकि गाड़ियाँ सीधे गेट के सामने न खड़ी हों। My suggestion is that the school create a separate parking area a bit away from the gate, so that cars don't park directly in front of the gate.
आदित्य यह एक अच्छा विचार है, लेकिन इसमें ख़र्च भी होगा। शायद स्कूल और अभिभावकों को मिलकर इसका ख़र्च उठाना पड़े। That's a good idea, but it will also cost something. Perhaps the school and the parents will have to bear the cost together.
कबीर एक और आसान कदम भी हो सकता है — स्कूल शुरू होने और छूटने का समय थोड़ा बदल सकता है, ताकि सारी गाड़ियाँ एक ही समय पर न आएँ। There could be another simple step too — the school could slightly change its start and dismissal times, so that all the cars don't arrive at the same time.
मीरा जी तो, अंत में, क्या हम कह सकते हैं कि पार्किंग क्षेत्र बनाना और समय में बदलाव करना, दोनों मिलकर इस समस्या को कम कर सकते हैं? So, in conclusion, can we say that creating a parking area and changing the timing, together, could reduce this problem?
प्रिया जी हाँ, मेरे हिसाब से यह एक व्यावहारिक समाधान है। Yes, in my view, this is a practical solution.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. प्रिया के स्कूल के पास पार्किंग की समस्या का क्या कारण है? (What is the cause of the parking problem near Priya's school?)
  2. इस समस्या का क्या नतीजा हुआ? (What was the result of this problem?)
  3. कबीर और प्रिया कौन-से दो समाधान सुझाते हैं? (What two solutions do Kabir and Priya suggest?)

Reading Practice

Read the short advice-column response below, in which a community-forum writer replies to a resident's question about a shared problem, and then answer the questions.

सामुदायिक मंच — Community Forum: सलाह स्तंभ — Advice Column
आपने जो समस्या बताई है, वह कई मोहल्लों में आम है। सड़कों पर कूड़ा जमा होने का मुख्य कारण यह है कि कूड़ा उठाने की गाड़ी हफ़्ते में सिर्फ़ एक बार आती है, जबकि घरों से कूड़ा हर दिन निकलता है। इस वजह से, लोग कूड़ा सड़क के किनारे रख देते हैं, जिससे मोहल्ले में गंदगी और बदबू फैलती है। इसका एक नुकसान यह भी है कि बरसात के मौसम में यह समस्या और गंभीर हो जाती है। मेरे हिसाब से, ज़रूरी है कि नगर निगम कूड़ा उठाने की व्यवस्था को सप्ताह में कम से कम तीन दिन करे। इसका एक फ़ायदा यह होगा कि सड़कें साफ़ रहेंगी, हालाँकि इसमें नगर निगम को अतिरिक्त ख़र्च भी उठाना पड़ेगा। बेहतर होगा कि पहले एक छोटे इलाक़े में यह बदलाव करके देखा जाए, और अगर यह कारगर हो, तो इसे पूरे शहर में लागू किया जाए। अंत में, मेरा मानना है कि यह समस्या धीरे-धीरे ही हल होगी, एक दिन में नहीं।
The problem you've described is common in many neighborhoods. The main cause of garbage piling up on the streets is that the garbage truck comes only once a week, while garbage comes out of homes every day. Because of this, people leave garbage on the roadside, which spreads dirt and smell in the neighborhood. One disadvantage of this is that the problem becomes even more serious during the rainy season. In my view, it's necessary for the municipal corporation to make the garbage collection system at least three days a week. One benefit of this would be that the streets would stay clean, although this will also require extra cost for the municipal corporation. It would be better to first try this change in one small area, and if it works, apply it across the whole city. Finally, I believe this problem will be solved gradually, not in one day.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. Identify the main cause of the garbage problem, and the result described in the second sentence.
  2. Where does the writer mention a disadvantage, and what is it?
  3. What solution does the writer propose, and what hedge or concession accompanies it?
  4. How does the writer's conclusion frame the timeline for solving the problem?

Writing Workshop

Write a short problem-solution paragraph, एक समस्या और उसका समाधान ("A Problem and Its Solution"), about a practical problem in your community, school, or workplace (for example, traffic, water supply, waste collection, noise, or another topic your instructor approves).

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write a connected 130-190 word paragraph in standard Devanagari that includes:

  1. A clearly named problem, stated in the first sentence.
  2. At least one cause, introduced with क्योंकि or के कारण.
  3. At least one result, introduced with इसलिए, जिससे, or नतीजा यह हुआ कि.
  4. At least one advantage or disadvantage of your proposed solution.
  5. A realistic recommendation, using चाहिए, ज़रूरी है कि, or बेहतर होगा कि.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • The problem, cause, result, and solution are all present and appear in a logical order.
  • Cause-result connectors are used correctly and are not overused in a single sentence.
  • The recommendation uses an appropriately softened register for the audience.
  • If पड़ना is used, the infinitive agrees correctly with its object.
  • Gender, number, agreement, and oblique forms are applied correctly throughout.
  • Spelling, matras, conjuncts, word spacing, and dandas are correct throughout.

Speaking Practice

नियंत्रित अभ्यास — Controlled practice: समस्या और कारण

With a partner, take turns naming a practical problem (traffic, water, waste, noise) and explaining its cause using क्योंकि or के कारण.

संचार कार्य — Communicative task: समाधान सुझाना

After your partner explains a problem and its result, propose a realistic solution using बेहतर होगा कि or ज़रूरी है कि, and mention one advantage and one disadvantage of your proposal.

अकेले अभ्यास — Solo alternative

Record a one- to two-minute spoken explanation of a practical problem, following the problem-cause-result-solution structure, and then listen back to check that each part is clearly present.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Softening advice and recommendations

As the Model Dialogue and Reading passage show, recommendations about shared problems are often softened through forms like बेहतर होगा कि or by acknowledging cost and difficulty (इसमें ख़र्च भी होगा) before proposing a next step. A direct, unhedged command can sound blunt in many settings, especially when the problem affects a whole community rather than one individual. Directness is valued differently across relationships, institutions, and individual speakers, so learners should adjust based on context.

Culture in Context — Community problems as shared responsibility

Both the Model Dialogue and the Reading passage frame community problems as issues to be solved collectively (पूरे मोहल्ले को मिलकर काम करना होगा), rather than assigning blame to one person or group. This reflects a common pattern in everyday problem-solving conversation, though it is not the only possible approach, and learners should avoid assuming this reflects one fixed cultural rule for every context or region.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Stacking too many cause-result connectors in one sentence

Error: Combining क्योंकि, इसलिए, and जिससे in a single overloaded sentence, such as क्योंकि गाड़ियाँ बढ़ गईं, इसलिए भीड़ हो गई, जिससे दुर्घटना हुई, इसलिए...

Correction: Split the explanation into two or three shorter sentences: गाड़ियों की संख्या बढ़ गई है। इसलिए, सड़क पर भीड़ हो जाती है। इस वजह से, एक छोटी दुर्घटना भी हुई।

Common Mistake — Confusing चाहिए and पड़ना

Error: Using पड़ना to give general advice, as in स्कूल को पार्किंग बनानी पड़ती है when the intended meaning is a recommendation, not a forced necessity.

Correction: Use चाहिए or बेहतर होगा कि for advice and recommendation: स्कूल को पार्किंग बनानी चाहिए। Reserve पड़ना for situations forced by circumstance: पार्किंग की जगह न होने के कारण, अभिभावकों को गाड़ी दूर खड़ी करनी पड़ती है।

Common Mistake — Proposing a solution with no acknowledged cost or drawback

Error: Presenting a solution as if it has no disadvantages at all, such as stating only स्कूल को पार्किंग क्षेत्र बनाना चाहिए without any mention of cost, difficulty, or trade-off.

Correction: Acknowledge at least one advantage and one disadvantage before or after the recommendation: मेरा सुझाव है कि स्कूल पार्किंग क्षेत्र बनाए, हालाँकि इसमें ख़र्च भी लगेगा।

Answer Key

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. The cause is the sharp increase in the number of vehicles in the area over the past few years, leading to crowding near the school in the morning.
  2. The result was that parents began parking directly in front of the school gate, making it difficult for children to walk, and a small accident occurred a few weeks ago.
  3. Priya suggests building a separate parking area away from the gate; Kabir suggests slightly changing the school's start and dismissal times so cars don't all arrive at once.

Listening

  • The problem is that the water supply in Rajesh's neighborhood has become very irregular over the past few months.
  • The cause, according to Rajesh, is that new buildings are being constructed very quickly, increasing the demand for water.
  • The result is that many homes get water only two or three days a week, so people have to store water.
  • Rajesh suggests first checking the old pipes, since the real problem may be old, damaged pipelines rather than a genuine water shortage, and that neighbors should send a written complaint to the municipal corporation together.

Reading — Comprehension

  1. The main cause is that the garbage truck comes only once a week while garbage is produced daily; the result is that people leave garbage on the roadside, spreading dirt and smell.
  2. The disadvantage appears in: इसका एक नुकसान यह भी है कि बरसात के मौसम में यह समस्या और गंभीर हो जाती है।
  3. The writer proposes increasing garbage collection to at least three days a week, hedged with हालाँकि इसमें नगर निगम को अतिरिक्त ख़र्च भी उठाना पड़ेगा, acknowledging the added cost.
  4. The conclusion frames the problem as one that will be solved gradually, not immediately, tempering expectations rather than promising a quick fix.
