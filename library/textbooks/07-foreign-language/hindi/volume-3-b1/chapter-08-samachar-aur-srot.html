समाचार और स्रोत

Volume III, Chapter 8: News and Sources · Speak and Write Hindi: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Understand the main idea of an adapted Hindi news item or public-information text.
  2. Identify the source, speaker, event, time, place, and reported claim in a news text.
  3. Distinguish fact, report, quote, opinion, and uncertainty in a news or public text.
  4. Summarize a short news item or interview orally and in writing.
  5. Ask about where a piece of information came from.
  6. Write a short, neutral summary with clear source attribution.

Communication Scenario

At the group's eighth meeting, Meera जी brings a short local news item to class: a community news website has published a short report about the school parking problem the group discussed in Chapter 7. The group reads and listens to the report together, then practices separating what the report actually says from what they personally think about it.

  1. Notice how the report opens by naming a source before giving any claim: रिपोर्ट के अनुसार.
  2. Notice how quoted words are introduced with कहा कि, बताया कि, or a directly quoted sentence.
  3. Notice how the report marks uncertainty with phrases like यह स्पष्ट नहीं है कि rather than stating an unconfirmed claim as fact.
  4. This chapter's task is to move from explaining a problem in your own words (Chapter 7) to reporting what a source says about a problem, while keeping your own opinion clearly separate.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 8 continues Unit II, विचार, प्रमाण, और सार्वजनिक संवाद. It builds directly on Chapter 7's cause-result and recommendation language by adding reported speech, source attribution, and neutral summary organization drawn from adapted news and public-information texts.

Coming Into This Chapter

Learners arrive from Chapter 7 with cause-result connectors (इसलिए, के कारण, जिससे), a formal result clause (नतीजा यह हुआ कि), and modal recommendation language (चाहिए, ज़रूरी है कि, बेहतर होगा कि). They also bring Volume II's controlled use of कि to report simple information.

What This Chapter Does

This chapter introduces reported speech with कहा कि, बताया कि, पूछा कि, and लिखा कि; source attribution with के अनुसार and के मुताबिक़; and receptive-to-controlled passive-like public-information patterns such as कहा गया and माना जा रहा है. It also teaches the problem of keeping a neutral summary separate from personal opinion.

Looking Forward

Chapter 9 builds on this chapter's source-based reading and reporting skills to compare options, weigh advantages and disadvantages, and write a structured recommendation.

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

Identifying names, places, numbers, and dates

News passages pack a lot of specific information — names, places, numbers, and dates — into a small space. Before listening, scan the Listening Practice questions below so you know which details to listen for, then let the surrounding sentence carry the meaning rather than trying to catch every word.

Pace and intonation in news reading

Read-aloud news style tends to be more measured and evenly paced than casual conversation, with a slight pause after a source attribution phrase. Practice reading रिपोर्ट के अनुसार, ‑‑ स्कूल के पास पार्किंग की समस्या पर नगर निगम ने ध्यान देना शुरू कर दिया है aloud, pausing briefly after के अनुसार.

Schwa deletion in formal news vocabulary

Practice schwa deletion in this chapter's longer vocabulary: अंतरराष्ट्रीय (an-tar-raash-TREE-ya), प्रशासन (pra-SHAA-san), and अधिकारी (a-dhi-KAA-ree). Read each aloud in isolation, then inside a full sentence from the Model Dialogue below.

Listening for who said what and where information came from

While listening to the Model Dialogue and Listening Practice passage, listen specifically for (1) what the source is, (2) what claim or information is reported, (3) who is quoted directly, and (4) whether anything is marked as unconfirmed or unclear.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces two closely related systems: reported speech with reporting verbs such as कहा कि, बताया कि, पूछा कि, and लिखा कि, and source attribution with के अनुसार and के मुताबिक़. Both systems support the neutral-summary organization introduced at the end of this section.

8.4 Reported Statements with कहा कि, बताया कि, पूछा कि, and लिखा कि

Form: A reporting verb is followed by कि and a full clause containing the reported content.

Language Building Block — Reporting Verb + कि + Clause
नगर निगम के एक अधिकारी ने बताया कि स्कूल के पास एक नई पार्किंग जगह पर विचार किया जा रहा है।
A municipal-corporation official said that a new parking space near the school is being considered.

Meaning: कहा कि reports a general statement; बताया कि reports information given to someone; पूछा कि reports a question, often combined with an indirect question word (see 8.5); लिखा कि reports written content.

Use: Keep the reported clause close to the source's actual meaning. Do not silently add your own opinion inside a reported clause; if you want to add your own view, mark it clearly as separate, as in रिपोर्ट में कहा गया कि..., लेकिन मेरे हिसाब से...

8.5 Indirect Questions Inside Reporting Frames

Language Building Block — पूछा कि + Question Word
एक अभिभावक ने पूछा कि नई पार्किंग जगह कब तैयार होगी।
A parent asked when the new parking space would be ready.

Form: पूछा कि is followed by a question word (क्या, कब, कहाँ, क्यों) inside a statement-order clause rather than a direct question.

Use: When the original question was a yes-no question, keep क्या as the reporting question word: उसने पूछा कि क्या पार्किंग जगह मुफ़्त होगी। ("They asked whether the parking space would be free.")

8.6 Source Attribution with के अनुसार and के मुताबिक़

Language Building Block — Source + के अनुसार + Claim
स्थानीय समाचार वेबसाइट की एक रिपोर्ट के अनुसार, नगर निगम स्कूल के पास पार्किंग की समस्या पर ध्यान दे रहा है।
According to a report on a local news website, the municipal corporation is paying attention to the parking problem near the school.

Form: के अनुसार and के मुताबिक़ attach to a named source (a person, report, or organization) and typically open the sentence, often followed by a comma.

Meaning: Both mean "according to"; के मुताबिक़ is the more everyday alternative and is common in spoken and less formal written Hindi, while के अनुसार is common in both formal and everyday writing.

Use: Always name the source explicitly the first time a claim is introduced. Do not present a source's claim as your own established fact; keep the attribution visible, especially for claims that have not been confirmed.

8.7 Passive-Like Public-Information Patterns (Receptive-to-Controlled)

Form: Forms such as कहा गया, बताया गया, and माना जा रहा है present information without naming who is speaking, which is common in news and public texts.

Meaning: कहा गया है कि... ("it has been said that...") and माना जा रहा है कि... ("it is believed that...") let a writer report information whose speaker is unnamed or generalized.

Use: Recognize these forms in reading and listening, and you may reproduce short, familiar instances such as माना जा रहा है कि... in your own writing. Full productive control of the passive system is not a Chapter 8 requirement; do not attempt to generate unfamiliar passive forms freely.

8.8 Marking Uncertainty: यह स्पष्ट नहीं है कि

Language Building Block — Marking an Unconfirmed Claim
यह स्पष्ट नहीं है कि नई पार्किंग जगह कब तक बन जाएगी।
It is not clear when the new parking space will be completed.

Use: When a report does not confirm a detail, mark it with यह स्पष्ट नहीं है कि or इसकी पुष्टि नहीं हुई है कि rather than presenting the detail as settled fact.

8.9 Paragraph Organization: Neutral Summary with Source Attribution

Organize a short neutral summary using four parts:

  1. Source — name the source first: एक स्थानीय समाचार वेबसाइट की रिपोर्ट के अनुसार...
  2. Main claim — report the central piece of information: ...नगर निगम स्कूल के पास एक नई पार्किंग जगह पर विचार कर रहा है।
  3. Quoted or reported statement — include at least one attributed statement: एक अधिकारी ने बताया कि यह काम अगले कुछ महीनों में शुरू हो सकता है।
  4. Uncertainty, if present — mark any unconfirmed detail clearly: यह स्पष्ट नहीं है कि काम कब पूरा होगा।

Do not insert personal opinion into a neutral summary; the four-part structure above is the complete Chapter 8 target.

Model Dialogue

Setting: At the conversation group's eighth meeting, Meera जी brings a short local news report about the school parking problem from Chapter 7, and the group practices separating the report's content from their own opinions.

मीरा जी आज मैं एक छोटी-सी ख़बर लाई हूँ। एक स्थानीय समाचार वेबसाइट की रिपोर्ट के अनुसार, प्रिया के स्कूल के पास पार्किंग की समस्या पर अब नगर निगम ध्यान दे रहा है। Today I've brought a small news item. According to a report on a local news website, the municipal corporation is now paying attention to the parking problem near Priya's school.
प्रिया सच में? रिपोर्ट में क्या बताया गया है? Really? What does the report say?
मीरा जी रिपोर्ट के मुताबिक़, नगर निगम के एक अधिकारी ने बताया कि स्कूल के गेट से थोड़ी दूर एक नई पार्किंग जगह पर विचार किया जा रहा है। According to the report, a municipal-corporation official said that a new parking space a bit away from the school gate is being considered.
कबीर यह तो हमारे सुझाव जैसा ही है! क्या रिपोर्ट में यह भी बताया गया कि यह काम कब शुरू होगा? That's just like our suggestion! Does the report also say when this work will start?
मीरा जी उस अधिकारी ने कहा कि यह काम अगले कुछ महीनों में शुरू हो सकता है, लेकिन यह स्पष्ट नहीं है कि काम कब तक पूरा होगा। That official said this work could start in the next few months, but it's not clear when the work will be completed.
आदित्य क्या रिपोर्ट में स्कूल के समय में बदलाव के बारे में भी कुछ कहा गया? Does the report also say anything about a change in the school's timing?
मीरा जी नहीं, रिपोर्ट में केवल पार्किंग जगह का ज़िक्र है। एक अभिभावक ने रिपोर्टर से पूछा कि क्या स्कूल का समय भी बदलेगा, लेकिन इसकी पुष्टि नहीं हुई है। No, the report only mentions the parking space. A parent asked the reporter whether the school's timing would also change, but this has not been confirmed.
प्रिया मेरे हिसाब से, यह अच्छी ख़बर है, भले ही अभी सब कुछ पक्का न हो। पर हमें रिपोर्ट को अपनी राय से अलग रखना चाहिए, है न? In my view, this is good news, even if everything isn't confirmed yet. But we should keep the report separate from our own opinion, right?
मीरा जी बिल्कुल सही। रिपोर्ट के अनुसार जो बताया गया है, वह एक बात है; हम इसके बारे में क्या सोचते हैं, वह दूसरी बात है। Exactly right. What the report says is one thing; what we think about it is another thing.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. रिपोर्ट का स्रोत क्या है? (What is the source of the report?)
  2. रिपोर्ट में नगर निगम के अधिकारी ने क्या बताया? (What did the municipal-corporation official say, according to the report?)
  3. रिपोर्ट में कौन-सी बात स्पष्ट नहीं है? (What is not clear according to the report?)

Listening Practice

Ask your instructor, a study partner, or a fluent Hindi speaker to read the passage below aloud at a natural, news-reading pace. If no speaker is available, this is a Listening Script for Independent Use: read it slowly first, then at a natural pace. Any spoken model should be verified by a fluent Hindi speaker before being treated as a pronunciation standard.

"नमस्कार, यह आज की एक छोटी स्थानीय ख़बर है। एक स्थानीय समाचार वेबसाइट की रिपोर्ट के अनुसार, शहर के पूर्वी इलाक़े में एक सामुदायिक पुस्तकालय जल्द ही फिर से खुलने जा रहा है। पुस्तकालय की एक कर्मचारी ने बताया कि यह पुस्तकालय पिछले साल मरम्मत के कारण बंद कर दिया गया था। उसने कहा कि अब मरम्मत का काम लगभग पूरा हो चुका है और पुस्तकालय अगले महीने खुल सकता है। एक स्थानीय निवासी ने रिपोर्टर से पूछा कि क्या पुस्तकालय में नई किताबें भी होंगी। रिपोर्ट के मुताबिक़, नगर निगम के एक अधिकारी ने कहा कि कुछ नई किताबें ज़रूर आएँगी, लेकिन यह स्पष्ट नहीं है कि कुल कितनी नई किताबें होंगी। रिपोर्ट में यह भी बताया गया कि पुस्तकालय खुलने की सही तारीख़ की अभी तक पुष्टि नहीं हुई है। स्थानीय लोग इस ख़बर से ख़ुश हैं, हालाँकि कुछ लोगों ने कहा कि उन्हें लंबे इंतज़ार से थोड़ी निराशा भी हुई है।"

Reading Practice

Read the short adapted news notice below, published on a community website, and then answer the questions.

सामुदायिक समाचार वेबसाइट — Community News Website: छोटी ख़बर — Short Report
नगर निगम के अनुसार, शहर के तीन इलाक़ों में सड़क की मरम्मत का काम अगले हफ़्ते से शुरू होगा। नगर निगम के एक प्रवक्ता ने बताया कि यह काम पिछले साल की गई एक शिकायत के बाद शुरू किया जा रहा है, जिसमें कई निवासियों ने सड़कों की ख़राब हालत के बारे में लिखा था। प्रवक्ता ने कहा कि काम के दौरान कुछ सड़कें अस्थायी रूप से बंद रहेंगी, इसलिए लोगों को वैकल्पिक रास्ते का इस्तेमाल करना पड़ सकता है। एक स्थानीय दुकानदार ने रिपोर्टर से पूछा कि क्या काम के दौरान दुकानों तक पहुँचना मुश्किल होगा। इस पर प्रवक्ता ने कहा कि नगर निगम कोशिश करेगा कि दुकानों तक रास्ता हमेशा खुला रहे, हालाँकि इसकी पूरी गारंटी नहीं दी जा सकती। रिपोर्ट में यह स्पष्ट नहीं है कि मरम्मत का पूरा काम कितने समय में ख़त्म होगा, हालाँकि माना जा रहा है कि इसमें कई महीने लग सकते हैं।
According to the municipal corporation, road repair work in three areas of the city will begin next week. A municipal-corporation spokesperson said that this work is being started following a complaint filed last year, in which several residents wrote about the poor condition of the roads. The spokesperson said that some roads will be temporarily closed during the work, so people may have to use alternative routes. A local shopkeeper asked the reporter whether it would be difficult to reach the shops during the work. To this, the spokesperson said that the municipal corporation will try to keep the route to the shops open at all times, although this cannot be fully guaranteed. It is not clear from the report how long the full repair work will take, although it is believed that it could take several months.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. Identify the source of this report and the main claim it reports.
  2. What did the municipal-corporation spokesperson say caused this work to begin?
  3. What question did the local shopkeeper ask, and how did the spokesperson respond?
  4. Identify the sentence that marks something as unconfirmed, and explain what remains unclear.

Writing Workshop

Write a short neutral summary, एक छोटी ख़बर का सारांश ("A Short News Summary"), of a real or invented local news item (for example, a school, library, park, road, or public-service update your instructor approves).

लेखन कार्य — Writing task: Write a connected 130-190 word paragraph in standard Devanagari that includes:

  1. A named source, introduced with के अनुसार or के मुताबिक़ in the first sentence.
  2. At least one reported statement, introduced with कहा कि, बताया कि, or पूछा कि.
  3. At least one specific detail such as a name, place, number, or date.
  4. At least one marked uncertainty, using यह स्पष्ट नहीं है कि or इसकी पुष्टि नहीं हुई है कि.
  5. No personal opinion presented as part of the report itself.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • The source is named clearly and appears before or alongside the first claim.
  • Reported statements are introduced with an appropriate reporting verb and कि.
  • Uncertainty is marked rather than presented as settled fact.
  • Personal opinion, if included at all, is clearly separated from the reported content.
  • Gender, number, agreement, and oblique forms are applied correctly throughout.
  • Spelling, matras, conjuncts, word spacing, and dandas are correct throughout.

नियंत्रित अभ्यास — Controlled practice: स्रोत बताना

With a partner, take turns inventing a one-sentence news claim and introducing it with रिपोर्ट के अनुसार or के मुताबिक़.

संचार कार्य — Communicative task: ख़बर सुनाना

अकेले अभ्यास — Solo alternative

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Media Hindi varies by outlet, audience, and register

Hindi news style varies considerably across national newspapers, local community websites, television broadcasts, and social media. Headlines are often more compressed and formal than conversation, while community reports like the ones in this chapter tend to read more like connected prose. Learners should expect variation across sources rather than assuming one fixed "news Hindi."

Culture in Context — Distinguishing reporting from endorsement

Reporting what a source says is not the same as agreeing with it. As Priya notes in the Model Dialogue, a careful reader or speaker keeps the report's content separate from their own view, even when they are glad about the news. This distinction matters across many public and professional contexts, not only in journalism, and applies whether the news is positive, negative, or uncertain.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Reporting a claim without naming the source

Error: Writing नई पार्किंग जगह बनेगी as if it were an established fact, without saying where this information came from.

Correction: Name the source before or alongside the claim: रिपोर्ट के अनुसार, नई पार्किंग जगह बनेगी।

Common Mistake — Mixing personal opinion into a reported claim

Error: Writing रिपोर्ट के अनुसार, यह एक बहुत अच्छा फ़ैसला है, which presents a personal judgment as though it were part of the report itself.

Correction: Separate the reported content from personal opinion: रिपोर्ट के अनुसार, नगर निगम नई पार्किंग जगह पर विचार कर रहा है। मेरे हिसाब से, यह एक अच्छा फ़ैसला होगा।

Common Mistake — Presenting an unconfirmed detail as settled

Error: Stating काम अगले महीने पूरा हो जाएगा when the source only said the timeline was uncertain.

Correction: Mark the uncertainty explicitly: यह स्पष्ट नहीं है कि काम कब तक पूरा होगा।

Listening

  1. The source is the municipal corporation; the main claim is that road repair work in three areas will begin next week following a resident complaint about poor road conditions.

  3. The shopkeeper asked whether it would be difficult to reach the shops during the work; the spokesperson said the corporation would try to keep the route open but could not fully guarantee it.
