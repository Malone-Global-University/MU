आँकड़े, रुझान, और व्याख्या

Volume IV, Chapter 8: Data, Trends, and Explanation · Speak and Write Hindi: Professional, Academic, and Cultural Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe data, survey results, percentages, averages, and trends in clear Hindi.
  2. Compare figures using quantitative expressions such as की तुलना में, से अधिक, and से कम.
  3. Explain a possible cause or implication of a trend using cautious, hedged language.
  4. Distinguish fact, inference, and speculation when interpreting numbers.
  5. Ask clarifying questions about a data set or chart.
  6. Write a short, cautiously worded data commentary.

Communication Scenario

Ms. Ahuja's funding meeting went well, and the program director has now asked for one more page: a short explanation of a new district-level survey comparing attendance at rural training centers before and after a transport pilot. Priya has the numbers, but she knows numbers alone will not convince anyone. She needs to describe what changed, compare it carefully to the earlier three-district data from the article, and say — cautiously — what the numbers might mean, without claiming more certainty than the data actually supports.

  1. Notice how Priya reports each figure precisely before saying anything about what it might mean.
  2. Notice that she compares the new survey to the earlier data using clear comparison expressions.
  3. Notice how she signals uncertainty with hedging language whenever she moves from fact to interpretation.
  4. Notice that she avoids claiming a single cause for a trend that could have several explanations.

Note on Numbers in Formal Hindi Prose

Formal written Hindi commonly mixes Devanagari numerals and word-form numbers with Hindi-English terms such as सर्वे, डेटा, or परसेंट in casual speech, while आँकड़े and प्रतिशत remain standard in written reports. This chapter uses the more formal written forms as the productive default.

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

Numbers and percentages in formal delivery

When reading a percentage aloud, such as तीस प्रतिशत (30 percent) or पैंसठ प्रतिशत (65 percent), keep the number and प्रतिशत in one phrase group, with a slight rise on the number and a fall on प्रतिशत. Do not pause between the number and the word — a pause there can make the figure sound uncertain, as if you are unsure of it.

Phrase grouping in comparison sentences

Comparison sentences such as पिछले साल की तुलना में इस साल उपस्थिति बीस प्रतिशत अधिक है contain several phrase units. Group पिछले साल की तुलना में as one breath unit before moving to the main clause, rather than reading the whole sentence as a single unbroken string.

Intonation for cautious interpretation

Hedged interpretation such as यह संभव है कि or हो सकता है कि should sound noticeably more tentative than a plain factual statement. Let the pitch stay slightly level or rise gently through the hedge, saving a clear fall for the end of the full sentence, so the listener hears the caution before the claim.

Listening for statistics and trend descriptions

In the listening passage below, listen once for the specific figures mentioned, and a second time for which sentences are factual reporting and which are the speaker's own cautious interpretation.

According to the data, [figure] is [higher/lower] compared to [last year / the earlier survey]. This suggests [cautious reading]. One possible cause of this trend may be [possible cause], though it would be premature to conclude that [overclaim to avoid].

8.7 Impersonal Data-Reporting Style

Formal data commentary often reports what a survey or data set shows rather than foregrounding the speaker:

Personal framing: मुझे लगता है कि उपस्थिति बढ़ी है।

Impersonal data-reporting style: आँकड़ों से पता चलता है कि उपस्थिति में वृद्धि हुई है।

Both are correct Hindi; the impersonal form is preferred when the claim rests on the data itself rather than the writer's personal impression.

Boundary Reminder

This chapter does not require statistical methodology, sampling theory, or economic terminology. The goal is clear, cautious description of straightforward figures — counts, percentages, averages, and simple comparisons — not technical data analysis.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Priya reviews the new transport-pilot survey results with Deepak before writing her data commentary.

दीपक नए सर्वेक्षण में क्या मिला? What did the new survey find?
प्रिया आँकड़ों के अनुसार, पिछले साल की तुलना में इस साल उपस्थिति बीस प्रतिशत अधिक है। According to the data, attendance this year is twenty percent higher compared to last year.
दीपक यह तो अच्छी खबर है। इसका कारण क्या हो सकता है? That's good news. What could be the cause of this?
प्रिया यह संभव है कि नई बस सेवा का असर हो, क्योंकि यह उन्हीं तीन ज़िलों में शुरू हुई थी जहाँ उपस्थिति सबसे कम थी। It's possible that the new bus service had an effect, because it started in the same three districts where attendance was lowest.
दीपक तो तुम कह सकती हो कि बस सेवा ने उपस्थिति बढ़ाई? So can you say that the bus service increased attendance?
प्रिया इतनी जल्दी नहीं। इससे यह निष्कर्ष निकालना जल्दबाज़ी होगी कि यह अकेला कारण है — उसी समय स्कूल में नई शिक्षिका भी आई थीं, और यह भी एक कारक हो सकता है। Not that quickly. It would be premature to conclude that this is the only cause — a new teacher also joined the school at the same time, and that could also be a factor.
दीपक समझदारी की बात है। तो तुम रिपोर्ट में क्या लिखोगी? That's sensible. So what will you write in the report?
प्रिया मैं लिखूँगी कि उपस्थिति में स्पष्ट वृद्धि हुई है, और इसके पीछे परिवहन में सुधार एक संभावित कारक है, लेकिन अकेला कारण नहीं कहा जा सकता। I'll write that there has been a clear increase in attendance, and that improved transport is one possible contributing factor behind it, but it cannot be called the sole cause.
दीपक यह आँकड़ों के साथ न्याय करने वाला तरीका है। That's a way of doing justice to the data.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. इस साल उपस्थिति में कितने प्रतिशत की वृद्धि हुई है? (By what percentage has attendance increased this year?)
  2. प्रिया एक ही कारण बताने से क्यों बचती हैं? (Why does Priya avoid stating a single cause?)
  3. दूसरा संभावित कारक क्या है? (What is the second possible factor?)

आँकड़ा नोट — Data Note: परिवहन पायलट परियोजना का प्रभाव
तीन ज़िलों में चलाई गई परिवहन पायलट परियोजना के आँकड़े अब उपलब्ध हैं। पायलट शुरू होने से पहले, इन तीन ज़िलों में औसत उपस्थिति दर लगभग पचपन प्रतिशत थी। पायलट के छह महीने बाद, यह दर बढ़कर लगभग पचहत्तर प्रतिशत हो गई — यानी बीस प्रतिशत की वृद्धि।

इसके साथ ही, अभिभावकों के एक छोटे सर्वेक्षण में साठ प्रतिशत से अधिक ने कहा कि दूरी अब पहले जैसी बड़ी समस्या नहीं रही। दस प्रतिशत से कम अभिभावकों ने कहा कि परिवहन अब भी एक बड़ी बाधा है।

इन आँकड़ों से पता चलता है कि परिवहन में सुधार का उपस्थिति पर सकारात्मक प्रभाव पड़ा है। हालाँकि, इसी अवधि में दो नए शिक्षकों की नियुक्ति भी हुई, इसलिए यह निष्कर्ष निकालना जल्दबाज़ी होगी कि वृद्धि का पूरा श्रेय परिवहन को दिया जाए। यह संभव है कि परिवहन और शिक्षक-नियुक्ति, दोनों ने मिलकर यह बदलाव लाया हो। अगले सर्वेक्षण में दोनों कारकों को अलग-अलग मापने की कोशिश की जानी चाहिए।
Data Note: "Impact of the Transport Pilot Project." Data from the transport pilot project run in three districts is now available. Before the pilot began, the average attendance rate in these three districts was about fifty-five percent. Six months after the pilot, this rate rose to about seventy-five percent — a twenty percent increase. Alongside this, in a small survey of parents, more than sixty percent said that distance was no longer as big a problem as before. Fewer than ten percent of parents said transport is still a major obstacle. This data suggests that the improvement in transport has had a positive effect on attendance. However, two new teachers were also appointed during this same period, so it would be premature to conclude that the increase should be fully credited to transport. It is possible that both transport and teacher appointments together brought about this change. The next survey should try to measure both factors separately.

  4. What does the note recommend for the next survey, and why?

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • Every figure stated is precise and matches the source data, not invented or rounded without saying so.
  • At least one quantitative comparison expression is used correctly.
  • Interpretation is clearly separated from fact and marked with a hedging expression.
  • At least one alternative factor is acknowledged rather than a single cause being overclaimed.
  • The commentary avoids specialized statistical or economic terminology.
  • Spelling, matras, conjuncts, numerals, and punctuation are correct throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Caution as a professional norm

In Hindi professional and institutional writing, as in many other professional traditions, overstating what a small or limited data set proves can damage credibility. Careful writers routinely name alternative factors and avoid single-cause claims, as both the Model Dialogue and the Reading Practice note do.

Culture in Context — Hindi and English in data contexts

Media, institutional, and academic-adjacent Hindi frequently mixes Hindi and English data vocabulary — a report might use डेटा alongside आँकड़े, or सर्वे alongside सर्वेक्षण. This is a normal feature of contemporary usage, not a sign of incorrect Hindi, though this chapter's productive model favors the more formal Hindi terms for written commentary.

Culture in Context — Numbers and trust

Numerical claims carry social weight in public and institutional communication. Presenting figures precisely, and marking interpretation as interpretation, helps a Hindi-speaking audience trust both the writer and the data itself.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Stating interpretation as if it were fact

Error: Writing बस सेवा ने उपस्थिति बढ़ाई as a flat, unhedged claim of causation.

Correction: Mark the claim as an interpretation with a hedging expression, as Priya does with यह संभव है कि नई बस सेवा का असर हो.

Common Mistake — Claiming a single cause for a trend

Error: Crediting one factor entirely for a change when other plausible factors exist, as Deepak's first suggestion does before Priya corrects it.

Correction: Name at least one alternative factor and use इससे यह निष्कर्ष निकालना जल्दबाज़ी होगी कि... to signal that more than one explanation remains possible.

Common Mistake — Vague or missing figures

Error: Describing a trend only in general terms, such as उपस्थिति काफ़ी बढ़ी है, without stating the actual figures.

Correction: State the specific figures first, as in आँकड़ों के अनुसार, उपस्थिति दर पचपन प्रतिशत से बढ़कर पचहत्तर प्रतिशत हो गई, and only then move to interpretation.

Answer Key

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. Attendance has increased by twenty percent this year compared to last year.
  2. Priya avoids stating a single cause because a new teacher also joined the school at the same time as the bus service began, so more than one factor could explain the change.
  3. The arrival of a new teacher at the school is the second possible factor.

Listening

  • The average attendance rate this year was around seventy-five percent.
  • The speaker is careful because new teachers were also appointed in the same period, so transport cannot be given full credit alone.
  • संभवतः दोनों कारकों ने मिलकर यह बदलाव लाया हो shows the speaker is speculating rather than stating fact.

Reading — Comprehension

  1. Attendance rose from about fifty-five percent before the pilot to about seventy-five percent after it, a twenty percentage-point increase.
  2. The note mentions improved transport and the appointment of two new teachers as possible explanatory factors.
  3. Model answer: "यह संभव है कि परिवहन और शिक्षक-नियुक्ति, दोनों ने मिलकर यह बदलाव लाया हो" signals caution, using the hedging expression यह संभव है कि.
  4. The note recommends measuring the two factors separately in the next survey, because the current data cannot distinguish how much each factor contributed on its own.

Chapter Review — भाग B

  1. (a) की तुलना में — this expression correctly introduces the point of comparison. (b) लगभग — an approximate figure calls for an approximation marker, not a comparison expression.

Looking Ahead

अब आप क्या कर सकते हैं — What you can do now

You can describe data and trends precisely, compare figures using clear quantitative expressions, and interpret a trend cautiously without overclaiming a single cause. This care in separating fact from interpretation prepares you for building a fuller argument from multiple sources.
