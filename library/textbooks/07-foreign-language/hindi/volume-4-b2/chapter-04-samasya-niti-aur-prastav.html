समस्या, नीति, और प्रस्ताव

Volume IV, Chapter 4: Problems, Policy, and Proposals · Speak and Write Hindi: Professional, Academic, and Cultural Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe an institutional or community problem, including its causes, stakeholders, constraints, and consequences.
  2. Compare two or more possible solutions and justify a recommendation.
  3. Use passive-like and impersonal constructions such as किया गया, माना जाता है, and लागू किया जा सकता है to foreground process rather than agent.
  4. Use recommendation frames such as चाहिए, ज़रूरी है, उचित होगा, and बेहतर होगा.
  5. Discuss risk, benefit, cost, and feasibility in cautious, professional Hindi.
  6. Distinguish respectful critique of a process from personal accusation.
  7. Write a structured proposal or recommendation memo in Hindi.

Communication Scenario

Two weeks after the program review meeting, Priya's revised mid-year plan surfaces a bigger issue: the two schools that started late did so because of a recurring transportation problem for volunteer trainers reaching rural sites. Ms. Ahuja asks Priya to prepare a short problem-solution memo before the next meeting, comparing two options — hiring a shared van service, or shifting some training online — and recommending one. Priya has to describe the problem without blaming any one person, weigh costs and risks for each option, and write a memo that Ms. Ahuja can circulate to the board using calm, impersonal institutional language.

  1. Notice how the problem is described in terms of process and constraint (देरी हुई, पहुँचना मुश्किल होता है) rather than naming who is at fault.
  2. Notice how each option's cost, benefit, and risk is presented in parallel, so the two options can be compared fairly.
  3. Notice how the final recommendation uses बेहतर होगा and सुझाव है कि rather than a blunt command.
  4. Notice how the written memo separates background, analysis, and recommendation into clearly labeled sections.

Note on This Vocabulary

Notice that समस्या, कारण, and परिणाम naturally chain together in a single explanation: a problem has a cause, and a cause has a consequence. Practicing this chain (समस्या...इसका कारण...इसका परिणाम) will make your problem descriptions sound organized rather than list-like.

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

Schwa deletion in formal proposal vocabulary

व्यवहार्यता (vyavahāryatā) and कार्यान्वयन (kāryānvayan) are both long formal compounds with internal schwa deletion. Break each into its component parts (व्यवहार + या + ता; कार्य + अन्वयन) before reading it aloud in a full sentence.

Phrase grouping in long recommendation sentences

A sentence like इस समस्या का मुख्य कारण यह है कि प्रशिक्षकों के लिए ग्रामीण क्षेत्रों तक पहुँचना मुश्किल होता है should be broken into three breath groups rather than read as one long string: the cause statement, the कि-clause opening, and the difficulty itself.

Intonation for balanced evaluation

When comparing two options, Hindi speakers often use a flatter, more even pitch for each option in turn, reserving pitch emphasis for the recommendation itself. Practice saying both options at a similar, level pace, then let your pitch rise slightly on बेहतर होगा or मेरी सिफ़ारिश है कि.

Listening for problem, cause, option, and recommendation

A proposal discussion typically moves through four signal types in order: a problem statement (समस्या यह है कि), a cause (इसका कारण), one or more options (एक विकल्प यह है कि), and a recommendation (सिफ़ारिश है कि). Train your ear to track which of the four you are hearing at each moment.

Grammar and Language Systems

This chapter introduces passive-like and impersonal constructions in controlled productive use, recommendation frames, and cause-consequence-feasibility clauses. All three build on Chapter 3's impersonal meeting-summary style.

4.4 Passive-Like and Impersonal Constructions

Chapter 3 introduced one impersonal pattern for reporting group decisions (यह तय हुआ कि). This chapter widens that pattern into a fuller set of passive-like and impersonal forms used to describe processes, beliefs, and possibilities without naming an agent:

Language Building Block — Passive-Like and Impersonal Frame
[काम] किया गया। ऐसा माना जाता है कि [clause]। [काम] लागू किया जा सकता है। यह कहा गया कि [clause]।
[The task] was done. It is believed/assumed that [clause]. [The task] can be implemented. It was said that [clause].

Compare an active sentence naming an agent (प्रिया ने बजट की समीक्षा की, "Priya reviewed the budget") with an agent-backgrounded version (बजट की समीक्षा की गई, "The budget was reviewed"). The second version is common in reports and proposals when the process matters more than who specifically performed it, or when naming an individual would seem to assign blame or excessive credit.

माना जाता है कि ("it is assumed/believed that") introduces a widely held view without attributing it to one speaker: माना जाता है कि ऑनलाइन प्रशिक्षण लागत कम करता है ("It is generally believed that online training reduces cost"). लागू किया जा सकता है ("can be implemented") describes feasibility without naming who would carry it out: यह योजना अगले महीने से लागू की जा सकती है.

4.5 Recommendation Frames

Four frames express a recommendation with different degrees of force:

FrameForceExample
चाहिएshould — direct but not bluntहमें वैन सेवा पर विचार करना चाहिए।
ज़रूरी हैnecessary — stronger, process-focusedइस समस्या का समाधान ढूँढना ज़रूरी है।
उचित होगाit would be appropriate — softer, formalदोनों विकल्पों की लागत की तुलना करना उचित होगा।
बेहतर होगाit would be better — comparative, softerबेहतर होगा कि हम पहले एक महीने का परीक्षण करें।

Notice the force scale: ज़रूरी है is the strongest, framing an action as necessary rather than merely preferable; उचित होगा and बेहतर होगा are softer and more common in written proposals addressed to a board or committee, since they present a recommendation as reasonable judgment rather than an order.

4.6 Cause, Consequence, and Feasibility Clauses

Problem descriptions link cause and consequence, then evaluate feasibility:

Language Building Block — Cause-Consequence-Feasibility Chain
समस्या यह है कि [clause]। इसका कारण [noun/clause] है। इसका परिणाम यह हुआ कि [clause]। इसे [समाधान] से हल किया जा सकता है।
The problem is that [clause]. Its cause is [noun/clause]. Its consequence was that [clause]. It can be resolved through [solution].

This four-part chain — problem, cause, consequence, feasible solution — organizes a professional problem description far more clearly than a single long sentence attempting to state everything at once.

Boundary Reminder

This chapter introduces passive-like and impersonal constructions only in the controlled patterns shown above (agent-backgrounded past participles, माना जाता है कि, लागू किया जा सकता है). It does not introduce a full passive paradigm across all tenses, causatives, or advanced agentless constructions — those remain for later volumes. Everything else in this chapter recycles Chapter 3's reported-decision and hedging systems.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Priya's office, two days before the next program review meeting. Priya and Neha are drafting the problem-solution memo together before Priya presents it to Ms. Ahuja.

नेहा तो समस्या को हम कैसे लिखें? सीधे यह कहना ठीक नहीं लगता कि किसी स्कूल या किसी व्यक्ति की गलती है। So how should we describe the problem? It doesn't seem right to directly say it's any school's or any person's fault.
प्रिया बिल्कुल। हम यह लिख सकते हैं — समस्या यह है कि प्रशिक्षकों के लिए ग्रामीण क्षेत्रों के स्कूलों तक समय पर पहुँचना मुश्किल होता है। इसका मुख्य कारण उपलब्ध परिवहन की कमी है। Exactly. We could write — the problem is that it's difficult for trainers to reach rural schools on time. Its main cause is a lack of available transportation.
नेहा और इसका परिणाम यह हुआ कि दो स्कूलों में प्रशिक्षण की शुरुआत में तीन हफ़्ते की देरी हुई, है ना? And its consequence was a three-week delay in the start of training at two schools, right?
प्रिया हाँ, बिल्कुल। अब हमें दो विकल्प देने हैं। पहला विकल्प — एक साझा वैन सेवा किराए पर ली जाए, जो हफ़्ते में तीन बार सभी ग्रामीण स्कूलों में प्रशिक्षकों को पहुँचाए। Yes, exactly. Now we need to give two options. First option — a shared van service could be hired, which would take trainers to all rural schools three times a week.
नेहा इसका लाभ यह है कि प्रशिक्षक व्यक्तिगत रूप से पढ़ा सकते हैं, लेकिन इसकी लागत ज़्यादा है, और बजट की मंज़ूरी में समय लग सकता है। Its benefit is that trainers can teach in person, but its cost is higher, and budget approval might take time.
प्रिया सही कहा। दूसरा विकल्प — कुछ प्रशिक्षण सत्र ऑनलाइन किए जाएँ। माना जाता है कि ऑनलाइन प्रशिक्षण लागत काफ़ी कम करता है, और इसे अगले महीने से जल्दी लागू किया जा सकता है। That's right. Second option — some training sessions could be held online. It is generally believed that online training reduces costs significantly, and it could be implemented quickly starting next month.
नेहा लेकिन इसमें एक जोखिम भी है — कुछ ग्रामीण स्कूलों में इंटरनेट कनेक्शन भरोसेमंद नहीं है। यह बात हमें ज़रूर लिखनी चाहिए। But there's a risk here too — internet connections aren't reliable at some rural schools. We should definitely write this point down.
प्रिया हाँ, ज़रूरी है कि हम दोनों विकल्पों के जोखिम स्पष्ट रूप से बताएँ। मेरी सिफ़ारिश है कि हम दोनों को मिलाएँ — जिन स्कूलों में इंटरनेट भरोसेमंद है, वहाँ ऑनलाइन सत्र हों, और बाकी स्कूलों के लिए वैन सेवा का एक महीने का परीक्षण किया जाए। Yes, it's necessary that we clearly state the risks of both options. My recommendation is that we combine both — schools with reliable internet get online sessions, and for the remaining schools a one-month trial of the van service is done.
नेहा यह एक संतुलित समाधान लगता है। तो बेहतर होगा कि हम मेमो में यही सिफ़ारिश दें, और आहूजा जी के सामने दोनों विकल्पों की तुलना भी रखें। That seems like a balanced solution. So it would be better if we give this same recommendation in the memo, and also present a comparison of both options to Ms. Ahuja.
प्रिया ठीक है। चलो अब इसे लिखित रूप में तैयार करते हैं। Alright. Let's now put this in written form.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. समस्या क्या है, और इसका मुख्य कारण क्या बताया गया? (What is the problem, and what main cause was given?)
  2. दोनों विकल्पों के लाभ और जोखिम क्या हैं? (What are the benefits and risks of both options?)
  3. प्रिया की अंतिम सिफ़ारिश क्या है? (What is Priya's final recommendation?)

Reading Practice

Read the recommendation memo that Priya and Neha prepared for Ms. Ahuja, then answer the questions.

सिफ़ारिश ज्ञापन — Recommendation Memo
विषय: ग्रामीण स्कूलों में प्रशिक्षकों की पहुँच से जुड़ी समस्या
प्रति: आहूजा जी और कार्यक्रम समिति
से: प्रिया और नेहा

पृष्ठभूमि: समस्या यह है कि प्रशिक्षकों के लिए ग्रामीण क्षेत्रों के स्कूलों तक समय पर पहुँचना मुश्किल होता है। इसका मुख्य कारण उपलब्ध परिवहन की कमी है। इसका परिणाम यह हुआ कि दो स्कूलों में प्रशिक्षण की शुरुआत में तीन हफ़्ते की देरी हुई।

विकल्प 1 — साझा वैन सेवा: लाभ — प्रशिक्षक व्यक्तिगत रूप से पढ़ा सकते हैं। जोखिम/लागत — लागत ज़्यादा है, बजट की मंज़ूरी में समय लग सकता है।

विकल्प 2 — आंशिक ऑनलाइन प्रशिक्षण: लाभ — माना जाता है कि यह लागत काफ़ी कम करता है, और इसे जल्दी लागू किया जा सकता है। जोखिम — कुछ स्कूलों में इंटरनेट कनेक्शन भरोसेमंद नहीं है।

सिफ़ारिश: बेहतर होगा कि दोनों विकल्पों को मिलाया जाए — जिन स्कूलों में इंटरनेट भरोसेमंद है, वहाँ ऑनलाइन सत्र चलाए जाएँ, और शेष स्कूलों के लिए वैन सेवा का एक महीने का परीक्षण किया जाए। ज़रूरी है कि परीक्षण के बाद परिणामों की समीक्षा की जाए।
Subject: The problem of trainer access to rural schools. To: Ms. Ahuja and the program committee. From: Priya and Neha. Background: The problem is that it is difficult for trainers to reach rural schools on time. Its main cause is a lack of available transportation. Its consequence was a three-week delay in the start of training at two schools. Option 1 — shared van service: benefit — trainers can teach in person; risk/cost — cost is higher, budget approval may take time. Option 2 — partial online training: benefit — it is believed this significantly reduces cost, and it can be implemented quickly; risk — internet connections are unreliable at some schools. Recommendation: it would be better to combine both options — schools with reliable internet get online sessions, and a one-month trial of the van service is done for the remaining schools. It is necessary that the results be reviewed after the trial.

  1. Describe an institutional or community problem (3-4 sentences) using the problem-cause-consequence chain (समस्या यह है कि...इसका कारण...इसका परिणाम यह हुआ कि...).
  2. Present two options for solving it, giving at least one benefit and one risk or cost for each, using parallel structure.
  3. Use at least one passive-like or impersonal construction (किया गया, माना जाता है कि, or लागू किया जा सकता है).
  4. Write a final recommendation using at least two different recommendation frames (चाहिए, ज़रूरी है, उचित होगा, बेहतर होगा).
  5. Organize the memo with clear labeled sections: background, options, recommendation.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • The problem-cause-consequence chain is complete and does not assign personal blame.
  • Both options are presented in parallel, each with a benefit and a risk or cost.
  • At least one passive-like or impersonal construction is used accurately.
  • The recommendation uses an appropriately softened frame, not a blunt command.
  • Gender, number, agreement, oblique forms, postpositions, and `ने`/`को` patterns are applied correctly throughout.
  • Spelling, matras, conjuncts, word spacing, and punctuation are correct throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Backgrounding blame in problem description

Formal Hindi problem-solving language often avoids naming who caused a problem, focusing instead on the process or constraint involved. This is not evasiveness; it reflects a professional norm that separates diagnosing a process from assigning personal fault.

Culture in Context — Sanskritic vocabulary in institutional writing

Words such as हितधारक, व्यवहार्यता, and कार्यान्वयन are more formal and Sanskritic than their everyday equivalents. This is normal in public-service, institutional, and organizational Hindi, and does not make the writing sound cold or distant — it signals that the document is a formal deliverable rather than casual correspondence.

Culture in Context — Softened recommendations to committees and boards

When writing to a board or committee, Hindi professional writers commonly prefer बेहतर होगा or उचित होगा over a blunt करना चाहिए, since the final decision formally belongs to the group, not to the person writing the memo.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Naming a person as the cause of a problem

Error: विक्रम की वजह से देरी हुई ("The delay happened because of Vikram") used as the default problem statement in a formal memo.

Correction: समस्या यह है कि प्रशिक्षकों के लिए पहुँचना मुश्किल होता है ("The problem is that it's difficult for trainers to reach the site"). Describe the process or constraint, not the individual, unless the task specifically requires identifying personal responsibility.

Common Mistake — Using चाहिए for every recommendation

Error: आपको वैन सेवा चुनना चाहिए ("You should choose the van service") addressed directly to a board or supervisor as the only recommendation frame in a formal memo.

Correction: बेहतर होगा कि वैन सेवा चुनी जाए ("It would be better if the van service were chosen"). Reserve direct चाहिए for less formal or more urgent contexts, and prefer softer, impersonal frames in written proposals to a group.

Common Mistake — Presenting only one option as if no alternative exists

Error: A memo that states only one possible solution and calls it "the solution," without acknowledging any alternative or its tradeoffs.

Correction: Present at least two options with parallel benefit-risk structure (विकल्प 1...लाभ...जोखिम... विकल्प 2...लाभ...जोखिम...) before recommending one, so the reader can see that the choice was actually evaluated.
