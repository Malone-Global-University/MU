पेशेवर परिचय और नेटवर्किंग

Volume IV, Chapter 2: Professional Introductions and Networking · Speak and Write Hindi: Professional, Academic, and Cultural Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Introduce yourself in professional, academic-adjacent, and institutional contexts with appropriate precision.
  2. Describe your responsibilities, expertise, projects, and organizational role using nominalized role vocabulary.
  3. Ask tactful, well-formed questions about another person's work or field.
  4. Open and close a professional conversation appropriately in speech and in writing.
  5. Adjust formality across an email, a meeting, an interview, and an informal professional conversation.
  6. Use professional relationship frames such as के रूप में, के तहत, and से संबंधित accurately.
  7. Write a short professional bio and a register-appropriate networking follow-up message.

Communication Scenario

A week into her new role, Priya attends an inter-organization networking evening that Ms. Ahuja's nonprofit co-hosts with two other Delhi-based community organizations. She meets several new people whose work touches her own — a program officer from a partner organization, a university researcher studying community literacy programs, and, unexpectedly, an old family friend who now works at a government education office. Meanwhile, Aditya's newspaper internship sends him to cover the same event, giving him a professional reason to introduce himself to strangers rather than simply attend socially. Kabir, still deciding whether to take the volunteer coordinator role, tags along as a guest and watches how differently people describe their work depending on who is asking.

  1. Notice how each speaker's self-introduction changes depending on the setting: reception conversation, formal introduction, or interview.
  2. Notice how nominalized role vocabulary (भूमिका, ज़िम्मेदारी, क्षेत्र) lets a speaker describe work precisely without a long explanation.
  3. Notice how `आप` remains the safe default, even between people close in age, until the relationship is clearly established as informal.
  4. Notice how a written follow-up message after the event differs in structure and tone from the spoken conversation itself.

Note on This Vocabulary

Nouns such as भूमिका, ज़िम्मेदारी, and क्षेत्र let a speaker summarize complex work in a short, precise phrase instead of a long descriptive sentence. This compression is one mark of C1-level professional Hindi.

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

Formal name, title, and organization pronunciation

Institutional vocabulary such as संगठन (saṅgaṭhan) and विशेषज्ञता (viśeṣagyatā) contains internal schwa deletion that beginners to this vocabulary often misread. Say each new noun slowly before placing it in a sentence, and notice that the final ता in abstract nouns is always pronounced, never deleted.

Pace and clarity in professional introductions

A first introduction should be delivered a little more slowly and clearly than casual conversation, since the listener has no prior context. Practice saying मेरा नाम... है, और मैं... में काम करता/करती हूँ at a measured, welcoming pace rather than rushing through it.

Listening for role, organization, purpose, and next action

In a networking conversation, the key information is usually compressed into a few phrases: who the person is, what organization or field they represent, and what they propose to do next (आगे संपर्क में रहेंगे, मैं आपको ईमेल भेजूँगा/भेजूँगी). Train your ear to catch these compressed phrases rather than every word.

Grammar and Language Systems

This chapter introduces nominalized role description and a small set of professional relationship frames. Both use recycled Volume III postpositional and clause structures with new C1-level vocabulary and precision.

2.4 Nominalized Role Descriptions

Rather than describing work with a long verbal sentence, C1 Hindi often compresses a role into a noun phrase: मेरी भूमिका कार्यक्रम समन्वयन की है ("My role is program coordination") is more precise and professional-sounding than a long paraphrase. Build these noun phrases from familiar verbs using the pattern verb-stem + -न (infinitive) followed by की/का/के agreement with the head noun:

Language Building Block — Nominalized Role Frame
मेरी भूमिका [कार्य/परियोजना] का/की/के [समन्वयन / प्रबंधन / मूल्यांकन / विकास] करना है। मेरा काम मुख्य रूप से रिपोर्ट लिखने और आँकड़े इकट्ठा करने से जुड़ा है।
My role is to [coordinate / manage / evaluate / develop] [work/project]. My work is mainly connected to writing reports and collecting data.

2.5 Professional Relationship Frames

Five postpositional frames organize most professional self-description in this chapter:

FrameFunctionExample
के रूप मेंin the capacity/role ofमैं यहाँ एक स्वयंसेवक के रूप में काम करती हूँ।
के तहतunder (a program, project, or department)यह परियोजना शिक्षा विभाग के तहत चलती है।
के साथwith, in partnership withहम एक स्थानीय स्कूल के साथ काम करते हैं।
के लिएfor, on behalf ofमैं यह रिपोर्ट संस्था के लिए तैयार कर रही हूँ।
से संबंधितrelated toमेरा काम मुख्य रूप से साक्षरता से संबंधित है।

Notice that के रूप में marks a capacity or role a person occupies, while के तहत marks an institutional or programmatic umbrella. These are easy to confuse for English speakers, since English "as" and "under" do not map predictably onto Hindi postpositions.

Boundary Reminder

This chapter does not introduce new aspect forms, new honorific systems, or new clause types. Nominalization and the five relationship frames above are the only new productive systems; everything else in this chapter recycles Volume III grammar.

Model Dialogue

Setting: The networking evening. Priya is introduced to Dr. Malhotra, a university researcher, by a mutual colleague.

सहकर्मी प्रिया, मैं आपका परिचय डॉ. मल्होत्रा से कराना चाहूँगी। ये दिल्ली विश्वविद्यालय में सामुदायिक शिक्षा पर शोध करती हैं। Priya, I'd like to introduce you to Dr. Malhotra. She researches community education at Delhi University.
प्रिया नमस्ते, डॉ. मल्होत्रा। आपसे मिलकर बहुत खुशी हुई। मैं वर्तमान में एक स्थानीय संस्था में साक्षरता कार्यक्रमों की समन्वयक के रूप में काम करती हूँ। Hello, Dr. Malhotra. Very happy to meet you. I currently work at a local organization as a coordinator of literacy programs.
डॉ. मल्होत्रा दिलचस्प! क्या आपका काम किसी सरकारी योजना के तहत चलता है, या यह पूरी तरह संस्था की अपनी परियोजना है? Interesting! Does your work run under some government scheme, or is it entirely the organization's own project?
प्रिया यह मुख्य रूप से हमारी संस्था की अपनी परियोजना है, लेकिन हम कभी-कभी स्थानीय स्कूलों के साथ भी सहयोग करते हैं। मेरी ज़िम्मेदारी में कार्यक्रम की योजना बनाना और स्वयंसेवकों को प्रशिक्षित करना, दोनों आते हैं। This is mainly our organization's own project, but we sometimes collaborate with local schools too. My responsibility includes both planning the program and training volunteers.
डॉ. मल्होत्रा यह तो बिल्कुल मेरे शोध के क्षेत्र से संबंधित है। क्या मैं आपसे कुछ पूछ सकती हूँ — क्या आप कभी शोध के लिए अपने कार्यक्रम का डेटा साझा करने में रुचि रखेंगी? This is directly related to my field of research. May I ask you something — would you ever be interested in sharing your program's data for research purposes?
प्रिया बिल्कुल, यह एक अच्छा विचार है। आगे संपर्क में रहेंगे — मैं आपको इस हफ़्ते ईमेल भेजूँगी। Absolutely, that's a good idea. We'll stay in touch — I'll email you this week.

Later the same evening, Kabir and Aditya talk more informally near the refreshment table:

आदित्य यार, मैं अभी अख़बार के लिए इस कार्यक्रम को कवर कर रहा हूँ, तो मुझे हर किसी से थोड़ी औपचारिक भाषा में बात करनी पड़ रही है! Dude, I'm covering this event for the newspaper right now, so I have to talk to everyone in a somewhat formal register!
कबीर मैंने नोटिस किया! तुम प्रिया से जिस तरह बात कर रहे थे, और मुझसे जिस तरह बात कर रहे हो — दोनों में बहुत फ़र्क़ है। I noticed! The way you were talking to Priya, and the way you're talking to me — there's a big difference between the two.
आदित्य बिल्कुल सही पकड़ा! तुम्हारे साथ मैं ऐसे ही बोल सकता हूँ, लेकिन पहली मुलाक़ात में हमेशा `आप` और औपचारिक भूमिका वाली भाषा ही सुरक्षित रहती है। Exactly right! With you I can speak just like this, but on a first meeting, `aap` and formal role-based language always stays the safe choice.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. प्रिया अपनी भूमिका और ज़िम्मेदारी का वर्णन कैसे करती है? (How does Priya describe her role and responsibility?)
  2. डॉ. मल्होत्रा का प्रस्ताव क्या है, और प्रिया उसका जवाब कैसे देती है? (What is Dr. Malhotra's proposal, and how does Priya respond?)
  3. आदित्य और कबीर की बातचीत में भाषा किस तरह बदल जाती है, और क्यों? (How does the language change in Aditya and Kabir's conversation, and why?)

Writing Workshop

Complete all parts in standard Devanagari.

लेखन कार्य — Writing task:

  1. Write a short professional bio (4-6 sentences) describing your current or intended role, using at least one nominalized role description (मेरी भूमिका...है) and at least two relationship frames from this chapter (के रूप में, के तहत, के साथ, के लिए, से संबंधित).

  3. Write a short networking follow-up email (3-4 sentences) to someone you met for the first time, opening formally, referring to what you discussed, and proposing a next step.

संपादन सूची — Editing checklist:

  • At least one nominalized role description is used accurately.
  • At least two relationship frames are used, each connecting the correct two elements.
  • The follow-up email opens and closes with register-appropriate formulas.
  • The informal rewrite shows a genuine, explainable register shift, not just a shorter sentence.
  • Gender, number, agreement, oblique forms, postpositions, and `ने`/`को` patterns are applied correctly throughout.
  • Spelling, matras, conjuncts, word spacing, and punctuation are correct throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Hierarchy, age, and address

In many Hindi professional settings, address forms are shaped by hierarchy, age, and institutional role as much as by familiarity. A junior colleague may use जी and `आप` with a senior colleague of similar age, while using a more informal register with a peer at the same level.

Culture in Context — `आप` as the safe default

As Aditya notes to Kabir, `आप` remains the safe productive default in a first professional meeting, even between people close in age, unless the relationship clearly supports a different form. Moving to `तुम` typically happens gradually and by mutual signal, not by one person's unilateral decision.

Culture in Context — Code-switching in professional settings

Hindi-English code-switching is common in many urban professional contexts and is not inherently careless or unprofessional. However, this chapter's tasks ask learners to produce precise Hindi role descriptions, since the skill of expressing a role in full Hindi is itself a C1 target.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Confusing के रूप में and के तहत

Error: यह परियोजना समन्वयक के तहत चलती है (using के तहt to mean "in the role of," modeled on English "under a coordinator").

Correction: यह परियोजना एक समन्वयक के रूप में मेरे नेतृत्व में चलती है or, more naturally, यह परियोजना शिक्षा विभाग के तहत चलती है. Reserve के तहत for an institutional or programmatic umbrella, not a person's role.

Common Mistake — Describing work only with long verbal sentences

Error: Only ever saying मैं हर दिन कार्यक्रम की योजना बनाती हूँ और स्वयंसेवकों को प्रशिक्षित करती हूँ और... as a long, unbroken list.

Correction: Compress the role first, then elaborate: मेरी भूमिका कार्यक्रम समन्वयन की है। इसमें योजना बनाना और स्वयंसेवकों को प्रशिक्षित करना, दोनों शामिल हैं।

Common Mistake — Using the same register in every setting

Error: Introducing oneself to a stranger at a formal event with the same casual phrasing used with a close friend.

Correction: Use मैं वर्तमान में...में काम करता/करती हूँ and `आप` in a first professional meeting; reserve casual phrasing for established relationships, as Aditya explains to Kabir.
