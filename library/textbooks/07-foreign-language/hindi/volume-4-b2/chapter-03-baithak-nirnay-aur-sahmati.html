बैठक, निर्णय, और सहमति

Volume IV, Chapter 3: Meetings, Decisions, and Agreement · Speak and Write Hindi: Professional, Academic, and Cultural Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Participate in a structured meeting: state an agenda item, clarify a problem, and propose a next step.
  2. Agree, disagree, qualify an opinion, and ask for clarification politely.
  3. Report what someone suggested or decided using कि-clauses.
  4. Use impersonal meeting-summary style such as यह तय हुआ कि... and यह सुझाव दिया गया कि....
  5. Assign responsibility using frames such as के जिम्मे, के लिए, द्वारा, and तक.
  6. Summarize decisions and responsibilities clearly, orally and in writing.
  7. Write concise, accurate meeting notes or action items in Hindi.

Communication Scenario

Priya's organization holds its monthly program review meeting the week after the networking evening. Ms. Ahuja chairs the meeting; Priya, Neha, and two field coordinators attend, along with Dr. Malhotra, who joins as an outside observer since the data-sharing conversation from Chapter 2 turned into an actual invitation. The agenda covers three items: the literacy program's mid-year numbers, a proposed change to the volunteer training schedule, and Dr. Malhotra's data-sharing request. Priya has to state a problem clearly, listen to two colleagues disagree politely about the training schedule, and later write up the meeting's decisions so that everyone who could not attend knows exactly what was decided and who is responsible for what.

  1. Notice how disagreement is softened with मुझे लगता है, शायद, and हालाँकि rather than stated bluntly.
  2. Notice how the meeting's decisions are phrased impersonally — यह तय हुआ कि... — rather than naming exactly who insisted on what.
  3. Notice how responsibility is assigned using के जिम्मे and तक for a deadline, without sounding like a command.
  4. Notice how the written meeting notes compress the spoken discussion into short, scannable action items.

Note on This Vocabulary

Notice that most disagreement and objection vocabulary in this list (आपत्ति, असहमति, शर्त) pairs naturally with hedging language like मुझे लगता है or शायद. Bare disagreement without any softening is rare in professional Hindi meetings.

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

Schwa deletion in formal meeting vocabulary

कार्यसूची (kāryasūcī) and स्पष्टीकरण (spaṣṭīkaraṇ) both contain internal schwa deletion that can trip up a learner reading formal vocabulary for the first time. Read each word slowly by syllable before using it in a full sentence, and notice that the final ण in स्पष्टीकरण keeps a light final vowel rather than being fully silent.

Intonation for polite interruption, clarification, and disagreement

A polite interruption in Hindi typically rises slightly and pauses, rather than cutting in sharply: माफ़ कीजिए, एक बात स्पष्ट करना चाहूँगी... Practice a gentle rising-then-pausing contour here, distinct from the flatter, more confident pitch used when stating an agreed decision.

Pace control in turn-taking

In a meeting, speakers often slow down slightly right before stating a decision or an action item, to signal that the listener should note it down. Practice this natural slowing on phrases like निर्णय यह हुआ कि... and अगला कदम यह है कि...

Listening for proposal, objection, decision, and responsibility

Meeting speech compresses a great deal of information into a few marked phrases. Train your ear to catch the four key signal types: a proposal (मेरा सुझाव है कि), an objection (मुझे लगता है कि + hedge), a decision (यह तय हुआ कि), and a responsibility assignment (यह...के जिम्मे है).

Grammar and Language Systems

This chapter introduces reported decisions with कि, polite hedged disagreement, responsibility-assignment frames, and impersonal meeting-summary style. All four build directly on Volume III reported speech and postpositional control.

3.4 Reported Decisions and Suggestions with कि

Volume III introduced reported speech for statements, questions, and opinions. In a meeting, the same कि-clause pattern reports what was decided or suggested, usually with an impersonal main verb rather than a named subject:

Language Building Block — Reported Decision Frame
यह तय हुआ कि [clause]। यह सुझाव दिया गया कि [clause]। सबने सहमति जताई कि [clause]।
It was decided that [clause]. It was suggested that [clause]. Everyone agreed that [clause].

Compare a personal report (आहूजा जी ने कहा कि प्रशिक्षण अक्टूबर में होगा, "Ms. Ahuja said that the training will be in October") with an impersonal decision report (यह तय हुआ कि प्रशिक्षण अक्टूबर में होगा, "It was decided that the training will be in October"). The impersonal version is more common in meeting summaries because it records the group's decision without singling out one person as having made it unilaterally.

3.5 Polite Disagreement and Hedging

Direct disagreement in a Hindi professional meeting is usually wrapped in a hedge. Five hedging words do most of this work:

WordFunctionExample
शायदperhapsशायद हमें एक और सप्ताह चाहिए।
संभव हैit's possibleसंभव है कि यह समय-सीमा बहुत कम हो।
मुझे लगता हैI think, it seems to meमुझे लगता है कि हमें विकल्पों पर फिर से विचार करना चाहिए।
फिर भीeven soफिर भी, यह कोशिश करने लायक है।
हालाँकिhoweverहालाँकि, मुझे एक चिंता है।

These words rarely appear alone. A polite disagreement typically combines a hedge, the concern itself, and often a softened alternative: मुझे लगता है कि अक्टूबर थोड़ा जल्दी है; शायद हम नवंबर तक इंतज़ार कर सकते हैं ("I think October is a bit soon; perhaps we could wait until November").

3.6 Responsibility Frames

Four frames assign responsibility and deadlines without sounding like a direct command:

FrameFunctionExample
के जिम्मेmarks who is responsible for a taskरिपोर्ट तैयार करना नेहा के जिम्मे है।
के लिएmarks purpose or beneficiary of an actionयह जानकारी अगली बैठक के लिए ज़रूरी है।
द्वाराmarks the agent completing a task (formal)यह काम प्रिया द्वारा पूरा किया जाएगा।
तकmarks a deadline or limitयह रिपोर्ट शुक्रवार तक तैयार होनी चाहिए।

के जिम्मे and द्वारा both involve responsibility, but they work differently: के जिम्मे names whose job a task is, while द्वारा names who will actually carry it out, often in a more passive-like construction. Both are common in the same sentence: यह काम प्रिया के जिम्मे है, और इसे शुक्रवार तक प्रिया द्वारा पूरा किया जाएगा।

Boundary Reminder

This chapter does not introduce full passive constructions, causatives, or new honorific systems — those wait for Chapter 4 and later. The reported-decision frame, hedging vocabulary, and four responsibility frames above are the new productive systems here; everything else recycles Volume III reported speech and Chapter 2's professional register.

Model Dialogue

Setting: The monthly program review meeting. Ms. Ahuja chairs; Priya, Neha, and a field coordinator named Vikram attend, along with Dr. Malhotra as an observer.

आहूजा जी ठीक है, चलिए शुरू करते हैं। आज की कार्यसूची में तीन मुद्दे हैं — पहला, साक्षरता कार्यक्रम के मध्य-वर्ष के आँकड़े; दूसरा, प्रशिक्षण की समय-सारणी में बदलाव; और तीसरा, डॉ. मल्होत्रा का प्रस्ताव। Alright, let's begin. Today's agenda has three items — first, the literacy program's mid-year numbers; second, a change to the training schedule; and third, Dr. Malhotra's proposal.
प्रिया पहले मुद्दे पर, मध्य-वर्ष के आँकड़े उम्मीद से थोड़े कम हैं। समस्या यह है कि दो स्कूलों में शुरुआत में देरी हुई थी। On the first item, the mid-year numbers are a bit lower than expected. The problem is that two schools had a delayed start.
विक्रम मेरे विचार में यह चिंता की बात नहीं है — शायद हमें बस अगले महीने की योजना में थोड़ा समायोजन करना होगा। In my view this isn't a cause for concern — perhaps we'll just need a small adjustment in next month's plan.
आहूजा जी ठीक है। तो यह तय हुआ कि प्रिया अगले महीने के लिए एक संशोधित योजना तैयार करेंगी। अब दूसरे मुद्दे पर आते हैं — नेहा, प्रशिक्षण की समय-सारणी के बारे में आपका प्रस्ताव क्या है? Alright. So it's decided that Priya will prepare a revised plan for next month. Now let's move to the second item — Neha, what is your proposal about the training schedule?
नेहा मेरा सुझाव है कि हम प्रशिक्षण को अक्टूबर से नवंबर में ले जाएँ, ताकि नए स्वयंसेवकों को तैयारी के लिए ज़्यादा समय मिले। My suggestion is that we move the training from October to November, so that new volunteers get more time to prepare.
विक्रम मुझे लगता है कि नवंबर थोड़ा देर हो जाएगा — हमारे कुछ स्कूल दिसंबर से पहले प्रशिक्षित स्वयंसेवक चाहते हैं। फिर भी, मैं समझता हूँ कि तैयारी का समय भी ज़रूरी है। I think November will end up being a bit late — some of our schools want trained volunteers before December. Even so, I understand that preparation time is also necessary.
नेहा एक बात स्पष्ट करना चाहूँगी — क्या हम सिर्फ़ आधा प्रशिक्षण अक्टूबर के अंत में और बाकी नवंबर की शुरुआत में रख सकते हैं? I'd like to clarify one thing — could we hold just half the training at the end of October and the rest at the start of November?
आहूजा जी यह एक अच्छा समाधान लगता है। तो निर्णय यह हुआ कि प्रशिक्षण दो हिस्सों में होगा। नेहा, यह योजना तैयार करना आपके जिम्मे है, और इसे अगले शुक्रवार तक पूरा करना होगा। That seems like a good solution. So the decision is that the training will happen in two parts. Neha, preparing this plan is your responsibility, and it needs to be completed by next Friday.
आहूजा जी अब तीसरा मुद्दा — डॉ. मल्होत्रा, क्या आप अपना प्रस्ताव संक्षेप में बता सकती हैं? Now the third item — Dr. Malhotra, could you briefly explain your proposal?
डॉ. मल्होत्रा बिल्कुल। मैं यह अनुरोध कर रही हूँ कि हमारा शोध दल कार्यक्रम का गुमनाम डेटा उपयोग कर सके, केवल शोध उद्देश्यों के लिए। यह पूरी तरह वैकल्पिक है, और संस्था द्वारा किसी भी समय रोका जा सकता है। Certainly. I'm requesting that our research team be able to use the program's anonymized data, purely for research purposes. This is entirely optional, and can be stopped by the organization at any time.
प्रिया मैं इस बात से सहमत हूँ, बशर्ते कि डेटा वाकई गुमनाम रहे। संभव है कि हमें एक छोटा समझौता-पत्र भी बनाना पड़े। I agree with this, provided the data really stays anonymous. It's possible we'll also need to draft a short agreement letter.
आहूजा जी सहमति बन गई है। यह सुझाव दिया गया कि प्रिया और डॉ. मल्होत्रा मिलकर एक छोटा समझौता-पत्र तैयार करें। अगला कदम यही होगा। धन्यवाद, सबका। Agreement has been reached. It's suggested that Priya and Dr. Malhotra together prepare a short agreement letter. That will be the next step. Thank you, everyone.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. मध्य-वर्ष के आँकड़ों की समस्या क्या है, और इसका समाधान क्या तय हुआ? (What is the problem with the mid-year numbers, and what solution was decided?)
  2. विक्रम और नेहा किस बात पर असहमत थे, और अंतिम निर्णय क्या हुआ? (What did Vikram and Neha disagree about, and what was the final decision?)
  3. डॉ. मल्होत्रा का प्रस्ताव क्या है, और प्रिया की क्या शर्त है? (What is Dr. Malhotra's proposal, and what is Priya's condition?)

Speaking Practice

नियंत्रित अभ्यास — Controlled practice: प्रस्ताव और प्रतिक्रिया — Proposal and Response

With a partner, take turns proposing a small change to a shared routine (a class schedule, a household chore, a work process) using मेरा सुझाव है कि..., and respond with either agreement or a hedged disagreement.

संचार कार्य — Communicative task: बैठक का अभिनय — Meeting Role-Play

In a group of three or four, role-play a short meeting with a simple agenda (two items). One person chairs the meeting, states each item, and closes each with a reported-decision frame and a responsibility assignment. The others propose, agree, disagree, and ask for clarification.

अकेले अभ्यास — Solo alternative

Record yourself summarizing an imagined meeting's three decisions aloud, using यह तय हुआ कि... for each one, and assigning responsibility and a deadline for at least one item. Listen back and check that your hedges and reported-decision frames sound natural.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Softened disagreement and hierarchy

In many Hindi professional settings, how directly someone disagrees depends on relative seniority, age, and institutional role. A junior colleague disagreeing with a senior colleague's proposal will typically hedge more heavily than two peers disagreeing with each other.

Culture in Context — Impersonal decision language and shared ownership

Phrasing a decision as यह तय हुआ कि... rather than naming exactly who insisted on it is common in Hindi meeting culture. It frames the outcome as a shared, collective decision rather than one person's unilateral win, even when one person's proposal clearly shaped the outcome.

Culture in Context — Guests and observers in meetings

Dr. Malhotra's presence as an invited outside observer is common in many Hindi-speaking professional and institutional settings, where meetings often include guests whose contributions are treated with particular courtesy and whose proposals may still require organization members to reach internal agreement before formally accepting.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Disagreeing without any hedge

Error: यह गलत है ("This is wrong") stated bluntly as a direct objection to a colleague's proposal.

Correction: मुझे लगता है कि इसमें एक समस्या हो सकती है ("I think there might be a problem with this"). Pair the objection with a hedge such as मुझे लगता है, शायद, or संभव है.

Common Mistake — Confusing के जिम्मे and द्वारा

Error: यह काम नेहा द्वारा है (using द्वारा alone to mean "is Neha's responsibility," modeled on English "by").

Correction: यह काम नेहा के जिम्मे है ("This task is Neha's responsibility"). Reserve द्वारा for naming who will actually carry out an action, usually with a full verb: यह काम नेहा द्वारा पूरा किया जाएगा.

Common Mistake — Naming a person instead of using impersonal decision style

Error: आहूजा जी ने फ़ैसला किया कि... used in formal meeting notes as the default way to record every decision, even when the decision emerged from group discussion.

Correction: यह तय हुआ कि... Use the impersonal frame for group decisions in written notes, and reserve a named subject for cases where one person's individual statement, rather than a group decision, is being reported.
