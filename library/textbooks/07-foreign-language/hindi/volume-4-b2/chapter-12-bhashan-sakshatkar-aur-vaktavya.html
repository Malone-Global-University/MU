भाषण, साक्षात्कार, और वक्तव्य

Volume IV, Chapter 12: Speeches, Interviews, and Statements · Speak and Write Hindi: Professional, Academic, and Cultural Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify the rhetorical structure, audience, and main claims of a short speech or formal statement.
  2. Summarize what a speaker said and explain why the point matters.
  3. Ask tactful, well-formed follow-up questions in an interview or discussion setting.
  4. Quote a speaker's exact words and paraphrase their meaning, keeping the two clearly distinct.
  5. Reformulate a point when it has been misunderstood, using natural Hindi clarification language.
  6. Prepare and deliver a short, organized formal statement appropriate to a professional or public setting.

This chapter continues Unit III by moving from written news reports to spoken public communication. It extends the attribution and framing awareness built in Chapter 11 into speeches, interviews, and formal statements.

Communication Scenario

After comparing the two news reports with Arjun, Priya has agreed to speak with a community radio reporter, Kavita, about the transport pilot. Before the interview, Priya and Arjun rehearse: Arjun plays the reporter and asks the kinds of questions Kavita is likely to ask, including a somewhat pointed follow-up about the neighboring district where the scheme did not work as well. Priya practices answering clearly, quoting her own position consistently, and gently correcting a question that assumes something she did not actually say. Afterward, she prepares a short formal statement to read at the start of the actual interview.

  1. Notice how Priya organizes her statement: purpose, main point, evidence, and a closing line.
  2. Notice how Arjun's practice questions move from general to more challenging.
  3. Notice how Priya reformulates a point when a question misrepresents her position, without becoming defensive.
  4. Notice the difference between what Priya actually says (a quotable statement) and how Kavita might later paraphrase it in a broadcast.

Note on Register in Public Speech

Formal speech and interview Hindi often uses somewhat elevated vocabulary and honorific address, even when the underlying content is straightforward. A word like वक्तव्य is more formal than the everyday बात, but both can refer to something a person says; the choice signals setting, not truthfulness.

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

Formal speech rhythm and pause structure

Prepared speeches and formal statements are usually delivered more slowly and with more deliberate pausing than ordinary conversation. Listen for a pause before a key point and a slight drop in pitch at the end of a completed statement.

Intonation in questions and follow-up prompts

A polite follow-up question in an interview often rises gently at the end, even when it begins with a wh-word such as क्यों or कैसे. A sharper, more clipped rise can signal a more challenging or skeptical follow-up.

Schwa deletion in titles, names, and formal vocabulary

Practice वक्तव्य, स्पष्टीकरण, and साक्षात्कार aloud, dropping the inherent vowel where it is not pronounced. These words recur throughout this chapter's dialogue and reading material.

Grammar and Language Systems

12.4 Quoted Speech and Paraphrase in Public Contexts

Speeches and interviews are usually reported afterward using a mix of direct quotation and paraphrase. This continues the quote/paraphrase distinction introduced in Chapter 11, now applied to spoken exchanges rather than written news items.

HindiFunction
"हमने पूरी कोशिश की है," उन्होंने कहा।direct quotation of the speaker's exact words
उन्होंने कहा कि उन्होंने पूरी कोशिश की है।paraphrase of the same statement
वक्ता ने संकेत दिया कि आगे कोई घोषणा हो सकती है।reporter's inference from what was implied, not stated directly

12.5 Rhetorical Organization in Speeches and Statements

Prepared speeches and formal statements typically follow a recognizable structure. Learners should both recognize this structure when listening and use it when speaking.

  • सबसे पहले, मैं आप सभी का धन्यवाद करना चाहता/चाहती हूँ। (First, I would like to thank all of you.)
  • दूसरा मुद्दा यह है कि योजना का असर हर जगह एक जैसा नहीं रहा। (The second point is that the scheme's effect has not been the same everywhere.)
  • इसी कारण हम स्थानीय स्तर पर समीक्षा जारी रखेंगे। (For this reason, we will continue the review at the local level.)
  • अंत में, मैं यह स्पष्ट करना चाहता/चाहती हूँ कि निर्णय अभी अंतिम नहीं है। (Finally, I want to make clear that the decision is not yet final.)

12.6 Indirect Questions and Follow-Up Frames

Interviewers use indirect and follow-up question frames to request clarification or press for more detail without sounding accusatory.

HindiFunction
क्या आप बता सकते/सकती हैं कि...?polite request for information
आप यह कैसे समझाएँगे/समझाएँगी कि...?polite request for explanation of a discrepancy
क्या इसका मतलब यह है कि...?checking an inference before stating it as fact
एक आखिरी सवाल...signaling a final follow-up question

12.7 Emphasis, Clarification, and Respectful Disagreement

Speakers being interviewed need language to clarify, correct a misunderstanding, or respectfully push back on a question's framing without appearing evasive or hostile.

  • मैं यह स्पष्ट करना चाहता/चाहती हूँ कि मैंने यह नहीं कहा था। (I want to make clear that I did not say that.)
  • मैं इसे दूसरे तरीके से समझाता/समझाती हूँ। (Let me explain this another way.)
  • आपका प्रश्न महत्वपूर्ण है, लेकिन मुझे लगता है कि स्थिति थोड़ी अलग है। (Your question is important, but I think the situation is a bit different.)
Boundary Reminder

This chapter introduces reformulation and respectful disagreement as controlled interview-response patterns. It does not require advanced rhetorical or persuasive argumentation strategies, which remain outside Volume IV's scope.

Language Building Block — Interview Response Frame
आपका प्रश्न महत्वपूर्ण है। मैं यह स्पष्ट करना चाहता/चाहती हूँ कि [clarification]। सबसे पहले, [point one]। दूसरा मुद्दा यह है कि [point two]। इसी कारण, [conclusion]।
Your question is important. I want to make clear that [clarification]. First, [point one]. The second point is that [point two]. For this reason, [conclusion].

Model Dialogue

Setting: Arjun plays the role of reporter Kavita while Priya rehearses for her upcoming radio interview.

अर्जुन (कविता की भूमिका में) तो प्रिया जी, सबसे पहले बताइए — यह परिवहन योजना शुरू करने का मुख्य उद्देश्य क्या था? So, Priya-ji, first tell us — what was the main purpose of starting this transport scheme?
प्रिया धन्यवाद। मुख्य उद्देश्य यह था कि दूर के गाँवों के बच्चे नियमित रूप से स्कूल पहुँच सकें। Thank you. The main purpose was for children from distant villages to be able to reach school regularly.
अर्जुन (कविता की भूमिका में) लेकिन पड़ोसी ज़िले में तो यह योजना उतनी कामयाब नहीं रही। क्या इसका मतलब यह है कि योजना में कोई बुनियादी कमी है? But in the neighboring district the scheme wasn't as successful. Does this mean there's some basic flaw in the scheme?
प्रिया मैं यह स्पष्ट करना चाहती हूँ कि मैंने यह नहीं कहा कि योजना में कोई कमी नहीं है। मैं इसे दूसरे तरीके से समझाती हूँ — हर ज़िले की भौगोलिक और सामाजिक स्थिति अलग है, इसलिए एक ही योजना का असर हर जगह एक जैसा नहीं होगा। I want to make clear that I did not say there's no flaw in the scheme. Let me explain this another way — every district's geographic and social situation is different, so the same scheme's effect won't be identical everywhere.
अर्जुन (कविता की भूमिका में) आपका जवाब स्पष्ट है। एक आखिरी सवाल — आगे क्या कदम उठाए जाएँगे? Your answer is clear. One last question — what steps will be taken going forward?
प्रिया इसी कारण हम पड़ोसी ज़िले की स्थानीय परिस्थितियों की अलग से समीक्षा करेंगे। अंत में, मैं यह स्पष्ट करना चाहती हूँ कि यह निर्णय अभी अंतिम नहीं है — समीक्षा के बाद ही अगला कदम तय होगा। For this reason, we will separately review the local conditions in the neighboring district. Finally, I want to make clear that this decision is not yet final — the next step will be decided only after the review.
अर्जुन (अपनी असली भूमिका में) बहुत अच्छा था! तुमने कविता के सवाल को गलत नहीं ठहराया, लेकिन अपनी बात भी पूरी तरह स्पष्ट रखी। That was very good! You didn't dismiss Kavita's question as wrong, but you also kept your own position completely clear.

समझ के प्रश्न — Comprehension questions:

  1. प्रिया योजना का मुख्य उद्देश्य कैसे बताती है? (How does Priya state the scheme's main purpose?)
  2. जब कविता एक चुनौतीपूर्ण सवाल पूछती है, तो प्रिया अपनी बात कैसे स्पष्ट करती है? (When Kavita asks a challenging question, how does Priya clarify her position?)

लेखन कार्य — Writing task:

  • The statement has a clear opening, two organized points, and a closing.

  • Register remains consistently formal throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Elevated register in public speech

Public speeches and formal statements in Hindi often use somewhat more Sanskritic vocabulary and honorific address than everyday conversation, even among speakers who use much more colloquial Hindi at home. This shift is a register choice tied to setting, not a change in what the speaker actually believes.

Culture in Context — Answering a challenging question respectfully

In many Hindi-speaking professional and public settings, a direct "no, that's wrong" can sound unnecessarily confrontational. Reformulating a point — as Priya does — is a common and respected way to correct a misunderstanding while remaining polite.

Culture in Context — Interviewers and follow-up questions

A pointed follow-up question, such as Kavita's, is a normal and expected part of professional interviewing. It should not be read as personal hostility; the appropriate response is a clear, organized clarification rather than defensiveness.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Treating a follow-up question as an accusation

Error: Responding defensively or refusing to answer a challenging follow-up question.

Correction: Use a reformulation frame such as मैं इसे दूसरे तरीके से समझाता/समझाती हूँ to clarify calmly.

Common Mistake — Confirming an inference the speaker never stated

Error: Answering "yes" to a question like क्या इसका मतलब यह है कि...? when the inference does not match what was actually said.

Correction: Use मैंने यह नहीं कहा कि... to correct the inference before offering a clearer explanation.

Common Mistake — Overusing rhetorical connectors

Error: Stacking सबसे पहले, दूसरा मुद्दा, and अंत में in every sentence regardless of whether a new point is actually being introduced.

Correction: Reserve rhetorical connectors for genuine transitions between distinct points.

  4. Model answer: Priya's response directly addresses the substance of the question — why an independent committee is used — without agreeing to the negative inference Kavita's question implied.
