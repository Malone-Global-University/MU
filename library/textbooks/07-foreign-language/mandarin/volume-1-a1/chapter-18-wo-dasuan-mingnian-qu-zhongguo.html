我打算明年去中国

Volume I, Chapter 18: Plans and Future Reference · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

In Chapter 17, you learned to talk about experiences that happened at some unspecified point in the past using 过. This chapter turns from the past to the future. You will learn 打算 for stating plans and intentions, and you will review 要 and 会 in their future-reference functions. You will also learn to compare two things using 比, and to express the highest or greater degree of a quality using 更 and 最. Calendar vocabulary will help you talk about specific future dates.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State a plan or intention using 打算 + Verb Phrase.
  2. Distinguish 打算 (plan to), 要 (want to / intend to), and 会 (will, future likelihood) in talking about the future.
  3. Compare two people, places, or things using A 比 B + Adjective.
  4. Use 更 to say something is "even more" a certain quality.
  5. Use 最 to say something is "the most" of a certain quality.
  6. Talk about future months, years, and calendar dates.
  7. Read and interpret a simple travel-planning message and calendar.

Communication Scenario

After talking about their travel experiences in Chapter 17, 大卫 and 安娜 decide to actually plan a trip together. They compare two possible cities to visit and talk about when they plan to go.

  1. You will hear and read 大卫 and 安娜 planning a trip using 打算 and comparison with 比.
  2. You will practice comparing two places, activities, or plans with a partner.
  3. You will write a short passage about what you plan to do next year, comparing two options.

发音重点 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter introduces no new sounds or tone rules. Use the items below to review tone contrasts that come up often in this chapter's vocabulary.

  • The falling tone of 比 (bǐ), third tone — practice it in a full sentence such as 上海比北京大, where 比 comes before the two third-tone syllables of 北京, so listen for how the sentence-level rhythm carries across the comparison.
  • The rising tone of 更 (gèng) versus the falling tone — remember 更 is a fourth tone, distinct in contour from 跟 (gēn), which you may know from other contexts.
  • The falling tone of 最 (zuì) — keep it light and quick before the adjective it modifies, as in 最大, 最好.
  • Distinguishing 打算 (dǎsuàn) from 大算-sounding combinations — the first syllable is a third tone that often shifts toward a low, non-dipping pronunciation in this common word.
语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Subject + 打算 + Verb Phrase — stating a plan

我打算明年去中国。 Wǒ dǎsuàn míngnián qù Zhōngguó. I plan to go to China next year.

打算 introduces a verb phrase describing what someone intends to do. A time word such as 明年 usually comes right after the subject or right after 打算, following the time-before-verb pattern you already know.

A + 比 + B + Adjective — comparing two things

上海比北京大。 Shànghǎi bǐ Běijīng dà. Shanghai is bigger than Beijing.

比 sits directly between the two things being compared, and the adjective follows. Do not add 很 before the adjective in a basic 比 sentence — 上海比北京很大 is incorrect.

A + 比 + B + 更/最 + Adjective — intensifying a comparison

上海比北京更大。 Shànghǎi bǐ Běijīng gèng dà. Shanghai is even bigger than Beijing.

中国最大的城市是上海。 Zhōngguó zuì dà de chéngshì shì Shànghǎi. Shanghai is China's biggest city.

更 works inside a 比 comparison to intensify the difference. 最 is not used with 比; it marks the single highest-degree item within a group, often followed by 的 and a noun.

Language Note — 打算, 要, and 会 for the future

You have already met 要 (want to / intend to) and 会 (will, future likelihood) in earlier chapters. 打算 adds a third way to talk about the future, focused specifically on plans that have already been thought through: 我打算明年去中国 suggests a considered plan, while 我要去中国 can express a more general intention or desire, and 我会去中国 can express something you expect will happen. The three often overlap in natural speech, but this chapter focuses on 打算 for describing a plan you have already decided on.

没有 as a comparison alternative (recognition)

北京没有上海大。 Běijīng méiyǒu Shànghǎi dà. Beijing is not as big as Shanghai.

This pattern reverses the comparison using 没有 instead of 比. It is presented here for recognition; full productive control is developed in a later chapter.

对话 — Model Dialogue

Model Dialogue

大卫和安娜 — David and Anna:
大卫：安娜，我们决定去中国吧！你打算什么时候去？
安娜：我打算明年去，可能是三月或者四月。
大卫：北京和上海，你觉得哪个城市更好玩儿？
安娜：我觉得上海比北京更热闹，可是北京有长城，也很有意思。
大卫：对，长城是中国最有名的地方。我们应该先去北京，再去上海。
安娜：好，那我们的计划是：明年三月，先去北京，再去上海！
大卫：太好了！这次我们一起打算，一起旅游。

Notice how 安娜 uses 打算 to describe a considered plan, while 更 and 最 help her and 大卫 compare the two cities.

Speaking Practice

控制性练习 — Controlled practice: guided interview

With a partner, take turns asking and answering: 你明年打算做什么？你打算去哪个城市？你觉得哪个城市更好玩儿？

交际任务 — Communicative task: comparing plans

With a partner, compare two cities, activities, or plans using 比, 更, and 最 at least once each. Then describe one plan you both agree on using 打算.

独立练习 — Solo alternative

Record yourself describing one plan you have for next year, comparing two possible options using 比, and stating which one you think is best using 最.

Reading Practice

Read the short planning message and calendar note below, then answer the comprehension questions.

我们打算明年三月去中国旅游。北京比上海有名的地方多，可是上海比北京热闹。我们决定先去北京五天，参观长城，然后去上海四天。上海是中国最大的城市之一，我们很想去看看。这是我们最好的旅游计划！
We plan to travel to China next March. Beijing has more famous sights than Shanghai, but Shanghai is livelier than Beijing. We decided to go to Beijing first for five days to visit the Great Wall, and then go to Shanghai for four days. Shanghai is one of China's biggest cities, and we really want to see it. This is our best travel plan!

理解问题 — Comprehension questions:

  1. 他们打算什么时候去中国？ (When do they plan to go to China?)
  2. 他们打算在北京待几天？ (How many days do they plan to stay in Beijing?)
  3. 为什么他们想去上海？ (Why do they want to go to Shanghai?)

Writing Workshop

Write a short passage of five to seven sentences about a plan you have for next year. Use only vocabulary and grammar from Chapters 1-18.

写作任务 — Writing task: Type your sentences with Pinyin input if possible, or copy/handwrite the characters you have already been assigned for handwriting. Your passage must include:

  1. At least one sentence using 打算 to state your plan.
  2. At least one comparison using A 比 B + Adjective.
  3. At least one use of 更 or 最 to intensify or single out a quality.
  4. A closing sentence explaining why you chose this plan.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • 打算 is followed directly by a verb phrase, with the time word placed naturally.
  • 比 sits between the two things compared, with no 很 before the adjective.
  • 更 is used inside a 比 sentence; 最 is used to single out one item from a group, not inside a 比 sentence.
  • The passage reads as a connected plan, not a disconnected list of sentences.
  • A Chinese period (。) ends each sentence.

Culture and Communication

Culture Note — Comparing cities and places

Comparing cities such as 北京 and 上海 is a common topic of conversation among Mandarin speakers and learners alike, often centered on differences in pace of life, food, history, and atmosphere rather than a claim that one city is simply "better." When making comparisons in Mandarin, as in many languages, it is common to soften a comparison with phrases like 我觉得 (I feel/think that) rather than stating it as an absolute fact, since opinions about cities and places vary widely among individuals and regions.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Adding 很 in a basic 比 sentence: 上海比北京大 is correct; 上海比北京很大 is incorrect. Save 很 for ordinary descriptive sentences without 比.
  • Using 最 inside a 比 sentence: 上海比北京最大 is incorrect. Use 更 to intensify a 比 comparison, and reserve 最 for singling out the top item in a group, as in 上海是中国最大的城市.
  • Confusing 打算, 要, and 会: all three can refer to the future, but 打算 specifically signals a plan that has already been thought through. Choose based on what you actually mean to express.
  • Misplacing the time word with 打算: 我打算明年去中国 and 明年我打算去中国 are both natural; avoid placing the time word after the main verb, such as *我打算去中国明年.
