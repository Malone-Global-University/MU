昨天你做什么了？

Volume I, Chapter 16: Completed Events · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

Unit III ended by checking whether you could combine ability, directions, ongoing action, and health/advice language into one connected performance. Unit IV opens a new communicative area: talking about things that already happened. You already control change-of-state 了 from Chapter 14 — this chapter builds on that same character and introduces its second major job in Mandarin: marking that an action was completed. You will also learn to place events in time relative to each other with 以前 and 以后, and you will practice stringing several completed actions together into a short, connected narration of a day.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Ask and answer what someone did using 你做什么了？ and perfective 了.
  2. Attach perfective 了 correctly to verb-object expressions such as 看电影, 写信, and 打电话.
  3. Negate a completed event with 没/没有, distinguishing it from negation with 不.
  4. Sequence past actions using 以前 and 以后.
  5. Narrate a short sequence of events from yesterday or last week in connected sentences.
  6. Distinguish perfective 了 (an action happened) from change-of-state 了 (a situation changed), which you already met in Chapter 14.

Communication Scenario

安娜 is feeling much better after her cold. Her friend 大卫 wants to know how she spent her day off yesterday — what she did before lunch, what she did after, and whether she managed to finish the work report she mentioned in Chapter 15.

  1. You will hear and read 安娜 describing yesterday using completed-action language.
  2. You will practice asking and answering about completed events with a partner.
  3. You will write your own short narration of a recent day, connecting several completed actions in order.

发音重点 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter introduces no new sounds or tone rules. Use the items below to review tone contrasts that are easy to blur in fast narration.

  • The falling tone of 以后 (yǐhòu) compared with the rising-then-falling shape of 以前 (yǐqián) — say both slowly, then at natural speed.
  • The neutral-tone second syllable in 昨天 (zuótiān) and 电话 (diànhuà).
  • Third-tone sandhi in 我写 (wǒ xiě), where two third tones in a row become rising-plus-third.
  • Keeping perfective 了 (le) light and unstressed — it is a neutral-tone grammatical particle, not a heavily pronounced word.
语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Subject + Verb (+ Object) + 了 — perfective 了, marking a completed action

我昨天看电影了。 Wǒ zuótiān kàn diànyǐng le. I watched a movie yesterday.

Perfective 了 tells the listener that an action was completed, not merely that it happened in the past. Mandarin has no past-tense verb form; 了 together with a time word such as 昨天 signals completion within a known time frame.

Verb + 了 + Object — placement with simple verb-object expressions

我写了一封信。 Wǒ xiě le yì fēng xìn. I wrote a letter.

When the object has a number and measure word, 了 normally sits directly after the verb, before the object: 写了一封信, not 写一封信了.

没(有) + Verb (+ Object) — negating a completed action

我昨天没看电影。 Wǒ zuótiān méi kàn diànyǐng. I did not watch a movie yesterday.

了 disappears entirely in the negative. Use 没 or 没有 before the verb; do not add 不 here — 不 negates habitual or general statements, not completed events.

Time word + 以前/以后，Subject + Verb Phrase (+ 了) — sequencing events

吃饭以前，我打了电话。吃饭以后，我写了报告。 Chīfàn yǐqián, wǒ dǎ le diànhuà. Chīfàn yǐhòu, wǒ xiě le bàogào. Before eating, I made a phone call. After eating, I wrote the report.

以前 and 以后 follow the event they refer to, not precede it as English "before" and "after" do.

先...然后... — narrating a sequence

我先写了报告，然后看了电影。 Wǒ xiān xiě le bàogào, ránhòu kàn le diànyǐng. I first wrote the report, and then watched a movie.

Language Note — Two jobs for one character

In Chapter 14, you learned change-of-state 了, which appears at the end of a sentence to show a new situation: 我病了 (I've gotten sick). This chapter's perfective 了 shows that an action was completed, and it often sits right after the verb: 我写了信 (I wrote a letter). The two functions can combine in one sentence, but for now, focus on recognizing which job 了 is doing based on its position and the surrounding context.

对话 — Model Dialogue

Model Dialogue

大卫和安娜 — David and Anna:
大卫：安娜，你好点儿了吗？昨天你做什么了？
安娜：好多了，谢谢！昨天我整天都在家。吃饭以前，我先给妈妈打了电话，然后写了一封信给我的老师。
大卫：你完成报告了吗？
安娜：完成了！吃饭以后，我写了报告，也看了一个电影。
大卫：太好了。你今天想做什么？
安娜：今天我想去图书馆还书，昨天没去，因为我在家休息。

Notice how 安娜 moves from 以前 to 以后 to organize her day, and how she uses 没 (not 不) to say what she did not do yesterday.

Speaking Practice

控制性练习 — Controlled practice: guided interview

With a partner, take turns asking and answering: 你昨天做什么了？吃饭以前你做了什么？吃饭以后呢？你昨天打电话了吗？

交际任务 — Communicative task: yesterday interview

独立练习 — Solo alternative

Record yourself narrating your own day yesterday in four to six connected sentences, using perfective 了, at least one negative completed-action sentence with 没, and 以前 or 以后.

Reading Practice

Read the short message below and answer the comprehension questions.

老师，您好。对不起，我昨天没有写作业，因为我病了。可是今天好多了。今天早上，我先看了书，然后写了作业。下午，我完成了报告，也给同学打了电话，问了今天的功课。明天我会把作业带来。谢谢老师！
Hello, teacher. I'm sorry — I didn't do my homework yesterday because I was sick. But today I'm much better. This morning, I first read the book, and then did my homework. In the afternoon, I finished the report, and I also called a classmate to ask about today's assignment. Tomorrow I will bring the homework. Thank you, teacher!

理解问题 — Comprehension questions:

  1. 这个学生昨天为什么没有写作业？ (Why didn't this student do homework yesterday?)
  2. 今天早上，他先做了什么，然后做了什么？ (What did he do first this morning, and what did he do next?)
  3. 他给同学打电话问了什么？ (What did he call a classmate to ask about?)

Writing Workshop

Write a short narration of five to seven sentences describing a day you remember — yesterday, last weekend, or any recent day. Use only vocabulary and grammar from Chapters 1-16.

写作任务 — Writing task: Type your sentences with Pinyin input if possible, or copy/handwrite the characters you have already been assigned for handwriting. Your narration must include:

  1. At least three completed actions using perfective 了.
  2. At least one negative completed-action sentence using 没/没有.
  3. At least one sequencing expression using 以前, 以后, or 先...然后....
  4. A closing sentence giving your overall reaction to the day (for example, using 很 plus an adjective from an earlier chapter).

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • Perfective 了 is placed directly after the verb, before any measure-word object.
  • Negative sentences use 没/没有 with no 了, not 不.
  • 以前/以后 follow the event they refer to, matching the pattern practiced in this chapter.
  • The narration reads as one connected account of a day, in a sensible order.
  • A Chinese period (。) ends each sentence.

Culture and Communication

Culture Note — Sharing a day's events

In many Mandarin-speaking contexts, asking a friend, classmate, or colleague 你昨天做什么了？ is a common way to open or continue a casual conversation, similar to asking "how was your day" in English. Responses often move through the day roughly in order, which is part of why 以前, 以后, and 先...然后... are so useful. As always, how much detail people share, and how directly, varies by relationship and setting, so treat this as a common pattern rather than a fixed rule.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing perfective 了 with change-of-state 了: 我写了信 (I wrote a letter — action completed) is different from 我病了 (I've gotten sick — new situation). Check the position and the meaning, not just the presence of 了.
  • Using 不 instead of 没/没有: completed-event negation always uses 没 or 没有, and 了 disappears — 我没看电影, not 我不看电影了 unless you specifically mean a change of plan.
  • Placing 了 after a measure-word object: 写了一封信 is correct; 写一封信了 sounds unnatural in this context.
  • Reversing 以前/以后: remember that these words follow the event they describe: 吃饭以前 (before eating), not 以前吃饭.
