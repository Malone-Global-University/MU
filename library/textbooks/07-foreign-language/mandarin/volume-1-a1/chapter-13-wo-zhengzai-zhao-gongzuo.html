我正在找工作

Volume I, Chapter 13: Study and Work · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

You can already talk about ability, permission, and how to get around the city. This chapter turns to what you are doing right now — especially around study and work — and to the small connecting words that let you describe why you are doing it. You will learn to mark an action as ongoing with 在 or 正在; to expand 的 beyond simple possession so it can attach a description to a noun; to say who you are doing something with using 跟 and 和; and to give a simple reason using the fixed pattern 因为...所以....

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use 在 and 正在 to describe an action that is happening right now.
  2. Use 的 to attach a simple descriptive word to a noun, not only to show possession.
  3. Use 跟 and 和 to say who you are doing something with.
  4. Use the fixed expression 因为...所以... to give a simple reason for a statement.
  5. Talk about your studies, your major, and a job or internship search using controlled vocabulary.
  6. Combine study and work language with the ability, permission, directions, and location systems from earlier chapters.

Communication Scenario

安娜's language exchange partner 大卫 is finishing his degree and is currently looking for an internship. Over coffee near the library, he tells her what he is doing, why he wants the job, and who is helping him.

  1. You will listen to and read short exchanges about ongoing study and work activities.
  2. You will learn to describe what someone is doing right now and to give a simple reason for a choice.
  3. Everything in this chapter builds directly on the ability and permission language from Chapter 11 and the directions language from Chapter 12.

Chapter 13 continues Unit III, building immediately on Chapter 12. This chapter adds a new communicative domain — study and work — while continuing to recycle ability, permission, directions, and location language from earlier chapters.

发音 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

This chapter continues tone-pair practice and adds attention to the neutral-tone syllable that appears inside several new words.

  • 正在 (zhèngzài): both syllables are 4th tone. Keep them crisp and distinct rather than letting the second fall away — this is the same falling-tone discipline you practiced with 去图书馆 in Chapter 12.
  • 找 (zhǎo, 3rd tone) vs 到 (dào, 4th tone): these two verbs are easy to confuse because both can appear before a place or goal. Practice the dipping shape of zhǎo against the sharp fall of dào.
  • 兴趣 (xìngqù): the second syllable carries a 4th tone that beginners often flatten. Keep qù falling clearly, the same way you kept 去 falling in the previous chapter.
  • 因为 (yīnwèi) and 所以 (suǒyǐ): practice these two words as fixed chunks — say them together with their partner clause rather than pausing awkwardly in the middle.

Practice the model dialogue below aloud at least three times, paying special attention to keeping 正在 and 因为...所以 smooth and natural rather than choppy.

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

在 and 正在: describing an action happening right now

You already know 在 as a location word meaning "at" (Chapter 8). 在 can also appear directly before a verb to mark that action as ongoing. 正在 is a stronger, more explicit version of the same idea — "right in the middle of doing something." The two are often interchangeable in short sentences like these; 正在 simply places more emphasis on the ongoing nature of the action.

Pattern: Subject + 在/正在 + Verb (+ Object)

我在找工作。 Wǒ zài zhǎo gōngzuò. I'm looking for a job.

她正在写简历。 Tā zhèngzài xiě jiǎnlì. She is writing her résumé right now.

经理正在开会。 Jīnglǐ zhèngzài kāihuì. The manager is in a meeting right now.

A sentence may add 呢 at the end for a conversational, "still going on" feeling: 我正在找工作呢。 This is optional and does not change the basic meaning.

Expanding 的: attaching a description, not only showing possession

You learned 的 in Chapter 3 to show possession, as in 我的书 ("my book"). 的 can also attach a simple descriptive word directly before a noun, the way an adjective works in English.

Pattern: Description + 的 + Noun

我想找一个好的工作。 Wǒ xiǎng zhǎo yí ge hǎo de gōngzuò. I want to find a good job.

这是一个新的公司。 Zhè shì yí ge xīn de gōngsī. This is a new company.

电脑公司的工作 diànnǎo gōngsī de gōngzuò — a job at a computer company (noun + 的 + noun, as you already know)

In this chapter, practice only single-word descriptions before 的 plus a noun. Longer, clause-like modifiers built with 的 are developed in later volumes.

跟 and 和: saying who you are doing something with

跟 most often introduces a companion before a verb, describing who you do an action together with. 和 can do the same job, but it is also the ordinary word for "and" between two nouns.

Pattern: Subject + 跟/和 + Person + (一起) + Verb Phrase

我跟朋友一起找工作。 Wǒ gēn péngyou yìqǐ zhǎo gōngzuò. I'm looking for a job together with a friend.

我和他一起面试。 Wǒ hé tā yìqǐ miànshì. He and I are interviewing together.

和 as "and": 我和我朋友都在找工作。 Wǒ hé wǒ péngyou dōu zài zhǎo gōngzuò. My friend and I are both looking for jobs.

因为...所以...: a formulaic reason-result pattern

因为 introduces a reason and 所以 introduces the result. In this chapter, learn and use the whole pattern as a fixed, formulaic unit for giving simple reasons. Its full grammar — including sentences that drop one half of the pair — is analyzed in a later chapter.

Pattern: 因为 + Reason，所以 + Result

因为我喜欢电脑，所以我想找电脑公司的工作。 Yīnwèi wǒ xǐhuan diànnǎo, suǒyǐ wǒ xiǎng zhǎo diànnǎo gōngsī de gōngzuò. Because I like computers, I want to find a job at a computer company.

因为我很忙，所以我不能跟你一起去。 Yīnwèi wǒ hěn máng, suǒyǐ wǒ bù néng gēn nǐ yìqǐ qù. Because I'm very busy, I can't go with you.

Use this pattern as a memorized frame for now. Do not try to combine it yet with the more advanced connectors introduced in later chapters, such as 虽然...但是....

对话 — Model Dialogue

Model Dialogue

找实习 — Looking for an Internship:
安娜：大卫，你在忙什么呢？
大卫：我正在找实习。我的专业是电脑，我对电脑公司的工作很有兴趣。
安娜：你正在写简历吗？
大卫：对，我正在网上写简历，也正在找公司。我朋友小林也在找工作，我们跟一起找。
安娜：因为你喜欢电脑，所以你想找电脑公司的工作，对不对？
大卫：对！我明天有一个面试，是一个小公司，经理人很好。
安娜：太好了！你跟小林一起去面试吗？
大卫：不是，我一个人去，可是小林跟我一起练习面试问题。

Gloss: Anna asks David what he is busy with. He says he's currently looking for an internship — his major is computers, and he's very interested in jobs at computer companies. He's currently writing his résumé online and looking for companies; his friend Xiao Lin is also job-hunting, and they're searching together. Anna notes that because he likes computers, he wants a job at a computer company; David confirms this and adds that he has an interview tomorrow at a small company with a manager who is very nice. He goes to the interview alone, but Xiao Lin has been practicing interview questions with him.

Note: 我们跟一起找 is used here as a natural, slightly casual spoken shortcut for 我们跟他一起找; in your own writing, keep the person explicit after 跟, as shown in the Grammar section above.

Writing Workshop

Write a short message of five to seven sentences about your studies or a job/internship search, using only vocabulary and grammar from Chapters 1-13.

写作任务 — Writing task: Type your sentences with Pinyin input if possible, or copy/handwrite the characters you have already been assigned for handwriting. Your writing must include:

  1. An ongoing-action statement. Use 在 or 正在 to describe something you are currently doing.
  2. An expanded 的 phrase. Use a simple description + 的 + noun, such as 好的工作 or 新的公司.
  3. An accompaniment statement. Say who you are doing something with, using 跟 or 和.
  4. A reason-result sentence. Use the fixed pattern 因为...所以....
  5. At least one word from the study/work vocabulary, such as 专业, 工作, 面试, or 简历.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • 在/正在 comes directly before the verb, not after.
  • The descriptive word comes before 的, and 的 comes directly before the noun.
  • 因为 and 所以 are both included, with the reason first and the result second.
  • A Chinese period (。) or question mark (？) ends each sentence.

Speaking Practice

控制性练习 — Controlled practice: guided interview

With a partner, take turns asking and answering: 你正在做什么？你的专业是什么？你对什么有兴趣？你在找工作吗？你跟谁一起学习？

交际任务 — Communicative task: job-search interview role-play

With a partner, role-play a short conversation between two friends where one is currently job-hunting. Include an ongoing-action sentence, an expanded 的 phrase, an accompaniment statement, and a 因为...所以 reason.

独立练习 — Solo alternative

Record yourself describing your major or your current studies, one thing you are interested in, and one reason using 因为...所以.

Culture and Communication

Culture Note — Talking about study and work

In many Mandarin-speaking contexts, a person's 专业 (major or field of study) is a common early conversation topic, especially among students and young professionals. Questions about a person's major, workplace, or job search are generally considered ordinary small talk rather than intrusive, though comfort levels vary by relationship, setting, and individual. As with earlier culture notes, these patterns are common but not universal, and vary by region, generation, and personal preference.

Common Mistakes

  • Placing 在/正在 after the verb: avoid *我找在工作. The progressive marker comes before the verb: 我在找工作。
  • Forgetting 的 before the noun in a description: avoid *一个好工作 when you mean to highlight the description; with a single-syllable adjective plus 很, 的 is usually included before the noun: 一个好的工作。
  • Confusing 跟 and 和 in the "and" function: 和 is the more common choice for simply joining two nouns ("A and B"); 跟 is more common for "together with" before a verb. Both are acceptable in many contexts, but do not assume they are always interchangeable in every sentence position.
  • Using 所以 without 因为 (or the reverse) in this chapter: for now, keep the full pair together: 因为...，所以.... Dropping one half is analyzed in a later chapter.
