我的家人

Volume I, Chapter 3: Family and Relationships · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Show possession using 的, as in 我的家人.
  2. Talk about existence and possession using 有 and negate it with 没有.
  3. Ask "how many" with 几 and "how much/how many" with 多少, and know when each is appropriate.
  4. Use 个 and 口 as beginning measure words with family and people nouns.
  5. Name immediate family members and describe the size of a family.
  6. Produce accurate tone pairs across two-syllable words, including new neutral-tone words.
  7. Complete a short spoken and written exchange describing your own family.

Communication Scenario

After class, Sarah's classmate 大卫 shows her a photo on his phone. He wants to introduce his family, and he asks Sarah about hers. Sarah needs to say who is in her family, say how many people are in it, and figure out whether someone belongs to David or not — all without yet having any grammar system for tense, gender, or plural endings.

  1. This chapter gives you the language for talking about your family: who they are, how many there are, and whose photo or object something is.
  2. It also gives you your first true measure-word system, building on the fixed phrases 哪个 and 这个 from Chapter 2.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 3 builds directly on the demonstratives, negation, and question words from Chapter 2. 的 already appeared there in the fixed phrases 我的 and 老师的; this chapter analyzes 的 as a real, productive possession pattern for the first time. It also opens the measure-word system in full, using family vocabulary as the anchor.

Coming Into This Chapter

这/那, 不是 negation, 什么/哪个 questions, and the formulaic use of 的 from Chapter 2.

New in This Chapter

的 as a productive possession marker; 有/没有 for existence and possession; 几 and 多少 for asking quantity; the measure words 个 and 口; and tone-pair practice across two-syllable words.

Looking Forward

Chapter 4 introduces numbers, dates, weekdays, clock time, and the important 一/不 tone-sandhi pattern, building on the counting you begin here.

Tones and Characters

3.1 Tone pairs across two-syllable words

Many of this chapter's new words are two syllables long, and several end in neutral tone: 爸爸, 妈妈, 哥哥, 姐姐, 弟弟, 妹妹, and 孩子. In each of these words, the first syllable carries a full tone and the second syllable is short and light, with no tone mark.

Pronunciation Focus — Reduplicated kinship terms

Notice the pattern: the same syllable is said twice, but only the first repetition keeps its full tone. 妈妈 is mā (first tone) + ma (neutral), not mā mā. This reduced second syllable is a real, predictable feature of these words, not a casual shortcut, and you should practice it the same way every time you say the word.

Also review the tone pair in 没有 (méi yǒu): second tone followed by third tone. When you say the two syllables together at a natural pace, the second tone dips slightly less than it would as a standalone syllable, but you do not need to memorize a separate rule for this yet — simply practice the two syllables smoothly, one after another.

3.3 A first measure-word component: 口

Language Note — 口 as both a word and a component

口 means "mouth" on its own, but as a measure word it is used specifically to count people within a household, as in 三口人 ("three people," referring to a family). You will also see 口 appear as a small component inside other characters later in the course, where it often relates to openings, containers, or speech. Treat this as a pattern to notice, not a rule that predicts every character containing 口.

You are not expected to handwrite every character in this chapter. Continue applying the top-before-bottom and left-before-right principles from Chapter 2 to any handwriting practice you do.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

3.4 的 for possession

Purpose: show that something belongs to someone, or describe a relationship between people.

的 links a possessor to the thing or person possessed. The pattern is possessor + 的 + possessed noun, which is the reverse order of the equivalent English phrase.

Language Focus — [Possessor] 的 [Noun]

The basic pattern is: [possessor] + 的 + [noun]。 You already used this pattern formulaically in Chapter 2 with 我的 and 老师的. Now you can build it with any possessor and any noun.

  • 这是我的家人。 Zhè shì wǒ de jiārén. These are my family members.
  • 那是大卫的爸爸。 Nà shì Dàwèi de bàba. That is David's father.
Grammar Note — Close relationships without 的

With very close relationships, such as immediate family or close friends, speakers sometimes drop 的: 我爸爸 ("my dad") is just as natural as 我的爸爸. Both forms are correct in this chapter; a fuller discussion of when 的 may be omitted comes in a later volume.

3.5 有 and 没有 for existence and possession

Purpose: say that someone has something, or that something exists.

有 is negated with 没 or 没有, never with 不. This is one of the most important early exceptions in Mandarin negation.

Language Building Block — 有/没有
我有一个弟弟。 / 我没有弟弟。
Affirmative possession uses 有 directly before the noun (with a number and measure word when counting). Negative possession replaces 有 with 没有; 不有 is never correct.
你有姐姐吗？没有，我没有姐姐，我有妹妹。
Do you have an older sister? No, I don't have an older sister, I have a younger sister.
  • 我有一个哥哥。 Wǒ yǒu yí ge gēge. I have an older brother.
  • 大卫没有妹妹。 Dàwèi méiyǒu mèimei. David does not have a younger sister.

Note that 一 is pronounced yí here, shifting to second tone before the fourth-tone measure word 个. This is an early preview of the full 一 sandhi pattern, which is taught formally in Chapter 4.

3.6 几 and 多少 for asking "how many"

Purpose: ask about a quantity, choosing the right question word for the expected size of the number.

几 is used for numbers a speaker expects to be small, usually under about ten, and it is normally followed by a measure word. 多少 is used for larger or completely unknown quantities, and a measure word after it is optional.

  • 你家有几口人？ Nǐ jiā yǒu jǐ kǒu rén? How many people are in your family?
  • 我家有四口人。 Wǒ jiā yǒu sì kǒu rén. There are four people in my family.
  • 你的学校有多少学生？ Nǐ de xuéxiào yǒu duōshao xuésheng? How many students does your school have?
Grammar Note — Choosing 几 or 多少

Family size is small and countable, so 几 with the measure word 口 is the natural choice: 几口人. A school's student population is large and open-ended, so 多少 is the natural choice, and no measure word is needed before 学生. As you meet more nouns, ask yourself whether the expected number is small and countable or large and open-ended.

3.7 个 and 口 as measure words

Purpose: correctly link a number to a noun using the appropriate measure word.

Mandarin nouns cannot normally be counted with a number alone; a measure word must sit between the number and the noun. 个 is the broadest, most common measure word and works with most people and many objects. 口 is used specifically for counting people within a household.

Language Focus — [Number] + [Measure Word] + [Noun]

The basic pattern is: [number] + [measure word] + [noun]。 一个哥哥 (one older brother), 三口人 (three people, as in household size). Do not say *一哥哥 or *三人 when counting a household — the measure word cannot be skipped.

This chapter only asks you to use 个 and 口. A wider set of measure words develops starting in Chapter 9, once you have more nouns to count.

Model Dialogue

Setting: After class, 大卫 shows Sarah a family photo on his phone.

大卫 这是我的家人。我家有五口人。 Zhè shì wǒ de jiārén. Wǒ jiā yǒu wǔ kǒu rén. These are my family members. There are five people in my family.
莎拉 你有哥哥吗？ Nǐ yǒu gēge ma? Do you have an older brother?
大卫 没有，我没有哥哥，我有一个姐姐和一个弟弟。 Méiyǒu, wǒ méiyǒu gēge, wǒ yǒu yí ge jiějie hé yí ge dìdi. No, I don't have an older brother, I have one older sister and one younger brother.
莎拉 那是谁的狗？ Nà shì shéi de gǒu? Whose dog is that?
大卫 那是我姐姐的狗。你家有几口人？ Nà shì wǒ jiějie de gǒu. Nǐ jiā yǒu jǐ kǒu rén? That is my older sister's dog. How many people are in your family?
莎拉 我家有四口人：爸爸、妈妈、妹妹和我。我没有哥哥，也没有姐姐。 Wǒ jiā yǒu sì kǒu rén: bàba, māma, mèimei hé wǒ. Wǒ méiyǒu gēge, yě méiyǒu jiějie. There are four people in my family: dad, mom, younger sister, and me. I don't have an older brother, and I don't have an older sister either.

理解问题 — Comprehension questions:

  1. 大卫家有几口人？ (How many people are in David's family?)
  2. 狗是谁的？ (Whose dog is it?)
  3. Does Sarah have an older brother or an older sister? How do you know?

Writing Workshop

Write a short introduction of your own family, following the models above. Your introduction should use 的 for possession, at least one 有/没有 sentence, and one 几口人 statement or question.

写作任务 — Writing task: Write four or five short sentences in simplified characters, typing them with Pinyin input if possible, or copying the characters carefully by hand. Handwrite 的, 有, and 几 at least three times each, applying the stroke-direction principles from Chapter 2.

  1. Introduce your family. Start with 我家有...口人。
  2. Name family members. Use 我有... or 我没有... for each sibling relationship.
  3. Show possession. Use 我的... or 我...的... for at least one object or relationship.
  4. Check your measure words. Confirm you used 口 for household size and 个 elsewhere, and that no number appears directly before a noun without one.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • 的 is used accurately to show possession.
  • 有 and 没有 are both used correctly, with 没 (not 不) negating 有.
  • 几口人 or 多少 is used correctly for the expected size of the quantity.
  • Numbers are never placed directly before a noun without a measure word.
  • A Chinese period (。) or question mark (？) ends each sentence.

Speaking Practice

控制性练习 — Controlled practice: reading the model dialogue aloud

Read the model dialogue above aloud twice: once slowly, sounding out each tone, and once at a more natural pace. Pay close attention to the reduced second syllable in 爸爸, 妈妈, 姐姐, and 弟弟, and to keeping 没, not 不, in front of 有.

交际任务 — Communicative task: family interview

With a partner or small group, ask and answer 你家有几口人？ and 你有哥哥/姐姐/弟弟/妹妹吗？, responding with accurate 有/没有 sentences and at least one 的 possession statement.

独立练习 — Solo alternative

Record yourself describing your own family in four or five sentences, including your family's size with 口 and at least one sibling relationship with 有 or 没有. Listen back and check the tone of 没有 and the reduced syllables in any kinship terms you used.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Asking about family size

Asking 你家有几口人？ is a common, everyday question in many Mandarin-speaking contexts and is generally not considered intrusive, unlike some similarly direct personal questions in English-speaking cultures. As with any personal topic, comfort levels vary by individual, setting, and relationship, so read the situation as you would in any language.

Culture in Context — Birth-order-specific sibling terms

Notice that Mandarin does not have a single word for "brother" or "sister" the way English does — 哥哥, 姐姐, 弟弟, and 妹妹 each specify both gender and relative birth order. This reflects the general importance of birth order in family terminology across many Mandarin-speaking communities, though exact conventions and attitudes toward birth order vary by family, generation, and region.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Negating 有 with 不

Error: Saying *我不有哥哥, applying the general negation pattern from Chapter 2 to 有.

Correction: 有 is always negated with 没 or 没有, never 不: 我没有哥哥。 This is a fixed exception you should memorize now.

Common Mistake — Dropping the measure word

Error: Saying *我家有四人, placing a number directly before a noun.

Correction: A measure word must sit between the number and the noun when counting: 我家有四口人。 Review Language Focus 3.7.

Common Mistake — Confusing 几 and 多少

Error: Asking *你家有多少口人？ for family size, which sounds unnatural because family size is a small, countable quantity.

Correction: Use 几 with a measure word for small, expected quantities: 你家有几口人？ Save 多少 for large or unknown quantities, as in 你的学校有多少学生？
