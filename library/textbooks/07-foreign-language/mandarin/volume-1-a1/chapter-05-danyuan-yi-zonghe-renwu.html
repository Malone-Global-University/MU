单元一综合任务：认识你和我的世界

Volume I, Chapter 5: Unit I Assessment · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

This is a cumulative assessment chapter. By completing it, you will demonstrate that you can:

  1. Greet someone, introduce yourself, and ask and answer 是/不是 and 这/那/什么/哪 questions from Chapters 1-2.
  2. Describe your family using 的, 有/没有, and 几/多少 from Chapter 3.
  3. Ask and state the day, the date, and the time using the systems from Chapter 4.
  4. Place time expressions correctly before the verb.
  5. Produce the 一 and 不 tone-sandhi patterns with reasonable accuracy in familiar words.
  6. Read and write short texts combining introductions, family, and schedule information using only the characters, Pinyin, and grammar activated in Chapters 1-4.
  7. Complete an integrated listening, speaking, reading, and writing task without new instruction.

Communication Scenario

A new student, 小林, is joining Sarah's Mandarin class this week. Before the class gathering that Sarah and 大卫 arranged in Chapter 4, Sarah wants to help 小林 get to know the class: who everyone is, a little about their families, and the schedule for the week, including the gathering itself.

  1. This chapter does not teach anything new. It asks you to bring together everything from Chapters 1 through 4 — greetings and identity, family, and numbers, dates, and time — to help welcome 小林 into the class.
  2. Every task below uses only vocabulary, characters, and grammar you have already studied.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 5 closes Unit I. It does not introduce new productive grammar, new high-burden characters, or new pronunciation systems. Instead, it verifies that you can coordinate everything scheduled in Chapters 1-4 in one connected task: introducing people, describing family, and managing a simple schedule.

Coming Into This Chapter

Greetings, 是/不是, 这/那/什么/哪 from Chapters 1-2; 的, 有/没有, 几/多少 from Chapter 3; numbers 0-99, days, dates, clock time, time-before-verb order, and 一/不 sandhi from Chapter 4.

New in This Chapter

No new vocabulary, characters, or grammar. This chapter is entirely review and integrated assessment.

Purpose: confirm control of 的, 有/没有, and 几/多少 from Chapter 3.

5.5 Time-before-verb order

Purpose: confirm control of day, date, clock-time expressions, and their placement before the verb, from Chapter 4.

Language Building Block — Review: [Subject] + [Time] + [Verb Phrase]
小林今天九点见老师。
The time word 今天 and the clock time 九点 both appear before the verb 见, exactly as established in Chapter 4.

No new word-order rule is introduced here. This section only confirms that the Chapter 4 rule transfers correctly to new sentences about new people.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Sarah introduces the new student, 小林, to 大卫 before class.

莎拉 大卫，这是小林，他是新学生。 Dàwèi, zhè shì Xiǎo Lín, tā shì xīn xuésheng. David, this is Xiao Lin, he is a new student.
大卫 你好，小林！你有兄弟姐妹吗？ Nǐ hǎo, Xiǎo Lín! Nǐ yǒu xiōngdì jiěmèi ma? Hello, Xiao Lin! Do you have any siblings?
小林 我有一个哥哥，没有姐姐。 Wǒ yǒu yí ge gēge, méiyǒu jiějie. I have one older brother, no older sister.
莎拉 小林，我们的聚会是星期五，九月十二号，六点半。 Xiǎo Lín, wǒmen de jùhuì shì xīngqīwǔ, jiǔ yuè shí'èr hào, liù diǎn bàn. Xiao Lin, our gathering is Friday, September 12th, at 6:30.
小林 好，我星期五六点半到。 Hǎo, wǒ xīngqīwǔ liù diǎn bàn dào. Okay, I'll arrive Friday at 6:30.

理解问题 — Comprehension questions:

  1. 小林有哥哥吗？有姐姐吗？ (Does Xiao Lin have an older brother? An older sister?)
  2. 聚会是星期几？几月几号？几点？ (What day, date, and time is the gathering?)

自我介绍 — Introduction Note
大家好！我是马克，我是学生。这是我的家人：我有一个哥哥，一个妹妹，没有姐姐。我们家有四口人。今天星期三，九月十号。我明天九点上课，下午三点没有课。

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

Culture and Communication Review

Culture in Context — Introducing a new person

In many Mandarin-speaking contexts, a third person is commonly introduced before the new person speaks for themselves, as Sarah does for 小林 above. This is a common pattern in classroom and social settings, though individual and regional practice varies.

Culture in Context — Talking about siblings

Mandarin distinguishes older and younger siblings by separate words, such as 哥哥 (older brother) and 弟弟 (younger brother), rather than a single word for "brother." Keep this distinction in mind when describing your own family.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing 是/不是 with 有/没有

Error: Saying *我不是哥哥 to mean "I don't have an older brother."

Correction: Possession uses 有/没有, not 是/不是: 我没有哥哥。 Review Grammar 5.3 and 5.4.

Common Mistake — Placing time words after the verb

Error: Saying *我上课星期五, placing the time word after the verb.

Correction: Time words come before the verb: 我星期五上课。 Review Grammar 5.5 and Chapter 4, Language Building Block 4.5.

Common Mistake — Forgetting 一/不 tone sandhi in new combinations

Error: Pronouncing 一个 as *yī ge instead of yí ge when speaking quickly.

Correction: The sandhi rule applies regardless of speaking speed: since 个 is fourth tone, 一 always shifts to second tone before it. Review Chapter 4, Pronunciation 4.1.
