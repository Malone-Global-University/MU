今天星期几？

Volume I, Chapter 4: Numbers, Dates, and Time · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Count from 0 to 99 in Mandarin, including numbers already used informally in Chapters 1-3.
  2. Ask and state the day of the week using 星期几.
  3. Ask and state the date using 几月几号.
  4. Tell time using 点 and 分.
  5. Place time expressions correctly before the verb, following Mandarin's time-before-verb word order.
  6. Apply the 一 tone-sandhi pattern and begin the 不 tone-sandhi pattern in context.
  7. Complete a short spoken and written exchange about days, dates, and schedules.

Communication Scenario

Sarah's Mandarin class is planning a small get-together, and 大卫 wants to confirm the day, the date, and the time before he tells the rest of the class. Sarah needs to say what day it is today, what today's date is, and what time the gathering starts — all using numbers and time words she has not yet had a full system for.

  1. This chapter gives you the language for counting, for naming days and dates, and for telling time.
  2. It also gives you the word-order rule that time expressions come before the verb in Mandarin, and your first full look at the 一 and 不 tone-sandhi patterns you began to notice with 一个 in Chapter 3.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 4 builds on the counting and measure-word foundation from Chapter 3, where you used the number 一 with 个 and 口 and first noticed that 一 shifts to second tone before a fourth-tone syllable. This chapter expands your numbers up through 99 and puts them to work in two new practical systems: days/dates and clock time. It also gives you your first explicit word-order rule beyond basic subject-verb-object: time expressions belong before the verb, not at the end of the sentence as in English.

Coming Into This Chapter

The number 一, the measure words 个 and 口, and the preview of 一 shifting tone before 个 from Chapter 3.

New in This Chapter

Numbers 0-99; 星期几 and the days of the week; 几月几号 and the calendar; 点/分 for clock time; time-before-verb word order; and the full 一 and 不 tone-sandhi patterns.

Tones and Characters

4.1 The 一 tone-sandhi pattern

In Chapter 3 you noticed that 一 is pronounced yí in 一个 because 个 is fourth tone. This chapter makes that pattern explicit and complete.

Pronunciation Focus — 一 changes tone depending on what follows

一 is first tone (yī) only when said alone, at the end of a phrase, or before a pause — for example, when counting aloud: yī, èr, sān. Before a fourth-tone syllable, 一 becomes second tone (yí), as in 一个 (yí ge) and 一日 (yí rì). Before a first-, second-, or third-tone syllable, 一 becomes fourth tone (yì), as in 一天 (yì tiān) and 一年 (yì nián). This is a fully regular pattern, not an exception to memorize word by word.

4.2 The 不 tone-sandhi pattern

Pronunciation Focus — 不 before a fourth tone

不 is normally fourth tone (bù), as in 不是 from Chapter 2. Before another fourth-tone syllable, 不 shifts to second tone: 不是 is actually pronounced bú shì, not bù shì. Before first, second, or third tone, 不 stays fourth tone, as in 不喝 (bù hē). You have been hearing this pattern in 不是 since Chapter 2 without a formal rule; now you have the full pattern.

Both sandhi patterns affect pronunciation only. In writing, 一 and 不 are always written the same way regardless of their spoken tone.

Language Note — 天 as both "day" and "sky"

天 means "day" in words like 今天 and 明天, but the same character also means "sky" in other contexts. You will meet more characters like this, where one written form carries more than one related meaning depending on the word it appears in.

You are not expected to handwrite every character in this chapter. Continue applying the stroke-order principles from Chapters 2 and 3, and note that 月, 日, and 今 are common components you will see again in later date and time vocabulary.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

4.4 Numbers 0-99

Purpose: count people, dates, times, and quantities beyond the numbers used formulaically in Chapter 3.

Mandarin numbers 0-10 are single syllables. Numbers 11-19 are formed with 十 ("ten") plus a units digit: 十一 (11), 十五 (15). Numbers 20-99 are formed with a tens digit plus 十 plus a units digit: 二十 (20), 三十五 (35), 九十九 (99).

Language Focus — Building numbers 11-99

The basic pattern is: [tens digit] + 十 + [units digit], with the tens digit dropped for the teens and the units digit dropped for round tens. 十 (10), 十七 (17), 六十 (60), 六十七 (67).

Numbers above 99 are introduced starting in Chapter 6, once you have a communicative need for them.

4.5 Time-before-verb word order

Purpose: correctly place time expressions in a Mandarin sentence.

Unlike English, Mandarin places time expressions before the verb, most often right after the subject. This applies to days, dates, and clock times alike.

Language Building Block — [Subject] + [Time] + [Verb Phrase]
我们今天上课。
The time word 今天 sits between the subject 我们 and the verb 上课, not at the end of the sentence as "today" would in English.
聚会明天几点开始？
What time does the gathering start tomorrow?
  • 今天星期三。 Jīntiān xīngqīsān. Today is Wednesday.
  • 我们明天九点见。 Wǒmen míngtiān jiǔ diǎn jiàn. We will meet tomorrow at nine o'clock.
Grammar Note — Time word before place word

When a sentence has both a time word and a place word, time normally comes first: 我们明天在学校见。 ("We'll meet at school tomorrow.") A fuller treatment of place-word order begins in Chapter 8.

4.6 Asking and stating the day of the week

Purpose: ask what day it is and answer with the correct weekday.

Days of the week are formed with 星期 plus a number one through six, with Sunday as an exception: 星期一 (Monday) through 星期六 (Saturday), then 星期日 or 星期天 (Sunday).

  • 今天星期几？ Jīntiān xīngqī jǐ? What day of the week is it today?
  • 今天星期五。 Jīntiān xīngqīwǔ. Today is Friday.

Notice that these sentences use a nominal predicate pattern without 是, similar to time expressions in many languages. This is a fixed pattern for dates, days, and time; it does not mean 是 is optional everywhere.

4.7 Asking and stating the date

Purpose: ask what today's date is and answer with month and day.

几月几号 asks for both the month and the day, always in that order — month first, then day, the reverse of common English date order.

  • 今天几月几号？ Jīntiān jǐ yuè jǐ hào? What is today's date?
  • 今天九月十号。 Jīntiān jiǔ yuè shí hào. Today is September 10th.

4.8 Telling time with 点 and 分

Purpose: state and understand clock times.

点 marks the hour and 分 marks the minute. 半 ("half") replaces 三十分 for the half-hour.

Language Focus — [Number] 点 [Number] 分

The basic pattern is: [hour] + 点 + [minutes] + 分。 三点 (3:00), 三点二十分 (3:20), 三点半 (3:30). When the minute count is under ten, a 零 (líng) may appear before it in careful speech: 三点零五分 (3:05).

  • 现在几点？ Xiànzài jǐ diǎn? What time is it now?
  • 现在八点半。 Xiànzài bā diǎn bàn. It's 8:30 now.

This chapter only asks you to state times on the hour, the half hour, and simple minute counts. Quarter-hour expressions with 刻 and "X minutes to the hour" patterns are introduced in a later volume.

Model Dialogue

Setting: After class, 大卫 wants to confirm the class gathering with Sarah.

大卫 今天星期几？ Jīntiān xīngqī jǐ? What day is it today?
莎拉 今天星期三，九月十号。 Jīntiān xīngqīsān, jiǔ yuè shí hào. Today is Wednesday, September 10th.
大卫 我们的聚会是星期五，对不对？ Wǒmen de jùhuì shì xīngqīwǔ, duì bu duì? Our gathering is Friday, right?
莎拉 对，星期五，九月十二号。 Duì, xīngqīwǔ, jiǔ yuè shí'èr hào. Right, Friday, September 12th.
大卫 聚会几点开始？ Jùhuì jǐ diǎn kāishǐ? What time does the gathering start?
莎拉 六点半开始。 Liù diǎn bàn kāishǐ. It starts at 6:30.
大卫 好，我明天告诉大家。 Hǎo, wǒ míngtiān gàosu dàjiā. Okay, I'll tell everyone tomorrow.

理解问题 — Comprehension questions:

  1. 今天几月几号？星期几？ (What is today's date? What day of the week?)
  2. 聚会是什么时候？ (When is the gathering?)
  3. What time does the gathering start?

Writing Workshop

Write a short schedule note about your own week, following the models above. Your note should use at least one weekday, one date with 月 and 号, and one clock time with 点.

写作任务 — Writing task: Write four or five short sentences in simplified characters, typing them with Pinyin input if possible, or copying the characters carefully by hand. Handwrite 今天, 月, and 点 at least three times each, applying the stroke-direction principles from Chapters 2 and 3.

  1. State today's day and date. Start with 今天星期...，...月...号。
  2. Describe one activity with a time. Use 我...点... for something you do at a specific time.
  3. Mention tomorrow or yesterday. Use 明天 or 昨天 with correct time-before-verb placement.
  4. Check your word order. Confirm every time word appears before its verb, not after.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • Time words (今天, 明天, 昨天, 现在) appear before the verb, not after.
  • Dates follow the order month then day: ...月...号.
  • Clock times follow the order hour then minute: ...点...分.
  • Numbers 11-99 are built correctly with 十.
  • A Chinese period (。) or question mark (？) ends each sentence.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — The Mandarin week begins on Monday

In many Mandarin-speaking contexts, the week is commonly thought of as beginning with 星期一 (Monday), with 星期日/星期天 (Sunday) understood as the last day of the week, though calendar formatting and personal habits vary by individual, workplace, and region.

Culture in Context — Year-month-day order

Written dates in many Mandarin-speaking contexts commonly follow the order year, then month, then day, the reverse of common U.S. month-day-year order. This chapter focuses on spoken month-and-day questions; the full year-month-day writing pattern is introduced in a later volume.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Putting the time word at the end of the sentence

Error: Saying *我上课今天, placing the time word after the verb by analogy with English.

Correction: Time words come before the verb: 我今天上课。 Review Language Building Block 4.5.

Common Mistake — Reversing month and day

Error: Saying *十号九月, giving the day before the month.

Correction: Mandarin always states the month first, then the day: 九月十号。 Review Grammar 4.7.

Common Mistake — Forgetting 一/不 tone sandhi

Error: Pronouncing 一天 as *yī tiān or 不是 as *bù shì in connected speech.

Correction: 一 and 不 both shift to second tone specifically before a fourth-tone syllable. Since 天 is first tone, 一天 keeps 一 at fourth tone: yì tiān. Since 个 is fourth tone, 一个 shifts 一 to second tone: yí ge. Since 是 is fourth tone, 不是 shifts 不 to second tone: bú shì. Review Pronunciation 4.1 and 4.2 and check the tone of the syllable that follows before deciding whether a shift applies.
