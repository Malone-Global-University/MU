社会问题和公共语言

Volume IV, Chapter 8: Social Issues and Public Language · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Mandarin

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

Chapter 7 moved you from analyzing a report to taking part in a live interview and public discussion about it, where you learned to open and close a turn, request clarification, and state a stance with a concession. This chapter widens the lens from a single campus recommendation to broader social issues that affect a public or an institution as a whole. When the topic grows larger and more contested, precision matters more: claims must be carefully qualified, institutional vocabulary must be used accurately, and evidence must be treated cautiously rather than presented as certain. This chapter teaches you to hedge a claim appropriately, cite a source of information without overstating it, and discuss a social issue using institutional vocabulary while keeping the turn-taking, clarification, and stance language from Chapter 7 fully active.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use institutional vocabulary to describe a social issue, a policy, and its effects.
  2. Qualify a claim appropriately using hedging language rather than stating it as fact.
  3. Cite a statistic or source cautiously, distinguishing correlation from certainty.
  4. Recognize the receptive function of the 被 passive construction when reading about policy effects.
  5. Discuss a social issue in a structured way that moves from description to cautious evaluation.
  6. Combine hedged claims with the stance and concession language already consolidated in Chapter 7.

Communication Scenario

The international-student office's online-application recommendation from Chapters 6 and 7 turns out to be part of a larger university policy on digital services. The student newspaper is now preparing a longer piece on how this broader policy affects student resources, campus housing applications, and financial-aid processing. You are asked to help draft part of this piece, which requires discussing a social issue at the institutional level rather than a single office's decision.

  1. You will listen to a short segment from a campus panel discussing the broader digital-services policy.
  2. You will read a short institutional notice describing the policy's stated goals and resource allocation.
  3. You will practice qualifying claims about the policy's effects rather than stating them as certain.
  4. You will write a short paragraph that describes the issue and offers a cautious evaluation of it.

发音 — Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

This chapter introduces no new sound system. It continues developing intelligibility in extended formal discourse, where hedged claims often contain several short clauses in a row. Keep 在一定程度上 and 可能会导致 phrased as single rhythmic units rather than pausing awkwardly in the middle, since breaking them up can make a hedge sound like a flat statement instead. Distinguish 影响 (yǐngxiǎng) from 因为 in connected speech, since both are common in explanations of cause and effect and are easy to run together at a natural pace.

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Hedged Claims and Institutional Description

This chapter's productive focus is careful, qualified claim-making about a social issue, not a new grammar system. Four patterns support this: describing a policy and its scope, hedging a claim about effect or cause, citing a statistic cautiously, and recognizing the receptive 被 passive pattern when reading about policy effects. The stance and concession language from Chapter 7 remains active and combines naturally with the hedging language taught here.

1. Describing a Policy and Its Scope: 政策 / 影响 / 资源分配

Use 政策 to name a policy, 影响 to describe its effect, and 资源分配 to describe how resources are distributed under it.

这项数字化服务政策影响了好几个部门的资源分配，包括住房申请和财务资助处理。

2. Hedging a Claim: 在一定程度上 / 可能会导致 / 相关

Use 在一定程度上 to limit the strength of a claim, 可能会导致 to propose a possible rather than certain cause-effect relationship, and 相关 to describe two things as correlated without claiming one directly causes the other.

在一定程度上，把网上服务设为默认方式提高了效率；但资源集中在一个系统上，也可能会导致其他部门处理变慢，这两个现象看起来相关。

3. Citing a Statistic Cautiously: 据统计 / 值得注意的是

Use 据统计 to introduce a number from a source, and follow it with 值得注意的是 to add a caution about what the number does or does not show.

据统计，今年申请数量比去年增加了百分之十五。值得注意的是，这个数字并不能说明处理速度是否也相应提高了。

4. Recognizing 被 in Policy Description (Receptive)

You will begin to see the passive marker 被 in institutional writing, especially when a text emphasizes what happened to something rather than who did it. At this stage, recognize its function receptively rather than producing it independently.

财务资助的处理时间被这项政策间接影响了。 (Recognition only: financial-aid processing time was indirectly affected by this policy.)

Putting It Together

Discussing a social issue responsibly means naming the policy and its scope, qualifying claims about cause and effect instead of overstating them, citing statistics with an appropriate caution, and recognizing passive constructions that describe effects without assigning blame -- while still using the stance and concession language from Chapter 7 to state your own evaluation.

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: 校园数字化服务政策简报 — Campus Digital Services Policy Briefing

本学期，学校开始推行一项新的数字化服务政策，目标是提高各部门的处理效率，把更多日常申请转移到网上系统。国际学生办公室的网上申请推荐是这项政策的一部分。

据统计，网上申请的处理时间平均比现场申请短三天，学生满意度也有所提高。在一定程度上，这项政策确实达到了提高效率的目标。

然而，值得注意的是，资源集中在网上系统上，也可能会导致其他需要人工处理的服务变慢，比如住房申请和财务资助审核。财务资助办公室表示，他们的处理时间在过去一个学期里有所延长，虽然这个变化和数字化政策之间的关系还没有完全确定，但两者在时间上看起来相关。

学校表示会继续收集公众意见，并在下学期公布进一步的资源分配计划。

练习 — Guided Practice

Guided Practice

  1. Describe a policy's scope: 这项政策影响了______。
  2. Hedge a claim: 在一定程度上，______。
  3. Propose a possible cause: ______可能会导致______。
  4. Cite a statistic cautiously: 据统计，______。值得注意的是，______。
  5. Describe a correlation without overclaiming: 这两个现象看起来相关，但______还不完全清楚。

写作练习 — Writing Component

Writing Component: A Short Institutional Issue Paragraph

Write a short paragraph describing a social or institutional issue, moving from description to cautious evaluation.

  1. Name the policy or issue and its general scope using 政策 or 影响.
  2. Cite at least one statistic or source using 据统计 or 根据.
  3. Add a caution about that statistic using 值得注意的是.
  4. Hedge at least one claim about cause or effect using 在一定程度上 or 可能会导致.
  5. State your own stance using 我的看法是 or 依我看, including a concession with 不可否认...但是 from Chapter 7.
  6. Close with a one-sentence formal summary using 总的来说.

写作检查清单 — Writing checklist:

  • The policy or issue and its scope are clearly named.
  • At least one statistic is cited and immediately qualified.
  • At least one claim about cause or effect is appropriately hedged, not stated as certain.
  • The stance statement includes a concession, as in Chapter 7.
  • The paragraph closes with 总的来说 in consistent formal register.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Hedged public language across institutions

Institutions in Mandarin-speaking settings, as in many others, often prefer carefully qualified public language over blunt claims when a policy's effects are still uncertain, though the degree of hedging varies by institution, publication type, and individual writer, and also varies somewhat across Mainland, Taiwan, Hong Kong/Macau, Singapore, and diaspora institutional contexts. A single notice's tone should not be treated as representative of how every institution in every Mandarin-speaking setting communicates about social issues.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Overstating a correlation as a cause: use 相关 or 可能会导致 rather than presenting an unconfirmed link as a proven cause.
  • Citing a statistic without qualification: a number introduced with 据统计 should usually be followed by a caution such as 值得注意的是 when its meaning is limited.
  • Confusing 影响 and 引起: 影响 describes a general effect or influence; it does not by itself claim a specific, confirmed cause.
  • Producing 被 independently before it is scheduled: recognize 被 in institutional reading, but this chapter does not require independent production of the passive construction.
  • Dropping Chapter 7's stance and concession language: a hedged claim about a policy still benefits from a clear, supported personal stance.
