资料、出处和引用

Volume IV, Chapter 11: Sources, Attribution, and Citation · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Mandarin

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

Unit II asked you to combine media, interview, and institutional language into one dossier. Unit III moves further into research-based communication, and this chapter opens that unit by giving you the tools to handle other people's words and ideas responsibly: how to cite a source, how to paraphrase without copying the original wording too closely, how to signal that information comes from somewhere else, and how to comment on whether a source seems reliable. These skills matter whenever you summarize an article, quote a study, or explain where a claim comes from -- in academic writing, in journalism, and in ordinary professional communication.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify and use core vocabulary for sources, citation, paraphrase, and reliability.
  2. Distinguish a direct citation from a paraphrase in both reading and writing.
  3. Use attribution frames such as 据...报道 and 据...介绍 to introduce information from a source.
  4. Paraphrase a short passage using 转述, 概括, and 换句话说 without copying the original sentence structure.
  5. Comment on whether a source seems 可靠 or 权威, and note when a source's 出处 is unclear.
  6. Write a short paragraph that cites, paraphrases, and evaluates a source appropriately.

Communication Scenario

You are helping a classmate prepare a short research note for a class project. Your classmate has collected two articles and a set of interview notes but is not sure which claims are well supported and which are not, or how to write about them without simply copying the original sentences. Your task is to help sort the material: identify where each claim comes from, decide how reliable each source seems, and draft a short paragraph that cites and paraphrases the material responsibly.

  1. You will listen to a short recorded note in which a student explains where each piece of information came from.
  2. You will read two short source excerpts and compare how reliable they seem.
  3. You will practice turning a quoted sentence into a paraphrase.
  4. You will write a short research note that cites, paraphrases, and evaluates its sources.

语法和篇章 — Grammar and Discourse Focus

Grammar and Discourse Focus: Attribution and Paraphrase Frames

This chapter does not introduce new sentence-level grammar. Instead, it develops a discourse-level system: how to mark where information comes from, and how to reshape someone else's words into your own.

Attribution frames

  • 据 + source + 报道 / 介绍 / 统计，... — "According to [source], ..."
  • [source] 认为 / 指出 / 说，... — "[Source] believes / points out / says that ..."
  • 根据 + source，... — "Based on [source], ..."

These frames must appear before the claim they support, and the source should be named specifically (a person, an office, a publication) rather than left vague.

Direct citation versus paraphrase

A direct citation reproduces the original wording and is marked with 引号 (quotation marks) and often introduced with 引用. A paraphrase restates the content in different wording and sentence structure, introduced with 转述, 概括, or followed by a 换句话说 clarification. In academic and professional Mandarin writing, paraphrase is used far more often than direct citation; reserve direct citation for wording that is unusually precise or important to quote exactly.

Commenting on reliability

Use 可靠 and 权威 to evaluate a source positively, and 出处不明 to flag a source whose origin is unclear. These comments should be paired with a reason (who published it, whether it is an official body, whether other sources agree), not stated as a bare opinion.

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: A Short Research Note

据校报报道，今年校园图书馆的使用人数比去年增加了百分之十五。图书馆主任在采访中指出，这个变化可能与新的数字化服务政策有关。换句话说，学生现在更容易在网上预约座位和借阅电子书，这可能是使用人数上升的一个原因。这条消息的来源是校报的官方网站，属于比较可靠的参考资料。不过，网上也有一些说法出处不明，声称使用人数"翻了一倍"，这类数字没有明确的来源，需要谨慎对待，不宜直接引用。

  1. 这段话中，哪一句话是转述？哪一句话在评估来源是否可靠？
  2. 作者用了哪个词说明第一条消息的来源比较可靠？
  3. 作者为什么不直接引用"使用人数翻了一倍"这个说法？

口语练习 — Speaking Practice

Speaking Practice

交际任务 — Communicative task: 资料核对

Working with a partner, one person names a claim and a source; the other decides whether it sounds 可靠 or 出处不明 and explains why, using 据...报道/介绍 to restate the claim and 可靠, 权威, or 出处不明 to evaluate it.

独立练习 — Solo alternative

Record yourself presenting two short claims from different sources: one you consider reliable and one you consider unclear, explaining your reasoning for each.

阅读练习 — Reading Practice

Reading Practice: Comparing Two Sources

Read the two short excerpts below and answer the questions that follow.

来源一：大学官方网站
根据本校图书馆年度报告，今年图书馆的到馆人数比去年增加了百分之十五，电子资源的使用量也有明显上升。

来源二：学生论坛帖子（作者匿名）

听说图书馆人多了好多，具体多了多少不清楚，反正感觉现在很难找到座位。
  1. 这两个来源哪一个更权威？为什么？
  2. 来源二的说法可以直接引用为准确数据吗？为什么？
  3. 如果要写研究笔记，你会怎么处理来源二里的信息？
写作练习 — Writing Workshop

Writing Workshop: A Short Research Note

Write a short research note of one paragraph that draws on both sources above.

  1. Attribution: Introduce the official statistic using 据...报道 or 根据..., naming 来源一 specifically.
  2. Paraphrase: Restate the student-forum comment using 转述 or 概括, without quoting it directly.
  3. Reliability comment: Comment on which source is 可靠 or 权威 and which is less certain, giving a reason.
  4. Closing: Close with one sentence stating what, if anything, still needs a clearer 出处 before it can be used confidently.

写作检查清单 — Writing checklist:

  • At least one attribution frame (据...报道 / 根据...) names a specific source.
  • The forum comment is paraphrased, not quoted directly.
  • The note comments on reliability with a stated reason.
  • The note distinguishes 来源一 and 来源二 clearly rather than blending them.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Attribution norms vary by context

Expectations about citing sources, quoting directly, and evaluating reliability differ across academic institutions, publications, and professional settings, and these expectations continue to change as more communication happens online. A careful writer names a source specifically rather than relying on vague phrases like "some people say," and this expectation applies broadly across Mandarin-speaking academic and professional contexts, though exact citation formats vary by institution and field.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Paraphrasing too closely: a paraphrase that only swaps a few words is still too close to the original; restructure the sentence, not just the vocabulary.
  • Quoting without 引号: if you use the source's exact wording, mark it clearly rather than blending it silently into your own sentence.
  • Vague sourcing: avoid attributing a claim only to "网上" or "有人说" when a more specific source is available.
  • Treating 可靠 as a bare opinion: always pair a reliability comment with a stated reason.
  • Skipping 出处不明 flags: do not silently drop unclear claims; note that the source is unclear rather than omitting the issue entirely.
