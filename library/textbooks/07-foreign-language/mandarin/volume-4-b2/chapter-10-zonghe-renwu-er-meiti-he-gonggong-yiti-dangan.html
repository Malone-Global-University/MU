综合任务二：媒体和公共议题档案

Volume IV, Chapter 10: Unit II Integrated Assessment · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Mandarin

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

This chapter closes Unit II. It introduces no new grammar, no new high-burden character set, and no new pronunciation system. Instead, it asks you to bring together everything developed across Chapters 6 through 9 -- critical media reading, interview and public-discussion language, careful public-issue commentary, and rights-and-obligations language -- into one connected project: a short media dossier about the university's digital-services policy, followed by a plain-language public explanation of it. The task mirrors the kind of writing a student journalist, campus communications assistant, or informed member of the public might actually produce: something that reads a report, interprets a regulation, incorporates an interview, and explains the result responsibly to a general audience.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify the framing, source voice, and structure of a short media report (Chapter 6).
  2. Incorporate interview language, clarification, and personal stance into a written or spoken account (Chapter 7).
  3. Discuss a public issue using careful, hedged institutional vocabulary rather than overstated claims (Chapter 8).
  4. Identify the rights, obligations, and scope stated in a short regulation excerpt (Chapter 9).
  5. Combine all four systems into one coherent media dossier and plain-language explanation.
  6. Demonstrate repair strategies when vocabulary, characters, tones, or register choices are incomplete.

Communication Scenario

The student newspaper is finishing its coverage of the university's new digital-services policy. Your editor asks you to assemble the final dossier: a short report drawing on a published article, an interview excerpt, and the regulation text itself, followed by a plain-language explanation for students who have not been following the story. This is a synthesis task. You are not learning new language here -- you are demonstrating that you can move fluently among a media report, an interview, a regulation, and your own explanatory writing, the way a real dossier would require.

  1. You will listen to a short interview excerpt connected to the policy story.
  2. You will read a short media report and a short regulation excerpt side by side.
  3. You will conduct or record a short interview-style discussion about the policy.
  4. You will write a media dossier entry: a short report summary plus a plain-language explanation, with a supported personal stance.

听力评估 — Listening Assessment

Listening Assessment

Listening script for independent use. Read aloud slowly, then at a natural pace, or have two fluent speakers record it as an interview exchange.

校报采访片段 — Excerpt from a student-newspaper interview:
记者：您好，我想追问一下，学生对这项新的数字化服务政策反应怎么样？
学生代表：据统计，大部分学生对这项政策的态度是积极的，但也有学生提出，希望学校能进一步说明"符合条件"的具体标准。
记者：那这项政策对已经毕业的学生有影响吗？
学生代表：值得注意的是，这项政策的适用范围只包括在校注册学生，已经毕业的学生超出了这个范围，需要联系校友服务办公室。在一定程度上，这样的安排可能会导致一些毕业生感到不便，但我依我看，这是资源分配的合理做法。

阅读评估 — Reading Assessment

Reading Assessment: Report and Regulation Side by Side

Read the short report summary and the regulation excerpt below, then answer the questions that follow. Compare how each text frames the same policy differently.

校报报道摘要：
本报此前报道了学校新的数字化服务政策。据统计，这项政策自实施以来，受到了相关部门和学生的广泛关注。公众意见显示，多数学生支持这项政策，但也有部分学生对申请流程提出了疑问。值得注意的是，这项政策的资源分配方式在一定程度上优先考虑了在校注册学生。

第五条：学生的权利与义务（节录）

在校注册学生有权查看本人的申请记录，并有权就处理结果向相关办公室提出说明请求。学生有义务按时、如实提交申请所需的全部材料。本条规定的适用范围为在校注册学生，不适用于已毕业、已退学或休学期间的学生。
  1. 校报报道和规定原文在语气和详细程度上有什么不同？
  2. 校报报道中提到了哪些"公众意见"？
  3. 规定原文说明了学生的哪些权利和义务？
  4. 这两段材料的适用范围是否一致？你是怎么知道的？
口语评估 — Speaking Assessment

Speaking Assessment

交际任务 — Communicative task: 模拟采访

Working with a partner, one person plays a student journalist and the other plays a student representative or office representative. The journalist should ask at least one question using 我想追问一下, and the representative should answer using at least one hedge from Chapter 8 (在一定程度上 or 可能会导致), one rights-or-obligations term from Chapter 9 (有权, 义务, 范围, or 符合规定), and close with a personal stance using 我的看法是 or 依我看.

独立练习 — Solo alternative

Record a one-minute mock interview segment in which you play both roles, asking one question, giving one hedged answer, citing one right or obligation, and closing with your own stance.

写作评估 — Writing Assessment

Writing Assessment: Media Dossier Entry

Write a media dossier entry of two short paragraphs: the first summarizing the policy story as a brief report, the second explaining the regulation excerpt in plain language for a general reader, closing with your own supported stance.

  1. Paragraph 1 — Report summary: Summarize the policy story, citing at least one piece of information with 据统计 or 值得注意的是, and referring to 公众意见 or 相关 parties.
  2. Paragraph 1 — Hedge: Include at least one hedge with 在一定程度上 or 可能会导致 when describing an effect that is not fully certain.
  3. Paragraph 2 — Rights and obligations: Explain the regulation excerpt in plain language, stating at least one right with 有权 or 权利 and one obligation with 义务.
  4. Paragraph 2 — Scope: State the regulation's scope using 范围, including who is not included.
  5. Closing stance: Close with your own brief stance using 我的看法是 or 依我看, including a concession from Chapter 7 (不可否认...但是).

写作检查清单 — Writing checklist:

  • The report summary cites at least one piece of information carefully, not as an unqualified fact.
  • At least one hedge appears where an effect is uncertain.
  • At least one right and one obligation are stated accurately.
  • The regulation's scope is described, including any exceptions.
  • The entry closes with a supported stance that includes a concession.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Media coverage and institutional text as separate genres

A media report and a regulation text serve different purposes and audiences, and a responsible dossier should keep the two clearly distinguished rather than blending a journalist's framing with the regulation's own wording. This distinction, and the degree of overlap between media and institutional language, varies by institution, publication, and setting across Mainland, Taiwan, Hong Kong/Macau, Singapore, and diaspora contexts, and should not be treated as identical everywhere.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Blurring report and regulation language: keep the hedged, source-attributed style of the media summary distinct from the precise, obligation-stating style of the regulation explanation.
  • Dropping hedges under time pressure: in an integrated task it is tempting to state uncertain claims as fact; keep 在一定程度上 and 可能会导致 active wherever an effect is not settled.
  • Treating 义务 as optional or 有权 as a suggestion: these terms name firm obligations and entitlements, not tentative possibilities.
  • Omitting the personal stance or its concession: a dossier entry without a closing stance, or a stance without an acknowledged counterpoint, is incomplete for this task.
  • Producing 被 independently: continue to recognize passive-marked institutional and regulation text receptively; this chapter does not require independent production of the passive construction.
