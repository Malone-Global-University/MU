立场、论证和证据

Volume IV, Chapter 4: Position, Argument, and Evidence · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Mandarin

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

Chapter 3 taught you to classify, compare, and support a claim with a single figure. This chapter turns that comparison into a full argument. You will state a clear thesis, support it with more than one piece of evidence, acknowledge a reasonable objection through concession, and respond to that objection with a counterargument, before restating your position in a strengthened form. The professional and institutional vocabulary domain continues to expand, and the formal nominal groups and comparison structures from Chapters 2 and 3 remain fully active as the building blocks of the argument.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State a clear thesis or position using 我认为 and 立场.
  2. Support a thesis with more than one piece of evidence using 论点 and 证据.
  3. Concede a reasonable objection using 尽管, 虽然, and 不可否认.
  4. Respond to an objection with a counterargument using 但是, 然而, and 不过.
  5. Read a short opinion piece that states a position, offers evidence, concedes a point, and counters it.
  6. Write a short argumentative paragraph with a thesis, evidence, concession, and counterargument.

Communication Scenario

The international-student office from Chapters 2 and 3 has asked for student input before finalizing its recommendation between in-person and online applications. The student who compared the two methods in Chapter 3 has now been asked to write a short position piece: should the office recommend one method as the default for most students, while still allowing the other as an option? The student must take a clear position, support it with evidence, acknowledge the strongest objection, and explain why the position still holds.

  1. You will listen to a short student council discussion in which two speakers take different positions.
  2. You will read a short opinion piece that argues for a default recommendation.
  3. You will practice stating a position, conceding a point, and countering it aloud.
  4. You will write a short argumentative paragraph defending a recommendation.

发音 — Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

No new sound system is introduced in this chapter. As in Chapter 3, tone accuracy directly affects the clarity of the argument rather than only individual word meaning. Keep the falling tone steady and distinct across 立场 (lìchǎng) so it is not confused with similarly shaped words, give 不可否认 its full four-syllable weight as a discourse opener rather than rushing it, and keep the rising-falling contrast clear between 尽管 (jǐnguǎn) and words that begin with a similar initial. When reading a full argumentative paragraph aloud, pause briefly after each discourse opener (不可否认, 值得注意的是, 总的来说) to let it function as a signal to the listener rather than blending into the following clause.

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Thesis, Evidence, Concession, and Counterargument

This chapter develops four connected moves that together form a complete short argument: stating a thesis, supporting it with evidence, conceding a reasonable objection, and countering that objection before restating the position. These moves build directly on Chapter 3's classification and comparison structures, which continue to be useful for describing the options being argued about.

1. Stating a Thesis with 我认为 and 立场

Use 我认为 (I think/believe) to introduce an opinion, and 我的立场是 (my position is) for a more formal, explicit thesis statement appropriate to institutional writing.

我的立场是：国际学生办公室应该把网上申请设为默认方式，同时继续保留现场申请的选项。

2. Supporting the Thesis with Evidence

Introduce evidence with 论点 (argument point) and 证据 (evidence), and connect a piece of evidence explicitly to the thesis using 这说明 (this shows/indicates) or 这支持了...的观点 (this supports the view that...). A strong short argument usually offers more than one piece of evidence rather than repeating the same point.

PatternFunctionExample
第一个论点是...introduce a first argument point第一个论点是：网上申请的效率比现场申请高。
根据...，...这说明...cite evidence and state what it shows根据去年的数据，网上申请的比例超过一半，这说明大多数学生更倾向于这种方式。
此外，...add a second, distinct argument point此外，网上申请也减少了办公室的排队压力。

3. Conceding a Point with 尽管, 虽然, and 不可否认

A convincing position acknowledges its strongest objection rather than ignoring it. Use 不可否认 (it cannot be denied) to open a concession, and 尽管... or 虽然... to frame the objection as a subordinate clause before the main claim.

不可否认，材料复杂的申请人在网上申请时可能遇到困难。尽管如此，这个问题并不能否定网上申请对大多数学生的价值。

4. Countering the Objection and Restating the Position

After the concession, use 但是, 然而, or 不过 to return to your position, and explain why the objection does not outweigh the evidence already presented. Close with 总的来说 to restate the thesis in a strengthened form.

然而，这类申请人仍然可以选择现场申请，问题因此可以得到解决。总的来说，把网上申请设为默认方式既符合大多数学生的需要，也不会让少数申请人失去选择。

Putting It Together

A complete short argument should: (1) state a clear thesis, (2) support it with at least two distinct pieces of evidence, (3) concede the strongest reasonable objection, (4) counter that objection without dismissing it, and (5) restate the thesis in a strengthened form.

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: 网上申请应该成为默认方式吗？ — Should Online Applications Become the Default?

我的立场是：国际学生办公室应该把网上申请设为默认方式，同时继续保留现场申请的选项，供有需要的学生选择。

第一个论点是效率。正如上一章提到的，网上申请的效率比现场申请高，申请人不需要到窗口排队，也不需要占用工作人员大量的时间。第二个论点是学生的实际选择：根据办公室去年的数据，选择网上申请的学生比例超过一半，这说明大多数学生已经倾向于这种方式，而不是仅仅出于方便才勉强使用。此外，网上申请也减少了办公室在申请高峰期的排队压力，这对办公室和学生双方都有好处。

不可否认，材料情况比较复杂的申请人在使用网上系统时可能遇到困难，因为遇到问题不能马上得到工作人员的帮助。这确实是网上申请的一个缺点，也是许多学生仍然选择现场申请的主要原因。尽管如此，这个问题并不能否定网上申请对大多数学生的价值：材料复杂的申请人依然可以选择现场申请，问题因此可以得到解决，并不会因为默认方式的改变而受到影响。

总的来说，把网上申请设为默认方式，既符合大多数学生的实际需要和效率考虑，也通过保留现场申请的选项，照顾到了少数材料复杂的申请人。这是一个既支持数据、也考虑到不同申请人情况的合理立场。

阅读练习 — Reading Component

Reading Component: An Opinion Piece

Read the short opinion piece below, written by a student for the office's internal discussion, and answer the questions that follow. The term 前提 (premise) appears here as receptive vocabulary for learners who want to analyze the argument's structure.

学生意见：关于默认申请方式的建议

我支持将网上申请设为默认方式，这一立场基于两个前提：效率和学生的实际选择。第一，网上申请的效率明显更高；第二，去年的数据显示，超过一半的学生已经主动选择了这种方式。值得注意的是，反对这一建议的学生主要担心材料复杂的申请人会受到影响。这个担心不可否认是合理的。然而，只要现场申请的选项继续保留，这部分学生的需要依然可以得到满足。因此，把网上申请设为默认方式，并不意味着取消现场申请，而是根据大多数学生的实际情况调整优先顺序。总的来说，这一建议既有数据支持，也考虑到了不同申请人的情况。

口语练习 — Speaking Component

Speaking Component

交际任务 — Communicative task: 表达立场并反驳

Working with a partner, choose a policy question related to an institutional procedure (should a default option be changed, should a fee be added, should a deadline be shortened). One partner states a thesis and evidence; the other raises the strongest objection. The first partner must concede the objection using 不可否认 and then counter it using 然而 or 不过, before restating the position with 总的来说.

独立练习 — Solo alternative

Record yourself stating a position on a real or invented institutional question, offering two pieces of evidence, conceding the strongest objection you can think of, and countering it before restating your position.

写作练习 — Writing Component

Writing Component: A Short Argumentative Paragraph

Complete the following writing task, applying the thesis, evidence, concession, and counterargument patterns introduced in this chapter.

  1. Choose a position on an institutional question you know or can reasonably invent.
  2. List two pieces of evidence and the strongest objection to your position, informally in English or Mandarin as a planning note.
  3. Write a paragraph of seven to nine sentences that states a thesis with 我的立场是, supports it with two distinct pieces of evidence, concedes the strongest objection with 不可否认, counters it with 然而 or 不过, and restates the thesis with 总的来说.
  4. Check your draft against the checklist below before considering the task complete.

写作检查清单 — Writing checklist:

  • The thesis is stated clearly and explicitly using 我的立场是 or 我认为.
  • At least two distinct pieces of evidence support the thesis.
  • A reasonable objection is conceded explicitly using 不可否认 or 尽管.
  • The objection is countered rather than ignored or simply repeated.
  • The paragraph closes by restating the thesis in a strengthened form using 总的来说.
  • The register remains formal, matching the institutional context.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Conceding a point in institutional argument

In many Mandarin-speaking institutional and academic settings, openly acknowledging a reasonable objection before countering it — rather than arguing as if no objection exists — is generally seen as a sign of a careful and credible position, not a weakness. Phrases such as 不可否认 and 尽管如此 allow a writer or speaker to appear balanced while still maintaining a clear stance. This pattern is common in institutional writing generally and should not be treated as a fixed rule for every register or every writer.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Stating a thesis without evidence: 我的立场是... alone is incomplete without at least one clearly connected piece of supporting evidence.
  • Conceding a point and abandoning the thesis: 不可否认 should be followed by a counterargument, not left as the final word on the topic.
  • Countering without acknowledging the objection first: jumping straight to 然而 without a genuine concession can make the argument sound dismissive rather than balanced.
  • Repeating the same evidence twice: 此外 should introduce a genuinely distinct argument point, not restate the first one in different words.

Chapter Summary

This chapter developed thesis statements with 我的立场是, evidence structures with 论点 and 证据, concession with 不可否认 and 尽管, and counterargument with 然而 and 不过, closing with a restated thesis using 总的来说. Building on Chapter 3's classification and comparison tools, these structures allow learners to move from comparing two options to defending a clear recommendation between them. These skills prepare learners for the Unit I assessment in Chapter 5, which will require coordinating classification, comparison, and full argumentation in one integrated task.
