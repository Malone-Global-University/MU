高级比较和分析

Volume IV, Chapter 3: Advanced Comparison and Analysis · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Mandarin

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

This chapter develops the language needed to classify options into categories, state contrasts precisely, and connect a claim to supporting data — skills that extend naturally from Chapter 2's institutional procedures into comparing procedures, services, or policies against one another. Building on the formal nominal groups introduced in Chapter 2, this chapter adds classification structures (分为, 包括), a fuller set of comparison patterns (比, 不如, 相比之下), and the ability to cite a simple figure or statistic as support for a claim. The professional and institutional vocabulary domain continues to develop toward independent use.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Classify a set of options, services, or procedures into named categories.
  2. Compare two or more options using 比, 不如, and 比较.
  3. Mark a contrast explicitly using 然而, 不过, and 相比之下.
  4. Support a comparison with a simple figure, percentage, or processing time.
  5. Read a short comparative report describing two institutional options.
  6. Write a short paragraph that classifies, compares, and draws a conclusion from data.

Communication Scenario

The learner from Chapter 2 is now comparing two ways to submit the residence-permit application described earlier: applying in person at the counter, or applying through the office's newer online appointment system. A university international-student office has published a short comparison of the two options, including approximate processing times and satisfaction figures, to help students decide which method to use.

  1. You will listen to a conversation in which two students compare their experiences with the two application methods.
  2. You will read a short comparative report published by the international-student office.
  3. You will practice classifying and comparing procedures aloud.
  4. You will write a paragraph comparing two options and recommending one, supported by data from the reading.

发音 — Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

No new sound system is introduced in this chapter. Comparison and classification vocabulary includes several words where a tone slip changes the meaning of the comparison itself, so accuracy matters here in a way that affects the argument, not just the word. Distinguish 效率 (xiàolǜ, efficiency) clearly from similarly shaped words, keep the falling-falling contour steady across 不如 (bùrú), and keep the four-syllable phrase 相比之下 evenly paced rather than rushed, since it functions as a full discourse connector.

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Classifying, Comparing, and Supporting a Claim

This chapter develops three connected skills: sorting options into named categories, comparing them with a fuller set of comparison structures than earlier volumes required, and pointing to a simple figure or statistic to support a claim. All three build directly on Chapter 2's formal nominal groups, which continue to be useful for naming the categories and options being compared.

1. Classification with 分为 and 包括

Use 分为 (to be divided into) to introduce a set of categories, and 包括 (to include) to list what belongs to a category. This gives a comparison a clear structure before the actual comparing begins.

申请方式主要分为两种：现场申请和网上申请。现场申请包括排队、提交材料、等待审核这几个步骤。

2. Comparison with 比, 不如, and 比较

Use A 比 B + adjective for a direct comparison, A 不如 B when A is less good than B in some respect, and 比较 before an adjective for a softer, relative judgment rather than a direct comparison.

PatternFunctionExample
A 比 B + 形容词direct comparison网上申请比现场申请方便。
A 不如 B (+ 形容词)A is inferior to B in this respect现场申请不如网上申请方便。
比较 + 形容词relative judgment, not a direct A-B comparison这种方式比较适合时间紧张的人。

Note that 比 and 不如 both compare two named things directly, while 比较 offers a softer evaluation on its own. Choosing among them affects how strong the comparison sounds.

3. Marking Contrast with 然而, 不过, and 相比之下

Use 然而 (however) and 不过 (but; however, slightly softer) to introduce a contrasting point within a paragraph, and 相比之下 (by comparison) to shift explicitly from one option to the other.

网上申请比较方便。然而，材料复杂的申请人可能需要现场工作人员的帮助。相比之下，现场申请虽然比较慢，但是更适合这类情况。

4. Supporting a Claim with a Figure

Use 根据 + source to introduce a figure, and 比例 or a percentage to state it precisely. A single supporting figure, stated once and connected clearly to the claim, is more persuasive than a vague general impression.

根据这份数据，选择网上申请的学生比例超过一半，这说明大部分学生认为网上申请更有效率。

Putting It Together

A clear comparative paragraph should: (1) classify the options into named categories, (2) compare them using an appropriately direct or relative comparison pattern, (3) mark any contrast explicitly, and (4) support at least one claim with a figure rather than only a general impression.

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: 两种申请方式的比较 — Comparing Two Application Methods

国际学生办公室为大家整理了以下比较，希望能帮助大家选择更适合自己的申请方式。

申请居留许可延期主要分为两种方式：现场申请和网上申请。现场申请包括排队、提交材料、等待工作人员审核这几个步骤，优点是可以当面咨询问题，缺点是需要花费较多时间，尤其是在申请高峰期。网上申请的效率比现场申请高，申请人可以在网上系统填写申请表并上传材料，不需要到窗口排队。相比之下，网上申请更适合时间紧张、材料齐全的申请人；然而，材料情况比较复杂的申请人，网上申请不如现场申请方便，因为遇到问题时不能马上得到工作人员的帮助。根据办公室去年的数据，选择网上申请的学生比例超过一半，这说明大部分学生认为网上申请比较有效率。不过，办公室仍然建议材料复杂的申请人选择现场申请，以便及时解决问题。

Reading Component: A Comparative Report

Read the short report below, published by a university international-student office, and answer the questions that follow. The terms 满意度 (satisfaction rate) and 总体而言 (overall; on the whole) appear here as receptive vocabulary.

申请方式满意度调查简报

国际学生办公室对上一学期申请居留许可延期的学生进行了简单调查。结果显示，选择网上申请的学生比例约为百分之六十，选择现场申请的学生比例约为百分之四十。在网上申请的学生中，满意度较高，主要原因是节省时间、不需要排队；在现场申请的学生中，满意度也不低，主要原因是材料问题可以当面解决。总体而言，两种申请方式各有优点，办公室建议材料齐全、时间紧张的学生优先考虑网上申请，材料情况比较复杂的学生优先考虑现场申请。

Speaking Component

交际任务 — Communicative task: 比较两种选择

Working with a partner, choose two real or invented options related to an institutional procedure (two visa types, two banking services, two ways to register for a class). Classify the options, compare them using 比/不如/比较, mark at least one contrast with 然而 or 相比之下, and support one claim with a made-up but plausible figure.

写作练习 — Writing Component

Writing Component: A Comparative Paragraph

Complete the following writing task, applying the classification, comparison, contrast, and data-support patterns introduced in this chapter.

  1. Choose two options related to an institutional procedure you know or can reasonably invent.
  2. List each option's advantages and disadvantages informally in English or Mandarin as a planning note.
  3. Write a paragraph of six to eight sentences that classifies the options with 分为, compares them using 比 or 不如, marks at least one contrast with 然而 or 相比之下, and supports one claim with a figure using 根据 and 比例 or a percentage.
  4. Check your draft against the checklist below before considering the task complete.

写作检查清单 — Writing checklist:

  • The options are classified into named categories using 分为 or 包括.
  • At least one direct comparison is made using 比 or 不如.
  • At least one contrast is marked explicitly with 然而, 不过, or 相比之下.
  • At least one claim is supported with a figure introduced by 根据.
  • The register remains formal, matching the institutional context.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Comparative reports in institutional communication

Institutions in Mandarin-speaking contexts commonly publish short comparative summaries — often with a simple figure or percentage — to help the public choose among available options, whether for application methods, service tiers, or program choices. Presenting a balanced comparison that names advantages and disadvantages on both sides, rather than favoring one option outright, is generally seen as appropriately neutral for an institutional voice. The specific figures and options in this chapter are illustrative and should not be read as data from any real institution.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing 比较 with 比: 比较 alone offers a relative judgment, while 比 requires two named things being compared directly; using them interchangeably can make a comparison unclear or ungrammatical.
  • Overusing 不如 for simple contrasts: 不如 specifically means one option is worse than another in some respect; use 相比之下 or 然而 instead when you only want to note a difference, not rank the options.
  • Introducing a figure without connecting it to a claim: a number alone (选择网上申请的比例是百分之六十) is incomplete without a sentence explaining what it shows.
  • Classifying without naming the categories: simply listing options is weaker than explicitly using 分为 to introduce the categories before comparing them.

Chapter Summary

This chapter developed classification with 分为 and 包括, a fuller set of comparison patterns (比, 不如, 比较), contrast markers (然而, 不过, 相比之下), and the ability to support a claim with a simple figure introduced by 根据. Building on Chapter 2's formal nominal groups, these tools allow learners to compare institutional options precisely and back up a recommendation with evidence rather than only a general impression. These skills support the argumentation and evidence work developed further in Chapter 4 and across this unit.
