批判性阅读媒体

Volume IV, Chapter 6: Critical Media Reading · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Mandarin

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

Unit I ended with a single coordinated formal memo built from classification, comparison, and argument. Unit II opens by turning that same analytical attention toward texts you did not write: news headlines, short reports, and other people's arguments. This chapter introduces critical media reading -- noticing how a headline frames a story, recognizing whose voice is speaking in a report, and identifying the basic shape a news article typically follows. The register, comparison, and argumentation systems from Unit I remain fully active; this chapter adds the skill of reading someone else's formal text with a critical eye rather than only producing your own.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify the basic structure of a short news report: headline, lead, body, and closing context.
  2. Recognize framing choices in a headline and explain how a different headline could change a reader's impression.
  3. Identify whose voice is speaking in a report using source-marking expressions such as 据报道 and 根据.
  4. Distinguish a reporter's own statement from a quoted or attributed statement.
  5. Summarize the main point of a short report in your own words, in formal register.
  6. Discuss a short report orally, commenting on its framing and sourcing.

Communication Scenario

The campus newspaper has published a short report on the same application-method question the international-student office has been discussing since Chapter 2. A student reading group meets weekly to read one short report together and discuss how it is written, not only what it says. This week's report covers the office's new recommendation. Before the group can discuss the content, they first want to understand how the report is put together: what the headline emphasizes, whose voice appears in the article, and how the report is organized.

  1. You will listen to two students discussing a headline before they have read the full article.
  2. You will read a short campus-newspaper report and identify its structure and sources.
  3. You will practice discussing a headline's framing and a report's sourcing with a partner.
  4. You will write a short formal summary of a report's main point, structure, and sources.

发音 — Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

This chapter introduces no new sound system. It develops formal reading-aloud within a new genre: the short news report. Headlines are typically read with slightly more weight and a clear pause before the body of the article begins. Practice the falling tone on 报道 (bàodào) and the even, unhurried pacing of 据报道 as a discourse opener, distinct from the way 不可否认 and 总的来说 were paced in Chapter 5. Maintain clear tone contrast between 引用 (yǐnyòng) and 来源 (láiyuán), both fourth-tone-initial words that beginners sometimes flatten.

这个标题强调了效率，而不是公平性。clear stress on 强调 without over-lengthening the sentence

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Reading Structure, Framing, and Source Voice

This chapter's productive focus is reading strategy expressed through language, not a new grammar system. Three patterns support this: marking a source explicitly, describing framing, and distinguishing a reporter's voice from a quoted voice. The comparison structures from Chapter 3 and the position language from Chapter 4 remain active and are reused here to discuss someone else's writing rather than your own.

1. Marking a Source: 据报道 / 根据

Use 据报道 to mark that a claim comes from reporting rather than from your own observation, and 根据 to name a specific source.

据报道，办公室已经决定把网上申请设为默认方式。根据这篇报道，这个决定是根据去年的数据做出的。

2. Describing Framing: 强调 / 突出

Use 强调 to describe what a headline or report emphasizes, and compare it with what it leaves out.

这个标题强调了效率的提高，但没有提到材料复杂的学生可能遇到的困难。

3. Distinguishing Voices: 记者认为 vs. 引用

Distinguish the reporter's own framing from a quoted source's words using 记者认为 or neutral reporting verbs versus 引用了...的话.

这篇报道引用了一位办公室代表的话，但记者本人并没有直接表明自己的立场。

4. Summarizing in Formal Register

Combine these patterns with the comparison and position language from Units I to produce a formal one- or two-sentence summary.

总的来说，这篇报道的标题强调效率，引用了办公室代表的话，立场比较中立，但对反对意见着墨不多。

Putting It Together

Reading a report critically means identifying its structure (headline, lead, body, context), naming its sources, describing what it emphasizes, and separating the reporter's voice from quoted voices -- then summarizing all of this in formal register, using comparison and evaluative language already consolidated in Unit I.

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: 校报报道 — 办公室新建议引起讨论 — Campus Report: New Office Recommendation Sparks Discussion

标题：网上申请成为默认方式，部分学生表示担忧

据报道，国际学生办公室日前宣布，将把网上申请设为默认的申请方式，现场申请仍作为选项保留。办公室在声明中表示，这项决定是根据去年的数据做出的：网上申请的处理时间更短，选择这种方式的学生比例也更高。

本报记者采访了几位学生。一位学生表示："网上申请确实更方便，我一直都是这样申请的。"另一位材料情况比较复杂的学生则表示担忧："如果只能选网上申请，我担心遇到问题没有人可以马上帮忙。"办公室代表回应说，现场申请的选项仍然保留，材料复杂的学生可以继续选择这种方式。

这篇报道并未对这项决定做出明确评价，但标题突出了"部分学生表示担忧"这一点，而对多数学生支持网上申请的数据着墨较少。

练习 — Guided Practice

Guided Practice

  1. Mark a source: 据报道，______。
  2. Name a source: 根据______，______。
  3. Describe framing: 这个标题强调了______，但没有提到______。
  4. Distinguish voices: 这篇报道引用了______的话，但记者本人______。
  5. Summarize: 总的来说，这篇报道______，立场______。

Listening Component

Listening script for independent use. Read aloud slowly, then at a natural pace, or have a fluent speaker record it.

阅读小组讨论 — Two students discussing a headline before reading the full article:
小李：你看这个标题，"网上申请成为默认方式，部分学生表示担忧"。你觉得这个标题给你什么印象？

小李：对，这个标题强调了担忧，但没有提到支持这个决定的数据。我们看看正文里是怎么写的。
小王：好，读完以后我们再比较一下标题和内容是不是一致。

阅读练习 — Reading Component

Reading Component: Comparing Two Headlines

Read the second version of the same report, published by a different section of the campus newspaper, and compare it with the model text above.

标题：办公室根据数据推出网上申请新政策，多数学生表示支持

据报道，国际学生办公室宣布将网上申请设为默认方式，现场申请继续作为选项保留。办公室表示，这一决定基于去年的数据：网上申请处理时间更短，选择这一方式的学生比例也更高。

校报采访的多位学生对这一决定表示支持，认为这能提高效率。也有个别学生提到，材料复杂的情况下更希望有工作人员的帮助，但办公室已表示会继续保留现场申请的选项，以照顾这部分学生。
  1. 这两个标题分别强调了什么？
  2. 两篇报道引用的内容有什么不同？
  3. 哪一篇报道的立场看起来更中立？为什么？
  4. 如果只读标题，读者对这个决定会有什么不同的印象？
口语练习 — Speaking Component

Speaking Component

交际任务 — Communicative task: 阅读小组讨论

Working with a partner, discuss the two headlines above before reading further, predicting what each report will emphasize. Then discuss the full reports: identify the sources, compare the framing, and decide together which report seems more neutral and why.

独立练习 — Solo alternative

Record yourself comparing the two headlines and reports: describe what each emphasizes, name the sources each report uses, and give your own assessment of which report is more neutral, using 立场中立 and 强调.

写作练习 — Writing Component

Writing Component: A Short Formal Summary

Write a short formal summary of one of the two reports above (or a report you find elsewhere and can summarize responsibly in your own words).

  1. Identify the structure of the report: headline, lead, body, and closing context.
  2. Name the sources using 据报道 or 根据.
  3. Describe the framing of the headline using 强调, comparing it briefly with the body of the article.
  4. Distinguish the reporter's own statements from any quoted material using 引用.
  5. Give a brief assessment of whether the report seems 立场中立, supporting your assessment with one specific example from the text.
  6. Close with a one-sentence formal summary using 总的来说.

写作检查清单 — Writing checklist:

  • The summary identifies the report's basic structure.
  • At least one source is explicitly marked using 据报道 or 根据.
  • The headline's framing is described and briefly compared with the body.
  • Quoted material is distinguished from the reporter's own statements.
  • The summary gives a supported assessment of neutrality, not just an opinion.
  • The summary closes with 总的来说 in consistent formal register.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Reading headlines critically across media environments

Headline conventions and framing practices vary across Mandarin-language media environments, including Mainland outlets, Taiwan outlets, Hong Kong and Macau outlets, Singapore outlets, and diaspora publications, as well as across institutional, campus, and independent media within each of these. A single report's framing should not be treated as representative of an entire media environment, a region, or a political position. This chapter's skill -- identifying structure, sourcing, and framing -- applies across these contexts, but specific conventions and norms should be labeled rather than generalized.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Treating a headline as a full summary: a headline frames a story; it does not always represent the entire content of the report.
  • Confusing the reporter's voice with a quoted voice: always mark which words are attributed to a source using 引用, rather than presenting a quotation as the reporter's own claim.
  • Assuming neutrality without evidence: a claim of 立场中立 should be supported with a specific observation about sourcing or framing, not asserted alone.
  • Introducing new grammar or vocabulary beyond this chapter: this chapter checks reading strategy expressed through the source-marking and framing patterns taught here, combined with previously consolidated Unit I systems.

Chapter Summary

This chapter introduced critical media reading: identifying a short report's structure, marking its sources with 据报道 and 根据, describing headline framing with 强调, and distinguishing a reporter's voice from quoted voices with 引用. These patterns were combined with the comparison and position language consolidated in Unit I to produce a formal summary and assessment of a report's neutrality. This opens Unit II, which continues with interviews and public discussion in Chapter 7.

Answer Key

Guided Practice

  1. 据报道，[the claim]。
  2. 根据[the source]，[the claim]。
  3. 这个标题强调了[what is emphasized]，但没有提到[what is left out]。
  4. 这篇报道引用了[the quoted party]的话，但记者本人[the reporter's own stance or lack thereof]。
  5. 总的来说，这篇报道[main structural/sourcing observation]，立场[assessment]。

Listening Component

  • 小李觉得这个标题强调了学生的担忧。
  • 小王认为"部分学生"这个说法可能只代表少数人，容易给读者错误的印象。
  • 两人打算读完正文后，比较标题和正文的内容是否一致。

Reading Component

  1. 第一个标题强调学生的担忧；第二个标题强调数据支持和多数学生的支持。
  2. 第一篇报道引用了一位担忧的学生和办公室代表的回应；第二篇报道引用了多位表示支持的学生，也提到了个别学生的担忧。
  3. 第二篇报道的立场看起来更中立，因为它同时呈现了支持和担忧的声音，并且引用了多位学生的意见。
  4. 如果只读标题，读者可能会认为第一篇报道中大多数学生反对这个决定，而实际上正文显示情况更复杂。
