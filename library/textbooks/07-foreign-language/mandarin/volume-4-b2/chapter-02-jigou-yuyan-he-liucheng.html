机构语言和流程

Volume IV, Chapter 2: Institutional Language and Procedures · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Mandarin

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

This chapter introduces the vocabulary and structures learners need to describe, request information about, and follow administrative procedures in Standard Mandarin — the kind of language found in university registrar offices, visa and residency permit offices, banks, and other institutions a learner might deal with while living or studying in a Mandarin-speaking environment. The chapter opens the professional and institutional vocabulary domain for the first time in productive form, and begins developing formal nominal groups — noun phrases dense enough to name a procedure, a requirement, or a document precisely. Building directly on Chapter 1's register work, this chapter also continues to develop attention to appropriately formal wording, now applied to describing steps, requirements, and regulations rather than personal requests.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe the steps of a common administrative procedure in order.
  2. State what documents or materials a procedure requires, using appropriate formal vocabulary.
  3. Ask an institutional employee about a procedure's requirements, timeline, and conditions.
  4. Read a set of posted instructions or a notice describing an application process.
  5. Use formal nominal groups to name procedures, requirements, and documents precisely.
  6. Write a short set of instructions explaining how to complete a procedure.

Communication Scenario

A learner living abroad in a Mandarin-speaking city needs to renew a residence permit at the local public security bureau. Before going in person, the learner calls the office to ask what materials are required, how long the process takes, and what the fee is. At the office, a clerk explains the steps and hands over a printed notice listing the requirements.

  1. You will listen to a phone call between the learner and an office clerk.
  2. You will read a printed notice listing application requirements.
  3. You will practice describing procedure steps in order.
  4. You will write a short set of instructions for completing an administrative procedure.

发音 — Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

No new sound system is introduced in this chapter. Institutional vocabulary introduces several multisyllabic compounds that benefit from careful tone control, since a tone error can change a word's meaning in a way that matters for accuracy in an official context. Pay particular attention to the two third-tone syllables in a row in 手续 (shǒuxù), where the first syllable shifts toward a rising pronunciation before the second, and to keeping 材料 (cáiliào) and 审核 (shěnhé) clearly distinct from similar-sounding words.

PhrasePractice Focus
换护照的手续不太复杂。third-tone sandhi across 手续; even pacing on the full noun phrase
材料审核大概需要五个工作日。clear tone contrast between 材料 (cáiliào) and 材 alone; steady rhythm through the number phrase

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Sequencing Procedures and Naming Requirements

This chapter develops two connected skills: describing a procedure's steps in a clear order, and naming requirements and documents precisely using formal nominal groups. Both draw on grammar systems already consolidated in earlier volumes — sequencing connectors, modal verbs of obligation, and noun modification with 的 — now applied with institutional vocabulary and formal register.

1. Sequencing Steps with 首先...然后...最后

Use 首先 (first), 然后 (then), 接着 (next), and 最后 (finally) to lay out a procedure's steps clearly, rather than stringing steps together with 和 or leaving the order to be inferred.

首先，您需要在网上填写申请表；然后，准备好所有材料；最后，到窗口提交材料并等待审核。

2. Stating Requirements with 需要 / 必须 / 应该

Use 需要 for what is needed, 必须 for a strict requirement, and 应该 for a recommended step. Choosing among these three verbs signals how strictly a rule applies, which matters when describing regulations accurately.

VerbStrengthExample
需要needed; practically necessary申请人需要提供一份在读证明。
必须strict requirement; no exception材料必须在有效期内提交。
应该recommended; advisable您应该提前一周预约。

3. Formal Nominal Groups for Naming Procedures and Documents

A formal nominal group packs a modifier, often built with 的, tightly in front of a noun so that a procedure, requirement, or document can be named precisely in a single noun phrase, rather than explained across several sentences.

Loosely statedFormal nominal group
这份证明是在读的时候开的。在读证明
这是需要在有效期以内提交的材料。有效期内提交的材料
这是审核申请材料的工作人员。审核申请材料的工作人员

Formal nominal groups are introduced here at a controlled level: a single modifying clause before a head noun. Longer, stacked modifiers are developed in later chapters and are not expected productively yet.

Putting It Together

A clear procedural description should: (1) sequence the steps explicitly, (2) state each requirement with an appropriately strong verb, and (3) name key documents and roles using formal nominal groups rather than long explanatory clauses.

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: 换居留许可的说明 — Instructions for Renewing a Residence Permit

各位申请人：

申请延长居留许可，请按照以下步骤办理。首先，请在网上系统填写申请表，并上传护照复印件和一张近期照片。然后，请准备在读证明或工作证明，以及能够证明住址的材料。所有材料必须在居留许可到期前三十天以内提交，逾期申请恕不受理。材料提交以后，工作人员会进行审核，审核时间一般为五个工作日。审核通过以后，请携带原件到三号窗口领取新的居留许可。如果材料不齐全或者不符合条件，工作人员会电话通知您补交材料。

阅读练习 — Reading Component

Reading Component: A Posted Notice

Read the notice below, posted at the entrance of an administrative office, and answer the questions that follow. The term 工作日 (business day) and 受理 (to accept and process, a more formal near-synonym of 办理) appear here as receptive vocabulary.

办理须知

一、申请人须提前预约，未预约者恕不受理。
二、请携带以下材料：护照原件及复印件、近期照片一张、在读证明或工作证明、住址证明。
三、材料必须齐全并在有效期以内，材料不齐全者需补交后方可继续办理。
四、审核时间一般为五个工作日，审核结果将以电话或短信通知申请人。
五、办理时间：周一至周五，上午九点至下午四点，法定节假日除外。
  1. 申请人在办理手续之前需要做什么？
  2. 如果材料不齐全，申请人应该怎么办？
  3. 工作人员会用什么方式通知申请人审核结果？
  4. 这份通知使用了哪些词语或结构表示这是正式的官方文件？

写作练习 — Writing Component

Writing Component: A Set of Instructions

Complete the following writing task, applying the sequencing, requirement, and nominal-group patterns introduced in this chapter.

  1. Choose a procedure you know well (renewing an ID, registering for a class, applying for a library card, or a similar real or invented process).
  2. List the steps informally in English or Mandarin as a planning note.
  3. Write a formal set of instructions of five to seven sentences, using 首先/然后/最后 to sequence steps, at least one of 需要/必须/应该 to state a requirement, and at least one formal nominal group to name a document or role.
  4. Check your draft against the checklist below before considering the task complete.

写作检查清单 — Writing checklist:

  • Steps are presented in a clear order using sequencing connectors.
  • At least one requirement is stated with an appropriately strong verb (需要, 必须, or 应该).
  • At least one document or role is named using a formal nominal group rather than a full explanatory clause.
  • The register remains formal throughout, with no casual sentence-final particles.
  • Characters, punctuation, and measure words are checked for accuracy.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Institutional procedure and written notices

Administrative offices in Mandarin-speaking contexts commonly rely on posted written notices, numbered lists of requirements, and printed forms to communicate procedures clearly and consistently to many applicants at once. Phoning ahead or checking a posted notice before visiting in person is generally seen as a practical way to save time rather than as an imposition, though the degree to which offices favor phone inquiries, in-person visits, or online systems varies by institution, region, and over time. Learners should treat any specific institution's requirements described in this chapter as illustrative rather than as a guarantee of what a real office currently requires.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Treating 需要, 必须, and 应该 as interchangeable: using 应该 for a strict legal requirement understates its force; use 必须 when a rule allows no exception.
  • Listing steps without sequencing connectors: simply stringing clauses together can leave the order unclear; use 首先/然后/最后 or 接着 to make sequence explicit.
  • Over-explaining instead of using a nominal group: a full clause such as 这是需要在有效期以内提交的材料 is correct but wordy where a tighter phrase like 有效期内提交的材料 reads as more formal and precise.
  • Dropping formal register when stating regulations: regulation language should avoid casual particles and vague phrasing, even when the underlying content is simple.

Chapter Summary

This chapter opened the professional and institutional vocabulary domain and introduced formal nominal groups as a tool for naming procedures, requirements, and documents precisely. You practiced sequencing procedural steps with 首先/然后/最后, stating requirements with appropriately strong verbs (需要, 必须, 应该), and packing modifiers into tight noun phrases rather than long explanatory clauses. These skills support the institutional, legal, and administrative language developed further later in this unit and across Volume IV.
