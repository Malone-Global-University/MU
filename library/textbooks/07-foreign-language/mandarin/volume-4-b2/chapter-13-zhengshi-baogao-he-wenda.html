正式报告和问答

Volume IV, Chapter 13: Formal Presentation and Q&A · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Mandarin

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

Chapter 12 taught you how to organize a written argument into a claim, supporting points, and a conclusion, and how to compress information into formal nominal groups. This chapter takes that same organizational shape into spoken, prepared formal Mandarin: giving a short presentation and handling questions afterward. No new sentence-level grammar is introduced here. Instead, you will learn the delivery conventions of a prepared talk -- opening framing, signposting through the body, closing a presentation clearly -- and the language patterns for restating a question, buying a moment to think, and answering directly or with appropriate hedging.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify and use core vocabulary for presentation structure and question-and-answer exchanges.
  2. Open a presentation with a framing statement and close it with a clear summary.
  3. Use spoken signposting language to move an audience through a prepared talk.
  4. Restate a question before answering it, and use hedging language when an answer is uncertain or partial.
  5. Deliver a short prepared presentation based on the academic paragraph developed in Chapter 12.
  6. Respond to follow-up questions with organized, register-appropriate spoken answers.

Communication Scenario

Your classmate's academic paragraph on library usage from Chapter 12 has been selected for a short in-class presentation. The assignment asks for a two-to-three-minute prepared talk covering the same claim and supporting points, followed by a question-and-answer period with classmates. Your task is to help turn the written paragraph into a spoken presentation and to prepare for the kinds of questions an audience might ask.

  1. You will listen to a short recorded presentation opening and closing and identify their framing language.
  2. You will read a transcript of a presentation Q&A exchange and identify how the speaker restates and hedges.
  3. You will practice converting written paragraph structure into spoken signposting.
  4. You will deliver a short presentation and respond to at least two follow-up questions.

语法和篇章 — Grammar and Discourse Focus

Grammar and Discourse Focus: Presentation Language and Q&A

This chapter introduces no new sentence-level grammar. Instead, it develops spoken delivery conventions built from vocabulary and discourse tools you already control, including the 首先/其次/此外/总的来说 markers from Chapter 11 and Chapter 12.

Opening and closing a presentation

A prepared talk opens by naming the topic and previewing its shape, and closes by signaling that the talk is finished. Compare the written paragraph opening from Chapter 12 with a spoken presentation opening:

  • 书面开头：今年校园图书馆使用人数明显增加，这一变化很可能与图书馆近年推行的数字化服务政策有关。 — written claim
  • 口头开头：大家好，今天我要报告的题目是图书馆使用人数的变化。简单来说，我认为这个变化跟数字化服务政策有关。 — spoken opening with greeting and preview

A spoken closing restates the claim briefly and signals the end of the talk, typically with 总的来说 followed by a formulaic close such as 以上就是我的报告，谢谢大家. Do not simply stop talking; give the audience a clear signal that the presentation is complete.

Signposting through the body

Where written paragraphs use 首先, 其次, and 此外 to organize supporting points on the page, spoken presentations add 接下来 to signal a transition between points in real time, since a listener cannot look back at a heading the way a reader can glance back at a paragraph break. Use 接下来 between major sections of the talk, and continue using 首先/其次/此外 within a section to organize supporting points, exactly as in Chapter 12.

Restating and hedging in Q&A

A prepared answer in a Q&A period follows a simple shape: restate the question in your own words with 换句话说 or a short paraphrase, then answer directly, adding 据我了解 when the answer is based on limited or uncertain information rather than settled fact. 这是个好问题 is a formulaic phrase that buys a moment to think before answering; it should introduce a genuine answer, not replace one. When time is limited, 时间关系 signals that the Q&A period is closing.

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: A Presentation Opening, Closing, and Q&A Exchange

报告开头：
大家好，今天我要报告的题目是图书馆使用人数的变化。简单来说，我认为这个变化主要跟图书馆近年推行的数字化服务政策有关。首先，我会谈谈官方报告里的数据；其次，我会介绍主任采访中的看法；接下来，我会说明电子资源使用量的变化；最后，我会做一个总结。

报告结尾：
总的来说，现有的可靠数据表明，数字化服务政策是使用人数上升的一个重要原因。以上就是我的报告，谢谢大家，现在欢迎大家提问。

问答环节：
听众：你怎么知道这个变化不是因为别的原因，比如说学生变多了？
报告人：这是个好问题，让我想一想。换句话说，你想知道的是有没有考虑其他可能的原因。据我了解，官方报告里也提到了学生人数的变化，但增长幅度比图书馆使用人数的增长幅度小得多，所以数字化服务政策仍然是比较重要的原因。不过，我要补充一点：这只是初步的分析，可能还需要更多的数据来确认。

  1. 报告开头用了哪些signposting词语来预告报告的结构？
  2. 报告人是怎么restate听众的问题的？
  3. 报告人在回答里用了哪个hedging表达？

写作练习 — Writing Workshop

Writing Workshop: Presentation Notes and Anticipated Q&A

Prepare written notes to support your spoken presentation.

  1. Opening line: Write a one-sentence spoken opening that greets the audience, states your 题目, and previews your claim with 简单来说.
  2. Signposted body notes: List your supporting points in the order you will present them, marking each transition with 首先, 其次, 此外, or 接下来.
  3. Closing line: Write a closing line using 总的来说 and the formulaic close 以上就是我的报告，谢谢大家.
  4. Anticipated questions: Write two questions an audience member might reasonably ask, and draft a restate-and-answer response for each, using 换句话说 and, where appropriate, 据我了解.

写作检查清单 — Writing checklist:

  • The opening names the 题目 and previews the claim.
  • At least three signposting transitions appear in the body notes, including at least one 接下来.
  • The closing uses 总的来说 and the formulaic close.
  • Each anticipated question includes a restatement before the answer.
  • At least one anticipated answer includes 据我了解 or another appropriate hedge.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Q&A norms vary by setting

The restate-then-answer pattern taught in this chapter is broadly useful, but expectations around question-and-answer periods vary by institution, discipline, and audience. In some academic and professional settings, questions are held until the very end; in others, brief clarifying questions are welcomed during the talk. Some audiences expect a confident, direct answer even to a difficult question, while others value visible caution and explicit hedging when evidence is incomplete. A careful speaker checks the norms of their specific setting rather than assuming one Q&A style applies everywhere in Mandarin-speaking academic or professional life.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Skipping the opening preview: starting straight into content without stating the 题目 or previewing the claim leaves the audience without a map for the talk.
  • Ending abruptly: stopping without a closing signal such as 总的来说...谢谢大家 can leave the audience unsure whether the presentation is finished.
  • Answering before restating: jumping straight to an answer without restating the question can lead to answering a question the speaker misheard or misunderstood.
  • Overusing 这是个好问题: using this phrase before every answer, including simple factual questions, sounds evasive rather than thoughtful.
  • Hedging every answer: adding 据我了解 to answers that are actually well-supported understates the speaker's own evidence; reserve hedging for genuinely uncertain or partial answers.
