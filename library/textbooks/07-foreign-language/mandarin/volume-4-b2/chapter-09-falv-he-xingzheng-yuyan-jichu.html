Volume IV, Chapter 9: Legal and Administrative Language for General Purposes · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Mandarin

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

Chapter 8 taught you to describe a policy at the institutional level, hedge claims about its effects, and cite statistics cautiously. This chapter narrows the focus from evaluating a policy's effects to understanding the language of the policy documents themselves: the rights a policy grants, the obligations it creates, the forms it requires, and the restrictions it sets on who may do what. This is not a law-school chapter; it teaches general-purpose administrative and rights-and-obligations vocabulary of the kind found in a housing contract, a financial-aid handbook, a workplace policy, or a university regulation, rather than specialized legal argumentation. You will keep the hedging language from Chapter 8 and the stance-and-concession language from Chapter 7 fully active while learning to state what someone has a right to do, what someone is obligated to do, and where a stated boundary or restriction begins and ends.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe a right using 有权 and an obligation using 义务 or 应尽的义务.
  2. Describe compliance with a regulation using 符合规定 and 按照规定.
  3. Describe a restriction or a scope using 限制 and 范围, including when something exceeds that scope.
  4. Read a short section of a form or regulation and identify its stated rights, obligations, and restrictions.
  5. Combine rights-and-obligations language with the hedging language from Chapter 8 when a rule's application is unclear.
  6. Discuss a general administrative situation using accurate, careful institutional language.

Communication Scenario

The student newspaper piece from Chapter 8 needs one more section: a plain-language explanation of what the digital-services policy actually requires of students and offices, based on the official regulation text. You are asked to read the relevant section of the university regulation, identify what rights it grants students, what obligations it places on offices, and what its stated restrictions are, and then explain this section in your own words for a general reader.

  1. You will listen to a short administrative-office explanation of a student's rights and obligations under the new policy.
  2. You will read a short excerpt from the university regulation itself.
  3. You will practice describing rights, obligations, and restrictions using accurate institutional vocabulary.
  4. You will write a short plain-language explanation of the regulation excerpt for a general reader.

发音 — Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

This chapter introduces no new sound system. It continues developing intelligibility in formal, regulation-style reading aloud, where several short qualifying clauses often follow one another. Distinguish 权利 (quánlì, "right") clearly from 权力 (quánlì, "power" or "authority"): the two are homophones in Pinyin, so listeners must rely entirely on context, word choice, and surrounding vocabulary to tell them apart, not on any difference in pronunciation. Keep 按照规定 phrased as one rhythmic unit before the main clause, matching the pattern practiced with hedge phrases in Chapter 8.

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Rights, Obligations, and Restrictions

This chapter's productive focus is describing rights, obligations, forms, and restrictions in general-purpose administrative language, not specialized legal argumentation. Three patterns support this: stating a right or an obligation, describing compliance with a regulation, and describing a restriction or scope. The hedging language from Chapter 8 and the stance-and-concession language from Chapter 7 remain active and combine naturally with the patterns taught here whenever a rule's application is not entirely clear.

1. Stating a Right or an Obligation: 有权 / 权利 / 义务

Use 有权 before a verb to state that someone has the right to do something, and use 权利 and 义务 as nouns to name a right or an obligation directly.

根据这项规定，学生有权查看自己的申请记录，同时也有义务按时提交所需材料。

2. Describing Compliance: 符合规定 / 按照规定

Use 符合规定 to say that something meets or complies with a regulation, and 按照规定 to introduce an action that follows from the regulation.

如果你提交的材料符合规定，办公室会按照规定在十个工作日内给出答复。

3. Describing a Restriction or Scope: 限制 / 范围 / 超出范围

Use 限制 to describe a restriction, 范围 to describe the scope something covers, and 超出范围 to say that a case falls outside that scope.

这项政策的适用范围只包括在校学生；如果你的情况超出了这个范围，可能需要联系另一个办公室。在一定程度上，这样的限制是为了确保资源能优先提供给符合条件的学生。

Putting It Together

Describing an administrative rule responsibly means naming the right or obligation it creates, describing whether a case complies with it, and stating clearly where its scope begins and ends -- while still using hedging language from Chapter 8 when a rule's application to a specific case is not fully certain, and stance-and-concession language from Chapter 7 when offering a personal evaluation of whether a rule seems fair or clear.

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: 学生权利和义务说明会 — Student Rights and Obligations Information Session

为了帮助学生更好地了解新的数字化服务政策，学校举行了一次说明会，向学生介绍他们在这项政策下的权利和义务。

办公室代表首先说明，学生有权利随时查看自己的申请记录，也有权要求办公室解释处理结果。同时，学生也有义务按时提交所需材料，并确保申请表格上的信息准确无误。

代表接着说明，按照规定，办公室应在十个工作日内对符合规定的申请给出答复。然而，值得注意的是，这项政策的适用范围只包括在校注册学生，不包括已经毕业或休学的学生；如果学生的情况超出了这个范围，需要联系另一个专门的办公室。

在一定程度上，这样的限制是为了确保资源能优先提供给符合条件的在校学生，但也有学生提出，希望学校能进一步说明"符合条件"的具体标准。

Guided Practice

阅读练习 — Reading Component

Reading Component: An Excerpt from the University Regulation

Read the following short excerpt from the regulation itself and compare its formal, precise language with the model text above.

第五条：学生的权利与义务

在校注册学生有权查看本人的申请记录，并有权就处理结果向相关办公室提出说明请求。学生有义务按时、如实提交申请所需的全部材料。相关办公室应在收到符合规定的申请后十个工作日内予以答复。本条规定的适用范围为在校注册学生，不适用于已毕业、已退学或休学期间的学生；上述人员如有相关需求，应联系校友服务办公室或学籍管理办公室。
  1. 这条规定说明了学生的哪两项权利？
  2. 这条规定说明了学生的哪一项义务？
  3. 这条规定的适用范围是什么？哪些人不在范围之内？
  4. 如果你的情况超出了这条规定的范围，你应该联系哪个办公室？

写作练习 — Writing Component

Writing Component: A Plain-Language Explanation of a Regulation

Write a short paragraph that explains the regulation excerpt above (or a similar rule of your choice) in plain language for a general reader.

  1. Name the rule or regulation being explained.
  2. State the right or rights it grants using 有权 or 权利.
  3. State the obligation it creates using 义务.
  4. Describe its scope using 范围, including who is not included.
  5. Add a hedge if any part of the rule's application is unclear, using 在一定程度上 or 可能会导致 from Chapter 8.
  6. Close with your own brief stance using 我的看法是 or 依我看, including a concession with 不可否认...但是 from Chapter 7.

写作检查清单 — Writing checklist:

  • The rule or regulation is clearly named.
  • At least one right and one obligation are stated accurately.
  • The rule's scope is described, including any exceptions.
  • Any uncertain point is hedged rather than stated as fact.
  • The paragraph closes with a supported stance that includes a concession, as in Chapter 7.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Plain-language explanation across institutions

Many institutions in Mandarin-speaking settings publish formal regulation text alongside a more accessible explanation for a general audience, though the degree of formality in the original text, and the amount of plain-language support offered, varies by institution, document type, and setting, and also varies somewhat across Mainland, Taiwan, Hong Kong/Macau, Singapore, and diaspora institutional contexts. A single regulation's tone should not be treated as representative of how every institution in every Mandarin-speaking setting writes administrative rules.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing 权利 (right) and 权力 (power/authority): these are homophones in Pinyin; rely on context and word choice, not pronunciation, to distinguish them.
  • Treating 义务 as optional: 义务 describes an obligation, not a suggestion; avoid softening it with hedging language meant for uncertain claims.
  • Overstating a regulation's scope: use 范围 and 超出范围 to state clearly what a rule does and does not cover, rather than assuming it applies universally.
  • Dropping hedging language when a rule's application is unclear: even formal administrative language should qualify uncertain points with 在一定程度上 or 可能会导致 from Chapter 8 rather than stating them as settled.
  • Producing 被 independently: continue to recognize 被ni institutional and regulation text receptively; this chapter does not require independent production of the passive construction.

Chapter Summary

This chapter introduced general-purpose legal and administrative language: stating rights and obligations with 权利, 义务, and 有权, describing compliance with 符合规定 and 按照规定, and describing restrictions and scope with 限制, 范围, and 超出范围. These patterns were combined with the hedging language from Chapter 8 and the stance-and-concession language from Chapter 7 to explain a regulation clearly and evaluate it carefully.
