采访和公开讨论

Volume IV, Chapter 7: Interviews and Public Discussion · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Mandarin

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

Chapter 6 taught you to read someone else's report critically: identifying its structure, its sources, and its framing. This chapter moves the same critical attention into real time. Instead of analyzing a report after it has been written, you will now take part in the kind of formal spoken exchange that produces one -- an interview -- and in the public discussion that often follows it. This chapter introduces formal spoken turn-taking, requesting clarification when something is unclear, and stating your own stance clearly and diplomatically in a live exchange. The source-marking and framing language from Chapter 6 remains active, since interviewers and discussants routinely refer to what "a report said" or "the office stated."

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Open and close a formal interview using appropriate turn-taking language.
  2. Ask a prepared interview question and a natural follow-up question.
  3. Request clarification when a speaker's meaning is unclear.
  4. Politely take or yield a turn in a public discussion without interrupting abruptly.
  5. State a personal stance clearly, using appropriately hedged formal language.
  6. Participate in a short public discussion that refers back to a report's framing and sourcing.

Communication Scenario

The student reading group from Chapter 6 has been invited to a short public forum organized by the international-student office. A student reporter will interview the office's representative about the new online-application recommendation, and afterward students in the audience will be invited to ask follow-up questions and share their own views. You are preparing both to conduct part of the interview and to speak during the open discussion that follows.

  1. You will listen to an excerpt from the interview with the office representative.
  2. You will read the interviewer's prepared question list and the representative's key answers.
  3. You will practice conducting an interview turn and requesting clarification with a partner.
  4. You will practice speaking up in a public discussion, stating your stance and referring back to Chapter 6's report.

发音 — Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

This chapter introduces no new sound system. It develops formal spoken Mandarin within a live, turn-taking genre rather than a read-aloud one, so pacing and pausing carry more communicative weight than in Chapter 6. Leave a clear, brief pause after 请问 before asking a question, to signal that a turn is beginning. Keep 澄清 (chéngqīng) and 追问 (zhuīwèn) tonally distinct from 采访 (cǎifǎng); all three begin with different initial-tone combinations that are easy to blur when speaking quickly in an interview setting. When yielding a turn with 不好意思打断一下, keep the phrase light and quick rather than heavy, since its pragmatic function is softening, not emphasis.

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Turn-Taking, Clarification, and Stance

This chapter's productive focus is spoken interactional strategy, not a new grammar system. Four patterns support this: opening and closing a formal turn, requesting clarification, softening an interruption, and stating a personal stance. The source-marking language from Chapter 6 remains active and is reused here to refer back to what a report or a speaker has already said.

1. Opening and Closing a Turn: 请问 / 谢谢您的回答

Use 请问 to open a formal question turn, and a closing formula such as 谢谢您的回答 to signal that your turn has ended.

请问，网上申请设为默认方式以后，处理时间会缩短多少？谢谢您的回答。

2. Requesting Clarification: 您的意思是...吗？/ 换句话说...

Use 您的意思是...吗？ to check your understanding of what a speaker just said, and 换句话说 to restate a point in your own words for confirmation.

您的意思是，现场申请不会被完全取消，对吗？换句话说，学生仍然有两种选择。

3. Softening an Interruption or Follow-Up: 不好意思打断一下 / 我想追问一下

Use 不好意思打断一下 before adding a point in a discussion, and 我想追问一下 to ask a natural follow-up question after an answer.

不好意思打断一下，我想追问一下：材料复杂的学生具体应该联系谁？

4. Stating a Stance: 我的看法是 / 依我看 / 不可否认...但是

Combine stance openers with the concession language already consolidated in Unit I to state a supported opinion in a public discussion.

我的看法是，网上申请确实提高了效率；不可否认，也有学生需要更多帮助，但办公室已经考虑到了这一点。

Putting It Together

Taking part in an interview or public discussion means opening and closing your turn clearly, checking your understanding through clarification, entering the conversation without interrupting abruptly, and stating your own stance in a way that is both direct and diplomatic -- while still referring accurately to sources and framing, as in Chapter 6.

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: 校报采访 — 与办公室代表的对话 — Campus Interview: A Conversation with the Office Representative

记者：谢谢您接受我们的采访。请问，为什么办公室决定把网上申请设为默认方式？

代表：据我们的数据显示，网上申请的处理时间比现场申请平均短三天，选择网上申请的学生比例也在逐年上升，所以我们认为这是更有效率的默认方式。

记者：您的意思是，现场申请以后就不能再选择了吗？

代表：不是的，换句话说，现场申请仍然作为选项保留，特别是为材料情况比较复杂的学生保留。

记者：我想追问一下，材料复杂的学生具体应该联系谁？

代表：他们可以直接联系办公室预约现场时间，工作人员会一对一提供帮助。

记者：谢谢您的回答，也谢谢您接受今天的采访。

口语练习 — Speaking Component

Speaking Component

交际任务 — Communicative task: 采访与公开讨论

Working with a partner, take turns playing the interviewer and the office representative. The interviewer should open and close each turn appropriately, ask at least one prepared question and one natural follow-up, and request clarification at least once. Then switch to a public-discussion format: one of you shares a stance on the recommendation, referring back to the report from Chapter 6, while the other practices softening an interruption to add a point.

独立练习 — Solo alternative

Record yourself conducting a short one-person interview, playing both roles, then record a thirty-second statement of your own stance on the recommendation, using 我的看法是 or 依我看 and at least one concession with 不可否认...但是.

写作练习 — Writing Component

Writing Component: An Interview Question List and Stance Statement

Write a short prepared question list for an interview on a topic of your choice, followed by a brief written statement of your own stance on that topic.

  1. List four or five interview questions in formal register, ordered from general to specific.
  2. Mark at least one question as a planned follow-up using 追问.
  3. Write a short stance statement using 我的看法是 or 依我看.
  4. Include a concession using 不可否认...但是.
  5. Refer back to a source or report using 据报道 or 根据, as in Chapter 6.
  6. Close with a one-sentence formal summary using 总的来说.

写作检查清单 — Writing checklist:

  • The question list moves from general to specific and includes a marked follow-up.
  • The stance statement clearly uses a stance-opening phrase.
  • A concession is included using 不可否认...但是.
  • At least one source is referred to using 据报道 or 根据.
  • The piece closes with 总的来说 in consistent formal register.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Turn-taking and stance in formal public discussion

Conventions for interrupting, softening disagreement, and stating a stance in public forums vary across settings, institutions, and individual speakers, and also vary somewhat across Mainland, Taiwan, Hong Kong/Macau, Singapore, and diaspora academic and civic contexts. The softening and turn-taking expressions in this chapter are widely usable in formal Mandarin-language settings, but a single forum's tone should not be treated as representative of an entire region or institution. Speakers, including the international-student office and its representatives, may reasonably differ in how directly they state a position.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Interrupting without softening: entering a discussion abruptly can sound rude in formal Mandarin; use 不好意思打断一下 or a similar softener first.
  • Stating a stance with no concession: a stance supported only by assertion is less persuasive in formal register than one that also acknowledges a counterpoint with 不可否认...但是.
  • Confusing 提问 and 追问: 提问 is asking a question in general; 追问 specifically means following up on an answer just given.
  • Introducing new grammar or vocabulary beyond this chapter: this chapter checks turn-taking, clarification, and stance expressed through the patterns taught here, combined with previously consolidated Unit I and Chapter 6 systems.

Chapter Summary

This chapter introduced interviews and public discussion: opening and closing a formal spoken turn with 请问 and a closing formula, requesting clarification with 您的意思是 and 换句话说, softening an interruption with 不好意思打断一下, asking a natural follow-up with 追问, and stating a personal stance with 我的看法是 or 依我看 supported by a concession. These patterns were combined with the source-marking language from Chapter 6 to discuss a report in real time rather than only in writing. This continues Unit II, which moves next to social issues and public language in Chapter 8.
