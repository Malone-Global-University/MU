综合任务三：研究型作业

Volume IV, Chapter 15: Unit III Assessment · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Mandarin

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

This chapter is a cumulative assessment. It introduces no new productive grammar, no new high-burden character set, and no new pronunciation or register system. Instead, it asks you to bring together everything you developed across Unit III: citing and paraphrasing sources (Chapter 11), writing in an academic register (Chapter 12), delivering a formal presentation and handling questions (Chapter 13), and mediating the same finding for different audiences (Chapter 14). The library-usage project you have carried since Chapter 12 now becomes a single integrated research task with a written component, a source-based component, and an oral component performed for two audiences.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Organize a short research-based written report using attribution, paraphrase, and a clear argument structure.
  2. Cite and paraphrase at least two distinct sources without distorting their claims.
  3. Deliver a short formal oral presentation of your findings and respond to follow-up questions.
  4. Mediate your findings into an internal version and a general-audience version without losing accuracy.
  5. Identify where your own work would benefit from more evidence, a clearer caveat, or a simpler explanation.
  6. Complete an integrated listening, reading, speaking, and writing task under level-appropriate time constraints.

Communication Scenario

The library director was satisfied with your Chapter 14 mediated updates and has now asked you to prepare the full research packet that will accompany next quarter's planning meeting. The packet must (1) present your findings in a short written report that cites at least two sources, (2) be deliverable as a two-minute oral presentation with a brief question-and-answer period, and (3) include the two mediated versions -- internal and public -- you already practiced. This chapter walks you through completing all three components using only the systems you have already developed.

  1. You will listen to a short recorded exchange from a planning meeting and identify which claims are supported and which are hedged.
  2. You will read two short source excerpts on library digitization and identify how each should be cited or paraphrased.
  3. You will prepare and deliver a two-minute presentation of your findings, then respond to two follow-up questions.
  4. You will write a short source-based report and the two mediated updates, then check all three for accuracy and consistency.

评估重点 — Assessment Focus and Systems Ledger

Assessment Focus and Systems Ledger

No new grammar is introduced in this chapter. The task below assesses the following systems, all already marked developed or consolidated in the Unit III matrices: source attribution and citation language (Chapter 11), formal written argument structure and nominal groups (Chapter 12), presentation and Q&A language (Chapter 13), and audience mediation (Chapter 14). It also draws on the cumulative grammar, particle, aspect, complement, and register systems consolidated across Volume IV.

What is assessed

  • 用引用和转述的方式呈现至少两个来源的信息，不歪曲原意。
  • 用论点、论据和让步结构组织一篇简短的研究型报告。
  • 做一个两分钟左右的口头报告，并回应至少两个提问。
  • 把同一个研究结果分别面向内部读者和大众读者进行合适的表达。

What is not assessed

No unstudied characters, no unlabeled traditional forms, no new grammar structures, and no specialized terminology beyond what Chapters 11-14 activated will be required. Receptive material may appear only with support and is not an independent production requirement.

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: A Short Source-Based Report

图书馆数字化服务使用情况简报
根据本馆年度报告，到馆人数比前一年增加了百分之十五，电子资源访问量增加了百分之二十二。据一项关于高校图书馆数字化的研究指出，线上预约系统的推行通常会在短期内提高实体到馆率，因为它降低了学生使用图书馆的门槛。另一项研究也提到，电子资源访问量的上升往往与校园网络覆盖的改善有关，而不仅仅与图书馆自身的服务有关。综合来看，本馆使用人数的上升很可能与数字化服务的推行有关，但目前的分析仍属初步阶段，还需要更多学期的数据才能确认因果关系。

  1. 这段报告引用了几个来源？分别支持了什么论点？
  2. 报告在最后用了什么词来说明结论还不确定？
  3. 如果把这段报告面向大众读者简化，你会保留哪一句，省略哪一句？

阅读任务 — Reading Task

Reading Task: Two Source Excerpts

Read the two short source excerpts below and answer the questions that follow.

来源一：
一项关于高校图书馆服务的研究指出，线上预约座位系统的推行通常会在短期内提高实体到馆率，因为它降低了学生使用图书馆的门槛，但这种提高是否能长期保持，仍需要更长时间的观察。

来源二：
另一项研究提到，电子资源访问量的变化往往受到多种因素的共同影响，例如校园网络覆盖的改善、课程作业的要求，以及学生对电子资源本身的熟悉程度，图书馆服务只是其中一个因素。

写作任务 — Writing Task

Writing Task: The Full Research Packet

Complete the three written components of the research packet, using the library-usage finding you have developed since Chapter 12.

  1. Source-based report: Write a 5-6 sentence report that states your finding, cites or paraphrases at least two sources, includes one hedge about the preliminary nature of the analysis, and ends with a clear concluding statement.
  2. Internal mediated update: Write a 3-4 sentence version for library staff, retaining specific figures and the hedge.
  3. Public mediated update: Write a 2-3 sentence version for the general campus newsletter, using 通俗 language and omitting unnecessary technical detail.
  4. Consistency check: Compare all three texts and confirm that the core claim is accurate and consistent across all of them.

写作检查清单 — Writing checklist:

  • The report cites or paraphrases at least two distinct sources without distorting either one.
  • The report includes at least one clear hedge about the preliminary nature of the analysis.
  • The internal update retains specific figures and the hedge; the public update does not require them.
  • The core claim (usage rose, likely linked to digital services, with other factors possibly involved) is accurate and consistent in all three texts.
  • No version adds certainty that the sources do not support.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Source discipline across settings

Expectations for how explicitly a source must be named, and how much hedging is expected around a preliminary finding, vary by institution, field, and publication context, and this variation is not unique to any one Mandarin-speaking community. A careful researcher or presenter checks the norms of the specific setting -- an internal planning meeting, a public newsletter, or a formal report -- rather than assuming that one level of source detail or caution fits every audience.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Citing without attribution language: stating a source's claim as though it were your own observation, rather than using 据...指出 or a clear paraphrase marker.
  • Letting sources contradict each other unexplained: presenting two sources' claims side by side without showing how they relate can confuse a reader or listener.
  • Dropping the hedge in the oral version: a caveat that appears in the written report should not simply disappear when you present the same finding aloud.
  • Answering a question with a new, uncited claim: in the Q&A portion, a follow-up answer should stay within what your sources and data actually support.
  • Letting the public version drift from the source-based report: the mediated updates must still trace back accurately to the same underlying finding and sources.
