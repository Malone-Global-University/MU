综合任务一：正式分析文本

Volume IV, Chapter 5: Unit I Assessment — A Formal Analytical Text · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Mandarin

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

This chapter is the Unit I assessment. It introduces no new grammar, no new character set, and no new pronunciation system. Instead, it asks you to bring together everything Unit I has built: the accuracy and formal register of Chapter 1, the institutional-procedure language of Chapter 2, the classification and comparison structures of Chapter 3, and the thesis-evidence-concession-counterargument sequence of Chapter 4. The task in this chapter is the same kind of task the international-student office scenario has followed since Chapter 2, now completed as a single formal analytical text: a written recommendation memo, supported orally, that classifies a set of options, compares them with data, takes a clear position, and defends that position against a reasonable objection.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Classify and describe an institutional procedure using accurate formal register.
  2. Compare two or more options using 比, 更, 最, and related comparison structures.
  3. State a clear thesis using 我的立场是 or 我认为.
  4. Support the thesis with at least two distinct pieces of evidence.
  5. Concede a reasonable objection using 不可否认 or 尽管, and counter it using 然而 or 不过.
  6. Produce one coordinated written and spoken formal analytical text that performs all of the above in sequence.

Communication Scenario

The international-student office has now received several rounds of student input on its application-method question: the procedure description from Chapter 2, the classification and comparison from Chapter 3, and the position piece from Chapter 4. The office has asked one student representative to prepare a single formal memo that a committee can review without reading every earlier document. The memo must classify the two application methods, compare them, state a clear recommendation, and defend that recommendation against the strongest objection — all in one coordinated document, followed by a short oral summary at the committee meeting.

  1. You will listen to a short committee exchange in which a representative summarizes a recommendation and answers a question about it.
  2. You will read a complete sample memo that classifies, compares, takes a position, and defends it.
  3. You will practice presenting a recommendation orally and responding to a follow-up question.
  4. You will write your own complete formal memo, coordinating all Unit I systems.

发音 — Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

Pronunciation and Reading-Aloud Focus

No new sound system is introduced. This chapter checks whether tone accuracy and phrasing remain intelligible across a longer coordinated text rather than a single paragraph. Maintain the steady falling tone on 立场 (lìchǎng), the full four-syllable weight of 不可否认, and clear pausing after each discourse opener (值得注意的是, 不可否认, 总的来说) as you move between the classification, comparison, and argument sections of a single text. Pay special attention to keeping tone and phrasing consistent when reading aloud a text that shifts between sections with different functions, since natural speakers do not flatten their pacing simply because a text is long.

语法复习 — Cumulative Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Cumulative Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Coordinating Unit I

This chapter introduces no new grammar. It requires you to sequence four systems from Chapters 1 through 4 inside one coordinated document: formal register and disciplined paragraphing (Chapter 1), institutional procedure description (Chapter 2), classification and comparison (Chapter 3), and thesis-evidence-concession-counterargument (Chapter 4). The pattern below shows how these systems are ordered inside a single formal memo.

1. Opening: Classification and Procedure (Chapters 1–2)

Open the memo by classifying the options under discussion and describing the relevant procedure in formal register, without yet stating a position.

目前，国际学生办公室的申请方式可以分为两类：网上申请和现场申请。根据现行规定，两种方式都需要提交同样的材料。

2. Middle: Comparison with Data (Chapter 3)

Compare the classified options using 比, 更, and data-linked explanation.

根据去年的数据，网上申请的处理时间比现场申请短，选择网上申请的学生比例也更高。

3. Position: Thesis, Evidence, Concession, and Counterargument (Chapter 4)

State the thesis, support it with at least two pieces of evidence, concede the strongest objection, and counter it.

我的立场是：办公室应该把网上申请设为默认方式。第一个论点是效率；第二个论点是大多数学生的实际选择。不可否认，材料复杂的申请人可能遇到困难；然而，这些申请人仍然可以选择现场申请。

4. Closing: Restated Position in Formal Register

Close with 总的来说 and a formally worded restatement, matching the register established in Chapter 1.

总的来说，这项建议既符合效率和数据的考虑，也保留了现场申请的选项，照顾到了不同申请人的情况。

Putting It Together

A complete formal analytical text follows this order: classify and describe (Chapters 1–2), compare with evidence (Chapter 3), state and defend a position (Chapter 4), and close with a formally restated recommendation. No individual section introduces new grammar; the task is coordinating four already-consolidated systems into one text.

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: 备忘录 — 关于申请方式的建议 — Memo: Recommendation on Application Method

备忘录：关于国际学生申请方式的建议

目前，国际学生办公室的申请方式可以分为两类：网上申请和现场申请。根据现行规定，两种方式都需要提交相同的材料，区别主要在于提交和审核的流程。

根据去年的数据，网上申请的平均处理时间比现场申请短，选择网上申请的学生比例也超过一半。这说明网上申请在效率和学生实际选择两方面都更具优势。此外，网上申请也减少了办公室在申请高峰期的排队压力，这对办公室和学生双方都有好处。

我的立场是：办公室应该把网上申请设为默认方式，同时继续保留现场申请的选项。不可否认，材料情况比较复杂的申请人在使用网上系统时可能遇到困难，因为遇到问题不能马上得到工作人员的帮助。这确实是网上申请的一个缺点。尽管如此，这个问题并不能否定网上申请对大多数学生的价值：材料复杂的申请人依然可以选择现场申请，问题因此可以得到解决。

总的来说，把网上申请设为默认方式，既符合大多数学生的实际需要和效率考虑，也通过保留现场申请的选项，照顾到了少数材料复杂的申请人。这是一个既支持数据、也考虑到不同申请人情况的合理建议。

阅读练习 — Reading Component

Reading Component: A Complete Formal Memo

Read the memo below, submitted by a different student representative with a different recommendation, and answer the questions that follow.

备忘录：关于保留现场申请为默认方式的建议

申请方式目前可以分为网上申请和现场申请两类。虽然网上申请在处理时间上比现场申请短，但这并不能说明它适合所有学生。根据办公室今年收到的意见，材料复杂的申请人普遍认为现场申请更可靠，因为遇到问题可以立刻得到工作人员的帮助。

我的立场是：现场申请应该继续作为默认方式，网上申请作为可选方式提供给希望提高效率的学生。第一个论点是可靠性：现场申请能够及时解决材料问题。第二个论点是公平性：并非所有学生都能顺利使用网上系统。不可否认，网上申请的处理速度更快，这也是许多学生愿意选择它的原因。然而，速度不能替代可靠性，尤其是对材料复杂的申请人而言。总的来说，保留现场申请为默认方式，同时提供网上申请作为选项，更能照顾到所有学生的实际需要。

  2. 作者的立场和上面范文中的立场有什么不同？
  3. 作者用了哪两个论点来支持自己的立场？
  4. 作者承认了什么反对意见？他是怎么回应的？
口语练习 — Speaking Component

Speaking Component

交际任务 — Communicative task: 委员会陈述

Working with a partner, one person presents a two-to-three-minute oral summary of a written recommendation (your own, or the model memo above): classify the options, cite the comparison data, state the position, and briefly defend it. The other partner plays a committee member and asks one follow-up question raising the strongest objection. The presenter must concede the objection and counter it before closing with 总的来说.

独立练习 — Solo alternative

Record yourself presenting a full oral summary of your written memo, including classification, comparison, position, concession, and counterargument, then record a follow-up question you anticipate and your response to it.

写作练习 — Writing Component

Writing Component: A Complete Formal Memo

Complete the assessment writing task, coordinating all Unit I systems into one document.

  1. Choose an institutional question you know or can reasonably invent (it does not need to be the application-method question used throughout this unit).
  2. Classify the relevant options and briefly describe the procedure, in formal register.
  3. Compare the options using at least one comparison structure and one piece of data.
  4. State a clear thesis and support it with at least two distinct pieces of evidence.
  5. Concede the strongest objection and counter it.
  6. Close with a formally restated recommendation using 总的来说.
  7. Check your draft against the checklist below before considering the task complete.

写作检查清单 — Writing checklist:

  • The memo opens by classifying the options and describing the relevant procedure in formal register.
  • The options are compared using at least one comparison structure and one data-linked statement.
  • The thesis is stated clearly using 我的立场是 or 我认为.
  • At least two distinct pieces of evidence support the thesis.
  • A reasonable objection is conceded explicitly and then countered, not ignored or repeated.
  • The memo closes with a formally restated recommendation using 总的来说.
  • The register remains consistent and formal throughout the entire document.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Coordinating a formal document from start to finish

In many Mandarin-speaking institutional settings, a formal memo is expected to read as one coherent argument rather than as separate sections stapled together. Readers generally expect the classification, comparison, position, and conclusion to build logically toward the same recommendation. This expectation is common in institutional and academic writing generally and should not be treated as a fixed rule for every genre or every organization.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Treating the sections as unconnected: the classification and comparison sections should lead naturally into the thesis, not sit as an unrelated introduction.
  • Skipping the comparison step: a thesis without at least one comparison or data point feels asserted rather than supported.
  • Forgetting the concession: in a full formal memo, omitting 不可否认 or 尽管 makes the recommendation seem one-sided.
  • Introducing new grammar or vocabulary beyond Unit I: this chapter checks coordination of existing systems, not new material.
