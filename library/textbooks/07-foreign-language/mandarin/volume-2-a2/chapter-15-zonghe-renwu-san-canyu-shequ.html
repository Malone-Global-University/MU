综合任务三：参与社区

Volume II, Chapter 15: Unit III Assessment — Participating in the Community · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

This chapter is a cumulative assessment. It introduces no new grammar, no new character set, and no new pronunciation or register system. Instead, it asks you to bring together everything you have built across Chapters 11 through 14: talking about learning methods and ability, writing about work responsibilities and email, reading and reacting to public notices, and stating and supporting opinions about community and environmental issues. The scenario below asks you to act as an engaged community member who can listen, read, speak, and write about a single connected situation from start to finish.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Understand a short spoken exchange about a community meeting and extract key information.
  2. Read a public notice and a follow-up community message and answer questions about their content.
  3. Discuss a community issue with a partner, stating an opinion, giving a reason, and responding to another opinion.
  4. Write a short, appropriately registered message about a community matter, using 因为...所以..., ability/obligation modals, and 我觉得/我认为 correctly.
  5. Self-assess your control of the systems developed across Unit III.

Communication Scenario

Your neighborhood community center has posted a notice about a new evening class and is also collecting opinions about the recycling program discussed in earlier chapters. You will listen to a short conversation about the class, read the notice and a follow-up message, discuss your own opinion about the recycling program with a partner, and write a short message combining a work-related update with your community opinion.

  1. You will listen to two neighbors discussing a new evening class at the community center.
  2. You will read a public notice about the class and a short follow-up message about the recycling program.
  3. You will discuss the recycling program with a partner, stating and supporting your opinion.
  4. You will write a short message that reports on a task at work and gives your opinion about the recycling program.

听力评估 — Listening Assessment

Listening Assessment

Listening script for independent use. Read aloud slowly, then at a natural pace, or have a fluent speaker record it.

对话 — Conversation:
男：你看到社区中心的通知了吗？他们晚上有一个新的中文课。
女：看到了。我觉得这个主意不错，可是我得先问问我的老板，因为我晚上有时候得加班。
男：如果你能来，我们可以一起上课。
女：好，我今天晚上就给你答复。对了，你打算怎么回答关于回收计划的调查？
男：我支持，因为现在垃圾太多了。你呢？
女：我也支持，不过我认为回收站应该更多。

QuestionAnswer
女的为什么要先问老板？______
男的对回收计划有什么意见？为什么？______
女的同意还是不同意男的意见？她还有什么想法？______
阅读评估 — Reading Assessment

Reading Assessment

Read the community center notice, then the follow-up message, and answer the questions.

社区中心通知：新中文课

从下个月开始，社区中心将开一个新的中文课，星期二和星期四晚上七点到八点。想上课的居民必须先在中心报名，而且需要每星期都参加。中文课的名额有限，所以请尽快报名。

另外，关于公园扩大计划和回收站的调查还在进行，欢迎大家继续提出意见。
Community center notice: New Chinese class. Starting next month, the community center will open a new Chinese class, Tuesday and Thursday evenings from 7 to 8 PM. Residents who want to take the class must register at the center first, and need to attend every week. Spots in the Chinese class are limited, so please register as soon as possible. Also, the survey about the park expansion plan and recycling stations is still ongoing; everyone is welcome to continue offering opinions.

王小姐的留言：我已经在社区中心报名了中文课，星期二和星期四晚上都可以参加。另外，我认为回收站应该更多，因为现在的回收站太少了，如果回收站更多，大家会更愿意回收。

  1. 想上中文课的居民必须做什么？
  2. 为什么居民应该尽快报名？
  3. 王小姐对回收站有什么意见？她的理由是什么？
口语评估 — Speaking Assessment

Speaking Assessment

交际任务 — Communicative task: 回收计划讨论

With a partner, discuss the recycling program mentioned in the notice above. State your opinion using 我觉得 or 我认为, support it with 因为...所以..., and respond to your partner's opinion by agreeing, disagreeing, or partially agreeing. Include at least one sentence using 应该, 得, or 必须.

独立练习 — Solo alternative

Record yourself giving a one-minute opinion about the recycling program and the new class, including a stated opinion, a supporting reason, and one sentence about what residents should or must do.

写作评估 — Writing Assessment

Writing Assessment

Write a short message (six to nine sentences) to a colleague or friend that combines a brief work update with your opinion about the community recycling program.

写作任务 — Writing task: Type your message with Pinyin input. Your message must include:

  1. A brief update about a work task or responsibility, using vocabulary and modal verbs from Chapter 12 (for example 应该, 得, or 必须).
  2. A statement of your opinion about the recycling program, using 我觉得 or 我认为.
  3. At least one reason for your opinion, using 因为...所以....
  4. A polite closing appropriate to a written message to a colleague or friend.
  5. Consistent register throughout, matching a written message rather than casual speech.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • Modal verbs (会/能/可以/应该/得/必须) are used accurately and match their intended meaning.
  • 我觉得 and 我认为 are not mixed inconsistently within the same message.
  • 因为 and 所以 are used correctly to connect a reason to an opinion.
  • Chinese punctuation (，。？！) is used correctly throughout.
  • No new 如果 sentences are produced independently; any conditional content only reflects recognition of the pattern.

Register and Pragmatics Check

Self-Check — Maintaining appropriate register

Before submitting your speaking and writing tasks, confirm that you have kept your register consistent with the audience. A message to a colleague about work responsibilities should stay more measured than a casual chat with a friend, even when both use 我觉得. When disagreeing with a partner's opinion in the speaking task, use softening language such as 不太同意 or 你说得有道理，可是..., as introduced in Chapter 14, rather than a blunt refusal. This pattern is common in many Mandarin-speaking communities but can vary by relationship, setting, generation, and region.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing 应该, 得, and 必须: 必须 is stronger than 应该; do not use them interchangeably when the notice or context specifies a requirement versus a recommendation.
  • Mixing 我觉得 and 我认为 within one written message: pick the register-appropriate choice and stay consistent, as practiced in Chapter 14.
  • Producing new 如果 sentences independently: continue to treat 如果 as receptive only; recognizing the pattern in the reading passages is sufficient for this chapter.
  • Disagreeing too directly in the speaking task: use softening language rather than a flat 不同意.
  • Omitting a reason for an opinion: both the speaking and writing tasks require 因为...所以... to support your stated opinion.
