学习方法和技能

Volume II, Chapter 11: Learning Methods and Skills · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

This chapter opens Unit III by moving from independent daily-life logistics toward learning, work, and community participation. You will talk about how you study, what you find easy or difficult, and what methods help you improve. The chapter refines your control of 会, 能, and 可以 — three words that beginning learners often treat as interchangeable — and introduces natural ways to talk about effort, difficulty, and progress in learning a skill.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Distinguish 会 (learned ability), 能 (capability or circumstance), and 可以 (permission or possibility) in study-related contexts.
  2. Describe a study method or habit and explain why it helps you.
  3. Talk about something you find easy, difficult, or are still working on.
  4. Ask a classmate or tutor for study advice and respond to advice you receive.
  5. Write a short paragraph describing your own learning goals and methods.

Communication Scenario

You are preparing for an important test and want to compare study methods with a classmate. You will talk about what study techniques work for you, what subjects or skills you still find difficult, and what advice you can offer each other.

  1. You will listen to a short conversation between two students discussing their study habits.
  2. You will read a short student blog post about learning a new skill.
  3. You will write a paragraph describing your own study methods and goals.
  4. You will speak with a partner, comparing study habits and giving each other advice.

发音和读写 — Pronunciation and Literacy Focus

Pronunciation and Literacy Focus

  • Neutral tone review: practice the neutral tone in 记住 (the second syllable is unstressed) and in 容易, where the second syllable is light and short.
  • Tone pair practice: 进步 (jìnbù) combines two falling tones — keep both syllables clearly falling without letting the second collapse into the first.
  • Typing: continue typing through Pinyin input, now including words that share initial letters with earlier vocabulary (for example, distinguishing 练习 from 练习's near-homophones when the IME offers choices).

No new handwriting characters are introduced beyond the productive inventory already scheduled through Chapter 10.

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

1. Refining 会, 能, and 可以

All three words can be translated "can" in English, but they answer different questions.

会 — a learned skill, something you know how to do because you studied or practiced it.
我会写汉字。
Wǒ huì xiě Hànzì.
I know how to write Chinese characters.

能 — capability given current circumstances: time, health, permission from a situation, or physical ability.
我今天很忙，不能复习。
Wǒ jīntiān hěn máng, bù néng fùxí.
I'm very busy today — I can't review.

可以 — permission or general possibility.
我们可以用中文回答问题吗？
Wǒmen kěyǐ yòng Zhōngwén huídá wèntí ma?
Can we answer the questions in Chinese?

A useful test: if the sentence is about a skill you learned, use 会. If it's about whether circumstances allow it right now, use 能. If it's about whether something is permitted or generally possible, use 可以. In many everyday contexts 能 and 可以 overlap, and both are acceptable — the important distinction to master first is 会 versus the other two.

2. Talking About Effort and Progress

Use 坚持 with a time expression to describe sustained effort, and 进步 to describe improvement.

我每天坚持练习听力，所以进步了不少。
Wǒ měitiān jiānchí liànxí tīnglì, suǒyǐ jìnbùle bù shǎo.
I keep practicing listening every day, so I've improved quite a bit.

3. Describing Difficulty and Ease

Use 容易 and 难 as adjectival predicates, and compare two skills with 比 (from Chapter 9).

口语比听力难，我得多练习说话。
Kǒuyǔ bǐ tīnglì nán, wǒ děi duō liànxí shuōhuà.
Speaking is harder than listening — I need to practice speaking more.

4. Asking For and Giving Study Advice

你有什么好办法吗？我觉得这些生词很难记住。
Nǐ yǒu shénme hǎo bànfǎ ma? Wǒ juéde zhèxiē shēngcí hěn nán jìzhu.
Do you have any good methods? I find these vocabulary words hard to remember.

我的建议是每天查字典，然后多练习写句子。
Wǒ de jiànyì shì měitiān chá zìdiǎn, ránhòu duō liànxí xiě jùzi.
My suggestion is to look things up in the dictionary every day, and then practice writing sentences a lot.

对话 — Model Dialogue

Model Dialogue

Context: two classmates comparing study habits before a test, informal register

安娜：你复习得怎么样了？下星期就要考试了。
马克：还可以。语法我基础不错，可是生词我总是记不住，很难。
安娜：我也是。不过我发现一个方法：每天晚上查字典，然后写五个句子，慢慢就记住了。
马克：真的吗？我可以试试。你每天都坚持这样做吗？
安娜：对，我每天都坚持，所以这个月进步了不少。你呢？口语练习得怎么样？
马克：口语比语法难多了。我会说一些简单的句子，可是说得不太流利。
安娜：那你可以每天跟我练习说话，我们一起进步。
马克：好啊，谢谢你的建议！

Anna: How's your review going? The test is next week. Mark: Okay-ish. My grammar foundation is pretty good, but I can never remember the vocabulary — it's hard. Anna: Me too. But I found a method: every night I look words up in the dictionary, then write five sentences, and slowly I remember them. Mark: Really? I could try that. Do you keep doing that every day? Anna: Yes, I keep at it every day, so I've improved a lot this month. What about you? How's your speaking practice going? Mark: Speaking is much harder than grammar. I know how to say some simple sentences, but I don't speak very fluently. Anna: Then you could practice speaking with me every day, and we can improve together. Mark: Sounds good, thanks for the suggestion!

口语练习 — Speaking Practice

Speaking Practice

控制性练习 — Controlled practice

With a partner, take turns saying one thing you 会 do well, one thing you can't do right now because of circumstances (能), and one thing you are permitted or not permitted to do in class (可以).

交际任务 — Communicative task: 我的学习方法

Interview a partner about their study habits: what they find easy, what they find difficult, what method helps them most, and what advice they would give a new learner. Then switch roles.

独立练习 — Solo alternative

Record yourself describing your own study routine, including at least one thing you find difficult and one method that has helped you improve.

阅读练习 — Reading Practice

Reading Practice

Read the student blog post, then answer the comprehension questions.

我学中文已经八个月了。一开始，我觉得发音很难，可是现在我进步了不少。我的方法很简单：每天听十分钟中文歌，然后把不懂的词写在笔记本上，晚上查字典。语法方面，我会做练习，可是说话的时候还不太流利。我的老师建议我多跟中国朋友聊天，我觉得这个建议很有用，因为跟朋友聊天比看书容易坚持。
I've been studying Chinese for eight months now. At first, I found the pronunciation very difficult, but now I've improved quite a bit. My method is simple: I listen to ten minutes of Chinese songs every day, then write down the words I don't understand in a notebook, and look them up in the dictionary at night. As for grammar, I can do exercises, but I'm still not very fluent when speaking. My teacher suggested I chat with Chinese friends more, and I think that's a useful suggestion, because chatting with friends is easier to keep up than reading books.

  2. 她用什么方法学生词？
  3. 老师给她什么建议？为什么她觉得这个建议有用？
写作练习 — Writing Workshop

Writing Workshop

Write a paragraph (six to ten sentences) describing your own approach to learning Chinese (or another skill you are learning).

写作任务 — Writing task: Type your paragraph with Pinyin input. Your paragraph must include:

  1. One thing you 会 do — a skill you have already learned.
  2. One thing that is 难 or 容易 for you, with a brief explanation.
  3. A study method you use, using 坚持 or 复习/预习/练习.
  4. A sentence about progress, using 进步.
  5. A piece of advice you would give another learner, using 建议.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • 会, 能, and 可以 are each used correctly and not interchanged.
  • At least one comparison uses 比 accurately.
  • The paragraph reads as one connected description, not a list of unrelated facts.
  • Chinese punctuation (。，？) is used correctly throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture Note — Talking about study and ability

In many Mandarin-speaking learning environments, modest self-assessment is common when describing one's own ability — a learner might downplay a skill even when reasonably proficient, saying something like 还可以 ("it's okay") rather than praising themselves directly. This tendency varies by individual, generation, and setting, so it should not be treated as a universal rule, but it is useful to recognize when interpreting a classmate's or colleague's self-description.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using 会 for temporary circumstances: 会 is for learned skills, not for "I can't today because I'm busy" — use 能 for that.
  • Overusing 可以 for everything: 可以 works well for permission and general possibility, but a learned skill still calls for 会.
  • Treating 难 and 容易 as fixed facts rather than personal experience: these words describe how something feels to the speaker, so pair them with 我觉得 when giving a personal opinion.
  • Dropping 坚持 into contexts without ongoing effort: 坚持 specifically implies sustained effort over time, not a one-time action.
