朋友、关系和社区

Volume II, Chapter 2: Relationships and Local Community · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

In Chapter 1, you learned to connect sentences about your own life into a topic chain. This chapter turns that same skill toward other people — friends, classmates, neighbors, and members of your local community. You will expand your vocabulary for relationships and titles, learn to describe people more fully using 的-modification, and practice keeping your reference to a person clear across several sentences instead of repeating a name every time.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Introduce a friend or acquaintance using expanded relationship and title vocabulary.
  2. Describe a person using 的-modification for more than simple possession — for example, "the friend who lives near me."
  3. Maintain clear reference to the same person across a short connected passage.
  4. Ask and answer questions about someone else's relationships and community connections.
  5. Read and write a short paragraph introducing someone in your life.

Communication Scenario

The online exchange group has a new weekly activity: "介绍一位朋友" (Introduce a Friend). Members post a short passage introducing someone important in their daily life — a friend, classmate, neighbor, or coworker — and explain how they know that person.

  1. You will read 大卫's post introducing his neighbor.
  2. You will listen to 安娜 introduce a friend from her Chinese class.
  3. You will write your own "介绍一位朋友" post.
  4. You will speak with a partner about someone in your own community.

发音复习 — Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review

This chapter introduces no new sounds. Use the checklist below to confirm control of familiar sound patterns in new words.

  • The neutral tone in 关系 (guānxi) and 认识 (rènshi) — the second syllable is short and light.
  • Third-tone sandhi in 邻居 (línjū) when preceded by 我的 (wǒ de), and in 很友好 (hěn yǒuhǎo).
  • The j/q/x contrast in 同事 (tóngshì) versus 同学 (tóngxué).
  • Tone accuracy on titles: 先生 (xiānsheng) and 女士 (nǚshì), including the ü sound in 女.
语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Describing People with 的, and Negative Ability

You already know 的 for simple possession, as in 我的朋友 (my friend). This chapter expands 的-modification so it can attach a short descriptive phrase before a noun, not just a possessor. This is an early, controlled step toward relative-like modification, which will be developed further in later chapters.

Simple possession (already familiar)

这是我的邻居。 Zhè shì wǒ de línjū. This is my neighbor.

Expanded modification: [description] + 的 + noun

这是住在我家附近的邻居。 Zhè shì zhù zài wǒ jiā fùjìn de línjū. This is the neighbor who lives near my home.

她是常常帮助我的同学。 Tā shì chángcháng bāngzhù wǒ de tóngxué. She is the classmate who often helps me.

Notice that the descriptive phrase comes before 的, and the noun it describes comes after — the opposite order from English "who lives near my home." This pattern is introduced here only for short, familiar descriptions; longer or more complex modifiers are developed in later volumes.

Negative ability: 不会 / 不能 / 不可以

他不会说中文。 Tā bú huì shuō Zhōngwén. He can't speak Chinese (hasn't learned how).

我今天不能去。 Wǒ jīntiān bù néng qù. I can't go today (circumstance prevents it).

这儿不可以吃东西。 Zhèr bù kěyǐ chī dōngxi. You can't eat here (not permitted).

As with the positive forms from Volume I, keep 不会 (lack of learned skill), 不能 (circumstance or inability), and 不可以 (lack of permission) distinct. Do not treat them as interchangeable translations of "can't."

对话 — Model Dialogue

Model Dialogue

安娜和大卫 — Anna and David:
安娜：大卫，那位是谁？
大卫：那是王先生，是住在我宿舍附近的邻居。他人很好，常常帮助我。
安娜：你们认识多久了？
大卫：认识一年多了。他不会说英文，所以我们常常用中文聊天儿。
安娜：真好！我也想认识我们社区的人。
大卫：这个周末社区有一个活动，你可以去认识新朋友。
安娜：太好了，但是我这个周末不能去，我要学习。

This dialogue introduces expanded 的-modification (住在我宿舍附近的邻居) and negative ability (不会说英文, 不能去) within a natural exchange about community relationships.

Listening Practice

Ask a partner, instructor, or fluent speaker to read the passage below aloud twice at a natural pace before answering.

If no speaker is available, this is a Listening Script for Independent Use. Read it aloud yourself, slowly at first, then at a natural pace. Any spoken model should be verified by a fluent Mandarin speaker before being treated as a pronunciation standard.

安娜介绍朋友 — Anna Introduces a Friend:
我想介绍一位朋友，她叫玛丽，是我中文课的同学。我们认识半年了，关系很好。玛丽住在我宿舍附近，我们常常一起学习。她不会说很多中文，但是她很努力。玛丽也认识我们社区的很多人，因为她常常参加社区的活动。我很高兴认识她这样的朋友。

Reading Practice

Read the short passage below, then answer the comprehension questions. Pinyin support remains reduced, as in Chapter 1; rely on familiar characters and context.

大卫想介绍他的邻居王先生。王先生是住在大卫宿舍附近的邻居，也是他认识一年多的朋友。王先生不会说英文，但是他很友好，常常帮助大卫，比如帮他找东西、告诉他社区的活动。大卫和王先生的关系很好，他们常常用中文聊天儿。大卫说，认识王先生是他在这个社区最好的事情之一。

Mr. Wang can't speak English, but he is very friendly and often helps David — for example, helping him find things and telling him about community activities. Writing Workshop

Write a connected paragraph of five to seven sentences titled 介绍一位朋友, introducing someone in your life.

写作任务 — Writing task: Type your paragraph with Pinyin input if possible, or handwrite the characters you have already been assigned for handwriting. Your paragraph must include:

  1. An opening sentence naming the person and their relationship to you, using 是.
  2. At least one expanded 的-modification phrase, such as "the classmate who lives near me."
  3. A statement of how long you have known them, using 认识.
  4. At least one negative-ability form (不会, 不能, or 不可以) used accurately.
  5. A connected sentence that omits a repeated subject, continuing your topic chain skill from Chapter 1.
  6. A closing sentence about your relationship or community connection with this person.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • All six required elements are present and connected, not a disconnected list of sentences.
  • The expanded 的-phrase places the description before 的 and the noun after, in Mandarin order.
  • 不会, 不能, and 不可以 are not confused with one another.
  • Only vocabulary and characters already taught in Volume I, Chapter 1, or this chapter are used.
  • A Chinese period (。) ends each sentence, and commas (，) separate connected clauses.

Culture and Communication

Culture Note — Titles and community relationships

In many Mandarin-speaking contexts, using a title such as 先生 or 女士 together with a family name is a common way to refer to a neighbor, an older acquaintance, or someone in a semi-formal relationship, even in an otherwise friendly conversation. Community relationships built through daily proximity — such as neighbors helping one another with small tasks — are a familiar pattern in many Mandarin-speaking communities, though the closeness and frequency of such relationships vary widely by individual, city, and building type.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Reversing the order of expanded 的-modification: the description comes before 的, and the noun comes after — 住在附近的邻居, not the reverse.
  • Confusing 不会, 不能, and 不可以: 不会 is about learned skill, 不能 is about circumstance, and 不可以 is about permission — they are not interchangeable.
  • Losing track of who "he/she" refers to: when introducing a person across several sentences, make sure a pronoun or omitted subject still points back to the same person you introduced first.
  • Overusing a name in every sentence: once a person has been introduced, use 他/她 or omit the subject rather than repeating their name in each following sentence.
