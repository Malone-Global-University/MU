习惯和变化

Volume II, Chapter 3: Habits and Change · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

In Chapter 2, you learned to describe people you know and keep your reference to them clear across several sentences. This chapter shifts to describing yourself — specifically, how your daily habits have changed. You will contrast what you used to do with what you do now, mark a change directly with the particle 了, and expand your control of 不 and 没/没有 so you can talk about habits you no longer have, habits you have not yet started, and things you still do not do.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe a habit or routine using 习惯 and related vocabulary.
  2. Contrast a past habit with a present one using 以前 and 现在.
  3. Mark a change in situation using change-of-state 了.
  4. Distinguish 不 (general negation) from 没/没有 (negation of a past or completed situation) in statements about habits.
  5. Ask and answer questions about how someone's routine has changed over time.

Communication Scenario

This week's activity in the online exchange group is "我的习惯变了" (My Habits Have Changed). Members post about one habit that has changed since they started their current situation — moving to a new place, starting a new class, or beginning a new routine.

  1. You will read 大卫's post about how his morning routine changed after moving into the dorm.
  2. You will listen to 安娜 describe how her study habits changed after meeting 玛丽.
  3. You will write your own "我的习惯变了" post.
  4. You will speak with a partner about a habit of yours that has changed.

发音复习 — Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review

This chapter introduces no new sounds. Use the checklist below to confirm control of familiar sound patterns in new words.

  • The neutral tone in the second syllable of 习惯 (xíguàn) and 有时候 (yǒu shíhou).
  • Third-tone sandhi in 已经 (yǐjīng) when it begins a sentence, and in 很有意思 (hěn yǒuyìsi)-type phrases.
  • Keeping the fourth tone crisp and falling in 变化 (biànhuà) and 改变 (gǎibiàn) — do not let it flatten into a neutral tone.
  • The repeated-syllable rhythm of 慢慢 (mànmàn): both syllables keep a full, though softened, second-tone-to-tone contour rather than reducing to neutral tone.
语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Contrasting Habits, Marking Change, and Expanding 不/没

This chapter's core task is describing how something used to be true and is no longer true, or was not true before and has become true. Mandarin marks this contrast with time words and with the sentence-final particle 了, which signals a change of state — a different function from the completion use of 了 you have already studied.

Pattern 1 — Past habit vs. present habit: 以前..., 现在...

以前我每天很晚睡觉，现在我十一点睡觉。 Yǐqián wǒ měitiān hěn wǎn shuìjiào, xiànzài wǒ shíyī diǎn shuìjiào. Before, I went to bed very late every day; now I go to bed at eleven.

Time words open the clause they belong to. Do not insert 是 between the time word and the rest of the sentence — Mandarin does not need a linking verb here.

Pattern 2 — Change-of-state 了

我习惯了。 Wǒ xíguàn le. I've gotten used to it (I wasn't before, but now I am).

我现在不喝咖啡了。 Wǒ xiànzài bù hē kāfēi le. I don't drink coffee anymore (I used to, but not now).

Here, 了 sits at the end of the sentence and marks that the overall situation has changed, not that an action was completed. This is a different function from the perfective 了 you practiced in Volume I. Note that 不...了 together mean "not anymore," a very common change-of-state pattern.

Pattern 3 — Expanding 不 and 没/没有 for habits

我不喝咖啡。 Wǒ bù hē kāfēi. I don't drink coffee (general habit/fact).

我以前没(有)习惯早起。 Wǒ yǐqián méi(yǒu) xíguàn zǎo qǐ. I wasn't used to getting up early before.

我还没习惯这儿的天气。 Wǒ hái méi xíguàn zhèr de tiānqì. I still haven't gotten used to the weather here.

Use 不 for a general, ongoing habit or fact. Use 没 or 没有 when describing a state that had not yet come about, often together with 还 (still/yet) or 以前 (before). Keep this distinct from the negative-ability forms 不会/不能/不可以 you learned in Chapter 2 — those describe skill, circumstance, and permission, not habit.

You may also recycle 不会/不能/不可以 when a change involves ability or permission, for example 我以前不会做饭，现在我会了 (I couldn't cook before; now I can). Notice how 了 attaches to the end of the whole sentence to mark that this ability is new.

对话 — Model Dialogue

Model Dialogue

安娜和大卫 — Anna and David:
安娜：大卫，你的生活习惯改变了很多吗？
大卫：改变了不少。以前我每天很晚睡觉，现在我十一点就睡觉了。
安娜：为什么变了？
大卫：因为我的邻居王先生一直七点起床，我也慢慢习惯了早睡早起。
安娜：真的吗？我还没习惯这儿的生活呢。
大卫：别担心，后来你也会习惯的。你有什么习惯改变了吗？
安娜：有时候我想家，但是我已经不像以前那么想家了。

This dialogue integrates the past/present contrast (以前...现在...), change-of-state 了 (改变了, 习惯了, 不...了), and the 不/没 contrast (还没习惯) within a natural exchange about changing routines.

Reading Practice

Read the short passage below, then answer the comprehension questions. Pinyin support remains reduced, as in Chapters 1 and 2; rely on familiar characters and context.

大卫搬到宿舍以后，生活习惯有了不少变化。以前他每天很晚睡觉，早上很晚才起床。搬到宿舍以后，他认识了邻居王先生。王先生一直七点起床，大卫慢慢也习惯了早睡早起。现在他十一点睡觉，七点半起床，觉得比以前更有精神。他说，虽然一开始不习惯，但是后来他已经不觉得难了。大卫觉得，好的习惯需要时间，但是改变是可能的。
Since moving into the dorm, David's daily habits have changed quite a bit. Before, he went to bed very late every day and got up very late in the morning. After moving into the dorm, he got to know his neighbor Mr. Wang. Mr. Wang has always gotten up at seven, and David gradually got used to going to bed early and getting up early too. Now he goes to bed at eleven and gets up at seven-thirty, and feels more energetic than before. He says that although it wasn't easy at first, afterward he no longer found it difficult. David feels that good habits take time, but change is possible.

理解问题 — Comprehension questions:

  1. 大卫以前几点睡觉，几点起床？
  2. 大卫为什么开始早睡早起？
  3. 大卫现在几点睡觉，几点起床？
  4. 大卫觉得改变习惯容易不容易？

Writing Workshop

Write a connected paragraph of five to seven sentences titled 我的习惯变了, describing one habit of yours that has changed.

写作任务 — Writing task: Type your paragraph with Pinyin input if possible, or handwrite the characters you have already been assigned for handwriting. Your paragraph must include:

  1. An opening sentence naming the habit you are describing, using 习惯 or 变化.
  2. A past/present contrast using 以前...，现在....
  3. At least one change-of-state 了 used accurately.
  4. At least one correct use of 不 or 没/没有, distinguishing general habit from a not-yet-true state.
  5. A connected sentence that continues your topic without repeating the subject unnecessarily, recycling your reference skills from Chapter 2.
  6. A closing sentence giving your opinion about the change.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • All six required elements are present and connected, not a disconnected list of sentences.
  • Time words such as 以前 and 现在 open their clause rather than following 是.
  • 了 is used to mark change of state, not confused with the perfective use of 了 for a single completed action.
  • 不 and 没/没有 are not confused with one another.
  • A Chinese period (。) ends each sentence, and commas (，) separate connected clauses.

Culture and Communication

Culture Note — Talking about routine and change

In many Mandarin-speaking contexts, discussing changes in daily routine — sleep schedule, meals, exercise, or study habits — is a common and comfortable small-talk topic among friends, classmates, and neighbors, and is often framed in terms of gradually "getting used to" (习惯) a new environment rather than as a sudden decision. How much detail people share about personal routines, and how directly they discuss habits they consider unhealthy or undisciplined, varies by relationship, generation, and individual personality.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Inserting 是 before a time word: say 以前我很晚睡觉, not a form that inserts 是 between the time word and the rest of the sentence.
  • Confusing change-of-state 了 with perfective 了: 我习惯了 means "I've become used to it" (a new state), not "I completed a habit."
  • Using 不 where 没/没有 is needed: a state that has not yet come about, especially with 还 (still/yet), takes 没 or 没有, not 不.
  • Confusing habit negation with ability negation: 不喝咖啡 (doesn't drink coffee, a habit) is different from 不会/不能/不可以, which describe skill, circumstance, or permission.

Chapter Summary

This chapter developed your ability to describe how your habits have changed. You added vocabulary for habits, change, and the pace of change, learned to contrast past and present routines with 以前 and 现在, used change-of-state 了 to mark a shift in situation, and expanded your control of 不 versus 没/没有 for habitual versus not-yet-true states. No major new grammar system beyond controlled change-of-state 了 and expanded negation was introduced.
