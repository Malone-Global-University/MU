目标、计划和建议

Volume II, Chapter 18: Goals, Plans, and Advice · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

This chapter continues Unit IV by moving from reporting news to talking about the future. You will learn how to state plans with 打算 and 计划, give advice with 应该 and 最好, and talk about future likelihood using 会 — a system you have already been consolidating since earlier chapters. You will also practice softening suggestions so that advice sounds like a recommendation rather than a command, using 吧, 你觉得...怎么样, and 要不要...还是... The chapter task asks you to describe a personal goal, explain your plan for it, and give and receive advice with a partner.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State personal plans and intentions using 打算 and 计划.
  2. Give advice and recommendations using 应该 and 最好.
  3. Talk about future likelihood using 会, and recognize 可能 in context.
  4. Soften suggestions and requests using 吧, 你觉得...怎么样, and 要不要...还是...
  5. Write a short paragraph describing a goal, a plan, and a piece of advice related to it.

Communication Scenario

A friend has asked what you are hoping to accomplish this year and what your plan looks like. You will listen to a short conversation about someone's goals, read a short message about a friend's plans, discuss your own goals and plans with a partner while giving each other advice, and write a paragraph describing a goal, your plan, and one piece of advice you have received or would give.

  1. You will listen to a short conversation about someone's goals and plans.
  2. You will read a short message describing a friend's plan and the advice a friend gave them.
  3. You will tell a partner about one of your own goals, your plan, and ask for their advice.
  4. You will write a short paragraph combining a goal, a plan, and a piece of advice.

发音 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus: 不 Sandhi in Advice and Plans

Giving advice and describing plans often involves negation with 不, and this chapter continues developing control of 不 sandhi in connected speech. Remember that 不 changes to second tone before a fourth-tone syllable, but keeps its normal fourth tone before first, second, and third tones.

WordPinyinNote
不建议bù jiànyìbù stays fourth tone before the second-tone syllable jiàn... note the tone of 议 (fourth tone) does not affect 不, since sandhi depends on the syllable immediately following 不
不要坚持bú yào jiānchíbù becomes second tone before the fourth-tone yào
不太好bú tài hǎobù becomes second tone before the fourth-tone tài

Practice this chapter's advice sentences aloud in pairs, listening for whether the syllable right after 不 is fourth tone. If it is, shift 不 to a rising second tone; otherwise, keep it as a falling fourth tone.

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

1. Stating Plans: 打算 and 计划

打算 and 计划 both describe plans, but they behave slightly differently. 打算 functions as a verb directly followed by another verb phrase. 计划 can function the same way, or as a noun meaning "plan."

PatternUseExample
打算 + verb phrasestate an intention我打算明年学开车。
计划 + verb phrasestate a plan, slightly more formal我们计划下个月去旅行。
我的计划是...introduce a plan as a noun我的计划是每天练习说中文。

打算 and 计划 both build on the intention system you already use with 要 and 想. Use 打算 for everyday spoken plans and 计划 when the plan is more organized or when you want to name it as a noun.

2. Giving Advice: 应该 and 最好

You have already consolidated 应该 (should) as a modal verb. This chapter adds 最好, which softens advice into a strong recommendation rather than an obligation.

你应该多运动。
你最好多运动。

You should exercise more. / You'd better exercise more (a stronger, more personal recommendation).

PatternUseExample
应该 + verb phrasegeneral advice or obligation你应该早点儿睡觉。
最好 + verb phrasestrong personal recommendation你最好先跟老师聊聊。
不应该 / 最好不要 + verb phrasenegative advice你最好不要一个人决定。

3. Future Likelihood with 会

会 remains your primary tool for talking about what is likely to happen. This chapter recycles and expands 会 into longer discourse about goals and plans.

如果你坚持练习，你的中文一定会进步。

If you keep practicing, your Chinese will definitely improve.

可能 (possibly) is receptive at this level: you may see or hear it in readings and dialogues, such as "明年我可能会去台湾", but 会 alone remains your productive tool for stating future likelihood.

4. Softening Suggestions: 吧, 你觉得...怎么样, and 要不要...还是...

A suggestion phrased as a flat statement can sound like a command. Mandarin speakers commonly soften suggestions using a sentence-final 吧, a question inviting the other person's opinion, or a choice question.

PatternUseExample
verb phrase + 吧soft suggestion我们先做一个小计划吧。
你觉得...怎么样？invite an opinion before advising你觉得这个计划怎么样？
要不要...还是...？offer a choice rather than a single directive你要不要先跟朋友聊聊，还是自己决定？

要不要...还是... builds on the choice-question pattern with 还是 that you have been developing since earlier chapters. Using a choice question to give advice keeps the tone respectful of the other person's own decision.

对话 — Model Dialogue

Model Dialogue: 我今年的目标

Context: two friends, informal register, talking over coffee about the year ahead.

小林：你今年有什么目标吗？
小陈：有。我打算把中文说得更好，我的计划是每天练习说半个小时。
小林：这个计划不错。你最好也多听中文新闻，这样进步会更快。
小陈：好建议。你觉得我应该先跟中文老师聊聊，还是自己先开始？
小林：我觉得你最好先跟老师聊聊，问问她有什么建议。
小陈：好，那我们先做一个小计划吧。如果我坚持练习，我相信我的中文一定会进步。

Xiao Lin: Do you have any goals this year? Xiao Chen: Yes. I'm planning to speak Chinese better — my plan is to practice speaking for half an hour every day. Xiao Lin: That's a good plan. You'd better also listen to Chinese news more, that way your progress will be faster. Xiao Chen: Good suggestion. Do you think I should talk to my Chinese teacher first, or start on my own first? Xiao Lin: I think you'd better talk to your teacher first and ask what advice she has. Xiao Chen: Okay, let's make a small plan first, then. If I stick with practicing, I believe my Chinese will definitely improve.

阅读 — Reading Practice

Reading Practice

Read the short message, then answer the questions.

给朋友的一条消息

我今年的目标是学会做饭。我打算每个星期学做一个新菜，我的计划是先从简单的菜开始。我朋友建议我先跟她一起做，她说这样比较容易学。她还说我最好写下每次做菜的问题，这样下次可以进步得更快。

虽然这个目标不容易，但是我决定要坚持。如果我每个星期都练习，我相信我的做饭技术一定会进步。你觉得我的计划怎么样？你有没有什么建议？
A message to a friend. My goal this year is to learn to cook. I plan to learn one new dish every week; my plan is to start with simple dishes. My friend suggested I cook with her first, since she says it's easier to learn that way. She also said I'd better write down the problems from each time I cook, so I can improve faster next time. Although this goal isn't easy, I've decided to stick with it. If I practice every week, I believe my cooking skills will definitely improve. What do you think of my plan? Do you have any advice?

写作 — Writing Workshop

Writing Workshop

Write a short paragraph (six to nine sentences) describing a personal goal, your plan for it, and a piece of advice.

写作任务 — Writing task: Type your paragraph with Pinyin input. Your paragraph must include:

  1. One goal stated clearly, using 目标.
  2. At least one plan expression (打算 or 计划).
  3. At least one piece of advice (应该 or 最好), given by you or by someone else.
  4. One future-likelihood sentence using 会.
  5. At least one softened suggestion or question (吧, 你觉得...怎么样, or 要不要...还是...).

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • The goal and plan are clearly distinguished from each other.
  • Advice uses 应该 or 最好 correctly, not a flat command.
  • 会 is used to express future likelihood; 可能 is only used if you are ready to treat it receptively.
  • At least one sentence softens a suggestion rather than stating it as an order.
  • Chinese punctuation (，。？！) is used correctly throughout.
  • No new grammar beyond this chapter and prior consolidated systems is required to complete the task.

Culture and Communication

Culture Note — Giving advice indirectly

Among many Mandarin speakers, direct advice softened with 你觉得...怎么样 or a choice question such as 要不要...还是... is often preferred over a flat command, especially between friends or colleagues of similar status. This habit respects the other person's ability to make their own decision while still offering a clear recommendation. The strength of 应该 versus 最好 can also shift depending on the closeness of the relationship and the seriousness of the topic; among close friends, 最好 often feels more natural and personal than 应该, which can sound slightly more like an obligation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Confusing 打算 and 计划 as nouns only: 打算 functions only as a verb before another verb phrase; 计划 can be a verb or a noun, but 打算 cannot stand alone as "my plan is..."
  • Treating 最好 as identical to 应该: 最好 is a strong personal recommendation, while 应该 carries more of a sense of obligation; they are not always interchangeable.
  • Overusing 可能 productively: 可能 is receptive at this level; use 会 to state future likelihood in your own speaking and writing.
  • Skipping softening language entirely: a string of 应该 statements with no 吧, 你觉得...怎么样, or choice question can sound like a list of commands rather than advice between equals.
  • Incorrect 不 sandhi: remember to shift 不 to second tone before a fourth-tone syllable, as in 不要 and 不太好.
