新的开始：我的生活

Volume II, Chapter 1: A New Start: My Life Now · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

Welcome to Volume II. This chapter does not introduce a new grammar system. Instead, it resets and expands everything you built in Volume I — identity, family, routine, location, ability, and simple reasons — into longer, more connected paragraphs about your life right now. You will practice linking short sentences into a single flowing account using words you already know, and you will begin reading and writing slightly longer character-only passages without heavy Pinyin support.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe your current life situation — where you live, study, or work — in a connected paragraph.
  2. Link several related sentences into one topic chain using 我、也、还, and reference words instead of repeating nouns.
  3. Recycle 是, 有, 在, and basic questions to build a fuller self-introduction than in Volume I.
  4. Read a short character-only paragraph about someone's daily life with minimal Pinyin support.
  5. Write a short connected paragraph describing your life now, using appropriate Chinese punctuation.

Communication Scenario

安娜 has just started a new semester and joins an online exchange group for Mandarin learners around the world. Everyone is asked to post a short update called "我的生活" (My Life) describing where they are now, what has changed, and what a typical day looks like.

  1. You will read 安娜's post about her new semester.
  2. You will listen to a short recording of a classmate introducing their current life.
  3. You will write your own connected "我的生活" post for the group.
  4. You will speak with a partner about what is new in your life this semester.

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Building a Topic Chain

No new grammar system is introduced in this chapter. Instead, you will practice a connected-discourse skill: linking several sentences about the same topic into one paragraph, instead of producing isolated sentences. This is called a topic chain — once a topic is established, later sentences can omit the subject or refer back to it with 我、他/她, or simply nothing at all.

Isolated sentences (Volume I style)

我是学生。我住在宿舍。我的专业是历史。 Wǒ shì xuésheng. Wǒ zhù zài sùshè. Wǒ de zhuānyè shì lìshǐ. I am a student. I live in a dorm. My major is history.

Connected topic chain (Volume II style)

我是大学生，现在住在宿舍，专业是历史。 Wǒ shì dàxuéshēng, xiànzài zhù zài sùshè, zhuānyè shì lìshǐ. I'm a university student; I currently live in a dorm, and my major is history.

Contrast with 以前 and 现在

以前我住在家里，现在我住在宿舍，也有一个同屋。 Yǐqián wǒ zhù zài jiā li, xiànzài wǒ zhù zài sùshè, yě yǒu yí ge tóngwū. Before I lived at home; now I live in a dorm, and I also have a roommate.

Notice that the subject 我 is stated once and then omitted or replaced with connectors such as 也 and 还. This is more natural Mandarin discourse than restating 我 in every sentence. No new particle or structure is required — only a new way of organizing familiar sentences.

对话 — Model Dialogue

Model Dialogue

安娜和大卫 — Anna and David:
安娜：大卫，你的生活现在怎么样？
大卫：还不错。我现在住在宿舍，有一个同屋，他很友好。
安娜：你的专业是什么？
大卫：我的专业是经济。以前我不太喜欢数学，现在我觉得还可以。
安娜：你这个学期忙不忙？
大卫：有点儿忙，但是周末比较轻松。你呢？你的生活有什么变化？
安娜：我现在也住在宿舍，也在学习中文。生活很不一样，但是我很喜欢。

This dialogue links several sentences about each speaker's life into one topic chain, recycling 是、有、在、比较, and 现在/以前, all already active from Volume I.

Writing Workshop

Write a connected paragraph of five to seven sentences titled 我的生活, describing your life now.

写作任务 — Writing task: Type your paragraph with Pinyin input if possible, or handwrite the characters you have already been assigned for handwriting. Your paragraph must include:

  1. An opening sentence stating who you are, using 是.
  2. A statement of where you live now, using 现在 and 在.
  3. A contrast with before, using 以前.
  4. At least one connected sentence that omits a repeated subject, forming a topic chain.
  5. A statement about your major or studies, using 专业 or 学习.
  6. A closing sentence about how you feel about your current life.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • All six required elements are present and connected, not a disconnected list of sentences.
  • At least one sentence omits the subject after it has already been established.
  • 现在 and 以前 are used accurately and are not confused with each other.
  • Only vocabulary and characters already taught in Volume I or this chapter are used.
  • A Chinese period (。) ends each sentence, and commas (，) separate connected clauses.

Culture and Communication

Culture Note — Talking about student life

Among many university students in Mainland China, dormitory life with assigned roommates is common, and conversations about one's 专业 (major) and daily schedule are a natural way to get to know someone new. Describing a busy week followed by a more relaxed weekend, as in this chapter's dialogue, is a familiar pattern in many student conversations, though individual schedules and living situations vary widely.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Repeating the subject in every sentence: once 我 is established, later sentences in the same topic chain may omit it or use 也/还 instead of restating it.
  • Confusing 现在 and 以前: 现在 marks the present situation; 以前 marks an earlier situation being contrasted with it.
  • Writing isolated sentences instead of a connected paragraph: use commas to link related clauses into one flowing sentence where natural, rather than a period after every short idea.
  • Overusing Pinyin as a crutch: try reading the character-only passage above before checking the gloss, rather than relying on a word-by-word Pinyin transcription.
