综合任务三：有根据的观点

Volume III, Chapter 15: Unit III Assessment — A Supported Position Using Evidence · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

The study group's café question is now settled, but the way they reached that decision is a model for this chapter's task: a position supported by evidence, tested against a counterpoint, and resolved through concession rather than confrontation. This chapter is a cumulative assessment. It introduces no new grammar, no new characters, and no new productive vocabulary beyond brief connecting language needed to link earlier systems together. Instead, it asks you to combine the stance, evidence, and counterargument language from Chapter 11, the comparison and evaluation language from Chapter 12, the data-commentary language from Chapter 13, and the discussion, suggestion, and concession language from Chapter 14 into one integrated, evidence-based position.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State a clear position on a practical question and support it with evidence or data.
  2. Compare at least two options or viewpoints using evaluative language.
  3. Acknowledge a counterpoint or concern using concession language such as 虽然...但是....
  4. Propose or respond to a suggestion that addresses the counterpoint.
  5. Reach and describe a conclusion, whether agreement or compromise, using 达成一致 or 妥协.
  6. Follow a multi-speaker discussion of a position and identify stance, evidence, and outcome.
  7. Read a data-supported discussion summary and identify its structure: position, evidence, counterpoint, and resolution.

Communication Scenario

The study group has been invited to share their café decision with a second group of students who are considering a similar arrangement for their own study sessions. Rather than simply announcing the decision, the group agrees to present it as a supported position: what they decided, what evidence supported it, what concern was raised, and how they resolved it. You will follow this presentation, then build a supported position of your own on a practical question, using evidence, addressing a counterpoint, and reaching a stated conclusion.

  1. You will listen to part of the group's presentation and identify their position, their evidence, and the counterpoint they addressed.
  2. You will read a written version of a supported position and identify its structure.
  3. You will present your own supported position on a practical question, addressing at least one counterpoint.
  4. You will write a short argumentative paragraph presenting a position, evidence, a counterpoint, and a conclusion.

发音 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus: Presenting a Position Aloud

This chapter reinforces intelligibility across longer stretches of connected speech rather than introducing a new pronunciation system. When presenting a supported position, keep tone-bearing syllables clear even as sentences lengthen, and use a brief pause after 虽然's clause before moving into 但是's clause, so the listener can track the two parts of the concession.

PhrasePractice Focus
虽然有一个问题，〈pause〉但是我们还是决定继续。brief pause between the two clauses; keep tones clear through the pause

我们同意妥协一下。calm falling tone on 同意, without a sharp or abrupt finish
语法复习 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns Review

Grammar and Sentence Patterns Review

No new grammar system is introduced in this chapter. The four patterns below, all previously activated, are the ones this chapter's tasks require you to combine.

1. Stating a Position with Evidence (Chapters 11 and 13)

A supported position typically opens with a clear stance, then cites evidence using 根据 or 数据显示.

根据数据，大部分人对咖啡馆的安排很满意。
数据显示，出席率一直在上升。

2. Comparing Options (Chapter 12)

比 comparison and 更/最 intensification support weighing one option against another.

咖啡馆比图书馆更方便，但是图书馆比咖啡馆安静。

3. Acknowledging a Counterpoint with 虽然...但是... (Chapter 14)

The concession pattern links an acknowledged concern to the position the speaker still wants to emphasize.

虽然高峰时间有点吵，但是大部分时间还是很安静的。

4. Proposing a Resolution and Stating the Outcome (Chapter 14)

建议 introduces a proposed resolution; 达成一致 or 妥协 states the outcome.

我们建议把开始时间往前调，最后大家达成了一致。

Putting It Together: The Four-Part Structure

A supported position in this chapter should follow this order: (1) position with evidence, (2) comparison if relevant, (3) counterpoint acknowledged with 虽然...但是..., and (4) proposed resolution and stated outcome.

根据数据，我们认为继续用咖啡馆是一个不错的选择：出席率和满意度都在上升，咖啡馆也比图书馆方便。虽然高峰时间有点吵，但是大部分时间还是很安静的。所以，我们建议把开始时间往前调一个小时，避开高峰时间。最后，大家达成了一致，同意妥协一下。

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: 向另一个小组介绍我们的决定

小林：大家好，我们想跟大家介绍一下我们小组的决定和过程。
小陈：根据我们的数据，出席率和满意度都在上升，所以我们一开始都同意继续用咖啡馆。
小马：不过，我补充了一点：虽然数据不错，但是高峰时间的噪音是一个问题，我不太同意什么都不改就继续。
小林：你说的也有道理，所以我们讨论了几个建议。
小马：我建议把开始时间往前调，避开高峰时间。
小陈：这个建议不错，比换地方更方便。
小林：所以，我们妥协一下：继续用咖啡馆，但是把见面时间从下午三点改成下午两点。
小马：我同意这个结论。
小林：最后，我们达成了一致。这就是我们的过程，也许对你们的决定有帮助。

阅读评估 — Reading Assessment

Reading Assessment: A Supported Position in Writing

Read the written position below, then answer the questions. 被 and 把 are glossed for recognition only, as in earlier chapters.

我们小组决定继续在咖啡馆见面。根据数据，出席率和满意度这几个月一直在上升，而且咖啡馆比图书馆方便，离大家住的地方都比较近。虽然高峰时间的噪音是一个问题，小马对此有点担心，但是大部分时间咖啡馆还是很安静的。经过讨论，我们建议把开始时间往前调一个小时，避开高峰时间；这个建议被大家认为比换地方更简单。最后，我们达成了一致，把见面时间从下午三点改成下午两点，其他安排保持不变。
Translation note: 被 in 这个建议被大家认为... marks a passive construction ("this suggestion is considered by everyone to be..."), glossed for recognition only, as in Chapter 14.
  1. 这篇短文的结论（立场）是什么？
  2. 支持这个结论的证据是什么？
  3. 小马提出的反对意见是什么？
  4. 最后大家是怎么解决这个问题的？

写作评估 — Writing Assessment

Writing Assessment: A Supported Position

Write a short argumentative paragraph presenting a position on a practical question, real or imagined. Your writing must include:

  1. An opening statement naming your position clearly.
  2. At least one piece of evidence or data, using 根据 or 数据显示.
  3. A comparison between at least two options, using 比 or 更/最.
  4. One 虽然...但是... sentence acknowledging a counterpoint.
  5. A closing conclusion using 达成一致 or 妥协.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • The position is clearly stated in the first sentence.
  • At least one piece of evidence supports the position.
  • A comparison between options is included and uses 比 or 更/最 correctly.
  • The 虽然...但是... sentence has both halves in the correct order.
  • The conclusion clearly uses 达成一致 or 妥协.
  • Chinese punctuation (，。？！) is used correctly throughout.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Presenting a group decision to outsiders

When a group presents a decision to people outside the group, it is common in many Mandarin-speaking academic and workplace settings to describe not only the final decision but also the process that led to it, including any disagreement and how it was resolved. This can make the decision seem more considered and can help the listening group avoid a similar disagreement. How much process detail is shared still depends on the setting, the relationship between the groups, and individual style.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Presenting evidence without a stated position: a supported position needs a clear stance before or alongside the evidence, not evidence alone.
  • Skipping the counterpoint: a position that never acknowledges a concern with 虽然...但是... can sound one-sided; this chapter's task requires at least one counterpoint.
  • Leaving the outcome unstated: end with a clear conclusion using 达成一致 or 妥协, not just a list of opinions.
  • Introducing new grammar or vocabulary beyond this chapter's review list: this is a synthesis task; rely on the systems from Chapters 11–14 rather than new structures.
