中英之间的意义转换

Volume III, Chapter 17: Mediation Between Chinese and English · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

The student magazine from Chapter 16 liked the interview so much that it wants to publish a short bilingual version for readers who read only English. That means someone has to decide, sentence by sentence, what to keep, what to shorten, and what to explain differently for a reader who doesn't share the Chinese-speaking readers' background knowledge. This chapter treats that work — summary, paraphrase, and functional translation — as a skill of its own, one that goes beyond simply substituting English words for Chinese ones. You will practice condensing a Chinese passage into a shorter Chinese summary, restating ideas in different Chinese phrasing, and adjusting a message for a reader who needs more context than the original audience did.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Summarize a short Chinese passage in your own words, keeping the main ideas and dropping non-essential detail.
  2. Paraphrase a sentence or short passage using different vocabulary and structures while preserving its meaning.
  3. Identify information a source text assumes its reader already knows, and decide whether a new audience needs it explained.
  4. Produce a short functional translation or adapted restatement suited to a specified audience.
  5. Distinguish close translation from summary and from free adaptation, and choose the right one for a given task.
  6. Read a short source passage and a paraphrase of it, and evaluate whether the paraphrase is faithful and appropriately adjusted.
  7. Present a short mediated explanation of a Chinese-language text to an audience who does not read Chinese.

Communication Scenario

The magazine's editor has asked the study group to help prepare an English-language sidebar based on the Chapter 16 interview and commentary, for an international newsletter that many readers who don't read Chinese will see. The editor reminds everyone that a sidebar is not a word-for-word translation — it is a shorter, adapted version that gives an outside reader enough context to understand the original discussion. You will read the Chinese source material again, decide what needs explaining for an outside reader, and produce a short mediated version of it, both in speech and in writing.

  1. You will listen to a short planning conversation about what the sidebar should include and what it can leave out.
  2. You will read a source passage together with a paraphrase of it and evaluate what changed and why.
  3. You will present a short spoken explanation of a Chinese passage for a reader who doesn't read Chinese.
  4. You will write a short mediated paragraph based on a Chinese source text.

发音 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus: Explanatory Pacing

No new sound system is introduced in this chapter. The focus is on pacing and phrasing when explaining or restating something for a listener who needs extra context. When you insert a short explanation into a longer sentence — for example, a brief aside marked by 也就是说 (that is to say) — slow down slightly on the explanatory phrase and pause briefly before and after it, so the listener can tell that it is added context rather than part of the original claim.

PhrasePractice Focus
也就是说，……brief pause before and after; signals an inserted explanation, not the main claim
简单来说，这段话的意思是……even pacing on 简单来说，slightly slower on 意思是 to highlight the restated core meaning
对英语读者来说，这个词需要解释一下。light stress on 需要解释一下 to flag that an explanation is coming, without sounding uncertain
语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Summarizing, Paraphrasing, and Adjusting for Audience

This chapter introduces no major new grammar system. It develops a small, functional cluster of expressions for mediating meaning, building on the reported-information language from Chapter 4 and the qualifying language from Chapter 16.

1. Introducing a Summary

概括 and 简单来说 introduce a condensed restatement of a longer passage.

简单来说，这段话主要是在说，年轻人跟父母那一代的生活方式不太一样。

2. Signaling a Paraphrase or Clarification

也就是说 (that is to say) introduces a restatement in different words, often adding context a new audience may need.

"内卷"是一个网络流行词，也就是说，它不是正式的书面语，而是年轻人常用的说法。

3. Deciding What to Keep or Explain (review, Chapter 4 and Chapter 16)

省略 and 保留 describe editorial choices; 对……来说 frames a choice from a specific reader's point of view.

对不了解这个背景的读者来说，这个词最好解释一下，不能直接省略。

4. Naming the Approach: 直译 versus 意译

These terms distinguish a close, word-for-word rendering from a sense-based restatement, and support explaining why a particular choice was made.

这句话直译不太自然，意译更容易让英语读者理解。

Putting It Together

A responsible piece of mediated writing in this chapter should: (1) identify the intended reader (对象), (2) summarize or paraphrase rather than translate word for word when a literal rendering would confuse the reader, (3) explain background the new reader may not share, and (4) note explicitly when something has been simplified or omitted.

这篇短文的读者对象是不太了解中国网络文化的英语读者。简单来说，短文讨论的是"内卷"这个词，也就是一种越来越激烈但结果并没有变好的竞争现象。对这些读者来说，"内卷"这个词需要解释一下，因为它是网络流行语，不是正式的书面语。有些细节，比如具体的城市名字，跟主要意思关系不大，可以省略；但是"这不能代表所有年轻人"这句话很重要，应该保留。

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: 编辑会议 — Editorial Planning Conversation

编辑：我们要把上次的采访和短文改写成一个给英语读者看的简短版本，你们觉得应该怎么做？
小林：我觉得不能直接翻译，因为有些词，比如"内卷"，英语读者可能完全不知道是什么意思。
编辑：对，这种词需要解释一下背景，不能只用一个英语词代替。
小陈：那具体的城市名字呢？采访里提到的那些细节？
小马：我觉得可以省略，因为这些细节对理解主要意思没有太大帮助；但是"不能代表所有年轻人"这句话应该保留，因为这是整篇文章最重要的提醒。
编辑：说得对。简单来说，我们的目标不是逐字翻译，而是让读者对象——也就是不了解这个背景的英语读者——也能明白这段讨论的意思。

原文： 最近，"内卷"这个词在网上很常见，用来形容一种越来越激烈、但结果并没有变得更好的竞争现象。一般来说，这个词常常被年轻人用来讨论学习和工作中的压力。这个现象在不同的城市、不同的行业里，情况也不完全一样，所以我们在讨论的时候，需要小心，不要把一个说法当成所有人的经验。

写作练习 — Writing Workshop

Writing Workshop: A Short Mediated Paragraph

Write a short paragraph that summarizes and adapts a Chinese source passage (your own or one from this book) for a specified reader who does not share the original audience's background. Your writing must include:

  1. An opening sentence naming your reader (读者对象) and the source passage's main topic.
  2. A one-sentence summary using 简单来说 or 概括.
  3. At least one clarifying restatement, using 也就是说 or 解释.
  4. An explicit note about at least one thing you chose to 省略 and one thing you chose to 保留, and why.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • The intended reader and source topic are clearly named in the first sentence.
  • A one-sentence summary appears, using 简单来说 or 概括.
  • At least one restatement is signaled with 也就是说 or 解释.
  • The paragraph explicitly notes at least one omission and one retained point, with a reason for each.
  • Chinese punctuation (，。？！) is used correctly throughout.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Mediation is a skill, not a shortcut

Traditional discussions of Chinese-to-English translation sometimes invoke the classical three-part standard 信达雅 (xìn dá yǎ) — faithfulness, fluency, and elegance — as a benchmark for translation quality. This chapter does not ask you to apply that standard formally; it is mentioned here only as background for readers who may encounter the term. What matters at this stage is simpler: a good mediator decides, deliberately and transparently, what a specific new reader needs explained, what can be safely shortened, and what must be kept because it changes the meaning if dropped. This is different from either a mechanical word-for-word rendering or a loose retelling that drifts from the source. Being explicit about your choices — as the editorial team does in this chapter's Model Text — is itself part of responsible mediation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Translating word for word without checking naturalness: a 直译 that confuses the new reader defeats the purpose; prefer 意译 when the literal rendering is unclear.
  • Silently dropping important content: if you 省略 something, make sure it does not change the reader's understanding of the main claim.
  • Forgetting the reader's background: always ask what your 对象 already knows before deciding what needs 解释.
  • Treating summary and paraphrase as the same task: 概括 shortens a passage to its main ideas; paraphrase restates a passage, often at similar length, in different words.
