综合任务一：故事和分析

Volume III, Chapter 5: Unit I Assessment — Story and Analysis · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

This chapter is the Unit I assessment. It introduces no new grammar, no new characters beyond ordinary recycling, and no new pronunciation system. Instead, it asks you to bring together everything Chapters 1 through 4 built: telling a connected story about a past experience (Chapter 2), explaining that experience using cause, result, and purpose (Chapter 3), and reporting and summarizing what someone else said about a similar experience (Chapter 4). You will listen to and read source material, then produce your own connected story paired with a short analytical summary — in speech and in writing.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Narrate a personal experience or change in circumstances using appropriate aspect particles (了, 过, 着) and topic chains.
  2. Explain the causes, results, and purpose behind that experience using 因为/所以, 为了, and 结果.
  3. Report and summarize what another person said about a related experience, using 说/觉得/认为, 听说, 据...说, 简单来说, and 换句话说.
  4. Interpret controlled listening and reading input and respond with an organized spoken and written analysis.
  5. Complete an integrated listening, reading, speaking, and writing task that combines narration and analytical summary at the B1–B2 boundary.

Communication Scenario

A community website is collecting short "life change" stories. You will listen to one story, read a second, and then produce your own combined story-and-analysis piece for the same collection: what happened, why it happened, and what someone else said or thought about a similar situation.

  1. You will listen to a short personal narrative and identify the sequence of events and their causes.
  2. You will read a second personal narrative and identify its main points and the writer's reasoning.
  3. You will orally narrate and explain your own (real or invented) experience, incorporating reported information from a friend or family member.
  4. You will write a connected story-and-analysis paragraph set that narrates, explains, and reports/summarizes.

发音 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus: Sustained Tone Accuracy Across a Longer Turn

This chapter introduces no new pronunciation system. The assessment focus is maintaining accurate tones, tone sandhi, and neutral tone across a longer, connected turn — a full narrated-and-explained story — rather than in isolated sentences. Pay particular attention to third-tone sandhi in strings of low tones, the toneless 说 in 听说, and clear separation of the reporting verb 说 from the reported clause that follows it.

FeatureReview Note
third-tone sandhiwatch for strings such as 我也想 where more than one third tone occurs in sequence
toneless 说 in 听说keep 说 light and unstressed in 听说, but full and clear in 他说
rhythm across connectors因为/所以, 为了, and 结果 should each begin a clear breath group, not blur into the surrounding clause

Tone accuracy remains a structural requirement for this assessment, but feedback should prioritize intelligibility across the full turn rather than perfect isolated-syllable production.

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Combining Narration, Explanation, and Report

This chapter introduces no new grammar. It requires you to combine three systems already consolidated in Chapters 1 through 4 within a single connected piece of communication.

1. Narration (Chapter 2 Review)

PatternUseExample
Topic + time expression + 了/过/着 + eventsequence events and background in a personal story大学的时候，我在一家小公司实习过。

2. Cause, Result, and Purpose (Chapter 3 Review)

PatternUseExample
因为...，所以...explain why something happened因为工作压力太大，所以我决定换一个方向。
为了 + goal，+ actionexplain the purpose behind a decision为了有更多的选择，我去学了一个新专业。
结果，...introduce the outcome of a decision or event结果，我找到了一份更适合我的工作。

3. Reporting and Summarizing (Chapter 4 Review)

PatternUseExample
Person + 说/觉得/认为 + clausereport what someone said or felt about a similar experience我朋友说，她也遇到过同样的问题。
听说 / 据...说mark secondhand information听说很多人都做过这样的决定。
简单来说 / 换句话说summarize or restate the point简单来说，这个改变虽然不轻松，但是很值得。

The assessment task requires you to move fluidly among these three systems within one connected piece — narrating events, explaining why they happened, and reporting what someone else thought about a similar experience — rather than treating them as separate, unrelated exercises.

对话 — Model Dialogue

Model Story and Analysis: 我的一次重要改变

Context: a learner tells a friend about a past decision, combining narration, explanation, and a reported comparison; semi-formal conversational register.

小雨：你上次说你换过工作，能跟我讲讲吗？
大明：可以。大学毕业以后，我先在一家大公司工作了两年。因为工作越来越没有意思，所以我开始考虑换方向。
小雨：那你后来做了什么？
大明：为了找到更适合自己的工作，我去参加了一个培训项目。结果，我认识了现在的老板，他们公司正好需要我这样的人。
小雨：简单来说，这次改变是成功的？
大明：对，我觉得是这样。我听说很多同事也做过类似的决定，据一个朋友说，他换工作以后也比以前开心多了。换句话说，敢改变是值得的。

Xiaoyu: You mentioned before that you changed jobs — can you tell me about it? Daming: Sure. After I graduated from university, I worked at a large company for two years first. Because the work kept becoming less interesting, I started thinking about changing direction. Xiaoyu: So what did you do after that? Daming: In order to find work that suited me better, I went and took part in a training program. As a result, I met my current boss — his company happened to need someone like me. Xiaoyu: Simply put, this change was successful? Daming: Right, I think so. I've heard that a lot of colleagues have made similar decisions, and according to a friend, he's also been much happier since changing jobs. In other words, it's worth it to be brave enough to change.

阅读 — Reading Practice

Reading Practice

Read the paragraph below, then answer the questions. Pinyin support remains reduced in this reading, continuing from earlier chapters in this unit.

周明大学的专业是会计，毕业以后在一家银行工作了三年。因为他觉得每天的工作越来越没有挑战，所以他开始考虑一个新的方向。为了给自己更多的选择，他利用晚上的时间学习编程。结果，他在一家小公司找到了一份和以前完全不同的工作。他说，虽然一开始很不容易，压力也不小，但是他现在每天都学到新东西，觉得很有意思。据他的一个同事说，公司里有好几个人都是从别的行业转过来的。换句话说，周明的经验说明，改变方向虽然需要勇气，但是并不是不可能的事。
Zhou Ming majored in accounting in university and worked at a bank for three years after graduating. Because he felt his daily work was becoming less and less challenging, he started considering a new direction. To give himself more options, he used his evenings to study programming. As a result, he found a job completely different from his previous one at a small company. He said that although it wasn't easy at first and the pressure was considerable, he now learns something new every day and finds it interesting. According to one of his colleagues, several people at the company transitioned in from other industries. In other words, Zhou Ming's experience shows that changing direction takes courage, but it isn't impossible.
  1. 周明为什么决定学编程？（用因为/所以回答）
  2. 他为了改变方向做了什么？结果怎么样？
  3. 同事怎么看公司里像周明这样的人？
写作 — Writing Workshop

Writing Workshop: Story and Analysis Paragraph Set

Write a connected story-and-analysis piece (eight to ten sentences) about a real or invented experience of your own, following the model of the Reading Practice passage above.

写作任务 — Writing task: Type your paragraphs using Pinyin input. Your writing must include:

  1. A narrated sequence of at least two events, using appropriate aspect particles (了, 过, 着).
  2. At least one cause/result explanation with 因为...所以... and one purpose or result sentence with 为了 or 结果.
  3. At least one reported comparison using 说/觉得/认为, 听说, or 据...说, describing what someone else said or thought about a similar experience.
  4. One closing summary sentence using 简单来说 or 换句话说.
  5. No direct quotation — report and paraphrase rather than copying exact words.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • The story presents a clear, connected sequence of events with appropriate aspect particles.
  • The explanation clearly connects cause, purpose, and result to the events narrated.
  • Reported information is clearly marked and distinguished from the writer's own experience.
  • The closing sentence accurately summarizes the point of the story.
  • Chinese punctuation (，。？！) is used correctly throughout, with no unnecessary quotation marks.
  • No grammar or vocabulary beyond what was consolidated in Chapters 1 through 4 is required to complete the task.

Culture and Communication

Culture Note — Sharing personal stories in Mandarin

When telling a personal story of change or difficulty in Mandarin, many speakers pair the narration with an explanation of reasoning (因为/所以, 为了) and often close with a brief evaluative summary (简单来说, 换句话说), rather than leaving the listener to draw their own conclusion. This pattern is common in casual conversation, interviews, and short written accounts alike, though the amount of explicit explanation and summary can vary by speaker, setting, and how personal the topic feels.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Narrating without explaining: a story-and-analysis task needs both the sequence of events and the reasoning behind them — do not leave out 因为/所以, 为了, or 结果.
  • Explaining without narrating: do not replace the story with a list of reasons; events still need clear time sequencing and aspect marking.
  • Dropping the reported comparison: the assessment requires connecting your story to what someone else said or experienced, not just describing your own experience in isolation.
  • Introducing untaught grammar or vocabulary: this chapter should showcase fluent, connected use of systems from Chapters 1 through 4, not new material.
  • Skipping the closing summary: end with 简单来说 or 换句话说 to give the piece a clear analytical close, not just a final event.
