Volume III, Chapter 20: Final B2 Portfolio · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

This is the final chapter of Volume III. The magazine's editorial team has invited the study group to assemble a small portfolio drawing together work from across the volume: a narrated-and-analyzed experience, a piece of public-information writing, a supported opinion, and the freshly revised report from Chapters 18 and 19. This chapter introduces no new grammar, vocabulary domain, or character set. It is a cumulative integrated assessment: it verifies that the systems developed across Chapters 1 through 19 can be coordinated into a coherent, independent body of B2-level Mandarin work, presented and defended in an interview-style conversation with the editor.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Select and organize a small portfolio of your own Mandarin work representing narration, public communication, argumentation, and revision.
  2. Introduce and contextualize your portfolio orally for an audience unfamiliar with the individual pieces.
  3. Answer follow-up questions about your choices, sources, and revisions in connected Mandarin.
  4. Read and respond to a capstone-level text that draws on news, opinion, and narrative registers together.
  5. Demonstrate control of tones, characters, word order, particles, aspect, measure words, complements, and register across an extended task.
  6. Reflect, in Mandarin, on your own growth from B1 to B2 across the volume.

Communication Scenario

The editor who has been guiding the study group's work throughout the unit has asked each member to assemble a short portfolio and present it at a final meeting. The portfolio should show a range of the writing and speaking developed in Chapters 16 through 19 — cultural commentary, mediation, research reporting, and revision — alongside at least one earlier narrative piece. At the meeting, each learner will introduce their portfolio, answer the editor's questions, and reflect on how their Mandarin has changed since the beginning of the volume.

  1. You will listen to a model portfolio presentation and the editor's follow-up questions.
  2. You will read a short capstone text that combines narrative, opinion, and public-information elements.
  3. You will assemble and present your own portfolio, responding to questions about it.
  4. You will write a short reflective statement on your progress across Volume III.

语法复习 — Systems Review

Systems Review: Coordinating What You Already Know

This chapter introduces no new productive grammar. It requires learners to coordinate systems already consolidated across Volume III: topic-comment organization, aspect (了/过/着/在), complements, measure words, comparison, concession (虽然/但是, 尽管), 因为/所以 and 为了 causal and purpose structures, reported and summarized information, and formal register choices between spoken and written style.

1. Framing a Portfolio Item

Use 这一篇是关于...的 to introduce what a piece is about, and 我写这篇的时候，... to give brief context.

这一篇是关于学生压力的调查报告；我写这篇的时候，还在学习怎么用数据支持自己的看法。

2. Explaining Growth Across the Volume

Recycle comparison (Chapter 12) and 虽然...但是... (Chapter 14) to describe change over time.

虽然我一开始写议论文的时候常常东拉西扯，但是现在我可以更清楚地说明原因和结果。

3. Answering Follow-Up Questions

Reuse concession, justification, and mediation language from Chapters 11, 14, and 17 to respond to unplanned questions about your choices.

您问得很好。我选这一篇，是因为它最能代表我这学期在论证和修改方面的进步。

Putting It Together

A complete capstone presentation should: (1) introduce the portfolio as a whole, (2) frame each piece with its purpose and context, (3) reflect on growth using comparison and concession, and (4) respond to questions using previously consolidated discourse strategies rather than new grammar.

范文 — Model Text

Model Text: 一段作品集介绍 — A Portfolio Introduction

大家好，这是我这学期的中文作品集，一共有四篇。第一篇是我在第二课写的一段个人经历，讲的是我第一次一个人旅行的故事；这篇代表我叙事能力的起点。第二篇是一篇公共信息类的短文，是关于我们学校新规定的介绍。第三篇是一篇有根据的议论文，讨论的是网络使用时间对学生的影响；虽然一开始我觉得用数据说明观点很难，但是经过练习，我现在可以比较自然地把证据和自己的看法连起来。第四篇是上周编辑帮我修改过的调查报告，从这篇可以看出我在简洁和正式风格方面的进步。总的来说，这学期我的收获不只是学到了新的语法和词汇，更重要的是学会了怎么把它们组织成完整、有条理的中文表达。

练习 — Guided Practice

Guided Practice

  1. Frame a piece: 这一篇是关于______的。
  2. Give context: 我写这篇的时候，______。
  3. Describe growth: 虽然我一开始______，但是现在我______。
  4. Respond to a question: 您问得很好。我选这一篇，是因为______。

Listening Component

Listening script for independent use. Read aloud slowly, then at a natural pace, or have a fluent speaker record it.

编辑和小林的作品集讨论 — Excerpt from a conversation between the editor and a student presenting a portfolio:
编辑：你的作品集整理得不错。能不能说说，这四篇里你最满意哪一篇？
小林：我最满意第三篇，就是关于网络使用时间的议论文，因为我第一次比较成功地用调查数据支持了自己的观点。

小林：应该是第一篇个人经历吧，因为那时候我的句子比较简单，连接也不太自然。
编辑：明白了，这正好说明你这学期的进步。你打算下学期继续加强哪方面？
小林：我想继续练习正式书面语，因为这是我觉得最有挑战性的部分。

Reading Component: A Capstone Text

Read the short passage below, which combines narrative, public-information, and opinion elements at the level expected for the end of Volume III. Answer the questions that follow.

上个月，学校公布了一项新规定：图书馆晚上十点以后不再对学生开放。这个消息一公布，很多同学都表示不同意，因为不少人习惯在晚上安静地复习功课。学生会做了一次小调查，结果显示百分之七十的学生希望图书馆能延长开放时间。虽然学校表示这样做是为了节省能源，但是学生会认为，学习需要和节能措施应该可以找到一个平衡点。目前，学生会已经把调查结果交给了学校，正在等待进一步的答复。

  2. 学生为什么不同意这项规定？
  3. 调查结果显示了什么？
  4. 如果你是学生会的成员，你会怎么向学校提出建议？请用"虽然...但是..."说明你的看法。

写作练习 — Writing Component

Writing Component: Assembling and Reflecting on Your Portfolio

Complete the following capstone writing task. It draws on skills from across the volume rather than introducing anything new.

  1. Select three to four pieces of your own Mandarin writing from Volume III, including the revised report from Chapters 18–19 and at least one earlier narrative or opinion piece.
  2. Write a short introduction (3–5 sentences) presenting the portfolio as a whole.
  3. Write a one- or two-sentence frame for each piece, stating what it is and why you included it.
  4. Write a reflective paragraph describing your progress from Chapter 1 to Chapter 20, using at least one comparison structure and one 虽然...但是... structure.

作品集清单 — Portfolio checklist:

  • The portfolio includes at least one narrative, one public-information or argumentative piece, and one revised piece.
  • Each piece has a clear framing sentence explaining what it is and why it was chosen.
  • The reflective paragraph names specific, concrete changes rather than only general statements like "I improved."
  • Characters, punctuation, and register are checked using the editing habits developed in Chapter 19.
  • The whole portfolio reads as connected, organized writing rather than a disconnected list.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Portfolios and self-reflection in Mandarin-language academic settings

In many Mandarin-medium academic and professional settings, presenting a collection of one's own work and reflecting on it aloud — sometimes called 述职 in workplace contexts or simply a course wrap-up in classroom contexts — is a common way for students, employees, and researchers to demonstrate growth to a supervisor, teacher, or editor. Being asked direct follow-up questions about one's choices is generally understood as a normal, even expected, part of this kind of presentation across many institutions and regions, not as a challenge to the presenter's competence. Framing a self-reflection in terms of specific, concrete change — rather than only general praise or general self-criticism — is typically valued in these settings.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Presenting pieces without framing them: simply listing what each piece is about, without saying why it was chosen, weakens the presentation; use 这一篇是关于...的 and explain your reason for including it.
  • Describing growth only in vague terms: "我进步了很多" alone is not specific enough for a capstone reflection; support it with a concrete comparison, as in the Model Text.
  • Treating follow-up questions as a threat rather than a normal part of the task: prepare to answer at least one or two unplanned questions about your choices, using language already consolidated in earlier chapters.
  • Introducing new, untaught vocabulary or grammar under pressure: a strong capstone performance draws on what has already been consolidated rather than reaching for unfamiliar structures.
