原因、结果和目的

Volume III, Chapter 3: Causes, Results, and Purposes · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

Chapter 2 taught you to narrate a change over time. This chapter asks you to explain that change: why it happened, what it led to, and what you were trying to achieve. You will organize explanations using 因为...所以..., state purpose with 为了, and mark outcomes with 结果. These are not new sentence types — reason-result and purpose framing have been part of your Mandarin since Volume II — but this chapter develops them into fuller, more explicit explanatory writing and speech, and connects them to the narration skills from Chapter 2.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Explain a cause and its result using 因为...所以... in both spoken and written form.
  2. State a goal or purpose behind an action using 为了.
  3. Report an outcome using 结果, distinguishing it from a simple sequence marked by 后来.
  4. Combine cause, purpose, and result in a short explanatory paragraph that also narrates events.
  5. Complete an integrated listening, reading, speaking, and writing task that explains why something happened.

Communication Scenario

A classmate wants to know why you made a recent decision — choosing a class, changing a habit, or making a change like the one you described in Chapter 2. You will listen to someone explain why they started a new habit, read a short passage explaining why someone made a decision, practice explaining your own reasons and goals with a partner, and write a short paragraph that explains a decision using cause, purpose, and result.

  1. You will listen to someone explain the reason and result behind a decision.
  2. You will read a passage that explains a decision using cause, purpose, and outcome.
  3. You will practice explaining your own reasons and goals with a partner.
  4. You will write a short paragraph explaining a decision you made.
生词 — Core Vocabulary

发音 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus: Rhythm in Explanatory Sentences

This chapter continues the -n versus -ng work from Chapters 1 and 2, and begins paying closer attention to syllable rhythm across longer explanatory sentences, especially those built from two clauses joined by 因为...所以... or a purpose clause with 为了.

PhraseNote
原因 yuányīnkeep both syllables level and clear; do not rush the second syllable
因为...所以... yīnwèi...suǒyǐ...give each clause its own small pause; do not run the two clauses together as one breath group
为了健康 wèile jiànkāngpractice the -n final in 健 and the -ng final in 康 without blending them

As explanatory sentences grow longer, break them into clear clause-sized breath groups rather than speaking the whole sentence in one uninterrupted rush. This is a continued refinement target, not a new sound system.

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Explaining Cause, Purpose, and Result

This chapter develops explanatory language by expanding the reason-result and purpose systems you already use into fuller, more explicit explanations, and by adding 结果 as a way to mark outcomes distinct from simple time sequence.

1. Explaining Cause and Result with 因为...所以...

PatternUseExample
因为 + reason，所以 + resultexplicitly connect a cause to its result因为工作的压力越来越大，所以我决定换一份工作。
因为 or 所以 aloneuse only one half when the other is already clear from context所以我决定换一份工作。

2. Stating Purpose with 为了

PatternUseExample
为了 + goal，Subject + Verb Phrasestate the purpose behind an action, placed before the main clause为了找到更好的机会，我开始学习新的技能。

3. Marking Outcomes with 结果

PatternUseExample
..., 结果 + outcomeintroduce an outcome, often one that was not fully expected or planned我申请了那份工作，结果真的被录取了。

结果 differs from 后来 (Chapter 2): 后来 simply continues a time sequence, while 结果 frames what follows as the outcome of what came before. Compare: 后来我换了工作 (a next event) versus 结果我换了工作 (the change is presented as a consequence).

4. Combining Cause, Purpose, and Result

PatternUseExample
为了..., 因为..., 所以..., 结果...build a short, fully connected explanation across several clauses为了减少压力，我开始每天运动。因为坚持了三个月，所以身体好了很多，结果我现在每天都很有精神。

The new skill in this chapter is combining cause, purpose, and result markers into one coherent explanation — not new individual grammar points, but a fuller, more deliberate use of connectors you have already met.

对话 — Model Dialogue

Model Explanation: 我为什么换了工作

Context: two classmates talk after class about why one of them changed jobs; casual, familiar register.

小美：你上次说你换工作了，能告诉我原因吗？
大伟：可以啊。因为以前那份工作的压力越来越大，所以我决定找一个新机会。
小美：那你为什么选了现在这份工作？
大伟：为了有更多学习新技能的机会，我申请了这份工作，结果真的被录取了。
小美：结果你现在觉得怎么样？
大伟：结果比我想的还好，工作虽然也不轻松，但是我觉得很有意思，压力也没有以前那么大了。

Xiaomei: You mentioned last time that you changed jobs — can you tell me the reason? Dawei: Sure. Because the pressure at my old job kept getting bigger, I decided to look for a new opportunity. Xiaomei: So why did you choose this current job? Dawei: In order to have more chances to learn new skills, I applied for this job, and it turned out I actually got hired. Xiaomei: So how do you feel now, as a result? Dawei: It turned out better than I expected — the work isn't exactly easy either, but I find it interesting, and the pressure isn't as big as before.

练习 — Guided Practice

Guided Practice

A. Complete each sentence with 因为, 所以, 为了, or 结果.

  1. ______工作的压力越来越大，______我决定换一份工作。
  2. ______找到更好的机会，我开始学习新的技能。
  3. 我申请了那份工作，______真的被录取了。

B. Combine each pair of sentences using 因为...所以... or 为了.

  1. 工作的压力越来越大。我决定找一个新机会。（因为...所以...）
  2. 我想减少压力。我开始每天运动。（为了）

C. Write two or three sentences explaining a decision you made, using at least one cause-result clause and one purpose clause, and ending with a sentence marked by 结果.

阅读 — Reading Practice

Reading Practice

Read the paragraph below, then answer the questions. Pinyin support remains reduced in this reading, continuing from Chapters 1 and 2.

我叫王强，去年决定回学校继续念书。因为我在原来的公司做了五年一样的工作，越来越没有意思，所以我开始想找别的机会。为了有更多的选择，我决定去学一个新专业。刚开始家人不太理解我的决定，后来我跟他们解释了原因，他们也就慢慢支持我了。结果我现在一边学习，一边在一家小公司实习，虽然比以前忙，但是我觉得这是一个正确的决定。
My name is Wang Qiang, and last year I decided to go back to school to continue studying. Because I had done the same job at my original company for five years and it kept getting less interesting, I started wanting to look for other opportunities. In order to have more choices, I decided to go study a new field. At first my family didn't quite understand my decision, but later I explained the reason to them, and they gradually came to support me. As a result, I'm now studying while also interning at a small company, and although it's busier than before, I feel this was the right decision.

  2. 王强为了什么决定去学一个新专业？
  3. 王强的决定，结果怎么样？
写作 — Writing Workshop

Writing Workshop: Explaining a Decision

Write a connected explanatory paragraph (six to eight sentences) explaining a decision you made, following the model in this chapter.

写作任务 — Writing task: Type your paragraph using Pinyin input. Your paragraph must include:

  1. One sentence using 因为...所以... to explain the reason behind your decision.
  2. One sentence using 为了 to state your goal.
  3. One sentence using 结果 to describe an outcome, distinct from a simple 后来 sequence.
  4. At least two words from this chapter's new vocabulary (原因, 结果, 目的, 决定, 机会, 压力, 为了, 于是).
  5. A closing sentence summarizing how you feel about the decision now.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • The paragraph reads as one connected explanation, not a list of separate sentences.
  • 因为...所以..., 为了, and 结果 are each used correctly and in a context where they are appropriate.
  • 结果 is used to mark an outcome, not simply to continue a time sequence better expressed with 后来.
  • At least two new vocabulary words from this chapter appear naturally.
  • Chinese punctuation (，。？！) is used correctly throughout.
  • No grammar beyond what was consolidated in earlier chapters and reviewed in this chapter is required to complete the task.

Culture and Communication

Culture Note — Explaining decisions to family and friends

When explaining a significant personal decision, such as changing jobs or fields of study, many Mandarin speakers take care to lay out the reasoning clearly and patiently for family members, since group input and understanding are often valued when a decision affects the wider family. This pattern is common in many everyday explanations, though individual families and speakers vary, and some prefer a brief, direct account, especially among close friends or in casual settings.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using both 因为 and 所以 when only one is needed: in natural speech, one half is often dropped once the connection is clear from context; using both every time can sound overly formal or repetitive.
  • Confusing 结果 and 后来: 后来 simply continues a time sequence, while 结果 frames what follows as a consequence of what came before.
  • Placing 为了 after the main clause: the purpose clause with 为了 normally comes before the main clause it explains, not after it.
  • Treating 因为 as equivalent to English "because" in every position: 因为 introduces a reason clause and does not need a comma-linked "so" in English, but Mandarin generally pairs it with 所以 when both halves are stated.
  • Introducing untaught grammar or vocabulary: this chapter should showcase fluent explanation using systems you already control, combined in a new way.
