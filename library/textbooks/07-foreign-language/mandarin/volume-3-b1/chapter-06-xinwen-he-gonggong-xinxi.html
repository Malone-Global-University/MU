新闻和公共信息

Volume III, Chapter 6: News and Public Information · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

This chapter opens Unit II and moves your Mandarin from personal narration into the language of news and public information. You will learn how Mandarin news items and public announcements are typically framed — who is reporting the information, where it comes from, and how a short report is organized — and you will begin using 根据 and 通过 as coverbs to introduce a source or a means, alongside the productive concession pattern 虽然...但是. You will also expand your public-information character set: words that appear on notices, in short news items, and in everyday announcements.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Recognize and use basic news-style framing: identifying a source with 据...报道, 根据, and naming who said or reported something.
  2. Use 通过 and 根据 as coverbs to introduce a means or a source of information.
  3. Use 虽然...但是 productively to present a contrast within a short report or public statement.
  4. Read short public notices, announcements, and simplified news items with reduced Pinyin support.
  5. Listen to and summarize the main point of a short news item or public announcement.
  6. Write a short, source-framed news-style paragraph on a familiar public topic.

Communication Scenario

A community bulletin board and a local online news page both cover the same event: a new bike-share program opening in your area. You will listen to and read short reports about it, then write and present your own short news-style summary for a class news bulletin.

  1. You will listen to a short radio-style news item and identify its main point and source.
  2. You will read a public notice and a short online news item about the same topic.
  3. You will orally summarize a piece of public information for a partner, naming your source.
  4. You will write a short news-style paragraph that frames information with a source and includes at least one contrast using 虽然...但是.

发音 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus: News-Style Rhythm and Reporting Verbs

News and public-announcement style in Mandarin tends to move at a measured, evenly paced rhythm, with a clear pause after the source phrase before the reported information begins. Practice separating the source-framing phrase from the content that follows it, and keep 报道 and 根据 clear and unrushed at the start of a sentence.

FeatureReview Note
pause after source phraseleave a brief, natural pause after 据...报道 or 根据... before continuing
third-tone sandhi in 根据根据 begins on a neutral first-tone syllable followed by a falling tone; keep the two syllables distinct and even
连读 rhythm in 虽然...但是treat each half of the pair as its own breath group; do not rush 虽然 into the clause that follows it

No new tone or sound system is introduced in this chapter. This is a rhythm and phrasing focus building on the tone control already consolidated in Chapters 1 through 5.

语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Framing Information by Source

1. 据...报道 and 根据 — Naming a Source

PatternUseExample
据 + source + 报道，+ informationattribute a news item to a source据当地新闻报道，市中心明年会有一个新的公园。
根据 + source，+ informationbase a statement on a notice, document, or report根据这份公告，图书馆下周三不开门。

2. 通过 — Introducing a Means (Coverb, Introduced This Chapter)

PatternUseExample
通过 + means + subject + verb phrasedescribe how information was obtained or an action accomplished我们通过一个朋友知道了这个消息。

通过 joins the coverb system already active for 从, 到, 给, 跟, 用, 在, 往, and 向. Like those coverbs, 通过 and its phrase come before the main verb.

3. 虽然...但是... — Introducing a Contrast (Introduced This Chapter)

PatternUseExample
虽然 + situation A，但是 + situation Bpresent a contrast or limitation within a report or statement虽然这个计划还没有最后确定，但是很多市民已经开始讨论了。

虽然 and 但是 each open their own clause. 虽然 does not need 可是 or 不过 to complete the contrast; 但是 alone is sufficient and is the default pairing for this chapter.

This chapter introduces no other new grammar system. Word order, negation, aspect, measure words, and the modal and complement systems from earlier chapters remain unchanged and continue to recycle throughout the reading, listening, speaking, and writing tasks below.

对话 — Model Dialogue

Model Exchange: 共享单车的消息

Context: two neighbors discuss a public notice about a new bike-share program; neutral, informative register.

王阿姨：你看到公告栏上的通知了吗？
小刘：看到了。根据这份通知，下个月市中心会有一个共享单车计划。
王阿姨：是吗？这是怎么开始的？
小刘：据当地新闻报道，是几个市民先提出的想法，后来市政府决定支持。
王阿姨：听起来不错。不过，自行车放在哪儿呢？
小刘：虽然公告上没有说得很清楚，但是我通过朋友知道，车站附近会有专门的停车点。
王阿姨：那目前计划是什么时候开始？
小刘：公告说目前还没有最后的日期，专家还在讨论细节。

Auntie Wang: Did you see the notice on the bulletin board? Xiao Liu: I did. According to this notice, there will be a bike-share program downtown starting next month. Auntie Wang: Really? How did this get started? Xiao Liu: According to local news reports, a few residents first raised the idea, and later the city government decided to support it. Auntie Wang: Sounds good. But where will the bikes be kept? Xiao Liu: Although the notice doesn't say very clearly, I found out through a friend that there will be dedicated parking spots near the stations. Auntie Wang: So when is the plan currently set to start? Xiao Liu: The notice says there isn't a final date yet — experts are still discussing the details.

Reading Practice

Read the public notice below, then answer the questions. Pinyin support remains reduced in this reading, continuing the phase-out from earlier chapters.

公告：市中心共享单车计划
根据市政府的最新公告，市中心共享单车计划将在下个月开始。这个计划是几位市民通过一份网上提议提出的，后来得到了市政府的支持。据当地新闻报道，第一批车站会设在地铁站和公园附近。专家认为，这个计划可以帮助减少交通问题，也能让市民有更多的出行选择。虽然具体的车站数量目前还没有确定，但是市政府表示，会在开始前一周公布详细信息。市民如果有问题或者建议，可以通过市政府的网站联系相关部门。
Notice: Downtown Bike-Share Program. According to the city government's latest announcement, a downtown bike-share program will begin next month. The plan was proposed by several residents through an online proposal and later received the city government's support. According to local news reports, the first stations will be set up near subway stations and parks. Experts believe the program can help reduce traffic problems and give residents more travel options. Although the exact number of stations has not yet been finalized, the city government has stated that detailed information will be released a week before the start. Residents with questions or suggestions can contact the relevant department through the city government's website.

写作 — Writing Workshop

Writing Workshop: A Short News-Style Paragraph

Write a short news-style paragraph (six to eight sentences) about a real or invented piece of local public information, following the model of the Reading Practice passage above.

写作任务 — Writing task: Type your paragraph using Pinyin input. Your writing must include:

  1. A clear source-framing opening, using 据...报道 or 根据.
  2. At least one use of 通过 to describe how information was obtained or how something was accomplished.
  3. At least one contrast using 虽然...但是.
  4. At least two public-information vocabulary items from this chapter's core vocabulary list, used naturally.
  5. No direct quotation — report and paraphrase the information rather than copying exact wording.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • The paragraph opens by clearly naming its source.
  • 通过 is used correctly to introduce a means, before the main verb.
  • 虽然 and 但是 each open a complete clause and are not mixed with 可是 or 不过.
  • Chinese punctuation (，。？！) is used correctly throughout, with no unnecessary quotation marks.
  • No grammar or vocabulary beyond what has been consolidated through Chapter 6 is required to complete the task.

Culture and Communication

Culture Note — Public notices and information sources

Public notices in Mandarin-speaking contexts commonly name their issuing body or source directly, whether that is a government office, a school, a workplace, or a news outlet, and they often state clearly what is confirmed versus what is still being decided. This pattern — naming a source, then distinguishing settled information from pending details — appears in many settings, from a printed community bulletin board to an online city-government announcement, though the exact format and level of formality vary by publication and region.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Dropping the source: a news-style report should name where the information comes from; do not state public information as if it were the writer's own unattributed claim.
  • Confusing 根据 and 通过: 根据 introduces the basis for a statement (a document, notice, or report); 通过 introduces a means or channel by which something was learned or accomplished. They are not interchangeable.
  • Splitting 虽然...但是 with the wrong pairing: keep 虽然 paired with 但是 in this chapter rather than substituting 可是 or 不过, which are not yet productive here.
  • Overloading the paragraph with new vocabulary: this chapter's writing task should showcase the source-framing and coverb patterns clearly, not a long list of unfamiliar public-affairs terms.
  • Treating 目前 as interchangeable with 现在: 目前 is a more formal, report-appropriate way of saying "at present," well suited to public-information writing.
