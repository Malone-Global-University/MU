观点、证据和反驳

Volume III, Chapter 11: Opinion, Evidence, and Counterpoint · Speak and Write Mandarin Chinese: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Overview and Learning Objectives

This chapter opens Unit III. Having followed a public process to its resolution in Chapter 10, Xiao Chen and Xiao Lin's study group must now decide something as a group: whether to invite a new member. The chapter introduces the language of stance, evidence, concession, and qualified disagreement — how to state an opinion clearly, support it, acknowledge a different view, and push back on a point without being impolite. It also introduces the potential complement, a new grammar system for describing whether an action is possible to complete.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State a personal opinion clearly using 我觉得 and 我认为.
  2. Support an opinion with evidence using 根据 and specific reasons.
  3. Acknowledge an opposing point before disagreeing, using 虽然...但是 and 不过/可是.
  4. Qualify a strong statement so it does not sound absolute, using 一般来说 and 不一定.
  5. Form and use potential complements such as 听得懂/听不懂 and 看得出来/看不出来.
  6. Follow a short discussion or lecture-style excerpt and identify the speaker's main stance.
  7. Continue recognizing 被, 把, 未, and 无 in reading material without being required to produce them.

Communication Scenario

With their fixed study-room time confirmed, Xiao Chen, Xiao Lin, and their study-group members are discussing whether to invite a new classmate, Xiao Wang, to join. Not everyone agrees. You will follow their discussion, listen to a short recorded opinion from a group member who could not attend, and form and support your own opinion on the question.

  1. You will read a short group chat exchange in which members state and support opposing opinions.
  2. You will listen to a short voice message in which a group member gives a qualified opinion.
  3. You will discuss the question with a partner, stating your own opinion and responding to a different view.
  4. You will write a short paragraph giving your opinion, with evidence and one acknowledged counterpoint.

发音 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus: Connected Phrasing in Opinion Speech

This chapter introduces connected phrasing: grouping an opinion, its softener, and its reason into one smooth breath group rather than pausing after every word. Practice keeping 我觉得, 不过, and 一般来说 attached to the clause that follows them, the way a fluent speaker would say them as a single unit rather than as an isolated tag.

PhrasePractice Focus
我觉得这个主意不错。say 我觉得 and 这个主意不错 as one connected phrase, no pause after 觉得
她很聪明，不过有点儿害羞。keep 不过 attached forward to 有点儿害羞, not isolated
一般来说，新组员需要一点儿时间适应。light pause after the comma, then one connected phrase to the end
语法 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

1. Stating an Opinion: 我觉得 and 我认为

Both introduce a personal opinion. 我觉得 is more conversational; 我认为 is somewhat more formal and often used when the opinion is supported by reasoning.

我觉得我们应该请小王加入我们的小组。
我认为多一个人不一定会影响效率。

2. Softened Counterargument: 不过 and 可是

不过 and 可是 both mean "but," but they are softer in tone than 但是 in casual disagreement. Use them to introduce a gentle counterpoint after acknowledging the other person's point.

你说的有道理，不过我还是有点儿担心时间问题。
我明白你的理由，可是证据好像还不够。

3. Qualification: 一般来说 and 不一定

Qualification softens an absolute claim so it does not overstate the case. 一般来说 frames a statement as a general tendency rather than a rule; 不一定 directly denies necessity.

一般来说，小组人越多，安排时间越难。
人多不一定学习效果不好，这得看小组怎么合作。

4. The Potential Complement (新): V + 得/不 + Complement

The potential complement states whether an action's result is possible to achieve. It is formed by inserting 得 (possible) or 不 (not possible) between a verb and its result or directional complement.

听得懂 (can understand by listening) / 听不懂 (cannot understand by listening)
看得出来 (can tell by looking) / 看不出来 (cannot tell by looking)

At this stage, control is limited to the two verb-complement pairs above. Broader potential-complement patterns are developed in later chapters of this unit.

Putting It Together

我觉得我们应该请小王加入。她很努力，我看得出来。不过，人多了以后，时间可能更难安排，这是我们得考虑的问题。一般来说，小组人数不宜太多，但这不一定是绝对的规则。

对话 — Model Dialogue

Model Dialogue: 该不该请小王加入？

小陈：我觉得我们应该请小王加入我们的小组。她很努力，我看得出来。
小林：你说的有道理，不过我还是有点儿担心。人多了以后，安排时间可能更难。
小陈：我明白你的理由，可是证据好像还不够。我们现在也才四个人，加一个人应该没问题。
小林：一般来说，小组人越多确实越难安排，但你说得对，这不一定是绝对的规则。
小陈：那我们先问问小王愿不愿意，再听听别的组员的看法，怎么样？
小林：好，我支持你这个做法。

练习 — Guided Practice

Guided Practice

  1. Complete each sentence with 我觉得 or 我认为, choosing the more natural option for the context: ______我们应该早点儿决定。
  2. Rewrite as a softened counterargument using 不过: 你的理由很好。但是证据不够。 → ______
  3. Add a qualifier using 不一定 to soften this absolute claim: 小组人多，效率就低。
  4. Form the correct potential complement: 她说得很快，我 (听/懂) ______。
  5. Form the negative potential complement: 这张照片太模糊了，我 (看/出来) ______这是谁。

阅读 — Reading Practice

Reading Practice: A Group Chat Exchange

Read the group chat messages below, then answer the questions. 被 is glossed for recognition only, as in earlier chapters.

小林：说实话，我不反对请小王加入，但是我担心效率问题。
小周：我认为这个担心是有道理的，不过证据不多——我们还没试过，怎么知道效率一定会受影响？
小陈：对，我看得出来大家其实都不反对，只是想法不完全一样。要不我们先试一个月，看看效果？
小林：这个主意不错，如果被大家接受，我也同意。
Translation note: 被 in 如果被大家接受 marks a passive construction ("if it is accepted by everyone") and is glossed for recognition only.
  1. 小林最开始的担心是什么？
  2. 小周怎么回应小林的担心？
  3. 小陈提出了什么解决办法？
  4. 小林最后同意了吗？条件是什么？

写作 — Writing Workshop

Writing Workshop: An Opinion Paragraph

Write a short paragraph giving your opinion on whether the study group should invite a new member. Your writing must include:

  1. A clear opening opinion statement using 我觉得 or 我认为.
  2. At least one supporting reason, introduced with 因为 or 根据.
  3. One acknowledged counterpoint, introduced with 不过 or 可是.
  4. One qualified statement, using 一般来说 or 不一定.
  5. At least one potential complement from this chapter's vocabulary, used naturally.

修改清单 — Editing checklist:

  • The opinion is stated clearly in the first sentence.
  • The supporting reason is relevant and specific.
  • The counterpoint is acknowledged respectfully, not dismissed.
  • The qualifier avoids stating the claim as an absolute rule.
  • The potential complement is formed correctly.
  • Chinese punctuation (，。？！) is used correctly throughout.

Culture and Communication Check

Culture Note — Disagreeing without confrontation

In many Mandarin-speaking contexts, disagreement in a group setting is often introduced gently — acknowledging the other person's point before raising a concern — rather than stated as a flat contradiction. Words like 不过 and 可是 tend to soften a counterpoint more than 但是 does in casual conversation, though all three are grammatically similar. This is a tendency in many settings rather than a fixed rule, and directness varies by relationship, workplace, region, and individual style.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using 我认为 in every casual context: 我认为 can sound overly formal in a relaxed conversation; 我觉得 is usually more natural among friends.
  • Skipping the acknowledgment before disagreeing: jumping straight to 不过/可是 without recognizing the other person's point first can sound abrupt.
  • Confusing 得 and 不 placement in potential complements: remember the pattern is verb + 得/不 + complement, not verb + complement + 得/不.
  • Trying to produce your own 被 sentences: 被 remains recognition-only at this stage; productive control comes later.
