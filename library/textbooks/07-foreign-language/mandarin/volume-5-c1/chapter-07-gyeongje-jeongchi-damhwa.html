Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Read and interpret Korean texts that report on policy, markets, and governance, including short news items, economic commentary, and public statements.
  2. Use specialized economic and political vocabulary accurately, including terms for policy, markets, governance, public claims, and analytic framing.
  3. Explain a policy issue or economic trend in formal Korean, sequencing cause, effect, and evaluation clearly for a general audience.
  4. Distinguish factual claims from evaluative or contested claims within a public statement, and identify the source of a claim.
  5. Present a short analytic summary of a policy or economic issue, framing it neutrally for readers with different views.
  6. Apply the register, argument, density, and mediation judgment consolidated in Chapter 5 and applied to institutional Korean in Chapter 6 to the economic and political domain.

This chapter introduces no new particles, endings, speech levels, honorific systems, counters, or sound changes. It applies systems already active through Chapter 6 to a new domain: economic and political discourse. All practice material in this chapter is fictional and general-purpose; no real officials, parties, or governments are named or characterized.

  1. You will study specialized vocabulary for policy, markets, governance, public claims, and analytic framing.
  2. You will examine how Korean policy announcements and economic commentary are structured.
  3. You will read a short policy announcement and a brief commentary passage, distinguishing fact from evaluation.

발음 초점 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus: Reading Statistics and Figures Aloud

This chapter introduces no new sounds or sound changes. It reviews how already-active number pronunciation and pacing patterns apply to economic figures, percentages, and comparative statistics, which appear frequently in this domain.

Pronunciation Note — Reading percentages and comparative figures

Percentages such as 3.2퍼센트 or 3.2% use Sino-Korean numerals read digit by digit through the decimal point, followed by 퍼센트. When a figure is compared to a previous period, as in 작년보다 2퍼센트 올랐다 ("it rose 2 percent compared to last year"), place a brief pause after the comparison phrase so the listener can register what is being compared before the direction of change. Read growth and decline verbs such as 오르다, 내리다, 늘다, and 줄다 with clear, even pacing rather than rushing through the number.

문법과 표현 — Grammar and Patterns

Grammar and Patterns: Reporting Claims and Framing Analysis

This chapter introduces no new particles, endings, speech levels, or honorific systems. It applies structures already active through Chapter 6 — indirect quotation, passive forms, comparison, and noun-modifying clauses — to the specific communicative demands of economic and political discourse, where distinguishing fact from claim is essential.

1. Attributing a claim to its source

Public discourse requires clearly marking who said what. The already-active indirect quotation pattern -다고 하다, together with -다고 주장하다 and -다고 밝히다, lets a writer attribute a claim precisely rather than presenting it as established fact.

  • 정부는 이번 대책으로 물가가 안정될 것이라고 밝혔다.The government stated that prices would stabilize as a result of this measure.indirect quotation, government as source
  • 일부 전문가들은 이 대책의 효과가 제한적일 것이라고 주장한다.Some experts argue that the effect of this measure will be limited.indirect quotation, attributed to a specific group, not to "everyone"

2. Marking a statement as contested or evaluative

Evaluative claims should be marked as such rather than stated as fact. The noun 평가 combined with -다는 평가가 있다 ("there is an assessment that..."), and the noun 논란 combined with -을 둘러싸고 논란이 있다 ("there is controversy surrounding..."), let a writer signal that a claim is disputed rather than settled.

  • 이번 대책이 실제로 효과가 있을지에 대해서는 엇갈린 평가가 있다.There are differing assessments as to whether this measure will actually be effective.evaluative framing, explicitly marked as contested
  • 세금 인상 여부를 둘러싸고 여야 간에 논란이 계속되고 있다.Controversy continues between the ruling and opposition sides over whether to raise taxes.controversy framing, generic 여야 without naming a real party

3. Comparison with prior periods and alternatives

Economic commentary frequently compares a figure to an earlier period or an alternative scenario, using the already-active comparison particle 보다 and the connector -에 비해 ("compared to").

  • 이번 분기 성장률은 지난해 같은 기간보다 낮았다.This quarter's growth rate was lower than the same period last year.comparison, 보다
  • 전문가들은 새 정책이 기존 정책에 비해 소비자 부담을 줄일 것으로 전망한다.Experts forecast that the new policy will reduce the burden on consumers compared to the existing policy.comparison, -에 비해, forecast framing

4. Noun-modifying clauses identifying affected groups

As in institutional Korean, noun-modifying clauses, already active since earlier volumes, precisely identify which group is affected by a policy or trend.

  • 이번 대책의 혜택을 받는 대상은 소득이 일정 기준 이하인 가구이다.The group receiving the benefit of this measure is households with income below a certain threshold.noun-modifying clause defining scope
  • 물가 상승의 영향을 크게 받는 소비자들은 정부의 대응을 요구하고 있다.Consumers who are heavily affected by rising prices are demanding a government response.noun-modifying clause identifying an affected group
Building Block
[사실/발표] + [-다고 밝히다/주장하다 (출처 명시)] + [평가/논란 (엇갈린 반응 표시)]
A balanced explanatory sentence typically states the fact or announcement first, attributes any claim to its specific source using already-active quotation patterns, then signals whether an evaluation is contested rather than settled.
정부는 물가 안정 대책을 발표했다고 밝혔으며, 이 대책의 실효성에 대해서는 전문가들 사이에서도 엇갈린 평가가 나온다.
The government stated that it announced a price-stabilization measure, and even among experts there are differing assessments of how effective this measure will actually be.
Grammar Note — Precision over new structure

Every pattern in this section — indirect quotation, passive forms, comparison with 보다 and -에 비해, and noun-modifying clauses — is already active from earlier chapters and volumes. What is new is the discipline of using these familiar structures to keep fact, attributed claim, and contested evaluation clearly distinct in a single passage.

Model Dialogue: Discussing a Policy Announcement

동료 뉴스에서 정부가 물가 안정 대책을 발표했다고 하던데, 구체적으로 뭘 한대요?I heard on the news that the government announced a price-stabilization measure — what exactly are they doing?
수아 일부 생필품에 대한 세금을 일시적으로 완화하고, 저소득 가구에 보조금을 지급한다고 밝혔어요.They stated that they will temporarily ease taxes on some daily necessities and provide subsidies to low-income households.
동료 효과가 있을까요?Will it be effective?
수아 그건 전문가들 사이에서도 의견이 갈려요. 단기적으로는 부담이 줄겠지만, 장기적인 효과는 제한적일 거라는 평가도 있어요.Opinions differ even among experts on that. There's an assessment that the burden will decrease in the short term, but the long-term effect will be limited.
동료 여야는 이 대책에 대해 뭐라고 해요?What do the ruling and opposition sides say about this measure?
수아 세금 완화의 규모를 둘러싸고 아직 논란이 있대요. 다음 주에 관련 협의가 있을 예정이라고 하더라고요.I heard there's still controversy over the scale of the tax easing. Apparently there's related negotiation scheduled for next week.

분석 — Analysis: Identify where 수아 attributes a claim to its source, where she signals that an evaluation is contested rather than settled, and where she reports information she heard secondhand rather than confirming it herself.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Balance and attribution in South Korean public-affairs reporting

    Mainstream South Korean news outlets generally expect economic and political reporting to attribute claims to a named source, such as a government office, a research institute, or a named expert, rather than presenting projections as settled fact. Reporting that clearly separates confirmed figures from forecasts and clearly marks contested policy questions as contested is commonly regarded as more credible than reporting that blurs the two.

    Institutional Note — Variation across outlets and publication types

    Different news outlets, government agencies, and research institutes in South Korea may use somewhat different conventions for framing policy coverage, choosing which experts to cite, and presenting figures. The vocabulary and patterns in this chapter reflect general, broadly applicable usage for reading and producing balanced explanatory Korean; learners engaging with a specific outlet's coverage should remain attentive to that outlet's own framing choices rather than assuming all sources present issues identically.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Common Mistake — Presenting a claim as fact

    Error: Writing a projection or evaluation as though it were a confirmed fact, such as 이번 대책으로 물가가 안정된다 stated flatly, when the source text presents this only as something the government claims will happen.

    Correction: Attribute the claim to its source using an already-active quotation pattern, as in 정부는 물가가 안정될 것이라고 밝혔다, and reserve unattributed statements for confirmed facts such as reported figures.

    Common Mistake — Presenting only one side of a contested issue

    Error: Summarizing a policy debate by presenting only the supporting view or only the opposing view, so the reader cannot tell that the issue is genuinely contested.

    Correction: Include at least one supporting view and one differing view, using neutral framing such as 엇갈린 평가가 있다 or 논란이 있다, without the writer taking a side.

    Common Mistake — Naming or characterizing real officials or parties

    Error: Substituting a real government, official, or political party's name into a practice sentence or exercise, or attributing a specific real-world position to a named public figure.

    Correction: Keep practice material generic, using terms such as 정부, 전문가들, and 여야 without naming a real administration, official, or party, consistent with this chapter's fictional practice scenarios.

    Reading analysis

    1. Sample response: The sentence stating what was announced is the description of the tax easing, subsidies, and business support measures; the sentence attributing a projected outcome to the government is the one using 정부는 ... 것이라고 밝혔다.
    2. Sample response: One expert view projects that consumer burden will decrease in the short term; the opposing view holds that the measure may only have a temporary effect without addressing the underlying cause.
    3. Sample response: The sentence about controversy surrounding the scale and target of the tax easing (여야 간의 논란) signals an unresolved issue.
    4. Sample response: The government's projected outcome in 자료 1 is presented with attribution (정부는 ... 밝혔다), which signals it is the government's claim rather than a confirmed, independently verified fact.
    5. Sample response: 정부의 물가 안정 대책에 대해 전문가들의 평가는 엇갈리며, 세금 완화의 규모를 둘러싼 논란도 계속되고 있다.

    Listening comprehension

    • Sample response: This quarter's growth rate is 0.5 percentage points lower than the same period last year.
    • Sample response: The government claims the lower figure is a temporary phenomenon.
    • Sample response: Some experts say the possibility of continued economic downturn cannot be ruled out.

    Section D — Sample explanatory brief (abridged)

    정부는 최근 물가 상승에 따른 국민 부담을 완화하기 위해 세금 일부 완화와 저소득 가구 보조금 지급을 포함한 대책을 발표했다고 밝혔다. 이 대책의 효과에 대해서는 전문가들 사이에서도 평가가 엇갈리며, 세금 완화의 규모를 둘러싼 논란도 이어지고 있다.

    Facts: the government announced tax easing and subsidies for low-income households. Attributed claims: the government's projection that price growth will slow; experts' differing assessments of the measure's actual effect.

    Section E — Sample discussion

    Sample response: A balanced explanation states the announced facts first, attributes any projection to its source using -다고 밝히다 or -다고 주장하다, and then presents at least one supporting and one differing evaluation using neutral framing such as 엇갈린 평가가 있다, without the writer endorsing either side.

    Looking Ahead

    지금 할 수 있는 것 — What you can do now
