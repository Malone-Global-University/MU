토론과 설득Debate and Persuasion

Opening Volume V, Unit IV · Speak and Write Korean: Mastery, Precision, Rhetoric, and Specialized Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Build and defend a position on a familiar public or institutional issue under conversational pressure, rather than only in prepared written form.
  2. Concede a valid point from an opposing position while still maintaining and returning to your own overall stance.
  3. Counter another speaker's argument directly and respectfully, distinguishing between attacking an argument and attacking the person who made it.
  4. Use rhetorical pacing — qualification, emphasis, and strategic repetition — to make a spoken argument more persuasive without becoming aggressive or evasive.
  5. Recognize and produce the turn-taking and interruption-management language needed to hold the floor, yield the floor, and re-enter a live debate.
  6. Read and evaluate two opposing position statements on a shared issue and identify where each writer concedes, qualifies, or refuses to engage with the other side.

This chapter draws on argumentation, concession, and stance systems already active since Volume V, Chapter 2 (Advanced Argument Structure) and reactivates them for live, real-time spoken debate rather than only planned writing. It does not introduce a new grammar system; it develops rhetorical control and interactional strategy within systems already active.

Opening Scenario

토론 동아리 모임에서 사회자가 이번 주제를 발표한다: "오늘 주제는 '대학 등록금은 전액 국가가 부담해야 하는가'입니다. 두 분은 서로 다른 입장에서 논거를 준비해 오셨죠? 상대방의 의견을 들으면서 필요하면 인정할 부분은 인정하고, 반박할 부분은 근거를 들어 반박해 주세요. 감정적으로 반응하지 말고, 상대의 주장 자체에 집중해 주세요." 수아는 준비해 온 자료를 보면서도, 상대가 예상하지 못한 반론을 제기할 경우 즉석에서 대응해야 한다는 것을 알고 긴장한다.

  1. You will hear two debaters present opposing opening positions on a shared fictional issue.
  2. You will identify where each speaker concedes a point versus where each refuses to yield.
  3. You will practice interjecting, holding the floor, and yielding the floor politely but firmly.
  4. You will construct your own live counterargument in response to an opponent's specific claim.
  5. You will read two written position statements and evaluate their argumentative strategies.

문법 및 수사 구조 — Grammar and Rhetorical Patterns

Argument Under Pressure: Rhetorical Control and Counterargument

This chapter reactivates connective and stance systems already active since Chapter 2 (concession, counterargument, qualification) and applies them to live, real-time spoken exchange, where timing, interruption, and tone carry as much weight as the words themselves. No new productive grammar system is introduced.

Building Block — Concede, Then Return
[상대 주장 요약] + 는 점은 인정하지만 / 일리가 있지만 + [자신의 논지로 복귀]
A strong live counterargument names the opponent's point accurately before pivoting back to one's own claim. Skipping the concession sounds evasive; skipping the pivot sounds like surrender.
등록금 부담이 학생들에게 크다는 점은 일리가 있지만, 전액 국가 부담이 유일한 해결책이라고 보기는 어렵습니다.
It's a fair point that the tuition burden is heavy for students, but it's hard to see full state funding as the only solution.
  • 말씀 중에 죄송하지만, 그 부분은 제가 이미 자료로 반박했습니다.Sorry to interrupt, but I've already rebutted that point with data.polite interruption + reference back to prior argument
  • 그 논리에는 비약이 있다고 봅니다. 원인과 결과를 혼동하고 계신 것 같습니다.I think there's a leap in that logic. I believe you may be confusing cause and effect.naming a specific flaw, not the person's character
  • 끝까지 말씀드리겠습니다. 잠시만요.Let me finish my point. Just a moment.holding the floor without escalating tone
  • 그것은 근본적으로 다른 문제라고 생각합니다.I think that's a fundamentally different issue.rejecting a false comparison respectfully
  • 결론적으로, 두 분의 의견에는 공통점도 있지만 핵심 쟁점에서는 여전히 차이가 있습니다.In conclusion, our positions share some common ground, but a real difference remains on the core issue.closing move: acknowledge common ground, restate the unresolved core
Grammar Note — Distinguishing rebuttal from attack

Korean debate register consistently separates the argument from the person making it. Expressions such as 그 논리에는 비약이 있다고 봅니다 name a flaw in reasoning; they do not comment on the speaker's intelligence, honesty, or character. Learners should avoid constructions that shift from evaluating the claim to evaluating the person, even under pressure or when frustrated.

본문 대화 — Model Exchange

Model Exchange: A Live Debate Exchange

대학 토론 동아리에서 두 토론자가 '대학 등록금 전액 국가 부담'에 대해 실시간으로 논쟁하는 장면 (교육용 예시)

수아 등록금 부담이 학생과 가정에 지나치게 크다는 점은 분명합니다. 국가가 전액 부담해야 한다고 봅니다.It's clear that the tuition burden is excessive for students and families. I believe the state should bear the full cost.
민준 말씀 중에 죄송하지만, 부담이 크다는 점은 일리가 있지만, 전액 국가 부담이 유일한 해법이라는 결론에는 논리적 비약이 있다고 봅니다.Sorry to interrupt, but while it's fair that the burden is heavy, I think there's a logical leap in concluding that full state funding is the only solution.
수아 끝까지 말씀드리겠습니다. 다른 나라의 사례를 보면 전액 지원이 실제로 효과가 있었습니다.Let me finish my point. Looking at other countries' cases, full support has actually been effective.
민준 그 사례는 재정 구조가 전혀 다른 국가의 경우라서, 근본적으로 다른 문제라고 생각합니다.That case involves a country with a completely different fiscal structure, so I think it's a fundamentally different issue.
사회자 결론적으로, 두 분의 의견에는 공통점도 있지만 핵심 쟁점에서는 여전히 차이가 있는 것 같습니다.In conclusion, it seems the two of you share some common ground, but a real difference remains on the core issue.

Read both fictional position statements below on the same issue introduced in the Model Exchange. Both were written for this course as original instructional content; no real institution, policy, or study is named or claimed as fact.

입장문 1 — 전액 국가 부담 찬성 (교육용 창작 예시)

등록금 부담은 더 이상 개인이 감당할 수준을 넘어섰다. 국가가 전액을 부담한다면 경제적 배경과 무관하게 누구나 고등교육에 접근할 수 있게 된다. 물론 재정 부담이 크다는 반론이 있을 수 있다는 점은 인정하지만, 장기적으로는 노동력의 질적 향상을 통해 그 비용을 상쇄할 수 있다고 본다.

입장문 2 — 전액 국가 부담 반대 (교육용 창작 예시)

고등교육 접근성을 높여야 한다는 목표에는 동의하지만, 전액 국가 부담이 유일한 해법이라고 단정적으로 말하기는 어렵다. 재정 구조와 세금 부담이 전혀 다른 국가의 사례를 그대로 적용하는 것은 근본적으로 다른 문제를 같은 문제로 취급하는 것이다. 소득 수준에 따른 차등 지원이 더 현실적인 대안이 될 수 있다.

쓰기 워크숍 — Writing Workshop

Writing Workshop: A Position Statement with Built-In Concession

Using the tuition-funding issue above, or a comparable fictional public issue of your choosing, draft a position statement that anticipates and addresses the strongest opposing argument.

  • 입장 밝히기. State your position clearly in one or two sentences.
  • 상대 논거 예측하기. Identify the strongest argument the opposing side would raise.
  • 양보하기. Concede the valid part of that argument explicitly, using ~는 점은 인정하지만 or a similar expression.
  • 복귀하기. Return to your own position and explain why the concession does not defeat your overall claim.
  • 반박 근거 제시하기. Add one additional piece of reasoning or a rejected false comparison, using 근본적으로 다른 문제이다 if applicable.
  • 결론 맺기. Close with 결론적으로 and a statement that acknowledges any common ground while restating your core position.
  • 점검하기. Compare your draft against the checklist below.
    • 1차 초안 (no concession, one-sided) 등록금은 국가가 전액 부담해야 한다. 반대하는 사람들은 틀렸다.
    수정본 (concession, pivot, and closing) 등록금은 국가가 전액 부담해야 한다. 재정 부담이 크다는 반론에는 일리가 있지만, 장기적인 노동력 향상을 고려하면 그 비용은 상쇄될 수 있다고 본다. 결론적으로, 재정 우려는 타당하지만 그것이 정책 자체를 포기할 이유가 되지는 않는다.

    체크리스트 — Checklist:

    • The position is clearly stated in the opening.
    • At least one opposing point is conceded explicitly rather than ignored.
    • The writer pivots back to their own position after the concession.
    • Any rejected comparison is addressed with "a fundamentally different issue" language, not dismissed without reason.
    • The closing acknowledges common ground while restating the unresolved core claim.
    • No expression evaluates the opposing person rather than the opposing argument.
    • Hangeul, spacing, and particle use are accurate, with no romanization.
    • No structure appears beyond what is active through Volume V, Chapter 15.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Disagreement without confrontation

    In many Korean formal and academic debate settings, direct disagreement is softened through concession language before a rebuttal is delivered. Opening a rebuttal with an acknowledgment such as 일리가 있지만 is not weakness — it signals that the speaker has genuinely engaged with the opposing argument before disagreeing, which is often seen as a mark of a stronger, more credible debater.

    Register Note — Formality under pressure

    Even in a heated live exchange, formal debate register in Korean typically maintains -습니다/-습니까 style rather than shifting to casual forms, since the setting remains institutional and public regardless of how animated the exchange becomes. Dropping to casual speech mid-debate would be read as a register violation, not merely as informality.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Common Mistake — Rebutting without conceding anything

    Error: 그건 완전히 틀렸습니다. (a flat, unqualified dismissal with no acknowledgment of any valid element)

    Correction: 그 부분은 일리가 있지만, 전체 결론에는 동의하기 어렵습니다. Concede what is genuinely valid before disagreeing with the overall claim.

    Common Mistake — Attacking the person instead of the argument

    Error: 그렇게 생각하시는 건 정보가 부족해서 그런 것 같습니다. (implying the opponent is uninformed rather than addressing the claim)

    Correction: 그 논리에는 비약이 있다고 봅니다. Name the flaw in the reasoning, not a deficiency in the person.

    Common Mistake — Interrupting without softening language

    Error: 아니요, 잠깐만요. (an abrupt interruption with no politeness framing)

    Correction: 말씀 중에 죄송하지만, 잠시 말씀드려도 될까요? Frame an interruption as a polite request rather than a blunt override.

    Answer Key

    Reading analysis

    1. Sample response: 입장문 1 concedes that fiscal burden is a valid concern before arguing long-term cost offset; 입장문 2 concedes that improving access to higher education is a shared goal before rejecting full state funding as the only answer.
    2. Sample response: 입장문 2 uses 근본적으로 다른 문제-type reasoning by rejecting the direct application of another country's fiscal case, since tax structures differ fundamentally.
    3. Sample response: 장기적인 효과는 예측일 뿐이며, 그 효과가 실제로 비용을 상쇄한다고 단정적으로 말하기는 어렵습니다.
    4. Sample response: 입장문 2 uses more qualifying language (e.g., "hard to state categorically"), which can make it sound more measured and credible, though possibly less forceful than 입장문 1's more direct claims.

    Listening comprehension

    • Sample response: 민준 concedes that the tuition burden is heavy before arguing that full state funding is not necessarily the only solution.
    • Sample response: 수아 uses 끝까지 말씀드리겠습니다 to keep the floor after being interrupted.
    • Sample response: 민준 rejects the comparison because the other country's fiscal structure is fundamentally different, making it a different issue.

    Section E — Sample discussion

    Sample response: A well-controlled live rebuttal names the opponent's valid point specifically, pivots back to the speaker's own claim without dismissing the concession, addresses the specific reasoning rather than the person, and closes by naming the remaining unresolved disagreement rather than declaring total victory.
