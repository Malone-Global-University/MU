Voces y perspectivas

El estilo indirecto, la cita y el cambio de perspectiva · Speak and Write Spanish: Independent Communication and Connected Discourse

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Report what someone said using decir que and related reporting verbs.
  2. Report yes/no questions with preguntar si and information questions with preguntar plus an interrogative word.
  3. Make the pronoun and verb-tense shifts required when moving from a direct quotation to reported speech.
  4. Distinguish a direct quotation from a reported (indirect) version of the same statement, in speech and in writing.
  5. Use discourse-level demonstratives such as eso and esto to refer back to something already said.
  6. Narrate a discussion that includes several speakers' viewpoints without losing track of who said what.

Communication Scenario

The vote has taken place, and the park project has been approved. Now Camila is writing a summary of the meeting for neighbors who could not attend. She needs to relate what different people said and asked during the discussion, without simply repeating every quotation word for word. Rodrigo, meanwhile, is telling a friend by phone what happened, shifting easily between quoting people directly and summarizing what they said.

  1. Think of a meeting, class, or family discussion you later described to someone else. Did you quote people exactly, or did you summarize what they said in your own words?
  2. In English, what changes when you move from "I want more information" to "She said she wanted more information"? Which words changed, and why?

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation and Intelligibility

Reported speech in Spanish depends heavily on the conjunction que, which is unstressed and often pronounced quickly, almost merging with the reporting verb.

  • dijo que is typically pronounced as one smooth phrase, with the stress falling on di and on the verb that follows que, not on que itself.
  • In preguntó si, the í in si carries a written accent only when it means "yes"; the conjunction si meaning "whether" or "if" carries no accent and is pronounced with light stress.
  • Keep sentence-final intonation falling for reported statements, even when the original quotation was a question: Preguntó si vendríamos. falls in pitch at the end, unlike the original spoken question.

Grammar: El estilo indirecto

Purpose: to relate what someone said or asked without quoting their exact words, and to keep pronoun and tense references clear when reporting from a different point in time.

Cita directa vs. estilo indirecto

A direct quotation repeats someone's exact words, usually introduced by a colon or set off with quotation marks. Reported (indirect) speech restates the content inside a que clause, without repeating the exact words.

  • Cita directa: Rodrigo dijo: "Quiero más información antes de votar." Direct quotation: Rodrigo said, "I want more information before voting."
  • Estilo indirecto: Rodrigo dijo que quería más información antes de votar. Reported speech: Rodrigo said that he wanted more information before voting.

Notice three changes between the two versions: the subject pronoun shifts from first person to third person understood in the verb ending, quiero becomes quería, and the quotation marks disappear in favor of the conjunction que.

Cambios de tiempo verbal

When the reporting verb (dijo, explicó, comentó) is in the past, the reported verb usually shifts one step back in time. The pattern below covers the shifts needed at this level.

Cita directaEstilo indirecto (reporting verb in the past)
presente: "Quiero..."imperfecto: dijo que quería...
pretérito: "Voté a favor."pluscuamperfecto: dijo que había votado a favor.
futuro: "Votaré el sábado."condicional: dijo que votaría el sábado.
ir a + infinitivo: "Voy a hablar."iba a + infinitivo: dijo que iba a hablar.

When the reporting verb is in the present (as when summarizing a conversation that is still relevant today), the reported verb usually keeps its original tense.

  • Camila dice que el proyecto tiene mucho apoyo. Camila says that the project has a lot of support. (present reporting verb, present reported verb)

Preguntas reportadas

Report a yes/no question with preguntar si. Report an information question with preguntar followed directly by the interrogative word, which keeps its written accent.

  • Cita directa: "¿Vendrán ustedes a la reunión?" Direct quotation: "Will you all come to the meeting?"
  • Estilo indirecto: Nos preguntó si vendríamos a la reunión. Reported: She asked us whether we would come to the meeting.
  • Cita directa: "¿Cuándo empieza la construcción?" Direct quotation: "When does construction start?"
  • Estilo indirecto: Preguntó cuándo empezaba la construcción. Reported: He asked when construction started.
Language Note: El pronombre y la perspectiva

Reported speech is not only a grammar exercise; it requires tracking whose perspective is being expressed. Dijo que yo tenía razón and Dijo que él tenía razón report two different original statements. Before reporting a sentence, identify clearly who the original speaker was referring to, then adjust the pronoun to reflect the new narrator's point of view.

Practica: Cambia cada cita directa a estilo indirecto, haciendo los cambios de pronombre y de tiempo verbal necesarios.

  1. Rodrigo: "Estoy de acuerdo con la propuesta." → Rodrigo dijo que _____.
  2. Camila: "¿Cuándo vamos a empezar?" → Camila preguntó _____.
  3. Don Ernesto: "Consideraré el presupuesto." → Don Ernesto explicó que _____.

Model Dialogue

Contexto: Después de la votación, Rodrigo llama a su amiga Valentina, que no pudo asistir a la reunión, y le cuenta lo que dijeron los vecinos.

VALENTINA¿Cómo salió la votación? Cuéntame todo.How did the vote turn out? Tell me everything.
RODRIGOGanó la propuesta. Camila preguntó si todos estaban listos para votar, y casi todos dijeron que sí.The proposal won. Camila asked if everyone was ready to vote, and almost everyone said yes.
VALENTINA¿Y don Ernesto? Pensé que tenía dudas.And don Ernesto? I thought he had doubts.
RODRIGOExplicó que consideraría el presupuesto con más cuidado esta vez, y añadió que confiaba en el nuevo plan. Al final, dijo que votaría a favor.He explained that he would consider the budget more carefully this time, and added that he trusted the new plan. In the end, he said he would vote in favor.
VALENTINA¿Alguien preguntó cuándo empezará la construcción?Did anyone ask when construction will start?
RODRIGOSí, una vecina preguntó cuándo empezaría, y Camila respondió que probablemente en dos meses.Yes, a neighbor asked when it would start, and Camila answered that probably in two months.
VALENTINAMe alegro. Según lo que cuentas, todos están de acuerdo con eso.I'm glad. According to what you're telling me, everyone agrees with that.
RODRIGOCasi todos. Un vecino comentó que todavía no estaba completamente de acuerdo, pero no se opuso.Almost everyone. One neighbor remarked that he still wasn't completely in agreement, but he didn't object.

Preguntas de comprensión:

  1. ¿Qué preguntó Camila antes de la votación?
  2. ¿Qué explicó don Ernesto sobre el presupuesto?
  3. ¿Qué preguntó la vecina, y qué respondió Camila?
  4. ¿Qué comentó el vecino que todavía no estaba de acuerdo?

Reading

Read the following summary that Camila wrote for neighbors who missed the meeting.

Resumen de la reunión

Para los vecinos que no pudieron asistir, aquí va un resumen de lo que se dijo en la reunión del sábado. Al comienzo, pregunté si todos estaban listos para votar, y la mayoría respondió que sí. Rodrigo comentó que el nuevo plan le parecía mucho más completo que el primero, y añadió que confiaba en el equipo encargado del proyecto.

Don Ernesto, como siempre, habló con cuidado. Explicó que había tenido dudas al principio, pero dijo que esta vez consideraría el presupuesto con más atención. Según él, ese cambio marcaría una gran diferencia. Al final, anunció que votaría a favor, y varios vecinos aplaudieron.

Una vecina preguntó cuándo empezaría la construcción. Le respondí que, según el cronograma, empezaría en unos dos meses. Otro vecino preguntó si habría reuniones informativas antes de comenzar, y le dije que sí, que la asociación organizaría al menos dos antes del inicio de las obras.

Preguntas de comprensión:

  1. ¿Qué preguntó Camila al comienzo de la reunión?
  2. ¿Qué comentó Rodrigo sobre el nuevo plan?
  3. ¿Qué explicó don Ernesto, y qué anunció al final?
  4. ¿Qué preguntaron los dos vecinos, y qué les respondió Camila?

Writing Workshop: Un resumen con varias voces

Your writing task is to produce a short summary (seven to ten sentences) of a real or invented meeting, conversation, or discussion, reporting what at least three different people said or asked.

  1. Presenta la situación. Briefly describe the meeting or conversation you are summarizing.
  2. Reporta afirmaciones. Use decir que, explicar que, comentar que, or añadir que to report at least three statements from different speakers.
  3. Reporta al menos una pregunta. Use preguntar si or preguntar with an interrogative word to report at least one question.
  4. Usa una referencia con eso. Include at least one sentence that refers back to a previously reported idea using eso.
  5. Conecta las ideas. Use connectors such as según, al final, sin embargo, or por eso to link your sentences.
  6. Revisa. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.

Lista de revisión:

  • I made the correct pronoun shift when reporting each statement or question.
  • I made the correct verb-tense shift when the reporting verb was in the past.
  • I used preguntar si or an interrogative word correctly to report a question.
  • I used eso correctly to refer back to a previously mentioned idea.
  • My accents, punctuation, and inverted marks are correct.
  • I did not introduce any grammar system beyond this chapter and prior chapters.

Speaking Practice

Práctica controlada

With a partner, take turns reading aloud the Model Dialogue, paying attention to the pronoun and tense shifts each time a statement or question is reported.

Tarea comunicativa: El teléfono descompuesto

Work in a small group. The first person whispers a short original sentence, in the present tense, to a partner (for example, "Quiero llegar temprano."). Each following person must report what the previous person said, using a reporting verb and making the necessary pronoun and tense changes, before passing the message along. Compare the final version with the original sentence.

Alternativa individual

If you are studying independently: think of three things a friend, family member, or coworker said to you recently. Record yourself reporting each statement using dijo que, and one question they asked using preguntó si or an interrogative word.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context

In many Spanish-speaking communities, relaying the outcome of a meeting to neighbors who could not attend — in person, by phone, or through a written notice — is a common way of keeping a community informed and building consensus. The way a summary is worded, including whose statements are quoted directly and whose are only reported, can shape how balanced or one-sided the account seems, so careful and fair reporting is valued in these contexts.

Regional Note (Recognition Only)

In more informal spoken narration, some speakers keep the original tense of a quotation even when the reporting verb is in the past, especially when the information is still true (Dijo que el proyecto tiene mucho apoyo instead of tenía). Both patterns exist in natural speech; this course activates the standard tense-shift pattern shown above as the default for written and careful spoken narration.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake

Error: Keeping quotation marks and que together — Dijo que "quiero votar a favor."

Correction: Reported speech does not use quotation marks; the sentence must also shift pronoun and tense: Dijo que quería votar a favor.

Common Mistake

Error: Reporting a question with que instead of si — Preguntó que si vendríamos. used as the only pattern, without ever practicing the simpler form.

Correction: The clearest and most consistent pattern for this chapter is preguntar si alone: Preguntó si vendríamos.

Common Mistake

Error: Forgetting the tense shift after a past reporting verb — Dijo que vota a favor.

Correction: A present-tense original statement usually shifts to the imperfect after a past reporting verb: Dijo que votaba a favor.
