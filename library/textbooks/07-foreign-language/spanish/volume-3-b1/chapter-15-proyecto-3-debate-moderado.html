Proyecto 3: Debate moderado

Presentar recomendaciones, responder a la duda y negociar soluciones · Speak and Write Spanish: Independent Communication and Connected Discourse

Chapter Objectives

This is an integrated assessment chapter. By the end of it, you will be able to:

  1. Present a recommendation using the present subjunctive after expressions of influence, desire, necessity, or emotion (Chapter 11).
  2. Respond to doubt or denial using the subjunctive after expressions of uncertainty and indefinite antecedents (Chapter 12).
  3. Propose real and hypothetical conditions using si clauses with the present/future and the imperfect subjunctive with the conditional (Chapter 13).
  4. Concede a known or uncertain obstacle with aunque, and state a purpose or condition for a proposal using para que, antes de que, or sin que (Chapter 14).
  5. Participate in a moderated debate that negotiates a solution, combining these four systems in connected, spontaneous discourse.
  6. Edit spoken and written argumentation for accuracy in mood selection, agreement, and register.

This chapter introduces no major new productive grammar system. It consolidates and assesses the recommendation, doubt, conditional, and concession/purpose systems developed across Chapters 11 through 14.

Communication Scenario

The neighborhood association has scheduled a moderated meeting to decide how to expand the volunteer program discussed in Chapters 11 through 14. Some members want to recruit more volunteers immediately; others doubt the group has the resources to train them well. Camila has agreed to moderate. Each participant must recommend a course of action, respond honestly to doubts raised by others, and propose a condition or purpose that would make their plan work, while acknowledging at least one real obstacle.

  1. Before the debate, review the four systems this project draws on: recommendation, doubt, condition, and concession/purpose. You will need all four to participate fully.
  2. Think of a real disagreement you have witnessed or imagined about resources, time, or responsibility. Notice how people in that disagreement recommend, doubt, set conditions, and concede points without abandoning their position.

Grammar Review: El lenguaje del debate

Purpose: to review, without introducing new content, how the four systems from this unit combine naturally inside a single argument.

Review: Cómo se combinan los sistemas de la Unidad III

A single well-formed argument in this debate often moves through all four systems in sequence: a recommendation, a response to doubt, a condition, and a concession.

  • Recomendación: Recomiendo que contratemos a dos coordinadores más.
  • Respuesta a la duda: Entiendo que algunos duden que tengamos fondos suficientes, pero el presupuesto lo permite.
  • Condición: Si conseguimos el apoyo del ayuntamiento, podríamos contratarlos este mismo mes.
  • Concesión y propósito: Aunque el proceso de contratación tome tiempo, empezaremos ahora para que el programa esté listo antes de que termine el año.

Review the mood each connector requires before the debate: que after recommendation and doubt verbs, si clauses following the pattern that matches real versus hypothetical conditions, and aunque/para que/antes de que/sin que following the fact-versus-possibility and same-subject-versus-different-subject rules from Chapter 14.

Repaso controlado:

  1. Completa con la forma correcta: Sugiero que el comité _____ (buscar) más voluntarios este trimestre.
  2. Completa: No creo que _____ (haber) suficiente tiempo para organizar todo antes del evento.
  3. Completa: Si el ayuntamiento nos _____ (dar) más fondos, ampliaríamos el programa el próximo año.
  4. Completa: Aunque el presupuesto _____ (ser, hecho) limitado este año, seguiremos adelante con el plan.

Model Debate Excerpt

Camila moderates as Don Rafael and Marisol debate whether to expand the volunteer program this year.

Camila:Don Rafael, ¿qué recomienda usted para el próximo trimestre?Don Rafael, what do you recommend for next quarter?
Don Rafael:Recomiendo que ampliemos el programa a dos barrios más. Aunque el presupuesto es limitado este año, tenemos suficiente para empezar.I recommend that we expand the program to two more neighborhoods. Although the budget is limited this year (a fact), we have enough to start.
Marisol:Dudo que tengamos suficientes coordinadores capacitados para manejar dos barrios nuevos a la vez.I doubt we have enough trained coordinators to manage two new neighborhoods at once.
Don Rafael:Es una duda razonable. Pero si capacitamos a los voluntarios actuales antes de que empiece el trimestre, podríamos manejarlo.That's a reasonable doubt. But if we train the current volunteers before the quarter starts, we could manage it.
Marisol:Propongo un término medio: que empecemos con un solo barrio nuevo, para que tengamos tiempo de evaluar los resultados sin que el equipo se sienta abrumado.I propose a middle ground: that we start with just one new neighborhood, so that we have time to evaluate results without the team feeling overwhelmed.
Don Rafael:Estoy de acuerdo en parte. Aunque prefiera empezar con los dos barrios, acepto empezar con uno si revisamos el progreso en tres meses.I partly agree. Although I would prefer to start with both neighborhoods, I accept starting with one if we review progress in three months.
Camila:Entonces quedamos así: un barrio nuevo este trimestre, con una revisión en tres meses, a menos que el comité decida lo contrario.Then we agree: one new neighborhood this quarter, with a review in three months, unless the committee decides otherwise.

Reading Task

Reread the model debate excerpt above, then complete the following analysis independently or with a partner.

  1. Identify each clause that uses the subjunctive and state which of the four systems (recommendation, doubt, condition, concession/purpose) triggers it.
  2. Find the sentence with a real condition (si + present) and explain why it does not use the subjunctive.
  3. Explain how Marisol's proposal para que tengamos tiempo... sin que el equipo se sienta abrumado combines two connectors from Chapter 14 in a single sentence.
  4. Identify the moment where Don Rafael concedes part of his position without abandoning his original recommendation, and explain what language signals that partial agreement.

Writing Workshop: Propuesta para el debate

Before the debate, write a short position paper (eight to ten sentences) on the topic your instructor assigns, or on the volunteer-program expansion from the scenario above. Your position paper must include all four systems below. No major new grammar is introduced for this task; every structure comes from Chapters 11 through 14.

  1. Recomendación. Write at least one recommendation using the present subjunctive.
  2. Respuesta a la duda. Anticipate an objection and respond to it using an expression of doubt or an indefinite antecedent with the subjunctive.
  3. Condición. Include one real or hypothetical si clause.
  4. Concesión y propósito. Include one clause with aunque and one with para que, antes de que, or sin que.
  5. Revisa. Check your draft against the checklist below before the debate.

Lista de revisión:

  • My recommendation uses the subjunctive correctly after an expression of influence or necessity.
  • My response to doubt uses the subjunctive correctly after an expression of doubt or an indefinite antecedent.
  • I chose the correct pattern for a real versus a hypothetical si clause.
  • I used the indicative or subjunctive after aunque based on fact versus possibility.
  • I checked the subject of each clause before choosing between the subjunctive and the infinitive after para, antes de, or sin.
  • My accents, capitalization, and punctuation are correct throughout.

Speaking Task: El debate

Práctica controlada

With a partner, take turns reading aloud the model debate excerpt, paying attention to the shift between recommendation, doubt, condition, and concession in each turn.

Tarea comunicativa: Debate moderado

In groups of three, assign the roles of moderador/a, proponente, and escéptico/a. Using your position papers from the Writing Workshop, hold a five-minute moderated debate on the assigned topic. The proponent must recommend a course of action and concede at least one real obstacle; the skeptic must raise at least one doubt and one condition; the moderator must summarize the negotiated solution at the end, using at least one purpose clause.

Alternativa individual

If you are studying independently: record yourself presenting your position paper as if to a committee, then record a second version in which you respond to an imagined objection, set a condition for your proposal, and concede one obstacle. Listen back and check your verb forms after each of the four connectors reviewed in this chapter.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context

Moderated discussion in neighborhood associations, cooperatives, student assemblies, and similar community bodies is common across many Spanish-speaking contexts, and the language of negotiation often values acknowledging the other side's point directly (entiendo que..., es una duda razonable) before restating one's own position. This pattern of partial agreement followed by a counter-proposal reflects a widely used negotiation style rather than a single national custom, and it appears in many languages and communities in similar forms.

Regional Note (Recognition Only)

Phrases such as estoy de acuerdo en parte and propongo un término medio are broadly understood across the Spanish-speaking world. Some regions favor alternative negotiation phrases, such as a ver si nos ponemos de acuerdo; these are presented here for recognition only and do not replace the productive expressions used in this course.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake

Error: Using the indicative after an expression of doubt — Dudo que tenemos suficientes coordinadores.

Correction: Doubt and denial require the subjunctive: Dudo que tengamos suficientes coordinadores.

Common Mistake

Error: Mixing a real condition with the imperfect subjunctive pattern — Si consiguiéramos más fondos, ampliamos el programa este trimestre. (when the speaker means a plausible, near-future possibility)

Correction: A real, likely condition uses the present indicative and a future or present result: Si conseguimos más fondos, ampliaremos el programa este trimestre. Reserve the imperfect subjunctive and conditional for a condition the speaker treats as less likely or contrary to fact.

Common Mistake

Error: Conceding a point in a way that abandons the speaker's own recommendation entirely, rather than negotiating a middle ground — simply agreeing without restating a position.

Correction: Effective negotiation in this debate format acknowledges the other side and restates or adjusts one's own position: Estoy de acuerdo en parte, pero recomiendo que...

Reading Task — Model Analysis

Si el ayuntamiento nos da más fondos, ampliaríamos... is not present in this excerpt as a present-tense real condition; the excerpt's conditions use present indicative in the si clause, which does not require the subjunctive because the speaker treats the condition as plausible.   3. Marisol combines para que (purpose: so that they have time) with sin que (exclusion: without the team feeling overwhelmed) in a single sentence, both governed by the subjunctive because each clause has a different subject from the main verb.   4. Don Rafael says Estoy de acuerdo en parte and then restates a modified version of his original recommendation, showing that he concedes Marisol's point about starting smaller while keeping his underlying goal.
