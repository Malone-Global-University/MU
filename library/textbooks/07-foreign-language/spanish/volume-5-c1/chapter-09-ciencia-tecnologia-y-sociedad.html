Capítulo 9: Ciencia, tecnología y sociedad

Chapter function: Technical explanation, data commentary, and public science communication. With the permit application filed, Diego and Camila now need to summarize an environmental-impact report for their client, who must understand what the data means without a technical background. This chapter develops the specialized tense-aspect control, clause conventions, and connected multi-paragraph writing already active since Chapter 6 into the register of data commentary and technical explanation, while activating scientific and technological vocabulary as a new productive domain and recycling discourse deixis and superlatives from Chapter 8.

1. Chapter Overview

The municipal office has approved the permit, but the client's engineer has sent over a short environmental-impact report full of data and technical terms, and the client wants a plain explanation before the project moves forward. This chapter teaches learners to summarize findings, comment on data and trends, and explain technical concepts to a non-specialist audience — the everyday task of a translator, consultant, or informed citizen moving between expert and public registers. No new grammar paradigm is introduced; instead, the tense-aspect precision, clause architecture, and paragraph-level writing skills developed since Chapter 6 are extended into scientific and technological content.

2. Learning Objectives

  • Use scientific and technological vocabulary to describe findings, data, and technical processes.
  • Comment on data and trends using present, present perfect, and impersonal se constructions already active from earlier volumes, applied to a technical-explanation register.
  • Continue developing specialized clause conventions to connect cause, consequence, and contrast in explanatory writing.
  • Recycle discourse deixis (esto, eso, lo anterior) and superlatives to summarize and emphasize findings.
  • Continue developing connected multi-paragraph writing to move from a technical source to a plain-language summary.
  • Interpret a short technical report and a public-facing science article on the same topic.
  • Complete a role-play explaining a technical finding to a non-specialist and write a two-paragraph plain-language summary of a report.

3. Opening Communication Scenario

Camila has just finished reading the engineer's report and is explaining the key points to Diego before their meeting with the client.

Camila: Ya leí el informe completo. En resumen, el impacto ambiental del proyecto es mínimo, pero hay un par de datos que debemos explicarle bien al cliente.

Diego: ¿Qué tan grave es lo del ruido? Esa fue la parte que más me preocupó.

Camila: Según los datos, el nivel de ruido aumentará levemente durante la construcción, pero disminuirá en cuanto termine la obra. Eso es completamente normal en proyectos de este tipo.

Diego: ¿Y la tecnología que van a usar para reducir las emisiones?

Camila: Es bastante innovadora. El informe explica que se ha probado en proyectos similares y que ha reducido las emisiones muchísimo. Dicho esto, todavía se necesita monitorear los resultados durante el primer año.

Diego: Entonces, ¿cómo se lo explicamos al cliente sin usar tanta jerga técnica?

Camila: Con ejemplos concretos. Lo anterior es exactamente lo que vamos a preparar para la reunión.

5. Pronunciation and Discourse Focus

No new sound system is introduced. This section develops intonation and phrasing for presenting a data point clearly — pausing before and after a number or percentage, and using a falling tone to signal that a finding has been fully stated, as a presenter would when reporting results aloud.

6. Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter develops the specialized tense-aspect usage, clause conventions, and connected multi-paragraph writing already active since Chapter 6, and applies discourse deixis and superlatives — activated in Chapter 8 — to the task of commenting on data and summarizing technical findings for a non-specialist audience.

6.1 Commenting on data: present, present perfect, and future for trends

Data commentary in Spanish moves between three time frames: the present for general facts and ongoing patterns, the present perfect for what a study or report has already shown, and the future for a projected change. Choosing the right tense signals whether a claim describes the current situation, an established finding, or an expected outcome.

Time frameFunctionExample
Presentgeneral fact or stable patternLa tecnología reduce las emisiones de manera significativa.
Present perfecta finding already established by researchLos estudios han demostrado que esta tecnología funciona en proyectos similares.
Futurea projected or expected changeEl nivel de ruido aumentará levemente durante la construcción.
Language NoteData commentary favors los datos muestran que..., el informe indica que..., and según los datos... as neutral framing expressions. These recycle the hedged, source-attributing patterns already active from the professional register in Chapter 7, now applied to research findings rather than business proposals.

6.2 Specialized clause conventions: cause, consequence, and contrast

Technical explanation depends on connecting ideas precisely. This chapter develops connectors already available from earlier volumes into a more deliberate explanatory pattern, useful for moving from a technical source to a plain-language summary.

FunctionConnectorExample
Causedebido a (que), ya queEl ruido aumentará debido a la maquinaria pesada.
Consequencepor lo tanto, como resultadoLa tecnología ha reducido las emisiones; por lo tanto, el impacto ambiental es menor.
Contrastsin embargo, aunqueEl ruido aumentará durante la obra; sin embargo, disminuirá al terminar.

6.3 Recycling discourse deixis and superlatives in technical summary

The discourse-deixis expressions and superlatives activated in Chapter 8 are especially useful for summarizing a technical source in plain language: esto and eso let a speaker refer back to a finding just mentioned, and superlatives let a speaker highlight the most important point without repeating every detail.

ExpressionExample in this context
esto / esoEl nivel de ruido aumentará un quince por ciento; esto es normal en esta etapa del proyecto.
lo anteriorLo anterior explica por qué el informe recomienda monitorear los resultados.
absolute superlativeLa reducción de emisiones ha sido altísima en comparación con proyectos anteriores.
comparative superlativeEste es el hallazgo más importante del informe.

6.4 Ser/estar in technical description

Ser and estar continue to distinguish classification from current condition, now applied to data, findings, and technical processes.

ContextExample
Classifying a findingEl impacto ambiental es mínimo, según el informe.
Describing the current state of a processEl monitoreo todavía está en curso.
Describing a result stateLos resultados ya están confirmados por un segundo estudio.

7. Model Dialogue: Explaining Findings to a Client

Cliente: Entonces, ¿qué tan preocupante es el aumento en el nivel de ruido?

Camila: No es preocupante. El informe indica que el ruido aumentará levemente durante la construcción, pero disminuirá en cuanto termine la obra. Esto es normal en proyectos de este tamaño.

Cliente: ¿Y qué hay de las emisiones?

Diego: Ahí está el hallazgo más importante del informe: la tecnología que van a usar ha reducido las emisiones muchísimo en proyectos similares. Los datos muestran una reducción altísima, comparada con métodos anteriores.

Cliente: Suena bien, pero no entiendo mucho de la parte técnica.

Camila: No se preocupe, para eso estamos aquí. En resumen: el proyecto es sostenible, el impacto ambiental es mínimo y, debido a la nueva tecnología, las emisiones serán mucho menores que en construcciones anteriores. Lo anterior es, en pocas palabras, lo que necesita saber para tomar su decisión.

Cliente: Perfecto. Muchísimas gracias por explicármelo con claridad.

11. Reading Practice

Exercise 9.7 — Reading a short technical report

"Resumen técnico: Según los datos recopilados en la muestra analizada, el nivel de ruido aumentará aproximadamente un quince por ciento durante la fase de construcción, debido al uso de maquinaria pesada. Sin embargo, este aumento es temporal y disminuirá una vez finalizada la obra. En cuanto a las emisiones, la tecnología propuesta ha demostrado, en proyectos similares, una reducción altísima en comparación con métodos convencionales. Los estudios muestran que esta es la tecnología más eficiente disponible actualmente para este tipo de construcción. Por lo tanto, el impacto ambiental general del proyecto se considera mínimo. Dicho esto, se recomienda monitorear los resultados durante el primer año para confirmar estas proyecciones."

  1. By approximately what percentage will noise increase during construction, and why?
  2. What does the report say about the proposed technology compared to conventional methods?
  3. Identify one cause-consequence connector in the text and explain the relationship it expresses.
  4. Find the comparative superlative in the passage and explain what it is comparing.

12. Writing Workshop

Task: Write a two-paragraph plain-language summary (120–150 words) of the technical report in Exercise 9.7, addressed to a client with no technical background. Your summary must use at least three scientific/technological vocabulary items, at least one connector of cause, consequence, or contrast, at least one discourse-deixis expression, and at least one superlative.

Stage 1 — Plan

Identify the two or three findings the client most needs to understand, and decide which technical terms need a plain-language gloss.

Stage 2 — Draft

Write your summary in two paragraphs: one on the findings, one on what the client should expect going forward.

Stage 3 — Revise

Check that technical terms are explained rather than simply repeated, confirm that your connectors correctly express cause, consequence, or contrast, and verify that your discourse-deixis expression clearly points back to a finding you actually stated.

Interpretation-awareness checklist

  • At least three scientific or technological vocabulary items are used accurately.
  • At least one connector of cause, consequence, or contrast is used correctly.
  • At least one discourse-deixis expression clearly refers back to a previously stated idea.
  • At least one superlative is formed and used correctly.
  • The summary remains understandable to a reader with no technical background.

13. Culture and Communication

Public science and technology communication varies across Spanish-speaking countries in how directly technical findings are shared with the public, how much government agencies publish in accessible language, and how much responsibility falls on journalists, consultants, or educators to translate expert findings for general audiences. What is broadly useful for a learner is the underlying skill of moving between an expert register and a plain-language register — recognizing which vocabulary needs no explanation and which needs to be reframed — rather than assuming any single country's science-communication practices apply everywhere.

14. Common Mistakes

  • Treating los datos as singular and pairing it with a singular verb, instead of the correct plural agreement (los datos muestran, not el dato muestra when referring to data collectively).
  • Confusing la muestra (sample) with la prueba (test, proof) in a research context.
  • Overusing the future tense for findings that are already established, rather than the present perfect (los estudios han demostrado, not los estudios demostrarán, for a finding already published).
  • Stringing technical terms together in a client-facing summary without glossing them, defeating the purpose of a plain-language explanation.
  • Choosing ser where estar is needed to describe the current state of a process, or the reverse when classifying a finding.
