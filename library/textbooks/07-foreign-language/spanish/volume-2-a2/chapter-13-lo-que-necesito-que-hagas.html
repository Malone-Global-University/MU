Lo que necesito que hagas

El subjuntivo presente después de expresiones de deseo, necesidad y recomendación · Speak and Write Spanish: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Form the present subjunctive of regular -ar, -er, and -ir verbs.
  2. Recognize and use a small set of high-frequency irregular present subjunctive forms as controlled vocabulary items.
  3. Express desire, need, and recommendation using querer que, necesitar que, recomendar que, and es importante que with a dependent clause.
  4. Distinguish sentences that report information (indicative) from sentences that try to influence someone's actions (subjunctive).
  5. Continue using commands, podría/quisiera, and combined object pronouns alongside the new subjunctive pattern.
  6. Coordinate responsibilities with another person using polite, dependency-based requests.

Communication Scenario

The welcome gathering that Sra. Duarte organized went well, and now the building's residents are getting ready for a small community project: repainting the shared hallway and organizing a supply closet. Sra. Duarte sends a message explaining what she needs from each resident. Rodrigo, in turn, asks his friend Camila for help, and Camila makes a few recommendations of her own. All of these exchanges share a new pattern: instead of simply stating a fact, the speaker is trying to get someone else to do something — and Spanish marks that difference in the verb itself.

  1. Think of a time you needed to ask several people to each do something different. How did you organize the request?
  2. Notice the difference between telling someone what is true (Tú pintas bien) and telling someone what you want them to do (Quiero que pintes la pared).

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Note — Vowel Shift in the Subjunctive

The clearest audible signal of the present subjunctive is a vowel switch: -ar verbs, which normally end in -a in the present indicative, shift to -e (pinta → pinte); -er and -ir verbs, which normally end in -e, shift to -a (come → coma, escribe → escriba). Listen for this "opposite vowel" pattern — it is often the fastest way to hear that a sentence has moved from a statement into a request.

Stress stays on the same syllable as the related present-tense form. In pinte, the stress stays on pin-; in coma, the stress stays on co-. No new written accent is added for regular subjunctive forms at this stage.

Grammar: El subjuntivo presente

Purpose: express what you want, need, or recommend that someone else do, using a dependent que clause with the present subjunctive.

Grammar Note — Formando el subjuntivo presente regular

To form the present subjunctive of a regular verb, start from the yo form of the present indicative, drop the -o, and add the "opposite" set of endings.

Sujeto-ar: pintar-er: comer-ir: escribir
yopintecomaescriba
túpintescomasescribas
usted / él / ellapintecomaescriba
nosotros/nosotraspintemoscomamosescribamos
ustedes / ellos / ellaspintencomanescriban

Verbs with an irregular yo form in the present indicative keep that same irregularity throughout the subjunctive, because the subjunctive is built from the yo stem: tener → tengo → tenga, tengas, tenga...; hacer → hago → haga, hagas, haga...; salir → salgo → salga, salgas, salga....

A small group of very common verbs does not follow this pattern and must be learned as fixed forms at this stage: ser → sea, estar → esté, ir → vaya, saber → sepa, dar → dé, haber → haya. Treat these six as memorized items for now; their full irregular pattern is not analyzed further in this chapter.

Grammar Note — Deseo, necesidad y recomendación

When a sentence has two different subjects — one who wants, needs, or recommends something, and another who would carry it out — Spanish links the two parts with que, and the second verb goes into the subjunctive.

  • Quiero que pintes el pasillo. (I want you to paint the hallway.)
  • Necesitamos que organices el armario. (We need you to organize the closet.)
  • Le recomiendo que hable con la Sra. Duarte primero. (I recommend that you speak with Sra. Duarte first.)
  • Es importante que todos colaboren. (It's important that everyone pitch in.)

If there is only one subject for both actions, Spanish uses an infinitive instead of a subjunctive clause: Quiero pintar el pasillo (I want to paint the hallway myself) versus Quiero que tú pintes el pasillo (I want you to paint it). This subject-change rule is the single most reliable test for whether a dependent clause needs the subjunctive.

Grammar Note — Informar versus influir

Compare these two sentences: Sé que Camila pinta bien. (I know that Camila paints well — a fact, indicative) and Quiero que Camila pinte el pasillo. (I want Camila to paint the hallway — an attempt to influence her actions, subjunctive). The main verb determines the mood of the dependent clause: verbs and expressions that report or describe reality use the indicative; verbs and expressions that try to move someone toward an action use the subjunctive.

This chapter activates only desire, need, and recommendation as subjunctive triggers. Doubt, possibility, and emotion are introduced as subjunctive triggers in the next chapter.

Model Dialogue

Contexto: Rodrigo le pide ayuda a Camila con el proyecto del pasillo. Se conocen bien y usan tú.

CAMILAClaro. ¿Qué quieres que haga primero?Of course. What do you want me to do first?
RODRIGONecesito que compres pintura blanca. Yo me encargo de las brochas.I need you to buy white paint. I'll take charge of the brushes.
CAMILADe acuerdo. Te recomiendo que hablemos con la Sra. Duarte antes, para confirmar la hora.Agreed. I recommend that we talk to Sra. Duarte first, to confirm the time.
RODRIGOBuena idea. Es importante que todos lleguemos a la misma hora.Good idea. It's important that we all arrive at the same time.
CAMILAEspero que no llueva ese día.I hope it doesn't rain that day.
RODRIGOYo también. Y quisiera que trajeras música, para que sea más divertido.Me too. And I would like you to bring music, so it's more fun.
CAMILAPerfecto. Nos vemos el sábado, entonces.Perfect. See you Saturday, then.

Preguntas de comprensión:

  2. ¿De qué se encarga Rodrigo?
  3. ¿Qué recomienda Camila antes de empezar?
  4. ¿Qué quisiera Rodrigo que Camila traiga?

Reading

Mensaje sobre el proyecto del pasillo

Estimados residentes: Como saben, vamos a pintar el pasillo principal y organizar el armario de suministros este sábado. Necesito que cada persona confirme si puede venir, y que me avise antes del viernes.

Writing Workshop: Un mensaje de coordinación

Your writing task for this chapter is a short message in which you organize a group task and ask several people to do specific things.

  1. Estudia el modelo. Reread the message from Sra. Duarte above. Mark every use of que + subjunctive and identify which verb triggers it.
  2. Planifica. Choose a group task (a class project, a house chore, an office task) and decide who needs to do what. Decide whether you will address your audience with tú or with usted/ustedes, and stay consistent.
  3. Escribe el primer borrador. Write a message of at least six sentences that uses querer que, necesitar que, and at least one of recomendar que, sugerir que, or es importante que.
  4. Revisa. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.
  5. Escribe la versión final. Produce a clean final version of your message.

Lista de revisión:

  • Every dependent clause after a desire, need, or recommendation trigger uses the subjunctive, not the indicative.
  • I used the infinitive, not the subjunctive, whenever the subject of both verbs is the same person.
  • I used at least three different subjunctive triggers from this chapter.
  • My address form is consistent throughout the message.
  • My accents and punctuation, including inverted marks, are correct.

Speaking Practice

Práctica controlada

With a partner, take turns telling each other one thing you want, one thing you need, and one thing you recommend, using quiero que, necesito que, and recomiendo que.

Tarea comunicativa: Organizar el proyecto del pasillo

With a partner or small group, role-play the planning of a shared task. Assign responsibilities to each other using quiero que, necesito que, and at least one recommendation. Confirm the plan at the end.

Alternativa individual

If you are studying independently: record yourself giving instructions to an imaginary group of three people, assigning a different task to each person using the subjunctive.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context

In many Spanish-speaking workplaces, households, and community groups, a request phrased with necesito que or quisiera que can sound noticeably more collaborative than a blunt command, even between people who know each other well. Softening a request with the subjunctive or with formulaic politeness does not necessarily signal distance — it often signals that the speaker values the relationship and is inviting cooperation rather than simply issuing an order. As with other register choices, the exact balance of directness varies by relationship, workplace culture, region, and individual style.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake

Error: Using the indicative after a desire or need trigger — Quiero que tú pintas el pasillo.

Correction: A change-of-subject clause after querer que requires the subjunctive: Quiero que tú pintes el pasillo.

Common Mistake

Error: Using que + subjunctive when there is only one subject — Quiero que pinte el pasillo to mean "I want to paint the hallway myself."

Correction: With a single subject, use the infinitive instead: Quiero pintar el pasillo. Reserve the que + subjunctive pattern for sentences with two different subjects.

Common Mistake

Error: Forgetting that a verb's irregular yo stem carries into the subjunctive — writing tenga as tenoa or ignoring the irregularity entirely.

Correction: Build the subjunctive from the present-tense yo form: tener → tengo → tenga, tengas, tenga..., not from the infinitive.

Looking Ahead

Lo que ya sabes

You can distinguish when to use the subjunctive versus the infinitive, and you can coordinate a group task using these patterns alongside your existing command forms, conditional politeness, and combined object pronouns.
