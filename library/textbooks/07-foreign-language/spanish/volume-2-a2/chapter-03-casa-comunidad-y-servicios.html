Casa, comunidad y servicios

Ubicación, existencia y peticiones prácticas · Speak and Write Spanish: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Name common neighborhood places and services and describe what you can do there.
  2. Use ser, estar, and hay together accurately, distinguishing identity, location, and existence in the same conversation.
  3. Use core prepositions of place — en, cerca de, lejos de, al lado de, enfrente de, entre, detrás de, delante de — to describe where things are.
  4. Form and use the contractions al and del correctly.
  5. Recognize and use personal a with specific people.
  6. Make polite, practical requests for information and services in a neighborhood setting.

Communication Scenario

Diego has just moved to a new neighborhood and does not yet know where anything is. He stops a neighbor, Rosa, on the street to ask about the bank, the pharmacy, and the closest place to buy groceries. Rosa is happy to help and gives him clear directions using the kind of everyday location language that makes a new neighborhood feel manageable.

  2. What phrases do you already know in Spanish for saying where something is located?

Pronunciation Focus

Sound Focus — Linking Words in Connected Speech

Spanish speakers normally run words together in a phrase rather than pausing between each one. When a word ending in a vowel is followed by a word beginning with a vowel, the two vowels blend into a single syllable in natural speech: la_estación, está_enfrente. This is especially noticeable with the prepositions in this chapter, since so many end in -e or -a: cerca_de la farmacia, al lado_del banco.

Writing Note — The Contractions al and del

a + el → al and de + el → del are required contractions, not optional shortcuts: Voy al banco (not a el banco), Está cerca del parque (not de el parque). The contraction does not happen with ella, usted, ellos, or a proper name: Voy a El Salvador keeps a El separate because El is part of the country's name.

Práctica — lectura en voz alta: La farmacia está al lado del banco. El supermercado está enfrente de la panadería, entre la plaza y la parada de autobús. La biblioteca está un poco lejos, pero el parque está muy cerca.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Purpose: this chapter reviews and sharpens ser, estar, and hay together, introduces core place prepositions and the contractions they require, and adds personal a so you can talk accurately about people and places in your community.

3.1 Ser, estar y hay juntos

VerboFunciónEjemplo
seridentity, origin, what something isEl banco es grande.
estarlocation of something specific/knownEl banco está en la calle Ocho.
hayexistence of something non-specificHay un banco en la esquina.
Grammar Note — Existence Is Not the Same as Location

Hay introduces something the listener does not yet know about or cannot identify specifically: Hay una farmacia cerca de aquí (there is a pharmacy near here — any pharmacy, not a specific one the listener already has in mind). Once that pharmacy has been identified, use estar to say where it is: La farmacia está al lado del banco. A common learner error is using estar for both jobs. Compare: Hay un parque en el barrio (existence) versus El parque está cerca de mi casa (location of a specific, known park). Hay never changes form for number or agreement — it stays hay whether you are talking about one thing or several.

  • Hay dos farmacias en esta calle.There are two pharmacies on this street. (existence, plural, still "hay")
  • La farmacia grande está enfrente del correo.The big pharmacy is across from the post office. (specific location)

3.2 Preposiciones de lugar y las contracciones al/del

  • El supermercado está cerca de la estación.The supermarket is near the station.
  • La panadería está al lado del banco.The bakery is next to the bank.
  • La biblioteca está entre el parque y la plaza.The library is between the park and the plaza.
  • Voy al correo y después a la farmacia.I'm going to the post office and then to the pharmacy.

3.3 La a personal

Spanish marks a direct object with a when that object is a specific person (or a pet treated like one). This a has no direct English translation — it simply signals that a person, not a thing, is being talked about or acted upon.

  • Busco a mi vecina Rosa.I'm looking for my neighbor Rosa. (specific person → personal a)
  • Busco un banco cerca de aquí.I'm looking for a bank near here. (a thing, not a person → no personal a)
  • ¿Conoces a la farmacéutica de la esquina?Do you know the pharmacist on the corner? (specific person → personal a)

Personal a also combines with the contraction rule: a + el → al, so "I'm looking for the doctor" is Busco al médico.

Model Dialogue

Contexto: Diego, recién llegado al barrio, para a su vecina Rosa en la calle. Usan usted porque acaban de conocerse y Rosa es mayor que Diego.

DIEGOPerdón, ¿me puede ayudar? Soy nuevo en el barrio.Excuse me, can you help me? I'm new to the neighborhood.
ROSAClaro, con mucho gusto. ¿Qué necesita?Of course, gladly. What do you need?
DIEGO¿Hay un banco por aquí cerca?Is there a bank nearby?
ROSASí, hay uno en la próxima calle. Está al lado de la farmacia.Yes, there's one on the next street. It's next to the pharmacy.
DIEGOPerfecto. ¿Y dónde está la farmacia exactamente?Perfect. And where exactly is the pharmacy?
ROSALa farmacia está enfrente del supermercado, entre la panadería y el correo. No está lejos.The pharmacy is across from the supermarket, between the bakery and the post office. It's not far.
DIEGOMuchas gracias. Una pregunta más: ¿conoce usted a alguien que pueda arreglar mi lavadora?Thank you so much. One more question: do you know anyone who can fix my washing machine?
ROSASí, conozco a un técnico muy bueno. Vive cerca de la plaza. Le doy su número.Yes, I know a very good technician. He lives near the plaza. I'll give you his number.
DIEGOMuchísimas gracias por su ayuda.Thank you so much for your help.

Preguntas de comprensión:

  1. ¿Qué busca Diego primero?
  2. ¿Dónde está la farmacia exactamente?
  3. ¿A quién conoce Rosa que puede ayudar a Diego?
  4. ¿Por qué usan Diego y Rosa la forma usted?

Reading

Read the following practical neighborhood notice, the kind of short public text you might see posted on a community board.

Aviso del barrio

¡Bienvenidos al barrio Las Flores! Aquí tienen información útil para los nuevos vecinos. El supermercado más cercano está en la calle Ocho, cerca de la estación de metro. Hay dos farmacias: una está al lado del banco y la otra está enfrente de la plaza. El correo está entre la panadería y la lavandería, y abre de lunes a viernes.

Si necesitan ayuda, pueden hablar con Rosa Mendoza, la representante del barrio. Vive cerca del parque y siempre está dispuesta a ayudar a los nuevos residentes.

Preguntas de comprensión:

  1. ¿Dónde está el supermercado más cercano?
  2. ¿Cuántas farmacias hay en el barrio, y dónde están?
  3. ¿Quién es Rosa Mendoza y dónde vive?

Writing Workshop: Guía de mi barrio

Your writing task for this chapter is a short practical note, similar to the reading model, that welcomes a new neighbor and describes at least four places in your neighborhood and where they are.

  1. Estudia el modelo. Reread the neighborhood notice above. Underline every use of hay, every use of estar, and every preposition of place.
  2. Planifica. List at least four places in your own neighborhood (real or invented) and one detail about the location of each.
  3. Escribe el primer borrador. Write at least six sentences in Spanish using hay to introduce places and estar plus a preposition to locate them.
  4. Revisa. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.
  5. Escribe la versión final. Produce a clean final version.

Lista de revisión:

  • I used hay only to introduce something non-specific, and estar only for the location of something already identified.
  • My contractions al and del appear wherever a or de meets el.
  • I used personal a if I mentioned a specific person.
  • My articles and nouns agree in gender and number.
  • My accents and punctuation follow Spanish conventions.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context

Neighborhoods across Spanish-speaking communities vary enormously in layout, services, and daily rhythm, shaped by city planning, climate, and local custom rather than by a single shared pattern. In some neighborhoods, small independent shops and pharmacies are within easy walking distance and stay closed for part of the afternoon; in others, larger stores and pharmacy chains stay open continuously. Asking a neighbor for directions or recommendations, as Diego does in this chapter, is a common and welcome way to get reliable local information — avoid assuming that any single arrangement of neighborhood services is universal.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake

Error: Using estar to introduce something non-specific — Está un banco cerca de aquí.

Correction: Use hay for existence — Hay un banco cerca de aquí.

Common Mistake

Error: Forgetting the contraction — La farmacia está al lado de el banco.

Correction: De plus el must contract — La farmacia está al lado del banco.

Common Mistake

Error: Dropping personal a with a specific person — Busco mi vecina.

Correction: Mark the specific person with personal a — Busco a mi vecina.
