Proyecto 2: Contar y resolver

Evaluación integrada: narración en el pasado e interacción para resolver problemas · Speak and Write Spanish: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this project chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Narrate a connected sequence of past events using the preterite for completed actions and the imperfect for background, description, and ongoing situations.
  2. Use the present perfect to talk about experiences and achievements connected to the present moment.
  3. Describe a problem clearly, explain what went wrong, and propose or negotiate a solution.
  4. Combine narration and problem-solving in a single connected conversation and a single connected written account.
  5. Demonstrate consistent address-form use, agreement, and Spanish-specific orthography across an extended spoken and written performance.
  6. Self-assess a spoken and written performance using the criteria from this unit.

Communication Scenario

Camila's new apartment had a problem with the water heater during her first week living there. She called the building manager, explained what had happened, described what she had already tried, and worked out a solution together with him. Telling this story well means doing two things at once: narrating a sequence of past events clearly, and managing a real-time problem-solving conversation. This project chapter asks you to do both, drawing on everything from Historias recientes, Antes y ahora, Algo salió mal, and Experiencias y logros.

  1. Think of a time something went wrong at home, at work, or while traveling, and you had to explain the problem to someone and find a solution.
  2. Notice which parts of your story describe background or ongoing situations, and which parts describe specific completed events.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Note — Shifting Between Narrative Tenses

Fluent narration depends on smooth, unhesitating shifts between verb forms rather than on any single new sound. As you tell your story aloud, pay attention to keeping stress accurate on preterite forms such as llamé, salió, and llegamos, where the stressed syllable often carries the meaning-bearing accent. Keep the linked haber + participle pattern from Chapter 9 smooth and connected: ya he hablado, not ya... he... hablado with pauses between words.

Practice reading a short narration aloud at a natural pace, without stopping to search for each verb form. Fluency, not perfection, is the goal of this review.

Grammar Review: Narración y resolución

Purpose: combine three past-time systems and problem-solving language into one connected performance, rather than practicing any one system in isolation.

Grammar Note — Three Layers of a Problem Story

A well-told problem-and-solution story usually moves through three layers, and each layer tends to favor a different past-time system:

  • El fondo (imperfecto): what was going on before or during the problem — Era mi primera semana en el apartamento. Todo estaba tranquilo.
  • El evento (pretérito): the specific thing that happened — De repente, el agua caliente dejó de funcionar.
  • Lo que ya se ha hecho (presente perfecto): steps already taken toward a solution, connected to the present — Ya he llamado al administrador dos veces.

Moving fluidly among these three layers, rather than choosing only one, is what this project assesses.

  • Era temprano y yo estaba lista para ducharme cuando noté que no había agua caliente.It was early and I was ready to shower when I noticed there was no hot water.
  • Llamé al administrador enseguida, pero él ya había salido del edificio.I called the manager right away, but he had already left the building. (recognition: pluperfect)
  • Ya he hablado con él dos veces, y todavía no hemos resuelto el problema por completo.I've already spoken with him twice, and we haven't completely resolved the problem yet.
  • Finalmente, llegó un técnico y arregló el calentador en una hora.Finally, a technician arrived and fixed the water heater in an hour.

The pluperfect (había salido) appears above only as a glossed, recognition-only form to support natural storytelling. It is not a productive target of this chapter or volume.

Práctica — Elige la forma correcta según la función (pretérito, imperfecto o presente perfecto):

  1. Cuando yo _____ (llegar) a casa, _____ (ser) las nueve de la noche y _____ (llover) mucho.
  2. Todavía no _____ (nosotros, recibir) una respuesta del administrador.
  3. El técnico _____ (revisar) el calentador y _____ (decir) que _____ (necesitar) una pieza nueva.
  4. Ya _____ (yo, hablar) con el vecino, y él _____ (tener) el mismo problema el año pasado.

Model Dialogue

Contexto: Camila llama al administrador de su edificio, el señor Duarte, para explicar el problema con el agua caliente y buscar una solución. No se conocen bien, así que ella usa usted.

CAMILABuenas tardes, señor Duarte. Le escribo porque tengo un problema con el agua caliente. Esta mañana me desperté, entré a la ducha, y de repente el agua se puso fría.Good afternoon, Mr. Duarte. I'm writing because I have a problem with the hot water. This morning I woke up, got in the shower, and suddenly the water turned cold.
SR. DUARTEQué mal. ¿Ya ha revisado si es solo su apartamento o todo el edificio?That's bad. Have you already checked whether it's just your apartment or the whole building?
CAMILASí, ya hablé con mi vecina y ella todavía tiene agua caliente. Por eso creo que es solo mi calentador.Yes, I already spoke with my neighbor and she still has hot water. That's why I think it's just my water heater.
SR. DUARTEEntiendo. ¿Esto le ha pasado antes?I understand. Has this happened to you before?
CAMILANo, nunca. Todo estaba normal hasta esta mañana. Además, todavía no he podido lavar los platos.No, never. Everything was normal until this morning. Also, I still haven't been able to wash the dishes.
SR. DUARTELa entiendo perfectamente. Voy a mandar a un técnico hoy mismo. ¿Podría estar en casa entre las dos y las cuatro?I understand completely. I'm going to send a technician today. Could you be home between two and four?
CAMILASí, puedo estar en casa esa tarde. Muchas gracias por resolverlo tan rápido.Yes, I can be home that afternoon. Thank you so much for resolving it so quickly.
SR. DUARTECon mucho gusto. Le escribo cuando el técnico esté en camino.You're very welcome. I'll write to you when the technician is on the way.

Preguntas de comprensión:

  1. ¿Qué le pasó a Camila esta mañana?
  2. ¿Cómo supo Camila que el problema era solo de su apartamento?
  3. ¿Qué todavía no ha podido hacer Camila?
  4. ¿Qué solución le ofrece el señor Duarte?

Reading

Read the following message that Diego wrote to a friend describing a travel problem he resolved last month.

Un problema en el aeropuerto

¡Hola, Marisol! Quiero contarte lo que me pasó el mes pasado quiero contarte lo que pasó en mi viaje a Lima. Todo iba bien: yo estaba tranquilo en el aeropuerto, esperando mi vuelo, cuando de repente anunciaron que el vuelo se había cancelado por el clima. Me quedé muy preocupado porque tenía una reunión importante al día siguiente.

Fui directamente al mostrador de la aerolínea y hablé con una empleada muy amable. Le expliqué la situación y ella buscó otras opciones. Ya había otros dos vuelos ese día, pero los dos estaban completos. Finalmente, ella encontró un asiento en un vuelo por la noche, y yo acepté enseguida.

Ahora, cuando viajo, siempre reviso el clima antes de salir de casa. Nunca he tenido otro problema tan grande como ese, pero ya he aprendido a estar preparado. ¿Y tú? ¿Alguna vez has tenido un problema parecido durante un viaje?

Preguntas de comprensión:

  1. ¿Qué estaba haciendo Diego cuando anunciaron el problema?
  2. ¿Por qué se canceló el vuelo?
  3. ¿Cómo resolvió Diego el problema?
  4. ¿Qué ha aprendido Diego de esta experiencia?

Writing Workshop: Mi problema y mi solución

Your writing task for this project is a connected account of a real or invented problem, describing the background, the event, the steps you have already taken, and how the situation was resolved.

  1. Estudia el modelo. Reread "Un problema en el aeropuerto" above. Mark which verbs describe background (imperfect), which describe specific events (preterite), and which describe already-completed steps (present perfect).
  2. Planifica. Choose a problem. Note the background situation, the specific event that went wrong, at least two steps you already took, and how it was resolved.
  3. Escribe el primer borrador. Write at least ten sentences in Spanish that move through all three layers: background, event, and completed steps.
  4. Revisa. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.
  5. Escribe la versión final. Produce a clean final version organized as a connected account, not a disconnected list of sentences.

Lista de revisión:

  • I used the imperfect for background and ongoing situations.
  • I used the preterite for specific completed events.
  • I used the present perfect at least twice for steps already taken.
  • I used ya or todavía no at least once.
  • My address form is consistent throughout the account.
  • My articles, nouns, and adjectives agree in gender and number.
  • My accents and punctuation, including inverted marks, are correct.

Speaking Practice

With a partner, take turns completing the sentence Una vez, yo estaba _____ cuando de repente _____. using the imperfect for the first blank and the preterite for the second.

Tarea comunicativa: Contar y resolver

With a partner, role-play a phone call reporting a problem and negotiating a solution, similar to the model dialogue. One person reports the problem and describes what has already been tried; the other offers a solution. Use usted, since this is an interaction with a service provider.

Alternativa individual

If you are studying independently: record a two-to-three-minute account combining background, specific events, and at least two present-perfect statements about steps already taken to resolve a problem.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context

Reporting a problem to a landlord, service provider, or company representative typically works best when it combines a clear, factual account of what happened with a polite, solution-oriented tone, rather than an emotional complaint alone. In many professional and service contexts across the Spanish-speaking world, addressing the issue calmly and offering to work together toward a solution tends to produce a more effective and pleasant interaction than a purely confrontational approach. This is a matter of communicative strategy and personal style, not a fixed national rule.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake

Error: Using only the preterite for an entire story, even for the background parts — Era temprano becomes Fue temprano when describing an ongoing situation.

Correction: Background description and ongoing situations normally take the imperfect: Era temprano y yo estaba en casa, not Fue temprano y yo estuve en casa, unless you specifically mean a bounded, completed period of time.

Common Mistake

Error: Using the preterite where the present perfect is needed to connect an action to the present — Ya llamé tres veces hoy y todavía no resolvieron nada, losing the sense of an ongoing, unresolved situation.

Correction: When the action's relevance continues into the present, the present perfect fits better: Ya he llamado tres veces hoy y todavía no han resuelto nada.

Common Mistake

Error: Mixing tú and usted within the same conversation with a service provider — ¿Puede usted ayudarme? ¿Tienes un momento?

Correction: Address forms must stay consistent throughout an interaction: ¿Puede usted ayudarme? ¿Tiene usted un momento?, not a mix of usted and tú with the same person.
