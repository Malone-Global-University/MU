Matizar una postura

Expresar grados de certeza sobre la propia opinión con precisión y matiz · Speak and Write Spanish: Advanced Public, Professional, and Academic Spanish

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify and use vocabulary for hedging, evaluating, and signaling degrees of certainty about a stance.
  2. Distinguish expressions of certainty that take the indicative from expressions of doubt or evaluation that take the subjunctive.
  3. Use impersonal evaluative expressions such as es posible que, es evidente que, and no cabe duda de que with the correct mood.
  4. Soften or strengthen a claim without abandoning the argumentative structure introduced in Chapter 8.
  5. Discuss a debatable topic aloud, adjusting the degree of certainty in each statement according to the strength of the available evidence.
  6. Write a paragraph that states a stance with appropriately graded certainty, integrating hedging language with the concessive structures from Chapter 8.

This chapter continues Unit II. It introduces hedging and evaluative vocabulary and a broader indicative/subjunctive contrast tied to the speaker's own certainty as new productive content, while developing the claim-evidence-concession structure and the aunque / a pesar de que system from Chapter 8, and the paraphrase and attribution language from Chapter 7.

Communication Scenario

Daniela's hybrid-model proposal from Chapter 8 has moved to a planning committee. Some data support the proposal strongly; other data are still preliminary. Daniela must present her recommendation in a way that distinguishes what she is confident about from what remains uncertain, without sounding either overconfident or evasive.

  1. Think of a time you had to give an opinion or a recommendation while being honest about what you did not yet know for certain. How did you signal the difference between "I'm sure" and "I think, but I'm not certain"?
  2. As you work through this chapter, notice how Spanish marks that difference not just with vocabulary, but with a change of verb mood.

Purpose: to acquire the vocabulary needed to grade certainty, evaluate a claim, and soften or reinforce a stance without losing precision.

Grammar Focus: Indicativo y subjuntivo al matizar la propia postura

Grammar Note

Purpose: to select the indicative or the subjunctive after impersonal and personal expressions of certainty, doubt, and evaluation, so that the grammar itself signals how confident the speaker is.

Form: impersonal or personal expression + que + clause. The clause verb is indicative or subjunctive depending on the meaning of the main expression, not on any fixed list to memorize by rote.

Meaning and mood selection: Expressions that assert certainty or present a conclusion as an established fact — such as es evidente que, no cabe duda de que, or está claro que — take the indicative. Expressions that present a claim as merely possible, likely, doubtful, or subject to evaluation — such as es posible que, es probable que, dudo que, or es una lástima que — take the subjunctive, because the speaker is not asserting the following clause as a settled fact.

  • Es evidente que la encuesta muestra una preferencia clara por el modelo híbrido. Certainty (indicative): It's evident that the survey shows a clear preference for the hybrid model.
  • Es posible que algunos resultados cambien cuando tengamos más datos. Possibility (subjunctive): It's possible that some results will change once we have more data.
  • Dudo que el comité apruebe la propuesta sin una prueba piloto. Doubt (subjunctive): I doubt the committee will approve the proposal without a pilot test.

Negation and mood: Negating a certainty expression usually shifts it toward uncertainty and triggers the subjunctive: Es evidente que el plan funciona (indicative) becomes No es evidente que el plan funcione (subjunctive), because denying certainty removes the basis for asserting the clause as fact.

Use: Grading certainty is a way of building credibility, not weakness. A recommendation that marks what is well supported with es evidente que and what is still open with es posible que or hasta cierto punto is generally more persuasive than a recommendation that treats every claim as equally certain.

  1. Read each claim and decide whether the speaker is presenting it as certain or as an evaluation/possibility.
  2. Complete each sentence with the correct mood.
    • Es evidente que el nuevo horario (reducir / reduzca) el estrés de los empleados.
    • Es posible que el comité (necesitar / necesite) más tiempo para decidir.
    • No cabe duda de que la participación (haber aumentado / haya aumentado) desde marzo.
    • Dudo que todos los departamentos (estar / estén) listos para el cambio al mismo tiempo.

Reading: Una opinión matizada sobre el trabajo remoto

Read the following adapted opinion piece, which extends the debate from Chapter 8 by grading how certain the writer is about each point. As you read, identify which claims are presented as certain and which are presented as merely probable, possible, or doubtful.

¿Qué tan seguros podemos estar sobre el modelo híbrido?

Es evidente que el programa de bienestar, en su forma actual, no ha logrado una participación equitativa entre departamentos. La evidencia indica que el personal técnico participa mucho menos que el resto de la empresa, y no cabe duda de que este desequilibrio afecta la percepción general del programa.

Sin embargo, hasta cierto punto, todavía no sabemos con certeza si el modelo híbrido propuesto resolverá el problema por completo. Es probable que la participación técnica aumente con una opción remota, pero es posible que otros factores —como la carga de trabajo o la falta de tiempo— sigan limitando la asistencia incluso con más flexibilidad. Dudo que un solo cambio de horario sea suficiente para transformar la cultura de participación de la empresa.

En cierta medida, el éxito del modelo híbrido dependerá de cómo se implemente, no solo de que exista. Aunque los datos preliminares sean alentadores, es una lástima que todavía no contemos con una prueba piloto formal. Por lo tanto, antes de adoptar el modelo de manera permanente, es evidente que la empresa debería medir los resultados durante al menos un semestre.

This passage is an adapted textbook composition written for this chapter, not an authentic published article.

  1. Identify one claim the writer presents as certain, and explain which expression signals that certainty.
  2. Identify one claim the writer presents as only probable or possible, and explain which expression signals that uncertainty.
  3. Find the sentence where the writer expresses personal doubt, and explain what the writer doubts.
  4. Explain why es una lástima que takes the subjunctive even though the writer is confident that no pilot test yet exists.
  5. In two or three sentences, state which of the writer's claims you find most convincing and why, using at least one hedging expression from this chapter.

Guided Practice

Complete the following controlled practice before moving to the listening and speaking tasks.

  1. Choose the correct verb form (indicative or subjunctive) for each expression of certainty or evaluation.
    • Es evidente que el equipo (necesitar / necesite) más recursos.
    • Es posible que la propuesta (cambiar / cambie) antes de la votación final.
    • No cabe duda de que los empleados (valorar / valoren) la flexibilidad de horario.
    • Es una lástima que el informe no (incluir / incluya) datos del último trimestre.
  2. Rewrite each certain statement as an uncertain one by adding a hedging expression and adjusting the mood, for example: El plan funciona. → Es posible que el plan funcione.

Listening Task

Ask your instructor, a partner, or a text-to-speech tool to read the following short exchange aloud. As you listen, identify which statements are certain and which are hedged.

Two committee members discuss how confident they are in the hybrid-model proposal.

Rocío: Es evidente que la propuesta ha mejorado desde la primera versión. No cabe duda de que el equipo trabajó con cuidado.
Tomás: Estoy de acuerdo hasta cierto punto, pero dudo que tengamos suficientes datos todavía. Es posible que necesitemos una prueba piloto antes de decidir.
Rocío: Entiendo tu punto. Es probable que una prueba piloto nos dé más seguridad, aunque eso signifique retrasar la decisión unos meses.

  2. Identify one statement Tomás presents as uncertain, and explain which expression signals that.
  3. Explain why aunque eso signifique retrasar la decisión uses the subjunctive.

Speaking Task

Práctica controlada

With a partner, take turns stating a claim about the hybrid-model proposal, first as a certainty using es evidente que or no cabe duda de que, and then as a hedged possibility using es posible que or es probable que.

Tarea comunicativa: Recomendación matizada

In pairs or small groups, prepare a short recommendation about the hybrid-model proposal. State clearly which points you are confident about, which points remain uncertain, and which points you personally doubt, using the vocabulary and grammar from this chapter.

Alternativa individual

If you are studying independently: record yourself giving the same recommendation twice — once treating every point as certain, and once appropriately graded by certainty — and compare how each version sounds.

Writing Practice

Write a short paragraph (100 to 130 words) that presents a recommendation on a workplace, school, or public-life issue of your choice. Your paragraph must:

  1. State at least one point with confidence, using es evidente que or no cabe duda de que with the indicative.
  2. State at least one point as uncertain, using es posible que or es probable que with the subjunctive.
  3. Concede a counterpoint using a structure from Chapter 8, choosing indicative or subjunctive correctly.
  4. Close with a recommendation that reflects the graded certainty of the evidence rather than treating all points as equally certain.

  • I used at least one hedging or evaluative expression with the subjunctive.
  • I included a concession from Chapter 8 with the correct mood.
  • My recommendation reflects the actual strength of the evidence.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context

In professional and academic Spanish, distinguishing what is certain from what is merely probable or possible is often treated as a mark of careful, credible communication, particularly in reports, recommendations, and academic writing. A recommendation that grades its own certainty can read as more trustworthy than one that presents every point with equal confidence.

Regional Note (Recognition Only)

How directly a speaker voices personal doubt in a meeting or public discussion can vary by institution, workplace culture, and country. In some settings, a direct dudo que is common and unremarkable; in others, speakers favor softer alternatives such as no estoy del todo seguro/a de que. This chapter presents one workable formal pattern rather than a single universal norm.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake

Error: Using the indicative after expressions of possibility or doubt such as es posible que or dudo que, by analogy with expressions of certainty.

Correction: Use the subjunctive after expressions that present a claim as uncertain, doubtful, or merely evaluative, since the speaker is not asserting the clause as an established fact.

Common Mistake

Error: Keeping the indicative after a certainty expression once it is negated, as in no es evidente que el plan funciona.

Correction: Shift to the subjunctive once a certainty expression is negated, since denying certainty removes the basis for treating the clause as fact: no es evidente que el plan funcione.

Common Mistake

Error: Presenting every claim in a recommendation with the same level of certainty, regardless of how strong the underlying evidence actually is.

Correction: Match the certainty expression to the actual strength of the evidence; overstating certainty can undermine credibility as much as excessive hedging.

Answer Key

Reading Task — Model Analysis

1. Certain claim: Es evidente que el programa... no ha logrado una participación equitativa, signaled by es evidente que with the indicative.   2. Uncertain claim: Es probable que la participación técnica aumente..., signaled by es probable que with the subjunctive.   3. Personal doubt: Dudo que un solo cambio de horario sea suficiente...; the writer doubts that a single scheduling change will be enough to transform the company's participation culture.   4. Es una lástima que takes the subjunctive because it is an evaluative expression, not a certainty expression: even though the writer is confident about the underlying fact, the expression itself frames the situation as regrettable rather than asserting it as neutral fact, which is why evaluative expressions consistently take the subjunctive regardless of the speaker's confidence in the fact itself.   5. Model answer: learner responses will vary; a strong answer states a clear opinion and uses a hedging expression such as hasta cierto punto.
