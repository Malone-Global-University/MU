Capítulo 14: Voz pasiva, se e impersonalidad

Chapter function: ser + participle, passive/result contrast, and impersonal and passive se in professional and public-facing discourse.

1. Chapter Overview

In Chapter 13, you learned to hedge recommendations and propose conditions using the imperfect subjunctive. This chapter shifts attention from personal stance to a different kind of professional voice: the voice used when the agent of an action is unknown, unimportant, or deliberately left out. Formal reports, policy summaries, and public announcements rarely say who did something; they describe what was done. You will learn the true passive with ser + past participle, how to distinguish a passive event from a resulting state described with estar + past participle, and how the impersonal and passive uses of se let Spanish speakers describe general practices, procedures, and public information without naming an agent at all.

2. Learning Objectives

  • Form the passive voice with ser + past participle, including correct participle agreement, and identify when an agent phrase with por is included or omitted.
  • Distinguish a passive event (ser + participle) from a resulting state (estar + participle), building on the ser/estar system active since earlier volumes.
  • Form and interpret the impersonal se construction for general statements without a specific subject.
  • Form and interpret the passive se construction, including subject-verb agreement with the grammatical subject.
  • Distinguish impersonal se from passive se using clear diagnostic tests.
  • Select the most natural option — true passive, passive se, impersonal se, or an active sentence — for a given professional or public-information context.
  • Edit a short public-information text for correct passive and impersonal structures.

3. Opening Communication Scenario

Mariana's client has approved a phased version of her recommendations (Chapter 13). Now the company must communicate the decision internally and, in a shortened form, to the public through a press note. Internal minutes and public announcements in Spanish rarely name who exactly approved or is doing each action — they describe what was decided and what will be done.

Acta de la reunión (fragmento): La propuesta fue aprobada por la junta directiva el 12 de julio. El plan fue dividido en tres fases. Actualmente, la primera fase ya está aprobada y el presupuesto correspondiente está asignado.

Nota de prensa (fragmento): Se implementará un nuevo modelo de trabajo a partir de septiembre. Se espera que el cambio mejore la eficiencia del área. Se informará a todo el personal por correo electrónico.

5. Pronunciation and Orthography Focus

Past participles ending in -ído require a written accent because the vowel combination would otherwise form a diphthong: leído, oído, traído. This is a consolidation of the accent-mark system you have used since earlier volumes, now applied specifically to participles used in the passive voice. Practice saying these participles aloud, separating the two vowels clearly, before you write them.

Language NoteIn rapid formal speech, se constructions are often produced with very light stress on se itself and heavier stress on the verb — se implementará is heard almost as one unit. Listen for this pattern in the listening passage below.

6. Grammar and Sentence Patterns

6.1 The true passive: ser + past participle

Spanish forms a true passive the way English does: ser conjugated in the appropriate tense, followed by a past participle that agrees in gender and number with the grammatical subject. An agent may optionally be added with por.

La propuesta fue aprobada por la junta directiva.
The proposal was approved by the board of directors.

Tense of serExample
PreteriteEl plan fue dividido en tres fases.
PresentEl informe es revisado cada trimestre.
FutureLos resultados serán publicados en agosto.
Register NoteThe true passive with ser is common in written, formal, journalistic, and legal Spanish. In everyday spoken Spanish, passive se or an active sentence is usually more natural. Reserve the true passive for the professional and public-information contexts this chapter targets.

6.2 Passive event versus resulting state: ser vs. estar + participle

Building on the ser/estar contrast active since Chapter 1, compare the passive event with the state that results from it.

ser + participle (event)estar + participle (resulting state)
El presupuesto fue asignado ayer.
The budget was assigned (the action happened) yesterday.		El presupuesto ya está asignado.
The budget is already assigned (the current state).
La propuesta fue aprobada el 12 de julio.La propuesta ya está aprobada.

Ser + participle describes the action itself, often with a time reference. Estar + participle describes the condition that exists afterward, regardless of when the action occurred.

6.3 Impersonal se

Impersonal se describes what people in general do, without identifying who. The verb is always third-person singular, and the construction commonly appears with intransitive verbs, verbs already followed by a preposition, or verbs referring to people as the object.

Se trabaja mucho en esa oficina.
People work a lot in that office. / One works a lot in that office.

Se necesita personal calificado para este puesto.
Qualified staff are needed for this position.

6.4 Passive se

Passive se is used with a transitive verb whose logical object becomes the grammatical subject. The verb agrees in number with that subject, just as in a true passive, but no agent is normally expressed.

Passive se (singular subject)Passive se (plural subject)
Se implementará un nuevo modelo de trabajo.Se implementarán nuevos procedimientos.
Se informará a todo el personal. (indirect object, not subject — see note)Se publicaron los resultados en agosto.
Common MistakeWhen the noun following the verb refers to a person and is marked with personal a — Se informará a todo el personal — that noun functions as an object, not the grammatical subject, so the verb stays singular. This is impersonal se with a personal direct object, not passive se.

6.5 Diagnostic: impersonal se or passive se?

TestImpersonal sePassive se
Does a noun follow that could be the subject of an active sentence?No, or the following noun is a personal direct object marked with a.Yes — that noun controls verb agreement.
Can the verb be plural?No — always third-person singular.Yes, if the subject is plural.
Typical verb typeIntransitive, or transitive with a personal object.Transitive, with a non-personal logical object.

Se venden casas. (passive se — "casas" is plural subject, verb agrees)
Se vende bien en esta región. (impersonal se — no subject noun, verb stays singular)

7. Model Dialogue

Directora de comunicaciones: ¿Ya fue redactado el comunicado de prensa?

Mariana: Sí, ya está redactado. Se explican las tres fases y se aclara que el cambio será evaluado a los seis meses.

Directora de comunicaciones: ¿Y se menciona quién aprobó la propuesta?

Mariana: No directamente. Se prefiere decir que la propuesta fue aprobada por la junta, sin dar más detalles internos. Así se evita que el comunicado parezca una decisión de una sola persona.

Directora de comunicaciones: Perfecto. ¿Cuándo se enviará a todo el personal?

Mariana: Se enviará esta tarde, en cuanto esté aprobada la versión final.

11. Reading Practice

En muchas organizaciones, los cambios importantes no se anuncian de manera improvisada. Primero se analiza la situación, luego se redacta una propuesta y, finalmente, se comunica la decisión a todo el personal. En general, se evita mencionar nombres individuales en los comunicados públicos; en su lugar, se prefiere hablar de la organización como conjunto. Por ejemplo, en lugar de decir que una persona aprobó un cambio, suele decirse que la propuesta fue aprobada por la junta directiva. De esta manera, se protege tanto la privacidad de los individuos como la percepción de que la decisión fue colectiva.

Task: Write a short public announcement (120–160 words) about a change in your own workplace, school, or a fictional organization. Include at least one true passive sentence, one passive se sentence, one impersonal se sentence, and one contrast between ser + participle and estar + participle.

Editing checklist

  • Participle agreement in every true passive sentence
  • Correct choice between ser + participle (event) and estar + participle (resulting state)
  • Correct verb agreement in passive se (singular verb with singular subject, plural verb with plural subject)
  • Impersonal se kept in the third-person singular
  • Accent marks on -ído participles where required
  • Consistent formal register throughout

13. Culture and Communication

In many Spanish-language institutional and journalistic contexts, avoiding a named agent is not simply a grammatical preference — it reflects a broader convention of presenting organizational decisions as collective rather than individual. This pattern appears often in official minutes, press releases, and public notices across many Spanish-speaking workplaces, though the degree of formality and the preference for passive versus impersonal se constructions can vary by country, institution, and individual writing style, and should not be treated as a fixed rule for all contexts.

14. Common Mistakes

  • Using estar instead of ser to describe the action itself: La propuesta estuvo aprobada por la junta incorrectly frames the approval as a state rather than an event; the professional standard is La propuesta fue aprobada por la junta.
  • Forgetting participle agreement in the true passive: Los resultados fue publicado is incorrect; the correct form is Los resultados fueron publicados.
  • Making the verb plural in impersonal se when a personal direct object follows: Se informan a los empleados is incorrect; the correct form is Se informa a los empleados.
  • Adding an agent phrase with por to a passive se sentence, which is not standard: avoid Se implementará el modelo por la empresa; prefer La empresa implementará el modelo or simply Se implementará el modelo.

15. Chapter Summary

This chapter introduced the true passive with ser + participle, contrasted it with the resulting state expressed by estar + participle, and distinguished impersonal se from passive se. You practiced choosing among these structures — along with the active voice — for the impersonal, agent-free style typical of formal reports, meeting minutes, and public announcements.
