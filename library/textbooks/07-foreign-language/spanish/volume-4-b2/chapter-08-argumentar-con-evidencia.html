Argumentar con evidencia

Construir una postura con datos, contraargumentos y concesión razonada · Speak and Write Spanish: Advanced Public, Professional, and Academic Spanish

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify and use vocabulary for stating a claim, presenting evidence, and introducing a counterargument.
  2. Use aunque and a pesar de que to concede a point while maintaining your position.
  3. Distinguish when a concessive clause with aunque takes the indicative and when it takes the subjunctive, based on whether the conceded information is known or hypothetical.
  4. Build a short paragraph that states a claim, supports it with paraphrased evidence, and concedes a counterpoint without abandoning the argument.
  5. Discuss a debatable topic aloud, presenting a position and responding to an opposing view with concession and rebuttal.
  6. Write a structured paragraph of argumentation that integrates attribution language from Chapter 7 with the concessive structures introduced in this chapter.

This chapter continues Unit II. It introduces claim, evidence, counterargument, and concession vocabulary and the aunque / a pesar de que system as new productive content, while developing the paraphrase and attribution language from Chapter 7 and the academic connectors and formal subordination from Chapter 6.

Communication Scenario

Daniela's team is debating whether the wellness program should be expanded to include a fully remote option for the technical department, whose participation has lagged. Some colleagues argue that a remote option would resolve the scheduling problem; others worry it would reduce the sense of shared participation across the company. Daniela needs to state her own position, support it with evidence from the earlier report, and acknowledge the strongest point on the other side without losing her argument.

  1. Think of a debate you have had at work, school, or with family where you had to admit the other side had a fair point, but still defend your own position. How did you signal "yes, but..."?
  2. As you work through this chapter, notice how aunque and a pesar de que let a speaker concede a fact or possibility while keeping the main claim intact.

Grammar Focus: Concesión con aunque y a pesar de que

Grammar Note

Purpose: to concede a fact, detail, or possibility that could weaken your argument, while signaling that your main claim still stands.

Form: aunque or a pesar de que + clause, followed or preceded by the main claim. The conceded clause can appear before or after the main clause.

Meaning and mood selection: When the conceded information is a known fact — something the speaker accepts as true — the clause after aunque or a pesar de que takes the indicative. When the conceded information is hypothetical, uncertain, or presented as a possibility the speaker does not confirm, the clause takes the subjunctive.

  • Aunque la participación del personal técnico es baja, el programa ha reducido el ausentismo general. Known fact (indicative): Although technical-department participation is low, the program has reduced overall absenteeism.
  • Aunque la participación sea baja en algunos departamentos, la empresa considera que el programa vale la pena. Uncertain or unconfirmed (subjunctive): Even if participation is/turns out to be low in some departments, the company considers the program worthwhile.
  • A pesar de que el costo aumentó, los resultados justifican la inversión. Known fact (indicative): Even though the cost increased, the results justify the investment.

Use: Concession is not surrender. A strong argumentative paragraph often concedes a real point with aunque or si bien es cierto que, and then returns to the main claim with a connector such as de todos modos, sin embargo, or por lo tanto. Do not concede a point without returning to your position; an unanswered concession weakens the argument instead of strengthening it.

  1. Read each pair of ideas and decide whether the concession is a known fact or an uncertain possibility.
  2. Combine each pair using aunque, choosing the indicative or the subjunctive as appropriate.
    • Known fact: El nuevo horario flexible cuesta más a la empresa. / Reduce el estrés de los empleados.
    • Uncertain: Es posible que algunos empleados no aprovechen el nuevo horario. / La empresa debería ofrecerlo de todos modos.
    • Known fact: El informe tiene solo seis meses de datos. / Sus conclusiones son razonables.

Reading: Un debate sobre el trabajo remoto

Read the following adapted opinion piece about extending the wellness program's flexible scheduling into a remote-work option. As you read, identify the writer's main claim, the evidence offered, and the point the writer concedes to the opposing view.

¿Debería la empresa ofrecer un horario totalmente remoto?

La empresa sostiene que el programa de bienestar ha tenido éxito, pero la baja participación del personal técnico sigue siendo un problema. Un contraargumento común es que un horario totalmente remoto resolvería la falta de tiempo que mencionan estos empleados. La evidencia indica que, en departamentos similares de otras empresas, la participación aumentó considerablemente cuando se ofreció esta opción.

Sin embargo, aunque un horario remoto podría aumentar la participación individual, también podría debilitar el sentido de comunidad que el programa busca fortalecer. Si bien es cierto que algunos empleados prefieren la flexibilidad total, de todos modos muchos valoran las sesiones presenciales como una oportunidad para socializar con sus compañeros. Esto se debe a que el bienestar laboral no depende únicamente de la conveniencia individual, sino también de las relaciones dentro del equipo.

Por lo tanto, en lugar de eliminar las sesiones presenciales, la empresa podría ofrecer una versión híbrida: sesiones presenciales opcionales combinadas con contenido en línea para quienes no puedan asistir. Aunque esta solución exija más planificación por parte del equipo de recursos humanos, a pesar de que implique un costo adicional al principio, es probable que logre un equilibrio más sostenible entre flexibilidad y comunidad.

This passage is an adapted textbook composition written for this chapter, not an authentic published article.

  1. Identify the writer's main claim in one sentence.
  2. Find the sentence that introduces the opposing view, and explain what that view proposes.
  3. Find one sentence where the writer concedes a point to the opposing view, and one sentence where the writer returns to the main claim afterward.
  4. Identify one aunque clause in the indicative and one in the subjunctive, and explain why each mood was chosen.
  5. In two or three sentences, state whether you agree with the writer's proposed hybrid solution and why, using at least one attribution phrase from Chapter 7.

Guided Practice

Complete the following controlled practice before moving to the listening and speaking tasks.

  1. Choose the correct verb form (indicative or subjunctive) for each aunque clause.
    • Aunque el programa (costar / cueste) más este año, los resultados lo justifican.
    • Aunque algunos departamentos no (participar / participen) tanto como esperamos, seguiremos ofreciendo el programa.
    • A pesar de que la encuesta (tener / tenga) solo cien respuestas, las tendencias son claras.
  2. Complete each argument with a claim, a piece of evidence introduced by la evidencia indica que, and a concession introduced by si bien es cierto que.

Listening Task

Ask your instructor, a partner, or a text-to-speech tool to read the following short exchange aloud. As you listen, identify the claim, the counterargument, and the concession.

Two colleagues discuss whether to offer a fully remote wellness option.

Marta: Yo sostengo que deberíamos ofrecer un horario totalmente remoto. La evidencia indica que la participación técnica aumentaría de inmediato.
Iván: Si bien es cierto que resolvería el problema de horarios, de todos modos me preocupa que perdamos el contacto entre equipos. Aunque el trabajo remoto sea más cómodo, no siempre fortalece la comunidad.
Marta: Entiendo tu punto. Un contraargumento común es que lo presencial es insustituible, pero a pesar de que eso sea cierto para algunos, otros simplemente no participan si no hay flexibilidad.
  1. What is Marta's main claim?
  2. What point does Iván concede, and what concern does he raise afterward?
  3. Identify one aunque clause in the exchange and explain whether it uses the indicative or the subjunctive.

Speaking Task

Práctica controlada

With a partner, take turns stating a claim about the wellness program using sostener que, then respond to your partner's claim with a concession using si bien es cierto que or aunque.

Tarea comunicativa: Pequeño debate

In pairs or small groups, choose one side of the remote-work debate from the reading passage. Present your position, support it with paraphrased evidence, and respond to the other side's strongest point with a concession followed by a return to your claim.

Alternativa individual

If you are studying independently: record yourself arguing both sides of the debate, one after the other, using at least two concessive structures and one attribution phrase from Chapter 7.

Writing Practice

Write a short argumentative paragraph (100 to 130 words) about a workplace, school, or public-life issue of your choice. Your paragraph must:

  1. Open with a clear claim, using sostener que or a similar structure.
  2. Support the claim with at least one piece of paraphrased evidence, using language from Chapter 7 where useful.
  3. Concede one real counterpoint using aunque, a pesar de que, or si bien es cierto que, choosing indicative or subjunctive correctly.
  4. Return to your claim after the concession, using a connector such as de todos modos or por lo tanto.

Lista de revisión:

  • My paragraph states a clear claim at the beginning.
  • I supported the claim with evidence rather than opinion alone.
  • I conceded a real counterpoint instead of a weak one.
  • I chose indicative or subjunctive correctly after aunque or a pesar de que.
  • I returned to my claim after the concession.
  • I checked accents, punctuation, and capitalization.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context

In professional and academic Spanish, acknowledging an opposing view is generally seen as a sign of a stronger argument, not a weaker one. Conceding a fair point with si bien es cierto que or aunque before returning to your position is a common feature of careful, credible argumentation in reports, editorials, and formal discussion, rather than a sign of uncertainty.

Regional Note (Recognition Only)

Expectations for how directly a speaker challenges an opposing view in a meeting or public discussion can vary by institution, workplace culture, and country. Some settings favor a very direct rebuttal after a concession; others favor a softer, more indirect return to the original claim. This chapter presents one workable formal pattern rather than a single universal norm.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake

Error: Using the subjunctive automatically after aunque regardless of whether the conceded information is a known fact.

Correction: Use the indicative when the conceded fact is known or accepted as true; reserve the subjunctive for uncertain, hypothetical, or unconfirmed information.

Common Mistake

Error: Conceding a counterpoint and never returning to the original claim, so the paragraph appears to abandon its own argument.

Correction: Follow every concession with a connector such as de todos modos, sin embargo, or por lo tanto that restates or reinforces the main claim.

Common Mistake

Error: Conceding a weak or irrelevant point instead of the strongest real objection to the argument.

Correction: Choose the counterpoint a fair-minded reader would actually raise; conceding a trivial point does not make an argument more credible.

Reading Task — Model Analysis

The opposing view, introduced with un contraargumento común es que, proposes that a fully remote schedule would solve the technical department's time problem.   3. Concession: Si bien es cierto que algunos empleados prefieren la flexibilidad total...; return to claim: de todos modos muchos valoran las sesiones presenciales....   4.
