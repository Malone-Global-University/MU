Leer textos complejos

Idea general, detalle e inferencia en textos académicos · Speak and Write Spanish: Advanced Public, Professional, and Academic Spanish

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify the main idea, supporting details, and inferred meaning in a multi-paragraph academic-style text.
  2. Recognize and interpret academic connectors such as sin embargo, por lo tanto, además, no obstante, and en cuanto a.
  3. Identify a topic sentence and explain how the sentences that follow it develop, support, or qualify it.
  4. Use formal subordinating structures to connect ideas in your own analysis of a text.
  5. Discuss a complex passage aloud, distinguishing what the text states directly from what it implies.
  6. Write a short paragraph that opens with a clear topic sentence and uses at least two academic connectors.

This chapter opens Unit II. It introduces formal subordination, academic connectors, and topic-sentence analysis as new productive systems, while continuing to develop the formal register, passive/impersonal se, and other systems active since Unit I.

Communication Scenario

Daniela has been asked to prepare a short internal briefing based on an article about changes to remote-work policy at her company's regional office. Before she can summarize it for her team, she needs to read the article carefully: to separate its main argument from its supporting details, and to notice where the writer is stating a fact directly and where the writer is only suggesting a conclusion.

  1. Think about a time you had to read something dense — a policy document, a news article, an academic text — and pull out only what mattered for a specific purpose. What reading strategies helped you most?
  2. As you work through this chapter, pay attention to how connecting words like sin embargo or por lo tanto signal a shift, a consequence, or an added point, even before you reach the words that follow them.

Grammar Focus: Subordinación formal y la oración temática

Grammar Note

Purpose: to recognize how a topic sentence controls the paragraph that follows it, and to use formal subordinating connectors to link a claim to its support.

Form: A topic sentence typically appears first in an academic paragraph and states the paragraph's main claim in general terms. The sentences that follow narrow, support, qualify, or illustrate that claim, often using a connector such as por ejemplo, además, or sin embargo to signal the relationship.

Meaning: A connector does not simply join two sentences; it tells the reader what kind of relationship to expect — addition, contrast, consequence, or specification.

Use: In formal writing and reading, connectors are one of the clearest signals of a text's structure. Learning to recognize them lets you predict what kind of information is coming next, even before you read the full sentence.

  • La política de trabajo remoto ha beneficiado a muchos empleados. Sin embargo, algunos equipos han encontrado dificultades para coordinar horarios. Remote-work policy has benefited many employees. However, some teams have had trouble coordinating schedules.
  • La empresa amplió el horario flexible el año pasado. Por lo tanto, varios departamentos han reportado mayor satisfacción laboral. The company expanded flexible hours last year. As a result, several departments have reported greater job satisfaction.
  • En cuanto a la comunicación interna, la empresa introdujo una reunión semanal obligatoria. As for internal communication, the company introduced a mandatory weekly meeting.

Do not treat every connector as interchangeable. Sin embargo and no obstante both signal contrast, but no obstante belongs more to formal, written, or institutional registers; sin embargo is the safer default for most tasks in this chapter.

  1. Underline the topic sentence in each short paragraph your instructor provides, then identify one connector that signals how the following sentence relates to it.
  2. Combine each pair of simple sentences below using the connector indicated.
    • El informe llegó a tiempo. El equipo no tuvo tiempo de revisarlo. (sin embargo)
    • Se actualizó el sistema. Los empleados recibieron una capacitación breve. (por lo tanto)
    • El artículo describe el nuevo horario. El artículo menciona los criterios de elegibilidad. (además)

Reading: Identificando la idea principal

Read the following adapted article about a company's remote-work policy. As you read, look for the topic sentence in each paragraph and notice how the connectors organize the writer's argument.

La nueva política de trabajo remoto

La empresa introdujo el año pasado una política de trabajo remoto más flexible para varios departamentos. Según los datos internos, la mayoría de los empleados reporta mayor satisfacción con su equilibrio entre el trabajo y la vida personal. Sin embargo, algunos gerentes han señalado que la coordinación entre equipos se ha vuelto más difícil, especialmente cuando los miembros de un mismo proyecto trabajan en husos horarios distintos.

En cuanto a la comunicación interna, la empresa introdujo una reunión semanal obligatoria para mantener informados a todos los equipos. De hecho, varios departamentos han comenzado a usar esa reunión también para resolver problemas antes de que se conviertan en retrasos mayores. Por lo tanto, aunque la política todavía presenta desafíos de coordinación, parece haber contribuido a una comunicación más constante entre los equipos.

En resumen, la política de trabajo remoto ha traído beneficios claros para el bienestar de los empleados, pero su éxito a largo plazo probablemente dependerá de cómo la empresa continúe ajustando sus prácticas de coordinación y comunicación.

This passage is an adapted textbook composition written for this chapter, not an authentic published article.

  1. Identify the topic sentence of each paragraph.
  2. Find each of the four connectors from this chapter's vocabulary list in the passage, and explain the relationship each one signals.
  3. The text does not directly state whether the company considers the policy a success. Based on the final paragraph, what can you infer about the writer's overall view? Support your answer with one phrase from the text.
  4. Identify one detail in the second paragraph that supports the main idea of the first paragraph.
  5. In your own words, summarize the passage in two or three sentences using at least one academic connector.

Guided Practice

Complete the following controlled practice before moving to the listening and speaking tasks.

  1. Choose the connector that best completes each sentence: sin embargo, además, por lo tanto, or en cuanto a.
    • El nuevo horario flexible comenzó en enero. ______, la política de reuniones no ha cambiado.
    • ______ la capacitación técnica, la empresa ofrecerá un curso adicional en marzo.
    • Los empleados solicitaron más información sobre el proceso. ______, el departamento de recursos humanos publicó una guía detallada.
  2. Rewrite each simple two-sentence pair as one sentence joined by an appropriate connector, keeping the formal register.

Listening Task

Ask your instructor, a partner, or a text-to-speech tool to read the following short explanation aloud. As you listen, identify the main idea and one supporting detail.

A workplace trainer explains a scheduling change to new employees.

Capacitadora: La empresa cambió el horario de las reuniones de equipo el mes pasado. Antes, los equipos se reunían todos los días; ahora, se reúnen solamente tres veces por semana. Además, cada reunión ahora tiene una agenda escrita, para que las discusiones sean más eficientes. Sin embargo, esto significa que los empleados deben comunicar los problemas urgentes por otros medios, ya que no siempre habrá una reunión el mismo día.
  1. What is the main idea of the explanation?
  2. Identify one supporting detail and one contrast introduced by sin embargo.
  3. Based on the last sentence, what should a new employee do if a problem is urgent?

Speaking Task

Práctica controlada

With a partner, take turns summarizing one paragraph of the reading passage aloud, using at least one academic connector in your summary.

Tarea comunicativa: Discusión guiada

In pairs or small groups, discuss the remote-work policy from the reading passage. One person should state what the text says directly; the other should suggest what the text implies but does not state outright. Use sin embargo, por lo tanto, or de hecho at least once each in the discussion.

Alternativa individual

If you are studying independently: record yourself explaining the main idea and one inference from the reading passage, using at least two academic connectors.

Writing Practice

Write a short paragraph (80 to 100 words) analyzing a short article, policy, or announcement of your choice (real or invented). Your paragraph must:

  1. Open with a clear topic sentence that states your main point.
  2. Include at least two academic connectors from this chapter's vocabulary.
  3. Support your topic sentence with at least one specific detail.

Lista de revisión:

  • My paragraph opens with a clear topic sentence.
  • I used at least two academic connectors correctly.
  • My supporting sentence connects clearly to my topic sentence.
  • I maintained a formal, neutral register throughout.
  • I checked accents, punctuation, and capitalization.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context

Academic and workplace writing across Spanish-speaking institutions commonly favors clearly signaled logical structure — a stated topic sentence followed by connectors that guide the reader through supporting points, contrasts, and conclusions. Recognizing these signals helps a reader move efficiently through long reports, memos, and articles without needing to reread each paragraph multiple times.

Regional Note (Recognition Only)

Academic and journalistic writing conventions vary somewhat by country and institution, particularly in how formal or dense the connectors tend to be. A more conversational connector such as pero may appear even in relatively formal spoken commentary in some contexts, while written academic prose tends to favor sin embargo or no obstante.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake

Error: Using pero in every context where a contrast connector is needed, even in formal academic analysis.

Correction: Reserve sin embargo or no obstante for formal written analysis; pero remains appropriate mainly in more conversational registers.

Common Mistake

Error: Treating every stated detail as equally important, rather than identifying which sentence functions as the topic sentence.

Correction: Identify the topic sentence first, then treat following sentences as support, contrast, or specification of that main idea.

Common Mistake

Error: Confusing what a text states directly with what a reader can reasonably infer, and presenting an inference as if the text said it outright.

Correction: Support any inference with a specific phrase from the text, and distinguish clearly between direct statement and inference in your own analysis.
