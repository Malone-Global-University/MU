Tiempos compuestos y anterioridad

El presente perfecto, el pluscuamperfecto, y una primera mirada al futuro y condicional perfecto · Speak and Write Spanish: Advanced Public, Professional, and Academic Spanish

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Form and use the present perfect (he hablado, ha escrito) to describe actions completed before now, with correct irregular past participles.
  2. Form and use the pluscuamperfecto (había hablado) to describe an action that occurred before another past action, correctly sequencing two past events.
  3. Recognize the future perfect (habrá terminado) and the conditional perfect (habría terminado) in professional and academic contexts, and begin to produce them in controlled sentences.
  4. Distinguish the present perfect from the simple preterite according to the speaker's sense of relevance to the present moment.
  5. Sequence two past events correctly using the preterite/imperfect and the pluscuamperfecto together.
  6. Edit written work for correct auxiliary verb, participle form, and word order in compound tenses.

This chapter continues Unit III. It builds on the complex relative clauses introduced in Chapter 11 and on the formal register, professional vocabulary, and clause-combining skills consolidated across Chapters 1 through 10. It introduces the perfect and pluperfect systems as controlled productive systems for the first time in this volume, and introduces the future and conditional perfect for recognition moving toward controlled production.

Communication Scenario

Andrés, back at the public-information office from Chapter 11, is now drafting a year-end summary of the digital procedures system for his supervisor. He needs to describe what has already been accomplished this year, what had already happened before the system launched, and what will likely have been completed by the end of the following quarter — three different relationships to time that Spanish marks through compound tenses.

  1. Think of a project or process you know well. What has already happened up to now? What had already happened before some earlier point? What will probably have happened by some future point?
  2. As you work through this chapter, notice how Spanish uses a form of haber plus a past participle to mark all three of these time relationships, changing only the tense of haber itself.

Grammar: Presente perfecto, pluscuamperfecto, futuro y condicional perfecto

Language Note

Purpose: to introduce, for the first time in this volume, the family of compound tenses built from haber plus a past participle, and to show how changing only the tense of haber shifts the entire structure's relationship to time.

1. Formation: haber + past participle. All compound tenses share the same pattern: a conjugated form of haber followed by an invariable past participle (-ado for -ar verbs, -ido for -er/-ir verbs). Nothing may separate haber from the participle, and the participle does not agree in gender or number.

  • He hablado con la directora. / *He la hablado. I have spoken with the director. (No word may come between he and hablado.)

2. Presente perfecto — he/has/ha/hemos/habéis/han + participio. Describes a past action the speaker treats as relevant to the present moment, or as part of a period that includes now (hoy, este año, todavía).

  • Este año hemos resuelto el ochenta por ciento de las solicitudes pendientes. This year we have resolved eighty percent of the pending applications.
  • El organismo todavía no ha respondido a la solicitud. The agency still hasn't responded to the application.

Contrast with the simple preterite, which presents a past action as a completed, closed event without claiming present relevance: El año pasado resolvimos el setenta por ciento (last year we resolved seventy percent — a closed period) versus este año hemos resuelto... (an ongoing period that still includes now).

3. Pluscuamperfecto — había/habías/había/habíamos/habíais/habían + participio. Describes an action that had already happened before another past action or point in time. It sequences two past events relative to each other.

  • Cuando el nuevo sistema entró en vigor, la oficina ya había recibido miles de quejas. When the new system took effect, the office had already received thousands of complaints.
  • La vecina había presentado su solicitud dos veces antes de que se resolviera. The resident had submitted her application twice before it was resolved.

4. Futuro perfecto and condicional perfecto (recognition toward controlled production). The futuro perfecto (habrá + participio) expresses what will likely have happened by a future point, or a probable explanation for something already completed. The condicional perfecto (habría + participio) expresses what would have happened under different conditions, or a probable explanation for a past event viewed from an earlier vantage point.

  • Para fin de año, el organismo habrá resuelto todos los trámites atrasados. By the end of the year, the agency will have resolved all the delayed procedures.
  • Sin el nuevo sistema, el proceso habría tardado mucho más. Without the new system, the process would have taken much longer.
Regional Note (Recognition Only)

In much of Latin America, the simple preterite is strongly preferred over the present perfect even for very recent actions, so hoy comí temprano is far more common in everyday speech than hoy he comido temprano. Spain and some other regions use the present perfect more broadly in speech. This chapter's productive standard favors the present perfect mainly in formal, written, and professional contexts, where its use is broadly stable across regions.

Model Dialogue

Andrés and Marisol review the year-end summary before it goes to the director.

Marisol: ¿Cómo va el resumen anual? ¿Qué hemos logrado hasta ahora? How's the year-end summary going? What have we achieved so far?
Andrés: Hemos resuelto casi todos los trámites atrasados. Y para cuando lanzamos el sistema en marzo, ya habíamos recibido más de dos mil quejas. We've resolved almost all the delayed procedures. And by the time we launched the system in March, we had already received more than two thousand complaints.
Marisol: ¿Y el plazo de diciembre? ¿Crees que lo cumpliremos? And the December deadline? Do you think we'll meet it?
Andrés: Para entonces, habremos cerrado el noventa y cinco por ciento de los casos. Sin el nuevo sistema, habríamos tardado el doble de tiempo. By then, we will have closed ninety-five percent of the cases. Without the new system, it would have taken us twice as long.
Marisol: Excelente. Escribe eso exactamente así en el resumen. Excellent. Write that exactly like that in the summary.

Guided Practice

  1. Complete with the correct present perfect form: Este mes, la oficina _____ (recibir) trescientas solicitudes nuevas.
  2. Complete with the correct pluscuamperfecto form: Cuando llegó la nueva directora, el equipo ya _____ (resolver) el problema principal.
  3. Choose the correct irregular participle: La carta ya ha sido _____ (escribir) y _____ (poner) en el correo.
  4. Decide whether the present perfect or the simple preterite is more natural: El año pasado (resolvimos / hemos resuelto) el ochenta por ciento de los casos.
  5. Complete with the correct futuro perfecto or condicional perfecto, as the context requires: Sin la ayuda de la vecina, el trámite _____ (tardar) mucho más en resolverse.

Listening Practice

Listening Script for Instructor, Partner, or Text-to-Speech Use.

Mensaje de voz "Hola, Andrés, te llamo porque ya he revisado el resumen que me enviaste. Para cuando terminamos la reunión con el comité, ya habían aprobado el presupuesto para el próximo año, así que puedes incluir esa información. Para diciembre, habremos cerrado casi todos los casos pendientes, y sin el nuevo sistema, habríamos necesitado el doble de personal para lograrlo."
  1. What has the speaker already done before leaving this message?
  2. What had already happened by the time the meeting with the committee ended?
  3. What does the speaker predict will have happened by December?
  4. Identify the four compound-tense forms in the message and name which tense each one represents.

Speaking Practice

Práctica controlada

Take turns saying three things you have already done today or this week, using the present perfect (He..., Hemos...).

Interacción guiada

With a partner, describe a project or process at work, school, or home. Sequence two past events using the pluscuamperfecto: describe what had already happened before something else occurred. Your partner should ask one follow-up question about the sequence.

Alternativa individual

Record yourself making a short prediction using the futuro perfecto (what will likely have happened by a future date in a project you know) and a short reflection using the condicional perfecto (what would have happened without some key resource or decision).

Reading Practice

Read the following adapted year-end summary and complete the tasks that follow.

Balance anual del sistema de trámites digitales

Este año, la oficina ha resuelto el noventa por ciento de las solicitudes recibidas, un avance significativo frente al setenta por ciento del año anterior. Cuando el sistema entró en funcionamiento en marzo, el organismo ya había acumulado más de dos mil solicitudes atrasadas, muchas de las cuales llevaban meses sin respuesta.

Para fin de año, el equipo habrá cerrado prácticamente todos los casos pendientes, incluidos los más antiguos. Sin la automatización que ofrece el nuevo sistema, la oficina habría necesitado contratar personal adicional para lograr resultados similares, según explicó la directora del proyecto en una entrevista reciente.

  1. What has the office accomplished this year, and how does it compare with the previous year?
  2. Identify the pluscuamperfecto in the first paragraph and explain what earlier past event it describes.
  3. Identify the futuro perfecto and the condicional perfecto in the second paragraph and translate each.
  4. According to the director, what would have been necessary without the new system?

Writing Workshop

Tarea de escritura

  1. Choose a project, process, or period of time you know reasonably well (school year, work project, personal goal).
  2. Write two sentences using the present perfect to describe what has already been accomplished.
  3. Write one sentence using the pluscuamperfecto to sequence an earlier past event relative to a later one.
  4. Write one sentence using the futuro perfecto to predict what will likely have happened by a future point, and one sentence using the condicional perfecto to reflect on what would have happened under different conditions.

Lista de revisión:

  • The correct form of haber matches the intended tense (present, past, future, or conditional perfect).
  • Past participles are invariable and correctly formed, including irregulars.
  • Nothing separates haber from the participle.
  • The present perfect is used for actions relevant to now; the simple preterite for closed past events.
  • Accents, agreement, register, and relative structures from Chapter 11 remain consistent.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context

Year-end summaries, progress reports, and public accountability documents are common across many Spanish-speaking public institutions, and they commonly rely on compound tenses to distinguish what has already been completed, what had happened before a key milestone, and what is projected to be finished by a future deadline. Learning to control these tenses supports clear, professional reporting in workplace and civic contexts.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake

Error: Making the past participle agree in gender or number, as in *la carta ha sido escrita y puesta when used after haber in an active compound tense, or inserting a word between haber and the participle, as in *he la escrito.

Correction: Keep the participle invariable after haber and keep haber and the participle adjacent: la he escrito.

Common Mistake

Error: Using a regular participle ending for irregular verbs, as in *escribido instead of escrito, or *resolvido instead of resuelto.

Correction: Memorize high-frequency irregular participles individually: escrito, resuelto, puesto, devuelto, among others.

Common Mistake

Error: Using the pluscuamperfecto to describe a single past event with no second point of reference, as in *ayer había comido temprano when no earlier past event is implied.

Correction: Reserve the pluscuamperfecto for sequencing one past event before another: cuando llegué, ya había comido.

Answer Key

Guided Practice

1. ha recibido.   2. había resuelto.   3. escrita... puesta (participles agree here because they function as adjectives with ser, not as part of an active compound tense with haber; note for learners that this differs from the invariable participle after haber).   4. resolvimos (simple preterite; last year is a closed period).   5. habría tardado.

Listening Task — Model Analysis

1. Reviewed the summary Andrés sent.   2. The committee had already approved next year's budget by the time the meeting ended.   3. Almost all pending cases will have been closed by December.   4. he revisado (presente perfecto), habían aprobado (pluscuamperfecto), habremos cerrado (futuro perfecto), habríamos necesitado (condicional perfecto).

Reading Task — Model Analysis

1. The office has resolved ninety percent of applications this year, up from seventy percent last year.   2. ya había acumulado; it describes solicitudes that had already piled up before the system launched in March.   3. habrá cerrado (will have closed) and habría necesitado (would have needed).   4. Additional staff would have been necessary without the new automated system, according to the project director.

Chapter Review — Partes A–C (model answers)

A.1. escrito, resuelto, puesto, devuelto, hablado.   B.1. habrá terminado.   C.1. The action belongs to a closed past period (el año pasado) with no claimed connection to the present moment, so the simple preterite is appropriate rather than the present perfect.

Chapter Review — Parte D (model answer)

Model answer: Learners may note that distinguishing the futuro perfecto from the condicional perfecto, or remembering which past event happened first when using the pluscuamperfecto, often feels confusing at first. Other honest, specific answers are equally acceptable.
