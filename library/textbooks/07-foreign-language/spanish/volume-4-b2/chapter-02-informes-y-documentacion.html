Informes y documentación

El lenguaje de los resúmenes, los informes breves y el estilo nominal · Speak and Write Spanish: Advanced Public, Professional, and Academic Spanish

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Recognize the features of a document-oriented, nominal writing style used in short reports and summaries.
  2. Use a small set of impersonal formulas with se (such as se adjunta and se recomienda) to describe institutional actions without naming a specific subject.
  3. Continue using usted and ustedes consistently in a document-based professional context.
  4. Build longer noun phrases with accurate gender, number, and agreement to describe activities, results, and recommendations.
  5. Distinguish a small set of consonant sounds — r/rr, b/v, d, g/j, ll/y, and ñ — that most often affect intelligibility for English-speaking learners.
  6. Understand a short spoken workplace announcement or public message.
  7. Read and interpret a short activity report written in a nominal, document-oriented style.
  8. Write a brief summary of activities using appropriate document conventions.

This chapter builds directly on the formal register and consistent usted use established in Chapter 1. It introduces a small number of fixed impersonal expressions with se for describing institutional actions in reports. It does not yet teach the full passive and impersonal se system, which is developed later in this volume. Treat the expressions introduced here as formulaic units for now.

Communication Scenario

Daniela has completed her first month with the organization. Her supervisor, el señor Aguilar, has asked her to prepare a short written summary of the activities her team completed during the month, along with a brief report to share with a partner office. These documents must sound like organized, professional writing rather than a spoken account of her month, and they must state actions and results clearly without dwelling on who personally carried out each task.

  1. Think about a report, summary, or set of meeting notes you have read or written, in any language. How does that kind of writing sound different from an ordinary conversation about the same events?
  2. As you move through this chapter, notice how Spanish can describe an action, a result, or a recommendation without repeatedly naming the specific person responsible for it.

Tratarse de is introduced here as a lexical pronominal verb: its se does not mean that a person does something "to themselves." The whole expression simply means "to be about" or "to concern," and it is normally used only in the third person, as in el informe se trata de los avances del mes.

Grammar Focus: Estilo nominal y el se impersonal

Purpose: to develop a document-oriented nominal style within the agreement systems learners already control, and to introduce a small set of fixed impersonal expressions with se used to describe institutional actions in reports.

Grammar: Estilo nominal para informes y resúmenes

Reports and summaries typically favor longer, more precise noun phrases over strings of short sentences built around yo or nosotros. This is a matter of style, not new grammar: the same gender, number, and agreement rules learners already know simply apply across longer, more complex noun phrases.

  • Durante el mes se completaron tres actividades principales relacionadas con la coordinación de proyectos internacionales. During the month, three main activities related to the coordination of international projects were completed.
  • El equipo alcanzó un avance significativo en el objetivo principal del trimestre. The team achieved significant progress on the quarter's main objective.

Notice how each noun phrase above stacks several agreeing elements — article, noun, adjective, and sometimes a prepositional phrase — while every element still agrees in gender and number exactly as in shorter sentences.

Grammar: El se impersonal en fórmulas de informe (uso formulaico)

Spanish reports commonly describe an institutional action without naming a specific subject, using a small set of fixed expressions built with se. At this stage, learn the following expressions as ready-made formulas rather than analyzing their internal grammar in depth. The full system of passive and impersonal se is developed later in this volume.

  • Se adjunta el informe completo. The complete report is attached.
  • Se recomienda continuar con la misma estrategia. It is recommended to continue with the same strategy.
  • Se solicita una respuesta antes del viernes. A response is requested before Friday.

In each formula, the action is described without stating who specifically attaches, recommends, or requests. This is a natural and very common feature of report writing, not a way of avoiding responsibility.

Grammar: Usted y ustedes en un contexto documental

Even in nominal, document-style writing, direct address to a reader or recipient still uses usted or ustedes under this course's default model.

  • Le agradecemos su atención a este informe. We thank you for your attention to this report.
  • Quedamos atentos a cualquier comentario que ustedes puedan tener. We remain attentive to any comments you [plural] may have.

Práctica de repaso:

  1. Reescribe esta oración con un estilo más nominal, apropiado para un informe: "Hicimos tres cosas este mes y todo salió bien."
  2. Completa la fórmula impersonal: "___ (adjuntar) el informe completo para su revisión."
  3. Explica en una oración qué significa tratarse de en "Este informe se trata de los avances del proyecto."

Pronunciation Focus: Consonantes clave

Purpose: to introduce a set of consonant contrasts that most often affect intelligibility for English-speaking learners, using vocabulary already active in this chapter and the chapter before it.

Pronunciation: r/rr, b/v, d, g/j, ll/y, ñ

Spanish distinguishes a single tapped r from a trilled rr: compare pero (but) and perro (dog). The letters b and v represent the same sound in Spanish, so words such as avance and objetivo do not distinguish them the way English does. The letter d often softens between vowels, as in resultado. The letters g before e/i and j share a sound close to English "h," as in objetivo and trabajo. Under the broadly yeísta model used in this course, ll and y normally represent the same sound, as in desarrollo and actividad y resultado. Finally, ñ represents a distinct sound from plain n, as in desempeño.

These contrasts do not require a new pronunciation system; they simply deserve focused attention now that longer, more complex vocabulary is entering the course.

  1. Read aloud: resultado, avance, objetivo, desempeño, desarrollo. Pay attention to d, b/v, and ñ.
  2. Practice the contrast between pero and perro in two short sentences of your own.

Model Dialogue: Preparando el informe mensual

Context: Daniela and a colleague, Marisol, review the monthly report before sending it to el señor Aguilar. Relationship: colleague to colleague; setting: professional, document preparation.

Marisol: ¿Ya tienes lista la sección de resultados del informe? Do you already have the results section of the report ready?
Daniela: Casi. El informe se trata principalmente de los avances del proyecto de coordinación regional. Almost. The report is mainly about the progress of the regional coordination project.
Marisol: Perfecto. ¿Se incluyó la recomendación final que discutimos la semana pasada? Perfect. Was the final recommendation we discussed last week included?
Daniela: Sí. Se recomienda continuar con la misma estrategia durante el próximo trimestre. Yes. It is recommended to continue with the same strategy during the next quarter.
Marisol: Muy bien. ¿Y el anexo con las cifras completas? Very good. And the appendix with the complete figures?
Daniela: Se adjunta al final del documento. Solo falta revisar la concordancia en un par de oraciones. It is attached at the end of the document. I only need to check the agreement in a couple of sentences.
Marisol: Entonces quedamos listas para enviárselo al señor Aguilar esta tarde. Then we're ready to send it to Mr. Aguilar this afternoon.

Both speakers use the impersonal se formulas naturally when describing what the report does or contains, while still addressing their supervisor with usted in the surrounding professional context.

  1. Find every use of an impersonal se formula in the dialogue.
  2. Identify the phrase that uses tratarse de and explain what it means here.
  3. Rewrite Daniela's third turn using a more conversational, non-report style, as if explaining the same idea to a friend.

Reading Passage: Informe breve de actividades

Informe mensual — Área de proyectos internacionales

Este informe se trata de las actividades realizadas por el equipo de proyectos internacionales durante el mes de marzo. Durante este período se completaron tres actividades principales: la coordinación de una reunión regional, la revisión de dos propuestas de cooperación y la preparación de material informativo para los socios del proyecto. Se alcanzó un avance importante en el objetivo principal del trimestre, aunque todavía queda trabajo pendiente en la segunda propuesta. Se recomienda continuar con la misma estrategia de coordinación durante el próximo mes y se solicita una reunión de seguimiento antes de fin de mes. Se adjunta, a continuación, un resumen de las cifras principales del período.

This report is an adapted textbook composition written for this chapter, not an authentic institutional document.

  1. Identify each impersonal se formula in the report and state, in your own words, what institutional action each one describes.
  2. Find the sentence that uses tratarse de and explain its function in the report.
  3. List the three main activities mentioned in the report.
  4. In two or three sentences of your own, summarize the report's main recommendation.
  5. Explain how this report's style differs from the way you might describe the same month's work in an ordinary conversation.

Writing Workshop: Un resumen de actividades

Write a short activity summary (100 to 150 words) describing what a team, class, or project accomplished over a recent period. Use at least two impersonal se formulas from this chapter and maintain a nominal, document-oriented style.

  1. Indica el tema del informe. Open with a sentence that states what the report is about, optionally using tratarse de.
  2. Describe las actividades principales. Use longer noun phrases to describe what was done, checking agreement carefully.
  3. Menciona un resultado o avance. State a result or piece of progress in nominal style.
  4. Incluye una recomendación. Use se recomienda or a similar formula to state a recommendation.
  5. Cierra el informe. Use a closing formula such as se adjunta or quedamos atentos a sus comentarios.

Lista de revisión:

  • I used at least two impersonal se formulas correctly.
  • I used longer, agreement-accurate noun phrases rather than only short conversational sentences.
  • I maintained usted/ustedes consistently wherever I addressed a reader directly.
  • I included at least one result and one recommendation.
  • I checked gender and number agreement across longer noun phrases.
  • I checked accents and punctuation throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context

Written reports in many Spanish-speaking workplaces and institutions favor a somewhat impersonal, document-oriented tone, describing actions, results, and recommendations rather than centering on who personally performed each task. This tendency varies by institution, sector, and country, and it should not be mistaken for a lack of individual accountability; responsibility is often established elsewhere in the document, such as in a signature line or a named area.

Regional Note (Recognition Only)

Some organizations prefer a more personal reporting style that names individuals directly, especially in smaller teams or less formal sectors, while larger institutions and government bodies tend toward the impersonal style introduced in this chapter. Learners should adapt to the conventions of their own workplace rather than assuming one single "correct" report style across all Spanish-speaking institutions.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake

Error: Writing a report in a purely conversational style: Hicimos muchas cosas este mes y todo salió muy bien.

Correction: Use nominal, document-oriented phrasing: Durante el mes se completaron varias actividades con resultados positivos.

Common Mistake

Error: Treating tratarse de as an ordinary reflexive verb meaning "to treat oneself": Yo me trato de los proyectos internacionales.

Correction: Use tratarse de only impersonally, about a text or topic: El informe se trata de los proyectos internacionales.

Common Mistake

Error: Losing gender or number agreement in longer noun phrases: Se completaron tres actividad importante.

Correction: Keep every element of the noun phrase agreeing: Se completaron tres actividades importantes.

Answer Key

Grammar Focus Practice

  1. Model answer: Durante el mes se completaron tres actividades principales con resultados positivos.
  2. Se adjunta el informe completo para su revisión.
  3. Tratarse de here means "to be about" — the report's topic is the project's progress; no one is literally treating themselves.

Model Dialogue — Practice

1. The impersonal se formulas are "se incluyó," "se recomienda," and "se adjunta."   2. "El informe se trata principalmente de los avances del proyecto de coordinación regional" uses tratarse de to state the report's main topic.   3. Model answer: "Oye, el informe habla sobre todo de cómo va avanzando el proyecto de coordinación regional."

Reading Task — Model Analysis

1. The formulas are "se completaron," "se alcanzó," "se recomienda," "se solicita," and "se adjunta," each describing an institutional action (activities completed, progress achieved, a recommendation made, a meeting requested, and a summary attached) without naming a specific person.   2. "Este informe se trata de las actividades realizadas por el equipo..." states the report's topic using tratarse de.   3. The three main activities are coordinating a regional meeting, reviewing two cooperation proposals, and preparing informational material for project partners.   4. Model answer: The report recommends continuing the same coordination strategy next month and requests a follow-up meeting before the end of the month.   5. Model answer: The report avoids naming specific people and instead describes actions and results in longer, more formal noun phrases, unlike an ordinary spoken account of the same month.

Listening Task

Model answers: Anuncio 1 — el personal debe entregar el informe mensual antes del viernes a las cinco. Anuncio 2 — los coordinadores de área deben revisar sus presupuestos antes de la próxima reunión. Anuncio 3 — la reunión de seguimiento del proyecto regional se trasladó al próximo lunes.

Chapter Review — Parte D (model answer)

Model answer: Learners may note that recognizing impersonal se formulas in reading feels increasingly natural, while producing longer nominal sentences with correct agreement in their own writing still requires conscious attention. Other honest, specific answers are equally acceptable.
