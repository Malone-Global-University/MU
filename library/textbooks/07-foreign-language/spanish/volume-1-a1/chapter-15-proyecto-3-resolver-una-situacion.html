Proyecto 3: Resolver una situación

Solving a Situation · Speak and Write Spanish: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Combine food, shopping, directions, and travel-planning language to handle a real, connected situation.
  2. Make and respond to requests, and use repair language when a plan needs to change.
  3. Understand and produce spoken and written Spanish about a problem and its solution.
  4. Demonstrate control of vocabulary, grammar, pronunciation, and orthography from Chapters 11–14.
  5. Complete an integrated listening, speaking, reading, and writing performance task.

This chapter introduces no major new grammar system. It recycles and integrates the vocabulary and structures activated in Chapters 11–14: food and ordering, stem-changing verbs, direct-object pronouns, shopping and comparisons, demonstratives, directions and affirmative tú commands, and ir a + infinitive with introductory por/para. All material from Chapters 1–14 remains active.

Communication Scenario

Diego and Marisol's trip has a problem: their hotel reservation was made for the wrong dates. They need to call the hotel, understand the mistake, ask about alternatives, and decide what to do next — all while managing their food, shopping, and transportation plans for the rest of the day.

  1. You will listen to a phone call about a reservation problem and identify the issue and the solution.
  2. You will practice requests, polite complaints, and repair language in a role-play.
  3. You will read a message describing the situation and respond appropriately.
  4. You will write a connected message that requests a solution to a problem.

Language Note — Repair and problem-solving language

Handling a problem in Spanish depends on language you already have: polite requests with querer and poder, clarification questions with qué and cómo, and courteous phrases from earlier chapters such as Disculpe, Perdón, and ¿Puede repetir, por favor? This chapter combines those familiar tools with the travel, shopping, and food vocabulary you already control. No new grammar is required to solve the situation below — only careful use of what you already know.

Listening: La reserva con problemas

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Context: Marisol calls the hotel because the reservation she made appears to be for the wrong dates.

Recepción: Hotel Vista al Mar, buenas tardes. ¿En qué puedo servirle? Hotel Vista al Mar, good afternoon. How can I help you?
Marisol: Buenas tardes. Tengo una reserva para una habitación doble, pero creo que hay un problema con las fechas. Good afternoon. I have a reservation for a double room, but I think there's a problem with the dates.
Recepción: Un momento, por favor. ¿Puede darme su nombre? One moment, please. Can you give me your name?
Marisol: Sí, la reserva está a nombre de Diego Ramírez. Yes, the reservation is under the name Diego Ramírez.
Recepción: Aquí está. Veo la reserva para dos noches, del quince al diecisiete. ¿Es correcto? Here it is. I see the reservation for two nights, from the fifteenth to the seventeenth. Is that correct?
Marisol: No, no es correcto. Vamos a quedarnos por cinco noches, no dos. No, that's not correct. We are going to stay for five nights, not two.
Recepción: Entiendo. Lo siento mucho. ¿Puede confirmar las fechas correctas, por favor? I understand. I'm very sorry. Can you confirm the correct dates, please?
Marisol: Sí, necesitamos la habitación del quince al veinte. Yes, we need the room from the fifteenth to the twentieth.
Recepción: Perfecto. Voy a corregir la reserva ahora mismo. ¿Necesita algo más? Perfect. I'm going to correct the reservation right now. Do you need anything else?
Marisol: Sí, una pregunta más. ¿El hotel queda cerca de un mercado o de un restaurante? Yes, one more question. Is the hotel near a market or a restaurant?
Recepción: Sí, hay un mercado a dos cuadras. Doble a la derecha en la esquina y va a verlo. Yes, there's a market two blocks away. Turn right at the corner and you'll see it.
Marisol: Muchas gracias por su ayuda. Thank you very much for your help.
  1. What was the mistake in the reservation, and what are the correct dates?
  2. What does the receptionist do to solve the problem?
  3. How does Marisol get directions to the market, and what command form does the receptionist use?

Speaking: Resolver una situación

Práctica controlada — Controlled practice: Hacer una queja cortés

Say three sentences politely pointing out a problem with an order, a reservation, or a purchase, using language from Chapters 11–14.

Tarea comunicativa — Communicative task: Llamar al hotel

With a partner, role-play the phone call above. One person plays the hotel receptionist; the other plays the guest with a reservation problem. Change at least one detail (the dates, the type of room, or the number of nights).

Proyecto oral — Oral project: Un día con un problema

Describe a full day that includes ordering food, shopping, asking for directions, and discovering a travel problem. Explain the problem and how you solved it, using at least one ir a + infinitivo sentence and one affirmative tú command.

Práctica independiente — Solo alternative

Record or say aloud a short monologue in which you explain a mix-up (with an order, a purchase, or a reservation) and how you resolved it, using at least five sentences.

Reading: Un mensaje urgente

Un mensaje de Diego — A message from Diego

Hola, Marisol. Tengo un problema. Fui al mercado para comprar la comida para el viaje, pero la tienda no tiene las frutas que necesitamos. El vendedor dice que hay otra tienda cerca, dos cuadras a la izquierda. Voy a ir allí ahora. También necesito preguntar sobre el autobús: el que sale a las nueve no va directamente al aeropuerto. Creo que debemos tomar un taxi en vez de manejar. ¿Puedes llamar al hotel otra vez para confirmar la hora de llegada? Nos vemos en el mercado en media hora.

Tarea de lectura — Reading task:

  1. What problem does Diego describe, and what does he decide to do about it?
  2. What does Diego say about the bus, and what alternative does he suggest?
  3. What does Diego ask Marisol to do?

Writing Workshop

Complete the following linked writing tasks in order. Together they form the written half of this chapter's integrated performance.

Tarea 1 — Describir el problema: Write two to three sentences describing a problem with an order, purchase, or reservation, using vocabulary from Chapters 11–14.

Tarea 2 — Pedir una solución: Write two to three sentences politely requesting a solution, using querer, poder, or a request pattern you already know.

Model request patterns
¿Puede ayudarme, por favor?
Necesito cambiar la reserva.
¿Podemos pedir otra opción?
Quisiera resolver este problema hoy.
Use one or more of these patterns, or a similar one you already control, in your message.

Tarea 3 — Un mensaje completo: Combine your sentences from Tareas 1 and 2 into one connected message of six to eight sentences describing a situation (a shopping problem, a food order gone wrong, a directions mix-up, or a travel-planning issue) and requesting a solution. Include at least one ir a + infinitivo sentence, one direct- or indirect-object pronoun, and one affirmative tú or usted command if appropriate.

  1. Choose your situation. Base it on food, shopping, directions, or travel from Chapters 11–14.
  2. Write the problem sentences first. Keep them clear and specific.
  3. Add the request sentences. Use polite, familiar request language.
  4. Combine everything into one connected message. Add a greeting and a closing.
  5. Read your message aloud once. Listen for stress, linking, and diphthongs practiced in earlier chapters.
  6. Check every verb form, object pronoun, and accent mark. Confirm each matches the intended meaning.

Lista de revisión — Editing checklist:

  • The problem and the request are both clearly stated.
  • Object pronouns are placed correctly and agree with their reference.
  • Ir a + infinitivo, if used, is formed correctly.
  • Por and para, if used, match one of the four patterns from Chapter 14.
  • Any command form matches the correct address form (tú or usted).
  • Accent marks, punctuation, and capitalization are correct.
  • No vocabulary or grammar from outside Chapters 1–15 has been used.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Complaining and requesting politely

Across Spanish-speaking communities, pointing out a problem is generally done with courtesy markers such as Disculpe, Perdón, or Lo siento, pero... before stating the issue directly. Directness and softening vary by context, relationship, and region; in many service settings, a calm, specific description of the problem followed by a clear request tends to be effective. This is a general tendency, not a fixed rule for every situation or community.

Regional Variation — Requesting the bill or confirming a reservation

Ways of requesting a check or confirming a reservation vary by country and setting; some speakers say la cuenta, por favor, while others use different service phrases depending on the establishment. This course treats the request patterns modeled in this chapter as core productive forms, with the understanding that learners will encounter additional regional variants when traveling or interacting with different communities.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Confusing pedir and preguntar

Incorrect: Voy a pedir la hora del vuelo.

Correct: Voy a preguntar la hora del vuelo. Use preguntar to ask a question or request information. Use pedir to request an item, service, or favor, as in Voy a pedir la cuenta (I'm going to ask for the bill).

Answer Key

  1. The reservation showed only two nights (the fifteenth to the seventeenth) instead of the correct five nights (the fifteenth to the twentieth).
  2. The receptionist corrects the reservation immediately and gives directions to a nearby market, using the affirmative usted command Doble ("turn").

Escritura
