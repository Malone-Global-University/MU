Proyecto final: Mi mundo en español

Final Project: My World in Spanish · Speak and Write Spanish: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

This final chapter of Volume I does not introduce new grammar. Instead, it brings together everything you have built across nineteen chapters: introducing yourself, describing your world, talking about routines and preferences, making plans, handling everyday transactions, describing what is happening right now, and narrating past experiences. Mi mundo en español is your opportunity to show, in speech and in writing, everything you can now do in Spanish.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Introduce yourself and describe your identity, family, and surroundings using articles, agreement, ser, estar, hay, and tener.
  2. Describe your daily routine, preferences, and free-time activities using present-tense verbs, reflexives, and gustar.
  3. Handle practical transactions such as ordering food, shopping, and asking for directions.
  4. Talk about upcoming plans using ir a + infinitivo and describe what is happening right now using the present progressive.
  5. Narrate a past experience using the preterite and imperfect, distinguishing events from background description.
  6. Combine all of these systems into one connected oral and written self-portrait.

This chapter recycles vocabulary and grammar from Chapters 1–19. No new productive grammar, vocabulary domain, or regional pathway is introduced here.

Communication Scenario

Camila is finishing her first course in Spanish. Her instructor asks each student to prepare a final presentation, called Mi mundo en español, that introduces who they are, describes their daily life, and shares a memorable past experience. Camila also records a short video message for a new online friend in Mexico.

  1. You will listen to a short interview in which a classmate presents her own mundo en español.
  2. You will prepare and deliver your own final oral presentation, combining identity, routine, plans, and past experience.
  3. You will read a short personal blog post that models the same combination of systems.
  4. You will write a connected self-portrait that a new Spanish-speaking friend could read to get to know you.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Note — Reading your own presentation aloud

As you prepare your final oral presentation, review three pronunciation patterns from earlier chapters: the stable five-vowel system, the stress shift between present and preterite forms (hablo versus hablé), and the written accents on interrogative words and pronoun-attachment forms. Read your presentation aloud slowly at least twice before recording or performing it, listening specifically for these three patterns.

Grammar: Integrated Review

Purpose: to combine, without introducing new forms, the grammar systems needed to present yourself, your routine, your plans, your current activity, and a past experience in one connected text.

Identity and description: ser, estar, hay, tener

Review
soy / estoy / hay / tengo
identity · location/state · existence · possession/age
Soy de Colombia, estoy en Bogotá, y en mi barrio hay un parque muy bonito. Tengo veinte años.
I'm from Colombia, I'm in Bogotá, and in my neighborhood there's a very pretty park. I'm twenty years old.

Routine, preferences, and plans

  • Todos los días me levanto a las siete y desayuno con mi familia. Every day I get up at seven and have breakfast with my family. reflexive routine, present tense
  • Me gusta leer y salir con mis amigos los fines de semana. I like reading and going out with my friends on weekends. gustar + infinitives
  • El próximo mes voy a viajar a la costa con mi familia. Next month I am going to travel to the coast with my family. ir a + infinitivo

Current activity

  • Ahora mismo estoy estudiando para mi proyecto final. Right now I am studying for my final project. estar + gerundio

Narrating a past experience

  • El año pasado fui a la casa de mis abuelos y aprendí mucho sobre su vida cuando era joven. Last year I went to my grandparents' house and I learned a lot about her life when she was young. preterite events + imperfect background
Language Focus — Connecting five systems into one presentation

A complete mundo en español presentation typically moves through five stages: who you are, what your daily life is like, what you like to do, what you are doing now or planning soon, and a memorable past experience. Use connectors such as y, pero, porque, cuando, and mientras to link these stages smoothly rather than presenting five disconnected lists.

Práctica guiada — Try it:

  1. Write one sentence about your identity using ser and one using tener.
  2. Write one sentence about your routine using a reflexive verb.
  3. Write one sentence about a plan using ir a + infinitivo.
  4. Write one sentence combining a preterite event with an imperfect background description.

Model Dialogue: Mi mundo en español

Context: Camila records a video message for Valentina, a new online friend in Mexico.

Camila: ¡Hola, Valentina! Me llamo Camila y soy de Colombia. Vivo con mi familia en un barrio tranquilo de Bogotá. Hi, Valentina! My name is Camila and I'm from Colombia. I live with my family in a quiet neighborhood in Bogotá.
Camila: Todos los días me levanto temprano, desayuno con mi familia y voy a la universidad. Me gusta mucho leer y salir con mis amigos. Every day I get up early, have breakfast with my family, and go to the university. I really like reading and going out with my friends.
Camila: Ahora mismo estoy preparando un proyecto final para mi clase de español, y el próximo mes voy a viajar a la costa con mi familia. Right now I'm preparing a final project for my Spanish class, and next month I'm going to travel to the coast with my family.
Camila: El año pasado fui a la casa de mis abuelos y mi abuela me contó cómo era su vida de niña. Fue un día inolvidable. Last year I went to my grandparents' house and my grandmother told me what her life was like as a girl. It was an unforgettable day.
Camila: Y tú, Valentina, ¿cómo es tu mundo en español? ¡Espero tu mensaje! And you, Valentina, what is your world in Spanish like? I'm looking forward to your message!
  1. What five topics does Camila include in her message?
  2. Which sentence describes a plan, and which sentence describes a past experience?
  3. What connector does Camila use to link the event and the background information in her story about her grandmother?

Listening: Una entrevista

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Context: A classmate, Sofía, is interviewed for the class final project.

Entrevistador: Sofía, cuéntanos: ¿quién eres y de dónde eres? Sofía, tell us: who are you and where are you from?
Sofía: Soy Sofía, soy de Perú, y tengo diecinueve años. Vivo con mis padres y mi hermano. I'm Sofía, I'm from Peru, and I'm nineteen years old. I live with my parents and my brother.
Entrevistador: ¿Cómo es un día típico para ti? What's a typical day like for you?
Sofía: Me levanto a las seis, estudio por la mañana y trabajo por la tarde. Los fines de semana, me gusta cocinar y ver películas. I get up at six, study in the morning, and work in the afternoon. On weekends, I like to cook and watch movies.
Entrevistador: ¿Puedes contarnos algo interesante que pasó el año pasado? Can you tell us something interesting that happened last year?
Sofía: Sí, el año pasado viajé a la selva con mi familia. Hacía mucho calor y había muchos animales. Fue una experiencia increíble. Yes, last year I traveled to the jungle with my family. It was very hot and there were many animals. It was an incredible experience.
  1. What does Sofía do on a typical day?
  2. What did Sofía do last year, and where?
  3. Which words in her answer describe background rather than an event?

Writing Workshop

Complete the following linked writing tasks in order to produce your final written self-portrait.

Tarea 1 — Quién soy: Write two to three sentences introducing yourself: name, origin, age, and family, using ser, tener, and hay.

Tarea 2 — Mi rutina y mis gustos: Write two to three sentences describing your typical day and what you like to do, using reflexive verbs, present tense, and gustar.

Tarea 3 — Ahora y pronto: Write one sentence describing what you are doing right now (estar + gerundio) and one sentence describing an upcoming plan (ir a + infinitivo).

Tarea 4 — Una experiencia memorable: Write three to four sentences narrating a memorable past experience, combining preterite events in order with at least one imperfect background sentence.

Model sentence patterns
Me llamo... y soy de...
Todos los días me levanto... y me gusta...
Ahora mismo estoy... y el próximo mes voy a...
El año pasado fui a... Cuando llegué, había... y todos estábamos...
Use patterns like these as models for your own connected self-portrait.

Tarea 5 — Mi mundo en español: Combine Tareas 1–4 into one connected text (eight to twelve sentences) that a new Spanish-speaking friend could read to get to know you. Add an opening greeting and a closing sentence.

  1. Review your four completed tasks. Make sure each stage is clear and accurate.
  2. Choose connectors. Use y, pero, porque, cuando, and mientras to link ideas smoothly.
  3. Add an opening greeting and a closing sentence. Address your text to an imagined new friend.
  4. Combine everything into one connected self-portrait. Keep the five stages in a logical order.
  5. Read your text aloud once. Listen for stress patterns and natural phrasing.
  6. Check every verb form and connector.

Lista de revisión — Editing checklist:

  • Identity and description sentences use ser, estar, hay, and tener correctly.
  • Routine and preference sentences use correct present-tense and reflexive forms.
  • The current-activity sentence uses estar + gerundio correctly.
  • The plan sentence uses ir a + infinitivo correctly.
  • Preterite and imperfect are used correctly to distinguish events from background.
  • Connectors link ideas clearly and the text reads as one connected self-portrait.
  • Accent marks, punctuation, capitalization, and agreement are correct throughout.
  • No vocabulary or grammar from outside Chapters 1–20 has been used.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Sharing your world with new friends

Sharing a personal introduction that combines identity, daily life, and a memorable story is a common way to build new friendships and connections across Spanish-speaking communities, whether in person, by video message, or online. The specific balance of topics and the level of detail shared can vary widely depending on the individual, the platform, and the relationship being built.

Regional Variation — A course-wide reminder

Throughout this volume, you have used tú, usted, and ustedes as your productive address system, following the broadly understood Latin American production baseline of this course. You have also encountered vos and vosotros/vosotras as receptive forms. As you continue interacting with Spanish speakers from different regions, remember that all of these forms are legitimate; none is more correct than another.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Presenting five topics as a disconnected list

Incorrect (disconnected): Me llamo Ana. Tengo rutina. Me gusta leer. Voy a viajar. Fui a la playa.

Correct (connected): Me llamo Ana y todos los días me levanto temprano porque me gusta tener tiempo para leer antes de trabajar. El próximo mes voy a viajar a la playa, y el año pasado fui a un lugar parecido donde hacía mucho calor y todos estábamos muy contentos. Use connectors to link your ideas into one flowing self-portrait rather than five separate statements.

Common Mistake — Mixing preterite and imperfect roles

Incorrect: El año pasado iba a la playa y tenía un día increíble. (intended as a single completed trip and outcome)

Correct: El año pasado fui a la playa y tuve un día increíble. Reserve era, estaba, tenía, and había for background description, and use the preterite to report what actually happened and finished.
