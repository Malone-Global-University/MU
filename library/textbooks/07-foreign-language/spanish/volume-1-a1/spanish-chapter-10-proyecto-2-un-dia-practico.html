Proyecto 2: Un día práctico

Project 2: A Practical Day · Speak and Write Spanish: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

This project chapter brings together Chapters 6–9. By the end of it, you will be able to:

  1. Talk about dates, times, and schedules using regular -ar verbs.
  2. Describe your daily routine using regular -er/-ir verbs and reflexive verbs.
  3. Describe your home and neighborhood using estar, hay, and prepositions of place.
  4. Talk about your interests using gustar, and manage an invitation using querer, poder, saber, and conocer.
  5. Combine all of the above into one connected spoken and written account of a practical day.

This chapter introduces no major new grammar system. Its purpose is to consolidate and assess the material from Chapters 6 through 9 through integrated listening, speaking, reading, and writing tasks.

Communication Scenario

Elena's visit to Marisol and Diego's home is coming to an end for the week, and Marisol has asked Elena to help plan a full, practical day together — one that includes a morning routine, a stop at home, and an afternoon activity. Before the day begins, Elena writes back describing her own typical day and confirms what everyone wants to do.

  1. You will review and combine vocabulary and grammar from Chapters 6–9.
  2. You will describe a full day — schedule, routine, location, and plans — in connected speech and writing.

Unit Review: Grammar

Purpose: consolidate the grammar systems from Chapters 6–9 so that they can be combined in one connected account of a practical day. No form below is new.

Regular present tense

Regular -ar, -er, and -ir verbs in all persons, active since Chapters 6 and 7.

Reflexive verbs

Pronoun placement before a conjugated verb: me despierto, te levantas, se acuesta.

Estar and hay

Estar for location of known things and people; hay for existence of unspecified things.

Gustar and indirect object pronouns

Me/te/le/nos/les + gusta/gustan, agreeing with the thing liked.

Querer, poder

Stem-changing verbs e→ie and o→ue, used to want, to be able, and to invite.

Saber versus conocer

Saber for facts and skills; conocer for people and places, with personal a.

Language Focus — Connecting a full day

A connected account of a day usually moves through time markers (primero, luego, después, por la tarde), routine verbs, a location description with estar or hay, and a closing statement of interests or plans with gustar, querer, or poder. Use this order as a planning tool for the tasks in this chapter.

Model Dialogue: Un mensaje de Marisol

Context: Marisol calls Elena the night before to confirm the plan for their practical day together.

Marisol: Elena, ¿a qué hora te despiertas normalmente? Elena, what time do you normally wake up?
Elena: Me despierto a las siete, me ducho, y desayuno antes de las ocho. I wake up at seven, I shower, and I eat breakfast before eight.
Marisol: Perfecto. Por la mañana estamos en casa. Hay un mercado cerca de la casa, al lado del parque. Perfect. In the morning we're at home. There's a market near the house, next to the park.
Elena: Me gustaría ir al mercado. ¿Y por la tarde? I would like to go to the market. And in the afternoon?
Marisol: A Diego le gusta el fútbol. ¿Quieres ir a un partido con nosotros? Diego likes soccer. Do you want to go to a game with us?
Elena: Claro que sí, me encantaría. Sé un poco de fútbol, pero no conozco el estadio. Of course, I would love to. I know a little about soccer, but I'm not familiar with the stadium.
Marisol: No importa, yo conozco el camino. ¿Puedes estar lista a las nueve? It doesn't matter, I know the way. Can you be ready at nine?
Elena: Sí, puedo. Me acuesto temprano esta noche. Yes, I can. I'm going to bed early tonight.

Notice how the dialogue moves through Chapter 6 time expressions, Chapter 7 routine verbs, Chapter 8 location language, and Chapter 9 interests and invitations — the same integrated pattern used in this chapter's tasks.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening Script for Instructor, Partner, or Text-to-Speech Use
Me llamo Diego. Todos los días me levanto a las seis y media y hago ejercicio antes del trabajo. Vivo en un apartamento cerca del centro. En mi apartamento hay una cocina pequeña y un dormitorio. Al lado de mi edificio hay un parque donde me gusta correr. Por la tarde, después del trabajo, me gusta jugar fútbol con mis amigos. Sé jugar bastante bien, y conozco a muchas personas en el equipo. Este sábado quiero descansar en casa, pero mi amiga Sofía quiere salir. Tal vez podemos hacer las dos cosas.
My name is Diego. Every day I get up at six thirty and I exercise before work. I live in an apartment near downtown. In my apartment there is a small kitchen and a bedroom. Next to my building there is a park where I like to run. In the afternoon, after work, I like to play soccer with my friends. I know how to play pretty well, and I know a lot of people on the team. This Saturday I want to rest at home, but my friend Sofía wants to go out. Maybe we can do both things.
  1. Listen for Diego's morning routine and the time he gets up.
  2. Listen for what is in his apartment and what is near his building.
  3. Listen for what Diego likes to do, what he knows how to do, and his plan for Saturday.

Reading Practice

El día de Sofía — Sofía's day

Me llamo Sofía y vivo con mi familia en una casa cerca del centro. Todos los días me despierto a las siete y me ducho antes de desayunar. Mi casa tiene una cocina grande y un jardín pequeño detrás de la casa. Enfrente de mi casa hay una escuela y, al lado de la escuela, hay un café donde me gusta desayunar los sábados. Por la tarde, después de las clases, me gusta salir con amigos o quedarme en casa para pintar. Sé pintar bastante bien, y conozco a varios artistas del barrio. Este fin de semana mi amigo Rafael quiere venir a mi casa, pero no puede el sábado porque tiene que trabajar. Podemos vernos el domingo.

Tarea de lectura — Reading task:

  1. Describe Sofía's morning routine, including the time she wakes up.
  2. What is near Sofía's house, and what does she like to do there?
  3. What does Sofía know how to do, and why can't Rafael visit on Saturday?

Writing Workshop

Complete the following linked writing tasks in order. Together they form the written half of your Proyecto 2 performance task.

Tarea 1 — Mi rutina: Write three sentences describing your morning routine, using at least two reflexive verbs and one clock-time expression.

Tarea 2 — Mi casa: Write three sentences describing your home or neighborhood, using hay and at least one preposition of place.

Tarea 3 — Completar el plan: Fill in the blanks below to build a short plan for the weekend.

Fill in the blank
Los sábados me ______ (levantarse) tarde. Por la mañana ______ (haber) tiempo para descansar. Me ______ (gustar) mucho ______ [activity]. ¿______ (querer, tú) venir conmigo? ______ (Poder, yo) el sábado, pero no ______ (poder, yo) el domingo porque ______.
On Saturdays I get up late. In the morning there is time to rest. I really like ______. Do you want to come with me? I can on Saturday, but I can't on Sunday because ______.

Tarea 4 — Un día práctico: Combine your sentences from Tareas 1–3 into one connected paragraph of eight to ten sentences describing a full day — routine, home or neighborhood, interests, and a weekend plan or invitation. Use at least one sentence with saber or conocer.

  1. Write your routine sentences first. This reviews reflexive verbs and time expressions.
  2. Write your home and neighborhood sentences. Check hay versus estar and preposition choice.
  3. Complete the fill-in-the-blank plan. Confirm that querer and poder are conjugated correctly.
  4. Combine everything into one connected paragraph. Use time connectors such as primero, luego, and después to link ideas.
  5. Read your paragraph aloud once. Listen for correct stress and smooth linking, as reviewed in Chapters 6–9.
  6. Check every accent mark, agreement, and verb form. Confirm reflexive pronoun placement and gustar agreement.

Lista de revisión — Editing checklist:

  • Reflexive pronouns appear correctly before conjugated verbs.
  • Hay is used for unspecified things; estar is used for the location of known things or people.
  • Every use of gustar agrees in number with the thing or activity liked.
  • Querer and poder show the correct stem change in every form.
  • Saber is used for facts and skills; conocer is used for people and places, with personal a.
  • Time connectors help the paragraph read as one connected account rather than a list.
  • No vocabulary or grammar from outside Chapters 1–9 has been used.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — The shape of a practical day

Daily schedules vary across Spanish-speaking communities and depend on work hours, school schedules, climate, and family structure. Some households organize the day around a longer midday break; others follow a schedule closer to a typical North American workday. Neither pattern should be treated as more representative than the other.

Regional Variation — Naming the midday meal

El almuerzo is broadly understood for the midday meal, though some regions use la comida for the same meal and reserve la cena for the evening meal in different ways. This course uses el almuerzo and la cena as the core productive forms, introduced fully in a later chapter.

Answer Key

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. Elena normally wakes up at seven (me despierto a las siete); before eight she showers and eats breakfast (me ducho y desayuno).
  2. The market is near the house, next to the park (hay un mercado cerca de la casa, al lado del parque); Marisol knows the way to the stadium even though Elena does not (yo conozco el camino).

Escritura

  1. Sample answers: Me levanto a las siete. / En mi casa hay tres dormitorios. / Me gusta el fútbol. (Other accurate sentences are acceptable.)
  2. Sample answer: Sé jugar fútbol, pero no conozco el estadio nuevo. (Other accurate sentences are acceptable.)

Lectura

  1. Sofía wakes up at seven and showers before breakfast; a school and a café are near her house, and she likes to have breakfast at the café on Saturdays; she knows how to paint well, and Rafael cannot visit on Saturday because he has to work.
