A comer: alimentos y restaurantes

Let's Eat: Food and Restaurants · Speak and Write Spanish: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Name common foods, drinks, and restaurant items with correct gender and articles.
  2. Talk about quantities using numbers, containers, and simple measures.
  3. Use stem-changing e → i verbs such as pedir and servir to talk about ordering and being served.
  4. Use direct-object pronouns (lo, la, los, las) to avoid repeating a noun already mentioned in a transactional context.
  5. Order food and drinks, ask about a menu, and manage a simple restaurant interaction in speech and writing.

This chapter introduces new productive vocabulary for food and restaurants, the e → i stem-changing verb pattern, and direct-object pronouns before conjugated verbs. Chapters 1–9 vocabulary and grammar remain active throughout.

Communication Scenario

Elena, Marisol, and Diego decide to have lunch at a small neighborhood restaurant before the soccer game. Elena has never ordered food in Spanish before, so Marisol helps her read the menu, choose dishes, and talk with the server.

  1. You will learn vocabulary for food, drinks, and restaurant transactions.
  2. You will learn to order using stem-changing verbs and direct-object pronouns.
  3. You will practice a complete restaurant interaction in speech and writing.

Common Mistake — Article with food nouns

Incorrect: Quiero pollo con papás. — misspelling papas (potatoes) as papás (parents) confuses the accent mark and the meaning.

Correct: Quiero pollo con papas. Always check written accents carefully; a missing or added accent can change a word's meaning entirely.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — The letter j and soft g in food words

Words like jugo and frijoles use the Spanish j, pronounced with friction farther back in the mouth than an English h, but without the harsh sound of an English j. Practice saying jugo, frijoles, and naranja slowly, keeping the vowels short and clear.

Also review the stress pattern in café and menú: both carry a written accent because the natural stress rule for words ending in a vowel, n, or s would otherwise fall on the second-to-last syllable.

Grammar: Stem-Changing Verbs (e → i)

Purpose: talk about ordering, serving, and asking for things in a restaurant or any request situation.

Building Block — pedir (to order, to ask for)
pido · pides · pide · pedimos · piden
The stressed e of the stem changes to i in every form except nosotros/nosotras, following the same pattern as other -ir stem-changing verbs.
Yo pido la sopa y mi amiga pide el pescado.
I order the soup and my friend orders the fish.

The verbs servir (to serve) and repetir (to repeat, to have a second helping) follow the same e → i pattern: sirvo, sirves, sirve, servimos, sirven; repito, repites, repite, repetimos, repiten.

  • ¿Qué pides tú de postre? What are you ordering for dessert?
  • El mesero sirve la comida rápido. The server serves the food quickly.
  • Nosotros pedimos agua sin gas. We order still water. nosotros form: no stem change
Language Focus — pedir versus preguntar

Pedir means to request something (an item, a favor, an action): Pido la cuenta. Preguntar means to ask a question: Pregunto si hay mesa. Do not use preguntar when ordering food.

Grammar: Direct-Object Pronouns

Purpose: avoid repeating a noun that has already been mentioned, especially useful when confirming an order.

Pronombres de objeto directo — Direct-object pronouns
PronounRefers toExample
lomasculine singular¿El café? Lo quiero con leche.
lafeminine singular¿La sopa? La pido de primer plato.
losmasculine plural¿Los huevos? Los como todos los días.
lasfeminine plural¿Las verduras? Las prefiero al vapor.

A direct-object pronoun goes immediately before a conjugated verb, replacing a noun that receives the action directly and that has already been established in the conversation.

  • —¿Quieres el pescado? —Sí, lo quiero. "Do you want the fish?" "Yes, I want it." lo replaces el pescado
  • —¿Pides la ensalada? —No, no la pido. "Are you ordering the salad?" "No, I'm not ordering it." la replaces la ensalada; no appears before the pronoun
Common Mistake — Pronoun position

Incorrect: Quiero lo.

Correct: Lo quiero. With a single conjugated verb, the direct-object pronoun goes before the verb, not after it.

Attachment to infinitives, gerunds, and commands is not required for production in this chapter and will be developed in later chapters once those structures are active.

Model Dialogue: En el restaurante

Context: Elena, Marisol, and Diego sit down at a small restaurant before the soccer game. A server approaches their table.

Mesera: Buenas tardes. ¿Una mesa para tres? Good afternoon. A table for three?
Marisol: Sí, gracias. ¿Nos puede traer el menú? Yes, thank you. Can you bring us the menu?
La mesera trae tres menús.
Mesera: ¿Qué desean de tomar? What would you like to drink?
Elena: Para mí, un jugo de naranja, por favor. For me, an orange juice, please.
Diego: Yo quiero un café, y ella quiere un agua sin gas. I want a coffee, and she wants a still water.
Mesera: Muy bien. ¿Y de comer, qué van a pedir? Very good. And to eat, what are you going to order?
Elena: Yo quisiera la sopa de primer plato y el pescado de segundo. I would like the soup as a first course and the fish as a second.
Marisol: Yo pido el pollo con arroz y una ensalada. I'll order the chicken with rice and a salad.
Diego: ¿Y el pastel de chocolate? ¿Lo sirven hoy? And the chocolate cake? Are you serving it today?
Mesera: Sí, lo servimos todos los días. ¿Lo quiere ahora o después? Yes, we serve it every day. Do you want it now or later?
Diego: Lo quiero después, con la cuenta. I want it later, with the bill.

Notice how pedir, servir, and direct-object pronouns appear together in a natural ordering sequence, alongside review vocabulary from Chapters 1–9.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening Script for Instructor, Partner, or Text-to-Speech Use
Me llamo Rafael y trabajo en un restaurante pequeño cerca del centro. Todos los días sirvo el desayuno, el almuerzo y la cena. Muchos clientes piden café y pan por la mañana. A mediodía, piden sopa, arroz con pollo, o una ensalada grande. Mi plato favorito es el pescado con verduras; lo pido cuando tengo tiempo para almorzar. Los sábados servimos un pastel de chocolate muy popular. Muchos clientes lo piden de postre y lo repiten dos veces.
My name is Rafael and I work in a small restaurant near downtown. Every day I serve breakfast, lunch, and dinner. Many customers order coffee and bread in the morning. At midday, they order soup, chicken with rice, or a big salad. My favorite dish is fish with vegetables; I order it when I have time to eat lunch. On Saturdays we serve a very popular chocolate cake. Many customers order it for dessert and have it twice.
  1. Listen for what customers typically order in the morning and at midday.
  2. Listen for Rafael's favorite dish and when he orders it.
  3. Listen for what happens on Saturdays with the chocolate cake.

Speaking Practice

Práctica controlada — Controlled practice: ¿Qué pides?

Say four sentences naming foods and drinks you order, using pedir in the yo and nosotros forms.

Tarea comunicativa — Communicative task: En el restaurante

With a partner, role-play a server and a customer. The customer orders a drink, a first course, and a dessert; the server confirms the order using a direct-object pronoun (Lo sirvo ahora).

Proyecto oral — Oral project: Mi restaurante favorito

Describe a restaurant you like: what you usually order, what they serve, and what you would recommend to a friend.

Práctica independiente — Solo alternative

Record or say aloud a complete restaurant order for yourself and one other person, including a drink, a main dish, and a dessert.

Reading Practice

El menú del Café Central — The Café Central menu

Bienvenidos al Café Central. De primer plato servimos sopa del día, ensalada mixta, o pan con queso. De segundo plato, pueden pedir pollo con arroz, pescado con verduras, o carne con papas. Todos los platos vienen con un vaso de agua o un refresco. De postre, servimos pastel de chocolate, helado, o fruta fresca. Si quieren café, lo servimos con o sin leche. La propina no está incluida en la cuenta.

Tarea de lectura — Reading task:

  1. List the three first-course options and the three second-course options.
  2. What comes with every main dish?
  3. What does the menu say about the tip?

Lista de revisión — Editing checklist:

  • Stem-changing e → i verbs are correctly conjugated in every form except nosotros.
  • Direct-object pronouns agree in gender and number with the noun they replace.
  • Direct-object pronouns appear immediately before the conjugated verb.
  • Food vocabulary includes correct articles and gender.
  • Accent marks are preserved on words such as café, menú, and pedí.
  • No vocabulary or grammar from outside Chapters 1–11 has been used.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Ordering and the pace of a meal

Restaurant customs vary widely across Spanish-speaking communities. In many places, meals are treated as an unhurried social occasion, and a server generally will not bring the bill until it is requested. In other settings, especially in busy urban areas, service can move more quickly. Neither pace should be treated as more typical of "Spanish-speaking culture" as a whole.

Regional Variation — Words for common foods

Some food vocabulary varies by region: for example, el jugo is broadly understood, though el zumo is common in Spain; las papas is widely used in Latin America, while las patatas is common in Spain. This course uses el jugo and las papas as core productive forms under the Latin American baseline; other regional terms are recognition-only at this stage.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Confusing pedir and preguntar

Incorrect: Pregunto la cuenta.

Correct: Pido la cuenta. Use pedir when requesting an item, not preguntar, which is reserved for asking a question.
