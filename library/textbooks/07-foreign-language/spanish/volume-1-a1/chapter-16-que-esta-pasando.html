¿Qué está pasando?

What's Happening Right Now · Speak and Write Spanish: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Until now, the present tense (habla, come, vive) has covered both habitual actions and things happening right now. This chapter adds a form dedicated specifically to actions in progress: estar + gerundio, as in Estoy comiendo (I am eating, right now). You will learn how to form the gerund, how to decide between the simple present and the progressive, and how to place object and reflexive pronouns correctly when the progressive is used.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Form the gerund of regular -ar, -er, and -ir verbs, including common irregular gerunds.
  2. Use estar + gerundio to describe actions that are happening at the moment of speaking.
  3. Choose between the simple present and the present progressive depending on meaning.
  4. Place object and reflexive pronouns correctly before estar or attached to the gerund, including the required accent mark.
  5. Understand and produce a short spoken or written exchange describing what several people are doing right now.

This chapter builds on present-tense verbs, direct- and indirect-object pronouns, reflexive verbs, and location language from Chapters 1–15. All prior vocabulary and structures remain active.

Communication Scenario

It's Sunday afternoon, and Camila is on a video call with her family, who live in different cities. Everyone wants to know what everyone else is doing right now — cooking, studying, resting, or getting ready to go out.

  1. You will listen to a family video call and identify who is doing what.
  2. You will practice describing current actions using estar + gerundio.
  3. You will read a family group chat about a busy Sunday.
  4. You will write a short message describing what several people are doing right now.

Pronunciation Focus: Linking with -ndo

Pronunciation Note — The gerund ending

The gerund ending -ndo is stressed on the syllable just before it: hablANdo, comIENdo, viviENdo. In connected speech, estoy and the gerund link smoothly, with almost no pause: estoy leyendo sounds close to [es-toi-le-yen-do]. Practice saying estoy estudiando, está durmiendo, and están comiendo slowly, then at a natural pace, keeping the five vowels short and clear.

Grammar: Estar + gerundio

Purpose: to describe an action that is in progress right now, at the moment of speaking, distinguishing it from habitual actions expressed with the simple present.

Progressive versus simple present

  • Camila estudia los domingos. Camila studies on Sundays. (habitual) simple present — routine
  • Camila está estudiando ahora mismo. Camila is studying right this minute. (in progress) estar + gerundio — happening now

Use the simple present for routines, general facts, and near-future plans already active from earlier chapters. Use estar + gerundio only when the action is genuinely in progress at the moment of speaking. Do not use the progressive for future plans; ir a + infinitivo (Chapter 14) still covers that function.

Pronoun placement with the progressive

Direct-object, indirect-object, and reflexive pronouns may go in either of two positions with the progressive:

  • Lo estoy leyendo. I am reading it. pronoun before the conjugated form of estar
  • Estoy leyéndolo. I am reading it. pronoun attached to the gerund; written accent added
  • Se está arreglando. / Está arreglándose. He/She is getting ready. reflexive pronoun before estar, or attached to the gerund
Language Focus — The accent rule for attached pronouns

When a pronoun attaches to the end of a gerund, the gerund's natural stress does not move, but adding a syllable means a written accent is now required to keep the stress in the same place: leyendo → leyéndolo; arreglando → arreglándose. Both placements are correct and equally common; the accent is not optional once the pronoun is attached.

Práctica guiada — Try it:

  1. Complete with the correct gerund: Mi padre _____ (cocinar) la cena.
  2. Rewrite with the pronoun attached to the gerund: Lo estoy escribiendo.
  3. Choose simple present or progressive: Normalmente, ella _____ (trabajar) los lunes, pero ahora _____ (descansar).

Model Dialogue: Videollamada de domingo

Context: Camila video-calls her family. Everyone describes what they are doing right now.

Camila: ¡Hola a todos! ¿Qué están haciendo? Hi everyone! What are you all doing?
Mamá: Estoy cocinando el almuerzo. Tu papá está poniendo la mesa. I'm cooking lunch. Your dad is setting the table.
Papá: Sí, y también estoy escuchando música mientras trabajo. Yes, and I'm also listening to music while I work.
Camila: ¿Y Tomás? ¿Está estudiando? And Tomás? Is he studying?
Mamá: No, no está estudiando todavía. Está durmiendo. No, he isn't studying yet. He's sleeping.
Camila: Típico. Yo estoy leyendo un libro para mi clase. Lo estoy terminando hoy. Typical. I'm reading a book for my class. I'm finishing it today.
Papá: Muy bien. Nosotros vamos a comer en media hora. ¿Te llamamos otra vez después? Very good. We're going to eat in half an hour. Should we call you again afterward?
Camila: Sí, perfecto. Voy a estar arreglándome para salir con unos amigos. Yes, perfect. I'll be getting ready to go out with some friends.
  1. Who is cooking, and who is setting the table?
  2. Why doesn't Tomás answer the video call at first?
  3. What is Camila doing with the book, and how does she express it with a pronoun?

Guided Practice

  1. Look at the picture in your mind: a busy kitchen. Write three sentences with estar + gerundio describing what different people are doing.
  2. Transform each simple-present sentence into the progressive: Ana come. → ______. Los niños juegan. → ______.
  3. Rewrite each sentence, moving the pronoun to the other correct position: Estoy haciéndola. → ______. Nos estamos preparando. → ______.
  4. Decide: simple present or progressive? Todos los días, mi abuela _____ (caminar) por el parque. / Ahora mismo, mi abuela _____ (caminar) por el parque.

Listening: ¿Quién está haciendo qué?

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Context: A radio host describes a scene at a busy family gathering, room by room.

Locutor: Bienvenidos a "Un domingo en casa." En la cocina, la abuela está preparando arroz y los primos están lavando los platos. En la sala, el tío está viendo un partido de fútbol y está gritando mucho. En el patio, dos niños están jugando con el perro, y una niña está leyendo tranquilamente debajo de un árbol. Arriba, alguien está duchándose — probablemente porque va a salir esta noche. Welcome to "A Sunday at Home." In the kitchen, grandma is preparing rice and the cousins are washing the dishes. In the living room, the uncle is watching a soccer match and is shouting a lot. In the patio, two children are playing with the dog, and a girl is reading calmly under a tree. Upstairs, someone is showering — probably because they're going out tonight.
  1. What is happening in the kitchen, and who is doing it?
  2. What is the uncle doing, and how is he doing it?
  3. What are the two activities happening in the patio?

Speaking: En este momento

Práctica controlada — Controlled practice: ¿Qué estás haciendo?

With a partner, ask and answer ¿Qué estás haciendo? five times, each time inventing a different current action.

Tarea comunicativa — Communicative task: Video llamada

In groups of three or four, role-play a family video call like the model dialogue. Each person describes what they are doing right now, and asks at least one other person a follow-up question.

Proyecto oral — Oral project: La casa en este momento

Describe an imaginary house at this exact moment, room by room, saying what at least four different people are doing. Use at least one sentence with an attached pronoun (for example, Está preparándolo).

Práctica independiente — Solo alternative

Look around wherever you are right now (or imagine a place) and record yourself describing, in at least five sentences, what is happening at this moment.

Reading: Un chat familiar

Grupo familiar: Domingo — Family group chat: Sunday

Mamá: ¿Dónde están todos? Tomás: Estoy despertándome ahora, jaja. Camila: Yo estoy terminando mi libro. Lo estoy leyendo en el patio porque hace sol. Papá: Estamos esperándolos para el almuerzo. La comida ya está lista. Tomás: Voy a ducharme rápido y ya bajo. No me esperen mucho más. Mamá: Está bien. Mientras tanto, estamos poniendo la mesa y sirviendo el agua.

Tarea de lectura — Reading task:

  1. Where is Camila reading her book, and why?
  2. What is Tomás doing, and what does he say he is going to do next?
  3. Find two examples of a pronoun attached to a gerund in the chat, and explain what each pronoun refers to.

Writing Workshop

Complete the following linked writing tasks in order.

Tarea 1 — Describir la escena: Write three sentences with estar + gerundio describing what three different people are doing right now, in three different places.

Tarea 2 — Usar un pronombre: Rewrite one of your sentences from Tarea 1 so that an object or reflexive pronoun is attached to the gerund, with the correct accent mark.

Model sentence patterns

Está jugándolos en su cuarto.

Use patterns like these as models for your own sentences.

Tarea 3 — Un mensaje completo: Combine your sentences into one connected message of six to eight sentences describing what is happening right now in a place you imagine (your house, a family gathering, a classroom, or a café). Include at least two uses of estar + gerundio, one sentence with a pronoun attached to a gerund, and one sentence that contrasts a habit (simple present) with what is happening now (progressive).

  1. Choose your scene. Pick a place and imagine three or four people in it.
  2. List the actions first. Write down each action in the infinitive before conjugating.
  3. Form each gerund. Check regular and irregular patterns.
  4. Add at least one pronoun. Try both placements and pick the one you prefer.
  5. Combine everything into one connected message. Add a greeting and a closing.
  6. Read your message aloud once. Listen for the stressed -ndo syllable and smooth linking.
  7. Check every gerund, pronoun placement, and accent mark.

Lista de revisión — Editing checklist:

  • Every gerund is formed correctly, including irregular forms.
  • The progressive is used only for actions genuinely in progress, not for habits or future plans.
  • At least one pronoun is correctly placed, with an accent mark if attached to the gerund.
  • Simple present and progressive are not confused.
  • Accent marks, punctuation, and capitalization are correct throughout.
  • No vocabulary or grammar from outside Chapters 1–16 has been used.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Sunday gatherings

In many Spanish-speaking households, Sunday is commonly a day for an extended family meal, with several generations present and shared cooking and setup tasks. Household roles and schedules vary widely by family, region, and generation; this is a common tendency in some communities, not a fixed rule for every household.

Regional Variation — Alternatives to estar + gerundio

Some varieties of Spanish also use seguir or andar + gerundio to express ongoing or repeated action, with shades of meaning beyond simple progress. This course treats estar + gerundio as the core productive pattern; other progressive-like constructions appear here only as recognition-only background information.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Using the progressive for future plans

Incorrect: Estoy viajando a México el próximo mes.

Correct: Voy a viajar a México el próximo mes. Use ir a + infinitivo for future plans, and reserve estar + gerundio for actions genuinely happening right now.

Common Mistake — Forgetting the accent on an attached pronoun

Incorrect: Estoy leyendolo.

Correct: Estoy leyéndolo. Attaching a pronoun to the gerund adds a syllable, so a written accent is required to keep the stress on the same syllable as before.
