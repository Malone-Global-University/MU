Gustos, habilidades y tiempo libre

Likes, Abilities, and Free Time · Speak and Write Spanish: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Talk about interests and free-time activities using common leisure vocabulary.
  2. Use gustar with indirect object pronouns to say what you and others like.
  3. Use querer and poder to express wants and abilities, and to make and respond to invitations.
  4. Distinguish saber from conocer when talking about knowledge, skills, and familiarity.
  5. Extend, accept, and politely decline an invitation.

Communication Scenario

Elena is still visiting Marisol and Diego, and the three of them are planning what to do this weekend. Elena wants to know what her cousin and Diego like to do in their free time, and she also wants to invite them to a few activities before she goes home.

  1. You will talk about what you and others like using gustar.
  2. You will make plans using querer, poder, and invitation language.

Pronunciation and Orthography Focus

Pronunciation and Orthography Focus — Stress in gustar Forms and Question Intonation

The forms gusta and gustan both carry natural stress on the first syllable; neither requires a written accent.

  • Interesa and interesan follow the same default stress pattern for words ending in a vowel or -n.
  • ¿Quieres...? and ¿Te gustaría...? rise in pitch toward the end when spoken as genuine invitations, and both require the opening inverted question mark in writing.
  • Gustaría carries a written accent on the í because stress falls on a syllable that the default pattern would not predict for a word ending in a vowel.
  • Link te gustaría smoothly as one breath group rather than pausing between words.

Grammar: Gustar, Querer, Poder, and Saber/Conocer

Purpose: give learners the tools to talk about likes and interests, express wants and abilities, and choose correctly between two different Spanish verbs that both correspond to English "to know."

9.1 Gustar and indirect object pronouns

Building Block — Gustar pattern
(A + person) + me/te/le/nos/les + gusta/gustan + noun
Gustar does not work like the English verb "to like." The person who likes something is expressed with an indirect object pronoun, and the verb agrees with the thing that is liked, not with the person.
Me gusta el fútbol. · Me gustan los videojuegos. · A Diego le gusta la música.
I like soccer. (Soccer is pleasing to me.) · I like video games. · Diego likes music.
Language Focus

Use gusta when one thing or one activity is liked, and gustan when more than one thing is liked. Add a + name or a + pronoun (a Diego, a mí) only to clarify or emphasize who is doing the liking.

  • ¿Te gusta bailar? Do you like to dance?
  • Nos gustan los conciertos, pero no nos gusta el teatro. We like concerts, but we don't like theater.

Encantar and interesar follow the identical pattern as gustar and may be used the same way for stronger or more specific reactions.

9.2 Querer and poder

Building Block — Stem-changing present tense
querer → quiero, quieres, quiere, queremos, quieren · poder → puedo, puedes, puede, podemos, pueden
Both verbs change their stem vowel in every form except nosotros: e → ie in querer, and o → ue in poder. Querer expresses wanting; poder expresses ability or permission.
Quiero salir este fin de semana. · ¿Puedes ir al concierto conmigo?
I want to go out this weekend. · Can you go to the concert with me?
  • Queremos ver una película, pero no podemos hoy. We want to watch a movie, but we can't today.
  • ¿Quieres jugar básquetbol el sábado? Do you want to play basketball on Saturday?

9.3 Saber versus conocer

Building Block — Saber and conocer
saber → sé, sabes, sabe, sabemos, saben · conocer → conozco, conoces, conoce, conocemos, conocen
Both verbs correspond to English "to know," but they are not interchangeable. Saber is used for facts and for skills (with an infinitive). Conocer is used for familiarity with people, places, and things. Both verbs are irregular only in the yo form.
Sé jugar fútbol. · Sé que el concierto es el sábado. · Conozco a Diego. · Conozco el parque cerca de tu casa.
I know how to play soccer. · I know that the concert is Saturday. · I know Diego. · I'm familiar with the park near your house.
Language Focus

Use saber + infinitive to talk about a skill: sé bailar, sabe nadar. Use conocer with a person or place, and remember to add personal a before a known person: conozco a Marisol.

This chapter introduces personal a only as part of the fixed pattern conocer a + person. A fuller treatment of personal a is scheduled for later chapters.

9.4 Making, accepting, and declining invitations

  • ¿Te gustaría ir al cine conmigo? Would you like to go to the movies with me?
  • Claro que sí, me encantaría. / Lo siento, no puedo, pero tal vez otro día. Of course, I would love to. / I'm sorry, I can't, but maybe another day.

Model Dialogue: ¿Quieres salir este fin de semana?

Context: Elena, Marisol, and Diego are talking about plans for the weekend.

Elena: Oye, ¿qué les gusta hacer aquí los fines de semana? Hey, what do you like to do here on weekends?
Marisol: A mí me gusta mucho la música. También me gustan los conciertos en el parque. I really like music. I also like the concerts in the park.
Diego: A mí me interesa más el fútbol. Sé jugar bastante bien. I'm more interested in soccer. I know how to play pretty well.
Elena: ¿Conocen un buen lugar para ver un concierto este sábado? Do you know a good place to see a concert this Saturday?
Marisol: Sí, conozco un lugar perfecto. ¿Quieres ir con nosotros? Yes, I know a perfect place. Do you want to go with us?
Elena: Claro que sí, me encantaría. ¿Y tú, Diego, puedes venir también? Of course, I would love to. And you, Diego, can you come too?
Diego: Lo siento, no puedo el sábado. Tengo que trabajar. Pero tal vez otro día. I'm sorry, I can't on Saturday. I have to work. But maybe another day.

Marisol smiles and looks at Elena.

Marisol: No importa. A ti y a mí nos gusta la música, ¿verdad? It doesn't matter. You and I like music, right?

Notice how the dialogue moves from talking about likes with gustar, to knowledge and familiarity with saber and conocer, to an invitation with querer and poder — the same sequence used in this chapter's tasks.

Speaking Practice

Práctica controlada — Controlled practice: Mis gustos

Say four sentences about your own interests, using me gusta/me gustan for two of them and me interesa/me encanta for the other two.

Tarea comunicativa — Communicative task: Una invitación

With a partner, invite each other to two different activities using ¿Quieres...? or ¿Te gustaría...? One invitation should be accepted and one should be politely declined.

Práctica independiente — Solo alternative

Record or say aloud a short description of what you like to do, what you know how to do, and one activity you want to do this weekend if you can.

Reading Practice

Writing Workshop

Complete the following linked writing tasks in order. Together they form your written description of your interests and a weekend invitation for this project.

Tarea 1 — Mis gustos: Write four short sentences using gustar, encantar, or interesar to describe your interests.

Tarea 2 — Completar la invitación: Fill in the blanks below to build a short invitation exchange.

Fill in the blank
¿______ (querer, tú) ir a ______ este fin de semana? ______ (Poder, yo) el sábado, pero no ______ (poder, yo) el domingo porque ______. ¿______ (saber/conocer) un buen lugar cerca de aquí?
Do you want to go to ______ this weekend? I can on Saturday, but I can't on Sunday because ______. Do you know a good place near here?

Tarea 3 — Descripción completa: Combine your sentences from Tareas 1 and 2 into one short paragraph of five to seven sentences describing your interests and inviting a friend to an activity, including at least one use of saber or conocer.

  1. Write your four interest sentences first. This reviews gustar and related verbs.
  2. Complete the fill-in-the-blank invitation. Check that querer and poder are conjugated correctly.
  3. Combine your sentences into one paragraph. Add at least one use of saber or conocer.
  4. Read your paragraph aloud once. Listen for correct stress in gusta/gustan and smooth linking in te gustaría.
  5. Check every accent mark, capitalization choice, and verb agreement. Confirm that gustar agrees with the thing liked, not with the person.

Lista de revisión — Editing checklist:

  • Every use of gustar agrees in number with the thing or activity liked.
  • Indirect object pronouns (me, te, le, nos, les) appear before gustar and related verbs.
  • Querer and poder show the correct stem change in every form except nosotros.
  • Saber is used for facts and skills; conocer is used for people and places.
  • Personal a appears before a known person after conocer.
  • No vocabulary or grammar from outside Chapters 1–9 has been used.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Free time varies by community

Free-time activities and how people spend weekends vary widely across Spanish-speaking communities and depend on climate, city size, family structure, and personal preference. Public parks, plazas, and community sports fields are common gathering places in many cities, but the specific activities people choose differ from person to person and place to place.

Regional Variation — Words for soccer and video games

Some regions use el fútbol universally for soccer, while a few speakers of U.S. Spanish may use el soccer to distinguish it from American football. Similarly, los videojuegos is broadly understood, though some speakers also use borrowed English terms informally. This course uses el fútbol and los videojuegos as the core productive forms.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Treating gustar like the English verb "to like"

Error: Saying Yo gusto el fútbol as if the person who likes something is the subject of gustar.

Correction: The thing liked is the subject, and the person is expressed with an indirect object pronoun: Me gusta el fútbol.

Common Mistake — Using gusta with a plural thing liked

Error: Saying Me gusta los videojuegos when the thing liked is plural.

Correction: Gustar must agree with the plural noun: Me gustan los videojuegos.

Common Mistake — Confusing saber and conocer

Error: Saying Sé a Diego to mean "I know Diego."

Correction: Use conocer for familiarity with a person, with personal a: Conozco a Diego. Reserve saber for facts and skills: Sé jugar fútbol.
