14

Viajes y próximos planes

Travel and Upcoming Plans · Speak and Write Spanish: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Name common travel items, transportation methods, lodging, and weather expressions.
  2. Talk about upcoming plans using ir a + infinitive.
  3. Begin to distinguish por and para in a small set of common, high-frequency uses.
  4. Use common travel verbs to describe a trip.
  5. Ask about and describe the weather.
  6. Manage a short conversation about travel plans in speech and writing.

This chapter introduces new productive vocabulary for travel, weather, and lodging, the pattern ir a + infinitive for near-future plans, a small introductory set of por and para uses, and several new travel verbs. Chapters 1–13 vocabulary and grammar remain active throughout, including ir and venir, affirmative tú commands, and location expressions from Chapter 8.

Communication Scenario

Diego and his friend Marisol are planning a short trip together. They discuss where they are going to go, how they are going to travel, what the weather will be like, and where they are going to stay.

  1. You will learn vocabulary for travel, transportation, lodging, and weather.
  2. You will learn to talk about upcoming plans using ir a + infinitive and to use por and para in a few common patterns.
  3. You will practice a complete conversation about travel plans in speech and writing.

Common Mistake — Using estar instead of hacer for weather

Incorrect: Es mucho calor hoy.

Correct: Hace mucho calor hoy. Most common weather expressions use hacer, not ser or estar. Llueve, nieva, and está nublado are exceptions to learn individually.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — Diphthongs in travel vocabulary

Several new words in this chapter contain diphthongs, two vowels pronounced together in a single syllable: viaje, aeropuerto, avión, and vuelo. Say each vowel pair as one smooth glide rather than two separate syllables. Notice that avión carries a written accent because the stress falls on the final syllable, which is exceptional for a word ending in -n.

Continue to link words across boundaries, as you practiced in Chapter 12: Vamos_a_viajar should flow as one connected phrase.

Grammar: Ir a + Infinitive

Purpose: talk about what someone is going to do in the near future, essential for describing travel plans.

You have used the present tense of ir since Chapter 13. This chapter activates the full communicative pattern ir a + infinitive for talking about upcoming plans.

Building Block — Ir a + infinitive
[ir conjugated] + a + [infinitive]
Conjugate ir to match the subject, add a, and follow with the unconjugated infinitive of the action. This pattern works for any subject and any verb you already know.
Vamos a viajar a la costa en julio.
We are going to travel to the coast in July.
Ir a + infinitivo — by subject
PronounExampleEnglish
yoVoy a reservar el hotel.I am going to reserve the hotel.
tú¿Vas a manejar o vas a volar?Are you going to drive or fly?
usted/él/ellaElla va a llegar el sábado.She is going to arrive on Saturday.
nosotros/asVamos a quedarnos tres noches.We are going to stay three nights.
ustedes/ellos/ellasVan a salir muy temprano.They are going to leave very early.
Language Focus — Ir a + infinitive versus the simple present

Spanish speakers commonly use ir a + infinitive to talk about specific future plans, similar to English "going to." The simple present, which you have used since Chapter 6, can also express near-future scheduled events, especially with a time expression: El vuelo sale a las ocho (The flight leaves at eight). Both patterns are correct; this chapter focuses on ir a + infinitive for personal plans.

  • ¿Qué vas a hacer durante las vacaciones? What are you going to do during vacation?
  • Voy a visitar a mi familia y voy a descansar mucho. I am going to visit my family and I am going to rest a lot.

Grammar: Introductory Por and Para

Purpose: express a small set of common travel-related meanings involving purpose, destination, and duration.

Spanish uses both por and para where English often uses only "for." This chapter introduces a small, controlled set of high-frequency uses tied directly to travel. The full por/para system will be developed gradually across later volumes.

Usos introductorios de por y para
WordThis chapter's useExample
paradestination or recipient of travelSalimos para la costa mañana.
paradeadline or intended timeNecesito el boleto para el viernes.
porduration of timeVamos a quedarnos por una semana.
pormovement through/along a placeViajamos por la costa.
Language Focus — A controlled starting point, not the full system

Do not expect a single English equivalent for por or para. This chapter activates only the four uses above. Other common uses of por and para, including cause, exchange, and recipient of an action, are not yet active and will be developed in a later volume.

  • El tren para la capital sale a las nueve. The train for the capital leaves at nine.
  • Vamos a estar en el hotel por cinco días. We are going to be at the hotel for five days.

Model Dialogue: Planes para el viaje

Context: Diego and Marisol are planning a short trip together and discuss transportation, weather, and lodging.

Marisol: ¿Qué vamos a hacer para las vacaciones? ¿Vamos a viajar a la costa? What are we going to do for vacation? Are we going to travel to the coast?
Diego: Sí, quiero ir. ¿Vamos a volar o vamos a manejar? Yes, I want to go. Are we going to fly or are we going to drive?
Marisol: Prefiero volar. El vuelo para la costa sale a las ocho de la mañana. I prefer to fly. The flight for the coast leaves at eight in the morning.
Diego: Perfecto. ¿Qué tiempo hace allí en julio? Perfect. What's the weather like there in July?
Marisol: Hace mucho sol y hace calor. No llueve mucho en esa temporada. It's very sunny and it's hot. It doesn't rain much in that season.
Diego: Bien, voy a llevar ropa ligera. ¿Dónde vamos a quedarnos? Good, I'm going to bring light clothing. Where are we going to stay?
Marisol: Voy a reservar un hotel cerca de la playa. Vamos a quedarnos por cinco noches. I'm going to reserve a hotel near the beach. We are going to stay for five nights.
Diego: ¿Necesitas mi pasaporte o solo mi nombre para la reserva? Do you need my passport or just my name for the reservation?
Marisol: Solo tu nombre. Necesito la reserva lista para el viernes. Just your name. I need the reservation ready by Friday.
Diego busca su maleta y empieza a hacer una lista.
Diego: ¡Qué emoción! Vamos a tener unas vacaciones muy buenas. How exciting! We are going to have a very good vacation.

Notice how ir a + infinitive, introductory por/para, and the new travel verbs appear together with review vocabulary and structures from earlier chapters.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the script below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening Script for Instructor, Partner, or Text-to-Speech Use
Atención, pasajeros. El vuelo para la capital va a salir a las diez de la mañana desde la puerta seis. Hace viento hoy, pero no va a llover. Los pasajeros que van a viajar con equipaje grande deben llegar al aeropuerto con dos horas de anticipación. Si van a quedarse en la ciudad por más de una semana, recomendamos reservar el hotel antes del viaje.
Attention, passengers. The flight for the capital is going to leave at ten in the morning from gate six. It's windy today, but it's not going to rain. Passengers who are going to travel with large luggage should arrive at the airport two hours in advance. If you are going to stay in the city for more than a week, we recommend reserving the hotel before the trip.
  1. Listen for the flight time and gate number.
  2. Listen for the weather description.
  3. Listen for the recommendation about hotel reservations.

Reading Practice

Un correo sobre el viaje — An email about the trip

Hola, Diego. Ya tengo los boletos para nuestro viaje. Vamos a volar el sábado por la mañana y vamos a llegar a la costa por la tarde. Va a hacer sol toda la semana, así que no necesitas llevar paraguas. Voy a reservar el hotel para las cinco noches que vamos a quedarnos. El hotel queda cerca de la playa, y hay un mercado y un restaurante muy bueno cerca también. ¡Nos vemos el sábado en el aeropuerto!

Tarea de lectura — Reading task:

  1. When are they going to fly, and when will they arrive?
  2. What is the weather going to be like all week, according to the message?
  3. How many nights are they going to stay, and what is near the hotel?

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Travel seasons and holidays

Vacation timing and weather vary widely across Spanish-speaking regions, since the Southern Hemisphere and Northern Hemisphere experience opposite seasons. Many families plan trips around school holidays, religious observances, or national holidays, which differ by country. When discussing travel plans, it is useful to confirm the season and local context rather than assuming a single shared calendar.

Regional Variation — Words for car and swimming pool

The word for "car" varies by region: el carro is common in much of Latin America, while el coche is common in Mexico, Spain, and other regions. This course treats both as recognizable, with el carro as the core productive form; learners will also see el coche in receptive materials. Similarly, la alberca, la piscina, and la pileta all mean "swimming pool" in different regions; la piscina is used as the core productive form in this course.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Forgetting the a in ir a + infinitive

Incorrect: Voy viajar la próxima semana.

Correct: Voy a viajar la próxima semana. The word a is a required part of this pattern and cannot be omitted, even though English "going to" does not need an equivalent extra word.
