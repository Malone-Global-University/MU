Ripasso 3: Situazioni nella comunità

Volume I, Chapter 15: Unit III Review — Situations in the Community · Speak and Write Italian: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 15 closes Unit III by bringing together everything you have learned about moving through a community: asking for directions, making plans, handling a health concern, and managing school or work responsibilities. This chapter introduces no new grammar. Instead, it asks you to combine the language of Chapters 11 through 14 in longer, linked situations. By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Ask for and give directions, and discuss transportation, in a linked exchange.
  2. Make and adjust a leisure plan, including weather and timing.
  3. Describe a basic health concern and understand simple practical advice.
  4. Explain a school or work responsibility and make a polite institutional request.
  5. Move between two or more of these situations in a single connected conversation.
  6. Read and write short practical texts that combine community, health, and work-and-study language.

Communication Scenario

It is Friday afternoon. Giulia is meeting a friend downtown, but first she needs directions to a pharmacy because she has a headache, and she also has to send a quick message to her supervisor about Monday's shift before she loses signal on the metro.

  1. This chapter reviews how to move between separate community situations without losing track of the conversation.
  2. You will practice this through Giulia's afternoon, then apply the same skills to your own linked community tasks.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 15 reviews and integrates Chapters 11 through 14: directions and transportation (Ch. 11), leisure and weather (Ch. 12), health and well-being (Ch. 13), and school and work (Ch. 14). It introduces no new grammar, pronunciation, or vocabulary systems.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Review — rhythm across situations

This chapter reviews rather than introduces pronunciation targets. As you move between directions, weather, health, and work language, keep listening for word stress, doubled consonants, and clean final vowels — for example the contrast between a destra and a sinistra, and the modal-plus-infinitive rhythm in devo consegnare and posso venire.

15.1 Shifting register across one conversation

Notice how intonation changes when Giulia speaks casually with her friend but more formally when she messages her supervisor. Formal requests remain slower and more even, even inside an otherwise informal afternoon.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

No new grammar is introduced in this chapter. The boxes below review systems from Chapters 11–14 that you will need for the tasks ahead.

Review — Movement verbs and route instructions

andare, venire, prendere, uscire in scheduled present forms, plus formulaic route instructions such as gira, vada, prendi, prenda (Ch. 11).

Review — Weather, invitations, and current activity

Weather expressions with fa, c'è, piove, nevica; invitation formulas with vuoi...?, ti va di...?, andiamo...?; controlled stare + gerundio for current activity (Ch. 12).

Review — Physical states and modal advice

avere expressions such as ho mal di testa; stare for wellbeing; avere bisogno di; potere and dovere in high-frequency advice patterns (Ch. 13).

Review — Modals, sapere, and formal requests

potere, dovere, volere across additional persons; sapere versus potere; formal requests with può, posso, vorrei; recognition of Lei agreement in institutional contexts (Ch. 14).

Model Dialogue

Setting: Giulia is walking with her friend Marta downtown, as described in the Communication Scenario above.

Marta Ciao Giulia! Che bella giornata, no? Ti va di prendere un caffè? CHA-o Joo-LEE-a! Keh BEL-la jor-NA-ta, no? Tee va dee PREN-deh-reh oon ka-FEH? Hi Giulia! Beautiful day, isn't it? Do you feel like getting a coffee?
Giulia Ciao Marta! Sì, ma prima ho mal di testa. Sai dov'è una farmacia? CHA-o MAR-ta! See, ma PREE-ma o mal dee TES-ta. Sai do-VEH OO-na far-ma-CHEE-a? Hi Marta! Yes, but first I have a headache. Do you know where a pharmacy is?
Marta Certo. Vai dritto e poi gira a destra. C'è una farmacia vicino alla banca. CHER-to. Vai DREET-to eh poy JEE-ra a DES-tra. Cheh OO-na far-ma-CHEE-a vee-CHEE-no AL-la BAN-ka. Of course. Go straight and then turn right. There's a pharmacy near the bank.
Giulia Grazie. Aspetta, devo anche mandare un'email al mio capo prima di prendere la metro. GRA-tsee-eh. As-PET-ta, DEH-vo AN-keh man-DA-reh oon-eh-MAIL al MEE-o KA-po PREE-ma dee PREN-deh-reh la MEH-tro. Thanks. Wait, I also have to send an email to my boss before taking the metro.
Marta Va bene, non c'è fretta. Che cosa devi dirgli? Va BEH-neh, non cheh FRET-ta. Keh KO-za DEH-vee DEER-lyee? All right, there's no rush. What do you need to tell him?
Giulia Vorrei cambiare il mio turno di lunedì. Posso scrivere velocemente adesso, poi andiamo a prendere il caffè. Vo-RAY kam-BYA-reh eel MEE-o TOOR-no dee loo-neh-DEE. PO-so SKREE-veh-reh veh-lo-cheh-MEN-teh a-DES-so, poy an-DYA-mo a PREN-deh-reh eel ka-FEH. I'd like to change my Monday shift. I can write quickly now, then we'll go get coffee.

Domande di comprensione — Comprehension questions:

  1. What does Giulia need before she can join Marta for coffee?
  2. Where does Marta say the pharmacy is located?
  3. What does Giulia want to ask her boss to change?

Speaking Practice

Pratica controllata — Controlled practice: two situations, one conversation

With a partner, act out a short exchange that combines two review situations — for example, asking for directions and then discussing weather and plans.

Compito comunicativo — Communicative task: the busy afternoon

In groups of two or three, role-play an afternoon that includes at least three of the following: directions, an invitation, a health concern, and a work or school request. Confirm a plan before ending the conversation.

Pratica da solo — Solo alternative

Record yourself narrating a busy afternoon of your own, including at least one direction, one health or well-being statement, and one polite request.

Reading Practice

Read the two short texts below aloud, sounding out each Italian word slowly.

Un cartello — A public sign
Farmacia aperta dal lunedì al sabato, dalle 9:00 alle 19:00. La domenica è chiusa.
Pharmacy open Monday to Saturday, 9:00 to 19:00. Closed on Sunday.
Un messaggio — A message
Gentile signor Bruni, vorrei cambiare il mio turno di sabato con quello di domenica, se possibile. Posso parlarne con Lei lunedì? Grazie mille, Giulia.
Dear Mr. Bruni, I would like to change my Saturday shift for Sunday, if possible. Can I speak with you about it Monday? Thank you very much, Giulia.

Writing Workshop

Write a short connected paragraph combining two or more community situations from Unit III.

Compito di scrittura — Writing task: Write six to eight sentences describing a busy day that includes at least two of the following: directions or transportation, a leisure plan and the weather, a health concern, and a school or work request. Include at least one modal verb sentence and one formal request.

  1. Set the scene. Say where you are and what you are doing.
  2. Add a direction or plan. Use location or invitation language.
  3. Add a complication. Use a health, school, or work sentence.
  4. Make a polite request. Use vorrei... or posso...?.

Lista di controllo — Editing checklist:

  • Directions and location expressions are correctly formed.
  • Modal verbs (potere, dovere, volere) agree with their subject.
  • tu and Lei forms match the correct relationship.
  • Accents and apostrophes are spelled correctly, especially in c'è, dov'è, and un'email.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Moving between registers in one day

Many everyday days require moving smoothly between casual talk with friends, a quick pharmacy interaction, and a formal message to a supervisor. Practices around directness, formality, and timing vary by relationship, workplace, region, and generation, so this should not be treated as a fixed system.

Culture in Context — Pharmacies as a community resource

Pharmacies in many Italian towns and cities offer basic health guidance for minor concerns, in addition to filling prescriptions. Hours, services, and availability vary by location and are not identical everywhere.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Losing the thread between situations

Error: Answering only the last question asked when a conversation combines directions, a plan, and a request, and dropping the earlier topics.

Correction: Use sequence words such as prima, poi, dopo to keep track of more than one topic, as Giulia does in the model dialogue: Grazie. Aspetta, devo anche...

Common Mistake — Dropping formality mid-message

Error: Starting a message to a supervisor formally and then switching to tu-form requests partway through.

Correction: Keep Lei agreement consistent throughout a single formal message, as in Posso parlarne con Lei lunedì?

Common Mistake — Confusing sapere and potere in review contexts

Error: Saying Puoi dov'è la farmacia? to mean "Do you know where the pharmacy is?"

Correction: Use Sai dov'è la farmacia? for knowledge of a location. Reserve potere for ability or permission.
