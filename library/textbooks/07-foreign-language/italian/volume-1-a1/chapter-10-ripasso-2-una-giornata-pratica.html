Ripasso 2: Una giornata pratica

Volume I, Chapter 10: Unit II Review · Speak and Write Italian: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 10 is a review chapter. It does not introduce new grammar. Instead, it brings together everything you learned in Chapters 6-9 — home and neighborhood, daily routines, food and drink, and shopping — and asks you to use it together across one practical, connected day. By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe a home and neighborhood using c'è/ci sono and location expressions.
  2. Explain a daily schedule using present-tense regular and high-frequency irregular verbs.
  3. Order food and drink politely, using quantities and preference language.
  4. Shop for an item, ask its price, compare two choices, and state a preference.
  5. Combine these four everyday domains into a single connected spoken and written account.
  6. Edit a practical text for spelling, accents, apostrophes, agreement, and scheduled verb forms.

Communication Scenario

It is Saturday, and Aisha is spending the day the way she often does: a bit of housework and neighborhood errands in the morning, lunch with Marco in the early afternoon, and a stop at the market and a small shop before heading home. A friend, Chiara, calls partway through the day to ask what Aisha has been up to and what she still needs to do.

  1. This chapter reviews how to describe a home, plan a day, order food, and shop — the four communication domains from Unit II.
  2. You will practice this through Aisha's Saturday, then apply it to a practical day of your own.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 10 closes Unit II. It does not teach new grammar. It consolidates Chapters 6-9 and prepares you for Unit III, where you will move outward into the wider community.

Coming Into This Chapter

You can describe a home and neighborhood with c'è/ci sono (Ch. 6), talk about your daily routine in the present tense (Ch. 7), order food and drink with mi piace/mi piacciono and vorrei (Ch. 8), and shop, compare, and state a preference (Ch. 9).

In This Chapter

No new grammar. You will combine location, routine, food, and shopping language into one connected practical day, in speaking, listening, reading, and writing.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Review — Doubled consonants across Unit II

Unit II contains many high-frequency words with doubled consonants, such as settanta and ottanta, alongside words with a single consonant in the same position, such as costano. Say each pair aloud and listen for the extra length in the doubled forms: costano vs. ottanta.

10.1 Rhythm across a connected day

When you narrate a full day — morning, afternoon, and evening — keep each clause short and let your voice fall slightly at the end of each complete idea before moving to the next: La mattina faccio la spesa. Il pomeriggio pranzo con Marco. La sera guardo la televisione. Avoid rushing all three clauses together without a pause.

Grammar Review

This chapter introduces no new grammar. The boxes below review the systems from Chapters 6-9 that you will need for the integrated tasks in this chapter.

Review — c'è and ci sono

Form: c'è (singular), ci sono (plural).

Use: In cucina c'è un tavolo. In soggiorno ci sono due divani. (In the kitchen there is a table. In the living room there are two sofas.)

Review — Present tense of regular verbs

Form: regular -are, -ere, and -ire endings for io, tu, lui/lei/Lei, and noi.

Use: Mi alzo alle sette. Faccio colazione e poi vado al lavoro. (I get up at seven. I have breakfast and then I go to work.)

Review — mi piace/mi piacciono and vorrei

Form: mi piace + singular noun; mi piacciono + plural noun; vorrei + noun or infinitive.

Use: Mi piace il caffè. Mi piacciono i cornetti. Vorrei un cappuccino, per favore.

Review — Price questions, demonstratives, and comparison

Form: quanto costa? / quanto costano?; questo/questa/questi/queste; più/meno... di; preferisco.

Use: Quanto costa questa sciarpa? È più cara di quella, ma preferisco il colore.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Chiara calls Aisha on Saturday afternoon to see how her day is going, as described in the Communication Scenario above.

Chiara Ciao Aisha! Come va la giornata? CHOW ah-EE-sha! KO-meh va la jor-NA-ta? Hi Aisha! How's your day going?
Aisha Bene, grazie! Stamattina ho pulito la cucina e ho fatto la spesa vicino a casa. BEH-neh, GRA-tsee-eh! Sta-mat-TEE-na o poo-LEE-to la koo-CHEE-na eh o FAT-to la SPEH-za VEE-chee-no a KA-za. Good, thanks! This morning I cleaned the kitchen and did the shopping near home.
Chiara E per pranzo, cosa fai? Eh per PRAN-tso, KO-za FA-ee? And for lunch, what are you doing?
Aisha Pranzo con Marco al bar in piazza. Mi piace il loro panino al formaggio, e vorrei anche un succo d'arancia. PRAN-tso kon MAR-ko al bar in PYA-tsa. Mee PYA-cheh eel LO-ro pa-NEE-no al for-MAD-jo, eh vor-REH-ee AN-keh oon SOOK-ko da-RAN-cha. I'm having lunch with Marco at the café in the square. I like their cheese sandwich, and I'd also like an orange juice.
Chiara Buona idea. Dopo, vai ancora al mercato? BWO-na ee-DEH-a. DO-po, VA-ee AN-ko-ra al mer-KA-to? Good idea. Afterward, are you still going to the market?
Aisha Sì, e poi passo anche dal negozio di abbigliamento. Ho visto una sciarpa verde. Quanto costa, secondo te? See, eh po PAS-so AN-keh dal neh-GO-tsyo dee ab-beel-ya-MEN-to. O VEE-sto OO-na SHAR-pa VER-deh. KWAN-to KOS-ta, seh-KON-do teh? Yes, and then I'll also stop by the clothing shop. I saw a green scarf. How much do you think it costs?
Chiara Non lo so, ma se è più cara della rossa, prendi quella che preferisci! Non lo so, ma seh EH pyoo KA-ra DEL-la ROS-sa, PREN-dee KWEL-la keh preh-feh-REE-shee! I don't know, but if it's more expensive than the red one, take whichever one you prefer!
Aisha Hai ragione. Ti chiamo stasera e ti racconto tutto! AH-ee ra-JO-neh. Tee kya-MO sta-SEH-ra eh tee rak-KON-to TOOT-to! You're right. I'll call you tonight and tell you all about it!

Domande di comprensione — Comprehension questions:

  1. What has Aisha already done this morning?
  2. What does Aisha want to order for lunch?
  3. What does Chiara suggest about the scarf?

Speaking Practice

Pratica controllata — Controlled practice: narrate a routine day

Compito comunicativo — Communicative task: a full practical day

Reading Practice

Read the message below aloud, sounding out each Italian word slowly.

Un messaggio di Aisha — A message from Aisha
Ciao Chiara! Oggi ho pulito la casa e ho fatto la spesa. A mezzogiorno ho pranzato con Marco: un panino al formaggio e un succo d'arancia, buonissimi! Nel pomeriggio sono andata al negozio di abbigliamento. La sciarpa verde costa venticinque euro, più cara della rossa, ma la preferisco. L'ho comprata!
Hi Chiara! Today I cleaned the house and did the shopping. At noon I had lunch with Marco: a cheese sandwich and an orange juice, delicious! In the afternoon I went to the clothing shop. The green scarf costs twenty-five euros, more expensive than the red one, but I prefer it. I bought it!

The forms sono andata and l'ho comprata use the past tense, which is introduced formally in Chapter 16. Treat them here as recognition-only; you are not expected to produce past-tense forms independently yet.

Writing Workshop

Compito di scrittura — Writing task: Write eight to ten sentences describing a practical day. Include: a short description of your home or neighborhood; at least two routine activities with times; a food or drink order with a preference; and a shopping comparison with a price question, più/meno... di, and preferisco.

  1. Describe your home or neighborhood. Use c'è/ci sono and a location expression.
  2. Explain part of your routine. Use present-tense verbs and at least one time expression.
  3. Order food or drink. Use vorrei or mi piace/mi piacciono.
  4. Compare two shopping choices. Use a price question, più/meno... di, and preferisco.

Lista di controllo — Editing checklist:

  • c'è is used for one thing, ci sono for more than one.
  • Present-tense verb endings match the subject.
  • mi piace is used with singular nouns, mi piacciono with plural nouns.
  • Adjectives and demonstratives agree in gender and number.
  • Accents, apostrophes, and doubled consonants are spelled correctly.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — A practical day looks different for different people

Not everyone shops, eats, or plans a Saturday the way Aisha does. Household chores, meal times, and errands vary by region, household, income, and personal schedule across Italy and other Italian-speaking communities. Avoid treating one practical day as the practical day.

Culture in Context — Staying in touch during the day

Calling or messaging a friend partway through the day to check in, as Chiara does, is common among friends and family in many Italian-speaking contexts. The tone stays informal and friendly, using tu throughout, since Aisha and Chiara are close friends.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Confusing c'è and ci sono

Error: Saying c'è due divani instead of the correct form.

Correction: Use the plural form for more than one thing: ci sono due divani.

Common Mistake — Wrong verb ending for the subject

Error: Saying Marco pranza con Marco confusing subject endings, such as io pranza instead of io pranzo.

Correction: Match the ending to the subject: io pranzo, Marco pranza.

Common Mistake — Mixing up mi piace and mi piacciono

Error: Saying mi piace i cornetti instead of the correct form.

Correction: cornetti is plural, so use mi piacciono i cornetti.

Common Mistake — Comparison without di

Error: Saying più cara che la sciarpa rossa in this type of comparison.

Correction: When comparing two named items, use di: più cara della sciarpa rossa.
