La prima riunione

Volume II, Chapter 5: The First Meeting · Speak and Write Italian: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Word travels fast in Marco's neighborhood. Two days after Paolo's phone call, Rita has invited everyone who might be interested to her apartment to talk about the festival. It is a small, ordinary evening with coffee and too many chairs borrowed from other apartments — but a lot happens over the course of it: people arrive, ideas get proposed, someone changes their mind, and by the end there is an actual plan. Telling a story like this well means doing more than switching between two tenses once. You need to walk your listener through several connected moments in order, so the sequence is as clear as the tenses themselves. This chapter takes the interruption pattern from Chapter 4 and extends it across a longer story, adding the connecting words that hold multi-stage narration together. By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Organize a multi-stage story into a clear sequence using connectors such as prima, poi, nel frattempo, più tardi, and alla fine.
  2. Continue choosing correctly between imperfetto and passato prossimo across several connected sentences, not just one.
  3. Use prima di + infinitive to describe what happened before another action.
  4. Narrate an evening or event that unfolded in several stages, from beginning to end.
  5. Identify and correct common English-speaker errors in sequencing a longer past narrative.
  6. Understand and retell a connected account of a planning meeting with several participants and several stages.

Communication Scenario

Rita has invited Marco, Paolo, and several neighbors to her apartment to talk about the festival. People arrive at different times, ideas are proposed and reconsidered, and by the end of the evening the group has a rough plan and a date. Marco later tells his sister about the whole evening, from the first knock at the door to the last person leaving.

  1. You will read and listen to Marco narrating the full evening, stage by stage.
  2. You will then narrate a multi-stage story of your own — an event, an evening, or a meeting that unfolded in several connected moments.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — marking stages with pitch, not just words

In a longer spoken narrative, Italian speakers often mark a new stage of the story with a small rise in pitch right on the connector itself — a slight lift on poi or a un certo punto — before the pitch settles back down for the rest of the clause. This is one of the ways listeners follow a multi-stage story without getting lost, even before they process the verb tense that follows.

5.1 Rhythm of nel frattempo and a un certo punto

Both of these connectors function as short introductory phrases and are usually followed by a brief pause: Nel frattempo, || altri vicini sono arrivati. Practice saying the connector, pausing briefly, and then continuing at a natural pace.

5.2 Linking prima di to the infinitive

Prima di links directly to the following infinitive with no pause between them: prima di arrivare, prima di parlare. Keep this phrase tight and unbroken, unlike the longer pause after nel frattempo.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Language Focus — sequencing a multi-stage story

A story with several stages needs more than tense choice — it needs connectors that tell the listener where each moment sits relative to the others. Italian uses a small, reusable set of connectors for this:

ConnectorFunction
primamarks the earliest stage of the story
poimoves the story to the next stage
nel frattempomarks something happening at the same time as another stage
più tardimarks a stage happening some time after the previous one
a un certo puntomarks a turning point or unexpected moment within the story
alla finemarks the final stage or outcome

One connector per stage: a clear multi-stage story usually uses one connector to open each new stage, rather than piling up several connectors in the same sentence. This keeps the sequence easy for a listener or reader to follow.

Language Focus — prima di + infinitive

Prima di followed directly by an infinitive verb describes an action that happened before another action, without needing a separate clause or a second conjugated verb:

ItalianEnglish
Prima di arrivare, Paolo ha comprato dei dolci.Before arriving, Paolo bought some pastries.
Prima di parlare, Rita ha guardato tutti in silenzio.Before speaking, Rita looked at everyone in silence.

Notice that prima di takes the infinitive even though the subject of both actions is the same person — this is different from English, which can use "before" with a conjugated verb ("before he arrived"). Keep the subject of the prima di phrase and the subject of the main verb the same; a different-subject version belongs to a later chapter.

Language Focus — the interruption pattern across several stages

The interruption pattern from Chapter 4 still applies at each stage of a longer story: within any one moment, the imperfetto still marks the background and the passato prossimo still marks the one specific event. What changes in this chapter is that you now connect several such moments together into a full account, rather than telling only one.

You are still building this skill. Combining pronouns, relative clauses, and more advanced discourse organization continue to develop later in the volume. For now, focus on clear sequencing with the connectors above.

Everything else in this chapter — the internal formation of the imperfetto and passato prossimo, object pronouns, and reciprocal reflexive verbs — is recycled exactly as taught in Volume I and Chapters 1–4. No other new grammar system is introduced here.

Model Dialogue

Setting: The next day, Marco calls his sister Anna to tell her about the meeting at Rita's apartment.

Anna Allora, com'è andata la riunione da Rita? Al-LO-ra, ko-MEH an-DA-ta la ryoo-NYO-neh da REE-ta? So, how did the meeting at Rita's go?
Marco Bene! Prima sono arrivato io con Paolo, e Rita preparava già il caffè per tutti. BEH-neh! PREE-ma SO-no ar-ree-VA-to EE-o kon PA-o-lo, eh REE-ta pre-pa-RA-va JA eel kaf-FEH per TOOT-tee. Good! First I arrived with Paolo, and Rita was already preparing coffee for everyone.
Anna E poi? Eh PO-ee? And then?
Marco Poi, mentre aspettavamo gli altri, Paolo ha proposto una data: la prima domenica di settembre. Nel frattempo, sono arrivati anche i signori Bianchi e due studenti che abitano al terzo piano. PO-ee, MEN-treh as-pet-ta-VA-mo lyee AL-tree, PA-o-lo a pro-POS-to OO-na DA-ta: la PREE-ma do-MEH-nee-ka dee set-TEM-breh. Nel frat-TEM-po, SO-no ar-ree-VA-tee AN-keh ee seen-YO-ree Byan-kee eh DOO-eh stoo-DEN-tee keh a-BEE-ta-no al TER-tso PYA-no. Then, while we were waiting for the others, Paolo proposed a date: the first Sunday in September. Meanwhile, Mr. and Mrs. Bianchi arrived too, along with two students who live on the third floor.
Anna E tutti erano d'accordo sulla data? Eh TOOT-tee EH-ra-no dak-KOR-do SOOL-la DA-ta? And was everyone in agreement about the date?
Marco Più tardi, a un certo punto, la signora Bianchi ha cambiato idea e ha proposto ottobre invece di settembre. Pyoo TAR-dee, a oon CHER-to POON-to, la seen-YO-ra Byan-kee a kam-BYA-to ee-DEH-a eh a pro-POS-to ot-TO-breh een-VEH-cheh dee set-TEM-breh. Later, at a certain point, Mrs. Bianchi changed her mind and proposed October instead of September.
Anna E come è finita? Eh KO-meh eh fee-NEE-ta? And how did it end?
Marco Alla fine, prima di andare via, ci siamo messi d'accordo su settembre. Rita era davvero contenta. AL-la FEE-neh, PREE-ma dee an-DA-reh VEE-a, chee SYA-mo MES-see dak-KOR-do soo set-TEM-breh. REE-ta EH-ra dav-VEH-ro kon-TEN-ta. In the end, before leaving, we agreed on September. Rita was really happy.

Domande di comprensione — Comprehension questions:

  1. Who arrived first, and what was Rita doing at that moment?
  2. What happened while Marco and Paolo were waiting for the others?
  3. Find one sequencing connector and explain what stage of the story it introduces.

Un messaggio di Marco a Rita — A message from Marco to Rita
Cara signora Rita, grazie ancora per ieri sera! Prima di scriverle, ho pensato a tutta la serata. Prima sono arrivato io con Paolo, e Lei preparava già tutto per gli ospiti. Poi, mentre aspettavamo gli altri, abbiamo parlato della data. Nel frattempo sono arrivati anche i signori Bianchi e i due studenti, e la conversazione è diventata subito più vivace. Più tardi, a un certo punto, la signora Bianchi ha proposto ottobre, ma alla fine, prima di andare via, ci siamo messi tutti d'accordo su settembre. È stata una serata bellissima. A presto, Marco

Then, while we were waiting for the others, we talked about the date. Meanwhile, Mr. and Mrs. Bianchi arrived, along with the two students, and the conversation quickly became livelier. Writing Workshop

Write a connected paragraph telling the story of an event that unfolded in several stages, the way Marco's message does.

Compito di scrittura — Writing task: Write eight to ten sentences. Include: at least four sequencing connectors marking different stages, at least one prima di + infinitive phrase, at least one sentence using the interruption pattern from Chapter 4, and a natural closing.

  1. Open the story. Use prima to set the earliest stage.
  2. Move the story forward. Use poi and nel frattempo to add the next stages.
  3. Mark a turning point. Use a un certo punto for a change or unexpected moment.
  4. Close the story. Use alla fine, and include one prima di + infinitive phrase before the last action.
  5. Add a closing. End with a simple closing appropriate to your chosen audience.

Lista di controllo — Editing checklist:

  • Each new stage of the story opens with a clear sequencing connector.
  • Only one connector introduces each stage, rather than several stacked together.
  • Prima di is followed directly by an infinitive, with the same subject as the main verb.
  • The imperfetto and passato prossimo are chosen correctly within each stage.
  • Accents, apostrophes, and capitalization follow standard Italian spelling.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — planning meetings among neighbors

Informal planning meetings held in someone's home, often with coffee or a light snack, are one common way neighbors organize shared activities in many Italian cities and towns, though the format, size, and formality of such gatherings vary considerably by building, neighborhood, and region. Not every community organizes events this way.

Culture in Context — reaching agreement as a group

The expression mettersi d'accordo ("to reach an agreement together") reflects a common pattern in group planning: proposals, reconsideration, and eventual consensus, rather than a single person deciding alone. How directly disagreement is expressed, however, depends on the relationship, generation, and setting.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — using a conjugated verb after prima di

Error: Writing Prima di ha parlato, Rita ha guardato tutti.

Correction: Prima di takes the infinitive, not a conjugated verb: Prima di parlare, Rita ha guardato tutti.

Common Mistake — stacking too many connectors in one sentence

Error: Writing Poi, nel frattempo, alla fine sono arrivati i Bianchi.

Correction: Choose one connector per stage: Nel frattempo, sono arrivati i Bianchi. Save poi and alla fine for their own separate stages.

Common Mistake — losing the interruption pattern inside a longer story

Error: Writing Mentre aspettavamo gli altri, Paolo proponeva una data when describing one specific proposal.

Correction: A longer story still needs the same tense choice at each stage: the waiting is background (imperfetto), and the one specific proposal is the interrupting event (passato prossimo): Mentre aspettavamo gli altri, Paolo ha proposto una data.
