Storie e imprevisti

Volume II, Chapter 9: Stories and Unexpected Events · Speak and Write Italian: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

The festival has finally arrived. The chairs are out, the flyers are gone, the keys turned up right on schedule — and for the first hour, everything was going exactly as planned. Then, right as the music was supposed to start, something happened that nobody expected. Up to now you have used passato prossimo for completed events and imperfetto for background and habits, largely one at a time. This chapter asks you to do what real storytellers do: set a scene with imperfetto and then interrupt it with a completed event in passato prossimo — the classic shape of a story with an unexpected twist. By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use imperfetto to set a background scene — what was happening, how things looked, what people were doing.
  2. Use passato prossimo to introduce a single completed event that interrupts that background.
  3. Combine both tenses in the same sentence with mentre and quando.
  4. Sequence a short narrative using poi, allora, a un certo punto, and alla fine.
  5. Describe an unexpected problem and how it was resolved, using reason clauses to explain why it happened.
  6. Recycle combined pronouns from Chapter 8 inside a connected narrative.
  7. Identify and correct common English-speaker errors involving tense choice in narration.

Communication Scenario

It is the day of the festival. The square is full of people, the chairs are set up, the flyers did their job, and the DJ is testing the sound system. Everything seems fine — until, right before the music is supposed to start, the equipment suddenly stops working, and Marco, Paolo, and Rita have to figure out what happened and what to do next.

  1. You will read and listen to Marco, Paolo, and Rita telling the story of what happened at the festival, including the moment things went wrong.
  2. You will then narrate a story of your own about a moment when something unexpected interrupted a plan, using background description and a completed event together.

Where This Chapter Fits

It assumes full productive control of combined pronouns from Chapter 8, participle agreement from Chapters 6 and 7, and imperfetto for background description from Chapter 8's predecessor chapter in the unit sequence. Coming Into This Chapter

You can already say Il DJ preparava l'attrezzatura (background) and Il DJ ha controllato l'attrezzatura (completed event) as separate sentences, and you can say Gliel'ho ricordato ieri sera using a combined pronoun.

In This Chapter

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — narrative intonation and contrastive emphasis

A story with an unexpected turn uses a distinctive intonation shift: the voice stays relatively level and unhurried during background description (imperfetto), then drops slightly and lands with more weight on the verb when the interrupting event arrives (passato prossimo): Tutto andava bene... a un certo punto, la corrente è andata via. Practice pausing briefly before a un certo punto to signal the turn in the story.

9.1 Stress in common irregular participles

Several verbs used in this chapter's narration have irregular past participles whose stress can trip up English-speaking learners: accorto (from accorgersi), successo (from succedere), and risolto (from risolvere). Stress falls on the syllable before the final two letters in each: ac-COR-to, suc-CES-so, ri-SOL-to.

9.2 Rhythm of reflexive verbs in the past

Si è spaventata and ci siamo preoccupati keep the reflexive pronoun light and unstressed, exactly as with any reflexive verb, with the main stress falling on the participle: si è spa-ven-TA-ta, not on the pronoun itself.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Language Focus — background versus interruption

When you tell a story, you often need two different kinds of past information at once: what was going on (the background) and what happened at a specific moment (the event). Italian marks this distinction with two different tenses rather than one:

FunctionTenseExample
Ongoing background sceneimperfettoIl DJ preparava l'attrezzatura.
Single completed event that interrupts itpassato prossimoLa corrente è andata via.
Both together in one sentenceimperfetto + passato prossimoMentre il DJ preparava l'attrezzatura, la corrente è andata via.

Think of the imperfetto clause as the open, ongoing scene and the passato prossimo clause as the single moment that cuts into it. Mentre ("while") typically introduces the background clause; the event clause can come before or after it.

Language Focus — quando with both tenses

Quando ("when") can introduce either tense, and the choice changes the meaning:

ItalianMeaning
Quando è andata via la corrente, tutti hanno gridato.When the power went out (single event), everyone shouted (single event).
Quando è andata via la corrente, Rita parlava con il DJ.When the power went out (single event), Rita was talking with the DJ (background, in progress).

Quando does not by itself tell you which tense to use in the clause that follows — the meaning you intend does. Ask yourself: is this an ongoing background scene, or a second completed event?

Language Focus — sequencing the turn in a story

Use these connectors to move a narrative forward and to mark the moment things change:

ItalianFunctionExample
a un certo puntomarks the sudden turnA un certo punto, la musica si è fermata.
alloramarks the reaction or responseAllora Marco ha controllato i cavi.
per fortunamarks a lucky resolutionPer fortuna, il problema si è risolto in pochi minuti.
alla finemarks the outcomeAlla fine, la festa è continuata senza problemi.

These connectors work together with tense choice — the tense tells you what kind of information you are giving, and the connector tells you where that information sits in the story's shape.

Everything else in this chapter — individual uses of passato prossimo and imperfetto, combined pronouns, participle agreement, and reason clauses — is recycled exactly as taught in Chapters 5–8 and Volume I. No other new grammar system is introduced here.

Model Dialogue

Setting: The day after the festival, Marco, Paolo, and Rita meet for coffee and tell a friend, Sara, what happened.

Sara Allora, com'è andata la festa? Ho sentito che c'è stato un problema. al-LO-ra, ko-MEH an-DA-ta la FES-ta? O sen-TEE-to keh cheh STA-to oon pro-BLEH-ma. So, how did the festival go? I heard there was a problem.
Rita Sì! Tutto andava benissimo. La gente arrivava, le sedie erano quasi tutte occupate, e il DJ preparava l'attrezzatura. Poi, mentre lui controllava i cavi, a un certo punto la corrente è andata via. See! TOOT-to an-DA-va beh-NEES-see-mo. La JEN-teh ar-ree-VA-va, leh SEH-dyeh EH-ra-no KWA-zee TOOT-teh ok-koo-PA-teh, eh eel dee-JEI preh-pa-RA-va lat-tret-tsa-TOO-ra. Po, MEN-treh loo-ee kon-tro-LA-va ee KA-vee, a oon CHER-to POON-to la kor-REN-teh eh an-DA-ta VEE-a. Yes! Everything was going great. People were arriving, the chairs were almost all taken, and the DJ was setting up the equipment. Then, while he was checking the cables, all of a sudden the power went out.
Sara Oddio! E come avete risolto? Od-DEE-o! Eh KO-meh a-VEH-teh ree-SOL-to? Oh no! And how did you solve it?
Marco Mentre Rita si preoccupava per la musica, io mi sono accorto che il problema veniva da un cavo rotto. Allora l'ho sostituito subito con uno di riserva. MEN-treh REE-ta see preh-ok-koo-PA-va per la MOO-zee-ka, EE-o mee SO-no ak-KOR-to keh eel pro-BLEH-ma veh-NEE-va da oon KA-vo ROT-to. Al-LO-ra lo SO-sti-too-EE-to soo-BEE-to kon OO-no dee ree-SER-va. While Rita was worrying about the music, I noticed that the problem came from a broken cable. So I replaced it right away with a spare one.
Paolo Nel frattempo io ho spiegato alla gente cosa succedeva, così nessuno si è spaventato troppo. Nel frat-TEM-po EE-o o spee-eh-GA-to AL-la JEN-teh KO-za soo-cheh-DEH-va, ko-ZEE nes-SOO-no see eh spa-ven-TA-to TROP-po. In the meantime I explained to people what was happening, so nobody got too scared.
Rita Per fortuna, il problema si è risolto in cinque minuti. Quando la musica è ripartita, tutti hanno applaudito. Per for-TOO-na, eel pro-BLEH-ma see eh ree-SOL-to een CHEEN-kweh mee-NOO-tee. KWAN-do la MOO-zee-ka eh ree-par-TEE-ta, TOOT-tee AN-no ap-plow-DEE-to. Luckily, the problem was solved in five minutes. When the music started again, everyone applauded.
Sara Che avventura! E le chiavi del magazzino? Il custode ve le ha date in tempo? Keh av-ven-TOO-ra! Eh leh KYA-vee del ma-ga-tsee-NO? Eel koo-STO-deh veh leh a DA-teh een TEM-po? What an adventure! And the storage-room keys? Did the caretaker give them to you all in time?
Paolo Sì, ce le ha date la mattina, come aveva promesso. L'unico imprevisto è stato quello dell'elettricità. See, cheh leh a DA-teh la mat-TEE-na, KO-meh a-VEH-va pro-MES-so. Loo-NEE-ko eem-preh-VEES-to eh STA-to KWEL-lo del-leh-let-tree-chee-TA. Yes, he gave them to us in the morning, just like he'd promised. The only unexpected thing was the electricity.
Rita Alla fine, però, è stata una festa bellissima. Tutti si sono divertiti, e nessuno se n'è nemmeno accorto della corrente! AL-la FEE-neh, peh-RO, eh STA-ta OO-na FES-ta bel-LEES-see-ma. TOOT-tee see SO-no dee-ver-TEE-tee, eh nes-SOO-no seh neh eh neh-MEH-no ak-KOR-to DEL-la kor-REN-teh! In the end, though, it was a wonderful festival. Everyone had a great time, and hardly anyone even noticed the power problem!

Domande di comprensione — Comprehension questions:

  1. What was happening right before the power went out?
  2. How did Marco figure out and solve the problem?
  3. Find one sentence that combines imperfetto and passato prossimo and explain which clause is the background and which is the event.

Speaking Practice

Pratica controllata — Controlled practice: setting the scene

With a partner, take turns describing an ordinary background scene using imperfetto (weather, what people were doing, how a place looked), then add one interrupting event in passato prossimo introduced by a un certo punto.

Compito comunicativo — Communicative task: tell the story of an unexpected event

In pairs or small groups, tell the story of a time something unexpected interrupted a plan — a trip, an event, a normal day. Set the scene with background description, introduce the interruption clearly, and explain how it was resolved. Your partner should ask at least one follow-up question using quando or mentre.

Pratica da solo — Solo alternative

Record yourself narrating the festival story from this chapter in your own words, checking that you use imperfetto for background and passato prossimo for the interrupting event, and that any combined pronoun you use is placed and agreed correctly.

Writing Workshop

Write a connected short narrative about a moment when something unexpected interrupted a plan, the way Rita's message does.

Compito di scrittura — Writing task: Write eight to ten sentences. Include: at least two uses of imperfetto for background; at least two uses of passato prossimo for completed events; at least one sentence combining both tenses with mentre or quando; at least one narrative connector (a un certo punto, allora, per fortuna, or alla fine); and at least one combined pronoun recycled from Chapter 8.

  1. Set the scene. Describe what was happening, using imperfetto.
  2. Introduce the unexpected event. Use a un certo punto and passato prossimo to interrupt the background.
  3. Explain the reaction. Say what people did in response, using allora and, where natural, a combined pronoun.
  4. Explain the resolution. Use per fortuna or a reason clause to say how the problem was solved.
  5. Add a closing. Use alla fine to wrap up how things turned out.

Lista di controllo — Editing checklist:

  • Background information uses imperfetto; completed events use passato prossimo.
  • At least one sentence combines both tenses with mentre or quando.
  • Narrative connectors mark the turn, the reaction, and the outcome of the story.
  • Any combined pronoun is placed correctly and, in the passato prossimo, the participle agrees with the direct object pronoun inside it.
  • Accents, apostrophes, and capitalization follow standard Italian spelling.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — telling the story afterward

After an informal community event, it is common for organizers and friends to retell what happened, including minor mishaps, in a light and often humorous way, as a way of processing the experience together rather than assigning blame. How much detail is shared, and with whom, can depend on the closeness of the group and local conversational habits.

Culture in Context — handling small crises calmly

When something goes wrong at a public gathering, calmly explaining the situation to those present is a common way of preventing panic and maintaining trust, though the specific approach — a quick announcement, a joke, or simply continuing as if nothing happened — can vary by setting, generation, and the seriousness of the problem.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — using passato prossimo for ongoing background

Error: Writing Mentre il DJ ha controllato i cavi, la corrente andava via, reversing which clause is background and which is the event.

Correction: The ongoing background takes imperfetto and the single interrupting event takes passato prossimo: Mentre il DJ controllava i cavi, la corrente è andata via.

Common Mistake — forgetting the auxiliary with reflexive or essere verbs in a fast-moving narrative

Error: Writing Quando la musica è fermata, dropping the reflexive pronoun that the verb fermarsi requires.

Correction: Reflexive verbs keep their reflexive pronoun even in fast narration: Quando la musica si è fermata.

Common Mistake — treating every past sentence in a story the same way

Error: Using only passato prossimo throughout an entire narrative, even for scene-setting information.

Correction: Ask whether each piece of information is ongoing background or a single completed event before choosing the tense, rather than defaulting to one tense for the whole story.

Answer Key

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. The DJ was checking the cables and preparing the equipment while people were arriving and settling into their seats.
  2. Marco noticed the broken cable and replaced it with a spare one; the problem was solved in about five minutes.
  3. Sample: "Mentre il DJ controllava i cavi, la corrente è andata via" — the mentre clause with imperfetto is the background; "la corrente è andata via" with passato prossimo is the interrupting event.

Guided Practice

Esercizio 2: Sample answers: (1) Mentre il DJ preparava l'attrezzatura, la corrente è andata via. (2) Mentre Rita si preoccupava per la musica, Marco ha trovato il problema. (3) La gente ballava quando la musica si è fermata.
