Problemi sociali e soluzioni

Volume III, Chapter 11: Social Problems and Solutions · Speak and Write Italian: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Overview and Objectives

The neighborhood bulletin closed Unit II with Elena, Marco, and Chiara reporting on their community from the outside — summarizing, describing, quoting. Chapter 11 asks them to step further in: instead of only reporting what the neighborhood is like, they must say what should change and why. A local notice about reduced hours at the community library sparks a real disagreement among the three friends, and each has to build an actual argument rather than simply stating a preference. This chapter opens Unit III, Argomentare e mediare, by developing the congiuntivo presente further, adding concession with anche se, and introducing the connectors needed to organize a supported position. By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe a social problem clearly, using vocabulary for community challenges and their effects.
  2. Build a structured argument that states a claim, gives evidence, and reaches a conclusion.
  3. Use the congiuntivo presente productively after opinion, doubt, necessity, and evaluation expressions.
  4. Introduce a concession with anche se without abandoning your main point.
  5. Propose a solution and respond to an objection or counterpoint.
  6. Write a problem-solution paragraph organized with argument connectors.

Communication Scenario

A notice has gone up outside the community library: starting next month, it will close two hours earlier on weekdays because of budget cuts. Chiara is upset — she uses the library every afternoon after class. Marco thinks the change is probably unavoidable, "anche se non mi piace." Elena wants to write a short proposal to the library committee before the three of them simply accept the notice. Their conversation becomes the chapter's first real argument: not a disagreement between friends, but three people building a case about a shared problem.

  1. You will read and listen to Elena, Marco, and Chiara discuss the library's reduced hours, each supporting a position with reasons and examples.
  2. You will then complete your own task: reading a short opinion article about a local problem, and writing a problem-solution paragraph of your own.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — emphasis and contrast in argument

Building an argument means giving certain words more weight than others. Compare a flat reading of Non sono d'accordo. with a version that stresses non slightly and lets the voice fall firmly on d'accordo. Concession works the same way: in Anche se capisco il problema, non credo che sia la soluzione giusta., the voice should rise slightly through the anche se clause and then fall with more weight on non credo che sia la soluzione giusta, signaling that the main point is still to come.

11.1 Subordinate clauses and pausing

Longer sentences with che clauses after opinion or necessity expressions need a small, natural pause before the subordinate clause, not a dramatic stop: Penso che [pause] la biblioteca debba restare aperta di più. Practice this pausing with the model dialogue below.

11.2 Stress in congiuntivo forms

Review stress in high-frequency congiuntivo presente forms: che io PARLI, che tu PARLI, che lui/lei PARLI, che noi parlIAMO, che voi parlIATE, che loro PARLino. Many learners misplace stress in the noi and loro forms; read these aloud slowly before the guided practice.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Language Focus — congiuntivo presente after a wider set of triggers

Chapter 3 introduced the congiuntivo presente after penso che, credo che, and a few similar frames. This chapter develops the same mood into a wider set of triggers used in argument: doubt (dubito che, non sono sicuro/a che), necessity (bisogna che, è necessario che), evaluation (è giusto/ingiusto che, è un peccato che), and proposal (propongo che, suggerisco che). The verb form itself does not change; what changes is the range of situations in which you now use it productively. Example: Dubito che i tagli risolvano davvero il problema del bilancio. (I doubt that the cuts really solve the budget problem.)

Language Building Blocks — opinion/doubt/necessity + che + congiuntivo
PatternExample
doubtDubito che il comitato ascolti i cittadini.

Language Focus — concession with anche se

Anche se introduces a concession — a point you accept — before stating your real position. Unlike the congiuntivo triggers above, anche se is followed by the indicative, not the subjunctive, because it introduces an accepted fact rather than an opinion or doubt: Anche se capisco le difficoltà del comune, non sono d'accordo con la decisione. (Even though I understand the town's difficulties, I don't agree with the decision.) You may encounter benché and sebbene in reading; these take the congiuntivo and remain receptive at this stage.

Language Focus — organizing an argument

An organized argument in Italian typically states a claim, offers evidence with per esempio, acknowledges a counterpoint with tuttavia or però, and may weigh two sides with da un lato… dall'altro. inoltre adds a further point without contrast. These connectors do the organizational work that English speakers often do only with sentence order.

Review from earlier chapters remains active throughout: cause-result connectors (Chapter 4), relative clauses (Chapter 8), and reported speech (Chapter 9) all recur naturally in argument and should not be treated as new material here.

Model Dialogue: Un problema in comune

Setting: Elena, Marco, and Chiara stand in front of the library notice about reduced hours, each taking a slightly different position.

Chiara Non è giusto che chiudano la biblioteca due ore prima! Io ci studio tutti i pomeriggi. non EH JOO-sto keh kyoo-DA-no la bee-blyo-TEH-ka DOO-eh O-reh PREE-ma! EE-o chee STOO-dyo TOOT-tee ee po-meh-REEJ-jee. It's not fair that they're closing the library two hours early! I study there every afternoon.
Marco Capisco, ma bisogna che il comune tagli qualcosa dal bilancio. Anche se non mi piace, forse non c'è altra scelta. ka-PEE-sko, ma bee-ZOH-nya keh eel ko-MOO-neh TAHL-yee kwal-KO-za dal bee-LAN-cho. AN-keh seh non mee PYA-cheh, FOR-seh non CHEH AL-tra SHEL-ta. I understand, but the town has to cut something from the budget. Even though I don't like it, maybe there's no other choice.
Elena Dubito che chiudere la biblioteca sia il modo migliore di risparmiare. Per esempio, potrebbero ridurre le luci accese la sera invece di chiudere prima. DOO-bee-to keh kyoo-DEH-reh la bee-blyo-TEH-ka SEE-a eel MO-do meel-YO-reh dee ree-spar-MYA-reh. pehr eh-ZEHM-pyo, po-TREB-beh-ro ree-DOOR-reh leh LOO-chee AH-cheh-zeh la SEH-ra in-VEH-cheh dee kyoo-DEH-reh PREE-ma. I doubt that closing the library is the best way to save money. For example, they could reduce the lights left on in the evening instead of closing earlier.
Chiara Da un lato capisco Marco, dall'altro l'idea di Elena mi sembra migliore. Propongo che scriviamo una lettera al comitato prima che decidano definitivamente. da oon LA-to ka-PEE-sko MAR-ko, dal-LAL-tro LEE-deh-a dee EH-leh-na mee SEHM-bra meel-YO-reh. pro-PON-go keh skree-VYA-mo oo-na LET-teh-ra al ko-mee-TA-to PREE-ma keh deh-CHEE-da-no deh-fee-nee-tee-va-MEHN-teh. On one hand I understand Marco, on the other Elena's idea seems better to me. I propose that we write a letter to the committee before they decide definitively.
Marco Va bene, però bisogna che la lettera sia breve e concreta, non solo una lamentela. va BEH-neh, peh-ROH bee-ZOH-nya keh la LET-teh-ra SEE-a BREH-veh eh kon-KREH-ta, non SO-lo OO-na la-mehn-TEH-la. Fine, but the letter needs to be short and concrete, not just a complaint.
Elena Sono d'accordo. Inoltre, potremmo raccogliere qualche firma tra gli altri studenti. SO-no dak-KOR-do. een-OL-treh, po-TREM-mo rak-kol-YEH-reh KWAL-keh FEER-ma tra lyee AL-tree stoo-DEHN-tee. I agree. Furthermore, we could collect a few signatures among the other students.

Domande di comprensione — Comprehension questions:

  1. What is the social problem the three friends are discussing?
  2. What alternative solution does Elena propose instead of closing the library early?
  3. What two things does the group decide to do at the end of the conversation?

Listening Practice

Ask an instructor, study partner, or fluent Italian speaker to read the passage below aloud, at a natural pace, twice.

If no speakers are available, this is a Listening Script for Independent Use. Read it aloud yourself, slowly at first, then at a more natural pace. Any spoken model should be verified by a fluent Italian speaker before being treated as a pronunciation standard.

Una proposta al comitato — A proposal to the committee:
Molti residenti pensano che ridurre l'orario della biblioteca sia una decisione affrettata. Bisogna che il comune consideri altre soluzioni prima di tagliare un servizio così importante per gli studenti e per gli anziani del quartiere. Per esempio, si potrebbero ridurre altre spese, come l'illuminazione serale di alcuni uffici quasi sempre vuoti. Anche se capiamo che il bilancio comunale è limitato, dubitiamo che questa sia la soluzione più giusta. Per questo motivo, un gruppo di cittadini propone che il comitato organizzi un incontro pubblico prima di decidere definitivamente.

Reading Practice

Read the short opinion article below, adapted for this level.

Lettera al giornale del quartiere — Letter to the neighborhood newspaper
Secondo i dati raccolti dal comitato di quartiere, il numero di studenti che usa la biblioteca comunale nel pomeriggio è aumentato negli ultimi due anni. A fronte di questo aumento, la decisione di ridurre l'orario serale sorprende molti residenti. È vero che il bilancio comunale è limitato, e capiamo che il comune debba fare delle scelte difficili. Tuttavia, dubitiamo che tagliare proprio questo servizio sia la scelta più giusta, soprattutto per chi non ha uno spazio tranquillo per studiare a casa. Un sondaggio recente mostra che quasi l'ottanta per cento degli studenti intervistati considera la biblioteca essenziale per i propri studi. Per questo motivo, proponiamo che il comitato organizzi un incontro pubblico prima di rendere definitiva questa decisione, così che i residenti possano proporre alternative concrete.
According to data gathered by the neighborhood committee, the number of students using the community library in the afternoon has increased over the past two years. Given this increase, the decision to reduce evening hours surprises many residents. It's true that the town budget is limited, and we understand that the town must make difficult choices. However, we doubt that cutting exactly this service is the fairest choice, especially for those who don't have a quiet space to study at home. A recent survey shows that almost eighty percent of students interviewed consider the library essential to their studies. For this reason, we propose that the committee organize a public meeting before making this decision final, so that residents can propose concrete alternatives.

Domande di comprensione — Comprehension questions:

  1. What trend does the committee's data show about library use?
  2. What concession does the letter make before stating its main disagreement?
  3. What does the letter propose the committee do, and why?

Writing Workshop

Write your own problem-solution paragraph about a real or invented local issue.

Compito di scrittura — Writing task: Write an eight- to ten-sentence paragraph that describes a social or community problem, proposes a solution, and supports your position with at least one piece of evidence and one concession.

  1. State the problem. Describe it clearly in one or two sentences.
  2. Give evidence. Use per esempio to support your claim.
  3. Make a concession. Use anche se to acknowledge a fair counterpoint.
  4. Propose a solution. Use propongo che or suggerisco che with the congiuntivo presente.
  5. Add a further point. Use inoltre to strengthen your argument.
  6. Conclude. Restate your position in one sentence.

Lista di controllo — Editing checklist:

  • The congiuntivo presente is used correctly after at least two different trigger types.
  • At least one concession with anche se appears, followed by the indicative.
  • At least two argument connectors organize the paragraph.
  • The claim, evidence, and conclusion are easy to identify.
  • Accents, apostrophes, and punctuation are checked throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — expressive but organized argument

Public disagreement in Italian conversation can be animated, with raised voices, interruption, and visible emotion, without this signaling a breakdown in the discussion. Learners should distinguish passionate delivery from a lack of structure: even a heated exchange between neighbors often still follows a recognizable claim-evidence-conclusion pattern underneath its energy.

Culture in Context — writing to a local committee

A letter to a neighborhood committee or community group is typically direct but respectful: it names the problem plainly, avoids personal attacks on committee members, and proposes a next step rather than only a complaint. This differs from a fully bureaucratic or legal register, which Volume III does not require.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — congiuntivo after anche se

Error: Anche se sia difficile, dobbiamo provare.

Correction: Anche se è difficile, dobbiamo provare. Anche se takes the indicative because it introduces an accepted fact, not a doubt or opinion.

Common Mistake — indicative after a doubt or necessity trigger

Error: Dubito che la soluzione è giusta.

Correction: Dubito che la soluzione sia giusta. Doubt, necessity, evaluation, and proposal expressions require the congiuntivo in the dependent clause.

Common Mistake — argument as a list of unconnected sentences

Error: Stringing claims together with no connectors: La biblioteca è importante. Il bilancio è limitato. Dobbiamo scrivere una lettera.

Correction: Link the ideas: La biblioteca è importante; tuttavia, il bilancio è limitato. Per questo motivo, proponiamo di scrivere una lettera.
