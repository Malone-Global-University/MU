Cause, conseguenze e ipotesi reali

Volume III, Chapter 4: Causes, Consequences, and Real Conditions · Speak and Write Italian: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Overview and Objectives

The courtyard debate has moved out of casual conversation and into paperwork: the building administrator has circulated a detailed cost estimate, and the residents now need to explain, in writing and out loud, why costs are what they are, what will happen if the work is delayed, and what will happen if it goes ahead. Opinions alone are no longer enough — Elena, Marco, and Chiara need language for cause and consequence, and for describing realistic conditions and their likely results. This chapter shifts from stance to explanation, developing connectors such as dato che, siccome, perciò, quindi, and di conseguenza, alongside the real conditional pattern with se plus the indicative. By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Explain the cause of a situation and its likely consequence.
  2. Connect facts, reasons, examples, and conclusions in an explanatory paragraph.
  3. Use cause-result connectors such as dato che, siccome, perciò, quindi, and di conseguenza.
  4. Form and use the real conditional with se plus the present indicative to describe likely outcomes.
  5. Use the conditional present formulaically to soften a suggestion or request.
  6. Read and interpret a public notice or explanatory text about a practical problem.

Communication Scenario

The building administrator has posted a detailed cost estimate for the courtyard renovation, along with a note explaining what will happen if the residents approve the work now versus if they wait. Elena, Marco, and Chiara meet again to make sense of it before the next building meeting.

  1. You will read and listen to Elena, Marco, and Chiara working through the cost estimate, explaining causes, consequences, and likely outcomes.
  2. You will then complete a task in which you explain a real situation of your own — a problem, a plan, or a decision — using cause, consequence, and at least one real conditional statement.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — intonation across longer subordinate clauses

When a cause-result sentence opens with a subordinate clause, Italian intonation typically rises slightly through the clause and only falls once the main clause begins: Dato che i costi sono alti, è meglio aspettare. Practice a brief pause — not a full stop — right where the comma falls, and let your pitch drop noticeably on perciò, quindi, or di conseguenza when they introduce the consequence, since these words are doing important logical work in the sentence.

4.1 Introduced: stress in connectors and discourse markers

Cause-result connectors carry their own natural stress: dato che, siccome, perciò, quindi, di conseguenza. Say each connector on its own several times before using it in a full sentence, so the stress pattern becomes automatic rather than something you have to calculate mid-sentence.

4.2 Review: double consonants in explanatory vocabulary

Several words in this chapter's vocabulary depend on double consonants for their correct spelling and pronunciation, including peggiorare and conseguenza. Keep consonant length clear and deliberate, especially when reading an explanatory paragraph aloud at a natural pace.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Language Focus — cause-result connectors

Dato che and siccome both introduce a cause that the speaker treats as already known or established, and both typically open the sentence: Dato che il preventivo è arrivato, possiamo finalmente decidere. Perciò, quindi, and di conseguenza introduce the consequence that follows from a cause already stated, and normally appear after it: I costi sono alti; di conseguenza, alcuni residenti vogliono aspettare. All five connectors are indicative connectors: they do not trigger the subjunctive, because they present the cause or consequence as a stated fact rather than an opinion or evaluation.

Language Focus — the real conditional with se

Italian uses se plus the present indicative in both clauses to describe a real, likely condition and its expected result: Se aspettiamo troppo, il cortile peggiora. Se i residenti approvano il preventivo, i lavori iniziano a maggio. This pattern describes situations the speaker treats as genuinely possible or likely, not as remote, contrary-to-fact, or purely hypothetical. The se clause and the main clause can appear in either order; when the se clause comes first, a comma usually separates it from the main clause.

Language Focus — conditional present as a polite softener

You have already used forms like vorrei and potrei formulaically for polite requests. This chapter reuses that same formulaic conditional to soften a suggestion inside an explanation: Direi che è meglio aspettare; Suggerirei di parlare prima con l'amministratore. This chapter does not introduce the full conditional paradigm or its hypothetical uses; direi and suggerirei are treated as ready-made softening formulas, not as a new productive verb system.

This chapter does not introduce the hypothetical conditional pattern se plus congiuntivo imperfetto plus condizionale as productive material. If an authentic text contains it, treat it as a recognition-only item. That pattern is developed later in the volume.

Model Dialogue

Setting: A few days later, in the courtyard. The administrator has posted the cost estimate. Elena, Marco, and Chiara meet to look at it together before the next meeting.

Elena Avete visto il preventivo? Dato che i costi sono più alti del previsto, capisco perché alcuni residenti esitano. A-VEH-teh VEE-sto eel preh-ven-TEE-vo? DA-to keh ee KO-stee SO-no PYOO AL-tee del preh-VEE-sto, ka-PEE-sko per-KEH AL-koo-nee reh-zee-DEN-tee eh-ZEE-ta-no. Did you see the cost estimate? Given that the costs are higher than expected, I understand why some residents are hesitating.
Marco Siccome l'amministratore ha spiegato tutto nel dettaglio, però mi sembra più chiaro adesso. Se approviamo il preventivo entro marzo, i lavori iniziano a maggio. Seek-KO-meh la-mmee-nee-stra-TO-reh a spee-eh-GA-to TOOT-to nel det-TA-lyo, peh-RO mee SEM-bra PYOO KYA-ro a-DES-so. Seh a-pro-VYA-mo eel preh-ven-TEE-vo en-TRO MAR-tso, ee la-VO-ree ee-NEE-tsya-no a MA-jyo. Since the administrator explained everything in detail, though, it seems clearer to me now. If we approve the estimate by March, the work starts in May.
Chiara E se rimandiamo la decisione, cosa succede? L'illuminazione peggiora ogni mese; quindi, se aspettiamo troppo, il problema diventa più grave e forse più costoso. Eh seh ree-man-DYA-mo la deh-chee-ZYO-neh, KO-za soo-CHEH-deh? Lil-loo-mee-na-TSYO-neh peh-JO-ra O-nyee MEH-zeh; KWEEN-dee, seh as-peh-TYA-mo TROP-po, eel pro-BLEH-ma dee-VEN-ta PYOO GRA-veh eh FOR-seh PYOO ko-STO-zo. And if we postpone the decision, what happens? The lighting gets worse every month; so, if we wait too long, the problem becomes more serious and maybe more expensive.
Elena Hai ragione. Direi che è meglio non aspettare troppo, dato che il rischio a lungo termine sembra più alto del costo immediato. AH-ee ra-JOH-neh. Dee-REH-ee keh EH MEH-lyo non as-peh-TA-reh TROP-po, DA-to keh eel REE-skyo a loon-go TEHR-mee-neh SEM-bra PYOO AL-to del KO-sto eem-meh-DYA-to. You're right. I would say it's better not to wait too long, given that the long-term risk seems higher than the immediate cost.
Marco Suggerirei di scrivere una nota per la riunione, così tutti capiscono le cause dei costi e le conseguenze di un ritardo. Sood-jeh-REE-ray dee skree-VEH-reh OO-na NO-ta per la ree-oo-NYO-neh, ko-ZEE TOOT-tee ka-PEE-sko-no leh KOW-zeh day KO-stee eh leh kon-seh-GWEN-tseh dee oon ree-TAR-do. I would suggest writing a note for the meeting, so that everyone understands the causes of the costs and the consequences of a delay.
Chiara Buona idea. Se scriviamo tutto con chiarezza, forse anche i residenti più dubbiosi cambiano idea. BWO-na ee-DEH-a. Seh skree-VYA-mo TOOT-to kon kya-RET-tsa, FOR-seh AN-keh ee reh-zee-DEN-tee PYOO doob-BYO-zee KAM-byan-no ee-DEH-a. Good idea. If we write everything clearly, maybe even the more doubtful residents will change their minds.

Domande di comprensione — Comprehension questions:

  1. According to Marco, what happens if the residents approve the estimate by March?
  2. What consequence does Chiara predict if the decision is postponed?
  3. What do Elena and Marco decide to do before the next meeting, and why?

Reading Practice

Read the short notice below, posted by the building administrator alongside the cost estimate.

Avviso dell'amministratore — Notice from the administrator
Gentili condomini, come sapete, il cortile ha bisogno di lavori urgenti, soprattutto per l'illuminazione. Dato che i costi sono aumentati rispetto alle previsioni iniziali, capisco che alcuni di voi abbiano dei dubbi. Vi ricordo però che, se il preventivo viene approvato entro la fine di marzo, i lavori possono iniziare a maggio e concludersi prima dell'estate. Se invece la decisione viene rimandata, i costi rischiano di aumentare ulteriormente il prossimo anno, e il problema della sicurezza continuerà a peggiorare. Vi invito quindi a leggere attentamente il preventivo e a portare le vostre domande alla prossima riunione.
Dear residents, as you know, the courtyard needs urgent work, especially for the lighting. Given that costs have increased compared to the initial projections, I understand that some of you have doubts. I would remind you, however, that if the estimate is approved by the end of March, the work can start in May and finish before summer. If the decision is postponed instead, costs risk increasing further next year, and the safety problem will continue to get worse. I therefore invite you to read the estimate carefully and bring your questions to the next meeting.

Writing Workshop

Write a cause-result explanation about a plan, problem, or decision of your choice.

Compito di scrittura — Writing task: Write eight to ten sentences explaining a real situation. Include: an opening statement of the problem or situation, at least one cause-result connector (dato che, siccome, perciò, quindi, or di conseguenza), at least one real conditional sentence with se describing a likely outcome, and a closing recommendation, optionally softened with direi che or suggerirei di.

  1. State the situation. Briefly describe the problem, plan, or decision.
  2. Explain the cause. Use dato che or siccome to introduce a known cause.
  3. State the consequence. Use perciò, quindi, or di conseguenza.
  4. Describe a likely outcome. Use se plus the present indicative in both clauses.
  5. Close with a recommendation. Optionally soften it with direi che or suggerirei di.

Lista di controllo — Editing checklist:

  • At least one cause-result connector is used correctly and in a natural position in the sentence.
  • At least one real conditional sentence with se plus the present indicative appears.
  • The conditional present, if used, is limited to a softening formula such as direi che or suggerirei di.
  • Commas are placed correctly around fronted subordinate clauses.
  • Double consonants in words such as peggiorare and conseguenza are checked.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — explaining costs and responsibility

In Italian institutional and building-related communication, causes and responsibilities are often stated carefully and somewhat indirectly, especially in written notices from an administrator or public office. Learners should not expect every notice to state blame or responsibility bluntly; a cause is often introduced as background information rather than as an accusation.

Culture in Context — the building administrator

L'amministratore di condominio manages shared building matters, including cost estimates, notices, and meeting agendas, in many Italian apartment buildings. Communication with the administrator typically uses a more formal, document-based register than everyday conversation among neighbors, even when discussing the same underlying issue.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — using the future or subjunctive after real se

Error: Writing Se aspetteremo troppo, il problema peggiorerà or Se aspettiamo troppo, il problema peggiori.

Correction: Se aspettiamo troppo, il problema peggiora uses the present indicative in both clauses, because this chapter's real conditional pattern describes a likely outcome as a stated fact, not as a future promise or an opinion.

Common Mistake — placing the connector in the wrong position

Error: Writing I costi sono alti dato che to try to introduce a consequence.

Correction: Dato che and siccome introduce a cause and normally open the sentence, while perciò, quindi, and di conseguenza introduce the consequence and normally follow it: Dato che i costi sono alti, alcuni residenti esitano.

Common Mistake — missing the comma after a fronted se clause

Error: Writing Se aspettiamo troppo il problema peggiora without a comma.

Correction: Se aspettiamo troppo, il problema peggiora requires a comma after the fronted subordinate clause, before the main clause begins.
