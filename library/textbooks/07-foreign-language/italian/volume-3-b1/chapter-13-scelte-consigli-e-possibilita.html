Scelte, consigli e possibilità

Volume III, Chapter 13: Choices, Advice, and Possibility · Speak and Write Italian: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Overview and Objectives

The archive called Chiara back: the position is hers if she wants it, but the hours overlap with a seminar she needs for her degree. Marco has been offered an extension of his internship, which would mean postponing a trip he had already planned with Elena. Elena, watching both of her friends weigh a decision, finds herself thinking through a choice of her own about a part-time tutoring job. Chapter 13 moves from describing what has already happened toward weighing what could happen: comparing options, giving advice with appropriate nuance, and imagining outcomes that have not occurred yet. By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Compare two or more options and justify a preference.
  2. Give advice with appropriate softness or directness depending on the relationship.
  3. Use the condizionale presente productively beyond polite requests, for advice, possibility, and soft assertion.
  4. Recognize and produce, in controlled high-frequency frames, the hypothetical pattern se + congiuntivo imperfetto + condizionale.
  5. Discuss advantages and disadvantages using comparative structures.
  6. Review clitic pronouns in the context of advice-giving.

Communication Scenario

Chiara got the call: the library wants her, starting in two weeks, three afternoons a week — exactly when her required seminar meets. She could ask the archive for a different schedule, or she could ask her advisor about switching seminar sections, but she is not sure which request is more reasonable. Marco's supervisor has asked him to stay on through August, which is generous and useful for his degree, but it would mean missing the trip he and Elena planned back in Chapter 1. Elena, half-listening to both problems, starts wondering aloud whether she should take a tutoring job herself, if she can find one that does not conflict with her thesis work. Nobody in the group has an easy answer, but between the three of them, they are good at thinking a problem through out loud.

  1. You will read and listen to Elena, Marco, and Chiara compare options and give each other advice.
  2. You will then complete your own task: comparing two options in your own life and writing a short piece of advice for someone facing a similar choice.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 13 continues Unit III, Argomentare e mediare, carrying the professional and academic register from Chapter 12 into decision-making. It does not introduce a new mood from scratch: the condizionale presente has been available since Volume II for polite requests and was consolidated for professional softening in Chapter 12. Here it is developed further, for advice, possibility, and soft assertion about hypothetical outcomes. A single controlled hypothetical frame — se plus congiuntivo imperfetto plus condizionale — is introduced for the first time, but only in a small set of high-frequency patterns tied to advice and choice. Comparative structures from earlier volumes are reviewed and applied to real trade-offs, and clitic pronouns already familiar from Chapters 1, 9, and 12 recur naturally in advice-giving contexts.

Coming Into This Chapter

In This Chapter

You will use that same conditional form to give advice and weigh possibilities, and you will take your first controlled step into hypothetical meaning: imagining what would happen under a condition that is not (yet) true.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — intonation for advice versus command

Advice softened with the conditional should not fall as sharply at the end of the sentence as a command does. Compare the flat, closed fall of Chiedi un altro orario. (a command) with the gentler curve of Al posto tuo, chiederei un altro orario. (advice) — the pitch rises slightly on chiederei and settles more slowly, signaling a suggestion rather than an instruction.

13.1 Stress in conditional endings

The stress in condizionale presente forms falls on the ending, not the stem: chiedeREI, potREBBE, converREBBE, rinunceREBBE. English-speaking learners often misplace stress toward the earlier syllables of the stem; practice these forms slowly before using them in the rhythm of a full sentence.

13.2 Rhythm in hypothetical sentences

A hypothetical sentence such as Se fossi in te, non rinuncerei al viaggio. has two rhythmic halves, divided at the comma. Give the se clause its own breath group, pause briefly, and then deliver the consequence clause at a slightly lower pitch, as a considered conclusion rather than a new statement.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Language Focus — condizionale presente for advice and possibility

You have used the condizionale presente since Volume II for polite requests and, in Chapter 12, for professional softening. This chapter develops the same form for a new function: giving advice and describing possibility. Compare Dovresti parlare con il tuo relatore. (You should talk to your advisor.) with a flat command like Parla con il tuo relatore. The conditional turns a directive into a suggestion the listener is free to weigh. Al posto tuo, chiederei di cambiare l'orario del seminario. (In your place, I would ask to change the seminar schedule.) works the same way: the speaker offers a perspective rather than an order. No new verb form is introduced; what is new is using the conditional deliberately to advise rather than only to request or soften.

Language Focus — a first hypothetical frame: se + congiuntivo imperfetto + condizionale

When you imagine a condition that is not (yet) true and its likely result, Italian uses a specific pattern: se plus the congiuntivo imperfetto in the condition clause, and the condizionale presente in the result clause. Se fossi in te, chiederei di cambiare l'orario. (If I were you, I would ask to change the schedule.) Se Chiara accettasse il lavoro, dovrebbe rinunciare al seminario del martedì. (If Chiara accepted the job, she would have to give up Tuesday's seminar.) This chapter limits productive use to a small set of high-frequency frames built on essere (fossi, fosse), avere (avessi, avesse), and a few regular first-conjugation verbs relevant to this chapter's topics, such as accettasse and rinunciasse. You are not yet asked to build this pattern freely with any verb; that fuller control develops later in the series.

Language Focus — comparing options

Comparative structures from earlier volumes support real decision-making here: Il lavoro in archivio è più interessante del tutoraggio, ma richiede più ore. (The archive job is more interesting than tutoring, but it requires more hours.) Combine comparatives with da un lato... dall'altro to organize a two-sided comparison clearly before reaching a recommendation.

Review from earlier chapters remains active throughout: direct, indirect, and combined clitic pronouns (Chapters 1, 9, and 12), relative clauses for describing options and their conditions (Chapter 8, Chapter 12), and the congiuntivo presente after opinion and necessity triggers (Chapters 3 and 11) all recur naturally here and should not be treated as new material.

Model Dialogue: Due orari che si scontrano

Setting: Chiara, Marco, and Elena meet at a café near campus to help Chiara decide what to do about the scheduling conflict.

Chiara Il lavoro in archivio è tre pomeriggi a settimana, esattamente quando ho il seminario obbligatorio. Non so cosa fare. eel la-VO-ro in ar-KEE-vyo EH treh po-meh-REED-jee a set-tee-MA-na, eh-zat-ta-MEN-teh koo-AN-do O eel seh-mee-NA-ryo ob-blee-ga-TO-ryo. non so KO-za FA-reh. The archive job is three afternoons a week, exactly when I have my required seminar. I don't know what to do.
Marco Al posto tuo, parlerei prima con l'archivio: forse potrebbero darti un orario diverso. al POS-to TOO-o, par-leh-REH-ee PREE-ma kon lar-KEE-vyo: FOR-seh po-TREB-beh-ro DAR-tee oon o-RA-ryo dee-VER-so. In your place, I would talk to the archive first: maybe they could give you a different schedule.
Elena Ma se l'archivio non fosse disponibile a cambiare, dovresti scegliere tra il lavoro e il seminario, no? ma seh lar-KEE-vyo non FOS-seh dee-spo-NEE-bee-leh a kam-BYA-reh, do-VRES-tee sheh-GLYEH-reh tra eel la-VO-ro eh eel seh-mee-NA-ryo, no? But if the archive weren't willing to change, you would have to choose between the job and the seminar, right?
Chiara Esatto, ed è per questo che ho paura di chiedere. Da un lato il lavoro mi interessa molto, dall'altro il seminario è obbligatorio per la laurea. eh-ZAT-to, ed EH pehr koo-EHS-to keh O pa-OO-ra dee kyeh-DEH-reh. da oon LA-to eel la-VO-ro mee een-teh-RES-sa MOL-to, dal-LAL-tro eel seh-mee-NA-ryo EH ob-blee-ga-TO-ryo pehr la la-OO-reh-a. Exactly, and that's why I'm afraid to ask. On one hand the job interests me a lot, on the other the seminar is required for my degree.
Marco Ma pensaci: se tu non chiedessi niente, non sapresti mai se esiste un'altra soluzione. Conviene provare. ma pen-SA-chee: seh too non kyeh-DES-see NYEN-teh, non sa-PRES-tee MAI seh eh-ZEES-teh oo-nal-tra so-loo-TSYO-neh. kon-VYEH-neh pro-VA-reh. But think about it: if you asked nothing, you would never know if another solution exists. It's worth trying.
Elena E se proprio dovessi scegliere, io al posto tuo non rinuncerei al seminario — ma questa è solo la mia opinione. eh seh PRO-pryo do-VES-see sheh-GLYEH-reh, EE-o al POS-to TOO-o non ree-noon-cheh-REH-ee al seh-mee-NA-ryo — ma koo-EHS-ta EH SO-lo la MEE-a o-pee-NYO-neh. And if you really had to choose, in your place I wouldn't give up the seminar — but that's just my opinion.

  2. What does Marco advise her to do first?

Speaking Practice

Pratica controllata — Controlled practice: soften the command

Take four direct commands (Accetta il lavoro., Parla con il relatore., etc.) and restate each one as advice using the conditional, with and without al posto tuo.

Compito comunicativo — Communicative task: weighing two options

With a partner, describe two real or invented options you are considering (two jobs, two courses, two apartments). Use da un lato... dall'altro and at least one comparative form, then ask your partner for advice.

Portfolio orale — Oral portfolio: if I were you

Listen to a partner's dilemma (or invent one) and respond with at least one hypothetical sentence using se fossi in te or se fossi al posto suo.

Reading Practice

Read the advice-column excerpt below, adapted for this level.

Rubrica dei lettori — Reader advice column
Cara rubrica, ho ventidue anni e devo scegliere tra uno stage retribuito e un semestre di studio all'estero che ho sempre desiderato. Non posso fare entrambi. Da un lato lo stage mi darebbe esperienza immediata e un piccolo stipendio; dall'altro il semestre all'estero mi offrirebbe una prospettiva che non avrò più a disposizione così facilmente. Qualora scegliessi lo stage, temo di rimpiangere per anni l'occasione persa. A meno che qualcuno non mi convinca del contrario, penso che sceglierò lo studio all'estero. Cosa ne pensate?
Dear column, I am twenty-two and have to choose between a paid internship and a semester of study abroad that I have always wanted. I can't do both. On one hand, the internship would give me immediate experience and a small salary; on the other, the semester abroad would offer me a perspective I won't have so easily available again. Should I choose the internship, I'm afraid I'll regret the lost opportunity for years. Unless someone convinces me otherwise, I think I will choose to study abroad. What do you think?

Domande di comprensione — Comprehension questions:

  2. What advantage does each option offer, according to the writer?
  3. What has the writer already decided, and under what condition might that change?

Writing Workshop

Write your own comparison and advice text.

Compito di scrittura — Writing task: Write eight to ten sentences comparing two real or invented options you (or a friend) are facing, and give advice about which to choose. Use at least one comparative structure, at least one advice pattern with the conditional, and at least one hypothetical sentence with se + congiuntivo imperfetto + condizionale.

  1. Present the choice. Briefly state the two options and why a decision is needed.
  2. Compare them. Use da un lato... dall'altro and a comparative form.
  3. Give advice. Use a conditional advice pattern such as Dovresti or Al posto tuo.
  4. Add a hypothetical. Use one controlled se + congiuntivo imperfetto + condizionale sentence.
  5. Close with a recommendation. State clearly which option you would choose and why.

Lista di controllo — Editing checklist:

  • At least one comparative structure supports the comparison.
  • At least one conditional advice pattern is used appropriately.
  • At least one hypothetical sentence follows the se + congiuntivo imperfetto + condizionale pattern correctly.
  • The recommendation at the end is clear and consistent with the comparison given.
  • Accents, apostrophes, and punctuation are checked throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — giving advice among friends

Among close friends, Italian speakers often give advice more directly than English speakers might expect, especially when asked for an opinion — a friend may say plainly Io al posto tuo non lo farei rather than hedging extensively. This directness generally reflects closeness and investment in the outcome rather than a lack of tact.

Culture in Context — advice columns and public decision-making

Rubriche di consigli (advice columns) remain a familiar feature of Italian magazines, newspapers, and their online equivalents, where readers write in about personal dilemmas and receive a published response — a useful, level-appropriate genre for practicing comparison, advice, and hypothetical language.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — using the indicative instead of congiuntivo imperfetto after se

Error: Se io ero in te, non rinuncerei al viaggio.

Correction: Se io fossi in te, non rinuncerei al viaggio. The condition clause of this hypothetical pattern requires the congiuntivo imperfetto, not the imperfetto indicativo.

Common Mistake — using the future instead of the condizionale in the result clause

Error: Se Chiara accettasse il lavoro, dovrà cambiare seminario.

Correction: Se Chiara accettasse il lavoro, dovrebbe cambiare seminario. Once the condition clause uses the congiuntivo imperfetto, the result clause takes the condizionale presente, not the future.

Common Mistake — giving advice as a bare command

Error: Chiedi un altro orario! used where a softer suggestion was intended.

Correction: Al posto tuo, chiederei un altro orario. Reserve the bare imperative for situations that genuinely call for a direct instruction; use the conditional when offering a suggestion the listener is free to weigh.
