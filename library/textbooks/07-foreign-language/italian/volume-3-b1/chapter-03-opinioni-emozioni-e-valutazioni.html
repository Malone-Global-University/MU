Opinioni, emozioni e valutazioni

Volume III, Chapter 3: Opinions, Emotions, and Evaluation · Speak and Write Italian: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Overview and Objectives

The courtyard where Marco told his story is about to change: the building's residents are discussing a renovation plan, and everyone has an opinion. Elena thinks it is a good idea; Marco is not so sure; Chiara has doubts about the cost. Moving from narration to stance means learning to say not just what happened, but what you think, feel, and believe about it — and to do so with the grammatical tools Italian uses for opinion, doubt, and evaluation. This chapter introduces your first productive use of congiuntivo presente, always inside a small set of high-frequency frames, alongside the everyday work of supporting an opinion and disagreeing politely. By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Express an opinion, emotion, or evaluation using appropriate stance vocabulary.
  2. Recognize the contexts that commonly trigger congiuntivo presente.
  3. Produce congiuntivo presente after high-frequency expressions such as penso che, credo che, è importante che, and mi sembra che.
  4. Contrast indicative and subjunctive clauses based on certainty versus doubt or evaluation.
  5. Support an opinion with reasons and examples using connectors such as per esempio and infatti.
  6. Respond to disagreement politely, without abandoning your position.

Communication Scenario

A notice has appeared in the courtyard: the building's owners are proposing to renovate the courtyard and the entrance. Elena, Marco, and Chiara run into each other by the mailboxes and start discussing what they think of the plan.

  1. You will read and listen to Elena, Marco, and Chiara sharing different opinions about the renovation plan.
  2. You will then complete a task in which you state and support your own opinion about a plan, proposal, or issue, responding politely to at least one objection.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — intonation for opinion, uncertainty, and polite disagreement

Stating an opinion in Italian often begins with a falling-then-level pitch on secondo me, signaling "here comes my view": Secondo me, è una buona idea. Polite disagreement frequently softens the pitch before the contrast word: Hai ragione, ma… io non sono del tutto d'accordo. Practice raising your pitch slightly on ma to signal that a gentle contrast is coming, rather than a flat contradiction.

3.1 Introduced: stress in congiuntivo forms

Regular congiuntivo presente forms keep the stress in the same position as the corresponding indicative forms for most persons, but the endings sound different from what you may expect: che io parli, che lui creda. Listen for the shared vowel -i across most singular endings, which is one of the clearest audible signals that a clause has shifted into the subjunctive.

3.2 Review: clear vowels in evaluative phrases

Keep vowels full and clear in high-frequency evaluative phrases, resisting the urge to reduce unstressed vowels the way English does: è importante che, mi sembra che. Every vowel in these phrases keeps its full quality, even when unstressed.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Language Focus — what triggers congiuntivo presente

Italian shifts from the indicative to the subjunctive in a dependent clause when the main clause expresses opinion, doubt, emotion, desire, or evaluation rather than a stated fact. Compare So che il progetto è buono (a fact, indicative) with Penso che il progetto sia buono (an opinion, subjunctive). The verb in the main clause — penso, credo, mi sembra — signals that what follows che is being framed as a belief or judgment, not asserted as settled fact.

Language Focus — forming congiuntivo presente

For regular verbs, congiuntivo presente is built by replacing the indicative endings with subjunctive endings: -are verbs take -i, -i, -i, -iamo, -iate, -ino (che io parli, che tu parli, che lui/lei parli); -ere and -ire verbs take -a, -a, -a, -iamo, -iate, -ano (che io creda, che io senta). Notice that the first three singular forms are identical, which is why subject pronouns are often kept in subjunctive clauses to avoid ambiguity: Penso che io abbia ragione versus Penso che lei abbia ragione. This chapter limits productive practice to essere, avere, and a small set of high-frequency regular verbs in the frames listed above.

Language Focus — supporting an opinion

An opinion becomes persuasive when it is followed by a reason and, often, an example: Secondo me, dovremmo rinnovare il cortile, perché è ormai troppo vecchio. Per esempio, l'illuminazione non funziona da mesi. Infatti introduces a fact that reinforces what was just said, while da un lato… dall'altro lets you acknowledge two sides of an issue before stating your own view.

This chapter does not present a complete subjunctive system. You will practice congiuntivo presente only after the four scheduled frames — penso che, credo che, è importante che, and mi sembra che — with essere, avere, and high-frequency regular verbs. Broader subjunctive control develops later in this unit and across the rest of the volume.

Model Dialogue

Setting: By the mailboxes, a few days after a notice about a courtyard renovation plan appears. Elena, Marco, and Chiara stop to discuss it.

Elena Avete visto l'avviso sul rinnovo del cortile? Secondo me, è una svolta positiva per tutto il palazzo. A-VEH-teh VEE-sto la-VEE-zo sool ree-NNO-vo del kor-TEE-leh? Seh-KON-do meh, EH oo-na SVOL-ta po-zee-TEE-va per TOOT-to eel pa-LAT-tso. Did you see the notice about the courtyard renovation? In my opinion, it's a positive change for the whole building.
Marco Hai ragione che il cortile ha bisogno di lavori, ma non sono del tutto d'accordo. Penso che sia troppo costoso per adesso. AH-ee ra-JOH-neh keh eel kor-TEE-leh ah bee-ZOH-nyo dee la-VO-ree, ma non SO-no del TOOT-to dak-KOR-do. PEN-so keh SEE-a TROP-po ko-STO-zo per a-DES-so. You're right that the courtyard needs work, but I don't entirely agree. I think it's too expensive for now.
Chiara Anch'io ho dei dubbi. Credo che i costi non siano stati spiegati bene. È importante che i proprietari siano più chiari sui numeri prima di decidere. Ank-EE-o o day DOOB-bee. KREH-do keh ee KO-stee non SEE-a-no STA-tee spee-eh-GA-tee BEH-neh. EH eem-por-TAN-teh keh ee pro-pree-eh-TA-ree SEE-a-no PYOO KYA-ree soo-ee NOO-meh-ree PREE-ma dee deh-CHEE-deh-reh. I also have doubts. I believe the costs haven't been explained well. It's important that the owners be clearer about the numbers before deciding.
Elena Capisco i vostri dubbi, ma mi sembra che il cortile sia davvero pericoloso di sera. Per esempio, l'illuminazione non funziona da mesi. Ka-PEE-sko ee VOS-tree DOOB-bee, ma mee SEM-bra keh eel kor-TEE-leh SEE-a dav-VEH-ro peh-ree-ko-LO-zo dee SEH-ra. Per eh-ZEM-pyo, lil-loo-mee-na-TSYO-neh non foon-TSYO-na da MEH-zee. I understand your doubts, but it seems to me that the courtyard is really dangerous at night. For example, the lighting hasn't worked in months.
Marco Su questo hai ragione. Infatti, anch'io sono preoccupato per la sicurezza. Da un lato capisco l'urgenza, dall'altro credo che dobbiamo aspettare risposte più chiare. Soo KWE-sto AH-ee ra-JOH-neh. Een-FAT-tee, ank-EE-o SO-no preh-ok-koo-PA-to per la see-koo-RET-tsa. Da oon LA-to ka-PEE-sko loor-JEN-tsa, dal-LAL-tro KREH-do keh dob-BYA-mo as-peh-TA-reh ree-SPOS-teh PYOO KYA-reh. You're right about that. In fact, I'm also worried about safety. On one hand I understand the urgency, on the other I think we need to wait for clearer answers.
Chiara Sono sorpresa che siamo tutti così d'accordo su cosa non va, anche se non siamo d'accordo su cosa fare. SO-no sor-PREH-za keh SEE-a-mo TOOT-tee ko-ZEE dak-KOR-do soo KO-za non va, AN-keh seh non SEE-a-mo dak-KOR-do soo KO-za FA-reh. I'm surprised that we all agree so much on what's wrong, even if we don't agree on what to do.

Domande di comprensione — Comprehension questions:

  1. What is Elena's opinion about the renovation, and why?
  2. What doubt does Chiara raise?
  3. What do all three characters ultimately agree on, and what do they still disagree about?

Reading Practice

Read the short opinion comment below, which responds to a notice posted online about the courtyard renovation.

Un commento online — An online comment
Ho letto l'avviso sul rinnovo del cortile e vorrei dire la mia. Credo che il progetto sia necessario, ma mi sembra che manchino ancora informazioni chiare sui costi. È importante che i proprietari spieghino bene come verranno divisi i pagamenti tra i residenti. Da un lato capisco chi ha fretta di sistemare l'illuminazione; dall'altro, penso che sia giusto aspettare qualche settimana per avere tutti i dettagli. Secondo me, decidere troppo in fretta non aiuta nessuno.
I read the notice about the courtyard renovation and I would like to share my view. I believe the project is necessary, but it seems to me that clear information about the costs is still missing. It's important that the owners explain clearly how the payments will be divided among the residents. On one hand I understand those in a hurry to fix the lighting; on the other, I think it's right to wait a few weeks to have all the details. In my opinion, deciding too quickly doesn't help anyone.

Domande di comprensione — Comprehension questions:

  1. What is the writer's overall opinion about the renovation?
  2. What does the writer think is still missing?
  3. Identify two subjunctive frames used in the comment and the verb form that follows each.

Writing Workshop

Write a supported opinion paragraph responding to a plan, proposal, or issue of your choice.

Compito di scrittura — Writing task: Write eight to ten sentences stating and supporting your opinion about a plan, proposal, or issue. Include: an opening stance statement with secondo me or a scheduled subjunctive frame, at least two supporting reasons introduced with infatti or per esempio, at least one acknowledgment of another point of view using da un lato… dall'altro, and at least two total uses of congiuntivo presente across the scheduled frames.

  1. State your opinion. Open with secondo me or a subjunctive frame such as penso che.
  2. Give a reason. Use infatti to reinforce your stance with a fact.
  3. Give an example. Use per esempio to make your reasoning concrete.
  4. Acknowledge another view. Use da un lato… dall'altro to show balance.
  5. Close with your position. Restate your view clearly in your own words.

Lista di controllo — Editing checklist:

  • At least two correctly formed congiuntivo presente verbs appear after scheduled frames.
  • At least one connector for reason or example is used correctly.
  • Another point of view is acknowledged respectfully.
  • Apostrophes such as un'opinione and accents such as perché and così are checked.
  • Commas are placed correctly around subordinate clauses.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — disagreement in Italian conversation

Disagreement in Italian can be direct, animated, polite, ironic, or softened, depending on the relationship, setting, and topic. A lively exchange of opinions among neighbors or friends is not necessarily a sign of conflict; learners should not assume that raised voices or firm statements mean a conversation has become hostile.

Culture in Context — building meetings and shared decisions

La riunione di condominio, the building or co-op meeting, is a common setting in Italy for residents to discuss shared costs and decisions, such as renovations. Opinions in this context are often expressed formally, with attention to procedure, even when the underlying disagreement is quite ordinary.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — using the indicative after opinion verbs

Error: Writing Penso che il progetto è una buona idea, keeping the indicative after penso che.

Correction: Penso che il progetto sia una buona idea uses congiuntivo presente because penso che frames the statement as an opinion, not a fact.

Common Mistake — overusing the subjunctive after fact verbs

Error: Writing So che il cortile abbia bisogno di lavori, using the subjunctive after so che.

Correction: So che il cortile ha bisogno di lavori keeps the indicative because so che states a known fact rather than an opinion or doubt.

Common Mistake — forgetting the apostrophe in un'opinione

Error: Writing un opinione without the apostrophe.

Correction: un'opinione requires the apostrophe because opinione is feminine and begins with a vowel.
