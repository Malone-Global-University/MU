Mediazione, parafrasi e adattamento

Volume IV, Chapter 14 · Speak and Write Italian: Professional, Academic, and Cultural Communication · Mediation, Paraphrase, and Adaptation

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 14 completes the core instructional arc of Unit III, Cultura, memoria e mediazione, by turning the register-transformation skill practiced in Chapter 13 into a fuller skill: mediation. Professoressa Bianchi asks the class to take a dense, official university notice and turn it into something a new international student could actually use, then to do the same with a short passage about regional Italian drawn from Chapter 13's reading. Along the way, Chiara, Marco, and Elena learn to paraphrase accurately, simplify without distorting meaning, and adapt register for a specific audience and purpose. By the end of Chapter 14, you will be able to:

  1. Paraphrase dense institutional or academic-adjacent information accurately, in your own words.
  2. Simplify a formal text for a specified audience without losing its essential meaning.
  3. Mediate between a formal register and a more accessible one, choosing vocabulary and structure deliberately.
  4. Summarize a text's stance without exaggerating, softening, or distorting it.
  5. Adapt information heard aloud into a clear, organized written form.
  6. Identify the main point and supporting details of a dense text before mediating it.

Communication Scenario

Professoressa Bianchi brings in a dense notice from the university's international office, full of bureaucratic phrasing, and asks the class to imagine a new exchange student who needs to understand it quickly. She uses this task to introduce mediation as a distinct skill: not simply translating or shortening a text, but reshaping it, accurately and respectfully, for a specific reader's needs.

  1. You will read and listen to a connected scenario in which Professoressa Bianchi guides the students through mediating a dense institutional notice and then a short cultural passage for a new audience.
  2. You will then complete an integrated task in which you transform a dense text of your own choosing into an accurate, accessible summary for a specific audience, following the same steps Professoressa Bianchi models.

Where This Chapter Fits

Chapter 14 builds directly on Chapter 13's register-transformation practice, extending a single-sentence skill into a whole-text skill. It also draws on the summary and reported-speech work developed since Chapters 6 and 7, and on the nominalization and dense noun phrases you have handled in academic-adjacent reading since Chapter 7. This is the final content chapter of Unit III before Chapter 15's integrated review, so it deliberately pulls together heritage interpretation, cultural analysis, sociolinguistic awareness, and register control into one practical skill: mediating information for someone who needs it.

Pronunciation Focus

Reformulation intonation and pacing for mediation (developed)

When you mediate a text aloud, your pacing should signal that you are reshaping information for your listener, not simply reading it back. Slow down slightly before the main point, and use a small falling pitch to mark it as the key idea, then pick the pace back up for supporting details.

14.1 Marking the main point aloud

Compare a neutral reading with a mediation-style reading of the same information: In pratica: gli studenti internazionali devono presentare la domanda di iscrizione entro il trenta settembre. Say In pratica with a brief pause before it and a touch more emphasis on il trenta settembre, so a listener immediately hears which detail matters most.

14.2 Continuing controlled formal presentation rhythm

Keep the controlled, unhurried delivery practiced since Chapter 1 for the formal portions of a mediation task, and use a slightly warmer, more conversational pace only for the simplified version aimed at a general audience: Per dirla in modo semplice: dovete iscrivervi entro fine settembre, altrimenti perdete il posto.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Language Focus — nominal-to-verbal transformation (developed)

Dense institutional Italian often nominalizes actions into abstract nouns; mediation frequently requires turning them back into verbs so a general reader can follow the action more easily. Compare: Nominale (denso): "La presentazione della domanda di iscrizione deve avvenire entro la scadenza indicata." Verbale (mediato): "Gli studenti devono presentare la domanda entro la scadenza indicata." (Nominal (dense): "The submission of the enrollment application must occur by the indicated deadline." Verbal (mediated): "Students must submit the application by the indicated deadline.") Turning a nominalized action back into a verb with a clear subject is one of the most useful mediation techniques you can apply to institutional Italian.

Language Focus — simplifying relative clauses and appositions (developed)

Long relative clauses and appositions can often be split into shorter sentences without losing information: Denso: "Gli studenti, i quali devono aver completato almeno due semestri, che costituiscono il requisito minimo previsto dal regolamento, possono richiedere il tirocinio." Mediato: "Gli studenti possono richiedere il tirocinio solo dopo aver completato almeno due semestri. Questo è il requisito minimo previsto dal regolamento." Splitting a dense sentence at a relative clause or an apposition usually produces two clear sentences instead of one difficult one.

Language Building Block — clitic and reference tracking across a mediated text (consolidated)

When you split a long sentence into shorter ones, use clitic pronouns and clear reference so the shorter sentences still connect smoothly: La domanda va presentata online. Bisogna caricarla insieme a due documenti. Il sistema la conferma automaticamente. (The application must be submitted online. It must be uploaded together with two documents. The system confirms it automatically.) Track what la refers to across all three sentences so the mediated version stays coherent, not choppy.

No new productive tense or mood is introduced in this chapter. Reported information is refined for mediation purposes only within systems already active; professional translation certification, legal translation, and simultaneous interpreting remain outside this chapter's scope.

Model Dialogue: Un avviso per gli studenti internazionali

Setting: Professoressa Bianchi shows the class a dense notice from the university's international office and asks them to mediate it for a new exchange student.

Professoressa Bianchi Guardate questo avviso dell'ufficio internazionale. Immaginate una nuova studentessa Erasmus che lo legge il primo giorno. Capirebbe subito cosa deve fare? gwar-DA-teh koo-EH-sto av-VEE-zo del-loof-FEE-cho een-ter-na-tsyo-NA-leh. eem-ma-jee-NA-teh OO-na NOO-o-va stoo-den-TES-sa eh-RAZ-moos keh lo LEJ-jeh eel PREE-mo JOR-no. ka-pee-REB-beh SOO-bee-to KO-za DEH-veh FA-reh? Look at this notice from the international office. Imagine a new Erasmus student reading it on the first day. Would she immediately understand what she needs to do?
Marco Onestamente, no. C'è scritto: "La presentazione della domanda di iscrizione deve avvenire entro la scadenza indicata nel presente avviso, pena l'esclusione dalla procedura." Io stesso ho dovuto rileggerlo due volte. o-nes-ta-MEN-teh, no. cheh SKRIT-to: la preh-zen-ta-TSYO-neh DEL-la do-MAN-da dee ee-skree-tsyo-NEH DEH-veh av-veh-NEE-reh en-TRO la ska-DEN-tsa een-dee-KA-ta nel preh-ZEN-teh av-VEE-zo, PEH-na leh-skloo-ZYO-neh DAL-la pro-cheh-DOO-ra. EE-o STES-so o do-VOO-to ree-LEJ-jer-lo DOO-eh VOL-teh. Honestly, no. It says: "The submission of the enrollment application must occur by the deadline indicated in this notice, on pain of exclusion from the procedure." I myself had to reread it twice.
Professoressa Bianchi Esatto. Il primo passo della mediazione è individuare il punto principale, prima ancora di scegliere le parole. Qual è il punto principale di questa frase? eh-ZAT-to. eel PREE-mo PAS-so DEL-la meh-dya-TSYO-neh eh een-dee-vee-DOO-a-reh eel POON-to preen-chee-PA-leh, PREE-ma AN-ko-ra dee sheh-LYEH-reh leh pa-RO-leh. kwal eh eel POON-to preen-chee-PA-leh dee koo-EH-sta FRA-zeh? Exactly. The first step of mediation is identifying the main point, even before choosing words. What is the main point of this sentence?
Elena Che bisogna presentare la domanda entro una certa data, altrimenti non si viene ammessi. Il resto è la forma burocratica. keh bee-ZO-nya preh-zen-TA-reh la do-MAN-da en-TRO OO-na CHER-ta DA-ta, al-tree-MEN-tee non see VYEH-neh am-MES-see. eel RES-to eh la FOR-ma boo-ro-KRA-tee-ka. That you have to submit the application by a certain date, or else you're not admitted. The rest is the bureaucratic form.
Professoressa Bianchi Perfetto. Ora possiamo riformulare: "Dovete presentare la domanda entro la scadenza indicata, altrimenti non potrete partecipare alla procedura." Ho trasformato il sostantivo "presentazione" in un verbo, con un soggetto chiaro: "voi." Ho anche diviso la frase per renderla più chiara. per-FET-to. O-ra pos-SYA-mo ree-for-moo-LA-reh: do-VEH-teh preh-zen-TA-reh la do-MAN-da en-TRO la ska-DEN-tsa een-dee-KA-ta, al-tree-MEN-tee non po-TREH-teh par-teh-chee-PA-reh AL-la pro-cheh-DOO-ra. o trans-for-MA-to eel so-stan-TEE-vo preh-zen-ta-TSYO-neh een oon VER-bo, kon oon so-JET-to KYA-ro: voy. o AN-keh dee-VEE-zo la FRA-zeh per REN-der-la pyoo KYA-ra. Perfect. Now we can reformulate: "You must submit the application by the indicated deadline, or you will not be able to take part in the procedure." I turned the noun "submission" into a verb, with a clear subject: "you." I also split the sentence to make it clearer.
Chiara Ma non stiamo semplificando troppo? Non vogliamo travisare quello che dice davvero l'avviso. ma non STYA-mo sem-plee-fee-KAN-do TROP-po? non vo-LYA-mo tra-vee-ZA-reh koo-EL-lo keh DEE-cheh dav-VEH-ro lav-VEE-zo. But aren't we simplifying too much? We don't want to distort what the notice really says.
Professoressa Bianchi Ottima osservazione, Chiara. La mediazione non significa perdere informazioni importanti: significa renderle accessibili senza travisarle. Se un dettaglio è rilevante per il lettore, dobbiamo mantenerlo, anche in una versione più semplice. OT-tee-ma os-ser-va-TSYO-neh, KYA-ra. la meh-dya-TSYO-neh non see-NYEE-fee-ka PER-deh-reh een-for-ma-TSYO-nee eem-por-TAN-tee: see-NYEE-fee-ka REN-der-leh ach-cheh-SEE-bee-lee SEN-tsa tra-vee-ZAR-leh. seh oon det-TA-lyo eh reh-le-VAN-teh per eel let-TO-reh, do-BBYA-mo man-teh-NER-lo, AN-keh een OO-na ver-SYO-neh pyoo SEM-plee-cheh. Excellent observation, Chiara. Mediation does not mean losing important information: it means making it accessible without distorting it. If a detail is relevant to the reader, we must keep it, even in a simpler version.

Comunicato del comune — Municipal notice
Si rende noto che, in conformità con quanto disposto dal regolamento comunale in materia di raccolta differenziata, a decorrere dal primo giorno del prossimo mese sarà introdotto un nuovo sistema di conferimento dei rifiuti organici, basato sull'utilizzo di un contenitore dotato di microchip identificativo assegnato a ciascun nucleo familiare. Writing Workshop

Complete the plain-language adaptation task below.

Compito di scrittura — Writing task: Choose either the international-office announcement from the Listening Practice or the municipal notice from the Reading Practice, and write a 120–180 word plain-language adaptation for a specified audience (for example, a new international student or an elderly resident unfamiliar with bureaucratic Italian). Your adaptation should: (1) open by clearly stating the main point; (2) include only supporting details that are genuinely relevant to your chosen audience; (3) use at least one nominal-to-verbal transformation and one split sentence, as modeled in the Grammar section; and (4) avoid adding or removing any factual information from the original.

  1. Identify. Underline or list the main point and the supporting details of the original text before writing anything.
  2. Plan for your audience. Decide which details your specific reader actually needs.
  3. Draft freely. Write the adaptation following the required structure without stopping to correct yourself.
  4. Check the transformation. Confirm you have turned at least one nominalized phrase into a verb with a clear subject.
  5. Check for distortion. Confirm every fact in your adaptation matches the original; confirm you have not invented or dropped relevant information.
  6. Finalize. Check accents, apostrophes, punctuation, and capitalization throughout.

Lista di controllo — Editing checklist:

  • The adaptation opens with the main point stated clearly.
  • Only details relevant to the specified audience are included.
  • At least one nominal-to-verbal transformation and one split sentence appear.
  • No fact from the original has been added, dropped, or distorted.
  • Accents, apostrophes, punctuation, and capitalization are checked throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — plain-language communication in Italian public life

Italian institutions have historically favored dense, formal bureaucratic style, sometimes called burocratese, in official notices. In recent years, some universities, municipalities, and public agencies have promoted linguaggio semplice ("plain language") initiatives to make notices more accessible, particularly for international residents, older citizens, and people with limited literacy. This shift is uneven across institutions and regions, so avoid assuming that every Italian public notice you encounter will be either fully bureaucratic or fully plain; describe specific institutions and contexts rather than generalizing about all of Italian public communication.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — simplifying a sentence without checking for lost information

Error: Dovete presentare la domanda entro settembre. (drops the specific deadline date and the consequence of missing it, both of which matter to the reader)

Correction: Dovete presentare la domanda entro il trenta settembre, altrimenti non potrete partecipare alla procedura. Before shortening a sentence, confirm that every detail relevant to your audience survives, as Professoressa Bianchi insisted in the dialogue.

Common Mistake — leaving the nominalized structure unchanged

Error: La presentazione della domanda deve avvenire entro la scadenza. (kept exactly as in the dense original, without simplification)

Correction: Dovete presentare la domanda entro la scadenza. Turn the nominalized action back into a verb with a clear subject, as practiced in the Grammar section.

Common Mistake — softening or exaggerating the original stance

Error: Se non consegnate i documenti in tempo, probabilmente non succede niente di grave. (softens a genuine consequence stated in the original notice)

Correction: Se non consegnate i documenti entro la data indicata, sarete esclusi dalla procedura. A mediated text must preserve the original's stance and consequences accurately, not soften them to sound friendlier.
