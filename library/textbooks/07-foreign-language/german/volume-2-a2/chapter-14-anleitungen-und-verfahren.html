Anleitungen und Verfahren

Instructions and Procedures · Speak and Write German: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

Chapter 14 turns from describing places to explaining how to do things. You will activate imperative forms across all three address levels — du, ihr, and formal Sie — so that you can give instructions naturally in different social settings. You will also learn the impersonal man construction, which German uses constantly for general instructions and procedures, along with sequence connectors that organize steps clearly and modal verbs that express necessity and permission. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to read a set of instructions, explain how something works, and write a simple procedure of your own.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Form and use imperatives correctly for du, ihr, and Sie.
  2. Use the impersonal man construction to describe general procedures and rules.
  3. Sequence steps clearly using connectors such as zuerst, dann, danach, and zum Schluss.
  4. Express necessity and permission using müssen, sollen, dürfen, and können in instructional contexts.
  5. Read a set of instructions and identify each step.
  6. Write and explain a short step-by-step procedure.

Opening Scenario

Lukas has settled into his new neighborhood, and now his colleague Frau Yilmaz is training him on a few routine procedures at his new job: how to set up the shared printer, how to submit a simple form, and what to do in case of a small kitchen mishap in the break room. Each situation calls for the same core tools — clear steps, the right address form for the moment, and precise language about what one must, should, or may do.

  1. Work through the vocabulary, grammar, and model dialogue below before attempting the practice tasks.
  2. Notice which address form each instruction uses, and why.
  3. Pay attention to how man appears in written instructions where a specific person is not named.

Core Vocabulary — Instructions and Procedures

This chapter's vocabulary supports reading and writing instructions in everyday, workplace, and household settings. Gender and plural forms are included for every active noun.

  • das Verfahren, die Verfahren — the procedure
  • die Reihenfolge, die Reihenfolgen — the order, sequence
  • die Regel, die Regeln — the rule

  • die Warnung, die Warnungen — the warning
  • vorsichtig — careful, cautious
  • ausschalten — to switch off
  • einschalten — to switch on

Reihenfolge-Wörter (Sequence Words)

  • danach — after that
  • anschließend — following that, subsequently
  • zum Schluss — finally, lastly

Workplace note: procedure and safety vocabulary here is everyday and practical. It is not a substitute for official workplace safety training, which should always follow the specific instructions of an employer or institution.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns — Giving and Understanding Instructions

Chapter 14 activates the imperative system across all three address forms, introduces the impersonal man construction, and develops the sequence connectors and modal verbs that make instructions clear and complete.

Imperatives: du, ihr, and Sie

German forms the imperative differently for each address level. The du-imperative usually drops the pronoun and the final -st ending; the ihr-imperative uses the plain present-tense ihr-form without the pronoun; and the Sie-imperative inverts the verb and pronoun, keeping Sie in the sentence.

AddressInfinitiveImperativeMeaning
duschaltenSchalte den Drucker ein!Turn on the printer.
ihrschaltenSchaltet den Drucker ein!Turn on the printer. (to a group)
SieschaltenSchalten Sie den Drucker ein!Please turn on the printer.
duseinSei vorsichtig!Be careful.

Stem-changing verbs (e→i/ie) keep the vowel change in the du-imperative: nehmen → Nimm das Formular! (Take the form.) This does not apply to the ihr- or Sie-imperative.

The Impersonal man Construction

Man is a subject pronoun that refers to people in general rather than to a specific person. It is extremely common in written instructions, signs, and explanations of how something works, and it always takes the same verb form as er/sie/es.

  • Zuerst füllt man das Formular aus. First, one fills out the form. / First, you fill out the form.
  • Man darf hier nicht rauchen. You're not allowed to smoke here. (general rule)
  • Wie schaltet man den Drucker aus? How do you turn off the printer?

    • In natural English, man is often best translated with a general "you" or "one," depending on register. Do not translate it literally as "man."

    Sequence Connectors

    Sequence connectors organize a procedure into a clear order. Each of the following can begin a main clause and is followed directly by the finite verb in second position.

    Verfahren erklären — Explaining a Procedure
    Zuerst öffnet man das Formular, dann füllt man die Felder aus, danach unterschreibt man, und zum Schluss schickt man es ab.
    Four sequence connectors + man construction, each clause keeping verb-second order
    Zuerst schalten Sie das Gerät ein. Danach warten Sie zehn Sekunden.
    First you turn on the device. After that, you wait ten seconds.

    Necessity and Permission: Modal Verbs in Instructions

    Instructions often rely on modal verbs rather than the imperative, especially in written or formal contexts. This chapter contrasts müssen (necessity), sollen (recommendation or expectation), dürfen (permission), and können (possibility or ability).

  • Sie müssen das Formular unterschreiben. You must sign the form. (required)
  • Man sollte den Herd nach dem Kochen ausschalten. One should turn off the stove after cooking. (recommended)
  • Hier darf man das Gerät nicht ohne Handschuhe berühren. Here, one may not touch the device without gloves. (not permitted)
  • Sie können das Formular auch online ausfüllen. You can also fill out the form online. (possible option)
    1. Give the du-imperative: (nehmen) ___ das Formular vom Tisch!
    2. Rewrite as a general instruction with man: Schalten Sie den Drucker aus. → ___
    3. Choose the correct modal: Hier ___ (dürfen/müssen) man nicht rauchen.

    Model Dialogue

    Context: Frau Yilmaz shows Lukas how to use the shared office printer on his first day. Neutral-to-formal workplace register, using Sie.

    Frau Yilmaz:
    So, das ist unser Drucker. Zuerst schalten Sie ihn hier ein. So, this is our printer. First you turn it on here.
    Lukas:
    Und dann? Muss ich mich irgendwo anmelden? And then? Do I have to log in somewhere?
    Frau Yilmaz:
    Genau. Geben Sie zuerst Ihren Namen ein, dann wählen Sie das Dokument aus. Exactly. First enter your name, then select the document.
    Lukas:
    Darf man auch in Farbe drucken, oder nur schwarz-weiß? Is one allowed to print in color, or only black and white?
    Frau Yilmaz:
    Man darf schon in Farbe drucken, aber man sollte es nur bei wichtigen Dokumenten machen. You are allowed to print in color, but you should only do it for important documents.
    Lukas:
    Verstanden. Und zum Schluss? Understood. And finally?
    Frau Yilmaz:
    Zum Schluss schalten Sie den Drucker bitte wieder aus, wenn Sie fertig sind. Finally, please switch the printer back off when you're done.

    Guided Practice

    1. Change to the Sie-imperative: (das Fenster / öffnen)
    2. Change to the ihr-imperative: (leise / sein)
    3. Rewrite with man: Ich fülle zuerst das Formular aus. → ___
    4. Choose müssen, dürfen, or sollen and explain your choice: Hier ___ man nicht ohne Erlaubnis fotografieren.
    5. Put the steps in a logical order using zuerst, dann, danach, and zum Schluss: (a) man schickt die E-Mail ab, (b) man schreibt den Text, (c) man öffnet das E-Mail-Programm, (d) man liest die E-Mail noch einmal durch.
    Hören

    Listening Practice

    Ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the following script at a natural pace. Listen once for the general idea, then again for detail.

    Wie man einen Termin online bucht (Listening Script)

    Herr Base erklärt einem Kollegen ein neues Verfahren: Zuerst öffnet man die Webseite und klickt auf "Termin buchen". Dann wählt man die Abteilung aus und gibt seinen Namen und seine Telefonnummer ein. Danach sucht man ein passendes Datum und eine Uhrzeit. Man muss außerdem eine kurze Beschreibung des Anliegens schreiben. Zum Schluss bestätigt man die Buchung, und man bekommt eine E-Mail mit allen Details. Man sollte die E-Mail aufbewahren, falls man den Termin später ändern möchte.

    Verständnisfragen

    1. Was macht man zuerst?

    2. Was muss man außerdem schreiben?

    3. Was bekommt man zum Schluss?

    4. Find one modal verb and one sequence connector in the script.

    Speaking Practice

    Aktivität: Ein Verfahren erklären (Explain a Procedure)

    Work with a partner. Choose one of the two role-card pairs below. One person explains a familiar procedure step by step; the other listens and asks clarifying questions.

    Partner A — Ein Verfahren erklären

    Explain a simple procedure you know well (making tea, setting up a device, submitting a form) using imperatives or man, at least two sequence connectors, and one modal verb of necessity or permission.

    • Use zuerst, dann, danach, or zum Schluss.
    • Include at least one imperative or man-sentence.

    Partner B — Nachfragen

    Listen to the explanation and ask at least two clarifying questions about the steps, the order, or whether something is required or optional.

    • Use Muss man...? or Darf man...?
    • Ask about the order of a step: Macht man das vor oder nach...?

    Bewertungshinweise — Speaking Criteria

    • Clear, logically ordered explanation of a procedure.
    • At least one correctly formed imperative or man-construction.
    • At least two sequence connectors used naturally.
    • At least one modal verb expressing necessity or permission.
    • Appropriate address form for the situation.

    Solo alternative: Write both sides of the exchange yourself, then read the exchange aloud, taking each role in turn.

    Lesen

    Reading Practice

    Read this short set of instructions, then answer the questions that follow.

    Verhalten in der Küche (Kitchen Rules)

    In der gemeinsamen Küche gelten ein paar einfache Regeln. Zuerst wäscht man sein Geschirr sofort nach dem Essen ab und stellt es nicht einfach in die Spüle. Danach wischt man den Tisch ab, wenn nötig. Man darf den Kühlschrank benutzen, aber man sollte Lebensmittel mit dem eigenen Namen beschriften. Beim Kochen muss man vorsichtig mit dem Herd umgehen: Schalten Sie den Herd immer aus, wenn Sie fertig sind, und lassen Sie nichts unbeaufsichtigt auf der Flamme stehen. Zum Schluss: Wenn etwas kaputt ist oder fehlt, sagt man einfach im Team Bescheid, anstatt es zu ignorieren.

    Schreiben

    Writing Workshop — Writing a Short Procedure

    This task asks you to write a short set of instructions using this chapter's imperative forms, the man construction, sequence connectors, and modal verbs.

    1. Choose a procedure. Pick something simple and familiar: a recipe, a form, a device, or a small workplace or school task.
    2. List the steps in order. Aim for four to six clear steps.
    3. Write your instructions. Use five to seven sentences, including at least one imperative form, one sentence with man, and at least three sequence connectors.
    4. Add at least one necessity or permission statement. Use müssen, sollen, or dürfen to note something required, recommended, or restricted.
    5. Revise using the checklist below.

    Checkliste — Editing Checklist:

    • Steps are logically ordered and connected with sequence words.
    • At least one imperative form matches the intended address level.
    • The man construction is used at least once and formed correctly.
    • At least one modal verb expresses necessity, recommendation, or permission.
    • All nouns are capitalized; umlauts and ß are used correctly.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture and Communication — Instructional Style

    Written instructions in German-speaking workplaces and public settings often favor the impersonal man construction or the formal Sie-imperative, which reads as neutral and professional rather than distant. Spoken instructions among colleagues or friends who use du tend to rely more on the du-imperative or simple statements with modal verbs. Choosing the right form is less about strict rules and more about matching the relationship and setting already established.

    Regional Note — Workplace Norms

    Expectations around address, formality, and shared spaces such as office kitchens vary across workplaces, institutions, and DACH countries, and even from one company to another within the same country. Instructions and house rules like the kitchen example in this chapter should be understood as one common style rather than a fixed standard that applies everywhere.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Dropping Sie from the formal imperative

    Error: *Schalten den Drucker ein!

    Correction: Schalten Sie den Drucker ein! The Sie-imperative keeps the pronoun Sie directly after the verb; only du and ihr normally drop the pronoun.

    Using the wrong verb form after man

    Error: *Man müssen das Formular unterschreiben.

    Correction: Man muss das Formular unterschreiben. Man always takes the same verb ending as er/sie/es, regardless of the actual number of people involved.

    Confusing müssen and dürfen in the negative

    Error: *Man muss hier nicht rauchen. (intended: "not allowed to")

    Correction: Man darf hier nicht rauchen. Nicht müssen means something is not required, while nicht dürfen means something is forbidden. These are different meanings, not interchangeable negatives.

    Answer Key

    Grammar Practice

    1. Nimm das Formular vom Tisch!
    2. Man schaltet den Drucker aus.
    3. darf — the sentence expresses a prohibition ("not allowed to"), which uses nicht dürfen, not nicht müssen.

    2. Seid leise!
