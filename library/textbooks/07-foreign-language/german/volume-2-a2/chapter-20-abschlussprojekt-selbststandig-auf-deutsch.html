Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 20 is the capstone of Volume II. It does not introduce a new grammar system. Instead, it asks you to bring together everything you have built since Chapter 1 — housing, relationships, travel, work, health, shopping, media, narration, advice, spatial description, procedures, opinions, comparison, culture, and rules — into one linked, realistic scenario. You will read, listen, speak, and write your way through a single ongoing situation: helping a friend named Lukas settle into a new city, sort out a housing problem, plan an outing, and navigate a scheduling conflict, while also reflecting on your own progress across the volume. This chapter measures reliable everyday independence at the B1 level, not new or hidden B2 material.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Integrate case, article, and adjective-ending control across varied everyday noun phrases.
  2. Narrate a connected past experience using the present perfect and simple past of high-frequency verbs.
  3. Use two-way prepositions, subordinate clauses, and separable verbs accurately within longer stretches of discourse.
  4. State and support an opinion, make a comparison, and give advice within a single extended interaction.
  5. Contrast modal verbs to explain a rule, recognize a passive-voice sentence, and use a conditional wenn-clause.
  6. Read and write multiple everyday genres — a message, a notice, a short narrative, and a recommendation — within one linked project.
  7. Mediate information between German and English responsibly for a specific practical purpose.
  8. Reflect on your own progress across Volume II and identify what to prioritize going into Volume III.

Opening Scenario

Sophie's friend Lukas has just moved to the same city for a new job. Over the next few days, Sophie helps him get settled: he needs to find an apartment, he wants to know the neighborhood, he has a mix-up with his gym membership similar to one Sophie once had, he wants to hear about Sophie's trip to Paul's regional festival, and he needs advice about a scheduling conflict between a work commitment and a friend's invitation. Sophie also reflects on how much easier these situations feel now compared to when she herself was newly arrived at the start of the volume.

  1. Review the Volume II Systems Review to reconnect with the systems you will need.
  2. Work through the model exchange, in which Sophie and Lukas discuss his first week.
  3. Complete the listening, reading, speaking, and writing tasks, which build toward the capstone performance task: a linked, multi-genre project combining practical, narrative, explanatory, and opinion tasks.

Modelldialog

Model Exchange

Setting: Sophie and Lukas, longtime friends, meet for coffee during Lukas's first week in the city. They use du.

Lukas
Na, wie war deine erste Woche hier, damals? Ich fühle mich gerade ein bisschen überfordert mit der Wohnungssuche.So, how was your first week here, back then? I feel a bit overwhelmed with the apartment search right now.
Sophie
Am Anfang war es auch für mich chaotisch. Ich habe drei Wohnungen besichtigt, bevor ich mich entschieden habe. Im Vergleich zu meiner alten Stadt sind die Mieten hier höher, aber die Nachbarschaft ist ruhiger.At the beginning it was chaotic for me too. I looked at three apartments before I decided. Compared to my old city, rents here are higher, but the neighborhood is quieter.
Lukas
Und dann hast du auch noch dieses Problem mit dem Fitnessstudio gehabt, oder? Erzähl mir davon — ich habe nämlich gerade eine ähnliche Verwirrung mit meiner Mitgliedschaft.And then you also had that problem with the gym, right? Tell me about it — I'm actually having a similar mix-up with my membership right now.
Sophie
Genau. Ich hatte eine Kündigung geschickt, aber sie ist zu spät angekommen, und die Mitgliedschaft wurde automatisch verlängert. Wenn du eine Ausnahme brauchst, würde ich an deiner Stelle sofort die Rezeption anrufen und höflich nach einer Lösung fragen.Exactly. I had sent a cancellation, but it arrived too late, and the membership got automatically extended. If you need an exception, if I were you, I would call the front desk right away and politely ask about a solution.
Lukas
Gute Idee. Übrigens, ich habe auch einen Terminkonflikt: Meine Kollegen wollen am Samstag ein Teamessen machen, aber ich habe schon versprochen, mit dir zum Fest zu fahren.Good idea. By the way, I also have a scheduling conflict: my colleagues want to have a team dinner on Saturday, but I already promised to go to the festival with you.
Sophie
Das verstehe ich. Wenn dir das Teamessen wichtig ist, sollst du wirklich zuerst mit deinen Kollegen sprechen — wir können das Fest auch nächstes Jahr besuchen. Was meinst du?I understand that. If the team dinner matters to you, you really should talk to your colleagues first — we can visit the festival next year too. What do you think?

This exchange recycles narration (present perfect, simple past), comparison (im Vergleich zu), rule-related modal use, a conditional wenn-clause, and advice-giving — all from earlier chapters, now combined naturally.

Listening Practice

Ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the following short voicemail aloud. Listen once for the overall situation, then again for the specific request.

Anruf von Lukas — Listening Script

Hallo Sophie, hier ist Lukas. Ich habe gerade mit meinem Vermieter telefoniert, und es gibt ein kleines Problem mit der Wohnung: Der Herd funktioniert nicht richtig, und ich weiß nicht, wen ich anrufen soll. Der Vermieter hat gesagt, dass ein Techniker geschickt wird, aber das kann bis zu einer Woche dauern. Wenn du eine Nummer von einem Handwerker kennst, den man schneller erreichen kann, wäre das super. Außerdem wollte ich fragen, ob wir das Teamessen-Problem vom Samstag noch einmal besprechen können — ich glaube, ich habe eine Lösung gefunden. Ruf mich zurück, wenn du Zeit hast!

Verständnisfragen

1. Was funktioniert in Lukas' Wohnung nicht richtig?

2. Was hat der Vermieter gesagt?

3. Um was bittet Lukas Sophie?

4. Find one subordinate clause with dass and one with wenn in the script.

Nachricht von Lukas

Schreiben

Writing Workshop — A Linked Reply

This task asks you to write a reply to Lukas's message above, combining several genres you have practiced across the volume: a short narrative, a piece of advice, and a confirmation of plans.

  1. Respond to the good news. React to the gym and stove updates using appropriate reactions and connectors.
  2. Narrate briefly. Add one or two sentences narrating a related experience of your own, using the present perfect or simple past.
  3. Compare and comment. Respond to Lukas's comparison between the two cities, agreeing, disagreeing, or qualifying his view.
  4. Confirm the plan. Confirm the Saturday festival plan clearly, using at least one wenn-clause.
  5. Revise using the checklist below.

Checkliste — Editing Checklist:

  • The reply addresses all three genre threads: reaction, narration, and confirmation.
  • At least one subordinate clause and one wenn-clause appear with correct verb-final order.
  • Comparative language is used accurately and respectfully.
  • The tone is warm and appropriate for a message between close friends using du.
  • All nouns are capitalized; umlauts and ß are used correctly.
  • No claim is made that one city or institution represents all of Germany, Austria, or Switzerland.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Everyday Independence Looks Different Everywhere

The confidence to solve everyday problems — negotiating a rule, comparing two cities, or resolving a scheduling conflict — depends as much on local institutions and personal networks as on language skill. What worked smoothly for Lukas with one gym or one landlord may not generalize to another city, another building, or another country. Practical independence in German means being able to ask, clarify, and adapt, not memorizing one fixed procedure.

Regional Note — Comparing Cities and Institutions Fairly

When comparing rents, neighborhoods, or service experiences between two German-speaking cities or regions, remember that conditions vary by country, city, and even individual building or business. A single positive or negative experience should not be generalized into a claim about an entire country or the whole German-speaking world.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Losing the sentence bracket in longer sentences

Error: *Ich habe gesagt dass ich komme am Samstag.

Correction: Keep the subordinate clause's verb at the end and use correct punctuation: Ich habe gesagt, dass ich am Samstag komme. Longer, multi-clause sentences still require careful bracket and word-order control.

Overgeneralizing from one comparison

Error: *In Deutschland sind alle Mieten hoch und alle Vermieter kompliziert.

Correction: Attribute the comparison to the specific situation: In dieser Stadt sind die Mieten höher als in meiner alten Stadt. Avoid turning one comparison into a claim about an entire country.

Mixing address forms within one message

Error: *Hallo Lukas, wie geht es Ihnen? Kannst du mir helfen?

Correction: Keep address consistent throughout a single message: Hallo Lukas, wie geht es dir? Kannst du mir helfen? Since Sophie and Lukas are close friends, du should be used consistently.
