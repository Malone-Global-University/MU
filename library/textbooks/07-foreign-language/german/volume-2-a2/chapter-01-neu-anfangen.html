Re-entry and Personal Update · Speak and Write German: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

Welcome to Volume II. This chapter does not introduce a major new grammar system. Instead, it asks you to reintroduce yourself with more detail than you could in Volume I, and it gives you a diagnostic review of the case, verb-position, and tense systems you will keep building throughout this volume. Think of Chapter 1 as a bridge: everything here should feel familiar, but you will use it with more independence and more connected sentences than before.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Reintroduce yourself with richer personal, academic, and professional detail.
  2. Summarize your current life, studies, work, and near-future goals.
  3. Use nominative, accusative, and controlled dative forms in a diagnostic self-check.
  4. Review and apply the present perfect (Perfekt) and modal verb brackets in connected speech and writing.
  5. Confirm control of verb-second order and question word order across longer sentences.
  6. Read short learner biographies and identify their personal, practical, and narrative content.
  7. Write an updated learning profile that states concrete goals for Volume II.

Opening Scenario

A new term is beginning. Mia, Lukas, and Anna are back together after a break, and their instructor asks everyone to introduce themselves again — not as complete beginners this time, but as learners who already have a year of German behind them. Mia has changed apartments, Lukas has finished his internship in Freiburg, and Anna is preparing to move to Berlin. Before the group can move forward into new material, they take stock of what they already know and set goals for the new volume. You will do the same: update your own profile and diagnose where your German stands today.

  1. Skim back through your Volume I notes or the Chapter 20 portfolio you completed. You do not need to reread everything in detail — just refresh your memory of the vocabulary and structures you used most.
  2. As you work through this chapter, notice which structures feel automatic and which ones need more review. Nothing in this chapter is new; it is designed to confirm what you can already do.

Core Vocabulary — Aktuelles und Ziele

This chapter's vocabulary supports personal updates, current activities, and near-future goals. All items below are review or natural extensions of Volume I vocabulary.

Personal Update

  • der Umzug, die Umzüge — the move (of residence)
  • die Veränderung, die Veränderungen — the change
  • seit + dative — since / for (a period of time)
  • immer noch — still
  • nicht mehr — no longer

Studies and Work

  • das Praktikum, die Praktika — the internship
  • der Abschluss, die Abschlüsse — the degree, completion
  • die Erfahrung, die Erfahrungen — the experience
  • vor kurzem — recently
  • fertig sein mit + dative — to be finished with

Goals and Plans

  • das Ziel, die Ziele — the goal
  • vorhaben (separable) — to plan, intend
  • sich verbessern — to improve (oneself)
  • dieses Semester / dieses Jahr — this semester / this year
  • hoffentlich — hopefully

Classroom Review Language

  • wiederholen — to review, repeat
  • sich erinnern an + accusative — to remember
  • der Unterschied, die Unterschiede — the difference
  • diesmal — this time
  • wie gesagt — as I said

All plural and article information above is active; use it exactly as shown in your own sentences.

Aussprache

Pronunciation Focus

Review: long and short vowels, final devoicing

Before moving into new content, this chapter reviews two priorities from Volume I: the long/short vowel contrast and final devoicing. Compare Ziel (long ie) with Zimmer (short i), and notice that Abschluss ends with a voiceless s-sound even though it is spelled with ss.

Also review word stress in separable-prefix verbs: VORhaben stresses the prefix, while inseparable-prefix verbs such as verBESsern stress the verb stem, not the prefix.

Self-check: reading your own update aloud

Once you have written your personal update later in this chapter, read it aloud and listen for final devoicing on words like Abschluss and Praktikum, and for correct stress on vorhaben and sich verbessern.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns — Diagnostic Review

Review — Case Diagnostic

Nominative, accusative, and controlled dative

Use this diagnostic to confirm your case control before Volume II expands the dative system in Chapter 2. Every active noun should still appear with its gender and plural.

  • Ich habe einen neuen Kurs. I have a new course. accusative direct object
  • Mein Bruder hilft mir oft. My brother often helps me. dative with helfen
  • Ich wohne jetzt in einer kleinen Wohnung. I now live in a small apartment. dative, fixed location
    • Grammar Review — The Perfekt with haben and sein

    Review auxiliary choice: motion and change-of-state verbs (fahren, gehen, werden) take sein; most other verbs take haben. In this chapter, use the Perfekt to describe what has changed since Volume I ended.

    • Ich bin umgezogen. — I moved (residence).
    • Ich habe mein Praktikum beendet. — I finished my internship.
    • Wir haben uns lange nicht gesehen. — We haven't seen each other in a long time.
    Baustein — Modal Bracket Review
    Ich möchte dieses Semester mein Deutsch verbessern.
    modal (finite, position 2) ... time expression ... object ... infinitive (clause-final)
    Ich will nächstes Jahr nach Berlin ziehen.
    I want to move to Berlin next year.
    Review — Verb-Second Order with a Fronted Time Expression
    Dieses Semester habe ich schon viel gelernt
    "This semester I have already learned a lot." — the finite verb stays in position two even when a time expression opens the sentence.

    Diagnostic Practice

    1. Combine the following into one sentence with correct case: ich / haben / ein neu- Zimmer.
    2. Rewrite starting with Letztes Jahr: Ich habe viel gearbeitet.
    3. Build a sentence with a modal bracket: ich / wollen / mein Deutsch / verbessern.
    4. Form a yes/no question from: Du bist umgezogen.
    5. Form a question-word question asking where someone lives now.

    Model Dialogue

    Mia and Anna catch up before the first class of the new term.

    Mia und Anna treffen sich vor dem Kurs.

    Anna: Mia! Schön, dich zu sehen. Was ist bei dir neu? Mia! Good to see you. What's new with you?
    Mia: Ziemlich viel, ehrlich gesagt. Ich bin letzten Monat umgezogen und wohne jetzt in einer kleinen Wohnung in der Nähe der Uni. Quite a lot, honestly. I moved last month and now live in a small apartment near the university.
    Anna: Das ist ja praktisch! Und wie war dein Sommer sonst? That's convenient! And how was your summer otherwise?
    Mia: Ich habe viel gearbeitet, aber ich habe auch mein erstes Praktikum beendet. Es war anstrengend, aber ich habe viel gelernt. I worked a lot, but I also finished my first internship. It was tiring, but I learned a lot.
    Anna: Und hast du schon Pläne für dieses Semester? And do you already have plans for this semester?
    Mia: Ja, ich möchte mein Deutsch verbessern und vielleicht ein Praktikum in Österreich machen. Und du? Ist es wahr, dass du nach Berlin ziehen willst? Yes, I want to improve my German and maybe do an internship in Austria. And you? Is it true that you want to move to Berlin?
    Anna: Genau, das ist mein Ziel. Ich habe schon eine Wohnung gefunden, aber ich muss noch viel organisieren. Exactly, that's my goal. I've already found an apartment, but I still have a lot to organize.

    Comprehension — Model Dialogue

    1. Was ist bei Mia neu?

    2. Wie war Mias Sommer?

    3. Was ist Mias Ziel für dieses Semester?

    4. Was hat Anna schon organisiert, und was fehlt noch?

    5. Find one example each of a Perfekt form and a modal verb bracket in the dialogue.

    Listening Practice

    Ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the following script at a natural pace. Listen once for the general picture, then again for the details.

    Lukas' Update (Listening Script)

    Hallo zusammen! Bei mir hat sich einiges verändert. Ich bin mit meinem Praktikum in Freiburg fertig und wohne jetzt wieder in Düsseldorf. Es war eine gute Erfahrung, aber ich bin froh, wieder zu Hause zu sein.

    Dieses Semester will ich mich auf die Uni konzentrieren, und ich möchte auch mein Deutsch verbessern, besonders beim Schreiben. Ich habe letztes Jahr viel gesprochen, aber nicht so viel geschrieben.

    Ich hoffe, dass wir uns diesmal öfter treffen können, weil ich Mia und Anna vermisst habe.

    Verständnisfragen

    1. Was hat sich bei Lukas verändert?

    2. Worauf möchte sich Lukas dieses Semester konzentrieren?

    3. Warum hofft Lukas, dass sie sich öfter treffen?

    Writing Workshop — Updated Learning Profile

    This workshop guides you toward the chapter's main writing task: an updated personal and learning profile.

    1. List what has changed. Note two or three things that are different since Volume I (residence, work, studies, routine).
    2. Draft a personal update using at least two Perfekt forms.

    • At least two Perfekt forms with correct auxiliary selection appear.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture and Communication — Wiedersehen nach einer Pause

    Catching up after a break is a common social routine across German-speaking communities, though how much personal detail people share, and how directly, can vary by relationship, region, and individual preference. Some learners may prefer to keep their update brief and factual; others may share more. Both approaches are natural German communication.

    Regional Note — DACH Awareness Continues

    As in Volume I, remember that institutions, vocabulary, and pronunciation may vary across Germany, Austria, Switzerland, Liechtenstein, and Luxembourg. Mia's mention of a possible internship in Austria is a reminder that Volume II will keep labeling DACH variation as it appears.

    Common Mistakes to Review

    Review — Auxiliary choice with umziehen

    Error: *Ich habe umgezogen.

    Correction: Ich bin umgezogen. Umziehen describes a change of location, so it takes sein in the Perfekt.

    Review — Modal bracket word order

    Error: *Ich möchte verbessern mein Deutsch.

    Correction: Ich möchte mein Deutsch verbessern. The infinitive stays at the very end of the clause.

    Review — Dative after helfen

    Error: *Mein Bruder hilft mich.

    Correction: Mein Bruder hilft mir. Helfen takes a dative object, not an accusative object.
