Orte, Wege und Räume

Places, Routes, and Spaces · Speak and Write German: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

Chapter 13 sharpens how you describe where things are and how to get to them. Building on the two-way prepositions introduced in earlier chapters, you will now control the contrast between location and motion more precisely, use the common contracted preposition forms im, am, ins, zum, and zur, recognize prepositional adverbs such as dorthin and da drüben receptively, and expand noun phrases with spatial adjectives. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to describe a route, compare two neighborhoods or spaces, and give clear directions in a familiar setting.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Distinguish location (dative) from motion (accusative) with two-way prepositions in extended contexts.
  2. Use the contracted forms im, am, ins, zum, and zur correctly and naturally.
  3. Recognize common prepositional adverbs such as dorthin, hierher, and da drüben in context.
  4. Use spatial adjectives to describe places, interiors, and routes with more precision.
  5. Read a simple map or written description and follow or give directions.
  6. Write a short comparison of two neighborhoods, buildings, or spaces.

Opening Scenario

Lukas has just moved to a new neighborhood and is trying to find his way around. He asks a neighbor, Frau Berger, for directions to the nearest pharmacy, and later compares his new neighborhood with his old one in a message to a friend. Both situations depend on the same core distinction: is something located somewhere, or is someone moving toward somewhere? German marks this difference clearly through case, and this chapter gives you the tools to control it with confidence.

  1. Work through the vocabulary, grammar, and model dialogue below before attempting the practice tasks.
  2. Pay close attention to whether each preposition phrase answers wo? (where — location) or wohin? (where to — motion).
  3. Notice how contracted forms like zum and zur appear naturally in spoken directions.

Core Vocabulary — Places, Routes, and Spaces

This chapter's vocabulary supports describing places, giving directions, and comparing spaces. Gender and plural forms are included for every active noun.

Orte in der Stadt (Places in Town)

  • die Apotheke, die Apotheken — the pharmacy
  • die Kreuzung, die Kreuzungen — the intersection
  • der Platz, die Plätze — the square, the plaza
  • die Ecke, die Ecken — the corner
  • das Viertel, die Viertel — the neighborhood, district

Innenräume (Interiors)

  • der Eingang, die Eingänge — the entrance
  • der Flur, die Flure — the hallway
  • das Stockwerk, die Stockwerke — the floor, story
  • der Aufzug, die Aufzüge — the elevator
  • die Treppe, die Treppen — the staircase

Wege und Richtungen (Routes and Directions)

  • der Weg, die Wege — the way, path
  • die Richtung, die Richtungen — the direction
  • geradeaus — straight ahead
  • abbiegen — to turn (off)
  • gegenüber (von) — across from, opposite

Raumbeschreibung (Describing Spaces)

  • ruhig — quiet
  • zentral — central
  • barrierefrei — accessible, step-free
  • hell — bright, light-filled
  • eng — narrow, cramped

Accessibility note: barrierefrei is a practical, everyday word in German-speaking contexts and appears on signs, maps, and transit information. It describes step-free or obstacle-free access, not a general judgment about a place's quality.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns — Location, Motion, and Space

Chapter 13 develops the two-way prepositions you have already met and adds the everyday tools that make directions and spatial description sound natural: contracted forms, prepositional adverbs, and spatial adjectives.

Two-Way Prepositions: Location versus Motion

Two-way prepositions such as in, an, auf, vor, hinter, neben, zwischen, and über take the dative case when they describe a fixed location (answering wo?) and the accusative case when they describe movement toward a destination (answering wohin?). This chapter develops the contrast in longer, more connected descriptions rather than isolated sentences.

QuestionCaseExampleMeaning
Wo?DativDie Apotheke ist in der Nähe.The pharmacy is nearby.
Wohin?AkkusativIch gehe in die Stadt.I'm going into town.
Wo?DativDas Café ist an der Ecke.The café is on the corner.
Wohin?AkkusativWir fahren an den See.We're driving to the lake.

Contracted Prepositions

Certain preposition-article combinations are regularly contracted in standard spoken and written German. This chapter activates im (in dem), am (an dem), ins (in das), zum (zu dem), and zur (zu der).

  • Die Bibliothek ist im zweiten Stock. The library is on the second floor.
  • Wir treffen uns am Bahnhof. We're meeting at the train station.
  • Gehen Sie ins Rathaus, dann rechts. Go into the town hall, then right.
  • Wie komme ich zum Marktplatz? How do I get to the market square?
  • Der Weg zur Apotheke ist ganz einfach. The way to the pharmacy is quite simple.

    • Contraction is standard and expected in these frequent combinations. The uncontracted forms (in dem, an dem, zu dem, zu der) are grammatically possible but sound unusually formal or emphatic in everyday speech.

    Prepositional Adverbs (Receptive)

    German often replaces a preposition plus a place already mentioned with a prepositional adverb. This chapter introduces the pattern receptively: you should recognize and understand these forms, but you are not required to produce the full system independently yet.

  • Das Museum? Gehen Sie dorthin, es ist nicht weit. The museum? Go there, it's not far.
  • Kommen Sie hierher, bitte. Come here, please.
  • Die Bäckerei ist da drüben. The bakery is over there.

    • Spatial Adjective Phrases

    Attributive adjectives describing a place follow the adjective-ending patterns already active from earlier chapters. This chapter recycles that system in spatial description rather than introducing new endings.

    Raumbeschreibung — Describing a Space
    Das neue Viertel ist ruhiger als das alte.
    Attributive adjective (neue) + predicate comparative (ruhiger als) — both recycle earlier systems in a spatial context
    Die helle Wohnung im dritten Stock hat einen barrierefreien Zugang.
    The bright apartment on the third floor has step-free access.
    1. Complete with the correct case: Die Post ist neben d___ Supermarkt. (location)
    2. Complete with the correct case: Wir gehen neben d___ Supermarkt vorbei. (motion, "past")
    3. Contract the phrase: Der Weg zu der Bibliothek ist kurz. → Der Weg ___ Bibliothek ist kurz.

    Model Dialogue

    Context: Lukas asks his neighbor, Frau Berger, for directions to the pharmacy. Informal-to-neutral register, new neighbors getting acquainted.

    Lukas:
    Entschuldigung, wissen Sie, wie ich zur Apotheke komme? Excuse me, do you know how I get to the pharmacy?
    Frau Berger:
    Klar. Gehen Sie hier geradeaus bis zur Kreuzung, dann links. Sure. Go straight ahead here to the intersection, then left.
    Lukas:
    Und dann? Ist die Apotheke direkt an der Ecke? And then? Is the pharmacy right on the corner?
    Frau Berger:
    Fast. Sie ist gegenüber vom Supermarkt, im Erdgeschoss von einem kleinen Gebäude. Almost. It's across from the supermarket, on the ground floor of a small building.
    Lukas:
    Super, vielen Dank. Das neue Viertel ist übrigens sehr ruhig. Great, thanks so much. The new neighborhood is very quiet, by the way.
    Frau Berger:
    Ja, und alles ist ziemlich zentral. Zum Bahnhof sind es nur zehn Minuten zu Fuß. Yes, and everything is pretty central. It's only a ten-minute walk to the train station.

    Guided Practice

    1. Choose location or motion and supply the correct article: Das Buch liegt auf d___ Tisch. / Ich lege das Buch auf d___ Tisch.
    2. Rewrite with a contracted preposition: Wie komme ich zu dem Rathaus?
    3. Complete with a spatial adjective of your choice: Die Wohnung ist sehr ___, aber der Flur ist ziemlich ___.
    4. Give directions from your house to the nearest pharmacy or supermarket in three or four sentences.

    Reading Practice

    Read this short neighborhood comparison, then answer the questions that follow.

    Zwei Viertel im Vergleich

    Mein altes Viertel lag mitten in der Stadt. Es war praktisch, weil alles in der Nähe war, aber auch ziemlich laut, besonders am Wochenende. Mein neues Viertel liegt etwas außerhalb, aber es ist viel ruhiger. Die Wohnung ist heller als meine alte, und der Eingang ist barrierefrei, was für meine Großmutter wichtig ist, wenn sie zu Besuch kommt.     Schreiben

    Writing Workshop — Comparing Two Neighborhoods

    This task asks you to write a short comparison of two neighborhoods, buildings, or spaces, using this chapter's location/motion contrast, contracted prepositions, and spatial adjectives.

    1. Choose two spaces. Pick two neighborhoods, apartments, or buildings you know or can reasonably imagine.
    2. List three or four points of comparison. Consider noise, distance to amenities, accessibility, light, and atmosphere.
    3. Write your comparison. Use five to seven sentences, including at least one two-way preposition in a location sense, one in a motion sense, and at least one contracted preposition.
    4. Describe at least one space with a spatial adjective. Use a predicate or attributive form as appropriate.
    5. Revise using the checklist below.

    Checkliste — Editing Checklist:

    • Location and motion are each expressed with the correct case at least once.
    • At least one contracted preposition (im, am, ins, zum, zur) is used correctly.
    • At least one spatial adjective describes a place accurately.
    • All nouns are capitalized; umlauts and ß are used correctly.
    • The comparison avoids treating one type of neighborhood (urban, rural, or suburban) as automatically better.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture and Communication — Giving Directions

    Directions in German-speaking contexts are commonly given using landmarks, turn instructions, and approximate walking times rather than street-by-street formality. Many public buildings, transit stops, and cultural venues indicate accessibility information, including step-free access, directly on signage and websites, and asking about this information is a normal and expected part of planning a visit rather than an unusual request.

    Regional Note — Urban, Suburban, and Rural Spaces

    German-speaking communities include dense city centers, quieter suburbs, and rural villages, each with different everyday spatial vocabulary and different expectations about walking distance, public transit, and car use. A neighborhood described as zentral in a large city may look very different from a central area in a small town, so this label should always be understood in relation to its specific context rather than as a fixed standard.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Confusing location and motion case

    Error: *Ich gehe in der Stadt.

    Correction: Ich gehe in die Stadt. Motion toward a destination takes the accusative, even though in der Stadt is correct for describing a fixed location.

    Skipping the contracted form

    Error: *Wie komme ich zu der Bahnhof?

    Correction: Wie komme ich zum Bahnhof? Der Bahnhof is masculine, so the dative form after zu is zu dem, which contracts to zum in ordinary usage.

    Treating gegenüber like a two-way preposition

    Error: *Die Apotheke ist gegenüber den Supermarkt.

    Correction: Die Apotheke ist gegenüber dem Supermarkt. Gegenüber always takes the dative, regardless of whether the sentence describes location or motion.

    4. Sample: Geh geradeaus bis zur Ampel, dann rechts. Die Apotheke ist auf der linken Seite, gegenüber vom Park.
