Meinungen begründen

Reasons and Opinions · Speak and Write German: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 16 opens Unit IV. Having spent Unit III narrating experience, giving advice, describing places, and explaining procedures, you now turn to a new communicative task: stating an opinion and giving a reason for it. This chapter does not introduce a new sentence-bracket system. Instead, it consolidates a system you have already met in pieces — subordinate clauses with weil, dass, and wenn — and develops three connectors that let you link your own sentences together when you reason out loud or in writing: deshalb, trotzdem, and außerdem. You will also sharpen the contrast between nicht and kein in the context of disagreeing with someone.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State an opinion clearly and support it with a reason using a weil-clause or a deshalb-sentence.
  2. Report what someone thinks or believes using dass-clauses after verbs such as glauben, finden, and meinen.
  3. Introduce a condition on an opinion using a wenn-clause.
  4. Connect two related ideas with deshalb (therefore), trotzdem (nevertheless), and außerdem (furthermore), correctly placing the finite verb after each connector.
  5. Agree, disagree, and partially agree with another person's opinion, using nicht and kein accurately in disagreement.
  6. Read a short opinion text and identify its main claim, its reasons, and its connectors.
  7. Write a short, organized paragraph that states and supports a personal opinion on a familiar topic.

Opening Scenario

The residents' council in Lukas's neighborhood is discussing whether to close a small street to car traffic on weekends and turn it into a pedestrian zone with space for a market. Some neighbors are strongly in favor; others are concerned about deliveries and parking. Lukas has been asked to summarize a few opinions for the next meeting, and he needs to explain not just what people think, but why — and how strongly they feel about it.

  2. Work through the model dialogue, which shows two neighbors disagreeing respectfully.
  3. Complete the listening, reading, speaking, and writing tasks, which build toward the chapter's performance task: a short spoken and written opinion of your own.

Grammatik

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Consolidating weil, dass, and wenn

You have already met weil, dass, and wenn clauses in earlier chapters. This chapter consolidates them: you are now expected to use all three accurately and independently when you give and support an opinion, rather than relying on formulaic frames.

Grammar Focus — The Subordinate Verb-Final Pattern

All three conjunctions send the finite verb to the end of their clause. The main clause keeps normal verb-second order; the subordinate clause is verb-final. A comma always separates the two clauses.

  • weil (because) — gives a reason: Ich finde die Fußgängerzone gut, weil die Straße dann ruhiger ist.
  • dass (that) — introduces a reported thought or belief: Ich glaube, dass viele Nachbarn zufrieden wären.
  • wenn (if/when) — introduces a condition: Wenn es zu viele Beschwerden gibt, sollte der Stadtrat den Plan noch einmal prüfen.

When the sentence begins with the subordinate clause, the whole clause counts as the first element, and the finite verb of the main clause comes directly after the comma: Wenn es zu viele Beschwerden gibt, sollte der Stadtrat... — note that sollte immediately follows the comma.

Building Block — Opinion Plus Reason
Ich finde [Meinung], weil [Grund].
main clause (V2) + comma + subordinate clause (verb-final)
Ich finde den Vorschlag gut, weil er den Verkehr reduziert.
I think the proposal is good because it reduces traffic.

Connectors: deshalb, trotzdem, außerdem

These three words connect two separate main clauses. Unlike weil, dass, and wenn, they do not create a subordinate clause — they simply occupy the front position of a new main clause, which triggers verb-second inversion.

ConnectorMeaningWord Order Effect
deshalbtherefore, for that reasonOccupies position one; finite verb comes second, then the subject.
trotzdemnevertheless, despite thatOccupies position one; finite verb comes second, then the subject.
außerdemfurthermore, in additionOccupies position one; finite verb comes second, then the subject.
  • Die Straße ist eng. Deshalb ist sie für Fußgänger nicht sehr sicher. The street is narrow. Therefore it isn't very safe for pedestrians. deshalb + finite verb (ist) + subject (sie)
  • Viele Nachbarn sind dagegen. Trotzdem unterstützt der Stadtrat den Plan. Many neighbors are against it. Nevertheless, the council supports the plan.
  • Der Markt bringt mehr Leben in die Straße. Außerdem können kleine Geschäfte davon profitieren. The market brings more life to the street. Furthermore, small shops can benefit from it.

Learners commonly confuse these connectors with subordinating conjunctions and send the verb to the end. Watch for this in the Common Mistakes section below.

Refining nicht and kein in Disagreement

Disagreement often requires precise negation. Use kein to negate a noun that would otherwise take an indefinite article or no article at all; use nicht to negate a verb, an adjective, a specific noun with a definite article, or an entire statement.

  • Das ist kein guter Vorschlag. That is not a good proposal. kein negates the indefinite noun phrase "ein guter Vorschlag"
  • Ich finde den Plan nicht überzeugend. I don't find the plan convincing. nicht negates the predicate adjective "überzeugend"
  • Das stimmt so nicht ganz. That's not quite right. nicht softens and partially negates the whole statement

    • Partial disagreement is common and polite in everyday German. Phrases like das stimmt teilweise (that's partly true) or einerseits... andererseits... (on the one hand... on the other hand...) let you qualify a claim instead of flatly rejecting it.

    Modelldialog

    Model Dialogue

    Setting: Two neighbors, Frau Berger and Herr Yilmaz, discuss the pedestrian-zone proposal at a residents' meeting. They use Sie, as is typical for this kind of formal neighborhood setting.

    Frau Berger
    Ich finde den Vorschlag sehr gut, weil die Straße am Wochenende viel ruhiger wäre.I think the proposal is very good because the street would be much quieter on weekends.
    Herr Yilmaz
    Das sehe ich teilweise anders. Für mich ist das kein einfaches Thema, weil ich mein Geschäft in dieser Straße habe.I see it a bit differently. For me this isn't a simple issue, because my shop is on this street.
    Frau Berger
    Das verstehe ich. Aber ich glaube, dass ein Markt auch neue Kunden bringen könnte. Außerdem gibt es dann mehr Platz für Fußgänger.I understand that. But I believe that a market could also bring new customers. Furthermore, there would be more room for pedestrians.
    Herr Yilmaz
    Da haben Sie recht. Trotzdem mache ich mir Sorgen um die Lieferungen. Wenn die Straße gesperrt ist, wie kommen die Lastwagen zu meinem Geschäft?You're right about that. Still, I'm worried about deliveries. If the street is closed, how do the trucks get to my shop?
    Frau Berger
    Das ist keine schlechte Frage. Vielleicht sollten wir vorschlagen, dass Lieferungen morgens noch erlaubt sind.That's not a bad question. Maybe we should propose that deliveries are still allowed in the mornings.

    Notice that both speakers keep a consistent Sie register throughout, use weil, dass, and wenn for reasoning, and use außerdem and trotzdem to connect their sentences.

    Sprechen

    Speaking Practice

    Aktivität: Eine Meinung austauschen (Exchange an Opinion)

    Work with a partner. Choose a familiar topic from the list below, or use the pedestrian-zone scenario from this chapter. One person states an opinion and supports it; the other responds by agreeing, disagreeing, or partially agreeing, and gives their own reason.

    Partner A — Meinung geben (State an Opinion)

    State your opinion on the topic using ich finde, ich glaube, or meiner Meinung nach. Support it with a weil-clause. Add a second point using außerdem.

    • Use at least one dass-clause somewhere in the conversation.

    Partner B — Reagieren (Respond)

    Respond with agreement, disagreement, or partial agreement. Use trotzdem to introduce a contrasting point, and be precise about nicht versus kein if you disagree.

    • Use einerseits... andererseits... at least once if you partially agree.

    Themenvorschläge (suggested topics): Sollten Schulen mehr Hausaufgaben geben? Sollte man mehr von zu Hause arbeiten? Sind soziale Medien gut oder schlecht für junge Leute?

    Bewertungshinweise — Speaking Criteria

    • Clear statement of an opinion with a supporting reason (weil-clause).
    • At least one accurate dass-clause reporting a belief or thought.
    • At least one connector — deshalb, trotzdem, or außerdem — with correct verb-second word order.
    • Accurate use of nicht or kein in any disagreement.
    • Respectful tone, with room for partial agreement rather than only flat agreement or disagreement.

    Solo alternative: Write both sides of the exchange yourself, then read the exchange aloud, taking each role in turn.

    Ein Kommentar im Nachbarschaftsforum (Forum Post)

    Ich finde die geplante Fußgängerzone eine gute Idee, weil unsere Straße im Moment viel zu voll mit Autos ist. Meiner Meinung nach würde ein Markt am Wochenende das Viertel lebendiger machen.     Schreiben

    Writing Workshop — A Short Supported Opinion

    This task asks you to write a short, organized paragraph stating and supporting your own opinion on a familiar topic — the central writing skill of this chapter.

    1. Choose a topic. Use the pedestrian-zone scenario, one of the speaking-practice topics, or another familiar issue from your own life (school, work, media, community).
    2. State your opinion. Open with ich finde, ich glaube, or meiner Meinung nach, and support it with a weil-clause.
    3. Add supporting detail. Use a dass-clause to report a belief, and außerdem to add a second point.
    4. Acknowledge another perspective. Use trotzdem or einerseits... andererseits... to show you understand a different view, and be precise with nicht or kein if you disagree with part of it.
    5. Revise using the checklist below.

    Checkliste — Editing Checklist:

    • The paragraph opens with a clear, single main opinion.
    • At least one weil-clause and one dass-clause appear and are correctly verb-final.
    • At least one connector (deshalb, trotzdem, or außerdem) appears with correct verb-second order.
    • nicht and kein are used correctly wherever negation appears.
    • The paragraph acknowledges at least one other perspective respectfully.
    • All nouns are capitalized; umlauts and ß are used correctly.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture and Communication — Disagreeing Respectfully

    In many German-speaking public and institutional settings, directly stating a differing opinion is not considered impolite in itself — but phrases such as das sehe ich anders, das stimmt teilweise, or einerseits... andererseits... are common ways to disagree while still acknowledging the other person's point. How direct or indirect this exchange feels can vary by region, relationship, generation, and setting, so treat any single style as one common pattern rather than a fixed rule for all German speakers.

    Regional Note — Community Decision-Making

    Local processes for neighborhood decisions — residents' councils, public surveys, city-council meetings — differ across Germany, Austria, and Switzerland, and even between cities within the same country. This chapter's scenario reflects one common style of local consultation, not a fixed model that applies everywhere in the DACH region.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Treating deshalb/trotzdem/außerdem like weil

    Error: *Die Straße ist eng, deshalb sie oft gefährlich ist.

    Correction: deshalb, trotzdem, and außerdem are not subordinating conjunctions — they do not send the verb to the end. Use verb-second order instead: Die Straße ist eng. Deshalb ist sie oft gefährlich.

    Confusing nicht and kein

    Error: *Das ist nicht guter Plan.

    Correction: An indefinite noun phrase is negated with kein, not nicht: Das ist kein guter Plan. Use nicht to negate the adjective or verb instead, if that is your intended meaning: Der Plan ist nicht gut.

    Forgetting the comma before weil, dass, and wenn

    Error: *Ich finde die Idee gut weil sie sinnvoll ist.

    Correction: A comma always separates the main clause from a subordinate clause introduced by weil, dass, or wenn: Ich finde die Idee gut, weil sie sinnvoll ist.
