Wiederholung 3: Beruflich und gesellschaftlich handeln

Unit III Assessment · Speak and Write German: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression · Volume III, Chapter 15

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 15 is the Unit III assessment. It brings together the workplace communication of Chapter 11, the institutional and bureaucratic language of Chapter 12, the intercultural narration of Chapter 13, and the identity and community discussion of Chapter 14 into a single set of scenario-based tasks. Tobias, whose panel preparation with Mira closed the previous chapter, now moves through a connected sequence of professional and civic situations: he listens to a workplace voicemail, reads an official notice, writes a formal email, mediates key information for a friend, and speaks about an intercultural experience with clear framing. This chapter introduces no new grammar. It asks you to draw on formal register, passive and impersonal constructions, genitive phrases in institutional contexts, and Konjunktiv II formulas that Chapters 11 through 14 have already developed, and to use them together under normal discourse pressure.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Understand a workplace or institutional message and identify its key requirements.
  2. Interpret an official notice, including passive and genitive constructions, accurately.
  3. Write a formal email that requests clarification or information appropriately.
  4. Mediate the key content of a formal or official text for someone who has not read it.
  5. Narrate an intercultural incident and discuss identity or community experience with respectful, personal framing.
  6. Select register, tone, and structure appropriately across workplace, institutional, intercultural, and community contexts.

Opening Scenario

The week after his panel discussion, Tobias has a full schedule: a voicemail from his supervisor about a deadline, an official notice from his university's international office, an email he needs to send requesting clarification, and a conversation with a friend, Selin, who has just arrived from abroad and needs help understanding a notice she received. That evening, Tobias also tells Selin about something that happened to him when he first moved to his current city, to help her feel less alone in adjusting to a new place. This chapter asks you to move through a similar sequence of tasks, drawing on everything you have developed across Unit III.

  1. Review the Unit III Systems Review and Vocabulary Review.
  2. Complete the Listening, Reading, Formal Email, and Mediation tasks in order.
  3. Complete the Speaking Assessment.
  4. Finish with the Practical Performance Task, which integrates all components.

Vocabulary Review

The vocabulary below is drawn from Chapters 11 through 14 and will be needed for the tasks that follow.

Arbeitsplatz

  • die Frist, die Fristen — deadline
  • die Besprechung, die Besprechungen — meeting
  • der Bericht, die Berichte — report
  • etwas erledigen — to take care of / complete something

Institutionen

  • der Antrag, die Anträge — application, request
  • die Bescheinigung, die Bescheinigungen — certificate, confirmation
  • die Frist der Einreichung — the submission deadline (genitive phrase)
  • etwas einreichen — to submit something

Interkulturelles

  • das Missverständnis, die Missverständnisse — misunderstanding
  • sich zurechtfinden — to find one's way, to get oriented
  • klären — to clarify
  • am Anfang — at the beginning

Identität und Gemeinschaft

  • die Zugehörigkeit — belonging (usually singular)
  • die Perspektive, die Perspektiven — perspective
  • in meinem Fall — in my case
  • das gilt nicht für alle — that doesn't apply to everyone

All active nouns above appear with article and plural, consistent with the vocabulary standard established since Volume I.

Hören — Aufgabe 1

Listening Assessment

Ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the following voicemail aloud. Listen once for the overall content, then again for specific details.

Anruf in der Arbeit — Listening Script

Hallo Tobias, hier ist Frau Ecker. Ich wollte kurz Bescheid geben, dass der Bericht, über den wir letzte Woche gesprochen haben, bis Donnerstagnachmittag fertig sein muss, weil er am Freitag der Geschäftsleitung vorgestellt wird. Wenn Sie Fragen zu den Zahlen haben, können Sie sich gern an Herrn Berger wenden, der die Daten zusammengestellt hat. Es wäre auch hilfreich, wenn Sie mir kurz eine E-Mail schreiben könnten, sobald der Bericht fertig ist, damit ich ihn noch einmal durchsehen kann. Vielen Dank und einen schönen Tag noch.

Verständnisfragen

1. Bis wann muss der Bericht fertig sein, und warum?

2. An wen kann sich Tobias wenden, wenn er Fragen zu den Zahlen hat?

3. Was soll Tobias tun, sobald der Bericht fertig ist?

4. Identify one Konjunktiv II form in the script and explain why it is used here rather than a simple statement.

Lesen — Aufgabe 2

Reading Assessment

Selin has received the following notice from the university's international office and is not sure what it requires. Read the notice carefully.

Internationales Büro — Mitteilung

Sehr geehrte Studierende, im Rahmen der Anmeldung für das kommende Semester wird von allen internationalen Studierenden eine aktuelle Bescheinigung der Krankenversicherung verlangt. Die Bescheinigung muss bis zum Ende der Einreichungsfrist am Formular angehängt und im Studierendenportal hochgeladen werden. Anträge, die nach der Frist der Einreichung eingehen, können in diesem Semester nicht mehr berücksichtigt werden. Bei Fragen zur Bescheinigung kann das Büro der Studienberatung kontaktiert werden.

Verständnisfragen

1. Was wird von allen internationalen Studierenden verlangt?

2. Was passiert mit Anträgen, die nach der Frist eingehen?

3. An wen kann man sich bei Fragen wenden?

4. Identify one passive construction and one genitive phrase in the notice, and explain what each communicates.

Schreiben — Aufgabe 3

Formal Email Task

Selin is unsure whether her insurance certificate from her home country will be accepted. Write a formal email on her behalf to the Studienberatung, asking whether a foreign certificate is acceptable and what she should do if it is not. Use appropriate formal Sie style, at least one infinitive clause with zu, and a clear, polite closing request.

An: studienberatung@uni-beispiel.de

Betreff: Frage zur Bescheinigung der Krankenversicherung

[Write the email body here.]

Model answer appears in the Answer Key. Other accurate, appropriately formal versions are acceptable.

Mediation — Aufgabe 4

Mediation Task

Selin does not read German confidently yet. Summarize the notice from the Reading Assessment for her in English, clearly, accurately, and without adding information the notice does not contain. Then, in German, write two to three sentences of practical advice for her, using at least one Konjunktiv II form.

Aufgabe

1. Summarize the notice for Selin in English (3–4 sentences).

2. Write 2–3 sentences of advice for Selin in German, using at least one Konjunktiv II form.

Sprechen — Aufgabe 5

Speaking Assessment

Aufgabe: Eine Erfahrung erzählen und einordnen

Work with a partner. One of you narrates a short intercultural incident (real or invented), similar to Tobias's story for Selin; the other listens, asks a clarifying question, and then briefly summarizes what they understood.

Partner A — Erzähler/in

Narrate a short intercultural incident using at least one combined relative/subordinate clause and correct als/wenn distinction.

  • Frame the incident as your own experience, not a general claim.

Partner B — Zuhörer/in

Ask one respectful clarifying question, then summarize the incident, keeping pronoun reference clear.

  • Use at least one polite Konjunktiv II question, such as Könntest du das genauer erklären?

Bewertungshinweise — Speaking Criteria

  • Clear narration framed as personal experience.
  • Accurate als/wenn distinction and at least one combined clause.
  • At least one polite Konjunktiv II form from the listening partner.
  • Clear pronoun and reference use throughout.
  • Accurate, respectful summary by the listening partner.

Solo alternative: Record yourself narrating the incident and summarizing it as if for a friend, checking your clause combinations, tense choice, and pronoun reference.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Formal and Personal Registers Side by Side

A single day of communication in German-speaking contexts often moves between quite different registers: a formal notice from an institution, a workplace voicemail, and a personal conversation about identity or experience with a friend. Recognizing which register a situation calls for, and shifting between them appropriately, is itself a communicative skill this chapter assesses.

Regional Note — Institutional Language Across DACH Countries

Institutional notices, forms, and procedures vary across Germany, Austria, and Switzerland, and even between individual universities, offices, or employers within the same country. The notice and voicemail in this chapter represent one plausible style; always verify current requirements with the actual institution involved rather than assuming a single national procedure.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Using informal register in a formal email

Error: *Hey, kannst du mir sagen, ob meine Bescheinigung okay ist? (too informal for an official request)

Correction: Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren, ich wollte fragen, ob eine ausländische Bescheinigung anerkannt werden kann.

Adding information not present in a notice while mediating

Error: summarizing the notice as saying a specific extension will definitely be granted, when the notice does not state this.

Correction: Summarize only what the notice actually says, and clearly separate any personal advice from the notice's content.

Presenting a personal incident as a general cultural fact

Error: *In diesem Land begrüßt man sich immer so. (states a personal observation as a universal rule)

Correction: In meinem Fall war das so, aber das muss nicht überall gelten.

Chapter Summary

Chapter 15 assessed the integration of Unit III's workplace, institutional, intercultural, and community systems: formal register in workplace and institutional communication, passive and impersonal constructions and genitive phrases in official notices, Konjunktiv II formulas for polite requests and advice, and clear, respectful narration of personal and intercultural experience. No new grammar was introduced; the chapter measured how well these systems work together under ordinary communicative pressure.

Answer Key

Listening Assessment — Comprehension Questions

  1. Der Bericht muss bis Donnerstagnachmittag fertig sein, weil er am Freitag der Geschäftsleitung vorgestellt wird.
  2. Er kann sich an Herrn Berger wenden, der die Daten zusammengestellt hat.
  3. Er soll Frau Ecker eine kurze E-Mail schreiben, sobald der Bericht fertig ist.
  4. Example: "Es wäre auch hilfreich, wenn Sie mir kurz eine E-Mail schreiben könnten" — Konjunktiv II softens the request, making it a polite suggestion rather than a direct command.

Reading Assessment — Comprehension Questions

  1. Eine aktuelle Bescheinigung der Krankenversicherung wird verlangt.
  2. Anträge, die nach der Frist eingehen, können in diesem Semester nicht mehr berücksichtigt werden.
  3. Man kann sich bei Fragen an das Büro der Studienberatung wenden.
  4. Passive example: "wird verlangt" / "muss ... hochgeladen werden" — describes what is required without naming who requires it. Genitive example: "die Frist der Einreichung" — expresses the deadline that belongs to the submission process.

Formal Email Task — Model Answer

Sehr geehrte Damen und Herren, ich schreibe Ihnen im Namen einer Kommilitonin, die kürzlich aus dem Ausland angekommen ist. Sie hat eine Bescheinigung ihrer Krankenversicherung aus ihrem Heimatland und möchte wissen, ob diese anerkannt werden kann oder ob es notwendig ist, eine neue Bescheinigung zu beantragen. Für eine kurze Rückmeldung wären wir sehr dankbar. Mit freundlichen Grüßen, [Name]

Mediation Task — Model Answer

English summary (model): The international office requires all international students to submit a current health insurance certificate, attached to the registration form and uploaded to the student portal, before the submission deadline. Applications that arrive after the deadline cannot be considered this semester. Questions about the certificate can be directed to the student advising office.

German advice (model): Es wäre gut, wenn du die Bescheinigung so schnell wie möglich hochladen würdest. Falls du unsicher bist, ob sie akzeptiert wird, solltest du vorher die Studienberatung kontaktieren.

These integrated skills complete Unit III and prepare you for Unit IV, which turns to argumentation, hypothetical reasoning, and word formation.
