18

فِي المَاضِي وَالآنThen and Now

Using كَانَ to describe how things used to be, and comparing life then and now · Speak and Write Arabic: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Conjugate the hollow verb كَانَ (يَكُون) "to be" in the perfect tense for singular and plural subjects, recycling the hollow-verb pattern from Chapter 17.
  2. Use كَانَ with a following noun or adjective to describe how a person, place, or situation used to be.
  3. Negate كَانَ in the past using لَمْ يَكُن, recycling لَمْ plus the jussive from Chapter 16 and Chapter 17.
  4. Compare a past state with the present using قَدِيمًا, فِي المَاضِي, and أَمَّا الآن.
  5. Read a short passage that compares a place or a daily routine then and now.
  6. Write a connected paragraph of five to seven sentences comparing a topic in the past with the present.
  7. Recycle hollow-verb endings, sequence connectors, and travel and routine vocabulary from Chapters 16 and 17 inside a new comparative context.

Communication Scenario

Nadia is looking through old photographs of her neighborhood with her grandfather, Karim. He remembers what the neighborhood used to be like, and Nadia wants to compare it with how it looks today. This chapter continues Unit IV, التَّجَارِب وَالقِصَص (Experiences and Stories), moving from narrating a single trip in Chapter 17 into describing background states and change over time.

  1. You already know how to narrate a sequence of completed actions using the perfect tense and how a hollow verb such as زَارَ shifts its middle vowel. This chapter studies the most important hollow verb in Arabic, كَانَ, which does not narrate an action but describes a state that used to hold true.
  2. You already know how to negate a completed action with لَمْ plus the jussive. This chapter applies that same pattern to كَانَ, and adds the vocabulary needed to frame a comparison between the past and the present.

التَّرْكِيز النَّحْوِيّ — Grammar Focus

كَانَ and the Past

In Chapter 17 you met زَارَ, a hollow verb whose long middle vowel shortens before a consonant-initial ending. كَانَ follows the very same pattern, and it is the most frequently used hollow verb in Arabic. Instead of narrating a completed action, كَانَ describes a state, quality, or situation that used to hold true, and it is regularly followed directly by a noun or an adjective.

Building Block — Perfect tense of كَانَ (root ك-و-ن, "to be")
Same shortening pattern as زَارَ (Ch. 17): زُرْتُ / كُنْتُ
Subjectزَارَ (Ch. 17)كَانَ
أَنَا (I)زُرْتُكُنْتُ
أَنْتَ / أَنْتِ (you m./f.)زُرْتَ / زُرْتِكُنْتَ / كُنْتِ
هُوَ / هِيَ (he / she)زَارَ / زَارَتْكَانَ / كَانَتْ
نَحْنُ (we)زُرْنَاكُنَّا
هُم (they, m.)زَارُواكَانُوا

The rule from Chapter 17 still governs: the long middle vowel ا shortens to ُ before a consonant-initial ending (كُنْتُ, كُنَّا) and stays long before هُوَ, هِيَ, and هُم (كَانَ, كَانَت, كَانُوا).

كَانَ is regularly followed directly by a noun or adjective describing a past state. It does not require a linking word.

  • كَانَ الحَيّ هَادِئًا قَدِيمًا.The neighborhood used to be quiet long ago.كَانَ + adjective, describes past state
  • كَانَت المَدْرَسَة صَغِيرَة فِي المَاضِي.The school used to be small in the past.كَانَ agrees in gender with the subject
  • كُنَّا نَعِيش فِي بَيْت صَغِير.We used to live in a small house.كَانَ + imperfect verb, expresses habitual past; recognition pattern for this chapter
  • كَانُوا طُلَّابًا فِي هَذِهِ المَدْرَسَة.They used to be students at this school.كَانَ + noun, 3rd pl.
Grammar Note — كَانَ and adjective agreement

The adjective after كَانَ agrees with the subject in gender and number, exactly as it does in an ordinary nominal sentence. كَانَ الحَيّ هَادِئًا uses the masculine singular هَادِئًا because حَيّ is masculine; كَانَت المَدْرَسَة صَغِيرَة uses the feminine صَغِيرَة because مَدْرَسَة is feminine. This is the same agreement system you have used since Chapter 1, applied inside a كَانَ sentence.

  2. هُم ______ أَصْدِقَاء فِي المَدْرَسَة. (they used to be)

Negating كَانَ

You negated a completed action in Chapter 16 with لَمْ plus the jussive, and you saw a hollow verb negated the same way in Chapter 17 with لَمْ أَزُر. كَانَ follows the identical pattern.

Comparing Then and Now

A single كَانَ sentence describes the past. To build a real comparison, pair that past-background sentence with a present-tense sentence introduced by أَمَّا الآن.

Building Block — قَدِيمًا ... أَمَّا الآن
قَدِيمًا + كَانَ-sentence ... أَمَّا الآن + present-tense sentence
قَدِيمًا، كَانَ الحَيّ هَادِئًا. أَمَّا الآن، فَهُوَ صَاخِب جِدًّا.
Long ago, the neighborhood used to be quiet. As for now, it is very noisy.
  • فِي المَاضِي، كَانَت المَدْرَسَة صَغِيرَة. أَمَّا الآن، فَهِيَ كَبِيرَة جِدًّا.In the past, the school used to be small. As for now, it is very big.فِي المَاضِي frame + أَمَّا الآن contrast
  • الحَيّ الآن مُخْتَلِف عَن الحَيّ الَّذِي كَانَ هُنَا قَدِيمًا.The neighborhood now is different from the neighborhood that used to be here long ago.مُخْتَلِف عَن + relative clause with كَانَ, recycled Ch. 4
Grammar Note — Building a comparison paragraph

A well-formed then-and-now paragraph in this chapter typically opens with قَدِيمًا or فِي المَاضِي and a كَانَ sentence, then shifts to the present with أَمَّا الآن and an ordinary present-tense or nominal sentence. The sequence connectors from Chapter 17 — أَوَّلًا, ثُمَّ, بَعْدَ ذَلِك, أَخِيرًا — are not required here, since this chapter contrasts two time periods rather than narrating a sequence of events.

تَدْرِيب — Practice: Complete the comparison: قَدِيمًا، كَانَ _______. أَمَّا الآن، فَهُوَ _______.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Nadia looks through old photographs with her grandfather, Karim.
السِّيَاق: تَنْظُر نَادِيَة إِلَى صُوَر قَدِيمَة مَع جَدِّهَا كَرِيم.

NADIA جَدِّي، كَيْف كَانَ هَذَا الحَيّ قَدِيمًا؟ Grandpa, what was this neighborhood like long ago?
KARIM كَانَ هَادِئًا وَصَغِيرًا جِدًّا. كُنَّا نَعْرِف كُلّ الجِيرَان. It used to be very quiet and small. We knew all the neighbors.
NADIA وَهَل كَانَت هُنَاك مَدَارِس وَمَحَلَّات كَثِيرَة؟ And were there many schools and shops?
KARIM لَا، لَمْ يَكُن هُنَاك إِلَّا مَدْرَسَة وَاحِدَة وَمَحَلّ صَغِير. أَمَّا الآن، فَالحَيّ مُخْتَلِف تَمَامًا: صَاخِب وَكَبِير وَفِيهِ مَحَلَّات كَثِيرَة. No, there was only one school and one small shop. As for now, the neighborhood is completely different: noisy and big, with many shops.
NADIA هَل كَانَ ذَلِك أَفْضَل بِرَأْيِك؟ Was that better, in your opinion?
KARIM كَانَ أَهْدَأ، لَكِنَّ الحَيّ الآن فِيهِ أَشْيَاء لَمْ تَكُن مَوْجُودَة قَدِيمًا. It was quieter, but the neighborhood now has things that did not exist long ago.

أَسْئِلَة الفَهْم — Comprehension questions:

  1. According to Karim, what was the neighborhood like long ago? List two qualities.
  2. Find the لَمْ يَكُن phrase and explain what it negates.
  3. How does the neighborhood now differ from the neighborhood then?

Writing Workshop

In this chapter's writing task, you will write a connected paragraph comparing a place, a routine, or a family situation then and now.

  1. Choose your topic. Decide on a place, routine, or situation you can compare across time.
  2. Open with the past. Write one or two sentences with قَدِيمًا or فِي المَاضِي and كَانَ.
  3. Add one negative statement. Write one sentence using لَمْ يَكُن.
  4. Shift to the present. Write two or three sentences introduced by أَمَّا الآن, using present-tense or nominal sentences.
  5. Close with a comparison. Write one sentence using مُخْتَلِف عَن or نَفْس الشَّيْء.
  6. Draft the paragraph. Write five to seven connected sentences in total.
  7. Revise. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.

قَائِمَة المُرَاجَعَة — Editing checklist:

  • I opened with قَدِيمًا or فِي المَاضِي and a correct form of كَانَ.
  • I used at least one لَمْ يَكُن sentence.
  • I shifted to the present with أَمَّا الآن.
  • My adjectives after كَانَ agree with their subjects in gender.
  • My paragraph reads as one comparison, not two unrelated lists.
  • I wrote all letters and endings with correct right-to-left joining.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Talking with elders about the past

Asking older relatives how a neighborhood, city, or family custom used to be is a common and valued way of learning family and community history across many Arabic-speaking communities, much as it is in many parts of the world. Such conversations often mix nostalgia for what has been lost with appreciation for what has improved, so a balanced then-and-now account, like the one modeled in this chapter, reflects a natural way of discussing change.

Regional Variation — Words for "neighborhood"

The word حَيّ is standard MSA and is broadly understood, but everyday regional speech sometimes uses additional or different terms for a neighborhood or quarter of a city. Treat any such comparison your instructor introduces as receptive and comparative information at this stage, not a substitute for the MSA vocabulary practiced here.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Treating كَانَ like a sound verb

Error: Saying *كَانْتُ, keeping the long vowel before a consonant-initial ending.

Correction: Before an ending that begins with a consonant, the middle radical shortens, exactly as with زَارَ in Chapter 17: كُنْتُ, not *كَانْتُ.

Common Mistake — Forgetting adjective agreement after كَانَ

Error: Writing *كَانَت المَدْرَسَة صَغِير, leaving the adjective in the masculine form after a feminine subject.

Correction: The adjective after كَانَ agrees with the subject exactly as it would in an ordinary sentence: كَانَت المَدْرَسَة صَغِيرَة.

Common Mistake — Negating كَانَ with the wrong pattern

Error: Saying *لَا كَانَ هَادِئًا, using ordinary present-tense negation on a past-tense verb.

Correction: Past-tense negation of كَانَ uses لَمْ plus the jussive, as it does for other verbs: لَمْ يَكُن هَادِئًا.

قَدِيمًا، كَانَ حَيُّنَا هَادِئًا وَصَغِيرًا. كُنَّا نَعْرِف كُلّ الجِيرَان. لَمْ يَكُن هُنَاك مَحَلَّات كَثِيرَة. أَمَّا الآن، فَالحَيّ كَبِير وَصَاخِب. هُوَ مُخْتَلِف تَمَامًا عَن الحَيّ الَّذِي كَانَ هُنَا قَدِيمًا. Other well-formed, accurate answers are equally acceptable.

Cumulative Review — Model responses for Speaker B

ب: أَوَّلًا، رَكِبْنَا القِطَار، ثُمَّ زُرْنَا المَدِينَة القَدِيمَة.
ب: كَانَ الحَيّ هَادِئًا وَصَغِيرًا جِدًّا قَدِيمًا.
ب: لَا، لَيْسَ نَفْس الشَّيْء؛ أَمَّا الآن، فَهُوَ كَبِير وَصَاخِب.

Exact wording will vary. Check that the hollow-verb forms, لَمْ يَكُن negation, and the قَدِيمًا ... أَمَّا الآن frame are used correctly and consistently.

Looking Ahead

مَا الَّذِي تَسْتَطِيعُ فِعْلَهُ الآن — What you can do now

You can describe how a place, routine, or situation used to be using كَانَ, negate a past state with لَمْ يَكُن, and build a clear comparison between the past and the present using قَدِيمًا and أَمَّا الآن. You have extended your narration skills from a connected sequence of events to a structured comparison across time.
