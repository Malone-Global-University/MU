أَحْدَاث المَاضِيCompleted Past Events

Expanding the perfect tense to plural subjects and negating the past with لَمْ and the jussive · Speak and Write Arabic: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use the perfect tense with نَحْنُ, أَنْتُم, and هُم to narrate events involving more than one person.
  2. Recognize dual and feminine plural perfect-tense endings when you meet them in supported reading and listening.
  3. Negate a completed past action using لَمْ with the jussive form of the imperfect verb.
  4. Explain how لَمْ plus the jussive relates in meaning to the plain perfect tense you already know.
  5. Form a negative command using لا with the jussive.
  6. Distinguish the jussive from the indicative and subjunctive imperfect forms you met in Chapters 13 and 14.
  7. Read a short account of a group experience that includes plural perfect verbs and negated past events.
  8. Write a short narrative of four to six sentences describing what a group of people did and did not do.
  9. Recycle directions, plans, advice, and singular perfect narration from Unit III inside a new group storytelling context.

Communication Scenario

The neighborhood party from Chapter 15 has come and gone. Now Tom and Layla are comparing notes about the weekend with two other friends, Nadia and Karim, who both attended. As they talk, they need to describe what the whole group did together, and — just as often — what did not happen. This chapter opens Unit IV, التَّجَارِب وَالقِصَص (Experiences and Stories), which focuses on narrating completed events. It builds directly on the singular perfect tense and relative clauses from Chapter 14 and 15.

  1. You already know how to say what you or someone else, one person at a time, did in the past. This chapter adds the "we," "you all," and "they" forms so you can talk about a group.
  2. You already know how to make a simple present-tense statement negative. This chapter adds a new, very common way to say that something did not happen in the past.

التَّرْكِيز النَّحْوِيّ — Grammar Focus

Perfect Tense — Plural Subjects

In Chapter 14 you learned the perfect tense for "I," "you" (m./f.), "he," and "she." To talk about a group — the four friends comparing notes about the party — you need the plural forms.

SubjectFormStatus
أَنَا (I)وَصَلْتُconsolidated, Ch. 14
أَنْتَ / أَنْتِ (you m./f.)وَصَلْتَ / وَصَلْتِconsolidated, Ch. 14

The perfect stem stays the same; only the ending changes. This is the same pattern you already learned for the singular — the plural endings simply continue it.

  • وَصَلْنَا إِلَى الحَدِيقَة السَّاعَة السَّابِعَة.We arrived at the garden at seven o'clock.1st person plural perfect, new this chapter
  • هَل تَأَخَّرْتُم عَن الحَفْلَة؟Were you all late for the party?2nd person plural perfect, new this chapter
  • رَجَعَ أَصْدِقَائِي إِلَى البَيْت مُتَأَخِّرًا.My friends returned home late.3rd person plural perfect, new this chapter
Grammar Note — Feminine plural and dual stay receptive

The feminine plural (أَنْتُنَّ / هُنَّ) and the dual (أَنْتُمَا / هُمَا) perfect endings are shown here so you can recognize them in reading and listening. You are not expected to produce them independently at this stage of the volume.

Negating the Past with لَمْ

So far you have negated present-tense statements. Arabic has a separate, very common way to say that something did not happen in the past: the particle لَمْ followed by a special form of the imperfect verb called the jussive.

Grammar Note — Two ways to talk about the negative past

Arabic speakers can negate a completed action with مَا plus the perfect tense (which you can recognize receptively), or with لَمْ plus the jussive imperfect. This chapter activates لَمْ + jussive as your productive target, because it is the most common pattern in careful MSA.

  • ذَهَبْتُ إِلَى الحَفْلَة.I went to the party.affirmative perfect, Ch. 14
  • لَمْ أَذْهَب إِلَى الحَفْلَة.I did not go to the party.لَمْ + jussive imperfect, new this chapter
  • لَمْ نَصِل فِي الوَقْت.We did not arrive on time.لَمْ + jussive, 1st person plural
  • لَمْ يَرْجِع أَصْدِقَاؤُنَا مُبَكِّرًا.Our friends did not return early.لَمْ + jussive, 3rd person plural

Notice that لَمْ أَذْهَب and مَا ذَهَبْتُ both mean "I did not go." This chapter asks you to produce the لَمْ pattern; you may recognize the مَا pattern receptively if your instructor introduces it.

تَدْرِيب — Practice: Make each sentence negative using لَمْ.

  1. وَصَلْنَا مُبَكِّرًا. → ______
  2. تَأَخَّرَ كَرِيم. → ______

The Jussive and Negative Commands

The jussive is a mood of the imperfect verb, just like the subjunctive you met in Chapter 13. It appears after لَمْ and also after the negative command particle لا.

Building Block — Indicative, subjunctive, and jussive side by side
Root ذ-ه-ب, imperfect stem -ذْهَب
Environmentأَنَاأَنْتَ
Indicative (plain statement, Ch. 7)أَذْهَبُتَذْهَبُ
Subjunctive (after أَنْ, Ch. 13)أَذْهَبَتَذْهَبَ
Jussive (after لَمْ / لا, new)أَذْهَبْتَذْهَبْ

The three moods often differ only in the final short vowel or its absence. In careful pronunciation the jussive typically drops the final vowel; in ordinary speech the difference may not always be audible, but it is visible in vocalized spelling and controls verb behavior in derived and weak verbs, which you will study in Chapter 17.

  • لا تَتَأَخَّر عَن الحَفْلَة!Don't be late for the party!لا + jussive, negative command, new this chapter
  • لا تَنْسَ العُنْوَان.Don't forget the address.لا + jussive, receptive verb glossed for this item
Grammar Note — لا with the jussive versus general لا

You already know لا as a simple "no" and as a way to negate present-tense statements (Chapters 2 and 9). Here, لا before a jussive-marked verb has a different, specific job: it forms a command telling someone not to do something. Compare it with the affirmative imperative you learned in Chapter 11.

تَدْرِيب — Practice: Turn each affirmative command into a negative command.

  1. اِذْهَب إِلَى اليَمِين. → ______ (tell them not to go right)

Model Dialogue

Setting: The morning after the party, Layla, Tom, Nadia, and Karim compare what happened.
السِّيَاق: فِي صَبَاح اليَوم التَّالِي، يَتَحَدَّث لَيْلَى وَتُوم وَنَادِيَة وَكَرِيم عَمَّا حَدَثَ فِي الحَفْلَة.

LAYLA هَل وَصَلْتُم إِلَى الحَدِيقَة فِي الوَقْت؟ Did you all arrive at the garden on time?
TOM وَصَلْنَا أَنَا وَنَادِيَة فِي الوَقْت، لَكِنَّ كَرِيم تَأَخَّر. Nadia and I arrived on time, but Karim was late.
KARIM لَمْ أَتَأَخَّر كَثِيرًا! لَمْ أَجِد المَكَان بِسُهُولَة فِي البِدَايَة. I wasn't very late! I didn't find the place easily at first.
NADIA فِي المَرَّة القَادِمَة، لا تَنْسَ العُنْوَان يَا كَرِيم! Next time, don't forget the address, Karim!
LAYLA المُهِمّ أَنَّ الجَمِيع اِسْتَمْتَعُوا. هَل رَجَعْتُم مُتَأَخِّرِين؟ The important thing is that everyone enjoyed it. Did you all return late?
TOM لَمْ نَرْجِع مُتَأَخِّرِين جِدًّا، لَكِنَّنَا لَمْ نَنَمْ مُبَكِّرًا أَيْضًا! We didn't return very late, but we also didn't sleep early!
KARIM وَأَنَا لَمْ آكُل شَيْئًا قَبْل الحَفْلَة، فَكُنْتُ جَائِعًا جِدًّا! And I didn't eat anything before the party, so I was very hungry!

أَسْئِلَة الفَهْم — Comprehension questions:

  1. Identify two plural perfect-tense verbs and two examples of لَمْ plus the jussive in the dialogue.
  2. Why was Karim late?
  3. What advice does Nadia give Karim for next time, and what grammatical pattern does she use?

Writing Workshop

In this chapter's writing task, you will write a short narrative describing what a group of people did — and did not do — during a shared event.

  1. Choose your group event. Decide on a real or imagined event involving three or more people.
  2. State what the group did. Write two sentences using plural perfect-tense verbs (نَحْنُ or هُم).
  3. State what did not happen. Write two sentences using لَمْ plus the jussive.
  4. Add one piece of advice for next time. Write one sentence using a negative command with لا.
  5. Draft the paragraph. Write four to six connected sentences in total.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.

قَائِمَة المُرَاجَعَة — Editing checklist:

  • I used at least two plural perfect-tense verbs with correct endings.
  • I used at least two examples of لَمْ plus the jussive.
  • I used at least one negative command with لا.
  • I did not mix future markers with perfect-tense forms.
  • My paragraph is connected, not a list of unrelated sentences.
  • I wrote all letters and endings with correct right-to-left joining.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Recounting shared experiences

Comparing notes about a shared event — who arrived when, what happened, what was missed — is a common everyday conversation across many Arabic-speaking communities, as it is in many parts of the world. The specific expectations around timing and lateness vary considerably by country, city, and social context, so it is worth observing local norms rather than assuming one standard applies everywhere.

Regional Variation — Negating the past

The لَمْ + jussive pattern practiced in this chapter is a hallmark of careful written and spoken MSA. In everyday regional speech, learners will often hear different negation strategies for the past. Treat any such comparison your instructor introduces as receptive and comparative information at this stage, not a substitute for the MSA pattern practiced here.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using لَمْ with the perfect tense

Error: Saying *لَمْ ذَهَبْتُ, combining لَمْ with a perfect-tense verb.

Correction: لَمْ is always followed by the jussive imperfect, never by the perfect: لَمْ أَذْهَب.

Common Mistake — Confusing لا + jussive with the plain present negative

Error: Using لا + jussive to make a plain present-tense statement negative, as if it were the ordinary present negator from Chapter 9.

Correction: لا + jussive is reserved for negative commands: لا تَتَأَخَّر! ("Don't be late!"). An ordinary present-tense statement stays negated the way you learned in Chapter 9.

Common Mistake — Dropping the plural perfect ending

Error: Saying *نَحْنُ وَصَلَ, using the singular perfect form with a plural subject.

Correction: Match the subject to its ending: نَحْنُ وَصَلْنَا. Review the plural perfect table above.

Looking Ahead

مَا الَّذِي تَسْتَطِيعُ فِعْلَهُ الآن — What you can do now

You can narrate what a group of people did using the plural perfect tense, say that something did not happen using لَمْ and the jussive, and tell someone not to do something with a negative command. You have begun Unit IV by moving from individual narration to group narration and by adding a precise new tool for talking about the negative past.

السَّفَر وَالرِّحْلَاتChapter 17: Travel Experiences

Chapter 17 introduces weak verbs — verbs whose roots contain و or ي — along with sequence connectors and source/destination language, so that you can narrate a connected travel story. It builds directly on the perfect tense and jussive patterns you consolidated in this chapter.
