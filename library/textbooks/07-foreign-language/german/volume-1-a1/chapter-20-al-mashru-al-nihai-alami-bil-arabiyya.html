المَشْرُوع النِّهَائِي: عَالَمِي بِالعَرَبِيَّةFinal Assessment: My World in Arabic

An integrated A2 portfolio bringing together listening, speaking, reading, and writing from across the volume · Speak and Write Arabic: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

This chapter introduces no major new grammar system. By the end of it, you will be able to:

  1. Combine nominal and verbal sentences, gender and number agreement, definiteness, adjective agreement, construct state, and pronoun systems from Units I-III inside one connected portfolio project.
  2. Narrate a sequence of past events, describe past background with كَانَ, and add descriptive detail with relative clauses, drawing on Chapters 16 through 19.
  3. Link ideas with cause, result, contrast, purpose, and time connectors built across the volume.
  4. Recognize reported speech introduced by قَالَ إِنَّ / قَالَت إِنَّ, and produce one simple reported-speech sentence as a controlled extension of the recognition pattern introduced in Chapter 19.
  5. Read a short unvocalized or lightly vocalized connected passage and identify its main ideas, sequence, and description.
  6. Understand simple spoken and written data such as basic numbers, dates, and short percentage statements in a personal or community context.
  7. Complete a four-skill capstone project, عَالَمِي بِالعَرَبِيَّة (My World in Arabic), that presents your daily life, community, and one memorable experience.
  8. Self-assess your own reading, writing, listening, and speaking against the A2 outcomes of Volume I.

Communication Scenario

Your Arabic class is holding a small exhibition called عَالَمِي بِالعَرَبِيَّة, "My World in Arabic." Each student prepares a short portfolio about their own life — who they are, their home and daily routine, their community, and one memorable experience — and presents it to visitors who will read, listen, and ask questions. This chapter is not a place to learn new grammar. It is the place where everything from Chapters 1 through 19 comes together into one connected, personal, four-skill project.

  1. You already know how to introduce yourself, describe your family and home, talk about your daily routine, shop and eat, get around a city, talk about leisure, health, study, and work, and narrate a completed trip or memory. This chapter asks you to select and combine that material rather than learn something new.
  2. The portfolio project mirrors real communication: you will listen to a classmate, read a short text, speak about yourself, and write a connected account, exactly as you would in an actual exchange with Arabic speakers.

التَّرْكِيز النَّحْوِيّ — Synthesis Focus

Bringing the Systems Together

This section does not teach a new structure. It shows how the systems from Units I-IV combine inside a single connected paragraph, so you can plan your own portfolio writing with the same structure in mind.

Building Block — A connected personal paragraph (all systems recycled)
background (كَانَ) → sequence (أَوَّلًا … ثُمَّ) → description (الَّذِي/الَّتِي) → link (لِأَنَّ/لِذَلِك/لَكِنَّ) → close (فِي النِّهَايَة)

اِسْمِي كَرِيم. أَسْكُن فِي حَيّ هَادِئ فِيهِ كَثِير مِن الأَشْجَار. عَادَة، أَسْتَيْقِظ مُبَكِّرًا وَأَذْهَب إِلَى الجَامِعَة الَّتِي تَبْعُد عَن بَيْتِي عِشْرِين دَقِيقَة. فِي العَام المَاضِي، سَافَرْتُ مَع أُسْرَتِي إِلَى مَدِينَة سَاحِلِيَّة صَغِيرَة كَانَت هَادِئَة جِدًّا فِي ذَلِك الوَقْت. أَوَّلًا، زُرْنَا السُّوق القَدِيم، ثُمَّ مَشَيْنَا عَلَى الشَّاطِئ. اِسْتَمْتَعْنَا كَثِيرًا لَكِنَّ الجَوّ كَانَ حَارًّا جِدًّا فِي بَعْض الأَيَّام. فِي النِّهَايَة، عُدْنَا إِلَى البَيْت وَنَحْن سُعَدَاء بِالذِّكْرَيَات الجَمِيلَة.

My name is Karim. I live in a quiet neighborhood with a lot of trees. I usually wake up early and go to the university, which is twenty minutes from my house. Last year, I traveled with my family to a small coastal city that was very quiet at that time. First, we visited the old market, then we walked on the beach. We enjoyed it a lot, but the weather was very hot on some days. In the end, we came home happy with the beautiful memories.

Notice how the paragraph moves smoothly between present-tense routine description (Ch. 7), a relative clause (Ch. 4, Ch. 19), past narration (Ch. 16-17), background with كَانَ (Ch. 18), and closing connectors (Ch. 19). Your own portfolio paragraphs should combine systems the same way.

Grammar Note — Choosing what to combine

You do not need to use every system in every sentence. A strong A2 paragraph selects a small number of systems and uses them accurately and naturally, rather than cramming in every grammar point at once. Plan your portfolio around the communicative content first — who you are, your routine, your community, your memory — and let the grammar serve that content.

Reporting What Someone Said

Chapter 19 introduced قَالَ إِنَّ for recognition only. As the closing synthesis of the volume, you may now use one simple, modeled قَالَ إِنَّ / قَالَت إِنَّ sentence in your own writing or speaking, following the pattern below exactly. This is a controlled extension of a recognized pattern, not a new grammar system.

  • قَالَت جَدَّتِي إِنَّ الحَيّ كَانَ هَادِئًا جِدًّا قَدِيمًا.My grandmother said that the neighborhood used to be very quiet long ago.قَالَت + إِنَّ + كَانَ-sentence
  • قَالَ صَدِيقِي إِنَّهُ يُحِبّ مَدِينَتِي كَثِيرًا.My friend said that he likes my city a lot.قَالَ + إِنَّهُ (إِنَّ + attached pronoun)

You are not expected to build reported-speech sentences beyond this single modeled pattern. Full reported-speech production belongs to a later volume.

Numbers and Simple Data

Your portfolio may include a small amount of everyday data about yourself or your community, such as a date, an age, a distance, or a simple proportion. The frames below are recognition-and-light-production support for that purpose; the numeral system itself was fully introduced in Chapters 2, 4, and 8-9 and is only recycled here.

  • وُلِدْتُ فِي سَنَة أَلْفَيْن وَخَمْسَة.I was born in the year two thousand five.recycled numeral + date frame
  • تَقْرِيبًا نِصْف الطُّلَّاب فِي صَفِّي يَتَكَلَّمُون لُغَة ثَانِيَة.About half the students in my class speak a second language.تَقْرِيبًا نِصْف as a simple proportion frame; recognition-level extension, not a full percentage system

Full statistical and percentage language belongs to a later volume; here, simple proportion words such as نِصْف (half) and مُعْظَم (most) are enough to describe your community.

Model Dialogue

Setting: A visitor stops at Huda's exhibition table during the class's عَالَمِي بِالعَرَبِيَّة exhibition.
السِّيَاق: تَقِف زَائِرَة عِنْد طَاوِلَة هُدَى أَثْنَاء مَعْرِض عَالَمِي بِالعَرَبِيَّة.

VISITOR مَرْحَبًا! مَاذَا يُخْبِرُنَا مَشْرُوعُك عَنْك؟ Hello! What does your project tell us about you?
HUDA اِسْمِي هُدَى، وَأَسْكُن فِي حَيّ صَغِير قُرْب الجَامِعَة الَّتِي أَدْرُس فِيهَا. عَادَة، أَسْتَيْقِظ السَّاعَة السَّابِعَة وَأَذْهَب إِلَى الجَامِعَة سَيْرًا عَلَى الأَقْدَام. My name is Huda, and I live in a small neighborhood near the university where I study. I usually wake up at seven and walk to the university.
VISITOR وَمَا هِيَ الذِّكْرَى الَّتِي اِخْتَرْتِهَا لِمَشْرُوعِك؟ And what is the memory you chose for your project?
HUDA فِي العَام المَاضِي، سَافَرْتُ إِلَى قَرْيَة جَدِّي الَّتِي كَانَ يَسْكُن فِيهَا. أَوَّلًا، تَعَرَّفْتُ عَلَى أَقَارِبِي، ثُمَّ زُرْنَا بَيْتَه القَدِيم. قَالَ جَدِّي إِنَّ العَائِلَة عَاشَت فِي ذَلِك البَيْت لِسَنَوَات طَوِيلَة. Last year, I traveled to my grandfather's village where he used to live. First, I met my relatives, then we visited his old house. My grandfather said that the family lived in that house for many years.
VISITOR قِصَّة جَمِيلَة! شُكْرًا عَلَى هَذَا الشَّرْح الوَاضِح. A beautiful story! Thank you for this clear explanation.
HUDA عَفْوًا! أَنَا سَعِيدَة لِأَنَّك اِسْتَمْتَعْتِ بِمَشْرُوعِي. You're welcome! I'm happy that you enjoyed my project.

أَسْئِلَة الفَهْم — Comprehension questions:

  1. Which chapters' systems can you find in Huda's first turn? Name at least two.
  2. Find the relative clause describing the village. What does it add?
  3. Find the قَالَ إِنَّ sentence. What did the grandfather say?

Writing Station

In this station, you will draft the written half of your final portfolio: a connected account of eight to ten sentences covering your identity, your home and routine, your community, and one memorable experience.

  1. Introduce yourself. One or two sentences with your name, age, and origin, recycling Chapters 1-3.
  2. Describe your home and routine. Two or three sentences using present-tense routine verbs and at least one adjective-noun phrase, recycling Chapters 6-9.
  3. Describe your community. One or two sentences about your neighborhood, studies, or work, recycling Chapters 11-14.
  4. Narrate a memory. Two or three sentences in the perfect tense, with a كَانَ background sentence and at least one sequence connector, recycling Chapters 16-18.
  5. Add description and connection. Include one relative clause and one لِأَنَّ, لِذَلِك, or لَكِنَّ sentence, recycling Chapter 19.
  6. Optional reported line. If natural, include one modeled قَالَ إِنَّ / قَالَت إِنَّ sentence from this chapter.
  7. Close your account. End with فِي النِّهَايَة and a closing thought.
  8. Revise. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.

قَائِمَة المُرَاجَعَة — Editing checklist:

  • I introduced myself clearly, with correct gender agreement throughout.
  • I described my home, routine, and community using vocabulary from earlier units.
  • I narrated a memory with the perfect tense and at least one sequence connector.
  • I used one كَانَ background sentence.
  • I used one relative clause, with a resumptive pronoun where needed.
  • I used one cause, result, or contrast connector.
  • I closed with فِي النِّهَايَة.
  • My account reads as one connected text, not a list of separate facts.
  • I wrote all letters and endings with correct right-to-left joining.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Sharing a life story across communities

Introducing yourself through home, routine, community, and a personal memory is a communicative pattern that works across many Arabic-speaking communities, much as it does elsewhere. The specific details of daily life, family structure, and neighborhood life vary widely across North Africa, the Levant, the Gulf, the Nile Valley, and diaspora communities, so treat your own portfolio as one example of a personal account rather than a template for "the" Arabic-speaking experience.

Regional Variation — MSA in a personal portfolio

Your written and prepared spoken portfolio uses Contemporary MSA, the productive standard of this volume. In an actual conversation with Arabic speakers from a specific region, some vocabulary or pronunciation might shift toward local usage; that variation remains receptive and comparative at this stage, and your MSA portfolio is not "wrong" for staying in the standard you have been taught.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Listing facts instead of connecting them

Error: Writing a series of unconnected short sentences, such as اِسْمِي سَامِي. أَسْكُن فِي المَدِينَة. سَافَرْتُ العَام المَاضِي. with no links between them.

Correction: Use connectors, relative clauses, and background sentences to join ideas: اِسْمِي سَامِي، وَأَسْكُن فِي المَدِينَة الَّتِي وُلِدْتُ فِيهَا. فِي العَام المَاضِي، سَافَرْتُ إِلَى قَرْيَة كَانَت هَادِئَة جِدًّا.

Common Mistake — Overloading one sentence with every system at once

Error: Trying to fit a relative clause, a لِأَنَّ clause, and a قَالَ إِنَّ sentence into a single overloaded sentence.

Correction: Spread these systems across separate sentences within your paragraph, as the model paragraph in this chapter does. A clear, well-organized account is stronger than one dense, overloaded sentence.

Common Mistake — Treating this chapter as a place for new grammar

Error: Assuming Chapter 20 will teach new tenses, cases, or verb forms.

Correction: This chapter is a synthesis checkpoint. If you find a gap in a system from an earlier chapter, return to that chapter to review it rather than expecting new material here.

  2. الحَيّ الَّذِي أَسْكُن فِيهِ: it adds that the health project was for the neighborhood where she lives.
  3. The neighbor said that the project changed her daily life.
  4. لِأَنِّي ("because I") introduces the reason for Layla's sense of pride at the end of the passage.

Volume Review — Model responses

Section A: اِسْمِي رَنَا وَأَنَا مِصْرِيَّة. عِنْدِي أَخ وَاحِد اِسْمُه كَرِيم. نَحْن أُسْرَة صَغِيرَة وَسَعِيدَة.

Section B: أَسْكُن فِي شَقَّة صَغِيرَة وَجَمِيلَة. عَادَة، أَسْتَيْقِظ مُبَكِّرًا وَأَشْرَب الشَّاي. بَعْد ذَلِك، أَذْهَب إِلَى العَمَل.

Section C: اِذْهَب مِن بَيْتِي عَلَى طُول هَذَا الشَّارِع، ثُمَّ اِنْعَطِف يَمِينًا عِنْد السُّوق. أَدْرُس الهَنْدَسَة فِي جَامِعَة قَرِيبَة.

Section D: قَدِيمًا، كَانَت عَائِلَتِي تَقْضِي العُطْلَة فِي الجَبَل. فِي العَام المَاضِي، أَوَّلًا رَكِبْنَا القِطَار، ثُمَّ وَصَلْنَا إِلَى قَرْيَة جَمِيلَة يَسْكُن فِيهَا أَقَارِبِي. اِسْتَمْتَعْنَا كَثِيرًا لَكِنَّ الطَّقْس كَانَ بَارِدًا. فِي النِّهَايَة، عُدْنَا وَنَحْن سُعَدَاء جِدًّا.

Other well-formed, accurate answers are equally acceptable throughout this section.
