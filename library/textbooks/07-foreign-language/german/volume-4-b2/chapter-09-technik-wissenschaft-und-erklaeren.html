Technik, Wissenschaft und Erklären

Technology, Science, and Explanation · Speak and Write German: Professional, Academic, and Cultural Communication · Volume IV, Chapter 9

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 9 follows Tobias from budget reports into the technical side of his research institute's work. A colleague from the communications office asks him to help turn a dense internal project overview — describing a new data-monitoring system the institute has developed — into a clear explanation that a general, educated audience can follow. Explaining a technical or scientific process in German calls for a specific set of tools: relative clauses that add precise detail to a noun, passive and process-oriented sentences that foreground what happens rather than who does it, specialized compounds that name components and systems precisely, and clauses of cause, effect, and purpose that connect steps logically. This chapter develops all of these productively for the first time in combination, while continuing the genitive, nominalization, and impersonal man constructions from Chapters 7 and 8. Learners read a technical overview, practice defining terms clearly, and write and present an explanation of a process for a non-specialist but educated audience.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Read a popular-science or technical overview and identify the steps of a process it describes.
  2. Use relative clauses to add precise defining detail to technical nouns.
  3. Use passive and process-oriented sentence patterns to describe how something works.
  4. Recognize and use specialized compounds appropriately, without inventing pseudo-technical terms.
  5. Connect steps in an explanation using cause, effect, and purpose clauses.
  6. Write and present a clear explanation of a technical or scientific process for an educated non-specialist audience.

Opening Scenario

Herr Albrecht from the institute's communications office has read a dense internal project overview describing a new system that automatically monitors and records environmental measurements for one of the institute's research projects. He asks Tobias, who worked on the project, to help him rewrite the overview so that a general audience — for example, readers of the institute's public newsletter — can understand what the system does and why it matters, without needing a background in engineering. Tobias reads through the technical description, works out how to define its key terms clearly, and drafts an explanation that walks a non-specialist through the process step by step.

Wortschatz

Core Vocabulary

This chapter's vocabulary supports reading and explaining a technical process: the system and its components, the data it produces, and the language used to define terms and describe steps.

System und Technik

  • das System, die Systeme — system
  • der Sensor, die Sensoren — sensor
  • die Messung, die Messungen — measurement
  • das Gerät, die Geräte — device, instrument

Daten und Auswertung

  • die Daten (Plural) — data
  • die Auswertung, die Auswertungen — analysis, evaluation
  • die Genauigkeit, die Genauigkeiten — accuracy, precision
  • die Unsicherheit, die Unsicherheiten — uncertainty

Prozess und Ablauf

  • der Vorgang, die Vorgänge — process, procedure
  • der Schritt, die Schritte — step
  • speichern — to save, to store
  • übertragen (überträgt, übertrug, hat übertragen) — to transmit

Erklären und Definieren

  • die Definition, die Definitionen — definition
  • der Begriff, die Begriffe — term, concept
  • bezeichnen (+ Akkusativ) als (+ Akkusativ) — to designate/refer to something as
  • dienen (+ Dativ) zu (+ Dativ) / dazu, ... zu + Infinitiv — to serve (a purpose)

All active nouns appear with article and plural; verbs are shown with their case or preposition frame where one applies.

Aussprache

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Note — Stress in Technical Compounds

Technical compounds such as der Datensensor or die Messgenauigkeit follow the same first-element stress pattern as the organizational compounds in Chapter 8: DAtensensor, MESSgenauigkeit. When explaining a process aloud, place clear stress on the first element of each new technical term the first time you use it, so a listener unfamiliar with the term can register it, and slightly reduce stress on repeated terms later in the explanation.

Grammatik

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter develops three systems together for the first time: relative clauses used to define technical nouns precisely, passive and process-oriented sentences that describe what happens to something rather than who acts, and clauses of cause, effect, and purpose that connect the steps of a process logically. Specialized compounds, nominalization, the genitive, and impersonal man, all active from earlier chapters, continue to develop in this new domain.

Building Block — Defining a Term with a Relative Clause
Nomen + Relativpronomen (Kasus je nach Funktion) + Verb (Ende)
A relative clause lets a writer define a technical noun precisely within the same sentence, instead of adding a separate defining sentence. The relative pronoun's case depends on its function inside the relative clause, not on the case of the noun it refers to.

der Sensor, der die Temperatur misst

the sensor that measures the temperature

die Daten, die das System speichert

the data that the system stores

das Gerät, mit dem die Messungen durchgeführt werden

the device with which the measurements are carried out

Grammar Note — Passive and Process Sentences

Explanations of how something works often foreground the process rather than the person carrying it out: Die Daten werden alle zehn Minuten gespeichert ("The data are stored every ten minutes") keeps attention on the data and the system, not on an unnamed operator. Impersonal man, already active from Chapter 7, remains a natural alternative in more conversational explanation: Man speichert die Daten alle zehn Minuten. Both options are correct; the passive is more common in written technical prose, while man is more common in spoken explanation.

Grammar Note — Cause, Effect, and Purpose in a Process

Connecting the steps of a process clearly requires a small set of subordinating and prepositional patterns: weil and da for cause, sodass for effect, and damit or an infinitive clause with um ... zu for purpose. Um Energie zu sparen, misst der Sensor nur einmal pro Minute ("To save energy, the sensor measures only once per minute") uses a purpose infinitive clause; Der Sensor misst nur einmal pro Minute, damit die Batterie länger hält expresses the same purpose with a full subordinate clause, which is necessary when the subject of the purpose clause differs from the subject of the main clause.

  • Der Sensor, der im Feld installiert ist, überträgt die Messungen automatisch an das System. The sensor, which is installed in the field, transmits the measurements automatically to the system. relative clause (nominative) + specialized compound
  • Die Daten werden zunächst gespeichert und anschließend automatisch ausgewertet, sodass keine manuelle Auswertung nötig ist. The data are first stored and then automatically analyzed, so that no manual analysis is necessary. passive + sodass (effect)
  • Um die Genauigkeit der Messungen zu erhöhen, wird jeder Sensor einmal im Jahr überprüft. To increase the accuracy of the measurements, each sensor is checked once a year. um ... zu (purpose) + passive + genitive
Frequent Confusion — Relative Pronoun Case

Error: *das Gerät, den man benutzt, um die Messungen durchzuführen uses the accusative masculine den after a neuter antecedent, matching neither the antecedent's gender nor tracking the relative pronoun's own function correctly.

Correction: das Gerät, das man benutzt, um die Messungen durchzuführen — the relative pronoun agrees in gender and number with its antecedent (das Gerät is neuter), while its case depends on its own grammatical role inside the relative clause, here the accusative object of benutzt.

Modelldialog

Model Dialogue

Setting: the communications office, one week before the newsletter deadline. Context: Tobias and Herr Albrecht discuss how to explain the new monitoring system to a general readership.

Tobias und Herr Albrecht besprechen die Beschreibung des neuen Messsystems.
Herr Albrecht: Der technische Bericht ist gut, aber für unsere Leser zu kompliziert. Können Sie mir kurz erklären, wie das System funktioniert? The technical report is good, but too complicated for our readers. Could you briefly explain to me how the system works?
Tobias: Gern. Der Sensor, der im Feld installiert ist, misst die Temperatur und die Feuchtigkeit und überträgt die Daten automatisch an das System. Gladly. The sensor, which is installed in the field, measures the temperature and the humidity and transmits the data automatically to the system.
Herr Albrecht: Und was passiert dann mit den Daten? And what happens with the data then?
Tobias: Die Daten werden zunächst gespeichert und anschließend automatisch ausgewertet, sodass keine manuelle Auswertung nötig ist. The data are first stored and then automatically analyzed, so that no manual analysis is necessary.
Herr Albrecht: Sehr gut. Und warum misst der Sensor nur einmal pro Minute und nicht ständig? Very good. And why does the sensor measure only once per minute and not constantly?
Tobias: Um Energie zu sparen. Der Sensor läuft mit Batterie, damit er auch an abgelegenen Orten funktioniert. To save energy. The sensor runs on battery, so that it also works in remote locations.

Hörverstehen

Listening Practice

Ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the following short passage aloud. Listen once for the overall process, then again for the reason given for each step.

Kurzerklärung — Listening Script

Das neue Messsystem funktioniert in drei Schritten. Zuerst misst der Sensor, der im Feld installiert ist, die Temperatur und die Feuchtigkeit. Er misst nur einmal pro Minute, um Energie zu sparen, denn er läuft mit Batterie. Anschließend werden die gesammelten Daten automatisch an das zentrale System übertragen und dort gespeichert. Schließlich wertet das System die Daten aus, sodass die Forscherinnen und Forscher die Ergebnisse direkt am Computer ansehen können, ohne die Messungen von Hand auswerten zu müssen.

Verständnisfragen

1. Was misst der Sensor, und wie oft misst er?

2. Warum misst der Sensor nur einmal pro Minute?

3. Identify the sentence that explains what happens after the data are transmitted, and summarize why manual analysis is not needed.

Sprechen

Speaking Practice

Aufgabe: Ein Verfahren erklären

Working with a partner or recording solo, role-play a short conversation in which one person explains the monitoring system's process to a colleague who is unfamiliar with it and asks clarifying questions.

Rolle: Tobias

  • Explain what the sensor measures and how often.
  • Explain what happens to the data after they are transmitted.
  • Give one reason (cause or purpose) for a design choice in the system.
  • Offer to send a written summary afterward.

Rolle: Kollege/Kollegin (fachfremd)

  • Ask what a specific term means.
  • Ask why one step in the process happens the way it does.
  • Thank Tobias for the clear explanation.

Bewertungshinweise — Speaking Criteria

  • The process is explained in a clear, logical step order.
  • At least one relative clause is used to define a term.
  • At least one passive or process sentence and one cause/effect/purpose clause are used correctly.
  • Technical terms are defined rather than assumed, and no invented pseudo-technical compounds are used.
  • Comprehensible, natural pronunciation and pacing, including compound stress.

Solo alternative: Record both roles yourself, pausing between turns, or record only the Tobias role as a short monologue explaining the process.

Leseverstehen

Reading Practice

Institutsnewsletter — Kurzüberblick für Leserinnen und Leser

Wie das neue Messsystem funktioniert

Unser Institut hat ein neues System entwickelt, das Umweltdaten automatisch sammelt und auswertet. Ein Sensor, der direkt im Feld installiert ist, misst regelmäßig die Temperatur und die Feuchtigkeit der Umgebung. Damit die Batterie möglichst lange hält, misst der Sensor nur einmal pro Minute statt ständig. Die gesammelten Daten werden anschließend automatisch an ein zentrales System übertragen, das sie speichert und auswertet. Man bezeichnet diesen letzten Schritt oft als "automatische Auswertung", weil kein Mensch die Daten mehr von Hand überprüfen muss. Die Genauigkeit der Messungen wird einmal im Jahr überprüft, um sicherzustellen, dass die Ergebnisse zuverlässig bleiben. Kleine Unsicherheiten in den Messungen sind normal und werden im Abschlussbericht immer offen genannt.

Verständnisfragen

1. Was misst der Sensor, und wo ist er installiert?

2. Warum wird die Genauigkeit der Messungen einmal im Jahr überprüft?

3. Identify the sentence about measurement uncertainty, and explain in your own words why the text mentions it.

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Scientific Accuracy and Uncertainty

Good scientific and technical writing acknowledges uncertainty rather than overstating confidence: German popular-science texts commonly use phrases such as ungefähr ("approximately"), man geht davon aus, dass ... ("it is assumed that ..."), or explicit mentions of Unsicherheit ("uncertainty") when reporting measurements or findings. The system, figures, and process described in this chapter are constructed for language practice and are not drawn from a real published study; treat any real technical or scientific question as one to verify with current, qualified sources rather than a textbook example.

Culture and Communication — Avoiding Pseudo-Technical Compounds

Technical and scientific German favors established, verifiable compounds and terms over newly invented ones. When unsure whether a compound such as Messgenauigkeit or a term like Sensor is used the way a native technical text would use it, verify it in a current dictionary or technical reference rather than assembling a plausible-sounding compound from familiar parts.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Relative pronoun agreement with the antecedent, not the following verb

Error: *die Daten, das gespeichert werden uses the neuter singular das after a plural antecedent (die Daten).

Correction: die Daten, die gespeichert werden — the relative pronoun must agree in gender and number with its antecedent; its case then depends on its function inside the relative clause.

Confusing damit and um ... zu

Error: *Der Sensor misst einmal pro Minute, um die Batterie länger hält. mixes the infinitive construction um ... zu with a finite verb.

Correction: Der Sensor misst einmal pro Minute, damit die Batterie länger hält. — use damit with a full subordinate clause when the subject of the purpose clause (die Batterie) differs from the subject of the main clause (der Sensor); reserve um ... zu for purpose infinitives sharing the main clause's subject.

Overusing technical jargon for a general audience

Error: Writing an explanation for the newsletter using unexplained specialist terminology copied directly from the internal technical report.

Correction: Define each technical term the first time it appears, using a relative clause or a brief appositive, so that a non-specialist reader can follow the explanation.

Chapter Summary

Chapter 9 moved learners from organizational reporting into the explanatory, process-oriented register of technical and scientific German. Relative clauses became productive as a tool for defining technical terms precisely within a sentence, while passive and process-oriented sentences, cause/effect/purpose clauses, and specialized compounds combined for the first time to describe how a system works step by step. The genitive, nominalization, and impersonal man, active since Chapters 7 and 8, continued to develop in this new domain. Tobias's task of turning a dense technical overview into a clear explanation for a general, educated audience, without inventing pseudo-technical terms or overstating certainty, connected listening, speaking, reading, and writing around one realistic workplace situation.
