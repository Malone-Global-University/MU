Akademisches Lesen und Diskutieren

Academic Reading and Seminar Discussion · Speak and Write German: Professional, Academic, and Cultural Communication · Volume IV, Chapter 4

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 4 moves from the live negotiation of Chapter 3 into the more reflective world of academic reading. Tobias's project work has drawn the attention of a visiting research group at his company's partner university, and Frau Lindqvist has arranged for him to sit in on a seminar discussion of a short academic-style text related to his field. Before the seminar, he must read the text carefully, identify its thesis, evidence, and method, and prepare to discuss it responsibly — attributing claims to their sources rather than presenting them as his own. This chapter introduces the complex noun phrases and embedded clauses typical of academic prose, the frames needed to attribute a claim to its source, and a first, receptive look at Konjunktiv I, the form German uses to report what someone else has said or written.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Read a short academic-style text and identify its thesis, evidence, and method.
  2. Interpret complex noun phrases and embedded clauses in academic prose.
  3. Attribute a claim to its source using appropriate frames.
  4. Recognize Konjunktiv I forms used to report what a source claims.
  5. Participate in a seminar discussion, agreeing, questioning, and building on others' points.
  6. Write a short summary-response to an academic-style text.

Opening Scenario

A research group from the partner university is visiting to discuss how companies like Tobias's are adapting their internal coordination practices. Before the seminar, Frau Lindqvist sends Tobias a short academic-style article on cross-office collaboration and asks him to prepare two things: an annotated reading with the thesis and key evidence marked, and two or three discussion points he can raise in the seminar. Tobias must be careful to distinguish what the article's author claims from what he himself thinks, and to say clearly when he is reporting someone else's argument rather than stating a fact.

  1. Review the vocabulary for academic disciplines, arguments, methods, and evidence.
  2. Study the model seminar discussion, in which participants attribute claims carefully.
  3. Complete the Listening, Speaking, Reading, and Writing components in order.
  4. Finish by writing your own academic summary-response and preparing discussion points.
Wortschatz

Vocabulary: Academic Reading and Argument

This chapter activates vocabulary for identifying a thesis, describing evidence and method, and attributing claims to their sources.

Die These

  • die These, die Thesen — thesis
  • das Argument, die Argumente — argument
  • der Beleg, die Belege — piece of evidence, support
  • die Schlussfolgerung, die Schlussfolgerungen — conclusion

Die Methode

  • die Methode, die Methoden — method
  • die Untersuchung, die Untersuchungen — study, investigation
  • die Stichprobe, die Stichproben — sample
  • die Einschränkung, die Einschränkungen — limitation (recycled from Chapter 3)

Zitieren und belegen

  • zitieren — to cite, quote
  • sich beziehen auf (+ Akkusativ) — to refer to
  • behaupten — to claim, assert
  • zufolge (+ Dativ, often postposed) — according to

Diskutieren

  • die Diskussion, die Diskussionen — discussion
  • der Standpunkt, die Standpunkte — standpoint, position
  • einwenden — to object, raise an objection
  • anmerken — to remark, note

All active nouns appear with article and plural, and each verb is shown with its case or preposition frame where one is required.

Grammatik

Grammar Focus: Komplexe Nominalphrasen und Quellenangaben

Academic prose packs meaning into dense noun phrases and embedded clauses, and it constantly signals whose claim is being reported. This chapter introduces three tools: reading strategies for complex noun phrases, frames for attributing a claim to a source, and a first, receptive introduction to Konjunktiv I for reported speech.

Building Block — Quellenangaben: Attributing a Claim
Der Autor argumentiert, dass ...  |  ... zufolge ...  |  Laut der Studie ...
These frames make clear that a claim belongs to a source, not to the reader or speaker, which is essential for responsible academic discussion.

Der Autor argumentiert, dass sich die Zusammenarbeit zwischen Standorten grundlegend verändert hat.

The author argues that collaboration between locations has changed fundamentally.

Der Studie zufolge verbessert regelmäßiger Austausch die Entscheidungsfindung.

According to the study, regular exchange improves decision-making.

Laut den Autorinnen ist die Stichprobe relativ klein.

According to the authors, the sample is relatively small.

Grammar Note — Reading Complex Noun Phrases

Academic German often expands a noun phrase with an extended participial modifier before the noun, rather than a relative clause after it. Find the article, find the noun it belongs to, and then read everything between them as a single description.

die von den Autorinnen untersuchte Zusammenarbeit — the collaboration examined by the authors. Article die, noun Zusammenarbeit, and everything in between (von den Autorinnen untersuchte) modifies it.

die in der Studie beschriebenen Herausforderungen — the challenges described in the study. Article die, noun Herausforderungen, modifier in der Studie beschriebenen in between.

A useful reading strategy: locate the final noun first, then work backward through the modifier to reconstruct the meaning.

  • Die Autorin behauptet, dass kleinere Teams schneller Entscheidungen treffen, weist aber auch auf mögliche Nachteile hin. The author claims that smaller teams make decisions faster, but also points to possible disadvantages. behaupten + dass-clause; weist ... hin — separable verb hinweisen auf
  • Die Ergebnisse zeigen, dass regelmäßige Treffen die Zusammenarbeit verbessern, obwohl die Stichprobe klein war. The results show that regular meetings improve collaboration, although the sample was small. dass-clause + obwohl-clause — verb-final order in both
  • Der Studie zufolge lässt sich der Effekt nicht eindeutig auf die Teamgröße zurückführen. According to the study, the effect cannot be clearly traced back to team size. zufolge postposed after its noun phrase; sich lassen + infinitive for "can be"
Grammar Note — Konjunktiv I for Reported Claims (Recognition)

Academic and journalistic German often uses Konjunktiv I to report what someone else has said or written, without the writer taking a position on whether it is true. At this stage, you only need to recognize this form when reading; you are not yet expected to produce it.

Der Autor schreibt, die Zusammenarbeit habe sich grundlegend verändert. — The author writes that collaboration has changed fundamentally. (habe is the Konjunktiv I form of haben, signaling reported content.)

For now, when you see an unfamiliar verb form like habe, sei, or könne in a reporting context, treat it as a signal that you are reading someone else's claim, not a stated fact.

Frequent Confusion — Presenting a Source's Claim as Your Own

Error: *Kleinere Teams treffen schneller Entscheidungen. stated flatly, as if it were the reader's own established fact.

Correction: Die Autorin behauptet, dass kleinere Teams schneller Entscheidungen treffen. — attribute the claim to its source before discussing or evaluating it.

Modelldiskussion

Model Seminar Discussion

Setting: a university seminar room. Context: Tobias and two students discuss the article on cross-office collaboration before the visiting researchers arrive.

Tobias, Frau Ostermann (Doktorandin) und Kilian (Student) besprechen die Studie vor dem Seminar.
Frau Ostermann: Die Autorin behauptet, dass regelmäßiger Austausch zwischen Standorten die Entscheidungsfindung deutlich verbessert. The author claims that regular exchange between locations clearly improves decision-making.
Kilian: Das mag stimmen, aber die Stichprobe zufolge waren es nur acht Unternehmen. Ist das wirklich repräsentativ? That may be true, but according to the sample, there were only eight companies. Is that really representative?
Tobias: Ich möchte kurz etwas hinzufügen: In unserem eigenen Projekt haben wir Ähnliches erlebt, aber ich würde das nicht als Beweis, sondern nur als ein weiteres Beispiel verstehen. I'd like to add something briefly: in our own project we experienced something similar, but I would understand that not as proof, just as another example.
Frau Ostermann: Guter Einwand. Die Autorin merkt selbst an, dass die Ergebnisse aufgrund der kleinen Stichprobe vorsichtig interpretiert werden sollten. Good objection. The author herself notes that the results should be interpreted cautiously because of the small sample.
Kilian: Das ist ein wichtiger Punkt für die Diskussion nachher. Sollten wir das als Erstes ansprechen? That's an important point for the discussion afterward. Should we bring that up first?
Tobias: Ich fasse unsere drei Punkte zusammen: die These, die kleine Stichprobe und mein praktisches Beispiel aus dem Projekt. I'll summarize our three points: the thesis, the small sample, and my practical example from the project.

Listening Component

Ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the following short passage aloud. Listen once for overall content, then again to notice how each claim is attributed to its source.

Vor dem Seminar — Listening Script

Frau Ostermann eröffnete das Gespräch, indem sie die zentrale These der Studie zusammenfasste: Regelmäßiger Austausch zwischen Standorten verbessere die Entscheidungsfindung. Kilian merkte an, dass die Stichprobe der Studie zufolge nur acht Unternehmen umfasste, und fragte, ob das Ergebnis wirklich verallgemeinert werden könne. Tobias fügte hinzu, dass sein eigenes Projekt Ähnliches gezeigt habe, betonte aber, dass ein einzelnes Beispiel keinen Beweis darstelle. Frau Ostermann erinnerte die Gruppe daran, dass die Autorin selbst zu einer vorsichtigen Interpretation der Ergebnisse rate. Am Ende einigten sich die drei darauf, die kleine Stichprobe als ersten Diskussionspunkt im Seminar anzusprechen.

Verständnisfragen

1. Was ist die zentrale These der Studie laut Frau Ostermann?

2. Warum ist Kilian skeptisch gegenüber dem Ergebnis der Studie?

3. Identify one attribution frame and explain what claim it attaches to which source.

Sprechen

Speaking Component

Aufgabe: An einer Seminardiskussion teilnehmen

Work with a partner or in a small group. Each person takes on a role from the seminar and discusses the article summarized in the Reading Component, using attribution frames to make clear whose claim is being discussed.

Rolle: Seminarleitung

  • Open the discussion and summarize the article's thesis.
  • Invite participants to raise objections or supporting points.
  • Summarize the group's discussion points at the end.

Rolle: Teilnehmer/in

  • Raise one objection or question about the evidence or method.
  • Use an attribution frame before evaluating a claim.
  • Connect the article to a practical example if relevant.

Bewertungshinweise — Speaking Criteria

  • At least one attribution frame, clearly and correctly used.
  • A stated objection, question, or supporting point grounded in the text.
  • Clear distinction between the source's claim and the speaker's own view.
  • Comprehensible, natural pronunciation and pacing.

Solo alternative: Record all sides of the discussion yourself, pausing between turns to plan each participant's response.

Lesen

Reading Component

Zusammenarbeit über Standorte hinweg — Reading Passage

Die vorliegende Untersuchung befasst sich mit der Frage, wie sich die Zusammenarbeit zwischen verschiedenen Unternehmensstandorten in den letzten Jahren verändert hat. Die Autorin behauptet, dass regelmäßiger, strukturierter Austausch zwischen Teams die Entscheidungsfindung deutlich verbessert, insbesondere wenn Standorte unterschiedliche Zeitzonen und Arbeitskulturen aufweisen. Grundlage der Studie ist eine Reihe von Interviews mit Mitarbeitenden aus acht mittelständischen Unternehmen. Die Autorin merkt selbst an, dass die kleine Stichprobe die Verallgemeinerbarkeit der Ergebnisse einschränkt, und schlägt vor, die Untersuchung mit einer größeren Zahl von Unternehmen zu wiederholen. Trotz dieser Einschränkung liefert die Studie erste Hinweise darauf, dass klare Kommunikationsstrukturen wichtiger sein können als die physische Nähe der Teams zueinander.

Verständnisfragen

1. Worauf stützt sich die Studie methodisch?

2. Welche Einschränkung nennt die Autorin selbst?

3. Identify the complex noun phrase in the second-to-last sentence and explain how you located the noun it modifies.

Schreiben

Writing Component

Write a short summary-response to the Reading Component passage, followed by two or three discussion points you could raise in a seminar.

Kontext für die Zusammenfassung

Fassen Sie in vier bis sechs Sätzen die These, die Methode und eine Einschränkung der Studie zusammen, und formulieren Sie anschließend Ihre eigene Einschätzung klar getrennt von der These der Autorin.

  1. State the article's thesis using an attribution frame.
  2. Summarize the method and the sample briefly.
  3. Note the limitation the author herself identifies.
  4. Add your own response, clearly marked as your own view rather than the author's claim.
  5. List two or three discussion points you would raise in the seminar.
  6. Review your text against the checklist below.

Revision checklist:

  • Is the author's thesis clearly attributed rather than stated as fact?
  • Is the method or sample mentioned accurately?
  • Is your own view clearly separated from the source's claim?
  • Do the discussion points connect to specific evidence in the text?

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Academic Cultures Vary by Institution and Discipline

Seminar culture differs considerably across German-speaking universities and disciplines. Some seminars expect students to challenge a visiting speaker's claims directly and in detail; others favor a more measured style in which objections are raised gradually and with more hedging. Interdisciplinary settings, such as a business seminar attended by a mixed group of researchers and practitioners like Tobias, often blend these styles, so it is worth observing the room before adopting a particular level of directness.

Culture and Communication — Source Claims Must Be Attributed Carefully

In academic and professional discussion, presenting another person's claim as an established fact — or presenting your own opinion as if it came from a study — can seriously undermine trust in a discussion. Attribution frames such as der Autorin zufolge or laut der Studie are not just grammatical patterns; they are a core part of academic integrity in any language.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Stating a source's claim as an established fact

Error: *Regelmäßiger Austausch verbessert die Entscheidungsfindung. presented without any reference to the source.

Correction: Die Autorin behauptet, dass regelmäßiger Austausch die Entscheidungsfindung verbessert. — attribute the claim before discussing it.

Misreading a complex noun phrase word-by-word in English order

Error: reading die von den Autorinnen untersuchte Zusammenarbeit as "the by the authors examined collaboration" and losing the meaning.

Correction: locate the article and final noun first (die Zusammenarbeit), then read the modifier as a single unit describing it: "the collaboration examined by the authors."

Overgeneralizing from a small or limited study

Error: *Die Studie beweist, dass kleine Teams immer besser sind. stated as a universal, proven fact.

Correction: Die Studie liefert erste Hinweise darauf, dass kleinere Teams unter bestimmten Umständen Vorteile haben könnten, auch wenn die Stichprobe klein war. — note the limitation alongside the claim.
