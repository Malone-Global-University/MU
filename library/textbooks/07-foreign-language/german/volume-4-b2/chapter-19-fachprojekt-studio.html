Fachprojekt-Studio

Specialized Project Studio · Speak and Write German: Professional, Academic, and Cultural Communication · Volume IV, Chapter 19

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 19 opens a project studio in which Tobias applies a full unit's worth of professional, academic, and mediation skills to a single extended piece of work of his own choosing. Frau Aksoy proposes that he turn the courtyard project into a short portfolio piece: a proposal, a small annotated glossary of the specialized terms he has been using, a draft text in a genre he selects, and a short presentation to whoever might read or hear it next, whether that is a future volunteer, a funding committee, or a new neighbor. Unlike earlier chapters, this chapter does not introduce a new grammar system. Instead, it asks learners to manage a domain of their own choosing responsibly: naming and checking specialized terminology, choosing a genre and register that fit the audience, building an argument on real or plausibly sourced information, and revising a draft more than once. This chapter is a workshop, not a lecture; most of its language work happens inside the learner's own project rather than inside new explanations.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Select and narrow a domain-specific project appropriate to your interests, your language level, and the scope of this volume.
  2. Compile a short annotated glossary of specialized vocabulary with gender, register, and source information.
  3. Choose a genre and register that fit your project's stated audience and purpose.
  4. Draft a project text that uses source-based information responsibly and without fabrication.
  5. Revise a draft through at least two rounds using a structured checklist.
  6. Present a project briefly and answer follow-up questions about it.

Opening Scenario

Frau Aksoy tells Tobias that the neighborhood association would like a small portfolio piece documenting the courtyard project for future use — something a new volunteer coordinator could read to understand what happened, why, and what it required in specialized terms. She leaves the exact shape of the piece up to Tobias: he can write it as a short report, a project proposal for a similar future effort, an explanatory piece for newcomers, or something else that fits the material. She reminds him that whatever he chooses, it must name its sources honestly, use its specialized vocabulary correctly and consistently, and go through at least one real round of revision before anyone else reads it.

  1. Choose a project domain and a genre suited to it.
  2. Build a small glossary of the domain's specialized vocabulary.
  3. Draft the project text using source information responsibly.
  4. Revise the draft and prepare a short presentation of it.
Wortschatz

Core Vocabulary

This chapter's vocabulary names the tasks of project work itself rather than a single external topic, because each learner's project vocabulary will differ by domain. Recycle terminology-formation strategies from Chapter 8 and source-attribution language from Chapter 14 when building a project glossary.

Projektarbeit — Project Work

  • das Projekt, die Projekte — project
  • der Entwurf, die Entwürfe — draft
  • die Fassung, die Fassungen — version (of a text)
  • der Umfang (Singular) — scope
  • eingrenzen — to narrow down, to delimit

Fachwortschatz — Specialized Vocabulary

  • der Fachbegriff, die Fachbegriffe — specialized/technical term
  • das Glossar, die Glossare — glossary
  • die Quelle, die Quellen — source
  • belegen — to substantiate, to document with evidence
  • nachschlagen — to look up (in a reference work)

Gattung und Publikum — Genre and Audience

  • die Gattung, die Gattungen — genre, text type
  • das Zielpublikum, die Zielpublika — target audience
  • der Anlass, die Anlässe — occasion, purpose
  • sich eignen (für) — to be suited (to)
  • angemessen — appropriate, fitting

Überarbeitung — Revision

  • die Überarbeitung, die Überarbeitungen — revision
  • das Feedback (Singular) — feedback
  • die Rückmeldung, die Rückmeldungen — response, feedback
  • überarbeiten — to revise
  • straffen — to tighten (a text)

Gender and plural are given for every active noun above; learners should add their own domain-specific nouns to this pattern, with gender and plural checked in a current dictionary before use.

Aussprache

Pronunciation Focus: Presenting Specialized Vocabulary Aloud

Pronunciation and Delivery — Stress in Unfamiliar Specialized Terms

A short presentation often introduces a listener to specialized compounds and loanwords for the first time, and unclear stress on these terms can cost a listener's comprehension even when the rest of the delivery is fluent. Before presenting a project, identify the two or three specialized terms most likely to be new to the audience, mark their compound or word stress, and practice them in isolation and inside a full sentence before rehearsing the whole presentation.

This is a rehearsal strategy, not a new pronunciation system; it applies compound-stress and word-stress skills already consolidated in earlier chapters and volumes.

Grammatik

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Chapter 19 activates no new grammar system. Instead, it asks learners to apply the systems already consolidated in this volume — nominal style, passive and modal alternatives, complex clause hierarchy, source attribution, and register control — inside a project of their own design. The sections below function as a working checklist rather than new explanations.

Narrowing a Project Domain

A project domain that is too broad produces vague, unverifiable writing. A narrow, well-defined domain produces a text the learner can actually source and control. Narrowing usually means choosing a specific event, place, process, or question rather than an entire field.

Baustein — Building Block
zu weit gefasst → eingegrenzt
a topic narrowed from a whole field to a specific, sourceable project
Stadtgärten in Deutschland (zu weit) → Der Innenhof-Garten unserer Nachbarschaft: Planung, Förderung und erste Ergebnisse (eingegrenzt)
Community gardens in Germany (too broad) → Our neighborhood's courtyard garden: planning, funding, and early results (narrowed).

Building a Small Project Glossary

A project glossary should record each specialized term with its gender and plural, a concise meaning in context, its register, and where the term or its meaning was verified. This recycles the vocabulary-entry standards from earlier chapters and applies them to a self-selected domain.

  • die Berichterstattung (Singular) — reporting, the reporting requirement verified in the district's funding notice, Chapter 17 register: administrative; source-checked before use
  • der Fördermittelantrag, die Fördermittelanträge — funding application compound noun, head-final: Antrag (application) for Fördermittel (funding) gender inherited from head noun der Antrag

Matching Genre and Register to Audience

A proposal, a report, an explanatory piece for newcomers, and a public announcement each call for a different register and a different balance of nominal and verbal style. Choosing a genre means asking who will read the text and why, not merely which genre feels impressive.

Grammar Note — Register Consistency Across a Whole Project

A common project weakness is starting in one register and drifting into another, for example opening a report in neutral administrative style and closing it in informal spoken style. Reread a completed draft specifically for register consistency, separately from checking it for grammar or content, since the two kinds of error are easy to miss at the same time.

Using Sources Without Fabricating Authority

A project text should distinguish clearly between information the learner can verify, information that is plausible but unverified, and information that is invented for a fictional exercise. Presenting invented figures, invented institutions, or invented statistics as if they were real is not acceptable in any genre, including a proposal or a report.

  • Laut der Mitteilung der Kulturbehörde vom Mai wurde die Förderung bewilligt. According to the cultural office's notice from May, the funding was approved. attributed claim, source named, consistent with material from Chapters 17–18
  • Genaue Zahlen zu ähnlichen Projekten in anderen Bezirken liegen mir nicht vor; ein Vergleich wäre daher spekulativ. I do not have exact figures for similar projects in other districts; a comparison would therefore be speculative. honest limitation stated instead of an invented statistic
Common Project Error — Inventing Sources or Figures for Persuasive Effect

Error: *Laut einer Studie steigt die Zufriedenheit der Nachbarschaft um 40 Prozent. when no such study exists.

Correction: Viele Nachbarn haben in Gesprächen von einer positiveren Stimmung im Hof berichtet, auch wenn dazu keine Umfragedaten vorliegen. — states an observation honestly instead of fabricating a statistic.

Revising Across Multiple Drafts

Revision in a project studio should move from content and structure in an early draft toward register, cohesion, and mechanics in a later draft, rather than trying to fix everything at once.

Baustein — Building Block
1. Entwurf (Inhalt und Aufbau) → 2. Entwurf (Register und Kohäsion) → 3. Entwurf (Mechanik und letzter Check)
staged revision: content/structure, then register/cohesion, then mechanics
Der erste Entwurf klärt, was gesagt werden soll; der zweite prüft, wie es klingt; der dritte prüft Rechtschreibung und Kommas.
The first draft clarifies what should be said; the second checks how it sounds; the third checks spelling and commas.
Modelltext

Model Text: Project Proposal Excerpt

An excerpt from Tobias's first draft project proposal, documenting the courtyard project for a future volunteer coordinator, followed by his own note on what the second draft still needs.

Entwurf 1 Der Innenhof unseres Wohnblocks wird seit dem Frühjahr für ein Gartenprojekt genutzt. Die Idee kam von mehreren Nachbarn, die einen Ort für gemeinsames Gärtnern wollten. Frau Aksoy hat den Antrag bei der Kulturbehörde eingereicht, und nach drei Monaten Bearbeitung wurde die Förderung bewilligt. Als Auflage muss die Nachbarschaftsvereinigung jährlich einen Bericht über den Fortschritt einreichen. (Our building's courtyard has been used for a garden project since spring. The idea came from several neighbors who wanted a place for shared gardening. Frau Aksoy submitted the application to the cultural office, and after three months of processing the funding was approved. As a condition, the neighborhood association must submit an annual progress report.)
Notiz Für die zweite Fassung: den Fachbegriff „Auflage" im Glossar erklären, das Register im letzten Satz an den Rest angleichen, und einen Satz zum Zielpublikum (neue Freiwillige) ergänzen. (For the second version: explain the specialized term "Auflage" [condition/requirement] in the glossary, align the register of the last sentence with the rest, and add a sentence about the target audience — new volunteers.)

Hörverständnis

Listening Practice

Ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the following passage aloud. Listen once for overall meaning, then again to identify which parts belong to which stage of the project (proposal, glossary, draft, or revision note).

Tobias berichtet über sein Fachprojekt — Listening Script

Für mein Fachprojekt habe ich das Gartenprojekt in unserem Innenhof gewählt, weil ich die Unterlagen dazu schon kenne und die Fachbegriffe größtenteils belegen kann. Ich habe zuerst ein kleines Glossar erstellt, mit Fachbegriffen wie „die Berichterstattung" und „der Fördermittelantrag". Danach habe ich einen ersten Entwurf geschrieben, der erklärt, wie das Projekt entstanden ist und welche Auflagen damit verbunden sind. In der zweiten Fassung habe ich das Register vereinheitlicht und einen Abschnitt für neue Freiwillige ergänzt, damit der Text auch für Leute nützlich ist, die das Projekt noch nicht kennen. Zum Schluss möchte ich den Text noch einmal auf Kommas und Rechtschreibung prüfen, bevor ich ihn Frau Aksoy zeige.

Verständnisfragen

1. Why did Tobias choose the courtyard garden project as his domain, according to the passage?

2. What did Tobias add to the second draft that was missing from the first?

3. Welchen letzten Schritt möchte Tobias noch machen, bevor er den Text Frau Aksoy zeigt?

Sprechen

Speaking Practice

Aufgabe: Projektideen vorstellen und begründen

Working with a partner or recording yourself, present your chosen project domain in two or three minutes: what it is, why you narrowed it this way, who your target audience is, and which genre you have chosen. Then explain one specialized term from your glossary and one revision decision you expect to make between your first and second drafts.

Rolle: Vorstellende Person

  • State your project domain and why you narrowed it this way.
  • Name your target audience and chosen genre, and justify the choice.
  • Explain one specialized term and one planned revision.

Rolle: Rückmeldende Person

  • Ask whether the domain is narrow enough to source responsibly.
  • Ask how a claim in the project would be verified or labeled as unverified.
  • Give one honest, constructive suggestion for the next draft.

Bewertungshinweise — Speaking Criteria

  • Clearly states a narrowed project domain, audience, and genre.
  • Explains at least one specialized term accurately, including gender and plural.
  • Distinguishes verified information from unverified or speculative claims.
  • Comprehensible pronunciation, with attention to stress on specialized terms.

Solo alternative: Record yourself presenting your project plan and one glossary term, then listen back and note one thing you would revise before your next draft.

Schreiben

Writing Workshop

Develop your own Chapter 19 project. Choose a domain (professional, academic, or cultural) that genuinely interests you, narrow it to a project-sized topic, and produce the four components described below. This workshop spans the whole project; plan for at least two rounds of revision on the draft text.

Kontext für die Projektarbeit

Wählen Sie einen Bereich, der Sie wirklich interessiert, und grenzen Sie ihn auf ein Projekt ein, das Sie mit den Sprachmitteln dieses Bandes bearbeiten können. Erstellen Sie ein kleines Glossar, schreiben Sie einen ersten Entwurf, überarbeiten Sie ihn mindestens einmal, und bereiten Sie eine kurze Präsentation vor.

Die vier Projektbestandteile — The Four Project Components

  1. Projektvorschlag (Project Proposal): 4–6 sentences stating your narrowed domain, audience, purpose, and genre.
  2. Glossar (Glossary): 6–10 specialized terms with gender, plural, meaning in context, register, and verification source.
  3. Entwurf (Draft): a project text of 150–250 words in your chosen genre, using at least four glossary terms and clearly distinguishing verified from unverified information.
  4. Präsentation (Presentation): a short spoken outline of your project for the Speaking Practice task above.
  1. Narrow your domain and write the project proposal.
  2. Build the glossary, checking gender, plural, and meaning against a current dictionary or reliable source.
  3. Write the first draft, focusing on content and structure rather than polish.
  4. Set the draft aside, then revise it a second time for register consistency and cohesion.
  5. Check the near-final draft specifically for capitalization, commas, and spelling.
  6. Prepare and rehearse your short presentation, marking stress on any specialized terms likely to be unfamiliar to your listener.

Revision checklist:

  • Is my project domain narrow enough to source and control responsibly?
  • Does every glossary entry include gender, plural, and a verification source?
  • Is the register consistent from the beginning of my draft to the end?
  • Have I clearly labeled any unverified or speculative claim instead of stating it as fact?
  • Have I revised the draft at least twice, addressing different concerns each time?

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Project Genres Vary by Institution and Field

What counts as an appropriate project genre and register differs across academic disciplines, workplaces, civic organizations, and DACH institutional contexts; a report acceptable in one setting may read as too informal or too dense in another. Learners should treat genre conventions as context-specific rather than universal, and should check the expectations of their own project's actual audience rather than assuming one fixed template applies everywhere.

Culture and Communication — Honest Authority in Learner Projects

A learner project does not need to claim expert authority to be valuable; a well-sourced, honestly limited text is more credible and more useful to a real reader than an overconfident one built on invented statistics or unnamed studies. Community groups, classrooms, and workplaces generally value a contributor who states clearly what is known, what is uncertain, and what still needs checking.

Common Mistakes to Recheck

Choosing a domain too broad to source

Error: A learner selects a domain such as "die Umwelt" or "die Bildung" without narrowing it, producing a draft full of generalities that cannot be verified.

Correction: Narrow the domain to a specific place, process, or question, as in Der Innenhof-Garten unserer Nachbarschaft rather than Stadtgärten in general.

Fabricating sources or statistics

Error: *Laut einer Studie steigt die Zufriedenheit um 40 Prozent. when no such study exists.

Correction: State what is actually known, and label anything uncertain as such, as in Viele Nachbarn haben von einer positiveren Stimmung berichtet, auch wenn dazu keine Umfragedaten vorliegen.

Skipping revision rounds

Error: Submitting a first draft unchanged, correcting only isolated typos rather than checking content, register, and mechanics separately.

Correction: Use the staged revision pattern — content and structure first, then register and cohesion, then mechanics — across at least two rounds.

Answer Key

Guided Practice

  1. Sample answer: Sprachen lernen (too broad) → Wie ich in sechs Monaten mein Hörverständnis verbessert habe (narrowed to a specific, personal, sourceable project).
  2. Sample answers will vary by domain; each entry should include gender, plural, a concise meaning in context, and a stated verification source, following the pattern shown in the chapter's vocabulary and grammar sections.

  4. Wir sind überzeugt, dass unser Projekt für die Nachbarschaft nützlich war, auch wenn dazu keine internationalen Vergleichsdaten vorliegen. — removes the fabricated claim of being "the most successful of its kind" while keeping a modest, honest statement.

  1. Tobias chose the courtyard garden project because he already knows the material and can substantiate most of the specialized terms involved.
