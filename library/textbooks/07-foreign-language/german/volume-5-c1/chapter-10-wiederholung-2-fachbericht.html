Unit II Assessment

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 10 closes Unit II by asking Tobias to bring together everything the unit has developed: reading a research-oriented source critically (Chapter 6), writing a source-based section with proper attribution (Chapter 7), framing a decision in strategic, risk-aware language for leadership (Chapter 8), and explaining a specialized system precisely to two different audiences without inventing terminology (Chapter 9). Frau Vogt now asks him to produce a single Fachbericht — a specialized report — that documents the pilot's technical basis, its organizational rationale, and its practical rollout, and then to defend the report's choices orally in front of a small review panel. This chapter does not introduce new grammar or vocabulary systems. It assesses whether Tobias, and the learner working through this chapter, can integrate the nominal style, passive and impersonal process description, Konjunktiv I reporting, source discipline, strategic register, and audience adaptation that Chapters 6 through 9 activated.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Integrate academic, organizational, and technical registers within a single specialized report.
  2. Summarize a source responsibly using Konjunktiv I reporting and correct attribution.
  3. Describe a process and its rollout using passive, impersonal, and imperative style as appropriate to each section.
  4. Use a glossary consistently across a report, a briefing, and a question-and-answer exchange.
  5. Present a specialized report orally and defend its terminology, scope, and conclusions under questioning.
  6. Distinguish evidence-based claims from recommendations that exceed the report's scope.
  7. Revise a specialized report for register consistency, source discipline, and audience fit.

Opening Scenario

Frau Vogt tells Tobias that the pilot's steering committee wants one consolidated document rather than four separate memos: a specialized report covering the research basis for the new communication routine, the organizational reasoning behind adopting it, and the technical configuration required to run it. Tobias will submit the written report in advance and then present a short briefing to the committee, who will ask follow-up questions drawn from any section of the report. He is reminded that the committee includes people from research, operations, and IT, so the report's register must shift deliberately from section to section while a single glossary keeps its terminology consistent throughout.

  1. Reread your Chapter 7 source section, Chapter 8 strategic memo, and Chapter 9 glossary and technical notes.
  2. Draft a single Fachbericht that opens with the research basis, moves to the organizational rationale, and closes with the technical configuration.
  3. Prepare a short oral briefing that summarizes the report's key points for a mixed audience.
  4. Anticipate likely committee questions and prepare concise, evidence-based answers.

Aussprache

Delivery Focus: Reading Aloud Under Questions

Delivery — Staying Intelligible Under Unplanned Questions

A briefing followed by open questions puts more pressure on spontaneous pronunciation than a rehearsed presentation does. Keep the same priorities that have applied across the volume: maintain clear long and short vowel contrasts, keep final devoicing consistent even when speaking quickly, and slow down deliberately before a long technical compound rather than rushing through it and losing the listener.

When a committee member interrupts with a question mid-sentence, it is better to finish the current clause simply and briefly rather than abandoning it mid-word, since an unfinished German clause with the verb still pending is harder for a listener to parse than a short, complete one.

Grammatik

Grammar Review: Nominal Style, Passive, and Source Language

No new grammar system is activated in this chapter. The review below consolidates the systems from Chapters 6 through 9 that the Fachbericht must combine within a single document.

Shifting Register Section by Section

A Fachbericht that moves from research background to organizational rationale to technical configuration should shift its nominal density and its verb style deliberately rather than staying uniform throughout. A research section may use Konjunktiv I to report a source's claims; an organizational section may foreground risk and recommendation language; a technical section may rely on passive or imperative process description depending on whether it specifies or instructs.

Baustein — Building Block
Die Studie zeige, dass kürzere Kommunikationswege die Reaktionszeit verbessern; das Team empfiehlt daher, die neue Routine testweise einzuführen; die Einstellungen werden zentral verwaltet.
Konjunktiv I report (zeige) → recommendation clause (empfiehlt ... einzuführen) → passive process clause (werden ... verwaltet)
Using the same declarative present-tense style across all three clauses would blur the distinction between what a source claims, what the team recommends, and how the system actually functions.
The study reportedly shows that shorter communication paths improve response time; the team therefore recommends introducing the new routine on a trial basis; the settings are managed centrally.

Keeping Source, Opinion, and Fact Distinct

Grammar Note — Three Voices in One Report
  • Laut der Studie ... / Die Studie zeige, dass ... marks a claim as belonging to a cited source, not to the report's author.
  • Das Team empfiehlt ... / Es wird vorgeschlagen, ... marks a recommendation as the report's own position, distinct from the source.
  • Die Einstellung wird ... verwaltet. marks a plain statement of how a system functions, without evaluative content.
  • A report that blends these three voices without signaling the shift asks the reader to do work the writer should have done.

Model Text: Fachbericht Excerpt

A short excerpt from Tobias's Fachbericht showing the transition from the research section into the organizational section.

Forschung Die zugrunde liegende Studie zeige, dass kürzere und regelmäßigere Kommunikationswege die Reaktionszeit von Teams messbar verbessern; die Autoren weisen jedoch darauf hin, dass die Stichprobe auf ein einzelnes Unternehmen beschränkt war. (The underlying study reportedly shows that shorter and more regular communication paths measurably improve team response time; however, the authors note that the sample was limited to a single company.)
Organisation Angesichts dieser Einschränkung empfiehlt das Team einen begrenzten Pilotversuch statt einer sofortigen unternehmensweiten Einführung; das größte Risiko liegt in der ungleichmäßigen Akzeptanz zwischen den Abteilungen. (Given this limitation, the team recommends a limited pilot rather than an immediate company-wide rollout; the greatest risk lies in uneven acceptance between departments.)
Technik Für den Pilotbetrieb wird der Zugriffsberechtigungsstatus jedes Teammitglieds zentral in der Benutzeroberflächeneinstellung verwaltet; Abweichungen werden über eine Fehlermeldung angezeigt. (For the pilot operation, each team member's access-authorization status is managed centrally in the user-interface setting; deviations are indicated via an error message.)

Aufgabe: Bericht vorstellen und Fragen beantworten

Rolle: Berichterstattende Person

  • Summarize the research basis, the recommendation, and the technical configuration in under two minutes.
  • Answer each question by identifying which part of the report it concerns before responding.
  • Distinguish clearly between what the source study claims and what the report itself recommends.

Rolle: Ausschussmitglied

  • Ask at least one question about the research basis, one about the organizational recommendation, and one about the technical configuration.
  • Ask for clarification if a glossary term is used inconsistently.
  • Ask what would happen if the pilot's limitation (uneven acceptance) actually occurred.

Bewertungshinweise — Speaking Criteria

  • Summarizes all three sections of the report clearly and in appropriate order.
  • Keeps source claims, recommendations, and technical facts distinguishable in spoken delivery.
  • Answers questions with evidence from the report rather than improvised claims.
  • Uses glossary terms consistently between the written report and the oral briefing.
  • Maintains intelligible pronunciation and recovers smoothly from interruption.

Schreiben

Writing Assessment: Der Fachbericht

Produce the specialized report Frau Vogt requested, integrating the systems developed across Chapters 6 through 9 into three clearly marked sections, each shifting register appropriately.

Aufgabe — Task

Schreiben Sie einen Fachbericht mit drei Abschnitten: (1) eine Zusammenfassung der Forschungsgrundlage mit korrekter Quellenangabe und Konjunktiv I, (2) eine organisatorische Einschätzung mit Risiko- und Empfehlungssprache, und (3) eine technische Beschreibung mit passivem Prozessstil und einem Glossar von vier bis sechs Begriffen. Verwenden Sie die Begriffe aus Ihrem Glossar konsequent in allen drei Abschnitten.

Die drei Abschnitte — The Three Sections

  1. Forschung: summarize a source's claim using Konjunktiv I, and state at least one limitation.
  2. Organisation: state a recommendation distinct from the source's claim, and name the main risk.
  3. Technik: describe the configuration using passive or impersonal style, supported by a glossary.
  1. Reread your Chapter 7 source section, Chapter 8 memo, and Chapter 9 glossary before drafting.
  2. Draft the Forschung section, keeping the source's claim and its limitation clearly marked as belonging to the source.
  3. Draft the Organisation section, marking your recommendation as the report's own position.
  4. Draft the Technik section using passive or impersonal process style, reusing your Chapter 9 glossary terms exactly.
  5. Add a short combined glossary listing every specialized term used across all three sections.
  6. Reread the full report and confirm that source claims, recommendations, and technical facts remain distinguishable throughout.
  7. Revise for register consistency within each section and terminological consistency across all three.

Revision checklist:

  • Does the Forschung section mark the source's claim with Konjunktiv I or an equivalent reporting structure?
  • Does the Organisation section state a recommendation that is clearly distinct from the source's claim?
  • Does the Technik section use passive or impersonal style consistently, supported by a verified glossary?
  • Does every glossary term appear identically across all three sections?
  • Have I stated any conclusion or recommendation that the evidence in the report does not actually support?

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Transparency About Limits in Committee Settings

In German-speaking professional and academic settings, a report or briefing that openly names its own limitations is generally regarded as more credible, not less, than one that presents a confident conclusion without qualification. A committee member asking about representativeness, as in the reading excerpt above, is a routine and expected part of reviewing a specialized report, not a challenge to be deflected.

Culture and Communication — Answering Within Scope

When a question exceeds what the report's evidence can support, a well-prepared presenter says so directly rather than improvising an answer that sounds confident but is not grounded in the material. This applies equally to legal, financial, medical, or technical follow-up questions that arise unexpectedly during a briefing; naming the boundary of one's own report is part of professional communication, not a weakness in it.

Common Mistakes to Recheck

Blending source claim and author recommendation

Error: A learner writes "Kürzere Kommunikationswege verbessern die Reaktionszeit, deshalb führen wir die Routine ein" as one flat statement, without marking which part comes from the source and which part is the report's own decision.

Correction: Separate the two voices explicitly, e.g. "Die Studie zeige, dass ...; das Team empfiehlt daher, ..."

Dropping the glossary term used in an earlier chapter

Error: A learner uses "Zugriffsberechtigungsstatus" in the technical section but switches to "Zugriffsstatus" or "Berechtigungsstatus" elsewhere in the same report.

Correction: Check every technical term against the Chapter 9 glossary before submitting, and use one fixed form throughout.

Answering a committee question beyond the report's evidence

Error: A learner, asked whether the pilot will succeed company-wide, answers with a confident prediction the report's evidence does not support.

Correction: State plainly that the report's evidence covers only the pilot scope, and that a company-wide judgment would require further data.

Answer Key

Guided Practice

  1. "zeige" is a Konjunktiv I form, signaling that the improvement in reaction time is the study's claim, not a fact the report author asserts directly.
  2. "empfiehlt das Team einen begrenzten Pilotversuch" is the recommendation; "empfiehlt" marks it explicitly as the team's own position rather than a finding.
  3. "wird ... zentral ... verwaltet" is the passive construction; an active rewrite could be "Die IT-Abteilung verwaltet den Zugriffsberechtigungsstatus jedes Teammitglieds zentral in der Benutzeroberflächeneinstellung."
  4. Open production; a good answer notes that keeping the limitation visible lets the reader judge how much weight the recommendation can bear, rather than presenting the pilot decision as more certain than the evidence allows.
  5. "Zugriffsberechtigungsstatus," "Benutzeroberflächeneinstellung," and "Fehlermeldung" all reappear from Chapter 9.
