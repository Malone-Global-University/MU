Technik und Fachkommunikation

Technical and Specialized Communication · Speak and Write German: Mastery, Precision, and Specialized Communication · Volume V, Chapter 9

Chapter Overview and Objectives

The pilot phase that Herr Brandt approved in Chapter 8 now needs documentation: the partner teams who will actually use the new communication routines have never heard of the research behind them, and they need a explanation they can act on rather than a summary aimed at leadership. Tobias is asked to write a short technical explanation of the routine for two audiences — the team members who will use it day to day, and the IT staff who must configure the scheduling tool that supports it — and to build a small glossary so both groups use the same terms consistently. The chapter introduces definition structures for naming and explaining a concept precisely, the compound chains typical of German technical prose, passive and imperative process style for describing steps, and the audience-adaptation skills needed to explain the same system differently to a lay reader and a specialist.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Write precise definitions using standard German definition structures.
  2. Read and form multi-part compound chains and break them into their components.
  3. Describe a process using passive voice, impersonal constructions, and imperatives as appropriate.
  4. Adapt the same technical explanation for a lay audience and for a specialist audience.
  5. Build and use a small bilingual glossary consistently across a document.
  6. Explain a specialized system clearly without inventing terminology.
  7. Revise a technical explanation for terminological consistency and audience fit.

Opening Scenario

Frau Vogt asks Tobias to prepare two short documents before the pilot team's kickoff meeting: a plain-language explanation of the new communication routine for the team members who will follow it, and a slightly more technical note for the IT colleague who must configure the shared scheduling tool. Both documents must use exactly the same terms for the same concepts, so Tobias first drafts a small glossary, then writes each explanation, checking that neither version invents a term the other does not use.

  2. Draft the plain-language explanation for team members using accessible compounds and clear process steps.
  3. Draft the technical note for IT using appropriately denser terminology and passive process style.

Wortschatz

Core Vocabulary

This chapter's vocabulary supports technical explanation: the nouns used to name systems and their parts, the verbs used to define and describe processes, and the compound chains typical of German technical documentation.

Definition und Erklärung — Definition and Explanation

  • der Begriff, die Begriffe — term, concept
  • die Definition, die Definitionen — definition
  • das Merkmal, die Merkmale — characteristic, feature
  • die Bezeichnung, die Bezeichnungen — designation, name for something
  • der Zusammenhang, die Zusammenhänge — connection, context

System und Prozess — System and Process

  • der Ablauf, die Abläufe — procedure, sequence of steps
  • die Einstellung, die Einstellungen — setting, configuration
  • die Schnittstelle, die Schnittstellen — interface
  • die Voreinstellung, die Voreinstellungen — default setting
  • der Bestandteil, die Bestandteile — component, part

Nominalkomposita — Technical Compound Chains

  • der Terminplanungsassistent, die Terminplanungsassistenten — scheduling assistant (tool)
  • die Benutzeroberflächeneinstellung, die Benutzeroberflächeneinstellungen — user-interface setting
  • die Kommunikationsroutinenvorlage, die Kommunikationsroutinenvorlagen — communication-routine template
  • der Zugriffsberechtigungsstatus, (no common plural) — access-authorization status
  • die Fehlermeldung, die Fehlermeldungen — error message

Zielgruppe und Erklären — Audience and Explaining

  • die Zielgruppe, die Zielgruppen — target audience
  • der Laie, die Laien — layperson
  • die Fachkraft, die Fachkräfte — specialist, skilled professional
  • das Glossar, die Glossare — glossary
  • die Kurzanleitung, die Kurzanleitungen — quick guide, short instructions

All active nouns are given with article and plural where a plural is established. Compound chains are grouped separately to support the word-formation analysis below.

Aussprache

Delivery Focus: Compound Chains and Term Stress

Delivery — Parsing Long Compounds Aloud

Multi-part technical compounds such as Benutzeroberflächeneinstellung carry primary stress on the first component, with a lighter secondary stress on the head noun at the end. When reading such a compound aloud in a briefing, a brief pause after the first one or two recognizable components — Benutzer-oberflächen-einstellung — helps a listener parse the word into its parts without breaking the compound's status as a single word in writing.

When explaining the same term to a lay audience, it is often more natural to expand it briefly into a short phrase the first time it is used, for example die Einstellung der Benutzeroberfläche, also die Benutzeroberflächeneinstellung, before continuing to use the compound alone. This supports comprehension without changing the correct written form.

Grammatik

Grammar: Definitions, Compound Chains, Process Style, and Audience Adaptation

This chapter activates four interconnected writing and speaking targets: fixed structures for stating a definition precisely, the internal logic of longer compound chains, passive and imperative style for describing a process, and the deliberate adaptation of density and terminology to a specific audience.

Stating a Precise Definition

A technical definition names the term, places it in a category, and states the feature that distinguishes it from other members of that category. Skipping any of the three parts produces a definition that sounds authoritative but does not actually explain anything, which repeats, in technical language, the same failure a vague risk statement produced in Chapter 8's strategic writing.

Baustein — Building Block
Der Terminplanungsassistent ist ein Werkzeug, das Teammitgliedern hilft, gemeinsame Termine automatisch zu koordinieren.
term + category (ein Werkzeug) + distinguishing relative clause (das ... hilft, ... zu koordinieren)
Naming only the term ("Der Terminplanungsassistent ist wichtig") without category and distinguishing feature leaves the reader without a usable definition.
The scheduling assistant is a tool that helps team members automatically coordinate shared appointments.

Reading and Forming Compound Chains

Longer technical compounds are built by chaining components, each of which modifies the head noun that follows it, until the chain ends in the true head noun that carries the gender and plural of the whole word.

  • Kommunikationsroutinenvorlage = Kommunikation + s + Routine(n) + Vorlage communication-routine template head noun: die Vorlage → die Kommunikationsroutinenvorlage
  • Zugriffsberechtigungsstatus = Zugriff + s + Berechtigung + s + Status access-authorization status head noun: der Status → der Zugriffsberechtigungsstatus
Grammar Note — Verifying Rather Than Inventing Compounds

Every component in a chain must be independently verifiable, and the linking elements between components (such as the -s- in Kommunikationsroutine) follow established patterns rather than the writer's preference. When a new compound is genuinely needed for a system that has no established German term, mark it explicitly as an author-created working term rather than presenting it as standard usage.

Passive, Impersonal, and Imperative Process Style

German technical documentation typically describes a process either impersonally, with passive voice foregrounding the action rather than the actor, or directly, with imperatives addressing the person carrying out the steps. The choice depends on genre and audience.

Grammar Note — Choosing Between Passive and Imperative Process Style
  • Die Voreinstellung wird im Menü "Optionen" geändert. (passive, describing what happens, suitable for a specification document)
  • Ändern Sie die Voreinstellung im Menü "Optionen". (imperative, addressing the user directly, suitable for a quick guide)
  • Mixing both styles within the same short document without reason makes the text feel inconsistent; choose one dominant style per document and use the other only for deliberate emphasis.

Speaking Practice

Aufgabe: Ein System zwei Zielgruppen erklären

Working with a partner, a small group, or by recording yourself, explain the scheduling assistant orally twice: once to a partner playing a team member with no technical background, and once to a partner playing an IT colleague, using the same glossary terms consistently in both versions.

Rolle: Erklärende Person

  • Define each key term before using it with the lay audience.
  • Use denser compounds and passive process style with the specialist audience.
  • Use exactly the same glossary term for the same concept in both versions.

Rolle: Teammitglied oder IT-Fachkraft

  • Ask for clarification if a term is unclear or inconsistent.
  • Request a concrete next step or setting to check.
  • Point out if the same concept was named two different ways.

Bewertungshinweise — Speaking Criteria

  • Defines key terms clearly for the lay audience before using them.
  • Adjusts compound density and process style appropriately for each audience.
  • Uses glossary terms consistently across both versions.
  • Responds to requests for clarification without inventing new terminology.
  • Parses long compounds clearly enough for the listener to follow.

Solo alternative: Record both versions of your explanation, then listen back and check that every glossary term appears identically in each recording.

Writing Workshop

Complete the two documents Frau Vogt requested: a plain-language explanation for team members and a technical note for IT, both built from a shared glossary of four to six terms.

Aufgabe — Task

Erstellen Sie zunächst ein kurzes Glossar mit vier bis sechs zentralen Begriffen. Schreiben Sie anschließend zwei kurze Erklärungen desselben Systems: eine für Laien mit klaren Definitionen und Imperativen, und eine für Fachkräfte mit passivem Prozessstil und dichteren Komposita. Verwenden Sie in beiden Versionen dieselben Begriffe aus dem Glossar.

Die drei Teilaufgaben — The Three Sub-Tasks

  1. Glossar: define four to six key terms precisely, each with term, category, and distinguishing feature.
  2. Laienversion: write a short explanation using clear definitions and imperative steps.
  3. Fachversion: write a short explanation using passive process style and denser compounds.
  1. Choose a system or tool (real or invented for the task) and list its key concepts.
  2. Draft a precise definition for each concept using the term–category–feature structure.
  3. Write the lay-audience version, expanding any dense compound briefly at first use.
  4. Write the specialist-audience version, using passive process style and full compounds.
  5. Compare both versions against the glossary and correct any inconsistent terms.
  6. Reread both versions and confirm that no sentence in either version invents an unverified compound.
  7. Revise for concision, cutting any sentence that repeats information already given.

Revision checklist:

  • Does every glossary term appear identically in both the lay and specialist versions?
  • Does each definition include a term, a category, and a distinguishing feature?
  • Have I chosen one dominant process style per document rather than mixing both without reason?
  • Have I verified every compound rather than inventing one to fill a gap?
  • Would a true beginner understand the lay version, and would a specialist find the specialist version efficient?

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Specialized Accuracy Requires Source Control

Technical writing in German-speaking workplaces, as elsewhere, is expected to reflect the actual configuration of the system being described rather than a plausible-sounding approximation. A confident, well-formed technical compound does not substitute for verifying that the underlying feature, setting, or step actually works as described. Readers in Germany, Austria, and Switzerland generally expect technical documentation to be checked against the real system before distribution.

Culture and Communication — Avoiding Invented Terminology

When a genuinely new concept has no established German term, careful technical writers mark their proposed term explicitly as a working label rather than presenting it as standard usage. Inventing terminology silently, even when the compound is grammatically well formed, risks creating confusion across a team that later encounters the standard term elsewhere.

Common Mistakes to Recheck

Giving a definition without a distinguishing feature

Error: A learner writes "Der Terminplanungsassistent ist ein Werkzeug" and stops, without stating what makes it different from any other tool.

Correction: Add the relative clause that names the distinguishing feature, as in "...das Teammitgliedern hilft, gemeinsame Termine automatisch zu koordinieren."

Using different terms for the same concept across a document

Error: A learner calls the same setting "die Einstellung" in one paragraph and "die Konfiguration" in another, without signaling that these refer to the same thing.

Correction: Fix one glossary term per concept before drafting, and use that exact term consistently throughout both audience versions.

Inventing a compound instead of verifying it

Error: A learner creates a plausible-looking compound by translating an English technical term word for word, without checking whether the German term is actually used.

Correction: Verify technical compounds against a dictionary, established documentation, or a subject specialist, and mark any genuinely new coinage explicitly as a working term.
