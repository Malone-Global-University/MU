Portfolio und Fachspur

Portfolio and Specialized Track · Speak and Write German: Mastery, Precision, and Specialized Communication · Volume V, Chapter 19

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 19 asks learners to step back from single tasks and assemble a specialized portfolio: a small, coherent collection of work in one domain, revised and presented with commentary. Tobias has decided to build his portfolio around the process-change project that carried him through Chapters 17 and 18 — the dictionary and valency work, the precision editing, and the debate at the internal forum. Frau Arslan now asks him to gather this work into a short portfolio with a glossary, a brief written commentary on his choices, and a two-minute oral presentation. No new grammar system is activated in this chapter. Instead, learners consolidate genre control, domain vocabulary, and editing skill already active from earlier chapters, and practice explaining and defending their own language choices in a specialized track of their own selection.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Select and justify a specialized domain for a short language portfolio.
  2. Assemble a coherent portfolio of revised work, including a glossary of domain vocabulary.
  3. Write a brief commentary explaining and justifying key language and genre choices.
  4. Apply genre-specific conventions consistently across multiple portfolio artifacts.
  5. Present a portfolio orally, summarizing its contents and revision process.
  6. Identify remaining gaps in domain vocabulary or accuracy and describe a plan to address them.

Opening Scenario

After the forum, Frau Arslan tells Tobias, "Das war überzeugend. Könnten Sie das Ganze — die Recherche, die Korrespondenz, die Rede — zu einer kleinen Mappe zusammenstellen? Mit einem kurzen Kommentar und einem Glossar." Tobias realizes that his process-change project already contains most of what a portfolio needs: a briefing note, correspondence, speaking notes, and a glossary of terms he has checked for valency and register. What remains is to select the strongest pieces, revise them once more, explain his choices in a short written commentary, and prepare a brief oral summary for anyone who did not follow the whole project.

  1. Select three or four pieces of prior work that best represent the domain and revise each once more.
  2. Compile a short glossary of the domain's key terms, including gender, valency, and register notes.
  3. Write a brief commentary explaining the portfolio's scope, choices, and remaining limitations.
  4. Prepare and deliver a short oral summary of the portfolio and its revision process.
Wortschatz

Core Vocabulary: Portfolio and Genre Control

This chapter activates vocabulary for describing, assembling, and presenting a specialized portfolio. These items are introduced for productive use in commentary, glossaries, and oral presentation.

Mappe und Aufbau — Portfolio and Structure

  • die Mappe, die Mappen — the portfolio, folder
  • der Fachbereich, die Fachbereiche — the specialized field/domain
  • eine Auswahl treffen — to make a selection
  • der Kommentar, die Kommentare — the commentary
  • überarbeiten — to revise
  • das Glossar, die Glossare — the glossary

Reflexion und Präsentation — Reflection and Presentation

  • eine Entscheidung begründen — to justify a decision
  • eine Lücke feststellen — to identify a gap
  • der Entwicklungsstand, die Entwicklungsstände — the stage of development
  • zusammenstellen — to compile, put together
  • sich auf etwas beziehen (+ Akkusativ) — to refer to something
  • ein Fazit ziehen — to draw a conclusion

The verbs eingehen auf (+ Akkusativ) and widersprechen (+ Dativ) from Chapter 18, and the valency and word-formation vocabulary from Chapter 16 and 17, are review items here; they recur as learners select and justify vocabulary rather than being retaught.

Aussprache

Delivery Focus: Presenting a Portfolio Aloud

Delivery — Summarizing a Body of Work Clearly and Concisely

A portfolio presentation differs from a debate contribution in one important way: the speaker is summarizing a body of prior work rather than defending a position in real time. Use a calm, even pace, a clear falling pitch at the end of each summarized item, and a brief pause before moving from one artifact to the next, so a listener can follow which piece of the portfolio is being described at any moment.

This builds directly on the discourse intonation and presentation delivery developed in Chapter 18; no new sound or stress rule is introduced here, only its application to a longer, multi-part oral summary.

Sprachliche Muster

Language Patterns: Commentary and Justification

This chapter does not introduce a new grammar system. It consolidates connectors, valency-aware vocabulary, and nominal style already active from earlier chapters, applying them to the reflective genre of portfolio commentary.

Structuring a Portfolio Commentary

Baustein — Building Block
Fachbereich nennen → Auswahl begründen → Entwicklung beschreiben → Lücken benennen → Fazit ziehen
a compact structure for a short reflective commentary, recycling cohesive connectors and nominal style from Volume IV and V
In dieser Mappe habe ich mich auf die Kommunikation rund um eine Prozessänderung konzentriert, weil dieses Projekt mehrere Textsorten und Register verbindet.
In this portfolio, I have focused on the communication around a process change, because this project connects several text types and registers.

Justifying a Selection

Language Note — Explaining Why a Piece Was Included

A portfolio commentary must do more than list its contents; it must explain why each piece was chosen. Useful patterns include "Ich habe diesen Text aufgenommen, weil ..." and "Diese Rede zeigt, dass ..." For example: "Ich habe die Redenotizen aus Kapitel 18 aufgenommen, weil sie zeigen, wie ich unter Zeitdruck präzise auf einen Einwand eingehen konnte." ("I included the speaking notes from Chapter 18 because they show how I was able to address an objection precisely under time pressure.") This recycles the subordinate-clause connector weil and the verb eingehen auf from Chapter 18, now applied to self-evaluation rather than live debate.

Naming a Remaining Gap

Language Note — Identifying Limitations Honestly

An honest commentary names at least one remaining gap rather than presenting the portfolio as flawless: "Eine Lücke, die ich noch feststelle, ist ..." or "Bei Fachbegriffen aus dem Bereich X bin ich mir noch nicht ganz sicher." As in Chapter 16, any usage or frequency claim about a term should be checked against a dictionary or corpus rather than stated with false confidence; the commentary should reflect that discipline rather than overstate the learner's expertise.

Modelltext

Model Text: A Portfolio Consultation

A short original exchange between Tobias and Frau Arslan, written for this course, continuing directly from Chapter 18.

Frau Arslan Welche Texte wollen Sie in Ihre Mappe aufnehmen? (Which texts do you want to include in your portfolio?)
Tobias Ich habe die Vorbereitungsnotiz, die Redenotizen und ein kleines Glossar zusammengestellt. Ich habe die Rede aufgenommen, weil sie zeigt, wie ich auf einen Einwand eingehen konnte, ohne meinen Standpunkt aufzugeben. (I've compiled the preparation note, the speaking notes, and a small glossary. I included the speech because it shows how I was able to address an objection without giving up my position.)
Frau Arslan Gut. Und gibt es einen Bereich, in dem Sie sich noch unsicher fühlen? (Good. And is there an area where you still feel uncertain?)
Tobias Eine Lücke, die ich noch feststelle, ist die Verwaltungssprache. Bei einigen Fachbegriffen bin ich mir noch nicht ganz sicher, deshalb habe ich sie im Glossar mit einem Fragezeichen markiert. (A gap I still notice is administrative language. With some specialized terms I'm not entirely sure yet, so I've marked them in the glossary with a question mark.)

This exchange models the honest, specific self-assessment expected in the Writing Workshop and Performance Task below.

Listening Practice

Ask a partner, instructor, or text-to-speech tool to read the following short passage aloud. Listen twice before answering.

Was eine gute Mappe zeigt — Listening Script

Eine gute Sprachmappe zeigt nicht nur fertige Texte, sondern auch, wie diese Texte entstanden sind. Ein kurzer Kommentar erklärt, warum ein bestimmter Text aufgenommen wurde, welche Entscheidungen dabei getroffen wurden und welche Fachbegriffe besonders sorgfältig überprüft werden mussten. Ebenso wichtig ist es, mindestens eine Lücke ehrlich zu benennen, statt so zu tun, als sei die eigene Sprachkompetenz bereits vollständig. Ein Glossar mit Genus, Valenz und Register macht die Mappe außerdem für andere Leserinnen und Leser nützlich, nicht nur für die Verfasserin oder den Verfasser selbst.

3. Warum ist ein Glossar mit Genus, Valenz und Register laut dem Text auch für andere Leserinnen und Leser nützlich?

Sprechen

Speaking Practice: Presenting the Portfolio

Aufgabe: Die Mappe vorstellen

Working with a partner or by recording yourself, deliver a two-minute oral summary of your portfolio, then answer at least one follow-up question about a choice you made or a gap you identified.

Rolle: Verfasserin/Verfasser

  • Name your specialized domain and briefly explain why you chose it.
  • Summarize two or three pieces of work included in the portfolio and why each was selected.
  • Name at least one remaining gap and briefly describe a plan to address it.

Rolle: Zuhörerin/Zuhörer

  • Listen for a clear statement of the domain and the selection choices.
  • Ask one specific follow-up question about a piece of work or a glossary term.
  • Note whether the speaker names a gap honestly rather than avoiding the question.

Bewertungshinweise — Speaking Criteria

  • States the specialized domain and rationale clearly at the start.
  • Summarizes portfolio contents concisely, using accurate domain vocabulary.
  • Justifies at least one selection choice using a connector such as weil.
  • Names at least one remaining gap honestly and specifically.
  • Uses calm, evenly paced delivery appropriate to a reflective summary rather than a live debate.

Solo alternative: Record your two-minute summary, then write out and answer one follow-up question you anticipate a listener would ask.

Reading Practice: A Portfolio Commentary

Kommentarauszug (für den Kurs verfasst) — Commentary Excerpt, Written for This Course

Kommentar zur Mappe: Kommunikation rund um eine Prozessänderung

In dieser Mappe habe ich mich auf die Kommunikation rund um eine geplante Prozessänderung konzentriert, weil dieses Projekt mehrere Textsorten und Register miteinander verbindet: eine Vorbereitungsnotiz, eine mündliche Stellungnahme und ein kleines Fachglossar. Ich habe die Redenotizen aus Kapitel 18 aufgenommen, weil sie zeigen, wie ich unter Zeitdruck präzise auf einen Einwand eingehen konnte, ohne meinen Standpunkt aufzugeben. Eine Lücke, die ich noch feststelle, ist die Verwaltungssprache; einige Fachbegriffe aus diesem Bereich habe ich im Glossar mit einem Fragezeichen markiert, weil ich ihre Valenz noch nicht abschließend überprüft habe. Als Fazit lässt sich sagen, dass die Mappe meine Fähigkeit zeigt, ein Thema über mehrere Textsorten hinweg konsistent und präzise zu behandeln, auch wenn einzelne Fachbegriffe noch weiterer Überprüfung bedürfen.

Verständnisfragen

1. What domain does the commentary focus on, and what three text types does it mention?

2. Warum hat der Verfasser die Redenotizen aus Kapitel 18 in die Mappe aufgenommen?

3. What gap does the writer identify, and why has he marked certain terms with a question mark in the glossary?

4. Identify the connector used to justify a selection choice in the second sentence, and state what type of clause it introduces.

Schreiben

Writing Workshop: Assembling the Portfolio

Assemble a short specialized portfolio of your own (three to four revised pieces of work, a short glossary, and a commentary of about 120 to 180 words), based on the structure and language patterns introduced in this chapter.

Aufgabe — Task

Stellen Sie eine kleine Sprachmappe zu einem Fachbereich Ihrer Wahl zusammen. Wählen Sie drei bis vier überarbeitete Texte aus früheren Kapiteln oder eigenen Projekten aus, erstellen Sie ein kurzes Glossar mit Genus, Valenz und Register, und schreiben Sie einen Kommentar, der Ihre Auswahl begründet und mindestens eine verbleibende Lücke benennt.

Stilistische Ziele — Stylistic Goals

  1. Kohärenter Fachbereich: ensure all selected pieces genuinely relate to one specialized domain.
  2. Begründete Auswahl: justify each selection with a connector such as weil rather than simply listing titles.
  3. Ehrliche Selbsteinschätzung: name at least one real, specific gap rather than a vague or false one.
  1. Choose a specialized domain and select three or four pieces of prior work that represent it.
  2. Revise each selected piece once more for accuracy, register, and valency.
  3. Compile a short glossary of key domain terms, including gender, plural, and case or preposition frames.
  4. Write a commentary stating the domain, justifying your selections, and naming at least one remaining gap.
  5. Draft a one-sentence conclusion using "Als Fazit lässt sich sagen, dass ..."
  6. Reread the whole portfolio for consistency of terminology, register, and spelling across all pieces.

Revision checklist:

  • Do all selected pieces clearly belong to one coherent specialized domain?
  • Is each selection justified with a specific reason rather than a general statement?
  • Does the glossary include gender, plural, and valency information where relevant?
  • Is at least one real, specific gap named honestly?
  • Is terminology, register, and spelling consistent across all pieces in the portfolio?

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Honest Self-Assessment in Professional Contexts

In many professional and academic German-speaking contexts, naming a specific limitation is regarded as a sign of competence rather than weakness, provided the limitation is described precisely and paired with a plan to address it. A commentary that claims complete mastery of a specialized domain is generally viewed with more skepticism than one that names a concrete, well-defined gap. This expectation varies by institution, discipline, and individual, and should not be treated as a fixed national trait.

Regional Awareness — Portfolio and Assessment Conventions Across DACH

Expectations for a professional or academic portfolio, including how explicitly limitations should be stated and how formal the accompanying commentary should be, can differ somewhat across institutions in Germany, Austria, and Switzerland. When preparing a portfolio for a specific audience, note the relevant institutional or national context rather than assuming one practice applies everywhere, continuing the labeling habit from Chapter 14.

Common Mistakes to Recheck

Listing contents instead of justifying selections

Error: A learner writes a commentary that only lists the titles of included texts, such as "Diese Mappe enthält eine Notiz, eine Rede und ein Glossar.", without explaining why each piece was chosen.

Correction: Justify each choice with a reason: "Ich habe diesen Text aufgenommen, weil er zeigt, dass ..."

Claiming complete mastery instead of naming a real gap

Error: A learner writes a commentary claiming no remaining weaknesses, such as "Ich beherrsche diesen Fachbereich vollständig."

Correction: Name a specific, plausible gap instead: "Eine Lücke, die ich noch feststelle, ist ...", as modeled in this chapter.

Inconsistent terminology across portfolio pieces

Error: A learner uses two different terms for the same concept in different pieces of the portfolio, such as switching between Prozessänderung and Prozessumstellung without explanation.

Correction: Choose one term per concept, record it in the glossary, and use it consistently throughout the portfolio, as in Chapter 16's usage-note practice.

Answer Key

Guided Practice

  1. Open production; a good answer follows the pattern "Ich habe mich auf ... konzentriert, weil ..." with a clearly named domain and reason.
  2. Open production; a good answer uses "Ich habe diesen Text aufgenommen, weil ..." with a specific, plausible reason.
  3. Open production; a good glossary entry includes gender and plural for a noun, or valency (case and preposition) for a verb, e.g., "die Prozessänderung, die Prozessänderungen" or "eingehen auf (+ Akkusativ)."
  4. Open production; the sentence should name a specific, plausible gap, e.g., "Eine Lücke, die ich noch feststelle, ist die Verwaltungssprache."
  5. Open production; a good answer uses "Als Fazit lässt sich sagen, dass ..." to summarize the portfolio's overall strength or focus.
