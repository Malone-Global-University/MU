Chapter Overview and Objectives

  5. Organize a short argument section using a clear hierarchy of claim, support, and qualification.
  6. Integrate sources responsibly, with limited, attributed quotation.
  7. Revise a draft for academic cohesion, accuracy, and honest sourcing.

Wortschatz

Core Vocabulary

This chapter's vocabulary supports writing about sources, methods, and arguments: the verbs that report a claim, the nouns that name the parts of a research section, and the connectors that hold a paragraph together.

Quellenarbeit — Working with Sources

  • die Quelle, die Quellen — source
  • der Forschungsstand, (no common plural) — state of research, current literature on a topic
  • die Literaturübersicht, die Literaturübersichten — literature review
  • die Übereinstimmung, die Übereinstimmungen — agreement, consistency (between sources)
  • der Widerspruch, die Widersprüche — contradiction, disagreement (between sources)

Zitierverben — Citation Verbs

  • feststellen — to find, to establish (a relatively strong claim)
  • argumentieren — to argue (a position, not a settled fact)
  • darauf hinweisen — to point out, to note (often a caveat or limitation)
  • vermuten — to suspect, to conjecture (a weaker claim)
  • bestätigen — to confirm (a strong claim, used carefully)

Kohäsionsmittel — Cohesive Devices

  • während — while, whereas (contrast between sources)
  • im Gegensatz dazu — in contrast to this
  • darüber hinaus — beyond this, furthermore
  • in ähnlicher Weise — in a similar way
  • daraus folgt — from this it follows

Aufbau eines Forschungsabschnitts — Structure of a Research Section

  • die Argumentationslinie, die Argumentationslinien — line of argument
  • die Behauptung, die Behauptungen — claim, assertion
  • der Beleg, die Belege — piece of evidence, support
  • die Einschränkung, die Einschränkungen — qualification, limitation
  • die Kohärenz, (no common plural) — coherence

All active nouns are given with article and plural where a plural is established. Citation verbs are grouped by relative strength, not alphabetically, to support the grammar section below.

Aussprache

Delivery Focus: Presenting Findings Aloud

Delivery — Pausing at Cohesive Devices

When reading a research paragraph aloud, a brief pause before connectors such as während, im Gegensatz dazu, and darüber hinaus helps a listener track where one source's claim ends and a comparison or addition begins. Without this small pause, dense synthesis prose can sound like a single unbroken claim rather than a comparison across sources.

This builds on the compound-stress and delivery work from Chapter 6, applied here to the rhythm of connected argument rather than to individual compound words.

Grammatik

Grammar: Source Synthesis, Citation Verbs, and Cohesion

This chapter activates three interconnected writing targets: language for synthesizing sources rather than listing them, citation verbs chosen for accurate strength, and cohesive devices that connect claims into a coherent argument.

From Listing to Synthesis

A list of source claims is not yet a synthesis. Synthesis requires explicitly naming the relationship between sources: agreement, disagreement, partial overlap, or attention to different aspects of the same question.

Baustein — Building Block
Studie A stellt X fest. Studie B stellt Y fest. → Während Studie A X feststellt, weist Studie B darauf hin, dass Y ebenfalls eine Rolle spielt.
two independent statements combined into a single sentence that names the relationship between them
The synthesized version tells the reader how the two findings relate to each other, not merely that both exist.
Study A finds X. Study B finds Y. → While Study A finds X, Study B points out that Y also plays a role.

Citation Verbs and Claim Strength

Citation verbs are not interchangeable. Each one signals how confidently the writer treats the source's claim. Choosing the wrong citation verb changes the apparent strength of the claim, exactly as an unmatched hedge did in Chapter 6.

  • Die Autoren vermuten, dass sich das Muster auch in anderen Branchen zeigt. The authors suspect that the pattern also appears in other industries. "vermuten" marks a tentative, unconfirmed claim
  • Die Studie bestätigt frühere Ergebnisse zu diesem Zusammenhang. The study confirms earlier findings on this connection. "bestätigt" marks a stronger, more confident claim and should only be used when the source itself claims confirmation
Grammar Note — Matching Citation Verb to Source Strength

Before choosing a citation verb, identify how the source itself frames its claim: as a suggestion, a tendency, a finding, or a confirmation. Then select a citation verb of matching strength. Upgrading "die Ergebnisse legen nahe" (the results suggest) to "die Studie beweist" (the study proves) in your own writing repeats the Chapter 6 hedging error in a new form — this time in the writer's own citation choice rather than in a summary of someone else's words.

Cohesive Devices Across Sentences and Paragraphs

Cohesion connects individual sentences into a paragraph that reads as a single, organized argument rather than a sequence of separate statements. Connectors of contrast, addition, similarity, and consequence each perform a distinct logical function.

Grammar Note — Choosing the Right Connector
  • während and im Gegensatz dazu mark contrast between sources or findings.
  • darüber hinaus and in ähnlicher Weise mark addition or similarity.
  • daraus folgt marks a conclusion drawn from what precedes it, and should only introduce a claim that the preceding sentences actually support.
Modelltext

Model Text: Literature Summary and Argument Section

Frau Dr. Aydın's model research note, shared with Tobias as a benchmark for synthesis, methods description, and argument.

Literatur Der Forschungsstand zu Fernarbeit und Teamkommunikation ist uneinheitlich. Während die in Kapitel 6 besprochene Studie eine tendenziell höhere Kommunikationshäufigkeit bei strukturierten Fernarbeitsbedingungen feststellt, weist eine zweite, kleinere Untersuchung darauf hin, dass dieser Effekt vor allem in Teams mit bereits etablierten Kommunikationsroutinen auftritt. (The state of research on remote work and team communication is inconsistent. While the study discussed in Chapter 6 finds a tendentially higher communication frequency under structured remote-work conditions, a second, smaller study points out that this effect mainly occurs in teams with already established communication routines.)
Methode Für die vorliegende Notiz wurden die beiden genannten Studien sowie ein kurzer Kommentarartikel gesichtet und hinsichtlich Stichprobe, Methode und Einschränkungen verglichen. (For the present note, the two studies mentioned, along with a short commentary article, were reviewed and compared with regard to sample, method, and limitations.)
Argument Die verfügbaren Quellen legen nahe, dass strukturierte Kommunikationsroutinen die wahrgenommene Kommunikationshäufigkeit begünstigen können; sie bestätigen jedoch nicht, dass diese Routinen allein für den beobachteten Effekt verantwortlich sind. Daraus folgt, dass weitere, größer angelegte Untersuchungen nötig wären, bevor allgemeinere Empfehlungen formuliert werden könnten. (The available sources suggest that structured communication routines can favor perceived communication frequency; however, they do not confirm that these routines alone are responsible for the observed effect. It follows that further, larger-scale studies would be needed before more general recommendations could be formulated.)

Culture and Communication

Culture and Communication — Academic Integrity Is Mandatory

Responsible source use is a matter of academic and professional integrity, not merely a stylistic preference. Attributing claims accurately, quoting sparingly and with clear attribution, and choosing citation verbs that match a source's actual confidence protect both the writer's credibility and the reader's ability to trust the summary. These expectations apply whether the research note is a classroom exercise or a real professional document.

Culture and Communication — Quotation Must Be Limited and Attributed

Direct quotation should be used sparingly in a research note — typically only when the source's exact wording matters, such as a precise definition or a striking formulation. Most source material should be paraphrased and clearly attributed. Quoting extensively without clear attribution, or presenting paraphrased material as if it were the writer's own original idea, misrepresents the source regardless of the writer's intent.

Common Mistakes to Recheck

Listing sources instead of synthesizing them

Error: A learner writes two separate sentences reporting two sources' claims without stating how the claims relate to each other.

Correction: Combine the claims into a single sentence or connected pair of sentences using a cohesive device such as "während" or "in ähnlicher Weise" that names the relationship — agreement, contrast, or a different aspect of the same question.

Choosing a citation verb that overstates the source

Error: A learner reports a tentative finding using "bestätigt" (confirms) or "beweist" (proves), silently strengthening a cautious claim.

Correction: Match the citation verb to the source's own framing — "vermuten," "nahelegen," or "darauf hinweisen" for tentative claims; "feststellen" or "bestätigen" only when the source itself claims that level of confidence.

Drawing a conclusion the sources do not support

Error: A learner uses "daraus folgt" to introduce a conclusion that goes beyond what the preceding sentences actually establish.

Correction: Before using "daraus folgt" or a similar consequence marker, check that the conclusion is actually supported by the claims stated immediately before it, including their qualifications.

Answer Key

Guided Practice

  1. "Während" opens the paragraph, signaling a contrast between what the Chapter 6 study finds and what the second study points out.
  2. "feststellt" (finds) is a moderate-to-strong claim; "weist darauf hin" (points out) introduces a qualifying observation; "legen nahe" (suggest) is tentative; "bestätigen" (confirm), used in the negative here ("bestätigen jedoch nicht"), marks the boundary of what the sources do not establish.
  3. The preceding sentences state that the sources suggest but do not confirm that routines alone cause the effect; the conclusion that further, larger studies are needed follows reasonably from this qualified claim.
  4. Replacing "legen nahe" with "beweisen" (prove) would claim that the sources demonstrate the connection conclusively, which misrepresents a correlational, hedged finding as an established causal fact.
  5. Qualifications appear in "bestätigen sie jedoch nicht" (however, they do not confirm) and in the argument's final sentence noting that further studies are needed before general recommendations could be made.

Listening Practice — Comprehension Questions

  1. A good literature review should show how sources relate to each other — whether they confirm, contradict, or illuminate different aspects of the same question — not simply list them.
  2. The verb "bestätigen" claims more than most individual studies can actually show, so overusing it risks overstating the evidence.
  3. A citation verb should be chosen to match the strength of the original claim as stated by the source.

Reading Practice — Comprehension Questions

  1. Der Kommentar kritisiert, dass die Debatte sich zu stark auf Kommunikationsroutinen konzentriert und andere wichtige Einflussfaktoren wie Teamgröße, Führungsstil und individuelle Vorlieben übersieht.
  2. "weisen darauf hin" (point out) introduces the concern; it signals a moderate, attributed observation rather than either a tentative guess or a fully confirmed finding.
  3. Open production; a good answer uses a connector such as "darüber hinaus" or "im Gegensatz dazu" to relate the commentary's caution about other factors to the model note's own qualification about the limits of the studied effect.
