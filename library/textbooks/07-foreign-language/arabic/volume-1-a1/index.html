Arabic Volume 1 (A1)

Volume 1 introduces beginning learners to Modern Standard Arabic through practical communication, foundational grammar, everyday vocabulary, reading, listening, writing, and pronunciation. By the end of the volume, students can participate in simple conversations about themselves, their families, daily life, travel, and familiar topics.

Overview

Volume 1 corresponds to the CEFR A1 proficiency level and serves as the foundation for the entire Arabic language curriculum. Students learn the Arabic writing system, essential grammatical structures, high-frequency vocabulary, and practical communication skills through twenty progressively organized chapters.

Lessons emphasize real-world situations including greetings, family, home, food, shopping, health, travel, school, work, and storytelling while continually reinforcing previously learned material through review projects.

Chapters

  • Chapter 1 — Ahlan wa Sahlan: Liqāʾ Ūlā

    Introduces greetings, introductions, pronunciation, and the foundations of Arabic communication.

  • Chapter 2 — Fī Ṣaff al-Lughah al-ʿArabiyyah

    Classroom language, common objects, simple questions, and everyday expressions.

  • Chapter 3 — Al-Huwiyyah wal-Lughāt

    Identity, languages, countries, nationalities, and personal information.

  • Chapter 4 — Al-ʿĀʾilah wal-Aṣdiqāʾ

    Talking about family members, relationships, and friends.

  • Chapter 5 — Review 1: Litaʿāraf

    Integrated review of Chapters 1–4 through practical communication activities.

  • Chapter 6 — Fī al-Bayt wal-Ḥayy

    Describing homes, neighborhoods, rooms, and everyday surroundings.

  • Chapter 7 — Yawmī al-ʿĀdī

    Daily routines, schedules, time, and common activities.

  • Chapter 8 — Al-Ṭaʿām wal-Sharāb

    Food, drinks, restaurants, meals, and ordering in everyday situations.

  • Chapter 9 — Al-Tasawwuq

    Shopping, prices, clothing, and buying everyday items.

  • Chapter 10 — Review 2: Yawm Amali

    Review project reinforcing daily communication and practical vocabulary.

  • Chapter 11 — Fī al-Madīnah

    Getting around town, asking for directions, and public places.

  • Chapter 12 — Waqt al-Farāgh

    Leisure activities, hobbies, entertainment, and free time.

  • Chapter 13 — Al-Ṣiḥḥah wal-ʿĀfiyah

    Health, wellness, visiting the doctor, and describing symptoms.

  • Chapter 14 — Al-Dirāsah wal-ʿAmal

    School, work, occupations, and future goals.

  • Chapter 15 — Review 3: Fī al-Mujtamaʿ

    Integrated review emphasizing communication in community settings.

  • Chapter 16 — Aḥdāth al-Māḍī

    Talking about past events using introductory past-tense structures.

  • Chapter 17 — Al-Safar wal-Riḥlāt

    Travel, transportation, vacations, and planning trips.

  • Chapter 18 — Fī al-Māḍī wa-al-Ān

    Comparing past and present while expanding conversational fluency.

  • Chapter 19 — Sard al-Qiṣaṣ wa-al-Tawāṣul

    Storytelling, narration, and connected communication.

  • Chapter 20 — Al-Mashrūʿ al-Nihāʾī: ʿĀlamī bil-ʿArabiyyah

    Capstone project demonstrating beginner proficiency across all language skills.
