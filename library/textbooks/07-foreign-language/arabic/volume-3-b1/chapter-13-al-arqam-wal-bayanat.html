الأَرْقَام وَالبَيَانَاتChapter 13: Numbers and Data

Percentages, statistics, data commentary, and charts · Speak and Write Arabic: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Overview

In Chapter 12, you learned to compare two options, classify a set of items into groups, draw a contrast, and interpret what a comparison shows. This chapter extends that interpretive skill into the world of numbers: percentages, statistics, and simple charts. You will learn how to state a percentage, describe a rate that rises or falls, cite a figure as evidence, and comment on what a set of numbers demonstrates. No new clause type is introduced; instead, the interpretive expressions from Chapter 12 — يَدُلّ هَذَا عَلَى أَنَّ and تُظْهِر هَذِه المُقَارَنَة أَنَّ — are extended to work with figures instead of qualitative contrasts. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to read a short passage containing statistics, discuss a simple chart with a partner, and write a short paragraph of data commentary.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State and read percentages in Arabic, including expressions such as فِي المِئَة and بِالمِئَة.
  2. Describe a figure using بَلَغَ (يَبْلُغ) and شَكَّلَ (يُشَكِّل) to state what a number reached or represented.
  3. Describe a rate rising or falling using اِرْتَفَعَ (يَرْتَفِع) and اِنْخَفَضَ (يَنْخَفِض), with مِن ... إِلَى to mark the starting and ending point.
  4. Cite a source of data using وَفْقًا لِـ and حَسَب.
  5. Comment on what a set of figures shows using تُشِير البَيَانَات إِلَى أَنَّ and يَدُلّ هَذَا الرَّقْم عَلَى أَنَّ.
  6. Read a short passage containing statistics and identify the figures, their source, and the writer's interpretation.
  7. Write a short, organized paragraph of data commentary that states a figure, cites its source, and interprets what it shows.

Opening Communication Scenario

Your discussion group from Chapter 12 has now received a short report with actual figures on the volunteer-work programs you compared and classified earlier: participation rates, satisfaction percentages, and year-over-year change. Your instructor asks you to summarize the key figures, cite where they came from, and explain what the numbers show about which approach is working better — supporting your earlier qualitative comparison with concrete data.

  1. You will learn how to state percentages and describe rates that rise or fall.
  2. You will practice citing a data source and commenting on what figures demonstrate.
  3. You will read a short passage with statistics, discuss a simple chart with a partner, and write your own data-commentary paragraph.

التَّرْكِيب — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Percentages, Statistics, Data Commentary

This chapter introduces percentages, statistical expressions, and data commentary for the first time, and extends the interpretive pattern from Chapter 12 to work with figures. No new clause type is introduced: the أَنَّ-content clause recycled since Chapter 3 continues to carry the interpretation, now attached to a number rather than a qualitative contrast.

Building Block 1 — Stating a percentage
بَلَغَت النِّسْبَة + رَقْم + فِي المِئَة
This is the chapter's first newly introduced structure. بَلَغَ agrees with the feminine noun النِّسْبَة; the number precedes فِي المِئَة, which remains invariable.
بَلَغَت نِسْبَة الطُّلَّاب الرَّاضِين عَن البَرْنَامَج الاِخْتِيَارِيّ خَمْسَة وَسَبْعِين فِي المِئَة.
The percentage of students satisfied with the elective program reached seventy-five percent.
Building Block 2 — Rising and falling rates
اِرْتَفَعَت/اِنْخَفَضَت النِّسْبَة + مِن + رَقْم + إِلَى + رَقْم
This pattern names the starting and ending figure directly, so the reader sees the size of the change, not only its direction.
اِرْتَفَعَت نِسْبَة المُشَارَكَة فِي البَرَامِج الإِجْبَارِيَّة مِن سِتِّين فِي المِئَة إِلَى خَمْسَة وَثَمَانِين فِي المِئَة خِلَال ثَلَاث سَنَوَات.
The participation rate in mandatory programs rose from sixty percent to eighty-five percent over three years.
Building Block 3 — Citing a source
وَفْقًا لِـ / حَسَب + مَصْدَر، جملة بِرَقْم
Citing a source before or alongside a figure recycles the source-attribution pattern introduced for reported information in Chapter 4 and developed for media language in Chapter 6, now applied to numerical evidence.
وَفْقًا لِتَقْرِير الجَامِعَة، شَكَّلَ الطُّلَّاب الجُدُد نِسْبَة عِشْرِين فِي المِئَة مِن مَجْمُوع المُشَارِكِين هَذَا العَام.
According to the university's report, new students constituted twenty percent of the total participants this year.
Building Block 4 — Interpreting the figures
تُشِير البَيَانَات إِلَى أَنَّ / يَدُلّ هَذَا الرَّقْم عَلَى أَنَّ + جملة
This extends the Chapter 12 interpretive pattern يَدُلّ ... عَلَى أَنَّ so that it now follows from a number or set of statistics rather than a qualitative comparison.
تُشِير البَيَانَات إِلَى أَنَّ اِرْتِفَاع نِسْبَة المُشَارَكَة لَم يُقَابِلهُ اِرْتِفَاع مُمَاثِل فِي نِسْبَة الرِّضَا.
The data indicate that the rise in the participation rate was not matched by a similar rise in the satisfaction rate.

Recycled structures used in this chapter: content clauses with أَنَّ (Ch. 3-4, 11-12), source attribution (Ch. 4, 6), and the interpretive pattern from Chapter 12, now applied to numerical data rather than qualitative contrast alone.

أَمْثِلَة إِضَافِيَّة — Additional examples:

Guided Practice

تَمْرِين ١ — State the percentage. Complete each sentence with a correctly formed percentage expression.

  1. بَلَغَت نِسْبَة الطُّلَّاب الَّذِين يَدْرُسُون عَن بُعْد ______ (40) فِي المِئَة هَذَا العَام.
  2. شَكَّلَ العَمَل التَّطَوُّعِيّ المَحَلِّيّ ______ (65) فِي المِئَة مِن مَجْمُوع السَّاعَات.
  3. حَسَب الإِحْصَاءَات، يَبْلُغ مُعَدَّل الحُضُور ______ (90) فِي المِئَة تَقْرِيبًا.

تَمْرِين ٢ — Describe the change. Use اِرْتَفَعَ or اِنْخَفَضَ with مِن ... إِلَى to describe each change.

  1. نِسْبَة اِسْتِخْدَام وَسَائِل النَّقْل العَامّ: مِن 25% إِلَى 45%.
  2. نِسْبَة الطُّلَّاب الَّذِين يَخْتَارُون الدِّرَاسَة الفَرْدِيَّة: مِن 55% إِلَى 30%.

تَمْرِين ٣ — Interpret the figures. For each pair of statistics below, write one sentence beginning with تُشِير البَيَانَات إِلَى أَنَّ or يَدُلّ هَذَا الرَّقْم عَلَى أَنَّ.

  1. نِسْبَة التَّسْجِيل فِي الدَّوْرَات الإِلِكْتْرُونِيَّة اِرْتَفَعَت مِن 20% إِلَى 55%، لَكِنَّ نِسْبَة إِكْمَال الدَّوْرَة لَم تَتَجَاوَز 35%.
  2. وَفْقًا لِلتَّقْرِير، شَكَّلَت الوَظَائِف عَن بُعْد 30% مِن الوَظَائِف الجَدِيدَة، بَعْد أَن كَانَت 10% قَبْل خَمْس سَنَوَات.

Writing Workshop — A Data Commentary Paragraph

In this workshop, you will write a short, organized paragraph that states a figure, cites its source, describes a change, and interprets what the data show, using the structures taught in this chapter.

  1. Step 1 — Cite your source. Begin with وَفْقًا لِـ or حَسَب plus the source of your figures, real or invented for practice.
  2. Step 2 — State the key figure. Use بَلَغَت النِّسْبَة or شَكَّلَ to give a specific percentage.
  3. Step 3 — Describe the change. Add one sentence using اِرْتَفَعَت or اِنْخَفَضَت with مِن ... إِلَى.
  4. Step 4 — Interpret the data. Close with one sentence beginning with تُشِير البَيَانَات إِلَى أَنَّ or يَدُلّ هَذَا الرَّقْم عَلَى أَنَّ.
  5. Step 5 — Connect to Chapter 12 if useful. If relevant, add a contrast sentence with بَيْنَمَا from the previous chapter to compare two figures.
  6. Review your draft against the checklist below.

قَائِمَة المُرَاجَعَة — Editing checklist:

  • My paragraph cites a source for the figures.
  • I state at least one clear percentage using بَلَغَ or شَكَّلَ.
  • I describe at least one change using اِرْتَفَعَ or اِنْخَفَضَ with مِن ... إِلَى.
  • I interpret the data with تُشِير البَيَانَات إِلَى أَنَّ or a similar expression.
  • I did not introduce any system beyond what this chapter and prior chapters authorized.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Numbers in Arabic media

Arabic-language news and public-information sources frequently open a report with a key statistic before providing context, similar to the pattern practiced in this chapter. Citing a source with وَفْقًا لِـ or حَسَب is standard practice in formal MSA reporting and signals to the reader or listener where the figure comes from.

Regional Variation — Number formatting

Printed numerals vary by region: many Levantine and North African publications use Western Arabic numerals (1, 2, 3), while numerous Gulf and some other publications use Eastern Arabic numerals (١، ٢، ٣). Both are correct within their respective regional conventions, and learners should expect to encounter either form when reading authentic Arabic media.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Omitting فِي المِئَة

Error: stating a bare number as if it were already understood as a percentage, such as بَلَغَت النِّسْبَة خَمْسِين without فِي المِئَة.

Correction: always include فِي المِئَة after the number when stating a percentage, as in بَلَغَت النِّسْبَة خَمْسِين فِي المِئَة.

Common Mistake — Describing a change without both endpoints

Error: writing اِرْتَفَعَت النِّسْبَة إِلَى سَبْعِين فِي المِئَة without stating the starting figure, leaving the size of the change unclear.

Correction: include both points with مِن ... إِلَى, as in اِرْتَفَعَت النِّسْبَة مِن خَمْسِين إِلَى سَبْعِين فِي المِئَة.

Common Mistake — Presenting a figure without interpretation

Error: stating a series of figures and stopping there, without saying what they show, as this volume's interpretation policy from Chapter 12 requires.

Correction: close with an interpretive sentence such as تُشِير هَذِه الأَرْقَام إِلَى أَنَّ ..., so the data lead to a conclusion rather than standing alone.

Chapter Summary

This chapter introduced percentages with فِي المِئَة, rate description with بَلَغَ and شَكَّلَ, change description with اِرْتَفَعَ/اِنْخَفَضَ ... مِن ... إِلَى, and source citation with وَفْقًا لِـ and حَسَب. No new clause type was introduced; the chapter extends the interpretive أَنَّ-clause pattern from Chapter 12 so that it now follows from numerical evidence. Chapter 14 builds on this foundation by introducing passive production and formal process description, so that reports and processes can be described without naming an agent.

Assessment focus: a clearly cited source; at least one correctly formed percentage; at least one correctly formed rise/fall description with both endpoints; a controlled interpretive statement; comprehensible, level-appropriate delivery.

Reading Practice — Comprehension

  1. The figures come from a report by the Ministry of Education.
  2. The registration percentage rose from ten percent to thirty-five percent over five years.
  3. The satisfaction percentage rose from fifty to seventy percent, while the percentage reporting technical problems fell from thirty to fifteen percent.
  4. The writer concludes that improved program quality may be a main reason for the rise in both the registration rate and the satisfaction rate together.

Listening Practice

  • About ninety percent of young people use a smartphone daily, up from seventy percent five years ago.
  • The percentage reading paper books regularly fell from forty to twenty-five percent in the same period.
  • The speaker concludes that smartphone use may be affecting traditional reading habits.
