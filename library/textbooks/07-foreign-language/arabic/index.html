Arabic

The MGU Arabic language series is a comprehensive five-volume curriculum that develops practical communication skills from beginner to advanced proficiency. Organized according to the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR), the series builds speaking, listening, reading, and writing skills while introducing learners to the rich linguistic and cultural heritage of the Arabic-speaking world.

Overview

The Arabic curriculum consists of five progressively structured textbooks that guide students from complete beginner (A1) through advanced independent language use (C1). Each volume combines vocabulary development, grammar, pronunciation, reading, listening, conversation, and writing into an integrated learning experience.

Lessons emphasize real-world communication while gradually introducing more sophisticated vocabulary, authentic texts, formal writing, academic language, and cultural literacy. Throughout the series, learners develop confidence using Modern Standard Arabic in everyday, educational, and professional settings.

Volume 1 (A1): Beginner Arabic

Introduces the Arabic alphabet, pronunciation, essential grammar, foundational vocabulary, and everyday communication for beginning learners.

  • Volume 1 (A1)

    Twenty chapters introducing the Arabic writing system, basic grammar, everyday vocabulary, conversation, reading, listening, writing, and introductory cultural communication.

Volume 2 (A2): Elementary Arabic

Expands conversational ability through longer dialogues, practical writing, daily life situations, and increasingly complex grammatical structures.

  • Volume 2 (A2)

    Twenty chapters that strengthen communication skills through practical reading, listening, writing, and conversation while expanding vocabulary and grammar.

Volume 3 (B1): Intermediate Arabic

Develops independent language use through authentic readings, presentations, discussions, media, and more advanced written communication.

  • Volume 3 (B1)

    Twenty chapters emphasizing independent reading, speaking, listening, writing, analysis, and practical communication across academic and everyday topics.

Volume 4 (B2): Upper Intermediate Arabic

Builds advanced communication skills through formal writing, research, academic reading, public discussion, and professional communication.

  • Volume 4 (B2)

    Twenty chapters focusing on academic Arabic, media literacy, formal presentations, research skills, documentation, and advanced comprehension.

Volume 5 (C1): Advanced Arabic

Refines advanced proficiency through academic discourse, professional communication, literary analysis, translation, and sophisticated written expression.

  • Volume 5 (C1)

    Advanced chapters covering rhetoric, formal argumentation, translation, academic writing, literary texts, research, and professional communication in Modern Standard Arabic.

Series Progression

The five-volume curriculum follows the internationally recognized CEFR proficiency scale, allowing learners to progress from complete beginner through advanced independent language use.
