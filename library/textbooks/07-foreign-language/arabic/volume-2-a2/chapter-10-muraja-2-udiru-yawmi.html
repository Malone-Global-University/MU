مُرَاجَعَة ٢: أُدِيرُ يَوْمِيUnit II Assessment: I Manage My Day

Synthesizing housing, transactions, appointments, and travel · Speak and Write Arabic: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication
Assessment Chapter. Chapter 10 is a synthesis checkpoint. It introduces no major new grammar, vocabulary domain, or writing-system feature. It consolidates the systems authorized through Chapters 6–9 — housing and daily problems, money and transactions, appointments and public services, and detailed travel narration — through an integrated performance task.

Assessment Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Handle a connected sequence of everyday independence tasks — reporting a housing problem, completing a transaction, managing an appointment, and narrating a travel disruption — in a single integrated performance.
  2. Recycle passive recognition, numbers and quantities, subjunctive and jussive forms, and weak-verb narration accurately across new but familiar contexts.
  3. Sequence connected events using purpose clauses, conditional frames, and the time-clause connectors عِنْدَمَا, حِين, قَبْل أَن, and بَعْد أَن.
  4. Continue recognizing nominative, accusative, and genitive case endings in clearly marked phrases, and use the small set of fixed accusative expressions independently.
  5. Read and listen to practical texts spanning housing, services, appointments, and travel without new unscheduled support.
  6. Write and present a connected, multi-part account that draws on all four chapters of Unit II.

Communication Scenario

A demanding week: your apartment needs a repair, you must complete a bank transaction, you have a public-service appointment to arrange, and you are also planning a trip that does not go entirely as scheduled. Unit II has given you the language to manage each of these situations on your own. This chapter asks you to move between them the way real life does — one problem leading into the next — without introducing anything new.

  1. You will review vocabulary and structures from Chapters 6 through 9 in new combinations.
  2. You will recycle requests, complaints, numbers, formal requests, conditionals, subjunctive and jussive forms, weak verbs, and time clauses.
  3. You will continue recognizing case endings and using the short list of fixed accusative expressions.
  4. You will complete an integrated task that requires you to manage several everyday situations in one connected performance.

What This Chapter Reviews

Chapter 10 closes Unit II by drawing together the four chapters of everyday independence: housing and daily problems (Chapter 6), money and transactions (Chapter 7), appointments and public services (Chapter 8), and travel and transportation in more detail (Chapter 9). No new grammar, vocabulary domain, or script feature is introduced here; the chapter's difficulty comes from combining previously authorized systems into one connected sequence of tasks.

Recycled — Ch. 7

Recycled — Ch. 8

Recycled — Ch. 9

Grammar and Structure Review

This section combines four previously activated systems in new sentences. No new rule is introduced; each pattern below was taught in an earlier chapter of Unit II.

Review — Conditional frame (Chapter 7) + subjunctive purpose clause (Chapter 8)
إِذَا + perfect, ... لِـ / كَي + subjunctive

إِذَا لَمْ يُصْلِح الفَنِّيّ الثَّلَّاجَة اليَوْم، سَأَتَّصِل بِالمَالِك كَي يُرْسِل شَخْصًا آخَر.

If the technician does not fix the refrigerator today, I will call the landlord so that he sends someone else.

Review — Weak verb (Chapter 9) + time clause (Chapter 9)
عِنْدَمَا / حِين + perfect weak verb, clause

عِنْدَمَا عُدْت مِن البَنْك، وَجَدْت أَن مَوْعِدِي فِي العِيَادَة قَد تَأَخَّر.

When I returned from the bank, I found that my appointment at the clinic had been delayed.

  • قَبْل أَن أَذْهَب إِلَى المَوْعِد، يَجِب أَن أَمْلَأ الاِسْتِمَارَة.Before I go to the appointment, I must fill out the form.time clause (Ch. 9) + subjunctive after يَجِب أَن (Ch. 8)
  • لَمْ أَنْسَ الإِيصَال هَذِهِ المَرَّة.I did not forget the receipt this time.defective verb (Ch. 9) + jussive with لَمْ (Ch. 8)
  • إِذَا كَانَ الحِسَاب صَحِيحًا، فَلَن أَشْتَكِي.If the bill is correct, I will not complain.condition frame (Ch. 7) + negated future with لَنْ (Ch. 8)

Every structure above recycles a system already at status C or above by Chapter 10; no item introduces a new grammatical category.

Case Awareness Review

Language Focus — Reviewing the fixed accusative list

By this point you can independently use صَبَاحًا, مَسَاءً, يَوْمِيًّا, and مُبَاشَرَةً as whole accusative expressions. All other case endings remain a recognition target: notice them when clearly marked in a text, but do not attempt to add them freely in your own speech or writing.

  • يَفْتَح البَنْك يَوْمِيًّا مَا عَدَا الجُمُعَة.The bank opens daily except Friday.fixed accusative expression, productive
  • وَصَلَ الفَنِّيُّ مُبَاشَرَةً بَعْد اتِّصَالِي.The technician arrived immediately after my call.fixed accusative expression, productive; nominative on الفَنِّيُّ, recognition only

Model Dialogue

Setting: لَيْلَى tells her neighbor سَامِي about her complicated week.
السِّيَاق: تُخْبِر لَيْلَى جَارَهَا سَامِي عَن أُسْبُوعِهَا المُعَقَّد.

SAMI كَيْف كَان أُسْبُوعُك؟ سَمِعْت أَن عِنْدَك مُشْكِلَة فِي الشُّقَّة. How was your week? I heard you have a problem in the apartment.
LAYLA نَعَم، تَعَطَّلَت الثَّلَّاجَة، فَاتَّصَلْت بِالمَالِك كَي يُرْسِل فَنِّيًّا. إِذَا لَمْ يَأْتِ غَدًا، سَأُقَدِّم شَكْوَى رَسْمِيَّة. Yes, the refrigerator broke down, so I called the landlord so that he would send a technician. If he doesn't come tomorrow, I will file a formal complaint.
SAMI وَمَاذَا عَن مَوْعِدِك فِي البَنْك؟ And what about your appointment at the bank?
LAYLA عِنْدَمَا وَصَلْت إِلَى البَنْك، وَجَدْت أَن الحِسَاب كَان صَحِيحًا، فَلَم أَحْتَج إِلَى الشَّكْوَى الَّتِي خَطَّطْت لَهَا. When I arrived at the bank, I found that the bill was correct, so I didn't need the complaint I had planned to make.
SAMI خَبَر جَيِّد! وَهَل سَافَرْت أَخِيرًا كَمَا كُنْت تُخَطِّط؟ Good news! And did you finally travel as you had planned?
LAYLA نَعَم، لَكِن بَعْد أَن طَارَت الطَّائِرَة بِسَاعَة تَأَخِير. بَقِيت فِي المَطَار طَوِيلًا، لَكِنِّي لَم أَنْسَ أَمْتِعَتِي هَذِهِ المَرَّة! Yes, but after the plane took off an hour late. I stayed at the airport a long time, but I didn't forget my luggage this time!

أَسْئِلَة الفَهْم — Comprehension questions:

  1. What did Layla tell the landlord she needed, and under what condition will she file a formal complaint?
  2. What did Layla discover at the bank, and how did that change her plans?
  3. What travel disruption did Layla mention, and what did she do differently this time?

A Busy Week — Sample Text

كان هذا الأسبوع صعبا جدا. أولا، تعطلت الثلاجة في شقتي، فاتصلت بالمالك كي يرسل فنيا. إذا لم يأت الفني خلال يومين، سأقدم شكوى رسمية. ثانيا، ذهبت إلى البنك للتحقق من حسابي، وعندما وصلت، وجدت أن كل شيء صحيح. ثالثا، كان عندي موعد في العيادة، فملأت الاستمارة قبل أن أصل، وهذا وفر علي وقتا طويلا. أخيرا، سافرت لزيارة أهلي، لكن بعد أن تأخرت رحلتي ساعة كاملة، بقيت في المطار طويلا. مع ذلك، لم أنس أمتعتي هذه المرة، وعدت أخيرا وأنا بخير.

أَسْئِلَة الفَهْم — Comprehension questions:

  1. What condition does the writer set for filing a formal complaint about the refrigerator?
  2. What did the writer do before the clinic appointment that saved time?
  3. Identify one time-clause connector in the passage and the event it introduces.

Writing Station

In this station, you will draft a connected account of eight to ten sentences narrating a busy week that included a housing problem, a transaction or appointment, and a travel disruption.

  1. Set the scene. One sentence stating that the week was busy or complicated.
  2. Describe the housing problem. One or two sentences using a conditional frame or a subjunctive purpose clause, recycled from Chapters 6–8.
  3. Describe the transaction or appointment. One or two sentences using a formal request, a number or quantity, or an institutional expression, recycled from Chapters 7–8.
  4. Narrate the travel moment. One or two sentences using a hollow or defective verb and at least one time-clause connector, recycled from Chapter 9.
  5. Mention what did not happen. One sentence using لَمْ + jussive, recycled from Chapter 8.
  6. Close and revise. One closing sentence, then check your draft against the editing checklist below.

قَائِمَة المُرَاجَعَة — Editing checklist:

  • I included at least one situation from each of housing, transactions/appointments, and travel.
  • I used at least one conditional frame and one subjunctive purpose clause, recycled accurately.
  • I used at least one hollow or defective verb and one time-clause connector from Chapter 9.
  • I used لَمْ + jussive at least once, recycled accurately from Chapter 8.
  • I did not attempt to add case endings on my own beyond the fixed accusative expressions.
  • My account reads as one connected week rather than a list of unrelated facts.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Managing several errands in one day

Across many Arabic-speaking contexts, service hours, appointment systems, and repair timelines vary by country, city, and institution, so learners should expect some flexibility in how quickly a problem is resolved. Politely following up on a pending request, as Layla does in the model dialogue, is a normal and accepted part of managing daily errands rather than a sign of impatience.

Regional Variation — Words for everyday institutions

Terms such as بَنْك, عِيَادَة, and مَكْتَب الصِّيَانَة are widely understood across MSA contexts, but some countries and institutions use additional local terms, abbreviations, or English loanwords in signage and paperwork. Learners should expect to recognize both the taught MSA term and any locally posted variant.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Mixing subjunctive and jussive triggers

Error: لَمْ يَأْتِي الفَنِّيّ (using an indicative-looking form after لَمْ instead of the jussive)

Correction: لَمْ يَأْتِ الفَنِّيّ — لَمْ requires the following imperfect verb to take the jussive, as taught in Chapter 8.

Common Mistake — Treating a hollow verb as fully regular under a new topic

Error: عَادْت مِن البَنْك (keeping the long vowel in a perfect form that takes a subject suffix)

Correction: عُدْت مِن البَنْك — the hollow-verb pattern from Chapter 9 applies in any topic, not only travel narration.

Common Mistake — Adding case endings freely across a longer account

Error: attempting to add a full set of case endings throughout a long connected paragraph, beyond the fixed accusative list.

Correction: continue limiting independent case production to صَبَاحًا, مَسَاءً, يَوْمِيًّا, and مُبَاشَرَةً; recognize other case endings when clearly marked, but rely on natural pause forms elsewhere.

Answer Key

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. Layla told the landlord she needed a technician; she will file a formal complaint if he does not come by the next day.
  2. At the bank, Layla found the bill was correct, so she did not need the complaint she had planned to make.
  3. Layla mentioned that her flight took off an hour late; this time, she did not forget her luggage.

Listening Station

  • If the appointment is inconvenient, the listener should call back before five in the evening so a new appointment can be set.
  • Listeners are reminded not to forget to bring the receipt when the technician visits.

Reading Station — Comprehension

  1. The writer will file a formal complaint if the technician does not come within two days.
  2. The writer filled out the form before arriving at the clinic, which saved a long time.
  3. عِنْدَمَا introduces "when I arrived [at the bank]"; بَعْد أَن introduces "after my flight was delayed a full hour."
