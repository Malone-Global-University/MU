18

الرَّأْي وَالتَّفْضِيل وَالسَّبَبOpinion, Preference, and Reason

Opinion frames, comparison, justification, and agreement/disagreement · Speak and Write Arabic: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Overview

Chapter 18 turns from interpreting what other people say to expressing your own point of view. You have already compared routines in Chapter 3, reported what articles and announcements say in Chapters 13 and 17, and reacted to experiences in Chapter 16. This chapter brings those threads together into structured opinion language: stating a view, comparing two or more things, giving a reason, and agreeing or disagreeing with someone else's position. The chapter does not introduce a new tense, mood, or clause type. Instead, it teaches a small set of opinion frames and justification connectors, expands your existing comparison language into fuller argument, and introduces academic register as a new, more formal style found in essays, reports, and classroom discussion. You will discuss preferences and public topics, read a short opinion piece, and write a short paragraph that states, supports, and justifies a position.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State an opinion using فِي رَأْيِي، أَظُنّ أَنَّ، and أَعْتَقِد أَنَّ.
  2. Compare two or more things using أَفْضَل مِن، أَكْثَر مِن، and أَقَلّ مِن, recycled and expanded from the comparison language introduced in Chapter 3.
  3. Give a reason for an opinion using لِأَنَّ and بِسَبَب, and state a resulting conclusion using لِذَلِكَ.
  4. Agree and disagree politely using أُوَافِقُكَ الرَّأْيَ، لا أُوَافِقُ تَمَامًا، and مِن نَاحِيَةٍ أُخْرَى.
  5. Recognize academic register as a more formal, structured style distinct from both everyday and media Arabic, recycling register-labeling skills from Chapter 17.
  6. Read a short opinion article and identify its stance, supporting reasons, and register.
  7. Write a short paragraph of five to seven sentences that states an opinion, supports it with a reason, and closes with a conclusion.

Opening Communication Scenario

Imagine a classmate or colleague asks what you think about a familiar everyday topic — working from home, a local transportation change, or a popular show — and you want to give more than a one-word answer. You want to state your view clearly, explain why you hold it, compare it with an alternative, and respond respectfully if the other person disagrees. This chapter gives you the frames to do exactly that in Contemporary MSA, moving from a simple reaction to a short, organized opinion.

  1. You will learn vocabulary and frames for stating an opinion.
  2. You will practice comparing options and giving reasons for a preference.
  3. You will learn to agree and disagree politely in discussion.
  4. You will read, discuss, and write a short opinion paragraph.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — Stress and pacing in stating an opinion

When you open a statement with فِي رَأْيِي or أَظُنّ أَنَّ, place a small emphasis on the opening phrase and pause briefly before the أَنَّ-clause that follows. This slight pause signals to a listener that a personal view is coming, distinct from a simple factual statement. Continue applying natural pause-form pronunciation and hamzat al-waṣl linking across each sentence, exactly as practiced since Volume I.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter does not introduce a new tense, mood, or clause type. It consolidates the أَنَّ-clause pattern you have used since Chapter 13 with two new opinion verbs, expands the comparison language from Chapter 3 into fuller argument, and adds justification and agreement/disagreement frames built on familiar particles.

Consolidation — Opinion frames with أَنَّ-clauses

أَظُنّ and أَعْتَقِد work exactly like قَالَ, ذَكَرَ, and أَشَارَ إِلَى from Chapters 13 and 17: they are followed by أَنَّ and a full clause. What is new is the meaning — you are now stating your own view rather than reporting someone else's — and the fixed opening phrase فِي رَأْيِي, which introduces an opinion without any following particle.

  • فِي رَأْيِي، العَمَل مِن البَيْت أَفْضَل مِن العَمَل فِي المَكْتَب.In my opinion, working from home is better than working in the office.فِي رَأْيِي + comparison, no particle needed
  • أَظُنّ أَنَّ هَذَا البَرْنَامَج أَكْثَر فَائِدَة مِن البَرَامِج الأُخْرَى.I think that this program is more useful than the other programs.أَظُنّ + أَنَّ, recycled Ch. 13 pattern
  • أَعْتَقِد أَنَّ التَّغْيِير ضَرُورِيّ لِأَنَّ الوَضْع الحَالِيّ لَه عُيُوب كَثِيرَة.I believe that the change is necessary because the current situation has many disadvantages.أَعْتَقِد + أَنَّ + لِأَنَّ reason
New — Justification and conclusion connectors
لِأَنَّ + full clause = "because" | بِسَبَب + noun phrase = "because of" | لِذَلِكَ = "therefore, for that reason"

لِأَنَّ introduces a full clause with its own subject and verb, exactly like أَنَّ. بِسَبَب is followed directly by a noun or noun phrase, not a full clause. لِذَلِكَ opens a new sentence to state a conclusion that follows from the reason just given.

لا أُفَضِّل السَّفَر فِي الصَّيْف بِسَبَب الزِّحَام الشَّدِيد، لِذَلِكَ أُخَطِّط لِلسَّفَر فِي الرَّبِيع.

I do not prefer traveling in summer because of the heavy crowding, so I am planning to travel in spring.

New — Agreement and disagreement

Use أُوَافِقُكَ الرَّأْيَ (I agree with you) to accept another person's opinion, and لا أُوَافِق تَمَامًا (I don't completely agree) to disagree politely without rejecting the person outright. مِن نَاحِيَةٍ أُخْرَى (on the other hand) introduces a contrasting point within the same discussion, recycling the contrast function you met with لَكِنْ in earlier chapters.

  • أُوَافِقُكَ الرَّأْيَ، البَرْنَامَج فِعْلًا مُفِيد جِدًّا.I agree with you, the program is really very useful.
  • لا أُوَافِق تَمَامًا، فِي رَأْيِي البَرْنَامَج القَدِيم كَانَ أَفْضَل.I don't completely agree; in my opinion the old program was better.
  • هَذَا صَحِيح، وَمِن نَاحِيَةٍ أُخْرَى يَجِب أَن نُفَكِّر فِي السَّعْر أَيْضًا.That's true, and on the other hand we should think about the price too.
Recognition — Academic register (introduced here for the first time)

لُغَة أَكَادِيمِيَّة uses fuller sentences, fewer contracted or conversational expressions, and more explicit connectors such as عِلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ (furthermore) or بِنَاءً عَلَى ذَلِكَ (based on that). At this stage, you only need to recognize and label this register when you see it; you are not yet asked to produce full academic prose. That skill is developed later in the volume.

Model Dialogue

Context: two colleagues, on friendly professional terms, discussing a workplace change during a coffee break.

سَامِي: مَا رَأْيُكِ فِي نِظَام العَمَل الجَدِيد؟What is your opinion of the new work system?
هَنَاء: فِي رَأْيِي، هُوَ أَفْضَل مِن النِّظَام القَدِيم لِأَنَّه يُعْطِينَا وَقْتًا أَكْثَر مَعَ العَائِلَة.In my opinion, it is better than the old system because it gives us more time with family.
سَامِي: أُوَافِقُكِ الرَّأْيَ مِن نَاحِيَة الوَقْت، لَكِنّ لا أُوَافِق تَمَامًا مِن نَاحِيَة التَّوَاصُل بَيْن الزُّمَلَاء.I agree with you regarding time, but I don't completely agree regarding communication between colleagues.
هَنَاء: هَذَا صَحِيح، هَذِه مِيزَة لِلنِّظَام القَدِيم. وَمَا هُوَ أَكْبَر عَيْب فِي النِّظَام الجَدِيد فِي رَأْيِك؟That's true, that's an advantage of the old system. And what is the biggest disadvantage of the new system in your opinion?
سَامِي: أَعْتَقِد أَنَّ الاِجْتِمَاعَات أَصْبَحَت أَصْعَب بِسَبَب المَسَافَة، لِذَلِكَ أَقْتَرِح أَن نَجْتَمِع مَرَّة كُلّ أُسْبُوع فِي المَكْتَب.I believe that meetings have become harder because of the distance, so I suggest we meet once a week at the office.
هَنَاء: فِكْرَة مُمْتَازَة! أُوَافِقُكَ الرَّأْيَ تَمَامًا.Excellent idea! I completely agree with you.

Guided Practice

  2. Give a reason for this opinion using لِأَنَّ: أَظُنّ أَنَّ العَمَل الجَمَاعِيّ مُفِيد ___.
  3. Rewrite this sentence using بِسَبَب instead of لِأَنَّ: لا أُحِبّ هَذَا المَكَان لِأَنَّه بَعِيد جِدًّا.
  4. Respond to this opinion first with agreement, then with polite disagreement: "فِي رَأْيِي، الدِّرَاسَة فِي الصَّبَاح أَفْضَل مِن الدِّرَاسَة فِي المَسَاء."

Listening Practice

فِي رَأْيِي، اِسْتِخْدَام الدَّرَّاجَة فِي المَدِينَة أَفْضَل مِن اِسْتِخْدَام السَّيَّارَة، لِأَنَّه أَرْخَص وَأَسْرَع فِي الشَّوَارِع المُزْدَحِمَة. صَدِيقِي لا يُوَافِق تَمَامًا: هُوَ يَظُنّ أَنَّ الدَّرَّاجَة غَيْر مُرِيحَة فِي الطَّقْس الحَارّ، وَمِن نَاحِيَة أُخْرَى، السَّيَّارَة أَكْثَر أَمَانًا لِلْعَائِلَة. بِسَبَب هَذَا الاِخْتِلَاف، قَرَّرْنَا أَن نَسْتَخْدِم الدَّرَّاجَة فِي أَيَّام العَمَل وَالسَّيَّارَة فِي عُطْلَة نِهَايَة الأُسْبُوع.

مُهِمَّة الاسْتِمَاع — Listening task

  1. What opinion does the speaker state at the beginning?
  2. What reason does the speaker give for this opinion?
  3. What is the friend's opposing view, and what reasons support it?
  4. What compromise did the two people decide on?

Speaking Practice

تَدْرِيب مُوَجَّه — Controlled practice: Stating and justifying an opinion

With a partner, take turns choosing a familiar topic (a food, a city, a way of studying) and stating an opinion about it using فِي رَأْيِي or أَظُنّ أَنَّ, followed by a reason with لِأَنَّ.

مُهِمَّة تَوَاصُل — Communicative task: Agreeing and disagreeing

In pairs, discuss a familiar public or everyday topic. One partner states an opinion with a reason; the other responds with agreement or polite disagreement and adds مِن نَاحِيَةٍ أُخْرَى to introduce a different point. Continue the exchange for at least four turns.

لِلْمُتَعَلِّمِينَ المُسْتَقِلِّينَ — Solo alternative

Record yourself stating an opinion on a familiar topic in four to six connected sentences, including a comparison, a reason with لِأَنَّ or بِسَبَب, and a conclusion with لِذَلِكَ.

Writing Workshop

In this workshop, you will write a short paragraph of five to seven sentences that states, supports, and closes an opinion.

  1. State your opinion. One sentence using فِي رَأْيِي or أَظُنّ / أَعْتَقِد أَنَّ.
  2. Compare. One sentence comparing your preferred option with an alternative, using أَفْضَل مِن، أَكْثَر مِن، or أَقَلّ مِن.
  3. Give a reason. One or two sentences using لِأَنَّ or بِسَبَب.
  4. Acknowledge another view. One sentence introduced with مِن نَاحِيَةٍ أُخْرَى.
  5. Close with a conclusion. One sentence using لِذَلِكَ.
  6. Review your draft against the editing checklist below.

قَائِمَة المُرَاجَعَة — Editing checklist:

  • I stated a clear opinion using فِي رَأْيِي, أَظُنّ, or أَعْتَقِد.
  • I included at least one comparison using أَفْضَل مِن، أَكْثَر مِن، or أَقَلّ مِن.
  • I gave at least one reason using لِأَنَّ or بِسَبَب.
  • I acknowledged a different point of view using مِن نَاحِيَةٍ أُخْرَى.
  • I closed with a conclusion using لِذَلِكَ.
  • My paragraph reads as one connected piece of writing rather than a list of unrelated sentences.
  • I did not attempt spontaneous full case-ending production beyond the fixed accusative expressions already practiced.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Sharing opinions politely across Arabic-speaking communities

In many Arabic-speaking professional and academic settings, opinions are commonly softened with phrases such as فِي رَأْيِي or مِن وِجْهَة نَظَرِي before a direct statement, and disagreement is often introduced gently with phrases like لا أُوَافِق تَمَامًا rather than a blunt rejection. This pattern of softening a disagreement while still stating a clear position is a widely useful communicative habit, though the exact degree of directness varies by relationship, setting, and individual speaking style.

Regional Variation — Everyday debate across countries and platforms

Everyday spoken debate about topics like transportation, food, or entertainment often shifts into regional spoken Arabic, even among speakers who write and read in MSA. A classroom or written discussion, however, generally remains in MSA. Recognizing when a shift to a regional variety is happening, without treating it as an error, is a useful part of everyday listening comprehension.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing لِأَنَّ and بِسَبَب

Error: writing لا أُحِبّ هَذَا المَكَان بِسَبَب أَنَّه بَعِيد, following بِسَبَب with a full clause.

Correction: بِسَبَب takes a noun phrase, not a full أَنَّ-clause: لا أُحِبّ هَذَا المَكَان بِسَبَب البُعْد. Use لِأَنَّ when a full clause is needed: لا أُحِبّ هَذَا المَكَان لِأَنَّه بَعِيد.

Common Mistake — Overusing flat disagreement

Error: responding to every opposing view with أَنَا لا أُوَافِق and no softening or explanation.

Correction: soften disagreement and add a reason, as in لا أُوَافِق تَمَامًا، لِأَنَّ فِي رَأْيِي هُنَاك مِيزَة أُخْرَى لِلْخِيَار الثَّانِي.

Common Mistake — Treating academic register as identical to media register

Error: assuming that any formal-sounding sentence is لُغَة الإِعْلَام.

Correction: compare the sentence to all three register labels — لُغَة يَوْمِيَّة، لُغَة الإِعْلَام، and لُغَة أَكَادِيمِيَّة — before labeling it; academic register uses fuller connectors such as عِلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ and tends to appear in essays and reports rather than headlines or announcements.

  1. Model: فِي رَأْيِي، القِرَاءَة أَفْضَل مِن مُشَاهَدَة التِّلِفِزْيُون.
  2. Model: أَظُنّ أَنَّ العَمَل الجَمَاعِيّ مُفِيد لِأَنَّه يُوَفِّر وَقْتًا وَجُهْدًا.
  3. لا أُحِبّ هَذَا المَكَان بِسَبَب البُعْد.
  4. Agreement model: أُوَافِقُكَ الرَّأْيَ، الدِّرَاسَة فِي الصَّبَاح أَفْضَل فِعْلًا. Disagreement model: لا أُوَافِق تَمَامًا، فِي رَأْيِي الدِّرَاسَة فِي المَسَاء أَهْدَأ.
  5. The sentence is لُغَة أَكَادِيمِيَّة: it uses the connector عِلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ and refers to الدِّرَاسَات (studies), which is typical of formal academic or report style rather than everyday or headline language.

Listening Practice

  • The speaker states that using a bicycle in the city is better than using a car.
  • The speaker gives the reason that a bicycle is cheaper and faster in crowded streets.
  • The friend disagrees, believing the bicycle is uncomfortable in hot weather and that a car is safer for the family.
  • They decided to use the bicycle on workdays and the car on the weekend.

Reading Practice — Comprehension

  1. The writer's main opinion is that reading every day is one of the best habits a person can build.
  2. The writer's reason is that reading helps one think more clearly, since it leaves time for reflection.
  3. The writer grants that television has the advantage of bringing the family together in one place.
  4. The writer concludes that people should balance the two habits rather than choosing only one.

Chapter Review — Model responses

Section A.1: آرَاء; أَسْبَاب.

Section A.2: لُغَة أَكَادِيمِيَّة is the fuller, more structured style typical of essays, reports, and classroom discussion, using explicit connectors such as عِلَاوَةً عَلَى ذَلِكَ, while لُغَة الإِعْلَام is the more compact style typical of headlines, announcements, and short published articles.

Section B: أَعْتَقِد أَنَّ العَمَل مِن البَيْت أَفْضَل مِن العَمَل فِي المَكْتَب. وَذَلِكَ لِأَنَّه يُوَفِّر وَقْتًا لِلْعَائِلَة. لِذَلِكَ، أُفَضِّل هَذَا النِّظَام.

Other well-formed, accurate answers combining the required systems are equally acceptable throughout this section.
