بِدَايَة جَدِيدَة: حَيَاتِي الآنA New Start: My Life Now

Consolidating Volume I, expanding pronoun reference across connected sentences, and beginning routine unvocalized reading · Speak and Write Arabic: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Reintroduce yourself and describe your current life in three or four connected sentences, drawing directly on the identity, family, and daily-routine systems from Volume I.
  2. Track a pronoun or a noun across two or more sentences without repeating the full noun unnecessarily, using independent pronouns, attached pronouns, and demonstratives already introduced in Volume I.
  3. Expand a short list of separate sentences into one connected paragraph using simple sequence and addition connectors.
  4. Read a short personal paragraph that leaves some predictable short vowels unmarked, and use context and known word shapes to read it accurately.
  5. Recognize which Volume I systems this chapter recycles and identify any personal gaps to review before continuing into Volume II.
  6. Complete a short spoken and written self-update task that could realistically open a conversation with someone you have not spoken to in some time.

Communication Scenario

You are about to see an old classmate, أَحْمَد, for the first time in almost a year. You want to catch him up on your life — where you are living now, what you are doing, and how your daily routine has changed — without simply listing disconnected facts the way a beginner might. This chapter is a bridge: it does not teach a large new grammar system. Instead, it asks you to take everything you built in Volume I and use it more smoothly, in longer, better-connected stretches of speech and writing.

  1. You already know how to introduce yourself, describe your family and home, and talk about your daily routine in simple sentences.
  2. This chapter shows you how to connect those simple sentences into a short, natural update, and how to keep track of who or what you are talking about without restating the same noun again and again.

التَّرْكِيز النَّحْوِيّ — Grammar Focus

Pronoun Reference Across Sentences

In Volume I, you learned independent pronouns, attached pronouns, and demonstratives mostly one sentence at a time. This chapter asks you to use those same tools to keep track of a person or thing across two, three, or four connected sentences, so your speech and writing sound like one continuous account rather than a list of separate facts.

Building Block — Introducing a person, then referring back
full noun (first mention) → independent or attached pronoun (later mentions)

عِنْدِي صَدِيق اِسْمُه كَرِيم. هُوَ يَسْكُن قَرِيبًا مِن بَيْتِي، وَأَنَا أَزُورُه كُلَّ أُسْبُوع.

I have a friend named Karim. He lives near my house, and I visit him every week.

Notice that كَرِيم is named only once. After that, هُوَ (he) and the attached pronoun on أَزُورُه (I visit him) carry the reference forward. Repeating كَرِيم in every sentence would be grammatically possible but would sound unnatural and repetitive — exactly the pattern this chapter asks you to avoid.

  • هٰذِهِ أُسْرَتِي. هِيَ أُسْرَة صَغِيرَة وَسَعِيدَة.This is my family. It is a small, happy family.demonstrative + later independent pronoun
  • اِنْتَقَلْتُ إِلَى شَقَّة جَدِيدَة. هِيَ قَرِيبَة مِن العَمَل، وَأُحِبُّهَا كَثِيرًا.I moved to a new apartment. It is close to work, and I like it a lot.noun → pronoun هِيَ → attached object pronoun on أُحِبُّهَا
Grammar Note — Why this matters now

You are not learning a new pronoun system in this chapter. You already know these forms. What changes is the span of text you control: instead of managing agreement inside one sentence, you are now managing reference across a small paragraph. This is exactly the skill that turns a list of facts into a connected personal update.

Expanding a Paragraph

A second, closely related skill this chapter develops is joining short sentences with simple connectors you already know, so that a set of separate facts becomes one connected update.

Building Block — From separate facts to one update
fact 1 + وَ (and) + fact 2 + أَيْضًا (also) + fact 3

اِسْمِي هُدَى. أَسْكُن الآن فِي مَدِينَة جَدِيدَة، وَأَعْمَل فِي شَرِكَة صَغِيرَة. أَيْضًا، بَدَأْتُ أَتَعَلَّم السِّبَاحَة مُنْذُ فَتْرَة.

My name is Huda. I now live in a new city, and I work at a small company. Also, I started learning to swim a while ago.

  • كَمَا تَعْلَم، اِنْتَقَلْتُ إِلَى بَيْت جَدِيد.As you know, I moved to a new house.formulaic opener + perfect verb
  • لَا أَزَال أَعْمَل فِي نَفْس الشَّرِكَة، لَكِن بِالإِضَافَة إِلَى ذَلِك، بَدَأْتُ دِرَاسَة جَدِيدَة.I still work at the same company, but in addition to that, I started a new course of study.still + but + addition phrase

These are the same connectors you met in Volume I, now used together, back to back, to carry a listener or reader smoothly from one piece of your update to the next.

القِرَاءَة — Script and Reading

Reading Without Full Vocalization

Script Note — A staged change in how texts look

In Volume I, most texts you read were fully or heavily marked with short vowels. Starting in this chapter, short vowels on familiar, high-frequency words may be left unmarked, while new or ambiguous words remain marked to support you. This is not a new writing system and not a new set of letters — it is simply less visual support on words your eye already recognizes.

Two habits will help you read these texts smoothly:

  • Recognize whole familiar words by their consonant shape rather than sounding out every mark.
  • Use sentence context to supply the vowel pattern of a word you already know how to pronounce.

You will see this staged reduction in the Reading Station below. If a word ever feels genuinely unclear rather than simply unmarked, treat it as new vocabulary and look it up, rather than assuming you have made an error.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Huda runs into her old classmate Ahmad after almost a year.
السِّيَاق: تُقَابِل هُدَى زَمِيلَهَا القَدِيم أَحْمَد بَعْد قُرَابَة عَام.

AHMAD هُدَى! مِن زَمَان! كَيْف حَيَاتُك الآن؟ Huda! It's been a long time! How is your life now?
HUDA أَهْلًا أَحْمَد! كَمَا تَعْلَم، اِنْتَقَلْتُ إِلَى مَدِينَة جَدِيدَة. هِيَ مَدِينَة صَغِيرَة وَهَادِئَة، وَأَنَا أُحِبُّهَا كَثِيرًا. Hello Ahmad! As you know, I moved to a new city. It's a small, quiet city, and I like it a lot.
AHMAD جَمِيل! وَمَاذَا عَن العَمَل؟ Nice! And what about work?
HUDA لَا أَزَال أَعْمَل فِي نَفْس الشَّرِكَة، لَكِن بِالإِضَافَة إِلَى ذَلِك، بَدَأْتُ أَتَعَلَّم اللُّغَة الفَرَنْسِيَّة مُنْذُ فَتْرَة. I still work at the same company, but in addition to that, I started learning French a while ago.
AHMAD وَعَائِلَتُك؟ هَل تَغَيَّر شَيْء؟ And your family? Has anything changed?
HUDA أُسْرَتِي بِخَيْر. أَخِي الصَّغِير الآن فِي الجَامِعَة، وَهُوَ يَدْرُس الهَنْدَسَة. My family is well. My little brother is now at university, and he's studying engineering.
AHMAD مَا شَاء الله! سَعِيد لِأَنَّ الأُمُور بِخَيْر مَعَكُم. Wonderful! I'm glad things are going well for you all.

أَسْئِلَة الفَهْم — Comprehension questions:

  1. What has changed in Huda's life, and what has stayed the same?
  2. Find one place where Huda refers back to something with a pronoun instead of repeating the noun. What does the pronoun refer to?
  3. Find the formulaic opener Huda uses to begin her update. Where might you use it yourself?

Listening Station

Ask your instructor, a partner, or a text-to-speech tool to read the following short personal update aloud twice at a moderate pace. Listen without reading along the first time, then answer the questions below.

اِسْمِي سَامِي. كَمَا تَعْلَمُون، سَكَنْتُ فِي القَاهِرَة لِسَنَوَات طَوِيلَة. الآن، اِنْتَقَلْتُ إِلَى مَدِينَة الإِسْكَنْدَرِيَّة مَع أُسْرَتِي. هِيَ مَدِينَة عَلَى البَحْر، وَنَحْن نُحِبُّهَا كَثِيرًا. لَا أَزَال أَعْمَل مِن البَيْت، لَكِن بِالإِضَافَة إِلَى ذَلِك، بَدَأْتُ أَذْهَب إِلَى النَّادِي كُلَّ صَبَاح. أَيْضًا، اِبْنَتِي الصَّغِيرَة بَدَأَت المَدْرَسَة مُنْذُ فَتْرَة، وَهِيَ سَعِيدَة جِدًّا هُنَاك.

مُهِمَّة الاسْتِمَاع — Listening task

List, in order, the pieces of Sami's update (where he lives now, work, new habit, daughter). Then answer:

  1. Where did Sami used to live, and where does he live now?
  2. Find the sentence where a pronoun refers back to the city. What does هِيَ refer to?
  3. What has Sami started doing recently, in addition to his usual routine?

Speaking Station

تَدْرِيب مُوَجَّه — Controlled practice: Update frame

With a partner, take turns completing this frame aloud: كَمَا تَعْلَم/تَعْلَمِين، ______. الآن، ______. لَا أَزَال ______، لَكِن بِالإِضَافَة إِلَى ذَلِك، ______.

مُهِمَّة تَوَاصُل — Communicative task: Catching up

In pairs, imagine you have not spoken in a year. Take turns giving a one-minute spoken update about your home, work or studies, and one thing that has changed. The listener should ask at least one follow-up question using a pronoun that refers back to something the speaker mentioned.

لِلْمُتَعَلِّمِينَ المُسْتَقِلِّينَ — Solo alternative

Record yourself giving the one-minute update described above. Listen back and check that you connected your sentences with at least one connector and referred back to at least one noun with a pronoun instead of repeating it.

Reading Station

Read the short update below, then answer the questions that follow. Following the staged reduction described earlier in this chapter, some predictable short vowels on familiar words are left unmarked.

My Life Now — Sample Update Text

اسمي رَنَا. كما تَعلَمون، درست في جامعة كبيرة لمدة أربع سنوات. الآن، بدأت العمل في مستشفى قريب من بيتي. هي وظيفة صعبة أحيانًا، لكنني أحبها كثيرًا. لا أزال أسكن مع أسرتي، لكن بالإضافة إلى ذلك، بدأت أخطط للانتقال إلى شقتي الخاصة مُنذُ فترة. أيضًا، أختي الكبيرة تزوجت السنة الماضية، وهي الآن تسكن في مدينة أخرى.

أَسْئِلَة الفَهْم — Comprehension questions:

  1. What did Rana study, and what does she do now?
  2. Find the sentence where هِيَ refers back to her job. How do you know?
  3. What has Rana started planning, and what happened to her older sister?
  4. Identify one word in the passage whose short vowels you supplied from memory rather than from the marks on the page.

Writing Station

In this station, you will draft a short written update of six to eight sentences about your life now, connecting your ideas rather than listing them separately.

  1. Open with a formulaic update phrase. Begin with كَمَا تَعْلَم or كَمَا تَعْلَمِين, recycling Chapters 1-3 of Volume I.
  2. State where you live now. One sentence, using الآن and, if useful, a pronoun that refers back to the place in a following sentence.
  3. Describe your work or studies. One or two sentences, recycling Volume I work/study vocabulary.
  4. Say what has stayed the same. One sentence using لَا أَزَال / لَا تَزَال.
  5. Add one new thing. One sentence using بِالإِضَافَة إِلَى ذَلِك or أَيْضًا.
  6. Mention one family update. One sentence recycling Volume I family vocabulary and gender agreement.
  7. Revise. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.

قَائِمَة المُرَاجَعَة — Editing checklist:

  • I used at least one formulaic update opener correctly.
  • I referred back to at least one noun with a pronoun instead of repeating it.
  • I used at least one connector to join two ideas into one sentence or one flowing pair of sentences.
  • I used correct gender agreement throughout.
  • My account reads as one connected update, not a list of separate facts.
  • I wrote all letters and endings with correct right-to-left joining.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Catching up after time apart

Greeting someone you have not seen in a while and asking about their life, family, and well-being is a common opening move in many Arabic-speaking communities, much as it is elsewhere. How much detail people share, and in what order, varies by relationship, region, and personal style, so treat the model dialogue in this chapter as one natural example rather than a fixed script.

Regional Variation — Formulaic openers

Expressions such as كَمَا تَعْلَم and مِن زَمَان are widely understood in MSA and in many forms of formal spoken MSA, though speakers of specific regional varieties may prefer other everyday greetings in casual conversation. Using the MSA forms taught here remains appropriate and correct in the contexts this course addresses.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Repeating the noun instead of using a pronoun

Error: اِنْتَقَلْتُ إِلَى مَدِينَة جَدِيدَة. المَدِينَة الجَدِيدَة هَادِئَة. أَنَا أُحِبّ المَدِينَة الجَدِيدَة. (repeating "the new city" three times)

Correction: اِنْتَقَلْتُ إِلَى مَدِينَة جَدِيدَة. هِيَ هَادِئَة، وَأَنَا أُحِبُّهَا كَثِيرًا.

Common Mistake — Writing a list instead of a connected update

Error: Writing short, unconnected sentences with no linking words at all, so the update reads like a checklist rather than a conversation.

Correction: Join at least some sentences with وَ, أَيْضًا, لَكِن, or بِالإِضَافَة إِلَى ذَلِك, as the model paragraphs in this chapter do.

Common Mistake — Panicking over unmarked vowels

Error: Stopping at every unmarked word and assuming it is unreadable or that something has been left out by mistake.

Correction: Use the word's consonant shape and the sentence context to recall the vowel pattern of a word you already know, and treat only genuinely unfamiliar words as new vocabulary to look up.

Answer Key

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. Huda moved to a new city and started learning French; she still works at the same company, and her family is well.
  2. هِيَ مَدِينَة صَغِيرَة وَهَادِئَة: هِيَ refers back to the new city Huda just mentioned.
  3. Huda opens with كَمَا تَعْلَم ("as you know"); you might use it when giving an update to someone who already knows some background about you.

Listening Station

  • Order: new city, work routine, new habit (the club), daughter's school update.
  • Sami used to live in Cairo; he now lives in Alexandria.
  • هِيَ مَدِينَة عَلَى البَحْر: هِيَ refers back to Alexandria.
  • In addition to his usual work-from-home routine, Sami recently started going to the gym every morning.

Reading Station — Comprehension

  1. Rana studied at a large university for four years; she now works at a hospital near her house.
  2. هي وظيفة صعبة أحيانًا: هِيَ refers back to her job at the hospital.
  3. Rana has started planning to move into her own apartment; her older sister got married last year and now lives in another city.
  4. Answers will vary; learners should identify a familiar word such as اسمي, جامعة, or بيتي whose vowels they supplied from memory.
