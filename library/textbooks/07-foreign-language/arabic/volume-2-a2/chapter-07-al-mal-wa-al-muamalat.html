المَال وَالمُعَامَلَاتMoney and Transactions

Numbers beyond one hundred, quantities, formal requests, and basic condition frames · Speak and Write Arabic: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use numbers in the hundreds and thousands accurately when discussing prices, totals, and quantities.
  2. Describe quantities of money and goods using appropriate counted-noun forms.
  3. Make formal requests appropriate to banks, shops, and other transactional settings.
  4. Form a basic condition frame with إِذَا to describe what will happen under a stated condition, such as a discount or a required document.
  5. Read and listen to short transactional exchanges — at a bank counter, in a shop, or on a receipt — and answer questions about prices, totals, and conditions.
  6. Write a short, organized message describing a transaction, including price, quantity, and at least one condition.

Communication Scenario

You need to open a small savings account, buy several items at a shop, and understand a receipt that lists a total in the hundreds. Numbers beyond one hundred appear constantly in daily transactions — prices, phone numbers, account balances — but Arabic handles these numbers differently from the numbers you have used so far, and the noun that follows them changes depending on the number itself. You also want to ask, politely and formally, whether a discount is available, and to understand a clerk who tells you: "if you pay in cash, you get a discount."

  1. You will learn how hundreds and thousands work in Arabic, and what happens to the counted noun that follows them.
  2. You will learn formal request patterns suited to banks, shops, and official transactions.
  3. You will learn your first condition frame: إِذَا + a stated condition, followed by a result — for example, "if you pay in cash, you get a discount."
  4. You will use all three tools together to describe or complete a real transaction.

تَرْكِيب — New Grammar

Numbers Beyond One Hundred

This section introduces hundreds and thousands, and shows how the noun being counted behaves differently than it did with the smaller numbers you have already studied.

Building Block — Hundreds and thousands
مِئَة (100) · مِئَتَان (200) · ثَلَاثمِئَة (300) ... أَلْف (1,000) · أَلْفَان (2,000) · ثَلَاثَة آلَاف (3,000)

دَفَعْت مِئَتَي دِينَار ثَمَنًا لِلْكِتَاب.

I paid two hundred dinars as the price of the book.

مِئَة and أَلْف behave like singular nouns and are followed by a singular counted noun, unlike the plural counted nouns used with the numbers three through ten. مِئَتَان and أَلْفَان follow the same dual pattern you already know from other dual nouns. From three hundred upward, the number word combines with مِئَة/مِئُون or أَلْف/آلَاف according to the same agreement rules you learned for smaller numbers.

  • المَجْمُوع خَمْسمِئَة دِرْهَم.The total is five hundred dirhams.مِئَة + singular counted noun
  • فِي حِسَابِي أَلْفَا دُولَار.In my account there are two thousand dollars.dual of أَلْف + singular counted noun
  • اِشْتَرَيْت ثَلَاثَة كُتُب بِمِئَة وَخَمْسِين رِيَالًا.I bought three books for one hundred fifty riyals.compound hundred + tens

Full productive control of case endings on numbers is not required at this stage; focus on choosing the correct number word and the correct form of the counted noun.

Formal Requests in Transactions

This section extends the polite request patterns from Chapter 6 into banking and shopping contexts, and adds a new formal frame appropriate for addressing a clerk or bank employee.

Building Block — A new formal request frame
أَوَدُّ أَن + subjunctive verb...

أَوَدُّ أَن أَفْتَح حِسَابًا جَدِيدًا فِي هَذَا المَصْرِف.

I would like to open a new account at this bank.

أَوَدُّ أَن is a common, polished way to begin a formal request, similar in function to أَرْجُو أَن but slightly more common in spoken banking and retail interactions. It also introduces a subjunctive verb, continuing the system you began recognizing in Chapter 3.

  • أَوَدُّ أَن أَعْرِف السِّعْر النِّهَائِيّ مَع الضَّرِيبَة.I would like to know the final price with tax.formal request frame
  • هَل مِن المُمْكِن أَن تُقَدِّم لِي إِيصَالًا؟Would it be possible for you to give me a receipt?recycled request frame from Chapter 6

Condition Frames with إِذَا

This section introduces your first productive condition frame: a stated condition followed by its result, most often used here for prices, discounts, and required documents.

Building Block — Real condition: إِذَا + condition + result
إِذَا + perfect verb (condition), + perfect or imperfect verb (result)

إِذَا دَفَعْت نَقْدًا، حَصَلْت عَلَى خَصْم.

If you pay in cash, you get a discount.

إِذَا introduces a condition that is expected to be real or likely, not hypothetical or contrary to fact. The verb after إِذَا is normally in the perfect, even when the meaning refers to the present or future; the result clause may use either the perfect or the imperfect. At this stage, you are expected to produce this simple pattern accurately; more advanced conditional structures are reserved for later chapters.

  • إِذَا وَافَقْتُم عَلَى السِّعْر، سَنُوقِّع العَقْد اليَوْم.If you agree to the price, we will sign the contract today.condition (perfect) + result (imperfect/future)
  • إِذَا أَحْضَرْت بِطَاقَتِي، أَسْتَطِيع فَتْح الحِسَاب اليَوْم.If I bring my card, I can open the account today.condition + result, both perfect/present ability

Model Dialogue

Setting: سَامِي visits a bank to open a savings account and asks the clerk, الأُسْتَاذَة لَيْلَى, about fees and requirements.
السِّيَاق: يَزُور سَامِي المَصْرِف لِفَتْح حِسَاب تَوْفِير وَيَسْأَل المُوَظَّفَة، الأُسْتَاذَة لَيْلَى، عَن الرُّسُوم وَالمُتَطَلَّبَات.

SAMI صَبَاح الخَيْر. أَوَدُّ أَن أَفْتَح حِسَاب تَوْفِير جَدِيد. Good morning. I would like to open a new savings account.
LAYLA أَهْلًا بِك. الحَدّ الأَدْنَى لِفَتْح الحِسَاب خَمْسمِئَة دِرْهَم. هَل مَعَك هَذَا المَبْلَغ نَقْدًا؟ Welcome. The minimum for opening the account is five hundred dirhams. Do you have that amount in cash?
SAMI نَعَم، مَعِي أَلْف دِرْهَم. إِذَا أَوْدَعْت أَلْف دِرْهَم كَامِلَة، هَل تُوجَد رُسُوم؟ Yes, I have one thousand dirhams. If I deposit the full one thousand dirhams, are there any fees?
LAYLA لَا رُسُوم إِذَا كَان المَبْلَغ أَكْثَر مِن مِئَتَي دِرْهَم. أَرْجُو أَن تُوَقِّع هَذِه الاسْتِمَارَة، وَتَمّ فَتْح الحِسَاب. There are no fees if the amount is more than two hundred dirhams. Please sign this form, and the account is opened.
SAMI شُكْرًا. هَل مِن المُمْكِن أَن تُقَدِّمِي لِي إِيصَالًا بِالمَبْلَغ؟ Thank you. Would it be possible for you to give me a receipt for the amount?
LAYLA بِالتَّأْكِيد. تَفَضَّل، هَذَا إِيصَالُك، وَفِيه المَجْمُوع وَتَارِيخ الإِيدَاع. Certainly. Here you are, this is your receipt, and it has the total and the deposit date.

أَسْئِلَة الفَهْم — Comprehension questions:

  1. What is the minimum amount needed to open the account, and how is that number expressed in Arabic?
  2. What condition frame does Sami use, and what result does he ask about?
  3. What condition does Layla state for there being no fees?

Ask your instructor, a partner, or a text-to-speech tool to read the following short shop announcement aloud twice at a moderate pace. Listen without reading along the first time, then answer the questions below.

إِعْلَان لِلزَّبَائِن: أَسْعَار جَمِيع المُنْتَجَات هَذَا الأُسْبُوع بَيْن مِئَة وَخَمْسمِئَة دِرْهَم. إِذَا اشْتَرَيْتُم بِأَكْثَر مِن أَلْف دِرْهَم، تَحْصُلُون عَلَى خَصْم عِشْرِين بِالمِئَة. أَمَّا الدَّفْع بِبِطَاقَة الاِئْتِمَان فَمُتَاح دُون رُسُوم إِضَافِيَّة. نَشْكُرُكُم عَلَى تَعَامُلِكُم مَعَنَا.

مُهِمَّة الاسْتِمَاع — Listening task

Answer the following questions based on what you hear:

  1. What price range is given for this week's products?
  2. What condition earns a customer a twenty percent discount?
  3. Is there an extra fee for paying by credit card?

Speaking Station

تَدْرِيب مُوَجَّه — Controlled practice: Prices and totals

With a partner, take turns stating a price for an everyday item using a number in the hundreds, then asking the other partner to state the total for several items using a number in the thousands.

مُهِمَّة تَوَاصُل — Communicative task: At the bank counter

In pairs, role-play a short bank visit. One partner plays the customer, making a formal request with أَوَدُّ أَن or هَل مِن المُمْكِن أَن and describing an amount of money; the other partner plays the clerk, stating a condition with إِذَا and its result (a fee, a discount, or a requirement).

لِلْمُتَعَلِّمِينَ المُسْتَقِلِّينَ — Solo alternative

Record yourself describing a recent purchase: state the price, the quantity, and one condition under which you would have paid a different amount (for example, a discount for paying in cash).

Reading Station

Read the short receipt notice below, then answer the questions that follow. As in earlier chapters, some predictable short vowels on familiar words are left unmarked.

A Shop Receipt Note — Sample Text

إيصال شراء: ثلاث قطع بسعر مئة وخمسين درهما للقطعة، والمجموع أربعمئة وخمسون درهما. إذا أعدتم المنتج خلال أسبوع مع الإيصال، يمكن استرجاع كامل المبلغ. الدفع تم ببطاقة الائتمان، ولا رسوم إضافية على هذه الطريقة. شكرا لتسوقكم معنا.

أَسْئِلَة الفَهْم — Comprehension questions:

  1. How many items were purchased, and what is the total?
  2. What condition allows a full refund, and what result does it lead to?
  3. How was the payment made, and is there an extra fee for that method?

Writing Station

In this station, you will draft a short message of six to eight sentences describing a transaction to a friend or colleague.

  1. Open naturally. One sentence introducing what you bought, paid, or arranged.
  2. State the price or amount. One or two sentences using a number in the hundreds or thousands.
  3. Add a condition. One sentence using إِذَا to describe a condition and its result — a discount, a fee, or a requirement.
  4. Make a request, if useful. One optional sentence using أَوَدُّ أَن or هَل مِن المُمْكِن أَن.
  5. Reference a receipt or notice, if useful. One optional sentence recognizing a passive or تَمَّ-based phrase, such as something that تَمَّ دَفْعُه.
  6. Close and revise. One closing sentence, then check your draft against the editing checklist below.

قَائِمَة المُرَاجَعَة — Editing checklist:

  • I used at least one number in the hundreds or thousands correctly, with the correct form of the counted noun.
  • I included one condition frame with إِذَا and a matching result.
  • I used a polite or formal request pattern where appropriate.
  • I did not attempt an advanced or hypothetical conditional beyond the pattern taught in this chapter.
  • My message reads as one connected account of a transaction rather than a list of unrelated facts.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Bargaining and fixed prices

Expectations around negotiating price vary widely across Arabic-speaking regions and settings. In many traditional markets, a degree of polite bargaining is expected and even valued as a social exchange, while in banks, large retail stores, and most government transactions, prices and fees are fixed and bargaining would be out of place. Reading the setting — market stall, shop counter, or bank window — matters as much as knowing the vocabulary for money.

Regional Variation — Currency names

This chapter uses دِرْهَم, دِينَار, رِيَال, and دُولَار as example currencies, but the specific currency name and its grammatical behavior depend on the country in question. Learners preparing for a specific region should confirm the correct currency name and its plural or dual forms for that country rather than assuming one currency name applies everywhere.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using a plural noun after مِئَة or أَلْف

Error: مِئَة دَرَاهِم (using a plural counted noun after مِئَة)

Correction: مِئَة دِرْهَم — مِئَة and أَلْف are followed by a singular counted noun, unlike the numbers three through ten.

Common Mistake — Treating إِذَا as a hypothetical marker

Error: using إِذَا for situations that are clearly imaginary, impossible, or contrary to fact.

Correction: reserve إِذَا, as taught in this chapter, for conditions that are real or likely — prices, discounts, and requirements you might actually encounter. More advanced hypothetical conditionals are introduced in a later chapter.

Common Mistake — Overusing أَوَدُّ أَن in casual settings

Error: using أَوَدُّ أَن with a close friend or in a very informal exchange, producing an unnaturally stiff tone.

Correction: reserve أَوَدُّ أَن for formal settings such as banks, offices, and official transactions; simpler request forms are more natural among friends.
