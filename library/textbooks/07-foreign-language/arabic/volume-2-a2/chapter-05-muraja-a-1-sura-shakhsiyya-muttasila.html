5

مُرَاجَعَة ١: صُورَة شَخْصِيَّة مُتَّصِلَةUnit I Assessment: A Connected Personal Profile

Synthesizing personal description, relationships, habits and change, and biographical narration into one connected profile · Speak and Write Arabic: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication
Assessment chapter. Chapter 5 introduces no new grammar, vocabulary domain, or script system. It brings together the systems authorized in Chapters 1–4 so that you can demonstrate a connected, independent personal and social profile in speaking and writing.

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Present a connected personal profile that combines identity, family, relationships, routines, and past experience in one account rather than as separate facts.
  2. Use expanded plurals, construct chains, and relative description to talk about people and relationships accurately.
  3. Describe habits, changes, and comparisons across time using the imperfect, frequency expressions, and the "used to... but now..." pattern.
  4. Narrate a short connected biography using the perfect tense, كَانَ, and common weak verbs, sequenced with أَوَّلًا, ثُمَّ, بَعْد ذَلِك, and أَخِيرًا.
  5. Read and listen to connected personal narratives at the Unit I level and answer questions that require tracking pronoun reference and event sequence.
  6. Complete an integrated spoken and written personal and social profile that draws on all four chapters of Unit I.

Communication Scenario

A new community center in your neighborhood is putting together a short welcome booklet, and each new member is asked to submit a spoken and written self-introduction: who you are, the people in your life, how your daily life looks now compared with before, and a short account of your background. This is exactly the kind of connected self-presentation that Unit I has been building toward — not a list of isolated facts, but one coherent profile a stranger could listen to or read and come away understanding who you are.

  1. Chapter 1 gave you paragraph-level self-description and pronoun reference.
  2. Chapter 2 gave you the vocabulary and grammar to describe relationships and your local community.
  3. Chapter 3 gave you the tools to describe habits, frequency, and change over time.
  4. Chapter 4 gave you the perfect tense, كَانَ, weak verbs, and sequencing words for narrating a short biography.
  5. This chapter does not add anything new. It asks you to combine all four into one connected profile.

تَرْكِيب — Synthesis

Personal Description and Relationships

This section combines Chapter 1's self-description with Chapter 2's relationship and community vocabulary. No new grammar is presented; the goal is connecting the two systems in one paragraph.

Building Block — Combining self-description with relationship description
personal statement + عِنْدِي/لِي + relatives/neighbors + الَّذِي/الَّتِي relative clause

اِسْمِي لَيْلَى، وَعِنْدِي أُسْرَة كَبِيرَة تَعِيش قَرِيبَة مِنِّي. جَارَتِي الَّتِي تَسْكُن بِجَانِبِي صَدِيقَة قَدِيمَة لِعَائِلَتِي.

My name is Layla, and I have a large family that lives close to me. My neighbor who lives next to me is an old friend of my family.

Notice that the relative clause (الَّتِي تَسْكُن بِجَانِبِي) does exactly what it did in Chapter 2 — it adds description to a noun without starting a new sentence. Combining it with Chapter 1's self-introduction pattern produces one connected description rather than two separate statements.

  • لِي أَخ يَعْمَل مُهَنْدِسًا وَأُخْت تَدْرُس الطِّبّ فِي الجَامِعَة.I have a brother who works as an engineer and a sister who studies medicine at university.plural family reference + descriptive verbal clauses

Habits, Change, and Comparison

This section reuses Chapter 3's system for describing present routines and marking change from the past, now combined with the people introduced above.

Building Block — Present habit contrasted with the past
present-tense habit + كُنْت/كُنَّا + imperfect (past habit) + أَكْثَر/أَقَلّ/مِثْل for comparison

الآن أَزُور أَقَارِبِي كُلّ أُسْبُوع تَقْرِيبًا، لَكِنَّنِي كُنْت أَزُورُهُم نَادِرًا فِي طُفُولَتِي. حَيَاتِي الآن أَكْثَر ارْتِبَاطًا بِالعَائِلَة مِن ذِي قَبْل.

Now I visit my relatives almost every week, but I used to visit them rarely in my childhood. My life now is more connected to family than before.

The frequency words (دَائِمًا، غَالِبًا، أَحْيَانًا، نَادِرًا) and the كُنْت + imperfect pattern do not change here. What is new is only that they now describe the same people and relationships introduced in the previous section, producing a single connected account of "then and now."

Biography and Sequencing

This section reuses Chapter 4's perfect-tense sequencing, كَانَ, and weak-verb system to close the profile with a short life narrative.

Building Block — A full connected profile
identity + relationships → present habits vs. past habits → وُلِدْت + كَانَ background → أَوَّلًا/ثُمَّ/أَخِيرًا sequence of events

وُلِدْت فِي هَذِه المَدِينَة، وَكَانَت عَائِلَتِي تَعِيش هُنَا مُنْذُ زَمَن طَوِيل. أَوَّلًا دَرَسْت هُنَا، ثُمَّ عِشْت سَنَتَيْن فِي مَدِينَة أُخْرَى لِلْعَمَل، وَأَخِيرًا رَجَعْت لِأَكُون قَرِيبَة مِن أَقَارِبِي وَجِيرَانِي القُدَامَى.

I was born in this city, and my family has lived here for a long time. First I studied here, then I lived for two years in another city for work, and finally I came back to be close to my relatives and my old neighbors.

Every element in this paragraph — the relative clause, the frequency comparison, the perfect-tense sequence, the weak verb عِشْت — was taught in Chapters 1 through 4. This chapter asks only that you produce them together, accurately and in a natural order, as one connected profile.

Grammar Note — No new systems in this chapter

If a task in this chapter seems to require a structure you have not yet studied — full case-ending production, subjunctive or jussive verbs, or derived verb forms, for example — it does not. Every task in Chapter 5 can be completed using only material from Chapters 1 through 4.

Model Dialogue

Setting: At the community center's welcome event, هَنَاء introduces herself to a new neighbor, رَامِي, combining identity, relationships, habits, and background.
السِّيَاق: فِي حَفْل التَّرْحِيب بِمَرْكَز الحَيّ، تُقَدِّم هَنَاء نَفْسَهَا لِجَارِهَا الجَدِيد رَامِي.

HANAA اِسْمِي هَنَاء، وَأَنَا جَارَتُكُم الَّتِي تَسْكُن فِي البِنَايَة المُقَابِلَة. عِنْدِي زَوْج وَابْنَان يَدْرُسَان فِي المَدْرَسَة القَرِيبَة. My name is Hanaa, and I'm your neighbor who lives in the building across the way. I have a husband and two sons who study at the nearby school.
RAMI تَشَرَّفْنَا! وَمُنْذُ مَتَى تَسْكُنُون هُنَا؟ Pleased to meet you! And since when have you lived here?
HANAA وُلِدْت فِي مَدِينَة أُخْرَى، لَكِنَّنِي كُنْت أَزُور هَذَا الحَيّ دَائِمًا مَع أَقَارِبِي وَأَنَا صَغِيرَة. أَوَّلًا اِنْتَقَلْنَا إِلَى هُنَا لِلْعَمَل، ثُمَّ اِشْتَرَيْنَا هَذِه الشَّقَّة، وَأَخِيرًا قَرَّرْنَا البَقَاء بِسَبَب الجِيرَان الطَّيِّبِينَ. I was born in another city, but I always used to visit this neighborhood with my relatives when I was young. First we moved here for work, then we bought this apartment, and finally we decided to stay because of the kind neighbors.
RAMI وَهَل عَادَاتُكُم هُنَا مِثْل عَادَاتِكُم فِي مَدِينَتِكُم القَدِيمَة؟ And are your habits here like your habits in your old city?
HANAA لَا، حَيَاتُنَا هُنَا أَهْدَأ بِكَثِير. كُنَّا نَخْرُج كَثِيرًا هُنَاك، لَكِنَّنَا الآن نَقْضِي أَكْثَر الوَقْت مَع الجِيرَان وَالأَقَارِب القَرِيبِين. No, our life here is much calmer. We used to go out a lot there, but now we spend most of the time with the neighbors and nearby relatives.

أَسْئِلَة الفَهْم — Comprehension questions:

  1. Which relative clause does Hanaa use to describe herself, and what does it modify?
  2. Put Hanaa's three sequenced events in order using أَوَّلًا، ثُمَّ، and أَخِيرًا.
  3. What comparison does Hanaa make between her life now and her life in her old city?

Writing Station

In this station, you will draft a connected personal profile of eight to ten sentences that combines every system from Unit I into one coherent account.

  1. Open with identity. One or two sentences introducing yourself, recycling Chapter 1's self-description pattern.
  2. Describe one relationship. One sentence describing a family member, neighbor, or friend using a plural and a relative clause (الَّذِي/الَّتِي) from Chapter 2.
  3. Compare a habit now and in the past. Two sentences using a frequency word and كُنْت/كُنَّا + imperfect from Chapter 3.
  4. Open your biography. One sentence with وُلِدْت and one with كَانَ/كَانَت describing a past state or habit, from Chapter 4.
  5. Sequence three events. Three sentences using أَوَّلًا, ثُمَّ or بَعْد ذَلِك, and أَخِيرًا, including at least one weak verb.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.

قَائِمَة المُرَاجَعَة — Editing checklist:

  • I introduced myself clearly, recycling Chapter 1's pattern.
  • I described at least one relationship using a plural and a relative clause from Chapter 2.
  • I compared a present habit with a past habit using a frequency word and كُنْت/كُنَّا + imperfect from Chapter 3.
  • I used وُلِدْت and at least one correct use of كَانَ/كَانَت/كُنَّا from Chapter 4.
  • I sequenced at least three events using أَوَّلًا, ثُمَّ, بَعْد ذَلِك, or أَخِيرًا, including one weak verb.
  • My profile reads as one connected account, not four separate paragraphs stapled together.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Introducing yourself to a community

A self-introduction that mentions family, neighbors, and background rather than only personal facts is a common pattern in many Arabic-speaking communities, since who you are is often understood partly through your relationships and roots. Expectations for how much detail to share, and in what order, vary by setting, generation, and community, so the model dialogue and readings in this chapter should be read as illustrative rather than as a fixed script.

Regional Variation — Neighborly relationships

The closeness implied by جِيرَان (neighbors) in this chapter's readings — visiting often, helping with family needs, long-standing relationships — reflects a pattern reported in many neighborhoods across Arabic-speaking regions, but the intensity and form of these relationships vary widely between urban and rural settings, older and newer neighborhoods, and different countries. Treat these passages as examples, not as a single universal model of neighborly life.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Listing systems instead of connecting them

Error: writing four separate, disconnected paragraphs — one about identity, one about family, one about habits, one about biography — with no linking language between them.

Correction: use connectors such as وَ، لَكِنَّ، and the sequencing words from Chapter 4 to move smoothly from one part of the profile to the next, so the whole reads as a single account.

Common Mistake — Dropping agreement when combining plurals and relative clauses

Error: أَقَارِبِي الَّذِي يَسْكُن بَعِيدًا. (singular relative pronoun with a plural antecedent)

Correction: أَقَارِبِي الَّذِينَ يَسْكُنُون بَعِيدًا. — the relative pronoun and verb must agree with the plural noun, exactly as practiced in Chapter 2.

Common Mistake — Reaching for untaught grammar

Error: attempting subjunctive, jussive, or derived-form verbs to sound more advanced in this profile.

Correction: every task in this chapter can be completed accurately using only Chapters 1–4. A simple, accurate, well-connected profile using known systems is stronger evidence of learning than an inaccurate attempt at untaught grammar.

ب: أَقْرَب النَّاس إِلَيَّ أُخْتِي الَّتِي تَسْكُن بِجَانِبِي، وَكُنَّا نَتَقَابَل نَادِرًا فِي المَاضِي، لَكِنَّنَا الآن نَتَقَابَل كُلّ أُسْبُوع.
ب: وُلِدْت فِي هَذَا الحَيّ، وَكَانَت طُفُولَتِي سَعِيدَة. أَوَّلًا دَرَسْت هُنَا، ثُمَّ سَافَرْت لِلْعَمَل، وَأَخِيرًا رَجَعْت لِلْعَيْش قَرِيبًا مِن عَائِلَتِي.

Exact wording will vary. Check that a plural/relative-clause description, a frequency comparison, and a sequenced biography with كَانَ are all present and connected.

Looking Ahead

مَا الَّذِي تَسْتَطِيعُ فِعْلَهُ الآن — What you can do now

You can now present a single, connected personal and social profile that introduces who you are, describes the people in your life, compares your habits now and in the past, and narrates a short biography in correct sequence. This closes Unit I: every system introduced in Chapters 1 through 4 is now available to you for independent, connected communication.
