المُجْتَمَع وَالبِيئَة المَحَلِّيَّةCommunity and Local Environment

Reasons, results, conditions, and opinions about public issues · Speak and Write Arabic: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Discuss a local community issue, such as transportation, the environment, or public services, using vocabulary and structures from prior chapters.
  2. Give a reason for a situation and explain its result, using cause and result connectors consolidated since Chapter 6.
  3. State a real or general condition and its consequence for a community issue, recycling and expanding the conditional frames introduced in Chapters 7 through 9.
  4. Express a simple opinion about a public issue and give one supporting reason.
  5. Recognize basic percentage and quantity expressions used to describe a community or a public issue.
  6. Read and summarize a short passage about a local issue, identifying its causes, results, and any conditions mentioned.
  7. Write a short paragraph describing a community issue, its causes, its results, and a personal opinion supported by one reason.

Communication Scenario

Chapter 14 closes Unit III by turning from public information toward public issues that affect a community directly, such as traffic, waste, green space, noise, or local services. Community discussion depends on being able to explain why something is happening, what results from it, what would happen under certain conditions, and what the speaker personally thinks about it. This chapter consolidates cause, result, and conditional language that has been developing since Chapter 6 through 9, and adds a simple, bounded frame for stating an opinion and supporting it with one reason. It also introduces basic percentage and quantity language so that learners can understand and discuss simple community statistics.

  1. You will learn vocabulary for community issues, the local environment, and public services.
  2. You will consolidate cause and result connectors in the context of community problems.
  3. You will consolidate real and general conditional frames in the context of local solutions.
  4. You will use a simple, formulaic opinion frame supported by one reason.
  5. You will recognize simple percentage and quantity expressions describing a community issue.

What This Chapter Builds On

Chapter 14 completes Unit III by moving from public information toward public issues that affect the immediate community. It consolidates cause and result connectors developed since Chapter 6, and the real and general conditional frames introduced across Chapters 7 through 9. It also recycles the passive-voice recognition and reported-information language from Chapters 6 and 13, applying both to descriptions of community problems and proposed solutions. It introduces one new, closely related pair of systems: a simple, bounded opinion frame supported by a single reason, and basic percentage and quantity language for describing community data.

تَرْكِيب — Grammar in Context

Reasons and Results

You have used بِسَبَب (because of) and لِذَلِك (therefore) since Chapters 6 and 7 to connect a cause and its result. This section consolidates that system in the context of community issues, where explaining why a problem exists and what it leads to is essential.

Language Focus — Connecting a cause to its result

بِسَبَب is followed directly by a noun or noun phrase naming the cause. لِذَلِك opens a new clause naming the result. Together, the two connectors let you build a two-part sentence that explains a community issue clearly: first the reason, then what follows from it.

  • اِزْدَاد الاِزْدِحَام بِسَبَب عَدَد السَّيَّارَات الكَبِير.Congestion increased because of the large number of cars.cause connector, recycled from Ch. 6-7
  • لَا تُوجَد حَدَائِق كَافِيَة فِي الحَيّ؛ لِذَلِك يَخْرُج السُّكَّان إِلَى أَحْيَاء أُخْرَى.There are not enough parks in the neighborhood; therefore, residents go out to other neighborhoods.result connector, recycled from Ch. 6-7
  • تَزِيد النُّفَايَات فِي الشَّوَارِع بِسَبَب قِلَّة صُنَادِيق القُمَامَة.Waste increases in the streets because of the shortage of trash bins.cause connector, community domain

Continue to recognize the passive voice, recycled from Chapter 6, when a sentence about a community issue focuses on an event rather than on who caused it.

Conditions and Consequences

The real conditional frame إِذَا...، فَـ... (if..., then...) has been developing since Chapters 7 through 9 for practical situations. This section consolidates that frame for discussing possible solutions to community problems: if a certain step is taken, what consequence follows.

Building Block — Real condition + consequence
إِذَا + جُمْلَة الشَّرْط، فَـ + جُمْلَة النَتِيجَة — if + condition clause, then + result clause

إِذَا زَادَت البَلَدِيَّة عَدَد الحَافِلَات، فَسَيَقِلّ الاِزْدِحَام.

If the municipality increases the number of buses, then congestion will decrease.

As in earlier chapters, إِذَا introduces the condition and فَـ commonly introduces the result clause, especially when the result is a future action. The result clause may also stand without فَـ when the connection is already clear from context.

  • إِذَا زَرَعَ السُّكَّان أَشْجَارًا جَدِيدَة، سَتَتَحَسَّن البِيئَة فِي الحَيّ.If the residents plant new trees, the environment in the neighborhood will improve.real condition, consequence without فَـ
  • إِذَا لَم تَحُلّ البَلَدِيَّة مُشْكِلَة النُّفَايَات، فَسَتَزِيد الشَّكَاوَى.If the municipality does not solve the waste problem, then complaints will increase.negated condition, recycled negation from Ch. 7

These conditions describe realistic, likely situations. Hypothetical and counterfactual conditions are developed further in later volumes.

Simple Opinion on Public Issues

This chapter introduces a simple, bounded way to state a personal opinion about a public issue and support it with one reason. Treat the frame as a fixed pattern rather than analyzing every internal detail; a fuller system for opinion, comparison, and agreement or disagreement begins in Chapter 18.

Language Focus — Opinion + one reason

فِي رَأْيِي (in my opinion) opens the sentence and is followed by a statement of your view. لِأَنَّ (because) then introduces exactly one supporting reason. Keep the reason short and concrete at this stage.

  • فِي رَأْيِي، يَجِب أَن تَزِيد البَلَدِيَّة عَدَد الحَدَائِق العَامَّة لِأَنَّ الأَطْفَال يَحْتَاجُون إِلَى مَكَان لِلَّعِب.In my opinion, the municipality should increase the number of public parks because children need a place to play.opinion frame + one reason
  • فِي رَأْيِي، مُشْكِلَة الضَّوْضَاء مُهِمَّة لِأَنَّهَا تُؤَثِّر عَلَى نَوْم السُّكَّان.In my opinion, the noise problem is important because it affects residents' sleep.opinion frame + one reason

At this stage, one reason is sufficient. Do not add comparisons, counterarguments, or multiple reasons; that fuller structure begins in Chapter 18.

Language Note — Talking About Percentages

Short reports about community issues sometimes include a simple percentage, such as خَمْسُون فِي المِئَة (fifty percent) or ثَلَاثُون فِي المِئَة مِن السُّكَّان (thirty percent of the residents). At this stage, recognize these expressions in reading and listening rather than producing new percentage calculations; a fuller system for numerals, statistics, and data commentary develops later in the volume and series.

Listening Station

Ask your instructor, a partner, or a text-to-speech tool to read the following short passage aloud twice at a moderate pace. Listen without reading along the first time, then answer the questions below.

يَشْكُو كَثِير مِن سُكَّان الحَيّ مِن مُشْكِلَة الاِزْدِحَام بِسَبَب عَدَد السَّيَّارَات الكَبِير فِي الصَّبَاح. وَحَسَب بَيَان البَلَدِيَّة، حَوَالَي أَرْبَعِين فِي المِئَة مِن السُّكَّان يَسْتَخْدِمُون سَيَّارَاتِهِم الخَاصَّة لِلذَّهَاب إِلَى العَمَل. وَإِذَا زَادَت البَلَدِيَّة عَدَد الحَافِلَات، فَسَيَقِلّ الاِزْدِحَام تَدْرِيجِيًّا. وَفِي رَأْيِي، هَذَا حَلّ جَيِّد لِأَنَّهُ يُشَجِّع النَّاس عَلَى اِسْتِخْدَام النَّقْل العَامّ.

مُهِمَّة الاسْتِمَاع — Listening task

Answer the following questions based on what you hear:

  1. What is the cause of the congestion problem, according to the passage?
  2. What percentage of residents use their own cars to go to work?
  3. What condition and result does the passage describe as a possible solution?
  4. What opinion is expressed at the end, and what reason supports it?

A Short Community Report — Sample Text

تَزِيد مُشْكِلَة النُّفَايَات فى بَعْض شَوَارِع المَدِينَة بِسَبَب قِلَّة صَنَادِيق القُمَامَة وَعَدَم اِنْتِظَام جَمْعِهَا. وَحَسَب تَقْرِير البَلَدِيَّة، حَوَالَي ثَلَاثِين فى المِئَة مِن السُّكَّان يَشْكُون مِن هَذِهِ المُشْكِلَة بِاسْتِمْرَار. وَإِذَا وَضَعَت البَلَدِيَّة صَنَادِيق جَدِيدَة وَنَظَّمَت مَوَاعِيد الجَمْع، فَسَتَتَحَسَّن الحَالَة تَدْرِيجِيًّا. وَفى رَأْيِى، هَذِهِ المُشْكِلَة تَحْتَاج إِلَى حَلّ سَرِيع لِأَنَّهَا تُؤَثِّر عَلَى صِحَّة السُّكَّان.

أَسْئِلَة الفَهْم — Comprehension questions:

  1. What is causing the waste problem in some streets of the city?
  2. According to the report, what percentage of residents complain about this problem?
  3. Identify the condition and its result in the passage, and the opinion sentence with its reason.

Writing Station

In this station, you will write a short paragraph of five to seven sentences about a community issue in a neighborhood of your choice, real or invented.

  1. Introduce the issue. One sentence naming the problem, using vocabulary from this chapter.
  2. Give a cause. One sentence using بِسَبَب to explain why the problem exists.
  3. Give a result. One sentence using لِذَلِك to explain what follows from the problem.
  4. Propose a condition and consequence. One sentence using إِذَا...، فَـ... to propose a possible solution.
  5. Add a detail with a source, if useful. One optional sentence using حَسَب, recycled from Chapter 13, to attribute a fact or a percentage.
  6. Close with your opinion. One sentence using فِي رَأْيِي... لِأَنَّ... to state your view and one reason.
  7. Review your draft against the editing checklist below.

قَائِمَة المُرَاجَعَة — Editing checklist:

  • I used بِسَبَب to give a cause, recycled from Chapters 6-7.
  • I used لِذَلِك to give a result, recycled from Chapters 6-7.
  • I used إِذَا...، فَـ... to state a condition and its consequence, recycled from Chapters 7-9.
  • I used فِي رَأْيِي... لِأَنَّ... with exactly one supporting reason.
  • If I included a percentage, I used it as a recognized expression rather than inventing new calculations.
  • I did not attempt to add case endings on my own beyond the fixed accusative expressions.
  • My paragraph reads as one connected description rather than a list of unrelated facts.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Community issues across Arabic-speaking regions

Local community issues such as transportation, waste management, and green space are discussed in municipal statements, local news, and everyday conversation across cities in North Africa, the Levant, the Gulf, and elsewhere. The specific issues, institutions, and solutions discussed vary widely from city to city and country to country, and no single community or country should be treated as representative of every Arabic-speaking place.

Regional Variation — Naming local government bodies

The term used for a local governing body such as بَلَدِيَّة (municipality) may vary by country and context, and some regions use related but distinct institutional terms. This chapter uses بَلَدِيَّة as a widely understood MSA term; learners working with a specific country's public documents should expect to encounter that country's own institutional vocabulary.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing بِسَبَب and لِأَنَّ

Error: اِزْدَاد الاِزْدِحَام بِسَبَب أَنَّ السَّيَّارَات كَثِيرَة. (using بِسَبَب as if it could directly introduce a full clause with أَنَّ)

Correction: اِزْدَاد الاِزْدِحَام لِأَنَّ السَّيَّارَات كَثِيرَة. or اِزْدَاد الاِزْدِحَام بِسَبَب كَثْرَة السَّيَّارَات. — بِسَبَب is followed by a noun phrase, while لِأَنَّ is followed by a full clause.

Common Mistake — Stacking multiple reasons after فِي رَأْيِي

Error: فِي رَأْيِي، يَجِب أَن تَحُلّ البَلَدِيَّة المُشْكِلَة لِأَنَّهَا تُؤَثِّر عَلَى الصِّحَّة وَلِأَنَّهَا تُؤَثِّر عَلَى البِيئَة وَلِأَنَّهَا تُكَلِّف كَثِيرًا. (adding several reasons at once)

Correction: فِي رَأْيِي، يَجِب أَن تَحُلّ البَلَدِيَّة المُشْكِلَة لِأَنَّهَا تُؤَثِّر عَلَى صِحَّة السُّكَّان. — at this stage, support your opinion with exactly one clear reason; combining several reasons is developed later, in Chapter 18.

Common Mistake — Treating a percentage as an exact calculation task

Error: attempting to calculate or convert a percentage into a new number during a reading or listening task.

Correction: at this stage, treat percentages such as ثَلَاثُون فِي المِئَة as fixed expressions to recognize and understand, not as the basis for independent arithmetic in Arabic; further numeral and data language develops later in the series.

Answer Key

Listening Station

  • About forty percent of residents use their own cars to go to work.
  • If the municipality increases the number of buses, congestion will gradually decrease.
