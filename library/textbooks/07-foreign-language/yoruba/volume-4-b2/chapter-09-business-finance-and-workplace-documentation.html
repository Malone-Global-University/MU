Business, Finance, and Workplace Documentation · Speak and Write Yorùbá: Professional, Academic, Civic, and Cross-Regional Yorùbá

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Make a formal workplace request in writing and in speech.
  2. State and confirm deadlines using consolidated time-and-obligation frames.
  3. Use numbers, quantities, and money expressions accurately in a business context.
  4. Form comparisons between costs, quantities, options, or performance using ju ... lọ.
  5. Combine conditional and hedged frames from Chapters 7–8 with workplace consequences and uncertain outcomes.
  6. Recognize vocabulary associated with basic business documents, invoices, and procedures.
  7. Pronounce numbers, amounts, and formal workplace vocabulary clearly and accurately.
  8. Write a short workplace report or proposal in Standard Written Yorùbá.

Opening Communication Scenario

Adé's radio station is reviewing costs for its community programming, including the recent health advisory segment. His supervisor has asked him to write a short proposal requesting additional equipment, stating a deadline for approval, comparing two possible suppliers by cost and quality, and noting what will happen if the request is not approved in time.

  1. This chapter teaches how to make formal requests, state deadlines, use numbers and quantities, and form comparisons in Yorùbá.
  2. It applies the conditional and hedging frames from Chapters 7–8 to workplace consequences and uncertain outcomes.
  3. By the end of the chapter, you will write a workplace report or proposal, following the same process Adé uses for his equipment request.

Pronunciation Focus

Focus — Numbers, amounts, and formal terms in speech

Business communication depends on numbers and amounts being heard correctly the first time. This chapter's pronunciation focus is producing numbers, quantities, and money expressions clearly, and giving formal workplace vocabulary the same steady, informative delivery introduced in Chapters 7–8.

  • Practice number and amount phrases at a slightly slower pace than ordinary conversation, since a misheard number in a workplace document can cause a real error.
  • Pay attention to ju ... lọ in comparisons, keeping the tone of ju and lọ distinct from surrounding words so a comparison is not mistaken for a plain statement.
  • Practice reading an invoice-style line aloud, giving the item, the quantity, and the total amount as three clearly separated pieces of information.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter develops formal requests, deadlines, numbers and quantities, and comparison, and applies the conditional and hedging frames from Chapters 7–8 to workplace consequences and uncertain outcomes, inside the functional task of producing a workplace report or proposal.

Formal requests with `a ń bèèrè pé kí`

Pattern

A ń bèèrè pé kí [ẹnìkan] [ó ṣe ohun kan] kí ó tó [ọjọ́ kan].

a ń bèèrè pé kí ("we are requesting that") introduces a formal workplace request as a distinct clause, separate from the reason for the request and from any deadline attached to it. This keeps a written request precise and easy for a supervisor or office to act on.

A ń bèèrè pé kí ilé-iṣẹ́ ra ẹ̀rọ ìgbóhùnsáfẹ́fẹ́ tuntun kan kí ó tó òpin oṣù yìí.

We are requesting that the company purchase a new broadcasting device before the end of this month.

Deadlines with `ààlà ọjọ́` and `kí ... tó`

  • Ààlà ọjọ́ fún ìdáhùn sí ìtọ́rẹ̀ yìí ni ọjọ́ kẹẹdógún oṣù yìí. The deadline for a response to this proposal is the fifteenth of this month. ààlà ọjọ́ deadline vocabulary, developed this chapter
  • Ó yẹ kí a gba ìdáhùn kí a tó dé ọjọ́ tí a yàn. We should receive a response before we reach the date set. ó yẹ kí (Ch. 8) combined with kí ... tó deadline structure
Introduced — Numbers, quantities, and money with `iye` and `àpapọ̀ owó`

Workplace documents must state quantities and amounts precisely. iye ("amount, number of") introduces a count: iye ẹ̀rọ tí a nílò ni méjì ("the number of devices we need is two"). àpapọ̀ owó ("total amount") introduces a sum: àpapọ̀ owó fún ohun ìtọ́rẹ̀ yìí jẹ́ ọ̀kẹ́ márùn-ún náírà ("the total amount for this request is fifty thousand naira"). Numbers and amounts should appear as their own clear phrase rather than folded into a longer sentence.

Introduced — Comparison with `ju ... lọ`

ju ... lọ ("more than") compares two options by cost, quantity, or quality: owó olùpèsè kìíní tóbi ju ti olùpèsè kejì lọ ("the first supplier's price is bigger than the second supplier's"). The item being compared appears before ju, and the item it is compared to appears between ju and lọ. This frame keeps a comparison distinct from two separate factual statements.

Consolidated — Conditional workplace consequence with `bí ... bá`

The conditional frame bí ... bá, consolidated in Chapters 7–8 for precaution-and-outcome sentences, is now applied to workplace consequences: bí a kò bá fọwọ́sí ìtọ́rẹ̀ yìí kí ó tó ààlà ọjọ́, iṣẹ́ náà lè dúró ("if this request is not approved before the deadline, the work may stop"). As before, the condition clause states the circumstance and the result clause states the consequence, kept distinct from a flat statement of fact.

Consolidated — Hedged modality with `ó ṣeéṣe kí`

As in Chapters 7–8, an uncertain workplace outcome should not be stated as certain. ó ṣeéṣe kí ("it is possible that") is applied here to business uncertainty: ó ṣeéṣe kí owó ẹ̀rọ náà pọ̀ sí i ní oṣù tí ń bọ̀ ("it is possible that the price of the device may increase next month"). This keeps a report or proposal from promising a specific figure or event the source information does not guarantee.

Model Dialogue

Ipò: Adé àti Ẹ̀gbọ́n Fúnmi, alábojútó rẹ̀ ní ilé-iṣẹ́ rédíò, ń jíròrò ìtọ́rẹ̀ fún ẹ̀rọ ìgbóhùnsáfẹ́fẹ́ tuntun.
Setting: Adé and Ẹ̀gbọ́n Fúnmi, his supervisor at the radio station, are discussing a proposal for a new broadcasting device.

ADÉ A ń bèèrè pé kí ilé-iṣẹ́ ra ẹ̀rọ ìgbóhùnsáfẹ́fẹ́ tuntun kan kí ó tó òpin oṣù yìí. We are requesting that the company purchase a new broadcasting device before the end of this month.
ẸGBỌ́N FÚNMI Iye owó rẹ̀ mélòó ni, àti ta ni olùpèsè tí a yàn? How much does it cost, and who is the supplier chosen?
ADÉ A ní olùpèsè méjì. Owó olùpèsè kìíní tóbi ju ti olùpèsè kejì lọ, ṣùgbọ́n ẹ̀rọ olùpèsè kìíní dára ju ti kejì lọ pẹ̀lú. We have two suppliers. The first supplier's price is bigger than the second supplier's, but the first supplier's device is also better than the second supplier's.
ẸGBỌ́N FÚNMI Ààlà ọjọ́ fún ìdáhùn nkọ́? What about the deadline for a response?
ADÉ Ààlà ọjọ́ ni ọjọ́ kẹẹdógún oṣù yìí. Bí a kò bá fọwọ́sí ìtọ́rẹ̀ yìí kí ó tó ọjọ́ náà, iṣẹ́ àpèjọ ọ̀sẹ̀ tí ń bọ̀ lè dúró. The deadline is the fifteenth of this month. If this request is not approved before that date, next week's broadcast work may stop.
ẸGBỌ́N FÚNMI Mo ti gbọ́. Ó ṣeéṣe kí owó ẹ̀rọ náà pọ̀ sí i ní oṣù tí ń bọ̀, nítorí náà ó yẹ kí a yára fọwọ́sí i. I have heard. It is possible that the price of the device may increase next month, so we should approve it quickly.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Formality in Yorùbá workplace requests

    In many Yorùbá-speaking workplaces, a written request to a supervisor or finance office is expected to state the request, the reason, and the deadline clearly and respectfully, often opening with a courteous greeting such as ẹ kú iṣẹ́ before moving to the business matter itself.

    Regional Note — Currency and number conventions

    Specific currency terms and number conventions can vary somewhat between institutions and regions. This chapter's patterns describe a widely usable formal-register model using the naira as a familiar example; learners working with other currencies or institutional formats should treat those differences as labeled variation rather than as errors.

    Common Mistakes

    Common Mistake — Dropping `lọ` from the comparison frame

    Error: Writing "owó olùpèsè kìíní tóbi ju ti olùpèsè kejì" and omitting the closing lọ, which leaves the comparison incomplete.

    Correction: Complete the comparison frame with both parts, as in "owó olùpèsè kìíní tóbi ju ti olùpèsè kejì lọ."

    Common Mistake — Stating an uncertain price or outcome as certain

    Error: Writing "owó ẹ̀rọ náà yóò pọ̀ sí i" ("the price of the device will increase") when the source only states this as a possibility.

    Correction: Use the hedge frame when the source itself is not certain, as in "ó ṣeéṣe kí owó ẹ̀rọ náà pọ̀ sí i."

    • Ask a Yorùbá-speaking friend, tutor, or colleague to check whether your numbers and comparisons sound natural and precise.
    • Compare your report or proposal to the model shell and note any structural differences.
