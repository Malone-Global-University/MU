Àyẹ̀wò Àárín Ìdìpọ̀ B2

Mid-Volume B2 Bridge Assessment · Speak and Write Yorùbá: Independent Communication and Connected Discourse

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Narrate a connected personal or community event, tracking participants and time reference across several sentences (Chapters 1–2).
  2. Use serial verb constructions to describe actions, tools, and results without relying on English-style "and" (Chapter 3).
  3. Use ni focus to clarify, contrast, and correct information (Chapter 4).
  4. Describe people, places, and objects using tí relative clauses (Chapter 5).
  5. Explain reasons, purposes, and conditions using scheduled clause connectors (Chapter 6).
  6. State and support an opinion on an education, career, or community topic using contrast and reason clauses (Chapters 7–8).
  7. Attribute information to a source and track completed, ongoing, and expected events when summarizing a short media report (Chapter 9).
  8. Demonstrate accurate tone marking and full diacritics across connected narration, explanation, and summary.

Assessment Scenario

Adé has been asked to prepare a short community update for a neighborhood association meeting. The update must narrate a recent event, explain a decision using reasons and results, describe a person or place involved using a relative clause, clarify one point of confusion using focus, and summarize what the local radio station reported about it. This chapter brings together everything practiced across Chapters 1 through 9 into one connected task.

  1. This chapter introduces no new grammar, particles, pronouns, serial verbs, focus constructions, or relative clauses. Every task below draws only on systems already active through Chapter 9.
  2. Assessment evidence is gathered across listening, speaking, reading, and writing, with explicit attention to tone and orthographic accuracy.
  3. By the end of this chapter, you should be able to combine narration, serialization, focus, reasoning, opinion, and reported-speech summary into a single connected performance.

Grammar Systems Review

No new grammar is introduced in Chapter 10. This section recycles the productive systems from Chapters 1–9 that the assessment tasks require. Each system is reviewed at the level already established; none is expanded here.

Review: Narrative sequencing and reference (Chapters 1–2)

  • Ní ọjọ́ kan, mo lọ sí ọjà pẹ̀lú ìyá mi, a sì rí ọ̀rẹ́ wa níbẹ̀. One day, I went to the market with my mother, and we saw our friend there. time connector + pronoun/reference chain across two clauses

Review: Serial verbs (Chapter 3)

  • Ó lọ mú omi wá fún àwọn àgbàlagbà. He/she went and brought water for the elders. motion + transfer serialization, single event chain, no English "and"

Review: Focus (Chapter 4)

  • Kànga omi ni a kọ́kọ́ tún ṣe, kì í ṣe òrùlé ọjà. It was the water borehole that was repaired first, not the market roof. ni focus + contrastive negation

Review: Relative clauses (Chapter 5)

  • Obìnrin tí ó sọ̀rọ̀ ní ìpàdé náà jẹ́ olùkọ́ ní ilé ẹ̀kọ́ àdúgbò. The woman who spoke at the meeting is a teacher at the neighborhood school. tí relative clause modifying obìnrin

Review: Reasons, purpose, and modality (Chapters 6–7)

  • Ó yẹ kí wọ́n kọ́kọ́ tún kànga ṣe torí pé omi ṣe pàtàkì jùlọ. They should repair the borehole first because water is most important. obligation clause + reason clause

Review: Opinion and reported speech (Chapters 8–9)

  • Mo lérò pé ìpinnu yìí dára, ṣùgbọ́n agbéròyìn náà ròyìn pé àwọn kan ò gbà. I think that this decision is good, but the reporter reported that some people did not agree. opinion frame + contrast + reported-speech attribution
Assessment note — No new grammar

Every pattern above has already been introduced and developed in an earlier chapter. This chapter's grammar work is entirely recall and integration, not new instruction.

Ìjíròrò Àpẹẹrẹ Ìdánwò

Model Assessment Dialogue

Ipò: Adé ń múra sílẹ̀ fún ìpàdé àwùjọ, ó sì ń sọ fún àbúrò rẹ̀ ohun tí ó máa sọ.
Setting: Adé is preparing for the neighborhood meeting and tells his younger sibling what he plans to say.

ADÉ Ní ọ̀sẹ̀ tó kọjá, àwọn olùgbé àdúgbò pàdé láti jíròrò bí a ṣe máa lo owó àpapọ̀. Last week, the neighborhood residents met to discuss how to use the shared fund.
ÀBÚRÒ Kí ni wọ́n pinnu? What did they decide?
ADÉ Kànga omi ni wọ́n kọ́kọ́ yàn, kì í ṣe òrùlé ọjà, torí pé omi ṣe pàtàkì jùlọ fún ìlera àwọn ọmọdé. It was the water borehole they chose first, not the market roof, because water is most important for children's health.
ÀBÚRÒ Ta ni ó sọ bẹ́ẹ̀? Who said that?
ADÉ Obìnrin tí ó sọ̀rọ̀ ní ìpàdé náà jẹ́ olùkọ́, ó sì lọ mú àwọn àkọsílẹ̀ wá láti fi ẹ̀rí hàn. The woman who spoke at the meeting is a teacher, and she went and brought records to show evidence.
ÀBÚRÒ Ǹjẹ́ agbéròyìn kan ròyìn nípa ìpàdé náà? Did a reporter report on the meeting?
ADÉ Bẹ́ẹ̀ni. Wọ́n ròyìn pé olùgbé ti gbà, wọn yóò sì bẹ̀rẹ̀ iṣẹ́ náà ní ọ̀sẹ̀ tó ń bọ̀. Mo lérò pé ìpinnu yìí dára gan-an. Yes. They reported that the residents had agreed, and they will begin the work next week. I think this decision is very good.

Guided Practice

  1. Identify one sentence in the model dialogue for each of the following systems: narrative sequencing, serial verb, focus, relative clause, reason clause, opinion frame, reported speech.
  2. Rewrite Adé's third line (about the borehole and the roof) so that the market roof is focused instead of the borehole, keeping the contrast and reason clause intact.
  3. Combine the relative clause about the woman with a new reason clause explaining why she is a credible source (e.g., because she is a teacher).
  4. Identify the two tense/aspect particles (ti and yóò) in Adé's final line and state what each one marks.

Listening Assessment

Ask an instructor, study partner, or qualified Yorùbá speaker to read the passage below aloud at a natural connected pace, or read it aloud yourself and then cover it before answering.

If no speaker is available, this is a Listening Script for Independent Use. Machine text-to-speech must be checked by a qualified Yorùbá speaker before use as a pronunciation model.

"Ní ọ̀sẹ̀ tó kọjá, àwọn olùgbé àdúgbò kan pàdé láti jíròrò bí wọn yóò ṣe lo owó àpapọ̀ tí wọ́n kójọ. Kànga omi ni wọ́n kọ́kọ́ yàn, kì í ṣe òrùlé ọjà, torí pé omi mímọ́ ṣe pàtàkì jùlọ fún ìlera àwọn ọmọdé àdúgbò náà. Obìnrin tí ó dábàá èrò yìí jẹ́ olùkọ́ ní ilé ẹ̀kọ́ àdúgbò, ó sì lọ mú àwọn àkọsílẹ̀ ìlera wá láti fi ẹ̀rí hàn àwọn tó wà níbẹ̀. Agbéròyìn kan tí ó wà níbẹ̀ ròyìn pé olùgbé ti gbà lórí ìpinnu yìí, wọn yóò sì bẹ̀rẹ̀ iṣẹ́ kànga náà ní ọ̀sẹ̀ tó ń bọ̀. Àwọn kan, ní ìdàkejì, sọ pé òrùlé ọjà náà yẹ kí a kọ́kọ́ tún ṣe, torí pé òjò ń ba àwọn ẹrù jẹ́ nígbà gbogbo."

1. What did the residents choose to fix first, and what reason is given for that choice?

2. Who proposed this idea, and how is she described using a relative clause?

3. What did the reporter say the residents had already agreed to, and what did they say would happen next week?

4. What contrasting opinion do some residents hold, and what reason do they give for it?

Iṣẹ́ rẹ — Your task: Answer the four questions above in complete Yorùbá sentences.

  • Apply the Tone and Orthography Checkpoint below before finalizing.

    • Include

    • at least 1 focus construction with ni
    • at least 1 tí relative clause
    • at least 1 reason or purpose clause
    • at least 1 opinion frame
    • at least 1 reported-speech attribution

    Tone and Orthography Checkpoint
    • Every vowel that should carry a high or low tone mark has one.
    • Every mid-tone syllable is left correctly unmarked.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Community decision-making and shared accountability

    Community-level decisions about shared resources, such as boreholes or market repairs, are commonly made through open discussion in which residents weigh competing priorities and reach a collective decision, often followed by a public notice or broadcast update. Reporting such decisions accurately, including who proposed an idea and what evidence supported it, reflects a value placed on transparency and shared accountability within the community.

    Common Mistakes

    Common Mistake — Losing the focus construction when combining systems

    Error: Stating a choice as a plain declarative when the original meaning requires contrastive focus, such as *Wọ́n yan kànga omi when the point being made is that the borehole was chosen rather than the roof.

    Correction: Preserve the focus construction: Kànga omi ni wọ́n kọ́kọ́ yàn, kì í ṣe òrùlé ọjà ("it was the borehole they chose first, not the roof"). Losing the focus marker loses the contrast.

    Common Mistake — Flattening tense/aspect across a long combined passage

    Error: Using a single bare-verb tense throughout a passage that actually describes a completed agreement, an ongoing debate, and an expected future action, such as *wọ́n gbà, wọ́n bẹ̀rẹ̀ iṣẹ́ when the report distinguishes what has already happened from what will happen.

    Correction: Track each event with its correct particle: wọ́n ti gbà, wọn yóò sì bẹ̀rẹ̀ iṣẹ́ kànga náà ní ọ̀sẹ̀ tó ń bọ̀ ("they have agreed, and they will begin the borehole work next week").

    Answer Key

    Listening Assessment Questions

    1. The residents chose to fix the water borehole first, not the market roof, because clean water is most important for children's health.
    2. A woman who proposed the idea is described as "obìnrin tí ó dábàá èrò yìí" (the woman who proposed this idea); she is further identified as a teacher at the neighborhood school.
    3. The reporter said the residents had already agreed (ti gbà) on the decision, and that they would begin (yóò bẹ̀rẹ̀) the borehole work the following week.
    4. Some residents hold the contrasting opinion that the market roof should be repaired first, because the rain keeps damaging goods.

    Reading Assessment Questions

    1. It was decided that the borehole would be repaired first; the focus construction "kànga omi ni a óò kọ́kọ́ tún ṣe" ("it is the borehole that will be repaired first") expresses this.
    2. The relative clause "obìnrin tí ó dábàá èrò yìí, tí í ṣe olùkọ́..." (the woman who proposed this idea, who is a teacher...) identifies her role and credibility.
    3. She has already shown health records (ti fi...hàn) to the elders, and she explains that clean water will reduce illness among children (yóò dín...kù).
    4. There will be another meeting because some residents still want the market roof repaired first, so the matter needs further discussion before the borehole work begins.

    Guided Practice — Sample Responses

    1. Narrative sequencing: "Ní ọ̀sẹ̀ tó kọjá, àwọn olùgbé àdúgbò pàdé..."; serial verb: "ó lọ mú àwọn àkọsílẹ̀ wá"; focus: "Kànga omi ni wọ́n kọ́kọ́ yàn..."; relative clause: "Obìnrin tí ó sọ̀rọ̀ ní ìpàdé náà..."; reason clause: "...torí pé omi ṣe pàtàkì jùlọ"; opinion: "Mo lérò pé ìpinnu yìí dára gan-an"; reported speech: "Wọ́n ròyìn pé olùgbé ti gbà..." 2. Sample refocus: "Òrùlé ọjà ni wọ́n kọ́kọ́ yàn, kì í ṣe kànga omi, torí pé..." 3. Sample combination: "Obìnrin tí ó sọ̀rọ̀ ní ìpàdé náà jẹ́ olùkọ́, torí náà ọ̀rọ̀ rẹ̀ ṣe pàtàkì." 4. Ti marks the completed agreement (they have already agreed); yóò marks the expected future action (they will begin next week).

    Chapter Review — Apá A

    1. "Kànga omi ni wọ́n kọ́kọ́ yàn, kì í ṣe òrùlé ọjà, torí pé omi ṣe pàtàkì jùlọ fún ìlera àwọn ọmọdé" combines focus (identifying the borehole as the chosen option, in contrast to the roof) with a reason clause (explaining why); the focus clarifies which option matters, and the reason clause justifies it. 2. A relative clause narrows down which person is being discussed, while a reported-speech frame attributes a statement to a source; they operate on different parts of the sentence (identifying a participant versus attributing a claim) and do not compete for the same grammatical slot.
