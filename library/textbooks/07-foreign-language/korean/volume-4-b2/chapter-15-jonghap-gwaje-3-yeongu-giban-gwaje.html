종합 과제 3: 연구 기반 과제Unit III Assessment: A Research-Based Task

Synthesizing sources, academic writing, presentation, and audience adaptation · Speak and Write Korean: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Korean

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Read and listen to source-based content and identify citation language, argument structure, and register.
  2. Produce a short piece of research-based academic writing that uses attribution, nominal style, and argument sections as developed in Chapter 12.
  3. Deliver a short formal presentation and respond to questions, using presentation and Q&A language from Chapter 13.
  4. Adapt the same research content for a general audience and a specialized audience, and mediate a summary for someone who was not present, as practiced in Chapter 14.
  5. Recycle citation, attribution, and reliability language from Chapter 11 while completing all tasks in this chapter.
  6. Complete an integrated performance task that draws on reading, listening, writing, and speaking without introducing any new productive grammar system.

This chapter is the Unit III assessment. It introduces no major new productive system. It consolidates Chapters 11 (sources and documentation), 12 (academic writing), 13 (formal presentation and Q&A), and 14 (audience adaptation and mediation) into one research-based written and oral task.

Opening Scenario

박 교수님의 세미나가 마지막 주에 이르렀다. 수아와 마이클은 지난 네 챕터에서 다룬 야간 공사 소음 문제를 하나의 완결된 연구 과제로 정리해야 한다. 두 사람은 먼저 관련 자료를 다시 검토하고 출처를 정리한 뒤(11과), 짧은 학술적 글을 작성하고(12과), 그 내용을 발표하고 질문에 답하며(13과), 마지막으로 같은 내용을 일반 청중과 전문 청중에게 각각 맞게 전달해야 한다(14과). 박 교수님은 이번 과제가 새로운 문법이나 표현을 배우는 자리가 아니라, 지난 네 챕터에서 익힌 것을 하나로 모으는 자리라고 설명한다.

  1. You will review source material and citation language recycled from Chapter 11.
  2. You will listen to a short model presentation and answer comprehension questions.

읽기 과제 — Reading Task

Reading Task

Read the following short model academic paragraph, which synthesizes citation language (Ch. 11) with argument structure (Ch. 12). This is a general-purpose instructional text, not an authentic published source.

모범 학술 단락 (교육용 예시)

본 설문 조사에 따르면, 응답자의 60퍼센트가 야간 공사 소음에 대한 우려를 표명하였다. 다만 조사 대상 수가 제한적이라는 반론이 제기될 수 있다. 그러나 표본이 무작위로 선정되었다는 점에서 이러한 한계가 결론의 타당성을 크게 약화시키지는 않는다고 판단된다. 결론적으로, 주민들의 우려는 근거가 있는 것으로 볼 수 있으며, 담당 부서는 이 문제를 진지하게 검토할 필요가 있다.

내용 이해 — Comprehension questions:

  1. Identify the sentence that cites the survey finding and the sentence that states the counterargument.
  2. Identify the expression that signals the writer is drawing a conclusion.
  3. Which chapter's citation language does the opening sentence recycle, and which chapter's argument structure does the paragraph as a whole follow?
  4. If you were simplifying this paragraph for a general audience, which sentence would you shorten or restate first, and why?
듣기 과제 — Listening Task

Listening Task

Ask your instructor, a language partner, or a text-to-speech tool to read the following short model presentation opening aloud, using the flat, even formal rhythm introduced in Chapter 13. Listen twice without reading, then complete the tasks below.

수아가 세미나에서 발표를 시작하는 장면 (교육용 예시)

수아 오늘 발표에서는 야간 공사 소음에 대한 주민 설문 조사 결과를 말씀드리겠습니다. 먼저 조사 방법을 설명드리고, 이어서 결과와 반론, 그리고 결론 순서로 말씀드리겠습니다.In today's presentation, I will report the results of a resident survey on nighttime construction noise. First I will explain the research method, then I will present the results and counterargument, and finally the conclusion, in that order.
청중 표본 수는 얼마나 됩니까?How large was the sample size?
수아 말씀하신 대로 중요한 질문입니다. 응답자는 무작위로 선정된 100명이었습니다.As you said, that is an important question. The respondents were 100 people selected at random.

쓰기 과제 — Written Task

Written Task

Write a short research-based academic text (6–8 sentences) on a topic of your choice, following the same structure as the model paragraph above. You may reuse the nighttime-construction-noise material from Chapters 11–14 or select a comparable topic of your own with instructor approval.

과제 개요 연구 기반 짧은 학술문
[출처 인용] 본 조사/자료에 따르면, ~

[반론] 다만 ~라는 반론이 제기될 수 있다.

[반박 또는 인정] 그러나/다만 ~라고 판단된다.

[결론] 결론적으로, ~
[Citation] According to this survey/material, ~

[Counterargument] However, a counterargument that ~ may be raised.

[Response or concession] However/still, it is judged that ~

[Conclusion] In conclusion, ~
  1. 출처 확인. State the source of your information clearly, using attribution language recycled from Chapter 11.
  2. 주장 제시. State your position using argument language recycled from Chapter 12.
  3. 반론 다루기. Include at least one counterargument and respond to it.
  4. 결론. Close with a clear conclusion using 결론적으로 or an equivalent expression.

체크리스트 — Checklist:

  • The source of the information is clearly attributed.
  • A position is stated and supported with evidence.
  • At least one counterargument is included and addressed.
  • The paragraph closes with a clear conclusion.
  • Formal written register from Chapter 12 is used consistently.
  • Spacing, particles, and Hangeul spelling are accurate, with no romanization.
  • No structure is used beyond what has been activated in Chapters 1–14.
말하기 과제 — Oral Task

Oral Task

1단계 — 발표 — Formal presentation

Deliver a short oral presentation (60–90 seconds) of your written task, using presentation-opening language recycled from Chapter 13 (for example, 오늘 발표에서는 ~을/를 말씀드리겠습니다).

2단계 — 질의응답 — Q&A

Take at least two questions from a partner or instructor. Acknowledge each question before answering, using 말씀하신 대로 or an equivalent expression recycled from Chapter 13.

3단계 — 청중별 전달 — Audience adaptation

Deliver a one-sentence simplified version of your conclusion for a general-audience partner (using 쉽게 말하면), then a one-sentence formalized version for a specialized-audience partner (using 보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면), and finally a one-sentence mediated summary for a partner who did not hear the presentation (using 요약해서 말씀드리면).

혼자 하는 연습 — Solo alternative

Record all three stages back to back, naming the audience aloud before each ("발표," "질의응답," "청중별 전달") so the transitions are clearly marked.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Research presentation as a social event

In Korean academic and institutional settings, a formal presentation followed by a question period is a routine part of research culture, and the presenter's ability to acknowledge a question respectfully before answering — as with 말씀하신 대로 — is often viewed as evidence of preparation and composure, not merely politeness.

Important Boundary — This is a synthesis task, not a new topic

This chapter's task is a consolidation of Chapters 11–14. It does not require or reward the use of grammar, vocabulary, or honorific forms beyond what those chapters activated. Learners who introduce unscheduled advanced structures should be redirected to the systems already available to them.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Omitting attribution in the written task

Error: Stating a survey finding or claim without indicating where it comes from.

Correction: Use attribution language recycled from Chapter 11, such as 본 조사에 따르면, before presenting any cited finding.

Common Mistake — Ignoring a question instead of acknowledging it

Error: Answering a question during the Q&A portion without first acknowledging what was asked.

Correction: Acknowledge the question first, using 말씀하신 대로 or a similar expression, before giving the answer.

Common Mistake — Using the same wording for all three audiences

Error: Delivering the same sentence, only changing the sentence ending, for the general-audience, specialized-audience, and mediated-summary portions of the oral task.

Correction: Each audience portion should genuinely simplify, formalize, or compress the content, as practiced in Chapter 14, not merely change the ending.

Answer Key

Reading comprehension

  1. The citation sentence is 본 설문 조사에 따르면, 응답자의 60퍼센트가 야간 공사 소음에 대한 우려를 표명하였다. The counterargument sentence is 다만 조사 대상 수가 제한적이라는 반론이 제기될 수 있다.
  2. The conclusion is signaled by 결론적으로.
  3. The opening sentence recycles citation language from Chapter 11; the paragraph as a whole follows the argument structure (position, evidence, counterargument, response, conclusion) from Chapter 12.
  4. Sample response: the counterargument and response sentences would likely need the most simplification for a general audience, since they contain the most abstract methodological reasoning; a simplified version might restate the idea as "some people worried the study did not ask enough people, but since it was picked fairly, the result still seems trustworthy."

Listening comprehension

  • 수아 says she will present in this order: research method, then results and counterargument, then conclusion.
  • She acknowledges the question with 말씀하신 대로 중요한 질문입니다.
  • The sample size she reports is 100 people, randomly selected.

Section A — Sample response

출처 and 인용하다 and 신뢰할 만하다: Chapter 11. 주장하다 and 반론 and 결론적으로: Chapter 12. 발표하다 and 질문을 받다 and 말씀하신 대로: Chapter 13. 쉽게 말하면 and 보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면 and 요약해서 말씀드리면: Chapter 14.

Section C — Sample response

Check that: a source is clearly attributed near the beginning of the text, a position is stated and supported, at least one counterargument is raised and addressed, and the text closes with a clear conclusion using 결론적으로 or an equivalent expression.
