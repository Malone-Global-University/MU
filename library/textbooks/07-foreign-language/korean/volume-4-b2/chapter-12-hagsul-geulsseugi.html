Abstract nouns, nominal style, and argument-section structure · Speak and Write Korean: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Korean

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Form and use nominalized expressions with -(으)ㅁ and -기, and recognize common Sino-Korean abstract-noun suffixes such as -성, -화, -론, and -주의.
  2. Write nominal-style sentences appropriate to academic prose, including thesis statements built on the pattern 이 글의 목적은 ~는 데 있다.
  3. Organize a short piece of writing into 서론, 본론, and 결론, using section-transition expressions such as 첫째, 둘째, and 마지막으로.
  4. State a claim, support it with evidence, and briefly acknowledge a counterargument using 반론 and 반박하다.
  5. Read a short academic-style paragraph and identify its thesis statement, supporting points, and organizational markers.
  6. Write a short structured argument paragraph that combines nominal style, section organization, and this chapter's vocabulary with the citation and paraphrase skills from Chapter 11.

This chapter continues Unit III, 연구와 고급 글쓰기 (Research and Advanced Writing). It builds directly on the citation, paraphrase, and reliability language introduced in Chapter 11, applying those skills inside a more formally organized piece of academic writing.

Opening Scenario

수아 has finished gathering and evaluating her sources from Chapter 11 and now must turn her source notes into a short organized paragraph for 박 교수님's class. 박 교수님 explains that academic Korean writing favors a more compact, noun-centered style than everyday writing, and that a well-organized short paper signals its structure clearly: a thesis stated early, points introduced in order, and at least one counterargument acknowledged before the writer's own position is restated. 수아 realizes she already has the content — her citations, her paraphrase, her reliability evaluation — but now needs the language to organize that content into a recognizably academic shape.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — Nominalized forms and abstract-noun suffixes

Nominalized forms with -(으)ㅁ attach a batchim directly to the stem, so pay attention to how that batchim behaves before the following particle: 있음이, 전개함으로써. Keep the nominalizing -ㅁ clearly released rather than blending it into the next syllable.

Abstract-noun suffixes such as -성, -화, and -론 are frequent in this chapter's vocabulary. Practice keeping their vowels clear and full-value even in fast academic speech, since reducing them can make 타당성 and similar words hard to distinguish from related but different forms.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces two major new productive systems: (1) nominalization and nominal academic style, and (2) argument-section organization with thesis, support, counterargument, and conclusion. Both build on the citation and paraphrase patterns already made productive in Chapter 11.

1. New — Nominalization and Nominal Style

New Grammar — turning a clause into a noun

Korean can turn a predicate into a noun with -(으)ㅁ or -기, letting a whole clause act as the subject or object of another sentence. Academic writing favors this nominal style because it lets a writer name an action, state, or claim as a single compact unit rather than repeating full verb phrases.

  • 이 자료가 신뢰할 만함은 이미 확인되었다.That this material is trustworthy has already been confirmed.신뢰할 만하다 → 신뢰할 만함 (nominalized with -(으)ㅁ) + 은 = topic
  • 근거를 명확히 밝히기가 이 글의 목표이다.Clearly stating the grounds is the goal of this piece of writing.밝히다 → 밝히기 (nominalized with -기) as sentence subject
  • 이 글의 목적은 세 자료의 신뢰성을 비교하는 데 있다.The purpose of this piece of writing is to compare the reliability of three sources.이 글의 목적은 ~는 데 있다 = formal thesis-statement pattern

Use -(으)ㅁ for a completed fact or established state, and -기 for an activity, goal, or ongoing action. Do not overuse nominalization; ordinary sentence-ending predicates remain the default outside formal academic writing.

2. New — Argument-Section Structure

New Grammar — organizing 서론, 본론, and 결론

A short academic paragraph or essay states its thesis early, develops points in order using 첫째/둘째/마지막으로, briefly acknowledges a counterargument with 이에 반해 or 반론이 있을 수 있다, and closes with 따라서 or 이러한 이유로 before restating the position.

  • 첫째, 이 자료는 공신력 있는 기관에서 발표되었다.First, this material was released by a credible institution.첫째 = introducing the first supporting point
  • 이에 반해, 일부에서는 표본 수가 적다는 반론을 제기할 수 있다.In contrast, some may raise the objection that the sample size is small.이에 반해 + 반론을 제기하다 = introducing a counterargument
  • 그러나 표본 수의 한계를 고려하더라도 전반적인 경향은 뒷받침된다.However, even taking the limitation of the sample size into account, the overall trend is supported.responding to the counterargument before returning to the writer's position
  • 따라서 이 자료는 주장을 뒷받침하는 근거로 타당하다고 볼 수 있다.Therefore, this material can be considered valid grounds for supporting the claim.따라서 = concluding transition

Do not simply list points without transitions; the ordinal markers and contrastive/concluding connectors are what make the structure recognizable as organized academic writing rather than a loose collection of sentences.

3. Review — Combining with Citation and Paraphrase

Review and expansion — citation inside an argument

Combine Chapter 11's N에 따르면 and paraphrase markers with this chapter's argument structure so that each supporting point is both organized and properly attributed.

  • 둘째, 한 지역 연구소의 조사에 따르면 야간 공사에 대한 불만이 증가하고 있다. 다시 말해, 이 문제는 단순한 개인적 의견이 아니라 다수가 공유하는 우려이다.Second, according to a survey by a local research institute, dissatisfaction with nighttime construction is increasing. In other words, this issue is not merely one person's opinion but a concern shared by many.둘째 (organization) + N에 따르면 (Ch. 11 citation) + 다시 말해 (Ch. 11 paraphrase marker)
Building Block — The argument-paragraph sentence

[주제문: 이 글의 목적은 ~는 데 있다] + [첫째/둘째 + 근거 제시] + [이에 반해 + 반론] + [그러나/그럼에도 불구하고 + 재반박] + [따라서/이러한 이유로 + 결론]

주제문 = thesis statement; 근거 제시 = presenting evidence; 반론 = counterargument; 재반박 = counter-rebuttal; 결론 = conclusion

이 글의 목적은 야간 공사 소음에 대한 주민들의 우려가 타당한지 검토하는 데 있다. 첫째, 공신력 있는 지역 연구소의 조사에 따르면 응답자의 60퍼센트가 우려를 표했다. 이에 반해, 표본 수가 제한적이라는 반론이 있을 수 있다. 그러나 이러한 한계를 고려하더라도 전반적인 경향은 분명하다. 따라서 이 자료는 주민들의 우려가 타당하다는 주장을 뒷받침한다고 볼 수 있다.

The purpose of this piece of writing is to examine whether residents' concerns about nighttime construction noise are valid. First, according to a survey by a credible local research institute, 60 percent of respondents expressed concern. In contrast, one might object that the sample size is limited. However, even taking this limitation into account, the overall trend is clear. Therefore, this material can be considered to support the claim that residents' concerns are valid.

Practice — Building an Argument Paragraph

  1. Write one thesis-statement sentence using 이 글의 목적은 ~는 데 있다.
  2. Write two supporting-point sentences introduced with 첫째 and 둘째, each citing a source as in Chapter 11.
  3. Write one counterargument sentence introduced with 이에 반해.
  4. Write one concluding sentence using 따라서 or 이러한 이유로.

체크리스트 — Checklist:

  • The paragraph opens with a clear thesis statement using the target pattern.
  • At least two supporting points are introduced in order with ordinal markers.
  • Each supporting point cites a source, recycling Chapter 11's attribution language.
  • A counterargument is acknowledged and briefly answered, not ignored.
  • The conclusion uses a concluding discourse marker and restates the thesis.

  • Formal register is maintained throughout, without casual endings.
  • Spacing, particles, and Hangeul spelling are accurate, with no romanization.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Expository organization in Korean academic writing

South Korean secondary and university writing instruction commonly teaches 서론-본론-결론 as the basic macro-structure for expository and argumentative writing, broadly similar in function to introduction-body-conclusion structures taught elsewhere, though specific expectations for thesis placement, evidence density, and counterargument use vary by discipline and instructor.

Culture in Context — Nominal style as a register marker

Nominalized, noun-centered sentences are strongly associated with formal written registers in Korean, including academic writing, official reports, and news writing. Using this style in casual conversation would sound stiff and out of place, just as an overly nominalized style would sound unusual in casual speech in many languages.

Important Boundary — This chapter is not discipline-specific academic training

This chapter teaches the Korean language needed to organize a short argument paragraph at a general level. It does not replace formal instruction in a specific academic discipline's writing conventions, thesis standards, or citation style, and learners preparing an actual academic submission should consult their institution's or discipline's official guidelines.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Listing evidence without a thesis statement

Error: Beginning directly with 첫째, ... without first stating what the paragraph is trying to show.

Correction: Open with a thesis statement such as 이 글의 목적은 ~는 데 있다 before listing supporting points, so the reader knows what the evidence is meant to support.

Common Mistake — Ignoring the counterargument entirely

Error: Presenting only supporting evidence with no acknowledgment that another view is possible, which can make an argument seem one-sided rather than carefully reasoned.

Correction: Include at least one sentence introduced with 이에 반해 or 반론이 있을 수 있다, followed by a brief response, before moving to the conclusion.

Common Mistake — Overusing nominalization until the writing becomes unclear

Error: Stacking multiple nominalized clauses inside one sentence until the subject and main predicate become difficult to identify.

Correction: Use one nominalized expression per sentence where it genuinely improves concision, such as in a thesis statement, and keep other sentences in ordinary predicate-final form: 이 글의 목적은 ~는 데 있다. 첫째, ...

Reading comprehension

  1. The thesis-statement sentence is 이 글의 목적은 야간 공사 소음에 대한 주민들의 우려가 타당한지 검토하는 데 있다, stating that the purpose is to examine whether residents' concerns about nighttime construction noise are valid.
  2. The two supporting points, introduced with 첫째 and 둘째, are: (1) about 60 percent of respondents in a local institute's survey expressed concern about the noise, and (2) the survey was conducted by a credible institution with a clearly stated method and source.
  3. The counterargument sentence begins with 이에 반해 and raises the objection that the sample size is limited; the response sentence begins with 그러나 and notes that random sampling limits the impact of this weakness.
