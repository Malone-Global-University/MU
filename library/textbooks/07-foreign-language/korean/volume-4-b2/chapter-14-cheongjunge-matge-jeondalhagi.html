청중에 맞게 전달하기Mediation for Different Audiences

Audience adaptation, summary, and register-transfer language · Speak and Write Korean: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Korean

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify how audience, purpose, and setting change what content to include, what to leave out, and how formally to express it, using expressions such as 청중에 따라 다르게 전달하다 and 쉽게 풀어서 설명하다.
  2. Convert the same underlying content — your Chapter 12–13 argument on nighttime construction noise — into two different deliveries: one for a general, non-specialist audience and one for a formal, expert audience such as a professor or institutional reviewer.
  3. Simplify specialized or academic content for a general audience using expressions such as 쉽게 말하면, 일반적으로 말해서, and 한마디로, without distorting the underlying facts.
  4. Raise the formality and precision of content for a specialized or institutional audience using expressions such as 보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면 and 전문적인 용어로는.
  5. Summarize a longer text or presentation for a listener who was not present, using mediation expressions such as 요약해서 말씀드리면 and 간단히 전달하자면.
  6. Listen to two deliveries of the same content aimed at different audiences and identify what changed and what stayed the same.

This chapter continues Unit III, 연구와 고급 글쓰기 (Research and Advanced Writing), and directly follows Chapter 13's presentation and Q&A skills. It does not introduce a new topic; it teaches how to adapt the same content — thesis, evidence, counterargument, and conclusion — for different listeners and readers.

Opening Scenario

수아는 지난 세미나에서 발표한 논증을 이제 두 가지 다른 청중에게 다시 전달해야 한다. 하나는 아파트 주민 모임으로, 전문 용어 없이 쉽고 친근하게 설명해야 한다. 다른 하나는 구청 소음 민원 담당 부서로, 조사 방법과 통계를 정확하고 격식 있게 제시해야 한다. 박 교수님은 내용 자체는 같지만, 청중에 따라 무엇을 강조하고 무엇을 생략할지, 그리고 얼마나 격식을 갖출지가 달라져야 한다고 설명한다. 수아는 또한 이 발표에 참석하지 못한 친구 마이클에게 전체 내용을 짧게 요약해서 전달해야 한다.

  1. You will learn vocabulary and sentence patterns for adapting content to different audiences.
  2. You will learn expressions for simplifying content for a general audience and formalizing content for a specialized audience.
  3. You will learn mediation expressions for summarizing information for someone who was not present.
  4. You will listen to two versions of the same content delivered to different audiences.
  5. You will read a general-audience version and a specialized-audience version of the same content side by side.
  6. You will adapt your own Chapter 12–13 content for two different audiences and mediate it in summary for a third.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — Register-signaling intonation shifts

When moving from a formal, institutional delivery to a simplified, friendly explanation for a general audience, Korean speakers often shift not only vocabulary but also pitch range and pacing. A simplified explanation for neighbors or friends typically uses a warmer, more varied intonation with shorter pauses, while a formal delivery to an institutional audience keeps the flatter, more even rhythm introduced in Chapter 13.

Practice saying the same sentence twice: once with the flat, settled intonation used for 전문적인 용어로는 and once with the warmer, more conversational intonation used for 쉽게 말하면. Listeners should be able to tell which audience you are addressing from your delivery alone, even before the content itself changes.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces two major new productive systems: (1) simplification and formalization language for adapting the same content to different audiences, and (2) summary/mediation language for relaying information to someone who was not present. Both recycle the argument content and presentation framing from Chapters 12–13 rather than introducing new subject matter.

1. New — Simplifying for a General Audience

New Grammar — simplification expressions

When addressing a general or non-specialist audience, replace technical terms and dense statistics with everyday vocabulary, concrete examples, and shorter sentences, while keeping the underlying claim accurate.

  • 쉽게 말하면, 밤에 공사 소리 때문에 잠을 못 자는 주민이 많다는 뜻입니다.Put simply, this means that many residents cannot sleep because of construction noise at night.쉽게 말하면 = simplification transition, restating a formal claim in plain terms
  • 일반적으로 말해서, 응답자 열 명 중 여섯 명이 소음 문제를 지적했습니다.Generally speaking, six out of ten respondents pointed to the noise problem.일반적으로 말해서 + rounded, everyday number (열 명 중 여섯 명) replacing 약 60퍼센트
  • 전문 용어를 빼고 말씀드리면, 조사 대상 수가 적어서 결과를 조심스럽게 봐야 한다는 지적이 있었습니다.Leaving out technical terms, there was a point raised that, because the number of survey subjects was small, the results should be viewed cautiously.전문 용어를 빼고 말씀드리면 = explicit simplification frame
  • 한마디로, 주민들의 걱정은 근거가 있다고 볼 수 있습니다.In a word, residents' concerns can be seen as having grounds.한마디로 = compressed one-line summary for a general audience

Do not simply speak more slowly and use the same technical vocabulary. Genuine simplification replaces specialized terms and precise statistics with everyday equivalents and concrete, relatable examples.

2. New — Formalizing for a Specialized or Institutional Audience

New Grammar — formalization expressions

When addressing a specialized or institutional audience, restore precise figures, name the research method explicitly, and use formal discourse markers that raise the register above ordinary spoken delivery.

  • 보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면, 본 조사는 무작위 표본추출을 통해 응답자 100명을 대상으로 실시되었습니다.To put it more specifically, this survey was conducted with 100 respondents selected through random sampling.보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면 = formalization transition, restoring method and exact figure
  • 전문적인 용어로는 이를 소음 민감도라고 합니다.In specialized/technical terms, this is called noise sensitivity.전문적인 용어로는 = introducing a technical term for a specialized audience
  • 본 민원과 관련하여, 응답자의 60퍼센트가 야간 소음에 대한 우려를 표명하였습니다.Regarding this petition, 60 percent of respondents expressed concern about nighttime noise.공식 문어체 표현 (본 민원과 관련하여, 표명하였습니다) = formal institutional register recycled from Ch. 11–12
  • 담당 부서에 공식적으로 제출할 자료에는 조사 방법과 표본 수의 한계를 명확히 밝혀야 합니다.In the material to be officially submitted to the responsible department, the research method and the limitation of the sample size must be clearly stated.공식적으로 제출하다 + 명확히 밝히다 = institutional documentation frame

Do not formalize only by adding -습니다 endings. Genuine formalization also restores precise figures, names the method, and uses institution-appropriate vocabulary that a general-audience version would have simplified away.

3. New — Mediating a Summary for Someone Who Was Not Present

New Grammar — summary/mediation expressions

When relaying information to someone who did not hear the original presentation, compress the content into a short summary, using reported-speech patterns already active from earlier volumes together with this chapter's summary markers.

  • 요약해서 말씀드리면, 수아는 야간 공사 소음에 대한 주민들의 우려가 근거가 있다고 주장했어.To summarize, Sua argued that residents' concerns about nighttime construction noise have grounds.요약해서 말씀드리면 + 주장했다 (recycled reported speech) = mediated summary for an absent listener
  • 간단히 전달하자면, 조사에서는 응답자의 60퍼센트가 걱정했지만, 표본 수가 적다는 반론도 있었대.To convey it briefly, 60 percent of respondents were worried in the survey, but there was also a counterargument that the sample size was small.간단히 전달하자면 + -대 (recycled quotative ending) = casual mediation between peers
  • 핵심만 말하면, 소음 문제는 진짜 문제이고, 조사 방법에도 큰 결함은 없다는 결론이었어.If I just say the core point, the conclusion was that the noise problem is real and there is no major flaw in the research method.핵심만 말하면 = compressed mediation to a peer

Do not mediate a summary by repeating every sentence from the original presentation in order. A mediated summary compresses the thesis, the strongest supporting point, and the conclusion, and may adjust speech level depending on who is receiving the summary.

Building Block — Three deliveries, one set of facts

[일반 청중: 쉽게 말하면 / 일반적으로 말해서 / 한마디로] · [전문 청중: 보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면 / 전문적인 용어로는] · [부재중인 사람에게: 요약해서 말씀드리면 / 간단히 전달하자면 / 핵심만 말하면]

청중 분석 = analyzing the audience; 강조점 선택 = choosing what to emphasize; 생략 판단 = deciding what to omit; 격식 조정 = adjusting formality

일반 청중: 쉽게 말하면, 밤에 공사 소리 때문에 잠을 못 자는 주민이 많다는 뜻입니다. 전문 청중: 보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면, 본 조사는 무작위 표본추출을 통해 응답자 100명을 대상으로 실시되었습니다. 부재중인 친구에게: 요약해서 말씀드리면, 수아는 주민들의 우려가 근거가 있다고 주장했어.

General audience: Put simply, this means many residents cannot sleep because of construction noise at night. Specialized audience: To put it more specifically, this survey was conducted with 100 respondents through random sampling. To an absent friend: To summarize, Sua argued that residents' concerns have grounds.

Practice — Adapting One Fact to Three Deliveries

  1. Take one statistic from your Chapter 12–13 material and state it three ways: simplified for neighbors, formalized for an institutional department, and mediated in summary for an absent friend.
  2. Write one sentence using 쉽게 말하면 and one using 보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면 that describe the same underlying fact.
  3. Write a two-sentence mediated summary of your Chapter 13 presentation using 요약해서 말씀드리면, adjusting speech level for a peer listener.

Model Dialogue

수아가 아파트 주민 모임에서 발표한 후, 구청 담당자에게 같은 내용을 다시 전달하는 장면 (교육용 예시)

수아 (주민에게) 쉽게 말하면, 밤에 공사 소리 때문에 잠을 못 자는 주민이 많다는 뜻입니다. 조사해 보니 열 명 중 여섯 명이 이 문제를 걱정하고 있었습니다.Put simply, this means many residents cannot sleep because of construction noise at night. When we surveyed, six out of ten people were worried about this issue.
주민 아, 그럼 이게 그냥 몇 사람만의 불만이 아니라는 거네요.Ah, so this isn't just a complaint from a few people.
수아 (구청 담당자에게) 보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면, 본 조사는 무작위 표본추출을 통해 응답자 100명을 대상으로 실시되었으며, 응답자의 60퍼센트가 야간 소음에 대한 우려를 표명하였습니다.To put it more specifically, this survey was conducted with 100 respondents selected through random sampling, and 60 percent of respondents expressed concern about nighttime noise.
구청 담당자 표본 수가 100명이면 전체 주민을 대표한다고 보기는 어렵지 않겠습니까?If the sample is 100 people, wouldn't it be difficult to consider that representative of the whole resident population?
수아 말씀하신 대로 표본 수는 제한적이었습니다. 다만 무작위로 선정되었다는 점에서 이러한 한계가 결론을 크게 약화시키지는 않는다고 봅니다.As you said, the sample size was limited. However, since it was randomly selected, I believe this limitation does not seriously weaken the conclusion.
수아 (마이클에게, 나중에) 요약해서 말씀드리면, 조사에서는 응답자의 60퍼센트가 걱정했지만, 표본 수가 적다는 반론도 있었어. 그래도 무작위 선정이었으니까 결론은 여전히 타당하다고 봐.To summarize, 60 percent of respondents were worried in the survey, but there was also a counterargument about the small sample size. Still, since it was randomly selected, I think the conclusion is still valid.

Guided Practice

  1. Read the model dialogue again and underline every expression used to simplify, formalize, or mediate the same underlying fact.
  2. Identify the exact statistic that appears in three different forms across the dialogue (열 명 중 여섯 명 / 60퍼센트 / 60퍼센트) and explain why each form suits its audience.
  3. Using your Chapter 12–13 material, write one simplified sentence and one formalized sentence about the same fact.
  4. Write a two-sentence mediated summary of your own presentation for a friend who was not present.
  5. With a partner, take turns delivering the same short fact to a "general audience partner" and then to a "specialized audience partner," adjusting vocabulary and formality each time.

Listening Practice

Ask your instructor, a language partner, or a text-to-speech tool to read the following two short deliveries of the same content aloud, using a warmer voice for the general-audience version and a flatter, more formal voice for the specialized-audience version. Listen twice without reading, then complete the tasks below.

마이클이 같은 내용을 두 청중에게 전달하는 장면 (교육용 예시)

마이클 (일반 청중) 쉽게 말하면, 제가 조사한 자료는 출처가 확실하고 믿을 만합니다. 다만 조사 대상 수가 적어서 조심스럽게 봐야 한다는 지적도 있었습니다.Put simply, the material I researched has a clear and reliable source. However, there was also a point raised that, because the number of survey subjects was small, it should be viewed cautiously.
마이클 (전문 청중) 전문적인 용어로는 이를 표본 대표성의 한계라고 합니다. 보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면, 표본은 무작위로 선정되었으나 규모가 제한적이었다는 점에서 신뢰 구간이 넓어질 수 있습니다.In technical terms, this is called a limitation of sample representativeness. To put it more specifically, the sample was randomly selected, but because its size was limited, the confidence interval may widen.

Writing Workshop

Write three short texts based on your Chapter 12–13 argument content: (1) a simplified version (4–6 sentences) for a general audience such as neighbors, (2) a formalized version (4–6 sentences) for a specialized or institutional audience, and (3) a mediated summary (2–3 sentences) for a friend who was not present.

[전문 청중] 보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면, ~ / 전문적인 용어로는 ~

[Specialized audience] To put it more specifically, ~ / In technical terms, ~

[To someone absent] To summarize, ~ / If I just say the core point, ~
  1. 일반 청중용 작성. Simplify your thesis and one key statistic using 쉽게 말하면 or 일반적으로 말해서, and close with 한마디로.
  2. 전문 청중용 작성. Restore precise figures and method using 보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면 and name one technical term with 전문적인 용어로는.
  3. 부재중인 사람에게 요약. Compress the thesis, one supporting point, and the conclusion into two or three sentences using 요약해서 말씀드리면, adjusting speech level for a peer.
  4. 비교 검토. Underline what changed (vocabulary, figures, formality) and what stayed the same (the underlying claim) across your three versions.

체크리스트 — Checklist:

  • The general-audience version avoids technical terms and uses concrete, everyday numbers or examples.
  • The specialized-audience version restores precise figures, names the research method, and uses at least one appropriate technical term.
  • The mediated summary compresses the content into two or three sentences without listing every detail.
  • The underlying claim (residents' concerns have grounds) is preserved accurately across all three versions.
  • Speech level is adjusted appropriately for each audience (formal/institutional vs. peer).
  • Formal register markers from Chapters 11–13 are recycled correctly where appropriate.
  • Spacing, particles, and Hangeul spelling are accurate, with no romanization.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Audience-sensitive communication in Korean public life

In many Korean institutional and community settings, the same underlying issue is often presented differently to residents, officials, and media, with community-facing communication favoring plain, relatable language and institution-facing communication favoring precise figures, named methods, and formal written register such as 본 민원과 관련하여 or 표명하였습니다.

Culture in Context — Mediating between peers

When Korean speakers relay formal or institutional content to a friend or peer, it is common to shift both vocabulary and speech level, often moving from -습니다 or -아요/어요 toward more casual reported-speech endings such as -대 or plain forms, while still preserving the accuracy of the underlying content.

Important Boundary — This chapter is not translation or interpretation training

This chapter teaches audience adaptation and summary within a single language (Korean to Korean, adjusted for audience), not professional interpretation or translation between Korean and English. Learners preparing for formal interpretation, translation certification, or cross-language mediation work should seek specialized training beyond this general-purpose language course.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Changing only the ending, not the content

Error: Producing a "simplified" version that keeps all the same technical terms and precise statistics, only softening the sentence endings.

Correction: Genuine simplification replaces technical terms with everyday equivalents and rounds precise statistics into relatable comparisons, using markers such as 쉽게 말하면 to signal the shift.

Common Mistake — Losing the research method in the specialized version

Error: Delivering a "formalized" version to an institutional audience that still omits the research method and exact figures, as if speaking to a general audience.

Correction: A specialized-audience version must restore the method and precise figures using markers such as 보다 구체적으로 말씀드리면.

Common Mistake — Mediating a summary that is just as long as the original

Error: "Summarizing" for an absent listener by repeating nearly every sentence from the original presentation in order.

Correction: A mediated summary compresses the thesis, the single strongest supporting point, and the conclusion into two or three sentences, using markers such as 요약해서 말씀드리면 or 핵심만 말하면.
