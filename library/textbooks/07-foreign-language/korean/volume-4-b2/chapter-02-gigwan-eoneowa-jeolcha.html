기관 언어와 절차Institutional Language and Procedures

Administrative genres, procedure description, and formal requests · Speak and Write Korean: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Korean

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Recognize and use core institutional and administrative vocabulary in formal deferential register.
  2. Describe a multi-step procedure in order, using sequencing expressions appropriate to formal writing and speech.
  3. Make a formal request or inquiry to an institution, office, or public service, using appropriately deferential phrasing.
  4. Read and interpret a short administrative notice, application guideline, or office procedure text.
  5. Write a short formal request suitable for an office, school administration, or public institution.
  6. Maintain consistent formal register across an entire institutional interaction, applying the discipline developed in Chapter 1.

This chapter introduces no new particles, endings, or clause structures beyond what Volume III has already activated. It applies the register discipline from Chapter 1 to a new domain — institutional and administrative communication — and expands productive vocabulary and procedural sequencing language.

Opening Scenario

A week into the semester, 민준 needs to request an official enrollment certificate from the university's administrative office for a visa renewal, and 수아 must submit a formal request to extend a library book loan for her research project. Both need to communicate with institutional staff they have never met, in writing and in person, using language that is clear, procedurally accurate, and consistently formal from the first sentence to the last. 박 교수님 uses their two errands as an opportunity to introduce the class to the vocabulary and sentence patterns of institutional life — the language of offices, forms, procedures, and formal requests that appears throughout universities, government offices, banks, and other public institutions in Korea.

  1. You will learn vocabulary and expressions for describing institutional procedures step by step.
  2. You will practice making formal requests and inquiries appropriate to an office or public institution.
  3. You will read and respond to an administrative notice while maintaining consistent formal register.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — Sino-Korean compounds and clear syllable separation

This chapter introduces no new sound-change rule. Its productive vocabulary is dense with Sino-Korean compounds such as 신청서, 증명서, and 담당자. In careful formal speech, these compounds are pronounced with clear syllable-by-syllable articulation rather than the more contracted rhythm of casual conversation, since institutional staff frequently rely on precise pronunciation to identify which document or procedure a speaker means.

Pay particular attention to batchim before the following syllable in compounds like 접수하다 and 발급하다, where a rushed pronunciation can blur the boundary between syllables and cause confusion at a service window.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter refines procedure description and formal request-making. It does not introduce new particles, endings, or clause structures beyond what Volume III has already activated. The task is applying familiar systems — sequencing connectors, the formal deferential style, and polite request forms — to institutional communication.

1. Sequencing a Procedure — 우선, 그다음에, 마지막으로

Review and expansion — sequencing adverbs in formal procedure description

Describing an institutional procedure clearly requires marking each step in order. 우선 introduces the first step, 그다음에 introduces a following step, and 마지막으로 introduces the final step. These combine naturally with the deferential -습니다 style already active since Volume II.

  • 우선 신청서를 작성해야 합니다.First, you must fill out the application form.
  • 그다음에 서류를 창구에 제출합니다.Next, you submit the documents at the window.
  • 마지막으로 담당자가 서류를 접수한 후에 증명서가 발급됩니다.Lastly, after the staff member processes the documents, the certificate is issued.

2. Formal Requests — -아/어 주시기 바랍니다 and 문의드리다

Review and expansion — polite request forms in institutional register

-아/어 주시기 바랍니다 was previewed in Chapter 1's register comparison table. In institutional contexts, it is the standard way to make a formal request in writing, more distant and more formal than the everyday -아/어 주세요. The humble verb 문의드리다 ("to humbly inquire") softens a request further when writing to an institution or a person of higher status.

  • 증명서 발급 절차를 문의드립니다.I am writing to inquire about the certificate-issuance procedure. (humble, formal)증명서 발급 절차를 [object] + 문의드립니다 [humble formal verb]
  • 기한을 일주일 연장해 주시기 바랍니다.I would like to request that the deadline be extended by one week.

3. Describing Regulations and Requirements — -아/어야 하다 and 규정

Review and expansion — obligation forms applied to institutional regulations

The obligation pattern -아/어야 하다, active since Volume II, is central to describing institutional requirements. Combine it with 규정 to describe what a rule requires, and with the noun-modifying forms already active to describe required documents precisely.

  • 규정에 따라 여권 사본을 제출해야 합니다.According to the regulations, you must submit a copy of your passport.
  • 기한 내에 접수된 서류만 처리됩니다.Only documents received within the deadline are processed.
Building Block — The formal procedure description

우선, [step 1] + 그다음에, [step 2] + 마지막으로, [step 3, result] = 절차 설명

Model Dialogue

민준 visits the university's administrative office to request an enrollment certificate for a visa renewal.

Context: a student speaking with an administrative staff member at a university office window. Formal deferential register is expected throughout, since this is an institutional service interaction.

담당자 어서 오세요. 무엇을 도와드릴까요? Welcome. How may I help you?
민준 안녕하세요. 재학 증명서 발급 절차를 문의드리고 싶습니다. Hello. I would like to inquire about the procedure for issuing an enrollment certificate.
담당자 네, 우선 이 신청서를 작성해 주시기 바랍니다. 그다음에 학생증을 창구에 제시하시면 됩니다. Yes, first please fill out this application form. After that, you just need to present your student ID at the window.
민준 알겠습니다. 서류가 접수된 후에는 얼마나 걸립니까? I understand. How long does it take after the documents are received?
담당자 규정에 따라 접수 후 하루 이내에 발급됩니다. 다만 비자 관련 서류이신 것 같은데, 영문 증명서가 필요하십니까? According to the regulations, it's issued within one day after receipt. However, since this seems to be a visa-related document, do you need the certificate in English?
민준 네, 맞습니다. 영문 증명서로 신청하고 싶은데, 신청서에 따로 표시해야 합니까? Yes, that's right. I'd like to apply for the English-language certificate — do I need to mark that separately on the application form?
담당자 네, 신청서 하단에 영문 여부를 표시하는 칸이 있습니다. 마지막으로 서명하신 후 제출해 주시기 바랍니다. Yes, there is a box at the bottom of the form to indicate whether you need the English version. Lastly, please sign it and then submit it.
민준 잘 알겠습니다. 도와주셔서 감사합니다. I understand well. Thank you for your help.

내용 이해 — Comprehension questions:

  1. What three steps does 담당자 describe for obtaining the enrollment certificate?
  2. According to the regulations, how long does issuance take after documents are received?
  3. Why does 담당자 ask whether 민준 needs an English-language certificate?
  4. What must 민준 do at the very end of the procedure, according to 담당자's last instruction?

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Institutional service interactions in South Korea

In many South Korean institutional settings — university offices, government service centers, banks — service staff typically use consistently formal deferential register with visitors regardless of the visitor's age or status, and visitors are generally expected to respond in the same register. This differs from casual service encounters, such as ordering at a small café, where everyday polite speech is common on both sides.

Culture in Context — Written requests to institutions

Formal written requests to Korean institutions commonly open with a brief self-identification (name, affiliation, or student number), state the request clearly, and close with a polite expression of thanks for the reader's attention, as modeled in this chapter's writing workshop.

Regional Awareness — Procedures vary by institution

The specific steps, required documents, and processing times described in this chapter's dialogue and readings reflect common institutional patterns, but exact procedures vary by university, government office, and region. Always confirm current requirements with the specific institution involved.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using an everyday request form in a formal written request

Error: 기한 좀 늘려 주세요.

Correction: 기한을 연장해 주시기 바랍니다. — use the formal request pattern and precise vocabulary in written institutional communication rather than the casual spoken form.

Common Mistake — Listing procedure steps without sequencing expressions

Error: 신청서를 작성합니다. 서류를 제출합니다. 증명서가 발급됩니다. — technically correct but unclear as an ordered procedure.

Correction: Mark each step with 우선, 그다음에, or 마지막으로 so that the sequence is unambiguous to the reader or listener.

Common Mistake — Omitting necessary background in a formal request

Error: 연장해 주시기 바랍니다. — with no explanation of what is being extended or why.

Correction: State clearly what is being requested and provide brief, relevant background, as in the model email in the Writing Workshop.
