Consolidating register control, institutional language, comparison, and argumentation · Speak and Write Korean: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Korean

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Maintain a single, consistent formal register across an extended spoken or written text, without unmarked drift toward everyday polite or casual forms.
  2. Describe an institutional procedure or requirement clearly, using the administrative language developed in Chapter 2.
  3. Classify and compare two or more options using the contrastive and data-linked language developed in Chapter 3.
  4. State a position, support it with cited evidence, concede an opposing view, and respond to a counterargument, using the argumentative language developed in Chapter 4.
  5. Read and interpret an extended formal analytical text that combines description, comparison, and argument.
  6. Plan, draft, and revise a formal analytical text (공식 분석문) that integrates all four chapters of Unit I into a single coherent piece of writing and a related spoken presentation.

This is an assessment and synthesis chapter. It introduces no new grammar, particles, honorific systems, sound changes, or speech levels. Every task in this chapter draws only on vocabulary and structures activated in Chapters 1 through 4.

Opening Scenario

박 교수님's seminar has reached the end of its first unit. Over four chapters, 수아 and 민준's class has practiced writing with formal precision, describing institutional procedures, comparing options with evidence, and building an argument that takes an opposing view seriously. Now 박 교수님 asks each student to produce a single integrated document: a short formal analytical text, 공식 분석문, that describes a real or realistic institutional situation, compares at least two options or perspectives, and concludes with a clearly supported position. The task mirrors what learners will be asked to do in real academic, professional, and institutional settings — combine description, comparison, and argument into one coherent, appropriately formal piece of writing, and be prepared to present and defend it aloud.

  1. You will review and reactivate the language of Chapters 1 through 4 in an integrated way.
  2. You will read and analyze a model formal analytical text that combines description, comparison, and argument.
  3. You will plan, write, and present your own 공식 분석문, then check it against a shared rubric.

발음 — Pronunciation and Register Review

Pronunciation and Register Review

Pronunciation — Sustaining formal prosody across a longer text

This chapter introduces no new sound-change rule. The pronunciation focus is sustaining the measured pacing and even prosody of formal register across an entire spoken presentation, rather than only in isolated sentences. Pay particular attention to the pause before a pivot word such as 그렇다 하더라도 or 따라서, since these mark structural turns in a longer analytical text and should be audible to a listener.

Avoid letting pronunciation loosen toward casual rhythm in the later part of a long presentation. Formal register should sound the same in the conclusion as it does in the opening sentence.

Grammar Synthesis

This chapter introduces no new grammar system. It requires learners to combine four systems from Chapters 1–4 within a single coherent text: consistent formal register (Ch. 1), procedure and requirement description (Ch. 2), comparison and contrast (Ch. 3), and position-evidence-concession-conclusion argumentation (Ch. 4).

1. The Four-Part Analytical Structure

Synthesis — combining description, comparison, and argument

A 공식 분석문 typically moves through four functions in order: it describes the situation or procedure that makes the topic relevant (Ch. 2), compares the available options or perspectives (Ch. 3), takes a position supported by evidence while conceding an opposing view (Ch. 4), and closes with a conclusion — all in one consistent formal register (Ch. 1).

  • 기숙사 신청 절차는 다음과 같습니다.The dormitory application procedure is as follows. [description, Ch. 2]
  • 원룸과 기숙사는 비용과 자율성 면에서 차이가 있습니다.One-room apartments and dormitories differ in terms of cost and autonomy. [comparison, Ch. 3]
  • 저는 신입생이 기숙사에 거주해야 한다는 입장입니다.My position is that first-year students should live in the dormitory. [argument, Ch. 4]

2. Register Discipline Across a Full Text

Review — consistent formal register (Chapter 1)

Every predicate ending in a 공식 분석문 should belong to the same formal register established since Chapter 1. A single everyday polite ending in an otherwise formal text is a register error, not a stylistic choice.

Building Block — The Unit I synthesis paragraph frame

[절차/상황 설명] + [두 선택지의 비교: 차이점과 공통점] + [입장] + [근거] + 물론, [반대 관점] + 그렇다 하더라도, [반박] + 결론적으로/따라서, [정리]

기숙사 신청 절차는 학기 시작 한 달 전에 마감됩니다. 원룸과 기숙사는 비용과 자율성 면에서 차이가 있지만, 안전 관리 측면에서는 공통점이 있습니다. 저는 신입생이 기숙사에 거주해야 한다는 입장입니다. 학교 안전 보고서에 따르면 기숙사 거주 학생의 사고율이 더 낮습니다. 물론 원룸이 더 자유롭다는 점은 일리가 있습니다. 그렇다 하더라도 신입생에게는 안전과 적응이 더 중요한 근거가 됩니다. 따라서 신입생 기숙사 거주는 타당한 정책이라고 결론지을 수 있습니다.

The dormitory application procedure closes one month before the semester begins. One-room apartments and dormitories differ in cost and autonomy, but they share common ground in safety management. My position is that first-year students should live in the dormitory. According to the campus safety report, the accident rate is lower among dormitory residents. Of course, the point that one-room apartments are more free does have some validity. Even granting that, safety and adjustment are more important grounds for first-year students. Therefore, I can conclude that first-year dormitory residence is a reasonable policy.

Model Text

Context: a short formal analytical text on a familiar institutional topic, written in consistent formal deferential register throughout, as a model for the chapter's writing assessment.

설명 도서관 좌석 예약 제도는 학기 중 이용률이 높아지면서 도입되었습니다. 학생들은 온라인 시스템을 통해 하루 최대 네 시간까지 좌석을 신청할 수 있습니다. The library seat reservation system was introduced as usage increased during the semester. Students can apply for a seat for up to four hours per day through the online system.
비교 예약제와 선착순 제도는 공정성과 효율성 면에서 차이가 있습니다. 예약제는 계획적인 이용이 가능하다는 공통점이 있지만, 선착순 제도보다 절차가 복잡합니다. The reservation system and the first-come system differ in terms of fairness and efficiency. The reservation system shares the advantage of allowing planned use, but its procedure is more complex than the first-come system.
입장 저는 예약제를 유지해야 한다는 입장입니다. 도서관 이용 통계에 따르면 예약제 도입 이후 좌석 관련 불만이 크게 줄었습니다. My position is that the reservation system should be maintained. According to library usage statistics, complaints about seating have decreased significantly since the reservation system was introduced.
양보 물론 예약 절차가 번거롭다는 점은 일리가 있습니다. Of course, the point that the reservation procedure is cumbersome does have some validity.
반박 그렇다 하더라도 공정한 좌석 배분이라는 근거를 고려하면 이러한 불편은 감수할 만합니다. Even granting that, when the basis of fair seat allocation is considered, this inconvenience is worth accepting.
결론 결론적으로 도서관 좌석 예약 제도는 절차상의 불편을 감안하더라도 유지할 가치가 있는 정책이라고 생각합니다. In conclusion, I believe the library seat reservation system is a policy worth maintaining, even taking its procedural inconvenience into account.

Writing Assessment: 공식 분석문

This chapter's writing assessment asks you to write a complete formal analytical text (공식 분석문) that describes a situation or procedure, compares at least two options or perspectives, states and supports a position, concedes an opposing view, responds to it, and closes with a conclusion — all in one consistent formal register, following the structure of the Model Text and Reading Assessment above.

제목 공식 분석문: [학생이 선택한 주제]
[상황/절차 설명 — 한두 문장]

[비교 — 두 선택지의 차이점과 공통점, 한두 문장]

[입장 — 저는 ~라는 입장입니다]

[근거 — ~에 따르면 / 근거는 ~]

[양보 — 물론 ~라는 점은 일리가 있습니다]

[반박 — 그렇다 하더라도 ~]

[결론 — 결론적으로/따라서 ~]
[Description of the situation or procedure — one or two sentences]

[Comparison — differences and similarities between two options, one or two sentences]

[Position — my position is that ~]

[Evidence — according to ~ / the basis is ~]

[Concession — of course, the point that ~ does have some validity]

[Rebuttal — even granting that, ~]

[Conclusion — in conclusion / therefore, ~]
  1. Choose your topic. Select a familiar institutional situation with two comparable options or two reasonable sides (a campus, workplace-adjacent, or public-life topic drawn from Chapters 2–4 or a comparably familiar new topic).
  2. Describe the situation. Open with one or two sentences describing the relevant procedure or situation, using Chapter 2 language.
  3. Compare the options. Add one or two sentences comparing the options, naming at least one difference and one similarity, using Chapter 3 language.
  4. State your position and give evidence. Use 입장 and either 근거 or -에 따르면.
  5. Concede a point and respond. Use 물론 and 그렇다 하더라도 to acknowledge and then answer an opposing view.
  6. Close with a conclusion. Use 결론적으로 or 따라서 to restate your position.

체크리스트 — Checklist:

  • The text includes all four functions: description, comparison, position/evidence, and concession/rebuttal/conclusion.
  • Every predicate ending is in consistent formal deferential register from the first sentence to the last.
  • At least one comparison sentence names both a difference and a similarity.
  • At least one piece of evidence uses 근거 or -에 따르면.
  • A genuine concession appears and is followed by a clear pivot back to the writer's position.
  • Spacing, particles, and Hangeul spelling are accurate throughout, with no romanization.
  • No structure beyond Chapters 1–4 has been used productively.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Structured analysis as a shared academic and institutional expectation

In Korean academic and institutional writing, a text that clearly separates description, comparison, and argument, and that explicitly acknowledges an opposing view before responding to it, is generally read as more credible and better organized than a text that presents only one side of an issue without structure.

Culture in Context — Consistency as a marker of formality

Register consistency across an entire text or presentation is itself a marker of formality and care in Korean institutional and academic communication. A single lapse into everyday polite or casual language partway through a formal text can be noticed by a reader or listener even if the content itself is strong.

Regional Awareness — Institutional procedures vary by organization

The specific procedures referenced in this chapter's model texts, such as library reservation systems or recycling programs, are illustrative examples of the language pattern, not descriptions of any single institution's actual policy. Real procedures vary by university, workplace, and country.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Skipping the comparison function

Error: Moving directly from a description of the situation to a position statement, with no comparison sentence — this omits one of the four required functions.

Correction: Add at least one sentence naming a difference and a similarity between the options before stating a position, as in 두 제도는 비용과 편의성 면에서 차이가 있지만, 목표 면에서는 공통점이 있습니다.

Common Mistake — Register drift across a longer text

Error: Beginning a text in formal deferential style and shifting to everyday polite style by the conclusion, often because the writer is focused on content rather than form.

Correction: Check every predicate ending after drafting is complete, not only while writing. Read the text aloud from the last sentence to the first to catch drift more easily.

Common Mistake — A concession with no pivot back

Error: Ending the analytical text on the concession itself, so the reader is unsure whether the writer still holds their original position.

Correction: Always follow a concession with 그렇다 하더라도 or a similar pivot, and close with a conclusion that restates the position, as required in the checklist above.
