최종 과제: C1 포트폴리오Final C1 Portfolio

An integrated C1-oriented Korean portfolio · Speak and Write Korean: Professional, Academic, Media, and Institutional Korean

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Assemble a portfolio of reading, writing, listening, and speaking evidence that draws on formal, academic, media, institutional, rhetorical, cultural, historical, and negotiation Korean developed across Volume IV.
  2. Analyze a short multi-genre source set, identifying stance, register, and evidence across texts.
  3. Respond in writing to a formal or academic prompt using source-based paraphrase and attribution without new grammar.
  4. Deliver a short formal oral presentation that states a position, supports it with evidence, and responds to a follow-up question.
  5. Self-assess a completed portfolio against the Volume IV performance rubric.
  6. Identify which Korean systems are now consolidated for C1-oriented use and which remain areas for continued growth in Volume V.

This is the Unit IV and Volume IV capstone assessment chapter. It introduces no new particles, endings, honorific forms, connectors, counters, or sound changes. Its purpose is to consolidate and demonstrate productive control of systems already active from Chapters 1–19, applied together in an integrated, multi-genre task.

Portfolio Model: Multi-Genre Source Set

Read the following two short texts, written for this course as general-purpose instructional material rather than authentic published sources. They represent two genres reviewed in Volume IV: a short news-style report and a workplace notice.

자료 1 — 보도 요약 (교육용 예시)

최근 한 조사에 따르면 많은 직장인들이 유연 근무제를 선호하는 것으로 나타났다. 전문가들은 이러한 변화가 생산성에 긍정적인 영향을 줄 수 있다고 설명했다. 다만 일부 기업은 협업과 의사소통이 어려워질 수 있다는 우려를 나타냈다.

자료 2 — 사내 공지 (교육용 예시)

저희 회사는 다음 달부터 시범적으로 유연 근무제를 도입하기로 했습니다. 직원 여러분의 의견을 반영하여 일정을 조정하였으며, 문제가 발생하면 언제든지 인사팀에 문의해 주시기 바랍니다.

분석 과제 — Analysis task (Portfolio Component 1):

  1. What is the stance or purpose of each text, and how does the register differ between them?
  2. What evidence or attribution does Source 1 use to support its claim?
  3. What tone and speech level does Source 2 use, and why is it appropriate for that genre?
  4. Identify one concern raised in Source 1 that Source 2 does not directly address.

쓰기 워크숍 — Writing Workshop: Portfolio Pieces

Writing Workshop: Portfolio Pieces

Complete Portfolio Component 2: write a paragraph-length response (7–10 sentences) to the following prompt, synthesizing information from at least two of the sources above (the two reading texts and/or the listening dialogue) with clear attribution.

과제 — Prompt

유연 근무제 도입에 대한 여러분의 의견을 말하고, 위 자료들을 근거로 활용하여 설명하십시오.

State your opinion on the introduction of flexible work arrangements, and support it using the sources above as evidence.

  • 입장 정하기. State your overall position on flexible work arrangements in one clear sentence.
  • 출처 인용하기. Cite at least one claim from the sources using -에 따르면 or -다고 하다.
  • 양보하기. Acknowledge a counterpoint or concern from the sources using -지만 or -는데.
  • 조건 또는 제안 제시하기. Offer a condition or recommendation using -(으)면 or a softened proposal ending.
  • 결론 맺기. Close with a brief summary sentence restating your position.
    • Unsynthesized response (no attribution, no counterpoint) 유연 근무제가 좋아요. 회사가 도입해야 돼요.
    Synthesized response (Ch. 20 style) 한 조사에 따르면 많은 직장인들이 유연 근무제를 선호한다고 한다. 저도 이러한 변화가 긍정적이라고 생각한다. 협업이 어려워질 수 있다는 우려도 있지만, 회의 핵심 시간만 정해 두면 문제를 줄일 수 있을 것이다.

    체크리스트 — Checklist:

    • The response states one clear position in the opening sentence.
    • At least one claim is attributed to a source using scheduled attribution language.
    • At least one counterpoint or concern from the sources is acknowledged.
    • At least one condition or recommendation is stated clearly.
    • Vocabulary and predicate forms are limited to what has been activated in Chapters 1–20.
    • Spacing, particles, and Hangeul spelling are accurate, with no romanization.
    • The register remains formal and academic throughout.
    말하기 연습 — Speaking: Final Presentation

    Speaking: Final Presentation

    Complete Portfolio Component 3: prepare and deliver a short formal presentation (approximately one minute) based on your written response, then answer one follow-up question.

    1단계 — 준비 — Preparing the presentation

    Turn your written response into spoken presentation language, using formal presentation openings and closings reviewed from Chapter 13, such as 오늘은 -에 대해 말씀드리겠습니다 and 이상으로 발표를 마치겠습니다.

    Deliver your presentation to a partner, instructor, or recording. Have your partner or instructor ask one follow-up question (for example, about a concern you did not fully address), and respond using acknowledgment and condition language from this chapter and Chapter 19.

    혼자 하는 연습 — Solo alternative

    Record your presentation, then draft a plausible follow-up question yourself and record your spoken response to it.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Synthesis as a professional and academic skill

    In South Korean academic and professional settings, being able to bring together information from a news report, an official notice, and a colleague's spoken opinion into one coherent, attributed statement is often valued as a marker of maturity in formal Korean, whether in a report, a meeting summary, or a presentation.

    Important Boundary — A language portfolio, not a professional credential

    This chapter's portfolio demonstrates language proficiency at the end of Volume IV. It is not a professional certification, a workplace deliverable, or a claim to native-level command of Korean in every specialized field. Volume V continues to refine precision, rhetoric, and specialized expression beyond what this portfolio can demonstrate alone.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Common Mistake — Combining sources without attribution

    Error: Restating information from a source as though it were the learner's own claim, without any attribution language.

    Correction: Use attribution patterns such as -에 따르면 or -다고 하다, reviewed from Chapter 11, whenever a claim comes from one of the source texts.

    Common Mistake — Presenting only one side of the issue

    Error: Stating a position without acknowledging any counterpoint, which can sound one-sided in a formal academic or professional response.

    Correction: Acknowledge at least one counterpoint or concern using -지만 or -는데 before offering a condition or recommendation.

    Common Mistake — Shifting register between writing and speaking

    Error: Writing a formal academic response but delivering the presentation in an overly casual speech level, or the reverse.

    Correction: Keep the same formal or deferential register consistent across the written response and the spoken presentation, adjusting only the sentence-final endings to suit each medium.

    Answer Key

    Source analysis (reading)

    1. Sample response: Source 1 reports and interprets survey findings in a neutral, informative stance using media register; Source 2 announces a company decision in a more formal, directive-but-polite institutional register aimed at employees.
    2. Sample response: Source 1 attributes its claim to a survey, signaled by 한 조사에 따르면 ("according to one survey") and cites experts' explanations.
    3. Sample response: Source 2 uses deferential -습니다 style throughout, appropriate for an official notice addressed to all employees.
    4. Sample response: Source 1 raises the concern that collaboration and communication may become more difficult; Source 2 does not directly address this concern, only noting that employees may contact HR with problems.

    Listening comprehension

    • Sample response: Colleague A raises the concern that setting meeting times may become difficult, signaled by the softened phrase 좀 어려울 것 같아요 ("it seems like it might be a bit difficult"), which presents it as a mild concern rather than a firm objection.
    • Sample response: Colleague B proposes fixing a core time window for meetings; this depends on the team agreeing to treat that window as the shared availability period.
