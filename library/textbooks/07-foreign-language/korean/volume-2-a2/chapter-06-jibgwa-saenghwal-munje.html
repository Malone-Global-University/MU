6

집과 생활 문제Housing and Daily Problems

Making requests, describing complaints, expanding position and location language, and using repair language · Speak and Write Korean: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe rooms, furniture, and the condition of a home or apartment using position and location vocabulary such as 위, 아래, 안, 밖, 옆, 앞, and 뒤.
  2. Explain a household problem, such as something that is broken, noisy, or not working.
  3. Make a polite request using -아/어 주세요, softened with 좀 where appropriate.
  4. Make a polite complaint without sounding rude, using softening and indirectness strategies already practiced in earlier chapters.
  5. Use repair language, such as asking someone to repeat or clarify something, in a service or neighbor interaction.
  6. Read and listen to short exchanges about housing problems and requests for repair.
  7. Write a short message reporting a household problem and requesting help.

Communication Scenario

Unit II begins with 독립적인 일상생활, "independent daily life." 라일라 has just moved into a small apartment near school. The boiler is not working properly, a window will not close all the way, and the neighbor upstairs has been very noisy at night. 라일라 needs to describe these problems clearly to her landlord, 김 선생님, and to ask politely for help — without simply memorizing a list of complaints. This chapter builds on everyday polite -아요/어요 speech level and the request and softening patterns already introduced, and it adds vocabulary and functional language for describing household spaces, problems, and repairs.

  1. This chapter introduces new vocabulary and functional language for housing and daily problems.
  2. It does not introduce a new grammar system. Requests, softening, and speech level all continue from Chapters 1 through 5.
  3. Assessment in this chapter focuses on describing a household problem clearly and requesting help politely.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter reinforces final consonant neutralization and batchim behavior before particles, using household vocabulary. It introduces no new sound-change rule.

Pronunciation Note — 부엌 and other ㅋ-final words

부엌 ends in the batchim ㅋ, which is pronounced as an unreleased [k] sound in isolation, the same neutralized sound as batchim ㄱ. Before a vowel-initial particle, however, the ㅋ resurfaces through liaison: 부엌이 is pronounced with the ㅋ carried into the next syllable. Compare this with 방이, where the batchim ㅇ simply attaches to the following syllable without changing quality.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces no new grammar system. It expands the use of position nouns with the existential pattern already taught, and it develops requests, complaints, and repair language using patterns from earlier chapters.

Review and expansion: position nouns with 에

Position nouns such as 위, 아래, 안, 밖, 옆, 앞, and 뒤 combine with 의-less noun phrases and the existential particle 에, following the pattern already introduced with 책상 위에 책이 있어요.

PatternExampleGloss
[장소] 위에냉장고 위에 상자가 있어요.There is a box on top of the refrigerator.
[장소] 안에방 안에 짐이 많아요.There is a lot of luggage inside the room.
[장소] 옆에부엌 옆에 화장실이 있어요.The bathroom is next to the kitchen.
[장소] 앞/뒤에문 앞에 신발이 있어요.There are shoes in front of the door.

Review: polite requests with -아/어 주세요

  • 보일러 좀 고쳐 주세요. Please fix the boiler. softening 좀 + -아/어 주세요 (recycled from earlier chapters)
  • 창문을 확인해 주시겠어요? Could you check the window? softened request with -아/어 주시겠어요

Review: polite complaints with softening

A direct complaint can sound abrupt. Softening it with an opener such as 죄송하지만 ("I'm sorry, but…") or 그런데 ("by the way…") keeps the tone polite while still stating the problem clearly.

  • 죄송하지만, 보일러가 안 돼요. I'm sorry, but the boiler isn't working. softening opener + descriptive statement
  • 그런데 창문이 잘 안 닫혀요. By the way, the window doesn't close well. softening opener + negation 안

Grammar Note — Why no new system appears here

The request pattern -아/어 주세요, the softening particle 좀, and everyday polite speech level were all introduced in earlier chapters. This chapter's task is to apply them to a new communicative domain — housing and daily problems — rather than to learn new grammar. Learners should recognize familiar tools being reused for a new purpose.

Model Dialogue

Setting: 라일라 calls her landlord, 김 선생님, to report a problem with the boiler and a noisy neighbor.

김 선생님 여보세요, 무슨 일이세요? Hello, what's the matter?
라일라 안녕하세요, 선생님. 죄송하지만, 보일러가 안 돼요. Hello. I'm sorry, but the boiler isn't working.
김 선생님 네? 다시 한번 말씀해 주시겠어요? Sorry? Could you say that one more time?
라일라 보일러가 고장 났어요. 그리고 창문도 잘 안 닫혀요. The boiler has broken down. And the window doesn't close well either.
김 선생님 아, 알겠어요. 창문이 어디에 있어요? Oh, I see. Where is the window?
라일라 부엌 옆에 있어요. 그런데 이웃도 너무 시끄러워요. It's next to the kitchen. And also, the neighbor is too noisy.
김 선생님 소음 문제도 확인해 볼게요. 보일러하고 창문은 내일 고쳐 주세요… 아, 제가 고쳐 드릴게요. I'll look into the noise problem too. I'll get the boiler and window fixed tomorrow.
라일라 네, 감사합니다. 잘 부탁드려요. Yes, thank you. I appreciate it.

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task is a short message reporting a household problem and requesting help: 문제 보고 메시지 ("A Problem Report Message").

  1. Open politely. Greet the reader and identify yourself.
  2. State the problem. Use 고장 나다, 안 되다, or a descriptive verb, softened with 죄송하지만 or 그런데.
  3. Locate the problem. Use a position noun to say where it is.
  4. Make a request. Use -아/어 주세요 or -아/어 주시겠어요.
  5. Close politely. Thank the reader.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • The message names at least one specific household problem.
  • At least one softening opener (죄송하지만 or 그런데) is used.
  • At least one position noun (위/아래/안/밖/옆/앞/뒤) is used correctly.
  • At least one polite request (-아/어 주세요 or -아/어 주시겠어요) is used.
  • Speech level is consistent everyday polite -아요/어요 throughout.
  • Only Hangeul is used; no romanization appears in the final draft.
  • No grammar, particle, or vocabulary item beyond Chapter 6 appears in the text.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Reporting problems to a landlord

In many South Korean rental arrangements, tenants commonly contact the landlord (집주인) directly by phone or message for repairs, especially in smaller residential buildings. Softening a complaint with an opener such as 죄송하지만 is a common and expected politeness strategy in this kind of interaction, though exact norms vary by building, landlord, and relationship.

Culture in Context — Noise between neighbors

Noise between apartment units, particularly footstep noise from units above, is a widely discussed topic in South Korean apartment living and is sometimes referred to using the specific term 층간소음 ("inter-floor noise"). This chapter uses the simpler general word 소음 ("noise"); learners who want the more specific term may encounter it later as receptive vocabulary.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Complaining without softening

Error: 보일러가 고장 났어요. 고쳐 주세요. stated with no softening opener in a first contact with a landlord or service provider.

Correction: Add a softening opener, as in 죄송하지만, 보일러가 고장 났어요. 좀 고쳐 주시겠어요?, to keep the request polite.

Common Mistake — Confusing 안 and 못 with 안 되다

Error: Using 못 돼요 to describe a broken appliance.

Correction: Use 안 돼요 ("it doesn't work" / "it's not working") for a malfunctioning object. 못 describes inability tied to a person, not a broken machine.

Common Mistake — Overloading one sentence with position nouns

Error: 부엌 옆에 안에 위에 냉장고가 있어요. (stacking multiple position nouns without a clear structure)

Correction: Use one position noun per location phrase: 부엌 옆에 냉장고가 있어요. Add a second sentence if more detail is needed.
