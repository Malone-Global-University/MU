문제 설명과 조언Explaining Problems and Giving Advice

Describing a problem with background information, giving and receiving advice, and softening requests · Speak and Write Korean: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use the connector -는데 to give background information before explaining a problem, making a request, or asking a question.
  2. Give advice using -는 게 어때요? and -는 게 좋을 것 같아요, recycling the opinion pattern -는 것 같다 from Chapter 17.
  3. Soften a request using 좀 together with -아/어 줄 수 있어요?
  4. Make an indirect request using 혹시 ~아/어도 될까요? to ask permission politely rather than stating a demand directly.
  5. Explain a problem in a connected way, combining background information, the problem itself, and a request or question about it.
  6. Respond to someone else's problem with sympathy, a suggestion, or encouragement.
  7. Maintain consistent everyday polite -아요/어요 speech level and accurate Hangeul spelling while explaining problems and giving advice.

This chapter continues Unit IV, 경험, 의견, 그리고 프로젝트 (Experiences, Opinions, and Projects). It introduces the connector -는데 for the first time and expands advice-giving and softened, indirect request language, building on the goal and reasoning language from Chapter 18.

Communication Scenario

라일라 has run into a scheduling problem with the exercise plan she and 지영 agreed on in Chapter 18. She wants to explain the situation, ask for a small change, and hear 지영's advice. As they talk, 라일라 practices explaining background before stating her problem, and both friends practice giving gentle, softened advice.

  1. This chapter recycles goal, decision, and reasoning language from Chapter 18, along with opinion and contrast language from Chapter 17.
  2. This chapter introduces the connector -는데 for setting up background information before a problem, question, or request.
  3. This chapter also activates advice-giving and softened, indirect request language as its two other principal systems.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter recycles previously introduced sound changes and adds no new sound-change rule. Two items deserve attention because they appear frequently in this chapter's new connector and softened requests.

Pronunciation Note — 는데 and rising, open intonation

When -는데 sets up background information before a problem or request, speakers typically use a slight rise in pitch on 데 and a brief pause afterward, signaling that more is coming. Listen for this open, unfinished-sounding intonation in 약속이 있는데... compared with a sentence that simply ends.

Pronunciation Note — 혹시 and softened requests

혹시 is usually spoken with a gentle, slightly quieter tone at the start of a sentence, since it signals that a hesitant or polite question is coming. Compare the more direct 이거 바꿔 줄 수 있어요? with the softer 혹시 이거 좀 바꿔 줄 수 있어요?, and notice how 혹시 and 좀 work together to soften the tone.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces one new connector for background information and expands advice-giving and softened, indirect requests, connecting all three to the goal and reasoning language from Chapter 18.

1. Background Information: -는데

Grammar Note — Form, Meaning, and Use

Form: action verb stem + -는데; descriptive verb stem + -(으)ㄴ데; 이다/아니다 stem + -인데. This chapter activates -는데 mainly with action verbs and the copula. -는데 attaches directly to the stem and does not change according to speech level; the speech level appears on the sentence that follows.

Meaning: -는데 introduces background, context, or a mild contrast that sets up the next part of the sentence or conversation. It signals "here is some information — and here is what follows from it," without stating a strong logical connection like reason or condition.

Use: use -는데 before explaining a problem, making a request, or asking a question, so that the listener has context before the main point. -는데 is softer and more conversational than -아/어서 or -(으)니까 from earlier chapters, which state a reason more directly.

  • 내일 약속이 있는데, 시간을 바꿀 수 있어요? I have an appointment tomorrow — could we change the time? 있다 stem + 는데 (background) + request
  • 요즘 일이 많은데, 그래서 좀 힘들어요. I have a lot of work these days, so I've been a bit tired. 많다 stem + 은데 (background, mild contrast)
  • 저는 지영 씨인데, 라일라 씨 있어요? This is Jiyoung — is Laila there? 이다 stem + 인데 (identifying background before a question)
Common Mistake — Using -는데 as if it means "because"

Error: Treating -는데 as equivalent to -아/어서 or -(으)니까, and expecting it to state a direct reason.

Correction: -는데 sets up background or a mild contrast; it does not by itself claim that one thing causes another. Use -아/어서 or -(으)니까 when you want to state a reason directly, and use -는데 when you simply want to give context before your main point.

2. Giving Advice: -는 게 어때요? and -는 게 좋을 것 같아요

Grammar Note — Form, Meaning, and Use

Form: action verb stem + -는 게 어때요? or action verb stem + -는 게 좋을 것 같아요. 게 is a contracted form of 것이 ("the thing of ~ing"), so both patterns literally suggest that "doing ~" is a good idea. -는 것 같다 was introduced in Chapter 17 as an opinion pattern; this chapter connects it directly to advice.

Meaning: -는 게 어때요? invites the listener to consider a suggestion, similar to "how about ~?" -는 게 좋을 것 같아요 offers a slightly more confident recommendation, similar to "I think it would be good to ~."

Use: use -는 게 어때요? when you want to propose an option in a friendly, open way. Use -는 게 좋을 것 같아요 when you want to give a clearer recommendation while still softening it as an opinion rather than a command.

Situation-는 게 어때요? (open suggestion)-는 게 좋을 것 같아요 (clearer recommendation)

3. Softened and Indirect Requests: 좀 ~아/어 줄 수 있어요? and 혹시 ~아/어도 될까요?

Grammar Note — Form, Meaning, and Use

Form: [action] + 좀 ~아/어 줄 수 있어요? asks whether someone can do something for you, softened with 좀. 혹시 ~아/어도 될까요? asks permission to do something yourself, softened with 혹시 ("by any chance") and -아/어도 되다 ("it's okay to ~").

Meaning: both patterns turn a direct request or demand into a gentler question, giving the listener room to say no comfortably. 좀 ~아/어 줄 수 있어요? asks for help; 혹시 ~아/어도 될까요? asks for permission.

Use: use 좀 ~아/어 줄 수 있어요? when asking someone to do something for you, such as changing a time or explaining something again. Use 혹시 ~아/어도 될까요? when asking whether you may do something yourself, such as arriving late or changing your own plan.

  • 죄송한데, 약속 시간 좀 바꿔 줄 수 있어요? I'm sorry, but could you change the appointment time for me? 바꾸다 stem + 어 줄 수 있어요? (softened with 좀)
  • 혹시 다음 주에 만나도 될까요? Would it be okay if we met next week instead, by any chance? 만나다 stem + 아도 될까요? (softened permission question)
Building Block — Background, problem, and softened request

[background -는데] + [problem statement] + [혹시 or 좀 + softened request]

A natural way to bring up a problem gives context first, states the problem, and then asks for a small change or piece of advice, rather than stating a demand right away.

이번 주에 일이 너무 많은데, 그래서 운동할 시간이 없어요. 혹시 이번 주는 좀 쉬어도 될까요?

I have too much work this week, so I don't have time to exercise. Would it be okay if I rested this week, by any chance?

Practice — Combining the three systems

  1. Write one sentence giving background information using -는데.
  2. Write one sentence giving advice using -는 게 어때요? or -는 게 좋을 것 같아요.
  3. Write one sentence making a softened or indirect request using 좀 ~아/어 줄 수 있어요? or 혹시 ~아/어도 될까요?

Model Dialogue

Setting: 라일라 calls 지영 about the morning exercise plan they agreed on in Chapter 18.

라일라 지영 씨, 잠깐 통화 괜찮아요? 사실 문제가 좀 생겼어요. Jiyoung, is now an okay time to talk? Actually, a bit of a problem has come up.
지영 네, 괜찮아요. 무슨 일이에요? Sure, that's fine. What's going on?
라일라 이번 주에 회사 일이 너무 많은데, 그래서 아침 일찍 일어나기가 너무 힘들어요. I have too much work at the office this week, so it's really hard for me to get up early in the morning.
지영 그러게요, 요즘 스트레스 많이 받겠어요. 어떻게 하고 싶어요? I hear you, you must be under a lot of stress these days. What would you like to do?
라일라 혹시 이번 주만 저녁으로 시간을 바꿔도 될까요? Would it be okay if we changed the time to evening, just for this week?
지영 그럼요, 이해해요. 저도 가끔 아침에 너무 피곤하거든요. Of course, I understand. I get too tired in the mornings sometimes too, you see.
라일라 고마워요. 그런데 다음 주부터는 다시 아침에 하는 게 좋을 것 같아요. Thanks. But I think it would be good to go back to mornings starting next week.
지영 좋아요. 그리고 이번 주는 너무 무리하지 말고 천천히 하는 게 어때요? Sounds good. And how about not pushing yourself too hard this week, and taking it slowly?
라일라 네, 그렇게 할게요. 이해해 줘서 고마워요. Okay, I'll do that. Thanks for understanding.

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task is a short problem-and-request message: 문제가 생겼어요 ("A Problem Has Come Up").

  1. Give background. Write one sentence using -는데 to explain your situation.
  2. State the problem. Write one sentence clearly stating the problem that has come up.
  3. Make a softened request. Use 좀 ~아/어 줄 수 있어요? or 혹시 ~아/어도 될까요? to ask for a small change or favor.
  4. Add advice for yourself. Write one sentence giving yourself advice using -는 게 좋을 것 같아요.
  5. Close politely. End with a thank-you or an expression of understanding, recycling language from earlier chapters.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • Background information is given using -는데.
  • The problem is stated clearly.
  • A softened or indirect request is made using 좀 ~아/어 줄 수 있어요? or 혹시 ~아/어도 될까요?
  • Advice is given using -는 게 어때요? or -는 게 좋을 것 같아요.
  • The message closes politely.
  • Speech level is consistent everyday polite -아요/어요 throughout.
  • Only Hangeul is used; no romanization appears in the final draft.
  • No grammar or vocabulary beyond Chapters 1 through 19 appears productively.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Softening requests among friends and colleagues

Directly stating a demand can feel abrupt in many everyday South Korean interactions, especially among people who are not extremely close. Giving background with -는데 before a request, and softening the request itself with 좀 or 혹시, is a common and expected way to ask for a small change, favor, or exception without seeming pushy.

Culture in Context — Responding with 그러게요 and understanding

Responding to a friend's problem with 그러게요 ("that's true," "I know") shows sympathy and agreement before offering advice or granting a request. Expressing understanding first, as 지영 does in this chapter's dialogue, is generally seen as a considerate way to respond before making a suggestion.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Stating a request without any background

Error: Beginning directly with 시간 바꿔 줄 수 있어요? without any context, which can sound abrupt.

Correction: Give background first with -는데, such as 이번 주에 일이 많은데, 시간 좀 바꿔 줄 수 있어요?, so the listener understands your situation before you ask.

Common Mistake — Confusing 어때요? and 될까요?

Error: Using -는 게 어때요? when asking for permission for your own action, such as 제가 늦게 가는 게 어때요?

Correction: -는 게 어때요? proposes advice to someone else; use -아/어도 될까요? to ask permission for your own action: 제가 늦게 가도 될까요?

Common Mistake — Overusing 혹시 until it loses its softening effect

Error: Placing 혹시 in every single sentence of a conversation, which can sound repetitive rather than polite.

Correction: Reserve 혹시 for the specific moment you are softening a request or question, and let surrounding sentences stand on their own.
