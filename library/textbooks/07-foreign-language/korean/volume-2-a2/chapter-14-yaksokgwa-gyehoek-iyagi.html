약속과 계획 이야기Making Plans with Others

Suggestions, invitations, and scheduling language · Speak and Write Korean: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Make a suggestion using -(으)ㄹ까요?, such as 같이 저녁 먹을까요?.
  2. Invite someone to an activity and accept or decline the invitation politely.
  3. Ask about and state availability using 시간이 있어요/없어요 and 다른 약속이 있어요.
  4. Set a day, time, and place for a plan using scheduling vocabulary.
  5. Continue explaining feelings and reasons with -아서/어서 from Chapter 13.
  6. Continue using comparison with 보다/더 and noun-modifying descriptive forms from Chapters 11 and 12.
  7. Understand a short conversation and a short written message about making and confirming plans.
  8. Write a short message proposing a plan and responding to an invitation.
  9. Complete a practical task: making a plan with a friend, including a suggestion, a response, and a confirmed time and place.

Communication Scenario

Now that 라일라's presentation is over, 지영 suggests they celebrate together before her older brother arrives from abroad. 지영 proposes a day and place, 라일라 checks her schedule, and together they settle on a time. This chapter closes Unit III by bringing together everything learners have practiced about people, relationships, and feelings into the practical task of making a real plan.

  1. This chapter is the final chapter of Unit III (사람과 관계 — People and Relationships), following Chapters 11-13.
  2. New productive systems in this chapter are limited to suggestions with -(으)ㄹ까요?, invitations, accepting and declining, and scheduling vocabulary for days, times, and places.
  3. Kinship terms, comparison with 보다/더, closeness vocabulary, noun-modifying descriptive forms, emotion vocabulary, and the reason connector -아서/어서 continue to be used throughout.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter introduces no new sound-change rule. It reviews liaison in 약속을 and 시간이, where the final consonant of the noun connects smoothly to the following particle, and reviews the rise in intonation used with question endings such as -(으)ㄹ까요?.

Pronunciation Note — Intonation with -(으)ㄹ까요?

-(으)ㄹ까요? is a question ending and should rise gently at the end, similar to other polite questions you have already practiced. A flat, falling intonation can make a genuine suggestion sound like a statement.

먹을까요? · 만날까요? · 갈까요? · 약속을 · 시간이

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces the suggestion ending -(으)ㄹ까요?, invitation language, and scheduling vocabulary. It reviews the reason connector -아서/어서, comparison with 보다/더, and noun-modifying descriptive forms from earlier chapters in this unit.

New: making a suggestion with -(으)ㄹ까요?

-(으)ㄹ까요? attaches to a verb stem to propose an activity together or to ask what the listener would like to do. Stems ending in a vowel or ㄹ take -ㄹ까요?; stems ending in most other consonants take -을까요?.

Dictionary FormStem Type-(으)ㄹ까요? Form
가다vowel-final갈까요?
먹다consonant-final먹을까요?
만나다vowel-final만날까요?

Only this suggestion form is activated productively this chapter. Other modal or proposal endings remain for a later chapter.

Building Block

[activity] + -(으)ㄹ까요?

This pattern proposes doing something together or asks what the listener prefers. It is usually answered with acceptance, a counter-suggestion, or a polite decline.

이번 주말에 같이 저녁 먹을까요?

Shall we eat dinner together this weekend?

New: accepting and declining an invitation

  • 네, 좋은 생각이에요. Yes, that's a good idea. accepting a suggestion
  • 미안하지만 그날은 다른 약속이 있어요. I'm sorry, but I have another appointment that day. softener + reason for declining
  • 그럼 다음에 만날까요? Then shall we meet another time? counter-suggestion using -(으)ㄹ까요?

New: setting a day, time, and place

  • 토요일 저녁 여섯 시 어때요? How about Saturday evening at six? day + time + 어때요?, recycled from earlier chapters
  • 학교 앞 식당에서 만나요. Let's meet at the restaurant in front of school. location + 에서 만나요

Review: reason clauses with -아서/어서

  • 발표가 끝나서 기뻐요. 그래서 같이 축하하고 싶어요. I'm glad the presentation is over, so I want to celebrate together. recycled -아서/어서 and 기쁘다 from Chapter 13

Review: comparison with 보다/더 and noun-modifying forms

  • 평일보다 주말이 더 좋아요. The weekend is better than a weekday. recycled 보다/더 from Chapter 12
  • 바쁜 이번 주가 끝나서 좋아요. I'm glad this busy week is over. recycled -(으)ㄴ noun-modifying form from Chapter 11

Model Dialogue

Setting: 지영 calls 라일라 the day after her presentation to suggest celebrating.

지영 라일라 씨, 발표가 끝나서 정말 기쁘겠어요. 이번 주말에 같이 저녁 먹을까요? Laila, you must be so glad the presentation is over. Shall we eat dinner together this weekend?
라일라 네, 좋은 생각이에요! 그런데 토요일에는 다른 약속이 있어요. 일요일은 어때요? Yes, that's a good idea! But I have another appointment on Saturday. How about Sunday?
지영 일요일 좋아요. 일요일 저녁 여섯 시 어때요? Sunday works. How about Sunday evening at six?
라일라 좋아요. 어디에서 만날까요? Sounds good. Where shall we meet?
지영 학교 앞 식당에서 만나요. 그 식당이 우리 집보다 더 가까워요. Let's meet at the restaurant in front of school. That restaurant is closer than my house.
라일라 네, 그럼 일요일 저녁 여섯 시에 학교 앞 식당에서 만나요. 벌써 신나요! Okay, then let's meet at the restaurant in front of school on Sunday evening at six. I'm already excited!

  2. Why does 라일라 decline Saturday, and what does she propose instead?
  3. Where and when do they finally agree to meet?

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task is a short message proposing a plan and responding to an invitation: 약속 정하기 ("Setting Up a Plan").

  1. Suggest an activity. Use -(으)ㄹ까요? to propose doing something together.
  2. Respond to the suggestion. Accept with 좋은 생각이에요, or decline politely with 미안하지만 and a reason.
  3. Propose a day and time. Use scheduling vocabulary and 어때요? to settle on a specific day and time.
  4. Propose a place. Use 에서 만나요 to state where you will meet.
  5. Add a feeling and a comparison. Include one feeling explained with -아서/어서 and one comparison sentence with 보다/더.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • A suggestion is made using -(으)ㄹ까요?.
  • A response accepts or politely declines the suggestion.
  • A specific day, time, and place are proposed and confirmed.
  • At least one feeling is explained with -아서/어서.
  • At least one comparison sentence with 보다/더 is used correctly.
  • Speech level is consistent everyday polite -아요/어요 throughout.
  • Only Hangeul is used; no romanization appears in the final draft.
  • No grammar or vocabulary beyond Chapter 14 appears productively.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Softening a decline

Directly refusing an invitation without a softening word can sound abrupt in many everyday South Korean interactions. Beginning a decline with 미안하지만 and offering a brief reason or a counter-suggestion, such as 다음에 만날까요?, is a common and considerate way to decline while keeping the relationship warm.

Culture in Context — Confirming plans clearly

Among friends and classmates, it is common to confirm the exact day, time, and place before ending a conversation about plans, even in casual settings. This chapter's dialogue reflects that everyday habit of clear confirmation rather than leaving details vague.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using -(으)ㄹ까요? for a statement of fact

Error: Using -(으)ㄹ까요? to describe something that will definitely happen, such as predicting the weather without inviting a shared decision.

Correction: -(으)ㄹ까요? is for suggestions and shared decisions, as in 같이 갈까요? ("Shall we go together?"), not for simple predictions.

Common Mistake — Declining without softening

Error: Saying only 안 돼요. ("No, I can't.") when declining an invitation.

Correction: Soften the decline and give a reason: 미안하지만 그날은 다른 약속이 있어요.

Common Mistake — Forgetting to confirm the place

Error: Agreeing on a day and time but leaving the place unstated, such as saying only 일요일에 만나요. without a location.

Correction: Confirm all three details when possible: 일요일 저녁 여섯 시에 학교 앞 식당에서 만나요.

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. 지영 suggests eating dinner together on the weekend because she is glad 라일라's presentation is over.
  2. 라일라 declines Saturday because she has another appointment, and proposes Sunday instead.
  3. They agree to meet on Sunday evening at six at the restaurant in front of school.

Listening comprehension

  • The speaker chose Sunday because Saturday already had another appointment.
  • They will meet on Sunday evening at six at the restaurant in front of school.
  • The speaker already feels good about the plan.

Reading comprehension

  1. 라일라 feels glad because her presentation is over.
  2. They chose Sunday because 라일라 had another appointment on Saturday.
  3. 라일라 likes the restaurant because it is closer to 지영's house than her own.
