Unit III — 사람과 관계 (People and Relationships)
11

새로운 사람을 만나요Meeting New People

Describing people, first impressions, and everyday relationships · Speak and Write Korean: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe a person's appearance, personality, and job using noun-modifying descriptive forms such as 친절한 사람 and 키가 큰 사람.
  2. Ask about and explain how you know someone, using -아/어서 알게 되다 and related expressions.
  3. Introduce one person to another politely, using appropriate titles and address terms.
  4. Talk about ongoing relationships and feelings using vocabulary such as 친해지다, 편하다, and 어색하다.
  5. Use the noun-modifying form of descriptive verbs (-(으)ㄴ) productively in simple sentences.
  6. Understand a short conversation and a short written description introducing new people.
  7. Write a short paragraph introducing a new acquaintance.
  8. Complete a practical task: describing and introducing someone at a small gathering.

Communication Scenario

A few days after her busy day of errands, 라일라 goes with 지영 to a small gathering at 지영's apartment. 지영 wants to introduce 라일라 to two friends from her Korean class: 하준, a quiet but friendly graduate student, and 수아, an outgoing coworker. Over the course of the evening, 라일라 learns a little about each person and begins forming her own first impressions.

  1. This chapter opens Unit III, which shifts from independent daily-living tasks to describing people, relationships, and feelings.
  2. New productive systems in this chapter are limited to noun-modifying descriptive forms and basic relationship vocabulary.
  3. Everyday polite speech level, established particles, and past-tense narration from Units I and II continue to be used throughout.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter introduces no new sound-change rule. It reviews the tense consonant in 활발하다 and the liaison that occurs when a noun-modifying ending connects smoothly to the following noun, as in 친절한 사람 and 키가 큰 사람.

Pronunciation Note — Linking modifiers smoothly to nouns

When a descriptive verb ending in -(으)ㄴ is followed by a noun, read the two words as one connected phrase rather than pausing between them. Practice the phrases below at a natural pace.

친절한 사람 · 키가 큰 사람 · 조용한 사람 · 활발한 친구

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces the noun-modifying form of descriptive verbs, which allows learners to describe a person directly before naming them, and reviews -아/어서 to explain how a relationship began.

New: noun-modifying descriptive forms -(으)ㄴ

Korean descriptive verbs can attach directly before a noun using the ending -(으)ㄴ. This lets you describe a person or thing in the same phrase that names them, rather than using two separate sentences.

Dictionary FormModifier FormExample
친절하다친절한친절한 사람이에요.
조용하다조용한조용한 친구예요.
크다큰키가 큰 사람이에요.
작다작은키가 작은 사람이에요.

Only these four descriptive verbs are activated productively in this chapter. Other descriptive verbs remain receptive until a later chapter schedules them.

Building Block

[descriptive verb stem]-(으)ㄴ + [noun]

The modifier form replaces the dictionary-form ending -다 with -(으)ㄴ and attaches directly to the following noun, with no particle in between.

하준 씨는 조용한 사람이에요.

Hajun is a quiet person.

Review: explaining how you met someone with -아/어서 알게 되다

  • 한국어 수업에서 만나서 알게 됐어요. I met them in Korean class and got to know them. recycled -아/어서 (Ch. 3) + 알게 되다

New: closeness and comfort vocabulary in simple sentences

  • 아직 좀 어색해요. It's still a little awkward. 어색하다, everyday polite
  • 이제 훨씬 편해요. Now it's much more comfortable. 편하다, everyday polite

Practice — Building modifier phrases

  1. Combine 친절하다 with 사람 into a noun-modifying phrase and use it in a sentence.
  2. Combine 키가 크다 with 친구 into a noun-modifying phrase and use it in a sentence.
  3. Write one sentence explaining how you got to know a friend, using -아/어서 알게 되다.

Model Dialogue

Setting: At 지영's apartment, 지영 introduces 라일라 to 하준.

지영 라일라 씨, 이쪽은 하준 씨예요. 한국어 수업에서 만나서 알게 됐어요. Laila, this is Hajun. We met in Korean class and got to know each other.
라일라 안녕하세요, 하준 씨. 만나서 반가워요. Hello, Hajun. Nice to meet you.
하준 안녕하세요, 라일라 씨. 저도 만나서 반가워요. Hello, Laila. I'm glad to meet you too.
지영 하준 씨는 대학원생이에요. 그리고 정말 조용한 사람이에요. Hajun is a graduate student. And he's a really quiet person.
라일라 아, 그래요? 저기 있는 활발한 분은 누구예요? Oh, really? Who's that outgoing person over there?
지영 아, 수아 씨예요. 제 직장 동료예요. 성격이 아주 활발해요. Oh, that's Sua. She's my coworker. Her personality is very lively.
라일라 저는 새로운 사람을 만나면 아직 좀 어색해요. When I meet new people, it's still a little awkward for me.
하준 저도 그래요. 그런데 지영 씨 친구들은 다 친절해서 금방 편해질 거예요. Me too. But Jiyoung's friends are all kind, so you'll get comfortable soon.

내용 이해 — Comprehension questions:

  1. How did 지영 and 하준 first get to know each other?
  2. What is 하준's job, and how does 지영 describe his personality?
  3. Who is 수아, and how is she described?

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task is a short paragraph introducing a new acquaintance: 새로운 사람 소개하기 ("Introducing a New Person").

  1. Name the person and how you met. State the person's name and use -아/어서 알게 되다 to explain how you got to know them.
  2. State the person's occupation. Use a simple copular sentence.
  3. Describe the person. Use at least one noun-modifying descriptive phrase, such as 친절한 사람 or 키가 큰 사람.
  4. Describe your first impression. Say whether you felt 어색하다 or 편하다 at first.
  5. Describe how you feel now. Say whether your feelings have changed.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • The person's name and occupation are stated clearly.
  • At least one noun-modifying descriptive phrase is used correctly.
  • -아/어서 알게 되다 is used to explain how you met.
  • First impression and current feeling are both described.
  • Speech level is consistent everyday polite -아요/어요 throughout.
  • Only Hangeul is used; no romanization appears in the final draft.
  • No grammar or vocabulary beyond Chapter 11 appears productively.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Introducing people politely

When introducing two people in South Korea, it is common to state each person's name with 씨 or an appropriate title, briefly mention how you know them or what they do, and let both people exchange a polite greeting such as 만나서 반가워요. This chapter's dialogue and writing tasks follow that same pattern.

Culture in Context — First impressions and relationship pacing

It is common and socially accepted in many South Korean social settings for new acquaintances to feel some initial awkwardness, described as 어색하다, before becoming comfortable, described as 편하다. Expressing this honestly, as 라일라 does, is natural rather than impolite.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using the dictionary form as a modifier

Error: Placing the dictionary form directly before a noun, such as 친절하다 사람.

Correction: Change the descriptive verb to its modifier form before the noun: 친절한 사람.

Common Mistake — Confusing 편하다 and 어색하다 in context

Error: Describing a first meeting as 편했어요 when the intended meaning was that it felt awkward.

Correction: Use 어색했어요 for an awkward first impression and reserve 편했어요 for describing comfort, often after some time has passed.

Common Mistake — Forgetting the particle before 씨 in introductions

Error: Introducing someone without pairing their name consistently with 씨, which can sound abrupt in this context.

Correction: Keep names paired with 씨 throughout an introduction, as in 이쪽은 하준 씨예요.
