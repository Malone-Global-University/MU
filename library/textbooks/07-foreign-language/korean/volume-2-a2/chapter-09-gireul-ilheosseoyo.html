길을 잃었어요I'm Lost: Directions and Problem-Solving

Describing a problem clearly and asking for and giving detailed directions · Speak and Write Korean: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State that you are lost or confused about a location, using -길을 잃다 and related expressions.
  2. Ask a stranger for help politely, using softened openers such as 저기요 and 실례합니다.
  3. Ask for and understand detailed directions using landmarks, turns, and distance expressions.
  4. Use sequence connectors to follow multi-step directions, such as -아/어서 in its sequential sense and -고 나서.
  5. Describe the location of a place relative to a landmark using expanded location language (근처, 맞은편, 건너편, 사거리).
  6. Confirm and repeat back directions to check understanding.
  7. Read and listen to short exchanges about being lost and receiving directions.
  8. Write a short message explaining that you got lost and describing how you eventually found the place.

Communication Scenario

라일라's bank account is open and her package is on its way home, but her next errand does not go as smoothly. She has arranged to meet a classmate at a small bookstore she has never visited, and after leaving the subway station she quickly realizes she has no idea which direction to walk. This chapter gives 라일라 the language to explain that she is lost, ask a stranger for help without sounding abrupt, follow directions that involve landmarks and turns, and confirm that she has understood correctly before setting off again.

  1. This chapter introduces problem-description language (길을 잃다 and related expressions), softened openers for approaching strangers, and detailed direction-giving language built from landmarks, turns, and distance.
  2. Location particles 에/에서, the request pattern -아/어 주세요, and everyday polite speech level continue from Chapters 1 through 8.
  3. Assessment in this chapter focuses on describing a problem, asking for directions, and confirming understanding clearly and politely.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter reinforces liaison across particle boundaries in location phrases, such as 횡단보도에서 and 신호등 앞에서, where a final consonant links smoothly to the following vowel-initial particle. It also reviews the batchim behavior of 잃다 in its past form. It introduces no new sound-change rule.

Pronunciation Note — 잃다 and its past form

잃다 has a double batchim (ㅀ) that simplifies before a consonant-initial ending but keeps its ㅎ quality before a vowel-initial ending, giving the past form 잃었어요 a smooth, clearly aspirated glide from the stem into the ending. Say the phrase slowly at first — 길을 잃었어요 — keeping the object particle 을 firmly attached to 길 before moving to the verb.

길을 잃었어요 · 횡단보도에서 건너세요 · 사거리에서 오른쪽으로 가세요

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter activates two new productive systems: softened openers and problem-description language for approaching a stranger, and detailed direction-giving language built from sequence connectors, turn/location vocabulary, and a confirmation pattern. Requests, location particles, and everyday polite speech level continue from earlier chapters.

New: describing a problem and asking for help

To tell someone you are lost, use 길을 잃었어요. To approach a stranger politely before asking a question, open with 저기요 or the more formal 실례합니다, then use 여쭤봐도 돼요? or a similar softened frame before your actual question.

  • 저기요, 길을 잃었는데 좀 여쭤봐도 돼요? Excuse me, I'm lost — could I ask you something? 저기요 + 길을 잃다 (formulaic -는데 frame, glossed) + 여쭤봐도 돼요?
  • 실례합니다. 이 근처에 서점이 있어요? Excuse me. Is there a bookstore near here? 실례합니다 + 근처 + 있어요?

The connector -는데 appears above only as a glossed, formulaic frame around 저기요 and 길을 잃다. It is not an independent productive target of this chapter and will be developed in a later chapter.

New: giving detailed directions with sequence and turns

Direction-giving strings together short action phrases in order. Use -고 나서 ("after doing...") to link one direction step clearly to the next, and use 왼쪽/오른쪽 with 으로 to say which way to turn.

PatternExampleGloss
이 길로 쭉 가세요.사거리까지 이 길로 쭉 가세요.Go straight along this road to the intersection.
-고 나서 [action]횡단보도를 건너고 나서 오른쪽으로 도세요.After crossing the crosswalk, turn right.
[landmark] 맞은편/근처에 있다서점은 은행 맞은편에 있어요.The bookstore is across from the bank.
Building Block

[action]-고 나서 + [next action]

-고 나서 attaches directly to a verb stem and marks that the first action is completed before the second begins, making it useful for ordering multi-step directions.

신호등에서 왼쪽으로 돌고 나서 쭉 가세요.

Turn left at the traffic light, and then go straight.

New: confirming directions with 그러니까 and -(으)면 되다

To check that you understood correctly, restate the directions using 그러니까 ("so...") followed by a summary, and confirm sufficiency with -(으)면 되다 ("it's fine if...").

  • 그러니까 사거리에서 오른쪽으로 가면 돼요? So, all I have to do is turn right at the intersection? 그러니까 + 가다 + -(으)면 되다
  • 네, 그렇게 하시면 돼요. Yes, that's right, just do that. recognition-only 시- honorific in staff/stranger speech, glossed
Grammar Note — 저기요 versus 실례합니다

Both openers get a stranger's attention politely, but 저기요 is common and casual-neutral in everyday settings such as the street or a shop, while 실례합니다 is more formal and often preferred when approaching someone who looks busy, elderly, or in a more formal context. Either is acceptable for asking a passerby for directions; this chapter treats them as interchangeable openers.

Review: location particles 에/에서 and -(으)로

  • 사거리에서 오른쪽으로 가세요. Turn right at the intersection. 에서 (action location) + 으로 (direction), recycled from earlier chapters
  • 서점은 은행 근처에 있어요. The bookstore is near the bank. 에 (existence location), recycled

Review: polite requests with -아/어 주세요

  • 다시 한번 말씀해 주세요. Please say that one more time. recycled from Chapters 6-8

Practice — Building problem and direction sentences

  1. Open politely and say that you are lost.
  2. Ask whether a place is nearby using 근처 or 맞은편.
  3. Give one direction using -고 나서 to link two steps.
  4. Confirm the directions using 그러니까 and -(으)면 되다.

Model Dialogue

Setting: 라일라 leaves the subway station and stops a passerby to ask for directions to a bookstore.

라일라 저기요, 길을 잃었는데 좀 여쭤봐도 돼요? Excuse me, I'm lost — could I ask you something?
행인 네, 말씀하세요. Sure, go ahead.
라일라 이 근처에 서점이 있어요? 이름은 '한빛서점'이에요. Is there a bookstore near here? It's called "Hanbit Bookstore."
행인 아, 한빛서점이요? 이 길로 쭉 가세요. 사거리까지 가세요. Oh, Hanbit Bookstore? Go straight along this road. Go to the intersection.
라일라 네, 사거리까지요. Okay, to the intersection.
행인 사거리에서 횡단보도를 건너고 나서 오른쪽으로 도세요. 그러면 은행이 보여요. At the intersection, cross the crosswalk and then turn right. Then you'll see a bank.
라일라 그러니까 횡단보도를 건너고 나서 오른쪽으로 가면 돼요? So, all I have to do is cross the crosswalk and then turn right?
행인 네, 맞아요. 서점은 그 은행 맞은편에 있어요. Yes, that's right. The bookstore is across from that bank.
라일라 여기서 걸어서 얼마나 걸려요? How long does it take from here on foot?
행인 한 오 분쯤 걸려요. It takes about five minutes.
라일라 감사합니다! 정말 도움이 됐어요. Thank you! That really helped.

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task is a short message explaining that you got lost and describing how you found the place: 길을 잃었던 이야기 ("A Story About Getting Lost").

  1. Open with a greeting or apology. Address the reader and acknowledge being late or delayed if relevant.
  2. State the problem. Say that you got lost, using 길을 잃다.
  3. Explain why. Briefly say why the area was unfamiliar or confusing.
  4. Describe asking for help. Say that you asked a passerby for directions.
  5. Give the directions you received. Use -고 나서 to link at least two direction steps.
  6. Say where you ended up. Use 근처 or 맞은편 to describe the final location.
  7. Close politely. Add a closing comment or plan for next time.
  8. Revise. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • The message clearly states that the writer got lost.
  • At least one direction sentence uses -고 나서 correctly.
  • At least one location sentence uses 근처 or 맞은편 correctly.
  • Speech level is consistent everyday polite -아요/어요 throughout (unless intentionally writing to a close friend, in which case casual endings should be used consistently).
  • Only Hangeul is used; no romanization appears in the final draft.
  • No grammar, particle, or vocabulary item beyond Chapter 9 appears productively in the text.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Approaching strangers for help

In South Korea, approaching a stranger on the street to ask for directions is common and generally welcomed, especially when the request is short and polite. Opening with 저기요 or 실례합니다 signals that the interaction will be brief, and thanking the person clearly at the end, as 라일라 does in the Model Dialogue, is expected and appreciated.

Culture in Context — Landmarks over street names

Directions in South Korea are often given using nearby landmarks, such as banks, subway exits, or well-known stores, rather than street names alone, since many smaller streets are not commonly referred to by name in everyday speech. Learning to describe and recognize landmarks such as 사거리, 신호등, and 횡단보도 is often more useful for everyday navigation than memorizing formal addresses.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing 에 and 에서 in direction sentences

Error: 사거리에 오른쪽으로 가세요. using 에 where an action location is intended.

Correction: Use 에서 for the location where an action such as turning happens: 사거리에서 오른쪽으로 가세요. Reserve 에 for existence or destination, as in 서점은 은행 근처에 있어요.

Common Mistake — Approaching a stranger without a softened opener

Error: Asking a direct question, such as 서점이 어디예요?, without any opener, which can sound abrupt to a stranger.

Correction: Begin with 저기요 or 실례합니다 before the question, as in 저기요, 서점이 어디예요?

Common Mistake — Attaching -고 나서 to the wrong element

Error: 횡단보도 건너고 나서 without a clear object particle, leaving the sentence unclear about what is being crossed.

Correction: Keep the object particle attached to the noun before the verb stem: 횡단보도를 건너고 나서 오른쪽으로 도세요.
