습관과 변화Habits and Change

Using frequency words to describe routines, contrasting habitual actions with ongoing actions using -고 있다, and expanding descriptive predicates · Speak and Write Korean: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use frequency adverbs such as 항상, 보통, 자주, 가끔, and 거의 안 to describe how often you or someone else does something.
  2. Distinguish a habitual routine described with everyday polite -아요/어요 from an action that is happening right now, described with -고 있다.
  3. Form and use -고 있다 accurately with common action verbs.
  4. Describe a change over time using contrast connectors and expanded descriptive predicates, including 바쁘다, 피곤하다, 건강하다, and 달라지다.
  5. Continue building noun-modifier phrases from Chapter 2 while describing routines and habits.
  6. Read a short connected passage describing someone's weekly routine and a recent change in that routine.
  7. Describe your own routine, compare it to the past, and explain what you are doing differently now.

Communication Scenario

라일라 has settled into a routine at her new school, but her friend 지은 notices that her habits have changed since the semester started — she used to be free most evenings, but now she is often busy with her study group. To explain this clearly, 라일라 needs more than simple present-tense sentences: she needs frequency words to describe how often things happen, and a way to describe what she is doing right now versus what she usually does.

  1. This chapter introduces frequency adverbs and develops the contrast between habitual routines and ongoing actions.
  2. The chapter's two principal new focuses are the -고 있다 ongoing-action pattern and an expanded set of descriptive predicates for describing states, feelings, and change.
  3. Noun-modifier phrases and the possessive particle 의 from Chapter 2 continue to recycle here, now applied to routines and habits.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter does not introduce a new sound-change rule. It develops control of nasalization and liaison across the -고 있다 pattern, and continues developing the ㅡ deletion pattern seen in 바쁘다 → 바빠요.

Pronunciation Note — 있다 in connected speech

있다 and its conjugated forms end in the batchim ㅆ. Before a vowel-initial ending, such as -어요, the ㅆ liaises smoothly into the following syllable: 있어요 is pronounced as one connected unit, not as two separate syllables with a pause. This liaison pattern was introduced in Volume I and continues to apply throughout -고 있어요 forms in this chapter.

저는 지금 숙제하고 있어요 · 요즘 아주 바빠요 · 예전보다 생활이 많이 달라졌어요

Grammar and Sentence Pattern Review

This chapter introduces frequency adverbs, develops the contrast between habitual routines and ongoing actions with -고 있다, and expands descriptive predicates for states, feelings, and change.

New focus: Frequency adverbs

Frequency adverbs normally appear directly before the predicate, after the topic and any time or location phrase, as in 저는 주말에 보통 쉬어요.

New focus: -고 있다 for ongoing actions

Building Block — Ongoing Action

[action verb stem] + -고 있다 + [speech-level ending]

-고 있다 attaches to an action-verb stem and describes an action in progress at the moment of speaking, similar to English "-ing." It contrasts with the everyday polite present -아요/어요, which more often describes habits, facts, or general truths. -고 있다 itself conjugates like 있다: 있어요, 있었어요, and so on.

지금 밥을 먹고 있어요.

I am eating right now. (in progress at this moment)

SentenceMeaningInterpretation
저는 매일 운동해요.I exercise every day.habitual routine
저는 지금 운동하고 있어요.I am exercising right now.ongoing at this moment
동생이 숙제해요.My sibling does homework.general statement/habit
동생이 숙제하고 있어요.My sibling is doing homework (right now).ongoing at this moment

-아요/어요 is not "wrong" for a present action; the difference is one of focus. -아요/어요 emphasizes the habit or general fact, while -고 있다 emphasizes that the action is unfolding right now. Both forms will continue to be used throughout the volume depending on what the speaker wants to emphasize.

  • 저는 보통 일곱 시에 일어나지만, 오늘은 아직 자고 있어요. I usually get up at seven, but today I'm still sleeping.
  • 지은이는 지금 시험을 준비하고 있어요. Jieun is preparing for a test right now.

Expanded focus: Descriptive predicates for states and change

This chapter expands the noun-modifier pattern from Chapter 2 (-은/-ㄴ) with new descriptive verbs describing states, feelings, and regularity.

Dictionary formNoun-modifying formExample

피곤하다피곤한피곤한 사람

바쁘다 follows the ㅡ deletion pattern before -아요/어요: 바쁘 + 아요 → 바빠요, since the stem's final vowel ㅡ is deleted and the preceding vowel determines the ending. This pattern was introduced receptively in Volume I and becomes productive here.

Grammar Note — Describing change with -아/어지다

Adding -아/어지다 to a descriptive verb stem creates an action verb meaning "to become ___." 건강하다 ("to be healthy") becomes 건강해지다 ("to become healthy"), and 달라지다 itself follows this pattern from 다르다 ("to be different"). This chapter previews the pattern through the fixed vocabulary items 달라지다 and 건강해지다; the general rule for forming new -아/어지다 verbs is developed further in a later chapter.

Practice — Frequency, -고 있다, and descriptive predicates

Model Dialogue

Setting: 지은 calls 라일라, who is studying at the library.

지은 라일라 씨, 지금 뭐 하고 있어요? Layla, what are you doing right now?
라일라 도서관에서 시험을 준비하고 있어요. 요즘 아주 바빠요. I'm preparing for a test at the library. I've been really busy lately.
지은 예전에는 저녁마다 시간이 있었잖아요. 습관이 많이 바뀌었네요. You used to have free time every evening, right? Your habits have really changed.
라일라 네, 맞아요. 저는 요즘 거의 안 쉬어요. 그래도 일찍 자려고 노력해요. Yes, that's right. I almost never rest these days. Still, I try to sleep early.
지은 건강이 걱정돼요. 규칙적인 생활이 중요해요. I'm worried about your health. A regular schedule is important.
라일라 맞아요. 시험이 끝나면 다시 운동하고 더 건강해지고 싶어요. You're right. Once the test is over, I want to exercise again and become healthier.

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task is a short paragraph, 제 일상과 변화 ("My Routine and How It Has Changed"), describing your weekly routine and one change in your habits.

  1. Describe your typical routine. Use at least two frequency adverbs (항상, 보통, 자주, 가끔, 거의 안) to describe habitual actions.
  2. Describe something happening right now. Use -고 있다 at least once to describe a current action.
  3. Compare past and present. Use 예전에 and 요즘 with a contrast connector such as -지만 to describe how your habits have changed.
  4. Add a descriptive detail. Use at least one noun-modifier phrase built from an expanded descriptive verb, such as 바쁜 하루 or 건강한 습관.
  5. Close with a goal. Use -고 싶다 or -(으)려고 노력하다 to state something you want to change or are trying to do.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • At least two frequency adverbs appear and are placed correctly before the predicate.
  • -고 있다 is used at least once and correctly formed from an action-verb stem.
  • The paragraph includes a comparison between 예전에 and 요즘.
  • At least one expanded descriptive noun-modifier phrase appears.
  • Speech level is consistent everyday polite -아요/어요 throughout.
  • Only Hangeul is used; no romanization appears in the final draft.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Talking about busyness

In many everyday South Korean conversations, describing oneself as 바쁘다 ("busy") is a common and socially accepted way to explain why one has less free time, and it is frequently offered without being asked. Responding with sympathy, such as 힘들겠어요 ("that must be hard"), is a common and expected conversational response, though the exact expression of concern can vary by relationship and setting.

Language Note — 있다 has two related but distinct roles

Learners sometimes confuse the existence verb 있다 (Chapter 1, "to exist/have") with the auxiliary 있다 used in -고 있다 (this chapter, "to be in the middle of doing"). Both conjugate the same way, but they attach differently: existence 있다 follows a noun and a particle, as in 시간이 있어요, while -고 있다 follows an action-verb stem plus -고, as in 먹고 있어요. Keeping the two functions separate will help avoid confusion in later chapters when more auxiliary patterns are introduced.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Placing the frequency adverb after the verb

Error: 저는 아침을 먹어요 항상.

Correction: 저는 항상 아침을 먹어요. — frequency adverbs are placed before the predicate, not after it.

Common Mistake — Using -고 있다 with a descriptive verb

Error: 저는 바쁘고 있어요.

Correction: 저는 바빠요. — -고 있다 attaches to action verbs to describe an ongoing action, not to descriptive verbs, which already describe an ongoing state on their own.

Common Mistake — Confusing 거의 안 with 전혀 안

Error: Using 전혀 안 to mean "rarely" rather than "not at all."

Correction: 거의 안 means "almost never" and allows for rare exceptions, while 전혀 안 means "not at all" and allows for no exceptions. Choose the adverb that matches the intended degree.
