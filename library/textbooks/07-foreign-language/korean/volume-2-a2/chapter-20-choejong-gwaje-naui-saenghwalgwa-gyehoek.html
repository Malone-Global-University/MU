최종 과제: 나의 생활과 계획Final Project: My Life and My Plans

The Volume II final B1 portfolio: integrating personal description, routines, past narrative, housing and services, community life, learning and work, travel, opinions, goals, and problem-and-advice language into one connected presentation · Speak and Write Korean: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Present an integrated, connected account of your own life, routines, relationships, and past experiences, drawing on systems from across Volume II.
  2. Describe housing, services, transportation, and community participation using scheduled particle, request, and reason/condition patterns.
  3. Explain your studies or work, summarize a public notice or community issue, and discuss goals for the future.
  4. Narrate a travel or special experience, state an opinion, and support it with a reason or contrast.
  5. Explain a problem with background information, make a softened request, and give or receive advice.
  6. Produce a connected written portfolio text and a corresponding spoken presentation that integrate the above functions.
  7. Maintain consistent everyday polite -아요/어요 speech level, accurate Hangeul spelling and spacing, and appropriate register throughout an extended, multi-paragraph performance.

This chapter is the Unit IV and Volume II final assessment. It introduces no major new productive grammar, particle, speech-level, honorific, counter, or sound-change system. Its purpose is to consolidate and integrate the full range of Volume II communication into one connected portfolio.

Communication Scenario

라일라's Korean class is holding a small end-of-term sharing session. Each learner will prepare a short written portfolio and a spoken presentation called 나의 생활과 계획 ("My Life and My Plans"), covering who they are, how their daily life and relationships have developed, what has happened to them recently, where they live and how they get around, what they are learning or working on, what is happening in their community, a memorable travel experience, an opinion about something in their life, a goal for the future, and a problem they solved along the way. 라일라 and 지영 help each other prepare by reviewing the systems from every chapter in the volume.

  1. This chapter recycles vocabulary, grammar, particles, speech level, and pragmatics from all nineteen prior chapters of Volume II.
  2. This chapter introduces no new productive grammar, particle, honorific, counter, or sound-change system.
  3. The chapter's task is to combine previously activated systems into one connected, organized written and spoken portfolio.

발음 복습 — Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review

This chapter introduces no new sound-change rule. Two cumulative review points support extended, connected speech in the final portfolio presentation.

Pronunciation Note — Sentence-ending prosody across a long presentation

Longer connected speech, such as a portfolio presentation, still relies on the same sentence-ending intonation patterns learned throughout Volume II: a falling pattern for statements, a rising pattern for questions, and the open, unfinished-sounding rise on connectors like -는데 and -아/어서 before the sentence continues. Practice keeping these patterns consistent even as your sentences link together into paragraphs.

Pronunciation Note — Liaison and batchim across connected clauses

When clauses link together with connectors such as -아/어서, -(으)면, or -는데, batchim liaison continues to apply across the clause boundary, just as it does within a single word. Reading your portfolio draft aloud slowly is a useful way to notice and practice these connected-speech patterns before presenting.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns Review

This chapter introduces no new grammar system. It reviews and integrates the connective, modal, and pragmatic systems most useful for building a connected, multi-paragraph portfolio.

1. Connecting Paragraphs Across Sections

Review Note — Moving between portfolio sections smoothly

Use connectors reviewed across the volume — 그리고, 그래서, 하지만, 그런데 — at the start of a new paragraph to link one portfolio section to the next, the same way they link sentences within a single paragraph.

Clause-level connectors such as -아/어서 (reason/result), -지만 (contrast), -(으)면 (condition), and -는데 (background) remain available throughout the portfolio wherever they were originally scheduled.

  • 저는 요즘 회사에서 새로운 책임을 맡고 있는데, 그래서 시간이 많이 없어요. These days I've taken on a new responsibility at work, so I don't have much time. -는데 (background, Ch. 19) + -아/어서 (reason, Ch. 4)
  • 저는 아침에 운동하는 게 좋을 것 같아요. 그리고 저녁에는 가족과 시간을 보내려고 해요. I think it would be good to exercise in the morning. And in the evening, I plan to spend time with family. -는 게 좋을 것 같아요 (advice, Ch. 19) + 그리고 + -(으)려고 하다 (intention, Ch. 18)

2. Reviewing Tense, Modality, and Speech-Level Consistency

Review Note — Choosing the right time and modality across sections

A connected life-and-plans portfolio naturally moves between present routines (-아요/어요), past experiences (-았/었어요), ongoing situations (-고 있어요), and future plans (-(으)ㄹ 거예요, -(으)려고 해요, -기로 했어요). Keep track of which time frame each section describes, and keep the everyday polite -아요/어요 speech level consistent throughout, even as tense and modality shift from section to section.

3. Reviewing Softened, Indirect, and Opinion Language

Review Note — Keeping the portfolio natural and appropriately indirect

An integrated, natural-sounding portfolio uses opinion softening (-는 것 같아요, Ch. 17), advice softening (-는 게 좋을 것 같아요, Ch. 19), and request softening (좀, 혹시, Ch. 19) in the sections where they are relevant, rather than stating every idea as a flat, direct fact.

Building Block — A full portfolio paragraph

[topic/background] + [description or narrative] + [opinion or reason] + [goal or advice]

A well-organized portfolio paragraph introduces its topic, develops it with description or narrative detail, adds an opinion or reason, and closes with a goal, plan, or piece of advice, recycling systems from across the volume.

저는 요즘 사무실 근처로 이사했는데, 그래서 출퇴근 시간이 많이 줄었어요. 새 동네는 조용하고 편리한 것 같아요. 앞으로는 이 동네에서 오래 살고 싶어요.

I recently moved near my office, so my commute time has gone down a lot. I think the new neighborhood is quiet and convenient. I'd like to live in this neighborhood for a long time from now on.

Model Dialogue

Setting: 라일라 and 지영 practice their portfolio presentations together before the class sharing session.

지영 라일라 씨, 발표 준비 다 했어요? 한번 들어 봐도 될까요? Laila, have you finished preparing your presentation? Could I listen to it once?
라일라 네, 좋아요. 저는 대학교에서 일하고 있고, 요즘 새 동네로 이사해서 새로운 생활에 적응하고 있어요. Sure, sounds good. I work at a university, and these days I've moved to a new neighborhood and am adjusting to a new life.
지영 좋아요. 그다음에는 뭐가 나와요? Good. What comes next?
라일라 작년 여행 경험을 이야기하고, 요즘 취미에 대한 제 의견도 말할 거예요. 그리고 내년 목표도 이야기하려고 해요. I'll talk about last year's travel experience, and also share my opinion about my current hobbies. And I'm planning to talk about my goals for next year too.
지영 마지막에는 문제하고 조언 부분도 넣을 거예요? Are you going to include the problem-and-advice part at the end too?
라일라 네, 넣을 거예요. 최근에 회사 일이 많아서 힘들었던 문제를 이야기하고, 지영 씨한테 받은 조언도 소개할 거예요. Yes, I will. I'll talk about a problem I had recently — being overwhelmed with work — and I'll also introduce the advice you gave me.
지영 좋은 구성인 것 같아요. 혹시 시간이 너무 길지 않아요? I think that's a good structure. Is it not perhaps too long, by any chance?
라일라 조금 긴 것 같아서 좀 줄이려고 해요. 좀 들어 봐 줘서 고마워요. I feel it's a bit long, so I'm planning to shorten it a little. Thanks for listening to it.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Sharing personal milestones with a group

In many South Korean classroom, workplace, and community settings, sharing a personal update — a move, a new hobby, a goal, or a recent difficulty — with a group is common at the end of a term, project, or year. Presenters typically balance openness about challenges with a forward-looking, positive close, similar to the structure of this chapter's portfolio task.

Culture in Context — Acknowledging help and advice publicly

Naming a friend, colleague, or family member who gave helpful advice, as 라일라 does in the sample portfolio by crediting 지영 씨, is a common and appreciated way to close a personal update. It signals gratitude and reinforces the relationship in front of the group.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Mixing speech levels across sections

Error: Beginning the portfolio in everyday polite -아요/어요 and then sliding into casual or plain forms partway through, especially in the goal or problem sections.

Correction: Keep -아요/어요 consistent across all ten sections, since this is the volume's productive speech-level baseline; review each section aloud for accidental plain-form endings before presenting.

Common Mistake — Listing sections without connecting them

Error: Presenting each portfolio section as an isolated list item, with no connectors linking one topic to the next.

Correction: Use connectors such as 그리고, 그런데, or a background clause with -는데 to move naturally from one section to the next, as modeled in the Grammar Review and sample portfolio text.

Common Mistake — Introducing untaught vocabulary or grammar for a specialized topic

Error: Reaching for unfamiliar advanced vocabulary or grammar to describe a specialized job, hobby, or topic that goes beyond Chapters 1–20.

Correction: Simplify the topic to fit activated Volume II systems, or describe it in general terms using recycled vocabulary, rather than introducing unscheduled structures.
