앞으로의 목표Future Goals

Talking about plans, making firm decisions, and setting goals for the months ahead · Speak and Write Korean: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Expand your use of -(으)려고 하다 to express plans and intentions with more detail and in a wider range of everyday situations.
  2. Use -기로 하다 to state a firm decision or commitment, and distinguish it from the more tentative intention expressed by -(으)려고 하다.
  3. Talk about goals, plans, and steps toward a goal using level-appropriate vocabulary for study, work, health, and personal development.
  4. Explain a reason for choosing one goal over another, recycling connectors from earlier chapters.
  5. Ask someone about their plans and goals, and respond to another person's stated decision appropriately.
  6. Distinguish a plan that is still being considered from a decision that has already been made, both in speech and in writing.
  7. Maintain consistent everyday polite -아요/어요 speech level and accurate Hangeul spelling while discussing goals and decisions.

This chapter continues Unit IV, 경험, 의견, 그리고 프로젝트 (Experiences, Opinions, and Projects). It expands two systems introduced earlier in the volume — intention with -(으)려고 하다 and decision with -기로 하다 — into fuller, more independent use, and connects them to goal-setting vocabulary and reasoning language.

Communication Scenario

After comparing shows and hobbies in Chapter 17, 라일라 and 지영 start talking about what they each want to do differently in the coming months. 라일라 has been thinking about a few possible changes but has not committed to anything yet, while 지영 has already made up her mind about one specific goal. As they talk, they explain their reasons and encourage each other.

  1. This chapter recycles opinion and contrast language from Chapter 17, along with experience, desire, and intention language from Chapter 16.
  2. This chapter expands -(으)려고 하다 and -기로 하다 into fuller productive use as its two principal grammar systems.
  3. This chapter also introduces goal-setting and self-improvement vocabulary and practices explaining reasons for a goal.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter recycles previously introduced sound changes and adds no new sound-change rule. Two items deserve attention because they appear frequently in this chapter's expanded grammar patterns.

Pronunciation Note — 려고 하다 as a connected phrase

In -(으)려고 해요, the syllable 려 links smoothly with the stem before it, and the whole ending is usually spoken as one connected phrase rather than word by word. Listen for the phrase as a unit — 배우려고 해요 is one smooth breath group, not "bae-u" plus "ryeo-go hae-yo" spoken separately.

Pronunciation Note — 기로 했어요 and firmness of tone

Because -기로 했어요 announces a decision that has already been made, speakers often say it with slightly more even, confident stress than the softer, more open-ended -(으)려고 해요. Compare 운동하려고 해요 (a plan still being considered) with 운동하기로 했어요 (a decision already made) and notice how the second sounds a little more settled.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter expands two systems learners have already met — intention and firm decision — into fuller, more independent use, and adds language for explaining the reason behind a goal.

1. Intention, Expanded: -(으)려고 하다

Grammar Note — Form, Meaning, and Use

Form: verb stem + -으려고 하다 after a stem ending in a consonant, or verb stem + -려고 하다 after a stem ending in a vowel. In everyday polite speech, this becomes -(으)려고 해요. This chapter also activates the past-oriented use -(으)려고 했어요 for a plan someone had at an earlier point, and the question form -(으)려고 해요? for asking about someone's plans.

Meaning: -(으)려고 하다 expresses an intention or plan that a person is considering or working toward. It is more tentative than -기로 하다; the plan may still change.

Use: use -(으)려고 해요 to talk about what you are planning or hoping to do, especially when a decision does not yet feel completely final. Use -(으)려고 했어요 to describe what someone intended to do at an earlier time, including plans that did not work out.

  • 저는 내년에 자격증을 따려고 해요. I'm planning to earn a certificate next year. 따다 stem + 려고 해요
  • 사실은 어제 운동하려고 했는데, 시간이 없었어요. Actually, I was planning to exercise yesterday, but I didn't have time. 운동하다 stem + 려고 했는데 (past intention, unrealized)
  • 방학에 뭐 하려고 해요? What are you planning to do during vacation? 하다 stem + 려고 해요? (question about plans)

2. Firm Decision, Expanded: -기로 하다

Grammar Note — Form, Meaning, and Use

Form: verb stem + -기로 하다. In everyday polite speech, a decision already made is usually expressed with the past form -기로 했어요, since the decision itself happened before the moment of speaking, even though the action described may still be in the future. A decision announced at the moment of speaking may use -기로 해요 or -기로 할게요.

Meaning: -기로 하다 expresses a decision or commitment that has been settled, either by the speaker alone or together with another person. It is firmer and more final than -(으)려고 하다.

Use: use -기로 했어요 to report a decision you or someone else has already made, especially one you intend to follow through on. Use -기로 해요 when making or agreeing to a decision together with someone else in the moment.

Situation-(으)려고 하다 (tentative plan)-기로 하다 (firm decision)

  • 지영 씨하고 저는 같이 자격증 시험을 준비하기로 했어요. Jiyoung and I have decided to prepare for the certificate exam together. 준비하다 stem + 기로 했어요 (joint decision)
Common Mistake — Treating the two patterns as interchangeable

Error: Saying 운동하려고 했어요 to report a firm, already-settled decision, which makes the decision sound uncertain or unrealized.

Correction: If the decision is settled and you intend to follow through, use 운동하기로 했어요. Reserve -(으)려고 했어요 for a plan you had in mind, especially one that did not happen or is still tentative.

3. Explaining a Reason for a Goal: 왜냐하면 and -기 때문에

Grammar Note — Form, Meaning, and Use

Form: [Goal statement]. 왜냐하면 [reason] -기 때문이에요. 왜냐하면 begins a new sentence, similar to 반면에 from Chapter 17, and the reason sentence typically closes with -기 때문이에요.

Meaning: this pattern explains why a person has a particular goal or has made a particular decision. 왜냐하면 signals that an explanation is coming, and -기 때문이에요 closes the explanation.

Use: use this pattern after stating a goal or decision with -(으)려고 해요 or -기로 했어요, to explain your reasoning in a connected way. This complements -아/어서 and -(으)니까 from earlier chapters, which join a reason and a result within one sentence.

  • 저는 자격증을 따려고 해요. 왜냐하면 승진하고 싶기 때문이에요. I'm planning to earn a certificate. Because I want to be promoted. goal + 왜냐하면 + reason -기 때문이에요
Building Block — Goal, decision, and reason

[goal with -(으)려고 해요 or -기로 했어요] + [왜냐하면] + [reason -기 때문이에요]

A natural goal statement often states the plan or decision first, then explains the motivation behind it, connecting this chapter's expanded systems to reasoning language from earlier in the volume.

저는 올해 건강을 더 챙기기로 했어요. 왜냐하면 요즘 너무 피곤하기 때문이에요.

I've decided to take better care of my health this year. Because I've been too tired lately.

Practice — Combining the expanded systems

  1. Write one sentence about a plan you are still considering using -(으)려고 해요.
  2. Write one sentence about a decision you have already made using -기로 했어요.
  3. Add a reason to one of your sentences using 왜냐하면 ~ 기 때문이에요.

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task is a short goals paragraph: 나의 목표 ("My Goals").

  1. State a tentative plan. Write one sentence about something you are considering using -(으)려고 해요.
  2. State a firm decision. Write one sentence about something you have already decided using -기로 했어요.
  3. Explain a reason. Choose one of your goals and explain your reason using 왜냐하면 ~ 기 때문이에요.
  4. Add a contrast. Use 반면에 from Chapter 17 to contrast your tentative plan with your firm decision.
  5. Close with commitment. End with a sentence expressing determination, using 꼭 or 포기하지 않다.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • A tentative plan is expressed using -(으)려고 해요.
  • A firm decision is expressed using -기로 했어요.
  • A reason is given using 왜냐하면 ~ 기 때문이에요.
  • A contrast between the two goals is made using 반면에.
  • The paragraph closes with a clear statement of commitment.
  • Speech level is consistent everyday polite -아요/어요 throughout.
  • Only Hangeul is used; no romanization appears in the final draft.
  • No grammar or vocabulary beyond Chapters 1 through 18 appears productively.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — 새해 다짐 and shared resolutions

Talking about resolutions and goals, especially around the New Year, is a common social activity in South Korea, often summarized with the idiom 작심삼일 ("a three-day resolve"), a humorous acknowledgment that resolutions can be hard to keep. Friends and coworkers often share their goals with each other and check in on each other's progress, similar to what 라일라 and 지영 do in this chapter's dialogue.

Culture in Context — Encouraging others' goals

Offering encouragement with expressions like 제가 응원할게요 ("I'll cheer you on") is a common and expected response when someone shares a goal or decision, especially among friends or close colleagues. It is considered supportive rather than intrusive to ask a friend about their progress afterward.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using -기로 하다 for a plan that is still uncertain

Error: Saying 유학을 가기로 했어요 when the speaker is only thinking about the possibility and has not actually decided.

Correction: Use the more tentative 유학을 가려고 해요 until the decision is actually settled, and reserve -기로 했어요 for a genuine commitment.

Common Mistake — Forgetting the past tense with 기로 하다

Error: Saying 운동하기로 해요 to report a decision that was already made earlier, which can sound like the decision is happening only now.

Correction: Use the past form 운동하기로 했어요 to report a decision that was made before the moment of speaking, even though the exercising itself is still in the future.

Common Mistake — Starting 왜냐하면 without closing with -기 때문이에요

Error: Beginning a reason with 왜냐하면 but ending the sentence with a plain statement such as 왜냐하면 저는 승진하고 싶어요. without the expected closing pattern.

Correction: Close the reason sentence with -기 때문이에요: 왜냐하면 승진하고 싶기 때문이에요.

1. Sample answer: 저는 이번 여름에 새로운 취미에 도전하려고 해요.

2. Sample answer: 저는 올해 건강을 더 챙기기로 했어요.

3. Sample answer: 저는 매일 운동하기로 했어요. 왜냐하면 요즘 너무 피곤하기 때문이에요.

Section D — Sample "My Goals" paragraph

저는 올해 몇 가지 목표를 세우려고 해요. 저는 이번 달부터 매일 아침에 운동하기로 했어요. 왜냐하면 요즘 건강이 안 좋아졌기 때문이에요. 반면에, 자격증 시험 준비는 아직 언젠가 하려고만 하고 있어요. 그건 장기적인 목표라서 천천히 준비하려고 해요. 올해는 꼭 포기하지 않고 노력할 거예요.

Check that: a tentative plan is expressed with -(으)려고 해요, a firm decision is expressed with -기로 했어요, a reason is given with 왜냐하면 ~ 기 때문이에요, a contrast is made with 반면에, and no structures beyond Chapter 18 appear productively.
