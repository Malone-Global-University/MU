매일 무엇을 해요?Daily Routines

Expanding your action-verb system with 하다 verbs, adverbs of frequency, recurring time, and the inclusion particle 도 · Speak and Write Korean: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Conjugate 하다 verbs into the present polite form.
  2. Talk about your daily routine using common routine verbs.
  3. Use adverbs of frequency such as 보통, 항상, and 가끔 to describe how often you do something.
  4. Use the particle 에 with recurring time words such as 아침에 and 저녁에.
  5. Use the particle 도 to say that something is also true.
  6. Describe a typical day in connected polite sentences.
  7. Ask and answer questions about someone else's daily routine.

Communication Scenario

Class has a new getting-to-know-you activity this week: 하루 일과 인터뷰, a daily-routine interview. Students are pairing up to describe what they usually do every day, from waking up to going to sleep, and to compare their routines with a partner's.

  1. To talk about a routine, you need verbs for ordinary daily actions — waking up, washing, studying, working, exercising, resting — and a way to say how often you do each one.
  2. By the end of this chapter you will be able to describe your typical day from morning to night, say what you do every day versus only sometimes, and say that you and someone else share a habit, using only the systems activated through Chapter 7.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter does not introduce a new sound-change rule. It gives you controlled practice with the 하다 → 해요 pattern, which some learners rush through too quickly, and with batchim in 씻어요 and 늦게.

Pronunciation Note — 하다 → 해요, not 하아요

Every 하다 verb conjugates the same way: replace 하다 with 해요. Do not apply the ㅏ-stem rule from Chapter 6 here — the result is not 하아요. This is a fixed pattern that applies to every 하다 verb you will ever learn, including all five in this chapter's vocabulary.

Read the following words aloud, paying attention to the batchim in 씻어요 and to the ㅈ+ㄱ sequence pronounced together in 늦게.

일어나요 · 자요 · 씻어요 · 공부해요 · 일해요 · 운동해요 · 청소해요 · 전화해요 · 늦게

받침 점검: in 늦게, the batchim ㅈ before ㄱ is pronounced as an unreleased ㄷ-like stop, giving a sound close to 늗께. You will study this tensification pattern formally in a later chapter; for now, simply imitate the pronunciation as modeled.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

문법 1 — 하다 verbs

Communicative purpose: talk about a large family of common actions — studying, working, exercising, cleaning, calling — using one predictable conjugation pattern.

Many Korean verbs are built from a noun plus 하다 ("to do"). To conjugate any 하다 verb into the present polite form, simply replace 하다 with 해요.

Dictionary formChapter formPattern
공부하다공부해요noun 공부 + 하다 → 해요
운동하다운동해요noun 운동 + 하다 → 해요
  • 저는 한국어를 공부해요. I study Korean. 저는 [topic] + 한국어를 [object] + 공부해요 [verb]
  • 아버지는 회사에서 일해요. My father works at a company.
Grammar Note — Not every 하다 verb needs an object

Some 하다 verbs, such as 운동하다 and 일하다, are typically used without an object: 저는 운동해요 ("I exercise") is a complete sentence. Others, such as 공부하다, often appear with an object marked by 을/를, as in 한국어를 공부해요. Learn each verb's typical pattern along with its meaning rather than assuming every verb behaves the same way.

문법 2 — Adverbs of frequency

Communicative purpose: say how often you do something.

Frequency adverbs such as 보통, 항상, 가끔, and 자주 are placed before the verb they describe, usually near the beginning of the sentence.

  • 저는 보통 일곱 시에 일어나요. I usually get up at seven o'clock.
  • 저는 가끔 청소해요. I sometimes clean.
  • 저는 항상 아침에 운동해요. I always exercise in the morning.

일곱 시 ("seven o'clock") is glossed here for the example only; the full hours-and-minutes system is scheduled for a later chapter. You may substitute any time word you already know.

문법 3 — Recurring time with 에

Communicative purpose: say when, in a recurring or habitual sense, something happens.

You have already used 에 with specific dates and specific locations. The same particle attaches to time-of-day words like 아침, 오후, 저녁, and 밤 to mark a recurring time frame.

  • 저는 아침에 씻어요. I wash up in the morning.
  • 저녁에 친구를 만나요. I meet a friend in the evening.

매일 ("every day") does not take 에: say 매일 일해요, not 매일에 일해요.

문법 4 — Inclusion particle 도

Communicative purpose: say that something is also true, in addition to something already mentioned.

도 replaces 은/는, 이/가, or 을/를 on the noun it attaches to; it does not stack with them.

  • 저는 아침에 운동해요. 저녁에도 운동해요. I exercise in the morning. I also exercise in the evening. 저녁에 + 도 (에 remains; 도 adds "also")
  • 민준 씨도 매일 공부해요. Minjun also studies every day. 민준 씨 + 도, replacing 은/는
Language Building Block — Describing a routine
저는 보통 [시간]에 [행동]해요/아요/어요. [시간]에도 [행동]해요.
Habitual time sentence + inclusion sentence
저는 보통 아침에 운동해요. 저녁에도 운동해요.
I usually exercise in the morning. I also exercise in the evening.

Practice — Building routine sentences

  1. Conjugate 일어나다, 청소하다, and 쉬다 into the polite present form.
  2. Write one sentence with a recurring-time 에 phrase and a routine verb.
  3. Write one sentence using a frequency adverb.
  4. Write one sentence using 도 to say that a second time or person also does the same thing.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Layla and Minjun are doing the 하루 일과 인터뷰 activity, comparing their daily routines.

레일라 민준 씨, 보통 몇 시에 일어나요? Minjun, what time do you usually get up?
민준 저는 보통 일곱 시에 일어나요. 아침에 씻고 학교에 가요. I usually get up at seven. I wash up in the morning and go to school.
레일라 저도 아침에 일찍 일어나요. 저는 매일 운동해요. I also get up early in the morning. I exercise every day.
민준 저는 가끔 운동해요. 그런데 저녁에는 항상 한국어를 공부해요. I sometimes exercise. But in the evening I always study Korean.
레일라 저도 저녁에 공부해요. 그리고 자주 친구를 만나요. I also study in the evening. And I often meet friends.
민준 저는 밤에 늦게 자요. 레일라 씨는요? I go to sleep late at night. What about you, Layla?

내용 이해 — Comprehension questions:

  1. What time does Minjun usually get up?
  2. How often does Layla exercise, and how often does Minjun exercise?
  3. What does each person always do in the evening?
  4. What does Minjun do late at night?

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task is a short daily-routine paragraph, 저의 하루 ("My Day"), describing your typical schedule from morning to night.

  1. State your wake-up and morning routine. Use a recurring-time 에 phrase with at least one morning action.
  2. Add a frequency adverb. Use 보통, 항상, 가끔, or 자주 to describe how often you do at least one activity.
  3. Add an inclusion sentence. Use 도 to say that another time of day or another person shares one of your habits.
  4. Close with your evening or nighttime routine. Include at least one 하다 verb.
  5. Revise. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • Every 하다 verb is conjugated as 해요, not 하아요.
  • Recurring-time 에 attaches to 아침/오후/저녁/밤 correctly, and 매일 does not take 에.
  • Frequency adverbs appear before the verb they describe.
  • 도 replaces 은/는, 이/가, or 을/를ーit does not stack with them.
  • Earlier systems (은/는, 이/가, 을/를, -아요/어요, 안) are still used correctly where they appear.
  • No sentence relies on grammar not yet activated (Chapters 8 and beyond).

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — The productivity of 하다

하다 is one of the most productive elements in Korean: it combines with an enormous number of Sino-Korean and native nouns to form new verbs, from 공부하다 ("to study") to highly specialized professional and academic terms you will encounter in later volumes. Recognizing the noun-plus-하다 pattern will help you understand many new verbs before they are formally taught.

Culture in Context — Talking about routines

Describing a typical day is a common topic in everyday South Korean conversation, especially among students and coworkers getting to know each other. Questions like 보통 몇 시에 일어나요? are ordinary small talk rather than intrusive personal questions in most everyday, informal contexts.

Regional and Diaspora Note — Daily schedules vary

Typical daily schedules vary by city, school, workplace, and household throughout South Korea and among diaspora communities. The routines and time expressions in this chapter reflect commonly used everyday patterns rather than a single fixed national schedule.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Conjugating 하다 like a ㅏ-stem verb

Error: 저는 운동하아요.

Correction: 저는 운동해요. — 하다 always becomes 해요; it does not follow the ㅏ-stem contraction rule from Chapter 6.

Common Mistake — Attaching 에 to 매일

Error: 매일에 공부해요.

Correction: 매일 공부해요. — 매일 does not take 에; time words like 아침, 오후, 저녁, and 밤 do.

Common Mistake — Stacking 도 with another particle

Error: 저는도 운동해요.

Correction: 저도 운동해요. — 도 replaces 은/는, 이/가, or 을/를; it does not attach alongside them.

Common Mistake — Placing the frequency adverb after the verb

Error: 저는 운동해요 보통.

Correction: 저는 보통 운동해요. — Frequency adverbs like 보통 come before the verb, not after it.
