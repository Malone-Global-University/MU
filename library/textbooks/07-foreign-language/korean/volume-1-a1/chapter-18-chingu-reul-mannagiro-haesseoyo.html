친구를 만나기로 했어요Arrangements and Commitments

Continuing Unit IV: making firm arrangements with -기로 했어요, stating intentions with -(으)려고 해요, and taking a first productive step with -고 있다 · Speak and Write Korean: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Announce a firm arrangement or decision using -기로 했어요.
  2. State an intention using -(으)려고 해요, and distinguish it from a firm arrangement.
  3. Distinguish -기로 했어요, -(으)려고 해요, and the future/intention pattern -(으)ㄹ 거예요 from Chapter 11.
  4. Use -고 있다 productively for a limited set of high-frequency ongoing actions, moving beyond the recognition-only exposure from earlier chapters.
  5. Talk about turning wishes and plans from Chapter 17 into concrete arrangements.
  6. Maintain accurate Hangeul, spacing, particles, and everyday polite speech level while discussing arrangements and commitments.

Communication Scenario

라일라 and 수진 talked in Chapter 17 about wanting to travel to 제주도 together. A few days later, 수진 calls 라일라 to turn that wish into a real plan: she has already decided on dates and wants to confirm details. As they talk, 라일라 mentions that she is also in the middle of studying for a Korean quiz, so some of her free time this week is already spoken for.

  1. This chapter introduces -기로 했어요 for firm arrangements and -(으)려고 해요 for stated intentions.
  2. This chapter also introduces the first productive use of -고 있다, which learners have encountered receptively since Chapter 11.
  3. Every task below continues to draw on identity, routines, food, location, shopping, plans, directions, ability, health, past events, and experience/wish language from Chapters 1–17.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter's pronunciation focus is the batchim ㄱ of 약속 and 기로 carrying smoothly into the following vowel, and the contrast between the very similar-sounding endings -려고 해요 and -기로 했어요.

Pronunciation Note — 기로 했어요 versus 려고 해요

These two endings look and sound similar to beginning learners, but they are pronounced with different rhythm. 기로 했어요 has four syllables ending in the past-tense 했어요, spoken with a falling, settled intonation appropriate to reporting a finished decision. 려고 해요 has three syllables ending in the present-tense 해요, spoken with a slightly lighter, more open intonation appropriate to describing an ongoing intention. Listening for the tense of the final syllable — 했어요 versus 해요 — is the fastest way to tell them apart while listening.

Read the following aloud, paying attention to the batchim ㄱ liaison and to the tense contrast at the end of each phrase.

만나기로 했어요 · 가기로 했어요 · 가려고 해요 · 준비하려고 해요 · 공부하고 있어요

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

1. Announcing a firm arrangement with -기로 했어요

Purpose: to report a decision or arrangement that has already been made, usually together with someone else or as a firm personal commitment.

-기로 하다 attaches directly to the dictionary-form stem, with no vowel-harmony change, and is almost always used in the past tense -기로 했어요 because the decision itself is a completed event, even though the arranged action is still in the future.

Dictionary formPatternArrangement form
만나다-기로 했어요만나기로 했어요
가다-기로 했어요가기로 했어요
정하다-기로 했어요정하기로 했어요
  • 이번 방학에 제주도에 가기로 했어요.We've decided to go to Jeju Island this vacation.
  • 토요일에 친구를 만나기로 했어요.I've arranged to meet a friend on Saturday.
  • 날짜를 다시 정하기로 했어요.We've decided to set the date again.
Grammar Note — Arrangement versus simple future/intention

Compare three patterns that all point toward the future. 제주도에 갈 거예요. (Chapter 11 future/intention) simply states what will happen, without emphasizing that a firm decision was made. 제주도에 가고 싶어요. (Chapter 17 wish) expresses a desire, with no commitment implied. 제주도에 가기로 했어요. reports that a decision or arrangement — often with another person — has already been settled. Use -기로 했어요 when you want to communicate that the matter is decided, not merely hoped for or expected.

2. Stating an intention with -(으)려고 해요

Purpose: to say what you are planning or intending to do, as a personal intention that has not necessarily been arranged with anyone else and is not yet finalized.

-(으)려고 해요 attaches to the dictionary-form stem following the same batchim-based pattern as -(으)ㄹ 거예요 and -(으)세요: -려고 해요 after a vowel-final stem, -으려고 해요 after a consonant-final stem.

Stem typePatternDictionary formIntention form
vowel-final-려고 해요가다가려고 해요
consonant-final-으려고 해요준비하다 → 준비하다 (하다 verb)준비하려고 해요
consonant-final-으려고 해요읽다읽으려고 해요
  • 이번 주말에 여행을 준비하려고 해요.I'm planning to prepare for the trip this weekend.
  • 저는 오늘 일찍 자려고 해요.I'm planning to go to bed early today.
  • 주말에 시험 공부를 하려고 해요. 그런데 아직 확실하지 않아요.I'm planning to study for the exam this weekend. But it's not certain yet.intention, not a firm arrangement
Language Building Block — From intention to arrangement
[일]을/를 하려고 해요. 그래서 [사람]하고 [일]을/를 하기로 했어요.
Stating a personal intention, then reporting that it became a settled arrangement with someone else
저는 제주도에 여행을 가려고 했어요. 그래서 수진 씨하고 같이 가기로 했어요.
I was planning to travel to Jeju Island. So I decided to go together with Sujin.

3. A first productive step with -고 있다

Purpose: to describe an action that is currently in progress, moving from recognition (Chapters 11–17) to limited independent production in this chapter.

-고 있다 attaches directly to the dictionary-form stem, with no vowel-harmony change, and behaves like a predicate that itself takes the polite present ending: -고 있어요.

  • 저는 지금 한국어를 공부하고 있어요.I am studying Korean right now.
  • 라일라 씨는 시험을 준비하고 있어요.Layla is preparing for a test.
  • 지금 여행을 준비하고 있어요. 그래서 이번 주는 좀 바빠요.I'm preparing for a trip right now. So I'm a little busy this week.

This chapter's productive use of -고 있다 is limited to first-person and third-person statements about ongoing actions with the small set of action verbs already active in this chapter and in Chapters 1–17, such as 공부하다, 준비하다, and 기다리다 (recycled from Ch. 8). Broader productive control of -고 있다 across all action verbs, and its use for repeated or habitual states, is developed further in Volume II.

Practice — Forming arrangement, intention, and ongoing-action statements

  1. Change each verb into the arrangement pattern: 토요일에 만나다 / 날짜를 정하다 / 같이 여행을 가다.
  2. Change each verb into the intention pattern: 일찍 일어나다 / 시험을 준비하다 / 여권을 확인하다.
  3. Write one true sentence using -고 있어요 to describe something you are doing this week.
  4. Write one sentence that moves from intention to arrangement, using 그래서, as in the Language Building Block above.

Model Dialogue

Setting: A few days after Chapter 17, 수진 calls 라일라 on the phone about the Jeju Island trip.

수진 라일라 씨, 저 지금 제주도 여행 일정을 확인하고 있어요. 우리 언제 가기로 할까요? Layla, I'm checking the Jeju Island trip schedule right now. When should we decide to go?
라일라 음, 저는 이번 주 금요일에 시험이 있어요. 그래서 시험 공부를 하고 있어요. 금요일 전에는 좀 바빠요. Um, I have an exam this Friday. So I'm studying for the exam. I'm a little busy before Friday.
수진 그럼 토요일부터 가는 게 어때요? 저는 토요일에 시간이 나요. Then how about going starting Saturday? I have free time on Saturday.
라일라 좋아요! 그럼 토요일에 가기로 해요. 저는 오늘 여권을 확인하려고 해요. Sounds good! Then let's decide to go on Saturday. I'm planning to check my passport today.
수진 네, 저도 준비하고 있어요. 비행기 표는 이미 확인했어요. Okay, I'm preparing too. I already checked the plane tickets.
라일라 잘됐어요! 그럼 토요일 아침에 공항에서 만나기로 해요. That's great! Then let's decide to meet at the airport Saturday morning.
수진 네, 좋아요. 그때 다시 연락해요! Yes, sounds good. Let's talk again then!

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task is a short paragraph, 이번 주 계획과 약속 ("My Plans and Arrangements This Week"), combining an intention, a firm arrangement, and an ongoing action.

  1. State one thing you are currently doing. Use -고 있어요.
  2. State one personal intention. Use -(으)려고 해요.
  3. State one firm arrangement with another person. Use -기로 했어요.
  4. Connect two of the ideas. Use 그래서 or 그런데 to link an intention to an arrangement, or a reason to a busy schedule.
  5. Close with a time expression. Use a day, date, or time word from Chapters 4 and 18 to say when the arrangement will happen.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • Every sentence uses only Hangeul and only systems activated through Chapter 18.

  • Speech level is consistent everyday polite throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Confirming plans by text message

In South Korea, it is common to confirm a spoken arrangement with a short follow-up text message, even between close friends, to make sure the time, date, and place are clear. A message like 토요일 아침 8시에 공항에서 만나기로 했지요? serves both as a reminder and as a polite way of checking that both people remember the same plan.

Language Note — Why 기로 하다 is almost always past tense

Learners sometimes expect -기로 해요 without the past-tense -았/었-, since the arranged event itself is in the future. However, the decision to make the arrangement is what the ending reports, and that decision has already happened by the time you are talking about it. This is why -기로 했어요, not -기로 해요, is the standard everyday form for reporting an arrangement.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing -기로 했어요 and -(으)려고 해요

Error: 수진 씨하고 토요일에 가려고 해요. when describing an arrangement both people have already confirmed together.

Correction: 수진 씨하고 토요일에 가기로 했어요. — once both people have agreed and the plan is settled, use -기로 했어요. Reserve -(으)려고 해요 for a personal intention that is not yet a confirmed arrangement.

Common Mistake — Present tense with -기로 하다

Error: 내일 만나기로 해요.

Correction: 내일 만나기로 했어요. — the decision itself is a completed event, so -기로 하다 is almost always used in the past tense, even though the arranged event is in the future.

Common Mistake — Wrong stem before -고 있어요

Error: 공부해요고 있어요.

Correction: 공부하고 있어요. — like -고 싶어요 in Chapter 17, -고 있다 attaches directly to the plain dictionary-form stem, never to the polite present form.
