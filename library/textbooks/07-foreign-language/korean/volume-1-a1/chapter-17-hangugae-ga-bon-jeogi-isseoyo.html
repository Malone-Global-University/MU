한국에 가 본 적이 있어요Experience and Travel

Continuing Unit IV: talking about life experience with -아/어 본 적이 있어요, expressing wishes with -고 싶어요, travel vocabulary, and a first look at Hanja in familiar place names · Speak and Write Korean: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Ask and answer questions about life experience using -아/어 본 적이 있어요 and 없어요.
  2. Express a wish or desire using -고 싶어요.
  3. Talk about travel using vocabulary for countries, transportation, and trip planning.
  4. Recognize a small set of Hanja characters used in familiar Korean place names and travel vocabulary.
  5. Combine past-tense narration from Chapter 16 with experience and wish expressions in a single connected account.
  6. Maintain accurate Hangeul, spacing, particles, and everyday polite speech level while discussing experience and travel.

Communication Scenario

After class, 라일라 and 수진 keep talking about the weekend. 수진 mentions that her cousin just came back from 부산, and 라일라 realizes she has never been to 부산 — or anywhere else in Korea outside 서울. The conversation turns from what already happened to what 라일라 has or has not experienced, and what she would like to do someday. 수진 offers to help her plan a short trip.

  1. This chapter introduces -아/어 본 적이 있어요 for talking about experience, and -고 싶어요 for expressing wishes.
  2. Every task below continues to draw on identity, routines, food, location, shopping, plans, directions, ability, health, and past-event language from Chapters 1–16.
  3. By the end of this chapter you will be able to talk about what you have and have not experienced, and what you would like to do, entirely in everyday polite Korean.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter's pronunciation focus continues the past-tense contraction pattern from Chapter 16, now applied to the longer -아/어 본 적이 있어요 pattern, along with the connected pronunciation of 싶어요.

Pronunciation Note — 싶어요 and 본 적이 있어요

싶어요 is pronounced with the batchim ㅍ carried smoothly into the following vowel-initial syllable, similar to the liaison pattern reviewed in earlier chapters: 싶어요 sounds close to si-peo-yo, not sip-eo-yo with a pause. In 본 적이 있어요, listen for the batchim ㄴ of 본 and the batchim ㄱ of 적 carrying forward smoothly into 이 있어요, so the whole phrase flows as one connected unit rather than four separate words.

Read the following aloud, listening for smooth liaison rather than separated syllables.

가고 싶어요 · 먹고 싶어요 · 가 본 적이 있어요 · 먹어 본 적이 있어요 · 한 번도 없어요

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

1. Talking about experience with -아/어 본 적이 있어요

Purpose: to say whether you have or have not had a particular experience at some point in your life, independent of a specific time.

This pattern attaches to the same verb stem used for the polite present and past, following the familiar vowel-harmony pattern from Chapters 6 and 16: -아 본 적이 있어요 after ㅏ/ㅗ stems, and -어 본 적이 있어요 after other stems.

Stem vowelPatternDictionary formExperience form
ㅏ or ㅗ-아 본 적이 있어요가다가 본 적이 있어요
other vowels-어 본 적이 있어요먹다먹어 본 적이 있어요
하다 verbs해 본 적이 있어요구경하다구경해 본 적이 있어요

To say you have never had an experience, replace 있어요 with 없어요:

  • 부산에 가 본 적이 있어요.I have been to Busan (before).
  • 부산에 가 본 적이 없어요.I have never been to Busan.
  • 한국 음식을 먹어 본 적이 한 번도 없어요.I have never even once eaten Korean food.한 번도 + negative for emphasis
Grammar Note — Experience versus simple past

Compare 어제 부산에 갔어요. ("I went to Busan yesterday," Chapter 16 simple past, a specific completed event) with 부산에 가 본 적이 있어요. ("I have been to Busan before," an experience at some unspecified point in the past). Use the simple past for a specific time already mentioned or clear from context. Use -아/어 본 적이 있어요 when the point in time is not the focus — only whether the experience has happened.

2. Expressing wishes with -고 싶어요

Purpose: to say what you want to do, distinct from what you want to have (which uses a noun plus 을/를 원하다 or simply a noun with 있어요, patterns not active in this chapter).

-고 싶어요 attaches directly to the dictionary-form stem, with no vowel-harmony change:

  • 제주도에 가고 싶어요.I want to go to Jeju Island.
  • 사진을 많이 찍고 싶어요.I want to take a lot of photos.
  • 한국 음식을 만들고 싶어요.I want to make Korean food.

This chapter's productive use of -고 싶어요 is limited to first-person statements about the speaker's own wishes. Asking about someone else's wishes with 싶어요? is introduced receptively in the Model Dialogue and may be used in guided question-answer practice, but a full third-person form (싶어 해요) is not scheduled for this volume.

Language Building Block — Combining experience and wishes
[장소]에 가 본 적이 없어요. 그래서 언젠가 [장소]에 꼭 가고 싶어요.
Stating a missing experience, then expressing a strong wish to have it someday
저는 부산에 가 본 적이 없어요. 그래서 언젠가 부산에 꼭 가고 싶어요.
I have never been to Busan. So someday I definitely want to go to Busan.

3. A first look at Hanja: 한국, 외국, 미국

Purpose: receptive, analytical awareness only — recognizing that some Sino-Korean vocabulary is built from smaller meaningful units, useful for later vocabulary learning.

The word 국 appears inside several country names taught in this chapter: 한국 (Korea), 외국 (foreign country), 미국 (the United States), 중국 (China). In Hanja, 국 is written 國/国, meaning "country." Recognizing this recurring syllable can help you guess the meaning of new country-related vocabulary later in the course.

Hanja recognition in this chapter is receptive and analytical only. Learners are not required to write Hanja characters. This awareness supports vocabulary learning; it does not create a productive writing requirement.

Practice — Forming experience and wish statements

  1. Change each verb into the experience pattern: 기차를 타다 / 외국 음식을 먹다 / 여행 사진을 찍다.
  2. Write one true sentence about a place you have been to, using -아/어 본 적이 있어요.
  3. Write one true sentence about a place you have never been to, using -아/어 본 적이 없어요.
  4. Write two sentences using -고 싶어요 to describe two things you want to do on a trip.

Model Dialogue

Setting: After class, 라일라 and 수진 are still talking, now about travel.

수진 제 사촌이 지난주말에 부산에 갔다 왔어요. 라일라 씨는 부산에 가 본 적이 있어요? My cousin went to and came back from Busan last weekend. Layla, have you ever been to Busan?
라일라 아니요, 한 번도 없어요. 사실 서울 밖에 가 본 적이 거의 없어요. No, not even once. Actually, I've almost never been outside Seoul.
수진 정말요? 그럼 제주도는요? 제주도도 가 본 적이 없어요? Really? Then what about Jeju Island? Have you never been to Jeju Island either?
라일라 네, 없어요. 하지만 언젠가 꼭 가고 싶어요. 제주도가 정말 유명하잖아요. Right, I haven't. But I definitely want to go someday. Jeju Island is really famous, after all.
수진 그럼 이번 방학에 같이 갈까요? 저도 여행하고 싶어요. Then shall we go together this vacation? I want to travel too.
라일라 좋아요! 비행기로 갈까요, 배로 갈까요? Great! Shall we go by plane, or by boat?
수진 비행기가 더 빨라요. 여권 있어요? The plane is faster. Do you have a passport?
라일라 네, 있어요. 정말 기대돼요! Yes, I have one. I'm really looking forward to it!

  1. Where did 수진's cousin go last weekend?
  2. Has 라일라 ever been to Busan? Has she ever been to Jeju Island?
  3. What do 라일라 and 수진 decide to do together?
  4. Find one sentence with -아/어 본 적이 있어요/없어요, and one with -고 싶어요.

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task is a short paragraph, 나의 여행 경험과 바람 ("My Travel Experience and Wishes"), combining what you have and have not experienced with what you would like to do.

  1. State one experience you have had. Use -아/어 본 적이 있어요.
  2. State one experience you have not had. Use -아/어 본 적이 없어요.
  3. Connect the two ideas. Use 그래서 or 하지만 to link a missing experience to a wish.
  4. State two wishes. Use -고 싶어요 at least twice, describing places you want to go or things you want to do there.
  5. Close with a reason. Use -아/어서 (Chapter 14) to explain why one of your wishes matters to you.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • Every sentence uses only Hangeul and only systems activated through Chapter 17.
  • Experience statements use the correct ㅏ/ㅗ versus other-vowel form before 본 적이 있어요/없어요.
  • At least two sentences use -고 싶어요.
  • Speech level is consistent everyday polite throughout.
  • Spacing between nouns, particles, and predicates follows the patterns modeled in Chapters 1–16.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Domestic travel in South Korea

South Korea has a well-developed high-speed rail network connecting major cities such as 서울 and 부산 in under three hours, and short domestic flights to 제주도 are common and inexpensive. Many South Korean students and workers travel domestically during school breaks or public holidays, so questions such as 여행 가 본 적이 있어요? are natural, everyday small talk, not unusual or intrusive.

Writing System Note — Sino-Korean country names

Country names built with 국 ("country") are extremely common in Korean: 한국, 미국, 중국, and 외국 all share this element. Noticing recurring Sino-Korean syllables like this — without needing to write the Hanja yourself — is a useful reading strategy that will help you recognize new vocabulary more quickly in later chapters and volumes.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing experience and simple past

Error: 어제 부산에 가 본 적이 있어요. (mixing a specific time word with the experience pattern)

Correction: 어제 부산에 갔어요. — a specific time such as 어제 calls for the simple past from Chapter 16, not the experience pattern, which is used for unspecified points in the past.

Common Mistake — Wrong stem before -고 싶어요

Error: 가요고 싶어요.

Correction: 가고 싶어요. — -고 싶어요 attaches directly to the plain dictionary-form stem, never to the polite present form.

Common Mistake — Forgetting 없어요 for "never"

Error: 저는 제주도에 안 가 본 적이 있어요.

Correction: 저는 제주도에 가 본 적이 없어요. — negate the experience pattern by changing 있어요 to 없어요, not by adding 안.
