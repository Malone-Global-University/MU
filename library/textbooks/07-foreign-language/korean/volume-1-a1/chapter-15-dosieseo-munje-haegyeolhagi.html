An integrated performance chapter combining plans and future reference (Ch. 11), directions and movement (Ch. 12), ability, requests, and learning (Ch. 13), and health, needs, and reasons (Ch. 14) · Speak and Write Korean: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

This is a synthesis chapter. No new grammar, particles, or speech-level systems are introduced. By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Talk about weekend and future plans using -(으)ㄹ 거예요 and invitations with 같이.
  2. Give and follow directions using 로/으로, 에서...까지, and movement verbs with -(으)세요.
  3. Talk about ability and make polite requests using -(으)ㄹ 수 있어요, 못, and 아/어 주세요.
  4. Explain a health problem, state an obligation, and issue a polite prohibition using -아/어서, 해야 해요, and -지 마세요.
  5. Combine all four systems into a single connected spoken or written performance about solving a real-life problem in the city.
  6. Maintain accurate Hangeul, spacing, particles, and everyday polite speech level across an extended task.

Communication Scenario

라일라 is finally feeling better after her cold from Chapter 14. Her friend 수진 has invited her to spend Saturday together, but 라일라 also has to run several errands around the city: she needs to find her way to a new pharmacy, ask someone whether she can still pick up a package she was unable to collect earlier in the week, and explain to her Korean class instructor why she needs to reschedule a make-up session. Across a single Saturday, 라일라 must plan, navigate, request help, and explain reasons and needs — all using language from Chapters 11 through 14.

  1. This chapter introduces no new grammar. It integrates plans and future reference, directions and movement, ability and requests, and health, needs, and reasons into connected performance tasks.
  2. Every task below also continues to draw on identity, family, dates, food, routines, location, and shopping language from Chapters 1–10.
  3. By the end of this chapter you will be able to move through a full day of plans, directions, requests, and explanations entirely in everyday polite Korean.

발음 — Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review

This chapter introduces no new pronunciation target. It reviews liaison and batchim linking across the four Unit III patterns, since these grammar endings frequently attach directly to consonant-final and vowel-final stems.

Pronunciation Note — Linking across Unit III endings

Listen for smooth linking between the stem and the ending in phrases such as 갈 거예요, 가야 해요, 할 수 있어요, and 아파서. In each case the final consonant of the stem or the batchim of an attached particle connects forward into the following syllable rather than being pronounced as a separate, isolated block.

Read the following aloud, listening for connected, natural speech rather than separated syllables.

주말에 갈 거예요 · 똑바로 가세요 · 한국어를 할 수 있어요 · 머리가 아파서 쉬어야 해요

Integrated Dialogue

Setting: 라일라 calls 수진 on Saturday morning to plan the day and explain a change.

수진 라일라 씨, 오늘 저하고 같이 시내에 갈 거예요? Layla, are you going downtown with me today?
라일라 네, 같이 갈 거예요. 그런데 먼저 약국에 가야 해요. 목이 아직 조금 아파서 약을 사야 해요. Yes, I'll go with you. But first I need to go to the pharmacy. My throat still hurts a little, so I need to buy medicine.
수진 약국이 어디에 있어요? 저도 갈 수 있어요. Where's the pharmacy? I can go too.
라일라 여기에서 은행까지 걸어가세요. 은행에서 오른쪽으로 가세요. 그럼 약국이 보여요. Walk from here to the bank. Turn right at the bank. Then you'll see the pharmacy.
수진 알겠어요. 그런데 오늘 한국어 보충 수업이 있지 않아요? Got it. But don't you have a Korean class make-up session today?
라일라 네, 그런데 아직 목이 아파서 오늘 수업에 못 가요. 선생님께 이메일을 써야 해요. 좀 도와주세요. Yes, but my throat still hurts, so I can't go to class today. I need to write an email to my teacher. Please help me a little.
수진 네, 도와줄 수 있어요. 걱정하지 마세요. 우리 같이 이메일을 쓸 거예요. Sure, I can help. Don't worry. We'll write the email together.

  1. Where does 라일라 need to go first, and why?
  2. How does 라일라 tell 수진 to get to the pharmacy?
  3. Why can't 라일라 go to her make-up class today?
  4. Find one sentence with -(으)ㄹ 거예요, one with -(으)세요, one with -아/어서, and one with 아/어 주세요 in the dialogue.

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task is a connected paragraph, 토요일 계획 ("Saturday Plan"), that combines all four Unit III systems into one short piece of writing.

  1. State a plan. Use -(으)ㄹ 거예요 to describe something you plan to do this weekend.
  2. Give directions. Use 로/으로, 에서...까지, or -(으)세요 to explain how to get somewhere connected to your plan.
  3. State ability or inability. Use -(으)ㄹ 수 있어요 or 못 to say whether you can do part of the plan.
  4. Give a reason and an obligation. Use -아/어서 and 해야 해요 to explain why you need to do something.
  5. Make a request or a reassurance. Use 아/어 주세요 or -지 마세요.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the editing checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • Every sentence uses only Hangeul and only systems activated through Chapter 14.
  • All four Unit III systems appear at least once: plans, directions, ability/requests, and reason/obligation/prohibition.
  • Speech level is consistent everyday polite throughout.
  • Endings are formed correctly according to the vowel or consonant pattern of each stem.
  • Spacing between nouns, particles, and predicates follows the patterns modeled in Chapters 1–14.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Rescheduling politely

When a learner needs to reschedule a class, appointment, or meeting in South Korea, it is common and courteous to explain the reason briefly, propose an alternative time, and thank the other person for their understanding, much as 라일라 does in her email to her instructor above.

Culture in Context — Offering help without being asked

In many everyday South Korean friendships, offering to help with an errand or task, as 수진 does throughout this chapter's dialogue, is a common way of expressing care, and such offers are often made warmly and readily accepted between close friends.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing -(으)세요 direction instructions with -지 마세요

Error: 똑바로 마세요. (attempting to say "go straight")

Correction: 똑바로 가세요. — -(으)세요 gives a positive instruction; -지 마세요 only attaches to a stem to give a negative instruction, and the two cannot be mixed.

Common Mistake — Using 못 instead of -(으)ㄹ 수 없어요 inconsistently

Error: 한국어를 잘 수 없어요.

Correction: 한국어를 잘 할 수 없어요. or 한국어를 잘 못해요. — the verb stem 하다 must remain in the sentence; -(으)ㄹ 수 없어요 attaches to the verb stem, not directly to an adverb.

Common Mistake — Dropping the reason clause before the obligation

Error: 아파요. 약을 먹어야 해요. (two separate sentences where a connected reason is expected)

Correction: 아파서 약을 먹어야 해요. — when a reason directly motivates an obligation, Chapter 14's -아/어서 connects them into one sentence rather than leaving them as two disconnected statements.
