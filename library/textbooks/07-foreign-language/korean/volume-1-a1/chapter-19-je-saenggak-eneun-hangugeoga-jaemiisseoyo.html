제 생각에는 한국어가 재미있어요Opinions, Comparison, and Connected Discourse

Completing Unit IV: stating opinions with 제 생각에는, comparing options with 보다 더 and 제일/가장, and connecting longer ideas with -지만 and -고 · Speak and Write Korean: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Introduce a personal opinion using 제 생각에는.
  2. Compare two things using 보다 더.
  3. Identify the best or most extreme option in a group using 제일 and 가장.
  4. Connect two contrasting ideas within one sentence using -지만.
  5. Connect two related ideas within one sentence using -고, distinguishing it from the sentence-connector 그리고.
  6. Produce a short connected paragraph that states an opinion, gives a reason, and makes a comparison.
  7. Maintain accurate Hangeul, spacing, particles, and everyday polite speech level while sharing opinions and comparisons.

Communication Scenario

라일라 and 수진 have returned from their trip to 제주도. Back in the classroom, their instructor asks students to share their thoughts about learning Korean and about their recent trip. 라일라 and 수진 compare Jeju to other places they have visited, and 라일라 shares her honest opinion about which part of Korean is easiest and which part is hardest so far.

  1. This chapter introduces the opinion frame 제 생각에는 and the comparison patterns 보다 더 and 제일/가장.
  2. This chapter also introduces two clause-linking endings, -지만 and -고, for connecting two ideas inside one sentence.
  3. Every task below continues to draw on identity, routines, food, location, shopping, plans, directions, ability, health, past events, experience/wishes, and arrangements from Chapters 1–18.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

This chapter's pronunciation focus is the batchim ㅂ of 쉽다 and 어렵다 before vowel-initial endings, where it changes to a 우 glide sound, and the smooth linking of -지만 and -고 onto the predicate stem without a pause.

Pronunciation Note — ㅂ irregular stems before endings

Stems like 쉽- and 어렵- lose the final ㅂ and add a rounded vowel sound before endings that begin with a vowel, which is why 쉬워요 and 어려워요 sound quite different from their dictionary forms 쉽다 and 어렵다. Before -지만, however, the ending begins with a consonant, so the stem stays intact: 쉽지만, 어렵지만. Listening for whether the following ending begins with a vowel or a consonant tells you whether the ㅂ irregular pattern applies.

Read the following aloud, paying attention to the linking of the connective endings and to the ㅂ irregular contrast.

쉬워요 · 쉽지만 · 어려워요 · 어렵지만 · 재미있고 · 바쁘지만

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

1. Stating an opinion with 제 생각에는

Purpose: to introduce a personal opinion clearly, signaling to the listener that what follows is a viewpoint rather than a simple fact.

제 생각에는 literally means "in my thought," combining the humble possessive 제 ("my," recycled from earlier chapters), 생각 ("thought"), and the particle 에는 ("in, as for"). It is placed at the beginning of a sentence, followed by a complete sentence expressing the opinion.

  • 제 생각에는 한국어가 재미있어요.In my opinion, Korean is interesting.
  • 제 생각에는 발음이 제일 어려워요.In my opinion, pronunciation is the most difficult.
  • 제 생각에는 제주도가 정말 아름다워요.In my opinion, Jeju Island is really beautiful.
Grammar Note — Opinion versus fact

Compare 한국어가 재미있어요., a simple statement, with 제 생각에는 한국어가 재미있어요., which explicitly frames the statement as a personal viewpoint. Using 제 생각에는 is especially useful in classroom discussion, comparison, and disagreement, where signaling "this is my view" helps keep a conversation polite and open to other opinions.

2. Comparing two things with 보다 더

Purpose: to compare one thing to another and state that one has more of a quality than the other.

The particle 보다 attaches to the noun being compared against, meaning "than," and is usually paired with the adverb 더 ("more") before the descriptive verb. Word order is flexible: 보다 can appear directly after the noun it marks, and the sentence still ends with the predicate.

PatternExampleMeaning
[A]는/은 [B]보다 더 [descriptive verb]제주도는 서울보다 더 따뜻해요.Jeju is warmer than Seoul.
[A]는/은 [B]보다 더 [descriptive verb]한국어가 일본어보다 더 어려워요.Korean is more difficult than Japanese.
  • 이 식당이 저 식당보다 더 맛있어요.This restaurant is more delicious than that restaurant.
  • 쓰기가 말하기보다 더 쉬워요.Writing is easier than speaking.
  • 제주도가 부산보다 더 아름다워요. 그런데 부산도 정말 좋아요.Jeju is more beautiful than Busan. But Busan is really good too.

3. Naming the best or most extreme option with 제일 and 가장

Purpose: to say that something is the most, the best, or the -est among a group of three or more, rather than comparing only two things.

제일 and 가장 both mean "most" and are placed directly before the descriptive verb. 제일 is more common in everyday speech; 가장 is slightly more formal and appears more often in writing. Both are correct in everyday polite speech.

  • 저는 한국 음식 중에서 김치찌개를 제일 좋아해요.Among Korean foods, I like kimchi stew the most.
  • 발음이 제일 어려워요. 그리고 쓰기가 제일 쉬워요.Pronunciation is the most difficult. And writing is the easiest.
  • 제 생각에는 제주도가 가장 아름다운 곳이에요.In my opinion, Jeju Island is the most beautiful place.가장 + descriptive verb; noun-modifying form receptive only
Language Building Block — Opinion, comparison, and reason together
제 생각에는 [A]가/이 [B]보다 더 [descriptive verb]. 왜냐하면 [reason].
Combining an opinion frame, a comparison, and a simple reason in two connected sentences
제 생각에는 제주도가 서울보다 더 좋아요. 왜냐하면 경치가 정말 아름다워요.
In my opinion, Jeju is better than Seoul. Because the scenery is really beautiful.

왜냐하면 ("because") is presented here receptively to support the reading and dialogue in this chapter. Productive reason-giving continues to use -아/어서 from Chapter 14 and 그래서 from Chapter 11.

4. Connecting contrasting ideas with -지만

Purpose: to join two clauses inside one sentence when the second idea contrasts with the first, similar to English "but" or "although."

-지만 attaches directly to the predicate stem of the first clause, regardless of tense marked earlier in the sentence, and the sentence continues with the second clause ending in a normal sentence-final form.

Dictionary formPatternConnected form
어렵다-지만어렵지만
바쁘다-지만바쁘지만
좋아하다-지만좋아하지만
  • 한국어는 어렵지만 재미있어요.Korean is difficult, but it's interesting.
  • 이번 주는 바쁘지만 여행은 정말 좋았어요.This week is busy, but the trip was really good.
  • 발음은 어렵지만 저는 한국어 공부를 계속하고 싶어요.Pronunciation is difficult, but I want to keep studying Korean.

5. Connecting related ideas with -고

Purpose: to join two clauses inside one sentence when the two ideas are simply added together, without a strong contrast, similar to English "and."

-고 attaches directly to the predicate stem, the same way as -지만. Unlike the sentence-connector 그리고, which joins two separate sentences, -고 joins two clauses into one sentence.

  • 한국어는 재미있고 유용해요.Korean is interesting and useful.
  • 쓰기는 쉽고 말하기는 조금 어려워요.Writing is easy, and speaking is a little difficult.
  • 저는 어제 숙제를 하고 오늘 시험공부를 했어요.I did homework yesterday and studied for the exam today.
Grammar Note — -고 versus 그리고

그리고, active since Chapter 11, connects two complete sentences: 한국어는 재미있어요. 그리고 유용해요. -고 connects two clauses within a single sentence: 한국어는 재미있고 유용해요. Both are correct, and the choice depends on whether the speaker wants two separate sentences or one combined sentence. Combined sentences with -고 often sound more natural in connected speech and writing.

Practice — Forming opinions, comparisons, and connected sentences

  1. Add 제 생각에는 to the front of this sentence: 제주도가 아름다워요.
  2. Compare using 보다 더: 한국어 / 일본어 / 어렵다.
  3. Make a superlative sentence using 제일: 한국 음식 중에서 / 좋아하다.
  4. Connect with -지만: 한국어는 어렵다 + 재미있다.
  5. Connect with -고: 쓰기는 쉽다 + 말하기는 어렵다.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Back in the classroom after the trip, the instructor asks students to share their opinions about Korean and about their trip to Jeju.

선생님 라일라 씨, 한국어 공부가 어때요? Layla, how is studying Korean?
라일라 제 생각에는 한국어가 재미있어요. 그런데 발음이 제일 어려워요. 쓰기는 쉽고 말하기는 조금 어려워요. In my opinion, Korean is interesting. But pronunciation is the most difficult. Writing is easy, and speaking is a little difficult.
선생님 수진 씨는 어때요? How about you, Sujin?
수진 저도 라일라 씨 생각에 동의해요. 문법은 어렵지만 단어는 재미있어요. I agree with Layla's opinion too. Grammar is difficult, but vocabulary is interesting.
선생님 제주도 여행은 어땠어요? How was the Jeju Island trip?
라일라 정말 좋았어요! 제 생각에는 제주도가 부산보다 더 아름다워요. 경치가 정말 좋았어요. It was really good! In my opinion, Jeju is more beautiful than Busan. The scenery was really good.
수진 저도 그렇게 생각해요. 제주도 음식도 맛있고 사람들도 친절했어요. I think so too. The food in Jeju was delicious, and the people were kind too.
선생님 좋은 여행이었네요! 다음에는 어디에 가고 싶어요? That sounds like a great trip! Where do you want to go next time?
라일라 저는 부산에 가 보고 싶어요. 부산도 바다가 아름답다고 들었어요. I want to try going to Busan. I heard that Busan also has beautiful ocean views.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Softening opinions in group settings

In many South Korean classroom and group settings, opinions are often introduced gently, with a frame like 제 생각에는 or 제가 보기에는, rather than stated as flat assertions. This framing signals that the speaker is offering a personal view and leaves room for others to disagree politely. Beginning an opinion this way is a widely useful habit, though individual speakers and settings vary in how directly opinions are expressed.

Language Note — 제일 and 가장 are interchangeable in this chapter

Both 제일 and 가장 mean "most" and can be used interchangeably at this level. 제일 is somewhat more common in casual spoken Korean, while 가장 appears more often in writing and more formal speech. Later volumes will explore this register distinction in more detail; for now, either form is acceptable in this chapter's tasks.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Missing 보다 in a comparison

Error: 제주도가 부산 더 아름다워요.

Correction: 제주도가 부산보다 더 아름다워요. — the particle 보다 must attach to the noun being compared against; 더 alone does not mark "than."

Common Mistake — Using -지만 where -고 fits better

Error: 한국어는 재미있지만 유용해요. when the two ideas simply add together rather than contrast.

Correction: 한국어는 재미있고 유용해요. — use -고 for related or additive ideas, and reserve -지만 for genuine contrast.

Common Mistake — Ending -지만/-고 in the polite present form

Error: 어려워요지만 재미있어요.

Correction: 어렵지만 재미있어요. — like other connective endings, -지만 and -고 attach directly to the plain dictionary-form stem, never to the polite present form.

제 생각에는 한국어가 재미있는 언어예요. 문법은 영어보다 더 어렵지만 단어는 재미있어요. 저는 발음이 제일 어려워요. 그런데 쓰기는 쉽고 재미있어요. 저는 한국어를 계속 공부하고 싶어요.

Check that: 제 생각에는 begins an opinion sentence; a comparison with 보다 더 or 제일/가장 appears; one -지만 sentence and one -고 sentence appear; ideas are connected logically; speech level is consistent everyday polite throughout; particles and spacing are accurate.

Looking Ahead

지금 할 수 있는 것 — What you can do now

You can state a personal opinion, compare two or more things, and connect contrasting or related ideas within a single sentence using -지만 and -고.
