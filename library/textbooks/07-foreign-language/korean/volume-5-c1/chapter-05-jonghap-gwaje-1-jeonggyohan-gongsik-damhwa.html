Concluding Volume V, Unit I · Speak and Write Korean: Mastery, Precision, Rhetoric, and Specialized Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Produce a single polished formal Korean text that integrates register precision, argument structure, controlled density, and accurate mediation.
  2. Select and sustain a register appropriate to a stated audience and purpose across an extended piece of formal writing.
  3. Construct an argument that states a thesis, anticipates a counterargument, concedes a limited point, and qualifies its claim.
  4. Control the density of a formal text, expanding where clarity requires it and compressing where concision serves the audience.
  5. Mediate the same content faithfully across at least two forms — for example a formal written version and a spoken presentation for a different audience — without adding or omitting meaning.
  6. Evaluate a piece of formal Korean writing and revision against an explicit rubric, and revise it accordingly.

This is Unit I's synthesis chapter. It introduces no new particles, endings, speech levels, honorific systems, counters, or sound changes. Its task is to bring together, in a single sustained project, the register control of Chapter 1, the argument structure of Chapter 2, the concision and density control of Chapter 3, and the mediation and translation skills of Chapter 4.

전달 복습 — Delivery Review

Delivery Review: Formal Presentation Pacing

This chapter introduces no new pronunciation or phonology. It reviews and combines two delivery habits from earlier chapters: the argumentative pacing that separates a claim from its concession and counterargument, and the mediated-voice marking from Chapter 4 that signals a shift into paraphrase or summary. In an oral presentation that moves between a formal claim, a concession, and a mediated summary for a different audience, each shift benefits from a brief pause and a small, deliberate change in pacing so the listener can track which part of the structure is being delivered.

Delivery Note — Marking structural shifts in an extended formal presentation

Pause briefly before a concession such as 이 점은 인정하지만 and again before the counterargument that follows, so each move in the argument registers as a distinct step rather than blurring together. When shifting into a mediated summary for a different audience, apply the same flattened, evenly paced delivery introduced in Chapter 4, so listeners can distinguish the formal argument itself from the mediator's restatement of it.

통합 — Synthesizing Register, Argument, Density, and Mediation

Synthesis: Register, Argument Structure, Density, and Mediation

This chapter introduces no new grammar, particles, endings, speech levels, honorific systems, or connectors. Its purpose is to show how the four Unit I skills combine within a single polished formal text, using only structures already active through Chapter 4.

1. Register as the outer frame

Before drafting, a writer confirms the register the audience requires — for this scenario, formal written Korean for an internal opinion document, plain written style for its argument body, and a spoken register-transferred version for oral delivery. Every other decision in the text should remain consistent with that outer frame unless a deliberate register shift is marked, as in Chapter 4.

2. Argument as the internal skeleton

Inside that register, the text follows the argument architecture from Chapter 2: a clear thesis, at least one anticipated counterargument, a limited concession, and a qualification that keeps the claim honest without weakening its overall force.

  • 이 정책은 예산 삭감이 아니라 지출 순서의 재조정이므로 시행되어야 한다.This policy should be implemented because it reorders spending priorities rather than cutting budgets.thesis
  • 물론 일부 부서의 예산 집행 시기가 늦춰질 수 있다는 점은 인정하지만,Of course, it must be acknowledged that some departments' budget disbursement timing may be delayed, but —concession
  • 반면에, 이 지연은 사전 협의를 통해 충분히 완화될 수 있다.On the other hand, this delay can be sufficiently mitigated through prior consultation.counter to the concession, returning to the thesis
  • 다만, 그 협의 과정이 형식적으로 흐르지 않도록 충분한 시간이 확보되어야 한다.That said, sufficient time must be secured so that the consultation process does not become merely a formality.qualification

3. Density as pacing control

Within the argument skeleton, apply Chapter 3's density control: compress background information the reader already knows into a nominalized phrase, and expand a claim that needs careful support into a fuller clause. A synthesis document should not read as uniformly dense or uniformly expanded throughout; pacing should vary with the importance of each point.

  • 통근 시간 감소로 인한 피로도 완화는 이미 여러 부서에서 확인된 바 있다.The reduction in fatigue resulting from decreased commuting time has already been confirmed in several departments.compressed background, nominalized
  • 그러나 이 효과가 모든 부서에 동일하게 나타나는 것은 아니며, 협업이 많은 부서에서는 오히려 소통 지연이라는 부작용이 보고되고 있다는 점을 충분히 살펴볼 필요가 있다.However, this effect does not appear equally across all departments, and it is necessary to look carefully at the fact that departments with heavy collaboration are in fact reporting a side effect of communication delay.expanded, since this point carries real argumentative weight

4. Mediation as the delivery layer

Once the written document is complete, apply Chapter 4's mediation skills to produce the two delivery forms the scenario requires: a functional English translation or summary for colleagues who do not read Korean, and a register-transferred spoken version in Korean for an oral report to executives. Both must preserve the source's meaning without adding personal commentary that was not marked as the mediator's own.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Internal opinion documents in Korean organizations

    In many South Korean workplaces, an internal opinion document that will circulate to management is expected to state a clear position while still visibly acknowledging counterarguments and limitations, since a document that ignores likely objections can be read as insufficiently considered. Presenting a concession and a qualification is often not read as weakening the argument but as a sign that the writer has thought the issue through carefully.

    Regional and Generational Note — Written versus spoken delivery of the same position

    Some organizations expect a written opinion document to stand almost entirely on its own, with an oral report serving mainly to introduce it. Others expect the oral report to carry most of the persuasive weight, with the written document serving as backup detail. Learners preparing a synthesis task like this one should confirm which expectation applies in a given workplace rather than assuming one pattern is universal.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Common Mistake — Letting register slip across a long document

    Error: Beginning a document in formal written style and drifting into everyday spoken endings partway through, especially in later paragraphs written under time pressure.

    Correction: Reread the full draft specifically for register consistency as a separate pass from checking content, following Chapter 1's precision standards.

    Common Mistake — Concession without a genuine counter

    Error: Writing a concession such as 이 점은 인정하지만 and then simply repeating the original thesis without actually addressing the concern that was conceded.

    Correction: After a concession, state specifically why or how the concern is mitigated, following Chapter 2's argument architecture, rather than merely restating the thesis.

    Common Mistake — Letting the mediated summary drift from the source

    Error: Adding the mediator's own assessment of the policy into the English summary without marking it as personal commentary, so the audience cannot tell what the document argued versus what the mediator personally thinks.

    Correction: Keep the mediated summary faithful to the source document's claims, and clearly mark any added personal commentary, following Chapter 4's neutral-mediation standards.

    이 정책은 예산 삭감이 아니라 지출 순서의 재조정이므로 시행되어야 한다. 일부 부서의 예산 집행 시기가 늦춰질 수 있다는 점은 인정하지만, 충분한 사전 협의를 통해 그 영향은 상당 부분 완화될 수 있다. 다만, 협의가 형식적인 절차로 흐르지 않도록 충분한 시간이 확보되어야 한다.

    Mediated English summary: "This policy reorders spending priorities rather than cutting budgets and should move forward. Some departments may see delayed budget execution, but this can largely be mitigated through genuine prior consultation, provided that consultation is given adequate time rather than treated as a formality."

    Spoken register-transferred version: 이 정책, 예산을 줄이는 게 아니라 순서를 다시 정하는 거니까 진행하는 게 맞다고 봐요. 몇몇 부서는 좀 늦어질 수도 있는데, 미리 충분히 얘기하면 그 영향은 많이 줄일 수 있을 것 같아요. 다만 그 얘기가 그냥 형식적으로 끝나지 않게는 해야겠죠.

    Sample response: The formal document uses -습니다/-ㅂ니다-adjacent plain written endings and dense nominalized phrases suited to a written report, while the spoken version shifts to everyday polite -아요/어요 forms, shorter clauses, and more hedging language suited to a live team update, while both preserve the same thesis, concession, and qualification.
