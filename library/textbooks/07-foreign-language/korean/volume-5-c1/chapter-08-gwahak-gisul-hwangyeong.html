과학, 기술, 환경Science, Technology, and Environment

Continuing Volume V, Unit II · Speak and Write Korean: Mastery, Precision, Rhetoric, and Specialized Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Read and interpret Korean texts that explain a scientific finding, a technological development, or an environmental issue, including short reports, explainer articles, and public announcements.
  2. Use specialized science, technology, and environmental vocabulary accurately, including terms for research, process, mechanism, measurement, and impact.
  3. Explain a technical process or a cause-and-effect chain clearly and in the correct sequence for a general audience.
  4. Distinguish an established scientific finding from a preliminary or projected result within a technical text.
  5. Present a short explanation of a technology or an environmental issue, moving logically from mechanism to consequence.
  6. Apply the register, argument, density, and mediation judgment consolidated in Chapter 5, and the attribution and balance skills applied to economic and political discourse in Chapter 7, to technical and environmental explanation.

This chapter introduces no new particles, endings, speech levels, honorific systems, counters, or sound changes. It applies systems already active through Chapter 7 to a new domain: science, technology, and environmental discourse. All practice material in this chapter is fictional and general-purpose; no real companies, products, research institutions, or individuals are named or characterized.

Opening Scenario

  1. You will study specialized vocabulary for research, process, mechanism, measurement, and environmental impact.
  2. You will examine how Korean technical explanations and process descriptions are structured.
  3. You will read a short research summary and a brief explainer passage, distinguishing established findings from preliminary results.

발음 초점 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus: Loanwords and Technical Compounds

This chapter introduces no new sounds or sound changes. It reviews how already-active loanword pronunciation and Sino-Korean compound stress patterns apply to the frequent technical loanwords and dense compound nouns of this domain, such as 데이터, 시스템, and 에너지.

Pronunciation Note — Reading dense technical compounds

Long Sino-Korean technical compounds such as 재활용 소재 기술 or 온실가스 배출량 are read as a sequence of smaller meaningful chunks rather than as one unbroken string. Group the compound by its component nouns — for example, 재활용 / 소재 / 기술 — and place a very slight pause at each boundary rather than at random points, so that a listener can track which noun modifies which. Loanwords such as 데이터, 시스템, and 에너지 follow standard Korean syllable-block pronunciation; they are not read with English stress or vowel quality.

문법과 표현 — Grammar and Patterns

Grammar and Patterns: Explaining Process, Mechanism, and Status

This chapter introduces no new particles, endings, speech levels, or honorific systems. It applies structures already active through Chapter 7 — passive forms, causative-adjacent expressions, noun-modifying clauses, nominalization, and attribution — to the specific communicative demands of technical explanation, where sequencing a process correctly and marking the certainty of a finding are essential.

1. Describing mechanism with the passive

Technical explanation frequently describes what happens to a material, substance, or system rather than who acts on it. The already-active passive-form verbs such as -되다 and -지다 let a writer describe a mechanism from the perspective of the process itself.

  • 이 소재는 열을 가하면 원래 형태로 되돌아가도록 설계되었다.This material is designed to return to its original shape when heat is applied.passive 설계되다, process-centered description
  • 폐플라스틱은 세척과 분쇄 과정을 거쳐 새로운 소재로 재구성된다.Waste plastic is reconstituted into a new material after undergoing washing and grinding.passive 재구성되다, sequenced process

2. Sequencing a process clearly

Already-active sequence and result connectors — -(으)면, -아/어서, -고 나서, and the noun 단계 combined with ordinal reference — let a writer walk a reader through a process step by step without introducing new grammar.

  • 먼저 원료를 분류한 다음, 세척 단계를 거쳐 마지막으로 성형 단계로 넘어간다.First the raw material is sorted, then it goes through a washing stage, and finally moves to a shaping stage.explicit stage sequencing
  • 온도가 일정 수준 이상으로 올라가면 소재의 구조가 변형되기 시작한다.Once the temperature rises above a certain level, the material's structure begins to change.condition -(으)면 marking a mechanism trigger

3. Marking the certainty status of a finding

Public-facing science writing must distinguish an established finding from a preliminary or projected one. The already-active indirect quotation and evaluative framing patterns from Chapter 7 — -다고 밝히다, -다고 보다, and -(으)ㄹ 것으로 예상되다 — extend naturally to research status.

  • 연구진은 이번 실험을 통해 소재의 내구성이 기존 제품보다 높다는 것을 확인했다고 밝혔다.The research team stated that they confirmed, through this experiment, that the material's durability is higher than that of existing products.confirmed finding, attributed to research team
  • 다만 대량 생산에 적용했을 때도 같은 결과가 나올지는 추가 연구가 필요한 것으로 보인다.However, it appears that further research is needed to determine whether the same result will hold when applied to mass production.unresolved status marked with 것으로 보이다

4. Noun-modifying clauses identifying cause and effect

As in earlier chapters, noun-modifying clauses, already active since earlier volumes, precisely identify which cause produces which effect within a technical explanation.

  • 온실가스 배출을 줄이는 데 기여하는 기술로 주목받고 있다.It is drawing attention as a technology that contributes to reducing greenhouse gas emissions.noun-modifying clause identifying function
  • 기존 공정보다 에너지를 덜 사용하는 방식이 이번 연구의 핵심이다.A method that uses less energy than the existing process is the core of this research.noun-modifying clause defining the innovation
Building Block
[원리/공정 설명 (수동형, 단계 순서)] + [결과 (확인된 사실)] + [한계/추가 연구 (아직 불확실한 부분 표시)]
A clear technical explanation typically describes the mechanism or process using already-active passive and sequencing patterns, states what has actually been confirmed, then explicitly marks what remains uncertain or requires further research.
이 기술은 폐플라스틱을 분류, 세척, 재구성하는 단계를 거쳐 새로운 소재로 만드는 방식이며, 연구진은 내구성이 향상되었다고 밝혔지만 대량 생산 적용 가능성은 추가 연구가 필요한 것으로 보인다.
This technology is a method of creating a new material by sorting, washing, and reconstituting waste plastic, and while the research team stated that durability improved, the possibility of applying it to mass production appears to require further research.
Grammar Note — Precision over new structure

Every pattern in this section — passive forms, sequence connectors, indirect quotation, evaluative framing, and noun-modifying clauses — is already active from earlier chapters and volumes. What is new is the discipline of using these familiar structures to keep mechanism, confirmed result, and open question clearly distinct in a single technical passage.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Certainty and caution in South Korean science reporting

    South Korean science and technology reporting aimed at a general audience commonly distinguishes a laboratory-stage finding from a commercially proven result, and often notes explicitly when further verification or additional research is still needed. Explanatory writing that clearly separates what a research team has confirmed from what remains uncertain is commonly regarded as more credible than writing that presents early-stage findings as finished breakthroughs.

    Institutional Note — Variation across research institutions and outlets

    Different research institutions, companies, and media outlets in South Korea may use somewhat different conventions for describing research status, citing sources, and framing environmental claims. The vocabulary and patterns in this chapter reflect general, broadly applicable usage for reading and producing clear technical explanations; learners engaging with a specific institution's or outlet's coverage should remain attentive to that source's own framing choices rather than assuming all sources describe research status identically.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Common Mistake — Presenting a preliminary finding as a finished breakthrough

    Error: Writing a lab-stage or early result as though it were already commercially proven, such as 이 기술로 문제가 다 해결됐다 stated flatly, when the source text presents this only as a laboratory result that still requires further research.

    Correction: State the confirmed result precisely, then explicitly mark what remains unverified, as in 실험실 환경에서는 내구성이 향상된 것으로 확인되었으나, 대량 생산 적용 여부는 추가 연구가 필요하다.

    Common Mistake — Skipping steps in a process explanation

    Error: Summarizing a multi-stage technical process as a single vague action, so the reader cannot follow how the mechanism actually works.

    Correction: Use already-active sequence connectors and stage vocabulary, as in 먼저 ... 다음 ... 마지막으로, to walk the reader through each stage in order.

    Common Mistake — Naming real companies, institutions, or individuals

    Error: Substituting a real company, research institution, or individual's name into a practice sentence or exercise, or attributing a specific real-world finding to a named real source.

    Correction: Keep practice material generic, using terms such as 연구진, 전문가들, and 한 연구팀 without naming a real organization or person, consistent with this chapter's fictional practice scenarios.

    Select a fictional technology or environmental issue, either the recycling material used throughout this chapter or a comparable fictional issue of your own choosing. Produce a complete project with three parts.

    • Explanatory brief (Korean): A formal but accessible summary (100–150 words) of the technology or issue, its mechanism, and its significance.
    • Result/uncertainty breakdown: A short list separating which statements in your brief are confirmed results and which are projections or unresolved questions, each supported by one sentence from your brief.
    • Limitation note: A short note (2–3 sentences) naming at least one limitation or open question that requires further research or verification.

    한 연구진이 폐플라스틱을 분류, 세척, 재구성하는 단계를 거쳐 내구성이 높은 새로운 소재를 만드는 기술을 개발했다고 밝혔다. 실험실 환경에서는 내구성이 향상된 것으로 확인되었으나, 대량 생산에 적용할 수 있을지는 추가 연구가 필요한 것으로 보인다.

    Confirmed results: improved durability confirmed in lab experiments. Projections/unresolved questions: whether the same result will hold in mass production; whether the technology will meaningfully reduce emissions once commercialized.

    Section E — Sample discussion

    Sample response: A clear technical explanation sequences the mechanism using already-active passive and connector patterns, states the confirmed result plainly, and then explicitly marks any unresolved question using a pattern such as 추가 연구가 필요한 것으로 보인다, without presenting the finding as more settled than the source material supports.
