Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe the stylistic character of a piece of prose — its tone, rhythm, and diction — using precise Korean stylistic vocabulary.
  2. Identify and interpret figurative language, including metaphor and simile, in a literary-adjacent Korean passage.
  3. Recognize intertextual reference — where one text echoes, responds to, or reworks an idea from another — and describe that relationship in Korean.
  4. Distinguish a literal reading of a passage from an interpretive or symbolic reading, marking each clearly.
  5. Analyze how word choice and sentence rhythm shape a reader's impression of a text.
  6. Write a short interpretive commentary on a fictional literary-adjacent passage, integrating attribution and critique language from Chapter 11.

This chapter introduces two major new productive systems: stylistic and figurative-language description, and intertextual/interpretive commentary. All literary passages in this chapter are original, fictional, general-purpose instructional texts; no real author, title, or published work is quoted, named, or excerpted.

문법 — Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Grammar and Sentence Patterns: Style, Figuration, and Intertextual Commentary

This chapter introduces two closely related productive systems: (1) stylistic and figurative description — naming a stylistic feature and explaining the effect it creates, and identifying metaphor with 은유 versus simile with 처럼/같이; and (2) intertextual and interpretive commentary — describing how a text evokes or responds to another idea, and distinguishing literal from symbolic reading using 표면적으로는 ~지만 상징적으로는 ~.

1. Describing style: naming a feature and its effect

A stylistic observation names a concrete feature of the text — diction, sentence rhythm, tone — and then states the effect that feature produces on the reader, rather than offering a vague impression alone.

  • 이 글은 짧은 문장을 반복해서 사용함으로써 절제된 문체를 만들어 낸다.This piece creates a restrained style by repeatedly using short sentences.feature (짧은 문장 반복) + 문체를 만들어 내다 (effect)
  • 담담한 어조는 슬픈 사건을 오히려 더 깊은 여운으로 남긴다.The composed tone leaves the sad event with an even deeper resonance.어조 + 여운으로 남기다, effect-stating frame
  • 대구를 이루는 문장 구조는 두 인물의 대조를 강조한다.The parallel sentence structure emphasizes the contrast between the two characters.대구 + 강조하다

2. Identifying figurative language: metaphor, simile, and symbol

Distinguish an implicit comparison (은유) from an explicit one marked with 처럼 or 같이 (직유), and describe what a recurring image seems to symbolize (상징하다).

  • "시간은 강물이다"라는 표현은 은유이다.The expression "time is a river" is a metaphor.quoted fictional phrase + 은유이다, no 처럼/같이
  • "그의 목소리는 마른 나뭇잎처럼 부서졌다"는 직유를 사용한 표현이다."His voice broke like a dry leaf" is an expression that uses a simile.직유 + 처럼, explicit comparison marker
  • 이 글에서 창문은 단절과 그리움을 상징한다.In this piece, the window symbolizes separation and longing.상징하다 + abstract nouns as symbolized meaning

3. Literal versus symbolic reading, and intertextual commentary

Mark clearly which reading is literal and which is symbolic, and describe how a passage evokes or reworks an idea found elsewhere.

  • 표면적으로는 집을 떠나는 이야기이지만, 상징적으로는 정체성을 찾아가는 과정을 그린다.On the surface it is a story about leaving home, but symbolically it depicts a process of searching for identity.표면적으로는 ~지만 상징적으로는 ~, contrastive dual reading
  • 이 대목은 오래된 여행 이야기의 구조를 떠올리게 한다.This passage evokes the structure of an old travel narrative.~을/를 떠올리게 하다, intertextual echo
  • 작가는 이전 세대의 상실감에 응답하듯 비슷한 이미지를 되짚는다.The writer revisits a similar image, as if responding to the previous generation's sense of loss.~에 응답하다 + 되짚다, intertextual response
Building Block
[문체 관찰: 특징 + 효과] + [비유 식별: 은유/직유/상징] + [표면적 의미 vs 상징적 의미] + [상호텍스트적 연결]
An interpretive commentary names a concrete stylistic feature and its effect, identifies specific figurative language and states what it does, distinguishes the literal reading from the symbolic reading, and — where relevant — describes how the passage evokes or responds to another text or idea.
이 글은 짧고 절제된 문장을 반복하여 담담한 어조를 만들어 낸다. "시간은 강물이다"라는 은유는 되돌릴 수 없는 흐름을 나타낸다. 표면적으로는 한 인물이 고향을 떠나는 이야기이지만, 상징적으로는 상실과 정체성 찾기의 과정을 그린다. 이러한 이미지는 이전 세대의 이주 이야기를 떠올리게 하며, 그 상실감에 조용히 응답한다.
This piece repeats short, restrained sentences to create a composed tone. The metaphor "time is a river" represents an irreversible flow. On the surface it is a story of a character leaving their hometown, but symbolically it depicts a process of loss and searching for identity. This imagery evokes an earlier generation's migration narrative and quietly responds to that sense of loss.
Grammar Note — Keeping description, identification, and interpretation separate

A strong interpretive commentary keeps three moves distinct: naming a stylistic feature (문체 관찰), identifying a specific figure of speech (은유/직유/상징 식별), and offering an interpretation of meaning (해석). Do not skip directly to interpretation without first pointing to the concrete textual feature that supports it — a reader should be able to trace your interpretation back to a specific word, image, or sentence pattern.

Common Mistake — Interpreting without pointing to a textual feature

Error: 이 글은 슬프다. (a bare impression, with no textual feature named)

Correction: 짧고 절제된 문장이 슬픔을 직접 말하지 않으면서도 깊은 여운을 남긴다. Name the specific stylistic feature that produces the impression, rather than stating the impression alone.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Understatement and restraint in Korean literary prose

    Contemporary Korean literary and literary-adjacent prose often favors a restrained, understated presentation of strong emotion, letting concrete images and spare sentence structure carry feeling rather than stating it directly. Recognizing this stylistic tendency helps learners avoid mistaking restraint for a lack of emotional content.

    Institutional Note — Seminar discussion of a text

    Graduate literature and humanities seminars, as modeled in this chapter's dialogue, commonly proceed by asking participants to point to a specific textual feature before offering an interpretation, rather than accepting an unsupported impression. Learners producing interpretive commentary should expect this same expectation of textually grounded reading.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Common Mistake — Confusing 은유 and 직유

    Error: Labeling "그의 목소리는 마른 나뭇잎처럼 부서졌다" as 은유, despite the explicit comparison marker 처럼.

    Correction: A comparison marked with 처럼 or 같이 is 직유 (simile); an implicit comparison with no such marker, such as 시간은 강물이다, is 은유 (metaphor).

    Common Mistake — Treating the symbolic reading as the only correct reading

    Error: Stating a symbolic interpretation as the single fixed meaning of a passage, as if no other reading were possible.

    Correction: Present the literal reading first, then offer a symbolic reading as one plausible interpretation, using language such as 상징적으로 볼 수 있다 or ~로 해석될 수 있다 rather than an absolute claim.

    Common Mistake — Naming an intertextual echo without specifying the connection

    Error: 이 글은 다른 이야기 같다. (a vague claim of resemblance with no specific point of connection)

    Correction: 이 글의 떠남과 상실의 이미지는 이전 세대의 이주 이야기를 떠올리게 한다. Name the specific image, theme, or structure that creates the intertextual echo.
