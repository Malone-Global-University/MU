Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State a clear thesis in formal Korean and maintain it consistently across an extended passage of speech or writing.
  2. Build a structured argument using claim, support, concession, and counterargument in a logical sequence.
  3. Introduce and respond to a counterargument without abandoning your own position or sounding dismissive of the opposing view.
  4. Qualify claims precisely, distinguishing strong assertions from cautious or partial ones using already-active modality and hedging patterns.
  5. Signal authorial stance consistently across a passage, so that a reader or listener can identify your position even where it is not stated bluntly.
  6. Read and analyze the structure of a short argumentative essay, identifying its thesis, support, concession, and conclusion.

This chapter continues Unit I: 정확성, 문체, 그리고 매개 (Precision, Style, and Mediation). It builds directly on Chapter 1's register control and introduces no new endings, particles, or predicate systems. Its new material is structural and rhetorical: how to organize already-known Korean into a sustained, persuasive argument.

Opening Scenario

수아는 대학원 세미나에서 원격 근무 확대에 관한 짧은 논평을 발표해야 한다. 처음 쓴 원고는 여러 사실을 나열했지만, 지도 교수님은 이렇게 지적한다: "사실은 잘 모아져 있는데, 주장이 어디 있는지 잘 안 보여요. 이 글이 결국 무엇을 주장하고 싶은지, 그리고 반대 의견은 어떻게 다루고 있는지 명확하게 드러나야 해요." 수아는 원고를 다시 구성하여 분명한 주장, 근거, 예상되는 반론, 그리고 그 반론에 대한 대응을 갖춘 논증으로 고쳐 쓰기로 한다.

  1. You will study a model exchange in which a speaker builds and defends a position under gentle challenge.
  2. You will read a short argumentative essay and map its thesis, support, concession, and conclusion.
  3. You will listen to two short arguments and identify where each speaker concedes a point versus where each holds firm.
  4. You will revise a piece of your own writing to add a clear thesis, a concession, and a counterargument response.

발음과 억양 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation and Delivery Focus: Marking Structure Aloud

In spoken argument, listeners rely partly on delivery to track structure: where a concession begins, where the speaker pivots back, and where the conclusion starts. This chapter introduces no new phonology; it reviews how experienced speakers use pause and pitch to mark these structural boundaries.

Pronunciation Note — Marking concession and pivot

A short pause and a slight drop in pitch often precede a concession phrase such as 물론, signaling that the speaker is about to grant something. A brief pause and a return to a firmer, more level pitch typically mark the pivot word 그러나 that follows, signaling the speaker's return to the main claim. These cues help a listener track the argument's shape in real time, independent of the words themselves.

Practice: read the following two-sentence sequence aloud, pausing briefly before 물론 and again before 그러나, and notice how the pauses help the concession-and-pivot structure land clearly for a listener.

  • 물론 재택근무가 모든 직무에 적합한 것은 아니다. 그러나 그렇다고 해서 전면적으로 도입을 반대할 이유는 없다.Of course, remote work is not suitable for every job. But that is not a reason to oppose its adoption wholesale.
논증 구조 — Argument Architecture

Argument Architecture: Thesis, Concession, Counterargument, Qualification, and Stance

This chapter's principal system is discourse-level organization rather than new sentence-level grammar. Every ending, connector, and modality pattern below is already active from earlier volumes; what is new is arranging them into a five-part structure that a formal Korean argument typically follows.

1. Stating a thesis clearly

A strong formal argument opens by stating its position early and unambiguously, using already-active modality patterns rather than a blunt bare assertion.

  • 본고에서는 재택근무 확대가 장기적으로 조직의 생산성을 높인다고 주장하고자 한다.This essay argues that the expansion of remote work raises organizational productivity in the long term.

2. Supporting the thesis with grounds

Support follows the thesis and is explicitly introduced as evidence rather than left implicit, using connectors and nominalized structures already active from Volume IV.

  • 그 근거로는 통근 시간 감소로 인한 피로도 완화와 업무 몰입도 향상을 들 수 있다.As grounds for this, one can point to reduced fatigue from shorter commutes and improved work engagement.

3. Conceding a point genuinely

A mature argument acknowledges a real limitation or valid opposing point before returning to its own claim. The concession must sound genuine, not performative, and should identify a specific limitation rather than a vague gesture.

  • 물론 협업이 중요한 직무에서는 대면 근무의 효율이 더 높다는 것도 사실이다.Of course, it is also true that in jobs where collaboration is essential, in-person work is more efficient.

4. Pivoting back with a counterargument response

After conceding, the argument pivots back, narrowing the concession's scope rather than abandoning the thesis. The pivot connector 그러나 그렇다고 해서 ~는 것은 아니다 explicitly limits how far the concession extends.

  • 그러나 그렇다고 해서 모든 직무에 재택근무가 부적합하다는 것은 아니다.But that does not mean remote work is unsuitable for every job.

5. Qualifying claims precisely

Precise argument avoids both overclaiming and unnecessary vagueness. Scope-limiting expressions such as 적어도 ~에 있어서는 and 부분적으로, paired with already-active evidential hedges such as -는 것으로 보인다, let a writer commit to a claim at exactly the strength the evidence supports.

  • 적어도 정형화된 업무에 있어서는 재택근무의 효과가 뚜렷한 것으로 보인다.At least with respect to standardized tasks, the effect of remote work appears clear.

Grammar Note — Structure, not new endings

Every connector, ending, and modality expression in this chapter's examples — -다고 주장하다, -는 것도 사실이다, -는 것은 아니다, -는 것으로 보인다 — is already active from earlier volumes. The new skill is sequencing them so that a listener or reader can follow the shape of the argument: where it commits, where it concedes, and where it holds firm.

Model Exchange: Defending a Position Under Challenge

Reading: An Argumentative Essay

Read the following short argumentative paragraph, written for this course as general-purpose instructional material. It follows the five-part structure introduced in this chapter's grammar section.

재택근무 확대에 대한 소고 (교육용 예시)

본고에서는 재택근무 확대가 장기적으로 조직의 생산성을 높인다고 주장하고자 한다. 그 근거로는 통근 시간 감소로 인한 피로도 완화와 업무 몰입도 향상을 들 수 있다. 물론 협업이 중요한 직무에서는 대면 근무의 효율이 더 높다는 것도 사실이다. 그러나 그렇다고 해서 모든 직무에 재택근무가 부적합하다는 것은 아니다. 적어도 정형화된 업무에 있어서는 재택근무의 효과가 뚜렷한 것으로 보인다. 이상의 논의를 종합하면, 직무의 성격에 따라 근무 방식을 유연하게 조정하는 것이 바람직하다고 할 수 있다.

분석 과제 — Analysis task:

  1. Underline or list the single sentence that states the essay's thesis.
  2. Identify the sentence that introduces supporting grounds, and name the two pieces of evidence it offers.
  3. Identify the concession sentence and explain what specific limitation it grants.
  4. Identify the pivot sentence and explain how its scope differs from a simple repetition of the thesis.
  5. What does the qualifying phrase in the fifth sentence add that the thesis alone did not specify?

말하기 연습 — Speaking Practice

Speaking Practice

1단계 — 짝 활동 — Structured position and challenge

With a partner, choose a familiar issue (for example, whether university exams should be open-book). State a clear thesis using -다고 주장하고자 한다 or -다고 봅니다, then have your partner raise one genuine objection. Respond by conceding a real point with 물론 ~는 것도 사실이다, then pivot back with 그러나 그렇다고 해서 ~는 것은 아니다.

2단계 — 한정하기 — Practicing precise qualification

Choose one of your claims from the previous activity. Restate it twice: once as an unqualified, overclaimed version, and once using 적어도 ~에 있어서는 or 부분적으로 to narrow it to what your evidence actually supports. Explain aloud, in Korean, why the qualified version is more defensible.

혼자 하는 연습 — Solo alternative

Record yourself presenting a short five-part argument (thesis, support, concession, pivot, conclusion) on a topic of your choice. Listen back and mark where each of the five parts occurs.

쓰기 워크숍 — Writing Workshop

Writing Workshop: Building a Five-Part Argument

Write a short argumentative paragraph (5–7 sentences) on a familiar issue of your choice, following the five-part structure introduced in this chapter's grammar section.

  • 주장 쓰기. State your thesis clearly in the first sentence, using -다고 주장하고자 한다 or an equivalent formal frame.
  • 근거 제시하기. Add one sentence introducing at least two specific pieces of supporting evidence.
  • 양보하기. Add a genuine concession using 물론 ~는 것도 사실이다, granting a real limitation rather than a token one.
  • 반박하고 한정하기. Pivot back using 그러나 그렇다고 해서 ~는 것은 아니다, and add a precise qualification using 적어도 ~에 있어서는 or 부분적으로.
  • 결론 쓰기. Close with a conclusion sentence using 이상의 논의를 종합하면 that restates your position in light of the concession.
    • Unstructured draft 저는 온라인 수업이 좋다고 생각해요. 시간을 아낄 수 있어요. 물론 나쁜 점도 있긴 해요.

    체크리스트 — Checklist:

    • The paragraph states a single, unambiguous thesis in its first sentence.
    • At least two specific pieces of supporting evidence are named, not merely implied.
    • The concession sentence grants a genuine, specific limitation.
    • The pivot sentence narrows the concession's scope rather than repeating the thesis unchanged.
    • The qualifying phrase limits the claim to what the evidence actually supports.
    • Register remains consistently formal throughout; no unmarked mixing with everyday register.
    • Spacing, particles, and Hangeul spelling are accurate, with no romanization.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Concession as a mark of rigor, not weakness

    In South Korean academic and professional argumentation, openly conceding a limitation before pivoting back to one's position is often read as a sign of careful, credible reasoning rather than as backing down. A speaker or writer who acknowledges no counterpoint at all can come across as unpersuasive or naive about the complexity of the issue, especially in academic seminars and professional review settings.

    Regional and Generational Note — Directness in debate varies by setting

    How directly a counterargument is voiced, and how much softening language surrounds it, varies by institution, field, and generation. Some academic and professional communities favor blunter, faster-paced exchange of counterarguments; others favor more extensively hedged and softened disagreement. This chapter's patterns represent a broadly conventional formal register; learners should remain observant of the norms in any specific setting.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Common Mistake — Conceding away the entire thesis

    Error: Using 물론 ~는 것도 사실이다 to grant a point so broad that it undermines the original claim entirely, leaving the pivot sentence with nothing left to defend.

    Correction: Concede a specific, narrow limitation, and use the pivot sentence to show exactly why that limitation does not defeat the broader thesis.

    Common Mistake — Skipping the pivot after a concession

    Error: Ending a paragraph immediately after a concession sentence, leaving the reader unsure whether the writer still holds the original position.

    Correction: Always follow a concession with an explicit pivot, such as 그러나 그렇다고 해서 ~는 것은 아니다, that restates how the thesis survives the concession.

    Common Mistake — Overclaiming instead of qualifying

    Error: Stating a claim as universally true (전면적으로) when the evidence only supports it in a limited domain, which weakens credibility once a reader notices the mismatch.

    Correction: Use scope-limiting expressions such as 적어도 ~에 있어서는 or 부분적으로 to match the claim's strength to the actual evidence.

    Section D — Sample five-part argument

    저는 온라인 수업이 좋다고 생각해요. 시간을 아낄 수 있어요. 물론 나쁜 점도 있긴 해요.

    본고에서는 온라인 수업 확대가 학습자의 시간 활용도를 높인다고 주장하고자 한다. 그 근거로는 이동 시간 절약과 반복 학습의 용이성을 들 수 있다. 물론 대면 수업에 비해 상호작용이 줄어든다는 것도 사실이다. 그러나 그렇다고 해서 온라인 수업의 학습 효과가 낮다는 것은 아니다. 적어도 이론 중심의 과목에 있어서는 온라인 수업의 효과가 뚜렷한 것으로 보인다.

    Section E — Sample qualification practice

    Sample response: An overclaimed version might state 온라인 수업은 모든 과목에서 더 효과적이다, which is not defensible. A qualified version, 적어도 이론 중심의 과목에 있어서는 온라인 수업의 효과가 뚜렷하다, is more defensible because it limits the claim to the domain the evidence actually supports.

    Looking Ahead

    지금 할 수 있는 것 — What you can do now

    You can state a clear thesis, support it with specific grounds, concede a genuine limitation without abandoning your position, pivot back with a precisely scoped response, qualify claims to match your evidence, and defend a position aloud under a spoken challenge.
