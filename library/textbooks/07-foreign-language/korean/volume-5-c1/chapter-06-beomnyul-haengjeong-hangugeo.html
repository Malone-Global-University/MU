법률·행정 한국어Legal and Administrative Korean

Opening Volume V, Unit II · Speak and Write Korean: Mastery, Precision, Rhetoric, and Specialized Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Read and interpret regulatory, procedural, and institutional Korean texts, including notices, application guidelines, and clauses stating rights and obligations.
  2. Use specialized legal and administrative vocabulary accurately, including terms for rights, duties, procedures, deadlines, penalties, and institutional bodies.
  3. Explain a procedure or requirement in formal Korean, sequencing steps and conditions clearly for a reader who must comply with them.
  4. Distinguish rights language from obligation language, and identify which party bears responsibility in a given clause.
  5. Complete and explain institutional forms, applications, and formal requests using accurate register and required conventions.
  6. Apply the register, argument, density, and mediation judgment consolidated in Chapter 5 to the legal and administrative domain.

This chapter introduces no new particles, endings, speech levels, honorific systems, counters, or sound changes. It applies systems already active through Chapter 5 to a new domain: legal and administrative Korean.

발음 초점 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus: Reading Formal Compounds Aloud

This chapter introduces no new sounds or sound changes. It reviews how already-active liaison and tensification patterns apply to the dense Sino-Korean compounds common in legal and administrative vocabulary, and it develops pacing appropriate to reading a regulation aloud clearly.

Pronunciation Note — Reading clause numbers and dense compounds

Regulatory text is often organized by numbered articles, such as 제1조 ("Article 1") or 제2항 ("Paragraph 2"). When reading these aloud, pronounce the number using Sino-Korean numerals and pause briefly after the clause number before continuing into the clause itself, so the listener can register the reference point. Dense compounds such as 불이행 or 과태료 should be read as single prosodic units rather than broken artificially into separate words.

문법과 표현 — Grammar and Patterns

Grammar and Patterns: Stating Rights, Obligations, and Procedures

This chapter introduces no new particles, endings, speech levels, or honorific systems. It applies structures already active through Chapter 5 — passive forms, conditionals, formal written endings, and noun-modifying clauses — to the specific communicative demands of legal and administrative Korean.

1. Stating an obligation formally

Obligation in regulatory Korean typically uses the already-active pattern -아/어야 하다, often in its more formal variant -아/어야 한다, combined with plain written style.

  • 신청자는 신청서를 기한 내에 제출해야 한다.The applicant must submit the application form within the deadline.obligation, plain written style
  • 담당 부서는 신청서를 접수한 날로부터 5일 이내에 심사 결과를 통보해야 한다.The responsible department must notify the review result within five days of receiving the application.obligation, institutional subject

2. Stating a right or entitlement

Rights are typically expressed with -(으)ㄹ 수 있다 or with the noun 권리 directly, marking who holds the right and under what condition.

  • 직원은 규정에 따라 재택근무를 신청할 수 있다.Employees may apply for remote work in accordance with the regulation.entitlement, -(으)ㄹ 수 있다
  • 신청이 반려된 직원은 그 사유에 대해 이의를 제기할 권리가 있다.An employee whose application has been rejected has the right to raise an objection regarding the reason.explicit right, noun-modifying clause identifying the rights-holder

3. Conditional clauses in regulatory text

Regulatory Korean frequently states a condition before its consequence, using the already-active connector -(으)ㄹ 경우 ("in the case that") alongside -(으)면. 경우 is preferred in formal regulatory writing because it reads as more precise and institutional than the everyday -(으)면 alone.

  • 규정을 위반할 경우, 소속 부서장에게 그 사실이 통보된다.In the case that the regulation is violated, the fact is notified to the department head.condition + passive consequence
  • 기한 내에 서류를 제출하지 않을 경우, 신청은 자동으로 반려된다.If the documents are not submitted within the deadline, the application is automatically rejected.negative condition + institutional consequence

4. Noun-modifying clauses identifying parties and categories

Legal and administrative Korean relies heavily on noun-modifying clauses, already active since earlier volumes, to define precisely which party or category a clause addresses.

  • 본 규정이 적용되는 대상자는 입사 1년 이상의 정규직 직원이다.The eligible parties to whom this regulation applies are regular employees with one year or more of service.noun-modifying clause defining scope
  • 신청서를 제출한 직원은 심사 결과를 이메일로 통보받는다.Employees who have submitted an application receive the review result by email.noun-modifying clause identifying who receives an action
Building Block
[대상자/조건] + [의무 -아/어야 한다 / 권리 -(으)ㄹ 수 있다] + [절차 또는 결과]
A regulatory sentence typically names its subject or condition first, states an obligation or right using already-active endings, then closes with the procedural or institutional consequence.
재택근무를 신청하려는 직원은 신청서를 제출해야 하며, 담당 부서는 이를 5일 이내에 심사한다.
An employee wishing to apply for remote work must submit an application form, and the responsible department reviews it within five days.
Grammar Note — Precision over new structure

Every ending in this section — -아/어야 하다, -(으)ㄹ 수 있다, -(으)ㄹ 경우, passive forms, and noun-modifying clauses — is already active from earlier chapters and volumes. What is new is the density and precision with which these familiar structures identify parties, conditions, and consequences in institutional writing.

Model Dialogue: Explaining a Regulation

수아가 동료 직원에게 개정된 재택근무 규정을 설명하는 장면 (교육용 예시)

동료 이번에 재택근무 규정이 바뀌었다고 들었는데, 저도 신청할 수 있나요?I heard the remote work regulation changed — can I apply too?
수아 네, 입사 1년 이상의 정규직 직원이 대상자예요. 자격 요건이 되시면 신청서를 작성해서 담당 부서에 제출하시면 돼요.Yes, the eligible parties are regular employees with one year or more of service. If you meet the eligibility requirement, you fill out the application form and submit it to the responsible department.
동료 기한이 따로 있나요?Is there a specific deadline?
수아 네, 매월 마지막 주 금요일까지 제출해야 다음 달부터 적용돼요. 기한을 넘기면 신청이 반려될 수 있으니 주의하세요.Yes, you must submit by the last Friday of each month for it to apply starting the following month. If you miss the deadline, the application may be rejected, so please be careful.
동료 만약 신청이 반려되면 어떻게 해야 하나요?If the application gets rejected, what should I do?
수아 반려 사유에 대해 이의를 제기할 권리가 있어요. 인사팀에 문의하시면 자세히 안내해 드릴 거예요.You have the right to raise an objection regarding the reason for rejection. If you inquire with HR, they'll guide you through the details.

분석 — Analysis: Identify where 수아 states an eligibility condition, a deadline as an obligation, a consequence of missing the deadline, and a right available to the employee.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Regulatory language in South Korean workplaces

    South Korean workplace regulations are commonly organized into numbered articles (제1조, 제2조, and so on), a structural convention borrowed from formal legal drafting. Employees are generally expected to be able to locate the specific article relevant to a question rather than relying only on a general summary, so plain-language explanations that still reference the relevant article number are often considered more helpful and more credible.

    Regional and Institutional Note — Variation in administrative procedure language

    Government agencies, private companies, and universities in South Korea may each have their own house style for regulatory and procedural documents, with some variation in preferred formal vocabulary and document formatting. The vocabulary and patterns in this chapter reflect common, broadly applicable usage; learners working with a specific institution's documents should confirm that institution's own conventions rather than assuming one pattern is universal.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Common Mistake — Confusing rights and obligations

    Error: Using -아/어야 하다 to describe something that is actually optional, or using -(으)ㄹ 수 있다 to describe something that is actually mandatory, so the reader cannot tell whether an action is required or merely permitted.

    Correction: Confirm, from the source regulation, whether a clause describes a duty or an entitlement before choosing the ending, and keep the two forms distinct throughout a document.

    Common Mistake — Omitting the party or condition

    Error: Stating a rule without specifying who it applies to, such as writing 신청서를 제출해야 한다 alone when the source regulation restricts the rule to a specific category of employee.

    Correction: Use a noun-modifying clause or an explicit subject to name the party or condition precisely, as in 입사 1년 이상의 정규직 직원은 신청서를 제출해야 한다.

    Common Mistake — Dropping the consequence clause

    Error: Summarizing a regulation's procedure and deadline but omitting what happens if the deadline or requirement is not met, leaving the reader without practical guidance.

    Correction: Always state the consequence, using a conditional clause such as -(으)ㄹ 경우, so the explanation remains practically useful.
