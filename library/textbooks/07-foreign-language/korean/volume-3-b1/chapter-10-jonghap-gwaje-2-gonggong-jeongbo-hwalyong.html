종합 과제 2: 공공 정보 활용Unit II Assessment: Using Public Information

Consolidating news, technology, institutions, and formal correspondence · Speak and Write Korean: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Read and interpret a short news-style report, an institutional notice, and a formal email using the systems activated across Chapters 6 through 9.
  2. Recognize source framing, formal endings, and passive/causative forms in public-information texts without being required to produce them independently.
  3. Understand and respond appropriately to institutional procedures and requirements described in a semi-authentic notice.
  4. Write a complete formal email that combines formal email structure, honorific address, the honorific marker -(으)시-, and a Chapter 8 formal request pattern.
  5. Speak about a public-information topic using formal spoken register, restating a written inquiry aloud as if addressing an institution directly.
  6. Demonstrate control of formal -습니다/-습니까 style across listening, reading, writing, and speaking without mixing it with everyday polite endings.
  7. Identify what remains receptive or specialized at this stage (such as full productive control of the passive and causative systems) and distinguish it from what is assessable now.

This chapter is the Unit II assessment. It introduces no major new productive system. It consolidates the news style, source framing, digital vocabulary, institutional procedures, and formal email systems from Chapters 6 through 9 into one integrated task sequence.

Opening Scenario

지영, 민준, and 라일라 meet at the community center to compare what they have each dealt with over the past several chapters — reading news notices, filling out forms, sending inquiries to offices, and waiting for replies. Their instructor asks them to put it all together: read a short public notice about a new online civil-service system, understand what it requires, and then write and speak about a real inquiry connected to it, using everything from Chapters 6 through 9 without introducing anything new.

  1. You will read a news-style notice, a set of institutional instructions, and a formal email exchange, and answer questions that combine reading and listening evidence.
  2. You will write a complete formal email that inquires about or requests something related to the notice, using formal email structure, honorific address, -(으)시-, and a Chapter 8 request pattern.
  3. You will speak about your inquiry in formal register, as if addressing the institution directly.

발음 — Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review — -(으)시- and formal endings in connected reading

Before the assessment tasks, review the pronunciation of the honorific marker -(으)시- after consonant-final and vowel-final stems, and the smooth batchim-to-batchim connection in longer formal closings such as 확인 부탁드립니다 and 회신해 주시기 바랍니다. Read the Integrated Reading Task passages aloud once at a slow pace before answering the comprehension questions, paying attention to sentence-final formal intonation.

Grammar Consolidation

This chapter introduces no new grammar. It consolidates the following systems from Chapters 6 through 9 and checks whether learners can recognize and, where scheduled, produce them accurately.

1. Formal -습니다/-습니까 Style (Consolidation)

Grammar Review — Consistent formal style across a text

A well-formed formal Korean text — a news notice, an institutional instruction, or an email — maintains -습니다/-습니까 style consistently from beginning to end. Mixing in everyday polite -아요/어요 endings within the same formal text is a register error, not a stylistic choice.

2. Passive and Causative Forms (Recognition)

Grammar Review — Recognizing 되다-type passive forms

Forms such as 접수되다 ("to be received"), 처리되다 ("to be processed"), and 지연되다 ("to be delayed") describe an action happening to the subject rather than an action the subject performs. In institutional notices and formal emails, these forms are common because they describe procedures without naming who specifically performs each action.

  • 신청서가 접수되었습니다. — "The application has been received."
  • 서류 확인이 지연되고 있습니다. — "Document confirmation is being delayed."

You are expected to recognize and understand these forms in reading and listening. Independent production of new passive or causative forms beyond the specific verbs already activated (접수되다, 처리되다, 지연되다) is not assessed in this chapter.

3. Formal Requests and Honorific Marking (Assessable)

Grammar Review — Combining -(으)시- with formal request patterns

The Writing Assessment in this chapter requires accurate, independent production of:

  • the honorific marker -(으)시- with common verbs when the subject is the reader;
  • at least one honorific address term (귀하, 선생님, or title + 님);
  • at least one formal request pattern from Chapter 8 (-아/어 주시겠습니까? or -아/어 주시기 바랍니다); and
  • the full formal email frame from Chapter 9.
  • 신청서가 접수된 후에 담당자님께서 확인해 주시겠습니까? Once the application has been received, could the person in charge please check it? 접수되다 (passive, recognition) + 담당자님 + 께서 (honorific subject); 확인하다 + -아/어 주시겠습니까 (formal request)

This sentence type shows how a recognition-level passive form and an assessable honorific request pattern can appear together in one authentic sentence. You are only assessed on the parts marked assessable above.

Integrated Reading Task

Read the notice below, which announces a new online civil-service system, then answer the questions that follow. This passage combines the news-style register from Chapter 6, the digital vocabulary from Chapter 7, and the institutional procedure vocabulary from Chapter 8.

안내: 온라인 민원 신청 시스템 도입

서초구청은 다음 달부터 주민들이 직접 방문하지 않고도 온라인으로 각종 서류를 신청할 수 있는 새 시스템을 도입한다고 발표했습니다. 구청 관계자는 그동안 서류 신청과 확인 과정에서 시간이 오래 걸린다는 민원이 많았다고 밝혔습니다. 새 시스템에서는 신청서가 접수되는 즉시 담당자에게 자동으로 전달되며, 처리 상황도 온라인으로 확인할 수 있습니다. 다만 서명이 필요한 서류는 여전히 스캔하여 첨부해야 하며, 문의 사항이 있으면 이메일로 문의할 수 있습니다.

쓰기 — Writing Assessment

Writing Assessment

Write a complete formal email responding to the notice in the Integrated Reading Task. Choose one of the following situations, or a comparable one approved by your instructor:

  • You want to ask whether a specific document can be submitted through the new online system.
  • You submitted an application online but have not received confirmation, and you want to check on its status.
  • You had a problem attaching a scanned document and want to ask for help.
  1. Open formally. Use 안녕하십니까 or 안녕하세요, then identify yourself with 저는 ~입니다.
  2. State your purpose. Use 다름이 아니라 or a similar formal opening expression.
  3. Write the body. Include at least one verb correctly marked with -(으)시- referring to the reader, and use at least one honorific address term.
  4. Make your request. Use -아/어 주시겠습니까? or -아/어 주시기 바랍니다.
  5. Close politely. End with 감사드립니다 or a similar formal closing.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • The email follows the full formal email frame: greeting, self-identification, purpose, body, closing.
  • An honorific address term is used appropriately.
  • At least one verb is correctly marked with -(으)시- when referring to the reader, and not applied to the writer's own actions.
  • The draft uses -습니다/-ㅂ니다 style consistently throughout, with no everyday polite endings.
  • At least one Chapter 8 formal request pattern is used correctly.
  • Only Hangeul is used; no romanization appears.
  • Spacing and particles are accurate throughout.
  • No grammar or vocabulary beyond Volume III, Chapters 1–10, appears productively.

Culture and Communication Review

Culture in Context — Formal register consistency across a single interaction

A resident's email, an institution's reply, and a follow-up phone call about the same matter typically maintain a consistent level of formality throughout the interaction, even as the medium shifts from writing to speech. Shifting suddenly to casual language partway through a single institutional interaction can read as inconsistent or disrespectful, regardless of how well the underlying grammar is formed.

Institutional Awareness — Digital services do not eliminate formal correspondence

The introduction of an online system, as described in this chapter's notice, typically does not remove the need for formal written or spoken inquiries; it usually adds a new channel alongside existing ones. Learners should expect formal email and phone correspondence to remain relevant even as institutions expand their digital services.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Treating a recognition-level passive form as a productive pattern to imitate freely

Error: Attempting to freely construct new passive or causative sentences beyond 접수되다, 처리되다, and 지연되다 for this chapter's writing task.

Correction: Use these specific passive-form verbs receptively when reading, but build your own written and spoken sentences around the assessable systems: -(으)시-, honorific address terms, and the Chapter 8 formal request patterns.

Common Mistake — Mixing formal and everyday polite endings within one email

Error: Writing 저는 수아예요. 문의드립니다. in the same email, mixing everyday polite 이에요/예요 with formal -습니다.

Correction: Keep the entire email in formal style: 저는 수아입니다. 문의드립니다.

Common Mistake — Restating the written inquiry in casual spoken style during the phone role-play

Error: Switching to casual, unmarked speech when restating the inquiry aloud, even though the written email was formal.

Correction: Maintain formal spoken register throughout the phone restatement, using -습니다/-습니까 style and honorific forms consistent with the written email.

Answer Key

Integrated Reading Task — First passage

  1. The district office is introducing a new online system allowing residents to apply for documents without visiting in person, beginning next month.
  2. The system was introduced because residents had complained that document applications and confirmation took too long to process.
  3. 접수되는 ("is received/registered") describes an application being received automatically, without needing to name who receives it.
  4. Applicants must still scan and attach documents that require a signature.

Integrated Reading Task — Email

  1. 수아 wants to know whether membership registration is required to use the new online system.
  2. Honorific verb form: 바쁘시겠지만; formal request pattern: 알려 주시겠습니까?
  3. Greeting: 안녕하십니까?; self-identification: 저는 서초구에 거주하는 수아입니다.; purpose: 다름이 아니라...; body/request: 이용 방법을 간단히 알려 주시겠습니까?; closing: 시간을 내 주셔서 감사드립니다.

Listening comprehension

  • Residents must first register as a member on the website.
  • Documents requiring a signature must be scanned and attached.
  • Residents can check the status of their application on the website at any time.
  • Residents should send an inquiry by email if they have further questions.
