종합 과제 3: 근거 있는 주장Unit III Assessment: A Position Supported by Evidence

Synthesizing reason, comparison, data, and moderated discussion into one evidence-based position · Speak and Write Korean: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

This is an assessment chapter. By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Explain and support a reason using -아서/어서, -(으)니까, and result language from Chapter 11.
  2. Compare options and justify a preference using 보다, 가장/제일, and preference reasoning from Chapter 12.
  3. Explain data and numbers using percentages, statistics, and source attribution such as 에 따르면 from Chapter 13.
  4. Take part in a moderated discussion, softening disagreement, requesting clarification, and conceding a partial point with -기는 하지만 and -더라도 from Chapter 14.
  5. Combine all four systems above into a single organized spoken and written argument: position, reason, comparison, supporting data, anticipated counterpoint, and conclusion.
  6. Demonstrate consistent register, accurate Hangeul, correct spacing, and appropriate speech-level control across an extended task.

This chapter introduces no major new productive grammar, particle, honorific, or vocabulary system. It consolidates and integrates the reason (Ch. 11), comparison (Ch. 12), data (Ch. 13), and discussion (Ch. 14) systems already active, in line with the Unit III Assessment status for Chapter 15.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Review — Intonation across a multi-part argument

A full spoken argument moves through several intonation patterns in sequence: a level, confident tone for stating a position (저는 ~다고 생각해요), a slightly rising tone when giving supporting reasons and data (~ 때문이에요, ~에 따르면), a softened rising-then-leveling tone for anticipated disagreement (-는데요, reviewed from Chapter 14), and a level, closing tone for the conclusion (~다고 결론지었어요).

Practice reading your own written argument aloud, shifting intonation deliberately at each of these four points, rather than using one flat tone throughout.

Synthesis: Chapters 11–14

This chapter introduces no new grammar system. It reviews and combines four systems already active so that they function together inside one extended argument.

1. Stating a Position and Giving a Reason (Ch. 11 Review)

Review — Reason and result

A position statement pairs a claim with a supporting reason using 왜냐하면 ~ 때문이에요 or a clause ending in -아서/어서 or -(으)니까.

  • 저는 발표를 하나로 합치는 게 더 낫다고 생각해요. 왜냐하면 준비 시간이 줄기 때문이에요. — "I think combining the presentations into one is better, because it reduces preparation time."

2. Comparing Options (Ch. 12 Review)

Review — Comparison and preference

보다 compares two options directly; -는 게 더 낫다/좋다 states a preference with a reason attached.

  • 개인 발표가 그룹 발표보다 참여도가 더 높아요. — "Individual presentations have higher participation than group presentations."

3. Supporting a Position with Data (Ch. 13 Review)

Review — Data and source attribution

에 따르면 attributes a claim to a source; percentages and verbs such as 증가하다, 감소하다, and 차지하다 describe quantitative change or proportion.

  • 13과의 조사에 따르면, 개인 발표를 한 학생의 참여도가 65퍼센트를 차지했어요. — "According to the Chapter 13 survey, the participation rate of students who did individual presentations accounted for 65 percent."

4. Anticipating and Responding to a Counterpoint (Ch. 14 Review)

Review — Softened disagreement, clarification, and concession

A strong argument anticipates disagreement rather than ignoring it. Use -기는 하지만 to concede a known fact before restating a position, and -더라도 to concede an extreme or hypothetical case. Use 저는 좀 다르게 생각하는데요 to respond respectfully to a classmate's counterpoint, and 다시 한번 설명해 주시겠어요? to request clarification when needed.

  • 시간이 부족하기는 하지만, 발표 하나로 준비하는 게 더 효율적이에요. — "It's true that time is limited, but preparing one presentation is more efficient."
Building Block — The full evidence-based argument frame

[position: 저는 ~다고 생각해요] + [reason: 왜냐하면 ~ 때문이에요] + [comparison: A가 B보다 더 ~해요] + [data: ~에 따르면, ~퍼센트가 ~을/를 차지했어요] + [anticipated counterpoint + concession: -기는 하지만 / -더라도] + [restated position] + [conclusion: 그래서 저는 ~다고 결론지었어요]

This is the same frame practiced across Chapters 11 through 14. Chapter 15 does not add a new piece to the frame; it asks you to produce the whole frame together, in both writing and speech, with a real counterpoint and a real response.

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task asks you to produce your own complete, evidence-based position paper, combining reason, comparison, data, and a conceded counterpoint, exactly as 지영 and 수아 did above.

  1. Choose your position. You may argue either side of the class project debate, or a different topic your instructor assigns.
  2. State your position and reason. Use 주장하다 or -다고 생각해요 with a reason from Chapter 11.
  3. Add a comparison. Use 보다 or -는 게 더 낫다 from Chapter 12 to strengthen the position.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Conceding a point as a mark of a strong argument

Across the Unit III chapters, acknowledging the strength of an opposing view — rather than dismissing it — has appeared consistently as a marker of a credible, well-reasoned position in Korean academic, institutional, and public discussion. A speaker who says 그 점은 인정하기는 하지만 before restating their position is not weakening their argument; they are demonstrating that they have considered the evidence fairly.

Culture in Context — Data as a shared reference point

Notice that both 지영 and 수아 refer to the same Chapter 13 survey data but draw different conclusions from it. This reflects a common pattern in Korean public and academic discussion, where shared data is expected to ground a disagreement, rather than each side relying on unsupported personal claims.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Listing reason, comparison, and data without connecting them

Error: Writing three separate, disconnected sentences — a reason, a comparison, and a data statement — that do not clearly support the same position.

Correction: Make sure each supporting sentence explicitly strengthens the same claim: 저는 A가 더 낫다고 생각해요. 왜냐하면 ~ 때문이고, 자료에 따르면 ~이기 때문이에요. ("I think A is better, because of ~, and because according to the data, ~.")

Common Mistake — Conceding a point without returning to the original position

Error: Using -기는 하지만 or -더라도 and then trailing off, leaving the listener unsure whether the speaker still holds their original position.

Correction: Always follow a concession with a clear restatement: 참여도 문제가 있기는 하지만, 저는 여전히 발표를 하나로 합치는 게 낫다고 생각해요. ("It's true there's a participation issue, but I still think combining the presentations is better.")

Common Mistake — Citing data without stating what it means

Error: Stating a percentage or statistic and moving on immediately, without explaining why it supports the position.

Correction: Always interpret the data explicitly: 참여도가 65퍼센트를 차지했는데, 이는 개인 발표가 더 효과적이라는 것을 보여 줘요. ("Participation accounted for 65 percent, which shows that individual presentations are more effective.")
