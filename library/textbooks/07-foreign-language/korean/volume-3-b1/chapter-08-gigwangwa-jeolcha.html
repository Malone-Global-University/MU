기관과 절차Institutions and Procedures

Procedures, forms, passive/causative recognition, and formal requests · Speak and Write Korean: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use vocabulary for institutions, offices, forms, and administrative procedures to describe what a process requires and how it is carried out.
  2. Recognize a wider set of Korean passive-form verbs and identify their active-verb counterparts, applying the passive recognition begun in Chapters 6 and 7 to institutional procedures.
  3. Recognize the basic function of the causative pattern (-게 하다 and common causative-form verbs) when it appears in instructions and procedural explanations, without full productive control of the causative system.
  4. Continue using formal -습니다/-습니까 style, now applied to office procedures, forms, and printed institutional notices.
  5. Make and respond to formal requests using -아/어 주시겠습니까 and related polite request patterns appropriate to institutional settings.
  6. Read a short form, notice, or set of instructions and identify the steps, requirements, and deadlines it describes.
  7. Write a short formal request or set of procedural instructions appropriate to an institutional audience.
  8. Discuss a bureaucratic or administrative task with a partner, asking about required documents, steps, and deadlines using appropriate formal and everyday polite forms.

This chapter continues developing the formal style and source-framing systems from Chapters 6 and 7, now in an institutional-procedures domain. It expands recognized passive-form verbs, introduces receptive recognition of the causative pattern, and introduces formal request forms as a new productive system.

Opening Scenario

지영's discussion group meets at the community center this week, and the topic turns to paperwork. 라일라 needs to renew her residence registration and has no idea which documents are required. 민준 recently visited the district office to request a certificate and found the formal language on the forms harder to follow than he expected. 수아, who works part-time at a university administrative office, offers to walk the group through how Korean institutions typically explain procedures — what documents are required, what steps must be followed, and how to make a polite formal request when something is missing or unclear.

  1. You will build vocabulary for institutions, offices, documents, and procedural steps, so that you can understand and describe an administrative process.
  2. You will expand your recognition of passive-form verbs and begin recognizing the causative pattern in procedural instructions.
  3. You will learn to make formal requests appropriate to institutional settings, continuing the formal-register control developed in Chapters 6 and 7.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — Batchim before formal endings and Sino-Korean compounds

This chapter's vocabulary includes many two-syllable Sino-Korean compounds ending in batchim, such as 신청, 접수, and 발급. When these nouns combine with -하다 or -되다, pay attention to how the final batchim of the noun interacts with the following syllable: 접수되다 is pronounced with the ㅂ batchim of 접 linking smoothly into the following syllable, and 발급되다 shows a similar pattern with its double batchim ㄺ.

Practice reading these compounds slowly, syllable by syllable, before speeding up: 접수되다, 발급되다, 등록되다, 승인되다. Listen for how the final consonant of the first syllable carries into the next syllable rather than being dropped.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter expands the recognized passive-form verb set from Chapters 6 and 7, introduces receptive recognition of the causative pattern, continues formal -습니다/-습니까 style in a new domain, and introduces formal request patterns as a new productive system for institutional communication.

1. Expanded Passive-Form Verb Recognition

Grammar — 발급되다, 접수되다, 등록되다, 확인되다, 거절되다, 승인되다 (recognition and limited controlled use)

Institutional Korean relies heavily on passive-form verbs, because procedural language often describes what happens to a document or application rather than who acts on it. This chapter expands the small recognized set from Chapters 6 and 7 with six institutional passive-form verbs. As before, full production of the passive and causative systems as general grammatical patterns remains outside this chapter's scope; you are learning these forms as a recognized, high-frequency set of individual verbs.

  • 발급되다 — to be issued (vs. 발급하다, for the institution to issue)
  • 접수되다 — to be received/processed (vs. 접수하다)
  • 등록되다 — to be registered (vs. 등록하다)
  • 확인되다 — to be confirmed (vs. 확인하다)
  • 거절되다 — to be rejected (vs. 거절하다)
  • 승인되다 — to be approved (vs. 승인하다)
  • 신청서가 접수되었습니다. The application has been received. 접수되다 + -었습니다 (past formal)
  • 서류가 확인되면 증명서가 발급됩니다. Once the documents are confirmed, the certificate is issued. 확인되다 + -(으)면 (condition); 발급되다 (present formal)
  • 일부 신청은 서류 미비로 거절되었습니다. Some applications were rejected due to incomplete documents. 거절되다 + -었습니다 (past formal)

2. Recognizing the Causative Pattern -게 하다

Grammar — -게 하다 in procedural instructions (receptive recognition only)

Korean institutions often explain a procedure by describing what one party causes another party to do, using the pattern [verb stem]-게 하다 ("to make/let someone do ~"). This chapter introduces this pattern only as a recognized form so that you can understand procedural explanations; you are not expected to produce the causative pattern independently. Full introduction of the passive and causative systems as productive grammar is scheduled for a later volume.

  • 신청자가 서류를 다시 제출하게 합니다. — "(The office) has the applicant resubmit the documents."
  • 담당자가 신청자를 확인하게 했습니다. — "The staff member had the applicant confirm (the information)."

Notice that in each case, one party (the institution or staff member) causes another party (the applicant) to perform an action. Recognize this meaning when you encounter it in procedural texts; do not attempt to generate new causative sentences yet.

3. Formal Requests: -아/어 주시겠습니까 and Related Patterns

Grammar — Making and responding to formal requests

This chapter introduces formal request patterns appropriate to institutional settings, building on the everyday polite request pattern -아/어 주세요 that you already control. The formal equivalents are more indirect and more appropriate when speaking with an institution, an unfamiliar staff member, or in writing.

  • -아/어 주시겠습니까? — a polite, indirect formal request ("Would you be willing to ~ for me?")
  • -아/어 주시기 바랍니다 — a formal written request, common in notices and instructions ("Please ~" / "It is requested that you ~")
  • -아/어 주십시오 — a direct formal request or instruction, common in spoken institutional contexts (recycled from Ch. 7)

Use -아/어 주시겠습니까? when speaking directly to institutional staff, and -아/어 주시기 바랍니다 in written notices and instructions. You are expected to produce both forms in this chapter's controlled tasks.

4. Continued Formal Style in Institutional Notices

Grammar — -습니다/-ㅂ니다 in forms, notices, and procedural steps

As in Chapters 6 and 7, institutional notices and printed instructions use formal -습니다/-ㅂ니다 style consistently. This chapter applies the same speech level to describing application steps, requirements, and deadlines.

  • 신청서를 작성한 후, 신분증과 함께 창구에 제출하십시오. After filling out the application form, please submit it to the counter along with your ID. 작성하다 + -(으)ㄴ 후 (sequence, recycled); 제출하다 + -십시오 (formal command)
  • 수수료는 접수 시 지불해야 합니다. The fee must be paid at the time of submission. 지불하다 + -아/어야 하다 (obligation, recycled) + -ㅂ니다 (formal)
Building Block — The procedural-instruction frame

[step 1 with sequence connector -고 / -(으)ㄴ 후] + [step 2, often with a passive-form verb: 접수되다 / 확인되다 / 발급되다] + [formal ending: -습니다/-십시오/-시기 바랍니다]

This frame lets you describe or follow a multi-step institutional process, from submitting documents to receiving a certificate.

Model Dialogue

Setting: 라일라 visits the district office to renew her residence registration. The staff member uses formal style; 라일라 uses everyday polite style, as is typical when a resident speaks to a government office representative.

라일라 안녕하세요, 거주 등록을 갱신하려고 하는데요. 어떤 서류가 필요해요? Hello, I'd like to renew my residence registration. What documents do I need?
담당자 신분증과 신청서가 필요합니다. 먼저 이 신청서를 작성해 주시겠습니까? You'll need your ID and an application form. Could you please fill out this application form first?
라일라 네, 알겠어요. 다 작성했는데, 여기 제출하면 돼요? Okay, got it. I've filled it all out — can I submit it here?
담당자 네, 신분증과 함께 제출해 주시기 바랍니다. 신청서가 접수되면 서류를 확인하겠습니다. Yes, please submit it along with your ID. Once the application is received, we will confirm the documents.
라일라 확인은 얼마나 걸려요? How long does the confirmation take?
담당자 보통 3일 정도 걸립니다. 서류에 문제가 없으면 등록증이 새로 발급됩니다. 확인 과정에서 신청자에게 서류를 다시 제출하게 하는 경우도 있으니 참고해 주십시오. It usually takes about three days. If there is no problem with the documents, a new registration card is issued. Please note that in some cases, the applicant may be asked to resubmit documents during the confirmation process.
라일라 알겠습니다. 수수료는 얼마예요? I understand. How much is the fee?
담당자 수수료는 만 원이며, 접수 시 지불해 주시면 됩니다. The fee is ten thousand won, and you can pay it at the time of submission.
라일라 네, 감사합니다. Okay, thank you.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Formality and indirectness in institutional requests

Korean institutional staff typically favor indirect formal request patterns such as -아/어 주시겠습니까? over direct commands, even when a straightforward instruction is intended. This indirectness signals respect toward the person being addressed and is common across government offices, banks, and other public-facing institutions.

Culture in Context — Numbered tickets and waiting systems

Many Korean government offices, banks, and hospitals use a numbered-ticket system (번호표) at service counters (창구). Visitors take a ticket upon arrival and wait until their number is called, which helps manage order and reduces the need to physically queue.

Regional and Institutional Awareness — Procedures vary by office and by document type

Specific documents, fees, and steps described in this chapter are illustrative rather than universal. Actual requirements can vary by city, by office, and by the type of document requested, and they may change over time. Always confirm current requirements directly with the relevant institution.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using -아/어 주세요 in place of a formal institutional request

Error: Saying 확인해 주세요 to institutional staff in a context that calls for a more formal, indirect request.

Correction: Use 확인해 주시겠습니까? in spoken institutional contexts, or 확인해 주시기 바랍니다 in written notices, as modeled in this chapter.

Common Mistake — Confusing an active verb with its passive-form counterpart

Error: Writing 신청서가 접수합니다, which incorrectly treats the application form as the one performing the action.

Correction: Use the passive-form verb when the document is the grammatical subject: 신청서가 접수됩니다 ("The application is received"). Reserve 접수하다 for sentences where the institution or staff member is the subject.

Common Mistake — Treating -게 하다 as a pattern you must produce independently

Error: Attempting to construct new sentences with -게 하다 before this system is formally activated for production.

Correction: At this stage, focus on recognizing and understanding -게 하다 when you encounter it in procedural texts. Full productive control of the causative system is scheduled for a later volume.

  • A person who loses their certificate must fill out a reissuance application and submit it with their ID at the counter.
  • During confirmation, the applicant may be asked to submit additional documents.
  • Reissuance takes about five days from the application date and costs five thousand won.

Reading comprehension

  1. Step 1: Fill in name, date of birth, and address accurately. Step 2: Sign or stamp the signature line. Step 3: Submit a copy of your ID along with the form. Step 4: Once completed, submit it to the counter staff member.
  2. The application was rejected because some of the required documents were incomplete.
  3. The applicant must prepare the missing documents and resubmit within the deadline; no additional fee will be charged for the resubmission.
  4. Sample answer: 제출해 주시겠습니까? signals a polite, indirect spoken-style request, while 제출해 주시기 바랍니다 signals a formal written request typical of printed instructions; both show respect toward the person being addressed rather than issuing a blunt command.

Chapter Review — Section B

1. Sample answer: 서류를 확인해 주세요 → 서류를 확인해 주시겠습니까?; 신청서를 제출해 주세요 → 신청서를 제출해 주시기 바랍니다; 잠깐 기다려 주세요 → 잠시 대기해 주시겠습니까?

2. Sample answer: 신청서가 접수되었습니다. / 서류를 다시 확인해 주시겠습니까?

3. Sample answer: In 담당자가 신청자를 확인하게 했습니다, the staff member (the causer) had the applicant (the one caused) perform the action of confirming information, rather than performing the confirmation personally.

Section D — Sample response pair

Procedural steps: 1. 신청서를 작성합니다. 2. 신분증과 함께 창구에 제출합니다. 3. 서류가 접수되면 담당자가 확인합니다. 4. 문제가 없으면 증명서가 발급됩니다.

Formal request: 서류를 기한 내에 다시 제출해 주시기 바랍니다.

Check that: the procedural steps stay in -습니다/-ㅂ니다 style throughout and include at least one passive-form verb, and the formal request uses -아/어 주시겠습니까? or -아/어 주시기 바랍니다 correctly.
