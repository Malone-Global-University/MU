비교와 선택Comparison and Choice

Comparison with 보다, superlatives with 가장/제일, classification, and preference reasoning · Speak and Write Korean: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use vocabulary for comparing options, ranking choices, and explaining a preference.
  2. Compare two items using 보다 ("than") and describe the compared quality with a descriptive verb.
  3. Add emphasis to a comparison using 더 ("more") and 훨씬 ("much more").
  4. Identify the best option among three or more using 가장 and 제일 ("the most").
  5. Classify and select among options using 중에서 ("among/out of") together with 가장/제일.
  6. State a preference and justify it using -는 게 더 낫다/좋다 ("it is better to ~"), building on the stance and reason systems from Chapter 11.
  7. Read a short comparison passage and identify what is being compared, on what basis, and which option is preferred.
  8. Write and discuss a short comparison that ranks options and gives a reasoned preference.

This chapter introduces comparison, superlative, and preference-reasoning patterns as new productive systems. It builds directly on the stance-marking and reason-marking expressions from Chapter 11 (제 생각에는, 왜냐하면 ~ 때문이에요) by applying them to comparative judgments. It does not introduce new speech levels or new honorific systems.

Opening Scenario

After their discussion in Chapter 11, 지영, 민준, 라일라, and 수아 have been asked by their instructor to compare three possible class field trip options: a museum, a market tour, and a hiking trail. Each option has advantages and disadvantages, and the group needs to compare them, rank them, and recommend one option with a clear reason. 라일라 has strong opinions about which option would be most useful for practicing Korean, while 수아 is more interested in which option is most convenient for the whole group.

  1. You will build vocabulary for comparing, ranking, and choosing among options.
  2. You will learn to compare two things with 보다 and to identify the best of several things with 가장/제일.
  3. You will state and justify a preference using -는 게 더 낫다/좋다, combined with the reason-giving language from Chapter 11.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — Stress on comparison intensifiers

In comparative sentences, 더 and 훨씬 typically receive noticeably stronger stress than the surrounding words, since they carry the main comparative meaning. In 이게 훨씬 더 좋아요, both 훨씬 and 더 are emphasized, with 훨씬 receiving the heaviest stress of the sentence.

Also practice the batchim pronunciation of 낫다 and its polite form 나아요: the final ㅅ of the stem is dropped before a vowel-initial ending, a pattern recycled from the ㅅ-irregular verbs introduced receptively in Volume II and reinforced productively here.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces comparison, superlative, classification, and preference-reasoning patterns as new productive systems, and connects them to the stance and reason expressions already active from Chapter 11.

1. Comparing Two Things with 보다

Grammar — Noun + 보다 (+ 더) + descriptive verb

보다 attaches directly to the noun being compared against, and means "than." The item being described normally appears with 이/가 or as the topic, and 더 ("more") often appears before the descriptive verb for clarity, although it can be omitted once the comparison is clear from context.

For a stronger emphasis than 더 alone, use 훨씬 더 ("much more").

  • 박물관이 시장보다 조용해요. — "The museum is quieter than the market."
  • 등산이 박물관보다 훨씬 더 힘들어요. — "Hiking is much more tiring than the museum."
  • 시장 투어가 박물관보다 더 재미있어요. The market tour is more fun than the museum. 시장 투어 + 이/가; 박물관 + 보다; 더 + 재미있다
  • 이 옵션이 저 옵션보다 가격이 훨씬 더 싸요. This option is much cheaper in price than that option. 훨씬 더 (strong intensifier) + 싸다

2. Superlatives with 가장/제일 and Classification with 중에서

Grammar — 가장/제일 and [list] 중에서

가장 and 제일 both mean "the most" and are placed directly before the descriptive verb or action verb being intensified. 가장 is slightly more formal and more common in writing; 제일 is common in everyday conversation. Both are interchangeable in this chapter's tasks unless a task specifically calls for formal style.

To specify the group being compared, use [list of options] 중에서 ("among ~") before the superlative expression. This frames the sentence as a classification: out of a defined set, one member is identified as best according to some quality.

  • 박물관, 시장 투어, 등산 중에서 저는 시장 투어가 가장 재미있다고 생각해요.
  • 이 세 가지 옵션 중에서 무엇이 제일 실용적이에요?
  • 세 옵션 중에서 등산이 가장 저렴해요. Among the three options, hiking is the cheapest. 세 옵션 (noun list) + 중에서 (classification frame); 가장 + 저렴하다
  • 저는 이 중에서 박물관이 제일 좋아요. Of these, I like the museum the most. 이 중에서 (demonstrative + classification); 제일 + 좋다

3. Stating and Justifying a Preference

Grammar — -는 게 더 낫다/좋다

-는 게 더 낫다/좋다 ("it is better to ~") attaches to an action-verb dictionary form and states that one course of action is preferable to another. 게 is a contracted, conversational form of 것이 ("the thing of ~ing"), so the pattern is recognized here as a fixed frame rather than fully analyzed as a nominalized clause, which is developed formally in later chapters.

This preference frame combines naturally with the reason-giving expressions from Chapter 11 (왜냐하면 ~ 때문이에요, 그 이유는 ~이에요) to build a complete, supported recommendation.

  • 시장 투어에 가는 게 더 좋아요. — "It's better to go on the market tour."
  • 박물관에 가는 게 더 나아요. 왜냐하면 날씨와 상관없이 즐길 수 있기 때문이에요.
Building Block — The comparison-and-preference frame

[comparison: A가 B보다 (훨씬) 더 ~해요] + [superlative: (list) 중에서 A가 가장/제일 ~해요] + [preference: A에 가는/하는 게 더 낫다/좋다] + [reason: 왜냐하면 ~ 때문이에요 / 그 이유는 ~이에요]

This frame lets learners compare two options directly, rank several options, state a preferred choice, and support that choice with a reason from Chapter 11 — the same supported-opinion structure applied to a comparative decision.

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task asks you to compare three related options, identify the best one according to a clear standard, and justify your preference with a reason.

  1. Choose a topic. Pick three comparable options — for example, three restaurants, three ways to spend a weekend, or three study methods.
  2. Compare two options directly. Use A가 B보다 (훨씬) 더 ~해요 for at least one pair.
  3. Identify the best option. Use [list] 중에서 ~가 가장/제일 ~해요.
  4. State your preference. Use ~는 게 더 낫다/좋다.
  5. Give a reason. Use 왜냐하면 ~ 때문이에요 or 그 이유는 ~이에요 from Chapter 11.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • The paragraph follows the comparison-and-preference frame: direct comparison, superlative, preference, reason.
  • 보다 is used to compare two specific items directly.
  • 가장 or 제일 is used with 중에서 to identify the best option among the full set.
  • -는 게 더 낫다/좋다 is used correctly to state a preference.
  • A reason-marking expression from Chapter 11 supports the preference logically.
  • Speech level is consistent throughout (everyday polite, unless your instructor assigns formal style).
  • Only Hangeul is used; no romanization appears.
  • Spacing and particles are accurate throughout.
  • No grammar or vocabulary beyond Volume III, Chapters 1–12, appears productively.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Group decisions and 다수결

In many Korean classroom, workplace, and community settings, a group discussion that compares options often concludes with an explicit group decision, sometimes reached by 다수결 ("majority vote") after each person has had a chance to state a preference and a reason. Ending a comparison discussion with a clear next step, such as 수아's suggestion to vote, is a common and expected way to bring the conversation to a close.

Culture in Context — Naming a shared standard before comparing

Korean comparison discussions in group settings, such as choosing a restaurant or a vendor, often begin by naming the 기준 ("standard" or "criterion") being used, such as price, quality, or convenience, before comparing the options against it. This makes the basis for the final choice explicit and easier for the whole group to agree on.

Register Awareness — 가장 versus 제일

가장 and 제일 are interchangeable in most everyday contexts, but 가장 is somewhat more common in formal writing, news, and academic contexts, while 제일 is more common in casual spoken conversation. Both are treated as productive and level-appropriate in this chapter; later volumes will refine the register distinction further.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Attaching 보다 to the wrong noun

Error: Writing 박물관보다가 시장이 재미있어요., attaching 보다 to the item being described instead of the item being compared against.

Correction: 보다 always attaches to the standard of comparison, not the subject: 시장이 박물관보다 재미있어요. ("The market is more fun than the museum.")

Common Mistake — Using 가장/제일 without a clear group

Error: Writing 시장 투어가 가장 좋아요. with no indication of what it is being compared against, leaving the superlative claim unclear.

Correction: Name the group with 중에서 when the comparison set is not already obvious from context: 세 옵션 중에서 시장 투어가 가장 좋아요.

Common Mistake — Stating a preference with no reason

Error: Writing only 시장 투어에 가는 게 더 좋아요. and stopping there, as in Chapter 11's caution against unsupported opinions.

Correction: Add a reason, as required by this chapter's tasks: 시장 투어에 가는 게 더 좋아요. 왜냐하면 한국어를 쓸 기회가 가장 많기 때문이에요.

3. Sample answer: 저는 집에서 공부하는 게 더 나아요. 왜냐하면 더 조용하기 때문이에요.

Section D — Sample response

저는 세 가지 공부 방법을 비교해 봤어요. 혼자 공부하는 것이 그룹으로 공부하는 것보다 더 조용해요. 하지만 온라인 강의, 혼자 공부, 그룹 스터디 중에서 그룹 스터디가 가장 도움이 많이 돼요. 그래서 저는 그룹으로 공부하는 게 더 낫다고 생각해요. 그 이유는 서로 질문하고 설명하면서 더 잘 이해할 수 있기 때문이에요.

Check that: the paragraph follows the comparison–superlative–preference–reason frame, uses 보다 correctly for a direct comparison, uses 중에서 with 가장/제일 for the superlative judgment, and supports the stated preference with a Chapter 11 reason-marking expression.
