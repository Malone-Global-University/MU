Combining narration, cause-and-result explanation, and summary/report into one connected performance · Speak and Write Korean: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Narrate a personal experience of change using extended background, -고 있다, and time clauses from Chapter 2.
  2. Explain the causes and results of that experience using -아서/어서, -(으)니까, and result language from Chapter 3.
  3. Summarize and report someone else's narrative using summary markers and -다고 하다 from Chapter 4.
  4. Produce one connected spoken and written performance that integrates narration, explanation, and reporting without introducing any new grammar system.
  5. Identify, in your own work and a partner's work, where each Unit I system appears and evaluate whether it is used accurately.
  6. Self-assess your Unit I performance against the rubric provided in this chapter.

This chapter introduces no new productive grammar, particle, vocabulary domain, honorific, or speech-level system. It consolidates Chapters 1 through 4: discourse review and cohesion (Ch. 1), extended narration and -고 있다 (Ch. 2), cause-and-result explanation (Ch. 3), and summarizing/reporting with -다고 하다 (Ch. 4). All tasks below draw only on material already active by Chapter 4.

Assessment Scenario

지영's discussion group has reached its first checkpoint. To mark the end of Unit I, 지영 asks every member to prepare one connected piece: tell your own story of a life experience or change, explain why it happened and what resulted, and then listen to a partner's story and report it back accurately and briefly. 라일라, 수아, and 민준 each take turns being the storyteller and the reporter, and the group uses this session to check how well everyone can combine the tools they built across Chapters 1 through 4.

  1. This chapter does not introduce new grammar. It asks you to combine narration, explanation, and reporting into a single connected performance.
  2. The chapter's task has two halves: telling your own narrative-plus-analysis, and reporting someone else's.
  3. Success is measured by how accurately and naturally you use Chapters 1–4 tools together, not by new material.

Cumulative Grammar Review

This section reviews how Unit I's three systems fit together. No new form is introduced; the goal is to practice combining what you already control.

1. Narration + Explanation: Telling Your Own Story

Review — Narration and cause/result working together

A well-formed narrative-plus-analysis moves from background (Chapter 2's -고 있다 and time clauses) into a clear cause (-아서/어서, -(으)니까) and a named result (그 결과, 결국). The narration supplies the story; the explanation supplies why it happened.

  • 그때 저는 회사에서 새 프로젝트를 맡고 있었는데, 일이 너무 많아서 매일 늦게까지 남아 있었어요. At that time, I was in charge of a new project at work, and because I had so much to do, I stayed late every day. -고 있다 (background) + -아서 (cause)
  • 그렇게 몇 달을 보내다 보니까 건강이 나빠졌고, 그 결과 결국 일하는 방식을 바꾸기로 했어요. After spending several months like that, my health declined, and as a result I ended up deciding to change how I worked. 그러다 보니까 (accumulation) + 그 결과/결국 (result)

2. Summary + Report: Presenting Someone Else's Story

Review — Summarizing and reporting a partner's narrative

When you report a partner's story, open with a summary marker (간단히 말하면, 한마디로), keep only the essential cause, decision, and result, attribute the content with -다고 하다 conjugated correctly for predicate type and tense, and close with a restatement (다시 말하면, 즉).

  • 한마디로, 민준 씨는 일이 너무 많아서 건강이 나빠졌지만 결국 운동을 시작해서 훨씬 좋아졌다고 해요. In a word, Minjun says his health declined from overwork, but he ended up starting exercise and feeling much better. summary marker + condensed cause/result + -다고 하다
Building Block — The Unit I integrated frame

[background with -고 있다 / time clause] + [cause with -아서/어서 or -(으)니까] + [result with 그 결과 / 결국] + [if reporting: summary marker + -다고 하다 + closing restatement]

Your own narrative uses the first three slots directly. When reporting a partner's narrative, wrap their content in the summary-and-report frame from Chapter 4 instead of retelling it directly.

Practice — Combining the three systems

  1. Write two sentences narrating background using -고 있다 or a time clause.
  2. Write two sentences explaining a cause and a result using tools from Chapter 3.
  3. Write two sentences summarizing and reporting a partner's version of a similar story using -다고 하다.

Model Integrated Passage

Setting: 지영's discussion group, fifth session, the Unit I checkpoint. 수아 tells her own narrative-plus-analysis, and 라일라 reports it back.

수아 저는 그때 새 동네로 이사한 지 얼마 안 됐을 때였는데, 아는 사람이 하나도 없어서 답답했어요. 그래서 매주 동네 도서관에서 하는 모임에 나가기 시작했어요. At that time, I had just moved to a new neighborhood, and since I didn't know a single person, I felt frustrated. So I started attending the weekly meetup at the neighborhood library.
수아 처음에는 어색했지만, 계속 나가다 보니까 친한 사람들이 생겼고, 그 결과 지금은 이 동네가 훨씬 편해졌어요. At first it was awkward, but as I kept attending, I made close friends, and as a result this neighborhood feels much more comfortable now.
지영 라일라 씨, 수아 씨 이야기를 그룹한테 요약해 줄 수 있어요? Laila, could you summarize Sooa's story for the group?
라일라 네, 간단히 말하면, 수아 씨는 이사한 후에 아는 사람이 없어서 답답했지만 도서관 모임에 계속 나가다 보니까 친구가 생겼다고 해요. 그 결과, 지금은 동네가 편하게 느껴진다고 하네요. 다시 말하면, 작은 모임 하나가 새 동네를 진짜 집처럼 만들어 준 셈이에요. Yes, simply put, Sooa says that after moving she felt frustrated with no acquaintances, but by continuing to attend the library meetup she made friends. As a result, she says the neighborhood now feels comfortable. In other words, one small meetup made the new neighborhood feel like real home.

내용 이해 — Comprehension questions:

  1. Identify one sentence in 수아's narrative that uses background with a time clause, and one that uses a cause-and-result connector.
  2. Identify 라일라's summary marker, her use of -다고 하다, and her closing restatement.
  3. Explain how 라일라's report differs from simply repeating 수아's words.

  2. Draft two to three sentences of background using -고 있다 or a time clause from Chapter 2.

Reading Assessment

할머니께 들은 이야기

얼마 전에 할머니한테서 들은 이야기인데요, 할머니는 젊었을 때 낯선 도시로 혼자 이사를 가서 일을 시작했다고 해요. 처음 몇 달 동안은 아는 사람이 없어서 많이 외로웠지만, 같은 회사에 다니는 동료들과 점점 친해지다 보니까 그 도시가 편해졌다고 하셨어요. 그러다가 그중 한 분과 아주 친한 친구가 됐고, 그 결과 지금까지도 오십 년 가까이 연락하고 지내신대요.

한마디로, 낯선 곳에서의 외로움이 오히려 평생 가는 우정으로 이어진 셈이에요. 할머니는 그때 혼자라고 느꼈던 시간이 없었다면 그런 친구를 만나지 못했을 거라고 말씀하셨어요.

  2. Find one sentence using -다고 하다 or a related reported form, and explain what statement it attributes.
  3. Write a one-sentence summary of this passage in Korean, beginning with 간단히 말하면 or 한마디로.

Writing Assessment

This chapter's writing task is the Unit I capstone: 이야기와 분석 ("Narrative and Analysis"), combining your own story with a report of a partner's story.

  1. Part A — Your own narrative and analysis. Write four to six sentences narrating a personal experience of change, using background (-고 있다, time clauses) and a clear cause and result (-아서/어서, -(으)니까, 그 결과).
  2. Part B — Reporting a partner's account. Using a partner's story (or one of the model passages above), write three to four sentences summarizing and reporting it with a summary marker, at least two uses of -다고 하다, and a closing restatement.
  3. Connect the two parts. Add one linking sentence that compares your experience to the one you reported.
  4. Revise. Check your draft against the checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • Part A includes background using -고 있다 or a time clause.
  • Part A includes a clear cause and a named result.
  • Part B opens with a summary marker and closes with a restatement.
  • Part B reports at least two statements using -다고 하다, correctly conjugated for predicate type and tense.
  • No sentence in Part B simply repeats the source word for word.
  • Speech level is consistent everyday polite -아요/어요 throughout.
  • Only Hangeul is used; no romanization appears in the final draft.
  • Spacing and particles are accurate throughout.
  • No grammar or vocabulary beyond Volume III, Chapters 1–4, appears productively.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Sharing personal change stories in a group

In Korean conversation, personal narratives of hardship or change are often shared with a clear arc from difficulty to result, and listeners typically respond by acknowledging the effort involved before commenting further. When reporting someone else's story to a third party, attributing it clearly with -다고 하다 and a phrase like 듣자 하니 continues to show respect for the original speaker's ownership of their experience.

Culture in Context — Checkpoints as shared milestones

Marking the end of a study unit with a shared task, as 지영's group does here, is a common way for language study groups and classrooms to build accountability and camaraderie. Treating an assessment as a group milestone rather than only an individual test is a useful frame to carry into later, more formal assessments in the volume.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Blending narration and report into one voice

Error: Writing your own narrative (Part A) with reported forms like -다고 해요, or writing your report of a partner's story (Part B) as if it were your own direct experience.

Correction: Keep Part A in your own direct voice (-아요/어요 without -다고 하다) and Part B clearly attributed to the original speaker with -다고 하다 throughout.

Common Mistake — Omitting the result in your own narrative

Error: Narrating background and a cause but stopping before naming a clear result, leaving the story incomplete.

Correction: Always close your narrative with an explicit result using 그 결과, 결국, or an equivalent expression from Chapter 3.

Common Mistake — Reporting every sentence instead of summarizing

Error: Producing a "report" of a partner's story that restates every sentence in order rather than condensing to the essential cause, decision, and result.

Correction: Apply Chapter 4's summarizing technique: keep only the essential content, and use a summary marker and closing restatement to frame it.

Model Integrated Passage — Comprehension

  1. Background/time clause: 이사한 지 얼마 안 됐을 때; cause-and-result: 답답해서 ... 모임에 나가기 시작했어요 and 그 결과, 지금은 이 동네가 훨씬 편해졌어요.

Listening comprehension

  • Cause: the company's closure forced a months-long job search; result: he found a position at a larger company in a similar field and now has a more stable life.
  • Sample sentence with -다고 하다: 몇 달 동안 다른 일자리를 찾아야 했다고 해요 — reporting 민준's own account of his job search.

Reading comprehension
