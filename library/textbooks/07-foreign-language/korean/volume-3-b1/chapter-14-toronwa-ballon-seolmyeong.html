토론과 반론Discussion and Counterpoints

Turn-taking, respectful disagreement, asking for clarification, and participating in a moderated debate · Speak and Write Korean: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use vocabulary for discussion, argument, and moderated debate, including 주장, 근거, 반론, and 발언권.
  2. Take a turn politely and hold the floor using frames such as 제 생각을 말씀드려도 될까요?
  3. Interrupt or enter a discussion respectfully using 말씀 중에 죄송한데요.
  4. Express disagreement without causing offense, using softened frames such as 저는 좀 다르게 생각하는데요 and -다고 생각하지 않아요.
  5. Ask for clarification using 무슨 말씀이신지 잘 모르겠어요 and 다시 한번 설명해 주시겠어요?
  6. Concede a partial point while still maintaining a position, using -기는 하지만 and -더라도.
  7. Combine reason (Ch. 11), comparison (Ch. 12), and data (Ch. 13) frames inside a live, turn-taking discussion.
  8. Follow the basic structure of a short moderated debate: opening position, turn-taking, counterpoint, clarification, and closing summary.

This chapter introduces turn-taking, disagreement, clarification-request, and concession language as new productive systems for live discussion. It builds directly on the reason-marking (Ch. 11), comparison and preference (Ch. 12), and data-and-source frames (Ch. 13) already active, using them as the content that speakers exchange, disagree with, and clarify. It does not introduce new speech levels, new honorific systems, or unscheduled vocabulary domains.

Opening Scenario

With the data from Chapter 13 in hand, the instructor has organized a short in-class debate: should the class's semester project be a shared presentation or four separate individual presentations? 지영, 민준, 라일라, and 수아 are assigned to argue different sides, and the instructor moderates, reminding students to take turns, respond directly to what the last speaker said, and ask for clarification instead of guessing when something is unclear. 민준 tends to interrupt eagerly; 라일라 wants to make sure everyone is heard fairly; 수아 is not sure how to disagree without sounding rude.

  1. You will build vocabulary for argument, discussion, and moderated debate.
  2. You will learn to take turns politely, disagree respectfully, ask for clarification, and concede a partial point.
  3. You will combine reason, comparison, and data language from earlier chapters inside a live, turn-taking discussion.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — Rising and falling intonation in disagreement and clarification

Softened disagreement, such as 저는 좀 다르게 생각하는데요, typically ends with a gentle rising-then-leveling intonation on -는데요, signaling that the speaker is leaving room for a response rather than closing the discussion. A flat, falling intonation on the same sentence can sound more final and less inviting of reply; practice both and notice the difference.

Clarification questions such as 다시 한번 설명해 주시겠어요? use rising intonation typical of polite requests, recycled from earlier request patterns. Practice the batchim liaison in 끼어들다 and 덧붙이다, where the final consonant of one syllable links smoothly into the following vowel-initial syllable.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces turn-taking, disagreement, clarification-request, and concession language as new productive systems, and connects them to the reason, comparison, and data frames already active from Chapters 11 through 13.

1. Taking a Turn and Holding the Floor

Grammar — Requesting and yielding the floor

To ask for a turn to speak, use a polite permission-request frame: 제 생각을 말씀드려도 될까요? ("May I share my thought?"). To enter a discussion already underway, soften the interruption with 말씀 중에 죄송한데요 ("Sorry to interrupt while you're speaking"), followed by the new point. To yield the floor to someone else, use 먼저 말씀하세요 ("Please go ahead") or 계속하세요 ("Please continue").

  • 사회자: 이제 다음 분 말씀해 주시겠어요? — "Moderator: Would the next person like to speak now?"
  • 말씀 중에 죄송한데요, 저도 그 점에 대해 말씀드리고 싶어요. — "Sorry to interrupt while you're speaking, but I'd like to say something about that point too."
  • 제 의견을 말씀드려도 될까요? May I share my opinion? 말씀드리다 (humble) + -아/어도 되다 (permission, recycled)
  • 발언권을 주셔서 감사합니다. Thank you for giving me the floor. 발언권 + 을/를 주다 (formal discussion routine)

2. Disagreeing Respectfully

Grammar — Softened disagreement frames

Direct disagreement can sound abrupt in Korean discussion, so learners should soften a counterpoint rather than state it bluntly. 저는 좀 다르게 생각하는데요 ("I think about it a little differently") and 그 점에는 동의하지만 ("I agree with that point, but...") acknowledge the other speaker before introducing the counterpoint. -다고 생각하지 않아요 negates a claim gently by framing it as the speaker's own view rather than a flat correction.

These frames typically combine with the reason-marking expressions from Chapter 11 (왜냐하면 ~ 때문이에요) so that a disagreement is always supported, not merely asserted.

  • 저는 그 의견에 반대해요. 왜냐하면 시간이 부족하기 때문이에요. — "I disagree with that opinion, because there isn't enough time."
  • 그 말씀도 맞지만, 저는 좀 다르게 생각하는데요. — "That's true too, but I think about it a little differently."
  • 저는 발표를 나눠서 하는 게 더 낫다고 생각하지 않아요. I don't think it's better to split up the presentation. -는 게 더 낫다 (Ch. 12) + -다고 생각하지 않아요 (softened negation)
  • 그 점에는 동의하지만, 근거가 충분하지 않은 것 같아요. I agree with that point, but I don't think the evidence is sufficient. 동의하다 + -지만 (concession, recycled from Ch. 11)

3. Asking for Clarification

Grammar — Requesting clarification without guessing

When a point is unclear, ask directly rather than guessing at meaning. 무슨 말씀이신지 잘 모르겠어요 ("I'm not quite sure what you mean") is a polite, honorific-toned way to admit confusion about another speaker's point. 다시 한번 설명해 주시겠어요? ("Could you explain that once more?") requests a repeated or expanded explanation, recycling the -아/어 주시겠어요? request pattern.

  • 죄송한데, 방금 하신 말씀을 다시 한번 설명해 주시겠어요? — "Sorry, could you explain what you just said once more?"
  • 그 근거가 무슨 뜻인지 좀 더 명확하게 말씀해 주시겠어요? — "Could you state more clearly what that evidence means?"
  • 제가 오해했을 수도 있는데, 다시 설명해 주시겠어요? I may have misunderstood — could you explain again? 오해하다 + -았/었을 수도 있다 (softened admission)

4. Conceding a Point While Holding a Position: -기는 하지만, -더라도

Grammar — Concession connectors

-기는 하지만 concedes that part of the other speaker's point is true while still introducing a counterpoint, extending the contrastive function of -지만 from Chapter 11. -더라도 ("even if") concedes a hypothetical or extreme case and states that the speaker's position still holds regardless. Both connectors let a speaker sound reasonable and open-minded without abandoning their own 주장.

  • 시간이 부족하기는 하지만, 발표 하나로 준비하는 게 더 효율적이에요. — "It's true that time is limited, but preparing one shared presentation is more efficient."
  • 발표 주제가 다르더라도, 결론은 같은 방향으로 정리할 수 있어요. — "Even if the presentation topics are different, the conclusions can still be organized in the same direction."
  • 그 근거가 흥미롭기는 하지만, 자료가 충분하지 않아요. That evidence is interesting, but the data isn't sufficient. 흥미롭다 + -기는 하지만 (concession)
  • 아무리 바쁘더라도 발언권은 골고루 나눠야 해요. Even if everyone is busy, the floor should be shared fairly. 아무리 ~ -더라도 (even if, emphatic concession)
Building Block — The turn-taking-and-counterpoint frame

[turn request: 제 생각을 말씀드려도 될까요? / 말씀 중에 죄송한데요] + [acknowledge: 그 점에는 동의하지만 / -기는 하지만] + [counterpoint, using Ch. 11–13 reason/comparison/data: 왜냐하면 ~ 때문이에요 / A가 B보다 더 ~해요 / 이 자료에 따르면 ~] + [clarification if needed: 무슨 말씀이신지 잘 모르겠어요 / 다시 설명해 주시겠어요?]

This frame lets learners enter a discussion politely, acknowledge what was said, introduce a supported counterpoint using earlier reason, comparison, and data language, and request clarification when needed — combining this chapter's new systems with three chapters of prior evidence-based reasoning.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Softening disagreement to preserve harmony

In many Korean classroom, workplace, and community discussions, direct disagreement is often softened by first acknowledging what the other speaker said, using frames such as 그 말씀도 맞지만, before introducing a counterpoint. This does not mean disagreement is avoided altogether — it means the disagreement is framed in a way that preserves the relationship and allows the discussion to continue productively.

Culture in Context — The moderator's role in fair turn-taking

Building on the group-decision and data-based reasoning from Chapters 12 and 13, many Korean classroom and institutional discussions rely on a designated moderator (사회자) to distribute the floor fairly, especially when some participants tend to speak more readily than others. Asking the moderator for the floor, as in 발언권을 주시겠어요?, is a common and expected way to enter a structured discussion.

Register Awareness — 반박하다 versus everyday disagreement phrases

반박하다 ("to rebut") is common in formal debate, academic, and news contexts, while everyday classroom disagreement more often uses softened frames like 저는 좀 다르게 생각하는데요. Both are treated as productive and level-appropriate in this chapter; Volume IV will refine formal debate and argumentation register further.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Disagreeing without acknowledging the other speaker

Error: Responding to a classmate's point with only 아니요, 그건 틀렸어요., which can sound abrupt and close off discussion.

Correction: Acknowledge the point first, then introduce the counterpoint: 그 말씀도 일리가 있지만, 저는 좀 다르게 생각하는데요. ("That does make some sense, but I think about it a little differently.")

Common Mistake — Guessing instead of asking for clarification

Error: Responding to an unclear point by guessing at its meaning and building a counterargument on a misunderstanding.

Correction: Ask directly before responding: 죄송한데, 무슨 말씀이신지 다시 한번 설명해 주시겠어요? ("Sorry, could you explain once more what you mean?")

Common Mistake — Using -더라도 and -지만 interchangeably

Error: Writing 시간이 부족더라도 준비했어요. to describe a real, already-known situation, when the situation is factual rather than hypothetical.

Correction: Use -지만 or -기는 하지만 for a known, factual contrast: 시간이 부족하기는 하지만 준비했어요. Reserve -더라도 for hypothetical or extreme "even if" cases: 아무리 시간이 부족하더라도 준비할 거예요.
