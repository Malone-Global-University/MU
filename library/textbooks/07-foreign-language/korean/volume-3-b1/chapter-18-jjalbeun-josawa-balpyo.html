짧은 조사와 발표Short Research and Presentation

Integrating sources, structuring a short spoken presentation, and handling audience questions, using quotation, source-attribution, and register systems already active in this course · Speak and Write Korean: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Gather information on a familiar topic from two or three short Korean sources and organize it for a presentation.
  2. Attribute information to its source clearly, using -에 따르면 and -다고 하다 as reviewed in Chapters 6, 13, and 17.
  3. Structure a short spoken presentation using an opening, a body organized by point, and a closing.
  4. Use presentation-structuring expressions such as 먼저, 다음으로, and 마지막으로 to guide a listener through a multi-part talk.
  5. Introduce a presentation and invite questions using fixed presentation-opening and question-inviting frames.
  6. Answer an audience question directly, acknowledge it politely, and, when needed, admit that you do not know the answer.

This chapter introduces no major new grammar system. It consolidates source attribution (Chapter 13), indirect quotation (Chapters 4, 6–15, 17), and formal register control, and applies them to a new communicative task: researching, organizing, and presenting a short topic. Two presentation-management expressions — 발표를 시작하겠습니다 and 질문 있으시면 말씀해 주세요 — are introduced as fixed formulaic frames rather than new grammatical systems.

Opening Scenario

지영, 민준, 라일라, and 수아's Korean class is preparing short five-minute presentations on a topic of their choice, based on two or three short readings the instructor has provided. The instructor explains that a good presentation does not simply repeat the sources word for word — it selects the most useful information, attributes it clearly, organizes it so a listener can follow it without seeing the sources, and leaves room for questions afterward. 라일라 chooses to present on part-time work among Korean university students, drawing on a short news item and a survey summary. She practices opening her talk, walking through her points in order, and preparing for a question she is not sure she can answer.

  1. You will learn how to select and organize information from short sources for a spoken presentation.
  2. You will practice attributing information to sources clearly while presenting.
  3. You will structure an opening, body, and closing, and handle audience questions.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — Presentation pacing and signposting stress

Presenters typically slow down slightly at transition words such as 먼저, 다음으로, and 마지막으로, giving each one a short pause before and after. This is not a new sound-change rule; it is a pacing strategy, like the paraphrase-marking rhythm introduced in Chapter 17, that helps a listener track the structure of a talk without visual slides.

Practice reading the model dialogue below aloud, pausing briefly before each structuring word and keeping your pace slower and more even than in casual conversation.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces no new grammar system. It reviews indirect quotation and source attribution already active since Chapters 4, 6, 13, and 17, and adds a small set of fixed presentation-management expressions used to open a talk, guide the listener through its structure, and invite and answer questions.

1. Review — Attributing Information to a Source (Ch. 6, Ch. 13, Ch. 17)

Review — -에 따르면 and -다고 하다 in a presentation

When presenting research, attribute each piece of information to where it came from, rather than stating it as your own claim. Continue to use -에 따르면 ("according to") and -다고 하다 ("it is said that / [source] says that") exactly as in Chapters 6, 13, and 17.

  • 이 기사에 따르면, 대학생의 절반 정도가 아르바이트를 하고 있습니다.According to this article, about half of university students are working part-time jobs.
  • 조사 결과에 따르면, 학생들은 주로 생활비 때문에 아르바이트를 한다고 합니다.According to the survey results, it is said that students mainly work part-time because of living expenses.

Do not present information from a source as if it were your own personal opinion. Clear attribution is expected in academic and professional Korean presentations, just as it is in formal writing.

2. Structuring a Talk: 먼저 / 다음으로 / 마지막으로 / 결론적으로

Review and expansion — sequencing expressions in a spoken presentation

먼저 ("first"), 다음으로 ("next"), 마지막으로 ("lastly"), and 결론적으로 ("in conclusion") organize a multi-part talk so a listener can follow its structure without seeing an outline. These build on connectors already active in this volume and are applied here specifically to presentation structure.

  • 먼저, 아르바이트를 하는 이유에 대해 말씀드리겠습니다.First, I will talk about the reasons students work part-time.
  • 다음으로, 아르바이트가 학업에 미치는 영향을 살펴보겠습니다.Next, I will look at the effect part-time work has on academics.
  • 마지막으로, 이 조사에서 나온 몇 가지 제안을 소개하겠습니다.Lastly, I will introduce a few suggestions that came out of this research.
  • 결론적으로, 아르바이트는 학생들에게 도움이 되기도 하고 부담이 되기도 합니다.In conclusion, part-time work is both helpful and burdensome for students.

3. Presentation-Management Frames: Opening and Inviting Questions

New expressions — formulaic frames for managing a presentation

발표를 시작하겠습니다 ("I will now begin my presentation") and 질문 있으시면 말씀해 주세요 ("please let me know if you have a question") are fixed formal expressions used to open a talk and invite audience participation. These are formulaic frames, not a new grammatical system — they use the deferential and requestive endings already active in this volume.

  • 안녕하세요. 지금부터 발표를 시작하겠습니다.Hello. I will now begin my presentation.
  • 발표 중간에 질문 있으시면 언제든지 말씀해 주세요.If you have a question during the presentation, please feel free to ask at any time.
  • 이상으로 발표를 마치겠습니다. 질문 있으시면 받겠습니다.That concludes my presentation. I will now take questions.

4. Answering a Question — Acknowledging, Responding, and Admitting Uncertainty

Review — polite acknowledgment and honest uncertainty

When a listener asks a question, acknowledge it politely before answering, and, if you are unsure, say so honestly rather than guessing without signaling uncertainty. Use the supposition and hedging language already active from earlier chapters.

  • 좋은 질문입니다. 제가 조사한 자료에 따르면...That's a good question. According to the material I researched...
  • 죄송하지만 그 부분은 제가 정확히 조사하지 못했습니다.I'm sorry, but I wasn't able to research that part precisely.
Building Block — The short-presentation frame

[발표를 시작하겠습니다] → [먼저 ... / 다음으로 ... / 마지막으로 ..., each point attributed with -에 따르면 / -다고 하다] → [결론적으로 + summary] → [질문 있으시면 말씀해 주세요] → [acknowledge and answer, or admit uncertainty]

Use this five-step frame for any short research presentation in this course. Do not skip the closing question invitation, even for a very short talk — it signals that the presentation is genuinely open to audience interaction.

Practice — Organizing and attributing

  1. Combine the source and the claim using -에 따르면: 조사 결과 / 학생들이 방학에도 아르바이트를 한다.
  2. Order these three points using 먼저, 다음으로, and 마지막으로: 이유 설명하기 / 제안하기 / 영향 설명하기.
  3. Write a polite response to a question you cannot fully answer, using 죄송하지만.

Model Dialogue

라일라 presents her short research topic to the class and takes a question afterward.

Context: classroom presentation followed by a brief question-and-answer exchange. Formal presentation register throughout, addressed to the whole class.

라일라 안녕하세요. 지금부터 대학생 아르바이트에 대한 발표를 시작하겠습니다. Hello. I will now begin my presentation on university students' part-time work.
라일라 먼저, 학생들이 왜 아르바이트를 하는지 말씀드리겠습니다. 한 기사에 따르면, 학생들의 약 60퍼센트가 생활비 때문에 아르바이트를 한다고 합니다. First, I'll tell you why students work part-time. According to one article, about 60 percent of students work part-time because of living expenses.
라일라 다음으로, 아르바이트가 학업에 미치는 영향을 살펴보겠습니다. 조사 결과에 따르면, 아르바이트 시간이 너무 많으면 성적에 부정적인 영향을 줄 수 있다고 합니다. Next, I'll look at the effect part-time work has on academics. According to the survey results, if part-time work hours are too many, it can have a negative effect on grades.
라일라 마지막으로, 이 조사에서 나온 제안을 소개하겠습니다. 전문가들은 일주일에 15시간 이하로 일하는 것이 좋다고 말합니다. Lastly, I'll introduce a suggestion from this research. Experts say it's better to work fewer than 15 hours a week.
라일라 결론적으로, 아르바이트는 학생들에게 경제적으로 도움이 되지만, 시간 관리가 중요하다고 할 수 있습니다. 이상으로 발표를 마치겠습니다. 질문 있으시면 말씀해 주세요. In conclusion, part-time work is economically helpful for students, but time management is important. That concludes my presentation. Please let me know if you have any questions.
민준 raises his hand.
민준 그 기사가 어느 나라 학생들을 조사한 거예요? Which country's students did that article survey?
라일라 좋은 질문입니다. 죄송하지만 그 부분은 기사에 정확히 나와 있지 않아서, 제가 정확히 조사하지 못했습니다. 다시 확인해서 알려 드리겠습니다. That's a good question. I'm sorry, but that part wasn't specified clearly in the article, so I wasn't able to research it precisely. I'll check again and let you know.
지영 발표 잘 들었어요. 조사 결과가 흥미로웠어요. I enjoyed the presentation. The research findings were interesting.

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task asks you to write a short presentation outline and script, following the pattern practiced in 민준's notes above.

  1. Choose a topic and two sources. Use two or three short texts, real or provided by your instructor, on a single familiar topic.
  2. Identify your main points. Select two or three points worth presenting, and note which source supports each one.
  3. Write your opening. Use 발표를 시작하겠습니다 to introduce your topic.
  4. Organize the body. Present each point using 먼저, 다음으로, and 마지막으로, attributing each with -에 따르면 or -다고 하다.
  5. Write your closing. Summarize with 결론적으로 and invite questions with 질문 있으시면 말씀해 주세요.
  6. Revise. Check your draft against the checklist below.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • Each main point is clearly attributed to its source using -에 따르면 or -다고 하다.
  • The talk is organized using 먼저, 다음으로, and 마지막으로.
  • The opening and closing use the presentation-management frames introduced in this chapter.
  • Register is consistently formal throughout.
  • Only Hangeul is used; no romanization appears.
  • Spacing and particles are accurate throughout.
  • No grammar or vocabulary beyond Volume III, Chapters 1–18, appears productively.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Formal presentation etiquette

In Korean academic and workplace settings, a short presentation commonly opens and closes with clear formulaic markers, similar to 발표를 시작하겠습니다 and 이상으로 발표를 마치겠습니다. These markers signal the boundaries of the formal talk clearly to the audience and are widely expected in classroom, academic, and professional presentations.

Culture in Context — Honesty about uncertainty

Admitting that a question cannot be fully answered, and offering to follow up, is generally viewed as more credible than guessing. Presenters commonly use hedged, honest language such as 정확히 조사하지 못했습니다 rather than presenting an unsupported guess as established fact.

Regional Awareness — Presentation conventions vary by setting

The exact phrasing of presentation openings, closings, and question-and-answer transitions can vary somewhat by institution, workplace, and generation. The frames in this chapter represent a common, broadly usable formal standard; some settings may use shorter or longer variants.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Presenting source information as personal opinion

Error: 학생들은 아르바이트 때문에 성적이 나빠져요. stated as a flat personal claim, with no indication that the information comes from a source.

Correction: 조사 결과에 따르면, 아르바이트 시간이 많으면 성적이 나빠질 수 있다고 합니다. ("According to the survey results, it is said that grades can worsen when part-time work hours are high.")

Common Mistake — Skipping the structuring expressions

Error: Moving directly from one point to the next with no transition, so the listener cannot tell where one point ends and the next begins.

Correction: 다음으로, 두 번째 문제에 대해 말씀드리겠습니다. ("Next, I will discuss the second issue.") — a clear structuring expression before the new point.

Common Mistake — Guessing instead of admitting uncertainty

Error: Answering an unfamiliar question with an invented or unsupported guess stated as if it were fact.

Correction: 죄송하지만 그 부분은 정확히 조사하지 못했습니다. 확인 후에 알려 드리겠습니다. ("I'm sorry, but I wasn't able to research that part precisely. I'll let you know after I check.")

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. 라일라 covers, in order: (1) why students work part-time, (2) the effect of part-time work on academics, and (3) a suggestion from the research.
  2. She uses -에 따르면 and -다고 하다 to attribute the 60-percent figure to an article, the negative-grades finding to survey results, and the 15-hour recommendation to experts.
  3. 라일라 acknowledges the question as good, apologizes that the article did not specify the answer, admits she could not research it precisely, and offers to check and follow up.
  4. She uses 먼저, 다음으로, and 마지막으로 to move between her three points, and 결론적으로 to introduce her summary.

Listening comprehension

  • The segment says a presenter should organize source material in their own words rather than reading it aloud directly.
  • It describes introducing the topic first, then explaining researched content, then presenting a conclusion and suggestions.
  • It says stating where information came from is important, though it does not elaborate the reason beyond this.
  • It recommends honestly saying you do not know and offering to check and follow up later.

Reading comprehension

  1. 민준's topic is bicycle use near the university.
  2. He uses a school newspaper article, which reports increased bicycle use over three years, and a student council survey, which reports a lack of bike lanes as the biggest concern.
  3. He plans to suggest that more bike lanes be built near the school.
  4. He notices that the two sources surveyed different groups of people, and plans to disclose this honestly to the audience.
