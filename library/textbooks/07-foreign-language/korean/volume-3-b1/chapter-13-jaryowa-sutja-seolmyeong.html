자료와 숫자 설명Explaining Data and Numbers

Percentages, source attribution with 에 따르면, and describing increase, decrease, and proportion · Speak and Write Korean: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use vocabulary for surveys, results, graphs, and quantity description.
  2. State percentages accurately using Sino-Korean numbers with 퍼센트 or %.
  3. Attribute information to a source using 에 따르면 ("according to").
  4. Describe change over time or between groups using 증가하다 ("to increase") and 감소하다 ("to decrease"), and everyday equivalents 늘다/줄다.
  5. Describe a share or proportion of a whole using 차지하다 ("to make up, to account for").
  6. Combine data description with the comparison and preference frames from Chapter 12 to support a conclusion.
  7. Read and interpret a short chart or graph description in Korean, identifying what is measured, the source, and the trend.
  8. Write and present a short spoken or written summary of data that states a source, a key figure, a trend, and a conclusion.

This chapter introduces percentage and statistical-description language as new productive systems. It builds directly on the comparison, superlative, and preference-reasoning patterns from Chapter 12 (보다, 가장/제일, 중에서, -는 게 더 낫다/좋다) by using them to support conclusions drawn from data. It does not introduce new speech levels or new honorific systems.

Opening Scenario

After the group voted to recommend the market tour in Chapter 12, the instructor has asked each student to support the class's final decision with real data. 지영 finds a short survey about how much time international students spend using Korean outside class, and the group must summarize it, describe the trend it shows, and connect the numbers back to their earlier recommendation. 민준 is especially interested in how the numbers compare across different activities, while 라일라 wants to make sure the presentation clearly states where the data came from.

  1. You will build vocabulary for surveys, results, and quantity description.
  2. You will learn to state percentages, attribute information to a source with 에 따르면, and describe increase, decrease, and proportion.
  3. You will combine data description with the comparison and preference language from Chapter 12 to support a conclusion with evidence.

발음 — Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation — Sino-Korean numbers before 퍼센트

Percentages use Sino-Korean numbers, recycled from earlier volumes, spoken smoothly together with 퍼센트: 이십오 퍼센트 ("twenty-five percent"), 오십 퍼센트 ("fifty percent"). Listen for the batchim of 십 linking naturally into the following syllable when a number like 십일 ("eleven") precedes 퍼센트, an instance of liaison recycled from earlier chapters.

Also practice the pronunciation of 증가하다 and 감소하다: both are Sino-Korean compounds, and each syllable should be pronounced clearly and evenly, without the vowel reduction that might occur in faster native-vocabulary verbs. This formal, deliberate rhythm is typical of news and academic pronunciation, previewed receptively here and developed further in Volume IV.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces percentage description, source attribution, and change/proportion language as new productive systems, and connects them to the comparison and preference frames already active from Chapter 12.

1. Stating Percentages and Proportions

Grammar — Sino-Korean number + 퍼센트/%

Percentages are formed with a Sino-Korean number followed by 퍼센트, recycled from the Sino-Korean number system introduced in Volume I and expanded across Volume II. 약 ("approximately") may precede the number when the figure is rounded, and 거의 ("almost") or 절반 ("half") may replace an exact figure in less formal contexts.

  • 응답자의 약 60퍼센트가 한국 드라마를 통해 한국어를 배우기 시작했어요. — "About 60 percent of respondents began learning Korean through Korean dramas."
  • 거의 절반이 매일 한국어로 일기를 써요. — "Almost half write a diary in Korean every day."
  • 학생의 30퍼센트가 시장 투어를 선택했어요. Thirty percent of the students chose the market tour. 30퍼센트 + 가 (subject); 학생의 (possessive)
  • 전체의 절반 정도가 등산에 관심이 있었어요. About half of the total were interested in hiking. 전체의 절반 정도 (approximate proportion)

2. Attributing Information with 에 따르면

Grammar — Noun + 에 따르면

에 따르면 attaches to a noun naming a source — a survey, a report, an article, or a person — and means "according to ~." It typically opens the sentence, with the reported information following. This is the first formal source-attribution frame in the series and previews the fuller quotation and citation systems developed later in Volume III and Volume IV.

에 따르면 is commonly followed by a clause ending in -다고 하다 or by a plain statement of the result, depending on how directly the source is being quoted.

  • 이 조사에 따르면 학생들의 70퍼센트가 시장 투어를 가장 좋아해요. — "According to this survey, 70 percent of students like the market tour best."
  • 지영의 자료에 따르면 등산을 선택한 학생의 수가 지난달보다 늘었어요. — "According to 지영's data, the number of students who chose hiking has increased compared to last month."
  • 이 조사에 따르면 응답자의 절반이 한국어 뉴스를 이해할 수 있어요. According to this survey, half of the respondents can understand Korean news. 이 조사 + 에 따르면 (source attribution)

3. Describing Change and Share: 증가하다/감소하다, 늘다/줄다, 차지하다

Grammar — Change and proportion verbs

증가하다 ("to increase") and 감소하다 ("to decrease") are formal, written-register action verbs common in reports and news. 늘다 and 줄다 express the same meanings in a more everyday register and are equally productive in this chapter. All four verbs are often used with -보다 to compare two points in time, recycling the comparison pattern from Chapter 12.

차지하다 ("to make up, to account for") describes what portion of a whole a category represents, and is typically used with a percentage or proportion expression as its object.

  • 시장 투어를 선택한 학생 수가 작년보다 증가했어요. — "The number of students who chose the market tour increased compared to last year."
  • 박물관을 선택한 학생은 전체의 20퍼센트를 차지해요. — "Students who chose the museum make up 20 percent of the total."
  • 등산을 선택하는 학생 수가 점점 줄고 있어요. The number of students choosing hiking is gradually decreasing. 줄다 + -고 있다 (ongoing, recycled from Ch. 2)
  • 시장 투어가 전체 응답의 절반 이상을 차지해요. The market tour accounts for more than half of all responses. 차지하다 + 절반 이상 (more than half)
Building Block — The data-description-and-conclusion frame

[source: (자료/조사) 에 따르면] + [figure: (약) 숫자퍼센트가 ~해요 / ~을/를 차지해요] + [trend: 증가하다/감소하다/늘다/줄다] + [conclusion, using Ch. 12 comparison/preference: 그래서 A가 B보다 더 ~라고 생각해요 / A에 가는 게 더 낫다고 생각해요]

This frame lets learners cite a source, state a figure or share, describe a trend, and connect the data back to a comparative conclusion or preference — combining this chapter's new systems with the comparison-and-preference frame from Chapter 12.

Model Dialogue

지영, 민준, 라일라, and 수아 review survey results to support the class's field trip recommendation.

Context: classroom, small group discussion preparing a short presentation for the instructor. Everyday polite style, informal but respectful discussion between classmates.

지영 이 조사에 따르면 학생들의 65퍼센트가 시장 투어를 가장 좋아한다고 응답했어요. According to this survey, 65 percent of students responded that they like the market tour best.
민준 그러면 박물관과 등산은 나머지 35퍼센트를 차지하는 거죠? Then the museum and hiking make up the remaining 35 percent, right?
지영 네, 맞아요. 박물관은 전체의 20퍼센트, 등산은 15퍼센트를 차지해요. Yes, that's right. The museum makes up 20 percent of the total, and hiking makes up 15 percent.
라일라 작년 자료에 따르면 시장 투어를 선택한 학생 수가 계속 증가했어요. 재작년에는 40퍼센트였는데 지금은 65퍼센트예요. According to last year's data, the number of students choosing the market tour has kept increasing. Two years ago it was 40 percent, and now it's 65 percent.
수아 반대로 등산을 선택하는 학생 수는 점점 줄고 있네요. On the other hand, the number of students choosing hiking is gradually decreasing.
민준 이 자료를 보면 시장 투어가 박물관이나 등산보다 훨씬 더 인기가 많다는 게 확실해요. Looking at this data, it's clear that the market tour is much more popular than the museum or hiking.
라일라 그래서 저는 이 자료를 근거로 시장 투어를 추천하는 게 더 낫다고 생각해요. So I think it's better to recommend the market tour, based on this data.

내용 이해 — Comprehension questions:

  2. What share of the total do the museum and hiking each account for?
  3. How has the percentage choosing the market tour changed since two years ago?
  4. What conclusion does 라일라 draw, and what does she base it on?

Writing Workshop

This chapter's writing task asks you to summarize a set of data, describe a trend, and connect it to a supported conclusion using the comparison and preference frames from Chapter 12.

  1. Choose or invent a simple data set. For example, a survey of classmates' habits, preferences, or activities with approximate percentages.
  2. Name your source. Use (자료/조사)에 따르면 to open your summary.
  3. State the key figures. Use Sino-Korean numbers with 퍼센트, and 차지하다 where useful.
  4. Describe the trend. Use 증가하다/감소하다 or 늘다/줄다 to describe change over time or between groups.

체크리스트 — Editing checklist:

  • 에 따르면 is used correctly to name a source.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Citing a source before stating a figure

In Korean academic, journalistic, and workplace presentations, it is standard practice to name the source of a figure before stating the figure itself, using a frame like 이 조사에 따르면. Presenting a percentage without naming where it came from can make a claim sound unsupported, even in an informal class presentation.

Culture in Context — Using data to support a group decision

Building on the group-decision practices from Chapter 12, Korean classroom, workplace, and community discussions often move from individual opinions toward a shared recommendation supported by concrete evidence, such as a survey result or a comparison of figures. Citing data, as 라일라 and 민준 do here, is a common way to strengthen a recommendation before it is finalized, for example by a vote.

Register Awareness — 증가하다/감소하다 versus 늘다/줄다

증가하다 and 감소하다 are common in news reports, academic writing, and formal presentations, while 늘다 and 줄다 are equally correct but sound more like everyday conversation. Both pairs are treated as productive and level-appropriate in this chapter; Volume IV will refine the distinction between formal and everyday statistical language further.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Placing 에 따르면 after the figure

Error: Writing 응답자의 65퍼센트가 시장 투어를 좋아해요 이 조사에 따르면., adding the source attribution as an afterthought at the end of the sentence.

Correction: 에 따르면 normally opens the sentence, naming the source before the reported information: 이 조사에 따르면 응답자의 65퍼센트가 시장 투어를 좋아해요.

Common Mistake — Confusing 차지하다 with a plain existence statement

Error: Writing 박물관은 20퍼센트가 있어요., treating a proportion as if it were simple possession or existence.

Correction: Use 차지하다 to describe a share of a whole: 박물관은 전체의 20퍼센트를 차지해요. ("The museum makes up 20 percent of the total.")

Common Mistake — Stating a trend with no comparison point

Error: Writing only 시장 투어가 증가했어요. with no indication of what point in time or group is being compared, leaving the trend unclear.

Correction: Name the comparison point, recycling 보다 from Chapter 12: 시장 투어를 선택한 학생 수가 작년보다 증가했어요.

  4. 라일라 concludes that it is better to recommend the market tour, based on the data showing it is much more popular than the other two options.

Listening comprehension

  • 45 percent of students use online streaming, which has increased compared to three years ago.
  • Participation in in-person meetup groups has decreased, now at about 20 percent.
  • The percentage of students who exercise is about 30 percent, with little change.
  • The survey suggests that university students' leisure activities are becoming more individual/personal over time.

Reading comprehension

  2. The market tour's percentage increased significantly compared to last year, while hiking's percentage decreased.
  3. 민준 concludes that it is better to go on the market tour, based on the data showing it is far more popular than the other two options.

Section D — Sample response

이 조사에 따르면 우리 반 학생의 55퍼센트가 온라인 강의로 공부해요. 반면에 혼자 공부하는 학생은 25퍼센트, 그룹 스터디를 하는 학생은 20퍼센트를 차지해요. 작년 자료와 비교하면 온라인 강의를 선택하는 학생 수가 계속 증가했고, 혼자 공부하는 학생 수는 감소했어요. 이 자료를 보면 온라인 강의가 다른 방법보다 훨씬 더 인기가 많다는 것을 알 수 있어요. 그래서 저는 이 자료를 근거로 온라인 강의로 공부하는 게 더 낫다고 생각해요.
