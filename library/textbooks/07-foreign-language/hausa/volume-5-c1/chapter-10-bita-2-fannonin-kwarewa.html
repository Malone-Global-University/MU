Bita 2: Fannonin Ƙwarewa

C1–C2-oriented Specialization · Chapter 10 of 20, Unit II Assessment · Speak and Write Hausa: Advanced Precision and Specialization
Unit II Assessment — Integrating Chapters 6–9 · No New Vocabulary Domain, No New Grammar

Chapter Overview

Chapter 10 is the formal integrated assessment for Unit II, Fannonin Ƙwarewa (Domains of Expertise). It does not introduce a new vocabulary domain or any new grammar. Instead, it draws together the four specialized domains built across Chapters 6 through 9 — academic and research Hausa, business and finance Hausa, health, science, and technical Hausa, and legal, governance, and policy Hausa — and asks learners to move across all four in a single connected task. Amina, Ibrahim, Salamatu, and Zainab bring their four separate projects together: Amina's research summary, Ibrahim's neighborhood water-use notice, Salamatu's business plan, and Zainab's health-information text. The peer-mentoring circle spends this session helping one another present, cross-question, and refine all four pieces, modeling the kind of flexible movement between specialized registers that this unit has been building toward.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Recall and correctly use core vocabulary from the academic, business, health, and legal/governance domains introduced in Chapters 6–9.
  2. Move between these four specialized registers within a single connected conversation or text without confusing their vocabulary or tone.
  3. Identify the purpose, key claim, and audience of a short text from any of the four domains.
  4. Restate, summarize, and explain domain-specific content using sentence patterns consolidated through Chapter 5.
  5. Ask and answer clarification and follow-up questions across all four domains.
  6. Distinguish the register and caution appropriate to each domain, especially the neutral caution required for health and legal/governance content.
  7. Complete an integrated listening, speaking, reading, and writing assessment that draws on all four domains together.
  8. Identify areas of the four domains that need further personal review before continuing to Unit III.

Communication Scenario

Amina, Ibrahim, Salamatu, and Zainab meet to review the four projects each of them has been developing since Chapter 6: Amina's research summary, Salamatu's business plan, Zainab's health-information text, and Ibrahim's neighborhood water-use notice. Each person presents their project to the group, and the others ask questions, offer feedback, and help identify anything unclear or out of register, before the group produces a short combined update covering all four projects for a shared community bulletin.

  1. Domain: cumulative review and integration of academic, business, health, and legal/governance Hausa from Chapters 6–9.
  2. Target skill: moving accurately between four specialized vocabulary domains within already-consolidated grammar patterns.
  3. Grammar status: identifying predicates, sequencing, reference tracking, and causal explanation remain at Consolidated status; no new grammar is introduced.
  4. No unscheduled grammar beyond the Volume IV level, and no vocabulary domain beyond the four domains already scheduled in Chapters 6–9.
  5. Health and legal/governance content remains descriptive and cautious; the chapter does not offer medical or legal advice or take a position on any specific real-world policy or health claim.

Pronunciation Review

Sound Focus — Shifting register across four domains

No new pronunciation feature is introduced in this chapter. Learners instead apply consolidated tone and vowel-length control while shifting smoothly between four delivery styles already practiced: the measured explanatory tone of Chapter 6, the negotiating firmness of Chapter 7, the reassuring clarity of Chapter 8, and the careful neutrality of Chapter 9. Pay particular attention to maintaining the tone and vowel-length distinctions among near-homophonous domain terms, such as doka and ƙa’ida from Chapter 9, when moving quickly between topics.

Grammar and Pattern Review

This chapter introduces no new grammar. It reviews and integrates the identifying-predicate pattern, sequencing connectors, reference tracking, and causal-explanatory patterns already consolidated through Chapter 5, as applied across all four Unit II domains.

10.1 Identifying and describing a decision or finding

Recycles the identifying-predicate pattern used for a research finding (Ch. 6), a business plan point (Ch. 7), a health recommendation (Ch. 8), and a civic decision (Ch. 9).

  • Abin da Amina ta gano a binciken nata shi ne cewa yara da yawa ba sa samun isasshen ruwa mai tsafta a unguwarmu. What Amina found in her research is that many children do not get enough clean water in our neighborhood.

    • 10.2 Restating an announcement or recommendation

    Recycles the reported-explanation pattern from Chapter 6, applied across a business plan, a health recommendation, and a civic announcement.

  • Salamatu ta ce idan an fara sayar da kayan a kasuwar Litinin, sai riba ta ƙaru cikin wata biyu. Salamatu said that if the goods begin selling on Monday market days, profit will increase within two months.

    • 10.3 Explaining a reason and its effect

    Recycles the causal-explanatory pattern already consolidated in earlier volumes and applied in Sections 8.3 and 9.3, here moving between health and civic effects in the same conversation.

  • Ruwa mara tsafta na haifar da cuta, saboda haka Zainab ta shawarci iyalai su tafasa ruwa kafin sha, kamar yadda sabuwar ƙa’idar hukuma ta bukaci a yi taka-tsantsan. Unclean water causes illness, so Zainab has advised families to boil water before drinking, just as the new authority regulation calls for caution.

    • 10.4 Summarizing across domains

    Recycles the summarizing pattern from Section 6.2 and the reporting pattern from Sections 8.4 and 9.4, here condensing points from more than one domain into a single connected update.

  • Idan na taƙaita dukkan ayyukanmu, binciken Amina ya nuna matsalar ruwa, shirin kasuwancin Salamatu zai taimaka wa iyalai samun kuɗi, shawarar Zainab za ta rage cuta, kuma sabuwar ƙa’idar hukuma za ta raba ruwa daidai wa daida. If I summarize all our projects, Amina's research shows the water problem, Salamatu's business plan will help families earn money, Zainab's advice will reduce illness, and the new authority regulation will distribute water fairly.
    • Language Note — Review, not new structure

    Every pattern in Sections 10.1–10.4 was already consolidated by the end of Chapter 5, and every domain term was already introduced in Chapters 6–9. The task in this chapter is to move between four specialized vocabulary domains fluently within a single connected exchange, not to learn anything new.

    Common learner error: Do not let vocabulary from one domain bleed into another — for example, using ƙa’ida (regulation) when describing a business plan point, or using shirin kasuwanci (business plan) when describing a research finding. Keep each domain's vocabulary in its own context, as modeled in Sections 10.1–10.4.

    Model Dialogue

    Yanayin hali: Amina, Ibrahim, Salamatu, da Zainab sun taru domin gabatar da ayyukansu guda huɗu ga juna kafin taron unguwa mai zuwa.

    (Setting: Amina, Ibrahim, Salamatu, and Zainab have gathered to present their four projects to one another before an upcoming neighborhood meeting.)

    AMINA Bincikena ya nuna cewa yara da yawa ba sa samun isasshen ruwa mai tsafta a unguwarmu, musamman a lokacin rani.My research shows that many children do not get enough clean water in our neighborhood, especially during the dry season.
    IBRAHIM Wannan ya dace da sabuwar ƙa’idar hukumar unguwa da na bayyana a taronmu na baya — ita ma game da amfani da ruwa ce.That matches the new neighborhood authority regulation I explained at our last meeting — it's also about water use.
    SALAMATU Shirin kasuwancina zai taimaka, domin zan sayar da tulunan ruwa masu araha ga iyalai domin su iya ajiye ruwa cikin tsafta.My business plan will help, because I'll sell affordable water storage containers so families can keep water clean.
    ZAINAB Kuma ni na shirya gajeriyar sanarwar lafiya da ke gaya wa iyalai su tafasa ruwa kafin sha domin gujewa cuta.And I've prepared a short health notice telling families to boil water before drinking to avoid illness.
    AMINA Idan muka haɗa dukkan ayyukanmu, za mu iya gabatar da cikakken bayani ga taron unguwa: matsalar, ƙa’idar hukuma, dabarar kasuwanci, da shawarar lafiya.If we combine all our projects, we can present a complete picture to the neighborhood meeting: the problem, the authority's regulation, the business idea, and the health advice.
    IBRAHIM Ina tsammanin ya kamata mu fara da bincikenki, domin shi ne ke bayyana dalilin dukkan sauran ayyukanmu.I think we should start with your research, since it explains the reason behind all our other projects.
    SALAMATU To, bari mu rubuta gajeriyar sanarwa guda ɗaya da za mu gabatar tare, domin kada mu maimaita magana.Okay, let's write a single short combined update to present together, so we don't repeat ourselves.
    ZAINAB Yarda. Zan taimaka wajen tabbatar da cewa bangaren lafiya ya kasance a bayyane amma ba ya ba da shawarar kai tsaye kan kowane mutum.Agreed. I'll help make sure the health part is clear but doesn't give direct advice to any specific individual.

    Tambayoyin fahimta — Comprehension questions:

    1. What did Amina's research find?
    2. How does Ibrahim's regulation connect to Amina's finding?
    3. What does Salamatu's business plan offer?
    4. What does Zainab's health notice recommend?
    5. Why do the four decide to combine their projects into a single update?

    Reading Assessment

    Read the short combined bulletin below, which the group has prepared for the neighborhood meeting. Identify the purpose, key points, and audience of each part.

    Karatu — Sanarwar Haɗin Kai (Reading: A Combined Community Bulletin)

    Wannan sanarwa ta ƙunshi taƙaitaccen bayani daga ayyuka guda huɗu da wasu matasa a unguwarmu suka gudanar. Na farko, wani bincike ya nuna cewa yara da yawa ba sa samun isasshen ruwa mai tsafta, musamman a lokacin rani. Na biyu, hukumar unguwarmu ta amsa ta hanyar ƙirƙiro sabuwar ƙa’ida kan lokutan amfani da ruwa, domin kowane gida ya sami ruwa daidai wa daida; wannan ƙa’ida ba doka ba ce ta gwamnati mai girma, sai dai ƙa’idar hukumar unguwa ce kawai. Na uku, an fara wani shirin kasuwanci na sayar da tulunan ruwa masu araha, domin taimaka wa iyalai ajiye ruwa cikin tsafta ba tare da kashe kuɗi mai yawa ba. Na huɗu, an shirya gajeriyar shawarar lafiya da ke gaya wa iyalai su tafasa ruwa kafin sha domin gujewa cuta; wannan shawara ce ta gaba ɗaya kuma ba ta maye gurbin ganawa da likita ba idan akwai matsalar lafiya ta musamman. Kowa na da hakkin ya tuntuɓi hukumar unguwa idan yana da tambaya kan ƙa’idar da aka bayyana.

    Tambayoyin fahimta — Comprehension questions:

    1. What is the purpose of this combined bulletin?
    2. What did the research find, and what did the authority do in response?
    3. What does the business plan part of the bulletin offer, and to whom?
    4. What caution does the health part of the bulletin include?
    5. Write a three-sentence summary of this bulletin in your own words, mentioning all four domains.

    Jerin binciken gyara — Editing checklist:

    • Each of the four domain sentences uses vocabulary correctly matched to its own domain.
    • The combined bulletin flows as one connected text, not four disconnected statements.
    • The health sentence remains general and cautious, without diagnosing or prescribing.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Community projects across specialized domains

    Community initiatives often draw on several kinds of expertise at once: a small piece of research, a business idea, health guidance, and a local regulation may all address the same practical problem from different angles. Amina, Ibrahim, Salamatu, and Zainab's collaboration reflects a common and valuable pattern in which neighbors with different skills combine their contributions into a single, coherent community update rather than working in isolation.

    Regional Variation — Domain register across contexts

    The specific vocabulary, institutions, and conventions associated with research, business, health, and governance communication vary across Hausa-speaking regions and countries. Learners should treat the four projects in this chapter as illustrative examples of specialized registers, not as a description of any single country's actual academic, commercial, medical, or governmental systems, and should verify local terminology and institutions when using this vocabulary in a real setting.

    Common Mistakes

    Common Mistake — Mixing vocabulary across domains

    Error: Using a business term such as shirin kasuwanci to describe a civic regulation, or a legal/governance term such as ƙa’ida to describe a business plan.

    Correction: Keep each domain's vocabulary within its own context, as modeled in Sections 10.1–10.4 and the Model Dialogue.

    Common Mistake — Losing register control when combining domains

    Error: Writing a combined update in which the health section sounds like a firm business pitch, or the civic section sounds like a personal opinion rather than a neutral explanation.

    Correction: Maintain each domain's appropriate register — reassuring but cautious for health, neutral for civic content, as modeled in the Reading Assessment bulletin — even when the domains appear side by side.

    Common Mistake — Treating the combined update as four separate texts

    Error: Writing four disconnected sentences with no transitions, rather than one coherent combined bulletin.

    Correction: Use the sequencing and reference-tracking patterns already consolidated in earlier volumes to connect the four parts smoothly, as modeled in Section 10.4.

    3. Salamatu's business plan offers affordable water storage containers to help families keep water clean.
    4. Zainab's health notice recommends boiling water before drinking to avoid illness.

    Kula na sauraro (Listening responses)

    (1) Amina's research identifies that many children lack enough clean water, especially in the dry season. (2) The authority created a new regulation about water-use times. (3) Salamatu's business plan sells affordable water storage containers to help families keep water clean. (4) Zainab's notice recommends boiling water before drinking. (5) They combine their projects to present a complete update at the upcoming neighborhood meeting.

    Tambayoyin karatu (Reading comprehension)

    1. The bulletin's purpose is to combine four community projects — research, regulation, business, and health advice — into one shared update for the neighborhood.
    2. The research found a shortage of clean water for children; the authority responded with a new water-use time regulation.
    3. The business plan offers affordable water storage containers so families can keep water clean without high cost.
    4. The health portion notes that its advice is general and does not replace seeing a doctor for a specific health problem.
    5. Sample summary: A neighborhood research project found that many children lack enough clean water, especially in the dry season. In response, the local authority created a new water-use regulation, a resident started a business selling affordable water containers, and another resident shared health advice about boiling water before drinking.
