Unit II Continues — Health, Science, and Technical Hausa · New Vocabulary Domain, Consolidated Grammar

Chapter Overview

Chapter 8 continues Unit II, Fannonin Ƙwarewa (Domains of Expertise), by turning from business and finance Hausa to the language of health, science, and technical public information. As in Chapter 7, this chapter introduces no new grammar; every sentence pattern needed was already consolidated by the end of Chapter 5. What is new is a specialized vocabulary domain: the language of describing symptoms, understanding a health worker's advice, and interpreting a short public-health notice. Zainab, who has mentioned in earlier chapters that her aunt works at a community health clinic, brings the group a practical task this time: her aunt has asked her to help explain a public-health notice about seasonal illness prevention to neighbors who may not read it easily on their own. Amina, Ibrahim, and Salamatu continue their peer-mentoring circle, this time helping Zainab prepare to interpret and present the notice clearly.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify and use core health, science, and technical vocabulary: lafiya, cuta, alama, magani, rigakafi, likita, dakin gwaje-gwaje, and sanarwa.
  2. Read a short public-health notice and identify its purpose, the risk it describes, and the recommended action.
  3. Describe common symptoms using consolidated sentence patterns.
  4. Understand and restate a health worker's basic advice.
  5. Explain a simple cause-and-prevention relationship in a health or science context.
  6. Prepare a short spoken or written explanation of a public-health notice for a general audience.
  7. Distinguish the register of a public-health notice from the business register studied in Chapter 7.
  8. Complete an integrated listening, speaking, reading, and writing task built around a health and science scenario.

Communication Scenario

Zainab's aunt, who works at a community health clinic, has asked Zainab to help explain a short public-health notice about seasonal illness prevention to neighbors who find the printed notice hard to follow. Before she does, Zainab brings the notice to Amina, Ibrahim, and Salamatu so they can help her understand it fully, practice describing symptoms and advice clearly, and prepare a simple spoken explanation for her neighbors.

  1. Domain: describing symptoms, restating health advice, and interpreting a public-health notice.
  2. Target skill: using the new health, science, and technical vocabulary domain within already-consolidated grammar patterns.
  3. Grammar status: identifying predicates, sequencing, reference tracking, and causal explanation remain at Consolidated status, as in Chapters 5 through 7; no new grammar is introduced.
  4. No unscheduled grammar beyond the Volume IV level, and no vocabulary domain beyond health, science, and technical Hausa as scheduled for this chapter.

Pronunciation Focus

Sound Focus — The careful clarity of health explanation

No new pronunciation feature is introduced in this chapter. Learners instead apply consolidated tone and vowel-length control to a seventh delivery style: the careful, unhurried clarity needed when explaining health information to someone who may be anxious or unfamiliar with technical terms, distinct from the negotiating firmness of Chapter 7 and the academic evenness of Chapter 6. Pay particular attention to the vowel length in lafiya and the tone contrast between cuta (illness) and cutar-type possessed forms encountered in the reading passage, since a rushed or unclear delivery of health vocabulary can cause real confusion.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces no new grammar. It applies the identifying-predicate pattern, sequencing connectors, reference tracking, and causal-explanatory patterns already consolidated through Chapter 5 to the new health, science, and technical vocabulary domain.

8.1 Describing a symptom

Describing a symptom commonly opens by identifying the sign observed, recycling the identifying-predicate pattern used for a business plan in Section 7.1.

  • Alamar da nake gani a jikinsa ita ce zazzabi da ciwon kai. The symptom I see in his body is fever and headache.

    • 8.2 Restating a health worker's advice

    Restating advice recycles the sequencing and reported-explanation patterns from Chapter 6, applied to a doctor's recommendation.

  • Likita ya ce idan alamun sun ci gaba, sai a je dakin gwaje-gwaje domin a duba lafiyar jiki sosai. The doctor said that if the symptoms continue, one should go to the laboratory so that one's health can be examined thoroughly.

    • 8.3 Explaining cause and prevention

    Explaining a cause-and-prevention relationship recycles the causal and explanatory patterns already consolidated in earlier volumes and applied to profit and loss in Section 7.3, here applied to a health outcome.

  • Mutane da yawa suna kamuwa da wannan cuta a lokacin damina, domin sauro yana ƙaruwa a wannan lokacin; saboda haka, rigakafi kamar amfani da gidan sauro yana da muhimmanci. Many people contract this illness during the rainy season, because mosquitoes increase at that time; therefore, prevention such as using a mosquito net is important.

    • 8.4 Interpreting a public-health notice (sanarwa)

    Interpreting a notice recycles the summarizing pattern from Section 6.2 and the reporting pattern from Section 7.4, applied to condensing a public-information text into its main points for a neighborhood audience.

  • Idan na taƙaita sanarwar, tana gaya wa jama’a cewa a lura da alamun zazzabi, a je asibiti da wuri idan an gan su, kuma a yi amfani da rigakafi domin kare kai daga cuta. If I summarize the notice, it tells the public to watch for fever symptoms, to go to the clinic early if they see them, and to use prevention to protect oneself from illness.
    • Language Note — New vocabulary, familiar grammar

    Every sentence pattern in Sections 8.1–8.4 was already consolidated by the end of Chapter 5. The task in this chapter is to redeploy those patterns — identifying a symptom, restating advice, explaining cause and prevention, and summarizing a notice — using the new health, science, and technical vocabulary domain rather than to learn a new structure.

    Common learner error: Do not confuse cuta (illness, the condition) with alama (symptom, a sign of the condition). A symptom points to an illness but is not the illness itself, and the two should not be described with the same word.

    Model Dialogue

    Yanayin hali: Zainab ta kawo wa Amina, Ibrahim, da Salamatu wata sanarwa ta kiwon lafiya da inna ta ba ta domin a bayyana wa maƙwabta.

    (Setting: Zainab brings Amina, Ibrahim, and Salamatu a public-health notice her aunt gave her to explain to the neighbors.)

    ZAINAB Innata ta ba ni wannan sanarwa domin in bayyana wa maƙwabtanmu, amma ina son mu fahimce ta sosai tare tukuna.My aunt gave me this notice to explain to our neighbors, but I want us to understand it well together first.
    AMINA To, menene alamun cutar da sanarwar take magana a kansu?Okay, what are the symptoms of the illness the notice is talking about?
    ZAINAB Alamun sun haɗa da zazzabi, ciwon kai, da gajiya fiye da yadda aka saba.The symptoms include fever, headache, and unusual tiredness.
    IBRAHIM Idan wani ya ga waɗannan alamu a jikinsa, me sanarwar ta ce ya kamata ya yi?If someone sees these symptoms in their body, what does the notice say they should do?
    ZAINAB Ta ce kada a jira, sai a je asibiti da wuri domin likita ya duba, kuma idan ya kamata, a je dakin gwaje-gwaje.It says one should not wait, but should go to the clinic early so a doctor can examine them, and if necessary, go to the laboratory.
    SALAMATU Me ya sa cutar take yawaita a wannan lokacin na shekara?Why does the illness become more common at this time of year?
    ZAINAB Domin sauro yana ƙaruwa a lokacin damina, kuma sauro shi ne mafi yawan dalilin yaɗuwar cutar.Because mosquitoes increase during the rainy season, and mosquitoes are the main reason the illness spreads.
    AMINA To, wace irin rigakafi sanarwar take ba da shawara a kai?So what kind of prevention does the notice recommend?
    ZAINAB Ta ce a yi amfani da gidan sauro da dare, a rufe ruwa da ke tsaye kusa da gida, kuma a yi allurar rigakafi idan asibiti ya bayar da ita.It says to use a mosquito net at night, to cover standing water near the home, and to get the preventive vaccine if the clinic provides it.
    IBRAHIM Wannan sanarwa ce mai kyau. Za mu taimake ki ki taƙaita ta domin maƙwabtanki su fahimta cikin sauƙi.This is a good notice. We'll help you summarize it so your neighbors can understand it easily.

    Tambayoyin fahimta — Comprehension questions:

    1. What task has Zainab's aunt given her?
    2. What three symptoms does Zainab mention?
    3. What does the notice recommend someone do if they notice these symptoms?
    4. Why does the illness become more common during the rainy season?
    5. Which vocabulary item from this chapter refers to a sign or symptom?

    Speaking Practice

    Atisayen ƙa’ida — Controlled substitution

    With a partner, take turns describing a symptom using the pattern in Section 8.1, substituting a different everyday symptom each time (fever, cough, stomach pain, fatigue).

    Tattaunawa — Communicative task

    In a group of three or four, follow the Model Dialogue's structure: one person describes symptoms, a second asks what should be done, a third explains a cause and prevention method, and a fourth summarizes the exchange as a short spoken notice.

    Madadin karatu kai tsaye — Solo alternative

    Record yourself presenting the doctor's advice from the Listening Practice aloud, then record yourself summarizing it in your own words in two or three sentences, as a short spoken notice for neighbors.

    Reading Practice

    Read the short public-health notice below, which Zainab's aunt gave her to explain to the neighbors. Identify its purpose, the risk it describes, and the recommended action.

    Karatu — Sanarwar Kiwon Lafiya (Reading: A Public-Health Notice)

    Wannan sanarwa daga asibitin unguwarmu ce, domin sanar da jama’a game da cutar da take yawaita a lokacin damina. Alamun cutar sun haɗa da zazzabi, ciwon kai, da gajiya fiye da yadda aka saba. Idan wani a cikin iyalinku ya nuna waɗannan alamu, kada ku jira, sai ku kawo shi asibiti da wuri domin likita ya duba shi, kuma idan ya zama dole, a yi gwaji a dakin gwaje-gwaje. Babban dalilin yaɗuwar wannan cuta shi ne sauro, wanda yake ƙaruwa a lokacin damina saboda ruwan sama da ke tsayawa kusa da gidaje. Saboda haka, asibiti yana ba da shawarar cewa a yi amfani da gidan sauro da dare, a rufe ko a zubar da ruwa da ke tsaye kusa da gida, kuma a je asibiti domin allurar rigakafi idan ana da ita. Kula da lafiyar iyalinku shi ne mafi muhimmancin abu a wannan lokaci na shekara.

    Tambayoyin fahimta — Comprehension questions:

    1. What is the purpose of this notice?
    2. What three symptoms does the notice list?
    3. What is stated as the main cause of the illness spreading?
    4. What three prevention actions does the notice recommend?
    5. Write a two-sentence summary of this notice in your own words.

    1. Karanta misalai da hankali. Reread Sections 8.1–8.4, the Model Dialogue, and the Listening and Reading passages.
    2. Zaɓi wata ƙaramar matsalar lafiya ta yau da kullum don yin bayani a kanta. Choose a small, everyday health concern you can describe (a cold, a headache, seasonal allergies).
    3. Rubuta jumlar bayanin alama. Write a symptom-description sentence, following Section 8.1.
    4. Rubuta ɗan shawarar likita. Write a short restated piece of health advice, following Section 8.2.
    5. Rubuta bayanin dalili da rigakafi. Explain a cause and a prevention method, following Section 8.3.
    6. Rubuta gajeriyar sanarwa. Write a short public-health notice summarizing your health topic, following Section 8.4.
    7. Duba jerin bincike. Check your text against the checklist below.
    8. Rubuta siffa ta ƙarshe. Produce a clean final version of your short notice, then prepare to present it aloud, following the Speaking Practice instructions.

    Jerin binciken gyara — Editing checklist:

    • The symptom description clearly states what sign or signs are present.
    • The restated advice is clear and matches what a health worker would realistically say.
    • Cause and prevention are described accurately, and cuta and alama are not confused.
    • Any reason given for the illness spreading is clear and relevant.
    • The notice remains a concise, calm summary and does not cause unnecessary alarm.
    • Special characters (ɓ, ɗ, ƙ, ƴ) and apostrophe/glottal conventions are used accurately.
    • No unscheduled grammar beyond the Volume IV level is required to understand the text.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Community trust in health information

    In many Hausa-speaking communities, health information delivered by a trusted family member, neighbor, or community health worker is often taken more seriously than the same information printed alone on a notice board. Zainab's task of explaining her aunt's clinic notice to neighbors reflects a widespread and valuable community practice of translating public-health information into personal, trusted communication, rather than a formality unique to any one family or region.

    Regional Variation — Health vocabulary and access across contexts

    Vocabulary for clinics, laboratories, and preventive care, as well as the accessibility of formal health services, can differ between urban and rural areas and across regions and countries. Learners should treat Zainab's notice as one example of a health-communication register, not as a single fixed script for all health contexts, and should remember that access to clinics and laboratories varies considerably across Hausa-speaking communities.

    Common Mistakes

    Common Mistake — Confusing cuta with alama

    Error: Using alama (symptom) to refer to the illness itself, or using cuta (illness) when only describing a single sign.

    Correction: Use alama only for a sign or symptom, and cuta for the underlying illness, as modeled in Section 8.1 and 8.3.

    Common Mistake — Causing unnecessary alarm in a notice

    Error: Writing a public-health notice in an urgent or frightening tone that could cause panic rather than calm, clear action.

    Correction: Use calm, direct language, as the Reading Practice notice does, stating the risk clearly while focusing on practical, achievable prevention steps.

    Common Mistake — Omitting the recommended action

    Error: Describing a symptom or cause without stating what action a listener or reader should actually take.

    Correction: Always pair a symptom or cause description with a clear recommended action, as modeled in Sections 8.2 and 8.4.

    Answer Key / Amsoshi

    Tambayoyin fahimta na tattaunawa (Dialogue comprehension)

    1. Zainab's aunt has asked her to explain a public-health notice about seasonal illness prevention to their neighbors.
    2. Zainab mentions fever, headache, and unusual tiredness.
    3. The notice recommends going to the clinic early for a doctor to examine the person, and going to the laboratory if necessary.
    4. The illness becomes more common during the rainy season because mosquitoes increase, and mosquitoes are the main cause of the illness spreading.
    5. The term alama refers to a sign or symptom.

    Kula na sauraro (Listening responses)

    (1) Zainab is reporting the advice of the doctor at the clinic where her aunt works. (2) Many people are arriving with fever and headache. (3) The doctor says the main cause of the illness spreading is mosquitoes. (4) He recommends that people not wait, but come to the clinic early if they notice symptoms. (5) He mentions using a mosquito net at night and covering standing water near the home.

    Tambayoyin karatu (Reading comprehension)

    1. The notice's purpose is to inform the community about an illness that becomes more common during the rainy season.
    2. The notice lists fever, headache, and unusual tiredness as symptoms.
    3. The main cause stated is mosquitoes, which increase because of standing rainwater near homes.
    4. The notice recommends using a mosquito net at night, covering or removing standing water near the home, and getting the preventive vaccine if available.
    5. Sample summary: The clinic notice warns neighbors about a rainy-season illness spread mainly by mosquitoes, whose symptoms include fever, headache, and tiredness. It urges people to seek care early and to take preventive steps such as using mosquito nets and removing standing water.

    Sashi na B (Grammar and Style)

    1. Sample: Alamar da nake gani a jikin ɗana ita ce tari da ɗan zazzabi.
    2. Sample: Likita ya ce idan tarin bai tsaya ba cikin kwanaki uku, sai a kawo shi asibiti domin a duba shi sosai.
    3. Sample: Mutane suna kamuwa da mura a lokacin sanyi, domin sanyi yana raunana garkuwar jiki; saboda haka, sanya tufafi masu dumi yana da muhimmanci.
    4. Sample: Idan na taƙaita sanarwar, tana gaya wa jama’a cewa a lura da tari da zazzabi, a kawo yara asibiti da wuri idan an gan su, kuma a sanya tufafi masu dumi domin rigakafi.

    Sashi na C (Sample writing)

    Jumlar bayanin alama: Alamar da nake gani a jikina ita ce ciwon ciki da rashin ci abinci sosai.

    Shawarar likita: Likita ya ce in sha ruwa mai yawa kuma in ci abinci mai sauƙin narkewa har sai ciwon ya tafi.

    Dalili da rigakafi: Mutane da yawa suna kamuwa da ciwon ciki idan sun ci abinci da ba a wanke shi da kyau ba; saboda haka, wanke abinci da hannaye sosai kafin cin abinci yana da muhimmanci.

    Sanarwa: Idan na taƙaita sanarwar, tana gaya wa jama’a cewa a wanke hannaye da abinci sosai kafin cin abinci, domin rage yawan ciwon ciki a unguwarmu.
