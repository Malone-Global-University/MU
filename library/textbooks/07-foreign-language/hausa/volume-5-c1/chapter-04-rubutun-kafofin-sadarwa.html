Chapter Overview

Chapter 4 closes Unit I without introducing new grammar. Building on Chapter 3's work with proverbs and oral narrative, learners now turn to a different kind of public text: social media posts, short broadcast-style announcements, and audience-aware commentary. Salamatu has been asked to help manage a small community organization's social media presence, and she brings the task to Amina, Ibrahim, and Zainab for feedback. Together they examine how word choice, tone, and framing shift when a message is written for a broad, often unseen audience rather than for a single listener, using only vocabulary and grammar already consolidated through Volume IV and Chapters 1–3. This chapter directly prepares learners for Chapter 5's integrated Unit I assessment.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify the distinguishing features of a social media post, a broadcast-style announcement, and a short comment, using terms introduced in this chapter.
  2. Explain, in Hausa, how word choice and tone differ between a message written for one person and a message written for a broad audience.
  3. Draft a short, audience-appropriate social media post on a familiar topic.
  4. Compose a brief broadcast-style announcement using consolidated sentence patterns.
  5. Write and respond to a short public comment in a respectful, register-appropriate way.
  6. Compare the style choices in this chapter to the rhetorical, literary, and everyday registers consolidated in Chapters 1–3.

Communication Scenario

Salamatu has agreed to help a small community organization manage its social media page. She has drafted a short post announcing an upcoming event, but she is unsure whether the tone is right for a public audience she cannot see or hear responding in real time. Amina, Ibrahim, and Zainab meet with her to review the draft, compare it to a private message she might send a friend, and help her write a broadcast-style announcement and a short reply to an audience comment, all using vocabulary and grammar already within reach.

  1. Domain: reviewing and drafting short digital and broadcast-style public texts for a community organization.
  2. Target skill: recognizing and producing audience-aware digital and broadcast style using consolidated vocabulary and grammar.
  3. Grammar status: every system in this chapter remains at Consolidated status; no new grammar is introduced, and Unit I concludes at the end of this chapter.
  4. No unscheduled grammar beyond the Volume IV level, and no digital or media category beyond social media post, broadcast-style announcement, and public comment.

Pronunciation Focus

Sound Focus — Consolidated clarity for read-aloud broadcast style

No new pronunciation feature is introduced in this chapter. Learners instead apply consolidated tone, vowel-length, and phrase-rhythm control to the flatter, more even pacing typical of a read-aloud broadcast announcement, which contrasts with the slower, more deliberate delivery of a proverb from Chapter 3 and the more animated delivery of persuasive speech from Chapter 2. Reading the model dialogue and the sample announcement aloud supports this recognition.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces no new grammar. It consolidates identifying predicates, sequencing connectors, and reference tracking from Volume IV and Chapters 1–3, applying them to the task of drafting and evaluating short digital and broadcast-style texts.

4.1 Framing a social media post

A short post often opens by naming the occasion before giving the details, recycling the identifying-predicate pattern already consolidated in Chapter 2 and Chapter 3.

  • Wannan saƙo shi ne sanarwar taron al'umma na mako mai zuwa. This post is the announcement of next week's community gathering.

    • 4.2 Giving practical details in a broadcast-style announcement

    Announcing time, place, and purpose recycles the location and time expressions already consolidated in earlier chapters.

  • Za a gudanar da taron a ranar Asabar, da ƙarfe huɗu na yamma, a ɗakin taro na al'umma. The gathering will be held on Saturday, at four in the afternoon, in the community meeting hall.

    • 4.3 Inviting audience participation

    Inviting a broad, unseen audience to take part recycles the polite request patterns already consolidated in earlier volumes.

  • Muna gayyatar kowa da kowa ya zo tare da iyalinsa. We invite everyone to come together with their family.

    • 4.4 Responding to a public comment

    Replying briefly and respectfully to a comment recycles the thanking and clarifying patterns already consolidated in earlier chapters.

  • Na gode da sharhinku. Za mu sanar da ƙarin bayani nan ba da jimawa ba. Thank you for your comment. We will share more details soon.

    • 4.5 Distinguishing private and public tone

    Comparing a private message to a public post recycles the contrastive and comparative patterns already consolidated in earlier chapters.

  • Idan na rubuta wa aboki kai tsaye, zan iya amfani da kalmomi masu saukin kai; amma a shafin al'umma, sai in yi amfani da salo mafi tsari. If I write directly to a friend, I can use more casual words; but on the community page, I must use a more organized style.
    • Language Note — Audience awareness is not new grammar

    Writing for a broad digital or broadcast audience does not require any grammar form beyond what Volume IV already scheduled. What changes is the task applied to consolidated tools: identifying-predicate patterns for framing a post, location and time expressions for practical details, request patterns for invitations, and comparative patterns for contrasting private and public tone.

    Common learner error: Do not carry the same casual tone used with a close friend directly into a public post or announcement. Adjust word choice and structure for a broad, often unseen audience, as shown in Section 4.5.

    Model Dialogue

    Yanayin hali: Salamatu tana nuna wa Amina, Ibrahim, da Zainab wani daftarin saƙo da za a buga a shafin sada zumunta na wata ƙungiyar al'umma.

    (Setting: Salamatu is showing Amina, Ibrahim, and Zainab a draft post to publish on a community organization's social media page.)

    SALAMATU Na rubuta wani saƙo don sanar da taron al'umma, amma ban tabbata salon ya dace da masu karatu ba.I've written a post announcing the community gathering, but I'm not sure the style suits the readers.
    ZAINAB Ki karanta mana shi. Za mu iya taimaka miki kwatanta shi da yadda za ki rubuta wa aboki kai tsaye.Read it to us. We can help you compare it to how you'd write directly to a friend.
    SALAMATU Wannan saƙo shi ne sanarwar taron al'umma na mako mai zuwa. Za a gudanar da taron a ranar Asabar, da ƙarfe huɗu na yamma, a ɗakin taro na al'umma.This post is the announcement of next week's community gathering. It will be held on Saturday, at four in the afternoon, in the community meeting hall.
    IBRAHIM Wannan bayani ya yi kyau sosai domin sanarwa. Amma ki ƙara wata gayyata ga masu karatu, domin su ji ana maraba da su.That's good information for an announcement. But add an invitation for readers, so they feel welcomed.
    SALAMATU Muna gayyatar kowa da kowa ya zo tare da iyalinsa.We invite everyone to come together with their family.
    AMINA Ki tuna, idan na rubuta wa aboki kai tsaye, zan iya amfani da kalmomi masu saukin kai; amma a shafin al'umma, sai in yi amfani da salo mafi tsari, kamar yadda kika yi yanzu.Remember, if I write directly to a friend, I can use more casual words; but on the community page, I must use a more organized style, like you've just done.
    SALAMATU To, akwai wani sharhi da wani ya rubuta a ƙarƙashin sanarwar da ta gabata, yana tambaya ko za a sami abinci a taron.Well, there's a comment someone left under the previous announcement, asking if there will be food at the gathering.
    ZAINAB Ki mayar da martani kamar haka: "Na gode da sharhinku. Za mu sanar da ƙarin bayani nan ba da jimawa ba."Reply like this: "Thank you for your comment. We will share more details soon."
    IBRAHIM Wannan martani ya yi kyau domin ya nuna girmamawa ga mai sharhin, ba tare da yin alkawarin da ba a tabbata ba.That reply is good because it shows respect to the commenter, without making a promise that isn't confirmed.

    Tambayoyin fahimta — Comprehension questions:

    1. What is Salamatu unsure about regarding her draft post?
    2. What details does Salamatu's announcement include?
    3. What does Ibrahim suggest adding, and why?
    4. What contrast does Amina draw between writing to a friend and writing to the community page?
    5. What comment appears under the previous announcement, and how does the group suggest replying?

    Sauraro — Sanarwar Rediyo (Listening: A Radio-Style Announcement)

    "Sanarwa ga jama'a: Ƙungiyar al'ummar unguwar Kudu za ta gudanar da wani taro na tattaunawa a ranar Lahadi, da ƙarfe goma na safe, a makarantar firamare ta unguwar. Manufar taron ita ce, tattauna batun tsaftar muhalli da hanyoyin inganta rayuwar al'umma tare. Muna gayyatar kowa da kowa, babba da yaro, ya halarta.     Kula na sauraro — Questions: (1) Who is organizing the gathering, and what day and time will it take place? (2) Where will the gathering be held? (3) What is the stated purpose of the gathering? (4) Who is invited? (5) What should listeners do if they have a question?

    Reading Practice

    Read the short passage below. Identify the post's framing sentence, its practical details, its invitation, and its closing.

    Karatu — Saƙon Shafin Al’umma (Reading: A Community Page Post)

    Wannan saƙo shi ne gayyata zuwa bikin buɗe ɗakin karatu na unguwarmu. Za a gudanar da bikin a ranar Juma'a, da ƙarfe uku na rana, a sabon ɗakin karatu da ke kusa da makarantar firamare. Manufar bikin ita ce, tallafawa karatu da rubutu a tsakanin yara da manya. Muna gayyatar kowa da kowa ya zo, kuma za a sami littattafai da za a rarraba kyauta ga yara. Idan kuna da tambaya game da bikin, ku bar sharhinku a ƙasa, kuma za mu amsa muku da sauri. Muna jiran ganin ku a wurin!

    Tambayoyin fahimta — Comprehension questions:

    1. What is this post announcing?
    2. List the day, time, and location given in the post.
    3. What is the stated purpose of the event?
    4. What will be given away for free, and to whom?
    5. Write a two-sentence summary of the post in your own words.

    Writing Workshop

    1. Karanta misalai da hankali. Reread Sections 4.1–4.5, the Model Dialogue, and the Reading Practice passage.
    2. Zaɓi wani taron ko labari. Choose a real or imagined community event to announce.
    3. Rubuta jimlar tsari. Write a framing sentence naming the post, following Section 4.1.
    4. Ƙara bayanan aiki. Add the day, time, location, and purpose, following Section 4.2.
    5. Ƙara gayyata da rufewa. Add an invitation and a brief closing line.
    6. Rubuta amsa ga sharhi. Write a short, respectful reply to an imagined audience comment, following Section 4.4.
    7. Duba jerin bincike. Check your text against the checklist below.
    8. Rubuta siffa ta ƙarshe. Produce a clean final version, then prepare to present it aloud, following the Speaking Practice instructions.

    Jerin binciken gyara — Editing checklist:

    • The post opens with a clear framing sentence naming what it announces.
    • Day, time, location, and purpose are stated clearly and in a logical order.
    • An invitation is included and is welcoming without being overly casual.
    • The reply to the audience comment is respectful, brief, and avoids unconfirmed promises.
    • Feedback language, if used, is respectful and constructive, following the Chapter 1 mentoring register.
    • Special characters (ɓ, ɗ, ƙ, ƴ) and apostrophe/glottal conventions are used accurately.
    • No unscheduled grammar beyond the Volume IV level is required to understand the text.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Community organizations and digital presence

    Community organizations, religious groups, schools, and local associations across Hausa-speaking communities increasingly maintain social media pages alongside more traditional radio, mosque or church announcements, and word-of-mouth notices, often using several channels together for the same event. Public posts and announcements typically favor a more organized, complete-sentence style than a quick private message, even when the underlying vocabulary and grammar remain the same.

    Regional Variation — Digital and broadcast style across communities

    The specific platforms used, the balance between digital and radio-based announcements, and the degree of formality expected in public posts can vary between communities, generations, and regions across the Hausa-speaking world. Learners should treat the examples in this chapter as common, widely usable patterns, remaining open to variation encountered in other communities, as discussed further in Chapter 11's treatment of regional and generational variation.

    Common Mistakes

    Common Mistake — Using a private, casual tone in a public post

    Error: Writing a community announcement with the same casual wording used in a private message to a close friend, which can seem unclear or overly informal to a broad audience.

    Correction: Use a more organized style with complete practical details, as modeled in Sections 4.1 and 4.2.

    Common Mistake — Omitting practical details

    Error: Announcing an event without stating the day, time, location, or purpose clearly, leaving the audience unable to act on the information.

    Correction: Include all four practical details in a logical order, as modeled in Section 4.2.

    Common Mistake — Making unconfirmed promises in a comment reply

    Error: Responding to a public question with a specific promise, such as guaranteeing food or a particular outcome, before it has been confirmed.

    Correction: Thank the commenter and state that more details will follow, as modeled in Section 4.4.

    Answer Key / Amsoshi

    Tambayoyin fahimta na tattaunawa (Dialogue comprehension)

    1. Salamatu is unsure whether the style of her draft post suits the public readers of the community page.
    2. Her announcement includes the day (Saturday), time (four in the afternoon), and location (the community meeting hall).
    3. Ibrahim suggests adding an invitation so readers feel welcomed.
    4. Amina contrasts casual wording used with a friend against the more organized style needed for the community page.
    5. A comment asks whether there will be food at the gathering; the group suggests thanking the commenter and saying more details will follow soon.

    Kula na sauraro (Listening responses)

    (1) The neighborhood community association is organizing the gathering, on Sunday at ten in the morning. (2) It will be held at the neighborhood primary school. (3) The purpose is to discuss environmental cleanliness and ways to improve community life together. (4) Everyone, young and old, is invited. (5) Listeners with a question should leave a comment below the announcement, and it will be answered promptly.

    Tambayoyin karatu (Reading comprehension)

    1. The post announces the opening celebration of the neighborhood library.
    2. Friday, at three in the afternoon, at the new library near the primary school.
    3. The purpose is to support reading and writing among children and adults.
    4. Free books will be given away to children.
    5. Sample summary: A neighborhood is celebrating the opening of a new library with an event for the whole community. Free books will be given to children, and questions can be left as comments.
