Takardu da Saƙonnin Hukuma

B2–C1 Development · Unit I, Chapter 2 of 5 · Speak and Write Hausa: Advanced Functional Hausa

Chapter Overview

Chapter 2 continues Unit I by moving from spoken workplace exchanges into written institutional communication. Building directly on the professional greetings, identity clauses, and update patterns consolidated in Chapter 1, this chapter extends learners' reading and writing into the genres of formal notes, notices, forms, and institutional correspondence. No new grammar paradigm is introduced. Instead, the consolidated pronoun-reference, predicate, and TAM systems from Volumes I-III are applied to the more formal register and layout conventions of institutional Boko text, preparing learners for the presentation and report genres of Chapters 3-4 and for the Unit I integrated assessment in Chapter 5.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Identify the standard parts of a short institutional notice or memo in Hausa.
  2. Read a simple official form and correctly enter requested personal information.
  3. Interpret a short institutional message and identify its purpose, deadline, and required action.
  4. Write a brief formal note requesting institutional information or documentation.
  5. Use appropriate opening and closing formulas for institutional correspondence.
  6. Distinguish the register of institutional writing from the register of a casual workplace message.
  7. Ask for clarification about an institutional instruction, orally or in writing.

Communication Scenario

A few weeks into her new position, Amina receives a printed notice from the human resources office about updating personal records, along with a short form to complete. She also needs to write a brief note to the office asking about a document she has not yet received. She must read the notice and form carefully, complete the form accurately, and compose a polite, clearly organized written request.

  1. Domain: official documents, institutional messages, notices, and forms.
  2. Developed skill: reading and producing short formal Boko texts while recycling greetings, identity clauses, and pronoun-reference patterns established in Volume III and reviewed in Chapter 1.
  3. Recycled grammar in context: core predicate types, independent pronouns, and completive/future TAM forms, all Consolidated by the end of Volume III.
  4. No unscheduled TAM paradigm, dialect mixture, or productive Ajami writing is introduced in this chapter.

Pronunciation Focus

Sound Focus — Reading formal text aloud clearly

When institutional notices are read aloud, speakers typically slow down at key information such as dates, deadlines, and required documents, giving these words extra clarity of vowel length and tone. Practice reading takarda, sanarwa, and ranar ƙarshe with clear, deliberate pacing, and keep the special consonant in bukata's k distinct from the hooked ƙ in ranar ƙarshe.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces no new grammar system. It reviews and extends the core predicate types, independent pronouns, and completive/future TAM forms consolidated across Volumes I-III, applying them to the layout and phrasing conventions of institutional Boko text, consistent with the Volume IV Assessment Matrix's Chapter 2 status of Consolidated (C) for these systems.

2.1 Review: stating purpose and requirement in formal notices

Institutional notices typically open by stating who the notice is for, then state the required action, recycling basic nominal and verbal predicates already consolidated.

  • Sanarwa ga dukan ma'aikata. Notice to all employees.
  • Ana bukatar kowa ya cika fom ɗin nan kafin ranar ƙarshe. Everyone is required to complete this form before the deadline.
  • Don Allah a mika takardar zuwa ofishin albashi. Please submit the document to the payroll office.

    • 2.2 Review: opening and closing a formal written request

    A formal written request states a polite opening, the specific request, and a polite closing, recycling completive and future TAM forms already consolidated.

    Language Note — Structuring a formal request

    A useful pattern for a formal written request is: greet briefly, state the specific document or information needed, and close politely. For example: Ina so in tabbatar da ko an aiko takardar tabbatar da aiki na. ("I would like to confirm whether my work-confirmation document has been sent.") This recycles familiar predicate and TAM patterns rather than introducing any new structure.

    2.3 Review: asking for clarification about an instruction

    When an institutional instruction is unclear, learners should be able to ask for clarification in writing as well as in speech. Recall formulas such as Ban tabbata ba game da wannan bukata, don Allah a bayyana. ("I am not sure about this requirement, please clarify.")

    These review points recycle familiar structures from Volumes I-III and Chapter 1; no new sentence pattern is introduced in this chapter.

    Model Dialogue

    Yanayin hali: Amina ta karɓi sanarwa daga ofishin ma'aikata kuma tana tattaunawa da abokiyar aikinta, Hadiza, game da fom ɗin da za a cika.

    (Setting: Amina has received a notice from the human resources office and discusses the form to be completed with her colleague, Hadiza.)

    AMINA Hadiza, kin ga sanarwar da ofishin ma'aikata ya turo?Hadiza, have you seen the notice the HR office sent?
    HADIZA I, na gani. Ana bukatar kowa ya cika fom na sabunta bayanan sirri kafin ranar ƙarshe.Yes, I've seen it. Everyone is required to complete the personal-records update form before the deadline.
    AMINA Ban tabbata ba game da wannan bukata, don Allah a bayyana. Wace rana ce ranar ƙarshe?I am not sure about this requirement, please clarify. Which day is the deadline?
    HADIZA Ranar Jumma'a ce. Ka cika fom ɗin, ka sa hannu, sannan ka mika shi ga ofishin ma'aikata.It's Friday. Fill in the form, sign it, and then submit it to the HR office.
    AMINA Na gode. Har ila yau, ina so in tabbatar da ko an aiko takardar tabbatar da aiki na, domin ban gani ba tukuna.Thank you. Also, I would like to confirm whether my work-confirmation document has been sent, since I have not seen it yet.
    HADIZA Zan iya taimaka maka. Za mu rubuta ɗan gajeren saƙo zuwa ofishin ma'aikata tare.I can help you. We will write a short message to the HR office together.

    Tambayoyin fahimta — Comprehension questions:

    1. What must every employee complete before the deadline?
    2. When is the deadline?
    3. What document is Amina still waiting to receive?

    Writing Workshop

    1. Karanta misalai da hankali. Reread Sections 2.1-2.3 and the Model Dialogue.
    2. Tabbatar da manufa. Decide whether your message will request a missing document or confirm that you have completed a form.
    3. Tsara saƙonka. Plan a short message with a polite opening, the specific request, and a polite closing.
    4. Rubuta. Write 60-90 words in Hausa, in Boko, as a short formal message to an institutional office requesting a document or confirming a submitted form, using at least one recycled formal opening and one recycled TAM form.
    5. Duba jerin bincike. Check your draft against the checklist below.
    6. Rubuta siffa ta ƙarshe. Produce a clean final version suitable for submitting to an office.

    Jerin binciken gyara — Editing checklist:

    • The message opens with an appropriate formal, institutional register.
    • The main point (request or confirmation) is clearly and specifically stated.
    • At least one recycled TAM form is used correctly.
    • Register is more formal than the workplace message pattern from Chapter 1.
    • Special characters (ɓ, ɗ, ƙ, ƴ) and apostrophe/glottal conventions are used accurately in the Boko text.
    • The message closes politely and includes any necessary identifying information.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Formality in institutional writing

    Institutional written Hausa is generally more formal and more carefully structured than spoken workplace exchanges, often opening with a clear statement of audience and purpose, as in Sanarwa ga dukan ma'aikata. The degree of formality expected can vary somewhat by institution, sector, and generation, but a respectful, direct opening is broadly appropriate across institutional contexts.

    Regional Variation — Institutional form design

    The layout, headings, and required fields on institutional forms vary across offices, regions, and countries, and some institutions provide bilingual or multilingual forms alongside Hausa. The single-column form shown in this chapter is a widely usable model for this course, not the only acceptable format.

    Common Mistakes

    Common Mistake — Using casual workplace register in a formal message

    Error: Writing an institutional request using the same brief, casual phrasing appropriate to a quick colleague-to-colleague message.

    Correction: Use a more complete, formally structured opening and closing, as modeled in Sections 2.1-2.2, when writing to an institutional office.

    Common Mistake — Leaving out the specific requirement or deadline

    Error: Summarizing a notice without stating exactly what is required or by when.

    Correction: State the specific action and deadline clearly, as modeled in the listening passage and the Model Dialogue.

    Common Mistake — Guessing at unclear form fields

    Error: Filling in a form field without being sure what information it requests.

    Correction: Use a clarification formula such as Ban tabbata ba game da wannan bukata, don Allah a bayyana before completing an unclear field.

    Answer Key / Amsoshi

    Tambayoyin fahimta na tattaunawa (Dialogue comprehension)

    1. Every employee must complete the personal-records update form before the deadline.
    2. The deadline is Friday.
    3. Amina is still waiting for her work-confirmation document.

    Kula na sauraro (Listening responses)

    (1) Every employee must complete the personal-records update form, sign it, and submit it to the HR office. (2) The deadline is Friday. (3) An employee who is unsure about the requirement should contact the HR office directly.

    Tambayoyin karatu (Reading comprehension)

    1. The form requests the employee's name, department, address, and phone number.
    2. The HR office (ofishin ma'aikata) issued the form.
    3. A signature line and a date line appear at the bottom of the form.

    Sashi na B (Chapter Review Notices and Requests)

    1. Sample: Sanarwa ga dukan ma'aikata. Ana bukatar kowa ya mika takardar shaidar lafiya kafin ranar Alhamis.
    2. Sample: Ban tabbata ba game da wannan bukata, don Allah a bayyana. ("I am not sure about this requirement, please clarify.")
    3. Sample: A formal written request typically opens politely, states the specific document or information needed, and closes with a polite expression of thanks or respect.

    Sashi na C (Sample writing)

    Sannu, ofishin ma'aikata. Ina so in tabbatar da ko an aiko takardar tabbatar da aiki na, domin ban gani ba tukuna, kuma ina bukatarta don wata takardar banki. Na riga na cika fom na sabunta bayanan sirri kuma na mika shi ranar Talata. Don Allah a sanar da ni idan akwai wani abu da nake bukatar yi. Na gode sosai da hakuri.

    Other accurate, well-organized messages meeting the stated requirements are equally acceptable.
