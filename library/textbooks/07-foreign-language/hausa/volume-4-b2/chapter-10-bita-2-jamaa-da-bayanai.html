Unit II Integrated Assessment — Chapters 6–9

Chapter Overview

Chapter 10 is the Unit II assessment. It does not introduce new grammar or new vocabulary domains. Instead, it integrates the media, civic, economic, and science/health/public-information tasks built across Chapters 6–9, and it formally consolidates and assesses the imperfective/progressive/habitual TAM frame, the negative TAM pattern, and noun gender/plural tracking for the domain vocabulary opened in that unit. Learners will move across four connected scenarios — a media report, a civic-participation conversation, a business update, and a clinic/public-health exchange — using recycled vocabulary and consolidated grammar rather than newly taught material.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe an ongoing situation across media, civic, economic, and health/public-information contexts using the imperfective TAM frame with consolidated accuracy.
  2. Describe a recurring pattern — a broadcast schedule, a civic responsibility, a seasonal business cycle, or a seasonal health pattern — using the habitual TAM frame with consolidated accuracy.
  3. State clearly what a report, an official, a business plan, or a test result has not yet confirmed, using the negative TAM pattern with consolidated accuracy.
  4. Record and apply gender and plural information for nouns from the media, civic, economic, and health/science domains introduced in Chapters 6–9.
  5. Request clarification or repetition appropriately across these four domains, recycling repair formulas built since earlier volumes.
  6. Integrate listening, speaking, reading, and writing to complete a connected Unit II assessment task.

Communication Scenario

A community radio station in Amina and Sani’s neighborhood is preparing a special program that ties together the past month’s stories: the news report Sani discussed in Chapter 6, a civic-participation segment on local services, a small-business update from a market trader, and the vaccination-campaign update from Malama Hauwa’s clinic. Amina has been asked to help prepare interview questions and a short written summary for the program, drawing on everything the unit has covered.

  1. Domain: integrating media, civic, economic, and health/public-information communication into one connected task.
  2. Assessed skill: describing ongoing situations, recurring patterns, and unconfirmed details across four domains, and requesting clarification appropriately in each.
  3. Grammar consolidation: the imperfective/progressive/habitual TAM frame and the negative TAM pattern (Introduced in Chapter 6, Developed through Chapters 7–9, Consolidated and Assessed in this chapter), and gender/plural tracking for Chapters 6–9 vocabulary (Consolidated and Assessed).
  4. No unscheduled focus construction, no new preposition/relational-noun system (scheduled for Chapter 11), no dialect mixture, and no productive Ajami writing is introduced in this chapter.

Pronunciation Focus

Sound Focus — Consolidation across four registers

This chapter introduces no new pronunciation target. Instead, it asks learners to maintain tone, vowel length, and phrase-final rhythm consistently as they move between four different registers within a single task: the more clipped, headline-like rhythm of media language (Chapter 6), the measured rhythm of civic and institutional language (Chapter 7), the numerically dense rhythm of business language (Chapter 8), and the careful, explanatory rhythm of health and science language (Chapter 9). Listen for how tone and phrase rhythm remain stable even as vocabulary and sentence purpose shift.

Grammar Consolidation

This chapter formally consolidates and assesses the imperfective/progressive/habitual TAM frame and the negative TAM pattern, both Introduced in Chapter 6 and Developed through Chapters 7–9. No new grammar form is introduced. The review below restates the three meanings learners must now control productively and reliably across any of the four Unit II domains.

10.1 Imperfective/progressive meaning, consolidated

Use the imperfective TAM frame for an action or process currently underway, in any domain.

  • Gidan rediyon mu na shirya wani shiri na musamman a yau. Our radio station is preparing a special program today.
  • Ma’aikatan lafiya na yi wa yara alurar riga-kafi a asibiti. Health workers are vaccinating children at the clinic.

    • 10.2 Habitual meaning, consolidated

    Use the habitual TAM frame for a pattern that recurs regularly, whether a broadcast schedule, a civic duty, a seasonal business cycle, or a seasonal health pattern.

  • Gidan rediyon na watsa labarai kowace rana da safe. The radio station broadcasts news every morning.
  • ’Yan kasuwa na ƙara farashin kaya lokacin damina, saboda ƙarancin kayayyaki. Traders raise the price of goods during the rainy season, because of the scarcity of supplies.

    • 10.3 Negative TAM pattern, consolidated

    Use the negative TAM pattern to state clearly what a report, official, business plan, or health result has not yet confirmed.

  • Gwamnati ba ta tabbatar da sabon shirin ba tukuna. The government has not yet confirmed the new plan.
  • ’Yan kasuwa ba su san ko za su sami riba a wannan shekara ba, saboda yanayin kasuwa ba shi da tabbas. The traders do not know whether they will make a profit this year, because the market situation is uncertain.
    • Language Note — One system, four domains

    Chapters 6–9 taught the same three TAM meanings — ongoing, recurring, and not-yet-confirmed — inside four different subject-matter domains. Consolidation means recognizing that the grammar does not change from domain to domain: only the vocabulary changes. A learner who can distinguish these three meanings in a media report should be able to make the same distinctions in a civic, economic, or health conversation without relearning the grammar.

    Common learner error: Do not switch back to the completive/perfective TAM (consolidated since Volume III) when the intended meaning is still ongoing, recurring, or unconfirmed. Gwamnati ta tabbatar da shirin ("The government confirmed the plan," completed) is a different claim from Gwamnati ba ta tabbatar da shirin ba tukuna ("The government has not yet confirmed the plan").

    Model Dialogue

    Yanayin hali: Amina na taimaka wa Sani da wasu ma’aikatan gidan rediyo domin shirya tambayoyin hira don shirin musamman.

    (Setting: Amina is helping Sani and other radio station staff prepare interview questions for the special program.)

    SANI Amina, mece ce jigon shirin da za mu watsa yau?Amina, what is the theme of the program we will broadcast today?
    AMINA Muna haɗa labarai huɗu: rahoton gwamnati kan ayyukan al’umma, sabon shirin ’yan kasuwa, da kuma alurar riga-kafi da ake yi a asibitin unguwarmu.We are combining four stories: the government report on community projects, the traders' new plan, and the vaccination campaign happening at our neighborhood clinic.
    SANI Ban ji sarai ba. Za ka iya maimaita sunayen labaran hudu?I didn't hear clearly. Can you repeat the names of the four stories?
    AMINA Tabbas. Labari na farko game da rahoton gwamnati; na biyu game da haƙƙin jama’a wajen ayyukan gwamnati; na uku game da shirin ’yan kasuwa; na huɗu game da alurar riga-kafi.Of course. The first story is about the government report; the second is about the public's rights regarding government projects; the third is about the traders' plan; the fourth is about the vaccination campaign.
    SANI To, shin gwamnati ta tabbatar da wannan sabon shirin ayyukan al’umma?Well, has the government confirmed this new community-projects plan?
    AMINA A’a, gwamnati ba ta tabbatar da shirin ba tukuna, amma tana nazarinsa yanzu.No, the government has not yet confirmed the plan, but it is currently reviewing it.
    SANI Kai, ban gane ba. Za ki iya sake bayyana yadda ’yan kasuwa suke ganin wannan lamari?Oh, I don't understand. Can you explain again how the traders view this matter?
    AMINA Ina nufin cewa, ’yan kasuwa na ƙara farashin kaya kowace shekara lokacin damina, kamar yadda suke yi kowace shekara, kuma ba su san ko za su sami riba a wannan shekara ba saboda yanayin kasuwa ba shi da tabbas.I mean that traders raise the price of goods every year during the rainy season, as they do every year, and they don't know whether they'll make a profit this year because the market situation is uncertain.
    SANI To, sannan alurar riga-kafi fa? Me ake yi a asibiti a yanzu?Well, and what about the vaccination campaign? What is being done at the clinic right now?
    AMINA Ma’aikatan lafiya na yi wa yara alurar riga-kafi yanzu, kamar yadda suke yi kowace shekara a lokacin damina, kuma har yanzu ba a tabbatar ba ko wasu marasa lafiya suna da alaƙa da annobar da ake magana a kai.Health workers are vaccinating children right now, as they do every year during the rainy season, and it still hasn't been confirmed whether some of the sick patients are related to the outbreak being discussed.

    Tambayoyin fahimta — Comprehension questions:

    1. How many stories are being combined in the special radio program, and what are they?
    2. What has the government not yet confirmed?
    3. What recurring pattern do the traders follow every year, and why are they uncertain about profit this year?
    4. What is currently happening at the clinic, and what remains unconfirmed?

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Community radio as a connective institution

    Community radio programs of the kind described in this chapter often draw together news, civic information, market conditions, and public-health updates for a single local audience, reflecting how public information circulates through overlapping institutions rather than through a single specialized channel. Learners should not assume that every Hausa-speaking community relies on the same mix of media, civic, market, and health information sources, since local infrastructure and institutions vary considerably.

    Regional Variation — Public-information registers across domains

    Vocabulary and formality for media, civic, economic, and health/public-information language can vary between Nigeria, Niger, and other Hausa-speaking regions, and between urban and rural contexts. The Unit II chapters have presented a common, broadly understandable instructional standard across all four domains; learners should expect some local variation when engaging with real institutions in any of these fields.

    Common Mistakes

    Common Mistake — Treating an unconfirmed detail as confirmed across any domain

    Error: Stating that a government plan, a business outcome, or a health result is already settled when the source material only describes it as under review or not yet confirmed.

    Correction: Use the negative TAM pattern (Section 10.3) consistently across all four domains to state precisely what has not yet been confirmed, as modeled throughout Chapters 6–9 and this chapter.

    Common Mistake — Confusing a single occurrence with a recurring pattern

    Error: Using the imperfective TAM frame alone to describe a pattern that recurs on a regular schedule, such as a yearly price increase or a yearly vaccination campaign.

    Correction: Use the habitual TAM frame (Section 10.2) for patterns that recur regularly, reserving the imperfective for a single process currently in progress, as consolidated across Chapters 6–9.

    Common Mistake — Mixing domain vocabulary without tracking gender and plural

    Error: Using a media, civic, economic, or health noun in a sentence requiring agreement or plural reference without recalling its recorded gender or plural form.

    Correction: Consult the cumulative vocabulary ledger for each domain, as reviewed in the Core Vocabulary Review table, before producing sentences that require gender or plural agreement.

    Answer Key / Amsoshi

    Tambayoyin fahimta na tattaunawa (Dialogue comprehension)

    1. Four stories: the government report on community projects, public rights regarding government projects, the traders' new plan, and the vaccination campaign.
    2. The government has not yet confirmed the new community-projects plan.
    3. Traders raise prices every year during the rainy season; they are uncertain about profit this year because the market situation is uncertain.
    4. Health workers are currently vaccinating children, as they do every year during the rainy season; it has not yet been confirmed whether some sick patients are related to the outbreak being discussed.

    Kula na sauraro (Listening responses)

    (1) The special program combines four stories. (2) The government has not yet confirmed the new community-projects plan, though it is currently reviewing it. (3) Traders raise prices every year during the rainy season, as they always do, but are uncertain about profit this year. (4) Health workers are currently vaccinating children, as they do every year, and it has not yet been confirmed whether fever cases are related to the outbreak.

    Tambayoyin karatu (Reading comprehension)

    1. The government is currently reviewing the new community-projects plan, and it has not yet confirmed the plan.
    2. Traders raise prices every year during the rainy season; they remain uncertain whether they will make a profit this year.
    3. Health workers are continuing to vaccinate children at the clinic, as they do every year; it has not yet been confirmed whether the fever cases are related to the outbreak.

    Sashi na B (Grammar)

    1. Sample: Gidan rediyon na watsa labarai kowace safe. ’Yan kasuwa na ƙara farashin kaya kowace shekara lokacin damina. Ma’aikatan lafiya na yi wa yara alurar riga-kafi kowace shekara. Gwamnati na duba buƙatun jama’a kowace shekara.
    2. Sample: Gwamnati ba ta tabbatar da shirin ba tukuna. ’Yan kasuwa ba su san ko za su sami riba ba. Likita bai tabbatar da sakamakon gwajin ba. Gidan rediyo bai tabbatar da lokacin watsa shirin ba tukuna.
    3. Sample: The imperfective, habitual, and negative TAM frames express the same relationships between speech time and action — ongoing, recurring, or unconfirmed — regardless of subject matter. Media, civic, economic, and health language simply supply different vocabulary within the same grammatical frames, which is why the system transfers across domains without changes to its form.

    Sashi na C (Sample writing)

    A wannan yammacin, gidan rediyon unguwarmu zai watsa shirin musamman da ke tattare da labarai daga fannoni hudu. Gwamnati na nazarin sabon shirin ayyukan al’umma, amma har yanzu ba a tabbatar da shi ba. ’Yan kasuwa na ƙara farashin kaya kamar yadda suke yi kowace shekara lokacin damina, kuma ba su da tabbacin samun riba a wannan shekara. Ma’aikatan lafiya na ci gaba da yi wa yara alurar riga-kafi a asibitin unguwarmu, kamar yadda ake yi kowace shekara, kuma har yanzu ba a tabbatar da alaƙar zazzaɓi da annoba ba. Za a sanar da jama’a idan an sami sabon tabbaci.

    Other accurate, well-organized summaries that correctly distinguish ongoing, recurring, and unconfirmed details across at least two domains are equally acceptable.
