Sashe na 2 — Unit II: Jama’a da Bayanai (Public Life and Information)
9

Kimiyya, Lafiya, da Bayanai

B2–C1 Development · Chapter 9 of 20, Unit II Chapter 4 of 5 · Speak and Write Hausa: Advanced Functional Hausa

Chapter Overview

Chapter 9 continues Unit II by moving from economics and business communication toward science, health, and public information. Learners will interpret public-health notices, environmental patterns, and data-based information, while carrying the imperfective/progressive/habitual TAM frame and the negative TAM pattern into their final Introduced/Developed chapter before full consolidation at Chapter 10, the Unit II assessment. Noun gender and plural tracking continues for new health and science vocabulary, and market/service repair formulas recur here in the context of a clinic visit and a public-health announcement.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe an ongoing health situation or a science/environmental process using the imperfective TAM frame.
  2. Describe recurring health, seasonal, or environmental patterns, such as a yearly vaccination campaign or a seasonal illness, using the habitual TAM frame.
  3. State what test results, a diagnosis, or a public-health measure has not yet confirmed, using the negative TAM pattern.
  4. Record the gender and plural form of new health and science-related nouns as part of active vocabulary learning.
  5. Ask a health worker, official, or presenter for clarification or repetition, recycling market/service repair formulas in a health/public-information context.
  6. Discuss, in simple terms, a health situation or a piece of public-health or environmental information, in speech and in writing.

Communication Scenario

Amina, who discussed the radio report with Sani in Chapter 6, visits a community health clinic with her neighbor, Maryam, to hear about the yearly rainy-season vaccination campaign. The nurse, Malama Hauwa, explains what the clinic is currently doing, what happens every year at this time, and what the test results do not yet confirm, such as whether a mild fever is related to the recent outbreak. Maryam asks for clarification twice, using the same repair formulas from earlier chapters.

  1. Domain: interpreting or discussing public-health information, a clinic visit, or an environmental/seasonal health pattern with a health worker, family member, or neighbor.
  2. Developed skill: describing an ongoing health procedure and a recurring seasonal health pattern, qualifying what test results do not yet confirm, and requesting repetition from a health worker or presenter.
  3. Continued grammar development: imperfective/progressive/habitual TAM frames and negative TAM patterns (Introduced/Developed, continuing from Chapters 6–8, reaching full consolidation at Chapter 10), and gender/plural tracking for new nouns (Introduced/Developed).
  4. No unscheduled focus construction, prepositional system, dialect mixture, or productive Ajami writing is introduced in this chapter.

Pronunciation Focus

Sound Focus — Careful articulation in health and science explanations

Health and science explanations often include longer noun phrases and technical-sounding compounds, such as alurar riga-kafi and ma’aikacin lafiya. Pay attention to tone and vowel length across these longer compounds so that meaning stays clear even when several words are strung together, and to phrase-final tone patterns that mark the end of a stated result or instruction. No new consonant or vowel-length contrast is introduced; this chapter continues to apply tone and rhythm control already built across Chapters 1–8 to a health/science register that includes longer compound nouns.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter continues developing the imperfective/progressive/habitual TAM frame and the negative TAM pattern introduced in Chapter 6 and continued through Chapters 7–8, still at the Introduced/Developed level, consistent with the Volume IV Verb/TAM/Aspect Matrix. This chapter applies the habitual meaning to recurring seasonal health patterns, such as a yearly vaccination campaign or a seasonal illness. Both systems are formally assessed in Chapter 10.

9.1 Imperfective and habitual meaning in health and science contexts

Use the imperfective TAM frame to describe a health procedure or scientific process currently underway, and the habitual frame to describe something that recurs regularly, such as a yearly campaign or a seasonal illness.

  • Malama Hauwa na yi wa yara alurar riga-kafi a asibiti. Malama Hauwa is vaccinating children at the clinic.
  • Zazzaɓi na yawaita a wannan lokaci kowace shekara, lokacin damina. Fever cases increase at this time every year, during the rainy season.

    • 9.2 Negative TAM patterns with health and data claims

    Use the negative TAM pattern to state that test results or a diagnosis have not yet confirmed something.

  • Likita bai tabbatar da sakamakon gwajin ba tukuna. The doctor has not yet confirmed the test results.
  • Ma’aikatan lafiya ba su san ko zazzaɓin na da alaƙa da annobar ba, saboda har yanzu suna nazarin bayanan.The health workers do not know whether the fever is related to the outbreak, because they are still analyzing the data.
    • Language Note — A suspected cause versus a confirmed cause

    Just as Chapter 8 distinguished a planned profit from a confirmed one, health and science language distinguishes a suspected cause or preliminary observation from a cause that has actually been confirmed by data or testing. Use the imperfective TAM frame to describe what health workers or researchers are currently doing to investigate a situation, and the negative TAM pattern to state clearly that a conclusion has not yet been reached. Do not present a suspected cause as if it were already confirmed.

    Common learner error: Do not use the completive/perfective TAM (introduced in Volume III and consolidated through Chapter 5) to describe a health investigation or a scientific process that is still ongoing. Likita ya tabbatar da sakamakon ("The doctor confirmed the results," completed) is not the same claim as Likita na nazarin sakamakon ("The doctor is analyzing the results," ongoing).

    Model Dialogue

    Yanayin hali: Amina da Maryam sun je asibitin unguwarsu domin jin bayani game da alurar riga-kafi ta wannan shekara.

    (Setting: Amina and Maryam visit their neighborhood clinic to hear about this year's vaccination campaign.)

    MARYAM Sannu, Malama Hauwa. Me kuke yi a yau a asibiti?Hello, Malama Hauwa. What are you doing today at the clinic?
    MALAMA HAUWA Muna yi wa yara alurar riga-kafi, kamar yadda muke yi kowace shekara a wannan lokaci na damina.We are vaccinating children, as we do every year at this time during the rainy season.
    AMINA Ban ji sarai ba. Za ki iya maimaita wancan sashe?I didn't hear clearly. Can you repeat that part?
    MALAMA HAUWA Tabbas. Ina cewa, muna yi wa yara alurar riga-kafi domin gudun cutar da take yawaita kowace shekara a lokacin damina.Of course. I said, we are vaccinating children to prevent the illness that increases every year during the rainy season.
    MARYAM To, ɗana yana da ɗan zazzaɓi jiya. Shin likita ya tabbatar ko yana da alaƙa da annobar?Well, my son had a slight fever yesterday. Has the doctor confirmed whether it's related to the outbreak?
    MALAMA HAUWA Likita bai tabbatar da hakan ba tukuna, amma yana nazarin sakamakon gwajin yanzu.The doctor has not yet confirmed that, but he is analyzing the test results now.
    MARYAM Kai, ban gane ba. Za ki iya sake bayyana wannan?Oh, I don't understand. Can you explain that again?
    MALAMA HAUWA Ina nufin cewa, har yanzu ba mu san ainihin dalilin zazzaɓin ba, amma yakamata mu san sakamakon gwajin nan da kwana biyu. Idan an tabbatar ba shi da alaƙa da annobar, kada ku damu sosai.I mean that we still don't know the exact cause of the fever, but we should know the test results within two days. If it's confirmed not to be related to the outbreak, don't worry too much.

    Tambayoyin fahimta — Comprehension questions:

    1. What is Malama Hauwa currently doing at the clinic?
    2. What has the doctor not yet confirmed, according to Malama Hauwa?
    3. What is the doctor currently doing with the test results?
    4. What will happen within two days, according to Malama Hauwa?

    Writing Workshop

    1. Karanta misalai da hankali. Reread Sections 9.1–9.2 and the Model Dialogue.
    2. Zaɓi batu. Choose a real or invented health, science, or environmental situation (a clinic visit, a seasonal illness, or a public-health notice).
    3. Tsara sashi uku. Plan three short parts: what is currently underway, a recurring seasonal or health pattern relevant to the situation, and one detail that has not yet been confirmed.
    4. Rubuta taƙaitaccen bayani. Draft an 80–120 word explanation in Boko, using imperfective, habitual, and negative TAM frames accurately.
    5. Duba jerin bincike. Check your draft against the checklist below.
    6. Rubuta siffa ta ƙarshe. Produce a clean final version suitable for submission.

    Jerin binciken gyara — Editing checklist:

    • The explanation clearly states what is currently underway in the health or science situation.
    • At least one recurring or habitual detail uses the habitual TAM frame correctly.
    • At least one unconfirmed detail uses the negative TAM pattern correctly.
    • New nouns are recorded with correct gender and plural where relevant.
    • Special characters (ɓ, ɗ, ƙ, ƴ) and apostrophe/glottal conventions are used accurately.
    • The explanation does not present a suspected cause or preliminary observation as an already-confirmed result.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Community health information and trust

    Public-health information across Hausa-speaking communities is shared through varied channels, including clinics, radio announcements, community leaders, religious institutions, and family networks, depending on the country, region, and local infrastructure. Learners should not assume that one source, such as a single clinic or broadcaster, is the only or most trusted source of health information everywhere Hausa is spoken.

    Regional Variation — Health and public-information terms

    Terms for clinics, vaccination programs, and public-health measures can vary between Nigeria, Niger, and other Hausa-speaking regions, as well as between urban and rural healthcare contexts. The terms used in this chapter represent a common, broadly understandable instructional standard; learners should expect some local variation in health and public-information terminology when working with real institutions.

    Common Mistakes

    Common Mistake — Treating a suspected cause as an already-confirmed diagnosis

    Error: Assuming that because a fever occurs during an outbreak period, the fever has already been confirmed as connected to that outbreak.

    Correction: Distinguish a suspected or possible cause from a confirmed result, and use the negative TAM pattern (Section 9.2) to state clearly what has not yet been confirmed.

    Common Mistake — Confusing a single case with a recurring seasonal pattern

    Error: Using the imperfective TAM frame alone to describe a pattern, such as a seasonal rise in illness, that recurs on a regular yearly schedule.

    Correction: Use the habitual TAM frame (Section 9.1) for patterns that recur regularly, such as Zazzaɓi na yawaita a wannan lokaci kowace shekara, reserving the imperfective for a single process currently in progress.

    Common Mistake — Overgeneralizing one unconfirmed case to a whole outbreak

    Error: Concluding that an outbreak has been confirmed for a whole neighborhood because one child has an unconfirmed fever.

    Correction: State precisely what one test or health worker has not yet confirmed, as Malama Hauwa does in the Model Dialogue, rather than making a broader claim than the evidence supports.

    Answer Key / Amsoshi

    Tambayoyin fahimta na tattaunawa (Dialogue comprehension)

    1. Malama Hauwa is vaccinating children at the clinic, as is done every year at this time during the rainy season.
    2. The doctor has not yet confirmed whether Maryam's son's fever is related to the outbreak.
    3. The doctor is currently analyzing the test results.
    4. Within two days, they should know the test results and whether the fever is related to the outbreak.

    Kula na sauraro (Listening responses)

    (1) Health workers are currently vaccinating children at the clinic. (2) The announcement describes the yearly rainy-season pattern of increased illness and the vaccination campaign that responds to it. (3) Some parents brought their children for testing because the children had a slight fever. (4) It has not yet been confirmed whether the fevers are related to the outbreak mentioned in the media.

    Tambayoyin karatu (Reading comprehension)

    1. The clinic is currently giving children vaccinations this week, as it does every year during the rainy season.
    2. Health workers are studying the recurring seasonal rise in fever cases in nearby neighborhoods, to determine whether it is linked to the outbreak being discussed.
    3. The notice does not confirm whether there is a connection to the outbreak, and the clinic has not been closed for that reason; if a new illness is confirmed, the public will be notified immediately.

    Sashi na B (Grammar)

    1. Sample: Ana yin alurar riga-kafi a asibitocinmu kowace shekara a lokacin damina. Zazzaɓi na yawaita a unguwarmu kowace shekara a wannan lokaci.
    2. Sample: Likita bai tabbatar da sakamakon gwajin ba tukuna. Har yanzu ma’aikatan lafiya ba su san ainihin dalilin cutar ba.
    3. Sample: A suspected cause is something health workers consider possible based on timing or symptoms, marked with words like alaƙa or expressed through the negative TAM frame, while a confirmed cause is something testing has actually verified. Health and science language keeps these separate so that the public understands what is known versus what is still under investigation.

    Sashi na C (Sample writing)

    A unguwarmu, ma’aikatan lafiya suna yi wa yara alurar riga-kafi a wannan mako, kamar yadda suke yi kowace shekara a lokacin damina. Zazzaɓi na yawaita a wannan lokaci, don haka iyaye da yawa suna kawo ’ya’yansu domin gwaji. Likitoci har yanzu suna nazarin sakamakon gwaje-gwajen, kuma ba a tabbatar da ko akwai sabuwar cuta ba. Idan an tabbatar da wani abu, za a sanar da jama’a nan take.

    Other accurate, well-organized explanations that correctly distinguish ongoing, recurring, and unconfirmed details are equally acceptable.
