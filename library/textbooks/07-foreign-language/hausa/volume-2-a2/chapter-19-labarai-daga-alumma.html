Sashe na 4 — Unit IV: Shirye-shirye da Ra'ayi (Plans and Perspectives)
19

Labarai daga Al'umma

Community News and Messages · Speak and Write Hausa: Expanding Foundations for Independent Use

Chapter Overview

Chapter 19 continues Unit IV. In Chapter 18 you described a problem, proposed a solution with magani and warware, and negotiated next steps with watakila, while continuing to use tunda, idan, fiye da, and kamar, and tracking reference across sentences. This chapter keeps those same systems active and applies them to a new communicative task: understanding and producing short announcements, updates, and community messages. No new grammar system is introduced. Instead, the same reason, condition, comparison, and reference-tracking forms continue developing toward consolidation, alongside a small set of new vocabulary for announcing, updating, and informing a community.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Understand a short spoken or written community announcement.
  2. Introduce an announcement or update using sanarwa (announcement) and ana sanar da (it is announced that).
  3. Report a change or update using an canza (it has been changed) and yanzu (now).
  4. Give a reason for an announced change using domin and tunda, continuing from Chapters 16–18.
  5. State a condition attached to an announcement using idan.
  6. Compare an old arrangement with a new one using fiye da and kamar.
  7. Use the formulaic expression da fatan (it is hoped that) to close a message politely.
  8. Track a named event, place, or person across a connected announcement without unnecessary repetition.
  9. Read and write a short, organized community message or announcement.
  10. Identify and self-correct common Unit IV errors in announcement and update language.

Communication Scenario

Amina, Bala, and Musa's community group, last seen negotiating the leaking roof of the community hall in Chapter 18, now needs to inform the wider community about what was decided. Over the course of this chapter, you will help understand, and then write, the kind of short announcement a community group sends when plans change.

  1. No new vocabulary domain beyond announcements, updates, and community messages is required for this chapter.
  2. Reason and condition connectors (tunda, idan) and comparison expressions (fiye da, kamar) from Chapters 16–18 remain developing, not yet fully consolidated, and continue to appear in this chapter.
  3. Reference tracking across connected sentences, introduced in Chapter 16 and practiced in Chapters 17–18, is expected to continue without additional explanation of the underlying rule.
  4. Vocabulary and grammar from Units I, II, and III remain fully active and are expected to appear accurately.

Pronunciation Focus

Sound Focus — Tone and vowel length in sanarwa, canza, and gudummawa

Pay attention to the tone pattern and vowel length of this chapter's key words: sanarwa, canza, and gudummawa. Note the doubled consonant in gudummawa, which must be held slightly longer than a single consonant, similar to the doubled consonant already practiced in gaggawa in Chapter 18. Keep an even syllable rhythm across full announcement-style sentences such as Ana sanar da cewa an canza ranar taro zuwa Litinin mai zuwa, without letting the longer sentence speed up or lose its rhythm toward the end. This review consolidates pronunciation features already active since earlier chapters; no new sound or contrast is introduced here.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter continues developing the four systems active since Chapter 16 — reason and condition, comparison, reference tracking, and controlled reading/writing — and applies them to announcements and community messages. No new connector or comparison expression is introduced. Completive/perfective, negation, focus, and prepositional systems from Units I–III remain fully active.

19.1 Opening an announcement with ana sanar da

  • Ana sanar da cewa an canza ranar taro zuwa Litinin mai zuwa.It is announced that the meeting date has been changed to next Monday.
  • A sani cewa za a fara gyaran rufin zauren al'umma gobe da safe.Please note that repair of the community hall roof will begin tomorrow morning.

Ana sanar da cewa... ("it is announced that...") opens a formal community message, continuing the impersonal existential-style pattern already familiar from earlier chapters. A sani cewa... ("be it known that...") is a related fixed expression used to highlight an especially important point within a message.

19.2 Continuing: Reason and condition with tunda and idan

Language Building Block — Announcing a Change and Its Reason or Condition
an canza + [item], domin/tunda + [reason] idan + [condition clause], za a sake canza shirin
An canza wurin taro, tunda zauren al'umma har yanzu ba shi da rufin da aka gyara.
The meeting place has been changed, since the community hall still doesn't have a repaired roof.
Idan gyaran bai kammala ba nan da wata guda, za a sake canza shirin taron kaɗan.
If the repair isn't finished within a month, the meeting arrangement will be changed again slightly.

As in Chapters 16–18, tunda introduces a reason the reader or listener already accepts, while idan introduces a genuine condition attached to a future update. Continue to use these connectors carefully, since they remain developing forms at this stage.

19.3 Continuing: Comparison with fiye da and kamar, and the closing formula da fatan

  • Sabon wurin taron ya fi tsohon wurin sauƙi fiye da abin da muke tsammani.The new meeting place is easier than the old one, more than we expected.
  • Da fatan kowa zai zo taron da kansa, kamar yadda muka tattauna a baya.It is hoped everyone will come to the meeting in person, as we discussed before.da fatan = "it is hoped," closes a message with a polite wish or reminder

Da fatan ("it is hoped that") is a new formulaic expression that closes a message politely, often just before a final request, reminder, or wish. It is introduced here as a formulaic closer, not as a fully analyzed grammar system, and pairs naturally with a following clause stating the hoped-for outcome.

19.4 Continuing: Reference tracking while reporting an update

When an event, place, or decision has already been named in an announcement, later sentences should continue with a pronoun, an omitted subject, or a short connector rather than restating the full name each time.

  • Kwamitin gudanarwa ya yanke shawarar canza ranar taro. Ya bayyana cewa gyaran rufin har yanzu ba a kammala ba, kuma yana fatan kowa zai fahimta.The organizing committee decided to change the meeting date. It explained that the roof repair still hasn't been completed, and it hopes everyone will understand."Kwamitin gudanarwa" named once; "ya/yana" (it) tracks the reference afterward

Atisayen ƙa'ida — Grammar practice: Complete each sentence with the word or expression that fits.

  1. ___ (Ana sanar da/A takaice) cewa an canza ranar taro zuwa mako mai zuwa.
  2. An canza wurin taro ___ (tunda/idan) rufin zauren al'umma bai gama gyara ba.
  3. Sabon shirin ya fi tsohon ___ (kamar/fiye da) sauƙi.
  4. ___ (Da fatan/Watakila) kowa zai karɓi wannan sanarwa da kyau.

Model Dialogue

Yanayin hali: Bayan sun yanke shawara a kan matsalar rufin zauren al'umma, Amina, Bala, da Musa suna tattauna yadda za su sanar da al'umma game da canje-canjen da suka yi.

(Setting: After deciding what to do about the community hall roof problem, Amina, Bala, and Musa discuss how to inform the community about the changes they have made.)

MUSA Yanzu da mun yanke shawara, dole mu rubuta sanarwa ga al'umma domin kowa ya san canjin.Now that we've decided, we need to write an announcement for the community so everyone knows about the change.
AMINA Daidai ne. Ana sanar da cewa an canza wurin bikin zuwa makarantar al'umma, tunda gyaran rufin zauren bai kammala ba.That's right. It is announced that the venue has been changed to the community school, since the hall roof repair hasn't been completed.
BALA Kuma mu sanar da su cewa idan ma'aikacin ya gama gyaran da wuri, za mu iya komawa zauren al'umma.And let's tell them that if the repairman finishes the repair early, we can go back to the community hall.
MUSA Makarantar al'umma ta fi zauren girma fiye da yadda muke tsammani, don haka ba matsala ba ce.The community school is bigger than the hall, more than we expected, so it's not a problem.
AMINA A sani cewa kowa da kowa yana da gudummawa a wannan aiki, kuma da fatan kowa zai zo taron karshe kafin bikin.Be it known that everyone has a contribution to make in this effort, and it is hoped everyone will come to the final meeting before the celebration.
BALA Zan buga sanarwar a kan katin sanarwa na masallaci da kasuwa, domin mutane da yawa su gan ta.I'll post the announcement on the notice board at the mosque and the market, so many people can see it.
MUSA A takaice, mu tabbata sanarwar a fili take kuma a sauƙi ake fahimtarta.In short, let's make sure the announcement is clear and easy to understand.

Tambayoyin fahimta — Comprehension questions:

  1. What decision does the group need to inform the community about?
  2. Why has the venue been changed, and what is the condition under which they might change it back?
  3. How does Musa compare the new venue to the old one?
  4. What does Amina hope for at the end of the announcement?
  5. Where does Bala plan to post the announcement, and why?

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Sharing community news

Community announcements are often shared through multiple channels at once, including notice boards at a mosque, church, market, or school, word of mouth through respected community members, and increasingly through phone messaging groups. Many communities favor closing a message with a polite wish such as da fatan, even when the announced change is routine, though the level of formality can vary with the setting, the group's relationship to the reader, and the seriousness of the update.

Regional Variation — Announcement style

Some communities and registers use additional formal openers or closers for announcements beyond ana sanar da and da fatan, particularly in official, religious, or media contexts. This course presents the productive default forms for this level; further formal-register and regional announcement vocabulary is introduced for recognition in later volumes.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Treating sanarwa and bayani as interchangeable

Error: *Muna son bayani game da taro, don haka sai mu buga bayani a kan katin sanarwa. (using bayani where a formal announcement is meant)

Correction: Muna son sanar da mutane game da taro, don haka sai mu buga sanarwa a kan katin sanarwa. — Sanarwa refers to a formal announcement meant to inform many people at once, while bayani refers to information or an explanation more generally; the two should not be substituted for each other in this domain.

Common Mistake — Repeating a name instead of tracking reference

Error: *Kwamitin gudanarwa ya rubuta sanarwa. Kwamitin gudanarwa ya buga sanarwar. Kwamitin gudanarwa ya jira martani. (repeating the full committee name in every sentence)

Correction: Kwamitin gudanarwa ya rubuta sanarwa. Ya buga sanarwar a kasuwa, sa'an nan ya jira martani. — Once a group or event has been named, continue with a pronoun, an omitted subject, or a connector, as is natural in connected Hausa narration.
