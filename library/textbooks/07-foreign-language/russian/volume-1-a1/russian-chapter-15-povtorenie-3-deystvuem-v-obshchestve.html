Повторе́ние 3: Де́йствуем в о́бществе

Unit III integrated assessment · Speak and Write Russian: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Combine directions, transportation, and everyday movement, from Chapter 11, with plans, weather, and leisure, from Chapter 12, in one connected conversation.
  2. Describe symptoms, feelings, and needs using the dative experiencer, and give softened advice using мо́жно and нельзя́, from Chapter 13.
  3. Describe a job, a course of study, and a daily schedule using the instrumental case for role, means, and accompaniment, from Chapter 14.
  4. Compare schedules, workplaces, and routines using бо́льше, ме́ньше, and бо́лее, together with чем.
  5. Use communication verbs, such as звони́ть, писа́ть, and объясня́ть, with their correct case government.
  6. Demonstrate control of the dative case, the instrumental case, introductory aspect, and introductory motion verbs across connected listening, speaking, reading, and writing tasks.
  7. Complete an integrated performance task that draws on Chapters 1 through 14 without depending on any new grammar, case, aspect, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system.

This chapter is a cumulative assessment chapter. It introduces no new major productive grammar, case, aspect, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system. Every task below draws only on material already active in Chapters 1 through 14.

Communication Scenario

A week has passed since Серге́й felt unwell and rested at home. Now he and Ири́на meet again, this time with their friend Ната́ша, who is visiting from another city and needs directions, wants to know about the weather and weekend plans, and is curious about everyone's studies and jobs. The conversation moves naturally between getting around the city, making plans, talking about health, and describing study and work, exactly as it has in Chapters 11 through 14.

  1. You will review how to give directions, discuss plans and weather, describe symptoms and needs, and talk about study and work, all within one connected exchange.
  2. You will practice recognizing when each active case, the dative, the instrumental, and the recycled accusative, prepositional, and genitive, is required.
  3. You will complete listening, speaking, reading, and writing tasks that assess Chapters 1 through 14 together, as preparation for Chapter 16.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review — Stress across active cases Review, no new system

No new pronunciation system is introduced in this chapter. As you complete this chapter's tasks, keep applying the following patterns from Chapters 11 through 14.

  • Continue reducing unstressed о and а, as in пого́да and рабо́та, even when the word carries several unstressed syllables.
  • Continue marking the instrumental singular ending -ом, as in инжене́ром and врачо́м, on the syllable that already carries the word's stress, and remember that врачо́м shifts stress onto the ending, since the word belongs to the small stress-shifting group introduced in Chapter 14.
  • Continue distinguishing hard and soft consonants in dative and instrumental endings, such as -у/-ю and -ом/-ем.

Grammar Review, Units I–III

No new grammar system is introduced in this chapter. The subsections below reorganize, rather than expand, the systems activated in Chapters 1 through 14.

Directions and Motion Assessed

Review from Chapter 11: use в/на plus the accusative for destination, в/на plus the prepositional for location, and из/с plus the genitive for source, together with идти́/ходи́ть for movement on foot and е́хать/е́здить for movement by vehicle.

  • Я иду́ в университе́т.I am going to the university (on foot).в + accusative, destination
  • Она́ е́дет из це́нтра домо́й.She is going home from downtown (by vehicle).из + genitive, source

Plans, Weather, and Introductory Aspect Assessed

Review from Chapter 12: use the compound future for ongoing or repeated plans, and a perfective nonpast form for a single completed future action, following the introductory imperfective/perfective contrast.

  • Мы бу́дем гуля́ть в па́рке.We will be walking in the park.compound future, imperfective, ongoing plan
  • Мы погуля́ем по́сле обе́да.We will take a walk after lunch.perfective nonpast, one bounded event

Dative Experiencer and Softened Advice Assessed

Review from Chapter 13: use the dative case for the person experiencing a feeling, need, or condition, and use мо́жно and нельзя́ for permission and prohibition.

  • Ната́ше ну́жно отдохну́ть.Natásha needs to rest.dative experiencer + ну́жно
  • Тебе́ нельзя́ рабо́тать так мно́го.You should not work so much.dative + нельзя́, softened advice

Instrumental Case and Comparison Assessed

Review from Chapter 14: use the instrumental case for role, after быть or стать, for means, with no preposition, and for accompaniment, with с. Use бо́льше, ме́ньше, and бо́лее, together with чем, for comparison.

  • Ната́ша хо́чет стать учи́телем.Natásha wants to become a teacher.instrumental role, after стать
  • У Ири́ны бо́льше заня́тий, чем у Ната́ши.Irína has more classes than Natásha.бо́льше + чем, comparison
Language Focus — Choosing among the active cases

By this point, you actively use the nominative, accusative, genitive, prepositional, dative, and instrumental cases, each for specific communicative functions. As you complete this chapter, pause before each noun or pronoun and ask which function it performs, direction, location, source, possession, experiencer, or role, means, and accompaniment, before choosing its ending.

Model Dialogue: Дела́ иду́т хорошо́?

Context: Серге́й, Ири́на, and their friend Ната́ша, visiting from another city, meet near the metro station on a Saturday morning.

Ната́ша: Приве́т! Извини́те, я не зна́ю, как идти́ к це́нтру отсю́да. Вы мне не поможете? Hi! Sorry, I don't know how to get to the center from here. Could you help me?
Серге́й: Коне́чно. Отсю́да на́до е́хать на метро́, а пото́м идти́ пешко́м от ста́нции. Of course. From here you need to go by metro, and then walk on foot from the station.
Ири́на: Ната́ша, как тебе́ пого́да сего́дня? У нас за́втра бу́дут интере́сные пла́ны, е́сли бу́дет тепло́. Natásha, how do you find the weather today? We'll have interesting plans tomorrow, if it's warm.
Ната́ша: Мне сего́дня немно́го хо́лодно, че́стно говоря́. На про́шлой неде́ле мне бы́ло пло́хо, и я ещё не совсе́м здоро́ва. I feel a bit cold today, honestly. Last week I felt unwell, and I'm still not completely well.
Серге́й: Тогда́ тебе́ ну́жно оде́ться тепле́е, и, наве́рное, тебе́ нельзя́ гуля́ть до́лго на у́лице. Then you need to dress warmer, and you probably shouldn't walk outside for long.
Ната́ша: Спаси́бо, вы о́чень добры́. А кем вы рабо́таете, кста́ти? Я по́мню, что Ири́на хо́чет стать врачо́м. Thank you, you're very kind. And what do you work as, by the way? I remember that Irína wants to become a doctor.
Ири́на: Да, я студе́нтка и гото́влюсь к экза́менам с друзья́ми, а Серге́й рабо́тает инжене́ром и рабо́тает компью́тером ка́ждый день. Yes, I'm a student and I prepare for exams with friends, and Sergéy works as an engineer and works with a computer every day.
Ната́ша: У меня́, ка́жется, ме́ньше заня́тий, чем у тебя́, Ири́на, но я то́же хочу́ стать учи́телем. It seems I have fewer classes than you, Irína, but I also want to become a teacher.

Notice how this dialogue moves through directions and motion, Chapter 11, weather and plans, Chapter 12, the dative experiencer and softened advice, Chapter 13, and the instrumental for role and means, together with comparison, Chapter 14, all within one natural exchange.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Helping a visitor find their way

Offering clear, practical directions to someone who is unfamiliar with a city is a common and valued form of everyday helpfulness across Russian-speaking communities, much as it is in many other communities. The specific phrasing, level of detail, and willingness to walk part of the way with a visitor can vary by city, generation, and personal habit, so treat the pattern in this chapter as one common approach rather than a fixed rule.

Regional Variation — Talking about health with friends

How openly people discuss feeling unwell with friends, as opposed to only with family or a doctor, can vary by relationship, generation, and personal preference, echoing the softened advice patterns from Chapter 13. Some speakers share health details readily in casual conversation, while others prefer brief, general statements such as мне немно́го хо́лодно. Adjust your own directness to the relationship and the setting.

Common Mistakes

This section reviews mistakes already introduced in Chapters 11 through 14. No new error pattern is introduced here.

Common Mistake — Confusing идти́/ходи́ть and е́хать/е́здить

Error: Saying Я иду́ в друго́й го́род instead of Я е́ду в друго́й го́род.

Correction: Use е́хать/е́здить for movement by vehicle over a distance, such as to another city, and reserve идти́/ходи́ть for movement on foot: Я е́ду в друго́й го́род, not Я иду́ в друго́й го́род.

Common Mistake — Using the nominative instead of the dative for an experiencer

Error: Saying Я ну́жно отдохну́ть instead of Мне ну́жно отдохну́ть.

Correction: The person experiencing a need takes the dative case, not the nominative: Мне ну́жно отдохну́ть, not Я ну́жно отдохну́ть.

Common Mistake — Using мно́го instead of бо́льше in a comparison

Error: Saying У меня́ мно́го вре́мени, чем у тебя́ instead of У меня́ бо́льше вре́мени, чем у тебя́.

Correction: Мно́го means simply "a lot" and does not compare two things. Use бо́льше, more, or ме́ньше, less, together with чем, than, to compare quantities: бо́льше вре́мени, чем у тебя́.

Looking Ahead

Что вы уже уме́ете — What you can do now

You can give and follow directions, describe transportation, discuss weather and plans using introductory aspect, describe how you feel and give softened advice using the dative case, and describe your job, studies, and daily schedule using the instrumental case and comparison. You can connect all of these topics in one coherent conversation, reading, or piece of writing.
