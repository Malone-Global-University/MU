Пое́здки и путеше́ствия

Travel experiences · Speak and Write Russian: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Recognize and use a small set of prefixed motion verbs, including пое́хать, прие́хать, уе́хать, and верну́ться, to describe departure, arrival, and return.
  2. Distinguish source, route, and destination in a travel narrative, using genitive forms after из and с for source, and accusative forms after в and на for destination.
  3. Describe a trip in the past, choosing aspect according to whether you are narrating the journey as a completed event or describing what happened along the way.
  4. Talk about tickets, lodging, and simple travel problems using vocabulary and case patterns already active from earlier chapters.
  5. Ask and answer basic questions about where someone went, where they came from, and when they returned.
  6. Narrate a short connected account of a trip, combining prefixed motion verbs with the past-tense sequencing skills from Chapter 16.

This chapter continues Unit IV. It introduces prefixed motion verbs and source, route, and destination language as productive systems for the first time, and it further develops the aspect awareness introduced in Chapter 16. All vocabulary, cases, and other grammar from Chapters 1 through 16 remain active and continue to appear throughout this chapter.

Communication Scenario

Ната́ша has just returned from her trip, and she meets Ири́на at the station before catching a connecting train. They talk about where Ната́ша went, how she got there, what happened on the way, and when she is coming back. Later, Серге́й writes a short message to a friend about a weekend trip that did not go entirely as planned.

  1. You will learn how to talk about leaving, arriving, and returning using a small set of prefixed motion verbs.
  2. You will practice marking where a trip started, how it went, and where it ended, using the case patterns you already know.
  3. You will practice narrating a trip as a connected past-tense story, choosing aspect for each stage of the journey.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Prefix and root stress in motion verbs

When a prefix is added to a motion verb, stress can behave differently in different forms. In the past tense of прие́хать and уе́хать, stress generally stays on the syllable already familiar to you from е́хать, as in прие́хал, прие́хала, прие́хали. Compare this with пое́хать, where stress shifts onto the prefix in most past-tense forms: пое́хал, пое́хала, пое́хали.

  • пое́хал, пое́хала, пое́хали — stress on the prefix throughout.
  • прие́хал, прие́хала, прие́хали — stress stays on the root, as in е́хал.
  • верну́лся, верну́лась, верну́лись — stress on the ending, with a shift to the final syllable in the feminine form, as you saw with был/была́ in Chapter 16.

Continue reducing unstressed о and а, and continue distinguishing hard and soft consonants in the reflexive ending -ся, which is pronounced with a hard с in most conversational speech.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Prefixed Motion Verbs New

In Chapter 11 you learned идти́/ходи́ть for going on foot and е́хать/е́здить for going by transportation. This chapter adds a small set of perfective prefixed verbs, built on е́хать, that mark departure, arrival, and return as completed events with a clear beginning, endpoint, or result.

Building Block — Three travel-stage verbs
пое́хать — departure · прие́хать — arrival · верну́ться — return
all three are perfective; all three combine with destination, source, or route phrases already familiar from Chapter 11
Мы пое́хали в Санкт-Петербу́рг у́тром.
We set off for Saint Petersburg in the morning. (departure)
Мы прие́хали в Санкт-Петербу́рг ве́чером.
We arrived in Saint Petersburg in the evening. (arrival)
Мы верну́лись домо́й в воскресе́нье.
We returned home on Sunday. (return)
  • Она́ уе́хала из Москвы́ в понеде́льник.She left Moscow on Monday.уе́хать, perfective, departure from a place
  • Когда́ вы прие́хали в го́род?When did you arrive in the city?прие́хать, perfective, arrival at a destination

This chapter treats пое́хать, прие́хать, уе́хать, and верну́ться as a productive travel-stage set. Other prefixed motion verbs, such as those built on идти́, remain receptive for now and will be developed further in later volumes.

Source, Route, and Destination New

A travel narrative usually names where a trip started, where it went, and where it ended. Russian marks these three roles with case, using patterns you have already practiced with individual prepositions.

  • Он прие́хал из Каза́ни.He came from Kazan.из + genitive; source, a city
  • Мы пое́хали с ю́га страны́.We set off from the south of the country.с + genitive; source, a region
  • По доро́ге мы останови́лись в ма́леньком го́роде.Along the way we stopped in a small town.route stop; в + prepositional, location
  • Она́ пое́хала в Ирку́тск на по́езде.She set off for Irkutsk by train.в + accusative; destination, plus на + prepositional for means of transport
Language Focus — Из versus с for source

Just as в and на split location and destination between two prepositions, из and с split source between two prepositions in a related pattern. A place normally reached with в uses из for source, and a place normally reached with на uses с for source: from Chapter 11, в Москву́ corresponds to из Москвы́, while на по́чту corresponds to с по́чты. This pairing is not random, and it is worth learning place names together with their correct source preposition.

Aspect in Travel Narration New

The imperfective/perfective contrast from Chapter 16 continues to guide travel narration. Use a perfective travel-stage verb to move the story forward from one stage to the next, and use an imperfective verb to describe what was happening during a stage of the trip.

  • Мы е́хали в по́езде и разгова́ривали всю ночь.We were riding on the train and talking all night.imperfective, process, duration during a stage of the trip
  • Мы прие́хали у́тром и сра́зу пошли́ в гости́ницу.We arrived in the morning and went straight to the hotel.perfective; two completed events moving the story forward

Talking About Route Problems New

Describe a travel problem as a completed event, then explain what happened as a result, connecting the two with sequencing words already active from Chapter 16.

  • Мы опозда́ли на по́езд, и нам пришло́сь пересе́сть на друго́й.We were late for the train, and we had to change to another one.опозда́ть, perfective; result explained with пришло́сь, receptive for now
  • Рейс заде́рживался, и мы до́лго жда́ли в аэропорту́.The flight was delayed, and we waited at the airport for a long time.imperfective заде́рживался for an ongoing delay; жда́ли for duration

Model Dialogue: На вокза́ле

Context: Ната́ша has just arrived back in town after her trip, and she meets Ири́на at the station before catching a connecting train home.

Ири́на: Ната́ша! Наконе́ц-то! Отку́да ты прие́хала? Natasha! Finally! Where did you arrive from?
Ната́ша: Я е́здила в Каза́нь на неде́лю. Пое́хала в про́шлую пя́тницу, а верну́лась то́лько сего́дня у́тром. I went to Kazan for a week. I set off last Friday, and I only got back this morning.
Ири́на: Как прошла́ пое́здка? Как ты туда́ добрала́сь? How did the trip go? How did you get there?
Ната́ша: Я пое́хала на по́езде. По доро́ге мы останови́лись в одно́м небольшо́м го́роде на два часа́, потому́ что по́езд заде́рживался. Но в о́бщем всё бы́ло хорошо́. I went by train. Along the way we stopped in one small town for two hours, because the train was delayed. But overall everything was fine.
Ири́на: Где ты останови́лась в Каза́ни? Where did you stay in Kazan?
Ната́ша: Снача́ла я останови́лась в гости́нице, а пото́м перее́хала к дру́гу, потому́ что гости́ница была́ дорога́я. At first I stayed at a hotel, and then I moved to a friend's place, because the hotel was expensive.
Ири́на: А обра́тно то́же на по́езде е́хала? And did you also travel back by train?
Ната́ша: Да, но я почти́ опозда́ла на по́езд! Я вы́шла из гости́ницы дру́га сли́шком по́здно, добежа́ла до вокза́ла и се́ла в ваго́н за две мину́ты до отправле́ния. Yes, but I almost missed the train! I left my friend's place too late, ran to the station, and got into the train car two minutes before departure.
Ири́на: Ничего́ себе́! Ну, я ра́да, что ты верну́лась благополу́чно. А мой по́езд ухо́дит че́рез де́сять мину́т, мне пора́! Wow! Well, I'm glad you got back safely. And my train leaves in ten minutes, I need to go!

Notice how the dialogue marks each stage of the trip with a perfective verb, such as пое́хала, прие́хала, and верну́лась, while imperfective forms such as заде́рживался describe what was happening along the way.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Train travel and long distances

Long-distance train travel is a familiar part of daily life for many people across Russian-speaking regions, given the geographic scale of the Russian Federation and neighboring countries. Overnight trains, sleeper cars, and multi-hour journeys between cities are common, though air travel, bus travel, and shorter regional trips are equally normal depending on the distance and the traveler's situation. Do not assume that every trip described in this chapter reflects a single fixed national travel pattern.

Language Note — Motion prefixes carry real meaning

It can be tempting to treat пое́хать, прие́хать, and уе́хать as three interchangeable ways to say "go" in the past. Each prefix marks a distinct stage of the journey: по- marks the beginning of a trip, при- marks arrival at a destination, and у- marks departure away from a place, often with the implication that the person is now absent from that place. Choosing the correct prefix depends on which stage of the journey you want to highlight, not on stylistic preference.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Using е́хать instead of a prefixed form for a completed stage

Error: Saying Мы е́хали в Каза́нь в пя́тницу to report that a trip was completed, when the intended meaning is that the group set off and arrived.

Correction: Use the perfective префixed verb to report a completed stage of a journey: Мы пое́хали в Каза́нь в пя́тницу. Save the unprefixed imperfective е́хали for describing what was happening during the ride itself, as in Мы е́хали в Каза́нь и разгова́ривали всю доро́гу.

Common Mistake — Confusing из/с with в/на for source

Error: Saying Она́ прие́хала в Москвы́ instead of Она́ прие́хала из Москвы́ when describing where someone came from.

Correction: Source uses из or с plus the genitive, not в or на: Она́ прие́хала из Москвы́. Remember that a place normally reached with в pairs with из for source, and a place normally reached with на pairs with с for source, following the pattern from Chapter 11.

Common Mistake — Forgetting reflexive agreement in верну́ться

Error: A woman saying Я верну́лся домо́й instead of Я верну́лась домо́й.

Correction: Верну́ться is a reflexive verb, and its past-tense forms agree with the gender of the subject just like any other past-tense verb: a woman says Я верну́лась домо́й, and a man says Я верну́лся домо́й.
