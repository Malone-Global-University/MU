На уроке русского языка

In Russian Class · Speak and Write Russian: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Recognize, say, and write the complete thirty three letter Cyrillic alphabet, including the letters not introduced in Chapter 1.
  2. Identify people and objects in a classroom using Это....
  3. Ask and answer Кто это? and Что это?.
  4. Recognize the three noun genders in the nominative case, and match a first set of adjectives to a noun's gender.
  5. Describe classroom objects using basic colors.
  6. Understand and follow a small set of classroom instructions.
  7. Count from 0 to 20 in Russian.

Communication Scenario

It is the second evening of Anna's beginner Russian class. Before the group can talk about anything else, they need a shared way to talk about the room itself, the people in it, and the things on the desks. The teacher spends the evening pointing at objects, asking what they are, and asking who people are, building the class's first working vocabulary of the classroom itself.

  1. You will meet the complete Cyrillic alphabet in this chapter, building directly on the letters from Chapter 1.
  2. You will learn to identify people and things using the same zero copula pattern from Chapter 1, now expanded to a wider set of nouns.

Pronunciation Focus — Stress marking and the letter ё

The letter ё is always stressed wherever it appears in a word, so this textbook does not add a separate acute stress mark on top of it. Every other new multisyllabic word in this chapter's vocabulary is marked for stress, exactly as in Chapter 1.

  • Practice writing each new letter by hand, in both its printed and its capital form.
  • Say each classroom word aloud slowly, then at a natural pace, checking the marked stress each time.

Language Focus: What Is This?

Purpose: identify people and objects, ask who or what something is, recognize noun gender, and describe an object using a color adjective that agrees with it.

2.1 Это, Кто это?, and Что это?

Language Focus

Chapter 1 introduced Это [noun] with no verb "to be," using it for names. This chapter widens the same pattern to classroom people and objects. To ask about a person, use Кто это?, literally "who is this?". To ask about a thing, use Что это?, literally "what is this?".

  • Что э́то? — Э́то кни́га. What is this? — This is a book.
  • Кто э́то? — Э́то учи́тель. Who is this? — This is the teacher.

Use Кто for people and Что for things and ideas. This distinction is consistent throughout Russian.

2.2 Noun gender in the nominative case

Language Focus

Every Russian noun belongs to one of three genders, masculine, feminine, or neuter. In the nominative case, the dictionary form of the noun, gender is usually predictable from the noun's ending.

Recognizing gender from the nominative singular ending
Typical endingGenderExample
consonantmasculineстол, стул, каранда́ш
-а / -яfeminineкни́га, ру́чка, доска́
-о / -еneuterокно́, сло́во
-ьmasculine or feminine, learned individuallyтетра́дь (feminine), дверь (feminine)

Nouns ending in -ь do not show gender from spelling alone, so each one must be learned with its gender, as the vocabulary table above already does.

2.3 Basic adjective agreement

Building Block — An adjective matches its noun's gender
[adjective, masculine/feminine/neuter form] + [noun]
A Russian adjective changes its ending to match the gender of the noun it describes. At this stage, learn the three basic endings for the color adjectives in this chapter's vocabulary, without yet studying the full adjective declension system, which is scheduled for a later chapter.
кра́сный каранда́ш · кра́сная кни́га · кра́сное окно́
a red pencil (masculine) · a red book (feminine) · a red window (neuter)
  • Э́то си́ний стул. This is a blue chair. си́н+ий, masculine ending, agrees with стул
  • Э́то жёлтая ру́чка. This is a yellow pen. жёлт+ая, feminine ending, agrees with ру́чка

You are only responsible for producing the three basic endings shown here, applied to this chapter's color words. Full adjective agreement across all cases is developed gradually in later chapters.

Model Dialogue: В классе

Context: The teacher holds up objects and points to people, and the class answers together.

Учи́тель: Что э́то? What is this?
Кла́сс: Э́то кни́га. This is a book.
Учи́тель: Пра́вильно! А э́то что? Correct! And what is this?
Ми́ша: Э́то каранда́ш. Он си́ний. This is a pencil. It is blue.

The teacher points toward the door, where another student has just arrived.

Учи́тель: Кто э́то? Who is this?
А́нна: Э́то студе́нтка. Меня́ зову́т А́нна, а её зову́т Со́ня. This is a student. My name is Anna, and her name is Sonya.
Учи́тель: О́чень прия́тно, Со́ня. Сади́тесь, пожа́луйста. Nice to meet you, Sonya. Please, sit down.

Notice that the teacher's question changes word order, А э́то что?, without changing its meaning. Russian word order remains flexible even in a fixed classroom routine.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text to speech tool to read the lines below aloud, or read them aloud to yourself slowly, paying attention to the marked stress.

Listening script
Что э́то? Э́то тетра́дь. Она́ зелёная. А что э́то? Э́то стол. Он чёрный.
What is this? This is a notebook. It is green. And what is this? This is a table. It is black.
  1. Listen to (or read aloud) the script above, and write down the two objects that are named.
  2. For each object, write down its color in Russian.
  3. Listen for the stressed syllable in тетра́дь and in зелёная. Which syllable carries the stress in each word?

Speaking Practice

Управля́емая пра́ктика — Controlled practice: name the object

Point to five objects in the room around you, real or imagined, and say Э́то... followed by the Russian word and its color, choosing objects and colors only from this chapter's vocabulary.

Коммуникати́вное зада́ние — Communicative task: Кто это? Что это?

With a partner, take turns pointing at people and objects and asking Кто э́то? or Что э́то?. Answer using Э́то... and, where useful, a color.

Самостоя́тельная пра́ктика — Solo alternative

Record yourself asking and answering Что э́то? for five objects around you, and Кто э́то? for one person. Listen back and check your stress and your adjective endings.

Complete the following linked writing tasks in order.

Зада́ние 1 — Copy and mark stress: Copy each word from the Core Vocabulary tables by hand, including its stress mark, three times each.

Зада́ние 3 — Short written description: Write four short sentences describing four objects around you, using Э́то..., a noun from this chapter, and a color that agrees with it.

  2. Complete the adjective ending exercise. Check each ending against the gender of its noun.
  3. Draft your four sentence description. Keep every word within this chapter's vocabulary and Chapter 1's vocabulary.
  4. Read your description aloud once. Listen for the stressed syllable in each word.

Спи́сок прове́рки — Editing checklist:

  • Every word is written in Cyrillic, with no Latin letters.
  • Stress marks are included where the vocabulary tables show them.
  • Each adjective ending matches the gender of its noun.
  • Кто is used only for people, and что only for things.
  • No new vocabulary from outside this chapter or Chapter 1 has been used.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — The classroom as a shared script

Many Russian language classrooms, in person and online, rely on a similar small set of instructor phrases, such as чита́йте, слу́шайте, and пиши́те. Learning these instructions early makes it possible to follow a Russian speaking classroom, or a Russian language exchange partner, without needing to switch back into English for routine directions.

Regional Variation — One alphabet, many classrooms

The thirty three letter Cyrillic alphabet taught in this chapter is used for Standard Russian wherever it is taught, from Moscow to Almaty to a community classroom abroad. Some other languages that use Cyrillic script, such as Ukrainian or Kazakh, use a similar but not identical set of letters, a distinction this course will keep separate from Russian instruction throughout.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Confusing Кто and Что

Error: Asking Что э́то? about a person, by analogy with English "what," instead of using Кто э́то?.

Correction: Use Кто only for people, and Что only for things, animals, and ideas, even in casual speech.

Common Mistake — Leaving an adjective in one fixed form

Error: Using the masculine adjective ending with every noun, such as saying кра́сный кни́га instead of adjusting the ending to the noun's gender.

Correction: Match the adjective ending to the noun, as in кра́сная кни́га, since кни́га is feminine.

Common Mistake — Guessing gender from meaning rather than form

Error: Assuming a noun's gender based on its English meaning, rather than its Russian ending, especially for nouns ending in -ь.

Correction: Learn the gender of each noun as part of its vocabulary entry, and rely on the typical endings in the Language Focus section as a starting guide, not an absolute rule.
