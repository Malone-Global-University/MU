Учёба и рабо́та

Study and work · Speak and Write Russian: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Name common school and work settings, roles, tools, and responsibilities.
  2. Use the instrumental case to say who someone works as, using быть and стать.
  3. Use the instrumental case to say what tool or means someone uses to do something.
  4. Use the instrumental case, with с, to say who someone does something with.
  5. Compare people, schedules, and workplaces using simple comparative patterns.
  6. Use common communication verbs, such as звони́ть, писа́ть, and объясня́ть, with their correct case government.
  7. Connect study or work responsibilities to a change in plans caused by how someone feels, recycled from Chapter 13.

This chapter continues Unit III and introduces new grammar. It also continues to recycle health and well-being vocabulary from Chapter 13, along with earlier material on daily routine, plans, and preferences.

Communication Scenario

Sergéy feels better after his day of rest, and on Monday he is back at work. His colleague Ири́на asks him about his job, his schedule, and how he manages his workload, while Sergéy asks Ири́на about her studies at the university. Along the way, they compare their schedules and talk about how they communicate with their managers and instructors.

  1. You will learn the language needed to talk about jobs, roles, and responsibilities.
  2. You will practice describing the tools, people, and methods involved in study and work.
  3. You will practice comparing schedules and workplaces, and describing how people communicate at school and at work.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Stress in instrumental endings Review, no new system

No new pronunciation system is introduced in this chapter. Two patterns deserve special attention here, because this chapter uses the instrumental case so often.

  • The instrumental singular ending -ом on masculine and neuter nouns, as in инжене́ром, is always stressed on the syllable that already carries the word's stress, not on the ending itself, unless the word belongs to a small stress-shifting group you will meet later.
  • Continue reducing unstressed о and а, as in компью́тер and дире́ктор.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

Instrumental for Roles New

Use the instrumental case after быть, to be, and стать, to become, to say what someone's role, job, or profession is.

Building Block
Person + быть/стать + role in the instrumental
In the present tense, быть is not stated, so the instrumental role often stands with no visible verb, just as the zero copula worked in Chapter 2. The instrumental becomes visible once быть or стать appears in the past or future.
Ири́на — студе́нтка.
Irína is a student. (nominative, present, zero copula)
Ири́на хо́чет стать врачо́м.
Irína wants to become a doctor. (instrumental after стать)
  • Мой оте́ц был инжене́ром.My father was an engineer.instrumental role, past tense of быть
  • Я рабо́таю учи́телем.I work as a teacher.instrumental role, рабо́тать + instrumental
  • Она́ ста́нет дире́ктором.She will become a director.instrumental role, future of стать

Note the contrast: the present-tense zero copula keeps a role in the nominative, Ири́на студе́нтка, but быть in the past or future, and стать in any tense, take the instrumental.

Instrumental for Means and Tools New

Use the instrumental case, with no preposition, to say what tool, instrument, or means someone uses to do something.

Building Block
Verb + tool/means in the instrumental (no preposition)
The instrumental case answers the question чем, with what, when it expresses means. No preposition is needed for this function, unlike the English word "with."
Я пишу́ ру́чкой.
I write with a pen.
Мы рабо́таем компью́тером ка́ждый день.
We work with a computer every day.
  • Она́ звони́т телефо́ном колле́ге.She calls a colleague with her phone.instrumental means, no preposition
  • Он объясня́ет уро́к приме́ром.He explains the lesson with an example.instrumental means

Instrumental for Accompaniment New

Use с plus the instrumental case to say who someone does something with.

Building Block
с + person/thing in the instrumental
Unlike the instrumental of means, the instrumental of accompaniment requires the preposition с. Compare я пишу́ ру́чкой, with no preposition, and я рабо́таю с колле́гой, with с.
Я рабо́таю с колле́гой.
I work with a colleague.
Она́ говори́т с преподава́телем по́сле уро́ка.
She talks with the instructor after class.
  • Мы гото́вимся к экза́мену с друзья́ми.We are preparing for the exam with friends.с + instrumental, accompaniment
  • Серге́й обсужда́ет зада́чу с кома́ндой.Sergéy discusses the task with the team.с + instrumental, accompaniment
Language Focus — Three functions of the instrumental case

This chapter activates the instrumental case in three connected functions, role, with no preposition, means, with no preposition, and accompaniment, with с. Watch carefully for the presence or absence of с, since it changes the meaning from means to accompaniment.

Comparison New

Use бо́лее plus an adjective, or a small set of common comparative forms, together with чем, to compare two people, schedules, or workplaces.

  • Моя́ рабо́та бо́лее интере́сная, чем шко́ла.My job is more interesting than school.бо́лее + adjective + чем, comparison
  • У Ири́ны бо́льше заня́тий, чем у Серге́я.Irína has more classes than Sergéy.бо́льше + чем, quantity comparison, у + genitive recycled from Chapter 4
  • Ири́на рабо́тает ме́ньше, чем Серге́й.Irína works less than Sergéy.ме́ньше + чем, comparison

This chapter activates only бо́лее, бо́льше, and ме́ньше with чем as productive comparison. A fuller comparative and superlative system with short comparative adjectives is not active until later in the volume.

Communication Verbs and Their Case Government New

Learn the case that each communication verb requires. Звони́ть and писа́ть take a dative recipient, recycled from Chapter 13; объясня́ть takes an accusative topic and a dative recipient together; говори́ть с and обсужда́ть с take the instrumental of accompaniment.

  • Я звоню́ нача́льнику ка́ждое у́тро.I call my boss every morning.звони́ть + dative, recycled from Chapter 13
  • Преподава́тель объясня́ет нам но́вую те́му.The instructor explains a new topic to us.объясня́ть + accusative topic + dative recipient
  • Мы обсужда́ем прое́кт с колле́гами.We discuss the project with colleagues.обсужда́ть с + instrumental

Model Dialogue: Кем ты рабо́таешь?

Context: Ири́на and Серге́й talk in the office kitchen on Monday morning. Серге́й feels better after resting on Saturday.

Ири́на: Приве́т, Серге́й! Как ты себя́ чу́вствуешь сего́дня? Hi, Sergéy! How are you feeling today?
Серге́й: Уже́ намно́го лу́чше, спаси́бо. В суббо́ту мне бы́ло о́чень пло́хо, но я лежа́л до́ма и отдыха́л весь день. Much better already, thank you. On Saturday I felt very bad, but I lay at home and rested all day.
Ири́на: Хорошо́, что тебе́ лу́чше. Кста́ти, кем ты рабо́таешь здесь, инжене́ром? I'm glad you're feeling better. By the way, what do you work as here, an engineer?
Серге́й: Да, я рабо́таю инжене́ром. Я обы́чно рабо́таю компью́тером и звоню́ колле́гам, е́сли есть вопро́сы. А ты? Ты студе́нтка? Yes, I work as an engineer. I usually work with a computer and call colleagues if there are questions. And you? Are you a student?
Ири́на: Да, я студе́нтка в университе́те и хочу́ стать врачо́м. У меня́ намно́го бо́льше заня́тий, чем у тебя́ рабо́ты, ка́жется! Yes, I'm a student at the university and I want to become a doctor. I have far more classes than you have work, it seems!
Серге́й: Наве́рное, ты права́. А как ты гото́вишься к экза́менам? You're probably right. And how do you prepare for exams?
Ири́на: Я гото́влюсь с друзья́ми, и мы ча́сто пи́шем преподава́телю, е́сли нам ну́жно что-то объясни́ть. I prepare with friends, and we often write to the instructor if something needs to be explained to us.

Notice how the dialogue moves between the instrumental for role, инжене́ром, врачо́м, the instrumental for means, компью́тером, the instrumental for accompaniment, с друзья́ми, and comparison, бо́льше заня́тий, чем, all within a natural conversation that also recycles how Серге́й felt in Chapter 13.

Speaking Practice

Управля́емая пра́ктика — Controlled practice: roles and means

With a partner, take turns asking кем ты рабо́таешь, or кем ты хо́чешь стать, and answering with the instrumental case, such as я рабо́таю учи́телем or я хочу́ стать инжене́ром. Then add one sentence about a tool you use, such as я рабо́таю компью́тером.

Коммуникати́вное зада́ние — Communicative task: comparing schedules

With a partner, compare your schedules using бо́льше, чем or ме́ньше, чем, and describe one person you work or study with, using с plus the instrumental.

Самостоя́тельная пра́ктика — Solo alternative

Record yourself describing your job or your studies, including at least one instrumental role, one instrumental means, one instrumental accompaniment with с, and one comparison with бо́льше or ме́ньше.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Naming a profession

Across Russian-speaking workplaces, people commonly identify themselves by profession or role rather than by employer, so кем ты рабо́таешь, what do you work as, is a very natural question, while the name of a company is often secondary in casual conversation. Workplace norms, titles, and hierarchy still vary considerably by country, industry, and individual organization, so treat this pattern as a common conversational habit, not a universal rule.

Regional Variation — Directness in workplace requests

How directly colleagues ask each other for help or explanation can vary by workplace culture, generation, and relationship, echoing the softened advice patterns from Chapter 13. Some colleagues move quickly to a direct request, while others prefer to soften a request with phrases such as е́сли мо́жно, if it's possible. Adjust your directness to the relationship and the setting, not only to the grammar.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Using the nominative for a role after стать

Error: Saying Она́ хо́чет стать врач instead of Она́ хо́чет стать врачо́м.

Correction: Стать always takes the instrumental for the role someone becomes, unlike the present-tense zero copula: стать врачо́м, not стать врач.

Common Mistake — Adding a preposition to instrumental means

Error: Saying Я пишу́ с ру́чкой instead of Я пишу́ ру́чкой.

Correction: Instrumental means takes no preposition: писа́ть ру́чкой, not писа́ть с ру́чкой. Reserve с plus the instrumental for accompaniment, such as рабо́тать с колле́гой.

Common Mistake — Using мно́го instead of бо́льше in a comparison

Error: Saying У меня́ мно́го заня́тий, чем у тебя́ instead of У меня́ бо́льше заня́тий, чем у тебя́.

Correction: Мно́го means simply "a lot" and does not compare two things. Use бо́льше, more, or ме́ньше, less, together with чем, than, to compare quantities: бо́льше заня́тий, чем у тебя́.
