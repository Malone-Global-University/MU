Итого́вый прое́кт: Самостоя́тельное обще́ние

An integrated B1 portfolio and interview, drawing on Chapters 1 through 19 · Speak and Write Russian: Expanding Independent Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 20 closes Volume II with a final capstone project rather than a new lesson. It introduces no new case, aspect, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system. Instead, it asks learners to gather evidence of everything they have built since Chapter 1, location and destination, people and choices, quantities, plans and results, everyday and disrupted motion, messages and reported speech, study and work, health and support, community news, memories, event narration, opinions and explanations, and the presentation skills from Chapter 19. Ири́на, whose apartment search, opinions, and neighborhood presentation have carried the reader through this unit, now assembles a short written and spoken portfolio and sits for a friendly interview at her language exchange group, the same kind of event where she gave her talk in Chapter 19. Learners will do the same, building their own portfolio and interview from language they already control.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Assemble a short written portfolio that narrates a memory, tells the story of an event, and states and supports an opinion, using only case, aspect, and motion systems already active.
  2. Deliver a short organized presentation and answer follow-up questions, applying the topic-comment order, discourse markers, and repair strategies developed in Chapter 19.
  3. Interpret a written or spoken interview that draws on multiple communication domains from across the volume.
  4. Combine narration, comparison, explanation, and presentation into one connected act of communication.
  5. Reflect on personal strengths and remaining challenges across case, aspect, motion, and register control.
  6. Demonstrate independent, comprehensible B1 Russian across listening, speaking, reading, and writing without support from new grammar.

This is a cumulative assessment chapter. It gathers evidence for Chapters 1 through 19 and introduces no new major productive grammar, case, aspect, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system, in keeping with the Version 5 matrix architecture governing this volume.

Communication Scenario

Ири́на's language exchange group is holding its end-of-season meeting, and every member is asked to bring a short portfolio, a written memory, a short story of an event, and a paragraph of opinion, and to sit for a brief interview about their time in the group. Ири́на has decided to build her portfolio around the neighborhood she described in Chapter 19, adding a childhood memory, the story of the day she decided to move there, and her opinion about what makes a neighborhood good to live in. She expects the interview to move naturally across these topics, the way a real conversation would, rather than to follow a fixed script.

  1. You will read and listen to Ири́на's portfolio and interview as a model for your own.
  2. You will identify how narration, comparison, explanation, and presentation combine in one connected text.
  3. You will assemble your own short portfolio, drawing on memories, events, opinions, and community topics from earlier chapters.
  4. You will deliver your portfolio aloud and answer interview questions, in speech and in writing.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Sustaining intelligibility across a longer turn

No new sound, stress pattern, or consonant contrast is introduced in this chapter. Instead, this section asks learners to sustain the pronunciation control built across the volume, stress placement, vowel reduction, hard-soft contrast, and phrase-level pausing, over a longer connected turn than any single earlier chapter required.

  • Read your portfolio aloud once at a comfortable pace, marking with a light pencil check any word whose stress you are unsure of, then confirm it against the vocabulary and grammar sections of the chapters where it was introduced.
  • Practice the pausing pattern from Chapter 19, a brief downward pitch and pause at the seams between your memory, your event story, and your opinion, so a listener can follow the shift between them.

Recycled Systems Overview

This chapter does not introduce a new grammar system. Instead, it reviews, in one place, the systems this portfolio and interview will call on, so that learners can check their own control before producing connected speech and writing. Each system below was fully developed in an earlier chapter and remains active for independent use.

Narrating a memory (Chapter 16)
habitual past + time clauses + narrative connectors
A childhood or earlier-life memory typically mixes habitual past actions with one or two specific remembered moments, linked by time clauses such as когда́ and connectors such as пото́м and одна́жды.
Когда́ я была́ ма́ленькой, мы ка́ждое ле́то е́здили к ба́бушке в дере́вню.
When I was little, every summer we used to go to my grandmother's in the village.
Telling the story of an event (Chapter 17)
aspect in narration + motion in narration + object/reference cohesion
A single-event story typically uses perfective verbs to move the story forward, imperfective verbs for background, and motion verbs for arrivals, departures, and journeys within the story, all tracked with consistent pronoun and case reference.
В про́шлом году́ я реши́ла перее́хать в но́вый райо́н, и в пе́рвый же день я познако́милась с сосе́дкой.
Last year I decided to move to a new neighborhood, and on the very first day I met my neighbor.
Stating and supporting an opinion (Chapter 18)
comparatives + superlatives + потому́ что + поэ́тому + concession with хотя́
An opinion paragraph typically states a claim, supports it with a comparison or a reason introduced by потому́ что, draws a conclusion with поэ́тому, and acknowledges a limitation or counterpoint with хотя́.
По-мо́ему, э́тот райо́н лу́чше, чем мой ста́рый, потому́ что здесь бо́льше зе́лени, хотя́ он и намно́го гро́мче.
In my opinion, this neighborhood is better than my old one, because there is more greenery here, although it is also much louder.
Organizing and presenting (Chapter 19)
topic-comment order + во-пе́рвых / во-вторы́х / наконе́ц + question and answer repair
A presentation organizes known information before new information, sequences its points with discourse markers, and handles audience questions with a small set of polite repair phrases before returning to the topic.
Начнём с того́, что моё портфо́лио расска́зывает о трёх момента́х мое́й жи́зни здесь.
Let's begin with the fact that my portfolio tells about three moments of my life here.

Model Interview: Портфо́лио Ири́ны

Context: At the end-of-season meeting, the group leader, Оле́г, briefly interviews Ири́на about her portfolio in front of the group.

Оле́г: Ири́на, расскажи́те, пожа́луйста, немно́го о своём портфо́лио. С чего́ вы начина́ете? Irina, please tell us a little about your portfolio. Where do you start?
Ири́на: Начнём с того́, что я вспомина́ю своё де́тство. Когда́ я была́ ма́ленькой, мы ка́ждое ле́то е́здили к ба́бушке в дере́вню, и там всегда́ бы́ло о́чень ти́хо. Let's begin with the fact that I recall my childhood. When I was little, every summer we used to go to my grandmother's in the village, and it was always very quiet there.
Оле́г: Интере́сно. А во-вторы́х? Interesting. And second?
Ири́на: Во-вторы́х, я расска́зываю о том дне, когда́ я реши́ла перее́хать в свой ны́нешний райо́н. В пе́рвый же день я познако́милась с сосе́дкой, и мы бы́стро подружи́лись. Second, I tell about the day when I decided to move to my current neighborhood. On the very first day I met my neighbor, and we quickly became friends.
Оле́г: Пра́вильно ли я понима́ю, что вы дово́льны свои́м вы́бором? Do I understand correctly that you are satisfied with your choice?
Ири́на: Хоро́ший вопро́с. В це́лом да, я оста́лась о́чень дово́льной, потому́ что здесь бо́льше зе́лени и удо́бнее тра́нспорт, хотя́, как я уже́ говори́ла, здесь и гро́мче, чем ра́ньше. Good question. On the whole, yes, I remained very satisfied, because there is more greenery and more convenient transportation here, although, as I already said, it is also louder here than before.
Оле́г: Наконе́ц, каки́е тру́дности и дости́жения вы бы отме́тили за э́тот год? Finally, what difficulties and achievements would you note from this year?
Ири́на: Подыто́живая, са́мой большо́й тру́дностью бы́ло привы́кнуть к но́вому ме́сту, а са́мым больши́м достиже́нием, коне́чно, но́вые друзья́. Спаси́бо за интере́сные вопро́сы. To sum up, the biggest difficulty was getting used to a new place, and the biggest achievement, of course, new friends. Thank you for the interesting questions.

This interview combines memory (Chapter 16), event narration (Chapter 17), supported opinion (Chapter 18), and presentation organization and repair (Chapter 19) in one connected exchange, without introducing any new case, aspect, motion, or pronunciation system.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Sharing a portfolio with a group

Language exchange and study groups in many Russian-speaking communities and among Russian-speaking heritage and diaspora learners commonly close a term or a season with an informal sharing event, though the format, level of formality, and expectations around personal disclosure vary widely by group, generation, and setting.

Language Note — Combining systems is itself a skill

Individually, memory, event, opinion, and presentation language are each manageable. Combining them smoothly, so that a listener can follow the shift from one to the next, is a separate and higher-level skill, and it is the primary skill this capstone chapter assesses, rather than any single new grammar point.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Blending memory and event without a clear boundary

Error: Moving from habitual past memory into a single specific event without a discourse marker or time expression to signal the shift, leaving the listener unsure whether the story is still habitual or has become a one-time event.

Correction: Use a marker such as во-вторы́х or a specific time expression such as в про́шлом году́ to signal the move from habitual background to a single narrated event.

Common Mistake — Stating an opinion without support

Error: Giving an opinion, such as мне нра́вится э́тот райо́н, without a reason or a concession, leaving the portfolio feeling like a list of unconnected statements.

Correction: Follow an opinion with потому́ что and a reason, and where appropriate, acknowledge a limitation with хотя́, as developed in Chapter 18.

Common Mistake — Treating the capstone as a chance to try new grammar

Error: Reaching for an untaught structure, such as a participle or an advanced passive construction, because the portfolio feels like a special occasion.

Correction: Use only case, aspect, motion, and register systems already active from Chapters 1 through 19. A capstone task measures independent control of known systems, not exposure to new ones.

  2. Event, perfective narration with посмотре́ли and вы́брали, moving the story forward in sequence.
  3. Opinion, comparison with бо́льше and concession with хотя́, supported and summarized with в це́лом.
  4. Presentation, во-пе́рвых and во-вторы́х organizing two upcoming points.

  2. Event, о переезде́.
  3. Opinion, мне́ние о вы́боре.
  4. Summary, тру́дности и достиже́ния.

  2. Пе́рвый год был тру́дным, хотя́ второ́й год стал намно́го прия́тнее.
  3. Тра́нспорт стал ходи́ть ча́ще, поэ́тому доро́га до це́нтра занима́ет ме́ньше вре́мени.

Са́мой большо́й тру́дностью бы́ло привы́кнуть к но́вому го́роду, а са́мым больши́м достиже́нием, коне́чно, но́вая рабо́та. 3. Да, в це́лом я оста́лся дово́льным свои́м вы́бором.

Listening — Comprehension

  1. The speaker begins with a memory of her first job, connected to a feeling of difficulty because she understood little.
  2. She presents mistakes and help from her boss, getting to know colleagues, and a new position, introduced by во-пе́рвых, во-вторы́х, and наконе́ц.
  3. She acknowledges that the first months were not simple, introduced by хотя́.
  4. Sample answer, мне бы́ло о́чень тру́дно gives background with the imperfective-adjacent state, while мне предложи́ли но́вую до́лжность moves the story forward with a perfective verb.

Model Interview — Comprehension

  1. Irina describes summers spent at her grandmother's in the village, a habitual childhood memory of quiet.
  2. Irina names getting used to the new place as the difficulty and new friends as the achievement.

Reading

  1. Maxim describes walking to school with his brother every day, a habitual childhood memory.
  2. Maxim mentions starting university, his current studies, and finding friends, in that order, the first two functioning as event and the third as a closing point.
  3. Maxim acknowledges that his first year was difficult, introduced with но in his closing sentence.
  4. Sample answer, пра́вильно ли я понима́ю responds to the moment when a listener asks a follow-up question, checking Maxim's understanding before he answers.

Writing

Sample account: Начнём с того́, что я вспомина́ю свою́ пе́рвую кварти́ру. Когда́ мне бы́ло два́дцать лет, я ка́ждый день е́здила на рабо́ту на авто́бусе, и доро́га занима́ла мно́го вре́мени. Во-пе́рвых, я хочу́ рассказа́ть о дне, когда́ я перее́хала бли́же к це́нтру. Во-вторы́х, по-мо́ему, э́то бы́ло са́мое ва́жное реше́ние за после́дние го́ды, потому́ что тепе́рь доро́га занима́ет намно́го ме́ньше вре́мени, хотя́ кварти́ра ста́ла доро́же. Наконе́ц, я оста́лась о́чень дово́льной э́тим реше́нием. Подыто́живая, в це́лом мой пе́рвый год здесь был непросты́м, но сейча́с я чу́вствую себя́ гора́здо уве́реннее. Пра́вильно ли я понима́ю, что вас интересу́ет и́менно доро́га на рабо́ту? Хоро́ший вопро́с, я расскажу́ подро́бнее. Other portfolios, real or invented, that accurately combine the systems from Chapters 16 through 19 are also correct.
