Коро́ткая презента́ция

Organizing a topic, using discourse markers, and handling questions and answers · Speak and Write Russian: Expanding Independent Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 19 continues and closes Unit IV, and it turns from a private conversation between friends to a more structured, semi-public act of speaking, a short presentation. Ири́на, who has now decided on her opinion about Ле́на's apartment search, has volunteered to give a short talk at her language exchange group about how her neighborhood has changed over the past few years. This chapter does not introduce any new case or motion system. Instead, it develops the organizational tools that turn a string of true sentences into a presentation a listener can actually follow, topic-comment word order, so that a speaker can signal what a sentence is about before saying what is new about it, discourse markers that open, connect, and close a spoken text, and a small set of repair strategies for handling questions from an audience when a word is missed or a question is not fully understood. Learners will draw on the comparatives, reasons, and concessions developed in Chapter 18, and on the narrative language developed earlier in Unit IV, to build a five to six sentence talk with a clear beginning, middle, and end.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Organize a sentence around a topic followed by a comment, so that a listener knows what is being discussed before hearing what is new.
  2. Open a short presentation, connect its parts, and close it using discourse markers appropriate to spoken Russian.
  3. Signal a shift between one point and the next using markers such as во-пе́рвых, во-вторы́х, and наконе́ц.
  4. Deliver a short structured presentation combining narration, comparison, and opinion from earlier chapters.
  5. Ask a listener to repeat or clarify a question, and check one's own understanding before answering.
  6. Answer an audience question briefly and return smoothly to the topic of the presentation.

This chapter develops topic-comment word order, discourse markers, and question and answer repair. It recycles the narrative, comparative, and explanatory systems already active from Chapters 16 through 18, and it introduces no new case, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system, in keeping with the Version 5 matrix architecture governing this volume.

Communication Scenario

Ири́на's language exchange group meets every month, and each time, one member gives a short talk about a topic of their choice, followed by questions from the group. Ири́на has chosen to talk about her own neighborhood, comparing what it was like when she moved there several years ago with what it is like now. She has prepared a short, organized talk, and she expects two or three questions afterward from group members who want more detail or did not catch a particular point.

  1. You will listen to and read a short presentation about a neighborhood, then a brief question and answer exchange that follows it.
  2. You will identify the opening, the connected middle points, and the closing of the presentation.
  3. You will practice topic-comment order and discourse markers while organizing your own short talk.
  4. You will prepare, deliver, and answer questions about a short presentation of your own, in speech and in writing.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Pausing and pitch at the seams of a presentation

A short presentation is easier to follow when the speaker's voice marks the seams between points. Practice a brief downward pitch and pause after each discourse marker, во-пе́рвых, во-вторы́х, and наконе́ц, so that a listener hears each one as the start of a new point rather than as part of the previous sentence.

  • Say Повтори́те, пожа́луйста aloud with a polite, slightly rising intonation on пожа́луйста, distinct from the flatter intonation of a plain statement.
  • Practice reading a short list of two or three sentences aloud, pausing briefly before each new topic-comment sentence, so that the topic word receives a small amount of extra weight before the comment follows.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter develops three connected systems that together let learners organize and deliver a short presentation, and manage audience questions, topic-comment word order, discourse markers for opening, connecting, and closing, and repair strategies for questions and answers. All three systems rely on the case, aspect, comparative, and connector systems already active from earlier chapters, no new case or motion pair is introduced.

Topic-comment word order
Topic (known information) + Comment (new information)
Russian word order is flexible, and speakers use it to signal what a sentence is about before saying what is new. A word or phrase placed early in the sentence is usually understood as the topic, something already known to the listener or introduced by an earlier sentence, while the comment, the new information, tends to come later. This is not a rigid rule, but a pattern that helps organize connected speech, especially in a presentation where each sentence should build clearly on the one before it.
Мой райо́н си́льно измени́лся за после́дние го́ды. Ра́ньше здесь бы́ло ти́хо, а сейча́с здесь мно́го но́вых кафе́.
My neighborhood has changed a great deal in recent years. Before, it was quiet here, and now there are a lot of new cafés here. (мой райо́н opens as the topic of the first sentence, ра́ньше and сейча́с open the next two sentences as time-topics, with the new information following each one)
Opening, connecting, and closing a presentation
Начнём с того́, что... · Во-пе́рвых, ... Во-вторы́х, ... · Наконе́ц, ... Подыто́живая, ...
A short presentation needs signals that a listener can follow without seeing a written outline. Начнём с того́, что opens a talk by stating its first main idea. Во-пе́рвых and во-вторы́х mark a small ordered set of points, letting the listener count along. Наконе́ц marks the final point, and подыто́живая signals that the speaker is about to restate the main idea in one closing sentence.
Начнём с того́, что я живу́ в э́том райо́не уже́ пять лет. Во-пе́рвых, здесь появи́лось мно́го но́вых магази́нов. Во-вторы́х, у нас постро́или но́вый парк. Наконе́ц, тра́нспорт стал ходи́ть гора́здо ча́ще. Подыто́живая, мой райо́н стал намно́го удо́бнее, хотя́ и намно́го гро́мче.
Let's begin with the fact that I have lived in this neighborhood for five years already. First, a lot of new shops have appeared here. Second, they built a new park in our area. Finally, transportation has started running much more often. To sum up, my neighborhood has become much more convenient, although also much louder.
Question and answer repair
Повтори́те, пожа́луйста · Пра́вильно ли я понима́ю, что...? · Хоро́ший вопро́с. ... Возвраща́ясь к те́ме, ...
A speaker taking questions needs short, polite tools for two moments, understanding a question and returning smoothly to the topic afterward. Повтори́те, пожа́луйста asks a listener to repeat a missed question, using the formal Вы-imperative appropriate to a semi-public presentation setting. Пра́вильно ли я понима́ю, что... checks that the speaker has understood the question correctly before answering. Хоро́ший вопро́с buys a moment to think, and возвраща́ясь к те́ме signals a return to the main talk after answering.
— Повтори́те, пожа́луйста, ваш вопро́с. — Пра́вильно ли я понима́ю, что вас интересу́ет тра́нспорт? Хоро́ший вопро́с. ... Возвраща́ясь к те́ме, я хочу́ доба́вить, что...
— Please repeat your question. — Do I understand correctly that you are interested in transportation? Good question. ... Returning to the topic, I want to add that...

Model Presentation: Мой райо́н, вчера́ и сего́дня

Context: Ири́на delivers a short presentation to her language exchange group, followed by two questions from group members, Ната́ша and Серге́й.

Ири́на: Здра́вствуйте, сего́дня я хочу́ рассказа́ть о своём райо́не. Начнём с того́, что я живу́ здесь уже́ пять лет. Hello, today I want to talk about my neighborhood. Let's begin with the fact that I have lived here for five years already.
Ири́на: Во-пе́рвых, ра́ньше здесь бы́ло о́чень ти́хо, а сейча́с появи́лось мно́го но́вых кафе́ и магази́нов. First, before, it was very quiet here, and now a lot of new cafés and shops have appeared.
Ири́на: Во-вторы́х, у нас постро́или но́вый парк, и тепе́рь мно́гие семьи́ гуля́ют там по вечера́м. Second, they built a new park in our area, and now many families walk there in the evenings.
Ири́на: Наконе́ц, тра́нспорт стал ходи́ть гора́здо ча́ще, поэ́тому доро́га до це́нтра занима́ет ме́ньше вре́мени. Finally, transportation has started running much more often, so the trip to the center takes less time.
Ири́на: Подыто́живая, мой райо́н стал намно́го удо́бнее, хотя́ и намно́го гро́мче, чем ра́ньше. Спаси́бо за внима́ние, и я гото́ва отве́тить на вопро́сы. To sum up, my neighborhood has become much more convenient, although also much louder than before. Thank you for your attention, and I am ready to answer questions.
A pause follows, then two listeners raise their hands.
Ната́ша: Прости́те, я не совсе́м поняла́. Вы сказа́ли, что парк постро́или неда́вно? Excuse me, I did not quite understand. Did you say that the park was built recently?
Ири́на: Да, пра́вильно ли я понима́ю, что вас интересу́ет и́менно па́рк? Его́ постро́или в про́шлом году́. Yes, do I understand correctly that you're interested specifically in the park? It was built last year.
Серге́й: Повтори́те, пожа́луйста, что вы сказа́ли о тра́нспорте. Please repeat what you said about transportation.
Ири́на: Хоро́ший вопро́с. Я сказа́ла, что авто́бусы тепе́рь хо́дят гора́здо ча́ще. Возвраща́ясь к те́ме, хочу́ доба́вить, что э́то са́мое большо́е измене́ние для меня́ ли́чно. Good question. I said that buses now run much more often. Returning to the topic, I want to add that this is the biggest change for me personally.

This presentation moves through topic-comment organization, three connected discourse markers (во-пе́рвых, во-вторы́х, наконе́ц), a closing summary with подыто́живая, and two full question-and-answer repair exchanges, without introducing any new case or motion system.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text to speech tool to read the passage below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script
Начнём с того́, что моя́ рабо́та си́льно измени́лась за после́дний год. Во-пе́рвых, я тепе́рь рабо́таю из до́ма три дня в неде́лю, а не ка́ждый день в о́фисе. Во-вторы́х, у нас появи́лась но́вая кома́нда, и мы ста́ли рабо́тать бо́лее гро́мко, зато́ гора́здо быстре́е. Наконе́ц, мой нача́льник стал гора́здо бо́лее откры́т к но́вым иде́ям, чем ра́ньше. Подыто́живая, я счита́ю, что э́ти измене́ния бы́ли поле́зны, хотя́ снача́ла мне бы́ло тру́дно привы́кнуть. Спаси́бо за внима́ние, я гото́в отве́тить на вопро́сы. Пра́вильно ли я понима́ю, что вас интересу́ет и́менно но́вая кома́нда? Хоро́ший вопро́с, я расскажу́ об э́том подро́бнее.
Let's begin with the fact that my job has changed a great deal over the past year. First, I now work from home three days a week, instead of every day in the office. Second, we got a new team, and we started working, let's say, more loudly, but then again much faster. Finally, my boss has become much more open to new ideas than before. To sum up, I think these changes have been useful, although at first it was hard for me to get used to them. Thank you for your attention, I am ready to answer questions. Do I understand correctly that you are interested specifically in the new team? Good question, I will tell you more about that.

  2. What discourse marker introduces the speaker's overall conclusion?
  3. What question does the speaker check with the audience member, and how do they respond to it?
  4. Find one topic-comment sentence in the passage, and identify the topic and the comment.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Questions after a talk

In many Russian-speaking academic, professional, and community settings, a short talk is commonly followed by a brief period of questions, and a speaker is generally expected to answer directly rather than deflect, though the level of formality in this exchange varies by setting, audience, and generation, and some groups favor a more conversational back and forth than others.

Language Note — Discourse markers are organizational, not decorative

Во-пе́рвых, во-вторы́х, and наконе́ц do more than add formality, they tell a listener how many points to expect and where the talk currently stands. Overusing them in casual conversation can sound stiff, but leaving them out of a structured presentation can make a talk hard to follow, since Russian speech relies more on such explicit markers than on visual outlines or slides.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using во-пе́рвых without во-вторы́х

Error: Starting a list with во-пе́рвых and then continuing with a plain sentence, with no во-вторы́х to follow.

Correction: Во-пе́рвых sets up an expectation of at least one more numbered point: во-пе́рвых, ... во-вторы́х, .... If only one point is being made, leave out во-пе́рвых entirely rather than opening a list that is never completed.

Common Mistake — Answering a question without returning to the topic

Error: Answering an audience question and then stopping abruptly, leaving the listener unsure whether the presentation is continuing.

Correction: After answering, signal a return to the main talk with a phrase such as возвраща́ясь к те́ме, so that listeners know whether the speaker is continuing the presentation or has finished.

Common Mistake — Confusing повтори́те with я не совсе́м по́нял

Error: Using повтори́те, пожа́луйста when the real problem is not hearing but understanding the meaning of a question.

Correction: Use повтори́те, пожа́луйста only when a question was not heard clearly. If the question was heard but not understood, use я не совсе́м по́нял or я не совсе́м поняла́ instead, choosing the form that matches the speaker's own gender.

Chapter Review

Topic-comment order

Placing known information before new information helps a listener follow a sentence within a connected presentation.

Opening and connecting markers

Начнём с того́ что, во-пе́рвых, and во-вторы́х organize a talk into a countable, followable sequence of points.

Closing markers

Наконе́ц introduces the final point, and подыто́живая signals a one-sentence summary of the whole talk.

Question and answer repair

Повтори́те пожа́луйста, я не совсе́м по́нял, and пра́вильно ли я понима́ю let a speaker handle audience questions politely and accurately.

Answer Key

Grammar and Sentence Patterns — Identify the Pattern

  1. Discourse marker, начнём с того́ что, opening the presentation by stating that the neighborhood was once small.
  2. Repair strategy, пра́вильно ли я понима́ю, checking understanding of the audience's question about the new park.
  3. Discourse marker, во-вторы́х, marking the second point in an ordered list about new restaurants.
  4. Topic-comment order, мой дом opens as the topic, and the new information about its location and the park's recent construction follows.

Listening — Comprehension

  1. The speaker describes working from home more often, a new team, and a more open boss, in that order.
  2. Подыто́живая introduces the speaker's overall conclusion that the changes were useful.
  3. The speaker checks whether the audience member is asking specifically about the new team, and responds with хоро́ший вопро́с before continuing.
  4. Sample answer, во-вторы́х, у нас появи́лась но́вая кома́нда opens with the topic во-вторы́х and comments with the new information about the team.
