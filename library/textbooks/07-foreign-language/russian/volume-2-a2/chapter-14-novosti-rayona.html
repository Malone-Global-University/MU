Но́вости райо́на

Reading and discussing local events, announcements, and public information with impersonal constructions and recognition of passive-like and public style · Speak and Write Russian: Expanding Independent Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 14 continues Unit III, and it moves from the personal, supportive register of Chapter 13 into the more public register of local announcements and community life. Ири́на, feeling somewhat better after her conversation with Серге́й, stops to read a notice board near her building and later attends a short meeting of residents about a planned repair to the courtyard. This chapter does not introduce a new case, a new aspect contrast, or a new productive verb system. Instead, it develops impersonal constructions that describe actions without naming who performs them, such as говоря́т, что and изве́стно, что, and it develops learners' recognition of passive-like expressions and public style, so that residents' notices, community announcements, and short news items become easier to read and discuss, building on the case governance and support language developed in Chapter 13.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Report general or unattributed information using impersonal constructions such as говоря́т, что, изве́стно, что, and ста́ло изве́стно, что.
  2. Recognize short passive-like expressions in public notices, such as forms in -ся used to describe scheduled or ongoing actions, without producing them independently.
  3. Recognize the general features of public and announcement style, and distinguish it from everyday conversational Russian.
  4. Read and interpret a community notice, a short announcement, and a brief news item.
  5. Discuss local events and public information at a short residents' meeting, using only active systems.
  6. Write a short community announcement or notice using impersonal constructions and active case and agreement systems.

This chapter develops impersonal constructions using systems already active from earlier chapters, and it introduces passive-like expressions and public style for recognition only, in keeping with the Participles / Gerunds / Verbal Adjectives Matrix, which delays independent production of these forms to a later volume. No new case, aspect, or motion system is introduced.

Communication Scenario

A few days after her message to a stressed friend, Ири́на passes the notice board near her building and reads several short announcements, including one about a planned repair to the courtyard. That evening, she attends a short meeting of residents where a neighbor explains what has been decided and what is expected of everyone in the building.

  1. You will read and listen to notices, announcements, and a short news item about local and community matters.
  2. You will use impersonal constructions to report general information without naming a specific source.
  3. You will recognize, without producing, passive-like expressions and public style features common to notices and announcements.
  4. You will speak and write about a local event or announcement, using only active systems.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Stress in дворе́, жильцо́в, and сообщи́ть

The noun двор has mobile stress: the nominative singular is stressed on the single syllable, but the oblique cases move the stress to the ending, as in prepositional во дворе́. The noun жиле́ц also has mobile stress, with the vowel dropping in most oblique forms, as in genitive plural жильцо́в.

  • Practice во дворе́, о ремо́нте, на собра́нии aloud, and confirm the stress of each prepositional phrase individually rather than guessing from the nominative form.
  • Confirm that сообщи́ть and объяви́ть carry stress on the final syllable in the infinitive, and check each conjugated form separately, since prefixed verbs of this type do not always keep the same stress pattern throughout.

Grammar: Impersonal Constructions and Public Style

This section develops impersonal constructions that report information without naming a specific source, building on the impersonal patterns already active from earlier chapters. It also introduces recognition, not production, of passive-like -ся forms and other features of public and announcement style, so that notices and short news items become easier to read. No new case, aspect, or motion system is introduced.

Impersonal reporting: говоря́т, что and изве́стно, что

Grammar — Impersonal reporting
говоря́т / изве́стно / ста́ло изве́стно + что + clause
These patterns report information generally, without naming who is speaking or who knows the information. Говоря́т uses the third-person plural verb with no expressed subject, a pattern already familiar from earlier impersonal work. Изве́стно is a short-form neuter predicate that functions like the impersonal predicates practiced in earlier chapters, such as интере́сно or жаль.
Говоря́т, что во дворе́ ско́ро бу́дет ремо́нт.
They say that there will soon be a repair in the courtyard. (говоря́т + что clause, no named subject)
  • Изве́стно, что собра́ние начнётся в семь часо́в ве́чера. It is known that the meeting will start at seven in the evening. изве́стно + что clause
  • Ста́ло изве́стно, что но́вое расписа́ние объя́вят на сле́дующей неде́ле. It has become known that the new schedule will be announced next week. ста́ло изве́стно + что clause

    • Recognizing passive-like expressions in public style

    Grammar — Recognition only
    noun + verb in -ся, describing an action without naming who performs it
    Public notices, schedules, and announcements often describe an action happening to something, without stating who carries it out, using a reflexive-looking verb form ending in -ся. This chapter asks learners to recognize this pattern in reading, not to produce it independently. A full account of passive and impersonal -ся constructions belongs to a later volume.
    Ремо́нт двора́ начина́ется в понеде́льник.
    The repair of the courtyard begins on Monday. (recognize начина́ется as describing the repair beginning, without naming who is starting it)
  • Собра́ние жильцо́в прово́дится ка́ждый ме́сяц. A residents' meeting is held every month. recognize прово́дится; no independent production required
  • Информа́ция об измене́ниях публику́ется на доске́ объявле́ний. Information about the changes is published on the notice board. recognize публику́ется; no independent production required
    • Language Focus — Public style sounds different from conversation

    Notices, schedules, and short news items tend to use complete sentences, formal vocabulary, and impersonal or passive-like structures rather than the direct, personal style of everyday conversation. Learners are not expected to write in this style independently in this chapter. Instead, this chapter builds the reading skill of recognizing public style and pulling out the practical information it contains, such as what is happening, when, and who it concerns.

    Model Dialogue: На собра́нии жильцо́в

    Context: Ири́на attends a short meeting of residents in her building, where a neighbor, Пётр Никола́евич, explains the planned repair to the courtyard.

    Пётр Н.: Добрый ве́чер всем! Спаси́бо, что пришли́. Говоря́т, что ремо́нт двора́ начнётся уже́ на сле́дующей неде́ле. Good evening, everyone! Thank you for coming. They say that the courtyard repair will begin as early as next week.
    Ири́на: Изве́стно, ско́лько вре́мени займёт ремо́нт? Is it known how much time the repair will take?
    Пётр Н.: Ду́маю, приме́рно две неде́ли. Но то́чное расписа́ние объя́вят по́зже. I think about two weeks. But the exact schedule will be announced later.
    Сосе́дка: А э́то каса́ется всех жильцо́в и́ли то́лько тех, кто живёт на пе́рвом этаже́? And does this concern all residents, or only those who live on the first floor?
    Пётр Н.: Это каса́ется всех, потому́ что весь двор бу́дет закры́т на вре́мя рабо́т. Информа́ция об измене́ниях бу́дет публикова́ться на доске́ объявле́ний. This concerns everyone, because the whole courtyard will be closed during the work. Information about the changes will be published on the notice board.
    Ири́на: Поня́тно, спаси́бо. Я бу́ду проверя́ть доску́ объявле́ний ка́ждую неде́лю. I understand, thank you. I will check the notice board every week.
    Пётр Н.: Отли́чно. Е́сли у кого́-то есть вопро́сы, пожа́луйста, пиши́те мне и́ли приходи́те на сле́дующее собра́ние. Excellent. If anyone has questions, please write to me or come to the next meeting.

    This exchange integrates impersonal reporting with говоря́т and изве́стно, and it includes two passive-like forms, бу́дет закры́т and бу́дет публикова́ться, for recognition only, while keeping all learner speaking turns within active systems.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Community notices and residents' meetings

    The way residents receive and discuss local news, such as through a printed notice board, an online group, or a residents' meeting, varies widely across buildings, neighborhoods, and communities in Russian-speaking countries, so learners should avoid assuming that any single format represents every community's practice.

    Language Note — Public style is a reading skill first

    The impersonal expressions in this chapter, such as говоря́т and изве́стно, are appropriate for learners to use in their own speaking and writing at this level. The passive-like expressions in notices and announcements, however, are introduced here mainly so that learners can read and understand this type of public text. Independent production of these forms belongs to a later stage of the course.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Common Mistake — Naming a subject unnecessarily with говоря́т

    Error: Writing они́ говоря́т, что when no specific group of people is meant, by analogy with an English subject.

    Correction: The impersonal pattern normally omits the pronoun when no specific speaker is meant: говоря́т, что. Adding они́ suggests a specific, known group of people, which changes the meaning.

    Common Mistake — Trying to produce passive-like -ся forms independently

    Error: Attempting to build new passive-like sentences, such as inventing a form like ремо́нт де́лается людьми́, without a verified model.

    Correction: This chapter asks learners only to recognize passive-like expressions in reading, such as ремо́нт двора́ начина́ется. Rephrase your own sentences using active systems, such as a person plus an active verb, for example рабо́тники начина́ют ремо́нт, or use говоря́т or изве́стно to report the information impersonally instead.

    Common Mistake — Treating изве́стно as a personal opinion word

    Error: Using изве́стно to mean “I think” or “I believe.”

    Correction: Изве́стно reports something as generally known or established, not as a personal opinion. For a personal opinion, use expressions such as мне ка́жется, reviewed in Chapter 13.

    4. Sample answer: говоря́т is an impersonal expression, and бу́дет опублико́вано is a passive-like expression, recognized here for reading purposes only.

    Model Dialogue — Comprehension

    1. Пётр Никола́евич announces that the courtyard repair will begin next week.
    2. The repair concerns all residents, because the whole courtyard will be closed during the work.

    Reading

    1. The repair will begin next week and will last about two weeks, according to the notice.
    2. Residents will enter the building only through the main door during the repair.
    3. The next residents' meeting will take place next Tuesday at seven in the evening, in the building's common room.
    4. Sample answers: ста́ло изве́стно is an impersonal expression, and осуществля́ется or бу́дет обсужда́ться is a passive-like expression, recognized here for reading purposes only.

    Writing

    Sample account: Уважа́емые жильцы́! Сообща́ем вам, что говоря́т о ремо́нте двора́, кото́рый начнётся на сле́дующей неде́ле. Изве́стно, что рабо́та бу́дет продолжа́ться приме́рно две неде́ли, и в э́то вре́мя вход в дом бу́дет то́лько че́рез гла́вную дверь. Э́то каса́ется всех жильцо́в до́ма. Ста́ло изве́стно, что сле́дующее собра́ние состои́тся в сле́дующий вто́рник ве́чером. Е́сли у вас есть вопро́сы, пожа́луйста, пиши́те председа́телю собра́ния и́ли приходи́те на собра́ние ли́чно. Спаси́бо за внима́ние и понима́ние. Other accounts drawing accurately on this chapter's systems are also correct.

    Looking Ahead

    Что вы уже уме́ете — What you can do now

    You can report general local information using impersonal constructions such as говоря́т, что and изве́стно, что, you can recognize passive-like expressions and public style features in notices and announcements without producing them independently, and you can discuss and write about a local event or change, combining impersonal reporting with the active case, agreement, and verb systems already under your control.
