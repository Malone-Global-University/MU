Прое́кт 2: Реши́ть зада́чу

Unit II cumulative assessment: solving a travel, service, or communication problem across speech and writing · Speak and Write Russian: Expanding Independent Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Combine location, destination, and source expressions with quantities, aspect contrasts, and motion verbs to describe and resolve a practical problem.
  2. Use imperfective and perfective verbs accurately to distinguish plans, processes, and completed results, as developed in Chapter 6.
  3. Use unprefixed and prefixed motion verbs accurately to describe a trip, an interruption, and its resolution, as developed in Chapters 7 and 8.
  4. Name a recipient in the dative case after communication verbs, and report, at an introductory level, what someone said or asked, as developed in Chapter 9.
  5. Interpret spoken and written texts that combine these systems, and produce a connected spoken and written account of a solved problem.
  6. Demonstrate independent, integrated control of the case, aspect, motion, and communication systems activated in Chapters 1 through 9, without introducing any new productive system.

This is a cumulative assessment chapter. It introduces no new major productive grammar, case, aspect, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system. It gathers formal evidence for the systems activated across Chapters 1 through 9, in accordance with the Version 5 Assessment Matrix and the Unit II assessment rhythm set out in the governing volume scope.

Communication Scenario

Серге́й's stay at Ири́на's apartment is ending, and a new problem has appeared: the return train ticket he bought has the wrong date, and the ticket office is not answering the phone. Serge мust figure out where to go, how many days are involved, what he has already done about it, and who has said what about the situation, then explain the whole problem, and its solution, to Ири́на, to a station employee, and finally in a short written account.

  1. You will read and listen to texts about the ticket problem, tying together locations, quantities, planned and completed actions, and reported messages.
  2. You will answer integrated comprehension questions that require tracking who did what, where, when, and why across the whole unit.
  3. You will speak and write about a comparable problem of your own, an authentic exchange, or your own resolution of Серге́й's situation, using the systems activated in Chapters 1 through 9.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Stress across the recycled verb set

This chapter reviews stress rather than introducing new stress patterns. Before beginning the assessment tasks, read the following recycled forms aloud, checking each one against the stress you recorded in Chapters 6 through 9: пое́ду, пое́хал, пое́хала, прие́хал, уе́хал, вы́ехал, позвони́л, позвони́ла, сказа́л, сказа́ла, спроси́л, спроси́ла.

  • Notice that вы́ехал keeps stress on the prefix, unlike most of the other prefixed forms in this list.
  • Notice that сказа́ть and спроси́ть behave differently from each other in their first-person singular forms; confirm each one individually rather than assuming a shared pattern.
  • If any of these forms feel uncertain, return to the relevant chapter's Pronunciation Focus before continuing.

Grammar Review: Chapters 1–9

This section reviews, rather than introduces, the systems being assessed. Each Building Block below restates a pattern from an earlier chapter so that you can check your control of it before the assessment tasks.

Location, destination, source, and quantity

Review — Chapters 2 and 4
где? (prepositional) · куда́? (accusative) · отку́да? (genitive) · ско́лько? (genitive)
A single situation often requires all four questions in sequence: where something is, where someone is going, where someone is coming from, and how many of something are involved.
Серге́й купи́л два биле́та на по́езд из Москвы́ в Смоле́нск.
Sergei bought two tickets for the train from Moscow to Smolensk. (genitive quantity, genitive source, accusative destination)

Aspect, plans, and results

Review — Chapter 6
imperfective (process/plan) versus perfective (completed result)
Choose the imperfective for planning, habitual action, or an action still in progress, and the perfective for a single completed event with a clear result.
Он покупа́л биле́т це́лый час, но в конце́ концо́в купи́л не тот биле́т.
He was buying a ticket for a whole hour, but in the end bought the wrong ticket. (imperfective process, perfective result)

Motion, unprefixed and prefixed

Review — Chapters 7 and 8
unprefixed motion (habitual/one-direction) versus prefixed motion (arrival, departure, entry, exit, transfer)
Use unprefixed verbs like е́здить for routine or repeated trips, and prefixed verbs like прие́хать, уе́хать, вы́ехать, and перее́хать for a single trip's stages.
Он обы́чно е́здит на рабо́ту на авто́бусе, но в тот день он вы́ехал сли́шком по́здно.
He usually goes to work by bus, but that day he left too late. (unprefixed habitual motion, prefixed single-event motion)

Dative recipients and reported speech

Review — Chapter 9
verb + кому́ (dative, no preposition) · сказа́л/спроси́л, что/ли...
Communication verbs take a dative recipient without a preposition, and reported speech keeps the original tense of the statement or question being reported.
Сотру́дник вокза́ла отве́тил Серге́ю, что ста́рый биле́т уже́ нельзя́ поменя́ть.
The station employee answered Sergei that the old ticket could no longer be exchanged. (dative recipient, reported statement)
Language Focus — Integration is the point of this chapter

None of the four systems above is new. What this chapter checks is whether you can combine them accurately in a single connected account, the way a fluent description of a real problem naturally requires.

Пра́ктика — Review practice: Complete each sentence with the form that best fits the cumulative systems from Chapters 1 through 9.

  1. Серге́й купи́л ___ (two tickets, genitive quantity) на по́езд в Смоле́нск.
  2. Обы́чно он ___ (travels, unprefixed habitual) на вокза́л на метро́.
  3. В тот день по́езд ___ (departed, perfective prefixed) на час ра́ньше, чем он ду́мал.
  4. Сотру́дник вокза́ла ___ (told, perfective past) Серге́ю, что биле́т на непра́вильную да́ту.
  5. Серге́й спроси́л, ___ (whether it is possible) поменя́ть биле́т сего́дня.

Model Exchange: На вокза́ле

Context: Серге́й has gone to the train station to sort out his ticket problem. He speaks with a station employee.

Сотру́дник: Здра́вствуйте! Чем могу́ помо́чь? Hello! How can I help?
Серге́й: Здра́вствуйте. У меня́ пробле́ма с биле́том. Я купи́л два биле́та в Смоле́нск, но, ка́жется, на непра́вильную да́ту. Hello. I have a problem with a ticket. I bought two tickets to Smolensk, but, it seems, for the wrong date.
Сотру́дник: Мину́точку. Когда́ вы хоте́ли вы́ехать? One moment. When did you want to leave?
Серге́й: Я хоте́л вы́ехать за́втра у́тром, но в биле́те стои́т сего́дняшняя да́та. I wanted to leave tomorrow morning, but the ticket shows today's date.
Сотру́дник: Поня́тно. Сейча́с посмотрю́... Да, вы правы. Я могу́ поменя́ть биле́ты на за́втра, но нужно доплати́ть немно́го. I see. Let me look... Yes, you're right. I can change the tickets to tomorrow, but you need to pay a little extra.
Серге́й: Хорошо́, я согла́сен. Кста́ти, моя́ сестра́ звони́ла сюда́ у́тром и спра́шивала об э́том. Она́ вам не дозвони́лась? All right, I agree. By the way, my sister called here in the morning and was asking about this. Didn't she get through to you?
Сотру́дник: Нет, никто́ не звони́л. Но тепе́рь всё хорошо́: вот но́вые биле́ты. Переда́йте сестре́, что всё реши́ли. No, no one called. But now everything is fine: here are the new tickets. Tell your sister that everything has been resolved.
Серге́й: Спаси́бо большо́е! Обяза́тельно ей скажу́. Thank you very much! I'll definitely tell her.

This exchange integrates location and destination (Chapter 2), quantity (Chapter 4), aspect and result (Chapter 6), prefixed motion (Chapter 8), and dative recipients with reported speech (Chapter 9), the four principal systems assessed in this chapter.

Listening script
Приве́т, э́то Ири́на. Серге́й позвони́л мне с вокза́ла и сказа́л, что всё в поря́дке: он поменя́л биле́ты, и тепе́рь они́ с да́той за́втрашнего дня. He asked whether I would be able to drive him to the station in the morning, because the train leaves at eight o'clock. I answered that of course I could, and that we would leave the house at seven.   2. What does Серге́й ask Ирина to do, and when?

Writing Assessment

Complete the following linked writing tasks in order. This assessment asks you to describe a problem, its resolution, and the communication surrounding it, integrating the systems from Chapters 1 through 9.

Зада́ние 1 — Outline: Before writing, list the original plan, what went wrong, where the problem happened, how many of something were involved, what was done to solve it, and who said or asked what.

Outline prompts
1. Каки́е бы́ли пла́ны снача́ла? 2. Что пошло́ не так, и где? 3. Ско́лько (биле́тов, дней, часо́в) бы́ло свя́зано с пробле́мой? 4. Что сде́лали, что́бы реши́ть пробле́му? 5. Кто кому́ позвони́л, написа́л и́ли сказа́л, и что и́менно?
1. What were the original plans? 2. What went wrong, and where? 3. How many (tickets, days, hours) were involved in the problem? 4. What was done to solve the problem? 5. Who called, wrote, or told whom, and what exactly?

  2. Complete your outline. Choose a problem you can describe clearly from beginning to end.
  3. Draft your account in order. Describe the plan, the problem, the resolution, and the communication involved.

Спи́сок прове́рки — Final editing checklist:

  • The account clearly identifies the original plan, the problem, and the resolution.
  • At least one location or destination phrase and one genitive quantity expression appear correctly.
  • At least one imperfective/perfective contrast is used accurately to distinguish plan, process, and result.
  • At least one unprefixed or prefixed motion verb is used accurately.
  • At least one dative recipient and one instance of reported speech are included and correctly formed.
  • Cyrillic, stress where genuinely needed, and `ё` are used correctly throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Solving problems at a service window

Interactions at ticket offices, service counters, and similar windows generally move efficiently once the problem is stated clearly, and a polite, direct explanation of the issue is usually more useful than an extended apology. Conventions for who initiates the conversation, how much small talk precedes the request, and how flexible staff can be about fees or exchanges vary by institution, region, and individual, so learners should observe and ask rather than assume one pattern applies everywhere.

Language Note — Combining systems does not change any single rule

When several systems appear in the same sentence, each one still follows its own familiar rule: the dative recipient of a communication verb does not change because a motion verb also appears nearby, and a genitive quantity does not change because the sentence also reports speech. Check each system independently, then check that the sentence as a whole makes sense.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Losing aspect control inside a longer account

Error: Writing он покупа́л биле́ты и реша́л пробле́му to describe a completed sequence of single events.

Correction: Use the perfective for the completed steps: он купи́л биле́ты и реши́л пробле́му. Reserve the imperfective for genuine process, habit, or background description, as reviewed in this chapter's Grammar Review.

Common Mistake — Confusing a prefixed motion verb's direction

Error: Writing по́езd прие́хал в во́семь часо́в to mean the train left at eight o'clock.

Correction: Use отпра́вился or вы́ехал for departure and прие́хал for arrival: по́езд отпра́вился в во́семь часо́в. Confirm each prefix's direction rather than assuming from context.

Common Mistake — Dropping the dative recipient in a busy sentence

Error: Writing сотру́дник сказа́л, что... without stating whom the employee told, when the recipient matters for clarity.

Correction: Include the dative recipient when it clarifies who received the information: сотру́дник сказа́л Серге́ю, что... Do not let a longer, multi-system sentence cause you to drop a required recipient.

Listening — Comprehension

Model Exchange — Comprehension

  1. The tickets had the wrong date; the employee changed them to tomorrow's date for a small additional payment.
  2. The employee asks Серге́й to tell his sister that everything has been resolved.

Reading

  1. Тама́ра heard from Ирина that Серге́й had a problem with the train tickets, but that everything is now fine.
  2. Тама́ра reports that Ирина asked whether she is also free; Тама́ра answers that she cannot go, because she has three meetings on Saturday.
  3. Тама́ра can't go to Смоленск this weekend because of her Saturday meetings.
  4. Sample answers: три встре́чи is a genitive quantity expression, and уе́дет is a prefixed motion verb describing a single planned departure.

Writing

Sample account: Снача́ла Серге́й хоте́л пое́хать в Смоле́нск оди́н, но купи́л два биле́та, потому́ что реши́л пригласи́ть сестру́. К сожале́нию, в биле́тах была́ непра́вильная да́та. Серге́й пое́хал на вокза́л и объясни́л пробле́му сотру́днику. Сотру́дник отве́тил ему́, что мо́жет поменя́ть биле́ты, и спроси́л, ско́лько мест ну́жно. Серге́й сказа́л, что ну́жно два места́ на за́втра. В конце́ концо́в он купи́л но́вые биле́ты и позвони́л Ири́не, что́бы сказа́ть, что всё реши́ли. Other accounts drawing accurately on this chapter's integrated systems are also correct.
