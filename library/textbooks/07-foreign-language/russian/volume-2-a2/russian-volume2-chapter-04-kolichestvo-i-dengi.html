Коли́чество и де́ньги

Quantities and money · Speak and Write Russian: Expanding Independent Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Count people, things, and money accurately, choosing the correct noun form after each cardinal number.
  2. Apply genitive singular after 2, 3, and 4, and genitive plural after 5 and above, including in compound numbers.
  3. Use ordinal numbers to talk about dates, floors, and sequence.
  4. State and ask about prices, ages, and simple measurements.
  5. Read and interpret short texts involving numbers, prices, and dates.
  6. Write a short budget note or shopping plan that uses numeral government correctly.

This chapter continues to use only the cases already active in your Russian, the nominative, genitive, prepositional, and accusative. It does not introduce a new case. Instead, it develops the genitive more fully by connecting it to numbers, and it adds ordinal numbers as a new but closely related system.

Communication Scenario

Тама́ра is planning a small budget for the month and needs to buy a few things before Серге́й's birthday, which falls on a specific date this month. At the market and later at home, she and Серге́й talk about prices, ages, dates, and how many of each item she needs. As they talk, Тама́ра practices choosing the correct noun form after each number, whether she is counting friends, books, or rubles.

  1. You will ask and answer questions about prices, quantities, and dates.
  2. You will talk about ages and important dates using ordinal numbers.
  3. You will read and listen to short accounts involving numbers, money, and planning.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Numbers spoken quickly in context

In natural speech, Russians say prices, dates, and quantities quickly, often blending the number and the noun into one rhythmic unit. Learning to hear and produce this rhythm matters more than pausing carefully between each word.

  • Practice saying два рубля́, пять рубле́й, and два́дцать оди́н рубль at a natural conversational pace, without pausing between the number and the noun.
  • Contrast the stress pattern of рубль, singular, with рубле́й, genitive plural, listening for how the stress moves.
  • Read the Model Dialogue below aloud once, paying attention to every price and date you encounter.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This section develops the genitive case you already use for possession and absence, extending it into numeral government, and adds ordinal numbers as a closely related new system.

Numeral government: 2-4 and 5+

Pattern
2, 3, 4 + genitive singular  |  5 and above + genitive plural
Numbers ending in 2, 3, or 4, except those ending in 12, 13, or 14, take a noun in the genitive singular. Numbers ending in 5 through 9, 0, and 11 through 14, take a noun in the genitive plural.
У меня́ есть два рубля́, а у Серге́я пять рубле́й.
I have two rubles, and Sergei has five rubles.
  • Э́та кни́га сто́ит три́ста рубле́й.This book costs three hundred rubles.рубле́й, genitive plural after triста
  • У неё три сестры́, а у меня́ то́лько одна́.She has three sisters, and I have only one.сестры́, genitive singular after три

Оди́н agrees with the noun in gender and number and does not require the genitive. Compound numbers ending in оди́н, such as два́дцать оди́н, are followed by the nominative singular, not the genitive.

Ordinal numbers

Pattern
пе́рвый, второ́й, тре́тий, четвёртый, пя́тый ... + noun
Ordinal numbers agree with the noun in gender, number, and case, just like adjectives. Only the last element of a compound ordinal changes form, while the earlier elements remain in a fixed cardinal form.
Пра́здник у Серге́я деся́того ма́я.
Sergei's celebration is on the tenth of May.
  • Мы живём на тре́тьем этаже́.We live on the third floor.тре́тьем, prepositional after на
  • Э́то моя́ пе́рвая пое́здка в э́тот го́род.This is my first trip to this city.пе́рвая, feminine nominative agreeing with пое́здка

Age and price expressions

Pattern
[Da­tive person] + [number] + год / го́да / лет
Age uses the same numeral government rule as any other quantity noun, choosing among год, го́да, and лет according to the number. This chapter treats the dative person in fixed age formulas as formulaic, and does not open the full dative case for new production.
Тама́ре два́дцать три го́да, а Серге́ю два́дцать во́семь лет.
Tamara is twenty three years old, and Sergei is twenty eight years old.
  • Ско́лько сто́ит э́тот телефо́н?How much does this telephone cost?сто́ит, followed by a price in the accusative
  • Он сто́ит две ты́сячи рубле́й.It costs two thousand rubles.ты́сячи, genitive singular after два
Language Focus — Age formulas remain formulaic

Expressions such as мне два́дцать лет or Серге́ю два́дцать во́семь лет use the dative case of the person, но this chapter does not activate the dative case for independent production. Learn these age formulas as fixed patterns for now, and expect the dative to open more fully in a later chapter.

Model Dialogue: Коли́чество и де́ньги

Context: Тама́ра and Серге́й are at the market, checking prices before Серге́й's birthday, and talking about the budget for the month.

Тама́ра: Ско́лько сто́ит э́та кни́га? Я хочу́ купи́ть две. How much does this book cost? I want to buy two.
Продаве́ц: Одна́ кни́га сто́ит три́ста рубле́й, а две кни́ги сто́ят шестьсо́т рубле́й. One book costs three hundred rubles, and two books cost six hundred rubles.
Тама́ра: Хорошо́, я возьму́ две. А когда́ у тебя́ пра́здник, Серге́й? Деся́того ма́я? Good, I will take two. And when is your celebration, Sergei? On the tenth of May?
Серге́й: Да, деся́того. Мне бу́дет два́дцать во́семь лет. А тебе́ ско́лько лет, Тама́ра? Yes, on the tenth. I will be twenty eight years old. And how old are you, Tamara?
Тама́ра: Мне два́дцать три го́да. У меня́ ма́ленький бюдже́т, но я скопи́ла де́ньги на два хоро́ших пода́рка. I am twenty three years old. I have a small budget, but I have saved money for two nice gifts.
Серге́й: Не тра́ть сли́шком мно́го де́нег. Ско́лько ты уже́ потра́тила в э́том ме́сяце? Do not spend too much money. How much have you already spent this month?
Тама́ра: Я потра́тила о́коло пяти́ ты́сяч рубле́й, но у меня́ ещё есть де́ньги на э́ту неде́лю. I have spent around five thousand rubles, but I still have money for this week.

Notice how the dialogue keeps every number carefully matched to the correct noun form, whether the noun is a book, a ruble, or a year.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Prices, markets, and bargaining

In many Russian-speaking cities, both fixed-price stores and open markets are common, and price expectations can differ between the two settings. Bargaining is more common in some markets than in fixed-price stores, and practices vary considerably by city, region, and generation, so no single shopping custom applies to every Russian-speaking community.

Language Note — Currency and rounding in everyday speech

In everyday conversation, speakers often round prices and use approximate expressions such as о́коло, meaning approximately, followed by the genitive. Learn о́коло as a useful formulaic pattern for now, and expect fuller approximation language to develop in later chapters.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using genitive plural after 2, 3, or 4

Error: Writing два рубле́й when the number two requires the genitive singular, not the genitive plural.

Correction: Use the genitive singular after 2, 3, and 4: два рубля́. Reserve the genitive plural, рубле́й, for numbers 5 and above.

Common Mistake — Forgetting that оди́н agrees like an adjective

Error: Writing одна́ рубль or оди́н кни́га, ignoring the gender of the noun that follows.

Correction: Match оди́н to the noun's gender: оди́н рубль, одна́ кни́га, одно́ ме́сто.

Common Mistake — Treating ordinal numbers as unchanging

Error: Writing в тре́тий этаже́, leaving the ordinal number in the nominative instead of agreeing with the case the sentence requires.

Correction: Decline the ordinal number like an adjective: на тре́тьем этаже́. Ordinal numbers must agree in gender, number, and case, just as any other adjective does.
