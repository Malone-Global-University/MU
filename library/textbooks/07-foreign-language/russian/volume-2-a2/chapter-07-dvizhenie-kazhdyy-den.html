7

Движе́ние ка́ждый день

Everyday motion, unprefixed motion verbs in fuller use, and talking about routes and frequency · Speak and Write Russian: Expanding Independent Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Use идти́/ходи́ть and е́хать/е́здить accurately, choosing the unidirectional or multidirectional form according to meaning.
  2. Describe a daily or weekly commute, naming the means of transport, the starting point, and the destination.
  3. Talk about how often you go somewhere, using frequency expressions such as ка́ждый день, обы́чно, иногда́, and ре́дко.
  4. Describe a route using recycled prepositions of location and destination, including в/на with the accusative and prepositional, and из/с with the genitive.
  5. Distinguish a single trip happening right now from a habitual or repeated pattern of movement.
  6. Combine motion language with the aspect, future, and result patterns developed in Chapter 6.

This chapter consolidates unprefixed motion verbs that were first introduced receptively in Volume I. It does not introduce prefixed motion verbs, which are reserved for Chapter 8, and it does not introduce a new case.

Communication Scenario

Тама́ра's garden project is finished, and now she is talking with a new coworker, Серге́й, about their daily routines. Серге́й has just started a job across town, and he wants advice on the best way to get there. Their conversation moves between what someone does every day, what someone is doing right now, and how often someone makes a particular trip, so it gives natural, repeated practice with motion verbs in context.

  1. You will read and listen to a conversation about daily commuting and transport choices.
  2. You will answer questions about routes, frequency, and means of transport.
  3. You will write and speak about your own typical week of movement, choosing the correct motion verb for each situation.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Stress in the motion verb paradigms

The verb идти́ keeps stress on the ending throughout its conjugation, so иду́, идёшь, идёт. The verb ходи́ть shifts stress off the ending after the first person singular, so хожу́, but then хо́дишь, хо́дит. Listen for this shift, since it is easy to miss when reading quickly.

  • Practice иду́, идёшь, идёт, идём, идёте, иду́т aloud, keeping the stress on the ending in every form.
  • Practice хожу́, хо́дишь, хо́дит, хо́дим, хо́дите, хо́дят aloud, noticing the stress move after the first form.
  • Do not assume that е́хать and е́здить follow the same pattern. Both keep stem stress throughout, е́ду, е́дешь and е́зжу, е́здишь, so listen closely for the consonant change in е́зжу.

Grammar: Motion, Route, and Frequency

Russian motion verbs are a meaning system built on directionality, not simply a vocabulary pair. This section develops the unidirectional versus multidirectional contrast, connects it to route and destination language already active from Chapter 2, and adds frequency expressions for describing habitual movement.

Unidirectional motion: one trip, one direction

Building Block
идти́ / е́хать = movement in progress, toward one destination
Use the unidirectional verb for a single trip happening right now, or for a single planned trip described as one continuous movement toward a goal.
Я сейча́с иду́ на рабо́ту.
I am going to work right now. (one trip, in progress, on foot)
  • Он е́дет в о́фис на авто́бусе.He is going to the office by bus.unidirectional, single trip in progress, means of transport with на plus prepositional
  • Мы идём в магази́н по доро́ге домо́й.We are going to the store on the way home.unidirectional, single trip, formulaic route expression

Multidirectional motion: repeated, habitual, or round-trip

Building Block
ходи́ть / е́здить = repeated trips, habitual pattern, or a completed round trip
Use the multidirectional verb for a habitual pattern, a repeated action, or a trip that includes both going and coming back, without focusing on one single direction.
Я ка́ждый день хожу́ на рабо́ту пешко́м.
I walk to work every day. (habitual pattern, repeated over many days)
  • Он обы́чно е́здит в о́фис на авто́бусе.He usually goes to the office by bus.multidirectional, habitual pattern with обы́чно
  • В суббо́ту мы е́здили в го́род.On Saturday we went into the city. (and came back)multidirectional past, completed round trip
Language Focus — Choosing between the two motion forms

Ask whether the sentence describes one trip in one direction, right now or as a single event, or whether it describes a pattern, a habit, a repeated action, or a round trip. Do not choose a form only because the topic is walking or driving. A single walk to the store right now takes идти́, while a description of walking to that same store every week takes ходи́ть.

Route and destination with recycled prepositions

Route and destination language recycles the case system from Chapter 2. Destination uses в or на plus the accusative, location uses в or на plus the prepositional, and origin uses из or с plus the genitive.

  • Я иду́ в библиоте́ку.I am going to the library.destination, в plus accusative
  • Я сейча́с в библиоте́ке.I am at the library right now.location, в plus prepositional, recycled from Chapter 2
  • Я иду́ из библиоте́ки домо́й.I am walking home from the library.origin, из plus genitive, recycled from Chapter 2
Language Focus — Frequency and motion together

Frequency adverbs such as ка́ждый день, обы́чно, иногда́, and ре́дко are strong signals that a multidirectional verb is needed, since they describe a repeated pattern rather than a single trip. When no frequency word appears and the sentence describes movement happening right now, the unidirectional verb is usually correct.

Model Dialogue: По доро́ге на рабо́ту

Context: Тама́ра and her new coworker Серге́й are talking on the way into the office about their daily commutes.

Серге́й: Приве́т, Тама́ра! Как ты обы́чно е́здишь на рабо́ту? Hi, Tamara! How do you usually get to work?
Тама́ра: Я обы́чно хожу́ пешко́м, э́то недалеко́. А иногда́, когда́ пого́да плоха́я, я е́зжу на авто́бусе. I usually walk, it is not far. And sometimes, when the weather is bad, I go by bus.
Серге́й: А я живу́ далеко́. Ка́ждый день я е́зжу на метро́, а пото́м иду́ пешко́м от остано́вки до о́фиса. And I live far away. Every day I go by metro, and then I walk from the stop to the office.
Тама́ра: Смотри́, вот и на́ша остано́вка. Ты сейча́с идёшь пря́мо в о́фис? Look, here is our stop. Are you going straight to the office right now?
Серге́й: Да, я иду́ пря́мо туда́. А ты ре́дко опа́здываешь? Yes, I am going straight there. And do you rarely arrive late?
Тама́ра: Ре́дко, потому́ что я хожу́ по одному́ и тому́ же маршру́ту ка́ждый день, и я зна́ю, ско́лько вре́мени ну́жно. Rarely, because I walk the same route every day, and I know how much time is needed.

Notice how the dialogue contrasts habitual forms for a daily pattern, обы́чно хожу́, ка́ждый день е́зжу, with unidirectional forms for the single trip happening right now, идёшь, иду́.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Everyday commuting

Public transport, walking, and mixed commutes are all common ways to get to work or school in Russian-speaking cities, though the balance among them varies widely by city size, distance, climate, and personal preference. Asking a coworker or classmate directly about their usual commute, as Серге́й and Тама́ра do, is an ordinary, friendly way to make small talk rather than an intrusive question.

Language Note — Motion verbs carry more than direction

Choosing between иду́ and хожу́ tells a listener whether you mean a single trip happening now or a repeated pattern. This is not a minor stylistic detail. Using the wrong form can genuinely confuse whether you are describing today's walk or your daily habit.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using the unidirectional verb for a habitual pattern

Error: Writing ка́ждый день я иду́ на рабо́ту to describe a daily routine.

Correction: Use the multidirectional verb for a repeated pattern: ка́ждый день я хожу́ на рабо́ту. Reserve иду́ for a single trip in progress right now or at one specific moment.

Common Mistake — Confusing the two verb pairs by means of transport

Error: Using идти́/ходи́ть for a trip by bus or car.

Correction: Choose the verb pair according to whether the movement is on foot or by vehicle, then choose the unidirectional or multidirectional form according to whether it is one trip or a pattern: я е́ду на авто́бусе, not я иду́ на авто́бусе.

Common Mistake — Adding a prefix before it has been taught

Error: Attempting to build a form such as прие́хать or вы́йти before these prefixed motion verbs are introduced.

Correction: Use only the unprefixed forms идти́, ходи́ть, е́хать, and е́здить at this stage. Prefixed motion verbs are introduced in Chapter 8, once this chapter's unprefixed contrast is secure.
