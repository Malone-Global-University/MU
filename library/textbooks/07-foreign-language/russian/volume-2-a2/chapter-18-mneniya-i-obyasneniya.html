Мне́ния и объясне́ния

Stating opinions, explaining reasons, comparing options, and qualifying claims · Speak and Write Russian: Expanding Independent Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 18 continues Unit IV and turns from telling a story to talking about a story, or a choice, or a plan, and explaining why one thinks what one thinks. Ири́на and her friend Макси́м are helping a third friend, Ле́на, decide between two apartments, and their conversation moves naturally between comparing options, giving reasons, and admitting that a choice is not entirely simple. This chapter develops comparatives and superlatives so that learners can say that one thing is better, cheaper, or more convenient than another, and it develops the connectors потому́ что and поэ́тому, already familiar from earlier chapters, into fuller use alongside expressions of concession, such as хотя́ and зато́, that let a learner acknowledge a drawback without abandoning an opinion. No new case or motion system is introduced. The chapter builds directly on the cause and result language already active from Chapter 17.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State a personal opinion clearly and support it with a reason.
  2. Compare two or more people, places, or things using comparative forms.
  3. Identify the best or most extreme option in a group using superlative forms.
  4. Explain a reason using потому́ что and mark a result using поэ́тому, in longer and more connected explanations than in earlier chapters.
  5. Acknowledge a drawback or a contrasting point using хотя́ and зато́, without losing the thread of one's own opinion.
  6. Listen to, read, and take part in a discussion in which two or more people compare options and explain their reasoning.

This chapter develops comparatives, superlatives, потому́ что, поэ́тому, and concession. It recycles the case, aspect, and connector systems already active from earlier chapters, and it introduces no new case, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system, in keeping with the Version 5 matrix architecture governing this volume.

Communication Scenario

Ле́на, a friend of both Ири́на and Макси́ма, is trying to choose between two apartments before the end of the month, and she has asked her friends for their honest opinions. One apartment is closer to the center but smaller and more expensive, and the other is farther away but larger, cheaper, and quieter. Over the course of an evening, the three friends compare the two apartments from several angles, and each of them explains their reasoning, admits a drawback, and eventually states a final opinion.

  1. You will listen to and read a conversation in which three friends compare two apartments.
  2. You will identify the opinions each speaker holds and the reasons behind them.
  3. You will practice comparative and superlative forms while comparing real or invented options.
  4. You will state, explain, and qualify an opinion of your own, in speech and in writing.

The comparative forms лу́чше, ху́же, деше́вле, доро́же, бли́же, да́льше, and ти́ше are the simple, invariable comparative used predicatively, and they should not be confused with the longer compound comparative with бо́лее, which remains receptive this chapter.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Stress shift in comparatives and the rhythm of хотя́ ... зато́

Notice that several simple comparatives carry stress on the final syllable, even when the related long adjective is stressed elsewhere, as in дорого́й, whose comparative доро́же keeps the stress off the first syllable entirely. Say each comparative aloud from the vocabulary table above and confirm the stress mark before using it in a sentence.

  • Practice a short concessive sentence aloud, giving a slight pause before хотя́ and a slightly stronger, contrastive stress on зато́, so that a listener hears the pivot from a drawback to a compensating advantage.
  • Keep са́мый lightly stressed and let the stress fall more clearly on the adjective that follows it, since са́мый functions as a marker rather than the main carrier of meaning.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter develops three connected systems that together let learners compare, explain, and qualify: simple comparative and superlative forms, fuller use of потому́ что and поэ́тому, and concession with хотя́ and зато́. All three systems rely on the case and agreement systems already active from earlier chapters, no new case or motion pair is introduced.

Simple comparatives and superlatives
X + лу́чше / деше́вле / бли́же + (чем) + Y · са́мый + adjective + noun
A small set of high frequency simple comparatives, invariable in form, allow learners to compare two things directly, either with чем before the second item or, in casual speech, with the second item alone in the genitive. The superlative уses са́мый, which agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies, placed directly before a long-form adjective.
Э́та кварти́ра деше́вле, чем та. Э́то са́мая ти́хая у́лица в райо́не.
This apartment is cheaper than that one. This is the quietest street in the neighborhood. (деше́вле is invariable, са́мая agrees with у́лица, feminine)
Explaining a reason and marking a result
Statement + , потому́ что + reason · Reason + , поэ́тому + result
Потому́ что answers the question почему́ and always follows the statement it explains, while поэ́тому opens a new clause that states the result of what came before. In this chapter, learners extend both connectors from single cause result pairs, already active from Chapter 17, into fuller opinion statements that combine a claim, a reason, and sometimes a second supporting reason.
Я ду́маю, что э́та кварти́ра лу́чше, потому́ что она́ бли́же к це́нтру и, кро́ме того́, в до́ме есть лифт. Она́ ме́ньше, поэ́тому Ле́не сто́ит поду́мать о ме́бели зара́нее.
I think this apartment is better, because it is closer to the center and, besides that, the building has an elevator. It is smaller, so Lena should think about furniture ahead of time.
Concession with хотя́ and зато́
Хотя́ + drawback , main claim · Main claim , зато́ + compensating advantage
Хотя́ may open a sentence or sit between two clauses, and it introduces a fact that might seem to work against the speaker's main point without actually overturning it. Зато́ always follows the point it compensates for, and it introduces a genuine advantage that balances a stated drawback, letting a speaker qualify an opinion honestly instead of ignoring its weak points.
Хотя́ э́та кварти́ра да́льше от це́нтра, она́ намно́го деше́вле. Кварти́ра ма́ленькая, зато́ о́чень ти́хая.
Although this apartment is farther from the center, it is much cheaper. The apartment is small, but then again it is very quiet.

Model Dialogue: Кака́я кварти́ра лу́чше?

Context: Ле́на, Ири́на, and Макси́м sit together in the evening, comparing two apartments that Ле́на is considering.

Ле́на: Я про́сто не могу́ реши́ть. Кака́я кварти́ра, по-ва́шему, лу́чше? I simply cannot decide. Which apartment, in your opinion, is better?
Ири́на: По мо́ему мне́нию, пе́рвая кварти́ра лу́чше, потому́ что она́ бли́же к це́нтру и к твое́й рабо́те. In my opinion, the first apartment is better, because it is closer to the center and to your work.
Макси́м: Согла́сен, что она́ бли́же, но втора́я кварти́ра намно́го деше́вле и, кро́ме того́, гора́здо ти́ше. I agree that it is closer, but the second apartment is much cheaper and, besides that, much quieter.
Ле́на: Э́то пра́вда. Втора́я са́мая ти́хая из всех, кото́рые я смотре́ла. That is true. The second one is the quietest of all the ones I looked at.
Ири́на: Хотя́ втора́я и деше́вле, доро́га на рабо́ту займёт намно́го бо́льше вре́мени. Although the second one is indeed cheaper, the commute to work will take much more time.
Макси́м: Да, э́то её недоста́ток, зато́ у неё есть большо́й балко́н и втора́я спа́льня. Yes, that is its drawback, but then again it has a big balcony and a second bedroom.
Ле́на: С одно́й стороны́, мне ва́жно вре́мя, с друго́й стороны́, мне ва́жны де́ньги. Поэ́тому я, наве́рное, вы́беру втору́ю. On the one hand, time is important to me, on the other hand, money is important to me. So I will probably choose the second one.
Ири́на: Я понима́ю твою́ причи́ну, хотя́ я бы, наве́рное, вы́брала пе́рвую. I understand your reason, although I would probably choose the first one.

This exchange moves through simple comparatives (бли́же, деше́вле, ти́ше, доро́же), a superlative (са́мая ти́хая), fuller потому́ что and поэ́тому explanations, and concession with хотя́ and зато́, without introducing any new case or motion system.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text to speech tool to read the passage below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script
Мои́ роди́тели до́лго выбира́ли, каку́ю маши́ну купи́ть. Оте́ц счита́л, что бо́лее дорога́я маши́на лу́чше, потому́ что она́ наде́жнее и удо́бнее в до́рого́й пое́здке. Ма́ма же говори́ла, что втора́я маши́на, хотя́ и попро́ще, намно́го деше́вле в обслу́живании, а э́то то́же ва́жно. С одно́й стороны́, оте́ц хоте́л ко́мфорт, с друго́й стороны́, ма́ма ду́мала о бюдже́те семьи́. В конце́ концо́в они́ вы́брали втору́ю маши́ну, потому́ что она́ была́ практи́чнее, зато́ ка́ждый год они́ отклада́ли ра́зницу в це́не на о́тпуск. По-мо́ему, э́то бы́ло у́мное реше́ние.
My parents took a long time deciding which car to buy. My father thought the more expensive car was better, because it was more reliable and more comfortable on a long trip. My mother, however, said that the second car, although simpler, was much cheaper to maintain, and that mattered too. On the one hand, my father wanted comfort, on the other hand, my mother thought about the family budget. In the end they chose the second car, because it was more practical, but then again, every year they set aside the price difference for a vacation. In my opinion, that was a smart decision.

  2. What was the mother's opinion, and what reason did she give for it?
  3. Which car did the family finally choose, and what compensating advantage does зато́ introduce at the end?
  4. Find one simple comparative and one superlative or concession expression in the passage.

Спи́сок прове́рки — Final editing checklist:

  • The text states a clear personal opinion.
  • At least two simple comparatives and one superlative appear, correctly formed.
  • A reason is introduced with потому́ что, and a result is marked with поэ́тому.
  • At least one drawback is acknowledged with хотя́ or balanced with зато́.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Stating an opinion directly

In many Russian-speaking settings, a friend who is asked for an opinion is generally expected to give one clearly, including a stated drawback, rather than offering only vague encouragement, though speakers vary in how bluntly they phrase disagreement, and softeners such as по-мо́ему or мне ка́жется are common ways to offer a direct opinion while remaining polite.

Language Note — Opinions may remain unresolved

As the model dialogue shows, two speakers may compare the same two options, acknowledge the same facts, and still reach different conclusions. A well formed opinion in Russian does not require convincing every listener, it requires a clear claim supported by a genuine reason.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Adding бо́лее to an already comparative form

Error: Writing бо́лее лу́чше or бо́лее деше́вле, combining the compound comparative marker with a form that is already comparative.

Correction: The simple comparatives лу́чше, ху́же, деше́вле, доро́же, бли́же, да́льше, and ти́ше already express comparison on their own: э́та кварти́ра деше́вле, not бо́лее деше́вле. Reserve бо́лее for adjectives that do not have a simple comparative form, which remains receptive this chapter.

Common Mistake — Placing зато́ before the drawback instead of after it

Error: Writing зато́ кварти́ра ма́ленькая, она́ о́чень ти́хая, putting зато́ before the drawback rather than before the compensating advantage.

Correction: Зато́ always introduces the advantage that balances a drawback already stated: кварти́ра ма́ленькая, зато́ она́ о́чень ти́хая. State the drawback first, then use зато́ to introduce what compensates for it.

Common Mistake — Forgetting agreement with са́мый

Error: Writing э́то са́мый ти́хая у́лица, leaving са́мый in the masculine form when the noun is feminine.

Correction: Са́мый agrees in gender, number, and case with the noun it modifies, like any long-form adjective: э́то са́мая ти́хая у́лица. Check the noun's gender before choosing the form of са́мый.

Concession with хотя́ and зато́

Chapter 18 Performance Task

Сравни́те и объясни́те своё мне́ние

Complete the following connected tasks, in order. This task gathers evidence for the systems developed in this chapter, including comparatives, superlatives, потому́ что, поэ́тому, and concession.

  1. Complete your outline. Use the four prompts from the Writing Workshop above.
  2. Speak your opinion aloud, at least ninety seconds long. Include a clear opinion, at least two comparatives, one superlative, a reason, a result, and one acknowledged drawback.
  3. Write your eight sentence opinion from the Writing Workshop. Apply the final editing checklist.
  4. Reflect briefly in English or Russian. Note whether comparatives, superlatives, or concession felt more difficult to control, and which one you would like to review before Chapter 19.
CriterionWhat is checked
Opinion and reasonThe learner states a clear opinion and supports it with at least one genuine reason.
Comparative and superlative controlThe learner uses at least two comparatives and one superlative, correctly formed and agreeing where required.
Connector useThe learner uses потому́ что and поэ́тому accurately within a longer explanation.
ConcessionThe learner acknowledges at least one drawback with хотя́ or balances it with зато́, without abandoning the stated opinion.
Overall comprehensibilityThe completed spoken and written opinion communicates clearly and stays within active case, agreement, and verb systems appropriate to A2 to B1 Russian.

This chapter is not a cumulative unit assessment. It develops comparatives, superlatives, потому́ что, поэ́тому, and concession as new productive systems for Unit IV, while recycling all case, aspect, and connector systems already active from earlier chapters.

Answer Key

Grammar and Sentence Patterns — Identify the Pattern

  1. Comparative, лу́чше располо́жена and деше́вле, comparing the two apartments directly.
  2. Superlative, са́мый удо́бный, identifying one bus route as the most convenient in the neighborhood.
  3. Concession, хотя́ ... зато́, weighing the lack of a balcony against a large kitchen.
  4. Comparative inside a потому́ что explanation, лу́чше supported by ти́ше as the stated reason.

Guided Practice — Exercise 1

  1. Автобус идёт до́лго, а метро́ бы́стрее.
  2. Э́та рабо́та интере́сная, но пе́рвая рабо́та была́ лу́чше.
  3. В библиоте́ке всегда́ ти́ше, чем в кафе́.

Guided Practice — Exercise 2

  1. Я счита́ю, что э́тот райо́н лу́чше, потому́ что он ти́ше и безопа́снее. Он споко́йнее, поэ́тому здесь прия́тно гуля́ть ве́чером.
  2. По-мо́ему, э́та рабо́та интере́снее, потому́ что там бо́льше зада́ч. Там бо́льше зада́ч, поэ́тому я получу́ бо́льше о́пыта.
  3. Я ду́маю, что пе́рвый вариа́нт удо́бнее, потому́ что он бли́же. Он бли́же, поэ́тому я бу́ду тра́тить ме́ньше вре́мени в доро́ге.

Guided Practice — Exercise 3

  1. Кварти́ра ма́ленькая, зато́ у неё прекра́сный вид из окна́.
  2. Хотя́ рабо́та дале́ко от до́ма, зарпла́та о́чень хоро́шая.
  3. Хотя́ пого́да сего́дня плоха́я, мы всё равно́ пойдём гуля́ть.

Listening — Comprehension

  1. The father thought the more expensive car was better, because it was more reliable and more comfortable on long trips.
  2. The mother thought the second, simpler car was better, because it was much cheaper to maintain.
  3. The family chose the second car, and зато́ introduces the yearly vacation savings as the compensating advantage.
  4. Sample answer, деше́вле is a simple comparative, and практи́чнее functions as a comparative here as well, describing the chosen car.

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. Irina thought the first apartment was better, because it was closer to the center and to Lena's work.
  2. Lena chose the second apartment, because saving money mattered more to her than the shorter commute.

Reading

  1. Lena chose the second apartment, mainly because it costs much less each month.
  2. She acknowledges a longer commute, introduced with хотя́.
  3. Sample answer, a quieter setting and a nearby park are named as advantages.
  4. Sample answer, са́мое разу́мное реше́ние describes her decision as the most sensible one she could make in her situation.

Writing

Sample account: По-мо́ему, вы́брать рабо́ту в ма́ленькой компа́нии лу́чше, чем в большо́й. Ма́ленькая компа́ния бли́же к мо́ему до́му, и там гора́здо ти́ше рабо́тать. Хотя́ зарпла́та там ме́ньше, зато́ у меня́ бу́дет бо́льше свобо́дного вре́мени. Я счита́ю э́то са́мым ва́жным преиму́ществом. Больша́я компа́ния плати́т деше́вле по вре́мени сотру́дников, но тре́бует гора́здо бо́льше часо́в. Поэ́тому я, наве́рное, вы́беру ма́ленькую компа́нию. Other opinions, real or invented, that accurately combine the systems from this chapter are also correct.

Looking Ahead

Что вы уже уме́ете — What you can do now

You can state an opinion, support it with reasons using потому́ что and поэ́тому, compare options using simple comparatives and superlatives, and qualify your opinion honestly using хотя́ and зато́, without losing the thread of your own conclusion.
