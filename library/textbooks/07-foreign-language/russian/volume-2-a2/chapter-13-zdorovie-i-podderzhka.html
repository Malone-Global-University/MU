Здоро́вье и подде́ржка

Discussing well being, appointments, needs, and support resources with an expanded dative experiencer system, genitive absence, and advice aspect · Speak and Write Russian: Expanding Independent Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 13 continues Unit III, and it moves from Серге́й's workplace visit into a quieter, more personal domain. On his way back from Smolensk, Серге́й starts to feel unwell and stops at a pharmacy, where he describes how he feels and receives some friendly advice. Ири́на, meanwhile, writes to a friend who has been under a lot of stress at work, offering support rather than instructions. This chapter does not introduce a new case, a new aspect contrast, or a new motion system. Instead, it expands the dative experiencer pattern already active from earlier chapters, such as мне ну́жно and мне нра́вится, to a wider set of expressions describing physical and emotional states, develops genitive absence in the specific context of energy, time, and appetite, and introduces the advice aspect pair сове́товать / посове́товать, building directly on the case governance and formal request systems developed in Chapter 12.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe how you and others feel, using an expanded set of dative experiencer expressions such as мне пло́хо, мне лу́чше, мне ка́жется, and мне жаль.
  2. Describe the absence of energy, time, or appetite using genitive absence patterns such as у меня́ нет сил and у меня́ нет вре́мени.
  3. Give and receive advice using сове́товать / посове́товать plus the dative and an infinitive.
  4. Ask about someone's well being and offer support without giving medical diagnosis or treatment instructions.
  5. Read and interpret a short message or notice describing well being, an appointment, or a support resource.
  6. Write a short paragraph offering support to someone, including at least one dative experiencer expression, one genitive absence expression, and one instance of advice.

This chapter develops the dative experiencer system already active from the Impersonal / Dative Experiencer Matrix in the governing volume scope, expanding it beyond the basic need and possibility formulas from earlier chapters. No new case, aspect, or motion system is introduced. This chapter provides general language for describing well being and support only. It does not provide medical diagnosis or treatment advice, and it should not be used as a substitute for consulting a qualified professional.

Communication Scenario

On the way back from Smolensk, Серге́й starts to feel tired and unwell, and he stops at a small pharmacy to describe how he feels. The pharmacist listens, offers general advice, and suggests he see a doctor if he does not feel better soon. That evening, Ири́на writes to a friend who has been under a lot of pressure at work, checking in on how she is doing and offering encouragement rather than instructions.

  1. You will read and listen to texts describing well being, appointments, and support between friends and colleagues.
  2. You will use an expanded dative experiencer system and genitive absence to describe how someone feels and what they lack.
  3. You will speak and write about well being and support, giving and responding to advice appropriately, without offering diagnosis or treatment.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Stress in уста́ть, врача́, and посове́товать

The noun врач has mobile stress: the nominative singular is stressed on the single syllable, but the oblique cases move the stress to the ending, as in genitive врача́ and dative врачу́. Confirm each form rather than assuming the stress stays fixed.

  • Practice уста́л, уста́ла, уста́ли aloud, and notice that the stress stays on the same syllable across the past tense forms of this verb.
  • Confirm that посове́товать keeps stress on the same syllable as its imperfective partner сове́товать, unlike some other aspect pairs where stress shifts with prefixation.

Grammar: Expanded Dative Experiencer, Genitive Absence, and Advice Aspect

These systems build directly on case forms and formulas that are already active in this volume. This section expands the dative experiencer pattern, develops genitive absence for energy, time, and appetite, and introduces the advice aspect pair, without introducing any new case, aspect, or motion system.

Expanded dative experiencer expressions

Grammar — Dative experiencer
dative pronoun/noun + adverb or short-form predicate (+ infinitive)
Recycled from earlier chapters, the dative experiencer pattern places the person who feels or perceives something in the dative case, with no grammatical subject required. This chapter widens the set of predicates beyond the basic need and possibility formulas from Chapter 11 to include physical and emotional states.
Мне сего́дня немно́го пло́хо, но, ка́жется, мне уже́ лу́чше.
I feel a bit unwell today, but it seems I already feel better. (dative мне + predicates пло́хо, лу́чше)
  • Мне ка́жется, что вы уста́ли. It seems to me that you are tired. dative мне + ка́жется
  • Ей жаль, что она́ не смогла́ прийти́ на рабо́ту сего́дня. She is sorry that she could not come to work today. dative ей + жаль
  • Нам бы́ло интере́сно узна́ть о но́вом враче́ в кли́нике. We found it interesting to learn about the new doctor at the clinic. dative нам + бы́ло интере́сно

    • Genitive absence: си́лы, вре́мя, аппети́т

    Grammar — Genitive absence
    у + genitive (person) + нет + genitive (thing lacking)
    This pattern is recycled directly from the genitive absence system developed in Volume I and Chapter 4, applied here to energy, time, and appetite. The person who lacks something appears after у in the genitive, and the thing lacking also appears in the genitive.
    У меня́ совсе́м нет сил сего́дня, поэ́тому я хочу́ отдохну́ть.
    I have absolutely no energy today, so I want to rest. (у меня́ нет + genitive plural сил)
  • У Ири́ны нет вре́мени на отдых на э́той неде́ле. Irina has no time for rest this week. у Ири́ны нет + genitive вре́мени
  • По́сле дли́нного дня у меня́ ча́сто нет аппети́та. After a long day, I often have no appetite. у меня́ нет + genitive аппети́та

    • Advice aspect: сове́товать / посове́товать

    Grammar — Advice aspect
    subject + сове́товать / посове́товать + dative (person advised) + infinitive
    The imperfective сове́товать describes advice as an ongoing or repeated practice, while the perfective посове́товать presents a single, completed piece of advice. Both take a dative complement for the person receiving the advice and an infinitive for the recommended action, and neither is used to give medical diagnoses or treatment instructions in this course.
    Фармаце́вт посове́товал мне бо́льше отдыха́ть и обрати́ться к врачу́, е́сли не ста́нет лу́чше.
    The pharmacist advised me to rest more and see a doctor if I did not feel better. (посове́товал + dative мне + infinitive отдыха́ть, обрати́ться)
  • Врач всегда́ сове́тует пацие́нтам мно́го отдыха́ть. The doctor always advises patients to rest a lot. сове́тует (imperfective, repeated) + dative пацие́нтам
  • Я посове́товала подру́ге поговори́ть с нача́льником о её расписа́нии. I advised my friend to talk to her boss about her schedule. посове́товала (perfective, single instance) + dative подру́ге
    • Language Focus — Support language is not medical language

    The dative experiencer and advice patterns in this chapter allow you to ask how someone feels, express concern, and offer general encouragement such as suggesting rest or a visit to a doctor. They are not intended for diagnosing a condition or recommending treatment, and a course in general communication should not be used as a source of medical guidance.

    Пра́ктика — Grammar practice: Complete each sentence with the correct form.

    1. ___ (I) сего́дня пло́хо. (dative experiencer)
    2. У ___ (she) нет ___ (time, genitive) на отдых.
    3. Фармаце́вт ___ (advised, perfective) мне бо́льше отдыха́ть.
    4. У меня́ совсе́м нет ___ (energy, genitive plural) сего́дня.
    5. Мне ___ (it seems), что вы уста́ли.

    Model Dialogue: В апте́ке

    Context: Серге́й stops at a small pharmacy on the way back from Smolensk, because he does not feel well.

    Фармаце́вт: Здра́вствуйте! Как вы себя́ чу́вствуете? Hello! How are you feeling?
    Серге́й: Че́стно говоря́, мне не о́чень хорошо́. Я уста́л, и у меня́ совсе́м нет сил. Honestly, I don't feel very well. I'm tired, and I have absolutely no energy.
    Фармаце́вт: Понима́ю. А у вас есть вре́мя, что́бы отдохну́ть сего́дня? I understand. Do you have time to rest today?
    Серге́й: Наве́рное, да. У меня́ нет ва́жных встреч до за́втра. Probably, yes. I don't have any important meetings until tomorrow.
    Фармаце́вт: Тогда́ я сове́тую вам сего́дня бо́льше отдыха́ть и пить бо́льше воды́. Е́сли за́втра вам не ста́нет лу́чше, посове́тую обрати́ться к врачу́. Then I advise you to rest more today and drink more water. If you don't feel better tomorrow, I would advise seeing a doctor.
    Серге́й: Спаси́бо большо́е за сове́т. Мне уже́ немно́го лу́чше от того́, что я поговори́л с ва́ми. Thank you very much for the advice. I already feel a bit better from talking with you.
    Фармаце́вт: Пожа́луйста. Забо́тьтесь о себе́, и не забыва́йте отдыха́ть. You're welcome. Take care of yourself, and don't forget to rest.

    This exchange integrates expanded dative experiencer expressions, genitive absence for energy and time, and the advice aspect pair сове́товать / посове́товать, the three principal systems developed in this chapter, while keeping the pharmacist's language general and support oriented rather than diagnostic.

    Culture and Communication

    Culture in Context — Talking about well being among friends and colleagues

    The comfort level and directness with which people discuss health, stress, and well being varies widely across families, generations, workplaces, and communities in Russian-speaking countries, so learners should avoid assuming that any single way of asking about or responding to someone's well being represents every Russian-speaking community.

    Language Note — Advice softens concern, it does not replace professional care

    The advice language in this chapter, such as сове́товать with the dative and infinitive, is well suited to everyday encouragement between friends, family members, and colleagues. It is not a substitute for the specific guidance of a qualified doctor, pharmacist, or other professional, and a caring response to someone who is unwell often includes suggesting that they seek that kind of professional support.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    Common Mistake — Using a nominative subject instead of a dative experiencer

    Error: Writing я пло́хо by analogy with an English subject and adjective.

    Correction: Use the dative pronoun with the impersonal predicate, regardless of the English translation: мне пло́хо. There is no grammatical subject in this pattern, only the dative experiencer.

    Common Mistake — Using the accusative instead of the genitive after нет

    Error: Writing у меня́ нет вре́мя without adjusting the case of the thing lacking.

    Correction: Нет always requires the genitive for the thing that is absent: у меня́ нет вре́мени. This is the same genitive absence pattern recycled from earlier chapters.

    Common Mistake — Confusing the aspect of сове́товать with the strength of the advice

    Error: Assuming that посове́товать is stronger or more urgent advice than сове́товать.

    Correction: The aspect distinguishes an ongoing or repeated practice, сове́тует, from a single completed act of advice, посове́товал, not the strength of the recommendation. Confirm which aspect fits the situation, a habit or a one-time instance.

    Expanded dative experiencer

    Мне пло́хо, мне лу́чше, мне ка́жется, and мне жаль all place the experiencer in the dative, with no grammatical subject.

    Guided Practice — Exercise 1

    1. Мне пло́хо сего́дня у́тром.
    2. Ей ка́жется, что она́ мно́го рабо́тает.
    3. Нам бы́ло жаль, что мы не смогли́ помо́чь.
    4. Вам уже́ лу́чше по́сле отдыха?

    Guided Practice — Exercise 2

    1. У меня́ нет сил по́сле дли́нного дня.
    2. У Серге́я нет вре́мени на отдых сего́дня.
    3. У неё нет аппети́та, потому́ что она́ пло́хо себя́ чу́вствует.

    Guided Practice — Exercise 3

    1. Я сове́тую Серге́ю бо́льше отдыха́ть.
    2. Я посове́товала колле́гам Ири́ны пить бо́льше воды́.
    3. Я посове́товал уста́лому студе́нту пойти́ домо́й ра́ньше.

    Listening — Comprehension

    1. Ири́на says her friend has no time at all for rest.
    2. Ири́на advises her friend to do nothing this evening and just rest.
    3. Ири́на says she cannot give medical advice, because she is not a doctor.
    4. Sample answer: мне ка́жется is a dative experiencer expression, and у тебя́ нет вре́мени is a genitive absence expression.

    Model Dialogue — Comprehension

    1. Серге́й says he is tired and has no energy at all.
    2. The pharmacist advises him to rest more and drink more water, and suggests seeing a doctor if he does not feel better by tomorrow.

    Reading

    1. The clinic tries to schedule appointments as quickly as possible for patients with little time.
    2. A patient who feels unwell or thinks they need help should contact the staff by phone or at the front desk.
    3. The clinic advises patients not to delay a visit if they do not feel better within a few days.
    4. Sample answers: сове́туем пацие́нтам is an advice aspect verb, and вам ка́жется is a dative experiencer expression.

    Writing

    Sample account: Приве́т! Как ты себя́ чу́вствуешь по́сле э́той неде́ли? Мне ка́жется, что у тебя́ совсе́м нет вре́мени на отдых, и я немно́го беспоко́юсь о тебе́. У меня́ то́же иногда́ не быва́ет сил по́сле дли́нного дня, и тогда́ мне помога́ет просто́й о́тдых. Я сове́тую тебе́ сего́дня ве́чером ничего́ не де́лать. Е́сли тебе́ не ста́нет лу́чше на э́той неде́ле, пожа́луйста, обрати́сь к врачу́. Я всегда́ гото́ва помо́чь, е́сли тебе́ что-то ну́жно, и мне действи́тельно жаль, что у тебя́ была́ така́я тяжёлая неде́ля. Other accounts drawing accurately on this chapter's systems are also correct.

    Looking Ahead

    Что вы уже уме́ете — What you can do now

    You can describe how you and others feel using an expanded dative experiencer system, describe the absence of energy, time, or appetite using genitive absence, and give and respond to general advice using сове́товать / посове́товать, combining all three in a connected spoken or written account offering support to someone.
