Воспомина́ния

Telling personal memories with background and events · Speak and Write Russian: Expanding Independent Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 16 opens Unit IV and turns to a new communicative task, telling a personal memory. Ири́на has been visiting her grandmother, and the two of them have been talking about the past, about childhood summers, an old apartment, and a school that no longer exists. Up to now, most narration in this course has stayed close to the present or the near future. This chapter asks learners to move fluently between background description, which usually calls for the imperfective aspect, and specific past events, which usually call for the perfective aspect, and to connect those two kinds of information with time clauses and narrative connectors. No new case or motion system is introduced. The chapter builds directly on the past tense and aspect control already active from earlier chapters.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Distinguish background description in the past, expressed with the imperfective aspect, from specific completed events, expressed with the perfective aspect.
  2. Describe habitual or repeated actions in the past, using imperfective verbs together with frequency expressions such as ка́ждое ле́то and обы́чно.
  3. Connect background and events using time clauses with когда́ and пока́.
  4. Organize a spoken or written memory using narrative connectors such as снача́ла, пото́м, зате́м, вдруг, and наконе́ц.
  5. Listen to and read a short personal narrative and identify which parts describe background and which parts report specific events.
  6. Tell a short, organized memory of your own, real or invented, using the systems developed in this chapter.

This chapter develops past aspect, habitual past, time clauses, and narrative connectors. It recycles the case, motion, and verb systems already active from earlier chapters, and it introduces no new case, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system, in keeping with the Version 5 matrix architecture governing this volume.

Communication Scenario

Ири́на is visiting her grandmother, Ве́ра Никола́евна, on a Sunday afternoon. Over tea, her grandmother begins to talk about the apartment where she grew up, the school she attended, and a particular summer that Ири́на has heard about before but never in detail. Ири́на wants to follow the story closely and later tell a friend about it, and she also wants to share a memory of her own childhood.

  1. You will listen to and read a grandmother's memory of her childhood apartment and school.
  2. You will identify which sentences describe ongoing background and which report a single completed event.
  3. You will practice connecting background and events with когда́ and пока́.
  4. You will tell and write a short, organized memory of your own, using narrative connectors to sequence it clearly.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Stress shift in вы́расти and phrase stress in narrative connectors

Confirm the stress of вы́расти on the prefix in the infinitive and in the masculine past form вы́рос, while the feminine and plural past forms, вы́росла and вы́росли, keep the stress on the same syllable. This is a fixed, mobile-stress verb, and the stress must not be guessed from a related word.

  • Practice the narrative connectors снача́ла, пото́м, зате́м, вдруг, and наконе́ц aloud in a short chain, keeping each word's own stress clear and giving вдруг slightly extra emphasis, since it marks a turn in the story.
  • When reading a пока́-clause aloud, keep the pace of the пока́-clause slightly slower and more even than the perfective event that follows it, since it describes ongoing background rather than a single sharp action.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter develops four connected systems that together let learners narrate a memory: background versus event in the past, habitual past, time clauses with когда́ and пока́, and narrative connectors. All four systems rely on the case, aspect, and verb systems already active from earlier chapters; no new case or motion pattern is introduced.

Background versus event in the past
imperfective past = ongoing background · perfective past = a single completed event
A narrative usually opens with imperfective verbs that set the scene, and moves to perfective verbs to report what actually happened. This is a meaning contrast, not a difference in “tense.”
Мы жи́ли в ста́рой кварти́ре. Одна́жды к нам прие́хала ба́бушка.
We lived in an old apartment. (background, imperfective жи́ли) One day grandmother came to see us. (event, perfective прие́хала)
Habitual past
imperfective past + frequency expression (ка́ждое ле́то, обы́чно, ча́сто)
Habitual past reports what regularly happened over a period, not a single occasion. The imperfective aspect is required, and a frequency expression usually accompanies it.
Ка́ждое ле́то мы е́здили к ба́бушке в дере́вню.
Every summer we used to go to grandmother's in the village. (habitual, imperfective е́здили, multidirectional motion already active)
Time clauses with когда́ and пока́
когда́ + past (either aspect) · пока́ + imperfective past
Когда́ can introduce either a background clause or an event clause, depending on the aspect chosen inside it. Пока́ specifically requires the imperfective, because it frames one ongoing action as the backdrop for another.
Пока́ ба́бушка гото́вила у́жин, де́ти игра́ли во дворе́.
While grandmother was making dinner, the children were playing in the yard. (both verbs imperfective, two parallel ongoing actions)
Narrative connectors
снача́ла … пото́м / зате́м … вдруг … наконе́ц
These connectors organize a story into a clear sequence for the listener or reader. Вдруг marks an unexpected turn and typically introduces a perfective verb.
Снача́ла всё бы́ло споко́йно, но вдруг пошёл си́льный дождь.
At first everything was calm, but suddenly heavy rain began. (снача́ла with imperfective бы́ло, вдруг with perfective пошёл)

Model Dialogue: Ба́бушкин дом

Context: Ири́на sits with her grandmother, Ве́ра Никола́евна, over tea, and asks about the apartment where her grandmother grew up.

Ири́на: Ба́бушка, расскажи́ мне про дом, где ты вы́росла. Grandma, tell me about the house where you grew up.
Ве́ра Н.: Снача́ла мы жи́ли в ма́ленькой кварти́ре в це́нтре го́рода. Ка́ждое ле́то мы е́здили к мои́м роди́телям в дере́вню. At first we lived in a small apartment in the center of the city. Every summer we used to go to my parents' place in the village.
Ири́на: А кака́я была́ шко́ла? Ты по́мнишь её? And what was the school like? Do you remember it?
Ве́ра Н.: Коне́чно по́мню. Пока́ я учи́лась в шко́ле, мы ча́сто гуля́ли по́сле уро́ков в ста́ром па́рке. Пото́м, когда́ мне бы́ло два́дцать лет, семья́ перее́хала в друго́й райо́н. Of course I remember. While I was studying at school, we often used to walk in the old park after lessons. Then, when I was twenty, the family moved to a different district.
Ири́на: А что случи́лось с ва́шей ста́рой шко́лой? And what happened to your old school?
Ве́ра Н.: Вдруг, ле́т де́сять наза́д, зда́ние закры́ли, и тепе́рь там о́фисы. Но я до сих пор вспомина́ю ту шко́лу, когда́ прохожу́ ми́мо э́того ме́ста. Suddenly, about ten years ago, the building was closed, and now there are offices there. But I still recall that school whenever I walk past that spot.
Ири́на: Наконе́ц я понима́ю, почему́ ты так лю́бишь э́тот райо́н. Now I finally understand why you love that district so much.

This exchange moves between habitual past (е́здили, гуля́ли), background description (жи́ли, учи́лась), single completed events (перее́хала, закры́ли), and narrative connectors (снача́ла, пото́м, вдруг, наконе́ц), without introducing any new case or motion system.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Sharing memories across generations

Family memories are commonly shared informally, over tea, during holidays, or in casual conversation, in many Russian-speaking households and elsewhere, though the specific traditions, and how openly personal history is discussed, vary from family to family and community to community.

Language Note — Personal narrative may be invented

Not every learner will want to share a genuine personal memory in a language classroom or in independent study. An invented memory, built honestly from the grammar and vocabulary of this chapter, serves the same communicative purpose, and learners should feel free to invent a plausible story rather than disclose personal material they would prefer to keep private.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using the perfective for habitual past

Error: Writing ка́ждое ле́то мы пое́хали к мо́рю, using the perfective for a repeated action.

Correction: Habitual past requires the imperfective: ка́ждое ле́то мы е́здили к мо́рю. Reserve the perfective for a single, specific occasion, such as одна́жды ле́том мы пое́хали к мо́рю в пе́рвый раз.

Common Mistake — Using the perfective inside a пока́-clause

Error: Writing пока́ ба́бушка пригото́вила у́жин, мы игра́ли во дворе́, using the perfective inside the пока́-clause.

Correction: Пока́ requires the imperfective, since it frames an ongoing action: пока́ ба́бушка гото́вила у́жин, мы игра́ли во дворе́. The perfective event, if there is one, belongs in the main clause, not inside пока́.

Common Mistake — Overusing пото́м and losing narrative variety

Error: Connecting every sentence in a story with пото́м, producing a flat, repetitive narrative such as пото́м мы игра́ли, пото́м пришла́ ма́ма, пото́м мы пое́ли.

Correction: Vary the connectors according to their function: снача́ла to open, вдруг for an unexpected turn, and наконе́ц to close, keeping пото́м and зате́м for the ordinary middle steps: снача́ла мы игра́ли, пото́м пришла́ ма́ма, и наконе́ц мы пое́ли вме́сте.

Chapter Review

Background versus event

Imperfective past sets the scene, and perfective past reports what actually happened at a specific moment.

Habitual past

Repeated past actions take the imperfective aspect and are usually paired with a frequency expression such as ка́ждое ле́то or обы́чно.

Time clauses

Когда́ can introduce background or an event, while пока́ requires the imperfective to frame an ongoing action.

Narrative connectors

Снача́ла, пото́м, зате́м, вдруг, and наконе́ц organize a memory into a clear, varied sequence.

Guided Practice — Exercise 2

  1. Когда́ мы жи́ли в ста́рой кварти́ре, роди́лся мой мла́дший брат.
  2. Пока́ ба́бушка расска́зывала исто́рию, мы слу́шали о́чень внима́тельно.
  3. Когда́ мне бы́ло де́сять лет, мы перее́хали в но́вый райо́н.

Guided Practice — Exercise 3

Sample order: Всё бы́ло споко́йно в до́ме. Снача́ла ба́бушка на́шла ста́рую коро́бку на чердаке́. Пото́м мы нашли́ ста́рую фотогра́фию. Вдруг мы поняли, кто был на э́той фотогра́фии. Other logical orders using at least three connectors are also correct.

Listening — Comprehension

  1. Every summer the family used to go to the sea and usually stayed there for three weeks.
  2. A strong storm suddenly began while they were walking along the shore.
  3. The speaker was sixteen when the family moved, introduced by наконе́ц.
  4. Sample answer: е́здила and остава́лись are habitual past; начала́сь and перее́хала are single completed events.

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. Every summer, grandmother's family used to go to her parents' place in the village.
  2. Her old school was closed about ten years ago and is now offices; she still recalls it whenever she walks past that spot.

Reading

  1. Grandmother was nine that summer, and she and the neighborhood children played in the yard every day until evening.
  2. An ice cream truck suddenly arrived one day, introduced by вдруг.
  3. Her younger brother was born at the end of the summer, changing the whole family's life.
  4. Sample answer: ка́ждый день мы игра́ли is habitual past; пока́ мы игра́ли, вдруг прие́хал грузови́к is a пока́-clause framing an interrupting event.

Writing

Sample account: Когда́ мне бы́ло семь лет, мы жи́ли в небольшо́м го́роде у реки́. Ка́ждое ле́то мы с бра́том пла́вали в реке́ и игра́ли с сосе́дскими детьми́. Обы́чно ве́чером вся семья́ ужи́нала во дворе́. Одна́жды, пока́ мы игра́ли о́коло воды́, вдруг начала́сь си́льная гроза́, и мы бы́стро побежа́ли домо́й. Снача́ла все испуга́лись, но пото́м мы посмея́лись над э́тим. Наконе́ц, когда́ мне испо́лнилось де́сять лет, семья́ перее́хала в го́род, и та́к зако́нчился тот пери́од мое́й жи́зни. Other accounts, real or invented, that accurately combine the systems from this chapter are also correct.

Looking Ahead

Что вы уже уме́ете — What you can do now

You can distinguish background from specific events in the past, describe habitual actions with the imperfective and a frequency expression, connect background and events with когда́ and пока́, and organize a personal memory with narrative connectors, combining these systems in one coherent spoken or written narrative.
