Прое́кт 3: Городска́я ситуа́ция

Unit III cumulative assessment: interpreting notices and coordinating a community response · Speak and Write Russian: Expanding Independent Communication

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 15 is the Unit III cumulative assessment, and it closes the sequence that began with study routines in Chapter 11 and continued through work responsibilities in Chapter 12, health and support in Chapter 13, and community news in Chapter 14. Ири́на now has a fuller picture of her building and her neighborhood, and this chapter asks her, and the learner, to bring several kinds of information together at once, a schedule, an obligation, a request for help, and a piece of general news, and to respond appropriately in speech and in writing. This chapter introduces no new case, aspect, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system. It gathers evidence for the systems already introduced and developed across Chapters 11 through 14.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Interpret a set of connected notices describing a city or building situation that involves a schedule, an obligation, and a request.
  2. Use case governance after work and communication verbs, and formal requests, in a coordinated written or spoken response.
  3. Use the expanded dative experiencer and genitive absence to describe needs, problems, and what is missing from a situation.
  4. Give and understand advice using the aspect choices developed in Chapter 13.
  5. Report general information using impersonal constructions such as говоря́т, что and изве́стно, что, and recognize passive-like expressions in public notices without producing them independently.
  6. Coordinate a short spoken and written community response that combines these systems into one coherent task.

This chapter is a cumulative assessment chapter. It introduces no new major productive grammar, case, aspect, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system, in keeping with the Version 5 matrix architecture governing this volume.

Communication Scenario

The courtyard repair announced in Chapter 14 is now underway, and a second notice has appeared on the board, this one about a change to the building's hot water schedule during the same week. Ири́на's neighbor asks her for help understanding both notices and deciding what the household needs to do. Ири́на also wants to raise the matter of the shared stairwell, which has not been cleaned in some time, at the next residents' meeting.

  1. You will read two connected notices describing a city or building situation.
  2. You will help a neighbor understand what is required, when, and why, using case governance, requests, and the dative experiencer.
  3. You will give advice about how to prepare for the situation, choosing aspect carefully.
  4. You will speak and write a coordinated response that raises a related concern and asks for information, combining impersonal reporting with active systems.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Review: расписа́ние, обя́занность, and сове́товать

This chapter reviews rather than introduces stress patterns. Confirm the stress of расписа́ние on the fourth syllable, of обя́занность on the second syllable, with the ending unstressed in all case forms, and of сове́товать on the second syllable, with the stress remaining on that syllable throughout the present-tense conjugation.

  • Read the two notices in the Reading Practice section aloud, and mark any word whose stress you are unsure of before checking it against the vocabulary table or a prior chapter.
  • When reading impersonal expressions such as говоря́т and изве́стно aloud, keep the phrase stress even and avoid placing extra emphasis on the impersonal verb itself, since it is not the most important word in the sentence.

Systems Under Assessment

This chapter does not introduce new grammar. It brings together, for assessment, the systems developed in Chapters 11 through 14, so that learners can show they can combine several active systems in one coordinated task. Each system below is reviewed briefly, with a reminder of its active status and a short example, before the model exchange and practice sections apply it.

Review — Case governance after work and communication verbs
verb + required case, as fixed by the verb's government
From Chapter 12. Verbs such as занима́ться, сообща́ть, and каса́ться each require a specific case, and that case does not change to match English preposition patterns.
Э́то каса́ется всех жильцо́в до́ма.
This concerns all the residents of the building. (каса́ется + genitive жильцо́в)
Review — Dative experiencer and genitive absence
dative experiencer (мне ну́жно, у меня́ нет) + genitive of absence
From Chapter 13. The dative experiencer expresses what a person needs or lacks, and genitive absence expresses what is missing, without a nominative subject performing the action.
У нас не бу́дет горя́чей воды́ в четве́рг.
We will not have hot water on Thursday. (у нас не бу́дет + genitive воды́)
Review — Advice and aspect choice
сове́товать / посове́товать + dative + infinitive
From Chapter 13. Advice about a single, specific recommended action typically uses the perfective infinitive, while advice about an ongoing or repeated practice typically uses the imperfective infinitive.
Я сове́тую вам напо́лнить ва́нну водо́й зара́нее.
I advise you to fill the bathtub with water in advance. (perfective infinitive, one specific action)
Review — Impersonal reporting and passive-like recognition
говоря́т / изве́стно + что-clause; recognize but do not produce -ся forms in public style
From Chapter 14. These patterns remain fully active for production. Passive-like forms in notices, such as отключа́ется or публику́ется, remain for recognition only.
Изве́стно, что горя́чая вода́ бу́дет отключена́ на оди́н день.
It is known that the hot water will be shut off for one day. (recognize отключена́; do not produce independently)

Model Exchange: Собра́ние жиле́й до́ма

Context: Ири́на talks with her neighbor Ольга Петро́вна in the stairwell, after both have read the two new notices about the water schedule and the ongoing courtyard repair.

Ольга П.: Ири́на, вы уже́ ви́дели но́вое объявле́ние? Говоря́т, что в четве́рг не бу́дет горя́чей воды́. Irina, have you already seen the new notice? They say there won't be hot water on Thursday.
Ири́на: Да, ви́дела. Изве́стно, ско́лько вре́мени э́то продли́тся? Yes, I saw it. Is it known how long this will last?
Ольга П.: То́лько оди́н день, но мне ну́жно бу́дет помы́ть посу́ду и постира́ть зара́нее. У вас есть сове́т? Only one day, but I will need to wash dishes and do laundry in advance. Do you have advice?
Ири́на: Я сове́тую вам напо́лнить ва́нну водо́й в сре́ду ве́чером. Э́то поможет с посу́дой и уборкой. I advise you to fill the bathtub with water on Wednesday evening. That will help with dishes and cleaning.
Ольга П.: Хоро́шая мысль, спаси́бо. А вы бу́дете на собра́нии в сле́дующий вто́рник? Good idea, thank you. And will you be at the meeting next Tuesday?
Ири́на: Да, бу́ду. Я хочу́ подня́ть вопро́с о ле́стнице, она́ не убира́лась уже́ давно́, и э́то каса́ется всех, кто здесь живёт. Yes, I will be. I want to raise the question of the stairwell, it hasn't been cleaned in a long time, and this concerns everyone who lives here.
Ольга П.: Соглаша́юсь с ва́ми по́лностью. Напиши́те, пожа́луйста, об э́том в о́бщий чат, что́бы все зна́ли зара́нее. I completely agree with you. Please write about that in the group chat, so that everyone knows in advance.

This exchange combines the dative experiencer and genitive absence from Chapter 13, advice with careful aspect choice from Chapter 13, case governance after каса́ться from Chapter 12 and Chapter 14, and impersonal reporting from Chapter 14, without introducing any new system.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Household preparation for service interruptions

Scheduled interruptions to hot water or other utilities occur in many buildings across Russian-speaking countries and elsewhere, and households commonly prepare in advance once a notice appears, though the exact practices, and the format in which notices are shared, vary from building to building and community to community.

Language Note — Combining systems is the point of this chapter

Unlike earlier chapters, which each developed one or two principal systems, this chapter asks learners to move between several active systems within a single conversation or piece of writing. This kind of combination, rather than any single new rule, is what independent B1 communication increasingly requires.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Losing case government while combining ideas

Error: Writing э́то каса́ется все жильцы́, keeping the nominative form when combining several sentences quickly.

Correction: Каса́ться always governs the genitive, regardless of how many other ideas surround it: э́то каса́ется всех жильцо́в. Check each verb's government separately, even inside a longer, more complex message.

Common Mistake — Advising with the wrong aspect

Error: Using the imperfective infinitive for a single specific recommended action, as in я сове́тую вам наполня́ть ва́нну в сре́ду, which suggests a repeated or ongoing practice.

Correction: For one specific, bounded recommended action, use the perfective infinitive: я сове́тую вам напо́лнить ва́нну в сре́ду. Reserve the imperfective infinitive for advice about a repeated or general practice, such as я сове́тую вам всегда́ проверя́ть доску́ объявле́ний.

Common Mistake — Producing a passive-like form independently

Error: Inventing a new passive-like sentence, such as вода́ отключа́ется рабо́тниками, without a verified model.

Correction: Rephrase using an active system instead, such as рабо́тники отключа́ют во́ду, or report the fact impersonally with говоря́т, что во́ду отключа́т. Passive-like forms remain for recognition only at this stage.

Chapter Review

Case governance

Verbs such as каса́ться, сообща́ть, and занима́ться keep their fixed case government inside longer, combined messages.

Dative experiencer and genitive absence

Needs and missing items are expressed through the dative experiencer and genitive absence, not through an invented nominative subject.

Advice and aspect

Advice about one specific action normally takes the perfective infinitive, while advice about an ongoing practice normally takes the imperfective infinitive.

Impersonal reporting and recognition

Говоря́т and изве́стно remain fully productive, while passive-like -ся forms in public notices remain for recognition only.

  2. The announcement advises residents to fill the bathtub or other containers with water in advance.
  3. The condition of the stairwell, which has not been cleaned in a long time, will be discussed.
  4. Sample answer: ста́ло изве́стно is an impersonal expression, and бу́дут отключены́ is a passive-like expression, recognized here for reading purposes only.

Model Exchange — Comprehension

  1. Ири́на advises Ольга Петро́вна to fill the bathtub with water on Wednesday evening, so that she has water for dishes and cleaning.
  2. Ири́на wants to raise the condition of the stairwell, which has not been cleaned in a long time.

Reading

  1. Hot water will be unavailable on Thursday from eight in the morning until eight in the evening, and residents should fill the bathtub or other containers with water in advance.
  2. The next meeting is next Tuesday at seven in the evening in the building's common room, and it will address the repair schedule and the condition of the stairwell.
  3. The first notice concerns all residents without exception, as stated directly in the text: э́то каса́ется всех кварти́р, без исключе́ния.
  4. Sample answers: напомина́ем functions similarly to the impersonal patterns of this chapter, and бу́дет отключена́ or бу́дут обсужда́ться is a passive-like expression, recognized here for reading purposes only.

Writing

Sample account: Уважа́емые сосе́ди! Сообща́ю вам, что говоря́т о том, что в четве́рг не бу́дет горя́чей воды́ с восьми́ утра́ до восьми́ ве́чера, потому́ что продолжа́ется ремо́нт двора́. Мне ка́жется, что э́то каса́ется всех кварти́р, без исключе́ния. Я сове́тую всем напо́лнить ва́нну водо́й в сре́ду ве́чером. Кро́ме того́, я хочу́ подня́ть вопро́с о ле́стнице, кото́рая давно́ не убира́лась. Изве́стно, что э́тот вопро́с бу́дет обсужда́ться на сле́дующем собра́нии. Е́сли у вас есть предложе́ния, пожа́луйста, напиши́те председа́телю собра́ния и́ли отве́тьте в э́том ча́те. Спаси́бо всем за понима́ние. Other accounts drawing accurately on the systems from Chapters 11 through 14 are also correct.

Looking Ahead

Что вы уже уме́ете — What you can do now

You can interpret a set of connected notices describing a city or building situation, coordinate case governance, formal requests, the dative experiencer, and genitive absence within one response, give advice with careful aspect choice, and report general information impersonally while recognizing passive-like public style, all within one coherent spoken or written task. This completes Unit III.

Продолже́ние: Глава 16, Воспомина́ния — Continuing on: Chapter 16, Memories

Глава 16, Воспомина́ния, opens Unit IV and begins to develop past aspect in fuller narration, habitual past meaning, time clauses, and narrative connectors, so that learners can tell personal memories with background and events. It builds on the past-tense and aspect control already active from earlier chapters, and it introduces no case, motion, or orthographic system beyond what the Case / Declension Matrix and Verbs of Motion Matrix already authorize for this stage of the volume.
