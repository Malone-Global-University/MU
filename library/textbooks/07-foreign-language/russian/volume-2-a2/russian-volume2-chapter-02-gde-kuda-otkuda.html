Где, куда́ и отку́да

Where, to where, and from where · Speak and Write Russian: Expanding Independent Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Explain where a person or thing is located, using в or на with the prepositional case.
  2. Explain where a person or thing is going, using в or на with the accusative case, and distinguish this pattern clearly from the location pattern above.
  3. Explain where a person or thing came from, using из or с with the genitive case, and connect this pattern to the destination it reverses.
  4. Choose correctly among в, на, из, and с according to the noun involved, not according to a single English translation.
  5. Ask and answer practical questions about city locations, errands, and everyday movement around town.
  6. Read and write a short connected account of a day spent moving between several places.

This chapter expands a system you began in Volume I. It does not introduce a new case or a new preposition family. Instead, it brings location, destination, and source together so that you can use all three with confidence and speed, using only cases and prepositions already active in your Russian.

Communication Scenario

Тама́ра, the new student from Chapter 1, is settling into life near the language center. Her neighbor, Серге́й, offers to show her around the neighborhood on a Saturday, and the two of them plan a route that takes them from the dormitory to the market, from the market to the post office, and finally back home by way of the park. As they plan, they talk constantly about where things are, where they are headed next, and where they have just come from.

  1. You will describe where several places are located, in relation to the city center and to each other.
  2. You will plan a short route around town, saying clearly where you are going and where you are coming from at each stage.
  3. You will read and listen to short accounts of other people's errands, tracking their movement through the city.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Preposition and noun as one rhythmic unit

In connected speech, a short preposition such as в, на, из, or с loses its own stress and leans on the noun that follows, forming a single rhythmic unit. This pattern was introduced in Volume I and continues to matter here, since this chapter uses these four prepositions constantly.

  • Practice saying в го́роде, на у́лице, из до́ма, and с рабо́ты as single rhythmic units, with no pause and no separate stress on the preposition.
  • Notice that с before a voiceless consonant, as in с по́чты, devoices naturally, while с before a voiced consonant, as in с вокза́ла, does not.
  • Notice that в and с may add a helping vowel before certain consonant clusters, as in во вто́рник, so that the phrase remains pronounceable.
  • Read the Model Dialogue below aloud once, paying attention to where each preposition attaches to its noun without a separate beat of stress.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This section brings together three patterns you have already met separately in Volume I, so that you can move between them quickly and accurately in one conversation.

Где — Location, with в/на and the prepositional case

Pattern
в / на + [noun in the prepositional case]
Answers the question где, describing a fixed location rather than movement.
По́чта нахо́дится в це́нтре, недалеко́ от вокза́ла.
The post office is located downtown, not far from the train station.
  • Ры́нок нахо́дится на у́лице Пу́шкина, ря́дом с апте́кой.The market is located on Pushkin Street, next to the pharmacy.на + prepositional
  • Тама́ра сейча́с в общежи́тии, а Серге́й на остано́вке.Tamara is now in the dormitory, and Sergei is at the bus stop.в/на + prepositional, zero copula

Куда́ — Destination, with в/на and the accusative case

Pattern
в / на + [noun in the accusative case]
Answers the question куда́, describing movement toward a destination, not location.
По́сле обе́да мы пойдём на ры́нок, а пото́м зайдём в апте́ку.
After lunch we will go to the market, and then stop in at the pharmacy.
  • Тама́ра идёт на по́чту, потому́ что ей ну́жно отпра́вить письмо́.Tamara is going to the post office, because she needs to send a letter.на + accusative destination
  • Ве́чером мы пойдём в парк, е́сли бу́дет хоро́шая пого́да.In the evening we will go to the park, if the weather is good.в + accusative destination

Compare the prepositional and accusative pairs above carefully. The preposition stays the same, в or на, but the case ending on the noun changes, and that ending alone signals location versus destination.

Отку́да — Source, with из/с and the genitive case

Pattern
из / с + [noun in the genitive case]
Answers the question отку́да, describing where someone or something came from. Из reverses в, and с reverses на.
Серге́й то́лько что пришёл с ры́нка, а Тама́ра пришла́ из общежи́тия.
Sergei has just come from the market, and Tamara has come from the dormitory.
  • Э́то письмо́ пришло́ из Каза́ни, от ба́бушки Серге́я.This letter came from Kazan, from Sergei's grandmother.из + genitive source
  • Мы то́лько что верну́лись с вокза́ла, где встреча́ли друзе́й.We have just returned from the train station, where we were meeting friends.с + genitive source
Language Focus — Matching the preposition, not translating it

A noun that takes на for location and destination, such as ры́нок or по́чта, must take с for source, not из. A noun that takes в, such as общежи́тие or парк, must take из for source. This pairing is a property of the noun, not a free choice, and it does not translate directly from English "from," which does not distinguish these two Russian patterns.

Model Dialogue: Где, куда́ и отку́да

Context: On Saturday morning, Тама́ра and Серге́й meet outside the dormitory to plan their route around the neighborhood.

Серге́й: Приве́т! Ты уже́ гото́ва? Снача́ла, я ду́маю, пойдём на ры́нок, он недалеко́, на у́лице Пу́шкина. Hi! Are you ready already? First, I think, let's go to the market, it is not far, on Pushkin Street.
Тама́ра: Хорошо́, а где то́чно нахо́дится ры́нок? Я ещё пло́хо зна́ю э́тот райо́н. Good, and where exactly is the market located? I still don't know this district well.
Серге́й: Он нахо́дится ря́дом с апте́кой, недалеко́ от остано́вки. По́сле ры́нка мы зайдём на по́чту, мне ну́жно отпра́вить посы́лку. It is located next to the pharmacy, not far from the bus stop. After the market we will stop in at the post office, I need to send a package.
Тама́ра: А по́чта далеко́ от ры́нка? And is the post office far from the market?
Серге́й: Нет, она́ в це́нтре, мину́т де́сять пешко́м. А пото́м, е́сли бу́дет вре́мя, мы пойдём в парк, он нахо́дится ря́дом с вокза́лом. No, it is downtown, about ten minutes on foot. And then, if there is time, we will go to the park, it is located next to the train station.
Тама́ра: Здо́рово. А отку́да ты идёшь сейча́с? Из общежи́тия? Great. And where are you coming from now? From the dormitory?
Серге́й: Нет, я уже́ был на по́чте у́тром, отправля́л друго́е письмо́, и то́лько что верну́лся отту́да. Тепе́рь я иду́ отсю́да пря́мо на ры́нок, вме́сте с тобо́й. No, I was already at the post office this morning, sending another letter, and I have just returned from there. Now I am going straight from here to the market, together with you.

Notice how the dialogue moves constantly between где, куда́, and отку́да, using the same nouns in three different case patterns depending on the question being answered.

Speaking Practice

Working alone or with a partner, choose five places from the Core Vocabulary table, and for each place, form three sentences, one answering где, one answering куда́, and one answering отку́да. Say all three sentences aloud before moving to the next place.

Коммуникати́вное зада́ние — Communicative task: plan a route

With a partner, plan a Saturday route through your own town or a town you know well, visiting at least four places. Take turns describing where each place is located, why you are going there, and where you are coming from at each stage of the route.

Самостоя́тельная пра́ктика — Solo alternative

Record yourself describing a route through a familiar neighborhood, moving through at least four places, and using где, куда́, and отку́да patterns naturally, as though you were explaining your plans to a friend.

Writing Workshop

Complete the following linked writing tasks in order. This workshop asks you to describe your own movement through a familiar place, using где, куда́, and отку́да patterns accurately and in a connected order.

Зада́ние 1 — Route outline: Before writing, list at least four places you will mention, noting for each one whether в or на governs it, and jotting a short phrase in Russian for why you are going there.

Route outline prompts
1. Куда́ я иду́ снача́ла, и почему́? 2. Где нахо́дится э́то ме́сто? 3. Куда́ я иду́ пото́м? 4. Отку́да я иду́, когда́ направля́юсь туда́? 5. Где я оказыва́юсь в конце́ дня?

Зада́ние 2 — Мой день в го́роде: Write a connected account of at least eight to ten sentences, describing a day spent moving between four or more places. Use в or на with the prepositional case for at least two locations, в or на with the accusative case for at least two destinations, and из or с with the genitive case for at least two sources.

  1. Review the Grammar and Sentence Patterns section above. Confirm which case follows в or на for location, which for destination, and which preposition and case describe source.
  2. Complete your route outline. Choose real or imagined places you can describe clearly.
  3. Draft your account in order. Move through your day place by place, connecting each stage with a word such as пото́м, снача́ла, or по́сле того́ как.
  4. Read your draft aloud once. Listen for the preposition and noun forming one rhythmic unit, as practiced in the Pronunciation Focus section.
  5. Check every в, на, из, and с against its noun. Confirm that each preposition matches the case ending you have written, and that the case matches где, куда́, or отку́да.

Спи́сок прове́рки — Final editing checklist:

  • The account moves through at least four places in a clear order.
  • At least two sentences use в or на with the prepositional case for location.
  • At least two sentences use в or на with the accusative case for destination.
  • At least two sentences use из or с with the genitive case for source.
  • Cyrillic, stress where genuinely needed, and `ё` are used correctly throughout.
  • Connectors such as пото́м, снача́ла, and по́сле того́ как link the stages of the account.
  • Punctuation, capitalization, and spelling rules follow Russian conventions.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Errands, distance, and everyday movement

In many Russian-speaking cities and towns, daily errands such as visiting a market, a post office, or a pharmacy are often described casually in terms of walking distance and nearby landmarks, rather than exact addresses. Phrases such as недалеко́ от, ря́дом с, and мину́т де́сять пешко́м are common, practical ways of giving directions among neighbors, and they vary by city, neighborhood, and personal habit rather than following one fixed national pattern.

Language Note — Отсю́да and отту́да as pronoun equivalents

The adverbs здесь and там describe where something is, while сюда́ and туда́ describe destination, and отсю́да and отту́да describe source, matching the где, куда́, and отку́да system you are building with nouns in this chapter. These adverbs let you refer back to a place already mentioned, without repeating the noun, as Серге́й does in the Model Dialogue when he says иду́ отсю́да пря́мо на ры́нок.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using the same case for location and destination

Error: Writing Я иду́ в магази́не when describing movement toward the store, using the prepositional case instead of the accusative case.

Correction: Use the accusative case for destination after a motion verb: Я иду́ в магази́н. Reserve the prepositional case, в магази́не, for describing where something is located or where an activity is taking place.

Common Mistake — Mismatching из and с with their noun

Error: Writing Я иду́ из по́чты for a noun that takes на, when с is required to reverse на.

Correction: Match the source preposition to the destination preposition already established for that noun: на по́чту reverses to с по́чты, while в апте́ку reverses to из апте́ки.

Common Mistake — Overusing full noun phrases instead of adverbs

Error: Repeating a full place name in every sentence, such as Я иду́ на ры́нок. На ры́нке я куплю́ фру́кты., when the place has already been established.

Correction: Use там, туда́, здесь, сюда́, отсю́да, and отту́да to refer back to an already established place, keeping the account natural: Я иду́ на ры́нок. Там я куплю́ фру́кты.

Chapter Review

в or на with the prepositional case, describing where a person, place, or thing is.

в or на with the accusative case, describing where a person or thing is going.

Model Dialogue — Comprehension

  1. The market is located on Pushkin Street, next to the pharmacy, and after it Тама́ра and Серге́й plan to go to the post office.
  2. Серге́й has just come from the post office, where he sent another letter that morning.

Reading

  1. Дми́трий visits, in order, the market (куда́/где), the bookstore (куда́/где), the post office (куда́/где), and the park (куда́/где), returning home at the end (куда́).
  2. At the market he buys fruit and bread, and at the bookstore he buys several books for work.
  3. He goes to the post office to send a package to his parents, and it is located next to the bookstore.
  4. Sample destination sentences: он пошёл на ры́нок and он пое́хал в це́нтр. Sample location sentences: кото́рый нахо́дится на у́лице Че́хова and кото́рая нахо́дится ря́дом с магази́ном.

Writing

Sample route account: В суббо́ту у́тром я вы́шел из до́ма и пошёл на ры́нок, кото́рый нахо́дится на у́лице Гого́ля. Там я купи́л о́вощи и хлеб. С ры́нка я пошёл в апте́ку, она́ нахо́дится в це́нтре, недалеко́ от вокза́ла. По́сле апте́ки я зашёл на по́чту, что́бы отпра́вить письмо́ роди́телям. Отту́да я пое́хал в парк, кото́рый нахо́дится ря́дом с вокза́лом, и там я немно́го отдохну́л. К ве́черу я верну́лся домо́й. Other accounts drawing accurately on this chapter's vocabulary and case patterns are also correct.
