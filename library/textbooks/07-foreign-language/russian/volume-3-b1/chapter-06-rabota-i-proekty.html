Рабо́та и прое́кты

Opening Unit II with the language of projects, processes, and results, developing aspect choice for ongoing work versus completed outcomes, instrumental case for professional roles, and formal request patterns · Speak and Write Russian: Complex Communication and Discourse

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 6 opens Unit II and turns from the interview and profile writing of Unit I toward the workplace itself. Having closed Unit I with Ната́ша's newsletter profile, you now follow her into a team meeting, where she must describe an ongoing project, report a finished result, refer to colleagues by their professional roles, and make a polite formal request of her manager. This chapter does not introduce a new case or a new tense. Instead, it develops a distinction you have used informally since Chapter 3, the difference between describing a process and reporting its result, and it develops the instrumental case specifically for naming someone's role or function at work. It also introduces the formal request pattern that professional Russian requires.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe a project or task as an ongoing process, using imperfective aspect.
  2. Report the completed result of a project or task, using perfective aspect.
  3. Distinguish process from result in the same conversation, choosing aspect deliberately rather than by habit.
  4. Name a person's professional role using the instrumental case after рабо́тать and быть.
  5. Describe means and manner in a workplace context, using the instrumental case.
  6. Make a polite formal request of a supervisor or colleague, using не могли́ бы вы and related patterns.
  7. Participate in a short team meeting, reporting on a project's status and responding to a request.

Communication Scenario

Ната́ша's newsletter profile was well received, and her manager, Татья́на Никола́евна, has now placed her on a small team preparing a report for a client. At Monday's team meeting, each person must explain what they are currently doing, what they have already finished, and what they still need from someone else. Ната́ша works with two colleagues, Дми́трий, who serves as the team's designer, and Ольга, who serves as the team's analyst. You will follow this meeting and then prepare your own short status update for an imagined or real project.

  1. You will listen to and read a short team meeting, identifying process and result in each speaker's report.
  2. You will practice naming colleagues by their professional role, using the instrumental case.
  3. You will practice making a polite formal request for help or information.
  4. You will write and deliver your own project status update, combining process, result, role, and a request.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Stress in aspect pairs

Several verbs in this chapter shift stress between their imperfective and perfective forms, and between conjugated forms of the same verb. Confirm each form before you use it, do not guess from the infinitive alone.

  • гото́вить has stress on the second syllable throughout the present tense, гото́влю, гото́вишь, гото́вит, but подгото́вить keeps the same stem stress in its own forms, подгото́влю, подгото́вишь.
  • The instrumental ending on role nouns is unstressed after a stressed stem, диза́йнером, анали́тиком, listen for the reduced vowel rather than a full, clearly articulated о.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This section develops three systems already active from earlier chapters, aspect choice for process versus result, the instrumental case for professional roles, and a formal request pattern. None of these is a brand new case, tense, or aspect category, each is a controlled application of a system you have already met.

Aspect for process and result
Я гото́влю отчёт. · Я подгото́вила отчёт.
Use the imperfective when you describe an activity as ongoing, ordinary, or in progress, with no claim about whether it is finished. Use the perfective when you report that the activity reached a completed result. The same verb pair can describe the same task from two different angles within one conversation.
Сейча́с я гото́влю отчёт для клие́нта, а вчера́ я подгото́вила пе́рвую часть.
Right now I am preparing the report for the client, and yesterday I finished the first part.
Instrumental for professional role
рабо́тать кем · быть кем
After рабо́тать and быть, name a person's profession or role in the instrumental case, not the nominative. This use of the instrumental was formulaic in earlier volumes, in Volume III it becomes a controlled, productive pattern that you should apply to any role noun the chapter activates.
Дми́трий рабо́тает диза́йнером, а О́льга рабо́тает анали́тиком.
Dmitry works as a designer, and Olga works as an analyst.
Formal request pattern
Не могли́ бы вы + infinitive?
This conditional pattern softens a request and is appropriate for a supervisor, a client, or anyone addressed with вы. It is more formal than a bare imperative, and more appropriate in a professional meeting than Ты мо́жешь. Follow it with an infinitive, not a conjugated verb.
Татья́на Никола́евна, не могли́ бы вы посмотре́ть пе́рвую часть отчёта до пя́тницы?
Tatyana Nikolaevna, could you possibly look at the first part of the report before Friday?

Model Dialogue: На совеща́нии

Context: Ната́ша, Дми́трий, and О́льга report on their project to their manager, Татья́на Никола́евна, at a Monday team meeting.

Татья́на Никола́евна: Ната́ша, на чём вы сейча́с рабо́таете? Natasha, what are you working on right now?
Ната́ша: Я гото́влю отчёт для клие́нта, вчера́ я уже́ подгото́вила пе́рвую часть, а сего́дня рабо́таю над второ́й. I am preparing the report for the client, yesterday I already finished the first part, and today I am working on the second.
Татья́на Никола́евна: Хорошо́. А Дми́трий, вы рабо́таете диза́йнером в на́шей кома́нде, что вы уже́ сде́лали? Good. And Dmitry, you work as the designer on our team, what have you already done?
Дми́трий: Я уже́ сде́лал весь диза́йн для са́йта, но сейча́с я де́лаю но́вую ве́рсию по про́сьбе О́льги. I have already done the whole design for the site, but right now I am making a new version at Olga's request.
Татья́на Никола́евна: О́льга, вы рабо́таете анали́тиком, ска́жите, когда́ бу́дет гото́в второ́й гра́фик? Olga, you work as the analyst, tell me, when will the second chart be ready?
О́льга: Я гото́влю его́ сейча́с, наде́юсь зако́нчить за́втра. I am preparing it right now, I hope to finish it tomorrow.
Ната́ша raises her hand to make a request.
Ната́ша: Татья́на Никола́евна, не могли́ бы вы посмотре́ть пе́рвую часть отчёта до пя́тницы? Tatyana Nikolaevna, could you possibly look at the first part of the report before Friday?
Татья́на Никола́евна: Да, коне́чно, я посмотрю́ её сего́дня ве́чером. Yes, of course, I will look at it this evening.

This meeting combines aspect for process and result, instrumental role reference, and a formal request, all systems already active or developed through this chapter.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Formal requests in the Russian-speaking workplace

In many formal or hierarchical workplace settings among Russian speakers, a direct imperative toward a supervisor can sound abrupt, while a conditional request such as не могли́ бы вы softens the tone without weakening the content of the request. Workplace norms vary by organization, generation, and region, so this pattern should be treated as a widely useful default rather than a universal rule for every Russian-speaking workplace.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistakes — Aspect, case, and register
  • Error: Я вчера́ гото́влю отчёт, when the result is intended. Correction: Я вчера́ подгото́вила отчёт, use the perfective for a completed result.
  • Error: Дми́трий рабо́тает диза́йнер. Correction: Дми́трий рабо́тает диза́йнером, the role noun after рабо́тать must be in the instrumental case, not the nominative.
  • Error: Не могли́ бы вы посмо́трите отчёт. Correction: Не могли́ бы вы посмотре́ть отчёт, this pattern requires an infinitive, not a conjugated verb.
