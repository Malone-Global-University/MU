Прое́кт 1, Профи́ль и интервью́

Unit I cumulative assessment, combining discourse transition, media literacy, reported speech and biography, and education and skills into a single interview based profile and oral report · Speak and Write Russian: Complex Communication and Discourse

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 5 is the first cumulative assessment chapter of Volume III. It does not introduce any new productive grammar, case, aspect, motion verb, orthographic rule, or pronunciation feature. Instead, it asks you to bring together everything you built across Chapters 1 through 4, discourse level review and register audit, reading and reporting on media, interviewing and narrating a biography with correct aspect choice and accurate use of names and patronymics, and discussing education and skills with case government, comparison, and infinitive clauses. You will interview a real or invented person, write a connected profile of that person, and deliver an oral report based on your interview. This is the moment where the separate skills from the first four chapters become one coherent piece of communication.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Conduct a structured interview using questions built from active Chapter 1 through 4 systems.
  2. Report what your interview subject said, using reported speech and correct aspect choice in past narration.
  3. Refer to your subject accurately and appropriately, using names, patronymics, and consistent address forms.
  4. Describe your subject's education, skills, and specialization, using correct case government after educational verbs.
  5. Compare two aspects of your subject's path, using comparative structures.
  6. Use infinitive clauses to describe what your subject found easy, difficult, necessary, or possible.
  7. Write a connected multi-paragraph profile and deliver a short oral report based on it.

This chapter introduces no new major productive grammar, case, aspect, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system. Every task below draws only on systems already active through Chapter 4, in keeping with the Version 5 assessment principles governing this volume.

Communication Scenario

Ната́ша's manager was pleased with her earlier work, the media summary from Chapter 2, the interview profile of Михаи́л Петро́вич from Chapter 3, and the training program comparison from Chapter 4. Now the manager has asked Ната́ша to put together a full profile piece for the company newsletter, combining a short biography, a discussion of the subject's education and skills, and a comparison of two paths that person considered. You will complete the same kind of task, either continuing with Ната́ша's project or choosing your own real or invented subject.

  1. You will plan and conduct an interview, using questions drawn from Chapters 1 through 4.
  2. You will listen to or read model interview material and identify how the speaker organizes biography, education, and comparison.
  3. You will write a connected, multi-paragraph profile combining biography, education and skills, and comparison.
  4. You will deliver a short oral report based on your profile and answer a follow up question.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review — No new targets, only cumulative accuracy

This chapter introduces no new sound, stress pattern, or spelling rule. As you prepare your interview and profile, review stress on mobile paradigms you will likely need, past tense forms of verbs such as вы́брать and реши́ть, comparative forms such as ле́гче, лу́чше, and ху́же, and names and patronymics for your chosen subject.

  • Confirm stress on any past tense form before recording or delivering your oral report, mobile stress errors are the most common source of confusion in connected speech.
  • Confirm the irregular comparatives ле́гче, лу́чше, and ху́же before using them, these forms were flagged as lexical items in Chapter 4 and must be produced from memory, not derived from a rule.

Cumulative Grammar Review

This section reviews, rather than introduces, the systems your interview and profile will draw on. Each building block below restates a pattern already active from an earlier chapter in this unit, so that you can check your own accuracy before writing.

Review, reported speech and past narration (Chapter 3)
Он сказа́л, что он учи́лся в Москве́, ...
When you report what your subject told you, choose the aspect that matches the meaning, imperfective for a repeated or background action, perfective for a single completed event with a result. Keep past tense agreement correct for your subject's gender.
Она́ рассказа́ла, что она́ роди́лась в Каза́ни, но пото́м её семья́ перее́хала в Санкт‑Петербу́рг.
She told me that she was born in Kazan, but afterward her family moved to Saint Petersburg.
Review, case government after educational verbs (Chapter 4)
учи́ться чему́ · интересова́ться чем · специализи́роваться на чём
Keep each verb's government straight as you describe your subject's education, учи́ться takes the dative of the subject studied, интересова́ться and занима́ться take the instrumental, специализи́роваться takes на plus the prepositional.
Он всегда́ интересова́лся исто́рией и специализи́ровался на междунаро́дных отноше́ниях.
He was always interested in history and specialized in international relations.
Review, comparison and infinitive clauses (Chapter 4)
интере́снее, чем ... · тру́дно/легко́ + infinitive
Use чем to introduce the second item of a comparison, and follow an impersonal expression such as тру́дно or легко́ with an infinitive, never a finite verb.
По её слова́м, вы́брать специа́льность бы́ло трудне́е, чем зако́нчить курс.
According to her, choosing a major was more difficult than finishing the course.

Пра́ктика — Find the error: Each sentence below contains one error involving a system from Chapters 1 through 4. Identify and correct each error.

  1. Она́ интересу́ется исто́рию с де́тства.
  2. Ему́ бы́ло тру́дно он вы́брал специа́льность.
  3. Э́тот курс интере́снее второ́й курс.

Model Exchange: Расскажи́те немно́го о себе́

Context: Ната́ша interviews Влади́мир Ива́нович, a senior colleague, for her newsletter profile, combining biography, education, and comparison in a single conversation.

Ната́ша: Влади́мир Ива́нович, расскажи́те немно́го о себе́, где вы учи́лись? Vladimir Ivanovich, tell me a little about yourself, where did you study?
Влади́мир Ива́нович: Я роди́лся в Но́вгороде, а учи́лся в Москве́, я специализи́ровался на эконо́мике, хотя́ снача́ла интересова́лся исто́рией. I was born in Novgorod, but I studied in Moscow, I specialized in economics, although at first I was interested in history.
Ната́ша: Почему́ вы вы́брали эконо́мику, а не исто́рию? Why did you choose economics, and not history?
Влади́мир Ива́нович: По-мо́ему, эконо́мика бо́лее практи́чная, чем исто́рия, и мне бы́ло ле́гче найти́ рабо́ту по́сле университе́та. In my opinion, economics is more practical than history, and it was easier for me to find work after university.
Ната́ша writes down his answer, then asks a follow up question.
Ната́ша: А что бы́ло са́мым тру́дным в university? And what was the most difficult thing at university?
Влади́мир Ива́нович: Са́мым тру́дным бы́ло сдать пе́рвую се́ссию, но по́сле э́того мне ста́ло намно́го ле́гче учи́ться. The most difficult thing was passing the first exam session, but after that it became much easier for me to study.

This exchange combines biographical narration with correct aspect choice, respectful reference using name and patronymic, case government after educational verbs, comparison with чем, and an infinitive clause describing difficulty, all systems already active from Chapters 1 through 4.

Listening Practice

If no audio recording is available, ask a partner, instructor, or text to speech tool to read the passage below aloud, or read it aloud to yourself slowly.

Listening script
Меня́ зову́т Еле́на Серге́евна, я роди́лась в Каза́ни, но пото́м моя́ семья́ перее́хала в Санкт‑Петербу́рг, когда́ мне бы́ло де́сять лет. Я учи́лась в Санкт‑Петербу́рге и специализи́ровалась на журнали́стике, хотя́ снача́ла интересова́лась медици́ной. По-мо́ему, журнали́стика интере́снее, чем медици́на, потому́ что она́ даёт бо́льше свобо́ды, но мои́ роди́тели ду́мали, что медици́на практи́чнее. Са́мым тру́дным для меня́ бы́ло реши́ть, кого́ слу́шать, роди́телей и́ли себя́, но тепе́рь мне ка́жется, что я вы́брала пра́вильно.
My name is Elena Sergeyevna, I was born in Kazan, but afterward my family moved to Saint Petersburg, when I was ten years old. I studied in Saint Petersburg and specialized in journalism, although at first I was interested in medicine. In my opinion, journalism is more interesting than medicine, because it gives more freedom, but my parents thought that medicine was more practical. The most difficult thing for me was deciding who to listen to, my parents or myself, but now it seems to me that I chose correctly.
  1. Where was Elena Sergeyevna born, and where did her family later move?
  2. What did she specialize in, and what was she interested in before that?
  3. According to Elena Sergeyevna, why is journalism more interesting than medicine?
  4. What did she say was the most difficult decision, and how does she feel about it now?

Writing Workshop

Complete the following linked writing tasks in order. This workshop asks you to write a connected, multi-paragraph profile of your interview subject, combining biography, education and skills, and comparison, all drawn from systems already active through Chapter 4.

Зада́ние 1 — Outline: Before writing, note your subject's basic biography, what they studied and specialized in, one comparison they made or you can make about their path, and one thing that was easy or difficult for them.

Outline prompts
1. Где и когда́ роди́лся/родила́сь ваш геро́й? 2. Где он/она́ учи́лся/учи́лась и на чём специализи́ровался/специализи́ровалась? 3. Что бы́ло ле́гче и́ли трудне́е для него́/неё? 4. Что вы мо́жете сравни́ть в его́/её пути́?

Зада́ние 2 — Профи́ль: Write a connected profile of at least twelve sentences, organized into at least two paragraphs. Include biographical narration with correct aspect choice, at least one reported speech sentence, correct case government after at least two educational verbs, at least one comparative structure, and at least one infinitive clause. Use only active case, agreement, and verb systems.

  1. Review the Cumulative Grammar Review above. Confirm which systems you plan to combine.
  2. Complete your outline. Base it on your interview notes or an invented subject.
  3. Draft your biography paragraph. Use correct past tense agreement and aspect choice.
  4. Draft your education and comparison paragraph. Confirm case government and comparative forms.
  5. Close with one reported statement and one infinitive clause. Attribute the statement clearly to your subject.

Спи́сок прове́рки — Final editing checklist:

  • Past tense forms agree correctly with the subject's gender and number.
  • Aspect choice matches the intended meaning throughout the narration.
  • Each educational verb is followed by the correct case, matching its government.
  • At least one comparative structure correctly uses чем.
  • At least one infinitive clause correctly follows an impersonal expression.
  • Names and patronymics, or another consistent form of address, are used appropriately throughout.
  • Cyrillic, stress where genuinely needed, and `ё` are used correctly throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Profiles as respectful representation

A newsletter profile, whether about a real colleague or an invented subject, should represent that person's choices and background respectfully, without turning their story into a claim about an entire country, generation, or field. Keep opinions clearly attributed to the person who holds them, using phrases such as по её слова́м or по-мо́ему, rather than presenting one person's preference as a universal fact.

Chapter Review

Biography and Reported Speech

Past narration with correct aspect choice, and reported speech introduced by сказа́л/сказа́ла, что, are reviewed and combined here rather than newly taught.

Case Government After Educational Verbs

Учи́ться, интересова́ться, and специализи́роваться, each with their established government, support the education portion of the profile.

Comparative Structures

Чем plus a comparative form lets the profile compare two paths, subjects, or choices clearly.

Infinitive Clauses

Impersonal expressions such as тру́дно and легко́, combined with an infinitive, describe what was easy, difficult, necessary, or possible for the subject.

Answer Key

Cumulative Grammar Review — Find the Error

  1. Error, интересу́ется исто́рию. Correction, интересу́ется исто́рией, интересова́ться governs the instrumental.
  2. Error, тру́дно он вы́брал специа́льность. Correction, тру́дно бы́ло вы́брать специа́льность, an impersonal expression must be followed by an infinitive, not a finite verb.
  3. Error, интере́снее второ́й курс. Correction, интере́снее, чем второ́й курс, a comparison requires чем before the second item.
