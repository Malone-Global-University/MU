Перехо́д к свя́зной ре́чи

A review and discourse-diagnostic chapter opening Volume III · Speak and Write Russian: Complex Communication and Discourse

Chapter Overview and Objectives

Chapter 1 opens Volume III, but it is not a first lesson in the usual sense. It introduces no new case, aspect, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system. Instead, it asks learners to bring the full B1 Russian they built across Volume II, cases, aspect and motion in narration, comparison and opinion, presentation and repair, into one connected diagnostic experience, so that both learner and course can see clearly what is already solid before Chapter 2 begins the work of Volume III. The chapter frames this review around a class reunion, a setting that naturally calls for memory, narration, comparison, and presentation all at once, the same combination Volume II's capstone rehearsed, now examined for how well its pieces hold together as genuinely connected discourse rather than as separate exercises.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Review and self-assess your control of case, agreement, aspect, motion, and word order across connected speech and writing.
  2. Identify where your own spoken or written Russian shifts, or fails to shift, between informal and neutral register.
  3. Interpret a longer connected exchange that mixes memory, narration, comparison, and presentation without new grammar support.
  4. Diagnose specific gaps in cohesion, reference, and register that Volume III will address chapter by chapter.
  5. Produce a short connected profile and self-assessment, in speech and in writing, using only B1 systems already active.
  6. Preview the discourse-level goals of Volume III, connected argument, media literacy, narrative precision, and register control.

This is a review and diagnostic chapter. It gathers evidence of Volume II outcomes and introduces no new major productive grammar, case, aspect, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system, in keeping with the Version 5 matrix architecture governing this volume.

Communication Scenario

Ди́ма is attending a small reunion of his old language exchange group, the same kind of group Ири́на described in Volume II. Several members he has not seen in a year are comparing how their lives have changed, what they remember from before, what happened in between, and what they think about it now. The evening moves naturally across memory, story, opinion, and short informal presentation, the exact combination Volume II built toward, but now without any single grammar point steering the conversation. Ди́ма's task, like yours in this chapter, is not to learn something new, but to notice how smoothly his known Russian carries him through a real, unscripted evening.

  1. You will read and listen to Ди́ма's reunion conversation as a diagnostic model.
  2. You will identify which B1 systems the conversation draws on and how they combine.
  3. You will audit your own case, aspect, motion, register, and cohesion control against a short checklist.
  4. You will produce your own short connected profile and self-assessment, in speech and in writing.

No new sound, stress pattern, or consonant contrast is introduced in this chapter. B1 Systems Review

This chapter does not introduce a new grammar system. Instead, it reviews, in one place, the systems Volume III will assume are solid, so that learners can audit their own control before Chapter 2 begins new B2 development. Each system below was fully developed across Volume II and remains active for independent use; discourse-level cohesion across these systems is the one dimension this chapter treats as receptive, to be developed productively across Chapters 1 through 4.

Case and agreement across a connected turn
nominative / accusative / genitive / prepositional / dative / instrumental, tracked across several sentences
A short connected turn typically shifts case several times as the topic moves from subject to object to location to recipient, and agreement must stay consistent with the noun each adjective or pronoun actually modifies, not with the noun nearest to it.
Мой ста́рый друг, о кото́ром я говори́л ра́ньше, тепе́рь рабо́тает в большо́й компа́нии в це́нтре го́рода.
My old friend, whom I mentioned earlier, now works at a large company in the center of the city.
Aspect and motion in narration
perfective for sequence + imperfective for background + motion verb for arrival or departure
Narration typically uses perfective verbs to move a story forward, imperfective verbs to describe background states or ongoing actions, and motion verbs to mark specific arrivals, departures, or journeys, all tracked through consistent pronoun and case reference.
Пока́ мы разгова́ривали, в зал вошёл ещё оди́н однокла́ссник, кото́рого я не узна́л сра́зу.
While we were talking, another classmate came into the hall, whom I did not recognize right away.
Comparison, opinion, and concession
comparatives + потому́ что + поэ́тому + хотя́
An opinion typically states a claim, supports it with a reason introduced by потому́ что, draws a conclusion with поэ́тому, and acknowledges a limitation or counterpoint with хотя́, exactly as reviewed in Volume II Chapter 18.
По-мо́ему, все мы измени́лись к лу́чшему, хотя́ не всем э́то бы́ло легко́.
In my opinion, all of us have changed for the better, although this was not easy for everyone.
Presentation, register, and repair
topic-comment order + discourse markers + ты/вы choice + question and answer repair
A longer turn organizes known information before new information, sequences its points with discourse markers such as во-пе́рвых and наконе́ц, keeps address consistent with the relationship in the scene, and handles unexpected questions with a small set of polite repair phrases before returning to the topic.
Пра́вильно ли я понима́ю, что ты тепе́рь рабо́таешь в друго́м го́роде?
Do I understand correctly that you now work in a different city?

Пра́ктика — Identify the system: For each sentence, decide which reviewed B1 system it primarily illustrates.

  1. Мы око́нчили университе́т пять лет наза́д, и с тех пор я рабо́таю в шко́ле, кото́рую сам когда́-то ко́нчил.
  2. Внача́ле все не́рвничали, а пото́м разгово́р пошёл сам собо́й.
  3. В це́лом ве́чер получи́лся тёплым, хотя́ снача́ла бы́ло немно́го неловко́.
  4. Во-пе́рвых, я хочу́ спроси́ть тебя́ о рабо́те, а во-вторы́х, о семье́.

Model Exchange: Ве́чер встре́чи вы́пускников

Context: At a small reunion, Ди́ма and his former classmate Ната́ша catch up for the first time in a year.

Ната́ша: Ди́ма! Как ока́зывается, ты то́же здесь. Ну, расскажи́, что у тебя́ но́вого? Dima! As it turns out, you're here too. Well, tell me, what's new with you?
Ди́ма: Начнём с того́, что я перее́хал в друго́й райо́н, о́коло го́да наза́д. С тех пор я рабо́таю бли́же к це́нтру, и доро́га ста́ла намно́го коро́че. Let's begin with the fact that I moved to a different neighborhood, about a year ago. Since then I work closer to the center, and my commute has become much shorter.
Ната́ша: Пра́вильно ли я понима́ю, что ты дово́лен э́тим реше́нием? Do I understand correctly that you're satisfied with this decision?
Ди́ма: Хоро́ший вопро́с. В це́лом да, хотя́, как ты по́мнишь, я всегда́ люби́л свой ста́рый двор. Но, поду́мав, я реши́л, что вре́мя ва́жнее, чем привы́чка. Good question. On the whole, yes, although, as you remember, I always loved my old courtyard. But after thinking about it, I decided that time was more important than habit.
Ната́ша: А как рабо́та? Ты по-пре́жнему пи́шешь програ́ммы? And how is work? Are you still writing programs?
Ди́ма: Да, но во-вторы́х, я хочу́ сказа́ть, что в про́шлом ме́сяце меня́ повы́сили, и тепе́рь я руковожу́ небольшо́й гру́ппой. Снача́ла бы́ло тру́дно, но постепе́нно я привы́к. Yes, but second, I want to say that last month I was promoted, and now I lead a small group. At first it was difficult, but gradually I got used to it.
Ната́ша: Поздравля́ю! Наконе́ц, скажи́, ты обрати́л внима́ние, как измени́лись остальны́е? Congratulations! Finally, tell me, have you noticed how the others have changed?
Ди́ма: Подыто́живая, как ока́зывается, почти́ все реши́ли что-то серьёзное за э́тот год, кто перее́хал, кто смени́л рабо́ту, кто на́чал учи́ться сно́ва. Как ты счита́ешь, э́то то́лько на́ша гру́ппа така́я, и́ли так у всех? To sum up, as it turns out, almost everyone decided something serious this year, some moved, some changed jobs, some started studying again. What do you think, is it only our group that's like this, or is it that way for everyone?

This exchange combines case and agreement across a connected turn, aspect and motion in narration, comparison and concession, and presentation register and repair, all reviewed from Volume II, without introducing any new case, aspect, motion, or pronunciation system. Notice that Dima's final question shifts naturally into an open discussion, the kind of connected discourse this volume develops from here.

Listening script

Наконе́ц, че́рез не́сколько ме́сяцев мне предложи́ли но́вый прое́кт, и я, коне́чно, согласи́лся. Подыто́живая, в це́лом я чу́вствую себя́ дово́льно уве́ренно, хотя́ иногда́ обнару́живаю пробе́лы, наприме́р, в специа́льной ле́ксике. First, in the first week one colleague helped me a lot, and gradually I got to know the others. Second, as it turns out, the job turned out to be more interesting than I thought at first. To sum up, on the whole I feel fairly confident, although sometimes I discover gaps, for example, in specialized vocabulary. Do I understand correctly that you're interested specifically in the first week? Good question, I'll tell you more about that.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Reunions and catching up

Reunions among classmates, coworkers, or language exchange partners are common in many Russian-speaking communities and among Russian-speaking heritage and diaspora groups, though the frequency, tone, and expected topics of such gatherings vary widely by generation, region, and social circle, and no single format should be treated as typical of Russian-speaking life everywhere.

Language Note — Diagnosis before development

Individually, case, aspect, motion, comparison, and presentation language are each systems you have already built. Noticing where they hold up smoothly under real, unscripted pressure, and where they still require extra thought, is a distinct skill from producing any one of them in isolation, and it is the primary purpose of this opening chapter, rather than any single new grammar point.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Treating review as unnecessary

Error: Skipping the diagnostic checklist or self-assessment because the material feels familiar, and moving straight into Volume III content without noticing which B1 systems still need attention.

Correction: Complete the diagnostic checklist honestly. A skipped system in Chapter 1 tends to resurface as a larger problem once Volume III begins layering new discourse structures on top of it.

Common Mistake — Losing register consistency in a longer turn

Error: Starting a conversation with ты and then switching to вы, or the reverse, without a clear reason, as attention shifts from grammar accuracy to content.

Correction: Decide on the relationship and register before speaking or writing, and check consistency during revision, as reviewed in Chapter 18's register work.

Common Mistake — Reaching for untaught structures to sound advanced

Error: Attempting a participle or a formal passive construction because the volume feels like a fresh start, even though these systems are not yet scheduled.

Correction: Use only case, aspect, motion, and register systems already active from Volume II. Chapter 1 measures independent control of known systems, not exposure to new ones; participles and verbal adverbs are introduced later in this volume, in Chapters 12 and 17.

Discourse markers, consistent register, and repair phrases organize a longer, unscripted exchange into followable discourse.

Presentation, register, and repairThe learner organizes the self-assessment with discourse markers, maintains consistent address, and handles at least one question with an appropriate repair phrase.
Overall independence and comprehensibilityThe completed spoken and written self-assessment communicates clearly across all four skills, using only case, aspect, motion, and register systems active through Volume II, appropriate to B1 Russian moving toward B2.

This chapter is a diagnostic review, not a cumulative assessment chapter. It introduces no new productive grammar, case, aspect, motion, orthographic, or pronunciation system, and instead gathers evidence of independent B1 control to guide Chapters 2 through 4.

Answer Key

B1 Systems Review — Identify the System

  1. Case and agreement, тракing case across the relative clause кото́рую сам когда́-то ко́нчил and the surrounding phrase.
  2. Aspect and motion in narration, contrast between imperfective не́рвничали and perfective пошёл.
  3. Comparison and concession, в це́лом summarizing the evening with хотя́ acknowledging early awkwardness.
  4. Presentation and repair, во-пе́рвых and во-вторы́х organizing two upcoming points.

Guided Practice — Exercise 1

  1. Aspect and motion, перее́хал and the time expression о́коло го́да наза́д.
  2. Comparison and concession, хотя́ contrasting old habit with the practical decision.
  3. Case and agreement, tracking рабо́та, гру́ппой, and the reflexive construction across the turn.
  4. Presentation and repair, подыто́живая introducing the closing question to Natasha.

Guided Practice — Exercise 2

Sample self-diagnosis, learner answers will vary. Case selection after prepositions is often solid for one-case prepositions but shakier for в/на with motion. Aspect choice in narration is usually solid for common perfective-imperfective pairs but shakier for less frequent verbs. Ты/вы consistency is usually solid in short exchanges but can slip in longer, less formal conversation. Connectors are typically solid for потому́ что and хотя́, with поэ́тому sometimes underused.

Guided Practice — Exercise 3

Sample answers, other accurate and appropriate answers are also correct. 1. За после́дний год я смени́л рабо́ту и перее́хал в друго́й райо́н. 2. Как ока́зывается, бо́льше всего́ измени́лось моё отноше́ние к рабо́те. 3. Я хоте́л бы повтори́ть аспе́кт и слова́ на -ся, потому́ что иногда́ пу́таюсь.

Listening — Comprehension

  1. The speaker begins with a memory of his first day at a new job, connected to a feeling of fear because he knew no one.
  2. He presents help from a colleague, the job turning out more interesting than expected, and a new project, introduced by во-пе́рвых, во-вторы́х, and наконе́ц.
  3. He acknowledges gaps in specialized vocabulary, introduced by хотя́.
  4. Sample answer, мне бы́ло немно́го стра́шно gives background with an imperfective-adjacent state, while мне предложи́ли но́вый прое́кт moves the account forward with a perfective verb.

Model Exchange — Comprehension

  1. Dima describes moving to a different neighborhood about a year ago, giving a shorter commute as his reason.
  2. Dima asks Natasha whether their group is unusual or whether most people go through a year of serious change, an open question inviting Natasha's own opinion rather than a simple yes or no.

Reading

  1. Natasha describes not recognizing anyone at first, then seeing Dima and immediately starting to talk, a specific remembered moment.
  2. Natasha mentions that many classmates changed their lives seriously, that everyone had a different story, and that the evening passed quickly, in that order, introduced by во-пе́рвых, во-вторы́х, and наконе́ц.
  3. Natasha acknowledges that it is a little sad they see each other so rarely, introduced with хотя́ in her closing sentence.
  4. Sample answer, пра́вильно ли я понима́ю responds to the moment when Natasha checks her understanding of Dima's feelings before he confirms them.

Writing

Sample account: Начнём с того́, что я вспомина́ю про́шлый год на рабо́те. Когда́ я то́лько начина́л, мне бы́ло тру́дно, потому́ что я ма́ло знал о прое́кте. Во-пе́рвых, я хочу́ рассказа́ть о дне, когда́ я зако́нчил свою́ пе́рвую большу́ю зада́чу. Во-вторы́х, по-мо́ему, э́то бы́ло ва́жное достиже́ние, потому́ что оно́ показа́ло мне, что я спосо́бен на бо́льшее, хотя́ пробе́лы в специа́льной ле́ксике иногда́ ещё меша́ют. Наконе́ц, я оста́лся дово́лен свои́м прогре́ссом. Подыто́живая, в це́лом я чу́вствую себя́ гора́здо увере́ннее, чем год наза́д. Пра́вильно ли я понима́ю, что вас интересу́ет и́менно рабо́та над зада́чей? Хоро́ший вопро́с, я расскажу́ подро́бнее. Other self-assessments, real or invented, that accurately combine the reviewed systems are also correct.
