食べ物と飲み物Food and Drink

Talking about foods and drinks, saying what you like, ordering, and asking for quantities · Speak and Write Japanese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Name common foods, drinks, and meal-related vocabulary.
  2. Say what you like and dislike using が好きです and が好きじゃないです.
  3. Mark the object of an action with を, including 何を食べますか and 何を飲みますか.
  4. Order food and drinks politely using ください.
  5. Ask for and state simple quantities using limited counters.
  6. List items using と for complete lists and や for partial lists.
  7. Read and write the extended katakana combinations used in loanwords, completing your core katakana.
  8. Recognize and read the eight Chapter 8 kanji in familiar words, with furigana support.

Communication Scenario

Tom has invited his study partner, Priya, over for lunch. Before she arrives, he needs to talk with Mrs. Tanaka about what to serve, since she has offered to help him shop at a small café near the station. Later, Tom and Priya order food together and talk about what they like and dislike to eat.

  1. This chapter teaches you to talk about food and drink using the object particle を you saw briefly in Chapter 7, along with new tools for preference, ordering, and quantity.
  2. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to order a simple meal, say what you like and dislike, and ask someone what they want to eat or drink.

Vocabulary Note — 好き and きらい are な-adjectives

好き ("liked") and きらい ("disliked") describe a quality, not an action, so they behave like other な-adjectives rather than like English verbs. You will study な-adjectives directly in Chapter 9; for now, use 好きです and きらいです as fixed, formulaic patterns for talking about preferences.

Script Note — Extended katakana is still one mora per combination

A combination such as ティ represents a single mora, timed the same as any other katakana symbol, even though it is written with two characters. You can see this in フィルターコーヒー ("filter coffee") from the vocabulary above: フィ・ル・タ・ー・コ・ー・ヒ・ー is eight morae, not nine. These combinations follow established, conventional spellings; do not invent new combinations to represent an English sound.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

8.1 Nが好きです — Saying what you like

Purpose: state a preference for a food, drink, or other thing.

  • わたしはさかなが好きです。Watashi wa sakana ga suki desu. — I like fish.topic は + N が + 好きです
  • トムさんはコーヒーが好きです。Tomu-san wa kōhī ga suki desu. — Tom likes coffee.
  • わたしはにくが好きじゃないです。Watashi wa niku ga suki janai desu. — I don't like meat.
Grammar Note — が, not を, with 好き

好き takes が for the thing that is liked, not を. This is because 好き is a な-adjective describing a quality of the liked thing, not a verb acting on an object. Compare this with を食べます below, where 食べます is a true action verb.

8.2 何を食べますか／何を飲みますか — Asking what someone eats or drinks

Purpose: ask and answer about the object of an action using を.

  • 何を食べますか。Nani o tabemasu ka. — What will you eat?何を + verb + か
  • ラーメンを食べます。Rāmen o tabemasu. — I'll eat ramen.
  • 何を飲みますか。Nani o nomimasu ka. — What will you drink?
  • おちゃを飲みます。Ocha o nomimasu. — I'll drink tea.

This continues the object を pattern from Chapter 7's シャワーをあびます: the thing acted upon takes を, while the action verb follows it.

8.3 ください — Ordering politely

Purpose: request an item, such as when ordering food or shopping.

  • コーヒーをください。Kōhī o kudasai. — Coffee, please.N を + ください
  • すみません、ピザをください。Sumimasen, piza o kudasai. — Excuse me, pizza, please.

ください is a formulaic request pattern in this chapter. Its full grammar, built from the て-form, will be studied directly beginning in Chapter 11.

8.4 かんたんなかず — Limited counters for ordering

Purpose: ask for or state a small quantity when ordering.

  • コーヒーを二つください。Kōhī o futatsu kudasai. — Two coffees, please.N を + [number]つ + ください
  • パンを三つください。Pan o mittsu kudasai. — Three pieces of bread, please.

This chapter uses only the general counter つ (as in ひとつ、ふたつ、みっつ) for simple ordering. The full counter system, including counters for specific object types, is introduced in Chapter 9.

8.5 と and や — Listing items

Purpose: list two or more items, either completely or as examples.

  • パンとたまごを食べます。Pan to tamago o tabemasu. — I'll eat bread and eggs.complete list: N と N
  • やさいやくだものが好きです。Yasai ya kudamono ga suki desu. — I like things like vegetables and fruit.partial list: N や N
Grammar Note — と versus や

と lists every item you have in mind, with nothing left out. や gives a few representative examples from a longer, unstated list. やさいやくだものが好きです suggests that vegetables and fruit are examples among other foods the speaker likes, not a complete inventory.

Common Mistake — Using を with 好き

Error: *わたしはさかなを好きです。

Correction: わたしはさかなが好きです。 — 好き always takes が for the liked item, never を.

Language Focus

From naming to choosing and requesting. Earlier chapters let you name things and say where they are. This chapter adds the tools to talk about what you want: stating preferences with 好き, marking objects with を, requesting items with ください, asking for quantities, and listing items completely or by example.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Tom and Priya order lunch together at a small café near the station.

STAFF いらっしゃいませ。何を食べますか。 Welcome. What will you eat?
PRIYA わたしはラーメンが好きです。ラーメンを一つください。 I like ramen. One ramen, please.
TOM わたしはにくがきらいです。やさいやたまごが好きです。ピザをください。 I don't like meat. I like things like vegetables and eggs. Pizza, please.

PRIYA おちゃをください。 Tea, please.
TOM コーヒーを二つください。あ、ケーキも好きです。ケーキを一つください。 Two coffees, please. Oh, I like cake too. One cake, please.

Writing Workshop

In this workshop you will write a short note describing your food preferences and a simple order.

  1. List your favorite and least favorite foods and drinks in English first. Include at least three foods and two drinks.
  2. Write three to four sentences describing your preferences. Use 好き and きらい, and connect at least one list with と or や.
  3. Write two to three sentences describing what you typically eat and drink for breakfast or lunch. Use object を with an action verb such as 食べます or 飲みます.
  4. Write a short café order of at least two items, including a quantity and ください.
  5. Revise and check using the checklist below before finalizing your work.

チェックリスト — Editing checklist:

  • I used が, not を, with 好き and きらい.
  • I used を correctly to mark the object of 食べます and 飲みます.
  • I used と for complete lists and や for partial, example-based lists.
  • I used a quantity with つ correctly before ください.
  • I wrote the new extended katakana combinations accurately.
  • I used the eight new kanji correctly, with furigana.
  • I ended each sentence with 。.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Saying いただきます and ごちそうさまでした

Many people in Japan say いただきます before starting a meal and ごちそうさまでした after finishing, expressions of gratitude for the meal and the effort behind it. These expressions are widely used across many households, restaurants, and settings, though how consistently they are used can vary by family, generation, and occasion.

Culture in Context — Ordering and dietary needs

Many cafés and restaurants in Japan are increasingly able to accommodate dietary preferences and restrictions, though the range of options and the ease of communicating them can vary widely by establishment, region, and type of food served. Learning to state clearly what you like, dislike, or cannot eat, as this chapter practices, is a useful skill in many everyday situations.

Common Mistakes

Common Mistake — Using を instead of が with 好き

Error: *コーヒーを好きです。

Correction: コーヒーが好きです。 — The liked item always takes が with 好き and きらい.

Common Mistake — Omitting を before an action verb

Error: *パンを食べます。 written correctly, but *パン食べます。 without a particle is not natural in careful written Japanese at this level.

Correction: パンを食べます。 — Keep the object particle を between the object and the verb in written practice at this stage.

Common Mistake — Using や for a complete list

Error: Using や to list every single item on an order, implying there are unstated items when there are not.

Correction: Use と when the list is complete, such as ピザとコーヒーをください。 (exactly these two items, nothing else).
