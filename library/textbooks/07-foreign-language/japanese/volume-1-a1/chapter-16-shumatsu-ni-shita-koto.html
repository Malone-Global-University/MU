週末にしたことCompleted Past Events

Plain past expansion, sequencing with たり, reasons with から, contrast with でも, and connected past-tense accounts · Speak and Write Japanese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe weekend events, errands, visits, and meals using vocabulary for common past-time activities.
  2. Form and use the plain past (た-form) affirmative and negative for verbs, in addition to the polite past you have already practiced.
  3. List two or more activities using 〜たり〜たりします without implying a complete or exhaustive list.
  4. Give a reason for a past action using から.
  5. Introduce a contrast between two ideas using でも at the start of a sentence.
  6. Omit recoverable subjects and objects naturally when narrating a short sequence of past events.
  7. Read and write short diary-style accounts that connect several weekend activities.
  8. Recognize and read the ten new kanji introduced in this chapter, with furigana support where required.

Communication Scenario

It is Monday morning, and Tom and his classmate Yuki are back at the university. Over coffee before class, they talk about what they did over the weekend: errands, a visit to a friend's house, a meal out, and a bit of rest. Aki, who is still getting used to the neighborhood, listens and asks questions about what everyone did. This chapter opens Unit IV by moving from present-tense routines into connected accounts of things that have already happened.

  1. This chapter begins Unit IV. It expands the plain past introduced receptively in Chapter 14 and reviewed in Chapter 15 into a productive system for narrating completed events.
  2. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to describe what you did last weekend, connect several actions naturally, give simple reasons, and introduce a contrast, in both speech and writing.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — The plain past た-form and vowel length

Many plain past forms end in a short, crisp た or だ mora. Be careful not to lengthen this final vowel, since vowel length can distinguish meaning elsewhere in Japanese even though it does not change meaning here. Practice contrasting the plain past ending with the polite past ending in rhythm and length.

  • 行った いった — went (plain past, geminate っ before た)
  • 見た みた — saw; watched (plain past, single mora た)

Also review the falling intonation typical of a completed statement, and the slightly rising, softening intonation often used before でも when introducing a contrast in conversation.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This section introduces the plain past for productive use, the たり pattern for listing activities, reason-giving with から, contrast with でも, and natural omission in short narratives.

1. The plain past (た-form)

Purpose: to state that an action was completed, using the plain style that also supports patterns such as たり and から.

  • 友だちの家に行った。I went to my friend's house.行く→行った [Group 1]
  • 映画を見た。I watched a movie.見る→見た [Group 2]
  • 昨日は勉強しなかった。I didn't study yesterday.plain past negative

The plain past follows the same base as the て-form you already know: verbs that form 行って also form 行った, and verbs that form 食べて also form 食べた.

2. Listing activities with たり〜たりします

Purpose: to mention two or more activities from a weekend or period of time without claiming the list is complete.

  • 週末は買い物をしたり、映画を見たりしました。Over the weekend, I did things like shopping and watching a movie.
  • 友だちの家で話したり、食べたりしました。At my friend's house, we did things like talking and eating.

Unlike a plain list with と, たり signals that other unmentioned activities may also have happened.

3. Giving a reason with から

Purpose: to explain why a past action happened.

  • 疲れていたから、早く寝た。Because I was tired, I went to bed early.
  • 天気がよかったから、公園に行った。Because the weather was nice, I went to the park.

4. Contrast with でも

Purpose: to introduce a sentence that contrasts with the idea just stated.

  • 土曜日は忙しかった。でも、楽しかった。Saturday was busy. But it was fun.
  • 買い物に行きたかった。でも、時間がなかった。I wanted to go shopping. But I didn't have time.
Building Block — A connected weekend account
Time word + たり-list + でも-contrast + から-reason + closing feeling
A short account can move naturally from what happened, to a contrast, to why something happened, ending with a feeling.
土曜日の朝、買い物をしたり、友だちに会ったりしました。でも、夜は忙しかったから、早く寝ました。楽しかったです。
Saturday morning, I did things like shopping and meeting a friend. But since the evening was busy, I went to bed early. It was fun.
Language Note — Omission in narration

Once a time frame such as 週末は or 土曜日の朝 has been established, later sentences in the same account normally omit the subject if it remains the speaker. Repeating 私は in every sentence of a diary-style account sounds unnatural; the connected examples above omit it after the first mention.

Common Mistake — Using たり with only one activity

Error: *映画を見たりしました。 (with only one activity mentioned and no other implied)

Correction: 映画を見た。 or 映画を見たり、買い物をしたりしました。 — Use plain たり with at least two listed activities, or use a simple plain or polite past sentence when only one activity is being described.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Tom, Yuki, and Aki talk before class on Monday morning about their weekends.

ユキ 週末、何をしましたか。 What did you do over the weekend?
トム 土曜日の朝、買い物をしたり、友だちの家に行ったりしました。夜はお店で食べました。 Saturday morning, I did things like shopping and going to a friend's house. In the evening I ate at a restaurant.
アキ 楽しそうですね。日曜日は何をしましたか。 That sounds fun. What did you do on Sunday?
トム 日曜日は疲れていたから、家で映画を見た。でも、昼はちょっと勉強した。 On Sunday I was tired, so I watched a movie at home. But in the afternoon I studied a little.
ユキ 私は週末、あまり出かけなかった。でも、うちで本を読んだり、料理をしたりした。よかったよ。 I didn't go out much over the weekend. But at home I did things like reading and cooking. It was good.
アキ 二人とも、いい週末でしたね。 Both of you had a good weekend, it seems.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. What did Tom do on Saturday, and what did he do on Sunday?
  2. Why did Tom watch a movie on Sunday instead of going out?
  3. What did Yuki do over the weekend, and how did she describe it?

Reading Practice

Read the following diary entry and short message, then answer the questions below.

日記にっき — Aki's diary entry

昨日きのうは土曜日どようびでした。朝あさ、近ちかくの店みせで買かい物ものをしたり、公園こうえんを歩あるいたりしました。昼ひるはトムさんと会あって、いっしょに食たべました。夜よるは少すこし疲つかれていたから、早はやく家いえに帰かえりました。でも、とても楽たのしかったです。

メッセージ — A short message

週末はどうでしたか。私は忙しかったから、あまり休まなかった。でも、日曜日の夜は映画を見たり、家族と話したりした。今週も頑張りましょう。

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. According to Aki's diary, what did she do in the morning, at noon, and at night on Saturday?
  2. Why did Aki return home early, and how did she describe the day overall?
  3. According to the message, why didn't the writer rest much, and what did they do on Sunday night?

Writing Workshop

In this workshop you will write a short diary-style account of a recent day or weekend.

  1. Choose a day. Pick yesterday, last Saturday, or a typical recent weekend.
  2. List your ideas in English first. Note two or three activities, one reason, and one feeling or contrast.
  3. Write an opening sentence naming the day using a time word such as 昨日 or 週末.
  4. Write a sentence listing two activities using たり〜たりしました.
  5. Write a sentence giving a reason for something you did, using から.
  6. Write a sentence introducing a contrast using でも.
  7. Write a closing sentence giving your overall feeling about the day.
  8. Revise and check using the checklist below before finalizing your work.

チェックリスト — Editing checklist:

  • I named the day clearly at the start of my account.
  • I used たり〜たりしました correctly with at least two activities.
  • I gave a reason accurately using から.
  • I introduced a contrast using でも.
  • I omitted the subject naturally after the first sentence where appropriate.
  • I used this chapter's kanji correctly, with furigana where still required.
  • I ended each sentence with 。 and used consistent style within the passage.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Talking about the weekend

Asking about someone's weekend is a common way to open a casual conversation among classmates, colleagues, and friends in many Japanese-speaking settings, much as it is in many communities worldwide. Responses are often brief and modest rather than highly detailed, with more information shared if the listener shows continued interest.

Regional Note — Weekend activities vary widely

What people typically do on weekends varies by region, generation, occupation, and personal circumstance. The activities in this chapter's dialogue and readings are representative examples for practicing the target grammar, not a description of how all Japanese speakers spend their weekends.

Register Note — Plain style among peers

Notice that Yuki and Tom use plain-style sentences with each other in casual conversation, while diary entries and messages to a broader audience may mix plain and polite style depending on the writer's habits. This chapter introduces plain past production for grammar building and for informal peer conversation; it does not require learners to abandon the polite style as their general default in mixed or unfamiliar company.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing たり with plain listing と

Error: *買い物と映画を見たりしました。 (mixing と and たり in the same list)

Correction: 買い物をしたり、映画を見たりしました。 — Use たり consistently on each listed activity rather than combining it with と in the same construction.

Common Mistake — Forgetting the negative plain past forms

Error: *昨日、勉強しないだった。

Correction: 昨日、勉強しなかった。 — The plain past negative replaces the final い of the plain negative with かった, rather than adding だった after ない.

Common Mistake — Overusing でも as a general connector

Error: Using でも at the start of nearly every sentence in an account, regardless of whether a contrast is actually present.

Correction: Reserve でも for moments where the following idea genuinely contrasts with what was just said, as in the model dialogue and readings.

Practical Performance Task

パフォーマンスタスク

週末にしたこと — A connected account of a recent weekend

You will produce a connected spoken and written account of a recent weekend or day, using the systems introduced in this chapter.

  • Written: Write at least eight sentences describing a recent weekend, including at least one たり〜たりしました sentence, one から reason, one でも contrast, and natural omission of the subject after the first sentence.
  • Spoken: Tell a partner or instructor about your weekend aloud, using at least one plain-style sentence appropriate to a casual conversation with a peer.
  • Interaction: Answer at least two follow-up questions from a partner about your account.
  • Script check: Hand-write at least six of this chapter's ten kanji from memory, and read aloud a short diary entry furnished by your instructor.

Assessment focus: accurate plain past formation; correct use of たり for listing, から for reason, and でも for contrast; natural omission in a connected account; accurate use of this chapter's vocabulary and kanji; and an appropriately chosen register for the situation.
