復習２：実用的な一日Unit II Review: A Practical Day

Bringing together home, routine, food, and shopping into one connected day · Speak and Write Japanese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe a home, neighborhood, and daily schedule using ある／いる, location particles, and the polite verb system from Chapters 6–7.
  2. Talk about food, drink, and preferences, and place a simple order, using vocabulary and patterns from Chapter 8.
  3. Describe and compare shopping items using い-adjective and な-adjective forms, より, ほう, and いちばん from Chapter 9.
  4. Read and write practical texts that combine home, routine, food, and shopping information, including maps, schedules, menus, and receipts.
  5. Recognize, read, and handwrite the Unit II kanji from Chapters 6–9 in familiar words, with furigana support.
  6. Select and use the correct counter among つ, 個, and 枚 for common items.
  7. Complete an integrated spoken and written account of one full practical day, connecting home, routine, meals, and a purchase.

Communication Scenario

It is Saturday. Tom is writing a message to a friend back home describing an ordinary day in his new neighborhood: where he lives, what his day looks like, what he eats, and a small purchase he made at a shop near the station. This chapter's tasks follow Tom through that day, and you will complete parallel tasks describing your own day.

  1. This chapter does not introduce new grammar, vocabulary, or kanji. It reviews and connects everything from Chapters 6 through 9.
  2. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to produce one connected spoken account and one connected written account of a full practical day, and you will complete listening and reading tasks that mix all four Unit II topics.

Review Note — Connecting the four topics

Notice how these four vocabulary sets connect naturally: you wake up and move around your home and neighborhood (Chapter 6) according to your daily routine (Chapter 7), you eat and drink at various points in that routine (Chapter 8), and you sometimes stop at a shop to buy something you need (Chapter 9). This chapter's tasks ask you to move between all four domains in a single connected account, the way a real day actually unfolds.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Focus — Numbers, times, and prices together

This unit combined several number-based systems: clock time (時／分／半), quantities with the general counter つ, and prices with 円／百／千／万. Say the following aloud, tapping once per mora, and pay attention to the moraic ん and the sound changes in compound numbers:

  • 七時半 — しちじはん — 7:30
  • 三つ — みっつ — three (general counter, with a geminate consonant)
  • 三百円 — さんびゃくえん — 300 yen
  • 千二百円 — せんにひゃくえん — 1,200 yen

Keep phrase-level rhythm smooth across these longer number phrases rather than pausing between each digit.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Tom calls his friend Priya on the phone on Saturday evening to tell her about his day.

PRIYA トムさん、今日はどうでしたか。 Tom, how was today?
TOM よかったですよ。朝七時におきて、うちのちかくのこうえんをさんぽしました。 It was good. I got up at 7:00 and took a walk in the park near my house.
PRIYA いいですね。あさごはんは何を食べましたか。 Nice. What did you eat for breakfast?
TOM パンとたまごを食べて、おちゃを飲みました。それから、たなかさんとみせへ行きました。 I ate bread and eggs, and drank tea. After that, I went to a shop with Mrs. Tanaka.
PRIYA 何を買いましたか。 What did you buy?
TOM くろいかばんを買いました。あかいかばんよりやすくて、じょうぶでしたから。 I bought a black bag. It was cheaper than the red one, and it was sturdy.
PRIYA いくらでしたか。 How much was it?
TOM 三千円でした。プレゼントにノートも二個買いました。 It was 3,000 yen. I also bought two notebooks as a gift.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. What time did Tom get up, and what did he do first?
  2. What did Tom eat and drink for breakfast?
  3. What did Tom buy, why did he choose it, and how much did it cost?
  4. Identify at least one example each of a location expression, a time expression, a food/preference expression, and a comparison expression in this dialogue.

Writing Assessment

In this workshop you will write a connected account of one practical day that draws on all four Unit II chapters.

  1. Plan your day in English first. Note where you live, at least three sequenced activities with times, what you ate or drank, and one item you compared or bought.
  2. Write two to three sentences describing your home or neighborhood. Use at least one location expression with あります or います.
  3. Write three to four sentences describing your routine in order. Use time expressions and the connector それから.
  4. Write one to two sentences about food or drink, using 好きです or a request pattern.
  5. Write two to three sentences describing a comparison or purchase, using an adjective form, より or ほう, a counter, and a price.
  6. Revise and check using the checklist below before finalizing your work.

チェックリスト — Editing checklist:

  • I described my home or neighborhood using あります or います correctly.
  • I sequenced at least three routine activities with accurate time expressions.
  • I used まいにち, ときどき, or a similar frequency word appropriately.
  • I described a food or drink preference or made a request correctly.
  • I compared two items using より or ほう, or identified the best of several with いちばん.
  • I chose an appropriate counter (つ, 個, or 枚) and wrote an accurate price.
  • I used Unit II kanji correctly, with furigana.
  • I ended each sentence with 。 and organized my account in a clear order.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Everyday routines across communities

The daily schedule, meal habits, neighborhood layout, and shopping patterns described in this unit reflect one plausible everyday routine, not a single official Japanese lifestyle. Work hours, meal timing, shopping frequency, and neighborhood design vary widely by region, household, occupation, and generation across Japan. When you describe your own day in Japanese, describe your actual routine rather than assuming it must match a textbook model.

Culture in Context — Talking about your day with others

Sharing small details about an ordinary day, such as what you ate or bought, is a common way to maintain a relationship in many everyday Japanese conversations, whether by phone, in person, or through a written message. Keeping the polite です／ます style you have used throughout this unit remains appropriate for most everyday relationships at this stage of your studies.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing あります and います

Error: *へやにねこがあります。

Correction: へやにねこがいます。 — Use います for people and animals, and あります for objects and things without independent movement.

Common Mistake — Dropping the time particle に

Error: *七時おきます。

Correction: 七時におきます。 — Clock times normally take に before the verb, as taught in Chapter 7.

Common Mistake — Using じゃない with an い-adjective

Error: *安いじゃないです。

Correction: 安くないです。 — As reviewed in Chapter 9, い-adjectives form their negative with くない, never じゃない.

Practical Performance Task

パフォーマンスタスク

わたしのじつようてきないちにち — My practical day

You will produce a connected spoken and written account of one full practical day, integrating home, routine, food and drink, and shopping.

  • Written: Write a message of at least ten sentences describing your home or neighborhood, your daily routine in order with times, a food or drink preference or request, and a comparison or purchase with a counter and price.
  • Spoken: Give a spoken account of the same day from memory, without reading directly from your written text.
  • Interaction: Answer at least four questions a partner or instructor asks about your day, using vocabulary and grammar from any Unit II chapter.
  • Script check: Hand-write at least fifteen of the thirty-one Unit II kanji from memory, and read aloud a short mixed-script passage furnished by your instructor.

Assessment focus: accurate use of location and existence expressions; correct polite verb forms and time expressions; accurate food/drink preference and request language; correct adjective conjugation and comparison; correct counter choice and prices; accurate Unit II kanji with furigana; and a clearly organized, connected account of a full day.
