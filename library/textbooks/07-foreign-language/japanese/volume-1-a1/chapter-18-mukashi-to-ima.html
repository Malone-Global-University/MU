昔と今Then and Now

Ongoing and result states with ている, change with なる, 前は/今は, and past-present comparison · Speak and Write Japanese: Foundations of Practical Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe habits, technology, and town life using vocabulary for change, comparison, and the passage of time.
  2. Use ている to describe an ongoing action or a resulting state, in addition to its earlier use for habitual action.
  3. Describe change using なる with adjectives and nouns.
  4. Contrast the past and the present using 前は and 今は.
  5. Compare life, places, and habits across time in short connected sentences.
  6. Read and write short texts that describe how something has changed.
  7. Recognize and read the eleven new kanji introduced in this chapter, with furigana support where required.

Communication Scenario

Tom has returned from his trip to Okinawa and is now talking with Yuki's grandmother, who has lived in the same neighborhood for many years. She talks about how the town has changed since she was young, and Tom compares this with what he has noticed since arriving in Japan. Later, Tom writes a short passage for a class project comparing life now with life in the past. This chapter continues Unit IV by moving from a single trip account into broader reflection and comparison across time.

  1. This chapter builds on the ている pattern you have already seen for ongoing action, extending it to describe a resulting state and a background situation.
  2. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to describe how a place, habit, or situation has changed and compare it with how things are now.

Script Note — 前 and 後 carry more than one reading

前 and 後 already appeared in Chapter 17 in the words 前に and 後で, meaning "before" and "after" within a single sequence of events. In this chapter, the same characters appear in 前は and read as あと or ご in other words, but with a different function: comparing an earlier period of time with the present. Learn each word as its own unit rather than assuming the character always carries the same meaning or reading.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Long vowels and vowel-length contrast in comparison words

Several important words in this chapter distinguish meaning through vowel length. Practice these pairs carefully so that a comparison sentence is not misunderstood.

  • 昔 むかし — long ago (short vowels throughout)
  • 変わります かわります — to change (short vowel in か; do not lengthen it)
  • 便利 べんり — convenient (moraic ん held for a full mora before り)

Also review the falling intonation used when making a reflective statement about the past, such as ending a sentence with 〜でしたね or a similar softened past-tense ending in natural speech.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This section introduces ている for ongoing action and resulting states, change with なる, contrast with 前は/今は, and past-present comparison.

1. ている for ongoing action and resulting states

Purpose: to describe an action in progress, a habitual action, or a state that continues as the result of a past action.

  • 今、この町に住んでいます。I am living in this town now.て-form + いる: ongoing/result state
  • スマホを使っています。I use (am using) a smartphone.
  • 町はとても変わっています。The town has changed a great deal (and remains changed).

You have already used ている for habitual and routine actions since Chapter 7. In this chapter, notice that the same form can also describe a state that continues after a change has taken place, such as 変わっています describing the town's current, already-changed condition rather than the moment of changing.

Common Mistake — Confusing 変わります with 変わっています

Error: *町は変わります。 (intended: "The town has changed" as a present description)

Correction: 町は変わっています。 — Use ている to describe the current, already-changed state of the town. The plain nonpast 変わります describes a future or general change, not a present resulting condition.

2. Describing change with なる

Purpose: to state that something has become a new condition.

  • 便利になりました。It has become convenient.
  • 町が大きくなりました。The town has become bigger.
  • スマホを使う人が多くなりました。The number of people who use smartphones has grown.

A な-adjective or noun combines with に before なります, while an い-adjective changes its ending to く before なります. The past form なりました is common because change is usually reported as something that has already happened.

3. 前は and 今は for contrast

Purpose: to contrast an earlier period of time with the present.

  • 前は静かでした。今はにぎやかです。Before, it was quiet. Now, it is lively.
  • 前はテレビをよく見ました。今はあまり見ません。Before, I used to watch TV often. Now, I don't watch it much.

前は and 今は often appear as a paired set across two sentences, with the first sentence in the past and the second in the nonpast. This pairing is one of the clearest ways to build a two-part comparison at this level.

4. Comparing across time

Purpose: to combine time words, change expressions, and contrast into a short comparison.

  • 子供のとき、この近くに店がありませんでした。今は多いです。When I was a child, there were no shops nearby. Now there are many.
  • 昔は電話が少なかったですが、今はみんなスマホを持っています。Long ago there were few telephones, but now everyone has a smartphone.

These comparisons combine forms you already know, such as adjective past forms from Chapter 12 and existence expressions from Chapter 6, with this chapter's new contrast and change patterns.

Building Block — A short then-and-now comparison
Time frame + past description + 前は/今は contrast + change with なる + present resulting state with ている
A practical comparison can name a time frame, describe how something used to be, contrast it with how it is now, describe the change itself, and describe the present state that resulted from that change.
子供のとき、この町は小さかったです。前は店が少なかったですが、今は多いです。町はとても便利になりました。今、多くの人がこの近くに住んでいます。
When I was a child, this town was small. Before, there were few shops, but now there are many. The town has become very convenient. Now, many people live in this area.
Language Note — 昔 versus 前は

昔 refers to a more distant, general past, often "long ago" or "in the old days," while 前は can refer to any earlier point being compared with now, including a fairly recent one. Both are useful for comparison, but they are not always interchangeable.

Grammar Practice

  1. Write a sentence describing where you live now, using 住んでいます.
  2. Write a sentence describing something that has become convenient or has become another new state, using なりました.
  3. Write a two-sentence contrast using 前は and 今は.
  4. Write a sentence describing a resulting present state using ている.
  5. Write a short comparison of two or more sentences about how a place or habit has changed.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Tom talks with Yuki's grandmother about how the neighborhood has changed since she was young.

トム おばあさんは、この町に長く住んでいますか。 Have you lived in this town for a long time?
祖母 ええ、四十年ぐらい住んでいます。前は、ここは静かな町でした。 Yes, I've lived here for about forty years. Before, this was a quiet town.
トム 今はにぎやかですね。何が一番変わりましたか。 It's lively now, isn't it? What has changed the most?
祖母 店ですね。昔は店が少なかったですが、今は多いです。それから、便利になりました。 The shops, I think. Long ago there were few shops, but now there are many. Also, it has become convenient.
トム スマホを使う人も増えましたか。 Has the number of people using smartphones increased too?
祖母 はい、増えましたね。子供のとき、電話はありませんでした。今はみんな持っています。 Yes, it has increased. When I was a child, there were no telephones. Now everyone has one.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. About how long has Yuki's grandmother lived in this town?
  2. According to her, what has changed the most about the town?
  3. What does she say about telephones when she was a child, compared with now?

Writing Workshop

In this workshop you will write a short comparison describing how a place, habit, or object has changed over time.

  1. Choose a topic. Pick a real place, habit, or piece of technology that you know has changed.
  2. List your ideas in English first. Note how it was before, how it is now, and one specific change.
  3. Write an opening sentence naming the topic and a time frame, such as 子供のとき or 昔.
  4. Write a sentence with 前は describing the earlier situation.
  5. Write a sentence with 今は describing the present situation.
  6. Write a sentence with なりました describing the change itself.
  7. Write a closing sentence with ている describing a present resulting state.
  8. Revise and check using the checklist below before finalizing your work.

チェックリスト — Editing checklist:

  • I named the topic and a clear time frame.
  • I used 前は and 今は to build a contrast.
  • I used なりました to describe a change.
  • I used ている to describe an ongoing action or a resulting state.
  • I used this chapter's vocabulary and kanji accurately, with furigana where still required.
  • I ended each sentence with 。 and used consistent style within the passage.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Talking with older family members about the past

Asking older family members or community members about how a place has changed is a common and welcomed way to practice language while learning about local history. Listeners should stay attentive and ask simple follow-up questions rather than interrupting with corrections, since the purpose of the conversation is connection as well as comprehension.

Regional Note — Change happens differently in different places

The pace and nature of change described in this chapter, such as the growth of shops or the spread of smartphones, varies widely between cities, towns, and rural areas, and between different periods of time. The examples in this chapter are for practicing the target grammar, not a claim about how every town in Japan has changed.

Register Note — Reflective and conversational tone

Conversations comparing the past and present, especially with an older relative or acquaintance, often use a warm, polite, and reflective tone. The polite forms introduced in earlier chapters remain the appropriate default here, even when the topic feels personal or nostalgic.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Forgetting に before なります with a な-adjective or noun

Error: *便利なりました。

Correction: 便利になりました。 — A な-adjective or a noun requires に before なります, even though the plain dictionary form of the adjective does not otherwise use に.

Common Mistake — Using the plain adjective form before なります

Error: *町が大きいなりました。

Correction: 町が大きくなりました。 — An い-adjective changes its final い to く before なります.

Common Mistake — Mismatching 前は and 今は with the wrong tense

Error: *前は静かです。今はにぎやかでした。

Correction: 前は静かでした。今はにぎやかです。 — 前は normally pairs with a past-tense description, and 今は normally pairs with a nonpast description of the present.
