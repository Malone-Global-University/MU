Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Integrate the research question, draft, revision record, presentation, and portfolio from Chapters 16 through 19 into one coherent final project.
  2. Write a final project statement that connects the project's original question to what the finished work ultimately demonstrates.
  3. Deliver a short final defense that explains how the project changed between its first conception and its completed form.
  4. Respond to challenging follow-up questions about the project's method, limitations, and remaining open questions.
  5. Write a closing reflective statement assessing the project as a whole, rather than any single chapter's contribution alone.
  6. Apply a last full-project consistency check across grammar, register, script, and formatting.
  7. Submit a single, independently produced final project representing C2-level control across research, revision, presentation, and reflection.
  8. Distinguish active production expectations from receptive recognition across all systems drawn from the volume.

Communication Scenario

Emma's portfolio from Chapter 19 is complete, but 田中先生 has asked for one more thing before the departmental review closes: a short final submission packet that ties the whole project together. It should include a one-page final project statement connecting her original research question about dialect expressions to what her finished materials actually show, and it should prepare her for a short final defense in which she may be asked to explain how the project changed since Chapter 16, what its limitations are, and what she would investigate next. This is her last piece of coursework in the Speak and Write Japanese series, and she wants it to reflect everything she has learned.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Sustaining control under unscripted questioning

This chapter introduces no new pronunciation targets. Its purpose is to confirm that mora timing, length contrasts, moraic ん, phrase-level pitch, and formal presentation prosody, all consolidated in earlier chapters, remain stable when a learner must answer an unscripted defense question rather than deliver a rehearsed passage. Maintaining pace and clarity under the pressure of an unplanned question is itself part of C2-level spoken control.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter authorizes all active Volume V systems: precision grammar, collocation, style, keigo, academic and professional discourse, coordination, critique, digital discourse, variation, mediation, research, revision, presentation, and portfolio reflection. No new grammar paradigm is introduced; the focus is combining these systems into a single coherent final synthesis.

1. Framing the final project statement

Purpose: to open a final statement by restating the original question and previewing what the finished work shows.

  • 本プロジェクトは、方言表現が標準語話者にどう受け止められるかという問いから出発し、最終的には、地域差に対する意識が世代によって異なることを示す結果に至った。This project began with the question of how dialect expressions are perceived by standard-language speakers, and ultimately arrived at a result showing that awareness of regional difference differs by generation.〜という問いから出発し、〜という結果に至った (statement-opening frame)

2. Explaining how the project changed

Purpose: to describe a substantive change between the project's early and final form, and why it happened.

  • 当初は語彙の違いのみに焦点を当てていたが、指導教員の助言を受けて、話者の意識という観点を加えることにした。At first I was focusing only on lexical differences, but after receiving my supervisor's advice, I decided to add the perspective of speaker awareness.当初は〜が、〜を受けて、〜ことにした (project evolution)

3. Responding to a defense question about limitations

Purpose: to acknowledge a genuine limitation without undermining the project's overall value.

  • ご指摘の通り、調査対象が一つの地域に限られているという限界はありますが、その分、詳細な分析ができたと考えております。As you point out, there is the limitation that the survey subjects were limited to one region, but for that reason, I believe I was able to carry out a detailed analysis.ご指摘の通り〜という限界はありますが、〜と考えております (defense response, acknowledge-and-reframe)

4. Naming a remaining task for future study

Purpose: to name a specific, credible direction for further investigation.

  • 今後の課題としては、他の地域との比較調査を行い、今回の結果がどこまで一般化できるかを検証することが挙げられる。One remaining task is to conduct a comparative survey with other regions and verify to what extent this project's results can be generalized.今後の課題としては〜ことが挙げられる (forward-looking, specific)

5. Closing with an overall assessment

Purpose: to summarize what the project as a whole demonstrates about the learner's own development, not only about the research topic.

  • 以上を総括すると、本プロジェクトは、研究テーマそのものへの理解だけでなく、資料収集から発表、そして振り返りに至るまで、一貫した論旨を保ちながら日本語で発信する力が身についたことを示している。To sum up, this project shows not only an understanding of the research theme itself, but also that I have developed the ability to communicate in Japanese while maintaining a coherent argument from data collection through presentation and reflection.以上を総括すると〜ことを示している (final capstone summary)
Building Block — The final project synthesis pattern

[restate the original question and the final result] + [describe how the project changed and why] + [acknowledge a genuine limitation without undermining the whole] + [name a specific remaining task] + [close with an overall assessment of what the project demonstrates]

This pattern draws together the research-framing language from Chapter 16, the revision narrative from Chapter 17, the concession and correction language from Chapter 18, and the reflective annotation pattern from Chapter 19, reorganized into a single closing synthesis appropriate for a final statement or oral defense.

本プロジェクトは、方言表現に対する意識が世代によってどう異なるかという問いから出発し、話者へのインタビューを通してその違いを具体的に示す結果に至った。当初は語彙の違いのみに注目していたが、指導教員の助言を受けて、話者自身の意識という観点を加えることにした。調査対象が一つの地域に限られているという限界はあるが、その分、詳細な分析ができたと考えている。今後の課題としては、他地域との比較を通して結果の一般化を検証することが挙げられる。以上を総括すると、本プロジェクトは、研究テーマへの理解だけでなく、一貫した論旨を保ちながら日本語で発信する力が身についたことを示していると考える。

This project began with the question of how awareness of dialect expressions differs by generation, and arrived at a result concretely showing that difference through interviews with speakers. At first I focused only on lexical differences, but after my supervisor's advice, I added the perspective of speakers' own awareness. There is the limitation that the survey subjects were limited to one region, but for that reason I believe I was able to carry out a detailed analysis. A remaining task is to verify the generalizability of the results through comparison with other regions. To sum up, I believe this project shows not only an understanding of the research theme but also that I have developed the ability to communicate in Japanese while maintaining a coherent argument.

Grammar Practice

  1. Write one sentence framing your project using 〜という問いから出発し、〜という結果に至った.
  2. Write one sentence explaining how your project changed using 当初は〜が、〜を受けて、〜ことにした.
  3. Write one sentence responding to a limitation using ご指摘の通り〜という限界はありますが、〜と考えております.
  4. Write one sentence naming a remaining task using 今後の課題としては〜ことが挙げられる.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — The role of the final defense in academic and professional settings

In many Japanese academic and professional review settings, a short question-and-answer period following a final presentation is treated as an ordinary and expected part of demonstrating competence, not as an adversarial test. A calm, specific response that acknowledges a real limitation is generally received better than a defensive one that denies any weakness, provided the response also reaffirms the value of the work honestly rather than dismissively.

Regional Note — Final review formats vary by institution

The length, format, and expected components of a final review, defense, or capstone submission differ across universities, language programs, and workplaces. The short defense modeled in this chapter reflects one plausible departmental convention rather than a single fixed standard that applies to every academic or professional context.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Denying limitations instead of reframing them

Error: Responding to a question about a project's limitation by insisting the project has no weaknesses.

Correction: Acknowledge the limitation honestly using ご指摘の通り〜という限界はありますが, then reframe constructively, as modeled in the Grammar section above.

Common Mistake — Treating the closing reflection as a topic summary

Error: Writing a closing reflection that only restates facts about the research topic rather than assessing the learner's own development.

Correction: Close by naming what the whole project shows about your own communicative growth, following the total-project synthesis pattern in this chapter.

Common Mistake — Letting the final packet drift out of consistency with the portfolio

Error: Writing new final-packet documents with different spellings, furigana treatment, or file naming than the rest of the Chapter 19 portfolio.

Correction: Run the full-packet consistency audit described in the Script and Typography Focus section before submission.
