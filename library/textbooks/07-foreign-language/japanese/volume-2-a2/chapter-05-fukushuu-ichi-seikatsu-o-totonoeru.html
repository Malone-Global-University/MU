復習１：生活を整えるUnit I Assessment: Getting Life in Order

Integrated review of recent life, relationships, obligations, and planning · Speak and Write Japanese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Combine vocabulary and grammar from Chapters 1 through 4 to describe your recent life, relationships, obligations, and plans in one connected account.
  2. Use recent-life and frequency expressions such as「ようになりました」,「ことが多い」, and「前より」together with obligation and permission patterns.
  3. Describe favors, invitations, and social exchanges using「あげる」「くれる」「もらう」in a context that also involves obligations and plans.
  4. Ask about and confirm another person's obligations, permissions, and plans in a short guided conversation.
  5. Read a longer mixed-script message that combines Unit I vocabulary, kanji, and grammar.
  6. Write a connected life-management text of at least eight sentences that draws on at least three of the four chapters in this unit.
  7. Complete an integrated listening, speaking, reading, and writing performance task that demonstrates cumulative Unit I control.

Communication Scenario

A month has passed since Yuki and Emma began exchanging messages. Yuki's program coordinator has asked each exchange student to send a short "life update" message summarizing how their daily life has changed, who has been helping them, what they still must take care of, and what their plans are for the coming weeks. Yuki decides to write this update to Emma first, as practice before sending a similar message to her coordinator, and Emma agrees to read it and ask follow-up questions.

  1. This chapter does not introduce new grammar or new kanji. Instead, it brings together the recent-life expressions from Chapter 2, the relationship and giving/receiving language from Chapter 3, and the obligation and permission language from Chapter 4 into a single connected task.
  2. By the end of the chapter, you will complete a Unit I performance task that includes a written life-update message, a spoken explanation of your own recent life and obligations, and a listening task based on someone else's life update.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Focus — Mora timing across a longer connected text

Longer connected texts make it easier to let mora timing slip, especially across sentence and clause boundaries. As you read the Model Dialogue and Reading Practice passage aloud, pay particular attention to:

  • keeping long vowels in words such as きそく (rule) and ひつよう (necessary) full length, even when speaking at a natural pace;
  • giving moraic ん its own full beat in words such as せきにん (responsibility) and かんけい (relationship), rather than rushing into the following syllable; and
  • maintaining even phrase rhythm across clauses joined with て-form or ので, rather than speeding up toward the end of a long sentence.

Read one paragraph of the Model Dialogue aloud slowly, then again at a natural pace, checking your own timing against a recording if one is available.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns Review

This chapter authorizes assessed use of obligation, frequency, giving/receiving, sequencing, and reference tracking from Chapters 1 through 4. No new grammar paradigm is introduced.

1. Recent-life and frequency expressions (Chapter 2)

Purpose: to describe how routines and habits have changed recently.

  • 最近、早く起きるようになりました。Recently, I've come to wake up early.
  • 週末に掃除をすることが多いです。I often do cleaning on weekends.
  • 前より日本語で話すことが増えました。I speak in Japanese more than before.

2. Giving and receiving (Chapter 3)

Purpose: to describe favors, gifts, and help exchanged between people, showing the direction of benefit.

  • ホストマザーがゴミの出し方を教えてくれました。My host mother taught me how to take out the garbage. (benefit toward the speaker)
  • 友人に日本語のノートを見せてもらいました。I had a friend show me their Japanese notes. (speaker receives)
  • 友人にプレゼントをあげました。I gave a present to a friend. (speaker gives)

3. Obligation, lack of obligation, permission, and prohibition (Chapter 4)

Purpose: to describe what must, does not have to, may, and may not be done.

  • 今週中に申込書を提出しなければなりません。I have to submit the application form by the end of this week.
  • 今週は掃除をしなくてもいいです。I don't have to do the cleaning this week.
  • サインが必要です。A signature is necessary.
Building Block — A connected Unit I life-update account
Recent-life statement + relationship/giving-receiving detail + obligation or permission + closing plan
A Unit I life-update text can follow this shape: describe a recent change in your routine, mention someone who has helped you or someone you have helped, state one obligation or one thing you don't have to do, and close with a plan for the coming weeks.
最近、早く起きるようになりました。ホストマザーが毎朝、朝ごはんを作ってくれます。今週中に学校の申込書を提出しなければなりませんが、掃除は今週しなくてもいいです。来週、友人と勉強する予定です。
Recently, I've come to wake up early. My host mother makes breakfast for me every morning. I have to submit a school application form by the end of this week, but I don't have to do the cleaning this week. Next week, I plan to study with a friend.

Grammar Review Practice

  1. Write one sentence describing a recent change in your routine, using ようになりました or ことが多い.
  2. Write one sentence describing something someone did for you, or something you did for someone else, using あげる, くれる, or もらう.
  3. Write one sentence describing an obligation, using なければなりません.
  4. Write one sentence describing something you don't have to do, using なくてもいいです.
  5. Write one sentence describing a plan, using 予定です or つもりです.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Yuki sends Emma a longer life-update message, and Emma responds with follow-up questions before writing her own.

ユキ エマ、久しぶり。最近の生活について書きますね。前より早く起きるようになりました。 Emma, it's been a while. I'll write about my recent life. I've come to wake up earlier than before.
エマ いいですね。ホストファミリーとの関係はどうですか。 That's good. How's your relationship with your host family?
ユキ とてもいいです。ホストマザーが毎朝、朝ごはんを作ってくれます。それに、週末によく買い物に連れて行ってくれます。 Very good. My host mother makes breakfast for me every morning. Also, she often takes me shopping on weekends.
エマ それはいいですね。今、しなければならないことはありますか。 That's nice. Is there anything you have to do right now?
ユキ はい、今週中に学校の申込書を提出しなければなりません。サインが必要なので、明日、先生に聞くつもりです。でも、掃除は今週しなくてもいいので、少し楽です。 Yes, I have to submit a school application form by the end of this week. A signature is necessary, so I plan to ask my teacher tomorrow. But I don't have to do the cleaning this week, so it's a bit easier.
エマ がんばってください。来週は何か予定がありますか。 Good luck. Do you have any plans for next week?
ユキ 友人に日本語を教えてもらう予定です。それから、その友人にお礼としてお菓子をあげるつもりです。 I plan to have a friend teach me Japanese. Then, I intend to give that friend some sweets as thanks.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. How has Yuki's morning routine changed recently?
  2. Name two things Yuki's host mother does for her.
  3. What must Yuki do this week, and what does she not have to do?
  4. What are Yuki's plans for next week, and what does she plan to give her friend?

Writing Workshop

In this workshop you will write your own connected life-update message, using the Building Block pattern from the Grammar Review section.

  1. Open your message with a greeting and a sentence about a recent change, using ようになりました or ことが多い.
  2. Add a relationship detail, describing something someone did for you or something you did for someone else, using あげる, くれる, or もらう.
  3. State one obligation and one thing you don't have to do, using なければなりません and なくてもいいです.
  4. Close with a plan, using 予定です or つもりです.
  5. Revise and check using the checklist below before finalizing your text.

チェックリスト — Editing checklist:

  • My message includes a greeting and a recent-life statement.
  • I used あげる, くれる, or もらう correctly to show the direction of benefit.
  • I used なければなりません and なくてもいいです correctly.
  • I closed with a clear plan using 予定です or つもりです.
  • I used at least three different chapters' vocabulary or grammar from Unit I.
  • I kept furigana on developing Unit I kanji and removed it from consolidated Volume I kanji.
  • I kept one consistent register throughout my message.
  • I ended each sentence with 。 and used consistent script and punctuation throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Life updates and check-ins

Sending a periodic life update to a program coordinator, family member, or friend is a common practice for people living or studying away from home, and the structure Yuki uses here — recent changes, relationships, obligations, and plans — reflects a natural way of organizing that kind of message in many everyday and institutional contexts, not only in Japanese.

Regional Note — Variation in host-family and institutional practice

The specific chores, deadlines, and rules described across Chapters 1 through 4 reflect one plausible household and school context. Actual practices vary by family, school, workplace, and region, and confirming expectations directly with the people involved remains the most reliable approach, as noted in Chapter 4.

Register Note — Combining registers across a longer text

Yuki's message to Emma stays in one consistent register from beginning to end, even though it moves through several topics. When writing a longer connected text, choose one register for the whole piece rather than shifting between polite and plain style within the same message.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing the direction of あげる, くれる, and もらう

Error: *ホストマザーが朝ごはんをあげます。 (used to mean the host mother makes breakfast for the speaker).

Correction: ホストマザーが朝ごはんを作ってくれます。 — くれる is used when someone does something that benefits the speaker; あげる is used when the speaker or someone else benefits a third party.

Common Mistake — Overloading one sentence with too many chapters at once

Error: Cramming a recent-life expression, a giving/receiving verb, an obligation, and a plan into a single long sentence, producing an unnatural run-on.

Correction: Distribute these ideas across separate sentences, connected with それから, でも, or ので, as shown in the Model Dialogue and the Building Block example.

Common Mistake — Dropping furigana on still-developing kanji

Error: Removing furigana from 関係, 規則, or 必要 because they feel familiar after several chapters.

Correction: These kanji remain in the developing stage at this point in the volume and keep furigana until a later chapter's matrix marks them as consolidated, as explained in the Script and Kanji Review section above.

Unit I Performance Task

ユニット１パフォーマンスタスク

生活を整える — An integrated life-management portfolio

This performance task draws together the systems developed across Chapters 1 through 4. Complete all four components below.

  • Written life-update message: Write a connected message of at least eight sentences describing a recent change in your life, a relationship or exchange involving giving or receiving, at least one obligation and one thing you do not have to do, and a plan, using vocabulary and grammar from at least three of the four unit chapters.
  • Spoken life update: Present a one-minute spoken version of your life update to a partner, instructor, or recording, in polite style.
  • Listening check: Listen to another person's life update (a partner's, an instructor's, or a recorded passage) and correctly identify their recent change, relationship detail, obligation, and plan.
  • Follow-up exchange: After sharing your update, ask and answer at least two follow-up questions with a partner or instructor about the details in each other's updates.

Assessment focus: accurate combined use of Chapter 2 recent-life expressions, Chapter 3 giving/receiving language, and Chapter 4 obligation and permission language; correct furigana on developing Unit I kanji; consistent register across the whole text; and comprehensible pronunciation with attention to mora timing, long vowels, and moraic ん across a longer connected passage.
