Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Report on a project's progress and assign or accept tasks, using patterns from Chapter 11.
  2. Understand the main idea of a short news item or media post and respond to it appropriately, using patterns from Chapter 12.
  3. State an opinion with a supporting reason, and agree or disagree politely, using patterns from Chapter 13.
  4. Explain a process in order, describe a general result, a special case, and a purpose, using patterns from Chapter 14.
  5. Combine reporting, opinion, and explanation into one connected spoken or written text.
  6. Demonstrate control of Unit III vocabulary, kanji, and furigana reduction expectations.
  7. Complete an integrated listening, reading, writing, and speaking assessment covering Chapters 11 through 14.

Communication Scenario

The community event that Emma and Yuki have been organizing since Chapter 11 is now one week away. The volunteer team meets to check progress, share a short news item about a related event in the area, discuss opinions about a last-minute change, and finalize instructions for the day. This chapter brings together everything from Unit III: reporting on a project, responding to media, giving supported opinions, and explaining a process clearly. You will use all four systems together, the way real communication actually combines them, and demonstrate what you can do with Volume II's Unit III language.

  1. The team reports on what has been done and what is still 「ところです」 or still needs to be finished.
  2. Someone mentions a short news item about a similar local event, and the team responds with an opinion.
  3. The team finalizes step-by-step instructions for volunteers on the day of the event.

A single Unit III paragraph may now contain kanji at three different support levels at once: brand-new Chapter 14 kanji with furigana, Chapter 13 kanji with reduced furigana shown only once, and Chapter 11–12 kanji with no furigana at all. Pronunciation Review — Mora, gemination, and phrase rhythm across Unit III vocabulary

Review the following contrasts using vocabulary from Chapters 11 through 14:

  • 結果 (けっか) and 記事 (きじ): keep the geminate っ in 結果 distinct from an ordinary short vowel sequence.
  • 担当 (たんとう) and 賛成する (さんせいする): keep moraic ん as its own timing unit before the following consonant.
  • 情報 (じょうほう): a long vowel in both the first and third syllables; do not shorten either one.

Grammar Review

This chapter reviews and integrates the grammar systems introduced across Unit III: task and progress reporting (Chapter 11), hearsay and source labeling (Chapter 12), opinion and reason (Chapter 13), and process explanation (Chapter 14).

1. Reporting progress — ところです／までに／ので

Purpose: to state what stage a task has reached and why.

  • 今、チラシを作っているところです。Right now, I'm in the middle of making the flyers.
  • 金曜日までに終わらせなければなりません。I have to finish it by Friday.
  • 時間がないので、手伝ってもらえますか。Since there isn't much time, could you help me?

2. Responding to media — hearsay そうです／について

Purpose: to relay information from a source and to specify what a piece of information is about.

  • その記事によると、来月も似たイベントがあるそうです。According to that article, I hear there is a similar event next month too.
  • そのイベントについて、もっと知りたいです。I want to know more about that event.

Hearsay そうです attributes information to a source rather than the speaker's direct experience; always be ready to name where the information came from, as in 記事によると (according to the article) or 友達によると (according to a friend).

3. Giving opinions — と思います／から・ので／かもしれません

Purpose: to state a personal opinion, support it with a reason, and soften a guess when appropriate.

  • 私はその日にした方がいいと思います。理由は、天気がいいからです。I think it would be better to do it on that day. The reason is that the weather is good.
  • その方法は少し難しいかもしれません。That method might be a little difficult.

4. Explaining a process — まず／次に／最後に、procedural と、場合、ために／ように

Purpose: to sequence steps, describe automatic results, note special cases, and state purposes.

  • まず、受付で名前を書きます。次に、席を選んで座ります。First, write your name at the reception desk. Next, choose a seat and sit down.
  • 雨の場合は、イベントは室内になります。In the case of rain, the event will be indoors.
  • みんなが楽しめるように、いろいろな活動を用意しました。So that everyone can enjoy it, we prepared various activities.
Building Block — Combining Unit III systems
report progress (ところです／ので) → mention a source (について／そうです) → give an opinion with a reason (と思います／から) → explain what to do (まず→次に→最後に／場合は／ように)
Real team conversations move naturally between updating, informing, opinion-sharing, and explaining. This chapter's Model Dialogue and Performance Task ask you to move between all four smoothly rather than treating them as separate, unconnected skills.
今、招待状を書いているところです。友達の記事によると、去年も同じような会があったそうです。私は今年も成功すると思います。理由は、みんなが手伝ってくれるからです。当日は、まず受付で名前を確認して、次に席を案内します。分からない場合は、私に聞いてください。

Grammar Practice

  1. Write one sentence reporting on a task's progress, using ところです or ので.
  2. Write one sentence relaying information from a source, using そうです or について.
  3. Write one opinion sentence with a supporting reason, using と思います and から or ので.
  4. Write three sentences explaining a short process, using まず、次に、最後に、and one instance of 場合は or ように.
  5. Combine your sentences from items 1 through 4 into one connected paragraph, following the Building Block pattern.

Model Dialogue

Setting: The volunteer team, including Emma, Yuki, and a new volunteer, サトウさん, meets one week before the event.

ユキ エマさん、招待状はどうですか。 Emma, how are the invitations coming along?
エマ 今、書いているところです。金曜日までに全部終わらせます。 I'm writing them right now. I'll finish all of them by Friday.
サトウ そういえば、地域のニュースの記事によると、来月、近くの公園でも似たイベントがあるそうですよ。 By the way, according to a local news article, I hear there's a similar event at the nearby park next month too.
ユキ それはいいニュースですね。私はうちのイベントも成功すると思います。理由は、参加者がもう五十人以上いるからです。 That's good news. I think our event will be a success too. The reason is that we already have over fifty participants.
サトウ 当日の手順を教えてもらえますか。 Could you tell me the procedure for the day?
エマ はい。まず、受付で名前を確認します。次に、席を案内します。分からないことがある場合は、いつでも私かユキさんに聞いてください。 Sure. First, we'll confirm names at reception. Next, we'll guide people to their seats. If there's anything you don't understand, please ask me or Yuki at any time.
サトウ 分かりました。最後に何をしますか。 Understood. What do we do at the end?
ユキ 最後に、みんなが気持ちよく帰れるように、片づけを手伝ってください。 Finally, please help with cleanup so that everyone can go home feeling good.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. What stage has Emma reached with the invitations, and by when will she finish?
  2. What did Satō learn from a news article?
  3. What are the three steps in the day-of procedure that Emma describes, and what should Satō do if he does not understand something?

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Community volunteer coordination

Community organizations in Japan, like similar organizations in many countries, often coordinate volunteers through a combination of spoken updates at meetings and short written notices posted for reference. Combining a spoken team update with a written notice, as this chapter's dialogue and reading passage do, reflects a common practical pattern for keeping a volunteer team informed, though exact practices vary by organization, region, and the size of the event.

Regional Note — Local news and community bulletins

Local news sources and community bulletins vary widely by region and neighborhood in Japan, just as they do elsewhere. The pattern of citing a source with によると, as practiced in this chapter, is broadly useful across regions and registers, but the actual content, tone, and level of detail of local news can differ significantly from one community to another.

Register Note — Mixing report, opinion, and instruction in one conversation

The Model Dialogue in this chapter moves between reporting, responding to news, giving an opinion, and giving instructions, all within one polite team conversation. This kind of register consistency, staying in polite です／ます style throughout a single conversation with colleagues, is a common and appropriate choice in many workplace and volunteer settings, even as the communicative function of each sentence changes.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Presenting hearsay as personal experience

Error: *去年も同じイベントがありました。 stated as direct personal knowledge, when the information actually came from an article.

Correction: 記事によると、去年も同じイベントがあったそうです。 — attribute information from a source using によると and そうです rather than presenting it as your own firsthand experience.

Common Mistake — Giving an opinion without a reason

Error: *このイベントは成功すると思います。 left as a bare opinion with no supporting reason, in a context that calls for one.

Correction: このイベントは成功すると思います。理由は、参加者がもう五十人以上いるからです。 — Chapter 13's pattern of supporting an opinion with 理由は〜からです remains expected whenever an opinion is presented for discussion.

Common Mistake — Explaining steps out of order or without sequencing words

Error: *受付で名前を確認します。席を案内します。片づけを手伝います。 presenting three steps with no sequencing words connecting them.

Correction: まず、受付で名前を確認します。次に、席を案内します。最後に、片づけを手伝います。 — as in Chapter 14, まず、次に、and 最後に keep a multi-step explanation clear for a listener or reader encountering it for the first time.
