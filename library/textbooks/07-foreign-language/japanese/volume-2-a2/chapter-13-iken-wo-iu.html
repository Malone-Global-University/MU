13

意見を言うGiving Opinions

Reasons, preferences, agreement, and disagreement · Speak and Write Japanese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. State a personal opinion clearly and politely, using 「〜と思います」.
  2. Give a reason for an opinion, using 「理由は〜」 and 「〜から／ので」.
  3. Agree with another person's opinion in a natural, supportive way.
  4. Disagree with another person's opinion gently, without sounding blunt or dismissive.
  5. Soften an opinion or leave room for uncertainty, using 「〜かもしれません」.
  6. Take part in a short connected exchange of opinions about a familiar topic.

Communication Scenario

After talking about the news in Chapter 12, Emma and Yuki keep meeting at the community center to plan Emma's project. Now that Emma can follow local news and understand what is being reported, she needs to start forming and expressing her own views about the topics they discuss — the project itself, the neighborhood, and everyday choices. This chapter continues Unit III by giving Emma, and you, the tools to state opinions, support them with reasons, and respond to other people's opinions politely.

  1. Emma and Yuki discuss two possible days for the project event and each gives an opinion.
  2. Emma explains her reason for preferring one option over the other.
  3. Yuki partly agrees, partly disagrees, and softens her own opinion with かもしれません.

Script Note — 意見 and 見 across chapters

You have already seen the character 見 in 見出し (headline) in Chapter 12. Here it appears again in 意見 (opinion), read differently within this new word. Remember that a reading belongs to the word, not to the character alone: 見 is read み in 見出し but けん in 意見. Keep both words separate in your own notes rather than assuming one reading applies everywhere.

れんしゅう — Practice: Write each of the eight new kanji above once from memory, then use each one in a short phrase such as 私の意見 (my opinion) or 大切な理由 (an important reason).

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Gemination and long vowels in opinion vocabulary

Several new words in this chapter contain a geminate consonant, a long vowel, or both together:

  • 賛成 (さんせい) has a long vowel in the second syllable; keep it distinct from さんせ.
  • 反対 (はんたい) contains the moraic ん followed by a た-row consonant; keep the ん as its own mora rather than blending it into はんたい as one syllable.
  • 一番 (いちばん) ends in moraic ん and should not be shortened to いちば.
  • たしかに is often pronounced with a slightly stressed first syllable in natural speech, but all four morae should remain equal in length in your own careful pronunciation.

Practice tapping out the mora count for 賛成, 反対, and 一番 before using them in the Guided Practice section below.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter introduces four connected systems for expressing and responding to opinions: stating an opinion with と思います, giving a reason with 理由は and から／ので, softening a claim with かもしれません, and agreeing or disagreeing politely.

1. Stating an opinion — と思います

Purpose: to present a statement as your own opinion or judgment, rather than as an established fact.

  • その日がいいと思います。I think that day is good.plain form + と思います → opinion
  • この計画は難しいと思います。I think this plan is difficult.
  • みんな賛成すると思います。I think everyone will agree.

と思います attaches to the plain form of a verb, adjective, or noun + だ (the だ is usually dropped directly before と思います with noun and な-adjective predicates, for example 大切だと思います is also possible and common). It marks the statement as the speaker's own view.

2. Giving a reason — 理由は and から／ので

Purpose: to explain why you hold an opinion, so that your opinion is supported rather than simply stated.

  • 理由は、その日はみんな時間があるからです。The reason is that everyone has time that day.
  • この方法は簡単だから、いいと思います。Since this method is simple, I think it's good.
  • 駅から近いので、便利だと思います。Since it's close to the station, I think it's convenient.

から and ので both give a reason, but ので tends to sound slightly softer and more common in polite explanations, while から is often used with 理由は〜からです as a direct answer to "why." Either is appropriate in this chapter's tasks.

3. Softening a claim — かもしれません

Purpose: to present an opinion as a possibility rather than a certainty, which makes disagreement and uncertain statements sound more natural and less forceful.

  • その日は難しいかもしれません。That day might be difficult.
  • みんな賛成しないかもしれません。Everyone might not agree.
  • たしかに、それも大切かもしれません。Certainly, that might be important too.

4. Agreeing and disagreeing politely

Purpose: to respond to someone else's opinion in a way that keeps the conversation comfortable, whether you agree completely, partly, or not at all.

  • 私もそう思います。I think so too. (agreement)
  • たしかにそうですね。でも、〜かもしれません。That's certainly true. But, ... might be the case. (soft disagreement)
  • 私はちょっと違う意見です。I have a somewhat different opinion. (gentle disagreement)

Soft disagreement usually begins by acknowledging the other person's point with たしかに or そうですね before introducing a different view with でも or が. Disagreeing without any acknowledgment first can sound unnecessarily blunt.

Building Block — Giving a supported opinion
state the opinion (と思います) → give the reason (理由は〜から／ので) → soften if needed (かもしれません) → invite a response (どう思いますか)
A short opinion exchange can move through all four elements, matching how someone would actually explain a preference and invite a friend's reaction.
私は土曜日がいいと思います。理由は、土曜日はみんな時間があるからです。でも、天気が悪いかもしれません。ユキさんはどう思いますか。
I think Saturday is good. The reason is that everyone has time on Saturday. But the weather might be bad. Yuki, what do you think?

Grammar Practice

  1. Write one sentence stating an opinion using と思います.
  2. Write one sentence giving a reason using 理由は〜からです or 〜ので.
  3. Write one sentence softening a claim using かもしれません.
  4. Combine your sentences from items 1 through 3 above into one connected opinion, following the Building Block pattern.
  5. Write a short response that partly agrees and partly disagrees with the opinion 「日本語の勉強は毎日したほうがいいと思います。」

Model Dialogue

Setting: Emma and Yuki are choosing a day for the community center project event.

エマ イベントの日ですが、私は土曜日がいいと思います。 About the event day — I think Saturday is good.
ユキ そうですか。理由を聞いてもいいですか。 I see. May I ask your reason?
エマ 理由は、土曜日はみんな時間があるからです。それに、天気予報によると、土曜日は晴れるそうです。 The reason is that everyone has time on Saturday. Also, according to the weather forecast, I heard Saturday will be sunny.
ユキ たしかにそうですね。でも、土曜日は駅前でほかのイベントもあるかもしれません。 That's certainly true. But there might be another event in front of the station on Saturday too.
エマ ああ、それは考えていませんでした。ユキさんはどう思いますか。 Ah, I hadn't thought about that. Yuki, what do you think?
ユキ 私は日曜日のほうがいいと思います。日曜日なら、ほかのイベントの心配がありません。 I think Sunday is better. If it's Sunday, there's no worry about another event.
エマ なるほど。私もその意見に賛成です。日曜日にしましょう。 I see. I agree with that opinion too. Let's make it Sunday.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. What day does Emma first suggest, and what are her two reasons?
  2. What concern does Yuki raise about Emma's suggestion?
  3. Which day do Emma and Yuki finally agree on, and why?

私は日曜日のイベントについて、公園でやったほうがいいと思います。理由は、公園は広いし、静かだからです。それに、駅前よりも駐車場が近いと思います。でも、天気が悪い日は、公園は使えないかもしれません。その場合は、コミュニティセンターの中でやるのがいいと思います。

Writing Workshop

In this workshop you will write a short opinion post about a familiar topic, following the Building Block pattern from the Grammar section.

  1. State your opinion, using と思います.
  2. Give your reason, using 理由は〜からです or 〜ので.
  3. Acknowledge a different view, using たしかに or そうですね, if one exists.
  4. Soften a claim if appropriate, using かもしれません.
  5. Invite a response, using どう思いますか.
  6. Revise and check using the checklist below before finalizing your text.

チェックリスト — Editing checklist:

  • My opinion is clearly marked with と思います and not stated as plain fact.
  • I gave at least one clear reason for my opinion.
  • I acknowledged a different view before disagreeing, if I disagreed with anyone.
  • I used かもしれません at least once to soften a claim.
  • I kept furigana on this chapter's new kanji and reduced furigana on Chapter 12 kanji, following the furigana reduction schedule.
  • I ended each sentence with 。 and used consistent script and punctuation.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Sharing opinions in group settings

In many Japanese group settings, such as community meetings, workplaces, and classrooms, people often acknowledge another person's point before introducing a different view, rather than disagreeing directly and immediately. This pattern is common in many communication cultures around the world and is not unique to Japan, but it is worth noticing here because it shapes how the grammar in this chapter is actually used. Individual habits vary by personality, relationship, workplace culture, and setting, so this is a tendency to be aware of rather than a fixed rule that every speaker follows.

Regional Note — Softening expressions vary by community

The exact words used to soften an opinion or ease into disagreement can vary somewhat by region, generation, and community, though たしかに、そうですね、and でも are broadly understood and usable across contemporary standard Japanese. Some communities favor additional softening particles or phrases not covered in this chapter; those are left for later, more advanced volumes.

Register Note — と思います is not automatically formal or informal

と思います is compatible with both polite and plain styles, depending on what comes before it. Combined with a plain-form clause, と思います itself keeps the sentence at a polite level appropriate for most everyday and semi-formal situations. Dropping です／ます entirely and using plain style throughout, such as 「そう思う」, is possible among close friends and family, but that fuller shift to plain style is not the focus of this chapter and should not be assumed to be interchangeable with と思います in every context.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Disagreeing without acknowledging the other opinion

Error: *違います。日曜日がいいです。 used as an immediate, unsoftened response to someone else's suggestion, which can sound unnecessarily blunt in most everyday contexts.

Correction: たしかにそうですね。でも、私は日曜日のほうがいいと思います。 — acknowledging the other person's point first keeps the exchange comfortable before introducing a different opinion.

Common Mistake — Stating an opinion as if it were a fact

Error: *土曜日がいいです。 presented with no marker, so a personal opinion is expressed in exactly the same way as an established fact.

Correction: 土曜日がいいと思います。 — と思います clearly marks the statement as the speaker's own opinion rather than a settled fact.

Common Mistake — Overusing かもしれません for every statement

Error: *土曜日がいいかもしれません。理由は時間があるかもしれないからです。 softening both the opinion and the reason so heavily that the opinion loses its clarity.

Correction: 土曜日がいいと思います。理由は、時間があるからです。 — state the core opinion and reason with ordinary confidence, and reserve かもしれません for a genuinely uncertain point.

Looking Ahead

いまできること — What you can do now

You can state a personal opinion using と思います, give a reason using 理由は〜から or 〜ので, soften an uncertain claim using かもしれません, and respond to someone else's opinion with agreement or gentle disagreement. You can also combine these patterns into a short, connected opinion with supporting reasons.
