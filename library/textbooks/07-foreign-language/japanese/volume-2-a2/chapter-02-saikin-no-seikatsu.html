最近の生活Recent Life and Routines

Expanded routines, frequency, change, and habit · Speak and Write Japanese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe your current schedule, routines, and responsibilities in more detail than in Volume I.
  2. Talk about habits and changes in habits using expressions such as「ようになりました」and「ことが多い」.
  3. Compare your present routine with the past using「前より」.
  4. Sequence related actions using「てから」to show that one event happens after another.
  5. Use an expanded range of frequency expressions to describe how often something happens.
  6. Read a short mixed-script passage about someone's weekly schedule and daily responsibilities.
  7. Write a connected description of your own recent routine, including at least one change and one sequenced action.

Communication Scenario

Emma's new online conversation group asks each member to post a short weekly update describing their current routine, so that partners with different schedules and time zones can find good times to talk. Yuki reads Emma's post and replies with her own routine, noticing that both of their schedules have shifted recently. Over the next few messages, they compare how their responsibilities, study habits, and free time have changed since they last talked in detail.

  1. This chapter expands your ability to describe schedules, responsibilities, and habits, and to mark how those things have changed recently.
  2. By the end of the chapter, you will complete a performance task that includes a written routine description, a spoken comparison of past and present habits, and a listening task based on someone else's schedule.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Focus — Long vowels and gemination in new vocabulary

Several new words in this chapter contain long vowels and geminate consonants that are easy to shorten or drop when speaking quickly. Practice these contrasts before the Model Dialogue:

  • Long vowel: しゅうかん (habit) versus a shortened mispronunciation without the long ゅう
  • Long vowel: しゅうまつ (weekend) — hold the long vowel for its full two morae
  • Moraic ん in せきにん (responsibility), held for a full beat before the pause

Read the vocabulary tables above aloud slowly, then again at a natural pace, checking that each long vowel and each moraic ん keeps its full timing.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter authorizes 「ようになりました」as a formulaic pattern for describing a new habit, 「ことが多い」for describing what happens often, 「前より」for comparing present routines to the past, 「てから」for sequencing actions, and an expanded set of frequency expressions. No additional grammar paradigm is introduced.

1. Describing a new habit with 「ようになりました」

Purpose: to describe a habit or ability that has newly developed. In this chapter, this pattern is used formulaically with a small set of familiar verbs; its full internal grammar is not analyzed here.

  • 毎朝、早く起きるようになりました。I've started getting up early every morning (a new habit).
  • 週末に運動するようになりました。I've started exercising on weekends.

2. Describing what happens often with 「ことが多い」

Purpose: to describe an action or situation that occurs frequently, without claiming it happens every single time.

  • 平日は忙しいことが多いです。Weekdays are often busy.
  • 週末は家で勉強することが多いです。On weekends, I often study at home.

3. Comparing with the past using 「前より」

Purpose: to compare a current situation with an earlier one, without needing a full comparison-clause grammar system.

  • 前より忙しくなりました。I've become busier than before.
  • 前より早く起きています。I'm getting up earlier than before.

4. Sequencing actions with 「てから」

Purpose: to show that one action happens, and only after it is finished does the next action happen. This builds on the て-form reviewed in Chapter 1.

  • 朝ご飯を食べてから、学校に行きます。After eating breakfast, I go to school.
  • 宿題をしてから、テレビを見ます。After doing my homework, I watch television.

5. Expanded frequency expressions

Purpose: to describe how often something happens with more precision than 毎日 and たまに alone.

  • 週に二回、ジムに行きます。I go to the gym twice a week.
  • めったに朝ご飯を食べません。I rarely eat breakfast.
Common Mistake — Treating 「ことが多い」as a claim of 100% frequency

Error: Using「ことが多い」to mean the same thing as「いつも」(always), as in *「毎日、必ずジムに行くことが多いです」.

Correction: 週末はジムに行くことが多いです。 — 「ことが多い」describes something that happens often, not something that happens without exception. Reserve いつも or 毎日 for situations that are truly constant.

Building Block — A four-part routine description
Current schedule statement + one sequenced daily action with てから + one frequency statement + one comparison with 前より
A short routine description can follow this shape: state your current general schedule, describe one sequenced pair of daily actions with てから, add a frequency statement about something you do regularly, and close with a comparison to your past routine using 前より.
最近、平日はとても忙しいです。朝ご飯を食べてから、学校に行きます。週に二回、ジムに行くようになりました。前より早く起きています。
Recently, weekdays have been very busy. After eating breakfast, I go to school. I've started going to the gym twice a week. I'm getting up earlier than before.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Yuki and Emma message each other about their current weekly schedules.

エマ 最近、平日はとても忙しいです。新しいアルバイトの責任が多いです。 Recently, weekdays have been very busy. There's a lot of responsibility with my new part-time job.
ユキ そうですか。わたしも前より忙しくなりました。毎朝、早く起きるようになりました。 Is that so? I've become busier than before too. I've started getting up early every morning.
エマ 早いですね。朝、何をしてから学校に行きますか。 That's early! What do you do before going to school in the morning?
ユキ 朝ご飯を食べてから、少し運動する習慣ができました。週に二回はジムに行くことが多いです。 After eating breakfast, I've developed a habit of exercising a little. I often go to the gym twice a week.
エマ いい習慣ですね。わたしはめったに運動しません。週末はたいてい家にいます。 That's a good habit. I rarely exercise. I'm usually at home on weekends.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. Why does Emma say her weekdays have become busy?
  2. What new morning habit has Yuki developed?
  3. How often does Emma exercise?

Writing Workshop

In this workshop you will write a short description of your own recent routine, using the four-part Building Block pattern from the Grammar section.

  1. State your current general schedule in one or two sentences, noting whether weekdays or weekends are busier.
  2. Describe one sequenced pair of daily actions, using てから.
  3. Add one frequency statement about something you do regularly, using an expression other than 毎日 or たまに where possible.
  4. Add one comparison with your past routine, using 前より or ようになりました.
  5. Revise and check using the checklist below before finalizing your description.

チェックリスト — Editing checklist:

  • I described my current schedule clearly.
  • I used てから to sequence at least one pair of actions rather than listing them separately.
  • I used at least one frequency expression accurately.
  • I compared my current routine with the past using 前より or ようになりました.
  • I kept one consistent register throughout.
  • I kept furigana on 予定, 習慣, 責任, and 平日, and removed it from consolidated Volume I kanji.
  • I ended each sentence with 。 and used consistent script and punctuation throughout.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Talking about being busy

Describing oneself as 忙しい (busy) is a common way to open a casual update among many Japanese speakers, though how much detail follows, and how directly someone complains about being busy, can vary by relationship, workplace norms, and individual style.

Regional Note — Schedules vary by school, workplace, and household

Emma's and Yuki's routines reflect their own schools, part-time jobs, and households. Other students and workers in Japan and elsewhere may follow very different weekday and weekend patterns depending on their school system, industry, region, and family responsibilities.

Register Note — Reporting routines to different audiences

Emma's polite written post to the conversation group and her plainer spoken voice memo describe similar facts about her week, but the register shifts because the audience shifts from a semi-public group to a private recording. Volume II will return to this kind of audience-based register shift in later chapters.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using てから where simple て-form linking is more natural

Error: *学校が終わってから、忙しくなりました。 (used to mean "school ended and then I became busy," when the two events are not in a clear sequential relationship).

Correction: 学校が終わって、忙しくなりました。 — Reserve てから for cases where one action must finish before the next clearly begins, such as eating breakfast before leaving the house. Use plain て-form linking for events that are simply connected in time.

Common Mistake — Overusing 毎日 for anything frequent

Error: *毎日、ジムに行くことが多いです。 (combining "every day" with "often," which contradicts itself).

Correction: 週に二回、ジムに行くことが多いです。 — Choose one frequency expression that accurately matches how often the action really happens, rather than defaulting to 毎日.

Common Mistake — Removing furigana from brand-new kanji

Error: Writing 予定, 習慣, 責任, or 平日 without furigana because they resemble familiar kanji.

Correction: Check the Furigana / Reading-Support Matrix before deciding. These four kanji are newly introduced in this chapter and require furigana support until a later chapter's matrix marks them as consolidated.

Practical Performance Task

パフォーマンスタスク

最近の生活 — Describing recent life and routines

This performance task draws together the systems developed in this chapter. Complete all three components below.

  • Written routine description: Write a description of at least six sentences covering your current weekday and weekend routines, including at least one てから sequence, one frequency expression, and one comparison with the past using 前より or ようになりました.
