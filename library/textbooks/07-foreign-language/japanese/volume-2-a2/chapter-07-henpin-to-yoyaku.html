7

返品と予約Returns and Reservations

Transactional negotiation, conditions, and confirmation · Speak and Write Japanese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Talk about returning or exchanging an item, making a reservation, and cancelling or changing plans.
  2. Describe a conditional situation using「なら」, such as what will happen or what someone should do if something is the case.
  3. Describe a conditional situation using「場合」to talk about a specific case or circumstance, especially in more formal or written contexts.
  4. State what is or is not possible using「ことができます」.
  5. Make a polite request using「てもらえますか」and understand how it relates to「てもらえませんか」from Chapter 6.
  6. Ask and answer confirmation questions to check details such as dates, times, and conditions before completing a transaction.
  7. Read and write a short exchange about a return, exchange, or reservation, including relevant conditions and confirmations.

Communication Scenario

A few days after her afternoon of small problems in Chapter 6, Emma needs to return a jacket that does not fit, and she also wants to make a dinner reservation for Yuki's birthday. At the shop, she has to understand the store's return policy and ask about exchanging the jacket for a different size. On the phone, she has to make a reservation, confirm the date and time, and ask what will happen if she needs to change her plans.

  1. This chapter continues Unit II by moving from explaining unexpected problems (Chapter 6) to negotiating transactions: returns, exchanges, and reservations.
  2. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to state conditions, ask what is possible, make a polite request, and confirm details clearly with store or restaurant staff.

Vocabulary Note — 返品 versus 交換

返品 refers to returning an item for a refund or simply giving it back, while 交換 refers to exchanging an item for a different one, such as a different size or color. A shop's policy may allow one, both, or neither, depending on the item and the 期限 (time limit) stated on the レシート or 領収書.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Long vowels and geminate consonants in transactional vocabulary

Several words in this chapter contain long vowels or geminate consonants that change meaning if shortened or dropped. Keep the following contrasts clear:

  • the long vowel in よやく (reservation) and りょうしゅうしょ (receipt);
  • the geminate consonant in キャンセル (cancellation) and きゃく (customer, recycled from Volume I);
  • the moraic ん in へんぴん (return), こうかん (exchange), and へんこう (change); and
  • the distinct mora count in じょうけん (condition), which has four morae, not three.

Practice reading the Core Vocabulary list aloud slowly, tapping out the mora count for each word before increasing your speed.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter authorizes「なら」,「場合」,「ことができます」,「てもらえますか」, and confirmation questions.

1. なら — conditional based on a topic

Purpose: to respond to something just mentioned by saying what follows if that is the case, often to give advice or a suggestion.

  • サイズが合わないなら、交換できますよ。If the size doesn't fit, you can exchange it.
  • 予約を変更するなら、早いほうがいいです。If you're going to change the reservation, sooner is better.
  • レシートがないなら、返品は難しいかもしれません。If you don't have the receipt, a return might be difficult.

なら typically responds to a topic that has already come up in the conversation, rather than introducing a brand-new hypothetical from nothing.

2. 場合 — in the case that...

Purpose: to describe a specific case or circumstance, often in policies, notices, or more formal explanations.

  • レシートがない場合、返品はできません。In the case that you don't have the receipt, a return is not possible.
  • 予約をキャンセルする場合は、前日までにご連絡ください。In the case that you cancel the reservation, please contact us by the day before.
  • サイズが合わない場合、交換をお勧めします。In the case that the size doesn't fit, we recommend an exchange.

場合 is common in written policies and formal spoken explanations, while なら is more common in ordinary conversation.

3. ことができます — stating possibility

Purpose: to state what is or is not possible, often used for policies and permissions.

  • 一週間以内なら、交換することができます。If it's within one week, you can exchange it.
  • オンラインで予約することができます。You can make a reservation online.
  • この券は他の日に使うことができません。This ticket cannot be used on another day.

ことができます attaches to the dictionary form of a verb and is common in formal or written explanations of what is allowed or possible.

4. てもらえますか — polite request (confirming form)

Purpose: to make a polite request, closely related to てもらえませんか from Chapter 6 but with a slightly more neutral, straightforward tone.

  • レシートを見せてもらえますか。Could you show me the receipt?
  • 予約の日にちを確認してもらえますか。Could you confirm the reservation date?
  • サイズを変更してもらえますか。Could you change the size for me?

てもらえますか and てもらえませんか are both polite requests built on もらう. てもらえませんか, introduced in Chapter 6, tends to sound a little more deferential; てもらえますか is equally polite and very common in everyday transactions.

5. Confirmation questions

Purpose: to check that details are correct before completing a transaction.

  • 土曜日の七時ということでよろしいですか。So that's Saturday at seven o'clock, is that correct?
  • 二名でご予約ですね。A reservation for two people, correct?
  • こちらのサイズでよろしいでしょうか。Is this size all right?
Building Block — Stating a policy and confirming a detail
Condition with なら or 場合 + ことができます／できません + confirmation question with 〜ですね or 〜でよろしいですか
A typical policy explanation states the condition, states what is or is not possible, and closes with a confirmation question to make sure both sides agree on the details.
レシートがある場合は、一週間以内なら交換することができます。サイズはＭでよろしいですね。
If you have the receipt, you can exchange it within one week. The size is M, correct?

Model Dialogue

Setting: Emma brings a jacket back to a clothing shop because it doesn't fit.

エマ すみません、このジャケットのサイズが合わないんです。交換することができますか。 Excuse me, this jacket doesn't fit. Can I exchange it?
店員 レシートを見せてもらえますか。 Could you show me the receipt?
エマ はい、こちらです。 Yes, here it is.
(The clerk checks the receipt.)
店員 今日で三日目ですね。一週間以内なら、交換することができます。どのサイズがよろしいですか。 That's three days ago, I see. If it's within one week, you can exchange it. What size would you like?
エマ Ｌサイズがあれば、それにしたいんですが。 If you have an L size, I'd like that one.
店員 少々お待ちください。…はい、Ｌサイズがございます。こちらでよろしいですか。 One moment, please. ...Yes, we have an L size. Is this all right?
エマ はい、大丈夫です。ありがとうございます。 Yes, that's fine. Thank you.
店員 では、こちらの新しいレシートをお渡しします。サイズをＬに交換したということでよろしいですね。 All right, I'll give you this new receipt. So that's confirmed — you've exchanged the size for L, correct?

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. Why does Emma want to exchange the jacket?
  2. What does the clerk ask to see before agreeing to the exchange?
  3. What is the shop's time limit for exchanges, according to the clerk?
  4. What size does Emma ask for instead?

Writing Workshop

In this workshop you will write a short exchange (real or invented) about a return, an exchange, or a reservation, using the Building Block pattern from the Grammar section.

  1. Open with an attention-getter, such as すみません, and briefly state what you want to do (return, exchange, or reserve something).
  2. State the condition, using なら or 場合, that applies to your situation.
  3. State what is possible, using ことができます or ことができません.
  4. Make a polite request, using てもらえますか, if you need the other person to do something.
  5. Close with a confirmation question, checking that the details are correct.
  6. Revise and check using the checklist below before finalizing your text.

チェックリスト — Editing checklist:

  • My text clearly states what I want to return, exchange, or reserve.
  • I used なら or 場合 naturally to state a condition.
  • I used ことができます or ことができません to state what is possible.
  • I included at least one confirmation question.
  • I used at least three vocabulary items from this chapter's Core Vocabulary.
  • I kept furigana on this chapter's new kanji and on developing Chapter 6 kanji.
  • I kept one consistent, polite register throughout.
  • I ended each sentence with 。 and used consistent script and punctuation.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Return and exchange policies

Return and exchange policies vary considerably among shops, chains, and types of goods in Japan, and a receipt is usually required. Many shops are more willing to offer an exchange than a cash refund, and time limits are commonly enforced strictly. Reading a posted notice or asking directly, as in this chapter's dialogue and reading passage, remains the most reliable way to know what is possible at a specific store.

Regional Note — Variation in reservation practices

Reservation customs vary by type of establishment, region, and season. Some restaurants accept reservations only by phone, while others use apps or online forms. Confirming the specific method and cancellation policy with the establishment remains more reliable than assuming one national standard.

Register Note — 場合 in written policy versus なら in conversation

Notices, contracts, and formal explanations tend to favor 場合, which sounds more neutral and official. Ordinary spoken exchanges, especially responses to something just mentioned, favor なら. Recognizing this difference helps you read a posted policy and then discuss it naturally with a staff member.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using なら to introduce a topic that hasn't been mentioned

Error: *パーティーがあるなら、来てください。 said out of nowhere, with no prior mention of a party (confusing for the listener, who does not know what "if there is a party" refers to).

Correction: First mention the party, then respond: パーティーがあるんですが、来ませんか。 — なら works best as a response to something already introduced into the conversation, not as an opening statement.

Common Mistake — Confusing ことができます with can as general ability

Error: Using ことができます for every kind of ability, including physical skills better expressed with the potential form (introduced later in the series), such as *泳ぐことができます for casual conversation about swimming.

Correction: In this chapter, use ことができます mainly for policies, permissions, and formal statements of possibility, such as 交換することができます. Everyday physical abilities are addressed separately as the series progresses.

Common Mistake — Forgetting to confirm before ending a transaction

Error: Ending a reservation or exchange conversation immediately after stating a request, without checking that the details are correct.

Correction: Close with a confirmation question such as 〜ということでよろしいですか or 〜ですね, as shown in the Model Dialogue, so both sides agree on the outcome.
