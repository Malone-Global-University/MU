泊まる・移動するLodging and Longer Travel

Travel logistics, lodging, routes, and preferences · Speak and Write Japanese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Talk about checking in and out of lodging, handling luggage, and arranging transfers between places.
  2. Compare two or more options for lodging, transportation, or routes using expanded comparison patterns.
  3. State a scheduled plan using「予定です」and distinguish it from a personal intention stated with「つもりです」.
  4. Sequence actions in time using「前に」and「後で」.
  5. Describe a longer route or itinerary using a connected sequence of steps.
  6. Read and write a short travel plan or lodging inquiry that states preferences and confirms details.

Communication Scenario

Emma is planning a weekend trip with Yuki to a town a few hours away by train. Before they go, Emma has to compare two guesthouses, decide whether to arrive before or after checking in at the station, and figure out the transfer between the train and the bus. At the guesthouse, she has to ask about check-in and check-out times, luggage storage, and what to do if they arrive later than planned.

  1. This chapter continues Unit II by moving from short transactions (Chapter 7) to longer travel and lodging planning, which requires comparing options and sequencing steps.
  2. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to compare choices, state plans and intentions, sequence actions in time, and communicate clearly with lodging and transportation staff.

Vocabulary Note — 片道 versus 往復

片道 refers to a one-way trip, while 往復 refers to a round trip. Both words commonly appear with ticket prices and travel times, so it is worth confirming which one a price refers to before buying a ticket, especially when comparing 経路 or planning a 乗り換え.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Long vowels and mora count in travel vocabulary

Several words in this chapter contain long vowels or a mora count that is easy to shorten by mistake. Keep the following contrasts clear:

  • the long vowel in しゅくはく (lodging) and おうふく (round trip);
  • the four morae in のりかえ (transfer), which should not be rushed into three;
  • the moraic ん in けいろ's neighbor word こうつう (traffic, recycled from Volume I) and in あんない (recycled from Chapter 6); and
  • the distinct vowel sequence in もくてきち, which has six morae, not five.

Practice reading the Core Vocabulary list aloud slowly, tapping out the mora count for each word before increasing your speed.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter authorizes expanded comparison,「予定です」,「つもりです」,「前に／後で」, and longer route sequences.

1. Expanded comparison — choosing between options

Purpose: to compare two or more options for lodging, transportation, or routes and to state a preference with a reason.

  • このホテルはあのホテルより駅に近いです。This hotel is closer to the station than that hotel.
  • バスと電車では、電車のほうが速いです。Between the bus and the train, the train is faster.
  • 三つの中で、この経路がいちばん便利だと思います。Among the three, I think this route is the most convenient.

Comparison in this chapter builds on Volume I patterns with より and いちばん, extending them to three or more options and to reasons for a choice.

2. 予定です — a scheduled plan

Purpose: to state a plan that is already scheduled or arranged, such as a booking, itinerary, or agreed appointment.

  • 土曜日に出発する予定です。I am scheduled to depart on Saturday.
  • 午後三時にチェックインする予定です。I am scheduled to check in at three in the afternoon.
  • 日曜日の夜に帰る予定です。I am scheduled to return on Sunday night.

予定です attaches to the dictionary form of a verb and suggests that the plan is already set, such as a reservation or a fixed itinerary.

3. つもりです — a personal intention

Purpose: to state what someone personally intends to do, without necessarily being fixed by a reservation or schedule.

  • 新幹線で行くつもりです。I intend to go by shinkansen.
  • 荷物を駅に預けるつもりです。I intend to leave my luggage at the station.
  • 今回は個室に泊まるつもりです。This time, I intend to stay in a private room.

予定です describes a plan that is set or arranged, while つもりです describes a personal intention that may still change. Comparing the two examples 出発する予定です and 出発するつもりです shows this difference: the first suggests a fixed itinerary, and the second suggests a personal decision.

4. 前に and 後で — sequencing actions in time

Purpose: to describe the order of two actions, saying what happens before or after another action.

  • チェックインする前に、荷物を預けました。Before checking in, I left my luggage.
  • 乗り換える前に、経路を確認したほうがいいです。Before transferring, it's better to check the route.
  • 到着した後で、チェックインの手続きをします。After arriving, I'll do the check-in procedure.

前に attaches to the dictionary form of a verb; 後で attaches to the た-form. Both can also follow a noun with の, as in 出発の前に and 到着の後で.

5. Describing a longer route

Purpose: to connect several steps into one clear description of a journey or itinerary.

  • まず新幹線に乗って、それから在来線に乗り換えて、最後にバスで宿まで行きます。First I'll take the shinkansen, then transfer to a local line, and finally go to the lodging by bus.
  • 駅からホテルまでは、歩いて十五分ぐらいです。From the station to the hotel is about a fifteen-minute walk.
Building Block — Comparing options and stating a plan
Comparison with より or いちばん + 予定です or つもりです + sequence with 前に or 後で
A typical travel plan compares two or more options, states what has been decided or intended, and then sequences the steps of the journey.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Emma and Yuki are planning their weekend trip together before leaving.

ゆき 宿泊はどこにする？このホテルとあのゲストハウス、どっちがいいと思う？ Where should we stay? What do you think — this hotel or that guesthouse?
エマ ゲストハウスのほうが駅に近いし、個室もあるみたい。そっちにしない？ The guesthouse is closer to the station, and it seems to have private rooms too. How about that one?
ゆき いいね。じゃあ、土曜日にチェックインする予定で予約するね。 Sounds good. Then I'll make the reservation with plans to check in on Saturday.
エマ 経路はどうする？新幹線と高速バス、どっちにする？ What about the route? Shinkansen or highway bus — which one?
ゆき バスより新幹線のほうが速いから、新幹線で行くつもり。でも、乗り換えが一回あるよ。 The shinkansen is faster than the bus, so I intend to go by shinkansen. But there's one transfer.
エマ 分かった。乗り換える前に、経路をもう一度確認しよう。 Got it. Let's check the route once more before transferring.
ゆき うん。到着した後で、ゲストハウスに荷物を預けてから、少し歩こうか。 Yeah. After we arrive, let's leave our luggage at the guesthouse and then walk around a bit.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. Which lodging option do Emma and Yuki choose, and why?
  2. What day are they scheduled to check in?
  3. Which is faster, the shinkansen or the highway bus, according to Yuki?
  4. What do they plan to do right after arriving?

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Lodging types and expectations

Lodging in Japan ranges from large hotels to small guesthouses, capsule hotels, and traditional inns, each with different check-in and check-out customs. Many lodgings post their policies clearly, including check-in and check-out times, luggage storage options, and what happens if a guest arrives later than planned. Reading the posted information and confirming directly with staff, as shown in this chapter's reading passage, remains more reliable than assuming one standard applies everywhere.

Regional Note — Variation in transportation options

The balance between train, bus, and other transportation options varies by region. In some areas, a highway bus may be the more common or affordable choice for longer distances, while in others, rail options dominate. Checking current routes and schedules for the specific area remains more reliable than assuming one method is always fastest or most common.

Register Note — 予定です and つもりです in different settings

予定です often appears when describing a plan to someone outside the immediate decision, such as a staff member confirming an itinerary, because it sounds settled and official. つもりです is common between friends and family discussing personal decisions that may still be adjusted. Both remain polite in this chapter's です／ます style, but the nuance of "already arranged" versus "personally intending" affects how a listener interprets the plan.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using 予定です and つもりです interchangeably without thinking about the nuance

Error: *まだ決めていませんが、土曜日に出発する予定です。 (stating something as a fixed plan while also saying it is undecided, which contradicts the meaning of 予定です).

Correction: まだ決めていませんが、土曜日に出発するつもりです。 — つもりです fits an intention that may still change, while 予定です suggests the plan is already set.

Common Mistake — Reversing the order of 前に and 後で with the wrong verb form

Error: *チェックインした前に、荷物を預けました。 (attaching 前に to the た-form).

Correction: チェックインする前に、荷物を預けました。 — 前に attaches to the dictionary form, while 後で attaches to the た-form, as in チェックインした後で.

Common Mistake — Comparing more than two items with より alone

Error: *電車よりバスより新幹線が速いです。 (stacking より to compare three options at once).

Correction: 三つの中で、新幹線がいちばん速いです。 — use いちばん with 中で when comparing three or more options, and reserve より for comparing exactly two.
