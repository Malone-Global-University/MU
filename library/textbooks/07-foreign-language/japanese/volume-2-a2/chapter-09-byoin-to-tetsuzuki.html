病院と手続きClinics and Procedures

Health follow-up, forms, sequence, and explanation · Speak and Write Japanese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Make and confirm a clinic or pharmacy appointment, and state how long a symptom has continued using「前から」.
  2. Describe past health-related experience using expanded「ことがある」patterns.
  3. Give and understand advice using「ほうがいい」in a clinic or pharmacy context.
  4. Follow and describe a sequence of steps in a clinic or insurance procedure.
  5. Recognize instructions phrased with「ようにしてください」without being required to produce them independently.
  6. Read and write a short clinic or pharmacy form, and a short message describing symptoms and following instructions.

Communication Scenario

Emma has had a mild cough and a headache for the past few days. Before her trip with Yuki, she decides to see a doctor near her apartment. At the clinic, she has to fill out a form with her insurance information, explain when her symptoms started, and answer questions about past health experiences. After seeing the doctor, she has to understand the pharmacist's instructions about how to take her medicine and what to do if the symptoms continue.

  1. This chapter continues Unit II by moving from travel logistics (Chapter 8) to health-related procedures, which require sequencing steps and following written and spoken instructions.
  2. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to make an appointment, describe symptoms and experience, ask for and understand advice, and follow a clinic or insurance procedure from start to finish.

Vocabulary Note — 診察 versus 診断

診察 refers to the act of a doctor examining a patient, while a related word, 診断 (diagnosis), refers to the conclusion the doctor reaches. This chapter focuses on 診察 and the procedure surrounding it, such as filling out a 問診票 and receiving a 処方箋, rather than on medical diagnosis itself.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Long vowels and moraic ん in clinic vocabulary

Several words in this chapter contain long vowels or a moraic ん that is easy to blur when speaking quickly. Keep the following contrasts clear:

  • the long vowel in びょういん (clinic) and しんさつ (examination);
  • the moraic ん in ほけんしょう (insurance card) and しんさつ, each of which should keep its own timing rather than merging with the following sound;
  • the double consonant in やっきょく (pharmacy), which needs a clear pause before きょく; and
  • the seven morae in しょほうせん (prescription), which should not be shortened to five.

Practice reading the Core Vocabulary list aloud slowly, tapping out the mora count for each word before increasing your speed.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter authorizes「前から」, expanded experience with「ことがある」, 「ほうがいい」, procedure sequencing, and receptive recognition of「ようにしてください」.

1. 前から — stating how long something has continued

Purpose: to explain that a symptom or situation has continued since an earlier point in time.

  • 三日前から咳が続いています。I've had a cough continuing since three days ago.
  • 先週から熱があります。I've had a fever since last week.
  • 今朝から頭が痛いです。My head has hurt since this morning.

前から attaches to a time expression to mark the starting point of a continuing state. It commonly appears with 続いています or with a simple state such as あります or 痛いです.

2. Expanded ことがある — describing past experience

Purpose: to describe whether something has happened before, including health-related experiences relevant to a clinic visit.

  • 前にも同じ症状が出たことがあります。I have had the same symptoms appear before, too.
  • この薬を飲んだことがありますか。Have you ever taken this medicine?
  • アレルギーで困ったことはありません。I have not had trouble with allergies.

ことがある attaches to the た-form of a verb. This chapter extends its Volume II use into health and medical experience, building on the general experience pattern introduced earlier in the volume.

3. ほうがいい — giving and understanding advice

Purpose: to recommend a course of action, often used by a doctor, pharmacist, or friend giving practical advice.

  • 今日はゆっくり休んだほうがいいです。You should rest quietly today.
  • 熱が下がらない場合は、もう一度病院に行ったほうがいいです。If your fever doesn't go down, you should go to the clinic again.
  • この薬は食後に飲んだほうがいいです。You should take this medicine after meals.

ほうがいい attaches to the た-form for a recommendation and to the ない-form for advice against an action, as in 無理をしないほうがいいです (You shouldn't overdo it).

4. Sequencing a clinic or insurance procedure

Purpose: to describe the steps of a procedure in order, connecting earlier sequencing patterns to a clinic or insurance context.

  • まず受付で保険証を出して、それから問診票に記入します。First you hand over your insurance card at reception, and then you fill out the intake form.
  • 診察の後で、支払いをしてから薬局に行きます。After the examination, you make the payment and then go to the pharmacy.

This pattern recycles まず, それから, and 〜てから from earlier chapters, applying them to a new procedural context.

5. Recognizing ようにしてください

Purpose: to recognize a common instructional pattern used by clinic staff, without being required to produce it independently at this level.

  • 症状が続く場合は、必ず連絡するようにしてください。If your symptoms continue, please be sure to contact us. (recognition only)
  • 薬は決まった時間に飲むようにしてください。Please try to take your medicine at the set times. (recognition only)

ようにしてください softens an instruction into a gentle reminder. At this point in Volume II, learners should understand it when spoken or written by clinic staff, but are not required to produce it independently.

Building Block — Describing symptoms, giving history, and following advice
前から + symptom description + ことがある (past experience) + ほうがいい (advice)
A typical clinic conversation states when a symptom began, mentions relevant past experience, and closes with advice about what to do next.
三日前から熱が続いていて、前にも同じ症状が出たことがあります。今日はゆっくり休んだほうがいいと言われました。
I've had a fever continuing since three days ago, and I've had the same symptoms before, too. I was told I should rest quietly today.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Emma is at the reception desk of a clinic, and then speaking with the doctor.

受付 こんにちは。予約のお名前をお願いします。 Hello. Could I have the name on your appointment?
エマ エマです。十時に予約しました。 I'm Emma. I made an appointment for ten o'clock.
受付 保険証はお持ちですか。こちらの問診票にご記入ください。 Do you have your insurance card? Please fill out this intake form.
（エマは問診票に記入する。しばらくして、診察室に呼ばれる。）
医者 今日はどうしましたか。 What brings you in today?
エマ 三日前から咳が続いていて、今朝から少し熱もあります。 I've had a cough continuing since three days ago, and I've had a slight fever since this morning too.
医者 前にも同じような症状が出たことがありますか。 Have similar symptoms appeared before, too?
エマ いいえ、こんなに続いたことはありません。 No, it has never continued like this before.
医者 分かりました。今日はゆっくり休んだほうがいいですね。処方箋を出しますので、薬局で薬をもらってください。 I understand. You should rest quietly today. I'll write you a prescription, so please get the medicine at the pharmacy.
エマ 分かりました。支払いは受付でしますか。 Understood. Do I make the payment at reception?
医者 はい、診察の後で受付にお願いします。症状が続く場合は、次回また来るようにしてください。 Yes, please go to reception after the examination. If your symptoms continue, please come again next time.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. What time was Emma's appointment, and what does the receptionist ask her to fill out?
  2. Since when has Emma had a cough, and since when has she had a fever?
  3. Has Emma had these symptoms continue this long before?
  4. What does the doctor tell Emma to do, and where should she go after the examination?

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Clinic procedures and insurance

Clinics commonly ask first-time patients to complete an intake form and to present an insurance card at reception before the examination. Payment is typically handled at reception after the examination rather than during it. Procedures can vary between individual clinics, hospitals, and pharmacies, so reading posted notices and confirming with staff, as shown in this chapter's reading passage, remains more reliable than assuming one fixed procedure applies everywhere.

Regional Note — Access to clinics and pharmacies

The availability of clinics, specialists, and pharmacies varies between larger cities and smaller towns or rural areas. In some areas, a patient may need to travel farther for certain kinds of care, while in others, a clinic and pharmacy may be within easy walking distance of each other. Checking current information for the specific area remains more reliable than assuming one pattern of access applies everywhere.

Register Note — Doctor's advice and patient explanation

A doctor's advice, such as ほうがいいですね or ようにしてください, is typically phrased gently even when the advice is firm. A patient describing symptoms usually keeps the explanation brief and factual, focusing on when the symptom began and whether it has occurred before, rather than adding extensive detail. Both sides typically remain in polite です／ます style during a clinic visit, even between people who do not know each other well.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing 前に and 前から

Error: *三日前に熱が続いています。 (using 前に, which sequences two actions, instead of marking a continuing starting point).

Correction: 三日前から熱が続いています。 — 前から marks the starting point of a continuing state, while 前に sequences one action before another, as taught in Chapter 8.

Common Mistake — Attaching ほうがいい to the wrong verb form

Error: *休むほうがいいです。 as the only form used for both recommending and warning against an action, without adjusting for the negative.

Correction: Use the た-form for a positive recommendation, as in 休んだほうがいいです, and the ない-form for advice against an action, as in 無理をしないほうがいいです.

Common Mistake — Treating ようにしてください as required production

Error: Attempting to freely produce new sentences with ようにしてください in speaking or writing tasks, beyond quoting or recognizing it in a reading or listening passage.

Correction: At this point in Volume II, ようにしてください is receptive only. Recognize its meaning when a doctor, pharmacist, or notice uses it, but rely on ほうがいい or a direct request pattern for your own productive advice or instructions.
