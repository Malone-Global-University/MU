条件と選択Conditions and Choices

Conditionals and decision-making · Speak and Write Japanese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe conditions and their results using 「たら」.
  2. Use 「なら」 to respond to a situation someone else has raised.
  3. Recognize a limited, natural-result use of 「と」 in short conditional patterns.
  4. Ask and answer whether something is the case, using 「かどうか」.
  5. Talk about plans, weather, money, time, and availability using this chapter's vocabulary.
  6. Recognize and produce kanji for condition, choice, decision, possibility, and impossibility.
  7. Discuss two or more options and explain a decision, giving reasons and conditions.

Communication Scenario

Following the neighborhood association meeting in Chapter 17, Emma, Yuki, and a few volunteers are planning a small community clean-up event. But nothing about the event is fixed yet: the date depends on the weather, the location depends on the budget, and some volunteers are not sure whether they are free that day. As Emma and Yuki work through these open questions together, they need language for describing conditions, responding to situations, and stating whether something is decided or not — the practical decision-making language this chapter develops, and an important step toward Unit IV's final integrated project.

  1. Someone describes a condition and its result using たら.
  2. Someone responds to a situation using なら.
  3. Someone asks whether something is decided, using かどうか.

Script Note — 決める and 決まる share 決 but differ in okurigana and transitivity

The character 決 appears in both 決める (きめる, to decide something) and 決まる (きまる, for something to be decided). The okurigana marks which verb is intended and which particle pattern to expect: 決める normally takes an object with を, while 決まる is intransitive and normally takes が. Learn the pair together, as this volume's transitivity-pair policy requires, rather than treating them as one interchangeable word.

Pronunciation Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Long vowels and moraic ん in decision vocabulary

Several words in this chapter combine a long vowel with moraic ん or with a geminate consonant. Practice the following words carefully:

  • 条件 (じょうけん): long vowel in じょう followed by moraic ん in けん; do not shorten じょう to じ.
  • 決定 (けってい): geminate っ followed by a long vowel in てい; hold the small っ for a full mora before releasing て.
  • 可能 (かのう): long vowel in のう; keep の and う distinct in timing from a short のvowel.

When stating a condition and its result in one sentence, keep phrase-level rhythm even across the clause boundary before たら or なら, rather than pausing awkwardly in the middle of the conditional form.

Grammar and Sentence Patterns

This chapter develops practical language for describing conditions and making decisions. It introduces 「たら」 for conditions and their results, 「なら」 for responding to a situation, a limited recognition-and-production use of 「と」 for natural or automatic results, and 「かどうか」 for asking or stating whether something is the case.

1. たら — a condition and its result

Purpose: to describe what will happen, should happen, or is planned if a condition is met.

  • 天気がよかったら、公園で会いましょう。If the weather is good, let's meet at the park.verb/adjective たら-form + result clause
  • 予算が足りなかったら、場所を変更するかもしれません。If the budget isn't enough, we might change the location.
  • その日、都合が悪かったら、教えてください。If that day is inconvenient for you, please let me know.

たら attaches to the past-tense plain form of a verb or adjective, but the meaning is not necessarily past; たら describes a condition, and the result clause can be a plan, a request, a suggestion, or a possibility.

2. なら — responding to a situation

Purpose: to give advice, a suggestion, or a plan that responds directly to a situation someone else has just raised.

  • A：土曜日は都合が悪いんです。
    B：土曜日なら、日曜日はどうですか。    A: Saturday doesn't work for me. B: If it's Saturday (that's the problem), how about Sunday?
  • 予算が心配なら、公園で無料のイベントにしましょう。If it's the budget you're worried about, let's make it a free event at the park.

なら responds to something already mentioned or assumed in the conversation; unlike たら, it does not describe a general future condition so much as react to the specific situation just raised. Compare the two: 「雨が降ったら」 describes a general condition about rain, while 「雨なら」 responds to someone having just said that it might rain.

3. と — a limited, natural or automatic result

Purpose: to describe a result that follows naturally, automatically, or predictably from a condition, within a small set of practiced patterns.

  • この道をまっすぐ行くと、公園があります。If you go straight along this road, there's a park.
  • 予算を増やすと、選択肢が増えます。If you increase the budget, the options increase.

Productive use of と in this chapter is limited to natural or automatic results, such as directions or predictable cause-and-effect relationships. と is not used for plans, requests, or one-time personal decisions; those situations use たら or なら instead. Model new と sentences closely on the patterns above.

4. かどうか — asking or stating whether something is the case

Purpose: to ask about, or report, whether something is true, decided, or possible, without specifying which of two outcomes it is.

  • その日、都合がいいかどうか、教えてください。Please let me know whether that day works for you or not.
  • まだ、場所が決まったかどうか、わかりません。I don't yet know whether the location has been decided or not.
  • 予算内で可能かどうか、確認します。I'll check whether it's possible within the budget or not.

かどうか attaches to the plain form of a verb or adjective and functions as an embedded question inside a larger sentence; it does not require an explicit question word, since the uncertainty is between "yes" and "no."

Building Block — Describing an undecided plan
state a condition and its result (たら) → respond to a raised concern (なら) → note a natural result (と, limited) → state what is not yet known (かどうか)
A natural discussion of an undecided plan moves from describing what will happen under certain conditions, to responding directly to someone's concern, to noting an automatic consequence, to honestly stating what still needs to be confirmed. This chapter's Model Dialogue and Performance Task ask you to move through these stages in order.
天気がよかったら、公園でイベントをしましょう。予算が心配なら、無料の活動にすればいいと思います。予算を増やすと、選択肢も増えます。でも、その日、みんなの都合がいいかどうか、まだわかりません。
If the weather is good, let's hold the event at the park. If it's the budget you're worried about, I think we should just make it a free activity. If we increase the budget, the options increase too. But I still don't know whether the day works for everyone or not.

Grammar Practice

  1. Write one sentence describing a condition and a result, using たら.
  2. Write one short exchange in which one person raises a situation and the other responds, using なら.
  3. Write one sentence describing a natural or automatic result, using と (you may model it closely on the examples above).
  4. Write one sentence stating something you do not yet know, using かどうか.
  5. Combine your sentences from items 1 through 4 into one short connected discussion of an undecided plan.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Emma and Yuki are planning the community clean-up event with a volunteer, サトウさん.

エマ 天気がよかったら、来週の土曜日に公園でイベントをしませんか。 If the weather is good, shall we hold the event at the park next Saturday?
サトウ 土曜日ですか。実は、土曜日は都合が悪いんです。 Saturday? Actually, Saturday is inconvenient for me.
ユキ 土曜日どようびなら、日曜日にちようびはどうですか。日曜日なら、空いていますか。 If it's Saturday (that's the problem), how about Sunday? If it's Sunday, are you free?
サトウ はい、日曜日なら大丈夫です。それから、予算よさんはどうなっていますか。 Yes, if it's Sunday, that's fine. Also, how is the budget looking?
エマ 予算よさんが心配なら、無料の活動かつどうにすればいいと思います。予算よさんを増やすと、選択肢せんたくしも増えますが、無理をしなくてもいいと思います。 If it's the budget you're worried about, I think we should just make it a free activity. If we increase the budget, the options increase too, but I don't think we need to push ourselves.
ユキ 場所はもう決きまりましたか。 Has the location already been decided?
エマ まだ決きまっていません。公園が可能かのうかどうか、今週中に確認します。 It hasn't been decided yet. I'll check whether the park is possible or not by the end of this week.
サトウ わかりました。決きまったら、教えてください。 I understand. Please let me know once it's decided.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. Why does Sato say Saturday does not work, and what day do they agree on instead?
  2. What does Emma suggest if the budget is a concern?
  3. What has not yet been decided, and what will Emma check?

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Weather-dependent planning

Outdoor community events in Japan, such as festivals, clean-ups, and sports days, are commonly planned with a stated rain contingency, often called 雨天中止 (canceled in case of rain) or 雨天決行 (held rain or shine). Announcing the weather condition clearly in advance, as in this chapter's dialogue and reading, is a normal and expected part of practical event planning rather than a sign of indecision.

Regional Note — Public facility and park booking practices vary

Whether a public park or facility can be booked for a community event, and how far in advance, depends on the municipality and the specific facility. Some areas allow informal use of public parks, while others require a reservation or a permit. Checking local rules directly, rather than assuming that a park is automatically available, is common practical advice.

Register Note — なら as a responsive, collaborative style

Notice that Yuki responds to Sato's concern with 土曜日なら、日曜日はどうですか rather than simply repeating or ignoring the problem. Directly echoing the situation someone has raised, using なら, signals that their concern has been heard and is a comfortable, collaborative pattern in ordinary polite です／ます planning conversations.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Using と for plans or one-time decisions

Error: *天気がいいと、公園に行きます。 using と to describe a one-time plan for a specific day, rather than a general or natural result.

Correction: 天気がよかったら、公園に行きます。 — と is limited in this chapter to natural or automatic results, such as directions or predictable outcomes; a specific plan or personal decision about a particular day should use たら instead.

Common Mistake — Using なら for a general future condition

Error: *来年、日本に行くなら、京都に行きたいです。 as an opening statement with no prior situation to respond to.

Correction: This sentence is acceptable once someone has already raised the idea of going to Japan; if you are introducing the idea for the first time rather than responding to it, use たら instead, as in 来年、日本に行ったら、京都に行きたいです。 なら works best as a direct response to something already mentioned.

Common Mistake — Adding a question word inside かどうか

Error: *予算が何かどうか、わかりません。 combining かどうか with a question word such as 何.

Correction: 予算が足りるかどうか、わかりません。 — かどうか is used only for yes-or-no uncertainty; when the uncertainty concerns a specific piece of information, such as what, when, or who, use a plain question word with か instead, such as 何が必要か.
