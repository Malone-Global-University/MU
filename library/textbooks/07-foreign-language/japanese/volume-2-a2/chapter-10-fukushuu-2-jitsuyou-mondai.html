復習２：実用問題を解決するUnit II Assessment: Solving Practical Problems

Integrated review and assessment of Chapters 6–9 · Speak and Write Japanese: Everyday Independence and Connected Communication

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Explain a problem that happened outside the home, using explanatory 「んです」 and 「てしまいました」 to give background and describe an unwanted result.
  2. Negotiate a return, exchange, or reservation, using 「なら」, 「場合」, 「ことができます」, and 「てもらえますか」 to state conditions and make polite requests.
  3. Describe travel plans and preferences, using 「つもりです」, 「予定です」, and sequencing with 「前に」 and 「後で」.
  4. Describe a health-related situation, using 「前から」, expanded 「ことがある」, and 「ほうがいい」, and recognize 「ようにしてください」 without independent production.
  5. Read and respond to practical notices, forms, and messages drawn from service, travel, and clinic contexts.
  6. Complete an integrated written and spoken task that combines problem-solving, negotiation, travel, and health language from Unit II.

Communication Scenario

Emma's trip with Yuki is approaching. In the same week, she needs to sort out a problem with a delayed delivery, confirm a hotel reservation with a condition attached, finalize her travel plans, and follow up after her clinic visit before she leaves. This chapter brings all four situations together into one connected review of Unit II.

  1. This chapter is Unit II's assessment chapter. It does not introduce new grammar or new productive kanji; instead, it reviews and integrates the language from Chapters 6 through 9.
  2. By the end of the chapter, you will be able to move between explaining a problem, negotiating a transaction, describing travel plans, and discussing a health follow-up, all in one connected set of tasks.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review — Long vowels, gemination, and moraic ん across Unit II

Review the following contrasts, drawn from across this unit, before completing the listening and speaking assessments:

  • the double consonant in 故障 (こしょう) and やっきょく (pharmacy), which needs a clear pause before the following mora;
  • the long vowels in 交換 (こうかん), 宿泊 (しゅくはく), and 病院 (びょういん);
  • the moraic ん in 確認 (かくにん), 乗り換え's related forms, and 保険証 (ほけんしょう), each of which should keep its own timing; and
  • phrase-level intonation when sequencing several steps in a row, as in a procedure explanation.

Grammar Review

This chapter reviews and assesses explanatory 「んです」, obligation and permission patterns, reservation and return language, travel sequencing, and health-related sequencing from Chapters 6 through 9. No new grammar is introduced.

1. Explaining a problem — んです and てしまいました

Purpose: to give background for a problem and describe an unwanted result, as introduced in Chapter 6.

  • かさを忘れてしまったんです。The thing is, I ended up forgetting my umbrella.
  • 予約の時間に間に合わなかったんです。The thing is, I didn't make it in time for the reservation.

2. Negotiating conditions — なら, 場合, ことができます, てもらえますか

Purpose: to state a condition and make a polite request, as introduced in Chapter 7.

  • レシートがあるなら、交換することができます。If you have the receipt, an exchange is possible.
  • サイズが合わない場合は、返品してもらえますか。If the size doesn't fit, could you have it returned for me?

3. Describing travel plans — つもりです, 予定です, 前に／後で

Purpose: to describe intentions, fixed plans, and the order of travel steps, as introduced in Chapter 8.

  • 新幹線に乗る前に、駅弁を買うつもりです。Before getting on the shinkansen, I intend to buy a station lunch box.
  • ホテルに着いた後で、荷物を置いて出かける予定です。After arriving at the hotel, my plan is to leave my luggage and go out.

4. Describing a health situation — 前から, ことがある, ほうがいい

Purpose: to state how long a symptom has continued, mention past experience, and give or receive advice, as introduced in Chapter 9.

  • 三日前から咳が続いています。I've had a cough continuing since three days ago.
  • 今日はゆっくり休んだほうがいいです。You should rest quietly today.

「ようにしてください」remains receptive only at this point in Volume II; recognize it in the reading and listening tasks below, but do not produce it independently.

Building Block — Moving between four practical situations
explain a problem (んです／てしまいました) → negotiate a solution (なら／場合／てもらえますか) → confirm a plan (つもりです／予定です) → follow up on health (前から／ことがある／ほうがいい)
A single connected account can move through all four Unit II domains, using each chapter's key pattern to link one situation to the next.
荷物が届かなかったんです。レシートがあるので、交換してもらえますか。それから、新幹線に乗る前にホテルに確認の電話をするつもりです。実は三日前から少し熱もあるので、出発の前にもう一度病院に行ったほうがいいと思っています。
The thing is, my luggage didn't arrive. Since I have the receipt, could you exchange it for me? After that, before getting on the shinkansen, I intend to call the hotel to confirm. Actually, I've also had a slight fever since three days ago, so I think I should go to the clinic once more before departure.

Grammar Practice

  1. Write one sentence using んです or てしまいました to explain a problem.
  2. Write one sentence using なら or 場合 to state a condition for a return or reservation.
  3. Write one sentence using つもりです or 予定です to describe a travel plan.
  4. Write one sentence using 前から and ほうがいい together to describe a health follow-up.
  5. Combine two of your sentences above into one connected pair of sentences using それから or それで.

Integrated Dialogue

Setting: Emma calls a hotel to confirm her reservation and mentions a delivery problem and her recent clinic visit while making final arrangements before her trip with Yuki.

ホテル お電話ありがとうございます。ご予約の確認でしょうか。 Thank you for calling. Is this about confirming a reservation?
エマ はい。実は、先週注文した荷物が届かなかったんです。宿泊の予約は大丈夫か、確認したくて。 Yes. Actually, a package I ordered last week didn't arrive, so I wanted to confirm that the lodging reservation is still fine.
ホテル それは大変でしたね。宿泊の予約のほうは問題ございません。何かご変更がある場合は、いつでもご連絡ください。 That must have been difficult. The lodging reservation itself has no problems. If there's ever any change, please contact us anytime.
エマ ありがとうございます。それから、新幹線の切符ですが、乗り換えなしの経路にする予定です。 Thank you. Also, regarding the shinkansen ticket, my plan is to use a route without a transfer.
ホテル 分かりました。チェックインの前に、何かご希望はございますか。 I understand. Before check-in, is there anything else you'd like?
エマ 実は、三日前から少し熱があって、病院に行ってきたんです。今日はゆっくり休んだほうがいいと言われたので、部屋で少し休めたらうれしいです。 Actually, I've had a slight fever since three days ago, and I went to the clinic. I was told I should rest quietly today, so it would be nice to be able to rest a little in the room.
ホテル お大事になさってください。チェックインは何時でも大丈夫ですので、ご安心ください。 Please take care of yourself. Check-in is fine at any time, so please don't worry.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. What problem does Emma mention at the beginning of the call, and why does she call the hotel?
  2. What does the hotel say about the lodging reservation?
  3. What kind of shinkansen route does Emma plan to take?
  4. What health information does Emma share, and how does the hotel respond?

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Combining service, travel, and health communication

In practice, a single week of daily life often mixes several kinds of practical communication, such as a delivery problem, a hotel confirmation, and a clinic visit. Businesses and institutions typically expect customers to state conditions clearly, such as whether a receipt is available, while also responding with polite reassurance, as in お大事になさってください (please take care of yourself). Reading and listening for these combined situations, rather than treating each one as an isolated script, better reflects how practical Japanese communication actually unfolds.

Regional Note — Variation across service types

Policies for returns, reservation changes, and clinic follow-up can vary between individual businesses, regions, and types of establishment. A convenience store, a department store, a small local shop, and a hotel may each handle a similar request somewhat differently. Confirming the specific policy of the business or institution in question, as modeled in the notice in the Reading Assessment, remains more reliable than assuming one policy applies everywhere.

Register Note — Staying polite across a mixed conversation

Even when a conversation moves between several topics, such as a delivery problem, a reservation, and a health concern, both speakers in a service or institutional context typically remain in polite です／ます style throughout, as shown in the Integrated Dialogue. Plain style may appear inside a quotation, such as reporting what a doctor said, without changing the overall polite register of the conversation.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Switching registers mid-conversation

Error: *荷物が届かなかった。交換してもらえますか。 (mixing an abrupt plain-style statement with a polite request in the same service conversation).

Correction: 荷物が届かなかったんです。交換してもらえますか。 — んです softens the plain-style background information so it fits naturally within a polite conversation, as reviewed from Chapter 6.

Common Mistake — Confusing つもりです and 予定です

Error: *ホテルの予約はチェックインするつもりです。 (using つもりです for a fixed reservation detail that is already arranged).

Correction: チェックインは三時の予定です。 — 予定です describes a fixed schedule or arrangement, while つもりです describes a personal intention, as reviewed from Chapter 8.

Common Mistake — Treating a review chapter as a place for new patterns

Error: Introducing an unreviewed or unscheduled grammar pattern into a Unit II assessment task because it seems useful for the topic.

Correction: This chapter only reviews and connects language already authorized in Chapters 6 through 9. If a new situation seems to require unscheduled language, simplify the situation or rely on an already authorized pattern instead.
