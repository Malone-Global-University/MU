データと比較Data and Comparison

Charts, trends, numbers, and cautious claims · Speak and Write Japanese: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Recognize and use vocabulary for graphs, surveys, percentages, rankings, trends, preferences, and costs.
  2. Use 増える and 減る to describe increase and decrease.
  3. Use 〜に比べて to compare a figure or situation against a earlier or alternative point of reference.
  4. Use 割合 to describe a proportion or rate within a whole.
  5. Use 約 to present an approximate rather than exact figure.
  6. Use cautious claim language to describe what a chart or survey suggests without overstating certainty.
  7. Read and summarize a short passage describing a simple chart or survey result.
  8. Speak and write about a simple set of data using only vocabulary and structures explicitly introduced or previously activated.

Communication Scenario

Emma's newsletter piece on environmental education went well, and 中村さん has asked her to help present the results of a short community survey about the recycling project at the next volunteer meeting. The survey asked residents how often they participate in recycling activities and whether their participation has changed compared with last year. Emma has a simple chart of the results and needs a way to describe increases and decreases, compare this year with last year, state proportions, give approximate numbers, and describe what the data suggests without claiming more certainty than the small survey supports.

  1. The learner describes at least one increase or decrease using 増える or 減る.
  2. The learner makes at least one comparison using 〜に比べて.
  3. The learner states at least one proportion using 割合.
  4. The learner gives at least one approximate figure using 約.
  5. The learner uses cautious claim language at least once when interpreting the data.

Script Strategy — Reading numbers inside sentences

Chapter texts about data typically mix Arabic numerals for statistics with kanji vocabulary for the surrounding explanation, for example 回答者の約六十パーセントが「増えた」と答えた. Practice reading such sentences as a whole unit: identify the numeral, the counter or percent marker attached to it, and the verb that the number modifies, rather than reading each element in isolation.

As in earlier chapters, do not attempt every possible reading of a new compound. Learn each item, such as 割合 or 傾向, as a single lexical unit tied to its use in describing data.

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Reading percentages and approximate numbers

Percentages and approximate figures often appear together, as in 約六十パーセント or 約半分. Practice keeping 約 lightly attached to the number that follows it, with no pause between them, so that the approximation reads as a single phrase rather than as a separate word.

  • 約六十パーセントが増えたと答えた。 — about sixty percent answered that it had increased
  • 去年に比べて、割合が下がった。 — compared with last year, the proportion has dropped
  • この傾向は続くかもしれない。 — this trend may continue

Grammar: Describing Data and Trends

This chapter authorizes 増える and 減る, 〜に比べて, 割合, 約, and cautious claim language built on かもしれない and related forms already active from Chapter 18's preview and earlier stance work. Together these let you describe a chart or survey clearly while showing appropriate caution about what small data sets can and cannot prove.

増える and 減る — increase and decrease

Purpose: to describe a quantity, rate, or number going up or down over time.

  • リサイクルに参加する人が増えている。The number of people participating in recycling is increasing.増える + ている, ongoing trend
  • 去年よりごみの量が減った。The amount of trash decreased compared with last year.減る, past, plain
  • 今年、参加者が約二十人増えた。This year, participants increased by about twenty people.quantity + 増えた

増える and 減る are both intransitive; the thing that increases or decreases is marked with が, not を. Use ている for an ongoing trend and plain past for a completed change already confirmed by the data.

〜に比べて — comparing against a reference point

Purpose: to compare a current figure, situation, or trend against an earlier or alternative point of reference.

  • 去年に比べて、参加者が増えた。Compared with last year, the number of participants increased.Noun + に比べて + comparison clause
  • 他の地域に比べて、この地域は参加率が高い。Compared with other areas, this area has a high participation rate.に比べて marks 他の地域 as the reference point

〜に比べて attaches to the noun that serves as the point of comparison. The main clause then states how the current situation differs from that reference point.

割合 — describing a proportion

Purpose: to describe what part of a whole a group or response represents.

  • 「増えた」と答えた人の割合は約六十パーセントだった。The proportion of people who answered "it increased" was about sixty percent.Noun + の割合は + approximate figure
  • 若い回答者の割合が高い調査だった。It was a survey with a high proportion of young respondents.割合が高い／低い, common collocation

約 — giving an approximate figure

Purpose: to present a number as approximate rather than exact, appropriate when working from survey data or rounded figures.

  • この調査には約百人が参加した。About one hundred people participated in this survey.約 + Number + Counter
  • 費用は約五千円だった。The cost was about five thousand yen.約 + Price

約 precedes the number it modifies and signals that the figure has been rounded. Use it whenever a chart or survey source itself presents a rounded rather than exact figure.

Cautious claim language for interpreting data

Purpose: to state what a small chart or survey suggests without claiming more certainty than the data supports.

Building Block — Interpreting limited data carefully
[観察]。ただし、[かもしれない／とは言えない／と考えられる]。
State the observation from the data first, then add a cautious qualifier that limits how strongly the observation should be read.
参加者は増えているようだ。ただし、この調査だけでは、理由まではっきりとは言えない。
It seems that participants are increasing. However, based on this survey alone, we cannot say clearly what the reason is.
  • この傾向は今後も続くかもしれない。This trend may continue in the future.かもしれない, cautious possibility
  • 回答者の数が少ないので、全体を表しているとは言えない。Because the number of respondents is small, it cannot be said that this represents the whole picture.とは言えない, cautious limitation
  • 好みが変わってきていると考えられる。It can be considered that preferences have been changing.と考えられる, cautious interpretation

Grammar Practice

  1. Write one sentence using 増える or 減る to describe a change in a number of your choice.
  2. Write one sentence using 〜に比べて to compare two years, two places, or two groups.
  3. Write one sentence using 割合 to state a proportion, with an approximate figure marked by 約.
  4. Write one sentence that states an observation from data and then adds a cautious qualifier using かもしれない, とは言えない, or と考えられる.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Emma and 中村さん look over the recycling survey results together before the volunteer meeting.

中村さん 調査の結果は、どうでしたか。 How did the survey results turn out?
エマ 去年に比べて、参加者が増えていました。「増えた」と答えた人の割合は、約六十パーセントでした。 Compared with last year, participants had increased. The proportion of people who answered "it increased" was about sixty percent.
中村さん それはいい傾向ですね。減ったと答えた人もいましたか。 That's a good trend, isn't it. Were there also people who answered that it had decreased?
エマ 少しいました。でも、回答者の数が約五十人しかいないので、全体の傾向をはっきり表しているとは言えないと思います。 There were a few. But since there are only about fifty respondents, I don't think it can be said that this clearly represents the overall trend.
中村さん その注意は大切ですね。会議では、その点も説明してください。 That caution is important. Please explain that point at the meeting too.
エマ はい。グラフを見せながら、その割合と、今後も傾向が続くかもしれないという点を、両方説明します。 Yes. While showing the graph, I'll explain both that proportion and the point that the trend may continue going forward.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. What proportion of respondents answered that participation had increased?
  2. Why does Emma say the survey cannot clearly represent the overall trend?
  3. What two points does Emma plan to explain at the meeting?

Writing Workshop

In this workshop you will draft a short written summary of a simple data set, similar to the one Emma is preparing for the volunteer meeting.

  1. Choose a simple data set about a familiar topic, either real or invented for practice, with at least one number that changes over time or across groups.
  2. Write an opening sentence stating who or what the data describes and giving an approximate total using 約.
  3. Write one or two sentences describing an increase or decrease using 増える or 減る.
  4. Write one sentence comparing the current figure with an earlier or alternative point of reference using 〜に比べて.

  • My passage includes at least one comparison using 〜に比べて.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Small community surveys and public reporting

Community groups, volunteer organizations, and neighborhood associations in Japan, as in many places, regularly present small, informal surveys at meetings, in newsletters, and on notice boards to support local decisions. Such reports commonly note approximate sample sizes and add cautious qualifiers rather than presenting small surveys as fully representative, a habit this chapter's cautious claim language reflects.

Regional Note — Local variation in participation and reporting habits

How often communities collect and share this kind of local data, and how formally it is presented, can vary by region, by the size of the group, and by the organization involved. The patterns in this chapter represent common general practices rather than a single fixed national standard.

Language Note — Hedging across languages

Many languages have set ways of softening a claim drawn from limited data, such as English "it seems that..." or "this may suggest...". Comparing how your own language signals caution can help you notice that Japanese expressions such as かもしれない and と考えられる serve a similar function: they let a speaker report a pattern without claiming more certainty than the data supports.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Marking 増える or 減る with を instead of が

Error: *参加者を増えました。 treats 増える as if it were a transitive verb.

Correction: 参加者が増えました。 — 増える and 減る are intransitive; the quantity that changes is marked with が.

Common Mistake — Overstating what a small survey shows

Error: *この調査で、みんなの意見が分かった。 claims certainty from data described as small in the same passage.

Correction: この調査だけでは、全体の意見をはっきり表しているとは言えない。 — match the strength of the claim to the size and limits of the data described.

Common Mistake — Dropping 約 and presenting a rounded number as exact

Error: *回答者は五十人でした。 used when the actual source figure was only approximate.

Correction: 回答者は約五十人でした。 — use 約 whenever the source figure itself has been rounded or estimated.
