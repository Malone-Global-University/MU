専門に近づくApproaching Specialized Topics

Domain language and controlled terminology · Speak and Write Japanese: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Recognize and use domain vocabulary for education, technology, business, health, environment, law-adjacent public information, and culture.
  2. Use に関する and に対する to frame a noun phrase around a specialized topic.
  3. Use に対して to mark a target of an action, attitude, or comparison.
  4. Use として to identify a role, capacity, or category.
  5. Build a short definition of a term using a controlled definition frame.
  6. Read a short passage that introduces a specialized topic to a general audience.
  7. Write and speak about one domain topic using only vocabulary and structures explicitly introduced or previously activated.

Communication Scenario

Emma has finished her volunteer packet and now wants to introduce herself as more than a general volunteer: the recycling project's coordinators have asked each volunteer to prepare a one-page introduction of a topic they know something about, written so that someone outside the field can still follow it. Emma decides to write about environmental education for the community newsletter. To do this, she needs a way to talk about a specialized topic without assuming the reader already knows the field, and a way to define a few unfamiliar terms clearly and briefly.

  1. The learner selects one general domain (education, technology, business, health, environment, law-adjacent public information, or culture) as the topic of a short introduction.
  2. The learner uses に関する or に対する to frame the topic as a noun phrase at least once.
  3. The learner defines at least one term from the domain using a controlled definition frame.
  4. The learner uses として at least once to identify their own role or a role within the topic.

Script Strategy — Recognizing compound domain nouns

Many of this chapter's new words are two-kanji Sino-Japanese compounds, such as 環境 and 経済, that appear frequently in headings, article titles, and topic labels. Once you recognize a compound as a domain label, you can predict that it will often appear with に関する or について nearby, since domain labels are usually the topic being framed, not the main verb of the sentence.

As in Chapter 15, do not attempt to read every possible reading of these characters. Learn each compound as a single lexical item tied to its domain.

れんしゅう — Practice: Cover the readings in the kanji cards above and try to read each compound aloud from memory, then check your answers.

Pronunciation and Listening Focus

Pronunciation Focus — Reading domain compounds smoothly

Long strings of Sino-Japanese compounds, such as 環境教育に関する研究, can tempt learners to pause awkwardly between every word. Practice reading these phrases as connected units, with a single main stress fall inside the whole noun phrase rather than a separate emphasis on every compound, and with clear mora timing preserved inside each word.

  • 環境教育に関する研究 — research related to environmental education
  • この分野の専門家 — a specialist in this field
  • 経済政策に対する意見 — an opinion regarding economic policy

Grammar: Framing a Specialized Topic

This chapter authorizes に関する, に対して, として, and a controlled definition frame. These four tools let you introduce a topic, mark a target of attention or comparison, identify a role, and define an unfamiliar term, all without requiring vocabulary or grammar beyond this volume's scope.

に関する — framing a topic as a noun phrase

Purpose: to attach a topic to a noun, turning "about X" into a noun-modifying phrase, useful for titles, headings, and topic sentences.

  • 環境に関する記事を読んだ。I read an article about the environment.Noun + に関する + Noun
  • これは教育に関する研究です。This is research about education.に関する modifies 研究
  • 健康に関して、何か質問がありますか。Regarding health, do you have any questions?に関して, adverbial form, "regarding"

に関する modifies a following noun; に関して functions more like an adverbial phrase, "regarding" or "with respect to." に対する works similarly to に関する but more strongly implies a directed attitude, response, or relationship, rather than simply a topic.

に対して — marking a target

Purpose: to mark the target of an action, attitude, comparison, or contrast.

  • この政策に対して、賛成の人も反対の人もいる。Regarding this policy, there are both people who agree and people who disagree.に対して marks 政策 as the target of differing attitudes
  • 住民の質問に対して、担当者が丁寧に答えた。The person in charge answered the residents' questions politely.に対して marks 質問 as the target of 答えた

として — identifying a role or category

Purpose: to identify something or someone in a particular role, capacity, or category.

  • ボランティアとして、このプロジェクトに参加しています。I am participating in this project as a volunteer.として marks role: volunteer
  • この言葉は専門用語として使われている。This word is used as a specialized term.として marks category: specialized term

A controlled definition frame

Purpose: to define an unfamiliar term briefly and clearly for a general reader, without leaving the chapter's authorized grammar.

Building Block — Defining a term
[用語]とは、[分かりやすい説明]ことだ／ことです。
とは introduces the term being defined; the explanation that follows should use only vocabulary and grammar already active in the course.
環境教育とは、環境について学び、考えることです。
Environmental education is learning and thinking about the environment.
  • 「専門家」とは、ある分野について詳しく知っている人のことです。A "specialist" is a person who knows a great deal about a certain field.とは...ことです definition frame
  • 「政策」とは、政府や団体が決める方針のことだ。"Policy" is the course of action decided by a government or organization.plain-style definition frame

Grammar Practice

  1. Write one noun phrase using に関する to describe an article or a book about a domain of your choice.
  2. Write one sentence using に対して to describe a reaction to a policy, question, or plan.
  3. Write one sentence using として to identify your own role in a project, class, or activity.
  4. Write one definition using とは...ことです for a term from this chapter's vocabulary list.

Model Dialogue

Setting: Emma talks with 中村さん about her one-page domain introduction for the newsletter.

中村さん エマさんは、どんな分野について書くつもりですか。 Emma, what field are you planning to write about?
エマ 環境教育に関することを書こうと思っています。ボランティアとして、この分野に興味がありますから。 I'm thinking of writing something about environmental education. As a volunteer, I'm interested in this field.
中村さん いいですね。専門用語を使うときは、説明も必要ですね。 That's good. When you use specialized terms, you'll also need explanations, won't you.
エマ はい。「環境教育とは、環境について学び、考えることです」という文で始めるつもりです。 Yes. I'm planning to begin with the sentence, "Environmental education is learning and thinking about the environment."
中村さん 分かりやすいですね。政策に対する意見も書きますか。 That's easy to understand. Will you also write your opinion about policy?
エマ 少しだけ書きます。難しい用語は使いすぎないようにします。 I'll write just a little. I'll try not to use too many difficult terms.

ないようりかい — Comprehension questions:

  1. What domain does Emma plan to write about, and why?
  2. How does Emma plan to open her piece?
  3. What does Emma say she will try to avoid?

きくタスク — Introducing a field

今日は、環境教育という分野について発表します。環境教育とは、環境について学び、考えることです。この分野の専門家は、学校や地域でさまざまな活動を行っています。政策に対しては、いろいろな意見がありますが、多くの人が関心を持っています。私はボランティアとして、この分野にもっと詳しくなりたいと思っています。

Reading Practice

環境教育について — About environmental education

Writing Workshop

In this workshop you will draft a short one-page domain introduction, similar to the one Emma is preparing for the newsletter.

  1. Choose a domain from this chapter's seven domains, and identify one term from that domain that a general reader might not know.
  2. Write an opening sentence using に関する or に関して to introduce your topic.
  3. Write a definition of your chosen term using the とは...ことです frame.
  4. Write one or two sentences describing a reaction, opinion, or debate related to the topic, using に対して.
  5. Write a closing sentence identifying your own role or interest in the topic, using として.
  6. Revise your passage using the checklist below.

チェックリスト — Editing checklist:

  • My opening sentence clearly frames the topic using に関する or に関して.
  • My definition uses the とは...ことです frame and stays within vocabulary and grammar already studied.
  • I have used に対して at least once to mark a target of attitude, response, or comparison.
  • I have used として at least once to identify a role or category.
  • My passage avoids unexplained specialized terminology beyond the one term I define.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Writing for a general audience

Community newsletters, public information pamphlets, and introductory articles in Japan, as in many places, are often written by people who are not professional specialists but who have direct experience with a topic. These texts commonly open with a short definition of a key term before moving into opinion or detail, a pattern this chapter's definition frame reflects.

Regional Note — Variation in specialized terminology

Terminology for fields such as policy, technology, and health can vary somewhat by institution, industry, and region, and new terms continue to enter use as fields change. The vocabulary in this chapter represents common, general-audience terms rather than a complete technical vocabulary for any one field.

Language Note — Definitions across languages

Many languages use a fixed pattern for introducing a definition, such as English "X is..." or "X refers to...". Comparing how your own language signals a definition can help you notice that Japanese とは similarly marks a term as the thing being defined, separately from the explanation that follows it.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Confusing に関する and に対する

Error: *政策に関する意見がある。 used when the intended meaning is a directed attitude toward the policy, not simply a topic connection.

Correction: 政策に対する意見がある。 — use に対する when the noun phrase expresses a directed attitude, opinion, or response, and に関する when it simply names a topic.

Common Mistake — Defining a term with more unexplained terms

Error: *環境教育とは、持続可能性を推進する教育活動のことだ。 defines one unfamiliar term using several other unfamiliar terms.

Correction: 環境教育とは、環境について学び、考えることです。 — keep the explanation inside a controlled definition frame simple and within already-known vocabulary and grammar.

Common Mistake — Dropping として and losing the role meaning

Error: *私はボランティア、この活動に参加しています。 omits として and leaves the relationship between "volunteer" and the activity unclear.

Correction: 私はボランティアとして、この活動に参加しています。 — として marks the role in which the subject participates.
