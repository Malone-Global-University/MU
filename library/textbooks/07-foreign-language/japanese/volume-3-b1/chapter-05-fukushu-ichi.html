復習１：説明と意見Unit I Assessment

Extended explanation and opinion assessment · Speak and Write Japanese: Independent Communication and Advanced Expression

Chapter Objectives

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

  1. Describe and classify a person, place, system, process, service, or cultural practice in detail, using という, ような, 例えば, and 一方 to organize the description.
  2. Explain a cause-and-result chain using ため, せいで／おかげで, 結果, and によって, choosing the connector that fits the intended meaning and register.
  3. State an opinion clearly, support it with a reason and an example, and use べき at most once, softened with と思う.
  4. Concede a point with 確かに〜が or 〜ものの before disagreeing, and soften disagreement rather than stating it bluntly.
  5. Combine explanation, cause and result, and supported opinion into one organized multi-paragraph account.
  6. Read, listen to, and produce integrated explanation-and-opinion Japanese about the domains active in Chapters 1 through 4, using consolidated Volume III kanji with appropriately reduced furigana.
  7. Self-assess register consistency, discourse organization, and pronunciation across an extended spoken and written performance.

Communication Scenario

The discussion group Emma has been posting in is preparing a small collection of member essays about changes in everyday life, and Emma has been asked to contribute one on a topic of her choice. She decides to write about the spread of self-checkout registers at stores near her — a topic that lets her describe what self-checkout is and how it works, explain why it has spread and what results it has had, and give her own opinion about whether the change has been good overall. Before she submits her essay, Yuki reads a draft with her, checking that the description is clear, that the causes and results are explained rather than just listed, and that Emma's opinion is supported and gently expressed rather than one-sided. This chapter's Unit I Performance Task asks you to write and present a similar essay on a topic of your choice from education, work, technology, community, media, travel, environment, or culture, combining description, cause-and-result explanation, and a supported, gently expressed opinion.

  1. The writer clearly describes and classifies the topic before explaining or evaluating it.
  2. The writer explains at least one cause-and-result relationship connected to the topic.
  3. The writer states a supported opinion, concedes an opposing point, and softens any disagreement.

Script Strategy — Recognizing families of related kanji

By this point, several Unit I kanji share components that can support memory rather than serve as a decoding rule: 結果 and 結論 both begin with 結 ("to tie together, to conclude"), and 原因 and 影響 both describe forces that produce a later effect, even though their components differ. Reviewing words in these small families, rather than as an unconnected list, can make this chapter's cumulative kanji easier to recall under the pressure of a timed writing or speaking task.

In this chapter's readings, only names, unfamiliar loanwords, and any kanji not yet scheduled in this volume receive furigana; all other Chapter 1–4 kanji appear without reading support.

Pronunciation Review

Pronunciation Review — Length, gemination, and moraic ん under connected speech

This chapter reviews the long vowels, geminate consonants, and moraic ん introduced with Chapters 3 and 4's opinion and cause-result vocabulary, now inside longer, connected sentences rather than isolated words. Holding these contrasts clearly becomes more important, not less, as sentences grow longer, because listeners rely on them to track word boundaries.

  • 影響 (えいきょう): a long vowel in きょう that must be held for its full length, distinct from a shortened きょ.
  • 結果 (けっか): the geminate っ creates a clear pause in timing before か; compare with 消化 (しょうか), which has no gemination.
  • 賛成／結論: review the long vowel in せい and the moraic ん before ろ from Chapter 4.

Practice reading the Building Block example in the Grammar section aloud at a natural pace, paying attention to these contrasts across sentence boundaries rather than only within single words.

Grammar Review: Explanation, Cause, and Opinion

This chapter does not introduce new grammar. It reviews and integrates the description and classification patterns from Chapter 2, the cause-and-result connectors from Chapter 3, and the opinion-support patterns from Chapter 4 within one extended piece of discourse.

Review: Describing and classifying

Purpose: to introduce a topic clearly before explaining or evaluating it, using という, ような, 例えば, and 一方 from Chapter 2.

  • セルフレジというのは、客が自分でバーコードを読み取って会計をするレジのことだ。What's called a self-checkout register is a register where the customer scans the barcodes and pays by themselves.Nというのは、〜 — introduce and define a term
  • 従来のレジのような対面式の会計とは違って、セルフレジでは店員と話す必要がない。Unlike face-to-face payment like a traditional register, with self-checkout there's no need to talk to a staff member.Nのような〜とは違って — contrast a new type with a familiar one

Review: Explaining cause and result

Purpose: to connect a cause to its consequence clearly, using ため, せいで／おかげで, 結果, and によって from Chapter 3.

  • 人手不足のため、多くの店がセルフレジを導入している。Because of the labor shortage, many stores are introducing self-checkout.Nのため、〜 — neutral cause
  • セルフレジが増えたおかげで、レジに並ぶ時間が短くなったという客も多い。Thanks to the increase in self-checkout, many customers say the time spent waiting in line has gotten shorter.Vたおかげで、〜 — positive result

Review: Supporting and softening an opinion

Purpose: to state an opinion, support it, concede a different view, and soften disagreement, using べき, 確かに〜が, 〜ものの, 例えば, and かもしれませんが from Chapter 4.

  • 店はセルフレジの使い方をもっと分かりやすく説明するべきだと思う。I think stores should explain how to use self-checkout more clearly.Vる + べきだと思う — softened recommendation
  • 確かに機械が苦手な人には不便な面もあるものの、慣れれば時間の節約になると思う。It's true that there's an inconvenient side for people who aren't good with machines, but I think it saves time once you get used to it.確かに〜ものの、〜 — concede, then present one's own view

Building an integrated explanation-and-opinion essay

Building Block — An integrated explanation-and-opinion essay
introduce and classify the topic → explain at least one cause and its result → state your opinion, supported by a reason and an example → concede a point from an opposing view → soften your disagreement and restate your conclusion
This five-stage pattern combines the Chapter 2 description-and-classification pattern, the Chapter 3 cause-and-result pattern, and the Chapter 4 opinion-support pattern into one organized piece of extended discourse, and gives this chapter's Unit I Performance Task its underlying shape.
セルフレジというのは、客が自分でバーコードを読み取って会計をするレジのことだ。人手不足のため、最近このようなレジを導入する店が増えている。その結果、レジに並ぶ時間が短くなったという客も多い。私はこの変化に基本的に賛成だ。例えば、私がよく行く店でも、セルフレジができてから会計がずっと早くなった。確かに機械の操作が苦手な人には不便な面もあるものの、店員が近くにいて手伝ってくれれば大きな問題にはならないと思う。それも一つの考え方かもしれないが、店はセルフレジの使い方をもっと分かりやすく説明するべきだというのが私の結論だ。
What's called a self-checkout register is a register where the customer scans the barcodes and pays by themselves. Because of the labor shortage, more stores have recently been introducing this kind of register. As a result, many customers say the time spent waiting in line has gotten shorter. I'm basically in favor of this change. For example, at a store I go to often, checkout has become much faster since self-checkout was introduced. It's true that there's an inconvenient side for people who aren't good with operating machines, but I think it won't be a big problem as long as a staff member is nearby to help. That might be one way of looking at it, but my conclusion is that stores should explain how to use self-checkout more clearly.

Grammar Review Practice

  1. Write one sentence introducing and classifying a topic using というのは or Nのような.
  2. Write one sentence explaining a cause and result using ため or おかげで／せいで.
  3. Write one sentence stating a supported opinion using べきだと思う or と思う with a reason.
  4. Write one sentence conceding a point using 確かに〜が or 〜ものの.
  5. Write a short paragraph following the Building Block pattern above about a topic of your choice.

Guided Practice

タスク１ — Choose and classify a topic. Choose a topic from education, work, technology, community, media, travel, environment, or culture. Write one or two sentences introducing and classifying it, using というのは or Nのような.

タスク２ — Explain a cause and result. Write one or two sentences explaining a cause connected to your topic and its result, using ため, おかげで／せいで, 結果, or によって.

タスク３ — State and support an opinion. Write one sentence stating your opinion, one sentence with a reason, and one sentence with an example using 例えば. Use べき at most once, softened by と思う.

タスク４ — Concede and soften. Write one sentence conceding a different view using 確かに〜が or 〜ものの, and one sentence softening your disagreement using かもしれませんが or a similar hedge.

タスク５ — Plan your essay. Using the Building Block pattern from the Grammar Review section, outline your own five-part essay: classification, cause and result, supported opinion, concession, and softened conclusion.

Writing Assessment

In this workshop you will draft the written portion of your Unit I Performance Task: an integrated essay following the five-stage Building Block pattern from the Grammar Review section.

  1. Introduce and classify your topic in one or two sentences, using というのは or Nのような.
  2. Explain a cause and result connected to your topic, using ため, おかげで／せいで, 結果, or によって.
  3. State your opinion, clearly marked with と思う, supported by a reason and an example with 例えば. Use べき at most once, softened by と思う.

  5. Soften your disagreement and restate your conclusion, then revise using the checklist below.

チェックリスト — Editing checklist:

  • My topic is clearly introduced and classified before I explain or evaluate it.
  • I explained at least one cause-and-result relationship with an appropriate connector.
  • My opinion is clearly marked as an opinion, supported by a reason and an example.
  • If I used べき, it appears at most once and is softened with と思う.
  • I conceded at least one point from a different view before disagreeing.
  • My disagreement is softened rather than blunt.
  • My paragraphs connect to one another rather than reading as disconnected statements.
  • I maintained one consistent polite or plain style throughout.
  • I checked Chapter 1–4 kanji readings without relying on furigana.

Culture and Communication

Culture in Context — Explanation before evaluation

Across the readings, dialogues, and models in Chapters 1 through 4, a recurring organizational pattern has been to explain what something is and why it has happened before offering a personal opinion about it. This ordering is common in many kinds of Japanese-language writing, from discussion-group posts to short opinion essays, and it supports a discussion style that presents shared background before introducing individual stance.

Regional Note — Explanation-before-opinion structures elsewhere

Presenting background and cause before stating a personal opinion is also common in many other languages and academic traditions, though the expected length of the background section, and how explicitly the writer's stance is marked, can differ. Comparing your own writing habits with the pattern practiced across this unit can help you notice which parts of the structure are shared and which are specific to the conventions practiced here.

Register Note — Consistency across a longer piece

In a short sentence, a single register slip is easy to overlook. In an extended essay or presentation like this chapter's performance task, register inconsistency — for example, mixing plain-form endings into an otherwise polite です／ます essay — becomes more noticeable. Reviewing an entire draft specifically for register consistency, as in the Writing Assessment checklist above, is good practice for any longer Volume III writing task.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Common Mistake — Jumping straight to opinion without classification or cause

Error: *セルフレジはいいと思う。べきだ。 stating an opinion immediately, with no classification of the topic and no cause-and-result explanation to support it.

Correction: セルフレジというのは、客が自分で会計をするレジのことだ。人手不足のため、導入する店が増えている。私はこの変化に賛成だ。 — classify the topic, explain a cause and result, and only then move to a supported opinion, as modeled in this chapter's Building Block.

Common Mistake — Repeating べき across an extended essay

Error: *店はもっと説明するべきだ。客ももっと練習するべきだ。政府も支援するべきだ。 using べき three times in a longer piece, which sounds preachy across an extended text just as it did within a single paragraph in Chapter 4.

Correction: 店はセルフレジの使い方をもっと分かりやすく説明するべきだと思う。 — limit べき to at most one well-supported use in the entire essay, as required by this chapter's Writing Assessment checklist.

Common Mistake — Losing register consistency over a longer text

Error: *セルフレジは便利だと思います。でも、機械が苦手な人もいる。 switching between です／ます and plain-form sentence endings within the same essay without a clear reason.

Correction: セルフレジは便利だと思う。でも、機械が苦手な人もいる。 (or consistently: セルフレジは便利だと思います。でも、機械が苦手な人もいます。) — choose one register for the whole essay and maintain it throughout, as emphasized in this chapter's Register Note.

This completes Unit I, 説明と意見, and establishes the explanatory and opinion-support foundation that Unit II will build on as you move into keigo, passive forms, potential and causative forms, and conditionals.
