Personal Finance

Personal finance is the study of how individuals and households earn, spend, save, borrow, invest, and manage money throughout life. Students learn practical financial skills that support long-term financial stability, security, and wealth building.

Overview

This textbook introduces the principles of personal financial management, including budgeting, banking, credit, taxes, insurance, investing, retirement planning, housing, and consumer decision-making. Students develop the financial literacy necessary to navigate modern economic life, evaluate financial opportunities, and make informed long-term decisions.

Learning Outcomes

  • Create and manage a personal budget.
  • Understand banking services, financial accounts, and payment systems.
  • Build savings strategies and emergency funds.
  • Evaluate credit, loans, and debt repayment options.
  • Interpret paychecks, taxes, and tax filing requirements.
  • Analyze insurance needs and risk management strategies.
  • Understand investing principles, asset classes, and portfolio diversification.
  • Evaluate retirement planning strategies and long-term wealth accumulation.
  • Make informed decisions regarding education, housing, and major purchases.
  • Apply ethical and evidence-based financial decision-making throughout life.
