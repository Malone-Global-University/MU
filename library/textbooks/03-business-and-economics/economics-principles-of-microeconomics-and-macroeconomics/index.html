Economics: Principles of Microeconomics and Macroeconomics

Economics studies how individuals, firms, governments, and societies make choices under conditions of scarcity. This textbook develops microeconomic and macroeconomic reasoning, helping students understand markets, policy, incentives, growth, inflation, unemployment, trade, and the broader economy.

Overview

This textbook provides a structured introduction to economic reasoning and market analysis. Students learn the logic of choice, supply and demand, market structure, labor markets, government intervention, national income, monetary policy, fiscal policy, growth, and international trade.

The first half of the book emphasizes microeconomics, including consumer behavior, firm decision-making, competition, and market power. The second half shifts to macroeconomics, including GDP, unemployment, inflation, banking, monetary systems, stabilization policy, and long-run economic performance.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain how scarcity, incentives, and tradeoffs shape economic choices.
  • Apply supply and demand analysis to markets and policy questions.
  • Evaluate consumer behavior, production, and firm decision-making.
  • Compare market structures including competition, monopoly, and oligopoly.
  • Analyze labor markets, inequality, and the role of government intervention.
  • Measure macroeconomic performance using GDP, unemployment, and inflation.
  • Describe the roles of money, banks, central banks, and monetary policy.
  • Assess fiscal policy, deficits, debt, growth, and business cycles.
  • Interpret the effects of trade, exchange rates, and globalization.
  • Use economic reasoning to evaluate public decision-making and social outcomes.
