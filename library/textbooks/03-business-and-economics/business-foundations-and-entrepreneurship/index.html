Business Foundations and Entrepreneurship

Business is the study of how organizations create value by solving problems, serving customers, managing resources, and generating sustainable economic activity. Entrepreneurship extends these principles by transforming ideas into viable ventures.

Overview

This textbook provides a comprehensive introduction to business, entrepreneurship, management, marketing, operations, finance, and organizational leadership. Students learn how businesses are formed, operated, financed, and grown while developing practical skills for launching and managing entrepreneurial ventures.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain how businesses create value and operate within market systems.
  • Analyze different forms of business ownership and organizational structures.
  • Develop business models, strategies, and venture plans.
  • Apply entrepreneurial methods to identify and evaluate opportunities.
  • Understand legal, ethical, and regulatory aspects of business operations.
  • Manage teams, projects, operations, and supply chains effectively.
  • Apply marketing, sales, and customer relationship principles.
  • Interpret accounting information and financial statements.
  • Evaluate funding options, pricing strategies, and business performance metrics.
  • Design, launch, and scale entrepreneurial ventures.
  • Analyze the impact of globalization, technology, and emerging trends on business.
