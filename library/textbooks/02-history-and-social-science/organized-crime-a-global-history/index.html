Organized Crime

This textbook examines the history, structure, economics, and global evolution of organized crime. Students study criminal organizations, illicit markets, corruption, law enforcement responses, and the continuing transformation of organized crime in the modern world.

Overview

Organized crime has shaped politics, economies, and societies across centuries. This textbook traces organized criminal enterprises from their historical origins through the rise of the Mafia, Yakuza, Triads, cartels, and modern transnational criminal networks while examining the legal and institutional responses developed to combat them.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain what distinguishes organized crime from other forms of criminal activity.
  • Trace the historical development of organized criminal organizations across different societies.
  • Analyze the economic, political, and social conditions that enable organized crime.
  • Compare major criminal organizations including the Mafia, Yakuza, Triads, and international cartels.
  • Evaluate government strategies, law enforcement efforts, and international cooperation against organized crime.
  • Understand how globalization, technology, cybercrime, and digital finance continue to reshape organized criminal enterprises.
