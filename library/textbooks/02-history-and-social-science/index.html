History and Social Science

The History and Social Science Shelf organizes course-style learning texts that examine societies, governments, institutions, rights, civic life, historical change, conflict, reform, and global development across time.

Overview

This shelf gathers history and social science textbooks into one structured index. The collection is designed to support civic understanding, historical analysis, comparative systems thinking, and evidence-based study of societies and institutions.

These textbooks differ from reference pages because they are built for sustained instruction. They organize ideas into chapters, sections, examples, reviews, and guided explanations so students can move from foundational concepts toward deeper academic understanding.

African American History

Textbooks in this area examine the history of people of African descent in the United States, from African civilizations and the Atlantic slave trade through emancipation, Reconstruction, civil rights, and contemporary society.

  • African American History

    A comprehensive survey of African American history covering African origins, slavery, resistance, Reconstruction, segregation, migration, civil rights, political participation, culture, and modern developments.

African American Urban History

Textbooks in this area explore the development of Black urban communities, migration, segregation, neighborhood institutions, public policy, economic transformation, and contemporary urban change.

  • African American Urban History

    A specialized history of Black urban America covering the Great Migration, segregation, redlining, community institutions, deindustrialization, mass incarceration, gentrification, and the evolution of urban neighborhoods.

Government Civics and Political Systems

Textbooks in this area explain constitutional order, public institutions, civic responsibility, and comparative political organization.

  • Government Civics and Political Systems

    A civics and political systems textbook covering constitutions, branches of government, federalism, elections, courts, rights, public policy, and comparative government.

United States History

Textbooks in this area trace the historical development of the United States from Indigenous history through modern political, economic, and social change.

  • United States History

    A United States history textbook covering Indigenous history, colonization, founding, slavery, the Civil War, Reconstruction, industrialization, world wars, civil rights, and contemporary America.

World History Civilizations Empires and Global Change

Textbooks in this area examine world civilizations, long-term historical patterns, cross-cultural exchange, empires, revolutions, and global transformation.

  • World History Civilizations Empires and Global Change

    A world history textbook organized around civilizations, empires, regions, exchanges, belief systems, revolutions, modernization, and global connections.
