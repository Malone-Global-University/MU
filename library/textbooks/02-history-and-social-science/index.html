History and Social Science

The History and Social Science Shelf organizes course-style learning texts that examine societies, governments, institutions, rights, civic life, historical change, conflict, reform, culture, and global development across time.

Overview

This shelf gathers history and social science textbooks into one structured index. The collection is designed to support civic understanding, historical analysis, comparative systems thinking, and evidence-based study of societies, cultures, governments, and institutions.

These textbooks differ from reference pages because they are built for sustained instruction. They organize ideas into chapters, sections, examples, reviews, and guided explanations so students can move from foundational concepts toward deeper academic understanding.

World History: Civilizations, Empires, and Global Change

Textbooks in this area examine world civilizations, long-term historical patterns, cross-cultural exchange, empires, revolutions, modernization, globalization, and global transformation.

  • World History: Civilizations, Empires, and Global Change

    A world history textbook organized around civilizations, empires, regions, exchange networks, belief systems, revolutions, modernization, decolonization, globalization, and contemporary global change.

United States History: Indigenous America, Republic, Conflict, and Change

Textbooks in this area trace the historical development of the United States from Indigenous history through modern political, economic, social, and global change.

  • United States History: Indigenous America, Republic, Conflict, and Change

    A United States history textbook covering Indigenous North America, colonization, slavery, revolution, constitutional development, expansion, the Civil War, Reconstruction, industrialization, immigration, reform, world wars, civil rights, globalization, and contemporary America.

Africa: A History of a Continent

Textbooks in this area examine Africa's central role in human origins, migration, early societies, kingdoms, empires, religious traditions, trade networks, intellectual life, slavery, colonialism, resistance, nationalism, and global history.

  • Africa: A History of a Continent

    A continental African history textbook covering human origins, geography, ancient Egypt, Nubia, Axum, migration, Bantu-speaking peoples, West African empires, religious life, scholarship, art, philosophy, the Swahili Coast, Central and Southern African kingdoms, slavery, diaspora, imperialism, colonial rule, resistance, nationalism, and Pan-Africanism.

From Migration to Fragmentation: The Urban History of Black Street Organizations

Textbooks in this area explore Black urban communities, migration, segregation, neighborhood institutions, street organizations, public policy, economic transformation, and contemporary urban change.

  • From Migration to Fragmentation: The Urban History of Black Street Organizations

    A specialized urban history textbook tracing Black migration, segregation, redlining, neighborhood self-defense, car clubs, political organizations, deindustrialization, street organizations, mass incarceration, gentrification, and the changing structure of Black urban communities.
