United States History

This textbook surveys the history of the United States from Indigenous North America before European colonization through the colonial era, Revolution, Civil War, Reconstruction, industrialization, reform movements, global conflict, civil rights, globalization, and contemporary American society.

Overview

United States history examines the development of American society, politics, economics, culture, and global power across multiple centuries. This textbook begins with Indigenous North America and early systems of trade, diplomacy, and sovereignty before tracing colonization, slavery, revolution, constitutional development, expansion, sectional conflict, civil war, reconstruction, industrial capitalism, immigration, reform, imperialism, world wars, the Cold War, civil rights struggles, social movements, and the challenges of contemporary America.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain major developments in United States history from Indigenous North America to the present.
  • Analyze how colonization, slavery, sovereignty, migration, and expansion shaped early American society.
  • Evaluate the causes and consequences of the American Revolution, Constitution, Civil War, and Reconstruction.
  • Trace the development of industrial capitalism, immigration, labor conflict, urbanization, and reform movements.
  • Assess the rise of the United States as a global power through imperialism, world wars, the Cold War, and globalization.
  • Understand how civil rights, social movements, technology, immigration, and political change continue to shape contemporary America.
