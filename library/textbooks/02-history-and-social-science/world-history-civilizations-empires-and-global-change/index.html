World History

This textbook surveys the development of human societies from early human origins to the present. Students study civilizations, empires, religions, trade networks, revolutions, wars, decolonization, globalization, technology, environmental change, and the major forces that have shaped the modern world.

Overview

World history examines the shared and interconnected development of human societies across regions and eras. This textbook begins with human origins, agriculture, cities, writing, and early civilizations before tracing the rise of classical societies, religious traditions, trade systems, empires, revolutions, industrialization, imperialism, world wars, the Cold War, decolonization, globalization, and the challenges of the twenty-first century.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain major patterns in world history from human origins to the present.
  • Identify how geography, migration, agriculture, cities, trade, and technology shaped human societies.
  • Compare the political, economic, religious, and cultural systems of major civilizations and empires.
  • Analyze the role of exchange networks, conquest, colonization, industrialization, and globalization in connecting world regions.
  • Evaluate the causes and consequences of major revolutions, wars, imperial systems, and independence movements.
  • Understand how contemporary global issues emerged from long-term historical processes.
