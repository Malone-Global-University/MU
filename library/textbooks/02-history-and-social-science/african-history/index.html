African History

This textbook surveys the long history of Africa from human origins, early societies, agriculture, migration, ancient kingdoms, religious traditions, trade networks, and intellectual life through slavery, diaspora, abolition, imperialism, colonial rule, resistance, nationalism, Pan-Africanism, and Africa's role in world history.

Overview

African history examines the continent as a major center of human origins, civilization, migration, trade, knowledge, culture, religion, state formation, and global connection. This textbook begins with Africa's place in the human story and moves through environmental regions, village life, ancient Egypt, Nubia, Axum, migration, Bantu-speaking peoples, West African empires, religious change, scholarship, art, philosophy, the Swahili Coast, Central and Southern African kingdoms, slavery, the African diaspora, abolition, European imperial ambitions, colonial rule, African resistance, world wars, nationalism, and Pan-Africanism.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain Africa's central role in human origins, migration, early society, and global history.
  • Analyze how geography, climate, environment, disease, agriculture, pastoralism, and trade shaped African societies.
  • Compare major African civilizations, kingdoms, city-states, empires, and regional systems across different eras.
  • Evaluate the historical significance of African religions, Islam, Christianity, education, scholarship, art, architecture, philosophy, science, and medicine.
  • Trace the development and consequences of slavery, the Atlantic and Indian Ocean slave trades, abolition, and the African diaspora.
  • Understand how imperialism, colonial rule, resistance, world wars, nationalism, and Pan-Africanism transformed modern African history.
