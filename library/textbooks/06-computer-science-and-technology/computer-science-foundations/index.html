Computer Science Foundations

Computer science is the study of computation, information, algorithms, software systems, and the machines that process data. It provides the intellectual foundation for modern computing and digital technology.

Overview

This textbook introduces the fundamental principles of computer science, including computational thinking, programming, algorithms, data structures, computer architecture, networking, databases, cybersecurity, software engineering, artificial intelligence, and emerging technologies. Students develop both conceptual understanding and practical problem-solving skills used throughout the computing disciplines.

Learning Outcomes

  • Explain the fundamental concepts and scope of computer science.
  • Apply computational thinking to solve structured problems.
  • Understand how data and information are represented digitally.
  • Design and analyze algorithms for computational tasks.
  • Develop basic programs using modern programming principles.
  • Understand the operation of computer hardware, operating systems, and networks.
  • Apply foundational concepts of databases, cybersecurity, and software engineering.
  • Evaluate the principles behind artificial intelligence and machine learning.
  • Analyze ethical, legal, and societal issues related to computing technologies.
  • Apply computer science concepts to real-world systems and applications.
