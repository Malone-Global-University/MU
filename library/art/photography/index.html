Photography Overview

Photography studies the creation, selection, editing, and interpretation of images captured through light-sensitive technologies. It combines technical control with visual judgment, documentary observation, aesthetic composition, and narrative meaning. Across photographic technique, portraiture, landscape, documentary work, and photo editing, the discipline examines how images preserve moments, shape memory, communicate evidence, and construct visual identity.

Disciplines

  • Photographic Technique

    Study camera operation through exposure, aperture, shutter speed, ISO, focus, lenses, lighting, framing, and image capture.

  • Portrait Photography

    Learn photographic representation of people through lighting, posing, expression, environment, composition, identity, and mood.

  • Landscape Photography

    Examine natural and built environments through composition, light, atmosphere, distance, weather, viewpoint, and place.

  • Documentary Photography

    Study visual storytelling through observation, evidence, social context, sequence, ethics, realism, and recorded human experience.

  • Photo Editing

    Learn post-processing through correction, cropping, color grading, retouching, selection, sequencing, and digital image refinement.
