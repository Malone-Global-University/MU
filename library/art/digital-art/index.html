Digital Art Overview

Digital art studies creative work made with computer-based tools, electronic media, and virtual production systems. It combines traditional visual principles with software workflows, interactive environments, animation, modeling, and image manipulation. Across illustration, concept art, 3D modeling, motion graphics, and game art, the discipline examines how artists create visual worlds for screens, simulations, games, film, and digital culture.

Disciplines

  • Digital Illustration

    Study computer-based drawing and painting through digital brushes, layers, tablets, image software, color, and visual rendering.

  • Concept Art

    Explore visual development for characters, environments, props, costumes, vehicles, creatures, worlds, and production design.

  • 3D Modeling

    Learn digital construction of three-dimensional forms for games, animation, visualization, simulation, film, and virtual environments.

  • Animation

    Study motion, timing, character movement, visual storytelling, frame sequencing, and animated production techniques.

  • Motion Graphics

    Examine animated graphic design for titles, interfaces, advertising, branding, data visualization, media, and visual communication.

  • Game Art

    Study visual asset creation for interactive worlds including characters, environments, props, textures, interfaces, and real-time graphics.
