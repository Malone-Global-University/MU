Wellness Section Overview

The Wellness Section of The Malone Panther covers the habits, systems, relationships, environments, and supports that shape individual and community well-being. Wellness coverage should be practical and responsible. It should help readers understand health information without exaggeration, shame, miracle claims, or one-size-fits-all advice.

This section includes coverage of mental health, stress, sleep, exercise, nutrition, relationships, campus well-being, preventive health, public health, disability, accessibility, safety, burnout, time management, and healthy routines. Articles may explain research, offer practical guidance, report community resources, or connect personal habits to larger social conditions.

Wellness Coverage Standards

Wellness articles should be careful, inclusive, and clear about the difference between general information and personal medical advice. Coverage should avoid shaming language, miracle cures, fear-based claims, unsupported health trends, and oversimplified advice. When appropriate, articles should encourage readers to seek qualified professional help.

The Wellness Section exists to support healthier, more informed lives. Its purpose is to provide practical guidance, explain evidence, highlight resources, and connect personal well-being to community conditions.
