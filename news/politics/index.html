Politics Section Overview

The Politics Section of The Malone Panther covers the people, institutions, decisions, conflicts, laws, campaigns, and public arguments that shape government and civic life. Politics coverage should help readers understand not only what happened, but who holds power, what choices are being made, what rules apply, and how those choices affect ordinary people.

This department includes coverage of national politics, elections, executive power, legislative action, courts, public policy, political parties, local government, international affairs, civil rights, constitutional issues, and civic education. Articles may report news, explain policy, analyze institutions, summarize political controversies, or clarify public documents for readers who need a plain-English explanation.

Politics Coverage Standards

Politics articles should be clear, sourced, specific, and careful about the difference between confirmed fact, legal claim, political argument, institutional response, and editorial judgment. Readers should be able to tell what happened, what is alleged, what is proven, who benefits, who objects, and what questions remain unresolved.

The Politics Section exists to strengthen civic understanding. It should not reduce public life to slogans or partisan noise. Its purpose is to explain power, clarify rules, identify consequences, and help readers make better judgments about government and public affairs.
