One Woman One Bra Makes Historic Tribeca Sweep

Kenyan-Nigerian co-production wins major honors, signaling a new level of global recognition for African cinema.


By: Joseph Malone

Published: June 22, 2026

Updated: June 22, 2026

Section: Africa Culture and Arts

Summary

The Kenyan-Nigerian film One Woman One Bra has made history at the 2026 Tribeca Film Festival, taking home Best Narrative Feature and Best Screenplay. The film’s success has drawn praise for its cross-continental storytelling, its sharp social commentary, and its portrayal of women building economic power in informal markets.

Tribeca Crowns an African Collaboration

NEW YORK — The Kenyan-Nigerian co-production One Woman One Bra has emerged as one of the breakout titles of the 2026 Tribeca Film Festival, winning Best Narrative Feature and Best Screenplay in a sweep that is being hailed as a milestone for African cinema.

The film’s recognition marks a major moment for cross-continental filmmaking, with critics and industry watchers describing it as a strong example of how African producers and directors are building ambitious projects that travel well beyond their home markets.

Story of Survival Becomes a Political Statement

Directed by a breakout Kenyan filmmaker and led by a cast of Nigerian and Kenyan stars, One Woman One Bra follows three market women whose ordinary business deal spirals into a larger movement for economic survival and political leverage. Set between Nairobi and Lagos, the film blends comedy, drama, and social critique around themes of corruption, informal trade, and women’s resilience.

The story centers on Amina, a Nairobi trader who receives the wrong shipment of high-end undergarments, and Blessing, a Lagos wholesaler who helps turn the mistake into a profitable venture. Joined by Wanjiku, a former accountant turned market organizer, the women discover that the garments can be adapted to protect cash in market economies where theft, bribery, and predatory lending are constant threats.

What begins as a survival hustle grows into a grassroots financial network. As more women adopt the garments to shield their earnings, the business becomes a symbol of collective resistance, culminating in a cross-border strike and a showdown with political power brokers.

Strong Performances Anchor the Film

The cast has been widely praised for giving the film both emotional weight and sharp comic timing. Sarah Hassan plays Amina with restraint and determination, Funke Akindele brings energy and quick wit as Blessing, and Mumbi Maina gives Wanjiku a focused, strategic presence. Richard Mofe-Damijo rounds out the ensemble as a corrupt trade official whose polished charm masks the machinery of exploitation.

Critics say the performances help the film move smoothly between humor, tension, and political urgency without losing its grounded sense of place.

Why the Film Stood Out

Much of the praise surrounding One Woman One Bra has focused on its ability to connect local realities to a wider global audience. The film’s visual style contrasts the textures of Nairobi and Lagos, while its soundtrack fuses Nigerian Afrobeats with Kenyan Benga influences. The result is a production that feels both regionally specific and broadly accessible.

Industry figures say the movie also reflects a broader shift in African filmmaking, where collaborations across national borders are helping creators pool talent, financing, and distribution in ways that reduce dependence on traditional Western gatekeepers.

Global Release Plans

Following its Tribeca success, the film is expected to move quickly into international distribution. Netflix has acquired worldwide rights, with a global streaming premiere scheduled for October 9, 2026. Before then, the film is set to screen at select festivals during July and August, followed by a limited theatrical release in Kenya and Nigeria beginning September 4.

For many observers, the film’s trajectory is just as significant as its awards. Its reception suggests that African stories built for African audiences can also command major attention on the world stage.
