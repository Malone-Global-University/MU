A foundational entry point into understanding Africa’s geography, history, politics, economies, cultures, and global connections.

About This Series

The Africa Explainers series is a foundational educational section of The Malone Panther designed to prepare readers for deeper reporting and analysis across the Africa coverage desk.

Rather than focusing on breaking news or advanced geopolitical detail, these articles build essential background knowledge. They explain the basic structures, concepts, and historical context needed to understand African societies in a clear and accessible way.

Each article functions as a reference guide. Together, they form a structured introduction to Africa as a continent of 54 countries, thousands of languages, diverse political systems, complex histories, and rapidly changing economies and cultures.

The goal is simple: ensure readers are not encountering African news or analysis without context, but instead have the grounding needed to follow developments with clarity and confidence.

How to Use This Series

These articles are arranged in a progressive order, starting with broad foundational ideas and moving toward more specific themes such as governance, identity, diaspora, and cultural perception.

Readers can follow the sequence from beginning to end, or use individual articles as reference points when specific topics appear in later reporting.

Together, they function as a background framework for understanding Africa-related journalism across The Malone Panther.

Why This Series Exists

Africa is often misrepresented or oversimplified in global media. Important context is frequently missing, leading to confusion about geography, politics, and society.

This series is designed to correct that gap by providing clear, structured explanations of essential topics before readers engage with more advanced coverage.

It is both educational and practical: it helps readers interpret current events, understand long-term trends, and recognize the diversity within and across African countries.
