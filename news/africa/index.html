Africa Section Overview

The Africa Section of The Malone Panther covers the people, institutions, movements, economies, cultures, conflicts, innovations, and everyday realities shaping the African continent. Coverage should avoid treating Africa as a single story. It should recognize regional differences, local voices, historical context, linguistic diversity, and the connection between local events and global systems.

This section includes coverage of African governments, cities, rural communities, education systems, public health, technology, business, arts, sports, migration, climate pressure, peacebuilding, infrastructure, religion, youth movements, and Africa’s relationship with the wider world. Articles may report breaking developments, explain historical background, profile leaders and communities, or connect African news to diaspora readers.

As of May 22, 2026, the biggest Africa stories this month appear to be these:

1. Ebola outbreak in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo

This is the most urgent public-health story. Authorities in Ituri province have banned funeral wakes and limited gatherings as the outbreak grows, while WHO has raised concern over the risk inside Congo. Reports describe confirmed cases, many suspected cases, suspected deaths, community resistance around burial practices, and international financing from the UN, U.S., and World Bank. The story matters because it combines public health, conflict zones, weak infrastructure, regional travel concerns, and misinformation.

2. Sudan’s war continues to deepen, with drone attacks and thousands missing

Sudan remains one of the continent’s most serious crises. Reuters reported that Sudan’s army accused Ethiopia and the UAE of links to a drone attack on Khartoum airport, while Ethiopia denied the accusation and Reuters said it could not independently verify the UAE claim. Separately, AP reported that more than 8,000 people are missing from the war and that many victims have been buried in unmarked graves, underscoring the human cost beyond battlefield control.

3. Senegal’s president dismissed Prime Minister Ousmane Sonko

This is a major West African political story. President Bassirou Diomaye Faye dismissed Prime Minister Ousmane Sonko and dissolved the government after months of tension. Reuters framed the move as one that could deepen uncertainty while Senegal is already dealing with a debt crisis and IMF negotiations.

Africa Coverage Standards

Africa articles should be precise about country, region, language, community, source, date, and political context. Coverage should avoid flattening the continent into stereotypes of crisis, poverty, conflict, wildlife, or charity. Readers should be able to tell where an event happened, who is affected, what historical background matters, and what voices are missing.

The Africa Section exists to strengthen informed attention to African public life, creativity, institution-building, conflict resolution, economic change, and global influence. Its purpose is to report with specificity, explain with humility, and treat African communities as active makers of history rather than passive subjects of outside interpretation.
