History & Heritage Section Overview

The History & Heritage Section of The Malone Panther covers the past as a living part of public understanding. History coverage should explain events, institutions, people, movements, places, objects, and memories with evidence and context. Heritage coverage should show how communities preserve, debate, reinterpret, and transmit what they inherit.

This section includes historical explainers, archival features, timelines, biographies, anniversaries, heritage sites, monuments, genealogy, oral history, cultural memory, public-history debates, and connections between historical events and current public life. Articles may introduce readers to overlooked histories, clarify familiar stories, or document the ways memory is contested and preserved.

History & Heritage Coverage Standards

History articles should distinguish evidence from legend, scholarship from rumor, and interpretation from certainty. Heritage articles should respect community memory while still examining sources carefully. Coverage should avoid presentism, nostalgia, mythmaking, and oversimplified hero-or-villain storytelling.

The History & Heritage Section exists to help readers understand time, memory, inheritance, and responsibility. Its purpose is to make the past usable without making it false.
