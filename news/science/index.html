Science Section Overview

The Science Section of The Malone Panther covers discovery, evidence, research, technology, environment, health science, and the methods people use to understand the natural world. Science coverage should explain what researchers found, how they know, what remains uncertain, and why the finding matters beyond the headline.

This section includes coverage of biology, physics, chemistry, earth science, astronomy, medicine, climate, technology, engineering, data science, public health, science education, and research ethics. Articles may report new findings, summarize studies, explain scientific concepts, profile researchers, or help readers judge claims about evidence.

Science Coverage Standards

Science articles should explain evidence, method, uncertainty, and limitations. Writers should avoid exaggerated headlines, miracle claims, fear-driven framing, and treating early findings as settled consensus. Readers should be able to tell what was tested, what was found, what is unknown, and what experts actually claim.

The Science Section exists to strengthen scientific literacy and public understanding. Its purpose is to make research understandable without distorting complexity, and to help readers evaluate claims in a world full of data, technology, and uncertainty.
