Xi and Putin Meeting Highlights China–Russia Alignment

The Beijing meeting signaled continuing China–Russia coordination even without a major new gas breakthrough.


By: Joseph Malone

Published: May 22, 2026

Updated: May 22, 2026

Section: Global News

Summary

The May 2026 meeting between Xi Jinping and Vladimir Putin became a major geopolitical story because it showed continued China–Russia alignment during a period of war, energy disruption, and tension with the United States.

A meeting with global meaning

When Xi Jinping and Vladimir Putin met in Beijing in May, the symbolism mattered almost as much as the official agenda. The meeting took place while Russia’s war in Ukraine continued, the Middle East faced a major crisis, and the United States remained central to both diplomatic and military disputes.

China and Russia used the meeting to project stability and partnership. Their statements criticized U.S. security plans and emphasized cooperation, even though reports indicated that a long-discussed gas deal did not reach a major breakthrough.

Alignment without full agreement

The meeting showed that alignment does not always mean complete agreement. China and Russia share an interest in limiting U.S. influence, but they also have different economic needs, bargaining positions, and long-term priorities.

Energy remains a key part of the relationship. Russia wants markets for its gas and oil, while China wants reliable supplies on favorable terms. The lack of a major gas breakthrough suggested that Beijing was not simply accepting Moscow’s terms.

Why the story mattered

The Xi–Putin meeting mattered because it fit into a broader shift in global politics. Countries are increasingly watching how major powers group themselves during crises. China and Russia’s public coordination sends a message to Washington, Europe, and countries across the Global South.

For readers, this was not just a photo opportunity. It was a sign of how great-power politics may shape conflicts, trade, energy, and diplomacy beyond May 2026.
