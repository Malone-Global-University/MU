Special Features Section Overview

The Special Features Section of The Malone Panther is for work that deserves a larger frame than a routine news article. Feature coverage should give readers depth, narrative, human context, careful explanation, and a memorable structure. It may slow down a fast story, connect many smaller pieces into a series, or give fuller attention to a person, place, institution, idea, or public question.

This section includes longform reporting, interviews, profiles, photo essays, multimedia features, themed series, anniversary packages, issue guides, editor’s collections, narrative explainers, and cross-section projects. Articles may combine reporting, history, analysis, images, timelines, source notes, and related links.

Special Features Coverage Standards

Special features should be well-structured, carefully edited, and clear about what kind of work they are: reporting, interview, essay, guide, multimedia project, or editorial package. Features may be creative in form, but they should not blur facts, sources, quotation, interpretation, and opinion.

The Special Features Section exists to give important stories room to breathe. Its purpose is to combine readability, depth, evidence, human context, and editorial care.
