Opinion Department Overview

The Opinion Department of The Malone Panther publishes arguments, interpretations, public judgments, and reflective essays. Opinion writing is different from straight reporting because it openly advances a point of view. The goal is not merely to describe events, but to explain what they mean, why they matter, and what should be done in response.

This department includes official editorials, recurring columns, reader letters, guest essays, and media criticism. Each section has a different role. Editorials represent the newspaper’s institutional position. Columns represent recurring writers and regular voices. Letters give readers and community members a place to respond. Guest essays allow outside contributors to make developed arguments. Media criticism examines how stories, images, platforms, headlines, and narratives shape public understanding.

Editorials

Editorials express the official position of The Malone Panther. These articles speak in the voice of the paper rather than in the voice of an individual writer. They are reserved for major civic questions, institutional positions, public arguments, and issues that require a clear editorial judgment.

Use This Section For

  • Official newspaper positions
  • Major public statements
  • Institutional arguments
  • Endorsements, critiques, or formal recommendations

Letters

Letters are short responses from readers, students, community members, and participants in public discussion. A letter may respond to a published article, correct a claim, raise a concern, or add a community perspective to an ongoing issue.

Opinion Standards

Opinion articles may be forceful, personal, and argumentative, but they should still be grounded in evidence, fairness, clarity, and public responsibility. Writers should separate fact from interpretation, avoid careless claims, and make clear when they are presenting judgment rather than straight news reporting.

The Opinion Department exists to strengthen public reasoning. It should challenge readers, clarify disputes, expose weak arguments, defend serious principles, and give the MGU community a disciplined place for disagreement.
