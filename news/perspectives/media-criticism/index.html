Media Criticism Overview

Media criticism examines how stories are framed, distorted, repeated, sensationalized, or ignored. This section studies journalism, television, film, documentaries, social media, headlines, platform narratives, propaganda, misinformation, and the public meaning created by media systems.

This Section is used For:

  • Criticism of news coverage
  • Analysis of misleading headlines
  • Discussion of media bias and framing
  • Commentary on documentaries, film, television, and social platforms
  • Responses to viral narratives and public misinformation

Section Standard

Media criticism should be specific. Strong criticism identifies the claim, the framing, the missing context, and the effect on public understanding.
