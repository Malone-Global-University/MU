Investigative Reports Section Overview

The Investigative Reports Section of The Malone Panther is reserved for deeper public-interest journalism that requires evidence gathering, document review, interviews, verification, pattern analysis, and careful presentation of findings. Investigative coverage should reveal what is hidden, clarify what is confusing, test official claims, and give readers enough evidence to understand why a conclusion is justified.

This section includes investigations into institutions, public spending, safety, education, labor, business practices, civil rights, environmental risk, governance, data patterns, misconduct allegations, and systems that affect public life. Articles may use public records, interviews, site observations, data analysis, timelines, source documents, and follow-up reporting.

Investigative Reports Coverage Standards

Investigative articles must be evidence-based, fair, careful, and transparent about methods. Serious allegations require verification, context, response opportunities, and restraint. Coverage should distinguish documents, interviews, observed facts, statistical patterns, allegations, denials, and conclusions.

The Investigative Reports Section exists to serve the public interest. Its purpose is not to sensationalize, punish, or speculate, but to reveal evidence, explain systems, identify accountability, and help readers understand matters that powerful institutions or complex processes may obscure.
