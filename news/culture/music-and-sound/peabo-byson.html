Peabo Bryson: The Voice of Romance and Disney Magic Dies at 75 By Staff Writer The music world is mourning the loss of legendary R&B singer Peabo Bryson, who died on June 2, 2026, at the age of 75. According to his family, Bryson passed away in Georgia after suffering complications from a stroke. He was surrounded by family and loved ones during his final moments. For more than five decades, Bryson captivated audiences with his rich, soulful voice and timeless love songs. Widely regarded as one of the greatest balladeers in R&B history, he built a career that crossed generations and musical styles, leaving an enduring mark on popular music. Born Robert Peapo Bryson in Greenville, South Carolina, on April 13, 1951, he developed a passion for music at an early age. After performing with regional bands during the 1960s and 1970s, he launched a successful solo career that would eventually produce more than 20 albums and numerous chart hits. Bryson became famous for romantic classics such as "Feel the Fire," "I'm So Into You," "If Ever You're in My Arms Again," and "Can You Stop the Rain." His smooth vocals and emotional delivery made him a favorite among R&B and adult contemporary audiences throughout the 1980s and 1990s. While he enjoyed tremendous success as a solo artist, Bryson became internationally known through a series of celebrated duets. His collaborations with Roberta Flack, Natalie Cole, and other major artists showcased his ability to blend powerful vocals with heartfelt emotion. Perhaps his most famous recordings came through his work with Disney. Bryson won Grammy Awards for "Beauty and the Beast," performed with Celine Dion, and "A Whole New World," his unforgettable duet with Regina Belle from Disney's Aladdin. "A Whole New World" became a No. 1 hit on the Billboard Hot 100 and remains one of Disney's most beloved songs. Despite health challenges in recent years, including a heart attack in 2019 from which he recovered, Bryson continued to perform and record music. He was reportedly preparing new projects and live performances before his sudden illness. Following news of his passing, tributes poured in from fans, fellow musicians, and entertainment figures around the world. Many remembered him not only for his extraordinary voice but also for his kindness, professionalism, and dedication to his craft. Bryson's family said they were comforted by the immense love shown by fans and expressed confidence that his music would continue to inspire future generations. Memorial arrangements are expected to be announced at a later date. Peabo Bryson leaves behind a legacy of romance, elegance, and musical excellence. Though the singer's voice has been silenced, the songs that defined countless weddings, celebrations, and cherished memories will continue to resonate for years to come.
