Peabo Bryson: The Voice of Romance and Disney Magic Dies at 75

Grammy-winning singer Peabo Bryson, whose powerful voice became synonymous with romantic ballads and beloved Disney classics, has died at the age of 75.


By: Joseph Malone

Published: June 6, 2026

Updated: June 6, 2026

Section: Entertainment

Summary

Peabo Bryson, one of the most celebrated vocalists in contemporary R&B history, died on June 2, 2026, at the age of 75. Over a career spanning more than five decades, Bryson earned acclaim for his romantic ballads, Grammy Award-winning duets, and iconic Disney soundtrack performances. His death marks the end of an era for fans who cherished his smooth voice, emotional performances, and enduring contributions to popular music.

A Musical Journey Begins

Born Robert Peapo Bryson on April 13, 1951, in Greenville, South Carolina, Peabo Bryson developed a passion for music at an early age. Influenced by gospel, soul, and jazz, he began performing professionally while still a teenager. Throughout the 1960s and 1970s, Bryson honed his craft with regional bands before launching a solo recording career that would eventually make him one of the most recognizable voices in American music.

His early recordings established him as a gifted vocalist with a remarkable ability to convey emotion through song. As his popularity grew, so did his reputation as one of R&B's premier romantic singers.

The King of Romantic Ballads

During the 1980s and 1990s, Bryson became synonymous with love songs and heartfelt ballads. Hits such as "Feel the Fire," "I'm So Into You," "Tonight, I Celebrate My Love," "If Ever You're in My Arms Again," and "Can You Stop the Rain" became staples of radio stations and wedding playlists across the United States.

His rich tenor voice and sophisticated delivery distinguished him from many of his contemporaries. Bryson's music resonated with audiences seeking sincerity, romance, and emotional depth, helping him build a loyal fan base that spanned generations.

Memorable Duets and Grammy Success

Although Bryson enjoyed tremendous success as a solo artist, some of his most celebrated recordings came through collaborations with other vocalists. His duet "Tonight, I Celebrate My Love" with Roberta Flack became an international favorite and remains one of the most recognizable love songs ever recorded.

Bryson also collaborated with Natalie Cole, Regina Belle, and numerous other artists throughout his career. These partnerships showcased his ability to blend seamlessly with fellow singers while maintaining his own distinctive vocal identity.

His work earned multiple Grammy Awards and established him as one of the most respected performers in contemporary R&B music.

The Disney Years

Bryson achieved worldwide recognition through his contributions to Disney animated films. In 1992, he recorded "Beauty and the Beast" with Celine Dion, a song that became both a commercial and critical success.

One year later, he partnered with Regina Belle for "A Whole New World," the theme song from Disney's Aladdin. The recording reached No. 1 on the Billboard Hot 100 and won both a Grammy Award and an Academy Award.

For millions of listeners, these performances introduced Bryson's voice to a new generation and secured his place in Disney music history. Decades later, the songs remain beloved by audiences around the world.

Health Challenges and Final Years

In 2019, Bryson suffered a heart attack that required hospitalization and recovery. Despite the health scare, he returned to performing and continued engaging with fans through concerts and special appearances.

Friends and colleagues described him as dedicated to his craft and grateful for the support he received throughout his career. Even in his later years, Bryson remained active in music and was reportedly considering future projects before his final illness.

Death and Tributes

Peabo Bryson died on June 2, 2026, at the age of 75 after suffering complications from a stroke. Family members announced that he passed away peacefully while surrounded by loved ones.

Following the announcement, tributes poured in from musicians, actors, industry professionals, and fans worldwide. Many praised his extraordinary vocal talent, while others remembered his professionalism, humility, and kindness.

Social media quickly filled with memories of favorite songs, concert experiences, and personal stories illustrating the profound impact Bryson's music had on people's lives.

A Lasting Legacy

Few singers successfully bridged the worlds of R&B, adult contemporary music, and film soundtracks as effectively as Peabo Bryson. His recordings helped define an era of romantic music while also becoming part of the cultural fabric through beloved Disney classics.

Across more than five decades, Bryson demonstrated that great singing is not merely about technical skill but about communicating genuine emotion. His songs provided the soundtrack to countless weddings, anniversaries, celebrations, and cherished memories.

Though Peabo Bryson is gone, his music endures. Future generations will continue to discover the warmth, elegance, and passion that made him one of the most beloved voices in modern music history.
