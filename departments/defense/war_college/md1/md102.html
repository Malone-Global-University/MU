Course Overview

MD102 is a core leadership module that sharpens your ability to wield executive power with clarity and conviction. Where MD101 charts the history of government systems, MD102 equips you to lead one. This course focuses on the burden and brilliance of rulership—how to command, defend, and expand your domain under siege, sabotage, or rebellion. Executive authority is not granted—it is seized, held, and justified through results.

Learning Objectives

  • Define power as responsibility: internal order, external defense, and moral clarity.
  • Understand siege logic—how empires are weakened, infiltrated, and destroyed from within.
  • Master the principles of wartime leadership and sovereign decision-making.
  • Develop the mindset of a commander: decisive, accountable, and unshakable under threat.

Curriculum Breakdown

  1. Command Structure & Chain of Loyalty
    Building your cabinet; purging corruption; ruling through delegation without dilution.
  2. Sovereign Burden: The Cost of Command
    Isolation at the top; executive fatigue; decisions no one else can make.
  3. Siege Theory & Defensive Sovereignty
    How kingdoms are softened through media, famine, lies, and betrayal—before the gates fall.
  4. Righteous Violence & Just Authority
    The ethics of “by any means”; when to show mercy and when to make an example.
  5. Statesmanship vs Tyranny
    Authority without arrogance; commanding loyalty without theatrics or bribery.
  6. Militant Responsibility
    Ultimate power = ultimate duty. When you fail, your people suffer. When you win, they rise.

Assignments & Exercises

  • Command Memo: Write an internal doctrine on your executive philosophy. What are your non-negotiables?
  • Siege Map: Create a plan to defend your regime against ideological, economic, or cyber assault.
  • Simulation: You are under attack. Decide what to protect, what to sacrifice, and who must be removed.

Required Reading

  • Letters from a Stoic by Seneca (Leadership Selections)
  • The Dictator’s Handbook by Bueno de Mesquita & Smith
  • Founder's Notes: Siege Dynamics & Executive Burden (Internal Memo PDF)
