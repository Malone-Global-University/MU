MD103: Narrative Warfare

Narrative Warfare is the strategic use of story, symbols, and communication to seize control of public perception. As the battleground shifts from land to language, those who dominate narrative dominate reality. This course trains elite communicators, culture hackers, and propaganda masters to become weapons of influence.

Course Topics

  • Origins of Narrative Control: From oral tradition to state media.
  • The Architecture of Belief: How stories shape values and loyalty.
  • Psychographics & Audience Targeting: Segmenting the mind.
  • Weaponized Language: Framing, memetics, and strategic ambiguity.
  • Dominating the Discourse: Agenda setting, viral campaigns, and narrative preemption.
  • Counter-Propaganda: How to dismantle hostile narrative constructs.
  • Constructing Civilizational Myths: Writing the story that shapes the future.

Outcomes

Students will learn how to:

  • Create persuasive campaigns to shape mass behavior.
  • Identify and neutralize subversive narratives within culture, media, and law.
  • Write manifestos, speeches, doctrines, and treaties that move history.
  • Become master communicators trusted by allies, feared by enemies, and remembered by the future.
