Course Overview

The Commander Tier is the final evolution in the War College. This is where revolutionary strategists become rulers. You will learn to lead with autocratic clarity, diplomatic precision, and world-class executive function. Every lesson is designed to help you govern an institution, a people, or a nation under your vision — while defending it from all threats foreign and domestic.

Command-Level Outcomes

  • Form your own nation, political party, or sovereign institution under enforceable treaties.
  • Manage governments, corporations, and armies using clear doctrine, loyalty frameworks, and executive leadership.
  • Deploy asymmetric tools: sanctions, economic sabotage, information warfare, and diplomacy by force.
  • Write foundational texts (doctrines, manifestos, legal systems) to immortalize your vision.

Curriculum Breakdown

  1. Module I – Founding Your Nation
    Create your flag, legal system, capital assets, and defensive posture. Become the institution.
  2. Module II – Executive Command Theory
    Rule through truth, logistics, and reputation. Maintain clarity in chaos and make final calls without compromise.
  3. Module III – International Engagement
    Form alliances, issue sanctions, negotiate from strength, and operate across borders with precision.
  4. Module IV – Cultural Warfare
    Use music, film, books, and social codes to build loyalty and remake the hearts of your people.
  5. Module V – The Eternal Seat
    Preserve your rule beyond death. Architect succession, continuity, and relics of power.

Field Assignments

  • Sovereign Charter: Write your nation's first legal document or political manifesto.
  • Succession Doctrine: Design the plan for your heir, organization, or system to survive a century.
  • Diplomatic Offensive: Write a speech or document asserting power against an existing world institution (UN, IMF, NATO, etc.).

Required Readings

  • The Malone Doctrine – Volume III
  • The Prince by Machiavelli
  • Discourses on Livy by Machiavelli
  • Art of Executive Decision by unknown author (classified)
  • Leadership Principles from Pre-Colonial African Kingdoms
