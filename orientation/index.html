Orientation

Welcome to Orientation

Orientation is the first day of class. It establishes guidelines and expected outcomes. The goal is to better familiarize students with how the system works so that they can function properly. Orientation makes knowing what you are supposed to do and how to do it common knowledge.

This page introduces the basic documents, policies, and navigation tools that help students understand Malone Global University. Before using the larger library, departments, lessons, guides, and reference systems, students should know the purpose of the institution, the expectations for conduct, and the layout of the website.

The orientation materials are meant to create a shared starting point. Students should read each required page carefully so that later study, research, and participation can happen with fewer questions about where to go, what the rules are, and how the academic environment is organized.

Required Reading

The following pages should be reviewed before beginning regular study at Malone Global University. Together, they explain the founder, the mission, the standards of conduct, the privacy and terms policies, the physical-location status of the institution, and the basic method for navigating the website.

  • About The Founder Joseph Malone

    This page introduces Joseph Malone and explains the founding vision behind Malone Global University. Students should read it to understand the personal, educational, and institutional purpose that shaped the development of MGU.

  • Mission Of Malone Global University

    This page explains the mission of Malone Global University. It describes the broad educational purpose of the institution and helps students understand why the site is organized around open learning, structured knowledge, academic development, and long-term self-improvement.

  • Honor Code

    The Honor Code explains the standards of honesty, responsibility, respect, and academic integrity expected of students. It should be read carefully because it defines the kind of learning culture MGU is designed to support.

  • Privacy Policy

    The Privacy Policy explains how user information, site interaction, and privacy-related matters are handled. Students should read this page to understand the privacy expectations connected with using Malone Global University resources.

  • Terms

    The Terms page explains the conditions for using the Malone Global University website and its materials. It provides the basic rules for access, use, responsibility, and limitations connected to the site.

  • Physical Locations

    This page clarifies the physical-location status of Malone Global University. Students should read it to understand how MGU exists as an online educational project and how its current structure relates to physical campuses, offices, or in-person facilities.

  • Navigating The Website

    This page explains how to move through the Malone Global University website. It helps students understand the major areas of the site, including the library, departments, community pages, tutorials, guides, reference materials, and other navigation layers.

How to Use This Orientation

Students should begin by reading each required page in order. After finishing the required reading, students should return to the main navigation pages and begin exploring the areas most relevant to their goals. A new visitor may begin with the Library Guide, a department page, a textbook, a tutorial, or a reference page depending on the reason for visiting MGU.

The most important outcome of orientation is confidence. Students should leave this page knowing what Malone Global University is, what it expects, where the major resources are located, and how to continue learning without feeling lost inside a large website.
