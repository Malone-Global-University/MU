Delinquency

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Delinquency

Definition

Delinquency refers to a tenant’s failure to pay rent or other lease-related charges (utilities, fees, reimbursements) by the required due date. Delinquency directly affects property cash flow and may lead to late fees, payment plans, or eviction proceedings.

How It’s Measured

  • Delinquency Rate (%) = Delinquent Rent ÷ Total Rent Billed
  • Days Past Due: Number of days rent remains unpaid after due date
  • Collections Ratio: Rent Collected ÷ Rent Billed

Why It Matters

Delinquency impacts Net Operating Income (NOI), debt service coverage, and property stability. High delinquency can signal:

  • Weak tenant screening
  • Economic stress in the submarket
  • Poor property management controls
  • Tenant dissatisfaction or deferred maintenance

Operational Impact

  • Reduced immediate cash flow
  • Higher legal and administrative costs
  • Potential vacancy if eviction occurs
  • Increased bad debt expense

Underwriting Considerations

  • Apply realistic bad debt assumptions
  • Review historical delinquency trends
  • Stress-test economic downturn scenarios
  • Evaluate tenant mix and income stability

Example

A 100-unit property bills $150,000 in rent for the month. If $9,000 remains unpaid after the grace period:

  • Delinquency Rate = 9,000 ÷ 150,000 = 6%

A sustained 6% delinquency rate would materially reduce annual NOI and may affect loan covenant compliance.

Common Pitfalls

  • Confusing delinquency with vacancy (occupied units may still not pay).
  • Ignoring payment plans that artificially lower current delinquency but delay cash flow.
  • Failing to adjust underwriting in softening markets.
  • Overestimating late fees as sustainable income.
