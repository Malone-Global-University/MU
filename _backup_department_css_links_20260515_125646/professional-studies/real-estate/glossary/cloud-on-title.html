Cloud on Title

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Cloud on Title

Definition

A Cloud on Title is any claim, lien, defect, or irregularity in the chain of title that creates doubt or uncertainty about a property’s legal ownership.

In simple terms: something in the public record makes ownership unclear.

Common Causes of a Cloud on Title

  • Unreleased mortgage or lien
  • Judgment lien against a prior owner
  • Clerical errors in legal description
  • Forgery or fraudulent deed
  • Unknown heirs or probate issues
  • Boundary disputes or encroachments
  • Improper foreclosure process

Why It Matters

  • Prevents or delays sale of property.
  • May block lender financing.
  • Reduces marketability and value.
  • Increases legal risk for buyers.

How It Is Discovered

  • Title search conducted by a title company.
  • Review of a title report or commitment.
  • Survey identifying boundary discrepancies.

How a Cloud on Title Is Cleared

  • Recording a lien release or satisfaction.
  • Corrective deed or affidavit.
  • Settlement with claimant.
  • Quiet title action (court judgment).
  • Probate court resolution.

Cloud on Title vs. Title Defect

  • Cloud on Title: Broad term for any uncertainty.
  • Title Defect: Specific legal flaw impairing ownership.

Example

A contractor files a mechanic’s lien against a property but is later paid. The lien is never formally released. Years later, the owner attempts to sell, and the unreleased lien appears as a cloud on title that must be cleared.

Impact on Investors

  • Closing delays.
  • Additional legal costs.
  • Potential lost buyer opportunities.
  • Holding cost increases.

Risk Mitigation

  • Order title review early in due diligence.
  • Obtain title insurance.
  • Address recorded liens promptly.
  • Maintain accurate documentation of releases.

Investor Checklist

  • Review all exceptions in title commitment.
  • Confirm prior liens are satisfied.
  • Match survey to legal description.
  • Resolve issues before closing when possible.
