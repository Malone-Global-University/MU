Cash Flow Before Taxes (CFBT)

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Cash Flow Before Taxes

Definition

Cash Flow Before Taxes (CFBT) is the amount of cash income generated by a real estate investment after operating expenses and debt service are paid, but before income taxes are considered. It represents the actual cash available to the investor from property operations during a given period, typically measured annually.

Basic Formula

Cash Flow Before Taxes is typically calculated as:

  • Net Operating Income (NOI)
  • minus Debt Service (principal and interest payments)

The result is the amount of cash remaining before tax obligations.

Why It Matters

CFBT helps investors evaluate the income performance of a property. While accounting profits may include non-cash items like depreciation, CFBT focuses on the actual cash generated by the investment.

  • Measures real income available to the investor
  • Helps determine whether a property produces positive cash flow
  • Useful for comparing leveraged investments
  • Forms the basis for metrics such as cash-on-cash return

Example

Suppose a rental property produces:

  • Net Operating Income (NOI): $120,000
  • Annual Debt Service: $80,000

The property’s Cash Flow Before Taxes would be:

$120,000 − $80,000 = $40,000

This $40,000 represents the investor’s annual cash income before taxes.

Investor Uses

  • Income evaluation: determine whether a property generates positive cash flow.
  • Debt analysis: evaluate whether loan payments are sustainable.
  • Investment comparison: compare properties with different financing structures.
  • Cash-on-cash return: calculate return relative to invested equity.

Common Pitfalls

  • Ignoring capital expenditures: CFBT typically excludes large future repairs.
  • Confusing NOI with cash flow: NOI does not include debt payments.
  • Forgetting taxes: actual investor income may be lower after tax obligations.
  • Short-term focus: strong CFBT does not guarantee long-term appreciation.
