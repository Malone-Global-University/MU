Managing Member

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Managing Member

Definition

A Managing Member is the member of a Limited Liability Company (LLC) who has the authority to control operations, make decisions, and bind the entity contractually.

In real estate investments, the managing member typically oversees acquisition, financing, asset management, and disposition activities.

Core Responsibilities

  • Executing the business plan
  • Arranging debt financing
  • Hiring property management and vendors
  • Approving budgets and capital expenditures
  • Overseeing reporting and compliance
  • Making major strategic decisions

Authority Structure

The scope of authority is defined in the LLC’s Operating Agreement. Some decisions may require:

  • Majority member approval
  • Supermajority vote
  • Consent of preferred equity investors

Compensation

  • Acquisition fees
  • Asset management fees
  • Refinance or disposition fees
  • Performance-based promote or carried interest

Risk & Liability

Although LLC structures provide liability protection, managing members may assume additional exposure if they:

  • Sign personal guarantees on loans
  • Commit fraud or misconduct
  • Violate fiduciary duties

Example

In a multifamily syndication, the sponsor serves as managing member, contributing 10–30% of equity while controlling operations and decision-making. Passive investors hold non-managing membership interests.

Common Pitfalls

  • Unclear authority limits: Can create disputes among members.
  • Insufficient reporting: Reduces investor confidence.
  • Misaligned incentives: Fee-heavy structures without performance accountability.
  • Excessive leverage decisions: May increase downside risk.
