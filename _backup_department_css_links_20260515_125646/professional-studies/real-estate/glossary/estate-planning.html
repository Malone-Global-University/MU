Estate Planning

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Definition

Estate planning is the process of structuring ownership, legal documentation, and transfer strategies to manage and distribute real estate and other assets efficiently during life and after death.

Why Estate Planning Matters in Real Estate

  • Ensures smooth transfer of property.
  • Reduces probate delays and costs.
  • Minimizes estate and inheritance taxes.
  • Protects heirs and beneficiaries.
  • Preserves generational wealth.

Common Estate Planning Tools

1) Will

  • Specifies how assets are distributed after death.
  • Subject to probate.

2) Trust

  • Allows assets to be managed and transferred outside probate.
  • May provide tax and asset protection benefits.

3) Joint Tenancy with Right of Survivorship

  • Automatically transfers ownership to surviving co-owner.

4) Community Property with Right of Survivorship

  • Combines marital property rules with survivorship rights.

5) LLC Ownership Structure

  • Facilitates transfer of membership interests.
  • Provides liability protection.

Probate and Real Estate

Probate is the legal process of administering a deceased person’s estate. Proper estate planning can reduce or eliminate probate exposure for real estate holdings.

Tax Considerations

  • Step-up in basis at death may reduce capital gains taxes.
  • Estate tax thresholds vary by jurisdiction.
  • Gifting strategies may reduce taxable estate size.

Investor Considerations

  • Portfolio structure affects transfer complexity.
  • Partnership agreements should include succession planning.
  • Life insurance may be used for liquidity planning.

Example

A real estate investor holds rental properties in an LLC and places membership interests into a revocable living trust. Upon death, the trust distributes ownership according to pre-defined instructions without probate.

Common Pitfalls

  • Failure to update beneficiary designations.
  • Improper property titling.
  • Ignoring state-specific estate laws.
  • Lack of succession planning for partnerships.

Estate Planning vs. Asset Protection

  • Estate Planning: Focuses on transfer after death.
  • Asset Protection: Focuses on shielding assets from creditors during life.
