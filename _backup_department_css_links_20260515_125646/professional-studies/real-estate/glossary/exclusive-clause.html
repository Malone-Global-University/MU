Exclusive Clause

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Exclusive Clause

Definition

An exclusive clause (also called an exclusive use clause) is a lease provision granting a tenant the exclusive right to sell specific goods or provide certain services within a property. It restricts the landlord from leasing space to competitors offering similar products or services.

Purpose of an Exclusive Clause

  • Protects tenant revenue from direct on-site competition.
  • Supports anchor tenant negotiations.
  • Enhances stability in retail tenant mix strategy.
  • Improves long-term lease commitment.

How It Works

  • The clause defines a protected product or service category.
  • The landlord agrees not to lease to another tenant for that defined use.
  • Violations may trigger remedies such as rent reduction or lease termination rights.

Example

A grocery store signs a lease in a shopping center with an exclusive clause preventing the landlord from leasing space to another full-service grocery store within the center.

If the landlord later leases to a competing grocery tenant, the original tenant may be entitled to rent abatement or other contractual remedies.

Scope and Drafting Considerations

  • Definition of restricted products or services.
  • Exceptions for incidental sales by other tenants.
  • Geographic limits (within property vs. broader area).
  • Duration of exclusivity.
  • Carve-outs for anchor or national tenants.

Landlord Risks

  • Reduced leasing flexibility.
  • Potential legal exposure if violated.
  • Complicated lease negotiations with future tenants.
  • Operational conflicts in multi-tenant properties.

Tenant Benefits

  • Revenue protection.
  • Stronger market positioning.
  • Reduced competitive pressure.
  • Enhanced negotiating leverage.

Remedies for Violation

  • Rent reduction or percentage rent adjustment.
  • Termination rights.
  • Injunctive relief.
  • Damages for lost profits (depending on lease language).

Impact on Valuation

  • May strengthen anchor tenant stability.
  • Can increase tenant credit quality retention.
  • May limit upside leasing flexibility.
  • Requires careful review during acquisition underwriting.

Underwriting Considerations

  • Review all exclusive provisions in lease abstracts.
  • Assess compatibility with future tenant mix plans.
  • Evaluate risk of triggering remedies.
  • Confirm compliance with existing tenant agreements.

Common Pitfalls

  • Overly broad exclusivity language.
  • Failure to track exclusive conflicts across leases.
  • Ambiguous product category definitions.
  • Unintended violation due to incidental product overlap.

Exclusive Clause vs. Use Clause

  • Use Clause: Limits what a tenant can do.
  • Exclusive Clause: Limits what other tenants can do.
