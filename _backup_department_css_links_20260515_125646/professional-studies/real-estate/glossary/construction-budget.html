Construction Budget

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Construction Budget

Definition

A construction budget is a detailed financial plan outlining all projected costs associated with building or renovating a real estate project. It includes hard costs, soft costs, contingency reserves, and financing expenses.

Core Components

1. Hard Costs

  • Materials (concrete, steel, lumber)
  • Labor and subcontractors
  • Site work and utilities
  • Mechanical, electrical, plumbing (MEP)

2. Soft Costs

  • Architectural and engineering fees
  • Permits and entitlement costs
  • Legal and accounting services
  • Development management fees

3. Financing Costs

  • Loan origination fees
  • Interest carry during construction
  • Lender inspection and draw fees

4. Contingency

A contingency reserve (often 5–10% of hard costs) is included to account for unforeseen cost overruns or scope changes.

Why It Matters

  • Feasibility analysis: Determines project viability.
  • Lender approval: Required for construction loan underwriting.
  • Return projections: Total cost affects IRR and equity multiple.
  • Risk management: Controls cost overruns and margin compression.

Example

A 30-unit development has:

  • $6,000,000 in hard costs
  • $1,200,000 in soft costs
  • $500,000 in financing costs
  • $600,000 contingency (10% of hard costs)

Total construction budget: $8,300,000

Common Pitfalls

  • Underestimating contingency needs
  • Ignoring interest carry from delays
  • Scope creep during construction
  • Material price volatility

Investor Implications

  • Margin sensitivity: Small overruns significantly impact returns.
  • Equity exposure: Cost overruns often require additional capital.
  • Lender oversight: Budgets are monitored through draw schedules.
  • Execution discipline: Strong cost control protects project viability.
