Section 8

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Section 8

Definition

Section 8, formally known as the Housing Choice Voucher Program, is a U.S. federal housing assistance program that provides rental subsidies to eligible low-income tenants. A local Public Housing Authority (PHA) pays a portion of the rent directly to the landlord.

How It Works

  • Eligible tenants receive a housing voucher.
  • The tenant finds a qualifying rental unit.
  • The property must pass a Housing Quality Standards (HQS) inspection.
  • The PHA pays a portion of the rent directly to the landlord.
  • The tenant pays the remaining portion based on income.

Rent Structure

Total rent is typically determined by:

  • Fair Market Rent (FMR) guidelines set by HUD
  • Local payment standards
  • Unit size and market conditions

The tenant generally pays approximately 30% of their adjusted income toward rent, with the subsidy covering the balance (subject to program limits).

Advantages for Landlords

  • Reliable government-backed portion of rent
  • Reduced vacancy risk in certain markets
  • Stable tenant demand
  • Potential for consistent cash flow

Challenges & Compliance

  • Initial and periodic inspections
  • Administrative paperwork
  • Rent increase approval process
  • Strict lease and compliance standards

Example

Monthly rent for a unit is $1,500.

  • Tenant pays $450 (based on income)
  • PHA pays $1,050 directly to landlord

Investor Perspective

Section 8 properties can offer predictable revenue streams, particularly in workforce housing markets. However, investors should underwrite inspection timelines, administrative processes, and local PHA efficiency when evaluating cash flow stability.

Common Pitfalls

  • Ignoring inspection requirements
  • Underestimating approval timelines
  • Assuming unlimited rent flexibility
  • Failing to understand local PHA policies

Related Terms

  • Net Operating Income (NOI)
