Definition

Price is the amount of money paid or agreed to be paid for a property in a transaction between a buyer and seller.

In simple terms: it’s what someone actually pays.

Price vs. Value

  • Price: The negotiated transaction amount.
  • Value: The estimated worth of a property based on income, market comparables, or replacement cost.

Price may be higher or lower than market value depending on negotiation, competition, distress, or strategic motivations.

How Price Is Determined

  • Supply and demand conditions
  • Income potential (NOI and cap rate)
  • Comparable sales
  • Financing availability
  • Investor expectations

Common Pricing Metrics

  • Price Per Unit
  • Price Per Square Foot
  • Cap Rate Implied Price
  • Gross Rent Multiplier (GRM)

Example: Income-Based Pricing

If a property produces $1,000,000 in Net Operating Income (NOI) and the market cap rate is 5%:

Price = NOI ÷ Cap Rate
Price = $1,000,000 ÷ 0.05 = $20,000,000

Price in Negotiation

  • May include seller concessions or credits.
  • Can reflect off-market premium or discount.
  • May be influenced by timing pressure.
  • Often tied to due diligence findings.

Contract Price vs. Closing Price

  • Contract Price: Amount agreed in purchase agreement.
  • Closing Price: Final amount after adjustments, credits, and prorations.

Price and Returns

  • Lower purchase price generally increases projected returns.
  • Higher price reduces yield unless income grows.
  • Price discipline is critical to long-term performance.

Investor Considerations

  • Compare price to replacement cost.
  • Evaluate price relative to historical sales.
  • Stress-test downside value.
  • Assess price sensitivity to cap rate shifts.

Common Pitfalls

  • Overpaying during peak market cycles.
  • Ignoring deferred maintenance when evaluating price.
  • Failing to adjust for below-market leases.
  • Confusing emotional value with economic value.

Related Terms
