Implied Cap Rate

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Implied Cap Rate

Definition

The Implied Cap Rate is the capitalization rate backed into from a property’s price and its Net Operating Income (NOI).

It represents the yield embedded in the transaction price.

Formula

  • Implied Cap Rate = NOI ÷ Price

Example

If a property is marketed at $18,000,000 and generates $1,080,000 in NOI:

  • Implied Cap Rate = $1,080,000 ÷ $18,000,000 = 6.0%

The 6% yield is the implied cap rate embedded in the asking price.

Why It Matters

  • Quickly reveals pricing relative to income.
  • Allows comparison to Market Cap Rates.
  • Helps determine if a deal meets required return standards.
  • Exposes overpricing in competitive markets.

Implied Cap Rate vs. Going-In Cap Rate

  • Implied Cap Rate: Based on asking or negotiated price.
  • Going-In Cap Rate: Based on actual purchase price at closing.

Before closing, the implied cap rate is often used to analyze listing prices.

Implied Cap Rate vs. Market Cap Rate

  • Market Cap Rate: Prevailing benchmark in the market.
  • Implied Cap Rate: The rate reflected in a specific deal.

If the implied cap rate is below the market cap rate, the property may be priced aggressively.

Investor Discipline

Disciplined investors compare implied cap rates to:

  • Their required return
  • Their underwritten NOI
  • Prevailing market cap rates

If the implied cap rate is too low relative to risk, the price must adjust — or the investor walks.

Common Pitfalls

  • Using inflated pro forma NOI to calculate implied cap rate.
  • Ignoring upcoming tax reassessments.
  • Failing to adjust for abnormal or one-time income.
  • Confusing trailing NOI with stabilized NOI.
