Punch List

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Punch List

Definition

A punch list is a written list of incomplete, defective, or non-conforming construction items identified near the end of a project that must be corrected before final completion and closeout.

In simple terms: it’s the “fix-this-before-we-finish” checklist.

When a Punch List Is Created

A punch list is typically generated during or after:

  • Substantial completion walkthrough
  • Architect or owner inspection
  • Tenant delivery inspection
  • Pre–Certificate of Occupancy review

It marks the transition from major construction to final detailing and corrections.

Examples of Punch List Items

  • Paint touch-ups or drywall repairs
  • Misaligned doors or hardware adjustments
  • Incomplete caulking or trim work
  • Non-functioning electrical outlets or fixtures
  • HVAC balancing issues
  • Damaged flooring or finishes
  • Missing signage or accessories

Punch list items are usually minor relative to total project scope, but they must be resolved for final completion.

Punch List vs. Substantial Completion

  • Substantial Completion: The project is usable for its intended purpose, even if minor items remain.
  • Punch List Completion: All remaining items are corrected and formally signed off.

Substantial completion often triggers:

  • Tenant move-in
  • Rent commencement
  • Conversion of construction loan milestones

Punch List and Retainage

Many construction contracts withhold a percentage of payment (called retainage) until punch list items are completed.

  • Typical retainage: 5%–10% of contract value
  • Released after final inspection and lien waivers
  • Provides incentive for contractor to finish details

Punch List and Certificate of Occupancy

Some punch list items may be completed before a final Certificate of Occupancy is issued. In other cases, a Temporary Certificate of Occupancy (TCO) may allow occupancy while minor punch items are resolved.

Life-safety items generally must be completed before occupancy is permitted.

Developer & Investor Impact

  • Cash flow timing: Delays in punch completion can delay final payments or loan conversion.
  • Tenant satisfaction: Unresolved items affect early tenant experience.
  • Reputation risk: Poor finish quality impacts future leasing velocity.
  • Legal exposure: Uncorrected defects may lead to warranty claims.

Best Practices

  • Conduct detailed walkthroughs with architect and GC.
  • Document items with photos and deadlines.
  • Track completion in a centralized log.
  • Separate cosmetic items from life-safety issues.
  • Obtain final lien waivers before releasing retainage.

Common Pitfalls

  • Allowing tenants to occupy before critical items are resolved.
  • Failing to track subcontractor accountability.
  • Releasing retainage too early.
  • Ignoring warranty documentation at closeout.
  • Not defining clear deadlines in the construction contract.

Investor Checklist

  • Confirm substantial completion certification.
  • Review punch list log and status before funding final draw.
  • Ensure retainage aligns with completion milestones.
  • Verify compliance before final CO issuance.
  • Collect warranties, as-builts, and O&M manuals at closeout.
