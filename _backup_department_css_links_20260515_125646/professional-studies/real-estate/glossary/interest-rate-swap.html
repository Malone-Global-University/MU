Interest Rate Swap

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Definition

An interest rate swap is a financial derivative contract in which two parties exchange interest payment obligations. In commercial real estate, swaps are commonly used to convert floating-rate debt into fixed-rate exposure.

Why Swaps Are Used in Real Estate

  • Stabilize debt service payments.
  • Hedge against rising interest rates.
  • Improve predictability of cash flow.
  • Meet lender risk management requirements.

How It Works

  1. Borrower takes out a floating-rate loan (e.g., SOFR + spread).
  2. Borrower enters into a swap agreement with a counterparty.
  3. Borrower pays fixed rate under the swap.
  4. Counterparty pays floating rate to borrower.
  5. Net effect: borrower effectively pays fixed rate.

Example

  • Loan: SOFR + 2.50%
  • Swap Fixed Rate: 4.00%

If SOFR rises to 5.00%:

  • Loan rate = 5.00% + 2.50% = 7.50%
  • Swap offsets floating exposure, locking effective rate near 6.50% (4.00% fixed + 2.50% spread).

Swap vs. Interest Rate Cap

  • Swap: Converts floating rate to fixed.
  • Cap: Sets a maximum rate but allows participation if rates fall.

Key Risks

  • Basis Risk: Mismatch between loan and swap index.
  • Counterparty Risk: Swap provider default risk.
  • Mark-to-Market Risk: Swap may have negative value if terminated early.
  • Prepayment Risk: Early loan payoff may trigger swap breakage costs.

Mark-to-Market (MTM)

Swaps fluctuate in value based on interest rate movements. If rates fall below the fixed swap rate, the swap may have negative value, resulting in termination costs.

Underwriting Considerations

  • Match swap term to loan maturity.
  • Model termination scenarios.
  • Evaluate hedge effectiveness.
  • Review ISDA agreements and collateral requirements.

Investor Perspective

  • Improves DSCR stability.
  • Reduces NOI volatility from rising rates.
  • May limit benefit in declining rate environments.

Common Pitfalls

  • Failing to account for breakage costs.
  • Ignoring basis risk between loan and hedge.
  • Entering swap without understanding MTM exposure.

Related Terms
