Definition

A covenant is a legally binding promise within a loan, mortgage, or lease agreement that requires a party to take — or refrain from taking — specific actions. Covenants are used to control risk and protect contractual interests.

Types of Covenants

  • Affirmative Covenants: Require specific actions (e.g., maintaining insurance, providing financial reports).
  • Negative Covenants: Restrict certain behaviors (e.g., additional borrowing, property transfers without consent).
  • Financial Covenants: Require maintaining financial ratios such as DSCR or LTV thresholds.

Why Covenants Matter

Covenants provide early warning signals for lenders and landlords. A breach may trigger a technical default even if payments are current. This allows risk mitigation before financial deterioration becomes severe.

Examples in Real Estate

  • Minimum Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) requirements.
  • Restrictions on additional liens.
  • Requirements to maintain property condition.
  • Limitations on major capital expenditures without approval.

Investor Considerations

  • Understand all covenants before signing loan documents.
  • Stress-test financial projections against covenant thresholds.
  • Monitor compliance regularly.
  • Negotiate flexibility where possible.
