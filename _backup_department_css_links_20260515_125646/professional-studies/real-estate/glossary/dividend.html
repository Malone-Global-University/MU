Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Dividend

Definition

A dividend is a distribution of profits or cash flow from a real estate investment, REIT, or partnership to its investors or shareholders. Dividends provide a way for investors to receive returns without selling the underlying property or equity stake.

How Dividends Are Used

  • Regular income for investors in real estate partnerships or REITs.
  • Reinvestment opportunities to compound returns.
  • Signal of strong property performance and financial health.

Calculation

Dividends are typically calculated as a portion of net income or cash flow available after expenses, debt service, and reserves. For example, a REIT might pay out 90% of its taxable income as dividends to shareholders.

Example

An investor owns shares in a real estate investment trust (REIT) that collects rent from multiple properties. The REIT earns $1,000,000 in net income for the quarter and distributes $900,000 to shareholders as dividends. If the investor owns 1% of the REIT, they would receive $9,000 in dividend income.

Investor Considerations

  • Yield: dividend amount relative to investment price.
  • Tax treatment: dividends may be taxed differently depending on entity type and jurisdiction.
  • Reliability: dependent on property performance and management.
  • Reinvestment options: ability to reinvest dividends for compounding.

Related Terms

  • Cash Flow Before Taxes (CFBT)
  • REIT
  • Equity
  • Preferred Equity
  • Return on Investment (ROI)
