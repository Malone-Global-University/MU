Capital Stack

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Capital Stack

Definition

The capital stack is the layered structure of financing used to acquire or develop a property. It includes various forms of debt and equity, ranked by priority of repayment and risk exposure.

Typical Capital Stack Structure

  • Senior Debt – First priority repayment, lowest risk.
  • Mezzanine Debt – Subordinate to senior debt, higher yield.
  • Preferred Equity – Priority over common equity, fixed or preferred return.
  • Common Equity – Highest risk, highest potential return.

Repayment Priority

In a sale or liquidation:

  1. Senior lender gets paid first.
  2. Subordinate lenders next.
  3. Preferred equity investors next.
  4. Common equity investors last.

Risk and Return Relationship

  • Lower in stack = higher risk.
  • Higher in stack = lower risk.
  • Higher risk layers demand higher returns.

Example

A $10 million acquisition may be financed as:

  • $6.5M Senior Loan (65%)
  • $1M Mezzanine Debt (10%)
  • $500K Preferred Equity (5%)
  • $2M Common Equity (20%)

Each layer carries different risk and compensation expectations.

Relation to Distribution Waterfalls

Equity layers often operate under a waterfall distribution structure, where return of capital and preferred return are paid before promote participation.

Strategic Considerations

  • Higher leverage increases equity returns but adds risk.
  • Mezzanine layers increase cost of capital.
  • Institutional lenders favor stabilized assets.
  • Non-recourse structures alter risk distribution.

Common Investor Mistakes

  • Over-leveraging to boost IRR
  • Ignoring refinance risk
  • Misunderstanding subordination terms
  • Underestimating debt service coverage requirements

Investor Checklist

  • Model stress scenarios.
  • Understand repayment priority.
  • Review intercreditor agreements.
  • Evaluate blended cost of capital.
  • Align capital structure with business plan risk.

Strategic Insight

The capital stack determines who wins — and who absorbs losses — under every scenario. Sophisticated investors design capital structures to balance growth with durability.

Related Terms
