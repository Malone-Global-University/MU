Construction Loan

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Construction Loan

Definition

A Construction Loan is a short-term loan used to finance the development, ground-up construction, or major renovation of a property.

Unlike permanent financing, funds are disbursed in stages based on construction progress.

How It Works

  • Loan is approved based on project budget and projected value.
  • Funds are released through a draw schedule.
  • Borrower pays interest only on the amount drawn.
  • Loan typically matures in 12–36 months.

Key Features

  • Interest-Only Payments during construction
  • Higher Interest Rates due to increased risk
  • Loan-to-Cost (LTC) underwriting metrics
  • Inspections & Draw Reviews before funding

Loan-to-Cost (LTC)

Construction loans are often sized based on total project cost:

Loan Amount = Total Project Cost × LTC %

Example: If total cost is $20 million and lender offers 65% LTC, loan amount equals $13 million.

Exit Strategy

  • Refinance into permanent (stabilized) loan
  • Sell property upon completion
  • Convert to long-term hold financing

Why It Matters to Investors

  • Higher risk profile than stabilized loans
  • Exposure to cost overruns and delays
  • Sensitivity to interest rate fluctuations
  • Dependent on market absorption at completion

Example

A developer builds a 150-unit apartment project with a $30 million total budget. A lender provides a 70% LTC construction loan. As construction progresses, funds are disbursed monthly after third-party inspection approval.

Common Pitfalls

  • Underestimating contingency reserves
  • Construction delays
  • Interest rate spikes
  • Failure to secure take-out financing

Related Terms

  • Loan-to-Value (LTV)
  • Loan-to-Cost (LTC)
  • Interest Rate
  • Mechanic’s Lien
  • Permanent Loan
  • Special Purpose Entity (SPE)
