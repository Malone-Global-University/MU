Levered IRR

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Levered IRR

Definition

Levered IRR (Levered Internal Rate of Return) is the annualized rate of return earned by equity investors after accounting for debt financing. It incorporates loan proceeds, interest payments, principal amortization, and sale proceeds net of debt payoff.

What It Measures

  • Cash flows available to equity holders.
  • Impact of leverage on investment returns.
  • Timing of capital contributions and distributions.

Levered vs. Unlevered IRR

  • Unlevered IRR: return assuming no debt financing.
  • Levered IRR: return after incorporating borrowing effects.

Leverage can amplify both gains and losses. Positive leverage increases IRR when property returns exceed borrowing costs.

Example

An investor purchases a property for $10 million using $3 million in equity and $7 million in debt. After five years, the property sells for $13 million. After loan repayment and expenses, equity investors receive $6 million in total distributions. The IRR calculated on the $3 million initial equity investment represents the levered IRR.

Why It Matters

  • Capital efficiency: measures return on invested equity.
  • Risk assessment: highlights leverage sensitivity.
  • Investment comparison: enables comparison across capital structures.

Risks of Leverage

  • Higher volatility in returns.
  • Increased default risk during downturns.
  • Sensitivity to interest rate changes.

Strategic Considerations

  • Stress-test DSCR and exit cap rates.
  • Evaluate loan maturity and refinancing risk.
  • Compare levered returns to required return thresholds.
  • Align leverage with investment strategy (core vs. value-add).

Related Terms

  • Loan-to-Value (LTV)
  • Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
