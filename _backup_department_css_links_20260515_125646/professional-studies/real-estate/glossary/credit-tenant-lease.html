Credit Tenant Lease (CTL)

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Credit Tenant Lease (CTL)

Definition

A Credit Tenant Lease (CTL) is a financing structure where the lender underwrites the loan primarily based on the credit quality of the tenant rather than the real estate itself.

CTL loans are typically used for single-tenant properties leased to investment-grade companies under long-term triple-net leases.

How CTL Financing Works

  • Property is leased to a strong-credit tenant.
  • Lease term usually matches or exceeds loan term.
  • Lender evaluates tenant credit rating.
  • Loan terms are structured similarly to corporate bonds.

Key Characteristics

  • Long-term lease (often 15–25 years).
  • Minimal landlord responsibilities.
  • Predictable rent stream.
  • Often non-recourse financing.

Underwriting Focus

  • Tenant credit rating (e.g., investment grade).
  • Lease enforceability.
  • Corporate guarantee strength.
  • Remaining lease term vs loan maturity.

CTL vs Traditional Commercial Mortgage

  • CTL: Credit-driven underwriting.
  • Traditional Loan: Property value and cash flow driven.

CTL loans often have lower interest rates due to reduced perceived risk.

Advantages

  • Lower borrowing costs.
  • Higher loan-to-value ratios possible.
  • Stable and predictable cash flow.
  • Attractive to institutional investors.

Risks

  • Tenant default risk.
  • Limited property reuse if tenant vacates.
  • Concentration risk (single tenant).
  • Interest rate market fluctuations.

Common CTL Property Types

  • Corporate headquarters.
  • Build-to-suit facilities.
  • Government-leased properties.
  • National retail single-tenant assets.

Investor Checklist

  • Review tenant credit rating and financials.
  • Analyze lease structure and term.
  • Confirm loan maturity aligns with lease expiration.
  • Evaluate re-leasing risk at lease end.

Related Terms

  • Triple Net Lease (NNN)
  • Loan-to-Value (LTV)
  • Capitalization Rate
  • Non-Recourse Loan
  • Credit Enhancement
