Cap Rate Expansion

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Cap Rate Expansion

Definition

Cap rate expansion occurs when capitalization rates increase, typically due to rising interest rates, higher perceived risk, weaker market fundamentals, or tighter capital markets. Because property value is inversely related to the cap rate, expansion leads to lower property valuations.

Valuation Relationship

Property Value = NOI ÷ Cap Rate

When the cap rate rises while NOI remains constant, the property value decreases.

Example

A property generates $1,000,000 in NOI:

  • At a 5.0% cap rate → Value = $20,000,000
  • At a 6.0% cap rate → Value = $16,667,000

The 100 basis point increase in cap rate results in a significant decline in value.

Common Drivers of Cap Rate Expansion

  • Rising interest rates
  • Credit spread widening
  • Economic downturn or recession
  • Declining rent growth expectations
  • Increased risk premiums demanded by investors

Expansion vs. NOI Decline

Property values can decline due to:

  • Operational deterioration: Reduced NOI.
  • Market repricing: Cap rate expansion.

In many downturns, both occur simultaneously—compounding valuation pressure.

Impact on Leveraged Investors

  • Higher refinance risk at loan maturity.
  • Reduced loan proceeds due to lower valuations.
  • Potential covenant breaches (LTV or DSCR).
  • Equity value erosion.

Investor Applications

  • Stress testing: Model exit values under higher reversion cap rates.
  • Conservative underwriting: Avoid assuming continued compression.
  • Debt structuring: Maintain flexibility in volatile rate environments.
  • Portfolio risk assessment: Evaluate exposure to rate-driven repricing.

Common Pitfalls

  • Overleveraging in low-cap environments: Minimal buffer against expansion.
  • Ignoring capital market signals: Spread widening often precedes value decline.
  • Underestimating rate sensitivity: Small cap rate moves can significantly impact value.
