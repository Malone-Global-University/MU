Definition

Value in real estate refers to the estimated worth of a property, determined by income potential, market conditions, comparable sales, replacement cost, and investor return requirements.

Value is not the same as price — price is what is paid; value is what the property is worth under a given set of assumptions.

Primary Valuation Methods

1. Income Approach

Common for income-producing properties.

  • Value = NOI ÷ Cap Rate
  • Based on Net Operating Income (NOI)
  • Uses Market Cap Rate or required return

2. Sales Comparison Approach

  • Based on comparable property sales
  • Adjusted for size, location, and condition differences

3. Cost Approach

  • Land value + replacement cost of improvements − depreciation
  • Often used for new construction or special-use properties

Market Value vs. Investment Value

  • Market Value: What the broader market is willing to pay.
  • Investment Value: What the property is worth to a specific investor based on their required return.

A disciplined investor may walk away from a deal even if the market supports a higher price.

Example (Income Approach)

If a property produces $1,200,000 in stabilized NOI and the market cap rate is 6.5%:

  • Value = $1,200,000 ÷ 0.065 = $18,461,538

What Drives Value?

  • Income durability
  • Growth potential
  • Risk profile
  • Capital market conditions
  • Supply and demand dynamics

Value vs. Price

Market psychology, capital flows, and competition can push prices above or below intrinsic value.

Long-term investors focus on:

  • Underwritten NOI
  • Required return
  • Margin of safety

Common Pitfalls

  • Relying solely on recent comparable sales
  • Ignoring risk-adjusted return standards
  • Overestimating future rent growth
  • Assuming cap rate compression

Investor Discipline

Sustainable wealth creation in real estate is driven by:

  • Buying below intrinsic value
  • Growing income through operations
  • Maintaining conservative assumptions
  • Protecting capital first

Related Terms

  • Net operating income (NOI)
  • Market cap rate
  • Implied cap rate
  • Going-in cap rate
  • Reversion cap rate
  • Underwritten NOI
  • Margin of safety
