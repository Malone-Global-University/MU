Cap Rate Compression & Expansion

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Cap Rate Compression & Expansion

Definition

Cap rate compression occurs when capitalization rates decline, resulting in higher property valuations.

Cap rate expansion occurs when capitalization rates rise, resulting in lower property valuations.

Valuation Mechanics

Property Value = Net Operating Income (NOI) ÷ Cap Rate

Because cap rate appears in the denominator, small changes can significantly impact property value.

Example

NOI: $1,000,000

  • At 5% Cap → Value = $20,000,000
  • At 6% Cap → Value = $16,666,667

A 1% increase in cap rate reduces value by over $3 million.

Causes of Cap Rate Compression

  • Lower interest rates.
  • Strong investor demand.
  • Improved market fundamentals.
  • Reduced perceived risk.

Causes of Cap Rate Expansion

  • Rising interest rates.
  • Economic uncertainty.
  • Increased risk premiums.
  • Declining property fundamentals.

Impact on Returns

  • Compression increases exit value and boosts IRR.
  • Expansion reduces resale value and compresses equity returns.
  • Leverage magnifies both effects.

Market Cycle Considerations

  • Early expansion phases often see compression.
  • Late-cycle or tightening environments may see expansion.
  • Interest rate movements strongly influence trends.

Underwriting Strategy

  • Use conservative exit cap rates.
  • Stress test multiple cap scenarios.
  • Avoid relying solely on future compression.
  • Focus on NOI growth as primary value driver.

Common Pitfalls

  • Overpaying based on assumed compression.
  • Ignoring macroeconomic risks.
  • Underestimating impact of rising debt costs.

Investor Checklist

  • Analyze interest rate environment.
  • Model exit cap sensitivity.
  • Compare going-in cap vs exit cap assumptions.
  • Evaluate long-term demand fundamentals.

Related Terms

  • Capitalization Rate (Cap Rate)
  • Net Operating Income (NOI)
  • Internal Rate of Return (IRR)
  • Negative Leverage
  • Positive Leverage
