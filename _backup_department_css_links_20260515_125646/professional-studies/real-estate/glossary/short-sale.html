Short Sale

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Short Sale

Definition

A short sale occurs when a property is sold for less than the outstanding mortgage balance, and the lender agrees to accept the reduced payoff. It is commonly used as an alternative to foreclosure.

How a Short Sale Works

  • Borrower experiences financial hardship.
  • Property value falls below loan balance.
  • Lender approves sale price below outstanding debt.
  • Sale proceeds are applied toward the mortgage.

Why Lenders Approve Short Sales

  • Avoid lengthy foreclosure process.
  • Reduce legal and carrying costs.
  • Limit market exposure risk.
  • Minimize property deterioration.

Investor Implications

  • Potential opportunity to acquire discounted assets.
  • Extended approval timelines and negotiation risk.
  • Uncertainty regarding lien priority or secondary debt.
  • Property condition may be distressed.

Short Sale vs. Foreclosure

  • Short Sale: Voluntary sale with lender approval before foreclosure.
  • Foreclosure: Legal seizure of property after default.

Risk Management

  • Perform thorough title review.
  • Verify lien releases before closing.
  • Underwrite conservatively for condition risk.
  • Plan for timeline variability.
