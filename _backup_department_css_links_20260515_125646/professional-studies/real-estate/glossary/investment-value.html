Investment Value

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Investment Value

Definition

Investment value is the value of a property to a specific investor, based on their required rate of return, cost of capital, tax situation, risk tolerance, and strategic goals. It may differ from the broader market value.

How It’s Calculated

Investment value is typically determined using projected cash flows and the investor’s required return (discount rate).

  • Direct Cap Method: Value = Stabilized NOI ÷ Required Return
  • Discounted Cash Flow (DCF): Present value of projected cash flows + exit value

Example

A property produces $1,000,000 in NOI. The broader market values similar properties at a 6% cap rate.

  • Market Value = 1,000,000 ÷ 0.06 = $16,666,667

However, an investor requiring an 8% return would calculate:

  • Investment Value = 1,000,000 ÷ 0.08 = $12,500,000

The investor would not pay more than $12.5 million, even if the market supports higher pricing.

Why It Differs From Market Value

  • Different return requirements
  • Unique financing structures
  • Tax advantages or disadvantages
  • Synergies with existing portfolio assets
  • Risk tolerance and time horizon

Strategic Importance

Investment value protects discipline. It prevents investors from overpaying in competitive markets and ensures acquisitions meet predefined return thresholds.

Investor Perspective

Professional investors often establish a written, non-negotiable investment standard. They calculate maximum purchase price based on projected income and required return — not emotional bidding or market pressure.

Common Pitfalls

  • Lowering required return to win deals
  • Assuming market value guarantees investment performance
  • Overestimating future rent growth
  • Ignoring downside risk scenarios
