Leasehold Interest

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Leasehold Interest

Definition

A leasehold interest is a tenant’s legal right to possess and use real property for a specified term under a lease agreement. The leaseholder does not own the underlying land (fee simple), but holds contractual rights defined by the lease.

Where It Appears

  • Ground lease developments
  • Long-term commercial leases
  • Retail pad sites
  • Hotel and institutional land arrangements

Economic Value

A leasehold interest may have positive or negative value depending on:

  • Remaining lease term
  • Contract rent vs. market rent
  • Escalation structure
  • Assignment rights
  • Reversion provisions

Positive vs. Negative Leasehold

  • Positive Leasehold: Contract rent is below market rent, creating economic benefit.
  • Negative Leasehold: Contract rent exceeds market rent, reducing value.

Example

A tenant holds a 40-year ground lease at $300,000 annually, while market ground rent is now $450,000.

The below-market rent may create positive leasehold value that could be assignable or financeable, depending on lease terms.

Financing Considerations

  • Lenders review remaining lease term relative to loan maturity.
  • Subordination clauses may impact financing flexibility.
  • Ground rent payments are typically senior to equity distributions.

Valuation Impact

Leasehold interests are typically valued differently from fee-simple properties. Time limitations, ground rent obligations, and reversion risk can affect cap rates and investor demand.

Investor Perspective

Investors should carefully model lease expiration timelines, ground rent escalations, and residual value assumptions. Leasehold assets may offer capital efficiency but carry structural complexity.

Common Pitfalls

  • Ignoring lease expiration risk
  • Underestimating rent escalations
  • Overlooking assignment restrictions
  • Assuming fee-simple valuation multiples

  • Fee Simple
