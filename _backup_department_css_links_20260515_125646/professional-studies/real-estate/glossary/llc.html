Limited Liability Company (LLC)

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary LLC

Definition

A Limited Liability Company (LLC) is a legal entity that protects its owners (called members) from personal liability for business debts and lawsuits, while offering flexible tax treatment.

Why Real Estate Investors Use LLCs

  • Protect personal assets from property-level lawsuits
  • Separate risk across multiple properties
  • Facilitate partnerships and capital contributions
  • Allow pass-through taxation

Liability Protection

In most cases, claims against a property owned by an LLC are limited to the assets of that LLC. Personal assets of members are generally protected — unless:

  • A personal guarantee is signed
  • Fraud or misconduct occurs
  • Corporate formalities are ignored

Tax Flexibility

  • Single-member LLC: Taxed as sole proprietorship (default)
  • Multi-member LLC: Taxed as partnership (default)
  • May elect S-Corp or C-Corp taxation if beneficial

LLCs typically provide pass-through taxation, meaning profits and losses flow directly to members’ tax returns.

Operating Agreement

An LLC is governed by an operating agreement that outlines:

  • Ownership percentages
  • Voting rights
  • Profit distribution rules
  • Capital contribution obligations
  • Transfer restrictions

Example

An investor purchases a small apartment building through an LLC. If a tenant sues due to a premises liability issue, the lawsuit is generally limited to the LLC’s assets, not the investor’s personal home or savings — unless negligence or guarantees apply.

Common Mistakes

  • Commingling personal and business funds
  • Failing to maintain separate bank accounts
  • Ignoring annual state filing requirements
  • Not drafting a formal operating agreement
  • Assuming an LLC eliminates all personal risk

Investor Checklist

  • Form separate LLCs for higher-risk assets.
  • Maintain proper documentation and bookkeeping.
  • Review operating agreement regularly.
  • Coordinate LLC structure with insurance strategy.
  • Consult legal and tax professionals before forming.

Strategic Insight

An LLC is a foundational risk management tool. It supports scalability, protects capital, and creates a structured framework for partnerships and long-term portfolio growth.
