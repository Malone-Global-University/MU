Personal Guarantee

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Personal Guarantee

Definition

A personal guarantee is a contractual commitment in which an individual agrees to be personally liable for the debt or obligations of a company if that company defaults.

This effectively pierces the liability shield of an LLC or corporation for the specific guaranteed obligation.

Where Personal Guarantees Appear

  • Real estate loans (especially smaller or recourse loans)
  • Commercial leases for startup tenants
  • Construction contracts
  • Lines of credit

Recourse vs. Non-Recourse Loans

  • Recourse Loan: Borrower (or guarantor) is personally liable.
  • Non-Recourse Loan: Lender’s recovery is limited to the collateral, subject to carve-outs.

Many “non-recourse” loans still include limited personal guarantees for bad acts, known as carve-out guarantees.

Common Guarantee Types

  • Full Payment Guarantee: Guarantor responsible for entire debt.
  • Limited Guarantee: Liability capped at a dollar amount or percentage.
  • Completion Guarantee: Ensures project completion.
  • Bad Boy Guarantee: Triggered by fraud, misrepresentation, bankruptcy filing, or misconduct.

Why Lenders Require Them

  • Reduce credit risk
  • Align incentives
  • Protect against strategic default
  • Compensate for weaker balance sheets

Risks to Investors

  • Exposure of personal assets
  • Potential litigation
  • Credit score damage
  • Long-term contingent liability

Example

An investor forms an LLC to purchase a small multifamily property. The lender requires the investor to sign a personal guarantee. If the property underperforms and the loan defaults, the lender may pursue the investor’s personal assets.

Negotiation Strategies

  • Request step-down guarantees tied to performance milestones.
  • Cap liability to a defined amount.
  • Limit scope to specific obligations.
  • Seek removal after refinance or stabilization.
  • Ensure clarity around carve-out triggers.

Investor Checklist

  • Understand scope and duration of guarantee.
  • Evaluate personal asset exposure.
  • Review carve-out provisions carefully.
  • Coordinate with legal counsel before signing.
  • Consider long-term liquidity implications.

Strategic Insight

Personal guarantees shift risk from the entity to the individual. Sophisticated investors negotiate limitations, align incentives, and reduce exposure as the asset stabilizes.
