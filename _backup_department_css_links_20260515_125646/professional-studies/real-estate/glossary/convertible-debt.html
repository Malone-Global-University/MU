Convertible Debt

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Convertible Debt

Definition

Convertible debt is a loan that allows the lender to convert the outstanding balance into an ownership interest (equity) in the borrower under predefined terms.

It blends characteristics of debt and equity.

How Convertible Debt Works

  • Investor provides capital as a loan.
  • Borrower makes interest payments (or accrues interest).
  • At a triggering event, the lender may convert debt into equity.

Common Conversion Triggers

  • Equity financing round.
  • Property sale or recapitalization.
  • Maturity date.
  • Predefined valuation event.

Key Terms

  • Conversion Price: The price at which debt converts into equity.
  • Valuation Cap: Maximum valuation used to calculate conversion.
  • Discount Rate: Reduced conversion price relative to future investors.
  • Maturity Date: Date when principal is due if not converted.

Position in the Capital Stack

Initially structured as debt, convertible debt typically ranks above preferred stock and common equity.

Upon conversion, it moves into an equity position and becomes subordinate to debt.

Why Sponsors Use Convertible Debt

  • Bridge capital during development.
  • Delay valuation negotiations.
  • Provide downside protection to investors.
  • Offer potential upside participation.

Risk Profile

  • Lower risk than common equity while structured as debt.
  • Higher risk than senior secured debt.
  • Upside potential if project performs well.

Convertible Debt in Real Estate

In development projects or value-add syndications, convertible debt may be used to fund early stages before permanent financing is secured.

It is less common in stabilized income-producing assets but may appear in opportunistic structures.

Common Pitfalls

  • Unclear conversion mechanics.
  • Ambiguous valuation cap provisions.
  • Conflicts with senior lender restrictions.
  • Misalignment between debt and equity holders.

Investor Checklist

  • Review conversion formula carefully.
  • Analyze senior debt subordination terms.
  • Understand maturity and repayment provisions.
  • Evaluate sponsor track record and exit plan.
