Hard Money Loan

Real Estate Investing Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Professional Studies Real Estate Track Glossary Hard Money Loan

Definition

A hard money loan is a short-term, asset-based loan provided by private lenders and secured primarily by real estate.

Unlike traditional bank financing, underwriting focuses more on the property’s value than on the borrower’s creditworthiness.

Key Characteristics

  • Short-term (typically 6–24 months).
  • Higher interest rates.
  • Higher fees (points).
  • Fast approval and funding.
  • Lower documentation requirements.

How It Works

  • Borrower identifies an investment opportunity.
  • Hard money lender evaluates property value and exit plan.
  • Loan is issued based on loan-to-value (LTV).
  • Borrower repays through sale or refinance.

Common Use Cases

  • Fix-and-flip projects.
  • Bridge financing.
  • Distressed property acquisition.
  • Time-sensitive transactions.

Hard Money vs Traditional Loan

  • Hard Money: Asset-based, fast, expensive.
  • Bank Loan: Credit-based, slower, lower cost.

Pricing Structure

  • Interest rates often significantly above conventional loans.
  • Origination fees (“points”) charged upfront.
  • May include extension fees.

Risk Profile

  • Higher borrowing costs.
  • Short maturity risk.
  • Reliance on successful exit strategy.
  • Potential foreclosure if project underperforms.

Underwriting Focus

  • After-repair value (ARV).
  • Loan-to-value (LTV).
  • Exit strategy viability.
  • Sponsor experience.

Common Pitfalls

  • Underestimating renovation costs.
  • Overestimating resale value.
  • Failure to refinance before maturity.
  • Ignoring compounding interest and fees.

Investor Checklist

  • Confirm realistic exit plan.
  • Stress test market downturn scenarios.
  • Calculate total loan cost including points.
  • Verify clear title and lien position.

Related Terms

  • Bridge Loan
  • Loan-to-Value (LTV)
  • Collateral
  • Foreclosure
  • Distressed Property
