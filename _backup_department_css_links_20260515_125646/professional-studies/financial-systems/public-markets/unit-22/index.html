Unit 22: Trade Confirmation and Post-Trade Monitoring

Learn how investment organizations verify completed trades, coordinate settlement with brokers and custodians, detect errors, reconcile transactions, and monitor post-trade activity to ensure portfolio records remain accurate.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Public Markets & Portfolio Management Track Unit 22: Trade Confirmation and Post-Trade Monitoring

Where This Unit Fits

This unit follows Unit 21, where trades are executed in the market. After execution occurs, investment organizations must verify that trade details match expectations, coordinate settlement of the transaction, and update portfolio records accurately.

Post-trade monitoring ensures that completed trades are properly recorded, errors are detected quickly, and operational systems remain aligned with the actual positions held in the portfolio.

Unit Overview

Trade execution is only one stage of the investment workflow. Once a trade is completed, operational teams must confirm the transaction details, coordinate settlement with counterparties, and reconcile records across systems.

This unit introduces the post-trade workflow used by institutional investment organizations. Students study confirmation procedures, settlement coordination, reconciliation processes, error detection, exception investigation, and post-trade reporting infrastructure. These systems help ensure that portfolio records reflect the true economic positions of the portfolio.

Why This Matters in Portfolio Operations

Incorrect trade records can create serious operational risk. If trades are recorded incorrectly, portfolio valuations, performance reports, and risk analytics may all become inaccurate. Post-trade monitoring helps prevent these issues by ensuring that transaction details match broker confirmations and settlement records.

Institutional investors rely on structured confirmation and reconciliation processes to detect discrepancies early, correct operational errors, and maintain reliable portfolio records across trading systems, custodians, and internal accounting platforms.

What You’ll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How trade confirmations verify the details of executed transactions
  • How settlement coordination ensures securities and cash move correctly
  • How reconciliation processes align internal records with broker reports
  • How operational systems detect trade errors or discrepancies
  • How exception investigation resolves mismatches in trade records
  • How post-trade reporting supports operational transparency

Operational Competencies

  • Explain how completed trades are verified after execution
  • Understand how settlement processes transfer securities and funds
  • Recognize how reconciliation protects portfolio data accuracy
  • Describe how operational teams detect and investigate trade exceptions
  • Interpret why post-trade monitoring is critical for institutional portfolio management

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • How do firms verify that a completed trade was executed correctly?
  • What happens if trade records differ between broker and portfolio systems?
  • How do institutions ensure securities and cash settle properly?
  • Why is reconciliation necessary for accurate portfolio reporting?

Lessons in This Unit

Post-Trade Foundations

  • Lesson 22.1: Trade Confirmation Procedures

    Learn how firms verify trade details by comparing execution records with broker confirmations.

  • Lesson 22.2: Settlement Coordination with Brokers

    Study how securities and cash transfers are coordinated between brokers, custodians, and portfolio systems.

  • Lesson 22.3: Post-Trade Reconciliation

    Examine how institutions reconcile internal portfolio records with broker statements and settlement data.

  • Lesson 22.4: Trade Error Detection

    Understand how firms identify discrepancies such as incorrect quantities, prices, or settlement instructions.

Exception Handling and Reporting

  • Lesson 22.5: Exception Investigation

    Learn how operational teams investigate mismatches and resolve trade errors before they affect portfolio records.

  • Lesson 22.6: Post-Trade Reporting Systems

    Study how firms generate operational reports summarizing trade activity, settlement status, and reconciliation results.

  • Lesson 22.7: The Post-Trade Monitoring Framework

    Connect confirmation, settlement, reconciliation, exception handling, and reporting into a unified post-trade monitoring process.
