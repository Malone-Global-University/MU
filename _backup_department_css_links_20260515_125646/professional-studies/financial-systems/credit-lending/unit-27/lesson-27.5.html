Lesson 27.5: Payment Resolution and Cure Strategies

Learn how lenders pursue repayment plans, catch-up arrangements, and short-term accommodations to restore delinquent loans to performing status.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Credit & Lending Operations Track Unit 27: Delinquency Management Foundations Lesson 27.5

Where This Lesson Fits

Lesson 27.4 explained how lenders evaluate borrower hardship and determine why delinquency occurred. Once the cause of delinquency is understood, lenders must determine how the borrower can bring the loan back into good standing.

Lesson 27.5 focuses on payment resolution and cure strategies. It explains the methods lenders use to help borrowers resolve missed payments and restore delinquent loans to performing status.

Lesson Objective

By the end of this lesson, students should be able to explain how lenders use repayment arrangements, catch-up plans, and other cure strategies to resolve delinquency and return loans to performing status.

Lesson Overview

When a borrower becomes delinquent, the lender's goal is often to restore the account to performing status as quickly as possible. This process is known as curing the delinquency.

Cure strategies vary depending on the borrower's financial circumstances, the size of the missed payment, and institutional policy. In many cases, lenders work with borrowers to develop structured repayment solutions that allow overdue amounts to be repaid over time.

Immediate Payment Resolution

The simplest form of cure occurs when the borrower makes the missed payment shortly after the delinquency begins. Some borrowers simply forget a payment or experience a minor delay in income or cash flow.

Once the overdue amount is paid, the loan returns to current status and routine servicing continues.

Catch-Up Payment Arrangements

In situations where borrowers cannot repay the full overdue amount immediately, lenders may establish catch-up arrangements. These agreements allow borrowers to repay the missed payment gradually over several future installments.

For example, a borrower who missed one monthly payment may agree to add a portion of the overdue amount to several upcoming payments until the delinquency is fully cured.

Short-Term Payment Accommodations

Some borrowers experience temporary hardship that requires short-term flexibility. In these cases, lenders may provide temporary payment accommodations, such as adjusted payment schedules or brief deferrals.

These arrangements are typically limited in duration and designed to help borrowers recover from temporary financial disruption while preserving the lender's expectation of repayment.

Structured Repayment Plans

For more significant delinquency, lenders may establish structured repayment plans. These plans outline how the borrower will repay both current obligations and past-due amounts over a defined period.

Structured repayment plans help borrowers manage financial strain while providing lenders with a clear path toward restoring loan performance.

Evaluating Feasibility of Cure

Before approving any repayment arrangement, lenders typically evaluate whether the borrower has the capacity to meet the proposed payment plan. This evaluation often considers the borrower's income, expenses, financial obligations, and overall repayment capacity.

The goal is to create a resolution strategy that is realistic and sustainable. Unrealistic repayment plans may lead to repeated delinquency or deeper financial distress.

Monitoring Repayment Agreements

Once a repayment arrangement has been established, lenders monitor the borrower's compliance with the agreement. Servicing systems track whether scheduled payments are received and whether the borrower is meeting the agreed repayment schedule.

If the borrower successfully completes the repayment plan, the loan returns fully to performing status. If the borrower fails to meet the arrangement, the account may require escalation or additional review.

Balancing Assistance and Risk Control

Cure strategies must balance borrower assistance with sound credit risk management. While lenders aim to help borrowers overcome temporary difficulties, they must also protect the financial stability of the lending portfolio.

For this reason, institutions typically establish policies governing when repayment plans can be offered, how long they may last, and what documentation is required.

Connection to Escalation Processes

Not all delinquent loans can be cured through repayment arrangements. If borrowers cannot meet repayment plans or continue missing payments, the lender may escalate the account into more intensive delinquency handling or collections processes.

Understanding when cure strategies are likely to succeed helps lenders determine when escalation becomes necessary.

Real-World Example

A borrower with a personal loan misses two monthly payments after experiencing temporary medical expenses. After discussing the situation with the lender, the borrower agrees to a repayment plan that adds a portion of the overdue balance to the next six monthly payments.

Because the repayment arrangement is manageable within the borrower's income, the borrower successfully completes the plan and the loan returns to performing status.

Common Mistakes

Mistake 1: Offering unrealistic repayment plans

Repayment arrangements must match the borrower's financial capacity in order to succeed.

Mistake 2: Failing to monitor repayment agreements

Without monitoring, lenders may not detect when borrowers fall behind again during a cure plan.

Mistake 3: Assuming every delinquency can be cured

Some delinquent loans will require escalation rather than repayment accommodation.

Key Terms

Cure — The process of restoring a delinquent loan to performing status by resolving past-due payments.

Repayment Plan — A structured arrangement allowing borrowers to repay overdue amounts over time.

Catch-Up Arrangement — A payment schedule that gradually repays missed payments across future installments.

Payment Accommodation — A temporary adjustment to loan payment terms designed to help borrowers overcome short-term hardship.

Delinquency Resolution — The process of resolving overdue payments and returning the loan to current status.

Lesson Summary

  • Cure strategies aim to restore delinquent loans to performing status.
  • Common methods include immediate payment, catch-up arrangements, and structured repayment plans.
  • Lenders evaluate borrower financial capacity before approving repayment agreements.
  • Repayment arrangements must be monitored to ensure compliance.
  • If cure strategies fail, accounts may be escalated to more intensive delinquency management or collections processes.
