Credit & Lending Operations Track • Layer 6: Distressed Credit & Secondary Markets

Unit 29: Collections and Recovery

Learn how lenders recover capital when loans move beyond restructuring. This unit introduces collections workflows, repossession, foreclosure processes, liquidation strategy, and charge-off procedures.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Credit & Lending Operations Track Unit 29: Collections and Recovery

Where This Unit Fits

This unit follows restructuring and special servicing. When loan modification or workout strategies fail to restore repayment, the lender must shift from stabilization efforts to recovery operations.

Collections and recovery teams pursue repayment, seize collateral where legally permitted, liquidate assets, and attempt to minimize losses. These operations represent the final stage of the direct lending lifecycle before loans are written off or transferred to secondary distressed markets.

Unit Overview

Recovery operations focus on reclaiming value from defaulted loans. Institutions pursue repayment through borrower negotiation, collections actions, repossession of secured assets, foreclosure proceedings, and liquidation of collateral.

Each recovery pathway attempts to maximize the amount recovered while managing legal costs, operational expense, and reputational considerations. Lenders must determine whether repayment plans, asset liquidation, or legal enforcement will provide the best outcome.

Why This Matters in Lending Operations

Credit losses are an unavoidable part of lending. Effective recovery processes help reduce those losses and preserve institutional stability. Poor recovery management can increase loss severity and expose institutions to legal or reputational risk.

Understanding collections and recovery allows students to see how lenders complete the credit lifecycle. Loans that cannot be cured or restructured ultimately move into recovery operations, where lenders must balance financial discipline, legal compliance, and borrower treatment standards.

What You’ll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How lenders pursue repayment from defaulted borrowers
  • How repossession and foreclosure processes work
  • How lenders liquidate collateral to recover loan value
  • How recovery strategies influence loss severity
  • How charge-offs finalize unrecoverable balances
  • How recovery operations connect to distressed loan sales

Operational Competencies

  • Explain the role of collections teams in defaulted loans
  • Understand repossession and foreclosure pathways
  • Recognize how liquidation strategies recover collateral value
  • Identify the steps leading to charge-off accounting treatment
  • Describe how recovery outcomes affect portfolio loss performance

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • How do lenders recover value from defaulted loans?
  • When do lenders repossess collateral or pursue foreclosure?
  • How are recovery strategies chosen for distressed loans?
  • Why are charge-offs necessary in credit accounting?
  • How do recovery outcomes influence portfolio performance?

Lessons in This Unit

Recovery Foundations

  • Lesson 29.1: What Collections and Recovery Do

    Learn how lenders pursue repayment and manage defaulted loan accounts.

  • Lesson 29.2: Borrower Negotiation and Repayment Attempts

    Study how lenders attempt to recover funds through negotiation and repayment plans.

  • Lesson 29.3: Repossession and Collateral Seizure

    Examine how lenders recover secured assets when borrowers fail to repay.

  • Lesson 29.4: Foreclosure and Legal Enforcement

    Understand how lenders use legal processes to enforce loan agreements.

Loss Resolution

  • Lesson 29.5: Asset Liquidation and Recovery Strategy

    Learn how lenders sell recovered collateral to reclaim loan value.

  • Lesson 29.6: Charge-Offs and Loss Recognition

    Study how lenders recognize unrecoverable balances through accounting charge-offs.

  • Lesson 29.7: Connecting Recovery to Portfolio Risk Management

    Bring together recovery outcomes, loss severity, and institutional credit risk management.

Connected Units

  • Unit 28: Restructuring and Special Servicing

    Review how restructuring attempts to stabilize distressed borrowers before recovery actions begin.

  • Unit 30: Loan Syndication

    Study how shared credit arrangements distribute risk among lenders.

  • Unit 31: Secondary Loan Markets

    Learn how distressed or nonperforming loans may be sold to other investors.

Study Support

  • Templates & Tools

    Review recovery planning templates, liquidation analysis tools, and collections workflow guides.

  • Glossary Support

    Review key terms such as foreclosure, repossession, liquidation, recovery rate, and charge-off.

  • Case Examples

    Study real-world recovery scenarios involving collateral seizure, foreclosure proceedings, and loan loss management.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to explain how lenders recover capital from defaulted loans through collections, legal enforcement, collateral liquidation, and loss recognition processes.
