Insurance & Risk Management Track • Layer 5: Risk & Controls

Unit 25: Fraud Detection and Claims Integrity

Learn how insurers detect fraud, monitor suspicious activity, investigate questionable claims, and enforce integrity controls. This unit introduces the systems and workflows used to protect insurance operations from fraud and abuse.

Home Department Professional & Occupational Studies Financial Systems Insurance & Risk Management Track Unit 25

Where This Unit Fits

This unit continues Layer 5: Risk & Controls. After studying reserving and catastrophe risk, students now examine how insurers protect operations from fraud and misuse. Fraud detection and claims integrity are essential to maintaining financial discipline and fair outcomes.

Understanding this unit prepares students for further study of compliance systems, enterprise risk management, and governance frameworks.

Unit Overview

Insurance fraud can occur at many stages, from policy application to claims submission. Insurers use monitoring systems, analytics, and investigative processes to detect suspicious patterns and prevent improper payments.

This unit introduces fraud risk indicators, monitoring systems, suspicious claims investigation, integrity controls, coordination with external investigators, and escalation procedures. The focus is on how insurers maintain trust and protect financial stability through disciplined fraud controls.

Why This Matters in Insurance Operations

Fraud increases costs, distorts pricing, and undermines trust in the insurance system. If fraud is not controlled, insurers may pay invalid claims, leading to higher premiums and financial losses.

Understanding fraud detection helps insurance professionals identify risk signals, interpret suspicious activity, and ensure that claims are handled fairly and accurately.

What You'll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How fraud risk appears across insurance operations
  • How insurers detect suspicious claims and patterns
  • How monitoring systems support fraud detection
  • How investigations are conducted for questionable claims
  • How integrity controls prevent improper payments
  • Why escalation and reporting are critical in fraud cases

Operational Competencies

  • Recognize common indicators of insurance fraud
  • Understand how monitoring systems flag suspicious activity
  • Explain how fraud investigations are conducted
  • Identify when escalation is required
  • Interpret how fraud controls protect financial outcomes

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • How do insurers identify fraudulent claims?
  • What happens when a claim appears suspicious?
  • How are fraud cases investigated and escalated?
  • How do integrity controls protect the insurance system?

Lessons in This Unit

Fraud Detection Foundations

  • Lesson 25.1: Insurance Fraud Risk Indicators

    Learn how insurers identify signals that suggest potential fraud.

  • Lesson 25.2: Fraud Monitoring Systems

    Study how technology and analytics are used to detect suspicious activity.

  • Lesson 25.3: Suspicious Claims Investigation

    Examine how insurers investigate claims that raise fraud concerns.

  • Lesson 25.4: Claims Integrity Controls

    Understand how insurers implement controls to prevent improper payments.

Coordination and Escalation

  • Lesson 25.5: Coordination with External Investigators

    Learn how insurers work with law enforcement and external partners on fraud cases.

  • Lesson 25.6: Fraud Escalation and Reporting

    Study how fraud cases are escalated, documented, and reported.

  • Lesson 25.7: The Claims Integrity Control Framework

    Connect detection, investigation, controls, and escalation into one unified fraud management framework.

Connected Units

  • Unit 24: Catastrophe Risk and Exposure Management

    Build on risk control concepts by examining how fraud risk differs from catastrophe exposure.

  • Unit 26: Regulatory Compliance and Consumer Protection

    Extend fraud controls into regulatory requirements and consumer protection standards.

  • Unit 27: Enterprise Risk Management Programs

    Connect fraud detection to broader institutional risk management frameworks.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit students should understand how insurers detect fraud, investigate suspicious claims, enforce integrity controls, and maintain trust in insurance operations through disciplined monitoring and escalation.
