Unit 14: Pricing and Valuation Data Infrastructure

Every portfolio value ultimately depends on one critical input: price. Pricing and valuation data infrastructure determines how assets are valued, whether using real-time market prices, vendor-provided data, or internally modeled estimates.

This unit examines how pricing data is sourced, validated, and governed across systems, and how valuation frameworks ensure that portfolio values remain accurate—even when markets are illiquid or prices are uncertain.

Where This Unit Fits

Following Unit 13’s focus on reference data, this unit examines how pricing data is applied to those instruments to produce portfolio valuations. Reference data defines what an asset is; pricing data determines what it is worth.

Unit 14 provides the valuation layer that connects market data to portfolio accounting, performance reporting, and client statements.

Unit Overview

Pricing and valuation systems aggregate data from exchanges, brokers, and third-party vendors to assign values to financial instruments. For liquid assets, pricing may be based on observable market trades. For less liquid or complex instruments, valuation often relies on models and estimates.

Students will examine how pricing feeds operate, how vendor data is integrated, and how fair value methodologies are applied when market prices are unavailable or unreliable. The unit also explores how illiquid assets are valued and how pricing exceptions are identified and resolved.

Finally, the unit introduces governance frameworks that ensure valuation processes are consistent, auditable, and compliant with regulatory and accounting standards.

Why This Matters in Wealth & Asset Operations

Accurate pricing is essential to every aspect of portfolio management. Incorrect prices can distort performance, misstate risk, and lead to incorrect client reporting.

Valuation becomes especially critical in stressed or illiquid markets, where reliable prices may not be readily available. In these situations, firms must rely on models, controls, and governance frameworks to determine fair value.

Operations professionals play a key role in ensuring that pricing data is accurate, validated, and consistently applied across systems, making valuation integrity a core operational responsibility.

What You'll Learn

Core Concepts

  • How market price data is sourced from exchanges and trading venues
  • How pricing vendors provide consolidated and evaluated pricing feeds
  • How fair value pricing models are applied to non-observable markets
  • How illiquid and complex assets are valued
  • How price validation checks ensure data accuracy
  • How stale or missing prices are identified and handled
  • How valuation governance ensures consistency and compliance

Operational Competencies

  • Explain how pricing data flows into portfolio systems
  • Analyze differences between market prices and model-based valuations
  • Evaluate pricing exceptions and validation processes
  • Interpret valuation methodologies for illiquid assets
  • Understand governance frameworks for pricing oversight

Institutional Questions This Unit Helps Answer

  • Where do asset prices come from and how are they verified?
  • How are assets valued when market prices are unavailable?
  • What controls ensure pricing accuracy across systems?
  • How are pricing errors detected and corrected?
  • How do firms maintain consistent valuation standards?

Lessons in This Unit

  • Lesson 14.1: Market Price Data Sources

    Examine how pricing data is generated from exchanges and trading venues.

  • Lesson 14.2: Pricing Vendors and Feeds

    Understand how third-party vendors provide consolidated and evaluated pricing data.

  • Lesson 14.3: Fair Value Pricing and Models

    Analyze how valuation models are used when market prices are unavailable or unreliable.

  • Lesson 14.4: Illiquid Asset Valuation Methods

    Explore how complex and illiquid assets are valued using estimation techniques.

  • Lesson 14.5: Price Validation and Quality Checks

    Study the controls used to verify pricing accuracy and consistency.

  • Lesson 14.6: Stale Pricing and Exception Handling

    Understand how missing or outdated prices are identified and resolved.

  • Lesson 14.7: Valuation Governance and Oversight

    Examine governance frameworks that ensure valuation integrity and compliance.

Practical Application

By the end of this unit, students should be able to explain how pricing data is sourced, validated, and applied, and how valuation systems ensure accurate portfolio values across different asset types and market conditions.

This knowledge prepares students for roles involving pricing operations, valuation analysis, and financial reporting, where accurate asset valuation is critical to both internal decision-making and external reporting.
