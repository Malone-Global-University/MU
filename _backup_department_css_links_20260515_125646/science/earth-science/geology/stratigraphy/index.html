Stratigraphy

Stratigraphy studies layered rocks as records of time, environment, and geological sequence. Students learn how sedimentary layers form, correlate across regions, preserve fossils and environmental evidence, and help reconstruct Earth’s history through ordered relationships between rock units.

  • Rock Layers

    Examine bedding, layering, contacts, and sedimentary sequences as physical records of deposition and environmental change.

  • Relative Dating

    Learn how geologists determine the order of geological events using superposition, cross-cutting relationships, inclusions, and fossil succession.

  • Sedimentary Basins

    Study regions where sediments accumulate over time, preserving records of tectonics, climate, sea level, erosion, and biological activity.
