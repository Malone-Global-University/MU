Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical Engineering studies the design, analysis, and operation of machines, thermal systems, mechanical structures, and moving mechanisms. Students learn how physical forces, motion, energy, and materials combine to create engineered systems across industrial, manufacturing, transportation, and robotics applications.

  • Dynamics and Kinematics

    Study motion, velocity, acceleration, forces, and the mathematical description of moving mechanical systems.

  • Statics and Mechanics of Materials

    Examine force equilibrium, structural loading, internal stress, strain, and material response under applied loads.

  • Thermodynamics

    Learn how heat, work, energy transfer, and entropy govern engines, refrigeration, and thermal systems.

  • Fluid Mechanics

    Analyze fluid behavior, pressure, flow, turbulence, and fluid-system interactions in engineered applications.

  • Machine Design

    Study the engineering of gears, shafts, bearings, fasteners, and mechanical systems built for strength, efficiency, and reliability.

  • Robotics and Mechatronics

    Explore integrated mechanical, electrical, and software systems used in robotics, automation, and intelligent machinery.
