Dharmic Traditions

Study Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, and Sikhism, exploring philosophy, ethics, and spiritual practices across South Asia.

Home Department Religion Dharmic Traditions

Course Overview

This course provides an in-depth study of Dharmic religions, emphasizing philosophy, ethics, rituals, and cultural impact. Students will explore how these traditions shape spiritual and social life in South Asia and beyond.

Major Dharmic Religious Traditions

  • Buddhism

    Founded by Siddhartha Gautama (the Buddha) in India, Buddhism teaches the path to enlightenment through ethical living, meditation, and insight into the nature of reality. Central concepts include the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path.

  • Hinduism

    The world's oldest living religion, originating in India, encompassing diverse beliefs, rituals, and philosophies. Hinduism emphasizes dharma (duty/ethics), karma (action and consequence), and moksha (liberation from the cycle of rebirth).

  • Jainism

    An ancient Indian religion emphasizing non-violence (ahimsa), truth, and asceticism. Jains follow rigorous ethical practices to achieve spiritual liberation (moksha) and liberation from karmic cycles.

  • Sikhism

    Originating in Punjab, India, Sikhism is a monotheistic faith that emphasizes devotion to one God, equality, social justice, and honest living. The teachings of Guru Nanak and the ten Sikh Gurus form its spiritual foundation.

Topics Covered

  • Hinduism

    Explore Vedas, Upanishads, deities, rituals, yoga, and philosophical schools of thought.

  • Buddhism

    Study the life of Buddha, the Four Noble Truths, Eightfold Path, meditation, and monastic practices.

  • Jainism

    Examine principles of non-violence (ahimsa), asceticism, cosmology, and ethics in Jain philosophy.

  • Sikhism

    Learn about Guru Nanak, the Guru Granth Sahib, Sikh ethics, rituals, and community life.

  • Comparative Philosophy

    Analyze philosophical similarities and differences between Dharmic traditions and other religious systems.
