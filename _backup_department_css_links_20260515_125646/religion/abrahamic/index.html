Abrahamic Traditions

Study Judaism, Christianity, and Islam, exploring theology, history, and cultural influence worldwide.

Home Department Religion Abrahamic Traditions

Course Overview

This course provides an in-depth study of the Abrahamic faiths—Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. Students will examine theological foundations, historical development, sacred texts, rituals, and their cultural impact across societies.

Topics Covered

  • Judaism

    • Explore Jewish history, sacred texts (Torah), beliefs, rituals, and cultural practices.

  • Christianity

    • Study the life of Jesus, biblical texts, church history, denominational differences, and contemporary practices.

  • Islam

    • Examine the Qur’an, Prophet Muhammad’s teachings, practices, and the role of Islam in modern society.

  • Comparative Theology

    Analyze similarities and differences between the Abrahamic faiths in ethics, worship, and social structures.

  • Cultural Influence

    Understand how these faiths have shaped art, literature, law, and global history.

Timeline of the Abrahamic Religions

  • c. 2000 BCE — Abraham and the covenant tradition begin
  • c. 1200 BCE — Moses, Exodus, and the giving of the Torah
  • 1st century CE — Jesus of Nazareth and the rise of Christianity
  • 7th century CE — Muhammad and the revelation of the Qur’an
  • Medieval era — Development of Jewish, Christian, and Islamic law and philosophy
  • Modern era — Global spread, interfaith dialogue, and contemporary challenges
