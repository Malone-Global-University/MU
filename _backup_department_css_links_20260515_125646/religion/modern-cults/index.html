Modern Cults

Examine contemporary new religious movements, sects, and emerging spiritual groups around the world.

Course Overview

This course introduces students to the study of modern cults and new religious movements, exploring their origins, beliefs, practices, leadership structures, and social impact.

Modern cults and new religious movements (NRMs) are religious or spiritual groups that emerged primarily in the 20th and 21st centuries. These groups are often studied for their unique beliefs, charismatic leadership, high levels of social control, and separation from mainstream society. While some have had significant cultural, social, or spiritual influence, others have gained notoriety due to extreme practices, apocalyptic beliefs, or violent incidents. This list highlights notable examples from around the world, illustrating a wide spectrum of modern religious experimentation and organizational structures.

Modern Cults & New Religious Movements

  • Branch Davidians

    A sect that broke away from the Seventh-day Adventist Church, led by David Koresh. Known for the 51-day Waco standoff in 1993, which ended in a fire killing 76 people.

  • The Family International (Children of God)

    Founded by David Berg in 1968, combining counterculture ideals with evangelical Christianity. Notorious for extreme practices, including “flirty fishing” and allegations of child abuse.

  • Falun Gong

    A Chinese spiritual movement combining meditation, qigong, and moral teachings emphasizing truthfulness, compassion, and tolerance.

  • Heaven’s Gate

    Led by Marshall Applewhite, this UFO-focused movement believed members must leave their bodies to reach an alien spacecraft. In 1997, 39 members died in a mass suicide in California.

  • Peoples Temple (Jonestown)

    Founded by Jim Jones, this racially integrated congregation moved to Guyana and gained infamy in 1978 when over 900 members died in a mass murder-suicide.

  • LaVeyan Satanism

    An atheistic, modern religious philosophy founded by Anton LaVey emphasizing individualism, ritual symbolism, and self-determination rather than supernatural belief.

  • Love Has Won

    A small modern movement focused on "Mother God" Elena Young, known for its online-based teachings, controlling ideology, and unconventional spiritual claims.

  • Manson Family

    Led by Charles Manson in the late 1960s, this small apocalyptic cult committed a series of infamous murders in California, including the death of actress Sharon Tate in 1969.

  • Neo-Gnosticism

    Contemporary movements inspired by ancient Gnostic thought, focusing on personal spiritual knowledge, esoteric teachings, and the duality of spirit and matter.

  • NXIVM

    A self-improvement group revealed to operate as a secretive, abusive cult with hierarchical control over members, recruiting through workshops and charismatic leadership.

  • Order of the Solar Temple

    A secret society founded in the 1980s, claiming to achieve spiritual “transit” to the star Sirius. Known for mass suicides and murders in Switzerland and Canada in the 1990s.

  • Rajneesh Movement (Osho)

    Founded by Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh, this group built a utopian city in Oregon during the 1980s. Notable for the 1984 bioterror attack poisoning local restaurant salad bars to influence a local election.

  • Raelism

    A UFO-centered religious movement founded by Claude Vorilhon (Rael), teaching that extraterrestrials created humanity and promoting meditation, world peace, and scientific advancement.

  • Scientology

    A modern religious organization founded by L. Ron Hubbard emphasizing auditing, spiritual development, and the concept of the immortal soul (thetan). Often criticized as cult-like by ex-members and scholars.

  • Twelve Tribes

    A controversial restorationist group founded in the early 1970s, living communally and often scrutinized for strict discipline and requiring members to give up personal possessions.

  • Unification Church (The Moonies)

    Founded by Sun Myung Moon in 1954, known for mass wedding ceremonies and the belief that Moon was the messiah completing Jesus’s mission.

  • UFO Religions

    Belief systems centered on extraterrestrial beings, cosmic salvation, and modern mythology, often incorporating guidance from aliens or space-related prophecy.

Topics Covered

  • New Religious Movements (NRMs)

    Study emerging religious movements, their doctrines, community practices, and cultural significance.

  • Cults & Sects

    Analyze small, often controversial religious groups and their social dynamics.

  • Charismatic Leadership

    Explore the role of charismatic leaders in shaping beliefs and practices of modern cults.

  • Social & Cultural Impact

    Examine the influence of modern cults on society, law, and interfaith relations.

  • Case Studies

    Investigate specific modern cults, their history, controversies, and contemporary relevance.
