East Asian Religions

Study the spiritual traditions of East Asia, including Taoism, Confucianism, Shinto, and regional Buddhist practices.

Home Department Religion East Asian Religions

Course Overview

This course introduces students to the major religious and philosophical traditions of East Asia. Students will explore their history, rituals, ethics, scriptures, and cultural influence across China, Japan, Korea, and neighboring regions.

Major Asian Religious Traditions

  • Confucianism

    A philosophical and ethical system originating in China based on the teachings of Confucius, emphasizing moral virtue, social harmony, and proper conduct within family and society.

  • Shinto

    The indigenous spiritual tradition of Japan focused on reverence for kami (spirits), nature, and ancestral traditions. Shinto rituals play an important role in Japanese cultural life.

  • Taoism

    An ancient Chinese spiritual tradition centered on harmony with the Tao, the fundamental principle underlying the universe. Taoism includes philosophical teachings, meditation practices, and a rich tradition of ritual and mythology.

Topics Covered

  • Taoism

    Study Taoist philosophy, cosmology, rituals, and the pursuit of harmony with the Dao.

  • Confucianism

    Examine Confucian ethics, social philosophy, governance, and ritual practice.

  • Shinto

    Explore Japanese indigenous spirituality, kami worship, shrines, and seasonal festivals.

  • Buddhism in East Asia

    Analyze the development of Mahayana Buddhism, Zen, Pure Land, and their cultural adaptations.

  • Comparative Perspectives

    Consider interactions between Taoism, Confucianism, Buddhism, and local practices, including syncretism and cultural influence.
