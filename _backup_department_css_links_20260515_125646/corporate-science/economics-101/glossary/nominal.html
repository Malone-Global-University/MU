Nominal

Economics Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary Nominal

Definition

A value measured in current monetary terms without adjusting for inflation or changes in purchasing power.

Why It Matters

Nominal figures can mislead economic analysis if inflation is high, so economists often compare them with real values.

Example

A salary increase from $50,000 to $52,000 is a nominal increase before considering inflation.
