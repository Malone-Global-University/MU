Search Costs

Economics Glossary – Malone Global University

Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary Search Costs

Definition

Search costs are the time, effort, and money spent to find information or locate the best option, such as comparing prices, quality, or sellers.

Why It Matters

They can reduce competition, allow price differences to persist, and influence which markets work efficiently.

Example

A shopper spends hours comparing interest rates before choosing a loan.
