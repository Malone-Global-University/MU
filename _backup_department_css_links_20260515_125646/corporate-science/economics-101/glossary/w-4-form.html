Home Department Corporate Science Economics Glossary W 4 Form

Definition

A W-4 form is a U.S. tax form employees complete to tell their employer how much federal income tax to withhold from each paycheck based on filing status and other factors.

Why It Matters

It helps prevent large tax bills or large refunds by aligning withholding with expected tax owed.

Example

A worker updates their W-4 after getting married so the correct amount is withheld.
